\n Last night we had a informal mini-GM roundtable.

First context\u2026

NerdNYC's Gotham Gaming Guild starts tonight. We grabbed 30-35 people, mixed them up, rented space, and now we're going to play 5-6 session RPG mini-campaigns every other Friday night.

The point\u2026

We went back and forth discussing GM tips\u2026 a lot of which I'm sure you've heard before\u2026 but a few were less common bits of wisdom. 1 tip was really obvious in retrospect but I haven't seen it mentioned much and has worked wonders for me\u2026

"Bored? Have monsters burst through a door and attack!"

Not that but in the same vein\u2026

"Nervous? Introduce an NPC that's nervous and talk in character how nervous you are."

It works!

My fallback is an NPC who is new to their job or is coming out of retirement and is nervous because they haven't done this in a while. You almost immediately feel more relaxed and as a bonus often the players become more empathic to you and your NPC.

What uncommon, often not quoted in books, GM tips do you use?
