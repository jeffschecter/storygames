\n Today I thought about my life in roleplaying and it spans almost 30 years! I know that the list below leaves out quite a few games and the dates may be off a bit but here is my RPG r\xe9sum\xe9.

Basic and Expert D&D (1980)
I was 8 years old and this was the first game I played with a friend and his fathers group. The game was packed and you had to wait for an opening to play. I was so exited when I was finally allowed in the group.

That didn\u2019t last too long since my mother fell prey to the whole D&D scare and forbade me to play.

Star Frontier
This was the compromise game since it didn\u2019t deal with magic and demons. Crash on Volturnus rocked and I was hooked.

Eventually my mother came around and I was officially allowed to play D&D even though I was playing in secret for a while. I played in several groups and ran a few of my own. For a while that was pretty much it for me. I eventually graduated to Advanced D&D around 1983 and started playing with the \u201cbig kids\u201d. I was also making tweaks to my games and coming up with home brews that were very bad fantasy heartbreakers.

1983
Gamma World
My friend hipped me to this game during a D&D session where we were transported to the blasted earth of the future. I hated this game and thought it sucked.

1985?
The first RPG Golden Age
My friend Dan (who I played AD&D with) gave me this box of games his brother (I think) bought at the swap meet. He was never going to play them and knew I was a RPG fiend. That day opened my eyes to gaming in so many different ways. Up until that point it was D&D , Star frontiers and Gamma World. I knew of others from reading Dragon but had no idea there were others out there.
The games were:
Aftermath
Danger Incorporated (I think this was the name)
Villains and Vigilantes with supplements
Superworld
Call of Cthulhu with supplements
Stormbringer with supplments
Dragonraid

I kept all of them except Aftermath, Danger incorporated, and Dragonraid. I kick myself for that since now I would like to have them to look over from time to time.

Of the lot I loved Stormbringer the most followed by Call of Cthulhu and Villains and Vigilantes. Superworld wasn\u2019t as cool looking a super\u2019s game as V&V but it did share a common system with the other Chaosium games.

1986- (My experimental phase)
I became a gaming whore trying everything from Champions to Car Wars. I was a cheap RPG whore who didn\u2019t care whos dice I polished. I played everything with everyone. I stuck primarily with AD&D but everyone new that I was down for midnight booty calls with strange games.
I found Star Wars this year too. I played the hell out of this game and the other West End Game Ghost Busters. I can\u2019t tell you how many people I drug into gaming with this one.

That all came to an end when I found Robotech

AD&D and Stormbringer were my sold fixes for RPG entertainment but Robotech made me flip out. I loved the anime and was a huge fane of shows like Gundam and Dragonar so this game was made for me. I soon found Palladium fantasy (which I didn\u2019t like at the time), TMNT which I loved as much as Robotech being a comic fan too, and heroes Unlimited which I loved because I loved the other Palladium games.

    \n 1988?
    I lived at a ski resort for a few months while going to school and saved a ton of money. When I got home and went to my hobby store I found a new love: Warhammer 40k. I blew almost my entire wad on the books and armies. Bought a Space marine army, Eldar, am Ork and the core book with some terrain. I would have bought more but the salesperson actually stopped me and suggested I play with what I had to see if I liked it.

    I loved the game and played with anyone who would play or could afford to.

    This was also the year that I finally got a hold of one of the most awesome books ever printed. RIFTS was by far the coolest game I had ever come across and it blew my mind. I played this game like a fiend.

    1989
    My life long GM Sam Hyde god sick with leukemia and died that year. He was like a brother to me and I really loved him. I inherited all of his D&D miniatures (two full cases) all his book (multiple copies of almost everything TSR put out) and his dice. For a while I was lost.
    I played games and got hipped to Runequest, Twilight 2000, Traveler, Shadowrun, FASA Star Trek, Battletech, and Rolemaster\u2019s Cyberpunk game. This was also the year I tried my hand at really writing an RPG. The Hitchhikers Guide to the Galaxy RPG\u2026it sucked ass

    1990-93
    these were the years where things got really cool in my gaming life. I found a whole slew of new games that opened my eyes to a new world of possibilities. This was the year I found some of my truest loves in gaming; DC Heroes, Torg, Dark Conspiracy, Cyberpunk 2013, GURPS, and Vampire the masquerade. Of the ones mentioned DC Heroes did the trick for me. I had played in a lot of super games but none gave me what I wanted until then. DC Heroes flipped the scripted on how I thought about games.

    1993-96
    I played a lot of games during this period and was in a regular LARP game where I met my second wife (The 1st one I met during a Cyberpunk2020 game...i should have known I\u2019d get screwed). I played a lot of Vampire, Mage, Nephilim, Mekton, Streetfighter, Champions, Over the Edge, Pendragon, and GURPS. Cyberpunk and later Cybergenerations were two mainstays during this time.
    I played only two CCGs in my life Shadow Fist and Illuminati.
    A lesser known RTalsorian game, Dreampark, helped me with game design during this period. My roommate at the time was great because he was just as enthusiastic about comics and RPGs as I was and we vibed off of one another a lot. We pushed each other to run the best games and got a lot out of one another\u2019s company. During this time I started working on what would become GODSEND Agenda.

    1997-2001
    Played a lot of LARPS, Hercules & Xena, Feng Shui, Jovian Chronicles, Legends of the Five Rings, Fading Suns and a TON of In Nomine. SJ game\u2019s In Nomine is my second favorite RPG ever. I loved this game and played the heck out of it. All my friends were playing Armageddon at the time but I couldn\u2019t stop playing In Nomine. This game consumed me for quite a few years.

    2001
    I start to write GODSEND Agenda this year and play it and loads of Exalted.

    \n 2002
    The Second Golden Age of RPG
    I played a ton of RPGs up to this point but this year I had a huge paradigm shift. I was enlightened by a ton of games like Sorcerer, Little Fears, Star Children, Children of the Sun, Dark Continent, Seventh Seal, Legends Walk, Kult, Nobilis, Underworld, and a lot of others I\u2019m forgetting. Oh yeah, my game came out this year but went unnoticed since 3 other supers games came out that year. Hero5, Mutants and Masterminds, Silver Age Sentinels, and Godlike\u2026who knew. For years there wasn\u2019t a supers game in site and in the same year we all hit...or at least they did. I was the red headed stepchild
    A lot of smaller games got washed away when D20 hit and sadly only a few games survived the deluge. That year I met a lot of people I consider friends to this day.

    As a side note:
    When I got into publishing games a lot of people blew me off because they had their cool money making D20 product. Ron Edwards, Andy Kitowski, Mark Simmons, David Salisbury, Lewis Pollak, and Gareth Skarka treated me like a human being. They had no reason to beyond maybe simple human decency but I really appreciate it.

    2003-2006
    A lot of people have said that the gaming industry is dying but I don\u2019t see it. I\u2019ve played lots of new games and managed to find new ones each year. I see a lot of games out there that push the boundaries of what we think RPGs are or should be.

    2007
    Last Gencon I brought home a ton of new games that I have yet to play but are proud to won. Steal Away Jordan, Reign, Jihad, Blossoms are Falling, Heroes Banner, Spirit of the Century, The future looks incredibly bright to me and I have no worries that I will be playing RPGs until I\u2019m wearing diapers. I consider this hobby an activity and an artform.


    I look back at this list and I know I will be adding something here and there once I remember

    \n Oh, the high points would be:

    * Vampire and Werewolf (oWoD) for introducing me to disconnected attribute and ability, and bringing in more story elements.
    * Over The Edge, for introducing me to free-form do-it-yourself skills.
    * Ars Magica and Mage for introducing me to the open-ended magic systems I was looking for.
    * Theatrix for introducing me to diceless, story-based resolution.
    * TORG/Shatterzone/Masterbook for introducing me to systems where you can do other useful things in combat besides attack and defend, and for their neat card system that packs so much use in one feature.
    * Castle Falkenstein for melting character sheets down to something portable, enjoyable, and meaningful.
    * Everway for melting it even further and bringing in a whole new visual dimension to story creation.
    * 7th Sea for the best and longest campaign I ever played in (4 years), and true swashbuckling.
    * Feng Shui for introducing me to bonuses for good narration.
    * Truth & Justice for introducing me to abstract damage systems.
    * Primetime Adventures for being the game that most influenced my subsequent play even though I didn't like the game itself!
