Rolling below target heart rate:
1: You roll and fail, then you get to reroll to try again, if you push. Can you push again if you fail a second time?

Risky rolls:

Techniques:
9: Armed: You get +1 on Force rolls per CP spent. Is this per roll or do you spend it once on the weapon and after that you get that bonus for the rest of the mission? Can you spend this after a roll has been made?

    \n I'm not the authority, but I recently ran Lacuna, so here's my interpretation.\n
    1: You roll and fail, then you get to reroll to try again, if you push. Can you push again if you fail a second time?
    \nIn my game, I let players push as much as they wanted, until they hit their maximum heartrate.\n

    2: Do you always lose an attribute point from the attribute you rolled, i.e. lose Force if you rolled Force and Instinct if you rolled Instinct, or do you/Control choose which one is lost?
    \nI believe RAI, you should lose the stat point you used. But RAW, it's unspecified.\n

    3: Do you still lose a point if you push and succeed on the second roll?
    \nOn risky actions, if you push until success, you don't lose any ability points, no matter how many times you pushed.\n

    4: Do you lose two points if you push and fail on the second roll?
    \nIf you pushed a bunch, but still failed, you only lose 1 ability point.\n

    5: If you lose a point on the first roll, do you reroll with one less die?
    \nNo. You don't lose ability points until you stop pushing and still fail.\n

    6: If an attribute drops to 0 and you have to make an Access roll to eject or die, can you push this roll? I would guess not?
    \nI'm not sure why not. Unless you're at your maximum heart rate.\n

    7: When you push, you have to roll the same number of dice you rolled the first time, right?
    \nWhat makes you say that? I'd let players continue choosing the dice until they leave their target heart range.\n

    8: The rules say you get a new talent when you've earned 10 CP in a mission, but I assume that should be if, at the end of a mission, you've earned 10 CP in your carreer, since it seems impossible to earn 10 CP during one mission, at least without repeated use of Meditation?
    \nAgreed. I let people carry CP. When they have 10, they can spend them.\n

    9: Armed: You get +1 on Force rolls per CP spent. Is this per roll or do you spend it once on the weapon and after that you get that bonus for the rest of the mission? Can you spend this after a roll has been made?
    \nNot sure.

    \n Great! Thanks for the feedback!

    I think I'd want to be careful with letting people pushing as much as they want and then suffering no consequences other than the raised heart rate if they succeed on the final roll. Don't you get the effect that nobody fails a risky roll until they reach maximum heart rate?\n

    6: If an attribute drops to 0 and you have to make an Access roll to eject or die, can you push this roll? I would guess not?
    \nI'm not sure why not. Unless you're at your maximum heart rate.
    \nFirst of all, it seems strange in the fiction. you're dying and you need to eject immediately or die. It seems strange that you can "try again". If you fail, you're dead or insane.
    Second, it seems anticlimactic in game, since there's not that "finality" of the roll. Rather, you streatch it out until it's no longer dramatic.
    Third, there's never a reason to not reroll until you reach your maximum heart rate. If you have a low Access and a low heart rate, you'd be rerolling loads of times, keeping track of your heart rate, until you either reach your maximum or roll an 11+. this seems unfun.\n

    7: When you push, you have to roll the same number of dice you rolled the first time, right?
    \nWhat makes you say that? I'd let players continue choosing the dice until they leave their target heart range.
    \nI don't know. I guess it's no issue. I don't know why I assumed you couldn't change it.\n
    Agreed. I let people carry CP. When they have 10, they can spend them.
    \nRight. And of course you only get one CP per roll. But should it be one per attempt, or can you get two if you roll and reroll? I think I want to leave it at one per attempt, to discourage rerolling even more.

    \n I figured that, since they're dreaming, the only thing that can actually kill them is their heart rate. Therefore, I want them rerolling as much as possible so they can ramp it up to near max.

    I encourage rerolling.

    Mechanics-wise, at it's heart, Lacuna feels like a push your luck game. How long can agents stay in the dream without ejecting. If they get too close to their maximum heart rate, they'll lose the ability to push/reroll, therefore access rolls to eject can become deadly.

    I gave commendation points for every 6 rolled. Not just one per roll.

    \n Jared clarified some of the vague parts ages ago, but sdanic has everything correct.

    You don't fail an action if you push, roll again, and succeed, so you wouldn't lose an attribute point, but the rules do say that Static goes up when you fail the first roll, so there's that which depends on how you want to interpret it.

    For Armed I think the name might just be a name. I'd assume you get to spend the CP to boost the result after the roll, like other special powers let you add after you roll. It's for when you are 1 or 2 short of 11 and you just nudge it over the line.
