\n Hey all,
\n
\nMatt Snyder and I are organizing an Ashcan Front booth for Gen Con again this year. The main changes are:
\n
    \n[li]Gen Con raised its booth prices by quite a bit, and so we had to raise the buy-in cost by a little as well.[/li]
    \n[li]And we're partnering with The Playcollective on a two-booth endcap. It'll be a clearly divided space, with separate shelving and everything. But the Playcollective shares our enthusiasm for ashcans, and so we'll have some of theirs on our shelves and Matt and I will have
\nMy Life with Master and Nine Worlds in with their other games. (We're excited about this partnership, because it means Matt and I can represent for our core titles without diluting a stand-alone Ashcan Front presence with them, and because it means Ashcan Front participants will be right in with the community of designers, working together, making connections, feeding each other's enthusiasm, and talking about design.)[/li]
\n
\nA 2008 FAQ has been posted at http://www.ashcanfront.net/2008faq.html. Give it a look.
\n
\nPaul

    \n Hey Jason,\n
    Posted By: Jason MorningstarWhere do you stand nine months out from the Gen Con booth? This seems like useful information for prospective joiners.
    \nSorry, I totally missed your question among a flurry of whispers. The short answer is that I think every designer who participated last year received feedback on their game that they wouldn't have received otherwise.

    In some cases it got them to the next stage. In some cases it caused them to take the game back to their original concept for another effort. I can say that I'm within striking distance of my design goals for Acts of Evil largely as a result of playtesting and feedback I received from the ashcan.

    We have a pretty great lineup of participants for this year, so I'm already geeked. But we have room for a few more.

    Paul
