\n This is happening right now and you are all missing it. A 48-hour game design jam, all over the world. Near you probably.

Vidjagorms, you say!!! I don't program!

Well, it doesn't have to be videogames. You can make tabletop games, too.

But!

If you are a tabletop designer or tinkerer, you have plenty of skills to bring to the table. My team consists of me, a programmer, and an art/sound guy. They both have good ideas, but I've found (maybe because I'm firmly grounded in the tabletop space?) that the way I think about and communicate and organize game mechanics and procedures has been a big help to my group (the poor programmer has to do the heavy lifting, though: I'm all "Here's the game, now make it work!").

There's a "prompt", like a Game Chef theme (last year it was a picture, this year it was a sound file), and then a bunch of extra constraints you can put on your design or not. One of the big constraints this year is "Games for Change." Jane McGonigal is even hanging out at the Portland venue!

So, next year, do it. You can show up solo (like I did) or roll in there with your tabletop team and fuck the place up. Put it on your calendars. In Portland, at least, it's free and free food is provided all weekend.
