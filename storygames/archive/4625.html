\n I just recently watched The Duellists. I'm also a big fan of the "Sharpe's" series. I like me some Napoleonic setting. But, there's no game for it. Well, there's wargames with miniatures of whole regiments, but (at least as far as I know) no RPG I'm willing to try. But I've been haunted by the thought of trying Agon for it. Bear with me.

First off, I couldn't find my copy of Agon anywhere - if you have it, please return it. Or don't - I already ordered a new copy, this time with PDF I can sneak looks at while working. Here's what I'm thinking:

Name Die = Rank Die
Heroic Traits change to things like Noble, Brave, Chivalrous, Honourable, Keen, etc.
Instead of a patron god/goddess you have your military specialty: Grenadiers, Hussars, Sappers, Fusiliers, Lancers, Dragoons, etc.

Sword = Sabre
Spear = Bayonet
Javelin = Pistol
Bow = Carbine, Musket, or Rifle (depending on military specialty)

Haven't done the abilities yet, but seems like it'll be fairly straightforward.

For inspiration, look only as far as the Vinkhuijzen collection of military uniforms.

Obviously, there won't be Gods and Monsters, but there might be a few heroes vs. a whole slew of enemy soldiers, which is like a monster, right? or against a cunning Portuguese bandit captain, or a legendary French sappeur who beheads people with his monstrous axe.

Does this thing have legs? Or am I barking up the wrong tree?
