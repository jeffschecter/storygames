\nAlas Vegas, my long-in-gestation game of amnesia, sin, gambling and sand, went live on Kickstarter yesterday, chasing a funding target of \xa33000. It smashed through it in within eight hours and kept going. Right now it's on \xa34400, and the first two stretch goals are unlocked. The third is a completely new setting and adventure for the Fugue mechanics that drive the game.

Alas Vegas isn't exactly a story-game, but it's not exactly an RPG either. There's a GM and players, but while the GM runs the game in the present, the players control the mechanics for rebuilding their memories and therefore their pasts... which will eventually explain how they came to start the game by clawing their way out of shallow graves on the edge of the desert outside a massive, bizarre casino city. I'm really pleased with it, I think it's some of the best game-design work I've ever done.

There's lots more information on the Kickstarter page, and I'm happy to answer questions if you have them.

http://www.kickstarter.com/projects/jameswallis/alas-vegas-an-rpg-of-bad-memories-bad-luck-and-bad
