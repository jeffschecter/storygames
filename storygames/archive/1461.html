Shab al-Hiri Hack?

Iso
September 2006 edited September 2006 in Story Games
This morning (sometime between the shower and the door), it struck me that the Shab al-Hiri Roach is an imminently hackable game. All you need to do is come up with an alternative list of scenes and characters, perhaps an alternative model of specialties. Depending on the game, you might want to change the color of how the roach work (for example, Dark Side for Jedi game, perhaps with a brief spotlight scene for going over to the roach, err, dark side). Similarly, you need to establish the two realms (academy v. town could be easily transformed into Jedi Academy v. field missions, to go with the previous example).

The rules, the cards, those pretty much take care of themselves. Again, you might need to do some on the fly interpretation, but the cards (esp. Roach cards) are already built to be open to interpretation for maximal play use.

Has anyone tried to work out full-blown hacks like this for the Roach? Heck, if you have a group who shares a particular setting knowledge (like Star Wars fans), you could probably have the alternative scenes, NPC's, specialties, hammered out in almost no time, with the hack and the game still only being an evening of play.

    I think the cool thing, though, is that the Roach could easily be imported into settings that don't immediately bring up power inequity. BUT, as soon as you 'code' them into the Roach rules, you will get those power dynamics brought front and center. Let me be a little flip:

    Commune: The Roach Hack

    Everyone takes on the role of a member of an egalitarian commune in rural Oregon. Players choose the skills they contribute to the commune (yay, I can keep the solar generator going; yay, I'm a spectacular alfalfa sprout farmer). They then establish small jealousies and loves. Now, the commune has close ties to a small town through which they procure all the goods they can't grow, as well as sell their excess goods (Commune and town instead of town and gown).

    I bet you could take the literal roach out of the fiction, make it 'capitalism run amok' or something, and you will *still* get some serious mileage out of the game. Sure, the 'capitalism run amok' is going to be a bit cartoonish and excessive, but probably in a suitably creepy-funny way suitable to the roach.
