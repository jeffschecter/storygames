    This is something I've been interested in, actually.

    The weird thing about economic competition is that it's indirect. GM and Toyota can't fight in the sense of Toyota can't force GM to stop making cars; instead, they produce cars and customers pick which ones they like, and it might be the case that enough people prefer Toyotas to GM cars that GM can't afford to keep making them and gets out of the business. Businesses come into conflict, but they can't really take direct action against their rivals.

    This is interesting from a story-making perspective.
