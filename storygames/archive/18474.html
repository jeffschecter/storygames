Here's a link to the Nerd SoCal group. https://facebook.com/groups/250898448335673/ and here's the link to the June Game Day. https://facebook.com/events/260930277380304/?ref=3

    We also have a pretty strong presence at the Strategicons (Orccon, Gamex, and Gateway).

    \n That sounds very cool! Pasadena is two hours by bike, which is totally doable on a Sunday.

    I *love* running Apocalypse World. I have somehow acquired a reputation for running AW well, even though I've only done one-shots at conventions so far. It is ABSOLUTELY perfect for my style of GMing though (which is wild and lose and enthusiastically incorporating anything players come up with). There's a bunch of standbys that I love running/facilitating: various Fiasco playsets (Graham Walmsley's Gangster London playset is a favourite), my dear friend Sebastian Hickey's Hell For Leather (http://cobwebgames.com/index.php/games/hell-for-leather/), the various jeepform games I own (Montsegur 1244, Witch: The Road to Lindisfarne, Crossroads)... basically I love showing up with a bunch of games and see what people can get excited about.

    So I'll just show up Sunday two weeks from now and see what gaming there is to be had. Thanks!

    \n If you're a non-car person, you should get to know the metro bus and train systems. Most are pretty bike friendly. And the amount of weirdness is not any lesser or greater than I've seen in other public transportation systems.

    Not that I want to discourage you from 4 hours of biking on a Sunday. But if you wanted to cut it down a bit, that route actually has decent public transport options, especially supplemented by the bike.

    For future reference, when you want to go outside of LA, your public transport possibilities dwindle considerably. Hell, basically any routes that aren't toward or away from downtown from where you are will tend to have almost no options.
