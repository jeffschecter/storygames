Can anyone recommend good mechanics for characters

\n
    \n
  • Changing organizations and institutions
    • \n
  • Starting religions
    • \n
  • Shaping the collective dialogue
    • \n
\n

I'm thinking about characters like Martin Luther King Jr., Mikhail Gorbachev, Alexander the Great.

\n

Like, it's cool that my Wizard builds a tower, but it's even cooler if he starts a school of magic that will last for 1000 years.

\n

Alternatively, it's cool if my Fighter kills the Emperor's Champion, but it's even cooler if his victory marks the beginning of the end of an empire.

\n

Really, this is "just" stake-setting. But does anyone have good techniques for this stuff as a bolt-on to other games?

    \nMisspent Youth has this, though probably not as detailed as you're looking for. At the end of each session, the world is changed by creating new facts, destroying old facts, and/or converting existing ones. So, if we win in this session, we get to take over the psychic mind control devices that The Authority constructed, and begin to use it as a psychic Twitter-like tool to organize protest and action.

    \n I've been wanting to start up a 3:16 game, but keep toying with the idea of porting the general "Space Marines with Big Implausible Guns" setting to FATE because FATE seems have rules for beefing up vehicles, companions, minions, and so on as the characters get their level up points.
    \n
    \nIt would be more of a fuzzy strategy game flavor sprinkled on top than really digging into social issues within the game fiction changing. But in general, the idea of a game having mechanics to measure out how everyone connected to the PC is changed by his/her advances is big to me.
    \n
    \nHaving just come fresh off the very, very disappointing end to a long Descent boardgame campaign, i'm feeling very wary about the vertical number treadmill. Broadening a character "horizontally" such that who he is in the world becomes a deeper and more significant node in the fiction has extra allure right now.

    \n The old dystopian supers game "Underground" had a neat mechanic where each mission/adventure/whatever, if you manage to defeat the problem, you could improve the level of one of the community's traits, like "Medical Care*". This would also raise one other specific trait on the chart (maybe better health means less desperate criminals, so "Safety*" goes up too), but another would go down (a drop in "Income*", damn those medical taxes!).

    So, there was some strategy involved in trying to make lasting change.

    I don't know if that fits what you're looking for, but it might act as inspiration.

    (*I'm making up these stat names. I can't remember exactly. But the idea is there.)

    \n The default rules for this in AW are the "workspace" mechanics. The player tells the MC what they want to accomplish and the MC tells them the steps they need to go through in order to do it. There's no particular reason these mechanics have to be limited to building machines and, in fact, there's that Angel move that already extends it to working on people. The Catalyst mini-supplement I wrote lets you treat a community as your workspace once you've eliminated other potential challengers to your authority.
