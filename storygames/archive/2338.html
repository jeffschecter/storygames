Acts, Scenes, WTF

Jason_Morningstar
February 2007 edited February 2007 in Game Design Help
Help me out here. I'm looking for a logical, three-level division of content that corresponds to dramatic structure.

The game is divided into three big chunks, each of which is intended to take an evening of play, that I am currently calling Acts.

Within each Act are three smaller chunks, each of which contains some spotlight time for each character and lasts about an hour. There are nine of them and they must be referred to as #1 through #9. I am currently calling these Scenes. So you get "Act Three, Scene Nine."

Within each Scene are very small bits that focus on individual characters. They aren't numbered or ordered at all. I am currently calling these Vignettes.

But Vignettes are actually scenes in common parlance. And it sounds better to say "Your mission scene" rather than "Your mission vignette". Vignette is a weak-sounding word. I want to replace it with scene, and find better words for the next-higher levels of structure.

RobNJ suggested Act - Chapter - Scene, but that sticks in my brain because it mixes theatrical and literary conventions. Any suggestions would be appreciated.

    As an alternative, you might consider switching over to the literary model. One thing that occurs to me is that if you're numbering the nine sections #1 through #9, then you might just skip having an official top level of the hierarchy. As Jeph says, many people expect that "Part Nine of Act Three" would be the ninth part of Act Three, rather than the third part of Act Three. Within the book world, though, it is usual for chapters to be numbered sequentially, even if the book is divided into different volumes or parts.

    So rather than saying "Act III, Scene IX", you would usually just say "Chapter 9". However, you could still say that Chapters 1 to 3 are part of "Volume 1" (or maybe even just "Session 1" -- that's a little more explicit).

    Thanks for all the suggestions. I think I'm going to go with this:

    Session: An evening of play (was Act)
    Chapter: Takes place on a specific day of the Uprising (was Scene)
    Scene: An event that occurs during a Chapter (was Scene)

    ...so I obviate the need to distinguish "Act", which has no procedural weight at all, and call the two pieces that do have some procedural importance something obvious. The game has eight Chapters, a prologue and an epilogue. An evening of play is three or four Chapters. Each Chapter has two Scenes per player. The literary metaphor works fine and reads fine in text, unlike some of the more evocative suggestions like "turning point" or "flashpoint", unfortunately. Using references to days or datelines gets confusing because they scale linearly but refer to disjointed segments of time.
