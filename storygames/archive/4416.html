    \n Alright, let me try this again. Premise is this: I'm trying to make a system which can be used in a lot of genre settings. The game, in brief, is based on the idea of people before and during a time of crisis, firstly sitting around telling anecdotes about their pasts to pass the time before the proverbial hits the fan (and in doing so making their characters), and then taking on the impending doom-type affair.

    The stats were being generic because I wanted to be able to use them in any such situation, from French Nobles sitting around in a darkened chateau, listening to the mob roaring outside and knowing that sunrise brings their doom, to the British Naval galleon that knows it will catch up with the nasty privateer come daybreak, to the high school football team on the bus to the big game, joshing around with each other but all the while knowing that the final is coming up in a few hours. For now I'm concentrating on the French nobles, because its the idea I have set most firmly in my head.

    The Traits I'm looking for are to be specific to the individual setting though. So while "Superbuff!"(strength) and "Amazing long pass"(Combat?) might be suitable trait for the highschool football variant, "Master duellist"(Combat) would be more appropriate to the French Revolution game, or "Richest Man in Burgundy"(Strength/Charm) or "Seductive Beauty"(Charm). As you can see, I'm all about the lame traits right now though, which is where I thought you guys would be able to help me. I plan (at the moment) to have a set of traits to choose from for the players to buy for their characters during the course of the game. I'm not sure, but it seemed to work better if the pool was limited, and the traits presented on little cards. I'm still working on this though, so seeing what sort of traits people can come up with might clarify how it'll work in my head.
