    \n Sorcerer is party agnostic.
    \n
    \nBut to be clear. Sorcerer would be a pretty horrible game to use for Full Throttle.
    \n
    \nThe Sorcerer mechanical rules are simple and elegant enough to use for a wide variety of purposes and could do Full Throttle just fine.
    \n
    \nI'd almost certainly dump the Humanity concept altogether, and probably dump most of the demon subsystems as well...although a simplified Contact / Summoning / Binding structure to represent finding, acquiring and making a bike your own might be fun. But I'd dump the whole Need / Desire structure and limit the "powers" to just those bikes that can do cool extra things...like the rocket booster ones.
    \n
    \nAt which point...all you really have left is a super simple character structure and a super flexible dice pool mechanic.
    \n
    \n
    \nBut really...since the FullThrottle Fudge game already exists, I'd probably just go with that.

    \n In my opinion, the main reason Sorcerer would be interesting for Full Throttle is the lack of a definite social resolution mechanic. That is to say, if you want to actually affect something in a tangible, absolute, permanent way, you have to do so with your fists. Social conflicts can provide rollover dice, bonus dice, and colour, but cannot actually resolve anything for sure.

    Couple that with awesome, super-powered bikes. Wow.

    As for Humanity, the problem is not Humanity itself, it's how the Sorcerer rules make Humanity interesting by pitting it in opposition with the demons. I wouldn't personally remove the stat, because Full Throttle has a pile of interesting Humanity-type content which would be fun to play with. Some tweaking would be required to ensure that Humanity isn't diametrically opposed to the Demons (=vehicles) though, since that'd skew the story in a weird way compared to Full Throttle.

    Maybe minivans and hovercraft could be Demons, while motorcycles could be the first type of Angel (Sorcerer's Soul)? Humanity would be defined as "Autonomy, Rebellion, Freedom", that sort of thing. Heh.
