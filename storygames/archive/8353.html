    \n Me too. Fifty seems a bit high, not that it matters. Fun is fun, numbers is numbers. Tautologies aren't always, but both those might have been. I've had fun each year.

    Saint, to answer your question, scheduling doesn't seem to be a high priority. Folks kind of congregate and then they go play games. You might have to be prepared to strike up a conversation with some folks and engage in the negotiation dance. If your main motivation is visiting your friends when there is something to do, the Madison indie film fest is like the weekend before, if dancing doesn't sound like something up your alley. It's really pretty easy, you just talk to folks who are standing around, ask if they are looking to play a game and discuss what the group of you like. We typically also have name-tags (thanks Dave) so you have a better idea who is looking to dance.

    As for stuff to do outside of gaming, there's a thriving downtown area from campus to the Capitol called State Street. Saturday mornings (from like before I wake up until noon) there's an enormous Farmer's Market on the Capitol square. That's always fun to walk around - and totally get some spicy cheese bread yum yum. There's usually live music somewhere, there's a strong theatre community, lots of bars and dance places and tons of coffee shops. There are museums and lakes and malls and UW-M's Union which boasts lots of beer, a gorgeous terrace, and ice cream that's made a few buildings down.

    \n Roy, it's definitely a by-gamers-for-gamers thing. Some of those gamers happen to also be game designers, but there's nothing frightening about that. ^.^

    Come play stuff! It'll be a smorgasbord of different games and game styles. Bring games you want to run, and/or wander to a table and join in on whatever's getting played there. I know folks who bring huge bags full of different games and basically take requests.

    Last year there was an informal time/space set aside for designers to sell things, which I hope to take better advantage of this year, but it's pretty low-key on that count.
