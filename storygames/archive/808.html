    1= total failure make it painful

    Like Conan, before de Camp got his claws into him? Hmm... yeah, something like that.

    My thought was that the "continuity" in Venture Brothers mirrors exactly the riffing on previously introduced elements found in a narrative game. "Oh, remember that funny scene with... what did we call him... Turtle Man in the restroom from the first game? Well, uh, so Turtle Man is mowing his lawn, and..."

    This is different from a television "story arc," where the creator has roughed out from the beginning the A-story and when various elements will be introduced.

    Of course, PTA, being a creative game, actually does a little of both of these, rather than lock everyone into the Director's Chatanooga Choo-Choo.

    I'm only being pendantic about it because I think there's a useful game design distinction going on here.
