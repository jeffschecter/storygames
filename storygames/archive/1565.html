where are the sprawling, baroque games of yesteryear/tomorrow?

markv
October 2006 edited October 2006 in Story Games
it's part reality, part myth that most indie/story/forge style games are tightly focused. Since I've enjoyed trying these games, l'm assuming that focus is a good thing & produces fun play.

But I'm also starting to yearn for games that are big and baroque and sprawling while also incorporating some of the 'indie' techniques/ systems/ tools.

When I mentioned this last night to Joshua & Judson from our game group, they tried to get me to clarify what I was after.

An expansive, dense setting? Probably, but the setting isn't the whole measure of game sprawl/complexity. And a setting can be sprawling by being encyclopedic or by creating a huge canvas and just hinting at pieces of it, or starting players out in one corner at letting you define the rest.

Multiple interconnected systems, like cyberpunk games having different rules for combat, hacking, gear etc? That might be fun, if there is a reason why they are distinct.

Being able to 'do anything' in the game rather than defined missions/roles? probably - or at least multiple pathways to interact with the game/setting (see below).

Different modes of play? I do like the idea of having almost multiple focused games in the same setting.

Anyway, beyond the obvious that I should try to create something like this if it interests me, are there sprawling, baroque games in the works? Any ideas on bringing together focus & premise & the like to intersect with a 'big' game concept: either to make it more manageable/playable or to slice it up and make it even more allurinly complex?

  • Robert Ahrens
    October 2006
    I think HeroQuest also does this kind of gaming, particularly if you bring Glorantha into the mix.

    The HeroQuest rules are (arguably) pretty new-style: one focussed conflict resolution system for everything. But those rules in their application can be brought to bear on very different types of conflicts, be those strategic, political, emotional/romantic, spiritual, religious or what-have-you. Coupled with the Glorantha vibe of intense polygenreic weirdness(*) packed in next to each other, I really think HeroQuest has some pretty good opportunities for sprawl, and for locally focussed density -- where the focus is whatever the group needs it to be in the current story arc.

    ( * Riders of flying bees vs zombie hunting ducks, anyone? Or maybe you'd prefer a game about religious tensions between different flavours of monotheism that were isolated from one another by a centuries-long dream-smog?)

    Part of the reason for this is what HeroQuest doesn't do -- which is to say that it doesn't have any real "focussed" mechanics for handling any particular thing. It just has a handful of "universal" conflict res mechanisms which you are at liberty to use as you will.

    (I also agree with those who favour SotC and I think TSOY is a pretty good candidate too.)
