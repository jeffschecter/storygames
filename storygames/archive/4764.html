    \nBasic Game Terminology:
    \n
    \nGM/DM: Game Master/Dungeon Master; a role in a certain type of RPG who authors adversity, creates and plays characters other than those controlled by the non-GM players, and in some games, creates the world. Sometimes, major characters favored by the GM are referred to as GMPCs.
    \n
    \nNPC: Non-Player Character; usually used in GM-centric games to describe the major and minor characters played by the GM.
    \n
    \nPC: Player Character; a character directly controlled by one of the players, typically used in games with a GM, and not usually used to describe characters played by the GM. Normally, there is only one PC per player, though some games and game groups are an exception to this.
    \n
    \nGame Theory Terminology:
    \n
    \nGNS: Gamism/Narrativism/Simulationism; The core concepts of a theory created by Ron Edwards. Since the original coining of the phrase, it has been replaced by a more complete theory called the Big Model. For more information on both GNS and the Big Model, read the articles and the theory forums at the Forge (indie-rpgs.com)
    \n
    \nLines and Veils: Lines are themes, concepts and events that may be uncomfortable or traumatic for some members of the group, and which are explicitly not allowed to occur. Veils refer to themes, concepts and events that also may be uncomfortable, but which the players are okay with being addressed in play, but which should not be explicitly played out; It refers to "drawing a veil" over the events, or fading to black.
    \n
    \nSocial Contract: The spoken and unspoken agreements in a gaming group that form the basis for all system in play. Things included in Social Contract can include, but are not limited to, food and drinks, themes of play, lines and veils, etc.
    \n
    \nAdd more as appropriate.

    \n GMPC: A character that acts like a player character (PC) in most regards but is controlled by the GM.

    DitV: Dogs in the Vineyard

    AD&D: Advanced Dungeons and Dragons
