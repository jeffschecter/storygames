\n Starting a mini-campaign (3 games). My friend Matt of 30 Days of Gaming "fame" had been itching to play some core fantasy which is largely absent in many of the Indie games he's been exposed to. Therefore, before he heads off to Iraq we're going to get in a few episodes of a hack we came up with.

We're going to play Dogs in the Vineyard (maybe with Princes Kingdom character gen) set in a core fantasy setting playing Paladins.

We discussed some of the details of the setting last night on our way home from playing Contract Work. Basically the idea will be that we'll play in a pretty standard fantasy setting (no names have been worked out yet or if it will just be a published setting). We want monsters (ettins, and beholders, and dragons, oh my!) Magic will look like standard fantasy magic however the source will be important. All magic will be from worship and bestowed upon the worshippers by the god/creature. The idea of the campaign is that we'll be routing out practitioners of magic that come from places other than the gods - outsiders and dragons, mostly. We have determined that the last game will SO be about fighting a dragon.

The only rules issue we've raised thus far is that spells and magic items will work a little like Donjon in that they will be assigned key words and those words will have dice associated with them. When you levy the keyword from the item or spell you add in those dice.

More as we work out the details.

    \n You know, I basically figured you'd just take every mention of traits in the rules and apply it equally to powers. So you'd pick dice from your trait pool at chargen for both bonus and fallout.

    Perhaps the GM might assign additional dice to powers when s/he makes backgrounds for the particular game. But, I'm pretty sure that the normal number of trait dice can cover powers and enough other traits to flesh a character out.

    As for experience, fallout, and reflection, I'm assuming anything applying to traits there can also apply to powers. So you could choose to increase or decrease the die size of power bonuses and fallout ratings. In the case of power fallout, it'd probably have to work 'opposite' of the recommended thing - so where Experience lets you increase a die size, you'd decrease it for power fallout, 'cause that'd be the more beneficial result. As for learning new powers, maybe you need to bank two reflections/experience for it - one to give it a die in each category.

    Still torn on whether powers should be able to grow past one die, also... my gut tells me that'd break mighty quick.
