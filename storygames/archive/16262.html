If she\u2019s anything like the Pennsylvania Dutch kobolds I know, she\u2019ll trade you for a long, bad joke. Try this one out:

    After some pleading a young Snyder county Dutch boy made a "walk-along" date with a visiting girl of some charm. He was short; she was tall. Walking along a road leading from town he begged and begged for a kiss, but she was not interested. He begged further, but she said: "You are so young and little; I'm not gonna bend down to kiss you." Not far ahead at the outskirts of town at a blacksmith's shop the boy spied an anvil mounted on a block, and jumped on it, saying as he did so: "Now I'm as big as you, so how about the kiss?"
    "Oh, well, if you must," she managed to say.
    And so he got a sort of peck-like kiss, not exactly what he was looking for. He jumped down, and they continued to stroll on toward the familiar lover's lane in the woods. Further on he again begged for another kiss, saying: "How about it?"
    "No," she replied.
    "Aw, come on," he coaxed.
    "No, I said, and I mean it" she declared.
    Positive?" he asked, expectantly.
    "Positively, and that's that," as she glared at him.
    "Well, then," he said, "if you won\u2019t let me do it again, I'm gonna throw this damn anvil away!"

    Think you\u2019ve never heard this joke before? Look in your couch cushions. If your keys are there, you just made a trade.
