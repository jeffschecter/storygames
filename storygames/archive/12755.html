\n Inspired by this thread, I wrote this undoubtedly mathematically busted simulation game. Don't try to play it, because it will hurt your brain unless you computerize the "between the circuits" part.

Setup
Put 100 points in the pool (loose change where each penny represents a point, for example)
Everyone makes a GAME CREATOR. Give him/her a name and a SKILL of d6.

Play
Play goes around the table with each person taking a turn, moving clockwise. On the first turn, everyone performs the Write a game action. On subsequent turns, perform one of the following actions:

Write a game
Name the game.
Roll 2d6. Add your SKILL. The result is your game's INGENUITY. If this is the highest INGENUITY of your games, increase SKILL by d3.
Roll 3d6. The result is your game's COMPLEXITY.
Set the game's COST as 0.
Give the game a starting BUZZ equal to your SKILL.
Mark your name on the game's OWNED BY box.

Improve a game
\tChoose one of your games. Roll d20.
\tIf the value is higher than current INGENUITY, this value increases by d3. If this is the highest INGENUITY of your games, increase SKILL by 1.
\tIf the value is lower than current COMPLEXITY, this value decreases by d6. Increase SKILL by 1. Decrease COST accordingly.
\tIncrease BUZZ by SKILL.

Publish a game
\tChoose any one of your games.
Set the game's COST to be equal to current COMPLEXITY + GLOSS.
\tSet a PRICE between 0 and 50.
\tSubtract COST from PRICE and record it as REVENUE. If REVENUE is negative, set REVENUE to 0.
\tMark the game as IN PRINT.

Apply gloss
\tChoose one of your games.
\tRoll 2d6. Remove this amount of points from your pool (or all your points if the value exceeds your total).
\tIf the number of points removed is higher than the current GLOSS value, replace the current value with the new one.
\tAdd the value from the highest die to the game's COST.
Add the value from the lowest die to the game's BUZZ.
\tSet a new PRICE between 0 and 50 if you like.
Adjust REVENUE based on new COST and PRICE. If REVENUE is negative, set REVENUE to 0.

Buy and read a game
\tChoose one of your competitor's games.
\tPay its PRICE from your point total. The owner of the game receives REVENUE for the game.
\tIf this game has a higher INGENUITY score than any of yours, increase SKILL by d3.
\tMark your name on the game's OWNED BY box.

As a free action, you may also do either of the following on your turn:

Spread a game on the Internet
\tChoose a game which you OWN (your own or your competitors').
\tMark it as IN CIRCULATION

Download a game
\tChoose any one of your competitor's games marked as IN CIRCULATION
\tIf this game has a higher INGENUITY score than any of yours, increase SKILL by d3.
\tMark your name on the game's OWNED BY box.

Between full circuits
Order games marked as IN PRINT and IN CIRCULATION in order or BUZZ, highest to lowest. In case of equal BUZZ, put games in order of GLOSS, then COST.


Roll d20 - this value is called PURCHASING POWER (PP).
If PP rolled is lower than the amount currently in the pool, go down the list of games in order:

If the game is IN CIRCULATION and IN PRINT, roll 2d20.\n
If the value rolled is higher than its PRICE - BUZZ + COMPLEXITY:\n
Increase its BOUGHT value by 1.
\t\t\tIncrease the game's CIRCULATION by 1.
\t\t\tIncrease the game's BUZZ by 1.
\t\t\tRemove PP or COST (whichever is lower) points from the pool
\t\t\tGive the game's REVENUE value as points to the Creator
\n
If the value rolled is lower than its PRICE - BUZZ + COMPLEXITY:
\t\t\tIncrease the game's BUZZ by 1.
\t\t\tIncrease the game's CIRCULATION by 1.
\n\tIf the game is IN CIRCULATION but not IN PRINT:\n
Increase the game's BUZZ by 1.
\t\t\tIncrease the game's CIRCULATION by 1.
\n\tIf the game is not IN CIRCULATION but its PRICE - BUZZ + COMPLEXITY is lower than PP:\n
Increase its BOUGHT value by 1.
\t\tRemove PP points from the pool.
\t\tIncrease the game's BUZZ by 1.
\t\tGive the game's REVENUE value as points to the Creator
\n\tIf the game is not IN CIRCULATION and its PRICE - BUZZ + COMPLEXITY is higher than PP:\n
Decrease its BUZZ by 1
\n\tFinally, swap the game's position with the game just below it in the order (note: this is broken, games should trickle down - errata forthcoming)
If PP is higher than the amount currently in the pool\n
Reduce the pool by 1 and
\tStarting from the BOTTOM IN CIRCULATION game on the list, roll 2d20 as above and evaluate. Whenever a game is BOUGHT, remove it from the order.
\nWhen all points are gone from the pool\n
Reduce BUZZ on all games by 1.
\tRefill the pool with 100 points and start the circuit over.
\n
Quitting the game
A player may quit at any time, setting their own win conditions. Suitable win conditions could be: highest total POINTS, highest total BOUGHT, highest total CIRCULATION, highest total BUZZ, highest total INGENUITY, highest total GLOSS, or any combination thereof. I'd personally go for a high total SKILL among all GAME CREATORS.

When all players have quit, everyone has won, except those who claim they didn't.

    \n Another action, suggested by the ever-growing fan community, the implementation details inspired by this thread:

    Critique game

    Choose any game. Roll a d20.
    If the result is lower than your SKILL, increase or decrease the BUZZ of the game by the difference between your roll and your SKILL.
    If the result is higher than your SKILL, increase the BUZZ of the game by d3; lower the BUZZ of all your own games by the same amount (excluding the target game, if you own it)

    \n Another fan community contribution:

    (optional rule) Grognard factor

    During the end of the circuit-end phase, if any game has >10 BOUGHTs than its closest competitor, decrease the BUZZ of all games with higher INGENUITY by d3.
