    When we originally started working with RPI, our rep was Katy Byrom. She was uniformly professional, responsive and on top of things through two printings of the Roach. Unfortunately, she left the company just before we printed Drowning and Falling. Our new rep is Will Sinclair, who worked with us through the D&F printing and now (working for Alphagraphics) is our rep as we try to reprint the Roach.

    My impression of Will is that he's young and working part-time for the company while he goes to school. Lately I've had a lot of trouble getting him to get back to me (by email) with details about the jobs we want to run. However, when I got him on the phone Friday, I was able to get him to focus on our project and (I hope) finally get things moving.

    On the other hand, thanks to advice from folks on the Forge thread, we've been fishing for quotes from other companies, and have seen some competitive numbers. This print job (200 copies of a 78 page, perfect bound book with a color cover) is coming back at $611 from Alphagraphics. Other quotes we've seen include $1055, $824, and $570 for the same job.

  • Steve_Segedy

    No problem, Eric. I submitted the details to http://www.printindustry.com/cgi-local/new_forms1.cgi?value=dupform.htm, as recommended by Paul Czege. I submitted that about four days ago, and so far I've received three quotes from that. I also submitted another request directly to Publishers Graphics, based on a suggestion from Ralph Mazza.

    Here are the details of our printing requirements (based on our projects with RPI):
    - 200 copies
    - 78pp book, 5.5" x 8.5"
    - 12pt C1S cover 4/0, 1.7 mil layflat laminated
    - Inside 1/c on 60# Accent opaque

    In other words, a 78 page perfect bound book with black and white interior illustrations and a 4 color glossy cover.

    The quotes so far:
    Publishers Graphics - $454.01
    Fidlar Doubleday - $568.90
    Quality Print-On-Demand - $824.00
    Impact Media - $1055.00

    It's useful to keep in mind that these are rough numbers, and subject to change. There might also be better price breaks for a different number of books. QualityPOD, for example, was quick to point out that the per-book cost held at $4.12 no matter what quantity you needed, so (as with most POD outfits) you could order in very small numbers.

    I hope that's useful. If you'd like, I'll post more as I get them.
