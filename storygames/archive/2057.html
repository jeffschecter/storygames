Note: This is in the "little ideas" category for a reason. It's just a little idea - not a full-blown game yet. This thread comes out of this previous one about PBEM, but you need not read it if you don't want to.

So I had this idea for doing Amber on a Wiki. Not straight from Phage Press's book, but using it for inspiration. You have the GM write up a few base pages, like Castle Amber's Entry Hall, the Pattern Chamber and other important areas in the castle, Forest Arden, Shadow Earth, maybe the Logrus Chamber. Maybe brief descriptions of some random shadows out there, like the Golden Circle.

Everyone who pays character points for a shadow gets to write a page for it and link it appropriately. It's their shadow; they can write whatever they want. (GM approval might be good for things like a direct link to Castle Amber...) Those who don't have Advanced Pattern are on their honor not to use the browser's View Source to track down things like hidden links in, e.g. the second period in this paragraph.

Everyone with Pattern can write "open" pages that other folks can screw with. They "travel" by making little pages that link together into a changing string of places until you get to your destination. (This needs guidelines, I feel.)

You are on your honor to only follow links to get from page to page.

When you get Advanced powers you get varying degrees of write access.

If you have a trump deck, it has its own page, which only the GM writes to unless you have Trump.

All conversation happens on the Discussion parts of the pages.

I'm not sure how to do conflict resolution yet, or how/whether to handle Logrus, but the basic idea has a little bit of a hold on me.

--Colin

    Well, maybe not write the events...

    Perhaps set up the situation? I hate, as GM, to dictate such things.

    Meera, if you host, I'd like to moderate.

    Here's what I'm thinking:

    There wouldn't be "character creation" per se... instead, there'd be "character exploration" scenes... and then "conflict generation" scenes... probably numbering a few less than the number of players... and after that, things should start to flow organically.

    Each player would have one character that he's responsible for... but he wouldn't "own" it in the classic sense, not completely.

    During the "character exploration" scenes, each player in the scene would be responsible for defining one aspect of each other character in the scene. Kind of like Corwin's remeniscences with Flora, each player would have his character reveal things about the other characters. At the end of those scenes, I (as GM) could sit down and "stat up" the characters. Or we could do it cooperatively. Or something like that.

    During "conflict generation" groups of characters would be thrown together in scenes where their differences are highlighted. The requirement for each of these scenes is that by the end, the players would be required to either reveal a pre-existing conflict, or create a new one.

    After these scenes are played through, players would be able to help set scenes based on their characters abilities and motivations as established early on.

    I think Tir-na Nog'th is a perfect place to play this out. I'll start jotting down some ideas of what Tir-na Nog'th is like from the point of view of the inhabitants, but here are some early ideas:

    The pattern, and pattern initiates, are ghostly and insubstantial in Tir-na Nog'th. They have a disruptive effect on the inhabitants, causing them to conform to whatever twists of fate they bring with them. Inhabitants of Tir-na Nog'th often retain aspects of personality, or even power, after one of these encounters. When Corwin goes to Tir-na Nog'th and encounters a ghostly Benedict, he's not actually encountering Benedict, but rather a local whose form and personality have been affected by the Pattern's power. As in Rebma, inhabitants of Tir-na Nog'th cannot walk the pattern; if they do, they fade away and dissolve into mist. Amber is seen as a hellish place, dense and earthy and crude.
