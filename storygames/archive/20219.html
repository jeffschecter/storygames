    \n When it comes to typography, here are two sites to read through a couple of times:

    http://www.thinkingwithtype.com/

    http://practicaltypography.com/

    I would also recommend to get a book about typography. I recently got this one recommended below, and it's an all right read.

    The Elements of Typographic Style (50 Mb) - gives you the basics to build upon.

    But here are two others that I find can be more useful for you. They are in themselves examples of how to do layout.

    The Vignelli Canon (4 Mb) - You can skip the first 18 pages because it's just an ode to typography. Focus more on Grids, Margins, Columns and Modules. This is nothing you can't notice, unless you know about it, if you try to learn typography from studying layout in other books.

    Meet Your Type (6 Mb) - Everything you need to know about types/fonts, and how to handle them. Easy read, and pay extra attention to the use of white space in the document. Wish more roleplaying games looked like this (I can only think of Misspent Youth).

    \n If you're attempting to mimic/echo/evoke Apocalypse World...

    The distressed fonts are VTKS Good Luck For You (for covers and titles) and Crust Clean (for text headings). Crust Clean doesn't come with punctuation, so Vincent used Dirty Ego for that. All three are available for free at dafont.com.

    Consolas is the playbook body text, and Verdana Bold is used for the R Rating and the footers and numbering. Those are both standard fonts that you should have already, or you can get them from the MS website.

    The body text in the rulebook is Chaparral Pro, an Adobe font. It comes bundled with certain Adobe programs.

    Whatever original post I stole this info from is of the opinion that Consolas is hella ugly and monospaced fonts are completely illegible so you should just use Chaparral for everything, but ymmv.
