A couple of months ago, I got into a discussion about making an online narrative RPG. So I got down to work and put together a proof of concept game called "The City of Forgotten Gods", set in the world of John Harper's Stranger Things. The setting is a sprawling magical medieval/steampunk city where humans and demons (i.e. everything that's not human) live in uneasy proximity.

The game is 99% ready to go except for one thing. I need adventures. So I thought I'd toss the idea out and see if anybody wants to contribute a peice of their brain to this project.

An adventure in COFG is a short textual hook to spark narration from the player. An adventure is open-ended and evocative. It can also, optionally ask a question, or pose two options (which should also be open-ended). An adventure can lead to another adventure, as can either or both options posed by an adventure. An adventure can also be tied to a specific location.

Here's the "About" text for the game:

In a Strange Land

No one knows who built the City, or when, or why. Or maybe itâ€™s that everyone knows, but their accounts differ. The City is a magical place, a sprawling landscape of broad boulevards and labyrinthine alleys, proud temples and prouder taverns. Masked cults serenade sleeping gods, lecherous spirits stalk the living, and pale demons parlay in moonlit courtyards while all round the restless hum of commerce, love, birth, death, and the business of survival continues day and night.

Humans and Demons

In the City, Humans live in uncomfortable proximity with an awesome variety of other creatures magical, mundane, monstrous, and subtle, collectively known as demons. Demons in the City aren't Devils, and they aren't specifically evil, but their appearance and habits set them apart from humans. Where humans and demons meet, there is always friction. Some humans hunt and kill demons indiscriminately. The opposite is also true.

With a Single Step

In this city, thousands are born, live, die, and are forgotten; but not you. For fate has decreed your journey significant. And so the Librarians of Quaam have dutifully vowed to record it for the generations. You will take the role of a human or demon inhabitant of this City of Forgotten Gods. Fate has apportioned your journey a finite number of steps. At each step you will record your actions and their consequences. When your journey is complete, it will enter the permanent record in the Library, where others may read it.

In the Details

The City of Forgotten Gods is based on an upcoming role-playing game called Stranger Things by John Harper.

I'll do my best to adapt whatever cool stuff to the game. Naturally I'll also acknowledge people's contributions.

    Here are a few of the adventures I added myself. They don't quite have Brendan's flair, I'm afraid, but they may spark some more ideas. Adventures don't have to be dripping with setting (though it helps), they just have to spark some narration.

    "Spare a coin for a poor brother, cast out by kin and kind?" These beggars are the scourge of the city. Yet a beggar is often more than he seems. Who is this beggar?

    A voice cries out, "Stop him! Stop the murderer!" You turn to see a skinny demon fleeing towards you, pursued by a mob of humans armed with ropes and torches. Do you stop the demon, or help him escape?

    Somewhere, not here, they are plotting to kill you. Who are they, and why are they seeking your life?

    A group of night demons approaches you. "Brother, do you not know? None may hunt here, but that they join our pack."', Do you join the pack?
    IF YES: The leader leers at you. "Then tonite you will lead the hunt." Where do you lead them?
    IF NO: The pack falls silent. "Then you must pay the tax." What is the tax? Do you pay?

    A beautiful Amazon in white armor is canvassing the neighborhood with a proclamation. "The city guard is looking for special deputies to patrol the city and watch for illicit demon activity. You look like yo''ll do. What transpires as a result of your answer?

    Without warning you are set upon by a crazed figure in tattered rags wielding a bronze scythe. How do you defeat this foe?
