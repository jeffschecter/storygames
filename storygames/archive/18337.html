\n I mentioned the KS for Autarch's Domains at War when it launched in last month's Stuff to Watch, and have been talking about its Risks and Challenges section over in Game Design Help, but reckoned I'd start a Directed Promotion to point out some particularly cool stuff:

- designer's notes on sieges, historical touchpoints, and the role of magic in warfare, with more to come
- a glimpse of the process of making illustrations specified by backers, with compositional sketches that fascinate me as a non-artist, in the latest update
- a comprehensive walk-thru and review of the Campaigns and Battles manuscripts available to backers

Domains at War is written for the Adventurer Conqueror King System, but is easily compatible with any system that uses hit points and armor class. It's also playable as a stand-alone wargame, with the option to zoom in and resolve individual character-to-character duels, or heroic forays in which characters personally lead attacks on massed troops. The emphasis on the role of commanders, and the way the rules are extrapolated from the roleplaying scale, mean that it's easy to determine how these individual actions affect the course of the battle at large. In my experience, this creates greater identification with "my guy" as the commander of units when playing it as a wargame and engages the visualization gears in a way I'm trained to do when playing a RPG but don't always bring to a boardgame.
