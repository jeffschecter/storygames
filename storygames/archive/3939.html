\n Following the links from Ryan Dancey's latest post (with the aid of archive.org because Sean Reynolds' site is down), we get a summary of WotC's 1999 market research:
\n
\nWotC Market Research Results
\n
\nThe bit that really struck me concerns "Average Sessions before Restart (New Characters)", which is about three-quarters of the way down. The figures are as follows:
\n
\n
Years in Gaming --- Average # of Sessions
\n(all lengths) --- 15.4
\n<=1 Year --- 8.8
\n>1-5 Years --- 12.9
\n>5 Years --- 19.6
\n
\n
\nInteresting? Seems to support the common assertion, here and on the Forge, that long-term campaigns are rare, and that short runs are the norm.
