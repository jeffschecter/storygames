\nA couple weeks later our schedules finally normalized a bit and we had our first session last night.
\n
\nHere's the Authority we came up with:
\n
\nName: BTL, Inc (short for Better-than-Life)
\nDescription: Big technology company that produces all manner of software and hardware designed to make peoples' lives better, which of course makes them more complacent.
\n
\nVice: Utopianism
\nVisage: Corporate
\nVictim: Freedom
\nNeed: Complete market control.
\n
\nSystems of Control:
\n1. BTL's technology works so well at improving peoples' lives users of their products immediately start becoming more complacent.
\n2. Because the world is so connected there's a lot of pressure for people to isolate themselves and communicate solely through technological means (and of course the preferred means are all from BTL)
\n3. BTL needs money to keep making their stuff and it doesn't come cheap. Because of that those too poor are kept out of site lest the more well-to-do start asking questions.
\n4. Any technology that is going to be distributed to the public has to be sanctioned by an entity that's actually separate from BTL, but their fingers are in so deep, it may as well not be. Non-sanctioned technology is illegal.
\n
\nExploit:
\n1. Thanks to bureaucratic policies and the sheer amount of information that flows into the company, it's quite often possible to go (nearly) undetected by their systems. An alarm may get tripped, but it's just as likely to get buried in the shuffle and missed.
\n
\nRating:
\nWe didn't pick a hard and fast rating, but the only guideline we're going with is that swearing should have a purpose, not just "fuck" this and "fuck" that for the sake of it.
\n

    \n This looks pretty solid, minus the bits of characters that aren't yet fleshed out. Don't let them get away with that. Make them finish up before you start playing.

    GMing is like riding a bike: if you fall, you'll smash your testicles. Wait, no, that's not what I mean. Don't worry about being a little out of sorts. You'll pick it up quickly and Misspent Youth takes a lot of pressure off you as GM. Follow the story procedure and know your goals for each act, or whatever Rob is calling them now.

    The second system of control is hot ("Because the world is so connected there's a lot of pressure for people to isolate themselves and communicate solely through technological means (and of course the preferred means are all from BTL)"). Use that as much as possible in play. Set up situations where the YOs need to talk to other people and they have to use VR and text and vidphone and whatever else to communicate. No face-to-face.

    Let us know how it goes!

    \nAuthority Figures\n

    \n

      \n1. Victor: Why do you (Tyr) let Jason bully you around?
      \nTyr : The time I didn't let him push me around, there were consequences at work.
      \n(We decided that Jason is a huge brown noser (to Boss Hogg))
      \n
      \n2. Tyr: How did you (Hiro) cope after the FDA crackdown that took junk foods off the market?
      \nHiro: I prepared myself be detoxing myself. By the time junk food wasn't available, I was already on a healthy diet.
      \n
      \n3. Hiro: Who taught you (Jakey) how to hack?
      \nJakey: The guy that taught me to hack is the same that gets me my drugs: Chris.

      \nVictor: You need something from me.

    \nWhat's Up
    \nQuestion: Jason's bullying
    \nAF: Jason & Hogg
    \n
    \nThe clique is working on the line at work. Picture a factory floor with conveyor belts and such. The clique works on the same line. Tyris at the end doing quality control. Jason comes over and dumps the bucket of bad parts into the bucket of good parts that Tyr's been separating. Drama ensues and eventually Jason just walks off. As he's walking away, Hiro goes after him and throws something at him. It's at just that moment when Boss Hogg comes around the corner to check on things. Struggle!
    \n
    \nSTRUGGLE 1
    \nObjective: The entire clique gets written up for disciplinary reasons. (That'll be a second strike for Tyr!)
    \nHope: Jason loses face in front of Boss Hogg
    \n
    \nTyr gets in Boss Hogg's face (M.O. - Intimidate) to get him to back off, but he's having none of it. Hiro goes on a tirade (outrage) about the down-trodden. Hogg says he's one to talk about class this and class that because at least Jason is trying to better himself. In the mean time Jakey tries faking some errors into Jason's work (hacking). Hogg has some assistants looking into the errors then Jakey tries getting some pity (Orphan), but eventually Victor loses because Boss Hogg knows he has connections (M.O. Got Connections) with the president of a competitor: A. Tech, Inc and accuses him of being a spy. Everyone gets written up and Hogg is going to be keeping a closer eye on the group for now.
    \n
    \nKickoff: How is the clique going to deal with Boss Hogg?
    \n
    \nFighting Back
    \nQuestion: What Victor did.
    \nAf: B.e.T.H.
    \n
    \nThe next morning on the way to work, Jakey & Victor get a video message. It's a video of Jakey meeting with Anthony Patrick, the President of A. Tech, Inc. After a bit of role-play Tyr & Hiro also receive the video and Victor reveals that Anthony is his father! The clique starts arguing amongst themselves about the video, but because they're paranoid they're doing everything via text. Picture 4 teenagers sitting around a table furiously texting each other while shooting them dirty looks. Awesome! Things die down a bit, so I call for a struggle.
    \n
    \nSTRUGGLE 2
    \nObjective: Make it publicly known that Anthony is Victor's father.
    \nHope: Discredit Boss Hogg
    \n
    \nJakey starts out by discovering that the messages originated from a BTL server somewhere (hacking). Hiro loses and decides to sell out his sneaky! He manages to edit the videos in such a way that it looks like Boss Hogg made the video. He also splices audio from other videos of Anthony Patrick to make it look like whatever's going on in the video is illegal -- paying for drugs, child porn, whatever dispicable acts he could throw in and proceeds to distribute it across the internet. Oh, and he doesn't tell anyone about what he's done. Untrustworthy, indeed!
    \n
    \nBeat 1: Discovery: Anthony Patrick is Victor's father!
    \n
    \nHeating Up
    \nQuestion: Junk food being outlawed
    \nAF: Miss Johnson
    \n
    \nThe scene starts with Tyr & Jakey sitting around Tyr's garage. We establish that even though they're illegal, they manage to have a stash of junk food. Hiro and Victor come in arguing. Victor's upset about the video being circulated. Hiro doesn't see the big deal because Hogg and Anthony are both part of the system and it hurts them both. The whole clique joins in on the argument at this point (in the meanwhile, the camera pans over to show Miss Johnson snooping around and eavesdropping). At one point it's established that Tyr has put a ridiculous amount of planning into taking care of Jason (yay OCD). I can see a rift quickly forming between the clique so I want to come up with a conflict that might help get them back together. At one point while pacing around Tyr angrily slams his hand into his door and hears a yelp from outside. It's old lady Johnson!
    \n
    \nSTRUGGLE 3
    \nObjective: Miss Johnson reports the clique to Sheriff Jiminez for gathering together (gatherings are frowned against per a System of Control).
    \nHope: The clique gets closer together.
    \n
    \nTyr immediately goes outside to help her (altruism) because she nearly fell when the door came open. Hiro comes up with a story (fast-talker) about the 4 of them rehearsing for a short play at work that showcases bad workers. Victor immediately comes to his aid (wins by rolling what Hiro rolled for fast-talker) and embellishes the play story a bit. Old lady Johnson buys all of it and even seems to think a little better of the 4 of them for now.
    \n
    \nI narrate a bit more and tell them that after getting Miss Johnson to go away they all start remembering why they're friends in the first place and that arguments will happen, but they'll always get past it. Things are looking better and they talk late into the night about good times.

    \n So, thoughts so far. This will probably be a bit disjointed.
    \n
    \nOverall, everyone had a really good time, I think. By the end of the session I think we were all a little more comfortable with setting up the scene.
    \n
    \nSometimes it felt a little clunky to need to define a friendship question for the beginning of the scene. I wanted to focus on sticking to things as written as much as possible, so I tried to push to make sure the scene at least started right. Maybe one thing that took a little time to realize is that the scene, as a whole, doesn't necessarily need to focus on the friendship question chosen -- that's more a way to kick things off and to try and keep the clique's relationship in everyone's minds. I think that's the intention, but I could be off.
    \n
    \nAt first I was worried that there really wasn't much going on with BTL in this episode, but the more I think about it the more I don't really think that's a problem. Things are just getting started. I like the idea that in the beginning BTL wasn't really on the clique's radar. Sure, they know how BTL is, but more important to them was the situation at The Data Mine with their boss. In the end, I think that works just fine. Then I remembered that's even mentioned in the book towards the end; about how sometimes they start out knowing everything about BTL, but it can be just as fun having them discover all that. So, now I see it definitely going that direction. Time for them to start discovering some fucked up things BTL is doing! And now that they're on the radar, so to speak, because of everything that happened with Victor's father, it makes a lot of sense for BTL to start becoming more prominent in their lives. Before, the only reason I saw B.e.T.H. getting involved was to discredit Anthony, since he'd be a competitor of BTL's.
    \n
    \nI had a hard time trying to match the flow of the game to the scenes as far as building up to a climax and all that. I think one thing that felt like it fell sort of flat was the struggle in Scene 5. It's supposed to be this big climax. First, I had a tough time coming up with a good struggle that I felt like went for the juggular, but before I could even get my feet under me, the YOs won on their second roll! I know it's going to happen sometimes, and it was still pretty awesome, but I wonder if there shouldn't be another way to increase the tension in that struggle aside from what numbers you picked.
    \n
    \nIronically, the first struggle felt a lot more tense, because the rolls happened in such a way that the entire conflict map was full of markers, and it finally came down to the last roll and whether it would fall on a YOs marker or the Authority's.
    \n
    \nThe first time (of two this first session) someone sold out a conviction was a pretty great moment. I could see how hard he was struggling with the decision. The whole time I was holding up a sharpie and grinning. Just waiting for him to confirm the sell-out so I could redact his character sheet. When he finally relented, I cackled with glee.
    \n
    \nI was honestly surprised by how well the kickoff and the two beats fell into place. I read through the scene structure section at least half a dozen times and I kept coming back to those. But once we actually played and the scene was over, it was pretty damn obvious exactly what all those should be. Especially the first discovery that Anthony was Victor's father. That was awesome!
    \n
    \nAnother insight that just occurred to me. I'm starting to have ideas of things I can do to mess with the clique. But it requires having Authority Figures, and I technically have no real power over creating them. Sure, I can offer input and help flesh out ideas, but it's not actually my place to create an AF for a session. I'm kind of at the players' mercy as to what tools I'll have at my disposal each episode. It's an interesting dynamic.
    \n
    \nI'm super excited to see how the episode goes. This first session did a good job of stretching everyone's creative muscles, I think. Kind of like a warm-up. With one session under our belt, I have high hopes of the next session really popping, especially now that I'm starting to have a few flashes of inspiration of how I can push against the clique.

    \n That's a great AP report, Jeremiah! Fantastic! Especially the last post. I care more about what you and the players did than what the characters did, though obviously the fiction stuff is necessary for context.

    Hopefully, the rules talk about The Authority grinning with glee as they take a black marker to the YOs sold-out convictions. It's an important part of the game when I run it, and I think it should get codified (if it isn't already).

    As The Authority, your role in creating Authority Figures is pushing back on the players when they come up with weak choices. Encourage them to make stronger choices, with teeth. Also, in play, there's nothing wrong with tweaking them a little as long as you stay faithful to the players' original vision for the AFs.

    You're right that the GM is totally at the mercy of the players in Misspent Youth, though. If they don't bring the awesome, there's only so much you can do as GM to fix that. Keep a crib sheet of the YOs' convictions and push on them really hard.

    Since you're playing out a series, you have more time to get into things. In a one-shot game, you want to get to the BOOM quickly, but if you have a half dozen or a dozen play sessions, you can tease out the story. It'll be fine. (I've never run a Misspent Youth series, though, so I'm mostly making educated guesses based on my knowledge of the rules and years of GMing experience.)

    It sounds like you guys have an awesome thing going. Keep posting!
