\n I wrote a game recently called Gun Thief, and I want to play it as a play-by-post game. I think that'd work really well, as the game doesn't have a lot of crunch and naturally allows for large blocks of description from each player in turn.

The game's for three, so I need two others.
But we could get multiple games going, if there are more volunteers than that.

Post below if you're interested, and after a day or so I'll formulate groups and send peeps the PDF.


The back cover text, for your consideration:

Bank robberies, deals gone sideways, crooked dames, and the ugly smell of kerosene as it all burns down. The hollow sound when a lead pipe connects with a thick skull. Get-aways, evenings thrown away on a new piece of tail. Everything raw, everything smokey, everything halfway to hell before it even realizes it.

You\u2019ve only ever known two things: how to hold a gun steady, and how to take what ain\u2019t yours. Ain\u2019t no decent men in this world. Ain\u2019t no decent women, either. Still, you\u2019re going to make it through this shitstorm. You\u2019re a gun thief.

Gun Thief is a game for 3 players, but can accomodate up to 4. It is diceless, gives each player a unique role, and is easy to learn.
Gun Thief creates stories that are violently and sexualy graphic. It is not a game for children or easily discomforted adults.


Sources of Inspiration: Sin City, Pulp Fiction, Black Snake Moan, Apocalypse World.

It's definitely a game about sex & violence & chaos.

One player takes the role of The Gun Thief, while the others take the role of The Law and The Jagged Women.
