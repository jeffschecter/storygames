\nBlood Red Sands

A game of competitive role-playing, where powerful heroes confront the terrible might of the Witch Kings of Abalahn.

You are about to enter a savage and brutal world of dark sorcery, a world where the five Witch Kings of Abalahn slew the very gods and have reigned for a thousand years. Blood Red Sands is a game of competitive role-playing, where powerful heroes confront the terrible might of the Witch Kings of Abalahn. Only one hero will reach her goal. Will she throw down the Witch King, and if she does, will she take the place of the defeated tyrant on his throne? Blood Red Sands is a game for three to five players set in a brutal world of swords and sorcery. In each round, a hero is eliminated until only one remains. Will it be yours?

Blood Red Sands is written by Ralph Mazza.

    \n Savage has the main of it.

    Each Ordeal only one player is playing a Hero. Everyone else plays Factions. Factions can be anything from a single powerful sorcerer and his items of power, to a beast master and his menagerie, to a general and his army, to chief and his tribe, to a monster and its brood, or even just a single giant monster...whatever.

    Each Faction has its own objective completely independent of the hero. Conquer something, steal something, kill someone, things like that. And these are all aimed and targeted at each other in a big powder keg of tensions. What you do as Faction is try to 1) complete your objective, 2) stop others from completing their objective, 3) whatever side vendettas and gratuitous screwing with people you can get away with.

    Into that mess comes the Hero who is an immediate source of mechanical effectiveness. Crudely put, the hero is a weapon looking for a target and so immediately the next thing Faction players are trying to do is 4) point that weapon at someone else or at least dodge it yourself.

    After your hero is eliminated...that's what you are still doing. Each ordeal you will take the reins of a new Faction and try to maximize your chance of getting to play The Witch King by scoring as many points as possible yourself and hosing anyone ahead of you. No one is ever out of the game. Even in the final showdown you have the role of Kingmaker and the squabble to not come in last and earn the title of "Vanquished".

    One of the best places to be is to have the people who are ahead of you in points still have their heroes...that way they're out of the running for Witch King.

    \n 1 "set" of dice in BRS is d4, d6, d8, d10 so four dice no d12s or 20s.

    Players start with 4 of these sets or 16 dice, 4 of each. So for a standard 5 player game that's 80 dice.

    Then there are 5 more sets for other purposes in play for a total of 100

    The custom icons are not necessary to play, they are primarily for cool factor. And the icons that Brennen Reese has come up with are pretty cool (hopefully we'll) get some images of them up soon).
