    Premise
    This is a game where lantern-jawed heroes climb into the cockpits of giant robots and stride off to compose delicate haiku for the edification of the emperor. . . and then die for him.

    Goal of Game
    The play of the game centers round the reaction of the emperor when he hears of his champions' deaths: players control the emperor and the mistresses and illicit children of the pilots.

    Character Creation
    Players start by spending time crafting their character's haiku. Each haiku is both your character sheet and a description of your character's eventual doom.

    Start by generating key words for extra points by comparing them to the List of Images developed by the GM for the session.

    Extra points can be gained by:
    A. Including a special season word (the kigo) that is descriptive of the season in which the poem is set;
    B. Combining two different images in lines 1 and 2 into a unified impression through the use of line 3; or
    C. Including a major grammatical break (kire) usually at the end of either line 1 or line 2.

    Multiple haiku are combined into a linked verse form called haikai no renga, which allows you to generate multiple pools of keyword points, each relevant to that verse.

    Allocate points generated in a haiku to particular phrases within it, these will be used in conflict to convince the Emperor that your child, born to one of his concubines, is actually his, and therefore should be named the heir.

    Phrases that are assigned points are either applied to your child as positive descriptors, or negative descriptors of other players' children simply by writing them on a sheet of paper, crumpling the paper into a small ball, and competing for which player can snip them closer to the wall from 4.5 feet away.

    Another concatenation to make things easier

    Premise
    This is a game where lantern-jawed heroes climb into the cockpits of giant robots and stride off to compose delicate haiku for the edification of the emperor. . . and then die for him.

    Goal of Game
    The play of the game centers round the reaction of the emperor when he hears of his champions' deaths: players control the emperor and the mistresses and illicit children of the pilots.

    Character Creation
    Players start by spending time crafting their character's haiku. Each haiku is both your character sheet and a description of your character's eventual doom.

    Start by generating key words for extra points by comparing them to the List of Images developed by the GM for the session.

    Extra points can be gained by:
    A. Including a special season word (the kigo) that is descriptive of the season in which the poem is set;
    B. Combining two different images in lines 1 and 2 into a unified impression through the use of line 3; or
    C. Including a major grammatical break (kire) usually at the end of either line 1 or line 2.

    Multiple haiku are combined into a linked verse form called haikai no renga, which allows you to generate multiple pools of keyword points, each relevant to that verse.

    Allocate points generated in a haiku to particular phrases within it, these will be used in conflict to convince the Emperor that your child, born to one of his concubines, is actually his, and therefore should be named the heir.

    Phrases that are assigned points are either applied to your child as positive descriptors, or negative descriptors of other players' children simply by writing them on a sheet of paper, crumpling the paper into a small ball, and competing for which player can snip them closer to the wall from 4.5 feet away.

    Finally, the game is decided when the Emperor, played by the GM for that round (called a season), exhausts his Patience and declares one of the children to be his heir, and gives him lands somewhere in the Empire. GM duties then rotate, a new season begins, and the new Emporer declares a new List of Images and a new round of giant robot pilots writing poetry begins.

    Giant Robots

    What's all this about the giant robots, again? Simple: The Emperor is really a mad space god -- from space! -- who needs to generate tears to power his rocket engines, in order to return to the cosmic cycle to be reborn as a giant robot pilot. This will allow him, in turn, to expand the borders of the Empire's territory, die, and eventually be reborn as the child of one of the concubines in the Imperial Palace. Thus it is that Giant Robots are the connection between the Emperor's seasonal ascension, weeping, journey to the cosmic cycle, death, and reincarnation.

    Concubines

    All participants who are not currently playing the Emperor of the season or a declaiming giant robot pilot play the part of the Emperor's concubines. Thus, they are an audience -- probably hostile! -- for the pilot who is attempting to prove to his mistresses that he is the previous emperor though deadly giant robot combat. Of course, the players of the concubines also have control of the mistresses and illicit children of the pilots. Play of your current mistresses and bastards passes to the left when the season changes, except that the Emperor may claim control "out of season," but then control of the Emperor passes to the player who would have gained control of the mistresses and bastards.
