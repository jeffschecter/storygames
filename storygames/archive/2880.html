\n This weekend I will be assembling an ashcan release of my monster-within game, the Infected. It's a dirty-hippie style game about trying to just live your life when the whole world is going to hell. I mean, it's totally tough to reconcile your differences with your estranged sister when she sprouts batwings and just won't stop blathering on about the second coming, you know?

It's designed for single-session play, and generally plays out in three to four hours. There's no prep, no character sheets, and wonderfully little paperwork of any kind. You need a handful of tokens and six-sided dice (two different colors) for each player, along with a page or two of scrap notepaper for keeping track of the NPCs you're sacrificing for your own greedy ends.

For $10 you'll get a cleverly assembled 8.5x11 book that will yell "Office Depot!" at you. If you post up an AP report here, at the Forge, or even at RPG.net, and email me about it then you're in for a free copy of the finished product. If you like it so much that you toss up more than one AP report before the first finished printing, I'll even include a trio of custom dice that Mark and I had made just for this game.

If you're interested in being one of those dirty dozen, drop me an email at eric@ericsrpgs.com. Make sure to put "the Infected" or "Ashcan" in your subject line so that I don't mistake you for a spammer-type.

Oh yeah, I'm so excited about this that I'm willing to cover the shipping & handling to anywhere in the world.

-Eric

p.s. If you've played any iteration of the game before, and you wanna tell people what you think about it (even if you think it sucked) then this would be a totally awesome place for you to do so. Thanks.

    \n I've playtested the Infected and it's pretty cool. The core conceit is a "Shaun of the Dead" style dichotomy between a character's mundane relationships and the utter madness of the apocalypse you've collectively set in motion. I like the focused play, the way NPCs are handled (you create them and then basically burn them for mojo), and the way the game escalates mechanically through choices players make.

    That said, my last playtest was a little creaky and the game needs fresh eyes and some serious hammering. I think this is a good move, Eric.
