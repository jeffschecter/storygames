    \n I have a little Pool hack, which is basically like this:

    Play the game as usual. However, when you take a MoV, you can narrate in some complications for your character. For your game, those would be instances of gross incompetence. The GM then awards you 1 to 3 dice for the mishaps you narrate.

    You get:

    1 die - "Yep, that's a complication/case of incompetence, alright."
    2 dice - "Whoa! What a mess!"
    3 dice - You look around the table, and everyone's jaws are on the floor.

    Although I like the Polaris suggestion.

    Most importantly, if you've never read about incompetent superheroes in gaming (and particularly in GURPS), you must immediately go read this:

    Team Zed

    It's one of my favourite stories.

    \n Caveat - plugging my own game.

    If you want apparently incompetent characters get into lots of trouble screw-up, but eventually discover how to overcome their flaws well enough to take down the nemesis/nemeses (which to my mind mirrors the plot of Mystery Men), then Coming of Age works as is. At least as far as a one shot goes (or 3-5 session mini-campaign if needed). Another plus is that it doesn't have a GM role, so there's no risk of players feeling dependent on GM fiat for their ability to not suck.

    Regardless of the system, I suggest not to have any doubt about the final victory. It makes being painfully incapable interesting and fun, rather than simply annoying.

    - Mendel S.

    But part of the design goal (and this one was reached, I feel) was to make sure that your character sheet, when you looked at it, just made you feel really depressed about how pathetic your character was.
