    \n If you can get a hold of it Victory Games had a James Bond RPG - which has a seduction sequence rivalling its combat sequence.

    While I'm fond of both Spookshow (which is gritty except for the fact that you play both a spy and a ghost) and Conspiracy X (which implements influence in a very fun way), if I was to pick a quick game to run James Bond in I would choose Over the Edge, which is now available as as the open system WaRP, or FATE, especially Spirit of the Century.

    Alas James Bond style espionage doesn't fit so well into "story now" (or what have you), which is why I suspect it doesn't show up often in indie community games. Blowback (ala Burn Notice) and Spione (cold war spy movie) are excellent games but they hinge on a deep and emotional evolution of the main characters.

    - Mendel

    PS: I suppose I would be remiss if I didn't mention that one of the settings for Coming of Age is Scions of the Superspy, where you play the Superspy's children who have to team up to save the world after their deadbeat dad screwed up.

    \n Noah, "making up the spy adventure as you go along" is perfectly reasonable. But that that isn't the same thing as "Story Now" - which involved a thematic premise which is confronted and resolved during play. Basically James Bond as a character doesn't change significantly and there is little doubt that his methods will ultimately work, which essentially predetermines most moral or aesthetic statements you could make surrounding him. Where indie community games have focused on the espionage genre has been where things are less clear cut.

    - Mendel

    \n Nothing has topped Leverage for me!
    Love this game, and the show.

    Black Seven has some neat sneaking mechanics, but the rest of the game was...meh.

    Wilderness of Mirrors is a must play game, but it is really only good for a one shot.

    Blowback is great for the drama aspect of the game, but didn't really do it for on the action.

    Mistborn actually hit a lot of the "Spy" points for me. It was a surprising good game, awesome setting, and the crew/job mechanics are nice touch to tie it all together.

    Fate worked really well for a S.H.I.E.L.D campaign I ran. All the players were low powered undercover supers working for Nick Furry, and were trying to infiltrate various villainous organizations. We used the Fate Fractal to break down various parts of the mission (ie Disguise, Alias, Trust, Sneaking, Contacts, Too Deep, The Goal). That was great, but would probably hack some MHRPG + Leverage into that if I do another spy game.
