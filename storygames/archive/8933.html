    \n I think angels being terrifying is part of their inherent value.

    There's this phrase, "Put the fear of god into you", I'm unsure what the term is in English, but one of the proper verbs in regards to God in the OT is "Yir'oo", meaning that they shall fear him, but it is not quite that simple. It's sort of a worshipping fright, of knowing his measure and that it is grand.

    The only example I can think of in popular culture is from the LotR, specifically the movie, when Frodo offers Galadriel the ring, and she talks about a glorious and terrible queen... terrible in her glory.

    But yeah, that's something I don't know how to pass in a picture, having them look "normal" and yet have an air of terror.

    Edit: If anyone wants to read some of my crappy prose about the terror of angels, you can look here, direct link to 2 page pdf.

    Edit 2: I'm not sure I like the arachnid angel, but I like the other two. I also like the hues and tone of the last picture.

    \nFor this game, I definitely wanted them to be something unearthly and unsettling -- harbingers and servitors of a power that humans cannot comprehend. Some angels are described in the Bible thusly: four faces (cherubim, man, lion, eagle), four wings, cloven hooves of brass, and four hands. Strange! Cyclopean! Rugose! Okay, maybe that's going a little far. But that's the vibe I wanted to cultivate with this game. Besides -- if you're one of the 70,000 luckless bastards that's going to be killed with plague, just because King David took a census, then all you know is that some horrific entity has unleashed a gruesome and fatal epidemic, and oh hell you're dead. That was the initial hook behind the development of this game.
