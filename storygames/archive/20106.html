    \n[PDP hack] It Came From the Anti-Verse

    The Heroes:
    Fred L. as Dr. Spencer the Atomic Professor, irresponsible genius with a heart of nuclear fire
    D. Boctor as Astral the Psychic Daredevil, thrill-seeking superhero with powerful psionic abilities
    Michelle N. as Wisp the Ghost Warrior, spectre of justice, possesses the living to combat the undead
    Steve N. as The Shroud, Mystic Agent, sentient holy artifact cloaking a mummified host body

    The Threats:
    Dark Vortex (GM: DB), an unstable gateway leading to an anti-verse full of terrifying monsters
    Cryogenic Timebomb (GM: Steve), Dr. Spencer's latest project, a Dark Vortex stabilization device!
    Netherworldly Monstrosity (GM: Michelle), a horrific undead giant, hungry for all life on Earth
    Gigantic Parasite (GM: Fred), a huge swarm of semi-physical insects clinging to the Monstrosity
    Temporal Paradox (GM: DB), the Monstrosity's reality-warping effect, twisting timelines in its wake

    Complications: Public Relations Nightmare, Time Split, Lost Memories, Horrors of the Lost Worlds

    Manhattan 1965, the world watches in horror as news cameras broadcast live coverage of hideous creatures emerging from an experimental dimensional portal. Our heroes were able to close the Vortex in the nick of time using a Psi-Photonic Energizing Rocket while Dr. Spencer finished off the Monstrosity by throwing a Pocket Nuke into its roaring maw. Tragically, The Shroud was lost to the Paradox, sacrificing its own material to patch the fabric of space-time and repair causality itself.

    \n[Capes hack] The Technocrat's Hostile Takeover

    The Heroes:
    Michelle N. as Justru, Ex-Victim / Speedster, racing to save her sister from becoming a robo-minion
    Steve N. as The Shroud, Inhuman / Magician, finally free from last game's Paradox and back for more

    The Villains:
    D. Boctor as The Technocrat, Puppet Master / Robot, amoral efficiency expert with a computer brain
    Josh as The Faceless, Teleporter / Psychotic Loner, dimensional accident victim, blames The Shroud

    Primary Goals: Humans are Assimilated (defeated), Restore Technocrat's Humanity (achieved)
    Peripheral Conflicts: Destroy The Shroud (S/F), Free My Sister (J/T), Catch the Uncatchable Thief (F/J)

    Mind-control machines seize the floor of the New York Stock Exchange! Technocratized traders run riot in the streets and a high-speed brawl ensues as our heroes narrowly avoid assimilation themselves.

    The robo-minions moved with uncanny coordination but were no match for the dice-splitting power of Truth and Justice. In the end, the villains betrayed each other, old grudges were forgiven, and the homicidal brain implants were removed from (almost) all of the victims, including their own inventor.

    \n[PDP hack] The Fungi from Beyond Time

    The Heroes:
    Aaron as Rip Redrock the Mystic Detective, master of Savoir Faire and Je Ne Sais Quoi
    Josh as Kurzo the Alien Agent, space-gilled superhero with jetpack and raygun
    Josh (?) as D. Dodgers the Intrepid Warrior, star-fighter pilot with Truly Ridiculous Luck
    Tyler W. as The Steel Mind, a Psychic Warrior trapped in a deadly temporal fugue state

    The Threats:
    Fungoid Infection (GM: Tyler), an unprecedented epidemic on a quarantined moonbase
    Fungoid Enigma (GM: Aaron), the true nature of the illness and its origin from Beyond Time
    Lunar Cockroaches (GM: Dodgers), a horde of dog-sized infected mutant space beetles
    Temporal Ultimatum (GM: Kurzo), the bizarre effects of the fungus' trans-chronal nature
    Mutagenic Asteroid (GM: Aaron), an infected meteor on a collision course with Earth

    Complications: Free Roaming Contagion, Back to the Beginning

    The Danger Patrol battles a deadly moon-plague using laser guns, crystal balls and chicken soup, only to find themselves caught in a recursive time-loop that threatens to alter their backstories\u2026 forever!

    After a confusing journey through their own past and future, our heroes set their blasters to "Resolve Paradox," restored The Steel Mind's deleted marriage, and banished the fungus back in time to the beginning of the story, thereby solving the mystery of its origin, closing the loop and saving the world.

    \n[Capes hack] The Apotheosis of Ashuur Baan

    The Heroes:
    Kate as Verita the Puppet Master / Mind Controller, enlightened hero tormented by ethical issues
    Seth M. as Lightfoot the Spunky Kid / Speedster, accidentally absorbed Ashuur Baan's mystic lightning

    The Villains:
    John A. as Ashuur Baan the Inhuman / Magician, Commander of the Mysteries, Subjugator of Stygia
    Tyler W. as Grud Bonebreker the Simple Soul / Brick, Ashuur Baan's immortal barbarian bodyguard

    Primary Goals: Conquer the World (achieved), Grud is Free (defeated)
    Peripheral Conflicts: Convince the Other (AB/V), The Power is Stolen (L/AB), Catch Lightfoot (G/LB)

    The end of the world began with Grud's haunted mausoleum rising from the center of Times Square and unholy red lightning arcing up and down nearby skyscrapers. The good guys put up a valiant fight, both physically and metaphysically, but the villains proved too resourceful. At literally the last moment, Ashuur Baan used his "The Power is Stolen" Inspiration to lock Lightfoot into the Anti-Capacitor Chamber, allowing him to seize permanent control of the planet's ley lines and Conquer the World.

    In a chilling epilogue set 15 years later, the aging and defeated heroes try again to free Grud from his master's control, but their efforts are in vain. In the final panels, we see Ashuur Baan in orbit aboard his gigantic space-ziggurat, channeling ley line energy up into its engines and plotting a course for his next conquest. A minion reports, "FTL drive ready," and the Tyrant of the World(s?) orders, "Engage."

    \n[PDP hack] The Thing That Ate Our Universe

    The Heroes:
    Steve N. as Brace Wayland the Intrepid Commando, leader of the Screaming Eagle Squadron
    Tyler W. as Disembodied Al the Ghost Pilot, recently deceased Screaming Eagle janitor
    Michelle N. as Confundus the Psychic Detective, expert neuro-linguistic reprogrammer
    Josh as John Doe the Ghost Explorer, mysterious incorporeal amnesiac dimensional refugee
    Shuo M. as Dr. Holland Tomato the Psychic Explorer, cosmic anthropologist and ladies' man

    The Threats:
    Super-Dimensional Armada (GM: Shuo), a multiversal cabal secretly controlling the Danger Patrol
    Inexplicable Infiltrator (GM: Michelle), a star chamber spy sabotaging the heroes' investigations
    Nuclear Bandit (GM: Tyler), a petty thief blackmailing the galaxy with his nuke-fed monster
    Radioactive Creature (GM: Josh), the Bandit's monster, steadily growing to the size of a planet
    Transforming Devourer (GM: Michelle), its cosmos-consuming ultimate form from another reality
    Galactic Mystery (GM: Steve), the true identity of John Doe and his parallel counterparts

    Complications: Rogue Patrollers, Reality Backlash

    Banded together from various devoured universes, our heroes must find the Creature responsible and stop it before it feeds again. But the discovery of corruption within their own ranks leads them to uncover an inter-dimensional conspiracy that implicates every Danger Patrol in the multiverse!

    In the final scene, Dr. Tomato confronted the planet-sized Creature with its own loneliness, entreating it to end its rampage and seek out companionship instead. Convinced, the Creature withdrew through a Reality Backlash, where it met a very nice parallel version of itself and they orbited happily ever after.

    \n[PDP hack] Siege of the Chrono-Fortress

    The Heroes:
    Wes as Maxmillian Fortwright the Mystic Explorer, wealthy astral traveler in black suit and carnation
    John M. as The Whisper, Ghost Agent, ectoplasmic hero with the power to Pierce the Veil of Death
    Roger B. as Dr. Nick Forrest the Atomic Professor, inventor of the Omega Glasses and Atomic Watch
    Seth M. as Ace the Psychic Pilot, top-flight extra-dimensional navigator, judo expert and crack shot

    The Threats:
    Extra-Dimensional Fortress, a fractal dodecahedronic time-war platform emerging from astral space
    Radioactive Mystery, the unique energy signature and origin of the Extra-Dimensional Fortress
    Psionic Guardian, a featureless humanoid astral dust avatar controlled by the Fortress' commander
    Fungoid Mind Control, semi-physical astral dust infecting and hypnotizing the Guardian's victims
    Temporal Devourer, the Fortress' shadowy commander, obsessed with deleting the Danger Patrol

    Complications: Spreading Fungus, Manifesting Fortress, The Fortress is Learning, Devourer Revealed

    Earth's Future Utopia fades into improbability as every technological breakthrough made by the Danger Patrol is systematically erased from history by a mysterious force from the astral plane. Turning its own power against it, our heroes succeed in reversing time and restoring mankind's glorious destiny.

    The true identity of the Temporal Devourer finally became apparent near the end of the game when Maximillian sacrificed himself to take out the Fortress' Chrono-Cannons, resulting in his exile to the dawn of time. We all suddenly realized that it had been Maximillian all along, insane with immortality, forever seeking revenge against the friends who had failed to save him. In the end, the heroes deleted the Devourer and his darkest timeline from existence, but his impossible return is surely inevitable.
