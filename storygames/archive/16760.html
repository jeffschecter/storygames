\n Background: I loved reading Cthulhu Dark by Graham Walmsley, and I also loved Red Eye of Azathoth, an epic historical campaign. A couple weekends ago, I ran the first scenario (England, 887) with my new gaming group. It was awesome! Having played stuff like FATE and Fiasco, the minimalist system was right up their alley. (I added a Harm stat per Jason Morningstar's suggestion, so that they could fight zombies and the sorcerer who summoned them.)

Well, with one exception. The rules engineer of the group took issue with how counter-intuitive it is to roll Insanity & Harm under its current level. "Wouldn't traditional target numbers be more realistic?", he said.

Now, let me say that I find Cthulhu Dark to be very elegant. It has nearly everything I want, and adding Harm doesn't detract from it, imho. However, desiring to cater to the tastes of everyone at the table (and not wanting to open the monkey-barrels of BRP or Trail of Cthulhu), I was obliged to hack CD.

Cthulhu Grey is the fruit of my labors. This Saturday, we will tackle the second scenario (Feudal Japan, 1287), and see how it goes. Feedback is welcome. Graham is aware of its existence -- turns out that certain elements resemble an earlier draft of CD.
