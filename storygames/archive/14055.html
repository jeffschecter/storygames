    \n We found it easier when we played a slightly modified version in the pub last year. Instead of one person rolling the dice and making up their story, we rolled them into the center of the table and anyone could grab a dice and add an element to the story. This took the pressure off of any one person and made the game faster and funnier. We ended up telling a very long story made of lots of episodes. Random people stopped by and joined in.

    My son loves them to.

    \n I got them as part of a gift exchange over X-mas. Almost every day I roll them and make up a story. As to the modern story limitation, for the most part its not that big of a hassle since the images can be used to spark an idea and may not literally mean a modern object. For example, the light bulb often means that a character in the story got an idea rather than a glass object with a filament in it.
