    \n So we finally have a decent looking PDF put together with all the wonderful advise from the boards. http://www.mediafire.com/?1nxtwk5y0hm The file is about 4.5mb (PDF) right now.

    Specific things we'd like input on:

    * Natures: On p. 18 of the PDF, as well as all the creature Natures further on starting on p. 24. The rough rule is 3 descriptors per race and 4 per creature (including Orcs, etc.). Not concerned with gerunds, nouns... if you think something would work better, post here

    * Traits: Additions or removals?

    * Wises: Fleshing them out, though I've come up with a pretty decent list with ~90 wises

    * Environment Nature: Whether or not specific 'Places of Note' (Moria, Ettenmoors, Dead Marshes, Fangorn Forest, etc.) should have a Nature themselves

    * Tokens of Power: Does the concept make sense and is using a trait-like system the right way to go about it?

    * Anything else you spot or can think of!


    If you may, please use the Burning Wheel forums thread http://www.burningwheel.org/forum/showthread.php?t=7491 for peer review or playtest results. If that's not an option, I'll respond here as well. Thanks so much.

    \n Now that Saint has brought this cool hack back to the spotlight, I feel excited about sharing this.

    Browsing at the Hunt for Gollum webpage, I came upon this very appropriate production.

    The project (named Born of Hope) is an independent one hour film, made for fans by fans. As HfG, the idea is to make a non-profit project everyone will be free to watch, once its on-going production is over (they say it should be ready by fall).

    The original story is that of Arathorn, chieftain of the Dunedain and father to the future king. The movie will feature many original (and a few of the already existant) rangers, and it looks very promising (and a very interesting source of inspiration for those willing to play this hack).

    The webpage is: www.bornofhope.com
