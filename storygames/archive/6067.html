\n This is an idea that was originally meant for Van Dread. I decided to stop adding stuff on Van Dread(it was getting bogged down) and instead make Orphanage into a smaller game. What's the Orphanage you say? Well...

Orphanage is a game about kids in an orphanage, sometime around the 1950s. Life in an orphanage is pretty shitty on itself, what with the dead parents, bullies and limited food selection, but it's about to get a lot worse.
See every orphanage, as any self-respecting institution has it's own scary legends. Unlike most legends though, in Orphanage, the legends are all true and something prowls the orphanage at night, looking for victims...
So do you fight for your new home, your friends in the Orphanage and the other innocent kids? Or do you plan an escape and run away from this horrible place? Will the cute girl ever talk to you? And you still have to steal back your swiss army knife from the headmaster. '

I'm going hippie on this one, and using a mix of aspects(from Fate) and ''skills'' from Ditv, with no normal skills or attributes.
I hope to be done with it soon, although the monsters are giving me a bit of trouble. I will possibly using urban legend-like bogeymen.
I'm also concerned about the setting's date, since I know very little about the 50s and i should probably read up on that.

    \n Orphanages in the 40's and 50's are a tricky matter, since many of them were places of horror and suffering already. Although things like the Magdalane Laundries were somewhat earlier at their worst we know now that the rule in many of these places was physical and sexual abuse of children who had no one to defend them (certainly in britain and probably other countries too). Now of course you don't have to play like that, but if you set a horror game in an orphanage you have to aware of that background, which could have a big effect on the feel. (Look at the recent publicity of Haute de la Garrene in Jersey to see how many people see Orphanages and homes now).

    Now I don't know if it is intentional but I get a more light and humerous feel from your description above, more hi-jinks than beatings, more famous five than abusive Nuns. If so you might want to set it in a Boarding School than an Orphanage. Yes, it means the kids have parents, but the parents aren't there and in many cases want nothing at all to do with the kids (doubly so if you move it back another 10-15 years as well).

    Just a suggestion though

    \n A boarding school would probably work better, but I'm really into the whole Orphanage thing. I'm aiming for US orphanages in the 50s(they whole orphanage thing begun changing after that and it wouldn't really work for my game). I'll allow some artistic liberties to make orphanages not-so-abusive. I think USA orphanages during the 50s were a lot better than before.
    What I left out about the Orphanage is that it's very Orwellian in nature and does in fact involve strict headmasters and staff. The point is that when you're living in an orphanage, you still live your life. You're a kid, you care about stuff like your favorite knife and friendship.

    \nIt is indeed inspired by the Devil's Backbone and maybe a bit by Orfanateo to a lesser degree.
    What I meant by aspects and skills from DitV is a mix of the two, not both. As an example, a character gets 6 skills, which he gets during chargen, as per Fate(2 for each Phase, 3 phases in all).
    So from his childhood years, he gets:
    I can climb really well
    I can shoot things with a slingshot

    From the events of him becoming an orphan, he gets:
    I will always protect my little sister
    I'm not afraid of the dark anymore

    From his stay at the Orphanage, he gets:
    I have a secret hiding place
    I know where the headmaster hides his key

    So these are both skills and resources, which is a mix of DitV skills and Fate stunts and aspects.
