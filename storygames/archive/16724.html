    Bandwidth on my personal site
    \nCounting downloads: This implies you have a personal site. Do you have access to that personal site's web logs? If so, run something like Webalizer on them.

    Replacing files: A linux-ish way to do this is to save versioned files, but symlink to the most recent one, then only publish the URL to the simlink.

    \n The way I do it is by using WordPress. You can download it for free at wordpress.org or just create your own blog at wordpress.com. If you use the dot com, it come with all sorts of metrics you can measure such as hits, downloads, link backs, where your hits are coming from country-wise, search terms used to your find your site and more. I seldom give people direct links to download the files, instead sending them through the wordpress site so I can track stats, which as you know is huge for motivation. It's awesome to see that your games are getting downloaded by hundreds of people from all around the world. If you use the dot org custom site, you just have to download the free stats plugin for wordpress. Its all very easy to work with and makes releasing your games so much more exciting.

    As for quickly altering your files, I use FTP to send them up to real hosted web server. After you finish a new revision, just FTP the new file up with the same name, replacing the old file, and you're good to go.
