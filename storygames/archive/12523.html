\n I was at my local friendly gaming store, Drexoll games at Kitsilano, with my brother and girlfriend for boardgaming night. We were checking out the RPG section there, and a girl asked if we were going to start up a game (I thought boardgames, she meant RPGs, we sorted that out in 5 seconds). We talked a bit, and settled on Zorcerer of Zo, something I'd wanted to buy for ages but I've stopped buying anything I don't know I can get a game for.

I had never read it all the way through, but I did read the introduction, particularly all the info on types of fairy tales. I was also familiar with the PDQ system. Since it was literally a pick up game I streamlined the character generation even more, making it a combination of FATE and HeroQuest.

The 3 characters that got made up were a girl who got into Zo by pushing through a ravine from the real world, and found a princess killed in a landslide. She took on the princess' identity and was to go to magic school in the Jade City, but was a bit concerned that she might get found out and executed. Second character was a magical teddybear who dressed as a tiger because she wanted to be a tiger because they were brave and had stripes. The third character was a violander alchemist & engineer who wanted to make a contraption to fly beyond the stars.

I literally just made stuff up as I went along, drawing on tropes, and since it was a fractured fairy tale, I got to turn some of them on their heads. It was somewhat absurd, and I could have done some things better, but at the end everyone achieved their goals (3 hours later), generally with a little modification.

I'm now going to sit down and actually read through the whole book entirely, which I'm looking forward to even more after finding out how well it played.

    \n One of the best PDQ games, I think.

    Six Serpents is also very 'fire and forget' in its setup. PDQ lets you do the Risus/FATE trick of giving anything a stat rating and "Go!" with little to no need for massive game prep beyond a basic idea of where you want to go.

    I love that kind of "orienteering" kind of game. I like to read the map while I am walking the trail, so to speak.
