Guys,

I'm part of a group looking into organising a small con in Southampton, at some point early(ish) next year. Late enough to avoid clashing with Conception and early enough to avoid clashing with Gen Con. So probably April/May.

I want to gauge interest from people on this board. In short, would you be interested? Would you travel to Southampton for such an event? What would you like to see there? That kind of thing.

I'm not talking about a purely indie/hippy gaming event. I'm thinking of an event where there are lots of gamers of different types and one of those types is Story Gamers (if that makes sense?). But personally I'd like to see lots of people playing DitV, Shock and The Roach, as well as the inevitable D&D and M:tG tables.

Or is this just a fool's errand?

    Not a fools errand at all.

    Not wishing to pre-empt the Big reveal, but a lot of us small-press guys in the UK have been banding together in order to promote exactly this kind of stuff in the UK and Ireland. We'll be showing our hand a Dragonmeet in London on 2nd December, but we are in the process of setting up a web presence where people can talk about design and play, getting organised to appear at various cons and so on and so forth. At the moemnt the collective is made up of myself, Gregor Hutton, Iain McAllister, Joe Prince, Matt Machell, Joe Prince, Andrew Kenrick and Jonathon Ridd, so we're covering a pretty big chunk of the UK small press scene.

    So, yeah, this is exactly the kind of thing we'd like to encourage and support.

    As an aside, I was at Gaelcon in Dublin a couple of weeks ago where I ran games for their Indie Games Track, a series of one hour games (15 in all) devoted to showing the world the range of cool stuff that's available. Very similar to the event that we ran at Conpulsion in Edinburgh in March of this year. Conpulsion 2007 will see a bigger and better Indie Games Track: more games, longer games, better organisation and so forth.

    Fancy coming to Edinburgh in March? (bring hat and scarf)

    Cheers
    Malcolm
