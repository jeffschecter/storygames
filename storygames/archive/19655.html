\n I'm currently writing a super-hero RPG system which fits on a pocketmod.
Inspirations : Story Bones, Marvel Heroic, Supercrew and others.
I have to playtest it soon...

Everybody has 4 traits, an energy pool, optionally some weakness. Action : roll all traits as d6's, 6's are winners.
Traits are either :
- fixed : never lost (eg : skills, gifts...)
- stable : difficult to recover, rarely lost (eg : sidekick, mutant power...)
- unstable : easy to recover, but lost for a long time if you lose a combat (eg : weapon, magic spell...)
The player choses the type of each of their traits. Energy are kind of hit points. Unstable and stable traits are useful as a kind of ablative armor. All non-fixed traits (including energy and weakness) may be spent to re-roll dice.

Of course, I can't give lots of advice in a pocketmod, but it's a very classic RPG with a few narrativist elements (trait management).

I write in the French language, but I've just translated the core pocketmod into English :
- Pocketmod : https://drive.google.com/file/d/0B8B6JH_5YJHDdHdBS1Y2b290RDg/edit?usp=sharing
- Booklet : https://drive.google.com/file/d/0B8B6JH_5YJHDaWVjMXRNVTFWbzg/edit?usp=sharing\ufeff

There are 2 supplements so far in the French language : Golden Age Heroes (Daredevil, Black Terror, etc.) and Golden Age Villains (The Claw, Queen Camilla, etc.). Very soon, I'll publish some Golden Age Campaign.
Web page : http://philippe.tromeur.free.fr/super.htm

    I really like it, except the resolution confuses me. How many dice do you roll (4)? It says I need a 4 or a 6, how about a 5?
    You roll 4 dice generally, except if :
    - you've lost some traits
    - you have 5 traits or more (only for very powerful experienced characters)

    Rolling at least one 4, 5 or 6 is a success for ordinary, easy tasks.
    Rolling at least one 6 is required for heroic tasks.

    In combat, you need to roll 6, but if each opponent rolls the same number of 6's, you compare the 5's, and if each opponent rolls the same number of 5's, you compare the 4's, if it's still a tie, nothing happens.

    Not easy to explain in short sentences...\n
    For unstable traits, why don't you treat them as powers/abilities that require charging, concentration, or power source.
    I want to kep things very abstract, and use the same system for powers requiring charging/concentration (spells...) and powers requiring nothing but easily lost and easily recovered (weapons...).

    Here's the idea behind types of traits.

    Fixed traits :
    - advantage : can't be lost
    - drawback : can't be sacrificed

    Stable traits :
    - advantage : hard to lose
    - drawback : hard to recover

    Unstable traits :
    - advantage : easy to recover
    - drawback : easy to lose

    The ideas behind each type of traits is clear, but traslating those ideas into rules is less easy.

    Currently, I find some rules a bit awkward...
    Concerning stable traits :
    - Recovering stable traits requires rolling at least 2 success on recovery rolls (1 success is enough for unstable traits)
    - Deliberately disabling your foe's stable trait requires 3 success. (same for unstable traits, but you probably prefer to target stable traits)
    Are those rules clear enough ?

    The example that comes to mind is the Superman Movie where Clark Kent gave up his powers willingly.
    It's an example of a Hero willingly losing a stable power, maybe as an aftereffect of a Defeat ? (I don't remember the movie)

    Here is how we can rationalize the loss of traits as aftereffects of Defeat (or Escape) :
    - Affilation : going rogue, getting fired, losing means of communications, being wrongly accused of treason...
    - Object : stolen, broken, emptied...
    - Stable Power : deliberately dismissed (despair, atonement...), broken (powered armor...), vampirized *...
    - Unstable Power : dismissed by the gods, mana lost, shattered soul...
    - Sidekick : kidnapped, in a coma, dead...
    - etc.

    When the trait is sacrified to re-roll some dice :
    - Affilation : abusing one's authority...
    - Object or psionic power : supercharge...
    - Sidekick : self-sacrifice...
    - etc.

    * Traits like "power theft" (or "energy reflection") works like that :
    - if fighting a powered enemy before they lose a power, the die is rationalized as an attack to their power(s)
    - if fighting a powered enemy after they lose a power, the die is rationalized as some stolen power
    - if fighting an un-powered enemy, the die is rationalized as some power previously stolen
    But mechanically speaking, it's just a dice in the pool rolled in combat...

    (i) How does the Defend An Ally action work? Does it automatically negate all attacks directed at an ally or only ones where you get a higher margin than the attacker?

    Example: Hero 1 defends Hero 2 against attacks. Both Villain 1 and Villain 2 declare attacks against Hero 2.

    Hero 1 gets 2 successes
    Hero 2 gets no successes

    Villain 1 gets 1 success
    Villain 2 gets 3 successes

    Hero 1 has a winning margin of 1 vs Villain 1. Does this mean that Villain 1's attack against Hero 2 is blocked?

    Hero 1 has a lower score than Villain 2 (margin is -1). Does this mean that Villain 3's attack is resolved against Hero 2, scoring a 3 margin hit?
    (i) All attacks against the original target are negated, the defender takes all attacks originally aimed at himself and the original target.

    In your example : Hero 1 blocks all attacks, but takes 1 damage from Villain 2.
    Hero 2 is safe (and can recover or flee, if he has chosen one of those options)
    \n
    (ii) Do you only get to recover if you beat any/all attacks directed against you that round? If no attacks are directed against you do you compare your score against a result of 0 when determining the margin?
    (ii) Yes you recover if you beat all attacks against you. If no attacks are taken (for example if someone defends you), you roll against 0.


    After some recent playtests :

    In the version currently online, recovery is very easy (recovery = margin).
    I'll probably switch to something like "Only one trait is recovered: energy or unstable trait with a margin of 1; other traits with a margin of 2+." or something similar. Otherwise combat can last much too long...

    Other changes I'm considering :
    - When you're attacking, and several characters are attacking you, you can change the target of your attack anytime (= ganging up against a powerful target is never completely safe !).
    - Damage : 1st taken is chosen by the target, next ones are chosen by the attacker (In the version currently online, you take energy damage first ; but a margin of 3+ is rare, something like 3%)


    By the way, here are the probabilities, in the 1st round of a duel (4 dice against 4 dice) :
    Margin +4: 0,4 %
    Margin +3: 3,1 %
    Margin +2: 10,9 %
    Margin +1: 21,9 %
    Margin 0: 27,3%
    Margin -1: 21,9 %
    Margin -2: 10,9 %
    Margin -3: 3,1 %
    Margin -4: 0,4 %
