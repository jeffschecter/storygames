This leaves Covertly and With Violence untouched, aside from negotiation. Also, this "feels" right to me, but I don't know how well this will work in play.

My question is, what do you think? Any ideas?

    \n It seems to me that there are two approaches to deciding which forms should be untouchable in a IaWA hack.
    \n
    \n1) think of the story from beginning to end. How do the characters start...how do the characters end. When the final moment of the climax is reached...what is it that powers the hero through. For instance in Die Hard theres all kinds of Covertly and Directly with Violence and For Myself going on. But what finally wins the day is For Others With Love. Thus in a Die Hard Hack it would probably be For Others With Love that I'd privilege as being untouchable.
    \n
    \nWhat about for Batman? I won't claim to be a Batman fanatic myself, but in the stories that I am familiar with Batman seems to start out all Covert and Violent...dropping from the roof tops at night to lay the vigilante beat down on some thugs. But by the end of the story theres not so much sneaking and more desperation than violent beat downs. My first impulse would be to privilege Directly and For Others in a Batman tale.
    \n
    \n
    \n2) The "Dump Stat" approach. One of the great things about IaWA is that the whole feel is to be wicked: to be sneaky and cruel, or barbarous and cruel...to be savage...to be Wicked. "With Love" then becomes something of a dump stat...a "My guy would never act 'with love'" kind of thing. Which makes "With Love" being privileged in the damage rules a thing of beauty. What would a "dump stat" in Batman look like? I don't know how you envision the players interacting, but if its like a Batman story more than one of them are probably playing the over-the-top villains. Batman villains typically have sneaky master plans (so covertly isn't a dump stat), they're all about themselves and like to blow things up (so with violence and for myself aren't dump stats). But that again leaves Directly and For Others as good candidates. I'd say With Love would make a good candidate too, but that's shading more into Superman territory than Batman. Batman always gets the short end of the love stick so taking damage to With Love is totally appropriate in Gotham City.
    \n
    \n
    \nIn a Superman story... I'd privilege Directly and With Love because while Superman always STARTS doing things for others, in the end it always comes back to Lois...and I've never seen the Man of Steel not deal with badguys very directly.
    \n
    \nIn a Spiderman story... I'd privilege For Others With Love because ultimately for Spidey its all about With Great Power comes Great Responsibility and MJ.
    \n
    \nIn an X-Men story...I might privilege For Others if I wanted to emphasize how at the end the team always comes together...but I also might privilege For Myself, because the X-Men team always has those personality conflicts and the whole Mutants vs. Humans story lines highlights a more self centered take on heroism. I probably would privilege violence in an X-Men story...at least one modeling the movies, because the end of X-Men is always about the big budget fireworks display and lots of collateral damage.
    \n
    \nThe Hulk...With violence, With Love...that's probably self explanatory.
    \n
    \nI don't know. Those are my thoughts.

    Beaten: reduces Directly, With Violence.

    If you let "enraged" stand from chapter to chapter, instead or restoring those forms, you can call it "entrenchment" and think of Bruce Wayne's whole Batman deal, where his ability to connect with others and act with love STILL haven't recovered.

    \n Ok, I think I've worked out a good balance here. And yes, I'm using this thread partially as open design.

    BATMAN
    Can be Enraged, reducing For Others and With Love
    Can be Beat-up, reducing With Violence and Covertly
    Untouched is Directly and For Myself

    If Batman is not careful, he'll end up being a very solitary figure. Plus he eventually has to abandon the sneaking around and simply confront things head on.

    EVERYONE ELSE
    Can be Terrified, reducing For Others and Directly
    Can be Beat-up, reducing With Violence and Covertly
    Untouched is With Love and For Myself

    Citizens, Cops, Villains, and Thugs don't get mad as often as they get scared. Love is the one prime motivator in all of them, especially the villains like the Joker (who often claims to love Batman).

    Everyone after taking enough abuse just ends up looking out for, or protecting themselves... even Bats.

    NPCs:
    Beat-up reduces Action
    Terrified reduces Maneuvering
    Untouched is Self-Protection

    Batman is never an NPC.

    \nI think that's essential to effective oracle writing. Don't use the entries to just convey facts...use them to convey Gotham...the way Tim Burton conveyed his vision of Gotham in the opening scene of the first movie.
    \n
    \nSo your first oracle might be: "An office, well appointed, yet sterile. Within, the body of a woman, hair prematurely gray, face starkly white, eyes open as if in fear. The third dead psychiatrist this month."
    \n
    \nYour second might be: "The docks at night. A beam of light from the only working lamp slices through the murk. Two thugs are speaking with a delicate seeming woman. They are more afraid of her than she of them."
    \n
    \nIf each entry paints a scene that reads like how you'd describe a panel from the comic, I think you'll be happy with the result.
