    \n I had a system similar to this worked up for a western game I was doing until Dogs came out and made that moot.
    \n
    \nGun fights worked like this:
    \n
    \nOne of the categories was speed, another was accuracy, another was dodge, another was grit.
    \n
    \nRoll and assign dice: highest die in speed drew and shot first. If you shot and your accuracy was higher than your opponent's dodge than you hit and your damage was inflicted. If your damage was higher than their grit then they went down without shooting back. In a formal shoot out at high noon, no one got to dodge so it was just a high die to hit.

    \n You state an outcome that might happen, I state an outcome that might happen, and continue alternating until we have enough stakes for the conflict. Now we roll a number of dice equal to the number of stakes we determined might happen. 1-3 you get what you want for a given stake, 4-6 I get what I want in that category. Now we alternate assigning dice to the potential outcomes.

    \n What about a stat for each die slot that reflects you control of that kind of action? If your "self Safe control" is higher than my "opponent Safe control" stat, then you get to assign your safe die from your pool. Basically, it would add an initiative system to the die assignments.

    To get rid of the stat but still have this, simply start on the conflict starter's side and have a 1-to-1 die roll-off for the right to select that die (from the appropriate pool of rolled dice), then go to the other player's first die slot and do the same. It avoids a stat requirement and it makes for some very random but exciting outcomes, as tension rises.

    You could also opt to start with the single highest die roller to choose what slot goes first in the roll-off and proceed in some ordered fashion from there. That way, you better your options if you win the roll-off for the slot you most want control of.
