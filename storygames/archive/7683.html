    \n btw, when I was speaking of grids I was obviously thinking about the templates for online play (like you should have seen from Tony, Eero and other's thread). The standard method of "draw something where the dice fell" is too fun to eliminate. And it would work pretty much the same way, too.

    Better yet: the city structure does not change the grid, but merely dictates the movement and "road building" directions you can choose when following the various steps.

    Imagine a procedure like this for a (roughly) radial style of city:

    * Toss a die on the map, consult a table and mark that spot as a city centre of the kind dictated by the table (a square, a well, a main street, a fort, a tower, whatever).
    * On subsequent turns when you toss dice to create other city features and/or move and spawn citizens you'll create connector streets, bridges and stuff as rough 'radiuses' towards the centre. This way the city will take its shape naturally.
    * If an important new feature shifts the focus of the city, start to use THAT as the gravity center: the building of a Cathedral tends to change the geography of a city. Same thing for a merchant era with an important market square. This should ensure, after 2 or 3 turns, a pretty messy chaotic shape... exactly what most medieval cities look like
