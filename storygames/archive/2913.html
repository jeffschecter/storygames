\n On another site, I posted the following:

"What I would love to see [...] is a proper UK indiecon.

I enjoy a lot of these [storygames] games, but not every week you know? I like it as a change of pace from time to time, but it's not the main thing I'm in the hobby for. I'd love to be able to go to a proper indie con, play some Mortal Coil and PTA with people there for that, then on a Monday night kick some D&D ass or stop some cultists in CoC. I suspect I'm not alone in that."

Leaving that quote aside, is there anything like this? A UK indiecon focussed on storygames but accessible to those who don't play them all the time? I'm not aware of anything like that, but it seems to me a real gap. I suspect a lot of people would love to give stuff a go, maybe not then convert to primarily playing storygames but still welcome the opportunity to check them out and have some fun.

I mean, there's lots of storygaming stuff happens at Dragonmeet, but can be hard to get into if you're not already in that scene. Something a bit more open and yet more focussed would be cool.

    \n I looked into organising something along these lines for some time this year.

    What it boiled down to was this:\n
      \n
    • I live in Southampton. That's right on the south coast and pretty out of the way/a long way for most people. Realistically, I can't organise something that's not pretty local to me.
    • There's no local scene except for D&D and Vampire larp.
    • So I was left with the reality of organising am event revolving around a Vampire Larp, Living Whatever and a M:tG tournament with an indie games track. The other stuff (Larp, Living and M:tG) would generate enough attendance to pay for the venue and I could hope to grow the indie contingent over time.
      • \n
    \nIn the end I gave up for a number of reasons:\n
      \n
    • It seemed like a lot of work to organise a bunch of people getting together to play shit games*, plus a few to play game I care about.
    • There would be considerable financial risk without a free or very cheap venue.
    • The local games shop died an unseemly death, removing said cheap venue and not exactly filling me with confidence when it comes to the fiscal safety of a local RPG based venture.
    • I bought a house and found that DIY and painting took away a lot of my free time and money.
    • I started earnestly trying to write and publish my own game. Lost more free time and money.
    • I bought a Wii. Which takes up any remaining free time. (hey, at least I'm honest about it)
      • \n
    \nMost importantly though:\n
      \n
    • I found that it was actually very possible to get as much indie game goodness as I wanted at existing cons.
    • I organised Spodley Grange, a get-together for playtesting in Northants in mid-May. (there's a space available if you're interested actually)
      • \n
    \nI spent most of Conception playing DitV, PtA, Mortal Coil, Contenders and a couple of other things. In fact, the only time I didn't play indie was when I decided to run Savage Worlds or pay Star Wars d6 (which I have a serious weakness for). I spent ALL of the last ConCrete Cow playing indie (PtA and Covenant).

    Don't get me wrong, if someone organises an "indiecon" somewhere I can get to on a day I'm not working, I'll be there for sure. But the point is that the UK con scene is pretty crowded right now and there's a lot to be said for running a "con within the con" which is much, much easier to organise.

    Dragonmeet's not really the best one for playing, it's very trader-centric. Great place to go and meet people, but not somewhere I think of when it comes to actual play. The other cons I've been to (Conception and Concrete Cow for example) are more play oriented.

    * defined as "ones I have bugger all interest in playing".

    \n Thanks for all the kind comments about Concrete Cow. Yes, there are quite a few indie games on offer at Concrete Cow, reflecting the interest of many of the MK RPG club members. We've had Cold City, PTA, Covenant, Dogs in the Vineyard, Mythic Russia, and many others. If anyone wants to come along to sunny Milton Keynes on 29 September to try a few out, you're more than welcome. If there's something you specifically want to play, ask on the mailing list and see if anyone else is interested.

    Balbinus, if you're interested in doing a small gaming get-together, just go for it. It doesn't take much to find a room (though I'd strongly advise finding one where you've got exclusive use: mixing gamers with mundanes is not popular). The hard part is finding a date that's not too close to other cons, and getting the word out to gamers. Forums like here, RPG.net, UKRoleplayers, Collective Endeavour, etc. are your friends. Drop emails to Darran Sims and Phil Masters, to put it on their lists of upcoming cons.

    Neil.
