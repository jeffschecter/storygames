\nRecess FAQ:\n
\n
What is Recess? \u2013 Recess is a quarterly gameday hosted by NerdNYC. Recess exists to provide opportunities for our attendees to socialize and play. None of the games require any previous experience and rules will be taught as needed. We want every person attending Recess to have good time. Anyone who cannot play nicely with others will be asked to leave (without refund). This has never happened, please help us keep it that way!
\n
\nHow do I sign up to play games? \u2013 Registration for games is done on site. We put up the sign up sheets a half hour before the two blocks of games begin. Make sure you show up on time to sign up! To be fair to all, you are only allowed to sign-up for yourself. Since there are limits to how many people can play any given game, It is entirely possible that some games get filled up early. Don't worry if you didn't get into a game that appealed to you \u2013 that's just an opportunity to play something else that you may not have otherwise considered!
\n
\nWhat will the food situation be like at Recess? \u2013 The area around the 440 Studios is chock full of places for food and beverages. There is a two hour break between sessions to allow people to get food and come back without missing any scheduled events.
\n
\nIs there any other way I can help out besides running a game? \u2013 Occasionally we need additional help. In those instances we'll put a call out for volunteers well in advance of the Recess date. So keep an eye out!
\n
\nI've got cool nerd-stuff I'd like to share with the world, what do i do? \u2013 A Recess tradition is the "Swap Table". This is a place for attendees to leave any nerdy gear (books, games, art, toys, videos, etc.) that they no longer want or have space for. Anything placed on the Swap Table is considered to be free for anyone to take. If you bring items for the Swap Table, please check at the end of the day and take any unclaimed items home with you!
\n
\nGot a question or concerns? \u2013 The Recess Staff and NerdNYC officers will be available throughout the day. Or you can email us at recess@nerdnyc.com

    The most popular iPhone game of all time is now coming to a tabletop near you! Only thing is, *this* time the story will be told from the point of view of those poor, oppressed piggies! Players will take on the role of the Knights Architectural as they strive to keep those houses of wood, glass, and stone bird-free.

    Note: this game is a setting-hack of Ben Lehman's award-winning tragic fairy-tale RPG Polaris. It is a GM-less game and as such players will rotate between protagonist pig roles, antagonist bird roles, and support roles.

    Yes, there will be "minis".

    \n Usually I run Dogs and Mouse Guard but I'm going unconventional to practice for the upcoming Gen Con Iron GM tournament!

    I'm going to run:

    - A hack of D&D 3E that will use custom feats to introduce stunt, keys, and momentum mechanics.

    - Play a family of grief stricken, rage-full dwarves on a quest to take back their home.

    - Players come up with 3 elements that I need to incorporate into the game. An OGL monster, a location, and an object.

    - Players make characters by answering questions via a Choose Your Own Adventure style handout customized for each character class that will tie them directly into the game's scenario and make it personal!
