\n Hi everyone,

The recent thread on one off games (with some great suggestions!) made me wonder if folks had similar suggestions for easy-to-learn games that can be run in an hour. I work on a creative team for my company and for fun over lunch one day we played a super short Dungeon World session- none of my colleagues had ever played a tabletop rpg before, but they got into it fast. They definitely would like to play more games but it's very hard to get more than one hour. These are people who are very into stories and have strong senses of humor, if that helps.

Any suggestions for non-experienced players in an hour or less?

    \n Short and easy-to-learn is really my thing, these days, as I'm demoing RPGs a lot to "nonplayers", in addition to traveling gaming conventions as I've always done.

    First, I offer my own design for your consideration (Enter the Avenger, link to full text on a webpage). The upcoming Little Candy House will be a very different take on a short time-frame and easy-to-learn game, as well.

    I've had an excellent time with Ben Lehman's SKEW - a milestone in ease of learning, and it scales to virtually any number of players (well, no less than three, actually). I'm playing this a lot.

    Fables of Camelot, by Sami Koponen and Eero Tuovinen, can easily be run within 1-hour (it's actually a stated design goal to have 30' sessions), though I'm not sure how well you can learn how to run it from just these notes (I was lucky enough to play it with Eero in person).

    Off the top of my head, Clover by Ben Lehman, Wilding Tales by Joli St. Patrick, Murderous Ghosts by Vincent Baker, GxB and its variants, and (selecting a simple letter) D\u014d: Pilgrims of the Flying Temple by Daniel Solis all fit the bill.

    I also want to try Hot Guys Making Out (again by Ben Lehman), Cartooner (by Giovanni Micolucci), and I'm sorry for how many more I'm sure I'm forgetting.

    \n Vincent Baker's Murderous Ghosts - a two-player game. You're the adventurer. I'm the murderous ghosts. There's some blackjack-inspired resolution. I sincerely try to terrify you. Ready? Go.

    Ben Lehman's Clover. Clover is a five year-old girl. She lives with her dad and has some friendly neighbours. She goes on light-hearted adventures. It's cute and fun.

    My Keep It Sunny (scroll down for it) - a game inspired by Its Always Sunny in Philadelphia. Takes 30-45 minutes. Reliably produces stories that mimic the show. Is fun. Requires only the game cards and a handful of pennies.

    Jackson Tegu's Superhero. You collectively play Superhero. He has a burned-out car and a high-powered rifle. Everything is over-the-top. Reliably leaves everyone sore from too much laughter. Takes 15-45 minutes to play.

    Emily Care Boss' Breaking The Ice might fit into one hour. Three dates. One big setup twist. Play to find out whether a romance blossoms.

    \n Witness the Murder of Your Father and Be Ashamed, Young Prince ndpdesign.com/

    Plays in exactly an hour, and doesn't need a central GM (authority is spread neatly around.) It completes itself in 60 minutes, but has replayability.

    I very sincerely second Puppetland. It is demanding sometimes, but that's why it's timed. At the 60 minute mark you stop (and debrief if you have the time.)

    I'll say this, both of these games are dice-less, so the strategy is in the moves you use, rather than in calculated risk.
