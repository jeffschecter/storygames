\n The more I read about it, the more I want to play a wizard. There are still a lot of rules loophooles even in PF, though a lot of dangerous stuff got corrected from 3.5. Some of those corrections rendered some spells almost useless or pretty circumstancial. So I was wondering, to all of you who have played a wizard, which spells do you think have the most potential for creative applications? Which of those you've used and to which degree of success?

For example, I'm reading here the Teleport object spell description:\n
School conjuration (teleportation); Level sorcerer/wizard 7

Range touch

Target one touched object of up to 50 lbs./level and 3 cu. ft./level

Saving Throw Will negates (object); Spell Resistance yes (object)

This spell functions like teleport, except that it teleports an object, not you. Creatures and magical forces cannot be teleported.

If desired, the target object can be sent to a distant location on the Ethereal Plane. In this case, the point from which the object was teleported remains faintly magical until the item is retrieved. A successful targeted dispel magic spell cast on that point brings the vanished item back from the Ethereal Plane.
\nAnd though there are rules, rulings and recommendations that now refrain GMs and players to use this to teleport, say, swords into people's heads or boulders over them, what prevents the caster from readying the spell against an attack and teleport away his enemy spear as soon as it touches him? Or his armor, equipment, etc?

    \n One of my favorite loopholes was something that happened when Pathfinder nerfed (appropriately!) Alter Self, which was Hellabusable under 3.5 (I regularly had the highest AC in the party--judicious use of Mage Armor, Shield, and Alter Self into trogdolyte form for the +6 Natural AC.)

    So now, Alter Self can only transform you into a humanoid, rather than 3.5's "creature of the same type."

    However, you can still cast spells with target of Self on your familiar.

    So yeah, a second level spell can change your cat familiar into a gnome. :-)

    \n My favorite is Summon Instrument, just because it is a cantrip, which in Pathfinder means unlimited uses per day. Examples here.

    Silent Image is still, to me, the quintessential spell for flexibility based upon Player creativity.

    These days Paragon Surge is the most flouted broken spell. Use it to get Improved Eldrich Heritage (Arcana Bloodline) as a spontaneous caster, or Preferred Spell as a prepared caster. Ta da! Your PC can now spontaneously cast nearly any spell.

    As for your Teleport Object example, items that are in use always get saving throws.
