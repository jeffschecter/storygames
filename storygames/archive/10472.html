\nIntro
\n
\n This booklet is a complete Story Game freely published by Odds-Are, and sponsored by fans and companies of the Story Game movement. We hope that by putting these booklets out there we can bring the enjoyment of our hobby to you!
\n We ask that if upon you and a few friends or family members playing this game you do not enjoy it, you please kindly place it back in a public place for some one else to find. Weather you do or do not like this game we also suggest that you visit Odds-Are.com/storywhat to see if one of our many other free games might appeal to you!
\n
\nStory What?
\n
\n A Story Game (some times called a role playing game) is very simply any game in which narrative control of the game is used as a key element of play. Quite simply it is a bunch of people kicking back and in a very casual manner developing stories based around some core rules to guide the direction of play. The best part about Story Games though is that they are truly equalizers; you don\u2019t need to be funny, or strong, or smart to play a Story Game just willing to have some creative fun!
\n Story Games also have the added benefit of nigh infinite replay ability unlike video games whose repetitive plots become tiresome and give far less bang for your buck. With a Story Game you and your friends can sit down brining new energy to a game time and time again and the system will not fail to funnel your creativity into something awesome.
\n Aside from a great gaming experience the various communities of Story Gamers both on and offline is a phenomenal group of people. For more information on where Story Gamers are check out Odds-Are.com/storywhere to get linked up to some incredibly active groups.
\n
\nTools of the trade
\n
\n One of the greatest aspects of Story Games is no matter what game system you play the controllers are all pretty much the same. Pen and paper, as well as dice are usually enough to get you by. Some games use a pack of cards instead of dice for this game you can use either a pack of cards or a hand full of any type of dice.

    \nI wonder though if such a deeply geek culture embedded game is the best one to pair with a booklet targeted at introducing non gamers to story games. Or lone crime fighting vigilante superhero ala Spiderman, Batman, or the Spirit.
    \n
    \nSomething that there's already a wealth of movie imagery and character archetypes to draw from that would be familiar to people who...well...aren't us...
