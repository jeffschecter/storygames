    \n 3.5" x 5", about 3/16" thick. So, uh, really tiny. If you can get the game with the bag and peripherals stuff as well, do, as the book seems a tad fragile and it keeps the cards and everything together.
    If you like tiny books containing excellent games about map-drawing and communities on the brink of falling apart, yes. I don't really know what games you like though so I can't really answer that.

    \n The book is this small, produced by this manufacturer: scoutbooks.
    I think the cards are quite beautiful, produced by a professional offset card printer.

    Some people really love it. Some people play it often and excitedly share their maps afterwards.
    Other people hate it, or play it and don't really understand what it does that's special.
    The game asks you to adhere to a weird social contract, and some people don't like that.
    It has produced some truly beautiful play experiences.

    I wrote it so everything I say is probably biased and untrustworthy.

    \n Physically, the game components are all well-made and attractive. You will like looking at them and handling them. Yep!

    I found the game itself to be similar to Microscope, at least superficially: you've got structured turns that churn out the story of a place. Honestly, I had fun with it but it didn't blow me away. That could've just been my session, I dunno, but it left me wanting a little more. It's very rules-light, very inspiring, not a lot of mechanics but heavy on the social contract. Does that sound like your thing?
