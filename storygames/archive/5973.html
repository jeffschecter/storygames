    \n Hm, as a cover, I'm not too keen on the art. It doesn't tell enough about the setting. My first thought was: horror? It looked like ghouls and a teenage werewolf. I much prefer the 1st edition cover, which shouts "space opera". I'd recommend keeping the one above for an inside splash page.

    \n May I be a dissenting voice here?

    William, to be honest, I don't know what the game's about. It looks like a fantasy game, from the new cover, but the back cover mentions planets.

    The back cover text isn't doing you any favours, really. The first thing I read is that it's a complete game: fair enough, but it's not setting me on fire with enthusiasm. The second is online support: but I want to know what's in the book I'm about to buy, not what's available elsewhere.

    Then, in the last bit, I start to get a clue of what the game is: interstellar organisations and planets. But what's it really about? Is is gritty sci-fi or Traveller or horror or what? Why should I buy this game? I want the back cover text to make me excited about it.

    On the front cover: I'm not a fan of the new art, I'm afraid. I thought the original cover was much more interesting. That made me stop and look at it. This one just looks like generic fantasy art, to me: whatever is unique about your game, this art doesn't show me it.

    \n If you believe Ryan Dancey, it's the players, rather than the GM, that choose which games get played. And the conventional wisdom on designing covers to appeal to players is to show characters that can inspire a "I want to play that dude" reaction. First edition Unknown Armies has a gorgeous painting of a dead guy on a kitchen table. The cover appeals to prospective GMs, not prospective players. No one looks at it and says "I want to play that dude." First edition Over the Edge has a high concept illustration of two well-dressed characters walking unawares toward a broken pavement drop-off into oblivion. No one looks at it and says "I want to play that dude." Covers like the current cover of Primetime Adventures inspire a "many GMs" group, but you're aiming at a traditional group. So, although I love the art style of your latest iteration, the problem is that there's no figure that can provoke the "I want to play that dude" reaction. The human figure is too clearly in distress. My recommendation is to rework the dynamics of the figures. The Talislanta 4 cover has the right figure dynamics. But do it in full color.

    Short answer: The human needs to be edging out a win in the conflict.

    Paul

    \n Nice idea, but I think you need something on the front cover to say "sci-fi". Personally, I'd be making the lizard's weapon a techy light-sabrey thing.

    You're using a lot of space saying "HinterWelt Enterprises". Even White Wolf and Wizards of the Coast (whose brand will help sell the game) don't use up that much space. Personally, I'd replace it with a tagline on the front cover to explain the game ("An Otherworldly Game" or "Adventures with Other Races and Worlds" or preferably something less lame than those two) and centre "Nebuleon" on the spine. There's so much HinterWelt Enterprises that it threatens to overwhelm the name of the game.

    \n The cover has evolved in quite a piece of awesomeness.

    The one critique that I'd still give, though, is that it doesn't explain anywhere on the cover what the game is about. I know the name of the game, that it's the second edition, that it's complete and has online support, and that there's a detailed setting... but I still don't know what it's about. Is it an SFRPG? If so, is it hard SF? Space opera? Planetary romance? Is there some big hook, like a galactic war? ("The war begins...")

    I'd use space for that info rather than what you have now. Online support, complete game in one book, and lots of setting info is kind of common these days. If you want to sell me, tell what I'll be doing when I play the game, and why it's cooler than any other SFRPG.

    Just a thought.
