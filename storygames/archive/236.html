    Ack. Courier is a non-proportional font, meaning that each character takes up the same horizontal space as any other. An "i" will be as wide as an "m" (the "i" will have more white space around it). Proportional fonts squish (okay, kern) the characters together appropriately.

    If you're really sold on a typewriter looking face, use Officina Serif or maybe ITC American Typewriter. Like Adam says, Courier's almost universally a bad idea for any length of text.

    There are plenty of "grunge" fonts you can find that might work well for titling/display. These are the distressed-edged faces you've probably seen in lots of stuff from 90s games and other non-game designs. I just did a google search, and found a font called called Guilty. That seems pretty appropriate! But, I've never used that font, so I don't know whether it works well or not. This would only be used in titling or diplay headlines and the like.

    http://www.webdesigntimes.com/article/25-free-grunge-fonts
    (Scroll down to the last one)

    Also, a stencil font (like "Stencil"!) might be a nice fit for a display typeface as well. Kind of has a sprayed-on prison or military feel somehow.

    You can also probably make good use out of plain sans serif fonts like Helvetica and Futura. It's prison! I think of plain, maybe even condensed, block lettering. Either face, especially Helvetica mixed with some Helvetica Condensed faces, could work like a charm. But, be careful not to overdo it. Too much main copy text in Helvetica makes me cry.

    We move all manuscripts in non-proportional fonts, too, although we almost never print in them. The reason why is simple: it's easier to gauge the length of a segment with a standard width, rather than trying to figure out how long this font made the segment and translating that into how long the other font will make it in the finished product.

    Also, if the page is going to be scanned and subjected to text-recognition, non-proportional will increase fidelity of the scan (which may be why your courts ask for it).
