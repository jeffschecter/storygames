\n THIS THREAD IS EXCLUSIVELY FOR: Rob Bohl, Brian Peters, Kingston Cassidy, Joel Shempert, Joe McDonald.

So, we've got our road trip in mind: We're a punk-rawk burlesque troupe on tour. We're encountering a world where sell outs and hardcores share a lifestyle, and that's weird.

Now, we hit play on the second song of our Poverty! Bigotry! Rebellion playlist and get to making characters. Rob Bohl hands us all copies of the lyrics so we can read along if need be.

You will create your character while this song is playing. We pause the tape again after the song ends, while we finish creating our characters. Then, we discuss our characters.

The rule is: At least one Trait or Future is inspired by the song.

Create for your character three Traits: words or short phrases that summarize parts of your character's personality or experience. Each of us might have one Trait about our job in the performance troupe.

Create for your character two Futures: Futures are goals or objectives that your character has. They see this road trip as a possible venue for addressing those goals or objectives.

One catch in Long Road Nowhere is this: You aren't allowed to talk about the future, in game. The only exception is what's listed in your Futures. So if you want to talk about finding a nice hotel to sleep in when we reach our destination, or finding a nice man and settling down, you need to write that into a Future.

Alright. Hit play. Write a character. Then share.
