    \n I don't think that'll throw advancement out of whack. In my experience people get one experience from Hx every, like, three or four sessions.

    With one PC, they are likely to get more than the average experience per session simply because no one else but they are the focus.

    Have you thought about the player controlling two characters? It's by the book, and it's probably how I'd do it if I ran it 1-on-1. In any case, I think you'll be fine.

    \n I played a few sessions one-on-one and it was great! Just use the guidelines in the advanced fuckery section for having Hx with NPCs (and the associated help/interfere rules). You have to make your triangles a little more oblique than normal, considering that there are two NPCs vying for the one PC's resources, but that's OK. Just ask lots of provocative questions as normal and re-incorporate like crazy.

    Its tiring. Slow down a piece, cause once the snowball starts, the tendency is to frame scenes hard to get at the drama you both want. This is good, but it can be great just languidly enjoying colour scenes and building up your rather intimate two person vision of your apocalypse world.

    If your player plays with two PCs, put them in situations where their wants are at odds. Make the ugly choice between the two characters, make the hard bargain sacrificing one character's ambition for the other.

    Have fun!
