\n Hey all,

Long time lurker...think I may have posted once, maybe(?). This is a bit long, and I apologize, so if you just want to skip to the good stuff, it's this: relationship map + GM-less play.

First, a smidge of background: my friend and I (who used to play D&D, GURPS, and...uh, Battletech back in the 80's & 90's with a larger group), got together a few months ago and started playing and playtesting all the cool new games that we'd be oblivious to for the decade or so that we weren't into RP. The Mythic GM emulator was a big catalyst to this, and has been a faithful companion as we took FATE, Wushu, Savage Worlds, Reign, and others out for a spin, occasionally with other players dropping in for cameos. We quickly discovered that we're both more into story than mechanics, but that cool mechanics still give us a bit of a thrill. Also, with the exception of Reign, the role of GM as been largely superfluous.

Anyway, we've been taking the approach of "two spies/normal guys/space marines are plunged into such-and-such situation, and need to figure out the mystery/stop the international conspiracy/reach the goal," plot-oriented stuff, and it's been a lot of fun. But last time we got together, we started tossing around the idea of creating an ensemble of characters, and mining the inevitable conflicts between them for some character-oriented goodness. The idea is that we frame a scene we both find interesting, pick the characters we each want to play from the pool, and play it out. We did one short proof-of-concept using Wushu, and it was pretty cool.

But now we're considering other systems that may work better for this, and so I come to you.

Because of the character issue focus, we're looking to use PTA next time, but all the countless "[PTA] I don't quite get it" threads makes me a little gun shy, especially since we'd be hacking it on our very first use of it.

So...I guess what I'm getting at is this: I know this general idea will work, but I was wondering if anyone had any suggestions on a system that might really shine in this instance.
