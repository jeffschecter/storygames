Boredom, free time and a dearth of games lead to a creative, fun and enthusiastic pet project. This is truly awesome, and I wanted to give it its own thread outside of the July Stuff thread.

This gets a big fat Laser Princess Thumbs Up from me. In fact, as I find some time I'll be doing it myself.

I just wanted to open this thread up to be a rolling, gigantic fucking mess of a thread. Most of the "Story Games" that we talk about here are seeped in this "first you create situation, then you create characters" or at the very least "you create characters in an environment where your group is together, hashing out ideas". Damn straight.

However, for the purposes of this thread, I'd love to see people just throw in characters for games they own (better yet: Try also focusing on games you own but haven't played yet, just so you can crack the cover again, see what makes it tick, etc). One to a post, two to a post, or a link to a post on your blog each time you update it with a new character.

For the purpose of this thread, pretend that for games where group character generation is a must (shock:), or situation comes before character generation (Legends of Alyria), pretend that you already hashed out the details with your group and this is your contribution. If you wish, you can post your imaginary group's contributions in a sentence or two and how your character emerged from that.

Any system is kosher, from Hippie Filth Madness to Dungeons and Dragons/GURPS (for the latter, though, we'll probably be more impressed with the stats plus cool character concept attached, more than just "Awesome Combat Loadout").

And if you see a character that looks cool for one of those "group chargen" games, feel free if you want to post your character as a companion to an earlier one (Example: Person A posts a sentence about her imaginary "shock:" concept, and the character that fits in that concept. Later, Person B comes along, thinks that Person A's concept rocks, so she also creates a character that fits into Person A's campaign concept, as if they were players at the same game).

EDIT: And so that this doesn't turn into a big "My Awesome Game is Awesome" cheesy promo thread, I'm gonna ask for a common rule of "If you create a character for a game that you have designed or are designing, that's fine, but at least do one for a game that you DIDN'T design first."

-Andy

    WITH GREAT POWER

    The Setup: The Great Horror has burst from the Earth in Lima, Peru, spitting seeds of chaos into the atmosphere. Those few hit with the seeds develop creepy superpowers. Imagine Clive Barker meets Superfriends. Lots of guys use their powers for personal gain. A very few use their powers For Evil (which is also a quick way to get the seeds to react, granting more power). Another few band together to make sure the evil/personal gain folks stay in line.

    Hero: Penny Jackson - AKA - Kabbaksuul
    Concept: Penny was a schoolteacher in the inner city of Chicago. Infected on her way home one day, she gained a new name and powers as KABBAKSUUL. She now fights evil in and around the city, and still tutors schoolkids in area schools in her free-time.
    The Struggle: Serving the People vs Rejection by the People.

    Serving the People Aspect: School Teacher
    Risk: Ejection from PTA. Losing job as tutor. Losing standing in community.
    Serving the People Aspect: Lashawn Price (elementary schoolkid)
    Risk: Kidnapped by enemies. Mistrust. Breaking appointments.
    Rejection by the People Aspect: KABBAKSUUL (identity and powers)
    Risk: Powers become unstable. A deeper personality emerges and takes over.
    Rejection by the People Aspect: Lucius Jackson (uncle and preacher)
    Risk: Uncle discovers her identity. Lucius preaches intolerance in his ministry.

    \nLegends of Alyria

    Setup: Two months ago the Ark opened up, and its inhabitants spilled into the wastes, forming outpost villages and entering/joining with other local villages. Recently, there's been a lot of martial conflict with the Citadel's house troops over issues like resources and the like. The Arm of Seven, a team of fierce and loyal soldiers of the Ark, have been activated and sent into the villages to protect their interests. Unfortunately, the masters back at the Ark did not forsee that key members of the Arm of Seven would develop identity problems when mingling with non-Arks that would make them question themselves. Identity and personality struggles take place while the real menace grows.

    Note: This is built on the scale:
    Unicorn
    Blessed
    Ark
    Misbegotten
    Dragon
    Devil's Hour

    Arm Corporal Three
    A loyal Ark soldier who will readily put down any threat as commanded, but even so is having doubts about the Ark way (lack of identity, etc)

    Virtue: Ark

    Attributes
    Determination: Ark
    Insight:Misbegotten
    Force: Ark

    Traits:
    Chivalrous - Unicorn
    Steel Resolve - Blessed
    Loyal to Ark - Misbegotten

    I/C: Arm Corporal Three begins with one point of Inspiration, one point of Corruption
