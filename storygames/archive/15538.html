      \n OK, now that I have more time to respond, let me do a more full and informative post.

      1) The Sitch: Indie Hurricane is a small press expo that happens within Gamestorm. This consists of a dedicated gaming space (three tables in a room, plus three tables in the lobby outside the room), and a table in the dealer hall where anyone who asks me can put their self-published works up for sale, at no charge, and keep 100% of their sales. There's also, y'know, a whole convention going on. Boardgames, other RPGs, dealers, panels, a game library for open play, and a swag room where scheduled GMs can trade in their hours logged for donated prizes--a bunch of weird hand-me-downs with occasional gems.

      2) The Logistics: So, there's a scheduling system, which is LIVE right now. Register games, sort 'em to the Indie Hurricane track, and I'll approve and schedule 'em. You can request times; last year I arranged all the games in set blocks with meal breaks, a la Go Play NW, and it worked out well, I think. Last year we also designated a lobby table as an Open Gaming HQ, with a whiteboard on an easel, and allowed folks to post games looking for players and play at any open table. That worked out pretty well too; in fact it seemd like it was easier for folks to find games through the board than to find them via the system of posted signup sheets for the scheduled games! I recommend posting any game with free slots n the whiteboard, whether it's in the scheduling system or no.

      For the dealer table, it's open to anyone with self-published anything, but space is limited. We've never had to turn anyone away, but it DOES get a bit crowded on there! So, bottom line: the earlier and better you communicate me the better we can accommodate and promote your merch. We'll be making a Booth Menu with brief descriptions of each game, provided creators submit soon enough. More info to come!

      Also, there are tables available in the Dealer Hall for open gaming and demos, which we took advantage of a weee bit last year. It requires solid booth staffing to make it work: a designer or otherwise qualified demo-er PLUS an additional staffer to run the store.

      And finally, I have free passes! (well, in Gamestorm terms, "Memberships.") Firstly, I need staffers for the booth! If I recall, I'm allotted 4 Staff Memberships for the con, free provided you work at least one dealer table shift. I've got one spot filled, so let me know if you're interested in filling the other three. If you worked Staff last year, you get first shot. And secondly, I need candidates for "Industry Guest" memberships. What this means in Indie-land terms is that you're a somehow involved in a self-published/small press game, AND committed to scheduling at least one session of it. More details on complimentary Memberships to come!

      3) The Hassles: My earlier comment was a little flip, but the real deal is, last year was my first year Hurricane Herding, and despite some warnings from past organizers to expect a lot of frustration and resistance, I found the Gamestorm Committee helpful and accommodating. Everything I asked for, I got, in terms of scheduling, playspace, program book listing, supplies reimbursement, etc. The only "pain" involved was learning the ins and outs of the bureaucracy, sitting in a lot of meetings, and dealing with institutional inertia and miscommunication. There's room for improvement; for instance the Open Gaming/Games on Demand concept doesn't mesh well with GS's scheduling paradigm, and I hope to get that facet of the Hurricane more integrated and supported this year. But really, the whole thing went well last year and I look forward to even greater heights this year, especially with y
      all involved!

      The only truly negative thing I have to report is that we lost one of our dedicated gaming rooms this year--we used to have TWO plus the lobby space. The con needed another room for administrative use, and ours got the cut. I lobbied for IH as hard as I could in this matter, but there wasn't much I could say in the face of the observation that most of our tables went unused last year. Fact is, attendance was lowest its ever been last year, and some game-runners didn't show, some game-PLAYERS didn't show, etc. The stuff that DID happen was a rockin' good time, though, and some cool people (like Ben Robbins and Ross Cowman) came down for the first time and rocked the shit out of the con, carrying that goodwill and enthusiasm forward. With that buzz, and the interest I'm seeing in this thread, not to mention some renewed enthusiasm I'm hearing from locals, I expect a big turnaround, demonstrating that the Hurricane can indeed fill space with concentrated fun-having, and expanding us for next year. In the meantime, we have an overflow room available, shared with other departments, in case we get too big for our britches.

      Any questions, fire away! If you wanna volunteer for anything, whisper me!

      Peace,
      -Joel

      \n This was my fifth Gamestorm, and far and away my favorite. It was also the first year I attended all four days, and that was definitely worth it, though I was pretty exhausted by the end. I was busy enough with Indie Hurricane stuff and games that I'd scheduled that I barely saw the rest of the con - I spent maybe half an hour in the boardgame room and two minutes in the dealer's hall. Anyway, here's what I ended up playing:

      We Are Here To See The Evil Wizard Kormakur with Robert, Ross, Michael W, and Morgan. This was a great way to start things off - one of Bully Pulpit's free little experimental games that they were giving away last month. We played for about an hour and a half, which was a great way to kind of ease into the convention mindset.

      My Daughter, The Queen Of France with Abi, Michael P, Nicholas, Matt, Morgan, and Al. This was a tough one. I broke both my "no MDQF with strangers" vow and my "no story games with more than four people" vow because, well, MDQF is my favorite game, and apparently I can't pass up an opportunity to play it. As it turns out, playing with strangers was no problem (everyone was great), but seven people is more than a little unwieldy, and as Shakespeare, I completely forgot to write Abi's character into any of the scenes. Don't get me wrong: I still really enjoyed it. Next time, though, I'm playing with three others, and we're finding a quiet corner of the hotel to play in.

      Penny For My Thoughts with Ross, Morgan, and Al. We decided we were still up for another game after finishing MDQF at 9:30, so we started up a game of Penny, figuring we'd be finished by midnight. WRONG. I think we finally wrapped up around 1:30 - it's great game, and I might have found my character's arc the most satisfying of the four times I've played, but we were falling asleep at the table by the end.

      Mouse Guard with Candy, Adre, Justin, and Paul. I think this went really well! It's funny, though: Joel had asked me if I'd be interested in running this in the Children's Programming track, which I was, but Friday was still a school day, so I ended up playing with four adults. We had a four-hour slot, so I scrapped my plans to run a pregen scenario and had everyone make characters (which was a nice way to introduce some of the rules) and then whipped up a mission on the fly. Everyone really seemed to enjoy themselves, and we got to hit all the stuff that I think is important to show off in MG: Nature, Fate, Nature, Persona, Nature, scripted conflicts, Nature, Beliefs, Nature, Goals, Nature, Instincts, Nature, etc.

      Umbra with Carl, Twyla, Seth, and John. This is Carl's Shadowrun-inspired hack for AGON. I suspect that I don't have a competitive bone in my body, so AGON isn't the best fit for me, but I love to see it in action, and it was great to see Twyla and John tie for Rep (Glory). And of course, it's always a treat to play in a game that Carl's running.

      Fiasco with Brady, Johnzo, and Cathe. One of us had never played before, so we went with the Suburbia playset, and everything went swimmingly: teenage Tile World manager in love with his stepmother, middle-aged Tile World sales attendant setting the manager's car on fire, dog turd held triumphantly aloft in divorce court, etc.

      Monsterhearts with Brendan, Jackson, and Michael P. I'd never played Monsterhearts before, and man, was that a mistake. We played part one of Joe's "Misty Harbour" scenario, and things went great. Which is to say, um, things went horribly wrong.

      Slammin' with Morgan, Hans, and Jonathan. Pretty silly, and a lot of fun! It took a little while to get the hang of how the turns work, but this one definitely takes the "AXE COP Danger Patrol" award for most giggling of the con for me. I hadn't realized Jackson had these at the IH booth - I would've picked up a copy.

      Shelter In Place My first time running (or playing, for that matter) a LARP. The LARP folks were terrific about organizational stuff, and I had fun, but I definitely realized I have a lot to learn about setting up this kind of thing. More about that here.

      Murderous Ghosts with Bryan. We only played for a few minutes on Sunday morning while we waited for the early games to start, and I just felt like I was starting to get the hang of it when we stopped, but I'm pretty excited about this one! I wanna find a copy of my own soon.

      Zombie Cinema with Johnzo, Sherry, and Brendan. Along with Fiasco, this is my go-to pickup game for three or four people. We ended up telling the story of a pathogen carried by in-flight meals and of the four survivors trapped in quarantine at PDX.

      Also boardgames! I think I played Catacombs with Michael W, Samurai with Adre, Justin, and Ross, Dominion with Bryan and Sev, and a great game of Battlestar Galactica with Brendan, Christian, Johnzo, Matthew, and Twyla.

      So yeah, I think that was it! I was really excited about this year, and I was just thrilled with how everything turned out. It was especially great to play with folks like Christian and Johnzo, who I've hung out with a fair amount but have never actually played anything with. Thanks so much to everyone I played with, everyone that facilitated open gaming, and especially to Joel and Hans for organizing the Indie Hurricane stuff and Fi for coordinating LARP stuff.
