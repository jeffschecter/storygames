\n This afternoon I'm running a game for our friend Nikki (who has played rpgs before, but long ago), and my wife Sarah (who's agreed to play since my two regulars have other plans today and it would be a bit weird for just Nikki and myself). I really want to knock their socks off. I really want both Sarah and Nikki to be regular players. I know there are lots of issues there, and I'd like to discuss them, but not now.

I'm going to be using a slightly modified Wheel of FATE for the adventure.

They are going to be playing supposedly prim-and-proper victorian ladies who are actually investigators from either a private or church-run organization. They've been hired by an agency that places governesses because several young women have disappeared from one client's employ under mysterious circumstances.

I need the who, what, why, and how. Who is the villian? Why are they disappearing? How are the children involved? Etc...

Thanks.

    \n The awesome was brought. Sarah, my wife, who has been resistant to trying to play absolutely loved the story. Nikki was awesome. Think "Wuthering Heights" meets "The Howling" meets "Clue." I'm going to try to post an actual play report when the dust settles.

    The key to success was the Wheel of Primetime FateSpectres system that I've been hacking together. They learned the basics of the system in about 5 minutes and gave me such awesome flags that I couldn't help but succeed. After I initiated the story, I let them take turns framing scenes. If there was any kind of conflict, I'd roll some dice and for each "whammy" (beat me by five), I had them narrate an additional detail. Prep? Who needs prep?

    Meanwhile, I had Halloween sound effects playing in the background.
