    EDIT : just one small thing, I don't need a release date, just to know that you still want to finish your game.
    \nThe philosophy of game text openers is probably its own blog post. And I've been meaning to blog more, but for the moment I'm working around 50+ hours a week, and no one pays me to blog (or to do Master Plan).

    I wouldn't say I'm aggressively playtesting Mythender anymore. But I have decided that all games are in development up until you submit it to the printer. And I do occasionally bring it out, partly to remind myself how it works. (I let it sit since January, and played two games of it last month at GPNW and Origins. That turns out to be awesome, because the last of the "unsolvable" problems were solve by reexamining the game from a distance.)

    Christian St. Pierre and I have been talking off and on. We're both *very* interested in working together, but we haven't committed yet because I need the text more or less done so I know what I need art for. I like him, we have a rapport, and I like his line art style. He does good horror fantasy, and I think he can bring what I want to Mythender. And I'll be working with Daniel Solis for layout. Wait, I'm sorry, I mean Daniel Fucking Solis.

    What's left to do? Writing the damned thing. Butt-in-chair. Then first round editing, then beta playtesting. I want to make sure the text is usable by others. I have had 70 people play Mythender is various states, which has helped me refine the math, the conceit, the language choices, the way I explain things at the table, etc. but that's useless if I fuck it up in the book.

    How can you help? I'll be mouthy about getting beta playtesters, when the time comes. And it seems that harassment is working to remind me people want it.

    The usual? In the morning, a vanilla latte with no foam. In the evening, a Manhattan.

    Wait, you didn't mean that.

    I'm not going to give a release date, because it's not done enough to where I can see the future. Working with others means meshing schedules, so I can't just declare when I'm going to have it ready. I'd *like* to have it out by next con season. We'll see. But I totally am keen to finish it. I want to show the world my little epic fantasy story game that uses more dice than your mother.

    I'll answer more later, but I'm swamped with work right now.

    Thanks! \\m/

    - Ryan

    Posted By: jackson teguRyan: i have a hilarious (different) word-play deviation of Mythender to make up, as soon as you have a playable version of the game.
    \nIf it's playing as the Build Team from Mythbusters, Carl Rigney came up with that idea many moons ago.

    And Sophie's misreading of "My Thender" has left me figuring out what a Thender is, to stat it up for the game. Perhaps that's a content entry.

    Oh, and Daniel Solis just wrote down the word "Mythbender." *THROWS THE FUCKING HORNS* \\m/ \\m/

    But, here's something I have been thinking about for the last 18 months, as a potential follow-up micro product: REICHENDER.
    It's an alternate WWII setting where the Manhattan Project was a super soldier serum, and the Axis refined theirs first. So, you have an army of \xdcbermenschen who have conquered the world. You're part of a ragtag underground who has realized that the way to return freedom to the world isn't to build a *safe* serum like the Axis has, but one that can overpower.

    Or, it's about how the line between being Captain America (hero of a people) and being the Hulk (a source of fear and justification for the conquering Axis that has sworn to purge you). I have a few notes down about how the game changes as this scale, but that's as far as I'm allowing myself before Mythender itself is done. Still, just saying that to say "yes, we're all about the wordplay on Team Mythender!"\n
