\n There's not a whole lot I can add to the title of the thread, but perhaps a little background is in order.

One of the forums I frequent is Snail's Pace, a Vanilla-based forum designed expressly for the playing of indie-type games in a PBP environment. There's been some promising stuff there and I go back often to see if anything new has come up since my last visit. The problem? There's never anything new that's come up since my last visit.

I know it's the Curse of the Format that says all PBP/PBeM games shrivel up and die, but I'm hoping that isn't the case with the whole forum. I like the fact that it exists and I like its clear intentions. I would be very disappointed if it just sort of petered out of existence.

So tell me Snail's Pace isn't dead and is just resting. Maybe it's pining, maybe it's not, but tell me things aren't as dire as they seem.

    \n Hi!

    Unbeknownst to most, I set up and run Snail's Pace. I'm still approving new accounts for the site, and I check in every so often to answer questions.

    I set Snail's Pace up when I lived in Japan and didn't have a lot of people around to play with. Now I live in New Zealand and have a lot of people, so I don't use the site much. I never found forum gaming very satisfying, so I'm not very motivated to keep it up. My best experiences were with Jonathan Walton's "Kazekami Kyoko Kills Kublai Khan" which everyone should check out.

    If someone is really keen on the site and has a vision for where they want to take it, I'd be happy to set up someone else with an Admin account.

    Cheers,

    Simon
