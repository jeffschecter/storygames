\n So this thread started interesting, but quickly turned into an exercise in one-shot word gags:

http://forum.rpg.net/showthread.php?t=535642

But honestly? If someone proposed a 5-session PTA or Smallville or whatever game where the concept was, "You are vampires. And you run a bakery." I'd totally be all in. I mean, the game wouldn't be 100% serious WoD-style play, but at least I would make it as serious and interesting as an interaction between, say, Pushing Daisies and True Blood (leaning towards the True Blood side).

There's something about a juxtaposition about something really cool and something really mundane that does it for me. Not a "I will play this game and nothing else for a year", but at the same time not "Yeah, this will be a one-shot laugh and then over" either.

I have actually run a game called "The Gardeners of Dune", where the players were gardeners for the Harkonnen during the Dune series Harkonnen-Atreides power shift. Big, awesome universe filled with Gom Jabbars and Mentats and Fremen Ninga, and you five are gardeners, and must GARDEN HARD FOR THE EMPIRE. It was, in fact, a one shot at a local con a few years back, but the session was filled before the convention even began (I used BESM as the rules-set). Sure there were some awesome fights and stuff, but still the concept was "awesome world, but you are mundane". Opposite of "normal mundane world, but you are awesome" like the Vampire Bakers idea, but I think the overlap is 100%.

Anyone else like this? Anyone have any ideas for a series of their own?

-Andy

    \n I once had a hankering for a game in which you play temporary employees hired by a company who sent you throughout time to clean up anachronisms and paradoxes that threaten the very fabric of reality. Some day I'll run that one once I find a system for it.

    I did run a two-part Dread game where the player characters were a family living in the Midwest in the early 1950s who woke up one morning to find that they were vampires. Everything was kept to a mundane, survival level with just the right amount of creepy. How were they going to survive? Were they going to have to take Billy out of school before he got to quarterback at the big game? Young Sally had to step up to be the provider by luring neighborhood children into the woods at night.They were hounded by a lone door-to-door bible salesman who managed to kill one of them by whispering a psalm to them. It was lovely stuff.
