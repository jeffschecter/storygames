    \n\nChristian_Griffen \n\n\n
    \n
    \n\nMarch 2007 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n They're two very different beasts (hah!). Vs. Monsters deals with challenges exclusively through card draws. It's a completely mechanical resolution system. The whole idea behind the Beast Hunters rules is to allow players to use their creativity as much as possible in addressing challenges and only use dice as a last resort. BH also has a limited GM budget, player-set difficulty level, ritual space opening and closing, and, as you pointed out, a very different setting.

    So the only similarity is that you hunt monsters in both, but the setting, rules, dynamics, and so on are vastly different.
