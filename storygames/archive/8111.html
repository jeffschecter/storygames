    \n Kitty Explosion! - the completely original game of cat action that has not been influenced by Panty Explsion at all and definitely hasn't stolen anything from Cat either!

    To make your cat first choose a Cat Type. Give it three names: one name that humans call you. one name that cats call you and name name that is your real name (remember to write it backwards on the sheet so no-one learns it by mistake!). Then you distribute 1, 2, 3, 4 and 5 dice to your stats. The stats are:

    Coat - you can tell a hardy cat by it's coat. Use this pool to endure, persist and exert strength. (Earth)
    Legs - good legs make a quick cat. Use this pool to jump, climb, run and dodge. (Air)
    Claws - if running away isn't your thing you can use some sharp claws. Use this pool to threaten, assault and generally be aggressive. (Fire)
    Face - eyes, nose, ears, mouth - they're all in the face. Use this pool to be perceptive, figure things out, express feelings and be social. (Water)
    Tail - somethings are unseen by the naked eye, yet evident in the way a tail moves. Use this pool to sense if someone is lying, see the unseen and - if you possess the power - do magicks. (Void)

    Select Traits based on:
    Your highest stat
    Cat Type (Tom Cat, Top Cat, Alley Cat, Ceiling Cat, Kitty Cat, Evil Lazy Lasagna Loving Genius Cat, etc)
    Something you've learned from a human (maybe your owner if you have/have had one)
    What you do for play
    A whim - you can't expect cats to make sense, can you?

    Cats who know magicks are feared and can never be voted most popular. Any normal action can be enhanced by magicks but only by using the Tail pool can you do more obvious stuff.

    Or something like that. =^.^=

    \nOdd. I've got to say I never expected to see this kind of a thread here. Usually it's an rpg.net thing with an immediate dog-pile of "everybody mention your favorite games". Nothing useful every comes out of 'em from what I've seen; most folks don't even bother to say _why_ this or that game would be a good choice, they just cough up a few names like a reflex action.
    \n
    \nI'd say it really depends on what you're wanting to get out of the game in the first place. If you just want a game system offered up, I'd offer up PDQ. I'd probably go with the Zorcerer of Zo implementation, although the new PDQ# (which is what's being used in the upcoming Swashbucklers of the Seven Skies) would work just fine as well.
    \n
    \nThe main advantage to PDQ being the whole "player named traits" thing. You can have the player make their cat in whatever fashion they want and the mechanical bits are all reflecting stuff that the player is going to feel is important.
    \n
    \nThe system has been my "go to" system for a while. There's a number of similarities between it and the system you find in Spirit of the Century, but it doesn't have the extra fiddly bits that SotC comes with (stunts and skills). PDQ# does move a bit more in that direction than the version used in Zorcerer, but it's still got less stuff going on than Sotc; at least it feels that way to me.
    \n
    \nBut.... yeah, it depends on what you're wanting out of the whole thing. Beast Hunters would be an interesting lens to filter a cat rpg through. Kinda appropriate since you're a hunter and all. Just have to change the nature of the critters you're hunting. Tattoos could simply shift a bit and become special tricks of some sort or another that cats learn.
    \n
    \nAgon might be amusing filtered through the kitty lens as well. I'd be more inclined to have folks be something like lions and they could go around beating up hyenas and jackals.
    \n
    \nIf you feel like playing system monkey a bit, Houses of the Blooded might be an amusing filter for a cat game. Shift the houses to different cat breeds and... I dunno, that might be enough. I haven't had much of a chance to really read it.
    \n
    \nI suppose if you're going for re-skinning, Faery's Tale Deluxe would probably work as well.
    \n
    \nAnd for the old school dungeon inspired cat, you could always hack Red Box Hack. For extra points it could be combined with How to Host a Dungeon. The dungeon gets to be the neighborhood and then the cats go through it killing or whatever it is that cats are supposed to do in the game.

    \n You could easily fiddle with Solar System to make the three Pools about cats, even taking cues from the Cat RPG, with the Pools being Fur, Claws and Meow.

    Fur is socially getting along with other cats. Replenish this pool by infringing on another cat's territory, grooming another cat, teaching a young cat something, learning from an older cat or rutting under the porch.

    Claws is the ability to survive in the world. Replenish Claws by bragging about a great battle, telling the story of how you got a scar or a notched ear or asking about the weakness of a creature you plan to do battle with.

    Meow is the ability to manipulate humans. Replenish Meow by watching a human's dreams while it sleeps, being pet by a human, taunting a human by not allowing it to pet you but listening to it beckon you to come closer, destroying a human's stuff.

    Add Secrets and Keys to taste.

    GO!

    \n I give you the most tightly defined Sorcerer One-Sheet I have ever written.

    Sorcerers are all members of the Bast Society an exclusive, high-class club committed to leisure and pleasure. The Bast Society's goal is to transcend the stresses and responsibilities of mundane existence through pleasure and sensuality. Not all members of the Bast Society are Sorcerers or even desire to be so. The Bast Society is head-quartered in New York but has "dens" in almost every major city in the world. Within Dens social cliques tend to form and often refer to themselves as "Prides" but the Bast Society does not formally recognize these naturally occurring sub-circles as part of the organization.

    Demons are fully loaded with cat imagery. Cat familiars (passers), cat spirits (possessors and inconspicuous), cat rings and statues (objects), cat implanted teeth or claws (parasites). You get the idea. Common Desires are: Knowledge, Sensual Gratification, and Mischief.

    Rituals are based on sensuality, pleasure and sometimes predator/prey behavior. You might actually have to hunt down and dominate a demon or indulge in throws of languishing sexual extacy.

    Humanity is the ability to take pro-active responsibility for your own life. At zero Humanity you are completely dependent on someone or something to take care of your wants and needs. (The Bast Society is slowly turning into a kind of welfare-substate commune which is breeding dysfunctional Master-Slave relationships among its members).

    Have fun!

    Jesse
