    Posted By: AnarchangelAnarchangel loves Spike loves Buffy.
    \nThanks! I created it midway through Season 5. I was bummed by how sappy and weak-spined that whole drama got in late Season 6, but for a while, it was really excellent.

    When Spike was the only one who knew that Buffy had fallen from heaven, and he used that as sexual leverage... man. That was some good pathos. But Spike is apparently really hard to write for the long haul, because he always gets really pathetic, and then needs a re-invention, and then is awesome for a bit, and then gets really pathetic again.

    Posted By: Anarchangel(I'd never heard of 8tracks. What a cool service!)
    \nI really like it. I've put a bunch of my Ribbon Drive mixes up there, as well as using it as a design inspiration whenever I work on a game.

    There are some weird bumps in the software to accommodate licensing restrictions: you can only have up to 2 songs by any one artist on a mix, mixes are on shuffle every 2nd time you listen through them. The 2-song-limit restriction is good mix design anyways, so I"m not bummed about that one. The second-time-shuffle thing is weird but kind of ok.

    \n Dionysus,

    You'd asked a little while ago where Monsterhearts was available, but at the time it wasn't. Good news: times have changed!

    Monsterhearts is finished, and all of the preorders have been shipped out. It's for sale now. You can buy the PDF for $10, and the Print + PDF combo for $25. If you think money is stupid, or perhaps don't have very much of it, then you can alternately purchase the game using good deeds as currency. The details are all here: Monsterhearts.

    I played it twice this week. Lots of awkward sex, awkward violence, teen rebellion, murderous betrayal, and weird hallucinogenic gazes into the abyss. If you're interested in reading some AP of the game, the Monsterhearts page links out to 11 different AP posts/recordings.
