\n Just poking the pyre's ashes a bit, here--I realize that this is going to be a bitch-post.

Has anyone else tried to reach out in their sorrow--outside of the hyper-narrow community which consumes so may of our hours--and been faced with the deafening silence of incomprehension?

It vexes me. I am terribly vexed.

I have not faced such proof of the marginalization of what has been so paramount in my life, until yesterday. I don't know whether to pity the ignorant or to doubt my commitment to such a fringe culture. RE the former: read the Internet today--I am certain that I lean on the shoulders of millions who mourn. RE the latter, I have met exactly four people, since yesterday, who know the name Gary Gygax. Four. [FWIW, I know about 100 people in my "local scene".]

Fuckin'-A, I verbally bitch-slapped a lovely, young (i.e. ignorant) woman at the pub today, because she laughed at my attempt to explain how "gamer geeks" could feel such inchoate sorrow, over the death of a hero, icon, influence, mentor, etc...

Am I insane? Are we? To invest such passion...... I wonder if I am becoming That Guy who bemoaned the death of the horse carriage....

Confused and hurting;
David

    \n When I heard that Gygax died, I felt pretty bizzarely wrenched. I haven't tried to express my reaction to anyone outside - and very few inside - our narrow community, because my feeling makes very little sense, even to most other gamers I know.

    Not because of the game, exactly. But because he was central to creating a transferrable sense of community some of us geeks, of a kind that did not exist before, one that is transferred by means of a social activity rather than a shared non-social body of knowledge. There's this thing that came into the world around him; he wasn't the only one, but he was the icon.

    Thing is, that sounds cold and analytical. But it isn't meant to be. Because it means that I have had friendship, warmth, light and laughing in places where, otherwise, I would have been a completely lonely nerd. I value that. I value who it has made me. To know that the iconic starting point from which our strange and endlessly welcome contagion has gone from this earth gives me a small, weird, and hollow feeling, in the midst of everything else passing through my life.

    I'm not sure if I'm making sense. Fukit.

    \n On the flip side, the posts in here and at the RPG.net and apparently all over the place make me feel an outsider in roleplay culture. I don't find Gary particularly important - all the roleplaying I've done has been a result of my hard work - and what I read about others roleplay has always seemed a result of their hard work too. It's like we got some vague instructions from Gary on how to do something with bricks - we then go on to build the pyramids or castles - but I see all these posts about 'Oh my god, we couldn't have done this without Gary!'. Yes we could AND we did! Seriously it amazes me how people put in a crap load of work, then act as if Gary put in all the work. It reminds me of the fable about stone soup. I'm really surprised - all this time I thought people knew how much it just came down to what they did and do.
    \n
    \nNow, try explaining to the young woman that you feel disconnected with a fringe culture because you don't think the same about a person she has never heard of! Hehe.

    Posted By: Callan S.On the flip side, the posts in here and at the RPG.net and apparently all over the place make me feel an outsider in roleplay culture.
    Same here.

    I, basically, have no real connection to Gary Gygax, beyond enjoying the things that developed out of his stuff. I'd be much more sad when Pratchett dies or maybe Daniel Bayn, 'cause those works I enjoyed directly and not just through the lens of 20 years of RPG evolution. (And in the case of Bayn had contact with him on the internet and really enjoyed his online persona.)

    Now, I do feel sad about the news, but this comes from two sources: One, I feel for the people Gygax was more central for then me. As we can see here, there were many people for whom he was extremely important, and I feel for their loss. Second, I'm always sad when I hear about anybody dying, simply because every death robs the world of something awesome.

    But I don't grief directly for Gygax. I didn't know him and I had, basically, nothing to do with him.
