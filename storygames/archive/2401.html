So, elemental dice, like those that come with "ElementaliS". There are five custom dice, which have these faces:
* fire
* water
* earth
* air
* "Ether" (a star-ish symbol)
* Sun OR double-fire OR double-earth OR double-air OR double-water

What would you do with these dice?

    First, some help:



    Next, I'd say "These are the dice that Fred Hick's godling RPG needs". Damn, I forget the name of it (I have it around here somewhere in PDF form), and have yet to play it, but it's a game where you use but one 1d6 to resolve conflicts, and the die results all represent these elements.

    Otherwise, I'd not even make it into a central game mechanic, but make a set of rules to append on to a secondary game, be it a generic sub-system or specific to a game or setting.

    I'm more interested in setting-specific uses (ie the elements represent the game setting, and don't really apply to the core rules), but here's something that jumped into my head when coupling it with an existing rules system:

    Legend of the Five Rings. The dice above become like the TORG "Fate Deck". Every 'game round' of action, battle, seduction, whatever, take four of the dice and roll them together, two for each "side" of the conflict. The results are bonuses:

    Side A gets a Water and a Double Earth. Side B gets an Air and an Air. That means that if Side A uses abilities that fall under the Water attribute, they get +1 die (or +1 Keep). Double Earth means +2. Side B gets +2 to air abilities. After that round, they roll again. It would require the GM (or players) to think fast about what things might be happening that would give these bonuses.

    Ether would be... hmmm... Maybe this?

    1 Ether on either side means some sort of benefit. Either something changes in the landscape, or just everyone on that side gets a +1.

    If 2 Ethers are rolled on one side and none on the other, that's a miraculous turn on events: +3 to all actions.

    However, if BOTH sides roll an Ether, that's a setback for both sides. -1 to all abilities, and some sort of fallout from the background (zombies appear, stakes increase, etc).

    If one side has one Ether, and the other side has 2 Ethers, then this happens: The 1-Ether side gets the +1 benny or good background fallout, and the 2-Ether side gets a Horrendous Setback, -3 (to a minimum of one) on all abilities and bad background fallout (rocks fall, NPC retainers flee, the courtesean's husband appears with an axe).

    If both sides get 2 Ethers, then fuck the world explodes or something. Fu Leng shows up in front of you and shares a riceball.

    You have a supernatural ability. You can shoot fire from your hands, let's say. To use it, you have to power up, which is risky. In play, when you want to activate your ability, you get to roll 1-5 elemental dice, and you need to get at least one element match for your ability to function (fire, in this case).

    Each die you choose to roll adds to your "power" meter, though, which is a pool that you have to roll when you're stressed or freaked out. If you roll element matches then, your power goes all haywire and bad things happen.

    The more matches you roll (in either situation) the more potent your ability is.
