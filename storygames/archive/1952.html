Being nice

Graham
December 2006 edited December 2006 in Forum Discussion
In the Story Games Pow-Wow thread, Matt Snyder was talking about the "be nice" attitude on this board:
There is no mechanism to be critical, because the moderation is "Hey, gang, play nice." I DO play nice and yet am not pleased with a handful of discussions I've participated in. I think the "dont' harsh anyone's buzz" vibe going around is ... annoying. As in: Counter the nicey-nice community in some way, and they'll not respond your ideas....

So, I'd love to see a bit more discussion and a bit less can't-we-all-just-get-along hippiness, because I think SG is becoming more of a comfort blanket and less a place in which valuable content develops to RPG hobbyists
So, let's talk about this, in a nice, non-threatening way, which respects everyone's point of view.

I don't agree. I like the fact that, on Story Games, I can:

1. Post a comment to say "That's a great idea!" without having to add anything to the discussion. I understand the idea on the Forge ("Don't post anything unless you've got something to add") but I like the Story Games way better.

2. Post without worrying about some bulletin-board addict ripping my nascent game idea to shreds.

3. Post without worrying about whether I'm talking crap. Seriously, I love this. I love the fact I can post "How would a Meat Loaf LARP work?" and, despite the fact the idea is patently crazy, I'll get enough ideas to develop the game.

What does everyone else think? Does the niceness irritate you? Do you think we're being so nice, that people are afraid to voice criticism?
