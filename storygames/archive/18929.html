\n You start off with a list of sci-fi sounding words that have no actual meaning.

Then you give everyone a role, a security chief, a starship captain, the science officer, the medic, etc. Someone says that they try to do something using one of the meaningless words in the sentence, as a verb or the subject or object. There's no tension in the early game, it starts out as a routine operation.

Then another person gives the word its meaning by responding to it. And records how the word was used and what it now means. The response should also include a fact about the setting which leads into a new problem for the next player to talk about. Reincorporating a previously established word is also required in sentences in the second half of the game.

Eventually you populate the dictionary and establisth a setting through the conversation and activities of the crew of the ship.

Does a game like this already exist?

    \n I could see something like this being a fun addition to "Video Scream" to create a parody of sci-fi dilemmas. As with many games, it would take the right group to pull it off entertainingly. Particularly, people adroit with tongue twisters or a difficult turn-of-phrase.

    - "Bridge, the torqued-down spindle rollers are causing the sphincter valves to oscillate."
    - "Well, by-pass the main power couplings with the auxiliary capacitors."
    - "But - isn't Medical using the auxiliary capacitors to complete their Level 3 Berillion sweep of the ship?"
    - "Great Googily Moogily! You're right! We'll just have to invert the phase inducers and hope for the best."

    I doubt I could pull it off for more than 10 minutes but I'd definitely watch others playing it.
