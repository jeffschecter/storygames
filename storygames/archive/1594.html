The Amber Playtest that Didn't Happen at Camp Nerdly

Vaxalon
October 2006 edited October 2006 in Meetups / Conventions
I was pleased to see that there were people who were interested in playtesting the Amber rules that I brought to Camp Nerdly. I was a little disappointed that the interest wasn't quite high enough to make it happen, but I went in knowing that was a risk, so no hard feelings on my end.

If you had a look at the rules I left behind, you know that they were sketchy and incomplete. (BTW, Dave... did you pick those up? The rules are no biggie but I like that folder) They're getting less sketchy and less incomplete as time goes on.

What I'd like to do now is attempt to arrange for a one-day, eight-hour playtest of these rules. This will probably involve a significant amount of fits and starts, and restarts, and perhaps some flashes of coolness and probably some disappointments.

What I'm looking for is six to twelve interested people. Here are the requirements:

1> A basic familiarity with the Amber universe. Having read "Nine Princes in Amber" (or committing to reading it before the game) is the bare minimum; the "Corwin Chronicles" (that is, books 1-5) would be better. Any decent library will have them.

2> Ability to travel to the Northern Virginia area. Exact location TBD; since this event will hopefully be bigger than I can comfortably accommodate in my house I can't say where it will happen yet. Unfortunately, due to the way these rules are set up, an online playtest isn't feasible.

3> A willingness to try new things.

If you're interested in this project, please either post here or email me at vaxalon-at-gmail.com.
