\n ...is that, I dunno, I like leaving things in public places for people to find. I remember being very small and finding a little chick tract in a restroom somewhere, and being fascinated by it (I grew up in a Christian family, so the content wasn't strange to me).

So, recently, I've been very excited about XXXXtreme STREET luge and XXXXtreme-O-Phile Magazine, first issue. These are brilliant little chunks of hilarity, and they make me want to game them. I love the punk/zine feel. So I've begun to print out a bunch of these things and cut and staple 'em together, and then drop them off in public places (restroom, bench, etc.)

I'm at this point: Why am I doing this? Do I actually think a random person will pick this up and be interested enough in it to try to play it (XSl is decidedly not newbie-friendly, and I quote, "This is a roleplaying game so fuck off if you don't know what that is.")? I suppose there's a certain type of person (like me), who would read that "fuck off!" and only become more interested.

Anyway, do any of you think there's any value in this activity beyond me getting my weird kicks? "No" is a perfectly acceptable answer. I'm not quite sure why I'm posting this, except that I've really been enjoying this activity and it's kind of strange and I'm curious to get outside of my own mind on this.

    \n This is hardcore brilliant. One of the things the old-skoolers often circle-jerk about is that back in the early days of the hobby, everyone was playing a fundamentally different game since there was no clarity in the rules, and hardly anybody owned the same set of rules. That said, someone should reformat Ghost/Echo or some other oracle game into something 'zine-style and then watch the Internet for reports of people playing some incredible, far-out, unique-as-can-be games.
