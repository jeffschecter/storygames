\n Hi, folks! Thou Art But A Warrior is available as a PDF at the Un-Store! For those of you who might not be familiar with it...\n
Though the great Caliphate has been dissolved and the unity of Iberia\u2019s Muslims lost, their power has not failed entirely. The cities have their armies, and at the heart of those armies are the noble orders of Muslim Knights. These Knights swear solemn oaths to revere Allah and his Prophet, to fight for the defense of the innocent, and to honor a strict code of ethics and morals in order to bring about peace and justice for their society. They are warriors and poets, and they are the last best hope of a people who do not see their doom before them.

Their inevitable failure is a thing to be mourned most deeply.

Thou Art But A Warrior is a game in which you play members of the orders of Muslim Knights sworn to defend Islamic Spain at the beginning of the Crusades. Winner of the Forge\u2019s Setting Design Contest in 2007, Thou Art But A Warrior is a supplement of Polaris, by Ben Lehman of TAO Games.
\nAlso, if you're curious, a good actual play can be found here. I also did an interview with Rob Bohl on the Independent Insurgency about it way back when, which you can listen to here.

    \n Currently, it's a supplement of Polaris - so you do need a copy of Polaris to play right now. However, I have a complete edition in the works that will be released at the end of the year, which will include the Polaris rules. (Thanks, Ben!) If you spring for the PDF now, I'll send you a copy of the revised supplement material once the new edition comes out at the end of the year. Also, if you already own the dead tree version of Thou Art But A Warrior, whisper me your email and I'll send you the PDF for free. I'll also put you on the list of people to get a revised supplement PDF when the new edition comes out. This will ONLY be the material covered in the supplement, however, and not any of the new stuff being added into the complete edition. Just so you know.

    If you don't currently own a copy of Polaris, it might be better to wait until the complete edition is released to pick it up. I'll be releasing a PDF of the complete edition concurrently with the book. (But I'll still keep the supplement PDF around for those of you who don't feel like paying up Polaris twice.)
