\n We love short shorts! What's not to love about a game that you can fit into 15 minutes? This is my first try at seriously creating some short games, also known as 'game poems', so there's a whole new section of the site that contains not one, not two, but three brand new short games for you to try out. The first was played at Conception '13 and worked very well, the second is a cut down version of a mini-game previously available here and the third is something completely new which should appeal to all fans of Rich Burlew and the Order of the Stick!

Char. Gen: This short game is most suitable for anyone who\u2019s played an RPG before and had issues with the character creation process; it\u2019s a comedy game in which everyone collaborates on creating a single character for an unnamed RPG.

Mystery of Life: This short game has the potential to contain a lot of adult material and touch upon themes suitable only for a mature audience, so be sure everyone is on the same page before sitting down to play. The game takes place in a celestial courtroom, where the disposition of a newly deceased soul is being contested by an Angel and a Demon; will they go to Heaven or to Hell?

The Crayons of Time: This short game is most suitable for anyone with an interest in fantasy fiction; it has a competitive element and can be played as either drama or comedy, or both! All the players are Gods who are going to collectively create a world, whilst trying to claim absolute authority over aspects of what is created without wrecking the whole creative process.
