What you missed on those online chat thingies

Thomas Robertson
June 2006 edited June 2006 in Meetups / Conventions
Since Adam put together the Foundry a month or so back the place has really been hopping. It's been a great place to hang and find games. What's been really cool is that, at the same time, the #indierpgs IRC channel has been just as busy as it always has been, so no one's really robbing activity from anyone else. That's pretty cool.

Adam has this pretty cool thread in which he's been trying to keep up with what's going on on the Foundry so that people who don't hang there can see and (hopefully) get excited and join us. I was going to do a similar thread for #indierpgs, but then I figured it made just as much sense to just make a single thread for both.

(For reference, the Foundry is a MUSH located on the server "legendary.org" on port 7777. #indierpgs is an IRC channel located on the server "irc.magicstar.net" in the channel #indierpgs. #indierpgs is the IRC channel attached to the Indie Netgaming Yahoogroup.)

So, recently on the Foundry:
  • Joao, Dave, and I played a scene of Capes last night. It was rather fun.
  • I badgered Mo into agreeing to playtest C+P next Wednesday at "8pm", no timezone was specified.
And, recently on #indierpgs:
  • Some of the regulars from the weekly HeroQuest game ran a supplemental scene last night.
  • Speaking of weekly HeroQuest, it's Thursday, which means it's HeroQuest night. Newcomers are welcome, though we will pressure you to play with us. Knowledge of the game is completely unneccessary.
  • Alex's Monday night playtest of Ensemble, which welcomes curious observers and interested players.
I'm sure I've missed some important stuff, so hopefully people will jump in and point that out. If you've ever been curious about any of the games being talked about, or about playing online in general, what are you waiting for?

Thomas

  • Thomas Robertson
    July 2006
    Yeah, #indierpgs tends to be a bit more spread out time-wise. There are generally people there round the clock, and some amount of activity from 1700-0800 GMT. Things tend to pick up around 2200 GMT, and then again at 0000 GMT.

    The Foundry is, at the moment, a smaller community composed primarily of Americans. Almost all major acticity there falls between 2200 and 0500 GMT.

      Foundry MUSH has some slick new dice code that tracks die type and lets you pick up and discard or reroll specific dice from your pile.

      Example (actual command syntax and output may vary slightly):

      > roll/sorted 2d6 3d8 1d10 1d4
      DICE: Adam rolls 2d6 3d8 1d10 --> 7(d8) 6(d6) 6(d8) 4(d6) 3(d8) 2(d4) 1(d10)

      > add/sorted 1d10
      DICE: Adam adds 1d10 to his roll and gets 5 --> 15(d20) 7(d8) 6(d6) 6(d8) 5(d10) 4(d6) 3(d8) 2(d4) 1(d10)

      > reroll 9(d10)
      DICE: Adam rerolls 1(d10) and get a 9 --> 15(d20) 9(d10) 7(d8) 6(d6) 6(d8) 5(d10) 4(d6) 3(d8) 2(d4)

      > discard 2(d4)
      DICE: Adam discards 2(d4) --> 15(d20) 7(d8) 6(d6) 6(d8) 5(d10) 4(d6) 3(d8)
