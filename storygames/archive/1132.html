    Posted By: Mark CauseyI'm not sure if anybody could make those assurances. I haven't had the best luck with Gamedays either but I've always made the best of them.

    It's funny because a lot of the guys from my gaming group have complained about not having had the most wonderful time at the Game Days too. I've definately seen some games there that I know I wouldn't have enjoyed all that much. But there are also some absolutely TOP NOTCH GM's there and I've learned a whole lot about running fun games from those folks. You just need to be a little selective about what you sign up for and who is running it.

    As for using the venue as a place to play games with just your friends, thats fine by me. Certainly for now it is. We've always had plenty of space and I don't see placeing requirements that you open up your games to "the public" if you you'd rather not. We've got a group that comes and plays Paladium almost every time and that's mostly been a "friends only" group. I would encourage you to consider letting other people in if they show genuine interest in the game and if you've got space. I think that's good for the hobby and just plain nice. But required? No way.

    I know that these forums are very "not about d20" and I'm totally fine with that. One of the things I like most about the NC Game Days is that it gives folks a great chance to try out and play different settings and systems than they're used to while having a GM who knows (and presumably likes) the system introduce it to them. It seems like the perfect venue to expose other gamers who may have led more sheltered gaming lives to new ideas in gaming.

    Hope to see you guys there.
