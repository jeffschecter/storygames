In the recent years the has been a growing number of games that offers explicit rules to GMs. This is IMO the best development from the Forge dimension.


So what types of rules are there.


- Limit decisions.
Games like The Puddle offer no mechanical decisions to the GM.

- Session resources.
PtA offers the GM a pool for every session to create challenges.

- Random values.
DitV uses some kind of random NPC creation.

- Conflicted decision.
Donjon does a good job to give every GM decision a good and a bad side from the players' perspective.

- Structured Plot
Several games use this is in some way: Inspectres, Donjon, Ganakagok....

- Omitting rules.
I've only seen this explicitly in Ganakagok. In this game there are three (named) rules that are left for the GM to set.



For a game I design I try to create another type of GM resource that is not completely bound to sessions but challenges. That means if the players solve a challenge imperfectly the remaining points of a major challenge carry over to later play in a unique way. (Like in Babylonf 5 for example, when they get the Drakh after the drive away the Shadows.)


Are there other types of GM rules? Which have you used? Where are ads and disads?

    My sci-fi heartbreaker is going to limit GM decisions and the budget. Basically, he has to accumulate points to use against the PCs. Simply said, each time the PCs make a skill check, they either make it and gain points for their side or fail and give the GM points to use against them. The enemies and gear that can be thrown against the PCs depend on the points available.

    That way, every check, every decision, has a consequence.

    I guess that's a combo of limit decisions and session resources. And it's not a death spiral, because the GM can set the difficulty number for those skill checks. And that doesn't lead to high skill checks all the time because the player characters get more experience for higher failures, or can select alternate skills to use.
