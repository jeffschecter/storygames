\nWhat stuff should we watch, read, play?

1.Feel free to respond here in-thread to another link posted.

a. If you're looking to start a conversation, perhaps take your comment and create a new thread with it in the Stuff to Watch category.

b.Otherwise, to prevent too many comments in the thread, number your response (first response: [1]; second response [2]; third response [3]). If you see that you're the second person to post, "Wow, that looks cool!" to a post here, then put a "[2]" at the top of your post. Three posts ([3]) is the limit: The fourth person to post should just take their comment and create a new thread, to carry the discussion over there further.

Why?

Because these threads are great: We can link to little things being talked about on the web in general, on blogs, or other forums that might be noteworthy, without having to dedicate a new thread to each and every one (although that, too, is fine if you want to discuss the item further or "open up a discussion").

Responding to these posts with a comment or two is understandable (other times there might be no comments, just cause the link was self-explanatory: nothing to say, lots of heads nodding in agreement). But we don't want to create a huge discussion in the middle of the thread: Some folks get a lot out of these threads, and don't want to look back to see the "10 latest posts" just to see that it's a heated discussion of something from a previous page. Cool? Cool.

    \nThis interview with @sucklord from Work of Art tweaked my interest. Note that Work of Art is reality TV and Sucklord is a reality TV contestant, so value it as you will. Sucklord's a DIY toymaker in a fine-art setting. Some of how he positions himself really reminds me of the indie games path that a lot of us have been following, and his Star Wars-fueled aesthetic puts me in mind of the values of the old school. I find it kind of intriguing and exciting, so maybe some of you will too.

    "I think more artists who are struggling to find a way in the structure of the contemporary art world should look to the business model of the toy world, for the reasons we just said. It\u2019s more democratic, and you can do it yourself. You can actually make a living at it without waiting for some stupid fucking art critic or some douchebag gallerist to give you the nod of approval."

    Posted By: jenskotGames and Toys Booming at B&N
    \nInteresting, I was actually going to blog about this somewhere.

    But basically, I went to a B&N at the Southpoint Mall (one of the bigger shopping malls in NC) for the first time in about a year or so. On a whim, I wandered over to where the roleplaying games normally are (at the end of the SF section, usually a small sad section of a few core books or supplements of various games): Nothing. There is no more roleplaying section at B&N. Not even a single copy of a D&D book.

    However, they had a very large area (about the size of perhaps the entire fiction section) for board games. And (aside from fucking Munchkin) GOOD boardgames, too! 7 Wonders, Dominion, Power Grid, Apples to Apples, Small World, Dixit, Tsuro, various social games, and so on.

    Mixed feelings on that: Sad at the final death of RPGs in the big box stores, but happy to see the resurgence of board games, with the local big-box bookstore being a place to pick up some very good games.
