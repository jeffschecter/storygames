    We here know that you mean "Planet Crashers was written in a 48 hour span of time" - go ahead and say that if you wish, but don't call it a "48 hour RPG".

    \n "48 hour RPG" made perfect sense to me. But it may just be due to familiarity with terms like "24 hour jam".

    Regarding your game:

    I love the Tropes and how they're based on rewarding a certain roleplay action. A couple of things that might not work, though. The Sidekick's extra bonus for making other players laugh feels a bit too rooted in politics - if a player is shy or new within the group, the other players will be less likely to laugh. I'd keep it like the others, straight cause and effect, without contigency on out-of-character reactions. As for the Hellseeker recharging by putting allies in danger is, well, it could certainly be "fun", I just hope you realize what you're setting yourself up for.

    The Abilities could probably use something to keep their range under control. What's to stop me from making my three abilities, "Being awesome" "Winning" and "Cool space guy stuff" and invoking all three of them for every check? Perhaps you could borrow this rule from Mortal Coil: "aptitudes that are more specific gain a +2 bonus against those that are more general."

    I'm not sure I like this cards/energon/wager system. It seems like a way of obscuring how much you have left of the one resource you spend to accomplish anything. But I'm personally not a huge fan of these systems that use playing cards, so we may just not see eye to eye on that one.

    Overall nice job for two days' work. I might borrow your Tropes for my next Lasers & Feelings game.

    \n Thanks a lot for your feedback Riovanes.

    I definitely see where you are coming from with both your points. I agree that there may be some difficulty in using the witty Sidekick Trope when you are new to the group. It is an angle I have never really considered when designing the game. I will have to keep that in mind for my next one.

    Your comment about the abilities is a rather pertinent one as well. Especially with the competitive nature of the game. I do agree with the mortal coil method of solving it, another option though would be to limit abilities to choosing from a list, and just generate a more expansive list.

    Thanks again for your response, Every comment and critique helps me improve my future designs.
