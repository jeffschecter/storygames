    \n Does it really say that? I've got a perfect bound copy of ReCoil from Lulu, and it's 36 pages (numbered pages, not sheets) with a wrap-around cover.
    \n
    \nI've also got a saddle-stitched copy of Rats in the Walls (read: stapled) at 24 numbered pages that looks pretty decent.
    \n
    \nOne thing I've noticed, make sure if you do the wider border for the inside edge of the page (recommended for perfect bound) that it's the correct pages. I didn't do it right in ReCoil, and if you are looking for it, it's pretty obvious, especially because I've got a light background image beneath the text area.
    \n
    \nI guarantee you that unless you're doing Ashcan copies, you'll want to order a proof copy to be sure, because even if you love the binding, if it's your first time setting up a print book, you've probably muffed something else up.
    \n
    \nAs my games are still in late alpha testing, they are mostly vanity copies, and they're there just in case an opportunity to playtest comes around. It's much better to pull out a hardcopy of a book when you're volunteering to run something than pulling your laptop out. I'm glad I did them though, because it feels good to have them, and it helps me to refine what needs to happen for retail versions down the line.

    \n I've had a couple of things done saddle stitched (stapled), eg QMR - Book of Bewildering Beasts at 32 pages IIRC, and they came out OK.

    The main thing I learned from doing it was that going for a smaller page size and therefore more pages would have resulted in a much more pleasing physical artifact.

    Do check out the Lulu prices for different sizes in different countries, because since the recent changes there's a lot of variation.
