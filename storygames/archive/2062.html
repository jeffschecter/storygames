So, I was thinkin' about why SotC's Aspect system bugs me, and I dunno for sure what it is, but here's an idea for what I'd do if I wanted to make it into a more Shreyas-friendly game.

The GM shall not be a tyrant. All that noise about "You can use an Aspect if the GM says it's okay" goes out the window - I trust the people I play with utterly to use their things in cool ways. No one has the ability to block them.

We dump the compelling system as well.

The GM has no ability to make mechanical challenges for free, or maybe he can make them require a +0 result at most. For anything further, he has to invoke character Aspects, or Aspects that the players have assigned to other entities. For each Aspect he uses in a scene, any player may award him with a Strife coin (of which there is an unlimited supply); he can only recieve one coin per Aspect. (That's to say, we trust the GM too; he gets coins based on the least demanding player.) A Strife coin can buy an Aspect for the GM, or +2 to a skill, or a stunt, they he can apply to some entity, or +2 difficulty to some one-time challenge.

If he invokes a player Aspect to directly create adversity, then the player gets a Fate point for that. If he uses it for just kinda fun happy time, then there's no Fate point involved.
