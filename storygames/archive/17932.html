    \n I'm overselling the difference, probably.

    It seemed like, from my experience of KublaCon, Big Bad Con, an Endgame Mini-con, and talking to folks in the Bay Area, there was a lot more emphasis on GM prep and GM performance. I heard games being praised often as being "really well run," whereas at Go Play NW or Gamestorm it seems like I'd be more likely to hear a game being praised as having "an all-star group."

    There are GM crews/labels/banners/brands that run games in California, like Good Omens. Maybe it's actually a San Francisco thing, and not a California-wide thing. I'm not sure. But it struck me as weird, and indicative of a different play culture.

    (Not to suggest that there is a single monolithic play culture within each region. Just noting some brush strokes of difference I've seen between the two.)

    The big SoCal conventions these days are run by Strategicon: OrcCon in February, Gamex in May, and Gateway in September. There's the RPGA, and then everything else. We have a pretty healthy small press/indie game offering that I'd like to think is inclusive, newbie-friendly, safe, etc. In all fairness, though, I do tend to play with the same 15 or so people at all three cons, so my own perspective may be a bit skewed.

    The past couple years, the Happy Jack's podcast has turned its Sauron-like eye on Strategicon, which has meant a fresh influx of games and gamers. Most of the former are trad/old-school, but many of the latter have taken a keen interest in our wacky storygames and whatnot, so that's been good.
