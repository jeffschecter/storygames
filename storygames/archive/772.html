Well-known board games as RPGs

Graham
May 2006 edited May 2006 in Story Games
It's just a stupid idea. But I wonder whether you could do a Scrabble-based RPG?

You'd have to set it in an appropriately literary setting: say, the PCs are investigators, who trawl through mounds of files to build a case. Perhaps they're reporters (in the manner of All The President's Men) or spies (John Le Carre novels).

Then, when the PCs want to search for something in a file, they put down a word on the board. They must use that word in narrating what they find in a file.

Then the next player puts down a word and they use that word to describe where the paper trail leads next.

Could you use Monopoly in an RPG? What about Snakes and Ladders? Would some games be more suited than others?

    You could use Snakes and Ladders (along with a billion other games) as an abstracted success mechanic. Set an overarching situation - a bunch of kids trying to get into dance school, with the aim of winning the dance-off against Crazylegs Pavlova, the greatest dancer the world has ever seen. Then every time you go up a ladder, narrate a big step forward, ever time you go down a snake, a crashing defeat. If two events happen successively (I go up a ladder, then you go down) then the two are tied - my victory caused you to fail. When someone gets to the end, the game could end, or it means you achieve your explicitly described goal (you can tell I've been playing Capes...) or cause another person's to never come to be, and then start at the bottom again.
    You could do similar things with Sorry!, and Backgammon, but the feel would be slightly different - for example, when you double up counters this could represent reaching a point of safety, and breaking one up represents a gamble.

    Does anybody actually know how to play Mousetrap? All we ever did was set up the contraption and then watch it go off, over and over again. We might have played once. If I recall correctly, it was pretty much roll-and-move, with some cheese tokens for scoring.

    My little group-game-design blog toyed with a couple of ideas for games based on Rube Goldberg devices, which is the real thrill of Mousetrap, but neither got off the ground. Maybe a Mousetrap RPG could cast you as two teams of Tinker Gnome-like mice, trying haphazardly to modify a giant machine to capture or crush members of the opposition. Lots of dice tables telling you what your modified part was going to do next, and how it affected the functionality of the machine as a whole (based on the checksum of the components). Big repairs or alterations would require you to move into more dangerous areas, but also let you make bigger shifts on the functionality table. Choose among the classes of Mechanic, Saboteur and Walking Sacrifice. Game over when the doomsday clock summons the cat.

    Hmm.

    Solitare. Standard bog-simple solitare with 7 columns of 1 2 3 4 5 6 and 7 cards each. Remaining cards in a deck.

    You have a conflict in the story, you get the deck and draw three-down into the deck and try to play your card. If you can, you succeed, if you can't, you're done.

    If you can, and then that play results in cascading options of further "moves" on the Solitaire "board", each additional move you can manage is a stage-up on the level of success.
