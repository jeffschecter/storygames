\n (Game Chef 2012)

There was a lot to see, and you may have missed something awesome when you're looking at just a little. Let me share stuff that stuck on my mind.

Firstly, Jonathan Lavallee has been attempting capsule reviews of every game over on Gamish Designer. Wow! Thanks for your generosity, Jon.

Snowy Mountain Syndrome is a remarkably complete game, with ready-to-play characters and situation, intricate currencies to track your state, and the great old premise that you and the rest of the settlers are not going to make it out of there. There's lots to look at (and steal) from this game, especially as more people look for designing ready-to-play situations into the game.

Handle With Care is a really touching and charming LARP idea involving monsters. It's a joy to read the text.

Into the Void [fixed] was a promising entry into the space madness genre, and like it's approach to the creep of insanity/disaster while the crew of the Lantern moves along to its destination.

This Match is Scheduled for One Fall has some smart stuff about pro-wrestling, but also hidden goals, interlocking mechanics between players, and splitting a character into multiple playable personas. And wrestling!

    \n Kira Scott's Into the Void: This is a "space madness" game that does some interesting things with traits that migrate on and off the character's sheet which are used to inspire the GM to come up with horrifying/hallucinatory imagery. I'm not sure I got everything in the game, but I liked that there was mechanical support for trying to make things scary and personal rather than just asking the GM's instincts to carry the entire load.

    Zachary Donovan's Young Turks at the Cafe: The "comfortable people discuss politics" premise of this game was not something I expected, but I thought there was a cool and subtle thing going on with the "put this question in the void" mechanic that makes the game more about discussion than about bickering or debate.

    \n Since I'm one of the few folks that actually read them all I know I have a couple that I think deserve extra special mention.

    Seriously, Big Chiefs is something that Liam is going to have to make into a full game. I'll help if need be because ... damn I couldn't stop laughing.
    Farewell, My Dear Post Apocalypse looks enjoyable even with the fiddly bits that usually drive me nuts.
    The fact that Sweating Seven took Battleship and made it entertaining again.
    Heist captured the feel of a bank job, it just needed a little cleaning up.
    Like Moths was a lot of fun in concept as well in execution, playing Moreauvian monsters trying to get to the light first.

    I'd go through more but my eyes are a little blurry when it comes to these games. Read the reviews, pick something you like, mention it here and stuff. We can have that "conversation" thing.
