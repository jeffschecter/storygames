\n Look at the thread [Your Game] Update Us Again and say what interests you.

I'm very much looking forward to Business Solutions. It fills a much-needed gap in the market for games about photocopier repair salesman. Oh, sure, it's been done in D20, but not well.

Nice to see The Committee for the Exploration of Mysteries up there. I've always had a thing about that game. I read Around The World In 80 Days recently and it reminded me of this game.

I'm vaguely intrigued by How Can You Say You Love Her If You Can't Even Eat Her Poop?, if only because it clearly comes from the same school of titling as The Gay Recruitment Squad Wants YOU!.

I've barely heard anything about Sufficiently Advanced, but I like the Arthur C Clarke quote, so if it's what I think it is, it'll be great.

Graham

P.S. If we don't mention your game in this thread, that does not mean we don't love you. Probably we feel threatened by your genius.

    Posted By: Epidiah RavacholThis weekend I had the opportunity to play in Robert'sMisspent Youth(which is in that thread) and Ben'sAdventures in the Land of a 1,000 Kings(which might be in the thread but I missed it). And I haven't stoped talking about them since.
    \nMisspent Youth impressed the hell out of me. I would play a campaign of Misspent Youth. And I rarely ever play campaigns. We accomplished more in 2 hours than I have seen in most 4-5 hour games. This was a fine example of what Ron refers to as Story Now. It's not this will be cool. Or that was cool. THIS IS COOL! RIGHT NOW! The resolution system captures the impulsiveness of the teenagers in the fiction and the built in assumptions and mechanics makes teamwork very rewarding. And this was an alpha playtest! I can't wait to play the next version!
