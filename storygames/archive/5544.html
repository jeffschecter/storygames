    Mouse Guard is about the mice staving off the tide of ruin within the Mouse Territories. In this game you'll play Saxon, Lieam, Sadie and Kenzie traveling about and getting into trouble. There are two resources - Food and Axes that the mice collect and manage. Players read off scenarios to one another where the current player must make a decision as to what sorts of tasks the mice must undertake. Success leads to rewards within the game and failure leads to dire consequences. When it's all said and done you look at the board and if the group has successfully kept the Mouse Territories safe then the group wins. It's easy to let ruin get the better of the mice, especially for those that are trying to get ahead at the expense of their responsibilities.

    Mouse Guard is a semi-cooperative game. The players are rewarded for working together toward common goals. You learn how to better play the game and in time can attempt to go for a rare individual win. Keep in mind, though, that even if you are winning, if the group is not succeeding as a whole you can't succeed either.

    Only a quarter of the game's 60 challenges are ever in play with two different options per challenge being spread among 2 to 4 players. The individual stories are repeated with different tasks and rewards to avoid players memorizing the cards. At an hour and a half play time you can expect to get hundreds of hours out of this game. Hopefully just in time for any expansions

    Are you interested in sitting at a table to play Mouse Guard before it's GenCon release? You can find us at several gaming conventions throughout the year.

    I will update this list periodically with dates and links but thus far it looks like our tentative schedule will be:

    GAMA Trade Show - April 20 - 23 in Las Vegas, NV
    Origins Game Fair- June 26 - 29 in Columbus, OH
    GenCon - August 14 - 17 in Indianapolis, IN
    DexCon - July in Woodbridge, NJ
    UberCon - in NJ
    Philcon - in Philadelphia

    We'll also have it at major comic conventions in NYC, Philadelphia and Baltimore.

    David Petersen's BlogIn preparing for the Mouse Guard Role Playing game, I started drawing pages of small mice in different cloaks and with different weapons or representing different trades. Some of the excersice was for me to quickly nail down some design ideas of mice that have been mentioned, but never seen. The other goal was to push the idea of how many lifestyles, roles, etc make up the Guard and support the Guard's work. Role Playing isn't about who has better stats or just snicker-snacking hundreds of foes...it's about character.
