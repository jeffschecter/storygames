\n So... this is more of a general topic than a specific question. I want to talk about RPGs played with large groups, particularly in classrooms etc. Suggestions, experience, game references, ideas etc are very welcome.

One Norwegian game designer has played RPGs in his class. About 20-30 students controlled a party of PCs (so there were probably around 4-5 students pr. PC). Good team decision training, I believe.

I'll be trying out a game idea for about 20 people in a few weeks. GM-less, distributed sort of thing. It's based on players picking interaction cards (from a stack) that instruct them to play out a particular type of scene - for instance, "Play out a romantic scene that ends in a jealous fight". They then go find another player to interact with, play out the scene, and get a new card. When the stack of cards is empty, the game is over. The stack can, of course, be programmed to make a semi-coherent story. I'll be combining this with a reward system where players get tokens from other players for good play.
