\n Hey Everyone,

Now that all my initial reviews are in, it's time to get serious about the 2010 Game Chef Playoffs.

Out of 59 solid games that were submitted to Game Chef 2010, I've gone through and narrowed them down into 24 very diverse Finalists. These Finalists are listed here, along with the guidelines for the playoffs, a current record of each game's "playoff score," and (where the designers have submitted them) links to supplemental material and ongoing discussions about their games. Basically, whichever game sees the most play by the most distinct groups of people between Nov 5th and Dec 15th will win Game Chef.

Please consider logging your vote by playing one or two of the 24 Finalists by Dec 15th. You get to help a game win just by playing something that catches your eye!

If you would like more information on the Finalists and other submitted games, my reviews of all of them are up here:

Designers! IF YOU ARE A FINALIST and have not yet submitted a new, shorter description of your game and links to updated rules or supplemental materials, PLEASE DO SO IMMEDIATELY so that the folks running through the list of Finalists have everything they need to choose between different games to play.

Thanks, and best of luck to everyone in the Playoffs!
Master Chef Jonathan

    \n I don't know, Tomas, I see 17 discrete sessions of play happening across 8 different games, which is definitely more than has happened for Game Chef drafts in recent years. Several of the other games were playtested during the design process so, in general, I think there's been more play this year, overall.

    I'm pretty excited by this initial effort and hope, with more preparation and experience, we can build on this going forward. And I'm still planning on playing a couple of these before Dec 15.
