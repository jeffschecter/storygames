    \n Well listen to it first and let me know if you still feel the same way.

    My current position is this: there are many different forms a podcast can take currently and those forms appeal to different audiences to some degree. Personally, as a consumer, they all appeal to me in different ways. I like short, tight shows and I like long, expansive ones.

    I deliberately choose to use the long form in our interviews for many, many reasons which I won't get into right here. I'm no enemy of editing and cutting, but an interview is not the same as a book or game and sometimes it's best to leave it mostly as is, with editing purely for sound and clarity. In a world where everything has to be short and tight, I am grateful for the opportunity the podcast medium gives us to really allow an interview to breathe and good deep and long.

    However, I do seem to be getting a bit of pressure to go short. My question is (given the very minimal feedback I get in general), is this just a proportion of the audience who prefers the short-form or a general complaint from the majority?
