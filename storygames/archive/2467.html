CoC has "Investigator," other games have them as well (but they slip my mind): Characterful alternates to "Player Character."

In Thought Disorder, my submission for the Biblioddysey Design Challenge, players have to choose one of these for their character, called their Idiom.
Problem is, I am having trouble coming up with a decent list of examples. Genre is generally unimportant though modern and near-modern is prefered (the setting is somewhat reliably portrayed as the 1920's.) The characters are schitzophrenic, but I hesitate to portray them as that radically seperated from reality.

Any suggestions for replacements for the heading that usually reads "Character Name"?

    The top of every character sheet has a line. Usually this is labeled "Character Name." In CoC, it is instead "Investigator Name," in other games it could be "Agent Name" or "Sorceror Name" or "Hunter Name," depending on the game.
    In Thought Disorder, it is labled _______ Name. The player chooses what to fill that blank with. The character could have "Officer Name" if he was/believed himself to be a police officer. "Templar Name" if he was/believed himself to be a Knight Templar. "Victim Name" if he had a persecution complex. This choice is meant to be open, but I would like to give a fairly substatial list of examples for inspiration.
    This choice is somewhat important in the game, as it defines the game that the patient is playing. At the core, the game is about reconsiling reality and other delusions with the idiom that the patient is living in.
