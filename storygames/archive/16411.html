    Posted By: WPTunesMatteo, I just read the English translation, and I must tell you that I will never play your game. I can't really expand on that, but trust me that it's a compliment.
    \nThank you, I believe you.
    You're not the first person that says that they would never play the game, and considers that a compliment; I take it as that Ninety Minutes' mechanics address in a right way the premise of the game (or, well, at least they seem to do it, given that it has never been playtested yet, not even by me!).
    _
    Among the games that I've read, I really liked Aokigahara, by Julia Ellingboe, too: it has a gripping premise, and some interesting mechanics. I also enjoyed (well, "ejoyed" isn't probably the right word) reading Liam Burke's Big Chiefs, and - from the Italian section - I liked most Ultimo viene il coyote, by Daniele Di Rubbo, and L'ombra del cambiamento, by Luca Ricci.
    I've also seen some titles that caught my attention but I haven't managed to read yet, like Iacopo Frigerio's Precious, Coyotesong by Joel P. Shempert, or The Simurg, by Chris Edwards. I'm happy of having been part of this year's edition of the Game Chef.

    Anyway, thank you everybody for the congratulations!
    I must set aside one special big thank you for Giulia Barbano, who managed to write an awesome translation of Novanta minuti in such a brief time span.
    And to Giulia again, Mario Bolzoni and Paul Czege, who all worked for the Italian edition of the Game Chef (and to Raffale Manzo too, who initially volounteered to translate the Italian finalists).
    And, obviously, to Jonathan Walton and Mike Holmes, for the contest itself. Thank you all.
