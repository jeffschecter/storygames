\n So I'm at K-mart last Thursday, my third stop trying to find a loaf pan (long story), and I end up in the office supplies aisle looking at a tablet of graph paper. And I'm overcome with a quite powerful desire to design a dungeon (something I haven't done in more than twenty years).

And today almost a week later I still haven't kicked the compulsion.

So one link leads to another and I find myself really interested in a game called Tomb Reavers. It apparently used to be available for free download here:

http://www.dreamingmerchant.com/tombreavers/downloads.html

But the link is broken and the Wayback Machine has nothing but excerpts and promo text:

http://web.archive.org/web/20041011112919/http://www.dreamingmerchant.com/tombintro.html

Searches and forum conversation definitely suggest the author had the game up for free download:

http://forum.rpg.net/archive/index.php/t-297092.html

Anyone happen to have the files?

Thanks,

Paul

    \n Hey Paul,

    I'm no real help either. I don't have those files.

    But I am gonna slide the Redbox Hack under your nose again and be all nudge-nudge, wink-wink at you. Although I haven't actually done it yet, I'm about 99% sure that the hack can handle dungeons. Traps and all. There's no proper support for it yet, but read between the lines in the non-combat chapter. What's the danger of trying to get through the pendulum trap? What's the danger of letting the DarkUnderOwlBear-a-Corn out of it's cage?

    I think it's totally possible and even potentially awesome. So, I dare you to draw up your dungeon and then read over the hack again, asking yourself if there's anything in your dungeon that the hack wouldn't support. I double-blinkdog-dare you.
