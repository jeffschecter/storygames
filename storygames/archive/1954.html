Good Die and Bad Die

Bryan
December 2006 edited December 2006 in Story Games
I don't remember where I saw this, but someone had a Star Wars Jedi game for young kids that used two dice for resolution. A Good Side of the Force die and a Darkside of the Force die.

I was thinking about running a simple game like that for my nieces (9 and 11) at Christmas, and wanted their characters to slowly turn evil over the game, so I'm toying with the following idea. It has to stay simple, though, because they loved InSpectres Start-up edition, but got bored with the full version.

First, we group brainstorm a setting and a situation and the characters - some kind of haunted house or something. The characters get three traits: mind, body, spirit. The traits are rated at 3, plus they get 3 points to spend on improving the traits as they wish up to five max. I was thinking of a simple two word description system for the characters also. Each word is rated like a trait. Maybe, powers or something like that. Maybe stats aren't needed?

Then the resolution system works like this. State what the character is doing. Character actually does this, like in Wushu. And what they want to happen and what they don't. Roll tells us how close we are to resolving the deal-e-o. Roll two dice. One black. One white. The die that is the closest to the stat used but still equal or under is the winner. With ties going to the white.

The dice that are used is a d6 for white. And the black one starts out as a d20, but each time the black die wins, they have to change a die size of either black or white, down or up, respectively.

The kids describe what happens in the scene and their character according to which die won. Black = Bad. White = Good. Or if the white die loses, the opposite of what they wanted to happen happens. So as the black die gets smaller and the white gets larger, the chance of the what they don't want to happen happens, so maybe they'll start having their character do bad things to get the good things to happen.
