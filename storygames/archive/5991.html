\n Straw Poll Time!

Would you enjoy a game -- would you be engaged, would you think it fair, would you dive in -- if you were rewarded with more character stats if you drew your character? In other words, "Draw your guy and get 10 character points." Quality of the drawing aside, you do it, you get points. Does your answer change if you have to share that drawing with the table on a regular basis throughout play?

Now, one step further. Would you enjoy a game -- would you be engaged, would you think it far, would you dive in -- if the GM was rewarded with more NPC stats and more adversarial NPCs if she drew the monsters, the dungeons, the beleaguered townsfolk? In other words, "Draw the cave mouth and get guards at the cave mouth." Quality of drawing aside, she does it, she gets points. Does your answer change if she (or you, GMing) have to share those drawings with the table throughout play?

    \nHere's how I would dig drawing in an RPG: if someone said "Let's do something that's kind of like an RPG, but where character creation and the resolution mechanics are all Pictionary-based."

    Like, you make a character by drawing it. You're not allowed to say anything, and you're not allowed to put words in the drawing. Then, you show your drawing to everyone else, and they all guess what it's supposed to be. But here's where it differs from Pictionary: it doesn't matter what you were trying to draw, all you get to do is pick one (or more) of their guesses and that's what your character is.

    Then you do typical RPG stuff for a while, framing scenes, describing actions, etc. until a conflict arises... or at least until someone decides a conflict arises.

    As soon as someone yells "conflict!" and points at you, you immediately shut up and start drawing. If they point at you and someone else, you both start drawing. Since there's no discussion allowed after "conflict!", your drawing needs to covey both what you think the conflict is about and how you're trying to resolve it. Then you show your drawing(s) to the group and again, pick someone's guess and that's what happens.

    With the right group, I can see getting a couple solid hours' worth of beer and pretzels fun out of a game like that.
