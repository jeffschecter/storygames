\n Some folks running a small game convention one town over from me in late March have invited me to run a one-shot. Most of them are D&D and L5R enthusiasts with some LARP experience, and a fair amount of natural curiosity. I'd like to run a game with the following properties:

- enough number-crunching and a randomizing element (dice, cards, whatever) to placate trad RPG-ers
- a reasonably recognizable genre setting, so that it doesn't come across as too perverse or "artsy"
- light (or scaleable) enough rules that I won't have to spend too much time explaining stuff
- enough novelty value that they'll get at least a taste of the difference between storytelling games and the sort stuff they're used to
- suitable for entirely self-contained single games, rather than something that feels like it ought to be part of a larger campaign.

Suggestions, anyone?

    \n *If you're familiar with FATE, that might be the way to go. Spirit of the Century, default setting is pulp, rules hit the mid point between too simple and complex, player input takes some load off the GM, not need for extensive prep. Just lower the number of Aspects required (10 is too many, especially for first time players and an one-shot game), and possibly lower the Stress threshold.

    *In A Wicked Age - communal setting/story creation, no prep required, very simple rules that still offer a decent degree of variability, very easy to just pick up and play (if the GM knows the rules beforehand). Although the game only starts to blossom at the third session.

    *InSpectres is pretty good, as mentioned.

    *What else...Oh, oh, oh...Danger Patrol. http://www.dangerpatrol.com/ Can't go wrong with this one I think.

    *Or maybe Lady Blackbird: http://www.onesevendesign.com/ladyblackbird/
