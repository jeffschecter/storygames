    \n I think it was that I made a town that was RIFE with sin; all the sins were understandable, human sins; it would be tempting for the Dogs to just shoot 'em all and let the King of Life sort them out; but solving the problem that way would likely just encourage it to happen again.

    Basically, the town had just overthrown their leadership and had set up a democratic form of government (pride leading to sins of stewardship). However, the overthrow was assisted by the Steward, who was a former Dog himself: he said "we used to overthrow government all the time back when I was a Dog, so it must be right." Therefore, when the players showed up and found out about the stewardship issue (as well as the adultery and apostasy that were spin-off problems) they had to think long and hard before shooting anybody.

    I guess the magic of it was that the badness was really subtle, but really "bad" as per the dogma that had been established.
