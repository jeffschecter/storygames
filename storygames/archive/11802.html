    \n Hi Moreno. There are 2 editions of Nine Worlds. I released the second edition in 2005, and called it the "Aristeia" edition. Both editions existed in print and PDF, as I recall.

    Aristeia edition included rewritten rules section with several rules changes and simpler language. I think it also included an expanded Survey of the Nine Worlds with more NPCs. I don't recommend using the first edition.

    Dust Devils will not be released for free. In fact, I'm gearing up to offer it for sale again soon.

    \n Wanna hear my blasphemy?

    I can't get too much into the world of Nine Worlds. I just can't get my head too far around it.

    However, I think the system, as it stands, is the perfect system for playing a game set in the Exalted: Sidereals setting. No rules hacking, etc, required: Simply substitute one setting for another wholesale. The themes, power levels, "epicness" and drama seem to line up exactly 1 for 1.

    So if you can't get behind the setting, but you can get behind the setting of Exalted (namely Sidereals, not so much the others) but don't like the rules behind Sidereals, you might also find some chocolate to rub peanut butter on.

    \n I guess it'd be Arete and Hubris, Matt. The Sidereals are unique among the Exalted in that they basically work for the gods, in salaried positions no less. So it's not really stretching things to interpret their role as being linchpins that determine whether the divine order continues or gets replaced by some alternative. There's even a sort of cosmology for doing things according to the Fate and Destiny or against them - you might well say that Arete is doing things in accord with the celestial bureaucracy, while Hubris is going rogue and doing things your own way, without asking for permission.

    \n Pretty much what Eero said. Natural order (letting things go as they are) vs Imposed order (pushing your own reality, changing fate).

    In Sidereals, the many of the "uber powers" are magical and fate-changing (in WoD terms, many powers allow you "lower the difficulty level" which is normally an unchangeable number set at "7" for anyone else).

    But rulewise, they're totally the same: Do you use physical, natural, kung fu abilities (Arete), or do you use tricky magic, fate-bending, change-the-order-of-things ("charm") abilities (Hubris). No conversion needed, not even in name.

    But note, the above is why I think Sidereals is a great setting mod for 9W, and not just "Exalted" or any other Exalted splat (Abyssals, Dragon-Blooded), "Star Wars Jedis!", etc.

    -Andy
