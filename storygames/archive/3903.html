    \n Mister Bohl, I'd be happy to talk about it.

    Quick Concept: The Scourge, ancient enemy of humankind, have returned at last, and if no one stops them, they'll surely doom the human race to annihiliation. It's up to you, a starship captain, to do something about it. What are you prepared to do?

    Gameplay: There's a GM and players. Each player is both the captain of a ship and a crewmember on everyone else's ship. So unlike Primetime Adventures where your character's spotlight time changes, this game gives you both spotlight and non-spotlight characters. So maybe Andy is playing Mal Reynolds, but he's also playing Chewie to someone else's Han Solo.

    To complete the game, a captain has to finish three quests and a finale. You get to set up your own quests (sort of like kickers in Sorcerer) and contribute things to other people's quests. The GM's job is similar to the Producer's in Primetime Adventures.

    Mechanical stuff: The idea for this game, mechanics-wise, is to provide both indisputable facts while also providing interesting cues for narration. In Primetime Adventures, for example, the game doesn't tell you whether you succeeded because of an edge or connection. In Galactic, you know whether or not the Space Pirate Veronica bailed you out in the nick of time.

    There's a few layers of potential consequences worked into the system as well. In order for your captain to succeed in the long term, you may need to accept losses in the short term. Those losses include relationships and more. Maybe this victory will require Wash to be a leaf on the wind. Maybe you have to let the city fall in order to save the planet. The trouble is, you'll never know when you have to make that choice.

    Philosophy: The game tries to do something uncomfortable with the idea of human beings as resources. I think Trollbabe does a lot with that, but I wanted to take it further. Hopefully you'll be wondering things like, "can I succeed without having to risk this crewmember's life?" or "what if I risk this crewmember's life and fail anyway?" Not every moment will be like that, but for sure one or two per game will be nail biters.

    Setting: It's a slightly grungy, post-apocalyptic space opera/fantasy. Human beings have crawled up out of a dark age, unsure of their origins (Earth? What's that?), nosing around a ruined galaxy filled with grouchy aliens and ancient and deadly things.
