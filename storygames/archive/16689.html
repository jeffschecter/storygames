Because it was inevitable, I'm creating a master thread for all the post Vanilla 2 upgrade concerns, questions, comments and stuff.

Know that there's still a lot of change coming with the forum structure (not to mention the new social rules we're looking into), including:

* "Ignore" feature
* Discussion or Comment "+1"s (currently discussing)
* Another theme, something variable width so that it's not just a small column when you have a big-ass monitor
* Eventual thoughts of folding Praxis back into S-G (this is going to be more than just moving it over, there's a lot of concerns and talk that'll have to be done).
* A better way to see if you're being Whispered to. Currently there's only a little thing that says "Private Between: [You], [Other Person's Name]" in whispered posts. Could be easy to miss.
* Possibly a "Who's Logged In Now" thing
* Removing or editing the weird gender questionnaire in your account info (its a default thing, don't understand why it's there)
...etc

For now, get used to the new features of the site, the organization etc. There's all sorts of stuff like
* Auto-saved draft posts
* Reporting posts for their content
* Mentioning others like: @jenskot
* Account notification changes
* Getting notifications for threads you create, or that you bookmark

And... well, enjoy!

This was 97% brought to you by user @kobutsu , aka Kit La Touche. Seriously, he had the time, energy and skill to make this upgrade as painless as it ever could have been. Serious kudos to him!

    \n Stuff I miss (or am just missing how to do in the new software?):

    - It used to be ridiculously easy to quote just a section of someone else's post; you'd highlight the part you wanted, hit "Quote," and the highlighted section was the only part that got quoted. That functionality seems to be lost.

    - Also, quoted text really needs to have the name of the person you're quoting attached to it and be visibly different from non-quoted text. Right now, all you get is indented text (which is hard to distinguish from the rest of the post) with no immediate way of seeing who originally wrote it, and that's pretty awful.

    - Posts in a thread used to be numbered, which made it easy to make a link directly to a specific post (or even just to say "look at post #8").

    - Is there a faster/better way to whisper to someone than having to type in their username? Something like the old "Whisper" link on the posts of yore?

    - The star icon to bookmark threads sits on top of long thread titles, obscuring them; it'd look better if it was off to the side instead.

    - I'm also looking forward to different themes (especially ones that are more readable and make better use of widescreen monitors).

    \n Peter: Clumsy, but I figured it out.

    Take your string: Dragon World.
    Then use quotes: "Dragon World". This is normally enough for Google. But since this new search engine goes directly using MySQL queries, you have to do this: Add a + in front of the string.

    So, search for +"Dragon World"

    Again that full string includes a plus sign and quotation marks:

    +"Dragon World"

    It will take a bit to get used to, I'll create a help thread later on this.

    \n Heya: Lots of good comments! Here's a few responses:

    @zircher : Yeah, over the next few days/weeks you'll see features disappear and reappear. I need to do testing, especially as bugs appear. I'll also be testing themes indiscriminately.

    @jenskot : We're almost exactly on the same wavelength in regards to styles. I took up @jjafuller on his offer and he's slowly looking into a design based on some specs. Well, mostly the color scheme of the old site. Actually, lots of things from the old site. :-)
    Namely:
    Displaying "Thread started by"
    NOT colorizing threads you start and posts you make.
    Colorizing threads you haven't read or have a new post.
    Colorizing whispers so that you can see them.
    I like the "lightly coloring every other post. That is extremely sensible, or at least worth a try. @jjafuller , add that to the plan!
    Removing the "New" icon. Seriously so not needed, especially with proper coloring.

    @jjafuller : I missed your bit about the icons on the mobile design. If you want to edit that to names, LMK. I'm behind that, especially when I use the mobile site and I have to scroll through two inches of SERPENTOR each time.

    David: I'd be up for individuals who are interested in the "hover to see full name" thing to, once we eventually come to a set theme, create a new theme to pull that information. However, for now you'll just have to click on their userid... and hope they added their first and last name to their profile page.

    =-=-=-=-=-=-=-=-=-=-=-=-=

    First and Last Names
    So, unfortunately in the upgrade, that information didn't carry over. It was important for Van1, but the developers didn't see the need for it in Van2: So everyone's first and last name info is gone.
    However, there was a First/Last Name info plugin which I added and turned on. So new applicants will supply their name. And existing members can edit their profile to add their first and last name (and later I'll make a call-out thread requesting they do so). But until folks add that info, it'll be blank.

    Follows/Views/Comments
    I like them! Maybe not the color scheme per se, but their existence.
    Follows: Meh, I could give or take on that one. It doesn't really say anything about the nature of the thread... or does it? I'm curious to see.
    Comments: ...is duplicated in text under the thread title. I could go either way on which to remove.
    Ahh, but Views: Views was created to solve one specific problem, and it appears that it may be working.

    Ben and others have pointed out to me that Actual Play, one of the core tenets/activities of the site, doesn't have as much activity thread-wise.
    The breakdown for that, I believe, is that (outside of the "Bite Sized" thread collecting mini-reports)... there's really not much to say about Actual Play posts. "Cool"? I do that sometimes just to show that I was amused. However, they're great to read but in themselves not really conversation starters unless someone has questions usually about the game outside of your play experience ("NIce. But how do you handle the problem I've seen where X..."). Which leads people to think that no one is seeing their threads.

    I've actually seen some people leave S-G, posting elsewhere that "no one was listening to me" or the like. Sure, some of that was that they were posting stuff that didn't really call out for responses, which is a kind of bias. But in the end, there simply is not a feedback mechanism in the old forum that showed such a user that, "No, in fact about 50 people were listening to you. It's just that they agreed with you, and didn't have anything to add." We now have the technology to track that, and I think that's good.

    Now, whether it's in a blocky box on the right side, or in a text field underneath the thread title... I could go either way on that too. But I think Views is a good way to show people that You Are Being Listened To, even if no one posts a reply.

    However, there's the concern there too of people going, "Why did MY thread only generate 200 views while 500 people are looking at HIS thread?" I'll actually post about this behavior later, ESPECIALLY as we look to eventually be implementing a kind of "+1" mechanic here. And that's going to trigger all sorts of obsessive, competitive, jealousy-based lizard-brain behaviors in people. Hell, it does to me, and I try to stay aloof to that sort of thing. But hopefully we can live with it for the benefit it will bring to people who post interesting things that don't scream for response.

    -Andy
