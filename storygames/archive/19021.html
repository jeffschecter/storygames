\n I've got this idea lingering in my mind, but I'm not quite sure how to get to where I want it to be. I'm missing a few cogs entirely for this machine as well.

The outset of the idea is the impending doom of a village. Something bad is coming. And through play, you set out to discover what that bad thing is. Because the outset is vague, this is tending more to a system than a specific setting, though I'm thinking that, to get things started, I'll set out from a concrete example and see if it translates.

There might be a common or setting specific intro to set up the mood, the angst. Strange things have been happening: birds falling from the skies, geysers popping up where they weren't before, mysterious illnesses, etc. These give a prompt to start the investigations, and introduce a pretext to something Bad.

My system idea would consist of rumors. These are randomized elements that come into play, one per scene. How these take form? Right now I'm thinking of abstract doodles on cards, that could be interpreted as the player sees fit. It's about discovering the BigBadThing, and it's your BigBadThing. So you draw a couple of rumor cards, play out a scene of how you go out to investigate some trail, to find out about some rumors.

The next step is turning rumors into truths. It's a group storytelling activity, so to balance power within, I'd like to have a mechanism that when one player creates a rumor, the other players get to decide whether it's a truth or not. What's critical here, is that you can't go about denying every rumor: I need a pacing mechanism. There has to be pressure to find out about the BigBadThing, cause it's coming, and the sooner you know, the sooner you could act. Then again, I don't want players to game the system and accept everything for a sure win. That being said, the game is about discovery, not about beating a big monster (though it could be); but you might as well all perish in the end.

On the one hand, the BigBadThing will be very much on the foreground of the tale. It drives all other characters in a sense. But it shouldn't be centre stage. Each character will have a different motivation for going after it, perhaps even conflicting. The BBT will change the characters. Perhaps the quiet fisherman wants revenge above all, whilst another character needs the BBT alive to cure some illness, learn of his past (who said the BBT needs to be a brainless monster?),...

In order to advance, I tried to answer the big three:

1. What is your game about?
It\u2019s about the coming of doom and destruction. Everyone knows that it is coming, but no one really knows what it is. The characters each have a motivation to go after the source. As players play, they discover the true nature of coming event. Potentially, they\u2019ll be able to stop it. The game offers a structure that can be applied to different genres, but the main aspect is the same: doom is coming, weave the story of what or why and resolve your character\u2019s quest along the way.

2. What do the characters do?
The characters get riled up by recent events. They each have a motivation to go after the source of the mysterious happenings. They set out to find information, coming across snippets at a time in the form of rumors. These rumors will lead them to the truth. In the end, they will discover the BBT. They will then need to decide how to face it, if they can at all.

3. What do the players do?
Players first build a character. The character is chosen according to a role plus a motivation. Players proceed to draw rumor cards. The rumor cards give indications on what the BBT might be. Players play out scenes of how their character comes about this information, and they are free to interpret it. Scenes can play out as monologues if the characters are alone, or as dialogues if other players are present.

--

Thoughts, help, sources I should read on design, etc, all are welcome

    \n I would say: playtest it. Then you will see what you would need. You already got a spine, and the cards can be written by all the participants before you start. Be sure to decide on themes when you write the cards. What kind of rumors are you thinking of?

    I would first decide a theme for the village and create characters from that. I would probably myself not limit myself to a village, but a group of people. A guild, a family and so on.

    Are the secrets known by everybody? It's easier if people know about them. They can still be revealed under the cause of the game, but no player should hold any information because that can create blockage.

    \n Really interesting concept. I think having interesting rumors is the hardest part of what you're considering. They need to be vague but full of flavor. Players could create them on their own, but it wouldn't be very fun to interpret your own, and then you have the concern about consistency of tone. I love the idea of the players having to assemble all the rumors they hear, weed out the dross and figure out what is threatening their community. Maybe have rotating spotlight scenes to start, where three or four players play the town and interpret something for the spotlight player. That player then takes their impression and in the second phase gets together and talks about what she learned in the first phase and see where things intersect.

    I am sort of reminded of one of my favorite Game Chef entries from last year, Coyotes in Dark Alleyways. It may be instructive for you to read: http://semielgames.files.wordpress.com/2012/04/coyotes-in-dark-alleyways.pdf

    Best of luck and keep us updated!
