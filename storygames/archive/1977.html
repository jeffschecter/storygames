Your bad gaming habits

Robert_Bohl
December 2006 edited December 2006 in Story Games
What are your bad habits, as a gamer? What things do you do while gaming that later (be it moments, hours or days later) make you cringe?

I'm a loudmouth. That's probably an uncharitable way of putting it, but better to err on the side of self-deprecation. I have an effusion of what-I-consider-to-be-good ideas and I have a very hard time not sharing them. The kinds of games I'm into lately--the kinds of games which encourage input into other players' decisions--unfortunately tend to give me lots of opportunity to commit this sin.

I'm a know-it-all. I have a hard time letting something that is incorrect (or that I think is incorrect) just go by without trying to rectify the sitchumation. It may be rules, it may be a statement of fact, it may be what I consider to be a bad decision. It makes my teeth itch to see something "wrong" and not try to "right" it. That can be extremely annoying.

I am judgmental of other people. I will often feel like after a few minutes, I've got someone sussed out and drop them into a category of one kind or another. Guy's wearing a metal headband? As in a Warner Brothers cartoon where people are starving to death, his head shimmers out of existence and turns into a bear-trap before my eyes and I want to escape! My angst over this may be just a twinge of guilt as a geek cliche--the one that says that people should never be ostracized--is triggered.
