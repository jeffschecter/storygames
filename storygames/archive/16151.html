\n Today is the last day for Gen Con Indy event submission.

I want all the games I run to be kid-friendly - and for one in particular, my nephew who is my reason for going to Gen Con this year. He is 12 and has been having a really tough year. He's not been hugely into role-playing, but has had some fun with his cousins playing kid superheroes in PS238, and in a simple LARP that I ran (with pirates). One problem is that he isn't very confident in his speech - so the more you can do with simple statements, the better. He's into superheroes as well as fantasy and various machines, but not so much into animals.

I'm thinking of trying Marvel Heroic - probably with a bunch of simplifications. I also will be doing some kind of custom LARP.

I'm pondering about other indie games to try, though. My son has really loved Hellcats & Hockeysticks, but I think my nephew won't want to play a teenage girl. I might consider an H&H variant.

I'm also thinking of Monsters and Other Childish Things.

    \nHere's what I have saved on Google Docs. Still can't access my MediaFire account at work, so it'll have to do for now...
    It pares down the rules quite a bit (two pages), using the suggestions from the original game. I gave it a bit of a starting scenario, where players are Elementary School kids, friends who live in the same neighborhood that had been drawn together because of their strange, magical abilities. It includes a character sheet that has some starting questions that not only builds the relationship between the characters, but also creates some starting seeds of adventure.
    Also made cards for Gifts and Powers (basically more expensive Gifts), and resolution is pulling a certain number of dice from a bag, depending on whether they have any applicable Gifts, and trying to get at least one white die. Essence is still used as normal.

    Hoping to have some APs of it posted after running it at GameStorm this weekend.
