    In my view the design i's backward looking, nostalgic over forward-looking, and not what it could be in terms of SCRAPPING a lot of assumptions about what this potentially cool product could be.

    I say this only as a man who is obsessed with what's happening with how we get our entertainment on the Internet, watches carefully how children are growing up with the Internet, who just closed a deal with ABC to build and anthology series for them for the Internet.       I really like attacks of opportunity; they were one of the really interesting tactical dimensions of the game but all a bit too complicated for my liking. I wonder how the new system will fair.
      \nImmediate actions show up in most 3rd ed supplements, and making AoOs immediate actions would make a lot of sense.

      The 4th ed designers have said that AoOs will still exist, but be made a lot easier and better. Which sounds good: the idea that certain actions in combat are risky is a good one, but adjudicating AoOs in 3rd ed was sometimes a sticking point (and about a hundred times worse without miniatures).
