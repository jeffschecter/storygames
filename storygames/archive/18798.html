    \n ugh, i can't remember the name of the game, but in 2006 or 2007 someone put out a very short game about samurai. at the start of the game, your character sheet was a big unblemished circle on a piece of white paper. over the course of play, every compromise you make adds a mark to your sheet, until eventually you have a sheet full of blemishes that reflect the difficulty of adhering to a rigid code of honour in a world that's full of ambiguity and complexity.

    that was the best character sheet ever.

    also, each scene was comprised of seven sentences, exchanged between two players.

    does anyone remember anything about this game?

    \n I like character sheets that pack in useful information, as opposed to ones that just look pretty (though prettiness is a plus). The earliest example that comes to mind for me is Champions which included the list of maneuvers and the speed chart on the sheet - though there are probably earlier ones. Dogs in the Vineyard was an early example among modern story games. Most of the Apocalypse World games do a fair job of putting information you need on the sheet.
