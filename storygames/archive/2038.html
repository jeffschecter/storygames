Jason_Morningstar
December 2006 edited December 2006 in Play Advice
Hey friends, I'd love a little feedback on a tough nut I'm trying to crack. I'm also cracking it over at The Forge. So in Grey Ranks, you've got Missions, which are collective - everybody has a little Vignette in which they further the mission and contribute a die to a group pool. Higher is better but you have a limited set to work with. Just prior to the final player's Vignette, somebody rolls all the dice to see if the team wins or loses, overall, and the final player gets to wrap that result into his narration. These Vignettes are all talk - you can invoke anything, include anyone, do whatever within the broad context of moving the Mission forward as framed. At the end somebody (the Leader guy who framed the overall Mission) gets to single out the Weak Link and punish them, using whatever criteria he likes. So it's all a bit loosey-goosey, and that's OK (trust me). But I want to encourage a little tension between characters, and I want the Leader to have some guidance about who to pick on as Weak Link. So here's my thought:

The size of the die you contribute to the Mission pool influences the color of your narration. The higher the die size (and the more helpful), the more you have to fuck things up.

If a player contributes a d4, their Mission Vignette must include some collective triumph. Something goes right for the entire crew, and there is a noteworthy group accomplishment toward the overall Mission goal.

If a player contributes a d6, their Mission Vignette must include some personal success. Their particular moment in the spotlight should go well, and their character should achieve something worthwhile.

If a player contributes a d8, their Mission Vignette must include some personal failure. Something goes wrong and it is their fault, or some task in their care is neglected.

If a player contributes a d10 or d12, their Mission Vignette must include some disaster for the crew. Something very unpleasant happens, or something tragic, or otherwise heartbreaking.

Thus the more helpful you are mechanically, the more hardship you cause, and the more likely you'll be seen as the Weak Link. Which will encourage you to shirk. Which will make everybody hate you. So my question is - does the "your narration must include" seem tiresome, or overly restrictive, or like narrativist hand-holding? I'm too close to it to tell.

    December 2006 edited December 2006

    I have one minor tidbit I'd like to add, J. It's the order of the If/then statements connecting die sizes to narration. Right now you've got this;

    If [you want this die] then [you must narrate this]

    Which encourages players to think of the die size they want first, then attempt to construct narration to fit. Which may be just fine. But I happen to enjoy this version better;

    If [you narrated this] then [contribute this die size]

    Mechanically it's the same thing. However, it tells the players that they're free to narrate what they like then consider what they narrated and pick out the right die. Of course players will still be considering the die size they want when they construct their narration. But I just happen to think it's groovier to think of the different ways I can narrate my scene first, then consider what effect those choices may have on the game, rather than the other way around.

    I have this feeling like there might be some way to explain why the second choice is just hands-down a better way to do things than the first. But since I can't seem to articulate it, I'll just say that it's personal preference.
