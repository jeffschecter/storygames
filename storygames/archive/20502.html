    \n I am in the same situation as you are, so I have to depend on knowledge of our fellow German players. I believe Tanelorn would be the forum you are looking for (most "indie" and "story games" I have found so far).

    About story games... there are many Rollenspiele in German - two most iconic are Das Schwarze Auge (German equivalent of Dungeons and Dragons, awfully rules heavy but there are best adventures for it aviable as I was told), Splittermond (same theme, created by group of guys who worked at Das Schwarze Auge before that, should be interesting), Degenesis (available also in English, but pretty awesome), Opus Anima (really cool thing) and there are also quiete a lot translations of English story games to German.

    \n Well, story games in general in Germany is a difficult topic... as mentioned before, games like Das Schwarze Auge (which requires you to roll 3d20 for the most basic task and also know dozens of volumes of setting history) have set a different expectation.
    At least in my experience, it's not so easy to find story-minded players and groups.

    That being said, people gradually open up to Fate and PbtA, Fiasco is quite popular and we had a lot of success in our local community with "Nano Days" where we played rules-light small one-shots.

    Online, besides the mentioned Tanelorn, the German G+ Rollenspieler community is active and diverse.
    The only real German-speaking story-games community I know is story games Vienna.
    You might also want to check the web magazine http://www.teilzeithelden.de

    Good luck!
