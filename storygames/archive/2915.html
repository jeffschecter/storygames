\n This is just a little idea that I had the other day while watching Smok'n Aces. If you haven't seen the movie, it's about a bunch of assassins hired to kill the same guy. The target is trying to become an FBI witness, and the price on his head is huge. Every hit man is out to get him and stop every other hit man from getting to the dude before they can.

I thought it might be fun to play a game like that, but I'm not real sure how to do it. Here are a few thoughts that I had.

1. Build a set of different hit men and hit groups that are trying to make a hit on the same dude. And make a couple of FBI agents. The players can change characters and character groups, so that all the players are playing in every scene.

2. The dude is going to the FBI, so a time line for the fbi to catch them is ticking. Some sort of time line counts down the number of scenes until the FBI show up. This is somewhat random and the FBI might appear ahead of time. A known is that as the game goes longer, the FBI shows up.

3. These hit men will run into each other in plot scenes where they can fight or just pass each other by.

4. The hit men will position themselves through scenes to move up the ladder and get closer to the hit. If a hit man kills the mark before the final scene that hit man is the winner.

5. If the final scene occurs, all the hit men left standing and the FBI go at it.

What kind of mechanics and game would you build around this to make it work?

    \n So here's how I'd go about doing this as a card game. Each player has a basic hit man card in front of them. A target from the target deck is revealed. Each player draws from an action deck -- this deck would include complications, intel, devices, and things like that. You'd throw down complications on the target face down (this would be things like Safe House, Security System, and Bodyguards). Complications are things your hit man has to get through to get the target. Thing is, you bypass any complications you put down on the target. Play intel cards from your hand to look at any one complication card. Play devices on your hit man to defeat the complications and kill the target -- weapons do killing damage, diversions would distract guards, electronics kits get you past the security systems. There'll be multiple ways to get around complications, so you won't be stuck looking for that elusive Fire Alarm card. Then if you get through the complications, you can reach the target and kill him, gaining points. Clear all complications and reveal a new target.

    For a role-playing game, I'd use Wilderness of Mirrors. Everyone plots together, but works separately.

    \n I saw Smokin' Aces tonite. It stank.

    However, I did come-away with a way to handle the scenario mechanically.

    Rule: anytime 2 assassins are in the same location, there is a mandatory, automatic mexican standoff.

    In other words, all Player vs. Player situations are Mutually Assured Destruction. No one ever 'has initiative', or whatever you want to call it.

    When the players reach such a juncture, stop the game and negotiate the nature of the standoff-- then proceed play.

    \n Let me add a bit of complexity to my previous post's idea,
    by suggesting a third option:

    1. Characters pass each other by, without killing each other (but with all sorts of other possibilities, like how James Bond always has sex with enemy-chicks)
    2. Any attempt to kill another PC will trigger catastrophic results for the character attempting the kill.

    or

    3. Any attempt to kill another character will merely fizzle. (The gun jams; the killer mistakenly targets a reflection in a mirror, etc.)


    In play, each player would declare whether they intended to go for #1. And, if not, then either 2 or 3 would ensue.
    Chosen randomly? Or by some player(s)? I dunno.
