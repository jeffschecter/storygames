    Posted By: northerainI wonder what kind of gamethesecould be used for.
    [1] Not being schooled by a WoD LARPer who can "read" folks in Roshambo. (Back in the 90s, I used a set of three cards to do RPS, because there were so many good throwers).

    But, yeah... they have utility even beyond a "circle of success" resolution structure. Maybe as dice for a dice pool game, with each "suit" (R, P, S) representing or associating with a "stat" or "mode" of some kind (Rock = resolve, Paper = flexibility, Scissors = manipulation). To deal with a situation or task in one of the three primary "modes," you get some number of dice (equal to the stat/mode?) and try to roll over a difficulty.

    Nah, that's dull. How about the modes that are rolled dictate how you must narrate dealing with the situation, one "Fact" (per Universalis) per rolled result, with an extra die roll for rolls that match the stat used?

    Say I want to use my Resolve 3 to disregard my superior officer's orders and go rescue the fair village girl. I pick up three dice and roll: Rock, Paper, Paper. Now, because I rolled a mode that's the same as the mode that I am using (Rock/Resolve), I get to roll another die (with a Scissors result, as it turns out). So now I have to narrate how I resist my training and leave my regiment to track down the girl:
    --"First, I steel myself for the eventual court martial to come (R), realizing that I have a lot of skills that are in demand in this volatile region (P) and that I can always put aside the training and weapons to live a quiet life as a fisherman (P). But one of the privates in another division notices me and challenges me, and I had to cut him down before he could raise the alarm (S)."

    And maybe the GM or another player may roll a die for each "winning" result you got (win against your mode--the two Paper results, in the Resolve roll above). So he rolls two dice and can narrate complications into the situation--hey! Maybe that's how the other soldier notices me leaving--my opponent rolls BEFORE I get my re-rolls... and if I don't have re-rolls, I have to deal with the consequences of his narration as he states them, rather than with my re-roll of Scissors that let me cut him down or could have let me persuade him to look the other way?).

    Or something like that....

      \nFavreau (Iron Man director) says D&D helped to make him a creative filmmaker.\n
      From Link:Some filmmakers get their start making shaky home movies, others catch the bug in a high school drama class or maybe through an art institute where they put paint to canvas. Favreau has more of an eight-sided education.

      "It was Dungeons & Dragons, but I wouldn't have owned up so quickly a few years ago," Favreau said sheepishly.

      "It's rough. It's one of the few groups that even comic-book fans look down on. But it gave me a really strong background in imagination, storytelling, understanding how to create tone and a sense of balance. You're creating this modular, mythic environment where people can play in it."

      Maybe there should be a new Hollywood respect for eight- and 10-sided dice and a talent for troll tales: Robin Williams, Mike Myers, Stephen Colbert and Vin Diesel have all professed their passion (past or present) for the role-playing game.

      For Favreau, it was the fantasy element that pulled him in, but it was the sense of story that he carried with him.

      "It allowed me to not tamp down my imagination; I think there's a tendency to turn that part of you off," he said.

      "Every kid has imagination, but at a certain age, that spigot gets turned off. I set it aside in high school. I really couldn't do it now," Favreau said, shaking his head. "There's something in my heart -- there was such a stigma to it.

      "When I was young, it was exciting, but as I got older it felt like it was keeping me from progressing. You're social in your small circle, but it's asocial to the wider world."

      I haven't tried Galaxy Trucker, but have heard lots good about it. Then they made Space Alert and I got a copy from Essen. It is the most amazing 4-5 player cooperative board game I've ever played. Everyone runs around the ship dealing with Marauders and Interstellar Octopuses and Unstable Warheads and such together, under severe time constraints, and has a heart attack, and then revamps your theories about "tension" during the resolution phase. Get Space Alert and play it if you care at all about theory because the resolution phase will be very interesting. And aside from that it's the most amazing 4-5 player cooperative board game I've ever played.
