\n I've been doing a series of posts about making Agon work for longer term / campaign games instead of just one-shots:

Deeper Quests

The basic "I'm a god, here's your quest" gets old in the long run, so I've laid out a bunch of other ways to get quests started and keep things interesting.

Wrath of the Gods

A new system for tracking relationships between the heroes and the gods. Makes the results of quests more pertinent since your past deeds come back to haunt you, and it also means what you do in quests can directly affect your relationship with your patron god.

    \n To his credit, Virgil did ask for the Aeneid to be destroyed before he died (so the story goes). He intended to write a state myth making Rome the heir to history, but I think he knew he had unintentionally revealed a deep bitterness towards his own Roman culture (the unfairness of virtuous people being crushed for the sake of the master plan, etc).

    Rome didn't see anything wrong with it, which pretty much proves Virgil's point. Virgil was really too wise for his own happiness.
