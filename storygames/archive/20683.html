    \n "Systems" can't be copyrighted, only the words used to describe them. (Technically, the mechanics could be patented, but I doubt that's the case.)

    If you use the system, don't copy any of the language used to describe it--write again in your own words. It's common courtesy, of course, to always acknowledge the debt to the original designer, and many folks like to first reach out to the designer to discuss the derivative project, but that's not legally required.

    Edited: oh right, I'm thinking within the US context. YMMV in other jurisdictions.
