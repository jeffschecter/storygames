\n A word of explanation. Before heading overseas on a tour of duty with the military, my brother Mel charged me with taking care of his actual play podcast Virtual Play. I managed to produce a few episodes, but then something happened and *poof* the whole site went away. I swear I had nothing to do with it! To keep the flame, I created a site called Actual Replay, and I just posted the first new episode.

You can find the episode on the Internet Archive here. It consists of excerpts from interviews I conducted with Paul Tevis, Emily Care Boss, Chad Underkoffler (with Rob Donoghue putting in his oar), Ryan Macklin, Jason Morningstar, and jeeper Frederik Berg \xd8stergaard, and so it's a departure from the normal AP podcast format. But I think it's interesting in its own right, and it offers a glimpse of game designers grappling with ideas that challenge their assumptions about how games work. It also connects to an essay I'm working on on the same topic that I've submitted to the proceedings book for the "larp summit" at Wyrdcon I in June.

I should mention that if you find either the podcast or the article interesting, there's some desultory conversation you may wish to join going on at Structured Freedom, the forum Remi Treuer set up as a place to talk about these things. But any comments or feedback you'd care to offer here are cool as well.

    \nThe article and the podcast actually wind up saying two slightly different things; more precisely, they use the same information to tell two slightly different stories, one for an external and the other for an internal audience, in the sense that the article is directed toward larpers who may not have much familiarity with the whole indie RPG scene while the podcast assumes some familiarity with the indie RPG scene in general (mainly because it's excerpts of me listening to folks who are in the main not strangers talk about something that's not unfamiliar to me).

    In any event, the effect is to make the tone of the article more like, "Hey, there's something interesting going on way over there" while the podcast is more like, "Hey, there's something interesting right under your nose." Something like that, anyway.
