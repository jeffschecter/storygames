\n Know how there's a plethora of online dice rollers? My latest fave is Dice-y, a dice rolling 'bot for Google Wave. (Anyone concerned about Google Wave's ability for anyone to freely edit blips will be relieved to hear that you can "play back" a full log of changes to a wave, thus validating the results if you're concerned).

However, the prototype I need to test uses cards, not dice. Further, its use of cards is rather original, in that it doesn't easily map onto a game of Hearts or something like that.

Have you come across any websites that offer (preferably validated) persistent decks of virtual cards? Ideally it would support multiple hands, discard piles etc but if necessary I can simulate those assuming I have at least "draw" and "shuffle" functions.

Failing that, can you recommend an offline method to handle web-mediated Play-By-Post games / playtests? My fallback is to handle all card drawing myself using a real deck of cards next to the keyboard and have the players query me for draw information, but this is non-optimal as the design relies quite heavily on secrecy.
