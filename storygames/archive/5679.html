\n I've only done A-D, but despite Mu's awesomeness am unlikely to play with it soon. Anyone want to help out with the rest?

A demon who appears as a suave noble, who desires corruption and needs to take souls from the sleeping, or with a pinprick at the neck.
A spirit of hunger, an ancient connoisseur who exists only to feast on that which differentiates us from beasts.
A written warning, detailing the true name of a demon and tempting the reader to call upon it.
A clique of nobles, lounging in crystalline palaces and eating sweetmeats, emblematic of a state wallowing in arcane decadence.
A fiend who dominates the land, handing out noble titles to any witch or warlock who can entice his passions or summon a demon strong enough to take a parcel of land.
A treasure of wood, left as fair payment in the stead of a stolen child who spent too long facing north.

The letter B, a well-wrought rune, making dictionarists grateful for its inner peace in the celestial order.
A place where the community still rests for the Sabbath, to ensure that none among them are thirsty.
A great stadium, hollowed out of a black basalt monolith to accommodate death and carnage within.
Three sorcerers follow a prophecy to find a lost messiah

Cyclopean Mantischora roam in great herds.
Terrible beasts that can take any beast's shape so long as they have tasted its blood
A statue facing the sky, gazing up at the stars, a key to lost pyramids.
A warrior caste of atlantis whose era is long gone
Pampered concubines vying for the favor and appetite of their Witch-King, guarded by hateful mummies that revel in the slow deaths of their other lovers.

Human warriors ride giant worm-steeds whose chattering legs brush along the grass
A ghost held to Marr'd because a deed was left undone
the echo of a race of people whose civilization was wiped from the face of marr'd by falling stars
a tempermental dragon, lumbering, great horned juggernaut whose rider defeated great enemies
A damsel messiah who brings more and more desperate Marr'dians to her fold every day, and whose legions of warrior-priests are lead by her Apostles
The demon of a method of entertainment wiped from thepublic mind by a censorious despot
a great dragon, breathing dust and stars and forcing a foe to meet first-hand the magick that caused the lizards' extinction
one who went to forbidden places and bound terrible and haunted things to do their bidding
a page from a strange dictionary, that provides knowledge and danger at once
a dragon - a lithe-winged beast allowing its rider to travel far and wide
The spirit of a dead entity because, like so many, it has trouble letting go of the red dust world
the devilexicon, embodiment of lost knowledge, finds new hands that will tell of something about to be lost
a pack of dragons, quick, with taloned feet sharper than steel, their riders could unite many tribes under them if they chose

    \n 'E' and 'F' are a bitch, since 'E' has only one listed word and 'F' has only two! Here goes:


    Time marked by the falling of stars and the destruction of civilizations.
    The wrath of the Star Gods, dispensed upon the red globe of Marr'd.
    Ideas which have not merely died, but been killed.
    Craters, and the tattered unfinished histories of entire continents and peoples.
    Sand, the blood of the world's wounds.
    Prophecies of great deeds to come, legends of great deeds already done.
    A sniveling philosopher, concerning himself with worthless things.

    Farrows, who navigate the stars by a black magic called 'science' and a language called 'maths'.
    Mathematical glyphs, appearing on their own on nearby walls.
    Spirits crowded 'round the places they spent their lives, within the pyramid tombs.
    A roaring wind that whistles through an empty tomb.
    The hopeless folk of Marr'd, infected with dreams of leaving the dead planet in a chariot bound for the heavens, through the cold, unforgiving void.
    Someone facing angry spirits on another's behalf, checking the edge on his knife.
    A friend, who will tell you when you pass from foolery into deviltry, and will stand by you through both.


    I find myself basically pulling entire phrases verbatim from the book, because Paka's phrases are so damned evocative. When (if) the whole thing gets completed, we should get his permission to put it up on Abulafia with all the other fan oracles.
