    \n At the November Endgame minicon I set it in a dive bar at the edge of a small town, set to a Jim Steinman soundtrack, on a hot summer night under a full moon where bikers gathered to race. PCs could be bikers, or students from the high school on a dare, or the band members who swore they'd never return to that small town but circumstances... (Inspired by ideas from Brand Robins Jim Steinman RPG and Graham Walmsley's Meat Loaf LARP.)

    The evening game was set 21 years later on another hot summer night under a full moon, as the ghosts of those bikers returned to that dive bar. Both worked pretty well.

    I've also thought about setting it at a summer camp, but haven't done so yet. I'd love to try setting it at Japanese High School, but would need new Monster skins, and players familiar with the genre.

    I usually set it in small towns, but have thought about it setting it in large cities where there's still a sense of desperation to get out. I've thought about running Gossip Ghoul but haven't done so, because again it's unlikely all the players in a random pickup game at a convention will be familiar with the source material. A setting that tempts me although it would be really tricky is a small town in the mid 19th-century west, except not our world, where the Faithful have fled the corrupt and wicked east to settle in small towns, unaware that they also harbor monsters. Then 4 Chosen in colorful coats ride into town to save it... Monsterhearts in the Vineyard.

    \n I was toying with MonsterGlee with some custom moves depending on what kind of song you picked as your solo, but this one is so much more promising. Especially for anyone who feels that PCs sometimes have it too easy. I imagine the family boss as a menace with custom moves that allow him to destroy your life and put a bounty on you if he ever suspects you of being a) disloyal or b) supernatural.

    There were moments in The Wire and a lot in The Sopranos where characters were desperately hiding something (whether they had flipped or were gay) and knew they would be killed if it were ever revealed. Like high school, but with more than just your social life on the line.

    \n No, I think you need an additional backstory step a) to explain how they came to be there (have they grown up in the family? Are they a wannabe? Are they an outsider with a useful skill?) and b) to give them something that can be threatened apart from themselves (e.g. a relative, a favourite haunt, a business interest, a job). Too easy otherwise for them all to be cautious loners who care for nothing and no-one and sleep with a gun in their hand.
