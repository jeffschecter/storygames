I had a dream last night in which a group of people tried to teach me a game. The game was called Tengu. There were five or six people playing and a bunch of people around them watching. They would take turns rolling dice and making up stories from the dice. The dice were regular six sided. There were chips that were used for keeping score and betting whether the player would "hit his mark", which involves getting all of the dice accounted for in the time allotted. It went something like this. The guy at the head of the table picks up a bunch of dice (like 6 to 10 he had them cupped in his hand) he shakes them up and someone turns over an egg timer (a little one three minutes) then the guy arranges the dice. He sets them up in a line and begins to narrate. He had six minutes to make a chapter of his story. Then the next player would do the same thing. And so on around the table.

The only rule I know for sure is that the story had turn-arounds, which followed the dice. A 1-3 was bad thing that happened to the character in the story, and 4-6 was a good thing. A 1 was something worse than a 2 or 3 and a 6 was similarly better than a 4 or 5.
