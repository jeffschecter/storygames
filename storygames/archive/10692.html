\nDATE Saturday, November 7 November 14, 2009, 2 PM (DATE CHANGE)
LOCATION Alexandria, Virginia
EVENT NAME Ice Station Nerdly III
QUICK DESCRIPTION A day of games, food, and fun at my home.

Hey everyone! It's fall, so that means it's time for another Ice Station Nerdly. (Follow this link to see the last ISN thread). As before, this is an opportunity for people in the Washington, DC, Metropolitan area (and beyond, we love meeting new people!) to get together and game. And this year, I have a new and much bigger house! We have at least 4 rooms that can hold games (Spare bedroom, dining room, living room, and Gamer Central, i.e. my basement). That means we can support 20 or so gamers!

We are very playtest friendly. If you have a game that needs testing, bring it or just e-mail it to us, and we'll try and play it. We also have an Xbox 360 in the living room, tons of board games (and more than enough table space for them) and we will supply food and drink.

The festivities will start around 2PM. We'll have a 4 hour afternoon game session, break for dinner, and then another game session in the evening. That means we can have up to 8 games over the day. Please contact me if you want to run a game, and I'll start putting the schedule together.

We are still near the Huntington Metro station, so we can pick people up from there. The new house is up a privacy drive, so you'll probably have to park on the street and walk up it, so plan accordingly. I'll whisper or e-mail my address to any attendees who post here.

Feel free to bring food, friends, and fun. Hope to see a lot of you there!

P.S. -- We have two dogs, Caesar and Captain Jack, who will no doubt be SO.VERY.HAPPY to make your acquaintance. They don't bite, but they are known to shed, lick, and jump. We'll do our best to keep them under control, but Jack likes a good game (we have pictures of him trying to play Verge , and Caesar just loves to meet people. So if you have allergies or problems with dogs, be warned and please let us know. They'll probably spend a lot of time locked in a bedroom, but we'll have to let them out periodically throughout the day.
