\n So it's been established by the players that the Gunlugger of my group is a badass who keeps the Huge-Ass Bears (HAB's) from overrunning the Hardholder's holding (It's an eternal winter apocalypse). The group's been pretty consistent in showing up, but the player is going to be busy with midterms for the next couple of weeks. Is it appropriate to disable him as a front ("Shit Kobe, Barbarossa is walking in limping hard and bleeding all over the place. He won't be able to fight for a couple weeks at least. What do you do?"). I was thinking that'd it fit as a Affliction: Condition, but is it out of line to use an absent player in this way? Obviously I wouldn't actually cost his character anything, he'd just be lying in the hospital until the player comes back.

Also, how should I stat a HAB? They're about twice the size of a car and their drool shoots steam up from the snow.

    \n That's a beautiful opportunity. I'd ask the Gunlugger's player, "So your gunlugger has to leave the Hardholder's holding for an extended period of time, leaving it undefended. Why? What could be so important that you'd have to take off like that?"

    Then you've got (a) a great opportunity for the rest of the PCs to try and defend the holding from the HABs, and (b) good material to work with if you want to write a love-letter for the Gunlugger on their return, and (c) nice juicy conflicts between the gunlugger and everyone else when he shows back up after mysteriously disappearing.

    \n If possible, work together with the player. Explain that since he won't be involved for a while, you'd like to use his character's situation to create some trouble for the others. He may have better ideas than you do! And if you have his buy-in, he'll be more excited about the game and getting back into it.

    When I've done this, I've found that players tend to feel comfortable having their character get into far wilder shit than I'd ever put them through! ("How about my character gets mauled by the Bears and then tortured for weeks by the warlord across the bay? And when comes back, he'll be limping and missing an eye! Yarr!")

    As for statting up the bears... well, you don't really need to do anything. Just play up how scary they are by showing their effects on the rest of the fiction and the NPCs. I'd assign them 3-harm and some armour (just to show how big they are, and hard to kill, not necessarily that they're wearing armour).

    If there's something specific you see them doing, you could write a custom move for that, but you don't really need to.
