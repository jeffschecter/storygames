\n After many years of writing directly into desktop publishing software, I've realized that my process of writing sections has changed, that I jump around a lot more. At first I tried TiddlyWiki, but I didn't like how things cluttered up the screen. I want to type my game text directly into webpages, with hyperlinks at the side or top so I can jump to sections as the inspiration takes me. I don't intend to put everything up on the web, just have some html pages on my computer.

Now, I know this isn't a new idea, because I've seen a few people do it, but I want to ask a few questions. Are there difficulties transferring the information to a print format later? Any surprises, good or bad? What software have you used?

    \n I have been using Treepad when I write or prep adventures for games. I have heard a lot of good about Onenote (part of the Microsoft Office 2007 suite) although both aren't ''web pages'' per say they are organizer tools that allow you to write different chapters/parts at the same time and alloy you to gather all in a fairly organized document when your done.

    I stop writing chronologically when I stop having to do Essays for school. It so much easier to write when you let your brain free of chronological restrains.
