\n So we're gearing up for some kind of non-earth game, and I posed this to my players because I think it's the question I most need to answer when I'm creating a setting:

There's two ways we can go in terms of the mindset of the setting, they're mutually exclusive, and they\u2019ll affect what rules go well with the game:

Science at the heart: The arc that plumbs the depths between the stars is really an alien creation, with unearthly beasts trapped within, but its workings can be deciphered, repaired, retrofitted, or disassembled. Even if there are psychic abilities that others would describe as magic, rationality underlies this universe. This is the path to Frank Herbert's Dune.

Myth at the heart: The arc that plumbs the depths between the stars and can bring you to another world requires blood \u2013 metric tons of human blood \u2013 to make its journey, and then you must bend its demon navigator to your will. Metaphor underlies this universe, making it a place of weird magick. Dictionary of Mu, Polaris.
