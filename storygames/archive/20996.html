    \n Okay, I admit to a bit of confusion about the minis/no minis thing, and what precludes you from talking about your design on certain sites.

    Partly I'm confused because I can think of a number of commercial games where one can use minis if they wish, but games are commonly played with just markers. The old Harpoon naval rules are often played like that, because the it's Cold War/Modern Era, and ships/planes/subs/missiles are just plain so far apart and moving at such speed that any minis would generally become a dot, even if distance scale was extremely compressed vs, model scale.

    So there's precedent for that.

    Is it an issue that markers in your game represent whole formations of ships? That it's more operational or strategic scale, rather than even vaguely tactical scale?

    I know that's generally uncommon for naval or aerial miniatures rules, although common for ground combat.

    If your concern is that you have no means or intention of making minis for the game, please don't let that stop you. Plenty of minis games are written with no capability by the designer/publisher to also make/sell minis for the game.
