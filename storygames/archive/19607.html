\n I wanted to run a game where somewhere in the vein of 1998 film Elizabeth. The players would take the role as the queen's court and protectors (spies, advisers, family and council) in a kingdom that is under unrest and shaky at best. It doesn't have to be historically accurate, it could a be composite of the time period if necessary. Some of things I was looking for would be...

-Some sort of fun system for intrigue
-Some domain management and strategy
-Destructive violence (people should die, and players should fear death)

    \n The key factor in making intrigue and politics into a good story is to absolutely adopt the conservative, "Great Person" view of how politics gets done. Don't do institutional stuff, don't do "social movement" stuff. Literalize everything - everything - into a human character and make what happens to that character be what the politics is about. Don't like the Spanish? Don't make the game about the army, make the game about the ambassador, the ambassador's daughter, the general's wife.

    \n If it's more about intrigue and emotional drama, then DramaSystem (SRD here) would seem a good fit. If it's more focused on the procedural rather than the dramatic, then you might use FATE.

    Reign's good if you want a mechanic for the different factions, although it does seem, in my limited experience, that the PCs have to be on the same side, which is definitely not the case in DramaSystem. You could also use Prime Time Adventures if you want it to be structured like a TV show.
