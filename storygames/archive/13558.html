    Posted By: The MagusHow shall we sit about and discuss it?
    \nThe London Indie Games Meetup is conveniently situated in a boozer.

    Let's take our warm beers to the upper bar and sit on the comfy seats and spend time - a chilled couple of hours - in the evening slot to have a natter. A chat like that works for me - I don't want to write a considered 1000-word essay. Perhaps someone could come armed with some questions to provoke conversation beyond "what did you think of that then?"

    I'm thinking questions like:\n
      \n
    • what was fun, and how can we keep nailing that?
      • \n
    • what sucked, and how can we avoid that?
      • \n
    • what advice would you give to other folk thinking of playing/GMing it (I never get enough of this.)
      • \n
    • did the system address the premise - oh yeah, how?
      • \n
    • what are we gonna play next in Andy's RPG Club?
      • \n
    • where are the entry points for hacking the game? (It's a bit freewheeling this last one, but I like to cut loose and talk shit once in a while.)

    \n Some more thoughts:

    * For now, I'm going to aim for 6-week blocks of time. One month (considering my own work situation and all) seems almost to harried to sustain, and two months seems to be perhaps a little long. This may change over time, perhaps quickly, based on the first two or so rounds.

    * I may include out-of-print books. But whenever at all f'ing possible, it'll be books available for purchase or legal download in PDF/other format. If not, I'll make sure that the product is not sooooper rare/impossible to get for a reasonable price. In my mind, I've already locked (for the future) on the possibility of two regarded games that are OOP but not in PDF format, but are available without too much problem on eBay, Amazon (used) and the like.

    * I'm going to try to find a sense of polarity in choices: A balance between games that consider mechanics, scope ("traditional", "hippie" etc), design background, location of the designer globally (if known, in order to draw on various communities), etc. So we might see a more traditional game followed up by a hippie game.

    * Having said that, I'll be aiming at games that are more on the hippie end of the spectrum than traditional. This is Story Games (the site) after all, not RPGGeek or RPGNet. If you're here, you're interested in playing those kinds of games. While traditional playstyle games will be introduced from time to time, they'll be usually in the categories of:
    -- Free
    -- Independent
    -- Something unique about it (setting-wise)
    -- Something unique about it (rule wise; even if it's just the One Hippie Rule stapled to an Otherwise Tradditty-Trad-Trad game)

    * I'll be drawing on lots of free or at least very inexpensive games (you'll see when I announce the first round/format). I don't want this to turn into a Purchasing Club, but a Playing Club.

    * Having said that, I'm aiming at games with the promise of (at least in theory) being complete, and no playtest games.

    * Just a quick aside, in case folks were wondering: I too will be aiming for games that I haven't played at all (or that much) that look interesting. In case there were fears of "Oprah's Book Club", trying to sell you on the games that only I like or something.

    * I'll be offering limited choices in selection. We'll see how that works. See the first round for details when announced.

    * The first round is "sho ga nai", but in further rounds I'll try to announce a week or two early to give folks time to procure the games in physical form and all.

    -Andy

    \nFor now, what I'd encourage is if people want to rock a campaign, that they go ahead and do so, even if that means passing on the Next Month game... but will likely not make it part of the actual format for now. I feel good enough about this project that I can "commit people" to playing 1-2 games in a 4-6 week period: If they didn't like the game, one session was enough; if they like it a lot, they can extend their run a month or more. But committing people to a 3-month game seems difficult at this time. Maybe if it was "choose one of these three games" or something.

    Might be worth thinking about again in the summer/"convention season", as things get crazy then and a longer 2-3 month format for an extended campaign (or 1-2 sessions of those three months for folks caught up in the convention circuit) would be better.

    \n Alright, will make something more official-y later on, but for now here's how we're going to start:

    Period one will be January 1st (yesterday!) to February 15th.

    Intentions: Play at least one session. Drop a report about how the session went, even if it didn't go well. There will be a discussion thread in a bit.

    If you want to hack it to use house rules, alternate setting, etc that's your prerogative. However, just note in the discussion what you changed about the game and why.

    Now, the whole point of a "game club" is to play a game together and find common discussion, interests, etc. I am currently waffling fully on the aspect of making people buy new games: In a book club, the book is like less than $10(US) and often cheaper used or in libraries; but in the RPG world, games can be significantly more expensive. And yet, I don't want to stick to a certain price range ("All games under $10", "Only Free Games", etc).

    So my thought for now is to tag one game of Whatever price, and one game of a price between Free and $5.00 (or thereabouts; strongly swinging towards Free), and let people pick which game they want to play. With six weeks, those that wish to can try doing one session of both, or 1+ sessions of one. There is no push to playing both, I think that if people sign on in for one or the other that we'll have enough people to create a discussion (I'm hoping at least 3 people per game at the minimum).
