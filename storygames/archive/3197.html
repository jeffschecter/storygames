    \n
    \n There's something very surreal about that article. Not to belittle Brennan's work, but it has the feel of a Nascar reporter interviewing a kid with a soapbox derby car.
    \n
    \nBrennan: "Our car is now 50% faster than last year, up to 12 MPH top speed."
    \nReporter: "How do you account for this tremendous increase?"
    \n
    \nI just keep shaking my head, trying to make it all make sense inside. It's very much like the reporter can't concieve of a business this small, and, therefore, can't figure out what questions make sense to ask. I think that IPR is interesting, and should merit reporting in such a magazine. But there's just no sense of perspective.
    \n
    \nWeird. I hope it benefits you in some positive way, Brennan. But I can't imagine how.
