    \nmicroscope -- You build a vast history from the outside in. You start knowing only something very simple, a beginning and an end (like "humanity settles the solar system, then falls into civil war"), and then you zoom in and discover all the things that made that big picture happen. It's basically fractal gaming.

    You don't have your own character. You laugh in the face of chronological order.

    Players have the freedom to jump back and forth across time and space to focus on parts of the history they want to explore. So you have a scene where the king gets assassinated, but the next player decides they want to jump back to the king's happy youth and find out where the seeds of his doom were planted, and so on. If you know something is going to happen (the king gets assassinated) you can still go back and explore why or how it happened (who really killed him? why?). The past is never closed.

    It's been in playtesting since March. A bunch of folks in Seattle have tried it, but it craves more victims. I'm really enjoying playing it. More posts on microscope.

      \n My answer is the same as it was 19 months ago, because that's how I roll:\n
      Posted By: Ben Robbinsmicroscope-- You build a vast history from the outside in. You start knowing only something very simple, a beginning and an end (like "humanity settles the solar system, then falls into civil war"), and then you zoom in and discover all the things that made that big picture happen. It's basically fractal gaming.

      You don't have your own character. You laugh in the face of chronological order.

      Players have the freedom to jump back and forth across time and space to focus on parts of the history they want to explore. So you have a scene where the king gets assassinated, but the next player decides they want to jump back to the king's happy youth and find out where the seeds of his doom were planted, and so on. If you know something is going to happen (the king gets assassinated) you can still go back and explore why or how it happened (who really killed him? why?). The past is never closed.

      \n What I'm working on right now that might (or probably will) be English-language is Pangaea, a game of sword & sorcery set in the very first dawn of man, when remnants of the master race that came from the stars and gave mankind both civilisation and slavery still hide in isolated enclaves in the jungles or beneath the seas, worshipped by savage cannibalistic tribes; where drums beat endlessly beneath mountains shrouded in eternal rainclouds; where the realms of men are weak, tiny city-states built on the ruins of what came before. Basic savage jungle S&S. With dinosaurs.

      The game is GM-less and heavily influenced by Polaris, In a Wicked Age and (among other things) Mouse Guard. I had a version ready for playtesting that I really liked, but then I was hit by Apocalypse World and got a bunch of new ideas. After a bit of tinkering and few playtesting sessions, the game should be ready for public playtest, though. Just have to write down the rules so someone else could understand them. I kinda have high hopes for this one.
