\n In Geiger Counter it's always better mechanically for protagonists to team up against the menace and pool their dice rather than fight solo -- the menace attacks everyone in the scene whether they pool or not.

The downside is that encourages a very united front, even when it would be more interesting for characters to weasel.

What if you said: if the menace is facing more than one side (aka the characters don't all pool dice) and the menace wins, the highest rolling loser gets to avoid a condition and escape.

- it won't change the odds at all if all the protagonists do join forces (since it doesn't apply in that case)

- it could tempt selfish protagonists to go for themselves -- they might not be able to beat the big menace, but if they can roll higher than the guy next to them they get away scott free...

Thoughts?

    \n The potential flip-side abuse/tactic would be the sacrificial lamb: most of the protagonists pool, but one doesn't, knowing that even if the menace wins the group will probably roll higher, so the group will escape while the solo person takes the hit for the team.

    Is that good or bad? I can certainly see that happening in the fiction but it might be mechanically too easy.

    It could be pretty sexy: "I'll slow it down! You get out of here!" "No, I won't let you throw your life away!"

    Maybe the sacrificial lamb beats the menace and turns out to be a hero after all.
