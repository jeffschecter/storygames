\n What's wrong with you Swedish people? Why won't you tell anyone about your games?

Yes, this is a friendly tease. (Non-scandinavians: If you don't think I'm being friendly here, you should see what Norwegian-Swedish communication usually looks like ) The reason I'm asking is that I'm browsing Lulu, clicking on the tag "rollspel", getting some links to stuff I've never heard about... then googling it, and getting only a few obscure references, some of which point to other stuff I've never heard about.

I'm talking about stuff like Sju sm\xe5 rollspel ("Seven Small RPGs") by Tobias Rades\xe4ter (the Supercrew guy) and Svante Landgraf. Or Nostalgi ("Nostalgia") by Martin Ackerfors, where retired adventurers brag about their past. Or Haragada, about a city of sinners in the desert.

How come I've never heard about any of these games? They look cool!

If you're a Swedish designer, I'd like to invite you over to promote your game(s( at the Norwegian forum rollespill.net. I've set up a special thread for you. You might even generate some sales there!

    \n I can't speak for the Kaleidioskop guys (all of the games you named are from the same "collective"), but personally, I can't be bothered. My game appears in my signature at rollspel.nu, where I post frequently, and there will be a review in Sweden's only RPG magazine, and that's about it. I find that sort of stuff a terrible bore, and as I don't make any money from my game anyway, I don't really care.

    The Kaleidioskop guys, though, do make money from their games, and I find them sitting around at conventions selling their games instead of playing games and having fun. However, I imagine trying to promote a game in Swedish outside of Sweden will yield little result compared to the effort put in. I, for one, know of only a single Norwegian RPG at all (Itras by), and all I know about it is the name. The communication between the nordic countries is very sparse, in my experience, and not only when it comes to RPGs.

    Posted By: MatthijsI'm really finding this hard to understand. Don't you want people to play your game? Why did you publish it, then? Promoting outside Sweden does work - as you know, just mentioning your game here on SG sold two copies (that I know of) to Norway.
    \nOh, it's hardly that I actively DON'T want people to play it, I simply don't find it rewarding enough to put in an effort. It might have something to do with the fact that the one game I've published is not this awesome game I'm totally psyched about, but rather a good game I had on the backburner for years and finally decided to make into a book. The game I'm working on at the moment I'm much more involved in, and might feel differently about (though I might well have tired of it by the time I get it published). I had the following reasons for publishing Oskrivna blad:

    * People had been playing the free PDF I had put out and were nagging me to make it into a book.
    * I wanted to have a game published, as an achievement in and of itself.
    * I wanted to finish the project so I could move on to other stuff, and making a good-looking PDF takes about as much effort as publishing a book nowadays.

    Anyway, "publishing" the game might not be that accurate a term, as all I did was upload a PDF to lulu. I ordered five copies to myself (for selling to people who asked me about it), though I accidentally mailed them to someone else who has sold most of them already. When I go to conventions, I will not bring a bunch of copies of my game with me to sell. I like to travel light and will only bring the games I want to play (i.e. I'll bring my own copy of the game, for play). I certainly don't want to give up any gaming time for sales.

    I'm an extremely busy person, and any time spent promoting my game will have to be taken from some other activity. And, as I said, I find the promoting of the game to be a terrible bore, and though it's fun if people play my game, I know that they will do it anyway. It makes little difference to me wether it's 20 people or 200, and I'm pretty confident it will never be 2000.

    I hope that might elucidate things. I really just wanted to make my game into a book, y'know?

    \n Simon, okay, I think I get it. Thanks for the reply!

    Like Svante, I like sitting at cons with friends selling my games or fanzines. It's like we're basking in the sunlight of our own amazingness We meet people, and talk about games.
