    \n Interesting list. I sent you an email to see if I can send you a copy of my game to see how it fits in.

    There is a scenario opener that helps people imagine the world they are about to play in - "The Matrix" for instance evokes a mental image.

    Each turn players make up actions (narration) that is judged by a fellow player who sets a to happen roll. Rolls can rule out actions.

    Play goes around the table with players usually building on other player's actions. One time round the table is a turn. Players may disagree and force a roll at the end of the turn to see which one action happens.

    Once an action happens it becomes part of the world matrix. Facts build on facts to tell the story.

    There is also a plot track that guides players by suggesting what they should resolve in their actions each turn. As players gain confidence they usually start making up their own plot tracks.

    That's it. I can see parts of it in some of your enteries but not quite. It's shared narration but also has a competitive potential. The rules are only 7 pages in a word document.

    Chris Engle
