    \n 1. SimpleQuest - my own take on Fantasy d100 RQ/BRP built on a massively modified Mongoose RQ SRD and geared up for Storytelling and an OGL release in Sept/Oct. Its a massive trip down nostaliga lane for me.

    2. Monkey - again another one of my games, just played a bundle of games at Continuum and me,the players and the other GM who was also running it had a hoot!

    3. Wordplay - Graham Spearing's D6 story telling game, while it has obvious influences from HeroQuest and Burning Wheel its very much its own man. I'm helping by reviewing it and writing one of the themes (short settings).

    Links
    d101games.co.uk for more info on Monkey and SimpleQuest (including the Simple Quest Zero Edition as a free pdf download)
    wordplay.ukrpgblogs.net for more info on Wordplay and download of current playtest draft.

    \n Hm. These are more of all-time favorites than August favorites, because what I like is what I like, and my tastes do not change with the phase of the moon. I stand, adamant, unyielding, a paragon of sophistication and refinement for all time. You sad scurrying gadflies may fling your allegiances wherever your incomprehensible whims direct you: I shall only smile indulgently at your excesses.

    Also, I ain't going to put in any effort trying to assess my August gaming in particular rather than just regurgitating my usual preferences.

    1. Any iteration of the Marvel franchise -- FASERIP, SAGA, MURPG, I don't care, they all worked great for me.

    2. Star Wars, any version...although I really like WotC's Saga edition and would rank it highest among 'em; it's the first one since WEG's 1st edition that I've really loved right out of the box, and I think it handles long campaigns and Force powers way better than WEG's game ever did.

    3. Call of Cthulhu (BRP edition, mostly; the d20 version was okay); it's a little creaky after so many years, but still a lot of fun.

    \n 1 - Reb Box Hack : I played a lot of excellent games with this one. I am waiting for Eric's last modifications but I am still quite happy with the current state. I hope to finish my french translation soon to give it to my english-reluctant fellow gamers. Still my favorite game for some quick and dirty sword and sorcery goodness. My own Pulp mod is currently paused (cause : see n\xb03).

    2 - D&D4 : I love the way they arranged the powers, weapons and talents combinations both within a character and through the group. Still need a bit too much prep for me but as a player it's a wonderful game.

    3 - Ryuu Tama : I can't read the whole thing yet but I undertook to study japanese pretty much to read this book so you can bet it's on my favorite list for this month (and next's and next's and...). I am currently mining the book for sentences to put in my spaced repetition software, following the AJATT method. I can't stress enough how much RPG books are great material for language learning.
