    \n Hey, I love this system so far! Here's some feedback. Assume that if I don't say anything about it, I liked a lot. I reserve comments for things I LOVE or things I'm concerned about. (And there's a lot to LOVE in this system!)

    Re-allocating stats each season change: Love it. Your flavor explanation is perfect, too.

    Always succeeding on a career roll: This I'm concerned about. Failure is extremely important in Dungeon World based systems, as it pushes the story forward like nothing else, and a good DM is capable of using failure to excite and to escalate.

    I don't think it's too big a deal, though - hence mere concern and not dislike - because it does come with a "harder complication or cost". Perhaps just being explicit that, compensation for the automatic 7-9, the "harder complication or cost" translates to the fact the GM is allowed to automatically make a move in response to their success, as opposed to a normal success, where they just present you with a choice or complication.

    Examples of allies: In the parenthetical examples, you forgot to say "etc.". You'd be surprised how many people assume those are the only options.

    Patterns: So casting patterns through writing runes requires dexterity but not eerieness? I'm not sure I understand - someone who's not eerie at all can still rune-pattern, as long as they're dextrous? Or do you mean they roll dexterity in addition to rolling eerieness? What if it was "roll + dexterity or eerieness, whichever's lower"?

    Spinning Patterns Backwards: You can spin Closing backwards. Can you spin any other Song backwards, or just Closing?

    Fate and Transcendence: These seem vague and powerful. Fate is seeing the future and messing with it? And Transcendence is erratic shapeshifting of others?

    Fate seems poorly described, and Transcendence seems really strong.

    Perhaps Transcendence could just be replaced with 'shapeshifting other people temporarily' to serve as a counterpart to Shifting.

    Overall: Excellent. I truly loved it. Look forward to seeing more of your stuff!

    \n Paul_T: thank you! I'd like to fix the typos eventually, but english is not my first language and it takes me double effort to notice them.

    BeePeeGee: feel free to do it, and please be sure to tell me how it worked

    Inanimate: So it was you who wrote in that /tg/ thread! i could'nt answer because it 404d

    On your concerns: the "succeed even on a miss" in your careers, is something reworked from World of Dungeons (when you never failed if you had a relevant skill). Its one of my main concerns when playtesting the whole thing

    I'll take the "etcs" into account, thanks! on Dexterity being used to cast patterns: i think i'm keeping the "roll + dexterity or eerieness, whichever's lower" thing.

    Spinning Patterns Backwards: You're right on this. Maybe i'll just add the pattern section that some patterns can be spun backwards; and leave them on the GM's fiat to decide which ones.

    Fate: this pattern is poorly explained. I intended for it to be a kind of "bless" or "curse". You don't necessarily read the target's fate (that would maybe require having a clairvoyancy-related career). The pattern just inscribes a line on fate: "You'll be a king someday". "You'll face an horrible death"; and GM has to accomplish that prophecy to the best of his skills. Of course, failing a roll can bring you troubles, more likely to be proportional as your blessing/curse.

    Trascendency: You're right; this is really powerful. But it appears on the Loom videogame; a main inspiration of this game. Maybe i'm just curious about what can happen if you give a character that much ability. And of course, the bigger the power, the bigger the risks. Also, trascendency is not equal to death. The target just shifts into a trascended form (and this is so open that it can mean whatever a GM wants to mean)

    I'll take all your critics into account for the final draft. I didn't expected so good reviews! i'm really glad someone liked it!
