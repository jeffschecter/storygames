\n After a long time away from the game table, last weekend gave me the opportunity to run two games at a local con, which turned out to be a lot of fun. They also got me thinking about games and settings I've been meaning to try for a long time.

One of them - as you might be able to tell by the title - is Doctor Who.

Now, I'm aware that there is a very good Doctor Who RPG in print right now. But the part of my brain that likes to think about design ideas, choices and how various rules interlock makes me want to think about using different games to run a Doctor Who.

So which game would you hack to run a quick-and-easy Doctor Who game?

    \n My Life With Master.

    When you first stepped into the blue box, he promised to show you the wonders of the universe. You followed him into death traps and disasters, watched him slay monsters and destroy civilizations. If only you had known then what you now know to be true -- he is mad and alien, and utterly dependent on fragile, human companionship. The story always begins the same -- a raggedy man with a mysterious smile whisks you off to the stars. But how will your story end? When you finally escape his pull, what will it cost you?

    \n Hmm... I don't know. I think that's too much of a deconstructionist take on the Doctor for my liking. I'd prefer a more straight-forward adventurous and optimistic tone for a Doctor Who game. Focussing on the tragedy - while definitely part of the storytelling palette of Who - doesn't strike me as being true to the overall appeal of the setting.
