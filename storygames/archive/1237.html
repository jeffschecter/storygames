After Tuesday night's promising playtest I started putting together a proper rules document for my new version of the Infected. Problem is, I don't have much experience writing for an audience. Most of the stuff I write is just for me. There's less than a dozen pages to the new rules and it took me most of a day to put it all together.

So, if you've got a few minutes, I'd love it if you could take a peek at the rules. Let me know where they seem confusing or incomplete. That would be awesome.

Also, there's one part I haven't finished writing yet. I just don't know what to do about an afterward or epilogue. See, the idea of an epilogue seems to be a bit contrary to the genre. Can't have a happily ever after in a horror film, now can we? Besides, there always has to be room for a sequel! But then... there's the issue of letting the players have one final say after the last act. I really just don't know what to do with it. Hit me with some ideas.

-Eric

    Here's a thought that occurred to me this morning.

    I was thinking to myself; What kind of patterns are there in the endings of monster movies? Especially zombie movies?

    And the answer that came to me was that the happiness of the ending seems to be inversely proportional to the number of protagonists that survive the climax. If only the geeky guy and his hot girlfriend survive the end, then they get the happy music and sail off into the sunset. On the other hand, if only one or two of the characters get torn apart by the mobs of living dead, then during the credits we hear the suspense music and get to see blurry images of people running for their lives.

    It seems like a potientially cool mechanism. If more than half the PCs survive, then you get the violent ending. If less than half of them survive, then you get either the happy or mournful ending, but it's peaceful.

    But that still leaves me with; Who gets to narrate?

    -Eric

    Mark came by to play some DitV last night, and afterward we sat and chatted about the Infected for a bit. I think the system I'm going to try for my next few playtests is this;

    There is no epilogue. The final scene wrapts it all up. With one caveat and one possible addition. The caveat being that all of the players need to be aware of the pacing that's induced by the GM's use of his infected pool. In our last playtest, when I was down to two dice during the second act I alerted the rest of the players to this fact. That's probably something that should be in the rules. The possible addition would be, after the final scene of act three, having the GM ask the players to collaboratively answer the question; "What scenes, if any, do we see while the credits are rolling?"

    September 2006 edited September 2006

    Good question, Ian.

    It's true, the first time an NPC is involved in a conflict he or she isn't worth crap for helping out a player. However, just because you are introducing the character does not mean that the character is there to help you. Also, you may use an NPC card for an NPC who's already been introduced by someone else.

    It's legit to introduce a character who's in conflict with one of the PCs, yours or someone else's. It's also legit to write the name of an NPC who's already been authored by the GM onto a card. In either case, if you choose to hand that card over to the GM to play, then the GM gets to roll those three dice.

    Make sense?
