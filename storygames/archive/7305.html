\n THIS THREAD IS EXCLUSIVELY FOR: Rob Bohl, Brian Peters, Kingston Cassidy, Joel Shempert, Joe McDonald, Remi.

Alright. We've just gotten together to play a session of Long Road Nowhere. Each of you brought a themed mix tape with 10-20 songs on it.

We gather some snacks - maybe a bottle of wine, some satay chicken skewers and a fresh baguette. We sit down at the place we game at. I gather the mix tapes and shuffle them, and randomly draw Rob Bohl's "Poverty! Bigotry! Rebellion!"

None of you know the game, but I am going to explain it as we go. I put in the tape, Rob Bohl passes a copy of the lyrics of the first song out to everyone at the table, and I hit play.

We play the song in silence, reading along, closing our eyes, or picking at food as the song plays. Let's simulate this now. Everyone in Group Highwayman listen to the song in silence.

Based upon that first song and the mix tape title, we decide upon the premise of our road trip. Where are we going? Why? Who's in the car?

We have a casual conversation (without musical accompaniment), and in the end we know: the initial tone, our destination, where we are leaving from, why we are on the road, and vaguely who is in the car (we want very broad brush strokes, like "the crippled grandfather" and "the idealistic poli-sci major" and so on).

So, let's listen to that song and read the lyrics, and then spend the next few days deciding upon our road trip.

    \n Well, I guess I'll start. Hmm, the song is so self-referential that it's hard to think of the trip as anything other than the Clash themselves, on tour, fighting against every authority in their lives, from label to cops to judgmental fans. If we take it a step removed, it could be a fictional band? Young and rebellious and confused, trying to navigate a treacherous world of fame and stardom and anarchist ideals? And maybe they're traveling to a big city for a climactic concert and/or label signing opportunity?

    That seems a bit cliche, but maybe appropriately so.

    Assigning the occupants of the car could be a matter of iconic strokes such as "the romantic guitarist," "the hedonistic drummer," "the anarchist bassist," and such.

    Thoughts?

    Peace,
    -Joel

    \n Joel,

    That's for sure a potential starting point. Maybe this really edgy, anarchist band is on a road trip up to sign their name to the big contract... it's a breakthrough for them, but they've gotta battle against the thought that they're selling out, so all along the trip they are desperate to prove how punk they are.

    Alternately, if we step back a bit from the singer aspect and focus on what else is in the song, I see a tale of outcasts on the road, seeing what happens when their ideals come clashing up against the real world. It could be hippies returning from Woodstock to their small, ass-backwards logging town, and having to face the music that the world would still be the same world that it had been before they had this experience.
