\n I'm stealing TonyLB's excellent idea on [MB] Stat up characters in the new system to see if my Authority classes "work" for Misspent Youth, and because it was fun to do with his game. If there's interest, I may do the character one later.

Pick a bad-guy with a great deal of power over the good guys and see if you can stat it out with these:

Vice: What motivates The Authority to act
o Sadism
o Greed
o Fear
o Stability
o Utopianism

Visage: What form The Authority takes
o Government
o Business
o Organization
o Person
o Machine (unreasoning process, like the SATs, the invisible hand of the market, a planned eugenics movement, etc.)

Victim: What The Authority is destroying/preying upon
o The Natural World
o Human Life
o History
o Progress
o Freedom

EDIT: And answer if you would 1) was it easy? 2) was it fun? and 3) do the categories need tinkering?

    \nThe visage categories are important because they tell you how to "dress" the Authority. In that sense, the only category that I think in insufficiently evocative on its face is "Organization"--could be a church, could be a, um, pyramid scheme, could be whatever. You have to decide which name you think best (a) encapsulates the various possibilities that exist, so as not to constrain player imagination, but (b) make the same sufficiently concrete that it gives players an idea of what an Authority that takes that form looks like.

    So rethinking what categories "need" to be there:

    Government <--> State [political authority] The Law
    Business <--> Corporation [bureaucratic authority] The System
    Organization <--> Church [dogmatic authority] The Godhead (God or Mammon)
    Individual <--> Demagogue [charismatic authority] The Will of the People (Vox Populi)
    Machine <--> Science [epistemological authority] Reality (the Way Things Are)

    So a "business"-type authority might either be the System ("Megacorp owns you now!") or the Godhead ("Greed is good!"), depending on whether it sees the YOs as malfunctioning cogs in its machine or as recalcitrant converts to its way of thinking.

    Ok, each individual ACF has its own 4th Trait, called its Viciousness. They are assigned by The Authority as that player creates these characters and forces on the fly. Here are the Traits:

    Violence
    This CF causes destruction, pain, and death.

    Corruption
    Corrupters attempt to appeal to the YOs' weaknesses and flaws, offering them aid or comfort which is tainted.

    Enslavement
    Enslavers attempt to control, imprison, or addict YOs.

    Division
    Dividers primarily attempt to sow strife between YOs.

    Infiltration
    Infiltrators pass themselves as friends or allies, perhaps even as leaders.
