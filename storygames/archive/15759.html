    \n I saw one once, at an all ages open mike through the Vera project. She alternated between beat poetry and strumming on her acoustic guitar. Her skin was shiny, she had greasy hair and acne. But she wore a long thin cotton skirt; is that called a peasant skirt? It moved when she tapped her foot.

    I had just moved out of my parent\u2019s and into my first apartment. Most of my friends were younger and they were still in school. They\u2019d come over every week, but that left a lot of nights without cable and no one around. I stared at this girl and was overcome with the need to talk to her. I wanted to interrupt her \u201cset\u201d to introduce myself.

    Luckily, some inner strength held me back. After she left the stage, I still longed to speak to her and follow her through the crowd, but I didn\u2019t. I\u2019m really lucky I got away.

    \n The thing about sirens is that they're all female and they live underwater. They can only reproduce by coming onto land and attracting a male as their mate (usually through a mesmerizing song). A siren's saliva is a potent narcotic and paralytic agent. During the 'joining' process, the male is implanted with the fertilized eggs and then left to gestate. Roughly a two weeks later, the new siren will emerge, by the dozen, from the still paralyzed male, devour him, and make their way to the sea. Only twice the size of a man's thumb, many of the young siren will not survive but a few always seem to. Implantation by a siren requires immediate medical attention - there are toxins you can use in the first days but you'll need a barber's tools to remove the gestating sirens after that. With the proper tonics, one so afflicted can be on their feet in only a few days.

    \n As Mathalus suggests, they're the danger of intimacy embodied.

    Love, intimacy, even simply approaching someone requires immense trust. Every secret given, even your name, empowers the other over you. Your dreams and desires can be celebrated by the other, sure, but they can also be ridiculed and smashed, instruments of your own destruction.

    The siren I see in modern horror is the lover that wins your trust and hidden desires, takes all your secrets and seems to love you for it. Then they make the public private, tear apart your friendships, and leave you estranged and isolated simply because they take pleasure in wrath.

    Love flies and wounds.

    Names
    Waverly, Ariel, Meadow, Jay, Ava, Daya, Avis, Sanny.

    Moves
    When someone hears your music, you gain a String on them.

    At the beginning of the session, explain your \u201cplaylist\u201d. Once during the session, announce what song is playing and take a +1 to your next roll.

    To keep someone from leaving you call out to them. Spend a String and roll Hot. On a 10+, they stay as long as you like. On 7-9 if they stay, they mark experience.

    Someone has been warning others that you are dangerous and to stop listening to you. Give them a String.
    You have this song. Take a String on whoever it is about.

    \n The secret to how sirens work is very simple: everybody wants something, and no one gets what they want.

    It's all about the destructive pursuit of an aching, obsessive, grasping need. You hate the siren for offering you something it has no intention to give. The siren hates you for being too weak to resist its offer. In the end, everyone is disappointed and unfulfilled: also, you are dead.

    In a world where horny sailors are less important (if not necessarily in shorter supply), what a siren offers will have to change. Maybe it's easy money. Maybe it's a better job. Maybe it's a way to shed unwanted pounds. The what doesn't matter, all that matters is that you want it too much. A siren can see you coming from a mile away, and already knows how your story will end.

    \n Sirens don't just feed on lust, they can feed on any emotions. Living in a modern age, they see fewer and fewer ships, and those they do see tend to be the big commercial kind that don't draw many meals. So the Sirens moved to where the people were, the land.

    The Siren's usual plan of singing to lure in unsuspecting lovers became too dangerous without sharp rocks and churning tides to protect them. The matrons of the Siren community studied the communities of men long and hard, and settled upon an event where a delightfully heady mixture of emotions was conjured up- arrests. The dread, anticipation, futile anger and desperate bargaining that preceded the police was a wine far more complex and intriguing than the pent up lust of sailors the Sirens once persisted on.

    At the height of high tide the Siren Queen sacrificed a thousand desperate lovers and completed her race's transformation into a new form, one with more varied emotions, more frequent feasting, and vastly diminished fear of danger.

    And so was born the Police Siren, heralding lost futures for all who heard its call.

    \n Sirens are artists. The problem is, the human brain finds their songs irresistible. When a sailor hears them, he can't help but jump overboard in an attempt to get closer, and like any sailor, not knowing how to swim, he quickly drowns. But is this the fault of Sirens, or simply a matter of sailors not preparing themselves for the terrible, beautiful hazards of the sea?

    \nProof that inadequate, even childish measures, may serve to rescue one from peril:

    To protect himself from the Sirens Ulysses stopped his ears with wax and had himself bound to the mast of his ship. Naturally any and every traveller before him could have done the same, except those whom the Sirens allured even from a great distance; but it was known to all the world that such things were of no help whatever. The song of the Sirens could pierce through everything, and the longing of those they seduced would have broken far stronger bonds than chains and masts. But Ulysses did not think of that, although he had probably heard of it. He trusted absolutely to his handful of wax and his fathom of chain, and in innocent elation over his little stratagem sailed out to meet the Sirens.

    Now the Sirens have a still more fatal weapon than their song, namely their silence. And though admittedly such a thing has never happened, still it is conceivable that someone might possibly have escaped from their singing; but from their silence certainly never. Against the feeling of having triumphed over them by one's own strength, and the consequent exaltation that bears down everything before it, no earthly powers could have remained intact.

    And when Ulysses approached them the potent songstresses actually did not sing, whether because they thought that this enemy could be vanquished only by their silence, or because of the look of bliss on the face of Ulysses, who was thinking of nothing but his wax and his chains, made them forget their singing.

    But Ulysses, if one may so express it, did not hear their silence; he thought they were singing and that he alone did not hear them. For a fleeting moment he saw their throats rising and falling, their breasts lifting, their eyes filled with tears, their lips half-parted, but believed that these were accompaniments to the airs which died unheard around him. Soon, however, all this faded from his sight as he fixed his gaze on the distance, the Sirens literally vanished before his resolution, and at the very moment when they were nearest to him he knew of them no longer.

    But they--lovelier than ever--stretched their necks and turned, let their cold hair flutter free in the wind, and forgetting everything clung with their claws to the rocks. They no longer had any desire to allure; all that they wanted was to hold as long as they could the radiance that fell from Ulysses' great eyes.

    If the Sirens had possessed consciousness they would have been annihilated at that moment. But they remained as they had been; all that had happened was that Ulysses had escaped them.

    (Kafka.)
