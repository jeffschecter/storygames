\nADAMANT AND EVIL HAT: TWO-FISTED PULP!
\n
\nAdamant Entertainment and Evil Hat Productions are announcing a licensing agreement involving the two companies' pulp RPG products, THRILLING TALES and SPIRIT OF THE CENTURY.
\n
\nThis June, Adamant Entertainment will be releasing the THRILLING TALES ADVENTURE OMNIBUS, a print and pdf collection of eight previously-released d20 pulp adventures. The ADVENTURE OMNIBUS, which will be available in print as well as PDF, will feature, via license with Evil Hat, the adventures newly re-statted for SPIRIT OF THE CENTURY, as well as the original d20 rules.
\n
\n"THRILLING TALES is the best d20 pulp game on the market, and SPIRIT OF THE CENTURY is the best non-d20 pulp game on the market," says Gareth-Michael Skarka of Adamant Entertainment. "With the games 'joining forces' via the Adventure Omnibus, we've got pulp gamers covered, no matter what rules system they prefer."
\n
\n"Evil Hat is, well, thrilled to join forces with Adamant," says Fred Hicks of Evil Hat Productions. "We believe that pulp games work best with their powers combined. By putting SPIRIT OF THE CENTURY together with THRILLING TALES in the Adventure Omnibus, both games will get exactly what they need to hit the ground running. Gorilla Khan's monkey minions won't stand a chance!"
\n
\nThe book will contain the following adventures, now dual-statted for SOTC and d20: Dragon Island, Lost Temple of the Incan Blood God, Rocket Raiders, Spears of the Tisangani, The Mind Murders, The Mummy's Revenge, The Steel Legion, and Zeppelin Adventures. In addition, it will also feature the brief encounters released in Suddenly....Encounters. Further information on these titles can be found via the products link on Adamant Entertainment's website.
\n

    Posted By: YokiboyWhat I'm asking is, are the adventures linear (branching or not), event-based, or just full of cool stuff that you can use to hook up with your PCs' flags?
    \nI'm not 100% clear on the jargon you're using, but I'd describe them as event-based, leaning towards linear. They're presented in chapters, with each chapter containing events which are more like set-pieces that can occur, depending on the course of action taken by the PCs, etc.\n
    Posted By: iagoThis very likely means sales through Adamant's current storefronts, however those manifest, possibly with a Lulu printing. (I'm strongly interested in there being some kind of print option, and I think GMS's plans for the product include it.)
    \nThis is primarily intended as a print product. The PDF will be sold via our storefronts (RPGNow and DTRPG), and the printed version will be sold direct from Adamant via our website (as is the case with MARS right now). We *could* offer it via traditional distribution to stores, but given the niche-y nature of the product, I'm not sure if that makes sense -- given the size of the audience, it makes more sense to offer it direct, since Adamant & Evil Hat would make more money that way.
