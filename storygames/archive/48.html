    To be pedantic, I think we're looking at Genre Expectations here. The term Trope has started to be used more widely, but I believe it started out meaning something more like a character archetype.

    But I digress. I like to refer to Assimov on his ideas about Sci-fi, which is to say that it's largely a useless term. That what tends to get categorized as Sci-fi really would be better labeled as "Speculative Fiction." That is, it's all about "what if?" So you can go on and on about genre expectations looking at things that don't exist but might in the future. The problem is you'll go on ad infinitum.

    Space Opera, OTOH, might be more manageable.

    But for sci-fi, there's a current design out there in development, can't remember who or what the name is off the top (Shock?), but it's all about figuring out what the "difference" under examination is, and then looking at repercussions. Which is an interesting angle.

    Just some random thoughts.

    Mike
