Judson_Lester

Okay, so I just dropped a comparitively small amount of cash to round out my personal Indie library, and I've come to a conclusion:

* I would most like to buy printed books, with the PDF as an extra
* If I can't have that, I'll buy just the printed book.
* If I can't have that, I'll buy the PDF.

Why? Because I dig holding the book in my hand, dragging it around with me, showing it to friends, and holding something in my hand for my money.

The PDF is nice for that semi-instant gratification thing. I love skimming a game I just bought on the laptop while I wait for it to arrive in the mail.

Now, the art of self-publishing an indie game is certainly trucking along, and everyone still has their own way of doing things, but here's what I like best as a purchaser, and what I want to do when I finally get over my stage fright and start seriously playtesting my games with an eye towards publication.

Two options:

# Hardcopy book for $20, plus the PDF for free!
# Just the PDF (possibly multiple versions) for $8-12.

The actual figures are merely intended as typical examples.

Call to action: what have you liked to see in your games buying, and what have been the rationale behind your offerings?

    I have the same buying priorities (book + pdf, then just book, then just PDF).

    I'm offering Timestream in the following breakdown:

    * 8$ b&w PDF
    * 12$ color PDF. If you want, you get the b&w PDF for free (it's nicer for ink conservation). This version is also internally bookmarked and hyperlinked, which the B&W is not. Same content.
    * 16$ b&w, coilbound book. You get the color PDF with your purchase. I, personally, prefer coil-bound books for actual play, which is the main reason this one is offered. Same content, except differently formatted for the smaller size (6x9 instead of 8.5x11), and I think it has one less picture than the PDF.
    * 26$ full-color perfectbound book. You get the color PDF with your purchase. Same content as the coil-bound.

    So far, I've sold more of 26$ than anything else, by far. Which doesn't make any sense to my economy-geared mind - but hey, I'm not complaining.

    Is that the kind of thing you're looking for?

    Just a point or two of data to add to this thread - creating a professional, sellable .pdf is nontrivial. Some of this is logistical and can be factored into design, but the final product can and should look and behave a little differently from a print book. With The Roach, for example, we intend to offer a .pdf eventually, but we're not going to do it until we can do it right, which means taking time we don't have right now to reformat and add layers.

    Another point about print books - Bully Pulpit Games makes slightly less on international orders per unit by keeping shipping at a flat rate, but 35% of our business thusfar has been overseas, with big responses from the UK and Germany.
