\n My wife is writing some science fiction, and she's looking for good hard sci-fi world building resources, role-playing related or otherwise. What can you recommend? If you can come up with a sci-fi resource that includes inhabited double-planets, bonus!

To start it off, here's what I've already got:

Universe RPG from SPI included planetary system charts with variable habitable zones based on spectral types, and a wild array of planetary terrain
Traveller RPG, naturally, with its planet creation tables
Centauri Dreams blog for the latest science of space travel and habitable expolanets
Systemic blog for more habitable planets and astrobiology
Life as we do not Know it astrobiology book by a world leading expert
The Weird World of Winchell Chung for accurate 3d starmaps of the near universe

    \n I had looked into this pretty exhaustively at one point.

    GURPS Space and GURPS Traveller: First In should be mandatory for this, particularly the latter. Really, the point where you compute black body temperature for each planet should clue you in that someone has done his homework.

    Tyge Sj\xf6strand has a pretty nice system-agnostic document you can
    download for free

    StarGen is based on an enhanced version of the ACRETE algorithm developed by the RAND Corporation.

    AstroSynthesis is pretty impressive, but I have found it's more of a fun toy and a frustrating tool. It might be exactly what she is looking for, though.

    Celestia will let you cruise around our nearby region of space. It can be modified to add science fiction-y goodness.

    2300AD RPG has sort of reasonable tables. They use pre-Hipparcos data for their star charts, though.
