\n Not last week, not two years ago, I want to hear right now. What's most eating your brain-space? You can make qualifiers if you want ("Up until recently, it was X; but now..." etc), but in truth I imagine that this list changes *constantly* for many of us, depending on what recent book we read, movie we watched, excellent game session we had, etc. I know that two months ago I'd probably have a different list (probably with Dread: The First Book of Pandemonium*).

Mine, as of Right Now (July 2008), are:

*With Great Power*: Glancing over my shelf, it's hard to find a game which produced a more awesome gaming campaign in the last year without hacking it up or adding a million house rules (like I'm doing now with L5R, essentially turning L5R into Primetime Adventures meets The Shadow of Yesterday). Playing by the rules, we had a dark comic story set in the Night Watch universe (in Seattle), with plenty of twists, character exposition, and explosive roleplaying.

*The Shadow Of Yesterday*: Although I haven't played it for a year or so, I pretty much played it weekly (and then at one point 2 times/week) for over two years before that. The mix of fast start-up, goals written right into your character, and Fantasy World are pretty hard to beat. Currently I'm having a fight in my brain to redo the system from Fudge to Numbers because I recently love numbers again. This system has become my GURPS.

*Dungeons and Dragons 4th Edition*: This game fucking poisoned my brain. I can't do "normal combats" in RPGs anymore without wishing I had a mat to throw down, and real (not mealy-mouthed) tactics. However, I totally realize I am still in the Honeymoon Phase with it, and perhaps in 6-12 months my interests may turn elsewhere. Still, this is the RPG that kicked out Qin and some Japanese RPGs to be "the game next to my nightstand that I read before I go to sleep".

-Andy

* Ohp, there I go with the qualifiers already.
