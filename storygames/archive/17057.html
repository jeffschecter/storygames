[Comedy] Non-shock/gonzo humor in RPGs?

\n
\n
\n
\n\nJon_Shepherd \n\n\n
\n
\n\nAugust 2012 \n edited August 2012 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n There are definitely comedy RPGs out there. Quite a bit, in fact. But it seems to me that all of them gravitate towards shock humor/gonzo as a source of laughs (this excludes games where the rules themselves are a joke rather than the gameplay or fiction produced being particularly funny in and of itself).

I'm on the lookout for comedy games utilizing different flavors of humor. Any "dry workplace commentary" games? Or what about "coming-of-age fish-out-of-water"[1] games? Dare I ask, should there be a game based entirely around awful puns?

----

[1] It seems to me that Teenagers From Outer Space could be played this way, but I doubt it's directly supported by the rules.

    \n The Shab-al-Hiri Roach comes to mind. Fiasco can get pretty zany, but the Roach tends to be a more subdued, black comedy kind of affair. There's a rather obscure game called MSG that seems like it would give you a sort of cyberpunk Dilbert kind of feel. (Or maybe future Better Off Ted?)

    With Dragon World I'm aiming to make a game in the style of 90s comedy fantasy anime like Slayers and Dragon Half. It's pretty gonzo, but I think part of what makes it work is that it nonetheless lends itself to having some semblance of a story.

    With Clover Ben Lehman did a game for simple slice of life stories in which the main character is a wide-eyed little girl. It can be a very funny game, but it's grounded in reality and more about characters being silly. I'm aiming to do something similar with my own Raspberry Heaven game.

    \n InSpectres can be run non-Gonzo with the right level of detail.

    If you're a poor franchise, concentrate on the day-to-day humor of trying to stave off the electric company and the malfunctioning coffee pot. Then when the action heats up, Hellboy-style humor works the best: a ridiculous beast is lying at the end of the search, but one can beat it with fairly conventional means \u2013 fists and wisecracks.

    If you're a rich franchise, focus on the social costs of coming from old/new money, and then make the money only able to buy things that we've got available IRL.

    Either way, it's usually the Tech Roll that ratchets up the Gonzo in InSpectres.
