Posted By: Andy
I'm thinking of perhaps creating an ongoing list (maybe on the Wiki?) of RPGs/"Story Games" as they are released, or with their intended release date (or perhaps when they come out). It would be kind of a catalog of everything, with no arguing over what qualifies or not. If Matt from Mongoose wants to drop his release schedule in there with the dirty hippie 1-page free RPGs that exist only on Blogs, then he's free to.

It would be like a focused "sonar" version of John Kim's site, basically. What's coming, what Just came out, etc.

I love the new hotness. Even if I don't buy/play it all, I like to know what's going on out there, on the off chance that there's something cool going on that might appeal to me, even if it's 1 in some 20 games. I only knew about 3 games listed in this thread, so for my own benefit I'm thinking of starting something like that.

The Wiki would be the obvious place for it. I'm wondering if there's some other sort of software tool that I could implement to be a better mousetrap to log/organize releases... hmmm...
\nLet's talk about this.

    Posted By: Jonathan WaltonI think "intended release date" is a mistake, given the slower and more deliberate development that we're trying to encourage. Can I suggest more general categories, like, "playtest draft in progress" and then "playtest draft available" and then "ashcan in progress" and "ashcan available" and then "final text in progress" and "published."
    \nAbsolutely. Also, I'm actually far more interested in all the stuff in print/published that I/we might have missed, to focus on that. Bold those titles or something? Dunno.

    Also: I'm not at all interested in listing "playtest draft in progress"/"ashcan in progress", because that would be like other various RPG creation sites: A million little links to "Coming Soon!" pages for games that might never get done, blog posts by well meaning but busy designers who may never complete their works, etc. Everything that is "Playtest draft available" and above, I'm all for though. That includes all the Game Chef submissions, if their individual authors wish to submit them.
    Whether we include "Ashcan" or not is up to the crowd. For me, Ashcan is another word for Playtest. If someone wants to claim that their work is not a mere playtest draft, but an ashcan, then that would be cool by me though. Otherwise, if we had to come up with hard fast categories, I'd smell something like "playtest available", "coming soon" ("final text in progress"), and "published". With the option that people can post "Ashcan available" in the area reserved for "Playtest available". How's that sound?

    But with the above in mind (namely just the "playtest draft available and above; and not including "draft coming soon" works" item), if anyone wants to run with that be my guest. I'd suggest also writing, on that page, guidelines/instructions for people who wish to add their works/other works without knowing a lot of Wiki-Fu.

    Later, once we get started, we can discuss rotation cycles, yearly archiving and the like.

    -Andy
