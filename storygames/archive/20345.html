CLANDESTINE PATROL
Shortwave crackling to life, "Charlie, Oscar, November, Papa..." The lady's voice is melodic even, "Ready, ready, ready, ready...  Six, two, three, niner; eight, niner, four, seven; three, one, two..."  Numbers repeating on a loop.  
"End transmission."

Lying in bed--awake.  Heart racing.  Pupils dilating.  Then dressing.  Grabbing.  Running.  Running fast.  Running where?  Running to what?  Then there's jumping.  And kicking.  And punching.  And shooting.  Yeah, lot's of shooting.  And god, lot's of running. 

Looking for what?  Pulling the threads together.  Holding them together.  You...  Why?  Are you remembering, yet?  Never mind.  There's always fighting.  Crashing.  Shooting.  And of course, running.  And then darkened-dreaming.  And then the numbers again, repeating. "Two, seven, three..."
"End Transmission."


Clandestine Patrol plays as Pocket Danger Patrol with the following changes.

Genre Inspiration:  Jason Bourne, The Transporter, Ethan Hawke, Ronin, Person of Interest, Black-List, Nowhere to Run, Jack Ryan, The Rock, Enemy of the State, The Kingdom, The Peacemaker

CHOOSE AN EDGE
Reckless, Intrepid, Vengeful, Mysterious, Resourceful, Seasoned, Cynical, Zealous

CHOOSE A TRADE
Operator, Special Agent, Hacker, Transporter, Psychologist, Civilian, Criminal, Handler, (Analyst), (Cop)

ASSIGN EDGE ABILITIES (MAX: 2, MIN: 0)
Steel, Power, Will, Luck

ASSIGN TRADE ABILITIES (MAX: 2, MIN: 0)
Action, Combat, Tech, Deception

LIVING THE DREAM
Each agent starts with at least one personal complication.  Choose from: 
hunted, amnesic, burdened, brain-washed, naive, haunted, paranoid, betrayed, gone-to-ground, or create your own.

KEEPING IT TOGETHER
The Conspiracy Meter replaces the Danger Meter.  When the Conspiracy Meter reaches ten, start the next round with catastrophic  complications in play (full-scale invasion, martial law, crashing stock market; nuclear, or ecological disaster, etc.)

THREATS
Threats should be set up in a line representing the pursuit or flight of the agent.  Choose one threat as the controlling threat to put at the end of the line.  The nature of the controlling threat should remain hidden until the end.  Unless the agents have some intelligence about it, don't roll it up until the agents are about to face it (for GM-less play).  At least half the threats are count-down threats directly controlled by the controlling threat (staged, or guarding it) or being manipulated by it. 

Each threat has a location for color, complications, and rewards.  If an agent rolls 7+ hits while confronting a threat, he/she gets that threat's reward and gain a new bonus die for it.

Count-down threats don't advance the Conspiracy Meter; instead, when they reach zero, they add new complications (activated security systems, radiological fallout, alert guards, secret police, counter-intel, disinformation, pursuers, hostages, etc.)

\nHere's the rough draft for a playset for Pocket Danger Patrol that I've been working on. I found an interesting, unfinished, playset idea by @Jarvis, called Action Squad, in a 2009 Story Games post. I talked to Jarvis about finishing it up; he gave me the green light. When I looked at the original material, I thought that there were more that one playset idea in it, so I carved off a section. This is Clandestine Patrol (maybe it needs a catchier name!) So, I'm playtesting this on Friday. I thought you might be interested to try it out too. Comments are welcome. Thanks: @John_Harper, @Jarvis, @johnzo, @Seth_Magdich, and @stupidgremlin (Mike?), and others I don't know about or have forgotten.
