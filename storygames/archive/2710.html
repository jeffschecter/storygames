    \n D12s are officially the nerdiest of all nerdy dice.

    In AD&D, the main use of the d12 was for longsword damage against "larger than man-sized" creatures. This made it sort of a special treat.

    I've noticed d12s get mentioned in popular culture when somebody wants to make a knowing allusion to RPGs.

    As it turns out, Lou Zocchi (who else?) actually makes d14s and d16s. They aren't platonic solids, but are dipyramids, similar to ten-siders. There seem to be d18s around, too, but those are even stranger.

    Posted By: John HarperInAgon, the d12 represents the authority of the gods. As is only right and proper.

    \nAccording to Plato's Timaeus (right before he talks about Atlantis, IIRC), the universe is shaped like a d12. The d12 represents the Quintessence, and the structure of the heavens. The other four Platonic solids were tied to the four traditional elements, in a way just begging for a game designer to make a game out of. "You want to swim across the river? Roll your water dice for that. That'd be d20s."

    According to some cosmologists, Plato was right about the universe's shape.

    Posted By: EricYeah. Where are the d14, d16, and d18?
    \nWell, the d14 and d16 are in my dice bag. In copious quantities, for the latter. Pretty certain nobody has yet manufactured a d18--the huge, unlabeled, unequal-sided, bouncing-ball-rubber things that someone linked to don't count, IMHO.\n
    As they are neither regular polyhedrons nor correspond to standard counting bases, I fear there is little hope for them. A d16 can be nicely simulated with 5 different coins, though, should you have the need.
    \nAny polyhedron with an even number of sides can be made as two pyramids sharing a base, a la a d10. Any polyheddron with a number of sides divisible by 4 can be made as two pyramids sharing a base, with their edges lined up. They may not be strictly fair, but they can certainly be close enough for our purposes--especially if you presume a random initial toss.

    And wouldn't it just be 4 coins for a d16?\n
    Posted By: iagod12 is the true apex die, not the d20, because you can do a clean incremental progression d4-d6-d8-d10-d12
    \nIt depends on how you're comparing them. When we were developing Four Colors al Fresco, i wanted each die to have roughly the same chance of beating (in this case, rolling less than) the next-smaller die, and vice versa. IOW, i wanted even step sizes. While using integer-sided dice this isn't exactly possible, d3-d4-d6-d8-d10-d12-d16-d20 is actually really close.
