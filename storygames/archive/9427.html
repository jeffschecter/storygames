    \n I played it and found it so good that I made the official french translation, so I might be a little biased ^^

    As far as I'm concerned I think that the rules capture the Sword & Sorcery genre pretty well :
    _all characters are equally competent in combat.
    _the career system allows for a quick character creation and gives the player the tools to build a nice backstory.
    _the advantage/flaw mechanics are tied to the character origins (which is nice for building that backstory too)
    _sorcery is powerful, corrupting and rare.

    To sum it up there is nothing groundbreaking in the BoL rules but once you start to play it just works like a charm. All the people I gamed with get the "feel" of the game once chargen is finished and always play characters that end up being very true to the genre.

    \n Just played it yesterday. Lots of flavor, good feel, extremely quick to get in motion... but. I felt there was very little tension in play. Characters are ridiculously competent, so much so that I never felt much concern that we would fail at anything significant. While this does kind of fit the source material, it wasn't terribly satisfying.

    As is usual with this sort of game, it really seems to be all about the genre trappings and color. If your group brings those and revels in them, it's actually a really good fit. And the material in the text is very evocative and gives you plenty of fuel for it. But I don't feel that the game mechanically brought a great deal to the table in support of play.

    On the other hand, this was 4 guys picking up the game and skimming it. There may be a bunch there that we didn't see.

    ART was perfect.

    \n I haven't run it as much as I'd like. But, then, I don't do S&S gaming as much as I'd like.

    My experience was that it was a straightforward system that didn't break my assumptions about how the genre should operate. It captures the "odd jobs and adventures" feeling of characters like Conan, without bogging down character creation in complicated determinations of their history. I felt it was an ideal light system for sword and sorcery gaming.

    I felt it paired nicely with the interpretation of the genre in Sorcerer and Sword, although it's much more cheerfully all-encompassing.

    I like the sorcery system in principle, but haven't used it in practice.
