    \n alright, I couldn\\'t help myself...

    so here\\'s my first take on hacking DRYH to play Delta Green

    the character-sheet would look like this:

    My code-name is\u2026
    - characters are part of the same cell, so all code-names start with the same letter

    And I am\u2026
    - short description, including agency

    What led you to join Delta Green? (was: What\u2019s been keeping you awake?)
    - events that led the character to join DG, as well as goals
    - gives the GM ideas for allies, foes, missions, problems...
    - allows the player to determine how much his character has already seen

    What\u2019s just happened to you?
    - since the characters are part of the same cell, the answers to this question should probably fit together
    - players must agree whether the action starts while the cell is already on a mission or not

    What\u2019s on the surface?
    - just like in the original

    What lies beneath?
    - just like in the original (maybe focus more on fears)

    What are you determined to do before you die? (was: What\u2019s your path?)
    - the character should have a shot at addressing this

    Discipline
    - just like in the original

    Madness
    - just like in the original

    Stress (was: Exhaustion)
    - as the situation gets more stressful (injury, conspiracy discovered...), the agent\\'s training and determination kick in

    Special Training (was: Exhaustion Talent)
    - something the agent is especially good at

    Dark Power (was: Madness Talent)
    - some kind of horrible power the character has acquired (spell from an ancient tome, taint from a faced creature or phenomenon...)
    - the character may not realize he has this power

    Comments
    - this hack is probably more \u201cgroup-oriented\u201d than the original game

    what do you think?

    any ideas on how to improve this?

    Posted By: hoogI don\\'t really agree with this
    \nWellllllllll... I'll just flesh out my argument, then!

    "What just happened to you?" is the kicker, use it to start the first scene. You could technically rewrite it every time you take a break, even if its just to record what happened to you in the last scene. Putting your orders here doesn't specify a starting scene, and kinda prevents you from starting your character off in media res - it just gives you some vague idea of what's gonna happen in the scenario.

    "What's your path?" is your motivation. Whenever you space out or come back from a break or get confused, you think "what do I do now?" and you look down at your character sheet and say "what's my motivation?"

    And what you've got written down under "What's your path?" tells you. That's why you put your orders there.


    For example, my name is Special Agent Wilson, and I am an FBI agent, working for Delta Green.

    What's the difference between these two pairs of answers:

    1.

    What are your orders?

    Investigate Dale County, look for signs of those missing hikers, and any other suspicious activities.

    What is your path?

    To find my sister who was kidnapped by aliens.


    2.

    What just happened to you?

    I got punched in the face in the bathroom of a truck-stop diner.

    What are your orders?

    Investigate Dale County, look for signs of those missing hikers, and any other suspicious activities.


    The first one sounds like motivation for a long campaign (or several seasons of a tv series...), and the second one gives you adventure right now.
    Truck-stop diner, bathroom, go!

    The reason I discount something like "What is your path? To find my sister..." is that it will just as easily show up in play - unless it comes up in the first adventure, you don't really need to figure it out right away.

    The reason "What are your orders? Investigate Dale County..." can't replace "What just happened..." is because it doesn't put you in the truck-stop diner's bathroom.

    And finally, the reason "What are your orders?" ties into the end is: because once you accomplish the objective, as stated in your orders, or find that impossible, the mission is over. That IS the end.


    So, there you go. If that doesn't convince you, I dunno what will.
    You could always try using all three, if you prefer that. When you get people to play, ask them what they think, before and after the game.

    Hey, hope it goes well!
