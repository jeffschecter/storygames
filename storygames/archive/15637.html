    \n How long is the planned session? I love Geiger Counter but I've sometimes had trouble completing the game with six players, even with a fair amount of pre-session pitch and prep.

    You'll need to get rolling immediately. Have a core concept pre-picked, i.e. "We are going to play a survival horror game about space marines investigating the Roanoke V outpost. It's gone silent."

    I recommend letting the players flesh out their characters but provide a list of twelve names and twelve archetypes that fit your concept for them to pick from, e.g. The Commander, The Recruit, The Perfect Soldier, The Valkyrie, The Deserter, The Prisoner, The Veteran, The Squirrel, The Empath, etc.

    Maybe you mostly just facilitate the game yourself and/or play all the unnamed characters only? Nine will be tough.

    \n At this point, there's a lot of people who've played Geiger more recently than I have, so I'll defer to them. In general, though, I've always found Geiger works best with 4-5 players, with people doubling up on characters rather than trying to play with a huge number of folks. With a large number of characters, you need people to start dying almost immediately (some even taking the Dead condition long before they need to) if the game is going to have a prayer of ending in a single session, and that's harder to make happen if that's somebody's only character ("my guy" / cold dead fingers / etc.).

    I have faith that Geiger (and Mist-Robed Gate, for that matter) can work with large numbers of people, but it's going to take new editions for that to be well supported.
