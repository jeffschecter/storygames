\n Hey, all.

I'm building a little locked-room science fiction LARP set on a space station in the distant, transhuman future. The players will, naturally, be the crew, but I need a list of jobs and skills for the crew to have.

I'm thinking two separate lists: firstly, the Role of the PC and the basic responsibilities of that Role, and then another list containing Skills, things that any PC could potentially have in their background that aren't tied to any Role. Overlap is OK.

For example:

ROLE: Commander; you're responsible for keeping crew morale up, as well as having a basic knowledge of the combat subsystems of the station.

SKILL: Your time on a poorly-maintained mining colony has left you with a greater-than-average comfort in extra-vehicular/microgravity operations.

Thanks for any help!

-AD.

(Edit: I'm also looking for some guidance on my conflict-resolution system over on Praxis.)

    Some ideas, starting with Roles:

    - Industrial Spy: You're a spy for a rival of the company that designed the station's internal ecosystem, posing as a harmless Space Tourist.
    - Cryptologist: Your job is to examine data from the station's radio antenna to filter out noise and determine whether you're receiving data from anywhere other than Mission Control.
    - Poet: you're on the station because the Terran Institute For Innovation in the Arts contributed 30% of the mission's funding. You're supposed to write a 50,000 word epic poem about your experience of space travel.

    Here are some possible Skills:

    - The time you spent providing therapy for the miners who were stuck underground for four months after the infamous Io cave-in has made you an expert on the treatment of Confinement Anxiety.
    - Your years of training and performance as a Zero-G acrobat makes you the best person to perform difficult space walks.
    - Your victories in the worldwide championships of Human vs. AI Lincoln-Douglas debating mean that you can often persuade the ship's computer to divulge information it's supposed to keep hidden.

    \n Mental Health Officer: Most humans find it difficult to adapt to being trapped in the small confines of a space station. Prolonged stay aboard a space station might cause severe anxiety and claustrophobia, which might manifest as destructive, violent and anti-social urges. Your job is to monitor the inhabitants of the station for early signs of the so-called "Space Madness", and administer proper treatment, be it psychotherapy or drugs. When more drastic measures such as confinement are required, you may advise the station commander or security officer, but you may not undertake such measures yourself.

    According to the station protocol, you may declare the captain mentally unfit, but you are not allowed to replace him during such an emergency.

    \nRoles:

    - Comm Officer - You are in charge of the Communication and Data Gathering technology and use in the station. Your authority is only surpassed by the Station Commander.
    - SysOp - The System Operator, in charge of all the computerized systems and wet-ware technology ports.
    - Astro Physicist - Your job is to make the needed calculations to keep the Station in a stable, efficient and convenient orbit around the system's star, taking into account the different celestial bodies, debris and energy surges and fluctuations. Most of the time it is a very boring and uneventful job...


    Skills:

    - Back home, when you were a teenager, your father used to take you to (licensed) hunting expeditions. You turned out to be an amazing shot.
    - You minored in foreign languages and linguistics at the Academy. You are the only one who fluently speaks all the languages that the different Station inhabitants speak.
    - Your secret omni-net addiction has left you the skills of a competent hacker.

    \nChief Accountant/Corporate Overseer: The commander and his lackeys can keep the station running, but you're here on behalf of your corporate masters to make sure they do so efficiently. If they had their way, everyone would be eating synthetic meat with real corn syrup! You must make sure resources are spent reasonably and that a healthy profit margin is maintained at all times. Your brain enhancement allows you to track and annalyze most activities going on at the station, and can access the crew's personal expense reports. You do not, however, have access to classified information.

    Skills:
    * You bought your forged diploma with all the money you made from shuttle races. You know how to navigate anything that has engines, how to keep it from slamming against asteroids, and how to keep your cool during crisis.

    * You are an organically grown replicant. So far, you have been successful at hiding this fact from the humans due to your incredible abilities to learn and adapt. You begin the game with no skills of your own; during the game, you might undertake complex actions with other crew members, who will make use of the various skills available to them. Afterwards, these skills will be available to you as well. You have limited memory capacity, so you will only be able to utilize the skills of the last person or persons you worked with.

    \n Skill: Layabout. You're adept at avoiding work, but looking busy. You don't have any skill yourself. But when you "supervise", you can count as a second skilled person assisting in any skill where another person is already on the job.
    Skill: Lied on your resume. You aren't actually qualified at your primary Role. But you are actually an excellent liar.
    Skill: Nepotism at work. You got put in place here because your uncle is the Vice President of the company that owns a controlling share int he space station (or whatever). If you're put in a dangerous situation, your uncle will give the captain hell. So the captain has to go easy on you.

    Role: AI Psychiatrist. You're in charge of keeping the artificial intelligences functionally normally. Occasionally, their evolutionary algorithms will decay in unusual, unpredictable ways. You're supposed to monitor them, ensure that the AIs don;t make unauthorized access to the network and aren't hiding things from their human users. Of course, each AI is smarter than the smartest human in history, and has a completely alien set of needs and desires. So there's no way you can effectively keep them under control. But that's your job nonetheless.
