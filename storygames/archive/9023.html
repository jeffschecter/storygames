    \n It's like map farming in a video game where baddies respawn and you keep running the same map over and over again killing them for the XPs or loot drops. But scene farming is a purely Narratavist technique in tabletop gaming where you repeatedly return to the same npc(s) over and over again in order to wring every last bit of emotional angst out them that you can... before killing them for the XPs and loot.

    \n\nWolfe \n\n\n
    \n
    \n\nMarch 2009 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n

    It's a new mutation of My Guy syndrome which has mutated to take advantage of indie games that allow players to have directorial control. Where the old strain of My Guy syndrome was characterized by defending your actions with "that's what my guy would do!" the new strain involves framing scenes as often as possible to spotlight some aspect or other of your guy's personality, so as to make it perfectly clear why he does some thing. When framing the scene yourself isn't possible, you find ways to manipulate the events in other scenes so as to emphasize your guy, basically upstaging the intended focus.

    \n

    It's essentially an application of "Show, don't Tell."

    \n Personally, I think we went wrong when all the traditional scene farmers were bought out by Big Corporations. Now it's all genetically-modified, monocultural scenes. Everything's soaked in harsh chemical theories that I'm sure aren't healthy for us, and sure, now you can get the same scene in NYC that you can in Orlando or Boise or Los Angeles, but at what cost? An entire way of life has been destroyed, and I'm not convinced that the quality of the scenes has improved all that much.

    If you want to do any scene farming these days, you end up either working for the corporations or you're just puttering around in your backyard. You certainly can't make a living at it. It's sad, really.
