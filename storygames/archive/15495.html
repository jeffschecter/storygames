\n The Edwin and Terry Murray Collection of Role-Playing Games at Duke University has finally been cataloged and is open for research. This is a 10,000 item collection of gaming stuff in my home town and I'm pretty excited about checking it out. I'll be talking to the curator about accessions, since there are big, obvious holes in the collection. I want to know if they plan on growing it beyond the gift or not.

Here's the inventory.

Stuff I'm excited about seeing: Tollenkar's Lair, White Bear and Red Moon, and twelve packs of 15mm Traveller miniatures from Martian Metals. If you're curious, take a look and tell me what you see that is interesting, rare, or inspirational.

    \n
    \n Oh man, it is pretty incredible. Do these guys must have been junior archivists, because many of the items are in pristine condition. I pored over an original Empire of the Petal Throne box set from 75-76 that looked brand new. Like it dropped out of a time machine, I kid you not. Better than mint copies of all the original white box books, Runequest miniatures (!), an assortment of stuff they thought would be interesting that they dragged out to show off.

    To access the collection you just need to call in advance because it is all kept offsite. Give them a day and tell them what you want to look at.

    The Murray brothers are still collecting and will donate more to increase the collection, so Duke is not interested in other materials right now.

    It was a fun event and well attended. Clinton won the trivia contest, which was super hard.
