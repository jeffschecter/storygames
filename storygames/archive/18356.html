\n You know, stuff like shared narrative control or scene framing/resolution. Usually, a player needs to shift from the in-character perspective to address these story elements, but there must me some cases where playing with the narrative is something triggered by the character's behavior or current condition. I'm looking for something like TSoY's bringing down the pain, but perhaps more broad. For example, the game may focus on task resolution, but, if a character is somehow wounded or hampered in some way, he or she is forced to act purely through scene resolution. Or, let's say there is a GM that can occasionally take control of a player's character, but, when that happens, that player can set the scene, for example.

    @Rafu Kind-of, I'm not sure. Is it like in Monsterheart's move "turn someone on"?
    \nNo. What Rafu is referring to is the basic Apocalypse World/Monsterhearts interaction where the MC, instead of inventing some bit of the fiction herself, instead turns it into a question and asks that question of a PC. These questions, like everything else in the game, are directed at the PC, not the player, and so the fictional contribution (originating from the player, obv.) comes out through the lens of the PC's worldview. It's in-character worldbuilding, except of course that the PC does not see it as worldbuilding, just describing what is already the case.
