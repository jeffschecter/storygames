    When used in a disapproving manner, "geeky" means one thing and one thing only: the speaker believes the subject is an inappropriate thing to be excited about or interested in.

    It does not reflect the subject's complexity, laser-sharkiness or anything else. It is purely socially constructed and simply reflects an instinctual reaction of intellectual squick.

    That said, the collective social construction of geekiness does tend to spiral around a few attributes, including but not limited to complexity, laser-sharkiness, escapism, utility, gender and sexuality, and a bunch of other stuff. Over time, the "geek community" not only created the other meaning of the word "geek," but (more to this paragraph's point) self-selected towards people who tend to be interested in systematizing things.

    One of their favorite things to systematize, and to argue about the systematization of, is the definition of the word "geek."
