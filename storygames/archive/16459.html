    \n It looks like I am unavailable on Tues 5/15, also Sun 5/13. Other dates remain good for me. I'm in Manhattan and can just recommend cafes in midtown as play spaces.

    Places to eat not so far from you: Village Yokocho near St. Mark's Place for yakitori, and Burp Castle for beer. Both get special notice for the murals; Village Yokocho is painted to resemble a Japanese street scene, with rooftops inside the room to complete the illusion, and given that Dave Trampier was hired because Gygax liked his drawings on the walls of a local hangout, there is a great alternate universe in which D&D instead emerged from NYC and the art thus had more Burp Castle-inspired scenes of drunken carousing. Also worth checking out, but without murals, are Nyonya for Malaysian in Little Italy and Spicy & Tasty for Szechuan in Flushing, Queens.

    Everyone in New York has their own favorite dim sum and pizza place. Mine are Dim Sum Go Go (or Golden Unicorn if you want the rolling-tray experience) in Manhattan's Chinatown and Sal and Carmine's on the Upper West Side. Mentioning these will cause people to argue with you, which will generate more recommendations.
