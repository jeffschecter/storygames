    Posted By: northerainAre the different ''classes'' really supposed to be one-offs or is it just that there's only one of each per story?
    \nWell, the book says something like "there are more hardholders in the world, but you're the only Hardholder". I take that to mean that there may be other dudes with holdings and gangs, and we can call them hardholders if we wanna, but that doesn't mean they have the attributes and moves of the Hardholder "class". To draw a D&D parallel, there is no Monster Manual entry for taking the "Hardholder" class from the PHB and constructing a "Level 5 Elite Hardholder" for the players to fight. Other hardholders are NPCs that we look at through crosshairs just like any other NPC. When they are narrated as shouting orders from the palisade, they don't use the Hardholder Leadership move (though they might be using a custom move from the Front they represent, I suppose).

    \n I think of it like Firefly. Wash is a pilot, and that's something people say. "Meet Wash, our pilot." Mal is an operator, and that's just a word you'd use to describe him; "operator" doesn't have a concrete meaning the way "pilot" does. The concrete word for Mal is "captain," but that's not what his character class would be. Inara's a companion, and that means something even more specific than "pilot" does.

    To the people living in Apocalypse World, "angel" might be any of those three. "Meet Kal, our angel." "Yeah, Kal, he's an angel all right." "Kal's not just some skinpatch. He's an angel, one of the last that Mercy certified."

    There might be any number of NPCs running around that Apocalypse World's residents would call angels, in exactly the same ways. "Meet Dremmer, our angel," and so on. Lachek's exactly right: you can call them angels in character, but they aren't mechanically angels.

    \n John, I'm almost certain that you're misreading me.

    Posted By: Will Hindmarch
    Put another way, things should get more interesting with every roll*, not just 7-9. If the reward for 10+ is "wait, now, while someone else plays," I think the system is subtly undermined.

    A side point here is that one of the strengths of the system in AW is that the most statistically likely outcomes\u2014that is, results from 7-9\u2014are still unpredictable and engaging. I'm pedantic, though, about keeping scales facing the same direction whenever possible, so that, for example, high rolls made by players should always be good for the players or always be bad for the players in a given system. To state the obvious, lest I be misunderstood, I know that this is my personal bias and AW isn't obligated to reflect it.
