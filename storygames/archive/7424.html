\n So I'm seeing a lot of game designers getting called out on these board to justify the games they've created. It is a bit of a "Explain to me why you created a game I don't like?" often with a harsh, offended tone thrown in for good measure.

WTF? Why do you have to justify your game? To me? To him? To her? To anyone? If I don't like it, fine. I don't like it. I tried Coke Zero one time and thought it sucked. I didn't go to a forum somewhere and demand the chemist explain to me why he/she/they created a drink I didn't like. I just stopped buying it. Hell, I even gave mine away after a few drinks, and the next person in line seemed to like it just fine. I never told them them how much they sucked for liking a drink that sucked.

So designers, what is going on here?

What other product out there has to justify its own existence?

    \n Well, in the Big World of the Internet, where things aren't just Serious, they're Internet Serious, we have an interesting thing going on.

    1) People can post their likes or dislikes of games in new threads as they see fit. In fact, I wish this happened more (maybe I should make it happen; although I tend to focus on likes more).

    2) Many poeple who are asking the questions know that the author posts/reads here, and thus are looking to start an open forum with them, inviting them into something that looks like a MMA octagon crossed with the set of Inside the Actor's Studio with James Lipton.

    Luckily, I never really see it get too flamey, or where the original thread poster is out to stir shit or stab the author in the face. Even the "I don't want to play your games, Jason Morningstar!" thread was started by a friend of Jason's (in fact, the horror from that thread from my point of view was people acting like it's *wrong* to not like Jason's games).

    In any case, it's a cross between an interview and a question and answer session. These questions can be asked directly to the author in private, and indeed I'm sure they are at times, but some times the poster wants to hear from The Crowd, not *just* the author.

    In any case, from a modding/admin point of view, they almost always are more explanatory than flamey, more a conversation than a soapbox, so I'm totally fine with their existence here.
