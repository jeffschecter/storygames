\n I realized I never posted this on S-G, but it might be a cute little term to add to the vocabulary:

Mechaphor
noun. A game mechanic acting as a metaphor for a concept in your game's setting, philosophy or general theme.

Example:

In Castle Falkenstein, the system uses cards instead of dice because cards are a sign of more civilized culture than the thuggish brutes who roll dice in alleys. This is a mechaphor for the aloof Victorian attitude.

In Agon, you roll attack dice in your right hand and defense dice in your left hand, just as you would a sword and shield. This is a visceral mechaphor for brutal ancient combat.

In Do: Pilgrims of the Flying Temple, a bag with black and white stones allows each player's decisions in the short-term to have long-term consequences for the whole group. This is a mechaphor for karma.

    \n The knife ritual in Mist-Robed Gate is a pretty good mechaphor too, symbolizing emotional or physical violence with an actual weapon which you can ultimately stab into people's character sheets when you kill them.

    Or, like, in It's Complicated where you physically draw lines between different characters' abstract representations to illustrate growing connections between them.

    Some things cross the line into miming or actual physical representations, right? Like when you remove Legos off the other players' mechs when you shoot them in Mechaton, it's still not as if you actually shot them off, but it's a much closer physical analog than rolling your sword dice in your right hand. Or if, say, every player in Dead Weight brought a backpack and filled it up with actual objects over the course of playing the game. Mechaphors lie somewhere between that and just writing down the bag's contents and calculating encumbrance, yeah?

    \nDead Weight had me realize a grid backpack would solve the annoying math of inventory managing in a dungoen crawling game. Sweden even have large silvery 5 SEK coins and smaller, thicker 10 SEK coins that could very well represent the weight: value rating of silver or gold. Also since the backpack is so Diablo-like it might be possible to have long unwieldy weapons like longbows and pikes actually be a pain in the ass to carry as secondary weapons since their shape (perhaps like L-shaped Tetris blocks) fucks up a big part of the inventory.

    One could even have the inventory space be the adventurers height times his strength to somehow encourage long wiry dudes to fight with bows and spears and shorter beefier ones with crossbows and axes and carry the gold.



    Next year the small copper coins will be taken out of circulation: time to stockpile!
