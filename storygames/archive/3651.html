As I just announced over on my blog, Shock: version 1.1 is now at the printer. I've been working on this for several months, cleaning up the text, rewriting rules, and laboring under the editorial scrutiny of Ben Lehman and Cauley Greene to polish the game up and get it to where I want it to be.

\n

If you've turned in an error bounty, that means that you'll be getting your long-promised game soon! If you wrote one of the reviews on RPGnet last week for Sci Fi Review week, you'll be getting it, too!

\n

But if you're not one of those people, you can pre-order the game very simply: by ordering it as normal at the glyphphress or IPR. Your copy of 1.0 will be sent out immediately with a signed copy of 1.1 following after Gen Con in a month, or of course you can pick it up a the Playcollective booth along with your copy of Shooting the Moon, Capes, The Cold City Companion, Dictionary of Mu, and Afraid.

\n

I really look forward to seeing some more AP from this new, clearer edition.

    The following takes place in late June, 2007. Only the names have been changed to protect the innocent.

    \n
    \n

    Oshuajay: So, Vincent, is Afraid done?
    \n Incentvay: Yeah! I'm just writing it now.
    \n Oshuajay: It'll be done in time for Gen Con?
    \n Incentvay: Oh, yeah, totally. I just have to finish writing it.
    \n Oshuajay: And that's not a lot?
    \n Incentvay: Naw, naw, I, well, no.

    \n
    \n

    And here we are, today.

    \n

    Jason, I got 10 responses or so. Almost all very very useful. One or two were merely useful. Some were extraordinary, like Ron's AP an Jono's review (though that was way late in the game). Ben Lehman has also been extraordinarily helpful, and between him and his girlfriend, they probably represent the greatest expertise in the game outside of... well, me.

    \n

    Haakon, I'm glad you liked the game! There's a change or three in the rules now that might punch things up a little bit, too. Antags get 13 Credits for instance, and there are canonized Audience rules, which means you can have fewer Protags, if you like. Minuti\xc3\xa6 have the same huge effect at first, but get decreasingly important in the course of a Conflict. Stuff like that.
