    \n No. (not a response to Hoog) Because anyone who's any good at improv is going to know all the rules that any small press RPG is trying to hammer into the average gamer's head already. Whether it's Prime Time Adventures or Polaris or Sons of Liberty, these games are going to seem really, really restrictive compared to, say, Bus Stop or even a more strict improv game, like Symphony.

    On the other hand, if your friend is the kind of person who can put up with a few false starts and a group of people who don't know how to stop making sense, then she'll be able to lord it over them with barely any effort in just about ANY small press game story-oriented. Her theatrical skills will help her shine in ways the rules in these books are only beginning to cover.

    Here's an example:
    Prime Time Adventures uses the "Say Yes" rule of classic improv troops to get normal gamers to work together when putting their game in motion.

    Here's another, it seems that Mortal Coil uses a variation on free association when helping players put together world-building sessions.

    Short answer: you want a small press game that relies on improv ideas? Throw a brick.
    But if your friend is used to people who can really improv, you're going to have to find some way to soften the blow of dealing with normal gamers... who don't always realize how much improv they need to do, anyway.
