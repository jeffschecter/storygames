\n While convincing my family that gaming overall is a cool and acceptable pastime and hobby has run into a few bumps along the way, one game I've had great success with is Happy Birthday, Robot! I've played it with all my siblings, with extremely high levels of silliness - see here for details - and I played a couple of rounds yesterday with my brother, using the two-player variant rules.

Our first story turned into a dark comedy of sorts, foreshadowing the Mega Man series (full story here). It's funny how little things can set the tone of the story; when my brother started a sentence with 'You are a perfect creation', the rest of the story practically wrote itself. Mad science dialog is fun!

For our second story, we used a different starting sentence. I've recently gotten my entire family hooked on the Dresden Files, and I remembered someone suggesting, "I need help, Harry Dresden!" as an idea in a comment on Daniel's blog, so we rolled with it. This story came out rather sillier - it was the flamingos that did it, I think.

One of the great things about Happy Birthday, Robot! is how quickly it plays - I'd played with my brother before, so we both knew the rules which made things go faster, but even so, we came up with both of these stories in under an hour, including the time spent giggling at each others' additions. I am a fan.
