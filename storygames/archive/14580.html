    \n Five Aspects, 5 Fate Points at most. If you want to make play more immediately more "Compelling", (literally), maybe even start people with only Aspects/2 rounded down Fate Points: two.

    I used options 1 and 3 from here: whenever I ran SotC (reduce the number of stress boxes, change how consequences work), and felt that worked best for me - a base of three stress for Pulp action and a base of two for most other genres.

    As I recall, the Evil Hat guys were doing the same within weeks of SotC's release.

    Note, I still haven't managed to wade through most of the DF:RPG yet despite pre-ordering it and continuing to play a hacked Fate 2.0 now and then, so those two options combined may be exactly what Ogre is referring to.

    \n No-stress, all-consequences is the way to go, IMO. Every hit matters and adds to the narrative. It's just more exciting.

    If I have to use stress tracks (like I did with the FATE edition of The Kerberos Club), I do it Diaspora-style. Check a box, backfill all boxes to the left of it. If one of those boxes would be checked again, it rolls up to the next empty box. For me, it's a nice compromise between stress-as-hit-points and SotC-style stress. (With stress-reducing consequences, of course.)

    \n I think I recall some people from the yahoo group having some success in the Fate 2.0 early days experimenting with Aspects before/after, whether you're talking about +1, +2 or re-rolls.

    I ran a game for a few sessions where tags would get you +2 beforehand OR +1 after and up to one re-roll. I didn't get to play it enough to make a final decision, but at least a couple players seemed to like the "gambling" of that mode. Next time I run Fate I might try it again.

    It took away some of that, *roll* *grimace* "I've got three Fate points and can easily justify these three Aspects" *tag*/*tag*/*tag*: Auto-win! That's something I don't always care for in some modes of Fate - when the dice-roll doesn't seem to matter much or create much suspense relative to how many Fate points a character has and how powerful a +2 bonus is relative to the Fudge bell.

    edit: I know Fate isn't Apocalypse World but that "know what your bonuses are beforehand/ROLL/what you see is what you get, no take-backs, no do-overs, now deal with some irrevocable shit" sure as hell works for me in that game. So I suspect there's a hack/mod for Fate out there that does something similar and which would also yield some fruit.
