\n I was eating lunch this afternoon when I suddenly had a small thought.

What if, in Primetime Adventures, you earned Fan Mail normally, but you could not spend it on yourself? What if you could only spend it on others behalf?

Jesse

    \n Then there wouldn't be any reward for you in getting fan mail. In other words, when I give you fan mail, it's because I like what you're doing with the narrative and I want you to do more of what you're doing, not that I want you to help other people do more of what they're doing. Why wouldn't I give the fan mail to them if I wanted to help them do more of what they're doing? I like the versatile way it is now, where you can spend it on either side of anything.

    \n The Mountain Witch kind of works like that, with the Trust mechanic. You get Trust from other people, but you can only spend it on rolls that involve the person who gave you that Trust. You can aid them, change the narrative outcome of a roll they're involved in, or betray them.
