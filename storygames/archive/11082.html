\n Considering that I have yet to run or play a game of 3:16 \u2014 Carnage Among the Stars, I sure am obsessed with ways to hack it. First there was the whole Nazis-killing-Commies-in-Vietnam thing \u2014 the less said about, the better, I think the consensus is \u2014 and then there's something FigureFour and I talked about briefly over on #playnow (find a pickup game today!).

There's already been discussion of changing the default setting of 3:16 from "space marines kill all life in the universe" to "space marines fight for the Imperium in a grim future where there is only war." But what about taking things one step further and incorporating some more of the 40k universe?

Can 3:16 be hacked to play Battlefleet Gothic?

I'm not a game designer and I don't even play one on TV, so you'll have to excuse me if my ideas are vague and unformed. But it seems to me, even in my relative ignorance, that it would be fairly easy to do. The ships involved, from the biggest battleships to the cruisers to the fighters, all possess a certain number of kills they can absorb while the weapons with which they're equipped work pretty much the same way they do in the regular game. Ranges are likewise the same, though scaled up to accommodate what's happening.

Am I nuts to think this? Does anyone have a clearer idea of how to implement the ruleset to model this kind of battle?
