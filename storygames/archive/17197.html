    \n I played it twice at Gen Con GoD, once with four people I didn't know because it was a new-to-me game and I didn't have anything to do, and once the day after with my friends because I wanted to show them how freaking cool it was. And then I went and bought it, as did several of my friends.

    I pitch it as "Fiasco meets Pandemic". It shares some superficial elements with Fiasco for sure, as well as the overall scene structure and freeformy/GM-less-ness. However, since it's cooperative (the group plays against the game for a good outcome) instead of competitive (people judge each others' characters and try to give bad outcomes to characters who deserve it, or just for fun), it has a very different dynamic.

    The only thing that worries me is replayability with the same group, given that despite the different "playsets" the game will always have the same central theme of saving the world, secrets and fears shared between characters, etc. It's definitely worth picking up even if you just end up playing it a few times as a pick-up game, though.

    \n Hey folks!

    I'm Mark Diaz Truman from Magpie Games. Thank you for the interest and nice comments. This thread warms my heart.

    @Axkidson - Yeah, Harm can be pretty rough at times. It's a slippery slope that usually pushes characters toward death pretty quickly. The hope is that you get to go out like a hero...

    And I also share your concerns about the "roll-play" that the game can sometimes produce. In early playtests, people rushed through things a little quickly. When I facilitate games, I make sure and ask the following questions after each roll "What happened? How did the threat get worse/better, and what did your character do?"

    @lacheck - I'm thrilled that you had a good time at GenCon and thanks for buying the book. One of the ways we're hoping to increase the replay value is to introduce some really different playsets. We've got dungeons, superheroes, and horror planned for the Companion book, and I'm hopeful those will keep the game fresh. Oh, AND DANGER PATROL (@John_Harper).

    @all - We should have the PDF up on RPGNow soon, but we have to finish shooting all the videos (accessible through QR codes) and those have taken us a bit longer than planned. Retailer copies are going out to stores this week (we finally got all our printing issues sorted out), and we should have videos finished soon. If you'd like to order copies through our website, we'll get them out to you right away and if you're dying to get a PDF of the game, feel free to drop us an email at info@magpiegames.com and we'll see what we can get worked out.

    So excited that you guys are excited!
