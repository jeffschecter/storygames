\n With the amount of self-published and experimental games, I am wondering how many story games are there that do not tell a story of characters. Instead it focuses on collaborative creation of an event or a place.

*Are there any games that focus on a life of a estate/village/world? What, if I wanted to collaborate to create backstory for a place, or decide what happened in that place in few months?
*Are there games that let players create some important event in town/nation/world? If I wanted to explore/create a war that spanned years and affected nations? What if I wanted to create a neighboring nation for my campaign?

I am asking, because I have this idea of playing some grand campaigns, but using different games for different aspects of it. And a lot of important events are beyond the scale of few adventurers. I would like to compile a list of such games and give guidelines for how they can be used to achieve that. But outside of few games that I am aware of, I am hitting a wall. So I am asking you, my fellow storygamers, to point me in the right direction.

If you know of such game, post its title (with link to where one can get the game, if possible) and a brief overview of what the game is about and how it plays. I will populate this topic with the games I find, but your help would be greatly appreciated.

    \nVox Populi by James Mullen

    The game explores what happens to a kingdom/nation after the heroes have saved it. It has some similarities to "Model UN". One player introduces an issue that the kingdom is facing and others give possible outcomes. Then a vote is taken and the majority rules which outcome is put into action. The game can be used to (re?)create kingdoms and nations after a big, historical event. Instead of having only two players voice their solutions (as per rules), any number of players can give their own opinion and vote can be taken as per Quick and Easy voting for Normal people by youtuber CGP Gray.

    \n Microscope and The Quiet Year are the best examples I know of.

    Microscope has you all playing a timeline, rather than people. You have a start and end, then in a non-linear fashion ask and answer questions to flesh out history. It's great for making a story of worlds, empires, everything at a grand scale which only sees you looking at a personal level for the occasional snapshot in time.

    The Quiet Year is amazing. In 2-3 hours you're making a community and determining whether or not they survive the winter. You use a piece of paper to draw a map which changes and evolves as you go. Each player adds to it, ideally without any words. You use dice to measure time taken on projects, tokens to represent contempt at someone's decision for where the community goes and a deck of cards for events. It's really cheap online and a way I've introduced non-RPG people into story games.
