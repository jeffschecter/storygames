Sudden inspiration while editing a lab activity about water pressure!

What if all players at the table create a Patriot, and each scene has a different Tory player, determined by the cards in everybody's hand? Perhaps at the end of each scene, the guy with the lowest card remaining gets to frame the next scene and will choose which Objective it will addres. Everyone is dealt their hand of three cards, the scene-framer frames the scene, and then whoever's new hand has the lowest card (I imagine it's one of those, "who has an ace? a two? a trey" things) becomes the Tory player? (This would also ensure that the Tory Player has at least one low card, and can't immediately end the scene.)

This would make the Secret Objective difficult; perhaps everyone draws a Secret Objective at the start, and can reveal it in any scene where they are playing Tory?

The first scene needs to be framed by someone (picking between "chase scene," "big fight," and "explosions"), but the Tory player could easily be determined by the initial hand.

With the Tory player selected after the scene frame, there's also the slight problem that the Patriot that won't be played will somehow be central to the scene-as-framed. Would that be a problem? Would the Patriot still be in the scene, just not played by their normal player, or would they step out? Is that something that the Tory player can declare as they take up control of the adversity?

Here's the big question (probably not THE big question): would finding out that you're the Tory player in charge of the scene's adversity after the scene is framed be a let down? Would you get all fired up to do something and then wham, all the sudden you can't? Alternately, would framing the scene not knowing which Patriot will be suddenly taken out of the action add an entertaining uncertainty?

    Actually, Mark, I'm thinking it goes:
    a) at end of scene, lowest card gets to narrate a "cut scene" that sets up the next scene (like in video games of this ilk)
    b) deal out cards
    c) cut scene narrated, explaining how PCs got from where they were to where the action is going to be, and stakes for the upcoming scene are set
    d) Tory player selected.

    So this would work a great deal like Judson's final scene frame in our playtest: he narrated right through kidnapping the wife and getting her to the governor's mansion, and play started when you set foot on the grounds.
