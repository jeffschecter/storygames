\n We were watching the HBO show Westworld, which if you haven't watched you should, but in the show people are in a very advanced simulation theme park of the wild west. Essentially they are gaming as a wild west characters.

It got me thinking, are there games that are patterned after the idea that the player characters are themselves knowingly playing a game? Like, you are players playing a game? As you are the foremost brain trust for all things story games, I thought I'd ask.

    \n I can't remember this one's name, but there was a game about 10 years ago where you played office workers. In your off-time, you could log into a sort of MMORPG (which was like D&D), and it was quite explicit that you were intended to play a D&D character in there AS your office worker character. You weren't k_man321 playing an elven druid, you were k_man321's character, Max the lazy floor manager, playing an elven druid.

    I don't think it was ever published for sale, but it was an entertaining read. I have my own hack of it somewhere in my notes, if I look back far enough (I wanted more modern mechanics to run it with).
