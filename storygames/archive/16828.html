    \n I can only comment on my experiences, but in every hobby I've ever had and in every career/job I've ever had, I've been one of very few (sometimes the only) woman. I've only had one slightly sexist boss, yet I got the better of him from day one and that was when I was a very naive college kid. Otherwise I've never encountered anything negative, if anything men were actually pleased there was a female around for a different perspective on things, and to break up the monotony, as long as that person was really there because she was interested. By that I mean, I wasn't someone's "drug along for the ride air-head girlfriend" who was bored and just killing time, but instead I was usually more into the game (board, RPG, video, cards) than most people there, and I chose to be there.

    This started long ago when I was a kid, and now days I run game rooms and mini conventions where I coordinate GMs, almost all of them are male. I chalk up the mostly male table top gamers to some of the same reasons IT positions are overwhelmingly held by males, there are reasons but nothing misogynic/sexist/underhanded/or otherwise wrong, and some day this will balance out more. However, I'm aware that female gamers for console/video and online/computer games are going strong and it's almost a 50/50 gender split at this time, I also used to be one. So I'm a little incredulous that anyone could be so ignorant as to be threatened by females interested in their hobby, and be misogynic or sexist now days, when I've never seen it in the past.

    However, if it happens, and isn't just being flirty or banter btwn fellow gamers, this is wrong and should be stopped. I applaud your support to try to stop this poor behavior and encourage more positive behavior, I just have never experienced anything except what I described above, which was well meaning but trying to be historically accurate.
