\n So, after reading the results and the resulting discussion related to the Little Game Chef contest, it has got me to thinking: how could I make my game of Victorian-esque drawing room comedy, Quite, Indubitably more of an actual roleplaying game, as opposed to a parlor game?
...not to mention making it simpler?

After having devised it, even though it was thrown together almost on the spur of the moment, I am kind of fond of the concept behind it.
I am thinking, if anything, keeping the four narrative abilities and their connection to card suits, and forgoing the dice. The Diction Cards would now be individual cards (which would make this a much more involved game), where words would specifically tied to a particular suit.
A primary Narrator (the Game Master), would be using the standard deck of playing cards to lay out situations based on their suits, that would represent either people the characters meet, situations they are in, or discussions they are having (again, trying to make it feel like an Oscar Wilde play or a Jeeves and Wooster story). The characters would choose their narrative ability to deal with it. Matching suits and colors would gain so many points to overcoming the situation, while adding Diction cards of certain suits would add or subtract from that score.
Maybe having a rotating Game Master, much like Robin Laws' RUNE game?

Still, I would like to hear from anyone who bothered wading through the verbose morass that was my entry. Anyone?
