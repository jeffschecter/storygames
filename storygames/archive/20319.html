\n After laying the groundwork with some good conversations at Dexcon and Gencon, I've decided to move forward with trying to pull together an academic book proposal for a "Forge retrospective" that will serve the double purpose of exploring ideas about gaming that emerged at the Forge and serving as a case study for the field of game studies of a "community of production." I know that there has been a lot of conversation here lately about the Forge and its intellectual legacy, so I thought I'd make a pitch for participation. The call for chapters is available, and I encourage you--if you're interested, and an academic publication is something that would be worthwhile to you--to submit a proposal in the next few weeks.

I'm looking forward to your submissions!
