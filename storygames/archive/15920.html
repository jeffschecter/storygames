    \n(Edit: To future generations who read this: This post doesn't explain how you must treat a 7-9 result when you act under fire in Apocalypse World. It explains an alternate way to think about it to alleviate a particular problem some people have in play. If the move works for you as-is, hooray!)

    Here's where the trouble lies, I bet:
    Posted By: skinnyghost"in this moment, right now, what's an additional cost that I can attach to the results?"
    \nDon't do that! Trying to come up with an additional cost is stressful! I know, because I totally tried it that way and it stressed me out and I didn't like it. So I stopped.

    Instead, do this: When someone is doing something under fire, there's fire. Duh, right. What is it? By the time anyone is rolling this move, the concrete fictional details of the character's action and the "fire" they're acting under (or enduring) should be crystal clear to everyone. MC, think of it this way: The fire is something bad that may come to pass.

    Now, when they hit the 7-9 result, that bad thing, the fire that you have formed concretely in you mind, it doesn't come to pass the way we feared it might. A 7-9 is a hit! The PC succeeded. So, hooray!

    But, it's not a 10+ result. The PC succeeded, but there's a cost. This doesn't have to be a brand new cost! If you're stumped and stressed for heaven's sake don't try to think of something new and exciting.

    Just choose this cost instead: The fire takes effect in a lesser way (for the PC).
    How does the lesser effect manifest? Choose one:
      \n
    • The thing you said was the fire happens just like you said, but its effects are weak.
      • \n
    • Look at your list of MC moves and say one.
      • \n
    \n"The cannibal splits you open with a swipe of his machete!"
    "Shit, no I get out of the way!" 7-9:
      \n
    • They get out of the way of the cannibal's machete! Instead of taking 3-harm they take 1-harm or 0-harm and make the harm move.
    • They get out of the way of the cannibal's machete! But it catches their gun and rips it from their hands.
    • They get out of the way of the cannibal's machete! But they fall backwards through a window into the river.
    \nHow's that? Any stress relief? Ask me questions and I can say more stuff.

    \n Here's one from my game last night.

    The Battlebabe, Problems, has been hurt for a while. He has a bit of a transitional moment, in a safe place, with the Angel, Grip. Grip is out of supplies, but wants to help. He's had some very bad luck with his Healing Touch (so far, people keep dying). He and the Battlebabe talk a little bit and Problems decides to let Grip lay on hands.

    Grip rolls, gets a 7-9 result. Acts under fire from Problems' brain. The narrative part is easy - Problems' brain is a mess of labyrnthine corridors and Grip feels more and more lost in his mind as he tries to find the place that'll let him heal his friend.     \n Problems, for me, stem from the first thought or impulse, the simplest and most straightforward one, isn't always the best one. Sometimes I'll go for the easy win with "You get what you want but you / someone gets hurt in the process. 1 harm." and that doesn't always feel grabby or interesting enough. It doesn't snowball, it stops, you know?

    For reference:

    When you try to seduce or manipulate someone, tell them what you want and roll+hot. For NPCs: on a hit, they ask you to promise something first, and do it if you promise. On a 10+, whether you keep your promise is up to you, later. On a 7\u20139, they need some concrete assurance right now. For PCs: on a 10+, both. On a 7\u20139, choose 1:
    \u2022 if they do it, they mark experience
    \u2022 if they refuse, it\u2019s acting under fire What they do then is up to them.

    and

    When you do something under fire, or dig in to endure fire, roll+cool. On a 10+, you do it. On a 7\u20139, you flinch, hesitate, or stall: the MC can offer you a worse outcome, a hard bargain, or an ugly choice.

    I read, in a lot of cases, "worse outcome" to mean "success with an additional or unexpected cost" which I often have to think up on the spot, which puts me in this awkward position.

    \n I think one of the easiest things to do, if you're trying to think up a good 7-9 results and don't have one right away, is to ask for more detail. More detail about what the characters are doing, how they feel about it, have they done this before and if so what happened, how exactly this thing they are doing works (especially if it's supernatural/weird), etc. Y'know, if you're hesitant to say something concrete, ask a question. This gives you a minute of space, as well as ideas for what could happen, based on the fiction itself. Maybe something another player says gives you an idea you can use, or maybe not, and the detail just adds to the verisimilitude.

    Another thing, which is a bit more pre-emptive is to cultivate apocalyptica. Don't just think up threats and disaster areas, but keep considering at various points during the game how things could go wrong for characters, what sorts of moral dilemmas they would have problems with (which is stuff you can ask them questions about), what would various NPCs think about what they are doing and how could they mess things up, etc. And then when a miss or a 7-9 comes up, you have already been thinking about this stuff and you can call on it for situations.
