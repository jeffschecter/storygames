\n Howdy y'all.

Got an interesting problem for your consideration I'm looking for some suggestions for a quite large group that will soon be starting a new game/system...

We have a wide variety of likes and experience with roleplaying, and range from early 20's to nearly 40 We're just coming off a year and a half campaign of Exalted that was amazingly fun - about a session a week!

C: rookie - played dozen sessions of Exalted. Doesn't like the dice/rules, just says what she wants to happen and lets others roll, loves story
relative newbie Advanced D&D. gonzo high fantasy fan.
H: some exposure - WoD and Exalted. Gets majorly in character, loves following stories and builds on other character's activities. Loves being the sidekick.
*J: "rookie" of about 30 odd sessions of Exalted. Tends to be contrary and was the group Necromancer - also the doctor/healer in a bunch of "heroes"... lots of laughs out of that though
K: Indie-game fan. Plays for the narrative and character discovery through play.
*M1: Veteran. Burning Wheel, Reign, WoD, Pendragon, etc. Driving player, loves intimate, character driven plotting.
M2: Played everything under the sun Details driven player. Also loves being in-character all the time.
*S1: old veteran, Call of Cthulu, Runequest, WoD, etc. RulesLawyer + loves crunch. Driving player
S2: some exposure. Pathfinder, WoD, etc. Loves gonzo.
*T: WoD and Exalted only. Political intrigue driving player. Also somewhat sociopathic characters

So, thats generally us. The 4 starred ones are the "driving players" as I call them. They are the ones that tend to hog the spotlight a lot and/or drive the actions of the players at the table.

We ended the last game as the party pretty much was breaking into 2 distinct groups that basically were too far away and had such different goals that they either were not interacting or pretty much social PvP which wasn't that comfortable at our table.

Anyway, we are all enjoying the group dynamic and having a ball, and want to continue on, and are now starting to look for another game to play.

Major requirements for potential games:
- handles anywhere 3-9 players at a single session.
- can cope with players dropping in or out at different sessions. (might miss up to a couple of months of game at a time)
- not too crunchy... although they handled Exalted really well, its at the very far limit of crunch and we houseruled it a lot to simplify.
- can do dungeon crawl style, up to political intrigue, or small scale organisational (merchant house owners, spy ring etc) games

yep, nothing too hard right?
We've mainly played nWoD Mage and Exalted. With some Apocalypse World and Sage of the Icelanders thrown in a few times. Also regularly play boardgames (which will keep us going till we get next game up and running...)

So - ideas?

    Honestly, the GM is mostly backseat all the time, except the first few sessions to kick everything/setup situation.

    K and S1 used to play games of Mage and Exalted in another group years before

    Icelanders was actually done during the playtesting for that game - and considering this is in Iceland, it was a real hit. Everyone loved it and we got some amazing results out of it - Actually that was one of the seeds of this group here in Reykjavik Played a few times with different people until the first core group K, S1, M1.

    Apoc world next - that lasted around 6 sessions of just pure badassery 6 players. Lots of comparisons to movie "Doomsday" and game "Fallout"

    Word got around and we did a one-shot Mage game for K, H, M1, M2, S1 and T (T and H being complete rpg newbies), - that kicked off a 50 odd session game that just plain rocked. Was GMd a lot like Apoc World, but more freedom to the players to make up what/how they did things. Story developed pretty much by players introducing ideas from their rolls, or from feedback after game sessions. Ie "I liked X, can we find out for about that" or "i'm going to check if anyone is watching us (rolls excellently) well fancy that, there is someone shiftily looking at you - what do they look like? Or "That magic blows up in your face, what happened?"

    They literally changed the world - went up into "Supernal world" and changed the rules of the world thanks to some artifacts/ritual.

    Then Exalted was put forward as "The afterworld - what the world was like once you changed it". We got some uncovering of stuff that tied back to the mage game (Lost temple with statues/writing. References to "Lords" who had legends that recalled the Mage characters. But the game was very much its own thing. Actually the more than 2/3rds of the game was played as non-exalts. This probably set the tone much better and when they became exalts was when the motivational issues came to the front - they started to overcome things... too easily? It was exciting to go, oh wow I can roflstomp those buggers from a few sessions ago, but then they "fixed" many of the more mundane problems that had been driving the characters and never really got the hang of the "Epic Motivations" that Exalted seemed to want. Due to the way we played the game (more drop-in drop out semi sandboxy/west marches) once they got exalted they started drifting - even had 2 different "secret bases".
    But it went really well and kept growing as friends of friends were brought in due to word-of-mouth

    \n If you want another game to play for 50 sessions, I hear Pendragon is great for that. The basic percentile dice task system doesn't contribute much, but you sound like you know how to make such games work, and the large-scale systems for time passage and family fortunes and reputation are very cool.

    I imagine some of your players will love Marvel Heroic, but others might be annoyed by the dice-pool building or all the comics references they don't know. I wouldn't call the system crunchy, but it does require a bit of attention. I also don't know anyone who's played it with their own characters instead of the Marvel ones. I'm sure you could make it work, but you'd probably want to get on the same page first about challenge (do we need to optimize or can we just roleplay?) and Marvel content.

    My guess would be that Cthulhu and Wicked Age don't fit what you're looking for long-term, but if you're looking for a change of pace, both games have their awesome parts. Ars Magica makes sense to me, but I haven't played it, so now I'm really guessing. Dunno much about Agon.

    I think The Shadow of Yesterday or its system book Solar System would be a good long-term fit in that the systems for character change and growth are front and center, and you can do either epic or gritty fantasy with it pretty easily. Personally, I loved the TSoY setting but found that the book lacked some GMing and facilitator orientation that I needed; I've heard Solar System is better on the "how to run a good game with this" advice.
