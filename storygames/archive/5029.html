    \n Paranoia is a salute to nostalgia.

    I found some notes from one of my first ever run games, Star Frontiers, from back in 1986-7 or so. The PCs infiltrated a base that was one mile under the ground of Volturns. They shot a lot of guys. They grabbed some credits, guns and a super microchip.

    All of the players were equipped with Exoskeletons, even the Dralasite IIRC. Basically in SF, exoskeletons can turn you into NEO, and they don't cost all that much.

    One of the enemies once the doors opened at the bottom of the elevator shaft was "a group of 6 mercenaries that fought like ballerinas, with sonic swords on their feet". They would pirouette around the players, slashing them the fuck up with their toes.

    -Andy

    \n Last time I played Paranoia, a clone was launched via rocket to assist us immediately after his previous iteration's death. He had no piloting skill, and died on impact. This was my introduction to non-GURPS-with-all-the-rules-military-sim-minis-wargaming. Visualizing, to my horror, a human being screaming through the skies in a glass tube, his first memory of life being that of his imminent doom.

    About fifteen minutes later it started being funny, and it hasn't stopped since.
