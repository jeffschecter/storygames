\n
\n I've developed a free web app for creating and visualizing Fiasco setups, and today I'm releasing the first version of it. Instead of giving you a feature list, I'll let the app speak for itself.

Here it is: Fiascomputer

Fiascomputer comes bundled with all 36 official playsets of the month (thanks to Bully Pulpit Games for those!) but you can also add your own playset PDFs and Fiascomputer will do its best to parse them.

While I've got additional features in mind for the future, I'm putting this initial version out there to get some feedback first. Some of the things I already have in mind include:\n
  • Multiplayer across multiple devices
  • Dice management
  • Tilt and aftermath tables
  • Creating brand-new playsets (and not just editing existing ones)
\nI appreciate any feedback, including bug reports. The app is designed to work with all devices but I haven't had the resources to test on a huge selection, so errors that you encounter might be device-specific.

Have fun creating fiascos!

PS. For more software like this, check out TabletopSoftware.net.

    \n Paul: Would you be willing to send me your playset so I can determine why the parser didn't understand it specifically? If so, send it to jakob@tabletopsoftware.net.

    The parser doesn't make many assumptions about font styles, but it does expect a certain sequence of pages. The problem with some PDF exports is that they mangle the text horribly, so some PDFs are far beyond the reach of a parser like this.
