\n So over on Jeff Rients' blog (Jeff is a hardcore oldschool gamer with passion: His love of the oldies had me picking up and going through Star Frontiers again, my first ever tabletop RPG, and getting into playing it again), he threw down the "Grognard Challenge", a collection of trivia questions related to founding/oldschool classic tabletop RPGs: http://jrients.blogspot.com/2008/06/trivia-time.html

Honestly, most quizzes I see like this are often of the "stump the chump" variety, with questions so obscure that no one could solve more then 2 out of 20 without being a total shut-it. This quiz, however, has a solid mix of all sorts of questions of the games of old, including SF, fantasy, supers... and a mix of companies as well (not, like, "All TSR" or whatever).
(EDIT: Oops, maybe spoke too soon: The later ones in the last half of the quiz start to get pretty far detailed, perhaps too much so: I couldn't answer CoC questions, frex, even though I played it nonstop for almost a year. There's also perhaps a few too many "which company?" questions, rather than game questions)

How did you do? For me, I don't have any experience with Traveller or Rolemaster or the original Marvel, so I flailed on a bunch. But here's the questions I was able to answer:

Undoubtedly correct. Probably:

3) Which of the following was NOT a commercially released roleplaying game?
4) In which of the following gaming worlds are you most likely to see a character take 10,000 points of damage from a relatively normal attack?
6) Which of the following listed terms would be the more powerful rank in TSR's Marvel Super Heroes game? (I only got this because I knew the "F......" acronym, I actually never played it)
8) Which of the following RPG names is not nor has ever been an abbreviation?
9) Who created the Forgotten Realms campaign setting?
10) What was the name of the dungeon featured in module B1 In Search of the Unknown?
13) What was the first roleplaying game written by Steve Jackson? (I only got this because Mike Holmes ran it for us last year in the Games On Demand/Forge Throwdown area at GenCon)
14) What company once held the licenses to produce both Star Trek and Dr. Who roleplaying games?
17) Which of the following monsters does NOT appear on the cover art of the original Monster Manual?
18) Which of the following is not a Chaosium RPG?
19) In Pacesetter's Chill RPG, what was the name of the organization the PCs all worked for? (totally know this only by accident)
22) What roleplaying game started as a Metamorphisis Alpha campaign? (by process of elimination, I knew the other's weren't)
28) Complete the following game title: Mercenaries, Spies & _______.
29) What do Castle Falkenstein players use instead of standard dice?
33) What was the name of Aaron Allston's tour-de-force Champions supplement detailing his personal house rules and campaign setting?
35) What are cannon-fodder level warriors called in Ars Magica?
36) What game resolves most actions by rolling d10 and multiplying it by another d10 roll?
37) What island is the setting for Over The Edge?
40) What is the name of the rules system designed to be compatible with both Champions and R. Talsorian games? (this was almost a trick question, but I knew it because it was one of the first efforts to be done almost all on the web back in the mid-90s)
44) Mayfair released which of the following RPGs?
45) What game are you most likely to encounter a foe carrying a Panther Assault Cannon?

Also:

15) Who singlehandedly wrecked havoc in the Temple of Elemental Evil?
I didn't get this one, but it was because the correct answer, "Gary Gygax", wasn't an option. Fucking moathouse, I got killed three times there.

So for me: About 21 of 50.

I would have loved to see more on CoC, Shadowrun, Cyberpunk, Stombringer, Prince Valiant, Star Frontiers and maybe some alternate D&D game world stuff (Krynn, etc). But hey, I can always make up my own quiz later!
