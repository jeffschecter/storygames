    \n I'd say don't stress about learning ALL the rules. Just play with the ones on the character sheet, and you can even skip the advancement rules as well. Once you get rolling with more sessions, then you can add cool stuff that you are excited about.

    ALSO- Don't feel bad about flipping through the book to find something during the session, but make sure you limit it to 1-2 minutes each time. If you don't find it in that amount of time, wing it. Even the people who made the game keep it handy for reference.

    PLAY PLAY PLAY it a bunch.

    The Burning Wheel forums will cut right to the point with their advice.

    \n Nature can be tricky. Be well versed on how tapping nature works, as that is Mouse Guard's method to superpower their way through a challenging situation. It comes up a lot, and you'll have to adjudicate what is within nature and what is outside of nature. That can cause some disagreements at the table if you don't have a little discussion with the other players and get on the same page regarding the Nature of mice.

    Really remind the players they need to earn checks. I've had four or five games end with the players lamenting that they totally forgot to earn checks for the player's turn, and it soured the evening a bit. Try to offer situations where they can, if they like, use their traits against them, or to benefit the GM, or when there is a tie, and be sure to bring it up throughout the GM's turn. It can be quite easy to forget that they need those checks for the Player's turn.

    Don't over-prepare GM turns. You have Situation 1 and situation 2, then you can come up with one or two twists. Personally I like to come up with one twist, and have failing the other situation be success with a condition. You don't need to go much further than that.

    I like to count out factors for difficulty out loud. Recently, when they hit a swollen river the squad had to cross, I ticked off the reasons for the obstacle number. I find it keeps things transparent, I can parse through the difficulty easier, and it lets' the players know why the difficulties are the level they are. It also paints a picture of what difficulties they have to deal with. "Base 1 to cross. Add one cause it's raining, and one more cause the river's flooded, and one because of the territorial beavers lurking in the water, ferrying big logs of wood. Difficulty 4 total."

    Print out the cards on the website, on the wiki over here. They are INCREDIBLY useful, and quite pretty. The weapon cards especially, as they can change the rules of combat.
