\n So, over a decade ago now (dear gods) I made a game called Great Ork Gods which enjoyed some modicum of interest. Possibly it even did something that was genuinely new. Ever since it's been languishing on my hard drive/a dark corner of the internet waiting, hoping, that one day I would pull my finger from my bum and turn it into a nicely presented game for the edification and enjoyment of all. This is clearly never going to happen.

However, I think it would be a good thing if Great Ork Gods were to be enjoyed by a wider audience and I know it has a few fans out there. Would anyone be interested in taking it on as a project and producing a finished article? Please reply/whisper/otherwise contact me if so.

    \n I am also quite fond of the game - it used to be in my go-to selection for a few years last decade. I'm not in position to work on it right now, but I commend your choice of actively looking for help.

    One thing you might consider would be to release a public license for folks to do their thing with it, and trust in the marketplace. This is what Paul Czege did with his Acts of Evil, and although I haven't gotten around to it yet, I certainly haven't forgotten about that excellent game either - it shall be finished, sooner or later, thanks to Paul's decision to unilaterally welcome others to work on it at their convenience.
