    \n I think it's more important to make the Lover an interesting, sympathetic, three-dimensional character then it is to hit the other player's romantic buttons -- so to speak. You want to play towards the Hero more than the player. In that way, regardless of whether the Hero rejects or accepts the Lover, the story will make sense and have meaning. In the same way, the Hero player should play the Hero not as a personal extension of his own romantic proclivities, but rather as a fully-realized fictional character.

    In other words, don't force it. Just play the characters as characters. The rest will take care of itself.
