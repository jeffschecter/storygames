    This game is soooooooooo good.
    \nAgreed. Played it at a local con with someone who had the Kickstarter edition, and I loved it.

    Also, I will echo catty_big's recommendation of the collection of Icelandic Sagas. The introduction by Jane Smiley is fantastic, as in the introduction to history and context for the stories. I love the stories because, like the Homeric epics and Mallory's Le Morte D'Arthur, they're accessible stories that also reveal the different kinds of stories people have told that are very different in many ways than the kinds of stories we're used to from popular television and film produced in the 20th century. So often it seems what people mean by "story" is late 20th century television and film -- and that ain't the case at all.

    [Edited to Add: Just bought my copy of the game...]

    And adding this:

    Oh, final thought: In the essays in the book (and I still don't know if it's there) there's one essay about how Iceland described in the tales is an example of a functioning Libertarian state. Ummm... no. The stories show that the culture is dysfunctional, which is why these feuds keep raining down on everyone. Remember, the Icelanders eventually ask a king to come in an rule the land, because they haven't been able to function without an effective executive branch. I think it's important to note this. But it's important to see the essay (again, if it's still there, I don't know) expresses the ideals and ambitions that the Icelandic settlers arrived with.
