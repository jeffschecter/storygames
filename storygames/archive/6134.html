    \n Color I like. Simple con game:

    Rainbow Coalition
    Report to the con gaming desk with one player each in ROYGBIV shirts, each with a different number. Sell all colors with the numbers 1 through, say, 15 (8 x 15 = 120 shirts--yikes!).

    Like Game of Hats, BTW. hats are well-suited to frequent switching, whereas shirts serve better for "permanent" ownership. Even the idea of Obfuscate or Majesty shirts would, generally, remain with the player with said ability.

    Posted By: MattAlso, you could go with people being able to do something only if they have two people with the right combination of shirts...
    Wonder Twin Powers ACTIVATE!

    Though, actually, that would be kind of cool as a "couples matrix": basically, for each role, cooperating with another role opens up a particular capability. Exs:
    * Judge + Police Officer = Incarcerate another player for X minutes.
    * Judge + Politician = Change a single couple capability.
    * Politician + Lobbyist = Use any other couple's capability.
    * Citizen + Police Officer = Force another player to reveal his or her "secret agenda" (victory condition?)

    Simon: Could be tricky keeping folks from just checking in a mirror or when they first put it on; but I like the basic idea. Sort of like Injun Poker (apologies to all Native Americans, but that's the game name).

    Another brainshower:
    Are You Looking At My Chest?
    Players attempt to learn all the "facts" printed on a given role's shirt (in relatively small letters), but if ever caught staring at the other player's chest, must leave the immediate vicinity.
    AYLAMC? Advance Edition
    Same shirts on front, but in 'long' versions--the ones that reach, like, your upper thighs.
    There are ALSO "facts" printed across the player's butt, which can be easier to check without getting busted, but might require sneakiness.
    [edit]Hell, why not: print a fact or two on the crotch region. Bonus-point facts, maybe.

    What this game would need, though, is a sort of random fact distribution, per printed shirt--maybe plug in an Oracle-like generator (i.e. would need a front end for generating a unique set of facts, before sending as art to CafePress). Otherwise, there's the inevitable grognard who will know the totality of any shirt by glancing at one fact on it.
