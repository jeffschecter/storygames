\n So, Fred was on to something awesome here with his thread for designers about their "2 sentence Hollywood Pitch".

There's been a lot of design/designer/game-you're-working-on threads: How about one for folks playing and running games! I think it'll work.

I'd love to see the Hollywood Pitch for the game you're currently playing/running, or a game that you just finished in the last few weeks/months.

First, Fred's description of the Hollywood Pitch:\n
So, one of the things I like to have in my mental arsenal is the "hollywood pitch" for every small-press game. Here, I mean "hollywood pitch" as something which does the following:

- No longer than 2 sentences.
- Tantalizes, but doesn't explain. (Explanations are what happen after you hook someone with the pitch.)
- Talks about the game in terms of a "mash-up" between familiar elements ("It's like Mad Max on the high seas." or "Dead Inside and Lacuna in a fistfight for supremacy.")
\nHere's me:

With Great Power :
The craziness of high-powered actiony supers, meets the unique blend of mystery and suspense of the Russian Night Watch suspense horror books. And it's set in Seattle.
The real danger isn't the werewolves, vampires and dark mages. The real danger is that they are FBI agents, City Planning Board Members and Venture Capitalists.
