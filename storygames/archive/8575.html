    \n I would say that the cultists would only have their demon dice, not their own separate pools. PE isn't Call of Cthulhu. In fact, I'm not persuaded that any of the girls have to be psychic in order to be an effective team.

    Some things that might help:

    -There are no opposed rolls. You roll your own die against the target number.
    -You roll one die at a time. Having extra dice doesn't help in the moment of conflict.
    -The Administrator doesn't have a Best Friend or Rival; he adjudicates his own successes and failures.
    -A whole passel of cultists can be simulated by one die roll. On the flip side, a katana-wielding schoolgirl can cut them all down with a favorable narration.

    -Grant

    Posted By: peccableI thought you could roll any number of applicable elemental dice in a conflict. What I mean is, if I narrate a Fire action for my character and I have a 5 pool available in it, I could roll anywhere from 1-5 dice on that single roll.
    \nYou can, but you only need 1 success to win your action. PE is pretty kind about that. So you have a good chance of winning with just a single die. no need to roll a bunch if you don't want to.

    What I would recommend is not stating out individul cultists, but stating the cult as a whole. Something like:

    Fire 5
    Earth 3
    water 2
    Air 1
    Void 4

    Whenever the girls run into any element of the cult, you'll us ethis profile. So if they face off against a guy in a spooky hood early in the game, you may want to just roll one or two dice before drawing the conflict to a close. Save those dice for later when the girls track down the cultist that's been dragging off teachers and planting mind rot weeds into their eye sockets. that's when you go ahead and blow 5 or 6 dice in one go. Save the rest for the big showdown at the end of the session, when the cult leader sends the zombie teachers after the girls.By then you should have some extra dice to play with anyway.

    Does that make sense?

    \n Or you can go a step further and not stat your demon at all. Just treat it as a pile of 15 dice that you can roll without having to justify the action to a specific element. That's what I've been doing for awhile, and it seems to work pretty well.

    Also, I usually end up giving control of the demon about half way through the game. We usually end up with an audience of boyfriends and girlfriends, so I hand the dice off to one of them, and the narration duties to another. Then I usually go get some dinner.

    I'm not really suggesting you should do this, but it is an option.
