It's now live.

My IndieGoGo project, the Goblin Tarot.

If you are interested in tarot, fantasy worlds, steampunk artistry or goblins, then you should find something within the deck to draw your interest. If you know someone who is fond of these subjects, then the deck might make a good present for the festive season at the end of the year.

Thanks for reading, and thanks for your support so far.

For a complete look at the finished cards so far, the relevant deviantart gallery is here.

    \n Some valid questions (sorry I haven't been able to answer them sooner.)

    Peter

    1. I've budgeted in such a way that if I miss my target, I'll still be able to get decks to everyone who has participated. I've charged less than the cost of getting a single deck (and assorted extras) printed, but economy of scale means that I only need a dozen or so backers to break even. I'm up to 4 after a couple of days so I think that should be no problem.

    2. The final cards will be using the B&W artwork, but I'm toying with some colour treatments now. I'm leaning toward a sepia effect, but I'm still experimenting. The final option may cme down to a popular vote.

    3. I've just uploaded some card back options at DeviantArt.



    4. The cards will be marked around the outside border (on the long edges with glyphs in each corner). They are designed to be used for the goblin live-action/miniatures hybrid game that I'm developing, so they were always intended to be marked in a method suitable for card play.


    Hans,

    I'm using SuperiorPOD, who describe their cards as:

    High Quality custom full color, full bleed Poker Size (2.5" x 3.5") playing card decks""

    The deck will come in it's own box with goblin imagery. I'm doing some further investigation regarding the output quality now.

    \n Just another reminder about the Goblin Tarot project.

    http://www.indiegogo.com/The-Goblin-Tarot

    At this stage, we're almost halfway with our funding and almost halfway through the funding period.

    All contributors will be receiving their nominated perks, whether decks of cards or packages of cards, books or exclusively hand crafted goblin deck boxes.

    This means that everything is looking good for the second stage of the process, the combined miniatures/live action hybrid rpg that will be based on the world depicted throughout the deck. As thanks for their support, everyone who contributes to the project will get a free beta-version pdf of the rpg once it becomes available early next year.

    If you'd like to see all of the images in the deck and it's gradual evolution so far, visit http://vulpinoid.deviantart.com/gallery/30718134
