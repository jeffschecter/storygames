    \n The most important thing is that there be a good variety of relationships among the Leads. If you look at the Relationship and can't think of how it would compel you to get up in the other character's business, you are not done working on the Relationship yet. Like, they have a problem - why is it your problem, why do you have a stake in it?

    A good Lead Relationship really makes things run.

    Posted By: JDCorleyThe most important thing is that there be a good variety of relationships among the Leads
    \nI got this, too. We did a trial run with one of my groups just to see if it would work for us or not (conclusion: HELL. YES.). We did a pregen based on a con scenario that one of the designers ran, using Green Arrow and his crew. And every single character had "Relationship Lazers" focused on every single other member of the PC stable. Not all of them "happy/nice" ones, but all of them compelling.

    And honestly, we drove pretty much every scene on those. Plus, awesome kick-ass action: I was the Green Arrow and many things indeed got arrow'd.

    \n I'm on my second read-thru and I'd like to know how people's Smallville / Somethingville games are going. What do you think of it? I'm especially interested in whether you've used it for running a drama like Carnivale, Boardwalk Empire, Mad Men or The Wire instead of something like Heroes or some DC or Marvel application.

    I love a lot of the ideas in it (almost all *strangely* familiar...), but it's also feeling like it might have too many levers for me and some of the people I game with. If your group likes things like Apocalypse World, Otherkind, In a Wicked Age, PTA, Dust Devils, and Archipelago II, stretches to play something like Sorcerer or Diaspora and is left cold by things like Burning Wheel do they have much hope of enjoying Smallville? Maybe if you hack it a bit and work really hard on the presentation of it?

    My main group really likes TV dramas and I'm tempted to make some kind of Smallville/In a Wicked Age/Lady Blackbird hack for television dramas just for them. We really like PTA but if I'm running I think I want something with just a few more switches this time around. Just maybe not on the level of Smallville... But maybe!

    \n Actually for each non-GM player there's not much to cope with moment-to-moment in play. Basically you're rolling a die pool and picking the highest two dice, and if you need to roll more or pick more, you spend some stuff.

    I ran a Marvel Movie Trailer-verse game with it to get the feel of it, that's how I came up with the best practices I listed above. People really grasped it fast. The main advantage is that EVERY time someone is doing something they are telling you WHY they are doing it. This appeals to my Victorian novel-reading sensibility.
