    I'm GMing the "Battlestar Galactica in a Dungeon" game Tony's in. I actually emailed folks a few days before that post of Tony's that I thought were 2-3 sessions from wrapping up, which would put us at 7-8 sessions total, I think.

    Now, I based that call off different cues than Tony does, I think: As GM, I know how many major NPCs are on my relationship map, and at this point the player-characters have encountered every single one and pretty much crushed or subdued all but two of them (that'd be Tony's character's scary grandmother, who looks younger than her grandkids and just possessed our priest, and the ox-sized yet incredibly stealthy tiger named Khan, whom our barbarian werewolf and general good guy recently bit on the ear).

    I wasn't originally thinking of Keys so much, actually. But, as of the last session, of the three player characters, one has jettisoned her entire existing belief system and constructed another one on which she's beginning to act (Tony's character, Yoshi); another has jettisoned his entire belief system and started playing a dangerous and unsustainable game with the powers of darkness (Eric's character, Brother Vedis); and the third has resolutely held to his belief system despite mounting and increasingly intolerable pressure, including winning a conflict with the stakes of "Okay, if I beat him, I don't have to become King" (Jenn's character, Khaidu). So, again, we're clearly headed towards a climax.

    It's also worth noting that while the time betwen our sessions is fairly long -- anywhere from 1-3 weeks, given the confusions of summer scheduling -- the time in-game is freakin' intense. From our first session, when the player-characters pelted over the Long Bridge into the abandoned city barely ahead of the Horde, to the latest session, when they hauled their ragged band of refugees into better shelter only to find it occupied by a demon-summing psycho, it's been about 48 hours in-game. During which time, by the way, none of the PCs has actually had a chance to sleep that I'm aware of (though we can presumably handwave in a few naps here and there), and opportunities for rest and recreation have been so rare that at least two PCs have NEVER had a chance to refresh their Vigor pools and can only get new pool dice by spending advances.

    Which they have some ungodly number of, luckily, but I don't have my data handy, nor did I actually track the XP very closely compared to the resulting changes on their sheets.
  • TonyLB
    July 2006
    We've had ... what ... five sessions?

    We've undermined our existing power structure and started making a new society (leaving enemies along the path). We're on a journey to (comparative) safety. When/if we get there, we'll have faced pretty much every bad thing in the city (except the ones Sydney hasn't thought up yet) and our people will have been tested by fire.

    Khaidu, our barbarian, has gone from a curmudgeonly outsider to the leader of our little tribe, and developed a very serious case of honor about the safety and good treatment of everyone, particularly those who cannot fight back.

    Brother Vedis has gone from believing in the Gods and his sacred mission to worshipping those who claim to be Gods ... but his worship is a ruse. He thinks, now, that faith is just a dodge to get enough information to take them down, because ultimately their ideas for him are toxic and wrong.

    Yoshi has gone from a subordinate, quiet girl to an outspoken bitch who will take you down as soon as look at you, and who believes vengeance is the purest form of human connection.

    Those are the overall indicators that I have that the story is coming to a conclusion: these characters are no longer conflicted in themselves. They're horribly dysfunctional, but we're happy with the dysfunction. We no longer want them to change.

    I've probably gotten somewhere between fifty and sixty advances. I suspect that the average is more around forty.

    I am, actually, a little awed and scared by Jason's description of a TSOY game in which people were averaging less than one advance per session. That's ... that's very different from what we're doing.
