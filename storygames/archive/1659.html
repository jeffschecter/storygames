[Dead of Night] Resurrecting DoN from the ... err ... dead

Andrew Kenrick
October 2006 edited October 2006 in Directed Promotion
I started this post to announce that Dead of Night is now (finally!) on sale from IPR! Yay! Then I thought - wait a minute Andrew, you know Story Games isn't a place to shamelessly post press releases. Shame on you! So instead I thought I'd turn it into a question.

Here's the deal - my pocket rpg has been out for almost a year now, and sales have been disappointing. There are reasons - short answer, I dropped the ball when it came to generating buzz, failed to pick it up again when it came to pimping it and promoting it, and completely lost track of the football analogy by spending too much money up front.

So it's a year on and I don't want to write the little fella off, so I'd like to perform a little necromancy and raise it from the dead. I've started to post about it over at the Forge, posting some actual play reports and playtesting retrospectives, and I've got some adventures to release too, but what else can I do? What's the best way to reinvigorate interest in my game (or indeed anyone's game that's been out for a while)? Anyone got any killer marketing strategies that will blow me away?
