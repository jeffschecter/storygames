    \nFriform people sometimes do a limited variant of this. You start with the last scene of the story, which is heavily scripted by GM. Then you let the players (with some structural help from the GM) through scenes narrate their way from the beginning until the already told end scene. Not really Memento, but a good technique that could be used in all types of games I believe.\n

    \n I'd like to see a game based on Beyond the Valley of the Dolls and other Russ Meyer films. Something full of sex, drugs, rock and roll, very gonzo -- his movie Up! is "about" the murder of a wealthy pervert (who looks suspciously liek Adolph Hitler and listens to Uber Alles Duechland...) by pirahna (dumped in his tub) and a mysterious bodaciously stacked woman who shows up in town shortly after. The murderer turns out to be the daughter of Eva Braun. I want a game that delivers that sort of insanity.

    I'd like to see a traditional game based on Apocalypto. That movie was so bad-ass. Another Mel Gison movie I'd like to see as an RPG is The Patriot, which I'm ashamed to admit I loved every single exploitive, jingoistic and historically inaccurate minute of. Both would be easy to do with games already on the market.

    I'd like to see a game based on District B-13, and I'm including a parkour based chase mechanic in a murder of crows because of this movie. But the setting itself is pretty cool, and playing a handful of honest denizens in the District, trying to keep it together while everyone around them descends into savagery, has a lot of potential. Could easily be done with various system, I'd probably go with PTA.

    I'd like to see a good Blaxploitation game for doing great classic movies like The Black Six, Coffy and Detroit 9000. System isn't really the issue here, I'd just like to see a book dedicated to the blaxploitation genre, something that gets past the jive talk and ghetto stylin' and talks about the real social issues that informed these films.

    I also think a game based on Requiem For A Dream, about a group of interconnected characters whose lives spiral into madness and despair, told in a sequence of "seasons," could be very awesome. But I doubt I'd actually play it.
