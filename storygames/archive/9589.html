\n One guy is a VETERAN. One guy is a WOODSMAN. One guy is an INTELLECTUAL. These are all, in their own way, useful and positive archetypes.

This other guy, the guy in the ill-fitting uniform fiddling with a bugle, is young, energetic, optimistic, and thinks he'll live forever. He's a greenhorn, a n00b, a kid among old bitter dudes. But these are all entirely perjorative - what's his positive all-caps archetype that falls in line with the above examples?

Best answer gets out of vedette duty tonight.

    Posted By: Jason MorningstarOK, The Kid doesn't work for me, too perjorative.
    \nConsider that the pejorative is as much endearing/light-hearted as it is insulting. The Kid is about the hopefulness & innocence of youth -- positive definitely, and useful if you think of the kid as someone who helps remind the others more jaded by life and combat/battle/whatever of hope & innocence.

    Plus, who wouldn't want to shout "Damnit, not The Kid! Noooo!" after the Kid's wounded or somesuch.
