    \n In my experience, even a very little successful experience may be actually better than you think. The last playtesting I made with my group revealed that while the game we were testing could be functional and fun on it's own, it was still a half-baked game. And that wasn't something nobody on the table realized but me, since it was my design and what we played that day wasn't the game I wanted it to be. So I'm back to the design table trying a new improvement.

    It would be better if we could have a true playstorming, but in my group I'm the only one designing new games and somehow even I haven't been too interested when other friends of mine tried designs of their own. I realized that most of the cases that was because I found quickly that we weren't in the same page and that the game they wanted and the game I wanted were quite different. There's nothing bad with that, but it just makes me feel like I can only give them some feedback on whatever they try, instead of adding or changing stuff from their design, because that would be tearing apart what they actually want.

    I dunno, I think it's for the best to have only one designer on the team and as much playtesters as you can get, unless you can work in a game just for the fun of hearing and adapting to other people's ideas and not care much about for the result (I mean, care enough to do a good job but not enough as to grow too attached to it's current form)

    \n Let's make this more concrete. I want some general thoughts on how to get the most out of Metatopia by choosing the right game to bring. I figure I can't be the only person who is making a decision like that.
