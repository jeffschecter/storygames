    Posted By: Paul T.
    How well does Monsterhearts stand up if you remove or change the default "American high school" setting to something else?
    \nThere's a section in the text that talks about playing Monsterhearts outside the teenager paradigm, possible pitfalls, and what mechanical conceits are tied into the teenage world.

    The short answer that I've come up with: it's easy to drift it to playing adult monsters who still act like teenagers, but hard to play a game about stable people who've got a handle on life. True Blood is an example of the former. They're all in their mid-twenties or mid-two-hundred-twenties, but they act like fucking teenagers all the time. Monsterhearts will have no trouble doing that. It'll have trouble doing "stately vampires with refined tastes and careful politics," but I wouldn't personally want to play that game anyways.

    Posted By: Paul T.Would "stately vampires with refined tastes" who nevertheless act like teenagers work? I'm guessing yes.
    \nThe difference is essentially one of size. The Wire portrays a collection of institutions and how they affect each other, and how inter-institutional politics affect the people involved. That's too big for Monsterhearts, which is a game for social politics within one institution (or other group).

    If you want to play, for example, a brujah, a ventrue, and a tremere because those are recognizeable archetypes, and you end up playing the punk vampire, the queen vampire, and the wizard vampire, all of whom are part of The Vampires, then yeah, it doesn't matter how stately or refined they are, or how many covert ops ninja ghouls or municipal mayors they control, they're still going to act like teenagers to each other.

    But if you want to play a brujah, a ventrue, and a tremere in a setting where Clan Brujah, Clan Ventrue, and Clan Tremere are all stable institutions (and that's the essence of The Wire right there), then Monsterhearts is NOT gonna do it for ya.
