    Posted By: hans ottersonHmm, has anyone playedThrash, Ewen's fighting-game RPG? I'm curious how the two stack up, and curious to hear from Ewen how/if Thrash emulates the feel of fighting games. But if this is stealing the thread, I'll take it elsewhere.
    \nAt the moment (and for the foreseeable future) Fight! has a massive edge over Thrash in that its current version is complete and actively supported by the designer. ^_^;

    I haven't gotten around to reading through Fight!, but I can tell you that Thrash is a bit RPG-like, and a bit geared towards the kind of fighty anime/manga that fighting games kinda sorta fit into. Thrash does have special moves, super bars, and so on, but skimming the playtest document I suspect that Fight! will get much closer to the feel of actual fighting game play. My concept for the Second Edition of Thrash was basically a cleaned-up and streamlined version of the combat from the original version, plus a "Destiny" system to bring more role-playing stuff in, partly inspired by how Truth & Justice marries a simple PDQ variant and a Hero Point system.

    (Also, Thrash uses a hex grid, but if I ever get back into working on it I'll have to either make better use of positioning or replace it with a more abstract positioning system.)

    Posted By: TulpaI skimmed a play by post in the google group. I think blocking and damage dealt don't QUITE emulate fighting games and still has some vestigial rpg-ness to it.How customizable will the parameters of the fighting game rules be? I am mostly thinking how much fun could be had in a board game equivalent of Garou: Mark of the Wolves, with an interesting choice at every moment (Do I gamble for just-defended versus a regular defense? Do I eat a combo, hoping I don't KO, so I can counter with a desperation move?).
    \nLet's take this point by point.
    Defenses are definitely based on emulation. If you skimmed the pbp, what you saw were Power Level 1 characters, which is basically the equivalent of two people button-mashing, i.e, defense is often non-existent. This changes very rapidly, as evidenced in the Power Level 3 fight on the rpg.net thread.
    Damage is, OTOH, a bit "game-y." I was very much opposed to emulating video games by making standardized damage. To me, it wasn't fun. But I do have explanations in the rules to "justify" or "metagame" random damage to represent random jabs that hit, counter hits, block damage, all which averages out over the length of the fight.
    As far as your Garou example goes, that situation is absolutely the way Fight works. Please don't take this merely as marketing hype - I have worked hard to make a game that feels like a fighting game yet plays fast enough to be a tabletop RPG.

    As for your earlier post, priority is a specific aspect of the rules, as is positioning. I've had a few hardcore fighting game players try it out, and while nothing is going to match actually playing a video game, they were pleased at how many aspects of fighting games felt "right" in the rules.

    I hope you'll check it out.
    Christopher

    Posted By: jenskotCool. Can you tell us more about Accuracy / Control?
    \nSure. Accuracy is simply how likely you are to hit. Of course, hitting in a fighting game is really nothing more than making sure your hit box lands on the opponent. So Accuracy is actually more representative of a move's execution speed.

    Initiative is how fast you act in combat. But acting fast doesn't mean acting well necessarily (again, see: button mashing). This is where Control comes in.

    Control is the factor that determines how elaborate a Special Move you can perform at that moment or how long a Combo you can string together. If you have Initiative, you have your full Control. But if you get struck, you suffer Hit Stun and your Control drops significantly (often, but not always, causing you to lose your action for the turn).

    Does that help?
