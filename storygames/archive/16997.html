\n Hey guys,

I've been slowly plugging away at my game these last months. Recently I decided to make it a futuristic spy game. Something between Serenity and Star Wars mixed with Mission Impossible and peppered with some Shadowrun.

The pull is very strong towards the spy angle. The gist is that there is a cold war going on between two interstellar factions. The team was trained by one of the factions to infiltrate enemy territory and conduct espionage/sabotage missions. I think Mission Impossible's over the top action fits really well in a space adventure like Star Wars.

I'm almost ready to go into alpha testing for it, but Before I do, I want to get some last minute setting ideas. Specifically, what tropes and thematic elements do you think would be necessary for a game to feel like a spy adventure? Cover identities, moles, wide-reaching plots, etc. I've got a pretty good idea, and so far I've had no problems blending setting with mechanics, but just in case...

Thanks for your help.

    \n Do you want futuristic Le Carre... or Bond? Mission Impossible the TV show falls somewhere in the middle, as sometimes the missions are pretty small scale (remove a mole, make a switch of a recording so the bad guys have the false info they think is correct). I think it makes a difference and so it could go either way. A game that is trying to do somewhat "realistic" (ie low body count, dealing with own organization's bureacracy and turf defending) could be really, really cool. But having cosmic martini's while donning insta-tux while paragliding from orbit is its own kind of fun and probably much easier to do in an RPG.

    But a list of tropes?
    *Double crossed by your bosses is a classic... Bourne Identity, 3 days of the Condor in SPAAAAAACEEE!!
    * Cover identities could have low level telepathic implants as well as sophisticated chameleon surgery to actually BECOME the role. The Agent actually believes he/she is the cover identity.
    * Some thought should go into how war (space naval) is conducted... so are there giant fleets of drydock ships, can espionage be overt (blowing up drydock ships).
    * Is there some galacticNET and hacking more powerful? Or is an internet pretty much confined to one planet or system.
    * Super stealthy...but super expensive teleportation (for a more Bondian side of the genre). Great for getting behind enemy lines.
    * Cold War needs a border. Some kind of dimensional wall?
    * Missions aimed at preserving the status quo at times...which means working with the other side when your own side's other agency(ies) have done something to the balance
    * Orbital embassies!
    * System S-Bombs. As in one bomb takes out an entire solar system.
    * Contact lenses that are recording cameras (took this one from Stand Alone Complex...a show I highly recommend for its political oriented cybernetic police procedural).
    * Seducing alien princesses to work for your side. Or alien princes.
    * Bugging a key intel satellite to show what your side wants, without the other side knowing that their trillion dollar satellite has been compromised.
    * Neuro paralysers as thin, evening wear gloves
    * micro everything. Flash drives in earrings. Programmable hallucinagenics in cufflink. Communications in ones jawbone. ECCM implanted in hipbone.

    Hows that to start?
