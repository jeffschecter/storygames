    \n Rob, there seem to be two philosophies when it comes to CN1. Those who really desire a highly-structured Nerdly experience, and those who are going for a more laid-back camping experience. If you are around, and want to start a game, I'm sure you'll be able to find one. The downside to this is that you may occasionally have periods of dead space. If you want to know what you're doing all the time, that is an option as well, the outcome being that you may not have the flexibility to do things that emerge spontaneously.

    I'm trying to stay 50-50. I'd like to do some pick-up gaming, some hanging out, and some structured stuff. My hope is that wonderful things will happen in the structured spots, and wonderful things will happen spontaneously, and I'll get a little of both.

    Also, the games that are up are very much slot-scheduled. Evening games may run over a bit, but that's it. The Draft Schedule Fred linked to is your friend. If you post a game, be sure to ping Dave Younce (keeper of Scheduling) so it gets put up on the Draft Schedule.

    \n Hey Rob, for your info, I'm on the boat with Kevin and Remi. I scheduled one game. I signed up for one game as a "Definitely" (and 1-2 others as "alternates"). So that's 8 (or so) scheduled hours the whole weekend.

    The reason that I don't want to sign up for more? Because the people that are on the ball, and signing up rapidly for stuff, are awesome people who I already know.

    I'm here to game with people I've never met. I want to make new gamers feel welcome. I want to play with people from NerdNYC and Canada and the one dude from Florida, not with my buddies in NC (who rock like cyborg freddie mercuries). In fact, this is why I'll probably be backing out of Matt Gandy's Seiyuu playtest, even though I am salivating to try it out: I can play with Matt and Mark and Lisa anytime, and they are awesome. I want someone who doesn't know these people to sign up for these games and have fun.

    I want to share the wealth, and that means running and playing stuff with Nerdly newbies and strangers, and specifically not signing up for fun looking games where 3-5 of my Area regulars are already signed up. I want other people to experience gaming with Remi, and Mark, and Lisa, and Eric, and Matthew, and the whole works.

    This means that I will come with a few games ready to run that aren't on the schedule. I'll approach new folks, shake their hands, and ask them if they want to throw down with some game or other right then. Or sign up for games where I only know 1-2 of the players.

    -Andy
