    Nathan: There was a space for an alternate, so I'd have been playing even if you showed up. The trophy was never yours to do more than gaze at adoringly from a distance.

    But yeah, the Double-Ex conventions are ... it's like the difference between having a professional landscaper come in and rework your entire back yard, and letting your cousin Eddie try out his new hedge-trimmer. With all due respect to the people who put a lot of love and effort into other conventions, Double Ex is the only group that strikes me as having systematically thought about not merely the How of running a convention, but the Why behind the How.

    Why is it important to have communications between your staff, and to have your staff in the corridors? Given that "Why," what sort of network of communications do you need? How many radios, treated in what manner, and used to relay what information?

    Why is it important to treat your guests well? Given that "Why," what specific courtesies are most important? Is it more important to talk with them for an hour once in the convention, or is it more vital to do a two minute spot check twice a day?

    They've thought about these things. When you look at the way things are run, you can tell. There is too much elegance, too much synchronicty of "We need this" with "Turns out we have that, in exactly the quantity and place that we need," to be accident.

    They're convention theorists, man.

    I would so love to get an Indie Games Explosion sort of thing happening at a convention near me, but ... urgh ... I totally fear dealing with another group. I fear for my patience when I encounter the sort of troubles that are just par for the course in a convention built on love and effort but only improvised structure. Convention heart-breakers. Oy.

    July 2006
    Rob Donoghue wrote:
    Actually, I'm hoping someone can tell me what's different between Dreamation & Dexcon. I've gotten the sense that there are differences, and I'm curious to know what they are.
    Well, it's mostly a matter of degrees. DexCon is their larger convention. You saw it. It's pretty sprawling, and people can get a little insular. I didn't see much of the LARP folk, for instance. But the insular islands are big enough that you still get very good play. At its most basic I would say that DexCon is a somewhat stronger convention for playing scheduled slots with new people.

    Dreamation is smaller, both in terms of numbers and in terms of floorspace. It doesn't sprawl quite as far. It doesn't offer quite as much. But! But it's not just a random 50% (or whatever ... I don't have numbers) of the people who would be at DexCon. It's the most enthusiastic of those people ... the most dedicated. That means your concentration of really cool people per cubic foot goes way up. Also, because everything is within a hop and a holler of everything else, Dreamation does a better job of knitting connections between its various sub-communities. The LARPers are usually passing through our floor-space on their way from one quest to another, and we spent our first day there (this year) in amidst all the card and board games. That gets exposure to folks who don't know our stuff, and that (along with some other factors) conspire to make Dreamation a somewhat stronger convention for moving product.

    And that's one man's opinion.
