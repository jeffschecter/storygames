\n I'm not game savvy or know much about game design. I have not played many RPGs. I've always liked the premise of PTA. I didn't like the cards for conflict. I do love FU's "Yes, and", "Yes", "Yes, but", "No, and", "No" & "No, but".

I've got a rough idea in my head of what I want. I'll be using FU most. Player-characters will have an automatic one die for each descriptor. Screen Presence will be handled just as it is described in PTA. Now based on their Screen Presence score for the episode they will get either a bonus 1, 2 or 3 dice to help out in conflicts. They will get two pieces of gear for that episode that is appropriate for the PC concept for the show. The PC's will also get 2-3 FU dice (much like Fan Mail in PTA) to help in dramatic important points in the episode (or whenever the player thinks they need the FU point for whatever reason). Earning more FU points is in the FU core rules. The used FU dice (Fan Mail) goes to the FU Narrator (same as PTA's producer) for his/her budget increasing his/her 1 automatic die when needed for scenes and oppositions. FU has no such thing as \u2018opposed rolls\u2019 or \u2018contested actions\u2019, \u2018damage rolls\u2019 and \u2018rolling to hit\u2019. The beat the odds roll is the only kind of roll used in FU (As per FU rules) So the Narrator/producer uses his/her bonus die to apply bonus/penalty die to a PC's roll.

Something about the Narrator/Producer's 1 die budget is not right. I keep the see-sawing between keeping the PC's as actual characters in a story (like PTA suggests) to having them be actors and roll for acting situations.

Other than the above it's all a jumbled mess in my head. I don't know, things seem to be missing and I don't know what. I just don't know how to combine the two into one game. I could use your help, please.

    \n Hi Meatball,

    I've worked up something very similar based on FU, drawing from The Pool, the Dying Earth Quickstart Rules and FATE (1e is the one I cut my teeth on). I don't have any particular advice going forward, but I'm running a session with these rules tonight, so I'll see how things work out.

    1d + 1d if it's your speciality + 1d if using a spell (this is based on Jack Vance's writings, so Vancian magic), etc. Then have a session budget per player of extra dice they could spend for rerolls.

    I would probably use a FATE style system where the producer budget gets some of the spent extra dice plus an original amount. Hope this helps.
