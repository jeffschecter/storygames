I'm working on a project where I want to use the basic dice mechanic from Alternity (1d20 +/- 1dN under a Target number). Is anyone here lawyery enough to at least give me an idea about if that is going to get me in trouble. I can think of other games that share similar mechanics that seem to be okay. HeroQuest is basically a d20 + bonus under a TN... just like the d20 System. Numerous games use 3d6 added together under a Target Number. Lots of games use similar dice pools.

Am I answering my own question here?

    Not a lawyer, so grain of salt, yadda yass, but rules are pretty much fair game. Don't use their text, and you'll probably want to use your own terminology, but the core 1d20 +/- 1dN under a Target number should be fair game. if you're _really_ worried, turn it not roll-over instead of roll under, and you're good to go.

    Eric --

    I had a great time with Alternity. We played a non-gonzo game of Gamma World with it, and it was just about perfect for our gritty desert mutants to get their violence on.

    I really liked how action difficulty was represented by physical dice because it brought an extra level of real-world physicality to action resolution. When the players took up the d12 or d20 as their challenge die (or whatever the variable die was called) we knew that critical stuff was going down.

    Andrew, go right ahead and use the dice system. Don't use the text (that's copyrighted), don't use the Alternity name (that's trademarked), but the dice system is an idea and it's not patented, so you can use it freely.

    Also, in my experience, you may start with Alternity dice, but by the time you're done, all of your "minor tweaks" pile up to make it barely recognizable (cf: Stranger Things).
