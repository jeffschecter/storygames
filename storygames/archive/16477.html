\n Hey GMs!

I'm thinking about writing up a con scenario for Monsterhearts - I've run the game straight in the past, but I know that some of you prefer to have things set up so you toss the players into a big bloody mess from the start. Could you tell me how you've done this, and how it worked out? Were there themes or issues you wanted to foreground? How much did you narrow the skin options? Did the players like it?

    \n This is more of a general comment/request about writing up scenarios for AW-hacks that other people might use, based on my own experiences with things besides Monsterhearts:

    -- Leave some things for the MC to wonder about
    -- Put blanks in the scenario for the MC + players to fill in with their own content (like: "Ask which one of the PCs has a messed up relationship with Ms. Lake, and what that is")
    -- It really doesn't need to be more than a couple pages at most (some situations, some threats, some custom moves, a rough map, a few thoughts on approach, done!)

    Posted By: Ethan K.Could you tell me how you've done this, and how it worked out?
    \nMake sure each PC is connected to the next by: a current romantic relationship, a past sexual relationship, or an immediate and pressing need.\n
    Posted By: Ethan K.Were there themes or issues you wanted to foreground?
    \nI've enjoyed setting up situations that highlight how two characters are both Othered, but how they still can't understand one another. An easy example is a Ghoul & a Ghost, or a Ghoul & a Hollow. They're just similar enough to gravitate toward one another, and just different enough to "miss the point" of one another's struggles.

    I've enjoyed setting up situations where the town's wider social problems are wound up in the petty teenage antics that are taking place. Maybe The Serpentine (from an old money family that runs the mine) and The Werewolf (a miner's boy with a wicked bite) start sleeping together, and their relationship captures the awkward and complicated class politics of the town.\n
    Posted By: Ethan K.How much did you narrow the skin options?
    \nPretty narrow. I've tried leaving players only two decisions (pick your final move, allocate your Strings), and it's worked great. I've also rewritten flavour text to be character-specific (rather than Skin-specific), and I've retooled and removed moves to cultivate the ideal version of a certain monster.
