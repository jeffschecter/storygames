\n Hey guys, quick question for you!
\n
\nWe're playing In a Wicked Age. We get into a three way conflict between PC's. Both Player A and C are opposed to Player B. Dice hit the table.
\n
\nSo, Player's A roll stands and Player B has to answer to it. Player B rolls, does not exceed Player A's roll, but does roll better than half. Player B is still in, Player A takes the advantage die.
\n
\nPlayer B does not get to make a move since they already answered Player A's move.
\n
\nPlayer C, rolling the smallest and fewest dice, opts not to make a move at all. The reasoning being (and this was vocalized at the time) that the result of making a move now would probably allow Player B to take an advantage die.
\n
\nRound two begins, all three players roll their dice, conflict continues.
\n
\nMy question: is Player C's pass legal, or does he have to make a move?
\n
\nMy gut reaction is that since he is already in the conflict he either has to make move or negotiate out, and is no longer participating in the conflict. I don't recall this issue being mentioned in the text, but I don't have the text on hand to check it out (we are rotating GM's and the book rotates with them).
\n
\nThanks for the help!

    \n Rules are a bit fuzzy, but I usually rule that if C doesn't want to do anything to A or B and vice versa, there's no need for a roll. Only those that are tying to oppose others should roll, even if round to round.

    \n The FAQ says no passing. You can try to negotiate out of the conflict, and if they don't let you out, now you've got something to roll for.
