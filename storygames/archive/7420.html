\n A group of friends sees a whale stranded on the beach. The whale reminds them of their mortality and shows them the value of every tiny moment of their lives.
\n
\nThis poem should be played among friends and consists of scenes in which one person is lying on the floor and the others gather around him or her. One of the standing persons kneels by the whale and starts to stroke him in a soothing way. The whale tells him or her one short episode from his or her life from the perspective of a third person. Then in the next scene different persons play the whale and the soother. When everybody's been the whale and the soother the whale dies and everybody says a short mourning speech (three or four sentences).
\n
\nThe end.
