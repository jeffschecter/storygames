    \n With the success of the first Con-Quest we are going to do it all again in 2011 only bigger and better than before.
    \nDates are Saturday 30th April 2011 and we are at the Derby Assembly Rooms again.
    \n
    \n
    \nRun A Game - Get In Free!
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \nWe endeavour to provide a wide and far reaching games programme for our attendees to showcase what UK role players can offer.
    \nTo avoid clashes and overlaps the day will be split into two slots for games that will run for 3.5 or 4 hours with a gap in between for food, refreshments, wandering the trade hall, etc.
    \n
    \nDay will be split into slots\n
      \n
    \nSlot 1 = 10:30 until 14:00
    \nSlot 2 = 15:00 until 18:30
    \n
    \nTo register your games before the convention, you will need to email me at the gaming hotline address at darransims@googlemail.com and I\u2019ll see to it that you\u2019re booked in with a table and advertised on the website.
    \nPass on to me the usual sort of details:
    \n
      \n
    \nGM name
    \nTitle of Game
    \nGame System
    \nGame Slot     [morning or afternoon?]
    \nQuiet or Display Game? [In a quieter room or in the main hall]
    \nMax Number of Players
    \nLength of Game
    \nShort Description     [up to 500 words]
    \nTags [any short, snappy key words, such as age, experience needed, drop in or sign up?]
    \n
    \n
    \nWe\u2019re happy to get any kind of game with a variety of settings or genres for all tastes and gaming philosophies.
    \nFrom D&D; 4e to Polaris via Dead of Night, RuneQuest and Savage Worlds, traditional to radical, chess to Heroclix, demo game to playtest, Last Night on Earth to Warmachine with a little Call of Cthulhu along the way \u2013 label up what you want to run and we\u2019ll do our best to promote it and provide you what you need from the venue.
    \n
    \n
    \nFor those that are unsure or are not confident GMing a game for strangers we will offer support and help.

    \nAlessio Cavatore
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \nAfter studying biology at Turin\u2019s university, Alessio Cavatore has moved to the UK at age 25 to work as a translator for Games Workshop.
    \n
    \nOne year later he became a games developer, and has continued to do that job for the last fifteen years. For Games Workshop he has worked on several game systems and supplements, like Warhammer Fantasy Battle, Warhammer 40,000 and the Lord of the Rings Strategy Battle Game.\u2028He is now the managing director of the company he co-founded River Horse Ltd, in order to publish games of his own making. The first one is Shuuro, the game of creative chess, soon followed by its four-player expansion Turanga.
    \n
    \nHe also hires his games-design skills as a consultant for many gaming companies out there, like Mantic (for which he has written Kings of War) and Warlord Games.

    \n The relaxed and friendly gaming convention Con-Quest is back again at the Derby Assembly Rooms on Saturday 30th April 2011!
    \n
    \nOur inaugural year was a great success, with hundreds of delegates coming along and joining the fun, including fifty games masters, a live action performance group and several demo teams, retailers, traders and more!
    \n
    \nWe have a top class programme of games on offer already, with great game masters , old and new, signed up to run wide variety of fantastic games - but we aim to go bigger and better, so can still squeeze a few more in.
    \nWe also want some more wargames, battleboards that really impress and show off our hobby.
    \n
    \nIf you want to run an RPG, or some drop-in games like skirmish wargames, card games and board games, you can get in to the event early and totally free with one of our sassy Volunteer Wristbands. What's more, we have a GM Lounge with a water cooler, tea, coffee and hot chocolate for you to relax and freshen up between games.
    \n
    \nIf you want to help out in other ways, volunteers are also wanted to run the Bring & Buy stall and assist at the game sign-up boards, so if you want to support us that way, there's all the benefits above for you too!
    \n
    \nIf you are interested in helping out then send Darran a line at darransims@googlemail.com

    \n
    \n
    \n So Con-Quest 2011 was a great success with plenty of great games running and a busy trade hall. Though numbers were down a little on last years\u2019 event due to the double bank holiday, royal wedding and sunny weather; the atmosphere was great and all the attendees were very enthusiastic.
    \nWe had a great line-up of varied games delivered by top class GMs, in our humble opinion one of the strongest game programmes we have seen at any con, and a wide selection of traders and exhibition stalls.
    \nI, personally, enjoyed myself even with all the hard work I had to do and it was great to see everyone happy.
    \n
    \nFirst off I would like to thank my co-organiser Dave McAlister, not only for his sterling work in keeping the Con-Quest website and forum ticking over, but for his patience and hard work on the day keeping everything running and covering the Bring & Buy stall for most of the day. His good humour and steadfast demeanour certainly helped me keep working on the day.
    \n
    \nWe would also like to thank the Con-Quest volunteers for their hard work in making the day a success. Russell, my brother, for his printing of the Con-Quest 2011 Programme Books as well as his help on the day. My Sister, Amy, for helping on the door and Laura for her help on the door and the Bring & Buy stall.
    \n
    \nWithout all the Game Masters who volunteer their time and creative energy in running some fantastic games for us, Con-Quest couldn\u2019t happen. We would like to thank them all for stepping up to the plate [Gaming Table?] and delivering some magnificent scenarios to the players.
    \nWe would especially like to thank the following GMs for the extra effort they went to present and organise their games:
    \nTeam 8; John Wilson, Sue Wilson, John Dodd and Ash Elliott for their great organisation and total dominance of one of the gaming rooms.
    \nPaul Lawrence for his Realms of Cthulhu games with great homemade props and game aids.
    \nSimon Bell for his two Traveller games that transported the player to distant realms.
    \nJez \u2018Jonny Gray\u2019s Brother\u2019 Gray for his entertaining Apocalypse World double-slotter game.
    \nAll the Pathfinder GMs for all their organised play.
    \nChris Tregenza and the 6D6 GMs for all their enthusiasm as well as running games for those players who missed the sign ups.
