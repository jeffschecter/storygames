\n Howdy,

I have been playing The Mountain Witch for the first time a being the GM.

We\u2019ve play the first two chapters in two sessions of play, during witch the players (three) have climbed up to the top of the mountain and entered the courtyard of the fortress.

At the end of the last session (ending of Act 2, Chapter 1) one of the players got taken out by Yuki-Onna (O-Yanma\u2019s wife) in a conflict were she wanted him to stay with her instead of pursuing his goals.

The other two players found him lovingly in her arms and understood he was under her charm, so they decided to duel her one at the time, both of them got Taken Out (+5 on the dice in my favour) also (lucky dice\u2026).

So now my problem is, all my players are Taken Out and charmed by Yuki-Onna and I have no idea how to push play forward.

On a procedure level I can realize two things:
1)\tThe stakes may have been badly stated on my part, she wanted to charm them in not proceeding to the castle and now that she won I think the story is kind of stoped;
2)\tI could have chosen not to deal damage on every duel and taken just the narration, but I didn\u2019t remembered that;

But I really don\u2019t want to reverse reality and say \u201cthis never happened\u201d let\u2019s do it again.

My best thought so far is to initiate a series of new conflicts focusing on the Dark Fates (Revenge, Worst Fear and True Motives) in witch the players break free from her spell. But it\u2019s kind \u2018a sketchy for the time being.

Has anyone been in this place before or if not has some advice to give on how to manage this situation?


Thanks in advance for all your help *bow*
Red

    It may not be much help now, but I can say that as a Mountain Witch GM I have occationally interpreted an NPC's margin of success of 5 as just a double success result instead of as Taken Out. I mean, a lone tengu could theoretically take out a single ronin in the first act. That would suck.

    \n

    So, my advice is to leave the story alone but ret-con the Taken Out result. Call it a double-success. Say that the ronin both fell under her spell and then add something really nasty on top of it. I suggest something like "And many years pass before you are strong enough to break free of her spell."

    \n

    I'm willing to bet that your players will be so satisfied with the loss they've suffered that they won't mind the minor ret-con that allows the game to continue.
