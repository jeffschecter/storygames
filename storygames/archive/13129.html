    Hello, responders! I log off for the weekend and look at all the action!

    John: I love your suggestions. I'm gonna send a few of 'em along to my "panel of convention organizers (poco)" as that they are so deliciously choice. I kind of like "Get out of the basement, Olympia!", but imagine all the indie musicians as misreading this as "don't attend house shows anymore, olympia!" Sigh. If we use the FROG (free-range olympia gaming) we'll have to note which games have no pieces with wheat products, or animal products, or refined sugar... and also which games were designed with their writers not being confined to cages. (guess Microscope is off the list! If someone could stop by and feed Ben Robbins this evening, i'd really appreciate it. He needs to clarify the Drama mechanic a little before i can let him stretch his legs)

    LOGO is in the running.

    (i'm glad you asked, Paul...)
    Location confirmed!
    Downtown, one big room (might be loud, but we're gonna figure out curtains or cubicles or something), huge windows, big heaters to blow cards or pieces around randomly (more randomizers! yeah!) walking distance to many food and coffee joints, at least some of which are 24 hours or pretty late anyway.

    the loft on cherry

    Parking has yet to be dealt with, but will be dealt with well before you're circling the block looking for a spot.
    sooo....
    Hans, is four blocks away from Sizizis good enough for you? Booyah!
    Harry, there are tons of great food joints nearby, including gluten free and vegan options. (probably no refined sugar, too, if that's an ish.) I'm pretty stoked about the new diner and also the new bar, both by the same gothic dentist slumlord who opened sizizis, both within walking distance. Those might be good spots for Joe or Matthew to pull folks to and chill a bit in between throwing down.

    Unfortunately, the only grocery store vaguely within walking distance has some 50's-era morals that they like to impose on some of their customers, but the food co-ops or grocery giants are only a five minute car-ride away. We're gonna have sodas on-site, other snacks perhaps... and we're across the street from a great brew-pub. The beer is actually brewed beneath the play-space. Really. There's talk of having burritos or something available, but not in that Gamestorm-hotel-employee-with-the-cart-interrupts-your-moment-to-shine-and-offers-you-the-chance-to-purchase-pizza sort of way.


    oh, the other thing i wanted to mention is one of the pillars this con is being hoisted up on: the desire for constant, round-the-clock gaming possibilities. Now, that doesn't mean that I'm gonna be using them, but the freedom to start pre-dawn and roll through to the next sun-up is totally available. This may also ease some of the constraints felt by haggard story-gamers who just want to hang out with their peeps.

    CONstraint?

    So i'm thinking that there will be daytime blocks built in, some night-time blocks built in too, but more free-gaming in the evening and morning. Um... maybe free gaming will continue until someone has a conflict, then you enter the more structured game-resolution phase? Some kinks are still being worked out.

    Kink Con. "You can watch us work them out".

    no. too literal.


    Yay, i'm so glad everyone's excited!
