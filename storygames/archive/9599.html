    \n I'm back from Origins and it was a fun con. I got to run my Gen Con release "Thunder Hamsters and the Temple of Cheese". It's a hybrid board game/Engle Matrix Game so you can tell a story with it but the real focus is on stealing things. I did get lots of mothers and daughters playing though so I was happy - women are a hard demographic to pull.
    \n
    \nAs to Columbus - I like it. The convention hall is large enough for the size of the convention. The food court is open late and you can walk over to the North Market for great ethnic food. That being said if Gen Con were in Columbus you'd see how small the facility is compared to the Indy Convention Center.
    \n
    \nIndianapolis is expanding the convention center (which may make Gen Con this year challenging) so there will be lots of room. They are also adding down town hotel rooms. The Circle Center Mall has all that Mall stuff and a food court that is open late. Across the street from the convention center is the Eideljorg Art museum (Western and Native American art), the Indianapolis museum with IMAX theater, and a base ball stadium. Maybe six blocks west of the convention center is the Zoo. Maybe six blocks east of the convention center is a farmers market (not the equal to North Market but still okay). There are a lot more restaurants to select within only a few block of the convention center (ranging from the Slippery Noodle, to Shaperios Deli, to the Ram etc.) There is a delightful canal complex north west of the convention center and the host of monuments etc. So there really is a lot down town - really a lot more than in Columbus - and I'm not dissing Columbus.
    \n
    \nAs to the air quality I'm not there at night because I commute in but you're probably right about the air not moving much. It is summer in Indiana. At least it's not Terra Haute!
    \n
    \nChris Engle
