\n My sister tried out a little boffer LARPing when we were in Montreal and had a blast. She's got two tween girls who thought it was cool too. I thought I would get the family a couple of swords and boards for Channukah. So, this is NOT for serious LARPing whatsoever--basically just whacking each other in their backyard. I'm thinking the heavily padded ones as opposed to the more realistic latex style would suit their needs. Any good sources for ordering such things?

    \n There was a group at gencon, based in WI, that sold fairly colorful cloth-covered boffer swords of various sizes, along with axes, shields, etc. The swords were flat, padded and didn't have crossbars, (although they could still smart a little if someone got a good whack in - still good for play fighting, though). I've used that kind before, and they're really good; unfortunately, I can't remember the name of the place. If anyone knows which place that was, I'd be grateful.

    \n Or!

    Just buy 1/2" (or 3/4") PVC pipe from the store (plus end caps), pipe insulation, and duct tape. It ends up looking like this. Why yes, that is my wife and I sparring. Why do you ask?

    Point being, $10 and a trip to your local hardware store could have you set up with everything that you need for several boffers.

    \n Oh my gosh. That was so much fun. I once found white and yellow duct tape, and wrote up special rules for vampires and werewolves. The white was the Silver Sword and killed werewolves on a touch, and the yellow was the Sun Sword and did the same to vampires.

    We didn't do actual boffer RP (though we were almost all of us roleplayers) but we effectively had character types -- healer, necromancer, Highlander immortal, the aforementioned vampires and werewolves, all with special rules.
