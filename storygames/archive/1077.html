GenCon GLEEEEEE!

TonyLB
August 2006 edited August 2006 in Meetups / Conventions

Less than twenty-four hours now until I am wheels-up and in the sky flying toward Indianapolis.

Everything is (cross fingers, knock wood) done. My bags are packed and I am ready to go.

Now there's room for me to get really stoked about the stuff that's gonna happen. And man, am I. Starting off the convention (before the convention even starts) with a clinic where I can learn all sort of ten minute demos? ROCK ON! I can get caught up on all this stuff that I wish I could pitch better, all at once. Plus, it'll probably be fun.

And, of course, as a second-timer ... I know better than I did last year. I know exactly where the booth is (exactly the same place as last year, just bigger) and I know where the hotel is at (right where I wished I was staying last year) and all that jazz. I know I can get orange juice and danish while hanging around waiting for the exhibitor's hall to open, but anything more than that will require me to leave the premises. I know and knowing is half the battle.

Man, I've already 50% kicked GenCon's ass! GI Joe says so, and Joe don't lie.

    My big mistake last year was simply trying to schedule too much. It made me less adaptable, pinned me to the whims of people who paid $3 for a ticket to make me their dancing monkey. Now I'm perfectly happy to be a dancing monkey, and I had a ball with all those people, but this time through I'm running zero scheduled slots, and I will do my monkey dance only for those I... uh ... okay, ramping back the metaphor before it gets ooogie, I'll be able to game with whoever I want.

    GenCon is not a convention where you'll need to scramble in order to find things to do. It is, to the contrary, a convention in which you will have to constantly be missing out on spectacularly cool things in order to do something even cooler. Fuckin' heartbreaking.

    In terms of stuff to bring? My mongo bags of throat-lozenges got fairly heavy attention on saturday and sunday, so you might pack a personal supply. Judd swears by the importance of comfy shoes ... never having flouted this wisdom I can't compare and contrast. Anything that you're bringing in order to make your time in the hotel room more comfortable? Leave it. You won't be spending any conscious time there.

    Pack light: some t-shirts, socks, undies, your game, dice and Jungle Speed.

    Most important thing is probably comfortable shoes. And maybe a hoodie if you're the type who gets cold spending the whole day in air conditioned halls.
