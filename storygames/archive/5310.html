    \nCard Games:
    \n
    \nWe have two card games we hope to get into print in 08. Kingdoms: The Card Game will surely see print, as will some of its early expansions. Kaiju: The Card Game may or may not see print. Both games come at the "expandable" card game concept at fairly radical different approaches.
    \n
    \nKingdoms. The Kingdom's card game will sell both single player and 2 player starter sets. The game will sell card sets based on border color, so there will be black bordered cards, blue bordered cards, yellow bordered cards, etc, etc. We are planning on supporting 8 different colors to begin with. This is so different players may collect different color sets for multi player games. The core game will be customizable in that there is deck building so players can pick from any of the available card sets and buidl custom decks to fit their own strategies. Within that core game deck there will be different game units that have special abilities which let them draw bonus cards from "expansion" decks. So wizards will draw cards from a spell deck, cards with the summoning power will draw cards from a summoning deck, generals will draw cards for a tactical deck, etc, etc. So far we have card designs that allow for up to 5 expansion decks. Presently we have a starter deck, then 30 expansion sets planned (expansion sets being 18 cards each), though its doubtful we'll release all of that in 08. I'll settle for the core game set and the first 8 exansion sets.
    \n
    \nThe Kaiju card game is expandable and customizable, but much less customizable. We plan to release various player/faction decks, then put out small 9-18 card expansion sets for each faction deck. So the amount of customization behind this game is much smaller, but we can continue to release new faction/kaiju decks as warranted by demand.
    \n
    \nThat's the bulk of our 2008 publishing plans. A few titles might get bumped onto the schedule and a few might get bumped off. Plus we have a few existing titles scheduled to have new editions this year, but I don't count them as fundamentally different enough to call them new releases.

      \n As the thread has drifted a little way from cast-iron certainty, my planned releases for 2008:

      The Arsenal of Heaven - a mini-game based around my NUGGET rules. Modern folks acquire the power items of mythic gods - Thor's hammer, Monkey's staff, etc - and get caught up in supernatural battles with demons and sorcerers and whatnot.

      Albion - Reforged Edition - finally, the completed version of my Celtic-themed fantasy game, slicker and shinier than before.
