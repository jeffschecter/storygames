\n My quick Con Report from Origins:

I was there primarily to sell stuff and run demos, meet people and have fun. Here's how that went:

The Good
I got to meet a lot of new people, like Marhault/Jamey, Bill, and others (lots of putting faces to names)
I got to run some really good sessions.
Columbus has lots of excellent food and entertainment. Near our place was a Tea House, and it was incredible. Northern Market, with its collection of about every ethnic food out there (save African), was awesome as well: I had many "fast meals" which were healthy and not shitty-tasting.
Bought lots of cool, unexpected shit like the board/debate game "Who Would Win?", and the RPG "Vox" (which I knew was coming, but it totally caught me off-guard with how awesomely it is put together. Mike doesn't do enough promo for his shit).
Lots of cool small-press gaming events going down, a lot being run by people I'd never heard of/met.
Relaxing time: A good vacation-oid.

The Bad
ORIGINS was the worst-organized con of any con that I can recall, even local cons run by college kids. Now, I realize that others had no problem, but:

* Every single event I listed ended up listed online correctly, but incorrectly in the Con Book. Which led to two of my games getting canceled due to no-shows.
(later, I was able to put together some great pick-up games instead, but I feel sorry for the folks who came expecting a game but got a canceled event due to no-shows)
* Every single event that Jerry G listed ended up having problems except for one: Two events got listed in the same time slot, another event was listed 2 weeks before the con was supposed to begin! In the end, "the computers" had the info listed correctly, but the guidebooks were fucked.
* The guides didn't have maps of the gaming areas. They printed out some later, but no one could have one because they only had about 6 copies. So you had to go to the info booth, look at the map, and "remember it" all the way to your event. Wasn't a huge deal once we got used to the con, but for newcomers this was fucking annoying.
* RPG/LARP information booth had no information. "Sorry, they were supposed to give us a laptop, but there's no wireless, so we can't actually help you with any questions about scheduled events".
* Far less attendees than last year. Some of it I'd put on "The Economy", which is true.

I've heard awesome stories, like from Bill from The Escapist, who ran extremely successful kids-oriented sessions. About half the stories I've heard have been "The con was organized great and my events were cool" and the other half "All my events had problems/were listed wrong/gave the wrong info leading to cancellations and confusion."

Dunno if I'll be back. I think it'll need to improve for a few years (attendance, organization) before I'll give it my time again. I loved meeting folks, but local cons do what Origins did, but better. Still, Columbus is a far more awesome city than I first thought.

Anyone else? Chime on in.

-Andy
