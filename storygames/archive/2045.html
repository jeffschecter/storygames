    Hey Peter,

    I already sent you a whisper, but I thought of one more thing....

    The back of the character sheet. You guys filled it out for all the players, right?

    What I would recomend doing is making photocopies of the back of the sheet (or reproduce the element the player wrote down on seperate sheets -- shouldn't take too long.)

    Keep them with you. During the game, whenever your mind starts to wander, or your afraid you don't know what to do (whether it's because you just had a great scene and don't know what to do, or just had a calm scene that was fine but didn't add much and don't know what to do!) just look down at the backs of the sheets and let the Supporting Characters, Objects, Events, Institutions and anything else there slam your brain for a moment for inspiration.

    Some more thoughts:

    â€¢ Back in the day, GMing for me was being a personal party-clown hopped up whatever drugs kids take these days -- always keeping myself talking and engaging them, never letting them have a dull moment.

    Not so much anymore. The players are part of the mix, too. And if I'm just babbling the whole time they'll never get a chance to know that.

    Moreover, those little moments, like I suggested above when you look at the character sheets, require a moment to actually let inspiration have some breathing room. So, don't rush. If you ask the Player a question about something and the player is stuck, don't always go for that GM trick of, "If you don't do something quick you're gonna die!" Just give him a moment and see if he doesn't smile and say something really cool. And give yourself the same luxery every once in a while as well.

    â€¢ Random notes:
    - If a Bang feels like it's too much too soon, it probably is.
    - If you're afraid a Bang is too much too soon, it probably isn't
    - Don't be afraid to take your time, especially at the beginning. You'll have several sessions, and the first Sorcerer session is often a chance for the Players to pick up all the cool pieces you guys have built, turn them around a bit, and start "naming" the ones they are most intrigued with. It'll pick up pace...
    - You're not there to entertain or to GM "right." You're there to engage in creative acts springboarded off shared imaginative elements you created as a group (Humanity definition, Demons, Price, Cover, Lore, the Kicker) Whenever you get stuck (if you ever do), circle back to those elements in regard to a Protagonist (which are all on the back of the characcter sheet and bring them into play.
    - The cycle of Sorcerer is based on the Humanity rolls. If you can tie the imaginitive just mentioned into scenes that steer toward a Humanity driven choice, you've got gold. If you're group keeps generating Humanity rolls based off moments and scene material from the Humanity definition, Demons, Price, Cover and Lore, and the Kicker, you're probably having a great time, with a lot of leaning in and smiles and cringes from the players. And remember, the players will should and will be happily taking a hand in steering their Protagonists toward the Humanity rolls as well.

    Pedyo, two bits of advice:

    1. Don't have a plot in mind. I once had a "half-plot"-- okay, in the last scene, the enemy army shows up outside the city. And in the very first scene, a player was like, "I get my troops together and attack the enemy army in their city. And I said, "Nope." (In part because I was surprised, and in part because no, the war happens in the final scene, everyone knows that.)

    This was wrong of me.

    If your players say something that surprises you, roll with it. Ditch your plot, and ask for a little bit of time to think.

    2. Fights are hugely important in Sorcerer. So are rituals. These aren't something you do casually. Slow down. Ask everyone to get involved, suggesting details, strategies, colorful things to do, swashbuckling maneuvers, etc. By slowing down, you give these scenes extra importance. Also, the players get a chance to "prep" by adding in other scenes, setting up the ritual or getting the right kind of sacrificial virgin. This will give a roll-over bonus. It also makes the story richer and more complex.
