I've been to Tracon in Tampere, Finland. It's a con for role-playing and anime enthusiasts; mostly the latter, it seems.

This is the first time I've been a true and proper Guest of Honor, and I just loved it, every minute of it.

What did I enjoy the most? The feeling of being taken so well care of - both because that's a wonderful feeling in itself, and because it meant I could focus totally on the work I was doing. Because there was a lot to do.

In the two days I was there, I...:
- Was interviewed by a newspaper. The journalist was curious and positive, but did ask me about a billion times if people ever had trouble getting out of character (known as the "does gaming make you crazy" question).
- Had a Q&A session in the caf\xe9. Not many people showed up, but an older woman who was sitting there knitting did applaud politely afterwards.
- Was in a panel on the everyday life of an RPG designer. Is it all about sex, money and drugs? The other guys had never done any drugs.
- Participated in a panel on railroading vs sandboxes (mostly in a video game context, but I just babbled away about tabletop RPGs anyway).
- Held a lecture on indie RPGs in the US and Scandinavia. I tried to make sure people understood that games by me & my friends were the best in the world.
- Held a GoH speech about me, myself and my games. I think 10 people showed up, and I love them all for it.
- Ran "Love in the time of Seid" with a great group of dramatic and imaginative people, who approached disturbing and horrific scenes with gusto.

In addition, there was a lot of eating and drinking and hanging out with cool people - everything was tightly structured, which was mostly a blessing, but meant I wasn't able to join a spontaneous game I was invited to.

The high point was most definitely the Dead Dog Party after the con was over. Drinking licorice Koskenkorva in a cabin by the lake, hanging out in the sauna, skinnydipping in the hot Finnish night, playing games and talking about games, getting to know people - it was just amazing.

I'm really glad I met so many nice and amazing people. Still trying to get in touch with a few, so if you read this and you met me at Tracon, drop me an e-mail at matthijs1000 (at) hotmail (dot) com!
