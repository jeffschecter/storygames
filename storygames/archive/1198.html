Design: That Last Climb

Levi Kornelsen
August 2006 edited August 2006 in Story Games
I'm nearing the end of actual design for the game I'm working on (The Cog Wars), and god, I thought the first run of playtests was hard. What's left for me to do is just get written, set, and edited something like 25 pages (out of 128), all of which are sketched out in concept, and most of which are flavour, examples, advice, and appendix-type stuff. And then, finally, to take it from "written and tested" to "clear and crisp".

There's that temptation to just leave whitespace, even though I have spare art, or to half-ass some part of editing and final playtests, or whatever. Gah. Can't give in.

So, for those of you who've made it there, how'd you deal with this bit? Or was this bit easy for you?
