What should we call players that can't handle a GM?

\n
\n
\n
\n\nGraham \n\n\n
\n
\n\nApril 2011 \n edited April 2011 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n I've noticed, in my extensive observations of gaming groups*, that some gaming groups can't handle a GM.

They're stuck playing outdated games like Fiasco, Montsegur 1244 and A Taste For Murder, having something that they clearly think is fun, but which I know isn't.

They just can't handle anyone making a decision. They're too disorganised to plan a scenario in advance, so they have to rely on hastily-scribbled "relationship maps". Most importantly, they are so frightened of GMs that, at conventions, they have to book separate rooms to get away from GM-ed games.

Has anyone else seen players like this? What other characteristics do they have? Let's dredge up memories of players who were a bit like this and exaggerate them into pathological cases.

Most importantly, let's invent a perjorative word to describe this strawman style of gaming I've just invented. I suggest Kindergarten Players. Can anyone do better?

* This thing I think I saw once.
