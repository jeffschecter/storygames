\n S-G might not the most appropriate medium to ask this question through, but I figure it'd be one of the fastest.

When an Agent hits their Target heart rate, the book states "While within their target heart rate, Agents may roll any number of attribute dice when making a Force or Instinct roll". In the past I have taken that to mean that the player may opt to roll literally any number of dice, whether greater or lesser than their actual Attribute score. The impact of players being able to guarantee a success (which would mean rolling 11 dice rather than the usual 3 or 4) or be reasonably sure of a success (rolling 4 to 5 dice) seems to be negated by the fact that they'll be out the other end of their Target range faster the more dice they roll.

However, I've since been told, by someone who should know, that "any number of attribute dice" in this context is intended to mean "any number of dice up to your regular attribute value". This makes a big difference, because it means that being in your Target heart rate doesn't increase your chance of success, it actually just allows you to use your really big guns for longer while potentially reducing your chance of success.

Can you clarify how this is done in your game, and/or what your take is on it?
