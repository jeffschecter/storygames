\n It's an amazingly well-crafted book with lots and lots of content!

But alas, I grew up in an era of 5x8-pamphlet indie games. THERE ARE SO MANY PAGES, YOU GUYS.

I've promised a one-shot of DFRPG for a friend who's leaving town. I have ideas about what a faerie knight, a white court virgin and a mortal hunter allying in NYC to maintain a balance between the cities' powers, probably at the the behest of the Dragon that lives under the city, but otherwise, I'm just stumped!

No one here is obligated to do my game prep for me.

    \n And well we shouldn't.

    The point is that you don't need the prep time. Sit down with your players and build magical NYC with your friend who's leaving town. If you focus on only two major power factions you should be able to get through the city creation in about an hour, maybe less.

    Instead of following the directions exactly, just list off three or four major factions verbally and ask the players to pick two. Then define a representative and a heavy for each of them.

    How much time have you set aside for this one-shot?
