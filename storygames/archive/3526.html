Does your gaming group have a "Name"?

\n
\n
\n
\n\nAndy \n\n\n
\n
\n\nJuly 2007 \n edited July 2007 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n So the other day Mark showed me a link to a Japanese analog game convention (mostly RPGs, some board games). I noticed that the events weren't organized by "people" per se, but rather by teams of people. Namely, "Gaming Circles/Gaming Clubs". In Japan, it seems that often gaming groups, be it the same 6 people who meet bi-weekly, or a large city-wide guild/event group that meets monthly with a hundred people, all adopt a name. It's kinda like a small local baseball, dodgeball etc teams.

We see this sort of thing all the time with online gaming groups: Guilds/Clans (in MMOs) and Clans (in FPSes like Counter-Strike and the like). I'm not going to explore the sociological elements of naming the group and the like (but anyone else is free to run with that). I've seen some groups do this, namely the "Durham 3".

Anyone else's gaming group or local meetup group have a name?

On that Japanese website, there was:
House of Fumble
Berserk
"Cool Site GEAR" (in English)
Atarinbou (made up name)

I'm a part of a gaming guild in the Triangle (Ral/Dur NC) called "Dinner and RPGs", though that name started more as a descriptive title than a team name.

My bi-weekly Wednesday group has no name. Yet.
My Thursday group (which is actually more of a group of friends that hang out than a hardcore gaming-hardcore group) recently adopted a name (after creating a mailing list for it) that's seeped in a phrase that is a core of the group's history: "I Kill it With My Axe"

Anyone else?
-Andy
