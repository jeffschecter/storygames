Threaded and Overlapping Narratives

Jason_Morningstar
January 2006 edited January 2006 in Story Games
Imagine a game based on the 1972 Munich Olympic village hostage-taking.

This game is structured so you play it three consecutive times - as the PFLP hostage-takers, as the hostages themselves, and as the Germans and Israelis trying to free the hostages.

You know how the game begins, how it progresses, and how it ends in great detail. You know who dies, and when. What you don't know is how these groups interacted among themselves and with each other.

First of all, given a *single* constrained, linear narrative, I'm absolutely convinced that you can craft a compelling RPG experience. But what about intersecting narratives? How would you address events morphing from one "version" to the next? Would this lead to unsatisfying play? Any thoughts on how to structure overlapping play like this?

    | 1

    Thus, as we play through a couple of scenes, we're only bound by what preceeded 1. Then we play scene 2:

    | 1 ?? ?? ?? ?? 2 |

    We have a rough idea of what preceeded 2 because we know how we ended 1 and that there's something that will end 2, the |, because it is predefined by the game. Using my original map of gameplay, we'd come back to day one when we started playing scene 7:
