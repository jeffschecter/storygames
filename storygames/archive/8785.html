\n I've had Zone-based movement on the brain since Red Box Hack 2 was a thing. If I were to build E6 from scratch the game would have a zone-based movement system, which is basically what I was doing with my RBH ripoff a few months back.

Unlike E6's core concepts, it's not trivial to just graft on zones to d20 - 5-foot-increments are essential to differentiating classes, tactical positioning, and making monsters different from each other. I don't have a reference point for something like this that works. A handwavy "move an appropriate amount" doesn't mix well with E6's simmy feel either.

So I'm wondering if anyone can point me to some Zone rules that work well.

    Also, Ryan, imposing smaller Zones could fix your problem automagically.
    There are other interesting things that zones do in SotC: the boundary that you have to cross between them (which can represent lots of things), the fact that weapons/guns/stuff has different "X zones away" conditions, aspects tied (and/or imposed by the players) on the zones, maybe even stunts that let you do stuff with zones.

    The level of crunch and graininess (??) can be easily changed, I think.

    \n I don't know if it's quite what you're looking for, but Meikyuu Kingdom has a sort of abstracted range map. (Here's a PDF of it.) It's basically a map of six locations arranged in a linear fashion. Characters' movements, attack ranges, etc. are all in terms of space on this map. (e.g., a character can normally only move one rank at a time, but a character on a horse can move two.) It also has a simple 3x3 map for dungeons.
