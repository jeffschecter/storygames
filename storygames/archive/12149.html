    \n Hey Colin,

    Of all the games I've designed, I believe only The Valedictorian's Death has never been playtested. Maybe the requirement that everyone who's going to play spend time getting familiar with the yearbook, and the implication that you need to be well prepared, maybe with a few pages of notes if you want to win, has been too high a barrier to playtesting.

    My gut tells me the starting values for Actions, Reactions, and Consequences should be between 5 and 7. And that they should all start at the same value. I'd probably playtest with a value of 5 or 6.

    There's a thread from 2003, from when I first posted The Valedictorian's Death to the Internet, where I answer some questions about starting values, and also about how they change:

    http://www.indie-rpgs.com/forum/index.php?topic=6410.0

    And in that thread there's also some of my thinking about how a GM should prep the game, and how players would use dialogue to introduce details from the yearbook.

    Paul

    \n Yearbooks in electronic format would certainly alleviate the issue of getting everyone familiar with the yearbook. If you've got the yearbook in PDF or some other electronic format, then it's easy for everyone to have their own copy to look at.

    With a little searching, you can find old yearbooks in PDF format online. Here are a few sources:
    http://www.sailsinc.org/durfee/yearbooks.htm
    http://www.jphs.org/sources-archive/
    http://www.saintpatricks.ca/yearbooks/index.htm
    http://www.rhs1970.com/3/miscellaneous1.htm

    I'm sure there's plenty more to be found too.
