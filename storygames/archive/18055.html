\n Hi, all!

I've put together an AW hack based on the Feng Shui RPG and the Shadowfist CCG. For folks not familiar with those games, we're talking about crazy Hong Kong action movie mash-ups. Maverick cops fight back to back with Shaolin kung fu masters, futuristic cyborgs, ancient Taoist sorcerers and just about any other character you've ever seen in those kind of films.

There are fifteen playbooks (like the maverick cop, the old master, the big bruiser and plenty more) and a bunch of genre-appropriate basic moves (like face off, wheel and deal, speak from the heart and make a stand).

Characters have Blood they share with their friends and enemies, Hooks that tie them to the other PCs, Conditions to track all the crazy stuff that can befall them, an assortment of moves themed for their specific playbooks, and death moves for the climactic moment when they put it all on the line.

I'm on the verge of getting a playtesting group together to see how it works. Feedback of any kind would be greatly appreciated!

The PDF is available here.

    \n Not that there's anything wrong with the way this works already, but a fun alternative I'd like to see sometime:

    When you meet a worthy foe, or offer a foe mercy, you can choose to name them an Adversary. If you do, mark experience (or take Blood with them, or some similar good thing).

    That person now gets some better stats/abilities/whatever when you see them next.

    This is cool because the players get to decide who the significant villains should be. They fight a group of thugs, and one gets away? A player can say, "Ooh! I take a Blood with him. I'll track him down someday, and next time he won't get away!"

    The players will surprise you. They can decide who they want to recur and become important.
