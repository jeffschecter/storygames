\n If you're familiar with Baron Munchausen: I've wanted to play games of it with shorter stories, at times - perhaps to work as a brief warmup or to fit a shorter timeframe. Is using complications a kosher way of nudging people into faster stories? LIke:

"But Baron, no matter how you managed to procure a boat, it must have impeded your quest to Stygia - how could you refrain from fainting once you looked upon its grim shores?"

Or will this turn the game into antifun?

    \n We've done that before. "But Baron, isn't it true that your strange tattoo was actually a map?" You've always got the option to push back.

    For me, though, the key to faster stories is actually playing with a sandtimer. We regularly play it with twenty people at PaulCon, and the timer is indispensable.
