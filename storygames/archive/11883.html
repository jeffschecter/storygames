    \n What if we talk about using the iPad for gaming? I think that might be what this thread is for.

    I have several simple uses in mind:
    - Carrying all my game PDFs and referencing them during play
    - Showing images of people, places, things during play
    - Making maps during play, using a simple drawing program
    - Rolling dice (for those rare instances when I'm not carrying my dice bags)

    These are all things you can do with a laptop, but the form factor of the tablet makes it easier to share during play.

    And, of course:
    - Playing games designed for the iPad (Small World is first, but there will be many more)

    As a game designer, I'm exploring ways of making iPad-friendly content, too. I think the multi-touch tablet has a lot of potential as a shared device used by the whole group during play.

    \n Looking at John's pad last night, it seemed as if you'd need to be sitting around a relatively small table for everyone to be able to see and use the iPad at once. The conference table we were grouped around was too big for that. And that means that, like a board game, you probably really can't have more than 4 people seated around it. Plus, some people are gonna be on the short sides while others are on the long sides. Really, you have to think about what you can do with a screen the size of a sheet of printer paper. It's not a Microsoft Surface or something huge and it's important to consider the limitations as well as the possibilities when it comes to usability.

    If everyone has an iPad, that makes things more interesting but you don't want everyone to be looking at their own screen rather than connecting with the other players at the table, unless you're playing over wifi from multiple locations (then it's like a tabletop MMORG). So aside from linking all the iPads together into a huge screen, you still want to be looking at the same screen a fair bit, rather than each doing their own thing. I'm thinking about Yo Ho Ho Puzzle Pirates and how, in that game, each member of the crew is playing different mini-games at the same time but each game contributes to the overall cause of the group. Something like that might work, where you pick up your individual screens to run combat or chase scenes or whatever, each doing their own thing while yelling frantically to the others. But then you put them down for relationship focused scenes, just making small adjustments here and there.

    I can also imagine semi-larps in the vein of Montsegur 1244 where the iPad is some kind of in-game object, a scifi device or a magic tome, capable of consulting oracles ("Your chaos spell just turned your companion into a goat") or handling the magic system ("fireball, scorches everything in the area") or allowing you to "scan" various things and people in the game ("infected with the Anola virus, must be destroyed"). Or where you link it to Google maps and use it to map out various locations in the city, marking where the vampire covens are for a WW-style thing.

    I'm just trying to brainstorm things that it could do aside from the obvious stuff like record-keeping.

    \nThe Mac Mini would be fine for iPhone/iPad development. It's actually quite a nice little machine. And, in any case, disk speed is generally the bottleneck for development, not CPU, and iPhone apps tend not to be that complicated, code-wise.

    Dev platform you'd want is XCode. It is free, including the SDK for the iPhone/iPad; however, in order to publish iPhone/iPad apps to the AppStore, you need to pay $99 to enroll in the iPhone Developer Program.
