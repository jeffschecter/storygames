\n I've probably asked similar questions before, but I don't think I've asked exactly this one.
If I were to attempt to introduce a friend to role-playing who has never played any sort of these games before, what would be a good one to use?
Let's leave on considerations of personal taste for the moment.

I'm looking for something . . .
. . . that can be played one-on-one. Either one GM, one Player, or something GM-less that works with just 2 people.
. . . with a really snappy "elevator pitch." Something that I can explain in just a few sentences have have my friend "get it."
. . . with really fast, easy character creation. I'd like to be able to give the pitch, make characters, and be ready to fly in 5 to 10 minutes, tops.
. . . with relatively simple mechanics. I don't want something completely free form, but I'd like something with rules that can either be explained easily in the course of play, or that can be added to the end of the pitch and character creation without pushing the time past the 10 minute mark.
. . . that can be played to a satisfactory completion in one shot. 3 to 4 hours, tops. If it takes a little tweaking of the normal game to make this happen, that doesn't bother me.

suggestions? i've got a few games on my shelf that i think could probably work for this purpose, but i'm curious to hear what other people think.

    \n Don't Rest Your Head. Fits the bill perfectly.

    Works for 1 GM, 1 Player: the protagonsits often never meet anyway

    Short pitch: Don\u2019t Rest Your Head is a sleek, dangerous little game, where your players are all insomniac protagonists with superpowers, fighting \u2014 and using \u2014 exhaustion and madness to stay alive, and awake for just one more night, in a reality gone way wrong called the Mad City.
    More here: http://www.evilhat.com/home/?page_id=101

    Fast character creation: answer 5 questions and invent two talents (think superpowers). Bam, done.

    Mechanics: quirky at first, but really easy, and short. Only a single mechanic to remamber. The full rules fit a single sheet of paper, so you can keep them there for the first 3-4 die rolls. After, you can run with just the dice. The GM has to stat exactly ONE statistic for the on-the-fly created opposition.[*]

    Duration: I've run con games with 4-5 players, and in 4 hours some of them had reached the tipping point of exhaustion. With a single player it will work perfectly

    [*] I've done some "howto" like examples in slideshow format, but they are for the italian edition of the game (Non Cedere Al Sonno) I've sent one of them in english to Fred, and when I manage to redo the other I'll send it too. When the man will have some time to breathe, he'll probably post them on the EvilHat website.

    Posted By: fnord3125Steal Away Jordan probably wouldn't really be too hot with just a GM and a player, would it?
    \nI think the biggest problem would be that you wouldn't have other PC's to help out in times of need. But if someone wanted to hack it a little for a 2 player game, it probably wouldn't be impossible. If you focused on a more positive relationship between a master and slave it could be interesting. So say you had an old widow who lived in a city with her equally old slave. An old white woman living alone with no family close by is quite vulnerable, and would have to rely on her slave more and more as she got older. Have the slave and mistress be related. Give the slave the chance to escape with ease, but give her the moral dilemma of leaving her mistress alone and unable to care for herself, so she might face institutionalization, being taken in by abusive family members, etc. Blur the lines of who has the upper hand in the relationship. I think you'd have to tweak the rules to make it work, though.
