    Nathan, I just read the Google Alerts FAQ and I think you have it wrong.
    A 'News' alert is an email that lets you know if new articles make it into the top ten results of your Google News search. A 'Web' alert is an email that lets you know if new web pages appear in the top twenty results for your Google Web search. A 'News & Web' alert is an email that lets you know when new articles related to your search term make it into the top ten results for a Google News search or the top twenty results for a Google Web search.
    It appears to be alerting you to web pages and news articles that have a high Google Ranking. It doesn't give you any kind of information about what other people are searching for ("when someone searches for any of those terms").

    bq. Seems Google likes it when you link from one of your pages to the other sites regarding your topic and both benefit.

    One of the ways Google's PageRank thingy figures out where a certain page should go is by seeing what links to it. If other pages link to your page, especially if the link text matches a search term, that counts high towards upping your ranking.

    Much of the semi-shady industry of SEO(Search Engine Optimization) relies on this fact, and farming links out and back between sites. To be honest, you're better off ensuring that your site uses pure, valid XHTML for it's markup, and talks a lot about it's core subject up front. Because Google's been known to get snarky when people play with PageRank.
