\n So here's a challenge I'm now facing.

In the "City of Petrana" clockpunk game I'm running, I have a player who's character is an automaton-creating tinkerer, and who had two Keys:

Key of the Craftsman
Building is what you love to do the most; having some concrete representation of your efforts in life is something you strive for. Gain 1 XP every time your character successfully completes a Crafting project. Gain 3 XP every time your character completes a Crafting project that is met with great appreciation from many. Buyoff: Destroy one of your creations.

Key of (Metaphorical) Impotence
You ought to be able to do something just fine, but for some reason you can't. This difficulty could be sexual, social, or physical; the source of the problem may be self-doubt, overconfidence, or an evil curse. In any event the player should select a single skill when choosing this key, and may apply a penalty die to that skill at any time to reflect these mysterious difficilities. When the penalty die applies to a routine situation, gain 1 XP. When the penalty die applies to an important situation, gain 2 XP. When the penalty die applies to an urgent, life-or-death, out-of-control-story-madness situation, gain 5 XP. Buyoff: Overcome the source of this anxiety.

At the end of the last session, she had the opportunity to (a) destroy one of her creations (an automaton that had run amok) and thereby (b) get rid of Key of the Craftsman. She wasn't very happy this key (since it created, in her words, a "mage scroll-scribing economy" where the character could just sit in a locked room and make cuckoo clocks to churn xp, and didn't seem to 'fit' with the TSoY-mojo of 'interacting with other characters is where it's AT), so she dumped it.

Now, the plan is to replace that Key with a new one that is much less about 'creating new stuff all the time' and much MORE about 'making the stuff that I create more fun for everyone playing.' In essense, some kind of Key, that functions in the same philosophical vein as this Secret:

Secret of the Heart of the Machine
Your character has more oil in his veins than blood. For the purposes of refreshing ability pools, your character may count machines as characters, whether they are sentient or not. In other words, spending a long night tinkering with the engine of an airship is the same for you as drinking with a buddy.

In short, a key that rewards the player for (re)incorporating her creations into scenes with other characters. Or something.

Problem: I'm a bit stumped; I call upon the power of the Internets to help me put together a good key for this.

Thoughts?

    \n In contrast (or perhaps complement) to Jason's suggestion, from a Changeling character I never got around to playing:

    Key of the Blind Machinist
    Your character loves to make things -- it's why s/he gets up in the morning -- and in the grip of invention s/he often overlooks the moral implications of her creations. Machines are just tools, of course, but for whatever reason -- fate, an evil curse, or just plain bad luck -- your character's tools always seem to end up being used for evil. Gain 1 XP whenever your character creates a new machine that could potentially be used for evil (that's all of them.) Gain 2 XP whenever your character struggles to keep his/her creations from falling into the wrong hands. Gain 5 XP whenever a seemingly innocent or actively good device of your character's invention is subverted to an evil cause. Buyoff: stop making things.

    I'd say the buyoff needs some work, depending on the tone of the campaign. The PC for which I originally developed this concept had stopped making useful objects -- instead he invented increasingly elaborate Goldberg-esque devices that did totally useless things (like turning Coke into Pepsi). But of course they always turned out to be useful after all (the innocuous spife/spoon-knife that turned out to be the perfect size and shape to unjam the Army's assault rifles, etc.)

    \n Key of the Jealous Artist
    Your character is not just a craftsman, trying to make it through the day -- his/her creations are works of art, deserving of public acclamation and praise. But despite his/her incredibly inventions, nobody seems to recognize your character's genius! Gain 1 XP whenever the brilliance of your character's inventions are ignored or overlooked by someone s/he wants to impress. Gain 3 XP whenever your character causes serious trouble for his/herself or his/her friends in an attempt to prove that his/her work is superior to that of a rival inventor. Gain 5 XP whenever your character's inventions are recognized and praised by a public authority or institution of particular importance. Buyoff: Let someone else take credit for your work.

    That's kind of two-in-one: one part wanting to be famous, one part being jealous of other inventors who are more famous than you.
