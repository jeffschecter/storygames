Your Thing when it's Specialized Badass

Josh Roby
September 2006 edited September 2006 in Story Games
So this is a split off of Your Thing.

In that thread, I'm hearing a repeated theme of "incredibly competent in their niche" or "really damn good at one thing" which I find interesting. If nothing else, this seems to be a strong enough theme that I may want to design for it or keep open that possibility in my designs.

Personally, when I play a game for the first time, I often do this if only to have some certainty that I can reliably do at least one thing, and I do this very consciously. In later games, I usually let that pass and focus on what's really "My Thing" -- the irony and conceptual carnage.

Can those whose Thing is "specialized badass" talk about it a bit? Is the appeal the reliability and certainty that you as a player will have some control over the game? Are you interested in the themes that sprout up around massive competency (Beautiful Mind type stuff)? Is it just wish fulfillment?

What's the appeal in playing a specialized badass?

    I sometimes use this as a way to explore particular character traits that interst me by taking them to their extreme. In Dogs, I played someone who's good at giving orders. It was a chance to explore the meaning of leadership. I found out that being the leader, and knowing where to lead are different things. In Burning Wheel I played a master at the Battle of Wits, and found out that a coerced action is only worth so much. In TSOY I pursued a solipsistic goal and discovered the irony of attracting others to your cause.

    In each case, however, my exaggerated character was paried with an in-game competence. I do this to keep my character concept from being rendered irrellevant by mechanics.

    Or maybe I've just beens scarred by plaing the Bard too many times in D&D - jack of all trades, master of none.
