\nANDY: What is it, doc? Tell me straight!
[DOC DRAMATICALLY, SLOWLY TAKES OFF GLASSES]
DOC: I'm afraid that our tests came back positive. You have Character Naming Anxiety.
ANDY: NOOOOOoooooooo!

In the transhuman/cyberpunkish game that Eric P is running ( Mark talks a little bit about it here: http://story-games.com/forums/comments.php?DiscussionID=15574 ), I made my character in full. I came up with his contacts and their names. I came up with ten distinctive pieces of futuristic equipment (and statted them), including coming up with company and product names for every single one (and little backstories in my head for some of them as well).

Between each of these activities, I swung back to my own character name, but kept stalling or coming up blank. I even had the Story Games Names Book in front of me, as well as the Power Of the Internet, and simply could not come up with a name for the life of me. I think this is a habit of mine, for some reason coming up with a Great Name for my PC is not as hard as doing all other generation/preparation tasks combined.

In games where PCs have a codename, I usually don't have a problem with the codename, but when it comes back to their "real name" again my brain-meat comes to a grinding halt.

WTF?

Anyone else suffer from this terrible blight? Anyone have advice?

-Andy

( I eventually came up with a mixed Portugese-Vietnamese name, "Jaime Le" )

    \n I used to have this problem quite a bit when I first started gaming. I would name my characters after whatever was my favorite comic character at the time (I had characters in four different D&D campaigns named "Logan," for instance).
    I found my naming diversity improve when I started reading more Douglas Adams and Terry Pratchett books, and was exposed to more British media in general. There always seemed to be a plethora of things to name people.

    Nowadays, if I can't think of a name immediately, I'll refer to the Gary Gygax Extraordinary Book of Names.
    Barring that, I'll just grab a phone book and flip to a random page.

    \nI'm the other way around. Real names aren't a problem -- flip open a phonebook, stab my finger at a page, repeat until I find a last name and a first name that sound okay to me (as in, I can imagine someone would name their kid that, and I don't think it'll be hard for me or anyone else to use it in the game).

I agonize over codenames, and the closest thing to a shortcut I've found for generating them is to flip through a thesaurus, which isn't all that helpful. It's a nightmare, is what I'm saying.

    \n Generally if I'm selecting from a list organized by genre or part of the world (e.g. from the Story Games Names Project or the Melting Pot Book of Baby Names), I usually just scour it for the least dorky sounding name and go with that. If none of the names work then I move to the next-most appropriate region and start over. Sometimes I'll take pieces of two different names and smash them together. The important thing here is I have to decide ahead of time what part of the world my character comes from and/or where his ancestors came from; that makes it easier to figure out which name category to start with. If it's a fantasy world I try to come up with what might be an appropriate analogue and maybe morph the name a bit if necessary (for example, for some reason all of my halfling characters have French last names - I have no idea why but it just sounds right).
