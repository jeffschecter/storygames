Idea for noodling:

I think starting with theory to solve problems in game design or play from the top-down is kind of silly. However, I think it'd be neat to try to solve problems from the bottom-up: you have a problem, so you go to the FAq or knowledgebase, and look for solutions.

The interesting thing here that I'm thinking about is that the answers should fork into two streams: one to take the design end, and one to take the play end.

Also, while I think the advice should be system-agnostic, direct refs to specific implementations or solutions in other games are kosher.


Example:
Q. A character in my game is too powerful, easily beating all challenges set before him.

Design: blah blah balance blah rules loopholes blah peer review/playtesting.

Play: blah blah intent blah rule-lawyer blah maybe it's you, not him blah increase the challenge level blah



Thoughts, comments?
