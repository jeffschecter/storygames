Story Games: Too Damn Nice? (S-G Fireside Chat)

Andy
December 2006 edited December 2006 in Forum Discussion
Hey all, maybe it's the end of the year, or the fact that we're 11 months into this little project called "Story Games", but I've been reflective about the direction of this site (and even the concept of "Story Games" as a 'game type' or whatever). So bear with me, I'm not normally gonna be opening all these "heart to heart" threads regularly. :-)

But Matt brought up a good point in the Survey stickied thread, and I wanted to discuss it here.
I want to like it here, but it's too damn nice. I'm really, really not kidding.

There is no mechanism to be critical, because the moderation is "Hey, gang, play nice." I DO play nice and yet am not pleased with a handful of discussions I've participated in. I think the "dont' harsh anyone's buzz" vibe going around is ... annoying. As in: Counter the nicey-nice community in some way, and they'll not respond your ideas. I found this annyoing enough to take a breather from the forum altogether. I still don't read anywhere as much as I used to. I came back because I noticed that this forum was the No. 1 referrer to my web site. I've managed to read a handful of threads since, and post once. I think that sucks.

So, I'd love to see a bit more discussion and a bit less can't-we-all-just-get-along hippiness, because I think SG is becoming more of a comfort blanket and less a place in which valuable content develops to RPG hobbyists. But, really, I expect this to be met with "What-the-fuck-is-up-with-Snyder-this-year silence."
Here's my thoughts, and they're not from the Voice of the Commanding Moderator, but rather just one guy. So feel free to disagree publicly, there's nothing wrong and maybe some ideas will rub off:

First, let's look at why I created this place:

* I created this site initially to be kind of a lounge area of The Forge, but with the possibility to build bridges; to get folks in various camps (mainstream and indie; traditional systems vs wild/out-there systems; etc) to come in and relax. Basically, I created a place that would be a "Forge Birthday Forum" (which I love), but with a focus back more into chatting about gaming in a relaxed atmosphere.

* I never really imagined "valuable" content produced. I don't really like theory discussion where the rubber isn't meeting the road. Rather the content I imagined would be pretty unfocused general gaming discussion, with a pronounced focus on what's called "Craft": Basically tricks and tips to make individual gaming sessions better, to improve the experience for everyone at the table, to troubleshoot gaming issues and problems, and (which happens a lot here, and is awesome) discussions on cool ways to start/prep for/think about games and game systems.

So with that in mind, here's more of what I wanted out of the forum:

* I didn't want "fights". They're ugly to look at and hard to moderate. So, namecalling and all was right out.

* I wanted to emphasize the "cross-pollination of ideas" throughout this community (pretty much everyone is on board with "we like mainstream games; we like non-mainstream games or game ideas A Lot", but /within/ that view there's a huge breadth of views and ideas). In other words, to have a place where the likes of Chad Underkoffler could talk with the likes of Shreyas or J Walton without having to talk over/past each other. To create an environment that facilitates that, I had to:
- Come right out and say 'please be lovey-dovey gentle, every idea is a bew-ti-ful flower that deserves to be nurtured' and make moderation actions when someone was forceful, domineering, or abrasive, or
- Constantly monitor all discussions and make fine 'moderator action' adjustments to create this "zone" where there was a maximum of 'people talking the way they want to' vs 'keeping the environment up in a way that won't drive folks off'.

For me, the latter looked like a lot of work so I opted for the former. It's totally the sissy path, and because of it it prevents people from speaking as enthusiastically (or agressively in some cases) as they would want to, but it's the easiest way, and has worked pretty well thusfar.

In the last 11 months, we had a dustup or two (Push/Pull), but for the most part people have been civil, and whether out of respect or fear or whatever have generally refrained from publicly pushing each other's buttons. Some folks have left (publicly even), others just read posts and never post themselves.

...And yet, look at all the awesome discussions that have occurred. I'm always first to lend an ear to new ways of doing things (I outright beg folks for their ideas!), but I sure as hell want to point out the successes of the last year first.

I'm looking for more opinions on this matter, on any side. Feel free to reply.

-Andy

    I have noticed a sort of softening of discussion lately, and not a "softening our razor-sharp blades of rhetoric" thing but more of a "softening into a little blobby pile of goo." I think it's a couple things, but the number one culprit is, I think, a misinterpretation of the nicey-nice ethic that we started with.

    One of the things that I love about story-games is that it's a bunch of people just talking about games. Most of those people really and profoundly do not agree on a number of points, but in this forum that has never prevented them from, well, talking about games. This place has been a forum of common ground, points of connection, and bridges, and I love it for that. Most of the really productive conversations here have, in fact, been people from different gaming backgrounds comparing notes.

    However, of late people have been getting a little more hands-off in regards to participating in discussions. It's an important point to note that "playing nice" does not ever mean that you can't say, "I believe strongly in X." It does not mean that you cannot take a strong position and defend it. It just means you can't say, "I believe in X and if you don't, you're a moron." That's lots of other forums, and it's bullshit. But I have noticed that participants are taking fewer positions, saying less, and consequently contributing less. Perhaps it's because people are being a little too careful, lest their "I believe in X" gets misconstrued for "and if you don't, you're a moron."

    This forum was, for a good long time, a place where lots of people who believed different things about gaming came together to discuss gaming and what they believed. We've kind of lost a little bit of that, and now it's a place where people come together and kind of talk about gaming tangentially, lest they take a strong position. I would really, really like to see more folks standing up and representing what's important to them. I think if we lose that, we lose the productivity that we've enjoyed for months.
