    I've thought about submitting a few of the individual tryant games from Heads of State to some literary journals that publish eclectic material. Sort of like a short story writer would try to get individual stories in print to build before trying to publish a collection as a book. I'm not sure if I'll actually try since that could slow down my plans to self publish the 9 games. I hadn't thought as much about submitting to a specialist mag related to the subject matter (political science, latin american fiction, post-soviet democratization, whatever - if they even exist). I think it's a good idea but maybe better for a shorter game if I finish other projects.

    In terms of pitfalls, you'd have to convince a journal that focuses on fiction or non fiction to consider a game; the more well known and higher circulation mag you submitted to the more competitive it gets; even if your game is accepted there can be a long lag time for some journals between acceptance and publication; the editors might require first publication rights so you couldn't self publish in the meantime; a game in a non-game mag might come off more as a gimmic than as something readers would get interested in actually playing; the time you spend submitting, dealing with edits, etc could get you less exposure and actual play than a similar investment in outreach to gamers, etc.

    But (if your psyche can handle getting rejected) these seem like minor points compared to the upside of reaching new audiences and helping spead the word of the innovations of rpgs/story games today.
