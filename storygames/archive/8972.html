    \n TV Tropes is strange. It's like the media criticism's answer to McDonalds or something. It's amusing how trope typology is used to substitute genuine analysis, and how all art is condensed into "this is like that" comparisons. Especially amusing this is when the tropes get thinner and thinner as their number expands. The atomic assumption of splitting genre similarities into tropes starts to really strain with a lot of what gets included in that wiki.

    This viewpoint is mainly based on reading some forums that have gotten addicted to TV Tropes. Now, instead of discussing what a piece of art means, the discussion has turned into a game of trope-finding in an effort to trivialize and explain away the personal contribution of the artist himself. Instead of telling a story about some people the artist finds himself regurgitating some list of tropes a smart audience member trawled from the all-knowing TV Tropes wiki. The most ardent follower of the tropes might even criticize the artist for not doing a perceived trope "right". This is especially amusing when somebody gets the mistaken impression that the TV Tropes terminology is somehow authoritative or in use outside the wiki community itself.

    The wiki itself is pretty amusing to read, though, especially the apocryphal explanations for why movie logic works the way it does.
