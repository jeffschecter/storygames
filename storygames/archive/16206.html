\n I'm not sure how much the word has gone out, but Emerald City Comic Con has expanded to include a gaming track for the first time this year. It's a lot like PAX and video games: their core is comic books, but there may be enough cultural crossover that people there will want to role-play too.

There are D&D and Pathfinder sections, but Story Games Seattle is flying the indie flag and running five tables of pickup gaming. We've got a crack squad of volunteer facilitators lined up to lure innocent passers-by into the arcane world of role-playing games.

Emerald City Comic Con (March 30-April 1)

If you're at ECCC, come by and say hi (read as: game with us). We're in the lower level of the Conference Center. The map legend got switched, so we're the tables marked "Demo & Freeplay". Just look for our sign or follow the sounds of raucous gaming.

    \n It was gross! I showed up there and actually had to run games for new people. I totally hate new people (which is why I am covered in spines), but I was thinking that with that many facilitators that we would just end up playing together. Instead, I played Microscope twice and Shock once with pretty much just new people. Gross! I thought we were just going to keep Story Games to a carefully selected group of hipsters and not let newbs in. I only showed up for a bit on Saturday, but when I left, we had already run 25 games, for just that day. Gross.

    Plus, those newbs bought up all the indie games that Gamma Ray had brought and I\u2019ve already seen six of them sign up for Story Games Seattle meetup citing Comicon as the place they heard about it. Cooties!

    Dan, too bad you can\u2019t make it to GPNW if you are a newb. Because Fabricated Realities is not for newbs. It is only for the elites, yo. No one there will be ready to teach new people how to play new games and you definitely cannot stay in a tent in Ross Cowman\u2019s backyard, because he is a misanthrope.
