    Posted By: Luke WheelThe Walking Eye reviewed FreeMarket.Listen to them complain like a Polish Men's Club for 90 minutes.They did NOT like it -- even though in their actual play recordings, they clearly had a good time. Go figure!
    \nLuke: having listened to the review, do you have any responses to some of the issues they raise? Some of them were more ephemeral: deciding which Experiences match the current situation, certain circumstances not covered by the rules (the "tying someone up and throwing them in a fridge" discussion), having tags that can fit in almost-any-situation and the like, but some dealt directly with what they saw as issues the math/economics of Flow or the percentages of success in any particular situation.

    For example, they bring up the point that they think it would take an incredibly large number of sessions to reach anything higher than a Tier 4 MRCZ, and so they felt that a party of 4 players simply wasn't expected to carry their group that far.

    Have there been any major changes since the beta with regards to the economy or the Flow rules since the beta? Or would this group experience the same issues in the final product?

    Thanks!

      Posted By: Luke WheelThe Walking Eye reviewed FreeMarket.Listen to them complain like a Polish Men's Club for 90 minutes.They did NOT like it -- even though in their actual play recordings, they clearly had a good time. Go figure!
      \nGreat podcast!

      I think the complaining is a sign of their passion and eagerness to love the game clashing with expectations and rule mistakes. The GM even said he would run it again.

      There is a lot to discuss, and I don\u2019t have time to tackle it all but lets break it down\u2026

      You can\u2019t ever get better at Ephemera because to use it you fight yourself?

      Ephemera was played incorrectly. I looked through the book and its pretty clear that you do not use the PCs statistics against themselves\u2026

      \u201cSuperuser plays the target. As the target, the superuser represents the user\u2019s inner demons\u2014self doubt and inadequacy. The superuser has access to geneline 2 (Giant Useless Loser: artist, failure, useless) and ephemera experience 2.\u201d

      The podcast mentioned that 2 players frequently used Ephemera, one player\u2019s character concept relied specifically on Ephemera and the GM heavily featured Ephemera in the game. The issue of Ephemera was also frequently brought up and occupied a considerable amount of time on the podcast.

      But I think this goes to my original point\u2026 these guys have good intentions and are super enthusiastic. Misinterpreting this rule made it so that one player couldn\u2019t do anything substantial throughout most of the game! And they still played\u2026 tried like crazy to enjoy it\u2026 and even thought they spent much of the game sucking\u2026 had plenty of nice things to say about the game. I think that\u2019s a great sign on both FreeMarket and the players.

      It\u2019s too bad the mistake occurred though and affect so much of the game. Late in the podcast they mentioned that these bad experiences colored their overall experiences.

      You can\u2019t gift Ephemera?

      They wanted to use Ephemera to gift art but Ephemera uses songs, speeches, artworks, philosophies and even religions to spread ideas. Ephemera doesn\u2019t create physical objects. Cultivating, Printing, and Recycling do. That said, I can understand the confusion. Song, speeches, philosophies, and religions aren\u2019t things but the use of the word artworks may confuse the issue. And Ephemera in real life is physical things but at the same time, they are meant to be used and discarded and don\u2019t have lasting value.

      Picking the right skills is crucial.

      There was mention that you really have to know what the experiences (skills) are since you get so few of them and if you make the wrong choice\u2026 it really limits what you can do. I think that\u2019s a very fair point! But there are only 14 experiences in the game and as part of character creation they are listed on 1 page with 1 sentence next to each clearly stating what they do in summary. You can then read them in more details under the specific experience which after re-reading it\u2026 is very short, clear, and labeled for quick reference.

      That said, I\u2019m sure there are things that can be done to improve understanding\u2026 I wouldn\u2019t fault the game for this\u2026 but there is plenty that can be learned from the experience.

      FreeMarket doesn\u2019t have money?

      There was a complaint that FreeMarket does indeed have money because it has an economy of Flow and that since there is an economy, there is currency so there is money. I have to admit that when I first heard about FreeMarket\u2026 I thought the same thing. But my expectations and confusion were based on what other people were saying\u2026 not reading the game which communicates something different\u2026 repeatedly and clearly.

      The game text states multiple times that there is an economy in FreeMarket. They even use the word \u201ceconomy\u201d and phrase, \u201creputation economy\u201d.

      A free market in real life is a market without economic intervention and regulation by government except to enforce ownership and contracts. It is the opposite of a controlled market, where the government regulates how the means of production, goods, and services are used, priced, or distributed.

      When people say money in present day, many people mean Fiat Money (ha\u2026 GM fiat!). Fiat Money is all about control and flies against free market principles. Fiat Money\u2019s value is controlled and regulated by governments.

      So in FreeMarket (the game), one of the few things that are regulated is your basic survival needs. Everything else is up to you, including assigning value. The people determine value through reputation\u2026 social capital\u2026 measured by flow. The people have power to vote with their flow. And it fluctuates and can be manipulated.

      But with no want\u2026 aren\u2019t you breaking the rules of drama?

      People in FreeMarket don\u2019t have needs\u2026 but they have plenty of wants! And those wants can get pretty aggressive\u2026 pretty dramatic\u2026 very quickly! People go from slackers to type A personalities at neck snapping speeds!

      And these wants are baked into the game. The GM has tools for taking your Short Term Memories and grinding them up into toxic blends of screaming problems drenched in your experiences to splash back at you.

      You also need Flow. The best ways to get it (not the only ways) is to give gifts and help each other. People helping each other often for groups called MRCZs. MRCZs have goals, mission statements, and pretty clear wants.

      FreeMarket also has scarcity. The most obvious is space and station resources. Space is very limited\u2026 you want lots of friends\u2026 there are lots of people that are awesome and want to be your friend\u2026 but you don\u2019t have room for them (kind of like NYC). But the more powerful your MRCZ is, the more space you get.

      But the less explicit scarcity is relevance. Some games are about old vs. new. This is all in vs. out.

      What\u2019s old and out today can be old and in tomorrow. And because the market is free, barely regulated, the waves come quicker\u2026 the rises sharper, the crashes deeper.

      Drama abound!

      Since picking the right experience (skills) and group conflicts are so important\u2026 shouldn\u2019t we all have the same skills or we get screwed?

      If you don\u2019t have appropriate experiences or gene lines, you can still participate in a conflict! You can Go For It 3 times, use tech, error correction, burn, and call.

      If I have 0 flow, not only can\u2019t I initiate actions till I get flow\u2026 but I can\u2019t defend myself!

      If you don\u2019t have flow and you are targeted you can indeed still defend yourself. It just means the attacker only risks 1 flow.

      In other games you create an adventuring party first and then characters to fit into that group\u2026 why not in FreeMarket?

      You have a lot of freedom in FreeMarket. Including not being part of an \u201cadventuring party.\u201d Players can create their own groups, MRCZs\u2026 they can join separate MRCZs, then can invite NPCs to join, they can compete against each other and more!

      Also, check out the box art (gorgeous).

      Imagine an overfilled tiny MIT or Harvard college dorm room bursting with genius immigrants constantly being shipped in running around with star trek level technology competing to be noticed, lead their sorority, get published, become professors, or change the world!

      There are rules for joining groups, quitting groups, and creating groups. Groups aren\u2019t static, and you can find or make the right group in play rather than before play.

      People can steal and sell each other\u2019s memories or kill anyone who gets in their way. Except nothing stays dead\u2026 but nothing ever comes back the same! Everything about the game is about change. It\u2019s quirky and unsettling. Never boring. It\u2019s steroids for your imagination.

      The tension of trying to fit everyone together\u2026 no matter how much they don\u2019t fit\u2026 is part of the game. And if you don\u2019t like it, you can do something about it!

      I want to play that in play!

      FreeMarket would make the perfect Planscape game. Hell\u2026 the station is even shaped like Sigil! A giant donut. Yum.
