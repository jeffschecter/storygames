    This needs a bit of explanation. When examined in a vaccum, the price is just dandy. When examined in a world where hundreds of free games like Risus exist that can do everything that PDQ does with comparable flair, paying $20 to $30 may be a hard sell for many people. It is for me.

    \n Hi Chad,
    \n
    \nA couple of years ago, I was interested in T&J. But I looked at the PDQ core rules download and found they were missing some things I like to have in my games.
    \n
    \nThe resolution system in that download talked a lot about tasks -- whether a character did or did not perform some action or achieve some influence. But it didn't address how to go from "Yes, you did" or "No, you failed" to affecting the outcome of the story in play.
    \n
    \nLet me explain using the old safe example. Say my character encounters a big safe in a villain's hideout. Previous play has established that some incriminating evidence may exist that could reveal the villain -- who poses as a straightup pillar of the community. I don't know if it's in the safe and the GM hasn't told me, but I'm hopeful. I roll to see if my hero can open the safe. If I succeed, how does the group decide whether the evidence is in there? There's no mechanic in the rules for this.
    \n
    \nI like to play a game where the task roll also decides whether I get the evidence.
    \n
    \nThat, in a nutshell is why I didn't buy T&J. I assumed it had the same resolution rules. If I bought the game, I'd have to propose a set of rules for that last part to my group, get their buy in, and adjust things like the damage rules to support the change. Rather than invest the effort, I've chosen to buy games that do include this aspect of resolution. It's a lot easier to get group understanding if it's right there in black and white.
    \n
    \n(For great task-resolution systems that do have this sort of mechanic, see Burning Wheel and Full Light, Full Steam).

    Posted By: chadu. . . and the print price is set to assure that printing doesn't lose me money.
    \nYour products are POD via Lulu, right? That being the case, the consumer eats the cost (save for Lulu's minimal fees) when a product is purchased from that site. Now, if you're buying printed copies of your own books from Lulu for redistribution via other outlets (e.g., IPR, etc) I can see you needing a high price point to recoup costs because, at that point, you are the consumer (i.e., you're paying full cost for your own books via Lulu). I've actually been wondering about this recently. . .

    The act of publishers buying their own books from Lulu for redistribution seems very inefficient. First, it is not the intended purpose of Lulu to act a large-scale printing press for physical products. Second, there are literally hundreds of large-scale printers out there who offer deep discounts for bulk print runs. Sure, I can see the convenience in using Lulu to print your own stuff (especially if you already have a POD account set up) but I fail to see the wisdom in doing so. From a financial standpoint, it makes ZERO sense.

    If you do, in fact, use Lulu to stock your own warehouse, this begs the obvious question -- why? Why put yourself in the consumer's shoes and pay full retail for your own books when you can commission a significantly larger print run via a traditional press for significantly less money per unit?

      Posted By: T-BoyOooh, make an exception and tell us! I'm always looking for new ideas for the next session I have with my players.
      \nSure, though I'll try to keep the "dude, let me tell you about my character!" to a minimum. Relevant stats for Mendeleev, Matter Transmuter (which was totally inspired by watching Fullmetal Alchemist):\n
      • Master [+6] Science! Quality
      • Master [+6] Matter Transmutation Meta-Power -- relevant Power Stunts (all Good [+2]):\n
        • Phasing (By affecting things around him, not actually affecting him)
        • Matter Control (Altering matter, like changing lead to gold, changing density, etc.)
      \nThe power has limitations, chief among them that he cannot affect living (or recently living) matter. There's a bit more, but that's enough for this example. That should be enough to explain why the character can't alter Thug with Bat directly.

      The key that makes the character fairly badass: the same way that someone can take a "Olympic Bodybuilder" quality and stack it on a "Super-Strength" power, Mendeleev can take "Science!" and stack it on "Matter Transmutation," since he's using my knowledge of science to achieve specific transmutations. Some GMs might count this as overkill, but in T&J games I run it's pretty kosher -- I hope this example demonstrates why.

      So, how would he deal with Thug with Bat with the character? If TwB attacks, then he would defend by:
      * Phasing the bat: Science! [+6] + Phasing [+2]
      * Turning the bat into a pile of feathers: Science! [+6] + Matter Transmutation [+2]

      He would attack with:
      * Phasing the ground around him for a moment, trapping him: Science! [+6] + Phasing [+2]
      * If it was a vicious fight, turn his bat into a bomb: Science! [+6] + Matter Transmutation [+2]
      * If this was a really dark game, transmute the air around him into chlorine: Science! [+6] + Matter Transmutation [+2]
      * A "lighter" version of the above would turn the air around him into nitrous oxide.

      What's important: This is *exactly* the same, mechanically-speaking, as Olympic Bodybuilder [+6] + Super-Strength [+2]. Sure, I'm narrating "I'm altering the composition of the matter" rather than "I'm totally punching a dude," and there is a difference in the situation I can apply the character's powers and what you can defend his attacks with, but there's nothing overwhelming about this character that makes him unworkable in a team situation.

      How could Thug with Bat mess his day up? By throwing a punch. I think that's my favorite part about the character: he's the ultimate science nerd...but he can still be beaten up by the ultimate jock.

      Anyway, that's what I get out of T&J: awesome characters that work well in a group situation and are truly equals. I get the JLA without having to fudge stuff.
