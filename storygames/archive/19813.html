\n I\u2019m a prolific designer of new playbooks for Dungeon World. The system really lends itself to manipulation in both style and subsystems, and I find that I have no shortage of creative ideas to write. However, lately I\u2019ve been growing concerned that in the pursuit of more intricate systems for playbooks and cooler character options, I\u2019m diverging too much from the core experience of DW. Some of what I\u2019ve created doesn\u2019t mesh well with the style of the game.

While a good class should by stylish and fun to play, front-loaded complexity slows the game down too much. My newer designs are intended to make something that is easy to pick up and play, yet offers both a distinct style and fun choices. A little extra complexity can be added in from advanced moves, along with specialization.

That said, here\u2019s my take on the Warlock. I found this class to have more in common with a bard rather than the wizard, although it can certainly evolve in that direction. I\u2019m hoping to strike a balance between easy to understand and fun/stylish to play. Different people will take different approaches to this idea, and I tried to give as many avenues to explore as possible. Comments and critiques are welcome, as always.

    \n Hey Morrius,

    I thought I'd give you some feedback about the Warlock, and my thoughts on DW class design might emerge from that.

    EVOCATION
    I think the attack move and the utility move should be split. Maybe that means that the Demonic Minion absorbs the utility parts of this move; maybe it warrants a discrete move. But something like adding the fiery tag to just one of half a dozen Evocation choices (the Infernal Patron) suggests to me that the move is trying to do too many things.

    I also think one of the liberating things about DW not having - for example - concealment rules is that you don't need to limit yourself to filling an area with magical darkness. You could have a choice like: "Bring darkness down upon a person, place or thing". Now the one move lets you make people upset or corrupted, places dark - metaphorically or literally, and cloak things in shadow.

    I also think you should ditch the final price (about getting a CHA penalty).

    MISTER NICK
    This seems to suit a cleric much more than a warlock. What about, "made someone acknowledge the power of your patron"? This also puts a pressure on warlocks. Hide your patron and your power, and escape the witch hunters, or reveal them and get sweet XP?

    DEMONIC MINION
    I think your minion has been short-changed in comparison to say the Fighter's signature weapon. What does your minion look like? What's its relationship with you? What's its relationship with the patron? These are all worth a list of options, I reckon.



    Hope that helps.

    \n Keep in mind that MAD isn't as big of a deal in Dungeon World at all. In D&D, MAD means that you need great scores all around to simply be competent. In Dungeon World, you're encouraged to make moves with all of your ability scores, and it's not the end of the world so long as you have at least a +0 in the relevant scores. That gives you a strong chance to at least get a 7-9 result on moves.
