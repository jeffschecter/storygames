\n A ripping playtest of my Game Chef 2012 entry, Fin de Sielce, at the London Indie Meet today: the rules and setting both took only a few minutes to explain, so we were able to fit in character creation and the full scenario in under 3 hours. It was a very gonzo game, but not in an absurd way: given that the game is unashamedly Victorian Fringe, some Walter Bishop-like eccentricity didn't feel amiss. There was an exciting moment for some respected gentleman when they discovered a Magic Lantern slide of an elk and spent an hour or two on the train journey to Cambridge just looking at it and pulling the lever to appear to make it's legs move!
\n
\nAlso, an errata, of course: OK, I totally put my hands up this, but the scenario should obviously be called The Dog Who Walks Through Walls and the murder weapon is a phantom dog. Sometimes a good title pushes out all common sense...
