    \n This is a great question. I hope someone has suggestions.

    One thing I have just learned is that my first and obvious thought, Shangri-La, was in fact invented by James Hilton for his novel Lost Horizon\u2014I didn't know that he'd invented it himself\u2014but Wikipedia suggests it may have been inspired by Shambhala, which I'd heard of but thought was a state of mind rather than supposedly a physical place.

    The Shambhala article also links to Agartha, which I had also heard of and in this case knew was a mythical place, but don't know much about. I think it's an uncommon enough reference that it may not count as "one of the standard ones" for your purposes.

    Apart from these, though, I don't know of any offhand, and as I said, I hope others have suggestions. I would think there would be some kind of East Asian or Polynesian sunken-land equivalent to Atlantis or Ys/Lyonesse.

    \n Ben's right. There's a lot of fantasy islands/continents in Chinese writings, some of whose names were later applied to actual islands. They also have a thing for fantasy mountains, like Mount Kunlun (not the same as the real Kunlun Mountains) where the Queen Mother of the West lives. Also, Penglai/H\u014drai.
