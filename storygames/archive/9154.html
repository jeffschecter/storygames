\n So, I'm working away on an expanded version of Spirited, reformatting it for booklet size, experimenting with with Ottoman Empire decorative patterns, editing the rules, and such, but I've hit a bit of a problem. I have no clue what to do for the cover. So, I need your help.

I don't need you to make up a cover, I just need ideas for inspiration, a bit of description that sparks something. The one rule is no faces. A feature of the game is that the characters are anonymous.

I suppose I should summarize the game premise for those of you who don't want to read the 2-page document. It's a romance game, featuring a young man on a manic horse ride through modern-day Istanbul to get to the port before the love of his life is shipped away by her parents. Along the way he encounters various situations, and his valiant nature prevents him from simply passing by.

    \n I would suggest trying to find some public-domain images of ottoman miniature painting.

    Most ottoman miniatures do have faces, but here's the thing: islamic art forbids portraiture. So all the faces in ottoman miniatures are designed to be anonymous, archetypal faces with no identity. Seems to fit your overall aim very strongly.
