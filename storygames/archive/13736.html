The London Indie RPG Meetup are having a Fiasco Playset Design Day in London on Saturday the 12th of February 2011.

\n

\n

What is this?

\n

The Fiasco Playset Design Day is a friendly event where folk gather in a delightful London boozer of an afternoon to create playsets for the Fiasco role-playing game and then play the heck out of them.

\n

There will be banter, brainstorming, laughs, crazysomekindofcool ideas, fun with crayolas, and beer and fine wines too. It\u2019s going to be a creative, fun winter\u2019s afternoon, and at the end of it all you\u2019ll have a nice playset to show off as well.

\n

We\u2019ll be splitting into groups in order to create the playsets that we\u2019ll swap and play later in the afternoon, but there\u2019s no competitive element at all.

\n

Okay, what do I need to bring?

\n

Just yourself. You don\u2019t need any special designer kung fu or much of anything else really. The event itself is free, but do have a few quid in your pocket so that the pub that is letting us use their space benefits from your custom.

\n

If you are planning on coming, you can help us out by joining the London Indie Games Meetup group and RSVPing. You could also drop us a tweet to the same effect @playdesignplay if you don\u2019t fancy signing up.

\n

Gimme the details

\n

Here you go, we hope to see you there.

\n

When: Saturday, 12th February, 2011, 12pm-6pm (12:30pm start)

\n

Where: St Christopher\u2019s Inn, London (map)

    Hi Noam

    \n
    \n

    This sounds like an awesome event. I'm interested in doing something similar in the Bay Area! (anyone else?)

    \n
    \n

    I'll do a write-up after the event, with photos, and share the process that folk used.

    \n
    \n

    How are you going to produce the playsets? Simple paper and pencil? Do you have some sort of form? You mentioned crayons. How are they going to be involved?

    \n
    \n

    I've been to a handful of code mashups aka Hackdays in the past, and I've really dug the energy and collaboration at these events.

    \n

    I'm keen to keep the focus on collaboration and content while cutting away the opportunity for focusing on tools and presentation. For that reason there's no laptops or the like, which are replaced with poster-sized cardboard for big-picture brainstorming, crayons and marker pens, pastel-coloured index cards, an energetic lightning talk about playsets and process to kick the event off, and moustaches. I will format the resulting playsets into usable form using the OpenOffice template and make them available for public download later that same day.

    \n

    Yeah, I don't know where the moustaches fit into things, but this is an experiment so we'll see what pops out of the other side. If anyone has any further suggestions on how to make this pop, I'm all ears.

    Cheers

    \n\nFebruary 2011 \n

    \n
    \n Have to say, the day lived up to its expectations!

    We had three groups designing and then a handful more playing.

    We designed playsets for Egypt in the 1920s/30s - archaeology taking a page from Indie, The Mummy, Tomb Raider and maybe Romancing the Stone - one playset for Victorian London circa 1890, with murder, mad science, opium and other weirdness; and a playset for a Carnival, with lots of freak shows, clowns, hushed up goings on and such like. To acomodate the excess players, the overflow played Boomtown which apparently went very well, if somewhat dark.

    Personally I had a hand in designing the Egyptian Expedition playset which we painstakingly researched by throwing down suggestions like flesh eating beetles, crackpot theories and cultists, then balanced with revolutionaries, colonialism and a touch of romance. From what I gather it went well in play - though some of the wilder elements came out and a secret temple became the base for revolutionaries, Nazis, cultists and at least to one crackpot - the aliens' spacecraft. Hopefully it can also manage something a tad more sedate too.

    I got to play the London 1890 playset. We had an Indian Mystic who was a consulting detective for Scotland Yard, Beatrice Lyons a criminal mastermind and co-owner of the Lyons Tea Shop with her sister, Prudence Lyons (my character) who was of a more innocent ilk and played nurse to a prince raised by wild apes, who was in turn manipulated in his opium dreams, by the shadowy Rubrecht, who had his finger in every pie - including that of the Mystic. Things went from bad to worse, as they are wont in Fiasco, with threats, blackmail, revelations and a strange case of idol smuggling. It all ended rather well for everyone except for the two sisters who each ended trapped - Prudence in a loveless marriage and Beatrice behind bars for her crime. Rubrecht got off scot free becoming richer than a king and wielding considerable power, whilst the prince headed back to the jungle and the mystic escaped, no worse for wear from his experiences.

    All in all it was a fun day and I wholeheartedly encourage people to both participate in something similar and to play the three new playsets designed today. (Coming soon to a pdf download near you).
