\nAlthough still just an idea.

I came up (and my partner helped me further structure) a system to use relationship maps and make them change and directly influence fiction.

the idea does like this, every relationship in the map (either personal, professional or whatnot) has a numeric value attached to it. You get relationship-point(?) and by using them you get to challenge, either for better or worse, a relationship between two parties. This challenge happens at the beginning of a session and from that point it come to happen into fiction.

This is just an ambiguous idea. some obvious question we have not jet answered are.

Do you get to challenge relationships between two parties your character has no influence over?

Is the character actively challenging the relationships or does this happen just by changing the setting?

So yeah... I have to guess there must be some similar systems out there, I'm really new to the whole story-games scene, so I don't jet know >.<

Any advice? anything you know works similarly or completely the same?

    \n Hot War attaches numbers to relationships and has a method for changing them, It's Complicated creates relationships but without the use of any numbers (and very elegantly too).

    Koenig Hospital uses different colours on a relationship map to indicate whether relationships are positive or negative, and arrows to indicate direction. The dice indicate whether these can be change in a scene. I will need to change this though, because I wrote the game for someone before I knew he was colourblind.
