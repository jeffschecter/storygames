\nCommunity Moderation 1.0\n
This extension is intended to help create self-moderating forum communities. The idea is simple: Users receive points for positive contributions to the community, lose points for negative contributions, are permitted to vote whether comments are positive or negative contributions a number of times proportionate to their current number of points, and are not allowed to contribute to the community at all if they lose too many points.

The process works as follows. Please note that every number is configurable through Vanilla's settings page, and the defaults are used in this description for the sake of an easy presentation.

When a user accesses the forum for the first time, he receives 10 points to start with. Each subsequent day the user logs in, he gets 2 points as a bonus for visiting. For each intervening day the user has not logged in, he is penalized 1 point for absence from the forum, up to a maximum penalty of 10 points. This penalty is also limited to the positive points that the user has received for non-moderation actions, specifically to the net sum of the user's sign-up bonus, login bonuses, and absence penalties on record. This prevents a user from having a negative score unless he has actually received negative moderation votes.

Users cannot have more than 25 points, without the appropriate permission assigned to their role.

If a user has a negative points score, he will not be permitted to post or edit comments until his score rises out of the negative range again.

In any 24-hour period, a user is permitted to enter 1 vote per point he has. A user can vote 'up' or 'down' on a comment. Each 'up' vote increases that comment's scores by one point. Each 'down' vote decreases the score by one point. For every 10 positive moderation votes received for comments posted by a given user within the past 30 days, that user gets a 1-point bonus. This bonus is temporary and simply calculated based on moderation activity in the past 30 days.

If a particular comment reaches a net score of 10, then the user who posted it will receive a permanent 1-point bonus. If a comment reaches a net score of -10, then the user who posted it will receive a permanent 1-point penalty. The comment bonus or penalty will be revoked if the comment's score crosses the relevant threshold again.

Each discussion has a score, calculated as the sum of the scores of all comments in that discussion. If a particular discussion reaches a score of 10, then the user who initiated the discussion receives a 2-point bonus. If a discussion reaches a score of -10, then the user who initiated it receives a 2-point penalty. The discussion bonus or penalty will be revoked if the discussion's score crosses the relevant threshold again.

When a comment has a score of 10 or more, or a score of -10 or less, then users will be able to vote that its moderation was 'unfair.' If 10 unfair votes are recorded, then the moderation actions that caused it to reach such a high or such a low score are revoked and each user who moderated the comment in that direction receives a 1-point penalty.

If a comment reaches a score of -15, it will automatically be hidden. If a discussion reaches a score of 10 or higher, then a 'good discussion' marker will be added by way of adding a CSS class to the discussion's HTML representation. If a discussion reaches a score of -20 or lower, it will automatically be closed to further comments being posted.

- The Author
\nI think the time has come for the Story Games forum to finally itself become a story game. The game is about productive, supportive, inclusive discussion. The rules of the game should support and encourage those goals, rather than just saying "My buddy Harry's a great moderator: he can make any forum great!" The above ruleset looks like an excellent start. Fan-mail-style feedback mechanic, heavier investment leads to greater responsibilities...

I'm pretty much entirely serious there. My gaming habits have led me to like self-regulation more than self-restraint.

But moving to full seriousness, this is the greatest scheme for adding a point-based system for moderation for small-to-middling forums that I have ever seen. It's apparently already in use by a few folks (who sadly don't provide links to their forums), who are enjoying it, and have only found that it conflicts with some other silly extensions that we don't have. The author seems to be responsive, available, and an okay guy. I think this is a good bet, even though, with my habits, it will not at all favor me at all.

Off the top of my head, arguments against: It's a big change. According to the documentation, it adds a panel to the UI, which it has to in order to give people useful feedback of how many points, votes, etc, they have, but that might crowd things more than desired. Without seeing it in action, I don't precisely know to what degree it's self explanatory. It might take some thinking and adjusting to set up as, in addition to the various numbers that can be customized, there are more than a dozen different roles with different levels of permissions.

    \n So, actually, I saw this extension a few weeks ago when I did a regular New Extension Sweep.

    My instant first thought was: "Huh, interesting."

    Followed by: "...but absolutely, positively, no fucking way."

    Not at Story Games, anyway.

    I've seen where this kind of mechanism quickly creates toxic environments and relationships quickly. This is a great mechanism if S-G was a huge mechanical beast with dozens of members joining daily, where we needed something to really separate the "real contributors" to the site from the "non-contributors/griefers" beyond simply things like post count, how long they've been on the site, remembering that on that one thread months ago this one dude was helpful and polite, etc.

    In an environment that I want to create here, which is totally a "We understand this place is not meant for everyone" place, where the discussions require empathy... voting for Good or Poor contributors in a mechanical way would simply tear the only thing I really care about this site out, and send it spinning towards a horrible end that I simply would walk away from, quickly.

    It's also meant to be a "low participation == A-OK!" site... A place where some people can feel free to walk away for days or weeks because they have other stuff on their minds or in their lives, and then pick up again in a few weeks without missing a ton of stuff because of the low overall flow (there are only a few topics per day in general, etc).

    But really, this is why I hate it:

    Sometimes unpopular (that is, "unliked by more than a few people, usually due to a contrary stance on theory, certain games, etc") people here say things that are worthy of note, when they convey the message respectfully. However, because of some past grievance, or past conflict, the reader reads these otherwise harmless comments as inciting, aggressive, "against the Zen"... when an otherwise calm mind might go, "No, that's not actually happening here". This isn't a hypothetical issue, I've seen it happen here. These people would get quickly ganged up on, and shit on through the mechanism of penalties.

    Likewise, if someone popular (that is, "generally liked or respected by more than a few people, usually due to being more vocal, or self-published, or whatever") says something that's hostile or "against the zen", then I really don't have any say in asking that person to calm the fuck down: They have 10 buddies that say "I agree! Bonus!", and their comment, which is anathema to the site, ends in a reward.

    Which can quickly lead to games of "Hey all, I think that comment right there was in poor taste. Please all Penalize him." or "I've been in this community for 2 months now, but none of my comments are receiving Bonuses like X, Y, and Z's are. What's the *real reason* behind that behavior?", and stuff like that. This sort of thing ALWAYS adds to a dominant effect sort of behavior: Someone who says something jerky once or twice gets painted with that brush: With the mechanism of bonuses and penalties to follow that user around, folks can get all wired up carefully reading their posts, looking for attacks that aren't there, so that they can hit the button and label them Bad again. This sort of social (not mechanical) behavior happens ALL THE TIME on other various gaming forums which have off-topic areas, I would hate to see this social behavior backed up with a mechanical tool. It would be simply maddening to me as the "admin/social facilitator" of the site to watch that sort of behavior, I'd simply walk away.

    Again, this mechanism is GREAT for huge sites where people need to distinguish themselves from others because there's too many people, or because nobody knows each other (which is a condition that happens with some new users here), so without the stupid tricks of "she has more posts than you" or "he's been here longer", peer review is a great mechanism for pushing forward thoughtful speech or good behavior.

    But it's not really meant for this environment. Especially in an area where disagreeing with another's views on game theory or some gaming stance or other can directly lead to feelings of being attacked.

    -Andy
