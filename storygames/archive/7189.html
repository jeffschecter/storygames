\n Jason's videos here reminded me of something I've been meaning to ask about for a while now:

My game Zombie Cinema is intended as very friendly towards different target audiences. A part of that is the notion that the rules of the game are rather lightweight in instruction; I'll want prospective players to learn the game from web-sources, not from a thick rulebook that is largely unnecessary for those parts of the audience who already know how to play any similar games.

One thing I'd be interested in trying would be a video demo of the sort Ron did for Spione. However, there are some constraints:\n
  • I'm targeting an American audience for the most part. Consequently, I'd want to make the demo movie in America to make speech and such intelligible.
  • I'll be in America for Gencon, myself, which will surely aid in making a video.
  • I don't much feel like carrying a video camera, microphones and whatnot to USA and back.
\nThe obvious solution to my woes is to ask an interested native to take the equipment to Gencon and help me in filming a session of the game. The game, from start to finish, takes a couple of hours, so it wouldn't be impossible to outright film the whole thing. I think that I could edit all the internet video I want from one passable session of the game, assuming we could fix the audio in an acceptable manner.

Is this feasible, do you think? Anybody with camera experience and a suitably lightweight camera want to give it a try? I'm most interested in the audio quality insofar as production quality goes, myself; that's the most annoying flaw in most amateur videos. I'm probably not up to paying a lot for this, but there'd certainly be a copy of Zombie Cinema in it for anybody willing to cameraman for me. We could burn the footage on a couple of disks and take it back to Europe that way, or something of the sort.

If anybody happens to have significant video-making experience, feel free to point out flaws in the plan. I haven't done anything of the sort myself, really.

Although it's probably premature, if anybody wants to star in my zombie-rpg-movie, we'll see if we can make it work at Gencon some night. Should take 2-3 hours with 4-5 players, all told. Rumour has it that Zombie Cinema is not only the most Romero-faithful zombie game around, but also a top contender for a simple and immediately playable drama game where you get to eat your friends alive, metaphorically speaking.
