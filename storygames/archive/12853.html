\n So! We've started an AW campaign.

The very best part, for me as a MC, was the beginning where we talked about the characters and their environment. After everyone'd set up their characters from the playbooks, I did the "ask questions like crazy", we drew maps, and it was beautiful to see how all of a sudden a whole environment sprung forth from seemingly nowhere. It all seemed to make sense and fit. Having a Hardholder as one of the characters is great, probably essential.

We experimented a bit with the characters' moves, just establishing how they worked and what was what. I did some "future badness", set up a (very) few PC-NPC-PC triangles, tried to make the world seem real. It was pretty easy, really (though, of course, I've spent a lot of time studying the rules and threads).

The players really seemed to like it; one commented that it was "the best RPG so far".

I do think, though, that this session was more fun for the players than for me, simply because they're in character so much and experience things that way. I was worried that they wouldn't have enough to do, but they did, and we had fun.

Looking forward to trying out the fronts and threats next time. And making more NPCs. And most of all, getting out of the hold they spent almost the entire session in.

Weird game thing: One character, a Brainer, got all his experience boxes checked, so he got an advance. He chose his own hold. In the fiction, that's pretty much out of nowhere, so we'll need to have something interesting happen between sessions, I think, to justify it. I considered going against it, but hey, two holds, it'll be fun.

    \n Yeah, there is no explicit setting, but there's so much implicit stuff in the playbooks, moves and rules that it all emerges as a coherent world pretty easily.

    Also, once you've done the first session and have your threats and fronts it gets easier to MC, imo. You still have to do a lot of work, but most of that is done before play, by setting up fronts, then in play you just do your moves and follow your prep, whereas in the first session you have to hold everything together, with barely any character glue to work with.

    A second Holding right out of the door, eh? What has been established about the Brainer so far? Maybe, instead of giving him a different hold, give him the current one. Well, half of it, if you have a Hardholder PC already. Have them juke it out for control, maybe.

    \n Dear Brainer,

    You know how you got that holding at the end of the last session? That was awesome! You just went in guns blazing and killed everyone and took it! At least... hmm... now that you mention it, I don't really know how you got that hold. Weird, huh? Well, you're really weird, so I guess that's par for the course.

    Tell you what, why don't you roll+hard and then,
    -- on a 10+, tell me what hold you seized and how,
    -- on a 7-9, pick one of the following...
    + you've seized it, but there are still a few denizens who are unconvinced of your authority; where are they hiding out?
    + you've seized it, but your new dominion got fucked up real good in the seizing; what are the locals desperately in need of now?
    + you've seized it, but the original holder wasn't killed; no, they got away scott free and are gunning to take it back; who are they?
    -- on a failure, all of them.

    Hugs and kisses,
    MC Matthijs

    Posted By: MatthijsSo these love letters, they're handed out at the start of the session, right?
    \nYeah, they can pretty much go one of two ways, either they:

    1. summarize stuff that happened off-screen
    2. work like the pre-session moves (Fortunes, etc.), you roll them and then have their results play out over the course of the session

    Or maybe a mix of the two, depending on the particular letter. Like, you could clearly open with the scene where the Brainer is standing amidst the ashes of the conquered hold. Or just have that already have happened, days or weeks ago. Or start with their plan to conquer the hold and have that conquering be the opening scene or two.

    Kinda depends on how you want to roll with the narrative. Framing the opening of a session is a place where the MC is pretty powerful, since generally after that -- at least in my experience -- the players are like "Man, I'm gonna track that fucker down and shoot him in the face," so you don't have to worry about where the next scene is coming from. But, then again, a good way to start is like, "So, X amount of time has gone by, where are you and what are you doing?" Or you can always start with "It's noon on a Sunday. A strange smoke cloud has appeared on the horizon [threaten badness]. What are you doing?"
