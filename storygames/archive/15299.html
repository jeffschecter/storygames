    Posted By: MatthijsWill this book be A) a fun read no matter what, and/or useful for my gaming in general?
    As to your point A: If you're the kind of person who enjoys reading about how people play a game, yes. And here you are, posting on this forum, and presumably reading even more threads than you post in. So the Companion will probably be a fun read for you, no matter how much Fiasco you actually play.

    For your B: Maybe. The advice on writing playsets is easily generalized to anyplace where you're trying to endow the other players at the table with tension, choice, and the seeds of conflicts.

    \nI don't know. How can I tell?A rousing game of Zombie Porn will settle the "living or dead?" issue.Unfortunately, even a rousing game of Zombie Porn is unable to distinguish P-zombies from ordinary humans. A consultation with Dr Searle, to determine whether your brain is secreting intentionality, is the only way to be sure.

    Seriously, though, amplifying the earlier points: if you're interested in hearing how people from a variety of backgrounds approach the same game, the Companion is for you. If you think that the playsets you've read are good gaming inspirations, and want to read about the thinking that went into them, the Companion covers that nicely. If you want the soft option for tilt and aftermath, that's also in the Companion. And if you want to see an example of an advanced playset, the Companion has a good one, although there are good alternatives elsewhere ("All the Damn Time", e.g.).
