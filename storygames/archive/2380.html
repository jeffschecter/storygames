    Bryan,

    The probability of rolling 3 or less on a single roll is 3/6, or 1/2. So the probability of doing that three times is:

    1/2 * 1/2 * 1/2 = 1/8

    If you want it four times:

    1/2 * 1/2 * 1/2 * 1/2 = 1/16

    Then the probability of rolling 4 or less is 4/6, or 2/3. So the probability of doing that four times is:

    2/3 * 2/3 * 2/3 * 2/3 = 16/81 = 0.19ish

    ...and so on.

    It all gets much more complicated if you're talking about rolling the dice lots of times during a game and saying "What's the chances that, sometime during that game, I'll roll 3 or less 3 times in a row?"

    Graham

    d6 3 or less 3 times in a row: 1/8 (1/2 x 1/2 x 1/2).
    4 in a row: 1 in 16
    d6 4 or less 4 times in a row: 16/81 (about 1/5, 2/3 x 2/3 x 2/3 x 2/3)
    5 in a row: 32/243 (about 1/15)
    d6 5 or less 5 times in a row: 3125/7776 (about 4/10, 5/6 x 5/6 x 5/6 x 5/6 x 5/6)
    6 in a row: 15625/46656 (about 1/3)

    Rolling on a d6, you will get a three or less half the time (3 / 6 = .5). Doing it three times in a row will happen twelve and a half percent of the time (.5 x .5 x .5 = .125). Four times in a row will happen six and a quarter percent (.5 x .5 x .5 x .5 = .0625). Make sense?
