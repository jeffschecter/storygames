The Pitch:

Something is out there in the darkness, watching you. You feel it\u2019s eyes on you. You fight against it, but you can\u2019t struggle forever. Soon, it will come for you.

What will you do?

Annalise is a game that creates tales of Gothic horror. You and your friends take on the roles of the objects of a dark creature\u2019s desire. Over the course of play you determine the nature of the Vampire - and then you find out what happens when you finally confront the darkness.

You play the hunters, lovers and servants of a Vampire. What tales of pain, hunger and redemption will you tell?

Game Features:

-> No-prep. Sit down and start playing immediately!
-> GM-less. Every player has a protagonist character.
-> Satisfying. The rules of the game create Gothic horror fiction.
-> Annalise\u2019s Journal, a 24-page illustrated short story dripping with atmosphere.
-> Six ready-to-play Guided Play Scenarios showcasing the diversity of the game.
-> Works well for 2, 3 or 4 players and takes between 1 and 6 sessions to play.

What Others Are Saying:

    \n I gotta jump in on this. I played it a little out of the Interim Edition, but this year I got to play a single session pick-up game that was pure awesome.

    The claims alone are brilliant. Not only do they ensure a consistent backdrop for the events of the game, they help keep players interested when it's not their scene, and the whole process provides instant feedback on how grabby and evocative your descriptions are.

    The way this game builds, and the inevitable release during the Confrontation phase is just awesome. And having all those scenarios right there lets you throw down with a game already in motion, pushing towards the end-game spiral. I'd really recommend running through one of those first, as knowing first-hand what's coming in the final phases adds weight and intensity to the earlier building segments.

    \n I'm a bit biased towards this game, but I'll add some comments anyway. You can't stop me.

    Annalise is a great two-player game. Find a friend, make stories. It's that easy.

    Claims create foreshadowing of things you didn't know you wanted to foreshadow. Brilliant!

    Zero prep! Character generation during play! Start playing as soon as you sit down.

    Annalise isn't just about vampires. In fact, it's not even just about horror. It makes a pretty nifty conspiracy game, too. Really, try it and see. Or a police procedural. Want to play out a story about a creepy serial killer who turns out to be stalking one of the police officers? Or a story like Dexter (from the point of view of his coworkers)? Play Annalise.
