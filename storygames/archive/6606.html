\n In this episode, I speak with Anna Kreider about her supplement for Ben Lehman's game Polaris, Thou Art But A Warrior. Thou Art But a Warrior is a game where your Muslim knights seek (ultimately, in good Polaris style, in vain) to fend off Christian crusaders. We recorded this on the first night of Camp Nerdly 2 in the woods of Triangle, VA. Hope you enjoy it.

    \n Good episode, enjoyed it a lot. I have been hearing things about this game for a while, so it was good to get a lot of info in one sitting. I'm a sucker for historical games, and the Golden Age of Spain is one of my favorite time periods of my ancestral homeland (even more so in recent years as I have gone on to explore and learn about the unique symbiosis Jews and Muslims had in that kingdom which has not been repeated again to that level - which I am curious to know if and how it was addressed in the game). This is officially my first must-buy at Gen Con 08, and heck, I'll try to play it right there and then.

    Thanks for addressing the question of the designer's religion in writing this game; I had been wondering that from the start.

    When the time comes, I can help out with some promotion for this in my historical games magazine.

    (And funny story: when Rob mentions about a Master Mines member with a worry over his game being a PTA hack, I was like, who was it that said that? Then I remembered, that was me, over my game Grand Tour!)
