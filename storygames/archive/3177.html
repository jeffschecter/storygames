\n Over on RPG.net I've posted that I am working on an adaptation for Tribe 8 to Spirit of the Century, tentatively called Spirit of Vimary. I have the Synthesis and Sundering stunts done, but I'm looking for feedback on Technosmithing. Since I might get the attention of fellow T8 and FATE lovers here, I figured I'd ask.

Basically, Engineering and Science in Spirit of the Century encompass much of what Technosmithing does (and more). The "and more" part I like, I think a lot of the existing Stunts add a lot of character to Keepers. However, unlike Sundering and Synthesis, Technosmithing does not have any obvious trappings to justify a skill as its own. And Engineering and Science a) take away some of the quasi-mystical edge from Technosmithing and b) seem like overkill for Tribe 8's setting.

So what I'm wondering...wrap some of Science and Engineering into one skill called Technosmithing? Just make up the Stunts that Technosmithing has beyond Science and Engineering Stunts and call it a day?

Just an FYI, I've already replaced Academics (and parts of Science) with a skill called Lore plus including a skill called Craft for some of the Engineering related stuff that the Tribes would be capable of. As such, I'm not necessarily interested in keeping the existing Science/Engineering breakdown as I think it's pretty irrelevant to Tribe 8; I'm heavily leaning towards blending most of the trappings of Science and Engineering into Technosmithing. To me this is good, because it makes the technological Keeper stuff even more "mystical" than the "I have x, y and z tech skills and Technosmithing." Technosmithing *is* tech skills. I just want to hear what others think of the idea.

Can I have Synthesis, Sundering, and Technosmithing?     \nThe short answer is: Yes, you can have all three.

    The long answer is that there are aspects that have to be taken in order to get Synthesis, Sundering or Technosmithing. There are also stunt requirements, with aspect requirements as well, which makes it pretty steep for any character to branch out. For example, a Fallen or Tribal character is going to at least one aspect (and probably two) related to their Tribe or Outlook. In order to learn Synthesis, they have to take the Synthesis skill and then two stunts (Eminence and Conjunction) in order to accomplish what a Synthesis user can in Silhouette. To learn Sundering, there would need to be an aspect related to how they learned it; plus taking the Sundering skill, plus any additional stunts they need to take (2 minimum to be able to do anything useful). So now we have a character with a minimum of two aspects, two skill choices and four stunt slots taken up to be a "dual wielder" - and Tribe 8 characters have fewer aspects, Stunt slots and skills than their SoTC counterparts.
