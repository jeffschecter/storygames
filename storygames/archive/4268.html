\n I got the most amazing e-mail from a fan of Passages. Here it is in all its glory:\n
PASSAGES is my favorite role-playing game, ever ! I ran my first session last Saturday, and everyone really enjoyed it. The structure of our gaming club (the venerable Athens Wargamers) is such that I'll run four episodes, and a single one-shot (Marcus Rowland's "Queen's Own Aerial Hussars") per season, for a total of twelve 4-hour, inter-connected PASSAGES episodes.

This year's campaign is set during the early Edwardian period, and will explore many of the classics of Victorian imaginative fiction (my self-mposed parameters are: no creations that appeared after August 1914). I've started with Frankenstein's creation perpetrating some mischief in Limehouse with one of Dr. Henry Jekyll's discarded formalae; the next episode continues with a chase into the Centre of the Earth, and a show-down near Hrothgar's Hall.

I've already started work on next year's campaign (a few of my regular players are in Japan now, and cannot attend this year's meetings): a variation I'm calling "PASSAGES: An American Iliad". It will be set in 1862 New England and New York, and will use only elements by American authors who were extant before or during the American Civil War (Bierce, Cooper, Hawthorn, Irving, Longfellow, Fitz-James O'Brien, Poe, Harriet Beecher Stowe, Twain, and Lew Wallace, as well as Davy Crockett and Joseph Smith). It is inspired by Matthew Pearl's THE DANTE CLUB (2003).

I had always incorporated fictional elements (as well as historical personages) in my role-playing games (SKULL & CROSSBONES, BOOT HILL, PRIVATEERS & GENTLEMEN, CALL OF CTHULHU, FLASHING BLADES, SPACE: 1889, LACE & STEEL), so PASSAGES was just the thing. I also LOVED the "Thursday Next" stories. I've been playing since 1976, and GMing since 1981.

PASSAGES is just great. I enjoyed "Spooring of the Jabberwock" [Have you read Richard Wallace's wierd little book, LIGHT-HEARTED FRIEND (1996) ? It puts Dodgson forth as a "Ripper" candidate !], and look forward to "Cast of Thousands"...

Thanks again,

Dutch Waits
\nDamn if this didn't perk me up for the rest of the day and make me want to get off my ass to finish the Narrator's Companion and start Cast of Thousands.

So what's the best fan mail you've ever gotten. (Real fan mail -- not that fake PTA stuff.) If you've saved it somewhere and the sender has no objection (I checked with Dutch), please share it with us. Otherwise, feel free to summarize.

    \n Mine wasn't so much a letter, as someone coming up to me at a convention.

    My friend Geoff introduced me to one of his old gaming group, Dee, because I'd written Dead of Night, and she was a big fan. She came up to thank me and shook my hand, telling me that DoN was the only game she'd ever felt confident enough to GM herself. She said a lot of this was because she was dispraxic and couldn't do complicated maths, so the simplicity of Dead of Night really helped. It was really sweet, and even more so when her players all came up afterwards to shake my hand and thank me for getting Dee to GM for them!

    Heart warming makes it sound a bit cliched, but it really did make it all worth while.

    I don't have a letter. Shock: fans are, in the mean, illiterate and hard-hearted. But in person, they're articulate and gushing!

    \n

    This is at Gen Con this year. We'd just played a game of Shock: (Ron Edwards, Malcolm Craig, Julie Stauffer, Ralph Mazza and me) and I'd left my shoes under the table. I came back to get them before playing Mechaton and I was stopped by some folks. Some of them are probably here! Hi folks!

    \n

    Girl (stopping me, shoes in hand): Are you a famous game designer?

    \n

    Me: No, I'm the other kind.

    \n

    Girl: Well who are you?

    \n

    Me (Looking down, I realize that my name tag is flipped backwards, so I flip it around): Oh, sorry. I'm Joshua Newman.

    \n

    Girl: You wrote Shock:!

    \n

    Me (suddenly nervous, shoes now held up a little, hiding behind them): Yes?

    \n

    Dude Behind Girl: You wrote Shock:? Awesome!

    \n

    Me: Bnuh? I? Thanks!

    \n

    Dude Behind Me: Oh! You're Joshua Newman! I love Shock:! I'm Russel!

    \n

    Me (now panicking \xe2\u20ac\u201d how many Russels do I know?): Hi!? It's nice to ... see ... you ... now?

    \n

    Russel: Thanks! It's a really cool game!

    \n

    Girl: He keeps talking about playing it!

    \n

    Me: Thanks! I, um, my feet are cold! I have to go!

    \n

    So, hey, Russel, if that's you, sorry for being such a scatterbrain.
