\n Hey,

I just saw Fast Times at Ridgemont High tonight, and now I really want to play a game that ends up like a teen sex comedy. Particular sources I'm thinking of: Fast Times at Ridgemont High (obviously), John Hughes movies, American Pie (1 and 2), 10 Things I Hate About You.

Help me brainstorm more sources!

Help me brainstorm themes & plot devices! I'm seeing:
-shy guy obsesses over perfect girl (and gets her in the end, through dumb luck and earnest patience)
-shy guy constantly gets advice from a "player", which usually is semi-viable advice that goes awry in his hands
-Someone sleeps with that guy (or that girl), as a way of demonstrating that they've forfeited what they really want.
-Nerdy art-school girl gets the prom queen makeover, and is inexplicably way hotter (though, to my tastes, way less hot)
-Blatant high-school stereotypes.

How about specific scenes & scene locations? Brainstorm!
-English class, as they cover Romeo & Juliet.
-The class trip.
-Prom Night.
-The local 50's style diner, where all the hip teens hang.
-Montage to badass 80's pop music.

Now, what games could play this type of game well? Brainstorm!
-Misery Bubblegum
-It's Complicated
-Primetime Adventures

Finally, what mechanics could you port from other games, if creating your own frankenstein hack for teen sex comedies? Brainstorm!
-Keys from Shadow of Yesterday. Character development through obsessing over goals (like losing your virginity, say) and constantly introducing them into every scene, only to eventually realize that they don't matter as much as you thought they did? Hells ya.
-Relationship maps, specifically stealing from Misery Bubblegum
-Trust mechanics?

I could just say "Oh, I've got Misery Bubblegum, I'll use that." But, I'd just like to explore more options, and yeah!

    \nDreams are totally good as is. In fact, changing them to "who do you want to sleep with" would undermine them.

    Check it out:
    I really like Becky. Becky's a great friend, and we're really emotionally honest with one another. But I want to pull off that pink blouse and pour chocolate on her skin. Obviously, that might complicate our friendship. And, it's probably no use anyways, because Scott is really hot, and Becky's always talking about how she just wants to lose her virginity with Scott, and get it over with.

    Now, you can't just up and tell Becky how you feel. That'd ruin everything! So, you invent a plan. In this case, you decide it's best if you get a hot rod, start working out, and be a jerk to Becky. Because she clearly likes manly men.

    That's how Dreams currently work. Dreams first have "choose someone. Explain the good thing you have going with that person. Now, what do you want, in addition to that good thing? And why might going for it undermine what you already have? Great, now, who's getting in the way?" And then, the other part is that you can't explain any of this, so instead you have a plan.

    Tony doesn't explain this in his text as well as he does in person, but Dreams are rock-solid and pitch-perfect for teen sex comedies. They're the one part of Misery Bubblegum I wouldn't tamper with.
