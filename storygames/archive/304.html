"Star*Drive" (yes the asterisk is part of the title -- sigh!) was a sci-fi setting for TSR's mid-90's science-fiction RPG Alternity, which I played for several years before discovering these here story games. Star*Drive is a classic case of a setting with an interesting theme which was totally obscured by rules for Drowning and Falling in Spaaaace. (Not literally. But that design philosophy.) I'm wondering what system would work best for it.

Star*Drive is about the pro's and con's of Imperialism. The setting is your pretty generic space opera--there were a bunch of big wars, but things have finally settled down thanks to the Space United Nations, which looks good on paper but is powerless in practice. Now that the wars are over, they're making recontact with a region that became abandoned for 150 years, the Verge. The setting is mostly about how the arrival of the Big Governments will affect the locals, who have had their ups and downs.

* Tendril - Space Factories, about to be bought out by Arrivers
* Corrivale - Space Refugees, about to be repatriated to Arrivers
* Lucullus - Space Pirates, about to be reigned in by Arrivers
* Aegis - Space Switzerland, about to be co-opted by Arrivers
* Oberon - Space Gold Rush Town, about to be conquered by Arrivers
* Algemron - Space Civil War, about to be diplomatically settled by A.
* Hammer Star - Space Stalingrad, to be defended by Arrivers

Would this work for Dogs in the Vineyard? If I understand Dogs properly, the analogy would be a bunch of folks from the Territorial Authority riding in, and fixing the locals' problems, sometimes for the worse. Which doesn't quite seem like Dogs.

    Joshua - I kind of picture the players as traveling problem-solvers, either for the Arrivers or the Locals.

    The core story from the setting book is pretty obviously, "Welcome to Planet X, where society revolves around Future Shock Y! But Arriver Nation Z wants to change Y completely. Supporting cast members A, B, and C all have something at stake here, and will take drastic action in support of their own agendas. What do you intend to do about it?"

    ...which only makes sense if the players have...
    * Some authority on Planet X
    * The ability to influence, but not dictate, the policies of Nation Z
    * Vulnerability to the machinations of A, B, C
    * (and maybe stuff I'm overlooking)

    The "campaign setting book" never spells this out in detail, which means that most of the players opt for the "a Rabbi, a Half-Elf Necromancer, and a Monkey meet in a bar" style of play, and just wander around doing whatever.

    However, it does make mention of a group of Dogs in the Space-yards who roam around fixing things in the name of the Space United Nations, which is pretty close to the aforementioned core story.
