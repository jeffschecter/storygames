\n OK SG peeps,

It is 2050 and I'm sending twelve people far, far away. They have been meticulously trained and they represent the best and the brightest our planet has to offer. Sure, we may squabble and fight, but this trillion-dollar mission is all about peace and cooperation. There's somebody from every continent in the crew. Men and women. Our goals are scientific and they are ambitious. More than that, I can't really say.

What do I call the mission? Something grand international and resonant and public-relations-friendly. Something that is universal rather than mission-specific. Any ideas?

    \n Is China still governed by the Chinese Communist Party, at least as an elected majority (rigged or fairly), in your 2050? My guess is a multi-party semi-democracy in which the CCP remains the ruling party because few other groups can really compete. In the best case, it might look like Labor in Britain. In the worst case, like Zimbabwe.

    Chinese spacecraft names: Long March rockets, the East Is Red satellites, Dawn Light, Moon Goddess, Divine Vessel (also a pun with a traditional name for China), Firefly (future Mars mission). Seems like lately they've been going with two-character names with strong natural or traditional imagery, yet clear patriotic symbolism. Also, if it's a peace and cooperation mission, they might mix in some of that evolving language about "peaceful rise" / "harmonious society" / "scientific development" / whatever the next political catchphrase is.

    So... um... if I had to pick one I'd probably go with Datong ("Great Unity"), an old Confucian concept which has just recently reentered conversations as a bit of jargon / ideology / propoganda / what have you. It may be out of style by 2050, but it's hard to predict what Chinese reformers will come up with next as a catchphrase for trying to improve the current system.
