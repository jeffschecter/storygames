    I've found the Skotos articles to be a pretty mixed bag in terms of insight and relevance. One thing to keep in mind is that they're written primarily about "online prose games". This means that 1) they assume far more players than most games will have to deal with, 2) they assume really stupid NPCs (since AI hasn't developed well enough for smart ones), and 3) the options for meaningful inter-player interactions are combat and freeform.

    I like the more mechanics/strategy-centered of the Skotos articles, but I'm kind of a mechanics geek.
