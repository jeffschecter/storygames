    \nI feel cheap in doing this, but I want to make clear that this is a classically "indie" production. Meaning that we can't afford anyone pricey. And while I'd love to buy some art, we're probably more interested in just licensing it for use. I just want to be up front about that. It might change, but I rather think not. I just want to set the expectations realistically. I probably should have done that up top.
    \n
    \nSo if any of you talented people want to pull out based on that, because you know you can command more, go ahead and let me know, and we'll totally understand. There may well be a point at which it makes sense for an artist to price themselves above what producers like us can afford. Sorry for not mentiong this earlier, and apologies if we've wasted anyone's time. I was making assumptions about expectations that I probably should not have.
    \n
    \nDo you guys have a guild or something? Have you considered it? Not really something to discuss here, but I'd be interested to hear why or why not elsewhere.

    \nUnderstood, I expected as much for project calls posted here. I'm still interested.
    \n
    \nI think the real option is seeing that what you might not be able to afford monetarily -- could translate into what you can offer as creative opportunity/freedom.
    \n
    \nAlso how you work with an artist\u2026I'd rather work for someone who respects my work and is happy for what I can offer, than some high pressure high budget project with an Art Direct who makes Machiavelli look like a cream puff (I've been there).
    \n
    \nAs a side note there are guidelines and guilds for Illustration and pricing. Graphic Arts Guild (GAG) and Society of Illustrators in the US put out pricing guidelines updated every year. Most clients, other than mainstream Ad Agencies can't usually afford the prices they suggest, so most illustrators charge a range of variable lower rates -- but some still stick to those guidelines. For example my "corporate" (read uninteresting and dry projects for big companies) work rates are usually too high for "indie" publishers.
    \n
    \nI do projects ("indie" especially) for the creative opportunity they offer and my interest in the project itself -- which for me are both parts of the compensation "package." The fact that I love gaming also figures into my decision. If I can make my work more Play/Hobby and less Work/Grind it's a good thing. Life's too short otherwise.
    \n
    \nIf you are inferring to more of an "indie-rpg" illustrator's guild -- that's an interesting idea. I don't know if a lot of illustrators hang out here. If they do (could someone chime in on this) maybe I'll start a thread on that topic and see what comes out.
