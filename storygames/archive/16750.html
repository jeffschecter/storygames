\n Have you seen Chronicle? Three high schoolers from various social circles end up with super powers and, being teenagers, manage to muck things up in spectacular and ever grander ways.

Now, I am not generally a fan of supers games, but I saw this movie many months ago and it's been bugging at my brain. I feel like this would be a terrifically fun game to play. But, not being a fan of supers games (remember the first sentence of this paragraph?) I wasn't sure where to start looking for a system. Honestly, my initial thought was hacking Monsterhearts from a teen monster drama to a teen supers drama, and maybe pull in some of the PSI*RUN uncontrollable power elements. But is there something better? Smallville maybe? I haven't a clue, which is why I'm asking you. Thoughts?

    \n While I guess you could technically play Chronicle using those systems, I don't think any of them would optimize the experience. It's like how you could technically cross the ocean in a dinghy; you could make it work, but there are much better options. I think a system to play Chronicle needs to have:

    1) PC and NPC interconnected-ness
    2) Some mechanic for escalating power
    3) Some mechanic for losing control of one's power

    The Face of Angels looks promising, although I am still drawn to a Monsterhearts hack. I think PSI*RUN with the chasers dice allocation replaced with corruption could also do a fairly nice job (although a bit lax on my first requirement).
