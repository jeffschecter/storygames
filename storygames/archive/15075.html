\n I'd really like to know that a game works before buying it or supporting it on Kickstarter. Neither Do: Pilgrims of the Flying Temple, or the recent Shelter In Place say anything substantial about the actual gameplay. I bought Do for its graphic design and theme but the game itself was not my cup of tea.

Lots of popular boardgames makers publish the rules in their entirety online. I would like to see more game publishers release sample chapters so consumers can get a better idea if their game is for them.

    \n I think that different things will get different kinds of people to support. Some projects have awesome design, like Do or Far West, some will have a built-in fan base - either from previously established work like Borogove or the new Alien Frontiers expansion, or the instant grabbiness of something like Shelter In Place ("Zombie LARP! Done!"). Although some of these projects will have had a good amount of discussion of "how it works" somewhere on or off their Kickstarter page, I think that I'm just as likely to donate to a project for any number of reasons besides that, and I'm sure that there are plenty of folks that will behave similarly.

    That said, I'm still figuring the making-kickstarter-work thing myself, and I'm trying to provide a lot more information about how my game works to try to capture the attention of people like you, who prefer to know more before hitting the Donate button. I'd never argue against putting gameplay or mechanical information up on a project page, but I've seen plenty of kickstarters explode past their goal in the first day or two to know that's not always the primary driving factor.
