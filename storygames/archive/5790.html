    \n Forget about a standard Ouija board! I imagine play being something like this:
    1) Setting - The group brainstorms elements of setting and situation, which are written randomly onto a largish piece of paper. Then, a Ouija session "builds" the world." (Universalis & IAWA)
    2) Characters - On a new sheet, appropriate character archetypes for the setting are written into boxes, as are as many relevant traits or aspects that they players want. A Ouija session, then, "creates" characters.
    3) Resolution - Whenever outcomes are at stake between two or more adversaries, they are written in random locations on a new sheet (small is fine, as it's per-conflict). A Ouija session determines stakes resolution.
    4) Reward - Every time a player wished, he or she can write (in a random location) a reward for a character. An end-of-play Ouija session determines who gets what rewards.

    Long story short: making the board(s) is at least as significant as "cooperative resolution" using a Ouija planchette.
