  • Josh Roby
    February 2007
    Or, Dev, you can set it up so that the PCs are exemplars of their common group, and begin play with common keys from that group. As long as a majority of them have any individual key, the group has that key. So as long as three of five characters have Key of Bloodlust, their tribe is kind of scary and intense when it comes to conflicts. But when one of those three ditches bloodlust, for whatever personal reason, it mirrors a similar change in the larger group. The tribe has settled down. The two remaining characters are now the Old Guard, and are maybe looked on as relics of the past, increasingly irrelevant.

    Similarly, when a majority of player characters pick up a new key, it is mirrored in the group they are exemplars of. So three of the five pick up the Key of Unrequited Love (for their own, individual reasons), and their tribe also starts valuing and emphasizing love of the unreachable variety. This can be either because the tribe is following the exemplars' example (see medieval monarchies for this sort of aping-what-the-King-likes), or because the exemplars are swept up in the same broad motions of history that the rest of their tribe is caught up in.

    Now, the really interesting part to me is that, if the player characters ditch their common keys and don't replace them with new common keys, the group of which they are exemplars... just sort of dissolves into the background with nothing left to define them. They used to be Bloodthirty savages who fought over their Unrequited Loves, but they settled down and lost the Bloodthirsty thing, and got over the Unrequited Love theme, and now... now there's not really anything to make them the same group. They're likely to wander off or pledge loyalty or join the ideology of some neighboring group. They're ripe for conquest, proselytization, and exploitation. So the players must be conscious of maintaining some sort of identity, remaining true to their culture even as they try to develop the culture beyond where it started them off at.
