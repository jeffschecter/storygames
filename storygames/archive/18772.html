    \n I started running Masks of Nyarlathotep for Call of Cthulhu, starting with a prologue adventure from the Masks Companion, which let folks settle into / establish character.

    Journalist Helen Mayweather was accompanied to Campeche by her friend, Math professor Catherine Blume, who also knew Father Peter O'Connor, as, when he visited her college's campus, as the woman, aka the person who obviously couldn't be doing anything important, she was told to show the good father around and make him coffee. Of course, the good father was also in Campeche.

    Helen: I need a drink. You coming?

    Catherine: I don't drink.

    Helen: Okay. See you later.

    Catherine: No, no! I'm coming! I'm coming!

    Player: I see how this dynamic works.

    Peter: So, what are you doing in Campeche?

    Catherine: Keeping Helen safe -- although I'm regretting it already.

    Peter: I hope you're doing more than just making coffee.

    After the hangover, Helen agreed that Catherine could cut her off after one Tequila in the future.

    \nPlayer 1: Tell me about this organisation that has been hunting us
    GM (me): Mr. Robinson resigns himself to his fate and starts telling you about his time at.... Hey, I hadn't prepared a name for it - what do you guys think is a good name for this shadowy organisation?
    Player 2: How about "The Foundation"?
    Player 3: Yeah, that sounds suitably shady.
    GM (me): Yeah, that's great! So, he starts telling you how he was recruited by The Foundation...

    We've been playing mostly traditional GM-knows-all games so far, so it was kind of striking to see how easy it is to change that dynamic and not even break the flow of the game.

    \nViewScream

    The "link" is bite-sized; the game lasts almost exactly 45 minutes. Super tight game, everyone staying focus and pushing the whole time.

    (ViewScream is a live-action video augmented RPG. We all play characters who are isolated from one another physically, but who need each other to survive. Each of us has a sheet of "solutions" we can offer, which we (secretly) know before the game begins will succeed or fail. For four players, each of us has 3 problems, and each of those problems must be addressed by a different crew member. Get all three of your problems fixed? You live. Otherwise...)

    There was some clever bargaining and almost blackmail in the beginning of the game, and an unexpected sacrifice towards the end. Top notch, all around.

    \n Ribbon Drive - four peace corps members driving back after a 'tour'. The hippy decides she'll become a civil rights lawyer, but never adopts a baby from Africa, the putz who followed her out to Africa sees the relationship for the non-existent sham it is and decides to go back to a new family in Africa, the rich daddys girl realises that she can have true friends who really love her but still follows her path to study at Brown and become a society wife. The world weary older guy manages to get them all to their destinations safely but decides that for the good of the Corps he can never reveal the secret of what happened on the Ivory Coast. Best scene, Sweep (the putz) finally snapping at Elf (the hippy) and telling her he isn't going to moon after her any more. It's over. Very strong.

    Dogs - The Outsiders and the Townsfolk are reconciled to help each other survive the winter thanks to a healing miracle on the dying doctor and a successful shootout (for the Dogs) when the Store owner turns out to be a Sorceror determined to stop the heresy of giving aid to the stranded wagon train. There was theology, angsting and everything. Best point for me was revealing the Sorceror with the line "Br Elijah appears shouting 'I will not abide by this heresy' and shoots the doctors wife in the head. What are you doing?" The look on the players faces was wonderful even if it was a bit more *World than DitV.

    Ribbon Drive - two old friends drive back to Idaho from New York with a Party Girl and a DJ they don't know is failing to make an impact. They relive the car crash that the uptight friend is denying as they crash in to the back of a car transporter. The Party Girl decides to go back and finish studying classical violin, but never accepts her step dad, the DJ admits he is a failure although finds a way not to give up and go home thanks to finding out he can have people care about him, the uptight guy faces the fact that he was driving the car that put his best friends brother in to a coma and left him barely able to function, when his best friend recovers from the head injury in the current crash he finally finds he can truly smile. The player of the best friend ran for a bus, but we'd like to think that he did go back to Idaho and make a go of that farm although he probably never came to terms with the fact that his brother would never be the person he was before the accident... Fantastic game, but the phone call between the Party Girl and her step dad at the end, where it turned out he truly was a Dick, stood out. "You've been in a car crash in Idaho? Were you driving drunk?"

    Yes, it was a wonderful Concrete Cow 13.5 :-)

    \n Apocalypse World. Playing in your own city (in our case, Gothenburg) really adds something to the game! We got out a tourist map and started filling in :
    "Okay, here's my holding."
    "That's pretty big. Ooh, you lay claim to [very big school building] as well? That's like a fortress."
    "Uh, yeah, there are people holed up in there, I don't know how many. Technically they're part of my hold, and someone comes out when it's time to pay taxes, but no-one knows what they're actually doing in there."
    It was also fun to fill in areas with the blue pen and imagine those familiar blocks flooded.

    \n [unnamed horror game I'm working on]

    We're trying to escape a reality warping house full of horrible monsters. Somehow, I wound up clinging to a ledge, desperately trying not to fall into the sea of rats that had been prophesied to be my death. I reached out for help to another PC who had killed in a fit of rage and was seeking redemption. I ask "Will you pull me up to safety?". He agrees, but only if I help him get what he wants. What does he want? To be free of the guilt. To escape his life somehow. To be someone else. I agree to help, and he takes my hand. Somehow, the supernatural forces at work change me into him, and I pull him up from the ledge.

    That is when my companion realizes his mistake. I had a dark secret as well, and one much worse. I was in fact the half-human progeny of a disturbing Lovecraftian entity. Instead of finding purity and freedom, he found himself a more corrupted, more morally repugnant being than before. Realizing the error of his ways, he steps back over the ledge to fall to the sea of rats, so my body still died just as foretold.

    \nDungeon World
    Priscilla the Barbarian and company, in the depths of the Temple of the Frog God, encounter an aballin, a creature of living water. Priscilla stabs it with her sword, but the thing slides up the blade, up her arm, up her neck and into her mouth.

    "It's going to drown you. What do you do?"

    She hesitates -- this is her first time playing DW -- and then says, "I drink it!"

    "Defy danger using CON!"

    She makes the roll, and that act becomes the game's defining moment, as the players take the cue and turn the corner into the pure awesome of DW.

    \n [Dungeon World LongCon]

    The PCs have accidentally/on purpose released a horde of malevolent spirits, one group of which is trying hard to make itself corporeal enough to hurt the Barbarian built from the parts of the beast that killed them (long story). The Druid wants to draw all the spirits to one area to try and restrain them. She knows a certain five-pointed intersection in the city that would have especial meaning for them. The Barbarian cleverly thinks to abduct the son of the son of the son of a man who likely also wronged the spirits. This man's throat is promptly slit at the intersection. As the blood trickles down each of the five roads, howling gales of spirits visible only to the Druid come rushing down them. They enter the gash on the man's throat and the flesh around the wound starts to churn. The effect spreads as more spirits enter him, and all the flesh of his chest and torso is roiling. More spirits enter, and more, and now his body is spastically writhing and bouncing off the ground. His arms and fingers simply vibrate off his body and shoot in divergent directions. The Druid is aghast at what she is done, but realizes she still doesn't know how to contain the spirits. She consults her own spirits and is met with a disturbing vision. She steels herself, transforms into a panther, and begins to feast...
