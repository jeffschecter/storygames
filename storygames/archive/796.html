I was thinking of making an espionage game where for each operation, each player had a role that relates to one of the steps of "IIEE":http://www.lumpley.com/comment.php?entry=206:

* Intention: CounterIntel gathering determines the time, place, and nature of the threat that the team will be coping with. (Stopping this threat becomes the team's collective intention.)
* Initiation: Forward Command begins setting up a base of operations at the locale, and allocates resources to deal with the threat. (This is the resource gathering phrase, where each additional phase nets you more resources but possibly entails new risks. This play determines when the allocation stops, and the actual operation begins, i.e. initiation of the counteraction.)
* Execution: Operatives move assets and allies to neutralize the threat. (So, how is this op carried out? What happens in the course of that action? Tactical-level choices go here.)
* Effect: The Political Bureau "cleans up" the situation afterwards, and asses damage to targets as well as political fallout. (So, how did the team address the stakes? What else happened?)

How close is this to actual IIEE?
