    Posted By: Jason MorningstarSo, Story Games, what's on deck for 2008?

    What are you itching to play? What do you plan to accomplish? What are you most excited about in your gaming life?
    \nJason, this is an awesome post. It is so awesome, that I must compose this sentence in sparkles to express my enthusiasm. I was thinking about this lately, and considering making a blog post or something about it, but now I have an excuse to talk about it!

    2007 has been all about working on Tenra Bansho Zero, and also learning more in general (mostly from this site) about tips and tricks to use in game to make me a better player, and a better GM. With that, I've looked upon the works of gaming past, the games that at the time seemed cool, but had so much excess baggage with them that I never thought about returning to them... and I've been wanting to take the tools in my "Story Games (the site) Toolbox" and play those old games again, with some modifications. Not so much "Playing Shadowrun, but using the system from My Life With Master", but rather "Playing Shadowrun, but using a modification of the system that uses pretty much the same rolls, but using them in new ways; and streamlining combat, buying things and other bits so that they can be used in-game quickly without tons of chart consulting". Eric Provost has been on the same kick, and we've basically fastened at the hip so that we can systematically hit up these older games in new ways.

    So in 2008, here's what my current plans are:

    Playing

    * I have a monthly D&D 3.5 game set in this fictional region called "The Frozen North". I'm a 3rd level ice elf spirit shaman/thief. It's hella fun, and I'm looking into playing it, what, 12 more times this coming year! I realized that I haven't used my Diplomacy 8 skill at all yet, but in January's session it's looking like a diplomacy smorgasboard.

    * Other than that, which is set, my weekly and bi-weekly games are not set, but always changing. On my plate now is a game that's inspired by Necessary Evil (supervillains must stop the evil alien menace because the superheroes are dead) using the RISUS system, which my weekly group is going to start playing once the holidays are over. Other than that, here's what's going on:

    Destined to Play
    Shadowrun (Eric P GMing and using his hack, a medium/long term game, starting this weekend)
    Seiyuu (Matt Gandy's anime game)
    INUMA (I want to play again)
    EDIT: D&D 4e. Holy crap yes.

    Destined to Run or Play
    Earthdawn (either set in Barsaive, or set in the Iron City, an India-themed campaign world that my weekly game group uses on and off for years)
    Tenra Bansho Zero, a game that shows a war between three countries in three mini-campaigns, with three perspectives.
    Virtue and Valour (that's right Tim, I'm RUNNING IT!)
    Together by Jack Aidley
    Legend of the Five Rings, the Crab Falls campaign I mentioned in another thread here. In no fewer than 5 sessions.
    Best Friends AMBER Throne War, stolen straight from Fred. It will get run this year.
    If In a Wicked Age gets put together, I will run a generational game based on that (we had great experiences when we played from cobbled notes about a year or two ago).
    A gritty game based on World War Z, with multiple mini-campaigns and one shots that build up to a larger campaign. Haven't decided a system, and AFMBE just doesn't seem quite right. Nemesis seems closer to what I want with its Unknown Armies style horror stuff.
    Colonial Gothic: Planning a 2-session game for Winter/Spring, set in colonial New Jersey.
    ...and I'm open for just about anything else.
    If Do comes out, that will go down. But I don't want Daniel to rush.
    If Stranger Things comes out, that will go down. But I don't want John to rush.

    Writing or Doing:

    Running this site, cause it's been worth its electronic weight in gold in ways that its improved my own gaming, from D&D 3.5 to Dogs in the Vineyard.

    Running Game Chef next year. That will be exciting, I wager.

    Doing some events, namely GenCon, Camp Nerdly, Go Play Southeast (if it happens again), some house cons, most likely MACE, and maaaaaaybe Trinoc-Con

    Tenra Bansho Zero, which is finally drawing to a close. A few more months, tops.

    Secret Anime-Themed Project, which will be revealed in a few weeks.

    A playable version of the new version of the Ghost Killers. It will actually be up in a few days.

    \n Got a lot of pokers in the fire. Here are a few I am excited about.
    \n
    \n1) Getting our retail store open. It will have in store gaming, so it should be fun to see gaming happening on a near daily basis, and it should be an exciting growth opportunity for our company.
    \n
    \n2) POD Playing Cards. I can't wait to get our first card game, Kingdoms, into print. Its a game design I have been sitting on for many years now, awaiting a means to release it. And on the industry side of things, I look forward to the impact it will have to be able to offer POD card printing to others. I hope to give rise to an indie card game movement ultimately as strong as the indie RPG movement.
    \n
    \n3) Empires of History: WWII. At long, long last, this king of our board game lines will finally see print. I had it nearly complete way back in late 2002 when my house got broken into and my computer stolen and my briefcase stolen. Between the two, I had lost all map designs and most other work on that game. Had to start it over. The good news, this time around, is the game is, I think, much better than even it had been in 2002.
    \n
    \n4) 1483 Online: Full World Edition. Its been in development 3 years now, with the 1483 Online: Europe Edition in beta for coming up on two years. The original concept had been to launch the game with the smaller 28 player Europe version and use that to get the ball rolling and do much of the beta testing on the software base, then plug in the larger 95 player Full World map and game. Only when we plugged it in, we found the shier size of the thing made it virtually unusable. We had programmed that version of the software in Visual Basic and while it ran the 28 player Europe version just fine, the 95 player Full World version consumed so much memory that it even ran slowly on a computer with 8 gig ram. So 5 months ago we had to embark on a total rewrite of the software base using C+ and OpenGL. Well, going into January, we will begin alpha testing on the Full World software. Been a long road to get here.
    \n
    \n5) This coming summer we are reviving the Dark Realms RPG brand. Though while we are reviving the name, we're actually going to use the game system from our Worlds of Heroes & Tyrants RPG Line. This line will see 5-10 books released as dollar booklets and sold through the dollar store markets, among others. Many other sourcebooks, race books, etc, will then normally ne priced at $2. Only a handful of world settings and deluxe rule booklets will roll out with pricing more in the $15 to $30 range. What will be different about our marketing approach this time is that the game books will not be the centralize method of distribution or support for the line. Instead that will be online, where the core game books will be available for free download, online for reading, and the world settings will all be put online, in finite detail, and partially open to a shared world building environment. The online site will provide both player and Realm Master computerized tools, eventually to include online character sheets, random background generators, random NPC generators, random encounter generators, random city builders, etc, etc. Revenue from the project will predominantly come from online advertising, subscriptions and dollar store sales, though the other books will be for sale through our normal channels. The Dark Realms will be our company's first product launch where the whole thing is being treated as an IP for development specifically for multi platform licensing and revenues, which is a fundamental shift from the older model of just developing a game line to sell game books.
    \n
    \n6) Rising Empires. My oldest non published game design. Been working on it off and on lately with my spare time. The empire building, world conquest game with maps in total being larger than a football field, will be released in later 2008. It will be a PDF game product and clock in around 1200-1400 total pages.
    \n
    \n7) A license which I can't speak about. Yet. But we've been in negotiations for it for months now. I think we may be getting near an agreement on this one and with luck that will mean a contract signing in early 08. This will be our first major media licensed game line.
    \n
    \n8) POD Art Prints sold at retail. Its surely going to be a fairly small income stream for our new store, but simply because its new and different and can help support a lot of good artists, I'm interested to see how well this goes.
    \n
    \n9) PDF shopping in store. We'll be setting up a computer station in our store specifically so customers can shop for game products through our PDF store (currently an OBS affiliate store). This will give our B&M customers access to over 6,000 electronic gaming titles they may have never known existed. We'll then offer those customers a 20% discount on the printing and binding services for any PDF gaming product they order from us online. This will be a unique combination of PDF shopping and print/binding that no other store offers presently, so again, a worth experiment to see what potential it has.
    \n
    \nWell, those are my our interesting projects on the horizon for 2008. Some are big events for our company, other merely ones I find interesting for more personal reasons.
