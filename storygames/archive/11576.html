\n Hey people of the world,

I went to Gencon in 2006 and enjoyed it. I went to Gencon in 2009 and really didn't.
I'm trying to sort out what changed, and whether it's worth it in the future.

Pros:
1. Lots of cool people are there.
2. I can talk to game designers about their cool new products.
3. The rush of being at the center of it all, in the big city, where the buzz is being generated, etc, etc.

Cons:
1. There are no vegetables or fruits available in the downtown core. (please, correct me. I couldn't find any.)
2. Hella expensive.
3. Sweaty and hot.
4. I dislike the heavy commercial aspect to it.
5. I find it hard to actually get a solid gaming schedule in.

So, I'm debating its value.
I don't like buying stuff. I don't like fried food.
I do like games. I do like people. I do like talking about games and new ideas in gaming.

Is Gencon the place for me?

    \n To address your cons\u2026

    1. Food:

    Quality places to eat if you are vegetarian are scarce but I really enjoy\u2026
    - Cafe Patachou
    - Noodles & Company
    - There is a good make your own burrito place in the mall

    2. Money:

    In terms of expenses, I suggest bringing a mini inflatable mattress and crashing on a hotel room floor. Usually roommates won\u2019t charge you an even split but a reduced amount. And I find this a better option than staying far away.

    3. Heat:

    This is extremely variable but you don\u2019t have to spend too much time outside. You can even use the walk ways between the convention center and hotels to get to many places.

    4. Commercial Emphasis

    The buy emphasis is minimal at Games on Demand and the Embassy Suites or pretty much anywhere outside of the dealer room. You can fill your schedule with out of print or very old games that no one is even selling at the con. The boardgame ballrooms are also fantastic as they have plenty of open tables and games you can try and play for free. They sometimes even have people that will teach you the rules and give you free food! The Rio Grande room last year even had a veggie buffet!

    5. Gaming

    I\u2019d love for us to develop a backchannel for playing easy pickup games. It\u2019s already very easy between Games on Demand and the Embassy Suites. Maybe using twitter, texting, a mobile wiki, or ?

    My understanding is there will be way less of an indie presence in the dealer room. No Pirate Jenny, possibly no Forge Booth, no Play Collective, and no Ashcan Front. It will likely be Design Matters (which is wonderful but small) and IPR. Even more of a reasons to focus on Games on Demand and backchannels.

    In conclusion, I would add up the expenses in terms of time and money and explore all options. If I had to choose between Gencon or going to several small indie cons (Go Play Northwest, Camp Nerdly, Dreamation, Jiffycon) I would go to 3-4 indie cons instead. That said, due to travel, Gencon can be cheaper (and uses less vacation) although going to several small indie cons will give you more personal face time with people.

    \n Before I became a dealer tending a booth (Hamster Press will be there with my latest Matrix Games all made at my workshop in Southern Indiana) I went to Gen Con to run games - to spread the word about my style of play. If I had not had that prophetic zeal I doubt I would ever have started going, which would have been a mistake for me.

    Gen Con always has very high quality games in all the genres of play. Some of the best role playing I've ever done I did at Gen Con. That alone makes it worthwhile to me. I wish I could play now but I'm commercially driven anymore.

    As to the food situation. Convention Hall food is always bad. I always leave the hall to eat. Vegetables make me feel good so I get then at every meal. The Food Court in the mall has veggies and steak places always have salads.

    Then comes the cost. For me, anymore, Gen Con is a profit making trip because I'm selling stuff at my booth and keeping expenses real low because I commute in to the con from Bloomington (an hour south of Indy). When I paid for a hotel I shared space with friends which made it less costly but it was always a big cost. Gen Con is a spectacle and they cost. I think it is the major trip for many people. If it isn't for you then maybe don't go. It will always be costly.

    All in all I love Gen Con and look forward to attending for many years to come. I anticipate a time in the future when I get out of the commercial side and get to play again. That will be nice.

    Chris Engle
