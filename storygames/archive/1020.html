      If someone is hurt, that tends to stick a while.

      If someone suggests that a person's labour of love is a derivative or otherwise is an unmeritorious product, that tends to hurt.

      This is also by the way why some folk have a long time hate-on on the Forge IMO. Leaving aside the whole brain damage thing, there are a lot of folk who at one point or another have gone over there with their game and said "here it is, look at my baby, isn't it beautiful?"

      The Forge culture likes honest and direct criticism. Not all games are things of beauty. Sometimes the game may in fact be a thing of beauty but distinctly not to the taste of one or more prominent posters, either way same result. Harsh words are spoken.

      Once somebody has said your baby's ugly you're not going to forget it, 12 years later at the bar mitzvah you'll be looking over at them thinking "hey, that bastard thought my baby was ugly, how dare he come here now?" even though the guy in question has probably long since forgotten the conversation and may have changed his mind anyway.

      The heartbreaker phrase does much more harm than good in this respect as well, even though personally I entirely sympathise with the essay that coined the term I think with hindsight it's proved hurtful to a lot of people who felt their game and thus they were being judged. It doesn't help that Ron can at times from outside seem to be unnecessarily aggressive and brusque. I've seen him say he doesn't think it's wrong to be hurtful, leaving aside whether I agree a likely consequence of that is some folk will get hurt and those folk will bear grudges, that's being human.

      So, some folk will always hate the place and anything associated with it. The Forge, The Forge Bookshelf, IPR (after all, Indie is in the title). The whole scene. Nobody likes the person who said their baby was ugly.
