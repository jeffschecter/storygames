    \n I'd probably go arcane on this: look up the corresponding metals or planets or gems or platonic solids or potted plants for the sins, as they're going to have something like that anyway, even if I don't remember seeing any off-hand. (Medievals were crazy for claiming arbitrary symbolic connections, or that's how it looks like to us through the bias of history.) Once you've got the correspondences, just use the associated alchemical sigils.

    Another medievalist idea would be to use concentric circles in reference to the Divine Comedy. Obviously more than three circles is going to be visually confusing, which is why you alternate black and white stripes in a clever manner to create a logical visual series from one to seven. The end-result might look like a series of target plates, but the idea's worth playing around in a graphics program for a bit.

    If circles don't work but you like the idea of numbering the sins, a stylized set of Roman numerals might work. Draw them in a calligraphically interesting yet consistent manner, basically.

    If the idea of relying on Dante pleases, then maybe you could pick a famous visual from Dante's Hell for each sin. Bleeding trees, icicles, burning tombs, whatever. A consistent aesthetic, and while the associations won't be transparent to an audience unfamiliar with the Divine Comedy, they'll at least be memorable.

    Finally, Wikipedia tells me re: the first point that there actually is an authentic tradition of associating famous devils with the sins. Perhaps drawing up little caricature demons that look clearly different from each other would work for your purposes. As long as you don't repeat visual traits like horns and tails unnecessarily, you should be able to give each of Lucifer, Baal, Mammon, etc. a clearly differing physique.

    \n There are some color associations existing for the seven sins, though not complete consensus. One palette is here.

    So, colored circles could serve as symbols.

    People with color-blindness aren't going to find that accessible, though. It's worth noting that if you list Avarice instead of Greed (as different listings of the seven deadly sins use either/or of those two terms), then each sin has its own letter of the alphabet (A, E, G, L, P, S, W). So... colored circles with a letter inside each of them.

    \n Very cool ideas, everybody. I ought to have mentioned that the rest of the art and typography is a circa 1920's America theme, so it needs to jive with that. My original thoughts were representative objects, e.g.

    Envy - eye looking to the side
    Gluttony - leg of turkey
    Greed - coins
    Lust - pair of lips
    Pride - peacock feather
    Sloth - pillow
    Wrath - knife

    I was also looking medieval, and there are lot of representations but they were mainly full paintings (like by Pieter Bruegel the Elder - crazy stuff). I had checked out gameicons, but that were a little too modern looking (which is a shame as I've been wanting to use them for something). I think @Nathan_H 's idea of cartoon faces, if made less cartoon-y, could be terrific, and @Mcdaldno 's colored/lettered circles could really work with the minimalist design. Thanks for the brain food!
