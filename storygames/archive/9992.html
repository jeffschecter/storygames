    \n At the Hamster Press booth I've got a bunch of new games.
    \n
    \nThe new hotness is
    \n
    \n"Thunder Hamsters and the the Temple of Cheese"
    \n
    \nwhich is a board game about hamster heroes who run into the temple of cheese steal things. Not strictly speaking a story game but it use Matrix game rules so it can be if the players want it to. Oh! They also get to pee on things... needless to say I'm expecting this one to sell well.
    \n
    \nI also have two new story games out:
    \n
    \nSherlock Holmes: The Return of Col. Moran - in which Holmes has tracked Moriarty to Dover where he meets Moran - just back from France. There are strong Dreyfus affair themes which give this game intrigue potential that Emile Zola would appreciate.
    \n
    \nThe Graveyard Shift - a Cthulhu game set in a graveyard with spooky grave diggers, juvenile delinquints, a mad scientist and a hero sheriff.
    \n
    \nFrom late last year (but after Gen Con) I have
    \n
    \nOswald's Genie - a kid who looks remarkably like me in 5th grade finds a genie in the market. It used to belong to the vizier who wants it back. A chace ensues.
    \n
    \nPunch and Judy - in which you make up a Punch and Judy puppet show.
    \n
    \nBlood and Puppets - another puppet show game but with pirates and ninjas!
    \n
    \nAll of these use the Engle Matrix Game so players have 10 to 15 coins to buy the right to make actions happen. They make up the actions and another player decides how likely they are. Other players may buy counter-arguments. A big dice-rolling contest settles outcomes. The top three games are being run by my demo team.
    \n
    \nI'm psyched!
    \n
    \nChris Engle
    \nHamsterpress.net

    \nGanakagok will be available at Gencon this year at IPR. For those unfamiliar with it, it's a story-game about how the people who live on a vast iceberg beneath a starry sky in a world of eternal night deal with the rising of the Sun for the first time ever, and the changes that brings. Think Jean Auel's Clan of the Cave Bear meets Isaac Asimov's "Nightfall"; it's a quasi-Inuit Silmarillion as seen from the inside looking out. This much-revised version of the rules puts a heavy emphasis on procedural explanation for the GM so that it's ready to run "out of the box," literally--it's a zero-prep game that relies on an oracular "Ganakagok tarot" to generate situation and prompt the narration of consequences. I'll be available for demos, and I'm running the full game as part of the Indie Games Explosion on Saturday, Aug 15, at 1:00 pm in Omni Fisher Ballroom 10; as far as I know, all 5 slots are still empty.
