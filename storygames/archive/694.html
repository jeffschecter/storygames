    Hurrah for pedantry!

    bq. Assuming you (your ship) is a negligible mass (in comparison to the planet), then yes, Matt.

    Regardless of your mass, relationships to g (which is acceleration due to gravity) are necessarily accelerations. Your mass matters for determining force of gravity, but it cancels back out again when you compute acceleration.

    In terms of falling damage, while it might be feasible to use simple kinematics to compute the jerk at impact, trying to compute terminal velocity at the table might be too arduous. Perhaps more reasonable would be to assign v ~T~ at creation (even set a base for all humans) and adjust it for equipment modifiers. A derived stat of x ~0~ to achieve v ~T~ might be a handy convenience.

    Oh good god! I just did this in class. F=GMm/R^2. F is force where you are. G=6.67*10^-11. M is mass of the planet. m is mass of the second body. R is the radius from the centre of the planet to the second body. But F=ma, so GMm/R^2=ma. The m's cancel and a=g (gravity) and you get g=GM/R^2. Just in case you wanted the math. :P
