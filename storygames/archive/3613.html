\n I've been itching to try out writing a random generator for Abulafia, but I've been trying to stay focused on writing stuff for Do. Fortunately, I figured out how to accommodate both desires: Pilgrim names!

To recap the premise of the game:\n
You're a young quasi-buddhist pilgrim flying to many strange worlds, responding to letters written by people in need of your help.
\nOne of the first parts of character creation is choosing a pilgrim name.

The first part of your name is a word that describes your early years at the temple, like rambunctious, quiet, smart, cool, witty or graceful. Later, this leads into a Best Friends/SotC-style, round-the-table character creation process where each character describes an anecdote from your character's childhood at the temple that explains why that descriptor fits. (You can see an example in my conversation with Char Underkoffler here.) I went kinda crazy with the Thesaurus and came up with 400ish descriptors.

The second part of your name is an object or place in the temple that is the source of fond memories, like a pond, library, statue, column, or roof. The Flying Temple is only briefly described in the game text. The idea is that in creating your character, you are also creating the temple. If you say "mosaic," then suddenly the temple has a mosaic. As the whole group chooses their names, the temple's most relevant details are revealed. There aren't any mechanics related to this necessarily, I just thought it'd be a cool way for each group of players to have their own version of the temple. I also thought it was cool that the pilgrims aren't just representing the temple, they are the temple.
