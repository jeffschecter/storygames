\n I just finished running three seasons of Mouse Guard. I liked the system, but my players weren't as keen on "stepping up" to the problems I threw at them during the GM's turn, and they definitely didn't seem to catch on to how powerful they were during the character's turn.
\n
\nI'd like to find a game that is low-prep and high on player agency, but still suitable for more than one session.
\n
\nI've been looking at HotB, but I keep waffling on whether I grok it or not, so I am tabling it (again) for now.
\nMy group really enjoys Fiasco, but I want to keep it as our "Not everyone showed" game.
\n
\nI want to challenge PCs, but I need more practice, and my players do too. I want to ease the players into a "failure is interesting" situation, where they are more interested in creating the story than "winning".
\n
\nI've got 5 players, 2 of who are story-focused, 2 of which are mainly social and could go either way, and 1 gamist who has some confidence issues but he tries.
\n
\nWhat should I look into? What advice do you have to help boost the confidence of your players?

    \n Apocalypse World.

    \n Two thoughts on AW:

    1) IME the initial prep can be a bit more than folks are suggesting here. Good Front-writing takes time, as does any custom moves you may want to put together. I probably spent an hour per Front initially: 3 Fronts, each with 3 or 4 Threats. After that, hardly any time at all other than daydreaming time.

    2) The sex moves are IMO *the* killer app of AW. We found that the mechanics have the effect of distancing the players from the intimacy of characters having sex, which is a prominent component of *most* adult fiction. The very first time two of my players decided to go down that road there were lots of eye-rolls and nervous laughs, and then we got into the mechanics of it, and then it was totally dandy.

    One more bonus thought:

    3) AW is only as GW as you want to make it. GW is explicitly gonzo in its setting assumptions, while AW is more, I don't know...interested in themes one finds in postapocalyptic fiction. I'm not really sure GW prompts the players to address stuff like desperation and anarchy the same way AW does.

    Anyway, good choice and it fits all your criteria (once you get past the Fronts prep).

    Other leads for your consideration:

    * InSpectres is always a popular choice in these situations. The game's various mechanisms guarantee the players will continue dealing with, even prompt, interesting failures. Good training wheels to get into that head space. Prepless!

    * Dust Devils is my personal favorite for high-agency, low-prep, chase-what-you-care-about gaming. Very thin on mechanics but what there is works really well. I can prep a session in under 10 minutes, but I've played a *lot* of it.

    * PTA maybe? Although it doesn't really have a challenge-the-PCs kind of dynamic. But it's a classic for a good reason.

    \n I've had to same reaction to the special moves, depending on the group. They're definitely a key part to the AW feel, but I'm not sure that feel is something I really want with every group I play with. I feel like AW has far more killer features, so I don't mind missing them.

    Posted By: jbristowI'd like to find a game that is low-prep and high on player agency, but still suitable for more than one session.
    \nLady Blackbird is exactly what you want.

    Prep is zero (tho the GM should read the game completely beforehand.) All characters are pregenerated.

    Players award themselves xp when they behave according to their characters' keys. That's amazingly empowering and really gets people out of the mother-may-I mode where everything has to be reality-checked with the GM.

    The LB ruleset includes great advice for how to judo GM questions about the situation into PC challenges.

    And it plays best over a few sessions, I think.
