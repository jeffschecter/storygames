\n My favourite games, with the group in Clapham, have been mini-campaigns that last three sessions. We played a Trail Of Cthulhu adventure this way; then Lacuna, across three missions with a connecting backstory; then my game Bleakworlds; then Poison'd.

In these mini-campaigns, we get slightly attached to our characters and the world we're in, but not afraid to take risks. They're great.

What other games should we try? To be clear, I'm looking for:\n
  • A game whose natural length is about three sessions
    • \n
  • ...that Steve can get immersed in...
    • \n
  • ...with a strong setting, because I like settings...
    • \n
  • ...and a GM, because games don't work without GMs...
    • \n
  • ...and preferably power dynamics between players, because Simon seems to like that.
\nI'm only interested in released games, not Your Game In Playtest, and please for the love of God don't just post your game without thinking. ("My ninja game plays better in ten sessions, but I thought I'd throw it out there"). In fact, I'd prefer you posted about other people's games, unless your game is a really good fit.

Don't suggest 3:16 or In A Wicked Age. We've tried them already.

Graham

    \n Graham,

    Have you tried Sorcerer? I find that game length is almost directly proportional to number of NPCs in GM created backstory + intimacy of the Kickers.

    So if you have a back story that involves no more than about 5 NPCs plus really immediate Kickers then three sessions sounds about right. In fact, I recommend using only the NPCs mentioned by the Kickers plus maybe one or two to provide "missing links" if necessary.

    About the only thing missing in your list is "strong setting." Uh, I can send you the details on my Ravenloft-esq Sorcerer & Sword setting if you like. It isn't "strong" in that "fully fleshed out locations and trade routes" way but it is "strong" in that "this is what it feels like and this is the kind of stories you get out of it" way.

    \n My first thought was also Grey Ranks, although the lack of GM makes it...well...not have a GM.

    If you're okay with a rotating GM thing, then I'll suggest Dirty Secrets. Yes, it's my game, and no, it doesn't formally have a GM. However, I do think that it fits your other requirements really well. It was specifically designed for one role (the investigator) to be highly immersive, and the conflict system is a bluffing game that pits the Investigator player against the rest of the table. And, of course, the setting is your home town, and it doesn't really get stronger than that. A Novel-length game would be about three sessions long. So that might actually work.

    Lastly, Bliss Stage could probably run well in three sessions. When we played, I think it took us four sessions or so, but one of them was a dedicated prep session, and all the sessions were abbreviated. But, it has a GM, definitely allows for immersive play, has a strong setting, and does involve some power dynamics among the players (though essentially mediated through the characters).

    \n Okay. I'm going to be egotistical here and suggest Thou Art But A Warrior. The natural length is three sessions for the full-length variant. It doesn't have a GM, but it has setting and power dynamics between players. It's not a perfect fit, but... *shrug* It certainly fits your criteria for setting, and for length.
