\n Kobo just announced their Touch Edition E-Reader, Engadget released a hands on video here which shows viewing a PDF and panning around quite smoothly, which is pretty necessary for viewing most game PDFs. I was leaning towards the Kindle DX before, but especially for the price this is looking nicer. (B&N also announced the Nook 2, although I haven't seen it demoed with PDFs).

I'm hoping the tech keeps advancing and the price keeps being reasonable, as once they really catch on, it gives indie game devs (and even small press) some good delivery options beyond PoD.

    \n E-Ink device battery life is measured in page turns (although also brought down in terms of total time if they use Infrared touch sensors), and each page turn is equivalent to a frame in a video. A device rated for 10,000 page turns playing a video at 12FPS would maybe play a single episode of Betty Boop.
