    \n Why can't this be a really awesome thread about dragons?

    The last dragon I used in a campaign was an old wyrm, investigating the ancient secrets of the world. The party met him underground, in a buried city. They sensed him before seeing him, the buildings blocking an immediate view. The cavern ceiling, about a kilometer overhead, shook with each roar. Several ancient pillars collapsed. Dust and rock rained down.

    The party moved onward toward what looked like the epicenter of a disaster in the middle of town -- they had seen it from a colosseum rampart, where they entered the underground. As they got closer, the air got thicker with smoke and smog.

    From beyond visibility they saw several human-size figures emerge: wearing something like diving gear, with weapons held under their arm. Electric zaps accompanied balls of light, fortunately missing the party members. Several minor injuries later, the figures were down and the glass helmets broken. Two party members had laser rifles, while the rest didn't trust the unknown technology. The party proceeded toward their quarry, apparently inside the crashed metal object.

    To make a long story short:
    - they restored power to the rest of the ship
    - ... awakening other beings in deep sleep.
    - they met the dragon in the main control room
    - ... pondering a strongly anti-magic orb in the middle.
    - the paladin, questing for the dragon's head, took it.
    - I modified my fronts to reflect "the awakening", a rebellion of newly-conscious alien beings, into the world.
