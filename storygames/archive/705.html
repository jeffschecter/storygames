[Console] Role Abilities guide the plot

Colin_Fredericks
May 2006 edited May 2006 in Story Games
Background: Console is the console RPG parody game. The next edition of it is an entirely story-oriented game, and a real break from the current version. None of the old "beat up monsters to get stuff to beat up bigger monsters to get more stuff to..." cycle. The game is GM-less, and progresses in Acts. Characters are communal property and possess roles (e.g. Protagonist, Sidekick, Love Interest) instead of classes (e.g. Fighter, White Mage, Ninja). End background info.

So, in creating the story-telling mechanic in Console III, I needed a way to constrain the stories that could be told (a la Ms. Borgstrom's Structure and Meaning in RPGs article). This needed to be not just a story-telling game, but one that specifically tells stories similar to the ones seen in console RPGs.

I considered for quite a while how to do this with rules, written guidelines, etc. All sorts of externally imposed stuff. It went nowhere - I simply couldn't define what made a console RPG distinct.

Then, when I was working on creating ability progressions for the different Roles (that is, what abilities each Role gets at each Act), it just happened. I think that the Role abilities do it on their own - they, in and of themselves, can define what the plot of console RPGs. I can not only protect the Roles' niches, but interweave them!

    Consider not just the abilities, but their combinations. When the Protagonist is brooding, the Love Interest is supporting him, and the Sidekick steps up to run the party. The Man Without a Past is saying "he needs to get through this on his own," the Badass Woman is slapping the crap out of annoying people, and eventually the Comic Relief will talk the Protagonist out of his funk.

    In an earlier example, the Badass Woman and Man Without a Past have already noted how important Event X is, but the Sidekick missed it and the Protagonist and Love Interest are too busy with each other. It takes the Comic Relief to point out what's really going on in Act 4 or 5.

    Two of the abilities (Cheat Death and Beyond the Grave) only work when the character is dead.

    Most of these abilities can be used regularly in later Acts as well, though some of them might be limited to once per Act, unlimited tiimes in a single Act, or even just a single use.

    So uh, yeah. That's how I did it.
