\n In the math and flaking thread, Wolfe talked about the successful V:tM game he GM'd with himself and 3 hardcore PCs, but with up to 9 other people who would sometimes show up.

He used a bookending framework, similar actually to a West Marches-style metarule where you have to get out of the dungeon by the end of the session, which was that each session was a single night, dusk to dawn. And that's great! But I also suspect there were other things at work that made it possible to have a successful game with this framework: Vampire tends to be highly political, and it's easy to imagine 2-3 core players being the main factional leaders, with the less-frequent attendees taking on specialist roles as needed.

What I want from this thread is a list of campaign ideas that would work with this structure where you invite a bunch of people to play, but privately know that only a core set of 2-3 of them are going to show up *every* week.

Besides politics, could a military missions style game work well here, where the hard core are the commanders? Or perhaps a Trek-style space exploration game, where your hard core are Kirk, Spock, and McCoy? Other ideas?

Matt

    \nSpymasters: The core PCs are the core of the spy network, who relay intel and orders between Langley (or London, your call) and the agents on the ground. The occasional PCs are a cadre of specialists brought in on certain jobs, local sleeper agents, informants and innocent bystanders.

    Variants: the occasional PCs are a different city's network each session, and the core PCs are specialists brought in to perform the same task wherever in the world it is needed (Assassins, robbers, forgers, whatever). Or the regular PCs are the local spies to a given city and the occasional PCs are travelling specialists.


    Wacky Sitcom, a la Fawlty Towers
    The core PCs run a hotel or place of business of some sort. The occasional PCs are regular customers or guests or business clients or part-time employees at the hotel who help create new problems for the PCs.


    Shamans
    The PCs are shamans of a tribe, trying to shepherd the people through times of difficulty. The tribe comes to them with problems and the shamans ask the spirits for help solving them. But spirits are fickle and uncertain. The spirit realm phases in and out of contact with the mortal realm. The shaman never knows what spirit will be on hand to help at any time.

    The occasional PCs are the spirits, each with their own unique mystical abilities. Can the shamans and spirits figure out how to use their abilities to help the village?

    \n Villain of the week! The core PCs are superheros/monster hunters/whatever. The other occasional guys make up their rogues gallery of villains. Maybe this episode has the Joker teaming up with the Penguin to blow up Gotham City Hall. Next week, you might have The Riddler and Catwoman team up to steal a priceless artifact or something. Every week, though, you have Batman and Robin bringing the justice. Probably needs one consistent GM figure to provide adversity if no one else shows up, and some simple systems for coming up with a supervillain plot.

    \n This sort of play emerged in a big AW campaign I had. Because of the way advancement was set up (and because one player built his character as an XP-getting monster) the two main PCs had their fingers up the butts of most of the named NPCs. The contrast between the regular and the sometimes PCs was not so much power level (even if that existed) but the level of entanglement. NPCs would often turn to the sometimes PCs just because of their outsiderness because turning to the often PCs they'd get a whole lot of agenda and enemies and allies that might not want with it. Example:

    The Maestro'D and Skinner are the informal rulers of Deepwater horizon having eliminated or subjugated every other power holder and are having a physical and psychic war with a biker gang. A smaller neighbouring village is scared as fuck (so small I don't even remember the same) and their militia sucks on all levels so they ask the Gunlugger for help getting it into shape because she is not an enemy of the bikers and she is also not the ex wife of the dam builders up stream they plan on fighting later on.

    \n League of Non-Aligned Worlds: central PCs are Humans, Minbari, Narn and Centauri. Irregular players are the minor races (Corpse Eaters, Draz, Vorlons, etc.)

    Or just the UN, with the U.S., China, Russia, etc as core members and Estonia, Indonesia, Paraguay etc as minor members that drop in when a matter concerns them or they have a seat on the appropriate council.
