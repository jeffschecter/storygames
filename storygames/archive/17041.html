    \n Legally, you don't have permission from the copyright owner to make derivative works of the Star Wars copyrighted materials. Generally speaking there's no special "you're allowed to copy stuff if you promise to give it away for free" clause in copyright law (most copyright holders want people to pay them to get access to a copy of their stuff, after all), although noncommercial use can be a factor arguing in favor of something being considered fair use. There's a long history of officially licensed Star Wars RPGs, so I'd be surprised if anyone considered an unlicensed Star Wars RPG fair use even if it was noncommercial (although you're on safer ground if your game is some form of commentary or parody of Star Wars rather than a straightforward Star Wars RPG). As a practical matter, nobody who has any relationship with Lucasfilm will probably notice or care enough to come after you. Copyright only applies to specific expressions, not ideas, so if your game isn't about Jedi, Stormtroopers, or Kessel Runs but mystical warriors, space soldiers, and smuggling than you don't have to worry about copyright (i.e. filing off the serial numbers is the safest way to go).

    \n10 Big Myths about copyright explained

    Lucasfilm is pretty okay with fan creations, but they also have a pretty strict policy for what is allowed. Note that to whatever extent Star Wars references are permitted, they are entirely at Lucasfilm's discretion and not anything inherent in copyright law.

    Hans, part of -- in fact as far as I can tell, the only part of -- their policy that allows any use covers "parodies and documentaries" (reference here but that's almost 15 year old information); the Unauthorized Star Wars Compendium pretty firmly seems to me to be documentary.

    I am not a lawyer nor an expert on copyright, etc., so anything I've said here may be completely and totally wrong. But do look at that copyright myths link.

    \n Also, even licensed works can have odd clauses. For example, if you've ever read L. Neil Smith's Lando Calrissian novels, the actual references to the main body of Star Wars canon are few and far between. I asked the author about it once and he confirmed my suspicion; when those novels were written, he and the publisher had extremely limited ability to refer to anything outside of the very limited reach of Lando and the Millennium Falcon. There are a few refs to the Empire and the Emperor in there, but I'm not even sure anything relating to Han Solo, Darth Vader, and so on.

    As far as I know, it wasn't until much later that Lucasfilm policy made everything in them canon to the extent that they didn't conflict directly with George Lucas' own ideas for the franchise. (See also here.)
