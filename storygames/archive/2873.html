\n So, one of my nuuuuuuuumerous categories here that I created was "The Game Report".

I totally stole the concept after seeing it over on Levi's awesome GameCraft Site. It's called "Library", although before I could have sworn it had another name, or the description was different, more focued on "firsthand experience game reviews, critiques, etc".

It is basically a place to review and critique a game, effectively getting into a dialog about that game. No simple "read the game, post a 3 page long review", this is more of a thing where you talk about the game, or even review it as normal, but at the same time starting a conversation on it. Inviting others to share their experiences (good or bad), etc.

What I'd like to do, especially in the face of the fact that:
* We just had a big discussion on giving open, honest reviews and
* A number of such reviews popped open in the past 24 hours,

I figured I'd direct people to perhaps use this category to it's fullest potential.

Here are some thoughts:

1) If you have questions about a game you haven't played or bought, or want to rave about a game briefly, feel free to post in any applicable group.
2) If you have a specific question or problem with a game ("How does movement work in Conan OGL?"), then that's probably best for the Games and Gaming category.

3) "The Game Report" category is best used for games that you've already played. Or that you've read thoroughly and want to critique it. However, our focus is on play.
4) Please think of your post as a mini-review that others can read and reply to.
5) It's a great place to post your first thoughts after playing a new game. Be it traditional, hippy, etc.
6) If you also want to review a game that already has a The Game Report thread somewhere, feel free to either post your review inside the existing Game Report thread for that game, or open up a new thread if you think that would be best for comments and feedback.

Anyway, it's been there for a few months now, I just wanted to shine a little spotlight on it. Think of it as:

"The Game Report" : Story Games' Category of the Month. Mmmmm, sexy.

-Andy
