\n Ref: RPGNow's bundle of $1,400 worth of PDFs in exchange for a $20 donation to Haitian relief efforts

This is not a slam against the bundle. There's so much amazing stuff in there! But, wow, there are some totally out-there products, too. So, leaving aside the various heartbreakers and such, what did you think was the wackiest RPG product included in the bundle?

My current vote is an RPG called "The Lady's Rock". It's a fantasy mish-mash of Discordia and cat-woman porn that I think is trying to be the next Synnibar. I honestly can't tell if it's for real or not, and I think therein lay its primary appeal. Yowzers!

    \n Maybe not "wacky" per se, but the most WTFiest thing I found was "\xc1pocrypha: Myths Of The World", billed as "a collection of the greatest myths of the world, designed to be a handy inspirational toolkit for GMs and players."

    Why WTF? I noticed right away that despite its claiming to be (c)2009, its language is rather antiquated, including old spellings like "to-day", and it seems to have been written for eight-year-olds. I googled a bit and found that the text is an exact copy of "Classic Myths" by Mary Catherine Judd, published 1901. Which you can read and download for free thanks to Project Gutenberg, thus saving yourself 99\xa2. As a bonus, the Gutenberg copy doesn't block the Copy command and doesn't come with a three-page license agreement at the end.

    In fairness, once I pointed this out to the publisher he admitted the authorship and copyright notice were a mistake and says he's corrected it. Nevertheless, it still seems kind of sleazy to repackage a century-old kids' story book as an RPG reference tome.

    \nAgain, no slight meant to RPGNow, but the bundle is pretty representative of how low the bar is set for PDF publishing. And, really, I'm more surprised at the placement of that bar in the minds of the people who upload their products, not the people who run RPGNow. I.e., it's awesome that they've provided an outlet for anyone to sell a PDF who wants to, it just amazes me what some people will consider worth putting out for the world to see.

    My next vote for crazy product is a JPEG bundle called "Art Fantasies" which contains wonderful images like this:



    Not exactly sure what kind of game this is supposed to be used for, unless it's that one you play by yourself, alone and in the dark....
