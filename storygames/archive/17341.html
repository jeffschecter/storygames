    \n 1) Drop the project length to 30 days. You will make the bulk of your money on your first week and your last week. Additionally, a shorter timeframe adds urgency to your project. Finally... you will be neurotic enough after a 30-day Kick. Don't drive yourself crazy with another 2 weeks worth of worry and F5-pressing.

    2) One of the risks for your project is that there might not be a large enough audience for your game to justify the costs/efforts of creating physical copy vs. PDF. And I assume it will be a softcover book, yes? It might be good to declare that point. Helps establish trust.

    3) $40 is a lot of money to be asking for a T-shirt as an incentive.

    4) This game looks VERY interesting. Make sure to promote it in non-RPG places. My friend teaching ESL in S. Korea, for instance, would love this game, even though as a general rule she hates RPGs.

    \n This looks great, man. Probably not a game I would back, but it's the right sort of quality that I would trust to back, if that makes sense?

    I'm wondering if maybe you want to limit the sketch bundles, though. The last thing you need is it to be a runaway success and you owe 1300 people each a sketch, you know? Maybe you could limit it at the start and open up more if it looks popular?

    Also, one other thing that I've been seeing around Kickstarter lately - a few "early backer" bundles. Limited number, but get the product for a slightly cheaper rate than other people. The idea is to drive early traffic to pledge early ("if I pledge now, I save 5 dollars") and thus get your numbers up at the start, which tends to drive more traffic (some people will only fund a project that's nearly funded, or already funded). If it were me, I'd offer a couple of your 25 dollar bundles at 20 bucks, but I obviously don't know what your profit margins are on the books. Just something to think about.

    Good luck.

    \n Hey KK:

    Kickstarters that go even a bit over over 30 days make less money. Set the Kickstarter to exactly enough days to end on a Sunday without going over 30 days. That means, ideally, start it on a Friday. Is $6,000 your absolute minimum for doing the project? A lower goal might actually earn you more money -- there's a momentum to the way Kickstarter works. (On the other hand, international currency transfers will probably mean your losses from processing will be on the high side -- 10% or even a little more.) Just a couple of thoughts.
