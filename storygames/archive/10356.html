\n The Pennies Randomizer thread was on my mind last night and I made this little game at 6am in a groggy haze. It's kind of a mash-up of all those movies where two armies crash into each other, then the camera zooms in on the one-on-one fight that we actually care about. The fight goes on between two opponents parrying, attacking, mustering up their strength and trying to gain an advantage over each other. Meanwhile, they are influencing the tide of the overall battle and of the war itself.

Fighting Fighters Fight!

Not really a story game as much as just a fighty mini-game, but I thought you might like to check it out.

    \n Jonathan, you gotta finish that series, that is awesome.

    Thanks, Micah! But don't be too impressed yet. The inestimable Mark Sherry ran some probabilities and discovered that there is one strategy in particular that will win the vast majority of wars. He ran 10,000 wars between several different player profiles, each of whom stick to the same strategy throughout the whole war. You can see the results of 10,000 simulations here.

    always_attack: This player always attacks at every opportunity.
    always_muster: This player always musters at every opportunity.
    alternate: This player switches between mustering and attacking at regular intervals.
    two_to_one: This player attacks twice, then musters once at regular intervals.
    two_to_one2: This player musters twice, then attacks once at regular intervals.
    muster2_then_attack: This player musters up to two coins, then attacks at every opportunity. If she goes below two coins, she musters until she gets at least two coins.
    The rest of the muster_then_attack players follow this same strategy, mustering to a certain number of coins then attacking at every opportunity.

    Finding #1: Mustering up to three coins then attacking is the most optimal strategy. In the most severe case, against muster5_then_attack, this strategy wins 6,995 out of 10,000 wars. Even at its weakest, against alternate, this strategy wins 5,097 out of every 10,000. In a related finding....

    Finding #2: The first player has a statistical advantage, apparently. That's what gives muster3_then_attack those extra 97 victories. Mark suggests to declare your intent to muster or attack in secret, then reveal this intent in simultaneous turns.

    I am curious how the tweaks noted at the end of the game might affect each of these strategies odds of winning the war.\n
    Weapons
    -----------------------------
    Is it big? (At least as tall as your hips.) If so, start with +1 coin.

    Is it sharp? (Can cut through vegetables and stuff.) If so, start with +1 coin.

    Is it heavy? (Takes two hands to hold it steady.) If so, start with + 1 coin.


    Willpower
    -----------------------------
    Points can be spent to flip your tails coins into heads.


    Magic
    -----------------------------
    Points can be spent to flip an opponent's heads into tails.
    \nI guess the way to model the weapons would be to create three player profiles, each of which have one, two, or three extra coins. I predict that muster3_then_attack with a +1 weapon will be the victor in most fights.

    I have no idea how magic or willpower will change things. I suppose it depends on the minimum number of points a player wants to have before she is willing to spend any.
