\n I'm very impressed with GM-less games after trying them out this year. I'm interested in seeing how far they can be taken. Can you recommend any GM-less horror games? Essentially, I'm looking for something that can convey fear and mystery even though there is no GM keeping the secrets and directing the horror.

    \n That's rather interesting, and it could be pretty effective. I wonder if you could use Fiasco as the skeleton for such a game. I don't know of any current ones, though.

    Or an Apocalypse World hack with explicit miss consequences like "ask the player on your left how you cope with the unending horror invading your mind" or "tell the player on your right how you desperately attain their help". That's actually a really interesting thing--in one sense, the *World engine can be readily used to remove a degree of agency from characters, because of what it explicitly spells out. That lack of agency can be part of what makes horror thrive.

    \n I think A Penny For My Thoughts is very much a horror game, but it's an extremely personal horror game. All the players are amnesiac, and usually for a damn fine reason (something incredibly awful happened and your brain decided it was better off not remembering) (more often than not it turns out it was you who did the awful thing rather than you who had it done to them). It's GM-less in a brilliant way that effectively turns every other player into your GM (kinda). Secrets are absolutely central to Penny, but the answers are invented on the fly. Give it a try!

    \n The thing with ViewScream is, although you could theoretically play it all table-topish, it's really meant to incorporate things like
    "staying in character" and "muting your mic/shutting off video."

    \n\nHybridartifacts \n\n\n
    \n
    \n\nOctober 2015 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n I have a kickstarter going right now for a Tarot based Story game - its essentially a system for 'reading' the cards to create characters, settings, encounters, resolve tasks etc. It can be used alongside existing games, to create your own settings or with the ones we are making and played like a traditional rpg or GMless and improvised (or a mix if you want). Its best played in a group with everyone sharing GMing but can be played solo as well.

    The setting book is for a horror conspiracy game - because conspiracies can hold as much fiction as truth and you dont really ever know fully which is which you can go with whatever you think is true about the conspiracies, piecing it together form clues yourself and coming to your own conclusions. The horror - well the system is interpretive of the cards and you can build adversaries and situations bit by bit as you go so you are always taking a step into the unknown. Many of the creatures in the game form from your subconscious both in the game world and as you interpret the cards in play so they link together (the system and the setting) to produce them on the fly.

    For instance - I was playing a round with it myself today and drew cards for a random setting - it ended up being based around the Strength card that traditionally shows someone wrestling with or taming a lion - so a circus setting seemed apt. Other cards were suggesting some sort of struggle happening to do with weakness so I used the cards to create a vampire ghost that drains peoples strengths and lives in the circus preying on visitors. I have no idea where it is or how deadly it will be until during my explorations of the circus I get a card that would indicate its attacking - and then I draw the cards to see how dangerous it is as I go. Being a vampire - every time it hits my character and does damage it would grow stronger from it, so the longer I fight it the harder it gets... and its all based on card draws that nobody can know the results for in advance. For an added aura of fear you could always have it already feeding and simply grow its power as you explore the circus - the longer it takes to 'find' it with an appropriate card the more dangerous its getting... each card you draw its power gets 1 stage higher.

    To make it worse - these creatures in the game, called shards, that form out of your subconscious and feed on you can also increase in number - so when the first vampire shard gets to its max strength, another one starts to form... exploring the circus becomes rather...tense.
