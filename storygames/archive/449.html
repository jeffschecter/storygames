    I agree entirely, and have been thinking about such a game since I first encountered Button Men. But every game winds up being "play a game of Button Men to resolve conflicts".

    One key bit that is interesting and might be useful: the size of die you capture is how many points you get. So a big die is good, but also a liability, because if your d20 rolls a 2, you lose 20 points in a single turn.

    I had considered using the Button Men rules in a Game Chef entry as they are so cool! But as Mr. Teapot said, it moved the game's focus away from where I wanted it to be.

    I'd suggest making it a bit "Dogsy". You get a big die for big, powerful yet clumsy Traits ("My big hammer", d20. "Just SHUT UP!", d20.) You also get small dice for nimble, fast, minor Traits ("Small knife", d4. "I beg your pardon?", d4). You take the Traits which apply to your conflict, and that gives you the set of dice you want to use in that conflict.
