\n My group is going to be using In A Wicked Age to run a spy/techno-thriller anthology that jumps back and forth between the modern day and the cold war era. It'll feature super-soldiers, spys, runaway-science and some paranormal elements; though all filtered through a very gritty, 'matter-of-fact' lens. While we've tossed the terms 'supers' and 'post-human' around, we're thinking of featuring mostly low-level stuff. For example, rather than being able to command and channel electricity a 'special' individual's nervous system might be able to generate enough current to stun another person or disrupt electrical equipment, two or three times a day. And even that with considerable fatigue after each 'jolt'.

Some of our inspirations include:

The "Bourne" series of films
Warren Ellis' Global Frequency comic
The Manchurian Candidate
Ed Brubaker's run on the Captain America comic
Just about any Tom Clancy novel

I've listed the Oracle elements we've come up with so far, and I'd love to see what suggestions you all might have.

Persons

An skilled but aging spy-master, being forced to retire.
A stern Russian chairman looking for asylum.
A dead body in an alley who's ID doesn\u2019t match his face.
A waiter carrying a wine bottle, secretly poisoned.
A famous singer found dead in her dressing room, no apparent wounds.
A weapons dealer, waiting for his extremely late buyer to arrive.
A man hiding in the back seat of a car, bullets shattering the windows of the vehicles around him.
A victim fallen from a twelve-story balcony, stumbling into a nearby, dark alley, uninjured.
A sniper, waiting patiently on a rooftop, his eyes blind with white cataracts.
A clerk dousing desk, computer and cabinets with kerosene, smoke wafting in from other offices already burning.
A sleeper agent, brainwashed by the other side, a trigger codeword buried in his sub-concious.
A betrayed agent gone AWOL, seeking revenge against his handlers.
A poor mexican boy amusing his friends by raising pencils off his desk, without touching them.

Places

A dilapidated safe-house, in the red-light district, marked for demolition.
A dimly lit interrogation room, blood freshly split on the floor.
A park bench, marked for a clandestine pickup.
An long abandonded POW camp, fresh tire marks leading into the gates.

Things

A convoy of missile-trucks, covertly surveilled by ambushers.
A cache of man-portable surface-to-air missiles, hidden in village elder\u2019s hut.
A mysterious package containing a cryptic note and key labeled \u201c#106\u201d.
A plane, descending, out of control over the De-militarized Zone.
A train station locker containing a booby-trapped leather case.
Two satellite photos of same man - one on a Paris street corner, the other at the Kremlin - timestamped a minute apart.
A internal CIA file on the desk of an investigative reporter, it's pages listing known government paranormal agents.
A suitcase EMP device, its faulty circuitry fated to activate immediately as the timer is set.
Crates of guns escorted throught the jungle. Sweat, machetes, and mosquitos.
An ICMB carrying a nuclear warhead disappearing from radar 20 minutes after launch.
Two fingerprint sets taken from the same man twenty years apart, they don't match.
The robbery of a Hong Kong bank backed by Chinese intelligence, the culprits operating with military precision.

Any thoughts or comments?

    \n Dave, that rocks. Just the right combination of imperial pretension and seediness for what I'm trying to do. I was tempted to keep hitting refresh to see if any allusions to Lady Di come up, but I think I'd rather not know.

    And I came up with a d6 resolution for this, building off of what JD said:
    1--American/British
    2--Russian
    3--Third Nation (either an ally or a neutral country)
    4--Non-state Actor (i.e., the Catholic Church, or an oil company, or a terrorist group, or a crazy local militia)
    5--Roll twice, ignoring rolls of 5 or 6; first roll is formal allegiance, second is true allegiance
    6--Roll twice, ignoring rolls of 5 or 6; first roll is formal allegiance, second is the faction that is manipulating or recruiting that person.

    What should we call this -- the "LeCarre Oracle"? "In a Cold Age"?

    By the way, is it kosher to use more than one Oracle for a game, or is that drifting it? Or does it even matter?

    \n Vasco,
    I've given your entries some thought and I think they're cool, but they'd be cooler if they were noisier, I think. I mean, picture the beginning of any Tom Clancy novel. There's usually a scene where some normal guy noticing something is wrong -- too many ships in the harbor, too many air tanks on the divers, a sudden huge purchase of computer-controlled lathes being delivered to a remote atoll -- something weird and anomolous. Then he brings it to somebody's attention, and more often than not gets offed. The protagonists (and the reader) don't find out what the significance of it all was until later on. It would be cool to do something like that with the Oracle.
