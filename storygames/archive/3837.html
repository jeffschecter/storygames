    \n I've been playing guitar for twenty years, and I used to be in bands that actually played clubs and got paid.* A group of long-time friends and I** get together about once a year and spend a weekend recording an album. We've done twenty-two so far, not including our repertoire of weird Xmas songs we add to each season; among the former is a rock opera we managed to get produced as part of a local director's showcase about ten years back. The latter we performed at a local theater just 2-3 years ago.

    Right now, I have Apple Logic Pro and Bias Peak running on my G5, and I use a MOTU 828mkII as my audio interface. For recent sessions, my goofy band will track using my rig, but then another guy takes the audio back to his home ProTools setup and does mixing. Back in the day, though, we'd use a 4-track tape machine, and plain ol' stereo cassette tape before that.

    Instrument-wise, I've got a Fender Clapton Strat, Gibson 335S Professional, Taylor 6- and 12-strings, and a Warwick bass. I have a VHT Pitbull 45 amp that's gathering dust, as it's just easier these days to use my Yamaha DG-Stomp and go direct.

    Whee! Non-RPG gear pr0n!


    * In as much as any local band "gets paid."

    ** N necessarily SFW. The most listenable music is probably the last 3-4 albums under the Sessions menu.
