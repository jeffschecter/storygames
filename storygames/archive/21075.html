\n This is the project I was working on when I stumbled on the the slot grid as a solution.

Four default NPC:s.


The fill-in-boxes (and I suggest using scrap paper on the side instead, so you can reuse these sheets for every NPC) are completely deterministic so you only need the NPC:s level written down in the prep.

Prep overrides details on these. Spells are overwritten from left to right (i.e. "Fly" is overwritten before "Lightning Bolt") unless the prep says otherwise. If the prep says they have some armor that gives them AC 19, well, then they have AC 19. If the prep says they have magic items, +1 weapons etc, well, then they do have that. If the prep says they're dwarven, well, then they can see in the dark obv. But having a default is valuable. Makes it a lot easier to only prep relevant things.

When I make 5e stuff I always use existing stat blocks instead of rolling up a full NPC. But I'm converting some 2e stuff right now and it's always "She's a level 11 Wizard" and stuff like that, and that's why I need these. They use the same simplified NPC rules that the monster manual uses, i.e. all classes have d8:s for hit dice etc and they don't have all the abilities that a full NPC would have.
