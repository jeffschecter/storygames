Since the writing for the game section of the Blowback text is 80% done, I'm starting to think about the appendices that will help get people into that spy mindset. Besides a glossary of terms, I'd like to have a section with overviews of various towns and cities, small and large, that provide jumping-off points for people who've never been there. (The idea is that you either run the game someplace everyone knows really well, or someplace that no one has ever been to.) By giving a big-picture explanation of what the place is like, there will be just enough reality to give people an idea of what to make up.

I figure it'll give a good mix of geographic locations, population sizes, and whatnot. Write one (or more) up, and I'll put it in the book along with a thank you. Coolest one gets a free hardcopy of the finished product! Let's give it 'til the end of the month.

Here's an example of my hometown:

Great Falls, MT

Population: around 50K
Terrain: entirely flat, few trees, Missouri River cuts the town in half
Strategic locations: 5 large hydroelectric dams; three bridges, one is in disuse; Malmstrom Air Force Base, a nuke base from the cold war
Racial makeup: Mostly white, with Hispanic and Native American populations; some African Americans on base
Neighborhoods: Fox Farm (middle class), Sun Valley (impoverished), The Hill (upper middle class)
Area with heavy foot traffic: Holiday Village Mall; Central Ave
Areas with little traffic: Great Falls International Airport, Paris Gibson Museum
If the population is less than 100K, nearest population center: Billings, MT

BONUS, optional--
Recent local headlines: Superintendent accidentally fires gun during class; [The town of] Belt hurries to replace failing water tank; Tribes up the Ante in Montana Trout Tournament

    \nPueblo, CO

    Population: just over 100K
    Terrain: flat arid steppe just in front of the Rockies; Arkansas River runs through center of town; heavily vegetated near river, scrubland otherwise; nearly a mile above sea level.
    Strategic locations: Pueblo reservoir (fresh water supply for a surprisingly large area, "whiskey's for drinkin', water is for fightin'"); Pueblo Army Depot (assumed to contain large quantities of mustard gas); the old CF&I steel mill (now Rocky Mountain Steel Mills), once the largest steel producer west of the Mississippi; Colorado Mental Health Institute at Pueblo (a large mental hospital, soon to be a state of the art high security facility); Commanche Power Plant (coal); though locals don't really know it is there, the Federal Citizen Information Center might be considered "strategic"; Vestas has announced plans to build the largest plant for building towers for wind turbines.
    Racial makeup: About half anglo, 45% Latino, 2% African American, 2% Native American. Handful of Asians, mostly of Japanese decent, from when nearby area was used as an internment camp in WWII. For a game like Blowback, it is probably relevant that the local university has a larger than average Iranian population (as Pueblo's climate is similar to Iran's, from what I understand). Though not racial, it may also be worth noting that, being an old steel town, Pueblo is a strongly Democratic stronghold in a politically mixed state.
    Neighborhoods: To many to mention. Neighborhoods often grouped into "sides": South Side (post 1960's residential, more affluent), Central (stone Victorian houses, city center, mixed economically), North Side (post 1930's residential, middle class), East Side (less affluent), Pueblo West (newest, most affluent)
    Area with heavy foot traffic: Pueblo Mall, Colorado State Fair (when running), Main Street (weekend nights), Colorado State University-Pueblo, City Park.
    Areas with little traffic: extensive river trail (foot and bike) system, most museums, Pueblo Country Club, Sangre de Cristo Art Center.

    Recent local headlines: Pueblo Reservoir at highest level in a decade; Study: No big impact from exploding mustard shells; Colorado State University Board Unanimously Bans Concealed Weapons; Puebloans in poor health, says study; Court Gives Custody of Grandmother's Frozen Head to Cryonics Company

    \n Nanjing, China

    Population: 7-8 million
    Terrain: the core of the city is nestled between the Yangtze river (to the north and west), Black Tortoise Lake and Purple Mountain (to the northeast), and the remnants of the old city wall, and a series of ancient canals that are polluted and full of algae, unless there's recently been a clean-up campaign
    Strategic locations: the Yangzte River Bridge, which connects it to the north; the Port of Nanjing, biggest port on China's biggest river; Nanjing Railway Station, major hub of transportation; Nanjing Lukou International Airport; the brand-new Nanjing Metro; Nanjing Sample Technology Co, which makes intelligent traffic control systems for the Chinese government; Jiangsu Provincial Government; tomb of "father of the nation" Sun Yatsen, an icon of Chinese nationalism on both sides of the Taiwan Strait; headquarters of the Nanjing Military Region of the People's Liberation Army, responsible for the forces pointed at Taiwan, among other things.
    Racial makeup: 98.5% Han Chinese; of the remainder, 84% are Hui, ethnically Chinese Muslims.
    Neighborhoods: Black Tortoise (south of the lake, where the old Presidental Palace of the Nationalist Party government is), Qinhuai (near the old city gate, home to the massive Lantern Festival at the Confucius Temple every year), Drum Tower (the center of the city, where Nanjing University is), Pukou (on the other side of the river, a historical area known for being the birthplace of the historian Sima Qian), Baixia (the commercial district between Drum Tower and Qinhuai).
    Area with heavy foot traffic: New Crossroads shopping area; gaudily retro-looking 1912 Entertainment District by the Presidential Palace; massive, brand new Nanjing City Library; any major street (it's China, there are people everywhere)
    Areas with little traffic: Taiping Rebellion History Museum, the wacky Avant-Garde Bookstore built into an abandoned carpark beneath the 50yo concrete Five Mountains Sports Complex, Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception (1870), Jiangsu Provincial Kun Opera Theatre, on-top of the ancient city wall

    BONUS, optional--
    Recent local headlines: Snowstorms Close Roads, Disrupt New Year Travel; Baby Dies While Doctor Plays Online Game; Official Outed by Netizens Gets 11 Years; Japanese Prime Minister to Visit Nanjing Massacre Museum; Breeder Names $200,000 180-Pound Purebred Tibetan Mastiff After Obama

    \nDublin, Ireland

    Population: 1.4 million
    Terrain: The lazy, brown Liffey slithers through the city centre like a languid serpent. Beyond the city you can sometimes make out the Wicklow mountains and, as such, the land gets hillier the farther you travel from the centre. Phoenix Park, the largest park in Europe, is busy with forests and wild deer.
    Strategic locations: Dublin castle, the GPO, several small bridges across the river Liffey, Hill 16 (the IRA pub\u2014local knowledge says so but it's not official, not sure you want to put this in your book!), Hueston Station, The D\xe1il
    Racial makeup: Mostly white, though there's a varietal demographic closer to city centre. If 80% of Dubliners are Irish, 15% are Polish (by passport, not heritage), 3% are Chinese and the rest are mixed. However, Dublin City is packed full of tourists, meaning every shop, street and shopping centre is packed with an eclectic mix of foreign language. Great for a spy if he's pretending to be someone he's not.
    Neighbourhoods: Howth (upper class, blue blood), Dalkey (upper middle class), Dolphin's Barn (lower middle class, dangerous at night), Bluebell (industrial zone, dangerous), Ballymun (impoverished, dangerous)
    Area with heavy foot traffic: Grafton Street, Temple Bar (it's a district, not a bar)
    Areas with little traffic: Phoenix Park, Mountjoy Square, Kilmainham Hospital

    BONUS, optional--
    Recent local headlines: St. Patrick's Day Violence Warning, Six held in Belfast dissident investigation, Irish debt stands at \u20ac1.1 trillion, Seven held over murder conspiracy

    BONUS, extra info--
    There are no guns in Dublin. That is, the only people to carry weapons are a special tactics unit called the Emergency Response Unit (ERU), who dress in an appropriately scary manner. Normal police, called the Garda\xed, carry batons. Crime in Ireland is very rarely gun crime (unless drugs are involved). However, it is rumoured that the Russians and the Chinese gangs are armed. The thing is, crime syndicates keep to themselves. It is supposed that if a mafia style organisation started pushing too hard they'd get a call from the locals (a certain disbanded military organisation).

    \nMilford, CT

    Population: 50K+
    Terrain: coastal, founded at mouth of the Wepawaug and larger Housatonic River, rolling farmland to the north being turned into developments, beach areas split between huge multi-million dollar homes and small single family ranch houses, Audubon and State parks along seashore: Silver Sands State Park created after holdout "Chicken Lady" who lived/squatted on the beach in a mobile home died and allowed work to go forward.
    Strategic locations: Washington Bridge: historic site, longest drawbridge on the Boston Post Road; Igor Sikorsky Memorial Bridge--crosses the Housatonic river just south of Sikorsky Aircraft Corporation, a foundational innovator of helicopters, supplier of the Presidential helicopter since 1957, and the builder of the UH-60 Blackhawk, SH-60 Seahawk, and experimental crafts such as the Sikorsky S-72 and the Sikorsky Cipher; Milford Hospital; Milford Highway--connects Rte I-95 and Merrit Parkway/Wilbur Cross Pkwy
    Racial makeup: Mostly white, with marginal representation of minority populations unlike the impoverished nearby urban centers Bridgeport and New Haven.
    Neighborhoods: Milford center (commercial, middle class), Devon (working class), Woodmont Borough (upper middle class), Wheelers Farms (working to middle class, old farm land converting into developments), Ft Trumbull (middle class beach area, historic site of battle with British)
    Areas with heavy foot traffic: Skyview Cafe at Westfield Connecticut Post Mall, Milford Center, Harbor Walk (home to submarine designer Simon Lake's last boat: the 'Explorer') in the summer, Milford Green during Oyster Festival, Metro North Rail Station, Milford Cinema Fourplex, Gulf Beach
    Areas with little traffic: Milford Town Hall, Duck Pond, Charles Island (in Long Island Sound, accessible via tidal sand bar), Milford Public Library, Silver Sands Beach
    If the population is less than 100K, nearest population center: Bridgeport and New Haven, CT

    \nGeelong, Victoria, Australia

    Population: Urban - 160k, Greater Geelong (including rural areas and Bellarine penisula) - 210k

    Terrain: The city sweeps around the trailing end of Port Philip Bay (Corio Bay), and onto the Bellarine Penisula. As a port, Geelong orients itself towards the water and the beach. The central business district is located directly above the city's major beach, which features an old 19th century waterfront boardwalk - shared by tourists during the day and drug dealers at night. Generally, the more affluent suburbs are found to the south and west, or a little along the Penisula, while the industrial northern region of Corio and North Shore is where the vast majority of crime and poverty is located, as well as the bulk of the city's industry. The Barwon River also winds its way through the city's west and south, but flows out into the huge saltwater Lake Connewarre and from there into the ocean, rather than making its way through the CBD to the bay.

    Strategic locations: The Port of Geelong is the major grain port for the entire state, with massive silos lined up along the shore. It also provides an attractive location for some of the state's heavy industry - the Alcoa aluminium refinery at Port Henry, the massive Shell oil refinery at Corio (located next door to the country's most elite private school, Geelong Grammar), and the Ford motor plant. A few kilometres north of the town sits Her Majesty's Prison Barwon, the most secure prison in the state.There is also a major airport (Avalon Airport, located north of the city), and the country's largest animal research laboratory (the CSIRO Australian Animal Health Laboratory), which has a massive incinerator tower for destroying the corpses of diseased animals (and possibly unlucky spies).

    Racial makeup: Primarily white, but with increasing an increasing Asian-Australian presence, and a small but growing population of Sudanese and Somalian refugees.

    Neighbourhoods: North Geelong, North Shore, Norlane, Corio (impoverished northern suburbs, shading to bushland at the fringes of Corio); Newtown, Geelong West (moderately affluent, old inner-city suburbs); Bell Post Hill, Hamlyn Heights, Manifold Heights (hilly affluent western suburbs); Waurn Ponds, Belmont, Grovedale (southwestern middle-class suburbs on the urban fringe); Newcomb, Breakwater (growing eastern suburbs); Leopold, Drysdale, Clifton Springs (Bellarine towns, still maintaining somewhat of separate identity and some rural area between the city and themselves, but essentially Geelong suburbs); Torquay, Ocean Grove, Barwon Heads (oceanfront beach towns along the Bellarine Penisula, rapidly growing in size)

    Areas with heavy foot traffic: The central business district (especially on a Friday night), Deakin University at Waurn Ponds, Pakington Street (the new, cooler shopping district), Eastern Beach and the boardwalk, Geelong Railway Station

    Areas with little traffic: Barwon riverbanks, the entire city during a Cats Grand Final, the port district at night

    BONUS, headlines:
    - GRANNY KILLER TWIST: Judge weighs into naming child criminals debate
    - Geelong knife-threat schoolgirl's fate known today
    - Nasty wake-up as car smashes St Albans Park house
    - Geelong Cats roll out the big gun for Perth practice match

    BONUS, local knowledge:

    The local (Australian) football team, the Geelong Cats, are central to the city's self-identity. When the Cats are doing well, the city does well - and they're doing well right now. The Geelong Advertiser (the Addy) devotes multiple pages to the Cats, even in the off-season, and if the Cats are playing in a premiership match, the entire city is deserted as everyone flocks either to the stadium or to their couch to watch it on the box. While the Grand Final is on the television, anyone could do anything, even in the Central Business District, and it would go un-noticed.

    Geelong Grammar is probably the Australasian region's most well-known and well-respected private school. The place is packed with the children of the Indonesian, Malaysian, and Thai elites, along with those of wealthy Australians and occasional members of the British Royal Family. Someone looking for serious leverage could probably find it by kidnapping the right student from the school's boarding houses. Oddly enough, the campus is set within the city's poorer northern district, directly under the looming stacks of the Shell oil refinery, an island of privilege in a sea of poverty and industry.

    One prominent local property developer, Frank Costa, seems to have his fingers in almost every pie going around the city. He's the president of the Geelong Cats, the key sponsor of many of the city's cultural events, and he has the main hall of Deakin University's Waterfront Campus named after him - and is simultaneously well-loved and suspected to be a massive crook by the public at large.

    The city's marina is filled with hundreds and hundreds of private boats and yachts, most of which are used once or twice a year by their owners, if ever. Security on the waterfront is relatively lax and traffic is light on the actual piers, so it would be fairly easy for someone desperate for a boat to pick one up from the marina.
