    \n I have the book, and got a chance to play it with Tayler Stokes at Technicolor Dreams in Portland a couple weekends ago.

    It's seriously fun, but does need the right GM to walk noobies (like myself) through it. Once I saw it in action, it was actually pretty easy to grasp (reading it three times through didn't quite help me grok the rules, but the second time I got to pick up the dice, things seemed to click) and MAN did we go crazy. Charlie Sheen + Jesus + Dinosaurs = Winning.

    As for replay value? Hell yes. Really, all you need is some nonsensical-sounding premise and see how people react.

    \n How is it that I missed a notification for this?

    It is definitely replayable. You can do campaign style, if you want.

    One of the best games I've ever played was a Time & Temp game. But it is highly dependent on the creativity at the table. Encourage Incident Reports, and riff off of those.

    Easy? Well. Simple and subtle. There is little at-the-table what-do-I-say-now GM advice. Sometimes I feel like I know what I'm doing, and it sings and sings, and other times I feel lost as hell. I think the trick is to act strong on all fronts (with the villain, in the office, with anomalies) and make them do stuff. Sounds obvious, but with the players making all of the rolls, choosing success or failure, and having the anachrometer count down in actual time, I often feel like the leverage is out of my reach. But that may be all in my head.

    I sense there is an art form to developing a useful MO for the villains, but I think that is a spiritual journey you will have to take yourself.

    If you have some specific questions I might be able to be more helpful.

    Tayler
