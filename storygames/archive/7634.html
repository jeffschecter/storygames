\n I'm often able to tell gamers immediately, even if they don't identify themselves as gamers, by the way they talk. One word I hear gamers use often that I never here "normal" people use?

"Indeed." Said like that. So, "Man, that shit was crazy." "Indeed."

Know any other mannerisms, words, turns of phrase, etc. that strike you as gamerish?

Oh and if you're going to come in here and bust up this thread with talks of stereotypes and get all angry, gtfo.

    Posted By: David Artman"my character" - I almost never hear actors say this, but I hear it routinely from gamers.
    \nAlso, "his/her character." As in, "Gah, I hate her character!" where the "her" does not refer to say, the actress, but to the uh, actual character. Really means, "I hate that character."

    Attributing alignments to real people, whether textbook alignments or parodies like "chaotic stupid."

    Another one, probably peculiarly American: Speaking in a kind of pseudo-accent, vaguely "British," neither a convincing dialect nor a fiercely bad like exaggerated Stage Irish. It sounds instead like the speaker isn't daring enough to take a chance on spectacularly awful, and is sort of holding the words in his mouth lest they escape. Meant to convey "how fantasy characters talk" in a general sort of way.

    Though maybe that one's just my localized peeve.

    \n I think maybe this is wandering a bit from the original intent?

    As I'm understanding, you're looking for non-obvious references to gamer culture, right Bret? Or are the obvious "Hello I'm a gamer!" references also what you mean?

    I do use "indeed" thusly. I also say things like "thusly" and "thus". I'm also a big fan of "excellent", instead of cool, or awesome.

    I know there are others that I'm guilty of. I don't know that they have more to do with me being a gamer, or just a fan of fantasy and science fiction literature, or even just a fan of leisure reading in general.

    I also have an instinct for picking out gamers. I don't know if it's unconscious recognition of this gamer parlance, or if there's an accent that gamers pick up, or what, but I refer to it as my gamer-dar. I'm rarely wrong when someone "pings my dar"; in all cases, the person is at least a fairly big fan of fantasy and sci-fi media, even if they're not actually into RPGs.

    \nI've seen people who do that, some of whom were gamers, and all of those, proudly declared their gamer identity when questioned.

    But no, I don't think that's a gamer thing. Lots of people aren't good at communicating, and some of them find the structured interactions of D&D et al. pleasant, but I refuse to say that it's a necessary trait of people who play games.

    Actually, I mildly seeth at the term "gamer" (as opposed to the admittedly unusable "person who regularly plays games" or "person who recognizes the importance of games"), because it leads to reductionist, tribalist, and sometimes even eliminationist rhetoric and behavior. The truth is, there are humans, and all of them can play games, but only some of them have played games. They form a class, not a group or nation.

    It's the Geek Social Fallacies all over again. Many people (in my experience) who self-identify as gamers do it to found social anchors that they and others can limpet themselves too. They repeat the going popular catchphrases (the cake is a lie, or whatever) as well as their own personal ones (clear tags for others in the group to relate to, rather than the messy business of their actual feelings and natures), and they blather about their characters and they falter when it comes to open and honest and serious and (I would say) loving discussion about...anything. Anything genuine.

    Hmm. Maybe that needs a different thread.

    In any case, I've yet to notice common threads in gamer speak across all types of games, and I doubt there are any. What speech patterns could possibly be held in common by people who play a lot of board games, people who play card games, people who play roleplaying games, and people who play video games that are not also common with sports fans?
