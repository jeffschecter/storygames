    \n\nDavid Artman \n\n\n
    \n
    \n\nJuly 2008 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n Um, OK... I had, what, a week to do all the layout? I can't really recall. I remember wanting to find more public domain art, and I still think the custom art was too "front-loaded (i.e. it seemed to "run out of budget" before it got past the middle of the book). I'd have liked a bit more time to finagle the header and footer art, though it turned out better than OK in the end (just not, well, perfect). And that damned red F on the cover--I'd have fought HARD to get that corrected, up to and including doing it to the source myself, with some careful PhotoShopping. What else... I guess the back cover turned out just-OK (to me) but I could have gone further with it with time to "doodle" and futz around with it. White text boxes on a subtle gradient is not really "groundbreaking," but it jibed with the relatively clean, spare presentation of the rest of the book (except the cover--which was awesome art--but which had a bit more "frills" than all the rest). I'd have interspersed the public domain art more with the custom art, but the custom art pretty much HAD to be where it was in the context of the book contents.

    How does this help? Advice, advice... OK, prioritize!
    * I HAD to get a solid edit pass--the rules are king, with a smaller budget product.
    * I made sure I built my template using Styles, not overrides (those fucking buttons at the top of the Word window are all overrides). That would make it easier to reflow, tweak font sizes, etc for the final "scrub" of the book.
    * Reuse, re-purpose: Rather than draw those header and footer scrolls, I found a font which had them embedded as glyphs and did some scaling and rotating and stacking. Saved drawing time, but still was "unique," not clip-art.
    * I'd have used something with more power than Word, but Joe needed to be able to self-maintain his source (ex: to fix an error I missed and re-PDF). With Word, I spent more time than I should have fighting to control exact placement of artwork and text around it. I got all but one piece of art set perfectly... and that one piece I had to do all sorts of "hacks" to make right (guess which one).
    * I had to make the back cover in, like, a couple of hours. I reckon the advice, here, would be: If you're pressed for time, make a super-simple cover with iconic "art" (see the first edition, "yellow screamer" cover of Dread: TFBOP, or Hero 5E) to allow yourself to use solid tones and bold blocks of text. High detail front cover pretty much wants the same on the back, and that can make getting a perfect back-plate (text stuff) trickier and more time-consuming as you iterate.

    That's about all I can really advise on "rushing" a job: prioritize, organize, simplify, minimize. Rushing with no plan will ALWAYS take longer than the time it would take to fully plan and only THEN rush. Hope this helps;
    David

    \n Our 2nd RPG, the WHAT? Customizable RPG went from concept to finished layout in about 12 hours. It was back in 1997 and we had just a single little $5 miniatures rules set in print and were heading to Gen Con. Our Dark Realms RPG, which was suppose to be our newest game release to be for sale at the show was held up at the printer (for 6 months). So I decided it would be a good idea to have another "something" to sell at the show and I had an idea for a alternate style of game mechanic for an RPG. The problem was, I had just one day before I was suppose to take off for Gen Con. So the W.H.A.T.? Customizable RPG, a 20 page digest booklet, was created, vetted through one game session, layed out and run up to Kinks for printing all in 12 hours.
    \n
    \nThe funny thing is, the W.H.A.T? RPG eventually went on to have 14 expansion sourcebooks over the next 3 years or so and ultimately proved, as a system, to me much more popular than the Dark Realms RPG syste, which took over 3 years to create.
    \n
    \nBoth systems were allowed to go out of print for a time, but later this year we are officially relaunching the Dark Realms RPG, but we've retiring the Dark Realms game system and instead the new Dark Realms game will be using the old W.H.A.T.? RPG system instead.
