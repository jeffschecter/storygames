  • TonyLB
    April 2006

    The thing that gets me about 'drills' is that it presupposes that the author/creator of the drill knows something about roleplaying that you don't, and they are, in their vast wisdom, bestowing that secret knowledge to you. Which is bullshit.

    No, on several levels.

    First: We aren't always able to do all the things we know. If I create a game that teaches a certain skill (like gleeful opposition in Capes) does that mean that I must be the ultimate master of that skill? No, it does not. It means that I figured out a good way to teach the skill. Personally, I learn from playing Capes, every single time. I practice the skill, and I hone it. So the assumption that a drill-maker must be perfect (or even good) at the skill is bogus.

    Second: As JMendes points out, drills are useful to those whose skills are superior to the drill-maker's. Just as I can become better at providing opposition by playing Capes, a roleplayer far my superior can also improve their skills with the same tool.

    Third: Many people are better than you at certain skills, and you'd be dumb to ignore the possibility that they in fact do know some wisdom that you would benefit from. Pretending that we're all equal in all ways is both arrogant and ignorant.
