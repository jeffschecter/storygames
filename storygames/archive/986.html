  • Rob Donoghue
    July 2006
    Our fighterey guys were as happy as clams. For the kung fu, ass kicking part of things, the system was all that and a bag of chips.

    Problem was, for our Scholar and Magistrate, it didn't hold up so well. The Courtier/Medicine/Taoist magic system is very intellectually interesting, but it didn't have the pure coolness of the kung fu, and those players were really feeling deprived of an opportunity to shine. Those concerns, combined with the fact that we were all pretty much impressed with 2nd edition, was enough for the players to go to the GM and say "Let's change it."

    Since we switched, the combat is definately less fluid, but the utility charms have gone a long way towards rounding out everyones personal sphere of badassity, so it seems a fair exchange. Also, as we start bringing in other Exalt types as friends and foes, it's a bit easier to do: As an example, we never really got Lunars to be very satisfying with WOTG.

    We still dig WOTG, and the GM has stated this just means that sometime we'll run a straight WOTG game, and I'm all for it. That said, i think if someone were feeling both crazy and willing to ignore copyright, the two systems could be knitted together very tightly, but the GM in question doesn't have the patience for that, and I try to limit my madness to games I run.

    -Rob D.
