\n So here's Andrew, over in Appraise my Hoard:\n
Posted By: Andrew KenrickIgnore all these guys Jason - try putting it on DMG-bay to get the best price for it.
\nOkay, so imagine this website. People make up interesting treasure and put it up for bid. When you join you get a set amount of GP as fake money in your account to bid on stuff with, plus, when you put an item up and it sells, you get that GP. That's still not enough GP in the economy, and we want to encourage creativity, so there's a mechanism whereby you have some Creativity Points you can give to auctions you're browsing but not necessarily bidding on, and the creator of the auction/treasure gets that translated as GP, too.

When you 'buy' something from another user, it goes into your 'treasure trove'. You can either put it up for auction again, or you can leave it there for people to check out when they look at your profile.

It's like a fantasy market for fantasy goods. You get more wealth by creating more cool things to sell, but they have to be 'affordable' at least at first, so treasure of all types ought to emerge from it.

Whaddaya think?

    \n Jason, yes, exactly right with the example.

    Listing is unlimited; but you still have to get people to buy them with your creativity. Currency flows like this: (Money from an account startup) -> (Buy something little) -> (Oh crap now I need more money) -> (List some awesome stuff) -> (Get cash from sales and creativity points) -> (Still not enough to buy cool stuff) -> (List more items)-> (Get more GP) -> (Buy that Longship you've had your eye on in Kevin's trove - it's a Jacobson original that's been passed around the community). In the meantime, you get your weekly allotment of creativity points to pass out, so you go browse some auctions, even ones you don't want to buy, and give out some points, which translate to GP for the creators. If you haven't spent your CP by week's end, they go away but you get your new allotment. You can't save them up, or spend them on items you are the creator for.

    \n Several notes:
    \n
    \nFirst, I'm not sure why the second currency is even under discussion. It's a closed economy, and there will always be money in it. Don't have enough to buy what you want? Then sell something cool to somebody who does have money. Sold a lot of stuff? Then spend your cash on other people's cool stuff.
    \n
    \nSeems pretty straightforward, really. No need for a secondary currency. You folks need to read your Ricardo (or even Smith).
    \n
    \nThis is, of course, assuming that there is no "win" condition. You can't do this with a win condition in, because that'll incentivize metagaming that will cause the economics to go wonky. At the end we'll all compare, sure, and we'll all brag about having the coolest stuff, and/or the most money.
    \n
    \nIf you charge for items sent to auction - a generally good idea - the end result will be is lower and lower prices throught the cycle as money slowly drains from the economy. In fact, this could be a good way to play... play until nobody has money left to bid on anything, and then check for the cool troves. Eventually the game would end when the last object had sold (or no bids came in), and nobody had enough money to post any new items.
