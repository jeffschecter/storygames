    Posted By: MatthijsWikipedia says /\u0251rk\u0268\u02c8p\u025bl\u0259\u0261o\u028a/ - which means that the emphasis is on "PEL", right?
    \nYeah, the high stress mark goes in front of the stressed syllable in IPA.

    If I wanted to get really technical and nitpicky (and I do and I will), the initial syllable should be /\u0251\u0279/ (or rather /a\u0279/ as it is not the /\u0251/ in saw) as the rolled r is pretty rare in English.

    What Joel and Jason are saying is they pronounce the /k/ as the initial sound of the second syllable, so it is aspirated, like so: /a\u0279.k\u02b0\u0268.\u02c8p\u025b.l\u0259.\u0261o\u028a/ (or /a\u0279.k\u02b0\u0268.\u02c8p\u025bl.\u0259.\u0261o\u028a/),
    (as opposed to /a\u0279k.\u0268.\u02c8p\u025b.l\u0259.\u0261o\u028a/ or /a\u0279k.\u0259.\u02c8p\u025b.l\u0259.\u0261o\u028a/, which is how I pronounce it).
