\n So, I dig the Paizo Pathfinder art, with the strong black paladin-women in non-revealing armor, and badass middle-eastern looking princes and the like.

This is different. Basically, an anime take on various cultures, with a deep fantasy Vein.

I've promoted the idea of using art books (for video games, modern artists, etc) for game background material, but this is the first time my eyes did that "jump out of my head" thing; clammy hands, cold sweat, that feeling of "I *MUST* buy this".

So on a recent trip to Japan, my wife picked up some art books (CG design mags, game/manga art collections, etc) for an artist friend. One of them, my fave of the bunch is the Illustrated Monster Hunter book.

Monster Hunter is the new hotness over in Japan. It was one of the first PS2 games to make use of the PS2 Internet interface that that system had. Huge amounts of subscribers, tons of content, for a game which amounts to an MMO where you team up with 1-3 strangers and shoot/kill monsters for loot. Since then, it came out for PS2 (again, several versions of escalating content), PSP, Wii even. It's now up there in the realm of "Killer Soft", like the Final Fantasies, Dragon Quests, Zeldas, etc.

So like many games that start with concept sketches, there's been a recent release of Monster Hunter illustrations in a fat book (a single set of 2 books actually; one color, one B&W; the color one is better).

Basically, it's 240 pages long, and many of the pages (about 1/3?) is all character concept art. And well over half of those are high concept character art for african-themed, aztec-themed, inuit-themed, etc fantasy characters.

Here's the book review from Parkablogs:
http://parkablogs.com/content/book-review-monster-hunter-illustrations

Just a few samples:







And here's a link to a flickr flip-through of the book:
http://www.flickr.com/photos/teohyc/3422556891/

Lots of Monsters (of course) and swords and stuff, but yeah fully 1/3 of that book is character sketches, most of them wildly multicultural, and cool while keeping a firm leg in that culture.

But yeah, I flipped through this book myself, and in just a few dozen pages saw about 20 mulicultural-themed characters that I Wanted To Be, Right Fucking Now. So, I guess this an endorsement?
