Time to see TORG get Awesome-O-Fied



The Possibilities Wars are here! Well, they're kinda stuck in the early 90s, actually, but you get the picture.

Restating the Rules/Conventions of Awesome-O-Fied discussions:

Purpose: You all know what happened with Battlestar Galactica. That corny sf period-piece got not only a facelift, but an entire re-imagination almost from scratch. Most of the character names were the same, and the Cylon Threat remained, but after that it was basically put through some really creative gymnastics, and what emerged was awesome, pure awesome. Well, much of that is the screenwriting and direction, but still, you get my point.

Conventions: If you're tangentially familiar with the setting, that we get a brainstorm session together to think of some things that would awesome-o-fy the setting. Just list some ideas or statements out.
* You do not have to build upon yourself or any other ideas (but you can if you want)
* You are absolutely free to contradict or ignore other ideas on the thread (but again, you can expound further on them if you want).
* Even in your own post, you are free to contradict yourself (or build upon yourself).

It's not group setting building, it's simply brainstorming.

In previous Awesome-O-Fied threads, we stuck mainly to the setting, and coming up with setting mods and tweaks. TORG, for all it was, was a couple settings in one. Feel free to take on any individual sub-setting of TORG, Core Earth, or the Whole Shebang. Also, if you have rules ideas (like "Using entirely new rules"), that's cool too.

-Andy

    It's tough for me to awesome-ify TORG because I think it's plenty awesome already.

    I mean, it's pretty coherent. Its canonical adventure design (like a video game, first you fight lackeys, then middle managers, then big baddies) is reinforced by the card mechanics. There are loads of pre-programmed Things To Do, from uprooting stelae to becoming legends. The weird settings are all echoed in the rules. The Possibilities are more than just the currency of the game, they're the currency of the setting. The Miracles subsystem, which was driven by the the power of the community of the faithful, is like HeroQuest before its time. The cards reward creativity in encounters. And I thought the no-second-transformation-for-Ords-rule was full of drama and tragedy.

    The fundamental problem with TORG and me is that it's tough to do it justice. It's just like There's Too Much Stuff. You can wall yourself off in a corner cosm somewhere and break people in slowly, but then it seems like you're denying the balls-out wacky goodness of the game.

    So my awesomification for TORG is that it has to teach itself better. I think the rules for the various reality hacks you can do (reality bubbles, reconnection, contradictions, etc) need to be explained on a sheet similar to JasonP's Fabricated sheet. Same for every one of those tricksy power subsystems. And I think that agendas ought to be explicitly outlined for every level of power -- so you start out defending people against the invaders, and then you start troubleshooting big plots, and then you really start taking the war to them with Glory and the like.

    I've never even heard of TORG, other than on here, but i've always felt you could get some interesting mileage out of a crossworlds game where your character was slowly changed to match the alien world they were in.

    Like, if each alternate World is defined by a SHOCK-type grid spackled with (the range of possible) character Features, then the multiverse is defined by a "stack" of those grids, the closer the more elements in common. And then you build your character sheet around your world's grid - say, circling those Features that belong to or inform your character. (In effect, you could make the world from the character more easily, in all likelyhood, by adding elements as they come up in play!)

    So, you could be "influenced by the alien world" by taking an alien Feature to put on your character's sheet everytime you leave an alien world you've been visiting (and/or every session that ends with you still stuck away from home). Although, i would imagine you would want to make sure that the change isn't quite so fast, to keep the characters' world identities distinct. (Make it a choice? Spend a resource to resist the changes?)

    Anyway, my crazy crossworlds notion!
