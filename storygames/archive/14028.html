\n If you live anywhere near Durham, NC and would like to play some Fiasco, consider coming to the next "Taste of Fiasco" event at Sci-Fi Genre Comics and Games on Saturday, April 2 from 1pm to 5pm. Kee-ripes man, that's next week!

Jason and I will both be there, along with (we hope) Chris Bennett (world-renowned author of the Los Angeles 1936 and Dallas 1963 playsets) Our hope is to get a good mix of folks who have experience with the game and folks who've never played (perhaps any RPG) before. If you have friends that match that description, bring them along! If you want to make sure you get a seat or have any questions, feel free to chime in here or drop me a line.

Sci-Fi Genre Comics and Games is located at 3215 Old Chapel Hill Rd. Durham, NC 27707.

    \n
    \n Ah yes. Mike had to leave about an hour in, before the Tilt. Not the best time, I'd hoped he'd see that but at least he was there for some of it.

    Big Jack Dallas and his scheme to get him and his son out of Vegas for good on triple stolen funds was slowly played out by the following method:

    1. When it would have been Big Jack's scene, we narrated a color scene and decided if he ended up with a black or white die.
    2. He kept the die before the Tilt, not handing it to anyone.
    3. We only gave the remaining three players dice from there on out.
    4. We rolled for him during the Tilt, but I'm not sure what we would have done if he had won.

    We repeated step 1 for the rest of the game after that and then rolled up his ending and narrated that, too. Poor guy got a pretty low result due to mixed dice.

    Since the next player in rotation was his son, his scenes were kind of preambles to the next player's scenes.
