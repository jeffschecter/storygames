\n Okay, I really like the forms of action from Vincent Baker's In a Wicked Age and want to create something along those lines rather than more traditional traits. But I don't have much other than this basic idea:

I will ACT . . .
(choose TWO)

. . . in SECRET
. . . in SIGHT
(penalty if HURT)

. . . with VIOLENCE
. . . with COMPASSION
(penalty for REGRET)

. . . for MYSELF
. . . for OTHERS
(penalty if TIRED)

. . . on INSTINCT
. . . on UNDERSTANDING
(penalty for DOUBT)

My initial thought was to build a d6 pool from here by choosing two means. So before moving on to the next step, you'd have either: 2D or 1D/1P or 2P (where D = 1 Die and P = 1 Penalty Die). But is this too arbitrary? How can I better differentiate between characters? Should they carry a numeric value(so you could have Instinct 2 or Violence 3)?

    Posted By: ivanwhat are cleaner, closer, fencer, fixer?
    \nThey sound like 1970s-90s US criminal parlance.

    A Fixer sets gangs up with targets, such as opportunities for heists, robberies and extortion.
    A Fencer accepts and sells stolen goods, usually in a way it's difficult for the law to detect or recover.
    A Cleaner is usually an assassin or someone who comes in and makes it difficult to assess a crime scene. They also get rid of bodies and wipe prints off of available surfaces. Later ones specialized in contamination DNA evidence - but that was usually just a big show.
    Closers are...well, I guess they're talkers. I've most often heard of them as another type of fence, but I suspect, in context, it's talking about a deal-closer, someone who acts as the gang's public face - a con man.

    Of course, I just noticed that Jason Bourne is listed as a closer and Bourne is a fictional assassin.
    Maybe it's supposed to be spy talk? Why not terms like Field Agent and Operative, then?

    I prefer 1940s criminal terms myself, like mouthpiece (lawyer), roper (con man who approaches victim), operator (con man to whom the roper leads the victim) and torpedo (a guy hired mostly for the muscle, not usually big on brains - a thug.)

    Really they are labels for personality types (often used in business). It can vary for different settings.

    I'm just using slang from the Merrill-Reid method, which categorizes personality types into: Driver [CLEANER], Expressive [CLOSER], Amiable [FENCER], and Analytical [FIXER]

    Four Personality Characteristics:

    Driver [CLEANER]:

    ~ Objective-focused
    ~ Know what they want and how to get there!
    ~ Communicates quickly, gets to the point
    ~ Sometimes tactless and brusque
    ~ Can be an "ends justify the means" type of person
    ~ Hardworking, high energy. Does not shy away from conflict

    Expressive [CLOSER]:

    ~ Natural salesmen or story-tellers
    ~ Warm and enthusiastic
    ~ Good motivators, communicators
    ~ Can be competitive
    ~ Can tend to exaggerate, leave out facts and details
    ~ Sometimes would rather talk about things than do them!

    Amiable [FENCER]:

    ~ Kind-hearted people who avoid conflict
    ~ Can blend into any situation well
    ~ Can appear wishy-washy Has difficulty with firm decisions
    ~ Often loves art, music and poetry Highly sensitive
    ~ Can be quiet and soft-spoken

    Analytical [FIXER]

    ~ Highly detail oriented people
    ~ Can have a difficult time making decisions without ALL the facts
    ~ Make great accounts and engineers
    ~ Tend to be highly critical people
    ~ Can tend to be pessimistic in nature
    ~ Very perceptive

    Here is a link: http://the247entrepreneur.com/knowledge/4-personality-temperaments/

    They use the characters from Seinfeld as an example: Jerry = Driver, Kramer = Expressive, Elaine = Amiable, and George = Analytical.

    Another link to personality charts: http://www.2h.com/articles/personality-tests/personality-types.html

    Now most people are unique blend of four different types of personality, where all of the traits are present to some extent. However, some of these traits will be predominant. While you most probably display all four temperaments to some extent; for game purposes I want to identify two leading temperaments - your personality type and your secondary temperament.
