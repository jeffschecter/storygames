    Posted By: Jason MorningstarWe're planning some more elaborate ones for Fiasco, maybe with girls.
    \nWhat would you do differently?

    The only things I could think of after watching that two-parter for Grey Ranks was to maybe splice in some close ups of the various parts you were about to talk about, which didn't come out quite as clearly, or maybe some cutaways where the player talks about what they are/were thinking.

    Another side question: What impact could these kinds of videos have on how a book ggets written out?

    I'm especially curious about this in the context of bookselling primarily online where a significant part of the likely potential buyers will have an ability to run those videos.

    \n I want to make them more focused and more engaging. So, for example, I want to shoot them better (we used my still camera on a tripod in video mode for the Grey Ranks ones), maybe from multiple cameras, and edit them better. I want to focus on particular bits and really hammer them, so that in sequence you get a complete "lesson", but if you are just confused about, say, what Establish and Resolve mean, you can watch that and it will be very clear. If I do it right you'll watch all four (or whatever), because there's a fun story as well.

    I don't ever want to depend on some technological synergy to make my games understood, but embedding videos in a rich media version of the eventual PDF makes sense to me.

    \n\nOctober 2009 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n Right, lots of visual and experiential learners out there. Games are transmitted person to person. Fiasco isn't rocket science but it is a little weird, so explaining the differences in as many ways as possible seems like a good idea.

    So the things I'd like to show:

    1. Engineering the fiasco, playsets, making as situation, making characters, lashing it all together
    2. How to make a kick-ass scene, how to agree, how to point at each other, how to move the action forward in other's scenes
    3. Establishing and Resolving, and how they differ in tone and outcome, positive and negative outcomes, conflict
    4. The Tilt and how it works, the Aftermath and how it works, dice tricks for supporting both
