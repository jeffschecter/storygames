\n Hello there, larp-heads and freeform-heads! I'm currently teaching some English-as-a-second-language classes as a part-time jig, I've just been reassigned to an "upper intermediate" group mid-course and found these people are quite good at English and eager to practice it as a real communication tool. Thus, I'm considering, why not have them larp in the classroom for a change?

Too bad I browsed through my English-language scenario collection and still couldn't found one that fits my logistical requirements exactly.

I need something which:\n
  • can accommodate 12-20 players,
    • \n
  • has minimal rules,
    • \n
  • can be played in a classroom with no costuming and no or very few props,
    • \n
  • includes some short written materials for players to read (character sheets and/or other handouts), but not a lot,
    • \n
  • [VERY IMPORTANT] plays out in 90 minutes or less. Ideally, I would love something which is designed to take 90 minutes briefing included, or something which has a 60 minutes designated playtime. That's because I nominally have a 2 hours time-slot at my disposal (but it can never be 2 full hours) and I know I have to plan for the worst, including spending much longer than expected briefing them, and maybe having to debrief them at least a little.
\nThese people are a very mixed group aged anywhere from maybe 18 to maybe 65, though half of them are guys in their 20s and 30s. I fully expect this to be the first encounter with the very idea of a larp for all or nearly all of them. I would like to avoid exceedingly geeky topics, or at least things that require too much setting exposition.

Do you know of any scenarios to point me toward?

    \n If you're still lacking options, I'll suggest something from my own larp scenarios. They're have complete player materials, but only brief notes on how to run them. I think the closest to your requirements is "A Hero's Welcome" - which is a comedic fantasy larp for up to 13 players. It was designed to be kid-friendly, so it's pretty simple with little introduction required. It would need to be adapted some to be run in 90 minutes, but I think it is possible. It is technically fantasy along the lines of The Princess Bride or Pirates of the Carribean, but the fantasy is low-key (there are no wizards or monsters).

    http://darkshire.net/jhkim/rpg/larp/
