    \n The title's a quote from Lovecraft (The Whisperer in Darkness) and a reference to the Great Depression's general attitude toward the growing transient population (particularly a matching "unwholesome" descriptor I found in a book called "On Hoboes and Homelessness.")

    "Of the five remaining pictures, three were of swamp and hill scenes which seemed to bear traces of hidden and unwholesome tenancy." -- referring to Henry Akeley's photographs of alien habitation in the hills near his home.

    I still feel Lovecraft is more fun when approached as an initiation into anachronistic transhumanism rather than an occult safeguard against inhuman things. But I didn't want to get people in a Cthulhu frame of mind.

    The goal of the game was to help players evolve their characters from seemingly worthless to persons of value by offering chances outside the artificial norm established by the beginning narration. It still needs some tweaking, I'd say, but I'm pleased with your responses to it.
