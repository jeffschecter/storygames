\n I'm MCing a one-on-one game of Monsterhearts and the local Queen is the captain of the school's junior year lacrosse team. The player has ended up joining the lax team and is making fast enemies with the local rivals. Given this focus, and my player's love of rolling dice, I was thinking of playing the match as some kind of mini-game within the rpg.

I realise it may be too great a hope that there's some kind of tabletop pen'n'paper lacrosse simulator out there, but I figured I could adapt it as required. Has anyone ever tried to do something like this? Is there anything like a pen'n'paper version of subbuteo that I can mod?

    \n Okay, I wrote you a Lacrosse Mini-Game for Monsterhearts.

    Here's how it works:

    Prep

    Any PCs who are on the lacrosse team just play the game using their own stats.

    For each remaining player, ask them to create a lacrosse player for the other team: assign two stats at +1, and two stats at -1. If their temporary character gets taken out of the match, they'll just create a new character using this same method.

    In pencil, mark the middle of the 5-point victory track. The section labeled "Game Start." Various moves will shift that marker. Once the marker hits either the leftmost bubble or the rightmost bubble, the game has been won. (This lacrosse mini-game is pretty short! If you want it to go on for longer, you can say that you have to get one additional marker-sliding-point once your marker is already on your side of the slider.)

    Play

    Starting with the home team, teams get to take turns making 1 move apiece.

    Injuring PCs

    An actual PC (temporary PCs that were just created for this min-game don't count) can't be the first person on their team to get injured and taken out of the game. So the away team needs to injure at least 1 NPC character before they can take out this hot-shot team captain guy.

    When a PC is taken out of the match, you can do one of two things. If there are other PCs in the match, you can totally have them stat up an NPC for the home team, and keep playing on. If there are no PCs remaining in the match, though, you might prefer to just end this mini-game fun and ask, "So, how bad is your injury? What do you do next?"

    How clear is this? Are there questions?

    \n So, two sports type things happened in my Monsterhearts game last night:

    Dodgeball during gym class. The Chosen decides to throw the dodgeball, hard, at the Infernal. Lash Out Physically! (she failed the move, so he caught the dodgeball, and she was totally out, but it was a hefty throw nonetheless) I think the Infernal chose not to play rough. Then Coach Flowers sounded out the Chosen for the football team, having her go long for a dodgeball catch. Which she then turned around and threw, hard, at the Serpentine. Ouch!

    (There might have been another Lash Out in there- I basically said, it's dodgeball, perfect time for anyone to smack a ball at anyone else.)

    The other was the Homecoming football game itself, which had the following custom move:
    When you try to lead the football team to Victory, roll + Volatile. On a 10+, pick 3. On a 7-9, pick 2.
    *You win the game.
    *You don't take 1 harm.
    *You gain a string on another member of the team.
    *You gain a string on a spectator.
    *The Football Team will act as a gang for you until you do something to blow it.

    (And yes, the Chosen was a last minute substitution and won the homecoming game! Not bad for a freshman girl.)

    \n Apologies for resurrecting, but just wanted to share our first game in action:



    Basically, we just made up a field and, instead of shifting a marker on the rulesheet, shifted the marker on the field. Then to make the player positioning relevant we made it so that only a player in the segment could use a 'move' - and so we added a Move 'move' that just let you move a player from one segment to another. Most of the home team gave up all sorts of strings to an unholy power when the players figured they could use the temporary PCs to Dark while the 'real' PCs avoided it - but I'm confident that being surrounded by a lax team deeply in debt to the devil won't be any picnic for the PCs going forwards.

    It was great fun! I love the Homecoming Match move mentioned above, but I've just the one PC and the lax team has become their primary social circle we're going to use this method for all our future games.

    \n If you really want to go all high-school sports on it, I'd suggest a move like:

    Before a match, and again at the end of the half-time break, hold 1 for each of the following that is true:
    - Everyone on the team is getting along with each other
    - You've all been showing up to regular practices
    - The coach is trusted and giving good advice
    - Someone's made a genuinely inspiring speech
    During the match, any team member can spend one hold to roll +1

    Also, there's no reason a match can't be played exactly like a combat, i.e. to do it, do it. Say where everyone is (use minis!), describe the situation, react, and make moves as appropriate. Maybe make a few "play" moves, like "drive", "defend", "shoot" or whatever. That's how I'd do it if sports were going to be a focus of play. Maybe for less important matches, just let the outcome be decided by a single play.
