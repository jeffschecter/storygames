    \n True Believer is a great move, although its effects should be automatic. You don't need to make them roll. This move is already limited by whoever frames the scene (if you don't want the minion there, don't have the gang there).

    Did you reference the Velvet Underground in this skin? Take 1 forward.

    I wish one of these moves let me be more like our leader. Oh wait, I can buy the Hollow's move to steal moves. Or crap, I can just buy the Queen's move and be just like her. Okay, now I want to play the Acolyte.

    \n I like the Interfere version of True Believer. But it's only interesting if another PC is part of the gang- a lot of Acolyte moves have this problem- and that's not likely to happen by default. Just like the Mortal would be kind of flat with an NPC lover, the Acolyte will be flat with an NPC leader. I wonder if you could push picking a PC leader more, either in the backstory or in Playing the Acolyte? I wouldn't want a player to pick this skin, have them not realize the dynamics at character creation, and spend a whole session orbiting a character no one else cares about. It also wasn't clear to me on reading if a PC leader needed to have a gang already (through being the Queen or spending an advance) or if by virtue of declaring them leader in my backstory I was giving them one.

    It's kind of strange that you join another characters gang, but simultaneously there are several explicitly cultist-like bits. Like, if you're part of the Queen's clique, are they actually a cult? Or do you *also* belong to a cult? The cult things I'm thinking of: all the origins ("indoctrinated", "runs in the family", etc) and the talk of "faith" in the Darkest Self. Maybe it's "faith" that Regina George is your best friend and would never steer you wrong? If that's what you're going for it it's neat, but it could be clearer.

    I like I'll Be Your Mirror, Omega, Seventh Sign, and Rebel Scum. I think I get Tribunus Plebis now, and it looks like it could be cool as long as the other PCs buy into it.

    I don't like Without Number, not because it isn't interesting, but because doesn't mesh well with MH's themes. We're supposed to Give Everyone a Life, and that means even if they look like it from the outside, the Queen's flunkies aren't just carbon copies of each other with no distinguishing features. An official version of that D&D cliche of replacing your dead character with a copy and going on like nothing changed would be really jarring for me as another player at the table. I'm supposed to care about Alex now because I cared about August before?

    Also, if I were handed a pile of skins, it would stand out to me that the one and only Arabic name was on the cultist, right next to the one and only West African name. I assume you were just going for name diversity, but there's unfortunate implications there.

    \u2022 Regarding names, it seems I have a choice between Eurocentrism and negative associations
    \nI struggled with this, too. In the end, I think the best strategy is to list names from a variety of cultural/ethnic sources. The key is to pick those sources somewhat randomly, rather than asking yourself, "What cultures have creepy cults?" Don't typecast, instead sample from diverse sources.

    I strove to pick symbolically meaningful names for each Skin. Once I'd picked out an ethnic or cultural group, I'd scan a names list (baby names lists work great) looking for one with a fitting meaning. I did this because it was on-genre (having rich symbolism in each name choice), though to be honest, sometimes I feared that it might be considered appropriative. Your mileage may vary.
