\n So I'm writing this game, which involves the following seduction techniques.

1. Confusing the other person
2. Doing things to please the other person
3. Giving a sense of transcendence: for example, through music or religion.
4. Being falsely open with the other person.
5. Being cruel.
6. Bonding, perhaps over common interests.
7. Physical contact
8. Listening and observing the other person.

For each of these, I need defences: traits of the victim which might help them resist these tactics. For example:

1. Level-headedness.
2. Suspicion.
3. Earthiness.
4. Inner contentment.
5. Self-esteem.
6. Haughtiness.
7. Self-control.
8. Inscrutability.

Could you give me lots of ideas, please? Good ones, though. Thanks.

Graham

    \n Variant defences

    1. Obtuse (I'm too stupid to understand enough to be confused)
    2. Spoiled/Privelege (I'm so used to getting my way that the fact you're giving me my way means nothing)
    3. Philistine (tone deaf, unartistic etc.)
    4. Exploitive (being open with me doesn't seduce me, it just makes me think of ways I think I can use you)

    I'm reminded of the Dying Earth RPG's forms of argument/defence.

    \n I take it from the use of the word defense, that this is an opposed process? If it sort-of isn't, so that defenses could be both forms of flirtation and means of avoiding seduction...

    delaying (hesitant)
    redirection (shy)
    leaving (cowardly)
    self-deprecation (low confidence)
    coming on too strong (awkward)
    disqualifier comment (aloof)

    Just thinking that way because I always find seduction doesn't quite fit with an opposed tests, and so I come up with alternative mechanics to deal with it.
