\n Hi all! I'm about to start running a 3:16 game, and I've noticed that the rulebook seems to have some implied assumptions about the setting that my setting violates. I thought that I'd ask whether there's anything in particular that should be taken into account when changing those assumptions.

From the rules, one gets the impression that the game is supposed to be run as what's essentially a set of unconnected missions, with the human troopers being the only thing in common between them. For example, "play the game by going through the list of aliens one by one" means that each planet will have a different kind of alien - there's no mention of recurring enemies or any implication of there being any vast alien empire to combat. Just a set of separate alien species to be squashed one at a time. Also, the only mention of possible human enemies comes in the form of fallen marines: basically it seems like there's just one human empire that's basically internally united.

Another assumption that I notice is that each planet is assumed to have basically just one kind of alien.

I was thinking of tweaking those assumptions a bit. The setting I came up with basically has a mixture of one-planet aliens, beast-like aliens that move from world to world (think the Alien xenomorphs), as well as larger alien empires, some of which are manipulating the lesser races behind the scenes. So an unlucky squad of troopers might be taking it easy on an AA 4 world and enjoying a well-deserved straightforward xenocide of a defenseless alien species that never did anything wrong, only to find out that the whole species was just a diversion for the AA 10 mastermind aliens who suddenly pop up with (number of players) threat tokens of their own and do not appreciate having been found out.

Another tweak would be that in addition to the alien species, there are a number of traitor colonies which Terra is struggling to keep in check, some of which may be secretly allied with the aliens. As the game begins, none of the player characters have yet encountered a member of an alien species before - all of their pre-campaign kills are from missions fought against the traitor colonies, before they were transferred to the Expeditionary Fleet. The fleet's responsibility to eradicate alien life may occasionally bring it back to contact with human traitors that are aiding the aliens.

I'm wondering if there's anything in particular to keep in mind when changing these assumptions. The one thing that immediately comes to mind is that the "going bug-hunting from planet to planet" setup from the original rules seems to be designed to make it more obvious that the whole war is pointless, and that the humans are just genocidal oppressors bent on destroying all life even if that life posed no real threat to them. That assumption seems to be undermined in a setting where there actually are malevolent enemies that genuinely threaten humanity. On the other hand, this could be easily worked around by making it so that the humans are the original aggressors, and that most of the other races would have been peaceful if they hadn't been attacked first.

Is there anything else I should take into account?

    \n This is all very interesting. You're definitely correct about the point of the game's default assumptions, which makes me feel less nervous about your decision to change them. My concern, however, is that without that emergent thematic morality play thing, the game may not have enough going for it to maintain player interest. It is, after all, quite simple. You won't break the game by springing different tokens on them part-way through a mission, though. I'm working on a Clone Wars hack that is going to use this idea, with different-colored tokens for different types of droids.

    \n I notice that I'm also starting to tweak this game in other ways. About to throw in a list of "history" similar to Apocalypse World and other Move Engine games to help build some of that tension: basically I was thinking that I'd have the players taking turns picking an entry from the list below and choosing some other character as the object of the entry, until everyone's happy or they've used up all the entries.

    Everyone introduces their characters by name, appearance, and reputation. Take your turn, then go around again. On your turn, pick one from the list below. Then go around and pick one again, and keep going until there are no more unpicked entries or everyone agrees to stop here.

    - One of them served in the same unit as you before both of you got transferred here.
    - One of them said or did something that makes you think they're reckless and possibly going to endanger the whole unit.
    - You think that one of them is cute (in terms of looks, personality, or both).
    - One of them covered your ass when you screwed up and were about to get into a lot of trouble
    - One of them reminds you about your little brother - either in a good way or bad
    - One of them strikes you as just the kind of person who'd sympathize with the Rebel Colonies, and you're suspicious of them because of that
    - One of them clearly seems like the smartest guy of the bunch
    - One of them told a story that offended you, though you didn't say anything out loud
    - One of them makes you want to impress them
    - One of them makes you want to put them in their proper place
    - One of them confided their fears to you
    - One of them just barely beat your score in a training session, and was awarded extra free time that you really would have wanted
    - One of them you'd totally love to fuck
    - You got into a heated argument with one of them, which was interrupted and never resolved

    (Will probably come up with a few more before the first session.)
