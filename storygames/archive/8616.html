    \n
    \n

    The Camp Nerdly kid policy is pretty simple. Once the new wiki is up, it'll be covered there. However, I think I can give you all the big picture here:

    \n
    \n

    Kids are awesome and are welcome at Camp Nerdly. However...

    \n
    \n

    >

    \n
    \n

    If you bring someone to camp who needs supervision, then you are responsible for supervising them or arranging for another camper to supervise them. All campers under sixteen years old need a supervisor within eyesight at all times. No exceptions.

    \n
    \n

    >

    \n
    \n

    There's a cabin on the hill we like to call "The Castle". The Castle is an adults-only zone. No one under sixteen will be permitted to hang out in the Castle. Parents and guardians do not have permission to allow their kids to hang out in the Castle. This rule is for the folks who are uncomfortable playing adult-themed games around kids, not to protect the kids from adult-themed games.

    \n
    \n

    >

    \n
    \n

    We're not comfortable with anyone under sixteen playing with the handmade boffer weapons. NERF makes a great line of boffer weapons for kids/adults who act like kids. They're lighter weight and less likely to cause facial rug-burns or bend the frame of someone's glasses.

    \n
    \n

    >

    \n
    \n

    No one under sixteen will play with fire or around the fireplaces. Seriously.

    \n
    \n

    >

    \n
    \n

    Any camper big enough to push a broom will have a chore to do at Camp Nerdly. If you're thinking about bringing someone who's adverse to any activity labled as a 'chore', then you should really let them know just what will be expected of them.

    \n
    \n

    That being said, I do want to remind folks that Camp Nerdly is all about everyone chipping in to make it happen. If you want to bring a little one who's going to need supervision, but you want a little adult time, then I'm totally willing to help out and spend some of my Nerdly time as a Jr Owlbear supervisor. Just let me know who you're bringing, how old they are, and what kind of activities they enjoy. I'll make sure to bring something for them to do. I'm up for anything from coloring books to macaroni art to simplified Mechaton rules.

      \n Paul:
      1. Dulles or BWI should serve you well. Dulles flies you right into DC, and it's easy to take a train in from BWI.
      2. There should be a Baltimore/DC contingent that can help you out.If worse comes to worse, peeps driving up from North Carolina can pick you up/drop you off in DC. Alternately, there will be a car or two coming up from NC. We can, and have, swung transportation out of RDU for Nerdly.
      3. The cabins have cots, but yeah, bring a sleeping bag and pillow.
      4. Insects have not been a problem in the past. Bring a sleeping bag, pillow, warm clothes, rain gear, something to eat with/off of, a flashlight, a hat, maybe some sun screen, and any toiletries you might need (toothbrush, soap, shampoo, etc). I always pack an extra day or two's worth of clothes. The organizers will probably put up a recommended gear list at some point.

      \n
      \n Paul, the cabins have bare cots, so you'll definitely want bedding and/or a sleeping bag. It's probably going to be a lot colder at night than you expect, so err on the side of caution. A flashlight is absolutely required if you want to go anywhere after sunset -- the bigger, the better. If you can find one or two of those camp lights that are about three pounds worth of battery and a few ounces of flashlight, that'll probably be the best dollar to light output. Any kind of bug repellent is good, but you want something meant for forest use, rather than the stuff that's good for trips to the park. Repelling ticks is the primary concern. I can show you how to put it on for maximum effectiveness when you get there. A snake-bite kit can't hurt, either, since there was a diamondback there two nerdlys ago.

      Not sure about airports, but let me know what flight time you end up with, and I'll see if I can give you a ride one way or the other.

      \n Yeah, I wasn't trying to scare anyone, just reporting what I heard. If you're in the woods, snakes and spiders and other creepy-crawlies are going to happen, so I didn't consider it to be a huge deal, just something to point out.

      As to the species, I didn't see it, so I can't confirm what it was. And while NC is pretty much the common geographical limit, animals are very bad at respecting state borders -- I've heard from people who found diamondbacks as far north as Delaware. The Audubon guides say that they can range as far as Maine, in extreme cases. But yeah, they're not common in VA...it's much more likely it was another snake, which is better.
