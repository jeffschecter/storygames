    One thought I had was the opposite -- have a blog engine slightly tweaked to also submit to LJ (including any updates/changes), though it might just be simpler to have said blog engine just create an LJ post with the title of the entry & a link to the blog.
    \nWordPress does this. If you've got a WordPress blog, you can tweak it so that everything you post is crossposted to Live Journal.

    So then you create a WordPress design blog which crossposts to your LJ. It's kind of the best of both worlds: you've got a dedicated design journal that you can show to people who don't know you; and you've got your existing network, who see all the stuff about the new game.

    \nHow to subscribe to an RSS feed with only certain LJ tags

    It's described in LiveJournal's FAQ #149.

    Jason Mcintosh (lj user prog) describes how to do this:\n
    You can syndicate LJ posts with a specific tag attached by adding "tag=tagname" to the RSS feed URL's query part.

    I just set The Gameshelf's blip-blog (cough) feed URL to http://prog.livejournal.com/rss?tag=the+gameshelf, and it works great; you can read all my gameshelf-tagged posts (and only those posts) on the show's blip page. Cool. This saves me from spinning off an entirely new LJ just for the show.
