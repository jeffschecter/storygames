\n I wasn't sure where to post that...

Grimtooth (I) was published in french in 1984. Since them, there was very few things about traps here.
I can't understand why (thought, i'm begining to have a theory). What would have been Raiders of the lost Ark without traps ?

Anyway, we decided to publish a new version, taking the best of the serie, with new traps, new illustrations, and a new way of using them (it will include "stats" for T&T, Donjon, DW)

You an find a preview here: http://www.narrativiste.eu/2013/grimtooth-3-preview/

At this point, i have to say that i'm not trying to sell you anything, because you can find that stuff in your native language.
I'm just here to ask for people to tell us about their experiences with traps. It can be grimtooth traps or not. Best if it take place in the old good times, but it don't have to.

We will include the "best" stories in this Grimtooth 3.0. And we will include (almost) all stories in a free pdf.
Thanks for reading !

(french article about that: http://www.narrativiste.eu/2013/a-table/)

    \n My favourite trap which I used in many different games, often with the same players, often in France in the early 80s, was a big slab of a door. There are no apparent doorhandles or any kind of mechanism. In fact the door is balanced so that if you push it on the bottom half, it will fall outwards, and on to whoever is standing within 10' of the entrance. Splat.

    I can't believe the number of times someone got squished this way.

    \n A buddy of mine and I used to make traps for each other in school all the time, but with a MacGuyver influence more than D&D. Mostly Rube Goldberg-esque contraptions that one had to figure out how and in what order to disarm, rather than "do this and die" traps like many in D&D. We preferred "hard skill" (figure out the puzzle) over "soft skill" (roll against a stat) trap resolution.

    \n I ran an adventure for AD&D 2e. The characters ranged between 6th and 12th level. They experienced a two-part trap.

    Part 1: They come to a dead-end corridor that really ought not to be. They detect magic and determine that the end of the wall is magical stone of some kind, but it can be dispelled. The paladin, I think, steps forward and dispels it and it disappears, revealing a chamber full of rot grubs, which pour down upon the party by the truckfull.

    Some save against getting covered in rot grubs. The others are getting their flesh eaten. They manage to use magic and whatnot to toast the pile of nasties and cure themselves.

    Part 2: Further along, they come to another magical dead-end wall. "Aha! Another trap!" they say, and they are right. This time, the party stands well back from the wall and the magic-user, I think, dispels it. This wall was all that was between them and an underground lake, which is now filling up the dungeon corridors. The safest escape is up, towards the 100-foot-distant surface of the lake. The water is inhabited by some nasty swimming monsters, of course, and much of the party is in heavy armor.

    Someone had a magic item to breathe water. The magic-user had some kind of spell for the same. There was some quick scrambling on the part of the paladin to get out of armor and get to the surface. They managed to survive...
