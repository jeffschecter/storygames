    Posted By: Ben LehmanThere's a little bit (a few online forums) but nothing that actually produces game text.
    \nThat's not strictly true. There is a fair bit of game text produced, but it is mostly freely distributed online materials for D&D, since the core books are available in Chinese, even with 4E. Not much in the way of original or "indie" TRPGs that I know of, but I'm a bit out of date.

    Poking around a bit on the web, I found these great pictures of a D&D meetup in a Beijing coffee house, complete with shots of Chinese language 4E materials. On another site, I see, aside from D&D, the bulletin board lists "40K, Fallout (like the video game), Sword World, and COC" under the heading "other rules systems." Is there a Fallout game available in Chinese or just a common house setting, given the video game's popularity? Hmm... must investigate.

    Okay, apparently it's a Chinese fan translation of an English fan-created game (zipped PDF) by "necroman," which some Chinese fans encountered on a file-sharing site, translated, and posted all over the Chinese TRPG internet scene. Not sure how many people are playing it, but how's that for indie RPGs in China?

    I've said for a while that there are huge opportunities for popularizing indie games in China if we can somehow get indie games texts into the hands of people who would be interested in translating and releasing them for free. Honestly, I can imagine there being a huge interest in that on the Chinese side too, if we could just make the necessary connections.
