\n Our workplace is having a Christmas party and wants some ideas for games to play. Do you have any ideas of good group games for very casual not-at-all-hardcore-gamers?

Here are my specs:

- Games are that are scalable from 5 to up to 25-30 players would be great
- A game duration of between 5 to 30 minutes
- We have three big (30 person meeting room) indoors spaces \u2013 so we can have multiple games (or a few separate games)

Ideally, simple rules and well-playtested games would be great.

Some things that spring to mind include:

- Werewolf (but I\u2019d love a link to some good rules for it)
- Sage Fight

\u2026 and I\u2019m sure I read a blog post about a games conference (in London?) a few years back which played a variety of games, including a \u2018steal this item and sneak it past some guards\u2019 game, if that sounds familiar.

Any help would be appreciated!

    \n If you want the feel of Mafia with a smaller group in a shorter amount of time without any players being eliminated early, I recommend The Resistance. I've played that with groups of strangers up to ten and it is always a blast. If you ant a simple abstract board game that scales up to eight, get Tsuro. I would recommend SageFight, but it's sometimes hard to get people to come out of their shells.

    \n I've heard of a zombie game called Shelter in Place that can accomodate large numbers but don't know what sort of playing time that requires.
    \n
    \nThere's a game by white Wolf called Long Live the King which seems to be particularly party orientated but, again, I've not played it (and it'll possibly take a while to get through).
    \n
    \nMiller's Hollow is a good game (or the Ultimate Werewolf version, with loads of characters) - the Miller's Hollow version also has a variant called The Village which comes with nice cardboard buildings to represent your village and roles in the village.
    \n
    \nOf course, they're no good if you need to purchase something in a hurry, and are unable to get your hands on these. Werewolf/Mafia is easy enough to fudge without anything other than playing cards. Alternatively there's a lot of stuff online that can be freely downloaded if you have people happy to get into character, stretching from theatric LARP style stuff to sitting around a table in character. I remember stumbling across a Shakespearian murder mystery once, where players were characters from Shakespeare's plays, trying to discover who has murdered their creator. Also, from the top of my head, their Executive Decision, where players represent the President and his advisors, and tackle various problems (that's a free download on Greg Stolze's site).
    \n
    \nSorry, that's a big rambling post from someone who's just stumbled across this site fter being absent from it for years!

    \n On the Board game front -

    Wits and Wagers - Not an RPG, but a very fun party trivia game where you don't have to know the answers to win. Playing with teams it easily scales up to 15 or so.

    Bang also scales up to 7 reasonably well and who doesn't like to shoot their co-workers.

    Incan Gold is a great push your luck game for up to 8.


    FunAgain has a page of specifically party games you might want to check out
