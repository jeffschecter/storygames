    I'm surprised it took five replies to get to this.

    I am so totally confused and ambiguous about Wave now, it's not funny. Part of me likes keeping email and chat (and forums, and news feeds and social networks) separate. Another part of me thinks it would be cool to be able to kill actual socnet participation, with email forwarding from the socnet to Wave. Another part of me thinks it shouldn't be a client but should be in The Cloud (duh). Another part of me sees the collaborative power of Wave (plus Google Docs, maybe). And still another part thinks, "yeah, that's cool, if EVERYONE adopts it... otherwise, it's just another sandbox."

    So, yeah, I just need to try it out and see if it's worth the effort. ("Effort," you ask?!? Well, how easily will it sync between three or four clients? 'Cause I have three or four PCs I use routinely. Which is why I DON'T use a client at all, for email or chat--sick of having my stuff scattered over multiple machines, always having to keep resyncing (or deleting, or remembering to set a client NOT to download from the server).)

    I'll try. I expect to like it somewhat, for very specific uses (e.g. occasionally asynchronous RPG by mail/chat), but I doubt I'll be consolidating my email accounts.

    My initial thoughts -

    As a tabletop replacement wave has some really nice features. Widgets are neat, and mean that we can have a toolbox of pieces to assemble to hopefully make playing new games even easier.       \n So, the thing with asking for invitations?

      You quickly realise that the people you'll invite are the people you'll collaborate with. When you fire up Wave for the first time, you rapidly understand that it's only useful if you know people who are on it. The best thing you can do, then, is invite people you'll work with.

      Similarly, from your point of view, there's little point being invited unless you have people to collaborate with, which means it's good if you're invited by someone you will work with.

      So rather than asking for invitations, on a might-as-well-throw-my-hat-in-the-ring basis, I would suggest you contact people you've already collaborated with or are willing to collaborate with.
