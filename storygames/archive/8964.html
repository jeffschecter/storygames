\nMy girlfriend's kids, actually.

Anyway, they are two girls (13 and 11) and a boy (9, neurologically 7(he had some birth problems, but he's fine)) and we sometimes play RPGs when I "babysit" them.

We've played some DnD, d20 Modern, nWoD (I scared the shit out of them >.<) and even some <a href="http://www.story-games.com/forums/comments.php?DiscussionID=8706&page=1#Item_75">GHOST/ECHO.

The thing is:

The two girls only care about getting a boyfriend in the game-world (And fight over the fictional guys fiercely), and making some investigative and narrative play. and the boy is only interested in combat and... well... some more combat, and he has a hard time understanding the fact his sisters are not the enemy in-game.

I understand they are not compatible players, but I really need to make the game work for them all. Any advice?

So far I've been playing the game as two separate-but-interlaced session. One for the little guy and one for the girls. but it can only do so much...

    \n Well, if you haven't found it yet, there's this yahoo group:
    http://games.groups.yahoo.com/group/kids-rpg/

    Mike Holmes is a member and was active for a long time and has some pretty interesting things to say about gaming with your kids.

    As my daughter has gotten older (12), I've eased back a lot on participation.

    I don't have any great advice, but you could try switching the spotlight a great deal. Maybe introduce a method for allowing the players to request the style of the next scene? Make it really clear that everyone gets a turn to engage with something they enjoy?

    They all seem to be at a good age for doing stuff normally considered "out of game" too, like blue-booking or semi-larpish stuff, too. Have you read HG Wells' Floor Games? It's a sort've AP report on proto-RPGing, you might check out. I know Project Gutenberg has some text-only versions up.
