\n I've been looking at Microlite20 a bit. I find it appealing as a very simple rules-lite way to get access to the mountain of d20-compatible scenarios out there, without having to buy and read and use stacks of 3.5e rulebooks. So I've printed out the "Microlite20 Pureset Essence", all 16 pages, which is really nice looking.

The only problem is how darn terse it is. It's like a crib-sheet more than a rulebook. "Simple" and "short" aren't necessarily the same, and in this case I had to pore over it to work out all the mechanical details, like stat/race/skill interactions, that weren't clearly spelled out. (Maybe the problem is that I've only ever played super old school 1st ed. AD&D, not 3e/2d0.) And I know that the booklet would be pure gibberish to anyone who hasn't played D&D at all, such as my kids, whom I want to game with.

So...

Have any of you had good/bad experiences using Microlite20?
Do you know of any alternative descriptions of the rules that go into a little more detail?
Are there any other contenders for rules-lite-d20-compatible gaming?
