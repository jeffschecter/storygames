\n Ah, another light-passtime post. These are fun.

Anyway, if you know me at all, you know that I've talked about Culling My RPG Collection: I have "two shelves" (now that I keep my RPGs in these Ikea cube-things, the equivalent space is 4-5 "cubes"). Even though I've culled off games one year, only to buy them again in a future year when inspired to actually use them (nWoD Vampire; L5R 1st Edition; Aberrant; and I've sold Continuum twice, bought it three times), there are some games that stay on my inglorious collector's shelf, the one cube I have hollowed out and turned into my "trophy case", the RPGs that I probably will not touch again (or at least, not for a long while) but keep around because I simply can't let them go.

Here are my games. What are yours? And any interesting stories about any of them?
(not including my J-RPGs, which are a story all their own)

The ones I plan on playing again someday, one day, long from now:

Blue Planet 2e and all supplements: Love the world. Now that I know more about setting up situation beforehand, I want to give this another go sometime.
The End (d20 Lost Souls edition; although I have the original on my shelf, too): The best post-apoc game out there. Even the d20 version does things that d20 wasn't meant (at the time) to do, and well at that. Fantastic read, too.
Over The Edge: A classic. Honestly, I'm waffling on running this again, but I figure it would be good for a one-shot at some point.
Conspiracy of Shadows, 1st Edition: I definitely consider this the stronger release of the two editions. A fantastic game, I want to run a campaign of it again at some point. (or maybe the new edition, if it looks like it returns to the roots)
Rifts (Wormwood, Juicer Uprising, Africa): Hell no, not on its own. But using another system, I want to do either Wormwood or the Juicer Uprising. Africa is a pile of shit, but I'd like to re-imagine it at some point into a COOL Rifts-like setting.
The Whispering Vault: Like OTE, I'm waffling on whether I'll actually run/play it again: DreadTFBOP really does well everything I loved about Whispering Vault, and better. But whatta game.
The Riddle of Steel: Will run again sometime. If I'm feeling especially courageous, I'll actually use the magic rules from that game.
GURPS 4th Edition: Will give it another whirl at some point, using Lite options only.
Feng Shui: Love the game, system is dated as balls. Need to rework the core die mechanic, but I want to play again someday.

The ones I have no intention of ever playing again, but I can't seem to let go (mostly as reading resources):

GURPS Voodoo: I gave up/sold all of my other GURPS books, even Reign of Steel, but I can't seem to let go of this one. GURPS tried to do its own World of Darkness. A neat niche product.
Planescape (all supplements, lots signed by Tony DiTerlizzi):
Skull and Bones: TS Luikart's writing is phenomenal in here, and I love the pirate stories. Otherwise, it's just d20 pirates. Hum.
Continuum: Probably the best read in an RPG I've ever had. Phenomenal world, great read, super premise, shitty system. WiIl never try to play it again, but it's a great read. Same with Narcissist (which has an even *worse* system, but still great writing and concepts).
Artesia: It is Pretty.
BESM d20: It, too, is pretty. Plus it's the full-color "collector's item" version. Great art, no resale value, might just keep on to it as a piece-in-time reminder of like how awesomely things went wrong at GoO.
Sengoku: Great resource for Japanese-style roleplaying. As a game, it kinda falls down though, suffering from "But what do I *do* with it?". Still, when I set a game in historic Japan, I use this book a lot.
Dungeons and Dragons: Kingdom of Nithia: Awesome take on ancient Egypt. Love the art inside as well, especially for the Egyptian-style warrior subclasses.
Dread: The First Book of Pandemonium, 1st Edition: You want my copy of Dread? Fuck you. You can't have it.
Advanced Dungeons and Dragons, 2nd Edition: While I had and played Star Frontiers and Paranoia before I got AD&D 2E, this was the game that made me a Gamer For Life, back sophomore year of high school. Spent more time in that book than any other, I'd wager, even though even today I still can't find a good explanation on how to make an attack roll. :-) Still, great concept art, and it basically drove a decade of awesome adventures with friends.

If time worked differently, I'd also have a copy of the TORG boxed set on that "never playing again, but keeping" shelf, with the Cyberpapacy, Nile Empire and Orrorsh sourcebooks. Oh, and the original Dark Sun boxed set, with Will and the Way, Veiled Alliance, and Earth,Air,Fire and Water.

    \nGames I still want to play some day
    There are three games that fall so, so solidly into this category:
    * Traveller, Classic only please (although maybe Mongoose's mechanics for some parts, especially skill resolution).
    * Pendragon, final edition, with the Great Campaign book; I have lots of other books as well to use as additional material; my strong notion here is to run a Pendragon campaign as if it were done by Miyazaki (viz Nausicaa, Mononoke-Hime)... the SCAdian chilvary stuff holds no interest for me whatsoever, but a strongly anime-ish Boy-Who-Would-Be-King vibe with lots of mysterious nature spirits wandering around, oh, yes please.
    * Tribe 8: I got a complete set of books on a deal one year, and I'm rather taken with bits of the background; the mechanics I don't mind at all, but I rather thought they suited Jupiter Chronicles best. If I ever get around to running with this, I might just use HeroQuest (the new to-be-published "Quest Worlds" edition of the game, probably).
    * RuneQuest, 2nd edition. I have all the Moon Design reprint books, and there's a wonderful campaign sitting there waiting to be played. The problem is which rules set to use? The answer might end up being the new BRP rules, but there are bits I'd like to keep from RQv2, and bits from RQv3. I'm not sure I really want to use HeroQuest for it. This last bullet might also fall into the "keeping but probably will never get to" category.

    Games I'm keeping but probably will never get played
    * Some other BRP stuff (RingWorld, Superworld, Hawkmoon, for example).
    * Anything I own by FGU, most probably (Daredevils, Bushido, Aftermath, C&S, Space Opera, Flashing Blades, etc).
    * Burning Empires; I'd really like to play in a BE game, but I'm not sure I know anyone in my gaming circle who would like to run it (and who'd be really interested in running it); the same almost certainly goes for Burning Wheel (I tried running it for a year; we all seemed to have fun, but in the end, the game remained a mystery to us, pretty much).
    * Dying Earth
    * Lyonesse (in French, by a Swiss company, that produced the rulebook and a screen, and promptly vanished); this is one of my favourite fantasy settings, far more than the DE setting, and the rulebook as a setting guide is fabulous: art, maps, acres and acres of description. In French. Getting through it is possible for me, but a bit of a slog.
    * All my Tekumel stuff: have most of the TOME stuff, some of the books published by the guys who did Different Worlds, and have the hardback rulebook by Guardians of Order. They're lovely to pore over, but I'm not sure I'd ever fell comfortable running a Tekumel game. There was a fellow who serialized his campaign in Alarums & Excursions for months, and his submissions kept me participating in A&E for a year longer than I might have.
    * All my D&D Cyclopedia/Gazeteer era stuff. If D&D4E hadn't come along, then it's entirely possible I would have put this on the table at some point; but the 4e stuff is very fun, for now. It's possible that I might some day use the Mystara background as the setting for a 4E campaign (Hollow World and all), but I rather doubt it.

    I'm really not clearing out any of my RPG stuff (other than bits and bobs: gave away my Jorune set to a friend last night, for example) until the Boardgamegeek fellows launch rpggeek... I'm hoping that once that gets off the ground, my collection will get enumerated there and slowly dispensed with. But in the meantime, the collection does need to be collated and re-packed for more sensible storage.

    \n My shelf space is limited (no fancy-shmancy Ikea cubes for me...yet), so for the most part the books that are on them are ones that have been used and will most likely be used again. I don't even consider buying a book unless I'm nearly positive that I'm going to use it these days (blame both the economy and getting older), so I don't have anything on the shelf right now that I haven't played or used yet. And generally once I've decided I'm not going to use a book again, I just trade it in and use the credit for something else, so most of my "will never use again" books are already long gone.

    Still, I know I have some hangers-on packed away in a closet, so I'll bust out a flashlight and a pith helmet and go exploring:

    Okay, a bunch of AD&D2e books, just as expected. These are definitely in the "will never play again" category, and if I ever absolutely have to throw out books to make room for other things, they'll be the first ones on the heap. Can't sell 'em, after all, even if they weren't all banged up from high school. Although I still sort of like the Complete Thief's Handbook, so maybe that one would be spared. There were a lot of neat ideas in it.

    Looking deeper...oh, hey, Vampire: The Dark Ages. Huh. I remember that one, sort of. We did a game of it long ago that turned out okay.

    And what's this under it? Jesus, I can't believe I actually have a copy of Werewolf 1st edition. Was that the one that was thrown in as a freebie when I bought a bunch of other used books? I don't think I've even opened it. And given that it's the first edition Storyteller system, I can't imagine ever wanting to play it. But it was free, it's likely unsellable, and...there's just something wrong with the idea of throwing away a book. So it can stay for now, I guess.

    Ooh, Planescape stuff! Okay, I know I'm not going to play with these again, but...um...I think I'll put them up on the shelf for a little while anyway. So pretty. So much fun. I can move some of those GURPS supplements I'm not going to be using into storage to make room for them.

    Some mined-out Star Wars Adventure Journals (d6) back here, too; I'm pretty sure I stole all the best parts from them already, so unless our gaming group's roster changes completely, I'm not likely to be able to use them again. I should probably put them in the "To Sell" bag out in the kitchen. May their next owner use them in good health and forgive me for all the notes I scrawled in the margins.

    And last but not least, both Amber DRP and the Shadow Knight supplement. I wondered where those had gotten to! I doubt I'd ever use the system again, but they'd probably be useful references if I ever got a wild hair to run an Amber game in a different system.
