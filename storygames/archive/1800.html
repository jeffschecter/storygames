  • Andrew Morris
    November 2006
    Matthew, I've never had a problem getting into a game. Occasionally, they fill up, sure, but most of the time there's a free spot or two, or the GM is willing to add one more player than they expected.

    But if you're concerned, there are two things working in your favor here. First, if you went to a table full of designers, I'd be shocked if one or two didn't bow out so that a non-IGE-affiliated gamer could get in. Second, don't underestimate the power of pre-registration. Almost no one uses it, but you can email the convention folks a list of the games you want to get in (assuming you pre-registered, of course). Once you're approved for those sessions, the GM can't refuse you a seat. If you do this, you've got about a 99 percent chance of getting in, since the chance of it filling up with pre-registers is vanishingly small.

      I just wrote my blurbs. I'm going to post them here.


      Polaris: Chivalric Tragedy at Utmost North

      Once upon a time, as far north as north can go, there was the greatest civilization that this world will ever know. Though they will be destroyed just as the world destroys all beautiful things, we will look into the time that they are not yet gone, for though the sun blights their sky and the demons blight their earth, the Knights of the Order of the Stars still stand as a last defense for the remnants of their once great city. One by one, the knights will fall, until none are left and their people are destroyed.

      Polaris is a game for 2-4 players, focusing on the tragic downfall and death of the Knights of the Order of the Stars. The game uses a unique system of key phrases to structure play. Also note that while the organizer will provide support for the game, each player will be expected to play a protagonist, secondary characters, and antagonists in turn.

      Bliss Stage: Final Act

      Seven years ago, an alien attack known as the Bliss trapped the adults of the world in an endless slumber. Society's children have made do as best they could, warding off alien robots and trying to scrape out a living in a world gone mad, but most of the world has collapsed into chaos. Within a generation, humanity will be extinct.

      Enter a group of bold teenage rebels. Using technology stolen from the invaders, they have constructed dream robots called ANIMa out of their trust and friendship, and have used these tools to strike out against the invaders. With a series of daring raids and recon missions, they have tracked the alien invasion to a secret base on the dark side of the moon. Now, just as alien forces are amassed to destroy them, they will lead a daring last-ditch assault on the alien base.

      But in a war where love is a weapon, can humanity survive?

      Bliss Stage is a game for 3-5 players, drawing its themes from such giant robot anime as Gunbuster, Evangelion, and Bubblegum Crisis. The focus of the system is on the interpersonal relationships between the pilots and their friends as they struggle from the future of humanity against unknowable alien invaders.

      Because of sexual content and adult themes, this game is for adults only.

      Hero's Banner: The Season of War

      Martia is fallen, Askavar divided amongst 8 brothers, and the victorious heir of Valice has met his end on a traitor blade of his false lieutenant. The three kingdoms have been thrown into a chaos unknown since the coming of Artos. The crops lie rotting in the fields as peasant farmers take up arms for ambitious landlords, little better than brutes themselves. It is hard to say whether there are more dead on the field of battle or from the crucible of starvation and illness.

      Yet such times are times of opportunity for youth of quality. You are the child of a noble family, the blood of Artos runs in your veins, and in this season of war you shall take up the sword and make your way in the world. Pushed by the pressures of your ambition, your family, and your all-too-human heart, your path will not be easy. The land cries out for a hero to save her. Will it be you?

      Hero's Banner is a game for 2-4 players, with the players portraying young nobles in a dark age low-fantasy world. The game will make use of the unique multi-generational rules of Hero's Banner to span two generations of strife, love, and duty.

      1001 Nights, a game of enticing stories

      In the palace of the Sultan are many rooms. In these rooms are many people, and the people have many stories. Most intriguingly for our tale, these people cannot leave these rooms. The ornately carved walls surrounding the fragrant pools and exotic gardens are high; the lace-like doors are webs of iron; the delicately cool tones and publicly refined manners raise the significance of an arched brow by feverish degrees. This 'place apart' holds the Sultan's peace, for to disturb it would be foolish indeed. But without the ability to laugh or cry or shout, the prisoners of the court have found other means to pursue their heart's desires. In their stories are found their daily pains, frustrations, hopes, and desires, and through the stories these things might be realized or relieved.

      1001 Nights is a game for 2-5 players, focused on storytelling in the court of an oppressive Sultan. Players should be ready to freely improvise and think flexibly.
