    \n From the back cover:\n
    Imagine an infinite city ruled by creatures older than time.
    Imagine that you hanged up your shoes a long time ago, in another place in another time.
    And now you're alive.
    And you're Branded.
    And you must obey their orders.
    Or everything will truly end.
    Imagine you have the need to escape from all this, knowing that sooner or later rebellion will become your own doom.
    Do not imagine. Play.
    \nIn Necrorama, you play a Necrolite, a human taken away to the Iron City while about to die, transformed into a servant of the Patron Saints of the city by the process of Tanatocreation and study dark magic in the Necroversities. Iron City is an Art-Deco steam-punk nightmare that may or may not be the Purgatory. As a necrolite, you're a chosen servant of the Patron Saint, and you're job is to collect souls for the Patrons. But something went wrong with your Tanatocreation. You start remembering that you were human once, and now you want to recover the memories of your life. But the Patrons are wise, they created you with an Termination Date, you don't have much time.

    As the subtitle says, the game style is Dark Pulp. Campaigns are called "radio serials" and you create their premise in collaboration with the rest of the players. The main idea, mentioned above, is that you're rebel Necrolites searching for lost memories, but there are other options.

    Character creation is done by phases, previous life, Tanatocreation, Necroveristy, etc... Each Necrolite is made for a special purpose and taught certain "spells", based on the Major Arcana, to go about their jobs. Careers are things like "Quantic Necromancy" or "Dialectic Hermeticism". Jobs are things like Cabalist, Necroworker or Antiquarian.

    The system is reminiscent of PDQ. You have a number of States that define your character. Special States, Called Key States, let you replenish your "karma pool" when you use them. There are a number of subsystems to reinforce the idea of the game, like the spells, the search for memories, the rules for traveling around Iron City and your Termination Date, for instance.

    Hope that paints a clearer picture.
