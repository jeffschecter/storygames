    \n Used to happen to me many years ago, but I realized that way I wasn't getting anything done. I ended up trying to slow down, stop focusing my creativity on different things at once and commiting to come back to the same project, and never let it go until finished, tested and used. Also, whatever else shiny appeared out there or popped on my mind, I wrote it down and saved it for later. It certainly worked wonders, I'm finally getting things done.

    \n Yeah, I read that Jackie Chan gets tons of ideas. And what he does is write them down and comes back to them later, instead of getting distracted by those ideas. I've heard others supposedly do that, but I have not confirmation of that.
    I started doing something like that (His was post it notes and scraps of paper, mine is a Google Doc) and it has helped me keep the creative juices flowing, while allowing me to stay on target. I can think about new settings, mechanics, etc. But instead of dropping everything and writing a new game, or trying to do more than one game at a time, I can focus on my current project and keep the other ideas filed a way for the next bi thing.
    Dave M
