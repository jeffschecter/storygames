\n So, I mentioned in my last AP post that I'd made an Excel tool that is used for quickly creating and managing a bunch of NPCs.
\n
\nI've decided, largely as a means of exercising my rusty programming muscles, to try to do a better job with a java app.
\n
\nThe previous tool had ~500 different options for "Reputation", but most of them were pretty generic. Sufficient for my purposes, but I'd like some more interesting, evocative ideas. There were also ~65000 names, (I've got a list of 88000 or so, but Excel could only handle a maximum column length of ~65000 cells). This list is fairly comprehensive. Only one of my co-workers' names doesn't show up, though a close variant does.
\n
\nSo, while I'm planning this, I'd like to poll the S-G crowd for unique and evocative Reputations, or even names. In the case of names, try to go for real (last) names of people you may know, and I'll check it against the list to see if it's already there. Mostly, I want Reputations, though.

    Hey all. Been doing a little work since I wrote this. Some of you read me on facebook, so this will be a repeat performance. Nothing to see here you haven't seen there or vice versa (except any ensuing discussion, of course) I ended up going with another option than Java, because apparently there are weird sanctions on software exports to Iraq, even or especially via download, making it difficult for me to get the JDK.

    \n

    Anyhow, this is what I've got:

    \n

    http://photos-g.ak.fbcdn.net/hphotos-ak-snc1/hs022.snc1/2645_1055508905616_1162630028_30151998_2292818_n.jpg

    \n

    (the format buttons aren't working?)

    \n

    the above is a screenshot of the application. It's a bit older than the current version, but cosmetically it hasn't changed enough to warrant a new screenshot.

    \n

    And here's a status update:

    \n

    As of right now, the basic functionality is in. It will create troopers with randomized ranks, names, reputations and FA/NFA. It will roll for them all, including AA (currently a static 6) and determine pass/fail, and whether or not the trooper takes a kill from the aliens. If the trooper is crippled, it will take an unused armor hit before going to dead. It will heal all the troopers (except the dead ones, natch) with a button click.

    \n

    Rank, name, reputation and FA/NFA can be changed individually per trooper. Armor may be toggled off and on individually. Troopers may be deleted en masse, or individually.

    \n

    Current additional functionality includes the ability to differentiate the troopers by colored 'squads'. This has little application at this time, but eventually will allow for rolling just for a squad, healing a squad, or deleting a squad.

    \n

    Other functions to be added:
    \n-Sorting by rank, name and squad
    \n-Settings, allowing for changing AA, and setting maximum rank for random trooper generation
    \n-Saving/Loading of existing troopers.

    \n

    Anyone who would like to check out the program as it currently exists, I can make it available now. It will allow for you to suggest function tweaks and additions. Leave me a note if you're interested.

    \n

    Note: This program only works on Windows. I make no promises whether it will work on WinE or any similar Windows emulators.
