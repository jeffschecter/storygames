    \n I play and run tabletop RPGs often in play-by-post. I concentrate on running games with conflict resolution rules vs. task resolution rules and prefer ones where the player can engage a die roll and make choices from their result. Apocalypse Engine games are perfect for this.

    I've been blessed with a group of players who are actively involved in daily posts (a paragraph or so, normally), and we push through scenes about every week, and a "session" (usually 7 to 10 scenes per PC) a month. I've run satisfying miniseries (little campaigns) in 3-6 (edited to add) months with this mode of play.

    Apocalypse World and Monsterhearts are my two faves for PBP right now, but I also enjoy Don't Rest Your Head and I'm trying out Impulse Drive at the moment.

    \n +1 to Chuubo's, it's the first game coming to my mind.

    On a second thought, i really think that any game without too many discussion between GM-Player for a roll is a good one. The idea is to be able to know the result of a roll and keep the fiction flowing in the next post. You describe and narrate, make the roll, the GM gets to interpret that roll in the fiction and respond accordingly in fiction, then you keep playing as normal.

    In play-by-post, you dont know when the other players or the GM is going to respond, so you have to compress everything in one single post. OOC, narration and dice rolling. So, if you are able to compress the mechanics in order to make it fast, it would be really nice.

    \n I've been wrestling with this one myself, and agree with Edgargso - players need to be able to go with the flow, and scenes need to have a minimum of negotiation. So far, so good (what's helping with Storium is when I realized that outcomes had to be either "yes, and" or "yes, but" - everything else fell into place after that).
