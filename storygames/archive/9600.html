\n So at Nerd NYC's Recess June 09 event, I sat down and talked with Luke Crane and Jared Sorensen about the layout and writing on Misspent Youth. In particular, they were kind enough to tell me I was a fucking idiot if I went to final game with terms like "midpoint," "denouement," "world building," "themes," etc., ad nauseum, in my game (especially next to flavorful shit like "Vice," "Victim," "Absolutism," etc., ad delighteum).

So, I'm on board for the most part. "Midpoint" becomes "We're fucked!" and the like. I recommend anyone doing a game own their terms like this. It's really good advice and it makes your game much more yours. Anyway:

I'm currently a bit stumped. One of the words called out for execution was "scene." I have no fucking clue what to replace it with. I don't want to be obviously coming up with something different just so it's different. I want something that's going to be thematic. (If you don't know what's thematic for my game, check out the "Misspent Youth" link above, that should make it clear.) Can't use "part." It's not really thematic and I'm already going to use it to replace the idea of acts.

Movements?
Riffs?
Moments?

I fucking hate all of these.

Any suggestions?

    \n While I am fully onboard with scrapping the truly Story 101 terms like denouement, I agree that 'scene' is a fine word to keep -- it's neutral and descriptive, and unlike 'denouement' it's not going to stand out and aggravate anyone. I think it's worth distinguishing between 'new terms the players will need to learn anyhow to play the game' (those can and should be coloured however you like, since there's no cost, it's a new word/context either way) and 'terms the players probably already know that I will use to explain how to play the game' (renaming these has a cost, since it adds to the things the player must learn/relearn). Scene is definitely in the latter category, unless you feel that you are using scenes in such a way that they constitute a new term due to their new context/use (in which case you should absolutely rename it.)

    That doesn't mean it mightn't be worth changing, though. Of the suggestions so far I like 'Incident' the best -- 'throw' makes no sense to me, it sounds like it is confusing the mechanics with the setting (there's no dice in the game world, particularly, and all the other term-transformations sound very in-game.) I'm not really a fan of the current scene names, but they might just need more polish ("intensifies" doesn't sound right there, for example it's still abstract/story101ish -- how about 'gets crazy' or 'gets thicker'?)

    Back to scene-renaming, how about "clash"? I'm pretty sure that's what punks and authority figures do, and there's a bonus punk rock reference for kicks. The only problem is that (based on vague memories of having playtested the game all of once) I don't think every scene is a clash with The Authority? It would feel weird proposing that the next "clash" should be a scene (oh, whoops) where all of the kids hang out in their clubhouse and talk shit, or whatever.

    \n This is the opposite of the advice you're getting, but I don't necessarily see the benefit in straining so hard to occlude what you mean. You have terms that actually can be replaced with colorful language because there is no prior usage, but "scene"? That's a bog-normal technical term in this sort of game, used outside rules in in actual practice. Dozens of games use it, and gamers use it all the time when discussing games that never mention it in the text. Are you going to replace "dice" too, considering how it's not punk enough? I don't necessarily understand the need to get cute about stuff that is not actually different - if you're using scenes the exact same way other games use them, then that's the best word you could use.

    (That last sentence is important - if you're not actually using the theoretical and practical game element called "scene", then you don't need the word for it, either. There are games that do not have scenes as explicit elements.)

    "World creation" is a good example of what I mean because it straddles the line: if you're just looking for a snappy title, then sure, "fucking up the future" works fine. But if it's a technical term the players are supposed to read and reference again and again, it'll get old right quick, and slow down teaching the game to others who won't be reading the book at all, but rather listening to somebody explain it to them. If a person needs to know what the term means, then it's much easier to remember if it's not intentionally occluded.

    Might be that the game is so atmospheric that it's worthwhile to edit out all references to the players and the real world activity they are doing; some games get lots of mileage out of never compromising on their external elements; they become a sort of art piece as texts, making the act of play itself an aesthetic thing. Others just get so pretentious that it becomes a barrier to entry when you need to jump through the gimmick hoops to do the same shit you get to do casually in the next game. Maybe your game belongs in the first category, I don't know.

    (I'm the guy who decided that "Bringing Down the Pain" wasn't good enough a name for the long conflict procedure in The Shadow of Yesterday - changed it to "extended conflict". Others might disagree.)
