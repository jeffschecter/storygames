    \n I am thinking of the "science" version of game theory, which doesn't necessarily is connected to card/board games, but, with Wikipedia's words: "Game theory is a branch of applied mathematics that is used in the social sciences (most notably economics), biology, engineering, political science, international relations, computer science, and philosophy. Game theory attempts to mathematically capture behavior in strategic situations, in which an individual's success in making choices depends on the choices of others." And that's what this lecture is about as well.

    I guess it's easier to apply scientific game theory to card/board games I suppose, but not as straightforward to a story game (say I, without actually knowing it). Maybe there are even story games out there utilising or riffing on this type of game theory?
