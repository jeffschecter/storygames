    Man, you know what would have been cool? If I hadn't been punted off of the forum and lost my response to your question. Let's try that again:

    essence - n The intrinsic or indispensable properties that serve to characterize or identify something.
    substance - n That which is solid and practical in character, quality, or importance
    -- American Heritage Dictionary, Fourth Edition

    Admittedly, the AHD witlessly defines substance as essence (but not vice-versa). Someday I'll subscribe to the OED and get some decent definitions.

    In any case, you can think of essence as the character of a thing, and substance as the stuff that it's made out of. The essence of Exalted is badassery; the substance is giant swords and magic charms. The essence of Dogs in the Vineyard is tough moral choices; the substance is towns, coats, and guns.

    I define Color as essential details because they're the things that are really important -- they're the feel, the heat, the juicy goodness. System I define as the substantial details because they're what communicate that essence -- they're the vehicle, the conduit, the expression.

    It's why you don't need mechanics for essence (and probably, in fact, shouldn't) but you do for substance -- it is the purpose of substance to do the heavy lifting that expresses the essence.
