http://erebaltor.se/rickard/imagine_RickardElimaa.pdf (172 kb)

This storytelling game is 4 pages long.

- No fantastic elements.
- No reward mechanics.
- No dice or similar.
- No game master.
- No prep.
- No conflict.
- No acting-in-character.

- Based roughly on the ideas from the kishotenketsu thread.
- Has a hidden Yes, and-mechanic.
- Heavily based on the knowledge I got from the book Impro.

Don't focus on the language or the layout. It might change radically as this is only the second iteration. I do think I need feedback so I know that I'm heading in the right direction before I can go into the third iteration.

I can't wait until I can try this game to see how it's played out. The no conflict part is what excites me the most.

    This game is the peak of the vision of what I've been talking about since I came here. About how to design without using tangible rewards, about how most Forge games are following the same pattern where they ferociously seek conflicts in every scene, about how roleplaying games has too much text, about how we could form structures/rules of interaction that teaches people how to talk to each other, that you can immerse in more than the character, and what I started to question about how "roleplaying games" are not a genre in itself but a subgenre. This is very noticeable when some people have to describe their games as of what they don't include: dice, game master, acting in character, etc. Roleplaying games are just a minor area in a large subject.
