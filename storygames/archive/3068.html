\n I had what I thought was a great idea ...

... and it fell apart like most other ideas I have for a game. (This is not a case of feeling sorry for myself or self-deprecation - I have seen this cycle before.)

So, I'll see if anyone wants it or can help me rescue my scattered brain.

The primary idea is one that I can't seem to separate from a primary scenario of play that I had begun to imagine. Maybe by extracting back out the root idea, I'll help myself.

Here we go:

-Game is for four players with distributed GM roles. There would be a book of scenarios, each would take about 2-3 hours.
-The scenes of the game are kind of like the Shab-al-Hiri Roach (i.e. a particular scene will have its description already set for it), except that each scenario would come with 9-12 scenes with only 4 being played out, one in each act. Each time the scenario was played, there would be some baseline likeness but chance for newness.
-There would be four player roles with each scenario, and four characters (maybe more?). Players would chose their role (e.g. Betrayer, Seducer/Seductress) and, like the scenes, the system would chose which character the player would use. Whomever it was would now take on the role. For example, if Mark Twain were a character, in one game he could be the Betrayer and in another he could be a Seducer.
-For those roles, each would have a 'reveal' wherein the hidden nature of the character would come to light. Based on which act that happened in (1-4), the player would have instructions for the rest of session (e.g. if a Betrayer revealed herself in the first act, she would have to seek Redemption by the last act). Each act would require one Reveal for it to end.
-Each scenario would have some core chunk (currently undefined) that could be taken from it to the next and all the rest of the scenarios. Which order a group played them in would lead to a completely different arc. Otherwise, each scenario would basically be a reset. Unless that chunk was a character (like in AG&G/In a Wicked Age), there would be four new characters in each scenario.
-When all scenarios were completed, the combined chunks would lead to a final scenario of the play group's design which would wrap up their continuing story.
-The rules would be exactly the same for each scenario, so that when a group had made their way through one game, all the rest would be easier.
-The basic 'setting' would be Alternate History Earths with Cool Historical Celebrities.

So, with all that, I was trying to make the first scenario for Christmas, 1913, in Chihuahua, Mexico - where and when the awesome Ambrose Bierce disappeared.

And now I'm stuck. What are the basic roles that each scenario would have to have? Would you play Ambrose and others, or visitors? I've thought of time-travelers and tax collectors, but nothing seems to ring true. Would all scenarios involve a celebrity disappearing/dying? Would history be immutable (Ambrose must go missing, whatever that means) - or would you Quantum Leap it and say that history was changed for the better?

    \n OMG I think I have something.

    I've been torn on where the focus of a scenario should be. With so many possible Celebrities in any given scenario, if I, the creator dictate who the game should focus on (Ambrose Bierce) I'm limiting the fun. But how to allow for other Celebrities, with group control, and still have the aforementioned Betrayal-Redemption Sequence?

    THIS IS A QUICK THUMBNAIL OF AN IDEA:

    Christmas-time, 1913, Mexico

    Primary Cast
    Ambrose Bierce seeks to understand the pain that is War ... will his Cynicism overcome his Sentimentality?
    John Reed seeks fame and fortune as a 'War Correspondent' ... will his Ideals overcome his Integrity?
    Pancho Villa seeks to restore the Constitutional Government ... will his Needs overcome his Loyalty?
    La Coronela Mar\xc3\xada de la Luz Espinosa Barrera seeks to secure her place in history ... will her Desires overcome her Bravery?

    Primary Roles
    Betrayer
    Seducer
    Murderer
    Corrupter

    So, now we know who the big people are and the roles are assigned. Scenes get framed around certain individuals. Either mechanically or by negotiation, play will gravitate towards certain characters and that will determine, in the end, who the important characters really were. Also, as the Roles aren't already assigned, we could see Ambrose as a Corrupter in one scenario, and as a Murderer in another. Each Role is created to have an evolution, but involves other characters (because who wants to roleplay by yourself?). Note, you don't have to Betray, Seduce, Murder or Corrupt one of the Celebrities.

    My head is swirling. Does the game still have a sense of coherency or should I write up another document?

    \n I'm currently taking ideas for a placeholder title for this game other than, "We're Here to Rescue You, Mr. Beirce!"

    Here's my current idea for this game:

    Players play the role of Reality TV actors from the future. In the future, people want the 'real thing' when it comes to History. So they send the contestants back to interact with the Real Players of History.

    Gameplay revolves around two factors: Executing the Script and Maintaining your Popularity.

    Executing the Script: The Directors have not only chosen the time and locale of the episode, but they have certain things they want done, whether it be product placement of just plain old Plot. This is the easy stuff (but see below).

    Maintaining your Popularity: You will be vying for the opportunity to go to the next scenario. Voting is accrued throughout the session. Here's the thing: To get more Audience Points, you have to evoke an emotional attachment with them by revealing your (heretofore undefined) issues. These personal issues will conflict with the Scenario, because the Directors chose the Script based on what they knew about you already. So, as you make your way towards the next episode, you're having to struggle with your issues made manifest in the script you're performing.

    Just another braindump.
