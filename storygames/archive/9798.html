Over here, Dyson was wondering what sorts of things could be done, mechanically, to reinforce the importance of the three background stats (Next of Kin, Background, Last Night) in Black Cadillacs.

\n

I don't want to derail what could be a fun house rules thread, but I am burning with desire to talk about what those three stats do accomplish for me, when I play. I also want to know what they do for you!

\n

The obvious thing they do is give a quick, sketchy idea of the character -- it's a fun, collaborative way to do author characters. As Charles points out in the parent thread, the fact that they're authored not just by you is incredibly important. You'll never get "your character" back, and I find that a lot of fun as a player (incidentally, it also helps me avoid a perpetual pitfall of playing "Darcy's Stock Character #1").

\n

The other thing that they do for me is to provide a solid foundation for cooking up the Memory NPCs that I'll need to create as I play. With a decent sense of who the Trooper was (and what his relationship with his family was like) before going to war, I find it much easier to tease out new & re-occurring NPCs as I need them. Sometimes the new NPCs are actually new, and only inspired by the background stats. Sometimes, the new NPCs are actually featured in the background stats.

\n

Interestingly, since the inclusion of an NPC in a Memory is mandatory, the Backgrounds do have a mechanical benefit -- they help me meet the game's fictional content criteria for getting my big honkin' mechanical bennie.

\n

What do they do for you?
