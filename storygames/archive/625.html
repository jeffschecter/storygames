This is one of those I'm sharing something cool postings. The attacks of opportunity blog recently posted this link: http://jbdgames.blogspot.com/ It's a pretty cool blog about boardgame design and theory. The author of the blog writes:

>...I wrote a series of four articles, entitled Game Theory 101, which examined characteristics of game mechanics that I felt took games to a higher level. These four - never intended to be all inclusive - were:
>1) Story Arc - the sense of a game developing differently in its beginning, middle and endgame.
>2) The Bomb (I still wish I had a better term) - mechanics which create tension by offering players consequences that are disproportionate to the resources invested
>3) Agonizing decisions - confronting the players with decisions which defy analysis
>4) Nervous systems - an inherent instability in the game which forces players to always reevaluate their strategies.

That peaked my interest, so I found the original articles at:
* http://www.thegamesjournal.com/articles/GameTheory1.shtml
* http://www.thegamesjournal.com/articles/GameTheory2.shtml
* http://www.thegamesjournal.com/articles/GameTheory3.shtml
* http://www.thegamesjournal.com/articles/GameTheory4.shtml

They are well worth reading and might make good questions to ask when designing rpgs.
