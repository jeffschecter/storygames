    \n Halfjack, I hate to be a killjoy but that's not quite right. In fact 'burzum' means darkness and 'ishi' means within. This is because 'ishi' is a suffix placed onto the affected noun rather than a prefix. I do like the alphabet though. It's not dissimilar from Akkadian Cuneiform, and if orcs are going to have any kind of written culture it'd probably be something you could carve in stone with a few quick strokes of a chisel.

    Incidentally, Uruk-Hai is from the black speech too, and translates loosely as 'People', but as an extension of 'Uruk' meaning Soldier Ork, this probably refers to ork-kind rather than people in general. There are a few other words that have been discerned, including Snaga (slave), sharku (old man) and Lug (tower), but certainly not enough to make poetry from.

    [Edit] Oh, there they are in the margin... didn't see them there
