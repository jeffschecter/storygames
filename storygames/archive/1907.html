I've got a bee in my bonnet to run an old-skool dungeon crawly thing. I'm going to use TSOY and I've got four rockin' pre-gens that will be at each other's throats in a trice (it ended up looking a lot like The Sword, Luke's BW demo, because great minds think alike).

You can see my stuff here, where you can visualize the dominoes lining up - everybody has opposed/mutually exclusive Key action.

Anyway, I need help with the actual dungeon part of it. I've got an ancient Dwarf tomb, overrun by (D&Dish) goblins, who are (for some reason) hoarding a magical Elf weapon. There should be plenty of ass to kick, but I'm behind the times on modern tomb-stocking and I love plausibility. So:

1. Why do the goblins care about the magic sword? Why would they hoard it? Would they use it? Could they? Why the hell are they living in a moldy old tomb?

2. What sort of beasts could goblins conscript to help guard the tomb they've won? I've got dogs and ogres ... giant spiders and cave bears seem like tasty local megafauna. Plus living statues to guard sacred Dwarf stuff maybe. What's the new hotness? How about cool traps to kill interlopers?

3. What sort of pathetic things are going on down there to hit that Key of Conscience? Why do we pity those poor goblins? How can I introduce Instinct and Reason-based conflicts?

Any help much appreciated!

    1. Why do the goblins care about the magic sword? Why would they hoard it? Would they use it? Could they? Why the hell are they living in a moldy old tomb?

    The sword is a mighty powerful weapon of peace on par with the fabled sword of Arthurian legends. And just like Excalibur only those of pure heart or pure soul (or insert something) can handle the sword which will unite the kingdom of elves. In order to make the sword, the elves had to contract the help of dwarves to tap the energy of the earth and the heat of the planet to forge (insert crazy metal name) and the magic into the sword. Unknown to the elves, the contracting dwarves cut a few corners and bam! a little bit of evil made it into the combination. This evil drives everything to want to own it, but because only the pure of whatever can handle this blade, those not pure are turned to goblins. All the dwarves and all the elves were turned into goblins. The goblin shaman (formally an elf priest) has prophesied that one of pure whatever will eventually show up, and he or she will have the power to turn everyone back into whatever being they were before hand. Thus they all hang out in the caverns of the dwarves. Thus was born the Gossamer Sword of the Elvish Kind.

    2. What sort of beasts could goblins conscript to help guard the tomb they've won? What's the new hotness?

    Since, the goblins are depressed, they don't clean up after themselves and they need a gelatinous cube. And a smart dragon knows the power of the sword, but once committed a terrible sin, so is no longer pure of whatever, so it hangs out watching the sword in (insert emotion here). The dwarves were also gamblers and had set up an area for underground cock fights -- thus the chickens in the pens. Well, giant man-eating chickens in the pens. Giant ferrets and cobras for the snake fights. And giant pitbulls - just because giant pitbulls are cool. Probably a minotaur animal keeper who has no interest in swords, but is paid well by the dwarves now goblins. Plus, he likes to hang out in mazes. The dwarves built wonderful mazes for the cockfights and giant rat races. I didn't mention the uber-smart maze running giant rats yet? Hmmm... Looks like I haven't played D&D for awhile.

    What Paul said about "conscripted prostitution enterprise." The twisted mind of the goblin elves makes them search out creatures of the woods like sprites, nymphs, dryads, and capture them to bring them back to the dungeon. This has threatened the health of the local forests, because without these key species the woods can't survive. This has threatened the economy of the local towns. They depend on the forestry industry to export mast for the warships involved in the 100 year naval conflict happening off the coast of (blah, blah). As a mater of fact, this is the only forest in the whole kingdom that grows tall straight strong trees. The rest of the kingdom is small shrubs and twisted tree. The speculations among the kingdom's ecologists is that the heat released from the tunnels of the dwarves has created a mirco-climate that allows these tall mast trees to grow so far north in the tundra. That's probably why the king, president, or council sent the dungeoneers out. They had to fix the problem. Plus, the sword could probably win the war.

    How about cool traps to kill interlopers?

    There's only one trap worth using, ever! It's a classic. The dungeoneers walk into a room and the door locks behind them. Lava comes into the room from one side. Poison gas floats down from the ceiling. And water rushes in from the other side threatening to drown them. When the water hits lava the steam forces out the poison and no one gets hurt. Well, save vs. heart attack, sucka, or die.

    3. What sort of pathetic things are going on down there to hit that Key of Conscience? Why do we pity those poor goblins? How can I introduce Instinct and Reason-based conflicts?

    Don't know TSOY well enough to answer the Instinct and reason-based question, but we pity the goblins, because they were and are just like us. They had a small fault that turned them into goblins, and all they want is to be normal and back to their original self. They are tied to a sword they desire and one that can cure them but only if someone has the heart to do so. Of course, this means that if they see a dungeoneer who they think will help them more than the others...
