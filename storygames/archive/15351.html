\n Two new games have gone up on my website this week, in the post-Indiecon rush of energy...
\n
\nPixel Bitch 2.0: An expanded version of the micro-game available elsewhere on this site, giving more guidance to the players and a structure that better supports depth in the storytelling, Pixel Bitch 2.0: High Res is a tribute to an art form: the Graphic Adventure Game. This game doesn\u2019t need a computer or games console though, just a group of friends with some dice, pencils, tokens and a stock of interesting pictures...
\n
\nIn Camera (post Indiemeet 2nd draft): This is a game that's meant to capture the style & tone of films likes The Blair Witch Project, Cloverfield, Paranormal Activity and Apollo 18.
\n
\nIn the first two rounds of play, everyone creates cameras, using them to introduce locations or characters that will appear frequently in the story.
\n
\nIn subsequent rounds of play, each player can direct an event: during this, a deck of cards is used to pace the event, with red cards equating to light or positive situations and black cards equating to dark or negative ones. The director of the scene has a small hand of cards to hand out to the other players, facing their characters with an appropriate situation as he does so, but the director can make use of the cameras in the scene to 'edit' the footage with cuts, pans and zooms, changing the cards he has and therefore the situations.
\n
\nThere is a reciprocal process in action, in that players decide how their characters respond to the situation they are dealt and this decision feeds back into the cards that are available in later events, weighting them towards light or dark content.

    \nThe Final Voyage of The Selene: I liked Witch: the Road to Lindisfarne so much that I've been inspired to have a got at writing my own guided narrative-style freeformish game; this is a first draft of an attempt at a tale of a disaster in space, beginning from the start of a voyage from the frontier of human space and ending just when the ship had almost made it back to Earth.
    \n
    \nThis isn't quite finished: in particular, you will look in vain for the Roles and Agendas mentioned in the game, as I haven't finished them yet, but I'd still appreciate any feedback on the basic play-structure and the tone of the text.

    \n The last bit of stuff out of my head for now is It's Not Paranoia If They're Really Out to Get You.
    \n
    \nThis is not actually a scenario for Paranoia, but a set of house rules for playing it using any edition of the game; it is, in fact, a reverse hack, where the setting of the original game remains intact, but I've ported in an entirely new ruleset. This is for faster, lighter, indier play (Indier? Did I just make that word up?)
    \n
    \nAnyway, if you're curious, have a copy of Paranoia and would like a rules-lite option for play, you can download it here.
