\n
\n My wife and I have recently moved and during the unpacking I found my first gen Ipod shuffle. You know the one, it looks like a stick of chewing gum. I plugged it into the computer to see if the thing still worked after all these years and I was overjoyed to see a directory full of podcasts that I listened to back in 2005, maybe even earlier. I fondly re-listened to episodes of Gamer: The Podcasting, The Sons of Kyros, The Game Master Show and many more. It brought back a great deal of good memories. What podcasts do you remember from 5 or more years ago that have since either podfaded or only release episodes once in a great while (I'm looking right at you Game Master Show).

    \n The main ones that I miss are Ryan Macklin's Master Plan and Paul Tevis' Have Games, Will Travel, though their voices haven't disappeared or anything. Ryan's episode on Reincorporation probably influenced me more than any single other podcast episode. I may have to go through some of the archives again.

    I liked The RPG Haven Podcast, especially since walkerp did a good job of covering the kinds of small press games that got neglected for being neither mainstream nor forge-y.

    (My own podcast isn't dead, but it's become a thing I only do when I feel like it or when I corner someone particularly interesting for an interview.)
