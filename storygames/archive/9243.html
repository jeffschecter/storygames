    \n I learned this at Knutepunkt:

    Apparently at some Danish game conventions they pay a group to clean toilets and so forth, and the group they hire treats it as a paramilitary larp, with uniforms, medals, and missions. The guys who join this group act like they are a kind of larpish Foreign Legion, and get fired up to go on "tough missions" because they know it will advance their standing in the group.

    The guy who told me this was the principal of the Danish boarding school that uses roleplaying pedagogically called Osterskov Efterskole. They take their students to some of these conventions, he said, and every year they "lose a few students" to the janitorial task force for the duration.

    Posted By: TheFunkyPhantomRandom question- How vegetarian friendly will the food be?
    \nThe food will be very vegetarian and vegan friendly.

    There are a good number of vegetarians attending Camp Nerdly. This year, we also chose to reduce the price, which meant we cut the meal budget. Instead of hiring a chef for the weekend, we're going back to the model of volunteers preparing food. The first year, when we did this, it cut severely into game time, so we're trying to eliminate both cost and prep time. Meat is expensive to buy and store, and takes time to prepare, and is unsafe if prepared incorrectly. Therefore, the menu is almost completely vegetarian, and is mainly prepared ahead of time.

    A provisional menu - it likely will have some changes - looks like this:

    Friday evening - Snacks: chips, dips, and salsa, fruit, cheese and crackers, veggies and dip.
    Saturday morning - Pastries, bagels, cereal, fruit. Oatmeal, toast, and eggs.
    Saturday noon - Sandwiches and soup. Sandwiches: PB&J, cheese, hummus, veggie burgers. Soup: one soup, vegan.
    Saturday evening - Salad and pasta bar. Pasta sauce, olive oil, cheese. Lettuce, spinach, cucumbers, peppers, tomatoes, salad dressings. Rolls. Pudding afterwards.
    Sunday morning - Pastries, bagels, cereal, fruit. Oatmeal, toast, and eggs.
