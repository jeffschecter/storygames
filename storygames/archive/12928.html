\n Are you going to be at PAX? Do you want to do something besides wait in line for videogame demos? This post is for you!

So you want to run a game?
Awesome! Please do! If you're looking for more players, you can use the #ttlfg (tabletop - looking for group) tag on twitter. There's also the @pax_indie_rpgs twitter account, which has a bunch of followers just waiting to get in on games. You can post messages to that account by talking to me or tweeting at me (@olde_fortran on twitter). If you ask nice I'll just give you access to the @pax_indie_games account so you can promote your games yourself.

Where?
This is where it gets interesting. We don't officially have a room, so we're trying to make room 304 the unofficial official indie games room. 304 is a freeplay room, so the only way we can make it 'ours' (and give people a central place to find indie games) is to have games going in there. We can do that, right?

Room 304 should be just off the escalators as you some in from Pike street. I'll be there 10 am (when the con opens) every day to try to establish some indie presence. The more people the better.

So you want to play a game?
That's just as awesome as running one! The only way we can make room 304 'ours' is by having games happening in it pretty consistently, so your warm body filling a spot at a table is just as awesome. If you're between games, just taking up space in 304 is still kind of useful, until there's a game that wants to happen there.

What can I do to help?
So glad you asked. The best thing you can do is come and play games in room 304. Really. That easy. If you really want to promote, we'll have some flyers to hand out with information, or you can just tell people about it. In particular it'd be cool to have someone outside some of the RPG panels handing out flyers, particularly the GeekNights guys panel, they did a lot of promoting of indie games last year.

    \n Got there late on Friday, after a long, shitty day of other stuff, stumbled upon John Harper heading down the escalator to 304, about to play some Dungeon World (Sage's hack of Tony's hack of Apocalypse World). Other folks were playing Fiasco and preparing for Dresden Files. Wandered through the exhibition hall where I was unexpectedly impressed by the new Mortal Kombat game (evocative, back to roots, beautiful, brutal) and drooled along with everyone else to Portal 2. Stumbled upon Lesley, who was working the show in a support role for a number of booths in the same general region (including Chessex). She showed me where the secret Hidden Level is where a lot of the tabletop stuff happens (part of the Dreaming's booth, some open play tables). It was cool but a bit disappointing, which is true of most things tabletop-related at the show. Even WOTC's booth was mostly about playing Magic online, despite the fact that the Castle Ravenloft boardgame just dropped and the new Red Box is coming out next week. Went back to 304 and ran Apocalypse World for a bunch of folks who hadn't played it. It rocked. Then got amazing-but-pricey drinks at Vessel with some folks, including Ryan Macklin From The Internet and Chris Hanrahan of Endgame in Oakland (the store that sells more indie games than any store in the country). Got home after midnight. Skipping Saturday but will be back Sunday with my brother.

    Posted By: Caesar_XYou'd think a non-game con like PAX would sell maybe 10-20% of Gen Con.
    \nPAX is the ultimate game con. It's mostly enthusiastic gamers looking for new and hot. There are three people on my team at work (two of them RPGers waaaaaay back in the day, would probably be receptive to trying again) who were planning a trip to PAX East: Affluent, 30s-40s hipsters just looking to see what's up, with money to spend and eyes on cool things to do. Conversely with GenCon, it seems that most people are "locked in" for exactly what they're going to do for those four days of gaming long before they book tickets to the event, and seldom venture outside those circles.

    There might be a burn effect later: Once something is hot for a time, it will quickly disappear from the public mind (lots of indie PC games had this happen; Sins of a Solar Empire, etc). So don't think that a mention on a blog == infinite sales and press. Still, it's an interesting scene.

    Also, I find it totally weird that no one here posts on the Penny Arcade tabletop gaming forum. I mostly lurk there, posting when there is an upcoming gaming or anime con (for meetups/face to face gaming, etc) but there are a LOT of folks on that forum, lots of interesting actual-play style discussion (a lot of it D&D 4e). Might be a good time to see if you can reconnect with some of those Fiasco players, if they frequent that forum, and see how they are enjoying their game after the con.

    -Andy

    Posted By: sageGetting Wil or Gabe or Tycho to mention our stuff is an ongoing battle.
    \nReal quick: It's not a "battle." It isn't out job to get nerdfamous people to mention our tribe's wares. That way lies a dangerous road, where we're too pushy. They're already mentioning our stuff -- for people like Paul or Jason, they've already won with the amount of buzz given to their games.

    If our task is to promote, then our job is to be awesome. Very, very loudly awesome. If we are, people will take note. It might take longer that some folks are cool with, but slowly, day by day, more people will take note. (Yes, I say this as a guy who gave W, G&T stuff at PAX.)

    If it's going to happen, let it happen naturally. Let our enthusiasm be the catalyst, not a drive to achieve an agenda.

    - Ryan
