\n I saw RPSLS on the Big Bang Theory. The system expands the traditional RPS with two more options while preserving the rest of the structure of the original, but reduces the number of ties and works better with multiple participants.

Scissors cut paper
paper covers rock
rock crushes lizard
lizard poisons spock
Spock breaks scissors
scissors decapitate lizard
lizard eats paper
paper disproves Spock
Spock vaporizes rock
rock breaks scissors

Thus, there is only a tie when the participants choose the same object; all other combinations produce a winner and loser.

Given that I'm a year behind in my Big Bang theory, has anyone put the lizard-Spock addition to good use?

    \n At one point I had a sketch of a kung-fu fighting game where you used the five Chinese elements (see Dave's Facebook game post above). Depending on your skill, you might know two to five moves: an unskilled warrior might only be able to pull Water and Metal, say, whereas an expert could do everything except Wood. Only a true master would know all five. This meant that each time you found someone new, you'd need to figure out what moves they could do, so the first few rounds of a conflict would be feeling each other out, until you had an idea what moves they could do, then you could choose your attacks strategically to pick the moves that beat their moves.

    Additional note; I've always liked how Mind's Eye Theater uses The Bomb to provide a bonus throw (e.g. for having Potence). Bomb beats rock and paper, but scissors cuts its fuse. You have to announce "I have the bomb" to let the opponent know it's possible... but you don't have to throw it. Nice tweak.
    \nI've always thought that being forced to announce 'the bomb' completely negated any value it might have because it doesn't really change the odds of winning (assuming vaguely competent players) just produces an alternate outcome sequence. Rather than changing increasing your number of usable signs it actually decreases the number viable choices. In effect you are reduced to two viable symbols (rock) and (bomb), as (paper) is effectively subsumed by (bomb) having the same weakness and defeating the same opposition, and (scissors) is irrelevent as (bomb) defeats it's opponent without the weakness. Conversely the opponent is reduced to two viable symbols as well (scissors) and (paper) while (rock) can be beaten by the same thing as (paper) but can not produce as positive an outcome. The only value to pulling one of the vestigal symbols for either side would be if you were specifically pushing for a tie, but since ties have an internal resolution it would almost always make no sense.

    \n Asymmetrical RPS seems very interesting to me, but it seems easy to throw the balance off.



    These days, if I made a RPS based rpg, you'd have story elements tied to each sign, much like Nine Worlds tying methods to each card suit. You need to narratively justify throwing Paper by tying your love of your kid brother into the conflict, somehow, or else you're stuck with throwing Rock (as your skill at martial arts or whatever has already come into play and can be freely invoked).

    As for the benefit (or lack thereof) from using "The Bomb".

    This introduces a great psychological element to the game. I always found in MET that if you announce the Bomb, people have a much higher chance of using scissors (to cut the bomb's wick)...so a better than average chance of succeeding came from declaring the Bomb then throwing Rock.
