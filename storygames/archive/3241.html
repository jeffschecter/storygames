\n Hey, all you WWII history buffs and war movie / novel / game fans out there, help me out!

While I'm busy crafting my 44 ashcan, my mind inevitably wanders. I've been kicking around a game design for about a year tentatively called The Code Breakers. It's about WWII espionage and covert and clandestine action.

So, I'd love to hear some media favorites from the crowd to keep this backburner lit, but not buring down my design house just yet.

I'm specifically looking for movies that are about special action, rather than military epics. So, Saving Private Ryan, no. Enigma, yes.

Some of my favorite films:\n
  • The Guns of Navarone
    • \n
  • The Bridge Over the River Kwai
    • \n
  • Enigma
    • \n
  • The Dirty Dozen
    • \n
  • Das Boot (Barely qualifies? There's an Enigma machine at least? Ok, not. I just love it.)
\nOnes I've seen only bits of or am aware of, sorta (and I'm assuming they qualify based on the little I know about them): \n
  • Where Eagles Dare
    • \n
  • Force 10 From Navarone
    • \n
  • A Bridge Too Far
    • \n
  • U-571 (I've seen, but it pretty much stinks)
\nI confess not having read much in the way of fiction. My list so far is pretty much Cryptonomicon (reading now, and enjoying it greatly) and a couple of so-so W.E.B. Griffin novels my dad lent me (maybe just need to get into 'em again -- they left me mostly flat).

So .. \n
  • Cryptonomicon
    • \n
  • The Last Heroes, The Secret Warriors, etc. (Griffin's Men At War series)
    • \n
  • Hitler's Spies (nonfiction)
    • \n
  • Couple other odds and ends non-fiction wise
\nI also have a nice and growing horde of non-ficion material, including some doozies on German espionage and some less academic but fun and full of pictures on the OSS.

And, of course, there's the computer game geek in me. \n
  • Return to Castle Wolfenstein
    • \n
  • Medal of Honor: Allied Assault
    • \n
  • Call of Duty 1 & 2
\nAnyone have other favorites? Jason Morningstar, I don't even want to know how many thousand nonfiction pages you might suggest, but bring it on. I may actually read a dozen or so pages.

    \n Hmm, you could do worse than look up M.R.D. Foot's books on SOE: SOE in France : an account of the work of the British Special Operations Executive in France, 1940-1944, SOE : an outline history of the Special Operations Executive, 1940-46, and SOE in the low countries.

    For OSS you can get a lot of traction with government documents and compilations:

    War report of the OSS, prepared by History Project, Strategic Services Unit, Office of the Assistant Secretary of War, War Department

    Robert Louis Benson's A History of US Communications Intelligence during World War II: Policy and Administration. Series IV, Volume 8, National Security Agency Center for Cryptologic History, 1997

    The Secrets War: The Office of Strategic Services in World War II (Washington: National Archives and Records Administration, 1991)

    Russell Miller's Behind the Lines: The Oral History of Special Operations in World War II is a general overview.

    CIA's suggested reading list.

    I don't have much on the Abwehr or German side. One suggestion: Charles Wighton and Gunter Peis' Hitler's Spies and Saboteurs.

    Another suggestion: Ron's Spione bibliography?
