    \nMetamorphosis Alpha: Cyclops' Tear

    The Black Stars have aligned - the passage is open. As the shaman for the Unseen Eye tribe*, you must go beyond the known world into the unknown. Few have ventured, but none have returned in many generations. You must go forth and secure the favor of the Gods for your people. Cyclops' Tear is a solo adventure for the remake of Metamorphosis Alpha. This adventure uses a number of random tables to increase the replayability of this adventure. You player's powers, the crisis that the tribe faces, and your goals can vary from one game to the next.


    * The mutant cyclops of the Unseen Eye tribe one have one natural eye. Their gifted eye has other senses and abilities depending on the player's random mutations. Ignore the dufus in the back with two eye holes. That's Conrad, one of his eyes is actually painted in.

    \nOpening Ceremony

    This is a game about genius and relevance, for two players.

    The first half of the game is played in isolation. "The One" needs to outline a work of fearful majesty and sweeping implication. "The Many" needs to outline an insurmountable problem that lies before them, and the potential ruin that they face.

    The second half of the game is played upon meeting up. "The Many" reveal the insurmountable problem. "The One" reveals their work of genius. If "The One" can help "The Many," disaster is averted. If not, all perish.

    After playing, both players switch roles: now you're "The Many" and I'm "The One." We go our separate ways, play the first half in isolation, and play the second half whenever we meet up again. The emergent element of the game is that we learn to anticipate and challenge one another, and the game eventually becomes a tool for understanding one another.
