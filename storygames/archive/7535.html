    Posted By: Jason MorningstarOne thing that struck me was that using Solar System, you'll very much have to make it your own, authoring new Keys and Secrets appropriate for your game. This is a good thing.
    \nAmen. I think it's a brilliant thing. Character options that work off of making the characters the exception to the rules. like Secrets or Feats or Stunts or what have you, wreak havoc on stake-setting resolution. They're little landmines waiting for someone to set the wrong kind of stakes and then . . . Hold up! I spent good XP on being able to disarm, and now you're telling me John can do it with just a roll? There are quite a few games where I've had to read through their list of character options and make a list of all those hidden rules-proven-by-exceptions before sitting down to play.

    But when you and yours author your own, all those fiddly little bits are out in the open from the get go. So you not only know which toes might be stepped on, but you have a clearer picture of what you want the characters capable of in the game and where you want the stakes to go.

    \n It "adds" one mechanical piece in the form of Effects, which basically is a formalized way to delay ability check results. Effects were implicit in the TSOY system, i.e. in the Ammenite Poison rules or in parts of 3 Corner Magic. They complement secrets in a way that they give character preparation mechanical weight. They are to Solar System what Declarations and Assessments are to Spirit of the Century.

    \n I'm not very motivated by the pdf thing myself, because the text is essentially free; I CC licensed it. So anybody buying a PDF would just get the same thing that me or somebody else is going to put up in the Internet pretty soon anyway.

    As for when I have a tidy website to point people to when they want to look at my version of the system... we'll see. I seem to remember that Harald or somebody expressed interest in taking the text and helping in inputting it into Clinton's wiki - or I can do it myself when I get over my awful post-Gencon crunch and have some time for hobbying. Or if the wiki proves unfeasible, then perhaps I'll work up a nice-looking html version. Whatever.

    As for whether you'd want to get my version if you already have TSoY: I recommend the new text to TSoY-heads, the sort of folks who make a point of understanding this particular rules system at expert level. Those who just like Near or might even not have any special preference for TSoY... why bother? It's just a rewrite with slight changes, nothing more. I'd wait for the new edition of TSoY, THAT'll have interesting stuff in it.

    Posted By: Paul T.
    I piped in because I thought it was unfortunate I couldn't find any reviews of Eero's work. How have people liked it so far? It could use some words, good or bad, that people googling the thing could find. Of course, Clinton's comment in this thread is enough for me.
    \nWell, you get a detailed description of how the system works, gming advice, instructions for creating your own Keys and Secrets - either before play starts or on the fly -, some nifty variant rules and several examples of advanced crunch (gadgets, drugs, werewolves and martial arts), all at a great price and with a simple but effective presentation. The only thing I don't like is the way the vignettes (short narrative examples showing how the rules are applied) interrupt the flow of the text. Apart from that, these rules are an excellent Solar System toolkit. The rules for 'Effects' are really cool, by the way.
