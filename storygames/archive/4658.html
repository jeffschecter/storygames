    \n Hang on, got it. It's to do with the paper size.

    I'd tried to create a 6x9 book. Lulu assumed, behind the scenes, that I wanted to print this in the US, so charged me huge amounts for shipping.

    If I try to create an A4 book instead (I've created a blank test book), shipping goes down to a reasonable amount, because it's printing here.

    That said, one of the options for international sizes is 15.24cm x 22.86cm, which is 6x9 inches. So I'm a little confused. Still.

    There are a couple of other quirks, too. The first time I tried to print my A4 book, I still had a 6x9 book in my cart. Lulu decided to print the whole damn thing in the States and charge me the huge amount for shipping. Even when I deleted the 6x9 book, so that only the A4 book was left in the cart, it still decided to print the whole thing in the States.

    It only worked when I emptied my cart, went to the page with the A4 book on it and started the whole buying process again.

    Mmm. OK. I will have to do some thinking about paper sizes, then.

    \n No...wait...I'm wrong. It's not the paper size.

    Yesterday, I attempted to order my 6x9 book again: and it told me the same huge shipping fee. I experimented: I created a blank A4 book, which produced the normal shipping fee (about 5 dollars). I created a blank 6x9 book, and got the 5 dollar fee.

    Mystified, I went back to my first book. This time, it gave me the smaller shipping fee. And, now, I've ordered it, for the small shipping fee.

    Later (and, I must say, extremely promptly), I got this message from Lulu:\n
    Dear Graham,

    If you click on the General Preferences link for your account on the left side of the My Lulu page, then you can change your Currency Preference to Pounds Sterling. That will enable the EU Saver Shipping option for orders being sent to the UK.
    Once you do that, you can click on the Shopping Cart link in the upper right of most Lulu pages and see the new shipping option.

    Please feel free to contact us if you have any additional questions.
    \nSo there you go. I'd guess that, while experimenting with blank A4 books, I tripped some option, which made Lulu decide to print it in the UK.
