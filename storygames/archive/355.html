But the two ideas I'm most likely to actually write and submit both seem to be of equal worth and interest to me. So i'm looking for feedback on the two ideas, to see what people think of each.

    The first idea is a game using the 4 sessions of 2 hours rule, with Committee, Emotion and something else (not sure which yet). You play a trial sequence, wherein one player is the Accused, one the Victim, one representing the Committee that judges, and one a Witness to the crime. It is clear from the start that the Accused committed the crime he is acused of, but the Committee needs to determine his motives for doing so. So each participant tells his or her version of the crime over the course of a session (Committee going last, as a judgement of what happened). Each version will be similar in broad strokes, but different in details and, importantly, in the motives of the participants. At this point, I'm thinking of some card based mechanic wherein each suit is a particular emotion or motive, and in conflicts you play cards and narrate why you're doing things as well as how. Each session the Trump suit changes, too, so you are portraying your character with different motives than before. (Think Rashomon or Hero, or courtroom dramas.)

    The other idea uses the ten sessions of one hour apiece. One player takes the role of the Actor, who is in a precarious osition that will end very badly for him within an hour and is trying to avoid that fate. The other players are playing NPCs and impartial forces trying to push the Actor toward a given ending, most of which are bad. Each session these roles rotate around, so probably everyone gets to be the Actor. Each session also plays out over the same span of time: the Actor reaches the same crisis, then tries to avoid in in some novel way, and the other oppose this in various ways. So onceagain you have multiple different versions of events, though in this case they're more like alternate realities than alternate viewpoints. I've got the start of a system whereby the hour time limit affects the difficulty of the Actor's actions, whereas the session number increases the other player's actions.(Think Run Lola Run.)

    I'm too busy to enter, in any case, but I have some musings. It's all peanut gallery stuff, so take it with a grain of salt.

    I really like the time theme, and I'm pretty happy with either of the two ingredient sets, actually. At the same time, I think there may be too many choices to make, with the unexpected consequence of closing off rather than opening up design options.

    Here's what I mean by that -- I think the theme and the ingredients may be in competition. (I thought this last year as well, for what it's worth.) So the theme may give you a great idea (or two or three), and then you want to run with it, but then you have to consider the ingredients -- and this would be an issue regardless of what the specific ingredient sets are. So you can't focus your efforts on nailing the theme as much as you could with wide open ingredients.

    On the other side, with the "here are four ingredients, choose three, go do anything with them" set up (ie with no theme), even though you have a very tight ingredient limitation, you can focus on it immediately. You go straight at doing cool things with your ingredients.

    Basically, you'd be working within just one constraint set rather than within two distinct sets of constraints. So with either themes or ingredients, you have limitations in one area, but things are wide open in everything else. But when you combine both ingredients and a rules theme, each set of constraints (and each set of inspirations, because the constraints are all inspirations as well of course) competes with the other for your design attention. You end up with divided priorities.

    I think that would have been the speed bump for my design process. But again, I'm too busy to seriously consider taking part this year (and I was last year as well), and I'm being cowardly and dodging even helping with judging, so I'm in an easy position to make loud noises of no particular consequence. I still think the competition looks cool this year, and I still wish I had time to join in.

    If I were making a game? I'd probably go with ancient, committee, and emotion. It'd be probably be 1 two hour session. And it'd be pretty meta -- you'd go into it playing those wizards and demons and elves and whatever at the ancient committee meeting to put another dungeon down in the world somewhere, and of course you know how committees go when people get their little pet projects and pet peeves, and get too emotional. And you'd end the two hour session with an actual, playable dungeon to go into with your D&D group. (The core books of your favorite version of D&D would be required, and everything would have to be by the book, just pull it out and put it in rather than creating new stuff, due to the time limitation.)

    Something like that. It wouldn't just be a brainstorming session, of course -- it'd have to be a game, all its own, and an RPG proper. The characters would probably be pre-gens, and the pet peeves and pet projects would be among their relevant traits... hm. I haven't thought it out in any real detail because I know it ain't gonna happen with my current schedule.

    Rich

    Blue, that sounds very similar to the idea I'm working on. I haven't announced it anywhere, but I'll do so now, if anybody is interested.

    The Ancient Greek World, during the Age of Gods and Men: Zeus, father of all the Gods has decided that he wants a new religious festival for the Mortals to honor him. He has declared that it will be known as the "Olympics" and shall be held in four years time in the most worthy City-State in all of the Greek World - anywhere from Iberia to the Black Sea.

    But there are more city-states than Zeus can be bothered to remember, so to discover which is most worthy, he has chosen a number of his Godly offspring to do it for him. They will be known as the "Mt. Olympus Committee", and will report back in four mortal years, or Zeus shall rip all of Creation asunder.

    Now, each of these Gods has a particular city-state where they are worshipped more than the others, and they want "their" city-state, and by extension, them to receive Zeus' favor. So, to come to an agreement, the Gods each summon a Hero to champion their city-state, and then the champion who completes the most heroic tasks shall win the right for his city-state to host the Olympics.

    But Gods cannot stop themselves from meddling in Mortal affairs, and the Committee is no different. They bicker, bribe, lend favors, call in favors and persuade the others to set ever greater challenges in the way of the other champions whilst doing the same to grant Boons to aid their own champion. The city-state to receive the games will have less to do with the exploits of champions in the mortal world, and more to do with the deals happening at Mount Olympus.

    Ancient/Committee/Emotion and 4x2hrs, with one session covering one year. Each hero has a number of emotional 'flags' which form the basis of the Situation creation system, but I'm struggling with that part at the moment.
