    \n Don't go to Pax East thinking it's Gen Con or Dreamation. I did that and was disappointed (my fault).

    You will enjoy Pax if you:

    - like video games.
    - enjoy playing demos at booths, including many indie RPGs.
    - enjoy playing D&D encounters.
    - like being around lots of awesome, young, energetic, creative, diverse people.
    - want to be around gaming culture, video game music, old school arcades, cosplay.
    - love playing boardgames, the boardgame area is incredible, many games are available, it's easy to find players.
    - enjoy exhibit halls.
    - go to seminars.
    - are a game designer / publisher.


    It's possible you won't enjoy Pax East if you:

    - dislike waiting on lines.
    - want to play scheduled RPG events that aren't D&D (I've heard they want to change this).


    I also highly suggest staying in a connected or near by hotel, or rent a car. We took public transportation and that wasn't a good idea (trains stop working past a certain time).

    Posted By: Steve SegedyHey Joshua, can you break down how the Bazaar will work at PAX? I'm interested (and I'd imagine other people would be as well) in how you'll be organizing schedules, assigning roles, handling sales, etc. Is it a profit-sharing gig, or does everyone just pay out for booth costs?

    \n* I'm handling booth scheduling. We'll have at least 3 people at a time
    * As far as "assigning roles, handling sales, etc." Crotechetocracy**? Really, other than booth scheduling, it's just booth design and setup (we will slap something together on Friday, then Joshua will arrive on Saturday and fix it), and scheduling everyone who's available to help set it up and break it down.
    * Sales will be handled with a cash box and Square - Joshua's got a Square account and there are likely enough dongles and smart devices to allow us to take cards the whole time. Also, we stopped using paper receipts at the last Dreamation, relying instead on the Square app to track cash sales as well; I expect we'll continue this.
    * Even split of costs of booth per-publisher. (Total cost of the booth / x publishers = cost per publisher), you get 100% of what you sell, back to you
    * We'd still be open to people coming on

    ** This means whoever cares the most about it is in charge of it.
