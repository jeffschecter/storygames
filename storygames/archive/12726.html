\n
\nSo if you liked the game you just demoed would you want to brand the shirt your wearing with the game's logo? baring of course if your the kind of person who was wearing some kind of fancy clothes at the time. or maybe brand on top of a swag shirt some other company gave you at the show (i'm thinking of doing this at pax, people get free shirts there like crazy)
\n
\nI almost feel like someone has done this before.

\ni imagine we would brand the shirts outside using a can of t-shirt spray paint.

    \n You might consider getting some disposable dust masks (either the flat filter-paper kind that doctors use [from a pharmacy I'll bet] or the you use for construction/power tools/yard work) so that you manikins don't breath in too much of the aerosol.

    You might also get good results with a well wrung sponge and some tie die (laying on the stencil and dabbing with the sponge). The advantage here is that you can do it indoors. The downside is you probably want latex gloves to keep your hands their natural color.

    A wood-block stamp might also work well. You could experiment with tracing a design onto a thin piece of wood and cutting a stamp with a scroll saw - glue on a handle and you have a big stamp you could brush ink onto and stamp shirts with.
