    \n My take on it?

    I'm happy to pay for the registration. I'd pay for the registration even if I did register to run enough hours. DexCon 2006 is where I met most of you guys. DexCon is how I got to feel like I belonged to something. I've never done enough there to get a free badge, and that's OK by me. I get to see you jokers. It's worth it at twice the price.

    I admit that on some level I laugh a little (not in a condescending way or anything like that!) when people worry about doing 16 hours of GMing. I'm thinking back to AmberCon Northwest, where the Evil Hat core crew of the time (Rob, Lydia, myself) would show up and run several 8-hour games, using three GMs, occupying between 12 and 18 players at a time. 4-hours-and-that's-it gaming has always seemed a little lightweight to me by comparison.

    No less *awesome*, mind you. Just smaller scale.

    Anyway, it's that sort of experience that makes me have a kneejerk, "What's the big deal? Cut it down to 12 hours and do 5 / 5 / 6 players, or 8 hours and do 8 / 8."

    And, yeah, we're not all Luke Crane, but I don't think we NEED to be for the 12-hour version. The 8-hour version? Yeah, okay. Point. Whenever I go over 6 players, I'm definitely into the second GM territory.
