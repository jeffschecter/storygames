\n My first micro-game (of the awkwardly long title above) is now available for general purchase. It's a $5 8-page magazine or a $2 pdf. The magazine comes with the pdf, and buying it at all gives you access to the ndp microgames pdf library, which will be updated with screen-ready, vertical-as-printed and no-art easy-printing pdfs of each microgame as they come out.

More details and links to all outlets are here. The print version is through MagCloud here, and if you just want the PDF I prefer you buy direct here (though it's also on onebookshelf).

The pitch:

The King has been murdered. The only witnesses, his sons, gather to determine who committed the foul deed - and, more importantly, decide upon the heir to the throne. There is not much time. If a successor is not named by sundown, the agreements that the King had struck with Crow, the demon-god of trickery, will be broken and the kingdom will perish in a tide of blood and fire. Can you save your fathers kingdom?

This is a short-form fiction game for 3-6 players. It has a one-hour time limit, and uses a blind-draw mechanic to structure how much weight each player can give each other player's story about what happened to the King. It also has a "traitor" aspect, where one of the players may have drawn a coin that means they are the Ravensworn, secretly consecrated to Crow - if the princes can agree on an heir in the hour, everybody wins, but if the heir is the Ravensworn, he turns the Kingdom over to Crow and the other princes all lose.

I couldn't have done it without the amazing Kickstarter backers, so thanks again to them! And many thanks to J Walt for running his Murderland contest back in the day, without which this game wouldn't exist.
