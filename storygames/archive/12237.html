\n A little while ago I finally got to play Fiasco with my husband and our friend Travis over Skype. None of us use Google Wave, which I know has been previously mentioned as a tool for running Fiasco online. But we've used Graham Walmsley's dice roller before for previous games over Skype. We figured that with judicious use of labels that we could make it work.

It wound up working pretty well, although I found myself wishing that we had more vertical room so that we didn't have to scroll sideways as much. Since there weren't preset black and white options for dice we went with red for black and blue for white - although it should be noted that you can use color codes for custom dice if you feel like it.

After rolling up all the dice and finishing the setup, things looked like this:



After the setup, all the dice got dragged to the left side so we could keep track of the pool. Things went pretty smoothly overall. And here's what it looked like at the end of the game:



All in all it went pretty well. We used the Southern Town playset and wound up cramming the whole game into about 2 hours, although we could have broken it into two sessions since games on Skype are always much longer than in person. And we were still able to put all the elements together into a total Fiasco that resulted in the mafia and a Mexican gang going after two characters in the hospital and killing each other all off, a football team hopped up meth setting loose a tiger, and the final scene being my character facing down the tiger with a halligan bar just before the meth lab exploded.



Good times
