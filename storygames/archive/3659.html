\n Inspired by the way Jason was thinking in the index card thread, and having a little free time on my hands, I made a couple of scene starters on Abulafia:

Fantasy Scene

Spycraft Scene

I intend to use them (esp. the Spycraft one) for quick warm-up 'in media res' scenes at the start of a game to get blood pumping and creativity flowing. And I fully intend to use the 'Mountain Witch Trick' when possible for setups like \n
You're with a slow-witted Greek tourist at a private yacht, about to leave the party together, when an old rival shows up unexpectedly...
\nWho is he and what were you rivals for?

But I can use help brainstorming interesting things for the last bit (what I refer to here as 'Situation', but I know thats misuse). Especially things that might happen anywhere to make a scene suddenly require action on the part of a character. Some of my current ones for the Spycraft generator:

1,an ex-lover shows up unexpectedly
1,an old rival shows up unexpectedly
1,armed thugs arrive, firing guns into the air
1,the red dot of a laser sight begins to creep across your companion's face
1,smoke from an unknown source begins to seep into the area
1,a dangerous wild animal enters the area
1,you spot a concealed explosive device, which may be on a timer or remote-activated
1,gunfire erupts nearby
1,you begin to suspect you've been drugged
1,a nearby explosion knocks you to the ground
1,you notice that your companion's cell phone is better than yours
1,you get the distinct impression your luck is about to change
1,your companion asks you a question you know you shouldn't answer truthfully
1,you begin to wonder which side you're really working for
1,you notice an earpiece in your companion's ear
1,your palms begin to sweat; always a bad sign
1,you notice your companion's unusual tattoo


So, help with that is appreciated, or give ideas on how/when to use such generators, or whether they're useful at all.
