[MB] Why can't I be as cool as you think I am?

So, Misery Bubblegum's core mechanic is that you give someone else an opinion ("You are cool!") and that helps them when they are living up to that opinion (i.e. the other player gets to roll your dice (and then return them to you!) when they act "cool").

Over in this thread we talked a lot about why you would want to reject other people's opinions of you. I want that to be a meaningful choice, to drive a second angst-and-suffering economy that will inform and enliven the opinion economy.

Much of what that other thread boiled down to is that some opinions are hard to live up to ("You're an honest, decent guy") and some of them you don't want to live up to ("You're a trouble-maker, just like your father was"). But as the rules stand, it's always the players choice whether to use any opinion or not. So the mechanics aren't yet driving the choices that indirectly sustain the later choices.

What I'm thinking, right now, is that one of the outcomes of conflict resolution is that a player can lose or regain the ability to use one of the opinions on her character. So, like, if someone has given me the "Cool" opinion, one of the outcomes of the conflict resolution could give someone the right to block that ... I can still act "cool," but I can't spend the dice on that opinion, which means that (a) I don't get any advantage from being "cool", (b) the player who awarded me the "cool" opinion doesn't get their dice back and (c) that player cannot increase their "cool" opinion of my character to higher levels.

I think that roughly corresponds to the "coolness" being just shallow, surface posturing rather than something more. And I'll admit that I sort of like the idea that external events can result in people being unable to truly address opinions. Like, if your little kid sister thinks you're incredibly cool, but then you lose the one-on-one basketball game then maybe you just can't feel that any more ... and so her faith, be it ever so pure, becomes powerless to help you until some other external conflict unlocks your hangup.

Does that sound like it would make sense with the way people actually act? Does this sound like the kind of thing that people do and feel? Or is there some other way that people become unable to live up to the expectations of others?
