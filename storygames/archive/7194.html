\n When running my Lacuna games I've always ended them when the last agent either gets off the slab, or dies. Primarily this is because I run it once every 4-5 months and don't have a firm grasp on mythography and focus my energy and attention on the mission itself.
\n
\nTypically I then handle the debrief as a fait accompli posted in the post game wrap-up online, instead of specifically playing it.
\n
\nHow do you run the debriefs? Any tips on making my games better with them?

    \n Debriefing is my favorite part of Lacuna, particularly if we're in a campaign-style game. It's a chance to develop relationships between the agents and their mentor.

    One theme that has worked well for us is to treat the debrief as a "satisfaction and improvement" survey: Do you feel Control provided adequate support during your mission? Can you share any opinions on the leadership qualities of your lead agent? Were you comfortable with the mission parameters as they were presented to you? Do you have any recommendations for future improvements?

    (Naturally these will relate to the freakiness of the mission -- Control disappeared for a while, the team leader was given contradictory orders, the mission parameters were totally fucked, everyone nearly died, etc.)

    Typically my debriefer will turn that last question into an opportunity to launch into psych analysis of the character/player: Why do you think you feel such a strong need for explicit mission parameters? Did your parents provide adequate boundaries for you during childhood? How do you feel about your mother? Do you regularly masturbate? And so on.

    p.

    \n I tell the players what their bosses think of them, and how their job goes for the next little while. Out of Blue City, out of the game, no roleplaying.

    I also ask them what they put in their reports. This may, or may not, influence their bosses.
