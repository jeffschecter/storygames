    \n I'm pretty psyched about Origins this year. Last year I got in some Shock, Annalise, and Hellas, but events filled up so quickly online because of their system skitzing out. This year it seems like there's so many more of the games I like to play available.

    Not sure what you guys are looking to do in Columbus in the minutes you have between gaming till your eyes fall out and your fingers cramp from rolling dice, but I have a few cool things in mind.

    1. Debating a house party. We live about a 5 minute walk from the convention center. Thoughts? Cheaper than a bar, but people might not want to leave the hotels/convention center.
    2. Fun bars in the area range from the Surly Girl that's a saloon and has women brewed beers to the hip pizza/bar/punk rock place called Hounddogs. Bodega has like, 80 beers on tap, is also pretty hip, and has a super cheap happy hour that's half off, even their local and international brews! Circus and Skully's are fun local bars/clubs on 5th that often have live music. The Union is a fun outdoor gay bar, and Havana has regular drag competitions (although probably not the crowd for that). Betty's is a nice bar too, good food, with a pinup theme.
    3. The North Market is the best and closest bet for delicious food of all kinds, but it can get crowded. The vietnamese place will give you a bowl of noodles the size of your head. If you want to walk a little further, there's Elevator (fancy bar food), Mac's (scottish bar food), Northstar (au naturale), Pattycake Bakery (vegan), Jeni's Ice cream (eat all of it its delicious homegrown columbus ice cream!) Tasi is good for breakfast. Insomnia cookies delivers cookies late night. If you want to drive a bit there's Schmidt's Sausage Haus just 5 mins south in the German Village, there's a smorgasboard of homemade sausage and saur kraut, all you can eat!

    I'm sure there's more, I just can't think of it. The Short North is fun to shop in too, lots of galleries and boutiques. And food. Just north of the convention center, like most of the things I've mentioned are.

    Anyway! I'll definitely stop by IGE wherever it is, with my offerings of games to run (shock, iawa, other things i haven't planned yet but will be awesome). I'm excited to try Freemarket, and I'm totally gonna try to get into a game of Fiasco and Penny for my Thoughts. And many other things. Funfunfun!
