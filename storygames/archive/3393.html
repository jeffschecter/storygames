\n I'm trying to get some actual play going with my local group. We've done a lousy job of prioritizing getting together and playing. And there are a thousand reasons for that. The quick version that matters right now is that there is clearly some interest in them for some cyberpunk roleplaying of some kind. So, I want to harness that enthusiasm and get something going!

They (they being many of my gaming pals who play as a group more than I'm able to play with them) play a lot of Shadowrun. Which is fine. Whatever. But, they're interested in more "straight" cyberpunk play sans magic.

So! I'm wracking my brain trying to think up a solution that would satisfy. Here's what I'm looking for:

1) Something a little more complex than PTA. That was my initial reaction. It's not likely to gain much traction because there's not enough "stuff" to play with. Ok. Sigh.

2) Something that isn't Shadowrun where we ignore the magic bits. I don't even want to begin digging through that right now.

3) Something that at least gives me some opportunity to do some story gaming with 'em. Maybe it's just some house rulesin' of some kind.

4) Something that won't cost me $50 or more.

Right now, here's what I've got ideas for:

1) Spirit of the Century. Any idea how the hell to get this 'modded' for near-future fun?

2) Ex Machina -- the Tri-Stat thing. Sounds like it has good ideas. The system probably isn't my cup of tea. But, then, I'm picky.

Others ideas? More info on these?

    \n Modding SotC is totally doable. You'll need to mess with the skill list a little, to get high-tech and computer-y stuff in there.

    Cyberwear is probably going to be your main source of stunts for the game, pretty much akin to powers in other genres. You're probably going to have to make them up. A stunt in Fate essentially does the following:

    * Adds a new trapping to a skill, which you can now use the skill for.
    * Adds a bonus, roughly equivalent to two shifts, to a certain application of an existing trapping for one skill.
    * Breaks one game rule regarding the use of a skill.

    If you want to make hierarchies of stunts, apply the effects above more than once: the second stunt in a chain would allow yet another trapping, or apply to two skills, give a four-shift bonus, break two rules, etc. Or, if it fits the "scale" of your campaign, you can just allow this to happen by default.

    So, an example:

    Cyberoptics - The character is no longer penalized by most environmental conditions or darkness when using Alertness or Investigation for vision-based actions, because he can see in several spectrums of energy and zoom in or out as need be.

    Telescopic Vision [requires Cyberoptics] - The character's Alertness and Investigation skills are effective without penalties up to two more zones away than normal humans are capable.

    And so on. If you want some kind of thing where being teched-up makes you less "human", apply a penalty to the refresh rate of -1 or more per stunt. So if the default is 10 and you have five stunts in cyberwear, your refresh is now 5. Now, in order to have as many fate points as your non-teched companions, you need to take five points in compels during a session - in other words, you're more controlled by your baser nature and impulses, as represented by your aspects.

    Check out the Evil Hat wiki (evilhat.wikidot.com) for ideas about how to make conflicts grittier and faster, if that's your bag.

    As for character creation, much depends on how closely you want to stick with the lifepath/phase format. So, let me know what you're thinking about in that regard, and I'll give you targeted feedback.

    You may also want to check out Chad Underkoffler's Truth and Justice, for more on the cyberwear = powers angle.

    \n We currently run our Shadowrun game using FATE. Cybered stuff is represented by an aspect called Cybered Up or Cybernetic Edge. People are assumed to have some basic cybernetics (datajacks and the like) unless they outright declare that they don't have anything. Someone that's Cybered Up might have augmented muscles, a replacement cybernetic arm, or the like. The more times they purchase that aspect, the more metal they are.
