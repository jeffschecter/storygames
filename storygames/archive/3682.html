\n On the down side,

It is very hard to run a Jane Austen relationship drama with SotC, even if you do hit things with a wrench occasionally (thanks Tony for bringing the wrench).

Some players just don't want Mortal Coil, they unfortunately want D&D, and you will spend all session in a push pull over task and conflict resolution. This will suck your energy worse than any lack of sleep.


On the plus side,

PTA is atill one of the greatest games ever created.

Ganakagok is even cooler with space Nitu (so sayeth my wife).

WEGS is strangely compelling to play (it like all my bad high school D&D games condensed into a four hour slot combined with blackjack and bad puns. Like playing D&D in Vegas only you get to keep your money...) And everyone must fear Bill's Lyrd.

What did others learn?

    \n I learned, to my vast surprise, that not only is Beat the Clock a very viable game now that I've made a few mods to it beyond the rules I submitted for the compact RPG challenge (which never quite got off the ground), but it was my runaway big thing of the convention. I ran it in a midnight slot on Friday, only to find the next day that one of my players wanted to run it himself, again, the next night's midnight, giving me a chance not only to playtest it the first time, but playtest the changes suggested by the first playtest the next day. Plus, it got a new name and a small enthusiastic initial fan-base. Pretty exciting.

    In short, this was a shotgun of awesome, and I'm still picking the pellets of awesome-shot out of my brain.

    Schizonauts got a good playtest too, but still has a number of problems with its endgame. The first two thirds of the game are pretty tight; I need to figure out how to make the last third a messy *fiction* but a non-messy *play experience*.

    I suffer from a lack of slots. I still have not played Carry. I still have not played Primitive. I still have not played Capes. These things vex me.

    Manning the booth is good fun. Manning the booth to the point that I miss the 12-2-ish Ethiopian thang on Sunday tinges the happy with a smidge of bitter. Will fix that come Dreamation, comma dammit.

    \n What I learned. I learned that I have no good idea how to make my Artesia game sexier. Both slots I got zero players. D&D got more players in the indie room than I did. Oh, that hurt.

    I have to remember that I love to play and I love Burning Empires. I don't know why but I get in this weird rut where I secretly hope my BE games don't go off. Then I get a table full of players and no matter who comes to that table it rocks the house. No doubt BE veterens might be disappointed in the rudimentary level but the stories they are coming up with are spot on. Four sessions into my BE campaign I can't wait for the next.

    I shall avoid allowing characters with 5s in Ganakagok. In fact I came up with an interesting piece of drift, the idea that each player should have one scene for each attribute.

    No doubt I learned more but I am too focused on my Artesia Story Game, Mouseguard and GenCon to remember it all!

    Thanks for a great weekend, all.
    - Don
