    \n The playbooks should be back up on the web site tonight. Turns out they're too big for the PHP uploader (and I can't get it to pick up my higher upload limit) so I'll have to wait until I'm at home and can deal with it.

    Bard, Paladin and Ranger are all pretty widely played. We cut them from the Dungeon World Basic (the red book) because a) the four classic classes fit better and b) they all had a few remaining fundamental issues we want to toy with. They're much like the XP system at present: completely playable, but not what we really want.

    I don't know if Adam even remembers this email thread, but we actually have a start to a Barbarian. The Druid's also been the topic of discussion, but we got less far along with that one. The Monk's also been talked about, I know Harper had some thoughts there.

    Basically: yeah, we love more playbooks! When the new site is up we'll make sure to add a section linking to other people's playbooks (that they want linked, of course). I'll also look at sharing the InDesign file, both in template form and maybe for one completed class, just so you can see what we do. The DW font (Newcomen) isn't free though.

    \n The last version of D&D that I ever played was AD&D, so I feel like I have a much broader and more chaotic vision of what D&D is than most people who played through all the editions -- AD&D was a crazy grab-bag of different ideas and weird little ad hoc systems -- it worked though, when you took it together with the ongoing Dragon magazine, because you could totally see that the game was just what a whole bunch of gamers had put together, and it was very empowering to know that you could bolt on your own ideas and rules and it wouldn't damage the integrity of the whole, because there wasn't really any integrity.

    This is by way of saying I'd love to see some odd and unique playbooks, too. The City Ranger. The Dungeoneer. The Sworn Brother. The Eyeless. The Dead Wizard. Crazy stuff like that.

    \n Custom sub-classes for flavour, custom additional race moves (for those who absolutely MUST have, say elven paladins), custom sub-classes (Cavaliers, Duellists, etc) are all totally fantastic fuel for the DW fire.

    I was running a long 4e game of Dark Sun while we were writing Dungeon World - and it got me thinking it would be really easy to repurpose for setting, even just by removing a few playbooks, altering the race or alignment moves slightly and adding your own new playbooks.

    It's evidence of the excellently modular nature of Apocalypse World - we made our own game from that and you can make YOUR own game from ours.
