\n Okay, I'm one session into the first game of Ribbon Drive that's spanned multiple sessions. And due to scheduling, we've been unable to get back to the game for two weeks. I'm def jonesin' to finish it right now.

But I can't. So, instead, I am just going to spend my workday thinking about it, and the elements that inspired it.
Maybe you want to join me!

The title track to this road trip was "Sixteen, Maybe Less" by Iron & Wine, off Daniel Wood's (icecreamemperor's) mix CD "My Disappointment".
Beautiful, crushing, melancholy song.

It has lyrics:
Beyond the ridge to the left, you asked me what I want
Between the trees and cicadas singing round the pond

One grin and wink like the neon on the liquor store
We were sixteen, maybe less, maybe a little more
I walked home smiling, I finally had a story to tell

And though an autumn-time lullaby sang our new-born love to sleep
My brother told me, he saw you there
In the woods one Christmas Eve, waiting

I met my wife at a party when I drank too much
My son is married and tells me we don\u2019t talk enough
Call it predictable, yesterday my dream was of you

Between the trees and pond you put your hand in mine
Said, \u201cTime has bridled us both but I remember you, too\u201d

And though an autumn-time lullaby sang our new-born love to sleep
I dreamt I traveled and found you there
In the woods one Christmas Eve, waiting

Here's the activity:
If this song were the inspiration for a road trip - if this were the credit song or the theme song to a road trip movie - what would the trip be like?
What would be the initial situation?
Who's on the trip? Where are they going? What do they think they want?
What's the tone?

Feel free to engage one another's ideas or just shout out your own.

This song is tricky in that it has a strong narrative built-in, with a limited cast. See if you can extrapolate from it a set of 3-5 characters to be on this trip.

    \nIf this song were the inspiration for a road trip - if this were the credit song or the theme song to a road trip movie - what would the trip be like?

    Do you remember the film Sideways? It's like that, for me: older people driving, with the shadows of former relationships haunting them.

    What would be the initial situation?

    It's four days until Christmas and the guy, who's newly divorced and unemployed, decides to get away from Los Angeles. He's going to visit the girl he left twenty years ago.

    Who's on the trip?

    Him, his old schoolfriend who's an unemployed actor and a girl who thinks she's psychic.

    Where are they going?

    Up Highway 1 to San Francisco and into wine country.

    What do they think they want?

    To rediscover their childhood, but they're actually grown-up now, and they'll probably find that's preferable.

    What's the tone?

    Melancholy, but with hope.
