\n So I'm playing in this Aliens-based game that concerns itself with the Colonial Marines. It's a long-running game that I've only just joined, so I've had to settle in by inches, get to know people and so forth. But there's one guy....

His character's name is Dirk Pitt. Dirk Pitt as in the hero of many Clive Cussler novels. They don't have a lot else in common, so far as I can tell (he's bedded no willing lasses), but he's still called Dirk Pitt and I don't care how you slice it, that's just goofy. It's akin to playing a spy game and deciding your character's name is going to be James Bond. It's just not done.

Now I understand that coming up with the right name can sometimes be trial. But some names just have to be off limits because they have too much baggage attached. So no Han Solos or Luke Skywalkers, please. Or some names are just too silly for the subject matter. Like "Phil the Samurai."

There's not anything to be done about this particular player or his character's name. They're involved in the game and that's that. But I wonder what techniques you may have employed to, ah, gently discourage similar choices in your own games.

    \n There's names and there's what people call you. That may be his given name but there's no reason, especially in an aliens based game, that the character can't have a nickname or handle. It could be a holdover from boot camp or something the crew came up with. In fact, you could have each player pick a nickname for each other character based on what they are like, then each player must pick from among the nicknames given them. Goodbye Dirk Pitt, hello Dirk "Sunshine" Pitt.

    \n Naming a character is often the hardest, and often last, part of character creation. Many names I like are quite authentic and in character (e.g. Bransanthilis) but thoroughly unpronounceable. Others capture the feel of the character but are distracting (e.g. Dirk Pitt = Brad Pitt as Dirk Gently). Personally, I think names are important and that duping a name is is usually uncalled for. As [sort of] noted above, a duplicate name should [in my opinion] always been done intentionally to avoid it being distracting - you should give a duped name because you want to confront it. Can you live up to the name? Is the name your bane, given by foolish parents. Is your name apt to those who know you? Is there a story about your naming? Justify the dupelication!
    \n
    \nAlso, how often are you called by your full name? In this case, Dirk is a perfectly good thing to be called.
