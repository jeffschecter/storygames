\n I'm working on a little idea for a game in a school, but I'm having trouble thinking of relationship model that fits what I need. Basically, interactions should have a chance of making friends or making enemies. Factions form. Etc.

What systems do a decent job of modeling relationships dynamically? If you can give an example, that would be keen.

Specifically, I'm not interested in purchase-based relationship systems or side-effect relationship systems. For instance, I can buy a relationship with resource points in Burning Wheel. I can get relationships as fallout in Dogs in the Vineyard, but that's a side effect. (It's not the only way in either of these systems, I'm just using these as examples of a method). I'm more interested in these interactions being important in game, with mechanical effects, as in Bliss Stage or Breaking the Ice.

Thanks,

    \n The Shadow of Yesterday get's kinda close to this...it certainly stares at it...intently.
    The relationship thing is somewhat of a side effect, but a really important one.
    So, in the Shadow of Yesterday you buy these things called Keys, they're a reward mechanic.
    Use them and you get rewarded.
    Also, and more importantly they inform the other players what that character is all about.
    It's like shorthand.
    I've not played Bliss Stage nor Breaking the Ice, so I'll just assume were generally talking about the same thing.
    There's a bunch of games that do the relationship thing, but many of them do it rather implicitly.
