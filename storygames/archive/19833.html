\n So here is the idea for a game I am going to be running soon.

Far Future Fantasy setting
The players are members of a sort of Dungeon Crawling Sports Team that plays crazy games for live entertainment
In the off season they are hired out by various factions within the galactic empire to be a sort of SWAT team for all the old dangerous places of the empire that need cleared out and explored.
There will be team management, intrigue between the sporting games and the reality tv show that follows them, and the things they are sent out after, as well as off camera machinations between the teams.
It will be tactical, and simple enough to support mini-games (each of the Team Games will have a few different rules), and rules for social intrigue that matters on the field (ie You have a good relationship with the local Lord for rescuing his daughter in the off season, you can spend a point to undo an illegal trap the game organizer was going to put in.)

I think this is going to be a full game I will have to write up, as opposed to the hack I had been planning.

Right now I am kinda thinking of starting with something like Smallville, adding in some tactical combat, and coming up with some team management rules (kinda had an idea of re-tooling the Infection mechanic from Burning Empires for that).

...I don't know, thoughts? I am having trouble figuring out where to start, and I have no one to talk to about this sort of thing.

    In the rough outline of the setting as described, what you\u2019re talking about really reminds me of X-Crawl, which was originally released for D&D 3.5 but apparently has scene rerelease as Maximum Xcrawl with an intentional compatibility with Pathfinder.

    \n

    From the website:

    \n
    \n

    In Xcrawl, the players are superstar athletes taking their chances in a

    live-on-pay-per-view death sport. It\u2019s a modern-day world with a

    fantasy twist, and the game is simple: the Dungeon Judge, or DJ,

    creates an artificial dungeon under controlled \u2013 but lethal \u2013

    conditions. He designs the maze, and stocks it with monsters, secret

    doors, magical traps, treasure and prizes. The players must go through

    the dungeon and fulfill whatever conditions the DJ puts forth in order

    to win.*

    \n
    Xcrawl is a sport and the challenges are created, but the danger is no less real.

    If you die, you die. There are no second chances.

    Citizens of the North American Empire tune in every week to watch

    their favorite celebrities get eaten, paralyzed, turned to stone, and

    ripped apart. The nation\u2019s hunger for blood and mayhem grows with

    every contest. How will you fare?*
    \n

    Mechanically, I\u2019m not sure that introducing a new system is going to get you enough traction in the marketplace to attract attention. I could be completely wrong, but building off of someone else\u2019s successes is generally a pretty solid strategy.

    \n

    My own suspicion is that building off of one of the games that was released during the short burst of \u201cspy drama\u201d RPGs \u2013 Hollowpoint, Blowback, or Killshot \u2013 would get you the vast bulk of the way to where you want to be in terms of system positioning. That all of the above have a sort of \u201cmission oriented\u201d design plays into what you\u2019re looking to achieve; even the dungeon crawling events are effectively just missions with specific parameters, which should work fairly neatly.

    Like Contenders, you say \u2013 with more mechanical crunch and stuff going on?

    \n

    Have you seen Joe Prince\u2019s follow-up to Contenders, Eternal Contenders? It takes the Contenders engine, essentially, and moves it to a fantasy setting in which the PCs are assumed to be \u2013 well \u2013 the best way to summarize it is \u201ccharacters in a 2-D side scrolling fighting game.\u201d Not for the actual combat mechanics themselves \u2013 those you\u2019re already familiar with \u2013 but because their backgrounds are all hinged around their personal dreams and aspirations, and ritualized duels or gladiatorial combat are the primary kinds of physical conflicts the characters will get into (though there are mechanics to cover things like going on quests).

    \n

    If you\u2019re already a fan of Contenders, this might be right in the game space you\u2019re looking for.
