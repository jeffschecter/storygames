    \nIt really just depends on what the market will bear. Over in San Francisco's Japantown they tried to have a Japanese-style manga cafe, but it flopped and closed after a few months.     Likewise, for Japanese gamers, playing in someone's house is an order of magnitude more difficult for not only physical but social reasons, and on top of that the hobby scene is small enough that there's not a lot of specialized places to go.     While it makes a certain amount of sense to offer a good play space and charge a modest fee for it, Americans have so many free options that it'd be damn hard to keep such a venture afloat. Listening to Chris Hanrahan (of Endgame) on podcasts, it seems that having play space benefits game stores in the long run by building a community around the store, and keeping regular customers coming back. I personally don't really use my FLGS' play space, but I know for a fact that with the RPGA and CCG stuff, there is a core community that does so very regularly.
