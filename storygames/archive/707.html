    Well ... but what if the story of the publishing is really, really boring?

    I mean, you can go and find the entire story of my developing the Capes ruleset and playtesting it in loving, real-time detail on the boards. There's even an index thread somewhere (I'm pretty sure). That's all pretty much the entirety of what I did with other people.

    After that I wrote it, layed it out, illustrated it, organized it into a PDF and sent it to the printers. Which, yeah, was a lot of work ... but beyond the fact that it's a lot of work, what are you going to learn from a description of that? I write, I draw, I lay out, it's finished.

    I guess the people who have stories of having recruited entire teams (luxury!) have valuable stories to tell. Putting a team together is something that there's probably a right and a wrong way to do. But I don't see that my story stands in the same class. Sorry :-(
