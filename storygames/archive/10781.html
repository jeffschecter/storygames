\n Continuing the grand tradition, I want your best advice for running Houses of the Blooded. I just finished reading the book, and I'm running the first game of a long-term thing on Wednesday. My head is spinning a little. The heart of the game is super-simple and cool, but I'm having some difficulty seeing it at the moment; digesting the book with its myriad layers and sub-systems has made it hard to look at the core. I'm not worried at the moment about all the additional, complex stuff; we'll work up to it. I do definitely need to go back and look at the Risk, Character, and Aspects chapters.

Running it is supposed to be pretty simple, but I always worry that in simple-to-run games I won't prepare enough. What I want most is advice on good situation/story/adventure creation--how much does Houses need, and what are the nuts and bolts? I know John talks about this in the book; if you can build on that or explain it some more, that'd be great!
