    \n I've been enjoying the discussions on the Dresden Files episodes quite a bit because a lot of the problems Jesse seems to have with FATE are similar to problems I've had with the system.

    Some of the talk in the "when does it become an aspect?" discussion surprised me. Allowing characters to do assessments or maneuvers as free actions to get free-taggable aspects isn't how I understand the system. Doesn't that undermine the fate point economy? Why would a player want to spend an action to do a maneuver or spend a fate point to invoke an aspect if you can get the equivalent effect for free? My understanding is that things should crystallize into aspects whenever it's appropriate, but that an aspect only become free-taggable if you use an action and a skill roll to create and/or reveal it, so it doesn't really matter when an aspect is created because it still costs fate points to invoke them unless you use an action to earn the free tag. (Which I think also ends up putting me in Jesse's camp about feeling like that puts an uncomfortably large amount of discretion in the GM's hands in terms of invoking or compelling previously undefined aspects, but I think that the GM's relationship to the fate point economy is pretty problematic in other areas, too).
