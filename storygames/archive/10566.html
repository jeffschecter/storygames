\n OK,
\n So, I had a list of 290 possible people, I narrowed it down to 87 (hooray for me).
\n The problem I have is Quantity vs. Quality and the Faction Mapping question.
\n
\n So it breaks down into 4 possibilities:
\n1) Keep the quantity, do not map each person to a faction.
\n2) Keep the quantity, map each person to an existing faction.
\n3) Cut the quantity and describe each in further detail, do not map each person to a faction.
\n4) Cut the quantity and describe each in further detail, map each person to an existing faction.
\n
\n The point of this list/feature is to provide plot hooks, setting details and inspire the players with cool ideas.
\n
\nWhat would you suggest?

    \n The thing about factions is that you get something by belonging to one. So I usually go for the one that is in line with my own priorities and values. That does say a lot about me. But that doesn't mean I have to stereotype my behavior.

    That is, factions add context.

    Now, what do I do with that behavior? Am I really into that faction's ideologies? Am I into it because it is convenient to me or my projects? Am I only paying it lip service? That is a full level to consider and "play with". There is the faction I belong, and there is the reason I belong to it in the first place. Then there is my relationship with others in my faction and those outside of it.

    Limits tend to favor creativity, not the other way around.

    So yeah. More work. Lots of potential.
