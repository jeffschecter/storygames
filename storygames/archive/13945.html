    \n My crystal ball predicts a renaissance for WoD in some form, possibly something like the OSR, but definitely not the same.

    Lamentations of the Flame Princess will become one of the most important publishers of D&D-related materials that is not 4E or Pathfinder.

    WotC will continue experimenting with various formats, boardgames, cards etc. Essentials will remain the core format and money will be sought through other venues.

    Vincent will release at least one of the following: Storming the Wizard's Tower, Knife & Candle, The Dragon Killer or something completely unexpected.

    Street teams, open tables and pickup games will become more common, trying to bring in new people, revitalizing the community part of the RPG community.

    Vidoegames: Mojang's new game Scrolls will capitulate the AAA-title gaming industry. Both upcoming D&D videogames will suck massive balls and CRPGs will generally continue to be shit.

    I guess that's it for my (overly optimistic) gaming-related predictions.

    \n Guild Wars 2 will have a sizeable impact on MMORPGs in particular, and computer and tabletop RPGs in general. Single player game fans will decide to buy into MMO, and tabletop players who didn't like how artificial it feels now will like the improvements enough to be drawn in.

    Final Fantasy XIV will remain free to play throughout the year.

    Another FATE game will be announced, continuing to broaden the genres it covers, and cementing FATE as the unintended progeny of Ryan Dancey.

    Kickstarter will lose steam, as enough people have been burned by paying for projects and not getting anything out of it.

    At least one of the 2nd Ed retro-clones will be released as a 1.0, it'll be barely distinguishable from OSRIC and will not include kits.

    Daggerfall will be overshadowed by Diablo III, but Torchlight will have already shown more people that gamepads are the way to go for Hack n Slash RPGs.

    Catherine will be mindblowing for anyone who manages to avoid spoilers. Persona 5 will get officially announced.

    Classic game styles that have been lost from PC and Console gaming for decades (Wing Commander & the like, for example), will make a return thanks to webOS having more form factors, iOS and OS X moving closer together, so games that have only made a comeback on mobiles will move back to the PC. Despite that, Escape Velocity will still be the benchmark in the space sim genre.

    \n Last year, I predicted that there would not be a killer app for tabletop gaming on the iPad in 2010, but one would come out in 2011. I recant that prediction. There won't be a killer app for tabletop gaming on the iPad in 2011. You will be able to count the number of new apps released in support of tabletop gaming using only your fingers.

    No mainstream game manufacturer will offer any type of iPad (or similar platform) support.

    Internally, White Wolf will seriously contemplate launching Exalted Third Edition as an electronic-only, mechanics-free, all-setting-fluff line of products, with a license that encourages share-alike systems to be built against it. Eventually, the idea will be rejected when the decision-makers collectively fail an Intelligence + Bureaucracy roll (or, perhaps, suffer some kind of pride-related limit break). (Good luck verifying this one.)

    I will win a prize I don't actually want in the One Page Dungeon 2011 contest. No one will be envious.

    Wizards of the Coast will realize how flipping cool the new take on Gamma World is, and will try to leverage this. They will learn entirely the wrong lessons from it all, though, and their efforts will fall flat. Part of the failure will be due to really bad pricing decisions.

    Out of the previously mentioned WotC debacle will come a variation on their Top Secret line that turns out to be a decent simulator of "cinematic", firearm-based small unit tactics. Mis-marketed as an espionage game, it will get hacked by others to more useful effect.

    Someone will release a game that enjoys modest success, in spite of being published solely in the ePub format.

    The old school notion of numbering sections in the rulebook (e.g. "5.1", "3.2.1.4") will make a comeback for some games, as people figure out this is the only way to make sensible page references within an ePub file.

    The "Pink Mohawks" Fiasco/Shadowrun playset will get heavily revised and turned into a real PDF, even if I have to do it myself.

    At least 70% of the predictions in this thread will be wrong.

    At least 3% of the predictions in this thread will be right.

    I will resist the urge to name my first child using the periodic table of elements. ("Please meet little Boron Phosphorous Ward..."*)

    * She could go by the initials B. P. W., but then everyone would think her last name was "Tungsten".
