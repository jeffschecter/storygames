What you missed on the Foundry

Adam_Dray
May 2006 edited May 2006 in Meetups / Conventions
In case you've been hiding under a rock, Foundry is an online community for us gamer folk. It runs on a TinyMUSH server, which is like IRC but with rooms and objects you can pick up, plus a built-in coding language for those inclined. I and a few others are building tools for rolling dice, tracking cards, managing tables (the kind you sit at), recording character sheets, and even playing specific games like Dogs in the Vineyard, Capes, Polaris, and others.

You can connect to the Foundry using telnet -- connect to legendary.org on port 7777 and create an account there -- but I strongly recommend downloading a free mush client like SimpleMU (Windows), Cantrip (Mac), or TinyFugue (Linux/UNIX).

People log in and idle sometimes and spontaneously reach some kind of critical mass where they start having cool conversations about life or game design. Sometimes they discuss dental dams. Other times, someone suggests playing a game and people head off to a spare room to play Capes or something. Sometimes a designer logs in and grabs a couple people and starts playtesting something, the way Tony playtested The Death March of Happiness a few nights ago.

The only way you can have this kind of fun with us is to log in and hang out, even if it means idling and ignoring the mush client window for an hour while you get dinner or do something else. People eventually show up, huddle together like the gamer refugees they are, and fun happens magically. Sorta.

Anyway, this is an ongoing thread for people to post a brief account of what they did on Foundry so you'll be jealous and wish you were there. Don't post some monster Actual Play report here. There are better places for that on Story Games and the Forge and so on. Start a new thread for that. Just post a teaser and, perhaps, a link. I'll try to update once a day, but we'll see how well that keeps up. I don't always know what happened there, too, so help me out if you had fun on the Foundry.
