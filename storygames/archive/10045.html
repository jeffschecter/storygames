\n Hey everyone,

Here I am considering running a slot or two (at most ) at a convention coming up in the fall. That said, While Dnd 4th edition is my main squeze at home, I do play various games, and I do not want to run Dnd 4th edition in public with strangers. So oddly enough I was thinking of an Indie/Free type game, and I thought I would come here for suggestions.

If you had to suggest 1 Free Game (that may or may not be indie) to Play with Strangers at a Con setting, what would it be.

Suggestions

~Quick Start Rulez (for a priced game), are not what I would consider a Free game.
~Games that do not require me printing a metric shit ton of pages are a definite plus.
~Games that have adultish content are fine, an excuse to shut down people using games as a baby setting service is not the goal but ...
~Games that do not require excessive retraining/rethinking about the games/genres/real life issues are a plus as well. My agenda here is free games not advancing interpersonal narratives, understanding or revisionist experiance.

Thanks ahead of time for your suggestions.

Logos

    Was always really blah about lady blackbird, but surprisingly The Bloody Forks of Ohio are after my first read threw, surprisingly positive in my eyes. I'm impressed (clean layout helps too)

    So Far so good I guess. Its still quite a ways off, and I may try these with my home group while we figure out whats what.

I think I just may hate lady blackbird is that okay? is it a serious obstacle to running the game, because I'm all up with the sky goblin and bodyguards and keys ,and then I look at Lady Blackbird I'm like Ugh.     Anyway thanks for the feedback people , keep it coming, although we can probably pipe down on the Lady Blackbird/Bloody Forks, and Ghost/Echo. They are already strong contenders and I know/have them so if you have some love for something else, hit me with it.
