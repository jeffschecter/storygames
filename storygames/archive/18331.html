\n Milton Keynes RPG Club are holding another Concrete Cow on 14 September 2013.

WHO?
We're Milton Keynes RPG Club. Find out more about us at http://www.mk-rpg.org.uk.

WHEN?
Saturday 14 September 2013. Doors open at 9.00am, first games start at 10.00am. We'll finish at 11.00pm.

WHAT?
Any roleplaying or board game. You want to play it, bring it along and run it. A full list of games on offer is on the Concrete Cow games page and you can see the list of games offered at Concrete Cow 13.

If you want to run a game, let us know and we'll put it on the list.

WHO'LL BE THERE?
Traders are TBC, once we've done the whole diary-checking-and-confirmation thing.

More importantly, there's all the fantastic games gamers who'll be there and all the fantastic games you'll bring along. If you let us know what you're planning to run, we'll put it on the Concrete Cow games page.

(Note that for child protection reasons, under-16s will need to be accompanied by an adult at Concrete Cow.)

WHERE?
Probably the Old Bath House, Wolverton, in the north-west corner of Milton Keynes. However, the venue is under new management and we're still in the process of confirming our bookings. We'll let you know as soon as we know anything.

HOW MUCH?
TBC, depending on venue. The costs will just be to cover the cost of hiring the hall. Under 16s are free, but note that we can't take responsibility for them.

FOOD?
Details TBC, but we're planning to arrange another lunchtime curry buffet.

For more information, visit our Yahoo group, email concrete-cow-owner@yahoogroups.com, or ask here!

    \n Less than two months to Concrete Cow 13\xbd and it's about time I did an update!

    My apologies for the long silence. It was caused by a combination of Real Life(TM) and the venue (the Old Bath House) changing management while I was trying to prevent another double booking. Happily, both of these are now resolved and its all systems go for 14 September!

    We'll still be in the Old Bath House, the same as ever. The church group should have the message that their meeting is cancelled that day, and we should be able to get into the kitchen.

    That also means the entry cost will remain at \xa35.00.

    Leisure Games have confirmed they'll be attending again with lots of shiny books. Mike will be turning up at different times, however. Rather than getting up at the crack of dawn, he'll be appearing mid-morning and staying until the evening games have started. That should give you all more time to peruse his stall and buy everything you can.

    We're after a couple of things from you.

    First is games! We have one eager beaver, Pete, who's already offered a game of Torchbearer (blurb below). But we need more. Please send be your game blurbs, by whatever means, and I'll add them to the Concrete Cow games list.

    The other thing is that we'll be having another charity raffle at Concrete CowThe last one raised \xa3120 for the Alzheimer's Society, so we'll be seeing if we can beat that total in September. If you have any items you'd like to put up as prizes, please let us know.

    (I know someone has already offered a prize for the raffle, but I've managed to lose your email. Could you please contact me again?)

    And without further ado, here's the Torchbearer blurb:
    Torchbearer

    Black Deeds in Blackmoor
    Adventurer is a dirty word. You\u2019re a scoundrel, a villain, a wastrel, a vagabond, a criminal, a sword-for-hire, a cutthroat.
    Torchbearer is a dungeon crawl role-playing game and love letter to Basic D&D. In it, you take on the role of an adventurer seeking his or her fortune. To earn that fortune, you must explore forlorn ruins, brave terrible monsters and retrieve forgotten treasures.

    * Players: 3-4
    * DM: Pete

    \n A quick update, just to keep people reminded about this!

    Games continue to come in. The Concrete Cow games list is now:

    The Marquis of Ferrara
    You play a scheming signore at the Court of Niccolo d'Este, Marchese de Ferrara in the late 15c, who at the start of game has just been assassinated. You will have to negotiate the labyrinthine politics of this Renaissance city in order to ensure that your signore's interests are served. Treat with everyone but trust no-one, because whatever they might profess about their love of the city and their desire to serve it, ultimately everyone is out for themselves.

    The Marquis of Ferrara is a game in progress by Richard Lacey, one of the co-authors of Witch: the Road to Lindisfarne.

    Un-GM: Leo
    Additional Players: 3-5
    Slot: Afternoon

    My Life With Master: Rock God
    Rock God is unhappy; Rock God is confused; Rock God is angry.

    You play a member of the entourage of a faded former rock 'n' roll superstar, who, as his ego has spun out of control, has lost the respect of his friends and former band members, audiences, the music press, basically everyone in fact apart from you, his psychologically damaged but fiercely loyal team.

    Why do you stay with this madman? You don't know, but somehow you do, carrying out his increasingly deranged orders even though in doing so you descend ever deeper into a pit of weariness and self-loathing, until one day, one of you has the courage to take the raging bull by the horns and...

    Ah but let's just see what happens shall we?

    GM: Leo
    Players: 3-5
    Slot: Morning
    Age: 18+

    Quest!
    A GM-less adventure with map making and world building\u2026 create an interesting world as you play.

    A character-driven world drawn straight from the imagination of the players with pirates elves goblins love gods of death the first cool thing that jumps into your mind.

    Amazing stories guaranteed!

    Session the first
    Un-GM: John
    Slot: Morning
    Tags: GM-less, fantasy, narrative, mapping

    Session the second
    Un-GM: Pete
    Slot: Afternoon
    Tags: GM-less, fantasy, narrative, mapping

    Squadron UK
    * British Superhero Role-playing
    * Silver Age Superheroes
    * Contemporary British Setting
    * Brand new but classic-type game system


    Classic Squadron UK
    "I'm not going to tie myself to an exact scenario. I've got loads to choose from depending upon whether I get new players or a mix. Generally people get to make their own Heroes as part of each session because the character generation is so much bloody fun (though I do say so myself)."
    GM: Simon Burley

    Finest Hour
    The birth of Squadron UK in the dark days of WWII.
    GM: Simon Burley

    If you're attending and thinking of offering a game, please let me know and I'll add it to the Concrete Cow games list

    See you all in a month!

    \n Thanks for the update Neil. I really enjoyed my first Cow in March this year, and I'm already looking forward to the next one. For those who haven't yet been, it's a nice, friendly, local con which punches firmly above its weight. It draws a wide variety of gamers, including some big name designers like James Mullen and Scott Dorward, who also act as hosts. Expect lots of cracking games and a warm, welcoming atmos. Not to mention the legendary Indian lunch buffet, a snip at \xa310/person. Namaste, dudes!

    \n Only a week to go and time for another update.

    Things are just about sorted out. Which is good, seeing as there's only a week to go. I've confirmed all the bits necessary that I need to sort out: the hall's sorted after the change in administrators and the church group know not to arrive, Leisure Games are coming (remember, Mike's turning up mid-morning and staying later), and the Eastern Paradise are expecting us for lunch.

    A couple of other updates. I've arranged for Paul Baldowski to send down some All Rolled Up dice+pen+card organiser bag thingies (look at the website for details). I'll be doing my best to sell them to people, purely because I think they're a nifty idea.

    The charity raffle is sort of coming together, but Real Life(TM) managed to get in the way of begging for prizes. If you have some prizes you'd like to donate to raise money for the Alzheimer's Society, please get in touch.

    But most importantly, the list of games is growing apace! We now have 20 games on offer, with a few more awaiting confirmation and blurb. The full list is on the Concrete Cow games, and I'm sure there will be more games offered on the day.

    See you all in a week or so!
