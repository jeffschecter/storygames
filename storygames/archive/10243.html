\n Ryan Macklin and I played a quick game of Happy Birthday, Robot over lunch to test out a two-player solution to the original rules. This is the AP, a bit of commentary will follow.

Happy Birthday, Robot!
Robot is very small, but has big heart.
Robot misses friend Melissa, and she misses him as well.
Robot finds Melissa at the Spaceport and Melissa is crying because her dolly is missing.
Robot knows where dolly is, but cannot reach her without John's help.
Robot asks John for help and John takes Melissa and Robot up into his spaceship.
Robot is very excited, but still needs help to find Melissa's lost dolly.
John, Melissa and Robot all see dolly and she's almost in reach of the spaceship!
John says "Hold on tight!" and pushes the throttle to the max!
The spaceship goes ZOOM! and dolly is recovered, much to Melissa's relief.
With John's help, Robot saves the day.

Total play time was about 26 minutes.

I really enjoyed this, and I do think that the two-player setup works. When Ryan was the storyteller, I had the opportunity to use my words to set up my next sentence, because I was the next player. Ryan and I both felt we had a lot of control over the direction of the story. The whole experience was a lot of fun, and I look forward to my daughter getting home from visiting her aunt so we can play.

    The player who is not the Storyteller may use either "but" or "and" as a free word, but not both. The non-Storyteller player adds both the + and - rolls for their word total.

    If you are playing with two people, why not throw three coins each round instead of dice? Storyteller gets words for heads, assistant gets words for tails. Same rules for re-rolls, but when the turn is over the storyteller keeps up to three coins (fewer if she said fewer words), heads up. Otherwise rules for coins are the same.
