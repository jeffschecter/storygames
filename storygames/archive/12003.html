\n Since our group has been running a Basic Fantasy game lately, we've been running into some of the standard mapping challenges- "okay, you go left and walk 10, 20, 30 feet and there's a door, on the right. Then down the hall is another door, maybe 20 feet away." Our sessions are short, so nobody is going to bother mapping it out at the table, so I was hoping to find some kind of really simple online mapping tool to let us share the map between sessions.

I've looked around and come up short- everything I've seen has been an application for download or is far more complex than we need. I'm picturing something possibly Flash-based that lets you paint corridors and dungeon features in a pretty basic way, and then export that image or otherwise share what you've done.

Hivemind, I summon thee! Does anyone know of such a thing?

    \n Out of curiosity, why is downloading an app a deal-killer? Native apps often have better UI and performance, and you can use them while offline. (I speak as someone who's spent his career writing apps, so I'm biased of course.)

    That being said, every free mapping tool I've seen, even the downloadable apps, has been heeeeediously ugly and clumsy to use. The only really good ones appear to be fairly expensive Windows-only apps (the latter is a deal-killer for me...)
