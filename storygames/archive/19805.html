\n In a recent game, there was heavy in-game usage of posting on Facebook or Whatsapp usage etc. on mobile devices.
That means the PCs (not the players!!) communicated and posted a lot with their mobile phones.
This gave a whole different dynamic to the game. PC/NPCs could be contacted via distance or large groups of NPCs could be mobilized via social media.

I find this interesting and would like to know if you've made any notable experiences with this.

    \n OK, I can give more detailed feedback on what happned during a Maid RPG one-shot:
    - Someone posted a Facebook party, hundereds of people crashed the house
    - Embarassing things happening in the house that were posted online in real-time, some important people were "not amused" and reacted negatively (including mayor of town...).
    - We kept in touch with our master and also with other PCs all the time via Whatsapp etc.
    - Some dangerous traps were activated via mobile devices.
    - The house security system with AI also posted regularly in social media.

    I could also think of cyber-mobbing scenarios, identity theft etc

    A TV series that is using mobile phones etc. quite often is Vampire Diaries. Product placement marketing might be an important reason for this. However, it makes sense in the series because vampires have these super-sensitive ears but can't "hear" text messages.
    Also, it is a very useful plot element: villains calling each other without telling where they are, friends helping each other with research (sending maps/pictures), hostages taken with ransom demands, captured friends asking for help...
