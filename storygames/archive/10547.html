    \n While retro, it isn't a retro-clone per se. It takes the rules from another retro-clone (Labyrinth Lord - the clone of the Moldvay & Cook Basic & Expert D&D rules sets from 1981) and uses them to create something that is very close to Gamma World.

    I haven't actually played it, as I'm currently running a 1e/2e Gamma World campaign (although I'm a fan of 3e over 2e, and a big fan of Tweet's Omega World for sheer awesomeosity)

    And it (Mutant Future) *is* an OGL release.

    \nI have! I have!
    \n
    \nI've run a con one-shot and a one-shot for some friends, including their 11-year old son who was just getting into gaming (his great quote of the night: "Ooh, a damsel in distress! Can I eat her?").
    \n
    \nI am also a huge Gamma World fan and had been looking for a good mutated PA game that could capture the same kind of over-the-top GW gaming from my youth. Mutant Future really fit the bill and on top of it it was an excellent introduction to some of the tenets and ideas of the old school movement. It took me a few sessions, but I finally realized that with this game you don't need anything other than characters and a very basic starting situation to have a totally crazy kick-ass adventure. Just the PCs and their dynamic and rich combos of mutations interacting with whatever the wandering monster charts deliver can make for an amazing game. My first adventure involved a mission to find what was happening to the missing villagers. I had a whole adventure with a mining AI off its meds, but we ended up getting totally sidelined (in a good way) by the monsters that were encountered on the journey to the mine. It was a very good lesson for me in the power of the dynamics at the table between the system, the players and the GM and how that is often all you need.
    \n
    \nWhat I recommend anyone who is interested in Mutant Future do is simply download the PDF and generate a character or two. Your fun starts there.
