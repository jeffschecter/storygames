\n While replying in another Dogs in the Vineyard related thread, I got to thinking about some of the more interesting traits of some of my Dogs' characters and thought it would be fun hear about the most interesting, humorous, or entertaining Traits you've created for your Dogs.

I'll start.

In a recent Dogs game, my Dog, Ethelbert, and a burly blacksmith in training, confronted a herd of possessed cattle with his fellow Dog. At one point, one cow, in a demonic rage, charged Ethelbert. Considering himself strong, he figured he could stand up against this oncoming bovine, and thus attempted to shoulder check it out of the way. Unsurprisingly, the cow prevailed.

Upon rolling fallout after the conflict, I got long term fallout, so I decided to take a trait relating to my experience with the cow. My new 1d4 trait became "Faith Can Move Mountains, But Not Cows".

That trait has become one of my all time favorite Traits from my characters.

Let's hear some of yours...

    \n I still think my favorite is one straight from the book, Example 1: "I'm a Dog of the Lord of Life". No matter what character type I've taken (and I've played about 8 times), that one always ends up on the sheet in some form or other.

    Not just because it's the "Universal Swiss Army Knife" for each conflict, but because it's a good call to how much your character believes in himself. Some of my characters had it at 4d10, others had it at 2d6.

    It really says a lot about the character, I think.

    \n My wife's first Dogs PC had "I hate heathens and atheists" at some decently large value. This was a great trait because as a Dog she could apply it to nearly any conflict she got into. But it required that she declare her opponent to be be some sort of heretic, going against church teaching. So she was encouraged to make for a very black and white view of morality: anyone who disagreed with her was clearly not a proper worshipper of the Lord of Life.

    Her narrow definition of moral teaching wound up with another, more accepting sort of Dog killing her in a duel, I think.
