    \n Rob,

    I think this is an interesting idea. I've seen similar type games done in school to fully engage students in the material. Very few people learn from lectures, but when you recall climbing over a muras to sneak past a legionaries tent with your friends, you'd prolly never forget that.

    Specific to your game, has it been run by teachers yet and if so, how did it go? On reading your game, I see there's some advice for players, but as a teacher running this, I don't think I'd have the tools necessary to do it. The teacher has only this advice plus some diagrams: "Teacher describes everything surrounding the character and moves all the other characters. Asks to roll dice every time he/she thinks is appropriate."

    Unless said teacher is quite familiar with rpgs, then I don't see them understanding how to run this. Just my thoughts and I could be wrong.

    Good luck with this and future efforts of a similar nature, as I think there could never be too many educational games for students.
