\n So I was flipping through Burning Empires last night and skimming the "Names in the Iron Empires" list on pgs 280-281. I randomly noticed two of the name: Jared + Sorensen.

I did something like that in Passages. Two of the iconic characters -- Jeannine and Jadyn -- are named after my wife and (first) daughter respectively. I particularly like Jeannine's entry, which notes that her husband died under "mysterious circumstances".

And, of course, the most famous of all RPG easter eggs: The Egg of Coot from Blackmoor.

What others have you come across? Or, perhaps, slipped into your own designs?

    \n I don't want to talk too much about stuff that's not out yet, but pretty much entire chapters of the Maid RPG Ewen and I are working on are nothing more than huge swaths of Easter Eggs.

    A lot of them point to manga and anime that never made it to the US, so that's problematic (I changed some references).

    But some hats are tipped to (that people might have heard of):
    Death Note (the notebook is a random item)
    Evangelion (plug suit is an optional costume)
    Yu-Gi-Oh
    Jesus Christ
    Fist of the North Star
    Macross (a song which, like Minmei, lets you change people from evil to good)
    Power Rangers (greenmerang)
    Clock Tower (giant scissors)
    Cho Aniki Protein Densetsu (the homoerotic surreal shooting video game: THe brothers from the game are "pets")
    Cthulhu (Nameless Cults)
    Desperado/El Mariachi (gun-filled guitar case)
    Killer Condom (killer condom, from the German movie)
    Takeshi's Castle/MXC (an obstacle course/pond, the one with the styrofoam blocks of increasing size)
    Zelda (hookshot)

    I also penciled in a reference to Krull.

    Basically, if you've read any manga or anime, you'll hit a point where you're like, "Oh, I know where that's from/I've seen that kind of thing before", but it's in a way that's less "trope/convention" and more "easter egg".

    -Andy
