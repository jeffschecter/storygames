\n I have been running the London Indie RPG Meetup Group for a number of months and it has proved reasonably successful. We recently had a Fiascon where a number of people designed Fiasco playsets:

1890 Shadows of the Empire
A Day At The Fair
Egyptian Expedition

There is a groundswell for a further design event and people seem keen to design games. We would have a 9 hour slot in which to do it with people forming teams (or doing things on their own should they wish). I want to get a sense of what people here think would be a good task to set the designers.

Should I just let everyone sit down, organise themselves however, and get on with it?
Should I have a set of themes as Game Chef does?
One idea I had was to design a game that could be played within a time limit, such as 15 minutes, half and hour or an hour. The Meetup Group has been asked somewhat provisionally to run a few tables at the next Dragonmeet in London. Rrather than plunging those unfamiliar with Indie RPGs/story games straight into something like Polaris I thought that having some short games short games might impart some of the philosophies present in these sorts of games to punters more familiar with D&D.

I don't really need organisational tips. I'd rather hear from people who design and have attended events like these. What works? What falls flat?

Thanks
Piers
