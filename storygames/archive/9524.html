    \n I've seen it done, but you could have some REAL problems with using any kind of auto-generated index. I recall the person was on FrameUsers Mailing list and had to do some funky junk with the Index tag, essentially making the index marker's alternate term the term plus the definition (like one would do if one was, say, expanding an acronym within the index).

    Practically speaking, I think term should be defined on first usage, as much as possible with your text flow. Parenthetically, in a sidebar, whatever--I don't want to flip to the back of a book to look up what a term means while reading (particularly if it's any kind of neologism). And then, what you can do, is set your index marker to tag the definition specially:

    Flargle ......... 20, 25, 45, 123-124
    - defined ....... 20
    - increasing .... 45
    - for monsters ..... 123

    HTH;
    David

    \n Hey Rob,

    Thinking about this recently, I'm inclined to say that the proper place for a glossary is at the front of your text, and the index at the end. That's based on my sense of their use cases (people need to know what you're talking about before you talk about it), and the ways in which we failed to get some concepts across in Grey Ranks (our glossary was hidden in the back, with the index). So my two cents, it would be better to keep them separate.

    \n I, too, find a lead-in glossary better. In practical terms, for RPGs, most folks never reference a glossary after they've read the text and played a bit--one can't understand the interrelations of parts (i.e. the procedures) without understanding the parts (i.e. terms). Now mind you, it needn't be a massive text dump before word one: in fact, it might be clever (or merely precious) to have a per-chapter glossary, as part of the intro to a chapter. In fact....

    \n I'm being very careful in the text to almost never introduce a term without defining it. Where I am unable to do this, I'm providing both page references forward, and enough context to get it for now. Sometimes I'll even say something like, "You don't need to know about this yet, but...."\n

    \nI'm endeavoring to provide a definition for every game term, every thing that has a specific meaning different from the one at use in the real world, or which is rare enough in common parlance (yet important enough in my game) to require definition (for example: inciting incident).
