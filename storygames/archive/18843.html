    Well, you mentioned the idea of various life tracks. Maybe it's tied to recovery?
    * When you take downtime, restore physical life.
    * When you live out your code, restore moral health.
    * When you take down the bad guys, restore reputation.
    The second and third lead into the first, but if they just stay down your reputation starts to get worse, and chances are your moral health degrades too as you ignore threats. There's also the reciprocal mechanic, which puts a bit more of the pacing in the GM's hands.
    * When you ignore evildoers and your code, lose moral health.
    * When people were hurt under your watch, lose reputation.
    (and of course, * when you get beaten up, lose physical)
    So you want to go fighting to heal your morality and reputation, and when fights are coming to you, you can't really ignore them.

    This is a blunt instrument to get the players into conflicts. It also feels decently true to the genre. My mind's first jump was The Dark Knight Rises, were Batman was ignoring the evils and it was rending his morality apart (already kinda ripped up after Harvey), he got some back when he started the hunt again, but when he was broken he had to take downtime, and in the meantime bad stuff was happening and his general reputation shattered. But it IS a blunt tool, and is a little more about stomping on heroes neglecting their duties rather than rewarding those who actively pursue them.
