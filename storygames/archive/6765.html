The Bleak Die

\n
\n
\n
\n\nGraham \n\n\n
\n
\n\nJune 2008 \n edited June 2008 in Game Design Help
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n Steve and Simon complained that, although they liked my game Bleakworlds, the mechanics could be bleaker. So I've added a new Bleak Die, just to make the whole thing really bleak.

This is how it works. I'd welcome feedback.

1. There is a Bleak Die. It is three inches across and made of steel.
2. There is nothing on the sides of the Bleak Die, because it is bleak.
3. You must keep it in the freezer for an hour before the game.
4. When the game starts, put it in the middle of the table. It sits there, emanating cold.
5. When you do something bleak, add the Bleak Die to your roll.
6. If the Bleak Die rolls a +, you automatically succeed! If it rolls a -, you automatically fail! If there is nothing on the side you roll, you never find out whether you succeed or fail, because the game is that bleak.

What do you think?

Graham
