    \n Just remembered a cool thing you can do after a convention. Anyone who is still high on the nostalgia, I invite you to join in:

    This week, I was a recently-captured slave who served as a seismology research assistant for a steampunk british empire. I was a badass warrior with a bizarre magnetized & blood-seeking bow, capable of hitting its targets even in the pitch dark of night. I was a demon in a fancy black-on-black suit, casually packing his pipe while explaining that Shuang's parents had to die. I was a teenage boy trapped in a confused sexual relationship with a frankenstein's monster. I was my most honest and sincere self. I was the voice of trust and progress in a community being torn apart by the proposition of nuclear invention. I was a teenage boy, tentatively investigating a house and its attendant mysteries. I was a half-sibling who went from feeling a crush to feeling crushing pity. I was the voice of healers and teachers in a post-civ community. When that failed me, I was the voice of the sin-witching blood-courts of that same community. I was Ricky Tamwell, a 36 year-old nice guy tragically learning about the politics of gentrification, as a result of opening my trendy new cafe in a low-income neighborhood. I was the eventual voice of Gerry, the eventual ears of Alex, and the eventual voice of Katrina.

    I was a lot of different things this weekend. And I thank you all for giving me those opportunities. So great.
