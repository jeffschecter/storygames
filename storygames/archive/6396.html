    Posted By: GeekGirlsRuleSee, now that's part of the problem. The excuse of "Well, it doesn't matter because it's not reality." That irritates the hell out of me. It's really just an excuse for shoddy art, and it alienates women.
    \nUnderstand that the body-image issues bother me as well. They bother me on both the male and female side as recent studies have shown that young men are beginning to shift culturally towards over-identification of fantasy images; the perfect body, the perfect weight... these are issues which are impacting our entire society. As the father of a little girl who will always have a facial scar, body-image, self-image, and our cultures obsession with "perfection" are things that are *really* important to me.

    I don't think the discussion of what could or couldn't be done in, say, gymnastics with large breasts has any bearing on the body-image issues, though. And, really, I never (and would never) said that "it doesn't matter." I said it's always surreal to me. Like the episode of Fraggle Rock where Gobo tries to tell Wembly that ghosts aren't real. It's part of the point of the episode that you shouldn't be afraid of ghosts because they're not real... but it was *Fraggles* making the point. It's surreal to me. I think the discussion is interesting, and there's no doubt that it's true (you really never see larger than a C cup on any female athlete). I also don't doubt that the comic and fantasy artists are *very* aware of it. They just don't care. If they cared about reality, women would have smaller breasts, Batman's cape would be gone, and Super Man wouldn't be able to fly.

    Not to put too fine of a point on it, but I want to be perfectly clear: I don't think the discussion of how realistic it is is relevant, but that does not mean I think the popular portrayal of boobs in comics and fantasy art is okay. It's not, IMO. It's a low-brow tactic to sell to our base instincts and helps promote negative self-image and objectification.\n
