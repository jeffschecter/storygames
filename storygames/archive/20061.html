\n I'm having a hard time in D&D with situations that are similar to the volley move in DW so I'd like to take a look at it:\n
When you take aim and shoot at an enemy at range, roll+Dex. \u2734On a 10+, you have a clear shot\u2014deal your damage. \u2734On a 7\u20139, choose one (whichever you choose you deal your damage):

You have to move to get the shot placing you in danger as described by the GM
You have to take what you can get: -1d6 damage
You have to take several shots, reducing your ammo by one
\nIt's a bit of a tame / safe situation, often boring. With 5e, we're keeping track of every arrow, it hasn't been a problem yet.
How can I kick it up? We've had a couple of times where a few people are having a wonderful m\xeal\xe9e and one player character is standing a bit further away, just shooting, missing (which I narrate as "great shot, but didn't accomplish what you wanted" somehow), wasting ammo, being bored.

    ...it seems weird having the DM pick that.
    \nDo it DW style: don't actually narrate the character having to run to a new bit of cover, but rather narrate the enemies/environment applying pressure that will elicit that sort of behavior, and then ask what the character does.

    Ex: "As the arrows whistle over their heads, three of the goblins snarl and turn toward you. They spread out and sprint from cover to cover, clearly trying to surround you. What do you do?"

    \n If it seems weird, you can always ask the players how it works. You definitely shouldn't go soft on that. (I don't think it's DW style to "tempt" the character into doing it--that stretches the action out, because now you're potentially turning the response to a move into something that may or may not actually happen, and you should never give the character the ability to dodge a rules-specified consequence like that.)
