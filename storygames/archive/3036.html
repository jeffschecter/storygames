\n So here's a thought, and not one I'm sure I agree with.

Jargon, and the concepts and theory behind it, perform a gatekeeping function for a community's game design discussions. A potential participant's understanding of the theory and facility with the accompanying jargon signifies her willingness to apply herself to learn the basis of discussion. It shows that she is really interested and committed to spending time and effort to understand. It also serves as a throttle for the number of participants in the discussion: it winnows out the folks with only casual interest and leaves only those who want it enough to work through it. And let's face it: if you can come to a functional understanding of something as convoluted as the Big Model and all its accompanying terms, you can probably come to a functional understanding of what other people are talking about in this or that thread.

Now that said, I think it's important to point out that an understanding of Forge jargon is almost certainly not supposed to be the gatekeeper for participation here at Story Games. Further, I don't think it has been -- although I may be mistaken and some folks have felt shooed off of SG because they don't speak Forge. That would be a shame. I have, however, been rather confused why there's been all this jargon talk here on SG, which has never, to my mind, been about game theory or throwing jargon around. It's like SG is talking about the Forge's standards as if they were the standards of SG, which seems... off.

So: have people felt that they needed to be Forge-fluent to participate here? Can anybody find some threads where this was the case (preferably not the threads about jargon)? Or was this last week just a well-executed troll that got everybody in a tizzy?

    \n Jargon is appropriate when, and only when, explaining a point in simpler terms would take longer, and would be less clear (often due to length) than explaining without.

    For example, explaining a point about how an engine handles "Torque" is deserving of that jargon, because spending the time actually explaining the nature of torque would simply confuse the point. Torque is a very clear concept.

    To my eye, when you assert that use of the current jargon is appropriate, you're also saying that the jargon is clearly-defined enough, on the whole, that it's use increases clarity and decreases length for those that have done a reasonable amount of research.

    I submit that on the whole, that isn't true. It is true of certain terms (kickers, bangs, and relationship maps come to mind), but only certain terms.

    Which means, to me, that it is about tolerance of neologisms.

Theory, Design and "Process" (? Do you mean, "How to sell your game?"). SG's point was always about fluffy, as well as substantive, chat about "Awesome". Awesome here being general game chat, focused discussion on tips and tricks to make games in general or specific games more fun.

    I even allow room for discussion of game design, most recently adding a category for it. But again, this place was never meant to focus on it. It was mainly a place where people who post here, who also happened to be working on a game, might go to get some directed advice.     I love the works that came out of the Forge. I love the relationships I made from interacting with people there. The idea for this forum comes from the Forge Birthday Forum. But this was never meant to be the place to Discuss Theory.

    I've made it clear before that the only "theory" that works here is theory that applies to running or playing in games. Practical stuff to make gaming more fun. GNS and other Forge theory (which I don't want to distance myself from, because I use some of it) is primarily meant for the game designer, not the player. It's stuff to be used in conjunction with critical thought about game design. In other words, it really has no place here.

    But at the end of the day, what I want to make very clear is that this is a place to discuss game playing first and foremost. Not game design. Theory and jargon used to discuss game playing is... acceptable. I've had to kill discussions here in the past because people were talking about high concept theory that they really couldn't explain in terms of actual play, "dice hit the table" examples. This isn't a place to come and:
    * Talk about game design
    * Promote your product
    ...UNLESS you've come here to talk about Other Stuff, too.
