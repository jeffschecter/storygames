\n No crude remarks, please...

I need to figure out a few "minigames" that a single person could challenge themselves to. I have challenges that work with two people:

Given a color, the two competitors take turns naming foods that correspond with that color. The first player to hesitate or fail to issue a reply loses.

The competitors each build a paper airplane out of a single piece of letter- or A4-sized paper and fly it. Furthest flight wins.

The competitors each guess the date on a coin after examining its tails side. The closest to the actual date wins.

But I need simple little fun games of skill or speed or knowledge or luck that a single person could engage in and clearly win or lose in less than 60 seconds. They must be easily managed (you have to know when your time is up or activity has ended) and do-able in a casual office environment by a wide variety of people (so no push-ups, etc). No special equipment, no dice, a pack of cards is allowed.

As an example, I could provide six letters, and the challenger would have 60 seconds to make words, Boggle style. Then you get rated pass/fail based on your productivity. But self-timing is sort of difficult. Any ideas? Thanks! Cool ideas will be immortalized forever in an upcoming thing.

    \n Shuffle and cut a deck of playing cards. Flip cards over one at a time, and silently keep a count of how many of each suit. Check when you're finished.

    Balance a stack of coins on your elbow held out in front of you, with your palm face up by your ear. Drop your elbow and catch the coins in the hand of the same arm.

    Have someone place a number of coins on the top of your head, and guess how many (bald people have an advantage here).

    Coin rugby (maybe you call this coin football?): Place a coin with its edge protruding over one side of a desk. With no more than three flicks of a finger, get it so it's protruding over the edge of the other side of the desk. For extra points, flip it up off the desk with your fingers and catch it in the same hand.

    "What am I thinking of?": One player thinks of something, and the other player has one chance to guess what it is. This game is pretty hard. We play it a lot, and we've had four successes, ever. The fun of the game is in acting disappointed and surprised when they don't guess right. For an easier game, one player gives a category, another thinks of something in the category, and then a third person must guess. "Round things" or "things in this room" are good, but the game gets really fun when the category is "things you don't like about me" or "people in the room you think are hot".

    \n It turns out I only needed three (I anticipated needing more). Here's what I went with:

    1. Reflexes

    Place your non-dominant arm in a bent position as if you were holding a serving tray beside your head. Set two coins on your elbow. Drop your elbow and catch the coins before they fall using the same arm they were resting on.

    2. Knowledge

    List countries and overseas territories or departments in South America. (You are then referred to a canonical list and you succeed if you reached a certain threshold)

    3. Precision

    Lay a series of playing cards on the edge of a table, protruding about a quarter of their length into open space. Using one hand, pick up as many cards as you can without any two cards touching. Three four cards wins. Any contact or dropped cards fails.
