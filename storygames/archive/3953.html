    \n Well, I'm Zachary, I like a bit of everything as far gaming goes, and I'll be starting up a blog soon to keep the entire judging process as open and visible as possible (thanks to the inspiration of Jeramy Ware, in large part). I don't give a good goddamn about author, fad, legacy, label, or company, just if a game is high quality, inspires fun and great gaming, and "brings the awesome". No free passes.

    That's about it from me, but I promise I'll do my best by the hobby to bring you guys the best candidates from the ENTIRE hobby in '08.

    \n No, I would relay stuff to him as directly and neutrally as I could manage. You have him to thank for about 80% of the snark.

    I've seen a few GC reports here and there--lord knows there's enough people who show up as press, you'd think there'd be a fair bit of it. The podcasters seem active here, but other press I've seen seem more interesting in prostituting themselves/begging to the point of embarrassment for free review copies of everything. Uncool, especially with small press guys, most of whom are very happy to give you a full demo of their game and talk to you for 8 hours about it, but really can't swing review copies for every blogger with a press badge. After a while, it becomes whiny and poor manners. Then you have guys like Matt McElroy (not the same Matt who helped me with the 4e liveblog, btw) from Flames Rising, who is a workhorse, does about 400 interviews a day somehow, and seemed to be everywhere right before I was. There are some really good guys out on the floor like that, too, but I have no time for the product beggars.
