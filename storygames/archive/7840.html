    \n Ah, this didn't work out as planned.

    What I intended with this thread was to have a friendly joke at Jason's expense - "hey everyone, he doesn't know about the VERY IMPORTANT SECRET RULES, wink wink wink" - which I hoped people would get after a few moments of confusion ("what on earth are the Fiveling Guidelines? why doesn't anything come up in a search??? are there no Fiveling Guidelines???? oh, wait - he's just pulling everyone's leg!")

    I was sort of envisioning that other people might join in and add meaningless absurdity - "oh, you mean the October Phenomenon? After the Polka-Dot Cat Incident?" - but it didn't work out that way.

    Sometimes, trying to be funny on the internet crashes, and this was one of those times.

    Apologies to everyone who read this thread and thought I was being serious, and especially to Jason, of who I have nothing but the highest thoughts. It was never my intention to give him a hard time; quite the opposite, it was meant as a silly sort of welcome.

    \n Every minute of every day I have the same reoccurring nightmare.

    As mayor, I give the golden key to the town to Jason and embark on a long personal journey, secure that everything will be all right.

    When I return to the forums years later, I find that the place has become a barren wasteland, with the surviving humans wearing pink mohawks and assless leather chaps, driviig around on motorcycles looking for the last of the good posts.

    Over on the highway, JDCorley speeds across the desert driving a tanker full of play advice, but Levi (sporting a revolver, a metal facemask and 120 extra pounds of muscle) and his gang are chasing after it on jury-rigged motorcycles and el caminos.

    In the ashes of the fallen cities, Matthijs and his tribe capture nomadic survivors of the Theory Wars, and cook and eat them in a giant stew pot. At night, in the shadows, they recount the days when man walked the earth without fear.

    On the outskirts of the forum, Emily Care Boss and GBSteve gather the remaining survivors and nurture them in peaceful enclaves. While they are more than capable of defending themselves from threadjacks and trolls through a disciplined civilian guard force, differences in leadership between the two are resulting in fissures in the community. An usurper, Eric Boyd, stirs trouble from the shadows. Civilians will have small disagreements, upon which a crowd forms around them whopping, stomping, and screaming, "Conflict!", "Drive to Conflict!" and the like. Both fear that collapse may be inevitable unless their differences can be resolved.

    Horrified of what has become. I confront Jason in his iron citadel of publishing advice overlooking the ruins of the forum. We have a heart to heart about how things came to be, then we have a kung fu fight. Jason explodes, and I shed a single tear over what once was. Then I turn away from the forums and journey forth into the desert, bringing civilization and peace to the darkest corners of Usenet.

    Sometimes, my dreams are awesome.

    -Andy
