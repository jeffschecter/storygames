    \n True. Also, in Maid, we did a few things to help out sales due to piracy:
    * Kept the price low ($8). I even feel that that price might be too high.
    * Post a highlighted notice in the game itself, indicating that if they are looking at a pirated copy, that they should give it a play: If they liked it, they should consider throwing some money our way. Including a description of how the money helps us bring over more Japanese/Anime-themed games in the future.

    Because of that, I'd say that we got easily 1/5 of our sales by people who found a pirated copy of the game, then bought a regular copy after reading it or trying it.

    It's gotten to the point that when we see the PDF posted here or there, we see people chime in to the comments with a "Hey, check out their announcement regarding piracy, it's pretty laid back...", a link to IPR (or the original Maid site, etc)... And they're not people that Ewen and I know. The extended community is fueling those efforts, which is cool to see.

    Still, it was really cool to have people email us (or posting to places where the game PDF was posted) telling us they bought a copy after reading and playing it (then looking up IPR stats to see, in fact, that they did actually buy a copy).

    And in the end, yeah it's probably better than obscurity. Most people DL stuff just because it's there, without even cracking the file open to even read a page of it. If you're lucky, people will crack it open and read it. Or even play it. And from there, if you're *really* really lucky, people will throw you a fiver if they liked it.

    -Andy

    Posted By: AndyKept the price low ($8). I even feel that that price might be too high.
    \n(edited to clarify I didn't steal BHB)

    Piracy aside, it is the reason I snagged it. If a PDF is $10 or less, it is more attractive to me, sight unseen (I will go higher if I know the game is a worthwhile purchase). I saw the price, saw the cover art, and knew at least one or two pals who would like to play it at some point and snagged it. Was glad I did.

    Not to pick bones, but I agonized over buying "Bounty Head Bebop" because of its much higher price-tag. For what the PDF cost me, I could have gotten a printed version of a paperback game with many more pages. Once I did break down and purchase it, I was much more willing to spend money on the add on for cyber-stuff.

    I know you have to cover your costs and make a profit, but without printing and distribution costs (or lower ones from shipping distribution), you can slice the margin closer and still do well. I wonder what pricing elements went into a pricetag so high. Some of it, I am sure is that it is a niche game and you expect to sell fewer units, but I would love to see how PDF games get priced. What is a good sales unit volume for an indie game?

    I know that was disjointed, but it was something that was on my mind recently when I made that purchase - almost a month after I knew of the game and figured I would enjoy it (I do, by the way).
