\n This thread is largely for those who know about Perfect already.

In previous iterations of Perfect (every last one), you had four Aspects. Stuff like "the scent of my mother's perfume" and "a concealed coil of wire" and "passionately stubborn". You invoked those for bonuses.

Recently, instead of attaching bonuses directly to all four (which then required you to shoehorn your aspects in all the time for maximum bonuses) I had you create four Aspects, and seperately choose two Gains (mechanical bonuses)... During a conflict/test you would invoke an Aspect to tag a Gain... thus only ever invoking two Aspects in a given conflict, because there were only two Gains to tag.

I liked that...

In working on the most recent version of the character sheet, I have broken down some of the rigidity of class / profession / background, and used open-ended questions (kinda like Don't Rest Your Head): What is your role in society? What makes you commit crimes? What do you hope to achieve?

It occurred to me that you could just remove Aspects all together... and have people invoke these questionnaire answers to tag their Gains.

So... people who have played Perfect, what do Aspects provide that these answers couldn't?
Should I keep Aspects?
Should I try dropping them from the newest version?

The answer of: I don't know, but there's no harm in trying it, is there? is not a particularly useful answer, because there is a harm in trying it - I could invest a lot of time and energy in seeing if this new model works, and if it doesn't I will have to return to the drawing board / old model.

So, my question: is this idea good? why or why not?

    This strikes me as a parallel to the evolution of Aspects in FATE. Originally they were check-off-once affairs, then they went to check-off, then pay a fate point to uncheck. Finally the FATE guys realized they were just channeling fate points through Aspects and that's where they are at now.

    \n

    I like what Brad has been doing with this with Diaspora and a new super-secret project. Namely, you can only use one Aspect from one scope: personal, opponent's aspects, environmental, and global to the campaign. I may be missing one but you get the idea. With the new project, in chargen you can choose to either buy another Aspect, or a new scope. So you might in one phase buy "Must get revenge on Dr. Schloss," and the additional scope "My lab". Then in future phases you can buy Aspects to do with "My lab".

    \n

    I know your Aspects are of course somewhat of a different deal, but it seems to me there may be another axis lurking in there, that you can tap more explicitly. I think it's good to have Aspects, and a source of "fuel" for them so that they are not, as you say, shoehorned into every possible available interaction. I think the Aspect + Gain is going to add more interest & flavour than just dropping aspects.

    \n

    Does that make sense?
