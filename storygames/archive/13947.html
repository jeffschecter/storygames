AP
In the three hours of play we did we built Grimwards, a Prussian 19th century academy for officer sorcerers destined for the Kaiser\u2019s army, and we followed five boys during their first two years at the school.

We saw Eberhardt bullying the other cadets, Maximilian revealed his affinity for healing magic when a friend was in dire need and Sigmund\u2019s battle of wills with the one eyed teacher of magical history Herr Braun touched our hearts. Eberhardt, Sigmund and Voldermars were involved in a constant struggle for the top position in the pecking order in the Himmelsfeuer dormitory. And we learned of Franz-Josef\u2019s internal strife when he was confronted with his memories of that fatal and fiery first display of magical talent.

    Well, it all started as a Harry Potter RPG, But after a while I realized that it was not the specific setting of JKR that I wanted for my game. But the general idea of youths at boarding schools threatened by some kind of dark menace.

    Harry Potter, Before the Fall, Tom Brown's Schooldays and Ender's Game are all examples of stories that can be told through this game. Not to mention the plethora of school themed anime/manga that can be emulated, e.g Battle Athletes.

    While Panty Explosion* is sort of similar at first glance, The Cadet Game aims to let the players play through the entire 3 (4, 7 or whatever) years of schooling in one game session. Allowing the players to see the protagonists grow up from children to young adults within the scope of the game. It's a very cool experience, trust me.



    * I played PE for the first time last weekend, it was awesome!

    \n In that case I hereby declare you, DeBracy, as judge and jury in the Name the game, and get your own name into the game - competition.

    Rules

    The contestants are invited to suggest better names for the game, formerly known as 'The Cadet Game'. Submit your entry, or entries, a maximum of three entries per contestant, either as replies here or as replies to this blog post if you prefer.

    Start your response(s) with the phrase "I think the game formerly known as The Cadet Game should be called:" Then follow with your suggestion.

    I get the rights to the winning name for use in the game.

    Deadline for submissions is midnight on March 31 2011 Swedish time. The winner(s) will be announced here in this thread April 30 2011, at the latest.

    The rules of the contest are subject to change without warning. The judge and jury's decisions can not be contested.

    Prizes
    The best five (5) entries, as decided by judge and jury DeBracy. Win a PDF of the final game, emailed to them once it is done.

    From of these five entries DeBracy and I will select the winner who will get to see his or her name used in the game as the name of an example player, PC or NPC of his or her choice, together with some minor control of the personality/role of that person in the game (I'll discuss it with the winner, I'm sure we'll work out something cool.)
