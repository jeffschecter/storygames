\n Hey, look, I have an idea about how to do Star Wars using Spirit of the Century! Aren't I special?

Actually, though, this is less about SW and more about making a quasi-traditional-ish character advancement system for SotC, at least through the first four sessions of play. I had this idea awhile ago, so let's see if I remember it all.

You start out with five Skills at +1, one Stunt, and two Aspects. (The exact number of starting Fate Points is negotiable; I'd suggest three or four instead of two, just to make sure people have a few to play with; maybe even more given how limited starting characters will be compared to normal SotC heroes.) At the end of the session, you advance four out of the five Skills to +2, take four new Skills at +1, take another Stunt, and two more Aspects, the latter based on what happened during the session. (I actually think it would be wise to define one Aspect mid-way through the session and the other at the end, so that you wind up with sort of an in media res Aspect and a reflecting-back-on-what-happened Aspect.)

At the end of session two, repeat the process, only you advance nine Skills: three from +2 to +3, three from +1 to +2, and three from +0 to +1 (to avoid gaps in the pyramid).

At the end of session three, you advance eight more Skills: two from +3 to +4, two from +2 to +3, two from +1 to +2, and two from +0 to +1.

At the end of session four, you advance five Skills: one from +4 to +5, one from +3 to +4, one from +2 to +3, one from +1 to +2, and one from +0 to +1.

I like the idea that new Skills you add during play should be ones you either see other people using, or attempt to use untrained at least once, though this probably isn't strictly necessary.

It should also be noted that whatever you wind up with at the top of your pyramid will be one of the five Skills you started with, so your "dessssssstiny" is kind of apparent from the beginning (also, your starting Stunt should obviously be based on one of those five starting Skills).

I feel this system would allow for a nice "farmboy becomes badass hero" character arc across the five session campaign, while not preventing further play with the more standard "no advancement" SotC rules.

    \n Hi Matt!

    I think that would work but that it would be very fast, very far advancement for FATE, even for the genre you want to emulate. There is a much bigger difference in FUDGE/FATE between someone who is Good (3) at something and someone who is Great (4) at something than a differential of 1 in most systems would indicate. I think you'll find yourself tempted to begin with really low opposition in the first couple sessions and inflate it for the last two sessions, rather than keep it somewhat level such that you'll risk lose that feeling of increasing in potency; the space station that was full of computer defenses that were Fair difficulty to hack into will suddenly have Great defenses to maintain challenge and interest.

    I also think that 2 Aspects is a little spare to define a character.

    SotC has a secret formula. A SotC pulp hero has 40 "Skill" Points which have been used to buy 35 points worth of Skills in a 5 tier pyramid and 5 Stunts at a cost of 1 each. They have 10 Aspects because 40/4 is 10 and a SotC character gets an Aspect for every 4 Skill Points in its makeup.

    Here's how I would do it.

    Session 1:
    20 points = 5 Aspects, 5 Skills at Average, 4 at Fair, 2 at Good(19), and 1 Stunt.

    After each session, give them 5 Skill Points of advancement, one of which must be used to buy a Stunt. Their only prohibition is that they must maintain the pryamid shape/rules. If they can't, they can spend the points next time (haven't worked this out exactly).

    25 points = 6 Aspects, 2 Stunts, Skills as desired within pyramid shape.
    30 points = 7 Aspects, 3 Stunts, Skills as desired within pyramid shape.
    35 points = 8 Aspects, 4 Stunts, Skills as desired within pyramid shape.
    40 points = 10 Aspects, 5 Stunts, and a full SotC-style pyramid.

    Others might be able to provide links that explain this formula better, and it is just one way of determining advancement in FATE. My main point is that your current mode is really steep. As proposed, your Jedi Master at the end of 4 sessions isn't 5 times better than your farm boy - he's like 20 times better, perhaps.

    There are some Star Wars suggestions on the FATE yahoo group you might find helpful.

    Cheers!

    \n Well... don't compel for Dark Side. A player should want to accept the compel: it's a complication that makes the story better.

    On the contrary, give the players the option to invoke an aspect in a "dark manner" and get a +3. Or an automatic success, even.
    I think it's important that the temptation is there, and is powerful, but that the action is initiated by the player.

    When they do, remove 1 from their refresh. If they manage to act piously later, maybe they can regain refresh, and maybe not
    When the refresh goes to zero, they pass to the dark side, and become PNGs (very Dresden inspired, actually).

    Or, if you want to allow dark side characters, transfer points from the normal refresh to a "dark side refresh": the highest measures which side a character is in, and is the only one in effect (a character with a refresh of "light" 4, "dark" 6 is a dark side character with a refresh of 6).
