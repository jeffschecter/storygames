    Posted By: GrahamEither answer yes, no or take me through your indecision in excruciating detail.
    \nSo, I'm going to Origins this year. The reasons are many:

    * Playing/Attending: About a year ago, I realized that I was looking for something new. So I was thinking of simply not going to GenCon for a few years, then later go again. Actually, that's still kind of the plan. However, whereas this was going to be the last year I was going to go to GC, other factors accelerated me making it this year to do something new instead.

    * Attending: I was paying about $80-90 a night for the hotel at GenCon. At Origins, I've got a rental house for a week, for $200 total.

    * Playing: I've heard thrilling tales of how Origins is so laid back compared to GenCon. Just more mellow. This appeals to me in a primal way, GC has been too glitzy recently.

    * Selling: The friends I helped sell stuff with last year are primarily hitting up Origins this year, after running the numbers.

    * Selling: Gencon keeps increasing their booth prices in an effort to drive out all the low-cost RPG publishers, and replace them with boffer weapons, rennaisance clothing, used CCG traders, and funny T-shirts. Origins is still less expensive at this point, and from what folks are saying, sales are about equal between Origins and GenCon.

    * General: I've gotten kinda sick of Indianapolis, actually. Someplace new == nice. Heck, it's Columbus, but still.

    * Attending: It's likely that I can drive to Origins. It's about 8 hours from my house: Driving with friends, that means Fun Road Trip. Albeit driving with Matthew Gandy, it means Anime Soundtracks. :-)

    * Burned out: Just kinda burned out on GenCon. Too many in too few years, probably. And too many friends flying in all directions, it's hard to decide what to do. Having a more limited crowd at Origins means that there are limitations to play with: Make more of a time with fewer people, rather than trying to string atoms on a chain with too many people.

    -Andy
