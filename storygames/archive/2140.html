Posted By: stack0v3rflowThe setting is presented as a series of loresheets that can be purchased and unlocked using some kind of secondary currency. They are plain prose for the most part with certain wordsbolded. Once a loresheet has been purchased, those words inboldcan then be used as character aspects.
Stack totally stole this idea from Weapons of the Gods but dammit, it's a good idea and it deserves to be preserved.

I want to make it more participatory, though, and draw it out of the context in which it was originally proposed.

What if, between sessions, one could buy the right to write a new loresheet?

All the information must fit on an 8 1/2 x 11 piece of paper, and the player has to bring copies that can be given to people when their PC's learn about the new lore.

    Like, let's say it was an Amber game. You could have a loresheet titled "Keep of the Four Winds" with a few paragraphs description of the place, followed by benefits that a character gets for knowing about the place, then for studying it, and then for attuning to it. If you're playing with a points-based game, you'd have the points cost, too.
