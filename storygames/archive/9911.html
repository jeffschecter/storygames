\n By now we all know the joke about drowning and falling rules. Jason made a lovely game about it, in fact (someone throw down a link!). Why does your game about neophyte gods have rules for falling damage? Plush toys can't drown, so why are there rules in your game for holding your breath? Etc.

Somewhere there is a game concept that deserves and requires a detailed mechanical system for anything you can think of. And we're going to prove it now.

So, here's the rules:

When you post, you have to include two things. The first is an answer to a challenge someone has already put forth. Answers take the form of an RPG concept which really and truly deserves a substantial amount of rules focus on the idea stated by the challenge. Let's keep answers short, let's say, no longer than Twitter length. The second, is, of course, a new challenge. State an idea for other people to build game concepts around.

You can do more than one answer in a post, but no more than one challenge per answer. You can also answer challenges that have already been answered by someone else.

Alright, let's try this. I'll start us off.

    \n Replying to: Quality of Light in the Room

    Everyone knows the monsters come out when it gets dark. If you want to stay safe, you'd better stay in the light. Monsters can't get you then. Light burns them like acid. But watch out! The smaller monsters don't have so much skin surface area to burn. They can bear small amounts of light for a while, long enough to tear out your eyes my dear. The more light you have, the less dangerous the monsters become and eventually you might just get some sleep.

    Look at all the other people in the room. They have light. And they're going to have to sleep soon too. Oh yes, maybe you could share, but what happens when the circle of firelight is too small to contain the people sheltering around it? What happens when the fighting starts?

    Take this candle and don't let go of it for anything. You'll need to sleep soon too.

    We'll be waiting.

    Dragon Liver: +5 to Eats (sharable)

    \nImpotence

    Seems easy enough to me: The elderly king is dying, ever so slowly, of a fatal disease. At this time, he has no clear heir, despite having tried for decades to father a son with the queen. The PCs are assorted royal hangers-on: a queen unable to have the king father an heir before he dies, the king's physician unable to find a cure for the king's illness, a bastard son unable to be recognized as the heir, a nephew of the king hoping the rest are incapable of making their own heirs.

    The rules focus less on what your character can do than on what they cannot do. Your character sheet starts as a list of problems and obstacles you cannot overcome, and as the game goes on you cross things off the list. Eventually, if you cross enough things off, you can cross of the main item at the top: the physician can find a cure, the queen can become pregnant, etc.
