\n I present to you the new Crowdfunding Collection no. 76, including every tabletop project started between 05.21 and 06.10.

After a small summer break I am back and present a total of 62 projects.
Among the english projects I am intrigued by the games I put into the \u201eStory Game RPG\u201c category. I am most fascinated by The Mountain Witch, despite its apparent PvP aspect \u2013 something I usually don\u2019t fancy. Though the Blackwind Project is quite interesting as well, as are Triumvene, Gather (a GM-less game) and Helheim.
The new edition of Black Hack is the Fantasy RPG I am most interested in. The package sounds i, on the other hand,e something. Harvesters on the other hand does feature small animal creatures in a fantasy setting, which looks quite cute and charming. Though Quest as well as Dragonscales look solid as well.
Among the \u201enormal\u201c RPGs I consider Starship Infernum and Secret Agents of CROSS worth a look. Though, as with the previous collection, my current budget is quite tight, thus no backing this time around.
You\u2019ll find quite a plenty of other projects, supplements, adventures, dice and other projects. Even three cool looking notebook projects. Well, and I do like well done notebooks.

If you are interested in foreign language projects. There is the Changeling 20\xba Aniversario by Nosolorol, which does offer quite a cool looking version. Among the French projects I find Sneffels \u2013 for the name alone \u2013 and Tecumah Gulch \u2013 which might produce a Far West edition before GMS worth having a look. The Italian game Caos will apparently be as well available in English.
In regards of the German crowdfundings, there is the translation of Mythic Britain on the one hand, on the other hand you\u2019ll find Havena \u2013 Versunkene Geheimnisse a sourcebook for The Dark Eye, which comes with a cover Havena \u2013 Uh nana which I happen to quite like.

    \n I present to you the new Crowdfunding Collection mo. 77, including every tabletop project started between 06.11 and 06.17.

    In this collection you\u2019ll find three new tabletop rpgs and a recurring one.
    Velvet Generation offers a Science Fiction Rock\u2019n Roll game, in which the player characters form a band and trying to get a musical revolution started. The game uses d6, looks for doubles and adds some more narrative mechanics into the mix. The creators are experienced as this will be the second edition of the game. Dark Times on the other hand combines Cyberpunk and Supers with its setting. The players do assume the roles of superpowered human beings that have been created by companies who view them as their property and nothing more than tools. The system is somewhat similar and based on Wicked Packs as well as Spy and there\u2019s a video explaining it. Survive the Night is a horror role-playing game in which a group of teenagers has to survive several scenarios. Based on the video there are 5 ready-made characters and 4 pre-made scenarios to get started with. Blackwind is the recurring entry. I mentioned the game in the previous collection, yet missed the Patreon. Thus, if you want to support the creators and the game on a regular basis, there you go.
    I did notice Advanced Labyrinth Lord and Tavern Guide as two supplements that caught my eye as they appear to offer a lot. Among the applications you might like to take a look at YARPS, the idea looks nice, yet the creators do need some more backing to realize it.
    Bolsa Drag\xf3n, a spanish project for dice bags caught my eye, because it\u2019s done by El artesano del Rey, who\u2019ll be as well creating the luxury edition of V5, and offer quite an eye-catcher.
