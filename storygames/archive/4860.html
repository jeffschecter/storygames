\n I didn't get a chance to read thoroughly the PDF file of Beast Hunters that I received a while back. I did read enough of it to get hooked and buy a copy tonight at the FLGS (they love you folks down there, man). Beast Hunters is the roleplaying game of primal fury. It's the sound and the thunder,. the blood and the sweat, the battle cry of tribal warriors with spears held high. If I've ever seen a system poised to tackle the brutal, desperate, romance of Snorri Sturluson's Icelandic prose, Beast Hunters is it.

Snorri Sturluson could write some mean prose. I have always and forever envisioned a roleplaying game with that kind of flair but have never found a good system for capturing it. I can think of a couple of games capable of doing generation-spanning epics out of the box (e.g., Hero's Banner, Aria), though each of these fails to evoke the primal rage of Sturluson's prose by design. I could add it in, I suppose, though I think it would be much easier to add the 'generation-spanning epic' part to an appropriately primal game.

Which brings me to Beast Hunters.

This game is all about primal warrriors with tatooed flesh performing awesome deeds of heroism in the form of slaying monsters. This game gives me the tools to capture the battle with Fafnir, the transformed giant (or, going another route entirely, mighty Beowulf's battle with Grendel). This is great stuff and it really has me hankering to try (speaking of hacked games lately) a short myth cycle inspired by Sturluson's prose. I don't know exactly where I'll start, though I'm going to attempt tapping my own savage nature when I set the stage.

I think that a story of revenge seems to be in order.

Blood for blood!
