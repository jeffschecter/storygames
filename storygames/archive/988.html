    It's worth noting that there are two types of GMless games. One type, like Capes, Universalis, or The Shab-Al-Hiri Roach, gives all players the exact same role. Another type, like Polaris, Shock:, and Breaking the Ice, splits up power differently between different players, but does it along non-traditional lines (the guide in Breaking the Ice is a rotating role that is really nothing like a GM, but very different from the active player as well.)

    You may well be interested in both of these, though...

    July 2006
    Well, it's like saying you want a car with a V8. You're not specifying horsepower, revs, or power:weight, but you know it'll be kind of a big engine. It also doesn't tell you much. But since a V8 will cost tens of thousands of dollars and a GMless RPG will cost you tens of ones of dollars, the experimentation is liable to be more cost-effective.

    The games I've written so far have had a particular distribution of responsibilities: in Under the Bed, the player who won the last conflict sets up the next conflict because it tends to encourage an escalation of gnarl. In Shock: the player to your left plays your Antagonist. That way, you develop an intimate relationship with them and can easily read each others' notes.

    These are two of my attempts at eliminating the conflict of interest that a GM faces in an older game structure. Other designers do it different ways; Vincent strictly outlines the responsibilities of the GM in Dogs, for instance, as establishing situation and playing opposition in order to push the players toward making a decision. Tim does the same in The Mountain Witch by distributing scene framing responsibilities and putting the dynamics in the hands of the Trust mechanic. Matt strips almost everything away from the GM in Prime Time Adventures, leaving only the initial scene and opposition in the form of a Budget.

    Me, I like to distribute it widely. Lots of GM stuff is fun and I like everyone to share that fun; plus, protagonist stuff is fun, and it's a drag to not have one when you're GM (because having one constitutes a conflict of interest).
