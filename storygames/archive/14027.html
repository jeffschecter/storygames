\n It was suggested at our last game night that our next game be spy-themed and I want to pick something a bit more story-ish. My group is pretty traditional, they choose d20 for nearly every genre and I'd like to get away from it a bit. Spy Craft and Top Secret were suggested but I just heard a podcast where Wilderness of Mirrors was reviewed and it sounded good. I'm also interested in looking at Spione and Blowback.

I know these are all very different sorts of games so I'm asking for some help picking one of them (or maybe one I haven't considered).

    \n Spycraft is really quite damn good. Although 2.0 is a bit harder to digest, once you do, the dramatic conflict system is SUPER fun. If you want something with an easier learning curve, find a first edition book.

    Both have the Mastermind budget system which really helps give both a sense of "we're taking on SPECTRE!" without the GM going "shit, how good is SPECTRE really?" and having to constantly wing it.

    \n I had lots of fun playing Wildersness of Mirrors at GenCon last year. I think it would be better if everyone was on the same page about tone. There was some silly input from some of the other players I found off-putting. Fortunately our GM was spectacular. If you want a campaign, I'm not sure it is built for that.
    Unless I'm mixing it up with something else, Spione was really more of a history book than anything. Good resource, but I'm not sure about it on its own.
