\n When your game has awesome artwork, people pick it up off the shelf and talk about how much they like the artwork. When it's well laid out, sales rise and it wins awards. When the rules are well written, people praise it, but nobody every picks up a game and says "Wow, this game is really well edited!" On the other hand, if your game is not well edited, it lowers the value of the whole project.

So how about some kudos for the editors and well-edited games out there? Any of you game publishers want to give a shout out to your editor? How did their work help? Have you picked up a game that you noticed was particularly easy to read and navigate?

    \n There's also editing in style. Making sure that the writing isn't boring. It's something I can't put my finger on, but some games, like Agon and True20 are just incredibly boring to read. Others are interesting to read, even if the system isn't particularly good (GURPS Lite 4e and JAGS-2 are the big ones that jump out to me in that area). Rifts is incredibly well written, and with full of enthusiasm, even if it doesn't have great grammar. JAGS Wonderland is very similar, it has some typos, layout glitches, and looks very much like a first draft stream of consciousness, but it's captivating because you can feel the enthusiasm. Other games are somewhat dry in their enthusiasm, but are still interesting because of how everything ties together. David Pulver's books are excellent examples. THS, CK and the Complete Druid's Handbook don't have particularly evocative prose, maybe even a bit stale, but with the content and writing style they really are INTERESTING.
