    \n I've run Cold City as both a short campaign and as a one-off convention game and in all situations, players milked the Trust; they totally rely on it to get them the outcomes they really want. Every last die counts and players won't hesitate to bring in Trust, one way or another.
    \n
    \nOne thing I always do when running CC now is to let players get up to the point in character creation just before distributing Trust, then stop and run a very short scene with all the characters. I start it off in media res by banging the table hard and shouting at them "The basement is on fire! The last of the zombies are burning, but one of them stumbles from the flames towards you! (Pointing at random player) What does everybody do?" Give everyone a chance for one or at most two simple actions: answer questions affirmatively to give them further opportunities ("Yes, there is a fre extinguisher here", "Yes, there are confidential files stored here"). After that scene, when all the players have had a chance to see what their comrades do in a crisis, let them distribute their Trust points. :-)
