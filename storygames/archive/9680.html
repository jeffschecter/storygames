On an unrelated, but good, post, I saw this comment by K. Bailey:

\n
\n

I'm still surprised that WotC did not insert a CCG aspect into 4E. Card systems can handle resource management and random events (and fairness!) well, much more within the tolerance of the average lazy RPG-er. Ask them to track food and encumbrance and they'll scream. Ask them to track some cards and they'll have fun.

\n

Or they could have used it for their blasted skill challenges. The DM flips a card off the "Social" deck. "Suspicious Guard. Half the players must make Disguise, Bluff or Streetwise checks." After that, "Hungry! All players must pay for food." Put some of the gruntwork and time management stuff in there too. Just let the DM run the deck and take a load off.

\n

Mix up the cards, keep selling new ones.. play to WotC's strength. Heck, replace maps with a deck. Sell "Encounters in the Dark Forest" as a deck of cards instead of as a boring module. Has your group found the Haunted Ruins card in there that leads to module H10?

\n
\n

If you want to make a mint, here's your idea. Your first $1K will probably come from me.

\n

Seriously, there's a gem of a good idea somewhere in this. I'd love to hear your thoughts on it, and ideas for implementation. I'd love to try this in some of my games: remove most of the DM duties through a card deck.

    \n I am reminded of Warhammer Quest; let me 'splain:

    Let us say that you have a dungeon deck - it shows rooms, which you also have cardboard cutouts for; as you explore a ruin / dungeon, you flip cards and clip them on. The "objective room" is pre-shuffled into the bottom X cards of the deck.

    Each round, you roll a die; on roll X, monsters show up (in WH quest, this is also the "power" die for magic users). They are drawn from a monster deck. They give loot cards if defeated - this, by the way, I would change to "when generated", so the goblin with the magic sword at least tries to use it.

    So, building a dungeon = building a deck.

    Then there are tables for overland travel, which could just as easily be yet another deck, and for town encounters (another again). As you explore the town, you might draw out from the town deck; "events" being resolved right away, while "institutions" would be left out in play.

    Merchants, of course, would offer a random selection from the loot deck, in addition to some basic fixtures.

    Explore The Shadowed Bogs!
    20 new overland cards, focusing on swamp and night encounters.
    10 new treasures, the dark artifacts of the land!
    5 new town encounters, including the Oracle well...
    10 new monster cards, with poisonous dangers and traps abounding.
    5 sinister objectives.
    2 blank cards.
    Suggested deck configurations for the shadowed bogs included!
    New minis tiles are not included or required; all objective areas use existing tiles.

    \n I'm not sure about cards about a map /replacement/, but here's something that I was thinking about the other day:

    I love the idea from Iron Heroes about putting more tactics on the board:

    "There's a guard tower over here. It takes a DC 15 to climb up here, but if you make it, from the next round all your missile attacks will receive +2 for the rest of the encounter".
    "There's three casks of oil over here. Break them apart or throw them down with a DC 20, or else make an AC 20 melee attack for 4+ HP of damage, and this 10x20 foot area over there becomes slippery: -2 AC to all enemies in the area, DC 15 acrobatics checks to remain upright and not fall."

    etc.

    I'd love to see those in cards. Like, I'd basically make a large card deck with three sections: Top is Dungeon, Middle is Wilderness, Bottom is City.

    At the beginning of every encounter, the DM draws four cards and plays at least one of them. These will add "things" to the field. Like, one card might look like this:

    OAO
    OXO
    XXX
    XOX

    (all the above are "squares" with X'es darkened, O'es empty, and A = a bright square)

    Dungeon: The A square is a rotting tapestry featuring the faded image of a knight with red eyes behind a helmeted head. An arcane lore DC 20 check from within 3 squares will reveal the name of the knight. When spoken, necrotic energy will erupt from the tapestry, and do 2d8 damage to all standing in the marked area.
    Wilderness: The A square is a forgotten idol of a dark God, with a jibbering viseage which leaks a purplish, thick sludge that is pooling in the marked area. Anyone who steps on the marked area is Slowed.
    City: The A square is a raised platform above a tavern or inn that features limited visibility into the surrounding area. A climbing check at DC 20 is required to make it to the platform, but from there all ranged attacks made in the marked squares receive +5 to attack. From the A square, a melee-weapon using character may leap onto any enemy in the marked areas as a standard attack, getting +5 to attack and doing an addition [Weapon] damage.

    Then the DM decides where to fit these things into the encounter. At least one needs to be implemented, but all four cards may be used if needed. The GM marks the board appropriately.

    -Andy
