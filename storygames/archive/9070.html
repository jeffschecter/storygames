Seriously, it's 428 pages long, 8.5" x 11", and the hardcover weighs like seven pounds. I don't know if it's a great game, but you could fell an ox with it. Lay it on top of a can of house paint and you have a coffee table. Bitch is huge.

Also, it's in print! Finally! It was supposed to be a sourcebook for Dread: The First Book of Pandemonium. Like, maybe 96 pages, some new demons, a couple spells, a few scenario hooks. Things just got out of hand, and two years later, I'm holding this massive book, wondering what the hell happened.

So, it's about people who fight angels. Big horrific angels. Unlike Dread, this one's all about long-term campaign play. Lots more setting material, crazy magic, bloodthirsty kaiju angels blowing up your hometown...

The game's available at Lulu (under thirty bucks): http://stores.lulu.com/pandemonium

Or, you can snag the PDF at DriveThruRPG ($9.99): http://rpg.drivethrustuff.com/product_info.php?products_id=61050

PDF Bundle (Spite, Dread, Pent) is seventeen and change: http://rpg.drivethrustuff.com/product_info.php?products_id=60638

The entire player section is available here: http://www.neoplasticpress.com/spite-player-section.pdf

w00t me, for I have released a new game into the wild!

    \nThank you! I wrote paragraphs and paragraphs of description for most of the pieces. I also included references images (photos of octopus and squid tentacles, faces of supermodels, close-ups of intestinal parasites, et cetera).\n
    Posted By: jenskotAre there art credits in the main book? Or any way to tell who did which individual piece?
    \nGood question! I did that with Dread, because there were only a couple dozen illustrations. This time, given the hundreds of pieces, it just wasn't viable. So at the back of the book, Spite instead features biographies for each of the artists. Each artist bio includes a long paragraph of personal information (whatever they felt like writing), and contact info, and the URL for an online gallery. Given the sheer volume of art in the book, I figured that it would drive me completely bonkers to try to figure out who drew what (just doing the index and table of contents manually was enough to drive me to drink -- admittedly, I drink Shasta, but that's beside the point), so I just included all their online galleries, figuring people can see what kind of work they do that way (since all the artists retain ownership of the work, and can post it on their web sites or re-sell it as stock art or whatever). That was a long sentence.

    Thanks, guys!
