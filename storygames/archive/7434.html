    That actually touches on a really cool idea. How would you design a game specifically for people with a disability? And that would have applications not just for blindness or vision impairment. Dyslexia comes to mind.

    ME
    \nMichael, if you're interesting in seriously exploring it I would love to. I've worked with people with developmental disabilities and learning disabilities before a bit, and have thought pretty extensively on this.

    Not to derail this thread, but short answer: you would design a game that was cool and interesting, just like any other game you would design, but you would be conscious about reducing barriers and providing intrinsic supports. Make the game complex and intricate all you want, but think about the specific barriers that you can eliminate. There is a version of The Princes Kingdom that Clinton was developing that used coloured dice instead of numbered dice. It didn't reduce the game's complexity, it just eliminated a barrier. It would likely be possible to design a RIFTS hack that would be playable by people who faced illiteracy and innumeracy and really slow processing times. It wouldn't need to be a simpler RIFTS, just an altered one.
