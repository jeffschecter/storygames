    \nLate spring? Early summer? Late May? Early June? I have to have every word written by tomorrow, anyway.

    I've often seen groups unconsciously dispense with the montage Aftermath in favor of a conversation everyone contributes to. It's a play style thing; some people find the structure valuable, but either way it does no harm.

    You know how black Aftermath skews physical and white skews mental? In the softer package, black skews social and white skews emotional. There's a Tilt to match with really cool elements like "Somebody is pregnant" and "You can't afford to lose it, but you just did".

    Posted By: Dionysusbut then we didn't know what to do with the dice
    Didn't you roll your final dice, to get your Aftermath total and color? There's a mechanical REASON for the dice one might try to Keep, in Act II--you want a lot of one color, if you think your PC deserves a shot at redemption or happiness; or you want a mix of color, if you want the highest odds of your PC being screwed and broken in the end.

    Not saying you can't do whatever you want. but the dice mechanics are sort of the killer app (hell, the ONLY app, after Playsets) in Fiasco. Ignoring the Give->Tilt, Keep->Aftermath bit pretty much means the dice might as well be white or black M&Ms that key you to the tone of the scene, but that you eat when you get them.

    \n I don't know if anyone is interested in this, but.....

    Over spring break, we took the kids to an indoor water park for a few days. Floating around the lazy river a couple of time, I started to wonder about the lives of the teenagers who were life guarding the place, handing out the towels, etc.

    I know that Jason has been working a version of Fiasco with 80's teen movies in mind, and I thought, what the hell, I wonder if I could pull off a teen comedy/romance movie playset based on the imagined lives of these kids working the water park. Being a teen in the 80's, I fed myself on quite a bit of the John Hughes-esque staples, but probably the biggest direct influence would be the more recent movie "Adventureland."

    Anyway, it was surprisingly tough to come up with a good tilt table that didn't involve REALLY sordid affairs and interesting weapons. I also hacked the aftermath a bit.

    I can post a link to it or email if anyone is interested.
