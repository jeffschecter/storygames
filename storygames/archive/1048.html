[Zen Theory] Rolling the dice you do not have

TonyLB
August 2006 edited August 2006 in Story Games

Okay, so I'm trying to streamline the system for Misery Bubblegum and ... well, lemme set the stage.

I want a system like fanmail, but different in the sense that it (a) gives the people you reward power over specifically you and (b) rewarding another person also empowers you, the player.

The current best idea I have is this: You start with a pool of (say) eight dice in your own distinctive color. You can give a die to someone, like fanmail, and they know forever that it's yours because it's your color.

Now how does this empower you? Well, my take on the idea is that you can grab dice from somewhere (about which, more later) and roll them, as long as you aren't holding more than eight of your own dice, grand total. So if you've got eight dice (i.e. you haven't rewarded anyone) then you cannot roll any of your dice (though you can roll things that others reward you with). But if you've given away all your dice then you can roll up to eight.

See how it gets a little zen?

Now my question is: How do I handle this in the most intuitive way?

Because, right now, this requires (a) people to do subtraction, which isn't all that hard but if I can avoid it that would be cool and (b) people to always have a bowl of dice in their color on-hand, in order to draw from.

I would like people to be able to pick up their pool of dice and wander around. I would like a game that could be run at a cocktail party, not requiring people to all cluster around a table full of props.

Right now I've got what I think of as a "two-pocket" solution. You keep your working dice (the eight-or-less in your own color and any that people have given you) in one pocket, and a generous handful of your own color in another pocket). Does anyone have a way to bring that complexity down to something even more manageable?

Side-note: What would it imply if, for instance, there were two distinct modes of the rules. When you're sitting down with the GM (and the bowl of spare dice) you can roll the dice that you do not have (presumably against conflicts mostly external to the group of protagonists) but while you're wandering around having side chats with people you can only roll the dice that you do have (presumably against conflicts internal to the group of protagonists)? That would reduce down to a one-pocket solution, especially if feedback from conflicts was by dice-trading (crediting Vincent for NAStW and Andrew Morris for Unistat).

Does someone have something more elegant?

    Tony, to slash wildly at your Gordian knot here: Have you considered playing cards? They

    1) can be used to generate semi-random numbers standing, sitting, or crawling as long as you have one hand free to hold them, backs out, and the other player has one hand free to "pick a card, any card", with no need for a flat surface

    I keep drifting back to this mechanic while writing Contract Work.

    Having the players give up dice to others at the start not only determines the opening relationships but also sets up the pecking order. Guy with the most dice is the most popular kid in school!

    If players can give dice away that aren't their own the cross relationship drama increases tremendously.

    Jerry: "Y'know Kim, I hear that Bobby likes you. He almost asked you out after the game on Sunday." *hands over one of Bobby's dice.*

    Later that same night; Jerry: "Hey Alex, you know Bobby? Well he's kind of "curious" if you get my drift. I saw him eyeing you up in the locker room after football." *Hands over one of Bobby's dice.*

    Bobby now has to choose to reject the relationships that have been attached to him (take his dice back maybe?) or roll with them. Ultimately the player decides what is true about himself, but others can start rumors to inspire ideas.
