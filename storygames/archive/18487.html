If you have, I'd love to hear your comments on the XP/development stuff in this game - absolutely fascinating to me. Sort of a strange version of "keys" and character specific quests.

Just that alone made me excited to add more to my backing, and is making me think of ways to add the XP system to other game systems I already run - I adore how their are basically "genre specific" keys that everyone contributes to to get shared XP, and then character quests and "arcs" of quests that improve your character in specific ways.

    \n Donated and read through -- not everything (it's 1000 pages!) but enough to get a feeling, while skimming through the various arcs, quests, etc.

    I find it fascinating. The worked examples Ms. Moran's put out made some of the ideas clearer. The XP and the genres encourage specific types of stories/events in the near term, while the Arcs are where your character's going (both mechanical advancement and their story-arc) in general.

    I've been dying to do a Persona 3-type RPG forever, and I've toyed with hacks of various systems, including Panty Explosion, Apocalypse World. Chuubo's the latest. It just seems perfect: the "at school" part of the game is Pastoral. During the Dark Hour, you're in Adventure Fantasy. Then, once per month (since Chuubo's really good for keeping track of "now it's next week"), you hit a Full Moon, and suddenly you're on the Road of Trials. And it seems to me that the Tarot/Arcana of the Personas should map wonderfully to the Arc types.

    Dunno how well the game will work in real-life, but it sounds great on paper. That said, I showed it to some of my fellow players and Pastoral play just seemed to fly entirely over their heads. "Watching a Hayao Miyazaki film about pastoral, country life in rural Japan is one thing. PLAYING it, though?" seemed to be the general response.
