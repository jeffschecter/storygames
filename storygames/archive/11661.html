\nEvery 3 months we host an all day gaming event in NYC called Recess. As part of Recess we often offer potlucks and swap tables. At the last 3, for reasons I can't possibly fathom no matter how much I try, people abandoned their copies of the Complete 2nd Edition Dungeons & Dragons Psionics Handbook on the swap table!
\n
\nI love this game!
\n
\nI now have 3 of them!
\n
\nA lot of the powers made no sense and were often completely left up to interpretation. Which led to amazing creativity. We had no idea what to do but the fantastic descriptions and art inspired us to make up cool things. Of course it's inclusion completely wrecked many D&D; games but...
\n
\nDetonate...
\n
\nI can blow inanimate objects and plants (it specifies plants) including undead. Next to the power is a picture of some bad ass character exploding skeletons all around him. I was like... I want to play that guy! Then I read the power and I'm like... detonate does 1d10 points of damage? What? That's it? Then I keep reading and in addition to damage you completely destroy a percentage of the target based on your psionic power score roll.
\n
\nSo I'm fighting a giant skeleton with 200 hit points, I hit, roll 1d10, do 7 points of damage but based on my roll I also completely destroy 70% of the skeleton's body. What does that mean? Can it still attack me? Does it still have 193 hit points? Can it walk?
\n
\nMadness!
\n
\nConversely magic using character felt more like scientists compared to psionics. Many of their spells did very precise things. But psionics were all over the place. I remember using the disintegrate power to remove 8 cubic feet of mass from the center of a Red Dragon's body. How many hit points did it lose? Is it dead?
\n
\nThere are powers that let you perform psychic surgery! You could awaken psionic powers in other character's minds!
\n
\nThe flip side is if you rolled poorly you suffered drawbacks. I lost 2 characters trying to use disintegrate and instead disintegrated myself!
\n
\nOf course we were 14 and 15 years old at the time which was a huge factor but man, every time I see this game I'm simply filled with joy!
