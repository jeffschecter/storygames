    \n I'm hoping we get to play it again, I'm a sucker for the simpler games and it was entertaining enough despite some grammatical niggles. One player's numerically dyslexic and another from Norway. But, I quickly whipped up an IRC bot to count the rolls more quickly and have been adding a little bit onto it just in case we do play again so tracking the rolls, free words, and coins are easier.
    \n
    \nThe rolling nothing was a bit discouraging cause it happened -first- turn, but, you really get 3 'turns' a round in most cases anyways due to the 'next' player and 'previous' player. It took us halfway through the game before I realised that I kinda read the rules for player to the left, and player to the right backwards. Since we were on IRC. we have a vertical nickname list, and we used that for determining who was 'next' and 'previous' and thought +/- was logically backwards in that situation... but, came later to realise if you're playing in 'clockwise' order, that the 'next' player is indeed on your left. So, despite that mishap (that didn't -really- matter since we were consistent the entire game... and I babble). So... back on track. The rolling nothing on a turn, isn't really that crippling due to the additional turns provided. You're almost always gonna get 3 chances to contribute during a round.
    \n
    \nThe game Jane mentioned is a children's party game, and is very much the same basic idea. Each 'turn' a player rolls a normal six sided die. That determines how many words they added to the sentence, then the next player, and next, all repeat this, working on one sentence. When it gets back to the original player the sentence is finished. The player after that then rolls and starts the next sentence. Continues this way for a complete round, depending on the number of players. A smaller group may do more rounds. There's no buying free words or coin exchanging going on.
    \n
    \nThanks for the fun game :)
