    \n Strands of FATE has a limited GM pool that is added to when important NPCs enter the scene. It works okay, so long as you don't take anything into your pool except when players are invoking/compelling on NPC Aspects. Overall the flow goes from your pool to the players, then they spend it, and it usually leaves the game.

    Fan Mail is SUPER different, and actually I've run into problems with saying "hey, have a fate chip for doing cool things". Don't do that, you will fuck up the system a bit, you won't feel the inertia that can be provided if people get fate chips for doing things that are to their disadvantage as part of the core of their character.

    Posted By: agonyAm I understanding that correctly? So there is no mechanism dictating how often I can tag aspects as the GM?
    \nThere are a few built in brakes. You can only tag once per scope in a roll, so no matter how many fate points the GM has to bring to bear, eventually she's going to need to draw on the opposition (player characters in this case) aspects and that means they will get paid for the tag. Add to that the fact that characters have limited fate points no matter what the GM can bring to bear in other contexts, and that means that an NPC (including a space craft) in a fight still has a limited number.

    Posted By: agony
    How many Fate points do you typically give an NPC? Like 2-3 for a minor but named NPC, and like 5 for a major mover and shaker?
    \nA good rule of thumb is to use their skill cap as their fate pool -- a 3-cap NPC gets 3 fate points. I've run some very successful scenes with many NPCs giving them no fate points and playing only with fate points acquired when the players compel or tag them, too. When I play them this way I usually have one pool for all the NPCs.

    Ships are first class characters though -- give them five always.
