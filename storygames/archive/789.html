Okay, so I'm starting a story games-themed band. It's like an epic legends-themed band (Blind Guardian), a white trash-themed band (Southern Culture on the Skids), or an 80's pop metal-themed band (The Journey), except that all our songs are about story games.

Here's how this'll work.

1) Anybody is welcome to join the band. Respond to this thread and volunteer your musical talents. For example, I write songs, sing, play the banjo, and can make hot beats on my Gameboy (using Little Sound DJ). If you can't make music, maybe you can help write us some ridiculous lyrics (by which I mean "get into internet flamewars about our artistic direction").

2) Once we have a band (which can include, like, 6 bassoonists or whatever we have), we'll vote on a game to write a song about, come up with a ridiculous title ("You'll Never Escape from the Dogs in the Vineyard!"), some people will get together (off Story Games, so Andy won't kill us) and write some horrible lyrics, then some other people will get together and come up with a basic melody concept.

3) Then I'll make a basic drum/bass/melody track and we'll send it around so people can record their various parts. And I (or somebody more skilled, if we can find someone) will mix everything together. And, in the end, some great story game will have a themesong that is totally RAWK!!!

4) If it's fun, we rinse and repeat.

Requirements
- Have a set up where you can record stuff.
- Don't get too serious about this.
- We are not trying to make great music. We are trying to RAWK!!!
- Keep all band drama at least 85% fake. Fake drama is funny. Real drama is not.
- Try to keep this thread nominally about story games. If you just wanna blab about bands and music, take it elsewhere.
- DON'T MAKE US KICK YOU OUT OF THE BAND!!!
- DON'T KICK ME OUT OF THE BAND!!!LOL!!!11!!

Okay, then, obviously the first and most important thing we have to do is pick a story games-related band name. Feel free to suggest a handful of options, even if you're not in the band. I nominate Andy Kitkowski, if he's willing, to make the final selection from among the options, just so you guys don't think I'm hogging the band for myself. I'M A TEAM PLAYER!!! No, Dev, Thundersnow is definitely not in the running. Try to come up with names that are applicable to lots of story games and not just specific ones ("The Spiritual Attributes").

  • Graham
    June 2006
    Dev, you've basically got two options:

    1. Buy shitloads of music software and a decent microphone and do the whole thing properly.

    2. Find some very basic-level recording software on your computer (on Windows, it'd be Sound Recorder; there must be something similar on a Mac). Plug the best microphone you've got into the computer. Record the stuff and post it online somewhere.

    Then hope someone else does the work of integrating your stuff into a song. (They probably will, because people on this forum are nice). They might well run the music you played through some filters, to get rid of noise and so on, so don't be surprised if it sounds different in the end.

    When you're playing the music, try to keep everything else quiet and also (I can't emphasise this enough) keep time. Almost everything else can be corrected later, in some way, but if you don't keep time as you're playing, it's a nightmare.

    I'm sure other people have more experience with music recording than me, so correct me if I'm wrong.

    Graham
