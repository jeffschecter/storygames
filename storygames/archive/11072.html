    \n Are you truly looking for small things that will only fit in a stocking? Because most books would be right out, I think, unless you roll them up in a ball or something.

    I'd recommend
    dice
    a deck of solid or unique cards
    a pair of mechanical pencils 0.5mm lead
    a couple of Pilot V5 hi Tecpoint pens
    a really cool tie
    a pair of Thorlo hiking socks (sooo comfortable)
    one of those "it looks like an orange cut into wedges, but it is actually milk chocolate with orange flavor" orange chocolate things
    a personalized cockring sporting the D&D 4e logo
    a Gerber flip-out knife
    a sleeve of pink poker chips
    an mp3 recorder for recording your gaming sessions
    an iPod shuffle for listening to gaming podcasts while you pump iron at the gym

    If you're looking for relatively inexpensive geeky stuff you can point to your friends/family to buy for you that won't fit in a stocking, I can heartily recommend:
    Levenger's Storyboard Notepads as a GM for creating a draft of a game that is heavily scene-based. I love the shit out of my pads I got two years back. Also, I recommend their...
    Grid Annotation Pads. Endless use to be had from them, seriously. I use them from everything from work, research notes, book notes/study, Japanese study, campaign planning or situation planning for games, character sheets, etc. My single favorite, versatile pad of paper. Thick paper, solid for writing in pen or pencil. Best gaming gift for someone who is into stationery and graph paper and the like.

    -Andy
