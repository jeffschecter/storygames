\n I'm gearing up to run the new Marvel RPG at the beginning of March for a demo at my FLGS. Getting all excited, so I dug up ALL of my old HeroClix and started paging through the Breakout mini-Event to see what good guys and bad guys I could use for Action Order markers.

...in the end, I have most of the heroes, at least.

My option right now is doing a big 'ol Google Image search for shots of the various heroes and villains, and making little 1.5"x1.5" cardstock counters. Maybe instead printing them onto label stock, sticking that onto some thick chipboard, and cutting them out separately (though that'd be a pain in the wrist, let me tell you).
I was hoping that maybe someone had a huge collection of good-looking and updated fold-up figurines that could be printed and used, but most that I can find are extremely retro (not always a bad thing, but they still don't have nearly enough of the right characters).
My only other option (and one I may use for future games) is to make original illustrations of all the heroes and badguys, to make my own fold-up figures...but of course, it would take a LOOOOONG time to finish them all, and I'm nowhere near as familiar with the Marvel universe's latest and greatest as I used to be. Plus, my super-hero stylings tend to be...eccentric-looking.

So, here's the question: what do the rest of you use, plan to use, or would like to use as Action markers for Marvel?

EDIT: Changed the category to "Make Stuff," since this might be an opportunity for people to whip up something interesting.

    \n Marvel OverPowered cards is a super cool idea.

    Other idea: get a couple of card stands. Print out double-sided cardstock cards: your character in colour on one side, your character in greyscale on the other. Colour facing out: action remaining. Greyscale facing out: action taken.

    If you lack the ability to print double-sided on cardstock, you can instead: print single-sided on regular paper, each image in colour and again in greyscale. Cut the images out, glue them to either side of a piece of cardstock or a dud magic card or a playing card from an incomplete set. Voila!
