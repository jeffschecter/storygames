    \n One cool thing we did was follow up on the Puppetland game from last year, with 3 of 4 players returning to their original roles. It went darker, quicker, and really explored (I felt) the tension between trying to maintain Judy's innocent hopes, and being faced with the harsh realities of Punch, and the darker realities of Splotch Flaggy's transgressions...

    But it was cut short, with only 8 minutes remaining on the clock, we had to stop anyway. I will forever be tortured by the pleading in the children's voices. We had an audience of people watching at the table adjacent, and I took a recording of the session (partially to keep time) and there's a soft, adorable voice saying "No, keep going."

    Heartbreaking.


    Epic Kagematsu action was had as well, and The Mountain Witch, but with a notable absence of Joanna. Plus the first night was REALLY FREAKING COLD. I mean REALLY. So, there. That's about all the bad I have to say about it.

    \n\nMay 2012 \n

    \n
    \n My complaints:

    My head got cold when I slept and I wasn't smart enough to wear my hat to bed until Saturday night. And I didn't sleep enough because I was up till 4 AM hanging out at the bonfire and talking with awesome people.

    There were too many cool games to play in and I always felt like I was missing out. I'm really mad I didn't get to play in Jason's cool minis game, Task Force Roach. There were too many cool people there and I didn't feel like I got enough time with a lot of them. I already miss people and don't want to wait until next Camp Nerdly to see them. I made new friends and don't know how to contact them because they're not even on the Nerdly Attendees page and I don't know their last names.

    Everyone worked so hard to make the event awesome and I felt guilty that I spent only an hour helping Jule Ann make lentil stew on Friday evening and had no other chores all weekend, so I chipped in on Sunday clean-up and helped people carry stuff and pack cars.

    I thought of a better "One Cool Thing I Saw at Camp Nerdly" a while after I'd already said mine.

    I had a reallyreallyreally great Fiasco game and now I have to take back all of those things I said about Fiasco not pressing enough of my gamer buttons.
