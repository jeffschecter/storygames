\n Good stuff came out of those last 3 threads.

I'm refactoring to collect the central poitns that came up again and again.

1) Make the party first, characters second.
a) Each character has two specific ties to the party: "How did I come through for the party when they needed me the most?" and "How did the party come through for me when I needed them the most?" Each character also has a specific "Remember the time when..." connection to one other character.

2) Niche protection and specialization. I'm thinking about 4 key roles would be good, and I can put that right in the stats.
b) While niches are protected, Resolution rewards action where PCs work together.

3) Play the same characters over a long period.*
a) I want to reward long term play with increased effectiveness, and characters who grow together are a great source of camaraderie.
b) However, a real sense of risk is also important. I'm almost certain I'll use my Death Flag rules in this game.

4) Player control of level of adversity, which GM has the job of bringing on. Thus the GM is in the service of the players. However, the players have strong incentives to choose higher levels of adversity.

5) Situations should involve the PCs' necks being on the line against a threat that can only be defeated collectively, or illustrate the difference in qualitative outcomes between a group and a solo effort. Situations should relate to group goals, with individual character goals being less important or unstated.

* This is the hardest one on the list IMO. I'm trying to get "sit down, get camaraderie" out of the game in each session. So obviously I want it to work even if it's not long-term play. But the fact remains that people get a strong sense of camaraderie from long-term play in the same group, and I can't control how often these groups play.
