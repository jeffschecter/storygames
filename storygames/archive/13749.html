I'm apprehensive about posting this here, for some reason, but I will put on my Armor of Directed Promotion and say it:

\n

Worldnamer (John) and I, and our friend Austin, have started a blog where we wax pretentious about our thoughts on game design, both principles and process. It's sort of intended as a way to capture the thoughts generated by our conversations, as we work on the three or so games we're currently making.

\n

What we'd like is feedback and comments and critique, so if you're interested in reading what we have to say, please check it out, comment, tell your friends.

\n

The blog is here, at http://games.transneptune.net. Do pass it on to friends who might be interested!

    \n After reading a couple of the longer posts on your blog, I think you support your ideas with concrete details rather well, so I wouldn't call your thoughts pretentious at all. I definitely wouldn't mistake your assertiveness at times (blatantly saying "this is good," for example) because I see that you have definitely have some excellent rhetorical appeals here: you have played the games (the basis of your argument), you have played other games to form comparisons (like a counter argument!), and you have derived your conclusions from experience, both abstract and concrete (ethos!!!). I think you're on to a good start, and I'll definitely be coming back for more.
    \n
    \nThe only thing I think could use some improvement is the focus. I'd like to see you really develop some major ideas that you will refer back to. I know it's early in the blog's life-cycle, but consider refining your arguments around a few key elements that really make you think deeply.
    \n
    \nOh, and the writing style is very clear and it flows very nicely. A+
    \n~Klaus
