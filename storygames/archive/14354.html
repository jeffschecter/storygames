    Posted By: jenskotHow do you fit CoC character creation, scenario creation, and play into 4 hours?
    Practice, practice, practice.

    Longer version: If you can't create a BRP or d20 character in about ten minutes, you're doing it wrong - I don't think that's controversial. You can speed things up even more by pregenning or reusing existing characters - I have a fat folder full of slightly used Investigators for just that purpose. Scenario creation is optional, as I've said many times at great length*, and I prefer not to do it most of the time, since I'm a dirty hippie. I understand that if you don't agree with me on this point, it's not possible to run pickup Cthulhu (unless you use published modules, at least one of which I usually have with me in case of newbies or trad gamers). And four hours is plenty of time for collaborative storytelling, as evinced by the many games of all types that take place in that kind of timeframe. So the time budget is something like ten minutes for character creation, another minute to flip to a random page of More Tales of Terror - or The Golden Bough, or DG:C, or whatever is handy - read it, and turn it into a challenge, and three or four hours of quality collaborative storytelling time.

    * The TL;DR is that I hate knowing the answer to the mystery in advance. I want to be as surprised and delighted by everyone else's story as they are by my contributions, and I often resent the players their sense of discovery when I GM the traditional way. In consequence, whenever the crowd seems right, I don't.
