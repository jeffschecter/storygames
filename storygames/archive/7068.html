\n So, chatting with some of my gamer pals one mentioned this idea for My Life with Master he'd heard of somewhere... After searching I found that it was mentioned way back in Feb 2006 when a poster named "neelk" replied in a thread called "So, What's the best system for supers?"


Here's his reply...\n
"My Life with Batman.

The PCs are the second-stringers superheroes of Gotham -- Robin, the Huntress, Catwoman, Spoiler, Azrael, Batgirl. Their lives fall into the shadow of the Batman and his endless crusade for vengeance, and their wills are dominated and broken by his overwhelming mastery. Is their any part of their lives which is their own?

Batman is the Master, of course. He's a Beast/Teacher. The Outsiders are Superman and the Justice League, and Batman is obsessed with proving his superiority over them. "
\n

So....

#1. I've never played My Life with Master. I've barely looked at it, but heard that it was one of those "must play" games.
#2. I love this idea!! (from everything I've heard about My Life with Master)

Has anyone ran with this idea?
Does anyone have any ideas now?

Any thoughts on someone new to My Life with Master overall?


thanks!

    Posted By: GB SteveWhat about having The Joker as the Master with all the little superheroes running around performing his schemes so he doesn't blow up Gotham?
    \nOne of the Good Omens guys ran My Life With Joker at ConQuest SF this past weekend. We weren't superheroes, we were minions of the Joker. It was brilliantly done, and since we were being commanded to do bad things, it worked perfectly well with the system. We created the Joker (settled on him being a Brain/Feeder, feeding on Chaos to show Batman that he can live without rules, but what was great is that all the options could have worked) and our minions. Two critical components that seem to be noticable lacking from My Life With Batman. Since we worked for an evil master, all the rest of the structure still worked. I'm not sure how it can work with Batman. Maybe if his commands always enforce your role as a sidekick, and you need love for the confidence to break free? The general idea of Batman's sidekicks rising up against him seems odd, though, and you have to reframe all the stats. The actual play didn't go into how the mechanics were changed to make it about superhero sidekicks instead of broken monster minions, so I'm still not sure how that can work.
