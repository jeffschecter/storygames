    \n Bacchanal is available in English as well. The history of the game is that Paul wrote it for a IGC competition, then went on to publish the small booklet-form English edition, and then a couple years later Narrativa took it upon themselves to rework the game into a more elaborate Italian release. I can't remember off the top of my head whether the Italian version is also published in English.

    So yes, Bacchanal and Bacchanalia are the same game, just different editions. I understand that the dice have maybe been replaced with cards in the Italian edition or something like that?

    I do, of course, have the booklet-form English version in our webstore. It's an inventive game that has interesting things going for it both socially and technically; well worth the low asking price for anybody more interested in ideas than page count.

    Dear Paul Czege,

    My Life with Master was the first indie roleplaying game I bought. You were my first game design idol. I had assumed that, you being an idol and all, we would never interact as peers. I arrived at Gencon 2006 bright-eyed and terrified. You bought my game, and you had it in hand when we formally introduced ourselves to one another. You gave me a compliment that I still hold dear. My world spun and I suddenly realized that the people you aspired to be were also people you could reach out and interact with. That moment has continued to shape my understanding of the world and inform the better parts of who I am. Thank you.

    Sincerely,
    Joe

    \n Hey Johnzo,

    The minotaur game is called The Clay That Woke. It's my effort to design a game with a rich setting that inspires extended play, but with my kind of mechanics. Internal playtesting went very very well. You know how when you're running a campaign game it gets hard to schedule all the players as they start to lose interest? I ran 13+ sessions and never had that problem.

    Players are an underclass of minotaurs in an ancient, declining human civilization on the margins of a dense, encroaching jungle. The minotaurs adhere to a stoic philosophy called Silence: work for justice, do not want, do not use the names of women. In society the minotaurs are relegated to dangerous and menial jobs, but can move to somewhat higher status jobs by increasing their Name stat. Minotaurs also have a Silence stat, representing their adherence to Silence; when they bottom out their Silence stat they go frantic and break for the jungle, where they have dangrous encounters until they're refreshed enough to return to the city. Extending above the jungle canopy are trees that have few leaves. They're called the Watchers. It's said that when the society below enters a new age of greatness the Watchers will frondesce and bloom again. It's a campaign play game; you play until it's clear whether or not that will happen.

    Mechanically it's pretty much done, except for playtesting one specific late new mechanic over multiple sessions. It's the best game I've designed since My Life with Master. I'm about half way through writing the text for external playtesting.

    I know I'm bucking the 2hr/4hr game trend, but I don't care.

    Paul
