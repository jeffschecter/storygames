    \n Well... I kinda see your point Dan, but "Aspects as kinda skills" has always been there in Fate (think "Strong as an Ox").

    Fate has a couple of basic principles that get declined in a lot of moving pieces that are all part of the same engine, and interact with each other, so yeah, it's *yet another* thing you have to learn. But as I always say to people here in Italy (we published SotC in Italian), you don't *have* to get ALL the subtle interactions and clever aspect uses to have fun, and the system is sturdy enough that it won't break, either.

    Don't try to learn (or teach) all the uses and interactions in one go, remind them from time to time to the players, and stuff will emerge with time... if you think it induces confusion just gloss over it a bit: it's a shortcut, you don't have to use it.

    Posted By: JDCorleyThe example that's used, "Warden Connections" as an Aspect, is invoked to declare that a Warden is in town.Compare to the Contacts skill implementation of knowing someone. In other words, I invoke an Aspect to create something completely separate from my character. I use Contacts to establish my character's capabilities in the area.
    There's a reason why the example is clearly a declaration. The "Invoking for Effect" section in SOTC is pretty clear that this is essentially a minor Declaration, with the invocation allowing it to be less minor. It is there to give the players a bit of narrative input, rather than to make skills meaningless. It's not about "bypassing" rolls.

    Contacts is about getting information from knowing people. In the case of "I know a guy," invoking for effect could very well mean that you know the guy and he's around. That doesn't necessarily mean you find him. And it doesn't necessarily mean you get the info from him. But you know who your looking for (and he knows you).
