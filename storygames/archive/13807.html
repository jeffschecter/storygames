    \n Adebowale: I would recommend you to play some particular games, not necessarily lots of them.

    Prime Time Adventures is a story-game with fast and fun interplay, and one which is meant to cater for the premise you and your players prefer. Thus it is eminently suitable for use with whatever is unique to your own culture, within the scope of TV-entertainment.

    3:16 Carnage Amongst the Stars is a role-playing game of the classical kind (with a GM), but quite modern in its tight premise and furiously fast interaction. It's eminently suitable for seeing how you may create a game of great depth with simple methodic tools.

    Until We Sink ... is a modern roleplaying game with a revolutionary card-based method. This is a must to play and study. It points towards the future, in its use of a simple method to create the foundation for explorative, almost freeform, interaction.

    Some role-playing poems should be played, and designed. The short form of role-playing (15 minutes of game-play) is eminently suitable for trying out your own concepts as a designer, getting the feeling of completing a design, having it played and receiving the reactions of your players. And then it goes to show how simple a role-playing game can be, and still be effective.

    - there may be one or two games in addition, which is worth playing, to fill out the picture and to inspire your own design-endeavours. Good luck! Take care!
