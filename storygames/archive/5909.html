\n Does anyone have any experience printing a game on newsprint? I'm considering producing the ashcan of Misspent Youth in that way, since the book design is very much ripped off of the legendary punk zine MAXIMUM ROCKNROLL (which is also printed on newsprint). I love the idea of an ashcan being in that temporary format.

However I'd imagine it's not very feasible with POD publishers, is it? What about local copy shop type things?

    \n You can offset print on pretty much any quality of paper, but it's offset and the issue will be quantity. Newspaper format stuff is done on a web press; local papers usually do short-run work in between their scheduled jobs, but again the scale would be enormous. Couldn't hurt to ask, though. I'd start by talking to a local printer - not a copy shop.

    \n I would check with small local newspapers...particularly suburburban, or rural-town newspapers of the sort that are always on the verge of going under or being bought by a big corporation.
    \n
    \nMy old hometown newspaper (now owned by a big corporation) used to fit short run contract work (like bargain sheets, or church pot luck recipe collections, or a membership guide / locator for a neighborhood or alumni group) in between their regular paper runs. No idea on the cost, but high school reunions could use it to make a "program" for their reunion it couldn't have been too high.
    \n
    \nI don't know that anyone other than local newspapers (or printers of those ubiquitous bargain sheets full of used cars and lawnmowers) would have a web press to print on news print.

    \n
    \nBut yes, you can find printers that will print just a thousand or two items on new print and for cheap.

    \n Keep one other thing in mind, too: the way you set graphics changes when you use pulp stock, in surprising and esoteric ways. Pulp bleeds ink more, which can be a Good Thing or a Bad Thing (particularly with fine font strokes and serifs). You'll want to talk with the press operator about screen resolutions and such, before you "bake in" your art's DPI setting in your DTP software. Also, be aware that there will be a screen cost, to burn the separation sheets for the press: you buy those; they aren't "free" like with a digital printer. Likewise, if you do four-color or spot color art, you'll be buying more sheets (and you might be asked to provide color separations; not too tough with some DTP software, impossible with others).

    Meanwhile, you could POD a pulp-textured background using a "normal" POD production pipeline, and get nearly the same look (but a finer feel and more durability). Which begs the question: how critical is the tactile feel of the product, versus easy of production, reduced costs (i.e. $0.00 upfront), and durability? I get that you have this zine thing going... but, man, they made zines on that shitty stock for a reason--it's cheap and disposable. Read once and toss. Around the gaming table--with drinks and such--and being referenced frequently... I dunno. Seems a bad move, to me. But it is, as ever, Your Call.

    HTH;
    David
