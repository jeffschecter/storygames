\n I need you to write me a letter for my new game, Do. If you haven't heard of it before, here's a brief synopsis:
\n
Inspiration: Do is what would happen if Stargate SG-1, Dear Abby, and Avatar: the Last Airbender had an epic love triangle.

Premise: There are hundreds small worlds orbiting a flying sun-temple. Like islands, these small worlds often have strange cultures and unique problems. Whenever the people have a conflict that cannot be resolved on their own, they send letters to the temple requesting assistance from the temple's monks. When a young monk is ready for to learn enlightenment, she and her friends are sent on a pilgrimage. They get a stack of letters from across the known worlds with instructions not to return until they've resolved them all.
\nThe Mission: The bulk of Do will be a few dozen letters chock full of plot hooks and story seeds for the players to fool around with. Annie Rush and I are writing a lot of the letters, but to really get a variety of voices, I thought it would be good to tap the talents of the community as a whole.

Your mission, should you choose to accept it, is to write a letter of 500-1000 words. If you're interested, email me at omnimancer@yahoo.com and I'll add you to the list of letter-writers that already includes the talents of John Wick, Fred Hicks, Rob Donaghue and Shreyas Sampat. (How's that for some variety? )

Email me and I'll send you the fiddly details and inspiration.

    \n Actually, I intend for the title to have a double-meaning. The English do refers to the verb-oriented design of the game and the proactive temple philosophy. D\xc5\x8d refers to "The Way," a path towards enlightenment.

    There are a number of other double-meanings in the game terms. For example, "enlightenment" refers to wisdom but it also refers to the fact that monks can fly (they're lighter-than-air, get it? :P ). The "light" pun is further deepened because the temple acts as the sun for the universe, spreading light across the known worlds. And it gets even more punny because the temple uses the visible spectrum of light as a metaphor for their philosophy.

    I like puns, can you tell? ^_^

    \n Dear Temple in the Center of the Sky,

    It is with great sadness that I write to you. These words are not easy for me to communicate, so I will skip directly to the point.

    After your last visit to our sister world, Chu Nom, ended with such a debacle, a movement erupted on our planning that sought to \xe2\u20ac\u0153defend the Hundred Worlds against intrusive meddling by outsiders, however well intentioned.\xe2\u20ac\x9d

    A popular referendum was proposed, one calling for all agents of the Temple to be banned from our world. I personally led the campaign against the referendum\xe2\u20ac\u2122s passage, but we were not successful in our efforts. Subsequently, they have nominated me, as a \xe2\u20ac\u0153temple sympathizer\xe2\u20ac\x9d to inform you of the decision.

    It is with a heavy heart that I respectfully request that you no longer visit our world. I am very sorry that this has come to pass, but I am bound by the laws, traditions, and popular will of our people to convey this message.

    I hope that I will be able to confer with your agents occasionally on Chu Nom, where I will be coordinating a group of volunteers to assist in the rebuilding of that world. Perhaps we can plan for a day when and the disagreements between our peoples can be worked out.

    Yours in Light,
    Councilman Tila

    This whole crowdsourcing process has been a quick lesson in organization.



    Reds have expressed interest in the project, but not yet sent in a letter.

    Oranges have sent in a letter, but are pending the addition of titles, symbols and/or revisions. Generally, it's just titles or symbols.

    Greens have sent in one or more letters complete with title and symbols.

    If there are more than a couple multi-letter writers, I'll label them blue.

    If you notice that you're miscategorized, please email me and I'll correct it ASAP.
