\n It's just a small thing, but I spend a fair amount of time and money at Kinkos and I always have to go through this thought process of figuring out what all I need or don't need to print out to get ready for a session, whether it's from a pdf of a finished game or a playtest document or whatever.

"Printing
At some point you\u2019ll probably want to print this game. The
best way to get ready for a session is to print:
\u2726A few copies of the basic moves and special moves
(double sided, basic on one side and special on the
other)
\u2726Two copies of each class sheet, single sided
\u2726A few copies of the cleric and wizard spell sheets
(double sided, cleric spells on one side and wizard on
the other)
\u2726A few copies of the equipment and name sheets (double
sided, equipment on one side and names on the other)
\u2726The GM rules, double sided to save paper"

This was helpful. Some documents that need it more than Dungeon World does don't do this. Everyone please do this. Thanks.

    Steve, that's a tough question. There have certainly been less-gonzo sessions, John Harper had some pretty great stories to tell from PAX that sound about as serious as a dungeon can be, but I'm not sure it's really been that serious. The tone of John's game compares favorably with the tone of most D&D games I've been in, if that makes any sense. Roughly as serious as any classic fantasy can be, but not really dramatic.

    I don't think any characters have developed between sessions yet, but there's been some pretty decent within-session advancement.

    \n I think that if Dungeon World is about as gonzo as an average game of D&D was when you were a youngin' then we've hit the nail on the head. Like Apocalypse World, Dungeon World is flavoured lightly by the character books and the rest is a fill-in by the DM and the players. If you want grimdark fantasy, we can do that. If you want silly, we can do that, too.

    Con games tend to be short and catchy and it's easy to do that by being light-hearted and silly but, as in all things, your mileage may vary.
