\n There's a lot to track. Many attacks don't simply do damage. They also inflict modifiers and status effects as primary and secondary abilities. Many of these effects stack. Many don't. Many are only active in certain contexts (you attack or don't attack X person, you are within X range, you are adjacent to X person, you do X action). Many have different durations. 1 round. 1 encounter. Till you make a save. With the DM controlling multiple opponents, especially at higher levels with the players having a greater range of effects at their disposal, it can be overwhelming. Not to mention everything the players have to track themselves. Which is usually also useful information for the DM to have access to at a glance. What statuses do each players currently have?

I'd love if 4E borrowed even more from popular boardgames like Descent and included elements like status tokens. It would make things way easier!

I would also love power and feat cards that you can use to create a deck of options for your character!

The at-will, encounter, daily, utility powers already act like cards in a magic deck.

At first level you have 2 at will, 1 encounter, 1 daily, and 0 utility powers.
At tenth level you have 2 at will, 3 encounter, 3 daily, and 3 utility powers.

That doesn't mean you only have access to 3 encounter powers at tenth level. A Wizard has 15+ encounter powers to choose at tenth level. But you can only have 3 active at a time. But every level, you can switch which powers are active and when you gain new powers you can switch a new one for an old one.

Since your switching these powers in and out, a power deck would be awesome!

Rock,
John

    Ralph, funny enough tiny colored post it notes may be the best solution. Even withing different types of states and modifiers, there are context variables that change from use to use that would also need to be tracked. The ability to write what these variables are directly on the mini would be perfect.

    Matt, in terms of Descent I would say the sweet spot would be something in between the two. 4E is definitely influenced by WoW but I would say it's even more so influenced by Descent. 4E feels like Descent 2E. The main differences being that 4E is Descent on crack, death is less an issue, and you have more customization. But Descent is quicker to play and feels like a more complete game out of the box where so far 4E needs extra pieces to get the full experience. Descent also give the GM more tools to change elements on the fly and still keep elements balanced.

    Which would I rather play? It's hard to say. They scratch the same itch!
