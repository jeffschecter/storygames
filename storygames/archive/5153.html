\n So,
\n
\nI'm home for the Christmas holidays for 5 days. My brother is a hyperactive, 10-year old menace... but he does really like me and miss me.
\n
\nSo I asked him if he wanted to try playing a roleplaying game and he said yes. I pulled out Beast Hunters (probably the far limit of his attention span and creative energy. It's focused on immediate, concrete problems so I think it'll work out.)
\n
\nHe made a character, and when we finished he was pretty wiped out so we put it aside for later. I think the character creation process was finally clicking for him in the fourth stage, while we went over Beast Hunter training. At first it was a lot of blank-page, rather-be-playing-playstation looks.
\n
\nAnyways, here is his character. I wrote the blank character sheet and his name, but he did everything else.
\n
\nBeast Hunter: Kuchika
\nTribe: The Warriors (!!!)
\n
\nInitiative: Social 2, Mental 2, Physical 3
\n
\nDamages:
\nSocial: 00 / 0 / 0 / 0 / 0
\nPhysical: 000 / 00 / 0 / 0 / 0
\nMental: 0 / 00 / 0 / 0 / 0
\n
\n(Note: Astute playas will note that his Mental damage track is illegal, because there are more mediums than lights. Whatever, I didn't want to complicate or frustrate the situation by explaining technical violations. I just rolled with the little stuff.)
\n
\n
\n
\nOffensive Traits:
\nStrong PO+3
\nI was raised as a fighter PO+1
\nKnow how to fight PO+2
\nNavigate in snow MO+2
\nI hate Vikings SO+1
\nSwing a sword PO+1
\n
\nDefensive Traits:
\nPretective SD+3
\nDifferent Languages MD+2
\nLove Country MD+1
\nSelf Defense PD+2
\n
\nOffensive Resources:
\nCrossbow PO+2
\nTraps PO+1
\nCompass MO+2
\n
\nDefensive Resources:
\nKhamsa necklace MD+1
\nArmer PO+3
\nCalling horn SD+1
\n
\n
\nNote: Astute playas might note that Vikings don't exist in the canon setting, which is what I pitched to him. When I asked what his enemies were, he thought really hard... then his face light up and he proudly declared "Vikings!" Fuckin' rights, Greg. There are Vikings now.
\n
\nAt first I was really worried he might have a static character. Note the first three of his Offensive Traits.
\nBut, couple them with pretective/love country/self defense... and then throw in the fact that he's a good scout (navigate in snow/compass/traps/calling horn), I think there's some good potential there. A patriotic scout trekking through the snowy wasteland in search of Vikings to fight.
\n
\nWe agreed to play in the car ride, on the way to the Greyhound station (we live in an isolated community, so that's about 1.5 hours of solid play time).
\n
\nHas anyone played Beast Hunters with a younger audience? How about in a short time frame? How about in a car?
\nIf so, any tips?
\nIf not, isn't his character badass?
\n
\nI hate Vikings SO+1 makes my life complete, basically.

    \n That is, indeed, an awesome character In fact, it made my day.

    As for suggestions: Colin is right on regarding setup.

    For play: Be generous. Give him a good amount of AP. Let him get into the swing of being a real badass before you get to the part about challenging him.

    In my experience with my boys, younger kids tend to take more liberty with what they get to decide than traditionally-trained roleplayers. That's great! Tell him he can make up all sorts of shit in his maneuvers about how he takes out this guy and that one.

    Introduce a few colorful enemies. Make them ugly and gigantic and mean. They'll be memorable and fun to kill. If he narrates a kill immediately, well, just have others take that place instead of blocking his input.

    Also, I'd focus on physical conflicts first, then make other conflicts very intuitive for kids: if it's social, make it a showing-off contest, or something like that where different actions are easy to come up with. Like, "Impress the guardian of the temple" as a social conflict, and then he can lift rocks, do somersaults, or what the hell ever he wants.

    Good hunting, and let me know how it went!

    \n We played in the car on the way to the Greyhound station, for a total of about 45-60 minutes of play.
    \n
    \nIt went well.
    \n
    \nI started with a mental challenge of tracking the Vikings after they kidnapped his buddy late at night.
    \nMy brother decided to bring dogs and a huge hunting party.
    \nThere was a boat pursuit at one point, and the Vikings tried to encloud themselves in smoke by lighting special smoke torches.
    \n
    \nIt turned out to be not much of a mental challenge and more of a physical one, by the end, but that was okay.
    \n
    \nThe second and final challenge was storming the Viking camp and just taking names in a massive way.
    \n
    \nOverall, he had a good time, I think.
