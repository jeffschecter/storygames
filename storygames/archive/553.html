I'm running a Meat Loaf-inspired LARP at Maycon this year. You can imagine the set-up: it's a biker rally, there'll be lots of sex, there'll be bike races.

I need a very rules-light system and I'm hoping you guys could help me.

What I need is either: a, serious suggestions of useful mechanics I could use or b. jokey suggestions of mechanics based on Meat Loaf songs. Oh, and characters. You can suggest characters too.

So, for example, here's a mechanic: you can Want one person, you can Need one person and you can Love one person, but there ain't no way you're ever going to be able to do all three.

Any ideas would be really useful.

    Don't be surprised, Larry. The work of the Loaf contains some rich, detailed storytelling, ideal for live-action roleplaying.

    Here's what I have so far.

    Everyone starts off with a hand of cards. The red cards represent Love and the black cards Rock. The aim of the game is to get as many Love cards or Rock cards as possible.

    You can give Love cards to other people by having sex with them, marrying them and so on.

    You can claim Rock cards from other people by winning bike races or fighting.

    At the end of the game, the character with the most Love cards is saved by love, and rides off into the sunset, with another character of his choice. The character with the most Rock cards dies gloriously in a motorcycle accident, and the last thing he sees is his heart, still beating, breaking out of his body, like a bat out of hell.

    All the other characters are condemned to spend the rest of their lives in the god-forsaken town in which they live.

    How's that? Is there anything else I need?

    Graham

    Oh, the system? How do you resolve 'rock' conflicts (and love ones too, for that matter)? It seems like the cards should be used here too.
    It does, doesn't it? At a simple level, you could get each player to draw a random card from their hand. Highest card wins the draw. That's very simple, it doesn't interrupt the play too much and (as a bonus) it gives an incentive for people to give away their low cards.

    I'm not sure what a Love conflict would be, though. I mean, of course you can imagine a Love conflict with two bikers competing for the same girl, but that would always involve another PC.

    The biggest problem I'm having is working out a mechanic for the bike races. It needs to be a central event, so everyone in the room needs to be involved. And it needs to be exciting. And it can't just be resolved on one card draw. And I want room for dirty tricks: not just everyone driving fast but people kicking each other off bikes.

    Hi Graham,

    Meat Loaf (well, Steinman in general) fan and LARPer from way back here...

    Extended conflicts like bike races should be best out of three. Because two out of three ain't bad.

    Guitars should be usable as weapons -- even lethal, with the right power cords and the precise angle from which to strike -- even though that's no way to treat an expensive musical instrument.

    So many Meat Loaf songs are about being a horny frustrated teenager, your Love card play should really be expanded into a simulation mini-game that's an obvious metaphor for sex. (Sex can't just be a simple automatically-successful card transaction, because when the rubber meets the road, there's no such thing as safe sex. You can end up left in the dark, or out of the frying pan and into the fire.) I'd suggest basing it on baseball. (Are you familiar with the card game Congress?) Player-characters might do anything for love, but will they do that? And is there a way to make love out of nothing at all?

    There should also be a heaven in the game, because again, so many songs refer to it. Good players go to heaven, but the bad players go everywhere. There's no time limit, because heaven can wait. Those who don't want to go to heaven might be looking for an original sin instead, and end up bad for good. Or they'll just be all revved up with no place to go. (And if they have motorcycles, they can use them to go nowhere fast. Leading, possibly, back to that pit in the blazing sun...)

    This sounds like it could be great fun for all the wasted youth, lost boys and golden girls alike.

    - Walt

    Ben, yes, the idea of taking the loser's cards is very strong. It also meshes well with another idea I have: that the gang leader starts with all the Kings, his girlfriend with all the Queens and so on down the pecking order. So, at the start of the game, they'll win all their conflicts. Later on, they'll start losing.

    Jessica, the idea of running around the stations is nice, but I'd like something a little more narrated, I think.

    What I'm thinking at the moment is that there's five stages to the race. Everyone in the race writes what they're trying to do on a large piece of card and holds it up: "Drives really fast", "Knocks Mick off his bike". At the same time, I hold up a card with any road conditions that might affect the race: "Oil slick", "Chicane".

    Then everyone who's not in the race get to decide how all that plays out: who succeeds, who fails and who overtakes who.

    Larry, I hope you're quoting from the Loaf in order to learn from him, not to disrespect his work.

    Walt, your ideas are fantastic. Heaven (and hell) could definitely be in the game. And another major theme is dying and coming back, especially in Objects In The Rear View Mirror. And I really like that, because it provides an incentive for players to narrate their own deaths and then come back as ghosts or similar.

    Graham
  • Graham
    April 2006 edited April 2006
    Oh, a sex minigame. I haven't even thought of that. Superb.

    Yes, it needs to be a baseball metaphor. The obvious idea is: the man plays a Love card and so does the woman. If the man's is higher, he gets to First Base. Otherwise, he strikes out. Repeat for Second Base, Third Base and a home run.

    The only question is how the cards are played. The cards could be selected randomly from the hand, in which case all Ben's ideas about dilution of hands come into play. Or - and I think this is more interesting - they can play any Love card in their hand. That lets the woman effectively decide if she want the sex to stop early.

    And, at the end, the man keeps the woman's cards and vice versa.

    You also need to make sure that, if the couple fail at having sex the first time, they don't immediately try again. So you could say that the man has to wait 30 minutes before trying again.

    Graham
