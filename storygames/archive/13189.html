    \n Holy shit! People read my blog? That's news to me.

    Yeah, um. Doing the research that resulted in the list of links was one of the most unpleasant things I've ever done. I felt angry and out-of-sorts for weeks afterwards, especially after all of the material that I found about sexual harassment. (The fact that I needed to break that category into sub-headings is so depressing.)

    Responding to stuff people said in the Stuff to Watch thread:\n
    Posted By: ColinCWow...that really pulls things together. I play in two groups, both of which are (I feel) pretty sensitive in our portrayals of women in the fiction (the all-guy group more so than the mixed one, huh...), so this was eye-opening.
    \nHonestly, the treatment of women that I've experienced in the indie games community has been mostly positive. That's not to say we get a pass. The sexism of excluding women as protagonists is something that both men and women are guilty of (myself included). But I've certainly never encountered anything as cringe-worthy as things like EA Fail or the Jade Raymond incident within the indie community.\n
    Posted By: ColinCFirst thing that popped into my head when I read the editor's response from Dragon Magazine: 'Ah, ok. So basically Dragon is FHM or Maxim for geeks. It all makes total sense now.'
    \nOn the one hand, it makes sense. I mean, they're running a business, right? But on the other hand, it's definitely a disappointing response.
    \n
    Posted By: jenskotAgreed! I bet it wouldn't be too hard to get funding and support for a book.
    \nI started swearing at Simon through email when I realized that this probably is a book. I even have an outline on my hard drive and a pretty monstrous collection of awful game ads that I've been collecting. The only thing that puts me off is the huge issue of copyright, since a lot of the images I need to cite are pretty clearly copyrighted and I don't imagine the large and very rich companies who own the games in question would be too happy with me.
