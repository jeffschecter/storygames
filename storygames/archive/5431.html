\n So I will send here some monsters, NPCs and game prep stuff from my RBH games. And some pictures, because it's what I like to do.
I'd like people to do the same thing and post their stuff here so that we can have a kind of actually tested monsters and some insight on game prep. Drawings are totally optional. Doodles are welcome as usual.

First game : the heart ripping gargoyle


The Old Market has a lot of gargoyles but only one wakes at night to rip the heart from people touched with magic.
Actual play thread here : http://www.story-games.com/forums/comments.php?DiscussionID=4838&page=1

The gargoyle :
Size +3
Awarness : +3
Sneakiness : +6
Other attributes : +0
Weapons :
Claws (Heavy)
Tail (Ranged)
Armor :
Light
Powers :
Unliving (15 HP instead of 10)
Disguised (The monster can pass as something armless : automatic surprise on the initiative)
Please note that we were running with the first iteration of the rules and that I used a personal version of the attributes and the monsters powers of this version.
The creazy hit points load was lame because it made the fight drag on. I found in later game that it was better to have a monster with a good armor than with too much hit points.

For this game I came up with some backstory using Vincent Baker's oracles. Then I made the NPCs "grabby", in need of the visibly capable PCs or wanting them far from their busyness

Setting and backstory : an Old Market at a crossroad. The PCs are mercenaries seeking some employment with merchants. Murders start the first night. People are found dead, heart ripped. All the victims are magic users. There is a merchant possessed by the ghost of a wizard. This man commited suicide because of a love despair but cannot go because of guilt : he left behind him his animated gargoyle that has started to murder magic-users for their heart. He thinks it's because it needs magic to sustain itself. In fact the creature feels lonely and want to create a mate. So there are in fact 2 gargoyles. But the second one is not ready yet and will only be when the PCs kill the first one and start to feel safe.
To complicate the matters : the merchant has a wife who quickly realised that her husband was not her husband anymore because he doesn't beat her anymore. He also has a brother who realised something was wrong when his brother did not recognise him. The man is ruthless and has a lot of mercenaries following his orders but he doesn't want to hurt his brother so he went to find an exorcist on the market. The ghost want the help of the PCs to kill the gargoyle. The wife wants the help of the PCs with the brother problem. She wants to keep her man with the ghost inside.

The monster hunting and fighting part was realy great. Lots of crazy tactics. Players really immersed and all.

I did wrong with the treasure part in this game. It was a jewelled dagger that some NPCs had stolen from the dead wizard. The ghost told them who was the NPC and told them they could keep it if they could take it. It was lame from the start and it felt in game. It would have been better for exemple to have the brother possess something of great value.

I'll come back with more monsters. Now I am back to work. Your turn.

    \n The Sons of the Goat God.



    Basicaly, they are like Runequest's Broos : male humanoids with a goat head and a solid craving for sex and rape. This is one of my favorite monster of all time. They are realy monsters in the RBH sense : "creatures that do terrible things with terrible consequences".
    But in fact they came back too me through the Burning Wheel's Monster Burner and its Satyr. I was flipping, searching monsters ideas and ther was this thing about the satyrs that sometimes a village would offer them their virgins to ensure good crops. The core of my idea was there.
    You can see the actual play thread here.


    As I had 4 PCs, I stated 2 bosses, the Goat God Priest and the Champion and there was also 16 standard mooks.
    I wanted the monsters to be realy strong.

    The priest :
    Size : +3
    Awarness : +4
    Stubborness : +4
    Eloquence : +3
    Daring : +2
    Sneakyness : +1
    Weapons : Heavy (horns), Reach (Iron headed quaterstaff)
    Armor : light (lots of tattered leather clothes) (heavy with the reach weapon)
    Powers : Bloodlust, Minions, Secondary Weapon (the staff), Many headed (in fact, it could make a free attack with the horns), fast.

    The Champion :
    Size : +3
    Awarness : +3
    Stubborness : +2
    Eloquence : +0
    Daring : +4
    Sneakyness : +2
    Weapons : Heavy (horns), Reach (Spear)
    Armor : Very Heavy (Plate Armor) (\xdcber armor with the reach weapon)
    Powers : Vicious, Secondary Weapon (the spear), Many headed (in fact, it could make a free attack with the horns), fast. I had also added "Internal fire" but I dropped it almost immediatly because the monsters were already too powerfull.

    The Champion was a panzer division in itself : it could have 4 actions in one turn, at it's best : 1 normal action + 1 free attack (Many headed) + 1 move (fast) + 1 show-off (vicious with a successfull attack).
