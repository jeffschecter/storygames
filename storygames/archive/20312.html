\n While I love Little Fears (Nightmare Edition), there are two things I would like to play differently today:
1. Even more simplified system.
2. Even less dramatic setting.

1. Little Fears has a neat dice system and interesting Belief mechanics. Monster creation also works pretty well.
However, I'd like to simplify it even more. I can think of a lot of things ranging from Fate Accelerated, Monster of the Week (AW) hack to Dread.

I've actually built a Fate Accelerated (FAE) conversion:
- Fate Points for belief & monster fear token
- standard FAE approaches (on skill level of children)
- Fight: Attack (Damage: Stress, Physical Consequences)
- Grab/Chase: Create Advantage
- Scare: Attack (Damage: Stress, Mental Consequences)
- Spirit Draining: Attack (Damage: Stress, Spiritual Consequences)

Still, I would like to keep it even more light and story-focused, more rewarding for GM?


2. Even though the Nightmare Edition made it toned down compared to the original version, the Little Fears setting is still too powerless and terrifying to me esp. for playing one-shots.

I was thinking of playing it like a Dream World. A Shaman has taught a group of children dream walking. At night, when sleeping, they wander through a shared dream world and set out for missions. When they die in the dream, they simply wake-up and get to eat their pancakes & cereal for breakfast.

What do you think?

    \n Hasimir, the 1st edition was a very bold immersion into the world of childhood nightmares. It created this sense of childhood powerlessness surprisingly well and won some awards, I think. Yet, it was also quite disturbing because it included quite traumatizing child experiences. For a lot of people (including me), this made the game too hard to play with a regular RPG group.
    The 2nd edition (Nightmare edition) substantially toned down the disturbing elements (and polished the mechanics). For me, this became a more realistic game for a usual RPG crowd. Of course, at the same time, the setting lost some of its "oh my God, this is terrifyingly real" sensation which attracted a lot of people in the first place.
