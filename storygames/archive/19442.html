\n Perhaps you do. Fastaval, held in Hobro, Denmark, is one of the hard cores of Nordic roleplaying, with a strong, supportive and fast-developing community for writing games for serious entertainment. Fastaval's claim to fame is the prestigious Otto competition and the tradition of creatively ambitious chamber larps driven by the competition, but the convention supports a wide variety of games, and it's not always the obvious high art that wins all prizes.

For years now Fastaval has sported a significant minority of foreign visitors, including foreign game contributors. This brings in ideas from abroad, and has also helped spread Nordic/Fastaval-esque inspiration far and wide. We really like it this way, and we have an elite c\xf8rps of tr\xe5nsl\xe5t\xf8rs to smooth the way for foreign visitors and help make sure that most games have english-language runs. Whether you write or not, we want you to come.

The next Fastaval is during Easter 2015, 1.-5. of April. That's quite a way off. Rather closer is the deadline for submission of synopses for games for Fastaval 2015. We'll be back later with details about what a synopsis does and doesn't entail (brief version; more than an elevator pitch, a lot less than your full game, what we want is for you to give us a concrete notion of what your game will be like, so we can pick and choose on an informed basis).

Right now we're asking you to dream up ideas for Fastaval games. Games from one to six hours in length, unless you have a brilliant idea for a 15 minute game or a five day game, in which case we'll listen. Usually they involve five to seven people sitting (or standing) in a room, but again, we'll take a look at whatever ideas you have. Fastaval games tend to be kind of light on crunchy mechanics, unless they aren't. Games can be about just about any topic, and as serious or lighthearted as you like. We demand only that you be serious about making it good.

Eventually, the game will need to be written down so people who are not you can run it, and we'd really like for you to come to Fastaval yourself. Of course, something could come up, but Fastaval will get more out of your game if you're there to speak for it, or at least send a qualified ambassador. And so will you. We want you to plan to come to Fastaval.

What we offer at this point is help. If you're a Fastaval rookie and you think writing for Fastaval sounds intriguing, and you have an idea but you're not too sure how to make it work for Fastaval, write us and we'll hook you up with an experienced Fastaval writer as your sparring partner. Feel free to ask away here, and if you're interested, write us at scenarieansvarlig[at]gmail[dot]com, and we'll have a sparring partner get back to you.

    \n As someone who did Fastaval this year, I am going to strongly recommend taking advantage of this offer for anyone who wants to design tabletop, freeform, or LARP scenarios for an international audience.

    Fastaval is an amazing convention for designers, and has a strong tradition of producing quality games. At the same time, they also have a strong desire to improve, expand, and drive their own state of the art forward with new ideas from new voices.

    Even if you can\u2019t attend, Fastaval does some really nifty things for designers. Your game will get played multiple times in short order by a lot of different players, and they will almost all give you written feedback. If you actually can be there in person, they\u2019ll almost all give you feedback face to face as well.

    Mo\u2019s game \u2013 Run Them Again \u2013 got run 5 times at Fastaval, and we got feedback from 22+ players and 4 STs. The feedback was all solid, and ranged for short to the point concerns and specific areas that needed development to long talks full of tears about how the scenario touched individual\u2019s lives because of overlap with things they were going through in real life. This level of feedback, and the way it enables you as a designer to see both where feedback clusters and where it diverges across groups and players, is an amazing resource.

    The con is also a really good place to get international exposure. Since Fastaval Mo has been contacted by folks in Denmark, Sweden, Finland, Germany, and Ireland about Run Them Again, running it at different cons, and if/when she\u2019s doing a final for sale release candidate. (The Fastaval version will be up for free on the Fastaval Scenario database.)

    Finally, Troels himself is an amazing guy. There are a lot of really experienced, skillful scenario and LARPwrites who are willing to act as sparring partners, advisors, and general help in order to bridge the gap between the Danish tradition and various North American traditions. These folks are serious about not leaving you alone and working with you got get a quality scenario done.

    Those of you who know me know I rarely say nice things. I\u2019m saying nice things about Fastaval, and about this offer from Troels. Jump all over this.

    \n How detailed do the proposals have to be? Should they be more or less actual drafts, as in Game Chef, or would the following example do?

    Two Tribes
    Two tribes is a game about what happens when two groups of people- whether they be two influential families in a Renaissance Court, two gangs in South Central LA, or two companies producing similar products in a shrinking market- rub up against each other.

    Players take on the roles of members of one of these two groups, who have to struggle to survive in a harsh and unforgiving environment, dealing with external challenges as well as the day-to-day running of their organisation, including tackling dissent, rebellion and rivalries within the group.

    \n Sliberty: The games of Fastavals past are chronicled here: http://alexandria.dk/data?conset=1 Just click on the links for the individual years to see what was played in any given year. If there's a little disk symbol to the left of the game, it's available for download, and for more recent years, many are, including a fair number in English.

    catty_big: Somewhere in between, a page or two in length. We need to understand in *concrete* terms what play will be like, but we'll likely be getting 60+ proposals, so we don't read whole games. But the deadline for proposals isn't until September 7th, and we'll put out more detailed proposals guidelines when we have more of our ducks in a row regarding some technical details of scheduling of games and the like. For now, worry more about your game and how to make it work in a Fastaval context, and less about the synopsis/proposal itself.
