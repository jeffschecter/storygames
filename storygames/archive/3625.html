\n I used to play D&D, WoD and Ars Magica back in the day. About a year ago I tried to pick up D&D again with some old gaming friends (we're all in our 40s now), but the magic didn't come back. So we went back to our evenings of "German" boardgames. But hope springs eternal, and now we're trying to put together an Ars Magica game, with better success.
\n
\nIn the process of researching the game, I stumbled upon this site and became introduced to the SG concept. I'm interested in trying it out with my friends for an evening's play, and I have an idea for a 50s pulp horror/sci-fi setting, high school students confronting some menace that's escaped from the labs/crypts. Any recommendations for a system? I could just use The Pool, but I was hoping to find something a bit more fleshed out.
\n

    \n Well, my immediate thought would be Dead of Night. It's all about recreating horror movies, particularly those filled with the cliches and tropes of movie horror. I don't think you could go far wrong with giving that one a try.

    DoN would sem to me, through the way it works (in that bringing in cliches like the plucky girl going into the lab on her own) that it would support the kind of pulp SF horror you're looking for.
