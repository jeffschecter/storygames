The die; dangers and possibilities

\n
\n
\n
\n\nTomasHVM \n\n\n

\n\nJune 2008 \n edited June 2008 in Story Games
\n

\n
\n
\n The die is universally (lol) accepted as a nice tool to use for randomizing the outcome of conflicts. This is especially true for classical roleplaying games like Warhammer, Drakar & Demoner, Fabula and GURPS.

However: there may be inherent dangers in trusting too much in the die, especially for the would-be designers of the games of tomorrow. As the die has been used a lot, it may be that old ways of using it will produce "old" gameplay, and nothing fresh. It may be that the use of dice is a bad design-choice in new games altogether.

Still: the die is a very effective and applicable tool, that makes random results with little colour and great rapidity (a die is neutral in itself, and easy to read). There may still be ways of die-rolling that has not been explored yet, totally fresh ways of using the die. There may be ways of designing the die itself that will make it a new tool.

This thread is for thinking aloud on what dice may be/do in future games, and for examples of how dice have been used in more modern games. Post any thoughts you have on it! No strings attached!

You can pace your game based on who you play with - shorter games for children.
