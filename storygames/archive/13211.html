    \n Dread House is also fantastic! \n
    Posted By: stupidgremlinWondering how the thing plays, mechanically, more or less.
    \nIt's like an old text adventure game from the 1980's except the computer is replaced by a person and the software is replaced by a map and an outline of the adventure.

    One person, the parser, relays the game info to the players. They describe what the players see, the results of the player's actions, keeps track of the player's location, inventory, and score.

    To start, the parser welcomes everyone to the game, states the player's starting location, what they see, objects in the area, and the exits.

    "Welcome to Action Castle! You are standing in a cottage. There is a fishing pole here. Exits are: out."

    Then players take turns issuing commands. Commands are usually one or two words (a verb and noun)... Go west. Climb tree. Smell rose. Wear crown. But you can get as complex as... give fish to troll. The game tells the parser what commands are acceptable, what to say if they are, and sometimes what to say if they aren't. There are basic commands including... go, exit, leave, enter, east, west, look, examine, search, take, talk, kill, wear, use. Special commands including... help, inventory, save, load, score, quit.

    After players issue commands, play passes to the next person. Everyone takes turns playing the same character. And often you don't even know who you are playing at first! But the game usually gives you clues if you look through your starting inventory, interact with other people, look in a mirror and more!

    Each game has unique winning and losing conditions. And often there are multiple ways to win and lose. There is also a score total that lets you know if you've unlocked everything as you can win without exploring every corner of the game.

    It's a live puzzle game that you play with a crowd of people. It's like karaoke... more about entertaining everyone than singing well.

    I hope that helps!
