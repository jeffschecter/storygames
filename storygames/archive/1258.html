Are exchanges a good idea?

Graham
September 2006 edited September 2006 in Story Games
In Review Exchange, Jason raised the question about whether exchanging reviews was a good idea. Could we discuss that here?

(Colin, I hope this doesn't seem as though we're taking pot shots at your idea. The review exchange is a nice idea, but I think Jason's got a point, and it's worth discussing.)

It's fairly well-established, I think, that game designers "exchange" the work they do. I playtest your game; you return the favour by playtesting mine. You send me your game to read through and, a few months later, I ask you to read through mine.

And you do it with promoting games. You link to my web site; I link to yours.

Does this become problematic when we're doing things, like reviews, that are meant to be impartial? You and I agree to review each other's games. I post a nice review of your game. Now, you've played my game and you didn't actually like it, but you feel bad posting a nasty review: so you post a generally positive review and suppress your criticism to a brief paragraph at the end.

Or doesn't it matter? After all, this kind of thing probably happens anyway: you link to my game in your blog, I return the favour by posting a positive Actual Play report. Is exchanging reviews just a formalisation of the process?

(Jason, is this right or have I misrepresented your argument?)

I'm interested in what people think and, if this sort of thing has been tried before, how it has worked.

Graham

    Well, personally, I would think that a person who wrote an insincere review praising the few things that they could pretend to like about my game had completely failed me. I'd much rather that they posted a sincere review saying "This game does the following things, very strongly. I don't like those things, and so I didn't like the game."

    First: I'd rather have honest criticism than insincere praise.

    Second: Honest criticism generates a **lot** of sales. People say "Oh! *You* don't like the things that game does, but I think I very well might! And since you're slamming it, I lend more authority to your opinion, because I feel its sincerity."

    So if the idea is "Let's trade positive reviews!" then yeah, I think it's transparently a bad idea. It will neither generate healthy commentary nor generate sales.

    If the idea is "Let's trade insightful, honest reviews!" then that sounds like a spiffy idea.

    Full disclosure is definitely the way to go, as others have said. People will get angry, and justifiably so, if there is a hidden level they're not privy to. This way, they can ignore or interpret at leisure.

    Do I like the entire idea? Well, I really want to see more impartial reviews of indie games by the indie community (I've already said so in the Lumpley Kerfuffle thread), and though I'm not entirely comfortable with this "doing reviews to get credit to ultimately get exposure for your own game" approach, it does get things a little further on. As is, we only get indie community members reviewing when they already like the game and want to push it forward (which is totally to be expected); via this review group, we will see informed reviews by people who may not enjoy the game, even if they may soften the criticism because of group and norming pressures.
