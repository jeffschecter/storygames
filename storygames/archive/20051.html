\n What if all basic moves were done using an alternate attribute?
Here are the basic moves for this alternate Dungeon World (CON and WIS attributes are dropped). Now, all i have to do is to make up a setting

in which this moves are fictionally supported. For one thing, all main characters in this world are psykers, plot armor is best armor, fix

technology with brute force and are able to summon spirits.
What kind of enemies and troubles should they face?
which goals they use to pursue?
Should they be human after all?

TINKER (STR)

Whenever you try to kick down a lock, or handle technology by hitting it, roll + str.
On a 10+, you do it, no problem.
On a 7-9, you still do it, but the GM will offer you two options between a cost, danger, and suspicion.


LOCKING MINDS (INT)

Whenever you confront someone using your psychic powers, roll +int.
On a 10, you get a hold on your opponent, you deal damage or you can ask the GM any question about your foe; he'll answer you truthfully, and

you take +1 when acting on the answer. You deal +1d6 damage by exposing to a soft move.

On a 7-9, you still do it, but you're vulnerable to the enemy's mind: GM will give you two options between damage, debility or giving

information to your foe.

WITHSTAND DAMAGE (CHA)

Whenever you must endure a situation, or are left at 0 HP, roll +cha.
On a 10+, you scratch away the damage by saying a cool one-liner or revealing an unexpected advantage (make up one). If you have 0 HP or

less, raise it to 1. On a 7-9, the GM may impose a cost, or make you choose between two.

SUMMONING (DEX)

Whenever you dance from start to finish one of the 1000 dances, roll +dex
On a 10+, the spirit whose dance you've performed will answer a demand within it's domain.
On a 7-9, the GM will offer you two options between overexertion, costs, attracting troubles or other spirits, or retaining control.
On a miss, the spirit may use this chance you to fulfill his own agenda; with or without taking control over your body.


DEFY DANGER

When you act despite an imminent threat or suffer a calamity, you roll...

...+INT if you use pure thought or your mind force.
...+CHA if you claim a relevant quirk or invulnerability (Make up one: If you achieve a hit, things are like you say, at least for this time).
...+DEX if you rely on your agility
...+STR if you use your raw force.

On a 10+, you do what you set out to, the threat doesn\u2019t come to bear.
On a 7-9, you stumble, hesitate, or flinch: the GM will offer you a worse outcome, hard bargain, or ugly choice.

    \n I've edited the first post with the clean moves, to save some screen space. Do you like this approach?

    I'm also thinking on other way to use INT as the main combat mod: By the means of RIDDLE DUELS!

    The move should be something like:

    "When you ask or are asked a world riddle staring in the eyes of yor foe in a clear riddle contest, speak your question-answer and roll+INT.

    On a 10+, your answer is right (yes, whatever answer you said, that's the answer, your GM will make it work!) or your opponent failed to answer your riddle. This means that you roll your damage your opponent must grant you a demand, or be dishonored.
    On a 7-9, your answer is only partly right; or your question feels somewhat cheap and tricky. You win this time, but someone will hold a grudge against you; or it takes a cost or a promise to assure your victory.
    On a miss, you fail your answer (no matter how fitting you thought it was, your GM will build the world around it) or the opponent guesses your answer. Now you ow a demand to your adversary or face dishonor.
    Riddles in this game are more questions about the fictional world rather than complicated wordplays. This way, you also worldbuild.
    Example:
    NPC: "What there is in the top of the highest peak of the black mountains?"
    PC: "A chair"
    -rolls a 3-
    NPC: "Not any longer. I took that chair last winter and now there is only snow. You ow me a boon: get out of my yard and don't you ever come back"

    As a note: being dishonored is a status. All dishonored PCs suffer -1 to all rolls until they redeem somehow. All rolls against a dishonored NPC have a +1.


    So, which one is cooler? we go with psykers or riddle contests?

    \n A new idea for basic move that may fit this setting (I love using forums as sketchbooks!):

    KISMET

    When you act temerariously, putting your faith upon your star, roll+luck (yes, a new attribute!)
    On a 10+, fate had set for you to succeed in your task
    On a 7-9, you succeed in your task, but fate had spared you a bittersweet truth; or there are other costs.
    On a miss, you'll behold the hand of fate, and it may be terrible.

    By only explanation, i'll say that yes, it may sound like a catch-all, but it isn't. Is meant to represent how favorable are the stars to the PC, and also to be risky. It will usually imply the player activelly saying that he puts his faith in his star; and though it potentially perform miracles when all odds are against one, it also has a heavy backslash, as the GM has a lot of freedom picking the fitting hard move on a miss, because "it's fate".
