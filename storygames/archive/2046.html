Help me pick a domain name for Abulafia to live on

Dave Younce
December 2006 edited December 2006 in Directed Promotion
Those of you who've been around here a while probably know about my wiki/random generator mashup Abulafia, named for the computer in Foucalt's Pendulum, and by extension Abraham Abulafia. If you don't know it, check it out - theres lots of random generators there, and you can join and add to them. The biggest problems with the site currently are the slow hosting, sometimes intermittent database connections, and it sits on my only registered domain, gheron.net, which has no meaning (except as a d&d campaign world for <10 people) and is hard to remember.

I want to grow Abulafia, both as a site and as a community in the coming year, and it seems like step one of that will be moving it to its own domain with better hosting (the current site is sometimes torturously slow). All the obvious names - abulafia.com, abulafia.net, randomgenerator.com, etc are, of course, taken. Here are the possibilities that still look open:

abulafiawiki.com
story-generator.com
random-generator.com
storygenwiki.com

My concern about the hyphened ones is that people will forget the hyphen and end up on someone elses site - Andy, was that a concern for story-games?

Anyway, I'm hoping people here will vote on one of the above names (or suggest a new available domain name) based on
-which is easiest to remember?
-which best fits Abulafia?
-which do you like best for unquantifiable reasons?

Also, I'm thinking of using dreamhost.com for hosting. Anyone have experience with them, or particularly good experiences doing php/mysql hosting with another company?

Thanks.

    Rob

    page limit recommendation in mediawiki is about 32K. But! here's a trick: split it up into a few 'feeder pages' with significant chunks of your subtable on them, and then do the main table on another page. I'm a little unclear on what tags from IMDB you're talking about, so here's another example using names:

    you'd make a 'Rob Names A' page containing an

    ;main
    1,Andrew
    1,Alistair
    ...

    and so on down the line as necessary to avoid overload.

    then on your 'Rob Names' page you just have your top-level table with choices like

    ;main
    1,[Rob Names A.main]
    1,[Rob Names B.main]
    and so forth.

    That ought to avoid any problems. Thanks for the consideration - anxious to see just what you're generating.

    I find that using a hyphen doesn't cause too much trouble, and scores you access to tons more available domain names.

    IMO, adding a hyphen between two words or word fragments is far more ideal than not using hyphens but smashing two not-quite-so-related words or fragments together.

    Ex: I can remember story-generator.com or story-gen.com or the like. If I forget the hyphen and get to a page of horse porn, I slap my forehead and go "Oh yeah, Hyphen" and Never Forget Again. However, on "storyraffle" I'd be forgetting the "raffle" part every time I went hunting for that page.

    -Andy
