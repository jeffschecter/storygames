Alternate Universe RPGs

Jared A. Sorensen
January 2007 edited January 2007 in Story Games
I just got back from a parallel dimension. It was weird.

What was wierder was the WebNet (their version of our Internet) story games forum (also called Story-Games.com).

Games I saw:

Polaris: a game about snowmobile and other tracked and ski-based vehicles on an ice planet. Kinda like Blue Planet crossed with Hoth from SW: TESB.

The Shab-Al-Hiri Roach: a game where you play stoner high school students... kind of like Dazed and Confused: the RPG.

Burning Wheel: an Old West game about settlers vs. Indians

Dogs in the Junkyard: this reminded me of Wick's Cat game crossed with Mechaton... intra-pack warfare between canine gangs. It was a miniatures RPG.

Half-Time Adventures: A Football RPG? Yup, had to be seen to be believed. Good team-building system and combat reminscent of Agon. From the same company that did Octane (a NASCAR-inspired RPG). What next? Umpire: the Requiem?

- J

    The Sorcerer RPG, where you have to deliver a truckload of dynamite across the Andes. Conflict resolution is Jenga-based -- the tower falls, you all die. An Intense Role-Playing Game.

    Capes, a thrilling game about New England headlands fisherman. Risk the hazards of mother nature as you try to collect enough fish to support your family. The catch is good, but do you deserve it? It's sort of "The Perfect Storm" RPG. (In this universe, that movie was directed by David Cronenburg, but that's another topic.)

    Also, after waiting for years to get the title from some high-concept storygame with a detailed mythological setting, the makers of WarhammerQuest have finally released their long-awaited tabletop miniatures dungeon crawl, HeroQuest, to much critical acclaim. It's a real milestone in games publishing.

    Agon (pronounced "A gun"), a gritty game of glory hungry bank robbers who start out with a great plan but end up pointing guns at each other. Features the "range disk", a round peice of paper that shows how close the other characters are to shooting you in the head.

    Prince's Kingdom, the official licensned game of the musician formerly known as Prince. This game is actually quite fun, including world building, a competitive campaign system, and self-correcting character design, however it is marred by the fact that Prince has way higher stats than anyone else. By an amazing conincidence, in this world, Prince's name is an unprounceable glyph that looks exactly like the characters "D&D".

    Blue Planet, the game of self-medication.

    Spirit of the Century, the game of 1920's moonshine smugglers and the women that love them.

    Mecahtongue: a heavy metal remake of Hungry Hungry HIppos.
