\n So, I've been watching this relatively new anime series called "Ga-Rei-Zero," about spiritually-sensitive secret agents fighting phantoms and spirits that seek to harm humanity. It's going to be one of those rare ones that I'll probably give away to one of my friends when I'm done with it, because of some of the disturbing imagery of violence (I've got some major issues against brutal violence against women...to the point of feeling ill sometimes...but this isn't supposed to be some Freudian psychological examination of my problems).

But anyway, the point of this thread is some inspiration I received from the start of the show. The first two episodes are actually, chronologically, near the end of the series, with one of the main characters brutally slaughtering former teammates, friends, acquaintances and innocents, up until the point where she's about to strike down the girl she considers a sister. But before we find out what happens, time rewinds to a few years earlier, and progresses with how the girls met, grew up, worked with their secret agency to fight the phantoms, eventually leading up to the inevitable corruption of one of them, and the horrible events that we see in the first two episodes.
And so I was thinking, that would make for an interesting start to a game campaign. Well, not so much the brutal slaughter and horrible imagery, but to start a campaign at a point where two or more of the characters are in direct conflict with the others, and tensions have come to a critical point where some drastic decision must be made...and then rewind maybe several years. The players would know that something will go wrong, and they won't know what. They won't immediately know who might be in the wrong (or if there is a wrong...maybe they're being played against each other by outside forces? maybe they're actually trying to just pretend to deceive someone? they won't know for sure...all speculation).

So, here's the question: is there a way that such a campaign can actually be accomplished? Has anyone actually tried something like this? Are there tricks or ways the campaign should be handled to make this possible? Is there a game system that would work well with this concept?

...I'm just kinda wondering. Or, if this concept has been brought up before in a thread, please point me in the right direction.

    \nWell, yeah, we yuk it up a lot in our group, but I think you could use it for drama too:

    * The last time something happens is dramatic.
    * The first time something happens is dramatic if you imply it will happen again, people will start looking for it.
    * Something that will lead to something else is dramatic. ("At the time their animosity was apparently natural. Their later love affair would not invalidate this, unlike what some others have said...")
    * Something that is controversial is dramatic. ("He DROPPED the blaster, it was NOT shot out of his hand, the evidence is UNDENIABLE and ANYONE who thinks differently is a FOOL")

    And so on.
