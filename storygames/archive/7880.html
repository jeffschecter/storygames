    \n A Taste for Murder is fantastic.
    \n
    \nIt requires minimal prep. Which is all done together. I'd say less than 30 minutes. I haven't seen the written rules but Graham was able to describe them to us in less than 10 minutes. And you could probably complete a game in easily 2 sessions, possibly 1.
    \n
    \nIt's as easy as Zombie Cinema or 3:16. The playing in character quotient is very, very high. Tactical elements are very subtle but there are definitely ways to game it if so desired.
    \n
    \nBesides printed materials, I believe all you need is a bunch of d6s with 2 being unique colors.
    \n
    \nIt will probably be the easiest and most fun game you've playtested! Do it!

    \n Thank you all.

    Pete, I'm trying to think what else I can tell you. Basically, there's this family. Over the course of the game, their relationships are revealed to be more and more fucked up, until they provide motives for murder. Also, there's a random weather table.

    Adam, the minimum number of players is probably four.

    Brendan, I'm not quite sure what you mean by standard tabletop. The game can take six players. It's done seven or eight players before, and it doesn't break, but it's a bit crowded.

    Oh, Seth is playtesting it at the moment, so there'll be a delay while I incorporate all his feedback into the rules.

    Graham
