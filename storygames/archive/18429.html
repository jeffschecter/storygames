\n This sounds like a thread from the Forge that would have been killed by Ron within seconds. But here goes.

I have always wanted to use drinks, on the gaming table, for resolution. Whether it's a cup of coffee or a glass of wine, I'd like to use players' drinks to resolve conflicts.

However, I don't want to create a drinking game. And neither do I want to create an incentive for drinking, nor one for not drinking. (So, for example, if you resolved conflicts by comparing the levels of liquid in the glass? If the emptiest glass wins, that creates an incentive to drink: you can win by downing your drink. If the fullest glass wins, that creates an incentive to sit there and never touch your drink.)

So what can we do?

    \n In Morgan Stinson's game Fedora Noir, there are a number of props: a fedora, three shot glasses, a cigarette pack down to the final three, and a (fake) gun.

    While the detective is smoking, no one can end the scene and the detective can't do first-person dialogue. When you want a scene to keep rolling without having to answer anyone's nosy questions, smoke a cigarette.

    Whenever the detective takes a drink, "the hat" player gets muted for the rest of the scene. See, the detective is actually played by two players: one plays the drunken chimney of a private eye, and the other plays "the hat" - his keen crime-solving mind. Take a drink when you want to shut your smart half up, so you can better focus on dames and personal issues and the like.

    Whenever the detective draws his gun, it's because he heard the sound of armed men approaching. A fight breaks out!

    ***

    So that's one way to incorporate drinks: give props and specific player actions mechanical effects. When you the player take a drink, you seemlessly communicate an in-game effect.

    \n You can use it to send signals.

    - Raising the glass could mean that you want to say something.
    - Sipping the glass that you have no idea where your imagination is going with this one, so anyone can take over when they feel like it.
    - Banging the glass in the table means that you concur or that you just want to show your appreciation for what the others are playing out right now.

    Just some examples of ideas that I'm throwing out there without any thought behind it. I would limit to signals to having 3-4 at most.
