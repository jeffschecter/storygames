Die Bias

Jason_Morningstar
August 2006 edited August 2006 in Story Games
Last night Me, Clinton, and Remi were talking about Hero's Banner, which we are going to play next week. We're all pretty excited about it. And then, almost as an afterthought, Clinton says "Yeah ... I like everything about Hero's Banner except that it uses ten-sided dice." And I nod in firm agreement. The non-Platonic solid. The Taint of the Wolf.

And it occurs to me that I have an unreasonable prejudice there - more than one, actually, because I also dislike twenty-sided dice. Why is that? Is there a die type you loathe? Do you elide it in your designs?

    Man I hate percentiles. They're much less fun to roll individually than any other multiple of dice, as if you roll the ten's first then the second roll is redundant around 4 times out of 5*, and if you roll the units first then that roll becomes redundant nearly nine times out of ten. But I don't like rolling them together, for some reason...it feels like it draws a false equivalence between the two, as if they're of equal worth.

    Now, if I had one dice that was ten times the scale of the other - those I would be happy to work with.

    Chris - good point about the ease of grouping d6 by sliding them around. Also, the distribution of rolls makes it pretty easy to group by 10s when rolling that fistfull of dice (D&D fireball or Champions attack). 3d6 also has a nice curve (so does 2d6).

    On the 0 and percentiles - in one sense that's annoying, but it's also fun. Cold Iron gets rid of that whole deal by treating 00 as 00, not as 100 (in Cold Iron, you are rolling random digits of a random number between 0 and 1 (effectively exclusive since you roll additional digits when close to 0 or 1 because the number is being converted into a modifier using the normal distribution).

    Handfulls of dice are nice. Fistfulls are fun also.

    I don't mind the d20 at all. But the way they are used, the fact that you can't slide them around the table without losing the number isn't too bad (ok, it is bad when I use the icosohedral d10s in Dogs...).

    The unusual dice (d3, d5, d7, d14, d16, etc) are cute. Never use them.

  • Mike_Holmes

    I like dicebots and/or other computerized randomizers. And please don't give me that, "but it's not really random" BS.

    I think that preference for die type is only slightly less odd than people who believe that certain dice in their bag roll better than others (especially dice that they're not familiar with). Not becuse they're wrong - it's absolutely true that a poorly crafted die may be way far from a good chi-normal distribution. I think it's messed up because it indicates that y'all are still stuck with wanting to "WIN" yer damn RPGs. How's that for holdover behavior?

    They're randomizers and they randomize. If they don't, they're not working right. Even the old d4 is random if thrown after a good shake (they can be hard to pick up, however - so use the 8 sided D4 instead).

    Get a grip, folks. ;-)

    Mike

    Die bias confession? Mine's about die pools. Great heapin' handfulls of dice, of whatever shape. I hate 'em, irrationally and, for many years, without realizing I hated them. I can *perhaps* explain this bias by pointing at bad White Wolf experiences, but really, the key here is we're talking about an irrational reaction. If a game works by adding or subtracting dice from a pool, I would, until recently, have an irrational dislike for the mechanic.
