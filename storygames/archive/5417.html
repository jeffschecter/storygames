\n I always worry that the GM isn't having fun at the end of a game of Contract Work. I have fun, but I'm still worried that it's a bug in the game, rather than a feature. At the end, the players have worn down my resources so they walk in and blast the target. No climax. Just a resolution. What has helped this feeling is developing some detachment. I'm not the target, I'm the proctor reviewing how he was killed.

So how do I communicate this concept in the rules? How do I keep someone else running the game from getting invested in the character of the target and wanting more for him/from him? This may just be the disease of too many heavily plotted games (ala WoD) but I think I need to expect that any GMs who come to the game, and aren't already on this forum, may have trouble with the end of the job being a period ending a sentence.

Any good suggestions about how I should reinforce a GM's objectivity in the rules?
