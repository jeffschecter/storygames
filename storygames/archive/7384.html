    \n Yay! Making music is my other life-long obsession hobby, so this is something I can actually help with.

    There's all sorts of software samplers out there, some free, some costing hundreds of dollars. However, I think a sampler is way too much tool for the task. I think what you're looking for is a "soundboard"

    Soundboard.com lets you make embeddable Flash soundboards with up to 50 megs of sounds. I haven't played with it myself, but it looks like it does everything you want, except for shortcut keys -- you can't hit a key to play a sound, but instead have to click on the title.

    If that's a problem, or if you're looking for a non-Internet app, some half-assed Googling led me to The Sitter Downers Soundboard, which does feature keyboard shortcuts, along with the ability to play multiple sounds at once, loops sounds, pause and resume sounds, and set the volume of each sound independently. It's also free, and in the couple minutes I spent playing around with it, seems like a solid solution for what you want to do.

    A good source of sounds is freesound.org.

    You'll also probably want some kind of audio editing software, so if you have, say, a sound clip with gunfire, explosions, and screams, you could cut out the screams at the end or split the gunfire and explosions into separate files. Again, the music production software that I use would be total overkill for the task, but I've heard good things about Audacity, which is also free.
