(Pix here for my fellowfor the minis heads)

While not a completely problem-free success, it worked well enough that I'm interested in running another Minis Spectacular*.

I'm looking at going further afield with this one, making it slightly less action oriented and more diplomatic/social.

Here's the basic set up:
Race for the Goblin Throne Y2K: Life and Undeath on the campaign trail
It\u2019s April 1, 2000, and the Grand-Goblin of Monster-town, Nyarlathotep, is stepping down from the Goblin Throne and will not seek or accept a re-nomination by his party. While Nyarlie had a good initial run in the first half of his century-long term (two world wars and the development of nuclear armaments by humanity were big feathers in his cap), the second half of his term wasn\u2019t nearly so successful. His Wicked, Scary Monsters Party\u2019s (WSMP) promise to plunge the world into chaos, horror, and ruin on Jan 1, 2000 failed to materialize, and the citizens of the monster community have lost confidence in his party\u2019s capabilities. As is tradition, the election season will begin on May 1 and proceed through the summer, culminating with election night, October 31, when the next occupant of the Goblin Throne for the 21st century will be decided.

The remaining party faithful of the WSMP are busy trying to figure out how to regain the throne and present a new vision of the party. Opposition Parties are forming, but are fragmented with a variety of visions for the future. Most disturbingly, the liberal No Eat-ems Party seems to be making headway in organizing. The NEEP believes that a more sustainable future that doesn\u2019t involve the general massacre and/or consumption of humanity is desirable, and is backed by enough Hollywood money to get their message out. If the NEEPs win, it will represent a huge direction shift in Monster-town politics.

And, of course, there are any number of splinter factions of each of the two big parties, as well as charismatic individuals and single issue parties cropping up (especially those having an interest in more local Monster-town issues than Monstro-Human Relations).
______

What I need are ideas for political platforms and interest groups for the game. The follow-up post gives the factions that will be represented by minis. Right now, I'm not looking for any mechanical help, just general ideas.

I have no idea right now which monster will be picked by any player as their "candidate", nor how many players will participate, although hopefully less than ten players (with possibly a referee or two). In addition to voting bloc interests, i'm looking for particular resources a given voting bloc might provide to a candidate or a party boss.


*"Minis Spectacular" is my new jargon of choice for these kinds of games. few folks have heard of braunstein in any case, nad i wanted to put the idea of minis usage squarely up front in the concept.
