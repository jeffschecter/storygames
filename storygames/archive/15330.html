\n In my last thread, I wanted a list of Milestones in Zombie Games. Many were suggested. Now, I'm looking for moves that a character can do in a zombie game, such as fight, run, escapism, show affection/kindness and hunting.

This is what I have so far:\n
    \n
  • Fight/Run/Hide: When attacked by x number of zombies, you fight, you kill zombies or you run and hide. All three work, you just need to decide how likely your trope is to do one over the others.
    • \n
  • Escapism: With the future looking so bleak, it feels good to escape from it now and then. You might do it by pretending everything is rosy, you might take a drug or you might remember the past. Eventually, you need to return to reality.
    • \n
  • Show affection/kindness: There\u2019s so few left that you better work hard to find your soul mate.
    • \n
  • Hunting/prove can provide: When the group needs something, someone has to risk life to get it. Important items are food, cars, weapons, etc.
    • \n
\nAny ideas?

    \n I think a better term than Escapism is Recuperate. Surviving in apocalyptic conditions is exhausting. Trying to function while exhausted makes you function at far less than optimal levels. So you need to recuperate - rest, relax, recover, catch your breath, heal, reduce stress, etc.

    In a normal situation, recuperation is a pretty passive thing. It's what you do when you aren't actively doing other stuff. As I type this, I'm recuperating from the obstacle course run I did the day before yesterday. Most of my recuperating was done yesterday, when I spent the majority of the day relaxing around the house, puttering away at a few minor chores and not getting a lot done.

    You can also recuperate through slightly more active means. Have an enjoyable conversation with a good friend. Have a drink and unwind (though having too many is far less helpful). Have sex. Read a good book. These kinds of things engage the pleasure centers of the brain, relax the mind and body, and allow recuperation to happen.

    But in a tense, heightened situation, you have to spend most of your time alert and on edge so you don't die. Just because you aren't shooting a zombie right now doesn't mean you're chillin out recuperating.

    So recuperating becomes an active thing. You have to actively let your guard down and relax for a while.

    And doing so is costly. You put yourself at greater risk while you are recuperating, because you are not being on guard during that time. If you haven't invested enough trust in your situations and companions, then you are not relaxing enough to recuperate. That's pretty key - and it's the reason a lot of characters in zombie movies get stressed out (which often leads to them doing something stupid and getting themselves and/or others killed). They didn't trust their companions and/or situation enough to relax and recuperate. But, of course, trust opens the door to betrayal.

    Posted By: stefoidWhats a 'move' ?
    \nIt's an action that you take on your turn. It might be in response to an action taken by the previous player.

    Player 1: I look inside a store and find a bunch of guns and ammo. (Move: Scavenge)
    Player 2: We get the ammo, and all hell breaks loose. Zombies attack. I fight back, blowing the heads off of three with a newly acquired shotgun. (Move: Fight)
