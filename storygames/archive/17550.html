    \n There are various attempts around to codify GMing styles in *World terms:

    Sage Latorra's principles for 4E - http://www.latorra.org/2010/10/11/the-principles-of-dd-4e/

    ... and for Moldavy D&D - http://www.latorra.org/2010/09/28/the-principles-of-moldvay-dd/

    Jonathan Walton's Burning Wheel principles - http://corvidsun.com/2010/09/14/the-way-this-wheel-burns/

    My (new) attempt at principles for Vampire run in a Burning Wheel style - http://story-games.com/forums/discussion/17555/gm-principles-and-agenda-for-vampire-burning-wheel-style#Item_1

    This topic is one I'm pretty excited about. The key, I suspect, will be getting the essence of a style into something concise enough to live by during play.

    \n The best MC tool I've ported from AW and associated games is the Hx/Bonds/Strings questions at the beginning of a session. I've found that these establishing questions work especially well in one-shot and convention games where the PC group is supposed to have a working history together.
    This summer at Gencon I ran a Golden Age supers game where a group of heroes had to come out of retirement, team up again, and defeat a cosmic menace. With just a few minutes and maybe a dozen questions, a table full of strangers developed layered histories between their characters--friendships, betrayals, failed romances. It was amazing.
