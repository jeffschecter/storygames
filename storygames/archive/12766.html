\n On the Forge someone asked: Are there games that elicit positive emotional responses?

Well ...
- back in 1989 I made my role-playing game Muu. It has a method that makes a group of grown up players (not children, mind you) fall into a mood of tranquil all-encompassing love, or harmony. The game invariably leads to a state of harmony in and between the players. The game really does this to people (it has failed to deliver with one (1) player of mine during the years).

I believe it is time for me to go deeper into this game now, describing it in more detail, and make a decision about publishing it anew, and maybe translating it too ...

So I would like you to read the following, and speak your mind. What do you think? Is this a game you would contemplate buying, and playing? Would you like to be a "muu", and part of "Allmuu"?

The method (summarized)\n
  • You have a muu-leader (ML) and three to six players.
    • \n
  • The players get to make their own "muu" (a humanoid of clumsy nature, with no language, but with shared dreams and feelings; "emo-pathy").
    • \n
  • The muu is made by placing a given number of points on four "abilities": Strength, Quickness, Curiosity and Suspicion.
    • \n
  • The setting is simple; a clearing in the woods, with a big tree, a pond and a brook. It is described using a na\xefve-poetic game-language, forcing the players into a "muu-mode" of thinking and talking.
    • \n
  • The setting is given in a lightly coloured map, which depicts grass and trees and stones and water, with names derived from the na\xefve-poetic game-language. Muu's in the game is represented with a small glass-bead; one in the beginning and more during the game, as muu and muu and muu wanders their separate ways ...
    • \n
  • The muu-leader is responsible for setting the right play-mode intitially, and deepening it, by using a calm and friendly voice, giving each player good smiles, greeting them with a handshake, and by using the na\xefve-poetic game-language in all her descriptions.
    • \n
  • The game proceeds in a very calm fashion, alternating between feelings and actions, using playing cards (one suit from Ace to King per player) to indicate actions (each card represent one kind of action; walking, running, bathing, searching, looking about, etc.).
    • \n
  • The game was sold solely through a one-day "muu-leader school", where future muu-leaders learned how to use and develop the game-language, how to use their voice, and how to "muu'ify their descriptions" (to make players part-take in the wonder of being a muu).
\nThere where never any predetermined plot in the game. No pre-made scenario were ever used in a game of Muu.

"Being a muu" was always about awakening under "Bigtree", wandering about in the clearing, searching for "Starmoss" and "Pearlflowers" to eat, and the mythical "Daystrawberry" (very important myth in the game, propels a lot of dreaming and searching by muu), taking small naps (player going to the loo/the kitchen/the phone, muu sleeping and dreaming), investigating "Tree" or "Stone" or "Spring", or venturing into "Alltree" (the forest), gathering as "allmuu" (all muu's go together to cuddle each other, sharing emotions in a very strong and healthy way), or investigating the "Bigmuu" (a stave in the ground, with a stick fastened near the top, "the greatest entrepreneurical feat ever achieved by a muu"). Muu and muu and muu goes about doing these things, and when the day closes it is good to return to "Bigtree" again, and sleep, and dream ...

And in all these wandering, which may bring the various muu's in all directions, each and every muu is aware of the feelings in Allmuu.

Some singing may occur during a muu-day. The muu singing with joy is a very strong myth in the game, and a very strong tool to use by the players. The player of a muu singing, will have to make up a muu-song on the spot and sing it to the other players.
- NB: every single muu-song ever made in my muu-games, have been a resounding success. I've seen players cry with joy after singing their muu-song.

Actual play:
- with three muu's, and with the muu-leader using a calm and friendly voice all the time
ML: It is a nice day. Muu awakens. Bigtree is over muu, and Ground is under muu. Bigwarm-Muu is tickling muu with long, warm fingers. What do muu feel?
Muu: I feel happy!
Muu: I feel anticipation!
Muu: I feel anticipation!
ML: Allmuu is happy and full of anticipation. It is a nice day. What do muu do?
(the players choose a card, in secret, to indicate an action)
ML: So; what will muu do? Turn your cards, please.
Muu (turns a 3; run): Muu will run!
Muu (turns a 10; eat): Muu will eat!
Muu (turns a Queen; cuddle): Muu will cuddle with Bigwarm-Muu!
(the ML ponders if there are any actions that may influence other actions, and decides there are not, she then focus on one player in turn)

ML (to the player with running muu): Where will Muu run?
Muu: To Spring!
ML: And will muu run normal, or fast?
Muu: Fast!
ML: Then you will roll the die for Quickness, to see how fast muu is running.
(the player rolls the die, a D20, and gets 18, which is more than his Quickness, and a failure)
ML: Muu is running very fast! Muu see the arms moving faster than ever! And Bigtree is running alongside muu! It is very exciting!
(ML touches the glass-bead on the map, without moving it, to indicate that muu has not actually moved ... but give no further explanation)

ML (to the player with eating muu): What will muu eat?
Muu: Starmoss!
ML: Muu is hungry. Muu would like to eat starmoss. Muu has no starmoss to eat. No starmoss in the mouth of muu. No starmoss in the hands of muu. Muu is lying on the back thinking of starmoss, the taste of starmoss, the starryness of starmoss, the softness ... Mmmm-muu is, almost, in a starmossy state, but muu is still hungry ...

ML (to the player with cuddling muu): Muu wants to cuddle with Bigwarm-muu, and Bigwarm-Muu is cuddling muu. Bigwarm-Muu seems to be hiding up in Bigtree, so muu cannot reach Bigwarm-Muu to cuddle back. Muu stretches towards Bigwarm-Muu with arms too short.

ML: What do muu feel?
Muu: Happiness!
Muu: Sorrow!
Muu: Sorrow!
ML: Allmuu feels a lot of sorrow, and that is a strong feeling, so the happiness in muu (nods towards the happy muu) is changed to sorrow too. Allmuu feels very sorrowful! It is a sad day. What do muu do?
(each player picks a card, in secret)
ML: So; what will muu do? Turn your cards, please.
Muu (turns a 2; walk): Muu will go out of Bigtree!
Muu (turns a Queen; cuddle): Muu will cuddle!
Muu (turns a 5; climb): Muu will climb into Bigtree!
(the ML ponders if there are any actions that may influence other actions, and decides there are; the cuddling muu may interfere with either the walking or climbing muu)

ML (to the cuddling muu): Which muu will muu cuddle?
(player points to the player with climbing muu)
ML (to the cuddling muu): Roll your die for Strength.
ML (to the climbing muu): Roll your die for Quickness.
(cuddling player rolling 1 and climbing player rolling 12, giving the cuddling player an "impossible" result, and the initiative)

ML (to the climbing muu): Muu tries to climb Bigtree, but muu comes and cuddles muu, and the cuddling is so soft and warm and tender and good that muu forgets all about Bigtree and Bigwarm-Muu, and just enjoys the pure cuddly-mood muu gets into; there is no more sorrow in muu.

ML (to the cuddly muu): Muu cuddles with muu, and makes very soft and warm and tender and good cuddle. But muu does not cuddle back, so muu is left as sorrowful as before; muu gets one Hurt (points to the sheet, where "Hurt" has a small box, and tell the player to set a mark there).
ML (explains to all players): All [i]Hurt [/i]is minus to all die rolls, and will continue to give minus until [i]Hurt [/i]is removed by muu.
Muu: How do we remove it?
ML: By muu.
Muu: What?
ML (still in a calm voice, smiling): Exactly. Lets play on.

ML (to the walking muu): Muu rise from the starrybed muu has slept on, and walks out of Bigtree. Muu see Tree and Tree, and Alltree (puts a bead on the "wrong" side of Bigtree, to indicate that muu has left Bigtree in the wrong direction, not towards Spring). Bigwarm-Muu is trying to cuddle muu, but it helps not. Muu is still full of sorrow; muu gets one Hurt (indicates the box on the sheet).

ML (a little sadness in her eyes; nods towards muu with Hurt): Muu have Hurt and feel sorrow. Sorrow is not good for muu. Sorrow is Hurt.
ML (sad eyes, sad smile; nods towards second muu with Hurt): Muu have Hurt and feel sorrow. Sorrow is not good for muu. Sorrow is Hurt.
ML (smiling, looking at the third muu): What do muu feel?
Muu: Am I free to feel anything?
ML (a radiant smile now): Muu has been cuddled very, very good, so muu has very strong feelings now, and may feel anything.
Muu: Muu feels ecstatic!
ML (calm voice, smiling): Allmuu feels a lot of sorrow, and a strong ecstasy! Allmuu feels opposite! It is strange being Allmuu today. What do muu do?

- and so it goes on, and on, and on, in the same calm fashion, with feelings and actions alternating, until Allmuu is sated with muu'ish-ness and all decides to pick the Knave (sleeping). Then Allmuu goes to sleep, and muu and muu and muu have their dreams (which is written down on small pieces of paper, and read out loud).

Example of muu-dreams (actual dreams from played games):
- Muu dreams that Allmuu cuddles, and sleeps, and eats.
- Muu dreams that muu is bathing in Spring while muu dreams about the day muu eats Daystrawberries and is cosy.
- Muu dreams of Bigspring, and of Holespring which became Pokespring. Muu dreams of Heatherhoney and cuddling ...
- Muu dreams that muu eats a lot. Muu becomes full.

And all is well, and the game is over ...

    \n It's an impressive thing, laid out like this. I've seen others strive towards similar games (trying to make Moomin games is a pretty common thing in Finland), but you're clearly ahead of anything I've seen. I would fully expect this to make an impressive product when written down comprehensively and with conviction. I could easily believe that the time for an international publication for this is now. I'd buy it for retail.

    That being said, I doubt that I could be convinced to play this myself. It's too philosophically opposed to my own sense of being. The sort of ego manipulation and deprogramming going on in this is so very contrary to European civilization that I have an instinctive dislike for it, the same as I have for any communally transcendental religious experiences. I fear that I'd rather be a bitter, over-intellectual, individualistic egotist than a muu.

    Would you say that the systemic bits we see in the write-up are arbitrary GM preferences, or does this thing have rules? For instance, I could imagine the rule being that the game ends when a majority of the players picks the Knave, while a minority pick just leads to the one muu napping for a round while the others continue doing things. Is this the way it goes, or am I reading too much structure into it?

    It comes to me that this game could probably use custom cards instead of a normal deck, depending on how many different actions there are. Could print the cards inside the book as cut-outs, for example.

    \n One thing I've forgotten to mention, is the strong sense of wonder present in this game. Initially it derives from the impossibility of the game-language;

    - How do you react when the muuleader describes your action like this; "Muu run to the Spring, and suddenly looses the world. There is no world any more!" ?

    - How do you compute that Bigwarm-Muu cuddles muu, but muu can't touch Bigwarm-Muu back?

    - What do you do when muu not only fails in finding food, but fails in finding the Ground, and muu suddenly finds itself being pushed towards Allspring?

    And then, when you start to come to gripes with the baffling labyrinths of the simplistic language, you discover the deeper layers;
    - the miracle of simple, unmitigated happiness
    - the wonder of immersing in such a strange being
    - the unique experience of building Allmuu amongst you
    - the slowly ascending peace of mind

    There is a deep sense of wonder to the game, and that is the finest thing about it. :-)

    Posted By: Eero TuovinenYes, this sounds very much like a project created primarily in non-textual ways.
    It was, in fact, the fruit of years of turning ideas and experimenting with design between me and Jon, another gamer/designer. Three themes from our creative discourse crystallized in Muu:
    - role-playing in a non-violent environment (we discussed the overloaded focus on violence in rpgs a lot)
    - mind-reading characters (I had a bad experience with this in another game, and we discussed ways of doing it better)
    - secret action-choices (we discussed, and experimented with, lots of innovative game-techniques, some good, some baaad)

    So, for years me and Jon discussed things like this, and made small games to see our ideas in action. And then, one night, I could not sleep, and I sat down to write something ... and in the morning I had the core of Muu. During the next day I finished it (at work, skulking about), and brought it to my local club that evening. Jon was there, and joined in the first game of Muu, which he has recorded in our club-magazine, under the title; "Tomas strikes again"! It was tuesday, 2.may, 1989. Jon describes the game as "very entertaining and rewarding". :-)

    Muu has stayed the same in all essentials since then. A new edition will be true to the old game. No need to put anything new into it. The challenge is to convey how this game may be played by book (bar the idea to make a visual/audio-demo of the game), and really have Muu come to life all over the world ...

    \n How do the players "decide not to be disharmonious", do you think?

    There is a strong social force at play in any groups, of course, a "g-force of harmony"; as individuals in a group we try to stay in tune. The muuleader play on this by being very inclusive in her behavior towards the players, and by using conscious calming techniques. The techniques are simple, and effective. A vast majority of players become calm in this game, and are thus able to enjoy the tranquil flow of it. And calm people tend to drift towards harmony.

    So; if the player pointed to another Muu, the muuleader would go with it, transforming it into "muuness" (as is always done, of course); "Muu nibbles on muu, disturbing muu in cuddling muu. And muu makes a song that is not a song, when muu nibble it. Muu is still hungry, for nibbling muu fills no muu".

    In this way the player will get the "crunch" (muu does what he tells it to), but the game is not compromised and muuness is preserved. And of course; in this small action lies the seed of developing muu too; maybe muu could discover tickling, and the song of laughter ... ;-)
