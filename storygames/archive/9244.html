\n I'm working on a Sci-Fi setting and coming to blows with a problem I've always experienced; naming cultural entities. I'm not really talking about creating the actual details of the culture but merely the broader strokes of color invoked by a consistent naming scheme.

Like, I have this Sci-Fi culture that is primarily White, European, Western, whatever and it's the default culture the PC's come from but it is not where play will take place. Nothing I can come up with is satisfying, it's either too cheesy in a Sci-Fi sort of way or completely boring.

Usually I pick a real-life culture/language to base a society off of and derive the name/language from this base. For instance, I have a Nahuatl based Nomadic/Indian culture which will be called "Huac" which comes from the Nahuatl word for "star". That is satisfying to me.

How do you come up with names and balance the exotic with cheese?

    \n I usually assume one of two things for Sci-Fi - either cultures will run together such that any combo of names is appropriate (I also assume that ethnic differences will either blend together as we move out into the Black, or that we will develop some new ones through exposure to new environments).

    The other, and sometimes cooler, assumption would be that, as names are becoming less and less indicative of culture in the modern world, they might eventually be selected like you would your email address (or, even cooler, literally your email address). Thus, a name like FragDown666 or Arthur@security.asgardco.luminous_system.tarsus4_planet.carnival_nation.freehold_city might just be fine ways to name someone - even yourself.
