\n Hiya
\nI'm new here so I hope I've put this in the right place!
\nI've just released my new storytelling game 'Dance of the Damned',
\nand I'd welcome any comments and questions about it.
\n
\nThe game is based on Edgar Allan Poe's 'Masque of the Red Death' and tells the tale of a group of nobles trapped in a castle while a plague rages across the land outside. Will they survive, or fall to infighting, decadence and paranoia as the endless party wears on?
\n
\nMy inspirations for writing it run from Monteseguer to Umlaut, in terms of structure and setting. The game uses a deck of standard cards and plays in around an hour or so. It works best for three or more players and requires no Gamemaster.

    \n To answer both of those...
    \n
    \nThe idea of the game is to tell the story of the castle.
    \nTo do this I've built a framework for using playing cards that help you frame scenes.
    \nEach suit has a style attached to it,
    \nHearts - hope and love, clubs -violence and conflict, diamonds - wealth and decadence, spades - despair and plague
    \nYou play a card on one of the other players framing a scene where you force some plot on them.
    \nFor instance, play the 8 of clubs and narrate challenging another player to a duel.
    \nThe other player responds with a card, continuing the scene with their response relating to the card,
    \nso this one might play a heart, narrating how they try to sway the other character from the duel by appealing to their better nature.
    \n
    \nThe one who plays the highest card gets to narrate the outcome.
    \nThe loser has to keep the winner's card as well which they place in front of them.
    \nSo if the club wins the first player might narrate calling the other a coward and possibly wounding him,
    \nif the hearts wins the scene might end with the challenger realizing how he has lost his way and asking for help.
    \n
    \nAt the end of the game, the suit you have that most of, is the one that defines how you narrate your character's end.
    \nThe most dominant suit among all the players is the fate of the castle as a whole.
    \n
    \nIts very goth but a lot of fun :-)
    \nI labeled it adult on drivethrurpg as its a game you can get very, very dark with indeed if you so choose,
    \nand some of the art is (while tasteful) a little decadent.
    \n
    \nI was inspired by Umlaut as I love that with that game if you aren't sure about narrating the scenes the card structure allows you to keep the game moving.
    \nWhile I love Fiasco, if one of you can't tell a story (or just needs help) the game can stall.
    \nSo the plan was to make a game you can run with just cards and the amount of narration is up to the players.
    \n(which I hope really get into the scenes!)
    \nThe other influence is Monteseguer, as their setting has similarities (stuck in a castle waiting for the inevitable)
    \nand I love the style of that game, and how it lets you go into dark places without drowning you in them.
    \n
    \nHope that helps, ask me more :-)
