    \n Rotating after arcs as short as three sessions is no problem at all. I'd encourage this before experimenting with switching every session.

    If you are going to switch weekly, my first thought is to collaboratively make the fronts so they are transparent, have one fewer character than players, and treat them as a troupe. It seems like a game that strongly benefits from its particular arrangement of authority.

    \n Great question!
    Hm.... given what Jason said, specifically given the idea of switching every 2-3 or so:

    First: lean heavily on the starting Hx questions (and their answers) to give enough meat to the setting beyond the characters. Come up with a past, its events physically removed from the starting situation (say, on the other side of the desert).
    Second: let yourselves explore the setting; don't be afraid to "split up the party". I'm running an AW game right now where two of the three PCs (call 'em A and C) have low interest in leaving the town where play began.

    Honestly, if the Chopper leaves town for a bit on business with the local warlord, it would put that player (call him in a perfect position to swap with me (the MC) so I can make a character that walks into town. Later, if either of the "homebody" players have a yen to go after the Chopper, that could be an excellent excuse to let B go back to playing his Chopper again and give the reins to someone else, in turn.

    Seems reasonable to not allow someone to play both their main character AND other characters in one scene, all at once. Polaris and Montsegur 1244 (both GMless, fwiw) each handle things this way, and it seems to be a good guideline, but I'd happily hear other ideas for that (though that may merit another thread on Playing Just One Character?).
