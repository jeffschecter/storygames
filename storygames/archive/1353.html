    September 2006 edited September 2006
    Thank you all very much. That's very useful.

    The icons are from the online engine for Breaking The Ice, of course. When I played it with Jason Morningstar, it was obvious that some of the icons weren't very clear. So I wondered what people thought.

    Anyway, here we go.

    The first two seem very clear and most people understood the third one too:

    1. You rolled a success!
    2. You rolled a failure!
    3. You can reroll this die.

    The next one's slightly less clear:

    4. This die hasn't been rolled.

    Now, the hearts are a real problem. Of course, in the context of Breaking The Ice, they're...

    5. Permanent Attraction
    6. Temporary Attraction

    The Permanent Attraction one seems fairly clear. But the Temporary Attraction icon is a real problem and, in fact, this is the icon that me and Jason had a problem with last night. (I kept saying "Has the Attraction appeared?" and Jason said "No". In fact, a Temporary Attraction icon had appeared, but Jason didn't recognise the grey heart as being another attraction level).

    I'd wanted the grey to give the idea of impermanence: "This is a heart, just like the other one, but it might disappear soon!". It's interesting to note that most people saw the grey as negative, which isn't the idea I wanted to convey at all.

    Perhaps a little heart might be better?

    Halfway through the thread, I foolishly changed the grey heart to a purple one (I'm colour blind and thought it was pink):



    and people had no idea what to make of it.

    Finally, the icons with the three dice didn't work at all. They're used for Conflicts. They were meant to convey...

    7. Click these dice to ask your partner: "May I roll these dice?"
    8. Click to roll these dice
    9. You've asked your partner to roll these dice and you're waiting for a response

    It's perhaps not surprising that the question mark didn't convey "May I...?", especially as I'd already used it for "Unrolled" in the die above. I could probably make the red arrow into a circular one to convey "Roll".

    As for the waiting, I'm not sure: and this is going to become important, because, from a usability point of view, I've realised I need an icon to mean "You've asked the server to roll this die and you're waiting for a response".

    Anyway, I know nothing about icons, so any help is very welcome.

    Shreyas, I like the idea of only showing the number on the die that was rolled. The problem is that my girlfriend:



    ...saw your comments and said "But then they wouldn't be proper dice!". She was vehement about this, and slightly scary, and so I don't dare change the icons.

    Graham
