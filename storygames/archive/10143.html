    \n Jarrod, as I said, there's in part the psychological barrier of that one big expenditure.

    As for dice, I don't need to buy dice for new games, I've already got my old dice. Custom dice circumnavigate that.

    Furthermore, as a player, I can do well enough with just my PHB. And that's why I want to see whether people could opt to buy just the core rules. $100 for everything you need, but what if I, as a player, don't want it all, and don't need it all?
    Another part is that in most RPG cases, when I spend money on 3 books, I do it over 5-6 months. Yes, I should just save it up this time, but I rather have a constant influx of reading material, don't you?

    This is part of what makes this seem more like a boardgame, "Everything you need" means this is everything everyone needs, and that there's no need for single people to buy additional things (and then, it also means the game is perhaps a lot less fiddly? Cause then one person keeps the books, let us assume, so we don't all need to flip through our own copies?).

    This is another step towards muddying the RPG-boardgame divide. Bad? Nope, but it'll be interesting to see how they pull it off, and whether it works as an RPG, whether it works as a board game, whether it works as a strange new breed.

    So I wait and see.
    And I still think $100 might be too much.

      \n As an aside I think there is a higher potential for OOC tension in party based play then there is in more PCvPC play.

      \nWhen PCs are not part of a party, when they may not even be friends, when they have their own individual agendae that may actually put them in opposition to each other, and that's part of the expectation of the game; then its just a part of play. Of course you're having your character do things for his own selfish ends that will wind up costing me dearly. Of course I'm having my character walk away from helping yours because helping you is not part of my PCs game plan. Its expected.
      \n
      \nBut in party play there is a certain expectation of all for one and one for all play. So the exact same kind of "pursue my own agenda at your expense" sort of play that would make everyone excited in say...Sorcerer...is usually disruptive in party based games.
      \n
      \nThere aren't that many indie games that really promote a Party Mentality, so I wonder if folks who don't have the Party Play background or have drifted away from it are taking that into account. Having some character v character tension within a party is an interesting spice. Joe Abercrombie's novels do it well. But keeping that spice from overwhelming play is a fine balancing act. One that games that don't feature Party Play don't have to worry about.
      \n

The different party sheet choices I expect will be full of what we'd call Flags signalling the kind of tension we're supposed to be playing to. The blue "Boil Over" spaces will Flag the sort of break downs we want to see happen to us, and so expectations will be set. The setting of appropriate expectations is what good game design is all about, so I'm really hoping they executed this idea well.

But like with most of these updates, the proof will have to wait for play.
