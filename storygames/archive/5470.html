\n I'm going to try to run a game of Red Box Hack soon, and I know that my players are not big on the anthropomorphic animals thing. My solution is just to change the names of the classes, changing their "feel" without changing any mechanics. For the curious, here it is. I like how the existing Talents suit these new class names. The players won't even know they're really playing Foxes and Bears.

---

Class names:

*Old Name* --> *New Name*
Warrior --> Soldier or Gladiator
Bear --> Barbarian Warrior
Snake --> Naga
Fox --> Faerie Exile

The change of the Warrior name is purely cosmetic, so it doesn't get confused with "Barbarian Warrior". All the other "human" classes don't need to be changed. I've also considered changing "Mystic" to "Druid", but decided to stick with the original. The Snake/Nage is cool and D&D-traditional, so it gets to stay, just with a more "serious" sounding name. The Fox and Bear, on the other hand, get renamed:

The Barbarian Warrior is a giant, a proud, fiercely loyal warrior from a distant land where his or her people live in communion with Nature. A Barbarian Warrior is usually covered in tattoos (if from a Southern land) or adorned with bone decorations and furs (if from a Northern kingdom). If the Warrior's stature is not enough to identify them, most Barbarian Warrios also have a thick foreign accent and a very different colour of skin from the locals.

As for Faerie Exiles...

There was once an ancient race of faeries that now live deep inside inaccessible forests. No one has seen one for centuries, as they no longer deal with the outside world. All anyone remembers is that they were graceful, beautiful beings that flew around on wings that looked like they were made of stained glass. Some stories say they were wise magical beings who created great art; others tell how they used to steal into people's homes at night and kidnap infants from cradles.

However, one wanderer returned from a coastal port where he claims he met a very strange person--a petite, androgynous creature dealing stolen goods in the seedy underbelly of the town. He couldn't tell whether it was male or female, but it looked very much like a human until he spent some time observing it and noticed a few disturbing features. For starters, it slept during the day. Secondly, when it opened its mouth, it revealed an array of small, sharp, jagged teeth. Third, its eyes changed colours according to its mood and glowed faintly in the dark. Shadows and darkness didn't seem to bother the creature at all, in fact--if anything, it seemed completely at ease under such conditions. Finally, he claims that he once saw it remove its jacket, and that on its back, where a human would have shoulderplates, he glimpsed what looked like colourful glass shards, broken and cracked. As he tells the story, the creature explained to him that those were all that was left of its wings. It was exiled from its secret kingdom for some wrongdoing, and when it was exiled, those beautiful, radiant limbs were maimed and removed.

A Faerie Exile with the "Three Tails" Talent either escaped with its wings intact or has somehow managed to grow them back. "Shape Shifter" allows a Faerie Exile to switch to "human form" (+1 Size/-1 Alertness) or full "faerie form" (-1 Size/+1 Alertness), just as a Fox normally would.

Faeries are strictly carnivorous.

    \n ...just a final note:

    I reread my Faerie description, and realized I hadn't really communicated the style/feel I was going for.

    When I play a Faerie character, I will play them as a callous, cold, bloodthirsty little bastard. Someone who swears like a sailor, spits on people's corpses, and will slit your throat when you're sleeping. They have no understanding of human values like "honour", "compassion", or "love". And they sneer at those who claim to follow them.

    The Faerie Exile is an angry, angry creature.

    Other interpretations are possible, of course. That's just what I was envisioning.

    And I'm definitely going with the "Cyclops". That's perfect!
