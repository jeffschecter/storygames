    I'm looking for RPGs with good social mechanics, courtly (or other types) of intrigue mechanics (so you can play people like Varys or Little Finger in ASOIAF), and possibility the chance to advance and manage a house or realm (but this is less important).
    \nIf I may start with a shameless self plug:

    FateLess
    You can read in this article why it is not "just a gmLess Fate" along with more info about what it actually is, as its own unique thing.
    I suggest this game because it fits perfectly your quoted desiderata
    During a convention demo the group ended up spontaneously playing what can only be summarised as "a stone age mystic island version of Game of Thrones".

    Another title that fits pretty well is King of the City, where you start as the lord of your house/realm and play through the critical phase of a conflict against other similarly powerful entities and organizations.

    Beyond that I can think of...

    The Quiet Year where you play a community surviving in a post-apocalypse scenario. If you and the other players are into court politics, the tensions in the community will quickly ramp up and translate into amazing scenes and stories.
    I believe someone also made a dark fantasy hack of it.

    Blades in the Dark might be interesting.
    A non-2d6 but PbtA game about fantasy/medieval scoundrels doing heists and eventually building up their own criminal guild.

    Mars Colony is only for 2 players, and it is classic SyFy ... but it is THE political rpg. You play as one upstart political figure tasked to save the failing Mars Colony project, while another player moves the Colony and all its divergent factions.
    Very cool.

    Undying!
    Ancient vampires trading favors, debts and backstabs through the centuries.
    A diceLess take on the PbtA structure.
    I'm dying (ah ah ah) to play this game

    \n Unless other parts of the game turn you off, my # 1 suggestion would be Kagematsu. All the PCs (except the samurai) compete to climb their way up the ladder of favors for the samurai they hope will save them, with lots of decisions about how high to aim at a given moment, and major consequences for how they go about it. Gaining the samurai's love and his pity are two very different things...

    I've heard good things (see Ralph's 4th reply) about the social encounter system in The One Ring (dunno if you've already read through those rules). I'm also excited about Undying for reasons of social machinations.

    To expand on jangler's suggestion, in Monsterhearts you track leverage you have over other characters. Simple but integral.

    If you have a really tense, morally grey town, and PCs and NPCs who aren't too quick to violence, then Dogs in the Vineyard can be good for social showdowns too (maybe that's obvious to you already; just figured I'd mention it).

    Good old Vampire: the Masquerade had plenty of great inspiration for setting up schemes and tenuous alliances and power struggles and the like. There's also a lot in the game that can distract from that if you let it, but you don't have to let it. The social mechanics themselves aren't superb but they do bother to differentiate between more different ways of trying to get what you want than D&D does.

    Speaking of D&D, both Birthright (actual D&D product) and Adventurer Conqueror King (independent OSR game) emphasize advancing and managing a house or realm.

    Hope you find something in there you enjoy!

    \n +1 on Blades in the Dark. It's really one of the freshest and most exciting current RPG developments for me (not finalized yet, first version available on DTRPG).

    My current multi-purpose-go-to-system is Fate Core. I also still enjoy Dresden Files very much.

    Among the PbtA (Powered by the Apocalypse) games, I like these as a starting point:
    Apocalypse World (possibly 2nd edition): still covers some basic concepts like fronts best for me.
    Dungeon World: accessible fantasy setting & very good introduction guide for free (highly recommended for PbtA).

    Whereas PbtA is tightly locked to setting and works best for me with 2-3 players, I find Fate more universal and better for i.e. 5-6 players. Another multi-purpose RPG system I like is Cortex Plus (Leverage or Cortex Plus Action RP specifically).

    Fiasco is always worth playing and fun.

    Another personal favorite is Maid RPG. A Japanese RPG where you play... well, maids. If you like this setting.
