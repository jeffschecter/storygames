\n In the spirit of the "name my Unlockables" thread, anyone care to help me out too?

My zombie game, 'Til Death, needs a term for penalties. The conflict system is built on the idea of receiving penalties to your pool as you take "damage", and you lose the conflict when you have no units in your pool.

In a much simplified example, I might say: "I'm going to punch you, causing you to be unbalanced. Oh look, I received 2 successes. You now have a penalty unbalanced -2."
You might say "I'm going to scream at you to stop, causing you to be hesitant. I don't take any penalty from unbalanced since it doesn't apply. I get 3 successes."
To which I respond "I'm going to headbutt you, making you disoriented, but I'm at -3 because I'm hesitant. Crap, I failed."

And so on. As you may imagine, someone could keep going in a fight for quite a while using this system since they could switch strategies to use approaches where existing penalties do not apply, so a big part of the system lie in inflicting horrific, lasting wounds to force a compromise.

My problem is that I don't know what to call these penalties. "Penalty" is flipping dull, and the only other term I've come up with is *gulp* "Negatraits"... because they work sort of like Traits... but they're bad for you... yeah, it sucks.

What would you call them?

    Troubles work fine but sounds a little odd "You have a Trouble broken knee -3". Conditions work better I find.
    Strike is abstract but still kind of evocative and rolls off the tongue.
