\n Something I was thinking about after playing Apples to Apples the other day.

In our circles, when the game is done and winner declared, we finish it by doing "The Apples to Apples Fortune Teller": Basically the same effect as fortune cookies, horoscope based on stars/animals, etc: Every player basically says "I am..." and then read all the green adjective cards they collected over the course of the game.

"I am Earthy, Flamboyant, Expensive, Violent and Scenic" (etc)

It's good for laughs.

Anyway, I was noticing that when I looked at pretty much any three random Green (adjective) cards I drew, I could start to get an impression of a person. I guess rolling dice on tables works just as well theoretically, but in practice with each card I turned over (usually in sets of three), a character/personality basically erupted in my head, far more so than when I roll on "personality tables" and the like.

Just chucking this out there. If you have a set of Apples to Apples (party box is a great set), give it a shot if you're trying to come up with NPCs on the fly. Any genre should work.

    \n It's probably one of the option things that's at the end of the rulebook.

    They have other things, like playing backwards (what is the LEAST like the green card) and stuff like that.

    I played it once at a con with a group of RPG'ers. We totally ignored what was actually on the green cards (or used it for inspiration if we really wanted to) and made up little stories of a few sentences, usually silly fictional autobiographical stories, stopping with a cliff hanger sentence. The red card responses were to finish the story.

    And when it amused us to do so, we linked the mini-stories together. We ended up crafting a narrative about me going to jail for some sort of bizarre reason and having strange things happen there.

    Made for a nice "story game variant" of the standard game.
