\n Does anyone know of any RPGs/Story Games that are designed specifically to mirror the structure of the film Rashomon (or similar films, books, TV shows, etc.)?

Or, in other words, are there any games that designed around the conceit of revisiting the same sequence of events from the perspective of different characters, with characters' personalities, dialogue, and details changing to match the viewpoint of the character narrating?

    \n It's not precisely the same thing, but Daniel Wood's "My Daughter, the Queen of France" features scenes that are played multiple times and change over the course of that repetition, and those changes are partly a result of the different characters' perspectives of the events. I've never played it, but it sounds really great. PDF here.

    \n Tragedy Looper doesn't have much in the way of story, and it's also a strict time loop game, not a revisiting of the same events over and over. You try and change the way that a sequence of events play out.

    I'm not aware of such a game...although Fiasco could easily do this. You'd have to do it with an experienced group, though. (The Stunt Dice variant would be tailor-made for this. When you pick a Stunt Die, narrate an alternate version of a previous scene, or of a specific key scene.)

    I wonder if you could co-opt Microscope to do this.
