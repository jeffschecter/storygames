\n Finding the Game is a podcast spotlighting the skills cultivated from long form improv and how to apply them to your gaming table. Featuring the arbitrary architecture of the 8 Rules of Improv, we begin by discussing an old Gamestas favorite, trust. With anecdotes from the stage and applications to the table, this is the one-eyed leading the blind and having fun doing it, so come along with me and Jason in Finding the Game.

Tyler
thecollectivegames.com

    \n This is a real treat. I like the story sharing the stage with Del Close's group. The point about people coming from tabletop with an adversarial attitude toward the GM or storyteller having some culture clash with improv'rs is a good one too.

    And I think there's something about hogging the scene or blocking other folks from letting their character have good spotlight is something that may come up more in less obvious ways in longer improvised live play. It's easy to get caught in character, and have something you want to do, and lose track of what the other players around you may need for their character to shine too. Balancing your insights and continuing to be aware of the others can be a not-obvious thing, especially to less experienced or folks not socialized with the improve rules.

    I look forward to future 'casts!

My only experience in which I could relate to this was in LARP and in that setting I wasn't always concerned with bringing out or highlighting aspects of other people's characters. I was younger than everyone else playing, so I wanted perform and show that I belonged, but looking back the scenes I liked the best were the ones where I respected the person playing across me and I honored their contributions.
