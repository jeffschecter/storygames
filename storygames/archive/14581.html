Feel free to re-explain what defines 'close friend' and 'person sharing your interest' to explain your tipping point between the two.
As a follow up question - how many close friends do you have because you met them through Gaming?

    Posted By: graypawnOn average do you game more often with friends you consider to be close (meaning you spend time with them outside of gaming, share your feelings and daily life with, go out of your way to be around) or people that share your interest in a style of game (meaning you don't really engage each other outside of the games you play but you consistently show up to every session)?
    \nMost of our current group are my close friends (people I've known for at least a decade, some two decades). The rest are friends-of-friends: meaning that I haven't spent as much time around them, but they've spent a lot of time with my friends. More than a few times that has ended up with them becoming my friends, too.

    I remember gaming with people I didn't know well (or at all) when I was younger, and I'm pretty much never going to do that again. If I'm going to spend 4+ hours a week playing a game with people, then they're going to be people I like outside of gaming, too. It's so much more fun that way.


    (Fortunately for our gaming group, we have a couple of people who actually do enjoy meeting strangers and going to cons and all that stuff; they are the de facto gatekeepers for the rest of us. They go out and vet potential players, and so far everyone they've vouched for has been good. I think a long-term gaming group really needs people like that, to offset the natural attrition of the passing years.)
