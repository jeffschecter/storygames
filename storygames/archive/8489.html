    \n Yup, I organised a dice order a while ago. I decided to go with a UK company called Dice & Games - they were the best combo of quality and price I could find, and very easy to deal with.

    We got enough orders together to be able to have 200+ dice laser engraved - the price falls significantly the more you order. At this number we are talking about \xa31 per die, and we only had three sides made, so three blank sides, two wheels and one burning wheel (the 6). The graphics where a streamlined version of one of Chris Moeller's drawings for Burning Empires.

    As Luke says, the dice are extremely nice. When the company engraved dice they did slightly more than the actual order in case some dice turned out bad, so I ended up getting about 20 dice extra for free. So we decided to give Thor and Luke a set each

    I'm going to use mine for Mouse Guard as well

    \n Note that it's pretty darned easy to make your own custom BW/BE dice, using blanks and either a drill bit or an etching tool (Dremel tool). I mean, you have to put in a shallow dot (large diameter sphere bit) and some other symbol for exploding (an X)? Set up a simple wooden jig and brace for the dice (or say "what the fuck" and freehand) and you could crank out twenty or so black and maybe ten of gray and white in a matter of minutes. Then quick work with a paint pen and you're done.

    Point being blank dice are, what, 12 to 30 cents each, but a custom die is going to be around $2 each, at the numbers you're likely to buy. I'd pay myself about $45 a hour to etch my own (figured at one every two minutes or so).

    Just saying (and *ahem* see, Luke? They'd sell, slowly but surely, at BWHQ...).

    \n Oh, you're absolutely right, Andy... but that's true of the manufactured ones with blanks (prolly even more so). If one is seriously worried, find a paint that's about the same density (dry) as the plastic, and fill the etchings. But I was really speaking of a VERY slight etch; it won't be nearly as skewed as, say, drilling into a one pip (opaque dice), filling with lead, and then repainting. Not that I've done that.
