\n I ran a quick game of PSI Run at Viking Con - my second game, ever. I decided to juice it up and have three questions instead of six, which works beautifully. You get a shorter game, and some real focused tactical decisions about where to allocate dice - do you really want to end the entire story, or are you going to put a good die in Reveal? Here we are, playing in the chemistry classroom:


Frederik and I decided to be GMs, and Peter, Kjeld, and Thomas made Runners. We had a Runner who occasionally set things on fire when she was too focused, a Runner who tended not to be noticed (and could fold space and time), and (my favorite) a Runner who was a tattoo-covered old man who would black out and "explode" like an overloaded emotional battery, so people tended to die if he was angry and Thomas put a low die in Psi. Which happened twice.

We set it in Copenhagen, and the game opened with the Runners waking up in a life raft in the \xc3\u02dcresund, the channel between Denmark and Sweden. The opening was slow, and we didn't really get a chase going until later in the game - we should have, in retrospect, had the Chasers on their ass immediately. As it was they got run over by a boat, swam to shore at Helsingor, blew up both a police station and set Kronborg castle on fire, and the shifty firestarter, who we discovered was a master assassin, headed for Amager, a somewhat seedy Copenhagen neighborhood to meet her old boss. Eventually everybody got captured and taken to "The Institute" in Amager (does every PSI Run game have one of those?) and they discovered the awful truth - they were all secret weapons, and their task was to kill each other until one survived. In other words, a pretty typical PSI Run session. Our story track:

PSI Run is a great game. It ran smoothly despite 80% of the players using a foreign language. We had fun and ended up with a very exciting and slightly weird story - nothing like what Frederik and I had schemed at the beginning.

    \n It was a great little game! Indeed, the beginning was a bit slow as we were trying to figure what we were supposed to do - and I guess the game would have benefitted some from a harder GM-hand for the first couple of scenes. But once the ball got rolling, I had a blast. The fiction, although being the result of very little detail and a lot of improvisation, felt very coherent and engaging and as I also told you after the game, Jason - I had emotional reactions to the events in the game. It's game I'd play instead of a board game any time!
