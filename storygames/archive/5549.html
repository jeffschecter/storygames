\n I'm looking around for something I can bolt Camaraderie's rules onto.

But there's just too many to sift through.

Camaraderie rules will include
1) an oracle
2) create the party first and characters second
3) party has some kind of special benefit
4) players control the level of difficulty by putting in 0, 1, or 2 chips to a central pile. Difficulty is derived from number of chips, rewards come after difficulty is overcome.

    \n It sounds like you'll need something where the difficulty of an encounter can be fairly reliably gauged. For that, I think you'll need something that's either really simple (like Dungeon Squad) or really well known (like d20). I think things like an Oracle and set difficulties won't sit well with player-created content like Donjon and the like, since those thrive on no-one knowing what's coming next. You might find a way to make that work though.

    \n I see, yes, the GM still has control over difficulty. Example being, the GM decides a difficulty rating for a given task. The player rolls against his or her skill and attempts to achieve that result. I had been focusing more on #4, players control the level of difficulty, which can be implemented by either the GM or player also introducing the Gift of Dice from TSoY.

    I kind of envision it like: the GM sets the difficulty at X. A player could consciously choose 0, 1 or 2 penalty dice and, as with Raising the Stakes, achieve extra stuff upon success. Likewise, a GM or player could give 0, 1 or 2 bonus dice to the task; effectively, swapping doing Cool Things for just plowing through the task and getting it accomplished.
