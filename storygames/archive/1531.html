    HOW TO FIND LEGO-FRIENDS (in five easy steps over a 30 year period)

    Step 1. Find a kid in your neighborhood. Make sure his parents never let him have any good toys, in his estimation.

    Step 2. Befriend this kid in high school.

    Step 3. When this kid drops out of college, and finds some entry level jobs, do not scoff when he blows money that should go to rent (or resuming his education) on the toys he never had.

    Step 3A. Particularly do not scoff when he finds a way to engage in arbitrage in Toys R Us "Jeffrey Dollars" to turn US$15 into TRU$150, repeated ad nauseum to create a potentially infinite toy-stream. Hint: never analyze his opportunity costs.

    Step 4. Watch in horror as he turns another friend of yours, the one with more money than he knows what to do with and always seemed so practical, into another toy-collector.

    Step 4A. When the three of you are visiting, do not complain too voiciferously that you are wandering the aisles of the local Toys R Us looking for "sweet deals", even though you are all over 25.

    Step 4B. Do not point out that while you do not collect toys, you do have a girlfriend, and that you suspect there may be a correlation.

    Step 5. After several years of scamming, searching, and spending, your two friends will amass a large quantity of useless, vaguely shameful crap. Yet among this nonsense will be... LEGOS, the one thing that makes you say, "Gee, I would like to disapprove of this whole deal, but... but... LEGO's! Gentlemen, I applaud you!"

    (Optional Step 6. When you discover Mechaton, and have an urge to play so bad that you can't contain yourself, don't murder them when they say, "Nah, looks stupid.")

    November 2006 edited November 2006
    For me the list is actually worryingly lengthy! For almost all of them the reason 'not' basically comes down to a lack of personal time and a limited group where no one really enjoys GMing or has the time to do any decent prep work. Hopefully that last part is changing for the Burning Empires game that we've just begun; it's seeming good so far anyway! The length of the list itself is also something of a problem. I really don't have a huge amount of free time so I simply don't get the chance to play that many games.

    So, my games:

    Dogs in the Vineyard

    Why?
    Because I love the concept behind the game and the way it is written. I consider it to be one of the most concentrated distillations of awesome GMing advice ever and I'd love to play out the resolution mechanics. The poker-like bidding just seems very interesting to me and feels like, after a couple of tries, it would be extremely intuitive and flow really well. Plus the whole moral judgement angle and player-GM interaction feels right to me.

    Why not?
    The reasons above pretty much cover it, with the note that it would almost certainly be me GMing it and, I'll be honest here, I hate GMing. My family life always leaves me feeling too drained in the very limited free time that I do have to actually want to do any prep for a game, even the small amount that DitV would require, and I'm just not comfortable in the GM role.

    The Mountain Witch

    Why?
    Like DitV the concept of the game really gels for me and I really like the way that it's written. The main selling point for me, though, has got to be the trust mechanics and dark secret cards. They seem like they would really ramp up the tension and emotional investment in the game (and AP threads that I've read seem to bear that assumption out) and I'd love to experience them myself...

    Why not?
    ...as a player. Again it's a game that I'd almost certainly have to run myself and my comments for DitV and at the start of this post are relevant here as well.

    Sorcerer

    Why?
    The essence of the game, the conflict between humanity and personal power, is one that I'd love to experiemnt with in play. The mechanics seem very cool and I definately feel like it's a game that I really should try out at some point just because, well, it's Sorcerer!

    Why not?
    Ugh. I feel like a record on repeat here but it's the same as DitV and tMW. I hate GMing and it's the case that to experience it I'd need to run it myself and I have neither the time nor, to be frank, the inclination to do so.

    Weapons of the Gods

    Why?
    Sheer kung fu awesomeness coupled with the genius of Rebecca Borgstrom (I'm a massive Nobilis fan) and sweet, if relatively crunchy cf my usual tastes these days, mechanics make for a game that I'd love to participate in. Flipping out with the magnificently named techniques, destroying the reputations of courtiers and nobles with the Secret Arts, what's not to love?

    Why not?
    As above, I'd be having to run it and don't have the time or inclination to prep the game, especially as WotG would require more prep than, say, DitV or tMW. My aversion to GMing games is a real problem with regards to my ability to play the games that I'd like to given the size of my group (myself, 1 friend from my uni days and my 14 year old step-son who is even more averse to GMing than I am) and the lack of a natural 'GM-type' within the dynamic. I just wish that Andrew (Kenrick) lived closer (he's another old uni friend) as he loves putting on the GM hat!

    Blue Rose

    Why?
    I actually enjoy Mercedes Lacky and like the setting for the world of Aldea. That combined with the novel and interesting take on traditional d20 mechanics (adapted and updated for True20) makes Blue Rose a game I'd really like to try out.

    Why not?
    This one isn't actually a game that I'd likely have to run as my friend in the group (a guy called Jan) also owns the books and enjoys the settings. Whilst he's not a massive fan of GMing himself he is more prepared to do it than I am. As such I think that the only reason that we haven't played this yet is that we just haven't got around to it thus far! That and, much as I may dislike it, it's going to be my turn to GM next as Jan prefers to play too and it would be unfair to foist all GMing duties upon him.

    Of course that list doesn't cover all of the games that I'd love to try and don't even own yet but I figure that it will do for now!
