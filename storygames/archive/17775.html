\n I\u2019m about to start a new playtesting round for Left Coast, my game about science fiction authors living in California.

The inspiration for the game comes from the lives and works of authors like L. Ron Hubbard, Alice B Sheldon (aka James Tiptree Jr), and Philip K. Dick (especially Dick\u2019s real-world science fiction novels like Radio Free Albemuth and VALIS).



The latest draft has incorporated the feedback gathered from several different playtest group, three fantastic volunteer editors, and lots of conversation. Thank you to everyone who was involved in the last round of playtesting and who gave that feedback.

As the latest draft is 130 pages long, click on the image below if you want to read a summary of the game (12 pages, including front and back cover)




If you agree to try Left Coast, please get a game in between now and 30 July and let me know how it goes. The full playtest document contains questions I'm particularly curious about.

Download the latest playtest version of Left Coast here


Thanks!

    \n@huckabees: It definitely does Dickian paranoia - more in the real-world vein of Radio Free Albemuth and VALIS, rather than Ubik or Flow My Tears, the Policeman Said. In other words, the default assumption in a game of Left Coast is that the world is normal and the paranoia intrudes into it.

    \n@Biest and @Mcdaldno: Great to hear. I hope you enjoy it.

    @SmZA: Team Shieldmonkey's playtest of the game was really vital for me. It was informative to see your group grapple with the previous draft's complexity. I still think it's overly-complex (and I've started a thread over at the Adept Press forum to discuss how to simplify it) but I'm starting to get a sense on how to address that, now, and I'm looking forward to other's peoples' takes on it.

    @Paul_T: I'm a real fan of the flowcharts, too. Part of it is because I think it's a nice way to present and summarise procedures and subsections in the game; part of it is because I think the hand-drawn style really captures a 1960s underground-press production style that works for the time period the game's set in.

    \n I've been working on a 30 page quickstart version of Left Coast. The first draft is here:

    Radio Free Left Coast - Alpha draft (9 February 2013)

    I\u2019m writing this because (a) it's a useful introduction, and (b) the producer of a film adaptation of Radio Free Albemuth approached me (after seeing this thread) and asked if he could use it as a reward for a Kickstarter he's running in March. As a result, I've been head-down for the last few weeks, trying to get this into shape.

    I've done one playtest already, and received immensely useful feedback about how to structure the character creation part of the game. I\u2019ll be doing at least one more playtest and (hopefully) @Mike_Sands' group will do a playtest as well.

    If you have any feedback, I'd love to hear it by 1 March 2013, which is when I'll probably start the rewrite. You can make editing comments on this document, here: (Be warned: it's a google doc that has some terrible formatting.) Or send a message to @leftcoastRPG on twitter, reply here, or join the Left Coast community on Google Plus.
