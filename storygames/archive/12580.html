    \n Hey, I skimmed through this. Wasn't too pulled in by the flavour text, but I read the basic rules, and the dice mechanic seems to work pretty good.

    Since you have a 1-2, 3-4, 5-6 breakdown, you could use Fudge dice instead of d6. A Blank would be useless, and discarded. A Minus would be a minor success, and you could use it against the Obstacle, but not the Consequences. And a Plus would be a major success and could be used against the Obstacle or the Consequences.

    Now, normally, I hate Fudge dice (hate! HATE!), but in this case, I could use them and feel fine about it.

    \n I mention the possibility of using Fudge dice in the latest draft. I hadn't considered that the two non-blank faces could be the useful ones rather than the non-Minus faces, but it's all equivalent anyway. As long as it's consistent in play, that's all that matters. I'll probably leave the current definition as it stands as d6 are more common than dF.

    The setting is actually based on a manga/anime series (albeit with the serial numbers filed off). Perhaps you need to be familiar with that for the possibilities to jump out at you. I should probably insert some little bits of fiction to sell alchemy to the reader.

    Paul, there's actually a whole other chapter for the GM that wasn't written when I posted the draft, with any luck there'll be enough material in there that the GM isn't left to her own devices. Among the specific things it deals with are how to set consequences, move scenes forward and construct the Conspiracy. Is there anything in particular that you think I should cover?

    The playtest actually went far better than I had good reason to expect a playtest to go. The dice rolling resolution was slightly slower than your standard chuck dice/pass/fail but didn't bog down, even when there was a lot going on. There was a lot of investigation, brow-beating and sneaking around, and the final showdown was pyrotechnic but allowed the villain to escape. The characters felt solid, which is no mean feat in a one-shot, although coming up with the details for characters took longer than I anticipated it would. One of the players was completely new to roleplaying, and thoroughly enjoyed it, so that was good.

    Thanks, all.

    Posted By: timonkeyOne thing that feels weird is that if there are hits against you, those are the only possible consequence is that you lose; and if there are no hits against you if you already will suffer the consequence then there's no risk. It seems like there should always be some risk, and hits against you should increase the risk. Especially if you are doing some other action that would normally have it's own consequence.
    \nThere's a few things at play there- firstly, when crunching the numbers I decided that adding consequences on top of the usual one was too harsh, particularly for low-skill characters. Even if they take the normal (less serious consequence), it eats up a die so they're less likely to be able to fight back. So it was purely a balance thing. Then, I realised that keeping on recycling the same consequences in a contest (gum jams, out of ammo, shoot something you shouldn't, ad infinitum) would be lame, and this method meant that such risks only cropped up occasionally. It's the kind of thing you could house rule if you want, but I think it strikes a good balance as is.

    Another thing is that my intention is that if you suffer a consequence in a contest, future potential consequences are something different. I haven't made that clear enough, so I'll revise the text when I get home.

    \n A few other things:

    Library, Rank, Standing, and Wealth are not abilities. They're attributes, or characteristics, or even possessions or backgrounds, and they certainly give the ability to do things that you might not otehrwise be able to do, but they're not abilities themselves. Don't call them that.

    That's a nice Alchemical diagram. But why is the symbol for ice a combination of water and fire? Ice is a form of rock. It should be a combination of water and earth.

    "Other Character Types" is a really boring name. Maybe you should call them Civilian Types?

    These chapters are way out of order. Don't put skills first: they rely on knowing something about having a character, and knowing something about rolling the dice. Right now, it's a bunch of info that's hard to make sense of. I would suggest starting with an explanation of the character sheet, or putting most of the skill info in character creation, and the list with possible consequences after character creation, or after you explain how to roll dice.

    Resolving an attack with a consequence to the opponent's next action gives combat a real swashbuckling, parry-and-riposte kind of feel. I like that.
