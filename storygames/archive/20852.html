    \n I've given some thought to PvP PbtA mechanics in my design project and found a solution I'm satisfied with: Only the player who initiates the action rolls a move and the defender's opposing modifier gives the initiator either a bonus or a penalty depending on how high their stat is. For example if PC A wants to hurt PC B, the player of A rolls the Fight move and takes +/- X forward depending on PC B's Fight modifier. If player A rolls well, good for his character, if he rolls badly, good for PC B.

    This 1) makes PvP situations smooth and quick to resolve since there's minimal rolling involved, 2) encourages decisive action since weak hits favor the roller 3) takes into account the skill disparity, both when someone is outmatched or when they have the upper hand 4) does away with interfere rolls that were tough and abstract for me to resolve in other PbtA games and finally 5) takes advantage of the existing concrete moves.

    Whether this type of a solution works for you depends on your game and the style you're going for. I like this in a blood opera where the tie between mechanics and fiction is more concrete than abstract.

    \n Are you trying to design your own PbtA game, or just wondering how to MC/GM one?

    The standard AW answer is that the MC adjudicates the conversation between the players, making moves and ending each one with "what do you do?" When it's a player's turn, they can make a move, and if their victim or target is another player, so it goes.

    To make it "fair", the MC just makes sure that each player gets a move, in turn (unless the fiction mandates otherwise; it's designed to be flowing and believable, not "fair").

    This benefits the aggressor, generally. It works well for most (but not all) moves.

    Vincent did write an "opposed roll" version of the AW mechanic at some point:

    lumpley.com/index.php/anyway/thread/766

    See my comment, below, in particular, for how it works. Its only difference is that both sides' stats are taken into account (as @Glowie suggests).

    He's designed a set of AW-style moves to go with the technique, too, for Freebooting Venus.

    Aside from that draft, I have yet to see a PbtA game which is designed to function well for PvP play, from the ground up. However, it wouldn't be hard to do; I can think of various ways to do it.
