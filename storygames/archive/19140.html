\n The first is a two-player, half-hour activity in the vein of Doll. One takes on the role of an Adventurer, the other is a Troll holed up under a bridge. Both roles, the playstyle, and the suggested cues, can be interpreted broadly. I wrote it to explore asymmetrical power balances - in particular those where one side has immediate power (Trolls, presumably, could handily deal with a lone Adventurer) and the other has the weight of society behind them - and see what kinds of negotiations emerge. The presentation is mostly an artifact of how I was taking notes that evening, but when I revisited the text to lay everything out I liked the feel.
Adventurer & Troll
Feedback request: any overhaul-level criticism, any tweaks to improve playability.

The second is a single-player alternate reality journaling game intended to be played in a moleskine or tumblr or youtube channel. Play follows an alternate-reality you that becomes interested very interested problematically interested in an in-game representation of a random muse. I wanted to write something that would have players actually creating things as part of the gameplay. Inspiration flowed heavily from one of the Doppelganger playbooks for MH. The baked-in story follows a plot but leaves freedom for interpretation, hopefully coming across as more of a dog run than a railroad. Trigger warning: stalking.
New Best Friend
Feedback request: overhaul-level criticism, suggestions on how to de-railroad parts of the story, changes to daily cues

I'm still getting my bearings here, so if I misstep please let me know. Thanks!
