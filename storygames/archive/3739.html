\n (stemming from the "What is a Storygame" thread)

In case you missed the memo, there's a new standard definition of an RPG, generated by mr. kevin allen jr. To be a RPG, a game needs to include at least one sword in it. The more swords, the more of an RPG it is.

I'm going to apply this test to my games within reach, to give some examples.

Urchin: No pictures of swords, which is the first problem. However, some of the NPC's Gear includes "meat hook +2", "wrist spikes + 2", and "big fuckin' knife +2". Big fuckin' knife sounds like a sword to me. You barely made it, Krause! Verdict: RPG

vs. Outlaws: Again, no pictures of swords. I was thinking this was a clear case of not being an RPG, but wait! In the "Damage" section, there's stats for a Cavalry Saber! Verdict: Clearly an RPG

Beast Hunters: Hrm. On the fence. There's multiple illustrations of hunters with knives, and the big ogre thing on page 33 might have a sword, it just looks like a knife cuz of the perspective. A cursory purview of the text shows no mention of swords. Unless someone steps up with more evidence, I'm gonna have to call this one as Verdict: Not an RPG

Nine Worlds: There's one small knife on page 65, but I see no swords anywhere else. Again, I have yet to delve into the text, but based on my cursory examination...Verdict: Not an RPG

Polaris: Umm, every character has to start the game with a Starlight Sword. Verdict: Best RPG so far!

carry. a game about war.: No pictures of swords. No mention of swords. Verdict: so not an RPG.

Of course, any questions need to be turned to Kevin for adjudication.
