\n I dunno if any of you remember Barbarians of Lemuria, the free Conan-themed RPG that Simon W wrote up a few years back, but I do. It kept that backbone of hit points and fast combat, all the while tying skills to Risus-like Job-descriptors. It was fucking slick as all hell, but incomplete: The link above is for an updated version (so it might be different than the one I played), but magic wasn't complete, there were some missing parts. Totally playable in the end, as long as you weren't using magic or magical items.

Cut to... today.

Barbarians of Lemuria, a full game based on the original free game (and apparently tightened up) is now available over at RPGNow. Now, mind you, I haven't played it yet. In fact, I just downloaded it a few hours ago and have had no time to read for significant changes (though I did see that the character sheet was expanded). So I can't 100% vouch for it yet... But I can totally vouch for the original, and if the new one is even remotely like it, it's definitely a winner for those of you who want your dice and hitpoints without the maps and tactics. If Conan OGL disappointed (I loved its deep detail and background; hated the system with the armour point calculations and longwinded character generation and the like), this most likely will hit the spot.

Just wanted to drop that info for those who have been following Barbarians of Lemuria. It's out!

-Andy

    \n I finally had a chance to tear through it, cover to cover.

    As it stands, it is a thoroughly awesome game. I can't wait to give it a shot. The only thing I fear is loading up on armor ("4 points") might cause a lot of "0 damage hits", but I need to give it a run to know for sure.

    The take on cultures, and how they provide selectable benefits and drawbacks: This is hot stuff.

    Remove personality/personal goal stuff from TSOY, and instead replace with rules regarding bringing in character cultural and personal effects in play, and this is what you get.

    I've found my game for running Dark Sun, for sure. I've already drafted up a few cultures, with advantages and drawbacks (lowlands slave, elf tribe member, Tyr civilian, Tyr nobility, Nibenay civilian and Nibenay nobility).

    \nWhile not knowing what exact version of the PDF you have, some things were brought up on the Yahoo group that made it in (plus the usual bunch of typos) were: missing gazetteer entry for Oomis; rules for the the Zathog's poison bite; rules for Snow Worm's sleep breath; a clarification of damage done with a negative modifier; a clarification of the requirement that a spell greater than a cantrip use at least one casting requirement; and a clarification of the rules for Mighty and Legendary successes. I think all of them (along with some optional rules) can be found in the Rules_options_additions_variations.doc document found at http://games.groups.yahoo.com/group/barbariansoflemuria/files/BoL%20Rules/.

    In general, while the traffic is low, the Barbarians of Lemuria Yahoo! group is a fairly useful resource, although the author doesn't seem to have been around there much the last week or so, or is being very quiet.
