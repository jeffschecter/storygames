    \n\nMarch 2008 \n

    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n A few reasons, really:
    1) I rarely can plan two (now one and a half) months in advance. For camping, in particular, I'd want some idea of the weather and might not attend if it's shitty ($70 down the drain, literally).
    2) Being forced to smoke in the parking lot of an outdoor facility is, well... fucked-up. I just can't see myself walking across the whole campsite at the crack of dawn (or just before bedding down) to have a smoke. FWIW, I've played weekend-long LARPs without this restriction, and it was never a problem for sensitive folks nor a fire hazard.
    3) Food, in general. I'm a bit finicky and would rather make my own--especially while camping out!--but I don't see that as an option (nor as a price adjuster).

    \n My husband is not much into role playing games, and has no interest in going to fun stuff like this. So I go alone. Which is fine, and I go with his blessing, except it leaves him home alone with the kids. He's an awesome dad, that's not the problem either. It's not fair that I travel around and he stays at home, especially since he's working full time and in grad school. With GenCon, possibly DragonCon, and going back to Spelman in September, I'm still traveling more than he is.

    It really boils down to spousal politics, money, and a couple other personal reasons not worth getting into. Next year, perhaps. My daughters will be 4 and 10, and the older one at least, might have more fun with me.

    \n Wrong side of the country, and not the highest financial priority. I'd love to go, but flying to the East Coast for a camping trip isn't really practical for my wife and I right now. We're trying to balance our fun expenditures between roleplaying events and the SCA, with the SCA starting to take precedence as the eventing season rolls in.

    There's a spousal politics thing for us as well, but a bit different--my wife enjoys roleplaying, but not enough to make this particular trip, at least yet. Were it just me, I'd be more tempted to go, or at least try to budget the money and fail, then wish to God I was going.

    And one more thing: We'd be a stranger to most if not all those present. Not that we wouldn't enjoy ourselves, but again, relative priorities. If we manage to meet, socialize and game with more of the Story-games community, this could change for us. But then, that's a catch-22 if we never make it to an event like this. :P

    Anyway, that's it in a nutshell or two.
