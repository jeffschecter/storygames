\n Recently I've been noticing that a lot of really good games have come out of Game Chef. I think one thing that fueled the creativity that drove those games is the perimeters set up by the Themes and Ingredients that are part of the contest. I'm in a game designing mood, so I'm asking that people give me some Themes and Ingredients to work with. Don't worry about spending hours picking stuff, just throw some loosely-connected stuff out there and see where it goes.
Maybe I'll get something good out of it. Thanks!

    \n Take a random Wikipedia page that is not a person;

    the rules on page 36 of a random roleplaying game from your collection (if the first book has no rules on page 36, choose another until you get one with rules);
    (EDIT: Also, if you get a page that is so short the whole thing fits on you screen at the same time, you can skip it.)

    two of the following elements:
    sharashkas,
    dinosaurs,
    dreams,
    exotic dancers,
    finland,
    feng shui,
    poetry,
    motherhood,
    class warfare;

    and one of the following constraints:

    -- The rules from page 36 must address the random Wikipedia page specifically, and the two extra elements must be mechanically possible results stemming from the use of those rules;

    -- Characters must act self-destructively over the course of the game and see their resources dwindle to nothing before endgame decides their eventual fate via random resolution (influenced by previous in-game actions);

    -- The full game plays over the course of three sessions, or arcs, each one of which employs the rules from page 36, and meaningfully addresses an issue related to one of the three elements. The first one must be the random Wikipedia page, and it must also return throughout the following two sessions.
