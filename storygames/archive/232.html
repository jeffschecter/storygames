    Back in college, I used to use this dos-based tool called NNC, which was an awesome naming tool: You selected "dictionaries" of fragments to be combined, and it spit out 20 or so names every time you hit the "n" key. So you can say, like, "Load Japanese dictionary. OK, now spit out 20 names. Go! OK, Go! OK, Go!" And each list of 20 names I'd find about 1-8 that were good in structure and sound.

    Now, the cool thing about NNC was that you could mix and match dictionaries. Literally, my favorites were to mix Yiddish, Melnibonian and like Chinese or Persian, as it came out with some very cool, and yet *logically consistant* names.

    I haven't found a tool as awesome as that 10+ year old NNC tool... I've seen a graphical shareware one for Windows since, where you can build your own dictionaries (and dictionary for this tool, like NNC, means "particles inside names, combined to make a full name") for Dwarven Names, Indian Names, etc... But there was no feature that let you shuffle them together, to make "Dwarven Indian names" and the like, which was the coolest feature of NNC.

    I think you can still find it floating around out there, try hunting for "NNC Name Creator" or something.

    A baby name list

    What's not a real name: 'N'Hur'gh'th''
    What's a real name: Assab

    Some countries have an official name list. If the name's not on there, you can't name your child that. Russia and Austria have that rule, for instance. In the Netherlands, you have to apply for a name (leading to the occasional denial when you want to name your child something like Ningieon3norg88&34873hfish, which is not an actual denied name, but is indistinguishable from one I once read).

    Names have a tradition behind them. Look at the linguistic drift from Yehoshuah (my name in Hebrew) to Jesus (in Latin) and Joshua (in English). It has a meaning ("God of help", referring first to ending the Exodus, then the Christian apocalypse thing) and so you can figure out what kind of character would have it.

    Names are part of a language. Figure out what you want the language to be like (probably from its culture), and you'll have a font of names. If you've changed the language in particular, human ways ("p" becomes "b", "s" becomes "tz" and so forth) then change the name that way, too.
