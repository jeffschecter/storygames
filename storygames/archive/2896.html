    \n I am SO psyched about this. I loved CN0. Let me see if I can iterate why.

    When I go to a Gameday, even though I'm there with a lot of gamers, and even though I'm there with forum members I'm friends with, I still just can't bust out a game of TSOY and expect a huge attendance.

    I never went to camp as a kid. I'm kind of getting the whole camp thing. It's fun!

    I get to totally relax. At a con, you're in this mode (for me). It's defined by this weird hotel/restaurant environment. You have to do this ROI thing with getting your car out of the garage or getting on public transportation. For Nerdly, you go where you can walk and you don't worry about leaving (for me).

    I'm sure there are some other things, but that's what's on the top of my mind right now.

    Icebreaker ideas, anyone? I'd love to see a box of these where people can gather and throw down at any given point of the event, with people they don't know.

    \n one of my favorite icebreakers, that we used to do at larps to get everyones fake name right is called name tag. Its very physical. You play tag (staying in a smaller than normal boundry, like a tennis court instead of a baseball diamond) with normal "it" rules and tagging and everything. When you are about to be tagged you have to call out loud the name of another person participating in the game and suddenly that person becomes "it" and the person that was about to tag you isn't. When you are tagged (not when you are it) you're out, and generally you should stand on the sidelines and heckle.

    Its a lot of fun, it gets the blood up, and everyone learns everyone's name, and gets a chance to help each other/screw each other. There is a lot more strategy than you might think.
