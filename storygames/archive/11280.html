\n Ok, this is a sort of idea I have, which sounds kind of cool to me... i just wanted to know what you think about, and how would you translate into a mechanic, maybe (or if there's soem game which already does).
\nAs you may notice from the abundance of "sort","kinda", "maybe" and stuff like that, it's still quite fuzzy as a concept, so please bear with me.
\n
\nBasically, i was thinking that legendary and mythical characters are always in some way bound or compelled by their own legend (we could say, by the myth that bulids around them), and this often is a key part to their downfall, either by being totally ruled by this sort of larger-than-life destiny or by being defeated by its true opposite. Some examples of what i mean:
\n
\nKing Arthur is pure and chivalrous in a perfect way, and this virtue and courage alone make him able to face any threat (i guess that somewhere in the idea of the Perfect Kinght lies the assumption of not just facing anchy challenge, but actually overcoming it): the thing that ultimately brings him down it's the consequence of his only sinful deed, his bastard son Mordred.
\n
\nTydeus, the greek hero, is torn between his intelligent courage (he is protected and guided by the goddess Athena) and a bestial strength and violence dwelling inside him: in the moment this violence takes over, when he smashes his enemy's head and eats his brain, athena diverts his eyes from him just a moment, in which he is struck down.
\n
\nGoing more modern, the moment Paul-Muad'dib Atreides sees the future and starts using visions to guide his way, he is doomed to be trapped in his visions, without any chance to escape it. this is a case in which both the reader and the character are aware of this process (when he's young, he is often anxious about the Jihad he sees swarming the galaxy in his name)
\n
\nAs i always tried, in RPGs, to jump from the 'fantasy' to the 'myth' level and feeling, this is really something that i find interesting, and that maybe i would like to see as a mechanic: something like, the more the character gets larger-than-life and powerful, the more he is bound to a particular Fate. one way could be that of giving the character the chance to accomplish incredible deeds and influence the game reality in a very powerful way, but every time he does that he gets more trapped into a myth he won't be able to control. What to do, then? live on the edge? be free, in the shadows? or face your destiny, whatever it may be?
\n
\nis there a game accounting for such a narrative process? how would you develop such a mechanic or idea?

    \n White Wolf wrote Scion as well. Scion is about the modern day sons and daughters of the old gods being 'awakened' to their divine natures and kicking titan ass.

    A major part of the game is Legend. Legend is the general power level of the Scion, the pool they draw from to activate their awesome powers, and the curse of being more than a normal man.

    Whenever a Scion uses Legend in a scene to affect/harm/help someone, there's a chance (esoteric mechanic that needs simplifying) to see if that someone becomes "Fatebound" to the Scion. Fatebinding means the someone assumes a Fatebound Role (sidekick, nemesis, lover, mentor, apprentice, fan, traitor, etc) whenever the Scion is nearby.

    All these Fatebindings basically create the Scion's legend as his old friend becomes destined to hate him and the evil chick becomes destined to love him. All you'd need to do for the result you were looking for is to give the Scion himself a Fatebound Destiny that gets closer with each badass battle or maybe each time he causes a Fatebinding.

    ****

    HELLAS does something similar by starting every character with a Destiny and a Fate. The Destiny is the bad ass thing that you will accomplish if you live long enough. Fate is the horrible thing that will happen if you burn out too soon. Any time the player needs a little extra juice for a task, they can use Fate for a boost to their roll. A number of bonus points are added to the d20 roll for the task, but if the actual die reads 1-5 (no matter what the end total is) during a Fate roll, the character's Fate increases. A character only has 10 or so available steps on his Fate track.

    Also, mini-goals can be set by the player. Accomplishing those goals drops the character's Fate back down a step.
