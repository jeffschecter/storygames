\n So, as per request, here's a place to pre-pitch what you are bringing to Nerdly, in detail.

I'll go first.

Kagematsu:\n
"This game sets gender roles on their head, demanding a woman play the role of the male lead as the rest of the players convince him(her?) to make the ultimate promise.
The Village is under a deadly Threat, and all the able warriors are dead from war and disease. Only by cunning can the villager women win Kagematsu over. They will gain affections from him, from a simple Smile or Compliment, to the intense Shared Secret, Confession of Love, or scandalous Roll in the Hay. Every Affection 'unlocks' an equally intense Desperation, which can be used to threaten or cajole Kagematsu into yielding later on.
They will race to convince him to make The Promise, to face the Threat and save them all, but not too soon. Only the Love they earn with him will give him the strength to see his task through, but they will never be certain whether they earn Love or Pity. Kagematsu keeps his true intentions a secret (as Love is like warfare.)
A delicious gem of a game that sparks conversation every time"
\nFun, rotating scenes, so plenty of audience time too. (most players use this time to hone their own strategy) I facilitate, but don't play. It helps me referee what's going on, and takes some of the pressure off of being Kagematsu.


The Mountain Witch:\n
"As you watch the night sky start to gray over what appears to be a sea of clouds, you recall how you ended up on top of the icy slopes of Mount Fuji. As a ronin, you were use to a hard and lonely life, traveling from village to village, looking for whatever employment you could find. So naturally, when you were approached this time you quickly accepted the offer, though not fully realizing what you were getting into. Now you find yourself far from help on top this cold rock preparing for an assault on O-Yanma, the Mountain Witch himself. At your side is a group of men you neither know nor want to know. A group of men who all carry a similar story as yourself.

Who knows what the dawn of this day will bring? How will desperate men incapable of trust react when their only means of survival is to rely on each other?"
\nSlow burn, somewhere between 4 - 6 hours. Very player driven, creating a deeply interwoven mesh of lies and half-truths. Bloody, and awesome.

Annalise:\n
"Annalise is a game for making Vampire stories. You and your friends take on the roles of a shadowy creatures lovers, allies and enemies. What is watching you from the darkness? Why is is attracted to you? How will the story end? Answer these questions for yourself with this game of pain, hunger and redemption."
\nA very creatively demanding game that threads together (seemingly) random people as they face a looming threat. I love the instant feedback that the "claims" mechanic provides, and how easy it is to engage with your fellow player's stories.

Puppetland:\n
"The skies are dim always since the Maker died. The lights of Puppettown are the brightest beacon in all of Puppetland, and they shine all the time. Once the sun and the moon moved their normal course through the heavens, but no more. The rise of Punch the Maker-Killer has brought all of nature to a stop, leaving it perpetually winter, perpetually night. Puppets all across Puppetland mourn the loss of the Maker, and curse the name of Punch--but not too loudly, lest the Nutcrackers hear and come to call with a sharp rap-rap-rapping at the door."
\nReturning with this one, hopefully we can wrap up the game from last time... or just continue it?
60 minutes, everything you say is in character, no dice, negotiated narrative. (I usually say it's like playing chess instead of playing Chutes and Ladders... there's no dice, but that doesn't mean there's no strategy or challenge.) Had a great time and hope to repeat

    \n I want to run:

    Sagas of the Icelanders, which takes the Apocalypse World engine and dumps it squarely in the world of semi-mythic Iceland, 1000 AD. The Big Deals in the setting are the Big Deals in the sagas - gender, politics, violence and its stupid endless repercussions, and the coming of Christianity. It's super fun. No knowledge required but I will have you bustin' the \u0e23\u0e10's before the session is over.

    The Tribunal, which is a 12-person larp that takes about two hours to play. It's about a military unit in a totalitarian regime that needs to get its story straight before appearing before a ... tribunal. It is intense and the stakes for the characters could not be higher. It's also super fun and I am really hoping I can get enough people to play it late on Friday or Saturday night, like midnight.

    Durance is my new game about the evolution of power on a failing prison planet. I think it's pretty cool! It's GMless and we collaboratively build a planet and colony, build a pair of characters on opposite sides of the fence, and watch it all gloriously go to hell.

    Task Force Roach is my miniatures rules. Basically I obsessively built a whole Iraqi town and you get to have a gigantic fight in it at 1:72 scale. It is fast-paced and tense. From the coalition side you've got insurgents mixed in with the local population and difficult objectives. From the insurgent side you are outnumbered, way outgunned and have invaders at your front door.

    \n I know we're all kind addicted to the new, but I wanted to gauge some interest in some games so that I know what to pack and bring:

    Tenra Bansho Zero: Magic, technology, society and culture. A ninja, a betrothed geisha, a samurai and a faceless worm-user komuso monk have four hours to assassinate a god and rewrite the fate of the world. http://www.tenra-rpg.com

    Maid RPG: The yakuza shows up to take the mansion from the young master. How do you get it back? http://maidrpg.com

    Shinobigami: A Japanese "Story Game", a complete campaign in four hours. Basically think of it like a combination of FIASCO with dynamic fight rules and scenes, and every Burning Wheel convention game you've ever played (scene one: "The MacGuffin is at your feet. GO!"). Everyone is a ninja tied to a clan in a White-Wolf style World of Darkness setting, where Shinobi clans control the flow of history. Every character has a goal that you need to fulfill, and many of the goals are somewhat (if not COMPLETELY) in conflict.
    http://j-rpg.com/talk/discussion/48/shinobigami/p1
    http://yarukizero.wordpress.com/tag/shinobigami/

    Ryuutama: Heartwarming western fantasy. A bunch of Normal People (not heroes, not adventurers) get the strange feeling that many in the world get: The call to travel, to go on a long journey. You've turned over your shops, farms, and trades, found others with the same spirit, and set off into the dangerous world. A mysterious dragon watches over you. Think of it like "Hao Miyazaki's Oregon Trail"http://www.j-rpg.com/ryuutama

    Whisper or chime in, but only if you have a real interest. I only want to bring the materials for something that'll likely get played, thanks!

    -Andy

    \n I am bringing 13th Age. It's the ALL NEWNESS, second-round playtest edition (which is basically like the first-round edition). It's D&D with a little more player agency and "story stuff." It's like 3E with some of the cooler ideas from 4E but it feels more like 0E. It's all I want to play anymore, if that tells you anything.

    My games are set in Tenrook, a city much like 7th Century Constantinople in size. Its population of half a million has dwindled to a mere 50,000 in the last decade due to plagues and loss of grain rights. The agents of the High Druid are reclaiming the city block by block, and the remaining citizens have moved to the inner walled section. The outer bands of Tenrook are called the citywilds. The city is also built on the ancient dwarven capital, Scaffold, which was abandoned hundreds of years ago and magically sealed up. Someone's broken those seals and terrible things are erupting out into the streets of Tenrook. Dwarven ambassadors entreat the Prince of Tenrook to help them reseal Scaffold while tomb raiders plunder the old city. Meanwhile, the Prince seems estranged from his bride-to-be, the elven Princess Evening Star. Some say it's because he's changing into an orc but people point out that they've been apart for a while.

    In my campaigns, you can play a Gargoyle: a city statue that has come to life. Some are monstrous, some are pretty. Some stone, some bronze, some wood. They're all strangely tied to the neighborhoods they come from.
