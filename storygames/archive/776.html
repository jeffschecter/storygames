    Oooh, Beast Hunters, definitely. Salute in!

    Many of the best conflicts in Dogs revolve around disagreements among the Dogs themselves, so you lose that aspect. I'd suggest making sure all the personalities in the town are very big, and making sure any of them will have powerful motivations not only to hinder the solo Dog, but also to help him as well.

    Funny thing, that one on one game was the first time I actually met Jason- I was the other player. And, also, that was one of the greatest Dogs games I was in.

    You do lose the Dogs all being their own paladin and spiraling off in different dircections, and if the dice are stacked against you (you get cold rolls) you will get owned, as there's no backup. Other than that, just run it like a normal Dogs game.

    Jason was really good at not doing the Call of Cthulhu thing. Rather, I would walk up to someone, say "Hi, how are you?" and they'd be like, "Well, son, I'd be doing much better if those McFarleys would stop practicing Devil worship." BAM, throw the conflicts at the Dog. Don't make them dig too hard, cause that's not what it's about. Then again, that's pretty much standard Dogs advice.
