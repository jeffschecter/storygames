\n Hey, I'm looking for a filmography of fiasco movies. These are films that:

1. Are focused; location is character.
2. Feature small, flawed people with large ambition, for good or ill.
3. Degenerate into disastrous capers. If nobody dies it won't go on my list.
4. Ensemble pieces. it can be about one dude's themes, but there need to be meaty roles all around.

Some examples: Most of the Coen brothers' output (Blood Simple is the shining light, but Miller's Crossing, Burn After Reading, and Fargo), A Simple Plan, No Country for Old Men, that sort of thing.

I'm particularly looking for good examples from genre (Unforgiven, maybe) and non-Hollywood films (Coup de Torchon, for sure).

Thanks for your thoughts!

    \n Hmmm.... roles. OK, let's unpack it (think of Very Bad Things, as I recall it).

    You'll have someone with a hazy moral status (or an over-developed sense of altruism) who accidentally (or without thinking through it) gets involved in a very dangerous situation, social or physically. Others have a bond or relationship with that person, enough to make them consider helping correct (or resist or cover-up) the problem. At least one of the others is "on edge" or somehow willing to "go to 11" to deal with the initial situation. That will (somehow?) directly bump into a still-worse character, who will further threaten the initial mistake-maker, churning the cycle further. Further complications can happen--people's will breaks, betrayals, sheer accidents--and someone dies. Repeat as necessary to close all the loose ends (even if that means everyone dies).

    You know... the Big Lebowski suddenly comes to mine, though without the deadly consequences. (Or do some folks die? Can't recall....)
