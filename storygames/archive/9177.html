\n Just wanted to give a small report based on my experiences with this paper for the past 18 months or so.

So about a year and a half ago, I opened this thread over here, requesting recs for a specific type of graph paper I had seen within engineering organizations, but haven't seen on the streets. One search led to another, and next thing I was buying pads of this:


the paper


action shot!

and this:


the paper


action shot!

...for gaming puroses (actually the graph paper one I use for work, home, pretty much everything).

In short: Awesome.

My mind has always worked best on this kind of paper: The graph paper parts for writing, the blank space for sidelining. I used similar paper all throughout college in everything from Philosophy to Japanese. Turns out that it really works well for RPG-related stuff as well. The graph paper is where I put all my campaign and scenario notes, and I use the sidelines to make note of stuff I create on the fly, including names, NPCs, notes about directions the PCs take in new directions, etc.

The storyboard paper works well, too. I often put a description of an encounter in the actual box (notes, maps, etc) and put linear notes about outcomes, or stats, in the lined area on the right. It's perfect for scene or encounter-based games like TSOY.

Quality-wise, it's rock solid too. The paper is "60 bond", no idea what the hell that means, but it's thick and sturdy for writing. MEATY PAPER.

Anyway, just wanted to throw that out there, for folks who like taking their RPG notes on graph paper, and might be interested in a new way of doing it.

LEVENGER.COM

Direct link to the paper/notebooks section of their site.
