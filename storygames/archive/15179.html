\n I feel a bit bad this my very first post, but I promise not to promote and dash. I was steered to the site by someone who saw our Kickstarter project, and after taking a look around, I know this is someplace I intend to stay and be involved in.

I'm currently funding the first production run of my tabletop storytelling game, Bhaloidam.

With Bhaloidam, you move tokens across a Lifewheel, a personal game board, to represent your character and track your Influence upon the storyworld. By focusing on the types of relationships that exist across all genres, rather than lists of specific skills or actions, Bhaloidam enables you to play out any type of story, from soap opera to kung fu extravaganza. And by placing full control over their performances in the hands of the players, Bhaloidam encourages a distributed, cooperative style of play that allows everyone to participate equally.



Please visit our Kickstarter page and watch the introductory video. More detailed information can be found on our site here: bhaloidam.com/news.

Feel free to ask me any questions you might have and I'll do my best to answer them!

    \nMore Ways To Give!

    Brian Pederson is a Danish educator who believes that games are critical to a child\u2019s education and social development. In early 2010 he started a program called Kids Need to Game. KNtG solicites donations of new and used games for a games club at his school. The games are available through checkout in the school library.

    Now, nearly two years later, Brian runs this program for 5 schools and a summer program. More than 250 students are involved. And to help with this growth, Brian has started a fundraising project of his own.

    When we heard about KNtG, we knew we wanted to contribute in a big way. And because the design of Bhaloidam is in complete accord with Brian\u2019s belief in the power of games to teach, we wanted to do more than just give him money towards his humble goal of $2000 (which we\u2019re also going to do, by the way). We contacted Brian and made a proposal, which he eagerly accepted.

    So we\u2019ve launched a new backer reward today at the $27 pledge level. 100% of every pledge we receive that accompanies the new backer reward will go to providing Kids Need to Game with copies of Bhaloidam for their library. At minimum, we\u2019d like to provide them with 10 Skein Packs and 10 Spinnder bundles, but we\u2019ll let you set the maximum!
