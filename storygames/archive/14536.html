\n Happy Solstice! After a bit of break to catch up with ourselves, the good Judges of the RPG Solitaire Challenge are going to start writing about the winners of the challenge. We had 11 favorites all told and over the summer we'll post about the games in public forums or blogs to let folks know about the games, maybe help along the games development and all around revel in the good stuff people came up with.

I'll post links to the posts here as they come up. I'm working on one about What's the Frequency, Kenneth?, a game Mae Brussell (the Queen of Conspiracies) would have loved.

And in the meantime, we'd love to hear about what's been happening with all the games! Update us here.

Vive la solitaire!

    \n To be honest, I'm getting my solitaire kicks not from Storyleaves, which I'm calling "done" - apparently it's big in Spain (?!?) - but from pretending to be several people and playing multiplayer games both of my own design and other people's. I'm in the middle of a Danger Patrol session right now. Just determined that Lex Napier did, in fact, plant the bomb on Phantom Jack's spaceship - he was always jealous of Phantom Jack and the strange artifact they found on Planet X ... and something's brewing in the Temple of the Mystic Order - the last Steward was killed in the Inner Circle are pretty upset that the mystic calling himself "The Operative" didn't deign to show up for an emergency meeting ...

    More fun than videogames. For me, anyway.
