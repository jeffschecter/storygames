    Posted By: ziphtMichael, My wfile and I played CE at Dreamation 08, and had a blast.

    It's good to find you online, I have been wanting to take a closer look at your game. I can't wait to take it for a spin.
    \nHey, Nicholas! Great to "see" you 'round these parts as well. It was a great time playing with you and your wife, as well as everyone else in that crew. Your feedback at the end of the session was really useful as well.

    As for you playing the game, go ahead man! I'd love to hear some feedback from you about your experiences with the game. And hey, it's free right now, so you can't really beat the price.
    \n
    I see that trust was dropped we never did use it at Dreamation did we?
    \nYeah, the Trust mechanic was dead weight. I had been considering dropping it before Dreamation, and when I played with the crew you were in that pretty much clinched it. Instead, I'm refocusing on Vices and how they impact play. \n
    Most likely I will be demoing this for friends soonish.. Any advice? What Movie should I ask them to watch?
    \nThe first bit of advice I can give is to wait another week until I have the Shotgun Bible, my beta playtest, posted. That'll have a lot of tips on how to play the game in it, stuff I've learned at my own table. How to manage scenes and keep the pace fast and all of that good stuff. It'll also be reorganized so that it should be a pretty fun, light read with tons of dialogue that'll set the mood for gameplay, as well as bunches of examples.

    Pimping aside, my best advice is to put together a group of at three to five players that are up for the tension and struggle that is inherent to a good heist story. I make no bones about the fact that CE isn't written for dysfunctional gaming groups. The game is about desire and bullets. Players have to know that going in.

    As for inspirational movies... start with the one that inspired me the most: Heat. Michael Mann directing Al Pacino and Robert DeNiro in one of my all-time favourite films, and a cornerstone of the heist film genre. If you had to pick one movie to ask your crew to watch, it's Heat, hands down. If you guys are itching for more heist movies after your sessions of CE, I would suggest Sexy Beast, Killing Zoe, Reservoir Dogs, the director's cut version of Payback, The Taking of Pelham 1 2 3, Charlie Varrick, Inside Man, and a metric assload more. \n
    Also do what Con's do you plan on attending this year?
    \nWell, DexCon is definitely in the mix, as is PhilCon. Beyond that, I really want to go to GenCon but probably won't be able to afford it, what with trying to publish a book at the end of the year and all. After the book is published, or at least an ashcan is out, then I'll be much more mobile in terms of convetioneering. How's about yourself, man?
