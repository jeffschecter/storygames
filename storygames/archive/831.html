I'm not really one to run around going "these games rawk" and such. I'm generally not a big booster of SGs, at least, not on the internet.

But I've just gotta gush over what happened at the table tonight.

There's this guy in our group: let's call him Al. Great guy, we're good friends, he's easy-going but maybe a little on the introverted side. In fact, we'd known each other for well over 4 years before I ever found out he was into gaming. And it was his wife who had to tell me...

That's all stage dressing. The meat of the thing is this: he's always sworn up, down, left and right that he's "not a good roleplayer".

And yet, tonight he was the one grabbing the Town by the horns and messing with my NPCs in our kick-off Dogs game.

Man, if there's one thing that I *love* about SGs is that they've taught me & mine that "good roleplaying" does not equal "sounding as cool and slick as Deniro".

'cuz you know what?

    Let's put it this way:

    Typically, Al is the sort of player who will hang back from a conflict unless you actually confront him directly ("the sniper shoots at YOU"). And even then, his reactions are typically defensive in nature. I always (internally) bemoan the fact that no matter what I do, I'm just spurring him to inaction.

    That's what was different last night --

    1) introductory confict. It flowed right off of my question -- it was like this:

    me: So, what do you hope to gain from training? What that means is th--
    al: Uphold my family's reputation.
    me sits slack-jawed, flabbergasted over not getting to drag stakes out of Al.

    2) game proper.

    So, the dogs ride into town. I'm all busy throwing stuff at them -- how the town wasn't expecting them (shouldn't the town know that they're coming?), there's a crew working on a washed out road, everyone in town is all immaculately dressed, blah blah blah.

    The players are all soaking it in, asking probing questions, but mostly just setting stuff, like "is there a general store" sort of stuff. But every one's paying attention.

    Then, the community gathers to greet the dogs, sort of in a disorganized fashion. The Steward starts to say "hi" and there's this obvious rift in the crowd -- there's an entire clique of people who look nonplussed that the steward is speaking for them.

    I'm all busy getting ready to go on with GM narration when...

    al: Who's the ringleader?
    me: pause, disbelieving Um, this one tall lady in the middle of this gaggle of chattering old biddies.
    al: to tall lady Why do you question the Steward's right to speak for you?

    And you can guess where it went from there. It was a no-holds-barred awesomefest.

    I just can't stress enough how cool it is that the system rewards the players for giving a flying *#$% about the game. It's all about getting your mitts into it, in whatever way is comfortable for you the player.

    First person "dialogue" style narration? Sure, we can do that.
    3rd person "my guy does..."? Yup.
    Group kibbitzing? Hell, yeah.
    any number of other possible ways of introducing stuff into the SIS? un-hunh.

    Choose your flavour, boyz...hell, choose 'em all.
