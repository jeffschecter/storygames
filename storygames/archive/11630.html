\n I have been eager to play FreeMarket since I got my hands on the beta. It is definitely not a game that shows all its strengths in a convention game, but it doesn't show its ass either. We whipped up characters - a space biologist, a gambler, a rock star, a researcher/hacker, and an antiquities dealer. I wish I could have played the game across multiple sessions because some of the concepts would have had to be reworked as life on FreeMarket station requires some wrapping your brain around the way Flow and everything works.

The MRCZ was called The Pragmatists and their goal was to be successful and get Flow. Pretty straightforward. The first challenge was for them to make celebratory drinks using their new 3-D matter printer that they got through forming the MRCZ. It was a simple challenge, but none of them had Printing or any Interfaces or Tech to help with Printing so they struggled a bit here. In the end they failed the Challenge and got glasses of gray goo that smelled like burning tires.

The player of the rock star was, up to this point, playing his character like a bit of an in-your-face-jerk. There was another MRCZ sharing their HQ, a converted cargo container, with them, so he got the idea of getting them to drink the grey goo. Yeah. He got Frownied big-time which caused his Flow to tank.

The change in the way the player was playing the character was instant and dramatic. He immediately did his best to smooth things over with the other MRCZ, being helpful and friendly. They eventually revoked the frowny after he was contrite for awhile.

After that Cobblepot, the creator of the rock star (who was a blank), showed up and needed help. His MRCZ broke up and he needed to join a new one, so the Pragmatists decided to put on a show showcasing his talents and invite a bunch of MRCZ's to the show. The show was a success, some of the Pragmatists were able to socially engineer a MRCZ into accepting Cobblepot into their MRCZ, and much Flow was had, especially after Cobblepot sent them all Friend Requests. He also gifted the rock star with Axeaxe, a weaponized double-guitar.

The players got a little frustrated at one point I think, but I think that was because they had a hard time wrapping their minds around all the resources available to them. They burned some tech in the final scene in order to win the big Ephemera challenge (the rock show itself) and when they did they won easily and raked in the Flow.

The actual Challenges were a bit tedious, but that's because we were working from one hand-written deck that I wrote on index cards. When I get the actual cards from the Freemarket set and every player can have their own deck it will make things more tactile for the players. But no way was I hand-writing 180 cards.

I wouldn't run it again as a convention game because I believe it will shine in multi-session play where memories turn into Experiences, and the MRCZ grows, and the players figure out what they want out of the game and get a chance to affect the station.
