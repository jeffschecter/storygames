    \n We did this a few years ago - watch the movie, then play a short-format (1-3 session) game that springboards off it. One drawback - it was tricky to find movies that neatly encapsulated a genre. Let's see if I recall what we used...

    The Breakfast Club - high school friendship drama with FATE 2. Pretty good game.

    Pale Rider - "threatened community" Western with DitV. Meh.

    Don't recall what movie (we might not have been able to agree on one) - "road trip" light drama with modified DitV. Rocked.

    Cocoon - vaguely New-Wave-y first contact SF with FATE 2. Decent start, unfinished due to schedule conflicts.

    \n I'll slip in a link to our "Lessons Learned" from hosting a weekly gaming event at the local club. Our time frame doesn't permit us to have a movie and a game for the weekly games, but I think most of the discussion would still apply. The weekly grab-bag games have been a tremendous lot of fun for the past several years. Thanks to it, we've had a chance to try a bunch of game systems and settings, and get more savvy about what works and what doesn't for each of us.
