    \n I know nothing about the actual Rocky and Bullwinkle game (designed by Warren Spector? Really? It boggles my mind... turns out he also made Toon and Spelljammer.)

    But I keep watching DVDs of Rocky and Bullwinkle with my child and thinking its heavily ritualized structure would easily make a Story Game. R&B goes like this: Rocky and Bullwinkle get involved in some adventure, which Boris and Natasha interfere in. Events reach a crisis, whereupon the announcer give two possible titles for the next episode. Then you go to one of the secondary cartoons: Dudley Do-Right, Fractured Fairy Tales, etc. At least one of these (Aesop and Son) is explicitly a conflict between the two titles characters (to get the best moral for the story). Then the show returns to Rocky and the heroes narrowly escape the cliffhanger.

    So I think the secondary cartoons are the conflict resolution for the game: When a crisis is reached, the two competing players name titles for how they want the story to go. Then they play a minigame (randomly determined?) that decides who will get to narrate the outcome of the conflict. The version we see in the actual TV show nearly always has Rocky's player win the conflict, though.

    \n The rules are really, really simple.

    You tell the story. When you come to a decision point, spin the spinner appropriate to your character.

    If you get a yes, you succeed.
    If you get something else, the GM makes an interpretation.

    If you get into a conflict with another character, both you and the controller of the other character spin your spinner. If both come up yes, you have to come to some sort of compromise.

    If both come up no, the GM thinks of an amusing way for you both to fail.

    If you ever spin one of the special notches on your spinner, you get to make up something that goes along with that... that... I guess you could call it a knack.
    Or a pre-programmed event. Anyway, you get to work it into the story.

    I used to have fun making up spinners. I think I've still got one. I'll post it if I can find it and scan it.
