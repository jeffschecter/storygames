    Finalized:

    1001 Nights (Paul Tevis) - Friday 8pm
    System: 1001 Nights
    Description: In the palace of the Sultan are many rooms. In these
    rooms are many people, and the people have many stories. Most intriguingly
    for our tale, many of these people cannot leave these many rooms. The
    ornately carved walls surrounding the fragrant pools and exotic gardens
    are high; the lace-like doors are webs of iron; the delicately cool tones
    and publicly refined manners raise the significance of an arched brow by
    feverish degrees. This 'place apart' holds the Sultan's peace, for to
    disturb it would be foolish indeed. Yet, beneath the surface of any still
    pool are many currents, and this is no exception. Beginners Welcome.
    Players: 4-6

    Cold City (Paul Tevis) - Saturday 10am
    System: Cold City
    Description: As the Cold War rages in the full glare of the world
    media, the Underground War is fought in ruined bunkers, dank tunnels,
    building sites, and bombed out apartment blocks. In the divided city of
    Berlin, the Reserve Police Agency hunts down monsters left over from
    sinister experiments and twisted technology. Things from beyond our space
    and time, strange creatures altered by bizarre machines, the decayed
    corpses of undead soldiers, things that hide in the darkness. Beginners
    Welcome. Mature Players Only.
    Players: 4-6

    Ravenelfs (Jesse) - Saturday 3pm
    System: Elfs
    Description: Do you like to kill things and take their
    stuff? Even better, do you think that's kind of a
    silly way for an adventurer to spend his time? If so,
    then come play Elfs, a satirical send up of the
    hobby's most pervasive game and its classic I6
    Ravenloft module. Beginners Welcome. Mature Players
    Only.
    Players: 6-8

    Dogs in the Vineyard (Joshua BishopRoby) - Saturday 8pm
    System: Dogs in the Vineyard
    Description: You are God's Watchdogs, protecting
    the Faithful in a West that never quite was. You are
    responsible for justice and judgement, both of which
    may have to come from the barrel of a gun. Mature
    players only. Beginners Welcome.
    Players: 4-6

    In Death's Wake (Jesse Burneko) - Sunday 10am
    System: InTERRORgation
    Description: InTERRORgation casts the players as
    suspects in a mystery and the GM as the detective.
    Played out as an after-the-fact interrogation
    inter-cut with flashback scenes, this game offers an
    unusual role-playing approach to the mystery genre.
    Beginners Welcome. Mature Players Only.
    Players: 6-8

    Primetime Adventures (Joshua BishopRoby) - Sunday 3pm
    System: PTA
    Description: We will be playing from a blank scratch pad
    up, collaboratively creating a television series and playing through
    the pilot and perhaps one episode in the season arc. What kind of
    show will it be? We'll find out on Sunday. Beginners Welcome.
    Players: 6-8

    Shab al-Hiri Roach (Jesse Burneko) - Sunday 8pm
    System: The Shab-al-Hiri Roach
    Description: The Shab-al-Hiri Roach is a dark comedy
    of manners, lampooning academia and asking players to
    answer a difficult question - are you willing to
    swallow a soul-eating telepathic insect bent on
    destroying human civilization? No? Even if it will
    get you tenure? Experience this fun, GM-less
    alternative approach to Lovecraftian horror.
    Beginners Welcome. Mature Players Only.
    Players: 6-8

    September 2006 edited September 2006
    The Open RPG Area had zero traffic. So while I understand that you weren't talking about waiting on traffic to start games, I'm saying that even if we scheduled games there, there wouldn't be any traffic to watch/get snagged. This is, I suspect, why the room was axed. Accounts of the east-coast cons always confuse me; apparently over there all gaming takes place in big rooms with many tables, and Open Gaming is always hopping. Totally different experience.

    The Strategicon cons are really like five cons at once. There are RPGers, Boardgamers, Wargamers, CCGers, and RPGAfolk all there, and lots of them stay in their own little corners of the convention. Average attendance is, I understand, around 2000.

    Rayston, it's all about establishing a reputation and a name. The first time won't be crazy cool, but it should at least be satisfying in that you run a couple good games. The next time there'll be people you recognize and know and who recognize and know you. If you keep at it, it will not be long before people start seeking you out as the source of the new hotness. In our case, it took us three cons before we already had groupies.
