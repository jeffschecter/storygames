\n Hi there. Vincent Baker's sales numbers have inspired me to post this here, and in some other places.

In October, I decided to see if you really can make money off of this whole "100 Items" list thing you probably have seen on drivethrurpg and other places. The first few lists were my own, but soon others got in on the idea, and I was taking list submissions, formatting them to look nice (something a lot of the competition didn't seem to bother with), and taking a slight (20%) cut. Now we have over 200 items, and have grown beyond lists.

What started as a joke has become a fairly profitable part time job.

Here is a chart of our sales to date:


You can see that things have fallen off sharply, probably because we aren't the new guys any more, but also because I was sort of breaking the rules and posting more than two items per day in the beginning. When I corrected that, sales fell. In February, the list submissions started drying up, adding to the decline. March is looking good though.

November, our best month:
Total Sold: 1476\tGross Sales: $1537.40

February, a month that saw only 9 new submissions:
Total Sold: 400\t\tGross Sales: $470.08

Grand total, since October:
Total Sold: 5647\tGross Sales: $5228.82

So, I'm just sharing this in the interests of openness, and since there aren't a lot of good sales numbers out there for RPG stuff.

    \n Most of the sales come from the new releases section, so getting new lists out every day and keeping something of ours on there is important.

    Aside from that and the other tools that DTRPG has for promotion (banner ads, featured products), the only real promotion I have done is a rare post on some facebook groups and rpg.net. I post new lists and promotions on facebook and twitter too, and one reviewer/blogger guy retweets some stuff to his decent sized (at least in comparison to me) audience. We also had some products included in that pathfinder mmo kickstater pdf bundle. I'm not a fan, but we have some pathfinder products and including them and getting our name out there to the backers was worth the almost zero effort it took to get included.

    Promotion is something I plan on working on more, now that we are pretty much in the second half of our first year of existence.
