    \n I've been playing lots of "Brothers In Arms: Hell's Highway" on the xbox, and it's got a core "lay down suppressing fire and flank" teamwork mechanic that would translate well into 4E with very little modification.

    First, let Defenders' 'mark' powers work at range, with a gun, and call it "cover fire". 4E HP are already an ability to absorb threat without being compromised, not a literal ability to take axes to the face. Second, make it so that Flanking works at range unless the target has cover. Third, make the Warlord's various "Attack that guy in melee" power work at range.

    That way, a Rifleman (Fighter) lays down suppressing fire that doesn't score the kills, but directs the target's attention and forces an inefficient response (via Marks and the Fighter's high HP) while the Marksman (Ranger) actually lays down the really effective fire, and the Commando (Rogue) sneaks around for a clear shot. The Officer (Warlord) directs the fire most efficiently (at-will "you, attack him!" powers), and yells at people to keep their heads down, reducing their risk profile (via healing powers).
