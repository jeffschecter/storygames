\n I am looking for a fantasy RPG where character creation is as simple as choosing two or three words (maybe a few more...?). It might form a phrase like, "Mighty Elephantman Warrior" or not. Each word has actual mechanical weight. So, Warrior supplies fighty bonuses and Mighty makes strength-based tasks easier. Simple stuff.

There might still be a system for equipment and advancement... The underlying mechanics might not be as simple as the chargen process.

This isn't the same as, say, D&D where attributes, race and class have tons of mechanical implications. That said, I'm not 100% sure I know what I'm talking about here. Yesterday the sun cooked my brain whilst using my skull as a pot.

I'm sure this thing exists somewhere but my memory can't seem to recall. If it doesn't exist and my subconscious mind created it while I wasn't looking, I'm sorry.

    \n The standard version of FUBAR links these traits with a series of keywords that provide bonuses in specific situations...each trait gets five keywords, of which two are chosen at character generation and XP allows more to be purchased later to increase the versatility of the traits). I realise that this system may not be for you if you\u2019re after something with a bit more situational crunch, I\u2019ve toyed with the notion of tying specific trait keywords into system mechanisms (especially in the FUBAR supplements I've already released) but found that the style of play my groups have found these mechanistic links more restrictive than flavour inducing.

    I guess it could be said that genre conventions are simply limits on the narrative, and this type of linking between system and story help to promote specific styles of story. I just haven\u2019t found a good way to do this without seeming to interfere too heavily in the flow of the narrative.

    \n With minimal effort you could use the Dresden Files RPG using the Quick Character Creation and On-the-Fly City/World Creation to run a game of that start style by just generating a high concept (Mighty Elephantman Warrior, Outcast Priest of Ganoosh, British Playboy Superspy etc). Technically the game calls for choosing your best skill and any template you want but honestly those can come out in play by just paying off the FATE cost of the template when you introduce/reveal it.

    The system is a bit 'crunchier' mechanically than many story games but the furnace of the system is FATE points cycling through the characters aspects (the starting high concept is one aspect, usually the broadest). While technically intended to produce an Urban Fantasy in the vein of the Dresden novels it can be drifted into other genres and settings almost effortlessly.
