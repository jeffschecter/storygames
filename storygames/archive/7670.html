\n
\n
\n
\n\nDave Younce \n\n\n

\n
\n
\n
\n
\n So you know how, in every town or city, there's lots of places that have a commonly known purpose that's outside of what city planners had in mind? I need some of those, setting-independent is nice. Like:

The place where gangs of youths gather to taunt or challenge one another
The place where duels or planned fights are held
The place where men of rank or privilege who require discretion look for ladies of the night
The place where the police won't go after dark
The place where street-performers congregate

So when you think about your town, its hangouts, neighborhoods, and landmarks, what do you see and what is it used for?

    \n The smoky lower-class where City Council goes after its meetings
    The gas station at the corner where all the fights start
    The garage in the alley where the old men hang out and listen to music (one block over from my house!)
    The restaurant in the basement where the office crowd goes for lunch
    The bar that once was a warehouse
    The library where the homeless congregate
    The unassuming drug house on the corner
    The poorly-lit parking lot behind the restaurant

    I've been spending a lot of time trying to get to know Peoria in this way. Like, places have different meanings to different people, and I want to know all those meanings. Especially the less savory ones, because that way maybe I'll understand my city better. Maybe I'm being overly poetic, but I think of them as "the secret names of places". I want to know those names.

    And yeah, this came out of my work on Dirty Secrets.

    Seth Ben-Ezra
    Great Wolf
