\n I'm on the hunt for a game that works well remotely (using VOIP or some such) and suits three players.

I've been playing Dark Heresy and Savage Worlds over Skype with a couple of old gamers. We use that software Fantasy Grounds for rolling dice. The setup works pretty well, but now I'm itching to go GM-full. In fact, I've been wanting to go down this road for some time but the guys were a little traditional in their tastes. Their tastes may have changed. To that end, I'm looking for...

* a character driven game where each player has control over one character
* a game without a GM
* a game with little or no components (other than dice and a couple of sheets)
* a game that is optimised for three players
* a game that takes three to six sessions of play

I'd prefer recommendations for games that don't require much in the way of tampering. Like, we could play a GM-less version of PTA (if we found a good way to use cards remotely), for example, but I'd prefer to pick a game that didn't need a rework of the rules.

Whaddya think?

    \nThis is, beyond the shadow of a doubt, the perfect situation for Gun Thief.

    It's specifically for 3 players.
    There are 3 distinct roles (The Law, The Jagged Women, The Gun Thief). While there is no GM per se, The Law's job includes scene framing and introducing lots of the straight-up adversaries.
    The game requires only character sheets. It is diceless, and involves making hard choices and some list choosing.

    It's about a down-and-out thug, or perhaps a heartsick antihero, tryin' a get by in a world what's cruel and horrible. It's about the shitshow gangsters that won't leave well enough alone, and cops more corrupt that lucifer, and those steamy dames that could get ya doin' almost anything for a taste of their tongues.

    It's about a guy, who doesn't want to be a bad person, or even an interesting person... but who nevertheless gets dragged down some ugly roads.

    Structured Freeform, Quick, Easy to Learn & Play, Requiring No Material Components.
    I did lots of my playtesting as PBP, and it sang in that medium.

    Inspirations: Sin City, Pulp Fiction, No Country for Old Men, Grand Theft Auto, Apocalypse World, Black Snake Moan, Kill Bill (proving that the Gun Thief isn't always male, though often), the music of Buck 65.

    The PDF is $3. For $3, you could be up and running tomorrow.

    \n Shock: fits the bill, if everyone likes social sci-fi. It's not specifically for three players but based on my experience with the game I'd say three is a fairly ideal number, especially for a multi-session game. The dice mechanic is extremely simple, so no problem for online play. It might serve as a nice bridge towards GM-full games as well, since it does still have a fairly strong GM-player dynamic -- the roles simply rotate -- while also including crucial input from the third+ player.
