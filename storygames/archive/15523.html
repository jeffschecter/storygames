\n So to begin with, I just wanted to tell you all about an awesome twist my characters threw in for our LB game. They made it to Nightport and confronted the local scoundrels to get information about the whereabouts of our infamous pirate king. I pulled the Mountain Witch Trick and said upon the mention of his name, everyone went quiet. There was a look of shock and terror on their faces. I asked them collectively "Why?"
\n
\nThis was the point where they were finally getting comfortable with adding details to the setting/story. They collectively decided he was amassing a fleet to oppose the Empire. His secret weapon: a phonograph recording of our wonderful Lady Blackbird's voice. He used this as a song to control the sky squid. This is the reason he was in love with her....she was his weapon. How does LB feel about being used like this? What are the ramifications of the knowledge that LB's song can control the squid? We will find out next session!
\n
\nQuestion Time!
\n
\nThere are three possible outcomes to a die roll:
\n
\n1) success of the die roll; success of the action
\n2) failure of the die roll; success of the action BUT there is a condition
\n3) failure of the die roll: ? of the action BUT there is a twist/escalation
\n
\nIs the "?" a success or failure? If the action is a fail, can they try again? For instance: the jail cell. If they fail, are they still locked in AND the alarm goes off? If they are able to try again, what if they fail again? And again? Same situation with the Owl being grappled by a sky squid.
\n
\nFor each obstacle, are the individual obstacles listed under the overarching obstacle minor hurdles that must collectively be overcome in succession to overcome the overarching obstacle? Or is it a choice of which obstacle to overcome to get past the entire thing? Maybe a mix of both?

    \n Your number two is a rule from Mouse Guard, not Lady Blackbird.

    The dice outcomes for LB are:
    1. Success: you overcome the obstacle.
    2. Fail: You don't overcome the obstacle, and the situation escalates.

    There are no hard rules about trying again. If the situation allows you to try again, then you can. If not, not.
