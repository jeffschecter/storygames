\n Over in the Stuff to Watch thread I mentioned that I recently self-published a book while trying out several different venues for selling it. I wrote a lengthy blog post about the whole experience, but overall I was particularly happy with Amazon's CreateSpace, and found it better than Lulu. Even so, I'm definitely interested in other perspectives on various POD services.

Since Matthijs asked, here are the main bullet points for CreateSpace as I see it:

1. CreateSpace has an online checker and proofing tools, making it easier to spot potential problems before you pay for a proof.
2. With the CS and Lulu proofs side-by-side I found they had comparable printing quality for the interior, but the colors on the cover of the CS version were out and out superior.
3. Printing costs were substantially lower for CS. The proof literally cost about half as much, though the actual royalties work out to be roughly comparable.
4. CreateSpace gives your customers Amazon's shipping options, which means shipping can be cheaper if not free.

It's been all of two days since I launched the book, but so far given a choice people are overwhelmingly choosing Amazon over Lulu as the place to order it (~10 Amazon orders versus zero on Lulu). I'm still in the process of setting up POD printing through DriveThruRPG (which does fulfillment through Lightning Source).

    \n I finished the Metamorphica last May, and this is my experience from back then. Things may have changed. Like US postage for one thing, and I dunno about subcontracted printers. Also, my opinions about what is good and what isn't may have changed, too. A year is a long time. Also, this is just one dude's perspective, so I will try to explain it as best I can.

    Ease of use:
    1. Lulu was by far the easiest service to use. It took me like less than one hour to set up an account, load the files, order a proof, and pay for it.
    2. CreateSpace does have a checker and frankly it pissed me off. It gave me guff about my margins and blah blah blah and they required that it go through an approval process before they would take my order for a proof. I understand why and all that, but I just want to order the goddamn book that I made and I am more than able to see the mistakes in the physical product and fix them on my own. If I want help I'll ask for it.
    3. Back then I found the drivethrurpg process just too dense and complicated to even bother getting a proof. Since then I've found the pdf process at least quite easy.

    Print Cost and Shipping Cost:
    I live in Canada, so shipping options are different for me. Lulu's print cost was higher than CS, but the shipping cost from CS was higher than Lulu. I'm talking about a $2-3 difference in shipping one book to Canada, I think it was. The overall cost difference (print+ship) was about 25 cents in favour of CS, which ultimately didn't sway me. Although CS being cheaper than even the cheapo paper type at lulu definitely says something. If I remember correctly American shipping was about the same between the two, but of it comes down to a decision between my fellow Americans and my fellow Canadians, well, I'm 'a side with my side of the border.

    Product Quality (I'll take a few pics after my camera charges up):
    1. I have a black-and-white cover. The contrast on the CS cover was really high and there was a weird yellowish tint to it. The lulu cover looked more like the original art. Lulu's barcode on the back was smaller and more unobtrusive. The text on the CS cover was sharper and slightly better-looking.
    2. The blacks in the interior of the CS book were better, but the greys in the artwork were higher contrast and the dots looked all weird--perhaps a bit of that whachamacallit effect you get when layering zipatone.
    3. Lulu adds a final page with a small barcode on it so they know what cover to put on the book. But CS made a proof specifically, which means it has "Proof" in big letters on the last page. WTF? How 'bout you send me exactly what people are going to buy and I judge that, maybe, and not something you fucking graffittied up that I can't sell if there's no changes? It's not going to make me feel any more "professional" or some shit because ooh, I'm ordering a "proof" look at me. I'll feel professional when I cash the cheque. Also this is how you spell "cheque" properly.

    Also I make no money from the Metamorphica, so I can't actually say anything about revenue collecting for either site. I CAN say that Lulu just changed their revenue tracking system and the new one is fucking bullshit garbage and it pisses me off. It will give you a nice Excel file but I do not remember how to use Excel and all I want to know are total sales for individual products separated into pdf and print and the auto services won't do that for me and I am very mad about it.

    \n DriveThruRPG do pretty nice books (they print through Lightning Source), their customer service is great, their price point for printing books is very reasonable, and as a sales platform for me at least they have been very good.

    Lulu I've found do slightly nicer books (the interior paper seems better) but my sales volumes there are infinitely lower. Their price point for printing is quite a bit higher than DriveThru but they also take a lower cut of the profit. For me this meant that my profit on a sale was pretty much the same as with DriveThru, but buying copies for myself (or for direct sales to others) was much more expensive.

    Hammertime.



    Pictured above are my original proofs from both CreateSpace and Lulu (you may note that the batsnail logo is not on either of them, because it was added after I got these). You can see the contrast difference even with my camera's shitty resolution. While I normally default to preferring higher-contrast art, in this case the CS cover really put me off. Like a lot.



    Above are the back covers, so you can see the different bar codes. My camera is nowhere near good enough to show you that CS has slightly better definition and that Lulu has some... I dunno what to call it... crap? dots? distortion? around the lettering.

    On that note:\n
    The cover especially had duller colors and less contrast in the Lulu version (which is kind of a big deal when the cover design has pop dots in two shades of blue), and the interior paper is of an off-white color that I would not have chosen myself.
    \nLulu's publisher grade paper (the cheaper option) is white and requires a barcode (and only comes in 5.5x8.5 and 8.5x11). The standard paper is cream (and doesn't require a barcode). You don't get options other than that. The Metamorphica I always thought of as a white book, but really I just went with the cheaper option, while Adventures on Dungeon Planet is a Dungeon World supplement so it should be 6x9 on cream paper just like DW. Drivethrurpg gives you paper options for all sizes of book, I don't remember about CS. Also, worth noting that, unlike Ewen, I had no colour on my cover. Except for the unpleasant yellowishness on the CS cover.



    And pictured above is the final page, which is inserted as extra in your book. For Lulu, they add a whole page. For Drivethrurpg, they will print whatever you want on the second-last page, and just reserve the last page for that barcode.

    There are a few other negatives to keep in mind when dealing with Lulu. Here is the first one:



    First you will notice that the CS copy is thicker (because its heavier paper) than the two Lulu books. The Lulu books however, are a different height. One of them is 1/16th of an inch taller, or about the same height as the CS book.

    There is another height disparity, this time in the interior (of the Lulu book):



    Half-way through the book, the margins change, by a full 1/8th of an inch. The CS book did not have this problem at all, and there was the same distance between the edge and the text throughout.

    Unfortunately my camera is nowhere near good enough to capture the nuances of the interior art for you. I mean, it's pretty good for something I found on the ground at 4am on New Years Day when I finally decided to stumble home shitfaced from my friend's place, but that was nearly a decade ago and it just doesn't cut it in this case. So, just know this: If you like thick, deep blacks and think that Se7en is the greatest piece of cinematography ever made because of it, CreateSpace is good and Lulu's white paper is not, although the blacks in their standard (cream) paper are very nice. If you want your greyscale artwork to be made of differently-sized dots all plotted out on the same grid and not formed into weird-ass patterns that look like crap, then pick Lulu over CreateSpace.

    \n Here is another issue that has come up for me lately: BLEED.

    Neither Lulu nor Drivethrurpg will give you bleed on your B&W interiors (ie you can't get pictures/art/black ink to go all the way to the edge of the page). (EDIT: Okay, Lulu says it WILL do bleed if you set the margins right.) Can't remember what CreateSpace says, prolly the same. Or anyway, that's what they tell you, which doesn't mean you can't give them pdfs with art that goes to the edge of the page. Since Lulu doesn't give me any guff about my margins like CS does (and I'm a grown man) I can do whatever the fuck I want. So I give them pdfs with art all the way to the edges.

    EDIT: I checked the margin requirements for bleed on Lulu and ordered a copy with full bleed. Will post below when it arrives.

    Anyway, you can also order copies without bleed. The results are partly negative:



    As you can see in my proof copy of Adventures on Dungeon Planet above, because the file is 6x9 and the book is 6x9, but the cutting isn't super-precise, the image is offset by 1/16th of an inch, which leaves that much white space. This space varies a little bit throughout the book, but 1/16th of an inch is the most egregious it gets.

    But the results are also partly positive. Here is a picture of another page, which has luck on its side:



    This page has the appearance of full bleed! Unfortunately for me and this particular proof copy, the majority of my illustrations are on the left-hand pages. This not-bleed is sort-of a problem and I guess unfortunate, but it could be worse.

    It could be this, for example:



    This is a page from my backer copy of Barrowmaze II, which says it was printed by Lightning Source in Milton Keynes UK (via Drivethrurpg). While I'm happy with what I got from my contribution to the Barrowmaze II kickstarter, I'm not real happy with the quality of the printing and I wouldn't want it for any of my books. The white border is at least 1/16th of an inch and is on the top and outside of both left-hand and right-hand pages. It is worth noting that my Adventures on Dungeon Planet is 6x9 softcover and Barrowmaze II is 8.5x11 hardcover. Larger Lulu books might be set off more, smaller LS books maybe less, I dunno. However, the printing quality on Barrowmaze II is what I would call "photocopier quality" or perhaps "I'm not mad, just extremely disappointed." The blacks are not even as good as the cheaper grade paper option from Lulu.

    You may have seen the warning that comes with all the Vampire books on Drivethrurpg:\n
    Note: The Black & White print editions of this book are not full-bleed. Because of printing requirements there is a small white border around the edge of the pages.
    \nI have not actually bought any physical books from Drivethrurpg, BUT I have to assume those white borders look just like the ones in Barrowmaze II. Also I hope the paper isn't the same because it gets crinkly near the spine. In any case, if these admittedly small sample sizes are an accurate representation, you can almost fake it with Lulu and it looks really bad with Drivethrurpg/Lightning Source.

    Hey! Just for a bit of contrast, let's take a look at Joe Mcdaldno's game Gun Thief:



    This was printed by Ka-Blam, who specialize in comics. It is comic-book format, has rich deep blacks, nice white paper, and full bleed. I don't know what else they have to offer, although sometime in the near future I will tap the local comics scene for info on this area of indie publishing.

    So anyway, as Rafael explained in the Print and pdf thread, you can send people a thank-you email with a link to the pdf after they buy the print version. There are a couple of problems to this:

    1. You don't get a custom thank-you email for each product, you get one for each account. So every product you sell will send the same thank-you email. I solved this by selling the pdfs through the account that sells the Metamorphica, while selling the print version through a different account. This would become a major pain in the ass if I had a lot of products to do this with, but with one it's okay. It also lets me do what Lulu won't do for me (and I have to learn Excel to do), which is tell me sales numbers for a single product instead of just everything all at once.

    2. It doesn't always work. Not everybody gets their email. So again, I just do what Rafael does -- I let people know they can email me and get the pdf from me directly. There hasn't been very many of these, so it's hardly a hassle, but it's a glitch that does exist.

    Another weird thing about Lulu is that the preview image for the print version is taken from the CMYK cover image, so it looks weird, unlike the pdf preview image which is RGB and which I uploaded separately.

    So that's some notes on how to use Lulu.

    *****

    Did you get it? The answer is: the text on the spine goes all the way to the edge of the spine area! Their instructions are to leave 1/16th of an inch space between the text and the edge of the spine area, to account for a printing variance of 1/16th of an inch. Or that's what they said. But we'll get back to that.

    In the meantime, I emailed Drivethrurpg about this and talked to the guy there and he went over to LS to talk about it. This was a slow process. Two and a half weeks later (or one week after I started selling it) it was approved by LS. So I ordered myself a copy. Another two and a half weeks later (AKA yesterday) it arrived.     Apparently they don't have a problem with the placement of the black bar that covers the entire spine, it's just the placement of TEXT that is a problem.       I don't think there's any reason why you shouldn't have a product listed on multiple sites. Or is there? I'm new to this game so am happy to be corrected.
      \nIf you want to sell pdfs through multiple channels, Drivethrurpg takes a 35% cut of your revenue as payment.
      If you agree to not sell pdfs through any other third party, they take 30% of your revenue as payment.
      This is a per-account rate and goes for all products, both print and pdf.

      Another possible reason would be differing quality and a customer might be pissed off if they bought one version then realized they would be happier with another version. Like how Lulu's only option is creme pages and DTRPG's only option is white pages, and the guy who hates creme pages buys the book from Lulu because he just doesn't know how things are.

      And third because you get different amounts of revenue from each service -- the printing cost of one place is higher than another but the percentage cut is smaller, etc etc, and so selling on one particular platform when you could be selling on another might end up costing you money instead of getting you more and better advertising.

      If the advertising benefits make up for all that, then of course it is a good plan.

      \n It depends on what your other vendors are. If they will generate NEW sales in excess of the lost 5% margin, then yes, you should list them elsewhere. If you are like me and your current other option is just Lulu, then almost anybody who would buy the pdf from Lulu will buy it from DTRPG. If you're selling through IPR and the majority of your business is offset print hardcopies (bundled with pdf), a non-exclusive pdf-only deal with DTRPG is probably all icing, basically.

      Keep in mind that it's only the pdfs that are exclusive. You can still sell print versions through other channels, and you can still sell your pdf directly from yourself (or give it away). But that 5% difference also applies to the print version you sell through DTRPG. And I think the print version is the one that people have more of an issue with.
