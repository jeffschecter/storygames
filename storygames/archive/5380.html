    \n Me:

    Weekly: My weekly friends group is currently recovering from a recent birth by one of the members. We are playing a RISUS-based supers game based on the idea behind "Necessary Evil", where they are the bad guys who have to save the world from Something Worse. However, this group has recently become a half-RPG/half-talk group. No longer able to focus at great length on gaming, we often do boardgames, talk about politics/coding/science/culture, watch videos, etc. To that end, gaming has retreated a bit to more of a bi-weekly thing, and in turn the heavy gamers in the group have begun hanging out other nights/weekends to get their game on more.

    Bi-Weekly: We just came back together for the new year. Last session we played Panty Explosion/Star Wars. Next week we meet to discuss which game to play next and make characters (we roll in mini-campaigns running 2-4 months, sometimes longer). On the table is nWOD Vampire or nWOD Vampire: ROME, some playtests, and maybe a heavy game or two. I will probably do my World War Z game with this group.

    Shadowrun: We've got two sessions of Sombra Console down, and a third is happening in two weeks. This game is psychoawesome, and is making me love Shadowrun back and forth. I'm a great faceman/decent hacker, and Eric P is running it.

    D&D 3E: The monthly D&D game (using E6) continues in two weeks. We fought and subdued an evil mage with plans to bring hell to earth; next episode is going to be us bringing him back to town for justice, lots of side quests, then investigating an evil ice worm cult. My Ice Elf is level 1 Spirit Shaman, Level 2 Thief.

    Other: And I'm still meeting every other weekend or so to throw down with one-shots and mini-campaigns. Currently planning on soon throwing down some Red Box Hack, Tenra, and Lacuna over the next month or so.

    -Andy

    \n Snow, holidays, and players taking trips to various places and having silly things like "lives" have interfered with my usual weekly game lately. (Edit: I'm hugely jealous of all you people going 'Well, in my first weekly game I'm doing this, in my second weekly game I'm doing that, and in my fifteen monthly games we're rotating between these, these, and those.")

    We've only managed, in the past four weeks, a single one-shot session of Bunyip, a fun little game about trust, cooperation, and shapeshifting homicidal monsters impersonating your friends, whose situation a friend came up with but whose system I recently revamped by shamelessly stealing from quite a few worthy places. I look forward to giving it another try with a few modifications based on our discoveries of how it worked in play, which was still quite well, at least in my opinion.

    However, when (hopefully soon) we start up something longer-term again I got the rest of my group on board to give In Nomine a whirl. And I've got plans to try out some fiddly relationship-map-generating things before play, too, which I look forward to seeing the results of. (The PCs as a group of angels: "Name an angel you dislike, and why. Name a demon you like, and why. Name an angel you used to know when they were a demon, or vice versa, and how you knew them before they Redeemed or Fell.")

    \n Continuing three campaigns from last year seems probable although we haven't started back up since the holiday season.

    D&D 3,5: Age of Worms. We're in the last legs of the campaign, almost 21 and almost facing Dragotha. At it's best it has been quite a good campaign, but lately it has bogged down and isn't as fun as it used to be. It's not that many sessions yet and we might as well finish as it's more satisfying to have foiled Kyuss evil plans than just quitting. The next will either be the last 3.5 campaign (unless 4th ed is truly shite) or a Warhammer campaign. Currently playing a Fighter/Warblade TWF that's great fun.

    Call of Cthulhu: Tatters of the King. I used to be burned out on CoC, but this campaign has brought back what was fun with it. We're all very experienced CoC playes as well as the GM so we manage to hold the mood as well as going in both guns blazing. The GM added a lot, so although we have played in a year and a half we're just at chapter three. Tatters is centered on the Hastur Mythos and is up there with the great campaigns like Masks of Nyarlathotep and At the Mountains of Madness. My Hungarian Anthropologist is still quite sane although he has a looser trigger finger on his hunting rifle.

    The Shadow of Yesterday: Rinn, en kampanjesetting for dj\xe6rvravar (Rinn a campaign setting for hardasses). Rinn was originally a campaign world we made over at rollespill.net for a bit of Redbox nostalgia. We mixed up a lot of D&D'isms in a Norse setting framework which got more and more Norse elements as went on. One of us were very vocal about not wanting to play D&D so we landed on TSOY and has played a couple of sessions with it. Although I've had TSOY for a couple of years now, it was the first time I played it and although I was concerned it was not "crunchy" enough, I was pleasantly surprised and have come to love "Bringing Down the Pain".

    I've tested Spirit of the Century twice and I'm probably going to GM a mini-campaign in it.

    \n Earlier tonight I ran my first playtest of Raspberry Heaven (blog post here), and I think I'm on the right track with it now, so hopefully I'll do some more playtesting in the near future.

    But next week we'll most likely be getting back into our long-term OVA campaign called "Divine Machine," which is an anime-style dimension-hopping adventure thingy. The PCs have returned from their visit to Elysium (the dimensional hypercomputer that the gods use to maintain the multiverse) only to find that the Holy Velkan Empire has declared war on the Northstar Alliance.     \n I'm currently playing in a weekly game of Reign (as a hobo perfumer who hopes to some day have the power to topple empires), and that's fun so far. The group has food and drink at all of the sessions, and so I feel a good sense of camaraderie. Rock.

    I'm also running Vampire: the Masquerade (Revised) for a new group, and that's interesting. They're a mix of gaming noobies and gaming traditionalists, and I'm all hippy bathed in mud. But I think it may work. I'm disregarding combat manouvers and a lot of the finicky "If exposed to sunlight, you will burn at a rate of X times Y" stuff, and suddenly it is a far simpler and more beautiful game. I like the core of it, just not the finicky rules or the many splatbooks.
