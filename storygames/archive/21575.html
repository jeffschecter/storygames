    \n Good idea !
    That made me think of some 200 word RPGs that I label "non violent" :
    No sleep tonight (dada exquisite corpses with a theme and cool procedures)
    Ring: Telling the tales of trees (play outdoor, pick a tree, follow the steps to narrate its life)

    Very important : the following 2 make you experience bereavement. I suggest adapting them not by "hiding" or "disguising" the loss, but by adding a comforting final and making the kid experience both sides, with a debrief, and a new round knowing, in control.
    Holding on (2 climbers hold each other's hand, remove a finger on certain events, when you let go, one falls)
    Test 7 (one robot, one technician looking for anomalies. Too much anomalies gets the robot recycled).

    \n 4 years old is quite a constraint to meet. Most kid-friendly games I can think of are still aimed a bit older than that.

    Dread House (by Epidiah Ravachol and Emily Care Boss) claims to fit for 8+yo.

    Nick Wedig has several games specifically designed for kids: "Castle Attack" (7yo), "Star Level Ten" (9yo), "The Mind Of God", "Tricks and Treats", "Sophie\u2019s Pirate Game" (4yo, yay!), "Rosalind Wells, Ambassador to Space" (5yo). And they're free so you can check them out.
