\n Anybody know of any narrative party games? Games that can accommodate a large number of players (10 or more), are light and easy to pick up, very few rules, intuitive for new players, and are also designed for narrative play?

Anything come to mind?

If not... what would a game like that be like? What would you want to see in a game like that? Or, if you were to design one, what would be your approach?

Are there party games out there that could be hacked in a narrative direction?

    \n For starters, I can suggest two of my own devising...

    Watch the World Die. Our first playtest included 11 people and much alcohol.
    If your friends have a dark sense of humor, it's fun to destroy the world.

    Pocket Things. A silly storygame that can be played by anyone with pockets.
    Good for any number of players greater than 2.

    Both games can be found here: http://www.rpgnow.com/browse.php?manufacturers_id=6911

    And two more, not of my devising...

    The Quest-Giving Game by Neal Stidham. Adventurers boasting about their exploits and challenging each other.
    http://parenthesispress.files.wordpress.com/2014/04/questgiving.pdf
    http://parenthesispress.files.wordpress.com/2014/04/questgivingmat.pdf

    Doctor Magnethands by Grant Howitt. Why? Because SHUT UP AND DRINK, that's why!
    https://www.dropbox.com/s/swrsabebae8ydem/Doctor Magnethands.pdf

    \n Depending on the crowd, you could play The Daughters of Verona in such a group. At least early on, if your party is one of the drinking kind of parties people would probably be a bit too unfocused to be able to play after a couple of beers. Also, the game takes a couple of hours, so a certain level of commitment is useful.

    That said, the rules are simple, the premise of Shakespearean comedy/romantic comedy appeals to a surprisingly wide array of people, and players can participate at various levels. From being active and swapping characters and playing a part in every scene, to chilling at the back - playing the audience cheering, booing and aww'ing.

    -

    If you bought some bundle at Epimas two years ago you might have The Cat of Excellence already. It can swallow pretty much any size of group, and is quick enough that you can play it in a one-beer timeframe.
