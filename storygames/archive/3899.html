\n I've been wanting to do some Play-by-Post roleplaying for a while now, but I've been a bit turned off by the fact that the only big PBP forum is RPG.net, and there's not much support for less conventional games, and I'm just not a fan of the atmosphere there.

So I made my own forum.

Snail's Pace is a forum for indie or alternative play-by-post roleplaying. It's in its infancy at the moment, and I don't really expect it to be a big thing, but you're welcome to sign up and pitch a game. There's a category for PBP discussion, too, if you've got a game design that's suited to PBP, or you want to discuss the genre. I don't really know where the forum is headed yet, but hopefully it will be useful to some people. There are a few functions in Vanilla that are really fantastic for PBP, like whispers, and an in-post dice roller, so it's got some advantages over RPG.net.

Anyhow. It's a small thing, but maybe it's useful to some folk here.

    \n Huh. I had a thought.

    Given the Vanilla interface, everyone's play threads are all publically "out" there and all that. It's easy to lookat other games out there that you're not playing. And, one of the problems with PbP play is just general attrition due to its pace / slowness / pressure of IRL, but also because the anonymity of the internet makes it more comfortable to (speaking from my own experience) just flake out on games.

    But: you could make a forum with a strong theme of kibbutzing on others' play threads, and on providing the mutual community pressure to see games through. That could be interesting.
