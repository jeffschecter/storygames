    \n I was in the middle of running something along these lines, using Ben Lehman's High Quality RPG to do a sandbox style campaign. We're kinda on hiatus right now becuase of my rather chaotic schedule, but it was going pretty well. HQRPG does have some definite advancement mechanics, but not in the way most of the fantasy games do - while you do get to increase your number of talents, adding new occupations doesn't really let you keep the specialties or implied abilities of older occupations. Instead, it's assumed that after a while, you get rusty with your old occupation, and eventually it doesn't provide any advantage.

    But so far, the players had been pretty happy with that. They were seeing it as a way to change their character's abilities based on what they thought would be more important (though none of these things involve fighting monsters or ruling kingdoms).

    At some point, I'll have an AP posted, but it requires that I first get all my notes in some semblance of order.

    \n This is probably slightly off from what you are looking for but:
    Lone Wolf book series - simple mechanics, solo (well there is a multiplayer version now), meant for campaign, some advancement

    Fabled Lands solo books and now a multiplayer game. The solo books don't have an advancement system but your stats change

    Fighting Fantasy is another one I can think off.
