    Posted By: Paul T.Followup question: how would you do it? What would you want to see?
    \nLARP Diplomacy.
    3-9 players for each country dressed in politico suits and the uniforms of the admiralty and general staff of their nation. There would be a central war-room with a huge map of Europe (large enough to merit sticks for maneuvering pieces, naturally) with an 'embassy' room for each side adjoining and a few neutral conference rooms. After the tense meetings of each turn, national teams would retire, write their orders on a chalk-board, cover it, then wheel them into the War-room for the big reveal.
    The inclusion of a liquor cabinet and attendant humidors would add to the atmosphere nicely.

    \nBoard Games I Have LARPed(-ish):
    Descent - Pretty much any line module in a fantasy LARP is Descent.
    Zombies!!! - Pretty much any post-apoc survival Airsoft game is Zombies. We did it with paintball markers, though. Messy!
    Arkham Horror - One-shot at Dragon*Con using Cthulhu Live, set in an asylum. Close enough.

    Board Games I Would LARP:
    Chess - To make a move on the "non-in-game" game board, something must be done using the LARP system or through acting. Really it might be more like that old video game Archon, but with more than physical combat, allowing also "social combat."
    The Game of Life - It would be interesting to see if it could be a compelling framework for a "domesticity" LARP. But I'm not sure many folks want to LAP real life.
    Settlers of Catan - A resource-management-heavy game, perhaps with "harvesting rules," but mainly hitting the trade/negotiations elements.
    Shadows over Camelot - Possibly a GREAT framework for more "open goal" fantasy LARP play than line modules and the like.
