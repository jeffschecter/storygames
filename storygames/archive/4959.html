\n I'm pretty sickened by the Internet right now so here's a big warm bucket of soapy water and a stiff brush:

We're making up characters for Spirit of the Century. My guy's the wheel man with a crazy-ass "Mole Car" that drives underground. My brother, who I've been gaming with since gaming existed, is the flying ace with the flying boat called "Spirit of Cambrai". And he's taken the prototype plane stunt, and he's got one gadget left to install. He's thinking about it. And his eyes light up.

"I know! I've got a special bay for the Mole Car! I can drop it from the sky!"

And I add "air deployable", and we're both so happy.

What's a thoughtful, fun, cool thing one of your friends did recently?

    As the Dream [his ship, which happens to be a commercial cruise liner] approaches Rala for it's carefully timed leisure stop, the Captain Belinar is entertaining the captain's table with a story of his youth as an acid-crab fisherman on Caliban. His steward taps him on the shoulder, and explains that the "big black chest in your room, sir...it's making an odd noise".

    Belinar excuses himself, and summons Rev Ray to come with him to his cabin.

    Rev Ray carefully opens the door, and inside, they see that the black trunk, silent and cool for generations, is making an odd sound. As they carefully approach, the chest starts making more agitated sounds, and the ship lurches to the side.

    The chief navigator pages Nils, and tells him "The ship has just set itself for a landing on Rala!, and the helm has stopped responding!"

    \n Last week, with my weekly group we began designing characters for our next campaign, a total beast of a game that is combining a number of supers RPG into one giant megafuck RPG. I cobbled the rules and setting from a combination of the following:

    *Necessary Evil for the idea that "all the players are sueprvillains defending the planet from an evil(er) menace" as the setting backdrop
    *RISUS for its mechanics (specifically, 80-point supers, using the traditional RISUS scale).
    *Supercrew, for its Hero Point Mechanic, and its "use this 1-in-3 ability to get a Hero Point" mechanic (in fact, a "One Point/Dangerous Power" mechanic, where you roll 1 1d6, has been stolen and bolted onto my RISUS).
