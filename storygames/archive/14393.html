\n I notice in myself that there's a desire for the perfect system. It would be complex, it would be engaging in its own right, I would love to sit and fiddle with it.

It wouldn't even have to be played!

I think that a lot of roleplayers have this want for system interaction. A sort of aesthetical pleasure, appreciation of complexity, joyful tinkering.

Tell me about yours. When was the last time you enjoyed just playing with a system, because the system was so much fun?

    \n Well ... I've been tinkering for years now ... all the way since 1996, on the same system; Fabula, and I can't stop appreciating the small developments in it, the elegant and simple mathematical solutions that present themselves as I play and fiddle, and find out what is good and what is not.

    I'm not so much the designer of it anymore ... more like I'm the caretaker; a watchful eye that looks after the system, polish it, build the high domes and small annexes it demands, and so forth ...

    I love to do that. I love to play with it, and tinker with it, and to see how it, by high-tempo delivery of crunch, act as the perfect tool for establishing complex interaction between players.

    \n For me it has definitely been Mutants and Masterminds 2e. My version of it no longer even closely resembles that game anymore. I re-wrote stats and what they do, you now use pools of d6's that you wager or roll. It has aspects that are ranked like skills that you bring into your power checks. Turn order, initiative, and power activation has been replaced with an action point pool. Yeah, if I ever get around to cleaning it up a bit, it would make for a fairly awesome game on its own merit.

    Oh, and for all challenges, other than BBEG stuff, players roll for narrative control, and based upon how badly the player fails also determines the amount of stress they take.
