    \n Sure, I have "What is Dungeons & Dragons?" Butterfield, Honigmann, Parker. I tried to learn D&D from it, but it only had descriptions of the rules, not the actual rules (as in, tables, class descriptions etc). There's an interesting section speculating on the future. I still leaf through it occasionally, I like its atmosphere... and there's the nostalgia, of course.

    The trio went on to write some choose-your-own-adventure-type books called "Cretan Chronicles", which were good, IIRC; they required you to keep track of which gods liked and disliked you.

    I believe Butterfield also designed SPI's "Universe" and the solo board game "Ambush!" (also very good, but dated, I guess). He now runs an interactive information (?) company: http://www.johnbutterfield.com/index.html. There might be more than one John Butterfield, though.

    (Ian Livingstone's book is called "Dicing with Dragons", and has a solo adventure in it. It's also quite nice, but I much prefer "What is D&D").
