    I view the Burning Wheel skill list as a fractal. Sure, the Perception stat and the Observation skill have some overlap, but Observation has specific applications where raw Perception. Detecting traps, to use one example from the book. A character who has one but not the other has a distinct flavor in play.

    What's more important is that everyone should be on the same page about intent and the possible outcomes before the roll is made. Past that--as others have said--just run with it and you'll refine your instincts as the game progresses.

    Why is perception rolled more frequently than other stats?
    \nI don't know that it's rolled that much more frequently, it's just that the rules explicitly mention that it will be rolled.

    Page 17:\n
    Perception is the awareness and mental acuity stat. It's rolled all of time in game. Players always have their characters searching for information or clues, and this is the stat tested in those situations.
    \n(This should be tempered with just Saying Yes in most cases when the characters are just looking around and there's nothing at stake, of course.)

    The Observation skill is specifically delineated as a versus check when someone else has deliberately hidden something.
    Page 286:\n
    Observation is the skill used to spot the hidden[...] Test this skill in versus tests[...] Note: Do not use Observation for standard Perception tests--to spot a weakness in armor, to notice details of a flag, or to look for an escape tunnel.
    \nIt's not a big issue if you miss this sometimes, but in my experience the fastest way to level up as group is when each player gets interested in the details of their character's abilities and limits. So the dwarf player knows Greed in and out, the scout has an idea of when she's trying to use Observation and when Perception, etc.

    The bit about intents and tasks on p.24-25 may help, too.

    \n Stat tests are extremely rare, aside from Perception and Power.

    Beginner's Luck works as follows:

    -Test using the root stat if the character does not have the appropriate skill
    -If the Ob, before doubling, would make this a difficult or challenging test, this test gets marked for the stat
    -If the Ob, before doubling, would count as routine, mark a test towards learning that skill

    \n You always roll the number of dice listed unless you are taking penalties from injury (I think, can't imagine other scenarios off the top of my head).

    If you're rolling a Beginner's Luck test, it doubles the obstacle (ie, Ob 2 becomes Ob 4).

    More complete Example:

    Your character with a B4 Perception is attempting an unskilled Alchemy test. The GM has determined this particular concoction is an Ob 2 normally, making this an Ob 4 test. You would roll four dice since it is rooted in perception.
