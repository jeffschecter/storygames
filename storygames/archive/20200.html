    \n I still love Canon Puncture. I wish it came out more often. The great thing about Sons of Kryos, when it existed, is that it was about playing games more than it was about making games, and that's true about Canon Puncture, too. There is absolutely nothing wrong with shows about making games, and, obviously, you can't discuss making games without discussing playing them to some extent, but there is a difference. I imagine, such a large percentage of the indie-games community consists also of game makers, that it is natural for the indie-games podcasts to focus on making games so much. I think, though, that's why, years after it disappeared, people still talk about Sons of Kryos, is that the doug really deep into topics about playing games, but from an indie/story games perspective. And they were (well they still are) really smart and good at it.

    \n I'm going to toot our horn here a bit and recommend You Don't Meet In An Inn. We're an Actual Play podcast focusing on lesser known RPGs, the general rule being that if the setting or system has a video game adaptation it is too well known. We switch up the game we are playing every couple of weeks with a new story and cast of characters. Currently we are releasing episodes of Lady Blackbird.

    You can find the podcast on itunes here, on not-itunes here, and our RSS.
