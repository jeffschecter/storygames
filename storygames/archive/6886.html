\n In another thread, I mentioned this and was asked to say a little more.

I ran this for my wife and a couple of friends several months back and it went pretty well (although I got sloppy with the rules towards the end). The basic idea is to swap out Discipline, Madness and Exhaustion dice for Skill, Essence and Limit dice. Pain dice stick around, and some of the effects of dominance get twisted about. I remade the rules summary on page 34 of the book. Here's an excerpt:

Every time you roll, you roll your Skill dice and Limit dice (if you have any).
The GM selects a number of Pain dice to roll based on the difficulty of the roll.
Once per roll, you can channel your Virtue, increasing your Limit by one.
Any time you roll, you can add one to six Essence dice to your roll.
...
If Skill dominates, everything stays under control. You have the option of removing and Essence check mark or decreasing your Limit by one.
If Limit dominates, your Virtue dominates your personality causing you to behave erratically. Increase your Limit by one.
If Essence dominates, your resources are taxed. Check off one Essence box, and your Anima flares wildly.
If Pain dominates, you pay a greater price. Pay one token to the GM's Shadow bowl.
If your Limit is increased above 6, you Limit Break.
If you must check off an Essence box but can't, you Crash.
If you Limit Break, the Great Curse has a major effect on you. Your Limit clears out completely, you lose one Skill die, and gain one Curse die (which acts like a Limit die, but is permanent).
If you Crash, you have exhausted your magical reserves. You lose access to Essence dice and Limit dice (but not Curse dice) until you rest for a day or two, meditating to regain what was lost.

Characters also have an Excellency (something you do well) and an Essence talent (something you do that is impossible), which function like minor and major exhaustion talents.

Other than that, it's mostly the same.
Here's a short actual play that I emailed a friend of mine to get him interested:

Susie's (that's my wife) outgoing acrobat made an error on the trapeze and was plummeting to his doom when he Exalted as a Night-caste Solar... at the World's Greatest Circus, right in the middle of the Blessed Isle. In attendance was a circle of Terrestrial exalts (of course), so her solar flare not only bleached the room, but also make her an easy target for them. Or, so they thought. Their Air aspect pulled out his bow and shot a charm-enhanced arrow at Pip (the acrobat), which he caught and used the momentum to spin and toss the arrow back. His new skills and magic proved to work well; the attack decapitated it's original shooter.* Pip jumped onto his trusty elephant side-kick Mohinder (now stained white from her anima) and rode off.

Not far behind him, the Earth aspect had summoned a huge, six-legged war horse with his sorcery and gave chase. He eventually cut Pip off and swung hard with his tetsubo. Pip, as quick and dextrous as a monkey, scrambled around and after a few exchanges of blows and near-misses, he slid feet-first under the wide stance of the Earth aspect and crushed his groin with a vicious punch. The Dragon-blood went down like a sack of rocks, and Pip quickly mounted Mohinder, riding off to safety.

And that was in about 20 minutes of play, with short breaks for explaining the rules as we went.


* Crying children were sprayed with gouts of blood. It was... awesome.
