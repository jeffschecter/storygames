    \n These are just my opinions but...

    I've played several games of The Roach with 3 people and it worked very well.

    Polaris runs best with exactly 4.
    Dogs runs much, much better with 3 players than 4. With 4 players, even Sorcery doesn't provide much of a challenge.
    With Great Power is wonderful with small groups. I'd say 2 or 3 players and a GM is optimal.

    \n All these figures include all players, whether or not one of them takes on a GM role.

    Dogs: 3-5 players. More than 5 and it bogs down.

    Spirit of the Century: 3-7, although I'd like to try it with more.

    In A Wicked Age: 3-5, although I'd like to try it with more.

    Capes: 3-4, although I haven't tried it with 2, it might work.

    Beast Hunters: 2-9, although it bills itself as a 1 on 1 game, I played an 8 on 1 game that went fantastically. If I recall, 3 survived the battle with that beast.

    Primetime Adventures: 3-7, probably more with some tweaks.

    With Great Power: 3-6

    Wild Talents: 3-7

    Nobilis: 2-6

    World of Darkness: 2-6

    Also, to be clear, when I say "players" I include GM. So "Dogs works with 3 to 4" means "One GM and two to three players."

    TSOY: 2 to 4
    Wicked: 3 or 4, exactly.
    Nobilis: 2 to 4
    Dungeons and Dragons: 5
    Exalted: 4 or 5
    Unknown Armies: 2 to 4
    Spirit of the Century: 3 or 4
    Beast Hunters: 2. NO ONLY 2.

    And just to be a pain in the ass, I like Polaris best with 3.
