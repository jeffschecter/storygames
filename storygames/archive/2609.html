\n After checking out the Killing Your Father: Star Wars thread, I kept getting ideas for running Star Wars games using other systems.

Polaris is an easy match for Jedi who are destined to give into the Dark Side or die.

Dogs in the Vineyard, as I mentioned above. I'm working on some "Emotional Burnout" rules based on a really cool discussion our group had after our game, that's essentially an optional rule where you detail what you believe your overall burnout is based on your experiences in the town are (as our group has a tendency to leave a trail of bodies or burned buildings in our Dogs games, and we had to just think about how hellish this is to do again and again). Such as idea would lend itself well to Dark Side traits.

My Life with Master...*ahem*...the Dark Lord of the Sith. Shoot, even My Life with an Uncompromising Jedi Master. Either way, I could see both apprentices and droids involved. Or even the folks in the Millennium Falcon while Obi-Wan was on board.

Any other thoughts out there?

    Posted By: EldirEmotional burnout sounds interesting. Administering to what sometimes feels like a galaxy of shitheads has its price. In my campaign one PC's Jedi Master is a "broken" Jedi who became emotionally damaged after being an unwitting party to a massacre. Fallout seems excellent at simulating that kind of emotional scarring, though. What additions do you have in mind?
    \nAs I currently envision it, it would be something done between towns in a journal-like fashion. You would write down things you did or saw that horrified you, and assign some sort of dice rating based one something I haven't thought of yet. For example:

    "We decimated nearly every woman in this town the ones leading the sewing circle were sorceresses. 3d8
    I watched my friend kill a child possessed by a demon. 2d6"

    The dice are rolled like in Fallout, only traits gained in the "short term" are for the next town only, and "long term" means permanent.

    The idea behind this was that there were scenes where the GM in our last Dogs game said Yes, rather than roll the dice, when we said we were going to start killing everyone in the sewing circle and anyone who stood in our way. This was at the very end of the game, after we rooted out evil, killed demons & sorceresses, etc. After the game was over, each of us players looked at one another and basically went "What the hell did we just do?" We sat around for another hour and talked about the idea of Dogs burning out and leaving the life for any number of reasons, and I thought that some sort of journal-like mechanic for tracking "why" and "what happens" would be interesting.

    Say you go into another town and watch another child be killed because he's possessed. Is that still just 2d6? Maybe you don't feel it as much, so it's 1d6 (if you list it at all)? Or maybe it's worse now, and this time you jot down 3d6? The idea that it's player-driven means that they can list as much as they want and put the meaning down for them. The fact that it's optional -- you don't have to note down anything like that even if others are -- means (hopefully) it'll be used for great awesome, not just because it has to be used.
