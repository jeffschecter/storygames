    \n It depends on the kind of game you want. "Elric" is not enough.

    Some examples:

    Annalise: can be played with a player and a GM (even if it's not the default setup), stormbringer is the "vampire", Elric is the victim and is trying to find freedom

    Sorcerer: there is a supplement of Sorcerer & Sword called "Charnel Gods" where the PC have "fell weapons" like stormbringer as object demon, and it's their destiny to destroy the world when they will reach humanity=0. All you can do is delay the inevitable, you are cursed.

    Ars Magica if you simply want to play a bad-ass sorcerer with a magic sword (drop the standard setting) and you don't care about nothing else.

    In a Wicked Age if you want to spotlight the color and play short stories not in chronological order.

    "ruleless freeform" if you want to play the same exact stories

    \n Hoog,

    The old Stormbringer game is a terrific catalog of Elric's world, but the resolution mechanics are good ol' BRP and the one-GM, lotsa-players structure won't help. I recommend it as a setting sourcebook, though! And the art is great.

    4-player Polaris without rotating roles could work. You play Elric; the person to your left plays NPCs and happenings connected to Elric's family and loves; the person to your right plays Elric's official responsibilities; and the person across from you plays the nasty shit that wants to straight-up kill Elric and his world. Although "official responsibilities" might need to be replaced with something. It might work to break up different kinds of nasty among two players -- Yyrkoon, Arioch, Lern, Stormbringer itself... I could see any number of functional ways to divide those.

    3-player Sorcerer, with Elric, Stormbringer, and the GM, could work. The GM and the Stormbringer player could either work in tandem or not.

    What I'd really want to do is set up some game where multiple GMs throw options at the protagonist player, who goes with whichever one they find coolest, and there's some sort of economy around that. It's a design idea that's been floating around in my brain for years. Give me a kick and encouragement and maybe I'll do something with it...
