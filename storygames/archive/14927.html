\n There didn't seem to be any Fiasco playsets for a Gilligan's Island style game (at least, that I could find), and so I tried my hand at creating my first playset: No Luck Atoll. Thoughts and feedback very welcome.

    \n Good point, and I'll probably mention the fact that, in general, a more serious tone will mostly be achieved in Objects and Locations by opting for higher values.
    \n
    \nI do think a lot of fun could potentially be had with a mixture of tone, considering one of our use of the "serious" London East End playsets eventually resulted in Maureen Lipman on a quest for the Arc of the Covenant getting assistance from Dan Brown (who was killed twice) and opposed by a mystical unicorn... Still, best to check everyone's on board with that style of game before starting.
    \n
    \nEdit to add: I'm also thinking that in some cases, the softer fallout table from the companion may be more appropriate, although I have yet to pick up a copy to know for certain how well it would fit.
