\nEver since the very first time I played DnD I made a little illustration of the character I was playing (at the time a sourcerer)

And I have always made illustrations of my characters every time I get one I get to play at least twice.

Then, a year or so ago, a friend of my brother's made little clay models of the characters at their party, they were freaking awesom and detailed as hell (this omdels were used on the grid to play DnD too.)

I also know, for example, a girl who, when creating a neo character, searches de internet extensively until she manages to fing a picture of a character she thinks is close enough to what she sees in her head.

So, I was just wondering, how about other gamers?

I know I do this because I love and am passionate about character design, I actually do it for a living. And I make illustrations of them because I like drawing and I'm, I'd like to think, at least a little good at it.

Do you guys do anything like this? Is there any sort of system revolving around or having mechanics regarding the apparience of a character?

    \n Huh... interesting question. I used to draw my characters (with mixed success)... but now I don't at all, ever. And I've even taken figure drawing classes in the interim, but that hasn't made me draw PCs.

    Then again... most of my play the past decade has been convention play or one-shots (or "campaigns" that lasted one or two sessions), and so maybe I just don't want to take the time to do so for a PC that's just going to end up in my "past characters" notebook forever. OH, and I also got a hold of Hero Machine years back when it was free, and it can "draw" a quick study of a PC well enough for posting online to a game group or blog. So it made me lazy, too.

    But if I ever played a character for more than, oh, five sessions, I'd almost certainly try to come up with SOME kind of visual of it. If only to help others to see what I see when I am portraying it.

    Posted By: Omar_RamirezIs there any sort of system revolving around or having mechanics regarding the apparience of a character?
    For Mature Audiences requires drawing or painting on a plushie that represents a fictionalized version of yourself. You draw the Scars with which you want to deal in the fiction. Additional Scars can be drawn on your plushie in certain circumstance. Knitting needles are used to Spike Scars, which intriduces a character into a scene. Crochet hooks are used to damage plushies when you exit a scene without dealing sufficiently with the Spiked Scar. If an issue is resolved, its Scar can be patched or painted over. This cycle of Scarring and patching leaves a record of character progression.
    It's not "character illustration," per se, but it's drawing and the mechanics are exclusively engaged with the plushie and the drawings.
