\n I've read 3 of the Dresden books so far so the relationships among the faerie courts were a little vague for me. Most of the information was based on growing up there and thinking/googling for key local events and locations with an eye for the ghost/supernatural twist.



"The Magic City" (a safe haven for the supernatural in a cold dark world)

Birmingham Alabama http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Birmingham,_Alabama

City Aspects (NOTE: These aspects are specifically made for a one-shot convention game listed below. They are probably more specific than I would use for a long term game):

New Mt Olympus http://www.roadsideamerica.com/attract/ALBIRvulcan.html: City grew up at an incredible pace after Vulcan and the remaining Roman/Greek gods moved their home to Birmingham. Steps from the top of Vulcan's statue lead to the halls of the gods. As such, the city is under a special ban of the Accords.

Red Mountain (AKA Iron Mountain, AKA The Mountain of Blood) http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Mountain,_Birmingham,_Alabama: The mountain spitting Birmingham in half is a haunt of black magic and demons. The storms that break on its face bespeak the wrath contained within. On the new moons, moans and dripping blood comes from the great cut face of the mountain. No one knows what is contained within. What 'bleeds' out on dark nights seems to be a warning. This comes through a V shaped cut in the mountain made for an expressway that was completed in 1970. Several sorcerers have been put down by the council for attepting to tap this power. The White Council has tapped a local wizard to keep an eye on this but he just died. His apprentice will be a part of the game either as a PC or NPC. Of note, the Vulcan statue overlooks this area and rarely the gods send Invidia http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Invidia to punish those who disturb the mountain.

White Court feeding ground http://www.800alabama.com/things-to-do/activities/alabama-history/civil-rights.cfm: Birmingham had an influx of White Court vampires during the height of the civil rights struggle. Many have moved on but a strong contingent remains stirring up hatred and violence to slake their thirst.

Summer and Winter battleground: http://geology.com/news/2007/severe-drought-in-alabama-and-georgia.shtml Winter is attacking this Summer stronghold. The Accords are no protection beyond the city limits as both sides have claimed to be agrieved. Any Faeries may be challenge or attack if judged to have been a part of the breach.

Science! will not save you http://main.uab.edu/ The scientists at UAB are delving into the dark frontiers of science. Pushing the envelope to secure that next grant. They have tapped into forces they do not understand and can not control.



Other important locations include "Druid City" to the south in Tusacloosa, home to an very old and powerful non-White Council wizard organization.



More information about Birmingham:

Who cares about this city?
There was a wizard, Philip McGee, who watched over the city and tried to keep Red Mountain from being used for mischief but he died yesterday. The Summer Court claims this region under it protection since time
immemorial. A special group of non-White Council wizards (Druids) live a few mile to the south and are interested in any supernatural activities. The White Court (House Malvora) are throughout the upper escheons of Birmingham Society and take their continued freedom to feed very seriously.

Who keeps the peace?
Well, thats a funny question. Within the city no one dares openly attack others due to the special ban of the Accords. You see, this is the new home of the Roman Gods and they don't take kindly to anyone screwing around. Any open attack within the city limits between two different groups will be met with swift reprisal. This usually means Invidia destroys you but individual gods (cruel and capricious as ever) may develop an interest in what is going on. This as a practical matter means that the letter and spirit of the Accords is give great weight in the city. This has made Birmingham an unlikely but viable spot for negotiations and meetings between supernatural entities.

How do mortals cope?
Mortals have their police and others to help them. Although not protected by the ban the supernatural is very careful to stay 'behind the scenes' making most attacks and disturbances easier to explain away.

What kind of supernatural community is there is town?
The White Court vampires sow dissent and anger throughout town. The race issues have never fully healed and there are serious corruption and drug sub-cultures. There was only one White Court wizard and his appentice who kept to themselves at a small farm north of town. There are a few people who believe they have some real power (witches, vamp/goth groups etc) but these are just lunch if they run into a real entity.

What interests the supernatural tourist?
Seeing the gods, while not healthy, is an opportunity some can not pass up. There is a stable group of ghosts that live in and around the Tutwiler Hotel ranging from Indian medicine men, to civil war generals, to racial protesters. The "Devil Fountain" http://www.bhamonline.com/Storyteller/TheStoryteller.htm is a connection to a faerie spirit that will bargain with supernatural and 'aware' mortals (often under the influence of drugs).

I was tutored and toured the world while growing up. My parents were into the occult and died in a summoning went wrong when I was 20 years old.
