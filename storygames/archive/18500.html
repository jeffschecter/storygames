    \n It's called a borganza tree, and, although rumours about it abound, largely spread by travellers who say they've been to the Horus Dimension, up until now it's generally been assumed to not actually exist. Of course, the Horus Dimension itself is also generally believed to not exist, but never mind that for now. Anyway, the fruit of the borganza tree, which are supposed to look like bananas, except that they're smaller and more green than yellow, are indeed edible, and do taste like bananas, but have a bizarre effect, whereby for a couple of minutes after eating the eater feels extremely lightheaded and seems to leave their body, following which they feel as though they're travelling through all the dimensions at once. This feeling has been worked out as lasting roughly six minutes, and afterwards the person is left feeling absolutely drained, as if they've somehow had their soul ripped from their body and clumsily re-inserted.

    Yeah, crazy huh? I think those folks who claim to have been to the Horus Dimension in the first place, let alone eaten the fruit of this so-called borganza tree, are either loopy or have been dropping some seriously bad acid.

    \n I think it's funny that you're all calling them fruit.

    Count the legs, people. If those were fruit, that number would be zero, not upwards of 40 each.

    Yeah, yeah, maybe it's a distinction without a difference: the larva are perfectly edible and nutritious, you peel them like you would a real banana, you can make a pie out of them, all that stuff. But you might as well be accurate.

    Also, unlike a real banana, when they grow to full maturity, their wings can slice holes in the fabric of space-time. If you could domesticate and train those little bastards, you could find your way back home and get some real bananas, or at least go to some other place with more varied food choices. You might want to make that your next project.
