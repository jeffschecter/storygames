\n
\n So I played this game before I downloaded it, and read that writing about it (here) would be appreciated.
\n
\n
\nI played in a game at HolmCon, Matthijs' so-called "indie-Con" (some call it "hippie-Con"), and I loved it!
\n
\n
\nWe started out beeing chased by dogs and while running, after getting them off our immidiate tail we startet flinging out suspects. We were tree players, and what I loved most about the game except for our ([Fiasco]-like) epilogues and our astonishing success, was that it was really easy to help each others with building a character-within-a-crew. I played the consumate veteran .Coil, maybe 120 years old (the aging effects of our ghost worlds made it difficult to tell, drank a lot of rejuv-juice). Player One played .Grip, our tough and robust and resolute .crew-leader. It was a real pleasure to back him in everything, before shaking my head and speaking up against the stealing of a government pumping-station, a job referred to as a ".station-lol". Player 2 played our young, skilled, though overly ambitious tech'er .Hull, who had an addiction to buying techs and upgrades, I've surmised. I was found to admire his bravado, as I really couldn't consider anything other than .Ghostrunning.
\n
\nI loved how the game let us players just define our skills and props as we went. After firing (killing) our 'jumper' because he blocked .Grip when he was hit with a sniffer-trace, we decided to get my old wife .Wight, who was also .Grip's ex-lover, to help us make the transition to the Ghostworld. In a break, I just got Player 4 to play the .crystal-junkie .Wight, The addition of a fourth player worked like a charm, and the GM instructed brilliantly and the player responded with a similar performance. We all got the .station-lol operation going as a team, as .whight was a "jumper" that followed into the Ghostworld.
\n
\nSwitch was found as the one who sold us out, after gauding his wife in a stripping joint, and some great .net-tracing from .Grip., while .Hull set up the .Station-lol (job). After a great success, we all retired, though we had some tough spells. .Coil barely made it and one ghostworld-manipulation error more would make him a ghost.
\n
\nWe retired in New Hong-Cong (or was it New Holm-Con?)
\n
\nEpilogues:
\n
\nAfter taking care of some business, .Coil had to start his own crew to get his fix.
\n
\nThe channel .Hull opened after our success (.Station-hull) was always grey, to the fresh ghostrunners' chagrin.
\n
\n.Wight went back to .Switch after betraying us (for .Crystal I presume).
\n
\n.Grip would do just one more run...
\n
\n------------------------------
\nThanks to John Harper for making the game!
\n
\nOur GM had played a few times before, I'm sure that the great group had something to say with the experience. I look forward to trying the game at an other time.
