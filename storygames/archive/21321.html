\n I present to you the new Crowdfunding Collection No. 54, including every tabletop project started between 09.25 till 10.09.).

Recently returned from vacation there are no fewer than 62 projects started within the last two weeks.
The projects that sparked my interest the most, are those who I put in the story game category. Like Purgatory House, a quite cool Horror rpg, which sports an interesting Blackjack based system. Generations does intrigue me by offering a concept about characters once removed from life being reborn. Survive Et Al is quite exciting, though maybe a tad boardgamey. Next to those games gamers who like Fate might fancy into the rocking mice of Heavy Metal Thunder Mouse or dive into the GoT inspired fantasy world of Sustina. There\u2019s as well a new Edition of Outbreak: Undead.
Next to those I would like to point you to the Rider of the Black Sun game book by Mantikore, a book I heard is very well received, as well as the Chill Undead Sourcebook.
There are plenty of foreign language games as well. Like, you\u2019ll find quite some projects from Germany. Like the translation of Starfinder, which does quite entice me. Next to this there are two full games. At the one hand Fallen World with a system of its own, at the other hand Seelenf\xe4nger: T\xe4uscherland which is based on Fate Core. Yet, the most interesting project for someone who does not speak German, is likely TSUKUYUMI. Not a tabletop RPG, yet an interesting game.

    You\u2019ll find a few foreign language projects. The Sprawl is translated to German as Der Sprawl. Tales of the Loop will be translated to French and The Dark Eye will get a Dutch translation as Het Oog des Meesters. If you are curious about The Dark Eye and LARP, a crowdfunding for the (German) LARP rules for The Dark Eye is on its way.
    Though, as to get to the meat. I got enamored by CATaclysm. A game in which you play anthropomorphic cats, with a setting that does invoke memories of Nausica\xe4 \u2026 that\u2019s at least for me. Yet I do feel tempted by Jim Pintos Praxis: Black Monk game as well. I\u2019ve read one of the Praxis games and it\u2019s a really interesting concept.
    Then it\u2019s October again and Halloween is encroaching. Thus there are several games placed in the horror genre: Don\u2019t Look Back (Urban Horror), Aliens & Astroids (SF Horror) and B-Horror (well d\u2019uh). Next to those, The Road to Bethlehem is a tabletop rpg / boardgame that caught my interest. It is the first game focused on religion, Christianity to be specific, to actually look competent and fun.
    There are of course more games. Like the D20 / D30 RPG & DCC Dice Chain Sets set, which convinced me by presenting beautiful and strange dice. I am as well still considering getting the Wee Beasties kids book, as it looks really, really cute.
