    \n A 2008 patent for THAT? Dude, I'm surprised the USPO didn't reject it outright, with a trivial search on prior art. I can personally attest (in court, even) that I have used "information-bearing or action-initiating" devices in a LARP in 1997; and frankly, I've done so as far back as 1985 or 86, if plastic pellet guns count as action-initiating devices (Killer game at StellarCon).

    IANAL either, but I wouldn't even hire one, to defend against "infringement" of this supposed patent.

    I just searched the USPO patent database for both the number and elements in the title and got no hits--possibly a hoax? That number doesn't even match USPO patent numbering schemes--the number 20080061502 won't come up for many centuries ("All patent numbers must be seven characters in length, excluding commas, which are optional.")
