\n the discussion of permission to design games made me pull out a 25 year old notebook and scan my early 80s masterpiece quadruple amputee death wrestling. Setting aside the sophomoric & discriminatory subject matter I notice that I had stronger gamist design skills then than I do now :-) Having a subsystem for balancing then falling on one's opponent with a 'breath holding' stat for trapped characters seems particularly ambitious.

    Posted By: Jason MorningstarI think doing one thing really well is a strong choice
    \nexactly, QADW was ahead of its time in a number of regards. It had no setting, in fact it doesn't even have a situation, the situation is IMPLIED in the game's title, which is fairly elegant. You'll also appreciate that it doesn't have any rules for drowning or falling {other than on another character} or burning or freezing or starving. Unfortunately the game had random character generation and a critical hit chart, so I'll have to chalk this one up as a wrestling heartbreaker.
