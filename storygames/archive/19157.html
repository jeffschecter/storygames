    \n Dungeon World: After having established that magic is conducted using the LeGuin Earthsea rules (naming, things are said in the Old Speech, only dragons can speak a falsehood in the Old Speech), Morwen the Mage engages Sargon the Black, notorious serpentine dragon in a riddling contest that Morwen wins when she asks Sargon for her true name, only to reveal that she has none.

    The contest complete, she walks by the paladin. "Now," she says, and the paladin summons the trapped ghosts of Sargon's barrow to attack.

    "You said you would go in peace!" screams Sargon in the Old Speech.

    "I lied," says Morwen.

    \n 13 year old girl is playtesting The Queen's Cavaliers along with her parents. She makes up an alchemist/barrister, deliberately building a non-combat character. During the setup of the bar scene, she is sitting there brewing up an obnoxious-smelling concoction that even she doesn't know what it does.

    A fight breaks out. She wants to throw the strange substance on someone during the fight. I tell her she can do it, and what's more, she can use her Education (Science) as the attack roll. So she throws it! And it splashes into someone's eyes.

    "She blinded me...!" says the NPC.

    "... with science!" exclaim both her parents.

    \n Spectrum

    The Kid is camped out behind a truck-stop in 2008 when she hears a gun shot out front. She Bends time to find a remote-control car with a mounted camera, and sends it to investigate. Through the camera she sees that a time bandit has just shot a trucker. The trucker is bleeding out, and the time bandit is driving away. The Kid Bends time again to find a robot double of the trucker, and time jumps back to yesterday. She hides in the other end of the alley, so her earlier self won't see her, and spends the day building the camera car and planting it for herself to find. She tries to save the trucker by switching him with the robot before he gets shot, but when she's talking to the trucker she draws a Black Joker. The time bandit shows up, and since the Kid is in the way, he shoots her. We end the session with the Kid laying bleeding by the highway.

    The Bend for the remote-control car was resolved by the end of the session, but the Bend for the robot double wasn't, so it causes Paradox. Sophisticated robots are now invented in 2008, years before they were supposed to be.

    \n Yesterday's Eternal Lies (Trail of Cthulhu) dealt with the aftermath of an investigator, Lillian, who killed for the first time and a shattered pillar: Art is the Creation of Beauty and Meaning. The player had me explain the mechanics and filed that away. Some loose ends were wrapped, and there was a lull where folks could talk to their Sources of Stability. One of Lillian's sources was her cousin, potential fiance material, and she asked him to take her out dancing. The evening did not go well, as the investigator was uncharacteristically clumsy on the dance floor, apologized frequently, and had her cousin take her home.

    There, another investigator, Joyce, was playing the banjo, and Lillian asked for a song she could dance to. This was supplied, and she did her martial arts katas to "The Tennessee Waltz" and did them very well. When she was finished, she tried a simple dance step, but stumbled.

    Joyce offered herself as a dance partner, but Lillian turned her down.

    Lillian: I can only dance whe I'm trying to kill something now.

    Joyce (horror survivalist): I understand.

    Lillian (looking at her kukri, sadly): This is the only dance partner I'll ever have from now on.

    \n We played Fiasco at STURP for the first time in over a year, and it was a solid reminder of how awesome it is. Bookhounds of London playset, we had an into-S&M Jeeves & Wooster butler & toff couple, a scheming sacrificial virgin, and a blackmailed bookstore clerk. Needless to say, the two innocents (the bookstore clerk and the clueless toff) came off the worst, while the two Satanists came up smelling of roses. Best line of the night...

    "You can't just murder my fiancee.... EVERY time!"
