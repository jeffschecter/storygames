\n Read somebody mentioning that AW's stats were all about how characters approach threatening situations, and everything suddenly clicked into place. It's not about problem solving, exactly, it's about threat mitigation.

Hard characters beat threats up, maybe kill them. Obvious. Cool characters, in contrast, they do whatever they want in spite of the threat looming in their face. Sharp characters are savvy, they study the threat so they have the most information and can take the best course of action. Hot characters manipulate the threat, turning it to their side or against itself. And weird characters...they're a bit of an oddball, a handful of strangeness and hocus pocus.

This is probably obvious to everyone else, but it's all lining up for me now.

    \n I would say the numbers on the stat hinders people from realizing this. If it were more like "You can only use Hot moves", then it would be more apparent. Numbers steal focus from what something really is, because it makes people focus on the mechanics rather than the result from the mechanics.

    I kinda dislike this kind of rewards based game design, where you let people do whatever, but they get less for it if they don't do it the way the game designer or game master intended. It only results in blurred understanding of the system.
