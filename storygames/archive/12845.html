    \n The PDQ (Prose Descriptive Qualities) system uses d6's and user-defined traits and I believe all of the games written for it are available in PDF (there are at least 3 publishers putting out games in various versions of the PDQ system). Some particular examples that might fit your criteria:
      \n
    • Jaws of the Six Serpents -- Sword and Sorcery with sketched out setting.
      • \n
    • The Zorcerer of Zo -- Fairytale/Oz-like fantasy.
      • \n
    • Swashbucklers of the 7 Skies -- Flying ships, floating sky islands, musketeers, magic, pirates and such. Uses the slightly more complex PDQ# variant of the PDQ system.
    \nI just finished running a six session game of S7S and it was a blast. I don't think adjusting for a single character should be that hard.

    \n You might want to have a look at World vs Hero. I don't think I've seen it discussed here at Story Games yet.

    Caveat would be that it's really intended for 1 GM (World) and 1 player (hero), so it may not be extensible enough for you if you're looking to run with 3. The author has some rules variations that support this on his blog, but says he doesn't really recommend them . The other caveat based on your requirements is that it uses playing cards instead of D6, but if your issue is newbie accessibility or having dice readily available, then cards might be a plus.

    I picked up and and it's really intrigued me. It's very much a cooperate story telling game (establish scenes, resolve conflicts within those scenes), but there's a bit of a competitive vibe and the mechanics make it all feel a little less hand wavy. Basically, you establish the world and the character(s), the world player sets up the locations and conflicts, and the hero player kicks off the first scene. The world player then uses his list of conflicts for that location to narrate challenges based on a draw of cards. Conflicts will have an impact level based on the severity of the challenge and the cards available. The hero player tries to counter that impact level with his own cards and character abilities, and then narrates the result. It's a nice mix of structure and free-form.

    Art and layout is definitely small press, but the content is cool and the author has been very active via his blog and the semi-official Yahoo group forums.

    \nGHOST/ECHO is surprisingly fun for two players. It is exactly as crunchy as you want it to be.

    Eventually, Seth Ben-Ezra (greatwolf on this forum) is going to publish his game Showdown, which is my favorite two-player story game. You should gently remind him if you happen to see him. Zero prep, plays in an hour or so, makes really good fiction, and is really rather innovative.

    Speaking of "innovative", I also hear that Jackson Tegu will eventually release a new version of the smoke dream, which provides a one-of-a-kind two-player experience, above and beyond its excellent (default) solo mode. You could check in with him and see where that project stands.

    You mentioned being open to three-player games:
    Polaris, by Ben Lehman, is said to be fun with three players. It meets your other criteria very nicely.

    A Penny For My Thoughts is, in my opinion, at its absolute shining best with three players. Zero prep, lots of fun, has none of these "traits" of which you speak.

    Oh, and there's always Fiasco. Three players can play in well under two hours, with no prep ahead of time, and wind up with a really satisfying story. I don't suppose I'm telling you anything new on that one, but just in case...

    \n In this world, no thing is perfect. But some are close.

    S/lay w/Me would fit except, sadly, no PDF yet. It's a very cheap direct mail order dead-tree edition, though. It's not for one-shots, despite what you may have heard of it: it's for a long-ish cycle of (inter-)related short stories.
    I strongly recommend it because, frankly, it's the hottest of the hot. http://theunstore.com/index.php/unstore/game/64

    Trollbabe would be great. Too bad there's no PDF available for the most current edition. I'm sensing a pattern here... Ron, I told you!

    Beast Hunters involves some dice which are not d6. :P Check the rules out for free at http://www.berengad.com/ or buy from IPR/Lulu/RPGnow to also get the full setting information.

    Mars Colony is not fantasy and only for 2 players. It's a single scenario that, at a leisurely pace, may take a while to complete. http://theunstore.com/index.php/unstore/game/89

    Solar System/The Shadow of Yesterday would work, but it requires special dice. Better for three than for two, though.

    If there's three of you, the possibilities _explode_. Consider Polaris (though there's no PDF of it) or Anima Prime (maybe not at it's best for three? but it's free on http://www.animaprimerpg.com/main/ so check out for yourself).

    EDIT: I only listed games with very little to just a little crunch, C. Griffen's games and Solar System being the the crunchiest of the bunch.
