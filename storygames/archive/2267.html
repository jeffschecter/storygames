"The Lucky Ones" - suggestions for system

TheCzech
January 2007 edited January 2007 in Story Games
I have a game idea bouncing around in my head that I eventually want to do. Some of the details came from elements of The Forever War by Joe Haldeman. "The Lucky Ones" is about soldiers who go to war and the sacrifices they make to fight.

In 2057, the Earth discovers wormhole technology that allows them access to the starts and immediately ends up at war with another species. The United Nations recruits people to fight in this war and sends them in spaceships to far away star systems. The difficulty is that the wormholes have relativistic effects and while each tour is only three months long from the point of view of the soldiers, longer periods of time have passed on Earth when they return. Their lives change. Their loved ones get on with their lives. Governments rise and fall. Terrestrial conflicts begin and end. The time slips won't be as extreme as in The Forever War where after two tours, everyone the main characters knew had died of old age. This will skip in smaller increments, I'm thinking five years or so, enough to impact the lives of the soldiers.

The game is not focussed on the time spent at war but on the time spent at home in between tours. The PCs will likely be a group of friends who each enlists for his or her own reasons. Many of the soldiers they fight with die in the war, but they will always survive...because they are "The Lucky Ones".

I was inspired to think about this game by stories about soliders in Iraq and comparisons to those who served in VIetnam. There is a sacrifice they make that goes beyond the risk of injury or death. They give up a piece of the life they are defending, sometimes with lifelong consequences. Being a gamer, I think about this and then want to explore it in a game. And when you explore something in a game, you take your issue up a notch...like making a three month tour bring you back home five years later.

I don't want to design a system from scratch for this. First, because I am not a game designer and thus not motivated enough to do so. Second, because a new system for this could easily result in one of those "agenda" games which push a particular point of view even if I don't start out with that goal in mind.

So, the big question. What system do I use to do this? I'm leaning toward TSoY with a modified list of Keys, Secrets, and Abilities, but I'm curious about whether or not people have any other suggestions. My vision for the game is still cloudy and malleable.

    January 2007
    You build a new creation system.

    Absence leads to Weakness, Weakness leads to Change, Change leads to a Hard Choice, Hard Choices lead to Distance, Distance leads to Betrayal.

    Then you use that, plus the Fallout from the previous session, to see what sort of sins against the soldier people want to be judged and forgiven for.

    Absence: You weren't there, and so your son, Teddy, didn't have a father to look up to.
    Weakness: He was getting beaten up at school, and his grades were slipping because he was so miserable.
    Change: Your brother, Mike, started spending much more time with him.
    Hard Choice: Teddy wanted Uncle Mike to go to the father-son picnic with him. Mike wouldn't do it.
    Distance: Now Teddy barely comes home at all any more. He's hanging out with a bad crowd.

    Mike: Wants you to tell him that he did the right thing, but fears that he abandoned the nephew he loves
    Teddy: Wants his father to be easy to hate, but fears that he'll still love the old man, even knowing that they'll be separated again.

    Is that all about Mike and Teddy? I suppose. Sorta. But it's also all about the soldier, right?
