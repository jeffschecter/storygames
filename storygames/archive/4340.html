    \n I don't have the foggiest idea why, but this sounded like fun:

    Inside a wooden box with lid of garnet-inlaid gold (1,500 gp)AB:
    pair of gold and garnet shoulder-clasps (1,000 gp)AB
    huge golden buckle, 5 inches long (2,000 gp)B
    carved sceptre of whetstone with a bronze stag atop it (200 gp)B

    Inside a moldy leather purse:
    40 gold coins bearing an ancient King's name and likeness ( I assume these are worth 40 gold pieces, huh?) (40 gp)C
    2 small gold bars (each the weight of 4 coins) (8 gp)C

    In a rusted heap:
    A war helmet, inlaid with garnet (200 gp)
    A iron sword with a gold pommel (700 gp)
    A shirt of chain mail (100 gp)D
    An iron war hammer (20 gp)D

    Tied in a bundle:
    Twelve worm-eaten wooden spears (1 gp)D
    A decayed wooden shield with a decorative bronze boss (5 gp)

    Inside a rotted cedar chest:
    a set of ten silver bowls that fit into one another (70 gp)B
    a pair of silver spoons inscribed with the names of Orvar and Olrun (30 gp)B
    large bronze hanging bowl decorated in enamel (30 gp)B
    a large silver dish (55 gp)
    a pair of drinking horns decorated with silver gilt foil (20 gp)B

    Inside a ratty beaver skin bag:
    A bard's maplewood lyre (100 gp)

    Stuffed in a rusted cauldron with a long suspension chain:
    A pile of damp and ruined fur and textiles (0 gp)

    TOTAL: 6,079 gp -- Enough to buy the Horshoes of a Zephyr -- Enjoy!

    Notes
    A - Per DMG pricing, there are several different types of garnet of implictly varying values. I assumed the lowest value garnet.
    B - I'm assuming by the description that these items have artistic value beyond the value of the raw materials of sufficient measure to warrant an upshift.
    C - They could theoretically be worth more if they had numistmatistic value, but I'm assuming not given nothing else from the description.
    D - I assumed that these items were NOT masterwork.
    E - I assumed the spear tips were in good enough condition that they could be used in the making of new spears.

    \n Inside a wooden box with lid of garnet-inlaid gold: (the box itself is nice, worth 100gp on it's own)
    pair of gold and garnet shoulder-clasps (190-230gp each)
    huge golden buckle, 5 inches long (175 gp)
    carved sceptre of whetstone with a bronze stag atop it (as a curiosity and antique it's worth more to the right dealer, 150-200 gp)
    (clearly these items are some sort of set. If packaged together they would be worth 1500-2225gp)

    Inside a moldy leather purse:
    40 gold coins bearing an ancient King's name and likeness ( I assume these are worth 40 gold pieces, huh?) (these are actually worth less than 40 because they are alloy coins, they are only worth 20 or so GP. HOWEVER if you were to find a rare coin collector, they could be worth anywhere from double to triple face value)
    2 small gold bars (each the weight of 4 coins) (these are just gold and worth their weight, 4gp each)

    In a rusted heap:
    A war helmet, inlaid with garnet (book price for a helmet +35-40gp)
    A iron sword with a gold pommel (book price for special material + 20-25 gp, total is probably around 90 gp)
    A shirt of chain mail (book price)
    An iron war hammer (book price)

    Tied in a bundle:
    Six worm-eaten wooden spears (these are worthless)
    A decayed wooden shield with a decorative bronze boss (practically worthless, maybe a few silver)

    Inside a rotted cedar chest:
    a set of ten silver bowls that fit into one another (35 gp)
    a pair of silver spoons inscribed with the names of Orvar and Olrun (3 silver for both. HOWEVER, if returned to the children of Orvar and Olrun the would be considered family heirlooms and would pay a great deal more 250gp)
    large bronze hanging bowl decorated in enamel (30gp)
    a large silver dish (45gp)
    a pair of drinking horns decorated with silver gilt foil (20gp each)

    Inside a ratty beaver skin bag:
    A bard's maplewood lyre (12gp on the open market. If someone realizes it was once owned by Frensco de'Tembretine however, it would fetch upwards of 300-400gp.)

    Stuffed in a rusted cauldron with a long suspension chain:
    A pile of damp and ruined fur and textiles (these are so crappy and smelly that to carry them around will actually make you less likely to sell anything and will reduce the prices you get for other stuff)
