\n We played a game of Joe McDaldno's Quiet Year over the weekend. Our post-apocalyptic community inhabited a damaged airship in some demolished Final Fantasy esque world. They had to contend with gyro-raiders, octosharks, toxic waters and other such threats from the world. To further complicate things the population of mostly women (and few young people) was at odds with the all male "crew" (especially as the men started hoarding the technical manuals, damn you Chief Hard!) but the socialist/marxist/feminist reading group that arose to counter that influence created a lot of fun drama. Next time we play we plan to keep a closer eye on the "dispassionate" portion of the rules but we really had a blast (and a few of our group are a hard sell on "these" types games).

    \n In hindsight, it felt like aspects of the community became a little too \u201cprecious\u201d to some of us and there was not enough resistance put up to that current at times. We could have gone a little more balls to the wall and I think it would have been more exciting. This is not to say we didn\u2019t have a blast but when we discussed the game afterwards we came away with the general conclusion we would be a little more vigilant in our distance when appropriate the next time we play. I largely chock it up to the fact we were learning a new game though (Note: There is a strong \u201ctraditionalist\u201d strain to our group and we\u2019ve been gaming together since elementary school so certain understandable habits are set in- I\u2019ll tell you about my disastrous Poison\u2019d game I ran at some point Joe over a beer or something at the next con we bump into each other at.)

    On the project dice front, we stuck to 1-6 projects basically (though I think for one we started to wrangle and just made it a 7 weeker but it was at towards the end of the game so kind of moot anyway). My main reason I brought the 10's is that my swanky little jade dwarvenstones normally only get broken out when we play L5R.
