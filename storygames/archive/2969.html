\n
\n This weekend, we're off to a farmhouse in Northamptonshire to playtest roleplaying games. There's eight or nine of us, playtesting each other's games. Some are pretty complete, some are just ideas.

So it's a weekend of games, good food (Scott is cooking Japanese, I'm bringing my skillet, Andrew's doing fajitas) and of course alcohol. Lots of it. Because how can you have a gaming weekend without alcohol? And no kids. Of course.

Does this sound good? Would you like to come? Well, you can't!

So Malcolm's bringing Everlasting Empire, his sci-fi British Empire game, which has astonishing art; and also Okhrana, which I don't know anything about, but it's one of Malcolm's games, so it's probably bloody good and way better than anything I could design.

I'm bringing The Gay Recruitment Squad Wants YOU!, my hybrid board-homosexuality game, and Tales of the Ninja, a quick idea which uses red and black cards.

Andrew Kenrick is bringing his Kill Bill/Memento game, Six Bullets For Vengeance, which I'm very excited about, having played it a couple of months ago. And also Lost Days Of Memories And Madness, his game about bored elves. (Stop me if that's wrong).

Rich is bringing Umlaut, which is a mash-up between Contenders and HARD ROCK. And Danger Dudes, which is about action heroes, I think.

Iain is bringing Reel Adventures, which I played while completely pissed and enjoyed. I played a small robot who didn't talk, to the relief of all the other players at the table. And What A Shambles, which I think is about zombies, and Stitch, which I think is about time travel, and probably the latest iteration of his card game, which I also played while drunk and enjoyed.

James is bringing stuff too! But I can't find what at the moment!

Does this sound too good to be true? Would you like to be involved? Well, simply go to the Collective Endeavour site, right now, and you'll find it was booked up months ago! So you can't! Also, let's face it, we only know you through the Internet, and you might be really weird.

Anyway, I'm looking forward to it, and I hope everyone else is too. Beer. Wine. Beer. Roleplaying games. Wine. Wine. Beer. Whatever noxious alcoholic substance Malcolm has brought with him. Perhaps another roleplaying game. Beer. Wine.

So that's Spodley Grange, this weekend. We'd love you to be there. But you can't.

Graham
