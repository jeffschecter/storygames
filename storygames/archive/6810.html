\n Every year, at about this time, I get the idea to fly over to GenCon at the last minute.

Is this even possible? The flight would be possible, of course, but accommodation is probably booked up. The GenCon website is entirely unhelpful: I'd have to buy a badge, first, then be put on a waiting list for accommodation.

So. Is there a way? I'm just generally wondering if it's possible.

Graham

    \n There are hotels that are not on the GenCon list but are still close by. Since they're not on the GenCon list they can still have space. For instance, the Evil Hat guys followed our cue last year and went to the "University Place Conference Center & Hotel" which had a free shuttle bus taking us the mile to the conference centre. It was plush and not expensive.

    Otherwise, put the word around and find folks with space in rooms.

    \n Take the floors if you can get it. Having stayed outside of walking distance of the conference center, it's a hassle and tiring to get the shuttle in the morning, pretty much be stuck there all day with all the stuff you might need/want, and then taxi cab it back to your hotel (because many of the free hotel shuttles end their pick ups between 5 and 7, way before the day is over at the Con). Although places that are around a mile, that's an easy walk, though not sure one I'd want to make around two a.m. I stayed at a racetrack hotel (which are a stone's throw from the airport two, so those might also count as "airport" hotels).
