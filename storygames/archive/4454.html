    \n One trick: If you post, then get a 500 error, wait a few minutes, and hit Reload. In mozilla, anyway, you get a message asking if you want to resupply the same information or something. If you choose yes, it may 500 again, but eventually the post will go through. Sometime it double-posts though.

    Also, I think I found a problem: The IP History records (for members, visitors and guests) were literally 15 times larger than all the other records combined. The 70-80mb of posts and stuff were overshadowed by the 160+mb of pure IP history logs. Purged that table and noticed an immediate performance gain. Hopefully that's all that was needed to get the performance back, but I'll keep an eye on things this week.

    -Andy

    \n Yeah, that might have to change.

    Notifications on = The DB grinds to a standstill.
    Notifications off = The DB access is quick, and on top of that I can move the view to 100 threads per page, and increase the number of comments per thread page as well without any performance problem. With notifications on, this cripples the system within minutes.

    Unfortunately, Notify was a 3rd party extension that doesn't look to be supremely streamlined. I was wondering why the very few Vanilla installations that were larger than S-G were able to perform so well. The answer is apparently, "They're not using Notify".

    I'm going to think of some side-solutions for people who want to keep notified of their threads: Like offering a workplan for using the RSS feeds-by-thread on open threads to get the latest on a thread, etc.
