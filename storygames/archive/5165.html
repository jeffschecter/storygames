    \n Hmmm. I put my 2008 agenda in the 2008 Thread already, but a quick recap (I think I've had one or two additions since then):

    I'll condense my stuff form that list to the stuff that I'm not "destined" or lined up to play, and that I might not otherwise play unless I make an effort to learn the rules, set up a campaign, get folks organized, etc.

    D&D 4e, when it comes out. I like what I hear about the low-level stuff. Upper level stuff? Hmmmm. But low-level 4e sounds like it's going to rock. I'm going to try to get a game up within two weeks of its release.

    Earthdawn, set in the Iron City, an India-themed campaign world that my weekly game group uses on and off for years

    Best Friends AMBER Throne War, stolen straight from Fred. It will get run this year.

    In a Wicked Age, when it comes out.

    Tenra Bansho Zero, a campaign that shows a war between three countries in three mini-campaigns, with three perspectives.

    Virtue and Valour - a quick playtest

    A gritty game based on World War Z, with multiple mini-campaigns and one shots that build up to a larger campaign. Haven't decided a system, and AFMBE just doesn't seem quite right. Nemesis seems closer to what I want with its Unknown Armies style horror stuff.

    A Vampire: Rome game based on the new campaign material. One of the folks in my bi-weekly game is excited about that, and after taking a peek, I think I am, too.

    Those, above, are the ones that I want to do, that it would take some effort to put together (and not just "scheduled for play anyway). Resolution-worthy.

    Also, I'm raising my priority for exercise above gaming (exercise was slightly below gaming last year, meaning if I was tired I'd read through threads here rather than book it to the weight room). It's a very minor change, but could lead to getting away from the computer, and getting over to the gym more often. Could add up to maybe an additional 120+ hours of exercise or more this year over what I normally do, focusing mostly on yoga and weights.

    -Andy

    \n The 10-15 hour single player campaign a player will go through before they choose a server will give people a little extra time to "faff about".

    I forgot about the latency issue. That will be more important for this game than for any MMORPG preceding it.

    I have read thousands of computer game reviews, calibrated my preferences to certain reviewers on certain sites, and find them extremely useful. The problem of ad revenue does promote a general inflation of scores, but I think that there's an overall level of professionalism for video game reviews that roleplaying game reviews have a ways to go to meet. Most rpg "reviewers" have reviewed, like, two games ever, ones that have them particularly jazzed. Professional video game reviewers review at least a couple games a week and have language and norms that just aren't as universally present for rpgs.

    All in my opinion, of course, and all a bit off-topic for this thread and board...
