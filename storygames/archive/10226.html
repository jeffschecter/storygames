    \n Walked into Gamma Ray today and spent 20 minutes talking with Eric, the owner/manager, about indie games and what we could do to help support his store. Apparently, he's gotten frustrated trying to order stuff from IPR -- can't tell if that's him or IPR or both -- to the point of thinking about getting the Dreaming to place an order for Gamma Ray through their account. Crazy. I told him I'd try to help him sort things out.

    He also said he'd bought 6 copies of Carry through an Alliance sale (WTF?) and sold them all, so he found two more somewhere. I told him that I knew Nathan from Boston and that I'd did layout for Mortal Coil Revised. Those two together blew his mind, I think. Microfame is so weird. I also mentioned that I knew John Harper, but apparently he didn't know who that was (ICE BURN!). He'll know soon enough, I'm sure

    Anyway, the indie games he had on his shelf were: Burning Wheel (1), Monster Burner (1), Magic Burner (1), Carry (2), Seven Leagues (1), and maybe one more thing that I don't remember. But he, personally, is really interested in playing more indie games, so he's committed to making something happen on that end. I mentioned maybe running some Dogs or Mouse Guard demos and his eyes lit up. He said he was thinking of putting a Primetime Adventures game together and would let me know.

    I told him about Story Games Boston and how we'd run an open indie games night every Wednesday for almost 3 years. He said he'd be interested in putting something like that together, and I pitched him a few ideas about how to make sure it continued to support both indie games and the store (since SGB was really bad at generating revenue for Pandemonium and Pandys were really bad at taking our money).

    He said he more or less couldn't get enough people running 4E in his store to support the interest. He said the store had 150ish friends on Facebook who were "committed gamers" (I quivered in fear) and he was hand-booking people into slots for open games, to try to make sure a few folks didn't monopolize everything by just registering for whatever was open.

    I was sitting there reading GTM and waiting for him to help a customer buy some Magic cards when two 20-something girls walked in talking about the various merits of Scrabble. They looked around the shelves a bit but didn't really see anything worth picking up and looking at, so they walked out. The store itself is pretty interesting in that regard. It looks very much like a trendy boutique kind of place, all white and "spaceship-like" (as Eric put it) on the inside. Fancy shelves filled with $60-100 board games by Fantasy Flight. But it doesn't really carry many games that appeal to a hypothetical "general" audience that isn't excited by genre fiction trappings of zombies, dungeons, and spaceships exploding. The most mainstream games I saw were Dominion and Powergrid.

    Anyway, all in all a worthy experiment and I really hope the store does well. Hopefully we'll be able to work something out, both so the store can carry a bunch of indie games (though not as many as the Dreaming, since there isn't enough space) and so I can run some one-shots in the store and share the love with other folks how may not have had the opportunity to play indie games before.
