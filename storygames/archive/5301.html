\n So I was chatting with Dave Cleaver and we were talking about how much we dig IAWA. Then he says, "You know what I want that you'll hate?"

And I'm like "A republican for president?"

And he says, "No, Rifts IAWA."

So we talk about this bit of crack for a time, and discuss how we'd make Oracles. Things like "War is Hell" in which you've got the noble Coalition pilot who is in trouble for not killing an enemy girl; the juicer with only days to live, who has just found his lost sister; the expiremental glitterboy armor that vanished from storage; and a starving village, teetering on the edge of oblivion. Or "Fallen Angels and Demon Raiders" in which you have things like: blind slave warriors of the Splurgoth sent to gather souls; a mysterious standing stone that throbs with blue power; a mad plane walker with no memory; and the fallen Cyberknight and his wounds that will not heal.

Now I'm all like, "Wow, a Rifts game I'd think about playing. Is there anything IAWA cannot do?"

    \n In proper Rifts style we could have location-based oracles in addition to thematic ones. So, like, you go to Mexico and you draw from the "Desert Bandits & Vampire Gods" oracle. Or you could have a whole "Siege of Tolkeen" oracle. Or you travel to Wormwood ("Endless Crusade on the Living Planet") or Phase World ("Space Ghosts & The Cosmic Forge") and you have oracles for those dimensions. That way, the PCs would always be a mixture of the travelers and the people they encounter there. But that would make for a much more scattered game than the issues-focused North America-centered one that you're talking about.
