\n I've stopped passing the die in act one. Here is why...

-I like looking at the pile of dice in front of everyone at the end and it being representative of what they actually went through in the game.

-It always confuses people when I'm teach the game,
them -"so now I give this to someone? who?"
me - "well... if you want to screw someone up make sure they get lots of dice of the same color, if you want to help them out make sure..."
them - (wrinkled look of concentration / confusion)

any thoughts about this? Is there some rad fun I'm missing out on?

    \n That's a good way to describe it. I think (heh) it works pretty well as written, but you do need to be thorough about explaining not only what to do with the dice, but why. It takes only a minute after the Setup and before Act One.

    "OK, so the reason giving your dice away is important is because at the Tilt, halfway through, and at the Aftermath at the end, the number and color of dice you've accumulated is going to matter. A parity of black and white dice is very bad. Lots of black, or lots of white, is good. If you want someone's character to end up in a trash heap and on fire, help keep their black and white dice dead even in number. If you want them to get away and live happily ever after, help them build a single color across the game."
