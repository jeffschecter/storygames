\n So it appears that sometime in the last week, Yaplet went explodey. Doesn't appear to be live anymore, nor does there appear to be any discussion online as to what happened to it.

Too bad, but whatever. Ideas for alternatives? Are alternatives even needed?

I'd be very interested in something that:
Easy to Admin
Keeps discussion logs

I've been avoiding IRC in the past, simply because it's a lawless shithole at times, but as long as folks are able to accept that, and understand that S-G Chat =/= S-G (if Bad Stuff happens there, it doesn't mean that S-G is Bad, etc) I'd be up for throwing a chat room on an IRC server somewhere (MagicStar or other).

I was even thinking of a Japanese (at least, common on Japanese sites) ghetto web-chat client, which is basically the equivalent of a modified Guestbook: You type in a sentence, hit Post, the page reloads with your post plus the replies of others who posted in the interim.

As for directed or timed conversation/discussion, Google Plus seems excellent on that front: Messed around with it a few times, seems solid enough. Would love to try a "Story Games discussion of Some Topic" on it at some point.

-Andy

    Posted By: George CotronisWhat about a google hangout?
    \nWell, that's the thing: A Google Hangout is great for directed discussions happening at a specific time. Or if people want to meet other people for their own gaming/private discussions. But a Google Hangout is:

    * Tied to a specific user's account (frex, for me I can't create a general "Story Games" account which has an ongoing Hangout. At least, not yet anyway. I'd have to do it from my personal "Andy Kitkowski" Google Plus account), and

    * Not permanent. It has to be started and stopped by a human. You can't just have it go on forever and let people join and drop as they saw fit.
