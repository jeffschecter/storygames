\n Plans are beginning to come together for Games on Demand at PAX East 2013 in Boston (March 22-24) and now is a great time to raise your hand and volunteer to join us!

The event will take place in the Tabletop hall again this year, and we\u2019re working with organizers to secure space on the main aisle across from all the vendor booths. Complimentary guest badges will be available to volunteers, giving you access to the show for the whole weekend. Volunteering involves a combination of hosting and running games you love for enthusiastic strangers, for a total of 12 hours over the course of the show. Everyone who volunteered last year said that it was a lot of fun.

If you\u2019re interested in joining us, or if you have questions, add a comment here or drop us a line at the IGE Games on Demand site.

If you'll be at PAX as a panel speaker or to run a booth (@Luke_Wheel, @Jim_Crocker, @Lumpley, and everyone at the Indie Bazaar booth), please feel free to talk about your plans here as well. I'd love to talk about how we can coordinate the events better.

    \n That's a question of some debate, but in general there's not a list of approved games. You should come ready to run 3 or so of your favorites- ideally games that run well in a convention slot (roughly 2-4 hours) and that are appealing and easy to pitch to new players. You could also bring games that run faster or prepare demos of long-running games, so that people can get a taste before they run off to a panel or something.

    The crowd at PAX are pretty unfamiliar with our games. Many of them are familiar with D&D, even if they haven't played, and most seem to have played card games like Magic (both of which will be available elsewhere). We tend to use "tabletop" and "pen-and-paper role-playing" as qualifiers to help them realize it's not a video game (which is helped by the fact that we're in the tabletop gaming hall).

    This follow-up thread from the 2012 show might give you some useful information, and this one provides a lot of data on GoD, including the games that were run.
