\n I'm gonna try and run a one-shot of this next time my group gets together, so I made a sample character. Let me know if I did anything real dumb or real awesome so I can give good advice to my players (not gonna make 'em do full character creation, don't worry). In particular this is such a high powered game that I'm having trouble judging whether my choices are too conservative or too over-the-top or what. (Also I used a D6 to choose my Past, Heart, and Fate because shit, I don't know.)

Name: Void
Past: Exile
Heart: Bearer
Fate: Chaos

Heart's Questions
How did you gain your relic? It was always in my village - none could take it but me.
What does your relic whisper to you? "There can be only one - let it be you."
Do you trust your relic? No.

Past's Questions
Why were you cast out? I was the only one who could take the Relic. It made them afraid.
What skill has best served you in surviving all these years? Knowing how - and when - to stay hidden.
What about mortal nature is wrong? They give in to fear too easily.

Weapons
The ability to shape earth into objects. (Intrinsic)
I can move unheard and unseen. (Intrinsic)
A platinum, eyeless mask that lets me see emotional energy. (Relic)

Mortal Form: A filthy wander, long mangled beard and hair.
Personal Blight: People temporarily obtain small powers that they cannot control.
Paragon Form: I look otherwise normal, but with shadows for eyes.
Supernatural Form: My skin appears as if made of earth; my hair of shadow.
Godly Form: I appear as shadow and mud, writhing around randomly in a vaguely humanoid shape.
Fate's Powers: I can make any object not held by a Myth or Mythender fly about in random patterns around me.

First Gift: Grievous Harm (2)
Storm Rating: 3

    \n Thoughts, because I freaking loved this game when I got to play it...

    The weapons could use some re-tinkering, IMO. Earthbending is nifty, but it's not something that's phrased in a way that lends itself to Mythic actions. And you'll be wanting Mythic actions by the end. I would say "Mastery of earthen power." Remember--Mythenders are badass and not sorry for it. There is no Level 0.

    For reference: in the game I played, one of the other Mythenders had the following weapon: "Fishing lines made of the tears of my children." (who were killed by a plague) You will be using those weapons as springboards for your stuff...so yeah, I think you're a bit too conservative here.

    "I can move unheard and unseen" is actually pretty good; it scales well. I'd bump up the mask to something like "My mask, which shows me the turmoil in all other souls." That's freakin' delicious Mythender goodness there. Remember you can totally weaponize that turmoil. It's legit to say "I reach into Thor's chest and crush his most beloved memories." It totally works. That's Mythender.

    The forms look good, but I'd bump things up a bit more titanic by the time you get to Godly. Remember, you are almost a Myth at that point. Make Fate's Power bigger. "I can loose the bonds of entropy and unmake the ways of Creation." This is the guideline for your higher-up actions, after all.

    These are my thoughts, based on the in-play experience that I've had.

    Also, theme music for character brainstorming.

    \n As I recall, Fate's Power is more of a general color guide. (Also, have you tried the mechanics out, even solo? They become a lot clearer when you've actually played them out.)

    The answers to the questions basically serve to give the character a backstory, and that only comes up so much as the player wants it to come up. Generally speaking, it's the "why" of their fight, although players have likely just bought into "we're agonna end some Myths!" on its own. I suspect the answers to the questions will be far less important on the initial run-through, moreso if you actually embark on a campaign. The first time through, it's mostly coming to grips with the rules and the epic action and so on.

    However, it does help to give some context. For instance, I established through my questions that my Mythender (who was an Abomination) was sired by Odin, thus making her Thor's half-sister.

    Oh, and push the envelope when you get to the higher actions. I polished Thor off with the following Titanic action: from my "bag of devices that I have constructed to end myths (a relic weapon)", I drew forth bracers of incredible strength, and (since we were falling through a hole blasted through the center of the world...don't ask) slammed both sides of the world-hole on Thor. Yeah. I smashed him between the foundations of Norden. Like a pancake.

    We also had another player literally call the Moon down to Norden. Two of the Mythenders died, smashed into its surface.
