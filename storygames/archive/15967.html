    \n OK, you bastards. I just spent an hour digging around for every roleplaying game product I could dig up, and spent another hour choosing from among them.

    I held myself to one simple rule: if I clap my eyes on it and it's an RPG, in the pile it goes. Hilariously, I saw a copy of my own game, The Dreaming Crucible, something like three times and didn't once think to include it.

    I laid them all out on the floor in something like an 8 by 5 grid with a blank space in the middle. Each game had a "space" the size of one full-sized D&D type book, so that smaller books wouldn't be disadvantaged. I spun around with my eyes closed and tossed D12s one by one, with Annie watching where each one hit.

    I got Over the Edge plus Decipher's Lord of the Rings.

    I'm a little underwhelmed. Sure, I could use either game's resolution rules to play the other's fictional content, but nothing in there suggests to me OMG CRAZY GAME CONCEPT SUBVERSION!!!! like I was hoping.

    I let Annie try tossing for me. She turned up Jackson Tegu's Slammin' and Ben Lehman's The Drifter's Escape. a little more crazypants, so that's good! But I'm not sure how to make that work.

    Well, maybe once I've played one of those, I can try a combination that grabs me more. The possibilities! WEG Star Wars using s/Lay w/Me! 1st Edition AD&D using Bacchanal! Sea Dracula using the Mustang! Absolutely ANYTHING using the Bullwinkle and Rocky Role Playing Party Game!

    One month is too short for Play Games with Other Games Month!

    Peace,
    -Joel
