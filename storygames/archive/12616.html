    \n It's great to see Mondo 2000 getting dragged back out into the cold 21th century light of day. For me, that magazine was the link between the Gibson cyberpunk trilogy and the fringes of the internet we still see now.

    A few weeks ago I was reading some of the content a friend wrote for the old floppy disc-based Beyond Cyberpunk and flipping through his collection of Mondo and Reality Hackers magazines.

    If you were there in the late-80s/early-90s you should definitely check the Kickstarter out.

    Bring back Durk & Sandy!!

    \n Cool, thanks, Ryan!

    I'm definitely going to have to get my old issues out after I move. I expect to be alternately impressed and bemused by my bOING bOING and Mondo 2000 issues. A lot of people, geeks and gamers especially, tend to spend a fair amount of brainspace thinking about futurism from the 20s through the 60s. It is really weird to now be getting old enough such that futurism from what feels like yesterday (late 80s, early 90s) in my own frackin' lifetime is now retro futurism. We still don't have our jetpacks or full body immersion VR, but in a lot of ways the internet really changed everything such that twenty years ago might as well be from another eon.

    I didn't pack up everything yet. I think I'll get my Cyberpunk 2013 stuff out tonight. 2013 is now three years away, people. Can you smell the cyber-psychosis yet?
