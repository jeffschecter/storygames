    \n Simon...what I'd like to see in future is:

    1. More articles like Simon Carryer's "Transport in the 1930s". Setting stuff I can use in a game. How about, say, a guide to "Shanghai in the 1930s", which would link in with your Stunning Eldritch Tales adventures?
    2. Expanded articles on things like music in games. Articles in the last two issues have skirted around this topic, but they were very brief: the one in this issue basically says "Introductory music is good...and here's something I wrote!". There's definitely more to explore here.
    3. I find Robin interesting when he talks about gaming and GMing generally, but less when he talks about Gumshoe. So I'd like him to focus more generally, please, with less specific Gumshoe articles. You know, if he wants to.

    For your information, this is what I did with this issue of Page XX:

    1. Read the thing on introductory music, which was intriguing but too short.
    2. Read Robin's article.
    3. Read the first paragraph of Julia's article, didn't really understand what she was getting at and gave up after the third paragraph.
    4. Made a cup of tea.

    As a suggestion, perhaps you could mark things like the Crane/Baker interview clearly as "Audio", on the front page, thus managing expectations and shit.

    Graham
