\n I often have dreams about games that don't exist.

Last night I dreamed I was in a game store and White Wolf had come out with a new game that jumped up on the recent "boxed set" bandwagon. Unlike the new Gamma World or Warhammer Fantasy games, White Wolf had gone with smaller boxes more like Everway or the original "gift sets" for Magic: The Gathering (wow, that takes me back). Slightly smaller than the Freemarket box.

Plus, rather than having a single set for the entire game, White Wolf had a different box set for each "splat" in the game, each school of witchcraft that your character could be part of. Basically, you bought a boxed set to play a specific type of character and the game included solo or 1-on-1 rules so you could create and play a witch of that particular type either on your own or with a friend playing a very structured GM role (like playing the monsters in the new Castle Ravenloft). So it was like a cross between an Apocalypse World playbook and a stand-alone solo RPG boardgame.

Ideally, after doing some solo or 1-on-1 adventuring on your own, you could get some friends together, each of whom would have their own boxed set (perhaps of a different witch type) or you could all play out of the same box as members of the same clan of witches. Then you would have a multi-player game, with one player serving as a combined GM for everyone else. This wasn't in the dream but it strikes me that maybe the GM could have rules for playing out what the antagonists do off-screen, so they could have solo adventures on the side as well.

In any event, the witch types in the dream had really crazy White Wolf-y names:
-- Protectae
-- Swamp Misers
-- Gorgorath
-- Fabula
-- etc.

It was impossible to tell from the boxes what time period the game was set in, though I have an intuition that maybe it was set in the future, like Blade Runner-esque or on the verge of some environmental or resource-based apocalypse. The art on the boxes reminded me of the cover to the novel "2666", sections of fine art paintings with design elements layered on top, but the design work was mostly pretty terrible. Now, if only I could remember what the game was called.

    \n Oh yeah, and resolution and character creation was totally card-based. The game came with a few booklets, but most of the materials were printed on various sets of cards that were arrayed in little groups like the inside of the Dominion box. The cards probably also contained plots and antagonists and locations and the like.
