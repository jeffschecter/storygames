    \n Seth's forthcoming "Showdown" is my favorite game for play by Internet anything. I've played by email and IM, and it was great. The fact that each player has well-bounded turns, and can work without feedback within each turn, makes it sing even when played on high-latency media.

    It is also two-player and GMless, so dropouts aren't really a thing, you only have to wait for that one other person, and there is no bottleneck.

    \n I'd be interested to hear about PBF on RPGG. I had a discussion with Dave Bernazzani about some of the limitations and decided it would be totally inferior to e.g. Snail's Pace for a game of Apocalypse World. Though I see that Marshall's playing an AW game over there. What are the pros and cons?

    One substantial hardship I see is that RPGG expects your game to consist of exactly two threads -- one OOC and one IC (though that's not a hard rule). The AW game that I'm currently MCing has 39 including all the closed scenes and 9 different OOC threads to organize info. Also, if I'm seeing it right, unless you form a guild for your game (which you can) then all the PBF games of a certain game, are all in the same forum, mixed up. Without good thread-tagging or something that's kind of a mess. How do these weigh on your games? What else?

    \n There are some advantages to running games on RPGG:
    In the games I've run, you typically thumb other people's posts as you read them - this lets you know who's read which post.
    I love being able to tip people when they do awesome things.
    It works well for games that take place in one thread, e.g. Fiasco worked really well.
    You have all the text/link/etc. buttons to let you format your posts (which are, at least for me, absent on Snail's Pace where I also game, thus teaching me HTML).
    There is already a community of gamers and many want to try out these new games they hear about

    Downsides:
    People are there to do other things than PbF, you have to look a little harder to find people who won't flake on your game (once you find a good group, stick with them!)
    I'm not sure what the best way for doing multi-thread games is, probably a guild

    \n I recently started a pbem/pbp TSOY game. Actually, it is basically a sequel to our old high school D&D campaign (same players, same setting, same characters), converted to TSOY/Solar System, and run using a combination of Google Groups and Obsidian Portal. Not only is it cool that our old group is able to play together despite geographical distance and general busyness, it's also pretty bad-ass to be able to play an awesome story game on my iPhone while waiting for my burrito at El Zarape, if you know what I mean.
    \n
    \nAlthough pbp moves slowly, there are some interesting emergent features. For one thing, while tabletop games have elements of acting/improv, our pbp game is more about creative writing. This takes the whole cooperative storytelling thing in a little bit of a different direction. Also, because of the wiki, I'm finding that players are creating NPCs and setting details in a way that they normally wouldn't, without even being told to do so...
    \n
    \nWho ever thought that we would be playing a free, open-source RPG using a combination of e-mail, forum, and wiki on our *phones*? That is just a trip.
