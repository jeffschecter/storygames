    \n Interrobangs are bangs that change not just the character's stake in the story, but also the player's stake in the social contract.

    Interrobangs are bangs that are part of the climax rather than the build-up.

    Interrobangs are when an entire bandolier of bangs are triggered at once.

    Interrobangs are when a player both poses and answers their own bang.

    Interrobangs are the preplanned bangs that actually get used.

    \n Dunno, but I'll try.

    A player stresses that they want to play a seasoned explorer in a "no magic" pulp adventure campaign. You narrow that down and find that the player likes mummies and cursed/holy artifacts. It's wizardish magic they don't want in their pulp adventure. They want Indiana Jones, not Eberron.

    Okay, so now you've got Indy sneaking into the private museum of a wealthy mobster married to Indy's ex-broad who was recently kidnapped. Indy was just looking at a Sumerian tablet of curse-whoever-owns-it. Which of them does own it, and is that blood on the shelf behind it? Nearby, there's an urn with an etched scene resembling telekinesis, and inside the urn is a rolled map tagged "Nazi Secret Project."

    Bang! Somebody's coming this way, and they are dragging a struggling someone-else with them.

    A player who wants a slightly more realistic investigator than Indiana Jones might examine the bloody area for more physical clues. A player who wants par-for-the-Indy might try to steal the necklace. A player who wants something edging slightly closer to Eberron (or Indy 4), might go for the telekinesis clue.

    (And of course, a player who prefers action or negotiation over investigation may choose to rush or wait for the somebody coming this way, but those choices aren't as directly relevant to the example.)
