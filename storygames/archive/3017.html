\n Dear Story-Games;

I had a thread about my LARP rules (Dustbowl Prophets); this is kind of an up-to-date on the stuff I think might interest folks here, since many of these ideas came from here in various ways.

First, the rulebook is about halfway from "where it was" to "has integrated all feedback from here". The players were pretty pleased, and there was a fair bit of talk about where things are still weak, where they're now stronger in actual play, and so on. Good stuff. Vices were simply cut, along with the two colors of tokens. A lot of niggling details were cleaned up, and so on. It's still not at the point where I want to wave it around again online; I know where to work, and when that's done, I'll ask again if people see any more details that need adressing.

Second. That link to Pocketmods that got put up in the mini-book thread? My players have always been handing in sheets that are folded into eighths, so I built a pocketmod sheet. They love these. A lot. And then I discovered something fun - if you put a staple in the spine of a pocketmod, and then another at the very bottom of the first 'page', you get a pocket inside that fits business cards perfectly - and business cards are what the game uses for item chits. This is supercool. By tossing one of those big black paperclamps on top at the end of an event, players can then just toss their stuff in a bin, and the "filing" is done.

Third. I went shopping, inspired by all those threads on "awesome game kits". A ballot-box for site fees. A little stand for rulebooks. A lot of danish butter cookies, in tins (I repainted the tins as my new token bowls, and will be eating the cookies for weeks to come). Setup and cleanup are way, way faster as a result, and I don't need to resort everything once it gets home.

All in all? We had an even better game. You helped.

Thought you might like to know.
