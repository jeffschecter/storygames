    \n One thing that jumped out at me was losing your turn if you would take a third condition. Not getting to play doesn't feel like a good penalty for rolling poorly.

    An alternative might be to foist your third condition on to a teammate instead. That's bound to make the players interact more "Oh great, now I'm trapped in an elevator because you blew your hacking roll!" Blame becomes contagious. Now everyone's happy!

    \n Thanks folks. I didn't playtest it yet, but I'm planning to this week, I'll tell you how it went. Consider this a gross beta version. I just wanted to get an old idea out of my head and lay out something. I'm really not sure how it'll play out.
    \n
    \nAbout the condition : yeah, skipping a turn was the only deterrant I could think of but it's not that good an idea. Also, yes, conditions don't add dice. I'd like conditions to be a big thing, and taking a condition means taking one for the team : it's a danger that's for you and you only. As soon as you got one you start making rough choices.
