\n A new month, a new thread.

I present to you the new Crowdfunding Collection #45, which includes every tabletop project started between 26.Jun till 10.Jul.

Thanks to a cold I caught the collection got delayed a week, yet you will find an abundance of interesting projects.
There are 12 Role-Playing Game core books included. I personally backed into the spy game Top Secret, the action of Era: Hitman and the rebellious, Misspent Youth. Yet there are aplenty of other cool games. Whether the newest game by Mark Rein-Hagen Revolutionaries, the cool looking Ghost Ops or the modern day cinematic Graphic RPG and more.
I did got a bit inventive with the categories this time round. Thus I would like to point out that "historical" is more a loose term for games set in the past and that the story game section has some games more focused on storytelling and collaborative fiction creation than the usual.
Yet not only are there cool new RPGs but the other projects are worth a look or two too. Like the Fidget dice and other cool dice design. Like the possibility to support ConTessa (which I did) and get a cool plushy mascot. Like \u201eNight Forest\u201c that tries to enable larping in the Forest without Boffers. There\u2019s a whole bunch of interesting various games like a \u201escratch\u201c-character sheet for DCC or an audio play and more.
Plus, even though I count about 50 project, I noticed three \u201enormal\u201c games that looks like they are worth a mention. Though, take a look for yourself,..

    \n Thank you for all the kind words

    I present to you the new Crowdfunding Collection Nr. 46, which includes every tabletop project started between 07.10 till 07.17.

    This project does feature two interesting projects from Germany, and one which is actually more of a cooperation. The biggest one, available in English, is Cthulhu Wars 3, an immensely popular board game that I feel still deserves a mention. You\u2019ll find as well a pretty impressive and beautifully illustrated collection of token cards by Johannes Voss, that might be of good use for a tabletop game.
    If your interested in tabletop RPGs in foreign languages, take a look at Neciamus. Its developed by games in Aachen/Aix la Chapelle and offers a fantasy setting, placed during the times of reformation, as well as a fresh system.
    Among the \u201eEnglish\u201c projects I personally favor the \u201eparsley games\u201c. Which looks like an intriguing mix of boardgame, \u201ecomputer\u201c text adventure and RPG. Though Firelight comes in in as a close second and The Last Remnants appears to be quite fascinating.
    You\u2019ll find quite a bunch of other, fascinating project. Thus have a look for yourself,\u2026

    \n I present to you the new Crowdfunding Collection Nr. 47, which includes every tabletop project started between 07.17 till 07.24.

    Following the FeenCon convention, which I attended and where I GM\u2019ed a bunch of V5 games, I present to you the brief collection of current crowdfunding project.
    One of its many highlights is \u201ePaladin \u2013 Warriors of Charlemange\u201c. A fresh tabletop rpg by Nocturnal Media, which will use the system provide by Pendragon for its fresh setting. Another highlight, though quite a contrast, is Xas Irkalla. A new Horror RPG in which the player characters will be transported to an alien and hostile world made from the dreams of dead psychics. Compared to Xas the world described in the Spire RPG appears somewhat brighter. Though not for the dark elves who have to fight the system of oppression in this cyberpunk setting.
    There are many more interesting projects,\u2026 though have a look for yourself:
