    Honestly, I'll totally swing either way. The definition of an "S-G Game" is really vague and shifty- And I made up the definition there! :-)

    I totally don't want it to be, like, "Story Games are those games we like to play or talk about, as opposed to all the other fuddy-duddy games". Or basically equate "Story Games" to "Games We Like".

    Ex: Burning Wheel is on the Wiki?? Burning Empires I can totally see there, as it is really focused on the story generation process and all that... but Burning Wheel, while awesome, doesn't do it NEARLY as much. I'm kinda iffy on its addition to that page as anything other than the whole Forge League of Superfriends angle, and will think about perhaps taking that one down (and a few others) unless I see some decent justification.

    Meanwhile, just about ALL of the nWoD stuff has premise built into the character archetypes and the like, so if someone came up to me and was, like, "What about the new Vampire? I think that's a so-called 'Story Game' ", I'd say, "Awesome, you're totally right! Go throw up a few pages about it on the Wiki and tell us why!"

    So for Dead Inside, if you're up to it I'd love to see a page up for that. If you're not up for it, I'll probably throw one up there anyway. :-)

    As for you other games, don't think too hard, but if you think they fit somehow, even a little, go for it. If you don't think they fit, but you have something to say about Good Roleplaying in General, or about Good Tips to Use to Make Cooler Stories, then there are totally sections for that too.

    As I was editing and re-editing the top page (I noticed a few people re-arranged some stuff, so I re-re-arranged it further), I realized that I'm much more of a fan on making the wiki about How To Make Your Favorite Game more like a "Story Gaming Experience" (the first half of that Story Games definition), rather than glorifying a bunch of particular games that people cordoned off into a "Story Games Grouping/Pool". So I hope that gives you a little insight into that, and how I don't want it to be like this elitist group of narrowly and confoundly chosen games that We Happen to Like.
