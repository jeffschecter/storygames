\n I posted a thread about solo play, which few people seem to be really interested in. This is an alternate take on that concept.

I see Story Games as providing a structure and process that can be used to create a story in a way that is an alternative to traditional fiction writing. (I have a degree in Creative Writing.)

So a Story Game not as game per se, but as a writing process that culminates in a work that is essentially a collaboration between the game creator and the player/author.

    \n First - it's not solo play in the traditional sense, where one character represents the player; it's one person creating and playing a number of characters. (But I don't see how Poison'd or 3:16 could possibly lead to Mary-Sue-type characters anyway!)

    And there are really two audiences here: a general audience for a true stand-alone work of fiction this way - and those who are familiar with the underlying game (who would be able to appreciate some of the structural aspects in ways a general audience wouldn't).
