\n Those of you who know me know that I am always digging up hoards. I found a new one, and man, is it fabulous! It didn't come cheap - I paid the price in hirelings fallen before a screeching, clattering mob of undead horrors, but in the end look at all the cool stuff I brought home! Best Hoard Ever.

Anyway, I want to appraise it, but this time, I'd like you to whisper me your estimate, in general gold pieces, of its worth. Once I get a bunch of estimates, I'll make the range and individual appraisals public and announce who I'm trading my hoard to for cold, hard cash! As far as I know, none of this junk is magical - even the weird stuff.

Here's what I got:\n


COINAGE

612 silver coins.
200 gold coins.

JEWELRY

Two twisted neck rings and four matching arm rings, a giant gold buckle and a set of antler combs.
Several sets of matching bronze bangles, anklets, and hair ornaments.
Two silver arm bands.
A pair of gold torques.
An elaborate bronze headdress.
A leather bag containing a pile of gold rings and pins.
Seven bracelets and seven rings with inlays of rock crystal.
Two gold rings and a gold cloak pin.
Six gold hair rings.
40 wire arm spirals tied in a bundle.
A finger ring containing a gemstone bearing the figure of a woman.

TOOLS OF WAR

A gold-and-silver boot knife.
A set of fearsome-looking barbed spears, oiled and wrapped in leather.
A matched set, decorated richly in gold and garnet: A magnificent sword with a cloisonn\xe9 pommel, in its sheath; its harness and belt, fitted with a suite of solid gold mounts; and two shoulder clasps, with lugs and clasps for attaching a stiff leather cuirass.

KITCHEN GOODS

A nested set of ten silver bowls.
Two high seats, wooden, one with gold leaf and clusters of garnet and amethyst and the other with gold gilt and inlay.
A pair of drinking horns made from the horns of a long-extinct wild ox.
A tiny concave-sided bronze cauldron
A fluted silver dish with the relief image of a curious map, a small silver bowl and a very large round silver platter with elaborate ornamentation.
A bronze bowl with drop handles, decorated with figures of the old gods.
A drinking vessel of thin beaten bronze, decorated with a frieze depicting naked warriors carrying swords and shields in combat with a sea monster.

ODDS AND ENDS

A pouch full of beautifully carved cannel-coal jacket buttons.
A pair of beautifully worked harness ornaments from a horse\u2019s bridle, silver filigreed with gold.
An ornamental bowsprit and mast finial, both encased in gold leaf.
A sunstone in a drawstring bag.
A strange rectangular block of soapstone with life-sized human faces carved on each of the four long sides, with a bronze ring mount on the top.
A wand with a small mount depicting a wolf.
A staff with gold facing at the top.
A beaver-skin satchel containing a bronze-antlered human figurine made of wood.
\nLet me know what you think! And thanks.
