    \n When I used to run Wraith back in the day, I only ran it one-on-one. Even with the required WoD metaplot and Splats, the game as written just didn't give Wraiths a good enough reason to give much of a crap about hanging out in a tactical squad or about the Hierarchy.

    For my money, Wraith is all about the whats and whys of the Fetters. If you want to have a group of Wraiths, that's where you glue them together.

    One of my back burner ideas is based on this concept and has it's genesis in thinking about Wraith in light of these "Awesome-ofy X" threads.

    It's shame Wraith doesn't get more love. It was the most human and real game set in the WoD.

    \n A few items, for starters:

    Rewrite Attributes and Abilities to make sense for Wraiths

    Start by consolidating Attributes - say, for example, Brutish would represent the combination of Strength and Manipulation, Humour might represent the combo Wits + Charisma, and so on. If you're not with me on those examples it's because you don't share my headspace. The idea is to consolidate and rewrite Attributes that are useless left overs from oWoD dogma into Attributes that may have an actual bearing on a Wraith story.

    Secondly, if we're going with a Task based resolution system - which wouldn't bother me too much - start by dumping all Abilities and classifications and tell players to define Abilities as they wish, to fit with what their idea of their ghostly presence is. Ideally, dump Arcanoi while you're at it and make the ability to do ghostie things part of your Abilities. Instead of the "I have this-and-this Arcana (?) so I can inhabit this computer, so I roll Intelligence + Computers" crap, have an ability "Inhabit electronics".

    Focus

    You don't need all of Nature, Demeanor, Life, Death, Regret, Backgrounds, Passions and Fetters. You need a couple of things you can focus on. Passions and Fetters often overlap considerably - consolidate them or separate them entirely, and make the separation mean something tangible. Nature and Demeanor is a feature I really like about the ST system - the ones you pick and their interrelation say a lot about a character. "Regret" should factor into that equation, and not be a separate entity. Yeah, basically get rid of stuff, and pour the energy into Passions and Fetters instead.

    Flags

    Dump pre-scripted plots. Realize that everything on the (consolidated, leaner, tighter) character sheet is now a flag. The story is about those flags, and the only real adversarial power comes from the Shadow. A GM (not an ST) sets starting parameters based on the flags on the characters' sheets and then lets go - s/he plays any secondary parts that happen to arise through the story, but provides no further push or guidance other than perhaps occasional scene framing and pacing. Actually, on second thought, it'd be awesome to have the Shadow compete for scene framing rights using Angst points.

    Posted By: AgentFreshIt's shame Wraith doesn't get more love. It was the most human and real game set in the WoD.
    \nAbsolutely. I didn't really get to play Wraith much, and honestly, at the time, I didn't really know what to do with it. But the best session that I ran with it involved just one wraith. It's the Christmas Eve after she has died, and she's hanging around her house, watching everyone. The focus of the story: her son is going to kill himself tonight by throwing himself onto the railroad tracks. So she is trying to stop him, but her Shadow keeps getting in the way....

    On the one hand, it was such a small thing. But, at that moment, it was everything.

    She succeeded, by the way. And, as her son turned away from the oncoming train to walk home, he whispered, "I love you, Mom."

    Powerful stuff. And this was before my mom died.

    Every player at the table has a Shadowguide except one: the GM.
