\n I recently played Protocol for my podcast.

For those who are unfamiliar Protocol is a card based story game with a feel reminiscent of Fiasco. The basic Protocol system involves drawing cards to determine character traits, motivations, and scene inspiration. However, the core Protocol book arrives with over twenty different variations on core rules the tweak the game to accomodate different genres and settings. You can also purchase the different Protocol variants individually if you just want to dip your toe into the system.

The feel of Protocol is decidedly more dramatic than Fiasco. Protocol also provides players with more inspiration to draw on for their individual scenes. I have found that with less confident role players the open-ended Fiasco can be intimidating. The game stars out with a familiar assigning and defining of relationships. However Protocol aslo provides individual scene setting suggestions. For instance the game we played was a variation called "Plague," it was about the entier country being turned into a quarantine zone- the first scene we drew was "sending out a radio signal."

Protocol really shines in the "suit system." When drawing for scenes each suit is tied to one of five unique scene types:

1. Interludes: These are the standard two person scenes you might be familiar with from Fiasco. They are generally character driven.

2. Ensambles: These involve the full cast and are generally focused more on plot.

3. Interrogations: These are pretty interesting. They can either be one or more characters asking another character questions, or you can take a reality show "confessional" approach and ask the character questions sperate from your larger plot.

4. Vignettes: are descriptions of environment and scenery. There is little to no character interaction in these scenes. The create a sense of space and mood. Think Terrence Mallick style shots of nature.

5. There is an optional rule to play with jokers. If you do the joker calls for a character monologue

Overall Protocol is a great, quick play no prep game. You can listen to our playthrough right here.
