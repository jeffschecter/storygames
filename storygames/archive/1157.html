This is a mechanics I keep reinventing periodically, and always think I'll have a good use for...yet anything I've put it in has gone moldering. Maybe it'll be useful to someone else. It's just a resolution rolling method.

The player rolls a size of die depending on attributes, etc. d4, d6, d8, d10 or d12. The GM will probably do the same for NPCs, points he spends, whatever. Each person finds their success level below:

1-3 -- Banality
4-6 -- Solidness
7-9 -- Coolness
10-12 -- Awesomeness

You'll note that this puts a definite cap on how much an unskilled character can achieve, and yet increasing skill is also largely a matter of being more reliable, and even then success isn't automatic.

Nominally then, whoever has the higher level of success wins the conflict or succeeds at his action. You could easily have players rolling more than one die, and maybe consider just the best result then. You could also attach specific outcomes to each level of success, nevermind overall winner or loser of the roll.

I'm still looking for a home for this thing, but maybe you'll have one first.

    OK, some further thoughts on this.

    Rather than a "task resolution" approach I'm going to take a "conflict resolution" approach that assumes there are always dice being rolled against each other. (If the environment can have attributes in FATE, it can have dice in this system.) So the numbers are margins of victory.

    A d8 means you are average at whatever it is you are bringing to the conflict. A d6 is below average/wounded/impaired, a d4 incompetent/badly wounded/badly impaired, d10 above average, d12 some kind of outstanding hero or expert. Possibly a d2 (binary dice, 1 for odds, 2 for evens) could be introduced for when you're below average and badly impaired or something.

    So, margins of victory:

    0 - you didn't achieve your goal, but you are still well set up for another try.
    1-2 - you made some progress towards your goal but you need a good victory next time to achieve it.
    3-4 - you made good progress towards your goal and even a low victory next time will get it.
    5-6 - you achieved your goal in a "meh" kind of way.
    7-8 - you achieved your goal impressively.
    9-10 - you achieved your goal impressively and gained some kind of minor or temporary benefit (e.g. a larger die for your next roll).
    11 - you achieved your goal stunningly and gained some kind of major or permanent benefit. Some kind of fortunate coincidence may have been involved.

    Margins of defeat:
    -1-2 - you not only didn't achieve your goal, you now have a new minor complication between you and your goal; some kind of environmental change which could be an unfortunate coincidence, etc.
    -3-4 - you have a significant new complication between you and your goal.
    -5-6 - you definitely will not achieve that goal, you can't try again; circumstances have rendered it impossible.
    -7-8 - you have some minor or temporary drawback (e.g. a smaller die for your next roll).
    -9-10 - you have some major or permanent drawback.
    -11 - dude, you are so screwed. You're going to need at least one more scene to get out of this one.

    This ties all the outcomes to narrative directly, which I like.
