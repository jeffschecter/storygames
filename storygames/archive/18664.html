What is ExiStanc3 exactly ?
A new approach to tabletop role playing games and an exciting project, ExiStanc3 covers many aspects. Here they are :
\u2022\tA new way to write campaigns : a storyboard based on a calendar (time, not only size, will now matters)
\u2022\tOur own campaign set in a heroic fantasy world : Avalanche (not one, not two, not three \u2026 but five stories occurring at the same time. And all for the same price)
\u2022\tOur system : Shadow and Light (it\u2019s a game, you\u2019ll need some rules at some point, as you could expect)

What is tabletop role playing game ?
Tabletop role playing games are the real thing !! Here\u2019s how they work :
\u2022\tYou play face to face with, ideally, a bunch of your of friends (Around a table ? People still do that ? Yes and you can even drink alcohol if you\u2019re of aged)
\u2022\tOne player is the \u201cgame master\u201d and, more or less, animates the evening (not the drunken uncle type of animation)
\u2022\tThe other players have characters, usually heroes, who interact with the \u201cworld\u201d (sometime characters are kinky, and this is then another type of \u201crole playing\u201d game)

What is Avalanche ?
Avalanche is our first campaign that is specifically designed as a storyboard :
\u2022\tAvalanche, we believe, is a really gorgeous example of the vision we have for a new kind of product for the tabletop role playing games (From a scale of 1 to 10, how much gorgeous ? 11, of course !!)
\u2022\tIt describes a world, and five different major stories occurring over a twelve weeks calendar (Everything is in a unique product with over 50 hours of gameplay)
\u2022\tIt might be read, almost, as a novel or a comic book (Never heard of tabletop role playing game but you\u2019re a fan of epic fantasy ? You might want to stick around \u2026)
\u2022\tIt uses over 450 illustrations, all of them in \u201ccontext\u201d (just to make sure it\u2019s material you would use during your games)
\u2022\tIt proposes you mature stories with complex characters (No rescuing the princess from the troll for the evil mage who will betray us at the end because he wants to control the realm but we will face him and defeat him, thus saving the day ?)

What\u2019s unique about Avalanche ?
The setting of Avalanche, a somehow classic heroic fantasy world, is quite common. What is truly unique about Avalanche :
\u2022\tIt only provides you with the big picture, it\u2019s up to you to create the details (You\u2019ll have to put some serious meat on those \u201cbones\u201d)
\u2022\tIt uses a non-linear approach to describing the story, using a calendar (It turns out to be the newspaper of the world)

\u2022\tIt proposes many stories occurring at the same time (adventure, horror, mysteries, political intrigues, romance \u2026 choose your flavor)

S&L is our system, developed specifically for Avalanche.
\u2022\tAs easy to learn as a boardgame and design as such (you know, rules that stand in a 32 pages booklet)

\u2022\tCan be used with the new comers or the experts, at the same table (This should help you entertain everyone)
\u2022\tShadow and Light can be used with other campaigns (wow !! this is so unique!!)

    \u2022\tThey are just perfect to be put into a database (Someone should develop a tool, or a website around that idea \u2026)

    \n@J_Walton :

    - who are the characters?
    They are "people" living in the world of Avalanche, at the time of the described events. I don't know the specifics. Avalanche is a big place, they are lots of choices. I'll give an example : if you were to play a role playing game with a group, in the world of George RR Martin's a game of throne, at the time of the events of the first book, who would be the characters ? If the group decide to be GreyJoy, Lannisters or crows at the wall, it's going to give you very different games. But there are two things for certain : the players need to make choices (they won't be able to play every parts of the book) and, at some points, the events in the world are going to influence (or hit) them.
    - what do they want to achieve?
    Then again, I don't know the specifics. That said, in Shadow and Light, we have integrated the keys aspect (we call it "motivation") as a way for characters to progress. When players create their characters, they come to choose a starting location, some "circles" (to use burning wheel's word) and some motivations. After that you've got a pretty idea of the game (at least, how it starts).
    Now, remember that Avalanche is meant to be completed by the group/DM and you should start it at the character creation. In Avalanche, there are lots of room for all kind of adventures.
    That said, I would strongly recommend the use of mechanics such as keys with Avalanche to optimize the experience.
    - how do they try to do that?
    See the above. But then again, I would recommend the use of a system that supports well social conflicts, as there is a lot of political tension in Avalanche, as a way to optimize the experience.
    - what is the GM supposed to do?
    A classic role I would say. Except, he's also in charge of managing the calendar. For instance, characters enter a town and ask what had happened lately : the answer is in the calendar. And yes, the "world" does feel like a living force.
    - what does the experience of play feel like?
    From a "scenario" point of view, I would really like it to feel like an home brew campaign, but with a lot of "coherence" in the big picture. As for the system, it would feel like a simpler version of TSOY.
    - what other games inspired parts of this game? (to have some points of comparison)
    TSOY, burning wheel, for the system ... For the campaign, although I have never put my hand on it, I believe that Pendragon could have a similar feel : a strong context where you decide what you play and time is very present with major events occuring in the world.
