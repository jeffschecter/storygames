\n So! A question.

"The Shadow of Yesterday" sounds like it could have been a fitting title (although maybe a little too romantic-sounding) for "Apocalypse World". Both are story games with a strong connection to the "traditional" gaming many of us have known and loved.

Imagine you and your group playing a game of Apocalypse World. Now imagine that you rewound time and started the very same game again, but this time using the rules for The Shadow of Yesterday/Solar System. Now you have to parallel universes where you have the same players playing the same characters, but using these two rather different systems.

How do the two games look different? How does game A feel different from game B? Who dies and who lives? What different choices are the players confronted with?

    \n Actually, TSoY and Apocalypse World are not that different - they have the same model of duty distribution between players, the conflict resolution model is very similar, the role of game mechanization and even extensible mechanics is the same and so forth. It'd be the work of a day to convert the setting materials from one to the other, and I'd hazard that GMing either with the other's methodology wouldn't be a problem. Obviously the actual playthrough would be a bit different, but that'd be true for two runs of either game as well.

    \nThis is why I brought it up, in fact. Apocalypse World also encourages character growth and change (through new moves and especially the appeal of taking new playbooks) that's similar to TSoY's character arcs and acquisition of new Secrets.

    In addition, from what I've heard, the whole Transcendence mechanic sounds like it's present in AW, and at about the same game point (5-6 sessions for a quickly advancing character). The characters fight against a world full of trouble, but as the game progresses they quickly develop into forces of protagonism, and eventually "outgrow" the setting. In TSoY, it means changing the world. In AW, changing the world means making people you care about safe ("allies") and then "retiring to safety". I see a lot of parallels.

    And I have a suspicion that the prep methods for TSoY and AW are easily interchangeable--either game may work quite well with the other's GM style.
