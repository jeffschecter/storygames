\n Happy October!

In reverence of this most haunted of months I just launched a Kickstarter campaign for the revised and expanded second edition of Don't Walk in Winter Wood. DWiWW is a storytelling game of folkloric fear, designed to emulate the spooky atmosphere of campfire stories. It includes contributions from a few SG regulars.

If you have any questions about the game or the project in general, I'd be happy to answer them.

    \n Thanks, Todd!

    @Matteo: Sure!

    It's structured like a traditional rpg. We've got the Watcher (Gamemaster) and the Walkers (Players). The Watcher describes the environment and the situations that arise and the Walkers describe the actions of their individual characters. The twist is that all of the narration takes place in the past tense and third person. So, instead of "I walk into the abandoned cottage," I would say "Clint walked into the abandoned cottage." It usually takes people 10-20 minutes to get used to this, but once it clicks, it's very effective.

    As the walkers progress toward their goal (which inevitably leads them through many chills and thrills in Winter Wood), they accumulate Cold Tokens. Cold represents anything from sanity loss to physical wounds. Whenever there's a branching fork in the story, the Watcher asks a yes or no question of the Walker. "Was Matteo able to escape the carnivorous deer?" The Walker then rolls a d6. If the result is higher than the number of Cold Tokens you have, you answer "Yes" to the question. If not, you must answer "No." The neat thing about this mechanic is that you never actually mention the rules during the game. The result is very immersive.

    That's it in a nut shell.

    Here's a review of the older version of the game. His description is still pretty accurate, though in the second edition we really took his criticisms to heart and (I hope) fixed them: http://www.revolutionsf.com/article.php?id=3087
