    \n I recommend Contenders. You can read it in an hour, it drives great stories, there's no GM and you can play it as a campaign or a one-shot. It delivers interesting, dramatic story lines. Plus, the rules are very digestible.

    There's a scene choosing mechanic, which works well (especially for cross-over gamers). On your turn, you get to choose which type of scene you are setting, like a "connection scene" which builds up relationships for your character, or a "work scene" which has your character involved in nefarious shenanigans. Each turn you try to build up resources (like Hope and Pain), in order to bring your character closer to his goal. So, essentially, you are trying to get points for your character and this is done by framing particular kinds of scenes.

    The big boon for this mechanic is that anyone can do well with it. A lot of pressure is removed from scene framing just because there are a limited number of mechanical choices. E.g., My character's girlfriend is getting beaten up by the mob for her gambling debt. I need to get her some cash. I can either a) use a Connection scene to give her the cash I have, or b) use a work scene to build up more cash for her. Etc. The mechanical decision drives the scene framing. After that, the results become organic and tense\u2014loaded with consequence.

    Give it a look.

    \n It may be worthwhile to try to refine what you're after; do you want something prep-less by design or something that can be run prep-less if you want? I would guess the former, as I find the latter is something that happens when you become supremely comfortable with a system that usually requires prep. Do you want a game where chargen happens ahead of play, or one with little or no chargen required before you start? How Indie do you want to go? Do you want Indie stylings and rules wrapped around a more familiar, objective-based story or something Indie to the core?

    Posted By: 14thWarriorI'm going to throw in a recommendation forInspectres.
    \nI gotta agree with InSpectres, or its high-octane cousin octaNe, Really good fun, really easy to play.

    Also really good would be either Lady Blackbird or Danger Patrol from John Harper. Both take a little bit of time for dyed-in-the-wool old-school gamers (from my experience), but once everyone has the hang of it, they are EXCEPTIONAL. And FREE. Both are easily adaptable to a variety of different settings, and there's plenty of AP reports floating around on this forum that you can steal ideas from.
    Lady Blackbird, overall though, would be the easiest, since all you really need to do is print out the rules, and some extra copies of the characters for the players. Everything is self-contained. With Danger Patrol, there's a lot of extra bits you need to print and cut-out, and you have to get a few extra pieces ready (various dice, counters, markers, etc.) But when it comes to getting down and playing, Danger Patrol meshes better I think with some of the old-school mentality. Again, from my experience, at least.

    \n I will recommend three games, the last one being shameless self-promotion.

    * Lady Blackbird, already mentioned above. Excellent game, and really low prep. The GM needs 5-10 minutes head start to look through the papers before you start playing. I wrote some GM:ing advice for the game on my blog: http://wilper.wordpress.com/2010/04/21/playing-lady-blackbird-by-john-harper/. Nothing there that is not already in Johns game, just other words. Maybe you will feel safer diving into the game after reading them. Good choice for first indie-game anyway.

    * Universalis. http://ramshead.indie-rpgs.com/ Neat game that can emulate any genre and uses a bidding mechanism to determine who has narrative privileges. An old indie game, if there is any such thing. Great game but in my experience the first game you play always crashes and burns, since all the players will be testing the boundaries. Subsequent games will be much better. Perhaps something for when you have played a few sessions of other games.

    * While the World Ends. http://www.lulu.com/product/file-download/while-the-world-ends/6008357 It really isn't prep free, but the prep is part of the game and you do it together before your story starts. A game where you all control a character each, and you take turns controlling the NPCs. A bargain at $4, and you can check out this enthusiastic actual play/review, if you want more info before spending any of that hard earned cash.
