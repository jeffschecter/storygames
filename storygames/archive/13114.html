\n * Can you voluntarily drop out of a conflict without taking damage or exhaustion?

Example, we've got a 3-player conflict going on. The demon-god is whispering madness to the captain of the enemy light brigrade, and the strangler queen summons her guards to interfere. After the second round, the captain has responded to the demon's move and has the advantage, and now the queen has an opportunity to make a move. She's happy to just quit, though, as she now feels like things are in hand. The rules make it sound like you can't - so she tries to strangle the demon, and gets some unbelievably bad rolls so now the demon has the advantage and she's out. Bummer. She wished she could have just left well enough alone.

* Can you bring in a particular strength partway through a conflict?

We've heard that Vincent said yes, but don't know where that's written down.

* Even if you're not there, you can still roll conflict dice, right?

The captain and his soldiers attack a fortress on the border of the queen's territory. The queen's particular strength is *not* far reaching, but she wants to roll to defend her fort. I say "You can roll, but you can't use your particular strength." Another player says, "That doesn't seem right. I mean, she's not there." Who's right?

* Where can I find that cool flowchart that Johnzo had?

I played with Johnzo once and he had a printout of a flowchart for conflict that was quite helpful.

* Is there an errata or FAQ or place I can go for more details and answers?

Googling "In a Wicked Age" hasn't been too helpful for me.

    \n Hi Jamie,

    This is how I'd handle those things if they came up in a game. You may get better answers from someone else!

    Dropping out - Once you commit to a conflict, you're in it. You can, however, negotiate before the last round of dice. So if you're in a good position, it seems to make sense you could negotiate a graceful exit to the conflict.

    Particular strength - You can totally bring a strength in after the conflict starts. I've played this way many times, mostly because someone forgot about their particular strength.

    Conflicts far away - I wouldn't let someone join a conflict they weren't physically present for unless they had a "far-reaching" particular strength. The rules say something about rolling dice when someone takes a "concrete action" and another character tries to interfere.

    \n Ry, thanks for that flowchart. I realized how awesome it is on the day I tried to teach IAWA without it...

    Brian's answers sound pretty good to me.

    Cy, I don't believe you can pass your action -- if by "pass" you mean skip your action in one round but roll it in a subsequent round. If you're in the conflict, you have to roll.

    If you want to leave the conflict entirely, you can negotiate your exit with the other players in the conflict. If they won't let you leave the conflict, then you have something to roll for, don't you?
