So, What New Game is Next on your Plate to Play/Run?

Andy
February 2006 edited February 2006 in Story Games
Just wondering what folks are eagerly planning on running or playing, an idea of when you're planning it, and any extra information about why you want to play that game or what you're doing with it.

I mentioned "New Game" in the topic, I don't so much mean New Releases as a game that you haven't played before, no matter how long it's been out.

Here's mine:

Weapons of the Gods: Been waiting eagerly for this one to come out for a while: Now that it has, I find myself without time to actually carefully go through it enough to run it. However, it looks like a game might come together (with me as a player, perhaps) in the next month or so. I'm eagerly looking forward to playing this game with someone else at the helm, so that I can get eased into the rules.

With Great Power: I can't believe that I haven't played this game since the preview edition... since the "real edition" came out, I haven't found an opening for it. However, I recently determined to sit down and go through it, learn the new rules and changes, and kick off a small campaign within two months with most likely my regular Thursday gaming group. Likely theme will be X-Men (X-Men analogue, that is): Something "sorta realistic" that gets everyone pushed together at the beginning.

SHOCK: Social Science Fiction: This is coming out next week with my Thursday group (we were going to do it last week, but I kinda got ill so we ended up playing Settlers instead); Considering that all the folks in my group are big SF whores, and the girl's house we game at, her husband is like one of the big names of the indie SF movement and goes to that "GenCon equivalent for SF" convention yearly (even overseas), everyone's intruiged by this game. The responsibility of everyone to be each others' GM is new for this group, but I think we can swing it from the start, as long as we stay focused. I'll probably run it for two-three sessions, and keep it down to about two Shocks and Issues so that it doesn't get too out of control to begin with.

Stranger Things: I played this twice with my bi-weekly experimental gaming group and it went over really well; now it's time to run it again with the latest rule mods as more than a one-shot, maybe a 3-4 game mini-campaign starting in the next 1-3 months. I just have to ask John what the Dream Sheet is all about before I run it next...

-Andy
