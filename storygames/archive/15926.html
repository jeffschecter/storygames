\nAbove is the link to my playtest documents. I'm looking for commentary, critiques and suggestions for improving my game. It straddles OSR and games typically discussed here, primarily since I referenced different versions of Runequest for combat (and tried combining them) and Trollbabe for a lot of other stuff.
\n
\nI chose Trollbabe because I thought one on one was a great bridge for neophyte GMs like myself, and the game asks for more prep than TB, but significantly less than writing out a whole module.

    \n I've skimmed this, and it looks interesting. It's also an odd mix of tone and tools. For instance, it seems to be a "magic is evil" type setting, where, nonetheless, most characters have a reasonable skill at magic. This made more sense in Trollbabe's setting, since Trollbabes were by nature special. Maybe most magic is actually ancient technology? It also seems to combine a setting that's going to be heavily about combat with extremely lethal combat. Maybe a more cinematic combat system would make more sense? Also, all those neat GM tools for creating situation aren't going to be very useful if the characters all die right away.

    Have you played this yet? If so, how did it work in practice?

    \n Hi, Peter!

    The combat engine is probably the best part of the initial draw of the game, at least in that it was all the game had when it was a 1 page RPG. I've playtested it here:
    http://redbox.wikidot.com/forum/t-284340/doomquest-first-run-playtest
    http://muleabides.wordpress.com/2011/03/26/the-doom-quest-of-nightfang/

    The newer version of the game has some new untried wrinkles. Abstract range, new inventory system, etc..

    You have some good points, if I'm reading them correctly: Trollbabe is the way it is to protect the character and get some epic-storyness going on. RQ combat is even more visceral and lethal than D&D. Are the two a bad fit? I'm not sure.

    I'm also hoping that the new GM will take to this system as a step towards the improv of Trollbabe. I wanted to shift the game slightly away from that, because, well, that kind of improv seems ridiculously hard for me.

    The scenario creation is the only meat I have on the game so far. I don't plan (or want) to offer monsters, I'd rather let DMs make up their own. I mean, make 3 sides who want the stakes, create some lairs made of keywords, throw some other keywords down as well.... I'm unsure how much prep this really entails. I have to see if I can do this with players breathing down my neck.

    Combat is lethal, but, as a RQ player, I learned (through dying) things like: Don't get into melee. This means talking, missiles, running, ambushing, etc. In this way, I think I can say the middle stat guy who has high social could shine, the high sorcery guy could shine as he could scare off high melee guys, who are very vulnerable.

    Thank you again for reading.

    Here's a cover. I'm working on doing an alternate cover and switching them off as front/back if I ever do PoD.
