    \n We've done it a few times, but the amount of free time available to different players varies about as much as their interest in writing something and their comfort level with their own prose.

    It'd be great fun if everyone could or would participate in it to the same degree, but since that's never going to happen (and since nagging the non-writing players to contribute is such a pain in the ass for everyone involved), for the most part we just don't bother with it. Some players and/or GMs are willing to engage in it via forum posts or e-mail exchanges, but there's an understanding that it's for their own fun only: there's no mechanical reward for doing it, they try to steer clear of doing anything especially significant through it, and any significant events which do arise from bluebooking-type extracurriculars must be summarized and relayed to the non-bluebooking players.

    \n In Sign in Stranger, the characters are human colonists inhabiting an alien world. At the end of each session, you write a few lines in character in your "personal log". Then you start off the next session by reading off everyone's latest entries. To help make the writing relevant to the game play, you can get card for incorporating something somebody wrote into an investigation or conflict. And also you get some insight into what the characters are thinking about one another. The Durham Three, when they played, got a lot of mileage out of this kind of bluebooking.
