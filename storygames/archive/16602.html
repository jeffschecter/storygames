    If there's 2 of us
    Ouch, tricky. Would you like to play a board game instead? No?
    * Breaking the Ice
    * Perfect

    If there's 3 of us
    Let's play!
    * While the World Ends in GM:ed mode
    * Polaris with the one moon hack.

    If there's 4 of us
    Ah, a good number, how about these games?
    * While the World Ends in GM:less mode
    * The Phenomenon / Polaris
    * Montsegur 1244
    * Panty Explosion Perfect

    If there's 5 of us
    A solid group, look at all these games!
    * The Daughters of Verona
    * A Taste for Murder
    * Montsegur 1244
    * Zombie Cinema
    * Witch Quest
    * Love in the Time of Seid
    * Panty Explosion Perfect

    If there's 6 of us
    You brought four friends? No problem, I have games we can play.
    * Montsegur 1244
    * Zombie Cinema
    * Panty Explosion Perfect

    If there's 7 of us
    Seven? How about we split into two groups and play separately? No?
    * Witch Quest

    If there's 8 of us
    * Witch Quest, but one of the witches must have twin cats, and they'll share a character sheet. It'll be fun, trust me.

    If there's 9 of us
    * Witch Quest

    If there's 10+ of us
    I propose we play Werewolf instead, or find some mini LARP or something, sorry.
    \nAnd those games are the ones in my gaming bag that I take to cons. Possibly accompanied by the season's new hotness.

    \n Mine change all the time based on what I'm excited about. I don't really "throw down" as a facilitator too often. If I know I'm going to be doing some gaming I refresh myself on the rules and materials I'll need for that game and maybe bring one or two others that I also feel ready to run; I like to travel as light as possible most of the time. I do have materials prepped and organized for something north of 40 games.

    \n I don't run often enough or spend enough time with RPG gamers to have need for a spur of the moment rpg. (I play live combat larp games more often, but when I'm hanging with that crowd, it's usually at an event already.) But sometimes I can get family and friends to do board/card games on the spur of the moment.

    My go to for that is Bang. It's a wild west card game that's like Werewolf/Mafia, but more mechanical/strategic rather than all about convincing folks to lynch someone other than you, but it still has a strong social bluffing aspect. It's a small box (double deck size), so it's super portable. And it's a super tight and fun game for 4-7 players. It's very unusual in that the game gets better and better the more players you have, which I find is rarely the case for card/board games, which usually have a peak size in the middle and then start to creak at the edges when you add more. Several times a year I play with people who don't regularly play games, and they all love it. All. Really. (I play just the base set and usually take out the Dynamite card. The expansions don't really add anything significant to what is a super tight game design.)

    I highly recommend it for when you have 4-7 people and about 90 minutes to play. And it goes well with beer.

    \nNot being a threadjacking troll, just trying to provide some contrasting experience.
    \n
    \nI find that I need to have my mind prepped at least a day before I run a session of play. It is not so much that I need to have all the rules memorized: I usually like games with low-to-medium rules complexity. It is just that every game has its currency and its intrinsic rewards and agendas. Unless I visualize how play around the table might unfold, and how currencies and rewards and agendas might interact, I don't feel comfortable running the game.
    \n
    \nUsually I have 2 to 3 games prepped for a convention. And they are contrasting. So ahead of time I know that I will be transitioning from bringing together a Fiasco team, gettin' all up in players' faces with Sorcerer bangs, and colour spraying with a FATE game. I really can't jump into any of those experiences without having readied myself beforehand.
