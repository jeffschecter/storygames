\n I had posted this over on G+ a little bit ago, but thought maybe I should stick it here as well, just in case anyone has any interesting thoughts on the matter. It relates specifically to THIS discussion on Smallville started by Joel, and the mecha campaign he's been running for a few weeks.

Anyway, note I'm just copying and pasting what I had typed on G+, and I'll adjust formatting as much as I can:\n
Just trying to get my ideas down for what I am tentatively calling Mecha-Opera, a dramatic space-opera/mecha anime game that has arisen from Joel Shempert's Mechaville campaign, spurred by my desire to have a Cortex+ system game that supports giant fighting robots and dramatic anime scenes, while not being bogged down by unnecessary details like power-plant outputs, heatsinks, rates of fire, etc.

These are just the components of making a Lead, because, really, that's the only thing that makes it differ than...say...the base rules of Smallville. It's still a bit in flux, because I like to tackle with naming conventions...blame it on the obsessive compulsions in my brain.

VALUES
Those things that spur your Lead to action, and connect them to the story. Measured by ratings between D4 and D12, with related statements.
Honor, Glory, Power, Purpose, Heart, Wisdom

BONDS
How you are connected with the other Leads and important cast members. Also measured by ratings between D4 and D12 with related statements.

DRIVES
Motivations and driving forces behind your Lead's destiny. These function like Distinctions in Marvel Heroic, often brought into play as a beneficial D8, but also can be used as a disadvantage, giving the Master a D6 Trouble and earning a Plot Point.

TRAITS
Skills, talents, advantages, and special abilities. This covers everything from being a really good pilot, to being attractive, and even psychic powers (since the characters aren't superheroes, the effects of psychic powers are more narrative than anything else - "I use my Telepathy D8 to peer into his mind as I question him..."). Measured by ratings between D4 and D12.

EXTRAS
Temporary advantages, gear, contacts, and access you can use. Like most everything else, measured by ratings between D4 and D12. You can only use them once per Episode...if you use them again, you permanently Step Down its rating. Luckily, Extras will be easy to gain and replenish.

SYSTEMS
This is what most people will be interested in - the things that their Mecha, Powersuits, special Devices, or Spaceships are capable of. It's unlikely any single character will have more than one Mecha, or Suit, or whatever, but in case they somehow do, they can't use abilities from each, like with the Power Sets of Marvel Heroic. Just doesn't work that way.
These are all defined by their Systems, measured again in ratings between D4 and D12 only if the Systems in question actually have any effect on your performance!!! Sure your ship may have some armor, but if it isn't a major feature, it probably won't have a Defense rating to help it.
There will be one overarching Limit for your item, such as the Giant Size of Mecha, losing Gear, etc., and individual Systems may have their own Limits that affect how and when they can be used. They will also have Effects, which are added abilities and stunts your System is capable of (kinda like SFX in Marvel Heroic).
The Systems are:
Boost - Engine power and frame strength
Speed - Surface movement, either ground or water
Flight - Air and space movement
Sensors - Scanning and detection
Defense - Durability, shields, and forcefields
Assault - Weaponry and targeting systems
Network - Communications and encryption
Stealth - Cloaking fields and electronic counter-measures
Servos - Maneuverability and reflexes
Utility - Mechanical advantages (repair systems, tool-hands, etc.)
