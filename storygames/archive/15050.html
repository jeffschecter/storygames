\n How would you design a game to be played in bed, in the dark, while falling asleep?

It has to be played by two people and It could be either a GM-Player game or a Peer Rpg.

The game 'state' could be kept by incorporating some of the mechanics in the fiction (eg: your 3 Hero Points become 'the 3 lucky rings' you were given) or it could use fingers to keep count. I wouldn't know how to randomize resolution.

It would be cool if the haziness of memory before sleep could be incorporated as a feature of the game.

    \n Randomization, each player has a bag of beads and they can wager zero to three of them at a time. Should be playable by touch alone. Winner gets narrative control for that round, ties allow for partial success but usually come with a cost. If both sides bet zero, each side gets a minor negative outcome instead of a partial success. Expended beads are placed in a discard pile/pouch. The number of beads each side has determines the relative length of the scene.

    \n I'm assuming its for kids, but i've been wrong before. Set it in a fantasy environment, i'm almost thinking dragons and chess pieces. You can take anything you have around you -- a slipper, pjs, blanket or pillow or whatever, and you make that into something else (pjs become armor or a blanket becomes a robe, a pillow can be a shield or a chest or a book bag with tools in it, get creative). I'm thinking this would be a neat precursor to dream states.
    \n
    \nTheres no need to get dice involved in bed, do rock paper scissors or pick a number for random chance, but I hardly think its needed. A big list of rules or mechanics late at night sounds like a hassle.
    \n
    \n If we're talking about a bedtime story, I like the idea of exchanging narration control, maybe even have one person control the party and the other control the obstacles, then change off, maybe after a timer or something, set the alarm clock for sleep mode.
