\n I really love radio plays. From old time radio to modern radio plays and even some of the lower production quality dramatic podcasts. I'm a huge fan.

So what can be done for an RPG to produce these kinds of play?

Would PTA just work as is, or would some other approach be better?

Would it be best to narrow the focus a bit more and have things like murder mystery serials, old time sci-fi stuff like X Minus 1 and/or Dimension X? Or for horror ones?

I'm currently listening the Dalek Empire series put out by Big Finish in the UK. They have some seriously high production values and I'd recommend them-- especially if you like Daleks. They're definitely action adventure though. Their other productions are really good as well (Doctor Who, 2000 AD, Tomorrow People, Dark Shadows, etc.,).

At the moment I'm thinking PTA is probably the easiest way to go. Any thoughts about how to best incorporate some of the characteristics specific to the medium?

    \n I don't know how to put this, so I'll just come straight out with it: I've produced radio plays before. ("Dark Hill," USC and "Loosed in Heaven.")
    \nThey're a lot of fun and a hell of a lot cheaper than TV.
    \n
    \nThe scripts are a lot different than TV, because you have to show so much more of the action through the dialogue than you do when you can rely on televised images.
    \nAlso, casting for voices is much more rewarding than casting for looks.
    \n
    \nThat having been said, PtA is a good starting point, but you MUST look into puppetland at the same time, if you haven't already (as mentioned above.) The Puppetland rules for players expressing themselves are perfect for radio drama... well, they provide an excellent basis, anyway.
    \n
    \nAlso, if you use PtA, you can call special effects "Foley"
    \n
    \nOh, wait, here's another suggestion: the GM should try and read events like The Announcer on the radio show.
