Dice as Gaming Tax: What are your obligations?

Andy
October 2006 edited October 2006 in Story Games
From the "What do you want to learn from others" thread (man, that thread inspired about 5-8 new topics in my head):
I want to learn how to make myself stop buying dice that I don't need.
For me, there's a big "Hellz No!" tucked in there. Now, if one was buying blocks of dice ($7-13 a pop) and the like for no apparent reason, sure I can see that as a problem of sorts. But for me, here's a thing I've noticed:

* I like to go to the Friendly Local Gaming Store and leaf through stuff.
* I will read a few dozen books or supplements, skimming them for goodies, with absolutely no intention of buying them.
(ex: about a week ago I went to SciFi Genre in Durham, a new Gaming/Comic store in equal amounts: I looked through 3 Exalted supplements, 4 nWoD supplements, 2 HERO supplements including Ultimate Skill and Martial Artist, 3 GURPS books, Ptolus, and Spycraft 2.0, in the space of roughly an hour or so)
* The store is there to sell stuff, not to be my personal library. And yet, I'm there to politely pick a few tidbits from game books that again I'm not going to buy.
* The store sells only a few things under $3.00: They are mostly junk food and dice. I don't need junk food.

Therefore, when I go to a game store and flip through stuff for a long time, the more time I spend the more obliged I feel to toss them a little business. If I'm there to just browse the new stuff, sure I'm in and out in 10 mintues. But if I'm there to pull ideas from their shelves, I'll happily plop down a buck or two on pretty dice I don't need, usually d8s or d12s.

For me, single-purchase dice are Gaming Tax, for when I want to support the FLGS and yet don't want to drop $25 on some book I totally don't need.

Now, the above doesn't really lend itself to discussion, it's more like a blog post. So I'm wondering:

* What do you do when you feel those obligatory urges to buy something at your FLGS (assuming that it's Friendly, and Doesn't Suck)? Do you swallow it or just buy something?
* If there's a standard thing you buy when you feel obliged, what is it? What's your "Gaming Tax"?
