    \n We should be able to do partial color.
    \n
    \nI *think* our printer will be able to differentiate between a page with B&W and a page with color and print them appropriately all from one booklet. I think. But I've only seen a demo of the machine so far and won't have it on hand till we open the new retail shop. So final testing on a number of issues will still be required.
    \n
    \nIf I can't print a mix of B&W and color all at once, we'll still do color inserts. But where and how they get inserted will depend on how much color you want and where they will be inserted. Printing and collating on ur new laser machine would mean I print the B&W stuff all together, then print the color stuff separately and then hand insert the color stuff. A batch of color on the front end, back end, or a single batch of color pages inserted in the middle would be no big deal. Something more complicated than that I would have to charge extra for.
    \n
    \nWe have an older 15 bin collator so I in theory have the power to do a print run non collated out of new color machine and then collate it together on the older collator. If doing collating that way we could have any box of B&W and color pages. But...no way I could do it completely POD that way. Maybe in short runs of 50-100 books. The old collator also has an error ratio of like 1%, so doing this way would need to small per job bump in price also.

    \nI just set up a Yahoo Group list for those who have expressed interest in our POD printing and other POD services that we plan to offer through the Guild of Blades Retail Group. You can join the list at:

    \nI am presently working to help my Wife get her IT consulting company onto solid footing and don't plan to open our B&M retail space until that has been firmly established. However, we are contemplating getting the additional POD printing equipment soon and beginning to offer the POD Card and POD Book printing services. I'm presently trying to gauge how practical that would be, from a demand stand point, to start with only the POD printing from within our industry and then later expanding to include the B&M store and the extra volume of local copy and print that would involve.
    \n
    \nSo if you are interested, please join the list and let us know about your projects. I'm interested in taking preliminary pre orders on the POD cards for the moment (non binding, of course) to gauge up front demand. But since we don't have a hard and fast launch date, please don't submit anything to use that is time sensitive just yet.
