    Posted By: jhkimAre we saying that any historically inaccurate assumptions in games is "exoticization"?
    \nI don't think that's the thrust, here. Exoticization is making something exotic -- using exagerration and selective emphasis to separate it off from 'normal' and giving it a privileged (and often less humanized) status. While it is most often done to distinguish Self from Other, that's not its definition. It's more than possible to self-exoticize: you see it all the time in, for instance, performers embellishing their 'exoticness' to attract a bigger audience. I think the US does this a lot, distinguishing itself as THE champion of world democracy, not like those lazy Canadians (who participate in more international peacekeeping missions than we do).

    "America single-handedly defeating Nazy Germany" is exotic, not because it's wrong, but because it blithely implies that America was the only nation that could defeat Nazi Germany. America is the badass superpower that beat the Nazis. America is different. America is better. And then living on that myth for fifty years leads to America being totally schizoid trying to be both interventionalist and isolationist at the same time in the present day.

For example, I don't consider chivalric romance to be exoticizing. A fictional chivalric knight is likely to be more comprehensible to a more modern reader than a realistic medieval warlord. The rest is a post-apocalyptic wasteland made of voodoo tribes and AIDS. Asia is mostly dominated by China, Japan, Israel and a country known interchangeably as Aye-rack, Saudi Arabia, and the Middle East. North Korea is in there somewhere, which means there might be a South Korea, but I don't know. Vietnam used to be there as well, before we bombed it into the stone age. That's all the geography I need to know.
