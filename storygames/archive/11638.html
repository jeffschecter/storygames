    \n Man, I love Krakens. Seriously.

    I'm writing a Trail of Cthulhu supplement at the moment. One of the best things about it is the sea monsters. So, you've got Cthulhu, of course. And then Dagon. And the Deep Ones.

    But then you've also got John Wyndham's Krakens, a race of undersea things. They start by electrifying ships, killing everyone on board. Then, later, they construct sea tanks, with which crawl out of the sea and destroy coastal towns. They emit floating spheres, which fire out threads that adhere on contact and drag humans into the sea. Later, the Krakens start melting the ice caps to flood the humans out.

    Krakens are great.

    Posted By: skinnyghost
    That being said, who likes fighting ancient behemoths of the deep? Anyone have a favourite oceanic fantasy game?
    \nI ran this Shadow of Yesterday module a few times called The Earth Trembles in an Uncharted Language.

    The premise is that when the empire united, many knightly orders were proven redundant. So the Order of Knights of Earthly Patrol were tasked with doing the empire's tax collection. And the adventure starts as they enter the territory of the mud people, who live in stilted homes above a bubbling bog. A great storm destroys all of the bridges connecting the above-swamp village with solid ground... and the knights are forced to wait for the Pathfinders of the mud people to craft new bridges (a regular task, as storms wipe out their bridges often). While waiting, sorcery and Lovecraftian horror abound! Mud beasts are seen. Someone is inevitably murdered and fed to the mud beasts. A symbiotic relationship is revealed, between beast and human.

    The scenario was left open, so that any of a variety of factions and individuals could have emerged as the big bad, the sorcerous evil that was tearing apart the village and turning elder against elder. Regardless, in each time I ran it, it ended with something awful emerging from the mud, often with much devouring.

    Mud krakens!
