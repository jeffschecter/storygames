    \n OK, here's 2\xa2 from a former freelance graphic artist:
    1) It's awesome. Perfect general notion for the game, as I've read it. A little bit punk, a little bit artsy, a lotta attitude. Makes me want to open it.

    2) The title font size should be a bit larger and a bit further from the top margin. It looks shoved aside (intentionally?). Don't be afraid of getting nearer to the shoes--the pants legs are already muddied up and hard to recognize, anyway (see 4 below). And why is it so "neatly" centered, for this messy game? Hell, why is it perfectly horizontal? That display font sort of makes a pass at irregular baselines; kick that up a notch, maybe? (This sort of stuff is so easy to just copy a layer and tweak like mad, without any "risk," until you're happy and it's popping.)

    3) The subtitle font is, yes, too messy (affected) and also is too close to the bottom and right margins. The scrap paper with Sharpie scrawl is a GREAT idea, though--I'd bleed the scrap off the cover, so it doesn't look "pasted onto" it. Also, I would not align or justify the words on that scrap--make them look like a note scrawled to be left under a jilted boyfriend's windshield wiper. You might even want to foreshorten the text on the note, so that the scrap looks like it's lying next to (and overlapping some of) the photos

    4) I, too, can't quite get past the water color effect. If it's a downsampling artifact, fine; otherwise, I'd start massaging. Is there some reason the photo won't work without post-processing?

    5) I wanna see dysfunctions or oddities in those photos. Otherwise, at least make them photos of folks who, say, helped design or playtest--it would be a cool way to say "Thank you." (Perhaps they already are?)

    6) Toss in ONE more element, slightly above and to the left of the scattered photos, by the shoes, as if dropped there after seeing this scatter of photos. You gotta break up the layout so that it's less columnar and more dynamic or imbalanced, and an item about double or triple the size of a photo, bleeding off the left edge, would yank that column out of kilter. I'm thinking a half-empty coffee cup with a cigarette butt floating in it... a shattered cosmetics mirror... a high-quality, lacy, white bra dangling off the left of the cover (i.e. one cup plus a bit of strap--VERY cool contrast to the shoes, I'd think)... a flip-flop... something almost unidentifiable, but evocative, to make folks go "WTF is that thing there?" (the Thing I leave as an exercise for the reader).

    Looking forward to the release;
    David
