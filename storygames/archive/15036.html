...the game system specifics is only part of the experience of playing an RPG with different GMs. What's arguably more important is the individual GMs approach and philosophy on gaming. I recently happened on this concept, and as a public play organizer I love it. Unfortunately, I do not know how useful the particular set of badges here are but it is definitely a step in the right direction. I'd like to see a similar "rating" system for adventures, and players.

Is anyone else using something like this to communicate information with new players, and/or GMs?

    \n Lots of good feedback here.

    As a clarification, these badges are targeted towards more traditional gm'd games where specific game play elements are not always spelled out in the system which can lead to widely different play experiences.

    @JDCorley, your take would really make these "merit" badges, wouldn't it? However, I am not sure how useful that would actually be.

    As an organizer, this is how I intend to use them: for each module that is scheduled place the set of "badges" that describe game elements, and how it should be run. Basically, I think this could help establish expectations for the players, and of the game masters. In organized play such as RPGA, and Pathfinder Society I find that actual game play varies wildly from table to table, and what I am looking for is a way to establish a smoother flow. I am not sure if it would be better to say "this is the way this module should be run across tables" or work with the gm's and say "this is the way this gm is going to run this module" and let the players choose.

    I think another useful use could be to have a "gm set" that describes game elements I like to employ in games I run, and a "player set" that describes elements I like to interact with as a player. These later elements would not be constricting elements, but rather just a common vocabulary that can be used to communicating participant expectations, and desires.

    \n I think cons may be the wrong venue for this. In a store that regularly hosted games I could see giving voluntary "After Action Reports" where the players could give feedbak on the GMs (and the GMs on the players or Players for each other) and then awarding merit badges based on that feedback...

    It wouldn't all have to be game style related "Friendly," "Always on Time," "Good Sport" could all be badges.

    While the badges should always be positive, it could also help the venue get a jump on identifying players or GMs who might create problems. You're way more likely to get an early report of trouble behavior (GM X makes crude comments about female players, Player Y throws a tantrum if he loses) BEFORE it becomes a huge mess if you ask for regular feedback.
