    \n I assume Shreyas is asking about a flowcharty diagram that would start with born lifepaths at the top, with lines leading to the lifepaths that can be taken from that, and so on.

    A complete diagram would be very complex, though some kind of simplification could be done by primarily charting life paths that have prerequisites or fulfill those prerequisites. But if you also include lifepaths with leads that are necessary to follow some path (the prereq doesn't always have the necessary lead), then the diagram would still be quite huge.

    If you grouped the diagram by setting, you could use a solid line for a prereq that provides the necessary lead, and a dashed line for prereqs that don't provide the lead, then group all the lifepaths that don't participate in prereqs that have the same leads and have an arrow pointing into each setting they lead into.

    I did a diagram once for a limited set to figure out what lifepaths to use to burn up an NPC.

    I made it with Gliffy, which I love.

    The circle of lifepaths has a circle behind it, indicating that you can get to all of them from the others. Female-specific lifepaths are purple.

    I can see where extensibility becomes a problem as you get into larger and more heavily crosslinked things like the City or Seafaring settings. Gliffy has some collaboration features, so if you want to work on expanding this or cannibalizing it, just let me know. I'll need your e-mail address.

    Edit: the symbols were leads, in case that isn't clear. Adding more detail, like skill points or age spent, would start to clutter things a lot. The lifepaths with no arrows have no specific prereqs, they're just in that setting. I used to have arrows from "Born Peasant" but they weren't really necessary - no lifepath in the setting requires Born Peasant.
