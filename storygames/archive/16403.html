\n So by no means am I a game designer or trying to promote a game I created. I just threw together a game hacked from several different systems for a quick pick-up Avengers game for some friends before the movie comes out. And I'm sorta proud of it.
\n
\nHere are the character sheets I made up! Please remember I have no skill with graphic design or layout. I just put this together in old Microsoft Word! What I am happy about is that the entire character sheet fits on an index card. In fact, if you cut out the picture, the info fits on a post-it note! This is a double-edged sword because while all the info you need is readily available, its rather small.
\n
\nThe goal was to make a very quick pick up game. Pre-Gen characters, very simple rules. This is why I didn't want to use Marvel Super Heroes or other games.

    \n Here are the rules. Again, not a layout professional. It comes in at a whopping 3 micro-pages (2 inch border all around, tried to imitate Pocket Danger Patrol without success).
    \n
    \nSo this is pretty much Pocket Danger Patrol and Lady Blackbird hacked together with a dash of FATE and Mouse Guard. Maybe some inspirations from other sources.
    \n
    \nThe rules are actually for a generic game. The first page is character creation. The second two are rules.
    \n
    \nI enjoy how Lady Blackbird moves characters from obstacle to obstacle with a ton of freeform play in between. However, I wanted to draw out the obstacles a little bit, so made each obstacle a full fledged encounter ala Danger Patrol. Instead of obstacle to obstacle, characters move from objective to objective. Sort of like in Lady Blackbird: Escape the Brig, Reclaim the Owl, Escape the Hand of Sorrow, Refuel, Get information on how to get to Remnants, Get Lady B to her man so she can do what she has to do.
    \n
    \nAlong with that we have characters with Goals, Relationships, and other flags for more in depth roleplaying. All this with the intense action sequences of Danger Patrol.
    \n
    \nHowever, I hacked out the threat dice and replaced with the stress track from Fate and Conditions reminiscent of LB or Mouse Guard. I've played Pocket DP a few times in the past couple of weeks (what can I say, when I found out about it, I called everyone I knew!) but felt threat dice are just a bit too random, especially for the feel of game I'm aiming for.
