\n Drawing from the thread jjafuller started, "Why is gender static in fantasy?", I'd like to ask:

How would you build and structure a game about personal (gender) identity transformation?

I'm immediately inclined to vaguely flesh out a state, a refugee, and name Apocalypse World-style moves like When you take a new face or When you shed your skin by the lake...

That, or some sort of Oracle game loosely based on Janelle Monae's Metropolis: Several face-changers are on the run from the dystopian state for loving across boundaries between android and human.

    \n I've written a game that deals with gender role dynamics getting overthrown when one character changes gender. As well as facing up to the fact of your own mortality.

    It's a sci fi game, a nordic style tight setup with pregenerated characters and situation. A macho man in midlife crisis, a gay male ballet dancer and a reptiloid alien (of a species that changes sex every three weeks) are stuck on a little improvised raft in the middle of the ocean after a shipwreck on a primitive planet under protective tech embargo. Things slowly but surely go south, and relations change drastically when the alien changes, because hir culture prescribes and demands very different social role behaviour depending on current sex, and demands that you relate to people filling the opposite gender role.

    Awesome game, if I may toot my own horn here. The downside is, it's in danish and not yet available online. :-/

    \n I'd be very interested also in games based around this. I was intrigued by what I'd heard of the Swedish LARP Mellan Himmel och hav.

    Still, it isn't necessary to have a whole new game just for this. I'd be curious about people's experiences with gender transformation otherwise.

    My notable RPG experience with gender transformation was in 2006 in an Amber Diceless campaign. My character was an over-the-top gay man - and a supply officer in the German Army of an alternate WWII, inspired mainly by Donna Barr's "Desert Peach" but also a bit by the historical Ernst R\xf6hm. Paralleling his German origin, he also had a Chaos heritage. The gender part comes in that some Chaosites define a "demon form" as an alternate shape, one of the pieces of shapeshifting. A few sessions into the campaign, I gained a demon form - and secretly defined with the GM that my demon form was a "Bat-winged Bimbo from Hell" - i.e. a classic succubus appearing as a tall, well-built woman with red skin, large bat wings, and fangs.

    An interesting bit from group dynamics is that for a few sessions, the other players knew that I had a demon form out-of-character, but the other characters never saw it, so I never described it to them. I instead just talked to the GM, saying that "I change into my hideous demon form" and went on to describe my actions. The funny thing was that my character, Manfred, really did consider his demon form hideous. Although flamingly gay, he was in no way transsexual, and he prized manliness.

    When they did find out what his hideous demon form was, the other players were surprised and some a bit indignant. It was an interesting shift. In the cataclysmic end to the campaign, he dove down the throat of the Serpent (avatar of Chaos), and in the epilogue was revealed to still be around as a spirit, but only in a female form.
