\n Hey all! So I've had this odd little idea for a while, sort of as an supplement for Happy Birthday, Robot! Where HBR is a basic introduction to gaming, there could be a way to introduce other elements of gaming pretty seamlessly into that format. In other words, HBR is for elementary school, so this new game would be for early middle school or tweens. I'm going to post the basic playtest info here for anyone who would like to test out the rules. If you dig it, it might become a book in the future.

--------------------

Pop n' Locke
Meet Pop
"Pop" MacGuffin is a retired locksmith and was supposedly the sixth greatest cat burglar in the world. To hear him tell it, he was saving the world from dangerous objects with untold power. These days, he's got a hard enough time getting into a sweater vest as he does a bulletproof safe.

Meet Locke
Locke MacGuffin is Pop's only child and she would be the sole heir to carry his legacy. She was picking deadbolts before she could walk, climbing walls before Kindergarten and running from guards before her first kiss. That's the problem. She *could* have a normal life if it weren't for this family.

Meet the MacGuffins
Mom used to safeguard all the objects Pop collected years ago. When she disappeared, so did all the objects... but they didn't stay missing for long. Sure enough, Uncle Esteban has the stopwatch that can stop time. Cousin Maria has the glasses that grant heat-vision. And little Fernando has the hypnotic kazoo.

Now, Pop has to get the objects back all over again, but he needs help from Locke. Can they rescue the world from the rest of their family? Will they uncover the mystery of Mom's disappearance? Will Locke ever have a day where she's not called into the principal's office?


ABOUT THE GAME

In this game, you'll tell a story about Pop and his teenaged daughter Locke. They are trying to retrieve a normal-looking object with paranormal powers from a member of the MacGuffin family. Each member of the family has their own quirks, but none of them will want to give up the power easily. It's up to Pop n' Locke to be clever, skilled and lucky enough to save the world from their family.

This is a game for three players, each with some control over one character's part of the story. One player controls Pop, one controls Locke and the third controls the MacGuffin, the family member that Pop and Locke are targeting in this story. The MacGuffin player also controls all the goons, obstacles and distractions that get in Pop and Locke's way.

This game is an adaptation of "Happy Birthday, Robot!" While HBR is a first introduction to structured storytelling, "Pop n' Locke" adds the concepts of character ownership, equipment, GMs and victory conditions.

"Pop n' Locke" is intended for a young adult audience who can play without parental supervision if they wish to do so.


HOW TO PLAY

The game is pretty much just like HBR with some twists.


* First Sentence

The first sentence of each story is not always exactly the same, but does follow a specific format. See if you can spot it.

- Uncle Manuel has the cufflinks that raise the wearer's blood pressure!

- Auntie Martha has the nail file that dissolves metal!

- Cousin Claudia has the bulletproof desk calendar!

The first sentence of the story is always "(Relative) has the (Object)!" That establishes who Pop and Locke's target is for this story.


* The Second Sentence

The "MacGuffin" player takes the first turn rolling dice to create the second sentence. This will usually elaborate on something the relative is doing, using the power of the Object towards nefarious ends. For example:

- Uncle Manuel gives the cufflinks to Commissioner Stone, raising his blood pressure.

- Auntie Martha dissolves metal bars to free zoo animals.

- Cousin Claudia robs a bank, wearing the desk calendar.


* Turn Order

As noted, the MacGuffin always takes the first turn. Pop takes the next turn. Then Locke. That completes the round, at which point the MacGuffin begins the next round.


* ANDs and BUTs

The "Pop" player always writes the AND words for the "Locke" player.

The "Locke" player always writes the AND words for the "Pop" player.

The "MacGuffin" player always writes the BUT words for either the "Pop" player or the "Locke" player. The "MacGuffin" player also writes their own AND words.


* Free Words and Objects

Coins grant free words, just like they do in normal HBR.

Players also get specific free words if their character possesses an Object. For example, if the MacGuffin is playing Auntie Martha and he possesses "the nail file that dissolves metal." The Object grants the MacGuffin player all of those words as free words.

To put it another way, the MacGuffin player may use "nail," file," "that," "dissolves," and "metal," as free words at any time. As you can see, Objects are very powerful tools in storytelling, since they give their owner a lot of flexibility early in the game.


* Victory Conditions

Just like in normal HBR, the game ends when a player has 10 or more coins. However, the story ends in different ways depending on who gets 10 coins first.

If Pop gets 10 coins, the Object is retrieved and will grant either Pop or Locke its free words in a future story.

If Locke gets 10 coins, the relative is turned into the authorities.

If the MacGuffin gets 10 coins, keeps the Object and evades captures, regardless of whether Pop or Locke have 10 coins.

--------------------

And that's the gist of the game! If you have further thoughts, please share.
