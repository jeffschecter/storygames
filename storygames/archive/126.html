Social Mechanics vs. Immersion

I think this whole concept is a bunch of smoke.

What it really is, is "Social mechanics vs. This is how we ALWAYS play".

How so?

1. Blocking

Ever roleplay something totally awesome, reasonable, and oscar-award-winning... and have the GM totally shut it down because it didn't fit with the pre-plotted story?

That ruins my engagement with a game. In fact, it makes me NOT want to roleplay my character, and instead focus on just tweaking my character to maximum tactical advantage because that's the only place it seems I can actually affect the game.

2. "Realism"

"Realistically, you control the decisions you make." Bullshit. Realistically, you deal with social pressure, get fast talked, used, make and break alliances, get into pissing contests, and find decision making is a lot softer process than what you, and you alone might want.

Consider advertising. It works! And that's from people who don't even know you personally. Then consider all the times you've been talked into doing something you regret. In reality, we're social creatures and there's always social games going on.

3. "But it's 'role-playing', not 'roll-playing'"

Sigh. Why are you playing a game with dice even? Why is it always "just a game" when people are feeling bad about it, and then it's all "serious and better than just rolling dice" when you want to feel good about it?

How is NOT using dice better THAN using dice? For all the calls of theory-snobbery, this is pretty snobbish right here, especially since most of these folks are playing games that USE dice (at least in the rules!)

4. "Dice get in the way of my imagination"

Dice... get... in... the... way? You go on about being "a better roleplayer" and your imagination- is blocked by pieces of plastic?!?

5. "Social mechanics stop you from actually winning via roleplaying"

So what if someone says something completely awesome but the dice are against him or her? The other side gives up! DitV- you give! I've seen it happen! OR, you come up with something MORE AWESOME as a comeback! I've seen that happen too!

6. "Social mechanics make me have to take the chance of my character doing things beyond my concept of him or her"

OMG! You mean your character might actually be dynamic and changing!?! You mean you might actually have to accept input from other people in a game played with other people?!?

(deep breath)

Ok. I'm better now. Thanks for listening.

How about you?
