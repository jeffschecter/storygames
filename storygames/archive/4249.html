\n Here is a contest idea I actually had at one point:

The contest begins "now", but the "due date" would be 5-6 months later. In that time, you have to Obtain a License for a Media Property, ANY property, and then write a game based on it.
The media property can be ANYTHING, from movie to music to comic to whatever. But it has to be something that someone else did (that is, you can't write a short story, then license it to yourself). Bonus points if it belongs to someone you never exchanged words with before.

    Posted By: joepubDesign a game that requires absolute silence from the players from the moment a session starts to the moment it ends.
    \nThe Players play members of an alien race of insects that communicate through visual cues. Each player has a deck of symbols which they use to communicate with each other and the GM as they attempt to take over the house in which they have landed. Conflicts are resolved through pulling numbers from a hat and players have set number of insectiod minions they can eat to get more pulls or to void the last pull if they didn't like the results.

    Posted By: shreyasDesign a game where you take a preexisting game, and look at its mechanical entities (like, for D&D, classes, feats, skills...). Don't remove or add any entities. Redefine them.
    \nTake a game set in feudal Japan that has at least some divisions based on caste, class, role, etc. Something like Legend of the Five Rings or Usagi Yojimbo. Take all those rules, and then set your game in a relatively modern American high school. The different castes are instead specific social strata: The nobility become the rich kids, the jocks become samurai, artisans become geeks and other experts, the peasants become the vo-tech kids and the like, and the eta become the total social outcasts. Follow the rules of your samurai game from there.
