\n From the Stuff to Watch Thread:\n
Posted By: DevPRomantic Roleplaying. A blossoming LARP scene in Beijing (including plays of the Dance and the Dawn LARP).
\n
Posted By: ndp[Romantic Roleplaying 3] IIRC some of Dev's LARP-inclined friends from Boston turned the tabletop version into a LARP - Warren Tusk is the author/adapter, according to my google (link) (assuming that's the same one they're playing in the story - how wild would it be if some stranger made a LARP out of it too!).
\nAs linked above: there was a recent run in NYC, and from what I hear it went well! As such, a dance-infused social-LARP requires some musical control, and I hear the GM used an iPad (with the venue's internet) to remotely control the music from wherever the action was happening. So that's pretty cool.

There's a bit of a following for the LARP, and I hope we can see a published/packaged version of it some time in the future.

    \n That is so awesome! The picture of the dancers is gorgeous.

    I was in the tabletop game of Dance and the Dawn that Kat ran this weekend at Origins. I was pleasantly surprised by the mechanics and how well they flowed and how subtle yet effective the turn structure was at inspiring the witty banter. Especially the use of the chessboard! I wasn't sure what to think of the chessboard at first, but it was really fun in a light strategy and visually stimulating kind of way. My friend Evan actually bought it for me for Christmas saying "Kira, this looks like a game you would like! Romance and backstabbing and gender role themes!" Well, he was right! I'm really glad I got the chance to play and look forward to the next opportunity. I think the themes dealt with gender in a really interesting way, while the game itself was just solid fun.

    I'm curious to know more about your inspirations and intentions behind writing the game. I'm guessing you have a place somewhere online where I can read about that.

    \n Thanks for everyone's interest! To answer Kira: Some of my previous posts on the (tabletop) version:

    http://games.forgreatjustice.net/2009/07/29/dance-and-the-dawn-a-retrospective-and-a-hint/
    http://games.forgreatjustice.net/tag/tdntd/

    In brief: the idea formed while brainstorming a game for Game Chef after working through lots of anime and folklore, and I iterated off-and-on through some years until it felt ready to complete. The importance of gender roles (and resistance to them), ritual gameplay and a sense of musical rhythm were all key to me.

    Then I playtested the game for Warren (who was a prolific LARP writer in my crowd of people), and he had the Best Idea, being the combination of a LARP game with actual dancing. For our first run, I think the first 20 slots or so filled up in maybe 24 hours? So that was cool.
