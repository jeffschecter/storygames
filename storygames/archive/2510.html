\n ... in which you and your friends pretend (?) to be a group of mentally deranged game designers yearning to press the living ideas out of their minds and into the wet clay of human society.

Each kind of designer not only develops but also uses, within the game, an entirely different set of rules. Each has a score they are trying to reduce to zero, striving for their ideal game. For instance, the Bipolar Literary Designer attempts to reduce his five-dot Pretention attribute, while the OCd20 Rulesmith attempts to minimize her epic-level Perceived Imbalance score. Meanwhile they point out elements that they like (and that others might hate) about each others' games, which improves the game's Awesomeness but makes the other designers gnash their teeth at elements previously unnoticed, increasing their unwanted score.

Conception, discussion, and playtesting are just the beginning. Go on epic quests to GenCon (or just Genericon), raid the abandoned dungeons of the 20-by-20 room for notional ore, brave the RPG Site Trolls... and just when you have everything perfect, the game takes a violent left.

Each and every one becomes a terrible demon that must be summoned and controlled by main force of will, even by its original designer - a savage creature at turns forgiving and world-devouring, a wild, breeding concept both beneath and beyond the scope of what your character set out to accomplish.

And you know that It will not do.

You must start again.

I Must Write.

"Tiny voices, echoes of our heritage,
our long and sallow faces turn the other way,
tiny voices, harbored deep within
as we outwardly deny that they have something to say,
and if we don't confront them they will never go away"
--Tiny Voices, Bad Religion
