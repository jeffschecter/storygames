\n I haven't really finished this game, in any sense. I only started it on Saturday. But I'm rather excited by the character sheet.

Bleakworlds character sheet

It's not the design that excites me, you understand, although I like the thing in the middle, which took me ages to do.

What excites me is the stats and the way they're all in pairs. Look, there's a stat called Peacekeeping, paired with Military. And Deceit is paired with Honesty. I like that.

Excuse the bad colours (I'm colour-blind) and the adolescent title (I like it).

    \n Ooooh. That's a great idea, Kynn.

    Thank you very much for the feedback. You're right about Merchant/Tech. My new three pairs (before I read Kynn's thing, which I now have to think about) are:\n
      \n
    • Military / Peacekeeping
      • \n
    • Hi-Tech / Low-Tech
      • \n
    • Fair Trade / Foul Play
      • \n
    \nSo Military and Peacekeeping are obvious. Hi-Tech and Low-Tech are using high-technology (docking computers, nanotechnology) and low-technology stuff (pipes, tractors, smashing things with a plank). Fair Trade is earning money as a merchant; Foul Play is earning money through crime.

    The premise of the game, by the way, is that you're an escaped criminal, trying to avoid the law and build a life among the bleak, rough, lawless worlds of a corner on the galaxy.

    So all of those pairs represent choices you make in rebuilding your new life. Do you fight or keep the peace? Do you attempt to understand new technology or reject it? And do you make your living within the law or outside it?

    Thanks again.
