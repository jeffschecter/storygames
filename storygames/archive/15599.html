    The game rules are 5 chapters bundled together and called the Operations Manual. This content appears in the Basic Game and in the Premium Edition of each of the Event Books. Event Books are based on popular Marvel crossover events, like Civil War. All heroes and villains provided, multiple paths through the storyline, locations, unlockable resources, campaign stuff. You can buy the Essentials Edition if you already have the Ops Manual from another book. Each Event also has three Event Supplements. These add more stuff to the Event, secondary heroes and villains, more storylines, often peripheral. Sometimes building on the end of the main Event, other times at the same time.

    So we have 3 events planned for this year:

    Civil War (Premium or Essentials) + 3 Supps
    Annihilation (Premium or Essentials) + 3 Supps
    Age of Apocalypse (Premium or Essentials) + 3 Supps

    Total 15 SKUs, plus Basic Game for 16.

    Easy to imagine this being done for D&D-style game. Include rules, characters, campaign in a single book. Eberron, Dragonlance, whatever. No corebook, just rules or no rules as you like.

    Cheers,
    Cam

    The only thing in common is - you assemble a bunch of dice from various places on and off your character sheet, roll them, then you add the top-rolling two dice.     \n MHR scales back a lot of the "sit down and create our own setting and characters" model that Smallville used, and instead directs play more toward published events and pre-made heroes because that's a significant draw to our audience. It's not a generic super hero game, in other words. That said, in no way do the rules keep you from creating new heroes or events; in fact, the Operations Manual has all of those rules in it because I need people to write those events and create new heroes for our published events.

    One thing that you can do which works very well is take an existing hero, tweak it how you like, add power sets, change Distinctions, and you're set. It's a very easy to mod game, just like the other Cortex Plus games (moreso, I think, than Smallville, and about the same as Leverage).

    Cheers,
    Cam

    \nI quickly wanted to mention Milestones (which we had another thread about briefly) and some of the other stuff on your sheet absolutely should be modified, even if it's an "established character."

    Like, Frank Miller's Daredevil isn't just undergoing different circumstances than Brian Bendis'. He is distinguished by different things, he has different priorities, his life has different key elements to it. His character sheet will be different.

    "Is this what a nervous breakdown looks like for a guy who dresses up like the devil?"

    Jean Grey is another example: different creators just have different ideas about who she should be in this particular event. The main problem with the character comes when we try to assemble all these ideas into a single coherent personality. It's not possible. She can't play all those roles, they contradict each other and don't have strong direction, because with multiple creators over 50 years, that would be impossible or boring.

    \n FUUUUCK, I just lost my long post about Event procedures. The second time is never as good. Anyway, here goes:

    Events are basically "big crossover hoedowns". This is a technical comics term. They are definitely finite but definitely longer than one-shots. I would say that a short Event could be played out in 2-3 sessions and the longest might go 10-11.

    I'm giving just the general outline of each event creation step, the text and thoughts are my own tips and ideas for how to make a good event.

    1 - Get a basic idea for the Event. Normally you do this by standing on a chair and yelling what the Event is. "SHAPESHIFTING ALIENS INVADE EARTH!!" or "MARY JANE WATSON AND TYPHOID MARY ARE IN THE SAME BROADWAY SHOW!" Do this until the idea is cool enough or you get kicked out of the restaurant.

    2 - Divide the Event into Acts. It's okay if it's just 1. If you have more than 3 you probably have 2 Events. Basically Events are Big Developments. So Act 1 might be "The government Sentinel program is unveiled and it causes big problems for mutants!" and Act 2 might be "The government can't control the Sentinels anymore! OH MAN!"

    3 - Organize the flow of each Act. This is more a bullet point list/skeleton/very broad timeline of what might happen. So it might be: 1 - The girls get the part, 2 - Spiderman won't leeeeave Typhoiiiiid alooooone, Peter won't leave Mary-Jane alone? 3 - The skeevy producer needs insurance money to pay off the Kingpin, so he sets fire to the theater, 4 - A big showdown with the Kingpin's guys, Typhoid's personalities, Mary Jane, and Spiderman.

    4 - Get a Setting, Hook and Buildup for each Act. You probably already did most of this but if you are missing anything, fill it in now.

    5 - Sketch out a few Action Scenes. This is less in terms of outcomes (or even how the scene starts) but more in terms of physical locations, what's at stake other than who wins, etc. 100 ninjas hopped up on mutant growth hormone come to assassinate Matt Murdock while he's on a date with Milla Donovan.

    6 - Make sure there's some time for Transition Scenes, and if you have some ideas for them, go ahead and list them, but they're often more player-focused. A troubled mutant youth comes in between classes to talk to the PC, worried about their future. A sleazy Kellytown informant gives you the dirt, stuff like that.

    7- Make some Event Milestones (possibly) or unlockables (definitely) related to the event. We have a whole separate thread for Milestones, it's good, go read it. Unlockables are basically what you spend your XP on related to the Event. Spend 5 XP to get a sympathetic ear at the FBI. Spend 5 XP and Foggy files a countersuit against the Globe for accusing Matt of being Daredevil. And so on. They're basically upgrades/changes/advances for your character that help them out in this event right now.

    8 - Sketch out a conclusion. What happens if the heroes win, what happens if the villains win. What are the outcomes of various possibilities going forward for your campaign world? Sentinels from the future arrive to make sure they aren't wiped out by budget cuts. Or maybe the government doubles down on the Sentinel program and they become a recurring threat. Or maybe mutant terrorists cause havoc in response to the Sentinels. Whatever the "next thing" might be, depending on how things come out.

    And that's how you make an Event.

    \n The Basic Game is rules + mini-event + 23 hero datafiles. It's the only product with "Basic" in the title.
    Essentials Event Books contain only the Event campaign information + hero datafiles.
    Premium Event Books contain the Event campaign information + hero datafiles + the same rules first featured in the Basic Game.

    In other words, we give you the option of buying rules and setting/campaign/adventures in one volume, or if you already have the rules, buying just the setting/campaign/adventures.

    Both versions of Event Book are hardcover. The Basic Game, and Event Supplements that tie into each Event Book, are softcover.
