Come the Revolution: Inspired by V for Vendetta

Chris_Goodwin
April 2006 edited April 2006 in Story Games
We saw V for Vendetta last night, and this fragment insinuated itself into my consciousness.

Come the Revolution

a roleplaying game

"Stats" are:

* Family

* Work

* Relationship (this refers to your spouse or significant other)

* Politics

* Community

* Church (if you have one)

These represent how in tune you are with these parts of your life. They represent things that have influence over you, and things you have influence over.

You also have a Rank and a Secrecy attribute. Both start at 0. Rank is your rank within your revolutionary cabal, and Secrecy is how much of your life you keep a secret from the government.

If your Rank ever increases above your Secrecy score, you have to do one of the following:

* Spend one or more points from your stats to add points to your Secrecy on a one for one basis, until your Rank is no longer higher than your Secrecy. You then have to narrate how the stat (or the item represented by it) suffers as you ratchet up the secrecy in your life.

* Roll 1d10; you want to roll greater than the difference between your Rank and your Secrecy. If you fail at this roll, then you have potentially come to the attention of the government. Roll 1d10; you want to roll greater than your Rank. If you fail at this roll, you get the Midnight Knock (see below).

You decide whether you hang your personal life out to dry in order to protect the Revolution; if you don't, you're taking a big chance.



The Midnight Knock:

You have been taken away in the middle of the night by the secret police. You are at first asked to provide information, and threatened with torture if you don't provide the information they're looking for. You, of course, don't, and are tortured. At some point, they find out what they want to know from you and release you. Or they might just kill you instead, or disappear you, or something equally as bad. (Unperson?) The higher your Rank, the more likely you are to never return.

Maybe there is a Trust attribute which represents an agglomeration of all of the stats above. Not sure yet.

Start:

The player to your left narrates how you have come to know the Revolution and how it is drawing you in. The player to your left should be pushing you, hard. You narrate how you are living your life and balancing the demands of your family, job, etc., with your growing responsibilities in the Revolution.
