Which games work with Skype out of the box?

planetary
September 2006 edited September 2006 in Story Games
Many of the games we talk about here (and RPGs overall) just don't work well at all when you play them via voice chat. Too many fiddly bits, too much reliance on table visuals, etc. (To be clear, I don't think these are faults -- just properties. In a lot of cases, essential properties of their games.)

Many more games almost work well online, but you need someone to do a fair bit of extra work, or you need to create some special tracking tools to handle things.

But what games can be played via Skype almost right out of the box, with (1) almost no extra work required, and (2) almost no loss of game-generated player information?

Here's a list of games that I don't think meet the standard I'm imagining, just to be clear:
  • Dogs in the Vineyard. Too much effort to manage dice pools. I want to be able to look across the table and see who's got what. It's probably easier to play Dogs via IRC/MUSH than voice chat.
  • PTA. Hmm. Borderline. One person can roll all the cards without too much extra work. Tracking chips movements makes me squint and wonder. [EDIT: I'm starting to think this would work fairly well, actually, with just a little cost of announcing how much is left in the Producer and Fan Mail pools, or whatever.]
  • Primitive. It seems like playing online slaughters the language fun. You really want to be in person to make this work.
  • Agon. Without some kind of online tool thing, I can't imagine playing this cool game.
Of games that I suspect would work, there's Polaris. Just start by having everyone spend 30 seconds up-front to draw a table diagram to communicate who's where, and you should be good to go from there, yeah?

What else?

-JasonN

    Most of my recent role playing has been done over Skype. My old RPG'ing friends are geographically dispersed and when I discovered the indie rpg scene it was a natural choice. We also use a lightweight aid called Gametable which provides communal dice rolling and a shared "tabletop".

    Games tried so far:

    Dogs: Worked well but gametable was essential. It allowed us to push dice images around to during conflicts.
    Inspectres: Works well. Apart from dice rolling gametable is only used to contain the franchise sheet and and its dice plus the running franchise dice total. Neither of these are critical and dice rolling could be done privately.
    Breaking The Ice: Graham's online version wasn't available and the game still worked well. We used gametable for dice rolling but really didn't need to.

    Shooting The Moon: Another one that works well with no reliance on a shared space.

    I will shortly be running Lacuna which I can see no problems with and hopefully next year Burning Empires. For BE I will require gametable's shared space or something like Thinkature for Firefights.

    Games that I don't think I'll be trying even though I really want to

    Mortal Coil is unfortunately one game I won't be trying. I think conflicts have too many tokens and stuff moving about.
    Dust Devils. You would have thought that a simple card handling/sharing program would exist but if it does I can't find it. Its a shame. Graham?

    I'd like to play Mountain Witch and think it would work. The only question is how to distribute the secrets so that no one (including the GM) knows who has what.
