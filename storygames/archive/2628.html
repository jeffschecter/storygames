\n So, I've had kind of a bug up my ass of late about not getting interest in pick-up games into IRC, and a soemwhat related point that many people are stating that they don't enjoy short-form roleplay.
\n
\nIn an effort to get soem love for the short form flowing aorund, I've been pitching improv games - things like the games scene on Whose Line Is It Anyway?, where you act out a scene subject to some restraint or other specialised rules.
\n
\nMyself and Alexander Cherry played out the following scene playing a Word Step game - you start with a word ("Gold", in our case) and then speak one line with that word in. Then the next player takes the word, changes one letter, and says their line including the new word. And so on.
\n
\n "Gold! Cletus, I can't believe it, after twenty years... we've struck it rich!"
\n "Yeah, I really thought that Macready had sold us up the river, but here it is!"
\n "It's a good thing I was bold enough to buy the map! If you'd waffled any longer, I'm sure Gomez would have gotten it first."
\n "That bald fool can't prospect to save his life."
\n "Yeah, but he's good with people. His band of buddies would have swarmed down on this place. I hope they didn't follow us."
\n "What's that on the horizon? Quick, hand me your rifle."
\n "If they're expectin' anything other than a hard fight, they've got another thing coming." (*hands over rifle*)
\n "A few warning shots should ward them off!" *Fires*
\n "They better 'ware your crack shot, Cletus. And mine!" *Fires too*
\n "Lookit'um run off like a startled hare!"
\n "Here's where we found our gold, and here is where we'll make our stand. THIS LAND WILL NEVER BE YOURS, GOMEZ!"
\n *Suddenly pointing the rifle at your head* "You always were a fool, Ethan."
\n "It's a good thing I wore my derringer today." *quick draw and gut shot BANG*
\n "Aghggh, that's a sore one.." *Fires, widly*
\n "That's what you get, Cletus. And don't worry... I'll make sure your wife will sire a child or two... of mine." *bang bang*
\n *Staggering, coughing blood* "I tire of you" *One final decent shot*
\n "Ach. My heart is tore..." *staggering* "Gomez didn't need to come after us... we did ourselves in." *collapse*
\n
\nI like this sort of thing, for it's creative challenge, it's inherent disposability and focus, and for it's stretching of certain roleplaying muscles.
\n
\nWhat experiences have you had with improv games? Got any good ones to suggest? Stories to share about how they''ve improved your other roleplaying? Ruminations on the extent to which games like these are RPGs?

    \n I love short form roleplaying, but I don't have much experience with IRC. My group has a tendency to try Call of Cthulhu in different systems anytime we are sitting around and not playing video games. Because the mythos is a good reference point for all of us, it will often allow us to pick up different systems without needing everyone to read up on the setting material or the genre conventions of the "vanilla" setting.

    This has also lead us getting into improv and free form games using cheesy Lovecraft conventions, which has not been as successful. The problem is that a number of us tend to get too wacky when we get further and further from concrete rules.

    \n my current game chef entry Cocaine (which I need to write up) has five phases, one of which is all improv, the other four are introduced by improv games - 3 line solo, excluding, master servant disaster, and good bad and the ugly advice - then the improv-created situations are resolved with cards/bidding.

    My wife has been taking some improv classes and when i saw the class performance at the upright citizens brigade I decided to incorporate improv exercises into this project. It quickened the pace and lightened the tone of the game from my earlier conceptions, but there were still dark spots mixed in when we play tested it- and improv was a great way to build situations in a flash.
