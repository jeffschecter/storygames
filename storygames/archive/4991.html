\n In designing icons for DEAD cards, I've collected quite an eclectic bunch of symbols to put on the cards: a gun silhouette for Ammo, crossed fork and spoon for Food, a + for Recovery, a wrench for Tools, a syringe for Meds and Ye Olde Skull for, well, Dead.

Now comes the hard part: I need icons for the following concepts in DEAD. Not only are the crazy-hard to think of, but they need to be distinguishable in a 21pt font - both as objects or symbols and from each other. I also need to be able to draw the symbol in Illustrator or easily convert a picture to a black and white vector.

The game concepts are:

Weaknesses: Fear, Anger and Sorrow

Strengths: Guts, Cool and Hope (Cool can be replaced in opposition to Anger if you can think of a better word)

Fight and Flight

Action Points

and finally: Relationships (as in: "between people")

I'm thinking of a pair of wings for Flight and a raised fist for Fight, but the rest are stumping me. Any suggestions you have will be great - and if you provide me with an image to use, you can be on your very own DEAD card.

    \n Kuma, I love icon (and logo) design... but I'd need more info to be useful to you.

    * How "stylized" is your game; does it have a default (or requisite) setting and tone?

    * Will the icons always be presented in high contrast (ex: monochrome black on white or vice versa), or will they occasionally have to be presented in lower contrast (monochrome black on gray; color[s] on white)?
    The latter means you need icons which avoid the use of fine lines, hatching, or details.

    * Finally, what do some of these terms mean?
    Cool in a horror or wartime game would evoke a very different icon than Cool in a Hollywood action movie or teenagers game.

    Want to help... need your help to do so;

    \n Cool, thanks for the reply... though now I may have little help to offer.

    Random notes on your current full set of icons:
    * Fight doesn't look like it's part of the set. I'd make the Fight fist more solid, with less detail. (ex: http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Image:Fist.png)

    * Guts looks too much like Dead, at a glance; and what's a gas mask got to do with courage, anyway? I'd go with a basic stick figure guy (as you've already introduced that style with Run) and then show him standing in the classic superhero pose (ex: http://m3mnoch.files.wordpress.com/2007/02/windowslivewriterabitofillustrativefun-a222superhero-small6.jpg - minus the cape).

    * I like how your "item" icons are thinner and a bit more detailed than the "attributes" icons. I might, however, "bulk them up" a touch--not enough to make them dark and fat like the others, but just a bit, for clarity of some of the details (ex: the fork's tines, the syringe's needle).

    * I'd also fatten up Action, to put it more in the "attributes" class of icons and less in the "items" class of icons (I am guessing that's an appropriate assignment amongst those two rather nebulous subsets).

    * Cool bugs me, I don't know why, and I can't think of better. Fear runs a close second in that bothersome feeling. But talk about two TOUGH terms to iconify....

    Looking good, though; I very much like the general card design you linked above... uh, though I'd lighten the red background splatter, to help text contrast OR I'd change your card text font to one with heavier strokes, stems, and bars.

    HTH;
