\n I just wanted to put a post out here to say "thanks" to everyone I gamed with a this year's Dreamation. There was
such an incredible amount of great gaming that went on this weekend that I have to say, it was probably the best
convention experience I've ever had. I also got to meet a lot of new people this year and was able to put some faces
to the names I've seen on here for awhile.

Some highlights for me:

* Tales of the Fisherman's Wife: The creepy dead ghost baby caught in the fisherman's net. Thanks, Matt.
* Misery Bubblegum: Watching a gay/straight love triangle somehow spring up around a character that started as a
one-dimensional NPC in our first story.
* Trail of Cthulhu: Greta Garbo chanting over a dodecahedron in a not-yet-finished observatory in the Hollywood hills.
* Freemarket: THIS one really stands out for me: going crazy trying to copy a teddybear-biomorph based on my character's
ex-boyfriend, and watching as they all come out of the matter printer shouting recriminations in unison.
And THEN getting frownied.
* Cold Sleep: Watching the holographic resurrection of our dead psychologist trying to explain to the ship's priest that
he shouldn't care for a robot as if it were a real person, completely oblivious to the fact that she now considers herself
human.

What were your favorite moments (both gaming and non) that happened at this year's Dreamation?

    \n Three categories of favorite moments:

    1) The people:
    No offense to y'all's internet personalities, but you are much cooler in person. The entire con felt like one big reuinion with old friends, even though, for most people, I'd met you twice, once, or never.

    2) The playing:
    1st Quest: I refused an order I deemed dishonorable, and then was told I'd passed a test. A great unplanned synergy of responsive ad-lib. I worried I'd derailed the plot, but Judd found a way to make it push the plot forward.
    Don't Rest Your Head: killing the horrible truth with desperate lies; fighting an evil symphony that was never finished; blasting a feeble silence monster with jet engine-volume kazoo shrieks
    Grey Ranks: a girl asked to kill for the cause, flashing back to her friend beating her up in a crazed rage, and executing the messiest assassination ever
    Houses of the Blooded: Matt drunkenly yelling he'd steal the Duke's property -- as a diversion for us stealing the Duke's property, a still-beating heart in the shrine of a saint-in-waiting
    PtA: Cold Sleep: the techie struggling for status improves the creaky robot -- to the point where it can replace him

    3) The ideas and designs-in-progress:
    The way relationships and status change (or threaten to) with respect to various contexts during resolution in Bill White's The Great City.
    The way caveman color and adventure meshes with family squabbling in Dave Cleaver's Prehistoric Ties to produce a combo of reflection and hilarity.
    The way Anna, who's been gaming for 7 months, got the whole roundtable crowd stoked with her idea for a game about creating a new religion by living through what will be its Bible. (Someone suggested Writing Exodus as a title.)
    The way Mendel is finding better and better applications for his Coming of Age system ("illegitimate sons of James Bond cleaning up dad's mess" got a huge reaction).
    The roundtable forcing so many cool ideas on Ryan Macklin that he had to reconsider his decision to give up on his Addiction game (I didn't catch the title).
    Brainstorming the best ways to turn scene objects into action movie props for Matt Weber's Showdown.

    I think we all now know the Tevis Principle: Any game title is made funnier by replacing one of the words with "wang"

    \n

    ... though strictly speaking, it should be called the Tevis Wang.

    \n

    For me, it's all about the people.

    \n
      \n
      • \n
    • Tim Kleinert showed up and ran a typically excellent (if short) game of The Mountain Witch, selling his last five hardcopies at the IPR booth.
      • \n
    • He brought his friend Mike who had never tried any of this Indie stuff, found that he was in over his head, and then discovered that he could totally swim. He showed up to play Shock: later, too.
      • \n
    • Jared, who I hadn't seen in a really long time, sat with me and helped me work some shit out about Xenon: that promises to solve the problems the game had before with astounding. In a lot of meaningful ways, Jared's and my design philosophies are opposed, but we found sme really fruitful common ground. I think this game is going to start to happen for real over the next few months.
      • \n
    • I got to hang out with Remi, even though we never managed to be at a game table together. The fact that I regret not playing with him is a testament to just how fun he is to play with. Fortunately, I prioritized sitting and talking with him, which is even better.
      • \n
    \n

    I've also started to wonder about where Shock: is in its cycle of interest. Any doubts I had about its viability as a commercial endeavor were put to rest. There were 12 players for the game and there was some pretty good gnarl going on. At the second session, we shared the Shock: again \u2014 this time, Alternative Biology, that is, aliens from Earth \u2014 and addressed issues of abortion rights, social inequality, and governments that don't care about learning and exploration. I haven't played in a long time and I got to see some of the flaws in the game with new eyes. I have some new ways to explain things that I might integrate into the next print run.

    \n I love reading these!

    *carry: trying to work out how Jason (Temple's) watermelon crate booby trap would actually work. We settled on making it look like it had fallen from a supply helicopter or truck to avoid suspicion. Then, with perfect comic timing, Paul goes, "When they come, we should jump out from behind and yell "Supplies!!!"' I nearly cried from laughing which made narrating the follow-up failure rather difficult.

    *Misery Bubblegum: as with Remi: all of it. We squeezed in three episodes which really started to flesh out our characters and their world into this super-entertaining teenage soap opera. One amusing exchange involved two girls angsting over the secret that their friend was gay (and the impact it would have), but he'd told them in confidence separately. "Are you ok? What's wrong?!" "I'm not supposed to say! Do...do you know?" "I'm not supposed to say!"

    *Jeepform: Happy Ends: Kevin Allen Jr. playing a super-awkward, defensive, sexually-confused teenager with an imaginary friend. Brilliant and absolutely hilarious.

    *Jeepform: The Upgrade: all of it! My character was pretty flawed going in, had some fun, but hit new lows, took a bad record deal, and didn't get picked by anyone. Thanks to the epilogue monologue, though, she actually ended up ok in a way that totally made sense. I did not expect that, and I love how this game surprises me.

    *Grey Ranks: Whoa. We all rolled so poorly in our third episode of the session that despite drawing on everything we held dear, we achieved a Total Party Kill. I think my favorite moment was just before the the Grey Ranks destroyed themselves. Ryan's character Slaz is meeting my character Nina in the rubble of a park. They're both in a really low place after the relationship between them hasn't been working and so much of their exhausting progress in the Gray Ranks has been for nothing. He puts his arm around her and she slumps into him, letting him carry the weight of her world if only for a moment. It was really subtle and touching.

    *FreeMarket: totally earning the Frownie my friend gave me for kicking him when he was down. I'm such a jerk.

    *Shock: seeing the above-mentioned Mike go from being nervous about his "traditional" gaming backround to an Antagonist with Robert DeNiro acting skillz. Neat!

    *In a Wicked Age: using my serpent demon Queen NPC to seduce her son's girlfriend before giving the poor couple her blessing.

    Also:
    *Super yummy palak panir and rosemary naan!
    *Conversing excitedly about Burning Empires on more than one occasion to Daniel, Thor, and several other poor innocent bystanders!
    *Sneak-attack and flank hugging!
    *Serious and hilarious conversations about Jeepform with Tobias, Emily, and Terry! Jeepform miniatures! It's all KAJ's fault!
    *It was all good - it really, really was! I'm still after-glowing!
