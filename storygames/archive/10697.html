\n I started an exercise, but there's a lot of babble there, and I've made some changes to the exercise, so I'm starting a new thread where I talk less.

Three things to note, before beginning: these are the Dystopia Style Sheets used in Perfect, and I'm test-running them here; "control axioms" are the fundamental beliefs upon which dystopic laws are built; "conditioning" means brainwashing.

Fill out a style sheet:

1.) In three words, this society is ___________, ___________, ___________.
(blanks could be: steampunk, Victorian, futuristic, industrialized, modern, mostly rural, mostly urban, European, religious, capitalist...)

2.) It's like ______________ meets _______________.
(blanks could be: Blade Runner, Clockwork Orange, 1984, Dexter, Handmaid's Tale, Fahrenheit 451, Equilibrium, A Scanner Darkly, Survivor, any genre fic...)

3.) The three control axioms are ______________, ______________, ________________.
(blanks could be: Love is Vulgar, Colour is Banned, Women Are Weak, Violence Is Always Wrong, Immigrants Are Dangerous, Happiness Is Mandatory...)

4.) Name five social organizations in this society.
(Names, not descriptions. Anything that isn't "the government".)

5.) Who enforces law? _______________________________
(blank could include: inspectors clad in black, heavily armed riot police, women who pose as librarians)

6.) What technologies do they use to condition? _________________________
(blank could include: dehumanization tactics, psychic projections, neural uploading, electroshock therapy, waterboarding, memory flushes, hypnosis, starvation, physical torture, media barrages, implanting microchips...)

    \n 1.) In three words, this society is: green, ludite and isolationist.

    2.) It's like Earth Liberation Front meets Judge Dredd.

    3.) The three control axioms are: Technology Must Be Purged, Earth First and Enforcers Are Always Right.

    4.) Five social organizations: The Trappers Guild, The Border Patrol, Natural Arts Museum, Advocates of Savage Law, The Disassemblers Guild.

    5.) Who enforces law? The Disassemblers (commonly: Purgers) are responsible for violations of tech possession, and the Advocates of Savage Law (commonly: Savage Lawyers) are responsible for violations of social norms and environmental trespasses.

    6.) What technologies do they use to condition? tying you to a tree in the wilds, starvation, dehydration, dehumanization tactics, fire.

    Notes:
    I'm imagining a society that is intentionally and aggressively destroying its tech in favor of a sustainable, green, environmentally-prioritized lifestyle. While this seems good, they are destroying tech which its citizens depend on - stuff like prescription medications, food processing tools, vehicles, information channels. Their response: deal with it or we will kill you.

    \n Ah why not? This is an actual project I'm currently working on:

    1.) In 'three' words (ok, five) this society is culturally stagnant, overregulated and politically correct.

    2.) It's like 'Jet Set Radio Future' meets '1984'

    3.) The three control axioms are 'Personal Freedom breeds Discontent', 'Efficiency is God' and 'People are their Own Worst Enemy'

    4.) Name five social organizations in this society. - still in development. So far there's only really Cybo Corp, and they're not *that* important. Everyone else is either a rebel or owned by the government. I guess there's always the House of Troy, the Seventh Street Harlequins and so on but they're really all part of the same group, just different chapters of it.

    5.) Who enforces law? Jobsworth police officers who are by and large as blameless as anyone else; they just have a job to do.

    6.) What technologies do they use to condition? Misinformation, questionable psychology and the propaganda machine
