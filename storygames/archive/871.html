Playtesting Hurts

Levi Kornelsen
June 2006 edited June 2006 in Story Games
Working on a game called The Cog Wars. The game itself isn't the topic here (though feel free to ask about it).

Playtesting is. I've gone through about five completely rewritten versions of the playtest document, tested each, and it keeps getting prettier, simpler, and harder to rewrite. I've been at this for over a month, and I'm picking up the pace this week - I'll likely have three seperate and complete test games this week alone.

Gah.

For those of you that have managed to finally get your games done, how many trips through playtest-and-revise did it take you?

    I'd say twenty is the sweet spot for iterative playtesting - that's about what The Roach got as well. That includes blind tests I had nothing to do with, convention play with strangers, local play with gamers and a session with non-gamers.     I'll also point out that design and AP feedback on The Forge was huge for me, and having smart people think about and comment on your ideas and concerns, even outside the context of playtesting, is really useful.

    Back in the depths of the FLFS playtest, I actually ran or played in four games in the space of three days at one point. It was around playtest #20 that I got the sense that I'd beaten on the thing enough times to tease out all its problems and address them.
