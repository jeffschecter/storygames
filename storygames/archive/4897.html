\n I've been binging on anime lately, specifically that of the giant robot genre, and in particular, Macross (the original TV series, not Robotech), Macross Plus, the Gundam TV series, Gunbuster, Tetsujin 28-go, Voltron, Evangelion and RahXephon.

I'd like to create a a game where you play the pilots of these robots, obviously fighting in some sort of war. I'd like to leave it up to the players to create their setting, a la BW's world burning in it's varied incarnations. I'd also like to focus on the relationships between the characters in a big way, with lots of rivalries and love triangles messing things up. This all gets acted out against a backdrop of war, and the battles need to have a certain flashiness. In those battles, what I'd like to avoid is hardcore tactical sim stuff like in Heavy Gear or Mekton, but there needs to be some element of tactics in the battles (and maybe some gear fetish-ism).I don't think anyone would actually die in battle, unless it served the story in some way.

I've looked at Bliss Stage as well and while I like the concept, the execution doesn't quite work for me. Perhaps I don't want something so unrelentingly grim? Or at least a scalable level of angst?

Anyway, it's an idea I'd like to be able to try and flesh out a bit more. I'm just not sure how or where to start. So I'm throwing this out there - if anyone has any suggestions, fire away!

    \n To make Bliss Stage less grim, consider giving pilots an interlude for every mission goal they succeed at, instead of just one per entire mission. And possibly use tokens to keep track of those, and let players use them to give other people interludes, or give them to each other in appreciation. At each new mission, every pilot discards one token, if they have any left.

    With more interludes to reduce stress and build trust and intimacy, the death spiral won't be as steep.

    For more grim, watch Bokurano.

    Or consider using Polaris?

    Tactics doesn't really seem to be a strong point of any giant robot shows I know of, although something where you could choose degree of recklessness would be useful.

    That's a good list of shows; I'm glad to see you're ignoring Gasaraki and hope you'll continue to do so. But do seek out Gunbuster 2 for over the top giant robot action.

    As Wei-Hwa Huang once said:
    "Giant anthropomorphics have always been popular in Japanese pop culture. Godzilla, for instance. It may have to do with the conflict between a desire to be powerful and an aversion to being known -- in a mecha
    suit, nobody knows you're really a teenager with glasses."

    So maybe consider Best Friends, substituting "Pilot" for "Rich", unless Piloting is just a matter of "Cool".

    Anyway, I think Jake has spotted my target - gear fetishism! I like the focus on the equipment provided by games like Heavy Gear and Mekton, but in play, the systems move far, far, far too slowly (they are essentially wargames) to emulate the fast action of anime, despite the efforts of their designers to go quickly.

    So, I'm looking to maybe make my own game here instead of playing an existing one. I've got a specific feel I'm after too, which Andy pegged - military drama, and I definitely know how to do that. I was in a game of Carry earlier this year and elements of that experience may find their way into this as well.

    \n To be totally unconstructive--I'm a huge fan of the real robo genre (eg, "military stuff", as opposed to super robo), and I found Gasaraki to be possibly the most sleep-inducing anime I've ever seen.

    On the other hand, I see nobody has yet mentioned VOTOMS, which is the awesomest ever(tm). (If, uh... grim.)

    Being such, I've also always wanted to play a good mecha game, but when I stop to think about it, I can't actually figure out what I'd want out of it. I mean, essentially, the robots as such are just equipment, they could as well have been armor or magic weapons or what have you, except for the fact that Giant Robots Look Cool, and such purely visual appeals to my mind isn't really something that, for me, adds much or works very well for an rpg.

    So what, really, is it that they bring in terms of story and feel that one wants to include? (When not approaching it from a traditional tactical/simulation perspective, which there are plenty of decent games for.)

    The one game I've seen that actually does something interesting with it is Bliss Stage, but BS is very much a different genre and very super robo. (I'd consider Evangelion solidly super robo--the superficial plot is very much a homage to old-style unique super-mecha fighting unique Giant Space Monsters to Save The World--even if technically the mecha are militarily produced.)
