    But the mechanical differences for gender and [real world] religion are really problematic (try making a pagan swordswoman! Or the fact that Latin and Hebrew are both magical languages but Latin is just plain better) and a lot of the setting info ranges from goofy (European Renaissance + Elves, Orcs, Goblins , Dwarves and Halflings) to just weird (there's a ton of historical German Principalities but Scandinavia is some sort of ahistorical weirdo Viking land) to potentially problematic (the Ottoman Empire is run by Goblins) to outright offensive (all Basques are Occultists). The system looks interesting, but even there I wonder how well it has been playtested (look at the revised rules for The Tower).
