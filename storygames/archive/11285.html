\n Based off my 4E hack mentioned here I've been porting things over to a Maho Shoujo setting. Here is one of the character's new story (daily) powers, his power source is Music, he is an American exchange student who transforms into a boy band singer:

Love Song - Story - Item of Power, Social, Love, Music
Stunt: Lying, Friendship
Indirect (ranged) - Event (any where in the encounter)
Target: One Foe
Attack: Charisma vs Esteem
Hit: 2[I] + Charisma modifier music and love damage, and choose up to your Charisma modifier friends or positions (sides in conflicts), this foe cannot attack them (save ends).
Miss: half damage and choose one friend or position, this foe cannot attack them until the end of your next turn.
Effect: until the end of the event you and your target gain vulnerable 5 love.

Note, [I] stands for the damage die of the character's Item of Power, which is used for this power.

I've been wanting to do a magical girls 4th edition hack ever since I saw the first previews and now it's finally coming together.

    \n A few other notes on the power:

    The Social keyword means that if you reduce the target's Resolve (i.e. hit points) to 0 with it, they relent to you socially. So you can actually have someone fall in love with you using the love song.

    The stunt line provides alternate uses for the power, in this case for conflicts using either or both of the Friendship or Lying skills. Conflicts use the same defense scales as combat and social, and have positions with Resolve ratings as well, so you could, for example, stunt your Love Song power into a conflict (say Academics, Acrobatics, and Lying) with your history teacher in order to free your fellow magical soldiers from detention.

    The power could then attack and damage the position - "You stay in detention." and protect the position "You escape to the school courtyard." Which may help ensure you get to fight the monsters of the week away from the history teacher and the rest of the students.
