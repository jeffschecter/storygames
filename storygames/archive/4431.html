\n I thought it could be useful to compile hippy mechanics that are useful no matter what game you play; a sort of return to the generic meets toolbox. It's all swell and good with all this play focused on a specific premise, but I also like interesting rules that can be cut'n pasted into home brews and generic rules. I am aware that the ones I mention are well known on these boards, the point is to have a list, and by example show what i mean. So the ones that spring immediately to mind would be:

The madness mechanics from Unknown armies. Useful whenever your game focuses on the spiral of madness or the consequence of acting unlike normal, non-murdering people. (Use: Horror gaming)
Available through Nemesis/ORE:
http://arcdream.com/pdf/Nemesis.pdf

The Monologue of Victory. A handy way to relinquish some GM authority, for fun and unexpectancy. This idea could be used instead of critical hits for example. It's described in the pool. http://www.randomordercreations.com/thepool.htm

\xe2\u20ac\u0153Kickers & Bangs,\xe2\u20ac\x9d A kicker is events that the player designs to kick off the campaign, a puzzle or situation that play is meant to resolve. Basically it\xe2\u20ac\u2122s a way to have a victory condition. Bangs are events that gives play an exciting kick-off, or GM hook. These are useful to include in character generation. Sorcerer talks about them and buying that would also give me rules for evil- summoning based magic.

So could anyone be bothered to give me more examples and where to get/ purchase them?
If the mechanic/ idea can be described usefully in a post that is of course fine as long as no theft of intellectual property goes on.

    But it seems it wouldn't be any case of buying 2 editions of the same game with different settings.(?)
    \nWell, the core resolution system and the fundamentals are the same between both games: You have Beliefs, Instincts and Traits. You roll a pool of dice against a GM-set number to determine success or failure. You negotiate intent and consequences before rolling. Circles and Resources are approximately the same, although we use the BE Circles modifiers in my BW game. The Duel of Wits system is approximately the same. Beyond that, the games diverge in interesting ways.

    There are, in fact, several little upgrades in BE that work well in BW -- I posted a thread about BW2.5 on the BW forums some time ago that may be of interest to you.

    Monster Burner is interesting but probably not useful without BW.
