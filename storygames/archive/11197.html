    \n\nDecember 2009 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n My dad, who continues to actively prepare for the collapse of civilization, stockpiles sewing needles. Easy to carry, hard to fabricate and an urgent necessity - a perfect barter item.

    Items pre-industrial people were obsessed with: Footwear and the means to make and repair it. Salt as a preservative and way to disguise unpleasant flavors. Practical clothing that you can repair and replace, that keeps you warm and dry.

    Twenty years after the apocalypse there will be no more shoes, no more Gore-Tex, and no more cheap iodized salt (goiter returns to the iodine-poor upper midwest and intermountain west).

    Abundant vehicle tires will be cannibalized as boot soles. Wool, and the skills necessary to refine it into clothing, will be in huge demand. Natural salt sources will be well-protected trade enclaves.

    So my shopping cart has all kinds of needles, a well-stocked cobbler's tool set, maps of rich natural NaCl deposits, parts for a wheel and a treadle loom, a carding drum, a bunch of lambs, and an automatic machetegun.

    \n I figure I need two things. Shit to survive the first two years and an invaluable trade to practice once I hook up with a village. And I think a shopping cart is about fifteen cubic feet plus the big shelf underneath.

    A cubic foot of seeds. Two cubic feet of excellent hand tools. Three cubic feet of ammunition. Three cubic feet of books on low-tech survival, shelter, clay-composition and kiln-building. Three cubic feet of vacuum-packed cold-weather clothing. Numerous bungee cords. A few fresnel lenses and both flat and parabolic mirrors. My pistols and a new over-under rifle-shotgun. A crank-powered AM/FM/SW radio with light. An eReader full of low-tech survival, shelter, gardening, farming, and medicine as well as history, fiction, etc and a solar charger built to last twenty years. Very good cutlery and a whetstone. Two small tents. And as much cord, plastic bags, scissors, needles, antibiotics and iodine as I could fit in the cart.

    I once made money as a potter (not my primary income, but income), so I think I could get up to "invaluable" pretty quickly. I could also build most of what I need in the way of professional tools from plans that I currently own and scavenged hardware.

    I'm sure I'm missing vital stuff, too.
