\n[Note: The image below (a screaming face) was originally an image of blood dripping down a white background. It was pointed out that this bore a resemblance to the cover art of Dread, a game published by The Impossible Dream. To avoid confusion, I changed the cover art for my book, Dread: The First Book of Pandemonium. The new cover is what you will see posted below. Sorry about the confusion!]

Finally!

I've been working on this new edition of Dread, off-and-on, for about three or four years. But real nuts-and-bolts production didn't start until last summer. I just opened up the store on Lulu (http://www.lulu.com/content/1202640), and I'm dying to hear what people think of the new edition.

The first edition had some issues (questionable art decisions, editing issues, low-tech layout). Dread was an indie game on a shoestring budget, and it showed. I wanted the Unrated Edition to resolve all of these issues, so I spent a lot of time and money on the art, the layout, and the editing. This is the new cover:



Here's some of the artwork (the Qolishuul demon):



And this is some of the digital artwork that I think adds so much to the game:



So what do you guys think? Cool cover? Better than the first edition? Does anyone remember the first edition...?

-- Rafael

    \n Hi, all,

    This is the email that I sent to Epidiah this morning. I am reprinting it here because it's the quickest way to explain:\n


    Hello,

    I would like to apologize for my mistake. It's been brought to my attention that the cover of my new edition of Dread: TFBOP looks a lot like your game. This was not my intention. When the cover artists presented the image to me, my first thought was of Unsane, a band whose album covers often feature blood-splattered walls and crime scenes. I thought, cool. This is what my game is all about. Blood and death.

    However, it was thoughtless of me not to consider how this resemblance to your game's cover might cause some confusion. I'm sorry about that. I will correct the problem immediately.

    Incidentally, I was at Trinoc*coN yesterday, and heard many great things about your game. Matt McFarlane (an artist with White Wolf) ran a session of Dread (your version), and the players enjoyed it immensely. I wish you and your team continued success.

    Warmest regards,
    Rafael Chandler

    \n I'll have to do a thorough review later, but I got my hands on a copy of Dread: Pandemonium, and I must say:

    I'm fucking impressed. Before, I used to tell people about this awesome game "Dread", and when they got it they found most of the awesome was not in the book, but in the mind of the game creator but never made it into the book: Rather The Awesome was only expressed in actual gameplay with the designer, and passed from one gamer to another that way.

    Not anymore. I've spent the last 90 minutes reading through it, and it's got all that awesome shit Right There in the Book. GM advice, scenario construction, how to really use and utilize the demons, it's all there. The awesome is in the book. If you want gruesome action-horror, make this yours.

    I'll review it for reals later, after GenCon maybe. Plan on demoing this a lot at the Games on Demand booth.

    Thus far, I only found 3 hiccups:

    There was one place (I forget where) that says "The the" when it meant to say "Then the".

    The other thing is that I looked at the weapon chart: Short Bow, Long Bow and Crossbow does 0 damage. But an arrow does 1 damage. I had this image of a disciple saying "fuck it" and just stabbing demons and mobsters and drug dealers with arrows held in clenched fists.

    One question point: In the skill example with Scalpel: "She has a Medical Examiner skill of 5, therefore she rolls 6 dice to examine the body..." Why 6 dice and not 5? Seems to be a deliberate choice, but it's not explained why unless I missed something (perhaps Scalpel's player described it "cool" for the Cool Die? Or do skills work by giving you a number of dice to add to a 1-die default roll?)

    Demons rock. Wrath kicks ass. Fury is glorious, especially Cock Punch. Death Spiral makes it so it is impossible to create a "bad" scenario. Great stuff.

    -Andy
