\n MLwM is one of my favourite games and it always goes down a storm at cons when I run it for newbies and veterans alike, but I've never run it as more than a single session of play.It's currently on the MKRPG's wish-list of 'games we'd like campaigns of' and I'm tempted to offer it, but I'm not sure how it would work as an up-to-eight-week campaign.
\n
\nWho's played it in multi-session mode? What sort of numbers are appropriate for Fear & Reason? How do you keep the tension going and the relationships bubbling over that timescale? What sort of balance of scenes works best? And any other wisdom gained from practical experience would be greatly appreciated.

    \n My Life With Master is not really for one-shots. If you don't fidget with the rules (giving starting love points, for example) it's almost impossible to play in one slot, and even if you do, you have to run it quickly, over situations and scenes that would work better with more time to play them

    So, I suggest: simply follow the rules. Stop using the quick-play shortcut used in convention one-shot and play the game as it's written Take your time playing the character interaction. Use the time to increase the depths of the master's villainy that you can show. Make your NPC real (I can't believe some people think this is something you have to do only in AW...) and play them as individuals.

    The normal duration of the-game-as-written is about 2-4 sessions (6-8 hours of play), depending on your group's habits. If you choose a hight fear value and a low reason value the duration can be even much longer, but I don't suggest searching to play "long" as an objective: play the game playing the characters, and follow them to the natural conclusion, without hurrying or delaying the Master's death

    \n Yes, the game is designed for several-session play, but the text also includes rules for single-session play. It's not "almost impossible to play in one slot," as I've done it successfully many, many times. I've played some extremely creepy and satisfying games in 4 hours.

    However, I do believe you that play will be better if you let things play out over a few sessions. I just disagree that one-shot MLwM games require you to "fidget with the rules," since the rules tell you how to do exactly this (Single-session Play, page 52; Fear and Reason, page 17).
