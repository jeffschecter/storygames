\nUPDATE:

The Spreadshirt store is now open for business. Go here to purchase a shirt with the Go Play Monkey King graphic:

Go Play Monkey King Shirt Store

The store is set up with a few pre-made designs, but if you don't see the exact thing you want, you can fire up the Spreadshirt product editor and make your own!

Go Play Northwest gets $7 for every product you buy, which we will use to pay for the 2008 GPNW event and future game gatherings.

Note to GPNW 2008 attendees:
You'll be getting high-quality American Apparel shirts with a slightly different and special design. Black ink, in olive or cranberry (your choice). If you'd rather order a different color combo from the website, let me know and I'll refund your pre-order shirt payment when you arrive.

-----

So, we're doing a new shirt for Go Play NW this year. The idea is for the shirt to also be a generic/cool story games & Go Play shirt for a wider audience, too. Here's the design ("Monkey King Sasquatch"):



Questions:
- What do you think about the banner slogan? "Say Yes Or Roll The Dice" is pretty good, but maybe there's something better. Suggestions welcome.

- Olive or Cranberry?




(People who have pre-ordered shirts can potentially pick any color shirt they want, but for the extras I print up, I need to pick a color, probably. I'm leaning towards cranberry, despite its lack of Go Play Green.)

These shirts will be available for purchase outside of Go Play NW, in the next week or so. Proceeds go to helping to pay for current and future GPNW events.

      \n I just got mine from the Spreadshirt site. I went with a burgandy T and forest green printing.
      \n
      \nThe dark green on a dark red shirt has that nice low contrast look that makes people have to ask what it says cuz its only readable up close.
      \n
      \nIn dim light the green print looks more charcoal, but when the light hits it at the right angle it shimmers into a definite green in a very cool effect, that will probably fade after a couple of washes dull the finish.
