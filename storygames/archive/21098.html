\n Hi everyone! Here with a little shameless self-promotion

KARMA, the brand new story roleplaying game from Frenzy Kitty Games is now live on Indiegogo!


https://igg.me/at/karmarpg


The game is designed for 3-5 players and each session plays in about 2 hours. Additionally, there is no game master required - each player takes turns framing and directing scenes and having agency over the narrative.\xa0

KARMA features a brand new gmless engine known as the KARMA system. In this system\xa0other players will get to add a complication\xa0to your scenes (thus keeping everyone engaged in every scene, even when their character is not present) and each player tracks their KARMA throughout the game, which will have an impact on how their finale plays out. It is a \xa0game where good and bad people get what they deserve... usually.

    \n Hi Jeff.

    Thanks for the response. In a nutshell, yes

    The nudges one way or the other are extremely subtle and the system is fairly robust. Also, your character starts out with more good than bad karma so it would be fairly easy to game it into your favour. The KARMA system is a very gentle engine and the complications are generally smaller twists that lead to a better story all round They are rarely catastrophic for a character, more like little bumps in the road.

    Also remember that these complications are the things that affect your Karma Pool.

    For example, if your character kills someone another player could introduce the complication of the alarm potentially triggering. There's a (close to) 50-50 chance of that complication going either way. If you decided to not improve your chances and left your karma pool unaffected and the alarm did go off, you would then roleplay that situation out. Maybe you had a close escape? Maybe a security guard caught you and you had to kill him too? The way that goes is up to you as the active player.

    Remember, you only earn bad karma if you sway the odds of the alarm not going off heavily in your favour.

    2986AD

    You are all crew members aboard the space vessel
    known as the I.S.V. GEMINI. 24 hours ago,a fellow
    space vessel known as the I.S.V. ICARUS began
    emitting an automated mayday signal which lasted
    for 8 hours before cutting out. Right before the
    transmission went silent,there was a burst of static
    and a voice on the radio which left all who heard it
    visibly shaken.

    It is your job to find out what happened on the
    ICARUS. Your crew can see her,hanging dark and
    ominously in the void of space as your ship
    carefully approaches...

    It\u2019s time to discover the truth. One thing you all
