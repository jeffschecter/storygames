    \n I can answer this! (On account of I was writing it.)

    There were a lot of things I liked about Console Legends. Unfortunately, there was one major flaw:

    The primary game mechanic.

    It was very difficult to get a game where people actually a) described things that filled in each others' visions, and b) told a semi-coherent story. The central conceit of the game utterly crashed and burned on the first playtest. I'd be glad to post it if anyone was interested in working on it. If the core of the game could be fixed, I think it still has some cool ideas.

    --Colin

    \n The way the mechanic is written is as follows: Each person writes N "visions" for the game session; things they'd like to see happen in the game. When someone else describes one of those things happening, the person gets more dice to throw at the boss monster later on. The design goal was to have people foreshadowing like mad and then incorporating each other's ideas.

    The way the mechanic worked in playtest was as follows: Not at all. No one even got close to describing someone else's Vision, and people seemed to have this thing against involving each other's creations. It was an occasionally amusing two hours, but it was a waste as far as gameplay went, and the story created during play was... nonexistent. Very disappointing.
