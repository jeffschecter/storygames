\n
\n
\n
\nRequires:
\n- 2+ willing males
\n- with functioning and average or larger size nuts
\n- a 1+ pound throwing ball
\n
\nWin Conditions:
\n- someone wins when everyone else is eliminated
\n- you are eliminated when you have zero points
\n
\nSetup:
\n- sit on the floor
\n- 15 feet from all other players
\n- face the player to your left
\n- spread out your legs
\n- exposing your nuts
\n- place your hands on your legs
\n- you have 5 points
\n- choose arbitrarily who starts (usually the person with the least nut ball experience)
\n
\nPlay:
\n- look at the person to your left
\n- make eye contact
\n- violently throw the ball at their nuts
\n- they can try to stop the ball by grabbing or blocking it with their hands
\n- for every hand they use, they lose 1 point
\n- so if they grab or block the ball with 1 hand, they lose 1 point
\n- if they use 2 hands, the lose 2 points
\n- if they leave their hands on their legs, their points remain the same
\n- the person who had a ball thrown at their nuts now goes
\n- that person throws the ball at the person to their left (using all the rules described above)
\n- when someone has 0 points, they are eliminated and much leave the game
\n
\nDisclaimer:
\n- we are not responsible for any damages inflicted upon your person for playing nut ball

    \n jenskot,

    Thank you for introducing Jeepform to the American, uh, sensibility.

    Not only is this a most elegant implementation of cutting-edge techniques (no conflict resolution mechanics, no randomizers, no traits, no character sheets, practically zero-prep), not only is it amazingly flexible (easily portable to any setting, any number of players, and adaptable to all sorts of different objects in place of the ball), but it uses basic human drives and instincts as its basis (basic hand-eye coordination, motor skills, and pain avoidance), meaning instantly graspable mechanics and practically zero learning curve.

    More importantly, you've answered my prayers, and solved the question I was having in my "internal conflict" thread. This game handles internal conflict INTENSELY, for every player, and can do so entirely without any narration at all. Clever.

    Do you have a playtest/demo video? I hope so. This game might not, uh, fully come across in a podcast format.

    EDIT: I highly recommend that any players of nutball pick and peruse a copy of Graham Walsley's excellent "Play Unsafe". It was written for this game.
