    Hey everyone,

    \n

    It's really exciting to see that there are still RBH fans out there. The project isn't dead, just floating in limbo right now. I haven't had many opportunities to actually play any RPGs at all this summer. Without the inspiration of actual play, I find it hard to find the motivation to do any design work.

    \n

    I have been thinking about RBH on and off all summer though. I know I really want to adjust the 'tactics' of the thing. I want them to be a little softer. The arenas and powers and combat actions should guide and color combats. The restrictions those rules provide should, ideally, be very soft and subtle.

    \n

    Anyway, I'm back into a game group for the fall at least. More play = more inspiration = more RBH. Thanks for the continued support!

    \n Great to see you back Eric.

    I like the modifications you describe on your blog about removing the connections and adding a roll to enter some difficult Arenas.
    How do you imagine the difficulty to "enter" any given zone is set : does the GM set it, the player describing the zone, is it a group agreement ? Could it be something like : "I am covered by the bushes and walking realy quietly. To spot me and enter my zone, the monster must test Alertness against my Sneakiness" ?

    This last way of doing things (attribute vs attribute) seems great to me because it could make more use of Sneakiness, Size or even any other stat given some creativity, but maybe I am overlooking some lame aspect of doing that.

    Also, a thing I wanted to test was to accept things like players using their combat action to describe how they could change the nature of the arena into another one. It seems to fit the flair you want to give to combats. For ex, rolling Size to turn over the furniture and change the arena into a Hazardous one. Maybe, just using an action and describing something the group agrees to is good enough to do that without any attribute roll. What do you think ?

    By the way, how do you prefer to receive your playtesting data : here, in your blog comments, by email... ?

    \n\nOctober 2008 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n

    How do you imagine the difficulty to "enter" any given zone is set...?

    \n

    I'm not sure yet. That's why it's taken me this long to mention the rule. We've played it a couple different ways, and none of them felt exactly right. I just know we all enjoyed the idea of hard-to-enter arenas. Maybe the Arena should be given a stat? Like, if the Arena were Sneaky +1, then any player or monster who wanted to find a way in would have to roll their Alertness vs. the Sneakiness of the Arena.

    \n

    I probably wouldn't use character vs. character rolls for that. If a really sneaky character wanted to try to hide from a blind monster, I'd let them try that in any arena. As an impromptu action, naturally.

    \n

    By the way, how do you prefer to receive your playtesting data : here, in your blog comments, by email... ?

    \n

    I like to find playtesting data here. But please feel free to send it directly to me or post it on the RBH blog as you like. I'll find it in any of these places.
