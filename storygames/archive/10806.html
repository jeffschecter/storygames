\n I was going to post this up over the weekend but the Flu has given me some extra time to play with so here we go in as coherent manner as possible.
\nThrough a couple whispers in the webcomics thread I decided to give the model a try and I think the best bet for others interested in trying it out also would be to do it together. The idea would be a loose knit series of designers independently operating but cross promoting with one another when possible.
\n
\nWhat I propose is that anyone who would like to take a project they are willing to see possibly fail and for a year support it via the webcomic model. The project would start January, and until then potential participants will plan strategy, and potential avenues of collaboration. At the end of next year everyone participating will reflect back on their experience and compile it into a Q&A; to those who may be interested in pursuing the model.
\n
\nThe stipulation would be that by the last week of December your \u201cproduct\u201d must be complete as this is for support of a product line not a development blog. So basically if you are going to be supporting a core role playing system then that system should be playable by the project start. This is not to say that your game need be in or near a final edition just fully playable.
\n
\nThis temp forum can be used to discuss general strategy, project details, and anything else relating to the challenge. I figure there are a ton of different topics to discuss about publishing strategies, merch sources that it would stick to clog SG up with it or stuff it in one thread.
\n
\nFor now I am interested in suggestions, tap ins, and criticisms. I know there is at least one other person interested, but I would love to see at least 5 people total partake.

    \n I think this is a really interesting idea.\n
    Posted By: Seth DrebitkoThe stipulation would be that by the last week of December your \u201cproduct\u201d must be complete as this is for support of a product line not a development blog. So basically if you are going to be supporting a core role playing system then that system should be playable by the project start. This is not to say that your game need be in or near a final edition just fully playable.
    \nWebcomics don't have their compilation books ready as finished products before they start posting the comics. I think you should consider easing this constraint for some serialization strategies. Your constraint makes sense for the core rules + serialized supplements model, but there ought to be other viable serialized RPG models where the constraint is less useful.

    If that constraint were relaxed, and I was able to be ready by January, here is the serialized project I would do:
    Step 1: Do NaNoWriMo with an outline generated by my game's procedures (I blogged about this plan here and here)
    Step 2A: Edit that novel, and record a reading of it. Release it as a podcast novel
    Step 2B: Record playtest sessions of my game, and release those as an actual play podcast
    Step 3: Cross-promote both podcasts, to build an audience
    Step 4: Sell T-shirts or something
    Step 5: When I have final versions ready, sell the game and the novel through Lulu or CreateSpace

    It would be pretty tough to be ready to make that happen by January, though, especially since I'm still looking for playtesters for the game, and getting a novel written and edited that quickly would be a stretch. Having a very aggressive goal might be motivation for me, though.

    \nTotally up to you it just has to be gaming related. The constraint mostly only applies if you want to provide supplements for your game.     \n- Support for another OGL system like fate, d20, ext.

    1) I need to shake out some design cobwebs. Some things I was never quite happy with - like equipment rules. Other elements aren't entirely complete, such as the Enlightenment rules.

    2) I need to break down the system and setting elements into comprehensible pieces that work well for regular year-long posting.

    3) I need to determine if I'll have the time next year to keep this going.

    \n This year I've been blogging game related stuff with an aim toward generic systems that appear in multiple games and ideas for game mechanisms that I'd like to see in upcoming games (I've even referenced a few of the folks around here over the weeks...)

    If you want to have a look, it can be found here...

    Game Mechani(sm) of the Week

    Some of the ideas have had some good comments over the weeks, others have been left alone. But on the whole it's really helped me to work my mind through some designs and find a couple of like minded designers.

    My work for this serialised game content will follow my comments in the previous related thread.

    Maybe working on it two days a week....Mondays I'll upload some kind of in-game story revealing a part of the world in the style of an ongoing ships journal/travelogue across time and space. Thursdays I'll bring out some stats and more mechanically related features that link to the week's story.

    It'll be good to see where some others will be taking the idea.
