\n [I will update the title from "a rough idea" to other stages of the production as time goes on, to keep this in one thread.]

This is my idea for a multimedia role-playing boardgame in four pages & 5MB of audio content or less. This is inspired from Sight C, Sound B and Concept A.

You play escaped prisoners in a "medieval German forest"-esque setting. The game is sort-of GM-less, in that none of the players is the GM. The GM is instead a playlist set on random. The game puts you in a life-or-death morality situation where you have to choose between helping someone else or not -- but if you don't you're more likely to remain unscathed. The experiment is trying to work in Prisoner's Dilemma at the mechanic, but instead of with two people, it's you & the playlist/GM -- with various rewards/penalties.

Creating characters:
* What is your name?
* All the characters are guilty of a crime -- a crime that did in fact commit. What crime did you commit?
* How did you get caught?
* You owe a favor or are in debt to the guy on your right. Why? You & the guy on your right should hash this out together. Remember: the guy on your left owe you as well.

As a group, you also create your Friend. He's an NPC who was imprisoned like yourself, and orchestrated the breakout. The beginning of the game involves playing an intro audio file that sets the stage for the game: the Friend is bit by something, seems to be dying, tells you to the run for church, then starts growling -- a human growl, nothing more. Your Friend becomes the monster that will hunt you down, but the game will always refer to him as Your Friend and never describes his appearance.

The game is played on a map -- the physical layout of which I haven't completely defined. On one end is where you start, on the other is where the Church is -- the safe haven you're trying to flee towards. Whatever evil is in this forest may not pass the Church's threshold.

The Crunchiness Handles:
* Guilt Tokens -- you start with one Guilt Token. Depending on the choices you make, you'll gain more Guilt Tokens. (Earlier I was considering that any damage you take by Your Friend is expressed as Guilt Tokens, as only the Guilty are harmed. But I decided against this while writing this post.)

* Penance Tokens -- you start with zero Penance Tokens, and may gain them depending on the choices you make -- even if it gets you wounded, you can earn Penance Tokens for helping a friend. (Which is why I don't want Wounds to equal Guilt, because then it'll be a bit zero-sum if I'm looking to compare Guilt & Penance tokens.)

* Wound Tokens -- you start with zero, and you die when you get some number, like five or ten. (And perhaps if you die, the Strength of the attacks increases -- you get one Wound to start because there's only one monster, but when you die there's another monster. But, that might be pushing too much on helping someone for your own benefit.)

* Map Location -- Where you are on the map is something that the game can play with. You can only move if your choice lets you.

Endgame:
There are two triggers for Endgame:
* When there are fewer than two prisoners on the board (i.e. alive and not in the church).
* When an audio track starts with church bells, and there's at least one person in the church.

With the latter, you load up another randomized playlist that has three endings, and you play one:
(1) [Safe Haven ending] Those in the Church win.
(2) [Forgiveness of God ending] Those with more Penance than Guilt win (dead or alive).
(3) [I don't have a title for this one] Those in the Church with more Penance than Guilt win.

    \n Design goals:

    * Playable in 60-90 minutes
    * Easy to play -- the game walks you through playing with minimal (but probably some) pre-understanding.
    * Facilitates highly immersive gameplay -- I want to make an immersive board game, and it seems a good way to achieve that is to also make it a role-playing game and to engage the players' senses via the audio files.
    * Doesn't require "esoteric" RPG knowledge or special gaming stuff (more people have Winamp, Windows Media Player or iTunes than they do d4s & d12s).

    Edit: New goal: the non-audio elements should ideally fit inside a CD case when folded.

    Inspirations:
    * Sight C, the man in the shadows -- there's no definition, and there's something foreboding about him. That brought the idea of being chased by a person who, while perhaps acting bestial, is never really described -- letting your mind fill in the details.
    * Sound B, the sound of running -- that gave me the idea of being chased, and I want to actually incorporate that in the audio
    * Concept A, "liminal" -- with the "pertaining the threshold or entrance" idea, I decided to take that as evil not being about to cross a Church threshold, thus making that a safe place.

    \n The game scene sound files/situations:

    (1) [Church Bells chime*] You have a moment's reprieve. Discard one Wound token or move one space.
    (2) [Sounds of running**] Your Friend appears out of nowhere! Pick a player. [...] That player is wounded.
    (3) [Sounds of running**] Your Friend appears out of nowhere! Pick a player. [...] Your Friend stumbles, giving that player a chance to move one space.
    (4) [Sounds of running**] Pick a player near you. [...] He stumbles! Do you help? [...] If so, you are wounded. If not, your comrade is wounded while you watch.
    (5) [Sounds of running**] Pick a player near you. [...] He stumbles! Do you help? [...] If so, you scare off Your Friend with your numbers. Both move one space. If not, your comrade is wounded while you watch.
    (6) [Sounds of running**] Pick a player near you. [...] He stumbles! Do you help? [...] If so, he points out a shortcut he saw when he fell. Both move two spaces. If not, you move one space.
    (7) [Sounds of running**] Pick a player near you. [...] He looks rather wounded. Do you help? [...] If so, you are wounded as Your Friend hunts for easy prey. If not, your comrade is wounded while you watch.
    (8) [Sounds of running**] Pick a player near you. [...] He looks rather wounded. Do you help? [...] If so, that player removes one Wound token. If not, move one space forward.
    (9) [Sounds of human howling] Your Friend is on the hunt! Pick a space with at least one player. [...] Your Friend ignores everyone there. Everyone there may move one space.
    (10) [Sounds of human howling] Your Friend is on the hunt! Pick a space with at least one player. [...] Your Friend moves like the wind, wounding everyone there.
    (11) [Sounds of human howling] Your Friend pounces on the stragglers. Pick a player who is farthest away from the Church. If you are that player, you are wounded. Otherwise, do you help him? [...] If so, you are wounded. If not, your comrade is wounded while you watch.
    (12) [Sounds of human howling] Your Friend pounces on the stragglers. Pick a player who is farthest away from the Church. If you are that player, you are wounded. Otherwise, do you help him? [...] If so, you scare off Your Friend with your numbers. Both move one space. If not, your comrade is wounded while you watch.

    In addition to the effects, picking yourself/helping someone gives you a Penance Token, while picking another/not helping gives you a Guilt Token.
    The [...] is a few seconds of tense, word-less sound.
    Each audio file chimes with a a couple seconds of silence afterwards, to signal that it's the next player's turn.
    *Recall that the sound of church bells signals endgame, if someone is in the church.
    **I'm going to incorporate Sound #2 here with all the "sounds of running"

    There are seven situations that can cause you to move forward (there is no other direction currently). Three are mandatory -- the decision made doesn't affect that fact that it'll happen.
    There are seven situations that involve being wounded. Five are mandatory -- the decision made doesn't affect that a wounding will happen. Five mandatory might be a but much, but that could also depend on the cap for wounds & how play turns out.
    There are two situations that involve reducing a would, both of which are not mandatory.

    If you choose Penance: 5/12* of wounding yourself, 5/12 of moving forward
    If you choose Guilt: 0/12* of wounding yourself, 4/12 of moving forward
    (Extra chance if you're a straggler)
    You know, I think I like that. We'll see. The neat thing about the way this works is that the odds can be changed by duplicating any number of audio files in the playlist. That's freakin' cool.

    Now, the real trick: Getting the above to fit in around 3-3.5M worth of audio files. The 5M limit will be...interesting. I may end up disqualifying myself if I can't do it in 5M total (I also have the intro & endings to do) satisfactorily.

    \n Hmm. I'm doing some analysis, and if you can't target a dead person or someone in the safety of the church, the game also effectively ends when one person is alone outside.

    Other analysis shows that three players will end the game, on average, on the 35th turn. So, if each turn is a 1-minute audio file (which I think might be too long for the file), then the game will last 35 minutes + intro & ending time.

    Four players end, on average, around turn 48-49.

    But I'm not sure if I'm satisfied with the "last player is a rotten egg" vibe, even if he has a chance to win (thanks to the second ending).

    \n I recorded the audio tracks this evening. They aren't great, but it's good enough to work for playtesting. If you're interested, you can grab them at:
    http://www.tomorrowtheworldgames.com/lab/OnlyTheGuiltyRun_Audio_v1.zip (1.61M)

    Surprisingly, I was able to fit all the files in less than 2M thanks to encoding everything at 32kbps.

    I have one Intro track, twelve Game tracks and three Ending tracks. Each track type has the same length & same ID3 tags (aside from track number), so that you can't (or shouldn't be able to) guess which track you're on or about to be on if you're watching the screen. The trick will be seeing how well this works on shuffle* on various playing programs. In programs that don't pause between tracks, it should (ideally) sound like someone is constantly running in the game. That's incorporating Sound B, man!

    Each Game track is 22 seconds, and they flow from one to the other (without a tone or anything to break them up, we'll see if that's needed but I didn't want to break the atmosphere). So, if my simulations show a game being over in around 45-50 turns for 4 people, then the game will take between 18 & 20 minutes to play (adding in a one minute intro & 30 second ending, not adding any further set-up time, which is fairly minimal). So, I guess that's a game in 30 minutes. I'm not sure if that's not satisfying, but if not I could experiment with increasing the play with how many wounds you can take before you die and how many spaces it is from the start to the Church (which, right now, is 10 & 11 respectively -- and you start with 2 wound tokens.)

    Next up is the map portion of the game. The map and main play rules should fit on 4.75" by 19" -- one side of a four-fold CD insert (4.75" by 4.75"). For the contest, I'll split this up into two halves. The other content, like how to make characters, can fit on the other side of the four-fold.

    (*Which is to say shuffle playmode, not shuffle playlist -- the latter only re-orders once.)
