\n I'm making a list of skills for a game (players are supposed to choose only one after rolling stats); and finally I've reduced them to six.

1. weapon - (you can attempt anime style movements with your preferred weapon; perform area attacks, or do double damage)
2. tough - (you're used to the fight, you get double HP)
3. acrobat - (you can attempt anime-style jumps and evasion feats)
4. stealth - (you can always try to be stealthy; also you can once a day claim retroactivelly that you've done something while nobody was looking)
5. mage - (spend downtime or 1d6 hp to cast a spell; great effects may require consecutive spells to be cast)
6. specialist - (choose whatever: medicine, technology, nature, etc. When you tackle your speciality, you can always claim to know something, or have something helpful on your tools. Roll to see if there are costs or limitations)

Of course, i'd love some feedback on this, but I was just wondering:

If other people would have to reduce a skill list to six, how would they do it?

So feel free to post your own version (or even a shorter list if you dare). I love this gaming design challenges, and I know that many of you do too.

    \n Challenge: what world and game style do you automatically picture from these?

    1. manage - (you can retroactively declare a preparation which would allow you to avoid this conflict, roll to find out if anyone else is helped or hurt)
    2. react - (you can act in surprisingly extreme ways to deal with this conflict, name how and roll to find out if anyone else is hurt)
    3. heal - (you can recover from any psychological harm, roll to find out whether you lose parts of yourself)
    4. grow - (you can gain experience from a conflict, roll to find out if anyone else can)

    \n I picture something like the PCs being all workers on a weird and sinister corporate building. Something like american psycho and the Great National Bank from How i met your mother. High doses of sociopathy mixed with a fierce competitivity, all masked with a very thin coat of "for the good of the company".

    Manage is for those kind of guys who are always ahead and take work home in order to get a pat on the back from the boss.
    React is for those fuckers who always have an ace in the sleeve and do not care who or what must be treaded upon.
    Heal is for that new cute girl who comes from a small town, and that deep in her heart stills feels bad for what the company is doing and what it forces them to do
    Grow is for those new kids who have talent and make the best of the people they have around: tony montanas and proffesional motivators.

    \nNavigate Bureaucracy - Know who to talk to and where to go at the factory or within the Party
    Entertain - Throw a good party, be a fun guest, secure contraband, make a friend
    Work an Angle - Do a side job, hustle for hard currency, cut corners
    Make a Deal - Haggle, negotiate, cajole
    Deal With It - Abide, suck it up, hold in your frustration and disappointment
    Throw Your Weight Around - Name drop, gossip, appeal to connected relatives

    For a game where you play workers in a Polish radio factory in 1970

    \n I like your skills list, Cuchulainn! That's a lot of bang for 6 entries.

    This is a fun game. Here's my list:

    1. match - Communicate with a teammate as if by telepathy, using any means. Assess who you'd make stellar babies with. Nail timing of coordinated group maneuvers.
    2. dance - Give energy to the gathering, and partake in the greater energy the gathering in turn provides.
    3. nature - Be part of the pack or herd; understand their signals. Evade the dangers and harness the opportunities of terrain, weather and flora.
    4. provide - Craft useful things. Distill elixirs. Transform part of your body into anything of similar size from the natural world. (One-way.)
    5. teeth - Hunt. Endure physical hardship. That thing that needs to be done at the exact right time? Do it at the exact right time.
    6. innocence - Do not learn to seduce, manipulate, go aggro, or seize by force.

    What kind of setting / play does this evoke?

    \n David, I'm probably way off the mark.. but I wanna use those skills for a game about "surviving" a psychedelic music festival.

    An attempt:
    1. Coup - (embarrass or weaken an opponent without causing or taking harm. they suffer -1 (max -3) ongoing to act against you. You gain 1 Honor for each success.)
    2. Words - (recount a story of your people. those who listen and act in the spirit of the story take +1 ongoing while doing so. you also speak the enemy's language.)
    3. Radical - (your maximum Honor is reduced by 2, but you never lose Honor for using the weapons of the enemy. you start with an enemy weapon.)
    4. Hunt - (you can track prey, human or otherwise. when camp is made, you may provide food for one person for each hour you spend hunting.)
    5. Spirit - (when you ask the spirits to intervene, they will do so if you accept their bargain. If you fail to honor a bargain, they will not answer until you atone.)
    6. Ride - (when mounted, you travel twice as fast as the mount would normally allow. both you and your mount have 1-armor while you are mounted.)
