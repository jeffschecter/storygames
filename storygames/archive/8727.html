\n It's always fun to play new games. Is it fun to play old games as well?

Tell me about a game that:
- Was published in 2003 or earlier
- That you've played at least twice in 2008

Note: You need to have played the same version that was published in 2003 or earlier. In other words, playing D&D4 a zillion times in 2008 doesn't count, because it wasn't D&D4 in 2003.

    \n I'm running a game of Everway these days, which was published back in 1995, practically centuries ago in RPG time.

    It's got a lot of nice little things about it. Character creation is hella easy, with the possible exception of choosing a virtue/vice/fate if you're unfamiliar with the Fortune Deck. The resolution mechanics is very flexible to use in play, ranging from "Use character competency to determine success or failure," to "What would be cool? That's what happens," to "Draw a card from the fortune deck and interpret appropriately." Which is a pretty nice bag of heuristics to choose from.

    I'm also in a few online Nobilis games (one PbP, one IRC) which is a bit closer to the line at 2002.
