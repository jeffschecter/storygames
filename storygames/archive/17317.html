\n So I've been playing around with Guncho . For the record, it's a MUD/MUSH-esque game server that allows users to author "realms" (nearly entirely self-contained IF worldlets) in Inform 7 code. Players can telnet in and wander around the realms, chatting, interacting, and so on.

(The sad part is that almost no-one uses it, considering it basically only appeals to a fraction of the already abysmally small Interactive Fiction community. The realms are consistently ghost towns, and the only ones available are mostly little prototypes and sketches that never got upgraded to fully-fledged games.)

I wanna talk about what this amazing little platform means for story-gaming at large, and what things we could be doing with it but aren't. I'll put in my $0.02 when I can find them in the couch cushions.
