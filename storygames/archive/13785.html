The direction has shifted, new paradigms are settling into our nests. The gray skies of winter make us a little kooky here in the pacific north west, and as the mist cascades through the branches of the cedar, so too do strange visions cascade past our wide and searching eyes.

What if we held a story-gaming convention in a warehouse, and partnered with local installation artists to build themed sets for the gaming tables to each sit in the midst of?

We think that would be wonderful.
So come to Olympia, Washington and play with us at a story-gaming convention like no other.

Fabricated Realities, occuring June 3rd through June 5th 2011.

Website will be live soon with more info and tickets.

    Chapter 2
    Looking for attendees who will volunteer to run a game for new players:
    We'd like to make Fabricated Realities welcoming and accessible to new gamers. In pursuit of this,
    we're asking for a few practised game facilitators to each present a game especially for those attendees
    who aren't familiar with the hobby.

    Games that seem like a good idea -
    Danger Patrol, Archipelago 2009, Prime Time Adventures, Remember Tomorrow, Dogs in the Vineyard, Panty Explosion, etc, That Game That You Love Running.

    It would be my preference that you'd run the game several times and are quite comfortable with it, and are also very excited to share your love of the hobby with folks who haven't encountered this before. Ideally, you also have confidence in your ability to communicate the game through the gauntlet of incorrect assumptions that you might be faced with (as in introducing any weird thing to humans).

    Correct posts will include some or all of the following: You volunteering & naming the game you'll facilitate, names of other games that ought to be presented for new gamers, excitement, cautions.

    Incorrect posts will focus on new nicknames for Jackson Tegu.

    \n Volunteering:
    If you don't mind me being self-serving, I've been working on a game - working-title-that-will-probably-stick That's Drama - my #1 goal of which is accessibility. I've playtested it several times with an all-but-one success rate.

    Excitement:
    I'd *love* to run it.

    Cautions:
    My single biggest problem when playing story games with newbies - I've seen it a few times now - is when they're the Spotlight, they're like a spotlighted animal. Deer in headlights. Sometimes they shut down and say they don't want to play anymore. Fiasco pretty much fixes this with the "Let everyone else frame the scene for you" thing. Going non-serious is another fix.

    \n Point the first: The Dreaming Crucible above = self-serving.

    Point the second: The problem you mention, Jamie, is exactly why I'm considering Archipelago. It's got a really nice framework for supporting and helping each other, and Willem Larsen recently shared his techniques on the Norwegian Style blog for enhancing that effect even more. The powerhouses in this regard are "I'd like to throw something in, " and Willem's new techniques "I See You" (for building a shared vivid vision at the start), "Destiny Loaded Questions" (for, well, asking provocative questions that prompt the players to answer them) and "Help" for signaling that you're stuck and could use some input).

    Fiasco is good for the reason you said (If you are stuck you get to beg off the responsibility), but also for its scene-setting guidance: think of a question you'd like to answer (about a person, a relationship, an object, whatevs) and make a scene about that. Questions are great--open-ended ones, not "yes or no"--because everyone knows how to answer a question. Even if the answer is "I don't know," it's at least an icebreaker to tease out an idea. With the right coaxing guidance ("say the first thing that comes to mind," "go with the most obvious thing") you can access brilliance that the newbie didn't even know they had in them!

    Peace,
    -Joel

    Art Kitchen presents Olympia's first ever story gaming convention running June 3rd through 5th at The Procession of the Species Studio. The studio has been transformed by local artists into a collection of fantastic environments that the gaming tables are located within, including a floating castle, a crystal cavern, outer space, a post-apocalyptic cafe, a dark forest, and more!

    Full Passes cost $35 and are available online at fabricatedrealities.com and at The Danger Room on the corner of 4th and Columbia in downtown Olympia. Space is extremely limited so purchase your tickets early. Day passes will be sold at the door on an as-available basis.

    We at Fabricated Realities are using the term \u201cstory games\u201d according to Clinton R. Nixon's usage: to encompass all games, traditional or edgy, with a focus on creating fiction. This event will lean heavily towards games with creative equality amongst their players and story at the forefront of their design.

    Echoing the do-it-yourself music scene, the advent of print on demand publishing has allowed for many independent game designers to flourish. Though chiefly organized through the internet, there is an inordinately strong concentration of these designers in the Pacific Northwest and we're excited to welcome the entire spectrum of designers to the convention, from those who've tweaked a house-rule all the way up to those who've published their game into a product.

    Several of these story games have been brought to market within the last year, and are designed in such a way as to be enjoyed by old-hand and non-gamer alike. We're excited to play Apocalypse World, Anima Prime, Perfect: Unrevised, G X B, Microscope, and Panty Explosion Perfect, in addition to older favorites like Fiasco, Shock: Social Science Fiction, Polaris, and Dogs in the Vineyard.

    While Fabricated Realities is for people of all ages, some games we'll play have adult themes such as violence, sex, responsibility, compassion, hope, and transformation. There will be many good-hearted facilitators on hand to introduce new games to festival goers, and help people pick what to play. Our three-day schedule includes 10 gaming slots and lots of relaxed social time.

    Come dream, imagine and explore with us.
    Find out more about story games and Fabricated Realities by visiting fabricatedrealities.com
    Fabricated Realities is sponsored by Cicada Restaurant and Lounge

    Fabricated Realities
    A Story Game Convention
    June 3rd - 5th 2011
    @ The Procession of the Species Studio
    311 1/2 Capitol Way North
