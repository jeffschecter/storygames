\n Mike Mearls decided to have a goal to paint one miniature a week for the next year, then challenged others to do it with him, and the idea has now grown into http://52minis.blogspot.com/, where those of us participating will post pics of the miniatures we paint. Maybe you want to paint, too!

I'm planning on doing some Warhammer Catachan Jungle Fighters from 40k and some Chaos stuff from their fantasy line, even though I don't play any Warhammer (yet). I also have genuine, non-ironic ambitions to paint some Dogs in the Vineyard miniatures this year. I may just repaint some D&D Minis in ways that are interesting to me. It'll be fun! Grab a paintbrush!

    \n If it turns out that you think'll you'll buy a lot of minis from either company (or have historical wargamer pals that will) you might want to look into the club membership at Old Glory, which gives 40% discounts at both sites.

    Old Glory minis are not the finest sculpts; however, they are some of the cheapest minis around, especially with the club discount making them come in at something like $0.60 per figure on foot. They do sell other companies' minis as well, some of which are decidedly nicer ( and also have the 40% club discount).

    EDIT:
    Oh yeah, be aware that the Blue Moon minis are giants, coming in at around 30mm foot to eyes. that puts them more in line with Black Scorpion cowboys and significantly more massive than most minis, even Heroic 25mm ( aka bulky 28mm figures).

    Not that it ever stops me from mixing and matching.
