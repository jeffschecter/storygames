\n You'll have to excuse me if I sound incoherent. I'm half-asleep as it is.

So it's been a busy month for me, and I haven't had the time, energy or inclination to really participate on the Internets. But, one of the things I was working on was an extension of an idea mentioned in the Big List of RPG Plots, by S. J. Ross. Specifically this bit:\n
Somewhere in this article, S. John Ross says:Throw yourself a curve. Your players will, anyway, so practice early. Pick two random entries from the Big List and make your adventure on those, no matter what comes up - the first one is the hook layer; the second is the meat of the adventure. If the same entry comes up both times, go with it! Two layers can have a similar structure but very different roots or details.
\nSo, I initially drew up (or really, scribbled) a random results table and figured out a way to do it with a couple of dice, and then I remembered: Abulafia!

I even noticed Jacob's Instant Plot Generator, and I realized that maybe, just maybe, I could combine the both. Maybe even add a list of random twists. That sort of thing. It would be cool.

Except that Abulafia has a couple of limitations. It's really cool, but I hit a brick wall pretty early in the Abulafia implementation: Abulafia doesn't really recurse well. It does one pass to translate the randomizer tags into proper wikitext, and then that's it, so it kind of killed of a couple of ideas right there and then.

But then I remembered something from my earlier, troglodyte-in-London days: NBOS Software's Inspiration Pad Pro. I remember being kind of disappointed with it back then, but maybe the software matured a little after the last time I saw it.

It did. I've tinkered with it, and it's pretty damn powerful -- way more powerful than Abulafia is, frankly speaking. There are, though, a couple of problems:\n
  1. The final end result is this text file that has to be read by software you need to download, which isn't, as far as I can see, free, open-source software.
  2. Well, I don't have anywhere to host it, which is a pain. At least with Abulafia the end results can be easily shown to other people around the world.
\nThere's supposed to be some kind of web implementation of IPP, but I'm kind of suspicious of it. I mean, it's supposed to be just proof-of-concept, and I have no idea if the stuff you upload to it belongs to you or them, that sort of thing. Besides, the code I'm working on belongs to their beta version, which requires you to install the version 1.0 and then copy the executables, so that's a bit of a barrier to entry.

Anyway. Any suggestions where I can upload this file? It's hardly very big: it's about 30 KB or so. Would anyone be interested in seeing it? I can email it to interested parties and the like.

    \nMan, I'll try. It's been a while since I was working on Abu, and it'll take some time for me to remember what the problem was.

    Okay, one of the things I wanted to do in Abu was to generate a double-layered plot, in which each layer would have a random number of twists, specific to that layer itself. I also wanted to get a random number of general plot twists from a table I had made earlier, so I thought that I could use the following:

    <ol><sgdisplay iterations="6">[main]</sgdisplay></ol>
    <sgtable>

    ;main
    1,<sgdisplay iterations="2"><li>[twists]</sgdisplay></ol>
    1,<sgdisplay iterations="3"><li>[twists]</sgdisplay></ol>
    1,<sgdisplay iterations="4"><li>[twists]</sgdisplay></ol>

    ;twists
    //-- List of twists....
    </sgtable>

    That doesn't work. You get, instead, something like the following result, except possibly even more messed up because it's entwined in the <ol> and list tags:

    <sgdisplay iterations="(random number between 2 and 4)"><li>[twists]</sgdisplay>
    Basically I suspect that the generator only runs through page once. Which is understandable, since you've made mention on how bloody processor-intensive the damned thing was.

    Hope I got that right, at any rate.

    \n Ah. Well you're right that it only parses the page once, but that's because it's mediawiki. Nevertheless, that specific problem isn't impossible, it just needs to be done differently than you were going about it.


    <ol><sgdisplay iterations="1"><li>[main]</sgdisplay></ol>

    <sgtable>
    ;main
    1,[General Plot Twists.main]<li>[General Plot Twists.main]
    1,[General Plot Twists.main]<li>[General Plot Twists.main]<li>[General Plot Twists.main]
    1,[General Plot Twists.main]<li>[General Plot Twists.main]<li>[General Plot Twists.main]<li>[General Plot Twists.main]
    </sgtable>


    Seems to work for what you were describing. It's currently on the Sandbox page. But, you've found a tool that works better for you, and I hope your generator finds success.
