\n I've made this game about a chase. It's played by two people who share the narration and build a story together. I've used a lot of forum help designing the game although not much of SG as I am pretty new here. I think I have something that's works good and is fun. I would appreciate any help with play testing. I'm looking for people who might take the time to play it (about an hour of play time after reading) and even people who only have time to read it(about 2000 words long). Here is the intro/summary from the game:

ESCAPE miniRPG

Escape is a game of fanciful chases. There is the hunter who is more
knowledgeable of the landscape and much faster than the prey. Then there is the
prey who has a head start and uses their wits to complete the escape before they are
captured. The setting and genre are almost infinite and determined by the players,
you need only answer a few questions before starting the game.

The Gist
\xad-This is an RPG for two people who share the narration while playing a chase
scene.
\xad-There is the prey who has escaped and has a head start.
\xad-There is the hunter who knows the landscape and is faster than the prey.
\xad-6 sided dice are used to play. You will need a minimum of 2 but 4 is preferable.
\xad-You can use the play sheets provided or just use a pencil and paper to keep track
of play.
\xad-You must roll 2D6(keep the highest) for every action you take.

The play test documents are here:
https://drive.google.com/open?id=0B8U1saX6-rhIWHRVNDhPRVVGOWc


There is a questionnaire for readers and players. Which ever you decide to do. If you have any questions or comments I would be happy to talk to you about them. Thanks everyone.

    \n I really like the idea of your game. Not just as a mini-game, but larger story can certainly be a "chase" in the background even if smaller things happen there in between.

    In addition to this, you can define "chase" in fiction in myriad of ways. Romantic chase for example, or subset of crime thrillers where the cop chases after the serial killer and so forth. I really like when a game doesn't fix the fictional content, but rather has some underlying principle or "simulation" (or many of them) that is employed.

    I did a quick read and it seemed quite clear. I'll read it again soonish and answer to your questionnaire.
