    \n Codex is essentially an extension of the blue book concept for games where writing is at the centre of play. Characters are described using player-developed aspects of the setting. You can see sample PCs here. I made them using Wikipedia, but you can simply use pure fiction to develop a setting collaboratively.

    As play develops, you add entries in one of three book colours. These note the relative authority, scope and mechanical influence of the entry. To act, total applicable entries into a bonus, or use them to scale results to fight a war or win over a crowd. It's a very simple system.

    There's a review here. I've made corrections to the book since then.

    I've provided some support and options here.

    This game evolved from my reactions to De Profundis, but it isn't a genre game (though there is a fantasy setting included to ease people in). It is most definitely not a story game in the sense of having a limited narrative scope, supporting low-prep play or insisting on and endgame. It does none of those things. It is in fact, a game as a vehicle for story creation.
