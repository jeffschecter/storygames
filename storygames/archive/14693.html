    \n I hope this is a new edition with all new rules. Unfortunately while the angel demon info is pretty cool, and the new book looks to be beautiful, the rules of the old free version are...I want to say "ass" but I'll try to be more diplomatic and say "not to my taste". 2d10 + small range of modifiers vs target number. The text is full of "rules suck, they just hinder roleplaying" nonsense. There's even a brief line which I find very insightful...the key reason to roll is so the players feel like they have some control over the story. In other words, this game is meant to be...the GM talks alot and tells you what happens and occasionally you can roll dice and if you beat the target number (set by the GM based pretty much on how willing the GM is to let you win) you might sometimes get to have some input.

    Despite this obsession with minimalist rules what rules there are reflect a rampant distrust of players.

    There's a pretty cool idea for Grace...basically higher equals "in good with the Man" lower equals "some Purifiers might be after you soon to set you straight" You get Grace by doing things like enhancing the faith of mortals or banishing demons and the like. You spend it to add to your rolls. Here's the thing, Grace ranges around 5-10 normally and low "level" (not exactly levels but close enough here) angels can only spend 2 points at once to modify the roll. But you have to choose to spend them BEFORE you roll. What?! Not only is that pretty weak fictionally (cuz calling on divine aid to save you after the fact is alot cooler and you're supposed to be pretty badass) but you can only do this a couple of times and its pretty hard to get more...so why the hell would you want a rule where most of the time they're going to be wasted (fail anyway in spite of, or wouldn't have needed them to succeed). Really? You spend your brownie points with the Almighty for...nothing...and this is fun...? The choice of when to spend these points is about the only tactically mechanical choice players get to make (aside from describing actions to skew towards your good stats)...and the rules pretty effectively neuter that choice. Weak.

    I'm still glad I backed the thing, but man...I certainly hope they learned something from the last 10 years of indie game design and will have better rules than this in the new version.

    \nOr you can interpret this as the will of your creator: you can guess where you stand with the Almighty, but you certainly do not know where you will Fall.

    (Also: spending after-the-fact for success means that you will only spend when it is guaranteed to bump you to a success and will end up as a kind of standing bonus to your rolls, on average. There's a different psychology when you're wagering your fixed resource for a chance, but not a guarantee, of effectiveness. Blowing a roll after using Grace is deflating, but that's what losing a gambit is, after all.)

    \n Dev, sure. I get the difference. But why would "gambling on the whim of the Almighty" me an effective mechanic for a game about angels. The trait represents your "in" with God. You have to spend it to get a benefit, its not a standing bonus. Certain character types start with more points because God likes them better. But in a game about bad ass angels of vengeance sent to earth to wreak destruction on the unbelievers...then not so much. Especially when its about the only tactical choice in the game.
