    \n I didn't know about Burning Empires, or Chris Whatsisname's comics for that matter, until long after I'd started working on the game (most of the setting dates back to 2001). And I only recently picked up a copy of that game and haven't yet played it. It was, in fact, an awkward moment at Forge Midwest in spring 2006, when Luke asked, so what's this game you're working on, and I said, oh, it's this big galaxy spanning space opera that involves a dire threat to humankind.

    So, weird coincidence is the best answer I can give you. I was heavily inspired by Homeworld and LOTR. I haven't, since buying BE, incorporated any of its ideas. The way I picture the setting for Galactic in my head doesn't look like what I've seen of those comics. I'd pull much more from the Aliens movies, BSG, the Halo trilogy and Firefly.

    \n Agreed -- I tried to set up a one-shot for a minicon here in town and decided that it just wouldn't do the game justice.

    You could take on too much pre-gen as the GM and deny the players a substantial part of the experience the game is obviously intended to deliver and then hurry through play.

    You could generate only one captain and lose the spotlight management intrinsic to the game, which wouldn't help test the game all that much and I think would eat into the experience as well. And then hurry through play.

    You could just play for 30 hours straight and call it a one-shot.

    Instead I'm going to do the prep stage with a few groups and see if I can get at least one regular playtest group out them.
