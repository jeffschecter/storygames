    \n Eh, why not...
    * Fake Fur bag for three d20-sets of dice, with d30s (DitV, ASCII @HACK).
    * Small orange velveteen bag for one 36-die set of Aurora Borealis Black-on-Clear d6 dice (HERO, BW/BE, DitV).
    * Small tan leather bag for one 10-die set of of Aurora Borealis Black-on-Clear d10 dice (White Wolf, DitV).
    * Five (yes, fucking FIVE) 12- or 36-die sets, still in clear Chessex boxes, of d6 dice (Mechaton... just Mechaton. And BW/BE loaners, for my lazy-ass fellow player).
    * 4d8 x 2, in green- and red-with-white-numerals, still in Chessex special order baggies (Mechaton and DitV, as d4s or d8s).

    ----
    Which bring me to a perennial rant: those of you who do very dicey games (heh) ought to look into CustomDice.com, and their retail channel GameStation.net. Even if you don't use custom "meanings" for die values (i.e. custom faces; e.g. Burning Wheel Black, Gray, and White dice; Hero dice), you could at least bundle "minimal" and "optimal" sets of standard dice for playing your dice-intensive games.

    I spent more time than ANYONE should have to spend, setting up my custom order for Mechaton dice with my FLGS: two separate orders (their fault), difficulty getting a set of d8s (Chessex ordering policy), almost a month from first order to final delivery (WTF?). I'd have appreciated just having to order a "Mechaton Dice Set, 2- to 4-players;" and I doubt it would take much to get someone like GameStation to compile and offer it (if not Chessex, Koplow, or a larger manufacturer). GS sells dice-only games, with custom faces, so it would be neigh trivial for them to merely bundle existing die types.

    So, for a single DitV player, there could be a minimal set of, say, 4 to 6 of each die type, excluding d4s (which can be done with d8s); and an optimal set of, say, 6dX or 8dX or even a carefully analyzed distribution (based on the various starting trait dice). It's little more than a "bulk order template," with the option of changing the dice color. (Or, with Mechaton, every necessary color to play, but perhaps offering two or three sets with different "qualities": solid colors, translucent colors, transparent colors; with pips or with numbers; you get the idea...).
    [/rant]

    Posted By: Justin BowSince my game uses d8s, can you clarify your difficulties getting your hands on these dice and how many you were ordering?
    Special order with my FLGS, using Chessex because their prices worked out best, for 12- and 36-die sets--it was cheaper for me to buy 100-something dice using sets than top buy 40-ish per-piece.

    But the d8s were a bitch: there is no 10d8 set in any color, at Chessex (never mind the colors which matched exactly the various d6 sets I'd already bought). 12d6, 36d6, and 10d10 were fine: plenty of colors from which to choose (thanks to... yep, Hero and White Wolf). Xd12 or Xd8... impossible. I ended up paying a buck a piece for them: $8 for 8d8--more than custom ordering from Chessex! ("F" in FLGS must mean "Fucking"... but after a month's wait, I wasn't about to argue.)

    So, yeah, what I said above: talk to dice producers about making appropriate one-, two- or max-player sets for your dicey games. Even with a whole rack full of dice, my FLGS doesn't have any XdY sets, where X > 4 and Y = 4, 8, or 12. "Pounds of Dice" don't satisfy my aesthetic--I want a one-player DitV set in a dusty speckled tan with blood red numerals, dammit!

    In the end, it just a handful of SKUs on GameStation (or Chessex or Koplow.... hell, Koplow puts out frigging Hebrew dice, fer fuck's sake... they could do a DitV or Mechaton set!). Link from your game web site and yer done.

    \n So, I bought THREE of those RobBags from the Duluth Trading Co. Also got one for a friend, and 4 for Renato in Italy.

    So here's what I noticed:

    My ENTIRE dice collection, which is "One Metric Fuckload" of dice, fits in one bag. However, in that condition the bag will not close completely. However, it closes enough that I can pick it up and carry it around, like in case for some reason I decided to take them all to a con or something.

    I split up my collection into "all my dice", and "the dice I use the most": Normally I put them in the aforementioned Watchfield Cat Bag, but I split them into their own bag. In this config, both bags close fine.

    So I was actually considering selling the third bag locally, figuring someone in the area might want one. However, I found a better use for it: The Tenra Bansho Bag. Basically, I run a lot of demos of this game at cons and the like, and what I end up doing is dumping a bunch of d6es of all kinds in a bag, and have a second bag for poker chips (for the game's equivalent of Fan Mail tokens). However, now I've got a bag specifically tricked to run one game slick:

    So these bags have 6 sub-pouches. Looking from above when open, it looks like a Trivial Pursuit marker. In 4 of those slots I've got 36 12mm d6es (you know, the kind you normally get in the plastic cube) so that each player gets their own set/color. In the last two bags are the GM's d6es (A ton of the Chinese/Japanese d6es), and one last pocket for a ton of poker chips. A complete set, aimed at one game.

    I'll give it some rugged wear over the next few months and give a report later.
