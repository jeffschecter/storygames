    Publishing a book is easy
    \nSure, publishing a book is easy, but producing a professional-looking product is another matter. For the ashcan version of my soon-to-be-released game I did all you suggest, used Word for the writing, editing and layout, subscribed to a clipart website, downloaded and imported some basic graphics, manipulated them into the text and converted the whole thing to pdf. Now, I haven't yet seen the proof (which should arrive early next week) but I'm fairly confident that, although it'll look ok, a professional with any or all of the above skills would have made a better job of it.

    It really all depends what sort of game you're putting out and who/how big the audience is. If it's a small press indie narrative game no problem; if OTOH it's a fantasy-themed behemoth, with a metric tonne of rules, tables, spell charts and illos etc., I think I would consider forking out some cash for professional editing and layout, because I think my customers would notice the difference.

    I know I've slightly diverged from the OP to talk about editing rather than code monkeying, my point is that skills in the former are likely to be informed by knowledge of the latter.

    One cool thing about present day tech allows you to use other skills without knowing how computer programming works... Do you really need genius?
    \nExactly. I've just done what Johnstone talked about above, i.e. produced a book myself, from scratch, knowing only Word and Adobe, and how to manipulate text and graphics at a basic level. And I was on the verge of spending \xa3200 (about $300) for someone in a local copy bureau to do it for me on InDesign. And that would have been effectively mates' rates as they've known me a long time.

    I think as tech develops, it eventually evolves into a form that is highly accessible to outsiders- most wp and dtp programmes nowadays are 'for Dummies', to borrow a phrase from those books from the 90s- I mean, if you'd told me even five years ago that I'd be producing my own game books I'd have laughed (although I guess people might still laugh when they see them). Heck, I even, thanks to being on this and several other forums, know the difference between html and BB code. I mean, for a techno-sceptic like me that's crazy talk!
