\n The deal goes like this. At the weekend I was in Manchester while taking part in the VAGUE Student Nationals. I bought a copy of Mouse Guard before I even got to my hostel. It's very cool and is more of the awesome that I've come to expect from Luke Crane. I love the idea of playing a Mouse Guard game. However there is one thing I'm not too interested in is the setting. This isn't because it is a bad setting. I just like like to do my own world building.

So I've decided to do Samurai Mice!

To me it seems very little needs to be added to the game to do this. I will probably add/find a trait detailing dishonor because that's something I want to be part of the game.

Does anyone have any thoughts?
Are there any pitfalls I might come across?

    \n Daniel speaketh much wisdom.
    \n
    \nThe Nature Trait in any MG hack should represent the true nature of the people. In Mouse Guard Nature isn't the nature of the Guard...defending the weak, righting wrongs, and fighting weasels...its the nature of the mouse...scared, hungry, and skittish.
    \n
    \nThe Nature Trait in Samurai game isn't the nature of the Samurai...its the nature of people. Manipulative, dishonest, rapacious, back stabby, and selfish. Go ahead and tap that Nature for the big dice attack...go ahead...do it...you know you want to.
    \n
    \nYeah...THAT samurai game, I would totally play.
    \n
    \nBecause ultimately, that's what honor is. Its hosing yourself for a principal.
