    \n Hey Kate, hit up this thread when you're settled, and we'll make sure you're taken care of.
    Also, while this mailing list is not used often (because most folks on it already kinda know each other so we often communicate off-list), it's meant to be a thing to help new people to the area join to find or post about games:
    Google Groups Mailing List for Triangle Story Games
    (this list has the most active of us, I know there are others in Durham and Raleigh that we're trying to bring out of their shells).

    But yeah, between the various game groups in the area, you won't have a problem finding a game (everything from one-off meetups to organizing longer play sessions), as long as you can drive (and even then there might be someone near the area that can pick you up).
    But in short, here are a list of S-Gers on this forum that live in the area who haven't already posted above:

    Me! (Cary)
    Jason (Durham)
    Mark Causey (soon Durham, from Ral)
    Joey (Raleigh)
    Lisa P (Mebane)
    Eric P (Mebane)
    David Berg (Chapel Hill)
    Clinton Nixon-Dreisbach (Durham)
    Steve Segedy (Chapel Hill)
    Daniel Solis (Durham)
    Quintin Stone (Cary)
    Matthew Gandy (Durham/Raleigh)
    ...and a bunch of other folks who play in games that we run.

    Madison, eh? The closest you'll find to State Street is Fayateville St in Raleigh or Franklin St in Chapel Hill (or 9th Street in Durham, but only in as much as restaurants). It's like instead of one big city there's three smaller ones that orbit each other.

    Also, if gaming is your Thing, and you haven't locked in a house/apt yet, you'll probably want to live closer towards the left side of Raleigh, or way up north off of I-540, to get you better access to Durham/Cary/etc. But there are all sorts of cool places in downtown Raleigh to live, too.

    Plus, if you like beer, the Flying Saucer is awesome.

    -Andy

    \n Hi! I live in Durham and am actually trying to stump up a regular group amongst my old gaming friends. They're old... and lazy. It's not working.

    Check out the Raleigh Tabletop Roleplayers Meetup. Eric, the organizaer, is super-active and -dedicated; and he's got the story/indie games bug. They meet in west Raleigh, RIGHT off the Interstate, basically--very convenient to the Triangle (except, maybe, Chapel Hill). Although, when I tried to run a Story Games Night there, I got the distinct imprression that the owner wasn't keen on tables being taken up for games he doesn't (can't?) sell. I tried to make a point of buying some thing or another each night, but that's not really sustainable long-term (unless I indulge my Clix habit again). Perhaps he's changed his mind, in the past year--I haven't been there in a while.

    \n Wow awesome response!! Thanks all! This makes me feel much better about moving (especially because my husband will be staying in Madison for most of the year, due to grad school). I think it it also makes it easy for me to get to many places in the city.

    I joined the meet up mentioned (though it did seem to lean heavily to the Pathfinder side of things, but I did see some stuff about Dread, so yay to that!), and will post, when it gets closer on the list serve, and keep my eyes open for NC Game days and the upcoming cons.

    @Jason: I will be working at D.H Hill at the beginning, in User Experiences (basically, reference, instruction, assessment, tiny bit of web design). I am one of the new "fellows" there: basically people who are chosen right out of grad school to take semi leadership positions. My scene in Madison has been based around UW Madison's SLIS (school of library and Info Science) and people I met through Epic (a big health software company just outside of Madison). It was actually my husband and I who started to introduce people to these games (we actually first became good friends playing Lacuna in College), as most of them had been gaming for a while but mostly in D&D, WoD, and Shadowrun. So, its kind of isolated from any main gaming scene in Madison, but it is great. A good group of people who are always interested in trying new things. They even are willing to jump in when I say that I want to run a one off about dying for your belief by the Inquisition (Montsegur 1244). Recently I ran a game of In a Wicked Age, and we have been involved in a long running game of Burning Wheel (going on 2 years now I think). The one main problem with the group is that not a huge amount of people are willing to GM.

    Looking forward to meeting and gaming with you all, whee, so excited
