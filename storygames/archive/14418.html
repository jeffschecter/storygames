    \n Start with Wikipedia. China's a big place, depending on the region and microculture those myths are different. maybe do a "toe in the water" shallow dive on Wikipedia, then maybe ask if you have specific questions that Wikipedia only touches on?

    Also, it depends on the context. Are you running a game set in ancient China? Or looking to apply something to modern-day China?

    Reason I ask that is because if someone asked me about "Japan", I could spout off various creation myths, but:
    * In modern day, people still believe strongly in Shinto superstition, which originated such creation stories, but
    * Most people either don't know the full creation stories, or if they do know them they think of them as funny/bullshit of a bygone era (like we would look at Roman/Greek gods, etc).

    So in an ancient myth game or a game set in 7000BC to 1500AD or so, it might be topical. But by 1700-1900 most people rolled with Science where it conflicted with the tale of the Space Brother/Sister fucking and creating Japan with the afterward pull-out mess.

    \n Oh dude, that's easy. Thanks for clarifying.

    Go to your library, and check out SILVER PHOENIX by CINDY PON. It's a female-centric (tho not "just for girls") young adult story. Simple story, simple characters, not an exceedingly interesting plot or dialogue, but serviceable. Likely she's just finding her voice, and later books will be better. If you can't find it or want my copy, whisper me and for like $3 I'll mail my hardcover copy to you.

    It's a fantasy China setting in general, and within it even though the gods are ancient and hidden (no one really sees them in their lifetime), the main character and her friends get whisked to basically the Mythical China Domain of one of the major goddesses. That scene/chapter is really evocative, well-described, thoroughly asian* and can totally serve as gaming fodder.

    There's a sequel to this book (haven't read it) which may have more of the same. However, with the uprising of more asia-centric fantasy particularly geared at young adults, I'm sure that someone will post another author or two that writes similar events/locations/themes into her/his stories. Anyone?

    -Andy

    * That is, more than just jade shit splashed around and tea and bowing humbly. It is not from real Chinese myth/legend description, but works in enough Otherness that would have been missed without research into some real legends/stories.

    \n Ah, sorry I might have misinterpreted one point:

    Is the wold they can travel to basically a fantasy China analogue, or a fantasy China analogue where the ancient Gods live (like, their domains, as I was indicating in that Special Chapter of Silver Phoenix, above).

    If the latter, then yeah Silver Phoenix.

    If the former, then DOUBLE Silver Phoenix, plus go to its Amazon.com page and start looking at the "We recommend This as well" section, building your library list.

    A wonderful Japanese take on ancient China is to be found in the wonderful anime (probably quite literally the best anime ever, due to the material and budget that NHK was swinging back in 2002-2004) "12 Kingdoms".

    I would not recommend Wikipedia as much, then. Academic literature based on the era is dry shit with virtually no soul. Look to fiction written with heart by people who did the research for you for the best and juiciest RPG-usable text.

    Oh, and if the latter (just ancient China, no myth), check for the old Usborne-style picture-books-for-kids for visually striking guidebooks. Here's a list I made quickly from Amazon:

    Ancient China: See-Through History. YES PLZ.
    http://www.amazon.com/Ancient-China-See-Through-History/dp/0670871575

    Growing up in Ancient China:
    http://www.amazon.com/Growing-Up-Ancient-China/dp/0816727163

    Ancient China (prob not as useful as above two, looks more at History than Culture it seems):
    http://www.amazon.com/Ancient-Nature-Company-Discoveries-Libraries/dp/0809492482

    Life in Ancient China (dunno if a picture-book, maybe dig around for a review):
    http://www.amazon.com/Life-Ancient-China-Peoples-World/dp/0778720675

    Evocative picture books are the single best RPG sourcebook I have ever found, far more useful as a GM and player than RPG settings or textbooks.

    \nI think that's called "heaven."

    Here's my suggestions of things to read:\n
    • The first part of Journey to the West, where the Monkey King meets (and fights) a lot of the traditional gods and spirits is available as a lianhuanhua (single panel comic, with text underneath), here. It's quick and fun reading.
      • \n
    • 100 Chinese Gods is a great book for getting a sense of the kinds of deities that populated Chinese folk religion and some parts of state religion too. In case 100 isn't enough, the same illustrator has another book called 100 Buddhas in Chinese Buddhism.
      • \n
    • If you're specifically interested in creation myths and want to see a relatively modern (16th century) take on super early mythology, then the book you're looking for is The Creation of the Gods.
