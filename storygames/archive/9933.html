    \n
    \n At any time. Think of it like an Interrupt in Magic: the Gathering. The only limitation is that you cannot reply to a Strength with a Strength. You may reply to a Weakness with a Strength, or vice versa, though.
    \n
    \nYou may abandon your action by canceling (assuming you succeed). That cancels all actions that come after yours as well. This is tactically useful to keep others from taking kills, among other uses. Otherwise, what you rolled is what you rolled.
    \n
    \nThe typical ruling I've seen is that switching weapons takes up an NFA action. My ruling, which I believe was supported by Gregor, is that you can switch weapons along with other NFA actions, such as moving, or attempting to get a bonus on your next action, so long as it makes sense within the fiction. Unless the weapon says you can switch to it "for free", it requires an NFA roll.
    \n
    \nMake sure you know what weapons they're using as a "default" weapon for the mission. They can't just declare a new default weapon at the beginning of each combat, based on the positioning roll.

    \n Switching weapons:
    yes, switching back to the sidearm requires an NFA action which means you can't attack in the same round.
    Note however, when you do an NFA action you can do all three of these (or maybe just 2 depending on situation):
    * Change a weapon
    * move 1 range band
    * do some other action in the fiction

    The movement and changing weapons are essentially free actions when you do something else requiring an NFA action, so long as the actions all make sense in the fiction
