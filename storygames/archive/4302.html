\n As someone pointed out, my demonstrations are far and I am here.

What would you like to know about Reaper Beauties, my girls' manga story game about Reaper Beauties, the magical girls from a podunk, one Kentucky Fried Chicken town in Japan, who Reap the Hungry Ghosts that crave the emotions they felt in life?

In a nutshell, there are two basic rules -

You get your characters' desires by spending your characters Chi, and you lose control of your character (and the game, if no one else can win) when you run out of Chi.

The Game Master, as the Hungry Ghost's Victims, gets all the Chi you spend that lands you a desire: you get the Chi the Game Master spends that lands the Ghost a desire. The Reaper Beauties banish the Ghost when the GM runs out of Chi.

Simple? Yes. Hard? Very. Ask Becca. She couldn't do it - her character wanted her grandfather to never find out her mother was a criminal, and spent all her Chi to ensure it, choosing for her character to become the slave of the Hungry Ghost.

Sound cool to you?

Let's discuss. I will answer any and all questions you have about Reaper Beauties.

    \n misuba - my desire (ha) to design this game came from my long, long need to see magical girl games done right (Sailor Moon RPG and Resource Book, I love you, I love you to death, but face it, you aren't exactly my narrativist cup of tea), as well as my love affair with things Japanese and my spiritual awakening as a Buddhist.

    I kind of stubbed my toe on the two basic rules: First, to get what you want, give Chi to the GM. Second, a Hungry Ghost cannot survive when the GM loses all of his Chi. I realized precisely what those two rules together implied, and went, "Awesome." I had just started finding my Buddha nature when I tripped over that, so I realized I had a gem. Since then, I was cutting and polishing it, and setting it in simple Magical Girl grade silver bangles.

    Mark Causey - this game requires one Game Master and two to five other players, although it sings with three or four players and a game master. You can play it as a one shot, or as a continuing campaign. I suspect that it'll probably die from natural causes around the ninth session, as the characters gain enough Suffering to neccesitate getting written out of the story, as per the rules. (Suffering is sorta like Dogs in the Vineyard's Fallout, which = Experience Points + Damage.)
