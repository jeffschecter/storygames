\n I'm about to launch the Kickstarter for my new game Alas Vegas. And I don't know what to call it.

Alas Vegas is a self-contained character-centric RPG. The book contains a set of original rules and a story-structure that plays to completion in four game-sessions. The rules are specifically designed to play this story and no other.

If most traditional RPGs are soap-operas and Fiasco is a Coen Bros movie, then Alas Vegas is an HBO miniseries.

Is there a catchy name for this kind of thing? "RPG miniseries" sounds clunky and needs explanation. Am I going to have to come up with something better? Can you think of something better?

    >HBO miniseries RPG
    Yes please.
    \nI'll take that as a vote of confidence. Thanks!\n
    "A roleplaying game/story-game for creating short-form campaigns in the style of cable TV/HBO miniseries"?
    \nWell, it's not that\u2014it's more like one specific four-part miniseries. \n
    Does it contain pregenerated characters?
    \nWithout wanting to give too much away, no. Alas Vegas pulls the amnesia trick\u2014you start the game with no idea of who you are or how you got into your current situation. Remembering that and working out how you're connected to the other PCs and just what the hell happened to you all (and there's a nifty flashback mechanic connected to acquiring skills) will give players their character arcs. \n
    Or what I'm trying to figure out, is it like Fiasco in the sense that it gives you a "HBO miniseries" structure and I can play Generation Kill and The Corner and John Adams with it? Or is it more like Montsegur or Witch where you play just that specific story in various variations?
    \nIt's the latter: the setting and main plot are fixed, but the backstory is completely up to the players (and has a huge influence on how the plot plays out), and once the story is done it's hard to take the characters on to something else. It's designed as a four-episode one-shot: there's not a lot of replayability. So it's not really a storygame, but it's not a standard RPG either.

    What I'm looking for is a short phrase that sums up this idea: an RPG you'll play for four sessions and that'll be enough.

What I'm looking for ideally is something as punchy as 'story game', though of course the first story games didn't call themselves that, just as the first RPGs described themselves as 'wargames'.     I had wondered if the right phrase already existed\u2014I didn't really pay attention to the Forge, I admit\u2014but this conversation makes it pretty clear that it doesn't. But if anyone has any more suggestions...
