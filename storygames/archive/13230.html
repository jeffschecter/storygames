"Be kind to each other."

    \n I'm curious about relatively benign rules being subverted for nefarious purposes. Let's say you think of yourself as a benevolent ruler with only the best intentions for your people. What are the unfortunate side effects that happen despite following your rules?

    "Be not afraid."
    An army of fearless people with total disregard for their own safety or the safety of others.

    "Everyone has to learn as much as he can about his profession. No one but me can learn about more than one profession."
    A nation of drone-like specialists with little shareable or communicable knowledge.

    So, you set a rule. What goes wrong?

    \n "The gods favor my actions"
    -they only favor them the first time, if I try to change my mind they are wrathful

    "I am my own worst enemy"
    -while I have no enemies worse than myself, my limbs occasionally try to kill me

    "Mind your elders"
    -a society of elderly rulers over young drones, the presence of older/younger people in your presence determines your master/drone state

    "Leave now or forever hold your peace"
    -those who leave come back with a vengeance

    "You can't see me"
    -you can't help me

    "All food is equally satisfying"
    -no taste leads to monoculture which leads to blight which leads to famine

    "There is nothing past our borders"
    -whole town dies in forest fire because they refuse to move 20 ft outside borders

    "There can never be more than 10 people in a room a the same time"
    -the doctor is number 11

    "You must wear a red shirt if you are dissatisfied with the government"
    -everyone wears red coats out of the blue, instant revolution

    "My laws are the only laws"
    -natural laws disolve, cats chase dogs

    "No one shall come within 20 ft of me without my permission"
    -choking, can't, call, doctor...

    "Everyone must sleep from sundown to sunup"
    -I've fallen and I can't get up... ...till sunrise
    -eclipse

    "I am your father"
    -meant to be a means of demanding obediance and love, turns out to require you do what is expected of a father for every person at every social event - too many weddings, births, spankings, etc.
