\n So I'm working on this job application for superheroes, with four sorts of powers and four things they can effect, and I need an example of each to help potential applicants to place themselves.

I figure you can Create, Destroy, Control or Transform...Life, Matter, Energy or Space. So, sixteen combinations.

Some are pretty obvious - Destroy Matter, for example, could be "Shoots fireballs". Transform Space might be "Teleports".

Some are harder. Create Life? Any fool can do that after a little too much fortified wine. How does that map to a cool power? How do you Destroy Energy?

Anyway, help! I'm open to suggestions for changing my categories if these seem weak. These guys are not too smart and the job application needs to be really clear. Don't go all fanboy on me and point out that Disco Dazzler defies my neat categorization, though.

Create Life: I turn rocks into rock-men! or I raise the frickin' dead!
Create Matter: Out of thin air - an ice cream cone!
Create Energy: Lightning bolt! Lightning bolt!
Create Space:

Destroy Life: My hands are deadly cutting tools!
Destroy Matter: I shoot fireballs! or I have a disintegration ray!
Destroy Energy: I dampen inertia! or I freeze stuff! or I am made of darkness!
Destroy Space: I am telepathic! or

Transform Life: I turn into a shark!
Transform Matter: I can turn mud into diamonds!
Transform Energy: I can bake a pizza by punching dough!
Transform Space: I can teleport!

Control Life: I can summon an army of rats!
Control Matter: I have telekinesis!
Control Energy: I am the human magnet!
Control Space: I can fly! or I can stretch!

    \nWhat's the difference between Control and Transform?

    I imagine that Control would be making the subject do something and Transform means making the subject into something else. Controlling a river might allow you to change the direction it flows in, while Transforming the river might allow you to freeze it solid. Or, depending on how extreme you want to be, turn that river into a pitcher of lemonade.

    \n Jason,

    The problem I forsee with this system is it will ultimately promote mediocrity in superheroism. Captain Strongheart may use his super speed to save large numbers of orphans (and their pet frogs) from burning buildings. But on paper, some middle manager is just going to skim over his application and say, "Oh. Another speedster. That's all?" and chuck it in the reject pile. What exactly is Captain Strongheart to do? Save old ladies from muggers, then pull them aside and stammer, "Hey, um, could I get your contact information? I need a few good references..."? Meanwhile, Dwight Thunder has a complete suite of low-level powers which nicely pad out the application, but frankly, he's a bit of a slacker and only looking out for himself. Dwight Thunder is going to look like the safe hire. Meanwhile, Captain Strongheart is stuck trying to figure out how to get affordable health insurance.

    I mean, what possible fun could you have in pitting the cliches of superheroism against the banality of a modern corporate human resources department?

    \n I almost want to suggest a fifth verb, like "Understand" or "Sense" or "Observe," something like that. Sure, it's passive, but it also tends to produce really interesting effects.

    Sense Life could be effects like Telepathy. ("You can ferret out people with treasonous thoughts? Welcome to the CIA.")
    Sense Matter could be effects like Dowsing. ("You can sense explosive materials? Welcome to the TSA.")
    With Sense Energy, maybe you could have a power like Wireless did in Heroes, where she could sense and understand wireless communications. ("Welcome to the NSA.")
    Sense Space could, uh, I dunno, actually. But I'll bet NASA would know.

    Granted, it's not an active power and it tends to not be flashy, but maybe it's neat. Maybe it's already assumed as part of any other power, but I could see keen turned-up-to-11 uses of the Sense that wouldn't necessarily be a part of any of the other elements.

    Having played a super whose power was Create/Destroy/Control/Transform/Sense Matter, I can say that these verbs give an insane amount of latitude in what you can do, enough to where then alone might be useless to an HR department. Though, I could probably talk to you about me experience in taking civil service exams so I could work for the state of California, if that's a vibe you're interested in.

    \n Jason, is the verb/object form a necessary part of the game? 'Cause in the classic comic book super team you've got something like--

    * The Ultimate Bad-Ass
    * The Brick
    * The Super-Nerd
    * The Movement Guy
    * The Energy-Manipulation Guy
    * The Psychic Guy
    * The (Random Comic Cliche - usually some sort of shapeshifting thing)

    ...spread across 3-4 dudes, 2-3 hot chicks, and a couple of racial minorities.

    So it might be more like, just verbs -- Defeat, Endure, Discover, Transport, Manipulate, Influence, ________________. Pretty much every superhero team comic book or movie ends up exploiting these functions to set up combo's.

    Origins might work as the subject of the sentence: magic, technology, genetics, training, etc.
