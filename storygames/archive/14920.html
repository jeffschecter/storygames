    Posted By: Jason MorningstarPosted By: D-503Now I have to decide what gets chucked for it.
    \nFiasco, obviously.

    Nah, the writer left the core rules in that book so forcing me to have both. Supplement treadmill man, supplement treadmill. Edit: These big game designers, all they care about is the money. They don't care about the little guy at all.

    Ahem.

    Anyway, it was the Mission to Mercury playset. I played in a demo game at Leisure Games so it's possible others who were there are here. My only real query was the choice of playset since apparently that's an advanced one and we were all nnewbies, but we did ok.

    It was tons of fun. Our expedition fell apart in a welter of relationship issues (between my technical director and a drunken handyman), conflicts between the civilian and military mission commanders over jurisdiction and an ambitious number two who ended up assuming overall command but then suffered a tragic accident at the moment of triumph leaving him paraplegic and unable to enjoy his own success.

    The moment which sticks out most was when we had the funeral of the handyman who had been killed by security after attacking a senior officer in a moment of drunken fury. It was my scene and as my character tried to say some closing words to her former lover she played a recording she had found which she hoped would show the kind of man he was. I turned to the other player and asked him what was on the recording at which point he sang a drunken song in Russian and the rest of us had our characters standing there at this ghastly funeral pretending to be respectful while this blatantly inappropriate song played in the background.

    I also liked an early scene where the entire mission command structure lined up outside the dome following landing (which nearly went disastrously wrong as we established in a flashback). We all watched the Mercury landscape drinking in the history of the moment. Then the commander made a speech, someone quibbled about a point they made, and shortly afterwards the entire group dissolved into bitter arguments and recriminations - the Mercury landscape quite forgotten.
    It doesn't sound like much, but it was so petty on all sides that I rather loved it.

    Posted By: Steve SegedyPosted By: D-503Anyway, it was the Mission to Mercury playset
    \nI'm glad to see that you played Mercury! I think this is the first AP i've seen of it since the Companion came out.

    It's a very easy setting to picture. That said I kept forgetting it was on Mercury rather than Mars.

    The red dice mechanic didn't do much for me in all honesty, but flashbacks were pretty easy to work in. It just meant we had to check a couple of times that we all had the sequence of events straight in our heads. We had established there'd been an incident in orbit, and we flashed back to it during play and it helped flesh out character dynamics. That worked well. The confessionals didn't do any harm, but I thought they had less oomph than the more traditional scenes and the extra die colour seemed needless complexity.

    First playthrough though. With more experience I might well take a different view.
