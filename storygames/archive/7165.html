\n Over in the thread "What Is Story?" I wrote that "story" means a lot of things.

I think the useful question is not "what is story?" but "what structure and elements do you want in the stories you play?"

These are elements of form, because form is what gives meaning. Just ask a film editor -- he chooses to arrange clips in particular ways to produce particular impacts on the audience. That's all form. Form applied to content brings meaning. And for similar reasons the writer and the director each have input into what content to provide and in what order.

So for example, do you prefer a story to have a moment where a character gets what they deserve?

Of do you like stories that start in the middle, then introduce the elements, then resolve success or failure of the activity?

Do you prefer characters who are challenged by internal demons or external forces or both?

What structure and elements do you want in the stories you play?

Do you see them in the game designs you like?

    \n I'm currently writing the Pentasystem exactly to produce the kinds of stories I like.

    That is, stories in which people engage in conflict because of things they care about, are changed by the conflicts they engage in, and triumph through sacrifice and through putting their self-definition on the line.

    All of these are specifically built into the mechanics.
