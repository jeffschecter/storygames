\n Hey y'all, thought you might be interested in the new thing I wrote!

LINK TO DIADEM

Diadem is a game about the Roman Crisis of the Third Century, a period of the Roman Empire when everything was falling apart and it looked like it just might be the end. I've listened to a huge amount of Mike Duncan's "The History of Rome" podcast over the past few months, and wanted to do something with that knowledge and interest. The disastrous third century is really fascinating, and also presented a fantastic opportunity for a story!

In 270, the sitting emperor died suddenly of the plague. In real life, his presumed-successor Aurelian was primed and ready to take the job, and went on to reunify the empire. In Diadem, he's dead too. Now you, the high councilors of imperial army, are forced to make a difficult decision: who will be the next emperor?

This is a game of that squabble between five people with deeply crossed interests, all trying to get the best deal out of the new deal. It's a game of desperation, fear, ambition, and petty squabbling and maneuvering in a time when only unity can save the Empire.

The game's mechanisms were built out of Ben Lehman's incredible Amidst Endless Quiet, one of my absolute favorite games - it was a revelation when I realized my favorite sci-fi tough dilemma game was the root of my own Roman tough dilemma game. However, I've made some serious changes to just about every facet of AEQ (no central character, a vastly different tone, a steady worsening vs limited actions) to the point that I don't think it's all that recognizable.

I'm going to be playtesting it a bit at the end of the month, but I'm excited to share it and see what people think!
