    \n I visited Austin a couple of years ago for the NAMM convention (North American Music Merchants...I teach guitar for a living). You won't have any problem finding things to do.

    There's the bridge with the world's largest bat colony (Constitution Street?), tons and tons of really great restaurants (REALLY great restaurants), and the club scene of 6th street. I'd suggest finding the Continental Club and avoiding the dozens of clubs with decent bands covering Stevie Ray Vaughan tunes. There's a great psychobilly scene there, if I'm not mistaken. I think Miss Kitty (who was a runner-up for The Next Elvira) has a couple of musical projects in the area.

    If Redd Volkaert is playing the Continental when you're there, I'll hate you forever since he was out of town when I was there.

    Go to Stubb's barbecue. It's in an old warehouse or fortress or something, but it feels like you're in a castle. And the barbecue is amazing for not being Alabama or Carolina barbecue.

    As far as gaming? Eh. Sorry. I don't think I'd have time to even think about gaming in Austin. I'd move there if I could. Awesome town. Awesome.

    \n I'm in Austin, so I can talk about that. No idea about Houston.

    We've got a games on demand group Wednesday nights at a local game store (Dragon's Lair, directions) from 7-11pm. There's an improv superhero origins show running from this weekend through mid-October that I haven't seen yet but hear is awesome (Ka-Baam!). Another local game store (Great Hall Games) hosts fairly low key board gaming on Friday nights and Saturday afternoons and draws a small crowd.
