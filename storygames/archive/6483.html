\nCan D&D 3/4 even provide the "traditional" fantasy aesthetic?
\n
\nWhat I do know is that with 3e, between the system and the overall artistic style inferred by the book's art, it made it very hard to imagine that traditional fantasy "look", the LoTR or even the 1e/2e Forgotten Realms look.
\n
\nIts like, you can't really imagine with 3e a bar scene where you have a ranger in rusty chain mail, his scimitar leaning by his chair, smoking a clay churchwarden pipe sitting across from a gandalf-esque long-bearded wizard, getting ready to go on expedition to the orcish mines.
\n
\nYou'd have trouble imagining that because the "ranger" would in fact be a Ranger2/Thief3/Fighter3/Prestige Master3/Pipedude 2/Acrobat1/circus clown1/sportsman1; and the Wizard would actually be a Wizard4/sorceror3/thief1/barbarian1/shadow dancer3/Jedi2/pharmacist1.
\nYou'd have trouble imagining it because the Ranger wouldn't be in rusty chain mail, he'd be in some kind of gold-green coloured spiky bulby armour and have a giant flametongue +4 runesword with Dragonmarks (TM). And of course he wouldn't be smoking a pipe, that's politically incorrect.
\n
\nYou'd have trouble imagining it because the Wizard would in fact be a short half-tiefling chick in a revealing leather bikini (along with a bunch of buckles and straps in places where they're not really needed for anything) covered in tattoos with purple eyes and spiky green hair.
\n
\nAnd of course, they wouldn't be going to the Orcish mines; they'd be going to the Dire Gargantuan Orcs who have the half-draconic AND the half-celestial AND the half-infernal templates (that's right, that's 200% of orcs per orc: each orc is one-quarter dragon, one-quarter angel, one-quarter demon and one-quarter orc).
\n
\nAnd they probably wouldn't be in mines either, they'd be in motherfucking Mario's Palace.
\n
\n I dunno, what do you guys think of this post?
