    \n Torchbearer: our two characters make it out of the Vault of Stars with a treasure trove of books in hand, but run into the elf's ex-fiancee and mortal enemy outside of town. They're run to ground and trapped. Even though they're given an out, the elf and cleric start a kill conflict against the fiancee, who is a bad-ass elf warrior complete with chainmail and magical feinting sword. They roll high for disposition and she rolls low: they end up tied. Disposition goes up and down through attacks and defends, but she gets them on the ropes. They have one disposition left at the top of the round, and I choose feint for the fiancee's first action. They look and me and decide that I know that they know feint makes me most sense for her, and so they think I wouldn't do something that obvious. They choose defend.

    Total Party Kill.

    \n Playing "The Lost Generation" (Eclipse Phase setting, Don't Rest Your Head rules) at JiffyCon, we started the game with me in a tiny chair and five other players plus the GM opposite me, seated at a table. I was secretly given a character sheet, and the players were told to grill me with uncomfortable, nonsensical questions. Almost immediately:

    "Subject, how many times have you masturbated in the last month?"

    That really helped me get into my character. His character sheet happened to specify that he was an awkward nerd with maybe-not-unfounded paranoia that the doctors weren't really there to help him.

    (Also, it wouldn't be a gaming con if a stranger didn't ask me about my sexual habits.)

    \n [Archipelago in an awesome steampunk metropolis]

    One PC fulfills her destiny of leading an angry mob in rebellion against the police. Initially, the player just sort of outlines the rebels taking out the police station and moves to end the scene. I instead want to really get a sense for what happens, and so I ask her to 'describe it in detail'. The player thinks for a moment and starts describing how everything goes down. In the end, she brings her brother (a corrupt noble in charge of the police) up in a mock trial in front of the crowds and then she personally beheads him then and there. And then takes her personal airship into the sky so that she can drop her brother's head in a little box on a parachute down into the palatial estates where the noble families live.

    Which is a beautiful detail that we wouldn't have without those ritual phrases.

    \n Religious discussions in Masks of Nyarlathotep last session seemed to focus on the Catholic Church, probably because one of the PCs is a Catholic priest (and Jesuit). Two bits I remember:

    PC 1: Well, you already are a cult leader.
    PC 2: Now, now.
    Father O'Connor: Not a leader, not a cult.
    PC 1: Certainly, someone from his order could be Pope some day.
    Father O'Connor: That would mean something had gone horribly wrong.

    Later on (when I remembered to indicate who said what):

    Xian Shen: How may cults is the [Sinister Organization] tied up in?
    Father O'Connor: They're not worshiping the same god?
    Masha (former Russian nobility): Maybe it's related, like the Catholic Church and the Russian Orthodox Church.
    Father O'Connor (laughing): I was waiting to see where you'd go with that.
    Helen: Now, I don't remember much of Sunday School.
    Sarah Roth: So, one calls curses down on the other?
    Father O'Connor: No, not really.
    Masha: One is right; the other is wrong.
    Father O'Connor: And we'll leave it at that.

    \n Shadowrun - the players have found the mayor of Seattle in a very compromising situation and have blackmailed him. They are hiding away in the shadow clinic belonging to another character. The technomancer/hacker goes outside and says: yeah, I'll send some of our video to the mayor to remind him of the deal. Of course he gets hit by a hacker attempting to locate him, logs off, gets dumpshock (think getting hit over the head with a baseball bat) and can only just step on his commlink before he collapses. Luckily, we did not believe him.

    That made my top five of the most stupid actions I have ever seen in Shadowrun.
