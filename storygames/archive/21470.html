\n I\u2019m finishing up my demihumans classes and want different take on the Elf. More akin to a Killoren or Wilden (4e) or dryad.

These elves are tree-like fey. They are slender and graceful, but are much more feral in appearance. Their bones are hardwood, and their supple, barklike skin trails into viny tendrils and a cloak of leaves. As wilden age. their bodies move through the seasons. Young wilden are like trees in spring, filled with life and vigor. Their bodies a green hue. In the Summer phase. a widen's body is brown or tan, while the cloak of leaves remains vibrant green. Wilden in the autumn phase undergo a profound change, their skin darkening as their leaves turn color to reds, yellows, and oranges. Finally. the winter phase heralds the end of a wilden's life, as the body grows thinner and the wilden's leaves wither and fall. Each phase is around 100 years, so lifespan is 400 years.

As with the other fairies, they cannot abide the touch of cold iron. They also have a fear fires (anything greater than a lit torch or campfire). They have excellent vision in twilight or under moonlight\u2014though not in complete darkness. They have an aptitude for wilderness survival skills and can communicate with the animals of the forest. They can also cast faerie/glamour magic (natural\u2014no spellbook needed). They are attuned to the wild and while outdoors in natural surrounding, they gain bonuses on Dexterity and Wisdom. They have Second Sight as the other fairies. They are immune to charm and sleep spells. Hide ability when in their natural environment.

They do not wear armor. In battle, their skin turns hard as bark (natural armor)\u2014AC increases as they level. They are unrivaled in their skills with a bow.

This is the starting point, I need to tweak a bit. Also need to look at the dryad more closely.

Suggestions?

    \n Any interesting cultural elements? Sometimes those will inspire specific strengths and weaknesses, powers and limitations. Are they more like Tolkein's Ents or more like ElfQuest's Wolfriders? What sorts of communities do they live in -- large, small, isolated, intermingled with other races, etc.? Any particular religious/spiritual traditions?

    Supposing a Wilden wound up in a D&D adventuring party, what would that mean? Does mainstream Wilden culture view that sort of thing with esteem, indifference, disgust, or what? What expectations with the Wilden be bringing in regarding their adventuring comrades based on Wilden views?

    I could imagine any of this informing details about how they sleep, reproduce, deal with different sorts of dangers, etc.

    Two random ideas:
    - They could be very susceptible to fire. Maybe moreso when using barkskin.
    - They could be poisonous, and inflict poison damage on creatures who damage them with bite attacks.

    Finally, casting faerie magic with no spellbook and being immune to charm and sleep sounds like a very powerful package. I hope it balances well with your other character options.

    \n@David_Berg Thanks for the response!

    When I started looking into this Elfquest certainly came to mind as well as the Dryads and Wood Elves of Warhammer along with Birthright Elves.

    For my setting they are an ancient race\u2014long asleep\u2014who awoke at the Breaking of the World. There is only one race of elves and they are innately tied forests. These elves had no contact with humankind for countless years. After the cataclysm, those humans who survived eventually recovered and began migrating westward. They cut or burned down large tracts of forests. The elves fought back, but in the end knew that peace was the only choice for survival. The treaty allowed for wilderness preservations where the elves could dwell in solitude (like untamed islands surrounded by an ocean of humanity). They are a reclusive race, suspicious of humankind and many still harbor ill-will because of their forced containment.

    Each of these forests has a Heartwood, where an Elf-Queen holds court. Heartwood is where their lives originate from and where they will return to upon their deaths. The Heartwood is also home to many other fairies. An elven wood can be deadly perilous for human travelers, woodcutters and peasants gathering firewood are slain as brutally as warriors. There elven war bands charged with keeping their homelands free from outsiders. Beautiful, but alien, to behold they have a core of savagery that permeates their being.

    Most elves are xenophobic and reclusive. However, there are some who take a more pragmatic view and look to humans for ways to improve relations and increase cooperation. They will leave their woods\u2014often shunned by their own kind\u2014to find adventure and beat back the tide of evil growing on the far west borderlands. A few are overcome by an irresistible wanderlust to travel beyond the elven forests\u2014moving from one place to another, always observing but rarely making contact with the folk they meet. Elves have a foundness for trinkets (those not found in their woods) almost like the way Hobbits have Mathoms.

    Most humans view them with disdain and distrust, but also realize they can be powerful allies. So they are tolerated as adventurers.

    Not sure how in-depth I want to go with sleeping habits, reproduction, and so on\u2014maybe leaving some blank spots for others to adapt to their own campaigns.

    Yes, fire and cold iron they are very susceptible to.

    I don\u2019t think they\u2019ll be overpowered, once I tighten it all down.
