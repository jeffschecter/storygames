\n This is a crazy idea! Has anyone tried this?

Start a Monsterhearts game by going through the Setup phase of a Fiasco playset.

If it works, it could make a brilliant, simple way to put together a no-prep one-shot or con game scenario for Monsterhearts. I bet most of the playsets would work (even the ones in other genres).

There's some potential for the game to devolve into comedy, of course. (That could be a feature or a bug.)

Anyone more familiar than me with these two games want to point any other potential problems?

What could go wrong?

(Famous last words? I hope to find out sooner or later...)

    Some of the Fiasco playsets (especially the one about the Antarctic research station or whatever it was) could be really cool Monsterhearts settings, I think.

    \n I'm not familiar enough with Monsterhearts yet to speak to the mechanics, but we do talk a bit about using Fiasco as a starter for other games in the Companion. Basically we describe the process of using an appropriate playset and playing out a full game, then using the pieces of your story as the background for your (in this case) Monsterhearts game. That way you have a backstory to draw on, with characters, locations, etc. that can tie to your newly created characters.

    In any case, I'm very interested to hear how this works out!

    \n An interesting way to approach it is to have each player decide what skin they're playing but they don't get to play those in the Fiasco. In the Fiasco, they get to play regular students (or what have you) that are encountering indirectly the effects of being manipulated, preyed-upon, or savaged by the Monsterhearts characters the PC s will be playing. Give them an opportunity to see what it's like to be on the other side and to see their characters as being monsters with hearts somewhere within.
