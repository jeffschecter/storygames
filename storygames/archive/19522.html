This is a branching-off of the original "Hacking Mass Effect/Apocalypse World" thread.

Once upon a time there were as a great sci-fi video game by Bioware called Mass Effect. I really loved it, and so did a lot of other people! There's also this really fun and versatile game called Apocalypse World that is highly adaptable to other genres. A bunch of people have seen that game and wanted to play Mass Effect with it. And so they made hacks! There are no less than three other Mass Effect Hacks I've seen. Problem was, I don't like any of 'em. They don't do what I want them to do, and they don't seem to make the most of the framework and potential inherent in the Apocalypse World system.
I wasn't the only one who didn't like the existing hacks. JasonT made the thread above to start the process of building his own, and I helped pitch into the discussion a bit. Much later, he released what he'd come up with, and I personally still wasn't satisfied. So I thought, well, I have this pile of ideas I advocated for, and if the best way to get things the way I want is to make it myself, I should just do that!

And so I did.

This is the product of that experimentation. I've wrung the Mass Effect name off of the mechanics, though various elements are very obviously inspired by it. In general, The Shining Void is a science fiction hack of Apocalypse World meant to capture the action-packed lives of a crew of competent people aboard a starship, inspired by parts of Star Wars, Star Trek, Firefly, Farscape, and of course Mass Effect.

I'm very excited to finally present The Shining Void Version 0.1!

    \n So I played in a one-off of Shining Void this week. Overall, we had a really good time with it, and it felt very Mass Effect-y (playing the soundtrack helped). I loved the narrative focus of the basic moves and the two-part character sheets. It's very polished and playable for a 0.1 beta! Minor wording issues (which will almost definitely be polished in revisions), I only really have one significant piece of feedback.

    The fictional trigger for Support Your Team move felt too narrowly defined. There's only so many times the characters can pull aggro and distract the enemy before it starts feeling a bit boring; in practice, it's also pretty similar to the Bold Aggression move, which also involves occupying the opponents' attention (by trying to kill them).\n
    Support Your Team: When you ignore all other priorities completely occupy the opponents\u2019 attention, roll +Paragon. On a 10 or greater, your team is safe as you take on all the impending danger yourself, and you take +1 to rolls as you continue to distract your enemies from your allies. On a 7, 8, or 9, the next person you\u2019re supporting that makes a move gets +1 to the roll, but you must select one:
    * you\u2019re cut off and exposed to the full danger,
    * you only bought time, and not much of it,
    * they managed to get at your team a bit before you distracted them
    \n[I think there's also a typo in the move - shouldn't it be "your team takes +1 to rolls as you continue..." rather than just "you?"]

    What I felt the Basic Moves were missing was a broader Paragon-based "Support Your Team" move, that might include occupying the enemy's attention, but would also account for situations like rushing into fire to rescue a fallen comrade, or knocking down a pillar to provide cover for a teammate. I'm no game designer, but I would envision it something like this:\n
    Support Your Team: When you ignore all other priorities and your own safety to aid or protect your comrades, roll +Paragon. On a 10 or greater, your team is safe as you take on all the impending danger yourself, and your allies take +1 to rolls as you continue to support them. On a 7, 8, or 9, the next person you\u2019re supporting that makes a move gets +1 to the roll, but you must select one:
    * you\u2019re cut off and exposed to the full danger,
    * you only bought time, and not much of it,
    * your team is exposed to some smaller part of the danger
    \nAnyway, we're hoping to play again sometime soon. Thanks for putting together a great hack!

    \n I really like three of your stats and their accompanying moves - paragon, renegade, and experienced, along with their accompanying moves aggression, support, and dig in. One small thing I take issue with are a few of the names, specifically "Experienced", especially as you're keeping "XP" - maybe call the stat "Veteran", which makes sense alongside Paragon or Regenade, all three are more of an earned title or description than an attribute.

    What I'm not sold on is lumping everything remotely strange into the "Alien" category. As the game is set up right now, it's pretty solid for everyone who wants to play a human space marine with a big gun or a badge. Everyone else is lumped into one stat. I dislike how there's no stat-wise distinction from biotics and multitools, Adepts and Techs, Asari and Salarian. There's also, unless I'm mistaken, no roll for Perception/Read a Situation or Person.

    My suggestion would be to re-divide it into Sharp and Weird, or whatever names you go with. Put Multitool and some kind of perception roll on Sharp, and biotics and enact a crazy plan on Weird. I feel like these are not only different enough in-fiction to deserve a second stat, but also, and imho more importantly, the type of characters who would use each stat are distinct. I like the idea of a nerdy tech who doesn't know much about these weird aliens, or an intuitive biotic at home amidst strangeness but who's not what you'd call the sharpest knife in the drawer. It also forces the more straightforward characters to make a more interesting decision about what they're good at other than their specialty.

    \n Hey folks. The new version is in production still, and it's slow going. Just wanted to put a little update in: I think I solved Support Your Team.

    SYT needed a rewrite. It was bulky and not actually useful - Help was universally better. For a few minutes I just folded SYT entirely and just renamed Help to SYT, but lacking an exclusive Paragon move wasn't a good idea. Now I have this:

    Support Your Team (+Paragon)
    When you devote your efforts to aiding an ally\u2019s move, roll +Paragon. If they Trust you, add an additional 2 to your roll. You will share in any consequences of their move unless stated otherwise. On a 10 or greater, add 1 to their roll and choose up to two of the following choices. On a 7, 8, or 9, choose one of the following options.
    ~ Add an additional 1 to their roll,
    ~ You take on all the consequences of their move instead of them,
    ~ You suffer none of the consequences of their move.

    It folds in +Paragon, Trust, boosting rolls, distractions, and denying responsibility. It should be obvious that even if you roll 10+ you can't take both the second and third option, and this will be specified later in the text to avoid confusion. Anyone see any gaping holes that my 4am brain is missing?

    \n Things that flitted through my mind with the below- Mass Effect, Fading Suns, Eclipse Phase, Firefly, Star Wars, Star Trek, Alastair Reynolds, Peter Hamiliton, Iain Banks, and so on. That may or may not align with your list of inspirations, so YMMV with the below.

    'The Existential Threat'- All life in the galaxy is threatened!
    'The Ascended' - God-like beings return/continue to meddle. See above.
    'Pandoras Box' - Something very, very old and probably very, very powerful is discovered. Tampering with it, or not, will have consequences.
    'First Contact'- may overlap with existential threat, but can just be an equivalent civilisation with a countdown to war. May include newly sentient AI.
    'The Other'- Something even-more-alien, darkness-between-the-stars type. Maybe corrupted AI, Eclipse Phase style.
    'The Displaced' or Dispossessed - perhaps fleeing another threat, they are a drain on resources, compromise security and pose moral quandries in the face of other pressures.
    'The System' - oppressive space empires, security states, high taxes, repression
    'Panopticon' - everything, everywhere, is recorded and logged.
    'The War'- civil, cold, hot, we're just trying to survive on the margins
    'Prometheus' - unsupervised research at the edge of space, unleashed AI, Nanoware gone wild, rifts opened, our hubris comes back to haunt us.
    'TransSentientBeingness' - what does it mean to be Human/Krogan/AI/genespliced/Uplifted in a world of...

    Something I feel occurs in the fiction that you might want to consider a means to support is layers and revelations as Fronts are dealt with- how the resolution (or realisation) of one threat reveals it to be merely a precursor for something much, much worse (and usually older and further up the evolutionary ladder).

    Re-reading, not sure if I've got not a list of Fronts here, rather than Threats though.

    A brief look at existing PbtA threat models:
    Apocalypse World: Threat categories and subtypes, category moves and individual instincts.
    Monsterhearts: No threat types.
    Dungeon World: Threats exploded out into a full bestiary.
    Sagas of the Icelanders: If I remember right, also no threat types (which surprises me, maybe I need to read the Sagas book again with a look at how it handles long-form).

    Hmm. Maybe that last one would work. Maybe I don't even need a list of threat types so much as lists to help build individual Singularities (aka Fronts). I'd need some consideration of threat type (at the level of place/person/institution), motives, maybe strengths or other properties. Then taking the general properties of the Singularity to fractal inward and create the more specific aspects of it.
    If designed on the large-scale instead of individual entities generated as events unfold, it'd be easier to do that unfolding reveals thing.

    Okay, so I guess the new question I'm working with is:
    What are the important aspects of an antagonist structure overall? What elements need to be added in the transition from talking about it overall to talking about individual aspects of the structure in order to feel both specific and subordinate to the overall Singularity?
