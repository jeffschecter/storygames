      \n We're making a slight change in how we're handling pre-scheduled games. We realized it didn't make sense to ask GMs to blindly send in their games, not knowing what anyone else was offering; after all, GMs are players too! We want everyone to be able to see and discuss what they want to play.

      So, please see the Game Sign-Up page on the GPNW site. There's now a table with the session blocks; if you're interested in running a particular game during a particular session, just add your name and game system to the appropriate cell in the table. This way, everyone can see what people are proposing to do and there's time for GMs to move around their games to different slots if they want to avoid conflicting with a game they want to play in.

      Please continue to discuss here what you want to run or play, and use the wiki page just to see how the schedule's shaping up.

      Joe, to answer your question, I'd say if there's something you definitely plan to run no matter what, it's a good idea to schedule it up so other people know it's coming, but it's fine to leave blocks unscheduled too and arrange things in the moment. Ultimately we'll have a mix of pre-scheduled and impromptu games no matter what. Also, we have a row on the schedule for "open gaming" if people just want to indicate their willingness to be available to run whatever for whomever's available at that time.

      The game of Civilization, Tragedy and Triumph

Bloodshed and oppression are always on hand as tools toward your aims, but run the risk of increasing suffering in the world. When a Champion's ambition resolves in Victory or Defeat, you chart out its effect on the world, then continue play in a new Epoch with new Champions, as Hope and the beast race toward Utopia or Armageddon!
