\n Hi Everyone.

Children of the Fall (my new story game) is rapidly approaching the phase where it needs a final "blind" play-test. I humbly approach the community, cap in hand, asking for your assistance. I'd really appreciate help in the form of constructive play-tests. I'm able to offer a credit (obviously), along with a copy of the game when it is finished as well as PDF's of all the other Frenzy Kitty Games, along with my eternal gratitude. The game WILL come out (last year I published 2 full RPGs, namely KARMA and DUSK along) along with a handful of microrpgs so you know you will be contributing to a project that will see the light of day.

Here's the blurb from the book to give you an idea of what the game is all about. If you are interested in potentially play-testing please let me know.

In children of the fall, the players play as the sole survivors of an apocalypse that has turned all the adults on the planet into evil, bloodthirsty savages known as the fallen. As a group, the players will explore and develop the world and story together in a completely collaborative system. Players will need to keep an eye on the ever shifting hierarchy of their tribe, build and grow their haven and do the jobs that need doing to ensure the tribe\u2019s survival. Agency is shared amongst the players, and the game uses a brand new streamlined and simple rule set, which keeps all the players engaged in every scene.

    \n Hi frenzy,
    I co-organize a rpg playtest group in Seattle. It\u2019s called The RPG Workshop. You can look us up through Meetup.com. We have a cue, so I\u2019d have to put your game on that. Could probably test your game in the next 2-6 weeks. It all depends on how many people show up. Our sessions are about 4 hours and typically consist of a mix of regulars and new or infrequent Participants. The former category is composed of experienced story gamers many of whom are designing and sharing games. The later group is mostly rpg gamers of more traditional stripes but also contains people who show up with little to no rpg experience. It\u2019s often amazing how successfully they (rpg greenhorns) participate.
    Our group size ranges from about 12+ on busier days down to about 6 so we usually test 2-3 games per session.
    We\u2019ve heretofore tested only in-house games, but have discussed the idea of cold testing games in the way you are seeking.
    Before I commit, I\u2019d like to do two things: I\u2019d want to read through your \u201cChildren of the Fall\u201d to make sure it\u2019s a good match for us, and I\u2019d want to discuss this opportunity with the other organizer of the group.
    Let me know if you still have a need and want us to playtest your game.

    Congrats on finishing your game! It sounds very cool.
    Davey
