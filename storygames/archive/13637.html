    \n I'm still mulling over the details, but I'm imagining a co-operative economic game with a time limit. You and the other players are raising dragons to compete in the upcoming county fair. You're budgeting your money and time to raise the best dragons.

    For example, an early choice is buying dragon eggs. When you buy a dragon egg, you spend a coin to pull a card from the egg deck without looking at it. You can pull one card per gold coin you spend. You then look at the cards to see what kind of dragon will hatch from each egg. Choose one egg to keep. You shuffle the rest back into the egg deck. This emulates spending a bunch of money to have a pick of the litter or just tossing in a coin and taking your chances.

    Various foods could raise your dragon's stats, but take time to complete. I also imagine a slight twist on a typical co-op victory: There are multiple levels of success, like ribbons ranging from white to blue. There might also be more exotic fantasy-themed prizes like the Vorpal Trophy.

    \n This makes me think of a series of books I thoroughly enjoyed as a kid the "Pit Dragon Trilogy" by Jane Yolen. Basically, the main character is slave who must work his way up from washing dragons and cleaning poop to fighting his dragons and becoming a dragon master (ie. cock fighting) - It goes into detail about all the nasty aspects of taking care of dragons, from poop, to training, to breeding. Dragons are still big and mean, but basically just animals with nothing magical about them. It's kind of the same as Pern dragons - they're science fiction aliens on another planet, not magical beasts.
    \n
    \nI imagine the game of Dung and Dragons would be similar - you are a dirty stable boy and have to do the nitty gritty of taking care of a dragon, and your care eventually leads to the Dragons fate & personality - an angry fighter? a champion racer? a sweet gelding pet? or maybe they just go off to the slaughter house? Maybe you even have to develop skills to avoid the dragon killing you while you scoop?

    \n I am reminded of an old user name of mine; Stercus Accidit ("Dung Occurs"; latin does not appear to have any closer way of saying "shit happens". Perhaps this complete lack of fatalism was behind their tendency to mercilessly crush all who opposed them.)

    Dung and Dragons: a roleplaying game about constantly being shat on by fate.

    Your characters are peasants in a fantasy world. (If you must, pick some 0 level Peasant or Village Idiot NPC's from some other game supplement) Think Pythonesque Jabberwocky. Every day, roll initative. The winner gets to decide what horrible medieval Indignity strikes the character belonging to the player who is next in the initative sequence. While terrible disfigurement, bereavement and constant pain is perfectly acceptable, no *PC* ever actually dies, though he may beg you to. That is, until a Hero comes along.

    Sometimes, a Hero, or worse, a Band of Heroes, pass by the Village. They usually just pass trough, grabbing vital supplies, seducing the daughters of the village, and complaining about the beer. But sometimes (oh woe is you) they have a Quest, and you are the extras. Those guys who die screaming in the first scene as the demon erupts from the nostrils of the village midwife (now who is going to deliver your next kid in a few weeks?), or the, people whose orchard and mud hut gets incinerated when yes, the DRAGON finally catches up with the heroes and decides to reclaim its treasure, by melted, horrifically twisted weight if necessary.

    This will go on and on and on, until someone decides they've had enough of both Dung and Dragons, gangs up on the next bunch of heroes, kills them and takes their stuff. After that? Dragon? Phew. Bring it ON! We have three Rods of Lordly Might, the Lightning Spear of the Elven Archonts, and a pile of Wands of Fireball for the after-battle Dragon Roast. Switch to Seven Samurai mode.

    Maybe some of the rules from My Life With Master would do for this one...

      \n Holy, crap, we had a sweet-smelling game of Dung & Dragons last night!

      We ran into one problem early on. The rules for wandering around in the darkness are crap and the game went down the toilet. I swear I was Dung Mastering a perfect game when Kingsley ended up with a turd of a roll that forced the bullshit out into the open, and it really hit the fan. He kept calling me a shitty DM. Just then the wife interrupted and saved the night when she brought in plates of S.O.S. Usually, I like this English treat, but for some reason it tasted of buffalo chips. Ben got the runs.

      Back in the game, the characters made a movement into the sewage system where they were almost overwhelmed by the smell of nitrogen when stepping into the layer of Giant Bat Dragons. Covered in guano, they mucked around until they fell into a troll's compost pile. They failed a flatulence check, which woke the trolls, who tied the adventurers up and made them sit in the corner on stools. Soon, with several successful ca-ca rolls, Kingsley managed to escape, but not before he was poisoned by night soil. On the edge of death, he finally found the cure, a dingleberry. I about crapped my pants, because even though I know the system, I didn't see that one coming -- it came on so fast.

      Overall, it liked the system. It's a mother of a game. I describe it as a traditional game after a cold enema. The designer flushed out the bad and kept in the good.
