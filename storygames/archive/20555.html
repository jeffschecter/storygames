    \n At a long-con a few months ago I ran a Ghost in the Shell game using the quick start rules from Dark. My favorite version of GitS is Stand Alone Complex, so I wanted to highlight the investigation via intrusion. I used the lockpicking rules for beating any kind of security measure, including hacking a computer. I even had quick and dirty active-camouflage which seemed to work well.

    This version of Dark worked well, but it didn't support the story out of the mission very well, that was all on me and my players. I don't know if more narrative elements are coming in later versions. After having played, I would have changed the loot rules, but since I'm not planning another game of this right now, I haven't come up with a solution.

    It did do a great job of highlighting the action and allowed me to create fun and intense missions on the fly. All told, we played about 16 hours over the course of 4 sessions in three days. With 2 players, we ended up with a story that actually paralleled very closely with the Laughing Man event, I hadn't started out that way, but it kept falling into place.
