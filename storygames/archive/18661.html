    \n The best resource is probably the Fate Fractal post that Fred made. Aren't there a few Extras examples in that chapter, though?

    Fate Accelerated actually has a nifty template for Stunts. I'd download it (pay what you want!) and read up on that bit. I don't remember the formula off the top of my head, but it's something like "Because I'm X, when I do Y I get a +1 bonus to [Approach Type] doing [Action Type] (Overcome, Create Advantage, Defend, Attack)."

    So like "Because I've been trained in elite combat, when I'm in a hand-to-hand fight, I get a +1 bonus to Carefully creating advantage."

    \n Dresden Files is my most played Fate game, I believe in it there were a few general templates for stunts (or I extrapilated these templates from the examples in that book, one or the other):

    Substitute a skill for another skill.
    Ex:(snappy name here) - Use Shooting in place of Athletics to defend against being shot.

    +2 to skill in X circumstance
    Ex: Never Tell Me The Odds - when driving eratically to loose a persuer gain a +2 to Drive (or pilot if you're flying a starship ;-) )

    +4 to a skill in X circumstance at a -2 penalty to 2 skills
    Ex: There's always a Clue - +4 to investigation at a - 2 to notice and athletics.

    For a BIG list of examples check here http://www.jimbutcheronline.com/bb/index.php/topic,21952.0.html

    It's geared to Dresden, but the ideas should work for Core's smaller skill list just fine.

    \n If you're just getting going with Fate, it's worth remembering that Extras are just that - Extra, and in my experience you can get away without Stunts initially (the players can create them and write them in as they go) as long as you're clear about the scope and power level of the characters. Most of the time, Stunts just help in one specific circumstance, so it's only if that specific circumstance is triggered that they even come into play - in my experience, you might hit a couple of stunts once each during a session, as opposed to Aspects which players are hitting all the time.

    The list of stunts from the Spirit of the Century SRD might be useful for inspiration too (NB they aren't 100% balanced, I think I read on G+ somewhere, with Fate Core, but they do give you an idea of the scope and impact of Stunts in Fate). They're here.

    That said, my rough-and-ready formula for magic/clerical powers I'm using in my Fate Core Fantasy game is this: you need to have an Aspect reflecting you magical training/abilities, and a Stunt "You can cast magic" and some idea of the scope of the magic (this can start off fairly loose). This lets you buy a skill "Magic" or "Miracles" or "Vampiric Power" or something that you can use to Overcome, Create Advantage, and maybe Attack (in specific circumstances - for instance, my weather mage can only Attack if she's outside) - a different power might allow Defend to be used instead in specific circumstances. It seems to balance OK - but then, we're only 1 session in.

    Well, how would I do something like playing an android who can't take stress from things like lack of air, or food/sleep
    \nKeep in mind that characters don't normally stress from those things, regardless of whether they're androids. (Well, you might handle asphyxiation by inflicting stress, since a scene in which the air is running out probably has a tight time limit.)

    Fate stress isn't damage like D&D hit points. It's a pacing mechanism for conflicts. That's why stress goes away at the end of the scene.

    A character who's starving or sleep-deprived should probably have an attached situational aspect --- maybe a consequence.

    Your android can be handled either by just giving it an aspect, or by making it take a stunt, depending on how much of an advantage you think it grants, and how much of a downside there is to being one. (Androids might not suffer from human diseases, but they might be subject to computer viruses. Androids might not have to sleep or eat, but they might have to take regular downtime to defragment their memories and recharge their batteries. Etc.)
