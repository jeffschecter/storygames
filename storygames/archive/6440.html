\n Mike Holmes is thinking about El Dorado, and blending competition with story to produce a game. It's all good stuff, and I don't want to derail it. But something he said sparked a "wait, that's not true..." response in me, so I want to put it up here in a separate thread. I've underlined the part that I disagree with.\n
Posted By: Mike HolmesThe point being that in no other human endeavor called a "Game" does human judgment have so important an impact on the outcome as in a RPG. Usually in games we do all we can to ensure that the rules can be dealt with in as objective a fashion as possible. In RPGs this simply isn't usually possible.
\nI do want to add something that isn't meant as a tangent, but another avenue you could explore in order to gain some additional techniques or resources for building roleplaying games.

We have things called "reality tv shows" which are both games and competitions. In the vast majority of these, human judgment is vitally important. Shows like America's Next Top Model, Survivor, Top Chef, Beauty and the Geek, and Project Runway all incorporate both a competitive aspect and "game rules" based on subjective decisions.

The same phenomenon exists in other events -- such as beauty contests, or chili cook-offs, or dog shows.

What do these kinds of "games" or pseudo-games have to teach game designers about the role of subjective human judgment?
