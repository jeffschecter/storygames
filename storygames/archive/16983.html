\n Beyond the Wall and Other Adventures is an OSR game designed to get a group playing out of the box and done by bedtime.

We do this with a combination of playbooks and scenario packs. The players and game master build their characters -- and the adventure -- together by discovering each other's histories. We've started out with village kids on their first great adventure: a cult threatening their village.

Anyone with a background in OSR games will already be comfortable with the core rules of Beyond the Wall, although we have changed the magic system to better reflect our influences (A Wizard of Earthsea and The Chronicles of Prydain).

While no-prep oneshots with a familiar old system were the big deal, the game's core supports -- or should support -- full campaign play.

If any of you have some time to take a look and let me know what you think, I would greatly appreciate it. I am happy to answer any questions.

www.flatlandgames.com/btw/

    \nI know what you mean John, part of the reason WtDiG works is because people don't exactly remember what happened.

    But I spent a lot of time today at work pondering this (sorry work!) and I think you could do a hack if you wanted to run them up against each other. Firstly, because the players would be free to disagree about some of the interpretations of what happened in the first game (which could be fun) and secondly because they could misremember other events and things from the world that didn't get played though - even using some of the stuff in the playbooks and GM scenario as the story seeds.

    Not quite the same game but if you wanted to run them together (and it would be kinda cool!) I reckon it would be doable.
