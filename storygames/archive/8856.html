\n Having received my copy of Lacuna, I'm prepping for a game. I like playing music during my games for atmospheric purposes, and I thought this was the perfect album for lacuna: Nine Inch Nails: The Slip. It's a free download for those of you who would like to use it yourselves.

Anyone have any other suggestions for Lacuna music? I have other NIN songs that fit well, and I'm thinking of throwing in some Muse, but I'm sure there are hundreds of appropriate bands I've never even heard of, so fire away.

I'm also thinking of making one of the static gaining conditions being related to the music, but I can't think of a good criteria. My first thought was when a song ends, but that would met out about 20 static an hour, which I'm not sure is ideal (is it? I've never played. Is that too fast? too slow? or does it not really matter?). Ideas are welcome!

    \n If I were really going to sit down and invest effort in the musical experience, I'd make three different soundtracks: "Resting", "Target", and "Maximum".

    The first would be full of sad and melancholy tunes, with more than a few oldies. ("Blue City is beautiful in the way a sad girl can be beautiful...")

    The second would be your basic "pump you up" action sequence soundtracks. Stuff to play when you're in the zone, kicking ass.

    The last would be hard core, fast paced techno. If the theme of "Target" is "kicking ass", the theme of "Maximum" would be "out of control". Stuff incorporating sirens and alarms, or, of course, static. Almost painful to listen to.

    Progress between the soundtracks as appropriate.

    \n I find music to be a huge part of my game experience. Having background music was something I began doing years ago, when I ran some of my first Vampire games as a teenager. As my musical tastes changed, so did the way I incorporated music into my games.
    \n
    \nFor Lacuna, I would generally use minimalistic ambient. My Blue City has a somber tone amidst a surreal backdrop: Cliff Martinez's Solaris and Traffic soundtracks; soundtracks from the video games EVE and Homeworld; Phillip Glass; Bisosphere; Aphex Twin; etc... However, as all have noted in this thread so far, specific music styles will elicit specific setting aesthetics. As the game progressed, and heartbeat and static rose, I would increase the tension. Unconnected from that rise in tension, I would also change the mood and level of action in the music to suit the various scenes.
    \n
    \nHere's how that works: I GM from my laptop and I have multiple playlists which are numbered based on Action, Tension, and Mood. For example: 313 would be High Action, No Tension, Happy; whereas 231 would be Medium Action, Haunting (Tension), Sad. I use a cross-fader to transition between each (random) track on the playlist and between each of the various playlists. I also use a player toolbar that let's me change the playlist in 2 mouseclicks (which could be assigned to hot keys if one were so inclined). So, for Lacuna, any change in the scene's mood or level of action would warrant a different playlist; meanwhile, making sure the tension level moves ever upwards throughout the Mystery Agent's dive into Blue City.
    \n
    \nNow I just have to work on my GMing skills ;)
