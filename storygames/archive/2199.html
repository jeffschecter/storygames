I'm having some trouble with the board for GRS. It's...okay...but a bit dull. It just needs something and I don't know what.

Here is is, in the universally accepted and loved Microsoft Word format.

Front left of nightclub
Front right of nightclub
Back left of nightclub
Back right of nightclub

And it works like this. Those pink explosions are Insertion Points: where each Squad Member starts. Then they make their way through the nightclub. Those blue arrows are guard patrol routes.

Also, there's loads of security devices that aren't shown on the board. The first part of the game consists of the players putting down markers on the board and saying "This is a triple-strength steel security door with voiceprint detection access!".

And the blue star is the target: the guy you've got to turn straight.

Basically, it's meant to be a sort of Metal Gear Solid vibe. It's a throwback to a game called Escape From Colditz which I had as a kid. It's meant to be all secret passages and tunnels and guards to bribe and stuff like that.

But it's missing something. It's not grabbing me. What does it need? What kind of stuff would you want on here?

Graham

    Not gay enough. This board is almost completely divorced from your game's color, like you literally translated that Colditz game to a nightclub layout. The board needs to be littered with stuff that players can jokingly observe, "That's so gay!" or "That's so hetero!" I'm seeing only two opportunities that cry out for gay jokes: the "Back Passage," and the "manhole" in the women's bathroom.

    The more campiness, the better.

    Also, it seems that setting up some Mission Impossible-style series of objectives might be cool. Like, first you have to break into the office and steal a blank VIP ID card, then you have to charm one of the guards into parting with the VIP password, and finally you can get into that VIP room. (In video game terms, you have to get the red key, the blue key, the yellow key...) Otherwise, I'm not seeing why the players don't just make a beeline for the VIP room and ignore the rest of the complex.
