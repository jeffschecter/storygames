\n This is a thread about games you'd like to play in the near-future.

Bonus points if you make such a game happen and come back to post a link to an AP thread.

Conspiracy of Shadows in Ravenloft: I want it to be a crack team of paladins and clerics who were sent by the Gods and Goddesses of the Heavens to destroy the demi-plane of Dread but the Mists are doing their work and making them forget...

When you give in to Doom, you become the Lord of your own Domain.

With Great Power...: I'm dying to play this effing game, particularly a Fantastic Four, family of adventurers into the great unknown type of game with travels into infinity and human family drama.

Shock: Social Science Fiction: One session for each of the planets (including Pluto, dammit) in a shared universe with some character cross-over. Basically, 9 science fiction short stories in the solar system.

Dogs in the Vineyard: I'd like to play a series of Dogs towns, maybe based on holidays in the calendar. I've got a Halloween one in my head. I've played one-shots and I played about four towns in a mini-campaign but I'd like to commit to running X number of towns with the towns pre-made, then tailoring them a bit to the PC's as needed.

Sorcerer: I'd still like to give Heavenly Kung-fu a shot and I've got some vague-ass idea in my head concerning demons as playing cards and gambling or some such nonsense. Something about a finite amount of demons with a structure to them pleases me. And there's the idea about demons being contracts with Satan that has some legs to it.

Y'know, maybe I'll scrap my usual menu and run a few of these at Dreamation. Hm.

And you?

    \n Hmmm... I've got some new additions to the menu.

    First off, and always, more Criminal Element: A Game of Desperate Deeds. I came up with a new Trust and Betrayal mechanic that works like a champ in my head, but I need to get a playtest of it. Also have some clarified Advantage rules that cleans up a lot of the text, so need to give those a pass, too.

    Got Reign in the mail last week and I really like it. I don't feel like I'll be able to understand the scope of the Company rules until I actually run a game of it, though, so that goes on the menu as well.

    I've had a hankering for some Tribe 8 lately, as well. The problem is that I don't really care for the silhouette system. The resolution for this, of course, is to use Shadow of Yesterday for the rules set and just port over the setting, make some new Secrets, and bring over the funky magick rules. That's probably going to be sometime in November.

    Beyond that I'm still looking to get back to some Sorcerer. My last game fell apart under the weight of time, so I'm just itching to get back to running something Sorcerer-y.

    Finally, I simply must play Sons of Liberty. Must. I live in Philadelphia, grew up in New Jersey, and need to kick ass with clockwork mecha.

And I don't mean fun like the Roach, where the dark humor encourages inhuman behavior and infighting. GR is the kind of satisfying fun you get from weaving a really compelling story together with your friends, and it's not as challenging as you might think- the folks I played with at Gen Con were completely new to GM-less games, scene-framing, etc. and they picked it up in no time and had a blast!

I'd run the thing myself. The twist would be that each character is/was tied passionately with the witch. Possible connections (to be chosen or drawn randomly) would include:

    A The Witch's first love
    B The Witch's legal spouse
    C The Witch's current lover
    D The person who was dumped for the witch's current lover
    E A relative of the witch, once involved in an incestuous affair, or they at least considered it
    F A person madly in love with the witch, but rejected

    and so on, the possibilities are limitless. I'd tie these to Fates and Zodiacs in some way. The idea is to make the Witch an out-of-control, destroying force that is way too badass to be taken out, unless by people who are very close. But will the characters be able to cooperate when there are former rivalries there and when some of them are still madly in love?
