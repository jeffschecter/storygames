\n We haven't had many play challenges, lately, so here's one.

I like historical settings, but we don't use them much.

So, try setting your next game (that is, the next one you haven't planned) in the year 1851. It's a good year. There's a lot going on. Pick somewhere and something that interests you, then choose a system to suit and play a game around it.

Then, once you've done it, post on Story Games. And then we will have the most extensive historical knowledge of 1851 ever.

Meanwhile, if you like, use this thread to post interesting and playable things that happened in 1851.

    \n The word "scientist" appeared for the first time in 1840, as a deliberate coinage (see Raymond Williams's discussion in Keywords: A Vocabulary of Culture and Society). The term "science fiction" was used first in 1851 (in Chapter 10 of William Wilson's A Little Earnest Book upon a Great Old Subject): "Science-Fiction, in which the revealed truths of Science may be given interwoven with a pleasing story which may itself be poetical and true."


    Singer sewing machine company incorporated, and drum washing machine invented, 1851.

    The Great Exhibition of the Works of Industry of all Nations, aka the first world's fair, was convened by Prince Albert in 1851.

    Exhibits included The Tempest Prognosticator, also known as the Leech Barometer, is a 19th century invention by George Merryweather in which leeches are used in a barometer. The twelve leeches are kept in small bottles inside the device; when they become agitated by an approaching storm they attempt to climb out of the bottles and trigger a small hammer which strikes a bell. The likelihood of a storm is indicated by the number of times the bell is struck.

    \n Ho! First AP! I was showing the guys at work a storytelling card game I've been working on (working title: "That's Drama") and they, independently of me or this thread, decided they wanted it in Texas in the 1850s. So I said, "Let's make it 1851, there was a challenge on storygames."

    Unfortunately, we know next-to-nothing about the time period, and we didn't bother to look anything up. We thought the Mexican-American War hadn't happened yet. So I doubt this will help Graham's agenda.

    FWIW - our hero had his foot shot off by some bank robbers and was bent on revenge - and after he got his foot amputated at some federal fort somewhere near the New Mexico side of Texas, the Fort's twin-brother psychologist/counselors (looking it up now I see that Freud wasn't even born until 1856; we were inspired by 'Frontier Psychologist') agreed to go with him to hunt the bank robbers down. They tracked them to some caves in New Mexico, and then there was a lot of gunfire and being-eaten-by-coyotes - the sharpshooting psychologist, wounded in the battle and knowing he was done for, went down in a blaze of glory, running from cover and killing the remainder of the bank robbers. Then they had to choose between carrying the sack of bank robbers money out or their fallen friend - they chose the money, but they turned it into the authorities.
