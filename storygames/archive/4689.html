    I haven't the slightest idea what "EDC" and "CMS" are, but they look like attributes on a standard 3-18 scale (along with a "Luck" stat); there's a D&D-esque percentile XP modifier; what I assume is a "Language Read/Write" number; a place to list how many henchmen you have; a "Latent Psi" section; and some other vaguely old-school D&D stats scattered here and there.     \n It's a character sheet from "Squamous" the game of Lovecraftian horror, from the perspective of the Lovecraftian horrors. The stats you can see on the sheet are as follows:

    #HNCHMN: I'm not sure why this wasn't obvious, but it's the number of henchmen that can be eaten every round. It's modified by your "MAW" score (obscured by the pencil).

    CMS is "Cthonian Millenia Score", the number of centuries your character has lurked beneath the ground, awaiting the right alignment of the stars. As you can see, it modifies "Suasion", which is the initial impetus to enact the character's evil and alien plan.

    EDC is "Eidolon Counter", of course. It should be pretty clear what that does, when you see the "Lungs, Removed/Withered" score following it, and the box for marking your number of insane Professor worshippers.

    LCK is your tongue strength, which modifies the chance of victims exploding instantly(this character has a pretty low score), Sternutation (breath weapons), and so on. This is a first ed. character sheet, because in second ed. they removed "Gambolling", since it was rarely used.
