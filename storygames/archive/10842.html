\n Blake Deakin recently commented that this is the time of year his group looks to The Mountain Witch again, and it occurred to me that I've never played it in spring or summer. It's a fall sort of game.

Are there other games you associate with seasons? Games that just play better amid the blooms of spring, or the dead, icy heart of winter? What does a summer roleplaying game look like?

    \n I had a similar thread about this time last year, which I'm incorporating by reference.

    Specifically, I think of Polaris as being a winter game (duh), and I think I'll stand by In a Wicked Age as being a summer game.

    Oddly, I think I connect this with the movie release schedule. Summer games are full of life and energy and action. Winter games are games of tragedy and cold. That's different than a fall game, which is about decay or the coming dark. Ya know?

    Seth Ben-Ezra

    \n This has actually become more pronounced as I've gotten older.

    For me, Fall/Winter = Call of Cthulhu. I can run other stuff during these seasons, but I have a hard time running CoC in Spring/Summer. This past summer, I ran The Realm of Shadows for CoC and it felt a little off the whole time. Finally it dawned on me that the seasonal feeling just wasn't right. It just feels wrong to spend the day soaking up rays at the pool with the family and then come home for an evening of Lovecraftian horror. I'm actually lamenting that I'm not running a CoC game right now because my neighborhood is an autumnal wonderland.

    Also, for the last five years or so I've gotten the RC D&D itch every spring like clockwork.
