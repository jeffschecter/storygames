\n Hey folks,

I'm filing this one under Stuff to Watch, since I'm asking for references to other media? It seemed as good a fit as any.

Anyway, I feel like many of my story game-focused podcasts have dried up in the past while. And the fewer new ideas I have banging around in my head, the worse my games get, and the fewer new games are on my radar.

So, what is everyone listening to? I'm especially interested in podcasts which are not actual play; I like the style, but the time investment is prohibitive for me.

    \nThe Gauntlet is a good podcast covering a range of topics related to contemporary gaming, as is Misdirected Mark.

    On this side of the pond, The Good Friends of Jackson Elias deals with all things Cthulhu & horror related, including reviews of classic H.P. Lovecraft works, whilst What Would the Smart Party Do? regularly takes a broad look at issues in gaming, from running games at conventions to asking what a character sheet is for.

    \n Thanks for the recommendations, folks! I'll dig into some of these, and maybe report back.

    And I agree about Friends at the Table being great. But thirty-seven episodes into a campaign looks like weeks and weeks of content for me, and my work doesn't really allow me to listen while I work.

    I've seen some shows do APs of shorter games
    Jankcast has been doing some APs of two person games, like S/Lay w/Me. And InDices (which I'm only really aware of because the person is local to me, but seems pretty alright) have been doing APs of shorter games too, like Penny for my Thoughts.
