    \n George is right, there are a couple of different ways to do this.

    1) Forwarding, where anybody who tries to go to "someplace.com" (your domain) gets directed (by folks hosting your domain) to "someplace-else.com" (your webspace).

    2) Domain mapping, where you point the name servers of your domain to your webspace, meaning that, for all intents and purposes "someplace.com" and "someplace-else.com" are the same place. Even if your domain folks are being buttheads, they should be legally required to let you do this, since otherwise you can't really be said to own the domain at all.

    So try for the latter, I think. I use Dreamhost too, if you're looking for new webspace with decent customer service. Probably paying too much for it, but it's easier than moving elsewhere with all the uncertainties that would mean. I pay $80 a year, I think.
