    \n I actually wasn't thinking of assholes, just of players with conflicting visions. Like, if I come up with a really cool idea, and think I can use it, but then I try, and someone else isn't cool with it, and then we just kind of socially hash out which of us gets our way, then... well, I dunno, it seems unreliable. It'd be better for me to know, as the cool idea is forming in my mind, what I do and don't have the ability to narrate in, or at least how that'll be determined (and thus my odds).

    Perhaps good examples of edge cases in the "continuity" and "act as a team" text would help.

    Or perhaps you could let people stake out inviolate areas, like, "Here's the list of 5 things you can't make my Champion do."

    Or maybe spend tokens for vetoes, so you have a limited number, and will only use them when it counts.

    Or maybe I'm taking this in an unnecessarily adversarial direction, and what you need is really just a system for building off each other's narrations, so no one's contributing something "out of the blue". Maybe end each turn with a leading question that the next player must answer or follow up on?

    Just some ideas. It depends on what sort of social dynamic you're envisioning.
