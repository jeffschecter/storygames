\n You may recognize the name Joe Williams from Ron's critique of Legendary Lives here -- or from games such as Lost Souls or Khaotic (each of which served as a story-based evolution of the basic system first presented in Legendary Lives, and each of which was a significant departure from other RPGs on the market in terms of premise).

Joe has recently released both Legendary Lives (2e) and Lost Souls for free, as well as his latest endeavor Crimes People Play. This latest game is a deliberate effort to take the existing game system from past Marquee Press publications and bend it toward more story-driven play. As the title suggests, this is a mystery roleplaying game (which in and of itself is a subset of RPGs that I'm happy to see get more/better coverage).

I just downloaded a copy myself, though as a fan of the magic system and semi-diceless approach to NPCs taken by earlier Marquee Press games, I fear that I may be a bit biased (that Joe says he nipped the equipment rules in part from Simple 20 does nothing to help this). That said, I do think that we have a good candidates for the Free Game category of the Indie RPG Awards in 2008. You should check it out
