\n I was reading RPG.net a few ages back and an off hand "sell" by Mobunited people for Knights of the Hidden Sun, they suggested that a player character could be a starship--not just some transhuman brain thing but a living humanoid technological starship. On further examination that isn't the set up for their game, it is just pretty much ad-copy. While the final game may be awesome when it comes out but it doesn't do that concept.

Yet it makes me want to do it. Hyper advanced humans. No longer bothering with transhumanism in the primitive sense of Transhuman Space, or Eclipse Phase. Instead they are still living organic beings. Remade with technology so they are starships, living human vehicle who soar in space, can battle, or become advisor to new worlds, new civilizations. Essentially Sci-Fi Lensman/Green Lanterns but not pulp or superheroes. Instead ultratech humans. So far beyond the need for artificial and primitive means to travel they delight in exploration, joining together in microfleets to explore the universe, meet other creatures in the wide wide universe.

I just don't know where to begin other than in my own head. Green Lanterns actually make a good solid start--a micro peice of night magical technology that lets them do what their will demands? Good reference. Mechanically speaking though, what kind of game could be built around a premise of humans who can wave their hand and smash continents. (Following the rule that any sufficiently effective space travel device is a really good weapon as well.)

Any suggestions or references for this concept fiction wise? Any ideas on what a system for it might focus on? Social interaction between man and opposing ideologies from humans or aliens of near the same power? Dangerous aliens centuries more primitive but still powerful enough to be a threat with their minds inside starships? Thwarting suns going nova or worlds falling to cataclysm and ruin?

    \n Oooh! I love this idea!

    For inspiration, look at Farscape. The technology is different than you're thinking (the main ship in the series is Moya, a biomechanoid living ship who has to partner with a symbiotic sentient pilot) but it could give you some massive inspiration and ideas about how living ships could function in the game. Moya has lots of interesting traits: emotions like love, jealousy, and anger. The ship desires companionship in the form of a crew. She is sentient but cannot communicate on so simple a level as most bipedal lifeforms. She can hyper-jump, and has no weapons, because she belongs to a peaceful species. Pain is a real biological problem and she can give birth. Maybe in your fiction, a ship like Moya is actually an evolved species of human that just wants to live out in space peacefully exploring the wonders of the universe.
