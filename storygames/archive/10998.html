\n I've recently been introduced to cribbage, and I enjoy it quite a bit. The vestigial chariot race aspect of the game intrigues me. The 45-60 min. play time is something I'm looking for in my role-playing these days, and the different phases (the play, the show, the crib, even the shuffle and restart) offer structured play, which I enjoy in games.

How would you make cribbage into a role-playing game?

    \n I don't know much about cribbage, but your mention of "the vestigial chariot race aspect of the game" made me think of a Ben-Hur kind of game.

    Maybe take a cue from a big sports match today, and have some kind of "pre-game" show establishing the rivalries of the two charioteers? Then, the game plays out their climactic race.

    For campaign play, you could use teams--like the Blues and Greens in Byzantium. If you play after Justinian, teams could have political and even theological matters on the line.
