I'm starting this thread in an attempt to bring this other thread to an end, as the title of that one is confusing people. I no longer need a name for my game - it's "Errantry" - and the description "courtly romance" was leading people to expect a game about romance at court, for some reason.

For the sake of clarity: Errantry is a game of quests. Basically knightly quests, though you can be a warrior-maiden, troubadour, enchanter/enchantress, brave damsel etc. as well as a knight.

The system is based on Clinton Nixon's Solar System (as in The Shadow of Yesterday). I've "embraced and extended" it, adding on some bits, attempting to remove the role of GM/Storyguide entirely through a cunning character generation system (not yet on the wiki as I write), and putting in a couple of extras in the form of "quest tokens" and "obligations" (the latter being kind of like temporary keys).

I've also renamed the pools. Vigor becomes Valour, Instinct becomes Courtesie (and covers slightly different ground), and at the moment Reason is replaced by Piety, covering very different ground.

Isbo has suggested that "Piety" may not be quite the right term for what I mean. I'm intending "Understanding of how the universe works, like how to talk to animals, perform magic, find medicinal herbs, or anything else relating to knowledge and understanding of the natural (and supernatural), rather than social, world". But I can't use that as a name for a pool, so I'm soliciting suggestions. One idea so far: Scientia.

I'm also looking for a better name for "quest tokens". These are like "plot points", things that will assist you in fulfilling your quest (information, artefacts, helpers or whatever). I'm looking for a good medieval-sounding name, either Middle English, Norman French or Latin.

So, to summarize a long initial post, I'm looking for:

* A better name for the pool which deals with understanding of the natural and supernatural world.
* A better name for things/people that will help you in your quest.

Thanks for all the help so far, by the way.

  • MikeRM
    August 2006
    Yes, the primary characters are the knights/maidens/troubadours/enchanters who are questing. The structure is episodic, and in each episode you encounter someone or something, generated in a skewed-random way with a bit of intelligence built in, which I hope will work as well in practice as in theory. It uses the drawing of three playing cards as a random seed to generate large quantities of information.

    The character sheet for the "encounter character" (what would normally be called an NPC) is the same as for a player character, but obviously players fill theirs out by deciding what they would enjoy playing, whereas encounter characters are driven by the system.

    The encounter characters are played by the players (hence they are not, technically, "NPCs"), but on a temporary basis as required, whereas the primary characters are played in every scene. Which sounds weird until you reflect that this is what a GM does, traditionally, only now everyone's doing it.
