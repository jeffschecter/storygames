    All three of those have good "Con reps." I personally have only played PtA at a Con, and it was awesome! I think the key to that particular game is solid show creation, and starting off by having a discussion of what the players don't want to see in the game before you go into what you do want to see is very helpful. Also, being willing to go through 2 or 3 show pitches and then choosing the one of those that clicks the best. It may take a couple more minutes, but it'll make for a better game! Finally, once everyone has their scene presence settled, feel free to pick an episode to play that has multiple people at high screen presence, even if it's midseason.

    I hope some of that helps.

    Dogs is good. Be prepared to go through character generation at high speed: when I played, the GM, Steve (who posts here as GBSteve), went through it very quickly. He got us to choose names; he gave us the "background" options and let us choose one; and got us to assign traits fairly quickly. I think he used the "I'm a good shot" thing to prompt us occasionally. The whole character generation, including the initiations, was done in an hour, and it didn't seem rushed.

    I've also played MLwM, again with Steve. He had useful trick of skipping the communal Master generation. He played it with a sort of generic parts-of-body-collecting Master.

    In fact, now I think about it, you should probably be talking to Steve and not me.

  • Eric Provost

    I've run a bit of DitV at conventions recently. Two sessions each at Origins and GenCon. It's been good, but totally exhausting. Each time I've had four or five players who were eager to learn the game, but had little or no experience with it. I had some good prep ready, including a couple towns that were really convention friendly and a cut & paste handout all about character generation. We always went through full character creation, preludes, and right on into the town, all in four hours. The sheer amount of information that has to be relayed to the players, the minimum things that they really needed to know about the system to make interesting and informed decisions, meant that I spent lots of time explaining the ebb and flow of the dice.

    It's not on your list, but I've had my most reliable success and highest level of personal satisfaction in running The Mountain Witch. Granted, that may be because I've been regularly schedueling 6 hour blocks for that game.

    And, just for a little shameless self-pimping, I've designed The Infected with convention gaming foremost in my mind. I'm still working out some kinks, but I think it's going to be a super-hot game for playing with people you've never met before over a 4 hour block.

    -Eric
