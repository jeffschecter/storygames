\n So we've sponsored Dragonmeet London\u2019s foremost single day roleplaying, board game and collectable card gaming convention to do a one hour seminar on the state of story gaming ... or something like that. We can work on the title, but what would you - or what have you - include in a seminar to a crowd which will likely be a mix of indie-curious tradders, veteran story gamers, adventurous board-gamers and the complete newcomer?

    \n We've been doing panels like this for the last couple of years, when we go to big cons. It's worked great just to have 2-6 well-informed enthusiasts talk about whatever current thing excites them. Bring copies of your favorite new games along to hold up and pass around. We don't try to give a comprehensive view - the indie rpg world has gotten too big for that anyway - but just to be thoughtful and enthusiastic about what we like.

    \n You can focus a panel like this, too, by asking the panelists to come prepared to talk about their favorite game that does X. I was on a panel called "my best failure" which was really, really fun, for example. Any topic of interest to your audience works - your favorite game that addresses gender and sexuality, for example.
