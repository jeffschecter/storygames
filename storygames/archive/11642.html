    Chronosbane Kracken Lord Huge Solo Controller

    Found in the Astral Seas between planes by unfortunate adventurers. If they come from a work with clockwork. They will first notice the effect of Timeslip Aura before the Kracken reveals itself. They are intelligent, but cannot be reasoned with; they have an innate hatred for all things occupying a one-directional position in the timeline, which would be all creatures.

    Timeslip Aura This effect reaches out to an entire encounter space. If anyone has a timepiece or other means of determining the exact time, they will see time slipping backwards and forth by a few seconds. Warforged and other constructs will feel a strange confusion, and will take -10 to initiative checks.

    Paradox Aura At the beginning of a creature's turn, they take 2d10 damage for every Paradox Mark they have. Kracken-Lord gets a +2 to attacks for every Paradox Mark on a creature. Paradox Marks disappear when out of range from a Paradox Aura.

    Relativistic Poison
    Target takes 2d10 psychic damage and -1 to initiative. If their initiative is negative after this attack, they take a Paradox Mark.

    Slip Forward
    Roll d20; this is the Kracken-Lord's new initiative for the next round. The Kracken-Lord disappears from the game board until the beginning of its turn on the next round, where it may appear anywhere in the encounter. (The Aura remains while the Kracken-Lord has disappeared.)

    Interweaving Frames
    Trigger: Kracken-Lord is targetted or affected by another creature's action.
    Effect: Target one other creature who has not yet moved this round. That creature must either take their action now, or must give up their turn. However, if their action affects the Kracken-Lord, the creature who triggered the action will take 1 Paradox Mark. Resolve the original action after the interruption turn is complete.

    \nChronosbane Kracken High Lord Time-Eater Huge Solo Controller

    Chronosbane Krakens are occasionally encountered in groups. Each group is invariably ruled by a High Lord Time-Eater, quite possibly the most feared monster of the Astral Plane, if not the whole multiverse.

    The High Lord Time-Eater has all the powers of the Chronosbane Kraken Time-Lord, plus:

    Eat Time
    Roll d20; this initiative rating is erased for the rest of the encounter. Anyone starting their turn on this initiative loses his or her turn, but may re-roll their initiative before the start of their next turn. If the High Lord Time-Eater manages to erase all 20 initiative ratings, all characters who are not Chronosphere Krakens are no longer able to exist in time.

    Command Chronosbane Kraken
    The High Lord Time-Eater may command another Chronosbane Kraken to take its turn on a different initiative rating. This power is usually used when Chronosphere Krakens have an erased initiative rating.

    Chronosbane Kraken Probabilistic Tentacle (minion)

    Whenever a Chaosbane Kraken is encountered, designate a number of spaces (relative to the potency of the given kraken) to be the probabilistic locations of its tentacles. Remember that standard Chaosbane Kraken have 10 tentacles but those of the imperial ruling house can have 12 to 16. Older or warrior kraken may have had one or more tentacles severed in time combat.

    Each probabilistic tentacle shares its hit points with the Chaosbane Kraken it which it belongs and does not have seperate hit points like a standard minion. On the turn of the Probabilistic Tentacles, the GM is allowed to move 1d8 of them. The others do not get to move.

    Probabilistic Tentacles do not attack as normal. Instead, during a PC's turn, they must make a Save vs. Probability for every Probabilitic Tentacle space they are adjacent to or move through during their turn. On a failure, that space contains a tentacle, which Grabs them. On the next turn, the player first makes a Save vs. Probability for any Grabbing tentacles before trying to escape, as the tentacle may no longer even be there, rendering escape unnecessary.

      \n Okay, I think I'm happy with these:

      Chronosbane Kracken Lord
      Chronosbane Kracken High Lord Time Eater
      Chronosbane Kraken Probabilistic Tentacle

      It was interesting trying out the monster creation rules. The Time Eater's power names may have gotten a little bit powermetal, but that's a risk. More/better text for fluff and tactics is possible, and I'd take suggestions.

      The central mechanics of these creatures are rather preposterous, which I think is the point. I can see fighting it, and I can see easily getting timespammed out of existence by these powers. Aura of the Time Consumer + Shatter the Collective Clockworks can easily lead to players being stuck on erased initiative ratings (i.e. unable to act) for a while once most of the initiative ratings are truly gone. Perhaps a player on an erased initiative is dazed rather than completely missing a turn; that's still pretty brutal.
