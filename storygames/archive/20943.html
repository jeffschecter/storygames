\n I'm one of the organizers of the New York Story Games meetup, which has mostly been a fairly tight-knit group of gamers (we migrated to that meetup from the original Sci Fi meetup.) I've been meaning to get around to finding a good policy for us to adopt for the meetup but the insular nature of the group has let me slack off on doing so.

Tonight, though, one of the GMs reported an incident that makes it clear to me that we need to have one so that we can continue to keep up our high standards of open and inclusive play. Does anyone have a decent source of examples of this sort of thing, so I can bring it to the other organizers?

Thanks in advance!

    \n I rather enjoy this one from New York Comic Con. It's clear, it's serious. It's reasonable to expect of adults and makes everyone feel safe. http://www.newyorkcomiccon.com/About/Harassment-Policy/

    D&D's Adventurer League has a basic conduct policy in their PDF that's a lot softer while still addressing expectations of behavior. But in my experience it lacks the bite of enforcement needed to make it stick.
