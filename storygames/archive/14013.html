When the wars ended, it was because there weren't any grown men or women left to carry on the fight. The war-making machine had ground to a halt as the infrastructure of the country fell to ruin. But nations are built first on a foundation of ideals, which never die easily. And no one is more idealistic than a child.

    DON'T FUCK WITH JOHNNY ECK
    In a world of broken men, the one most broken stands the tallest - the Human Torso, Johnny Eck. Together with his grim gang of troubleshooting, ass-kicking roustabout clowns (and Big Chief the Death Row Elephant), Eck rights the wrongest wrongs you have never heard of - on and off the midway. Mafia chimps? Murderous mermaids? Draculas that need a punch in the face? Hollywood moguls in bear-skin coats? Ghosts hiding money? Johnny Eck will be burning rubber in his torso-sized hot rod until he's on the scene and ready to read evil the riot act.

    (Based on the true life of Johnny Eck. That car, by the way, was registered in the state of Maryland and street legal.)

    GLORY HOLE
    Before there was Jackass. There was Glory Hole. Say what you will of this depraved and ancient art, there's no denying the brave men who risks themselves for the art of entertainment. Glory Hole details the lives of freaks, geeks, and all-around nutters who brave the impossible for the chance to seen as the glory hounds that they truly are. Nothing arouses the audience quite like a man who puts his head into the mouth of yeti, or his hand into the burning hot coals to retrieve wrought iron. Glory Hole is not for the squeamish. It is for those men and women, jaded by the bearded lady. Unphased by the tattooed man. And who smirk at the daredevil driving nails into his tongue. "Child's play," you scoff? Then my friend, this one is for you.

    Stop me if you've heard this one: "It used to be better, of course it did. In the golden age of legend, when there was enough to eat and enough hope, when there was one nation under god and people could lift their eyes and see beyond the horizon, beyond the day. Children were born happy and grew up rich. Now that\u2019s not what we\u2019ve got. Now we\u2019ve got this. Hardholders stand against the screaming elements and all comers, keeping safe as many as they can..."

    I'll stop.

    SNUFFED OUT. Seriously, go beyond the facile (but hilarious!) parody of "Drowning and Falling" to a more seriously-toned game about world hazards. Suffocation is a favorite of mine because we relate to it viscerally; it's something you have a chance to die from every minute of your life. I want to make a game where the world hazard rules really hammer home the themes of the game: tension, vulnerability, the ubiquity of fatal danger. So: years later, the gas still lingers. People try to live their lives as best they can, but everything has to go just right, every day, or you're snuffed out. Even a child can accidentally dislodge your mask. Intimacy and belligerence alike carry a constant risk of sudden death. In this world, stories are necessarily short, conflicts almost always climactic, safety never at hand.

    Used Gods

    Cernunnos, the Horned God, the Lord of the Wild Hunt, and Morrigan, the Crow, the Three-In-One, have seen better days. Starved for believers, Cernunnos can only maintain the aspect of a young boy, albeit with antlers; and Morrigan sometimes forgets that she/they was/were more than just a bird. A four player GM-less game: one player plays Cernunnos, and three players play the Morrigan's triple aspect. Can you find believers and restore your powers in this new world, or will you be exploited by the new lords of the twentieth century?

    The Gentlemen of Taste

    Mr. Scale, Mr. Tremont, and Mr. Rapkin are the Gentlemen of Taste. They wear only the finest clothes. They haunt only the finest establishments, where they sip from only the finest brandies. Their canes are made from only the finest of woods, sculpted by only the finest of craftsmen. They kill only the finest of targets. They are the Gentlmene of Taste, after all.

    Assassins, serial killers, cultists making sacrifices to dark beings. All of these words might apply to the Gentlemen of Taste, if they were not terms so crass as to sully the good names of Scale, Tremont, and Rapkin. No, the Gentlemen of Taste will never accept such debasements; they have more class than that.


    The Gentlemen of Taste is a game for 4 players. Three play the Gentlemen of Taste, Scale, Tremont, and Rapkin, the strange and horrific murderers, each with his own unique talent, an eerie ability which extends beyond the realm of normality. The fourth plays the Victim, their target, in addition to filling in any other necessary roles. But this is the one time when the Gentlemen of Taste encounter difficulties they did not anticipate. And one of the Gentlemen of Taste will not be making it out of their endeavor fully intact.

    Which one of the Gentlemen will fall? Why are the Gentlemen of Taste pursuing the Victim in the first place? Who are the Gentlemen of Taste? Can they be stopped? Know they any mercy?

    These questions and more will all be answered in The Gentlemen of Taste.

    \nI love the Dead



    This is a one-on-one game for creeps, couples into weird lifestyles, and teenagers who don't appropriately know how to interact with the opposite sex.

    So yeah, the dead rose from the grave, as zombies and skeletons and ghosts and all sorts of other scary night time things. But they weren't out for blood and they weren't out for brains, they just wanted to get back to living their lives, as hard as that would be. The Player takes on the roll of a badger (a prostitute/con-artist) who is willing to do the dirty work of companioning with the newly risen, in the hopes of getting something for their trouble (secrets about where the money was hidden, blackmail info, etc). You must seduce and romance your Johnny-come-to-lately to exact a reward from them. The quality of your "work" gets you more rewards, and that leads to a better lifestyle, which leads you to a better class of corpse, leading to a better reward, and so on and so forth.


    You play with someone of the opposite gender and/or sexual interests. The game is specifically designed to be a flirting tool for spooky people. Like if Saywer from "Lost" was in "Necromantic."

    Barrels of Pain

    As a child I had laughed at the fools who rolled their lunatic slogans out onto the endless trail of dust. Life had been comfortable then, I had biscuits and pillows, windows to gaze from. I could not have foreseen my grim fate. Like many other damaged souls before me, one day I would daub a bitter slogan onto a barrel of my own, and roll it off into the generic wilderness to spread righteousness and hurt bad people... then put bits of them into the barrel. Today is that day, and it is not going to be a barrel of fun.
