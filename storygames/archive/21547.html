    \n The reference point for a lot of the culture and tech in Blades in the Dark is about 1870, I think. I really like the idea of making it dieselpunk, though -- it would be a very cool reskin of the setting. Somebody should totally do this as a Forged in the Dark game!

    Some of the elements that would contribute to a dieselpunk, interwar feeling:
    -Better guns, including machine guns. Even old-fashioned machine guns like the mitrailleuse and Maxim gun would make a difference.
    -Air travel for military and civilian use connecting the civilized islands.
    -Expanded industry on the edges of town or even reaching out into the deadlands.
    -A great war in the recent past of the setting which killed a lot of people, flooded the area with fresh ghosts, and introduced new weapons of war like the the machine guns mentioned above.
    --Instead of tanks, how about heavy-duty armored Hulls driven by devoted ghosts?
    --A new dispensation for the dead, with limited acceptance and licensing of selected ghosts and vampires as long as they are supportive of the Imperial government.
    --A more professional police force instead of the Victorian-style Bluecoats, and with it, a beefed-up bureaucracy. Everyone must have identification papers. If you kill a guy and throw him into the canal, he'll be fished out and identified by nightfall.
    --Increased colonial presence and tensions between the main island and its possessions. Lots of soldiers from every part of the Empire fought in the great war and returned home to expect greater respect and self-determination than they're getting.

    tl;dr Blades would make a great dieselpunk game but it would need some changes to setting and mechanics.
