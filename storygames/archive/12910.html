\n I will tell you mine in hopes you will tell me yours.
\n
\nSo one of my hang ups when I make a game is word length. I almost always try to find four letter easy to speak words for my game terminology and effect text. This is a side effect from many years of making card and board game prototypes physical space becomes really important then you have maybe two square inches to write a rule. I also always try to have unique first letters for each term in a category so for something like character ability scores i would have something like this.
\n
\nAble
\nMass
\nSavvy
\nWill
\nHave
\n
\nit tends to lead to awkward word choice
\n
\nso now i just use symbols.
\n
\nso what are you ocd about?

    \n Alphabetizing. Any information that can conceivable show up in list form, I'll rigidly put in proper A-Z order, to the point of making term choices based upon letter variety (so that I won't have too many things on a list starting with "S" or whatever).

    Layout. Header, sub-header, bulleted-list, everything must conform to a given scheme. This is a problem because I'll spend hours fiddling over layout rather than generating actual content. I must have a dozen unfinished homebrews that are really just empty layout schematics

    \n One thing that really gets on my nerves, and that I know a lot of people here may not agree with, is detailed mechanics for the acquisition of "Stuff".

    Almost every game out their has at least some concept of "Stuff" in a game.

    Take a look at FATE for example, DFRPG being my most recent irritation. How do I build a car, or a item that has an Aspect on it? How much more should a sword that does Damage 4 cost, as opposed to Damage 3?
    This is picky, and may not be the point of the game, if that is the case though, do not ever let the characters have equipment.
