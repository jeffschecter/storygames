    Recommend the Wrong System is a fun read. Buried in the thread are a few kernels for one-shots. The idea is to posit a game, post the totally wrong game system to play it in, and then put up some sort of justification as to why it's not really the wrong system. During the first five or six pages, the respondants forget about that last bit, so it starts off with game premise followed by random RPG (which is a bit funny). After that, most of the people start providing justifications and you end up with some funny stuff.

      Here's an interesting article from the New Yorker on the Nintendo Wii, and why it's a good business move even if it never becomes the #1 console. I thought it might have some relevance to the indie game movement.
      The point is that business is not a sporting event. Victory for one company doesnt mean defeat for everyone else. Markets today are so bigthe global video-game market is now close to thirty billion dollarsthat companies can profit even when theyre not on top, as long as they arent desperately trying to get there. The key is to play to your strengths while recognizing your limitations. Nintendo knew that it could not compete with Microsoft and Sony in the quest to build the ultimate home-entertainment device. So it decided, with the Wii, to play a different game entirely.
