\n So I've got this idea for Nerdly for a hack of Wicked I'd like to try. It's an experiment based on IIEE principles that were developed by Graham over here, and is also in direct response to some games I played at Nerdly last year.

So the basic new rule is this.. no wait, first I need to talk about the theory. I can't just go an say the point right off, that'd be cheating.

So, we all know that in IIEE you have times where you say stuff and other times where you roll dice and other times where you don't. The problem with this is that people at the table might actually say stuff, and that stuff might be stupid. So in order to help mitigate that I'd like to add a layer to IAWA where you have to roll for player intent. I got this idea from Poisoned, but figured I'd take it a step farther.

So before the initial intent of the character there comes the intent of the player (thanks to Ryan for helping me to figure this out when we stayed up all night drinking in Vegas). That is the thing that needs to be controlled. Not the character's intent, the players. Because players are full of stupid intents.

My hack will help control that. In order to say something at the table, you have to pick up a d6 and roll it. If you roll a 6 you can say what you wanted to say. If you roll a 1 someone gets to punch you in the mouth. If you roll anything else you neither speak nor get punched.

But! And here is the brilliant part! Other people at the table get input into this. Whenever you roll a d6 to be able to say something, anyone who has spoken with you at length outside of the game, or has a strong opinion about your moral character as a human being, can roll a d4. If many people do this, then the person who rolls highest wins. In the unlikely even that there is a tie on a d4 roll off then all tied parties are removed, and the next lowest wins.

Whoever wins the d4 rolloff can then add or subtract their d4 from the initial d6 result. Note that this can result in either the person being able to speak (if the result comes to 6 or more) or the person being punched in the mouth (if the result is 2 to 5 after modification) or to nothing at fucking all (if, say, they roll a 3 and you get a 2 and still only have a 5, so now you've rolled all those dice and still can't do shit with them).

I hope that this system will help us figure out who can speak and who can't at the table, and when. The punching in the mouth should really help with figuring out who is provoked too!
