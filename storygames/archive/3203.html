    Climbing Wudang Mountain
    \na new kung fu thingy

    \n

    This is a hack for Dogs. You get to do the rest of the legwork; here is the kung fu thing you bolt on.

    \n

    The escalation track for this game is: Talking indirectly, talking directly, physical, KUNG FU.

    \n

    Not everyone has KUNG FU! Martial arts arise from danger and passion. To gain access to KUNG FU, you must engage in conflict, and ALMOST DIE, and you get a Manual when there is a conflict about whether you live or die. Manuals are a resource that you have.

    \n

    To learn KUNG FU, you spend a Manual; it goes away. Then you name a relationship that your character has. This relationship becomes KUNG FU. You can use it a a method of supernatural combat. Give it a name. Maybe describe how it's special. From now on, you must escalate to KUNG FU to get access to this trait. But it's still a relationship, right? So whatever things that change it, they affect your relationship and also your martial prowess.

    I want to play, too!

    \n

    Tell Your Master I Kick His Ass, Too!

    \n

    an octaNe kung-fu mod

    \n

    You will need **octaNe* and **Blood & Steel to play this. Use the same four basic styles (Daring, Ingenuity, Craft, and Charm) as octaNe. Might and Magic are merged into Kung-Fu, but you don't begin with any points in Kung-Fu.

    \n

    Build your character the Blood & Steel way: describe an appropriate kung-fu character, beginning with an appropriate name, homeland, famous deeds, lineage, and most important relationship. Also pick a generic role (warrior, monk, commoner, noble.) Choose a historic philosophy or make one up, whichever suits the mood of your group, but sum up your philosophy in three short phrases.

    \n

    Now, start with 0 in each of the four main styles, and add points to each based on the following:

    \n
      \n
    • gender: males get +1 Daring or +1 Craft, females get +1 Ingenuity or +1 Charm (remember, styles don't limit what you can do, but what you earn rewards for.)
      • \n
    • role: add +1 to Daring for warriors, Ingenuity for monks, Craft for commoners, or Charm for nobles.
      • \n
    • philosophy: pick one or two styles that seem to sum up your philosophy and split +2 points between them.
      • \n
    \n

    Instead of rolling three dice for stunts, roll 0 to six dice:

    \n
      \n
    • if your action is related to your famous deeds, your relationship, your lineage, your role, or your philosophy, add +1 die for each.
      • \n
    • if your character is in your homeland, add +1 die.
      • \n
    \n

    If you have no dice to roll, you can either spend a plot point to get a die or take an automatic 1. If you get an automatic 1 in a physically dangerous situation, your character dies.

    \n

    To steal from Shreyas: you can only buy Kung-Fu after you have earned a plot point, then rolled a 1 or 2 in a physical conflict. Afterwards, you can spend a single plot point to increase your Kung-Fu. If you then earn another plot point and face another serious setback, you can spend another point. Kung-Fu reduces hazard ratings or acts as a hazard rating when attacked by other player characters.

    \n

    You can only do reasonably possible ("realistic") stunts if you do not have Kung-Fu. Once you get Kung-Fu, you can do over-the-top physical stunts, if you can overcome any hazard ratings involved, or you can spend a plot point to perform mystical or magical stunts.

    \n
