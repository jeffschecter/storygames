\n I used to be a big participant in the RPGA. This is D&D's Hobby/Club Marketing/Support Division. If you have played any organized D&D at a convention, then you probably know who they are. Anyhow, as a part of the RPGA's revamp some years ago, they put together several tests that you had pass in order to be a judge for them. They are open book and if you have the patience then they are really not that hard (just incredibly annoying.)

In a fit of nostalgia, I just re-took these tests on the RPGA website to see how much I could remember. You can see my results on the Herald Level and Master Level tests. Needless to say I did not pass. Anyhow, the point of all of this is that these tests have made me feel a WHOLE lot better about 4th edition. Here is the question from the Master Level test that drove this point home:\n
The sorcerer Hennet is casting a summon monster VIII spell defensively when he is struck by a secondary bolt from Ming's maximized chain lightning spell, and unfortunately fails his saving throw. Ming is a 17th-level wizard with a 20 Intelligence. What is the DC of the Concentration check that Hennet must make in order to continue casting his spell?
The answer is: 69

Value: Reason
10 Base for a Concentration Check
8 For the spell level of Summon Monster VIII
51 For the damage from the spell. Which is 17d6 maximized (102), halved for being in the secondary area of effect.
----
69

Now, if Hennet is able to make this Concentration Check, then for his trouble he can summon a Celestial Whale. Which I hope that he summons over the head of Ming and drops it on him from a height of about 50'. (All answers can be found here: www.d20srd.org)

When I look at all the depths of complexity that 3rd edition sank to, I think I have finally learned how to stop worrying and love the rules.
