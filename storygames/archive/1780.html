I don't think my group would like InSpectres itself, but I'm thinking of buying it for pointers on running player-driven investigative games, specifically street-level Unknown Armies. Will InSpectres help me in this way, or should I look elsewhere?
  • Adam Cerling
    November 2006
    I have played the game once, and it was a blast. But it seems very focused on the idea of investigators working for a franchise. You could easily play Inspectres within the Unknown Armies setting, I think -- but I don't think Inspectres would help much if you want to use the Unknown Armies rules with Inspectres techniques.

    Though you could probably implement the Confessional mechanic for some weirdness.

    Inspectres' thing is basically that the players are making up the mystery as they go along. The GM doesn't control its direction much at all. Is that what you want?

  • Jared A. Sorensen

    It's not a good sourcebook because it assumes that people know how to make shit up (which they do!). The rules don't tell you how to play games, just this specific game.

    People have used the InSpectres game as a basis for other kinds of games (Old West, pirates, Lovecraftian horror, emergency room melodrama...even My Little Pony, whatever that entails). But those people are still playing InSpectres, even if the group isn't creating/describing a ghost-hunting business and its exploits.
