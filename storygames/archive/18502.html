\n Although a super hero campain is still my big summer project , I still run some one-shots/ few sessions game in gaming clubs from time to time.
And I wonder if Hollowpoint could be my go-to system for those kind of low-prep games?
I just need a game where players can quickly create evocative characters. A game where you can play dense scenes with lot of action/descriptions and few rolls and few loss of time.
Hollowpoint seems fairly adaptable to any setting.
But, fellow veterans, what can('t) Hollowpoint do?
Obviously I could run some Shadowrun, Star Wars or Warhammer game with it. I even heard about a hollowpoint session based on Skyrim or the Avengers...

But which kind of scenes/mood can't be done with Hollowpoint?
Could it handle investigation/social scenes without a bloodbath?

    I'm also working out a hack that turns a lot of Hollowpoint's theme and mechanics on its head to make a high school comedy/drama/romance game.

    Investigation/social scenes could totally work. As Brad pointed out in one thread on the G+ community, the key to Hollowpoint is the effects. "What does it look like on the receiving end?" When those effects are bloody and violent, Hollowpoint is a bloodbath. When they're more things like "Found Out" or "Compromised", it's not.

    Like, imagine Hollowpoint focused around CON, DIG, COOL, and TAKE.

    \nWell...I think I could do it. There's a few nifty things I'm doing to extend the Hollowpoint framework, but I've essentially re-framed the conflict to use other methods, as well as the effects. And it's actually not all that sandboxy. The way I'm designing it, missions get replaced with Big Events--sorta the idea that something like prom or midterms or the homecoming game becomes an all-encompassing phenomenon that everyone's trying to get something out of.

    It's also a lot more heavily PvP in one sense. You're actually throwing in (on at least a meta level) to affect the relationships that other people have towards you, with the stress and other effects of upcoming Big Events limiting your capacity to resist change yourself.
