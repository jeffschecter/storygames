Posted By: AndyAgain, our "not official, but pretty close to it" rules summary for these threads:

-----
1. Feel free to respond here in-thread to another link posted.
a. If you're looking to start a conversation, perhaps take your comment and create a new thread with it in the Stuff to Watch category.
b. Otherwise, to prevent too many comments in the thread, number your response (first response: [1]; second response [2]; third response [3]). If you see that you're the second person to post, "Wow, that looks cool!" to a post here, then put a "[2]" at the top of your post. Three posts ([3]) is the limit: The fourth person to post should just take their comment and create a new thread, to carry the discussion over there further.
-----
Why?

Because these threads are great: We can link to little things being talked about on the web in general, on blogs, or other forums that might be noteworthy, without having to dedicate a new thread to each and every one (although that, too, is fine if you want to discuss the item further or "open up a discussion").
Responding to these posts with a comment or two is understandable (other times there might be no comments, just cause the link was self-explanatory: nothing to say). But we don't want to create a huge discussion in the middle of the thread: Some folks get a lot out of these threads, and don't want to look back to see the "10 latest posts" just to see that it's a heated discussion of something from a previous page. Cool? Cool.

    \n So, Mike Fiegel wrote this game called Vox.

    http://www.aeforge.com/vox/

    It's a psychological/surreal game (might even be considered "horror" depending on the bent) about people who start to get voices inside their head. And the voices are played by other players at the table. I'm about 60% done reading the book, and already planning out the first campaign I want to run, basically a surreal/dark game, like Memento meets Kult. This weekend I'll make a whole post about what I think is awesome about it.

    Anyway, it just came out last week at Origins. Book price is currently $35 (all copies sold out, he's reprinting), PDF is $15, but for the next few weeks, until the end of July, the author is offering a discount on the game so that you can get the PDF for $9.95 via the link here:

    http://www.rpgnow.com/index.php?discount=33163

    Just a shout-out:
    1) It's awesome in the way it twists up the surreal, and uses player choice and action to define character (in a little bit the same way as Cell Gamma from the No-Press Anthology).
    2) Character generation is built into the introductory scenario; you don't get stats until you start doing things. Your voice doesn't emerge or become defined until you start doing things.
    3) It has a really interested way of looking at tabletop chatter: Is out-of-character chat ("LOL, it would be hilarious if you just grabbed the girl scout's cookies and ran away!") actually another player talking out of character, or is it actually another player talking IN character but AS your Voice?
    4) Four default settings included, including turn-of-the-century Buffalo NY complete with Nikolai Tesla.
    5) Lot of stuff for the GM to do, so it's very GM-centric. Not so much as "The GM controls everything and says yes or no", but more of the "The GM has to bring his A-Game, in order to keep all the possibilities in her mind, so that they can be woven/re-incorporated back into the story like the conductor of a band".
    6) And yeah, the bit about where other players play the voices in your head. It's like the oWoD Wraith, except that the voices are not necessarily diametrically-opposed antagonists.

    Anyway, it's ten bucks - This Month Only - for some awesome surreal roleplaying.

    -Andy

      \n [Independent Insurgency] Episode 29: TTMN: Emily Care Boss on Pirate Jenny & RPGirl

      In this Talk To Me Now episode I talk to Emily Care Boss of Black & Green Games about two related topics: Pirate Jenny, the booth (number 2023) at Gen Con Indy 2009 featuring games designed by women; and RPG= Role Playing Girl (RPGirl), the zine about women in the gaming industry that she's worked on, which will be on sale at the booth. We touch briefly all of the new/interesting/focused-on games that you can find at Pirate Jenny, the purpose and function of the booth, and what you'll find inside RPGirl.

      WARNING: The Independent Insurgency is an "explicit" podcast.

      This episode is 25.2 MB big and 27:27 long.

      00:35: Defining Pirate Jenny
      02:01: Anna Kreider of Tasty Bacon Games has Thou Art But A Warrior, which is a Muslim knights setting supplement for Ben Lehman's Polaris, which I interviewed her about
      02:28: Danielle Lewon's Cream Alien Games has Kagematsu, a game about the seduction of a samurai by women who need his aid; Danielle is the wife of Paul Czege
      03:23: Kagematsu is based on a design by Scott R. Knipe; the game is partly an examination of Knipe's experience as a transgendered person
      04:52: Kat and Michael Miller's Incarnadine Press will have Serial Homicide Unit, a game where you play civilians at the mercy of a serial killer, and the cops who are out to catch him; I interviewed the Millers about this game
      07:14: Shreyas Sampat and Elizabeth Shoemaker's Two Scooters Press will have Homecoming, Elizabeth's game about the re-integration of returning war vets

      \n [Independent Insurgency] Episode 30: Emily Care Boss on Sign in Stranger

      In this design episode, I talk Emily Care Boss of Black and Green Games about Sign in Stranger, her game of alien exploration. Called "the Peace Corps in space" game, Sign in Stranger is about human beings who've elected to leave a quarantined Earth behind to try to build a life for themselves out in space, among aliens who at best regard them with curious pity for their benighted condition. I've played and enjoyed it, and since it's coming out at Gen Con this year, you can do the same.
