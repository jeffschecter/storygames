    \n Replace "Futures" with "Dark Secrets." They resolve in the same way. Protagonist determined in the same way. (It's now a game about growing up!)

    Instead of having general traits, you have one trait in each of the following categories:\n
    • Monster: list what kind of monster you are.
    • Look: describe your look (which will imply clique and social standing)
    • Sex: a short phrase describing your approach to sex
    \nExample Character:

    Candika
    Monster: Werewolf
    Look: Wiry athletic girl in a hoodie
    Sex: Predatory but romantic
    "I can't let anyone know that I love Darius."
    "My family are responsible for a series of brutal killings about town."

    When you introduce an Obstacle, it can either be an external situation (like in Ribbon Drive) or something dangerous and harmful that your character is doing to another.

    Instead of taking a Detour, you Suffer. When you suffer, there's a scene about your character's dark emotional torment. They might be bleeding out, or gazing into the abyss, or acting like their darkest self. The scene ends when you've figured out how to escape your suffering, which might require outside assistance or drastic action.

    How can that happen?
    \nIf I might provide my thoughts as to how I consider implementing the playlist as part of the game - though I am aware they are a touch crude.

    Firstly, I intend to have the music directly impact the fiction both in terms of inspiration for my description and background and with regards to drawing directly from the music in the moment of play.

    Next, I had in my mind, some custom moves born from some of the music in the playlist, for example: -

    When you tell "Billy" a Secret, roll+Dark and he'll tell you something in return.
    On a 7-9 pick one: -
    - The dead are not silent and Billy might speak your secret to others.
    - He tells you something that may not be true.
    On a miss, you are overheard.

    One of the songs is about how two people can only keep a secret if one of them is dead.

    Finally, people can gain in game benefits for quoting from the songs in play. An extra benefit if it's in context and in character.

    (Additionally, they can gain these rewards for quoting the media influences but that's tangential to the current discussion).

    OK, so it's not complicated by any means but I think it helps get the music involved as a player of the game.

    Of course, now I need players...

    \n Get out of my head, Joe. This is what I just wrote on Google+.\n
    When a Hot song is playing, claim +1 for Hot.
    When a Cold song is playing, claim +1 for Cold.
    When a Volatile song is playing, claim +1 for Volatile.
    When a Dark song is playing, claim +1 for Dark.

    And note that you claim it. So it works like this:

    You: "This is a pretty vicious song. I'm claiming +1 for Cold."
    Me: "OK".
    \nIt's intended to ensure that, during sexy songs, everyone shags. During dark songs, everyone stares into the abyss. And so on.
