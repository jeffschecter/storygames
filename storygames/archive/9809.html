    \n David Embury, the author of the definitive book on mixing drinks The Fine Art of Mixing Drinks, lists the six classic cocktails. The proportions given are his preferred ratio. You may wish to reduce the portion of the base liquors somewhat, but they should remain the most prominent of your ingredients.

    When he lists "American Whiskey" either Rye or Bourbon will work, depending on your personal taste. If you choose to use rye he insists on using an American rye, because Canadian whiskey is inferior to American, Scotch, and Irish whiskeys. The claim was based on Canada's laws concerning how whiskey is produced and labeled at the time. This may be dated information though.

    Martini

    * 7 parts English gin
    * 1 part French (dry) vermouth

    Stir with ice, strain into a cocktail glass, twist lemon peel over the top and serve garnished with an olive, preferably one stuffed with any kind of nut.

    Manhattan

    * 5 parts American whiskey
    * 1 part Italian (sweet) vermouth
    * dash of Angostura bitters to each drink

    Stir with ice, strain into a cocktail glass and serve garnished with a maraschino cherry.

    Old Fashioned

    * 12 parts American whiskey
    * 1 part simple syrup
    * 1-3 dashes Angostura bitters to each drink

    In an old-fashioned glass, add bitters to simple syrup and stir. Add about 1 ounce of whiskey and stir again. Add two cubes of cracked, but not crushed, ice and top off with the rest of the whiskey. Twist lemon peel over the top and serve garnished with the lemon peel and a maraschino cherry.

    Daiquiri

    * 8 parts white Cuban rum
    * 2 parts lime juice
    * 1 part simple syrup

    Shake with lots of finely crushed ice and strain well into a chilled cocktail glass.

    Sidecar

    * 8 parts Cognac or Armagnac
    * 2 parts lemon juice
    * 1 part Cointreau or triple sec

    Shake vigorously with ice and strain into a cocktail glass. Garnish with a twist of lemon if desired.

    Jack Rose

    * 8 parts Applejack
    * 2 parts lemon juice
    * 1 part Grenadine

    Shake vigorously with ice and strain into a cocktail glass. Garnish with a twist of lemon if desired.

    \nBooster Splashdown in the Seas of Venus

    This one's a lot of work, but worth the reward for the showyness of it.

    Fill a chilled pint glass (in this order) with:

    1oz Midori
    3oz Prosecco (Muscat and Gewurztraminer work equally well)
    10oz Ginger beer (this is slightly less than a can, if you dump a whole can in you won't have room for the rest of the drink)

    stir gently so as not to excite the carbonation in the ginger beer. Should end up a lovely light spring green color (like the 2nd planet herself).

    Next, fill a shot with Amaretto and top off with Bacardi 151. Light the surface of the shot on fire.

    Carefully (and quickly, that mother's on fire) holding the shot by it's sides, drop it into the pint glass with an auspicious splash. Be forewarned that the rim of the shot glass can be hot and should be treated as such.

    Enjoy.

      \nAtomic Professor

      2 parts Bailey's
      1 part Goldschl\xe4ger
      2 parts butterscotch Schnapps
      Splash of 151 proof rum
      Dash of cinnamon

      Shake first three ingredients with ice and strain into heavy cocktail glass. Float shallow layer of 151-proof rum on top. Light rum on fire and immediately sprinkle cinnamon on top (this will sparkle and flame). Extinguish before cream curdles. (This drink is sometimes called a Bailey's Comet.) (Also, you've heard of drinking responsibly? Goes triple for drinks on fire.)

      \nGhost Professor

      Should be done in an erlenmeyer flask, but martini glass will also work. Takes some prep, but the result is a color-changing marvel.

      7 parts vodka
      1 part dry vermouth
      Dry ice
      Backing soda
      Red Cabbage (no, I'm not kidding)

      1) Chop up a red cabbage leaf. Put in bowl with some water and microwave until boiling (or just steep in boiling water). The cabbage will release a purple pigment: water-soluble anthocyanin which acts as pH indicator (purple at neutral pH, and goes blue then green as the solution becomes alkaline).

      2) Add a teaspoon of the cabbage juice into the flask. Add just enough baking soda to get it to turn blue.

      3) Add the vodka and vermouth. Drink should be pale blue.

      4) Add dry ice to drink. This cools the drink and, more importantly, starts to neutralize the baking soda. So, as the drink bubbles, it changes color from blue to purple. Don't drink until the dry ice is gone.

      (You might try this with blueberries instead of cabbage, as they also contain anthocyanin.)

      \nPsychic Daredevil

      (Not from the '50's, but sort of feels like it.)

      1 part vodka
      3 parts Bawls energy drink
      Cotton candy (flavor to taste. Unflavored is usually better.)

      1) Gently shake vodka and Bawls with ice (Bawls is carbonated, so go easy).
      2) Add fist-side wad of cotton candy into martini glass. Do not compress.
      3) Dissolve the cotton candy with a slow pour of the shaker contents.
