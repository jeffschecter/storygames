\n A bunch of us got together to meet up and play some Indie RPGs. My weapon of choice was the growling chainsword that is 3:16.

I'll say this right off the bat, I love this game. It filled the Space-Marines-Exploding-Things-But-Also-Band-of-Brothers niche that I have always been seeking to fill. So, I rolled up a planet of terrible cultists under thrall of a space-egg full of evil monsters and set my Troopers against it.

Our party included Trooper Jordyn "Blenz" Chase (hot-headed maybe-lesbian), Trooper Jack "Blazer" Mahoney (showstopper patriotism poster-boy), Trooper ... umm, actually I can't remember his name because everyone kept calling him different things. Martinez, Santiago, Poncho, Valdez but mostly just Sanchez. He was a chain-smoking badass from Peru. So, peruvian. Not mexican. Apparently we're easily confused.

The group was led by the dumb-as-bricks Corporal Frank (who referred to himself as "Frank the Freedom Fighter") and their scheming Sergeant, Machus.

Quick story overview - they crash-landed on the planet and were swarmed, fought off some cultists and managed to survive without being too badly beaten. Following encounters were a solo between a sniper nest and Trooper Ramirez, a mortar attack on the Troopers' overnight hideout, a very short battle with the "blasphemy wagon" and a last-ditch battle with the hatched meteor-egg. It was chaos and bloodshed and awesome.

Outside the game, I think that it took a few mechanics attempts to get everything sorted out but there weren't any of those awkward stalls where people flip through the book. Everyone took to the Troopers-vs-but-on-a-team-with-Officers. A lot of this came from Machus' player being a sneaky fucker. Once people got the range concept in their head, we had a lot of fun with all the players trying to optimize their own kill and steal kills from each other.

Strengths and Weaknesses came into play pretty marvellously, too. Blazer single-handedly wiped out the only vehicle encounter with his "Perfect Spiral" Strength, hurling a grenade through the windshield. BAM. Their Sergeant bitched out at the start of a big scary encounter just because he "really just isn't here to die".

The players were all really happy and I got a ton of "let's play again!" before we moved on to other games. All in all, super fun. I felt a little odd about framing everything with these obvious "WE ARE PLAYING" board encounters vs. "WE ARE NOT REALLY PLAYING" sort of feeling in-between stuff, but I think that with a longer game, the players would get comfortable with their Troopers and their inter-relationships.

Gregor, thank you. I have already gotten my $20 of fun out of the game. Is there anything anyone wants to know about the game or the session? I'd be happy to answer questions!
