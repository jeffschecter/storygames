\n GNS is not a magic pill which will banish all your gaming woes, lead you from the path of badwrongfun and illusionism into the light of pure gaming goodness.

GNS was a process that some people underwent a couple of years ago. They thought through their gaming experiences and wrote down what they saw as supportive and unsupportive of the way they wanted to game.

This is very useful to anyone else who wants to think about the roots of their gaming, about how to have a better experience and to create and play games that are more suited to their way of playing. GNS (and other theory) looks at most of the aspects of gaming and has something to say about it.

This means that you can use GNS as a springboard for your thoughts. 'Oh yeah, I never really considered that the way we resolve things through social interaction is actually a system', or 'Pervy, abashed, illusionism, what the hell does all that mean?' or 'I always wondered why Bob never says anything during the games but is really enthusiastic about them afterwards'.

But you do need to have your own thoughts on the matter and if they don't agree with GNS, or the Big Model or whatever, don't worry about it. That's a matter of personal choice. GNS is not a gaming panacea. It's a description of a personal process that some people went through. Not you.

You may find yourself agreeing with everything that has ever been said in support of GNS (and other models). If you do, you are doing it wrong. Go back and thing about it for yourself for a bit. Don't just swallow the pill.

    Posted By: GB SteveIt's a description of a personal process that some people went through. Not you.
    \nUnsurprisingly, I have this tattooed on my ass.

    But yeah, I see this cycle a lot (in the interwebs like LJ, RPGNet, etc; not necessarily here):
    1) Someone posts thoughts on their gaming, perhaps a problem they were having. They use a word that has some attachment to the GNS spectrum.
    2) Someone else comes along and comments on their use of the word.
    3) GNS discussion ensues.
    4) Original poster vows to study GNS more so that they can competently talk about it to address the questions others (engaging with them only on the GNS items) are asking them.
    5) But the original issue doesn't get addressed/resolved. Or at least, it doesn't get addressed with basic analysis and straight talk that could fix the issue.
    6) (optional) GNS discussion continues anyway.

    I think that GNS is so big because no one has come up with a better/more comprehensive theory to displace it.
    But IMO, sometimes the more pragmatic approach isn't to replace the discussion of one theory with another, but rather to take a step back, use GNS as a tool where it fits, and totally discard it and use a straightforward approach when it does not.

    But yeah, I hear ya.

    Posted By: KobayashiThis should be posted as a sticky on every rpg forum. Honest.
    \nEh, more than that, it's a good mantra to say every once in a while.

    In the end, I firmly believe that fun play can reproduced systematically. In other words, it amounts to experience, with various tricks, tipcs edges and the like assisting. Play resulting in a fun session is not random, magical or simply the weavings of a good GM and nothing else. GNS is one of the tools that help some people put things in alignment to get at that reproducible fun.

    Personally, I think the tool is overused compared to simpler tools that work just as well. It's like a monkey wrench, but with a network packet analyzer and ethernet port attached to the side of the wrench: People seem to use it to tighten loose bolts and fix their issues, but then get caught up in trying to figure out how the packet analyzer component works, even long after they fix the issue.

    \n It does depend on which GNS you're using. Mileage will vary, there are about as many strains of GNS as there are HPV.

    One of the reasons GNS (in it's many forms) is so complex is that it's built to minimize the work needed to use it. A simple, but fairly complete theory of RPGs means you need to constantly work to remove your own assumptions and explore all the consequences of a given fact as you use the theory. Simple theories are much harder to use, even if they can be more expressive and accurate.

    - Mendel
