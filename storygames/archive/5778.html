If you could have one superpower...

\n
\n
\n
\n\nMcdaldno \n\n\n
\n
\n\nFebruary 2008 \n edited February 2008 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n This is a two part exercise:

1. Pick a superpower, describe how cool it is, what you would use it for, who you would affect with it, and whether you'd like wear tights and a mask when you used it and stuff.

2. Tell me what price you pay for the superpower. This could be a direct "when I use X, I suffer Y", or it could be something less direct and more a "character balance" thing.


Example:
1. My name is Jason Morningstar, and any time anyone mentions an American President that predates 1905, it triggers my psycho-psion neuro-attuners to psychically send me an email describing the conversation about the President in question, and also verifies the validity of those claims made. Whenever I check my inbox, I have hundreds of emails I've psychically sent myself about historical figures, and it rocks!
2. But the CIA knows about this power, and they know that I harbor some dark, deadly government secrets that predates most agents' birth. They are tracking me down, trying to remove me and my ultimate historical power!

Basically I'd be a genie who can grant his own wishes. Eat that "Invisibility."

    The first thing I do with this power is replace my own body with an ageless, indestructible duplicate, something that can survive a direct hit from a multiple megaton nuke. A better looking ageless, indestructible duplicate. With a snazzy costume. I'm thinking something in black and green, with an oriental flair or pulp era flair. Since supervillainy and overthrowing governments, crippling economies, and savaging people's silly little military toys appeals to me more than rescuing kittens from trees, I'm thinking I'd go with a Cobra Commanderesque hood and military uniform sort of deal, and a hood and robe combo for more relaxed, kickin' it in the lair moments. I'd personally lay a whupping on all the world's major militaries, knock over some fancy monuments, and set myself up as Emperor of the World. I'd whip myself up some minions, doing stuff like turning pebbles into ninjas and cars into giant foo dragons. I'd turn the President into a poodle and put a leash on him. Pretty much anyone who wanted to remain in power would have to kiss my feet and smile about it. The people not seeking out power, which is the vast majority of everyone, would suddenly find their governments and business leaders working to earn their respect and actually do their damn jobs.

    2. Ruling the world is lonely, boring work. Also, at some point, you stop wearing the mask, and the mask starts wearing you. Also, it seems almost inevitable that some young do-gooder will either find the source of my power and gain it for himself or take it away from me, and I'll come crashing up against my own hubris. But it'll all be worth, jut for the part where I get to engage with jet fighters in hand to hand combat while flying, and hurl tanks around. And turn world leaders into small yapping dogs. Glee!

    \n My name is Bret Gillan. I am completely invulnerable. I use this power to become a star of television and movies. One of my greatest stunts is being dropped into the Grand Canyon from a helicopter.

    I receive extreme pressure from the government to be involved in military training and deployment. I refuse, publically citing pacifism. Peace lovers and war objectors love me, hawks call me a coward and despise me for all the good I could but refuse to do for my country. Oddly enough, my power brings me fame and infamy not for my ability to live fearless of death, but for the controversy over what exactly I should be doing with it.

    Stressed out, I seek out more and more extreme tests of my power. I participate in dangerous sports, getting tackled by some of the heaviest linebackers in the NFL. I'm a guest star on nature shows where the most popular footage of my powers - wrestling with a grizzly bear and laughing while it mauled me - originated. But eventually, eventually, I become totally numb. An overload of adrenaline combined with a complete lack of fear make me emotionally deadened. Finally, in an act of ennui, I agree to be launched into the atmosphere in a space shuttle designed to break up when it reaches an extreme height. In an accident that many individuals accuse the government of doing on purpose, the shuttle breaks up over the Atlantic Ocean. I plummet flaming into the water and am never seen again. I'm presumed drowned and dead, or perhaps I sank to the bottom, entangled with a heavy metal piece of the spaceship and I'm pinned and trapped, going completely insane. Or maybe in the darkness and the cold, I just rest and listen to the sound of the water.

    \n 1. My name is John Harper, and I can create worlds. I write them down in notebooks, with little drawings, margin notes, descriptions of people and places, pasted-in swatches of fabric, reference photos, whatever. I put a notebook under my pillow, and then, when I sleep, I live in this other world. It seems completely real to me -- a perfect immersive reality. Time is flexible. A night spent in another world might seem like several weeks there. Eventually, I discover that I can take other people with me, if they're sleeping next to me when I cross over. I want to take my lover across with me, to show her my ultimate creation and explore it all with her, but by then I am too strange and disconnected to convince her. One day, I just don't wake up at all.

    \n My name is Jake Richmond, and I can stall time.Time is an abstract concept created by humans, and I can seriously do without it. I think a special thought, and time stands still for everyone but me, allowing me to move around the world in peace and work to my hearts content. I use my now unlimited amount of free time to draw comics, write games, read and play that giant stack of PS2 games that are sitting in the living room. I astound my employers by turning in assignments seconds after they've been assigned! I astound my readership by posting a dozen new pages of my webcomic every day! When I turn time back on, I make sure to spend my time with my friends and family, enjoying every moment as much as I can.

    I call myself "The Master of the 17 Indefensible Truths". I have a secret base called "the Superiority Complex". Why? Just because?

    The downside? No matter how much free time I have, I'll always find ways to waste it. Why get work done on my comic when I can spend countless hours playing Playstation and reading manga? Why work on a new illustration if I can spend a timeless afternoon carrying stacks of 20 dollar bills out of a bank vault? Why work on my new game if I can spend all day looking up the skirts of random inanimate women on the bus (Yes, I know that's awful. I'm sorry. I'm sooooo sorry). Why do anything if I can in fact do anything?

    Every time I think about this, I just feel bad about myself.Thanks, Joe!

    Jake
