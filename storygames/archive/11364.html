    \n It depends on whether it's funny. I'll read basically anything that's funny, and absolutely love anything that's both funny and insightful. Blogs, to me, are meant to be bite-sized and light-hearted...something that I can follow casually and get some entertainment value out of whenever I look in on them, no matter what my mood is.

    The more serious the tone, the less likely I am to read it in blog form: I'd just as soon wait until all of the thoughts are fully cooked and presented in a well-thought-out article or book. Serious, deep material is something I want to make room for. I want to set aside time for reading it, and I want that time to be well-spent. Blogs typically fall down on the first part (I am not accustomed to setting aside serious time for reading a blog) and frequently on the second part as well (since they are by nature less well-edited and coherent than an article or a chapter in a book).

    But I'm a link whore, so I'll basically look at anything anyone posts. If it is too earnest and unfunny to grab me in the first few posts, I'll drift away naturally with no hard feelings.
