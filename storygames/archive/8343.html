\n OK, quick question:

In IaWA, the Owe List is supposed to tell us which characters recur and which do not, right?

But, we also have the option to bring our characters in if an element suggests a character that could be them.

So, if I wanted to play my some character ALL THE TIME, I could bring them into each chapter, couldn't I? Like: "A grizzled warrior? Ok, that's my guy."

The only limitation seems that sometimes you'll have to accept fictional constraints about your character--say, if the grizzled warrior is an unwilling bride, then your character's about to get married!

Is this breaking the rules or just the spirit of the game? Will your friends boo you? I feel like I'm missing something.

    \n So in my group there's me and another guy whose characters rarely make the Owe List.

    Like four games ago, this other guy's character ended the game as a king. No mentions of him on the list, and so we figure he goes on to be a king even though he was an ass and didn't deserve it.

    Then in the next game we get the card with the tools of torture and ghosts that haunt them, and he calls up, "Yea, my guy is one of those ghosts. I guess the king thing didn't work."

    It was awesome.

    \n One more thing I don't see mentioned here - the character on the top of the We Owe list will occur with or without fitting them to an oracle element, other characters may occur either through scratching their names off the we owe list wherever they may be (with no fictional constraints necessary), or when an oracle provides an element and a player accept the fictional constraints for their character (in which case, unless the character's name was on top, you keep all instances of the character's name on the List).
