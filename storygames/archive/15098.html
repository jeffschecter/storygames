\n We're working on Playground Magazine #4 (yes, #3 is done - mailing it out this week, fingers crossed!) However, we'd like some more articles - and I thought I'd try this as an experiment:

Do you have something you want promoted? A game you're publishing, for instance?

Write us an article (or a proposal for an article) about something else - something you're not directly involved in. For instance...
- A review of a new, weird roleplaying game written by a friend or complete stranger
- A pictorial from an interesting roleplaying event (larp, festival, etc)
- An article about a new style, technique, idea that someone else came up with

If and when the article is published, you get a free ad in Playground Magazine.

The idea is that this would be a win-win-win: We get more articles about cool stuff; other people get word-of-mouth for cool projects; and you get an ad in the magazine.

If you're interested, let me know within one week from now.

    \n Marshall: Both directly and tangentially related are fine. One of our most directly RPG-related article so far is probably "Sex Moves of Apocalypse World"; one of the most tangential would be an article about scam-baiting. Performance art, interactive arts, pervasive games... all good.

    Rob: The theme for #4 is "Style". Our themes are very broad, and should serve more as an inspiration than as a constraint. "Style" could mean a specific style of play, for instance, or stylish gaming.

    In general, we want to show people what's new and different. We're not going to compete with traditional gaming magazines, mostly because there are thousands of people who could set up a stat block or design a fantasy scenario better than us. We're more about U. S. indie games, Nordic larp, jeepform etc.

    If you look at the samples over here: http://playgroundroleplayingmagazine.wordpress.com/issues/ - you'll probably get a feel for what we're doing. Generally, though, just pitch us an idea and we'll talk about it, that's the easiest.
