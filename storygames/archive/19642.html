    \n Tagline:
    "History shows us again and again how nature points out the folly of man."

    Pitch:
    Do you like Godzilla movies? Did you enjoy the most recent one? Did you appreciate the way none of the human characters really accomplished much as the monsters stomped around and wrecked things?
    This is a game about monsters stomping around and wrecking things.

    \n
    \n Yeah, I feel like these two pages need a lot more in the way of actual content. I loved Pacific Rim and the latest Godzilla movie, but there's no real context for these rules. Nothing about them makes me want to create a mecha or a kaiju.

    Also, there's not a lot here, and some of the rules don't make sense. For example, unless you start off fighting enemies that are of a much lower strength than you, you'll very rarely get to do anything. If both characters in a fight are strength 4, then you need to roll all successes to land a hit. Even adding one or two more dice to that pool, it's still really unlikely.

    I like the idea of dominance, as like a special attack meter you need to fill, but rolling dice based on the dice you already rolled seems clunky to me. Also, like I said, you'll very rarely actually connect a hit unless you're attacking really low level things to start, which is no fun. What's the point of being a mecha or a kaiju if you're taking out wimps?

    Also, I can't find anything in the rules about health or damage?
