Maltese Changeling
Tinat

Whom do I miss most from home?
I miss Nyesh and Lur, the Root Trolls who cared for me after I was abandoned in Banbur Forest by my human parents. The other Root Trolls weren't sure that they wanted the burden of a fragile human girl-child, but Nyesh and Lur took me in without any hesitation. They taught me to hunt and forage, to track and sneak.

Why did I join the temple?
Banbur Forest was under attack by the forces of Idgara, Graf of Gall. Because the Root Trolls knew nothing of writing, they had no way of contacting the temple--until I volunteered to be the message. It was hard to leave Nyesh and Lur, but I knew that the others weren't all that sad to see me go.

What test did I pass to enter the temple?
One of the monks asked me to shoot down a bird perched atop the temple gate. I asked him if it was for supper; he said it wasn't. So I refused to kill for his amusement. He wasn't angry, though: he smiled and welcomed me inside.

Jing Lao

Whom do I miss most from home?
I miss my sister, she serves as a nurse aboard a flying fortress. I don't know if I will see her again. My brother has already been killed by the Oceanians (or was it the Eurasians, I can't remember...). When I was younger, I testified against my parents, saying they were traitors to Brother Emperor, the ruler of Eastasia. I never saw them again after their trial. I can't even remember their face.

Why did I join the temple?
When I deserted the Youth Brigade I was chased by the Truth Police. I ran through the ruins of Middle City 1 and ended up in front of an old temple.

What test did I pass to enter the temple?
I had to tell the monks what crime my parents had commited. I said my parents were innocent and wept. One monk took me in his arms and led me into the temple...

Jonathan Walton
We are the Fourth and Fifth Brides of Dizang Bawang, the Infernal King; we were known in life as the Sacrificial Twins, Rasha and Lukka.

Who do I miss most from home?
We miss the feeling of grass between our toes, the taste of chocolate, the smell of rain. Mostly we miss our lord, Dizang Bawang, who was always kind to us.

Why I joined the temple?
When our lord, Dizang Bawang, came of age to fulfill his birthright and take up his position as the king of hell, he stepped into the fire that would render him into smoke and ash, transporting him to the realm of fire. We followed him, just a few steps behind his first three brides, but, instead of traveling with our lord to take up rulership over demons and ghosts, our smoke spirits intermingled and slowly drifted to the center of the sky. One of us cried tears of fire and admitted that she had put a letter in her pocket, asking the Temple to save us, for she did not wish to be a queen of the netherworld. The other cried as well, for now we were one, two smoke spirits locked together in a tearful embrace, trapped in the realm of earth and stone where we did not belong.

What test did I pass to enter the temple?
The prayer wheels that hang from the Temple's gates spin forever in the sky-wind, wishing blessings on the world but also providing a barrier to ward off evil spirits. At first we were afraid that they would tear us apart, forever separating us sisters -- who had never been apart for even one minute of our lives -- but they did not. Instead, we drifted over them into a courtyard where a monk was burning incense, drawing us into the sweet fumes. He had put out a plate of steamed rice on a low wooden table and we ate hungrily in gratitude before collapsing to the ground in sleep.

    \n After your private interview is completed, you're brought before Master Low Branch along with a few other would-be pilgrims. Here, you will be asked to declare your new pilgrim name. You will also discover how you are remembered by your fellow companions.

    First, each of you must declare your new pilgrim name, comprised of two words. The first is usually an action or description, the second usually a place or thing in or around the temple. Pilgrim names can be austere and regal, humorous and lively or plain old perplexing. Your name is unique to your personality and feelings towards your time in the temple.

    Examples: Eminent Spire, Celestial Chest, Whimsical Abbey, Lone Beam, Formal Organ, Intimate Bamboo, Forceful Abbey, Diligent Kata, Last Tower, Striking Chime, August Leaf, Deferential Plug, Sharp Courtyard, Elder Bucket.

    If you need some inspiration, feel free to play around with the pilgrim name generator.

    \nThe pilgrimage is dangerous, exciting and confusing. You'll need to trust your companions and they need to trust you.

    In actual play, it would be important to note the seating arrangement at this point. We'll be using the order of the list at the top of this thread instead.

    Each of you will remember how your companions showed wisdom, power and courage at some point in the past. Like the "test" question at the end of Step 1, this is an opportunity to reveal unique characteristics, but this time it's about the other characters.

    Previous playtests have shown that the pilgrim name provides a lot of inspiration for these little anecdotes. This version of character creation also provides you with a little bit more back story to reference as well.

    Here are some example memories from the playtest we ran at GenCon 2007. These range across the gonzometer from very modest deeds to great epic feats. Because these are told from your characters' perspective, the actual acts may not have been as stupendous as you remember \u2014 or maybe they were even more amazing. You know how memories can change. Feel free to go gonzo, if you wish, just be sure to focus on the three virtues being asked about: Courage, Power, Wisdom. (Bonus brownie points if your anecdote explains the pilgrim name, too.)

    Crimson Kite, tell us of a time when Outspoken Gift showed Courage, when Precise Book showed Power, and when Unexpected Blessings showed Wisdom.

    Outspoken Gift, tell us of a time when Precise Book showed Courage, when Unexpected Blessings showed Power, and when Crimson Kite showed Wisdom.

    Precise Book, tell us of a time when Unexpected Blessings showed Courage, when Crimson Kite showed Power, and when Outspoken Gift showed Wisdom.

    Unexpected Blessings, tell us of a time when Crimson Kite showed Courage, when Outspoken Gift showed Power, and when Precise Book showed Wisdom.

    \n Because these are other characters' memories of you, there may be inaccuracies compared to how your character sees themselves. Everyone's memories are prone to exaggeration or plain old error. (Especially if they're kids.)

    If there is any concern about authority, don't worry... The next, final step of character creation will give you agency over your character again and tie all this together.

    My recommendation would be to simply write your memories as your character would see them, unreliable as those memories may be.

    \nCourage - Precise Book
    Precise Book once accepted to took the place of a character in a story. He was so afraid to die at the end of the book that he didn't let anyone turn the last page.     There was a village where the dead refused to rest. They wanted to stay with their families and loved ones. They lived in their former houses, shivering with cold even when it was warm, never eating or drinking, unable to work. The wheel of life was stuck, the dead had to go so their loved ones could go on with their life. As we couldn't make them change their minds, I saw Unexpected Blessings, the two sisters that live as one, open the gates to Hell, engulfing the village's dead in a whirlwind made of angry spirits. The ghosts looked like a giant face that devoured the dead, bowing to the sisters and calling them "Queens".

    Courage - Crimson Kite

    One blustery spring day, the Brides and Tinat stood in the icy morning frost, taking turns flying our sister's kite. It was a beautiful, dangerous thing, like a brightly colored tropical fish with ten-thousand poisonous spines. Tinat was showing us how to make it dive and ripple, like a living thing in the air. A particularly spectacular maneuver startled a tree full of sparrows, scattering them to the heavens, ignorant of the dangers of sharing the sky with Tinat's battle kite. Not wanting the injure the birds, Tinat struggled to reel in her line. When the kite's spiny tail was within reach, Tinat grabbed it unhesitatingly and yanked the kite to the ground. Though her hand left a red trail through the frost, Tinat refused to the abbott attend to her before he had mended the wing of one unlucky sparrow chick.

    Power - Outspoken Gift

    The previous winter, Jing Lao and the Brides stood within the archway of the temple's great door, watching for the first signs of snow. Jing Lao spotted it first, a single perfect snowflake dancing slowly some 100 yards away from us, down the frosted steps that led up to the temple. "It is a tragedy," we said, "that the snowflake will simply melt, chilling the earth ever so slightly in preparation for its ten-thousand siblings, in the hopes that some will survive, even for a few hours." Before we had finished speaking, Jing Lao had closed to without a few yards of the snowflake, moving effortlessly but with incredible speed, skipping across the treacherous stones. Mouth agape, he caught the snowflake on the tip of his tongue, but, between the three of us, we could not decide if this was a nobler fate.

    Wisdom - Precise Book

    The previous fall, Vanya was telling stories around the central fireplace when a great wind descended down the chimney and extinguished the fire. Eager for the stories to continue, the Brides, along with the rest of Vanya's audience, helped relight the fire. However, Vanya had barely finished his next sentence when the winds came again and the fire was dead once more. The Brides reached for the tenderbox, but Vanya said, "The chimney spirit is trying to sleep and is angry at being awakened, even by our fanciful tales. Let us try again tomorrow." He left a ginger root standing in a bowl of milk on the hearth and, from then on, we always made an effort to be polite to the chimney spirit.

    Step 3: Bonds

    \nThis is the final step. If you've been keeping track of the Tiger Group's thread, you may already have thought about what you'll do in this step. I've made a few tweaks and edits to the list of options, so you probably want to review the list again. If this is your first exposure to this step of character creation, let's start from the top:\n
    ...Now that you've heard your companions' memories of you, the temple elders ask you to meditate on your relationship to each of your companions...
    \nIn this final step of character creation, you will choose the type of relationship you have to each of your companions. These relationships are called "Bonds."

    You should have some sense of what Bonds do in play before choosing one, even if we're not going into an actual session. They're kind of like a Key from Shadow of Yesterday, mixed with the Thematic Batteries from Full Light, Full Steam. A Bond has certain conditions that allow you to store up resources to make your character more effective in the future. The twist is that the only way to actually spend those resources is to change the Bond to something else.\n
    For example, in Futurama, Fry has been cultivating a Bond of the Crush to Leela for a few sessions. While on a delivery, Leela gets surrounded by some angry mutants. Fry is desperate to help, but there are too many for him to have any hope of defeating on his own. He's got to use the dice he's saved up, but in order do so, the relationship has to change. Fry's player decides to change the relationship to Bond of Fraternity. He accepts that they won't be lovers, but they will still be the best of friends.
    \nWithout getting too deep into the mechanical details, that's how bonds are used in play.

    Click here for an updated, but still brief and incomplete list of titles and descriptions for a number of Bonds. If you can think of another type of Bond that would be more appropriate for your character, go ahead and write that one down but please give me a description so that I can assess whether it should be added to the general list.

    Choose one bond for each of your companions. You cannot have the same type of bond to two or more companions. This Bond is your relationship to that character, but that doesn't necessarily reflect their relationship to you. For example, you might have a Bond of Adoption with your companion. You consider them like a sister to you. But that companion might not feel the same way. In fact, she might have a Bond of Jealousy towards you, meaning that she envies you for some reason.

    After choosing a Bond, explain how your, their, or both of your memories' led to your relationship being in that state.

    In Summary...

    Step 3:
    Crimson Kite, what are your bonds to Outspoken Gift, Precise Book and Unexpected Blessings?

    Outspoken Gift, what are your bonds to Precise Book, Unexpected Blessings and Crimson Kite?

    Precise Book, what are your bonds to Unexpected Blessings, Crimson Kite and Outspoken Gift?

    Unexpected Blessings, what are your bonds to Crimson Kite, Outspoken Gift and Precise Book?

    Finally, a question for all of you, what is it about you that fosters this bond?

    \nThe Bond of Adoption with Outspoken Gift

    Outspoken Gift lost his parents. Yes, he betrayed them, but he was a child when he was forced to speak against them. I also lost my parents. As much as I love Nyesh and Lur, I know that I am not their kin, not a Root Troll. Outspoken Gift and I are orphans, and orphans must stick together.

    The Bond of the Caretaker with Precise Book

    Precise Book is so smart--he sees what others do not. Unfortunately, he is sometimes so focused on the unseen that he misses what is right in front of him. He needs me to protect him from the world.

    The Bond of Jealousy with Unexpected Blessings

    As I said, Outspoken Gift and I have lost our true families. The twins may be dead, but they still have each other. I don't even know if I have a sister or brother somewhere out there.
