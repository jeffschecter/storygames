What I think of as science fiction is an emphasis on objective discoveries and Figuring Things Out. It kind of gets confusing because in some SF, it's the characters who Figure Things Out (usually optimistic in tone,) while in other SF, it's the author who Figures Things Out (and the tone is sometimes a pessimistic prophecy.)

    Holy crap. Of all the times for me to be away from the fora for days at a time!

    When I first started to write Shock: I made the hyperbolic claim that it was the first science fiction RPG ever written. Traveller is a game of space opera and derring-do. Cyberpunk's about maximizing your destructive potential.

    The reason I felt like that was the case is that science fiction isn't just the trappings: the laser guns, flying cars, time travel, and so forth. Instead, all that stuff is there to illustrate the differences between worlds of the reader (viewer, player) and that of the fiction.

    And the parts of those worls that really matter are the social structure, not the heroic cycle of the characters. The characters are tools that demonstrate a particular point of view. Deckard is the ambivalence between order and humanity. Paul Atreides is a force of justice in a world of Macchiavellian politics. Daneel is the triumph of Reason.

    Then I had a chat with Ben Lehman, who pointed out that, between Life Paths and Empathy (both removed in more recent version of the game), you could use Cyberpunk to tell a story about alienation in an uncaring world.

    And I realized that Paranoia totally supports its premise of humans versus their own order.

    Those social concerns are magnified by, but not defined by, the differences between the readers' world and the fictional one. Replicants let us talk about slavery and class systems. The Spice lets us talk about oil and religious fanaticism. Robots let us look at what what humans would be like if we could only do right.

    There's some discussion of this over here on my Forge forum. The topics remain open, even though some are pretty old.

    Give the Wikipedia definition, linked thereing, a good read. It's excellent.
