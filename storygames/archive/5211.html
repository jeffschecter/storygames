So this morning, the files for Sons of Liberty went off to the printer's, with assurances that the books would be coming back on time for the release on President's Day. Which was a good feeling.

But the files being complete also means that the Full PDF is now available for preorderers. I've also put together a Preview PDF for the perusal of folks who'd like to check things out. This is a fifty-five page booklet giving you a peek of every nook and cranny of the game: Patriots and Tories; Battle, Campaign, and Versus Mode; Options Screen, Index, everything I know I'd flip through at the store.

So, if you've got an itch for madcap clockwork revolutionary action, check out that Preview PDF or place your preorder to get the whole thing.

Also, a correction: earlier posts said this was going to be a 128-page book; it's exploded out of its bookplan, and will be, instead, 160 pages of clockwork power armor, assault ornithopters, and solar-powered laser beams.

And there's the cover, Jonathan!

    \n Hey, Seth!

    In the land of rules changes, there's:
    1) Cornwallis has been added to the Tories (p51)
    2) Travel Hand Tory Score is top two cards (p90)
    3) Tory player only sets out three Tyranny chips in each Battle Mode Level (p96)
    4) Forcing Patriots out of circumstances is an explicit rule (p100)
    5) In Versus Mode, Call for Cards allows you to steal cards from your opponent's support line. (p135)
    6) The Options Screen is live (p137-142)

    In the land of clarified rules, there's:
    1) Loads of examples.
    2) All "Steal Card" abilities are correctly restricted to ends of straights
    3) Traffic Cop section. (p95)
    4) Clarification on the Tory initial discard of face cards (p96)
    5) Added diagrams to show what plays go where (p85 and 135)

    Many of the Patriot profiles have been elaborated a bit, as well. The book has the full complement of art by Jake, Anna, and Leo. It should also be a lot easier to flip through and navigate.

    Scott, I expect Sons of Liberty will be at IPR on or a little after President's Day. I'm talking with Brennan now to make arrangements.
