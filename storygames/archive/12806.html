    Posted By: Kayfall"Do" comes from the Chinese character "dao," meaning "way" or "path," but it's pronounced in a Korean/Japanese way.
    \nI went with the current pronunciation and spelling as a sort of double-meaning. I liked the meaning of "path" since destiny and karma are some of the long-term arcs in the stories. I spelled it "Do" because it's also a verb in English that often comes up in RPGs: "What do you do?" Just seemed like a cute overlap.

    Unfortunately, it's a terrible google search term, so I added "Pilgrims of the Flying Temple." :P

    \n Well, one comment I have about the new character creation system, which I didn't think before, is that it doesn't have the characters' actual given names anymore, only their Temple name, with the Banner and the Avatar. Before it used to be Omambeh (which means "a collision of two worlds") the Crashing Wind. I think the "worldly name," as you used to call it doesn't add too much time to character creation, but still adds a ton of... well... character!

    It occurred to me because I wondered why people would be giving superheroes and villains Temple names, and then I realized that only having a Temple name makes your character more generic, and I believe, inherently more gonzo, than a character with an actual given name. It's like a Superman without the Clark Kent.
