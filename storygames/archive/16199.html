\nPlaying with Buddha and Joe is the highest-trust gaming environment I've ever had, and we let that trust and Joe Mcdaldno's rules take us crazy, crazy places and we loved it. Hope Mardock, my Hollow, was all over the map. I reflected back how other's behaved or projected on me: lovey-dovey with my boyfriend, violent and manipulative to turn the tables on cultist posing as a police to try to kill me, and playing the part of the victim afterward. My default state, though, was cheerful and disarmingly honest (which through the police Psychologist for a hell of a loop. "Dissociative" was definitely written in his notebook on me), though lacking the full context of what I said. Switching between that and "I want you to hurt me"/attempted suicide by cop darkest self mode was intense!

Hopefully this will lure my collaborators out so they can post some more substantive actual-play writing.

    \n Oh man, this game, this game, this game. We went to some straaange places. Places I certainly was not expecting go, so I guess that's keeping the story feral for you.

    (Apocalypse-Powered games practically run themselves, I think; it's definitely some of the easiest GMing I've ever done).

    Our setup brought some unexpected things out of the players-- George, who I usually think of as the most sweet-natured member of our group, left a smoking trail of physical and social chaos in his character's wake, and Buddha, who was almost literally begging to play a dark vicious game for a change, did his utmost to resist the demands of his character's Darker Self and give everyone the endings they deserved.

    I got one foot out the door, but I'll update with some personal highlights later. Coming attractions: Hope and Leatherface under a tree, K-I-S-S-I-N-G; and why stabbing yourself in the brain is never a good idea even if you're POSITIVE you can survive it.

The Mortal is the killer app of Monsterhearts for me, a hilarious laying bare of the tremendous emotional unhealthiness that lurks at the center of oh so many trashy teen romance stories, and adding Infernal on top of that puts your character into a codependent relationship with both your Lover and Satan.
