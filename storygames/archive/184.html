Playtesting early?

Levi Kornelsen
February 2006 edited February 2006 in Story Games
So, I'm working on my game, grinding away, and I've hit on an idea that's new to me.

I'm going to make playtest kits for the game before I finish writing it. - Basically, a set of pre-made characters, a situation, and enough rules to play it through, and then use the feedback from those to polish and help define the fiddly bits of the game as I go.

Has anyone ever tried something like this before? How did it work? Any thoughts?

    It's fair to playtest when you think that the rules are done, with the hope that actual play will prove that you are correct, and that the rules work as you intended.

    But yeah, some of my most useful (and painful) playtesting comes in the early stages where I have a sense that I know what I want when I see it, but can't explain what it is. I very rapidly get a chance to say "That's not it, that's not it ... oh, wait! That moment was cool! What made that happen?"

  • Malcolm Craig
    February 2006
    As Tony said, thinking that the rules are done and then playtesting is often the way to go. My recent experience with the ongoing playtest of Cold City has taught me a lot about this. I thought the rules were done, turns out that playtesting highlighted the fact that they are nowhere near done, that elements of the game do not achieve what I wanted them to and that there was much further work to be done.

    On the whole, it's a very positive experience and, to address you point, I wish that I had started playtesting at a much earlier stage, before I had many things fixed in my head.

  • Colin_Fredericks

    I prefer playtesting (at least with some one-shots) as soon as I set the concept of what the game and its world should really be like. Whatever basic set of mechanics I have at that point gets used, and refined based on what happens. One of my friends playtested Sufficiently Advanced for me when it was just the 30-page quickstart, and now it's an 83-page alpha version. Would it have helped him to wait until the alpha? Probably, but it would have taken a lot longer to *get* to the alpha without the playtest.
