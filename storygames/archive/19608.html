    What the heck can it do?
    It can get you in a metric fuck-ton of trouble! Burning down cities is a barrel of laughs, right? More seriously:

    1) Right. You roll the 10+ and pick two of the options. Then I, as GM, describe what my agenda and principles demand. The fire attacks the bubears. Was it a little fire or big? Are there only a few or a whole nation of bubears? What have we previously established about how fire elementals (or whatever) work? What have we established about bubear behavior? Give all that and everything else I know as the GM, I make a move. Maybe I just deal damage and maybe it's enough to slay all the bubears. Maybe not. Maybe the fire will burn them all to death but it'll take time because you commanded a candle flame. But remember there will certainly be problems, too, because you didn't choose all three of those options.

    2) fuel, time, blood, scars, future debt, unexpected consequences, etc.

    \n 1. It could mean damage is dealt, depending on what is happening. Fire does not have the ability to "kill" those bugbears, all it can do is burn them. If the fire is small and the bugbears are currently fighting the PCs then dealing damage based on the GM's judgment and the guidelines on page 21 (23 in the pdf). If the fire is huge and the bugbears are "over there" then just decide how it works out. Probably the bugbears are toast, but maybe some run away.

    2. Nature's Price: ask yourself "what does this element want?" If that doesn't give you anything, ask: "What does this land want?"

    Fire wants to burn, so you must allow it to burn stuff. It wants to run rampant across the forest, it wants that house over there, etc.

    Changing the weather could have disastrous effects. If you want it to rain where you are, maybe the price is drought on the other side of the mountains? Or maybe the price of rain is thunder and lightning. I hope none of the PCs are encased in metal on top of a horse riding over a hill!

    If you want to summon a river, maybe you must permanently divert the flow of water, which creates swamps and islands and messes up some steading's agriculture.

    If you want the earth to heave, maybe it also belches forth poisonous gasses. Or pieces of rock explode all over the place, or lava bursts forth from the earth, instead of just poisonous gasses!

    3. Shapeshifting: Yes, spend a hold, automatically take the action described by the move. Roll for damage if you think the opponent should be able to withstand the attack. If not, just kill them. Like, for real, bears are sick. Anyone who tells you they would rather swordfight a bear than a man has a serious deathwish. A bear will take a sword wound and raise you a single bite that shatters your skull. Be a fan of the PCs!

    \n A price is payed and that's the end of it. When something is out of control, when does it stop? Only when the PCs stop it. So if the fire says "I want the sacred tree" and the PC is like "no way Jose" and chooses out of control instead, that just means whatever fire they summoned already is going to stick around and get worse. If it's not near the sacred tree, it can't just automatically go for it, it has to spread from wherever it is until it gets there. If it IS near the sacred tree, that could be one hell of a hard choice!

    Basically, make the price a concrete thing that isn't ongoing (or is a permanent change) and then make out of control be that the element continues to do the thing it was asked to do and just takes it way too far.

    Yes, if the Druid takes on bird form, she can fly automatically. But flying isn't always such an easy thing, yeah? It requires space, air currents, it takes a lot of work to get up to the sky all the way from the ground. It takes effort, skill, concentration to fly through a storm. So it is totally legit to call for a defy danger when the bird is doing some super-difficult flying. Maybe the danger is exhaustion? Maybe the danger is that she doesn't take to flight fast enough to get away from the wyvern's jaws? If she spends a hold and says "yeah no, I take to flight, done and done" then there's no roll and the move just happens.

    Does all that make sense?
