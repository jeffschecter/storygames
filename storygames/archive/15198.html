    \n Did the final version of Fisherman's Wife ever come out? I hadn't heard anything since the ashcan a couple Gen Cons ago.

    The Cel*Style crew is doing a lot with playing cards lately; GxB (and its upcoming hack BxB), The Tulip Academy's Society for Dangerous Gentlemen, and Hot Guys Making Out all use a standard deck. I've played all three; they're all pretty short, very narration-heavy games that last an hour or two. Of the three, Tulip Academy most easily lends itself to ongoing play (the other two seem really one-shotty, since they have predetermined characters and situation), but there's not a lot of character advancement.

    GxB's card use is almost vestigial, but then it's a very very simple game; basically, Momoko's player gives her suitors' players a face-down card every time they charm her on their dates, and at game's end she falls for the suitor who drew the most Hearts.

    Tulip Academy has more of a "game" element; a Dangerous Gentleman solves problems by his skill at Art, Science, or Fencing, and each has their own competitive minigame based on an existing card game (conflicts of Science are similar to blackjack, for instance). The deck is also used as an oracle to frame scenes and pick the general theme of the adventure.

    Hot Guys Making Out falls between the two; there's no competitive element per se, but the actions your character can take are dependent on exactly what card you played this turn (the characters have a usual range of behavior, but playing a heart or the court card each corresponds to allows them to dramatically break character). The rhythm this enforces is surprisingly evocative of the languidly-paced romance manga it emulates, but I'll post a bit more about that in the Bite-Sized AP thread.
