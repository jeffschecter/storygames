\n Know how sometimes in conflict resolution all the fun bits are more or less aggreed upon before the roll/draw of cards? This way, you front load the conflict and make all the interesting choices and creative input before actually "playing" (I know that a lot of people consider this to be "wrong" -well, it happens and I've definetely been involved in it).
\nIn Nine Worlds you simply cannot do this! You start out by stating your goal of the conflict - but, and here's where the cool bits are, you cannot decide exactly what happens until you have drawn cards and chosen the Urge to use.
\nWant to escape from prison? Great, that's your goal - but depending on whether you choose Chaos, Cosmos, Metamorphosis or Stasis, the narration, what actually happens will only be settled as you narrate.
\nI love this feature. It keeps you on your toes, gives you just enough input to NOT feel like a blank slate yet leaves a lot of room for in the moment creativity.
\nMatt - it's a winner!

    \n And you're quite right, John! Yesterday we played the second session over Skype/irc.
    \nHere's a little gem from the first session:
    \nPer's Archon, Deucalion, a doctor who helped develop the plague that's currently raging on Sol, is en route to Sol to attend a medical conference. One of Deucalion's Muses - check this out! - is to infect Apollo with the plague! But Apollo wants to destroy Deucalion, so he cannot spill the beans. Apollo's forces intercept Deucalion's aethership and we enter conflict mode. In one conflict, over several rounds, Deucalion escapes from his aethership (1st round), reaches Sol (2nd round) but is then caught by Apollo just as he is about to poison him (as Apollo is about to enjoy a show at the theatre). Apollo grins at Deucalion and says words to the effect: "Did you really think I'd set a disease loose on Sol that I wasn't immune to?". And then Apollo strikes Deucalion down, sending him directly to Hades (although mechanically, Deucalion isn't dead) (3rd round). All this from one conflict, using different Urges.
    \nCoolness!

    \n Note that I'm not saying that most Story Games work in this manner! But some can be played in this, perhaps faulty, way. What springs to mind right now is PTA. You reach conflict and discuss the Stakes - this happens if I win, this happens if you win. If you are not careful, you may end up narrating the two different outcomes before resolution. All I'm saying is that Nine Worlds makes this almost impossible, and it's very noticable in play. You may have an idea of how you'll end up meeting your goal, but that idea may be totally screwed once you see your hand of cards. You wanted to create a new door in the wall of the prison that you want to escape (Cosmos) but ended up playing Stasis and decide to narrate that you freeze the guard in his tracks the next time he's in your cell, about to beat you up.
