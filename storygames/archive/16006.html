\n What d'yall think of this - it's trying to solve the problem that I want to play Ribbon Drive again, but the only venues I have to do it aren't going to let us play music and it'll probably be a pick-up game with no player prep.

To prepare: instead of making a mix tape, you make a list of several songs and poems with a running theme and a descriptive title. Bring a printout of the first two songs or poems.
If people didn't have time to prepare, you need to have a tablet or phone capable of web-browsing handy. Give everyone some time to come up a theme, title, and at least two songs/poems to fit the theme.

Pick a player at random; he's the current poet. Instead of playing their mix, they read their first song or poem aloud. Game proceeds as normal, discussing the nature of the road trip as it relates to the first song or poem.
We get a rough idea of our characters.

Then the same poet reads their second song or poem, and then Ribbon Drive as normal again - at least one of your character's traits must come from the poem.

Then, instead of restarting the mix, the poet reads their next poem/song until somebody else is inspired to start a scene, and we play Ribbon Drive as normal, with this difference: whenever you're stuck for inspiration, you may call out, "Line" and the current poet will read a line from a poem/song in their list. It doesn't even have to be the next line, but no wasting a lot of our time searching for 'the perfect line'. If you are the poet you can still ask yourself for a line.

Between scenes, the poet will also read-until-the-next-player-is-inspired. (On their own scene, they read until they're inspired.)

On a detour, a new poet is chosen at random, and they read their whole first poem/song aloud before play continues. (Shouldn't take too long - takes a lot less time to read song lyrics than to listen to the whole song.)

And yeah, I think that's it. Ribbon Drive that I can play at story games night. What do you think? I'll probably try it next Thursday.

    \n Jamie, that sounds really awesome! I've been wondering how to do a poetry-based Ribbon Drive game, and hadn't come up with anything as elegant as this.

    I have, however, come up with a illustration-based Ribbon Drive game, specifically designed for conventions. I'll share it here, in case you're interested. It's going in the new printing that'll be released over the coming months.

    Ribbon Drive: Sketchbook Edition

    Each player should prepare the following: a collection of 10-15 images, each on a separate page. They should all have a theme, and the album/collection should be given an evocative title. The game will work best if the images are symbolic/impressionistic/touchstones, rather than a storyboard. This could be 15 cartoons you did on consecutive sketchbook pages, or 15 collages, or an album of 15 photos. If you've got an iPad, these can be contained digitally as a slideshow.

    For the opening song of the game, use 1 image. For the character-inspiring song, use the 2nd image.

    For each scene, move on to the next image. If a scene is super short, the album creator can choose to let the same image roll on over for one more scene. If a scene seems like it needs new direction, the album creator can choose to flip ahead by an image.

    Detours involve switching albums.
