Instant Plot Generator?

kkriegg
February 2007 edited February 2007 in Story Games
Here's something I came up with that I was pondering for awhile. I'd especially appreciate any ideas that would make it more complete or otherwise more "inspiring." The details of course are to be filled in to fit a certain setting.

First, roll a D6. A roll of 1-2 means the first roll will deal with the Hook, and the second the Meat of the adventure. A roll of 3-4 means the first role is the Personal or mental aspect, the second the Physical aspect. A roll of 5-6 means the first roll is the primary adventure, but the second is the suprise.

Roll the D6 to determine if the adventure is dealing with a Person, Place, or Thing. Then roll the D6 again to get the details. The details are split between Even and Odd results, depending on what number you rolled to get that catagory. Repeat this process again, with the relationship between the two rolls as determined by your first roll (above).

1-2: People
1: Odd: VIP; protect somebody or somebodies important from something (or themselves), Even: Manhunt; track somebody down.
2: Odd: Rescue; person or people in trouble, need saving, Even: Capture; somebody needs to be captured or otherwise brought back for whatever reason.
3: Odd: Treachery; somebody has betrayed the players (or will), Even: Blackmail; the players or somebody close to them has been blackmailed.
4: Odd: Preparation; an army must be raised, a student taught, a purification ceremony, eta, Even: Recovery; Somebody has been altered in some way, you must bring them back to the Light Side, cure their illness, remove the nanites, eta.
5: Odd: Spy; players must spy on a person and report back with their findings, Even: Counterspy; players must find a spy and foil his attempts.
6: Odd: Defend; players are called upon to protect somebody in danger, Even: Attack; players must attack somebody or some nefarious group.

3-4: Places
1: Odd: Invade; players must invade or attack a place, Even: Defend; players must defend a place from invaders.
2: Odd: Sanctuary; players are in a big, bad place and must find a way into a nearby good place to be safe, Even: Stuck; players are in bad place and must escape it (prison, desert, planet, eta).
3: Odd: Explore; players end up in a different place and must explore it and learn more, Even: Visitor; somebody different shows up and the players must learn about him/her/it and where it came from.
4: Odd: Capture Target; there is one place the players must capture and defend for the greater good, Even: Defend Target; players must defend a vital point or risk endangering others.
5: Odd: Race; players must get to another location in a set amount of time, Even: Capture; players must prevent somebody else from getting to a certain place in a certain amount of time.
6: Odd: Cursed; there is some place that is passively cursed or dangerous, and threatens to have the danger realized, Even: Recent Ruins; some place gets destroyed and it is up to the players to figure out why.

5-6: Things
1: Odd: Protect; players must protect something important, Even: Steal; players must steal something important, or otherwise get it out of the wrong hands.
2: Odd: Deliver; players are entrusted with an object and must deliver it, Even: Intercept; players must change the destination of an object, or capture it in transit.
3: Odd: Create; players must fix the reactor, gather the seven stones, eta, Even: Destroy; players must crush the idol of evil, disable the shield array, eta.
4: Odd: Change; players must alter the properties of an object somehow, Even: Maintain; players must stop the properties of an important object from being altered.
5: Odd: Gather; players must collect alot of an object, be it food, or money for a crushing debt, Even: Disperse; players must break open the vault and give the money back to the masses, or steal the wizards collection of power amulets and bury them at the four corners of the earth, eta.
6: Odd: Sell; players must sell an object, or run a buisiness, or make a living, Even: Buy; players need something and must track it down and get the best deal possible, or they must run an institution or establishment, or budget something they are not used to managing.

    Can I say something negative without being considered a big negative guy?

    There's nothing wrong with random stimulus for helping to generate action in your game, I suppose. But coming up with a plot is dead simple. I mean pick a Shakespeare play, file the numbers off, and run with it if you want. If you can't come up with one just off the cuff. Which I'll bet all of you can.

    I spent years collecting plots from other RPG scenarios, or from books or plays, and writing them all up. In one campaign I prided myself on being no less than fifty plots ahead of the players at one point - enough that I could pick and choose, making it seem like there was nothing railroady about the plot selected (uber illusionism). I recall, however, one of my player's comments one night, "Ah, tonight's plot hook. Let's get on with it."

    It never really satisfied.

    The trick isn't getting a plot, IMO, but in finding a situation that's going to be interesting to play out. And randomization won't particularly deliver that. It takes actively looking at what interests the players, and playing to that. Sure you can modify the plot generated randomly above to do that, but then, as I say, you can do it without the generator as well.

    So... eh..

    Mike
