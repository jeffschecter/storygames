\n Last year, we launch the first edition of the Vieux Pots, Nouvelles soupes contest:

http://story-games.com/forums/comments.php?DiscussionID=14218&page=1#Item_55

It was only about francophones games, but it was a great experience.

The 2012 session is about to begin, and i'd want to open the contest to RPG originally published in english. People could send their submission also in this language.

I wonder if there would be some people here interested in being part of the "judges". Requirements:

- You will express yourself in english, you only need to be able to understand (a minimum) written french (you may have to read submissions in french, and read what others judges are saying, thought you won't have to read all submissions)

- You have published a "commercial" game OR a free game with an "award" of some kind (Game chef, etc...)

I will be more than happy to welcome people who may be interested...

    \n Hi Ben

    All the submissions in 2011
    http://www.narrativiste.eu/2011/vieux-pots-panorama-des-remakes-proposes-en-telechargement/

    the 2011 finalists
    http://www.narrativiste.eu/2011/vieux-pots-les-laureats2011/

    The winner was "Alienoid, the next wave", a remake of Alienoid (1992/3 ?), by G. Pogorzelski : players are violent and naive aliens in charge of a secret mission on earth, in order to prepare the invasion. In my opinion, and for all others judges, an excellent game (the remake), one of the best, if not the best storygame i've seen in french.

    The mains rules for the entries:


    - the game have to be 20 years old, at least (we can make exceptions)
    - Asking the author about it is better
    - You can make a hack or an original system
    - keep it concise: the less pages, the better
    - make it quick and fun to play : bring card, board, etc to give non-players and former RPG gamers want to try it
    - play also with the original background : prequel, sequel, spinoff, inversion, etc..

    I don't know if i've answered your question Ben, but if you want to join, it would bring to the contest a great oportunity (not to mention it would be a joy for me)

    About the actuals judges:

    - Laurent Tremel as been co-autor os Berlin XVII (1988) and Slirin. He's now sociologist, intererested in gaming. Berlin XVII was one of my favorites games ever.
    - Yann is the author Crimes, and co founder of Les Ecuries d\u2019Augias
    - Daniel Danjean is known for La m\xe9thode du Docteur Chestel, an cult game from 1989-1990 where the players are doctors entering the mind of their patients
    - Fabien Hildwein, author of Monostatos
    - Ludovic Papais, is the founder of La Boite a heuhh, publisher of games translated from english.
    - and myself, founder of Narrativiste Editions and Narrativiste.eu

    complete list:
    http://www.narrativiste.eu/2011/vieux-pots-jury/
