    \n [1] Dungeons: The Dragoning. If you've a love for over-the-top gonzo, you should download it while you've got the opportunity; it's full of so much copyright violation, I'm surprised it's lasted this long.
    There's a bookmarked version of it here: http://www.mediafire.com/?mrr05jppcijg3ag
    Creator's blog here: http://lawfulnice.blogspot.com/
    It's Spelljammer and Planescape with bits of 40K as setting, you get to play characters that pick a race from the ones in D&D and 40K, exaltation from White Wolf stuff and a profession selection of which is 40K + D&D. System is like L5R/Seventh Sea combined with White Wolf and 40K. Lots of things are bashed together, for example Dwarves = Squats, Eldarin = Eldar, Dark Eldarin = Drow and similar. There's apparently also some Deadlands bits in there. It's a mess, it looks playable, and page 36 made me grin. Gotta love the homebrewers.

    \nThe Booth at the End is a web series written by Chris Kubasik of "Play Sorcerer" fame. It's a very Sorcerer-esque setup: "I offer you a task; you do the task; you get what you want."     \n\nApril 2011 \n edited May 2011
    \n

    \n
    \n
    \n [3] THE BOOTH AT THE END

    First of all, I think it's "Christopher Kubasik of PLAY SORCERER infamy."

    Just so those involved know, I'm on the last leg of the book. I sent an email to my manager on Monday of this week and said, "Okay, don't bother me with any more ideas or work until I get this book done." I'm pushing forward with all the work I've done, shaping it into the volume that will become PLAY SORCERER. (I never stopped working on it -- I've just been working on it in little, constantly interrupted chunks.)

    Right now its rolling out across the globe via Fox International. We just premiered this week in England. We got this nice -- well, amazing, actually -- review in THE GUARDIAN, including this amazing passage:

    "There is more talent and effort poured into one 120-second nugget, demanding a corresponding surge in effort and engagement from the viewer, than there is in almost any hour of TV elsewhere that you care to name."

    I was convinced someone was paid off to write that -- but apparently it's sincere.

    Full review at the Guardian here.

    Everyone on the show did a fantastic job and I'm proud as all hell that people are liking it.


    So, again, having gotten THE BOOTH across a goal line and other projects close to being set up, I'm non-stop on PLAY SORCERER till it's done.

      \n Steven Pressfield, novelist, screenwriter, former Marine and all around great guy, wrote a book a few years back called "The War of Art."

      He publishes a new book today, "Do The Work", which is specifically focused on getting things designed, built and out the door.

      And I just found out as I poked around Amazon that you can get "Do The Work" for free for a Kindle Ap through May 20th. They've got a Kindle Ap you can download for Macs, PCs, Androiods and so on... if you're interested.

      I haven't read "Do The Work" yet, but I'm looking forward to cracking it open.
