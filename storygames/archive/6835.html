\n My FLGS had a policy where each person was alloted only one item from the free stuff table,
which i thought was harsh until i rolled up and couldn't find anything worth bringing home.

it was all either silly modules or fantasy heartbreakers; i'd flip it open and the words "task resolution system" would
virtually jump off the page and bite my eyes.

there was a live-action cthulu thing which provided hand-waving instead of instructions on how to play.
there was a tunnels and trolls thing, and a traveller thing, and the new WOD hunter thing which still looks boring.

yeah, everything was just so depressing! i eventually settled for a HarnMaster suppliment because it had a non-hitpoint damage system... turns out the system is more complicated and less intuitive. oh well.

i guess i'm just disappointed. i thought this would be a cool event, not that i was contributing in any way.
i heard Chris was putting out a little thing at his FLGS though, and i was wondering if there were other cool regional things that other folks experienced, or perhaps they enjoyed something that i turned my nose up at?

anyway.

thanks for listening, internet. [/blog]

    \nEndgame limited people to two free RPGs, and cleverly put some out at opening time, then a bit more every couple of hours, so there were things available throughout the day. It looked like the D&D4e adventure with map and mini went fastest (I got one!) and a couple tables were playing it upstairs, I heard.

    Endgame also had a few copies of a 5-page preview of Don't Lose Your Mind, the upcoming supplement to Don't Rest your Head. All of those were gone by day's end. I ran 3 demos for DRYH upstairs when I wasn't running Polaris, but a casual scan of the DRYH website doesn't show me where I got the demo from. Jimmy Hoffa cut a deal with the ethereal tiger, and DB Cooper got away, as he always does. Amelia Earhart saved the day, as is her way.
    The Polaris players got free copies of XsL too.

    There were still some games left at the end of the day, including some XsL, so maybe Sunday will be Free Game Day 2: Eclectic Bugaboo.

    Caeser_X bought a 4e DMG to go with his PHB, and compared 4e to college football.

    I finally bought Burning Empires, which I totally blame on Paul Tevis' recent podcast about his campaign.

    \n I'm still poring over the stuff I got for free ( I bought minis gaming goodness by Ganesha games, too, so that's been my reading focus).

    One that might have been overlooked that I'm digging with my mid-day coffee is Punjar: The Tarnished Jewel[Dungeon Crawl Classics line from Goodman Games].

    It's a little, grimy fantasy city along the lines of Sanctuary or Lankhmar, with a D&D fantasy spin. It's systemless, however, with no stats of any kind in the item. To me, that's a benefit, not a weakness. It's short ( 16 pages), and keeps to rough sketches and hooks that are left hanging to expand in whatever direction you see fit. In any case, it clearly mirrors that type of setting, minus the baggage that can accompany trying to set an adventure in an existing setting from a series of books.

    Despite the traditional warning to character players to keep out, this one could be used as a very cool open-book item with the Dirty-Hippy Game of your choice- IAWA would work well with this, as would other games containing a play phase where the group collectively fleshes out what they want from the game ( Universalis and PTA come to mind).

    Heck, it might work better with a DHG than with D&D...

    It isn't perfect by any stretch. Mysteriously little detail is given about the actual overlord of the city or his personal quarter, and it does hit about every grimy fantasy city cliche you've ever read.

    I suspect this one got overlooked with all the goodies there to choose from. At EG, there were a small stack of them left at around 5pm. Anyone who digs that sort of setting might want to check with their FLGS and see if they have any left.
