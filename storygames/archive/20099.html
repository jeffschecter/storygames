\n So, I had some family visit today. The kids found some of my gaming die and asked me what it was for. I told em it was for playing certain kinds of games called roleplaying games. They asked how it worked and then immediately asked to play. I quickly realized I had no games on hand that were good for kids their age range (4, 7, and 15). The 15 year old could obviously handle your standard rpg, but the minute I bust out the rules for anything even remotely complicated I would lose the other two and they'd probably go on an unholy terror full of kid energy. So... with their inspiration, I created Warrior Princess, an RPG for ages 4+.
Here's the PDF and here's a JPG.

There's a lot of weird mechanics in here. For instance, the game is based off a d10 roll, because that's the first die those kids found and wanted to roll a bunch. Then they found some of my other die and kept begging to roll them as well, hence the d4, 6, and 8 found a home within the system as well.

I also tried to shoehorn someway to get these kids to learn, so there's small bits of addition/subtraction and fact recall built into the system. Surprisingly, the kids really liked the spell casting system cause they got to brag about weird facts they knew.

At any rate, this was kid tested and adult approved. I hope you enjoy it. If you manage to run it w/ some youngins of your own, please let me know how it played. (For mixed gender groups, I called it Warrior Princess and Prince)
