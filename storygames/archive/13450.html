\n We created our cluster last night and my god was it fun. Very excited for our upcoming game.

Anyway, I have a couple rules questions while running through the book I was hoping Halfjack or someone could help answer.

1. How do defensive beams work in the beam phase of starship combat? My confusion stems from page 147 and this sentence: "A roll with no modification is made to oppose all incoming Beam attacks. Ships may have a Stunt (Vector Randomizer) that changes the base from 0 to 2."

That sentence seems definitive, but my gut is telling me that it simply implies if you do not have a beam weapons stat on your ship you can still defend with an unmodified opposed roll. If you do have beam weapons, however, can you fire defensive beams as a defense against a beam attack (I know you can against torpedoes)? This seems a little unclear to me.

2. Social Combat: Characters may not use the same skill twice in a row - does this include skills used in defensive actions as well as across turns? (page 179) Basically, I'm asking what does "in a row" mean.

3. Platoon Combat: If a unit separated from its Platoon takes a consequence and later recombines with a Platoon, is that consequence erased and effectively healed?

4. Platoon Combat: Since Infantry don't have a movement stat, do you still roll a movement roll (with 0 modifier) to attempt to move past a boundary such as a low wall? Or, can the infantry not move past boundaries?

Thanks for the help in advance, I appreciate it.

    \n 1. Defensive beams only help out vs. torpedoes - the only effect they have on the beam phase is determine how much capacity you can use without gaining heat. You always get a defense roll against beams, but the stunt is the only thing that alters it.

    2. I believe this is mean to be you can't "attack" with the same skill twice in a row.

    I haven't needed the platoon combat yet so I haven't read that carefully enough to answer 3 and 4.

    \n drnuncheon pegged 1.

    For 2 we actually intend that you can't use the same skill twice in a row regardless of context. This makes your skills a kind of resource (if you use your best skill defensively, you won't be able to use it offensively).

    For 3 no, the platoon now has the consequence.

    For 4 yes, roll against zero.

    And please feel free to ask away about anything! You may find a more focused group of Diaspora players in the Diaspora mailing list, the FATE mailing list, or the RPGGeek.com Diaspora forums. In the forums there is of course also a record of questions previously asked, which can be handy.
