    \n If you'd like just some morale boosting rather than specific advice, the idea seems solid, in that there's dozens of Old School fantasy RPGs out there, but I can't think of an Old School RPG with SF color - most SF games aren't about the delving and looting.

    Hopefully constructive criticism:

    Maybe reduce scope - any game other than D&D is hard pressed to get people to play it for more than a one-shot - that's why I haven't played Dogs or AW or Burning Wheel yet, because they'd require me to get some people to make a commitment. So making a game where the characters can go up to level 20 (which I assume will take at least 20 and maybe 40 sessions!) sounds like hubris.

    The lack of historical basis is partially solved by your cat-men, bug-men, lizard-men alien types - I think if I played one of those races or encountered them on a derelict I'd know what they're like without even having to ask. (Contrast with Mass Effect, where there's a bunch of different aliens they pulled out of their ass and I have almost no idea which are the smart ones, the tough ones, the fast ones, the psionic ones. 'Course ME was a huge success, so what do I know?) I think you can take this further - maybe get rid of the random alien generation (random content almost always ends up feeling like it has a certain sameness...they're all different, but somehow they're all the same), and go for tropes (or less charitably, cliches): the Greys from X-Files; little green men; klingons and vulcans; cthuloid or gigeresque; humanoids with the big foreheads and the pulsing veins for psionic; all with serial numbers filed off.

    All that said, I'm not into Old School these days, so it's not really in my wheelhouse. (Whatever that means.) If you wanted to make the derelict raid game I would be more likely to play, you could either - go real board gamey (Warhammer Quest, Space Hulk - I'm looking forward to these D&D board games coming out like Ravenloft) or go more hippie (you all make the map of the derelict *together*, and all the players have contradictory goals, and the rules fit on a single sheet of paper or two...) And you have to make it a one-shot.
