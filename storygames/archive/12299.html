\n Alright! My game, Gun Thief, is now officially available in hard copy!

BACK COVER BLURB

Bank robberies, deals gone sideways, crooked dames, and the ugly smell of kerosene as it all burns down. The hollow sound when a lead pipe connects with a thick skull. Get-aways, evenings thrown away on a new piece of tail. Everything raw, everything smokey, everything halfway to hell before it even realizes it.

You\u2019ve only ever known two things: how to hold a gun steady, and how to take what ain\u2019t yours. Ain\u2019t no decent men in this world. Ain\u2019t no decent women, either. Still, you\u2019re going to make it through this shitstorm. You\u2019re a gun thief.

Gun Thief is a game for 3 players, but can accomodate up to 4.
It is diceless, gives each player a unique role, and is easy to learn.

Gun Thief involves violence, and probably sex. It is not a game for children or easily discomforted adults.

The print + pdf combo is $5 (plus the cost of shipping).
The print version is sized and printed like a comic book, and runs 12 pages including character sheet.
The pdf version is optimized for the screen (letter-sized pages, landscape) and runs 9 pages total.\n

    \n When I mentioned on Twitter that the hard copies had arrived, Daniel M. Perez demanded pics.

    I can only assume that he meant that he wanted me to dress up like a Gun Thief, have my girlfriend dress up like a Jagged Woman, and then play out a live action scene of Gun Thief in my own backyard. Assuming this is what you wanted: Daniel, eat your heart out.

    Alright, and The Law frames me into a scene: I'm trying to break into some half-finished home on the outskirts of town, 'cuz Ricky's holed up in there, and he owes me a shit ton of money. So, right, I'm bein' cautious, making sure not to walk right into the hands of Ricky's goons.



    And then it's time for The Jagged Women to add a Twist. Jenna, an old flame who I never quite let go, she's with Ricky now. And she tries to play mother lion, and take me out, but all what ends up happenin' is that I've got her on the ground with the barrel of my gun up against her throat (She's trying to take something from you; She's endangering herself).



    Alright, and now it's time for me to make the hard choice. Looking over my options, I choose to keep all my items, get out unharmed, and get what I came for. And Jenna's in the way of that, which means Jenna better be ready to die now.

    Posted By: hans ottersonJoe & his games & how fucking fast he turns them out
    \nFast turnaround ain't necessarily a good thing.

    Though, looking back, my turn-around times:

    Perfect: 4 months
    Ribbon Drive: 11 months
    Gun Thief: 2-3 months

    Heart of Ashes: 13 months and counting
    Perfect, Unrevised: 3+ years and counting


    I feel like the reason that Ribbon Drive and Gun Thief had such short development cycles is that they're both short, diceless & simple.
    I'm happier with Gun Thief as a product, at the moment, because it's more accessible.
    While Ribbon Drive is hella pretty in its $30 box, it also does itself a huge disservice by being so pricey & bulky.

    That lesson on accessibility is something I wanted to bring to Gun Thief: $3 for the pdf, $5 for everything.
    Simple, concrete, no props required. All you need is the character sheet (with all essential reference material printed right on it).
