\n As you might have heard, The Fiasco Companion is coming out soon - next month even! However, we had way more excellent material than we could reasonably fit into the book, so we'll be releasing some of it as free bonus material. First up is the Mission to Mercury Technical Data + Bonus Material PDF, available for download from the Bully Pulpit site.

In this detail-packed PDF you\u2019ll find pre-defined characters, technical information, some nice jargon, and lots of good inspiration. This document lays out, in the very broadest terms, who is going to be on Mercury, what equipment is around, what the habitat is like, and what the various missions are. All of this is intended to provide a gritty, highly constrained backdrop for your session.

This PDF will give you a good taste for the full Playset and show you how it strays from the usual model- where most Fiasco Playsets are broad and open to interpretation, this one experiments with constraints, giving you a very tight situation and turning up the pressure on your story. We hope you like it!

    \n Mission to Mercury is really neat. Actually, the entire Fiasco Companion is really neat.


    My favorite idea in there is the "Cross-Town Rivals" setup, where you have two Fiasco games going simultaneously, using the same setting. Most of the time, that's just background color, but if you happen to have a high speed police chase through downtown or something, you let the other table know so that they can use that in their own story. Perhaps their team of small time insurance fraudsters get stuck in traffic because of the police chase, and miss their big meeting, or something. Then later on, when they dump a body over the side of a bridge, they let you know so that it can fall down on top of your guy as he holds his ex-girlfriend at gunpoint.


    You could have it set up so that each table has a different playset, but the sets are similar enough conceptually that they might work in the same setting. Like using Town and Gown and Alma Monster, or using Dragon Slayers along with Dwarf Fortress or Keeping on the Borderlands.

    Bully Pulpit Games Announces Pre-order of the Fiasco Companion
    Supplement to the popular game Fiasco now available for pre-release purchase

    Bully Pulpit Games is happy to announce the upcoming release of the Fiasco Companion, written by Jason Morningstar and Steve Segedy. Pre-orders for the book begin June 1st. The book will be released on June 22nd and available at the Origins Game Fair.

    The Fiasco Companion is a supplement for the award-winning game Fiasco, a game inspired by cinematic tales of small time capers gone disastrously wrong. Fiasco won the 2009 Ennie Judge\u2019s Spotlight Award and has been nominated for the 2010 Origins Award for Best RPG.

    Fiasco is particularly known for its Playset mechanic, a list of lists that provide setting and plot material to spark the imaginations of the players and start the game with a bang. Bully Pulpit Games welcomes fan-made Playsets, and to date over fifty Playsets have been written, covering a wide variety of genres and situations.

    The long-anticipated Fiasco Companion includes in-depth discussion of common pitfalls and solid techniques for making Fiasco games excellent as well as advice for writing your own Playsets and hacking the rules. In addition, the Companion features exciting rules variants, new Tilt and Aftermath tables and four new Playsets. The book also features a foreword by Wil Wheaton and interviews with an enthusiastic and growing cohort who are already taking the game into the classroom, the writer\u2019s room, and up on stage.

    \u201cWe are especially excited about the release of the Companion because we\u2019ve been working closely with our friends at Janus Design to publish the Italian translation simultaneously with the English version.\u201d said Segedy.

    To purchase the pre-order of the Fiasco Companion see http://www.bullypulpitgames.com/store

    For further information about the Italian release, see the Janus Design website at http://janus-design.it/

    Posted By: Alex FAs a newcomer to Fiasco, would you say there are any reasons not to play, eg Mission to Mercury first time in?
    \nIf everyone is on fire for it, I don't see why not. It isn't what I'd recommend, because it's a worked example of using some advanced techniques like playing with time and using a tightly focused cast and setting. But if everyone at the table is dying to give it a go, jump in with both spacesuit-clad feet.

    What I would recommend for first time play: The one closest to your real lives.
