    \n Hi Tony,
    \n
    \nA couple of the big ones for our computer games were really big. The Pantheons Online map worked out to me 175" wide in Photoshop, so at full size it was truly enormous. The 1483 Full World map was almost as large (Large enough to support 95 player kingdom/empires).
    \n
    \nI plan to do something similar for our shared world environment for the Dark Realms RPG. Going to put up a massive map at like 200 feet by 200 feet, though since that will be for a RPG world setting, it'll have a mixture of topography, roads, cities, towns, ruins, national boundaries, etc, rather than the territories and such that our board games use. Then I'll image map the thing so each region will lead to different web site branches for development at a more detailed level. I think it would be pretty cool to have massive maps like that to display at the kingdom and city levels as well, allowing for ultra detailed development. With luck I hope it will become an awe inspiring world development effort that we can get a lot of folks involved in.

    Posted By: tony dowlerBill: Tell me more about that game and how the map contributes to it. And do the people in Hub City look down on Manhattanites?
    \nTony -- The map is mainly set-dressing, to set the stage for the opening scene of the adventure, a rocket race at the Raritanic Naval Arsenal. My brother Mel devised it -- he's run the adventure several times -- and I like it because it implies that Hub City is the pre-eminent city on the Atlantic seaboard: the scattered and squabbling boroughs at the southern tip of New York are peripheral and provincial. "Manhattan City? Darling, why on earth would you want to go there?"

    Mel has used the map to set up little conundrums for his players. "You wake up in your Brooklyn City apartment--uh-oh, you slept through your alarm. Today's the day of the rocket-plane race and you're the pilot! How in the world are you going to get to Hub City on time?"
