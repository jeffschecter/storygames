    Graham, have a look at these pictures and note how vulnerable to damage the bike is. Basically, everything's out in the open. What's more, the bike is a fine-handling piece of machinery that goes all wonky when you fuck with things that affect its handling capabilities (eg suspension or tyres). Even minor accidents can be a real pain. I once dropped a bike and snapped off my clutch lever. Luckily, there was enough left to ride using two fingers, but if I'd snapped off any more the bike would have been unrideable--after a fall at about 10kph.

    Some other things that can go wrong with a motorcycle:

    Cracked head gasket (terrible mileage and horsepower)
    Dented radiator (overheats eventually)
    Busted radiator (squirts steam at the rider, overheats in a couple of minutes)
    Flat tire, duh! (rotten handling, terrible mileage, destroys wheels in under a mile)
    Fuel leak ("Hey, didn't I just fill up? I thought I... FIre! AH!")
    Missing seat (How long can you stand in a crouch?)
    Bent drive shaft (Man, I can only imagine how badly that would shake...)
    Busted battery (Everything's cool, but you can't restart the bike)
    Broken lights (Can't see at night!)

    To make it cooler? Rockets.
