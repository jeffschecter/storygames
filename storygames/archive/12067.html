    \n Hah, that's the spirit! We're not really in any particular hurry here, it's just the booth space reservation that runs out next week.

    Still, Spiel is a great convention - I recommend it to any gamers within the Schengen zone. Europe is becoming smaller all the time; even if my generation isn't used to idle travel over the continent, perhaps it's time for a change? The 2000 kilometers by open road from Finland to Germany mean a 50-times larger gaming convention compared to what we have here, I should think that's a worthwhile trip to take now and then. And I live pretty much in the periphery of this whole European Union deal, so anybody down there on the other side of the Baltic Sea should have all the more reason to go!

    I'm sure that some American will soon come here to tell us that bah, 2000 kilometers is nothing, that's how far they had to travel to go to school each day, one way.

    \n Well, theoretically Spiel should be roughly as feasible for Americans as Gencon is for us coming from this side. In practice I expect sales per unit of marketing to be somewhat lower at the Spiel - more non-roleplaying audience, less networking sales, language-issues and so on; you're mostly selling to people who have no idea about the indie culture, or even no idea about roleplaying. The audience pool is much larger, though, and this should be a growth market for anything that is new and fresh for the Spiel. I'm hoping that Zombie Cinema will fare well in this environment, boardgame-like as it is. I also wouldn't be surprised if the Spiel could become bigger for an indie designer than Gencon is with a couple of years of preparatory work; the potential is there in terms of audience and community, even if most of that community has English as second language.

    And of course, however the sales go it's not a bad trip as a tourism experience.
