So I played in this game last night. Our characters in Kaiju-Chan trainers (think pokemon) in New Toyko and we've come in from the states to find a school that will train us after our old master (in New York).

All the players are spread out all over the country. We're connected via Skype and with shared-desktop software.

The GM had this *incredible* flash animation of the challenge-boards with the different schools, trainers, etc. You could click on an icon and see the various matchups. The graphical design was incredible.

There were also subway maps overlaid onto orbital shots of the city sort of cyberpunked up with control-zones for each school (the Oda school is going around conquering the lesser ones).

Anyway, I was just blown away. The interactive visuals gave the whole situation a feel to it that I've never seen before. It really amped the energy up and gave the whole thing an ... electric ... flavor.

I just can't say enough good about it!

-Marco
