    \n Systemwise they're all pretty much completely different...essentially the same Potter's Lake setting grafted on to a succession of different house systems. Interestingly, they all seem to take a different approach to the core ideas of the setting...although its been to long for me to effectively give you a blow by blow compare and contrast.
    \n
    \nI remember thinking that the first version was the least complete in terms of available detail, but the most compelling in terms of creepy content and Twin Peaks meets the X-files vibe. Later versions seemed to scrap some of the creepy surrealism in exchange for more but less compelling detail. At this point I honestly couldn't tell you whether those are legitimate statements or just half remembered first impressions...but there you are.

    \n "Noumenon" has wicked style, but I have had no luck finding anyone else weird as me to play it with here locally. It is a bit like a spiritual puzzle box or a koan-as-RPG.

    "Aletheia" is a very cool look at an X-Files like research group that studies odd happenings, but everything is tied to an overarching mythos. The mythos is made for you, so not as much wiggle-room as I like. A bit like a prefab version of a long and cool Esoterrorrist game, with a bit less insanity and more crytorealism. (Does that even make sense?)

    "Exquisite Replicas" I just snagged, but have heard very good things about it.
