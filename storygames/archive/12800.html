\n Some games seem to be designed for telling a contained story within a one-shot (or perhaps through two or three sessions).

But there will be a bit in the text about joining a bunch of stories together for long-term play.

I am specifically thinking of :

-Hero's Banner, in which each session will lead to the protagonist's death. Te next session would be about a new generation of heroes;

-Dirty Secrets, where you could technically tell new crime stories with a recurring investigator;

-Shock: Social Science-Fiction, where the grid has room for new Shocks, so you could keep exploring the game universe, with the same protagonists but a new Shock and new issues;

-A Thousand and One Night, in which courtiers who survived and didn't ained their freedoms can keep on telling each oter stories.

I'm curious to know if anyone here actually tried long-term play with any of these games.

    \n Hello,

    We ran a campaign of Dirty Secrets with a recurring investigator. We ran a Novella, a Short Story and a Novel back to back. We ended up also having a recurring "NPC" more or less by accident. We liked the effect, a lot.

    We pod casted about it here: http://apap.libsyn.com/ beginning at Episode 25.

    I've also run a 9 Town run of Dogs in the Vineyard but I'm fond of saying that I should have stopped at Town 5.

    Oh! And from your list we also did three sessions of Shock in the same universe where the Shock was Robots. The neat effect there was that one character who was an Antagonist in one session was used as Protagonist in another session. Shock is great over multiple sessions.
