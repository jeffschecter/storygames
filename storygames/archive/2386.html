    Hi Joe,

    I'm not the Monkey King but that looks right to me, except that Failure Ranks are temporary (minor scrapes & contusions, embarrassment, discouragement, etc.) and Damage Ranks are more lasting (typically, physical injury--brain-searing Encounters with Gibbering Masses from Beyond might be Damage Ranks too).

    It looks as if, as written, Powers and Abilities are technically always Complicated Situations, since their descriptions include the text ". . . with a successful roll against Type Rank . . . " Second Sight is the only exception: it can be used passively.

    An ambiguous area I can think of is using a Power or Ability in a Conflict Situation. Does that require an activation roll, and then rolling the Power/Ability against some defending Quality, Power, or Ability? Reading a bit, it looks like no--normally you're always rolling against a GM-set difficulty.

    --JB

    Posted By: joepubDoes one spend the Soul Points for a Power before or after their Type Rank roll? If they fail their Type Rank roll, are the resources just... wasted?
    Personally, I think you roll after, and if you fail, no points are spent. But I believe in a forgiving Cosmos.

    You could have a bleaker Cosmos if you had to spend points even if you failed.

    You could have a medium bleak Cosmos by mixing and matching the two... and depending on how you do it, you could end up with interesting flavors. Compare a Cosmos containing a Spirit World where failure isn't penalized, but a Real World where it is, to a Cosomos with a Spirit World where failure is penalized, but a Real World where it is not.
