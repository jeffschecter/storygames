\n Hi,

We played another RBH game, this time with 4 players and me as a GM.

The players and their PCs :

Marie, my wife, played : Louise, Magus Level 2 (Enchant, Puppet String), Light Armor (Leather), Reach Weapon (Spear), Ranged Weapon (Throwing Knives).

Jean-Yves, a friend, played : Sven, Snake Level 1 (Yari Master), Very Heavy Armor (second skin plate armor), Reach Weapon (Scythe), Light Weapon (Small billhook).

Dounia, a friend and Jean-Yves wife, played : Jos\xe9phine, Longrunner Level 1 (Two things at once), Light Armor (Leather), Ranged Weapon (Bow), Light Weapon (Short Sword).

Guillaume, a friend, played : Charles, Fox Level 1 (Three Tails), Light Armor (Leather), Ranged Weapon (Bow), Light Weapon (Scimitar).

------------------------
To start the game, I described to the players their characters arriving at a village on an ocean coast. Then I told them, they've heard of a legendary treasure hidden in an undersea cave under a island just a few miles in the sea : the Crown of Poseidon. The cave appears during the great spring equinox tide that should take place in a few days.

In the village, Garlic (I've asked the players for an irish sounding name for the place... remember we are french...), the PCs find out that the young boys are taken away from the village for a few days and that the virgin girls are kept drugged in a cell. Every year, during the spring equinox night, the virgins of the village are sent to the Sons of the Goat God to ensure good crops for the next year. Their first child becomes then another son of the Goat God and is sent to the woods. That's why the village has turned away from the sea. Even if a lot of the people are descended from the ancient god Poseidon.



Instead of choosing between the treasure or killing the monsters, because the revel with the Goat God's Sons and the opening of the cave took place on the same night, the players decided to abduct the girls, take them to the treasure island and wait there for the goat monsters to fight them. See the doodles...

We had a hell of a fight where two of the PCs went down (no one died though) and the last two finished the two bosses with some clever Assist action.

After that they fought the monster garding the treasure (an giant octopus) and took the crown of Poseidon with them.

I think I overpowered my monsters a little in this game. I'll put them in the monsters thread as soon as I can. But overall, it was another great game of the RBH. Three of the players were new to the game and they loved it instantly : the classes, the talents, the awesome tokens system. The Assist action was the star of the game. They didn't use it at the beginning of the first fight and 2 PCs went down. The last 2 survived because they made great use of it. When they leveled up one player chose to cross-class and take the warrior talent of "Strategist". It was put to great use in the last fight.

I wanted to do some doodles, but instead I made some bad sketches (and I couldn't help but use some colors...). I'll do better next time, Eric.







Best,

Antoine

    \n Hi guys,

    Sorry to be so long to reply. Real life's busy these days...

    Thank you all for the compliments. It's heartwarming. And thanks Guillaume for the nice comments on the game. I realy enjoyed myself and it was nice to play another time with you after perhaps 3 years... Like in the good old days.

    About the monsters... I think I got my english wrong when I said I "overpowered" them. I meant that I had given them too many powers, making them too strong. It was the first time I had this many PCs at the table and I wanted my monsters to be real threats. So far I've had trouble making the monsters powerfull enough. I wanted them to be able to bring at least one PC at 0 HP. And I never cheat on the dice. But in game, I realised that if I played them fully, I would kill all the PCs in a few rounds. After 2 rounds, I decided not to use at least one of the powers. I'll check that, when I'll put them in the monsters gallery.

    Also, the PCs could survive because, against the last monster, the longrunner flew with the beast following her while the fox was doing Assist actions from the sky. When she was charged enough with tokens, she turned and killed it in one shot. But I made a mistake. My monster had a free move action and so could make 2 moves to catch the longrunner. I said to her that with the "two things at once" talent she could make 2 move actions too and so she could keep her distance. I remembered only after the game, that it was only 1 move action + another action. So she could not have done that. I realy think I've made my monsters too powerfull...

    Still, it was a great game and I can't wait to play again.
    Now, all characters are at least 2nd level and one is 3rd level. They are a real threat !

    Regarding the drawings. Don't be ashamed of your stick figures. It's what Eric realy asked for and I did some too. It's realy fun. It's just that I love to draw and I don't have that much time to do it those days. So when I do some visual actual play, it's realy tempting to do more than stick heroes... I realy had to stop myself before I made a real comics page with this game account. I decided to stop and post my sketches as they were. So : post your doodles !! Gambate !

    Best,
    Antoine
