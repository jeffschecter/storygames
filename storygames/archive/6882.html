\n So I'm finishing up the playtest doc for Tokyo Rain, and I've got two issues that are bothering me. One is a little meatier, but the other is an annoying terminology issue. Help please.

Tokyo Rain is a competitive, two-player RPG. One player plays the predator; the other player plays the prey. The game is played over a series of challenges. In each challenge, the predator's success is ultimatley assured. The prey's goal is to build up a stockpile of Glitches. In the final challenge, the prey uses these Glitches as a significant resource to balance the otherwise imbalanced sides. In each indvidual challenge, the players compete in phases. The Prey can spend his net successes on "Potential Glitches". At the end of that challenge, he rolls his "Potential Glitches" against the Predator's Psyche to determine how many Glitches he ends up with for his stockpile.

The problem is, obviously, the term "Potential Glitches" is: (a) too long, (b) too similar to Glitches, and (c) too boring.

Example: Rain is facing off against three of Belghazi's henchmen. Over the course of the challenge, the Prey (playing Belghazi) nets 3 Potential Glitches. These might have been narrated as, e.g., an injury to Rain, a henchmen getting away, a piece of evidence left behind. At the close of the challenge, the Prey rolls his 3 Potential Glitches against Rain's Psyche and nets 1 Glitch. This will be used in the final challenge, e.g., as Belghazi exploiting the injury Rain suffered in that earlier challenge.

So, what else can I call them? Alternatively, I am open to calling the "Potential Glitches" just "Glitches" and calling the second-order mechanic something else that's "above" Glitches.

    Posted By: Jonathan WaltonSo what not have them all be Glitches and you just roll to see whether the Glitches continue to bother you long-term, after a given scene.
    That's theoretically possible, but it becomes confusing over the course of several challenges. For example:

    In the first challenge, the prey gets 3 potential glitches and ends up with 1 glitch. In the next challenge, he gets 2 potential glitches and ends up with 1 more glitch, for a total of 2 glitches. In the next challenge, he gets 3 potential glitches and ends up with 2 more glitches, for a new total of 4 glitches.

    As you can see that the potential glitches are only relevant within the challenge, but the glitches are tracked over the course of the entire game. If I used the same term for both, it would get very confusing. (Which is also why I don't want to use Potential Glitches and Glitches -- for fear of confusion.)

    Thanks for the suggestions so far. It might be helpful to think of them as "Potential Complications" and "Complications", since that what they represent. (I originally used "Complications" but felt it was a bit of a mouthful.)

    "Okay, Chad, how many tags do you have?"

    "Okay, roll your tags to earn some Glitches."

    The bonus of using "tags" is that if there are any similar "translate potentials into actuals" mechanics in the game, they can also use "tags".

    "How much Friction do you have so far?"
