\n These days, I have less and less time to read roleplaying games. However, one time I do allocate to reading is during trips to the loo. At such times, I frequently look around for a book I can read.

Which RPGs are good for this purpose? It requires short extracts of text, which can be read at a sitting. Thus, Fiasco doesn't work, because the text sections are long, but a Fiasco playset is just the right length. Similarly, an Apocalypse World playset is excellent.

One excellent RPG to read on the loo is Mars Colony. While visiting Vincent Baker's facilities, I greatly enjoyed reading this game. Its small size makes it easy to hold while one is engaged. Sadly, I did subsequently drop it down the loo, but I genuinely did enjoy reading it.

Please feel free to recommend either your own game or games you have read yourself. If you have designed your roleplaying game with this purpose in mind, I am particularly interested in hearing from you.

(Note: Despite the title of this thread, I'd appreciate it if you avoided scatological humour. In that vein, please avoid describing RPGs which you consider suitable for hygiene purposes once one has finished one's visit to the loo. Thanks!)

    \n I've personally been reading through the Delta Green corpus lately. It's a second read-through for most of the books. The text is quite dense, but if you're skilled in reading, it's not difficult to make progress in short sittings. The overall literary quality and the fact that the books are almost entirely fluff of various sorts makes them quite entertaining as idle reading. This, in my opinion, makes for better casual reading than many rpg texts that concern themselves with procedures and game mechanics.
