So, a couple months ago, when Mark asked me if I was going to Dreamation, I casually said no. Somehow it didn't occur to me that there'd be a rockin' amout of indie game shit going down and that I'd be seriously in the mood for that scene come the end of January.

\n

So, to those of you that have been to Dreamation before, what am I missing? Should I make plans for going next year? Is it really that awesome? Tell me all about it.

    \n Dreamation is, bar none, the best place to play indie games (that I've experienced). It's positioned at a good time in the year for a ton of east coast small-press designers to make it, so there's a lot of direct access between fans and designers going on. The IPR booth is usually positioned right outside the play area, so it ends up being a focusing, social hub for a lot of that interest-segment. The convention staff are WILDLY supportive of the indie games scene.

    DexCon, its twin-cousin, comes later in the year, sandwiched right between Origins and GenCon. This makes it less ideal for a lot of people, so the designer-attendance tends to be lower, but it's still a good second option to pursue.

    There are four conventions I won't miss in a year if I can help it: Dreamation, Origins, DexCon, and GenCon. So take that for what you will.

    \n After hearing peoples' experiences with Dreamation, I've pretty much decided that that place is the Place to Be next year, even over GenCon. GC is a big release/selling/buying con with a good deal of play. Dreamation sounds like it's 100% high powered play, no selling/buying or other stuff to get in the way. I'm going to GC this year, and I'm envious of folks who are going to Dreamation and not GC.

    Yeah, I usually come away with a modest profit. But since the investment is so much smaller than GC, the selling doesn't need to take such center stage.

    \n

    What does take center stage is the frenzy of play. The Indie Games Explosion run by Kat Miller in the past and Emily Care Boss and Robert Bohl this year is an ever-increasing thing. Vinny et al. have made us extremely welcome, offering food and lodging and scheduling us as pretty much the central event of the con. Last year we were seriously overbooked and judging from my own registration numbers this year, that might happen again despite our best efforts. IGE was second only to a D&D event last year, if I recall (though that was a bit of a weird thing of Vinny to say \u2014 we've got dozens of games and D&D is only one...). We wound up taking extra rooms outside of the normal RPG spaces to fit everyone.

    \n

    And really, it's the "everyone" that's the best part. I've never been around such an enthusiastic, friendly, and odd cast of characters anywhere else in RPGdom. That's where I met Judd, Rob Bohl, Clinton, Mike and Kat Miller, Tony LB, Keith Senflowski, Jenn Rodgers, Luke Crane, and a bunch of other people central to my publishing universe all the first year I was there. I don't know who else is going to be central to my publishing universe by next year, but I think there's a good chance they'll be at Dreamation.

    \n

    I also had one of the best games I've ever played there, a late-beta of The Mountain Witch that hooked me up with Tim Kleinert and eventually had us producing two books together. How could I have forgotten that? I'm probably forgetting lots of other interesting people and thrilling events. It's just a big deal in a little place, you know?
