"Avalanche is a unique and crazily ambitious high-fantasy setting. Imagine the scope and ambition of the Dragonlance campaign, except no rules-system, no plot railroad, and the material has all been arranged into a reference system consisting of time-lines, dramatis personae and geographical atlases, so as to make it feasible for the GM to handle the high density of information. Very unique due to the novel timeline-oriented campaign arc, well worth the time for anybody interested in high-fantasy adventure rpgs."

    \n An update. I think there are lessons to be learned here.

    We're going to let the KS campaign run until at least half of his 'life' (21 days). We're going to give a little fight here. Anyway, I think it serves us well at the moment. Analytics are just plain bad. We've stopped all FB ads. Banners are still going on BGG, as they are paid for, but still no conversion.

    But I'm still using FB. Yesterday, we have decided to distribute for free the 'unedited version' of Avalanche. The text needs some solid work (Cal Moore is doing an excellent work for us), but all the illustrations are there. It also needs a good introduction, some guidelines. We're distributing the link for the pdf by email (so I've got those, at least) after a request on FB. We have distributed around 40 copies. I'm quite happy about it. We made the announcement in an post and use the 'promote function'. I do find it to be cheaper and more efficient than ads (that is not hard to beat). Also, a way to try to use my 1400 fans on FB. I don't expect all of them to play it, but I could feel some excitement.

    I should have been doing this a long time ago, before the start of the campaign. That said I only had the 'unedited' version about a month ago.
    Maybe a very important point here: the KickStarter really served us as a big motivation to 'complete' the project and turn it into a product. Now, we have this product, it still needs some edition, but I'm pretty sure I can sell it. It will take a long time and it will be a hobby and it's all fine. Surely I won't raise enough money for a website, but it is thanks to KS that I'm doing, what I think is the right thing.

    \nIt might be worth not including the illustrations, or replacing them with big placeholder blocks. Shiny, complete illustrations make the text look more done than it is. You don't want interested-but-doubtful people to see the unedited preview, assume it's complete, and be turned off by its incompleteness. Even if they "know" it's incomplete, if it looks polished enough, their instinct will treat it as if it's complete.

    \n I would add that you might want to find one community, and focus on making the game for that. In most of the systems you mentioned, to a greater or lesser extent, there's a need to stat out encounters before hand. That's often the least fun aspect of any of these games. If you made something that was an awesome Pathfinder or 13th Age or whatever multi-threaded campaign, that was just ready to run from the word go (rather than kind of still needing a large amount of prep week to week), you might find an audience there, and then other groups/systems would be interested in having the system ported.

    I think keeping a kickstarter up when you're not updating, nor have a shot of success, is probably a pretty bad idea: it'll draw attention towards it, and reinforce the failure. It's also, for people who aren't following this thread, potentially making backers feel like they took a bad shot. I'd at least keep them in the loop as to what's going on with updates. The people who were willing to pay money are your audience and people worth talking to much more so than the people in this thread are.
