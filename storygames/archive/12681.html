\n I'm taking pre-orders for the Rustbelt first edition starting now. Hit up my crude store here for details and the PayPal button.
\n
\nIt costs $24 plus $2.77 shipping. It's 164 half-letter pages long (it's roughly the size of DitV, if you, like me, have no sense of what page numbers actually mean). Buying this preorder gets you the interior PDF now (just the print file, not designed for screen) -- or at least as fast as I can email it to you -- the book once they're printed, and a homemade bonus CD of Rustbelt-themed music just to say thank you for pre-ordering.
\n
\nThe following is the back cover text:
\n
\n
Life here is nasty, brutish, and short. The Rust slowly eats everything away. It wears a man down. Corrodes him. Changes him. Makes him do things he wouldn\u2019t think himself capable of. If you had to, wouldn\u2019t you?
\n
\nIt\u2019s a hard world, and you gotta pay for what you want. Sometimes the price is so hideous it makes you think twice, but it might be your only chance. The Rust whispers in your ear, \u201cC\u2019mon, do the math.\u201d
\n
\nSo, how much are you willing to pay? What are you willing to do? What are you willing to become?
\n
\nYou gotta go a long way in the \u2018Belt to find a good man.
\n
\nTHE RUSTBELT is a post-apocalyptic roleplaying game featuring:
\n- The Blood, Sweat, & Tears Engine: a flexible and easy-to-use resolution system in which your resolve matters more than your abilities.
\n- The Psyche System: personality mechanics that keep the characters psychologically and emotionally realistic and compelling \u2013 no matter how inhuman they turn out to be \u2013 without taking away the player\u2019s control.
\n- Player-authored stories: your characters will be pushed against the wall until they\u2019ve revealed all their most monstrous and most admirable qualities, but the decisions are always yours to make, and the prices yours to pay. The end result is a gritty, desperate tale in the vein of The Stand, The Road, or Mad Max.
\n- Customization support: full guidelines for adapting the game to other settings, as well as three examples to get you started.
\n
\n"THE RUSTBELT is lean and mean. The laissez-faire, non-balanced character creation is excellent and totally appropriate; with the right Psyche traits, a one-legged stable boy could be the most dangerous guy in town. Fights are desperate, horrible affairs. I look forward to playing again.\u201d
\n- Ron Edwards, designer of Sorcerer, Spione, and S/Lay w/Me
\n
\n\u201cThe Psyche System is the most elegant model for human behavior I\u2019ve ever seen in an RPG. And I have a master\u2019s degree in psych.\u201d
\n- Jonathan Davis
\n
\n\u201cPsyche did a really good job of fleshing out all the characters even during chargen. One character started off as \u2018the Hulk as a mercenary in a Western\u2019 and turned into an immigrant who tries to buy acceptance, without sacrificing any of the player\u2019s original concept.\u201d
\n- Josh W
\n
\n
\n...In closing, lemme state that I sincerely do need the money. Also, the game is awesome.
\n
\nThanks!
\n-Marshall

    Matthijs,

Prep-wise, it's on par with Dogs or Sorcerer (the prep procedures are more like Sorcerer though -- i.e. more wide open, not so much steb-by-step). I've managed to successfully run the game immediately following a 30-minute prep session with one first-timer and another that had only played it once.
