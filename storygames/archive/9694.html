    \n 70 or so people, John.

    The thing I noticed about organization was that it was very centralized and efficient. Ping and I talked; it sounds like she and Tony balanced each other out very well and managed a very efficient middle-of-the-road style of event, organized without being draconian, flexible without being sloppy. As a participant it was completely trouble free - the information I needed was right there, an organizer was always nearby to help out, the event flowed nicely and I didn't have to think about anything but having fun. For me it was very chill - the few times I offered to help out, there wasn't that much to actually do. I suspect the prep work must have been epic, though.

    There was a sense of inclusiveness that I've never seen before but will definitely be emulating - where Nerdly is "you're responsible for bringing your own fun", GPNW was "no gamer will be left behind." No value judgment, because both work, but they work differently.

    \n Tell me more (anyone) about the inclusiveness. This is huge for me.

    I was thinking of doing thing following at the next Recess event:

    1. Having the game organizers pitch their games in 1 minute to everyone before on site registration begins (which would also mean no off site registration). Alternatively we could have a spokesperson speak for all the organizers and simply point to each organizer as they announce their game.

    2. Doing this twice for a 1 day event or once a day for a multiple day event.

    3. Having the con organizers introduce themselves and given them organizer badges.

    4. Have some sort of give away contest where we can put the spotlight on guests.

    5. Asking game organizers to introduce themselves and have their players do the same.

    6. Assigning someone to look for people who are not in games and helping them get into games.

    7. Group photos.

    8. Thank everyone at the end.

    9. Instead of 100% freeform game signups, have someone available to answer questions during signup.

    As an aside, last Recess we printed out maps of the area with places to eat which seemed to be a big win.
