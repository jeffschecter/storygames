    \n Hey folks. Jason and Graham are right. Thanks, guys. I'm working on a game called Sad & Miserable: The Secret Lives of Stand-Up Comics. Its inspirations are stuff like the FX TV show Louie, the Judd Apatow movie Funny People, and the podcast WTF? with Marc Maron.

    After a very successful playstorm at Dreamation this year, I have a really solid framework to go from. Unfortunately I'm in the middle of a move right now, but I'm going to try to get out with the functional piece and playtest / playstorm at the local cons around here to get the feel for a few things that need to be worked out about it. Meanwhile, I'm going to be doing some research in NYC (where I moved), maybe do a bit of open mic work, etc. But that will be delayed for a bit.

    I'd be happy to answer any questions people might have about the game.

    I'd agree that Fiasco is a great option, and the framework / structure of S&M currently looks similar to Condenders. However, as with almost all things, my first impulse (until S&M is done anyway) would be to play it in Primetime Adventures. (As a matter of fact, I might do that anyway just as an exercise.)

    PS: Thanks for pointing me to the thread, Judd.

    \n It's very early, and that part isn't designed yet, but my plan is to have players build a notebook and structure an act without the requirement to perform it. On a given performance night, you overlay the framework with some randomness around how the crowd reacts, what kinds of things you can predict about them and some you can't.

    \n In the research I've done, I've heard a number of times something like: "There's a very simple formula to stand-up that anyone can follow and make work." To me, that immediately sounded like a game system. If the artistry is in performance, the performance part can be mechanized and, hopefully, I can structure the game so that filling out that formula is easy.
