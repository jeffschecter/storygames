\n I've been mixing up a fast-n-dirty random system to generate situations. Really basic stuff: An antagonist, a motivation and a couple locations. When researching sources, I discovered Georges Polti's Thirty-Six Dramatic Situations. That maps perfectly to a d66 percentile roll, so its ready-made for my purposes as a motivation list (many of the specific plots are archaic, but the basic form can be built on easily). With that taken as my starting point, I decided to build d66 lists for the other elements as well.

I found lots of writer resources for mixing up characters, so building a d66 list of that will also be straightforward. But surprisingly, I haven't found any literary sourcers that addresses location. I mean, at all, I can't even find it addressed as a concept. The closest I've found are some gaming d100 lists, but those are all about incidental scene dressing like "goblin cave" or "alchemist's workshop." So it seems I'll have to come up with it on my own.

My parameters are archetypal, time and genre neutral locations. Rather than "the King's Court," it would be "the Seat of Power." Rather than "The Corporate Headquarters," "The Bastion of Wealth." The goal is that I (and maybe others) could use these table to generate (soft, easily moldable) workable game scenarios on the fly regardless of what context the larger game is taking place in. Also, these should be scalable. An entry should apply equally well to a single building and a whole country.

    \n Rafu, you mentioned a couple concepts I missed, thank you.

    Paul T, I think in most games with any sort of adventurous context, most places can be expected to be inimical.

    Asif, that's an intriguing approach, but more esoteric and subjective than I'm hoping for in this. I'm actually moving away from an older approach wherein I use a playing card deck with Tarot-derived qualities assigned to the different cards; real conceptually airy stuff. I've got a lot of good returns on it, but for this systrem I'm aiming for something more concrete.

    Lula, You're right there should be more of an eye towards human perspective, but as I said to Asif I'm trying to move away from the subjective approach

    Rickard, thanks for pointing out those playsets, they're chock full of ideas.

    I should clatrify, this project is part of building a general light "hamburger" rpg system. Direct meaty quippy action-adventure type stuff, not so much with the drama of the human condition and messy human relationships. So I'm looking more for the building blocks of cool sets, not soil to grow tendrils of emotional attachment.
