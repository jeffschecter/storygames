    \n I'm working on a horror game myself at the mo, so I'll chip in here with my observations.

    The thing about horror is that it can take place pretty much anywhere so long as the rest of the tone is accurate. Juxtaposition is a wonderful thing. So long as the area is abandoned, misty, neglected, filthy, dilapidated, actually falling down at the time, soaked in blood, dripping with slime, plagued by thunderstorms and of course very, very dark, then anywhere will do. You could use the Big Top at the circus - many people are frightened of clowns, as Stephen King's 'IT' demonstrated. You could use an everyday house if you wanted, a lot of great horrors happen in those. Some of the best horrors major not in placing you somewhere unfamiliar where unknown things lurk, but by placing you somewhere uncomfortably familiar and putting a cruel twist on it.

    The important thing is not where you are or what you have on hand, but how you use it. Horror isn't a place, it's an atmosphere.

    But I guess none of this is what you asked for. Has anyone mentioned mysterious circles of standing stones yet? How about dank caverns? A claustrophobic mineshaft?

    -Ash

    \n I don't really get nightmares anymore, but I can recall the doozies--the ones that taught me to awaken myself:

    Speaking before a crowd of people. Stark nekkid.

    Grinding you teeth in your sleep produces quite a shocking dream of losing your teeth by the handful, I've found.

    Can't. Find. All. Of. Them.
    Can't. Hold. All. Of. Them. When. I. Do.
    Must. Try. Again. And. Again.
    (Apparently, this one gets worse when it starts to involve one's children!)

    Being chased. Thinking you've escaped. The horrid truth that, no, you didn't.

    Falling. (I've "hit" the ground and scene-shifted to my own funeral... which was actually a rather pleasant affair.)

    The thing just out of sight--it's in the dark closet, NO! under the bed, NO! RIGHT BEHIND YOU! No... that's just to coat-rack. It's actually RIGHT TH-- *splat*

    \n Oh man I love horror.

    Moldy, crumbling institutions like mental institutions, hospitals, or schools. They are huge and cavernous and you can practically hear old voices echoing through them.

    Abandoned farmhouses. You drive through rural areas and you see them everywhere. When I was a kid I used to explore them. Houses, barns, garages.

    Run down and abandoned carnivals with old merry go rounds and funhouses.

    Basically anything that used to have the breath of life that's since expired is a really good setting. They evoke ghosts.
