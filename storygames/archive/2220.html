    Art needn't communicate. It's just one thing it's really good for. I'm totally with Jenn on this. The reception of a piece of art is an independent process from the creation of one.

    An "artist's statement" doesn't make something art. Of course not. Nor does its status as "art" mean that something's highbrow. That's a bourgeois, consumerist view of art, a view that's sold to people to decrease supply. It's a view that started with the rise of the middle class in the 18th century when aculturation was a way to prove that you really deserved your class mobility. But look, anyone who picks up a banjo and rocks out, or scrapbooks, or gardens, or whistles real good is making art. Making a video blog, a photoshopped picture of your friend's head on Abe Vigoda's body, they're all art. Art is what humans do when we're not starving to death or passively consuming someone else's art.

    Playing games is art. Making games is art.

    Hey, maybe I should flash my accreditation as a Whatisartologist. My dad's a sculptor. My family ate on his art for decades from the sixties into the nineties. He attended and taught at RISD for years, where much of our social circle comes from. I was a lecturer at the Yale School of Art for several years before I started to miss being a designer.

    This isn't to claim that my voice is somehow authoritative. But it is to say that it's been a discussion at every dinner party in my entire life. And I can tell you that narrow definitions of art are horseshit. They're proposed by people who want to make sure that someone else is out of the club. Sometimes pottery's not art because it's "a craft". Sometimes photography's not art because it's mediated by a machine (I know, it's crazy, but that was the argument in the early 20th century). But the most salient features of the art that people actually respect â€” the creation of an artifact (however ephemeral it may be) using the skill of its creators â€” is present in all of them. That's what we care about, isn't it? A phenomenon that reflects the human spirit (possibly through communication, but it could happen otherwise), a mark on the universe that lets us know that someone else was there. Maybe even someone we respect.
