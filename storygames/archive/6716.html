\n Inspired by the ongoing curiosity about D&D 4e, I was able to convince my friends to give 'Keep on the Shadowfell' a try. I've commented on our experience at the latest episode of Virtual Play. I enjoyed the game and I'm glad to have played but, having my curiosity sated, I'm in no rush to pick up playing again--except maybe to get better at the tactics in the game. We definitely had fun--more than some games, less than others--but it didn't 'feel' like D&D. I'm sure it's a perfectly good game--perhaps even 'the' game for fantasy miniatures combat, which I enjoy in measured doses. The trick will be to link the battles with the interests and goals of the characters, which is the key in any game.
Mel

    Posted By: M EryesenJust wondering what you mean by "didn't 'feel' like D&D."
    \nI'm referring mostly to AD&D 1st edition, which I played through high school although I dabbled a little in the later editions. There are two aspects. First, 4E is a brand new game so of course it's going to be different--different rules, different mechanics, different races. It's very likely if the game wasn't called D&D I'd be wishing that D&D had been like 4E at the start. The second aspect, though, is really what I was getting at and that is that I'm not feeling the sense of wonderment and 'fantastic' that I felt when I first started playing AD&D and that I can recognize in Tony's thread on playing with his son. That's the feeling I was hoping for--expecting, maybe--and so I'm a little disappointed that, from the introductory adventure only, I wasn't feeling it. It's not really the rules I don't think. I can see 4E playing a lot like AD&D--emphasis on the combat (at least the way I played it in high school), where the combats were important to the story and everything in between combat (or other conflicts) was achieved through roleplaying. One more point--since the character I most associate with AD&D was a wizard who was required to memorize spells in advance and adapt those spells to unexpected situations, all the while hoarding those spells for when they were really needed, I can't 'see' that wizard in 4E terms even though, as I mention on the podcast, I always wanted to somehow enable him to cast Magic Missile every round!
