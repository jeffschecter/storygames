\n I think that I'm going to go to Spiel Essen in 2009. That thread is about setting up a prospective independent game designer booth there, but the convention certainly has room for all sorts of other activities as well.

And of course, if anybody wants to experience being a booth person, now's a good chance - just like the Gencon Forge booth used to be in the earlier years, this booth is still largely unestablished and needs plenty of workforce that does not have to be actively exhibiting their own stuff. Booth monkeys/ninjas welcome, in other words.

    \n Ah, excellent. I'm thinking that it'd be great if we could be all inclusive with this "Forge booth at Essen" thing, the way Ron manages to be from year to year at Gencon. In that spirit I'd find it most excellent if we had folks like you, MJ - interested independent designers with their first game at the ashcan stage, or perhaps even finished. I'm already looking forward to meeting you and seeing what sort of game you're brewing. In general I'd say that anybody interested in independent game design and publishing should find a place in this, even if they don't have existing familiarity with the games and culture I and other Forge regulars are going to be pushing at the booth. Doubly so when we consider the fledgling status of the Forge community here in Europe - while there are quite many of us, ranging from players to designers, we haven't really done a lot of this sort of border-crossing yet. Time to start, I say, and get to know each other face to face.
    \n
    \nI agree that there is not much RPG activity at Spiel - something like playing full sessions in the convention premises is pretty much out, for example. Last year when visiting Gencon I was pretty impressed by the hotel-based night gaming setup the scene has there, perhaps something similar could be developed to give us some natural venues for roleplaying alongside the convention and especially after-hours. Some suitable restaurant, hotel or other public/semipublic place suitable for a dozen people in 2-3 tables, I'm thinking. Probably not even gaming at first, it's challenge enough to find a quiet place to exchange thoughts and speculate about play. I'm sure people with more Spiel experience and local knowledge have a much better grasp at what is feasible in Essen-the-city and what's not.

Like the saying goes "Build it and they will come..." ;o)
