    To celebrate, my subconscious brought me a dream about gaming. (I don't often have those.)

    I was with a group of people and we were trying to play Synnibarr (!!!).

    There were hilarious problems throughout. First, the text insisted that only "characters of Eastern European origin" were acceptable.

    Second, bizarre and nearly unplayable procedures. The one I remember was having to roll for the type of dice you were going to roll. We followed the procedure at one point and it told us that for this roll we had to use 5d9.

    There was lots of laughter, and we decided to keep trying until we hit one of the moments in play when the game would ask us to integrate a function (some game rules used integration!).

    We eventually did, and, true to our word, stopped playing there and all went home.

    Sadly, I don't remember any of the fictional content of the game.

    To celebrate, I am leaving for "Alphacon", the convention of the leading lights of Finnish roleplaying. It's going to be a weekend of cocky strutting combined with showing off our best gaming talents.

    My own contribution will come in the form of a method of GM journaling, the "gaming portfolio" - I'll give a workshop on how and why to maintain one, and how to use it to talk about gaming in a practical way.

    For gaming I ended up putting all my eggs in the Tales of Entropy basket - for some reason it's been inspiring me again after something of a break; might be because we've been translating my scenarios into English, or because it's interesting to see Petteri discuss his game with Ron at Adept Play.

    My Entropy stratagem relates to my portfolio thing, like so. Some bits are in English, e.g. page 3, which might help make sense of what it is for.

    \n No, it's just an experiment in how Ron's brand of consulting might help with marketing and indie game. It was my idea, actually - I checked out Ron's new site and got the idea that I should bring my mentor and mentee together, if I'm allowed such presumptuous titles [grin]. Both were interested, thinking that something useful might emerge, and we get to follow the process.

    The main theme of the consultation is marketing Entropy better, which is a topic that Petteri's broached here a couple of times as well over the last year. The game's not gaining much traction, and while the reasons are not a great mystery (generic game, little active marketing), Petteri (and I as well) is interested in whether Ron might have any suggestions for improving that. They are, of course, starting by studying the game in depth to figure out what they're marketing.

    I just watched that video (not a common occurrence for me) tonight. Pretty interesting stuff if you're as big into Entropy as I am (which I guess limits the audience just to me and Petteri, but hey...).
