    \n The CreateSpace costs are laid out here. It depends on whether you pay them a $39 fee to set up a pro plan for your book (which lowers the print costs for each book) or not. As far as I know, no other fees apply (aside from their share of the sales price). So you could set up your book without paying them anything upfront (but for you, I'm almost certain the fee would be worthwhile).

    \n I'm signed up for Create Space. I can't remember the exact mathematics, but it's fairly competitive. It costs nothing up front, like Lulu: alternatively, you can pay for their Pro Plan, like Christian says, and you get a better cut. If I was releasing a new book, I think the Pro Plan would be worth it.

    Honestly (and probably overreaching myself slightly), I think Create Space would be a good deal for anyone. Apart from anything else, it means your book's on Amazon. Even if you sell nothing, you haven't spent anything: I've sold four or five copies on that site, in a couple of months, without doing anything at all.

    \n It's worthwhile to note that your printing options are substantially different with both services.

    Me, I prefer saddle-stitch binding, so CreateSpace isn't even an option. All they offer is case binding.

    EDIT: It's also worthwhile to note that the comparison Doyce links is comparing the costs for putting books into distribution, which may or may not be a waste of your money. You can slap a PDF up on Lulu and point people to it for ZERO set-up cost.
