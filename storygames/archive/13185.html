\n In my experience, Penny for My Thoughts works best with three players, so that everyone gets to be involved in every step of the narration. Recently, a bunch of people have asked me to play with them, and it seems like our game this weekend will involve up to 5 or 6 people. I'm concerned that the game won't really work with this many people. Do you guys have suggestions for either modifications to the rules or play style to keep the game from dragging or being boring for people with this large number of players?

    \n I've only played Penny once, with 3 people, and it does seem like having 5 or 6 would slow it down a lot.
    \n
    \nIf you want a similarly simple story-game, what about Best Friends? It also uses 'tokens' (you could use pennies) as the medium of exchange in the game; creating your character depends on input from the other players and when you character acts, you have to give one of your tokens to one of the other players. It's not GMless though, but apart from that, not a million miles away from the experience of playing Penny, with the addition that it is designed for 6 players, though it does have modification rules for playing with fewer than 6.
