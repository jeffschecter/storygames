\n Okay, I have precisely two beefs with the Fate/Diaspora/SotC approach to play. One of them is how character creation works in games like Diaspora and SotC: it involves input from folks around the table. This is great and I think it produces really neat investment and ideas. What isn't so great is when new players join the group and/or when you're just not sure who's gonna show up.

SotC's pulp-serial style works just grand with people coming and going. Some of your team's around, some of them aren't, who cares, let's go punch a Nazi. This is a little iffier with Diaspora, but I suppose that comes down to your campaign premise.

Anyway, what I'm really looking for is good ideas to smooth over the process of injecting a new player into a Diaspora game in motion. The only idea I've had so far is to sort of randomly decide who's to the player's virtual left and virtual right and then request/require they fit their third and fourth phases into the phases that were already established. However, that kind of interferes with all that fun authorship.

Other ideas?

    \n I have had to do this a couple of times and I usually just ask them to wing it and get the stats down so we can get playing. Having them write at least one aspect about another player's character or two is helpful too. But really, at least for Disapora, there's so much going on in the first session that I don't try to replicate it -- a new player needs to get inserted fast while the car is moving.
