I got to play a kick ass game of DRYH at Camp Nerdly. By admission, I haven't read the rules and we went into the game for a burning the candle at four ends pace. Am I missing something? Is there campaign play of DYRH intended that I'm not getting (where the deprotaginaztion of falling asleep would need to be ameloriated)?

    Well, it's worth noting that the rules for what happen when you fall asleep are much less about the sleeping character and much more about everyone else, at least in my experience. When it happens, things can get _so bad_ that the best real choice is to let the gibbering hordes have their way. All you have to do is get out of the way. In our last game, when someone crashed, the moon split open and an army of giant gorilla-spider hybrids descended to add in a huge pile of pain on top of all of our existing problems. And in that case, the GM was really more generous than I would have been.

    That said, I suspect Fred will be here thirty seconds before me for a more complete breakdown.

    -Rob D.

    Nah, Rob has it about right. The fact of the matter is, the "postsomnia" stuff is there so that the game doesn't get quite so dark -- so people can rely on the safety of numbers, as it were. But really, the default is -- if you fall asleep alone, you're worse than dead, and will probably wake up to find yourself being carved up for the dinner table of your friendly neighborhood Nightmare.
