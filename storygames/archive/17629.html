    \n There are two 2-player-games that come to mind:
    There's Murderous Ghosts by Vincent D. Baker. It is his own hack of the Apocalypse World engine.
    Also, World vs Hero by John Fiore. It is a storytelling game where one player plays the world (environment, antagonists etc.) and one player plays the heroes. It's a card-based system, a littlebit tactial but also creative because you need to fit the story to the cards.

    \nOne Player Games
    Beloved - Imagine your beloved is kidnapped. Imagine you rescue them. Imagine it's never enough.
    How to Host a Dungeon - Create a dungeon. Watch it grow and change and collapse over the ages.
    Temper - Award yourself a point whenever you get angry. Spend those points to become a better person.
    Anna, the Miller's Daughter - Walk a mile in Anna's shoes, and draw a map of her small adventure.
    The Quiet Year - Hackers gonna hack. People have been playing this solo even though I never intended it as such.

    Awesome Two-Player Games
    Sweet Agatha
    Mars Colony
    Beast Hunters
    Ribbon Drive
    Breaking the Ice
    Shooting the Moon
    Perfect Unrevised

    \n Two-player game: I'm surprised nobody has mentioned Microscope. Even more than a Story Game, it looks like a History Game.

    Solo games:

    Hikikomori is one of the most impressive, since you play a young person that doesn't want to leave his/her room. The point is that you can tell his/her story via a blog or a short story, to relate each miserable day.

    Pocket Dungeon looks quite cool, and it's even a "stealth" game you can play unnoticed, using a 4-fold piece of paper with undecypherable codes on it (well... you may be able to understand the codes beforehand). As far as I can tell, it's possible to mix it with pre-generated Microdungeons or you can build one along the game.
