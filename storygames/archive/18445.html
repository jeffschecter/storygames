\n My buddy Paul and I played RPGs together for about ten or twelve years in the 1984-1995 time frame. He drifted away from the hobby toward other pursuits and only occasionally plays games now. But we stay in touch and share news sometimes. Yesterday, while I was playing some games, he texted me a question:

"What's the state of the art of computer assisted table top RPGs? I'm thinking of people sitting around the same table with iPads and laptops and such. Where the computing devices are essentially linked, somewhat intelligent, paper replacements."

And I have no idea. I'm aware that people do stuff, so I'm hoping maybe you have pointers for me/him to check out. Do you know anything about this? Where should we be looking?

    \n I've seen videos of some cool-looking demos of virtual tabletops on big touch screens like the MS Surface. I don't know if I'd want to play on one of those, though, because the tactile nature of maps and minis and dice (or boards and meeples and dice) is part of what I like about tabletop games.

    What I'd like to see is something that keeps track of logistics, like turn order/initiative, and updating the stats and conditions on your character sheet. This would be doable in an ordinary smartphone app, but it would be super sweet if done with one of those camera-enabled tables that could track die rolls and figure movements.

    Of course, the above is from the perspective of more traditional style RPGs (or board games). For story games it'd be fun just to have a big tabletop screen where you could draw out virtual index cards for characters and objects and goals and scenes, and connect them with relationships. Kind of like what happens in Fiasco or Microscope.

    \n Other than some dice rolling apps or apps for niche functions (e.g., IAWA Oracle Generator; Descent Condition Checker; HeroClix Rules; etc.), there's not a lot of digital play aides other than the bigscreen+laptop(s) configurations like Cheese Grinder. Though, I suppose you could have folks in the same location still using Roll 20, to have asynchronous input from multiple devices (whether or not you also include a bigscreen for the map/overview).

    As for opinions: unless it has SUPER fast handling time or only is used in speeding setup, I wouldn't like too much app-work at my tabletop. I'm in this hobby for the tactile and immediate, not to basically play a LAN party game with bits of conversational role-playing.

    \n I've seen people use handheld devices in the following manner:

    Store character sheets to do away with the clutter

    Communicate secretly to each other, to the DM or allow the DM to "Pass notes" on the sly.

    Taking notes, or writing a on the go journal

    I'm sure there are tones of other ways to use it. As far as a all inclusive service, I'm a fan or Roll20 and Maptools. Roll20 is webbased, so it's probably better for handhelds.
