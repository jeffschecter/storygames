\n This is a pleasant surprise. Play Unsafe has been reviewed on RPG.NET.

What is especially nice is the review is positive, without being hyper-enthusiastic. (When a reviewer's too enthusiastic, I distrust the review). What's nicer still is that I don't know the reviewer and didn't know the review was coming.

Also, I'd been worrying whether I should bug someone to write a review. ("Would you mind reviewing Play Unsafe? Say what you really think, but please give me three or more stars, please, otherwise I'll cry"). This stops me worrying. Good.

I mean, don't get me wrong. I'd prefer to Play Unsafe to be described as "a searingly intellectual analysis". But I can cope with "a sweet little read".

Graham

    \n I think, in its length, it practices what it preaches. That is, the author suggests that you shouldn't have to try too hard, memorize a whole bunch of stuff or prepare to play a roleplaying game. It's a prescriptive distillation of ideas which could have been twice or three times as long without any additional value. On that basis, it's like buying a big lump of fatty meat then having the butcher trim off the fat for you.
    \n
    \nDescribing in depth why each technique is in the book would be like a DVD extra or "making of" featurette. It might also destroy the illusion that Graham knows exactly what he is talking about. It's fun to bait Graham by quoting his own book at him in play. "Lose gracefully" was the last one I used. I find myself using that quite often. If you ever play with Graham, he loves it if you quote him.
