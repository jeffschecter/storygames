    \n Far West is Skarka's new project. It looks pretty promising using a highly stripped down d20 hack rules set and a setting which is basically Kung Fu Gunslingers in the wild west. They're really working to build IP around the setting, the RPG is just a piece of the overall multimedia plan.

    Adventurer Conqueror King just finished up and Children of Fire finished up a short while ago.

    Oh, and I just Indiegogo'd Awen.

    There's also a bunch of indie board games that are pretty sweet

    Imperial Crusade Armada is a space ship mini's game. Not the most exciting set of rules but instead of using actual minis the ships are printed on plastic credit card blanks which also double as the control sheets with dry erase pens...that concept was worth seeing in action for me.

    Lyssan, Fealty, Kingdom of Solomon, and Gype were all really sweet boardgames that I backed. Lyssan and Solomon have provided print and play versions of the game which I haven't had a chance to actually assemble and play yet, but the read quite well.

    Glory to Rome has already been published to pretty strong critical acclaim, but they're kickstarting a rerelease with all new art and packaging.

    \nThe Dance and the Dawn: A Theatrical Experience. (See also: other thread.)

    I randomly discovered: Open Gateways (where 4E means "For Everyone"). The author is putting together his own system (which promises to be compatible with D&D 4E while replacing classes with "Gateways", which might be a bit like a branching lifepath-like structure for character building). Also epic is his described setting, Kraterra, which may be a killer app:

    Essentially think Darksun with metal and guns, overrun by demons. There is definitely a Gamma World type of feel, as some items operate with concepts like batteries, whether this are Psi-Crystal, Mana-Stones, or even rounds of ammo depends on the item. Also: Black Power weapons and living symbiotic crystals. Let's get this funded, SG.
