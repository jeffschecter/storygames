    \n There are several ways to get tickets for you to attend Con-Quest 2012.
    \n
    \n1. Press this PayPal link below and buy a ticket on-line. Remember to fill in you name and contact details.
    \nYou will collect your ticket from the convention desk on the door on the day so avoiding any long queues at the box office.
    \nThe Early-Bird special rate is currently \xa36.00 if you buy before Wednesday 11th April 2012.
    \nThe PayPal button includes the Group Booking Discount as a single transaction only.
    \n
    \nPayPay Button on Con-Quest Website
    \n
    \n
    \n2. Send a cheque or postal order to:
    \nCon-Quest Midlands
    \n53 Stepping Lane
    \nDerby
    \nDE1 1GL
    \nPlease make them out to \u201cCon-Quest Midlands\u201d
    \n
    \n3. If you are in Derby you can buy tickets over the counter at the Derby LIVE box office either at the Assembly Rooms box office located at the Market Place or at the Derby Theatre box office in the Westfield Centre.
    \nOpening hours are 10:00 until 20:00 Monday to Saturday.
    \nTickets go live on Monday 31st October 2011.
    \n
    \n4. You can ring the Derby LIVE box office 01332 255800 and buy tickets over the phone. Both credit and debit cards are accepted for payment. Credit cards incur a \xa31 booking fee [debit cards don\u2019t incur a fee] and postage will incur a charge of 50p. When collecting tickets from the box office the card used for payment must be seen.
    \nOpening hours are 10:00 until 20:00 Monday to Saturday.
    \nTickets go live on Monday 31st October 2011.
    \n
    \n5. On-line through the Derby LIVE box office system.
    \nGo to the Derby LIVE Con-Quest website and click the \u2018Buy Now\u2019 link.
    \nCheck it says \u2018Con-Quest 2012 RPG Convention\u2019 and click \u2018Buy Tickets\u2019
    \nSelect the amount of tickets you would like to book and click the \u2018Continue\u2019 button.
    \nSelect your concessions and click the \u2018Continue\u2019 button
    \nCheck your ticket summary, if this is correct click the \u2018Continue\u2019 button.
    \nEnter your personal details and credit card details. Please note Derby LIVE charge a processing fee of \xa32 per transaction (not per ticket).
    \nClick the \u2018Purchase\u2019 button to complete the transaction \u2014 you may wish to print the Transaction Complete page for your reference though an e-mail confirmation will be sent to you.
    \nAll tickets purchased within the UK more than 7 days prior to Con-Quest will be posted to you.
    \nOpening hours are 10:00 until 20:00 Monday to Saturday.
    \nTickets go live on Monday 31st October 2011.
    \n
    \n
    \nGroup Booking Discount
    \nFor those in organised gaming groups or social groups you can get a Group Booking Discount. Please include the names of everyone on the ticket so that they can get in at the door.
    \nIndividual members can collect their own tickets from the convention desk on the door on the day so avoiding any long queues at the box office.
    \n
    \nFor Group Bookings of between 7 to 12 tickets the total price is \xa342.00.
    \nFor Group Bookings of between 13 to 16 tickets the total price is \xa355.00.
    \nFor Group Bookings of between 17 to 24 tickets the total price is \xa370.00.
    \n
    \nThese discounts are only available if paying via PayPal or by cheque and as a single transaction. We can not offer any discount on tickets already purchased or include them in as part of the group ticket.
    \nThey are not available from the Assembly Rooms Box Office.

    \n The aftershow party will be at the Brunswick Inn near the Derby Railway Station.
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \nFrom 19:00 to late in the upstairs part of the building. Some games may even break out there as well!
    \nAll are welcome to attend to discuss your war stories from the day\u2019s games, relax in good company talking about the gaming hobby or even have some more games!
    \n
    \nBuilt in 1842, and nearly pulled down in the Eighties, the Brunswick is a red brick, triangular shaped building at the end of a row of former railway workers' cottages. The station is a couple of minutes away, which makes it very handy if you have time to kill between trains.
    \nThey brew their own beer here, with the kit on show at the end of the stone-flagged corridor, such home-brewed delights as the bittersweet and refreshing Triple Hop or the dark and coffee-like Rockin' Johnny's Derby Pride. There were 14 real ales on offer, half of them brewed on the premises.
    \nBeer nirvana.
    \nNicely priced too!

    \nMalifaux is a miniatures skirmish game set in a universe infuenced by Victorian steampunk, gothic horror and the Old West, amongst others. Your crew of 32mm miniatures, led by your Master, will need to achieve a strategy and schemes, whilst preventing your opponent achieving theirs, and what's more you get to Cheat Fate along the way. All you need are a few figures with their stat cards, a tape measure, rulebook and fate deck (no dice here) to enter the world of Malifaux.
    \n
    \nCome along to the Old West town of Edgeport to learn how to play. See Sonnia burst here opponents into flame whilst Rasputina puts the chill on them, and Lady Justice lays down the law!

    \n We a delighted to announce the traders and stall holders that will be attending Con-Quest Midlands 2012.
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \nLeisure Games (established in 1985) has long been one of Europe's leading specialist Games Shops, stocking the widest ranges of RPGs, Board Games, War Games, CCGs, Miniatures, Dice, and gaming accessories. We have a large bricks & mortar store in North London, and also run a very fast and efficient mail order service/online store, and we also support a number of Gaming Conventions around the country.
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \nMagic Geek
    \n
    \nMagic Geek are CCG traders primarily focusing on Magic: The Gathering.
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \nWe stock a large range of single cards and boxed product. As one of the UK\u2019s Advanced Level tournament organizers we also run regular monthly events at our Birmingham and Stafford venues. Finally, as we are board game fans we stock a growing range of card and board games too.
    \n
    \n6d6 Fireball
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \nWe were asked to supply some blurb but rather than write some vague, self-congratulatory bollocks, we would rather you just played our game to find out why we have been receiving rave reviews and building a reputation as one the most innovative RPG companies around. Sign-up for any of the numerous 6d6 RPG games taking place or drop by the table for a quick game of 6d6 Shootouts. Alternatively, go to 6d6 Online and read all our products free of charge.
    \n
    \nSilver Branch Games
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \nSilver Branch Games is one of the UK's leading small press RPG publishers, run by Tim Gray just down the road in Nottingham. Albion, our Celtic-themed fantasy game, was recently voted UK Roleplayers 2011 Game of the Year. Jaws of the Six Serpents, a sword-and-sorcery RPG using the PDQ system, has been very popular at UK conventions - and has just got a line of T-shirts!
    \n
    \nUK Laser Tag Association
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \nWe're members of the UK Laser Tag Association, an umbrella organisation of various smaller groups that organize live-action role-playing games in various genres. The people coming to Con-Quest will be volunteers hoping to publicise some upcoming games, and "Tag" as a medium for LARP.
    \nAs you can see from the calendar we have several games coming up that we hope to interest people in.
