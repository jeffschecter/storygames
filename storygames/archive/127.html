    Actually, I had sort of a thing for that Tenra Bansho game I'm translating.
    Tenra Bansho came out as a visual book prelude to an RPG in 1996.
    Then Tenra Bansho came out as an RPG in 1997.

    After that, the "second edition" (a reimagining of the rules behind the game) came in 2000. This version was called Tenra Bansho Zero, because it was macking off the "reset to 0" vibe of the year 2000, but mainly because it was a reimagining of the game from scratch, a total realignment that streamlined the rules and made the game's focus a lot tighter.

    I have poked the thread in Malcolm's direction.

    From what I can recall... it was originally a slash (/), which became an unseemly apostrophe sort of thing (') in the font used in the logo. Paul, a|state's artist, then remade the logo using the now familiar vertical line (|). I think the main book uses the slash but elsewhere we almost exclusively use the "|" symbol. The keystroke had escaped Malcolm's notice on his laptop until either Brian Nisbet or myself pointed it out.

    Why there is punctuation in the name? Err, that's one for Malc! Though he has been known to smoke a pipe, or at least pose with one while resting an arm casually on his fireplace.
