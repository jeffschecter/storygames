\n This is an expansion of the Secret mechanic from a while back, which Vaxalon (Fred) is using to such good effect in our Wiki Amber game. I'm playing Garan in that game, and am currently trying to unlock the Secret of Oberon's Honour Guard - which, when you examine it closely, isn't actually a "secret" at all. Plus the game I'm developing draws on TSOY, much distorted, as a major influence, and while I don't use TSOY Secrets as such, the terminology might be confusing. I'm looking for a new word for these things that you unlock.

The essentials are:

- There is a situational thing that is pregnant with possibilities for change
- There is a condition or there are conditions under which that change will occur, and these are well-defined (and known to the players, though not necessarily the characters). This is what Fred is calling "triggers".
- There is an upside to bringing the change about - something you can do that you couldn't do before (Fred's "bonus").
- There's also a downside, a cost, a complication or what Fred calls a "consequence". So unlocking this thing full of possibilities gives you both more resources and more challenges.

I'm planning to make this a cornerstone of structuring situations for my Pentasystem game, though you could certainly use it in pretty much any game, including trad ones. (You could spec up a dungeon as a series of these, for example.)

So what should I call them? Unlockables? Caskets? Packages? Packets?

In my game text I'm trying to use words which convey fairly intuitively what things are, even though they're used in a specific technical sense. What's the intuitive word for what I've just described?

    \n Conversely, I hate "locks" as a name for this thing, because it has absolutely NOTHING to do with what's being described. No one in their right mind would ever refer to a "situational thing that is pregnant with possibilities for change" as a lock. In fact, in common parlance, that'd be exactly the OPPOSITE of a lock. When I say that the team championship is a lock for us this year, you know I mean that we are going to win: I am assuring you that there is no possibility of any other outcome. Using the word to mean something else in the game is how you end up with bad jargon.

    Within whatever you end up calling that situation, if you want to say that the upside/bonus/new ability is "locked" before the event and "unlocked" afterwards, that's totally fine. Just don't call the actual situation a "lock," or my Language-Sense will tingle so hard that my head will explode and chunks of it will FLY INTO YOUR EYES AND BLIND YOU. ;-)


    Turning point is pretty good, though. I'd say that as long as you go for a simple phrase, and make it one that someone could use casually without sounding weird, you're on the right track. Maybe call it a watershed moment, or a breakpoint, or a grand/unexpected opportunity, or a tipping point, or a surprise twist, or a climactic event, or whatever. It doesn't need a fancy-shmancy Proper Noun, it just needs a clear description in the rules that everyone will read and go "oh yeah, I get it -- dude, that's awesome, I wish more games had that!"

    \n I'm fourthing Gates. I was searching for a word describing the progression from one state to another, something to do with passages (which got me thinking of Giger's "Passages" series in which a machined slot turns into a vagina and back again, which turned into a cognitive dead-end pretty quickly) and Gates works extraordinarily well.

    It implies a one-way road - you cannot turn back and become ignorant again, ignoring the consequence.

    I can also see a tracing of a path (or better, a maze) between Gates. One Gate needs to have been passed before you get to a second Gate, and so on.

    - There is a situational thing that is pregnant with possibilities for change
    - There is a condition or there are conditions under which that change will occur, and these are well-defined (and known to the players, though not necessarily the characters). This is what Fred is calling "triggers".

    \n[b]Hinge[/b], noun: that on which something is based or depends; pivotal consideration or factor.

    -The possibilities for change _Hinge_ on this situational thing.
    -Triggering change _Hinges_ on this condition being met.
    -There's always an upside; the ability to do something new _Hinging_ on change.
    -And there's consequences _Hinged_ to all this.
    -To sum up, More Resources as well as More Challenges _Hinge_ on this thing happening.
