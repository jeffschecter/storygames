    \n The Guild of Blades Retail Group will be entering the distribution business in a short while. We are presently dealing with city bureaucrats in our plan to open a B&M retail store that blends our copy and print services together with games (with a major small press focus). Once we have everything on display and warehoused in our retail space, we plan to launch GOB Distribution. Our distribution business will be representing the majority of titles that we POD print for our clients together with Guild of Blades products and a select number of other small and mid sized publishers. Our focus, initially, will be on independent RPGs and Card Games and much, much less so on miniature gaming and board games (since GOB and a couple other small press companies we already intend to represent have the board game genre fairly well represented already).
    \n
    \nFrom small press/indie publishers we are most interested in seeing who we can work with on a POD printed capacity. Both for a program to shelf and sell the products in our store and to rep those same products through GOB Distribution. On both of those programs we bare the printing cost and will print as necessary to shelf/distribute the product and the publishers are paid as product is sold. Almost think of us as a One Bookshelf or other PDF retail site, but for printed titles printed via POD.
    \n
    \nWe will also be considering non POD printed products for distribution from publishers willing to give us wholesale terms (though as a distributor we will never be asking for "payment terms", preferring to pay as we order). We will be, however, much more selective of the non POD printed line we chose to represent, especially at the beginning. With all such ventures, you "have to start somewhere" and we'll be chosing those lines that best compliment our own and the POD products we'll be distributing, helping to round out our overall offering to retailers.
    \n
    \nThe timeline for the launch of GOB Distribution is approximately 1 month after we open the doors on our retail store. That _was_ planned for late September, but with red tape and all that, its likely going to be a little while after that.

    \n
    \n
    \nAs for GOB Distribution. I expect that enterprise to start small. Guild of Blades left distribution with its own products back in 2004. We have sold direct to retailers since that time, but as a single manufacturer, its has been a challenge to find retailers willing to order direct. Most simply don't like the time vs rewards efforts of ordering from single manufacturers. So as a consequence we do business with only about 20 stores direct currently. GOB Distribution will, however, greatly expand what we will be able to offer retailers. We'll be taking our 80-100 titles and combining 500-1000 more initially, attempting to provide strong stock and service on some great small press lines that we feel underperform in th market in large part due to the poor fill rates for said products through existing distributors. We will be striving to give retailers a place to order those products and be nearly guaranteed of getting a 100% fill on their orders. Additionally, our POD wholesale program will allow us to introduce the average POD title to retailers at a 48% discount, a strong discount that retailers are familiar and comfortable with while at the same time be able to deliver a stronger bottom line profit for the manufacturers we are distributing for. Being both the POD printer and the distributor knocks out several rounds of shipping (which is a considerable cost savings at todays fuel prices) expenses, letting us bring POD products to retailers at industry standard discounts. We can also operate the "distribution" service at essentially break even cost structures since GOB Retail itself directly profits from the POD printing, so in spite of being a "small" distributor, we'll be a fiscally healthy one. That plus the ability to guarantee availability should provide a number of solid reasons for retailers to want to do business with GOB Distribution. That all being said, its going to start slow and grow slowly as we add manufacturers that we represent and as we go through the motions of communicating our offering to a broader set of the overall retail base.
    \n
    \nEventually we intend to expand the IT infrastructure we've built to run this all so we can create dynamic direct marketing opportunities for our publishers. For instance, you could have the ability to create a number of fliers, catalogs, small posters or other promotional materials then be able to specify under what conditions said promotional material gets printed and included with retailer shipments. For instance, you could have a PDF catalog in our system and a note that the very first time a retailer orders one of your products, have it printed and included with that order. But any time an existing retailer of your line orders something, don't have it printed and included. We could track "last sale" for an item you make to a retailer and have the system "automatically" print a flier that served as a re-order remainder for your line if that retailer hadn't ordered anything new after X amount of time, but once sent, it would be recorded that it was sent and the next time after that you might opt not to send it again or you might opt to include a promotional "additional" discount or something to entice them to restock your product/product line. Build this system will let GOB Retail make a bit of extra money printing that marketing material for you, but more importantly, if a robust enough system is created to empower publishers to send various materials for an assortment of conditions, we'll hopefully bring introducing a higher level of target and intelligent marketing to the industry. Helping to bridge the massive communication gap that currently exists between retailers and manufacturers presently in the industry. I am a firm believer that in order for both manufacturers and retailers to better capitalize on the products we all sell, an open communication line needs to be opened between retailers and manufacturers. Information needs to be shared. Sales figures for individual manufacturers and product lines at specific retail locations need to be filtered back uphill to the manufacturer and a channel of communication for retailer feedback to those same manufacturers created. Most of that information is currently lost in the void of current middle man distributors within the industry and they all jealously guard any potential sharing of said information, fearing the forging of direct relationships between retailers and manufacturers. Frankly, most retailers are not best serviced ordering direct from individual manufacturers anyway. So distributors should look to facciliate greater levels of communication and make themselves indispensible in that role. Everyone will be better off for it.
    \n

    How much do you really want to know? The simple version, Aldo has limited storage space (he sublets from Chessex and then publishers sublet from him) so piles of books not moving (much) ties up his allocation of space in the warehouse. Many trad publishers are moving to POD now (I did it a long time ago and I think GoB did so even before us) and that makes for a better situation but you usually need to still have 300-500 in the pipe at initial release in order to fill distro demand. Times that by 40-50 publishers and at any one time you can have a pretty formidable surge. Have one that just has piles sitting there and you need to fight through that.

    And that is the simple version...;)
