Using Skype for Gaming

Doyce
November 2006 edited November 2006 in Story Games
Some of you Skype-using peoples chime in on this, please.

I've got a group of players I know out in NYC that I'd like to run a shortish game for. Probably Shadow of Yesterday. I could probably do it with chat, but I'd like to it be as close to the speed of Face to Face as I can, and that probably means live chat. GTalk can't do more than a pair of people at a time, so that probably means something like Skype. My questions:

- How many people can you get into a Skype call at the same time, using the free version?
- Can you text chat at the same time? (for pasting in data)
-- Related: do you usually use Skype in conjunction with something like IRC or the Foundry?
- Can you share files over the connection? (or would email work better for that)
- Dice-rolling. How do you manage it?

Any other related things that I didn't think of? Feel free to mention them, or point out threads that cover this already.

    I believe the call limit 5 people.

    Yes, Skype has an instant messenger setup that I think works quite well.

    For the Galactic playtest, we use IRC, importantly for funky dice rolling that Adam Dray programmed for us (Adam rocks!). Otherwise, I have not needed that stuff.

    I don't recall whether you can share files. Perhaps. But, we just use email or links via chat.

    I have played Nine Worlds (playing cards). It has the advantage that everyone needs their own deck, so I just let everyone handle their cards and announce their results. Worked fine. Dice would too. I see no problem to this at all. Either you trust people or you don't. If you don't, why bother?

    If you want to record it, good luck. It's tricky. Let me know, and I'll try to advise, but it's messy! (There may be a newer, easier way I don't know about, though.)

    Matt's answers pretty much map to my experiences. Two additional points:

    1) I use the Pamela extension for Skype for recording, and it works like a charm. The free version needs to be reDL'ed every 30 days, but it has lots of shiny features.

    2) Be prepared for some lag. I get the same lag from my GM in San Antonio (80 miles) as I do from Sean in Montreal (1700 miles). Using the text chat can help with it.
