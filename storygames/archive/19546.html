    \n Hmm. Sport is indeed a tough one. Let's try the (soccer) World Cup:

    Relationships
    Team captain and the one who should have been captain
    Club teammates on rival national teams
    Superstar and starry-eyed protege
    Retired legend and disappointing supposed heir

    Needs
    To win the next match... by any means necessary
    To humiliate a rival
    To score some cash, by throwing the game
    To get laid with the team captain's favorite groupie

    On the pitch of the big stadium
    Showers
    Press conference
    Announcer's booth

    Pictures of the referee with an underage girl

    I do, however, think there could be tremendous dramatic irony kind of stuff involved in playing through them.

    Watching "The Fellowship of the Ring" squabble over conflicting Needs and failing in a comedy of errors before falling apart might be a fun inversion of the epic genre, as would "X-Men: The Last Stand".     \n The reasoning behind my own suggestions was: For "A Serbian Film" you'd have to be very sick puppies to go further than the movie did already; and for "No Exit" - well, it's the most boring game imaginable and there's no exit!

    I actually imagined a No Exit playset. The people in the room mean nothing to you except in what you think of them, and what you think they think of you, so Relationships are like "I think you think I'm a bad person". The Locations are places in the room (the couch, the table, the corner). There are objects in the room but they mean nothing to you, only objects in your memory have meaning. Needs are still needs, but you have no way to attain them. The greatest need is the need to get away from other people, which is impossible.

    A typical scene: Marty looks at Jennifer, certain that she hates him. 'You hate me,' he says. 'Don't be stupid, I don't even know you,' she replies. They glare at each other for a minute. Marty's eyebrow twitches repeatedly. The camera zooms in on it and lingers there for five minutes. 'Okay now I DO hate you,' she says. 'A-HA!' replies Marty. 'But not the way you think,' says Jennifer.

    This goes on for 3 hours.
