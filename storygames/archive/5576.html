\n ... in a campaign based around my character coming up from a nobody to forging his own kingdom. Or a campaign that revolves around military fantasy where the characters are the leaders of a nation/domain/whatnot up against long odds and the game is split between intrigue, statesmanship, strategy and resource management. I guess that's why Birthright has always intrigued me. I've never gotten to play those campaigns though. I think it's probably because I'm always the GM in the games I play. Hard to GM that stuff for yourself. It would also explain why I like to play Diplomacy quite a bit. It scratches some of those itches.

Anyone else out there always wanted to play a specifc style or type of game and not been able to do it? What was it? Why didn't it happen?

I'll be frank here. I've got ulterior motives, though they aren't completely selfish.

1.) I'm just curious to see whether I'm the only loser who can't seem to get it together and manage to play in the game I want.
2.) I might see someone else's "need" as say to myself, "Hey, that sounds neat and I could help out with that." Then I might actually get to help someone play in that game they always wanted.
3.) Related to #2 above, someone might see my "need" and think, "I've always wanted to run that game!" and they might contact me and we might actually get to play.

So! Tell us what you've always wanted to play and never got to. We can make this the "Whiney Losers Who Never Get What They Want" thread.

    \n Oh man, I could go on all day with campaign ideas that I've had that I really want to run but have not yet gotten to.

    Birthright was a really interesting "first pass" at a game genre that never really got a second pass. Which is a shame because there was a lot of cool things there.

    The main reason that people don't get to play in the games they want is the same reason they don't lose the weight they want to lose, they don't finish the novel they want to write, and they don't learn to cook the dishes they want to learn to cook. Because they (by they I mean "I") don't organize the project, work through it, and get to what they want. There's no reason you can't play in that game. You just have to organize the project ("recruit players, organize a day, buy food, copy character sheets"), work through the project and overcome the barriers, and then you're there.

    \n I have a few campaigns I've always wanted to run, but have never found the right players for:

    A) A campaign set in a low-magic, fairly realistic medieval Britain (circa 1250) about siblings (I imagine brothers, but a sister could fit in there) from a small village who go from being callow youths to the greatest heroes of the land, possibly becoming kings. The campaign would be heavily influenced by the real folklore of region, where every ogre has a name and is a local legend, and unrestful spirits with names like Ginny Greenteeth haunt local ponds. It would start small, with the village and immediate environs being the whole of the world at first, and gradually growing as the player's grew too big for their pond. I'd always imagined using D&D for this game, but I think it's never happened precisely because I wanted to use D&D. Now I think I'd more likely use Burning Wheel.

    A campaign set in a mythical Africa about a group of young Maasi warriors chosen by the prophecy of the local witchdoctor to embark on a great quest to restore the health of their war chief, who has fallen ill. The campaign would be all about journey and discovery, as the boys are forced to leave the simple but harsh life of raising cattle on the savannah and travel north into the strange kingdoms of Ethopia and Egypt. Very low magic, and what magic there is would be rooted in the African animist traditions that inform Voodoo and Houdon. And again, very strongly rooted in the real folklore of Africa. Unfortunately I tend to encounter to much unacknowledged racism in the gaming groups I've played with to try to seriously run this campaign. Not sure what system I'd use, maybe Burning Wheel or my own system (since I'd practically have to rewrite Burning Wheel anyways).
