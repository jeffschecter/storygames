\n Dear Story Games

I am making a small story game, building on many of the things I learned here by lurking and playing the games that are discussed here. We\u2019ve been working on it for some time, and would like to start playtesting soon. It\u2019s definitely something that might interest you, so I\u2019d like your help once more for tips, suggestions and critiques.



Alien\xf2r is a roleplay about the downtime of ladies-in-waiting of the illustrious Alien\xf2r, or Eleanor, of Aquitaine, who secretly idolize her. They tell stories about their hidden lives, love and sex and about Alien\xf2r. Players invent and practise the elusive world of medieval women themselves.\n
  • No GM, and up to four players. It presupposes a certain cooperation and trust.
    • \n
  • Each player is narrator in turn, and makes a story with the help of the other players and conditions from tables.
    • \n
  • Alien\xf2r is no NPC. All players can roleplay her by random chance. Together, you will try to find out who this mysterious lady really was!
    • \n
  • It is deliberately up to you to fill in the blank spots in the rules and the story together. The hidden world is yours only to reveal!
\nThe roleplay originated as a couple of ideas that needed an RPG. It\u2019s not much and it\u2019s thinly spread. Don\u2019t be discouraged by the tables or historical blah-blah, as the story is explicitly about creativity and those bits of history that didn\u2019t make it. I\u2019ve also included some pictures to make it prettier! I hope you like it.

Tell me what you think! Questions, discussions, suggestions about content or rules, remarks the makeshift layout or drawings - everything is welcome! I am eager to discuss and to improve this!


Alien\xf2r

    Thanks, Jason! My aim is basically to see if this 'framework' can withstand the approaches and play of a wider audience. I want to know exactly how far I can stretch the minimalism (it is very much polluted already). A big concern of mine for instance is the Pieces mechanic - does it work if no condition at all for giving them away is given? The Pieces aim to create a 'dialectic' mood of trust and co-operation by letting players reward eachother.

    Secondly, I want to know if it makes any sense for four players to roleplay one character, Alien\xf2r, more or less seriously. She's not supposed to be a consistent, even believable character, but she's no John from Everybody is John either. Although a slapstick approach might be cool as well! (Women in Tights?)
    I hope the Alien\xf2rless framework and the personal player characters tackle this, but I'm not sure.

    Do you have a good answer on your own example question? It is a thought-provoking question as well.

    If you like period detail, there's some accurate dresses and hairstyles, but the one on p. 17 is rather fancyful.
