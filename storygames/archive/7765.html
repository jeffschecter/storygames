\n So in the appendix of the fifth supplement to New Changeling, there is a little bonus game called The Game of Immortals. It's a game meant to create the feeling of playing one of the True Fae in their competitions with each other. It's totally a dirty hippy game. It's GM-less. It's really freeform, based around competition via creating stories and legends. It uses dice, but not to determine winners of the contests. It's pretty goddamn funky and I haven't quite had a chance to absorb it completely, but I was pretty surprised to find it in a White Wolf book.

Has anyone else seen this? Or better yet, played it?

    \n Yeah, it sounds like you're talking about 1st ed Exalted: Fair Folk, which I think is awesome but crazy. And still, in it's way, a very trad game with lots of powers and stats and numbers and crunchiness. I'm interested to see what happens with 2nd ed Fair Folk, and I hope Borgstrom works on it again, or at least that whoever does really takes the awesome stuff in the first book to heart and focuses primarily and making the game easier for people to actually PLAY.

    The Game of Immortals, on the other hand, is all of 4 and a half pages in the back of The Equinox Road. It has NO traditional WoD mechanics at all. Basically, each player takes on the role of one of the True Fae. Everyone has either the same, predetermined, number of Titles, or a random number of Titles, and a Name. That's it. You pick your name, and the other people in the group, going around in a circle, pick your Titles. Then people challenge each other for control of the titles. Dice are only used to determine the "style" of the legend being told in the challenge, and the style is meant to inform the kind of story being told and also determines what happens to the winner and loser of the challenge. There are some rules about how the players go about describing the legend and finishing the challenge. The loser is the first person to break one of the rules of the challenge.

    It's... interesting. It really is a kind of mini-game, I think, because while in the smaller formats more common to indie games, I suppose it might be a dozen or fifteen pages, it doesn't feel substantial enough to me that it looks like a game you'd want to play for more than a session once every now and then. There are some suggestions for how to use it as a way to add another layer of, i guess, meta-structure to a normal Changeling game, though, which seems like a potentially cool idea.
