\n Jenna Moran just posted stats for the character of Max Lamner from Hitherby Dragons for (presumably) the new edition of Nobilis she's been rumored to be working on.

Some of the new Stats include Skills, Passions, and Cool, as well as Treasure. She also lists two traditional Nobilis stats, Bonds and Afflictions.

[If these were discussed previously in Society of Flowers, please let me know].

Passions is making me think of Mortal Coil. I'm hoping these stats are something you can use to better define mortals/anchors.

Any thoughts how these might work?

"I do want to do a Hitherby Dragons RPG at some point. Nob3 tech is a lot closer to being able to do it, but it\u2019s not there; maybe 2012, 2013."
-Jenna Moran

    \n Two new Hitherby examples via Nobilis 3 mechanics, Sid (an Excrucian Strategist) and Ipheginia.

    "In Nob3, Strategists don\u2019t really have a way to stay in the world for good; even dying\u2019s not enough!"
    -Jenna Moran

    I noticed that the Excrucian has Aspect, Domain, Persona and Treasure, with no rating in Domain or Persona.

    Re: Ipheginia, she has a Superior Attribute (Sun Goddess), which leads to this explanation:

    "Yeah, Superior Attributes are no longer strictly tied to Aspect. I tightened up how Aspect interacted with things like light spirits and when I was done I no longer felt mundane abilities needed to be yoked to the Aspect chart or expressed as Gifts."
    -Jenna Moran
