\n So the front cover art to my new game -- Scorn -- is in development, but I wanted to try something new and kind of loco for the back cover.

The way I figure, this game won't be sold in stores, so it's not like I need for the back cover copy to be legible, or even coherent.

Thus:



The game's creepy, brutal, gruesome... It's the follow-up to Dread: The First Book of Pandemonium, so if you know anything about that, then you know where I'm coming from. The difference is, Dread is about demons, but Scorn is about angels.

What do you think? Too incoherent? Effective? Been done already?

-- Rafael

    \n In no particular order:
    1) Yes, make the topmost title more visible, a bit larger, and further from the top of the page. Then, you can remove it from the first passage and middle of the text, which struck me as overly redundant. Speaking of which...
    2) I think three instances of your publishing company name is (at least) one too many... or, at least, appearing so close to each other. I'm half-and-half on the two repetitions of your web URL.
    3) For all the chaos, it's really fairly structured: color, pitch, contents overview, some art, About Us--just like every game book, in the end. To mitigate this conformity, I'd at least move one of the art pieces up, closer to or at the top. Also, play around with completely rotating (i.e. about 180\xb0) one of the text pages, ideally the one that reads "Morlock is driving."
    4) (nitpick) The repetition of that passage (on the overlapped page to its left) makes it read weirdly--it actually looks like a printing error, not the shredded notes of a paranormal investigator/fighter.
    5) (nitpick) Where will your bar code and ISBN number appear (if either)? I recommend the lower-left corner, perhaps superimposed over (i.e. replacing) the bar code of a pack of cigarettes shown on-edge?

    Otherwise, it's very, very cool. Evocative, good text, some humor
