\n I'm no wine connoisseur. Most of the wine I've bought says "Advanced Wine-like Product", or comes in a box. However, I've been a big fan of this sweet muscat wine, and have always been a fan of mead.

I was thinking, though, I rarely drink wine at games. Whether it's Dungeons and Dragons over a hexmap, or With Great Power all in character, we (all my gaming groups) usually roll with water only, or Sundrop soda, or beers like Hoegaarden or Blue Moon, Yuengling or Kirin. I think the only time we had wine in the past 5 years in any of my groups, was when one of the gamers had a party, and she had this huge box of box-wine left over: We chugged it down as we climbed a tower of snake-men in the Conan OGL game (before we gave it up).

So... anyone have wine at a game? Regularly? Does anything change when you do, or is it just like Beer/Soda/Water, yet Just Happens to be Wine?

-Andy

    Posted By: AndyIf you were a multiclassed gamer-gekk/sommelier, and I asked you, "Which wine goes best with Spirit of the Century", what would you recommend?
    \nSotC happens during the prohibition by the default setting, so I'd go with moonshine instead, or a French varietal. Or another option is to brew your own wine. Table wines were most popular, so unless you have a specific mix in mind, don't worry too much about the varietals you get, just get a 3-5 different grapes.

    Ultimately, though, it depends on what you're eating and what you like.

    I've found wine works good as a prop. It seems to go well with Vampire no matter what else is going on (red wine only, of course). For a fancy feel in another game, having wine about gives it the air of a dinner party.

    I prefer merlot or a tasty table wine (like Schoolhouse Red) since they go well with cheese and crackers - a good gaming snack. Or there's port if you're having sugary sweets. Another option is to have a white wine if you prefer fruit and veggies as snacks, but I'm not a fan of whites, so I'm not sure about a varietal to suggest.

    Edit: And for a game set in the Roman age, wine should be limited to anyone playing a Roman, and beer, ale and other drinks only for anyone not playing a Roman.
