\n
\n
\n
\n\nJudd \n\n\n
\n
\n\nNovember 2007 \n edited November 2007 in Actual Play
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n Where I am, the winds are turning cold and the sun's just about a stranger.

Our Sunday group just didn't work out. Sundays became a problematic day. But then Fridays took off and now those of us who can game regularly are playtesting Houses of the Blooded, a game I hope will scratch a long-term itch that we've been looking to scratch for a while. It feels like it will. The campaign's just starting out and the sucker's got legs. Feels like every little thing we do generates drama and blood and adventure.

There are other games around the house in limbo. I made up a Beast Hunters character with my roommate, Bret and we'll likely get to it next week if school allows. I hope like hell that it does. It'd be nice to have a nice, almost cozy game around the house for when we're both around without else to do. We made up Dictionary of Mu characters to with a mind towards Bret GMing me and another roommate but I'm not sure if that'll pan out. The house has problems with geek inertia.

I'd love to get a quick and dirty lunch-time game going. The idea of that just makes me smile.

How about you? What games are you cuddling up to as winter sets in?

    Posted By: JuddI love a game with a minotaur.
    \nI know, they feel so mythic.\n
    What are you loving about The Lost, Daniel? Between your love and Rob's on his blog, I'm damned intrigued.
    \nTo start with, the tagline: A Storytelling Game of Beautiful Madness. Those two last words there conjure images of a gorgeous downward spiral that, while you could escape, you just don't want to, because the beauty is to amazing. I'm diggin that it's set in Miami, and I am seeing my city in a whole new light. I'm diggin the freeform chargen system that allows me to dictate the shape and form of my changeling based on the descriptions I give, not forced pre-determined fae types as before. I'm diggin the whole aspect of Contracts as a source of power, whether social, physical or magical, and I am giving a lot of thought to how to exploit this side of the game. I'm diggin the sense of hopelessness inherent in being a creature that has now become an alien in her former world and life, and you can be sure whatever store my wife and I craft, it is going to feature heavily the concept of Fetches and how they can be a more real You and you are now. I'm diggin that it's fantasy and fantastic, but not dumbed down or over the top (though I'm sure it could be). I'm diggin the green color of the whole line; it draws me in, ensorcels me. I'm diggin that it is as much a toolkit as it is a game, and that I could recreate Wicked, Labyrinth, Dark Crystal or Wonderland with no problem. Above all, I'm diggin that my wife digs it, and we'll be able to play together again after way too much time.

    That helps?

    \n My weekly Monday group is moving into our second Aberrant arc. We all love the world, and have managed to come to a workable understanding with the rulez, and it's been very fun so far. The characters were made with 54 Nova Points (as opposed to the 30 you're supposed to start with), and the first arc started in 2014 with the characters being higher-powered and more established in the world than normal starting Novas. It's been really challenging and fun to run a high GM-prep game oriented at intrigue and shady dealings among mega-powerful beings. I have three players, and the plot of the first arc ended up centering on one of them, so the plan is to do at least two more arcs, each centering on a different character.

    I'm taking a hiatus from the Thursday GURPS group I've been playing with since the summer, cuz I need more time to work on Annalise. After the holidays I'll probably be back in with them for either an occult investigation or FBI psychics campaign, both of which I have established characters in.

    I also just got back from JiffyCon. It was fun! I ran Annalise and played Giants and Murder Ballad Blackjack. I'm planning to go to Dreamation and maybe Aresia come January, but other than that I don't have a lot of indie gaming planned. I'll try to stop into SGBoston soon, if my Wednesdays ever free up again.

    So far, been a good winter.
