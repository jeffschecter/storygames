    AW just has thick, glossy toner.

    \n

    I wanted to do a spot varnish on Human Contact, but I need to be confident of the volume of sales before I can do such a thing.

    \n

    Here's the deal: printing options invariably open up with volume. The more you print, the more options you have. I've sold something like a thousand hard copies of Shock: which means that, if I'd never done revisions, I could have taken that risk four years ago, when I didn't know if the game was going to sell at all. And I'm a fairly reliable third-tier publisher

    \n

    Vincent's getting that interesting effect purely as an artifact of the particular printer used by his press. If he didn't want it, he'd have to go scrounging for other options, which probably means larger print runs.
