\n So i signed up to run a LARP on saturday night, and a few of you have shown interest. This is the thread where we talk about what kind of thing we want to involve ourselves in. Questions to ask, answer, comment on:

\xe2\u20ac\xa2Do we want to do boffer (real combat with padded weapons)?
\xe2\u20ac\xa2If yes, a lot? just a little? Not for everyone?
\xe2\u20ac\xa2If yes, then we need a setting that validates folks haveing swords (just something to think about)
\xe2\u20ac\xa2What kind of setting do we want? Something traditional that we are all used to (fantasy? Modern time? Sci-fi?) or something that we aren't used to that might be interesting?
\xe2\u20ac\xa2Do you want a more player motivated free-wheeling (norwegian style) game, or something that is more directed by a cast member (that would be me). This is important as it will really affect what kind of rules i whip up.
\xe2\u20ac\xa2I strive for a what you see is what you get style of play (that is, in LARPs i try to never say something like "over these trees you see a dragon"). I however am not going to pack a moving van full of props and costumes and equipment and stuff (but i'll bring some). That all being said, how immersive an experiance are people interested in? Do you mind (or are you interested in) breaking the 4th wall?
\xe2\u20ac\xa2Do you yourself have any questions/concerns/comments, if so, add them.

    also, some depression era costumes can't hurt (no one is required to do any prep at all). And you might want to think about an archtypical character from that time period.

    NOTE: i will specifically changing around the rules for vices and red chips, so while you should read that stuff, it's going to be a little bit differant.

    This is the LARP i will be running.
