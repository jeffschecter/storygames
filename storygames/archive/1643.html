    My feeling is that Static is primarily about the isolation of the Agents -- the higher the Static, the more Blue City takes on a life of its own, at the expense of the Agents' connection to Control. Communication to Control becomes frayed, Control becomes inexplicably hostile or starts giving contradictory commands, and meanwhile the environment around the Agents undergoes inexplicaple changes -- changes that suggest it is more than it seems, not just 'random weird dream landscape' changes.

    I think an ideal high-Static situation is one in which the environment around the Agents changes enough that they feel disoriented -- enough that they want to rely on Control to give them direction. And then, as mentioned, Control can't provide that. The Agents should feel like the safety net is being pulled out from under them.

    A few random examples:

    * Control starts feeding the Agents objectives meant for other Agents in the field. The other Agents are on a mission with a darker classification.
    * A recurring Personality shows up dead. When the Agents report this information, Control reacts with seemingly paranoid suspicion, suggesting that the Agents were directly responsible.
    * Agents become separated through some arbitrary dream logic. Control doesn't seem to notice, reacting to the Agents as though they were all still in their previous location.
    * When a high-static mission ends, all the Agents eject but one. Attempts to communicate with Control fail. Agents who successfully ejected either a) have completely forgotten about the other Agent or b) continue to interact with the other Agent in realspace, who seems to be perfectly normal.
