    \n I've played three games, and while they've all involved a King, the answer as to who rules has been fuzzy.

    In the game we started last Friday, our shared fate was The Assassination of the King. One Heart takes orders from a regent. Another Heart (for whom I'm his Mistaken) is taking orders from the risen body of the dead King in defiance of the Regent. My own Heart isn't taking orders from a King, as most of his story so far takes place far from a Remnant. Our fourth Heart is taking orders from his demon-possessed Starlight Sword. (The latter two have stories that haven't really involved a ruler yet, but do involve other elements of the assassination.)

    So, really, it can differ within the same game.

    hoog, the ambiguous nature of Polaris' backstory really opens up opportunities for players to develop their own unique, custom-tailored setting for the game. And each game/campaign of Polaris you play might end up being wildly different than previous times. Considering the fact that there is no one GM here, that every player must take on that role in turns, it drives home the idea that your setting is one that belongs to all of you and pushes each player to take ownership.

    At least, that's how it felt to me when we played it.

    On the topic of actual Polaris, my take on the King and Queen is that they're both gone. Or at least no longer on the side of the People. I think I actually had the idea that the King and the Queen had become the Solaris Knight and the Frost Maiden, though I think there are at best only hints towards this and nothing explicit in the book.

    also, off the king and queen topic, but still on Polaris while i haven't played the game yet, one thing i think the game and system is remarkably suited for dealing with are certain things that would be difficult (if not downright impossible) to handle in a more "normal" system... for example, conflicts taking place within the heart and mind of a character. to bring up HG,WT again, i listened to Paul's AP recordings of Polaris, and I believe at one point a knight was possessed by a demon. what i was thinking, while listening to it, is that it would be really cool, in that situation to frame a scene taking place in a mental dreamworld where the knight and demon fight, each trying to shape the mental landscape to their own advantage.

    but that's a tangent. let us know how the game goes! i really want to play myself sometime soon!
