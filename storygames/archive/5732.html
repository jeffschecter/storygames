    \n Here's how I replied there and to your email. (for those of you keeping score at home this is a triple-x-posting, but not the dirty kind)

    It really is a sense of community that makes these events vroom. My advice is to set up a message board, get gamers really talking to each other, have several meet-ups for movies or dinner, or drinks, whatever beforehand, and make sure that people are really discussing what they're interested in playing and the kind of play they want.

    The real life meeting part of this is really important. Even if not everyone can show up for every event, people knowing each other as real people with real names and real faces is an immense asset to community.
