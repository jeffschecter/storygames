\n Here's a quote from another thread:\n
Posted By: DoyceThe problem, entirely my own, is when I mention a game like PTA and the guy says "Oh, I'd hate something like that -- I don't like the players authoring stuff," and that pretty much nut-kicks my attempt to suggest that game to that player.

And I'm frustrated, thinking to myself "but... you're already doing that."
\nI met this problem, and learned to come at it from the opposite angle.

That is, when I want people to try a new (odd) game, I go like this:

1. Figure out the thing that the game does more of.
2. Think of examples of times when the prospective players already do those things.
3. Bring up those incidents, and what I thought was cool about them.
4. Tell them I have this game that's, like, all about that thing.
5. Pitch them the game, based on that.

And, yeah, sometimes I feel like I'm playing dirty when I do this. But on the occasions when they really dig the game, it's totally worth it.

Just thought I'd toss that out there.
