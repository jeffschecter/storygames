    \n So the interesting thing that I was thinking about:

    There's games I want to mention that various folks are working on (Conspiracy of Shadows, The Next L5R 4e Sourcebook, the completed version of Bliss Stage, etc). But they're all "hoping". The only games that seem set in stone, that is "scheduled for release", then "hit the release date" like clockwork come from the few huge (read: "more than three dudes") companies. Everything else is "it'll be out when it's done". Or else, "it'll be rushed to meet GenCon and possibly be disappointing", etc. It's really hard to determine what is coming out soon, unless it's Done already and just waiting on a printer...

    \n Oh, I forgot to say more about the games I mentioned.

    Nobilis, I guess we all know. Sadly, I can't say more about the new edition, as I haven't been following the news, but it got people excited. I'll let someone else say more.

    The One Ring, the new LotR RPG is citing Pendragon as one of its influences spiritually and D&D4E and Warhammer 3E as influences of design that "does one thing but one thing well". The interview I read with the author says that it will be very focused: northern lands after the end of the Hobbit, emphasizing Tolkien's themes of humanity and heroism. It reminds me of Mouse Guard a lot.
    The author said their intent was "historical reconstruction", going back to the source material and try to really capture the themes and atmosphere of the stories instead of doing yet another "do anything RPG", consequentially, no playing High Elves or dueling Balrogs (at least not in the core set). Design-wise they supposedly really focused on "what people actually do at the table" instead of simulating world physics or discerning the differences between a +1 and +2 sword.

    Posted By: McdaldnoI'm curious about the drafting aspect. Is everyone drafting from the same deck of 35? Are there duplicate cards? Who gets to pick and how? Is it partially randomized? Why play with only 7 cards?
    \nEveryone drafts from the same deck of 35.

    Let's say we have 5 players (the maximum). I deal the deck. We each have seven cards. Pick one, pass the rest to your right, continue until you receive only one - and that's your hand done. So, there's a random element in what you pick from. But not in the rest. I call it a draft because, well, that's what it gets called in Magic (or used to, in the wayback).

    As for "why only seven cards", lots of reasons. To keep costs low and the game transportable. To allow for transport. Because in deckbuilding games (like Magic, Netrunner, Star Wars, all of which I like), it tweaks my nose to track down several copies of most cards and effectively have a deck of ten cards plus land - why not stab the middleman? Also, to allow for expansion (up to two card-based expansions sets are possible- one adding a board of sorts, and one adding character continuity & advancement). And, most importantly, because I tried it, and it works.

    \n Graham,

    In Kingdom of Nothing, you play a person who has experienced a tragedy and lost his memory. You spend the game learning about yourself either to regain the memory, or to avoid it and slip into the Nothing. You, the player, don't know what tragedy lies in your character's past, it's collectively scripted at the table by the other players at the start of play. You build other people's memories, but don't know your own. Resolution is a simple coin-flipping mechanic. You shake them all up in a cup and flip it on the table.

    Posted By: GrahamPosted By: KobayashiThe new edition ofShell Shock, fully illustrated should be available before summer. I'm adding new texts, a lot of GM advice, etc.
    \nSay more about this? What's interesting about it mechanically?

    There was a big AP report here for a time, you can find it here.

    To put it very briefly a character has four Trauma track : Guilt, Indifference, Fear and Violence. By allowing these traumas to grow you can survive more easily ("Nope this bullet touches another character, not me", "Ok, have a guilt point"). But if you let these traumas go too high... Well, shit happens.

    There are rules for frustration caused by the day-to-day routine of military life as well.

    There are no rules concerning the soldier's gear or weapons. The game is about the soldiers, not their stuff.
