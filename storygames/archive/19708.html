    \n Like zircher I have the original, actions, and voyages sets. I use them when riffing on scenario ideas. Usually I have a broad concept in mind, and if I'm having trouble filling in details I'll pick up the Story Dice, roll three dice from each set and arrange a story that fits into the overall scenario concept. I find them quite helpful; it's like having someone to riff back and forth with as I plan. They're particularly handy for NPC backstory.

    \n I have Original, Voyages, Prehistoria, Clues and Enchanted. I.e. everything except Actions. From the twenty-seven dice I have, I\u2019ve picked my 9 favorites (which changes depending on which genre I\u2019m looking at).

    Honestly I haven\u2019t gotten them to really fly. Weird, I never had any problems with Everway\u2019s cards or playing Dixit.

    My best practice for the story cubes so far is to be really concrete about what questions I want to ask before I roll them.
    Like \u201cWhat are the factions and conflicts in this village?\u201d and then using the icons to answer those questions.

    The standard \u201cJust roll nine and tell some story, any story\u201d haven\u2019t been working so well for me. Better and better, but...

    \n I find the original and actions sets problematic - players/characters should control the verbs.

    But the voyages set and some of the mysteries/supernatural ones are great as inspiration. I roll a fair handful and grab the 3-4 that spark something or reference already established fiction. Really handy.

    Inspired by them, I got a bunch of blank dice. I draw on them factions, places, characters that have emerged from the fiction and need to recur. Great fun for getting things to re-incorporate in interesting combinations.

    \n I too have all the sets out so far. I never game without them. Most of my use is for solo play, coming up with interesting scene setups by grabbing two or three at random from a big velvet bag and rolling. I find sometimes making lists of what each icon means is helpful. For example: a Clock -- afternoon, he's on your 2, time challenged, ticking bomb, clockwork control dial, Stonehenge, old guy halfway to a full-on 5 o'clock shadow, etc.

    I also find combination of two to work well. I always though voyages and enchanted sets to be most useful, but oddly, I get more mileage out of original and actions.

    Shortly, I will try running an improvised adventure with them and see how it works for the whole group.
