\nHammercon will feature one or possibly two RPG panels, where the panelists are all published game designers. I think that's awesome, because I can listen to game design discussion until the speakers are blue in the face! Maybe that's not universal among RPG enthusiasts, though.

If you were attending the convention, what topic(s) would you like to hear designers discuss? Ideally it'd be a topic that's useful for those who play games, not just those who like to tinker with them, but it seems a waste to ignore the fact that the panelists are all accomplished designers as well as players.

    \n Soooo.... RPG panels are dead, nobody gives a crap? Is that it?

    Let's do this instead. What would you like to hear the following people talk on? Who'd you pair up with who to get a really interesting conversation going, one that's controversial and yet productive?

    (yes, Anna and Darcy, I'm calling you out here - feel free to pipe up with subjects you'd like to talk on, if you're reading!)\n
      \n
    • Robin D. Laws - The Dying Earth, GUMSHOE, HeroQuest, Feng Shui, Mutant City Blues, and more - plus a metric crapton of GM advice
      • \n
    • Darcy Burgess - Black Cadillacs, Race
      • \n
    • Pieter van Hiel - Usagi Yojimbo, Ironclaw, Jadeclaw
      • \n
    • Anna Kreider - Thou Art But A Warrior, Black Sky (in playtest)
      • \n
    • Jonathan Lavallee - CyberGeneration, Suitors (card game)
      • \n
    \nI'd personally be interested in knowing what these folk think of the role story / plot should play in RPGs to make them entertaining and effective... also, how they see mechanics being used in interesting ways, or not. I've also had suggestions on "GM troubleshooting" (i.e. "my home campaign has this problem, how do I solve it") and "gaming and new media" (i.e. gaming in the age of podcasts, forums, wikis etc).

    Thoughts and ideas?
