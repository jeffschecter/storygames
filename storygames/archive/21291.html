    \n The DayTrippers Core Rules include a collaborative method of play with a "GM Lite" who uses ideas prompted by the group. Mechanics are similar to Cypher \xb7 Missions are created by playing a collaborative minigame \xb7 CharGen is point-buy \xb7 Progressive Character Generation \xb7 Experience Points work just like CharGen \xb7 LifeShaping Events make characters unique and dramatic \xb7 The system uses D6s only \xb7 Actions are stat and skill-based \xb7 Action Resolution based on "yes/no and/but" interpretations \xb7 Narrative interpretation shifts between GM and Players depending on rolls

    Jeff already mentioned my FateLess, but I deem it crunchy only if compared to ultra-light stuff. A truly cruncy-er but kind of similar game of mine would instead be Tactical Ops... you can easily roll 10-15 D6 all at once

    \n Mutant Year Zero is basically a PbtA game with the moves replaced by a skill list, so it may fit the bill. I believe Tales from the Loop and Coriolus use the same system but I'm not familiar with them.

    It's also worth looking at Unbound. It's definitely more to the story-game side than MYZ. There are some elements that are easily identified as story-gamey like player-initiated scenes, shared world-building and descriptive tags for characters. But at the same time it has very tactical combat with positioning, stated-up bosses, special moves, power progression and other elements that feel more trad-gamey.
