Bloody Roots

Christian_Griffen
February 2006 edited February 2006 in Story Games
I want a Bloody Roots RPG. Here's the basic idea:

You play native inhabitants of a conquered land. It might be in South America, or Africa, or even a made-up place. The important part is this: your people have been conquered and oppressed. The "more civilized" outsiders have taken your land and your property, and try to take your pride and dignity as well. It's time to strike back.

Here's the deal, though: this game is about how far you'd go to drive them out. What is the limit of vengeance? Does the end justify all means?

First, you set up your culture and the invading one. Determine the level of oppression. Make a character who is connected to the suffering of your people.

You're fighting against time, because as the oppression goes on, your people become complacent. There will be some sort of measure of this; let's call it the Machine. You roleplay and introduce NPCs on both sides, establishing a local representation of the national issue as well as a dynamic relationship map. Having connections empowers you, and if your connections get hurt, you gain Rage that allows you to strike harder at the outsiders.

When you take action, you have the chance of stemming the tide. The more cruel and vicious your action is, the more chance you have to fight back the Machine. You get bonuses if you hurt those on the oppressor side who don't directly oppress: especially their children. Those actions are more likely to succeed. Also, you gain bonuses if you strike fear into your own people so that they support your fight for freedom (death to all traitors!).

In between your actions, the Machine retaliates, but it cannot use force beyond what you've used. So once you start hurting their children, they can get to yours. And they gain points for the Machine, in a pre-determined way (i.e., D6 points each time or whatever). If the Machine reaches a certain level, it wins and the fight is lost; or, if it's fought back to 0, revolution sweeps the land and your people are free (though I think that the actual endgame situation might then also depend on how cruel you were, i.e., you might slide right into a dictatorship of your own people).

When it looks like the Machine will prevail, how far will you go to preserve your way of life?
