\n Hello Everybody,

Last week I and several others had a conversation about how we can all keep connected and aware of each other\u2019s work now that the publisher, connections, and convention forums on the Forge have been deactivated. It reminded me of an earlier conversation I participated in years ago with Vincent, Clinton, NinjaJ, and a bunch of others after the Theory and GNS forums closed.

Time and time again, any time somebody has said they wanted something we, as a community, have always said, \u201cbe the change you want to see.\u201d As difficult a pill that can be to swallow, I swallowed it and set to work. If this is something people would be interested in, awesome. If not, well, I\u2019m only out a few days\u2019 work:

I present: The Forge\u2019s Legacy Neighborhood.

Let me tell you what this place is, or what I hope it will be. First, it\u2019s a place for all the displaced publisher forums from the Forge to have a home. Anyone who independently publishes RPGs can have a forum here. I always thought it was nice to browse the publisher forums on the Forge and keep up with everything. I hope this place can provide the same service. Second, it\u2019s a place to gather in all the great work being done on RPG play, design, theory, and publishing. I have pages on there with RSS feeds from some terrific websites including podcasts and YouTube channels. Half this stuff I wasn\u2019t even really aware of until I started this project. So I wanted to create a site that brought it all into one place. Mutualism was a huge part of what made the Forge- especially the Forge booth- so great. Third, I want this to be a place where people can still make connections. I want artists, booth organizers, publishers, gamemasters, and so on and so on to be able to get together on this site and help each other out.

Now let me tell you what The Neighborhood isn\u2019t. It isn\u2019t a replacement for The Forge or for Story Games. There is an actual play forum, but that\u2019s more of an homage to the Forge. This site is not the new \u201cit\u201d place to go and hang out together. Story Games is ideal for that, and I don\u2019t want to take anything away from the great thing Andy has built here. In fact, I have an RSS feed for SG on The Neighborhood just so people can quickly find and join threads from there. As I hope that publishers who choose to participate in The Neighborhood direct people who buy and play their games to the Neighborhood site, they\u2019ll stumble on the Story Games feed and come here to talk more about RPGs.

Finally, let me just explain what\u2019s there. I tried to go with a sort of Newspaper/Newsmagazine motif for the site. So for instance, the News page is the feed from Ogre Cave and RPGnet News. The Radio page is a list of podcasts that talk about indie rpgs and actual play. The TV page is a little bare right now, but I do have feeds from Bully Pulpit Games, The Walking Eye, and The Burning Wheel. I hope this page grows as the ease of using Flip Cameras becomes more apparent. I also have a Chat room that\u2019s not great, but serviceable and a guest book for newcomers to give us a shout out. Consider these the streets and houses of the neighborhood.

The only way this site will work, though is if people want to use it, if publishers want their forums there. So if you\u2019re interested, let me know. If there\u2019s something you\u2019d like me to add to the site, let me know. I\u2019m wide open to feedback at this point. Is there a podcast or video cast I missed? Tell me. Is there something on there that shouldn't be? Tell me. Is there another forum I need to add? Tell me. Speaking of that, right now I just have the forums open to RPG publishers, just like the Forge did. But I\u2019m also considering making a third section of forums below the publishers for \u201cproducers.\u201d That would be folks like artists, webmasters, editors, cartographers, art directors, playtesters, and convention organizers. What do you think? Would that make the forums too cluttered?

If you\u2019d like to help bankroll this thing, whisper me and we\u2019ll exchange emails and phone numbers. Right now, I\u2019ve got the site set on free hosting. That means there\u2019ll be ads and stuff like that, and the bandwidth isn\u2019t very much. Upgrading it a little is fairly cheap (like $4 a month) and that would get us our own site name without that \u201c.webs\u201d part. But there\u2019s a tiered system of upgrades that progressively get more expensive. I wouldn\u2019t mind having some help on the financial end of things if I can figure out if co-sharing the site is even possible. It might not be. In which case, I\u2019ll just have to suck it up and do it myself.

That\u2019s pretty much all I have to say about it for now. The introduction on the home page is awful. I just don\u2019t have the words to express my hopes and feelings on it right now. I\u2019ll throw it open to anybody. If you feel like you could write a great introduction telling people what our neighborhood is all about, I\u2019ll put it up there and credit you with it. Anyway, let me know what you think of the site. Is it functional? Would you go there? Is there anything missing/what other features would you like to see?

Peace,

-Troy

Importantly, this includes a place where independent publishers can have a forum and speak directly to users who may be interested in their games or have questions about games they already bought.
