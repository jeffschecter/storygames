So now you read the play report, here is the dry and academic thoughts as Dungeon Mistress about the first ever THE STARS BURN IN OUR EYES:Frilled warrior saga game on g+
Also this post has some behind the wizards curtain secrets of the dm stuff so if you don't wanna have your child like wonder ruined may do not read on.
5 is a lot of people for this kind of game. It's not unmanageable but shapes the game into far more of a sugar high. Also coz Tina had a non-moving avatar face I forgot about her in combat a couple of times. Dang Soz +Tina Rowand Also my notes for the game were maybe 5 lines of text (treppanner steampunk dude freezing people in subway, t-rex, albino alligators, flamingo hatchet gang, transformer ghost train guardian I think was it in it's entirety ) and a rough stat block for these. Which is totally my homeplane but maybe did not have enough left over concentration to keep track of how some of the less experienced players were going . +Jenn Parnell how did you find it? Thought maybe you were unsure of what your character could do in combat , and would of liked to give you some pointers but was juggling far too much chaos.
Sweet sweet Chaos.
My rule of thumb for this game was that if it was animated as a tv show would it be awesome to watch? This session was a total success in that regard I do believe.
One thing I wanna experiment with is the dither factor.
Dither is the bits were the characters are scheming , reacting, fucking around and procrastinating. Which is very fertile ground for fun, and figured in this kind of game that these bits were a good chance to ground the characters in the enviroment.
Now in g+ it seems, it's not really that great for characters to start off in conversations and arguments in twos and threes. You either are addressing everyone or your not talking. So I did not want stuff to go on too much with just a couple of people directing the Dither. Felt people might get bored who were not getting involved. If it was face to face I would have a better feel for it, but g+ is its own animal.
And I'm a g.m who has a habit of throwing something through the window on fire or going time skip your are all over here now because it was getting boring. But maybe need to let dithering around and treading water happen more?
Because I'm all curious about how to go about encouraging a sense of a reactive real world environment . While still have it be violently derailed by near cartoon mayhem and surrealist horror. So figured the more characters get a chance to shoot the breeze in character, say oh Imma going shopping, I wanna check on my moms, I'm late for school now etc the more it will feel like a real world.
ANyway see how a game goes with 2 or 3 people with more dither. Maybe it play the same, that's cool, fun was had by everyone. Huzzah. Also gonna stat up some Random consquence tables with swat teams , cops , buildings on fire, gangs defending territory, stray toxic waste truck that I can roll on when the characters start forgetting that they are in a crowded city and not a dungeon. Also really want to see what play is like in closed enviroment like a cruise ship or building stolen by mole people. So actually like a dungeon.
Think maybe there will be games played with a regulars that are more meandering and then all in games with 4-6 people with a lot more "okay so theres this and this and this happening, whadda guys doing? Okay you are all going here? Bam , you's are all here, you see cat people with a fire truck. The fire truck appears to be filled full of liquid nitrogen which they are hosing everything down with. "
Viva la Fun and Player Agency!
