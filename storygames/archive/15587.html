    Posted By: Orly[...] playing Technocrats is way more sympathetic and heroic than playing Tradition Mages ever was. It's like if UN Peacekeepers, CDC researchers, Interpol agents and NASA astronauts were superheros. Plus, the the day-to-day struggles (intra-agency/ministry/department/office politics and romances, paperwork, late nights in the lab, families, etc) of the PCs seem much more human and relateable than those of other games.
    I played a nearly year-long Mage 2/Technocracy campaign a while back, and it was a real eye-opener for the whole group. I can't recommend it highly enough. It's the game M:tA could have been in the first place. In fact, when I read about the Technocracy in Mage 1e, it already seemed to me that they would make better heroes than the Traditionalists, a cowardly and superstitious lot. By the time the later Techno splats came out, that impression was cemented, and the Guide to the Technocracy is one of WW's crowning achievements, and a welcome acknowledgement that Mage was the worst of their games for playing pale, secretive, nocturnal outsiders. (Ghouls: Fatal Addiction, by the way, nailed down that role even better than its parent game, and also gets a top recommendation if you feel like White Wolfing.)

    TL:DR - You should consider playing a Technocracy campaign, or maybe checking out the Ghouls sourcebook. And I'm personally itching to play Dungeon World or Monsterhearts among newer games, and My Life with Master among classics.
