\n I'm running a game of "Fiasco" at a local family game store this coming Saturday and I'm looking for suggestions. Lately, each time I have
run "Fiasco" at an event or for friends, I have presented a binder of several choices (about 5) all with Insta-setups (which I have tweaked
on occasion). I have twice in the last yr run "Booksellers of London", it's done well both times, and I will probably still make it one of the
choices. My goal is to keep the game no more than PG or PG13, even if we allude to more, I want the concepts and scenes to not cross
that line. I'm running the game at a really late hour but the store's niche in the community is for being family friendly, and personally
at any public gaming I want to keep at PG13 or less.

I don't like slasher movies, so I'm probably not going to include "Camp Death", but I plan to include the "Hocus Focus" (Dresden), "Manna
Hotel" with some slight alternations to the Insta-setup play-set, and I was looking for a couple more suggestions. Once before I played
"Manna Hotel" with some friends, before playing we tweaked the Insta-set up to incorporate elements of "The Lost Room" movie; it came
out pretty well. What I'm looking for is more a feeling of creepy ("Lost Room", "The Shining", and "Unknown Armies") and I will probably
make Halloween the back drop date in both the Fiasco game and the "Zombie Cinema" game I'm running; as this Saturday IS the Halloween
gaming event at the game store. So if anyone has a play-set to recommend or some ideas to make the "Manna Hotel" Insta-set up more
creepy, pls let me know.
Thanks,
Shannon

    \n Since you are already planning on taking some pre-game time to doctor up existing playsets, why not just take it a step further and write your own new playset? That way, you can include the exact elements you want (including age ratings) and the players still get the full Setup experience (one of my favorite parts of Fiasco). And who knows -- maybe your new playset will have some life outside of next week's game!
