\n So I just peeped the new trailer for The Revenant if you haven't seen it, I'd definitely check it out. Anyway, it got me thinking - it would be fun to run a 1 to 3 shot game of survival horror in the early American wilderness. Where the players would have to battle it out with wild animals, angry native americans, other trappers, snow, wind, rain, disease and all the other things out there. I'm looking for something that could really frame the horror/terror element of it all and have a high body count. Any ideas?

    \n Including angry Native Americans is only iffy if your audience isn't on board with your representation. This sounds like a homegame project, not something you're planning on publishing, so general public reaction is not an issue. That said... How weird do you wanna get?

    Thomas Pynchon's "Mason & Dixon" depicts the untamed North American continent of the 18th-century imagination, a sort of "magical realism" in which you might encounter a talking salmon, or a pumpkin the size of a house.

    Most aboriginal tribes possessed several types of shamans or magic workers, some of whom were capable of miraculous feats such as flying, clairvoyance, dream divination and turning into animal form.

    Then you've got your mythic monsters... Everything from Sasquatch to Mothman to Chupacabra to Wendigo, etc.
