\n Whenever we play Montsegur 1244, the rule about choosing your main character in the first scene feels wrong.

One of the beautiful things about Montsegur is that characters grow in importance over time. You didn't think you were interested in Philippa, but you gradually realise she's your most interesting character.

The problem is: she's only a secondary character. So, at the end of the game, you narrate whether Bertrand, your main character, burns at the stake. But you don't really care about him. You care about Philippa now.

Is there a hidden reason for this rule I'm missing? I know it enables the additional rule that You Can't Kill Someone Else's Main Character Without Permission. But that doesn't seem a good enough justification.

    \n I tend to think that, after the 15-minute break, there should be a chance to change your main character. Just one chance.

    Or, even, that that should be the time you choose your main character. In the first two acts, you don't have a main character and nobody can be killed without consent. From Act Three onwards, you must commit.

    The thing is: I like the formality of having one main character. So, while we could always bend the rules, I'd rather the rules allowed us to change main characters.

    I'm interested in passing around secondary characters. In some way, I like the formality of that rule, too: if one of your characters is in a scene, the others cannot be there, and you must explain why.

    \n Hi Graham,

    It is a creative constraint. Here are three things I like about the rule:\n
      \n
    1. The rule is easy to communicate to new players - there are no exceptions or special cases.
      2. \n
    2. The characters introduced in the beginning of the story are those which stories are resolved at the end.
      3. \n
    3. There is no big group discussion up front - every player makes one smaller choice in turn, possibly as a consequence of what players before them have chosen and what have been created in the fiction so far.
      4. \n
    \nConsider your secondary characters tools for exploring the main characters of the other players.

    However, if you try out the option of changing characters after the break, let me know how it goes. It may have some interesting side effects.

    /Frederik
