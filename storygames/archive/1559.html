    I agree on the solo thing, but disagree on the foot-chopping bit. I think there's something here that's potentially awesome, something that may be almost entirely lacking in maiming.

    There are dancing movies, right? Everyone knows Dirty Dancing, and probably a lot of us know about the string of Latin dance-movies that have been around forever. So, what do these things have in common? From what I can remember, they all have at least these three things;

    1. Morality issues from the anti-dance people. There are others who think that your dancing is wrong-fun, and will do whatever it takes to stop you.

    2. Macho one-upmanship. Even when the guys are occationally violent with each other, the central realm of conflict remains; Who do our peers think is the best dancer? This shows up as often in the females of the genre, maybe even more often. It's the same thing, just with a different label. "Catty" comes to mind.

    3. Romance & seduction. The very reasons why the morality antagonists want you to stop that infernal dancing. Cuz it gets you all sweaty and horny. Someone's getting laid after a good dance, and everyone knows it. Or, in the PG-world, there's gonna be a big wet smooch. Same thing.

    You take those three ingredients, stir them well into a design, and I think you'll have something awesome.

    I like Fever as a stat. We need some tension where both high and low Fever can hurt you. A high Fever means amazing Limbo mojo, but it should also put the character on overdrive, a mechanically enforced inability to stop pushing his Issue. Doing this while preserving the character's protagonism may be challenging. If we use conflict resolution and FITM mechanics, perhaps each opponent in non-Limbo conflict gets the other's Fever in dice on top of his own relevant attributes, and is encouraged to play up the other's Fever-driven responses?

    Alternately, we could make Fever a positive as long as it lasts. Fever dice add to the pool available, and can be gained by success at any task with 1) a difficulty equal to or greater than the current Fever score and 2) sacrificing some aspect of the character's life to pursue Limbo greatness. My idea is that within a certain range, it's possible to really go on a tear and rev up the Limbo Fever. However, any time a character uses Fever dice to aid him in a Conflict and still fails, the Fever Breaks. The character suffers a moment of clarity in which he realizes how much he's sacrificed and how little he's gained. At that point, Fever resets to half its previous score. No single aspect of the character's life can be sacrificed more than once, so if Our Limbo Hero wants to make a comeback, he has to rebuild his life so that he'll once again have something to sacrifice. My worry is the IC/OOC divide - the character in the SIS is just trying to find something to live for beyond the circle, so will it break things somehow if the player is cold-bloodedly acquiring nice things that he can sacrifice later?
