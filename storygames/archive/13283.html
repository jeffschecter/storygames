\n Ok, straight to the point: This is The night face up, a two-player little game less than three pages long, based on the short story by the same name written by the very great Julio Cort\xe1zar.

This one is a playtesting version. More precisely it is a translation draft of the playtesting version. You see, I am not a native English speaker - (you didn't see that coming, did you? Well probably you did). I wrote the thingie a year ago in Spanish, and got lots of help and some positive feedback from fellow forum members at Salgan al Sol, a nice place to discuss story games matters if you are a Spanish speaker. But the game still needs more playtesting and some friendly beating.

So here you go. Read it, playtest it, comment it (or not). And because it is a rough translation you get to correct my grammar big time! You wouldn't miss an opportunity like that, would you?

    \n Maybe it's a little late for this but I think I should have told something more about the game. I mean I asked some fellows "shouldn't I have explained a little about the system and stuff"? And they were all like: duh!

    So: In this game there are two players, two worlds and just one player character called George K. who lives in both. In one world one player controls George K. and the other one acts as Game Master, and then in the other world they exchange their roles. The thing is near the end of the game one of these realities is going to be proven "the right one" and the other some kind of illusion, so the players compete trying to win the right to determine which one is which. There is a little more to the game of course, but you should read the pdf for that, shouldn't you?

    And it won a contest! "Primero tira despu\xe9s hablamos" it was called. It was a very little contest, but I feel all glamorous now.

    Of couse even if you are not playtesting the game grammar and sintaxis corrections, pointing out confusing portions of the text, criticism and witty one-liners are all welcomed.

    But speaking of the minimum number of sequences: It is just 3 (plus the final sequence). For this to happen a player has to win all those three sequences, and in at least 2 of them she must win two points instead of just one. I don't know how often this can happen, but 3 sequences is probably too little, so I should look into that.

    \n I wanted the "game starters" to hint a world, to hint George's place in that world, to say something about how George's visions could be interpreted within that world, and of course to be at least decent kickers. And I wanted all that while telling as little as possible, because I felt a good deal of ambiguity was necessary.

    So yes, I feel it is a feature. And the fact that the knight story seems more straightforward doesn't bother me. There is still plenty of unexplained stuff and I don't feel the worlds need to have the exact same degree of ambiguity. But who knows.
