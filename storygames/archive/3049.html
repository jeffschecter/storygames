    \n The disconnect you're experiencing is because you're expecting historical fantasy.

    There is nothing historical about this. This is going to be a fantasy world, invented whole-cloth. Imagine Middle-Earth, if Tolkien's sources were Westerns and Wuxia, instead of Anglo-saxon culture, Norse legend and the Finnish Kalevala..

    The elements of "western" and "eastern" will both be present.

    ...and yes, Ben. Thank you for your "good wishes." Yes, I said that design journals are largely efforts in marketing. That doesn't mean that every time I talk about one, I have to put up with snarky comments about sales.

    \n Yeah, I'm not 100% happy with that font, either. I hadn't heard of Kenrick's project, but searched after you mentioned it....and now I see that someone on the LJ compared the artwork to Keith Senkowski's, who I now found out is doing the art for Kenrick. Because they are not worthy of the respect to do so. They're just a bunch of dogs ready to jump, rend and tear), then you are mis-using this entire site.

      My concern there is this:

      GMSkarka says that the members of the community are good enough to Plug At, but they're worthless to try to Talk To. Folks try to say, "Hey, I'm not going to dogpile you: I really do want to hear what you have to say on this issue!" and get a slap in the face: I'm just going to post statements here. If you want to talk to me, you have to do so off-site.

      Treating others with respect, even when disagreeing, is what the site is all about. If you want to talk at people while convincing yourself that there is absolutely no reason to treat them with respect (enough to open up a conversation on the subject, to field questions, to get feedback), then why bother?

      The difference is that others who promote things here, hell even Malcolm Sheppard (who had been far more antagonistic towards this site before he joined, and of course us to him), have also contributed to other discussions while promoting. They haven't said, "Fuck, these guys are all zealots and not worth my time trying to talk to them. But hey, I'll still keep promoting my stuff at them."

      Directed promotion? Cool. That's why the category is there.

      Personally, I'm still interested in the project overall, especially given GMS' interest and knowledge of the material, plus the idea that it might be developed into its own system.
