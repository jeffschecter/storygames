\n(Trying to follow Jason's advice and make my writing a bit more welcoming. But if there's something you don't understand, please ask questions and me or someone else here will answer.)

I've said before how much I love game systems where there's just a certain chance to succeed, a certain chance to fail, and that's it. No four outcomes, no fail forward, just "Can I do it? Oh, I guess not."
But since I'm procrastinating on prep and thinking I want to make Dungeons and Dragons play a bit more like Dungeon World, I was reading the houserules at the back of The Seclusium of Orphone of the Three Visions and they remind me a lot of Apocalypse World. They are specifically for LotFP, where your mod is usually between -1 and +1.

I looked up the optional rules for partial success in the Dungeon Master's Guide. It suggested a difficulty class of 10 for one thing happening and 15 for another, better thing happening. Seems like a clean enough way to express it with no subtraction (i.e. "If you make your roll by so-and-so much, this happens"). But that means the partial successes will only happen 25% of the time. Looking at the particular examples they had in the DMG, that seemed appropriate. (When talking to a queen, DC 10 to not get thrown in the dungeon, DC 15 to get her help.)
But looking at the examples in Dungeon World and in The Seclusium of Orphone of the Three Visions (and looking back to my own memories of playing Dungeon World and Apocalypse World), the partial successes felt like they happened like a little over 41% of the time, with a "full success" only happening like a sixth of the time or so.

So that suggests that the difficulty classes for the two results should be eight steps from each other, to replicate that. Most of D&D seem to be calculated around having +7 in the areas where you're the most proficient, which you get at level five or so. +4 in the ability, +3 on the proficiency bonus. I am enamored with D&D's new policy of having fixed difficulty, that doesn't escalate by level like it did in the previous edition and I want to keep that. That means first level characters will have a little bit of a harder time and later level characters will have an easier time. This matches Apocalypse World.
Although... in Apocalypse World, you have +2 in your most proficient areas in the beginning, and at the end you pretty much have +3 in everything (or you're in a muddy grave singing the rust song).

At +2, that means you get a partial success on a natural 5 through 7 roll (still just over 41%) and you get a complete success on a natural 8 through 12 roll (also just over 41%).
That suggests in D&D terms that the DC for "partial success" should be 12 and the DC for "full success" should be 20.
For simple pass/fail checks, I'll still use 15. This is only when I have a "move" available a la DW or The Seclusium of Orphone of the Three Visions.
