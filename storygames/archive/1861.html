    Midnight is one of the best d20 settings out there: imagine a Middle Earth where Sauron won, the point-based magic system kicks ass, and the new mechanics are incredibly flavourful. The book on sale is a 256-pages colour hardback. Though the spell list in 2E was adjusted to fit D&D 3.5, you can find lists of the changes online.

    Blue Planet is hard sci-fi in a world whose surface is 97% water. There's a "gold rush" of sorts, ancient mysteries, cyberpunk-style crime and corps, Wild West atmosphere, etc. Jurgen Hubert puts it second only after Transhuman Space (which he worked on) when it comes to his favourite hard sci-fi games. Oh, you can also play dolphins and killer whales.

    Both of them have very positive reviews online.

  • Brand_Robins

    Fireborn is a game that had much in awesome ideas around what play should be, then didn't quite put enough effort and time into developing them into what they could have been. The massive list of erratta is just one small sign of this, the way that fighting combos do and do not work is another, and so on it goes down the line.

    The setting, alas, was much less awesome than it should have been.

    I love Dragons, "lost worlds and last lives" RP (Changeling, Exalted, etc), and funky crunchy combat systems, and I was totally jazzed when I heard that Fireborn was coming out. Now, with the books in hand, I can't ever quite get up the energy to even seriously propose playing a game of it.

    Blue Planet has ben described, and I'll add that even if you aren't going to like the Blue Planet system the setting is full of nifty cool bits that are totally worth ripping off for home grown SF games or games of Shock or whatever. At 5 bucks a book it's tempting to pick them all up just to have a ready stack of aqua-SF color ready to go.
