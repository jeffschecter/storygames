\n Hi all: Andy here.

Unfortunately, the S-G Wiki was hacked for the gillionth time, this time with rogue users adding almost 10gb of data, crashing my dbspace.

Keeping up with Wiki security is a battle of tactics and strategy and time the likes of which WW2 battles were fought and won. @misuba (Mike Sugarbaker) has been fighting valiantly for years, but he's moved on to bigger and more important projects.

So I'm looking to move the S-G wiki to a place or service that is 3rd party, so that someone else can do all that user management/spam-fighting jazz for us. Currently, the likeliest candidate is Google Sites (it doesn't appear to be marked for end of life), with a runner up being the Free version of wikispaces.com.

* If someone has a better solution than Google Sites, please let me know!

* We can probably get some people to pitch in for heavy lifting, but we could still use a manager/overseer of a new S-G Wiki. Anyone want to volunteer to be such an admin? You'd have complete control over the look and direction of the wiki.

Feel free to chime in in this thread, or email me in private (ziggurat atmark gmail).

Thanks!
-Andy

    \n Mike: Yeah, that's interesting. I found the maker's own Federated Wiki and played with it for a few minutes. It's good stuff! However, I think that even while I want something community-editable, there's a little bit of top-down architecture needed here re a wiki, to serve as an entry point to this fringe hobby. Sort of like how a minecraft community might have everyone doing their own thing totally, but then one guy (the server owner) says "Hey, how about we grow plants over here, and create a Starship Enterprise replica over there?", and through minimal work slowly coordinate the larger data flow, while still totally allowing a playspace for people to create all sorts of awesome craziness.

    Wordman: Indeed. Mike actually had a lot of that locked down. Unfortunately, and this is my bad: There was a mySQL update on my server/provider that left the wiki dead, to get it working again I had to overwrite a new version of MediaWiki... which basically undid all the user access controls and spam prevention. I even remember thinking at the time, "Oh, I need to come back to this in a week or two, because this is a ripe target for spammers; perhaps initiate the migration very soon!" Then my Kickstarter began, and I forgot like Everything I Meant To Do. :-)
