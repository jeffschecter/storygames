\n I'm trying to work up a set of three drives or motivators... things that'll promote certain kinds of actions and discourage others based on how they total relative to one another.

FEAR and ANGER are sort of obvious... fear makes you jumpy, edgy, sneaky, ready to bolt. Anger makes you aggressive, thoughtless, direct, and violent. And then what would the third thing do? I'm just stumped.

There would be ten points divided between these drives, and points would be shifted around among them based on certain role/frame combos in scenes, based on some mechanical outputs, and on some player or GM decisions. One of the simple outcomes I'd like from this would be a sense of conspiring against one's own character to bring the drama AND to gain some mechanical whammy. Get kicked now so yea might do the kicking later. Or, be the bastard now, so you can be the hero later or something like this.

More on this stuff later...

Right now, I need to figure out what would make a good third after FEAR and ANGER... something which could logically become more or less driving based on how the character is cast in a given scene. For example, being cast as the PATSY and taking one for the plot means you'll shift from Fear to Anger.

If it will help, the roles are HERO, HEEL, PATSY. Basically, the badass, the bastard, or the bitch.

What might be a good third, and what would shifts to and from it mean?

My brain thanks you in advance.

-Ben

    \n Most of the Greek 'heroes' as well... they alternate between being jerks, being abused by the plot, and being badasses.

    The alternative I had was more binary (and possibly more thematic for my setting... so, go with the mechanics I find more compelling or the ones which fit better?) would be a rift between DISSONANCE and CONFIDENCE.

    You get Dissonance when you act in ways which run counter to your sense of self (which fits wit the resolution mechanics), and you get Confidence when you act in ways which reinforce or are consistent with your sense of self. Relative difference in the two totals (which was bigger, whether one or the other was zero) would have mechanical upshots, and points could be spent off then for other things.

    This doesn't link up with the scene framing stuff I'm messing with though.

    \n If you're going to use emotions as drives you need to make them attractive or they will lack the compulsive property of all drives.
    \n
    \nAnger - desire to punish the blameworthy (this is rarely a thoughtless emotion). Fear - desire to remove the vulnerable from potential harm. Remorse - desire to atone and where possible make ammends.
    \n
    \nAnger and fear are good choices because anger (like love) moves a person closer to the object of their emotion. While fear does the opposite by increasing the distance between the fearful and the object of their fear.
    \n
    \nI believe that remorse is a good choice for the third drive as it can be result of misplaced fear or anger.

    \n The problem may be in limiting yourself to three drives. If you move away from restricting yourself to three drives you could have something like:-
    \n
    \nThe patsy starts with complacency and ends in fear. The hero starts with grief and ends in indignation. The heel starts in resentment or contempt and ends in frustration.
    \n
    \nAlternatively, you could start with the drives and let them inform what the roles are going to be:-
    \n
    \nFear that leads to anger is the path of a....
    \nX that leads to fear is the path of a....
    \nAnger that leads to X is the path of a....
    \n
    \nSubstitute X for the drive of your choice
    \n
    \nWhich brings me to another subject. Are roles absolutely necessary? Might dispositions better suit your purpose? The game I am making (which I hope to release early next year began with fortes (e.g. classes) and after much careful playtesting and editing those classes became leanings (e.g. dispositions). Anyway, its something thats probably worth considering.

    \nUh, I actually think this could totally work.

    Guilt can turn into Fear... when you find out you're about to discovered and/or punished. You become the "Scoundrel", maybe.

    Guilt can also turn into Anger... when you decide that you were wrong to feel Guilty, and you should just get in there and take whatever it is you were feeling Guilty about. Or maybe you realize you need to stand up to whoever was making you feel Guilty.

    Maybe I'm missing something, but both of those sound like classic character behaviors. For instance, imagine you're playing MLwM. You're the Minion, and you feel Guilty because of the bad stuff you've been doing... so it turns to Anger, and you kill the Master! Or, you're the Minion and you feel Guilty because you've been whispering behind the Master's back and making plans to kill him... but then he sees through you, so you feel Fear and you run away, with his hounds hot on your tail.

    I think the other four combinations work out nicely too! That could make two different cycles, or some sort of Roshambo thing.
