\n For "That's Drama" I've been continuing to work on these "motivation cards", the subject of this thread: http://story-games.com/forums/comments.php?DiscussionID=13701
I've playtested them several times and they're pretty cool, if I do say so myself. They could be used for a lot of games - any game where you're supposed to come up with a goal or two for your character before play...

I've got about 18 vague motivations which you roll a die to get more specific with, for example:

You want a closer relationship with them, but ...
1) They're too busy.
2) They don't feel that way about you.
3) They're not ready for children.
4) There's too much bad history there.
5) They don't know you exist.
6) They're involved with someone else.
Do you get it?

But I want more!

So ... same question as my other That's Drama thread. Should I put out the list I've got so far, so you can say "I can't believe you forgot X", or should I let you all go at it, unpoisoned?
