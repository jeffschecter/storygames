    I've made one solid read-through of the BRS doc, and there's one thing it needs in great profusion:

    \n

    Examples. A vast and mighty multitude of examples, one for every major mechanical bit, which puts a couple on every page at this point. Plus a fully-developed script of "how play works" at the end with an extended example where not everything works. Best example (heh) of that kind of bit I know of is in Nobilis, where the in-play extended example has a couple moments of just confusion and "judgement calls" picked out as such.

    \n

    Honestly, I can read the things over and over, but the best guides to how it's supposed to look would be some back-and-forth examples, plus a little bit more work invested in diagrams for areas like moving dice around the battle-mat, allocating them, etc. That last is largely just heavy drudge-work and not much fun, but it adds so much it's hard to pass on for such a visceral mechanical system.

    \n Thanks to some great feedback from the fine LA Games crew, I've updated the playtest doc to v3.1
    \n
    \nAnyone thinking of running a session should download the updated rules from the Ramshead site.
    \n
    \nChanges include:
    \n
    \nBetter definition around what can be challenged
    \nA reversed Contest system which eliminates the need for the klunky Immunity rules
    \nToning down the overpowered Blessed
    \nRandomized damage (Battered vs. Devastated) which actually returns to an earlier version of the rules...sometimes streamlining doesn't work.
    \nAmping up the hero Vow mechanics
    \nCompletely changing the way Weaknesses and Oaths work to be more like Oaths in Agon...thus continuing the "build this game from parts of other games" design philosophy.
