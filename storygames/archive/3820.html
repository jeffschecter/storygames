\n Over the weekend I had an idea for a conflict resolution mechanic for an upcoming Cyberpunk Hacking-Action LARP I'm trying to organize in my town and maybe a Convention or two. Everyone has a MP3 player on their person, pre-loaded with a 20-song custom playlist assembled by the GMs. Each player is set to shuffle, and to resolve a conflict you hit next a few times and compare songs. Success and Outcome is based on comparision of track length (the final digit, specifically), playlist position, and affinities for certain genres and styles determined at character creation.

Another idea in the same vein would actually be to have a central location (GM desk) where a stereo is playing a random set of music. Every character (or faction?) has an affinity to a certain style of music, and their success and outcome versus other affinities is based on whatever song is currently playing. Different characters could have abilities to pause, repeat, skip, stop, etc the music, and perhaps satellite locations could be set up, with different chances of outcome based on proximity, clash, and so on.

    \n I do see at least one major obstacle in the "Centralized Stereo" idea: multiple groups working to resolve things at once. Say you have two groups of players in two separate "time bubbles" of actions (aka, two separate initiatve instances). Both instances could easily feature people who affect the play of the music to affect the happenings of the instances in which they are involved, and this could lead to clashing over which takes precedence.

    Then again, it could be encouraged as such, with a focal point of the mechanics being that there are always others than yourself affecting the music, and when you affect the music youaffect it for everyone else as well.
