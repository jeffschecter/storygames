\n So, sometimes the WoD kinda pisses me off. Like, I love Changeling, okay? It's totally my favorite of the new WoD. One of the things I totally like is the Mask. The annoying issue in the old Changeling of "Well, physically, am I really like my fae-self or my human self?" is answered firmly and conclusively in the new one. That's good. the Mask is also useful in that it nicely takes care of the "Why don't normal people know?" issue. And that's important when you're playing changelings.

But when you're not, it's kind of a pain in the rear. It's almost TOO effective. I'm pretty sure it's the most effective "shield from mortal eyes" type effect in the nWoD. There's very little chance, outside of GM fiat, that any mortal will see through the Mask without the changeling wanting them to. This makes things kind of... awkward when the PCs are mortal hunters. I mean, if a changeling more or less always looks human, what makes him seem any different to a mortal hunter from, say, a mage? He's got funky powers, but he just looks like a dude.

I guess what I'm looking for from the smart people that might be listening is advice on two things:
1) Suggestions on how I might hint at the otherworldly and fae nature of a changeling (or hob or True Fae) without necessarily letting the hunter PC actually look through the Mask.
2) Suggestions on good times/circumstances to allow the PC to completely see through the Mask (at least for a moment) without the fae-critters complicity. Currently I already have two ideas for this. For one, I'll probably allow any detection/perception-based Endowments to work for fae stuff, or let them buy versions that work for fae-stuff without going through the mechanical R&D procedure. After all, the Hunter book tends to assume they'll be hunting a variety of things, and generally downplays fae-stuff a lot. (only one PC has access to any Endowments at the moment anyway, and that's Advanced Armory stuff but, for example, I'm thinking of having someone in TFV offer him a "prototype" variation on the Etheric Goggles that allows him to spot fae-type stuff, at least, you know, once he files some reports on things. Not gonna just give it to him as a total freebie.) The other idea I had was possibly allowing a glimpse through the Mask when a changeling activates a Contract or some other funky power.

Any thoughts?

    \nWell, I don't see this as a bad thing about Changelings, rather, a good thing about Mages. To a hunter every piece of magic whether originating in Changeling stuff, Mage stuff or even just Second Sight style demon-summoning and psychic stuff should cast a horrifically "wrong" pall over the scene.

    What happens in The Shining, exactly? Well, there may be some psychic stuff, and there may be some ghost stuff, and there may be some just plain old insanity stuff but in the end it doesn't really matter, the monster of the unknown keeps growing and escalating.

    Catching glimpses through the Mask is a cool idea and since your game is not yet a Changeling game, doing that is fine. From a campaign design perspective, as you point out, it's essentially a solution to several problems that you've identified as not being problems you want to mess with. So long as you don't encroach on those solutions (why mortals don't know, what they look like, etc.) then you are getting the benefit of the mechanic.

    I would keep careful track of situations in which the Mask slips, for the second half of your game, and watch to see when the PCs get into similar situations.

    \n So on first blush my instinct would be to run it TV-show-cinematic-style where when the hunters have done their research, know (more or less) what they're facing, and come to the final 10 minutes of the show with the climatic face-off, the FX guys finally get their time in the sun and BOOM the hunters just see through the mask for no terribly explained reason. In all the shows like Supernatural, Buffy, Dracula, whatever there will usually come a moment where the thing that could pass for fully human suddenly, for no really great reason, is revealed as not so human. So, doing the same thing would have a certain logic. (Even if it is refrigerator logic.)

    I mean, there should be emotional reasons why. The Hunters know what they're facing, they're up against something bad, and probably they should come in on the beastie while its doing something bad, or in its stronghold of badness, or both. Combining the knowledge, their special place as hunters, the emotional impact of the scene, and (possibly) the direct use of powers or nearness of the hedge (cause the Hedge can be just, wherever, right?) -- it should give just enough reason so that when the Mask suddenly comes down (even if just for a few seconds) it won't kill the flow of the scene.

    And, as JD says, when the PCs get on the other side of the stick, they can't be quite so sure that the Mask will always cover them.
