      \n I am reading all of these, and there are some very grand ideas in here!

      /aside
      So, I kinda cheated, a lot of my stuff is mostly stuff that I actually ran, designed, half-designed, etc:

      Voodoo Mecha - Originally I thought this idea had enough juice to be a full RPG or big campaign guide (in fact it was one of my original posts on the Forge, Google "Mekkazombi"). Later, I thought that it just had enough juice to be a Lady Blackbird style game. Now, after reading up a lot more on Voodoo ("Even Haitians pretty much think it's bullshit") than I did in the past, I'd need to redo it a lot in order to not be culturally retarded/"iffy". I'm now thinking more of C.S. Fredman's novel "This Alien Shore", where (long story short) Inuit migrants become the only humanoid race capable of piloting starships across the galaxy in a kind of Inuit-analogue-slipspace (allegories of cracks in ice, demons) ala "Spacing Guild" from Dune. Similar in feel/respect to that, with deep cultural influence (and not just "chicken bones and blood and shit, go!"), fighting a hybring Japan-Portugese dominant empire.

      Trek: Mobsters - From an idea stolen from Rafael Chandler (who ran this using LUG's Star Trek RPG rules), I've run this game three times using Primetime Adventures, it's my favorite setting for PTA to date: The Star Trek universe, just with assassins, mobsters, scoundrels, bodyguards, etc. Like Firefly or The Wire, but in Star Trek.

      Futurepunk Babylon - This is SPULTURATORAH. To note, I actually took the setting conceptually and blended it with the game Covenant d20, to make basically a biblical space opera game for a two-shot. The PCs were Egyptian and Jew heroes who helped Power Armor Moses run guns through the Red Sea split.

      Mistranslated Mecha - I wrote this in the middle of a Game Chef week, after having kind of a breakdown in the middle of translating some books and games from J->E (and seeing more "creative uses of English" in Japanese). It's "Take Back of Freedom!", and all the grammatical inconsistencies and errors aren't just "fakey Engrish", every single one is rooted in an actual grammatical issue/nuance between Japanese and English (many of which I corrected myself in the past)
      I originally stole this idea from a conversation with Jerry Grayson

      Buffy: 78AD - Note that this is the year before Pompeii. Maybe I'll give this a shot as a mini-campaign...
