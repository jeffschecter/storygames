    \n Hi Manuel, thanks for the query.

    Other Worlds has been on the backburner for a while but I'm back working on it now (and I mean right now - my other window is currently open to the section on sidekicks). I've had to rebuild the manuscript from an old print-out after my computer died, and I've been taking the opportunity to endlessly tinker with... I mean, purposefully revise... the text as I go. I've also been working in some of the lessons I learned from all the playtest feedback I got last year. I'm satisfied that, aside from a couple of little tweaks here and there, the design itself is basically finished. I'm hoping to get the final ms ready for editing over the next few weeks.

    The release of HQ2 hasn't affected anything on my end. I haven't read it, but I don't really like the sound of a lot of the changes they've made. I like augments and long lists of abilities dammit! I don't think that the criticisms people have been levelling at HQ2 really apply to my game.

    Thanks,

    Mark

    Posted By: Denys
    Here here!

    Making the game unfriendly to augments was not a good change at all. It's one of my fave bits about HQ.
    \nWhat I've tried to do with Other Worlds is address the problems with the lots of abilities/augments structure rather than move away from it. That's why each supporting ability after the first only has a fairly small effect, it's why failure is always supposed to be interesting rather than nullifying, and it's why you earn a spotlight point whenever you lose a conflict. The idea was basically to create an environment where players aren't afraid of losing and therefore don't spend too much time trawling for bonuses. I recognise that that didn't fully work out, which is why some people's games (including mine) sometimes got a bit too bogged down or maths-intensive. I've since added a new rule which I think does finally address the problem - you can only use a maximum of 3 supporting abilities per conflict, although you can spend 1 SP to ignore that restriction for the current conflict (i.e. if it's so important to the character or the story that spending some extra time going through all the variables really is worthwhile). I'm just about to start 2 different playtest campaigns so I guess I'll find out if it works or not very shortly.

    Mark

    Posted By: Denys
    Soviet -- I like your new rule about having to spend a spotlight point to include more augments. If it's *really* important, then you can tabulate.
    \nThanks. What I like about it is that it's sort of a double-edged sword. On the one hand it acts as a general limitation to enforce a certain amount of mandatory discretion. But on the other hand it also trusts players to identify those truly story-significant conflicts on their own, and then gives them explicit permission to really go to town with their descriptions without feeling like they are doing something dodgy. They paid for it, so it's OK.

    The only alternative would be for the GM to judge each conflict for 'story-worthiness' and that's just a whole can of worms I really want to avoid. Trusting players is good IMO.

    It does potentially change the spotlight point economy though, in terms of the effectiveness of buying new abilities and supporting characters. I might have to look at some of the costs again.
