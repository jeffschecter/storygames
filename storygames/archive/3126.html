    \n Well, there's a difference between what I'm doing for the con and how I'd do it "right." At the con I'm skipping Roles altogether. It's just distribute three points between styles, write down four skills, pick a major item, etc. I may give whoever plays The Doctor 5 Style points instead of three and, of course, madate that they put at least one point in Fantastic.

    Now, if I were doing a full Octane to Doctor Who conversion I would put back the Roles. I'd probably create one Role for each of the canon doctors (mostly varying skills and major items) and maybe another more generic Role for Other Doctors. I'd have Roles like Timelord, Modern Companion, Futuristic Companion, Alien Companion, etc. Since in the old show gender distinctions in companions was kind of important I might even make Male vs. Female versions of these Roles.

    One Role I'd really like see created and utilised is Non-Recuring Protagonist. I really like these characters in Doctor Who and they often get over looked. These are the characters who are already part of the situation when the Doctor Arrives, play a major role in the resolution of the situation and then are never seen again. Probably my favorite example from the new series is Nancy from "The Empty Child"/"The Doctor Dances" Again, in a full conversion I'd probably REQUIRE that someone always play the Non-Recuring Protagonist from story to story. I might mandate that the Role of The Doctor be played by someone different every session.

    The rule of Rock 'n' Roll becomes the rule of Theme Music. Every session must start and end with someone playing the Theme Music.

    But again, I'm not going to be this exacting for the con scenario. As to which Doctor, I'm going to leave that up to whoever decides to play him. They can pick a favorite incarnation or decide they're a fresh/different one.

    Otherwise, I really haven't changed the rules at all. That's the reason I picked Octane to begin with. It already feels like Doctor Who.

    Jesse

    Spending plot points to activate Might and Magic is the way the rules currently read (page 42, Case #2.) That's the part that seemed a crock to me: you're actually penalized if you take Might or Magic under the current rules. The mod changes this by basically just dropping Case #2 and letting you reduce hazards with your Might/Magic score.

    \n

    Notably, Blood & Steel also drops the "spend a plot point" requirement. For a Doctor Who game, I'd reserve plot points to allow players to invent details. "I recognize the homeworld of these aliens! It's Ginglefrazzart! Which means they are susceptible to common table salt!"

    \n I'll have to think about that. There is one other modification that I forgot to mention because I don't think of it as a modification. Strictly according to the rules die rolls and success/failure of character goals are unconnected. The die simply says who gets to decide and whether the other side can interject anything. I really dislike this. To the point that I don't ever really consider OctaNe functional if you play with this rule as it stands. So technically this is the rules mod:

    5 or 6 - Character succeeds, Player has full narration.
    4 - Character success but the GM can interject a twist.
    3 - Character fails but Player can interject a concession.
    2 or 1 - Character fails, GM is free to hose away!

    Jesse
