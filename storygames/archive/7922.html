    \n This... is not as easy a question as you might think.

    A quick note on "success" first, to give a sense of the scale I think on: Amagi has been successful (by my standards) right from the get-go as a blog - about 110-170 different visitors a day. With the new site, that's jumped up to 280-360. That's actual people; search spiders and the like are sorted out for me. They each read an average of 7.5 pages a visit.

    Now, the first purpose of the site was really simple: I have stuff. Little stuff like articles, big stuff like games, and stuff in between. I wanted to share it, and let people give me input, and let them share it, and so on.

    At some point, it got really obvious that wiki-type function would be handy on some of those things, at least potentially; rather than just giving feedback, people that were irked by a thing or wanted to expand on it could just do it. And I coul host files right on the site.

    Then, as soon as I actually had it, the thought occurred:

    There's no reason for me not to open up the offer much, much wider, and let people just add articles, games, do projects on the site, whatever they want to do. I needed a little playground for myself, and ended up with one that can basically accomodate as many people as want to mess about there; it's got slides and teeter-totters, all these gubbins and fiddly things. Oodles and buckets of stuff, and I haven't even turned on half the options.

    So, really, the place I'm at is one where I'm kind of casting around, trying to sort out "what do people want to collaborate on or share that they don't have a platform for?"... Because I have so much extra room, so many available tools, and such an amazingly open-to-fun-ideas audience, it seems like a horrible shame not to open it up.

    Er, if I'm making sense.

    (And, yes, I'm open to suggestions. If that wasn't obvious)

    \n (EDIT: There's no link here. Wow, I must be off my nut. Lemme fix that: Amagi Games

    Okay, a quick rundown.

    On the site, that block of links on the left? Those are all just wiki-links. Games is for listing games on-site.

    Gambits, Situation, and Advice are there to categorise any articles you drop on so they can be found fairly easily. If articles start showing up that need a new category, I'll made one and put in a shortcut for that, too.

    "News & Feed" is just what it sounds like; it's a series of articles that go out on the feed. At the moment, only admins can post to it, though that'll change. "Files" and "Images" are data repositories; if you want to host a whole block of connected files, let me know so I can make a new gallery.

    The forum is a "backstage" for site stuff and chat, and later a place to goof around with the others doing that same stuff. Visiting the site for the forum is backwards - it's a assistance tool, not the main show.

    "My Tiki" is a list of all the stuff you, user, have interacted with on the site.

    So that's what the stuff IS.

    ....

    As far as I can say, the wiki categories are best for sharing little things - an article, a treatise, a chunk of theory, a game review.
    The file galleries are best for sharing bigger things - PDFs, games, and so on.

    And to be honest, the best way to use the tools is to have a goal - a thing you want to do. Then poke the tools, see where it fits. If it doesn't fit the tools? Shout for me in the forums, and I'll see what I can do - I can tell the site to make blogs, and a whole host of other stuff. In many cases (people I know and feel reassured about), if it looks like you want to be active and might benefit from access to more tools, I'll just hit the button and give you admin powers.
