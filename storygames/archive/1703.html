    See, the trick with THS is that there's no "core story." It's a freaky shopping mall with lots of fun things to purchase, and fun things to be, but there's no clue about what dudes actually do.

    Incidentally: instead of "evolve" or "transcend," to me the setting strongly needs a dose of "civil war."

    THS isn't really that overwhelming, either, if you look at the big picture:
    * Mankind made it to 2100 with a fairly recognizable social order; no large scale disasters
    * We're in space, and this time it's for real
    * People can genetically modify themselves, their offspring, or grow mutant-dudes in vats
    * Artificial intelligence is all over the place in advanced societies; it's helpful but not "super-duper smart" (yet)
    * There's "matter-printers"--think Star Trek replicators, but way slower and less efficient
    * Brainwashing is advanced, fairly ubiquitous, and used for commercial and political purposes

    Mix and match these ad infinitum. Everything else is just fiddly details no one could ever hope to remember. Most of this stuff is implied in a lot of sci-fi movies, we're just not used to being socked with it all at once, in a big encyclopedia-type gaming book.

    Would it work? Who knows! Try it!

    It's not really what I'm talking about, though. I'm talking about generating all that stuff in play, and only what you need at the moment. Check out what's happening over here with our Mechaton game. We've set up institutions in opposition to each other. We don't know a lot of stuff, though: are we from the same planet? Is this Earth? Who are other political entities in the world? And so on. If we need that stuff, we'll make it up. Once we've played things out, we'll have a significant event in the course of the world.

    Now, imagine that lots of groups are playing in this campaign setting. Maybe they're playing with the factions we've created, maybe they're playing with others. Maybe they're playing in different parts of the world. But whenever a battle takes place, it's recorded in the Wiki and is now true.

    Journal of the Traveller's Aid Society did this to some degree. I don't think it necessarily came from actual play, though.
