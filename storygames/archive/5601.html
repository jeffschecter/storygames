\n Hi Everybody,

We've finalized a venue and location for Go Play NW 2008!

Dates are May 31-June 1

Go Play NW 2008 will be at the Watertown Hotel in Seattle's University district. map

There will undoubtedly be some sort of meetup on Friday night for socializing, making friends, and renewing friendships.

We don't having pricing finalized yet, but it will follow soon.

Go Play NW is an annual gathering of gamers and game designers. The goal of the event is for people from the Northwest and across the country to get together, make friends, renew friendships, and play games. This is our second year running the event.

Visit our Web site

You can learn more about the event at our Wiki. We'll be updating this page regularly as we get closer to the event.

Hope you can come game with us in Seattle!

    \n Pre-registration is open for Go Play NW 2008! Here's the registration page. Also, here's the Go Play NW 2008 Wiki.

    There are a few changes worth noting this year:

    First of all, our space is limited to 75 people. That gives us some space to grow this year, but it's probably still a good idea to pre-register.

    Also, if we get enough pre-registrations, we won't need to raise the price at the door very much, so that helps everyone too.

    You can order T-shirts with your registration. The theme is "robo-sasquatch". John Harper is doing the design again this year. We don't have a preview yet, but it should be pretty cool.

    This year we're launching the Go Play NW Scholarship Program. The purpose of this program is to help folks come to Go Play NW who might not otherwise be able to afford to attend. If you know someone who qualifies, please email goplaynw@gmail.com! You can donate to this program on our registration page. If by chance we have money left over, we'll be using it to improve Go Play NW in some other way.

    We're not booking accommodations through our Web Page this year. Go to the accommodations page on the wiki to see what options are available.

    \n Hi all,

    At a previous con in Bellevue, some feeback we got was that it would be nice to have some events that people could predictably sign up for.

    How are activity tracks going to be handled at Goplay this year?

    I liked the open, unscheduled approach last year, but I was wondering if we might also have a parallel track of scheduled games.

    In particular, how about a bunch of us volunteer to run a "Story Game 101" track with scheduled games and signup on the morning of play day? The games would be underplayed masterpieces and story game standards that introduce key concepts of story gaming. The "101" track games would only allow signup from people new to Story Games.

    I'll offer to run Trollbabe -- once in the 101 track and once in the open track, so all you old timers can have a shot at it.

    A total of 6 101 track games would probably give us a full two day schedule. Who else will step up?
