\n So... Jackson has demanded that I run Transantiago for him on his birthday, and I have very little time to put this together.

HELP!

I need to come up with short descriptions of the "look" (Apocalypse World-style) for the various saints, angels, and bodhisattvas that serve as the protagonists of the game. Here's an example:

The Butcher has...
1. the face of a (vulture, grey-eyed beauty, ritual mask)
2. hands with (six fingers, long yellow nails, bloody palms)
3. and is girded in (a cloak of raven feathers, vinegar-soaked bandages, a loin-wrapping of snakes).

Basically, each character has 3 descriptive characteristics and each of those has 3 possible options to choose from. They should be weird, mythological, and slightly unnerving. Think ancient Egyptian or Aztec or Tibetan or Miyasaki. I'm aiming for very physical and earthy descriptive traits, rather than more emotional (aura of fear) or supernatural (shoot flames).

Here are the names and thematic associations of all of them. Duplication is fine, since I'm going to remix whatever folks send me into the ones we use in play in the next few days.

\u2022 Paper & Wind \u2014 The Kite-Builder
\u2022 Wind & Horse \u2014 The Traveler
\u2022 Horse & Blade \u2014 The King
\u2022 Blade & Flesh \u2014 The Butcher
\u2022 Flesh & Temperance \u2014 The Virgin
\u2022 Temperance & Fire \u2014 The Blacksmith
\u2022 Fire & Vine \u2014 The Wine-Maker
\u2022 Vine & Flower \u2014 The Gardener
\u2022 Flower & Heart \u2014 The Rose-Bearer
\u2022 Heart & Ribbon \u2014 The Dancer
\u2022 Ribbon & Throat \u2014 The Assassin
\u2022 Throat & Music \u2014 The Songbird
\u2022 Music & Death \u2014 The Mourner
\u2022 Death & Ocean \u2014 The Corsair
\u2022 Ocean & Light \u2014 The Navigator
\u2022 Light & Paper \u2014 The Lamplighter

Thanks in advance! You're really saving my bacon.

    \nThe Kite-Builder...

    ... wears a cloak (of blinking eyes, that speaks with far-off voices, woven from ribbons of coloured paper)
    ... over the body (of a frail old man, made out of sticks and rice-paper, that shifts and floats like a soap bubble)
    ... and speaks the language (of the fourteen winds, of all four elements, of graceful hands)


    The Rose-Bearer

    ... has eyes (made out of coal, which see nothing, on the backs of her hands)
    ... carries a shield (made of glass, woven from the hair of her lovers, bearing the sigil of her father)
    ... and bears the rose (of eternal fire, tattooed on her skin, like the oxen bears its yoke)


    The Lamplighter

    ... wears (a dirty suit, a robe of ash and copper filament, the starlight mask)
    ... with hands (long-fingered and stained with ink, made of wax with wicks for fingers, bleeding light from a dozen thin cuts)
    ... accompanied by (a dog with stars for eyes, a host of tiny flickering flames, a clumsy child-servant)


    The Corsair

    ... has the face (of a slavering jackal, of a serene woman, covered by a black hood)
    ... is missing (his heart a gaping hole, his hand a bloody stump, his voice a howling gale)
    ... and always in his wake comes (the endless baying of dogs, three old crones, the sea)

    \n Holy crap. So just played Transantiago last night. We ended up using these more as inspiration instead of choosing directly from them, but the characters were super amazing and I'm already planning on taking all of this and using it to update the playtest document. Major thanks to Daniel, Steve, and Willow!! This game will be better due to your help and I will send you free copies if it ever gets formally published.

    As for the game itself, man. It was only the second time it's ever been played, the first in over five years I think (2006 or 2007 was the first?). And we had 5 players instead of the recommended 8 and were playing in a loud, busy bar. Before we started, I said: "Well, this game isn't going to feel as personal or emotional as it otherwise might, just due to the environment." But I was totally wrong. The game was epic and beautiful and quite unlike anything else out there. And, crazily enough, it felt very much like the version we played at JiffyCon all those years ago. I still feel a bit overwhelmed by the experience, actually. But I'm going to try to finish putting a playable draft together this weekend so the experience can be shared with others. Wow.

    Also, I think the name is going to gradually change to Metrofin\xe1l, since Transantiago is a major public transportation initiative in Santiago, probably copyrighted, and is a bit too reminiscent of that board game called Santiago.
