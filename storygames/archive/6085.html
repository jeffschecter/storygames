Hi gang,

\n

I'm pounding out the first proper long-form draft of Black Caddies (ramping up to ashcan, doncha know?), and I'm running headlong into a terminology problem.

\n

There's two different kinds of XP in the game. One is for individual soldiers, and the other is for the squad as a whole. Originally, XP just gave you more dice in your dice pool.

\n

I used to call the two kinds of XP Mettle (soldiers) and Clout (squad). I really, really don't like how precious those terms are. I flirted with Grizzle for the squad too, and that was even worse.

\n

During play, I started calling Clout "Squad Dice", and frankly, I like that better. I never found a better name for Mettle. Soldiering Dice just doesn't roll off the tongue. So that's the first problem.

\n

The second problem is that the XP aren't really just dice anymore -- they're resource points, and buying dice is just one of the things that you can do with them.

\n

I need some suggestions for what to call both kinds of XP, please! If it helps with context, the PCs' other stats are:

\n

Name
\nBackground
\nNext of Kin
\nLast Night
\nHorror
\nValour
\nHubris

\n

Thanks,

    Ok. Let's back up a sec, and do a quick terminology run-down.

    \n

    Fiction is the moment-to-moment stuff you create while you're role-playing the game. This is normal scene-to-scene/free-play/conflict resolution stuff.
    \nStories are the end-of-game retellings of moments from the fiction. They're literally "war stories". They aren't necessarily told by a player character. They are tales that make it back from the front, somehow (maybe a letter home, maybe a newsreel, etc.)
    \nMemories are the game's equivalent of character skills. They're invented on-the-fly, as needed, by the players. For instance, if you're in the middle of a firefight and you really, really need to make a good shot, you could create a memory for your character: "I used to shoot varmint back on the farm with my brother Jeb." Bingo! Your odds just got better during conflict resolution.

    \n

    Now, the two items I'm looking at renaming (I'll use the generic terms Character XP and Squad XP as placeholders).

    \n

    Character XP are meant to represent the character growing as a soldier. Things that make a character more likely to gain this award are: risking your life, risking your chance to go home. Things that this award can be used for: helping your fellow soldiers win conflicts. It can't be used to help yourself.
    \nSquad XP is meant to represent the squad as a whole getting more hardened and professional. Things that make the squad gain XP are: losing members (either to death or tickets home), and losing exemplary members (same reasons). This award can be drawn on by any player to improve their odds during conflict resolution.

    \n

    Both kinds of XP started out as Gift Dice (TSoY) clones.

    \n

    So, the names for the XP awards need to encapsulate their function as well as that notion that gaining them isn't necessarily good for the character as a human being.

    \n

    I'm thinking along the lines of "Blooded" and "Renown" (Character and Squad, respectively), but I'm not sold.

    \n I'm gonna be a little nostalgic and say that I like Mettle as a term for trooper points. For me it evokes weapons, extremes, and a testing of the sort that has to provoke change in that which it tests. It's an internal quality, not flashy or active. And I like the feel of the word in my mouth.

    There's something to be said for Callan's We Can't Forget thing for the squad points. They're all about why the Staff keeps that skull in her quarters for us and how we got it and why so few of us were there for it while the rest of us are replacements fresh out of Basic and what it means for us to have troopers who survived it fighting beside us. It's about our identity as a squad and profanity, profanity, profanity, Darcy, that's it!

    That's it, that's the thing, the ineffable, gappy . . . thing that I had.

    The game continues as long as one trooper survives the session, right? (By "survive" here I mean "live and not go home".) You could conceivably play a dozen sessions, see complete squad turnover five times, with just a fragile thread of eleven sole survivors to pass the baton of squad XP down through time.

    Dude, in a game about Memories and Stories, why is it that the only thing designed to persist throughout play is a numerical stat? What if there were a persistent thematic element, something like a trooper's Memories, but that newbies to the squad can tap, or that the squad can tap on behalf of the newbie. What if the squad could tap Stories?

    Or -- maybe you've already solved this by linking new to old trooper backgrounds or something. Or maybe the ending rule is actually that the game continues as long as one original trooper survives, in which case, please ignore my demented babbling.
