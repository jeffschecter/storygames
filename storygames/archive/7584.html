\n Oh yes, Spore. I played all night, y'all. This is a clever game full of great emergent qualities. Like, when your dude finally crawls out of the ocean, you get to tack on some legs so he can cruise around - but the default configuration for his spine is more or less horizontal. I was like "OK", and later realized that by getting him more vertical he could grab fruit not just from low-lying bushes, but from trees. Pretty soon he was a biped. There's no flashing sign telling you that this is a good thing, but it happens. Who knew evolutionary biology could be so fun?

    \n Again, I'm a mere noob, but bilateral symmetry isn't a given. You can make a one-eyed snake-man or a seven-beaked flying whale. But - fancy that - a bilateral bilateral arrangement carries certain practical advantages. If you want to do well you seem to be pushed in optimal directions. After some upright bipeds killed and ate me, I thought "I want to be that". Earlier I watched my wife ditch her protozooa's crazy multiple mouths for a single forward-focused one.

    I think there are 256 discrete parts you can meld to a very flexible frame. I know you can lengthen and shorten the vertebral column, although I have not played with the extremes.

    It strikes me as a very excellent set of tools to support game play, it being a game and all, and the internal logic of building creatures astonishes me.

    Oh, I should add that each body part (at least in the wild beast stage) has a cost, and makes you better at certain things by various degrees. So my guy has a unicorn horn that gives him the Charge ability, and leathery wings that allow him to jump and float briefly. I got the cash for these by making friends - my dude's an excellent dancer.

    * All players' designs go into a database, and...

    Can I get a "FUCKING GENIUS!" anyone?!? Not only is EA getting players too provide content, they've made it painless to snatch and grab content which actually suits your existing aesthetics!     \n Yeah - I'm really enjoying the game a lot, but some 'crunchier' expansions for each stage would be interesting. I made a sort of biped, armless herbivore that had feeders on long arms - one up high to grab tree fruit, one dangling (!) low for bushes, and a whole lot of poison spitters in-between. My only gripe with the Creature phase is that if you want to take the Social route, you need to have bits for every single one of the four attributes (Sing, Dance, Pose and Charm) - otherwise you can't match the movements of the later creatures and you fail. The kill-kill route is much simpler in that respect.

I can only assume that a "Sims 2" model is in store for Spore with all sorts of new goolies to add to your creatures - I just hope that there's also a series of gameplay expansions and/or mod support.

    As for the simplistic biology: Symmetrical bodies are pretty much the law, phylogenically, at least as we know it. Other than that, there's a computational question that comes into play with totally free-form core structures. From what I've seen, people are doing some incredibly creative things with the creature creator to overcome this shortcoming. I can imagine that an internal biology model would slow the game down tremendously. I'm shocked at how smooth the game is on my good-but-not-great PC, even with a ton of creatures on the screen.

    \n I haven't been able to play the final game yet. But I'm really pleased to see all of the hard work come to fruition. I think the two things that impress me the most are the tiny pieces of procedural code they use to generate content on the fly; and the system to suck content from users and propagate it to others. Brilliant.

    Right before I left Maxis in early 2003, the single engineer on the game at the time gave me a sneak peak at Spore. At that time, it was simply a series of tools that had yet to be expanded into game experiences. And there was no metagame to tie them together. So for instance, you could fly over a wireframe ocean of a young Earth to check out multi-cell creatures. Or look at a simple star map and use your mouse to find radio signals coming from systems that could contain intelligent life.

    These tools were nowhere near as far along as the prototypes you can download off of the Spore site (which are very cool!). But even then it was an exciting experience and you could tell it was going to be really different. I could see the potential in this game, and I'm happy that the team was able to bring it to life. EA is good at fooling with or killing off things they don't understand. But Will is a pretty persuasive guy. They even kept the name "Spore" from way back then:)

      Posted By: WolfeSo, has anyone tried this on lower end systems at all? Specifically, those that barely match the minimums, or maybe do not match the minimums at all? How about laptops?
      \nIt runs great on my MacBook Pro, but that's a bit beefy as far as laptops go. My computer at work is closer to minimum than my home PC, especially for the video card which is a Radeon x300, though I'm not sure on the specific processor speed. It runs it wonderfully, though. Unless you have a computer 4 or more years old, you'd be hard pressed to have a processor that is too slow. The main issue is the video card, since it's pretty easy to have a computer that's only a year old with a video card that won't support it if you purchased your system without an eye toward gaming. But if you meet the minimums, it should run quite smoothly, especially if you have a buffer with the processor and memory.

      So, I'm not sure what sort of laptop you currently have, but if purchasing a gaming laptop is an option, then you shouldn't have a concern.

      \n Ok, so I finally got done with the Tribe level, and went on to complete the civ level and now am in space...
      \n
      \nI have to say I'm pretty disappointed. I was looking forward to a game about civilization where the obvious solution wasn't to simply build more armies and kill the other guy.
      \n
      \nNow don't get me wrong...I love me some build armies and kill the other guy games. 4x (Explore, Exploit, Expand, Exterminate) is probably my favorite genre of PC game. The Civ series, the old Warlord Series, Masters of Orion, Galactic Civilization, the Total War series...yup...I've got nothing at all against building armies and committing mass electronic genocide.
      \n
      \nBut I was looking forward to Spore as a game that offered OTHER ways (and reasons) to play...and just don't see it.
      \n
      \nTribe stage had me befuddled. In order to make friends, I had to play music. But I had to use the other guys favorite instruments to do it...which means I had to acquire those instruments. Now maybe there was a certain order that I could have progressed...finding first the people who like the instrument I already had, and use that to acquire their instrument and find the next people who like those, and so on. But what a pain in the ass. Not to mention that I then had to spend valuable resources to build buildings to produce these instruments (one for each type) and then equip my limited number of people with them instead of with a weapon (so that when wild animals attacked me the idiots tried to fight them off with a Didgiroo instead of a spear). Then I had to move all those people to the other city...with armed guards because a troop of musicians is pretty vulnerable. Then I had to play some extended mini game which may or may not work, while all the while my children are at home guarded by just a couple tribesman...who inevitably get massacred by some other tribes raiding party who then proceed to set fire to my village...all while I'm trying to move some potential ally from a yellow neutral face to a blue i-sorta-like-you-but-not-really face.
      \n
      \nA huge collosal pain in the ass which defeated me several times.
      \n
      \nSo then I said...fuck it...when I started over I outfitted all my guys with axes, went to the stupid yellow neutral face village and turned them all into red angry faces because I just killed their ass dead. Then I burned their village to the ground, stole their tech, stole their food and went back home before any of the other people had a chance to raid me while I was away. Easy. Ok, so I did it again and again and again....and before I knew it...I'd completed the Tribal stage.
      \n
      \nUmmmm, ok.
      \n
      \nSo I went to the Civilization stage. Tried to set up a trade route, but apparently since I'd conquered everyone in the Tribal stage I was only good at fighting in the civ stage...so I built a bunch of tanks and went out and killed people. Then I created a bunch of ships and went out and killed people. Then I created a bunch of planes and went out and killed people. My biggest enemy was some "religious" civ who tried to convert me. Interestingly conversion in Spore looks an awful lot like conquering...you send out your tanks, ships and planes, only instead of them launching missiles that damage your city they fire humonguous holographic projections of God which damage your city. So I fucking killed them too.
      \n
      \nThen I went to space. I'm trying to complete quests and make friends and do a bunch of stuff to make people like me...but the two races I've encountered have now greatly surpassed me in number of planets and economic power. I suspect I can still complete the game without killing them...but I bet it would be alot quicker an easier to just fucking kill them and not piss around.
      \n
      \nWhy oh why did the creators of Spore decide to make the easiest and surest way to victory something as been-there-done-that as building troops and killing people. I already have Civ and Medieval Total War for that...and both of those games do it much better.
      \n
      \nI think they got so caught up in the tool kit to making creatures and wierdo buildings and vehicles that they forgot to make a game worthy of the creature creator...and truth be told...now that I'm in space and its asking me AGAIN to create Factory Buildings and Houses and shit...I'm pretty much just wishing they gave me a menu of buildings to choose from. My last building I selected one white cube, plunked it down and clicked done...another building to squish and drag and add funky windows and paint jobs to...yawn.
      \n
      \nAnd seriously...is terraforming in space supposed to be fun? What the hell do I care how tall the mountains are or what color the plants are? Those things don't actually DO anything...I guess playing with electronic clay isn't my thing.
