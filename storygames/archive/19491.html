\n So this is a general thread about updating the HeroQuest 2 game and the publishers of the Nameless Streets supplement maybe getting in the superheroes business.

I'm curious what people think of HeroQuest as a story game and what updates they'd make to the system, tweaks, cuts, expanded rules, etc in general, but with an eye toward superheroes gaming with HQ2.

In my experience HeroQuest 2 is a great generic system that can cover basically everything. A few tweaks and it could handle most genres. If anything it might have played a little too fast, feeling like there's not enough game there, if that makes sense.

Nameless Streets was a great supplement, and I consider the author a friend, but the book really only added a few pertinent rules and an interesting setting. That doesn't speak ill of the writer or the supplement, rather it speaks well of Laws' original. HeroQuest 2 is generic and sleek enough to handle most genres out of the box.

So again, for HeroQuest 2 fans, what kind of tweaks would you like to see for a supers supplement and what kind of setting would you like to see to make it worth the price of admission?

    \n HeroQuest 2 removed some of the 'special rules' that crept into HeroQuest from Hero Wars. The principle was that the core mechanic for conflict resolution: ability + augment + situational modifiers (inc. broad/stretch) vs. resistance should be able to handle all conflicts. After all its not task based resolution.

    The exception is an extended contest, which essentially decomposes the conflict resolution into task resolution. This adds some options such as resolution points, assists, etc. that become important when you decompose the conflict.

    So I would be cautious on adding special rules, but if you had to reserve them for extended contest options that reflected your genre better.

    Robin Laws gives some great advice in the section at the back of the book on how to handle ideas like powers, and it would have been great to see more people build HQ2 genre packs.
