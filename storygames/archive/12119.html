    \n The best people to talk about gaming to a non-gaming audience:

    1.) Judd Karlman (for sharing his unbridled, unabashed enthusiasm in an infectious manner; for being positive; for focusing on play, and what makes play great)
    2.) Vincent Baker (for being thoughtful; for sharing how games & life intersect and inform one another; for having amazingly insightful wisdom, regarding social dynamics in games)
    3.) Clinton R. Nixon (enthusiasm; support; a dynamic voice that's interesting to listen to; big love for the little guys; completely without pretensions or clique-ish biases)

    Runners-up: Emily Care-Boss (friendly, thoughtful, a female voice), Fred Hicks (knowledgeable, approachable, happy to share his perspective), Jake Richmond (adorable, laid-back, bridging the divide between other geeky communities), Jesse Burneko (exciting perspective, cares about gaming as an art, cares about gaming as socially vital), Joel Shempert (lots of awesome insights into the intersection between games and spiritual/social development, sharing a personal perspective/vantage point, shares such thoughts regularly and consistently), Ry (thoughtful, passionate, super creative), Ryan Macklin (a beloved podcaster, a cool guy, knowledgeable in gaming, con organizing, playtesting, publishing, GMing, podcasting & every other inch of our hobbies).

    \n Re-reading this thread, it seems a lot of people's criteria include "explaining the hobby to the mundanes". But that's not in my criteria. I mean, I think a little explanation of what roleplaying looks like is in order, but not much. What made me think of roleplaying and Fresh Air in the same thought-bubble was the stories of creativity, art, DIY, and most especially how literary some roleplaying games are (My Life with Master as genre deconstruction, anyone?) I think there's a lot there that Fresh Air's listeners and possibly Terry Gross would find fascinating.

    I wouldn't want description of what you can do in a roleplaying game, but rather, "Here's what Dogs in the Vineyard is about, and this is what it makes you do at the table." Stuff like that.
