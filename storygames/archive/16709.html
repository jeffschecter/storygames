    \n Here's how the startup went:
    1: We read out the skins and the players apparently found what they wanted immediately and selected a fey and the chosen.
    2: We created the town of Florence, Idaho and made up a few locations we might use. Also, we picke the name of the rival town.
    3: Created the homeroom map. I forgot to ask questions (my bad).
    4: Created families
    5: Started playing. Slow at first but quickly picked up speed. Actual playtime was about 3\xbd hours.

    Mistakes I made: Forgot to ask questions regarding the homeroom, and didn't get The Fallen (the Chosens arch-enemy) fleshed out. Other than that, it seemed to work pretty well. It wa just supposed to be a one-shot, but my players want MORE!!!!

    \n Jackson's awesome!!!
    In the scene where they find Daniella eating her step-father, Daniella has her back turned, so Jackson, being the Chosen, attacks. And misses something fierce (rolled a 3, I think). Actually, I only think Jacksons player, Terese, managed a single succesful roll the entire session. Jackson had soooo many conditions at the end.

    As for your question, yes, there was a turning on roll made. Succesfully. We kept the scene tender and loving instead of sexual. It made Daniella's death so much more tragic and touching. I actually had tears in my eyes. A flashback to real life for me, alas.

    I'm looking forward to seeing what they come up with next. The session actually ended with Daniella moving in with Jackson and her grandmother, eating breakfast, and Daniella looking at grandma, saying "I'm hungry...." Then biting into a sausage.

    I'm not really upset that we didn't get the Fallen, Jackson's arch enemy, into play, because I really want Jackson to discover what the Fallen actually is. All she knows is that he/she/it killed her family. Not the reason for it. Me, I'm thinking her father or mother may have been Infernal or part of a cult or something. Would be much more fun to find that her parents were responsible for summoning their own killer.

    I think I'll keep Daniella around for a while. First, seeing what mayhem she can cause and discovering what Jackson and Frey will do about her new dietary requirements should be interesting. It'll have to end up a hard choice between killing her or letting her kill.
    Second, MC'ing doesn't leave me with much to do. Asking hard questions and creating situations is almost too easy with these players because they happily do most of it themselves, coming up with scenes and such, playing NPC's. I see Daniella as a PC in her own right, played by me, although when a new player joins I'll happily hand her over, should the new player be willing and eager.
