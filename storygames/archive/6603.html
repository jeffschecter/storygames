\n These days, work is just nuts. I manage it with yoga (for stress) and energy drinks before gaming or writing sessions to try to get back some energy and focus.

I was wondering what other people use to keep them in the game? What recommendations do you have, what warnings would you advise, etc? Here's my list:

Coffee : A solid choice. I buy by the bean, grind, and serve as needed. The upside is that it's cheaper: You can also choose to do it hot or cold (on ice in the summer, etc). The downside is that I sometimes make it too strong, and head down the caffeine headache tunnel of no return: Caffeine headache from drinking the coffee, then the next day a caffeine withdrawal headache because the previous day's hit was enough to make my body dependent. A careful dance on a tightrope. Still, delicious.

Chain Store Coffee is a mixed bag: Starbucks is simply too fucking expensive for me to care about anymore. Like $4.00 for a Latte. Craziness. Plain coffee is pretty pricey, too. Other local joints, like the local Mister Toads or Bean Traders, are much more reasonably priced (and I'd say delicious as well; Starbucks burns their beans). Other chain stores are pretty OK, too: Caribou Coffee (now owned by Starbucks, sigh) is pretty good for the price. It's A Grind has a decent selection of coffees available for not a lot of scrilla (but they focus a little too much on sweet nasty drinks, IMO).

Loose Leaf Teas: Flavorful, but don't always pack as much of a punch. Upside is that there are many flavors. Downside is price, it can be hard to get a hold of sometimes, and that it can take longer/more effort to make right.

Yerba Mate: All the caffiene high, none of the caffeine side-effects. Good stuff. Hard to get a hold of in the Real form (loose ground leaf), and teabag form tends to be weak sauce with a high price. Downside is that it can be pretty bitter. But you can order it cut with mint and other herbs (usually 15% herbs), which makes it much more flavorful and less bitter without loosing any of the punch.

Sodas: Boo. Too much sugar/calories for the amount of punch they pack. Also, burping can suck in a dramatic speech.

MONSTER Energy Drinks: A mixed bag. They are gonzo, and come in flavors like KHAOS, M-80, and ASSAULT (as well as the original Greenish-Yellow flavor). They provide hot, sweet energy at a lower caloric cost. However, they contain some sucralose, which can set of migraines.

FULL THROTTLE Energy Drink: It's like Monster, only in a slightly different can.

ROCKSTAR Energy Drinks: FUCK Michael Savage and his son (inventor of Rockstar), founders of the hilariously racist Paul Revere Society". I wouldn't use a can to pour on my mother if she was on fire.

RED BULL : I don't know what it is about this rather pricey energy drink: It doesn't have a whopping ton of calories, it doesn't taste bad (to me), and holy cow it's like time slows when you drink one. Even after all the hippie organic coffees and teas I drink, Red Bull is still my drink of choice when I need to Wake the Fuck Up, Now.

DIET RED BULL: This is the same, but 10 calories instead of 110. They do it by replacing all sugars with high amounts sucralose (splenda). This means that if I drink one, I get debilitating migraines for about two days (it turns out to be a trigger for a lot of people). Would recommend that folks stay away just in case, but if you can eat that Splenda bullshit like nobody's business, more power too you.

So, that's my list. If I had to choose a best-to-worst for those gaming nights where you're just beat up from work, I'd start at Red Bull (albeit pricey), then Coffee, then Yerba Mate.

-Andy
