\n So, I've been in a real Cyberpunk mood(Recently got Technoir and loving it), and I have a few friends who want to try out Fiasco. I figured, why not mash both together?

But I'm getting a bit Stymied on the shear number of elements I need(Six times the number in Technoir), So I was hoping you'd all help? Feel free to throw out any ideas you may have for anything. Elements, Details, etc. I'll be adding ones as I think of them as well.

The Playset is called "Mirrored Shades" and is based primarily in \u201cNight City\u201d a generic futuristic American city.

THE SCORE:
\u201cIn the not-so-distant future...

...Night City is a dangerous place even when the Sun is at it's highest, but when it's namesake covers the rain-slicked streets even the air will try to kill you. But in this town the air is the least of your worries; Cops, Ripper Gangs, Freelance Soldiers purchased by the highest bidder. Any one of these could be the last face you see before your life slips away in a dark alley off 14th street. This town either swallows you whole or chews you up on the way down... You wouldn't live anywhere else.

So what made this night different than any other night? The neon lights of the shops made psychedelic rainbows in the acid rain puddles, just like every night. The sound of sirens two blocks down cut through the din of the clubs and street hawkers, just like every night. No, it was the air this night that set it apart, it felt electric. Like a static shock from your head to your toes that brought everything into focus despite the steam rising from the quickly cooling asphalt. Yes, it was the air tonight that was different, that and the .50 caliber hand cannon pointed right between your eyes."

Movies to watch: Blade Runner, Minority Report, Total Recall(2012), Renaissance, Looker, Nemesis, A Scanner Darkly

    \n I'm making a cyberpunk AW hack at the moment, and I'm amused by how little overlap there is between your project and mine. I'm focusing on mechanics that support an experience of wired-reflex combat, while you need six heaping tablespoons of evocative content.

    One thing you might look at is the "The Stratfor Glossary of Useful, Baffling and Strange Intelligence Terms" - that's a goldmine of interesting tensions that could easily be mined for Fiasco elements. It's not cyberpunk, but it's great for tensions and bureaucratic/political context.
