    \n First: Awesome, thanks.

    And now, my mini-review!

    The Taser article: I don't buy it. I mean, he makes really good points about the way tasers are viewed by pop culture, but the rest seems ... off. For one, "realistically" tasers are not the foolproof conflict-stopper he portrays them as. Often, they work. (If you hit.) Sometimes, they don't. And sometimes, people die. Which, to my mind, fits nicely to the way guns work "realistically", in that sometimes they kill, sometimes they don't and generally people hit don't want to keep fighting. So the argument that portraying guns as non-foolproof conflict-stoppers is "realistic", but that the Taser is somehow a magical fight-ender without danger or trouble seems off for me.

    The "Call of Cthulhu" Theme is frigging awesome and I'd love to see a short movie that uses it. Sorta like a Trailer for the game or something.

    Still listening/downloading/reading the rest...

    \n Not many people die from tasers but I think the point is more that if you've got some kind of superpower you probably should be using that instead of a taser. On the other hand, it spreads the load of the points spend if you use a taser. I only had two shots from my blast power so it was easier to taser things, so I did.

    Guns don't automatically take people down but the GUMSHOE rules aren't really about simulation. They're a narrative device. Health doesn't really represent how tough a character is, more how long you want a fight with that character to last or how dangerous you want them to be. By introducing tasers I broke that conceit.

    It's probably just as easy to say that tasers are banned or covered by the same legislation that applies to superpowers. It's a bit harder in the US with the gun nut enthusiast lobby.
