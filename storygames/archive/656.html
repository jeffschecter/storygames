[Console] Vote for Evil

Colin_Fredericks
May 2006 edited May 2006 in Story Games
My next version of Console is going to be much more a story-telling game than a role-playing one. It's almost certainly going to be GM-less, have "writers" for each character but be able to activate other characters' abilities, etc. I was pondering how it would be possible to have a Final Fantasy style story in that game without someone (the GM) whose job it is to keep all the big secrets, when one little way came up and tapped me on the shoulder.

What if, at the end of the game, everyone voted on which member of the party was secretly the Main Villain all along? Then that character's writer gets to fight everyone else.

Or, you know, variations on that. Maybe they've only been the MV for half the game, after said character was killed or captured. Maybe they're mind controlled by the even bigger villain you'll fight in the sequel. Whatever. Long story short, one person ends up having been the Main Villain the whole time, in every game.

That's my little idea for today. Maybe tomorrow I'll have a little idea for how to keep boss fights and lose random encounters.

    Mark: Super Console and its expansion pack, Monster Mash, are published. Console III, which this post was about, it still a good distance away, though I did a bunch of writing for it today.

    I finally have that boss fight mechanic, too; very "pull" oriented. Everyone writes down "visions" they want to see happen, but that they have to keep secret. When someone narrates one of your visions, they get a Boss Die that they use during boss fights. You can foreshadow while you're narrating to give other people a better chance to get Boss Dice, thus getting you more ammo to use against bosses. I'm pretty happy with it.
