\n We all know the tropes and themes of Pulp adventuring, but I'm specifically thinking of the "feel" for combat situations, sneaking around or escapes. Things like:\n
    \n
  • Dangerous environments: The bridge is collapsing, the boulder is rolling towards you, the train you're fighting atop is approaching a tunnel, etc.
    • \n
  • People switching sides: Pirates of the Caribbean had a lot of this, but I think it fits for pulp, too. Stuff coming out in mid-combat and people going "hey wait!" and switching their gun onto someone else. Everybody's got a slightly different agenda.
    • \n
  • BIG things: It's King Kong. It's a T-Rex. It's a giant mechanical spider. Whatever it is, it's massive and requires a certain degree of clambering about, death-defying leaps, etc.
\nAny other thoughts?

    \n *The "BIG" category can be expanded to anything really, not just physical size. Everything and everyone is oversized, over-the-top, larger-than-life, numerous, insanely wealthy...etc etc. Everything is described in superlatives.
    \n*Exclamation marks! At the end of every sentence! Sometimes even after every! Word!
    \n*Cliffhangers. I think cliffhangers are very pulp. Even when they're not in at the end of an episode/session. The PC's go over a waterfall in a raft? Let them hang. Everyone knows they survive, but yank the suspense strings a bit every now and then.
    \n*Ancient mystery. Everyone knows people in antiquity were so much smarter than us. Atlantis. Ancient ruins. Lost civilisations. And they aren't from 3000BC either. They're from 300000BC. And they rode dinosaurs! Which are awakening!
    \n*Semi-colonialistic exoticism. The civilised lands (ie. America, parts of Europe) are peaceful, ignorant, civilised, cultured. Everything else is savages and jungles and wild beasts and strange languages and unapproachable locations.
    \n
    \nI just realized I wasn't listing situations but tropes, so I'll stop.
