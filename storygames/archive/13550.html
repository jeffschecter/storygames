    Posted By: jdfristrom- it doesn't look like it was really intended for 2 player. If you wanted to play it two player, how would you do it? GM + 1 player, or jigger it to be more GMless?
    \nI'd do GM plus 1 player. The GM would have to be all the other NPCs. This isn't too hard, really: in most high-school dramas, there's just one central character.\n
    I can't even figure out how to play it as intended for more players. I've searched here and The Forge, and this looks like the most up-to-date information, but it's still pretty minimal.
    \nYes. Be prepared to make assumptions.\n
    -- What is this "What's your Dream" card? Am I missing it? Is it from an earlier draft?
    \nProbably. I think it just says that each character should decide what their dream is and who is standing in their way.\n
    -- Are players randomly assigned their blue cards, or do they get to choose?
    \nThose are the character ones, right? They choose. That's a fun part.\n
    -- How much prep does the GM do, if any? Does she need a kicker or NPCs or is she expected to do that on the fly?
    \nDo it on the fly. I follow standard high school plots: for example, if you have a nerdy character, have a science fair.

    Invent interesting antagonists for each PC. This is really easy. If there's a pretty PC, invent a prettier NPC. If there's a nerdy PC, invent a nerdier NPC or a love interest who's out of their league.\n
    -- Does the GM use blue cards for their NPCs or does everyone just keep them in their heads?
    \nIn their heads.

    It's a fun game!
