    \n I typically run "roleplay combat roleplay" or "combat roleplay combat", if the two tactical encounters are straightforward or "easy" ones (i.e. with an EL at or below the character level).

    Party size: Four (now five) players; encounters seem to be lasting about 10-15 minutes more with the fifth player.

    EL: The spread is as per the DMG's recommendations: most of the encounters are at party level or party level plus one, a few are at party level minus one, and a smaller amount are "tough", and are at party level plus two or more.

    Encounter time: Encounters last 60-90 minutes depending on EL; the tougher ones last closer to 90 minutes, but the average time is probably about 75 minutes.

    Extended rests: With a few exceptions, the party has taken extended rests after every two encounters (not optimal). There were extended rests after the "boss fights" in the first adventure they've just finished (single tough encounter), and an extended rest after three encounters that seemed "simple", but by the third the party almost got cleaned up because they were healing surge reduced, and had none of their dailies left.

    Treatment of hit points, etc: we try to treat all these gauges as they appear in the rules; the only exception is that we'd only recently been put in a position where we realized we were treating Death Saves incorrectly (you're only to fail three Death Saves in an encounter before you die, no matter what happens in between them: we had one fight where one PC spent time hopping up and down around death, and although in our case the Death Save rule didn't cause a problem, but it could have).

    Action point flow seems not to be working all that much for our group, meaning that, ideally the PCs should be trying to time things so that they take extended rests after only odd-numbered encounters (i.e. don't rest after encounter two, push on for one more encounter and then rest). But the fact that we only play for 120-150 minutes per session probably affects that somewhat.

    \n In my game, the players are playing a detective unit of the city watch in a cosmopolitan port city.

    -How many people are playing?

    Four PCs.

    -What level are your combat encounters? Same level as the party? Lower? Higher?

    The party just hit level 2 last night, so they've been L1 or L2 encounters.

    -How long do the encounters last?

    Between 60 & 90 minutes.

    -When do you take extended rests?

    So far, they've been taken between sessions, due to the story pacing. That'll change, though. I'm okay with this right now -- it gets them used to seeing what they can do with action points, and most the players are new to 4E.

    -Do you treat hit points, healing surges, action points, and milestones differently?

    Nope, but the closest thing the group has to a healer is a Bard.

    What I did do last encounter was make a bargain with them. They could have the 500 gold they got by clearing this one part of a human gang's hideout, but if they pressed on to the next section without even a short rest, they would be able to claim more. If they didn't, then the other force would take that part of the hideout and they would only get the 500G. They played out the decision and pushed on. So, I'm playing with what an encounter is, because of the format we're restrained to due to time.

    \n The one game that I am currently DM-ing doesn't QUITE fit that model, as we do longer sessions less frequently, but the usual pattern is similar.

    4-6 players + DM

    Encounter Builder, typically running between PL-1 and PL+1 for most encounters, PL+2 or 3 for "boss fights."

    Usually a ratio of about 3 combat encounters to 1 non-combat encounter (skill challenge or problem-solving/exploration scene), with BRIEF just-talking scenes here and there in between.

    Extended Rests are typically a "fade to black", but also serve as an opportunity for inter-PC banter, plot development, etc from time to time - I'll usually ask "anybody have anything they'd like to play out during the rest?" unless we're tight for time.

    Action Points are by-the-book, as are surges, etc.

    Encounters are all over the place in terms of length. Usually I try to go with about a 3-1 ratio of PL or lower encounters for each tough one. A standard or easy encounter typically runs 30-60 minutes, more toward the lower end of that scale. Hard or Boss encounters can take up around 90 minutes. I'll typically have monsters start to flee or surrender once things start to go south for them badly, or simply cut to "I think you guys won. Want to burn a surge and call it a win?" if I sense restlessness.

    Naturally, everything takes a bit longer when we have a full table than when only 4 people show up.

    \n Most of my sessions had one combat. Some had two.

    Usually me + five players. Sometimes four.

    The encounters were 90 minutes or more; they were difficult and usually had multiple stages along with changing goals and environments.

    In the story, combats were generally days or even weeks apart so there were plenty of extended rests between.

    I didn't change any of the rules with regards to hit points, healing surges, action points or milestones. The value of some feats, items, and ability scores changed dramatically as a result, but we were okay with that and it worked just fine.

    I learned that with the above methodology I should throw N+4 or even N+5 encounters at the party because of all the resources they had for each combat. But in doing that I would to raise the number of opponents and other hazards, NOT their opponents' level or the combat might feel like a grind. I thankfully learned that from others before running the game.
