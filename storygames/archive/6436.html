\n Hello everyone,

I'm fixing to run a fantasy campaign that will be (partially) about something you might call magical special forces unit reacting on urgent calls to fix a range of problems regular special forces would, just with detecting magic instead of looking for fingerprints and hurling a fireball instead of launching a rocket. I'm sure you know what I mean. I have a setting and a bunch of plot hooks ready in my head, but I'm still very open to suggestions.

Any ideas/advice from fellow storygamers before I start? Anything from "you should definitely use D&D 4ed for that" to "we've had this really fun gaming episode once..." is appreciated.

    \n I was pondering using a similar premise, maybe with PTA: a kind of SWAT team in a fantasy high magic city.

    The team would comprise, off the top of my head:

    1 - Recon/Stealth: lightly armed, protected with invisibility and stealth wards, ideally a thief/rogue skillset I guess.
    2 - Protector/Tower: as soon as the squad teleports on the site launches various area spells locking teleports out, reducing other mages powers, and so on. Ideally, he could plant a wide area ward building a "dome" to trap inside the wrongdoers.
    3 - One or more guerrilla fighters, specialized in close distance magical combat and disabling: fast, with weapons/tools that take down the offenders fast (with or without killing them, it's up to you)

    Optionally,
    4 - A "heavy weapons" guy, generally some kind of battle mage able to lay down suppression fire, area effect spells and the good old fireballs.
    5 - A crack shot, specializing in long distance magical disabling/killing... Imagine a magic wand/rod with a magic scope and shoulder mount... a magic crackshot rifle, basically.

    I'd call the series

    "The Tower Shield", and the squad leader would definitely be played by Michael Chicklis in full dwarven regalia (but always bald) :-D

    \n You could use Pinnacle Empty Quiver to run it. The latest version is much simplified. In the text, the Specialty Roles would have to be changed to more appropriate types and five skills per Specialty would have to be dreamed up. I think the Soldiering and Combat skills could be left much the same.

    Sample changes to Roles:

    Head Wizard = Commander
    Wizard of Power = Weapons Sergeant
    Tinkerer/Alchemist = Engineering Sergeant
    Cleric = Medical Sergeant
    Necromancer = Communications Sergeant
    Empathic Wizard = Intelligence Specialist

    Anyway, the game works now and just needs some fine-tuning and if the system is up your alley, it would work for this without too much modification.
