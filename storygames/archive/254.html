Critical Mass and Buy-In

Levi Kornelsen
February 2006 edited February 2006 in Story Games
So, I've got my playtest kit out there - the introductory demo. And a few different groups (three, thus far) have played through it. They liked it fine, but that's not what I'm interested in with this thread.

The groups all reported back stuff that translates as "my players bought in only partly, and then came apart at the end of the game". Asking a couple of the testers about this, many of them noted that since there was no indication of a way to take play anywhere further at this point, the players only 'bought into' the game to a certain degree.

I saw much the same thing with my demo of Dogs over the weekend. People got it, they bought in most of the way, and then a few of them came back out slowly as they realized the night was ending up, and there were no copies of the book left for them to get.

I begin to suspect that there's some level of 'critical mass' in terms of game material / continued group play where buy-in becomes more likely.

Anyone have experiences related to this, or thoughts on it to share?
