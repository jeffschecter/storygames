    Translating the "Hierarchy of Sin" is the big problem i see for any Dogs conversion.

    It might just be my personal philosophy, but the Sins really seem to need a "base of goodness" that has become corrupted - and can be uncorrupted with enough effort, enough sacrifice. I cannot quite see how that would work with a post-apocalyptic setting in which you're visiting *alien* communities.

    Have you thought about trying to build the "Sin tree" the other way around? Like, there is some big bad wrong in this place, and that leads to the place being in need of "saving" - and capable of being saved. I doubt that would look much like Dogs, however.

    Okay, what do you think about starting the "What's Wrong" tree with "How They Survived," then?

    How They Survived: They avoided the radiation by living underground, behind giant tanks of water.
    The Flaw: There was only so much space underground.
    Injustice: The elite of the community lived in the safest levels underground; the proles lived nearer or on the surface.
    Exploitation and Suffering: As they were genetically damaged, the proles nearest the surface were sterilized. Without mutant children to worry about, the proles are "able" to work up on the surface for the benefit of the elites, and are reduced to slave labor.
    Rioting and Violence: The proles are building bombs on the surface to take down to the caverns of the elites.

    A little one-sided, but you get the picture.
