\n Hi hi,
\n
\nName's Mark (yeah I know my nick is not my name, Rob, but I got this nick going so long ago, I'm kinda glued to it), and I'm working on the Elthos RPG Project. Basically I created a rules system and setting(s) in high school in 1978, and I've played it happily ever since. In 1994 I started programming my rules, and in 1996 began the project proper with a couple of high school friends. We're all D&Der's from the days of old. So basically the rules have been play tested with my friends between 1978 and 1994, and I've gotten the old double plus good thumbs up on them. So now I'm working on putting them into a form that can be used by others.
\n
\nIn 2006 I started the Literary Role Playing Game Society of Westchester with some local folks. We're dedicated to the proposition that we can create literary quality worlds, ala Tolkien, CS Lewis, George MacDonald, Charles Williams (note all members of the Inklings of Oxford) and apply them to our RPGs (which tend to be homebrews). We were having meetings at ye old local pub. I started hanckering for a mini-system we could use to run game ideas at the pub. So I began what was supposed to be a short phase of the project distilling down my main rules into a mini-system. That system resolved over the next six months into The One Die System. It's a miniature of my basic rules, and handles everything that the main rules do, but with tiny numbers and runs on one six sided die. Yup, I know it's impossible, but there you have it. Being a programmer by trade, and having the core system rules already programmed to completion (Elthos Prime), I decided it would be a great fun thing to do to migrate to a web interface. So I started with the ODS system to do that since the code is clean, the rules are a simplified version. So now I'm almost done with that aspect. Following? I know this is kinda complicated setup.
\n
\nAnyway, while all this was going on, I've also been working on Elthos setting. That's also a big project, and somewhat disconnected from the rules in so far as you could potentially play the setting with any rules system. It's just I've always played it with my rules. The setting is not so much a story with history and details about what happened in the world. It's more of a framework of symbolic relationships between geographies, deities, and various kinds of creatures and things in the world that form a network of correspondences that can be used by the GM (me) to help guide the back story of the game, and provide symbolism that remains coherent over time. I do, of course, have core Elthos histories and such that have come out of play over the years, but for other GMs I think those stories would serve most likely as simply and example of an Elthos Implementation, rather than a "You must use this history". So I'm also starting the process of working on the setting book (ok, so I'm almost almost ready to start working on that), but I do have a steadily cohesifying visual display of correspondence network, which is very handy (at least for me so far).
\n
\nThe main focus for me of the Elthos setting is The Hero's Journey, and as such I'm working on a system to template various world mythologies into what I'm calling Mythic Templates. These are story arcs that follow certain classical themes and utilized certain archetype characters, which I've associated to the Tarot and Astrology. The whole system is linked to a basic D&Dish alignment system which I've mathematically rationalized for use in my game. So far I think the system appears to be working out well, and I've been able to use the core concepts of the system to generate some pretty great stories from the game. I will be publishing examples as time permits.
\n
\nSo what am I up to at this point? Game testing on the ODS Rules, the Application, and the GM's use of the Elthosian Symbolic Framework are the next step. I live in Westchester NY. If anyone is interested in helping me with some game testing at any level, please drop me a line. Thanks!
\n
\nPS - Thanks you guys at Dreamation for the enthusiasm and hard-knuckle Game Design Panels! Ready to take the next leap! Luke, Fred, Rob, and the rest of you guys have really given me some great feedback and motivation! Thanks!

The purpose of the ODS is to provide a very light weight game for use by the Literary RPG Society or similar groups that want to run a RPG in a public setting where they won't need a lot of 'stuff' to do so, such as books, and dice, and stuff. It's a fast paced game, that may well serve, actually, as a fun drinking game. Characters can be rolled in five minutes or less. The rules can be memorized and the math done in one's head. It uses one six sided die for equipment. At the same time it allows for a wide diversity of RPG mechanics such as the use of skills and magic, combat, tactics, and so on. However, what it doesn't cover is the setting. The caveat is that the game is geared toward the traditional style of RPG - only with tiny numbers, and has basic D&Dish mechanics. If you're not into that style of play then you probably won't like this rules system. The other thing it doens't talk about is creative agenda. I'm leaving both setting and gamesmastering/playing techniques for another book in order to distinquish between the two and separate them. That way people who like the rules can use them for whatever setting and with whatever creative agenda rules they like, and still not be made to feel that they're "not playing this right" or having to "cheat". I will get to the other books later on, but am working on them or towards them.     \nSo... what is the game about?
    \n---------------------------------------------------
    \n
    \nWell, the game is open ended in terms of the ODS Rules (and the larger more complex core rules for the prime system). In terms of setting it is also open ended, in that the Elthos world covers a lot of territory in terms of genre by virtue of the geographic symbolism intrinsic to the world. So the setting is open ended in that the GM and/or Players can decide for themselves what they want to do with it. What is established in the setting is the mytho-symbolic framework, which is linked to the astro-tarot deck and the mythic template cards. Within that structure the GM and/or Players (depending on your prefered creative agenda model) can determine storylines, history, etc, that would make sense for their game.
    \n
    \nThat said, there is *my* Elthos story, and it has it's own history, politics, fables, etc. For that, which other people might like, the game is about The Heroe's Journey. It focuses on the Hallows Quests of Sovereignty, the path to kingship, the cause of the wasted land, and the reserection of the human spirit after catastrophic failure. I play it as a game that encourages teamwork, planning, risk taking, and the spirit of adventure.
    \n
    \nThat said, you can play it any way, dark and foreboding, chaotic and freedom-loving, lawful and chivilric, lawful and grim, ... just depending on what you are interested in and find fun. So... my example game will be my current World, which is about Chivalry and the Hallows Quest. Other GMs will be able to see in this example how the game system works and how things pin to the Astro-Tarot system to make them work for their own worlds settings. So, ultimately, the game is about what you as the world weaver want it to be about.

    \n Ok... the elevator pitch:

    The Elthos RPG is about The Heroe's Journey. It focuses on the Hallows Quests of Sovereignty, the path to kingship, the cause of the wasted land, and the reserection of the human spirit after catastrophic failure. I play it as a game that encourages teamwork, planning, risk taking, and the spirit of adventure. The setting is embodied in two sets of cards, an Astro-Tarot deck, and a set of Mythic Template cards which are used in conjunction to help build the story world, and guide it during play.

    It has two Rule Systems, either of which can be used, and both of which come with extensive computer support. One, there is an ultra light-weight RPG system that I designed for fast and furious games that require very little learning time, and very little equipment. The rules can be learned in a half hour and memorized.

    The second system is the larger more complete rule system which expands the first system outward and completes it, and would be used for more comprehensive long term games where players want to focus more on character and plot development within the game.

    Ok. How's THAT?

    \n Well that's a tall order because it's not a simple system, and it has a number of usage scenarios. However, I'll give you one example that pertains to how Cosmological Mapping of the Astro-Tarot System is intended to be used during the World Weaving phase of campaign design.

    -------------------------------------------------------------
    Pluto is in Sagittarius. Venus is in Libra. Mercury is in Venus. This means that the Libra has its home planet Venus within it\u2019s celestial region, and therefore Libra (Justice) and Venus (the Empress) are both double their normal power. Libra, an Air sign, also has the Air planet Mercury, and therefore is at an even more optimal state of power, but for a short period of time since Mercury moves swiftly through the zodiac as does Venus. Pluto, a water planet, on the other hand is in the Fire sign of Sagittarius. Water is neutral to fire (classical astrology), and so neither Pluto nor Sagittarius are effected by this in terms of power level. Pluto, since it moves very slowly, will be in Sagittarius for a long time, and therefore it\u2019s influences tend to be cultural, broad based, and change slowly. Thus, the temple of the Elkron Pluto (Hades), is at a neutral level of power, and for a long time. Someday it will be at the height of its power, and then for a long time as well, as it will also be at a low ebb of power when it enters Taurus, where it will also remain for a long time. Death moves slowly around the Celestial Island of Elthos. But for now it is neutral.

    Thus, the region of Sartar (the South Eastern part of the Obsidian Empire, known as the Desert of the Crimson Sands where the Red-Antmen dwell with the fiery races) is Neutral in power (assuming no other planets are in Sartor\u2019s region of the sky). The Crimson King is in a stable position, with his armies at the ready, and the population stable in terms of productivity and cultural mood (no rebellions flaring at this time).

    On the other hand the realm of Libra is at a (temporary) height of power, with Mercury and Venus in Libra at the same time (which happens once every year or so, and then only for a few days). Thus, Libra, whose Tarot / Jungian Archetype is Justice, is at the height of her powers. It is then that the King of Oswaldia is advised by the high priestess of Libra to send for the Hammer of Law from the temple of Justice so that Roland and his peers might go on the quest to seek the Spear of Destiny, for he is advised that only by Justice can the spear be wrested from its terrifying guardian - the Giant Scorpion Hzbrados (Death Sting). With the success of that Quest Oswald will be one step closer to aquiring the needed sacred Hallows to heal the wasteland, and set to rights the grievous wrongs which plague the land from the south.

    And so, with the Hammer of Law in his hands, Roland and the Peers venture forth, lances high, white banners flapping in the breeze, and gallantly charge off to the Western Mountains under the rising twin stars, Venus and Mercury, gleaming above the horizon...
    -------------------------------------------------------------

    That is just a hypothetical example, but gives some idea of how the system is intended to be used by world weavers to guide the symbolic influences that may shape a world and give depth, inner meaning and symbolic coherency to the back story. I'm leaving out, for the sake of brevity, the technical mechanics of how this is done. It should, I hope, at least provide you with the conceptual framework of how the Astro-Tarot section pertains to World Weaving. There are, as I say, other uses, but those are both in development and rather a bit more complicated to explain (mostly because they are under development). Nevertheless, does that help?
