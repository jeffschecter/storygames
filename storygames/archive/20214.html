\n I've just published Soth, my game about small-town cultists who are trying to summon a dark god. It's sort of an anti-Call of Cthulhu. You need to conceal your murderous crimes from family and friends, and deal with the investigators trying to stop you.

We maxxed out the posts in this month's 'Stuff to Watch' and Games in Development' threads, so I've started this one to answer peoples' questions.

I'll tag in @Catty Big, @Colinaut, @Cneph, @Meinberg (who previously expressed interest in being kept in the loop), @BeePeeGee (who wanted to see an example how Soth is played), and @ToddSpengo (who wanted to see an example of Soth's diceless conflict resolution system).


An actual play example

So, Soth uses the general structure of Apocalypse World. There are two major strands to its diceless mechanics:

* The Keeper (aka the GM) gains Suspicion by evaluating how effectively the cultists are covering up their crimes, and then spends Suspicion to put the cultists under pressure. I think of it as an economy for Apocalypse World's hard moves.
* Conflicts are resolved by determining an action order and then the Keeper evaluating each action's effectiveness.

I've just written up three examples of play at my blog: http://stevehickeygames.blogspot.co.nz/2015/06/soth-three-examples-of-how-game-works.html

This was an entry in Game Chef 2013. Most of the ensuing playtesting has been here in New Zealand: with about half-a-dozen different groups and GMs at three different conventions. (Actual play reports are a bit quiet.)
