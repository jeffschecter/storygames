    \n Yup, I remember Sean's hack. That link's link to the pdf is dead, but the one on his site HERE isn't.

    If you want a Brainer, the first thought that comes to my mind is a Native American shaman-type; they're set apart from the other people around in the west, they have strange customs and traditions and do things that Wild-West Science can't explain. You need to move carefully to avoid making it a really stereotypical thing, but it's the first idea off the top of my head.

    \n So, looking over the Brainer moves made me think that the playbook could also represent part psychologist, part mentalist, part hypnotist, and part conman.

    All the moves work fine since they all require time and/or contanct. One exception might be the need to add a caveat to Direct Brain Whisper and say that the "Brainer" has to have implanted the thought previously.

    And In-Brain Puppet Strings would have to have an explanation for harm... I got nothing for that other than self-mutilation or something (huh and Deep Brain Scan can do harm too).

    Well, just replace/remove the harm options I guess! I can think of a few ways to re-write the moves sans harm and have 'em work fine.
