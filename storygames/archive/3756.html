\n New, from the people who brought you Magestrike Ultimus: MageStory Ultimus, the ULTIMATE Story Game!

MageStory Ultimus takes all the best bits from Story Games you know and love and puts them in one game, for the ultimate Narrativist experience.

Previously a 24-hour RPG (freely downloadable), this $30 game features:\n
    \n
  • Fourteen Key Phrases, including "But, verily, I charge you to roll dice!" and "I crave excitement! Let us play another game"!
    • \n
  • d4 -1 GMs!
    • \n
  • Fifty two Keys: including the Key Of Awesome (get XP when you're awesome) and the Key Of Deep Meaningful Issues!
    • \n
  • Three types of Fan Mail: for "That Was Awesome", "That's Scary" and "You're Really Immersed, Aren't You?"
    • \n
  • Physical, Mental, Social and Spiritual traits!
    • \n
  • Seven mandatory rituals, including a circular dance at the start of the game, which are non-optional, an integral part of the game and mandatory!
    • \n
  • Full explanation of What The Game Is About, What The Players Do and What The Characters Do!
    • \n
  • One hundred pages of setting, except it's like the good shit, like Polaris, not like the stuff that White Wolf do!
    • \n
  • Guaranteed immersion and non-deprotagonisation!
    • \n
  • Certified narrativist!
    • \n
  • A fully playable game, in one book*, with no supplements**!
    • \n
\nMageStory Ultimus: available as soon as our printer decides to print it!

* Reading of Actual Play threads is required to actually play the game.
** Maybe a couple of settings.

Graham

    Posted By: Ian O'RourkeIt better have conflict resolution or I'm not buying it!
    \nOh, it does - in fact, the rules include the all-new Tear based conflict resolution system, where the two players in the conflict trade character sheets and let their tears (that is, the manifestation of their inner turmoil) erase the ink describing the other character's Keys. You'll be able to buy vials of salt-sensitive purple ink from the website once the game is published, and each game session has to start with a water drinking ritual for hydration.

    Optionally, you can use a deck of cards - you know, if your players just can't handle the intensity of the soul searching inherent in the Tear system. I recommend working out the group's Creative Agenda ahead of time to see if your players are really ready for this - if you have to resort to the cards, you may be better off playing a more light hearted game like Urchin. MageStory Ultimus is a serious game for serious players.
