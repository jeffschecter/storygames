\n WOW!

Gen Con's Games on Demand was way more successful than we realized!

Breakdown:

- We collected around 1,400 tickets.
- For our increased size, 900 would have been a success.
- This doesn't even count all the awesome scheduled events (like Marvel).
- GenCon is VERY happy and called it a BLOCKBUSTER success.
- Hopefully (but no guarantees) we will be promoted as a premier event for 2013.

Thanks:

I want to thank Steve our esteemed leader!

I want to thank the organizers Terry and Jason (and me but thanking myself is weird).

I want to thank the Origins GoD team, Kira and Jim for making Origins awesome which helped GenCon in terms of excitement, exposure, and education.

I want to thank all the hosts Matthew, Travis, Jeromy, Jeremy, Mendez, Kira, and Steve.

I want to thank all the GMs (and those who helped us with GMs): Steve, Jason, Terry, Mendez, Matthew, Travis, Jeremy, Tracy, Jason, Kira, Mark, Timo, Hamish, Ben, Marissa, Derrick, Chris, Adam, Tresi, Matt, Nykki, Mark, Neal, Ryan, Kit, Joe, Scott, Jeffrey, Mark, James, Andrew, Steve, Gregor, Jim, Bob, Chris, Jeromy, Mike, Trevis, Philippe, David, Cam, and Simon.

I apologize if I missed any GMs. We also had plenty of people run pickup games at or around Game On Demand like Eppy and Emily.

Thank you to Gen Con, Peter Adkison and especially Derek Guder!

Thank you to Indie Press Revolution, Celstyle, The Indie Game Developer Network, Hamster Press, Machine Age, Pelgrane Press, Margaret Weis Productions.

Thank you to Andy for organizing Story Games!

Thank you to all past Games On Demand organizers, staff, GMs, and players!

Thank you to all the players! There ARE SO MANY OF YOU! Wow! I just... wow! I want to say I don't have the words except I've already used a lot of words :-P

Success:

I want to add that success depends on your goals.

This was partly a success of size, excitement, organization, proving ourselves to Gen Con, increasing awareness, and simply rising to an ever greater challenge than ever before and not only meeting expectations.... far exceeding them!

But this was also a success of communication. The amount of feedback and surveys we collected has not only been appreciated but will fundamentally help shape GoD's future!

But while I can only speak for myself, I want to also add that now that we have proven we can deliver at a larger stage, the next step is even more important. Much more. I'd like to see more diversity in games and in GMs (and GM-less, GM-full games). I'd like to see a quieter (where possibly), more comfortable space to play (I've already begun field testing harassment and safety policies locally). I want to see smaller lines, faster signups, more communication, clearer procedures, and making GoD easier for all those involved while never forgetting what makes GoD different and special.

I'm ridiculously excited!

(buys his ticket to next year's Gen Con)
