\n There are some really great tools out there for playing online: Maptool, Fantasy Grounds, the upcoming EpicTable. There are also some really great people whom we don't get a chance to play with due to the circumstance of geography. I know I'd really like to play with a lot of you guys.

Fouruglymonsters.com organizes an online con every year called iCon, and it brings lots of people together through the magic of the internet. There are tons of d20 (ugg) games, and Savage Worlds (less ugg) games. Let's take this idea and improve on it...mainly by changing the games being played.

Does anyone want to jump on and help me steer the ship?

    \n The idea is this:

    We choose a weekend. We schedule lots of events (games played over skype and/or maptool). We sell pdfs at discount or give stuff away. We premiere ashcans and demo games online. We get as many creators as possible to run their games for people.

    My vision is to get lots of these indie gamers into virtual tabletops and to get a lot of virtual tabletop players into indie games.

    \n Many games can be played primarily on IRC, with say MapTool as a...well... map tool.

    The benefits are dice scripts, multiple channels (different games, IC and OOC, etc) that other people can lurk in to watch, logs for posterity, and it tends to be pretty stable.

    You could also do skype, but then you still need something for the dice and such.

    \n I'm in, if it doesn't conflict with an existing con that I am attending. In fact... consider a winter time (northern hemisphere) con, to take advantage of the "dead" con season.

    Other random ideas:
    * A central chat channel/web page which represents the "lobby."
    * Online registration, like a real con, so we know what will make and what won't.
    * A "dealer room" on the main site, which is just a shopping cart system and typical check-out (PayPal, credit card).
    * Slot setup 24 hours a day, to account for multiple time zones--maybe even slots chunked into sets of, say, four time zones (so all of US would have one set of slots, Europe another, central and east Asia--we got any Asians--would be two other sets of slot). Thus, one could register for whatever slot, any time during the 24-hour run, but would have to deal with time zone (e.g. I could register for a European game slot, but I'd be playing early in the morning; or vice versa).
    * As part of registration, have folks submit face shots, and let the reg site show them so that folks can see who they are playing with (sort of) when they are playing. Note that Google Chat (and other web-based chat, I'm sure) already has this functionality.

    ...in fact, if everyone had a Gmail account, this could all run under Google: register with Calendar (and Calendar synching), chat with GChat, distribute character sheets and campaign info via Google Docs, archive any and all exchanges (chat, email); hell, even setup a Google Store for the dealer room. Each game could even setup its own Google Group, to leverage the Pages and files functionality. No need to roll anything yourself or even find a web host.

    Hmm.....
