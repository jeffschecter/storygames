\n(These are tv shows where people play story games. AW in particular means a particular season of people playing Apocalypse World in a memory diseased water world.)

So to my surprise, I'm seeing way more similarities than I expected.
TG is a traddy game with a bunch of pre-written encounters and a sorta raily structure. But\u2026 looking at Adam generate the fronts, they kind of give off a similar vibe. \u201dI want to make an enclave of mutants\u201d
Both games feature player input into world descriptions (the golden beetle?from TG comes to mind). In both games, players seem happy.
Both games feature a lot of ability tests. In the case of AW, it's in order to generate 7-9 results to drive the game. In TG, I don't really understand why. We haven't really seen any failure yet except fail forward.
I know that I can't run games like Pramas or Wheaton does it, and kinda not play in them either (I'm too non-linear). That's the biggest difference. But in seeing how similar they are, AW comes across as traddier than I thought.

AW has more choices than TG, it seems. That's what makes PbtA more fun to run and to play than AGE (TG's rules). But when it comes to AW, the playground to make those choices in can sometimes come across as post-hoc and "no-myth" or just a few planned threats. In TG, the playground is like a guided tour, in AW it's "created just a few steps ahead of you".
Wil certainly seems ready to improvise when it comes to details and the same goes for Adam.

They are streaming their games like any other let's play show, and talking about their games. And it's great and it's within genre and it's pretty much replaced normal television for me.

      But when I see TitansGrave, it's heartbreaking. What I love:
      \u2022 They are so serious, they're in character most of the time
      \u2022 Everyone really, really cares about it
      \u2022 The art is great, there's music, sound effects, they built a big set in a studio...
      \u2022 Seriously, the sound production, their mikes etc. On RollPlay/Roll20/CritRole, there's crappy mic over skype which makes it tiring for the ears, all the noise. Here, it's a dream to listen to.
      \u2022 Wil's narration is great, it really shows that he cares.

      What breaks my heart?
      It's so much all of my prejudice against trad come to life, come to more life than I thought possible. In the first episode, everything's going great, Wil's great, the player's are great, and then there's gonna be like a story-telling contest at the club the characters are at. And Wil asks them all to roll to see if they know what this contest is about. And all four of them flub their rolls (this is very unlikely) and he is distraught and then he tells them that they know anyway.

      The roll became meaningless. Now, these sorta mistakes happen. I can't expect perfect play. I shut the episode off then and there. Then tried again a few hours later, resuming from where I was. And again something similar happened.

      It's from so much a different tradition of what an RPG even is than I'm used to that makes it hard to watch, that makes me go "no, stop, that's not how you... uh..."
      But that doesn't mean I'm right and they're wrong. They had fun around the table. A large part of the audience loved it. It was a hit.

      I just don't get it because it's like the old 'we love pigs' thing. We who love pigs, we saw wow, this beautiful production, these amazing players, this is going to be awesome!
      And then they started butchering the pigs and putting them on sandwiches and nom nom nom and we were like nooooooo!!! How could you!!!!

      And. They also love pigs. I'm not saying they're not talented sandwich eaters or what have you. They are, for sure. They did a very very good job at what they set out to do.
      Heartbreaking for us
