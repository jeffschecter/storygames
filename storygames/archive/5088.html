\n Graham,

Received and read Play Unsafe today. A lot of it was familiar to me, but I now have something I can pass on to others in one of my groups that can explain succinctly some things I've been trying to convey for years. When I can afford it, I plan on getting a copy for several people.

The Status chapter, in particular, was useful to me. Though I'd heard lectures on the subject from my improv instructors over the years, and read similar examples from Johnstone, I oddly hadn't thought much about it in the context of role-playing. I also appreciate its relative brevity. I anticipate scanning the section titles in preparation every time I GM. It's so easy to "forget" lessons you've "learned" when they touch on uncomfortable techniques.

I'm not as quick with my improv skills now as I was in my twenties, (I put that down to diet Coke or something), but your book has re-invigorated my desire to take the lessons I've learned from improv and apply them to my games whether as a player or a GM. I'm also going to try to make it to class more - it's amazing how rusty you can become in just a few weeks at things you thought you'd gotten down. It's even more important for me because my opportunities to role-play don't come along as often as I'd like.

Thank you.
