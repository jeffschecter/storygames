\n Hey friends,

Pelgrane Press is looking for some brave souls to test my Trail of Cthulhu adventure, The Black Drop. It's a big adventure that takes place in the mysterious and desolate Kerguelen archipelago, where things are seriously FUBAR. Just a few of the many highlights: Elephant seals, Dravidian ravings, sex on top of crates of high explosives, "southern ocean glass", Victorian astronomy, friendless Nazis, a failed colony, "the unholy Lambeth of the antipodes", and some kind of kill-crazy monster. Or monsters. It's badass. It is stuffed with real history that reads and plays like the weirdest fiction. One phrase that seems to come up a lot in my playtests: "For God's sake, please kill me next."

It's an open call. Get in touch with Simon Rogers (simon at dyingearth dot com), subject "Black Drop Playtest" and he will hook you up.

    \n Spoiler:

    The Mythos angle seems more redolent of Robert E. Howard than Lovecraft proper: a dark brooding antediluvian thing relict of vanished Mu waits in its ancient cave; it slumbers and slavers in its dreaming. Only the sacrifice of souls can appease it when it threatens to wake each time the stars are right.

    So it's something he cooked up himself, albeit with great fidelity to the feel of the Mythos, and I'd give it high marks for freshness and innovation as the starkness of the choice confronting the investigators slaps them in the face.

    \n I took a cue from Trail's approach to the mythos bestiary and some good advice from Graham - I think a Keeper eager to apply a taxonomy wouldn't have any trouble finding a very appropriate terrible old monster, but things in The Black Drop are deliberately vague, because I think it is way scarier not to know what you are dealing with.

    You do have all the stats you need if you are playing in pulp mode and want to try punching evil in the face, though.
