\n Hello everybody.

I have this new RPG thing I'm working on. Today I'm fleshing out the core setting and situation elements. I've written a pitch for it; a little back-cover type blurb. I think it conveys the goal of my game design pretty well but I'm not sure how catchy it is. I've read and re-read the dang thing too many times to be able to tell if I'm getting anywhere or if I'm making it worse.

If you would, please read my little blurb and tell me what you think. If you read this on the back of an RPG book, would you open it up to read more or would you put it back on the shelf?\n
In a the last megacity of Mahajiite you are part of an elite cybernetic paramilitary police force. You must weed out the last of the aberrations and eliminate their foothold on our world. Do so in the public eye so the citizens may know that they will never be slaves again.

Destroy all Aberrations
Protect Mahajiite
Be an Inspiration for the Citizens

    \n Hey Eric,

    I found "elite cybernetic paramilitary police force" to be too much of a mouthful. That's a fifteen-syllable descriptor. I almost stopped reading at that line.

    This ad copy makes me think "well, this sounds like fascist cop worship." If that's being presented unironically and uncritically, I am going to put the book down as quickly as possible. If that's being presented ironically or critically, I will start flipping through and reading it. At this point, I don't know which it is. ... So I'm going to look for my next clue and make an assumption based on that. I'll check the graphic design of the back cover. If that doesn't give me my answer, I'll check the front cover illustration + tagline. If that doesn't give me my answer, I'll check a single random interior spread.

    \n You're kind of burying the lede there, Eric! "Do it in public so that the citizens will know they will never be slaves again" is the interesting part IMHO, put that front and center.

    It sounds a little like the premise of the comic book Marshall Law to me, where a lonely superhuman fascist cop puts the beatdown on other superhuman criminals, all by himself so they can't brag that it took a whole SWAT team to take them down. Also, cut out every extraneous word and see what's left -- the name of the city doesn't really matter here. On the other hand, it would be nice to have some idea what aberrations are. Are they similar to White Wolf's Aberrant, which is about to be re-released?

    Also, what other folks said -- when you put the words elite, police, and megacity together, your average geek will think Judge Dredd clone, for better or worse.

    \n Typo in the first sentence ("In a the" should be "In the").

    I agree you're burying the lede. You're going: setting ("last megacity"), character ("paramilitary police force"), agenda ("weed out aberrations"), situation ("you're slaves, or were recently"). Start with the situation and hint at the setting through it. Then tell them what their characters are and what they do.

    Something like:\n

    You have freed your people from slavery. You have driven most of the Aberrations out of Mahajiite, the last of the megacities. As a select member of the cybernetic paramilitary force, you must weed out the remaining Aberrations and eliminate their foothold on your world. Your actions must inspire the citizens so they know they will never be slaves again.

    Destroy All Aberrations
    Protect the Megacity
    Inspire the Citizens

    \n Ooh, Caoimhe, the cover of the book could be a scene reminiscent of a genocide, with obviously religious (or whatever) figures being herded out of a city gate, the ones in back being killed mercilessly to spur the rest onwards. The juxtaposition between something like that and the text is what would draw me to look at the book further. But then, that's probably an entirely different game than what Eric is proposing. If I were looking to play something like his or Adam's pitch as written, I'd probably want the cops to be presented as underdogs still in the midst of their revolt. Something to make it seem like it isn't just a clean-up operation, but a fight to make sure the revolt doesn't turn. Something that implies slavery to abberations is still an ongoing thing in the megacity where the abberations still exist, or else that the abberations outside the city are boiling up over the walls unbeknownst to the citizens within.
