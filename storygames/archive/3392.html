\n For those who don't know what I'm talking about you can visit Go Play Southeast.

We're about 1 month away at this point. If everyone on the list shows up we'll pretty much be at capacity. This is not to discourage anyone who wants to come from giving me a commitment right now. It's still first come, first served. However, once I've gotten to the upper limit of the facilities if you want to come you'll either have to bring a tent and sleeping bag and camp in the yard or you'll have to get a hotel room.

I haven't heard anything from anyone about how you'd like to handle food. If I haven't heard anything in the next couple of weeks, I'll just assume everyone is fending for themselves. That is a great option as far as I'm concerned. It takes all the responsibility for such things off my shoulders and I'm lazy that way. :-)

I've posted a blank schedule and a list of facilities here. Jason had a good idea and I'm adopting it. Everyone should come with at least 1 game prepped that they can run. At the beginning of each session we'll gather around a white board, list the game options available and then see what the interests are. I put my own wishlist of games up on site linked above.

I look forward to seeing everyone who is coming! It'll be nice to put faces to names I've known online for a while.

Andrew

    Informal Rule Idea: You must switch activities along the boundaries of slots. This must include interacting with at least one person you did not interact with in the previous slot.
