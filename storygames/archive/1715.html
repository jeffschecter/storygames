Help me cold-up Cold City

Jason_Morningstar
November 2006 edited November 2006 in Story Games
I'm running Cold City at MACE. My plan is to have five pre-gens, but let the players fill in trust for one another as well as their three attributes. But I really need their national and personal agendas to tightly intertwine since it is a one-shot convention game. They need to explode all over each other, fast. I've got:

RUSSIA: A Jewish doctor and party stalwart whose family is essentially being held ransom until he delivers results.
AMERICA: A squirrelly dude who spent the war with Yugoslav partisans throwing fascist sympathizers down wells.
BRITAIN: A cheerful, combat veteran Welshman who tends to see the best in people, to his discredit.
FRANCE: A confused, politically appointed war hero and submarine captain.
GERMANY: A charming lady biologist who spent the war culturing skin cells for the Alternative program.

So please, give me some suggestions for national and personal agendas that will turn these characters into a seething morass of inter-party conflict and general fantastic-ness. I'm going to throw them down a murder-hole and let them deal with sort of the package tour of Cold City horrors, but the real meat has to be their relationships and conflicting agendas. I've got acceptable national agendas, but I'd love some ideas from outside my brain.

    Well, I don't know what happened to my post, but here it is again:

    Split the party regularly!

    In my understanding, that trust and betrayal bit only really works when you do this, and I suspect the personal and national agenda come into play better under those circumstances as well.

    As a second tip, and one I noticed from the pregens in the rulebook, have national and personal agendas of each character be at least slightly internally at odds.

    Also, from a roleplaying perspective, take the time to explain what each point of trust is actually related to event-wise.
