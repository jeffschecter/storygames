\n Endeavoring to be a better GM, and since the tech has finally gotten cheap/easy/portable enough, I've finally gotten an mp3 player with record capability to capture my groups sessions. Hopefully this will also allow me to do much more in depth AP posts from my con games (with everyone's permission of course).
\n
\nIs anyone using such a device. Is the built in microphone generally good enough, or is there a better conference style mic that you know about?
\n
\nMy set up is a iRiver T30 from newegg.com which was only $40, and I could add a mic like an AudioTechnica ATR-97 ($23.95) or a Sony (either the ECM-F8 ($15) or the ECM-F01($50)) to it if need be. I was hoping the podcasting folks would have some advice on this, but most of the on-line stuff I've found has been for uni-direcitonal interview mics, not omni-directional one built to pick up a whole table of folks.
\n
\nAny tips, tricks, gotchas folks can impart.

    \n I've had good experiences with AudioTechnica mics recording music, and the model you've picked has gotten good reviews. You're also on the right track with an omnidirectional mic; the binaural thing is probably 87 times more complicated than you (or anyone) really need (and playback only works with headphones; it's not designed for room speakers or mono). Just put the ATR-97 in the middle of your gaming table and record away.

    You should probably test the recorder's built-in mic the same way and see how it fares. Since your main purpose is to capture sessions for your own use, not broadcast, it may be all you need.

    \n I've been interested in the effect that recording sessions has on the games.

    Being recorded as player, I felt on the one hand constantly aware of the device and not as able to get into the flow of the game, and on the other fascinated by listening to the session some time afterwards and learning things like how often I was not, I guess, just not paying attention to the words of the other players. (He said what? That's interesting. How could I have missed that? etc.)

    I'd be curious to ask after the fact what impact other players felt from knowing they were recorded.

    Posted By: VaxalonA laptop running Audacity with a USB condenser microphone in the middle of the table might be a better choice.
    \nThat basically describes my setup: A Blue SnowBall USB microphone hooked up to my MacBook Pro. It's quality and omin-directional mode have been excellent for recording our current PTA sessions. I really wanted to use two mics to capture the sessions in stereo, but that would have proved too expensive.

    Surprisingly enough, our group hasn't suffered from much self-conciousness from being recorded. They were a bit nervous the first session, but about half an hour in, no one even remembered the mic was there.

    \n I use a Sony ICD-P320 voice recorder, and set it on the shelf behind me. The mic is sensitive enough to hear everyone, small enough that no one really notices, and has a standard red light that indicates it's working, so I can check it at a glance. The memory will hold 4-6 hours easily.

    The software can save the files into WAV, and I will eventually use Audacity to edit the poop jokes out.

    It's rather pricey ($50-60), but very useful in a variety of situations -- for instance, I've done several interviews with it for the Fnordcast.
