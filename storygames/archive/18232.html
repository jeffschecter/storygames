    \n Are you asking how many discoveries we have per game (or game per discovery)?

    Here's the thing for me. I can only learn if I can relate what I read. I read 1000 pages of LARP theory but I couldn't understand it because I have no relation to LARP. It wasn't until I played Montsegur 1244 that I could understand the articles about their kind of immersion.

    When it comes to movies, I normally have to watch it twice to remember it. The first time I see it, it's all about the sensation, but the second time I go through the movie I seem to notice more details and take them into consideration. It's the same thing with me and reading games. The first time I read a game, it's all about the sensation. I remember when I was reading through the GM chapter of Spirit of the Century and I thought it was an all right reading. It wasn't until the second read, when I started to compare the information I read to my own experiences, that I appreciated that chapter.

    So it's hard for me to answer your question. It's a combination of playing a diverse number of games, reading sources of game theory and actually reading roleplaying games. And the roleplaying games that I read is mostly games that people recommended as something special.
