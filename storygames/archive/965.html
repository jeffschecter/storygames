    When back in the day I bought Runequest 2nd edition it came with a fairly detailed map of Glorantha, or a bit of Glorantha anyway.

    None of it was explained, stuff had names like Dragon Pass and Devil's Tower and Snakepipe Hollow, all utterly unexplained.

    It was seriously cool. My group, we'd look at the map, get buzzed by a name and the party would go there and whoever was GMing would make up something based on the name.

    Then all the details got filled in, it turned out there were extensive gazetteers for every one of those locations, the map stopped being fun at that point.

    But until then, Vaxalon has the right of it, few things are cooler than a map with unexplained details (though nowadays I'd say explicitly you meant to do that, gamers are less forgiving now than in the early 1980s).
  • ffilz
    July 2006
    I definitely like maps, and no doubt, I like having unexplained things on the map. But I disagree that having gazeteers for places on the map is bad. The trick I think is that the gazeteer has to add more unexplained things. And it needs to give you lots of bits and pieces that you can glue together. RQ got a LOT more cool after Cults of Prax was introduced. And the Snake Pipe Hollow module didn't kill the potential of that location, because, while it gave you some clue about what was there, it also showed a bunch of cave entrances, and provided you with detail for one of those entrances. And the cave it gave you had it's own unexplained bits.

    That said, I think there is a limit to how much gazeteer information is actually useable. Of course the problem is that everyone wants a gazeteer for the part of the world they're interested in. I'm bummed we never got much gazeteer information for Dragon Pass given how central it seemed to the world, meanwhile we got all this information on Prax, but that was also kind of hard to use because it was all about working for the occupying force (well at least the Borderlands box that I got way back when, it wasn't until recently that I got Pavis and Big Rubble which aren't so dependant on working for the Lunars). So I guess my problem is that the original rules sort of aimed players at playing Sartarite barbarians, but then all the subsequent source material bent away from that.
