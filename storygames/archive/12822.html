\n I just finished reading the Bag Wars Saga comic. If you happen to run across this at your LGS or at your local comic store, I recommend that you pick it up. Knights of the Dinner Table is really quite brilliant the way it captures old school, antagonistic, powergamer play and takes it to its extreme logical limits. It's beautifully encapsulated in this compilation, where the party earns a hyper-powerful bag of holding and proceed to exploit its potential within the framework of the rules.

    \n I love KODT. I dont think I've ever missed an issue, although I dont have the Bag Wars book yet. (Is there much that wasn't in the monthly comics?)

    Also, I realize that the dynamic is supposed to be that of an old-school GM-is-in-charge D&D game, taken to a humorous extreme (the Hackmaster rules, GM accreditation, the RPG company settling disputes between groups, etc). But ... you can read it as the KODT players being super-committed story gamers. They're constantly referencing events that took place "offstage", and, like good improv-ers, they never deny or contradict one another.

    Like, Bob says "I can't fight that bouncer! I lost my helmet in Hagley Town!" and Dave says "Oh yeah, that little girl who stole your helmet. She was mean." They do that stuff all the time, describing scenes we haven't seen.

    I submit that they didn't play these scenes out, they're just inventing them on the spot to fill in narrative back-story. TOTAL HIPPIE GAMERS.
