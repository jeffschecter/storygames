    Thor: One of the main reasons BW and BE has done so well in Norway is that you go through Alliance/Diamond so that they're present in the FLGS's and thus are available for people that browse through the shops.
    \n"Outland" in the Oslo S train station (top floor, in the old terminal building) is one of the best-stocked book stores for sci-fi/fantasy I've ever been in (even though it is pretty small). Their RPG section usually has one of everything for all the major systems and indie games, plus many I've never seen before. I invariably stop there when in Oslo and see games never stocked on the shelves here in California.
    Another shop, "Avalon", a few blocks north and west of there (I forget which street) is even bigger and almost exclusively RPGs. While Games Workshop takes up a great deal of shelf space, Norwegian FLGSs seem to be more aware of new and/or cool and/or indie games than the US shops I frequent.

    Paul Austad

    \n Glad you liked the cover! It was done by Robert Elneskog, who also painted the cover for the Draug supplement "Beist og Borgerskap".

    Draug is set in Norway, 1801, a time when old folkloristic beliefs were finally succumbing to new, rational ideas - such as war profiteering, for example. However, in the game, everything people believed in is true; the N\xc3\xb8kken are still lying in forest lakes, waiting for young boys to steal; the Troll live in the mountains and sometimes come out looking for human brides.

    It uses a very light version of the FUDGE system.

    Some time ago, I was considering re-writing it in English. However, my changed design sensibilities & experience with the game and the market would make the re-write a complete re-design from the ground up. And so far, it hasn't happened; I may just be finished with the game altogether, though time wil show.
