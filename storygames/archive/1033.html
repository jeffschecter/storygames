    Hey very cool. Thanks! Mind if I chime in with a couple of points?

    1) Aspect is really important; more than just a descriptor, it is pretty much the final arbiter of what your character can or can't do.

    2) One thing you didn't mention, that I think is pretty important esp. in the Story-Game aspect of Seven Leagues, is Keywords. They are actually pretty cool, a little powerful, but most of all a way for players to take over the story for a bit.

    3) I find that waiting for players to come up with a Legend first, and then underline Keywords, works great with a committed Troupe, but for a quick pickup game (like your impending playtest), I let players just write out their Keywords, then add their Legend in the course of play or after the first session. Less pure, but less daunting.

    4) And for a minor point: the Narrative Bonus counter on the CharSheet only runs to +120 because of space; in fact, you "buy" p0w3rz according to this chart (p 33);

    Tally New Trait
    +99 Add a new Charm
    +88 Remove a beginning Taboo, one which the character has had since creation
    +66 Alter an existing Charm
    +55 Eliminate a Taboo gained in play
    +44 Alter an existing Taboo
    +33 Underline (add) a new Keyword
    +22 Substitute one Keyword in the Legend for another

    Note you have to work the new ability into a story; no just "walking into a magic shop" to level up.

    Thanks for the generous review. Consider me suitably chastised on the website thing; I'm working on it!
