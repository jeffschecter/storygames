    For me, the key is taking a break between work and play. We order pizza, socialize, and generally get ourselves 'in the mode' of play instead of all having our heads at work instead. Although Gloria Mundi technically starts at 6pm on Mondays, we actually have people show up as early as 5:30, and we usually don't start until shortly after 7pm.

    The other key to playing on weeknights is to keep session short and focused. We rarely end after 11pm (since, among other things, people have to go home!) and our best sessions tend to last around three hours instead of playing through to the bitter end.

    Finally, I've found it helps a lot to have decentralized play, so that people are almost never not-playing once you begin play. During a typical session of Gloria Mundi, you'll have one GM with a player in the kitchen, two players discussing things in the bedroom, and the other three players swapping around the attention of the other two GMs, so people almost never get out of the mode of play, and rarely have to ask themselves "Why am I here instead of at home, chilling out?"

    That's what works for us, but I'm really interested to hear what other people have to say. I'm sure there are lessons we can apply!

    --Jess

    PS-edit: Oh yeah, playing after work has also forced me and my co-GMs to hone our improvisational skills, as we often don't have time to prep session. We've figured out ways to make meaningful, deeply invested story with almost no planning ahead (by front-loading work, offloading meaning-making onto players, etc.) and we use them. A lot.

    I only have experience that reinforces the points already made here:
    • Shorter sessions with active participation
    • Commute convenience makes a huge difference, as do other conveniences
      • dinner preparation handled
      • interruptions at a minimum
      • dice (or other methods of determining events) available
      • pens, pencils, paper, outlets, whatnot, set up
    • Regular scheduling (if you know it's EVERY Wednesday, you plan around it, rather than our group's "Sometimes Saturdays", and that INCLUDES prep time issues)
    • Online reinforcement (always makes a difference if I can get characters done ahead of time, or even discuss "downtime" events)
    When the little details are taken care of, it's a lot easier to slip into character and spend the limited time wisely.

    Now, we have 2 players who show up reliably at 6:00, another two who generally show up by 6:30 (one new player, one of the old players, who seems to be not consistent now), and one player who finds it hard to show up even by 7:00. So we are now almost never finishing before 11:00. We've also got one player who has only played a few sessions last year, and one this year, not sure what his schedule will actually be like.

    All along, one of the players (the one who has become less consisent) has been a snoozer. Fortunately he usually snoozes lightly, but still he misses things occaisionally. Oh, there's also the young wife who often snoozes also, but she is generally so peripherally engaged I almost don't count her (and one of these days, perhaps I'll get up the guts to address that situation - though it doesn't seem to create any negativity).

    I have come to the conclusion that weekend gaming just is out of the question for regular play, so I'm stuck with weeknight play. I'm also pretty convinced that for long term play, I want something with tactical miniatures style combat (D&D, RuneQuest, Cold Iron, etc.). So a 4 hour session is really the minimum that works well for me.

    I may have to put my foot down and say that players have to be able to commit to showing up by 6:30. We do start before the 7:00 guy shows up, but it's tricky, and sometimes, the group is small enough that he's critical, so the other option is to cancel if we won't have at least 3 other players present.

    I also need to work on being ready to really launch into things once play is ready to start (after a suitable chit chat warm up time).

    One thought might also be to shift the time to 7:00 to 11:30. That would give me a little prep window. The other thought would be to probe as to why folks don't feel comfortable bringing their dinner with them and showing up a bit earlier (the young couple are the only ones who consistently bring their dinner or arrive at 6:00 already fed, occaisionally someone else will also bring dinner).

    I am sometimes low on energy after work, but once the game starts, I usually pick right up.
