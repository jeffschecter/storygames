\n This is something I've seen here and elsewhere and while I don't think it's a huge issue, I think it might be worth discussing (or re-discussing if it's come up before.)

When I see a new thread here, I find it's easy for me to fall into a default mindset of 'This person is posting because they have a problem with their game or their experience or are otherwise veritably thirsting for my learned advice.' Which, to be fair, is not the worst of default mindsets to fall into.

Sometimes, though, it's precisely the wrong mindset to use. Sometimes people just want to celebrate, or gloat, or share, and in those cases even very well-intentioned advice can be traumatic for everyone.

I wish I knew what to do about this but I don't. Maybe it just comes down to putting 'I AM GLOATING I WILL NOT TOLERATE HELPFUL ADVICE' at the top of the first post or something. Or maybe it's not as pressing an issue as I believe.

At the risk of becoming too recursive, I should state that I would like comments and criticism and advice on this. Thanks.



Cheers,
Roger
