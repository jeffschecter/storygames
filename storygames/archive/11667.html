    \n City map. The numbers are dimensions, but they're important cuz we have powers that need those specific numbers plugged in in order to adjudicate their use. Dark purple are buildings that don't matter (no numbers), pink are buildings that do matter, yellow are outdoor areas like parks, or stretches of public street that we can effect with our powers.

    The color palette screams "sysadmin" to me, I would choose softer complementary colors if you wanted it to be friender, and maybe using some kind of very subtle abstract pattern to back the color would make it easier on the eyes.

    I would like some kind of contextual imagery, but that would make it less generic, i suppose.

    \n It reminds me of a WPF app?

    It's some kind of processor/system on a chip diagram. Yellow are general purpose cores/units, processing speed x on core memory (registers, cache, etc). The pink/fleshy color are special purpose cores/units, and can only handle certain types of tasks while still performing at full speed. The purple are communication channels, the only way to get information on/off the system. Information can only travel between them based on some kind of connection mechanic, maybe range or direct line of connection.
