This hack aims to let players embody Heroes in Middle-Earth during the Third Age. Under here is the cover.



If you are curious of what it looks like inside, you can download the character's sheets and the final document here:

Ar-Ag\xf4n : La Communaut\xe9

If enough of you are interested in this hack, I may work during my spare time on a translation with a friend of mine.

Let me know what you think of all this!

    \n Thanks for your interest.

    Here are the main differences:
    - Heroes don't go on quests directly for the Valar but for their Emissaries (Gandalf, Galadriel, Elrond, Radagast, ...) as the period is set during the Third Age when the Valar have given up on interfering directly in Middle-earth,
    - the Name die is replaced by a Race die. Mortal Heroes (Hobbits, Humans) have a d6 die, Heroes with some elven blood (dunedain, half-elves) and Dwarves have a d8 die and a Fate of 8, and full elven blood (Sindar, Sylvan) Heroes have a d10 die and a Fate of 10,
    - the heroic traits are replaced by racial traits, mortal heroes having more choices than elves and dwarves,
    - most of the abilities have been renamed to fit a medieval universe, and some abilities have been replaced,
    - the list of weapons has been expanded,
    - a new type of oath. Heroes have to make oath towards the quest objective or other Heroes in their fellowship, and failing or betraying these oaths will cost them Fate points,
    - great deeds work as titles added to the "pedigree" of the Heroes, for example Aragorn will be presented as:
    Aragorn son of Arathorn, chieftain of the Dunedain of Arnor, Captain of the Host of the West, bearer of the Star of the North, wielder of the Sword Reforged, victorious in battle, whose hands bring healing, the Elfstone, Elessar of the line of Valandil, Isildur's son, Elendil's son of Numenor.
    - when Heroes meet their Fate, based on the legend score, their end will be different according to their race.

    Finally, I've added some ideas to emulate the Council of Elrond. The Heroes meet with some Emissaries and other NPCs on a council. The Antagonist (the Dark Lord) give the primary objective, then the players, in character, argue to propose secondary objectives with the aim to achieve the primary one. The details of the quest are settled by contests amongst the Heroes, the Antagonist adding twists and complications during play to surprise the players.

    The crux of Agon is the heroes striving to outdo each other. Are the Fellowship characters competing?
    \nYou tell me.
    Does Legolas outdo the other Fellowship characters while climbing Caradhras ?
    Does Frodo outdo the other Fellowship characters while fighting the cave-troll in the Chamber of Mazarbul ?
    Does Aragorn outdo the other Fellowship characters while chasing the orcs that have kidnapped Merry and Pippin ?
    Does Gimli outdo Legolas while slaying orcs at Helm's Deep ?

    Apart from the Fellowship in the Lord of the Rings, if you create a company of dwarves, sent on a quest to reclaim a lost heirloom, will they strive to be the one that has regained it ? If two Dunadan brothers are sent together on a quest, will they strive to prove that they are the one worthy to claim the title of their father ?\n
    Using source material where cooperation and...err...fellowship is the point of it all, less so.
    \nIn Agon, even if the Heroes are competing, they still are working together to accomplish the quest at hand. And the reason why they are competing is to get the highest Legend score before they achieved their last quest, so that their name will be forever remembered or that the end of their life will be pleasant. I don't see how this is not exactly what it's happening in the Fellowship of the Ring. Even if they are working together (well not Boromir...) to help Frodo destroy the One Ring, all the companions have different long term goals. Aragorn is working since several years to gain the rightfulness to claim the throne, Sam just hopes to make his way back home, Legolas languishes to reach the sea, ...

    You will find below the links to download the pdfs of Ar-Ag\xf4n: The Fellowship; the Heroes, Minions, NPCs, Legend and High Deeds sheets and some range tracks.

    I hope that the quality of this translation will be good enough. My english skill was mostly used some years ago to write scientific papers, but the help of a dear friend certainly has improved the content produced here.

    Please, do not hesitate to comment and suggest some rephrasing if needed, I may take all rewording into account to make a revised edition in the future. Also, if some wording seems obscure to you (due to the translation from french to english), ask questions here and I will try to answer and explain it.

    Of course this hack is my own vision, you are welcomed to adjust anything in this hack as you like, and also to share it here for everyone. Please, if you have the opportunity to test it and play a campaign or some one-shot, I would enjoy reading any actual play to see how it has gone.
