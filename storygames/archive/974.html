[Names Project] Ads?

Jason_Morningstar
July 2006 edited July 2006 in Story Games
Mo suggested we "sell" ads for other indie games in the back of the Story Games Name Project book. Andy and I talked it over and we're sort of on the fence. The principal advantage to doing this is that the SGNP will have pretty broad appeal and may be picked up by lots of people who have no exposure to dirty hippie gaming. The secondary appeal is that it will rake in the drachmas for the Carter Center, our charity of choice. The disadvantages are that it may be generally uncool for such a community effort project, and that it will be a pain in the ass for me personally. So I'm interested in your thoughts on this, story-gamers.

If it were to happen, my thinking would be to allow ads in the same way that playbills and yearbooks sell space in the back - all the money is going to the charity, so buy and use the space however you like at US$50 per 6x9 page. You'd send me a copy of the electronic receipt for your donation to the Carter Center along with your camera-ready artwork.

So - would that work? Is it a good idea? Would you "buy" a page? Is US$50 the right price? What do you think?

    OK, I talked it over with Super K and here's what we've come up with:

    $25 gets you a 1.5" by 4.5" ad connected with a list you specify, typically on the second page of the set but at the layout guy's discretion. This is the size of the sample I posted earlier.

    $50 gets you a 8.5" by 5.5" ad, wherever we want to put it, most likely in the back of the book in a group, but maybe clustered in the middle or something.

    Your ad will be inserted exactly as you give it to us. If you lack the ability to produce an ad, we'll be glad to just add some text you provide us in a distinctive font inside a border, but it won't look good or fancy. Consider asking a designer friend to help you out.

    Submit a camera-ready greyscale .tif file, ideally at 600 dpi, either as an attachment or a URL we can grab, along with a copy of the electronic receipt from The Carter Center indicating your donation of the desired amount (or more!). If you buy a small ad, also indicate which list you'd like it to be associated with if you have a preference. There will be a maximum of one ad per list, so first come, first served. Lists may change between now and layout, but we'll all work together to make sure your ad is as awesome and useful as possible. Feel free to buy an "ad" that promotes something other than a game - like, say, story-games.com for example, or your favorite podcast or whatever. If your ad will make the book look stupid, we'll reject it. Sound good? Any questions?
