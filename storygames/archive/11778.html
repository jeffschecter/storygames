\n So the hip in thing seems to be boxed sets; so, I decided I have to make a super premo box. My idea with Microlite20 is that it can easily be played with little prep so I want to emphasize the on the go style of gaming. The box will be a small wooden briefcase style box with slide out drawers to store all your game gear. When the box is open it folds flush and locks to reveal a grid wet erase marker playing surface. This way you can have a play surface any place that can be used to draw up rooms and push minis around even at a park.

So my questions I had were:

Other than 2 pencils, a pack of wet erase markers, wet naps, two 8.5 x 11 hard backs, a folder with paper, and 2 sets of dice; what would you want to bring to game?

Would you rather have a small fold out surface that is the size of two d&d books, or a full size brief case opened flat?

Is this the stupidest thing ever and I should just not do it?

    \n I wouldn't say dont do it, but I would question that the hippest thing ever is box sets.

    They disappeared in dnd for quite a while and the new red box seems very experimental to me (like the little 32 or 64 softcovers they are producing for the races, and a few other things). While im certain there is a large amount of 'Daww Nostalgia Box' I'm pretty certain price points are going to dictate your consumers more than if there is a box or not.

    That said you box sounds nice, you may also want to consider putting a magnetic underlay under your erasible surface and shipping with some adhessive metal/magnetic disks so that you can actually do that play anywhere thing.

    That said, If I was playing at home (or someone elses home. etc), space and supplies are not an issue, so it doesnt really sell it to me. Also if your briefcase size is anything like mine its fairly big, I wouldn't want to lug it anywhere, especially full of books and papers and stuff, which makes it an awkward torn between convience and completeness on the go, and actually lugging the thing on the go.

    Anyway I hope that this helps, and you get to do that thing you want to do.
