\n Although it hasn't seen a lot of discussion here, you may have heard about the Cel*Style project. Cel*Style is a label for anime and manga role playing games, and includes Maid, Bliss Stage, Panty Explosion and several other great games.

Anyway, you may have noticed that our site (CelStyle.com) is currently down. That's actually why I'm making this post. I want to make sure that anyone searching for Celstyle.com over the next week or so will know that it hasn't just disappeared. Or at least that it's only disappeared temporarily.

For the next week I'll be posting Cel*Style info at Atarashigames.com. You can contact my about Cel*style at jake at atarashigames.com.

Thanks,

Jake

    Posted By: fnord3125Posted By: jake richmondClassroom Deathmatch and new versions of Panty Explosion and Bliss Stage.
    Sometime, in this or another thread (whichever you feel is more appropriate) could you tell us more about the planned new versions of Classroom Deathmatch and Panty Explosion? I've got both of them (though sadly I haven't managed to get them to the table yet). I'd also like to hear more about the new version of Bliss Stage, if someone can talk about it (Ben isn't really around here much lately, is he?).

    I can't speak much for Bliss Stage, other then to say that I think the new book will be amazing. Back when Ignition Stage was originally released there was the promise of a prettier, more polished edition of the game somewhere down the road. This is it. I get a nerd boner just thinking about it.

    We're putting Classroom Deathmatch back in print, and taking the opportunity to clean up the text and reformat the book. It's a smaller package now (both in dimensions and page count). We made a few small changes to the rules (mostly dealing with flashbacks), added a bit of art and redesigned the character cards (which are now download only). So not a new game, just a cleaned up (and less expensive!) re-issue. We'll also be have a full PDF version of the game that you can download for free, just like before.

    Panty Explosion is pretty much a whole new thing. I realized that the game I've been running at cons for the last few years is very, very different from the one we published back in 96. Matt and I have redesigned the game completely. If you're familiar with the old game, the new one will be a cake walk to use, and you'll have a whole bunch of "ohhh,, that's soooooooo much better" moments. I'm thinking of it as the same game, but with different rules. Cleaner, faster, easier and more fun. Less pointless "rpg stuff" like dice stats, character statistics and long, drawn out character creation. More fun stuff that facilitates ease of play, shared narration and group story building.

    You won't NEED a copy of the new book, but you'll certainly WANT one. Especially if you've been having trouble getting people to play Panty Explosion with you. The new book is like a black hole. Players will either be helplessly pulled toward it or do everything they can to stay away from it.

    Posted By: fnord3125Will there be more concrete stuff in the new PE on the theoretical "gm-less" playmode I've occasionally heard you talk about? One of my biggest hurdles with potentially playing PE or CD is that my only truly reliable gaming group is 3 players, and the Rival/Best Friend thing only works if you have a minimum of 3 players (and if someone has to GM, well...)
    \nYes. PE works best with 4 or more players, but the new version of the game allows for GMless play, and makes 3 player play viable. You can play with a set GM who doesn't have a character of her own, or you can share the GM duties between the players. The sweet spot is in the middle, and the book details all three approaches.\n
    Posted By: Joe McGuffinJake, I'm very much looking forward to the new, cleaned-up PE (I only ran the original version the once and it didn't go over too well), but I have to admit I'm a little sad the new version's look is a lot more moefied. It looks poppy and snappy, but I have a special affection for the weirder, more Carrie-by-way-of-Mai-the-Psychic-Girl art style of the original. New look for a new version, I guess.
    \n

    We wanted to do something really, really different with this new version of the game. If old Panty Explosion felt like a combination of Ringu, Mai and Heathers, we wanted new Panty Explosion to feel like a cross between Persona 3, Cat Paradise and Blood: The Last Vampire, with a bit of Azumanga Daioh thrown in. Or to put it another way, I got tired of doing art based on photo reference. This is just closer to how I normally draw.

    If you do prefer the old style, we probably will be returning to it for a more spooky and subdued ghost hunting game in the spirit of the Fatal Frame series. but that's a bit of a ways off, because between now and then I have to finish...\n
    Posted By: merb101Jake, if'n ya don't mind me askin', where are you on Magical Land of Yeld?
    \nStill working on it. I took a year off to do Panty Explsoion and color a friends comic, and Nick has been busy with The Tulip Academy Society for Dangerous Young Gentlemen. Now that those projects are close to wrapping we can work on Yeld full time till it's done. At this point we have to assemble our giant, fragmented text, clean it up and condense it down into a usable rules set. There's also a bunch of art, which is really the best part for me.

    So... sometime next year? I imagine we'll be done around Jan or Feb. Look for more info on the Cel*Style site toward the end of this year.
