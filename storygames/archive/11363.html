\n I just added someone to my ignore script, and it occurred to me that this might be good information to share once again. Here's Brand's tutorial, which was written by Brand, who is probably ignoring me right now in perfect happiness:

Dave Cleaver's script is here: http://dscleaver.googlepages.com/ignoreusercomments.user.js

You've got to have greasemonkey an firefox to use it, obviously.

Go to the \u201cTools\u201d menu, and scroll down to select \u201cGreasemonkey.\u201d When you scroll over it a pop up will appear, allowing you to select \u201cManage User Scripts.\u201d Click on that selection and a window titled \u201cManage User Scripts\u201d will pop up.

In the window that just popped up locate the section titled \u201cIncluded Pages\u201d and click on the button that says \u201cAdd\u201d

A new window will pop up, in which you will enter the address of the new forum on which you wish to use the script.

To get this address, open a different window or tab of Firefox (using the Open New Tab / Open New Window command in the \u201cFiles\u201d menu.

- Go to the forum as you normally would.
- Enter one of the forum discussions. Any one of them will do.
- Copy the address of the forum thread page.
- Go back to the window or tab with the \u201cAdd Page\u201d box open. Paste the address into the field.
- You must now modify the address so that it will work with the script. This process has two steps:

-- Find the section of the address, located near the end, that says \u201ccomments.php?DiscussionID=\u201d. Delete everything after the equals sign. When you\u2019re done the address looks like this (with the name of the forum replacing \u201cyour-forum\u201d):
http://www.your-forum.com/forum/comments.php?DiscussionID=

Add an asterisk (*) to the end of the address. The example above would become:
http://www.story-games.com/forum/comments.php?DiscussionID=*

Click \u201cOkay\u201d to save the new forum and close the \u201cAdd Page\u201d box.

Repeat B and C for every new forum you wish to add.

Note: You may need to restart Firefox at this point for the script to take effect.

Add Names to the Ignore List. Now that the script is set up for the forums you want, you can add the forum names of other people on the forum that you want to ignore. Once someone\u2019s name is on the list the content of their posts will be hidden unless you deliberately choose to look at it.

Get the forum nickname of the person you want to ignore. Make sure you know how it is spelled, as a misspelled name will not result in the person being blocked from your screen.

Right click on the Greasemonkey logo in the bottom right corner of the screen. This will bring up a pop up menu.

On the pop up menu select \u201cUser Script Commands\u201d and then \u201cAdd user to ignore.\u201d This will cause a window to pop up titled \u201cPage at:\u201d with the address of the forum page as part of the title.

Enter the name of the user you want to ignore into the box and hit the OK button.

The user will now be blocked after you reload the page, or enter a new thread on the forum.

Repeat steps A to E for each person you want to ignore.

    \n I'll go ahead and say that I think automated ignoring is a shame; and if it really is common, then SG is in decline as a "community." I've been tempted a time or two to set this all up to ignore someone who got hyperbolic towards me in some (usually trivial and stupid) thread. Instead (as those of you with me on ignore know), I tend to strike back, harder, to make it crystal clear that I don't tolerate being insulted just because you don't agree with me. In some cases, these "unZen Volleys" have gotten ugly (and embarrassing all 'round) but I've never thought "fuck it, I'm wiping this guy off my screen." And in all but a couple of cases, I've been glad I didn't, when I read something funny from the adversary months later or learn something new in spite of abhorring the deliverer's general tone, demeanor, or sense of "humor."

    If someone is so bad that wiping them off the page is necessary for someone then, GENERALLY, I trust Andy to correct and, if necessary, ban. I don't recall anyone ever approaching that level of disruption (myself included).

    So I see it as a shame, is all. No reconciliation; no thought to "well I'll grin and bear it, or click past without much sweat;" no notion that today's adversary might be tomorrow's educator--nope, just blank them out of (apparent, virtual) existence, indefinitely.

    Imagine if you could do that in the real world--maybe with augmented reality specs and earphones? Forget all the "but I'd be rude, then" counters; forget all the "but then he could break my nose without my ever seeing it coming." Full power to utterly ostracize another human. I should think that would be one of the greatest, most decisive ways to dehumanize a person in history.

    (But of course, I know, I know--we aren't a "community" and our virtual presences aren't "humans." If you want to regurgitate the "what is story games community?" debate, go find the older thread and see if you've anything original to say. I work from the basic premise that an online presence is as "real"--or maybe as "relevant"--as an offline one.)
