\n Luke Crane, Keith Senkowski, and Matt Snyder challenge you to create an amazing book layout during the month of June, 2009. Each participant who meets the challenge receives two free PDFs. And, the big winner of the best book design receives free RPG books, including ENnie nominee Mouse Guard.

Featured in this game design is Conspiracy of Shadows: Dirty Hands, a new edition specially created for this contest. It includes free access to art by creator Keith Senkowski.

    \n Hi guys. Finally getting to a computer after jaunting to NYC yesterday and today. (Sitting in a window office right now looking out on Grand Central Station. Pretty cool for this Iowa boy.)

    Couple points of clarification:

    1) The contest was my idea. I approached Keith. He and I are friends, true. But, Keith also had an unbeatable combo -- free game text and he was willing to offer up his creator art. Keith is absolutely not seeking layout on spec, or anything of the kind. It's a contest, that's all. Should someone make something so amazing that he later wants to contact that person and work with them, wonderful. But, I know Keith. Even if that were to happen, he'd compensate the person fairly, and treat them well. Or, I'll kick his ass.

    2) The text is long. As the contest page indicates, this is deliberate.

    This is a book design contest. It's not a contest to see if you can create a snazzy dozen pages. It's a contest to challenge your design chops. Doing design work is hard work. The contest specifically challenges several skills including: \n
    • Creating and applying style sheets
      • \n
    • Understanding the content of an entire game and creating a layout to match
      • \n
    • Managing the art direction of many pieces of art
      • \n
    • Organizing a significant amount of content for readers and/or for players. This might include useful folios, a ToC, and other organizational features.
    \nI knew full well that many people would choose not to participate for the length of the text alone. That's ok. It's definitely not for everyone. No one's obligated to enter. No one's stopping anyone else from creating alternative contests.

    That said, the contest is exactly like a "real" freelance layout job. Heck, it's probably easier. There will be no rounds editing, last minute changes, art problems, and so on. It's all ready for you to design.

    I guarantee you that if you complete the contest, you will learn something. I mostly guarantee you you'll emerge as a better designer if you complete the contest.

    Graham - This is a really great project for someone still learning stuff. Because it's big and daunting and there's a lot to do, and ultimately if you fail there aren't consequences. There's no angry clients, there's no book that you wish you could take off the shelves and change, it's pure learning.

    Everyone crying about how this is hard and not worth your time - Boo hoo. Design is hard, and this contest is a great tool. It's not everyday you get to tackle a project of this size without fear of really screwing up. I understand not feeling like you have the time in your schedule to work on this (i don't know if i do) that's another argument entirely. Our community is not a "real world" industry. That may come as a shock to you and you may want to argue it, but you'll just be wrong. The money we move around between our wallets is chump change.

    I'm currently in the layout process for three RPGs. I'm excited about all of them, and I'm treating them just the same as I'm treating my "real" design contracts. I'm also charging about an eighth of what i do in the "real world" because that's what our market can bear. We're playing. Get over that, and then get into it.
