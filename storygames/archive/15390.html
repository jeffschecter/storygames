\n So, the party is pursuing the head of the thieves guild, who has twice escaped their clutches with the Eye of Erethrax (so called because said Orcish god apparently poked it from the eye socket of an Elven god) and 3/4 the party is Elves. Hey, if you make camp in the dungeon while chasing someone, expect them to get away.

So, I figure a canny operator like this chap has a buyer all laid up, and where do you go to broker deals for wonders and items of legend? The Great Souk of the fabled City of Brass, of course! So that's the next dungeon. But I need some evocative custom moves. And maybe a Tony Dowler map

My starters for 10+: Wanted some sort of Gold To Barter Equivalent, but then figured the gold price comes from fiction, Haggle is the spend.

When you enter the Great Souk for the first time, hold 1 Haggle

Also need some means of gaining more Haggle?

When you reject a never-to-be-repeated deal, gain and hold +1 Haggle

When you bargain for a small wonder in the Great Souk, roll +Haggle spent.
10+ You get what you bargain for at a keen price, but choose 3
7-9 choose 1:
It doesn't crumble to dust, vanish while you aren't looking, or fly off on its own
No-one powerful wants it back
What you buy is not dangerous, deceptive or a threat.
Great deal! Get +1 Haggle.

A pretty standard choose-from-a-list. Some other ideas?

(Reverse move) When you cash in a wish from an Efreeti, roll +number of wishes you have ever cashed in. This one counts, they all do.

Perhaps you can haggle for a wish at the Great Souk, or perhaps that's actually at the Obsidian Temple of the Everburning Flame?

Ideas welcome! Also suggestions for any captivating small wonders (for an Evil Elven Fighter, a twigs-in-hair Ranger, a the-Song-is-the-thing! Bard and a no-it's-the-gold-stupid! halfling thief). In the background is an expanding human empire eating into the territory of the last Elven kingdom.

    \n When you cash in a wish from an Efreeti, roll +number of wishes cashed in.

    On a 10+, you word your wishes carefully enough that they are carried out in the spirit of your intentions.
    On a 7-9, choose 1:
    - One of them is carried out with the most malign interpretation possible, while any others are handled fairly (if you only made one wish, do not choose this option)
    - All of them are carried out as literally as possible
    - At the last moment, you choke up: the smallest of the wishes you have made is handled fairly, but you cannot bring yourself to ask for anything more. (if you only made one wish, do not choose this option)
    - All of your wishes are carried out to your benefit, but at the expense of someone important, close to you, or vengeful
    On a miss, the DM must cackle and rub her hands together while she considers the most ... interesting way to carry out your wishes.

    When word of your failures reaches inquisitive ears, roll +cha.
    On a 10+, help arrives in some form. Your quest must be more important than you thought...!
    On a 7-9, help is sent to you, so that you may correct your many errors. You may not refuse this aid, because this is no longer just your problem.
    On a miss, take -1ongoing until you confront someone of some importance to you or to your quest and own up to your mistakes. When you do, mark XP.

    \n Cool, good stuff. The wish move needs to be reverse, with correct wording getting harder (and the Fates getting stingier) with multiple wishes, but thats mostly just rearranging. And while the package deals give some more options, I think each Wish deserves its own roll...

    When you cash in a Wish with an Efreeti, roll + number of Wishes you have ever made, including this one. Add +1 if it includes the words 'All', 'Every', 'Forever', 'None' or similar absolutes.
    On a 10+, your wish is carried out with the most malign interpretation possible or some other calamity befalls you in your hubris.
    7-9 choose one:
    - Your wish is carried out as literally as possible, but having to do so makes the Efreeti resentful and they will take petty revenge at some point
    - Your wish is carried out to your benefit, but at the expense of someone important, close to you, or vengeful
    6- you word your wishes carefully enough that they are carried out in the spirit of your intentions. Nothing annoys the Efreeti more, however.

    I like the word of your failures idea. Sort of a start-of-session letter move, and I know who would be dispatching aid. He's not on-site so some sort of representative to abase yourself before is appropriate.

    Posted By: CnephWhat happens whenwhen you bring a petition to the Keeper of the Everburning Flame? This might be to request a Wish, to undo a deal made, or to provide information.
    \nWhen you bring a petition to the Keeper of the Everburning Flame, roll+favor with him and then lose all favor. On a 10+, he'll complete your request as best he can, but it'll take one of (MC's choice) time, money, or effort on your part. On a 7-9, you'll need to earn more favor with the Keeper, but he'll do it (as per a 10+). On a miss, he's so angered with you wasting his time that he has his guards take you away - he'll burn you alive or devise some other suitable punishment, when he gets around to it.

    When you haggle with the Keeper of the Everburning Flame over some minor detail or good, roll+haggle spent. On a 10+, he's pleased with your bartering skill! Gain 1 favor with him (max 3). On a 7-9, he's amused at your effort and you take +1-forward with him.

    When you try to curry favor with someone important, the DMC will list 2 or 3 good ways to do so. If you complete one of them, take +1-favor (max 3). Some people, like the Keeper of the Everburning Flame, also have other ways to gain their favor.\n
    Posted By: CnephI think we'll need to know what occurswhen take something that does not belong to youor similar.
    \nWhen you take something that does not belong to you, if anyone could've possibly seen or heard you, well, it's your problem, buddy. If you think you're in the clear, roll+dex anyway. On a 10+, you are, in fact, in the clear, at least until the guy you took it from goes for it. On a 7-9, choose one:
    - Someone saw you.
    - Someone heard you.
    - You left something behind.

    On a miss, all three, sucker.\n
    Posted By: CnephAnd also, what happenswhen you look into the Eye of Erethrax?
    \nWhen you gaze upon the Eye of Erethrax, roll+cha. On a 10+, you feel ill will, but nothing else. On a 7-9, or a miss, choose one:
    - You feel a great urge to destroy the eye.
    - You move one alignment step closer to evil (good to neutral, neutral to evil). If you don't have the appropriate alignment listed in your class, the DMC will describe. If you're already evil, don't mark this one.
    - You feel a great hatred of elvenkind. When you next act against an elf, take +1 against them and mark XP.

    When you destroy the Eye of Erethrax, permanently lose 2d4 hit points (minimum hit points equal to your level or con score, whichever is higher) and take +1-forward when interacting with elves.
