    \n Thanks for all the comments and replies!

    The game is a sci-fi game, as already hinted in the first post, perhaps that steered your reasoning. But still, all the impressions from the cover are good ones. Those who pick it up and want to play stockmarket or Vegas-stories might be let down by the sci-fi tilted examples inside, but it is a game with shared world creation so such elements can easily be added by the players during play.

    As for the font and appearance I redid the cover with a sans serif font, and extended the subtitle to hint at sci-fi.



    Also I did some tilting of the text but I liked the "there are blinken-lights in the background and a title in the foreground"-effect that it had before, so I reverted. I moved everything a little to the left, when they cut the excess paper off at Lulu some of the cover will disappear too, this way I might get a centered title in the final product.

    The title on the cover reads:
    "While the world perishes"
    "A storygame about a future in change"

    I got some suggestions push the sci-fi theme even harder in the subtitle, but since there is no setting in the game I don't want to paste any limitations on the setting already on the cover. I checked out lots of old covers here and many of them just put "SCIENCE FICTION" in small print somewhere in a corner, perhaps I'll do that too.

    @anansigirl : I want to say "Pick me up! Read me! I contain the Awesome you have been looking for!" Also, it will draw attention to my other game H\xf6stdimma when the books are placed next to each other in a sales booth at a con. I hope the shiny lights will have that effect, in addition to giving the impression "This is a retro sci-fi game." since I think retro in general is awesome and the examples in the game are all of a sci-fi nature.
