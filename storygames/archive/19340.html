Star Wars FAE
\nWould like to hear more about this (maybe in a different thread)!

\nOkay! I haven't found anything for Bite Sized Actual play from this campaign, but I'm happy to talk about it.;

It started during one of my read throughs of FAE. I realized that Fate Accelerated practically stands up on a table and says, "Please use me to run Star Wars!" Because it seems like a super fit for the rules.

I discussed this with the organizer of my Tuesday night meetup, and he had an idea for doing a Star Wars campaign himself. We decided to join forces, and do a split campaign, where both halves would be related.

So, I led off. My campaign is set in between the trilogies. The other GM is playing one of the last survivors of a top-secret Republic attempt to create a unit of force-trained soldiers. (It didn't end well.) He's on the run from the Empire, which is gradually expanding into the Outer Rim.

I had a table rule that we had to have a droid, an alien, and a non-force using human as PCs; luckily that wasn't a problem and soon we had a database droid, the other GMs alien character, a roguish smuggler, his first mate, and a mercenary (we noticed the Firefly parallelism; it was enough that the first mate's player changed her PCs name to Zokaley, which is actually a good Star Wars name), and finally a bounty hunter and a Sith lord.

Yeah!

This was my first time running FAE for an extended period of time, but I was pretty happy with how easy it was to just have two black hat PCs--in fact, the campaign basically revolves around the Sith lord and his bounty hunter friend searching for the white hat PCs.

The other game is set just before the birth of Anakin, when some Jedi investigate the secret force-trained unit. In that one I play a mavericky Jedi Master and my padawan is played by the same player with the Sith lord PC--as you can guess, they're the same character. This has been kind of neat as well.

In terms of mechanics, we didn't do anything particularly radical. Force powers are handled by Aspects, enhanced with Stunts; this has worked pretty well so far, and keeps everyone around the same power level. (We discovered how tough combat Aspects can make a character when the bounty hunter nearly took out three white-hat PCs all by himself.) We haven't tried to define anything too much about Force powers, mostly letting it be Stunts like:

--Because I am a Sith Lord, I get +2 when dueling with Forcefully with a lightsaber

--Because I am a Telekinetic prodigy, I get +2 when Flashily creating an advantage with telekinesis

...but meanwhile letting the Aspects run things--like when my Jedi Master attempted to use the "old Jedi Mind trick." The bureaucrat PC proved remarkably strong-minded.

In short, it's worked pretty bloody well. Of course, it helps to make sure everyone is on the same page--Star Wars games need to have everyone comfortable with doing Star Wars-y stuff and not checking for traps every three meters. :-)

    Sharlinda Wu

    High Concept: Jedi Master
    Trouble: Maverick

    Careful +0
    Quick +2

    Flashy +2
    Sneaky +1

    Clever +3
    Forceful +1

    Aspects:

    The Jedi Council's Best Troubleshooter

    Anything Can Be Levitated

    Abin Is My Padawan

    Stunts:

    Because I am a Telekinetic Prodigy, I get a +2 when I Flashily Create An Advantage using Telekinesis

    Because I am a Master of the Ginach Style, I get a +2 when Cleverly Defending

    Because I know a Devastating Retributive Strike, once per session I can convert my Defend action into an Attack action



    Sharlinda is a member of the Jedi Council, something both she and the Council now regret. She has a reputation for being both a brilliant investigator and troubleshooter, but she frequently is in conflict with the more conservative members of the Council. She took the troubled young Abin Sur* as her padawan after most other masters refused him. She sees in him an opportunity to train another unconventional Jedi like her previous padawan, Qui-Gon Jinn. Whether she is correct, or if his dark destiny will destroy him, remains to be seen.**


    *Yeah, I know. It's intentional...
    **...Not really, because he becomes Darth Sinestro. Yeah, I know. But what the hell. There are only so many good Sith names ;-)
