\n I love Lilo & Stitch.

One of the things I like about the series is how Stitch is actually only one of several hundred "experiments". So at any time, one of the other experiments can appear in the plot - each with different abilities and weirdness attached. Instant plot hook!

And Superman's red kryptonite... it always has some sort of random effect on him, a new one every time.

These things are plot generators built into the setting. Whenever the writers need something to happen to get the story started, just drag in an experiment or a slab of red kryptonite, and hey presto!

Any other good examples of plot generators from known settings, RPG or otherwise?

    \n In a similar thing to Slider's portals, the doorways in Planescape. Since each one requires a completely arbitrary key, which can be anything from the tears of a god to a true confession of love spoken for the first time, there's guaranteed story there.
    \n
    \nAlso, practically anything in Ars Magica, from the way apprentices are acquired (often squirrelled away fro their families by magic), to the concept of covenants, to the process of tribunals, to the twelve houses of Hermes. It's a story-dynamo.

    \n The Hell Hole in Buffy as a "monster magnet" ~ making any monster showing up more plausible as well as the less frequent "monster with plan to open it".

    The "5 year mission to explore new worlds" of any Star Trek-like mission of discovery, a new planet, a new menace, a new babe for Kirk every week (I somewhat recycled this one in my last fantasy campaign the first part of which was 'voyage of exploration across uncharted ocean').

    \n Here's mine.

    "But there are more insidious demons yet; there are those which have no forms at all. Some of them are capable of taking the bodies of individuals amongst the people, whilst others are merely a feeling in the heart, a sinister cast to the light, or the bright color in a sunny rainbow. Like the demons of blood and flesh, they hunger for things from the people, but the things that they hunger for are not so simple as flesh: some devour joy, some feed from sadness, some require homage and dark pacts,
    and one there is will destroy the one moment that you were sure that she loved you, just because it can.

    These demons easily penetrate the remnant fortresses, and manipulate the people to their own agenda. Although the knights are often resistant to their powers, the people are far too susceptible, and such a demon is often capable of enormous harm before it is rooted out and destroyed, if it ever is.

    Such demons are difficult, though not impossible, to fight. They cannot be cut, even by the starlight swords of the knights, and they are often strong enough to overcome a simple exorcism. Fortunately, each one has a weakness but, unfortunately, each weakness is unique."
