    I don't understand how that site claims the institutions hold copyright over the maps. Even under the Disney-written laws of the U.S., everything before 1893 is public domain.
    \nLots of people do that, even academic institutions all over the world, as a way of exerting some control over materials they spent time and money creating. It's not against the law to claim copyright, so they have nothing to lose.

    If digital files are accurate reproductions, no copyright applies, even though they are new photos taken by somebody still alive and whatnot. Alterations (including clean-up, restoration, recolouring, etc) can make it a new artistic work, though. If work has been put into a photo that a court can find artistic merit in, they can ding you for copyright violation if you use it.
