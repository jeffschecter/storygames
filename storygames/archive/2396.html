In my first playtest of PEQ, which I wrote about here, I mentioned that the number one snag was what to do. I wrote in that post:
Snag number one occurred in Infiltration & Engagement and On the Roof with the Rogue player: What to do? Where to go? We ran into problems on where things were going. All the players were unfamiliar with the genre of this game, so they didnâ€™t know that sneaking around, meeting informants, planting explosives, etcâ€¦ are all cool things that Special Forces do on missions. Ideally, I think that a general outline of what must happen on each Threat Level would go a long way to solving this. The solutions that occur to me are: 1. Random situation creation tables, 2. A little pre-game planning by the GM, or 3. During Recon, each Threat Level is reconnoitered and decided on by the players. With failed rolls, the GM makes it up or uses random situations creation tables.

These are the new rules for Recon, which I hope will help eliminate the problems:
Recon

The first step of play is recon. This is where the players run reconnaissance, study satellite and aerial photos to determine the base layout, terrain features, enemy locations, where the base is located and any other information that may be useful during Insertion and Infiltration & Engagement.
The GM should provide satellite or aerial photos of a military base or a location that looks like a military base in this stage of the game. Samples are included for quick pick-up play. Alternatively, the players can draw a map during this stage.
Using the map, players can run recon on the individual features on the map and develop an attack plan on the facility. This is the Operativeâ€™s home base, so they should be familiar with the functions of the buildings, but they are reconnoitering the terrorist emplacements and the GM should make sure to remind the players of this. On a successful recon roll, the player determines what has been reconnoitered in that location. They should describe the feature. When a recon roll fails, that location can no longer be reconnoitered and the GM can either decide what is in that location now or later. The reconnoitering ends when the players have successfully or not reconnoitered six locations. The GM places Threat Level seven onto the map.
To develop an attack plan, the players should determine what they plan to do, when they want to move into reconnoitered location, and even what they intend to accomplish in each location. The next step is to label the steps of the plan on the map. These steps of action should correspond closely to the Threat Levels the players want to be at when they move into a new location. During play, everything may not go according to plan and the players may find that they have to have their Operatives improvise. Sometimes, the mission wonâ€™t move as quickly as they hoped, and sometimes, the team will be at Threat Level Two when they hit the ground. The players can decide which parts of their plans they can skip or what they need to add to make their plan work.
Each player may gain one Threat Level during recon.

For example, on the shown map of the Groom Lake Facility (Area 51), the players have decided that their Operators will insert to the west of the main groups of buildings where they have determined an outpost of terrorist guards have set up a monitoring station. This will be location one. Here the Operatives will disable the main alarm connections to the main set of buildings. After, disabling the security, they will head down the hill into a secure location to set up a SATCOM and computer uplink system that will allow them to remain in contact with their ODB via their TACcom Radio
systems (Threat Level 2). After the SATCOM is set up, they will stealthy move into the main set of building, split into two in order to take out both of the Bradley M6 Linebackers at the perimeter of the base. This will be a fall back plan for the team. If they fail, it will allow unhindered air strikes on the base (Threat Level 3). After the team destroys the M6s, they will move into a location that they were unable to get any info on (Threat Level 4). They will either regroup here, or in the hanger facility, which is at Threat Level Five. Here is the back-up security system for the base. They plan to utilize the security system to find the terrorists and gain a current view of the enemy situation. After gaining new knowledge, theyâ€™ll move into locations to attack the main terrorist hold-out in an attempt to gain advantageous locations in the sewer system and from above (Threat Level 6).
