    \n I'm the guy in question and... Wow... Its really great to see people who are willing to be so helpful! I wasn't sure particularly where to start, but some of this stuff has given me a good idea. Unfortunately, I'm not based in London currently, but I could travel to be there depending on dates and times.
    The things I've worked on so far have been:
    - a Scion rewrite for my home group
    - a Persona module using the Dark Heresy system
    - a new setting I've nurtured for a few years for D&D (That is currently slipping over to 13th Age, as D&D 5th is looking so-so to me)
    - a simple, quick play, anime-based game I made to run at a few cons in and around the UK that I've GMed at (I have been doing panels on RPGs at ALcon in Leicester for a few years, and our group has got some invites recently to a few other anime cons around the UK)


    Currently I'm trying to work on making a core system that I can use to run games for both a Fantasy and a Sci Fi setting I've been crafting away at, and it is largely this that sparked my attempt to .
    I've been running games for ten years and got fascinated by the process of game design whilst following the advent of 4th ed D&D, though since then I have gravitated towards lighter rulesets.
    Thats a bit of my background, I guess. I'd like to get into designing properly as a job, and I was a bit lost on where to start.

    Its only quite recently that I have decided to go from writing for home groups and societies, throwing out a few pieces I've written in homebrew forums(Which I've been doing for about 5 years now on and off), to trying to turn it into something a bit more professional and standardised.
