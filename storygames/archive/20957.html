    \n For me, the products don't have to be big and expensive and complicated. There's a lot to be said about a simple idea executed well.

    Nathan Paoletta did some beautiful stuff with his games. There were hand-bound editions of Annalise, and he did two covers (one dark, one light) for the main release. carry: a game about war is a simple perfect-bound book that mimics the look and lo-tech feel of a military Field Manual.

    Rob Bohl's game Misspent Youth is built like a punk 'zine. The ashcan version had a picture of a giant penis on one page as a watermark. He peppered the book with pictures of disaffected teens, all harvested from his gamer friends.

    \n I love the physical books/boxes of everything Burning Wheel HQ puts out (I have the Mouse Guard 1E Boxed Set + Book , Burning Wheel Gold + Codex, and Torchbearer on my shelf and admire them often).

    But for all-time favourite it just has to be the original AD&D Monster Manual. It was one of the first RPG books I got my hands on, at a used book store, and the wrap around cover art and the numerous weird and wonderful illustrations within just set my pre-teen heart on fire. I actually gave away my copy when I got the (vastly less attractive) mid-90s AD&D 2nd Edition reprints, foolishly thinking that I would never want to use it again. But wonder of wonders, I found a pristine copy a decade later at a garage sale for a dollar and it sits proudly on my shelf once again!
