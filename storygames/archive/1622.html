[headdesk] I accidentally reinvented the LARP

Colin_Fredericks
October 2006 edited October 2006 in Story Games
This is a little bit of rambling on my part.

Inspired partly by just the name of "Hollow Earth Expedition," and partly by wishing Sky Captain didn't suck so bad, and partly by the premise of some TV shows (including Lost), and partly by The Mountain Witch and a dozen other things, I went about writing a little game about a bunch of people who went to and got trapped in massive underground caverns that contain cities from the world that came before, when other creatures lived on Earth; the cities that someone based our current cities around for unknown reasons. It was to be set in the first half of the 20th century, presumably in America.

The game focused on interpersonal interaction, especially building up trust and character dice to use for various purposes. The engine was to be a variant on Console Legends, adding a GM, removing or altering Visions, but keeping the character abilities that showed up at specific acts. The GM handled (very sparse) NPCs and describing the environment, and the events that bookend each of the game's six acts, but was totally unable to knock the characters out of the game in any fashion - no deaths, no unconsciousness, no bind-and-gag, no solo separation from others. Interpersonal interaction was the big thing, so you couldn't take it away. You build up Trust and Character dice to make other people act in certain ways (towards your goal) and to get yourself out at the end.

Then I wrote, "Players are free to talk simultaneously, at the same table or a different one, even in a different room if they are in a different group." I was thinking, "you know, this fits really well with the 'chaotic play' idea, lots of people around different tables, events occasionally crossing from one table to another..."

And I suddenly realized that the whole game, which had few dice to begin with and no serious character sheet, might as well go to Rock-Paper-Scissors or a card draw mechanic and turn into a LARP. I had, somewhat inadvertently, recreated live-action gaming while trying to make a tabletop game.

Not necessarily a bad thing, I suppose. Just not what I expected to be writing.

    There's a crossroad down that path with two signs:

    - This way for LARP, where you are exalted by physical space, but it draws limits around your imagination.
    - This way for tabletop, where your imagination is unlimited, but you are not exalted by the physical space.

    And then you notice that the two paths actually lead to the same direction and you can walk in between them and see both of them.

    (That's just a complex way of saying that the two activities are very similar and most differences are direct or indirect results of the use of physical space. And you can end up in the no-man's land between them, mix and match)
