\n I\xb4m pretty much a "traditional" roleplayer who has become increasingly interested in both RPG Theory and thenew concept of "Story Games".
\n
\nRight now, school and other stuff is preventing me from starting a campaign which requires quite a bit of prep work (a 7th Sea game using the Spirit of the Century ruleset, with players who have never used the FATE system before). However, I\xb4d like to play something with this group in order to start knowing their play styles and gaming preferences.
\n
\nA lot of the games discussed here strike me as more agile than regular RPGs when it comes to prep work. I\xb4d like to hear our suggestions for a good prep-light game which can appeal to a group (4 players) of traditionally-minded gamers.
\n
\nThanks in advance

    \n I've got to recommend TSoY/Solar System, which is a great trad/indie crossover kind of game, and lots of fun (without all the heavy angst or face-stabbing). TSoY = The Shadow of Yesterday and is available for free online, which is a plus! The new Solar System booklet (a generic set of the same rules) has a lot of good notes on prep and setup, and the Adventure Map method in there should be quick and easy.

    In a Wicked Age... could also be great. I suspect "traditional" gamers would actually take to it better than hardcore "indie" gamers.
