\n Hell for Leather is available for pre-order. That is, I've had confirmation that the books are en route, so I guess it's time to see who's interested. I'll try to keep this really short:



Hell for Leather is a game that uses a tower of dice to tell stories. It generates party fun (shouting, laughing, swearing). Although you tell stories, Hell for Leather is more like a boardgame than a roleplaying game.

All the preparation is done collaboratively (world building, story building, and so on), and that takes about half an hour. When the action starts, players create scenes, pepper them with obstacles, and challenge each other to roll dice at a target, sort of like curling. Sometimes the player will have to build a stack of dice to help them succeed. Other times he will knock the stack over, often with derogatory consequences.

The game is 96 pages, costs $20 and includes the PDF. It was designed as part of Nathan Paoletta's Two Games One Name contest, alongside Joe Prince's Hell 4 Leather.
