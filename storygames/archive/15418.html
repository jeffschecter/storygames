    \n Monster of the Week is a hack of Apocalypse World.

    It's inspired by shows like Buffy and Supernatural, and is designed to tell self-contained monster hunts (but with the system constantly nudging you to create a bigger mythology about what the monsters are really up to).

    Adventure preparation is quick: like between 3 to 5 minutes.

    It's got a really neat Luck/Doom mechanic: you can spend luck to get juicy re-rolls, but once you're out of luck ... that's it. You're pretty much on the downward spiral, with the GM being allowed to target you and put you into difficult situations.

    I've been involved in the playtesting, and it's become one of my go-to games for quick RPG fun.

    \n Here's how a Monster of the Week session can play out - I just got home from running this one.

    Our cast are agents of a black monster control agency that's part of the US Department of Homeland Security. Our veteran agents have been finding evidence that something is rotten in the agency.\n
      \n
    • Team lead: Diana Weatherby (ex-Professional, ex-Hard Case, now Chosen)
      • \n
    • Bill Francis (Expert)
      • \n
    • Hoss Mitton (Monstrous. An orc, and new to the team)
      • \n
    • (absent tonight was Charlie Dexter: ex-Mundane, now Divine and last seen very angry with Bill)
      • \n
    \nThey were sent to a natural history museum, where three staff had been killed by some kind of burning or acid while working late.

    Arriving at the scene, the agents quickly divert the museum administrator who is getting in the way, and a detective checking out the unusual deaths. Their cover today is Health and Safety inspectors, so that went pretty easily.

    Initial investigation of the bodies revealed that whatever it was had been in the packing material of some fossils from Mongolia. A blown investigate roll from Hoss had him convinced that two of the bodies contained gestating monster spawn, like in Alien. He quickly ran the suspect bodies through with his sword, just to be sure. Some more productive tracking was tried next, and they realised the creatures had split up and got lost all over the museum. The team split up: Bill went to get plans of the ventilation system, and the other two chasing one that had gone to ground in the cellars.

    Diana and Hoss found a nest half constructed in a boiler room, and booby trapped it with a grenade. But, to keep things safe, they put "CAUTION OSH HAZARD" tape on the door!

    Upstairs, Bill heard a commotion in a colonial history exhibit. Running in, he found someone who had been sprayed with acid/venom and did the obvious thing: distract everyone else and quickly invoke the powers of Hell to heal that guy with some Satanic blood magic. He then pursued the creature into the colonial log cabin exhibit and got our first view of the creature: a big, red worm that was spitting venom. Yes, the Mongolian Death Worm! Ducking under the venom he pulled his gun and lit it up, doing enough damage to drive it off but not kill it.

    Downstairs, the others heard their grenade go off and headed back to check the damage. A very angry worm was hiding under the boiler, but Diana quickly pulverized it, although not without some venom splashes. Their investigations led them to suspect the worms were looking for the driest places to nest, so they headed for the next likely spot. Here's where I used my reserved "something really bad happens to Diana" from her missed Chosen start of session move: Hoss was distracted by a phonecall and the lights went down, and Diana walked into a worm ambush. The fight was brutal, but the first worm was soon dealt with. Hoss caught up here and then walked right under the other worm, but was saved by Diana (at some cost, including a Luck spend).

    While they were doing that, Bill had worked out that immersion in water dissolved the worm flesh, and also that the fourth and final worm had been back to its nest and then left again.

    After meeting up and a quick catch-up, they decided to pull the fire alarm to clear the museum while they hunted down the last worm. They quickly spotted a school group who weren't evacuating, and a teacher on the ground in pain. Diana vaulted the group and pulled the worm away from them, Bill used his Hell-magic again to heal the teacher, and Hoss tried to get the kids to run away. Hoss missed his roll, and they instead ran in the other direction (towards the worm). Diana dived on the worm and made an epic Kick Ass success, followed by a hit on the Hard Case "Finish Him" move to destroy it very convincingly.

    They quickly checked for any other worms or eggs, and concluded that the museum was now safe.

    Then they returned for a debrief where their superior, Hargraves, told them off for not handling things with sufficient subtlety. Diana walked out of the meeting after that, possibly this was the last straw for her.

    That concluded the session, and I have a strong feeling that we're in the final bit of their story: Diana's destiny as Chosen seems to be to bring down the agency, or at least the parts of it that are being corrupted (which might well include Bill and Hoss). The crunch time on that can't be far off. I'm also going to be interested to see what happens between Bill and Charlie: the angel has shown he's completely against the black magic that Bill keeps using. I'll be interested to see how he deals with an orc on the team, too.

    \n Here's another actual play update, next episode in the game mentioned above.

    All members of the team are present today, although because Hoss & Charlie are meeting for the first time they get to make history picks. It turns out Hoss saved Charlie's life back before Charlie knew he was an angel.

    Their mission, given by a rather terse Agent Hargraves (certain Agents were being less professional than he likes), was to check out a tiny West Virginia mountain village (scarcely even that) on the grounds that people seem to just disappear around there every so often. There was a little bit of pre-field organisation, and we found out that Diana's Chosen dreams had recently been nightmares of being chased through a dark forest. Quite like the sort of forest around that village... Then Bill does a spell to locate "where one of the victims is now". The map of West Virginia turns into a swirling mass of shadow and he realises that the victim's soul is in some horrible other dimension or something. No hint about where the body is, suggesting it is not anywhere The maps stays like that, too.

    Charlie wants to split up, with the others hitting town as forestry inspectors while he hams it up as a birdwatcher camping in the mountains.

    Diana, Bill, and Hoss checked out the village, comprised of a few run down shacks and some hostile locals just glaring at them. They interview a guy sitting on his porch, getting nothing out of him except a bad vibe and hostile replies, until they ask for a local guide. He immediately happily tells them that he sure can arrange for someone to guide them up into the forested mountains, they should come back first thing tomorrow. The team does not fall for this (the guy really thought he'd just found the easiest victims ever). They head back to the nearest real town, to plan and hoping that the people there are not so creepy (they weren't).

    Charlie does his camping birdwatcher impression and calls in his ally, another angel called Mirzam. They try a bit to understand camping (Mirzam is unconvinced). Charlie then sends Mirzam off to check out the village. Soon after, the ground and trees start attacking Charlie, bashing and grinding and generally the whole world's gone crazy. He uses his trick to teleport to the rest of the team, just back from hearing lots of "don't go up that way, people who go there don't come back" from the locals at the town bar.

    There's some discussion of what the attacks imply and some contingency plans are floated. Some investigation, magic, and other psychic stuff suggests that they are dealing with some kind of other-dimensional godlike horror thing and its cult on Earth.

    Then Charlie decides to check on Mirzam. He teleports to Mirzam's location to find him chained by shadowy magical manacles to the ground surrounded by people doing some kind of ritual. Charlie smashes the bonds with his fiery sword and drags Mirzam back to the others.

    They decide to quit for the night, as they appear to be safe in town. Next morning, it's back up to spring the trap they're expected for, but they find the village deserted. They take the opportunity to check it out, and find signs of murder and deformed footprints. Then they realise they're being watched from the trees... everyone heads to Diana's van to make a stand from the expected attack by village creepy folk and whatever it was that left those footprints. Hoss, on top of the van, momentarily gets a cellphone signal and receives a text message: "Get out now" from his unofficial boss. She's the head of R&D section 4, previously discovered to be doing experiments on captured monsters and now running the "Special Agent Training Program". Also, the team's immediate boss, Agent Hargraves, thinks she is up to no good.

    The team gets into the van and gets out of town. Diana successfully reads a bad situation in time to notice a telltale sunlight reflection, swerving the van before a sniper takes out the front tire. A little further down are anti-tire spikes, and the team decide to leave the van with Charlie, who crashes it over the spikes and teleports out before it goes down the bank. They start heading for town on foot, trying to work out who the sniper could have been.

    Then they hear, and see, a civilian SUV driving up towards the village. Charlie teleports down to stop them (rolling a success on his "trust me" move: tell them the truth about what's going on, and they do what you want no questions asked). He said "turn back, the village ahead is full of murderous backwoods cultists who will kill you".

    The team then decide to sort this whole village out, tool up and head back. They notice that they are slowly getting surrounded, so Diana fires her grenade launcher at some movement. That starts things, and suddenly the ground and trees are attacking everyone, villagers and deformed mutants are swarming them and the team are casting spells, shooting bad guys, and swinging swords. Diana's nightmares of running through the forest being chased come true as she takes the attention of the being making the world attack, and Charlie is getting swarmed by bad guys. Charlie and Hoss both end up burning some luck to stay in the fight.

    And that's when we ran out of time! "To be continued..."

    Next time: Will the team survive the cultist attack? Will they stop the extra-dimensional horror from manifesting fully? Who was the sniper and team who tried to stop them leaving? Find out next time!


    That was one creepy and exciting game. I was particularly pleased that the mystery was so creepy that the team decided (for the first time) to run away and leave it to a full Agency scorched-earth cleanup team rather than deal with it themselves. Only the need to save that couple in the SUV pulled them back in. I am very much looking forward to the next game.

    Another meta-note is that I'm aiming to wind up the game soon, as the characters are fairly maxed out in power (especially Diana, who has gone through the Professional and Hard Case playbooks before becoming a Chosen, and thus has many abilities and high stats). So I'm driving the game's main arc to a close. That arc is to do with the Agency and what's going on there, and what the team will do about it. I don't want to say anything in case my players read this, but they already got some clues that things aren't exactly as presented to them.

    \n So, now I've run the conclusion to the last mystery I recounted.

    They fought off the bad guys who started retreating and/or fleeing. Our heroes followed up and found that the remaining villagers seemed to be trying to finish up a big evil ritual as fast as they could. The team decided on the momentum-based assault to disrupt it, which was slowed down a bit when Bill (remembering his dark past) worked out that any killing nearby would help the ritual. They also discovered that the map they used for scrying right at the beginning, which still showed the entity, would alsp be a potential portal.

    Charlie decides to deal with the map, by taking it to the gates of Hell and tearing it into bits and discarding it there. I'm sure that won't turn out badly in any way.
    Diana and Hoss dealt with the rest of the villagers and mutants (which they established were "children of the entity", being birthed from trees in the forest). This wasn't exactly easy: Hoss was near death by the time they finished.
    Bill got Charlie to take him back to his lab to work on a ritual to bar the portal against the entity.

    Charlie then got into a bit of trouble caused by storing the tainted map in his "special place" - he has a Divine move that allows him to hide things away in a little pocket dimension, and the entity had pushed through into it (and kind of into Charlie). He managed to contain the damage, by letting it out to create another potential portal in our world.

    After Bill had finished the three hour ritual to close the first portal, he was not best pleased to have to do it again right away.

    So they return to base, with a certain amount of trepidation about the sniper and roadblock they encountered earlier. Hoss talked to the woman who'd warned him to get away, and she told him to just drop it. The team's supervisor Hargraves has "requested an indefinite leave of absence" and when Diana visits him she finds him under surveillance and reluctant to tell her anything "because they're listening".

    Hoss decides to request a transfer off the team, for a desk job. So the Agency puts him into Hargraves' job, now handler for the team who know he's close with at least some of the factions who have it in for them.

    I'm excited to see if next time we get to see the scene from Diana's Chosen One visions, where she's stalking something through the burning remnants of the Agency HQ. That certainly seems like it might happen quite soon!

    Campaign news: just $48 short of the $2000 bonus goal (which includes more illustrations for the text, so I'm very keen to get there).

    Steve Hickey, my editor, has let me know he's done with the last draft, so I expect to get stuck into rewrites quite soon. Looks like the next version will need to be reorganised a great deal to get to my goal of "the text goes in the same order you deal with things in play".
