The Regiment, Paul Riddle and John Harper's Apocalypse World Hack. Robo is playing an incompetent officer (sort of Captain America from Generation Kill without the gold-plated AK47). He's messing up bad; he's freaking out and ordering a withdrawal from a vicious ambush when the only way to avoid casualties is to face the fire and charge. And he's rolling real bad. So the way The Regiment works, you gain XP on failed rolls, and Robo is really pushing hard. By the time we're through my poor grunt has 2 XP, from getting wounded twice, and our incompetent Lieutenant has leveled up, entirely from failures.

JUST LIKE REAL LIFE.

    \n Been playing a lot more via Skype...

    Panty Explosion Perfect: I've started up a mini-campaign of this with four players. Two of the players do not have much anime experience. Character generation was a breeze for this rules lite game. I gave each of the players a 'care package' that contained blank character sheets, and text files of japanese names, example player details, and example goals. This went a long way towards streamlining the process. I also used a shared cork board web site to show the 'table seating' via virtual post-it notes. We went around the table with morning/school prep scenes to get the players familiar with the mechanics. The first two where a little rough, but by the third and fourth scenes everyone was laughing, cutting up, embellishing and even introducting re-occuring gags. Everyone's psyched for the next gaming session. Victory!

    InvaderZ: InvaderZ is a totally beer and crisps game targetted at zany one-shot missions. Best two bucks that I have spent in a long while (for the PDF.) Silly voices, monumentally bad intelligence, gratuitous violence, and poor impulse control abounded. I was getting hoarse after a few hours, but damn it was fun.
    Tactical notes: The White Castle has poor defenses and is easily breached, but watch out for the fry-o-lator and boiling hot coffee.

    \n Had a whirl wind weekend of gaming...

    Fringeworthy beta (Savage Worlds version via Skype), after helping the Victorians escape from Alt 0,-2, IDET team 2 heads back to Earth for an ambassadorial mission that is bound to shake up the powers that be. Quarantine, strange bio-medical readings, debriefings, and more training abound, but that is the life of an extra-dimensional explorer. My character's wife also ambushed the team with a huge Filipino variety show type event and IDET2 was at the center of it. Good role playing and almost no dice rolling for this session.

    Session two of Panty Explosion Perfect has the girls enduring the gauntlet that is known as lunch. Talking ravens, bloodthirsty swords, disturbed math teachers, and even more disturbing lunch boxes were present. The players are really picking up the style differences in the game. The most difficult thing is keeping the 'table order' of who is the rival and who is the best friend straight. Skype confuses that a bit with moving the avatars around.

    I was in two play test sessions last night of my game chef entry, The Trouble with Rose, and it went awesomely well. Justin and his crew of 20 somethings are pretty much traditional GURPS players. They decided to go with a Wild West theme of love, infidelity, and double crossing. The first few scenes were a little rough while they switched gears from simulation to narrative gaming, but they took to it like a fish to water. They were so psyched that we had to run another game about a near future zombie apocalypse (sorry Shakespeare, I hope you're not spinning too much in your grave.) Much craziness but the funniest part was during the reveal when it turned out that four out of six players were in love with Burning Rose and none of them got the girl!

    Just finished the start of a new game of Zorcerer of Zo via Skype. The GM is running with the unseeli setting which means things are a bit darker and grimmer than what you might hear on actual play sessions. It was interesting how the adventurers themselves were also grimmer and darker. All told, had a lot of fun as we worked to solve a big bad wolf mystery that had a staggering body count.

    \n Playtested my Game Chef game, both lots of fun and hugely informative. One spiral of tragic mayhem:

    A convict murders the Dimber Damber's enforcer and tries to cover it up as a native ambush.
    The colonial Marines respond with a punitive expedition.
    The natives hole up in an impenetrable series of slot canyons with ex-convict Bolter friends.
    The Marines are ordered into what amounts to a certain ambush.
    The Marines are ambushed; nine men are killed by the Bolters.
    The Captain of Marines orders a massive retaliation, but since the guilty party cannot be reached, her Marines burn all nearby peaceful native encampments.
    Natives retaliate in force.

    It escalated from there.

    \n Third session of my 8 players (and counting) AW-campaign. It's fantastic to see how different the sessions are depending on what characters are present. Second session was the Maestro'D and Skinner building webs of alliances in the holding, especially a bunch of deals with Rolfball. Third session neither of them were present but the Gunlugger and Faceless didn't have the gravitas to borrow his nightvision goggles and almost ended up killing him for it. The Faceless consults his mask who urges him to kill Rolfball and take his stuff which he does with a mix of stealth and punching through the wall of his giant turtle ranch. Then he burns it down and drags his broken bike all over to lay the blame on the chopper gangs. I wonder what hot characters do when they see that and what they do when they see the Faceless prancing around in his stolen high tech.

    \n Cthulhu Dark

    My friend hadn't played an RPG in a long time so I ran something on the fly for her based on some weird internet urban legend that claimed it was "well known" that the Lawrence Research Lab in Berkeley had captured several bigfoots (feet?). They were finding it very hard to contain them because their evasiveness involves quantum collapsing of light from particle to waves. Despite a silly premise, though tone and atmosphere (and because it wasn't really revealed till the end that the monster was quantum bigfoot) my friend got quite spooked!

    \n Play(test)ing the Gen Con Red Book version of the Dungeon World rules. We used one of Tony Dowler's awesome microdungeons.

    The wizard flubbed up a Magic Missile spell on some disgusting half-decomposed skeletons that rose up from a muddy graveyard in the driving rain to attack the party. The spell went off fine and blew the skeleton's head off. But my hard move was that the particular whistling sound from the spell just happened to be the mating call for a 50 foot long purple worm/umber hulk thing that broke from the tree line and started coming their way.

    The fighter Spouted Lore and found that the creature really liked the taste of dwarf. But instead of sacrificing their Dwarven Cleric, he tossed the dwarf to safely (yes, we went there) and dug up a chunk of Dwarf skeleton from the graveyard. Which he threw to the huge creature and gained them enough time to dash into the ruined tower and search for the girl who got kidnapped by raiding goblins. And that was before they got sucked into a magical beer drain and were knocked unconscious.

    Next session: the journey to Beer-vana.

    \n We played Jason's Game Chef submission (Durance) last night, and had a good time. The ice-cold criminal boss ("the Dimber Damber") ruled the colony underworld from the comfort of his palatial shipping container, while his lieutenants shook down the fungus farmers for boxes of mycoprotein rations. The colony governor issued orders that food theft- along with distillation and consumption of alcohol using food supplies- would now be a capital offense.

    Tensions rose until political activist "wreckers" stole half the food stores in a bid for colony control. "That's right, Governor, we're in charge now. While you and your marines are starving, we're going to be living high on the mushroom hog!"

    It didn't really go as planned.
