\n What if:

- Game designer has 1 artist create all the art for a game.

- The result is part art book, part game.

- It's sold at the artist's booth at Gen Con.

Inspired by:

Paul Czege and the Wilderness\n
I talked with Paul Czege in this Jams length shorts, about his explorations of alternates to Gen Con that might be useful for the crazy unknown designers out in the wilderness. Perhaps it might be helpful to uncrazy unknown designers, uncrazy known designers, and crazy known designers. It arose from this post.
\nI don't have games to sell or a direct stake in this conversation. But I love buying indie games and I know it's getting harder for designers to afford selling games at Gen Con.

My understanding is that artist booths are far cheaper but in exchange Gen Con takes a cut of everything sold. But in doing so, they handle all financial transactions. You just have to sell your game and not worry about credit cards or a cash register. I don't know if there are restrictions that would prevent this but I know artists sell art books, so a hybrid work may surpass any limitations and probably be a more attractive product for many people (not all of course).

Hope that helps someone.

Rock,
John

    \n Sneaky idea. I kind of like it. But it's also a question of exposure. People aren't going to the artist area to look for games. Some gamers probably don't even set foot in the artist area at all. I think you'd lose some eyeballs, and, therefore, some potential customers. Maybe the lower cost of entry would off-set this?

    Also, Gen Con has rules. I'd want to check out any restrictions on this kind of activity first before committing financially.
