\n Tell me something that a magician character did in a game you played which was interesting, cool or amazing. It must relate to them being a magician. It can just be a little bit of colour, and epic ritual, or some obscure spell combo.

For example, one magician devised a spell which enabled them to communicate with people in stasis, by entering a mental landscape under their control. Later, a group of adventurers used this spell for some aggressive psychoanalysis on a fellow party member.

Another magician absolutely refused to use any manual implement to perform labour, insisting on using magic even if peeling an apple.

    \n I've got a dragon-obsessed elf sorcerer in my Pathfinder game who, unbeknownst to him, is the progeny of two ancient draconic bloodlines. In the entrance chamber of the Sunless Citadel, he suddenly began to read draconic runes fluently, without even realizing what the language was. The player, of course, had chosen draconic as one of his languages, but purposely waited for a dramatic moment for his magical affinity for dragons to be revealed. Great roleplaying!

    \n In a setting inspired by feudal Japan and East Asia, I had an earth mage, a fire mage, a water mage, an air mage, and a spirit mage, construct a magical diving suit so that a humanoid elephant could walk through the heart of a volcano to retreat an artifact of great power.

    Sadly, they got their lines of power crossed and the spirit mage died a pretty horrible death.

    Every pragmatic, the group had backup mages on hand for each key role so that the mission wasn't a complete loss.

    \n In games of High Valor I've had:

    One character turn another to stone, so he could rest and heal after teleporting the party away from a Fallow-worm, a creature that threatened their entire long ship. Teleporting is very hard and he ruptured something and was bleeding to death.


    Another character disperse a deadly accursed "fog" that made everyone ill in their village by bringing forth an icy-rain which washed it away. The effect was thwarted a Fane-Lords powers. (Think "Sauron" level power here.)

    Another gave up his life to destroy a shadowy dragon which had infested the Fey "Bright Lands."

    A group gave up their lives to forge a ritual that allowed them to stand as spirits and guard a piece of the dragon Malevolence's heart so it would not rejoin after fighting her and killing her and splitting the heard in the first place.. (In Aiea, she is believed to be the first dragon--and very evil despite the fact that some dragons are good, and most uncaring and unconcerned abut anything but themselves.)

    A PC of mine took the form of a stag and carried a rescued maiden home.


    In AD&D:

    A single mage once shot a bee down a foes throat, killing the foe. (Magic Missile spell creatively interpreted.)



    In Mythus:

    I grew a treehouse to sleep in and killed a charging dark knight with long flying spears of wood. Man that game was complex to make PC's and magic was hard to get, but if you had it "BOOM!!"
