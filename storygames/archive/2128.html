  • Matt Snyder
    January 2007
    It's mostly the same as Forge Midwest, Mr. Impostor. Two important things changed, I think. Both involve using chips.

    First, you can delay endgame (now called The End) by spending chips to gain cards in place of your Devil.

    Second, you can spend chips during The End to both Harm characters and Redeem characters. Harm harms 'em 1:1. Redeem lets you heal them, even if they have a Score at zero.

    That last bit is otherwise impossible. (You can spend chips normally to recover your own Harm. You can do it before The End, but you can't recover a 0. It stays at 0 unless someone else redeems you first.)

    And, yep, the plan is to revisit ol' Forge Midwest. I'll be posting about it soon (next week or so). I've only barely started to look over hotels and such.

  • Matt Snyder
    January 2007
    The index is worth considering. I'm not sure what to do there. I've never indexed a book before, frankly, and I'm not sure I'd make one worth a damn. I may just do it, though. I guess I'd try printing it out, grabbing some stickies, and going to work on it.

    There are sample characters. There's also a straight-forward sample "scenario" ready to go. It's set in historical Abilene, Kansas. Characters are as detailed as Dust Devils characters get. The situation is less detailed, but ample for a game in which anyone call set up the next saloon or marshal with narration rights. The setting of Abilene is only basically explained, and there is no map, for example. I find no reason to include a map, for example.

    With respect to the reviewer, some of the things he's looking for (like a map of a town) have very little use in Dust Devils, but lots of use in more traditional RPGs.

    What's the map of the town in High Noon like? What state is it in (not sure we're ever given that)? Is the marshall's office around the corner from the barn where the shoot-out happens, or down the street? What about the hotel? And, where is the store? The saloon? The church? Well, the answer is you can get to all of 'em in less than the hour it'll take for Frank Miller to get to town on the train. (Seems like getting from the depot to the marshal won't take long, either.) And, that's all that matters.

    As for the setting expansions, it's true there are no example characters for Deathwish or Concrete Angels (Ronin has excellent sample characters). Deathwish does provide a sample situations, but it's pretty simple and straightforward. Ronin and Concrete Angels rely on people's shared assumptions of those genres. I'm not planning on devoting more time to them in their current format. I would consider adding, for example, online materials -- like a Dust Devils wiki or something like that.
