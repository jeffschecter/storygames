\n I need a small strategy game for use as a minigame. Something that plays moderately quickly, with room for variation, but which depends primarily on smart strategic choices.

Right now I'm using Dots, but it's just not variable enough. Chess is appropriately variable (in terms of both strategy and different moves for different pieces), but takes too long.

A short ruleset and small board are a plus.

    \n As an aside, Dots is actually surprisingly deep -- there's even a book dedicated to it.

    Generally, the smaller the board, the greater the risk of stereotyped play ,but some games get around this; for instance, Quarto! is sort of the apotheosis of tic-tac-toe with pieces that vary in four axes and a neat rule where your opponent chooses the piece you play. That would be an excellent choice if you don't have a problem with it being a proprietary game or are willing to file the serial numbers off.

    My game game Edges might work for you, and has the possible advantage that I wouldn't be bothered if you use it.

    There are many small Chess variants to be found at www.chessvariants.com, but they seem to be having database problems right now, making hard to actually find any of them.

    There are many fine Icehouse Games that would likely meet your requirements if you don't mind the special equipment requirement (and many of them, truth be told, don't really require Icehouse pieces).

    There's Meridian which plays fairly fast, particularly once you get the rules down. My partner and I used to play it at Denny's early on Sunday mornings and we'd finished before the food arrived. It requires no board and the only equipment is a deck of cards and two markers (a nickel and a penny work fine).

    To really speed it up, you could set up a timer and the winner is the one who's gotten closer to "home" when time's up.

    \n I played a great game on a PalmPilot once. But I have no idea how to track it down, because it was called "Stones", and there are, of course, about a million games out there by that name.

    There was a blank board, square, and you took turns by placing stones on it. One player is white and one is black. The stones are discs, black on one side, and white on the other. You can only place stones adjacent to your own stones, or by "jumping" over an enemy. Once placed, a stone "flips" all adjacent stones to your colour. I think. Something like that. It was simple, fun, and full of interesting suprises and reversals, since the losing player usually had a lot more interesting moves available to him (as opposed to chess or Go).

    Another option that allows for more participants: Chinese checkers.
