\n I know, it's a compelling thread title. Don't be disappointed that it's not the new game I'm working on.

No, instead I'm going to ramble to you about how I just watched this anime on Netflix called Black Butler, and it was one of those animes that slowly wormed it's way into my heart with it's characters, premise, and lovely artwork. It's all about the relationship between a boy lord and his demon butler. If you haven't seen it, you might like it. Or it might be a touch too verging on yaoi for you, but for me that's all in good fun.

ANYWAY... that's the anime. But what I'm getting at here is that the whole story is about the main character's death. He's made a pact with a demon. The demon has agreed to help him get his revenge, with the caveat that he can eat the child's soul once revenge is achieved. So the revenge becomes this red herring... the story isn't really about that, but the demon watching the boy make choices about how he's going to act in the time he has left. Morality, love, loyalty, and happiness are all big themes.

So my question: Is there a game out there that supports a narrative that leads to a character's death? Like, specifically, our characters are all going to die at the end of this game, how do we get to that death type of narrative. Do you think this is more of a game premise instead of a game in and of itself? I guess games like Polaris and Nobilis are kinda like this. I would like to explore that narrative in a game: The Beautiful Death.

    \n Sweet. These are great suggestions so far! And some of them super obscure, which is fun.

    I've noticed a pattern emerging from these games... they're super setting and circumstance specific. I mean, I know that my criteria is kinda specific too... but, maybe not so specific as "You're on this ship and it's going down" every single time or, in the case of the mountain witch "you're always going to climb this mountain and confront this witch". It's that sweet spot between specific and general I'd like to see a bit more I think, where you can build the scenario how you prefer with the only commonality being Death at the end.

    I'm not a huge fan of one-on-one games, it would be a group game. I imagine either everyone's fate being tied together to the same end epic fantasy style, or each person having their own individual doomed fate in more of a warhammer or pendragon or hellas name thy end type of way. In either case, in the last session, everyone will die.
