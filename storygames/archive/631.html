    what has worked out well for selecting the 'right' player or players?

    Hmm... good question. Funny enough, a card game or a board game has worked each time for me. Sit them down with one of your fave card and/or board games. For instance, I like to use Settlers of Catan and Fluxx. (Yes, I know this will cause groans in some of you...and I don't care. Thhpt!)

    But anyhoo, sit them down with that. See how they learn the rules, how they enjoy the game. I've noticed that once someone understands the rules to a board game, and you explain how strategy works out, their true side comes out pretty quick because the rules are simple and both are fun games.

    One way to find people is to use Findplay. Seriously... it works. Check out your local game store too and see what's on the boards that people are running. Go to one of their game days (I'm sure they have at least one a month) and see what stuff people are talking about. Then if any of it grabs your interest, get in on the conversation.

    Local game clubs can be great ways to meet folks. Sometimes local stores have game nights that might be a good way to meet folks (I've never tried such). Many game stores have a bulletin board (which has worked well for me in the past before the wide popularity of the internet [though I should note that my first post-college gaming connections were via BBSes]).

    One of the best ways to hook up with players is to find out if your area has some kind of online gaming resources (Yahoo groups, WIKI, whatever). Every time I have an opening, I announce on those lists, and usually find suitable players quickly. I also pay attention to recent requests looking for a game and respond to those.

    Once I've made a contact, I exchange e-mail messages to start the conversation of gaming styles and interests. Then about half the time, I'll meet with folks in person.

    Of course sometimes you won't ferret out what people actually want, and they'll show up, and not be a good fit. When that happens, see if there's anything you can learn about communicating your style better and move on.

    Oh, and Findplay is certainly worth checking out (though so far all it's gotten me is two Dogs in the Vinyard sessions, oh, and perhaps sped up meeting with one more person to chat). But I've heard good stories from other areas.

    Inspired by this thread: [PtA]The Belt: Creating the show and Stealth Gaming works, I created a list of eMail addresses of around 15 people.
    Every week I sent an invitation to play to all of them. At the beginning I was trying to play mainly single sessions to avoid problems of continuity. Typically I was getting from 2 to 5 answers. Some people were coming more frequently than others. After some time I got a regular group of 3 players.
