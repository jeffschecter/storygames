\n The horror of...

a) Becoming a real and proven danger to everyone you love

b) Having your life taken away from you piece by piece because the monster in you holds the highest trump card

c) Being hunted by people you know are right to hunt you

d) Needing help from monsters like what you will become if you manage to survive, but who might just kill you themselves. (But without becoming focused on monster society like WoD games tend to do. This is not a story about new beginnings. These monsters are a source of horror for you.)

e) Requiring the unattainable from someone whom confronting for it would, you know deep down, lead to the worst possible ending.

And of course the big 'F') The constant temptation of illusory freedom: just letting the monster have it all. But specifically, the juxtaposed horror of being so tempted.

Is there a game already well-attuned to this?

    \n Well Monsterhearts could possibly do it from a teenage angst point of view. Not ideal but changing into a monster (and an adult) is core of the system and there is a mechanic that turns you into your monstrous form and makes you do bad things. If you are comfortable with the sexual themes and playing teenagers it might be a good choice otherwise it can be modded.

    The World of Darkness can do all of these themes but the support by the rules is not ideal. Often the games do not turn out to be about how horrible it is to be a monster but finding a place in the society instead. Especially Werewolf I found lacking compared to the source material of werewolf mythology. A Humanity mechanic like in Vampire can work out as a rules help. Also the idea from old Wraith where players play their own character and each others monstrous sides can be used. To give the evil inside the characters a voice and take it out of their control.
    Changeling: The Lost looks like the best for what you want and the characters can be all kinds of monsters. There definatly is a feel of being hunted and having to trust others like you and interactions with the humanity you were taken away from can be a big part of the game.
    Making the societies of monsters a little less elaborate and not comforting at all can help a good bit for World of Darkness games that want to make being a monster suck.

    \n I'd suggest looking strongly at Sorcerer. It very much focuses on these sorts of themes. I imagine it would be pretty easy to shift the flavor from magic powered by tempting demons to power derived from the dark feral self.

    The central theme of "how far are you willing to go to get what you want?", combined with tracking Humanity as the measure of existential failure seems like it would be perfect for hitting what you are aiming at.

    I would suggest attempting to tweak it as little as possible. Shift the source of power from demons to the feral self (which can work basically the same way), but leave the whole rest of the game intact.

    Also - consider running the game small (no more than 4 players) and with not everyone running werewolves. Otherwise it can become hard to keep the game from focusing on superpowered badassery. Keep it intimate. Focus on parallel stories happening at the same time. Have players play the NPCs of each others' scenes, maybe. Look to Prime Time Adventures for ideas about story structure, to supplement the bangs structure of Sorcerer.

    Also, PTA is good in terms of thinking about a story with a conclusion (5-7 sessions is what they suggest, if I recall correctly), rather than an endless campaign. For the kind of theme-heavy game you're talking about, structuring it as working towards a conclusion rather than being eternally open-ended seems like a good idea.
