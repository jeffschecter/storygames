\n So I'm a big fan of the Gentleman Bastards series by Scott Lynch. For those of you who don't know, the series takes places in a fantasy renaissance-esque city, and follows a group of characters as they commit Ocean's 11 style heists. I've been chomping at the bit to run a game focusing on this type of play. I know there is Leverage, but how does it handle non-modern games? Also for those who have played it - does the flashback mechanics become too ridiculous? Or better yet is there an alternative system or hack that's been created to run this type of game?

    \n I don't know if you were the same person posting on the Burning Wheel forums, but I definitely think Leverage would handle a pseudo-medieval setting just fine. For Gentleman Bastards, swap out the Hacker role for Alchemist. Instead of circumventing security through cyber-methods, you're prepping curious substances that bypass obstacles in weird ways, such as sleep-inducing powder or distraction flashbangs. (There's also some wilder stuff in the books, like the playing cards from Book 2.)

    I'd have to go look and see how many Hacker talents would need to be revamped (and maybe a few other Talents that assume constant communication between heist members), but otherwise it'd work just great.

    \n Well, I mean, Leverage reskins pretty slickly. The Roles aren't from any time period, except Hacker. So you can use as-is. F'rinstance, Jean might be a Hitter/Grifter while Locke is a Mastermind/Grifter and Sabetha is a Grifter/Thief. Distinctions are 100% freeform, which works well. And the Attributes can stay the same, since they're just physical. Everything else boils down to the Fixer giving traits to everything, and Traits are freeform.

    The only thing you have to wrangle are the Hacker Talents (since the role doesn't exist any more), but the book has guidance for how to homebrew Talents. Plus, watch out for Talents that presuppose radio communication like the Archangel talent for the Mastermind.

    Also, I really like the poster who posted after me and pointed out how well BW would work for GB. There's good stuff there.

    \n We started with Project: Dark (the beta rules) and, when my group was more into the heist elements as opposed to the sneaking and stealing, I switched over to Leverage because it does heists so well.

    The reskinning is really easy. The Hacker is about gadgets and information. There are a few talents that don't work at all, but if you change most of the rest to medieval or steampunk gadgets and use their ability to get into quickly, you're gold. It's also very easy to make new talents.

    \n When I read Lies of Locke Lamora, I felt it was waaaaaay more Riddle of Steel than Burning Wheel: the characters are simply better at their jobs than BW characters tend to be. BW characters just fail more often than Locke and Jean et al do. Locke and Jean end up in rough situations partly because of occasional failed rolls (especially when they're completely out of their element like the sailing parts of Book 2) but mainly because the villains simply outclass them.

    YMMV of course. But I know that I personally would be leery of using BW to try to "do" GB and expect a similar feel in the fiction.
