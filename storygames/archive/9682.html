\n Thanks to everyone who came to Go Play NW 2009! In case you were wondering, we were 70 gamers strong for the weekend. Go Play NW 2010 wants to be even better!

If you\u2019d rather send your feedback privately, you can whisper to ping in this thread or send email to goplaynw_at_gmail_dot_com.

Thanks in advance!

Tony, Phil, Ping

    \n hey team,

    what a great con! i loved almost everything. over the last two years i've been getting comfortable in the crew of usual suspects who come to the con,
    and so those two years would have been electrically exciting even if we'd been playing in a parking lot in the rain and just eating pigeons that we caught.

    this year, my palate had been whetted, or whatever, so i'll say: this has been the best go play yet, logistically. i loved the venue, it felt kind of like a club house,
    and having it be a space which is usually used for the creation of art is awesome. i mean, compared to the university or the hotel. the university had the coolness of
    giving me the feeling i was doing something important (you know, like the architecture of universities is designed to do) and the hotel gave me the feeling i was in a freaky sideshow of geeks with five dozen friends. even the few non-gamers who wandered by gave me a weird feeling. all in all, the hotel was fine though, too. the lack of windows really got to me last year.

    this year, the space was awesome (i'll continue). the water and beer kept my friends hydrated and dehydrated in turn, and that was great. i didn't actually make it for the fancy dinner on friday as that traffic reared its ugly head, but what i had of the leftovers didn't impress me much. i suppose i imagined something less like cafeteria food. this being said, next year i'd like two things: 1, a menu ahead of time, feat. all the details. 2, something saucy and ricey with bell peppers and meat or tofu or something like that. i mean, that's what i was expecting. i'm also picky. c'est la vie. i can always bring my own ricey, saucy masterpiece.

    the weekend choice made me pick favorites, there was also a big festival in olympia that weekend, with music and theater and all sorts of stuff. there's nothing i'd rather be doing with a weekend than gpnw, though, so choose what you like and i'll go where you lead.

    the first year games could only fill half their slots online, the other half was left to be filled on the floor, in the "group hug" section. i liked that idea. that being said, while i didn't get to play with everyone i wanted to, i was glad of every game i played and don't feel as if i should have chosen otherwise.



    in conclusion: outstanding. and brendan's Rubble game added a fun dimension. thanks & let's keep playing!

    \n I also liked the location. I don't have any previous GPs to compare to, but as the Tegu has declared, the private clubhouse aspect made things feel welcoming, cozy and personal. Lots of cons are held at hotels or convention centers, and they feel. . .well, like cons. This felt different. Almost like a sleepover for grownups. It created a gaming space that was more socially intimate and less businesslike or clamorous. I liked it a lot.

    I wasn't in on the feast, having decided to come at the very last minute. The surplus of beverages throughout the weekend was wonderful, though.

    The noise thing was a bit of an issue, but not bad. It was no trouble clearing out the library room for something that needed less noise pollution. Some of the solutions Mike suggests sound worth trying.

    The gaming slots were a little off-putting in that so much cool stuff was already full before arrival. Granted, this is partly due to my last-minute attendance, but I like the idea jackson mentions of having only half the events pre-posted. I prefer to approach my gaming schedule more impromptu, and I had great success with that this weekend, getting in a lot of cool games with a lot of cool people. But there was other stuff I was keen on and had no chance of getting into.

    That said, on-site facilitation of the game sessions was phenomenal! At the top of every slot, Tony or Ping or someone would be coralling everyone and matching unattached players with open or empty games. it was so cool, especially for someone like me who wasn't in on the early scheduling. I was able to do cool stuff in every block because of the organizers' care and effort. Thanks so much, guys!

    Everyone's been telling me for like a year and a half that GPNW would change my life. Now I believe them! Thanks so much!

    Peace,
    -Joel

    \n I am one of those crazy super-organized people who likes the benefits associated with that; I took the day off to ensure I could sign up for things right at noon on the Monday of sign-up, and I'd registered for my hotel, etc. two months earlier.

    I wouldn't want fewer events posted early - if anything I'd like more events posted early. But I like the idea of people only being able to pre-sign up for three things. I signed up for 4 of the 7 slots within three minutes of "unlock," leaving the others intentionally open. A lot of the games were full within those three minutes. I had the extreme pleasure of being able to game most of the weekend with the other people who were equally crazy-enthused and able to sign up immediately, but I was with the same awesome people in most of those games! They and I should have been forced to do more gaming with all the other awesome people!

    The music coming through the floors got to me a little during Montsegur on Friday night but wasn't otherwise too bad. More mobile sound baffles might help.

    Finding parking was really difficult, but that's a hazard of a great downtown location with good nearby meal choices. I had to move my car from the meter early Saturday morning and then again Saturday evening back to metered parking. I had to drive around for almost a half-hour each time; I did find eventually find a relatively cheap place only a few blocks away.

    Anyway, this was even better than my first GPNW experience two years ago which was itself better even than either of my amazing Dreamation experiences. You guys rock and I can't wait for next year.

    \n I had a couple of very minor thoughts:

    1. The "group hug" thing is really smart, but I would have loved to see time carved out for a sort of mandatory "this is all of us, in one place, paying attention" thing. By the time people gathered for announcements and matchmaking, other folks were already playing. I think, on a social level, a moment when all the participants were in one place would be a good thing. Also: Group photo, maybe.

    2. You guys drink a lot of beer! There was a lot of delicious looking extra fancy beer at the feast, and as a non-drinker I wanted some delicious extra-fancy non-beer. I think providing an appealing non-alcoholic alternative at all times is a good idea from a liability standpoint if nothing else. I was really glad for the tons of water, though!

    3. It might be worth formally blocking off the library in advance for games that need it. On Sunday morning, we sat down there for a game and soon realized that our table (House of Cards) needed to leave the space to Chris' freeform sampler table, who required quiet and stillness in the same way we had to shout and jump around. So we got up and left, but I could easily see a situation where a game was spoiled by a boisterous, oblivious neighbor.

    4. Ping and I talked about sharing lessons learned and I just wanted to put that out there for follow-up. I think all the people who organize these events have a lot to share. I learned a lot by observing how you guys did things.

    Paul Riddle is canonized, as far as I'm concerned.

    So, for my thoughts:

    * The couch in the hallway was counter-productive. It caused people to congregate in the walk path. I was guilty of this as much as so many of us.

    * The quote boards would be better placed out of the walk path next time -- people stopping in the middle to read them + couches taking up space = hallway clog.

    * This is going to me bitching for a moment: There needed to be a designated "quiet room." On Friday night, I gathered my peeps for Montsegur 1244, and it seemed like there was a bit of "quiet room" vibe going on in the library...you know, where the two games of 3:16 where?

    Yeah, no quiet there. And the other rooms weren't either. After that night, I was going to talk with Tony/Phil/Ping about a quiet room for next year...and then I heard someone say "the quiet room" on Saturday.

    For a moment, I was livid. The 3:16 games could have been played anywhere, and I got stuck between the cacophonous middle room and the "everyone meets here" main room.     \n Hrmph, the internet ate my initial post, so I will try to summarize -- which may make some of this sound blunter than it is meant. Overall I though this year was great, and it was obvious that the organizers had put a lot of work & thought into the whole deal. My thanks for that!

    Location:

    While I thought the theatre itself was great, I preferred the location of previous years (the university district.) The first year venue remains, in my mind, the ideal, what with the meals-included/cafeteria socialization and the affordable/social dorm room setup. Much of this sentiment comes down to the stress/expense of parking in downtown Seattle -- but the downtown hotels also leave something to be desired.

    Parking:\n
    Posted By: hanselLet people know about the Seattle Center parking garages. $7 per 10 hours of parking is a sweet deal in my downtown-Seattle book.
    \nThere was a lot three or four blocks from the venue (on third, next to the Bath & Beyond) where you could park for the whole weekend for $15. Unfortunately, I did not find out about it until after I had already put down $8 for interim Friday evening parking. Triply unfortunately, the lot closed at 8pm on Sunday night, so my group had to pony up an extra $25 to spring our car out of the lot. But unless the lot vanishes within the next year, it should be possible to park for the whole weekend for $15.

    Sign-Ups:\n
    Posted By: nemomemeif anything I'd like more events posted early [...] A lot of the games were full within those three minutes.
    \nThe pre-sign-up situation was pretty aggravating to me this year -- though the aggravation very quickly subsided. But, channeling my initial reaction when I saw all the printouts: oh look, almost every single game posted was full within an hour of the sign-ups going live. Many of these games were unique opportunities to either play with Out-of-Town Guests or to play games that, due to the required organization/commitment (eg. The Upgrade), were unlikely to be run as pick-up options. It is obvious that people from out of town and people running games like that will be more likely to use the online pre-sign-up stuff, and I hardly blame them.

    But to come in and see every single game on the board (so far) that I wanted to play not only full -- but, based on the timestamps, full within minutes of their even being available -- was pretty frustrating. I'm sure it would have been even more frustrating if I hadn't purposefully avoided the online sign-ups, because I would not have been at the sign-up pages in time anyways. Luckily, as mentioned, the aggravation was easily washed away by the flood of awesome pick-up gaming. But even so, every time I looked at the sign-up boards I couldn't help but feel like there was a tier of the con that I didn't have access to -- an unreasonable feeling, most likely, but there you have it.

    Quiet Room:

    Mostly I'd like to echo what everyone else has already said. The library was pretty good for this but having it organized would be even better -- the only problem I see being that, by default, everyone wants a quiet space for their game. Because it was unofficial this year that meant that only groups that were willing to risk a certain amount of social friction (uh guys could you maybe move, really sorry, but...) were able to secure the space. That said, I think better to have it known up-front and have some pre-schedule jockeying than to have people feel like they can't run their awesome-quiet-weird game anywhere.

    Mostly I kind of wish the space had one more single-table room somewhere, or an out of the way hallway (as in previous years' venues), but you can't have it all.
