Spontaneous Online Game Center?

\n
\n
\n
\n\nChristian_Griffen \n\n\n
\n
\n\nApril 2007 \n edited April 2007 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n Reading James' thread on setting up a game via Skype or something else, and the various threads on online tabletop apps, one thing occurred to me: What about having a very simple place where anyone looking for a spontaneous game can log in and meet like-minded gamers and play a quick game of PTA or TSOY or whatever?

Now, I know we have a) forum communities and b) chat places. But I don't think either the indierpgs IRC channel nor the Foundry, a MUSH, work particularly well for this because they both are somewhat difficult to get into if you have no experience with either IRC or telnet. And the chat on here is getting maybe functional enough in the dice-rolling sense, but you can't create separate rooms, etc.

I'm talking more about a simple chat website that doesn't require irc or telnet software or knowledge, where people can jump in and easily make a new room to play in, then have the basic tools necessary to play a quick game. So we could easily do this with a basic chat and dice roller interface and without the need for all those advanced features people are trying to develop for the tabletop apps places. You can even make a room for the dice there, then move everyone to Skype for most of the play activity.

So do you think we already have a place like this that we can build up more? Is there enough interest in this to put it up (should be fairly easy, I could do it in like 20 minutes)? Would it fracture the community more because there's yet another place to go?
