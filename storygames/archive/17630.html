\n Hi all,

Does anyone know of any rpgs/storygames with community-building mechanics? I'm thinking of mechanics that model characters acting to kindle community spirit, improve social relationships, build up infrastructure; generally, mechanics for building those things that make a collection of people a community.

Specifically, I'm of this from a "outsider comes in and tries to help" or "ordinary guy tries to improve his town". So I'm less interested in rules for "you're the lord of the manor" or "as GM, here's some rules to help you run towns".

E.g. I've found DungeonWorld's steading rules, which track the status of towns, cities and forts in a game world. That's interesting, but purely GM-side; I'm looking for something that's more player-facing.

On the "you're the Lord..." side there are some recommendations here http://rpg.stackexchange.com/questions/3472/realm-management-rules-that-work

Googling "community-building rpg rules" fails because of "rpg community" and similar. Anyone got ideas for more relevant search terms?



Rob

    \n Rob,

    Others mentioned it already, but I wanted to give you a link to The Quiet Year. The game revolves around two things: Create a map of a post-apocalyptic landscape, and making decisions as a community about how the community will meet its needs. I'm pretty sure it's exactly the sort of thing you're looking for.

    Caveat: it's not a game where you play scenes or own specific characters. Rather, you represent the currents of thought in a community, and it's very "high-level" in its approach.
