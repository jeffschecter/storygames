\nINTRO
I'm working on a yet unnamed fantasy PbtA game.
Let's just call it UnPlayable Fantasy PbtA for now :P

It started as just a descriptive harm system, an idea prompted by @Paul_T in one of his posts from 2014 (LINK). Later in 2016 I posted there an embryo of what would then evolve into a thorough reimagining of the whole game.

The current project draws deeply from #ApocalypseWorld2 and #DungeonWorld and some cool ideas I've seen floating around in other projects, like the #Stonetop one. But it then goes down the rabbit hole following my own personal design goals and aesthetic.

*I'm currently looking for comments, opinions and critiques* on its various game elements (harm, basic moves, class moves, etc) while I work on packaging it all in something that could be actually used in a normal game session.
The complete WIP design gdoc is available to my Patreons, but here I would like to focus on a single element at a time, to make the discussion easier to follow and more fruitful.

    \nCLARITY & USABILITY
    [This is not in the book; it is just me commenting on the following mechanics]

    As mentioned in other posts I find that a fundamental problem most PbtA games have is an underlying vagueness and ambiguity in a few critical places.
    It is good, for me, to leave blank spaces so that Players and GM can fill them with their creativity.
    It is less good, and potentially problematic, when those blanks make the game procedure muddy and unclear, requiring discussions and negotiations in order to understand if/when/how to use the game mechanics.

    The work I've done with the Basic Moves goes in the direction of fixing such problems, in an attempt to bake right into the moves what is usually only expressed in the broader rulebook text in the form of suggestions, guidelines and best practices.
    My very personal view is that if a "best practice" exists at all, then it should BE the rule, not just a subsequent addendum to clarify the rule in the (inevitable?) case that someone at the table has doubts.

    I've used as a base the moves from AW2, then expander or modified them both to fit the fantasy genre, to recall DW memories, and to serve my needs and goals.

    One very obvious element is the clear spelling out of when the GM is given a Soft or Hard move to make. This is intended to supplant the general rule about "on a 6- make a move as hard as you want".
    Too many times I've seen GMs behave in a way I dislike because of (also) this snippet of rules.
    That is, when a Move has a specific 1-6 effect it is applied as an addition to the customary freely chosen GM Hard Move; so that on a 1-6 the Player suffers 2 Hard moves.
    This usually becomes the crack in the wall that eventually spirals gameplay back into a much more "traditional" mold where the GM ignores rules and procedures in favor of just winging whatever they feel appropriate for a situation: in my experience this turns out 9 times out of 10 as very low quality play... with either too bland or too harsh consequences that rarely move the fiction in meaningful ways.

    It's a subtle association of ideas, a slippery slope that plays on the "old habits" of many players.
    Apparently, I can make 2 Hard moves in these A B C instances.
    So, I can make more than 1 Hard move at a time, as a general possibility.
    So, I can find other occasion where multiple Hard moves would fit.
    So, I can make multiple Hard moves whenever I feel it appropriate.
    So, I can use multiple Hard moves to represent more complex situations and consequences.
    So, I can ignore the Hard move list and its rules and limits because (at this point) it is just so much easier to wing consequences out of the top of my head whenever I need to, and still believe that I'm following the rules.
    I have seen this happen countless times, and in time I have "reverse engineered" the whole mental process leading to this, by discussing with many GM "why are you behaving like this?".

    I hope with my rules and moves to, among other things, make it as difficult as possible for people to go down this path.

    Defy Danger\n
    When you take action in the face of immediate danger, under great pressure or despite adverse odds...

    ...describe how you do it and roll.
    If you do it by means of...
    .. brawn & power\t\t+STR
    .. speed & finesse\t\t+DEX
    .. endurance & stamina\t+CON
    .. cleverness & witt\t\t+INT
    .. resolve & knowledge\t+WIS
    .. charm & social grace\t+CHA

    10+\t= you do it and ... describe an additional positive side effect
    7-9\t= you do it but ... describe a negative side effect, or a positive one but granting the GM a Soft Move
    6-\t= Hard Move
    \n.
    ..
    ...
    Discern Realities\n
    When you observe something the GM will tell you what is obvious and clear. But when you focus your attention ona place or situation...

    ...roll+WIS to ask the GM questions. If you act on the answers you get Advantage.

    10+\t= ask 2
    7-9\t= ask 1
    6-\t= ask 1 + Hard Move

    where\u2019s my best escape route / way in / way past?
    which enemy is the most vulnerable / threatening to me?
    who\u2019s in control here?
    what has happened recently / is about to happen here?
    what here is useful / valuable to me?
    what here is not what it appears to be?
    what should I be on the lookout for?
    \n.
    ..
    ...
    Help or Interfere\n
    When you help or interfere with someone who\u2019s making a roll...

    ...describe how you do it. You are now exposed to any consequence coming from the roll. Say whether the target character has Advantage or Disadvantage.
    \n.
    ..
    ...
    Parley\n
    When you try to seduce, lie, bluff, manipulate, fast-talk to someone...

    ...tell them what you want them to do; tell them why they should do it, give them a reason. If they refuse or haggle, roll+CHA.

    For NPCs:
    10+\t= done deal; they\u2019ll go along with your request unless or until something betrays the reasons you gave them
    7-9\t= it is not over; their words or actions will make you understand one way in which you could get them to do what you ask; the GM will express it clearly. If you don\u2019t do it, it\u2019s their Soft Move.
    6-\t= Hard Move

    For PC:
    10+\t= both effects from the 7-9 list
    7-9\t= choose one:
    if they go along with you, they earn 1 XP
    if they refuse, they can\u2019t earn XP until they hit a 3 XP Key
    6-\t= Hard Move
    \n.
    ..
    ...
    Read a Person\n
    When you read a person in a charged interaction...

    ...roll+WIS and, while you\u2019re interacting with them, spend your hold to ask their Player questions, one by one.

    10+\t= hold 2
    7-9\t= hold 1
    6-\t= hold 1 + Hard Move

    is your character telling the truth?
    what is your character really feeling?
    what does your character intend to do?
    what does your character wish I\u2019d do?
    how could I get your character to __ ?
    \n.
    ..
    ...
    Spout Lore\n
    When you consult your accumulated knowledge about something...

    ...describe how your character knows about such things and explain what would be useful for you in this situation, then roll+INT.

    10+\t= the GM gives you truthful info in good detail, pointing out how and why it is useful
    7-9\t= the GM gives you truthful but vague or partial info, something she deems useful to your aim
    6-\t= the GM gives you truthful but vague or partial info + Hard Move
    \n.
    ..
    ...
    End of Session\n
    When you end a game session...

    ...first, review your list of Keys. You can now delete one. If you have less than 3 Keys you can work with the other Players and the GM to create new Keys for your character. All must agree that the new Keys are interesting to have at the table.

    Then review your Drives. You can modify or change your Personal Drive, but both GM and other Players must agree that the new Drive is interesting to have at the table. You can also suggest to modify or change the Fellowship Drive, again with everyone\u2019s consensus.

    Finally have the group look back at the game session just concluded. For each of the following tasks you can perform, every PC earns 1XP:
    - recount a list of memorable moments from this session, one moment for each PC
    - describe how the world is different because of the PCs latest actions
    - ??? [other options I need to come up with]
