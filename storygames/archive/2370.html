    Shock: has a system of establishment, rising action, and climax. For every player, every time. It's designed to tell short stories (perhaps in sequence).

    The Mountain Witch uses "chapters"; while they're not of huge mechanical significance, they're an indication to the players of what they should be doing at that moment with their characters.

  • jenskot
    February 2007
    Burning Empires has 4 types of scenes: color, building, interstitial, and conflict. Scenes are a resource in BE which not only create an awesome narrative structure that does a great job of replicating the comics the material is based but is essential to the tactical elements of the game.

    Vincent considers Otherkind sort of a cute early experiment. PTA, which came later, introduced most of us to mechanically supported story structure. Adding Issues (which are basically the story arc mechanic) is nontrivial, but I think if you find the relevant number of dice to add for a given position in the story arc, it could work. Maybe you decide what color dice your issue is, changing it only after your Spotlight?
