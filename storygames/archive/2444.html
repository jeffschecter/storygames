Web 2.0-ify Actual Play Reports

tony_dowler
February 2007 edited February 2007 in Story Games
This is a little idea I posted in the Audience Participation thread, but maybe that was the wrong place for it.

It seems to me that the community could really use a collaborative tool for sharing actual play reports more effectively. There are quite a few APs that have essentially become classics, and it would be nice if there were a way to share this better.

What I propose is a Web app where people can link to actual play reports, mark rate their favorites, filter, sort, and search according to game, player, or theme, and so on. If we get a decent level of participation, it will give people a way to quickly find and share really good actualy play. Does this sound like a tool anyone would use? Do you have any suggestions what kind of features it would need?

    Zak,

    I'd love to see how you write APs. I've only written a few myself, and I'm far from an expert.

    Shreyas,

    The vision is making them easier to find an use for whatever you want to use them for. Diagnostic tools is part of this, and you can't make good use of the tools if you can't find them.

    I've noticed that Actual Play is becoming more and more a part of how our group games. Here are some examples from our group:
    • We swtich systems every month or two. The last switch we had was motivated by an actual play one of the group read. Getting everyone to read the AP was an important part of prep for that game because it encapsulated what the GM loved about Dictionary of Mu.
    • Many times an issue comes up at the table and someone says "you should see how this was covered in Actual Play x."
    • When I get ready to run a system, I want to read good APs of it in use. There's
    • A few APs have become classics of a sort, well worth reading just for fun. "Moose in the City" and "Flaming Taft" come to mind.
    In our growing use of APs, there are a number of problems that come up again and again.
    • "Where was that AP again?" No standard place to find an AP that I want to read.
    • The community collectively knows where some really awesome APs are, but we have no standard way to share that intelligence.

    • There has been much rumbling about making the artifacts of gaming (APs among them) into objects of enjoyment in their own right. For this to happen, those artifacts need to be findable. Otherwise, it's like we're just waiting for the world to randomly stumble across our APs and decide they're art.
    • Categorization: What if I could suddenly see every AP with Ron Edwards in it? What if I could see Ron Edwards' list of favorite APs?

    Posted By: LarryAs I understand, in Japan there is actually a market for "replays" as products in and of themselves. One of the J-gamers could probably elaborate on what function they fulfill.
    As I understand it, replays are put into rulebooks as a way to demonstrate how to play the game; some Western games do this, but the ones in Japanese games tend to be longer (at least 3 full pages), and are done in a script format.

    Replays have also become a form of entertainment in themselves, as well as a way to show off a new game. Replays are published as doujinshi, in magazines, and even actual books, not to mention put up on websites. The issue of Role&Roll I ordered is chock full of replays, including ones for Meikyuu Kingdom and the new edition of Tunnels & Trolls, both of which just came out in Japan.
