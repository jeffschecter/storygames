\n So, I'm designing this game about Existentialism and self-definition in the context of others. I got to discussing this with the Man with the Master Plan, Ryan Macklin, and he pointed out that in explaining the game and what I wanted the players to get out of it, I kept going back to the example of High School.

A brief overview of the system: You want to be a certain person. You need to get traits to do that. Also, you're not alone in the world; you need to define traits on the other characters so that you can be what you want (the Rebel needs an Authority Figure, the Leader needs a Follower, and so on.) The more traits you have, the more difficult everything is.

Frankly, I've gotten the Alpha Playtest Document written, and if I am going to change the setting, now is the time.

ARE YOU READY TO RUMBLE?!?!?

IN THE BLUE CORNER: Weighing in at a hefty three hundred and fourty five pounds, Nuclear Armageddon!
The world has been destroyed by a massive nuclear war, and the players adopt the roles of survivors in the aftermath. With nothing left, the survivors are left awash in a painfully absurd environment. We follow their daily struggle for survival in a meaningless and uncaring world as they try to create identity.

And IN THE RED CORNER, weighing in at a spritely three hundred ten pounds, High School!
As Freshmen on the first day of high school you are a new person. You are perfectly free to be anyone you want. However, this project of self-definition is not in a vacuum, in order to be who you want, you've got to get the validation and cooperation of others.

Ready, and FIGHT!
