\n I think everybody has a "go to system" -- that familiar friend that you always seem to return to, despite regularly branching out and trying new things. For me, that system has been The Window since about 1998. There's a creator-blessed Deluxe Edition out there now, as well as a fair number of conversions (e.g., Shadowrun to The Window), rule mods (e.g., my own Window Frame and Live Action Window), and settings (e.g., Saint Saul, Warlock, etc). So, does anybody else here at Story Games play/use The Window on a regular basis? Or am I the one amongst hundreds

    Posted By: Christopher KubasikWhat's the difference between the basic and the Deluxe editions?

    CK
    \nGood question. The Deluxe Edition sports a stylish new cover (full color!), better layout for printing/binding, the original core rules, all of the offical optional rules, some fan-created optional rules (and die probability tables!), the Stage setting, and the old Character Menagerie once hosted at the MiM site. Basically, the Deluxe Edition is more of a full-on game that's ready to play out of the box. It's still free, to boot. And, yes, Scott was contacted (by me) and granted his permission for this 'new edition' of the rules.

    \n What Andy said. More specifically:

    It falls foul of the sin of being "revolutionary" by trying to hide numbers behind words. Which just makes it more fiddly.

    Quoting from their own rules:\n
    Posted By: The WindowEverything about a Window character is described with adjectives rather than numbers.
    \n
    Posted By: The WindowAs we have already explained, every ability a Window character possesses, be it an inherent trait or a skill, will be associated with an appropriate adjective or brief description. Once you've got an adjective, it should be a simple matter to decide which rung that ability falls into. Once you've got a rung, you've got a die.
    \n
    Posted By: The WindowThe Window Competency Ladder

    Incredible (D4)
    Very High (D6)
    High (D8)
    Above Average (D10)
    Average (D12)
    Below Average (D20)
    Low (D30)
    \n
    Posted By: The WindowRoyce Jenner has...

    Below average strength. (D20)
    Average agility. (D12)
    Pretty good health. (D10)
    Impressive knowledge of the world. (D8)
    Very high sanity. (D6)
    Strong powers of perception. (D8)
    Average luck. (D12)
    \nSo what they mean when you say "Everything about a Window character is described with adjectives rather than numbers", is that everything is described as an adjective, then translated into a number so that the game system can actually work, then written down and never spoken about. Well done. I really need you to tell me that I was allowed to describe my character as being a "Strong powers of perception" because I have a high Notice skill.

    Pillock.

    Ah well, I guess for 1998 it was pretty original, but the tone was still a show stopper for me. It's nice to hear that other people had fun with it though.

    Posted By: Rich StokesWhat Andy said. More specifically:

    It falls foul of the sin of being "revolutionary" by trying to hide numbers behind words. Which just makes it more fiddly.
    \nIn fairness, there are quite a few Indie games that fall prey to this specific trap -- The Window is hardly unique in this regard. Indeed, both Fudge and Fate suffer from this exact same flaw, as do games such as Hero's Banner (though they're admittedly quite a bit more humble about it). Many more indie games fall into the general trap of "It's revolutionary!" while very few of them actually do anything truly original. The biggest flaw of the Window is, arguably, hubris. And it's in good company there (much to my chagrin).
