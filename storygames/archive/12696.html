      \n I need to underline what a pleasantly bizarre experience this was.
      \n
      \nMany players were GMs who don't get to play often. And as a result, it didn't matter what games anyone played or who was GMing. It was the purest example of everyone is responsible for the group's fun (not just the GM) that I've ever seen!
      \n
      \nI go to easily 6-10+ cons a year and "Story Games" cons like Camp Nerdly get very close to what I'm talking about. 10 10 10 was similar with more subtlety (not everything needed to be awesome although awesome was appreciated). People playing quiet characters were rewarded and cherished just as much as the guy who jumped on the grenade with a back flip.
      \n
      \nLet me emphasize, not every GM was amazing. Everyone who attended (with exceptions) had to GM. So you had people with very little experience running (and in some cases nervous). But this was probably one of the best cons for them to get experience GMing. After a few games I played the players broke into discussions with the GM to detail what worked well and what needs refinement... all very direct with no pulled punches but also respectful.
      \n
      \n4 out of my 5 games were fantastic. But one was a bit of a mess (for multiple reasons) and even with the players trying to help it couldn't be salvaged. It wasn't the worst game but the GM was disappointed. But it turned into a great experience for him. We ended the game an hour or so early and discussed what could be done for next time and explored where things got bumpy. He seemed pretty excited and immediately started rewriting the scenario.
