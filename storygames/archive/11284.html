    \n Yeah. There are Terrain types that have various functions. There are cards for the terrain that are placed on the table top (like plopping down terrain in a minis game only less anal about distances and stuff). The effect the terrain has is on the card and it becomes a sort of abstracted "location" that you can move your figures to/from/around. I'd have to pop out my set to tell you what the types are and what they do. There aren't that many included in the core set, but it sounds like there will be a good many more included in the upcoming campaign set.

    But it wouldn't be hard to make your own using those as examples...I don't recall their mechanical effects being that difficult to figure out.

    \n Without the cards, the Gm can mostly add difficulty dice that have either failure or blank sides (Misfortune dice I think they are called/ the black one) to your actions to represent a bunch of effects like difficult terrain, and if you want to go the full Narrative way you can say that if the Player rolls any failure on his misfortune dice something bad happen (fall into the river, slip on rocks,etc...)
