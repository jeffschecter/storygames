    \n Uh... the Tick vs. the Red Scare?

    The problem with superheroes and the Cold War is that the Cold War is based on the threat of mutual annihilation. Superheroes screw that up, either by being able to prevent nuclear weapons from harming targets, or by replacing the balance with a balance between superheroes instead of nukes. The problem with the latter is that superhero balance doesn't work like MAD does. For one thing, nuclear weapons don't have agendas of their own. For another, when you reach defcon-1 and the heroes all fight, chances are there is a lot of collateral damage, but you don't kill billions of people.
