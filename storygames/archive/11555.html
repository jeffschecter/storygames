\n Going solo: Barrow on the Hill

As I am nearing the end of grad. school here my free time crops up in odd places. So I've been fiddling around with solo dungeoneering. Tony's microdungeons are pretty great for this. All though in the end I am creating my own opposition so it can get a little boring (still trying to figure that one out).


The dungeon is: Barrow on the Hill

The character
Goal: Retrieve the sword and quiet the Barrow

Elven Warrior - Hero (4 Men) HTK: 4
Wearing- Shadowed Maille
Wielding - the Fallen Hand Axe and Black Forged Lucern Hammer
Carrying: Backpack, 1 week iron rations, flint/steel, 2 bundles of torches, 30m of rope, iron spikes, small hammer, 4 flasks of oil, bag of chalk, 2 waterskins,

Troop Type
Hand Axe - Lt/Hvy
Lucern Hammer - Hvy/Hvy

Man to Man
Hand Axe - 4 dice
Lucern Hammer - 4 dice

    \n Combat with the skeletons.

    Round 1
    Elven Warrior - rolling 5d6 --> 1,2,5,5,6 so it's the attack is Hvy Foot vs Lt Foot (skeletons). I scored 3 hits so the number of skeletons drops from 6 to 3.

    Skeletons - rolling 6d6 with a -1 on the last die 4,3,1,6,3,3,2-1 so one hit on the elven warrior HTK:3

    Round 2
    Elven Warrior - rolling 5d6 - 2,1,2,2,6. One hit of the skeletons, so two remaining

    Skeletons - rolling 3d6 with a -1 on the last die 3,5,6-1 so the elven warrior gets lucky no hits

    Round 3
    Elven Warrior rolling 5d6 - 5,6,5,6,6 the undead hellspawns get blasted back into errr death
    Skeletons - rolling 2d6 1,5 no hits

    I sorted of ignored the initiative rules in Chainmail. I'll present the fight with rules in a little bit. But onto the greater glory and peril for the elven warrior
