    \n I am torn between picking something that is short and simple to play and adapt like Risus or Fudge or taking something with volume like D&D or Rolemaster.
    \n
    \nThe simple rules would allow for simple play and story telling but you don't really need rules for that you could make it up around the campfire. The complicated rules require the reference material which also would serve as reading material when you are not playing but would also require a higher level of sophistication in your desert isle. flat surfaces, good lighting to read the dice, miniatures, a supply of paper and extra people to join the group when some of them get bored with the game and want to try some indie games for a while.
    \n
    \nThere is also a small part of me that would want to take a game based on any ruleset that is quite detailed and is set on a deserted island. It would allow for a very immersive play experience and a more thorough playtest than any game system so far.
