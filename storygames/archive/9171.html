    \n Paul: I'm bringing both classic and jeepform Ganakagok ready to run. I'm still mulling over the differences between the two versions in terms of how they play (having only run the latter once) but I suspect that the classic game is "cooler" in the McLuhanesque sense: it's more intimate and character-intensive. The jeepform game seems more plot-centered, oddly enough. In any event, it'll be on the wiki.

    I've also got two Spirit of the Century adventures prepped: "Rex Rich and the Warlord of Mars" and "Yangtze Rocket Patrol" (my brother Mel wrote this one). These are pulpish high-adventure stories with an emphasis on derring-do.

    And I've got a Trail of Cthulhu adventure ready called "I, Governor..." that I think could be awesome. It's atmospheric Mythos noir that takes place mostly in 1934 Hollywood, ripped from the pages of history and featuring a cast of the notable and the notorious.

    So I've got a lot of stuff I'm ready to run!

    Obvious Monstrosities (Playstorm)
    GM/Organizer: Robert Bohl
    Description: Obvious Monstrosities will one day be a game about sex, violence, and cliquishenss where you play twentysomething werewolves. You are going to define in the "infection phase" what the rules of werewolfism are, character creation happens during play, and there are other phases planned as well (hunting phase, discovery phase, denouement). The thing is I'm not sure what the system will be like too much yet. That's why I need your help playstorming it. What else? It's GMless. Yeah. Sign up!
    Number of spots available: 4? 5?
    Participant Requirements: Everyone will be new to this game, including me.
    Age Rating: This is definitely an adult, or at least late-teen, game.
    Preferred Activity Space: wherever
