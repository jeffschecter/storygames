\n I was wondering if there was a game out there that already had a fictional astrological system in place which instead of using the zodiac (Aries, Cancer, etc.) or Chinese version (Ox, Monkey, etc.) uses mythological creatures to define personal characteristics?
\n
\nIf not, what 12 mythological creatures would you suggest and why? I'm looking for a system of 12 to correspond to the 12 months in a year. Also, I'm trying to find suggestions that are not completely from the classical Western cosmology so that the 12 signs are not just manticore, harpy, hydra, pegasus, and so forth.

    \n If I were doing it, I'd just mix and match animals and myths and make it up from scratch.

    A winged rat (cleverness)
    A massive cat with bony, tooth-like protusions sticking out everywhere (viciousness)
    A bull with gold skin, jeweled teeth, and silver hair (contentment)
    A skeletal ape (despair)
    A blue crow (watchfulness)
    A three-eyed shark (forwardness)
    A dog with horns like a ram (loyalty)
    A maned eagle with poisoned claws (deadly grace)
    A scorpion that paralyzes and drinks blood (parasitic)
    A whale with a bony, ridged back (rebelliousness)
    A beetle with a hypnotic song (deception)
    A lizard with a deadly, metallic tail (nobility)

    \n Exalted has a Zodiac. From first edition, there were 25 constellations, each corresponding to one of the skills in the game. Sidereals (Star Exalted) had special astrology powers tied to the constellations.

    They changed the skill list from 1st ed to 2nd ed, so I can't say how, if at all, that affected the constellations.

    The Oblivion (Elder Scrolls) computer rpgs have their own zodiac, with signs like the Mage, the Lady, and the Atronach (elemental demon things.) Your sign gives you special abilities.
