\n All right, Story Games, help me out a little.

A Taste For Murder is nearly ready, but the cover is giving me problems. It'll have the title, of course, and my name. It'll also have an illustration of Inspector Chapel, the main character, which will be something like this:



And hopefully it'll look a bit like an old novel.

But I can't think of a subtitle. Do I need one? Does it need to say "A game about death"? Should it be funny: "Another bloody roleplaying game"? Or should it even go before the title, as I remember novels used to do: "In 1930s England, a madman in a country house develops...A Taste For Murder"?

None of those are right. The game is about an English murder mystery in a 1930s country house. Does anyone have any brilliant ideas?

Graham

    \n The idea of doing a Classic Penguin edition is almost irresistible.

    There's a subtle difference between American pulp and British murder mystery, which I'm trying to put my finger on. Jason's cover straddles the boundary quite neatly.

    Many pulp covers, however, look more like hard-boiled fiction than murder mystery. Some of them verge into B-Movie horror. You can have a bit of pulp and horror, but not too much.

    So far, the best Agatha Christie cover I can find is this:



    Again, it's slightly pulpy, but not in-your-face screaming lettering.

    One of my favourites is not pulpy at all:



    And here's one that's nearly perfect, illustration-wise, if you imagine the lettering a bit more pulpy:
