\n I wrote a roleplaying game called \xc3\u2020ternal Legends. It's going to be published on August 24th.

\xc3\u2020ternal Legends is a modern fantasy game about heroes with ideals beating up cynical antiheroes and taking their magical power (kinda). There's a lot more to it, from the Gnostic setting to the magical archetypes based off the Sephiroth, from hidden lands inspired by Charlie Stross and Grant Morrison to manticores hiding in the lion prides of Africa.

The first 21 pages of Chapter III - The Power of Belief are up as a preview download from Lulu. More previews of artwork and other bits of the book are available on the preview LiveJournal.

    The setting's not entirely classic \u2014 the elves aren't pointy-haired immortals who are Better Than Humans\u2122 (a schtick that I'm not a fan of), they're the children of Gods and men, with their divine heritage showing through. Even goblins aren't cannon fodder, instead being rebels, iconoclasts, and antinomians.

    \n I'll tackle those in order, and hope that I make sense while doing so.\n
    Clade
    What kind of fantasy creature you are. In the default game mode, this is chosen from Human, Elf, Dwarf, Gnome. The latter three map to Orc, Troll, Goblin when the character deliberately contravenes his beliefs and falls to the Dark. "Clade" as a term came about because I hate using "Race" for the same term, especially with all the baggage that's attached to the latter word.
    Circle
    The party's name or title.
    Spheres
    The archetypal roles that Legends (magically empowered heroes, the default PC mode) take on. Based on the lower 6 Sephiroth. Characters can choose to become an exemplar of one Sphere or join many on the road to attaining a higher Sphere. A role is the character's specific approach to her Sphere, defined as an Attribute + Sphere combo that gives mechanical bonuses when it comes up.
    Beliefs
    Beliefs are used to evaluate a character's actions. The Light/Dark dichotomy (playing off traditional fantasy Good vs Evil) revolves around whether you embrace your beliefs or actively ignore them. Ignoring your beliefs tips you towards the Dark (and a possible change in Clade). Mechanically, Beliefs also serve as benchmarks to refresh Conviction and Degeneration. Beliefs themselves do not have to be "positive" or "good" \u2014 being true to your beliefs is more important than what others think of those beliefs.
    Degeneration
    Degeneration is the opposite of Conviction. Both are fairly traditional "hero point" pools, which vary in when you can spend them: Conviction when following your beliefs and Degeneration when working against them. Degeneration starts at 0, it's up to the character to raise it through play. A character's available magic points is equal to her Conviction plus Degeneration
