\n So I'm fiddling with the SRD again, for E6, and I'm trying a different tack.

When I see something in the rules that I think slows down play, I'm take it out and create an appendix with all those rules. This includes searching through the rest of the document and finding references, and rewriting those sections so the text is right, then updating the appendix.

The result, so far, is pretty ugly, but it's given me some better ideas about how d20 fits together. For example, taking Diseases out of the core text and into an appendix dragged along a bunch of monsters and the paladin. I also discovered that the Chaos Beast is probably the most un-fun thing I can throw at players of an E6 game. \n
The Original d20 3.5 SRDCorporeal instability is not a disease or a curse and so is hard to remove. A shapechange or stoneskin spell does not cure an afflicted creature but fixes its form for the duration of the spell. A restoration, heal, or greater restoration spell removes the affliction (a separate restoration is necessary to restore any drained points of Wisdom).
The E6 SRDCorporeal instability is not a disease or a curse and so is hard to remove. Good luck!

    \n Well, my main criteria is speed of play.

    Ability scores - yeah, I went the True20 route, experessing things in terms of bonuses instead of scores. The appendix covers how to convert back, if you want to. I find it easier to have the bonuses right in front of me if, say, I want to do a Strength check.
    Alignment - I find the Alignment entry in monster stat blocks superfluous. I sliced "Alignment" off to an Appendix, but I left aligned damage, aligned creature types, and aligned spells and so on in the rules. But the assumption that every character has an alignment is only present in the appendix.
    Use Rope and Escape Artist - again, it's more of a "what does my eye scan for" when I'm running a monster. These skills are used pretty rarely in my experience. Of course, if you want them, they'll be right there in the appendix.

    In general, I find the skill system to be the biggest source of crossreferencing headaches. I'm currently looking at farming the entire thing off to appendices so I can take a real look at how it fits together.

    I've had to reorganize the documents, so each appendix will come in a few parts, like this:

    Appendix 001A - Alignment
    Appendix 001B - Alignment and Other Appendices
    Appendix 001C - Alignment and Monsters
    Appendix 001D - Alignment and Magic

    That might seem crazy, but it's really a function of the interrelated nature of that iteration of d20.

    \n Here's my fifty-first appendix. Useless stuff included just for its own sake. Nothing else references this stuff.


    APPENDIX 051A
    THE LOCAL MOVEMENT TIMESCALE

    \u2022 Local is an optional timescale, for exploring an area, and is measured in feet per minute.
    LOCAL MOVEMENT
    Characters exploring an area use local movement, measured in feet per minute.
    Walk: A character can walk without a problem on the local scale.
    Hustle: A character can hustle without a problem on the local scale. See Overland Movement, below, for movement measured in miles per hour.
    Run: A character with a Constitution score of 9 or higher can run for a minute without a problem. Generally, a character can run for a minute or two before having to rest for a minute

    Table: Movement and Distance

    LOCAL MOVEMENT AND GETTING LOST
    Your chance of getting lost after an hour of local movement is the same as an hour of overland movement.

    \n In one way, I'm taking some serious liberties with how things work, but in another way it's all the same.

    For example, the Climb skill has been spun off into an Appendix, BUT the details for untrained climb checks are included with Strength checks, and there's a new category of feats (Training feats) that lets you add Level+3 to such checks. Fighters get to choose 2 such feats at 1st level, plus intelligence modifier (minimum 1).

    In the end, I think there will be chapters for

    Basics
    Ability Scores
    Level-based benefits
    Feats
    Combat
    Magic

    Threats
    Rewards
    Assets
    Problems

    And appendices for everything else.
