[The Muse] A try at making a "gateway game"

Colin_Fredericks
December 2006 edited December 2006 in Game Design Help
Coming fresh from reading some of the threads on de-geeking gaming, gateway games, and mainstreaming roleplaying, I took a few days and wrote this: The Muse. (That's a download link; 130k .zip file.) It's a story-telling game, in two pages, with sheets for characters and players in separate files.

The basic setup of the game is that every player writes Visions; things they want to have happen during the game. Players get points when they work other players' Visions into the storyline. The trick is that Visions are secret, so you have to foreshadow. It has a cooperative mode and a competitive mode, but they split only at the end of the game, so you want to both get as many points as you can for yourself and for your fellow players (at least, I hope that's how it will work).

Comments and suggestions are welcome, especially things that would make it more accessible.

    Well, if they aren't really needed, I'd suggest - though this is a relatively weak suggestion - that some manner of putting that information "in the middle" be used.

    As accessibility things go, people are used to the idea that privately held information in games is secret information - as with a hand of cards. If it isn't secret, it fits better with most people's impressions of how games work to put it "on the table".

    Again, it's a pretty weak suggestion. But I've been playing with fancy relationship maps and the like specifically because of this idea - that center-of-the-table stuff is shared, while held-by-myself stuff is secret.
