\n I\u2019m collecting slang terms for an appendix for my game The Rustbelt. I\u2019m looking for slang that is colorful, rough, earthy, gritty. Probable good sources include early 20th century hipster slang, sailor slang, army slang, cop slang, criminal and underground argot, etc. If you know any good ones, post \u2018em. Here\u2019s what I\u2019ve got so far:
\n
\nBeater: a car in really bad shape
\nBilk: to cheat
\nBlack Maria(h): a police wagon
\nBone orchard: a cemetery
\nBulldoze: to threaten, bully, coerce
\nBunko artist: a con-man
\nBurned down: played out, run into the ground, used until useless
\nC, Charge, or Charly: cocaine
\nCool: the opposite of Hot (below)
\nthe Chucks: the munchies
\nCroaker: a doctor, esp. one that will write prescriptions for illicit drugs
\nGo left: to go suddenly crazy, or to fly into a sudden rage
\nGospel mill: a church
\nH, Horse, or Henry: heroin
\nthe Heat: the police
\nHeater, Gat, Rod, or Piece: a gun
\nHeeled: carrying a gun
\nHot or Uncool: describes a person, thing, or activity that is likely to attract the attention of the police, or a place that is frequented by the police
\nHot shot: poison (usually strychnine) passed off as drugs (esp. junk) to a user, in an attempt to kill him
\nJoy bang: an occasional use of a drug by someone who does not have a habit (or claims they don\u2019t)
\nJunk: any drug derived from opium
\nLight a shuck: to get the hell out of here
\nMake: to recognize someone and/or their intentions; as in, \u201cShit, they\u2019ve made us; run!\u201d
\nMark: a target or victim, esp. for a con
\nMud: opium
\nOil burner: describes a habit (esp. a drug habit) that requires a lot to satisfy, constantly; from \u201coil burner\u201d as applied to a car that leaks oil such that it burns off, and you have to replace it a lot
\nPacked: carrying a weapon
\nPut down a routine or hype or con: to persuade someone through a story, esp. a mendacious one
\nRumble or Shake or Shake-down: search & seizure, esp. by the police
\nShove the queer: to pass counterfeit money
\nthe Simon pure: the real thing, the genuine article
\nSmash: spare money or change
\nSpeedball: a mixture of cocaine and junk, taken intravenously
\nSpike: a hypodermic needle
\nTea, Gage, or Greefa: marijuana
\nTouch: the act of asking someone for money; as in, \u201cJoe, is this a touch?\u201d or \u201cHe touched me for ten dollars\u201d
\nWalking Spanish: being made to walk somewhere you don\u2019t want to go, as at gunpoint
\nWooden overcoat or coat or kimono: a coffin
\nWorking the hole: robbing passed-out drunks
\nWrong: describes someone who is a known collaborator with the cops, as in \u201che\u2019s wrong\u201d or \u201che went wrong\u201d
\nYen or Jones: an intense desire, esp. for sex or drugs; also a verb: yenning, jonesing

    These are common Army slang, and as such may not be common, but it's good slang, I think. Some may not be appropriate as I've noticed a lack of any curse words in your list, but you didn't specifically prohibit them, so...

    \n

    Ratfuck: To ransack, take everything useful out of something, making it worthless
    \nAte Up: Jacked up, not right. "You're ate up, you need to fix yourself!"
    \nSoup Sandwich: synonymous with ate up. Sometimes used in conjunction: Ate up like a soup sandwich.
    \nMan up: Be tough, be a man about it
    \nSuck it up: Quit complaining, can be used as a synonym of man up.
    \nCrunchy: Armor-slang for ground troops; Can be generalized to be anyone you run over with a vehicle
    \nPop Smoke: Get the hell out of there; Referencing using smoke to disguise your movement
    \nExfiltrate/Exfil: The opposite of infiltrate; get out in a covert manner

    The Rustbelt is, to boil it down as far as I can, about what hardship and desperation do to people. To be more colorful about it, it's set in a near-future world in the throes of a slow apocalypse, as a cosmic force of corruption and dissolution (the Rust) slowly eats everything away. Structurally, the game is in the same family as Sorcerer and Over the Edge.

    The link that Stephen gave above is an older version of the game, but I guess it can give you some idea of what the game is about. The ashcan version is available at the UnStore here, and I'm currently working on the final edition.
