    That rule seems to trip folks up pretty regularly- I think the key to understanding it is that it's just meant as as an initial set of relationships, to get the game started with a bang. After the first scene, the players can develop those relationships in any direction they like.

    The wording in that example is a little strange- though "ally" does imply "mutual ally", it ought to say something more like "He's found an insipid patsy in the sculptor..."
