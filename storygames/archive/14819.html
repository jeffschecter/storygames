    \n Give it scarier custom moves. When you are in the presence of Scary Dragon, roll +cool...; When you do harm to Scary Dragon, roll +harm done...; When you speak the name of Scary Dragon, roll +weird...

    Give it more friends, ideally NPCs with which the PCs have relationships already.

    Tie it more strongly to existing scarcity. Maybe the dragon's very presence is somehow providing something that people need -- for example, maybe the heat it generates is melting glaciers that are providing fresh water to nearby holdings. Or maybe it is providing these things on purpose -- maybe the dragon provides valuable advice, for example, in exchange for human sacrifice.

    Alternately, make the tougher dragon more emphatically the cause of scarcity, ratcheting up pre-existing scarcity to a horrifying degree. Eg. its heat causes the local watering hole to evaporate completely; it eats all the food, or all the farmers who produce the food.

    Give it crueller, more insatiable desires.

    \n Be sure to give them armor and harm numbers. If they're bigger than a dude, use the gang size rules. It might make sense to treat their armor as vehicle armor, not just as a dude's armor, too, so be sure to read up on vehicles suffering harm. (In this case, the dragon itself would suffer only the harm that blows through.)

    If that's not enough, I'd adapt the vehicle rules to them. Give them stats (power and cunning, maybe, in addition to armor) and a custom move:

    A no shit dragon
    When you're up against a dragon...
    ...And you're acting under fire, subtract its cunning from your roll.
    ...And you're seizing something from it by force, subtract its power AND cunning from your roll.
    ...And you go aggro on it, dude, do your worst.
    ...And you try to seduce or manipulate it, subtract its cunning from your roll.
    ...And you read it, subtract its cunning from your roll.
    ...And you open your brain to the world's psychic maelstrom, add its power to your roll.
    ...And you use a character move, the MC might have you subtract its power, its cunning, or both from your roll, at her discretion.

    Then you can just give the tougher dragon tougher stats.

    \n I don't know...

    My hunch is that mechanical differentiation isn't going to be the best way to handle this in Apocalypse World.

    There should be something clear, in the fiction, that makes one dragon more badass than the other.

    For instance, if a dragon is bullet-proof and 30 feet tall, and you say "I go aggro on it!" ... I wouldn't want the answer to be, "Ok, tell me what you do, and roll at -2." I'd want the answer to be, "Wow! OK, how the heck are you going to do that? It's not afraid of guns, remember?"

    And everyone knows that you'd better come up with some kind of awesome plan. Because... what's a 30-ft tall bulletproof dragon afraid of?

    If the dragon can fly, or burrow underground, or breath fire (or whatever), all those things are going to change the way you approach it, and how scary and fearsome it is. For example, you wouldn't want to face a fire-breathing dragon in a long train tunnel.

    \nBack in the day I had a party of high levels who were feeling pretty smug about being able to statistically kick the crap out of just about anything, including dragons...so I learned 'em.

    First I learned 'em with some long narrow paths climbing up the mountain...you know, the kind where you're a sitting duck exposed to the elements with no cover facing a flying creature who knows a bunch of spells...yeah, that was a good lesson.

    The finishing touch was when the polymorph capable dragon transformed into a canary and flew down the long narrow tunnel that was the only entrace to its lair. You know the kind. The kind where everyone has to proceed to crawl on their hands and knees and the guy with 18 str and con and a full suit of plate...simply can't fit. Yeah, that kind. Not the best place to get caught when the dragon transforms back into dragon and puffs 70+ HPs of fiery breath damage down it. Save to take 1/2 damage? I think not.

    There's lots of ways to make dragons scary. And the fact that AW moves feed off of fictional reality should make that pretty easy to engage in fuckery.
