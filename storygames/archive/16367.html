    \n Hmm, pretty challenging. Would be easier to start with an empty chalkboard, so as to build up the ghost's style. Let's see what we've got:

    The ghost tries to give her a suspicious parcel she'll presumably refuse. The ghost starts leaving the parcel for her in obvious places. Then it starts appearing alongside anything she receives from other people - fast food plates, courier shipping, so on. The car keys she receives from her lover are given two-handed, the parcel out-tretched in the other hand. The parcel continuously drops from her home's mail hatch. Throwing the parcel away will obviously just cause it to come back, while putting it safely away will cause the same - only keeping it in sight keeps it down. Within the parcel is the tome she's been reading.

    The cop friend's skin starts peeling slowly. Later on it's easy to see tufts of different-coloured hair poking out of his skalp, for example. It's the same colour as the ghost's. She has a dream where her own fingernails start to drop out.

    She's just spent a day reading the book with nothing untoward happening. She's made good progress. That night, the GM rolls 50/50 for whether she wakes up to realize that it was just a dream. (This bit only works if you do explicit ironic game mechanics, of course.)

    The ghost convinces her that it's the ghost of her own child, or her own ghost from the future, or something along those lines. It tells her things only she knows. It asks for her help in avoiding a great disaster. It totally guilt-trips her for refusing to listen even after some impressive proof.

    In the morning hours after a failed night of haunting a bit of poltergeist activity starts to happen. The ghost is evidently frustrated due to its failures. After throwing things around for a bit it apparently calms down, focusing on a single needle taken from her sewing supplies. The needle floats two inches in front of her left eye for the rest of the day, wherever she goes.

    As a last resort, she or perhaps the cop friend fumbles with the book and drops it. Afterwards the letters are all mirrored, forcing her to read with a mirror. Nothing suspicious appears in the mirror, not even in low-light conditions or candlelight, or when she's reading with her back to the window or doorway. Be sure to reassure her of this regularly.

    Ah, that was fun. Perhaps I'll do more later. Do you have any hints about the ghost's style or theme?

    \n For one, I'd say steer far away from the plotline laid out in the adventure. Maybe have her wake up to a sunny day, where everything is cheery and warm and not-at-all crazy, with everything that had happened seeming like a terrible dream. Her cop friend is back on the force, working hard to keep the city safe.

    Once she's gotten used to the "everything's safe" bit (yeah, the player would be suspicious, but if she's playing in-character, it may work), start to introduce stuff that's strange, bit by bit.

    The neighbor's dog gets loose, and is found half-burrowed into the character's back yard, gnawing at its leg for no reason.

    Every time she succeeds in a skill check, have a crow fall dead from the clear blue sky.

    The pipes in her house start to rattle, with occassional discharges of severly rusty water.

    One of the light switches won't turn on when she tries to turn it on, but does when someone else does.

    She has an increasingly hard time reading books or the newspaper, as if dyslexic.

    Just start building these things up, cranking up the shadowy creep factor, until she's doing everything she can to break free from the nightmare she's been stuck in for the last five hours of actual character time. Break out the sanity checks and mythos lore to get free.

    Or something like that.
