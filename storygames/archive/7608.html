\n Today I went to a local game day event that runs once a month. It was my first time there. There was about 40+ people that largely play mini's and D&D. Their concept of a story game is a D&D session that with little to no combat. So, I brought 3:16. Having read the book once I figured that I could run it, but then I go and leave the book at home.

Anyway, after explaining that there wasn't any xp and only to stats we started. In about 3 to 4 hours we burned through 6 planets. I didn't know how to run the game. I kept forgetting the simplest details, like whether dominance needs to be rolled before each round in combat or just once. Throughout the game I kept saying, "Oh yeah, there's this rule..." In the end, they had a blast, 3 of the players asked me where I can get game.

So, my questions

When do you go up in rank?
Does the character with the highest kills always go up a rank?
If this is the case, then why is it hard to become a brigadier?
I read somewhere that it's next to impossible to reach brigadier. That you need to save up your strengths and wait for the right time to kill a superior officer. But, if someone is going to go up a rank no matter what, well, how is it next to impossible to reach brigadier?

Thanks alex

    \n Whoever has the most kills goes up a level, not a rank. Additionally, whoever rolls highest on a d10 at the end of a planet also goes up a level.

    You only go up in rank when you (a) use a Strength at some point during the planet, and (b) use your development roll after the planet to get a promotion (which is a straight NFA roll).

    Given that you only have so many strengths to use, I think you need to make a successful development roll each time you do so to get to Brigadier (making five rolls under 8 has a 32% chance). This also requires you to never be targetted by the lowest-ranking player's Force Weakness, which opens you up for a demotion after the planet is over.
