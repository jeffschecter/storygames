\n Divide the stats 3, 3, 1d10 and 1d10 among the four elements.

You are allowed to do this in any order: rolling first then assigning, or decide that the highest goes here, second highest here, third highest here and fourth highest there then rolling, or deciding that one 3 goes here, the other 3 goes here, and then roll randomly the two others.
Depending on what you need in the sitch.

I'd rather do this at prep time than at the table (also it mars the diceless nature of Everway) but in a pinch\u2665

For example you are making orc expies. You see them as soulful warriors and want them to have high water & high fire. You roll a 1 and a 2. You put the 3s in fire and water and the 1 in earth and the 2 in air. They are a weak kind of being. Or you roll a 5 and a 6. You put the 6 in water, the 5 in fire, and the threes in earth and air. They are a formidable kind of being.

You don't need to do this for EVERY monster/npc, if a certain number makes sense to you when you create the monster go for it. But this is one thing that kept tripping me up when running Everway, how to stat the enemies properly.

The fact that you're just as likely to get a 10 as get a 1 appeals to me somehow T_T \u2665
