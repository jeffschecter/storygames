    John Nephew talks about RPG pricing -- this thread shows a lot of information about what the "industry" looks like from the inside as of 3ish years ago. Lots of good information, and watch how fucked up a lot of the stuff is.

    Emily Cuts Up the RPG Pie and we talk about different ways and means of playing characters, and why people look to storytelling vs roleplaying games. Also followed up by this post and this one. Actually, most of Fair Games is pretty much required reading, in my view.

    Creating Theme in which Vincent makes the post that finally makes Egri go from "sure that works for a play" to "oh, that does work in an RPG"

    Vincent and Source Material -- in this one I like what Vincent says, but I also like several of the objections to what he says. A good thread for putting different modes of play into stark contrast.

    Then there is this whole series about sex and gender and Heroquest and RPGs in general that I found fascinating. Also they contain some great examples of kicker and bang based play: one, two, three

    February 2006 edited February 2006
    This is a great idea, Judd! I'm gonna have to think of some .. Mine are going to end up being mostly among ones I started or participated in. Let's see ..

    In no particular order ..

    1. The Art Deco Melodrama Threads.

    Explains how you walk away from group chargen (for Sorcerer) and return with a Back Story that drives investing play, without railroading.

    2. Vincent's Theory: Hardcore.

    He sets the standard for system support of telling stories and reckoning with character death.

    3. Writing myself into the Back Story.

    In which I finally figure out how to acceptably submit input for prepared scenarios, as a player, leading to real impact, without the GM having to jettison his prep.

    4. Clarified Combat Round Procedure for TROS.

    Some things you have to say your way before you really understand them.

    5. [BW] The Racial Campaign - IV of IV.

    Such denouement with that half-orc character. What a golden moment for my group.

    6. Sitting on my hands, observing the meltdown of a homebrew playtest.

    A pure epiphany of role-player type behavior. While not diluting the reality of agendas for play and the value of system support, this experience made the case to me that communication style must also be reckoned with.

    7. DitV: a Big GM Advice.

    This is gold. You can use it anywhere.
