\n I've recently started playing board games with some new people on a regular basis, and a couple weeks ago we started a Battlestations campaign. Normally, I only do RPGs with my two best friends, and while this is great in some circumstances, there are some games that just don't work that well with only 2 players, and I'm always interested in introducing new people to new games.

Only one of these people is currently doing any gaming of the RPG variety, and he's playing in a D&D 3.5 game. I think the other 3 have all played an RPG at one point or another, but not for several years. They all seem to be pretty willing to give something a shot, however.

So: any suggestions on what I should try with them?
I've been thinking of something slightly more "traditional" and possibly a bit competitive.
I thought first I might try something that's easy to get started quickly, possibly Agon or In a wicked age, maybe 3:16.
If they're interested in something more involved, I was thinking of maybe Houses of the Blooded, REIGN, or Weapons of the Gods Once I get my hard copy of Mouse Guard I'll probably want to try it out too, though I'm not sure whether any of these guys would be into playing a game about mice.

    \n Mouse Guard would be a terrific "welcome back to gaming" game. Emphasize the realistic-low-fantasy-due-to-scale angle, not the anthropomorphic angle.

    Reign's not bad, but not as light/easy as it looks. There's also quite a lot of trad-style GM prep involved.

    3:16 is terrific beer-and-pretzels, but provides very, very little to grab onto for an inexperienced gamer. I had a similar issue among some players with Lacuna.

    On that note, InSpectres is a tried-and-true intro game. We've used it here to jump in new players.
