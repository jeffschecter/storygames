\n Gave it two read-throughs and it looks like it's going to be a good time.

The thing that jumped out at me, though, was a tiny leeetle sidebar under the Cluster creation rules, about using the system to generate connected maps of, well, whatever. And that those whatevers could easily be defined by any three sets of nine-step scales. Simple, clear, kind of a genius idea.

It also clarified to me that the Technology, Resources and Environment values are where the "hard" bit of the hard sci-fi experience will emerge (!) from. It's also got me thinking about other trifectas of values, and what sorts of play might emerge from them.

    \n Hey Ken!

    Yeah, okay. What I mean is that the Big Picture stuff in the game is going to arise from disparities in technological levels, available resources, and environment. Since tech, resources and environment are how any given planet is defined, that to my mind prompts questions along those lines: what impact does that T3 planet in this cluster have on the prevailing T0 (or less!) systems around it? What's it like to operate on a planet on which you need a pressure suit and radiation shielding? And so on.

    You would probably end up with a very different kind of SF game if the defining qualities of planets were, say, Humanness (H4 = fully standard, platonic ideal of Earth-standard society; H0 = an integrated world comprised of a mix of human and nonhuman cultures; H-4 = incomprehensibly alien), Authority (A4 = every aspect of the planet is ruled with an iron fist; A0 = representative democracy; A-4 = total fucking anarchy), and Fecundity (F4 = planet is bursting at the seams; F0 = stable population and resource demands; F-4 = society in steep decline). I mean, those are totally lame and just off the top of my head, but you can imagine that that SF game would look a lot different.

    \n Paul, regarding your "first on the scene" observation, let me hit it tangentially with a discussion about our design process.
    \n
    \nWe played SotC and thought it was awesome. However, we often play a game by reading it through a couple times and then playing it with as few references back to the text as possible during play. This means we generate a ton of drift by playing what we remember and what seems cool (and therefore seems likely to be in a well-crafted game we remember) and not what's literally there.
    \n
    \nDiaspora encodes this. And so if a SotC mechanism doesn't exist in Diaspora, it's probably because we never used it and that's all. So "design process" needs scarequotes, obviously.
    \n
    \nI would get the same (similar I guess) result, however, one of two three ways:
    \n* an Apex declaration (for advanatage) -- "I have Nav 5 and here's a fate point. Fact is, we get into the system first." That might make the ref think it'd be fun to see "by how much" and roll Nav against the time track with some mutually agreeable baseline.
    \n* a requested compel (for disadvantage) -- "Well I am "an impetuous kid". [holding out a fate point] I run way ahead of the gang, with them all yelling for me to hold up, slow down, be careful, and arrive on the scene way before my friends do. Is it dangerous?"
    \n* a stunt. Military Grade Culture/Tech: on my familiar systems I always know what's going down when it's big, political, and dangerous. I am always first on the scene, before police, armed forces, politicians, and journalists.

    \n So my current favorite bit of Diaspora is the fact it provides an excuse for an afternoon of lonely fun. Here's my writeup of the mysterious T3 ship flown about by political-prisoner-turned-archaeologist-turned-political-refugee Isaac. The rest of the cluster peaks out @ T2, but mostly it's a very low-tech setting. I'll post up the cluster when I have time for another lonely-fun afternoon:

    * * * * *

    The ship\u2019s name is comprised of layered metadata expressed as shifting, three-dimensional iconography (standard for how other ancient data found on Jericho is expressed) so it\u2019s virtually impossible to provide an accurate translation. The current best candidate is \u201cTemple of the Five Winds.\u201d Context for \u201ctemple\u201d and \u201cwind\u201d is purely speculative at this point. The value \u201c5\u201d might also mean \u201cfifth\u201d, as in the fifth item of a larger as-yet-unknown set. If this ship\u2019s name follows nomenclature found elsewhere on and near Jericho, its name probably acquires context from other ships in its fleet/family/clan/brand, its home base/system/cr\xe8che/factory, perhaps even prior owners/pilots/shamans/wranglers. As you can see, nailing down Temple\u2019s provenance means wrapping one\u2019s head around a lot of unknown, maybe even unknowable, factors.

    Other than a hardpoint mount for what one would assume to be a sizeable energy weapon (mounted in direct parallel to the main thrust vector, lots of power hookups indicating many, many terawatts being projected forward, and several suggestive hooks buried in the ship\u2019s O/S \u2013 that is to say, what of the O/S has been translated so far \u2013 related to target(1) acquisition and what is probably the math leading up to a firing solution.), Temple (?) of the Five (?) Winds (?) does not appear to have been intended as a military vessel. However, it is trivially operated by a very small crew, thanks to a very, very clever set of interlocking expert systems. The entirety of the ship\u2019s original mission and capabilities are yet to be explored.

    (1)\tThe ancient word for \u201ctarget\u201d shares etymological traits with \u201cgoal,\u201d \u201cacolyte,\u201d and a verb that means \u201cto project enlightenment upon an unwilling student, and by doing so awakening that student\u2019s thirst for further enlightenment\u201d

    V-shift 5
    Beam 0
    Torpedo 0
    EW 5
    Trade 0

    Frame OOO OO
    Data OOO OOO
    Heat OOO

    T3 Slipdrive: Can traverse slipstreams
    Firewall: Base 2 defense against EW
    Skeleton Crew

    Aspects:
    Not fully operational
    The most wanted ship in the Cluster
    That probably isn\u2019t essential
    Diabolical alien O/S
    Ancient parts are hard to come by

    * * * * *

    In retrospect, this mostly smacks of T4 weirdness but it was still fun to doodle up.
