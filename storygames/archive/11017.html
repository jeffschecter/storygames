\n hey all,

For those who have checked out episode 79 of our lil RPG podcast (the Canon Puncture Show), you already know what a Game Advocate episode is. In case you don't know, we are looking for audio interviewees who consider themselves experts on or advocates for a particular RPG. I will interview you over Skype audio for about 20-40 minutes about said RPG and you get to share your thoughts about the game, where it came from, why you love it, any play advice and you can even share some war stories.

Here's what I'd like from a Game Advocate:
* the game has been published
* you have played it numerous times, with different groups
* you didn't design the game
* you can talk about the game without resorting to theory discussion
* (EDIT) you have a Skype account, internet access and a decent microphone

If you're interested, shoot me an email at orklord at gmail and let me know. We'll work out the details from there.

Thanks for your time!
Rich Rogers
Canon Puncture Show
www.canonpuncture.com

There's a case for "The Dunwich Horror", I guess, as a problem that gets more or less squared away, but in that case, it's done with secrets and magic. On the whole, nothing gets solved, no one is happy, and the problem will be back.

      hey all,

In case you don't know, we are looking for interviewees who consider themselves experts on or advocates for a particular RPG. I will interview you over Google+ Hangout or Skype audio for about 20-40 minutes about said RPG and you get to share your thoughts about the game, where it came from, why you love it, any play advice and you can even share some war stories.

      * you didn't design the game
