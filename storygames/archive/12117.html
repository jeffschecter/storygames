    Posted By: DenysThe show's structure is so concrete, it's clear that a game session would have to mirror that.
    \nSo I watch a lot of Law & Order (more the spin-offs than the original). It's what I do late at night when I can't sleep. I've been taking notes with just this idea in mind. The realization I came into is that doing it as an RPG is hard because personal vision of character would constantly be at war with fidelity to format. The Law & Order writers do a lot of interesting smoke and mirror stuff to cover this. Like how many times a character will be "suspended" right at the end of a season with understanding that the suspension ends right at the start of the next season. It's way to have character decisions LOOK like they have consequences without having to actually deal with the consequences so the format doesn't get broken.

    One VERY interesting idea Christopher Kubasik came up with was to basically have the GM play the cops and the players take on the roles of the suspects from week to week. The behavior of the suspects is often whats actually in doubt over the course of an episode.

    \n It'd probably be 6"x9". It'd have a black & white cover, perhaps with some spot color (blue & red, in the logo).
    There wouldn't be any artwork, per se, but probably some black & white "crime procedural" photos throughout.

    It'd have a very utilitarian body text, but bold, impacting headers. Something blocky & heavy.

    The text would include several flow-charts, especially in the Game Procedures chapter. All text would be in boxes (with borders in black, red and blue), which would have hard corners (no sissy rounding off for these bad boys).

    The first appendix would be a photo gallery of faces - all as expressionless as possible. Many would look creepy, some would look plain, some would look skittish. There would be a couple cops, a couple thugs, but mostly everyday people.

    The second appendix would involve lots of lists - locations, alibis, occupations, legal loopholes, etc.

    The back cover would involve some punchy, short sentences... appearing in a big bold font.

    \n There should be a sound chip, like in those annoying musical greeting cards, between each main chapter in the book.

    So you'd read along through the character creation section, and then turn the page to get to the rules section, and then suddenly....

    *DHUN DHUN!* (or however you spell "the Law & Order sound")

    ...there's the rules section.


    Also, there should be a chart you can roll on to determine:
    * who the two random New Yorkers passing by the crime scene are,
    * what they are doing, and
    * what they are talking about when they discover the corpse o' the week.

    Posted By: JesseOne VERY interesting idea Christopher Kubasik came up with was to basically have the GM play the cops and the players take on the roles of the suspects from week to week. The behavior of the suspects is often whats actually in doubt over the course of an episode.
    \nI worked on a World of Darkness LARP based on the adventures of police officers, in which each session, half the group would play officers, half would play suspects and witnesses. Then they'd switch in the next session, always going back to their police characters. The main location for the LARP would be "the station" and we would have supplemental locations that were crime scenes and witness homes and so on. The cool part is that you wouldn't even need to travel to the LARP to be in the LARP, the "cops" would come to you. It broke down on the large number of locations needed - apparently a lot of gamers won't volunteer their living spaces (or the living spaces are horrible) even for a few minutes for an in-character conversation.
