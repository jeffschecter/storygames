Contest: Is it ethical?

\n
\n
\n
\n\nRafael \n\n\n
\n
\n\nDecember 2007 \n edited December 2007 in Directed Promotion
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n I've got some cool prizes that I'd like to give away, so I've been brainstorming ways to give them away. A contest seems like a good idea, and it also generates buzz for my game.

I thought about a contest in which anyone who writes a review of Dread: Pandemonium, or who posts an Actual Play somewhere, gets their name in the hat, and then I pick out a few names later in 2008 and give away my prizes that way. I've got several review copies out there that people haven't written reviews of yet, so this might be incentive to get them finished.

But is that ethical? I feel like I might be paying for reviews. Then again, I'm happy to give someone a prize for writing a review that's unfavorable -- an honest review is an honest review. And, after all, I've given people free copies of my book in exchange for reviews, and again, the reviews don't have to be glowing.

What's your gut reaction? Is a contest like this a bad idea? Or is it fair play?

    \n FWIW, I'd lean against involving reviews, just because you can conceivably see how it would effect a person's choice of what to review (or how to review it). I'd say: stick to AP Reports (so you're incenting writing about your sessions), and don't require that they be posted to some public fora (though that's an option). Let people post it to your own blog, or submit it to somewhere else (like a wordpress blog you keep for the purposes of this contest & for linking to folks' AP reports).

    I wouldn't be all negative if you were incenting reviews, but it's better not even get the aspersions cast on your good name.
