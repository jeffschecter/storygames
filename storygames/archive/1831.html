[REIGN] Tinkering with a variant cover. Critique, please?

DanielSolis
November 2006 edited November 2006 in Directed Promotion
So, Greg's going with variant covers so people can choose their own when they order REIGN from POD.

In case you haven't heard about it yet, REIGN is a fantasy adaptation of the One Roll Engine used in GODLIKE, Wild Talents and Nemesis. The premise is that you begin playing from the point where most D&D campaigns end, with you being the ruler of a nation, cult, or group of people. It's not a setting-specific game, but he does include an extensively described world as an example of how to adapt the system to a world of your own creation.

I'm tinkering with one of the variant covers now. Rather than go with generic "old white dude on a throne" imagery, I thought it would be cool to see another take on the idea of "reigning." Greg found this photo and I immediately thought it was awesome. While you wouldn't know it at first, the image directly references the example setting Greg creates in the book. In it, there is an African-influenced civilization that's one of the world's superpowers (in the Cold War sense). There is also a school of magic where practitioners' eyes turn green and grow bits of bark and leaves on their skin once they become attuned to that particular style.

This is the cover as it stands now:


I've shown it to a few people already. Most comments seem to be that the gold bits are competing with the colors of the photograph and that either one or the other should be on the cover, but not both. I've also heard some comments about it being too White Wolfy, but I'm not so concerned about that. My primary goal is to communicate three prominent differences of this game to other RPGs:

1) The African analogues are the ones in power.
2) Magic physically changes humans into something that we'd normally consider a fantasy creature.
3) There is a distinctly non-European influence in the setting and the interior layout.

Those are the goals I'm concerned about primarily. If there's anything getting in the way of communicating that, please tell me so I can revise it. Any other comments are welcome, of course.
