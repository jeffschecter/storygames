    \n\nApril 2010 \n edited April 2010
    \n

    \n
    \n
    \n My proud moments:

    - 1987: my first campaign, in my own homebrew Auroma, long and dreadful, but still my first!

    - 1989: the five of us laughing our heads off in the first session of my game Muu.

    - 1989: realizing that rpgs are a way of expressing yourself/life/the world, and that I had made a game to show it (Muu).

    - 1989-91: winning the "Best GM"-award three years in a row at the Arcon-convention, getting the full 100 points from my players each time.

    - 1990-91: doing the Impro-series of five games with a friend of mine, exploring new ways of doing rpgs. Way ahead of our time!

    - 1991: playing my game Pervo on a bus to a convention, and seeing conventional players going totally bonkers.

    - 1995-2005: playing payed rpgs with kids, and stuffing my week with game-sessions, due to it being so popular.

    - 1999: my game Fabula; the first professionally published Norwegian rpg.

    - 2000-05: running a lot of different scenarios with my game Fabula on various conventions, getting a lot of enthusiastic feedback, exploring and expanding what a classical rpg may do to the players.

    - 2004: taking on the challenge of creating realistic, hearthwrenching combat in a classical rpg, with Fabula, and succeeding in it. To quote a player from the Arcon-convention (where the three scenarios ran first time): "Best ever rpg I have played, and absolutely horrible!" Another player: "I had no idea you could do this in a role-playing game ..."

    - 2005: Having the mother of a 15 year old boy recovering from cancer, declaring that my weekly game sessions saved his life. Not true, I believe, but still a great compliment. And it made me proud to have such a place in her heart.

    - 2008: making the game Livets h\xf8st (Autumn of Life; a game of life, friendship and death), and having such a simple game touch the players in such a profound way. My proudest achievement to this day. Not yet published.

    - 2009: Ending 11 years of gaming with nine youths from Oslo, they having played with me from the age of 9 to the age of 19. After the last weekend one of them shook my hand, and told me I had been his "second father". Made my eyes water.

    - 1986-2010: GMd more than 2500 game sessions. Approximately 70 campaigns. Longest campaign: 262 sessions.
