\n So we've had a few people express interest in bringing their kids to Go Play NW. Does anyone have any experiences or advice to share about fostering some family-friendly gaming? Presuming we get enough attendees, we should be able to designate one room "family-friendly" and designate one room open for all types of gaming. What else should we do?

Also, is anyone coming who's interested in running something for the younger set?

Go Play NW is a laid-back gaming convention to be held in Seattle June 23-24. You can find out more, help us plan, and register at the Go Play NW Web site.

    \n I would be happy to run such a game (as long as it's understood that it doesn't constitute a babysitting service where kids can be dropped off and forgotten.) I've long been interested in introducing kids to RPGs; in fact, I have a Web page on the topic. Since my games of choice were all very simple systems, I could pick any of them. I'm thinking Cat, Zorcerer of Zo, or InSpectres, open to all ages but giving preference to kids. What do you think?
