\n In this thread about his game Awkward, @Rafu talks about a 'hot seat', where the PC(s) would be subjected to some uncomfortable truths, or in some other way made to feel uncomfortable for mechanical reasons to do with the game (not GM sadism), which I\u2019m inclined to think has probably made appearances in other storygames but hasn\u2019t been talked about expressly. So let\u2019s do so here. Have you experienced a \u2018hot seat\u2019 situation in a game, either directly or vicariously (i.e. as a bystander when it was happening to somebody else at the table)? Are you designing a game with this kind of element in it? Do you, on the whole, either like it or dislike it?

    \n I had a quick read-through; in fact, I've started editing it, with a view to possibly running a playtest. I did see the various warnings scattered through the first few sections, but IMO these should be combined in a generalised warning notice in a 'Read me first' section at the beginning of the whole document. However, when I've finished reading it I'll have more of an idea of what kinds of warnings are needed. The main flashpoint I foresee is the bit where the players seem to be enjoined to try to 'arouse the other players'. Could be misreading that, but if I'm reading it correctly that could be a problem. Anyway, as I say, let me finish reading it, and I'll come back to you with any further comments.
