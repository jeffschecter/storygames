    Posted By: fnord3125And Buffy? Eh.
    \nOk, the Buffy thing was kind of a joke, but kind of not. The library in Buffy was thematically chosen. In the eyes of the series creator the library is, in the eyes of a typical teenager, the source of all evil. Meaning, the first season climax has the gateway to Hell located in that very same library they've been spending all of their time in all season.

    Which was my point. I think if I was going to have a key location, I would thematically relate it to what's going on. Like, if I view the library as our connection to our past (because, you know, books are written in the past and allow us to carry information forward in a way that we can't do verbally), then something like a ghost story might be strongly thematically linked (because a ghost is something from the past carrying forward). If I feel that a library is somewhere we do research, then a mystery might be thematically linked.

    Now, for me there's nothing about a library that screams to me plotting amongst the staff and whatnot, but I'm guessing that's only because I interact with libraries exclusively in a go in, return books, get new books fashion, if I worked there it might be different.

    \n I can see a library game - assuming you can get (legal) access to the library after-hours - set up as a kind-of LARP with mystery-solving ARGy elements. There is a situation which require the acquisition of certain knowledge by certain individuals, who all attempt to acquire the knowledge prior to the others, but they are forced to work together (due to possessing different skills or prior clues) to find it. Lots of mysteries in the vein of Da Vinci Code (just, not incoherent shit), all scattered throughout the hundreds of thousands of books. Toss in some triggered events like one of the PCs getting possessed or someone attempting to burn an instrumental book, but the core suspense should come from the players hiding their true intentions from the others, providing false interpretations and leads, and so on.

    Posted By: chearnsNow, for me there's nothing about a library that screams to me plotting amongst the staff and whatnot, but I'm guessing that's only because I interact with libraries exclusively in a go in, return books, get new books fashion, if I worked there it might be different.
    \nWell I've only worked at my library as an 8 hr/week student worker for two semesters and now as a full-time temporary summer worker. But to the best of my knowledge there is no vicious, underhanded, scheming, politicking, or backstabbing going on. Everyone I know who works there is super nice. This is actually the reason I thought that would be cool. I'm guessing most people don't normally equate librarians or library administrators with Machiavellian power politics, and I think that would make it stand out more. Plus if the library is some kind of magical nexus or inter-dimensional thingee-ma-bob it might be different.

    Thematically, for me, libraries are places with lots of books. Books are things with lots of words. Words are a source of power. Libraries have LOTS of words in them. All those words concentrated in one location... strange things will happen.

    And those are some weird/awesome ideas for games in an actual library. I don't know if I'd want to actually try it, but... pretty damn rad anyway.
