\n The thread title's something of a joke. I just did a quick tally. There are 95 characters illustrated in the Player's Handbook. Of those, 14 are dragon blooded and three are unidentifiable (wearing helmets or facing away). Of those remaining, 52 are obviously white, thirteen are tieflings who range between white and purple, but look "white" to me, ten have indistinct ethnicities, and three are obviously non-white.

It's kind of weird peering at a picture and trying to figure out the ethnicity depicted, so I'm not making any absolute claims. My figures are based on my own perception, which is not exactly a fair metric. It seems to me that by most standards, this is a pretty whitewashed presentation of characters. 3e. wasn't exactly perfect, but I thought Wizards would do better than this.

Don't get me started on the sexualised pictures of women, either.

      The reason that European fantasy worlds are the standard is simple. The primary market for RPGs (as it exists today) is white, male and American. This means a Europeanesque fantasy world has a number of advantages.

      First is accessability, Americans are far more familiar with pseudo-Europe than pseudo-Kampuchea so they will have to learn less about the culture to access play. Note that accessing play is the key goal of an RPG. Gamers want to play. The more cannon they have to learn about a setting, the greater barrier to entry. If your RPG is going to double as a text for Asian Studies 308: Khmer History and Culture, you're proposing a very steep barrier to entry, and consequent low sales. While a hobby publisher could do a short run and not mind losing a little bit of cash, a business like WotC can't accept making losses for an ideological stand. Case in Point: Al-Quadim was the worst selling campaign setting produced by TSR. They lost more money on it than Planescape, and they sold Planescape at a loss (cost more to produce than the sale price.)

      Second is identification. Many gamers--far from all, nor even most, but a sizable minority at least--want to play a character that is like an idealized vision of themselves. That primarially white audience means that designers that want to sell to this group need to have Honkey as an option. There are soime non-white individuals who are in this group, and most fantasy RPGs have a blurb about how any skin tone is a valid choice to placate them.

      L5R is not about actual Samurai, but about a western fantasy about what Samurai were like. It's nowhere near the reality of Feudal Japanese history, but it is accessable to American audiences. We Americans have a passing, katana-fettishy familiarity with Japanese culture. We have virtually no familiarity with Maori or Khmer culture, and asking players to learn in-depth about a culture prior to play will not succeed.

      Oh, and American cultural assumptions are standard because America makes up more than 2/3 of the english-speaking market. There are 15x more Americans than Australians. Bigger audience, bigger sales.

      The peoples of the northern continent are all fairly similar to the Tsoly\xe1ni \u2014 the Livy\xe1ni are taller and more slender, the Y\xe1n Kory\xe1ni tend to bow-leggedness and the Salarvy\xe1ni are shorter with sallow complexions and copious body hair \u2014 with the exception of the N\u2019l\xfcss, who are about 30cm taller than the average Tsoly\xe1ni, and considerably more muscular. They are often employed as mercenaries and bodyguards (and playing a N\u2019l\xfcss means that you will be treated as a \u2018barbarian,\u2019 even if you are strong).

      All Human Tekumely\xe1ni evince some degree of \u2018racism\u2019 (xenophobia and a sense of superiority to the citizens of cultures other than their own).

      The traditional \u2018races\u2019 (Elves, Dwarves, Vampires etc.) are absent, though other intelligent beings exist (and many can be played) \u2014 e.g., the P\xe9 Ch\xf3i, insectoid creatures, the Sh\xe9n, reptilian warriors, the Tinal\xedya, clever little beings with the flaw of being totally literal-minded \u2014 but, while they are referred to as \u2018races\u2019 these beings are in fact different \u2018species [2].\u2019

      And this is the problem with the term \u2018race\u2019 in RPGs. The Macquarie Dictionary (the Australian version of the OED lists twelve definitions of the word, the first five of which make up the general modern understanding of the word (\u201ca sub-group of humanity that shows similar physical characteristics, thought to be inherited from a common ancestor\u201d). The sixth adds another commonly-held dimension \u2014 a shared culture (\u201cWe Greeks are a race of philosophers,\u201d etc etc \u2014 add your own stereotype). Yet \u2018race\u2019 also refers to humanity itself (Definition 7). At Definition 8, we find that in zoology, it can refer to a \u2018variety\u2019 within a species \u2014 a notion that is carried to extremes is such phrases as \u201csome male firefighters still regarded women as a race apart.\u201d Finally at Definition 9, we get to the RPG meaning of \u2018race\u2019 as \u201ca natural kind of living creature\u201d (\u201cthe race of birds/fish\u201d etc) \u2014 does this mean that the Uruk-hai pulled from the mud in the movie are not a \u2018race\u2019 because they are not produced naturally? [3]

      So, if one wanted to be simplistically literal, one could say that there is plenty of \u2018racial diversity\u2019 in RPGs (Human, Elves, Dwarves etc). However this is disingenuous \u2014 what most people who raise this concern want to see in their games is a greater proportion of non-North-western-European faces and bodies. This I applaud \u2014 I would also like to see a greater number of \u2018non-de-protagonized\u2019 women dressed appropriately, a greater variety of body-types and sexualities (T\xe9kumel has it all over most RPGs in this respect \u2014 \u2018alternative\u2019 sexualities are well-tolerated), religions that are not clones of/patterned on those that have existed in \u2018the West\u2019 (or China/Japan), different, even totally \u2018alien,\u2019 customs, clothing, architecture, militaria, legal systems etc.

      Mind you \u2014 I won\u2019t expect everyone to get along, even if they are all of the same \u2018race\u2019 \u2026 certainly, as Graham has pointed out somewhere, the inhabitants of the UK often don\u2019t!
