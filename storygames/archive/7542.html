    \n Basically, as I see it, you've got two interpretations: one where E-dead is for-reals-broken-and-will-never-get-better, and one where E-dead is "loses that fight, but gets better afterwards".

    Now, in the latter interpretation, arguments will basically turn into "keep trading rolls until somebody is E-dead, that person loses."

    In the former interpretation, though, you hit an interesting point when you get E-Crippled. You can keep arguing, and with a little dice luck, you just might still win, but if your opponent succeeds in one more roll, your character is broken, for good. So are you going to give, or risk it all?

    Personally, I like the former interpretation. Plus, it means you can get insulted by your commanding officers so hardcore that you lose all self-confidence, quit your job, and join the Ship Maintenance Crew, which is full of bitter and broken ex-Troopers just like you.

    Posted By: LudantoI understand that aliens can use "emotional" attacks on Troopers, leaving them as useless mental wrecks. But what about when you're being berated by a superior officer. Once you get to "Dead", is that PC out of the game? Even if that wasn't the NPC's goal?

    On a related note, what about a "fist fight" between Troopers for (lowercase) dominance? If the PC wins, is the NPC dead?
    \nThe book is out there and I'm happy for people to do it how it fits their group best -- so any of the other answers might be better for you than mine.

    For me... if a character ends up at Crippled and then takes a "Kill" (whether that's physical or mental) then they are no longer a player character. They have either been mangled, shot up, disintegrated, lost their mind, had a nervous breakdown of the most severe kind or become some sort of hollow automaton. In any case, they are no longer playable.

    To avoid this, you can work out an agreeable solution in the talking at the table ("Sure, I do what he tells me to do...", or "I agree to do it but I don't do it very well...", or "I stand up to him and the whole squad backs me up too, so he backs down..."), you can use a Strength or Weakness (letting you win or lose on your terms), or you can roll dice for it, knowing that someone will get broken by the outcome, and hope to win.

    I'm wary, personally, of re-negotiating once I see what the dice have rolled, since that seems a have-your-cake-and-eat-it-too kind of thing. That's when you spend Flashbacks.

    Oh, for clarity, after an Encounter (where Threat Tokens are used) you recover from all E damage. At the end of the PC vs PC/NPC conflicts (where there are no Threat Tokens) then you also get all your E damage back.

    I hope this helps.
