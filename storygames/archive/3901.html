    \n I was in an impressionist role-playing session where the GM/initiator told us what to do at certain points, and cut to the next part when he felt like it. Scene/situation cuts were of the type "It's twenty years ago. You're the mother of the character you just played, standing in the exact same place on the beach, talking to your husband." - or "You're an old man lying in a hammock in an old house on the beach. The others here will tell you what smells come drifting past you. Tell us what the old man associates with them".

    The group was, authority-wise, structured like many RPGs - one GM who controlled scene framing etc, several players who played one character at a time. However, I wouldn't call it a game; it was more of a shared exploration of an atmosphere from many different angles.
