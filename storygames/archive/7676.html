    Nerdly rocked, and as always was a fun filled weekend of gaming, hanging, whiskey/scotch and food.

    \n

    I played:

    \n
      \n

    • An Awesome Polaris Friday night in which we all hit Veteran, and then I died tragically on the sword of Nancy's Knight trying to avenge her daughter\u2019s death that she had caused inadvertently through her Zeal.

      \n
        \n

      • "SotC" aka The Ryan Macklin Crazy Adventure Game that sometimes resembles SotC in which we were an UN Special Actions Team trying to stop the Russian Demon Tsar from getting Excalibur from Mutants in the Deep Depths of Darkest Canada (Vancouver).

        • \n

      • Maid RPG which I loved far too much and way more than I had expected too since I am not much of an Anime Fan or familiar with the whole maid thing. But the random charts made up for it and I ended up playing a Elf girl with a long sword who admired maids soooo much she had to become one, oh and we decided the Jesse's maid was my twin sister, who also was a vampire. Did I mention that the setting was Shadow Run with Horror? A definite highlight was the random event in which it was revealed that I had been a ghost the whole time. We also had carzy eldrich magics, secret diaries, bazooka wielding maids, black op teams and robo maids. Awesome, I am definitely ordering this game today

        • \n

      • Houses of the Blooded, I heart this game and it never fails to deliver for me. Had lots of fun playing a Serpent Spy Master for the Duke.

        • \n

      • the Ryan Macklin Drunken free-form UA game, nothing like making up F'ed up stories around the camp fire while consuming copious amounts of whiskey.

        • \n

      • Zombie Cinema, played 2 games of this Sunday morning the 1st one was Brazil with Zombies and the 2nd one was an attempt to do a serious game which took place during the 70's in an Office Building. The game definitely delivered the Zombie Action I love and is another game for me to order up today.

    \n There was much Rockitude.

    I played Mist-Robed Gate, A Dirty World, Chombie Cinema, and Houses of the Blooded, and ran cyberpunk/horror mashup Maid (Expect on AP on this one soon).

    From my perspective as one of the two prime organizers, it was the smoothest NBP yet. Everyone found the campsites without trouble. Food setup and cleanup happened without myself or Nancy needing to harangue anyone. There was lots of good alcohol, but an appropriately contained amount of hangover. Games fit their time slots, so we didn't have schedule creep. People were up and about for solid morning gaming. Even with investing a bit in "camp-owned" equipment, we came in comfortably under budget.
