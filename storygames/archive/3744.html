    \n Have you tried a version where the back figure was aligned with the silhouette? That is, if you pick up the actual book and the silhouette is opposite the binding on the front cover, the back cover would have the figure image opposite the binding as well, as if you're walking around the mysterious figure on the cover to discover OMG ITS A ROBOT. As images on the screen, the figure is aligned, but as a physical image where you have to flip the book, the image isn't aligned. The figure on the front cover is far from the spine of the book; the figure on the back cover is right next to the book.

    Aside from that minor composition issue, it's totally rocking. I love the cover. It's something I'd pick up off a shelf, but that's because I can see both covers. When displaying this at a booth, make sure you have two display copies: one showing the front and one showing the back. Or use a display banner poster to advertise the game, but show both front and back mockups as you've done in your first post with the silhouette and figure aligned.

    This is what I'm getting at:





    Oh, and I love the "Stories you play" tagline. The space between the word "stories" and "you" appears larger than the space between "you" and "play". It might be because of the busy background on that section, but it's probably because of the whitespace in the shape of the Y. For your final version, you might want to tighten that space to make it more visually pleasing.

    \n Thomas, that's a superb idea. I sorta tinkered with it early one, but dismissed it because I didn't want the background lettering to be reversed. But, that said, it's one of those design phases my brain goes through. I work on a design. Early on, sometimes, I'll dismiss an idea. Turns out later that with some sleep and a more focused eye, yep, that early idea was a good one and the supposedly great idea I had isn't so grand. So, I'm going to try it tonight and see if I can make it work. If not, that's ok. But, it's definitely worth a look-see.

    Oh! And I love the sketch!

    Jamey, this is absolutely an Invasion of the Body Snatchers game. I didn't realize it until later on in the design, but I've since referred to exactly that when explaining it to people. It's set in the early 50s. You could totally do a pod-people game with this.
