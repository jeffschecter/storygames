\n I'm going to be coordinating 5-15 writers, each producing 1-5 short role-playing scenarios. I'd like a tool to keep track of stuff like:
- Contact information
- How far along we are on each scenario (received idea; received synopsis; received first draft; received second draft; sent to editor etc)

Two questions:

1. Does anyone know a good tool for this? (Preferably free and online, but that's not an absolute requirement).
2. If you have any experience with similar projects, what are some things to be on the lookout for? For instance, what are typical first-time errors?

    \n 1. Get a small sample from each writer of the current work they're producing and proofread it. Have someone else you know proofread it. Depending on the type of project, they could be making silly mistakes like writing in the wrong tense.

    2. Make sure the writers are writing to spec, in terms of words allotted (if you have space constraints).

    3. Run a Google spreadsheet for tasks that have been assigned, when you're expecting them back, their current status, who's responsible for it. Make it public.

    4. Run another Google spreadsheet for persons involved in the project (maps to spreadsheet from 2), and any relevant contact information you might need. Make it public or private as needed.

    Alternately, consider a Wiki if you expect the author's fiction to be overlapping or for them to otherwise benefit from being able to see each other's work. You can get a wikidot, www.wikidot.com.

    \n Google spreadsheet for a listing of assignments, yeah. Make it so that the rest of your team can read it.

    I'm not sure if Google calendar does deadline/project management stuff, but you need an easy way to keep track of when what is due back to you. Ideally, you'll have deadlines for each stage of each writing assignment (not "send it to me and I'll send it back with revisions, and then we'll aim to be totally done by August 1st" -- that's suicide), and you'll stagger those deadlines so that you don't get giant tidal waves of submissions all at once. And, again, whatever you do, make sure those dates are accessible by the rest of your team.
