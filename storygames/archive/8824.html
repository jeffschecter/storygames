    \n The way I'd do it: When you add something substantial to the game world, write one "public" XP entry and one "hidden" XP entry.

    Display both on the chart, but use an indecipherable demon font for the hidden one. Keep a list for yourself.

    So say I add a red dragon that's terrorized the land for years... but no one knows his cave guards a lich's lair

    Player list:

    1. Slay the red dragon Flametard 20000 xp
    2. {XQTQQYAF EEO AGEYAGQW OI YEG QWFGHQWA} 5000 xp

    Your list:

    1. Slay the red dragon Flametard 20000 xp
    2. Discover the entrance to the Lichlair 5000 xp

    \nThere's different ways of handling it depending on how fancy you want to go.

    At the most basic, it's suppsed to be 13 encounters to reach a level. Doesn't get easier than that in my book.

    After that, you've got a couple of options here:Psion's blog

    And if you want there's a more complicated way of doing it (GM has a budget) here:
    Encounter Budget

    The budget thing is still easier than the default way of doing things.

    I ran an E8 game for about a year, and never calculated XP once. I went with the 13 encounters method.
