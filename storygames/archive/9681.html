\n So my buddy Sandy and I hang out, and he's all like "Man, I need a game that works like this" and I'm like "huh." I'm usually his go-to guy for system recommendations, and I drew a blank.

He wants a spy game with a focus on figuring out the missions and some kind of gradation of stakes (either stake setting or bidding or something).

As A GM he wants to spend his time cooking up missions but he wants it to be "wide open" how the PCs accomplish the mission.

He wants the players to be focused on figuring out how to accomplish the mission, and dealing with curve balls that show up mid-mission.

He wants the play to be fast (he's already gotten frustrated with d20 modern and Savage Worlds for this - when guns break out everything starts crawling along).

Can anyone point me in the right direction?

    Posted By: Saint&SinnerTo the extent everyone playing is on board. One person has an awful lot of power to change the senario if not...
    \nYeah, WoM can do any kind of spy action you want, but you definitely need everyone to be on the same page, especially during the briefing stage. You can always introduce a "No, too silly" rule giving the GM a veto on anything that deviates too much.

    Keeping the action low is also do-able. Just apply a table-rule that it costs 1 mission point to initiate violence, with the Mars special ability being an exception (since it already costs 1 MP to activate).
