\n I posted this already in "Stuff to Watch", but it probably needs its own thread - I (and "Where Are Your Keys?") will be in Boston April 14th-24th.

If you haven't heard of fluency play or "Where Are Your Keys?", in short it is a design system for generating accelerated learning games, primarily developed for revitalizing endangered languages. Its principles have been applied to all kinds of things, including story games. Joel Shempert (Dreaming Crucible) has blogged about it here and here, Jason Godesky (theFifthWorld, Storyjammers podcast) has blogged about it here, and podcasted about it here and been interviewed about it on the Jennisodes here, and I probably started it all here at the College of Mythic Cartography, applying "fluency play" to teaching/learning Polaris.

First I'll be presenting the "Where Are Your Keys?" accelerated learning design system at the Agile Games 2011 event, April 14th-16th.
http://whereareyourkeys.org
http://agilegames2011.com

Then I'll have a week free, the 17th-21st, to play story games, talk about (and do demo games!) of accelerated learning, language hunting, and story-game fluency play. I have a fluency-play hack of Archipelago II I've been working on for a while that I'd love to share.

Then I'm running three days of "Where Are Your Keys?" games, 22nd-24th, More info at http://tinyurl.com/WAYKboston. This is my first trip to Boston, and probably my last, so I'd love to connect up with anybody interested while I'm in town.

    \n If you are near Boston and can play WAYK it is totally worth your time - I was sort of skeptical and on the fence based on reading about it and watching videos and so forth, but actually playing made me a believer. I've only spent an afternoon with the techniques and it was enough to change the way I think about how I approach game design. Seriously!
