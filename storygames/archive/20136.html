    \n Ahh, the trains! I'm really glad you had fun

    I was in a run of it at Dreamation that involved the Chaos person bringing lizards to the date and I (being so committed to verisimilitude, obvs) had to pretend to not be delighted by that. It was also run (without me being there!) at a game day in NYC fairly recently, and I'm told that people had fun.

    Jackson Tegu also wrote a game called Overture that is a sort-of-prequel to Group Date, and it just came out in the latest Indie Mixtape: http://www.drivethrurpg.com/product/146942/Indie-Mixtape-Volume-3-New-Hotness

    It's about someone deciding what to do about their feelings for their neighbor (whom they can hear through the wall) and uses the same lust-optimism-empathy-doubt facets as Group Date.

    \n It's weird trying to figure out the source of fun, or perhaps to put it less subjectively, your own fun for an hour or so.

    It's funny that the most charming(interesting?) feature of play, the recombining of separate narratives to approach a singular one, was I'm assuming a kind of early surprise? We form a thing out of already made things, thinking it'll do this thing, but it turns out it does this other thing, which might be better then the thing we wanted it to do.

    The combining of separate narratives into something somewhat singular, is a huge part of traditional role-playing.

    I have more to say on this, but I just want to say that I enjoyed Group Date and that it was the highlight of my brief convention-going experience.

    I hope you continue working on games.
