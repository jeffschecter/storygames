\n I've got the first draft of Night's Black Agents, Ken Hite's GUMSHOE vampire thriller game, and we are looking for playtesters. This time, I decided to email the customers to ask for playtesters and we got 40 responses - pretty overwhelming. However, all of them know GUMSHOE, so I am also after playtesters who aren't familiar with GUMSHOE and like the sound of the background.

So, if you want to playtest please email simon@dyingearth.com.

I'd be really interested to hear your playtest experiences, both as publishers and punters, to see if we can improve the process.

    \n I playtested it as GM.

    GUMSHOE really shines in it. It makes the characters feel like competent badasses; ex-spies need to shine and do cool stuff. The sample scenario was an exercise in "how to use investigative abilities to plan and execute a job". The players had the initiative and it was as far from railroading as possible.

    The rules are clear, Hite really knows what he's talking about, and the spies vs. vampires theme is a match made in heaven. The GM section was especially impressive.

    I'm shocked that only 250 people had purchased it a week or so ago.

    \n The pre-order became available in November. Pre-order sales have been a little lower than I would have liked, but I have yet to pull the stops out on promoting the game. Pre-order sales help us immensely.

    I'll be running it myself in a couple of weeks, but it's been the most exciting read of all the GUMSHOE books we've done. Reading it really makes you want to play. Actual play and playtest feedback was excellent. I'm very keen for people to enter the conspyramid competition - it's only 200 words, and we'll publish all the entries.

    It's much more freeform in many ways than other GUMSHOE games, and it encourages players to use investigative benefit spends more actively. I am startled that there are quite so many types of vampires.

    \n We had eighty playtest volunteers, and received twenty playtest reports. In contrast, when we do adventures, we get a lower response, but a greater response rate. For new games we spread the net quite widely - each new game is a chance to test the system, variations on the system, and the way we've explained it to novice readers as well as existing Pelgrane game players. For Esoterrorists 2.0 as a result of feedback, for example, we've added sections addressing what people imagine might go wrong with GUMSHOE as well as what can in actual play.

    Night's Black Agents had a surprisingly narrow range of feedback, so there were few changes to the manuscript than, for example, Ashen Stars, where one section had to be completely rewritten as a result of polarised feedback.

    The number of playtesters is also a good guide to how succesful a game is likely to be, at least in its first year, so it's looking good for Night's Black Agents. Other games, not so much.

    We get additional feedback from our alpha customers in the pre-pre-order period which we also incorporate into the final ms.

    If there is demand, I could release an entire set of playtest reports for one book.
