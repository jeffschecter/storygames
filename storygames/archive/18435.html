    \n My read of Lovecraft is that there are a few categories of monster.

    Human cultists - Who you can shoot but there can be a lot of them of or they can assassinate you out of the crowd.

    Manageable monster - Deep Ones who can be shot. Maybe also the Elder Things in the Mountains of Madness. Reanimator things fall into this as well.

    Monsters you think you can manage till you really see them - then you're screwed. Shoggoths fit this bill. Shoggoths were servants. Guess how nasty their masters must have been?

    Monster that will scare you but won't hurt you - dream lands. You'll just lose sanity.

    Great Old Ones - You really don't want to become aware of them let alone see one but those darned books keep guiding you to them.

    \n It depends what kind of game you want. If you want to be true to Lovecraftian fiction, you have to make creatures that will be completely foreign to what is "normal". You don't necessarily have to make it something that is unbeatable or even strong. You can make it something that is weak or fragile by nature, just horrible. In the dreams at witch house, the main monster was a mouse with a human face (the witch's familiar). In another, it is mold in the basement. Still other ones have a bunch of ape/human hybrids that have less intelligence than a human and less strength than an ape. These things are not very intimidating by themselves. The familiar did manage to kill and drive the protagonist insane in the story, though. That's all that was needed. I don't find most RPG's do it well...

    The horror is sudle in most Lovecraftian stories because there has to be hope. There is no one in a story written by Lovecraft that saw Great Cthulhu and lived more than a couple weeks. Call of Cthulhu is a story about how a guy came and catigorized stories of people that had come into contact with this horrible force and died because they did. I have never felt that any game that has a Great Old One or an Outer God as opposition was Lovecraftian, but not very many people agree with a lot of opinions I have....
