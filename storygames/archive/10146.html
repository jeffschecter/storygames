[Gen Con] Confessions of a First Timer

\n
\n Just got back late last night from my first Gen Con \u2014 in fact, my first gaming convention ever. I had a blast. I'm exhausted and worn out, but it was all worth it.

I had a bit of gamer culture shock. I mean, my regular connection to the gaming community at large is forums like this, RPGNet, and a handful of blogs. And maybe it's just my personal tastes, but it seems that games like Spirit of the Century take up as much virtual shelf-space (if not more) in the collective consciousness of the intarwebs as games like CthulhuTech. But when everything is laid out in the exhibit hall, you really get a different perspective of what's what.

Not that I'm surprised. And not that Gen Con is necessarily an accurate reflection of gamer interests either.

To some people I'm sure that the booths on the indie road map looked a little sad, what with the lack of painted miniatures and LCD screens. They weren't. I had the best times of the con there. Darcy Burgess pulled my friend and I into an awesome demo of Black Cadillacs at the Forge booth which we played with Willow. (I wanted to try Willow's Escape from Tentacle City as well, but wasn't able to get back to the booth at a time when she was there.) I learned about Mist Robed Gate at the Pirate Jenny booth. I was already intrigued by the cover, but now I'm very interested in the game itself. I met lots of people at the IPR booth whose names are on some of my favorite games and where I got to run demos of my own game, Chronica Feudalis. Brennan, Matt, and the other volunteers at the IPR booth really made me feel like my book was in good hands.

I got Bill White to teach me to pronounce Gakanagok, Paul Czege personally explained to me the origin of the Chalk Outlines Principle, Gregor Hutton edified me on the rule of four.

Everyone is so damn friendly. And there's really no perceptible difference in attitude between the story-game designer with her ashcan and the lead line developer with his $50 hardcover, full-color tome. They both just want to tell you about their game. And I had a cool moment when Ross Watson, the lead developer of Dark Heresy and Rogue Trader, was telling me about the buzz he heard about my game.

There are a couple of though-experiments that keep going around in my head: First, what would the landscape of Gen Con look like if it accurately represented the interests of a site like RPGNet? Second, what would happen if you took the dirtiest, hippiest story-game and produced it like it was a high-end mainstream RPG \u2014 huge art budget, all color, hard covers, shiny?

I was really pleased to meet those of you I met and sorry to miss those of you I missed. Scott Acker, sorry we didn't get a chance to actually talk with the way our schedules overlapped. Next time.
