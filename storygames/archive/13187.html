    \n Nowadays, in the United States anyway, a hero is a victim... holocaust survivors, people in the world trade center bombing, slaves from America's past, all commonly referred to as heroes or as acting heroically.

    In the ancient past a hero was a cool guy who was good looking (normally) and good at fighting, also, he had lots of cool stuff.

    Strange how times change.

    Personally, I think heroism is when one risks their self interest for someone's else, the clearest case being risking or sacrificing your life to save others.

    \n I see two types of heroes.

    The active hero; One who chooses to act despite overwhelming odds for something they believe in.
    The passive hero; One who withstands incredible hardship and grows from it, rather then letting the hardship break them.

    The active hero is easy to model in design; the passive one? Much harder.
