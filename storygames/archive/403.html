    The Power 19 is Awesome. However, a few months back it started to feel like, I dunno, like dropping your cock on the table. It feels more like a personal "Mission Statement" for the game, and it's not written in a way that really encourages discussion about your game. Oh, I know, it's much like a marketing benchmark report (aimed towards fellow designers) for a car or computer hardware: "Here are 19 areas of consideration, and here's how our vehicle/video card/storage device handles it". That sort of thing.

    I'm wondering if I should work with Tony to maybe make another Power X, where the questions and responses are much more open to outsider feedback and could generate more interest. Cause, in most cases (even my own!), people post the Power 19 because they want to talk about their game. But the Power 19 are probably a bad tool for this, because they're all show and tell, and little on discussion-generating elements.

    Hmmm, worth some thought.

    Look at Unhallowed Metropolis, Fireborn, Weapons of the Gods, Demon: the Forsaken. I disagree.

    People confuse tools with the need to use them. To use an analogy: If you go to a gaming site, present your drawings and ask "What do you think?" you will get our opinions. If you go to a site where they teach drawing methods and tools, and ask "What do you think?" you will get the answer: "What do I think of what? The brush-strokes, the objects, the shadings?" That answer, or lack of answer that claims the question was too broad to begin with is the only unuseful thing.

    If you tell me "I like the shading, the object is interesting" and I really wanted to know what you thought of my brush-strokes, I still got something out of your response, unlike the above answer.

    You want to tell me that the way RPG.Net does reviews is bollocks because they do not address currency, GNS, Narrative-control or any of that? I'm calling bull. They're giving me their opinions, and that rocks.
    Knowing "Your game rocks because...." or "Your game could be better if...." is much better than me needling information out of you, or you not telling me what you think. The moment you view something you have opinions forming, share them, is that so much to ask?

    There is more than one method to do this, remember that most methods were created because you didn't want there to be only one. Use more than one. Stop being constrained by the context you impose on yourself: "This is community X and project subtype X:a so I will only reply to Xis questions".
