\n hey all, this is Rich. I wanted to give a quick heads up that I'm moderating two really cool panels for Indie+ (an online community for indie RPGs) tonight.

The first is called "Escaping the Legacy" and its about women and people of color making and playing fantasy RPGs and the problems that can cause. It's at 7 PM EST on Google +. Here's the blurb:
Most fantasy games are based on classic fantasy fiction written by white males which in turn are based on white european history and folklore. What happens if you love fantasy games, love making games and come from a different background? What bagage, if any, comes with the genre's history? Is the future of fantasy gaming more diverse or will orcs always be black and evil?

Host: +Richard Rogers

Panelists:
Emily Care Boss
Quinn Murphy
Stacy Dellorfano
Meguey Baker

The second is called "Is Fantasy Dead?" and I've gathered some real spitfires to talk about designing new fantasy games in a market dominated by games like D&D and Pathfinder. This is at 9 PM EST on Google+. Here's the blurb:
After forty years of Dungeons & Dragons, the recent re-release of PDFs of previous editions, Old School Renaissance, the playtest of D&DNext as well as the juggernaut that is Pathfinder, have we reached an endpoint in fantasy gaming? Has it all been done before?

Join Indie+ on #gamenight to hear from and talk with small press game designers about uncharted places in fantasy games.

Hosted by +Richard Rogers

Panelists:
John Wick
Brennan Taylor
James Raggi
David Hill

For either or both panels, follow Indie + on Google +. Also, the panels are On Air and will drop on the Indie+ YouTube channel later tonight.
