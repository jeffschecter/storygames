    \n I think Graham and Jonathan have it about right.

    In answer to Jonathan's question: you'd ask (and/or encourage) AW questions on the site if you wanted to expose AW to a larger audience, in theory. In practice, once the D&D edition wars took root, the atmosphere was going to be too toxic for the site to do well. There was this huge concern with having too many 4e questions on the site, but when you're trying to launch a new community, you need to be scrupulously careful about implying that some people aren't wanted. SE already has a strike against it insofar as it's not a discussion site and there's zero cultural context for an RPG-oriented site that's not about discussions. So you're going to piss off a certain amount of people that way no matter what you do.

    So: it's totally reasonable to want more OSR questions or more storygame questions. But the thing that gets you more users is encouraging people to ask the kinds of questions you want asked, because that's additive. Once you phrase the problem as "we have too many questions about X," you're screwed. C'est la vie.

    I am not entirely sure that it's practically possible to run an SE site about RPGs, though, because the discussion/question thing is a pretty huge cultural barrier. It was very interesting being part of the experiment, in any case.

The programming-related SE sites work because there's a certain professionalism inherent in the fact that people who post there mostly do so to support their living. (I spend a little time on SE programming sites, but not much, so maybe I'm totally wrong.)

    Anyway, my point is that as much as I cared about the site, I cared less and less as time went on because of the constant bickering. I realized that I was contributing to that bickering, if only for having a dissenting opinion, and it just wasn't worth a battle to me -- especially if RPGSE never manages to escape its perpetual Beta status. I'm pretty happy with my contributions to the site and I occasionally poke around still, but I don't consider myself an active member anymore.
