A few members of my regular group decided to get together on our off week to run a one-shot. One of the players invited her brother, a non-gamer, so I decided to run something light and straight-forward for his first gaming experience. I very nearly started to put together a paired down version of the Apocalypse World rules when I stumbled across the Ghost/Echo pdf on my hard drive (duh!).

\n

Since we don't know what type of story we'll be playing, what I ended up doing is putting together a very simple, one-page trifold of the Ghost/Echo rules with the cyber-punk color removed. I also added the Gift of Dice rule from TSoY and space to fill in a couple setting/genre specific Goals and Dangers.

\n

Mostly likely what we'll do is have a quick brainstorming session about the story's setting and characters, then fill in the custom goals and dangers, marking down any notes needed on 3x5 cards as we go.

\n

I have yet to run Ghost/Echo (though I have run it's close descendant, Apocalypse World), so I have one question: When a character commits to violence, the danger is that they "cause unintended harm". At first, the range of possible outcomes from this realized danger was pretty narrow in my imagination, until I came to the conclusion that the unintended might not be limited to other PCs, innocent bystanders or props or scenery. Would it be too much of a stretch (or to harsh) to include the PC them self or their equipment as the recipient of the unintended harm?
