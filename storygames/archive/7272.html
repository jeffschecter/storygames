\n ...and worth the wait! My pre-order copy arrived in the mail today, and at first read-through it is excellent.

Joshua refers to this as an ashcan edition, but it seems pretty polished to me. I love the layout and typography, and the text is well written and clear, with good examples. Some of the variations from the basic IaWA rules have not yet sunk in for me, yet. I will have to give it another read-through. In particular, the exact use of a particular strength that is 'numerous' are not yet clear for me. I like the default consequences -- 'debased' and 'maimed', and I like the staged structure of Wyrds: first drawing from 'The Hero Discovered', then from 'The Hero Defined', and finally, from 'The Hero Destroyed'. Each Wyrd is loaded with appropriate circumstances for that phase of an epic tale.

I can't wait to try out this variation on the Anthology Engine!

    I was reading through the rules again, and I have a couple of errata, and a couple of suggestions:

    On pages 31, 33, 35, and 36, Amek and Mekha make a surprise guest-starring appearance in the ongoing example. It's not tough to figure out if you've read IaWA, but I bet someone starting to learn the Anthology Engine from that example would be puzzled.

    That example might also be clearer if the Nicor were treated either as a PC or an NPC throughout, rather than trying to show both ways of handling the situation.

    On pages 46 & 47, the features of particular strengths are detailed. IMHO, 'numerous' should be expanded upon there. Also, some mention of how the features might be applied to particular weaknesses would be useful.

    On pages 66 & 67, in the Wyrd of the Hero Destroyed, entries for the suits of Diamonds and Hearts are virtually identical.

    And finally, a question: Will a pdf edition be available at some point? I love being able to carry an entire library full of rulebooks and reference material on a memory stick.
