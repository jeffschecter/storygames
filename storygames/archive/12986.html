\n Okay, so how do the dice in Steal Away Jordan work? I've run it once; I know the basics: Lucky 7's equal one point (2+5 or 3+4), 1's (aces) subtract a point. I know I've read or heard Julia Ellingboe saying that a cool, unintended part of the system is that the more dice you have (the Master, or GM, or bad guy, has way more dice than the PC slaves in the game) the more chances you have to fail.

I remember Jason Morningstar saying, in our game at GPNW 2009, that the dice seemed opaque to him, and I feel likewise. So what gives? I could roll and roll and roll, but I don't want to crunch the numbers like that, and I don't know how to write a program. Can anyone figure out (or help me figure out) what different amounts of dice give you, on average, in terms of points? Ideally I'd like a spread from 2 dice to 20. If anyone can show me a program that can be told to count like this (2+5 and 3+4 both equal +1, while 1 equals -1 and everything else is null), I'll do it myself.

Thanks!

    Posted By: hans chung-ottersonOkay, so how do the dice in Steal Away Jordan work? I've run it once; I know the basics: Lucky 7's equal one point (2+5 or 3+4), 1's (aces) subtract a point. I know I've read or heard Julia Ellingboe saying that a cool, unintended part of the system is that the more dice you have (the Master, or GM, or bad guy, has way more dice than the PC slaves in the game) the more chances you have to fail.
    \nI don't think that's quite accurate.

    Steve crunched the numbers once. As you get more dice, you get a slightly greater chance of winning, but not much. So rolling 20 dice is a bit of an illusion: you have more power than someone with 10 dice, but not twice as much.

    That part-illusion of power is, I think, the cool unintended part of the system.
