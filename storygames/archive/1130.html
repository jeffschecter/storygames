OK, so I keep having these opportunities to talk to people who are interested in roleplaying but are otherwise without vital clues. I usually talk at them and put a copy of PTA in their hands or something, but next time I'm going to SIT THEM DOWN AND PLAY A GAME. The goal is to get them energized and engaged without talking about what's going on - I want to embody GO PLAY as a real-life activity everybody can enjoy.

Here's what I've got. 7 characters, playable with 3 or more, everybody has a character with 4-7 things they know, the kicker is the death of the father and the kids all want the high seat he has vacated. I tried to make each character tightly interwoven, but I could use some help and suggestions for making them more adversarial and interconnected and balanced. I'd like to ramp up conflict between uncle, witch, and friend. Any suggestions are welcome! I also welcome thoughts on how to structure this to play out in 20 minutes without any resolution mechanics. Thanks, smart people!
