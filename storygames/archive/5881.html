    Posted By: Saint&SinnerTo those who played in the game, did the rules "work"?
    \nThe game felt done. I felt like the rules worked overall.\n
    Did everything click together or were there any rough spots.
    \nI think the rough spots were just standard RPG learning curve.\n
    What was play like? Did this deliver on creating the kind of exciting social manuevering situations discussed earlier in his LJ?
    \nJohn drove for that as a GM & explainer of the game, so I think it can deliver on it. I'm interested in looking at the text to see how well it enables someone other than John to help facilitate that. But the machinations between players did feel more tangible than in most other games I've played, due to various bits of mechanical weight.\n
    Were the FATE mechanics noticable or was this a "whole new beast".
    \nI would say it's not really FATE. Aspects were co-opted, yes, and in some cases I think better-utilized (or at least better-set up) than in SOTC, because he clearly makes you write down your Aspect's compel, tag & invoke. But the die mechanic isn't FATEy at all -- not a negative, mind you, because I find it interesting.

    I think the game is fun overall. There were a couple points I didn't care for, specifically in declaring facts about the world, but I know that's just my style -- and I also see why the game does those things I don't particularly care for, from an integrated mechanic perspective. Other than that, John has a huge box of interesting tools here. I'm looking forward to getting my hands on a copy.
