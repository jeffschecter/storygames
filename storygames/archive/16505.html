    \n Sorry to complicate your decision, but my experience with the two companies is the opposite of Michael's, though it has been about a year since I had anything printed. TGC seemed like a shoestring operation - looked like the GM had a day job at the company that built the infrastructure they used for their website. (And a fine website it is.) But they screwed up an order, getting only 1 out of 3 of the decks I ordered right, and then tried to blame me for the mistake.

    Whereas SuperiorPOD is already a printing company that has added a (mediocre) web front end to their business. They never made a mistake with my order, so I don't know how it would have shaken out if I'd had a problem.

    Also, a year ago anyway, TGC's cards were not high quality - they were pretty easy to crease and you could feel the nubs on the side where they punched out. SuperiorPOD's felt about on par with most professional games, though not as high quality as, say, a Bicycle deck.

    You pick out an option (I picked custom deck). It asks how many cards in the deck (I picked the standard 54 card deck).     I'm interested to see how it looks, when it will get here and how well it will hold up to use. I'll let you know the answers when I get them.

    \nPrinterstudio.com card printing review.

    I got the test deck yesterday - whew! Just in time for Origins!

    At first glance they look good. They come in a hard plastic box and look like professionally done cards. On openning the box I find that the deck is shrunk wrapped so no card wear.

    Openning up the wrap is pretty easy but did take a knife.

    The cards are like a standard deck of playing cards. The stock is not noticably thin but also not especially thick. The finish is only slightly glossy and is smooth - no linen texture here. The colors are sharp and distinct. My art work uses areas of solid color so contrast is important. I found the color vibrant and not muddy. It did not look like what comes off of an inkjet printer (I know because I had a hand made deck to compare it to). I do not know how gray tones or color photography would look but I suspect they would look fine.

    I'll be taking the deck to Origins to run games so it will get a nice work out this weekend. That will give me a good read on wear and tear. Righti now I think it will do well - it doesn't feel cheap.

    There is a down side. The really nice plastic box is top openning. It slides on and off with no locking method so once the cards are openned it could spill out. I put some stips of clear tape on it to make a hinge and latch. I think that will work and doesn't look horrible but still, not good. For selling purposes I'll shrink wrap the box so it will be fine - it's just the transport utility that I'm concerned with.

    The sides of the box are slightly wavy. Not a bad feature, subtle.

    All in all I'm very pleased with the quality and will certainly do a full print run (151 units this time) with them. I'll be getting the rules sheet printed separately.

    Here is a picture of the cover art.
