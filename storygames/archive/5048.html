\n I say: "occult detectives". What do you imagine?

(Fascinating subtext: I'm bringing people around to play a oneshot scenario for a backdrop that's "occult detectives and subtle urban magic". I then realized that my world is actually kinda out of sync with what folks might expect - I'm implicitly promising things I might not deliver. So I wanted to square my assumptions.)

    \n I think of detectives who, while they might know a great deal of occult lore, seldom actually use magic, as they know its risks. Magic in those stories is almost always of the plot device level, not utility or combat magic. (My favorate books of the genre are The Devil You Say and Strong Spirits by Elisa DeCarlo, which are, as well as being psychic/occult detective books, also take offs on P.G. Wodehouse's Bertie and Jeeves books.)

    \n In detective fiction the protagonists have to solve the case by circumventing a system/culture/society that doesn't want the murder to be solved.

    In occult detective fiction, the protagonists have to solve the case by circumventing reality.

    I have no idea where those two sentences came from but I like them.

    \n Mysteries based on and solved by occult and dream logic, symbolism, and magical archetypes rather than (or in addition to) the classic evidence gathering and people shaking-up stuff.

    You wouldn't solve a murder by finding clues, interrogating witnesses, or anything like that, but rather by ritually and symbolically becoming the victim and experiencing the murder first-hand.
