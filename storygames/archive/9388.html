\n This Saturday there will be an RPG contest which is organized by the publisher of the most popular Hungarian RPG, Magus. It's a traditional RPG like DnD, except there are more exceptions, etc. I knew once this game, but I really know nothing about the new edition which is a total overhaul. There aren't enough GMs and for personal reasons I really would like to help by GMing.

The problem is, that the adventure to be played will most probably have crucial points where system should be used (such as fights and magic - with huge spell lists OMG) and the GM game and most probably the adventure, too, will lack any help for the GMs (such as the threat level is in DnD).

On the other hand the number of GMs is scarce, because four parties sent their applications after the deadline. The developers didn't want to include them, but the publisher is pushing them, so they must GM them. I think that this means that some compromises are acceptable.

I'm curious if anyone has any advice to me. Shall I insist to GM to a late party and tell them (before or afterwards) that because of this they will have / had a less valuable session (not with these words)? How much should I dare to change the adventure to my likings to make it easier to GM?

Any ideas are welcome. Even the stupidest ones, so please don't hesitate. Thanks.
