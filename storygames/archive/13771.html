\n Back in 2007, I hosted the Festival of Flawless Victory, an event (not really a contest) for finishing work (usually a new draft) on unfinished projects. Over the course of the event, significant work was done on such games as: Geiger Counter, In a Wicked Age, A Flower for Mara, It's Complicated, Anima Prime, Mythender, and several others. Ultimately, SabreGreatWolf, Fey Wray, Powell Driver, D. Vincent and The Kenrick Man were declared champions and eternal grandmasters.

Now... the fighters have been recalled to the arena. The deathmatch for the fate of two worlds -- the real world and the corrupted world of imagination -- is at hand! Who will you fight? How will you write your name on the eternal role of champions?

The Eternal Rules of the Eternal Festival

Games are easy to start, but sometimes hard to finish. They are the like the green vegetables of the mind.

Here's the eightfold path to achieving Flawless Victory:

1. Name your unfinished business.
2. Pick a reasonable deadline for finishing it.
3. Laugh loudly and arrogantly. HA HA HA! This is not optional.
4. Pick a ridiculous deadline that is very imminent instead. One that makes Goro wince with its harshness.
5. FINISH HIM!
6. Post a link to your game and gloat.
7. The Chairman (that's me) will declare your Flawless Victory and award prizes.
8. Rinse and repeat, until the festival expires. I will suddenly close it with no warning, YOU FOOLS!

Example 1:\n
1. a playable draft of Super Suit, my unfinished and unsubmitted solo game from Emily's recent contest
2. By the end of the month
3. HA HA HA!
4. By this weekend
\nExample 2:\n
1. Responding to all the potential contributors to Magic Missile
2. By this weekend
3. HA HA HA!
4. By tomorrow
\nROUND ONE.... FIGHT!
