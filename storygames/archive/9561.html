\n ~edit~ please forgive spelling and grammar I am typing this all from my iPhone
\nSo as some know this weekend my computer fried itself the day before I was going to back it up on an external drive as opposed to the partitioned drive on my computer (my only prior concern for file loss being a virus) which cause me to lose dozens of books I have acquired over the years. More importantly though I have lost the work I have been doing on the story game system my little project. I am looking forward on everything though taking the Tao route and going with the flow. Sitting down to start working on things again I find that I feel free like a couple months of failed work is not drowning my vision and I even have come to a new conclusion of how I will finish the story game system.
\nI am going to in a spiral bound notebook hand write the book (not notes) in full without at all going back to correct anything. I will write all in pen from the intro forward through to character creation with no plan but what is in my head. I have done a couple pages and it is truly liberating and fun. So to those who are like me and constantly self sabotage yourself as you fix this and that I challenge you to finish something.
\nThe rules are as follows
\n
\nYou must hand write the book.
\nNo notes may be made about design theory or concept in regards to your work.
\nYou may post status updates of what has happened and others may speculate as to future development but you must only yourself create when physically buried in your note book.
\nYou may use no greater than 150 sheets of paper in creation of the book.
\nYou may only revise by tearing an entire sheet out of your notebook thus shortening you total notebook size.
\nResearch is ok but you may not keep any notes your findings must come directly from source to paper
\n
\nAny one who finishes is a winner
\nIf you cheat your only cheating yourself.
\nWhen you are finished you can change alter or destroy your work as you so choose but refining it through play is probably best.
\n
\nFigured I would post this since everyone loves challenges and it seems like a chance to just let loose and tumble through the creation process.
\n~Seth~
\nOpinions, rants, or throw ins welcome.
