\n This weekend I picked up a copy of Sherlock Holmes, Consulting Detective at a yard sale for fifty cents. It's a game about solving mysteries, deerstalker caps, Victorian ne'er-do-wells, London fog, etc.

But here's the thing - although billed as a board/party game, it is definitively a roleplaying game, and a GMless one at that. You play Baker Street Irregulars trying to solve mysteries before super brain Holmes can do it. The game has strong elements of programmed solo adventure as well, a research component (!) and a strategic map you must reference constantly. It has cooperative and competitive modes, but never a facilitator. The amount of fiction you generate is going to vary from group to group, but unless you are a robot it isn't going to be zero. There's nothing board gamey about it and it looks fun as hell. I've never seen anything like it, and it is a contemporary of Champions first edition and Aftermath!

Has anyone played? Am I late to the party?

    \n I had this in my teens, and we played it quite a bit. I have very fond memories of it, and recently picked up another copy. I recall we pretty much always failed miserably, the cases are not easy. We usually played in the competitive mode, if I were to play today I think I would choose cooperative. In the competitive mode, it never had anything that I would call much of a role playing feel. It was mostly a dry CYA mixed with a really hard Encyclopedia Brown book. I think playing cooperatively would amp up the sort of thing you're are talking about.

    My favorite bit was always scouring the newspapers in the included booklet for clues. They had these great little on-going subplots that might be just red herrimgs, or might pay off on later cases.
