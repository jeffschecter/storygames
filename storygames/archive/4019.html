\n Okay, I've had these on my desk forever, trying to find a way to scan them decently (they're green-marker on green paper). But screw it, you'll all just have to get the text only version.
\n
\nOne day, my older son (then five) said "Daddy, I need you to cut me up some paper. I need cards. I'm going to make a game."
\n
\nSo I dutifully did that thing. This is, word for word, what he created (less the wonderfully evocative stick-figure illustrations, which you'll just have to take on faith):
\n
\n- Move person: Move two spaces and shoot.
\n- Move rocks: Move rocks two spaces + throw them.
\n- Fire a missile from a house OR fire a missile and leave a track.
\n- Rocket Move: Move two fight Zurg, you only can move four places.
\n- Person attack: You can fight Zurg but not his spaceship. So you just will have to fight.
\n- Space cannon: You can shoot three places and hit Zurg's spaceships (some of them)
\n- Break Zurg's Ship: When Zurg's ship appears, take off a rocket with no people.
\n- Take off: You can take off on this card.
\n- Laser attack: Move three spaces + shoot your lasers.
\n
\nI swear, I don't have the faintest idea how it works. But he painstakingly made two identical decks ... so ... uh ... I still have no idea.

    \n Okay, we have the designer in the thread. He was watching me look over the Story Games threads, and he said very curiously " ' My Son's Game? ' " ... because he does not yet realize that there are other sons in the world, who are not him. But, in this case ... that works! It is him!
    \n
    \nHis first response, in awe: "Am I a little famous?" I agreed that he was a little famous, and he danced around the room calling "I'm famous! I'm famous!" So ... I must admit that he comes by that honestly.
    \n
    \nAnd now ... on to answers!
    \n
    \nHow do you play the game: "Well, each person gets a deck, and they take, well, and they ... take ... uhhh! No, not like that! No! (Hits daddy) Back up! BACK UP!"
    \n
    \nOkay ... the edited answers.
    \n
    \nHow do you play the game: "Well, each person gets a deck, and they take TURNS. The youngest person that's playing goes first. Each turn, somebody picks a card. There are no game-boards. So, don't worry to bring anything! I have everything. That's how you play!"
    \n
    \nDoes the game have a name: "Yep. It's name is Defeat Zurg or Zurg Defeats You! That's it's name."
    \n
    \n
    \nEDIT: Strikethrough on the verbatim description, at the author's sulking and violent insistence.
