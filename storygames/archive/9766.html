    \n If it's just for that purpose and not for actual security reasons, you can do it a couple of ways in Acrobat. You can use a stamp: Create a stamp of whitespace of an appropriate column width and one line high, and just start stamping. Or you can simply highlight the text, right-click and select "properties", and change the color to black.

    In one of the more premium versions, there is an actual redaction tool which removes the data instead of merely covering it up.

    Posted By: Thunder_GodTo take David's idea, no need to black out lines of text. Cut out squares from a blank page, glue them over, photocopy this page.
    OK, to improve Guy's idea: cut the sections out of the printout and just photocopy them as-is--most photocopiers have a white backer on the lid. If you get unpleasant artifacts (e.g an old photocopier with a dirty backer) then you can just put a whole white sheet behind the one you're copying. There's no need to make accurately sized covering paper.

    But, of course, neither his method nor my improvement can be run through a duplex copier with an auto-feeder (i.e. just about every copier made, and probably every copier at Kinko's).
