[The Mountain Witch] 6 Hour Fates

Eric Provost
May 2006 edited May 2006 in Story Games

I'm looking for a little brainstorming help in doing a bit of a hack to tMW.

See, I dig the game, but I always play it in a considerably shorter time than it was intended for, and some of the fates are a little tough to wrap your brain around in that short of time. Worst Fear always ends up being the trouble-child of the group. None of the new players that I sit down with ever know what to do with it. Hell, the last time I played I drew it, had what I thought was an awesome idea for it, and it totally went nowhere. Bummer.

So, what I'd like to do is come up with half a dozen 'fresh' Fates that drive the game just a little faster than the ones in the book. Here's my ideas on what would make good 6 hour Fates;

1) They must inspire a relationship of some flavor between your character and one or more of the other characters. Preferably more than one of the other characters. Four of the existing Fates already do this, but I wanted to underline how important I think this is.

2) They could inspire the players to author scenery, setting, props (items), and other bits of niftyness during play. I theorize that a strong bit of inspiration here would help inspire players to reveal their Fates earlier in play. Late-coming Fates have been a regular issue in my sessions, as has been players that aren't inspired to author NPCs or setting elements into the story.

3) I'd love to see the Fates actually be more um... "Fate-like". The standard Fates talk a lot about the characters' pasts, but I'd like to get the 6 Hour versions in line with the futures of the characters.

Now, to tie all that together, lemmie give you all an idea of what I've got in mind;

The steel of your ancestors, in the hands of your favorite sibling, will be your end.

Secrets are revealed to you when you express your deepest love to your greatest enemy.

Shit like that.
Anyone have any ideas? I'd love to hear them.

-Eric

    Eric,

    After refreshing my recollection of the existing Fates, I'm a bit torn. I can see how several of them are weak for a short-form game, requiring more time to develop and be revealed. And I heartily approve the 3 principles you've listed. I'd also add a fourth: the short-form Fates should all call for some action. This would drive the players forward toward some end, not allow them to wallow about unsure where to go. Overall, I think this means revenge and unholy pact could still be used pretty effectively in the short-form game.

    I'm also concerned your two examples are a bit too tightly constrained to allow the player freedom to interpret what the Fate is all about. For example, I would change the first one to "The steel of your ancestors, in the hands of someone you trust, will be your end," giving the player a lot more leeway.

    Let me mull on some other options.

    I just finished reading tMW, so let me try to come up with a few Fates off the cuff. Just brainstorming...

    Mercy You have promised your soul to the Gods, and thus have faith that there is something to be found in the Witch's life - some element of culture or fragment of his past - that proves he is worth salvation and forgiveness. Your fellows have only blood on their minds. What will it cost you to be the bringer of Mercy?

    Gluttony In the deep of your heart, where most find a soul, you know only a hunger for riches, luxuries, comforts, but you can enjoy none of them: you can only acquire, consume and destroy them. You know how to fake a brave face, but ultimately you're all about your own Greed - and you know that your avarice will cost a fellow man very greatly.

    Assassin This bounty is nothing compared to what drives you to an unforgivable deed, assassinating one of your fellow ronin in the course of this adventure. The terms of your contract are clear: firstly, by discussion, come to confirm that this man is conceivably the target of the contract; then, eliminate him. Make it look like an accident if possible, or kill him without any witnesses present. If this is not possible, you must destroy both of yourselves, but above all you must not return from Mt. Fuji without fulfilling your task.

    Artisan You are the master of some craft or true art, and through a dream you know with certainty: you will have your once chance to create a masterwork here, on Mt. Fuji, at the lair of the foul Mountain Witch. You may be able to take this moment to create a work of glory that will last thousands of years, but whether you do or you not, you know that your skill will fade quickly as you descend from the Mountain.

    True Power The Mountain Witch possesses a terrible power, and something drives you - a secret patron, or perhaps your own ambition - to take control of his manse once he is defeated, or die trying in the attempt. What is the nature of his power, and can it be controlled?
