My RPG repertoir consists only of:

DnD 3rd, 3.5 and 4th
Vampire: The Masquerade
Vampire: The Requiem

new World of Darkness
Dogs in the Vineyard (My favorite so far)

"The Simplest Little RPG Ever"
Maid RPG

So I hope you can see I've no idea where to start.

Thanks in advance for any comments n_n. If there is a game specifically design for such purpose or any similar purpose let me know (I hope there is)

    \n Prime Time Adventures seems well-made for this sort of thing, maybe? Lots of conflicts involving, "Does this guy buy your story," with losses contributing to characters problems instead of out-and-out blowing their game? I'd also include lots of non-conflict story beats where the players are setting up the con. The television reference reinforces this suggestion. I'm trying not to do the knee-jerk, "You can run anything in PTA!" thing. I'm thinking things like Leverage and the Soderbergh Ocean's 11, is that the correct thought-space?

    As for games specifically tooled to do genre replication of a light hearted con , I'm not as sure.

    Posted By: forlorn1Wilderness of Mirrors could be drifted from spies to con-me with each role being transformed to a different type/style of con.
    \nI'm planning on using this game for something very similar to Leverage, a television show about a bunch of con men (and women) who con the rich to help the poor. It works out almost exactly like Wilderness of Mirrors: there is a leader, a fighty guy, a techy, a beautiful actress, and a stealth infiltrator. At the beginning of an episode, they get job from a new client, come up with a plan, and then go out and con the hell out of some folks. The show can be hit or miss, but it's pretty fun. And I suspect there is a good evening in getting some friends together, having some food, watching the first episode, and then running a session with their own "Leverage"-style team.
