\n I suddenly want to play a character who is all about pets. Like a guy who has a pack of specially trained dogs that adventure with him. None of this "summon familiar" or druid or ranger crap either. I want a warrior who has three fighting dogs that dive in with him. Maybe a dachsund trained to sneak into small places and collect items. Etc.

When was the last time any of your characters had a pet? Was it a color item? A mechanical advantage? A convenience item (like the OotS familiar that disappears unless the spell caster remembers it's always by his side?) What system do you prefer for this kind of character? Do mounts count?

I don't know why I'm pet obsessed today. Maybe it's because we're trying a new food for the cat.

    \n Come to think of it, I don't think any character I've actually played has had a pet. One such character was statted up but never played, a noblewoman and avid huntress who kept a half-wolf dog. The idea was that she'd use the dog for hunting and tracking, sic it on the uppity, etc. This was in Fate 2.0 so the dog was an Aspect and Personal Extra, with its own Aspects and Skills. If I redid the character in Fate 3.0 the dog would likely be an Aspect and Stunt.

    I think system plays a part when it comes to characters having pets. 7th Sea, for instance, had hardly any mechanical support for pets. Just about the only animal rules I can think of are spiders for Fate Witches in the Vodacce book. Fate has Aspects and Stunts which encourage all sorts of PC extras, including pets. And of course, the title of the thread had me immediately thinking of Earthdawn with its Beastmaster class.

    When was the last time any of your characters had a pet? Was it a color item? A mechanical advantage?
    \nY'know, after thinking about it for a bit, I've never really seen a game where pets/animal companions were really used well: Mostly just color items, or to get some mechanical advantage (sometimes a little of both).

    I'm not thinking that someone needs to go as far as to write a new RPG or anything, but I'd love to see some solid advice for making PC pets (or, more accurately, animal travelling companion NPCs in fantasy/sf games and the like) something more than just a dab of flavor and a 1d4 bite attack.

    \n Well maybe Russ will exorcise his need for pets in our impending paladins game (whatever that may be called).

    Andy, would adding a pet as a relationship in a game like Dogs do what you're looking for? What sort of interaction would you be hoping for?

    Although I can't say the rules drive it forward Elfquest has a pretty strong wolf/Elf relationship, especially our campaign where the whole pack slept in a heap. Our wolves were our friends and we emulated them in our interactions with one another.

    I guess to answer the question we need a focus. Any ideas on what sorts of goals we'd have in such a game? What do we wish convey? Do we want to explore what sort of comfort and comaradere we gain from having pets? Does size and personal interests come into scope? Are you a cat person or a alpaca person or a psuedodragon person?
