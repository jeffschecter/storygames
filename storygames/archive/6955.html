    \n For Oralce, you could consider Mallory and the openings to each chapter of Morte d'Arthur (depending on edition you use there are about 500 of them to pick from). Most concern lesser knights etc. and are not simply the story of Arthur/Lancelot/Guinevere or the Grail. You can simply use them or develop more generic oracle entries from them.

    They are things like,

    How the devil in a woman's likeness would have had Sir Bors to have lain by her, and how by God's grace he escaped.
    How Sir Galahad departed, and how he was commanded to go to the Castle of Maidens to destroy the wicked custom.
    How Sir Tristram, at a tournament, bare the shield that Morgan La Fay delivered to him

    You can see one version here:

    http://www.sacred-texts.com/neu/mart/index.htm

    Mallory's premise of chivalry is very IAWA-ish friendly in that it leads into conflict. In the end a knight is a free agent with a duty to a moral code that goes beyond how you conduct yourself under arms and comes first before other interests. Some suggest he wrote the book partly as a plea/exhortation to the noble class of the 15th Century to "be true to ideals" and not descend into "legalism".

    Rob
