    \n Board game wise, Battlelore is a great two-player board game/war game... gorgeous pieces and art, cool scenarios and not that difficult to learn to play. I would definitely pick that one over Shadows Over Camelot, which I think tends to work better the more players you have at the table (mostly because of the Traitor mechanic).

    Battue: Storm of the Horselords is another cool game that would work well with two or three players, though it shines with four. You can find my review at http://www.boardgamegeek.com/game/27823

    Posted By: buzz...and/or very small groups, like 3 people.
    \nOh man, for three people, loads. That's how big our group generally is. So, I'm going over three people games mostly:

    With Great Power...
    Capes (might also be good for two)
    In A Wicked Age
    Dogs in the Vineyard (might also be good for two)
    Breaking the Ice (two only, I think)
    Primetime Adventures
    And there's are very hippie, but Nobilis and World of Darkness work really well with two - one GM and one player - or three. You can really get character and story focused and make it less "adventurey" than a larger group.

    Some board, well card, games for two:
    Munchkin
    Gloom
    Gubs (discovered this at Dundracon and have gotten to play it a couple of times - this is very cute and lots of fun)
