    Posted By: commondialogI've gotMechawhich *should* be out in time for Dreamation. Granted, it's centered on a very small slice of anime (mecha anime), but it was definitely inspired by Robotech, Big O, Gundam Wing, Code Geass and others.
    \nNothing wrong with going for a small slice of anime. If anything I think that's a good thing. Where can I found out more about Mecha?

    Here's mine (now that I have time to type more than a couple of sentences...):

    Raspberry Heaven, a GM-less card-based storytelling game about heartwarming slice of life high school girls, a la Azumanga Daioh and Lucky Star. (I have a completed draft, and I'm gearing up to do revisions and more playtesting).

    Dandelion Complex, a sister game to RH, which also uses cards but has a GM, and is basically an excuse to be weird and throw around random events. Heavily inspired by Sayonara Zetsubou-sensei.

    Slime Story, about a contemporary world where portals opened up and started spitting out cute monsters like something out of a Korean MMO (like Maple Story or Ragnarok Online). (Work in progress).

    Catgirl: The Storytelling Game, which started out as a parody of WoD, and has kind of grown into a relatively serious, interesting thing in itself. It runs on a simplified but quirky hybrid of ST and Fudge, and concerns Catgirls with magical powers trying to stay happy and free in a dangerous world.

    Moonsick, a surreal game inspired by Takashi Murakami's superflat art movement. It uses custom-made cards, and is about girls who live on the moon with the rabbits being sent to a radioactive earth to find the Celestial Cleanser, and becoming horribly mutated along the way.

    Zero Breakers is a game I've been trying to design forever, a diceless conflict resolution based fighting shonen manga RPG.

    \n In my "God, I've got to work on something not as bloody and villainous as Criminal Element" moments, I've been boiling up a mecha anime hack for Spirit of the Century/ FATE that I like to call Machina Deus HASHIN!. I'm doing this because I love FATE, I love mecha anime, and holy crap have you guys been watching Macross Frontier?! No, I mean, really, have you?

    Theoretically MDH! would cover most mecha sub-genre (real, giant, etc.), though I've only used my current build of the system to run a dark Real Mecha game. It's got your basic SotC/FATE core to it but with a bunch of added ideas, like explicit rules on changing and destroying your own aspects, and a mecha creation system that builds any kind of mecha as an extension of your own character. Also in MDH!, I blatantly rip off the battle map from Agon and use it for zones, chases, and dog fights.

    Right now, MDH! isn't anything more than an idea in my head, some notes on my computer, and a game at my table. I'm not sure if I'm going to do anything with the game, and I would have to contact Fred, Rob, and Lenny if I were going to, but I figure I'd throw this out there. Seemed to fit the subject of this thread just fine.
