    \n Link to Vincent's blog:
    http://www.lumpley.com/comment.php?entry=708

    I love how different playbooks make concrete statements about how the world behaves.
    & how including them, or not "fielding" them, as MC, directs the world we'll create.

    If you want to go nutsy after-the-bomb style with the Space Marine Mammal, all good & i bet it'll be easy.

    I'm interested in wondering about what sort of more-serious possibilities might be in store for us.
    I feel like the SMM will give us a Quarantine-esque solid piece from the past, & it could be really cool having both of them in play, seeing how their stories clearly don't match up. Or maybe how they do.

    I like the idea of these post-apocalyptic humans being faced with something so obviously & terrifyingly "other" as the SMM, having to fit that into their picture of the world.

    I also like the possibility that the SMM isn't necessarily "right" about the history of their people & how they came to be - i mean, if there's a quarantine or some other really old characters who remember how things've been, we could drift the SMM into all sorts of directions.

    I wonder if humans would worship the SMM? If there would be other SMM who'd be villainous? Also really stoked to see what will become of the "quest" aspect of the SMM character, that they seem to be trying to do a thing (y'know, reclaim the earth, no big).

    The best question of all, of course: Who Are The Dolphins At War With?
