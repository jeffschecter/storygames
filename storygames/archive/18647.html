    \n Here is the rules gloss:

    Dungeon World \u2013 How to play

    The game is a conversation between the players and the GM. When the players stop talking the GM starts. When the GM stops talking, they ask the players \u201cWhat do you do?\u201d

    The GM NEVER rolls dice\u2026ever.

    The GM describes what is happening by\u2026

    Saying what a monster does, or what happens at a location.
    Revealing an unwelcome truth.
    Show signs of an approaching threat.
    Say how much damage a character takes. Be kind, threaten don\u2019t kill.
    Say how they use up their resources.
    Tell them how their move backfires.
    Separate them.
    Offer a choice \u2013 with a cost or without one.
    Put a player on the spot.
    Tell them the requirements and consequences of a plan \u2013 do you want that?
    Ask the players what they see in addition to what you say.

    But above all else ask them \u201cWhat do you do?\u201d

    Never let the action slow down.

    The players roll dice when you ask them to. No roll and they get what they say. Roll and they may fail.

    Players roll 2d6: 10+\tFull success \u2013 say how the player\u2019s plan works
    \t\t\t7-9\tPartial success \u2013 give them a choice or hurt them some
    \t\t\t2-6\tFailure \u2013 GM says what happens. Make their lives harder and more
    Interesting

    Players may try the following moves:

    Hack and Slash \u2013 To fight (1d6 damage).
    Volley (bows)
    Defy Danger - To face a threat without fighting.
    Defend another person or thing
    Spout Lore \u2013 This is how they get to add to the world by making shit up.
    Discern Reality \u2013 Ask a question
    Parley \u2013 Talk to people
    Aid or Interfere \u2013 To stop or mess up things.
    Cast Spells \u2013 These are on the player character sheet.

    Throw monsters at them to make life interesting. 1HP for goons who need to die fast, 3 HP for tougher monsters, 6 to 10 HP for a boss fight.

    Have a basic outline of a dungeon but constantly ask the players to make up details.

    \n Rolling damage can fall to the players or the GM, and it uses the particular guidelines of what constitutes d4, d6, d8, d10 damage.

    The reason I mention the hard moves is because you're only allowed to drop a hard move under certain circumstances: the players fail a roll, fail to respond to a lead-up soft move, or give the GM a golden opportunity. You can't just say "you take damage from the falling boulders"; you have to lead up with a soft move of "the room starts shaking and falling apart". If the players don't respond, deal damage (and don't flinch from handing out d6 or d8 damage! As the GM, you have to portray the world honestly), and if they do respond (probably Defy Danger), you only damage them under some circumstances.

    What's more important than the hard moves (which are consequences of their actions or inactions) are the principles that you use to set up and drive forward situations. Things like "be a fan of the PCs" or "portray the world honestly" or "play to find out what happens". They're hard-coded into the rules because they do drive the game, and drive it quickly.

    \n WRT "Goons shouldn't kill you.":

    The PCs have a lot of fictional options for avoiding damage. They can be defended for. They can back off and use bandages, healing items. They can plink away with ranged weapons. If they're casting spells, they can avoid choosing the "you draw attention" options.

    I make sure to draw attention for new players to that 0 HP is "you have a good chance of dying full stop." It's also useful to say that damage tops out at about 8 or 9 in a hit, unless you're fighting something/doing something which is described as really scary.

    If a PC gets roughed up by goons, then you should give them the fictional outs to get out of danger. But if you describe how the ghouls are tearing away flesh and memories from a PC that has 5HP, and that limb of theirs is hanging on by a thread, and they continue to want to stand in the fight, and they're not invoking Defend, then they're making that choice. Be honest with the rules, and respect their choice to put themselves in continued danger.

    If it's just a shakedown fight, or if you're thinking "I need to wear them down", then drop a goon fight and make something exciting or important happen.
