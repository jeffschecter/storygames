    Hmm -- have you thought of actually using the Hellboy RPG? Your mileage on that might not exactly qualify for "the fun of story gaming", but it is a light, self-contained version of GURPS so it doesn't necessarily have the sprawl problems you might experience with the full-on GURPS game.

    Also, the thought of Spirit Of The Century immediately leaps to mind: I suspect that it might adapt rather well to a Hellboy-ish type game pretty much right out of the box. And it has a much better chance of fulfilling your "story gaming" desires, I suspect.

    Also, Dogs In The Vineyard could probably be tweaked to be a Hellboy sort of game: the general narrative structure of BPRD/Hellboy tales are alarmingly similar to the structure set out in DitV; I suspect you wouldn't even need to tweak the progression of sin for town creation all that much. On the other hand, DitV would force you to focus more on the plight of the normal humans caught between your agents and the apocalypta-beasties than you might want (if what you really want to do is more fun two-fisted monster bashing than investigative morality play).

  • TonyLB
    February 2007
    Oh, okay. Different possible emphases.

    Primetime Adventures will make a game of supers vs. monsters that focusses on characters following one issue that evolves over a carefully paced sequence of events.

    Dogs in the Vineyard will make a game of supers vs. monsters that focusses (as Viktor points out) on the fallout that such conflicts have on the people (and presumably the "bystander" monsters) nearby.

    Polaris will make a game of supers vs. monsters that focusses on the Nietzchean principle that "those who fight against monsters become monsters themselves," and will lead inevitably toward the supers causing the very apocalypse they try to prevent.

    The Shadow of Yesterday will make a game of supers vs. monsters that focusses on the weaknesses and strengths that drive the heroes, and the way that they change by confronting those issues within themselves in order to become something new.

    Capes will make a game of supers vs. monsters that focusses on what both sides stand for (good and evil) and the various irreconcilable differences between the characters (both the things the heroes and monsters fight over, and the things that the heroes fight over among themselves).

    The Mountain Witch will make a game of supers vs. monsters that focusses on a mythic quest to reach the source of the evil, and the question of whether the rather sketchy "superheroes" can afford to trust each other when the stakes are the entire world.


    Other folks can write up what other systems would do. My fingers are sore :-) I hope this helps!
