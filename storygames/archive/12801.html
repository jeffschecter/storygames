    \n And then I saw that this thread is all about specific inspiration:
    Posted By: SilverlionI'm not talking about a general idea of inspiration, but specific things which drive you to write or play specific games.
    - so; reasons why I've designed some of my games:

    - Pervo (ultra violent frpg): a "response" to the way many GMs works in classical games; as fun-fascilitators for demanding players. And inspired by sado-masochism. And inspired by pure devilry!

    - Muu (harmonic and poetic fable): inspired by my talkings on rpgs with Jon Venbakken (a great mind lost to rpgs); specifically on topics like telepathy and non-violent fiction. And inspired by nature, by our knowledge of how man came to be. And inspired by the workings of language. And finally; inspired by taoism.

    - Fabula (a classical fantasy rpg): inspired by the need to activate children in an after-school institution. Inspired by talking on and testing of many classical rpgs, and their methods. Inspired by ambition to make a better fantasy rpg.

    - Role-playing poems: inspired by literary poems, which seems to explore other facets of life than novels and short-stories. And inspired by the need to find ways of exploring such human conditions in rpgs too.

    - Stoke-Birmingham 0-0 (a rp-poem on dull people): inspired by dull people, and by the idea of exploring themes not suited for longer games (the idea of rp-poems).

    - Livets h\xf8st (Autumn Of Life; a game on ordinary people, friendship and death): inspired by ordinary people; their life and fate, and what makes them happy. And inspired by the game Until We Sink by Magnus Jacobsson.

    - Homunkel (on nice small-folks, and trolls): inspired by children stories, the dilemma of hate (violence) and love (peace), and by the word "homuncle" (meaning: small humanoid creature made by magic means).

    Have an inspired day!

    \n The game that I'm currently working on (who's name keeps changing) is specifically inspired by the following goals and issues:

    1- I like games that are cooperative but challenging.
    2- I hate the "whiff-factor" that certain games produce.
    3- I am old and busy and have less time to play and prepare for games.
    4- I want more interaction between characters and with the environment in the game.
    5- I am a softie GM and usually under-challenge my players rather than risk killing their characters.
    6- I want failing a challenge to be just as exciting as succeeding.

    The idea of solving these issues or achieving these goals is really inspiring to me. The gameplay that I envision from this (unnamed, yet to be designed) system is very motivational.

    7- ... and robots and explosions.

    \n I am inspired by dreams.
    I am inspired by off hand quips made by other people, in my person about media of all sorts.
    I'm inspired by media itself.
    I am inspired by the failure of other games to live up to their promise. Often with specific games the idea is there, the promise (or premise) but the tools are not, and I seek a way to take that one moment
    and run with it.

    Example: SMITE! Is a game I'm working on using some variation of the old MSH system geared for fantasy, what I feel D&D would turn into if it were left unrestrained. A cop show set in a bizarre
    fantasy world. Teams of officers (SMITE! Teams) taking down dangerous terrorists, lawbreakers, and like. Just to keep society together when goblins, magic, and such are real. Forensic magic, controlled substances like
    love potions, and helms of "alignment" change, fairy drugs, and so on. Another example would be the Knights of the Hidden Sun blurb I read which talked about people being living starships--and that not being the core aspect it focused on. So now brewing in my head is a game of living human starships. Human sized, human shaped, exploring the stars together as Fleets, or singly. I'm just trying to avoid it turning into Green Lanterns meet Rom

    I look at things I enjoy in gaming and try and find a game to fit those elements for a given "idea." If i can't find one I write one.
    I am inspired by reading other peoples games, and actual play of them.
    I am inspired by my dog.
    I am inspired by my wife. "Oooh Tunnels and Trolls, I never got to play that, I always GMed.." (What more could one want than one really willing player...:D)
