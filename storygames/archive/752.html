The Galactic Subcommittee on the Preservation of Civilizations

Josh Roby
May 2006 edited May 2006 in Story Games
I'm not sure if this is the saddest idea for an RPG or something actually viable:

The PCs are representatives of the Galactic Subcommittee on the Preservation of Civilizations. You have been assigned to a backwater planet that has only just figured out that the rest of the galaxy is populated and are almost completely ignorant of interstellar commerce, politics, technology, and culture.

Unfortunately, their sun is about to explode, and the entire population needs to be moved. Since the galaxy really is populated, there isn't anything so convenient as an empty planet they can be moved to.

The entire population must be placed, family by family, into the larger galactic body politic and enculturated into its manners and mores so that they are not taken advantage of, exploited, and their own cultural identity turned into a very small smudge on the ass-end of galactic civilization.

That's your job. It is next to impossible. Oh, and you have no budget. The sun explodes in three years. Start working now.

    So check this out.

    Each culture has a number of traits. Each trait has Strength, we know how strong.

    Society has Strength. We don't know what it is yet.

    When the cultures clash, we roll strength versus strength. A win for society weakens the culture -- they reject their old ways for new ones. The society is strong. Its strength goes up. A win for the culture means they keep their ways in the face of change, calcifying traditions. Trait goes up, society goes down.

    The stronger society is, the more resources the caseworkers have. The caseworkers have fondness for various culture traits. As these weaken, the caseworkers despair.
