\n I decided to take the chocolate that is Lady Blackbird and mix it with the peanut butter of Apocalypse World to create my own situation filled, move driven game. I managed to test it yesterday with the fantastic people at the London indie RPG meetup group, and I think it's safe to say that everyone enjoyed themselves and the characters keyed off each other and the situation appropriately.

Escape from Thanatos! is set in a Space Opera setting, where one takes on the roles of Captain Talia Mercury, Merchant Prince Marcus Dunn, ageing Space Knight Sir Lux Angelo, Francisco - Talia's stalwart companion, and Mona - a mysterious stowaway as they crash land on the planet Thanatos after escaping the Omegan Empire!

How will Prince Marcus and the others escape Thanatos?
What terrible dangers lie in their way?
What price will the Warlord Hephaestus demand of them? And will our heroes be able to pay it?
More importantly, will they want to accede to such a request?

Answer these questions yourselves as you play Escape from Thanatos!

Hope you all enjoy it as much as I have been!
Stuart.

    \n The lack of common basic moves was one of the driving factors behind the design. As it happened it seemed to work out OK - we didn't really hit a situation where a character's moves didn't cover what they wanted to do (except when Mona was supposed to be Deadly Death Racing where we just re-appropriated the Porpoise's moves).

    Hopefully play reflects the design which tried to make the moves broad enough to suit each character's needs, whilst being specific enough to be neither vague and as to be informative into the character. In the game on Saturday it seemed to be the case - Prince Marcus was haughty, Captain Talia was impulsive, Sir Lux was mysterious, Mona mischievous and Francisco was brusque but protective.

    Conditions also served to fill in some of the gaps. Conditions like "trapped" or "injured" operate in those places in the fiction where they're needed - after all being chased or sneaking about becomes exciting when you're injured and whether you cry out in pain or are slowed down become interesting.

    \n So as part of my ongoing experiments, Escape from Thanatos! has been joined by two partner games (with possibly more on the way): -

    Terror of the Serpent Men! is a Pulp Adventure following Dan Dynamo, Princess Aphasia, Mr. Franks, Zeno and Lucy LaBelle as they investigate and thwart the mysterious conspiracy which links a cabal of hidden rulers of the Earth, the downfall of Atlantis, and the Terrifying Serpent Men who hail from the Seventh Moon of Jupiter. Punch evil in the face! See the wonders of Venus! Learn the secrets of Shan Gri La!

    As the Tea Leaves Grow is a Martial Arts Quest which follows five Brothers and Students of the ineffable Master Iron Willow who has sent them to the four corners of the Empire to seek out the Four Great Tea Masters and find a brew which can rival the flower which Small Toad mistakenly gave away as a keepsake to a passing beauty. Can they find such a leaf before the Emperor comes to visit in a Month? Does such a leaf even exist? Will they be able to retrieve it if it does?

    If anyone does take any of these games for a spin - feel free to let me know how they go - good or ill!
