\n Australian gamers were dealt a terrible blow a fortnight ago when Ian Houlihan regretfully announced the cancellation of GenConOz 2010. This was widely regarded as a Very Sad Thing.

Several smaller groups started working on their own activities to fill the void, and after a week started talking to each other about working together. And that's how it is that from September 24-26 in Brisbane Australia there will be Uprising. If we can't have the Best Four Days of Australian Gaming, we'll cut it down to three instead.

Alongside Warmachine, L5R and Magic there will be seminars on GMing, seminars on game design and workshops on game design, featuring some of Australia's finest freelancers and indie game designers.

I'll update this thread with more details as they come to light, or you can subscribe to the RSS feed at The Stockade for news about this and other Australian RPG design news.

    \n Sydney currently has three major roleplaying conventions (and a bunch of comic conventions and other pop-culture exhibits, but there's little roleplaying at them unless you count Magic tournaments or Cosplay).

    Eye-Con. Held during easter (end of March or early April), depending on the moon and other celestial alignments. An independent convention usually attracting a couple of hundred people, it's been running for about 5 years.

    Syd-Con. Held on the first weekend of October. Once known as Necronomicon..it merged with the formerly named Syd-Con. I'm pretty sure the independent Necronomicon had been running for at least 20 years before it collapsed due to falling numbers, Sydcon had been run out of the University of New South Wales for 15 years or so...The combined convention has been running for about 5 years. It draws roughly the same crowd as Eye-Con.

    Macquarie-Con. Run out of Macquarie University, I think it's been an annual event for about 25 years. I haven't actually been along to Macquarie-Con since the mid 1990s, I'm told it's slightly smaller than the other conventions.

    There's a few smaller house conventions often run out of the various university roleplaying clubs, but these rarely draw more than a few dozen players and they are often restricted to members of those clubs and their immediate friends.

    There are also a few major wargaming conventions that draw crowds of 500 or more...Leviathan in Sydney's outer west, held on ANZAC Day (honouring Australia's fallen soldiers), and MOAB (Mother Of All Battlegames) which coincides with Syd-Con, but caters to the wargaming crowd and gets 600+ miniatures gamers.

    I'll be running Go Play: Cockatoo Island on the middle weekend in June next year (the middle of our winter on an island). Due to it being the inaugural convention, I'm only expecting two dozen or so players...hopefully with numbers to go up when word spreads.
