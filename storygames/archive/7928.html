\n Hi! I'm a video game writer (and I also do this tabletop D&D role-playing devil-worship thing that the kids are into these days), and I need your help!

Specifically, I would like to know what you think about storytelling in video games. I have a survey that I'd like you to answer, if you feel so inclined. I'm not interested in your personal information -- I just want to know your answers to some basic questions. For example, do you watch cut scenes, or do you skip them? Do you ever develop opinions about (or feelings towards) video game characters, the way that one might with a character from a TV show or movie?

If you're interested in participating, please email me (address below). I'll send you a copy of the survey, which will take 5 to 20 minutes to complete (depending on how much time you want to spend on the open-answer questions at the end -- the yes/no and multiple/choice questions won't take much time).

Here's the email address: gamesurvey [at] rafaelchandler [dot] com.

Thank you!

- Rafael

    \n Self-reporting is an excellent method for collecting data from the motivated hard core of your audience, because they've all formed an intricate and individual personal identity around their hobby and their play - when you ask somebody like that whether they skip cut-scenes, you're going to get an answer that reflects not only their practice, but the ideology behind that practice. Whether the guy actually skips cut-scenes or not is relatively unimportant in that context, as he's not going to buy your game or like it based on his actual practice, but on what he thinks he does. If he identifies as the guy who hates cut-scenes, then that's valuable information for you.
