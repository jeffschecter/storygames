    Posted By: Matthijs- How much do I have to read & do before playing?
    \nSetup is part of play, and you cannot pre-plan. So you need to photocopy some character sheets and that's about it. If you are facilitating, you should read the whole book so you can be an informal reference for the background (Kiva societies, clans, sins) and system.\n
    Is it a campaign game, or better for one-shots?
    \nIt is well engineered for short runs of 3-5 sessions. You can run one-shots but that is not the sweet spot. \n
    How's it different from other games out there?
    \nI think it is pretty different, particularly the way it infuses gender into everything and brings forth the culture through play. The conflict resolution system is pretty standard, but it is carefully balanced to encourage players to move their characters outside social norms and commit sins, which is chocolate-dipped awesome.\n
    What bits will I want to steal after playing?
    \nI think there's a pretty impressive connection between fiction and mechanics that pushes the game's universe to the fore in a clever way. As I said I love the way it handles gender.\n
    What else do I want to know?
    \nAs long as one person knows WTF is going on, nobody else needs to know anything before sitting down to play. Everything you need to know about the world is learned as you choose your characters background - Kiva society, mother's clan, father's clan. It's a very low-prep situation and it works great.

    \n Yeah, you could play with up to five people easily. The rules don't scale beyond three protagonists, though, so you really can't play with a larger group - it'd be a mess. You can also play with a single GM, but that is not optimal, because there is a cool give and take between Inside and Outside, and you also get two brains working on every problem. I think the sweet spot is two GMs and two players - that's how I'm playing now and it is rockin'.
