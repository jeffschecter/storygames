\n It's been a while since Benhimself plugged directly the creation of the IRC channel, #playnow. I thought it was time to exhort people to come by and give the channel a try, especially after we've had some success getting games run.

What games? Well, we've had:

\u2022 Apocalypse World
\u2022 Ninja Game
\u2022 Microdungeons
\u2022 War Inc!
\u2022 Rock of Tahamaat
\u2022 3:16 \u2014 Carnage Amongst the Stars
\u2022 Rock-Paper-Scissors
\u2022 The Scenario
\u2022 GHOST/ECHO
\u2022 The Starflower Throne

... and various other little games that either remain nameless or have just slipped my mind for the moment.

So why not drop by and run something, or suggest a game someone else might be willing to run? The channel has also become something of a hangout for Story Game regulars interested in just chatting about our favorite pastime. Just point your IRC client to irc.magicstar.net and join the conversation.

    \n Tim, I feel your pain about the silence thing. It's gotten to the point where I kick anyone who's idled for more than 24 hours, basically because if you're not there to at least talk \u2014 or, better yet, to organize a game \u2014 you really shouldn't be on the channel. It's a lobby for pickup gaming, not somewhere to park.

    I still want to run a Dead of Night game with folks from the channel, but it's now been so long since I read the rules that I've forgotten them all. Consequently I need to re-read them and prepare appropriately. Once that's done, I'll have something worthwhile to contribute besides my presence/chat.

    As Daumantas pointed out in (on channel) discussion yesterday, it was easier to get things started when we: 1) had a stable group of users who could be relied upon to be there on a regular basis, and 2) had a go-to game for pickup use. The game we used to play religiously was Apocalypse World, which lends itself pretty well to pick-up-and-play gaming, but the shine seems to have rubbed off that particular toy. Our regular users were all also ready and willing to run things, which meant as soon as three or four other people showed up, it was game on. Unfortunately now you're stuck with me as your most highly participative user and I \u2014 as I have discussed elsewhere, if you're curious \u2014 am not the best at this whole "story gaming" thingie.

    \n One thing #playnow was good for during the its peak of activity was online playtests of new games - generally, if there was something new out for players to test and at least one person was interested in the thing, we'd have at least one session of the new game.
    \n
    \nIt's not like #playnow is a playtesting channel essentially, but online pickup games do need either a freely available source everyone could reference or a knowledgable player/designer who would explain the game, which tends to be the case for games in the making for more often than for games made.
    \n
    \nSo if you're an aspiring game designer with a rough idea of a game (*you* know how to run it, but no one else does) and wants to test it, it could be useful to come to #playnow for a spin. Fellows there are pretty enthusiastic about this sort of thing.
