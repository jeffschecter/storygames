\n Everybody! I was cleaning out my basement and I found this great old D&D module from '78. Me and a few of my grade school friends used to play it at lunch. One time we were even playing it in the back of home room and Ms. Fleischer confiscated it. She gave it back\xa0at the end of the day. She said she'd thumbed through it and it looked like it was good for our vocabulary, but to leave it at home. Anyway, remember this one?

A. Suhaugin Gaol

Your party is trapped in a subterranean prison and your personal belongings are all gone! As you peer through the coral bars of your stockade you see a suhaugin jailer leaning sleepily against his guisarme. Through a portcullis beyond you can glimpse some sort of torture chamber.

Monsters: Suhaugin (1) Treasure: Lapis Lazuli medallion (25 g.p)

    \n B. Torture Chamber

    This area appears to have been carved out of a porous rock or some sort of coral. There are three live prisoners, and at least a dozen corpses in various stages of decay, their mouths open, their visages contorted in masks of unbelievable pain. Green-black, slimy vines or tentacles and the occasional eel slither in and out of holes in the floor, walls, and ceiling, moving over the bodies. The eels appear to be carrying some kind of species of off-white leech, parasites on their black, slimy skin. When the eels stop to feed on the prisoners, the leeches often detach themselves and attempt to enter their bodies through the nearest available orifice (mouth, eyes, ears, nose, genitals, anus, or any open wounds).

    At the far end of the chamber, there is an opening in the floor of the room: a hole in the ground that opens on an inky-black pool of water. Set into one of the walls is a massive door of rusted iron.

    Monsters: Torture eels and vine-tentacles (uncountable) Treasure: The leeches, if carried out of the dungeon alive, are valued at 10 g.p. a piece by Conclave alchemists.

    \n A(S). Postern Apparatus

    (secret panel in A)

    A small interstice on the wall of (A) hides the lever for the postern in (B).

    Monsters: 1d6 leeches from (B) make their home in the moist algae that grows around the apparatus. There is a 2 in 6 chance that they will attach to any member thrust into the cavity.

    \n E. The Scriptorium

    A small, surprisingly dry room, cluttered with battered, massive wooden desks and many lamps. Paper and dried ink and quills are present on all desks. A wooden scroll rack is against one wall. Everything in the room is very flammable -- if the room catches on fire, all treasure will likely be destroyed.

    Monsters: Skeletons in Monk's robes, armed with quill knives (daggers) (5)
    Treasure: 6 Illuminated Manuscripts (worth 1d6 G.P. each), 1 Palimpsest (Illuminated Manuscript written on top of cursed scroll, save vs. magic or be polymorphed into a giant leech), 1 1st level spell scroll.

    \n C. Medicine Antechamber
    \xa0
    A 10'x10' alcove etched into the southern wall of the hall immediately behind the rusted door reveals a lattice of shelves containing sandstone jars\xa0painted multitudinous colors.

    The jars hold various unguents and emolients intended to keep prisoners from dying too quickly. A careful and knowledgeable search will uncover the healing potions and a shark-kin skin scroll case with a vellum scroll inside. Written on the scroll in Aquan:

    "Use the others to wrest the location of the Iridescent Diadem from their leader. Capture their Essences in the phylactery conch for Her consumption. - Xuihcoatl"

    Treasure: Potion of Cure Moderate Wounds (2)

    \n F. Pit Pool

    A counter-balance trap in the corridor above drops unwary adventurers 20' down into a 10'x20'(x5' deep) pool in the middle of a 30'x50' room. The room has rough-hewn stone walls which are covered with a green-glowing lichen (save vs. poison when touched or become paralyzed for 1-4 rounds). There are no other exits from this room.

    Near the pool sits a half-starved Kuo-Toa Monitor armed with a whip and weighted net.

    Treasure: Medallion with an image of Blibdoolpoolp, made from a rare shell and adorned with pearls worth 500GP.
