    \n I am going to suggest that the Bay Area RPG scene is in some form of weird shift currently. While our MiniCons have gone gangbusters, I have intentionally cut them back from four to three this year. One of which will be co-sponsored by the Good Omens gaming group. By the end of last year, we were not only seeing less people offering to run games, several that were went back to more traditional type games. I also saw less player, and many committing to play only at the last minute.

    Why? I dunno. I could make some wild ass guesses. One possibly being that there was no "new hotness" toward the end of the year. We were seeing a lot of traction with titles in the SG community I would consider almost Evergreen (already), like SoC and Dogs...The second could be the amount of events happening in the area. If you look on the IPR retailer locator, we are one of five stores in California, with each one being within a 60(?) mile radius. EndGame along with Black Diamond run Minicons several times a year now. So, are we scratching that itch for Story Games that venues such as DDC are struggling to? I have been told on several occasions we are.We also make it really easy to show up for a four hour session, play, and get out of Dodge. That lack of structure really seems to appeal to this community (Damn Hippys!) Now, DDC will claim up and down they post what people submit, and when they run out of room, they stop accepting submissions. So, for number three, I might suggest that there aren't many SGs on the calendar because folks didn't step up to the plate fast enough. They are seriously impacted for space, have been for years, and have no desire to change any of it. So...for 2009, you wanna play there, jump in early and often and the disparity might start to shrink.

    yay!

    Ch
    EndGame

The Con gave us dedicated space for 4 days and pretty much a free hand in scheduling and using it. The Con was extremely supportive of the effort - best I've ever seen.

I'm happy to see others "colonize" as they'd like (and good luck) but you'll have to forgive me for getting completely turned off by the experience. It was very demoralizing - especially as other requests for help for the very same topic at other cons generated more enthusiastic response here during the same timeframe.
