\n The folk of Thunderfast are in desperate need. The dragon has been awakened, and the town is in danger.

This game is a very simple 1 round Engle Matrix Game. This post will explain the situation in Thunderfast. Post 2 explains how we\u2019ll use the Engle Matrix Game rules for this challenge. Post 3 is a list of characters you can choose from to complete the challenge. The goal of the challenge is to slay the dragon. My goal in posing this is to try out the suitability of Engle Matrix Games for resolving heroic fantasy challenges.

Thunderfast is a large town in the mountains on the border of a fantasy-Europe kingdom. It has the standard amenities and services, including an inn, blacksmith, temple (complete with resident healer), a small wizard\u2019s guild, and militia. The town is of primarily wood construction, behind a stout stockade, which is sufficient to deter the occasional brigands or goblin raiding party. The townsfolk mine coal from the hills and chop wood from the forests. Beyond Thunderfast is monster-filled wilderness.

Nearby Thunderfast peak is known to harbor a dragon\u2019s lair, but until recently the dragon had been sleeping for centuries. Some greedy miners started exploiting rich mineral veins on its slopes. Their digging opened the Labyrinth and awoke the Dragon, who flew out to Thunderfast to make his ultimatum: \u201cDeliver all your gold unto me in seven days, or I will burn Thunderfast to the ground.\u201d The people are terrified, and aren\u2019t even sure they have enough gold to satisfy the dragon. They\u2019ve offered the town treasury and their undying gratitude to any hero that can slay the dragon.

Thunderfast peak is a low, wooded mountain with a deep volcanic crater at its peak. The dragon lives in a natural cave at the bottom of the crater. The cave comprises several chambers, worn smooth by the Dragon\u2019s belly. The wrym itself sleeps (they say) atop a heap of coins, gems, and treasure from vanished kingdoms deep in the cave.

There are two barriers to approaching the cave: The crater\u2019s sides are loose skree slopes with little cover, allowing the dragon to easily detect approaching invaders. Beneath the cave is a treacherous labyrinth of stone and ice filled with pitfalls and winding passages.

The dragon is of enormous size, far stronger than any mortal and darkly cunning. It is greedy, vain, and cruel, and looks scorns all bipedal life. It can breathe gouts of magical flame. Its senses are as sharp as the keenest of men and its will is like iron. It has claws that can cut through steel and scales as hard as chainmail. A normal person cannot hope to defeat the dragon in an even fight. The whole town militia might, if it got very, very lucky. Even a hero would stand a less than even chance. To slay it will take more than walking up to it and whacking it in the head with a sword.

    \n To complete this challenge, choose a character (they are listed in the next post), then make an argument. An argument is a description of events and can include the actions your character takes, the consequences of those actions, the actions others take, their consequences, random events, and facts about the world. The referee (me), will rate the likelihood of your argument taking place on the following scale:\n
    • Can\u2019t miss: roll 6 6\u2019s in a row to fail
      • \n
    • Really likely: roll 2-6 in 6 to happen
      • \n
    • Pretty likely: roll 3-6 in 6 to happen
      • \n
    • Okay: roll 4-6 in 6 to happen
      • \n
    • Pretty unlikely: roll 5-6 in 6 to happen
      • \n
    • Really unlikely: roll a 6 in 6 to happen
      • \n
    • Impossible: roll 6 1\u2019s in a row to happen
      • \n
    • Then I\u2019ll roll the dice, and tell you if your argument occurs.
    \nAn argument will be rated as more likely if:\n
    • It\u2019s supported by facts already established in the situation
      • \n
    • It\u2019s reasonable in the context of the fiction
      • \n
    • It explains how barriers are bypassed
    \nAn argument will be rates as less likely if:\n
    • It contradicts facts established in the situation
      • \n
    • It\u2019s unreasonable in the context of the fiction
      • \n
    • It ignores barriers in the way
    \nYour first argument will receive a default rating of pretty likely.

    YOU GET TWO ARGUMENTS FOR THIS CHALLENGE. Your first argument regards how your character prepares to slay the dragon, and can include events up to, but not beyond, encountering the dragon. It can\u2019t include actually slaying the dragon. After the first set of arguments are judged, I\u2019ll make a new thread for the second set.

    I\u2019ll close this challenge after a few days, or when I get as many challengers as I think I can handle.

    Update: In the interest of keeping the challenge moving even if your argument fails, I'll propose a counter-argument. This is what happens if you're argument fails. It basically describes what happens when your argument fails at its' weakest point.

    Character 1: The Barbarian Warrior
    A wanderer of the wastes beyond civilization, the barbarian has fought and defeated many foes. Her strength, courage, endurance, and fighting prowess are unsurpassed by mortals. The barbarian is not subtle, but neither is she any fool. She fights for glory and to discover if there is any foe who can defeat her. All she owns are the clothes on his back and her great sword.

    Character 2: The Itinerant Wizard
    The Wizard has wandered a century amassing forgotten lore and arcane knowledge. Though weak of body, his intellect is formidable and he is deeply learned on every subject. He can work magic. Some of the most difficult magical feats he has ever performed include: producing a tower of flame from his palm, conjuring an iron anaconda to fight for him, and swaying a crowd to his will.

    Character 3: The Dark Elf Assassin
    Universally feared and mistrusted, the Assassin still has a reputation for delivering on every contract. She is lightning quick, dangerously, cunning, patient, and determined. She has trained endlessly with her bow, and rarely misses any humanly possible shot. A face-to-face battle against an armed opponent doesn\u2019t favor her light frame, so she prefers to strike from the shadows, a technique she has perfected.

    \n Irimitas considered the situation:

    1. 300 cubic yards of molten gold and other precious metals gathered behind the iron dam at the high-point of the cave.

    2. The narrow twisting tunnels of the entrance worn smooth by the worm's belly over hundreds of years.

    3. The beast itself, lithe and strong, covered in iron scales but with vulnerable wings.

    The situation itself was almost perfect: the tunnels provided the perfect conduit for the molten metal--little would be lost in cracks and crevices; it would be difficult for the creature to evade the flood and almost impossible for it turn around if caught at the right moment; its fiery breath and iron scales would provide no defenses.

    The searing flood pours over the dragon, burning its muzzle, searing its feet and a long swathe of its underbody, destroying its wings completely. The creature would probably die and die quickly, but there was no reason to leave things to chance, to allow the possibility that in its death throes it might destroy them.

    So, as the sluice gate is opened and the flood of gold unleashed, Irimitas stands apart from the his helpers, holding in his carefully gloved hands a beaten sheet of metal in the runnels of which a thin thread of gold trickles as he tilts and turns the sheet. He thinks back to another magic, his Iron Cobra, he'd conjured it, had he? Nonsense! Did they know nothing of magic? Rather, it had been constructed at great expense and animated by the powers of sympathy: like acts as like; do unto one, do unto the other. His brow furrows in concentration he moves the sheet, the gold dipped earlier from the molten pool turns, following the channels that are shaped just like the tunnels of the cave, and runs back again. Below the frantic strruggles of the wounded dragon are overwhelmed by the surging hiss of the molten flood, again and again. And Irimitas smiles...

The dragons flames blasted outwards in a fiery tornado of flames, melting the ice in front of the dragon, but failing to burn Jenna, deep under the icy water as she was. Not long after the dragons blast the remaining ice began to crack and sigh under the dragons immense weight, and it soon collapsed, sending the ancient wyrm into the frigid depths. The dragon did not have the benefit of having lived in winter for many years, and being a creature of fire that lived in a lava heated cave, was quite unused to such cold temperatures.

    Jenna swam back up to the surface, scurried up the dragons flailing neck and plunged her sword into its skull with all the force she could muster. A piercing shriek of agony was let loose by the dragon as the rest of its body was claimed by the icy pool, sinking to the dark depths of the lake. Jenna crawled to the passage that connected the lake to the rest of the cavern, totally soaked and freezing cold.

    "Well at least now I can call myself "dragon slayer" she said with a chuckle "although I've faced human foes greater than that before. I suppose I shall have to keep seraching in order to find a foe that is truly worthy of my skill."

Time and again she has employed such techniques. The trick is in the set-up. If it were not for the special nature of her quarry, she could finalize this contract wither her eyes closed.

Instead it will seek to quench the rage that the pain causes. And so, when the dragon first wheels into view and flies into range, bent on the destruction of the town, The Mosquito lets an arrow fly into the dragon's right eye. As if on cue, it screams in fiery anguish, turning to hunt whatever would dare to wound her so. To do so, the dragon must bring the left eye around to direct at the shadows in which The Mosquito hides. And just as that happens, the other arrow -- The Sting, emerges from the shadow and is buried deep within the head of the now blind, falling and dying dragon. Soon it is over.

    The Mosquito can recover her Sting and that of the dragon's treasure that she desires and on she moves. It is not her problem that the region around the town of Thunderfast will be plagued for a thousand years by monsters crawling from the pool of dragon's blood that soaked into the ground where the ancient beast fell.
