    Buncha things:

    1) Having scheduled slot is a great idea! Without them, only groups that start together finish together, and thus get "stuck" playing with the same people for game after game. Not torture, but certainly sub-optimal.

    2) I'll run a playtest of Foresworn, my space opera game-in-development. Maybe something easy like InSpectres. Maybe something that involves more work, like Sorcerer. And if Judd's running Mu, I want a seat. Like Clinton, we're gonna have to leave by 1pm on Sunday.

    3) It's my understanding that we'll be cooking primarily over charcoal grills and/or campfires, right? My years as a Boy Scout tell me that always takes longer than you expect. F'r instance, for games to start at 8, lots of folks will want to have coffee by 7:30. Which means you've got to put it on by 7:00, which means you've got to start the fire between 6 adn 6:30. While I volunteer to get the fire started each morning, there will be similar time demands at lunch time and dinner time.

    Perhaps schedule only two slots on Saturday, with a several-hour swatch in the middle for pick-up games, chatting, hiking, food prep, etc. Something like this:

    Saturday Morning 10-2
    Saturday Afternoon 2-6 for unstructured time
    Saturday Evening 6-10

    Or, if we rely on mostly cold cereal for breakfast, cold cuts or something uncooked for lunch, and only cook dinner, we could let the late-risers have their unstructured-time in the morning by skipping the first slot like this:

    Saturday Morning 7-11
    Saturday Afternoon 12-4
    Saturday Evening 6-10

    Just my $.02
