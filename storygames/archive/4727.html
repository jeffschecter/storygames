I've made mention of this before, I guess, but I'm a stingy, stingy bastard.

\n

Well, it isn't exactly true. Let's just say that due to economic limitations, priorities in my life and the limited spending power of the average Malaysian consumer, I don't have a lot of money to blow. At any one time, whenever things are good, I have twenty (US) dollars to spend; hence, the title of this discussion.

\n

This is, seriously, a hard limit; as much as it breaks my heart to do it, I can't get Burning Wheel because the damn game costs 25 USD, and this is all before shipping and handling.

\n

And yes, I can get The Shadow of Yesterday for 14.99 as a PDF, but... I've managed to score a copy of the TSoY rules, and I want to run it with a bunch of people I know before I commit to buying the whole shebag. Sorry, Clinton.

\n

Oh, and I've already spent my last two twenties to get a copy of David Gilbert's The Blackwell Legacy and Blackwell Unbound.

\n

At any rate, apart from the restrictions I've talked about so far, here are more:

\n
    \n
  • I need to be able to 'sell' this to my friends. While I find the concepts of Shock and Shab al-Hiri Roach fun, I don't know if I can leverage my rather lousy persuasive skills on the people I know. The premises need to be relatively simple and straightforward, and definitely on the "beer-and-pretzels" range of games.
    • \n
  • I'm not only stingy, I'm lazy. I'm talking about maybe a couple of hours available every week for prep time, here.
    • \n
  • While I'd love campaign-based play, I don't expect it to happen. So, at most, the game needs to be played and mostly concluded (or at least can be stopped) in one session. Spending one session just making characters? Not gonna happen.
    • \n
\n

So. Yeah. I have Twenty Bucks, Not a Whole Lot of Time and Energy, and All of You Lovely People.

    \n I'm pretty predictable on this. I'd recommend Lacuna. 15 dollars, last time I looked, for a print copy.

    Little to no prep time. Grabby setting to sell to your friends: you're Mystery Agents descending into a shadowy mindworld to hunt out undesirables. If they still look doubtful, tell them it's The Matrix RPG (which it can be). Simple mechanics. Fast character creation and playable in one session.

    Fits all your requirements, I think.

    Also, you could take a look at Rich Stokes' Umlaut. It's an ashcan, so fairly cheap, but it's a solid game last time I played.

    \n Yeah, with regards to TMW, if it sounds too dark, it probably is. I mean, we had a lot of laughs and I don't think we were really hitting "deep emotional issues", but there is PVP tension, lots of ominous imagery, and a lot of characters end up dying. I mean, it's like playing a samurai movie, so Yojimbo or Seven Samurai, with all the blood and backstabbing that implies.

    I don't know if there's a preview, but I can tell you that the text is incredibly well written and clear, and the game's very, very simple to teach. However, there's no getting around the tone. If in doubt, go for something else.
