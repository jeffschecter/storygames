\n This is a thread where you can tell us about any games that you're currently developing or playtesting. It's an opportunity to connect with potential playtesters.

Principles for posting:

* Tell us about your game(s) in one post. Feel free to mention your game even if you've talked about it in last month's thread. The idea is to inform us about what you're doing now.

* Optionally: tell us what you're hoping to achieve this month or what you did last month.

* If you want to link to another thread, post, or rules document, go for it.

* There's no maximum number of games you can talk about. If developing your game is a genuine focus for you this month, then put it up.


If you're responding to a game someone else has posted about:

* Number your response (first response: [1]; second response [2]; third response [3]). If you see that you're the second person to post, "Wow, that looks cool!" to a post here, then put a "[2]" at the top of your post.

* The fourth person to post should just take their comment and create a new thread (or talk on an existing thread about the game), to continue the discussion.

    So I decided to try my hand at making a more crunchy storygame that pushes risk. More to practice design than any feeling that I am making "the next big thing". Hopefully it is worth continuing. Here are the main design goals:

    - Have a 'feel' that encourages risk taking. (Anti 'death-spiral', rewarding pushing yourself)
    - Have the mechanics encourage and guide good storytelling, even if the group is new.
    - The mechanics should try not to pull the player 'out of character', or force actions.
    -Symmetric Mechanics for most social and physical conflicts
    - Allow players to participate even when their characters can't (working on it)
    - Keep cognitive load low during play.
    - Have character creation be relatively simple, and introduce things as the players are ready.
    - Keep it easy to gm, and provide decent flow for doing so (working on it).
    - Make it so 'minmaxing' the game leads to a better storyline.


    I would really appreciate any feedback.
