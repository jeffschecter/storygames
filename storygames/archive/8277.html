\n The roof the roof the roof is on fire
The roof the roof the roof is on fire
The roof the roof the roof is on fire
We don't need no water let the mother*ucker burn
Burn mother*ucker burn

- traditional Skandik war chant

Our 4E group just wrapped up a chapter of the "Sven Chronicles" and I'll be DM'ing the next chapter. One of the players, Nate Marcel, is an artist who sometimes draws these great pictures of some of the ingame action. I was in another D&D campaign with him years ago and he actually drew a series of comic books based on our adventures, cool artifacts that I'll always treasure.

Anyway, the characters had just about had it with all the goblins in the dungeon. One of the player characters had died at their hands, along with a series of NPC "red shirts." Once the party succeeded finally in slaying the villainous traitor at the dungeon's heart, they piled all the furniture, crates, and torture chamber implements into the "rat hole" pit in the dungeon's first room, doused it with oil and set it alight.

This is the party grimly returning to their longship for further adventures. My character, Harek Bjornsson in the foreground, broods over the men that were lost on this adventure. Nate's character, Vengarr, slings his battleax and considerable booty on his back. The "witch-men" from the south and west, Katsu and Anu bring up the rear.

    \n Reaching the second level of the Keep on the Shadowfell, the party bluffed their way past the hobgoblin guards saying they had an expected meeting with their leader. Before they left the "foyer" room, they could see a fearsome-looking giant spider in a cage in an adjoining room. Two hobgoblins escorted them towards another room nearby, one with a well, and told them to "wait here." One of the hobgoblins left, presumably to speak with their leader.

    The party members looked at one another knowingly. This was going to go all pear-shaped in a hurry.

    Once his partner was out of sight and hopefully earshot, the party rushed their escort. They failed to hurt him much, never mind drop him, ruining our chances at a stealthy gutting. My character bull-rushed him into the nearby well. Our smiles faded as he yelled "Release the Spider!!!" (in Goblin this sounds something very like "Releash dah Shpiedah!!!")

    Nate's character, our walrus-mustachio'd cleric-cook, Thom Thom, happened to know Goblin and tried a ruse; imitating the screaming hobgoblin to his best ability, he shouted back towards the chamber we'd come from, "Don't release the spider quite yet!!!"

    After we stopped laughing and wiped the tears from our eyes, the bloody melee was on...

    \n Skandiks fear no man nor goblinoid. But this thing was just... unnatural.

    We ran away only once in the Keep on the Shadowfell. In a strangely clean alcove Vengarr went to investigate some barrels and was immediately absorbed into this horror. It looks small perhaps, but Vengarr is over seven feet tall! It was only Katsu's magiks pushing the cube back and Anu's maelstrom of fey power immobilizing the gelatinous beastie that enabled us to escape after we all helped pull Vengarr out!
