    \n Not supernatural in any way, but I highly recommend the Harry Palmer movies featuring Michael Caine, starting in the '60s with The Ipcress File and two other films, and picking up again in the '90s with Bullet to Beijing and Midnight in St. Petersburgh. Emphasizes the mundane thrills of the spy trade rather than the over-the-top Bond variety.

    \nThe Invisibles! The comic book series, with the Grant Morrison and the Cthulhu Mythos and the conspiracies and the postmodern! How is it that it hasn't come up yet? It is about as perfect an example of supernatural espionage fiction as you could ask for. If you have your brain bleach handy, you might also "enjoy" (or even enjoy*, but probably not) Morrison's work in The Filth.

    * You know how in 30 Rock, Tracy Jordan's Oscar-bait movie was called Hard to Watch? The Filth might as well be subtitled Hard to Read. It tries very, very hard and is effectively self-parodying, which was likely Morrison's intent.

    \n And now I'm on a roll. Add Millenium for sure - and perhaps Evangelion? - to your TV list. Murakami's Hard-Boiled Wonderland and the End of the World might be a good inspirational book. The Technocracy supplements for Mage: The Ascension - especially the Guide to the Technocracy - are excellent RPG resources.

    I should mention at this point that I've spent years running games in this subgenre, making especially extensive use of Delta Green. I loves it. I loves it good.

    \n The aforementioned Barry Eisler's John Rain series makes a wonderful job of describing the ins and outs of surveillance and countersurveillance - the techniques, the mindset, the psychological cost of avoiding normal life. I've only read the first two but my roleplaying those skills has changed forever.

    Both Night's Black Agents and the upcoming Delta Green standalone RPG will have tradecraft writeups, both by Kenneth Hite.
