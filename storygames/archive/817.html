[Console] Turn-taking mechanics

Colin_Fredericks
June 2006 edited June 2006 in Story Games
During my vacation in Tanzania, I had plenty of time to think about game mechanics. Since Console III is a story-telling game, I need a way to let people take turns telling parts of the story, interrupt each other without stomping all over the story, etc. Here's what I came up with.

At the start of the game session, one person (the expert for the current region) has "the controller." This can be represented by any object; ideally, it's an actual physical game controller, like from an NES. That person starts telling the story for that session. Whomever has the controller is called the "narrator."

Narrators start with five minutes of time that is theirs and theirs alone. Others may request to interrupt (see below), but the narrator doesn't have to let them break in, even to activate role abilities.

To interrupt, the other players hold up their hand in different ways: one finger says you want to interject a quick comment or put a detail into the scene, two fingers says you want to speak for a particular character ("v for voice"), and the whole hand says you're requesting the controller. It's up to the narrator whether you get to do any of the above before the 15-minute mark.

Players can also hold up a closed fist, which *must* be allowed to break in any time after the initial five minutes have passed. This means you're activating a character's Role Ability (see previous post). The narrator can decide whether he or the activator gets to describe what happens because of the role ability.

After fifteen minutes, the narrator *must* turn the controller over to someone else, who then becomes the new narrator. The old narrator gets a token. You can't give the controller to someone with a token, though people with tokens can still break in with quick comments, voices, and character abilities. If a narrator passes the controller before the 15-minute mark, it still yeilds a token. Once everyone has a token, everyone loses their tokens. In short, everyone gets a turn.

I *think* it will work, but I need to playtest it. If anyone has ideas, comments, or stuff that I missed, let me know. I may also fiddle with the times.

--Colin
