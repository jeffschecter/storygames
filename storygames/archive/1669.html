      Editorial on the nature of a story:

      I think that to qualify as a six-word story, it first has to be a story. At the most basic level, it has to recount something that happened. Better, it should demonstrate some kind of change in situation or a change in reaction or emotion in a character. It doesn't have to tie everything up, but it should at least leave the reader guessing what happened.

      And it should go without saying, all this is strictly in my opinion, not any kind of authoritative definition of "story."
