    \n Brennan has many APs logged on his Galileo Games site if you want examples. The legendary game of Flaming Taft (We're getting the band back together) was run with Mortal Coil and I found my character (Crash) sheet last week. It is remarkably low on information for a game that was so involved but that's the beauty of the theme document where you record everything that you colloboratively create. It's not a difficult step but it's a real launchpad into adventure.

    MC also formalises world creation in play in a simple but effective way.

    There are now several campaign frames, five I think, written by Ken Hite, Brennan Reece, Julia, Brennan Taylor and me. It's all quality material at only $2 a pop if you want some inspiration.

    I've played in games in New Amsterdam (alternate NY punk magic), Hogwarts, Twisted 50s, hermetic Cambridge University (1500s), as Neanderthals, in Nazi occupied Channel Islands. They have all been good, if not excellent.

    \n I'm playing in a campaign of it right now.

    I haven't been totally satisfied with it. I thought that the campaign creation phase worked reasonably well, but after that the mechanics haven't provided a lot of support past "pay points to introduce cool stuff." The point-paying mechanics mean that there is somewhat balanced input to the magical and other facts of the game - i.e. one player can't come up with everything. However, for my group the various point pool mechanics haven't inspired or driven play much.

    Play has been pretty cool, though, as we work out various mysteries for dabbling college students in early 1990s small town Maine. Our mutual friend Alice disappeared, and we have been dealing with the our various families which include the sheriff, a preacher, and a witch.

    \n I like it quite a bit. I agree with others who have enjoyed the "paying points to define things" aspect of the game, but the characters' Passions are also really important and are really there to drive play. The conflict system is a lot more interesting than people give it credit for, with lots of rooms for intermediate, non-binary outcomes.
