    \n Yes, @davey's group, RPG Workshop, was spun off from SGS, and the membership is heavily bent towards the "story game" variety, but it is open to all tabletop role-playing games. SGS was explicitly only GMless games, and when we spun up RPGW we wanted to include GM'd games. We didn't want to dilute the mission of SGS by calling ourselves something like "Story Games Workshop," so we went with RPGW instead.*

    The new Narrative Games Northwest (mentioned by @horn_head_o above) is the direct progeny of SGS and its first meet-up is next week Thursday (12 April.)

    The membership of both groups is very intertwined; one might even call us "Sister Orgs" -- unofficially.

    * We've discussed changing the name because of the connotations of what "RPG" might or might not mean to different people, but for now RPGW we remain.

    \n About a gazillion gamers have come out of Story Games Seattle. There are hordes of them and they want to play, so it would be very surprising if all sorts of new groups and events did not arise in all shapes and sizes.

    Part of the reason I stopped Story Games Seattle, rather than just handing it off to someone else, was to make space for new things and see what would emerge. Maybe something even better than what we had.
