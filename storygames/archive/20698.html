\n So I wanted a Sc-fi space game and wasn't finding anything I liked, so when I found AW and its hacks I was pretty excited. Finally I just made my own.

I made all playbooks, all the moves, and I have it posted on the AW world forums and the files are here, So I guess what I am asking is how to progress from here? Have playtests, gather feedback sure but how do I do that?

Planet of the Week is a hack of AW and Monster of the week heavily influenced by television and movie tropes much like MoTW. Its got familiar modern and futuristic character archetypes. I have retained the class structure of MoTW (simple and straightforward) and the streamlined storytelling of AW. The game play is focused on working as a crew (with complications) going from planet to planet in a harsh universe. Much like the dirty feel of Alien and Firefly, the adventure of Farscape and Stargate SG1, and a little of the exploration instartrek. More gritty than space opera-y, but not necessarily serious either.

    \n I have indeed played my share of AW-derived games, in fact I introduced several of my friends to the system. I didn't mean to imply I was a complete noob, I have looked at a lot of posts and other hacks but I never thought of play testing new games that's a good suggestion!

    I guess I was looking more for a what are the steps to proceed from a hack I have only played at my house to fully fledged game. Are there better or worse ways to give out the material, pitfalls I should avoid. How do you select playtesters? How do you generate interest and where should I post to seek help (other than here and AW forums).
    If I plan to publish at some point should I hold stuff back? Should I make a blog dedicated to the game?

    I had a feeling I was being to vague before and that is my bad, I'm trying to not overshare, but I think simply feeling uncertain about things and looking for a little direction to shore up my nerves.

    I read the "be specific" advice and yet I'm still kinda ignoring it. But let me try:

    I have everything I need to begin playtesting, and have done a little with my friends, but I don't know enough people who game to get enough feedback. So I'm posting my game online and want to continue to develop it.

    What is my next step?

    \n Unless you're very lucky, you're unlikely to get many playtesters just by putting the game out there.

    I've found that running an in-development game at conventions is a good way to both introduce people to what is fun about it, and also to find a few people who are keen to play it themselves after.
