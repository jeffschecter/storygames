City of Birds on Lulu

Jasper
September 2006 edited September 2006 in Directed Promotion
The fitful process of going from "game done" to "game published" is complete once more. (And I'm reminded of why I took a long break from it after I released Trials of the Grail...although I learned a lot by doing TotG, which made this go a lot simpler.) In short: City of Birds is available on Lulu.

Now, I was trying for quite a while to figure out what to do with this game and how to get it out to people. It's a game with a message, so my playing it with people is cool but not quite the point. In the end, I opted to sell it because (1) the game and my effort are certainly worth some money and (2) that helps it look like a serious game, which => people buy it => people read it => people play it.

The only catch is it's a very short text, which brought issues of pricing sharply into focus. I actually compiled a big Excel spreadsheet of all the Indie games I could find, including their price, length, and size. I then adjusted for different sizes, by estimating total square inches of paper, and found the equivalent letter page length. That let me see how much games were going for per page. Varies a fair bit, of course. Books over 50 pages generally cost about .20 to $.30 a page, while shorter books hovered around .50 to .60.

I guess this might have given a ballpark figure...but City of Birds is shorter than anything else I saw on Lulu or IPR at just 27 pages. Projections from the trendline would have set it at $17+, which even I howled it. So in the end, I used some of the "rules of thumb" people have posted, and my own sense of what people would be content (not happy) seeing. Then I had to decide on a PDF price. That drove home the truth behind pricing: you're paying for the game, not the paper and ink. So the PDF is $7 and the print (once I get a proof) will be $15. Both are slightly on the low side by some estimates, I'm sure, but I'm content that they're reasonable.

Oh, one reason the print edition is actually a fair sum is that I decided to go for full color interior. At only 27 pages, most of the cost is binding anyway, so it's not the enormous deal it is for larger projects. There aren't illustration, but full color allows for blue headings and a decorative sidebar -- and white paper. I think the heading colors are important, so the book may as well be "perfect" and up to all snuff. Spot color printing would have been better, I guess, and you can scold me for using lulu, indeed, but I like them and now live in the same town. Anyway, I expect most sales to be PDF, since the thing is short enough to print comfortably at home.

That's my story. See you in the City.

    Thanks, Johnathan. Jason, ask and ye shall receive: Excel spreadsheet.

    Looking at it all again, I actually found a pretty simple way of getting good predictions/suggestions:

    - First, If your book's not letter size (8.5x11), estimate it's length if it were. (You can take height x width x page count, and then divide by 93.5.)
    - Start with a base cost of $13 and add $0.08 per page.
    - For PDF price, take 60% of that.

    For most games published recently, this formula is within 20% of real prices. It matches my decided cost for City of Birds, $15, and also reaffirms several opinions: Burning Wheel is underpriced (suggestion is $37), as probably are Unsung and Fastlane ($16/$17). Of course, this doesn't take into account artwork, layout quality, etc., nor any particular marketing requirement (i.e., Luke breaking into the fantasy market). But it may give an okay ballpark figure.
