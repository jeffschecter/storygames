\n [You know the drill - short and sweet.]

First time playing Microscope, two experienced players and one completely new to RPGs. All grad students, so numerous references to critical theory and philosophy. It was great!

Humanity encounters a race of fungaloid aliens who reproduce by merging with other species. There is peace and prosperity for a time as humanity expands into the stars with help from their new symbiotic allies, but problems with spore-enhanced agriculture lead to mass starvation and war. Eventually, the human species as we know it is extinct, living on in an evolved, hybrid fungaloid form... but is it sustainable?

Standout scene: a talk show featuring the first human-fungaloid union, speaking as one and introducing humanity to both the beauty and the horror of total entanglement with another entity. It was incredible the way the game allowed us to avoid ever fixing the aliens as Bad Guys or Good Guys - just agents in the process of history.

    \n [Usagi Yojimbo]
    A roadside duel, pride is wagered against coin.

    The spearman springs close enough to employ the reach of his weapon, striking hard enough to end the duel and then deftly pulling back, but the unarmed samurai grits his teeth and leaps towards the spearman, negating his retreat. Giant hands strike the shaft of the spear, cracking it audibly.

    The spearman takes a step back, trying to focus. The samurai closes ground, and the spearman takes an additional step back, expecting a rest. The unarmed samurai lowers his shoulders and recklessly attacks again, smashing the weakened spear with his huge fists. It finally gives, splintering jagged into at least two pieces. The unarmed man reels for breath from the attack.

    If this were a fight for life, that opening would be enough for the spearman to put his shortsword through the other samurai, but... instead, the spearman drops to his knees, saying nothing. A packet of coin falls to the ground, where the unarmed samurai takes it and tosses it to his travelling companion.

    \n The Big Night, with 5 and 7 yr-olds.

    The Bunny crashes the flying reindeer through an upstairs window. While she's distracting the sleepy woman in the house, the Teddy hangs by his feet into the broken window to throw a present into the house.

    Later, while the Bunny is trying to get the reindeer away from the police before they put him in the zoo, the Teddy is punching out ghosts at a haunted house. The third ghost asks "Did you bring me a present?"

    Teddy's player: I give the ghost a present.

    Me: Remember, you're carrying a present for the kids that live at this house. What will you give the ghost?

    Teddy's player: I make him a train.

    Me: What will you use to make a train? You're in the living room of the kids' house.

    Teddy's player: I use the kids' blocks to make him a train.

    \nThe Queen's Cavaliers.

    Players are in HMS Fury, an airship hovering over the crash of another airship on the side of a mountain, deep in the frozen wastelands of Talania. They rappel down to investigate the wreck.

    Suddenly... Varendish Snow Troopers armed with muskets come skiing down the slopes toward them! Cue the James Bond music!

    The courtier/mechanician grabs the controls of the Fury -- despite her not knowing how to drive it -- and attempts to pilot it to a better position from which to attack the Snow Troopers with her clockwork pistol, over the objections of the captain of the Fury, a privateer/witch who was on the ground investigating the crash site. He leaps onto the ropes and starts pulling himself up as the Snow Troopers open fire.

    The mechanician manages to not crash the ship, the captain gets on the deck, and fires the cannon. BOOM! Triggering an avalanche that wipes out the remaining snow troopers -- and the rest of the PCs barely escape by leaping for the other dangling ropes as the airship pulls away from the mountain.

    \n We just about wrapped Act One of Eternal Lies (Trail of Cthulhu). One of the investigators, Martin, had been deeply in love with a man named Jeremiah, who'd been killed about a year before the game started. Martin is self destructive, has a drive of Ennui, and a Pillar of Sanity of Pleasure is the Greatest Good. He'd hit some bad things that were a really, really bad combination with all of the above and been dragged away from them by his friends and fellow investigators, Joyce and Lillian, who had learned some unpleasant truths of which he was unaware.

    Martin: Tell me why I shouldn't go back until I die.

    Lillian: Because it killed Jeremiah.

    There was a long pause while the player looked absolutely stricken and we just let it sit there for a moment.

    Martin's player: This is the sound of a Pillar of Sanity shattering.

    Lillian: That's why.

    \n In Open the Pod Bay Door, just before the air runs out, the Lieutenant Commander, in all her wisdom, remembers that they have a can of spare O2 on the Snow Day. Unfortunately, it's on the outside of the hull and will take longer to retrieve than they have air. She heads out the airlock anyway.

    Meanwhile, on the bridge, as a last ditch effort, Lancer the pilot decides to crash the Snow Day into the Appleseed, currently controlled by the Homicidal Robot B.I.L. As testament to his piloting skills, he manages to punch through the hull, land the pod and everyone but Ruhan the computer expert, who fried himself earlier, survives.

    But, before they can celebrate, B.I.L fires up the Appleseed's internal defence system and projectiles hammer the Snow Day's hull. In two turns the projectiles will penetrate the hull and kill everyone aboard...
