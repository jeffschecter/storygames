    \n For me, I think the idea of force-ranking (without comments of quality, though perhaps a column indicating if it was fulfilled or not) is fine: But I would do it by number of backers, not funding amount; which would put Hillfolk and Dungeon World above Tenra, and Fate Core on top.

    Pretty much any angle you judge success by (other than raw idle dollars collected; but don't get me wrong, I've backed Numanera pretty deep too), Fate Core is the current leader.

    Support, backer ownership, and mutual respect is far more important than the bottom line of number of dollars raised IMO.

    \n It interests me as a gauge of "what we thought we'd raise" versus "what the community was actually willing to raise", although, yeah, sometimes the numbers are going to be screwy. There's no way the Fate guys only thought they'd raise 3 grand, for instance - I think they had stretch goals for the first $30,000 out in the first day, and there's no way they could have pulled that stuff together without having planned it earlier.

    I like it because it's an explicitly empirical, measurable metric - there's no real way to measure something like "backer satisfaction", especially not as someone on the outside of the campaign. It's definitely not the most important thing, in any definition, but if you're looking at it from the outside it's not a bad place to start.
