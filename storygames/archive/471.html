[Agora] Something More Interesting than "Crit Success"

Josh Roby
April 2006 edited April 2006 in Story Games
So in Agora, there's a sort-of critical success when you roll a one -- you wipe out your opponent's current position and force them to reshuffle resources to recover. You also lose the die that rolled a one -- it either goes into Fallout and rewrites your Ideals or goes into Burnout and decreases the value of one of your Resources. It's all sorts of flash-and-bang phyrric victory craziness. Only thing is, "critical success" seems kind of lame to describe that, especially when there's no critical failure anywhere in the game.

So I turn unto you, wise sages of Story Games. I petition you for suggestions for what to call Agora's "crit-succ".
