    \n My favorite thing about Knudepunkt last year was meeting so many brilliant people. SO many. Everyone I spoke to was motivated, energized, interesting, and had lots of knowledge and/or ideas about their chosen fields. (Except, perhaps, the naked drunks who pissed on our shoes and interrupted some highly-focussed events - but to me, they were an important part of the experience).

    It's not like a con. You don't meet people talking about their character, or people who just want to hang out and play games and not think so much about them. You do, however, meet opinionated bastards, many of which are more than able to defend their opinions.

    It can also be scary. The people here are the cream of the cream. If there's an elite in role-playing, in both positive and negative ways, this is it. You meet people who have arranged events gathering thousands of people over a decade. People who have created and developed whole new forms of play. People who don't care if you tear apart their work, because they've already done so themselves, and are recreating it for the umpteenth time. Academics. Professors. Charismatic and beautiful people. Alien people.

    Ah, fuck. I'm going all fan-boy again, and I'd promised myself not to do that. Well, whatever.
