\n So. I'm Running a MonsterHearts game set in a British Manor house in the 1920s. It's been really fun so far. One of the Players is playing a serpentine whose landed noble family has fallen on hard times recently, and whose father is now sort of pushing him into an insane revenge plot against the newly rich and generally doing all the sort of horribleness that a Serpentine's family should.

The Wyrm (the aforementioned Newly Rich) showed the Serpentine his collection of treasures (Jazz Records) and the Serpentine Fixated on Victoria Spivey's Black Snake Blues. He got the record from the Wyrm, but his father found it, called it N**r filth unfit to a person of the Serpentine's station, and smashed it.

The player has decided to take "Nest of Humans" as their next advance. Pretty sure it's going to be a Jazz band. With an African-American Singer as the highlight NPC within that band, and a potential love interest. So the question is this: Should that NPC have a snake motif? Because on the one hand, the presence of such a motif calls out the NPC, and the band as a whole, as the escape route/new family for the Serpentine. On the other hand, the absence highlights the difference between his potentially new family and his old one. After all, the advance is "nest of humans", not "nest of snakes"
