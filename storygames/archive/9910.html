    \n I make a reading in human entrails...

    Hm. Seriously?
    I found out that for me I need to have as much input as possible, from many different sources. And over time, it coalesces, a week, a month...

    So when I'm "gearing up" creatively I spend a couple of weeks watching TV series, watching the news, listening to the radio, reading books, comics, watching anime, just surfing the net.
    And then, usually, I have ideas.

    I often though have "Really good books", which I don't necessarily mean they are "good", but "full of ideas out of the wazoo". So when I go and watch TV, I put them on the chair next to mine. I open them and read a page here, a page there. And I soak them up, even if I don't read much of them; I soak up in their spirit.
    But it's less useful when I'm aiming for a general inspiration.

    \n When I'm at an ebb, I realize I need to "fill up" I do stuff I enjoy for a few days, usually some reading, video games, or TV watching.

    I've also got an "inspiration shelf" in my living room that I've been slowly adding to. It's got some art books (Perugino, Bosch, Yerka), technique books (calligraphy, drafting), game books (Suppressed Transmissions, Talislanta 4th edition), and random junk (Whole Earth Catalog, lots of books about maps, Penguin guides to history). I go there for ideas (I spent 45 minutes looking through it this morning for new ideas for the Principia cover).

    Then I've got a file with about 400 pieces of art I've found through the Web over the years. It's got star maps, weird stuff, Stuff found on Flickr, weird history, chart porn, you name it.

    Everyone should keep a file like that IMHO. Collect the stuff that inspires you and you'll always have inspiration.

    \n I find that travel really helps charge my creative batteries. Getting out of your everyday environment can really help shake your brain up and get you thinking. I only get overseas maybe once every 2-3 years, but you can get a lot out of a trip closer to home too. I like to drive to the ocean sometimes, hang out on the beach, poke around in old antique and bookstores.

    GPS-related activities like Geocaching, Geohashing, and letterboxing are great ways to get you to some random area close to home that you would have never thought of going to. Really helps get your mind working.

    Another thing that works for me, and this is going to sound odd, is going to the airport. I just go there, grab some coffee and maybe a magazine, and sit in the waiting area. Sometimes I free-write for a while, sometimes I sketch people or airplanes, sometimes I just watch and listen to the people around me. There's a lot of energy there - everyone is going somewhere to do something. Usually I come out of it with some ideas.
