\n Everyone! Please be aware that the selection process for choosing judges for the ENnies Awards 2008 is going on NOW!

So that the judges can be announced at GenCon, so that publishers can hand off their products at GenCon, so that the Judges have an entire year to read the materials (and maybe for more reasons than that), Judges are going through the election/selection process as we speak.

If you are interested, here are some links for you:

http://www.ennieawards.com/voting.html#2008

    \n It's not that I don't care, it's that I don't express my caring here.

    I'd also hasten to point out that a community cares more when it has more viable candidates. Simply put, the "indie"/story game side of the house doesn't, because publishers can't be judges -- that's Ennie policy. And given that the indie ethic is "everyone can publish a game", it's not too shocking. So we have to rely on folks from the other "mainstream" communities who are also keeping an eye on what's developing in the small press scene to give us a fair shake. Jeramy and Chris definitely did that this year, and they have my backing.
