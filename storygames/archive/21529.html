    \n The basic idea is that it is a versatile move you can call on when a character is trying to do something under tremendous pressure. Can they keep it together enough to pull it off?

    A classic example might be trying to sing a song in front of the whole school, while your crush is watching.

    It could also be trying to keep the car on the road after the local gang leader blew out one of your tires, though.

    \n I believe all Monsterhearts moves are handled the same way on a "miss": the MC makes a move (a Reaction, I believe, it's called in the 2nd Edition).

    Generally, I would make a move which represents the situation: whatever the source of pressure was in that scene, it comes to pass or turns out in a way entirely against the character's interests.

    \n Even the most mundane characters sometimes need to keep their cool, so it would make no sense for failure to automatically trigger a character's Darkest Self (which, on the other hand, has very specific triggering conditions that don't contemplate overrides).
    The Darkest Self would be a rare, extreme and normally indirect occurrence within a wide range of appropriate consequences (Conditions, Harm, Strings, etc.)

    \n Yes. As a general rule, I would never activate a PC's Darkest Self - let the other rules make that happen when it does, because doing so has such immense impact on the player's experience of their character.

    As (I seem to remember) Avery says in the book, that should be a rare occurence... but there may be a moment in play where it's just clear to *everyone* that the Darkest Self should happen, and that's when you may consider doing so as the MC.

    I think that, personally, even under those circumstances I would prefer to frame it as a choice. "The only way out of this mess is to give in to the monster within you. Do you want to do that, embrace that dark aspect of your self? Or will you hold on to your humanity and take the blow that's coming?"
