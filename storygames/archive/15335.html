    \n Almost didn't go. Glad I did!

    Quoting Michael Miller:
    http://stalwart1000.wordpress.com/2011/11/07/metatopia-2011-playtesters-paradise/\n
    I was skeptical before we went. By the time we left, I was a convert.
    \nBut I wasn't there as a designer, so I can't speak to that side of things. As a player I enjoyed myself and probably the most important review I can give... I will go again next year!

    Seminars I went to:
    - Playtesting Best Practices
    - Resource Management (working with artists, graphic designers, printers, budgets)
    - Marketing Your Game
    - Remixology (hacking existing games)
    - An Introduction to Kickstarter run by employees from Kickstarter!
    - The Game Design Roundtable
    - The Independent Gaming Roundtable
    - Game Mechanics
    - Talking to the Talent

    Games I playtested:
    - "Trouble For Hire" by Kevin Allen Jr
    - "Tangled Fates" by Kat Miller.
    - "The New World; Annals of Allohistory, Volume 1" by Bill White
    - "HorrorScope; Survive!" by Bradley Eggleston

    Random thoughts / observations:
    - Vinny bent over backwards to accommodate as many people as he could... we need to clone this man!
    - The convention size was intimate yet not too small. Made new friends and had great talks with friends I don't see enough.
    - I love the hotel. Free hot chocolate is awesome! Great gym, good food close by, easy access by public transportation, reasonable room rates.
    - Kevin Allen Jr knows how to make a seminar implode itself!
    - Several game designers didn't want to run their games multiple times after earlier session showed them all the problems they would rather fix first.
    - Having Sunday start at 10am instead of 9am is a great move! We stay up way too late Saturday night hanging out so the extra hour of sleep was crucial.
    - To attend as a player all 3 days is only... $20!
    - In a few cases, it wasn't exactly clear who was organizing / running / moderating individual seminars. It almost always worked out but I think a little bit more clarity might help. To be fair, seminars are new to the Double Exposure family of conventions. As a first effort it was much better than expected.
    - I think game designers might benefit from a pre-convention "playtesting techniques" and "how to get feedback" seminars. For example, everyone should steal Kevin Allen Jr's feedback process.
    - I was very impressed by Fred Hicks and Robert Donoghue's seminar powers! I've enjoyed their seminars in the past but I felt they reached a new level. It's hard to describe but they hit the right mix of high energy but calm explanations and right mix of information sharing and audience participation.
    - Dave "The Game" from Critical Hits was there! That was an unexpected but pleasant surprise.
    - Socializing was even more fun at Metatopia than Dreamation!

    \n So I had a pretty good time. Games as at any con went from meh to fantastic, though weighted towards the good end. Friday night I played Et In Arcadia Ego, which we ended up taking apart rather than really playing, but that is fun too. Saturday morning I played One Night, which got a little laser-sharky silly but was amusing, then I played Arboretum, a card game, which I was meh on but I'm not sure how much of that was because of some bad card draws which meant I lost horribly. I participated in a focus group for a kid's game, which was not all that fun but the designer seemed to think was really helpful. That night I played.... I think it's being called Starship 1 now which was whole bunches of fun even though we blew up the ship. Sunday morning I played Xenon which was cool, and finished early so I totally bailed on the video session of arboretum I was supposed to play in, in the interest of getting home to see the kid before bedtime.

    I do have one quibble... as a player not really interested in the design seminars there was to my mind a big hole in the schedule saturday afternoon, which was kinda disappointing.

    If I lived a bit closer I'd certainly go back but I'm not entirely certain it's worth it for me to make the drive (I think I spent more on tolls than I did on food).
    (I also need to give public props to my husband for staying with the kid all weekend)

    \nGood point!

    Maybe the players can run regular games in that slot.

    This design decision took a lot of thought. I put a hole in the board game schedule Saturday morning for the Board Game track, and a hole in the RPG/Story schedule Saturday afternoon for Kickstarter, Ken Hite and the Indie Games Roundtable.

    I think this worked perfectly, and while I understand that it meant there were very few RPGs running Saturday afternoon (Secret Fire was running, for example, with their beautiful 3D terrain), making sure those seminars were filled with people was paramount to the convention.

    Next year, I think I'll keep the same basic structure, but make sure that there are more alternate activities during that period. Any suggestions?
