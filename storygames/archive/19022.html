    \n Then you don't have to do what the Master says but the game continues. The Master might get angry or pout and look hurt or show faux sympathy but the minion is not required to follow The Master's orders.

    I once had a minion resist the Master's orders to kidnap the minion's own sister (but didn't have the Love to trigger end game). I had the Master get all weepy and apologetic. He assured the minion that he would, never ever want to upset the minion by having him do something terrible to his own sister. So the Master turned and immediately ordered ANOTHER minion to go kidnap the sister.

    \n Word to Jesse. In raw mechanical terms, if the Master wins, you are required to make one roll to carry out the order. If you resist, you're not.

    The minion might not even openly refuse the Master (sulky sneaky minion). If they resist they just aren't forced to do anything.

    \n Yeah, it's a pretty common stretch when I play that the minion superficially agrees to do what the Master wants, and then "resists" mechanically later. If they succeed, they can then go back to the Master and claim to have tried to do what they wanted. It all depends on the particular social dynamics of the relationship.

    \n Yes, all that. And don't forget that the rules clearly state that the Master is omniscient. A minion cannot hide something from the Master. The minion can subvert orders all he or she wants (as long as the player makes a roll towards the goal), but the Master will punish subversion in terrible ways. Easiest way to punish a minion: make another minion do something awful to their connections, or make them do it themselves. Or just threaten it. Invite the connection over to dinner...
