    \n One Can Have Her is a single session game where the PCs are all men with criminal pasts. Each PC has an enemy, life a goal and a relationship with the Femme Fatale. Each player has a hand of 7 cards they use to resolve conflicts. The resolution process is pretty much a simple I play a card, you play a card of higher value, back-forth kind of thing but there's some subtleties. The real magic is in the face cards which instantly end conflicts by having either the Police (Jacks), The Femme Fatale (Queens) or An Enemy (Kings) show up and intervene in specific ways.

    When all the players are out of cards a round of play is over and the GM secretly decides where this is the final round of play or not. Note: This isn't a big deal. Most of my One Can Have Her games are over after the first round. At MOST I've gone one additional round because there was some loose ends that needed exploring.

    Anyway, at the end of a round players choose whether they're going to "rat out" any PC to either the Police or their Enemy. If this really is the final round then any PC ratted out to the police is either killed or imprisoned as their final ending. If ONLY ONE PC is not ratted out then his character achieves his life goal and gets to determine the final fate of the Femme Fatal (hence the title of the game). If more than one PC is not ratted out than those PCs are not imprisoned or killed but they don't get their life goal either and the GM decides the fate of the Femme Fatale.

    If it isn't the final round of play then those who were not ratted out are dealt 3 more cards and those who were are dealt 1 card and play continues for another short round and then the ratting process is done again and so on until the GM decides to end the game.

    Jesse

    \n Oh! And in Dirty Secrets you select your length of play. Short Story, Novella or Novel. This determines the size of the crime grid you're playing with that constrains the maximum number of scenes you can have.

    A Short Story and Novella are easily single session plays. A Novel is likely two and at most three sessions. If you WANT to play in "campaign mode" there's a reoccurring investigator rule where you just keep the same investigator from story to story. That's what we did. We played all three types of stories back to back with the same investigator. It was neat.

    Jesse
