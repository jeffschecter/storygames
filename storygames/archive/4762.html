Using youtube or a similar method to explain/promote your game

\n
\n
\n
\n\ngamera_spinning \n\n\n
\n
\n\nNovember 2007 \n edited November 2007 in Directed Promotion
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n First, this comes from the perspective of a consumer of games, not a game designer.

Everyone knows what it's like to buy a boxed game and take out the booklet/folded sheet of rules and try to figure out how the game works. I've found this can be even trickier when you've just bought a new RPG and you're trying to explain the game's virtues to your group.

Wouldn't it be cool if when you bought the .pdf for a game, you also received a link to a few youtube files (or hosted MPEGs) with a basic video highlighting the features (or if you prefer, "the awesome") of the game, maybe showing some examples of play, or even a session of character creation. I know there were some great demos run at GenCon (and maybe they're online - I don't know), I just think it would SO much easier to get people interested in a game if I could more easily show them why they should care.

What do you think?

    \n People have three different preferences that describe how they learn:
    visually (by seeing)
    auditory (by hearing)
    kinesthetic (by doing)

    Folks seem to figure things out best when the person instructing them uses the learning preference of the person being instructed.

    Me, I'm a kinesthetic learner by nature, so a friend can try to explain to me how an RPG system mechanic resolves a conflict or does something unique, and that could help, or I can read about it, maybe even look at a chart that explains it, and that's also good, but the best way that I'm going to comprehend it is if I run a few conflicts myself or try out the mechanic in question.

    I wonder if there are different ways to appeal to those three methods of learning when writing a story game?

    \n Here is Jason filming an actual play of his General Hospital game: http://youtube.com/watch?v=dIXUW6HPuwI

    I'm totally down with more visually/tactile oriented instructions, primarily vids. I find that especially with board games, that:

    1) It's very easy to learn by someone who played the game teaching you the rules. In fact, this is how most board games are taught.
    2) And most board games have really, really shitty rules. Impossible to follow, they don't tell you WHY you're doing stuff, etc.

    I'd love to see more APs on vid, or at least small ones that explain simple issues. I'd love to see a Grey Ranks mini-vid that showed how the cube was moved about, frex. Or how a group initially negotiates and comes up with Shocks in shock:. Or a game of Universalis where people spend a coin to create an object template. Just 2-3 minute simple vids that focus on one simple aspect.

    And since we're on the issue, I had been planning for months to do this very thing once Tenra Bansho comes out, to explain things like The Anatman/Doctrine of No-Self Phase / Intermissions, How Karma works, etc.

    -Andy
