[TSOY] 4th session in the Floating Cities campaign

Eric Provost
December 2006 edited December 2006 in Actual Play

It's been better than a month since our last session, but yesterday's session still rocked.

Mark's character, Eva, and her newfound lover Grisha had crash landed their airship on a small flying island while trying to avoid the civil war that was erupting on the central island of Alderoo. There the two were picked up by the native gelflings of the island who, when they saw Emerelle's necklace (Lisa's character) promptly mistook Eva to be the Fey'Ri princess that they'd been waiting to return for generations.

A pivotal moment for Eva came when the gelfling highpriestess asked her quite directly "Are you a Fey'Ri?". Pivotal, because Eva chose to use her ability to summon the help of a chamelion spirit to make herself look exactly like Emerelle despite the fact that Grisha was standing right there. Having been raised in a culture that believes that all winged people are bad, and Fey'Ri are downright evil, Grisa freaked out. Passed out cold.

Later on we had this wonderfully awesome Bringing Down the Pain conflict where Eva was trying to win Grisha back while Grisha was trying to bash Eva's brains out of her skull. Mark's dice totally hated him during that conflict. But he was so determined. At one point, Mark had checked every Bruised and Bloodied box on Eva's sheet and hadn't yet even scratched Grisha. Despite the obvious loosing streak, Mark decided to press onward. Remarkably he won that round. And I folded. It was awesome. Eva begged and pleaded and professed her love while Grisha kept trying to destroy her. Eventually, after beating the living crap out of her, Grisha just couldn't take it any more and decided to carry her mangled body back to the gelflings for help.

Bringing Down the Pain is a wonderful mechanism. But I'm glad that the threads about Giving (in relation to DitV) had been going around recently. Giving up to Mark at that particular moment in time was killer awesome.

Any thoughts, Mark?

    We need to go over where we are, possibly moving time forward to help associate the time we've missed. We need to make sure we know what we want to do with our characters and stories so that we're on the same page and not rehashing old goals. Be sure that we're still playing what we want to play.
    We recapped to the point that everyone felt comfortable with the story in their minds. These were our mental notes, as opposed to a standard set of GM's notes. As it turned out, we weren't in the middle of anything. We'd actually completed a story arc and bought off some Keys (and bought some new ones). We knew where we were and where we wanted to go:

    I, as Eva, had stolen our airship and flown away with Grisha. Having been shot down, we were stranded on an island. We knew that the island had gelphlings an nothing else.

    I was unsure with where I wanted to go at this point, and struggled until Eric came up with a cool situation: The gelphlings on the island knew about the necklace I carried (being the most valuable thing on the ship, which I didn't want to leave behind). [The necklace was Lisa's character's necklace and it indicated her birthright as heir to the throne of Alderoo.] Anyway, once I realized that my major story arc of being shy and unable to act on my own due to no confidence had been completed, I knew that I had to take bold, reckless action.

    Special note: Don't get rid of your old Keys! They help you as much as the Keys that are still paying out, sometimes.

    Lisa's character, Elmerelle had gotten her revenge and overthrown the insurgent human goverment of Alderoo. She now needed the necklace back to begin the rest of her quest to recover her throne. Much happened here, just trying to get a fix on where I was (but actually on the behalf on another player's story). What happened here (and in my part) were more significant opponents, and some Bringin' Down the Pain.

    Special note: BDtP is where the game is at. It's weird, but you sometimes have to find a way to lose to someone before you can really rock their world. But who wants to win all the time, anyway?

    The whole Key Scenes thing just didn't click with me when we first started playing. I just didn't know how to handle them. The only things I could immediately think of were really railroady, so naturally I shunned away. But in that session, right there when we had that discussion about selling off all your Keys, something occurred to me. It occurred to me that I naturally have all these ideas in my head for cool scenes that may or may not happen. And I naturally tend to try to lead the game in the direction of those cool scenes. Like visiting the vast foggy lake of FeyRi turned to stone. It was in my head before we started playing and you took my lead and went right for it. So... then I give you XP because you followed that lead? Awesome!

    So, now, it's like I have permission to come up with a handful of scenes I really want to see happen and try to lead you to them. And if you get there I toss a couple of XP your way. Win-Win.
