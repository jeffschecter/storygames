    \n It's never easy. The best method tends to depend on the actual build. With metal miniatures, where irregularities in molding can complicate matters... I have no advice.

    For my GTM's I often will start by making my designs friendly to such things; for example, by putting the engine pods directly horizontal to the main fuselage, I can line them up simply by laying them on a flat surface.

    Another way to deal with the problem is to put a bendable part (such as heavy cardstock) as your "strut" and then (after achieving the best possible alignment) solidify it by infusing it with white glue.

    For most of my miniatures, though, I avoid the problem entirely by just not including such things in my designs. They are, indeed, fiddly and if I want to churn out a whole bunch of minis I'm best off avoiding them.
