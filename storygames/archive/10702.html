\n OK, here's the deal:

Each guy has a color - red, white, or blue. One color is very good for him, one is neutral, and one is very bad.

If you have three players, each gets assigned a color. He then gets six tokens in each of the other two colors that get used in play. All the tokens form a communal resource - they get mixed in a bag as they are used, and then the bag is drawn from to determine outcomes. This is cool, because what is useful to you is poison to your pals, and vice versa.

So with three players, there are 36 tokens, 12 of each color, and it is a nice closed system.

Adding a fourth player is easy - that guy gets 4 of each color token (including the one that is good for him) and the ecosystem is still balanced. Six players is three players, twice, so no big deal.

How do I handle five players?
