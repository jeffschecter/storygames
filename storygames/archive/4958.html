\n So, I was at Dragonmeet on Saturday and there was an announcement that Cubicle 7 are publishing a new RPG based on Doctor Who. They ran a seminar about their upcoming projects and needless to say, Doctor Who was talked about quite a bit. I didn't make it to that seminar because I was working the Collective Endeavour booth, but from what I hear this sounds really, really positive. Here are one guy's notes (pinched from his post of RPG.net)\n
AhniradesI attended the Cubicle 7 seminar yesterday. The following was revealed:

* The game will be released as a boxed set, and will contain a Players Guide, a GM's Guide, an Adventures Book, a GM screen and some funky Who-themed dice. Everything will be full colour and glossy, with plenty of photos from the new show. Scheduled for a 'Summer 2008' release it will sell for \xa325-\xa330.

* It will be possible to play Time Lords, Companions, UNIT soldiers or anyone else you can think of. The competence disparity between characters will be mitigated in a similar way to the Buffy RPG. Dave Chapman has great fondness for Blink, and wants to include the option of playing a Sparrow & Nightingale type of campaign. Playing the Doctor will also be an option.

* The system will be based off a single conflict resolution mechanic that can be applied to anything, from hypnotism to fisticuffs. The GM and players will narrate the specifics, tailoring it to the situation at hand. It sounded like a crunchier version of Risus to my ears. And yes, your characters can die if they lose a critically dangerous conflict.

* Characters will earn 'Story Points' during play, which can be used to effect the outcome of events. From the sound of it these effects will range from the minor (a bonus to a single di roll) to the truly mind-boggling (Rose absorbing the Time Vortex and blowing up the Dalek fleet). The system is designed to mimic stuff like the Doctor suddenly coming up with an arcane and convoluted solution to a problem that actually works. Think Adventure's dramatic editing mechanic, although with a much wider potential scope. The players are encouraged to be as creative as possible when spending Story Points, and the game will ideally run as a fast-paced dialogue over the screen.

* Devices will be created in the same way as PC's, and will possess unique characteristics, including a Story Point pool that allows them to behave in unusual ways. Thus the Sonic Screwdriver could be used to open mundane doors whenever you fancied, but to to use it as a weapon you'd need to spend SP's.
\nCould this be the properly handled entry level RPG licensed game that everyone else manages not to make? I mean, people generally get a great license, then they release a pretty cookie cutter RPG which has no appeal to non gamers. Or in the worst cases, they release a d20 setting based on a film which can't even be played or understood by someone who doesn't own the core D&D books. But this appears to be very firmly aimed at the 12 -16 year old market who love the show first and foremost. To anyone who doesn't live in the UK: You have no idea how massive the franchise is over here! This has the potential to be massive.

(As an aside, IIRC boxed sets are subject to VAT in the UK, whereas books are not. Ouch!)

    \n I helped the C7 guys get over the hill, as it were, with the BBC a little -- I did the layout on the proposal they took to their pitch meeting (and may end up doing layout on the end product as well). Definitely a great experience to be a part of the team there, howevermuch the layout part helped.

    Anyway: David Chapman (the lead developer who's authoring the core stuff) has some experience working with Eden, so I suspect we're going to get some fabulous mixture of Unisystem-flavored notions and story-game sensibilities with an orientation on being younger-kid as well as adult accessible here. Ultimately that's a pretty good mix, seeing as how Eden did Buffy pretty well, producing something that handled "one player's a slayer and then there's everyone else" in a way that didn't feel imbalanced. That said it's not going to *be* Unisystem for a variety of reasons; I'm excited to see what Dave ends up with.
