    \n Way back in the day, I used to play TORG. Way back in 1991-1993 or so.

    There were a lot of problems, though. I mean, the resolution system, magic spell creation system, and possibility/realm restoration rules were easy enough to understand and implement. The problem was that we didn't know how to create scenarios, since there was no Oracle mechanic in the game like its predecessor In a Wicked Age.

    So my friends and I would stumble around trying to determine who was the protagonist, who was the antagonist... we even used some of the sourcebooks as makeshift oracles (I was Cyberpope Malreaux, and my friend Jamie rolled up The Gaunt Man), but it was hard to create situation-loaded scenarios. Instead, the game was apparently crafted to have everyone but the GM play protagonists, and only the GM played antagonists. It felt pretty lopsided, but we gave it a try anyway.

    We ended up blowing up a cyber zepplin filled with werewolves with living spears, but in the end there was no inter-player backstabbing, and once the adventure was over we didn't know which characters we were supposed to keep and which we were supposed to throw away.

    Still, it was an interesting concept.

    -Andy
