\n Thor asked me to write 500 words on "What is a roleplaying game?", for his entry into Jared's competition, "Kingdom Of The Something". It was tremendous fun to write, although probably only fun to read if you remember White Wolf's similar sections. Here we go!

What is a roleplaying game?

Since the earliest days, we have told stories. Cavemen, sitting around the campfire, used to act out the stories of their daily hunts. Primitive dice were manufactured and rolled, around the fire, to add tension to the story of the hunt. Eventually, the cavemen painted their stories, as cave paintings, and sold them. When the buyers wanted more, the cavemen produced supplements and new editions, and sold those too.

You may think a roleplaying game is like this. You are wrong. Roleplaying games are complex marvels, which you probably would not understand. Just because you can tell a story does not mean you can play a roleplaying game. Just because you had a good idea for a screenplay, once, does not give you the right to play this game. You probably do not have the skills.

Does this excite you? Does it sound challenging? Do you want to play?

If so, then quite frankly, fuck off. Just by reading this section, you have proved you are not a roleplayer. If you were a roleplayer, you would currently be wanking off over the weapons chart, rather than reading this.

Or perhaps you are hoping you will pick it up as you go along.

Again, fuck off. The last thing I want to do, when I'm roleplaying, is babysit some smiley kid who needs the rules explaining every five minutes. Oh, sure, on the Internet, we're all welcoming and talk about "gateway games". But we only do this because we're hoping to lure hot women to play. Are you a hot woman? Of course not. You're reading a roleplaying game. So fuck off.

Even if you did play a roleplaying game, this one would be a bad choice. It has been written, in a hurry, for a game competition that turned out to be an elaborate joke. If you are reading this as a finished product, it has probably been thrown together, in a hurry, to sell at GenCon. If you are reading this at GenCon, I seriously suggest you put this game down and go to the Forge booth. They are choosy about who they let in, unlike the organisers of the booth at which you are standing.

What, then, is a roleplaying game? You and your friends gather to tell a tale. One of these friends, the "GM", acts like the director of a film: he tells everyone what to do and throws tantrums. You, and your friends, are like the actors in the film: you quibble about minor details and turn a fun process into a nightmare. Over time, you will turn against each other and forget to shower. Secretly, you will suspect that you play roleplaying games because talking to women is too scary. Why not save yourself now?

Of course, none of the above matters, because nobody will read it. Still, I'd better it end on a positive note, because the last line will catch people's eye. Here we go. Are you ready? Let's play!

    \n I wonder, if not specifically called attention to, if you could sneak this into a game and have it go unnoticed for over a year?

    I think so...it is a satire of the idea of a "non-gateway" game still having that whole "you picked this up first, not D&D - what the hell were you thinking?" section at the start.

    Having glanced at a few of these deals, and like David Allen Coe's "Perfect Country and Western Song", I have noticed some missing elements:

    * a reference to Cops and Robbers

    * a reference to "I killed you" and "no you didn't" as an excuse for all following rules.

    * a "what you need to play" section that makes huge assumptions that will likely be totally wrong (and will also mention snacks as if without such a mention you might not know to feed yourself periodically during prolonged activity).

    * The "the GM creates the story that the players contribute to" blurb

    * a hand wave about not reading a section of the book that has secret GM info in it.

    * a new and cool name for the GM.

    * a warning about the use of he/him or he for players her for GM (because all GMs are women - fact!)

    * something about how magic isn't real, but cybernetics are, and how very careful you have to be to avoid cyber-psychosis (that second artificial leg might eat the last of your soul!).

    * an aside to parents regarding the nature of your game about demon-summoning murderers who fight against drug-dealing Angels of a God Who Turned His Back On Man(tm) and how you might want to be careful about giving it to him now that you have purchased it.

    It is understandable how you might have missed these items, but I assure you they seem to be required.

    \n This is from "Simply Roleplaying". It is nowhere was pretentious as a WW intro, but has some of the hallmarks.

    It is reprinted here for educational purposes:

    "Welcome Back, Again!

    You say you\u2019ve never been here before? Ah, but you
    have and maybe didn\u2019t know it. \u201cCowboys and
    Indians,\u201d \u201cCops and Robbers,\u201d \u201cSpace Hero,\u201d and all
    the \u201clet\u2019s pretend\u201d games you played as a little kid were
    roleplaying games. You took on the role of someone
    else, someone who had a lot more exciting life than the
    typical eight-year-old... and you played as though you
    were that person. That\u2019s what Simply Roleplaying!\u2122 is
    all about.

    But Simply Roleplaying! is better. First, you don\u2019t
    run around outside (unless you want to.) And you don\u2019t
    get into arguments about who shot whom and who\u2019s
    dead and who isn\u2019t. Simply Roleplaying! has a fast and
    easy-to-learn set of rules to help you through those
    tricky questions and let you get on with the fun.

    Occasionally you\u2019ll be rolling dice to help settle
    questions and challenges. This game uses ten-sided dice
    (D10 in game shorthand) available at most hobby and
    game shops. When you see \u201c2D10\u201d you should roll two
    ten-sided dice and add the values together. \u201c1D10\u201d
    means just roll one die. Sometimes you\u2019ll see an
    expression such as \u201c2D10-2\u201d \u2014 this means roll two
    D10, add them, then subtract 2 to get your roll result. If
    no other direction is provided, a \u201croll\u201d is always 2D10."
