    \n Haven't really run Beta rules for Danger Patrol in quite a while, not since John Harper put out the Pocket Danger Patrol rules.

    I tended to run Suspense scenes much like Action scenes, but with Threats specifically tailored to mysteries and intrigue, rather than fighting. Each player would be given one chance to tackle one of the Threats on the board, trying to solve it, uncover some info about it, reveal the true Threat hiding behind the scenes, building the portal to wherever, etc. If the attempt was successful, then that was an opportunity for them to fill in details about what was going on, while not succeeding meant that there was some kind of complication...usually an Action scene where the players then had to fight it out in order to get the answers they wanted! Anything not touched upon by the players during the Suspense scene would go unanswered until next time...or were just not important enough to the story for anyone to care.
