Dragon Shell RPG: "Only for LIVE, Only for LOVE"

Andy
September 2006 edited September 2006 in Story Games
So Mark and I were screwing around looking for what's new in the Japanese RPG scene, when we came across an Aztec-Fantasy Dragon themed game called DRAGON SHELL RPG:

http://www.blueforest.jp/~fushimi/dshell/ds-wp02.jpg
http://www.blueforest.jp/~fushimi/dshell/ds-wp01.jpg
We will start Air Tactical Adventure at the far side from Blueforest.
There is a Land of Dragon Gods, Every Heroes havea a Dragon Spirit,
named =Ryukaku= means Dragon Shell.
Fight on Jungle, with Wild Creatures,
Fight on Air, riding =Ryukaku-Heiki= the Cloned Dragon Weapon.

Heros Be more Strong, Be real Dragons.
Only for Live, Only for Love.
I had a flashback to a converstaion with Jerry Greyson at GenCON last year, where we were talking about RPGs from Spain or something, and wanting to craft a small RPG that is written exactly like it was written in another language then poorly translated into English.

Not just "bad grammar", but consistent "pauses", where you have to wonder what the author was talking about a little, and give that sense of "There's something deep/awesome going on here, I can feel it between the broken phrases and strange conjunctions".

Just a Little Idea for the Little Ideas category.

    I think most of the magic could be duplicated by:

    1) Writing the document in English. Whenever possible, use idiomatic phrases, sprinkle in slang, and some real high-level vocabulary,

    2) Google Language Tool. Go from say English->German, then German->English.

    3) If that's not spun enough, THEN go to like Korean or Simplified Chinese and back.

    4) Base your final text on the results.

    -Andy
