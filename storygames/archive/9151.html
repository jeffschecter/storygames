\n Well, I think Einstein said it better than I can. What's the general consensus on "borrowing" mechanics? As I understand they're not blatantly copyrighted per se, and I'm not talking about the game text explaining them or anything like that. But I've seen several games since FATE that use aspects, a couple using keys, etc. Also there's the prevalence of D&D rip-offs that change strength to brawn and charisma into three sub-stats of personality, looks, and sex appeal that seem to suggest this is OK.

If I did do such a thing I wouldn't hide it as the thread title suggests, but while I think such borrowing and re-branding is fine, I'd like to "check with the experts" so to speak

What's story games take on such things?

    \n I don't think you need to ask people first as long as what you're doing is sufficiently different from the original. It is common practice to acknowledge the original designers in your notes, though.

    However, if you're commercially publishing something that is basically a supplement for someone else's game, then you need their permission first. If you're just releasing something for free on the internet, you probably only need their permission if you're reprinting a significant portion of their game rules (which could infringe on the value of their original product).

    \n I'm with Graham - be polite and ask first. Nobody is going to say no. The best reason to talk to the original creator about something you intend to steal is that they are very likely to support you in all kinds of ways you might not have thought of.

    Acknowledging and thanking your influences is also polite, and helpful to people who read and play your game - it gives them preceding or parallel branches in the design family tree to explore if they like your stuff.
