    November 2006
    'Story Games' are for 'Everybody'.

    But this forum ain't. AFAIK, only one person was ever actually denied approval to these forums (hint: "SATAN! ... Literally.") just because I knew it wouldn't benefit the community at all. People who hate the ideas that go on here tend to Not Apply for Membership, and I'm totally cool with that. I've not had to deny users like JimBob or Pundit and the like, simply because they wisely know that they don't like the games here, and talking here would be a waste of their time and most likely make both them and us upset for no reason. There's nothing wrong with that, and both sides benefit.

    I originally wrote Story Games for Everybody as a placeholder after I got tired of "Story Games Test Community". Maybe it's time for a new placeholder?

    "Story Games Insular Collective"?
    "Story Games Can Be Fun"?
    "Please to Play of the Story Games!"

    Also, is you co-worker a gamer? Just curious.

    This community will (most likely) never be "anyone can sign up freely and start posting without approval". Not because I screen people for their beliefs or anything (again, there's 600 users or so, and only one person has even been denied, and only one non-spammer has been banned), but because I reserve the right to be a dick and say "Sorry bub, this isn't going to go anywhere". As I mentioned when I started the place, this forum is my own Zen Garden. I treat it like a public community, but I've always reserved the right to scream "KWAP!" and burn it all down, or enforce a bizarre public posting order like some Space-Alien Mousillini. Luckily, people have trusted me/'the site' enough thusfar to not worry to much about that.*

    -Andy

    *all according to plan!
