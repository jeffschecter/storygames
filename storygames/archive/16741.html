\n I've been looking for a good rules-light space-opera game to sell my group on (as a palate cleanser after a long Call Of Cthulhu campaign), and ran across @neko_ewen's "Adventures Of The Space Patrol". It looks great to me \u2014 lighter than "Bulldogs", not as off-the-wall as "Danger Patrol", and with more 'there' to it than the disappointing "Cosmic Patrol" \u2014 but it seems to be a pretty early playtest draft.

So I'm wondering if anyone here has played it, and how it went? (And if @neko_ewen would care to comment on its status \u2014 is it something that's likely to be developed further, or a project that stalled?)

    \n Hey! Thanks for checking it out! I've playtested the game a couple of times, and it's been a lot of fun, albeit dependent on the players getting into the right frame of mind. (The players have to buy into the heartwarming aspect, or it'll kind of go off the rails. That's why I had to get rid of the original robot character, who was a little bit of a jerk.)

    I haven't done much with it in a while because there hasn't been a huge amount of interest, and because I've got other projects (like Magical Burst and publishing Golden Sky Stories) taking up a lot of my time. I'll definitely welcome any feedback on it. I've got other priorities, but I have a special fondness for Space Patrol.

    \n I had fun in the playtest session I played in, and several funny moments and good lines are still memorable for me even though the session happened quite a while ago. I'm not sure if much has changed since then, but I think in the version I played that knowing the expectations of the genre and playing to them was a part of making the session fun, and I'm not sure how the game would have gone if I hadn't been at least passingly familiar with some of the source-material Ewen was using for inspiration. (Calling it "space opera" seems a bit strange to me, though). I think the characters and situation are a good basis for a fun kind of play, assuming everybody can get into the right tone, frame of mind, etc.

    \n Oh, that's right. We did try it out with that Skype group that one time, with Billy and Craylor. That was fun! I made some revisions since then, and the PDF I currently have up on my blog is a newer version. I changed the selection of characters a bit, added the "Fusion Points" rule (a Fan Mail type mechanic), and refined a few other things.

    I have had a session of the game just not work when the players' expectations weren't in alignment with the intended tone of the game. Right now I don't really know where to go with it next, apart from being tempted to rework it as a Golden Sky Stories hack.
