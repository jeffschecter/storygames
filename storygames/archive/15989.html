\n Whereas,

(1) I own a lot of chairs,

(2) I'm pretty broke,

(3) I work ALL THE TIME,

(4) I have an awkward, large space between my front door and my bedroom, and

(5) I don't have a proper gaming table,

I'm wondering if anyone has any ideas or examples of a good gaming table that is,

(A) cheap (under $50, ideally under $30),

(B) easily stored (I need to use that front door, when I'm not gaming),

(C) assembled/acquired with not more than a solid afternoon's labor.

Any help would be much appreciated!

    \n Go to a reclaimed building supply place, and acquire a flat interior door. Make or buy two sawhorses that are almost as long as the door is wide. Bolt door to sawhorses. Cover top surface with large sheet of paper (butcher's, contractor, easel pad). The doorknob mounting hole is a cupholder - SUPER CLASSY!

    We used a table like that to play board games for years at my friends' house. It was up on really tall horses so it was comfortable to stand at, and it had squishy rubber feet that kept it from sliding around on the concrete basement floor. Great table.

    \nWe used this for years. I bought one large sheet ( I think it was actually plastic sheeting used to line showers ) , a sheet of this thin ply wood, some strong wall hooks and mounts, and some carpenters glue. In total it was less than $20. Glued the ply wood on the back to support it, and attached the wall hooks and mounts. During the week you could hang it up and have a large dry erase board for your wife/girlfriend to draw inappropriate caricatures of you on......... ;P . Then on game day you can put it on top of any other table you may have, or between two chairs, or even lay it across your coffee table ( I would recommend a table cloth though to protect any surface you put it on.
