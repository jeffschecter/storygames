\n OK, I'm looking for comments, criticism, ideas. So in Business Solutions you play a technician and, in rotation, a client business. The player sheet encodes the basic information about tech and client, and also shows you what to do on each business day (which is the framework for the game - six business days and a finite end condition). It's a three player game, and on each business day you are either the client or your technician (who might be the lead, or might be the goat). It sounds complicated but it really isn't - you just rotate which role to play every business day. When you play the client you also play the troublemaker, who is created collaboratively and vexes the technician who is that day's goat. There are also special events on Monday, Wednesday, and Saturday.

Anyway, I want to represent all this graphically so you can look down and know "OK, it's Wednesday, I'm the lead tech, Bill's the goat and in trouble, we're going to Sally's business, and we've got the mandatory Hump Day Party after work."

Praise and Blame are game currency represented by cards, which you keep in a pile next to your sheet.

I put together three player sheets (they are obviously different, because everybody has a different role every day) using soul-crushing iconography and glorious Arial. I want them to communicate clearly without looking too good, you know? I want a shabby business aesthetic, ultimately, but they must be functional and communicate a sense of fun. Sort of a tall order.

Here are the sheets. This is an open call for your impressions and suggestions.

    \n Man, I'm torn between making suggestions about making the layout better (my natural inclination) and worse (which is so much more fun). Things that stand out to me, so this will either tell you what to fix or what you're doing right:

    4. The vertical days-of-the-week text doesn't work for me because... I have a hard time imagining anyone being able to pull that off on office software from the early 90s. Like, it needs to actually be horizontal, I think. That would make it easier to read and even more awkward, probably.

    5. The page title and subtitle don't really do anything for me. They are neither interesting or sufficiently ugly. I feel like the sheet needs a horrible corporate logo, you know, with some abstract-yet-somehow-inspiring symbol on the left and then "Business Solutions" and then the subtitle.
