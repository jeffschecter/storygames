\n So, the book suggests that a combatant with higher Reflexes should get a +1D advantage in a Bloody Versus tests. I feel like there should be a difference between a B3 Reflexes vs. B4 Reflexes combat and a B2 Reflexes vs. B8 Reflexes combat. I would like disparity in Reflexes to matter in a combat without necessarily switching to the full Fight! rules.

Does anybody take Reflexes into account in a more granular way than +1D to the higher reflexes? And, if so, can you suggest a good rule of thumb for an appropriate advantage?

My initial thought is +1D if the character just beats his opponent's exponent; +2D if a character's Reflexes exponent is twice or more than his opponent's; +3D if it is three times, &c. I would treat a grey shade as if it was twice the exponent of a black shade. Would that work?

Thank you!

    Also: remember you need not use Bloody Versus tests. You can use normal versus tests with whatever Intents you want.
