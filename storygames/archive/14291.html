    \n I usually only become jealous if it's something I was also in the midst of doing... but better. Like, when I have the irrational feeling that they've beaten me to the punch and, if I'd only had my shit together, I would be the one getting attention for something cool and would be worthier of that attention somehow. So it's a weird, dysfunctional mixture of jealousy and self-loathing. Very My Life with Master. I try to strangle the feeling as much as possible, but sometimes it's difficult.

    \n In 2009 or so I was working on a cyberpunk game. I had this crazy idea that instead of a "core system" you could have a little subsystem for each of the skills, like there was a "Sniper" subsystem, and a "Lies" subsystem, and so on. It was an exciting idea, and the subsystems were fun, but in playtest it fell flat - it totally sucked!

    A couple years later, Vincent comes out with Apocalypse World, which has the same thing, with subsystems for different kinds of conflicts, and it totaly rules. What Vincent did, which I didn't, was to have "Basic" moves tied to different kinds of conflict, rather than different skills. Basically he was smart enough to make the idea work and I wasn't. I was a little bit jealous.

    \n I can see how it happens but I haven't been jealous of game designs as I make my designs to play myself not really to sell and I'm always to excited about good games.

    I do get jealous of the communiy support some designers get. I want to bounce my ideas around with other people who know what they're doIng to make them the best they can be, but irrationally I think more "famous" designers get more chance to do this.

    I hope this makes me a better person though, as I always try to stop by and look at what less "famous" designers are doing and give some feedback. (Come to think of it that is how I got into my latest AW hack, so it's all good!)

    Posted By: MatthijsDo you ever experience creative jealousy? Where someone does something you think is so cool that you become jealous?
    \nOh hell yes.

    But then I realize that a lot of this is a series of choices that I myself made, a path I set upon myself.

    For example, I work at a job where the more work I do, the better I succeed and get bennies/promotions/stuff (as opposed to previous jobs, where I could basically do 4 hours of work a day, stay mentally checked out for the rest, and leave at 5 on the dot). Making that choice left me less room for creative time.

    When I come home, I'm often in the mood to relax. So I consume, either J-Dramas, PS3 games, sometimes manga or books. I realize daily that I could be working on some shelved game idea, and that with a little bit of elbow grease I could get back into the groove, but I make the conscious decision to spend my time in other ways. Like going to the gym or jumping back into a low-pri work project, etc.

    Stuff like that. It's not so much for me about "not having come up with brilliant idea X". As JD says, I can just steal that shit and adopt it into my own game (my designs are a litany of this, actually), but I am sometimes jealous of others with free time, until I realize that I had worked myself into a trail of habits (or put conscious or unconscious priority on clearing that next level on the PS3 game) that simply don't leave time for design-work.

    -Andy
