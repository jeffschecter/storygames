    \n Lots of cool ideas there, expressed in very simple mechanics. "Headfirst" was the game I read last and I almost skipped it because I generally don't like that kind of game, but I'm glad I read it. Now my mind is spinning with an idea for a similar game based on the weird connections people make in dreams, but seeding the cards not only with random stuff but also with bits and pieces of an actual storyline known only by the GM. And I'd write the phrases on the cards instead of using a regular deck and looking them up, "Oracle-style."

    The GM plays a psychiatrist. The remaining players play parts of the patient's mind, each taking on a certain role: child self, adult self, protector self, dreamer self, etc. Each would have his or her own agenda and work to control what the patient (and psychiatrist) believed was true.

    \n I'd probably ask the GM to write elements of a traumatic story onto a set of cards. I'd ask the players each to write 10-20 random queues on cards. Shuffle. The GM starts play with, "So, Kelly, tell me what you remember about that day." A player draws a card and works it into play however. "Well, I don't remember anything about it. But I had this dream the other night about a bird..." and embellishes as she likes.

    Then the players fight for control over both the narration and the outcome of the "truth." Insert cool mechanics here.
