    \n thanks everybody.

    However, i think most of these games don't match (at least) the second criteria

    By focusing on story more so than "my" character, i mean that in CAS stories, the protagonist is just a vehicule to explore the (short) story and the ambient. There is'nt really a strong identification with the characters. In many stories, they can just be "people of...." with some proeminent local leaders. In others ones, the protagonist is a necromant or an horrible guy of some kind.

    In many stories, characters are just here to fall into their fate and die at the end.

    All this sound much more like "i'm going to tell you the sad story of..." than just "what do you do ? / ok, i'm going to buy a boat to sail to this forbidden island"

    Last but no least : have you ever heard of a (dark) "fate" mechanic ? I'm thinking about Polaris.

    Any ideas about if it would be possible to mix IAWA and Polaris into a frankestein ?
    \nPolaris is very character centric and the mechanics are, at least in my oppinion, not well suited to switch around the protagonists a lot.

    Similar to the way Wicked Archipelago (http://www.story-games.com/forums/discussion/16335/wicked-archipelago-roll-your-own-seid/p1) combines the IAWA prep with Archipelago play it should be very possible to do a Wicked Polaris game as long as you populate your cosmos sufficiently but Polaris is still played best as a multiple-sessions-with-set-protagonists game.

    Archipelago on the other hand dishes out goals for every session, has a degree of world building through exploration and the game mechanics are not locked to specific characters in the way of Polaris.
