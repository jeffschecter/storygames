    "Tahara (Arbiter System): The game itself is called Tahara and is a dark superhero game based on the legend of the bottle into which King Solomon is said to have conjured seventy-two demons.The seal on the bottle has been broken and the demons are loose in the world, hiding in people... including the player characters. Tahara is a game of battling the one's inner demons, literally, in order to regain the purity of soul to take on the raveners who wish to undo the world.
    As for the mechanical system, it's shares similarities with Exalted, Shadowrun, and Unknown Armies, to name a few. It uses pools of d6s (no other kinds of dice) rolled under the character's stats to generate successes. The system is universal, modular, and scalable to any setting or power level. It's most notable characteristic is that it's the only superhero-style roleplaying game without skill lists and power menus, instead using a skill and power generation mechanic that encourages fleshed-out characters and puts the game's power level in the hands of the GM, not the rulebook. "

    "Adventure Description:: F-Note's, your favorite music bar and cafe, is in deep in debt and on the verge of being bought-out by a snarky corporate chain. Can you and your friends band together, organize a local benefit concert, and raise enough money to get F-Note's out of the red before it's too late? Garage Band: It's a heady feeling, standing at the base of the stage, crushed by the bodies of your fellow fans as you share the experience of good music played live. In Garage Band, you can climb onto that stage, plug in your guitar, pick up your sticks, or lean in to the microphone, and become the object of your fans' adoration. You can make music. At least, that's the dream. And as soon as your band gets a gig, you'll be on your way. Bring your six-siders for a rocking-good time."

    So MACE recap...

    Tenra Bansho. I played a ninja who fell in love with a girl-shaped wooden doll and betrayed all his friends.

    Ganakagok (AP). I played a crippled Cassandra who destroyed the village's boats and left the only person he loved to freeze to death beneath an ice mountain. Good times!

    TSOY: Aztexican Stand-off, my Aztec-themed one-shot in which the players managed to slay three Gods and most of each other. Plus some genius (Matthew Gandy step forward) brought an Ahuizotl (cannibal ape) to the Heaven of Stillborn Children.

    Cold City (AP). The British guy ended up trapped beneath a flak tower with an Incursor and blowing his own head off.

    Dogs in the Vineyard. The best conflict of the con had something to do with drinking a cup of barley tea. Also, one angry husband held off four Dogs out to abduct his wife for, like, ages.

    Contenders. (AP) 1898 Seattle, wharf boxing, The Short-Track Kid, a Chinese immigrant, kicks unholy ass until meeting his match in an epic ten round bout with The Green Machine.

    Plus hanging out with friends and making new ones. A great time.
