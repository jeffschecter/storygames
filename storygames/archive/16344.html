    \n Heh, they've bought some of that stuff for Library 10 from us.

    I think I've heard of Ikuisuuden laakso having been planned for an English version at some point, too, but perhaps nothing came of it yet. The game is a basically straightforward adventure game suited for beginners; you play penguins in a fantasy take where they have a primitive society on the south pole. The penguins have all sorts of cool things like different tribes/castes of penguin, pilgrimages to the inner parts of the continent away from the sea and so on. I rather like the setting, even if the system doesn't grab me. (Some interesting mechanical ideas, though, like the fat reserves the penguins carry.)\n
    Posted By: WilhelmSeems the collection is missingAnkh - Kalevala heroes, which in spite of it's subtitle is in Finnish.
    \nI'd love to own Ankh! The name of the game despite referring to an Egyptian concept is an acronym for "Adventures of the Northern Kalevala Heroes" in English, while the actual game is in Finnish. I've read it and played it when I was young, but it was a loaner copy and it's a pretty rare find nowadays. Ankh is a crazy attempt at cloning Mentzer D&D for use with a Finnish fantasy setting. The overall feel is really weird as your presumably Kalevala-like wise men use spells like Burning Hands to slay goblins in dungeons. I'm sure that I might get more out of it today than I did then.
