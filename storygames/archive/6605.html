    \n Beer. Lager, to be precise. Or weissebeir, but usually lager. Lately it's Lech, a rather nice, crisp Polish lager which is now pretty ubiquitous here in Southampton given the massive Polish population we've acquired over the last twenty months. Or Zubr, which is heavier. There are nights when I buy food and also pick up a couple of 4 packs of Waitrose lager. There's something odd about drinking a supermarket own brand lager: I'd feel like a right pikey if it was Tesco or Asda Compass lager, but this is Waitrose we're talking about: their own brand stuff is better (and costs more) than most branded piss-beer like Fosters*.

    Sometimes I go to town and get a bunch of freaky stuff from all over the world. There's a great shop near me called Bitter Virtue where you can buy stuff from all over the world. Cristal from Cuba is a good one during summer, or Asahi. Floris lambic beers are also good, especially the honey one. I had a really great banana beer called Mongozo the other day, that was top.

    I like to drink. I think some people outside of the UK don't realise quite what a cultural cornerstone alcohol is for us.

    * Not that I'm saying all Australian beer is urinesque or anything...
