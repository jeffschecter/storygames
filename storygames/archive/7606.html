\n Hey guys, the Toronto Area Gamers want to do podcast interviews of our members so the other members feel like they can get to know each other. We're trying to come up with a good set of questions. We want to have a chart that we roll on.

Any suggestions for questions we could ask? This is meant to be pretty low-key, so no questions like "What was the most traumatic experience of your life?" But we want to get the juices flowing.

    Posted By: Clyde L. RhoerWho is your audience? Just each other?
    TAG (Toronto Area Gamers) has about 200 members, but we have a hard time getting ~100 of those members out to their first meetup. They're lurkers, essentially. So I think of those folks as our audience... but I also think it will be entertaining and helpful for people who just met at one of our meetups.

    \n How big is the group you play in?
    What's your favourite Internet hang-out point?
    What's the biggest influence gaming has had on your non-gaming life?
    What's the biggest influence your nongaming life has had on gaming?
    What's your guilty gaming pleasure?
    What's the game you haven't played you wish you could?
    How often does your group need a new player?

    Or, to deal with some misconceptions and lighten it up:

    How often does someone suggest you should shower?
    How often do you practice Satanic rites?
    What would you do if a member of the opposite sex showed interest in you?
    Where do you hope to live after moving out of your parents' basement?
    Which would win a spacebattle between the Battlestar Galactica and the USS Enteprise?
