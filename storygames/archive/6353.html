    \n You see, maybe it's just a perception thing, or we are even approaching the realms of "personal taste", but to me even DnDI is already too detailed. NWN is just... too much (It's very nice, mind you).
    What's not "spelled out" (in images, sounds and effects) can be imagined, and that's one of the best part of this hobby, to me.

    But then again, I don't use minis, either. My ideal virtual tabletop would replicate the table, and I'd use it to keep track of beads/chips, charsheets, dice and so on (actually, I have several tokens to that use in maptool). What NWN and DnDI instead is "shared MAP", which is not only overkill for most (of my) games, but also fulfills a different role. The map/mini/3dmodel partially substitute description and imagination, the table with abstract tokens just acts as a table replacement, keeping the "experience" as close as a tabletop game as possible.

    I guess with video conference and the abstract table you'd have a true "virtual gaming table". As I said, it all probably stems from the fact that I never use minis of battlemaps on my table... so I don't see them as part of the "tabletop rpg experience", but as a spiffy but unnecessary addon.

    YMMV :-D
