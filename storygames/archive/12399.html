    \n What I wish I had said to Jason's camera (or what I learned at Origins this year):

    Never trust the red furs, they steal grain and turn to piracy. And Michael Miller does a fantastic mouse impression!
    Memories in A Penny for my Thoughts can only lead to two things: tetanus and daddy issues.
    Ryan Macklin runs a mean impromptu game of Dresden. Especially with certain types of liquid incentive.
    a/state is my new favorite setting. Steampunk/cyberish crooked cop? I'm so there.
    Well, next to Freemarket. Our characters were: a Death Artist (moi), a Singularity Comptroller, an Ethics Aesthetician, and a Genetic Enhancement Enthusiast. Our goal? Move the World Forward.
    If you play a horrible, corrupt, and manipulative Meth Queen in Fiasco, you can come out on top!
    That Baron Von Munchausen is a viciously successful drinking game. I remember something about Teddy Roosevelt and Jaguars and Slaves in Cydonia and something called a Grindsmistress.
    The Kern, being plant frog creatures, have decidedly indiscernible facial expressions. And Luke Crane runs an exquisite game of Burning Empires.
    In 1983 America was attacked by Cuban frog women and the Russians, but five unlikely heroes fought for freedom, liberty, and all that other stuff. Best hack ever by Jason Morningstar. What was it called?
    Fire is dangerous. Really, really dangerous. So dangerous in fact, that it took the DANGER PATROL to quench it. With our daredevillish fists. And experimental pheromone machines. And flying German robot heads from the future. And, of course, Metal.

    In summation, I was balls deep in Origins this year. Good times, good people, good games!

    In the events book, none of Kat\u2019s events listed a GM name or a location. None of the seminars listed a location. None of the events listed a game system. Evidently, this lack of location had been caught shortly before the convention started, but the revised information was not disseminated to anyone we were able to speak to. We spoke to people at GM/event HQ, at RPG HQ, at Customer Service, and we continually got variations of \u201cI don\u2019t know. The person who would know is around here somewhere, but I don\u2019t know where.\u201d This was the beginning of frustration incarnate.

    Because of these scheduling issues, ALL of Kat\u2019s games had no players. With no game system listed, people who wanted to try out With Great Power\u2026 or Serial Homicide Unit couldn\u2019t know that the events were in those game systems. With no GM name listed, players who had played in Kat\u2019s games previously could not know that she was running them, and that they\u2019d be fun no matter the game system. It was very, very rough on her to sit at empty table after empty table.
    \nThis was Me and Jerry G last year. We cobbled together sessions and ended up running and playing several games, despite the persistent efforts of the shitty Origins organization/staff to prevent us from gaming.

    That sucks.

    \n Back in the 90s my friends and I would drive down to Origins from Michigan just to shop the dealer hall for the day. It was three hours down, and then three hours back at the end of the day. And it was always fun. But that was before you could get any game you want from sellers online. Nowadays the value proposition of the show is increasingly concentrated in the game events.

    And here Origins is slitting its own throat.

    Gamemasters like Kat put time and effort into preparing games to run at the show. This time and effort is a gift to Origins and to the hobby. And year after year they squander this gift through ignorance and ineptitude.

    Paul

    \n I worked the IPR booth, hung out a lot, and played in four games of Fiasco. I got to be:

    The Vegas showgirl who started out kind, virtuous and good but ended up in the ER with a gunshot wound, wearing only a strap-on.
    The deeply stupid ethanol plant handyman who finally learned what the word "compensation" meant during an F-2 tornado.
    The fading Dallas belle who was looking forward to the President's 1963 visit, but was a death sentence to anyone who touched her.
    The LAPD's original bad lieutenant, with a twin sister, rage issues and a burning desire to please everybody.

    So much fun! Failure never gets old.

    At one point I also rode around Columbus in a convertible, which was a little surreal. I really, really liked Origins.

    \n The first character I played at Origins was a handgun, urging its owner to use it.
    In A Penny For My Thoughts, one PC's pleasant memory was "accidentally" killing her mother. It just got darker from there. My pleasant memory was getting tetanus, which is pretty nice in comparison.
    In Fiasco, my crooked cop never managed to get it on with a freaky Mexican, even though she turned out to not really be his cousin.
    In Death School, my computer hacker nerd turned out to have been the villain the entire time!!! A sudden plot twist, and everything fell into place for me.
    I discovered that I was living in a suburb full of murderers in Unknown Armies.
    We failed at Jungle Adventure, but got managed to escape Spooky Manor by becoming a vampire.
    Luke and Jared's game design seminar made me look at every human activity as a form of game design. Parenting is about game design. And game design is mind control. Therefore, parenting is mind control.
    A few really silly Fudge games, some Cthulhu games (each with serious flaws but some good ideas) and a lot of Are You A Werewolf?, and that was my con. It was a load of fun, overall.

    \n This was my first Origins, and I had a great time, even if only about 50% of my scheduled events actually happened.

    - I got to try _Founding Fathers_ which is a new game from some of the same folks who brought you _Twilight Struggle_ and _1960: Making of a President_. Continues the tradition of combining euro style area control (and a bit of a bidding element) with CDG mechanics to creating a ripping multiplayer voting game that also gives you a lot of interesting historical insight into how the US constitution was written. Must buy for me.

    - Wings of War with minis and six players is good clean fun!

    - I got to experience Burning Wheel ala Thor, playing in the second and third installments of the "Trouble in Hochen" series ('Dinner for One" and "Your Day in Court"). YDIC was especially intense; it was a tight two player (plus GM) game, with me as an idealistic young priest and my companion, an honorable yet landless knight trying to clear our names. We barely squeaked that out! Burning Wheel at its finest.

    - I ran rip-roaring game of Danger Patrol for Luke, Thor, Dro and Kira, and then got lots of great feedback from them, that I will endeavor to pass on to John. Kudos to Dro for his marvelous characterization of Dr. Ezymon, who managed to harness the most dangerous force in the Universe (Love) to the service of the Danger Patrol. Thor's encyclopedic knowledge of metal lyrics powered his Dr. Strange inspired hero, Dr. Phlogiston. And you've not lived till you've seen Luke embody a Robot Viking from the future with the mind of Werner Herzog. And tip'o'th'blaster to Saucy Sally Savage who dived in to save the day when it looked certain the boys were going to be overrun by Lava Striders and Laser Bees.

    - I found a nest of consimmers in the back of the always humming board gaming area, and got to play _Maria_ (a "waro" on the War of Austrian Succession), which is a huge favorite of mine.

    - Just getting to wander the exhibit hall and peer at cool stuff, and hanging out with friends I only get to see rarely.

    \n So, I never like talking about my own thing on a One Cool Thing, but man seeing people be excited for the game our little group's been working on for years has been awesome. And I don't mean that in a trite way. When Fred gave me the implicit permission (later made explicit) to ditch my Evil Hat booth duties to run pick-up games, I was fucking ecstatic. I got to hang out and play with people who were way excited about my game. And I owe Greg (the guy who uncorked this, if you will, with his initial offer) for facilitating that awesome.

    I lost my GM sheets (left in the hotel ) so I can't thank everyone by name, but I remember how awesome my pick-up players were. But man, I am still geeking over the awesome sauce y'all were.

    "Big Sword, No Clue" is one of my favorite Dresden Files aspects now, particularly for Wardens.

    Obviously, a lot of my "holy shit, that was awesome" moments revolve around our game, since that's much of what I did at the con. But man alive, that Freemarket game that Peter ran for us makes me want to buy it right the fuck now (thus winning his Thin Slicing contest). Seriously, can't we just get a matter printer on that shit? So that's still my "has nothing to do with Dresden" highlight. We played a MRCZ called Ultimate Experiences, involving a Sexual Performance Artist (me!), a Death Artist (which makes two Death Artists this con!) and a Romance Artist. And our handler, a "Social Accountant" -- total Vegas sleaze. It was metal. I should have been tired at 1am on Saturday. I should have been like "man, why didn't I go to bed an hour ago?" But I didn't. I was so pumped[1] after that game that Lenny (playing Vegas) and I went to Barley's for a couple drinks to wind down.

    - Ryan

    [1] Freemarket will make you invent new sports like Bear-blasting because you'll be too energetic for normal sports.

    \n The organization was screwed up - but I've come to expect that from Origins. That made it much less annoying.

    I did a war college lecture on "How to lose the peace 1815 to 1848" which was fun because I get to gig conservatives. Then I ran "Mongolian Goat Rodeo" and "Thunder Hamsters and the Temple of Cheese" in the board game area. These went well and I got an on line order for a Thunder Hamsters today. Got to do some pre-release runs of the half hour dungeon game I've been talking about on Praxis. Well recieved. I think they will sell well at Gen Con.

    It was great fun having Diner with Jason Morningstar and watching him start one of his Fiasco games. The only role playing I got to do was play part of a QAGS game my brother ran ("It's the end of the world Charlie Brown").

    Much of the show for me is about networking. The Founding Fathers game is a good example of that. Jolly Rogers Games is run by Jim Dietz. I met him 12 years ago and used to squat at his booth in Milwaukee before I started having my own booth. I always say hello and trade information. This year I pitched a game to him and he told me which company might go for it if I made a little change. I found out who I needed to talk to at that company and may make the pitch at Gen Con. Networking is not as fun as playing games but if you are wanting to play the "Selling Games game" then it is the place to play it. The "industry" people are not evil and can be very helpful. Going up to the retail lounge is a way of meeting store owners... Not a bad thing to do.

    Chris Engle
