    \n Oh, no, don't take out Locations. There's easily 36 meaningful locations at a restaurant (fast food or not):
    Manager's Office
    Walk-In Cooler
    Dry Storage
    Grill Line
    Prep Line
    Fryers
    Drive-Thru Window
    Counter
    Men's and Women's Bathroom
    Condiments and Drink Stand
    Waiting Line
    Seating Area
    Playground
    Parking Lot
    Dumpster(s)

    ...shit, maybe there's not that many. It's hard with just a fast food joint--if it were a Shenanigan's, you get a number of additional spaces from the Bar area and adjoining businesses. And there's more material for actual restaurant fiascoes than strictly fast food.

    I'd maybe expand it a bit, then--how about a Strip Mall, a la Clerks or Fast Times at Ridgemont High (yes, that was a mall, but we only saw the food court and theaters)? Then you could get all sorts of both teen angsty shit AND creepy over-age managers and hangers-on and street bums and what-not. And a ton of locations, from the fast food joint to the anchor grocery store to the video store or pawn shop.
    -----
    What I can't help but ask is: Why are you writing a play set about something about which you need to poll for help? Seems to me either your own experience has to inform a play set or you have to have a LOT of exposure to the "canonical" media (books, TV, movies) surrounding your chose setting. That's why I'd do a "The Office Space" play set before I'd do an "Academia" play set--I work in a corporate environment, and have for a variety of businesses, and I adore the media surrounding such (my only comic strip feed is Dilbert; the only TV series I watch is The Office... well, and Caprica, but that started last night). But academia? I know precisely squat about what that's like, and I'd have to go re-watch a lot of stuff to get a renewed feel for where the "ambition" and "low impulse control" meet.

    I'd say that's a tougher question to answer, but it's the one I'd consider first if I were you, Nathan.

    \n One thing a friend did for an upcoming playset set in a fairly tight space was to make locations within locations.

    1. Behind the Motel
    * In the pool, under the diving board
    * In the pool, by the stain in the shallow end
    * In the dumpster
    * etc.

    2. The Parking Lot
    * The mulch island
    * Inside a faded red Econoline van
    * Under an 18-wheeler
    * etc.

    Another upcoming set has locations that can only occur in flashback, which allows you to reuse the same limited set in interesting ways. This works better if there is some built-in change, but it's food for thought.
