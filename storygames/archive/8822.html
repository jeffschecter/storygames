    \nPlanetarium is a game in which you create a username, which gives you access to the first chapter of the game. You can play that first puzzle as much as you want, but you don't get access to chapter 2 for one real life week. As you advance through the chapters, the answers to the puzzles form a much larger puzzle, which you gain access to on week (chapter) 12. Then, once you solve the game, you gain access to the web forum at which you can discuss the game. For one week. Then your username expires. If you want to discuss the game further on the forum you must solve it again, which, again, takes 12 weeks.

    \n Slightly old now but good: lots of free play actively benefits children, lack of free play actively harms them, according to a number of studies. I find particularly interesting the idea that adding structure to a play activity limits its value - like adding rules to pretend play, for instance.
    But, Pellegrini explains, \u201cgames have a priori rules\u2014set up in advance and followed. Play, on the other hand, does not have a priori rules, so it affords more creative responses.\u201d

    This creative aspect is key because it challenges the developing brain more than following predetermined rules does. In free play, kids use their imagination and try out new activities and roles.

    \n Catalyst Game Labs is releasing a 20th anniversary version of their Shadowrun, Fourth Edition Core Rulebook. I used to love Shadowrun!

    But the reason I am posting is they are releasing an interesting product called the Runner\u2019s Toolkit. A box set of tools containing:

    * A deluxe, 4-panel hard-cardboard GM screen featuring a stunning full-panoramic illustration of Seattle.
    * A 32-page booklet of additional sample contacts and adventure seeds.
    * On The Run, a 56-page \u201cfirst run\u201d.
    * Anatomy of a Shadowrun, a fictional account of a shadowrun, annotated with how game rules apply to the fictional run; a perfect \u201cfirst-time-player\u201d companion.
    * Big Book of Tables, a 64-page booklet drawn from Shadowrun Fourth Edition as well as all five core rules expansions for an easy-to-use guide when trying to equip your characters with the latest and greatest during character creation.
    * The Pre-generated Auxiliary Character and Kit System (PACKS) allows players and gamemaster\u2019s alike to quickly assemble player and non-player characters using plug-and-play character profiles and equipment kits.
    * 6 heavy cardstock, double-sided reference sheets provide quick and easy reference for a variety of tasks, from how to resolve combat, to hacking, to summoning and more.

    Pretty damn cool!

    \n [Independent Insurgency] Episode 22: Talk To Me Now: Carrie Bernstein on Quitting Gaming

    In this episode, I speak with Carrie Bernstein about how she fell away from the roleplaying hobby. We discuss creativity, socialization, and the way the culture views the hobby.

    This episode is 18.5 MB big and 20:15 long.

    NOTE: This is the first in a new sub-series within The Independent Insurgency that I'm calling "Talk To Me Now." In this series, I'm going to have brief conversations with friends (and sometimes strangers) about things they are interested in or want to say about roleplaying games.
