\n I present to you the new Crowdfunding Collection Nr. 55, including every tabletop project started between 10.19 till 11.03.

After taking a short break thanks to attending SPIEL and Halloween, am back with no less than 65 projects.
The collection is headed by Stacy\u2019s (Dellorfano) Cancer Fight Fund. A project I consider of utmost importance as it is literally about a life of a gamer.
In regards of the tabletop projects, I discovered Dragons Conquer America during the SPIEL event. It\u2019s a game where people can utilize dragons to either conquer the Americas or defend them, maybe doing something else. The game uses the same system as the Fate RPG and got me to pledge for it.
You\u2019ll find two more cool Fantasy games in Seeker and Far Off Land (a game using Fate). Though you might as well support playing a Bourne-like techno-thriller with the Operators RPG, experience SF Horror with Xenomorphs or try to pull a cyberpunk heist with Toonpunk.
Among the Story Games you could choose one of several games collected under the Tragedies of Middle School game, create stories using Storyworld and use Foreplay\u2026 to indulge into potentially erotic tabletop RPG fields.
I would like to give a shout out to the the gamebook NEVERLAND. I backed the Alice in Wonderland KS from the creator and am very happy with it.
Among the German projects I would point to the, kinda strange, dice machine, the nice LARP photobook and the Jagged Alliance boardgame. The projects are all in English, well and while JA isn\u2019t a role-playing game, it\u2019s imho still worth checking.
Next to those you\u2019ll find plenty more, cool projects. More enticing games, dice, beautiful cards, cool containers (specially the Spanish book-container), Series, Accessories, Software and more. Well, and a few which are cancelled/finished thanks to my break. Just, see for yourself:

    \n I present to you the new Crowdfunding Collection No 58, including every tabletop project started between 11.06 till 11.13.

    This collection presents the Tablecloth II Smirking Dragon which offers new opportunities to dress up your gaming table.
    Yet I personally fallen victim to the charm of Oubliettes, Sorcery & Reavers (OS&R), an OSD inspired game, offering an interesting look and fascinating system. If you like your OSR more traditional you might enjoy Basic Hovels and Humans.
    Next to those Kids on Bikes is a PbtA inspired story game continuing the ever popular 80s hype. Worlds Unkown does promise to provide a generation spanning game using the Three-Stat systen, Monster Hunters the adaption of the famous book series for Savage Worlds and Marauder Task Force a quite wargamey tabletop RPG experience.
    Next to those you find many cool projects, like smoking dice towers, frosty terrains, a dungeon lootbox and more. Take a look:

    \n I present to you the new Crowdfunding Collection No. 59, including every tabletop project started between 11.13 till 11.25.

    Back from a small vacation break and first time round ready on a Sunday.
    My personal favorite of the list is Capharnaum. It\u2019s an originally French game set in an Arabian \u201e1001 nights\u201c setting, it sports a solid and tested system and really nice artworks. Next to offering a quickstart for free. I do really hope it funds and cracks some of those nice stretch goals open. Next to that I like Nu\xc6ther. Mostly for the fact that a character can happily mutate body parts, which sounds fun and seems supported by the system. Changeling: The Lost 2nd Edition aims to fund the latest iteration of the Chronicles of Darkness game. While I personally don\u2019t play CofD games to often, I heard nothing but good stuff about this edition, and am tempted to back it.
    As for the other games, Flash Gordon works the setting to be used with Savage Worlds and the Aventuria Compendium extends world of the game \u201eThe Dark Eye\u201c.
    Next to those you\u2019ll find aplenty other projects. Among those, cool dices, nice audio stuff, a RPG themed Alphabet book, interesting looking terrains and some non-rpg games that tickled my fancy. See yourself:
