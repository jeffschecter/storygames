\n This is a thread where you can tell us about any games that you're currently developing or playtesting. It's an opportunity to connect with potential playtesters.

Principles for posting:

* Tell us about your game(s) in one post. Feel free to mention your game even if you've talked about it in last month's thread. The idea is to inform us about what you're doing now.

* Optionally: tell us what you're hoping to achieve this month or what you did last month.

* If you want to link to another thread, post, or rules document, go for it.

* There's no maximum number of games you can talk about. If developing your game is a genuine focus for you this month, then put it up.


If you're responding to a game someone else has posted about:

* Number your response (first response: [1]; second response [2]; third response [3]). If you see that you're the second person to post, "Wow, that looks cool!" to a post here, then put a "[2]" at the top of your post.

* The fourth person to post should just take their comment and create a new thread (or talk on an existing thread about the game), to continue the discussion.

    \n I'm almost playtest ready with Touch of Evil.

    It's a gm-less horror game for 2+ players, inspired by the Stealing Cthulhu essay, and a series of videos from the Extra Credits series about Cthulhu and horror games in general.

    It offers two game versions :
    - one with a more Lovecraft-ian vibe (more about the uncanny and the unknown, the sinister, the weird)
    - one with a more Barker-esque vibe (more about flesh and blood ... but not in a splatter/gore way).

    Either way there is no necessity for previous knowledge of the reference literature; the rules should suffice in guiding the contributions of the players in order to summon the expected mood and feel.

    The games wants to break free from the classic "group of investigators" stereotype, and actually from the "investigation" structure altogether.
    It instead starts down in the normal life of a few unrelated people, all touched by a catalyst event.
    They will experience a growing sense of weirdness and anxiety in their life, encountering more and more signs that something is wrong in some way, until they either do something about it, or the shadows will catch up to them.
    They CAN try to avoid being involved (it's a sensible action, right?) but there is no real escape from the shadows, and actually this will have consequences for their loved ones.

    I have yet no idea of how much time a full story arc will require, but ideally it should be somewhere in the one-shot / short campaign range (1-3 sessions).

    The game uses a map and some cards (all special made) to keep track of the story-progress and a few more details.
    Characters (called Touched Ones, or TOs) have basically no character sheet, using instead a common sheet to note down a few important names.

    \n After the third playtest, Offline is being streamlined a bit more. The rules are there and work, now all left is finish three more comic pages to explain them, add some illustrations to the character creation part and more work on the procedures to GM chapter; I prefer not to call it GM advice since from the beginning I was planning a game for people with no previous experience on roleplaying.

    So far I think it has succeeded; the youngest player of the party came to the session with his mom (who hadn't played an RPG in her whole life and probably only overheard us playing a bit of D&D a couple of times) and we invited her to play with us. She did great and told us that she enjoyed the game, which meant the world to us.

    There was a couple of interesting misunderstandings: at first she tought she had to play more than one character. Another player new to RPGs thought he was going to play as the king, a position that he was only meant to have for the world creation procedure. Yet I thought it was interesting and suggested him to play as the prince.

    Playtesting also made me create a few more procedures to create adventures on the go, which I'm still working to simplify. After that part is done I should be looking for an inexperienced player to try and GM the game and see how it goes.

    \n Josh Jordan and I are working together to revise and re-release two of my games through Ginger Goat (the publishers of Doll and Heroine and some other cool games). We want your feedback to help us make the games tighter and easier to learn.

    \u201cThe Devil, John Moulton\u201d is a GM led occult western, where you play demon summoning outlaws searching the old west for the man you to whom you sold your soul and now you want it back. It can be played as a single session or short campaign.
    http://nickwedig.libraryofhighmoon.com/2014/04/the-devil-john-moulton/

    \u201cNameless Horror\u201d is a semi-competitive GMless single session game about ordinary people accidentally entangled in something horrible. Most of you won\u2019t make it out alive. The game uses some Tarot-like cards of ambiguous imagery to create dreamlike, inexplicable tales of terror.
    http://nickwedig.libraryofhighmoon.com/2013/10/nameless-horror-version-0-2/

    Right now we're looking for groups willing to playtest the original versions of the game and give us their feedback. Comment below or sent me a message privately or email me at nickwedig@yahoo.com if you are interested.
