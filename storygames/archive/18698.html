    \n For me, The Quiet Year is post-rock and ambient glitch.

    Album recommendations! For a really heavy, despairing game of The Quiet Year, I'd play F\u266f A\u266f \u221e by Godspeed You! Black Emperor. For a more exploratory game, I'd play Winter Hymn Country Hymn Secret Hymn by Do Make Say Think. For a haunted game of The Quiet Year, I'd play asil liseli by Nult (available free at the link). For a more busy, erratic world I'd play either Interregnums or Preparations by Prefuse 73. Music By Cavelight by Blockhead would be a really fitting album too.

    Song recommendations! "Forest Crunk" by Blockhead and "guda bahai" by Nult are my quintessential Quiet Year songs of the moment.

    \n For me the soundtrack would depend on the nature of the setting generated during the start of play. Generally things eerie and apocalyptic would work well, but you could mix it up depend on the setting created.

    You could break the game up into two stages by first mapping the initial setting out complete with abundances and shortages, to give you the chance to build your soundtrack, and then start playing through the year once it has been sorted.
