    I love Exalted for many reasons, and I end up using it mostly because of the following:

    1) The Stunt system systematically rewards lush and inventive narration, a game world that from bottom to top priviledges actions like 'I push off of the crossed-arm block of my enemy to jump and kick him in the head while I backflip' over 'I hit' him.

    2) Its a game that lets me get my Mythic on without being constrained to one cultural group. I get Mythic China-y bits along side Mythic Greek and Mythic Irish bits, for example. Exalted takes all of the fun of absurdly over-the-top hero myths and legends and puts it in your hands. It's a potpurri where you can have a Cyborg Hooker Cowboy alongside a Two-Fisted Brawling (Demi)Goddess-Queen with an internally consistent world that supports them both.

    To be true, the rules I ignore are for the most part, the same chunk of rules I tend to ignore in most games, that being the very very specific rules for things that go into levels of complexity beyond the core mechanic. Most of what I 'ignored' in Exalted were the places where the book said 'you can't do this.' For example: Normally PCs can only use one Charm (magic powers that resemble feats, be they for melee or bureaucracy or awareness, etc) per turn. It has a system for making 'Combos' of charms you can use together, but rather than forcing my PCs to learn the combo rules, I just decided to let them combo everything.

    I can understand the question of 'Why do you play Exalted if you ignore its rules?' but part of it is just loving the setting, and I'm retaining enough of the system that it's still definitively Exalted, it's just house-ruled a fair bit, especially for this short-term 'Start at the last Act of the epic story' game that I'm shooting for.

    I've entertained the notion of running Exalted with, say, Weapons of the Gods or True d20, but I think the rules of Exalted 2E are solid enough that it's easier for me to subtract some of the extraneous (to me) crunch than to make a mod for Exalted out of something else.

    Posted By: MRUnderwoodI can understand the question of 'Why do you play Exalted if you ignore its rules?'
    Not really, as you're using MOST of the rules as-is, anyway. No big deal there, it's not like you're drifting so far that you're all "Exalted... but with no charms!" or something like that. Your drift is what people tend to do with most large mainstream games (like "ignore counting the number of arrows or encumberance rules in d20" etc).

    Plus, I ignore the combo rules, too. It adds too much complication to an already complicated game. They seem to put the combo rules in there as a new realm for player specialization of the system (tweaking it to get the best results), but if you load up the combo list one at a time with 2-shot combos, it'll get boring as you use the combos over and over again. So yeah, I'm in full agreement with your drift there.

    -Andy
