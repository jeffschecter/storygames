Unnatural Selection

A roleplaying poem about an odd couple and their many, variegated larvae. A laugh riot usually, but at times a poignant look at strife within the home, brought on both by cultural differences between Stan and Sylvia Bumblebeetle themselves and the outside pressures of social stigma that occurs when a beautiful young hornet from way up in the rafters of the shed marries a blue-collar cockroach from out behind the shithouse.

    \nThe Unveiling!

    Drawing from Kafka and Cronenberg (with a peppering of Burroughs), The Unveiling! is a game of body horror, existential dread, and post-humanity. Characters are defined by their personal anxieties as well as "metamorphic traits" - their mutations. Those metamorphic traits slowly, throughout the first two acts of the game, tie a character's personal anxieties to wider societal issues. The third act is always about trying to make sense of those connections. Welcome to absurdity. Here are your new wings.

    (This is a game I've been contemplating for months.)

    \nSix-Legged Saviors

    Jules and Helen knew their torrid affair would cause trouble, but neither imagined it would lead to all-out war between the Beetle and Wasp clans. Now they must do whatever it takes to keep their hybrid children alive long enough to reach adulthood and hope their joint heritage can forge a new alliance.

    Six-Legged Saviors challenges the players to hide for a number of turns from two advancing pre-programmed armies with distinct fighting abilities using only their wits, their familiarity with the battlefield (deserted farmhouse, suburban home, or trailer park, depending on what the players choose), and four children with unknown influence on the combatants.
