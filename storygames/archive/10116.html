    \n My thinking when I (too-loosely) used the term "porn RPG" was something more like "sex RPG" or "foreplay RPG" than porn qua porn. (Though Contenders re-skinned for porn stars is... interesting.)

    I also am thinking something more along the lines of a LARP game, probably a basic system of resource mechanics (clothes?) underlying individual products (booklets) that provide situation, setting, and color cues. Probably cooperative play, though a "competitive," who-gets-to-be-on-bottom thing might work well, too. Something to provide some spice, and maybe even develop a story (or more likely, help a couple or couples tell their own story more fully), that I could take to my purchasing friend at Adam & Eve to get into their catalog (and my other friend at Adam & Eve would end up writing the catalog description for it, probably).

    The basic idea being have SOMETHING more structured that pure freeform for mechanics, and use that mechanic consistently across a huge array of "fantasies" for setting, genre, orientation, fetish, etc. ("Fantasy" in quotes to indicate the generalized meaning, not the specific literary or game genre.)

    So... the system, then:
    * Fan Mail mechanic opportunities are obvious: kisses, bites, *ahem* ... and so forth and so on.
    * Clothes as a resource: over clothes (jackets, sweaters, etc) worth less than main clothes (shirts, pants, skirts), which in turn are worth less than underclothes.
    * Look at How to Host a Murder Mystery DVD games... a possible "GM" or "Moderator" for the game, via the DVD menu? Different for each game, certainly (esp. orientation, gender; maybe a couple as GMs, for hetero).
    * Maybe look back at that Touching Game idea from a ways back: non-verbal communication; touch location has mechanical impact; fingertips versus fingers versus open hand versus gripping; light rough-housing (or not-so-light, for the BDSM games); tickling.
    * Multiplayer variations (heh) or separate game products? The Twister 'mechanic,' but with more body parts on the dial, and NOT so much colors on a mat as place on a partner (hmmm... a-whole-separate 'board game' product, there)?
    * Each participant plays more than one character, allowing for multiple scene configurations, love-triangle complications (with only one couple), "advanced" techniques like having a character die in the story and playing out the consequences (could be a BIT too "serious" for sexual play, though).

    Anyhow... just brainstorming (obviously), but I firmly believe this line of products would outsell any existing RPG as we mean it within a year or so, if well written. In fact, if one believes the stereotype of the "typical RPG geek," the VAST majority of the "Sex RPG" players would, perforce, NOT be gamers.

    \n Some good thoughts amongst the typical SG panning. In no particular order:

    * If such things exist, they're not on Adam & Eve's radar. And I'm not thinking of something railroady (script books) or silly fun (pirate hats?!?). Something more along the lines of an actual story game that is replayable and variable and more about exploration than "mere" foreplay.

    * Sex already has role playing, yes. Many LARPs run freeform, too... but LARP rules still sell, even as a TON of LARPs write their own heartbreakers. Not dissuaded.

    * Interesting map... but I don't think "all RP in sex is fetish," which seems to be the implication. Think Desperate Housewives, done via PTA, with actual physical contact ensuing where the commercial breaks would "fade to black." Speaking of which...

    * The fade to black works just fine... in existing RPGs. Sex happens in RPG scenarios, but it is rare (in my experience) for the players to engage in physical interactions as part of the "mechanics" of their characters having sex. OK, you might have folks in LARP give backrubs to "simulate" sex, but kinda the point of this idea is to figure a way to bring the interactions into the shared imagined space. In boffer LARP, you hit each other with safe weapons, rather than roll a d20 to hit. OK, now think that, but sex. (Heh... boffer sex toys might be another accessory line!)

    Anyhow, I'm not championing a One True Way. But I do think there's an unexplored marketing space, and I do think knowledgeable design could produce a more viable product than the silly sex board games (truth or dare repackaged) in Spencer's Gifts. I, like the OP, kinda hope to get some actual design notions rolling in this thread (if the sophomoric humor doesn't kill it like it usually does to any thread outside of a handful of... prolific posters'... safe zones).

    \n Last year for Game Chef, I wrote Sex/Magic, a game about modern-day folks developing ESP-like powers through sexual acts. Players learn about each other RL through truth or dare and then apply that to the fiction to intensify it. They reward each other's narration when it arouses them. The game gives a slight disadvantage to male-gendered characters.

    The key conceits of the game are:

    Truth or Dare in the resolution mechanic

    While the game has a typical card-based Fortune resolution mechanic, it can be trumped by a player performing a "truth or dare" challenge. The (rotating) GM offers the player a challenge, and if the question is answered or the dare is performed to the group's satisfaction, the player just wins. The rules discuss careful brinksman ship because the goal is to actually get the player to do stuff -- the challenger wants to find the hardest question/dare that the player will actually answer/perform.

    Players are never required to accept questions/dares. Ever. But if you care about winning enough, you can accept the challenge and win.

    The rules discuss how players should use question challenges to learn more about the players and then work that knowledge into the game to make it more compelling for them.

    I imagine that a group could play this without "going there" or getting very intimate. Really, the rules have a built-in safety valve because you never have to accept a challenge. If it's worth it to win, you will do it; if not, you won't. So tame groups could get by with "soft" question challenges: Describe your first kiss. When did you lose your virginity?

    Fan mail for arousal

    Whenever a player does or says something that arouse you, you draw a card and give it to them. Furthermore, you must give it to them without comment. That is designed to reward people for being sexy or interesting or whatever ("arousing," really, and I intentionally don't define the term) and at the same time try to keep it from getting too awkward.

    The card just says, "I like that, hot," without anyone ever needing to explain why or interrupt play.

    Trumps based on gender

    If you play a male-gendered character, you are slightly disadvantaged mechanically: your trump card is a Jack. If you play a female-gendered character, you are at a slight [edit:] disadvantage: Queens are trump. If you play a character of a "atypical" gender/sex, then Kings are trump. Otherwise, if I remember right, the card order goes by face value within the suit (and out-of-suit cards lose).

    I wanted to give an advantage to people playing women in the game. Really, though, I am tempted to change the rule to apply to the sex/gender of the\xa0player\xa0because that's where the help is really needed. I think that's what I really intended, anyway.
