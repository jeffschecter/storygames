Let me preface this by saying:
(1) I am currently playing/running a successful PTA game every two weeks with the gaming group I have access to. (A) Next Generation of (DC-based) Super Heroes making a name for themselves in the Chicago of tomorrow.
(1a) I am running a weekly home-brew game for five-year-old. A young speedster (with monster catching powers) exploring the virtual world (highly inspired by your favorite [X]-mon series.
(2) With same group (or close enough), I have attempted to run d20 (also Donjun) and really had it crash and fail.

I was thumbing through the World of Warcraft RPG monsters book recently and got a real hankering to run something either in the WoW world or something highly inspired by it. Lots of cool humanoid species with actual cultures attached to them doing heroic, even epic stuff.

I largely need to be talked out of thinking about running another game. I am out of resources (both players and time). And if, heaven forbid, I am convinced to run a game, I need to be talked out of D20. I *want* to like D20, but it requires too much time - too much prep time (as much as I love tweaking critters) and too much time spent in combat.

I *love* the collaborative aspects of PTA - so much so that our PTA game rotates the producer by scene. We talk through the basic episode before play and the basic scenes before each and then just role-play - we often forget to actually roll conflicts.

Anyway, enough rambling. Thanks in advance for your thoughts.

    Hi Blue.

    It sounds like you're really stretched for time right now. I have a lot of games I'd like to run too, but at some point there's just no opportunity. So you have to restrict yourself to the best, deepest ideas that appeal to the players you've got.

    It also sounds like the players you're gaming with aren't really into the hack & slash style of fantasy games. So, between the time stuff and the group stuff, it's going to be tricky.

    I'd recommend writing fiction, or finding another creative outlet that allows a tighter turn-around time between creative impulse and finished product--otherwise those ideas will keep building up, and it will get frustrating.

    Alternately, it might be good to put the books down and do something else that's fun. I found that doing martial arts was good exercise, kept me focused on what was important to me, and enabled me to see a different set of friends.
