Shreyas' discussion on character sheets got me thinking.

As I note in that thread, I've had some success running a game where essentially all the materials were displayed on a 'Science Fair' board I picked up a local office supplies store. This original use was one-part organic and one-part necessity, as I wanted something that was easy to pack up and pull out but that was also very visual.

As I said, the thread got me thinking. What if the design was very intentional around using the stand-up board? What sort of game/play would result from this? What limits to information storage would result?

More abstractly, I find myself wanting to have the game story/setting/themes tie back to the concept of the Presentation Poster. Where is the engaging story to be found? Perhaps the illicit after-hours activities of individuals who go to strange towns for Academic or Professional Conferences and Conventions? A cadre of professional travelling presenters who fight crime?!? Maybe the politics underlying the world-design discussions of a pantheon of deities....
