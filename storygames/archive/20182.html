    \n Amusingly this is exactly what I did with TMW and Castlevania in 2006 or so, I think I even put it out in Finnish as a pdf

    I couldn't open your take on the matter, but looking at my notes for it, I placed the scenario in year 1717, I had a new more explicitly gothic set of new dark fates, and instead of zodiacs each character had a family bloodline, known to them or not, involving them in the grand legend of of Dracula. (Belmont being, obviously, one of the options.) Unlike zodiacs, two characters could share a bloodline, in which case one would be the primary inheritor of the clan, while others would be secondary (and in some sense expendable) members of the tradition.

    Anyway, this is a pretty natural palette swap for the game. A very nice change of pace when you've played the original so much that you've temporarily had your fill of tengus.

    \n That's really cool! I'm glad we are on the same wave-length.

    In my head, the Castle is a living breathing creature of Chaos. So even if a player character is doing the work of Dracula or Death or whatever, the Castle can oppose them on their rolls (because... Chaos).

    I did four character types, "Demon Hunter", "Treasure Hunter", "Belmont", and "Other..."
    The starting Trust is pretty lop-sided, so even if you are an Ally with one type, they may not be Ally with you. It helps spice things up, and that initial mismatch evens out in later chapter.

    I'm not sure why the files I have would not open for you. I can try to download them on my end and see what's what. Do you still have any notes on your hack? I would love to host them on my page as well, if you are interested.
