    \n My experience so far.

    Store Owner: Games that are different and don't repeat sales with splat books, mini's, etc. "Meh..."

    Store Owner: Games that are different and come in an all you need package to introduce non-gamers to games. "That sounds neat when can you demo".

    Its all in the packaging (and the money). I think a lot of these games could be packaged as board (or card) games and get the same (or better) play experience. Now that doesn't mean that you can make enough to make it worth it. I just think its a good strategy to get into the hobby store.

    \n Yeah, a boardgame-like packaging seems like a definite plus in selling games. They're hellishly difficult to make, though. Chris is some sort of insane small press boardgame superman, but for the rest of us making a rpg into a boardgame will pretty easily tack on 15+ dollars in price and an hour of extra work per copy made. It's not very attractive if your game can be reasonably published as a book. There's some signs in the boardgaming scene that they're going to a similar direction with the indie titles; if you publish your game as a pdf file, then the component creation work can be feasibly distributed to the end-users. Quality suffers, but the price of the game can be much lower.
