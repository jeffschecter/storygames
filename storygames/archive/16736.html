\n So I'm looking at a bunch of the apocalyptic offspring and trying to get my head around the ways they all handle different aspects of the system. Obviously there are similarities across the games, but I'm more interested in the differences.

Example: For the connections between characters we have Hx, Bonds, Strings, etc. How are they different? Which ones do what things well? Which ones have let you down?

Different Example: Each game seems to have a different take on awarding experience. Which ones have led to realistic character change? Which ones have you found most satisfying? Why?

A Third Example: Your example. Please give it.

    \nCombat!

    AW combat can get crazy and overwhelming (in the fiction, not the mechanics), but as long as you have armor you'll be okay (seriously, armor isn't foolproof but unless you're up against grenades, 2-armor is all you need). If you don't, you're going to die! Run!!

    In Sagas of the Icelanders, combat is very simple, and extremely deadly. Given that the traditional Icelandic battle was a duel, this is appropriate. Armor makes no difference (at least in the current write-up). There are no hit points. You roll accept a physical challenge to see how well you fight, and then you roll suffer harm to see if you died or not. It's pretty rough, but it's great how intense it makes each combat.
