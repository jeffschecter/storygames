    \n I haven't touched either Marvel or Dragonlance Saga since...uh...1999? Still have ALL the books, though, and at the time, I really liked them a lot, though there were some things (mostly in Dragonlance) that seemed untenable (certain monsters that could NOT be attacked because of their high scores, etc.)

    The most common things I've seen are losing the cards and rolling 10-sided dice instead, with 10s exploding.

    Uh...let's see. I've used the Dragonlance magic system by hacking into numerous other games (mostly home-brews or hacks of other games). Even tried to use it for World of Dungeons (though that didn't work out too well).

    \n I used quite a few of Steve Kenson's tweaks for DL Saga. I rarely used any for Marvel, but my favorite hack was "GM starts with all the Dooms!" Which helped make game play work a little smoother, and made the PC's more bold later on in an adventure (and knowing they'd struggle early on against the resource heavy foe.)


    Tommy Brownell over on Rpg.net (and elsewhere) recently shared a lot of the material he has backed up from a variety of sources.
