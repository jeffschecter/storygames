Scattershot [5]It's a simple mechanic. Roll 2 ten-sided dice and subtract the sum from your target number. Use dice rolls to introduce uncertainty, but never let them block play. The degree of success or failure is applied as complications rather than pass or fail.

Target numbers are any stat, skill or power on your pre-generated character write-up. The milieu is provided as the gamemaster's write-up.

In no particular order, take turns describing to the others what is happening in play. Anyone can propose the next scene. Base it on play so far or one of the write-ups, but not anything else.

Pacing is a gamemaster's top priority. From this proposal only he sets the scene and then play begins in medias res, with the relevant character's first significant action.

Experience dice are six-sided plot devices that can be rolled to modify any other die roll. For example, if you believe a roll should be more difficult due to the circumstances, penalize the roll with one of your experience dice. Once rolled, they are returned to the shared pool of dice.

Whenever you need more Experience dice, you can; invoke a trope on anyone's write-up to get 'keepers', roll to an advantage trope with 'gimmes', do something awesome and get 'freebies', see something awesome and give 'freebies' or you can always 'loaners'.

Tropes are color-coded into roughly three groups; red tropes involve overcoming opposition, blue tropes improve escapism and yellow tropes make play like a story.

Between scenes, you can use experience dice to add or subtract development points to any stat, skill, power or trope on your write-up. You can also spend a development point to 'buy back' one experience die. Roll as many as you want (and add more as you go); a total of 6 gives you 1 development point, 10 gives you 2, 14 for 3 and so on by fours.

It's only natural to have disagreements. If no one can offer an acceptable compromise, auction off the results for the situation. Taking turns, each party bids experience dice. The highest bid solution is followed and the lowest bidder receives all of the dice bid.

FAQ
What if I want to use a plot device when there isn't a roll to add it to?
Simply roll experience dice and treat that as the degree of complication.
What if I saw something really cool in the game book and want to propose a scene based on that?
Look for some way that it is relevant to play-so-far or any write-up. Otherwise, add it to the background section of your write-up; not everything that you expect from your write-up will necessarily be written down.
Can I keep any 'gimmes' that I don't roll?
Gimmes can only be used in the roll granting them and must be discarded afterwards.
Do I have to pay interest on 'loaners'?
No, but don't forget 'payback'! [This rule is undergoing revision.]
Can I use experience dice to change the past?
Yes. If anyone has problems with a retro-active change, you can always have an auction to solve it.
