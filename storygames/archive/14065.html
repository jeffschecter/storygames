    \n You could try to make each session or couple of sessions a chapter. Then, like a serial, when the chapter was over, you could the story to start at a new point in time, even before the previous session. Allow people to restat or change characters, and then make sure everyone's Keys are set up for the next chapter.

    Voila! You now have a robust version of In A Wicked Age and can do stories like Fafhrd and Grey Mouser and Conan the Barbarian.

    \n Changing the XP cost for an advance is good. You'll have to tune it based on how many characters you have, how long your sessions are, and how many sessions you plan to play. That's a tough thing to get right, so you'll probably end up tweaking it as you go (just let everyone know from the start that XP costs will fluctuate).

    Also, acquiring new Traits and Secrets involves fictional action. You need a teacher or a place to study or something. So the availability of those things has an effect on advancement, too.

    My general advice is: err on the side of easy advancement, even for a long game. Keys are more fun when the players can see a tangible reward from them sooner rather than later. The action and the reward should have a solid connection.
