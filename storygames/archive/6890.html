\n Too much theory talk and introspection around here. With your help, I will be the change I seek!

OK, it\u2019s 1937. I need:

One weird and scary animal (closed)
Three two one reprehensible villains who are not Nazis (closed)
Three two one mysterious treasures that are not supernatural but are awesome (closed)
Three two one truly exotic locations that are not in a jungle or desert (closed)
Three two one crazily dangerous situations that don\u2019t involve traps (closed)
Three two one plucky allies who are not sexy journalists, orphans, or grinning natives (closed)
One plausible-but-fictitious weapon or device (closed)
One surprising and vaguely unwholesome love interest (closed)

You can contribute, at most, three items, and none from the same category. I challenge you not to make this a joke - make your entries as fun and engaging as possible. The cheap laugh is a failure of imagination. Go!

    \n
    \nMysterious Treasure: The Antikythera Mechanism is an ancient mechanical calculator (also described as the first "mechanical computer"[1][2]) designed to calculate astronomical positions. It was discovered in the Antikythera wreck off the Greek island of Antikythera, between Kythera and Crete, in 1900. Subsequent investigation, particularly in 2006, dated it to about 150-100 BC, and hypothesised that it was on board a ship that sank en route from the Greek island of Rhodes to Rome, perhaps as part of an official loot. Technological artifacts of similar complexity did not reappear until a thousand years later.[3]
