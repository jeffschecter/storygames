\n Hey everybody,

My indie arcade group Babycastles has been asked by some of the folks working on IndieCade East to assemble a "zine reading room" that will be freely available to all attendees during IndieCade East at the Museum of the Moving Image, February 14-16.

In addition to game-related zines, we're also interested in including indie tabletop game books. We have a bunch of stuff, but if you or someone you know would like to make sure your zine or game is included, please get in touch with us (meow->babycastles_com).

Some particulars:
- Our budget for this installation is exactly zero dollars, so if you're not going to be personally attending IndieCade East, don't look to send us something you want back.
- Likewise, we're not going to print and assemble anything longer than a couple of pages, if at all.
- Any zines or games that are sent for this exhibition WILL ultimately end up in a more permanent zine library where they can be accessed for free by the public. Probably somewhere in Brooklyn.
- If you live in the New York area, or will be attending IndieCade East, you can loan stuff for the duration of the show and pick it up when it's over. But remember that the public will have had free access to it for 3 days, so some wear is to be expected, and we can't be responsible if somebody slips it under their coat and walks away with it forever (sorry).
- There will be no official games-on-demand-type folks to run games (although if it were to happen spontaneously, that's fine).
- You can say you've had your work featured at the Museum of the Moving Image, a major New York institution, as part of IndieCade East.

If that all sounds good to you, get in touch!
