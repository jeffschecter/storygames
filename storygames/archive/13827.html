\n I'm just wondering, because it seems like lately, I've been all over the map. In what proportions do people play games, versus design new games, versus talk about games? It seems like I personally go in cycles where I'm designing a lot, then playing a lot. Lately I've been talking a lot about games, which is new for me. So I'm just curious what other people's percentages look like, and how they change over time.

    \n Let's see, we typically play once a week, for 3-4 hours at a time. I spent maybe another hour spread out over the week on player prep (thinking about the game, making plans, organizing ideas, etc.), which I'll count as part of the playing time (because it is).

    Over a typical week, I'll read enough gaming-related stuff online to eat up 2 hours, probably. Most of that will just be reading, only a tiny fraction will be spent replying (I'm counting this one), so how much of that counts as "talking about games" is sketchy. I'll have to get kind of handwavey with my calculations here, and also factor in the time we spend at our gaming sessions talking about how the game is going and discussing the system, etc..

    Best guess: about 80% play, about 20% talking. Not a game designer, so that's about it.

    Play 80%
    Design 50%
    Talk 80%

    Play 40%
    Design 15%
    Talk 45%
