\n Got a chance to play Pocket Danger Patrol at GoPlay Thursday in East Portland last night, with Joel, two other Mikes, and a lady whose name I cannot remember for the life of me! (I should start writing down names at some point). It was a blast, though it turned out to be a cliffhanger-esque TPK.

It started with the Mayor of Rocket City giving a speech at the unveiling of his new pet project, the Ultra-Tram railstation. The members of the Danger Patrol, Urthak the Plutonium Man (Atomic Detective played by Joel), Professor Robot (the Robot Professor played by Mike #2), White Shadow (Ghost Warror played by Mike #3), and Purity Hammerhill (Psychic Daredevil played by Mike #3's wife) were all guests of honor after saving the project previous from Stygian saboteurs. Just as the festivities were about to start, strange harmonics started the magnetic rail system to start vibrating eerily, when SUDDENLY a Portal to Another Dimension bursts forth, sucking the Ultra-Tram and several technicians into its vortex. Out of this portal, quantum Zerosaur beasts rush through, herding the bystanders for an easy slaughter, directed by Extradimensional Wranglers in silvery suits and giant square helmets, who whipped them into a frenzy with their quantum-flux whips.
As chaos ensued, a lone guardian, the Champion of the Mastermind from Beyond Time and Space, stepped through the portal, keeping it safe from interlopers.

This looks like a job for DANGER PATROL!

Purity Hammerhill started things off by blasting away at the wranglers, taking them down pretty handily, but now the Zerosaurs were uncontrolled and even more dangerous than before! (Complication) White Shadow was doing his best to battle a few Zerosaurs, while Urthak went straight for the Champion, using his mental superiority to CONQUER HIS MIND! He discovered the true nature of the Mastermind from Beyond Time and Space, who was intent on using Rocket City as his staging point to conquer the rest of the Universe, and the Danger Patrol could NOT allow that to happen! But the backlash caused by the energies of the portal shook loose something EVIL within Urthak's atomic brain!! Finally, Professor Robot zoomed in, using his Quantum Processors to interfere with the harmonics that allowed the Portal to exist, and caused it to blink out of existence! Though not before the Mastermind popped out of the portal in his silvery Warp Ship, and smacked Professor Robot away with its etheral-mechanical Tentacles-That-Are-Not-Tentacles.
At this point, I (Mike #1) get to make the Danger Meter roll (three Hits), so the City is kind of imperiled, but not badly.
Play continues, and most of the threats get dealt with pretty easily, but at SERIOUS COST. Purity Hammerhill ends up weakening the Mastermind by psychically joining forces with Urthak (not knowing about the evil within him), but rolls TWELVE total Danger. The exertion weakens her to the extent that her body falls into a telekinetic coma, while her psychic consious combines with Urthak's, creating Kahtru, the PSYCHIC GESTALT WARLORD! (a new Threat). Professor Robot heroically sacrifices himself to finish off the Mastermind (in turn creating a radioactive fallout complication), Urthak's attempt to save the remaining citizens leaves him vulnerable to Kahtru's attack (taking Urthak down, and turning him into a living psychic power-gem), and soon, only White Shadow is left! After defeating most of the Zerosaurs, he, too, is brought down by the might of Kahtru, though he is able to disrupt the psychic connections, causing the Psychic Gestalt to dissipate.

...but could this truly be the end of the Danger Patrol??!! Turn in next week!

    \n When I was running the aforementioned session, I rolled for the first couple of Threats, and used that as inspiration for the plot and the other threats. If I remember correctly, the first three things I rolled were Interdimensional Vortex (which seems to always pop up), Psychic Soldier, and Ancient Creature. I gave some flavor to the current threats (making the Ancient Creatures be the Zerosaurs, the Psychic Soldier being the Mastermind's Champion, etc.), and built the other Threats based on those (multiple Zerosaurs, Wranglers, the Mastermind himself, etc.).
    As for the starting situation, I just pull some random plotline or starting scene from any number of movies or cartoons I've watched. In this case, the idea of the unveiling of the Ultra-Tram came from the start of the anime series Project Blue Earth S.O.S. (which, really, inspires half of my Danger Patrol nuttiness). I've had a few adventures based off episodes of Top Gear, a couple stolen from the old Flash Gordon and Buck Rogers black-n-white serials, and a bunch taken from the Men In Black cartoon.
    I also figure that with certain Threats, they may be sufficient trigger to develop a situation. Or you could just start with a battle in media res, and let the "Previously On..." bits fill in the details for an actual plot.

    \n Just downloaded Pocket Danger Patrol last night, and I'm looking for a spare slot in my schedule to try it out. I love the mechanisms, but it was a shame not to see the Previously On ... or Interludes/Investigation factored in. I know the former just gives players a chance to include something they want to see in the game, but I was wondering if they could have a mechanical effect as well.

    So, off the top of my head (and with a 'no play experience' caveat):

    Before the game each player narrates a Previously On ... This gives them one bonus D8 (so no danger), but also a Threat D4 which they can spend once per game - the two must be rolled together. ("Of course; this would explain that mysterious theft from the Museum!"). The Bonus Die represents investigation, preparation or prior knowledge. The linked Threat represents potential complications arising from it.

    Assuming the first action sequence ends with unanswered questions (and that should be easy to engineer whether the players win or lose), you get an interlude. Do this the same as the Previously On ... - narrate a short scene of investigation, interaction or preparation, and get your Bonus D8 and Threat D4. Then go into the next action scene.

    A simpler way would be to just have the Bonus D8, but allow it to generate Danger, but I do rather like the way that in the Pocket version each type of die has a specific function - D10 for Abilities, D6 for Danger, D4 for Threats and D8 for Bonuses.
