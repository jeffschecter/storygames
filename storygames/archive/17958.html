    \n It's crucial that the PCs are factions that compete or intrigue with each other, otherwise the game wont proceed. My group never grasped that the first time we played, so we tried to hang around like the posse in The Walking Dead. Needless to say, that session wasn't good. I would rather play it as Battlestar Galactica with factions fighting within the community while a threat (zombies/cylons) is growing outside the community.

    \nTry to find a conflict in each scene. Push towards one.

    It might be over who gets to hold the shotgun, or who eats the last donut, or who gets away with the bag of money.

    Zombie Cinema is about the characters, but they are in a tense and life-threatening situation, and even petty things drive a wedge sometimes. The game lives and dies on that tension.

    \n What Harlequin said. Explain to the players that the game is flat-out about the characters not sticking together in a crisis, not about fighting zombies. It's not just stylistic advice: mechanically you cannot move up the "escape path" if you don't have disagreements between the characters. If you all stick together, everyone gets eaten.

    Bring it up when people are making characters. Encourage them to make characters who will not get along. Watching your character get eaten after being a jerk should be fun.
