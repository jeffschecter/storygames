    \n define "pirate" game
    I mean, is this just vanilla fantasy adventuring with ships?
    Or something more focused like Poison'd?
    Do you need the rules to inject pirate-y elements in the game, or do you just need a system that will support YOUR pirate-y ideas and descriptions?

    Like... something like the TV Series "Black Sails" is just a very tense relationship/powerStruggle game in a pirate-y setting.
    (Game of Thrones on the high seas?)

    You could use Fate Accelerated for that.
    Or HillFolk.
    Or FateLess if you are of the gm-less persuasion.

    \n I don't really think that everyone being pirates is all that different from everyone being dungeon-crawlers. Maybe more honest to the theme in an architectural "material honesty" sense: we're here for the pillage and derring do, with maybe some heroism!

    I would look at rewriting a couple of the Alignment and Race moves. You can do the Inverse World thing of using Drive Moves instead, or Planarch Codex's heritage moves.

    Do grab a bunch of the other classes people have written. I've got a folder with printouts of all the classes I found interesting. Plus, Inverse World has some classes that would probably help, especially the Captain.

    Inverse World's vehicle rules work pretty well for ships, though you might want to also consider that Apocalypse World's Driver can have a boat for a vehicle. Inverse world also has an island generator that can be repurposed fairly easily.

    Now, some pirate-specific stuff would be nice, but like I said if I was making my own hack I'd start with that.

    Well now that you ask - I'm leaning towards something closer to One Piece level of Fantasy - a different world, some special abilities, and maybe a couple of fantasy races. How would Dungeon World's classes hold up under everyone being "pirates?".
    \nWith this in mind, I'd go for Anima Prime. "We're pirates together" is your Group Seed, while fantasy races are Marks - or maybe set packages of Powers. One opposing crew (+ ship) may just be one Swarm, for a simple battle, or for a more intricate "boss fight" treat the enemy ship as a huge monster, maybe made up of multiple Individual sections.

    Or, a more extreme take: Mist-Robed Gate. \\m/_
