    \n I like clean, simple things.

    In game systems, I look for a game that does a great deal with a very little; I'm looking for devices with smooth results, and for universal rules-tools that are as basic as a hammer, as well as simple and memorable bits. In Space:1889, I loved the fact that assigning stats was "write in the numbers 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, in whatever order you like". In Castle Falkenstein, the card-dueling struck me as ultra-cool. Reading In Spaaaace, the token-shuffle system had huge appeal to me, being a clean and systematised flow.

    Elsewhere, the cover of my most recent game is white with a logo on it, and that's it. The main page of my quiet little website is vanilla-plain. Speaking of vanilla, I love that look on this forum. I have no cell phone, very few gadgets; I wear plain colors without designs on them, mostly one-color turtlenecks and black pants; only one of my shirts has a collar.

    This is one slice of my stuff. There are others, that say different things. But that's one.

    \n I like Soul Gazing. I like to really know the people in my life in ways that maybe other people in their lives don't know them.

    To that end I like games that require us to reveal things about who we are. I like games that are played close to the characters and leave the setting as window dressing and plot as something best understood in hindsight. I like hard divisions of authority so that when my stuff, clashes with your stuff we BOTH end up some where uncomfortable. I like rules that back players into creative corners and disallow easy ways out. I like games that don't hard code genre assumptions as genre assumptions are often emotional safety nets.

    I want a game to change me, change you and teach us each something about each other that only the people who participated in that particular game will ever fully understand.

    Jesse

    \n I am lazy as I am clever. In this way, the low or zero prep style of many contemporary game has appealed to me greatly.

    I like big social questions and games that let explore alternative social arrangements. I also like asking hard questions and having characters that are weak but passionate (or desperate).

    So, mostly I play Vincent's games, or games I come up with.

    My favourite part of gaming is learning about how other people see the world, society and themselves (same things). Social imaginaries are the best ways to get at this. But that's me, the good sociologist.

    Did you know that most often I find that people value duty and love over freedom?

    \n I'm just going to spitball a bunch of stuff and let you (Joe, anyone else) speculate as to what it may mean:

    * Game styles run almost exclusively toward small press creations now. The most mainstream games I've played the past 5 years have been Trail of Cthulhu and Warhammer Fantasy Roleplay v3. Within the world of small press games, the whole family of Burning Wheel games (in order of preference: BE, Blossoms, BW, MG, Jihad) gets the most love.

    * I regularly participate in two game-related forums and irregularly participate in three others.

    * I have a standing Tuesday evening game night, which 80% of the time is one ongoing game or another. "Ongoing" in this case means something with more than a couple sessions. Games rarely last more than 10 or so sessions.

    * I have a fairly regular ever-other-Thursday group as well. The title turnover is way WAY higher here, and it's exclusively small press games.

    * Every regular roleplayer in my world is white and male. I am not the oldest but I'm in the top 20% bracket for age.

    * I spend maybe an hour at most doing game prep if I'm running something. On that note, I'm the one running something 75% of the time (both groups).

    * Both Tuesday and Thursday guys do a LOT of playtesting. Those playtests come either from me or Eric Boyd (Committee for the Exploration of Mysteries).

    * I rarely post about gaming subjects on my Facebook account. When I do it's almost immediately followed by "wtf?" comments from my non-gaming friends, and my response is to be self-deprecating about being an inveterate gaming nerd.

    * I feel most comfortable playing and running RPGs that require a strong GMing role as well as provide a clear delineation of responsibilities between players and GM. In turn I feel least comfortable, and derive the least amount of pleasure, from games that actively mess with that arrangement. So, like, I've had a lot of fun with Lacuna, InSpectres, Dust Devils and DitV but I've had trouble nailing down and extracting the fun from Shock:, Universalis, and PTA.

    * When I'm playing I'm usually either a total spotlight whore or find myself in a peer-coaching type of position. If I'm playing something I'm super-familiar with or have previous GMed, I'll often jump in with my own resolution rather than waiting for the GM to tell me what to roll.

    * I actively dislike most fantasy fiction but really love playing and GMing in fantasy settings. The opposite is (mostly) true of sci-fi: Love reading it, have a hell of a time running in it.

    * My fantasy settings are usually apocalyptic: fallen empires, lost secrets, strongmen leaders, creatures and other horrors unambiguously tied to whatever went wrong.

    * My scifi settings usually feature evil corporations and grand conspiracies.

    * If I buy a game it's only with the intent to run it and never, ever to just read it. I am rarely an early adopter of new hotnesses, and prefer a game be out a year or so before I commit to buying it. I'll typically read its rpg.net reviews, follow conversations on some forums and read its FAQ before buying. The last new hotness I jumped in on without enough research was WFRP3 and I regret that decision. The next new hotness I will be jumping in on without enough research is Freemarket.

    At no point in this exercise did anything jump out as me saying anything special about myself. I guess I hold games to high standards, don't want to waste my time or money, know my players very well, am generally protective of my hobby, and am kind of a showoff within my "circle of trust".
