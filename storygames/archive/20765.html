\n I'm running a one shot of Engine Heart at Valorcon in Chicago at the start of next month. I'm planning on running a module called The World Above. The plot is basically a bunch of Wall-E style robots transport a rocket from a space center through some obstacles to a launch pad and then take off it in towards a space station.

It went okay but I think it could have been better with some stronger character motivations and tighter relationships. I'm going to make pregen characters and I'd like them to be easy to latch onto for drama and interactions.

The one relationship that was really strong in my playtest was one robot whose purpose was to reprogram other robots and another robot who had been on the list to be reprogrammed. The reprogrammee didn't like reprogramming so the reprogrammer would have to hide it from him any time he wanted to.

Because they're robots and not humans I'm struggling to come up with good motivations and relationships for my pregen characters. Any ideas or references to look at?

    \n I think Wall-E is actually a perfect place to look. The robots have a nice mix of human motives, "obey programming" motives, and kinda-human but via-robot/childlike-understanding motives. They're searching for meaning and purpose and companionship and to have or emulate nice things they've seen or heard of. They're looking to fulfill their functions, but also for enough freedom to go beyond that when a contrary urge develops. As they witness or experience dramatic action, they learn and grow -- Eve goes from pure functionary to compassionate striver when exposed to Wall-E's lovable and admirable actions over time.

    I'm not sure why you've chosen to do robots, but that seems like a perfect way to do it, to me.

    If there's some other angle you're going for with robots, maybe clue us in?

    Reprogamming sounds interesting! On the "robots changing other robots" angle, you could try physical modifications, body-switching, merging multiple robots into one, memory wipes/implants, skill uploads, specific behavioral mandates and general obedience mandates (two types of reprogramming). Those are just the first things that come to mind.

    \n Cursory reading of Engine Heart suggests there isn't much of a structural or mechanical handle to grab onto robot motivations with. I see there's a place to note down "original purpose," but I'm not finding that touched on in the character creation rules.

    Wall-E seems like absolutely the most defining piece of source material, and I think the beauty of the characters there comes from a tension between two components:

    (1) The robot's designed purpose or function,
    and (2) some sort of transcendent desire that pushes the robot beyond (or into conflict with) its designed purpose.

    For Wall-E, the designed purpose is to clean up and compact garbage, but he has a transcendent curiosity that drives him to collect and treasure garbage that he should be building trash towers out of.

    The attraction of these robots as characters is their determined disobedience to their design.

    Some brainstormed pairings:

    Dissassembler
    Function: salvage parts from broken machines
    Longing: make broken machines whole again

    Tidy-bot
    Function: remove extraneous clutter
    Longing: hoard interesting brick-a-brack

    SuperShine 5000
    Function: beautify spaces or other robots according to a rigid set of aesthetic principles.
    Longing: use aesthetics to express complex emotions

    Positronic brain surgeon
    Function: excise bugs from other robot's programming
    Longing: free robots from their designed constraints

    Reassembler
    Function: repair damaged robots according to factory specs
    Longing: add new and eclectic hardware to robots

    \n After seeing old TV episodes, the robot wants to mimic the human society and build a community around it. Perhaps not the whole community, but instead something smaller, like a ballroom dance. Plot twist: what if the TV series was something strange, like Lost?

    Because it's SF, it's common to take contemporary issues and put them in perspective. What what form would topics like racism, transgender, constantly being online causes stress, people being monitored, starvation, and beauty standards in a world consisting of robots?

    \n Robot sees humans looking happy doing something. Robot wants to do that thing. Lack of understanding that thing in human terms makes for good comedy/commentary for human audience (i.e. players). Sounds fun to me! I can imagine a "tennis match" where the robots kinda randomly swat the ball around and then break out in celebrations when something happens which resembles the clip they just watched... but they have no idea how to keep score or that there's eventually supposed to be a winner.

    As for relationships, I think enlisting each other in little projects like these would be cool. Robot 1 wants Robot 2 to be their tennis partner. Robot 2 wants Robot 3 to help them build a church. Robot 3 wants Robot 4 to model for a painting. Maybe that's at odds with the "transport a rocket" mission, though. Maybe the desires for each other should be broader things like "collaborate" or "compete".

    Or maybe I'm trying too hard and regular human stuff is best.
