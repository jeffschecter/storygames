I was wandering around Melbourne yesterday listening to the Sons of Kyros democasts (which rock) from the GenCon floor. I had a couple of reactions:

Best Friends: The demo sounded great, but nowhere near as great as the demo Malcolm Craig ran for a few of us. The only difference, as far as I can tell, is that Malcolm took a couple of seconds to have the group establish a context before getting us to create characters. Without that, Jeff & Judd & co seemed to gravitate to high school girls, with fairly generic Stuff and Nonsense. We ended up as concubines to the Emperor of China at the funeral of his principal wife and heir. I had "Poisoned Hairpins" as my stuff and "Induced the heir to kill the Empress for my sake" as my Nonsense, and the other players were doing even cooler stuff. Seems like that one step - establishing the context before the characters - had a big impact.

Mortal Coil: This game sounds great. I'm thinking of using it for Mu, actually. But at this stage I comprehensively fail to grok it tactically. I'm not sure why you'd go only one action, why you'd go many, and what the consequences are. Anyone got a better handle on it?

Gregor? There MUST be a story there.... and it becomes clear that the rules aren't just a clever way of generating everybody's scores, they're a way of instantly building a seething twisted mess of insecurities, where nobody can ever see their own best qualities.

    Yeh, I'm totally stealing chargen from Best Friends. It is so freaking slick, I can't stand it.

    I actually wish that the Mortal Coil demo had had more of what I really dig about the game, creating the setting through the use of Magic Tokens. But hey, you can't do everything in a demo. Jye, the fact that your token splitting is hidden makes it very tactical as to whether you split actions or not. If you split your actions into two attacks, at least one of them will probably get through, unless the other person splits his to do two defenses - but the one that gets through won't be as potent. Or you could stack everything one on passion and hope to sledgehammer through. Or you could defend and attack...and so on. The more opponents, and the more you pile on passions, the trickier it gets. There's definitly a learning curve to how to and when to split actions.

    From the Mortal Coil FAQ on RandomWiki ((http://random.average-bear.com/MortalCoil/HomePage))

    Multiple Actions

    QUESTION: I'm not clear on why you would take multiple actions when it's only 1-on-1.

    ANSWER ''Say you have, like, 7 action tokens, and you're in a fistfight, right? Well, you could put all 7 into Force, and punch the other guy real good. But he could also put all his tokens into Force, and you both punch each other real good. Or, you could take multiple actions, and put 4 onto Force to punch him and 3 onto Grace to dodge whatever he's gonna do. Then, if he puts all his tokens into punching, he'll punch you less well, and still take a full-bore hit from you.
