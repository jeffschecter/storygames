    August 2006
    OK, I've got some raw stats from Dreamhost. Statwise, we're doing allright. Bandwidth is pretty steady about half of that of the Forge. MySQL impact is pretty cool on most days, not a huge amount of slowness and the like. And as you can see on the left, users online at any given time has gone from about 5 to about 20 during the (US) day.

    So, before, my thoughts were always, "OK, X is a problem and needs to be resolved" (with the software, hardware, etc). Right now, I don't really see any problems with the site.

    However, some things kinda make me nervous. Namely the number of active users joining. Now, mind you we've got probably about 50 or so users who joined early on and for one reason or another simply aren't visiting the forums anymore. So no problems there. But we're getting a lot more continuous, interesting conversations. The Categories division is cool and all, but I wager most people use the "see all categories at once" layout when browsing anyway.

    Long story short, I'm thinking that in another 100 active users or so, the architecture of this site's BB Tool (Vanilla) is going to go from "Cool, and not really hindering (or helping) discussion" to "Cool, but starting to hinder discussion".

    Truth be told, in some 7 months of being online, we've got half the topics going of the official Lussumo forums, and definitely a higher number of posts per day. I'd say that our board is probably one of the top five busiest instances of the Vanilla Forum software to date, if not THE busiest board for the software.

    That kinda makes me nervous (my job during the day is troubleshooting disaster recovery computer situations), because:
    1) Vanilla's coding is TIGHT. But, it's also pretty much the product of One Dude, with some helpers here and there.
    2) Vanilla's never been tested on high-impact public boards. No one knows what the upper limits are.

    So, here's my thing. There are currently 474 users here. Once we get to, say, 600, I'm going to seriously think about the future of this software on this site. See how, until then, the forums change. More topics per day? More concurrent conversations? More posts per day? More DB impact? New cool social networks forming that Vanilla just can't easily track because of its (otherwise awesome) "Everything on the Main Page" layout? We'll have to see.

    Worst comes to worst, at 600 users I might move the site over to phpBB (the same software used at the Forge, Burning Wheel, etc). It's tried and true when it comes to huge loads of users and constant conversations. Codewise, it's not quite as tight on the DB end as Vanilla (because it doesn't send close requests for every SQL query like Vanilla does). But there's a huge userbase of support. There's lots of 'tricks' and 'extensions to it'.

    Downside: It's easily hacked if I'm not constantly eyeing the phpBB updates panel. Also, spambot protection is a concern (I'm hoping to find an extension or function for phpBB like Vanilla where all new users have to be approved). Also... yeah, it looks pretty much like Every Other BB Out There. It'll be less unique, but there's less confusion for new users feeling their way around.

    So, that's gonna be on the back burner of my mind for the next few months.

    Socially, everything is awesome, but I totally need to address the two stickies on the site ("Rules", "What is Story Games") giving a more realistic description and goal list for these forums, as they've changed in the last six months.

    In the end, though, I have no plans of shutting this place down. Story Games is my Batcave. It's like a thin layer of insualtion from the horrors and bullshit of the dirtynet, spite-filled blogs, craptacular daily news, and the general hubub of daily work ("Whew; I just fixed Goldman-Sachs' email network after some 6 hours; Let's go back to the Batcave and see if anything new is... Oh! That looks like a cool discussion..." etc).

    So yeah, this place rocks, and it's all thanks to the participants.

    Are there specific issues you have in mind, or is this a case of not wanting to be the one who finds the problems nobody ever thought of?
    The latter, basically. I don't want to be the guy that discovers that on X async MySQL connections the board explodes or whatever.

    Also, if moderation gets to the level where I have to do a lot more real-moderation, that's more difficult as well: Stuff like "deleting users" and "deleting posts" is ABNORMALLY COMPLICATED in Vanilla. Essentially, the developer has come right out to say, "Oh, yeah, if you do X and Y you can do things like delete Users or Posts. But we don't recommend doing either, ever, because ya never can tell if it will simply destroy your DB and crash your forum".

    Me: ".....oh,kayeeeee..."

    (So, like, I'm eyeing the user accounts of the people who loudly got up and left X months ago citing OPRESSION!!! or whatever, thinking, "I could free up some room by deleting their accounts", but I don't want to take the risk of tearing the forums apart.

    It's a basic feature of almost any BBS that just isn't in Vanilla. There's a few things here and there that are like that.

    The other thing, though, is that sure, the technical aspects are a little ware-inducing. However, one of the other main points I have is that I'm feeling more and more that Vanilla is a great BBS installation for large or small communities where you have fewer posts and topics per day. Like somewhere around 1-10 new topics per day, and about 10-20 new posts per day or less. I feel that that is the "sweet spot" for Vanilla's architecture. With more posts per day here, it's harder to keep the information organized in my head as I read through it.

    It might mean I just have to reorganize the Categories to make a more meaningful split ("Game Playing" "Game Design" "Links" "Forum Discussion" Period). Not sure, though. I'd love to have suggestions on how to make entry and catching up to this place less daunting.
