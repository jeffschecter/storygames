    \nBreaking the Ice is a two player game, full stop. I think the design is lyrical perfection. Shooting the Moon adds a third player and an interesting competitive element. It is, in my experience, less intense because of this. Whether that's a good thing or a bad thing depends on your comfort level I guess. They are both fine games. I've never had any problems with either one.

    Posted By: Ben LehmanShooting the Moon wins in the Cage Match. Breaking the Ice just doesn't have the fight in it.

    \nI would pay money to see that. Have to talk to Jenn and Barry.

    Problems folks have talked about with Breaking the Ice involved the game hitting a bit too close to home, when playing with their RL partner. It can put relationship stress into high relief! One thing I've noticed about Shooting the Moon since publishing it is that it does tragedy really well. Comedy too--but that love triangle dynamic is just ripe for someone's downfall. However, there was an Elizabethan comedy game of StM, as well as sturm-und-drang-ful games like one set in Stalingrad in WWII. BtI is more often a comedy, like Graham and Elizabeth's recent game of James Bond via BtI. But it can get intense too.
