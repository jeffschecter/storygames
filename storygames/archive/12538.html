\n I saw Secrets & Lies on the Indie RPG awards list, and it piqued my interest because of how much I liked A Dirty World. $4 isn't much to sink on something like that, but I'd still like to hear a bit more about it. I did read the reviews, and what I liked was the automatic success (most of the time) bit, which is in line with Gumshoe. Something I'm not clear on at all is the resolution mechanic, beyond the fact that it's a dice pool system.

    \n The dice pools are used to track all those automatic successes. Nothing comes for free, you have to buy each auto-success with an escalating chance of future failure. At some point, you'll have to roll those dice and check for a match. Matches mean bad things, either complications or outright failure.
    \n
    \nThe really interesting mechanics allow you to move dice out of a pool before you roll. Those dice build up on Secrets and Relationships, also creating an escalating chance of Bad Things happening.
    \n
    \nIf there's anything you'd like to know, fire away.

    \n--Dan Bayn
