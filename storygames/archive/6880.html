    \n I did cut&paste to put the character creation questions from pp10-13 plus the rules summary from p34 on two sides of one sheet, which I put into a plastic sheet protector. The rules summary doesn't include what happens when you fail, but I can remember that much. It would be better to put it on laminated cardstock but the sheet protector works well enough.

    I give everyone a character sheet, the summary sheet, 3 white dice and five 5-yen japanese coins (to pay when pain dominates), and put one bowl of black dice and another of red dice in the center, enough that people can just take exhaustion dice and keep them ready as they gain exhaustion, which is very handy. Another bowl for despair and a lighter-colored bowl for hope, and I'm set.

    It'd probably be enough to just hand out the rules summaries, and tell them about character creation verbally, but some people process written information more easily than oral, so I do both.

    I highly recommend linking each PC to at least one other PC during character creation, or to some NPC that'll be an important factor, so everyone starts out somewhere on a relationship map. That's worked really well for me.
