Perfect-bound on the short edge - threat or menace?

Jason_Morningstar
June 2006 edited June 2006 in Play Advice
OK, so this one goes out to all the visual designers and print publishers and potential customers. So everybody.

I know printers don't care which edge they bind on, making horizontal layout books possible - but are they a good idea? My concerns:

1. Stress on a perfect bound book, say digest sized and bound on the 5.5" edge.
2. Form factor, ease of use - at the table and curled up by the fire.
3. Display. General goofiness.

I think a horizontal layout frees up the space in a digest-sized book, and just playing around makes me think it would allow for marginalia, which I like. What do you think? I know some folks are choosing this format in 6x9 - any anecdotal feedback?

Oh, and please don't take those mock-ups too seriously. I just wanted to see, you know, what it would look like. The final product will be in capable hands...

    I did layout for Ron's It Was a Mutual Decision. It's 8.5" wide, 5.5" tall (landscape digest). He was directly inspired by daily comics cartoon collection books and similar collections. Ron liked the "thumb-thru" quality.

    At that particular size, there shouldn't be much shelving space for most gamers. Many books are precisely as wide as is this book. Obviously, not so for many smaller formatted books, as most indie games are.\

  • Josh Roby
    June 2006
    The durability of the binding is going to be highly dependent on the glue used to do the binding. At my day job, we use different glues for different uses -- consumable workbooks use the weak glue that will only last a couple years; other books use a much better glue that will last nigh on forever. So mostly this just complicates your question, because the stress factor depends on who you're publishing through and what they offer.

    As for form factor and whatnot, I think this is an admirable size for a short-form game. This is highly inappropriate for a book that is meant to be used as reference material (most traditional games, TSOY, Burning Wheel, FLFS will fall into this category), but for something that players flip through and get a gist of the rules without having to consult the text, this is just right. It lends that feel of a casual game that a big tome can completely destroy.
