\n Well, there's a new free download available for Hot War, our game of friends, enemies, secrets, and consequences in 1960s post-apocalypse London (scroll to the bottom of the linked page to find the PDF). 'Firms & Hatchetmen' originally appeared in the first issue of the Collective Endeavour Journal, but we've now decided to make it available as a stand-alone PDF.
\n
\nThe four page article takes a look at real-life London gangs and gangsters from the 1950s and 60s, giving background, history, and how they might fare after the apocalypse. There's even a few little notes on British criminal slang.
\n
\nOne of the questions that's come up recently in a few discussions as how accessible Hot War might be for those not familiar with Britain, British popular culture, politics, and history. To my mind, there's plenty in the book that helps to guide and offer support if you're unfamiliar with the particulars. But, I hope that articles such as this, however brief they might be, will help to give a little extra background and make buy-in to the game and setting that little bit easier.
\n
\nThe same goes for the Hot War Transmission, our recently released quarterly mini-supplement. Hopefully, the information contained within the first issue (and future issues) aids in providing more useful, and gameable, information in an easily accessible format. And to give the Transmission a little plug, the full colour, 36 page booklet is available from our online shop for \xa35 (plus P&P;). Or, if you'd like to try before you buy, you can download the PDF of this first issue for free.
\n
\nFinally, if you enjoy Cold City, you might be interested in the latest issue of Protodimension Magazine. It contains two articles on the game: one is a complete scenario, the other looks at crossovers between Cold City and Dark Conspiracy.
\n
\nCheers
\nMalcolm
