\n I\u2019ve been re-reading Dune, and that\u2019s got me thinking about how one might run a political epic as an rpg. What distinguishes Dune from, say, the Lord of the Rings, is the high-end political conflict that functions as backdrop for the actions of the characters. You need to be able to run scenes between the Baron Harkonnen and the Emperor. At the same time you need to be able to run scenes involving Leto Atreides and his chief vassals in their struggles to outwit the Baron\u2019s machinations. Moreover, the main characters shift midway through the book when Leto is assassinated and his vassals are all suddenly made into marginal figures for a new drama involving Paul Atreides, his mother and various Fremen.

One easy solution would be for the Producer to intersperse the players\u2019 scenes with an occasional cutscene depicting key political developments. But I\u2019d prefer a more open-ended game, one where large-scale events depend on player choices, not GM fiat.

How might this be done? What would happen if players were each given a noble house to represent in the high-stakes political arena, in addition to a character within the Atreides House (or, later, the Fremen rebel group)?

    The multi-session Story Goal isn't a formal thing any more. That's a 1.0 phenomenon, and it really doesn't seem to add to the game, so it's out. It's all about the serial episodes now for long-term play.

    \n

    Simon, Praxes are The Way Stuff Gets Done In This Universe. They can change from episode to episode if you like, but they're a front-loaded thematic statement, so they have a huge impact on what can happen. They give you a way to define characters by the way they act.

    \n

    Other Praxis configurations I'd consider for Dune (depending on the episode in question):

    \n
      \n
    • Manpower vs. Desert Power
      • \n
    • Loyalty vs. Love (This is a good one! Consider Yueh vs. Leto I or Chani vs. Irulan. Boy, I'd like to see a story about Chani and Irulan. The women sure get short shrift, don't they? "History will consider us wives" my ass.)
      • \n
    • Strike from the shadows vs. Face my enemy
      • \n
    • Power of my Self vs. Power of my People
      • \n
    • Demagoguery vs. Intimacy
      • \n
