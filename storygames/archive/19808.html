\n So I've been hankering to run a game where the players are officers/sergeants in the Imperial Roman army, each taking command of a unit. I would like the game to have a couple of rpg elements, periods where the officers have to investigate, deal with politics, and maintain their unit's morale and other problems of maintain and navigating a standing army. Likewise, I would like the game to be tactical without being super crunchy. Any ideas?


ps. I'd like to find an rpg, but I wouldn't be opposed to a light tabletop wargame and dropping an rpg system over it.

    \n I sometimes feel like a shill for the same game designer, but Captains and Kings just came out from THW. From the site:

    ===

    What: Medieval Warband combat in the 9th to 13th Centuries.

    Scale: Each 6 to 12 figure unit represents 40 - 60 actual combatants.

    Your Role: Players start as a Captain in command of one unit. But if you succeed in battle, you gain fame and more units on your way to becoming King.

    Playability: Designed for solo, same side and head to head play.

    Captains and Kings can be played in a variety of ways and contains the following:

    \u2022\tFourteen different armies, from Viking raiders to crusading Knights.

    \u2022\tThree complete Campaigns including the Kingdom of Jerusalem and Danish raids on England and Ireland.

    \u2022\tComplete rules for carrying out sieges.

    \u2022\tTen unique but interlocking scenarios.

    \u2022\tRules for fighting small raids to large battles, with up to 100 figures per side.

    Captains and Kings can be played with any figures you already have whether metal, plastic, or paper. Play it as a Campaign where all battles are linked together or as a one-off game. Play it any way you like, just play it...you'll be glad you did!

    ===

    ... which seems pretty dead on in scale for what you describe in terms of mass combat, and you can work your way up to that fairly gradually. If you're looking for larger than that, you can get Rally 'Round the King, which covers much larger conflicts (and if you're looking for a pile more historical army lists they've got tonnes).

    Note the compatibility with Warrior Heroes: Legends, the fantasy skirmish/RPG-level game. It's pretty easy -- maybe too easy -- to nip the magic bits right out and run it as a straight up core RPG system. Warrior Heroes: Warbands is more targeted at taking your warband from -- well -- warband to kingship, which while not the goal of the Roman Legionnaire, might be useful as a sort of means of advancement.

    Checking my shelves, the most Roman setting RPG I have is FVLMINATA, which is basically a very light "what if? of "what if the Romans developed gunpowder?" Absolute beautiful piles of tight Roman history and social discussion and maybe one of the best historical non-historical sourcebooks I own. Plus, how often do you get a system that resolves things with sheep knuckles? Sadly, the developer has been working on a new edition with little luck, but has been posting some Savage Worlds conversion stuff on his site.

    I truly adore Roman stuff, so I wish you the absolute best of luck.
