    \n I've had very good results using sound effects and background music in my games; I have the game room wired with a relatively simple sound system, using a cartridge-loading CD player and a multi-input amp that feeds several sets of speakers. The room coverage is pretty complete, so I can keep the volume down very low so as not to interfere with the flow of the game.

    I normally have the 'soundtrack music' running, and I have a list of the audio cues handy so I can key up the specific audio snippets at the right point in the game. The big thing, at least for me, is to make sure that all of the technical stuff is transparent to the gamers - it's all about the game play, not about how fancy I can get with the technical stuff...
