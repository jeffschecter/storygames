\n What's Swords and Wizardry? It's one of the better OSR-style retroclones (in my opinion; but there are better ones).

And here is an excerpt:\n
Take note: Monsters do not always leap to the attack. Unless
they are on the alert for intruders, the Referee may roll 2d6
to determine whether the monsters will attack if they surprise
the adventurers. On a roll of 2-6, the monsters will be hostile
and attack; on a roll of 7-9, the monsters will withhold
judgment and wait to see if the adventurers are hostile; on a
roll of 10-12, the monsters will have a positive initial reaction
to seeing the adventurers, and might even be convinced to
offer some help \u2013 if they are well paid, of course!
\nSounds familiar, I thought! Cool.

    \n I was under the impression that Apocalypse World was Vincent saying something about RPGs in general. Specifically about what actions by which participants are appropriate at what points. Moves based on fictional positioning. It's neat that the S&W uses the same dice math, but I'd expect to see lots of "in this situation, these are the appropriate options for the GM, after you pick one, roll these dice to find out which one happens" in almost every RPG because what Vincent is saying with Apocalypse World is about RPGs in general.
