\n Dungeon World is absolutely fantastic when it comes to delving deep into the dungeon and looking for treasure. However, what if I want to transition to a political intrigue sort of game?

Strings, in my opinion, are the strongest relationship mechanic in any of the *W hacks, if not some of the best relationship mechanics of any game. How would replacing Bonds with Strings affect DW? What other mechanics would need to be altered? Definitely some Move equivalents of Turn Someone On and Shut Someone Down as well as adding strings with Hack and Slash to mimic Lash Out Violently.

With Strings, characters could constantly be shifting favor/rapport with themselves, NPC's, or even factions in the setting. Getting information and using as blackmail is definitely a String mechanic, not a Bond one.

Anything else need to be changed to make this work? Any opinions?

    \n Yeah, strings in Monsterhearts are all about manipulation. They're ways for the PCs to mess with each other.

    In a DW game, where the PCs are mostly working together, I think you'd need to expand strings so that they also encompassed Aid and Defend as well.

    Also, when you add in strings, you really change the nature of the game. The melodrama between PCs is emphasized dramatically. You end up with something less dungeon crawly and more Legend Of The Seeker or Merlin or something more inter-character focused like that.

    You need to make sure that's what you want the game to focus on, if you're going to give the players that many tools to focus on that.

    I've been pondering a rather extensive hack that would do just that. Once I started playing with the idea, I realized it wasn't anywhere near as simple as I'd originally thought. If you look at Monsterhearts, it works because strings are subtly woven through all the playbooks in clever ways.

    \n Are there any other story games out there can be used to model a Game of Thrones-esque game?

    I'll have to check out Saga of the Icelanders. I've heard of it, but haven't really had the opportunity to check it out with all the other games I've been meaning to get to first.

    I'm still wondering about the Strings though. I may be wrong, but I think they could be used to measure political leverage. Sort of a Godfather "you owe me a favor" kind of thing. I understand in their current setting, they are used more to fuck with each other in any way possible. But in a fantasy setting, things may be different.

    I understand other mechanics would have to be introduced to foster the play style. Maybe introduce Keys, giving each character a personal ambition. The Strings, or leverage, could be used as reverse strings. You offer another character the opportunity to take an action by spending a string, they gain XP if they follow through. You offer another character the opportunity to change their convictions by spending X strings, they take a Key and gain XP when they hit it.

    Players taking and giving each other XP. I kind of like that idea. I think it also creates a meta-game level sort of politics.....It'd still be focused on NPC's and Factions, but would work with other PC's.

    Maybe other things can be tacked on for additional effect. Assets like rank, title, wealth, holdings, etc. and things to increase Scale. Just ideas. But they are all still based around the Strings. I would definitely use World of Dungeons as the base, as opposed to DW proper in order to maintain some simplicity.

    To sum up, "Turn Someone On" = "Gain Leverage" in a fantasy setting (but as we've seen in GoT, there's plenty of "turning on" as well).

    \n I think is the biggest difference between Bonds and Strings is that Bonds are stable, and Strings are fluid. Like, a Bond is a relationship you've got with someone that's fairly (but not totally) static, whereas Strings change: one moment you've got the advantage, another moment the other does, the next you're both heavily entangled, the next neither of you is really interested in the other at all.
