\n I'm after a list of movies that can be used to introduce specific story games to new players. Something you watch just prior to the first session. Sure, for some more "generic" games/settings that could be many different movies depending on the upcoming campaign, but please humor me.

For starters here are my most obvious ones, but remember it's all IMO only:\n
    \n
  • Contenders = Raging Bull the RPG
    • \n
  • Dust Devils = Unforgiven the RPG
    • \n
  • octaNe = Mad Max 2: The Road Warrior the RPG
    • \n
  • The Riddle of Steel = The Princess Bride RPG (Inigo Montoya is the best example of Spiritual Attributes in play)
    • \n
  • The Sorcerer of Zo = The Wizard of Oz the RPG
    • \n
  • carry, a game of war = Platoon the RPG
    • \n
  • InSpectres = Ghostbusters the RPG
    • \n
  • Spirit of the Century = Sky Captain and the World of Tomorrow the RPG
    • \n
  • Dead of Night = Friday the 13th IV the RPG
    • \n
  • Wilderness of Mirrors = Ronin the RPG
    • \n
Please add your favorite examples. What movies would you use to introduce your favorite game to new players?

Better still, if you've designed a game yourself, then please tell us the game's most seminal movie?

    Lock Stock & Two Smoking Barrels/Snatch - we havn't really seen a good caper/heist game. Contract Work brings home the awesome but any scene based game could fit.

    The Great Escape = I'd love to see a great prison break game but I don't know that we have one yet

    \nEither could be done with Wilderness of Mirrors. It's the Bourne Trilogy RPG, essentially; but a caper movie and a spy movie are structurally the same. A team of specialists (or a lone specialist, Thomas Crown style) and a difficult and dangerous mission. A prison break is just the same thing in reverse -- how to get out of somewhere, rather than how to get in. The specialized roles in the game become the positions the characters hold on the 'escape committee'. Von Ryan's Express is another good prison break movie that would work, here...
