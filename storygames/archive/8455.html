    \n What if each of the proposed abilities worked on this principal?

    Roll to do a thing (or re-roll if rolling is already allowed)
    -OR-
    Automatically do a thing, but it costs you.

    Then the player can use the BIG version of the ability when it's really important, and the small version when it's not.

    Drive fast:
    (Once) per session, reroll a failed conflict if it involves driving a car.
    -OR-
    (Once) per session, automatically win a conflict driving a car, but you wreck the car.

    Useful contacts:
    once per session, have a scene (or conflict) to see if your contact knows something useful
    OR
    once per session, have a scene where your contact DOES KNOW something useful, but now your relationship with them is threatened.

    \n This is a cool idea, but what if you only designed the first level of your class, then everyone played for a while and afterwards, everyone else designed the next level. Then you alternate with everyone else in the group making your level (or someone in particular, "the person to your left" or whoever) on even-numbered levels and you doing it on odd-numbered levels. In this way, everyone gets to kick in on what your niche is and what they like seeing you do, and they get invested in your special abilities/rolls. "Hey look, he's doing that cool stunt I gave him!"
