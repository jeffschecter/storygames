\n Yay! People are talking about my game online! They've got honest criticisms, praise, compliants, observations, and suggestions. So do I jump into the discussion too, or let them have their conversation?

I try to do a good job of keeping track of when people are talking about my games online. But I'm not always sure if I should join in the discussion when I find it. I'm afraid that it might actually squelch the discussion in some ways. I want to answer questions and acknowledge when people say nice stuff about my game, but I also don't want to go popping up in communities where maybe I don't belong. What do you do?

    \n IANAGD, but here's what I'd do: wait until the conversation has stated to peter out, but not until the thread is obviously dead. Then join in and say "I'm the guy who wrote this game, so I was very interested to read what you had to say about it. If you have any more questions or comments, I'd love to hear them, either in this forum or privately at [email]. Thanks for playing the game, and for taking time to discuss it here."

    I suppose if there is some question that has come up in the thread that you can answer in one additional sentence, do that. But don't answer every little question or observation.

    I write infrequently for a book review blog, and reviewers always remark positively when the author shows up and leaves a polite comment.

    \n If other people are doing a good job, leave the thread alone. In such a thread though, if someone has a question which isn't contentious (they are genuinely interested) answer it. Don't get defensive, or argue with people who don't like the game. You are posting for an audience - talk to your potential audience, not the grief giver. If you really want to argue with someone then do it privately. An example of the latter would be if they are complaining about poor customer service. Sort them out politely off-line. I'm always a little at a loss to know what to do with threads where someone asks to sell/unsell on a game, and then the thread sinks with no response. I'm tempted to respond, but it's really tricky. Usually, I'd avoid this, but if I couldn't resist, I'd just link to a sell/unsell thread on another forum.
    \n
    \nThere is a really big difference between a thread discussing whether a game is any good, and a thread which talks about something within a game. So "Sell/Unsell" generally, no, "How do I handle this situation in Game X", yes.
    \n
    \nAn example - in an otherwise postive review of Trail, the reviewer, who must have had an impaired aesthetic faculty, said they didn't think much of the art. My response was simply to thank them for the reiview, then post a link to a gallery of the art and a layout sample, with no snarky comment.
    \n
    \nThe first review of Esoterrorists was negative, and I think unfairly so. In that instance though I chose to remain silent, because otherwise I would have had to issue a point-by-point rebutal in a very defensive fashion. The review had a definite negative impact on sales, though they later recovered due to postitive word-of-mouth and pther, positive reviews. I do sometimes wonder if I did the right thing, though.

    \n Even more important, when it comes to reviews, don't pull a Goodman.

    Reading a game takes time. Writing a review takes a bloody long time, and effort. That the person could've easily spent elsewhere.
    Reading a game when you plan to review it is also very different from just reading a game. Just like when I watch an anime while jotting down notes and taking screencaptures, I usually take double the time to watch an episode. You read differently, you stop every so often to jot down notes, and then you take the time to review it.

    If you find yourself hating everything the reviewer said, thank them and/or ignore the post completely. Perhaps point some people who like your game to offer rebuttals. But don't be an asshole. Reviews are some of the more prominent pages people will find when looking for your game. A bad review can be quite damaging, you being an ass on the review page? Even worse, no?
