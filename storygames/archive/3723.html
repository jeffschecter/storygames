\nOne Can Have Her is a game inspired by film noir for one game master and two or more players. The characters are each guilty of a crime, and it's up to them to set things straight. But the clock is ticking, so they'll have to make sure they get whatever they want done before things come to an end. It's going to end, one way or another. Will one of them reach their life goal and get the femme fatale, or will all of them die at the hands of their enemies or the police?
\n
\nOne Can Have Her started as one of the winners in the Ronnies competition at the Forge in 2005. Keeping the basic idea, the game has evolved into a 56-page illustrated black and white PDF now available for $10. You can download the file as many times as you like, and you're also given unlimited access to all future updated versions of the PDF.
\n
\nFor more information please look at previews of some of the pages, read an article on the game in the latest issue of The Escapist or ask me questions in this thread or through my web contact form.

    \n Thanks Judd! I look forward to hearing what you think.
    \n
    \nFred, a Lulu version is definitely planned. It's going to take a little while, but I want to have the game available in both PDF and print form in the future.
    \n
    \nOne reason I offer the PDF version first is that I want people to spot eventual errors and give suggestions on how the text can be improved before I commit to a print version. I want to have a chance to catch those small silly errors in examples and such that always seem to evade the proofreaders.
    \n
    \nBuying the PDF gives access to a download area where you always find the latest version. It's a lot easier to update the PDF and let people download it again than to update a Lulu version and compensate those who already bought the game.
    \n
    \nMaybe I'm being overly cautious, but this is the first of my games I'm charging money for and I don't want to feel like I'm cheating people. When I provide the print version I'll have some kind of package deal for the print and PDF version. People who pay $10 for the PDF now will be able count some or all of it off from the price of the print-PDF bundle.
    \n
    \nThanks for the offer on help with Lulu. I'll let you know if I need anything when I start working on the print version.
