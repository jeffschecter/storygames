    \n IndieCon is a rather pleasant convention situated on the English Riviera: sadly, at that time of year it is bollock-shrinkingly cold. Still, brisk walks along the shingle beaches affording bracing views across the Channel, with the promise of a warm cup of greased tea upon your return, are not to be scorned.

    The venue itself is nice. A large and warm central game hall features two nearby pubs that aren't expensive, with a fast food doorway nearby that serves the usual turgid fare that passes for English haute cuisine: the grub is warm and slides down a treat, especially after a few shots of rum and some pints of Guinness. The accomodation is in the form of furnished bungalows: clean beds, dishwasher, cutlery, pillows, and that kind of stuff is provided. The nearby town is a mere 10 minute walk away and you can go there for a curry or to shop at the local supermarket.

    Game-wise, the emphasis is on indie games. Indie is defined as independently published and/or indie in theme (story games, forge games). There's plenty of the type of games that folks jaw about on this forum, sitting alongside a slew of more traditional but independantly published games. Omnifray was a particular hit last year - at least, it was the game that everyone was talking about. There are 3 slots per day, running four hours each, and the gaming and organisation is good. If you have more specific questions I'd be glad to answer them. Oh, if you have games that aren't finished but that you want to playtest, then that's catered for too.

    Lastly, Graham Walmsley is probably going to be in attendance since he was there last year. (Portions of that last sentence may not be true.) Really, is any further encouragement required? With luck, you too will be able to garner your very own "WalmsleyMo" (Walmsley Mo-ment): this is a moment that ripples across the gaming hall like a silent Mexican Wave, where everyone is recommended to unobstrusively wander past the table where Graham is playing in a complete stinker of a session, there to see him affecting a pose of studied getmethehelloutofhere, his elegant fingers thrust deep into his furrowed thespian brow. I hear it was quite fun to see last year

    I myself will be there this year, probably with some Dying Earth, Six Bullets for Vengeance, Dogs in the Vineyard, and Mouse Guard.

    Cheerio
    Rick

    Posted By: MatthijsThanks, Rick. Now that I know about the weather, the food and the games - what's the culture of play like? That's what I'm really interested in hearing about.
    \nEr yes, quite I must learn to read and answer the actual question on the paper. Ha.

    The culture of play is... a mixed bag really. There is a social element that involves getting drunk and the games are a nice sideshow to that main activity; rough incestuous sex while gaming is near guaranteed - this is a British convention - and most especially if Walmsley is coming: last years game of Poison'd is still fondly mentioned, usually along the lines of "Haha! Walmsley wanted to play posh Poison'd and we turned it into pirate bum rape-fest from the first scene. Yarrrr!". Most folks are there to game, and especially yo try out new games that they usually wouldn't go for. Quiet contemplative feedback is not high on most folks agenda - if you want some reflection post-game then be sure to ask for it beforehand.

    I like it because it's a con just down the road from me, the venue is nice, the folks are friendly, and you get to play a whole whack of hippie games in a concentrated block of time.

    \n As someone who has been to Indiecon, I recommend it. While the name of the con refers more to the exhibitors and vendors than to the games on offer, there was a good mix last year. More to the point, there were a lot of great gamers there, and I had a better run of enjoyable gaming there than at any other con I can remember.

    And yes, there is a lot of beer involved. I seem to have missed out on the sodomy so far, but there's always this year.
