\n Hello,

So, this is one of those long shots. Actually, it's probably a bit spammy. Sorry Andy. I didn't know where else to go. So, uhm, unless you like stacking dice and you want to be in charge of something for me at IndieCon, don't read on.

The man at Leisure Games told me that if I was able to run a demo at IndieCon, it would help to spread the word about my game. So, now I have this itch but I can't afford to go to IndieCon this year.

Are you going to IndieCon? Would you like to help me out? If I sent you a shiny Target (the playmat for Hell for Leather) and a PDF of the rules, would you demo my game for 15 minutes? Here's a PDF of the demo (see the 5 minute demo on page 1).

I know, it doesn't sound like much fun, demoing someone else's game. You wouldn't have to read the whole book, or anything. I'd Skype you to tell you how it works and all. Anyway, think about it. I understand that a designer's chief problem is selling his own game, not someone else's.
