\n Hey all, this is kind of a weird request that's only kinda related to gaming, but I need your help. I'm looking for folks who consider themselves "fans of stationary and paper".

Basically, since like 1994, I've been using this same kind of paper for All My Notes. Gaming notes. School notes. Language learning notes. Work. Projects. Design. EVERYTHING. I've gotten pads and pads of it for free in the past, and now I'm running low and need to find some more. My father got pads of these when he worked at BASF and Sandoz, then I got some similar paper when I worked at STS: Unfortunately he's retired and I'm long out the door from STS so we can't just ask them where they got it. I want to find it myself, perhaps order my own pads.

When you glance at it, it looks MAINLY like graph paper (though I've been hunting through one paper site after another with absolutely no luck, so it's probably called something else "project paper" "magnificent architect planning prana paper" etc). However, it has a margin on the right side, and there's room at the top for project name and notes. Here's a sample:



I circled the main points.
1) The title area has usually whatever company's logo or name, and places to write things like your name and the project name. It's like a character sheet for a project.
2) The sidebar is an open area for writing notes. This has been exemplary for me with focusing on side notes when I was doing my language learning.
3) The graph paper itself is quadruled (I think), and the important thing is that it's quite light: Not nearly as prominent as on most graph paper pads.

Does anyone know what this paper is called, or know someone who would?

Thanks!

-Andy
