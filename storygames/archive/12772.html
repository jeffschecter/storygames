\n I only just got my copy of Mist-Robed Gate a day ago, so it's probably criminally early to be thinking about expansions to a game I have yet to play, but I find myself intrigued by the supplement, Forbidden Tiger Manual.

Maybe it's because there's so little available online about it. Basically the write up on Shreyas' blog says this:\n
Forbidden Tiger Manual is a supplement for Mist-Robed Gate. It has new rules for kung-fu action games, new recipes, and a new character sheet for the new rules.
\nThat doesn't tell me a whole lot. I mean, Mist-Robed Gate already has kung fu action, so what's so special about the kung fu action in the supplement? Is it worth $3 to find out? Should I be spending my time absorbing the basic rules before even considering adding to the mix?

It's got a cool name, that much is for sure.

    \n
    \n It adds rules (and new poker chips) for bodily injuries, adds rules for raising the stakes in the middle of the conflict, adds Methods (which are like secret arts or secret resources, supplemental to your Disciplines,) and the recipes aren't bad either.

    If you want to emulate Kung Fu Hustle, these rules go a long way. It doesn't really complicate the game, but it changes the flavor a bit. I found it worth the $3, but them's just my two cents.
