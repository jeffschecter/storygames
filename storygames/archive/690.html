    Well, I gushed and pimped the game a lot, and am now trying less-pressure methods. I trusted my pimpage to still exist in people's minds.

    Ok, here goes nothing:

    In Cranium Rats it is not the people who decide on what they are going to do, but rather, three "Aspects" do.
    You play these Aspects.

    The characters are on their road to Enlightenment, but if they reach Enlightenment you "lose" them.
    You'll be damned if you let them get what they are working for.

    You want to control the character, for that you need your Score to get higher. There is a limited pool divided between you and the other two Aspects.
    You will crush the opposition.

    Cranium Rats has a fairly involved system intended to cause and benefit from inter-player conflict. In fact, most of the game is about conflict, whether in the real world or between characters. You want to "Win", much like in board/card games, but this is a zero sum game, so you have to do it at the expanse of the other players, who obviously want nothing more than to win themselves.
    All the while, your attempts at victory push the character further away from its own success.

    Scene-framing and what the Characters are work to ensure the game doesn't stay put for long.

    CSI Game; Competitive Story Interaction Game.
