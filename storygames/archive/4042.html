\n This has been rolling around in my head for ages, but I finally got round to actually writing some notes down recently. I figured I'd share some of it, cos I think it's cool and it's kinda related to the improv stuff people have been talking about of late.

A Game of Hats is a small-group LARP, based on my experiences with both Freeforms and other LARP styles, and then pondering how to bring in some fun techniques in non-intrusive ways. It's playfully competitive:

Each person has a hat with a different colour. Each hat has a power: change hats with somebody else, being right, being obeyed, winning duels, bringing in news from outside, ignoring other powers, that kind of thing.

They also have medals, which are given as much like fanmail, and capital, which is stuff of value represented by props.

Finally, you have a win condition for the game, which is a context, and a mechanical definition. For example, declare yourself emperor with 3 pieces of capital, 2 medals and the black hat.

So basically you're thrown into a situation - courtly intrigue say - trying to reach a win condition, but your character's approach keeps shifting based on other's needs for their win condition and who currently has the swaping power.

As you can probably imagine, it'll be a comedy...

    \n Oh, that's nice.

    It's also interesting if the hats represent status. Gold hat: you can give orders to anyone and they must obey. Silver hat: you can give orders to anyone, except Gold Hat. White hat: you can exchange any two hats. Black hat: Physically defeat anyone you like.

    Not so sure about the win condition. I tend to think any win condition should be unrelated to the hats.

    I'm blogging about status in improvisation, at the moment, if you'll excuse the plug.
