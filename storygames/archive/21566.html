\n I haven't played anything like D&D or similar fantasy settings for years...
I'd like to revisit it - but with a focus on GMless play and leaning more towards story game style play.
I have two questions:
1. What do you consider key characteristics of D&D or similar fantasy RPGs that are relevant for a GMless story game?
2. Do you know any games that handle this well?

Let me answer my own questions as far as I can:
1. For me, the following aspects seem essential:
- Unexpected encounters in a perilous world (with monsters, fantasy creatures, adventurers, traps...)
- Specialized characters (with special racial/class abilities, special tools/spells/weapons)
- Discovery (of treasures, adventure, gold, magic items, lost princesses, fairy creatures...) in a fantastic world
2. My best bet would be on Mythic GM Emulator (along with the Location Crafter by the same author). Especially it's focus on random elements handle the unexpected encounters in a GMless game very well. Also, while it is open to different settings, it has a certain bias towards D&D style fantasy.

Any ideas & suggestions?

    \n OPing Sw/oM feels very different from being a trad GM. In most cases it's closer to being a a cameraman.

    Looks and feels very different than trad GMing.

    \n So, fantasy + GMless + unexpected encounters + discovery = Archipelago manages that all quite well. The discovery/unexpected angle benefits quite a lot from having each player being "in charge of" a theme all the time (areas of ownership) rather than people effectively "taking turns GMing", as in several other games (Dream Askew is another design employing and perhaps refining this same take).

    Specialized characters is trickier (and, I would argue, hugely overrated - at least the way this ended up working in mid/new school D&D and the "mainstream" of roleplaying games since the mid 1980s) and what qualifies as such is, I maintain, a tremendously subjective gut reaction. Straight Archipelago definitely doesn't have that - not even a nod - while my own Everway/Archipelago mash-up, Spherewalkers Unbound, inherited some from its oldest parent's genes (at the very least, there's a sharp mage/non-mage distinction). It drops areas of ownership, but gets comparable mileage out of other structural traits and techniques.\n
    BeePeeGee said:

    Rafu: GMless problem-solving can be tricky, also mysteries.

    \nI don't agree problem solving is that tricky! You just have to ensure the player introducing the problem doesn't also get to solve it. GMless games can accomplish that either by assigning neutral refereeing duty to a player who doesn't have a horse in the race, or by delegating arbitration to mechanics and/or randomness.

    I do agree mysteries are tricky in GMless games - very tricky. That said, we do those all the time, and there are a significant number of game designs centering on exactly that. It's challenging, prone to error and takes quite a lot focus, but GM-less mystery weaving is a tried-and-true component of the hobby.

    For what it's worth, I'd say problem solving is an essential, defining element of whatever I'd personally recognize as "D&D", while figuring out mysteries most definitely isn't.

You can find the rules for free online; here\u2019s a couple of links (either one will work): (link one; link two). For the first site use the > symbol to navigate the site; for the second site use the typical \u2018menu\u2019 symbol to navigate the site).

    Next use these instructions that explain how to play PbtA games without a GM. Making DW GMless will require some minor adjustments; but not many, and I think that any good solution (no matter what game or games you end up using) is going to require a little tinkering.
