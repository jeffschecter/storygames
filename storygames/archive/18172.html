\n So in another thread, Joe Mcdaldno said that "Vampires are basically: you need to satisfy all my needs because I'm pitiful, I can't satisfy any of your needs because it'd destroy you, and you're not allowed to leave because I'll kill you."

I had never thought of the Vampire that way, and it was very illuminating. It totally took my Vampire play in an awesome new direction.

So, what are the emotional centers of the other Skins?

    \n I'll take a quick stab at a couple, but I'm guessing.

    The werewolf is about being wild and free -- but, I think, also about claiming people, which is an interesting bit of double think.

    The fae is all about the promise. I think the fae may be Child -- if you make a promise, you gotta keep it, no matter what, and the sex move is triggered without sex. The fae is as ignorant of modesty as a baby.

    \n The Fae absolutely doesn't want to hurt anyone and never would, ever! All they have to do is keep their promises -- people who break promises should be punished, simple as that, right?

    It is a childlike way of seeing the world. Yes, you can go for creepy sinister Fae who want to find excuses to hurt people, and that's both viable and cool. But, in some ways, someone who doesn't want to punish anyone, who only wants to help is far scarier.

    Ever read Jonathan Strange and Mr Norrell? One character's life becomes a living hell because a fairy decides to befriend him and help him all the time.
