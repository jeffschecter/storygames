\n So as we were playing "In the Age of Chivalry" tonight the idea of doing a Star Wars gaming using elements of In a Wicked Age came up... we'd just have to compose an oracle.

Then I had an idea... why not use OTHER oracles and just paint them with a Star Wars brush?

So taking a look at some assorted oracles..
http://www.random-generator.com/index.php?title=In_a_Wicked_Age

I think these would be the most appropriate:
Space Opera
Wuxia
Western
and... let's go with
Pulp: Weird Science

So we'll take one of each and see what we get...
A blue skinned orphan fleeing an arranged marriage to her elderly tutor.
A mountain fortress, cunningly defended, but vulnerable to treachery from within.
A box canyon, with only a single exit, and rustlers lying in ambush.
A breakneck chase through the concrete canyons of a great city.

The first one is great, there's a bunch of weird colored aliens running around Star Wars, and the setting has royalty so arranged marriages seem reasonable.
So we have a remote/remarkable & well defended fortress... sounds familiar. Treachery from within? Well somebody had to get the Death Star Plans out...
Lots of Star Wars action takes place on the ground (for little or no reason).. the key here is a scene where the characters are ambushed by bad guys. This could easily be moved to a spaceship run through an asteroid belt or through the center of a watery planet...
Or through the planet-wide city of Coruscant! There's got to be plenty of places to ambush people in those high-tech canyons.

Not bad...
Let's mix it up a bit.
Space Opera
Warhammer 40k: There Will be War
Shakespeare
Pulp: High Adventure in Exotic Locales

A daring young explorer, fresh from her stint at the academy, eager to prove herself and taking on all comers.
Heavy weapons troops, barraging a fortified enemy.
A prince, arrogant and vain, rash of thought and too hasty to action.
A seedy bar, with a dangerous client\xe8le.

This one is even easier!
The "daring young explorer" and the arrogant prince sound like great Star Wars personas, and it shouldn't be hard to work them into most any story.
Heavy weapons troops.. we are talking Star WARS here. A grim battle is fitting for either the Clone Wars or Rebellion Era.
A seedy bar, with... OK this one is TOO easy.

Well, I'm satisfied.
I think I know what my Thursday night group will be doing next week.

    \n Mix-and-match oracles... I like it. At Trinoc*coN this weekend, I had to "modernize" the default Oracles on the fly, to help hook in some younger players (SR4 fanatics: drugs, gear, and crunch--HOORAY!).

    I found it remarkably easy, as you seem to have found with repurposing varied (and sometimes seemingly odd--Shakespeare? Brilliant!) Oracles sets. I just thought I'd mention that you can "file off the time period" on a lot of them and get close to any genre, as well. The supernatural elements, though... well, they need further expansion or reinterpreting ("ghosts, demons, spirits" - real or fake? World of Darkness or Scooby Doo?).

    I'm also keen to try having players each come up with one Oracle; or using random news headlines as Oracles; and of course, song lyrics...
