    \n Sometimes. Video games tend to be a little too expensive for my holiday shopping budget unless I find a really good bargain or split the cost with someone, and of my RPG-playing friends very few are really into owning games like I am.

    I do occasionally get games as gifts myself (especially video games; this year a friend got me Muramasa for Wii and Recettear on Steam), but usually by the time Christmas rolls around I'll have gotten myself all the new tabletop RPG stuff that interests me, and none of my friends are so in the know that they would know what to get in the first place. Basically I only get RPGs as Christmas presents if I've put them on a Christmas list, and usually not even then.

    I ought to think about buying board games as gifts for my sister and her husband, but between them having a huge wall of such and me not being into them myself, I seldom do.

    \n I give some kind of Nintendo or Playstation platformer or Zelda-like game to my partner every year. She likes pretty much all of them. I do too in theory but I never get around to playing more than a hour or two of any one of them.

    In recent years I've introduced Ticket to Ride, Dominion, Pandemic and now, Forbidden Island to lots of folks around Thanksgiving time. They usually end up liking them and then they might get their own copy from me as a gift come December. A few people are getting Forbidden Island this year and one person's getting San Juan because I know they're planning to buy Dominion for themselves.

    I think most people are potential card and/or board-game enthusiasts. They just don't know it yet until I get my hands on them because most of the games they grew up with stunk. I hear, "Man, I didn't know there were games like this!" over and over again...

    The same thing might be close to as-true for roleplaying-like games if presented the right way. The Wii and Guitar Hero/Rock Band showed us there were a lot more potential video-game players out there than we thought too.

    Man, I wish I was getting Command & Colors as a gift. No one ever buys me games because they figure I'd already have it if I wanted it. I went crazy this year and got this for myself for Christmas:



    I've made myself give away at least one game for each new game I bring home for a few years now. I'm going to have to give away several to justify the collector's edition of the War of the Ring.
