    \n Regarding Agon, I think it would be a mistake to disregard it because of the element of competition. My group is similarly not really competitive but still had some great games. The thing I like is that the competition between the heroes can be dialled up or down depending on how the group likes it. For us, the dial started pretty low but started creeping up as people figured out ways to mess with each other to get the glory, and this happened naturally and without and lessening of fun (or hurt feelings).

    I'd also vote for Hero's Banner as another one to check out - especially if you liked In A Wicked Age. They have some similarities in the way play fits together (short, related chapters, fairly open scope), but Hero's Banner focuses on a different kind of story (the coming of age of heroes). Plus there's a neat element of seeing how your heroes change the world as the generations pass.

    Posted By: agony
    Don't Rest Your Head sounds interesting but what does it do that other horror games don't? I just bought Trail of Cthulhu not long ago and despite the fact it's not exactly Indy (is half-Indy appropriate?) I don't feel the need for a new horror game...unless it does something interesting and different.
    Don't Rest Your Head does something very interesting and very different. The dice mechanic is constantly throwing curve balls. Where else could I get to narrate how I win the conflict, and then, because pain dominated, get to listen to the GM tell me how much my win ended up hurting and why? The dice have led to some pretty twisted game sessions. Like PTA, DRYH has a good blend of freeform narrative and mechanical constraint.

    \n Don't Rest Your Head is one of the best implementations of "dice results directly influencing narration" mechanics around. The way that you get both the results and a key word as to what dominates the results works really, really well.

    I would also say that it is not at all the same type of horror as Lovecraft's fiction (as depicted in Trail of Cthulhu). It's a secret world horror game. Where there's a really real reality hiding behind or in between the places of our regular reality. Like the movie Dark City or the novel/tv series Neverwhere. Everything is "scary" not in terms of dread or panic, but it terms of it being off or wrong. Like American McGee's Alice's rendition of Alice in Wonderland.
