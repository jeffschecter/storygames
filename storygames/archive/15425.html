\n So I picked up the DW Ipad app. Its a pretty neat alternative to simply pulling up a PDF rulebook in an e-reader. I feel like its only scratching the surface however.

There are a couple of pretty cool RPG toolkit apps on the market today. DunGen is a graphic minimal random dungeon generator. World Builder is a nice little relationship and location note taking app. There's plenty of dice roller apps. Seems to me that the DW app could really be expanded into a complete game in itself.

Imagine:
1) a Players version of the app that had limited function except to interface with an IPad running the main app available for free for players
2) Character functionality so that each player could have their own character complete with player level rules right on their respective IPad that would automatically sync with the GM's app and format a GM character reference sheet for the party.
3) A dungeon generator that could create a dungeon map on the fly complete with little random details (see the Infinity Dungeon app for how that might work).
4) An NPC creation app that automatically opened a new "note card" for an NPC and enabled tags and links to related NPCs and locations with a name generator.
5) A dice rolling app so that nothing is needed to play except 1 player with the main app and all the other players with the light app (which could be I-phone capable)

Something like that would be pretty sweet and really take advantage of the format.


On a related note: is the wizard spell list borked in the app? A big chunk of it looks like it copies the Cleric spells...but maybe that's how its supposed to be...

    \n Mystic Empyrean just put up an iPad app for their recently kickstarted RPG.
    \n
    \nIts $20 for an app, but its a great example of what can be done with an IPad App rule book vs. the usual PDF or Ebook. Includes a database for NPCs treasures and locations, a full resolution system so the game can be played from the app without needing cards and integration to the website where you can add your own creations and download fan material. Plus an art book containing full page images suitable for use at the table.
    \n
    \nIts a good bit pricey for an IPad app, or a straight PDF, but might be worth checking out to mine for ideas.
