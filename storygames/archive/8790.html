    Posted By: Jason MorningstarActually employing the Stanislavski system (I wasn't practically trained in the Method) requires a degree of self awareness. Now you've got me excited to go back and see if I can pull anything useful out of An Actor Prepares.
    \nHaving been trained in the Method, I can tell one of the main rules is "once outside of the stage, leave the feelings out of you". That is, you must remain very aware of the differences between character and actor (or player, if we translate it into gaming). That is, I relate to the character, I do not become him. I just "borrow" his self for as long as the play (game?) lasts.

    Now, I have never participated in plays written to act that way. However, we did have tons of excercises that had that exact effect. To sensibilize us, to affect us and allow us to connect and feel our characters. That always was as part of training or preparation for a Play. The interesting thing is that during those "closed room" excercises, most other actors not on scene acted as both audience and actors. That is, you did not just watch it as another actor waiting for your turn at the stage, you also experienced it from an audience point of view (which tended to give you a more extensive awareness of both the connections and the separations between character and actor).
