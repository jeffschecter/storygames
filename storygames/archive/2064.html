Bounty Hunters: In Space? and Which System?

DevP
December 2006 edited December 2006 in Story Games

The game in my head consists of a book of Wanted posters, each listing known acquaintances, alleged crimes, and a list of bounties. All these criminals are linked and wanted for similar crimes, and some (particularly the PCs) are hunting each other to score bounties, and possibly to buy amnesty. The amount of bounty defines the "level" of the character, the list of crimes becomes its own kind of beneficial resource, and the acquaintances become a list of NPCs that will be brought into the story.

Firstly: Should I put it in space (or some low key scifi setting)? I figure, why not? You can make all these criminals slightly superhuman soldiers who are wanted for involvement in a lost civil war.

Secondly: I think this wants to be a setting, not its own game ruleset - possibly an entrant in that setting design contest. So, sell me on Dust Devils, Fastlane, or Other for use here, and how it would integrate with the bits I mention above. (I know Andy preferred Fastlane, and I'd like to here why that.)

    Dude, do I really have to sell you on Dust Devils? I mean, seriously.

    "Level" of the bounty = the Devil rating for the encounters. Here's a crazy idea: Go nuts and let it fly up to as high as 4 or 5. This will get crazy, and possibly totally screw people over.

    Also, here are a couple optional rules:

    1) Harm is only permanent in two cases -- first, whenever Devils are in play (which, granted is a lot) and second whenever a Score actually reaches 0. Otherwise, ratings "bounce back" to their original levels.

    2) Replace Traits with Crew or Loyalties or whatever you want to call it. When you help a brother out, you get an extra card. When you screw him over, you get an extra Chip instead. Get really crazy and let people have more than just two Crew (like they would have only 2 Traits). So, this becomes a kind of secondary relationship-style mini-Devil. Boom, instant ship crew mechanics a la Firefly.

    Oh yeah! Reward Bounties with Chips. Why not.

    I think deciding your setting metaphor is the key. If you're going with a Western frontier/Unforgiven concept, then maybe Dust Devils or DitV with the serial numbers adjusted to fit Spaaaace. DitV appeals in light of maybe getting higher bounties to bring 'em back alive.

    However, if you go for a sort of "spy" or corporate extraction style bounty hunter concept, you might want systems that focus more on social connections, subterfuge, and resources. I'm not flashing hard on a system yet, but PTA or Burning Wheel come to mind. Maybe Heroquest would do. Or even Sorcerer, with the Demons being gadgets and networking.

    I'm guessing from the poster mention that you're looking at the former, and I agree with Iskander that embedding the setting wholly in the posters is an awesome idea. If the bounty rewards also translate into some sort of setting points for the players to mod the setting, that might be cool too. In any case, focusing on the play utility and survival necessity of the bounty would really add to the game. I can see a group having to take on bounty X because they're really desperate for the resources. Everything would have a cost: upkeep, equipment, advancement, connections, reputation, etc.

    I would also suggest looking at WHY the hunters are doing what they do. Motivation is a big flag for this setting.
