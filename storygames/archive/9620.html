    \nSword World does not, I totally agree.

    Sword World 2.0 does, though. A friend of mine (ironically, met him a few years back at GenCon when working at the Forge booth) performed an almost D&D4E-style reconceptualization of the game, from the ground up. The basics (ideas of classes, some of the table-based elements) are there yet streamlined, the game is more Modernish, which totally appeals to me: Fans of recent console RPGs and anime will find something to love in SW2.0, but fans of the older edition sometimes complain about the "modernness" or "non-Western-ness" of the changes in setting and feel.

    The old Sword World is a fossil: Interesting to know about, and maybe peek at, but not quite as fun to play. SW2.0 is a different beast: As easy to play as the original, with modern sensibilities in setting (anime-ish) and rules (no "choose one single class and pursue forever", rather you assemble your character by combining 2-4 character classes together).

    But yeah, I've already translated about 80% of the book in my head. Could probably even run it given a day's notice. But first, I need to finish my other huge outstanding project before I'll let myself get carried away further.

    \n I do dislike necro-bumping, but it has come to my attention that there is a small, but dedicated group of members over on 4chan's /tg/ board that has taken it upon themselves to begin translation of the books.     \n Hey, no getting on cases here!

    However, I did approach my friends at Group SNE to complete a SW2.0 official translation this April (I had begun in private on a workbench area of the j-rpg.com blog). When I got back and saw the 4chan crowdsource, I gave up*. :-)

    There were other issues, basically the difficulty of the SW license for an official English version** vs writing it out on paper, stapling it together, and selling it combined with copies of the Japanese books, like is done with new German Boardgame imports and the like.

    In the end, the developer of 2.0 (Kei Kitazawa) just wants people to play and enjoy the game, his words exactly. The others at SNE nodded their heads vigorously in affirmation. So the crowdsource, while possibly not "legal" (I don't really give a shit) is pretty ethical given the desires of the publishers (which I do give a shit about). I just hope they encourage people to buy the core book from Amazon or whatever if they enjoyed the translation of the game.

    -Andy

    ** Because the rights go back to a traditional book publisher, who are mired in BS old laws and rights management (unlike the new world of games, manga, anime etc) and thus have a vulcan death-lock on paperwork required to take a piss on a Thursday, much less the negotiation of rights for a book the size of the palm of your hand for play.

    * (I actually got pretty far through the first book, farther than the fan translation even, but in the end I didn't want to reinvent the wheel and all).

    \n Well, it's been a few weeks, so I figured now would be as good a time as any for an update. Remember, swordworld.wikia.com is the main site.
    \n
    \nAs it stands now, we're about a chapter and a half away from having the first book in a playable state. Sure, those chapters are mainly the combat/magic rules and bestiary, but still, the endless lists are completed. Heck, I've even busted out a good 42 pages' worth of rough translation, thanks to Denshi Jisho, Wikipedia, Google Translate and Rikaichan. Now sure, it may not be completely accurate, but that's what proofreaders are for, am I right? =P
    \n
    \nIn any case, as I said, it's crawling towards completion. As mentioned above, I'm shooting to get this done by the end of August (which isn't entirely unreasonable, so long as I don't slack). However, I would again like to ask for some help. Once the work on the first book finishes, there's still going to be the second and third to bang out, and I'm not sure if the current group of people will want to keep at it. And as I said before, I'm cranking out updates when I can, so there's really no excuse not to help out, aside from a lack of time (copy/pasting kanji and kana takes a surprisingly long time). Every little bit helps, from translating work to proofreading. It seems that the wiki has also begun to attract a blogger or two, so if you know of/write for an RPG blog, I'm sure the wiki wouldn't mind a little bit of limelight. =D
    \n
    \nSeriously, though, if you're interested, just go to the wiki, claim a page and go to town. Create one if you need to. We also have an IRC channel thanks to the sup/tg/ guys from 4chan, so if you have any questions, feel free to hop on and ask in real time. I'm fairly accessible, as are most of the other translators.
