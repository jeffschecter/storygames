[Falling & Drowning] - Stinks like mountain rain in here.

Eric Provost
January 2006 edited January 2006 in Actual Play
Ok, so we were supposed to do the spouses & board game night at my place tonight, but all the spouses but mine called off sick. And, as my spouse is a huge gamer chica, Jason suggested we ditch the boardgame thing and go right for some RPG fun. I wasn't prepared for any of my stuff, so Jason further suggested some F&D.

I don't think we could have picked anything better for tonight's play. It fucking rocked. I mean, we were having fun right from the very beginning.

We discovered that characters died a little too easily for any good fun. I was on my fourth character by the time we addressed that particular issue. Which was no problem, I mean, we had a great time joking and laughing about characters coming and going in the middle of the dungeon and all that. We just decided it would be more fun if characters died slightly less often.

I really can't even retell all of the changes we made to the rules tonight. I mean, sometimes we would change a major rule, play out one scene, and then change another rule right after that scene. Sometimes to ditch the rule we just adopted.

We came up with rules for treasure, monsters, which way to 'turn' in the dungeon, how to regulate the difficulty of the rooms, and a bunch of other things. Jason made lots and lots of notes, so I'm sure he can relate the changes we made during play tonight.

Our most daunting challenges of the night? The Venus Fly Kelp threatening to drown us in syrup. The slippery steps (no handrails!). And trying not to fall onto our swords immediately after -successfully- drowning our sorrows (getting drunk. That particular episode cost my character his -other- eye. Oh yeah.

We fell for an Ork Trap. All but one of us bought air purifiers from the Amway Ork Salesmen.

We all drowned in sorrow. From a woman oozing butterscotch-flavored sorrow.

We had to climb over a slippery tree over a pool of blood. Beware the blood squirrls!

Oh! My fighter sliced an entire pit of jello into small cubes and promptly placed them on tiny plates. Tiny plates of jello which Lisa's character promplty drowned in.

Oh, I forgot we started out with Feathers. Our asses nearly all got drown in feathers. Helluva first trap for the dungeon.

Also, early on we all fell in love with a princess. Who promptly murdered us, as we were still all dying whenever we lost a conflict. Yikes.

That's all the notes I can find here, except some of the tangently silly stuff.

New rule: Whenever someone rolls 10 or higher, someone must declare; "Pip frenzy!" Just because.

Our kewlest named treasure was the Vest of Booooooones!, which came moments after the Boots of Butterscotch Dodging.

Our highest level reached was 3. Our lowest level reached was -2. This all came after we decided that character death should only come from HP loss, not from loosing a conflict. Don't worry; it's still a comedic spiral of doom. I dare anyone to get a single character all the way through a dungeon with the rules we finished with tonight. Double-dog-dare.

Whew. It's pretty late. This game rocks. I can't wait to see the final polishing on these rules. Hell, if tonight's playtest was any indicator, that final polishing may not be very far away.

Anyway, sleepy time. Cathca all later.

-Eric

    I changed the way combinations are handled - boring or cool? It uses the monster mechanic so it is consistent.

    !!DROWNING AND FALLING COMBINATIONS

    If you choose to pair two drowning and/or falling challenges together, one of them becomes, in effect, a monster - it's difficulty becomes a penalty for the other challenge. The specific rules for the type of drowning or falling apply only to the "main" challenge - the second justmakes it harder.

    !!!EXAMPLE
    Johnette has a Deadly falling challenge paired with an Easy drowning challenge, and decides that the fall is more interesting. "You've entered a huge cavern with a narrow, slippery ledge," she says, "and there's a dark stream at the bottom. It's a deadly challenge, and the potential for drowning if you fail adds a minus one penalty."
