I dunno, this just came to me.

First off, how cool would pog-based resolution be for a game? For increased character effectiveness you get a bigger slammer.

I'm also thinking that (hear me out) Jungle Speed would be an awesome resolution mechanic. You either pull half the cards out of the deck or play with colors. You set stakes for a conflict, then play. Whoever gets a succesful grab narrates something good for the side they want to win. You play for three grabs, and whichever side gets two "good" narrations wins, with all of those facts entering the fiction.

The key is, whoever loses the grab gets currency that they can spend to do other stuff, including kicking someone out of the circle for a grab, continuing to play (best out of 5, out of 7, etc).

Or I'm just crazy. The point is, I don't see why a player-skill based resolution system couldn't be coupled with a system that gives those who lose at those particular activites increased effectiveness in other areas.

Are there games out there that use the physical skills of the players for resolution?

    I used some puzzle minigames in Console when I ran it at Genericon last year. We had a block-pushing puzzle (Jenga), a standard Towers of Hanoi puzzle (done with baby stacking cups), a lock to pick (simulated with a jigsaw puzzle), and a matching game (done with Set). People really liked it.

    In my mind, none of it is fast enough for everyday resolution, but for major obstacles it might be pretty cool.
