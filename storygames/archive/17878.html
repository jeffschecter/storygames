\n This New Years I had a few friends none-RPing staying with me, and somebody stumbled across my old Fighting Fantasy choose-your-own-adventure gamebook collection.

We started playing one of them ('House of Hell') collaboratively. We took turn - in your turn you read a paragraph, lead the group in choosing the option, and then they pass the book to the next player.
We needed some house rules - we axed the dice-based combat mechanic in favor of having the players take turns to describe a 'blow' of the combat until we felt it was resolved. We occasionally held pages and jumped back if we 'lost' the game, because that's a horrendously bad mechanic.

Now, most of the books are terribly designed (lots of false choices), but the actual play is really good fun and (this it the killer for me) really accessible to none-RPers.

It's important not to take it too seriously - we were treating it much like a group of friends enjoying a bad film - and to have house rules which lift the story off the page and give the players extra creative license.

Any thoughts? Anybody else tried this?

My initial idea is that a well written choose-your-own-adventure, passed-n'-played, could provide a group with an enjoyable narrative experience to rival popular novels like Harry Potter and the Hunger Games.
