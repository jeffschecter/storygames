\n So I bought "The Lost World: Jurassic Park(TM) Role-Playing Game Book". Published in 1997, and written by Greg Farshtey, it's sort of a solo gamebook for several players. The game is meant to be playable for one or more characters. However, at many, many points in the book, there's stuff like this:\n
Make an Agility roll to see if you can keep pace with the jeeps.

If your total is 12 or more, go to 9.

If your total is less than 12, go to 11.
\nWhat if half the group succeeds, and the other half fails?

I can't believe this was ever playtested. That annoys me - I'm sure there must have been kids trying to play this, failing, and deciding that role-playing books make no sense.

Oh well. It probably makes no sense getting annoyed at an 11-year-old game book. Still, though.

    You never played one of those solo gamebooks, right? ^_^

    "Go to 11" means "Go to section 11 and see what happens there". The various sections are "forks" or snippets of ingame fiction that, strung together, make the adventure. So a really simple solo adventure might look like this:\n
    1: You are standing at one side if a hole. Flip a coin. Heads, go to section 2, Tails, go to section 3.

    2: You jump over the hole with both flair and a majectic show of physical power. You are awesome.

    3: While jumping over the hole, you rupture your spleen, miss the other side and die a messy death at the bottom of the whole. You suck.
    \nNow imagine that with hundreds of sections, various cross references and a more complicated "rules set" and you get stuff like the Lone Wolf books and so on. Sometimes not as boring as it sounds, actually.

    \n Wolfe, the reason this exchange frustrates me, is that I've seen this pattern so many times before.

    A says: "This thing doesn't work".
    B says: "It works for me! I just do X and Y".

    Of course someone will be able to make it work. I could post just about any design example from any part of life, and someone out there would say "I have no problem with that! It's easy!" People are able to run epic stories of romance and intrigue using 1. edition D&D. That doesn't mean the design works. It means those people made it work for them.

    As a game designer, and someone who puts a lot of effort into making my designs intelligible and unambiguous, ambiguous design that requires player tweaking and interpretation - especially from a first-time player - is an atrocity. I get irrationally annoyed, and want to find the designer and make them admit their errors. This thread is, I guess, my way of venting that frustration. No more point than that, really.
