\n So the zombies are beating down your cabin door...gwaaah!

What are the three Pools you're drawing from to stay alive and safe?

My first thought was to divide it by interest focus - self, family, and society, but you can't really tie Abilities to such a diffuse set.

More practically, Courage (definitely a measured resource that you can run out of) - but what to pair it with? I really like the idea of Self Interest being a Pool - that just seems like wonderful and in-genre juxtaposition, but again, that's probably better handled with Keys.

Toughness, Cunning, and Cool? Why change them at all in that case? Hmm. I need suggestions.

Thoughts?

    \n How about Courage, Cleverness, and Virtue?

    -- Courage is related to physical bravery, willpower, and strength of character as well as to a certain degree physical strength. It helps you when you come face-to-face with a zombie intruder.

    -- Cleverness is cognitive flexibility, mental acuity, and powers of perception as well as to a certain degree physical coordination. It helps you come up with and implement clever stratagems for dealing with zombie hordes before you come face-to-face with them.

    -- Virtue is goodness of heart, likability, and luck, as well as to a certain degree physical beauty. It doesn't help you with the zombies so much as with other survivors.

    They may not be that different from Toughness, Cunning, and Cool, but the terms map on to character archetypes pretty well, I think.

    I think anti-pools can work just fine, even taking into account harm.

    Let's look at these two pools: Eaten By Zombies; Abandoned By Friends; Failed To Realize.
    Points in each and skills under each represent your ability to prevent this.

    Eaten By Zombies includes skills like: Gunfighting; Climbing; Jumping; Driving; Detect Traps.
    Secrets likely to feed off this pool: Secret of Shotgun; Secret of Crowbar; Secret of Chainsaw; Secret of Ammo Cache; Secret of Armored Car.

    Abandoned By Friends includes skills like: Planning; Jury-Rigging*; Cooking; Suppressive Fire.
    Secrets likely to feed off this pool: Secret of Rations; Secret of Safe House; Secret of Wealthy Father With Military Background; Secret of Medical Background.

    Failed To Realize includes skills like: Observation; Literacy**; Navigation; Tracking.
    Secrets likely to feed off this pool: Secret of Video Camera, Secret of Security System Access; Secret of AM Radio; Secret of Crazy Uncle's Famous Saying.


    *Jury-Rigging would go under Eaten By Zombies if it was all about making make-shift weapons/defenses, but would go under Abandoned By Friends if it was all about making plans work with the limited resources at hand, or bolstering up the group van.
    **Think about how many illiterate country hicks there are in zombie movies. Literacy is totally not an automatic skill.

    Here are my thoughts on Keys thusfar:

    How about a group XP pool, where certain Keys (group experience keys) don\u2019t reward the player, but reward the group, who can draw from the pool in times of trouble to benefit themselves? Maybe bonus dice are not automatic, but come from this pool. They are anti-keys, where you do fucked up stuff that hurts you but benefits the group.

    KEY OF THE SURVIVOR
    \u201cI. Will. Survive.\u201d

    1 XP for overcoming immediate danger.
    2 XP for survivng when others do not.
    5 XP for surviving because others do not.

    Buyoff: Risk your life for someone else.

    KEY OF THE EXTERMINATOR
    \u201cDesperate times? I\u2019m a desperate fucking measure.\u201d

    1 XP for killing a zombie in a new way.
    3 XP for killing double your Vigor zombies in one scene.

    Buyoff: Refuse to use violence.

    KEY OF TERROR (group experience)
    \u201cOh sweet Jesus, help me!\u201d

    1 bonus die to share for being terrified.
    3 bonus dice to share for refusing to help because you are afraid.
    5 bonus dice to share when your fear gets someone killed.

    Buyoff: Face your true fear.

    KEY OF WISHFUL THINKING (group experience)
    "They're still people. Maybe we can help them."

    1 bonus die to share for showing a zombie compassion.
    2 bonus dice to share for convincing someone else to leave a zombie alone.
    5 bonus dice to share for risking your life to save a zombie.

    Buyoff: Recognize zombies for what they are.

    KEY OF OBSESSION (group experience)
    \u201cI don\u2019t care if he\u2019s infected, I won\u2019t abandon a child!\u201d

    You have an unreasoning attachment to an object, person, or idea. It is the focus of your concerns and actions.

    1 bonus die to share for keeping the thing you are obsessed with safe.
    2 bonus dice to share for convincing another to obsess with you.
    5 bonus dice to share for risking your life or the lives of others in service to your obsession.

    Buyoff: Abandon or destroy the object of your obsession.

    KEY OF OVERCONFIDENCE (group experience)
    "Nuthin' I can't handle."

    1 bonus die to share for going into a situation with no plan or precaution.
    2 bonus dice to share for convincing others that a dangerous situation is really not a problem.
    5 bonus dice to share for going cockily into certain death, or convincing another to do so.

    Buyoff: Hesitate and make a good plan.

    The Dead Walk! uses these three: \n
      \n
    • Kick Ass!
      • \n
    • Make It Go!
      • \n
    • I'm Okay!
      • \n
    \nWhich correspond roughly to your standard "flesh, brains, guts" trio, mentioned many times above. The current draft* doesn't have them being pools, though - they're just straight stats ranked 1-6. TDW! does use three kinds of dice pools, though - each player has a Character Pool to draw from, and there is a Tension Pool and a Dead Pool which help fuel conflicts from within the group and without, respectively. There's a rough economy that helps dice flow from pool to pool, hopefully driving the aforementioned "zombies aren't the real threat, other people are" theme, and working towards the climax of the story. The system is intended for one- or two-shot games, though, so there's that.

    * The current draft is also all the way up on blocks, as well, so it's all up in the air, so to speak.
