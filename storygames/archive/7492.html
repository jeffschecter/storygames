\n There's so many cool games out there right now. I feel like every time I start thinking of designing something to play that doesn't exist, someone comes along and does it better than I could have. SOOO, my question is: What games out there do you wish you'd designed and why? And no fair saying "None of them." You don't have to play after all. This is for giving some love and playing Salieri.

Mine is "In a Wicked Age...", confusing text included. It's tight, elegant, good-looking, cheap, small, and always produces the fun. Plus no prep. Are you serious?

    Posted By: Tim BoserTechnically the game I wish I, or somebody would make is a reverse generational horror game.

    We start at the death of the final player character, then go in reverse scene order to the creation of the monster. I don't know how to connect the two points, but I think it'd be hella cool.
    \nI had actually been working on a similar game, but without explicitly being about horror (my mainline game was gladiators dying to the cheers of the crowds, or perhaps the last moments of a squad in the trenches of WWI). There's a lot of technical issues, though working backwards to a monster instead of the hero's birth (as I was trying) might help. I may have to pick this up again, I think there's potential, and now I know it's a decent enough idea that someone else has considered it.

    I was aiming for a feeling kind of like when you meet someone, think you know them, then realize they have an entire past you don't understand, or at least haven't experienced. Seemed like working back from their final moments would work well thematically, but mechanically it's a bitch.
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
