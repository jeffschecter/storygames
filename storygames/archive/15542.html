\n I'm sure its been done and maybe I'm forgetting a system that does something like this:

MARTIN BLANK -- PROFESSIONAL KILLER

0d6 = I watch Molto Mario
1d6 = I've seen Gross Pointe Blank
2d6 = I was loaned out to a CIA-sponsored program - it's called "mechanical operations"
3d6 = You must've done some naughty shit there
4d6 = There's a contract out on your life.
5d6 = I'm gonna put a bullet hole in your fuckin' forehead, and I'm gonna fuck the brain hole!
6d6 = I killed the president of Paraguay with a fork

Think of it as a skill/trait/role pyramid, at the base it is widest--but you've got the least dice. It narrows and you get more dice, until at the top it is very specific and you get the most dice.

Tough for me to explain, but hopefully you get the idea.

Thoughts please and as always thanks in advance!

    A character gets a skill pyramid, each of the six layers gets increasingly specific, and all related, which they can do. The real start to the 'skill' starts at 2D. At 0 you haven't got a clue, but are willing to take a chance despite the potential consequences (I'm a professional killer, but want to attempt open-heart surgery). At 1, you have cursory knowledge: I'm not a doctor, but I played one on TV--or something to that effect--you'd get a penalty for trying but its not as harsh as at 0.

    The descriptors act mechanically similar for each type of player role, just adds a bit more flavor to it.

    \n This might be relevant.

    An Ingenero character has 2-4 traits, like you might have pirate 5. Means you get to roll 5 dice and choose the highest value, so the higher the level the more reliable a pirate you will be.

    What separates one pirate 5 character fro another is the signature plays they have, such as +2 swing from a rope and knock an enemy sprawling. When you do this specific thing, you add 2 to your highest roll, so this demonstrates in what aspects of piratey behaviour your particular pirate character excels.

    You can have a bunch of these. but -2 cumulative penalty to spamming the same play in the same scene.
