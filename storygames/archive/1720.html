    On the subject of the table, there's a way to simplify that a lot. It's basically the method for how I do it in my head. Note that it's predicated on the idea that you still want all nine discreet results posibilities (From Complete Defeat, to Tie, to Complete Victory).

    Basically you just calculate the difference in levels between result level. If you get one better result than I do, it's a minor victory for you. Two better is a major victory. Three better is a complete victory.

    If the success level is the same, low roll gets a marginal. If the success level is the same, and the rolls are the same, it's a tie.

    Of course, for an extended contest this doesn't help (though you can create results that map to the 4 levels of victory for a simple contest).


    The d10 version has been proposed before, but the problem with it is that either you drop crits, or they happen 20% of the time, instead of 10%.

    So I don't find the Math hard. Just in case, I wanted to offer how we do it, in case technique is the issue.

    I have the quick reference sheets printed out at the table: http://www.glorantha.com/support/downloads.html

    To figure an augment: ability/10 round 1/2 and above up. So 12 is 1.2, we round down to 1. Whereas 16 is 1.6 round up to 2.
    If there is a mastery just add 20 to the total for each mastery before augmenting. So 3W1 is 23, divide to 2.3, round down to 2.

    I usually let the players total them up as they narrate how they want to bring them into the contest.

    For simple contests I just tend to remember that success vs. failure is a minor victory and success vs. success is marginal. That covers a lot of common results, and I can look the others up.

  • Ian Cooper
    November 2006
    Hi Shreyas,

    Sorry I understand now. Have you thought of riffing of Prince Valiant? Valiant is the grandfather of the system that appears in HeroQuest (Hero Wars is the father). My brief summary would be: There are two stats Brawn and Presence both rated on a 1-6 scale. These give you a pool. A skill adds to the pool by its value. PV uses coins and heads = success, but 50/50 on anything works as well. All successes give you a complete victory which counts as an extra head/success. Contests are either against a difficulty number for a passive resistance (default of 3) or an opponents pool for an active one. In simple contests the highest number of successes wins. In extended contests the difference is subtacted from the losers pool and the contests ends when one sides pool is exhausted. Weapons, equipment, darkness etc are all just additions to the pool usually +1 or so in scale (frx being mounted is +1 to Brawn). Some situations use skill alone (usually knowledge based contests). There is no specific way to handle an augment, but there is a rule that where the stat+skill combination is suspect you add half of the suspect stat or skill to the pool.

    So you could try using ability ratings as a pool of say D6, and allow a single augment to give half its value to the pool (hopefully this passes the math requirement). Skill magnitude and pool size would really be down to how many dice you like tumbling and how much you want skill differences to count. Then just follow the PV mechanics for simple or extended contests.

    Shreyas,

    There is a major change that occurs when you move from the current HQ system to any sort of pool in which you roll a die for every ability you are using. This change may not matter to the parts of HQ you like, but I figured I'd point it out just in case.

    If every ability is worth the same, then it doesn't matter which is 'primary'. In HQ as-written, if you are fighting then you have to start with a fighty ability. Then you pile all your relationships and gear and whatever on top of that. This means that if a conflict is in an arena you are bad at (fighting, diplomacy, whatever) then you start at a disadvantage. So, if you have Fighty 15, and Hates You Guts 10w5, in the current system you would have 6w when you fight whoever you hate. Moving to a 'one die per ability involved' shifts this balance.

    Which may not matter to you, of course. It may not be a part of HQ you care about. I just bring it up because you asked.

    EDIT: Pretend that that last sentence is 'I just bring it up because it's something that matters to me in HQ.'
