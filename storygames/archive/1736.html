How To Play Pirate Treasure

tony_dowler
November 2006 edited November 2006 in Story Games
I just had to share this with the Story Games community.

Today my wife received a box of mementos and personal things from her early childhood. Now my wife listens patiently when I ramble on and on about game design, but has never shown any inclination to design a game herself. But lo and behold, in the box was a completely realized board game including cards, board, and peices. She made it when she was in second grade. I just had to post the instructions here:

How to play Pirate Treasure is very simple. It's almost like any other going around the playing board. The almost is instead of the dice, cards with numbers up to nine (two each) are shuffled each turn and the player whose turn it is take the top card and with his/her marker goes on the board how many steps to take. Another thing is when a certain square says something to do what it says even if it will lead you to bad luck.
The object of the game is to get to the treasure chest first is the winner. Good luck! This game is a treasure you will want to keep!

    When I was in jr. high, my next door neighbor got a Macintosh. We used it to print up cards and rules for this elaborate scavenger hunt game. It was played on a huge map of an entire neighborhood. The idea was to move your family around picking up items looking for the stuff your neighbors had borrowed. Each family member could also attack their counterparts: dads could fight dads, kids could fight kids, and so on. The dog could bit anyone, but could only carry 1 item at a time. It took hours to play, but it was a blast.
