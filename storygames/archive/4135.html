    \n JACN... the Mountain Witch is meant to be played across multiple sessions. If by "instance" you mean "one character until he or the mountain witch dies" I agree. But there's this false notion floating about that TMW is meant to be played through in one session.
    \n
    \nI'd agree that PTA gets "abused" in that it often ends up just producing pilots. Then again, how like real life is that?
    \n
    \nThe real culprit, I've found, is Inspectres. It's fun as a single session, one mission game, yes. But it really shines in campaign play where the franchise rules produce long-term effects, and characteristics pile up on the characters. People are missing out if they're thinking of Inspectres soley as a one-shot game.
    \n
    \nMy Life with Master... you actually have to rig the starting levels to get it to be a one-shot, or even start characters with Love. It's hard-coded, for most set-ups by the rules that are reasonable, to take three or more sessions, usually no more than 6.
    \n
    \nDoes 5 games constitute a "Campaign?" Or playing more than once?
    \n
    \nI think there are actually several issues at hand.
    \n1. Is the game a "one-shot," meaning that it can be played in a session to completion?
    \n2. Does the game have an "endgame" to it, that drives players towards the game ending (in however many sessions that takes)?
    \n3. Does the game play the same way every time (few to no RPGs actually do)?
    \n4. Can the game support indefinite-length play? What people usually think of as a "Campaign."
    \n5. Is the game set up specifically to be serial or episodic? That is, does the game expect that plots will be ongoing between sessions, and resolve organically, or that there will be one plot each session that will be resolved (even if there are other ongoing sub-plots)?
    \n6. Are there character termination mechanics that can cut short a character's run suddenly - like character death mechanics (ones that require death as an outcome, rather than those that allow it)? Or like another game I read recently where your character could, at any time during play, be whisked off to his own personal Nirvana?
    \n7. Replayability - that is, you finish playing the game entirely (however that occurs for the actual play in question), and then come back to play it again later.
    \n
    \nMost games are somewhat agnostic on some of these, meaning that they don't specifically promote one or the other - even indie games are this way. For instacnce, Dust Devils makes an excellent one-shot, but there's absolutely no reason it couldn't do ongoing episodic play, or even indefinite serial play.
    \n
    \nI think that the question that the original poster is asking about is really only about the last question, replayability. But the thread has spawned other considerations.
    \n
    \nMike
