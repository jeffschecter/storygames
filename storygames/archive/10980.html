    \n Being the center of attention.

    Being the final arbiter of creative input. Being able to be the last word.

    Having a reason to do a little out-of-game homework between sessions.

    Broader variety of less-invested play experiences.

    \n I like the chance to present games that I know are good, but others don't. For example, I loved GMing Steal Away Jordan, because I basically get to point at this great game and go "Look! It's great!".

    \n
    \n
    \n
    \nFairness.
    \n
    \nFairness is hugely important to me. I enjoy insuring pacing and spotlight time are equitably shared. I\u2019m eager to resolve or mitigate personality and rule conflicts. I want to make sure everyone has a great time. I love hosting, making sure everyone has space, a comfy chair, and snacks. I\u2019m a pleaser.
    \n
    \nSurprise.
    \n
    \nI get a thrill from presenting open ended situations and being surprised by how people tackle them. It\u2019s common for me to run the same situation 10+ times for different people. It\u2019s one of my favorite parts of playing RPGs. It\u2019s always different.
    \n
    \nChallenge.
    \n
    \nI enjoy pushing buttons. Asking questions. Shaking things up. I also enjoy listening. By challenging players, I have more to listen to.
    \n
    \nEngagement.
    \n
    \nI don\u2019t play RPGs for escapism. I enjoy escapism and get it other ways. I love full on engagement and the best role to facilitate this seems to be as GM. I\u2019m constantly observing and interacting with people.
    \n
    \nExcitement.
    \n
    \nI\u2019m never bored as GM.
    \n
    \nEdit: Strangely, making worlds, npcs, and situation are not important to me. I don't use RPGs as a creative outlet. I'd be happy to run pre-generated material as long as it was easy to use and reference.
