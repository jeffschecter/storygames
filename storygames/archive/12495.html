    \n I agree with Jason that any game where you create relationships during character generation is creating "backstory".

    But I see where Chris is going in his OP. Those three games all require you to come up with a fair amount of "what came before" as an explicit part of character generation. So, like, if I'm making a dwarven ranger-thief (mmm ... dwarven ranger-thief ...) I can come up with cool backstory, but the rules don't MAKE me, in the same way that, say, Spirit of the Century does.

    So what are the mechanics of these games that create backstory?

    Dogs - The initiation mini-game
    SotC - writing and swapping character "novels"
    Mouse Guard - answering a background questionnaire
    Burning Wheel - lifepaths + required relationships

    I might add Dust Devils, since you need to invent your character's "Past" as a trait, plus a Devil that often tells us something about his/her history.

    It's definitely points on a continuum, rather than a certain "kind" of game.

    Chris, is that what you're driving at?

    Posted By: Paul BLike...does Exalted? Is there any aspect of Exalted RAW that hands you a useful preplay fiction? Or any WOD game, really. D&D, any edition?
    \nStoryteller games (Exalted, WoD, at least, I'm unsure about Aeon/Aberrant/Adventure!) have backgrounds. These often imply backstory (mentor, influence, artifact, there are lots), but they don't mandate coming up with fiction in the same way that SotC/Dresden Files do with their phases.
    In practice, these either get bluebooked before play begins, or are left dangling as plot hooks. If you take Influence 5 for your Solar, that's going to be prearranged, because the question of where you are the big kahuna is immediately important. If you take Mentor 5, that might be prearranged, or you could let the GM provide it in play when it becomes important.
    I think D&D has less of this. Secondary skills in AD&D implied a little about what your character did before he was an adventurer, but they weren't super-relevant to the ongoing game.
