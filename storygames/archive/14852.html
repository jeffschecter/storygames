\n My good friend Warren Tusk has announced a Kickstarter for The Dance and the Dawn: A Theatrical Experience, a live action re-imagining of The Dance And The Dawn. And in two days, it met it's first goal.

I'm very excited about this! And there's still time to get in on this project, secure some awesome rewards, and help this project reach its maximum potential. Check out the video; the LARP has been run many times before, and the document will contain everything you need to run your own run of this game. Worth noting that, if you aim to play, you should defer from reading the PDF until after you play. But I've read the text, and I'm a fan.

(And a note to SEA/PDX gamers: Run this thing!)

I'd like to point out the top-iter reward for donators:

All of the above, plus: You get the Deluxe Event Experience -- the author will show up and orchestrate a run of The Dance and the Dawn for you. No work, just magic! (NOTE: In addition to the listed donation, this reward may require a reimbursement of travel expenses, depending on your location and other logistical factors.)

    \n Update! Want to mix some Dances and Dawns into your Dungeons or Dragons?

    If we manage to hit $4000 (200% of the initial funding target), by your collective grace and generosity, then every one of our backers will receive an extra-special bonus prize. Dev Purkayastha, the designer behind the original tabletop game, has volunteered to compose a Dance-and-the-Dawn-themed adventure module for Dungeons and Dragons 4th Edition...

    The Ladies of Ash, instead of accepting the Queen's invitation to the Midnight Ball, have decided to storm her frozen palace and put an end to her cruelty once and for all. Their steel is sharp and their spells are mighty, and surely they will prove formidable adversaries even for so terrible a power as Her Majesty. But along the way they will have to contend with the glittering Lords of Ice, who are at once their destined loves and their deadly foes...
