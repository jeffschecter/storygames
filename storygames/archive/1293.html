Science Fiction for and by the masses

Joshua A.C. Newman
September 2006 edited September 2006 in Directed Promotion

I've just recently opened up my forum on the glyphpress and since it hasn't rocked over and caught fire, I thought it might be time for a round of invitations over here.

The forum is a place to share and discuss science fiction stories written by the board members and there are several pieces there by people we know and love, including Who Art in Heaven, the story included in Shock:, by Ben Lehman. You must have an account to see and submit stories, however; this is not a publication (yet). So apply for membership if you want to get your fiction on.

It's also a place to discuss Shock: including the Mediography, rules questions, and errata.

And lastly, it's a place to discuss book and graphic design for game designers. Trade ideas, references, ask questions, and help your fellows in their publishing endeavors.

I'll see you over there!

    Well, here's the thing: the rules Ben and I laid down eliminate a lot of the SF around on the Web: no fanfic, particularly, and no scraps of unfinished stuff.

    In particular, I'm interested in SF about social issues (that is, the kind of stuff Shock: generates), but some stuff outside of that mold is already over there and I'm not kicking it out.

    Now, what makes it into an eventual anthology (which, if it happens, will be a periodical in paper with book design and editing done by Ben and me) is another matter; we'll be looking for work with a particular bent to the stories.

    So, while you can, for the time being, put anything up there, the anthology stuff will be pulled from a subset. For instance, even though Steve's story is funny and interesting, it doesn't address the editorial concerns of the endeavor, so it won't be in the book.

    Now, do I want everyone and his mom showing up and writing science fiction? Shit, that would be awesome! If it got big enough, I'd want to have some sort of rating system so folks could figure out what to read. Right now, you could read it all in an hour, so I'm not too worried about it.

    Send your friend's husband over! If he knows his scene well, there's probably some synergy. Maybe a whole lot.
