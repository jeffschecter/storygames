    \n To be fair, on Modern Warfare 2 I've primarily played Team Deathmatch, which as far as I can tell is pretty Rambo lone-wolf shit for the most part. I've played a few rounds of Domination and Sabotage without respawns, and I've found that there isn't a whole lot of voice-chat or teamwork unless you're playing with friends.

    Bad Company 2 is a lot more team oriented, going so far as to prompt you to join a squad when you first join the server and provide you with a squad chat channel automatically. Uh, other than that the game is just a little bit more tactical (despite the lack of a goddamn prone posture). The maps are larger, the movement is a little slower, and the weapons behave with a bit more realism (bullets are affect by gravity, grenades don't cause quite so many out and out kills, guns can use all their mounts for scopes and tubes, etc). Basically, it's a Battlefield game through-and-through, if that means anything to you. If not, well: big maps, more inherent teamwork, neat vehicles, tons of unlockables, great sound, minor glitches.

    The game pokes a lot of fun at MW2, but they are fun for entirely different reasons. EA has become less of an evil corporate master, while Activision has become more so (15 dollars for three new maps and two old ones, really?). Then again, EA charged 10 bucks for a new game mode and if you don't have a VIP code the map packs for BC2 are five bucks each for threeish maps.

    Anyway, they compare nicely in some areas and differ in others. If you like military shooters, and if you love Modern Warfare 2, you'll probably dig Bad Company 2.

    \n Bad Company 2 has a hardcore mode which removes the hyper-realtime map and most of your hud, and makes you easier to kill (1-2 torso shots for most weapons).

    On the non-hardcore mode, though, you're still pretty easy to kill. 3-5 solid hits will bring you down, fewer if you have a hi-powered weapon like a battle rifle or a sniper rifle (a solid body shot from a .50 will take you out). Headshots are instant kills 99% of the time (at longer ranges with weapons that pack shorter cartridges, headshots aren't instantly lethal). The game has ballistic physics, so bullets don't travel in a straight line until impact. They gradually lose power and arc downwards/do less damage.
