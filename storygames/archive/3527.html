\n So on NPR the other day I heard this discussion about this book: A link to the interview.

Jobs in ancient Rome with no modern equivalents (therefore "strange"): Hair-setters, sandal-slaves, living PDAs (nomenclators), etc. Haven't read the book yet but it's on my next order list.

I was just speculating on the possibility of the Rome RPG where the PCs weren't all centurions fighting waves of barbarians for control of Europe, but rather calculating nomenclators and sandal-slaves, jockeying for opportunity and within their own houses.

Anyway, the RPG context is limited (unless it inspires strange jobs in your fantasy hybrid), but I figured a couple folks would be tickled by it.

Full Title: Working IX to V: Orgy Planners, Funeral Clowns, and Other Prized Professions of the Ancient World
