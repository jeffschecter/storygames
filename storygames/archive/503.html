This post is a direct spin-off of this thread about why some Indie games sell better than others.

Suppose you have a playtest version of your game, how do you go about getting it looked at, commented upon, how do you build interest, how do you build a salivating crowd just waiting to throw their money at you(I'm looking at you Josh, with Shock:)?

Most importantly, how do you get your game out there into the hands of people who will actually play the thing and return comments about it?

How much is too much pressure, how much and in what way should one keep pushing, or not?

What is a good time-frame for one to wait for playtesters to present themselves before giving up on it? What is considered a good response on average?

I ask in part because of my own limited success with my game, but because one can do better I think.
Got one person who will playtest this someday, an additional one that provides read through thoughts. That was enough to go to the beta revised, having them note what I forgot out(oops) and get me to clarify some points that needed clarification.
  • Jason_Morningstar
    April 2006
    How do you get people to playtest your game? Playtest their game. That works for me.

    How do you get people excited about your game? Write a good game, play it tons and post actual play. Persuade people who are excited about your game to do the same.

    How much pressure is too much? I'd say any pressure is too much. Ask, make it available, help others, be grateful for any contribution.
