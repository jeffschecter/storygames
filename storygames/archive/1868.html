Push 2 Contributor Guidelines

J_Walton
November 2006 edited November 2006 in Directed Promotion
I just posted proposal guidelines for Push volume 2. Those of you who have been thinking about (or just now started thinking about) writing for it might want to get working on your proposal.

I'm mentioning this on SG partially to let people know and partially to get some of you to look over the guidelines and see if I've forgotten to mention any crucial information. I'm planning to shop this around to some folks who aren't part of the indie roleplaying circles I normally run with and am hoping to anticipate as many questions as I can. What else do I need to say?

Also, nominate cool people! If you know someone who should write for Push, post their name, a link to their work, and what you want them to write about. I have a bunch of surprising ideas up my sleeves, but I'm sure you know of many, many more people that I might be able to seduce. I'm hoping for a wide diversity of viewpoints and backgrounds, while also gathering a group that'll will work well together.

Oh, and if you don't know about Push, there's more info on our website, including a 25-page PDF preview of Volume 1.

    Tim, it really depends on what your piece is about. I can get wordcounts on the articles in Push 1 if you want some real figures, but I'm open to a pretty wide range. If you want a general idea, Push 1 is 100 pages long (note that these pages are a different size that your standard 8.5 x 11) and includes 5 contributions and my introduction. Really short articles of a few pages are probably better suited for blogs or forums unless they're really hot stuff. Stuff that's over 30 pages should probably be its own book.
