    \n What's that thing again? That discusses a game, like an AP does, but is discursive and not particularly organized? That's what I tend to do.

    As I understood it, the purpose of AP post is to say something about what went right and/or wrong in a game, and the purpose of an AP thread is to investigate why. However, for me, if I'm playing in a game, I tend to enjoy it. If I enjoy a game, I tend to not be motivated to describe or discuss in minute detail. If I run a game, I have often not enjoyed it, but soon I decide what it was I did wrong, and thus am still not motivated to discuss it.

    Thus, when posting in response to a game, I either write what I believe is called a vignette, summarizing the action of the fiction, with occasional mentions of particularly amusing RL behaviors, or else I notice something that gets me thinking about something in a broader context than that particular play. Which would make them just plain posts which mentioned actually playing, rather than Actual Play Posts.

    It's possible that this makes me a bad AP person, but I don't see myself that way, and I don't see you that way, James, and I don't think you should see things that way either. You said what struck you, and what the game experience was for you.

    Something that we should also both believe of ourselves is that we will inevitably get better at writing these as we do it more often. So lets just resolve to play-and-post more.

    PS: Yes, Jason. You may put another notch in your internet bed-post.
