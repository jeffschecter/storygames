\n Having posted without good aim in Mike's thread ( http://www.story-games.com/forums/comments.php?DiscussionID=6438&page=1#Item_65 ), I thought I'd start my own here.

On reading it, it made me think more along the lines of making a sandwich. You take two ingrediants and mix them together. Say you had some nice bread (not the white foam crap, real deli bread) and sardines.

Now, how do you mix them so one flavour doesn't overwhelm the other, and you have a nice blend?

Well, you just add a certain amount of each, and if one is too much, use less of it next time.

In relation to the other thread, I thought the competitive part and the thematic choices story part could be broken down into components. Like say the competitive side was broken into ten bits. Each bit is playable, but as you add more the competitive side gets more complex. It's a bit hard to explain, but if you've ever owned a board game and then bought an expansion, that's the idea. The competitive part would have say 1 to 10 parts, each a sort of expansion and making the competitive part bigger.

And same with the thematic choices story side of it - it'd be divided into 1 to 10 parts, each an expansion on the thematic choice game.

And then - the play group just tries out a combo, like comp5/story7* and see if it's a nice blend. If it isn't, try a different ratio of ingredients in the sandwich.

And that's my idea. Making the expansions wouldn't be easy, I'll grant, but I think it's quite doable with a bit of sweat.



* Tehe, it sounds like a D&D character! I laugh cause I like that coincidence!

    \n John,

    I didn't suggest it as 'A game about designing games where you play a game', I suggested a labour of trying combinations of games, like making a sandwich is a labour. And I said some people might want to see that labour as an actual game in itself. And you've said you think this process is 'supposed' to be fun, explicitly indicating you want this exploration for El Dorado to be a game in itself.

    That contradicts itself and you say as much yourself "Well, the design game is complete and the activity becomes something different.". In other words, if you found the design game fun, then when you find El Dorado that fun ends. This means overall, El Dorado results in LESS fun. And if your not aware of the contradiction, this just triggers the conclusion 'Well, if it results in less fun, then it musn't have been El Dorado!', prompting more exploration - which is 'supposed' to be fun, which will trigger the contradiction over and over and over again.

    My suggestion is specifically for people who labour towards El Dorado, rather than want to play a game to get to a game.



    Mike,

    I don't understand what you mean by creating an agenda? Agenda's aren't created- if everyone likes wine, then you can have a wine drinking agenda. If half the people only like beer and not wine, your screwed - you can't make a wine drinking agenda. If everyone likes gamism, then you can have a gamist agenda. If half the people only like nar, your screwed.

    A hybrid isn't made, it's a matter of whats there in terms of what people like. If the whole group is gung ho gamist, they will just go flat out gamist. A blend of nar and gamism would require a group of people who want a blend and yes, want about the same blend. Get one person in the group who wants flat out gamism and the group can't achieve that. A system can't make up for a lack of shared personal preference. What's blocking El Dorado here isn't system, it's people.
