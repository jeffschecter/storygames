\n Play, Design, Thinking, Meetups, Whatever. Big or Small, but Notable For You. What are some milestones in your gaming life from 2011?

Most of mine are play-oriented, including:

* The completion of a long-running multi-generation HELLAS campaign. Elias the Amazoran Whoremaster was retired by choice and not by dagger!

* Getting a chance to play Freemarket.

* Just swimming in gaming: Visits to area tables, my bi-weekly game, a weird demigroup that formed and ended up playing pretty much weekly for months, etc. It's been a bountiful year.

* Going to GPNW and meeting new friends (Logan B, etc), seeing online friends in person for the first time (Christian Griffen, Mike Sugarbaker, Ben Robbins, Chris Eng, Adam Koebel and more) hanging out with other good friends like The Harps', Eric Boyd, Derek Guder, Tony Dowler and more). And playing a ton of fun stuff, from Norwegian to a "Lemony Snicket RPG" playtest to action-y stuff.

* Supporting about a dozen cool RPG Kickstarters, most of which manifested. Of particular note was the Hellas action deck (reminiscent of the TORG action deck) and companion vinyl play-mat, Dave Hill's game of immortal love Amaranthine among others. There's been a lot of creativity exposed due to Kickstarter.

* Camp Nerdly (I got to play, too, and not just oversee! I tend to spend a lot of time making sure that others are having fun even at the expense of my own gaming sessions), and meeting Zac F2F there as well.

* Going from "wanting to maybe sometime play this cool-looking Japanese RPG about ninjas" to "playing/running 10+ sessions of it in just a few months.

* Seeing lots of cool new S-G threads that helped me step up my game at the table.

The Design/Production/Blah stuff includes:

* Making huge gains in closing in on a Tenra Bansho Zero release
* Getting involved in a few more Japanese RPG projects

Problemy stuff:
* Heavy work, promotion, critical work stuff plus the aforementioned flood of awesome gaming pretty much sidelined a few small RPG projects I was working on. Too Much Life, had to jettison some gaming.

    - Ran a series of playtests of JB Mannon's game Boarsdraft, which is essentially Harry Potter with the serial numbers filed off. I'm a big Harry Potter fan, but the idea of Harry Potter gaming had never really excited me before. The creative constraint of making things almost-but-not-quite Harry Potter really sparked my imagination, though, and led to some stuff that I really enjoyed. The playtest also seemed useful to Josh in highlighting some issues he needed to address in the game.

    - Put out a bunch of episodes of my AP podcast Designer vs. Reality. I think I've gotten pretty good at editing podcasts now, but the show isn't generating the kinds of conversations I had hoped for, so I'm not sure whether I'll continue with it.

      \n
      \n This was the year of Apocalypse World and the many great games that came out of that one.

      Playing Kite the Battlebabe in Apocalypse World, getting into trouble and then shooting my way out all leading up to Queen Kite ruler of the Hardhold (I am sure Josh\u2019s Hardholder Parson may disagree with that point a bit)

      Running the Airfield in Apocalypse World (AP in James\u2019 post above) Now that the game is over I miss Cobra, Prowst, Specials and Robinson, they were so easy to be fans of.

      Running and playing so much Dungeon World, this game is really really great and effortlessly gives me the D&D experiences I always wanted. Over the year we have fought Undead Barbarians, Mindflayers, Githyanki, Orcs, Spheres of Annihilation, Dracoliches, Beholders, Iron Golems, Trolls and more.

      Monsterhearts - got a chance to play this up at BigBadCon run by Carl Rigney and it was easily one the best games I played all year. I cannot wait for this game to come out.

      In addition to all of the Apocalypse World fun -

      Sorcerer and Sword - played a short amazing game of this with Jesse run by CK, it was filled with Orcs, demons and buckets of blood. In the end my Orc killed a Demon with the shards of a giant cauldron artifact he had smashed. Big fun.

      Heroquest - I have really enjoyed digging into Glorantha with my friends and can't wait to return next year to see how our little rebellion against the Lunar Empire will turn out.

      Smallville - still enjoying the hell out of this game, got to play a D&D inspired hack, ran an Exalted hack and a military Sci-Fi game titled "Conquered Stars" which allowed me to roll the trouble pool as a swarm of incoming nukes.

      And more great con games than I can list.

      I have been really fortunate to have had a year filled with great friends and gaming.

      \n\nJanuary 2012 \n

      \n So many highlights! I went back through my email and tried to piece together all the games from the past year. It's been pretty crazy 'round here.

      Old School Hack! Only a short series (5 sessions) but so much fun. In a similar dungeon-bashy kind of way, we played a whole bunch of Castle Ravenloft and had a blast with it.

      A really cool Apocalypse World series set in semi-hi-tech-feudal-Japan-with-zombies. Think Afro Samurai meets Left 4 Dead. I got to play a Chopper, then a Gunlugger (first time for each) so that was extra cool.

      Monsterhearts. Man, what a great game! Playing the emo-ness for laughs, Twilight-style, was especially fun (though it's great straight, too). Can't wait for it to be released for reals.

      Also on the AW-hack front, had some really great sessions of Dungeon World, including a special event with The Doubleclicks at PAX (that's right, I'm name droppin' the muthafuckin Doubleclicks). I'm so happy that DW is getting all the love it deserves.

      Got a lot of fun and productive playtesting done on two of my own AW-hacks: The Regiment and Imps. Both are close to done-ness (at least in the game design department).

      Finally played a whole series of Freemarket. Wonderful fun. I played a sensualist who wanted to experience everything. I highly recommend this as a Freemarket character type.

      (Also finally) played Leverage. It delivered exactly what it promises: a killer heist game. Need to play a full series of that, still. Really made me appreciate Cortex Plus, and helped me understand the awesome inside Smallville even more.

      Tony Dowler ran some sessions of Talislanta 4th Edition for me. I was a Sarista Witch. You have no idea you guys.

      Murderous Ghosts! Only played it once so far, but I love it. I know, shocking right.

      Session four of the annual Burning Russia game. It delivered the goods, as usual. We added a new player (always tricky) and he was amazing.

      Saw lots and lots of Lady Blackbird love, including the LB Companion and a score of cool hacks, culminating in the too-exciting-for-words, kickstarting Always/Never/Now.

      Go Play Northwest! It was a huge love-fest, as usual. Also, discovered the wonder that is Lost Worlds (the duel-in-a-book game). PAX was also amazing, especially this year as I got to hang with even more online friends face to face.

      Call of Duty: Black Ops. Seriously got back into online play after a long hiatus and it was great. We had a little cadre of friends on Xbox Live there for a while, playing almost every night. I especially loved making little movies of my best moves and sharing them. And while I'm talking video games, let me also add: LA Noire, Uncharted 3, Batman Arkham City, Portal 2, COD: MW3, Bastion, Outland, inFamous2, Frozen Synapse, Just Cause 2 (on OnLive) and Skyrim. Whew!

      Lowlight: A very awesome PTA series concept and cool characters that managed only a pilot episode. It just lost steam and got mired in scheduling hell. Oh well. Such is the fate of many a cool pilot, I guess.

      Now that I've listed all that, I feel like there's something incredible from my game-o-sphere that I'm leaving out. It was a packed year! I must be forgetting something.

      \n I could go on and on, (and already did for a boardgaming re-cap elsewhere), but I'll keep it brief and leave almost everything out.

      GPNW and Gamestorm were both terrific as usual, but for gaming and conversing about games I really liked the three house cons I attended & hosted. I want more of that in 2012. Magical.

      Playing a ton of Apocalypse World, including MC'ing an ongoing game. I can't say enough nice things about AW and its hacks. I had some super games of Microscope and Archipelago II as well.

      My favorite single session was probably my Sunday group's penultimate session of Pacific City. Some great character arcs coming to intense fruition.

      Fifteen contests of my precious War of The Ring, the boardgame that tells a captivating story with each play.

      I did a lot more design work in 2011 than in previous years. It's satisfying but really difficult; have to duel The Perfectionist every bleeding moment. I'm close to sharing some of it maybe.

      It was a very good year.
