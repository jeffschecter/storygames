\n Note that i mean both Would you want to, a hypothetical question, and Do you want to, an actual offer.

The council, of which i am a part, of this yet-nameless convention for early may 2011 in olympia washington usa, have brainstormed many a way to bring in some additional incomes. One was to have an advertising package available for designers / production companies. Another is having a merch-style table, to be staffed by the con personnel, to sell small-press stuff by indie designers (especially those in attendance).

the advertising options that we're tossing around would include space for physical banner ads, website ads or inclusion in a link list, inclusion in promo material (logo on poster, ad in program). Though we conceived this more for local businesses, we could offer your products as an advertised prize for winning a particular instance of playing a game (I admit it would initially seem strange to win a copy of Panty Explosion for winning a game of Civilization, but there might be a really awesome tweak to this idea, or an awesome application of it: and games could be paired effectively). There was also an idea that attendees could earn a discount on buying certain games which they played at the con, based on game-producer participation.

The merch table could operate on a buy-in-per-product-you-want-sold-on-the-table, or on a percentage-of-how-much-you-made-in-sales-at-the-con model. What seems more sensible to you?

oh, for added info, we're looking at 6 on-site vendors, mostly hobby game stores or production companies, not private folks re-selling their collections. There would be a finite amount of advertising, exclusively in "good spots". Though monopoly-the-game might not get much play, we're looking at offering other types of monopolies to advertisers / vendors.



to state clearly: i know little about making money as an indie game designer. Do these options seem enticing to you? Solid? Flawed?

Thanks!

    \n If I were to come with products, I'd be most enticed by the model whereby I get to check my merchandise in at a staffed table, and they take a cut of everything they sell. If that percentage were 10-20%, I wouldn't bat an eye and would gladly accept. If that percentage were higher than 20%, I'd have to start number crunching, and might end up deciding to do backpack sales instead.

    The percentage-of-sales model involves zero risk, which means the little guy with his first product at his first con has nothing to lose by putting his art out into the world. That's a delightful notion.

    \n Simon's experience matches my own- while we'll take con advertising in trade (not cash), it's almost never worth the price. We will donate limited amounts of product to shows for their own use as gifts or whatever no problem. At most cons we also offer discount vouchers as prizes for events to drive people into the dealer rooms at all. I've never been to a show where the con itself offered a staff merchandise table, but since IPR as a guest would represent many of those indie pubs, it would be duplicated work if we were also there. When groups like the GPA offer merchandise showcases, it's generally paid for on a per-shelf basis to ensure everybody gets a fair amount of space; I'd be curious to see the results of a "pay a percentage of your sales" setup because I've considered that myself but never seen it done. dw
