    \n Because in D&D the GM is more than just a referee?

    He's also an adventure (think video game level) designer and responsible for playing the opposing forces.

    Have you ever played an asymmetrical board game? An asymmetrical board game is a board game where not all the players are doing the same thing or trying to achieve the same thing. Scotland Yard is an example. Some of the players are police detectives trying to find Mr. X. But one player is Mr. X who is trying to escape the police. Betrayal At The House on the Hill is an interesting example because it starts out as a symmetrical board game (everyone is trying to level up their characters and do so following the same rules) but turns into an asymmetrical board game in the second half (now someone is the betrayer and everyone is following two sets of different rules).

    I think of D&D like an asymmetrical board game. The DM does one thing and the PC are doing something else.
