    Tony, my suggestions:

    Make it scream "Exciting!" It's not just good marketing, it's genre appropriate.

    Front cover:

    squish the Capes logo a bit into the upper left. Down and to the right of it, have your slogan blasting out with visual interest -- starburst, motion lines, one of those cheesy effects -- in Comic Sans.

    Back:

    I really dig the comic panelling effect. Perhaps each box's header could be in Comic Sans or another good hand-lettered font, maybe even in the yellow "narrator" box.

    The three skinny boxes on the right suggest motion in comics. Maybe put the text in the first to the left, the second in the middle, and the third on the right.

    Put interesting superhero art in the blank boxes. Either something that looks like part of some actual comic, or even the simple figures from the click-n-locks.

    The Hite quote is the kind of thing you want to throw in people's face when they hit the website. A little long for this sort of fish food.

    I still think a "bonus" click-n-lock with intriguing abilies that people could cut out and use at demos would be fun marketing, but that doesn't seem to work with this format.
