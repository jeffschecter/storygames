\n Okay, so this magnificent bastard got a mention in one of the Stuff to Watch threads but I think it deserves its own, dedicated thing.

The Metamorphica

Johnstone made this book, right? It's a book of random mutations. Hundreds of them. It's insane. Also, remarkably useful. Not just for creating mutants or inflicting chaos effects on characters but for randomly determining stuff like color and body shape of alien species, for example. I just got my print copy in the mail and have been flipping through and imagining all kinds of weird shit. Highly inspiring.

So, if you like mutants, randomness or FREE excellent system-agnostic supplements, you owe it to yourself to check this book out.

Edit: Most in-depth bibliography ever.

    \n So, funny story.

    We were playing D&D last Thursday and the PCs encountered a demonic boss dude (with the stats of a minotaur). And of the two players, one suggested he have a random mutation from the book. So I had him roll the d1000 and it turned out that the minotaur dude had Armoured Skin. Even though the other player didn't like that too much, he suggested that the demonic boss dude have a sweet weapon. Great idea, right?

    Motherfucker, lemme tell you! There's that awesome d1000 table for demonic weapons in Realm of Chaos: Slaves to Darkness, but did I include THAT in The Metamorphica? Nooooo, it's for mutant creatures, not for technology! Thankfully, that player had a scan of the book on his tablet, so we discovered that the demon boss was able to summon a death knight with his magic sword of summoning death knights, but my original goal of not having to carry around that book has, apparently, not been achieved.

    \n Max (aka Ego, and maybe you met him at GPNW 2012), has posted his process for creating aliens with The Metamorphica, for use in his Stars Without Number game.

    Interestingly, he doesn't use the alien creation tables at the back of the book, he just uses the massive d1000 mutation chart and the various categories within it to generate sage-people with bendy bones and energy beneath their skin, dragon machines made of water and lava, and a savage race of weredemons with removable, nonreflective skin and hypnotic eyes and weaponized tongues in their lamprey-lizard faces. A nice example of taking a selection of random elements and making something interesting out of them.

    \n Thank you both! I love this book so much, it's a ton of fun to use.
    I like the Xenobiology rules in the back, but I think I like them more as an augmentation to an already built creature to fill in the gaps that helps slot them into your game, especially the natural habitat and consumption rolls. The Xenobiology tables technically make them extraterrestial, but I think it's the mutations that really make them alien.

    And Keith, I absolutely agree that there are too many furry people and warrior-species out there. They're good and simple for a game if you're in a pinch, but stuff like this book lets some truly interesting creatures out of the box, and those are what I really want to come across in my games. Warlike Races are a dime a dozen; lamprey-lizard weredemons with nonreflective skin are far more memorable.

    I need to see if I can't build some tables to mutate locations, 'cuz I feel like that would create some awesome places.

    \n Dungeon World stats!

    ------
    Northern spiniphant (a.k.a. tusked tumbler)
    Small, Group
    Tusks (d8 damage, 1 piercing) Close, Messy
    10 HP 1 Armour
    ------
    Calm and plodding creatures, Northern spiniphants are slow to react and not particularly social; preferring to feed off the winds that blast and tumble them across the stony tundra; preferring to exchange tales with the wind than with stiff bald bipeds. They have twelve limbs or thereabouts, oriented all around their body. Combined with their copious and eerily prehensile hair, they look like dread-locked soccer balls; albeit with huge tusks that emerge from their hairy depths when scratching frozen moss off a rock. Or when you punt one between two rocks and break some of its legs. and you notice the tusks of its gang flying at you in a screaming blood rage\u2014screeching across the sky like savagely pointy meteors with great red-brown manes and trails of hair and legs. That is until your eyes are blinded by muddy brown light, and blood, and you run and they pursue and pursue and run 'til you die or they do. Instinct: to roam
    - Leap great distances
    - Flash muddy brown light, disorienting and blinding

    Greater northern spiniphant (a.k.a. fucking huge tusked tumbler)
    Large, Solitary
    Tusks (d10+1 damage 2 piercing) Close, Reach, Messy
    14 HP 2 Armour
    ------
    Unless you're a stone giant, hopefully by now you know not to kick one. Instinct: to roam
    - Leap great distances
    - Flash muddy brown light, disorienting and blinding
    - Capture and strangle with masses of knotted prehensile hair\ufeff
