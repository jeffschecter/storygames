\n In our 4E game, we keep hitting this hump in combat.

Situation: We have taken down enough of the monsters that we are going to win the combat, but since there are more encounters to come the DM still wants to inflict some punishment on us. So we finish the combat and it takes at least another 15 or 30 minutes.

My thought is this:

At any point during the combat, one side can offer the other side a deal. Basically the encounter ends, the PCs win, but in exchange the PCs must expend resources. This would be kind of like the encounter bidding done in Beast Hunters, but it happens to end the combat early.

Typically resources that could be bid are:

* Healing Surges
* Hit Points
* Daily Powers
* Magic Item Uses
* Action Points
* Better/Different Magic Item Rewards.

DM says, "We can end this now for 4 Healing Surges and a Daily Power."
PCs could reply "We still have a bunch of encounter powers. How about 3 Healing Surges and an Action Point."

Then you narrate the rest of the combat showing who gets hurt and which resources are used.

--Thoughts.

    \n Does the GM really just want to inflict some damage on you?

    If he does, you could just negotiate the entire combat until he's satisfied with the damage inflicted.

    I'm inclined to think the GM doesn't just want to inflict some punishment on you, rather he wants to play out the game that combat is, to its proper end. Even if he's likely to lose...though the strong gamble element of D&D does make it possible to win (side note: I know the rolls are basically all averaged out...but luck can even break averages). Much like a problem gambler, in D&D you can always have a demented sense of being able to win (but unlike problem gambling, it's okay to get that feeling since your not going to lose anything big).

    I'd suggest adding a beserk condition, where the monsters get plus two to hit, but suffer 1 to 2 hitpoint damage per round (they lose more the higher their CR - work out some equation for it). The beserk condition only occurs when both players and GM both say they want it. Special note: No, that doesn't mean they negotiate when it happens - they just both say yes or no (saying nothing says no) to it each round. That's very different from negotiation. When both happen to say yes, all monsters go beserk as they see their imminent deaths.

    That's after guessing what your GM wants. I'm just skeptical about him just wanting to inflict some punishment.

    \n Anybody remember the old PC game "Wizard's Crown"? For each encounter you had to choose whether to engage in quick or slow combat. Slow combat was all terrain, battlemaps, turn-based movement and attacks. Quick combat just gave you a readout of your characters' health and expendable resources, and those of the monsters, and would quickly resolve.

    You never did as well in quick combat as you would've in slow, so it was a tradeoff. If QC wasn't going the way you expected, you could stop it and go into slow, but then you'd be in worse shape, because your characters would already be wounded and not at all in a good tactical formation.

    So yeah, I think some form of QC would be awesome for 4E wrapup. Bidding would be a great way to handle it, as long as the GM drives a hard bargain. Make sure you get more from the party than you would've gotten in full battle!
