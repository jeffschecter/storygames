    \n Generally, yeah, we used specials all the time, with no issues, though the level of comfort seemed to vary by player and even character. My first Angel character had sex in the first session (off screen) with an NPC hardholder. My second Operator/Gunlugger never had sex during the game, though it was part of the backstory with other members of his crew. My third Hocus had sex a few times (off screen, again, which is generally how we did things), at least twice with PCs. My fourth character, in a Monsterhearts one-shot, had sex with a PC off screen. Some of that may have been me warming up to both the idea of sex in games and also becoming more comfortable with the folks I was playing with, since I'd only recently moved to Seattle.

    However, I've been in a couple different games where people misunderstood the sex moves to apply to rape, which they explicitly do not, according to the official word from Vincent (and I think that's strongly implied in the book too). This led to some really weird situations where one player was driving towards rape based on a what they assumed was a mechanical incentive and what they imagined was a shared commitment by the group to support that kind of play, while everybody else was frantically looking around at people's faces and making sure everybody was okay with where things were headed. And sometimes that made the guy pushing towards rape look like a jerk, though I think the confusion often came from that player not having actually read the book, just looking down at their sheet and assuming that "have sex" includes rape (which says pretty awful things about our culture, but that's a whole different topic). Sometimes, as well, this happened between a PC and NPC despite sex moves not always applying to NPCs, so there was other confusion swirling around as well.

    There are definitely moves in Apocalypse World that are about violation -- heck, there's the Violation Glove -- but the specials aren't generally about that. There's something interesting going on with the Brainer special + Deep Brain Scan, where the Brainer can essentially get the effect of having sex with someone by invading their brain, though I generally think people interpret that the other way around, where sex lets the Brainer invade someone's brain without much effort.

    \n I suspect part of that comes from how you read the rules of the game. The game says "whenever you and another character have sex," and, the way I use English (whatever the dictionary says), if somebody sexually assaulted me, I wouldn't say "he and I had sex," you know? I would call it something else. Coercion definitely happens in play, though, both physically (typically through Go Aggro), emotionally (Seduce or Manipulate), and supernaturally (Brainer, Skinner, and Hocus moves). The way we've been dealing with that is -- I think I saw John do this first -- the MC asks the players: "So... you have sex?" or "So, does that count as sex?" That gives each player a chance to say, "yeah, we had sex" or "no, that was something else; whatever it was, it wasn't sex." That's really a "lines and veils" thing, not something directly derived from the game.

    I didn't really mean to imply that other folks are "playing it wrong," though I guess I stated it in those terms, more just bringing up an issue regarding sex moves that had come up in the games that I've been a part of.

    \n The first game of Apocalypse World I ever played featured a sex move--the Brainer Special. I was playing the Brainer, and I had Unnatural Lust Transfixion. Cybelle was a transsexual slut who loved people and loved to experience them fully. She was in love with the Savvyhead, Jessica. We had stolen/rescued Jessica from the Hardholder Esco, and when he sent his men to get her back, she was terribly wounded in the crossfire. After we were all captured Cybelle ended up seducing Esco with the Unnatural Lust Transfixion in exchange for letting Jessica go, and when her Special kicked in she learned that A) Esco was a good man who genuinely loved Jessica, what Cybelle had done had broken him, and C) his Holding would not survive if he wasn't made whole. The session ended with Jessica returning and effecting healing and reconciliation between the three of them.

    I obviously drove play toward this on account of the sex move. The Brainer's move is interesting in that not only is its effect a big fucking deal, there's another move (ULT) that plays into it, meaning that you either have a Brainer who's a "sex build" or you don't.

    It's great that the move isn't a straight-up attack. It's...potentially useful, but also leaves the Brainer vulnerable and at the mercy of the MC in terms of what knowledge she gains. I used that to push toward an emotionally raw character arc, full of sexiness and creepiness but also a sort of tenderness. It worked out great.

    I definitely wouldn't have happened if I hadn't see that sentence on the playbook about what happens if I have sex. I would've just been "doo-tee-doo, psychic chick, acts creepy, scans people, occasionally jerks their strings." Having a gender of "transgressing" on the sheet definitely enabled the arc as well.

    I blogged about it a year ago.

She convinces him to come back to her bombed-out apartment, but only at the cost of making a new enemy, Fel's on again off again woman, Tao. It's a price Brace is willing to pay.

    Her eyes tear up and she starts to say "You can leave if you want to," but she doesn't want him to know she can read him. And it's been so long... She tries to spoon with him and him and he turns away. She closes her eyes.

    Oh the fuckery...
