\n most of my friends, who are all Role-playing professional masters, have been hounding me to finish more of my sweet games which are for professional role-players only. the recipe is simple: take excellent and amazing games which are much beloved, and crank the awesomeness up to eleven by smashing them (like in a particle accelerator) into other amazing games and scraping the sweet cream off the top. here follows a list of the cream i've been skimming:

i'm most excited about my long-awaited "patch" game called The Shadow Of Yesterday D20, which takes the popular setting of Near and moves it into the much more familiar set-up of D20. many of my friends have been complaining about having to learn a new system, and being unable to bring the characters that they've been working on for years into this wonderful realm; their wait is almost over. i'm very excited.

a game that's just waiting on artwork is called SOL - Sorcerer Occupied Landfill. it's gonna be so amazing that i think the folks from white wolf might be flying me out to Gen Con this year, and i am very, very stoked. two of the most revered games of all time, twice as awesome as either and three times as daunting! oh yes!

a personal favorite of mine will be playtesting this weekend, Breaking The Agon. how can you go wrong? i'm particularly proud of the bonuses given for the distance-you-are-from-your-date chart, it swims like a charm. and be sure to talk about those lines and veils! things can get pretty hairy in a dating game where everything is a competition. let's just say that your handfuls of dice don't represent your sword and shield anymore. hot!

still on the writing board is Dogs In The Explosion. i know we've got mormons trying to navigate the japanese high school system, but i'm unsure as to how a) i can justify them being able to speak japanese, and b) whether contests such as passing an exam will require the students to escalate to firearms. and whether that will help things.

and similarly, i've got Deathmatch Hunters; tribal protectors who are trained from a young age to seek out classes or congregations of young people... who are fighting... and besting them, then getting tattoos from their blood. i guess i'm unclear on how that would not be the best thing ever, and also how to do it the proper justice.

recently i had a major breakthrough on my work with The Panties of Eternity, adding a endgame mechanic which makes the whole thing run towards a final showdown and clarifying gameplay. i'm kind of trying to avoid calling it My Life With Panties but i don't know if i should. suggestions?

a real gem is the shelf-sitter Perfect Inside. it's set in the country of Cadence, but now instead of playing the fiery rebels bent on pulling down the dystopic government oppressors, players take the part of the Inspectors who police the masses and keep them oppressed- EXCEPT! the players portray benevolent inspectors, whos hands are tied by the system but can help the anarchists by TURNING A BLIND EYE! response has been lukewarm. i'm concerned that the market isn't ready for a game of this magnitude, a almost zen game which has the players refusing to interact with their surroundings to this high degree... perhaps some of those i've assumed were role playing masters aren't really masters at all.

i'm not sure how to go about presenting The Zorcerer Of Your Head, because it kind of seems like equal measures of the same colour awesome, which are radiclly different flavours. i guess i'm imagining a world which takes place within this world AND another one AND only in the mind of the protagonist AT THE SAME TIME. and for one hour every night the narrative will be forced to harbour many familiar tropes and much symbolism. and i'm not sure where to go past that.

turning it up a notch, i'm preparing Breaking The Agon Your Head. it's mostly as presented above, but now adds exhaustion dice. now it's really time to break out those veils. i'm thinking of including with it two possible LARP options, but first i'll have to figure out which of the two is illegal in which states, and how that'll affect sales. it's all about the bottom. um, line. (hmmm. upon re-read, potentially homophobic joke not intended. other jokes stand as written.)

something i've run a couple of playtests of is kind of my pet project, A Thousand And One Nights: The Reckoning. players don't portray humans who hunt down zombies and wipe them from the face of the earth (and inexplicably using difficult-to-differentiate-between symbols composed of lines and dots to catagorize one another), no, instead players portray humans who are playing a game in which they, in turn, play in as humans who hunt zombies across the face of the earth! kill! what has made the game a local favorite is the mechanic i added in which, every time a die is rolled and comes up even, the players are removed from the hunters another "level" - immediately everyone draws up new characters, and now the humans playing are playing as humans who are portraying humans who are playing as humans hunting zombies, yes, across the face of the earth! kill, kill, kill! and when you stop to think about the odds of an "even" die result comes up, i'm not gonna do the math right now but i'd bet it's more than a third of the time, well damn very quickly the whole game explodes out into this big beautiful web, makes you kind of teary eyed. post here for the pdf, i'll look forward to reading your actual play reports.

and speaking of a big, beautiful web, i'm mostly done my Hard Immersion Simulation game, seeking to faithfully portray and re-construct the form and work of the RPG Advocacy boards, and the early days of The Forge. someone suggested play by post, but i think it would lose something in the translation. of course, it's titled Dogma In The Vineyard, and i'm very interested in the ramifications of communal art imitating life. there was another project that came from similar roots, as far as i understand, my friend Andy put together a more friendly, hug-y, dirty hippie version of this concept i've presented, but i've been unable to find it online. if anyone has a link to his project, i'd appreciate it.



yes, so there you have it! many good games, all of them deserving thanks to the solid games they spring from, those giants whose shoulders i stand on. much, much love.
so... if anyone has similar projects in the works, or suggestions for my projects, please post them below. and thanks to jake super atarashi twin for telling me in no uncertain terms to post what i've got in the works. tell me about your games!

    \n Last night, I finally got to play Burning the Ice. It was awesome! We spent about two hours making our characters (which the game calls "slow roasting" for some reason).     I'll definitely play again! And I want to try the sister game, Shooting the Bubblegum.
