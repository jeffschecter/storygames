Girl Genius gaming

Doyce
October 2006 edited October 2006 in Story Games
Okay, so we're putting one of our weekend playtest games on pause for awhile, and talking about what we want to play instead in that slot. There's been some talk with this group about TSoY (either some kind of Lankhmaresque fantasy thing, or ERBurroughs Sci Fi), and I'm potentially running a City of Lost Children/Parkour/Neverwhereish Mortal Coil game for some of the same folks Real Soon Now, so I've been reading up on that system as well.

And one of the players, the one I have the HARDEST time really 'getting' most of the time, pipes up with "I wanna play, like... Girl Genius."

So... steampunk derived from magic abilities -- basically all 'magic' is manifested in contructs, either steamdriven or frankensteinian. I ponder mixing in a little of the uber-arc from Planetary just to make it weirder.

And I'm thinking "well, Mortal Coil... we can make the rules for all that stuff in GG up as we go along."

And another player has just read the mini-supplement Screampunk for Gurps and swears that TSoY Pumpkin Fantasy would be perfect for GG.

And I'm ... undecided. I crave some input on what games would work really well for Girl Genius type stuff. -- Might help if I'd read more than the first collection of issues.

    Do a Don't Rest Your Head hack, only instead of the usual attributes, you have...

    Skill (replaces Discipline), where practice and hard work pay off.

    Zeal (replaces Madness), where your will to succeed pushes you forward. The problem is that the more of it you use, the bigger chance you have that it'll dominate your life to the exclusion of all else.

    Spark (replaces Exhaustion), where your creative lunacy pushes you forward into brilliance... until finally you create something that explodes, starts eating folks, or just plain screws everyone over.

    The GM rolls Wulfenbach dice. Or, depending on the threat, she might roll Other dice.

    Fitting non-sparks into the scheme would require something a bit different, I suppose, but they're all lower-power characters in the comics anyway.
