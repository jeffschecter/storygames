    \n Thanks to everybody for their feedback. I\u2019ll definitely be using some of these. I\u2019m very amused, however, that all of the people posting in this thread (besides me) are men. Funny.

    A specific point:

    To people who have problems with young-looking skinny women \u2013 YES. YES. A MILLION TIMES YES. I agree with you. However, I\u2019m doing these prints to help cover the quite substantial cost of buying into a booth and driving down from Canada, so I also have to consider WHAT WILL SELL.

    Gamers are a conservative lot, and I figured it was much better to take one issue at a time rather than trying to take on a bunch of different issues at once. Women as porn objects in fantasy art is a small but pervasive issue specific to the gaming community. Unrealistic portrayal of women\u2019s body shapes is a larger cultural issue and one with less commercial appeal.

    Yes the women that I drew are skinny and fit-looking. But I hope you\u2019ll notice that they are NOT anime-proportioned. And frankly, considering that I have spent a large amount of personal energy creating fine art that deals with women\u2019s body issues, I feel pretty comfortable not taking it on in this instance. And I feel I should point out that this is pretty much how *I* looked when I was younger and training 24/7 in tae kwon do and cross country. Most women just don\u2019t bulk up like men do.

    (Also, one thing that I managed to sneak in there that no one commented on is the fact that I snuck non-white women into my illustration as well. But the fact that no one commented on it was the reaction I was hoping for\u2026)

    /end pontification
