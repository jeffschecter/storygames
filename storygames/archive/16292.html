\n While I was at PAX, I talked to a guy who was super interested in our table full of indie and small press games, but lamenting the fact that he'd never have a chance to find and play them at home because he was from England. I told him that I knew there were several retailers in England that carry our games (notably Leisure Games in London and Patriot Games in Sheffield) and lots of gamers and mini-cons (I mentioned that Conpulsion was happening right at that moment in Edinburgh).

Finally, I told him to come here to find out more. So assuming he does, where can someone in the UK go to find and play story games, and to meet other like-minded gamers? What are the conventions and meetups that they should know about?

    \n The short version:

    Indie Friendly Conventions

    Dragonmeet in London in December-ish
    Concrete Cow in Milton Keynes, twice yearly in March and September
    Conpulsion as mentioned above
    UK Games Expo in Birmingham in June (at least one indie stand plus games to be played)
    Indiecon in November in Dorset
    Furnace in Sheffield in October

    Meetups


    Glasgow Indie Gamers
    London Indie RPG Meetup

    Plus others I'm probably forgetting.

    \n Can I ask what's happening design/publication-wise in UK indie games right now?     \n Jonathan, it's hard to summarise, but here's an attempt. There are various active RPG clubs: the Pompey Crew in Plymouth, the Milton Keynes RPG Club, the London Indie RPG meetup. There are also various indie-friendly RPG conventions: IndieCon, Concrete Cow, Conception, Furnace. And there's crossover between the two, so that the Pompey Crew does lots of stuff at IndieCon and Conception, while Milton Keynes RPG Club organises Concrete Cow.

    I think the new design stuff comes from those clubs and conventions. James Mullen is a Milton Keynes RPG Club stalwart. The excellent game Witch comes from the Pompey Crew. Stuart Chaplin attends the London Indie RPG Meetup. Sebastian Hickey goes to various conventions, including London Indie RPG Meetup.

    So I think the clubs and conventions are the things to watch. That's where new games are played and discussed and where some new designs get tested.

    (I'm in the South, so I'm probably missing the stuff that happens in the North and in Scotland.)

    \n Another good place for him to check out is www.ukroleplayers.com - and, in fact, it gives a pretty strong indication of what's going on around the place. There are plenty of regional clubs with their own forums on there (our own, STURP, has arguably a bigger web presence than we have IRL, and we're moving more and more storygamy as the weeks pass). Having just got back from Conpulsion, there was a decent amount of Indie games being run there (Mouse Guard, Chronicles of Skin, Monsterhearts, Monkey, Hot War, Dead of Night, .... and probably more that I've missed...) Furnace has a similar strong Indie representation, and I get the feeling there's lots of us about in the UK talking about them here and on ukroleplayers.

    For what it's worth (and all I know of the US scene is what's gleaned from this site), UK-based Indie games get more exposure over here than in the States (Monkey, Duty & Honour/Beat to Quarters, Hot War, Wordplay, others I've no doubt missed)... which I guess is to be expected.
