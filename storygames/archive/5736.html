    I love the old Cyberpunk. I want to play. I want to play a Rocker who was the Season 23 American Idol (that'd be 2023, two years before the presumed start date). Pointless achievements and meaningless, re-hashed celebrity is sooooo cyberpunk. Also, I never got the Rocker class way back then, but now, now I understand what pointless celebrity is all about, and I can totally get into it.

    I'd like to play Champions from back in the day, back before there was a "Hero System" and the Hero brand wasn't so damn "brandy." Back when they had Champions, Danger International, Justice Inc., etc. But I don't want to use the combat system. Ever again.

      Posted By: AnemoneSkyrealms of Jorune (new system, please!)
      \nJorune 3E is all about exploring the world as a mandatory part of the process of becoming a citizen of Burdoth. Therefore, I recommend using TSOY, where each character has a Key of the Tauther (candidate for citizenship) that awards XP for new experiences; transcendence by default means that your character returns to Burdoth, becomes a citizen, and retires from adventuring. (Of course, it's a Key, which means you *can* buy it off and renounce your intention to become a citizen... *and* that you could still become a citizen when you transcend, even though you no longer have the Key related to it. And of course transcendence could still mean something other than becoming a citizen.)

      I would also love to see a Jorune oracle for IAWA - hell, or just for helping to generate situations in TSOY-Jorune - but an IAWA game of Jorune would have a different focus than "standard" Jorune, since in IAWA the characters derived from the oracle aren't necessarily people seeking citizenship. I think IAWA-Jorune would be better for people who are already familiar with the Jorune setting and just want to tell cool stories in that setting.

      Oh, thread topic: I'd also love to play Jorune, as you can tell I've been thinking about how to do so. I'd love to play Planescape again, though like others have said, I think not with D&D but with another system, so maybe that's cheating. I'm right with John Harper on playing Talislanta, and I really hope to put together a few pieces soon to make that happen. I only ever played one unsatisfactory one-shot of Shadowrun, but I've always wanted to play in that setting; I'm working on the Agon conversion for it and hopefully that'll happen soon too. Finally, I've just been reminded that I have the TSR Conan game from back in the mid-'80s; I'm going to have to give that a read-through and see if it seems worth running at least a one-shot.
