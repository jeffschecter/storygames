    \n@Dragonfly would you be interested in Swedish story games? All of my games are free and public domain, and they have been played quite a lot at Swedish conventions over the years, and not just run by me. They are not available in English, but I speak passable Spanish and would be delighted to make an attempt to translate them if you guys could help me correct grammar mistakes and polish the language. Here are the games:

    Utpost
    http://www.urverkspel.com/utpost-nedladdningar/send/11-utpost/46-utpost
    You're a group of people on an isolated outpost in space. The cook thinks the captain is incompetent, the engineer is sleeping with the geologist even though she's married to the captain and the doctor is involved in a scheme to take control of the entire outpost. But a dark threat is looming. You play to find out if you can put your differences aside and unite against the threat or if you fight amongst yourselves and perish. this game has a GM-ish and works best for 4-7 players.

    Svart av kval, vit av lust
    http://www.urverkspel.com/sakval-nedladdningar/send/13-sakval/53-svart-av-kval
    A GM-less and diceless game about intriguing vampires and moral dilemmas. Can you hold on to your humanity despite the hungry beast inside you screaming for blood? It's my reinterpretation of Vampire: The Masquerade with a focused system that doesn't get in the way of the narrative. Rules are communicated by a small series of hand gestures and movement of markers, which means you never have to break the narrative by talking about the mechanics. 3-6 players or thereabouts.

    Det sj\xe4tte inseglet
    http://www.urverkspel.com/detsjatteinseglet-nedladdningar/send/16-detsjatteinseglet/66-det-sjaette-inseglet
    An angsty swashbuckling tragedy of heroics and zeppelins, for three players. You play swashbuckling heroes who struggle with moral failings like greed or ambition. As the game progresses, it gets more and more difficult to resist the temptations, and usually the characters will end up as corrupt and powerful, or die a tragic but heroic death (though happy endings have been known to happen in the game).

    Nerver av st\xe5l
    (No link yet)
    A game trying to emulate the classic era of film noir. Narration is done in first person past tense, in the style of film noir voiceovers, with a defined main character present in all scenes. The game begins by establishing a mystery and the players develop and finally resolve it through play. In play, you have to adhere to Hollywood's strict production standards forbidding you from using swears, showing nudity, etc, yet you constantly try to subvert these rules through hints and "plausible deniability" Three or four players.

    Drakar och bananer
    (No link yet)
    A silly, GM-less cooperative adventuring game pitting the players against the game and requiring you to come up with creative ways to use our skills and equipment to tackle the various threats. How can you use your Good Looks and your Dramatic Cape to defeat the Demon King? How can you leverage your Pointy Stick and your Climbing abilities to sneak past the guards and into the castle? Tactical and creative. And pretty silly. Maybe 3-5 players.

    ---

    Sorry about the long post. If any of those games sound interesting to you, let me know and I'd be happy to start working on a rough translation to send you. It'd be rad to have a Spanish translation of any of my games. The first two are more lengthy in terms of text, and the others are relatively short.
