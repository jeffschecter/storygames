\n I'm looking for a game that can be played by email, or by post on a forum.

I'll attempt to set out some things which I'm looking for, but what I think I want and what will actually be coherent and fun may not be the same thing! Feel free to advise me.

* Playable by two players. If possible, expandable to one or two more.
* Something pretty freeform. Few/no resolution mechanics. Perhaps this is a bad idea, but I feel like I'd like it to be closer to the collaborative writing end of things.
* At the same time, I'd like helpful tools to structure the kind of story that evolves. For example, the way Archipelago or Intrepid help you to create an oracle at the start that sets expectations for the world. Or the way Apocalypse World gets the GM to explicitly make some things questions to resolve through play - setting stakes. Structures at that kind of level, that give us tools to negotiate and decide some things, instead of just having to write fiction unaided.
* Ideally I'd like a system that can handle short stories, of a few tens of posts, as well as longer stories. But two systems is also fine.

Any ideas? By the way, I did search the forum for answers, but the things I could find were mostly from 5 - 10 years ago, so I thought it was time for a new thread.

I'm open to "ordinary" story games. But I'm more interested in things that are more specifically geared to play by email (PBEM) and play by post.

Thanks!

    \n That's an interesting set of criteria!

    I think Fiasco could work well (although that's not great for two players, you could each take on two characters and I think it would work fine).

    Gun Thief could be a good game for your purposes. It normally requires three players, but I think it could work just fine for two.

    Over here, Eero and a friend/colleague played S/Lay/w/Me by post, and it seems to have been quite successful. At least, I really enjoyed reading it, and it seems to suit/fit your criteria.
