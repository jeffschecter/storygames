Where: http://www.kickstarter.com/projects/1571708637/monsterhearts-second-skins

What: $1,475 and 70 hours left to get 11 (eleven) Stretch Goals:

Why:

1. Autumn Winters and Jason Morningstar design a stand-alone game about a family of monsters moving into a human town, each member trying to fit in, trying to help one another live a normal life despite being monsters (an analogue for assimilation among immigrants).

2. World of Dungeons expansion from John Harper (bundled with World of Dungeons itself).

3. Johnstone Metzger writing Space Wurm vs. Moonicorn, an adventure scenario to accompany Adventures on Dungeon Planet and Dungeon World with AT LEAST two new space fantasy themed Dungeon World playbooks based on the Second Skins!

4. Monsterhearts Live Action Role-Playing by Terry Romero, Kira Scott, and John Stavropoulos.

5. The Reanimator Skin by Renee Knipe and Bret Gillan.

6. Ben Wray's mini-supplement that can patch onto any Monsterhearts Skin - that is, whichever is the last of their kind!

7. Amy Fox preparing A Spectrum of Shadows - a short supplement on filling out the range of gender and sexuality; for MC's and players, for Monsterhearts and beyond.

8. A Harry Potter hack by Jonathan Walton.

9. Renee Knipe weave together her daydreams in Bahamian Rhapsody!

10. Ben Wray gives us Car Troubles!

11. Kira Scott and John Stavropoulos building a Monsterhearts one-shot/convention toolkit.

    Dungeonhearts
    Youth on the Borderlands

    You are all monsters, outcasts, runaways, the disturbed, and the abandoned. You don\u2019t really remember whose idea it was to hole up in the abandoned school way out on the outskirts of town, but it has become your home, full of seemingly endless corridors, old classrooms, underground maintenance tunnels, random oddments, and the occasional priceless treasure (a diary, a love letter, a photograph). There you live in a micro-society full of ambitions, petty rivalries, romances, crises, want, and excesses.

    You may despise some of the others, but there are bigger threats that drive you together: true monstrosities looking to take advantage of a bunch of street kids with nowhere else to go. They share this underworld with you, but you avoid them when you can and run like hell when you see them coming. There are monsters and then there are monsters.

    Occasionally, the braver kids with \u201cnormal\u201d lives band together in makeshift groups and venture into your domain, sometimes armed with sticks and bats, sometimes just with eyes that you could get lost in and skin that smells like dreams. You call them \u201cthe Adventurous,\u201d and despite your desire to befriend them, to become intimate with them, to drive them away before they draw the attention of real sociopaths or come to harm in this dungeon... you\u2019re a monster and, in the end, you can\u2019t help but destroy them.

    A two-page campaign framework for Monsterhearts
    by J.Walton, inspired by Joe, Jackson, & Ben Wray
