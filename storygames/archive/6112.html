    \n My little Risus hack went as follows:

    In a conflict, you can, at any time, voluntarily drop your Cliche by one die. In return, you get to roll its full value until the next time you lose a roll.

    Example: You have 6 dice in you main Cliche. After some fighting, you're down to 3 dice. You can voluntarily drop one more die. You're now at 2 dice, but you can now roll *all 6*... until you lose again. Then you'll find yourself with only 1 die left.

    It's sort of a fun gamble, since both sides can do it at any time.

    \n I don't have an issue with the dice. My biggest concern was that the system uses a broad resolution to tackle combat and a very narrow, task-based, system versus target number to handle everything else. I've recently been experimenting with assigning cliches to all challenges -- e.g., Mt. Everest in Winter (5) -- and using the 'combat' system to resolve them. So far, it has worked out very well.

    \n Oh. I also have been experimenting with the Ghostbusters influence by allowing people to 'buy' extra trait points in character creation be defining their character's fate (i.e., the conditions of their ultimate demise). Any time that one of the conditions applies to a challenge being resolved, the player rolls a Fate Die (little dice that are blank on all sides, save for one, which features a tombstone). Multiple conditions incurr multiple Fate Dice. These are rolled in addition to normal dice, with results of a tombstone cancelling out regular dice from lowest to highest, driving down the chance of success.
