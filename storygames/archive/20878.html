    \n I find the criteria "less than 20 pages" pretty arbitrary. I'd rather go with a "quick to learn, light systems that features a GM".
    For example, I'd recommend Blades in the Dark wholeheartedly. The current PDF (still pre-release) is 96 pages. But the real rules are just 19 pages.

    I would also see Fate Accelerated in the same range. The real rules are not much more than 20 compact well-designed pages.

    I believe also FU 1st edition fully meets your criteria.

    \n I'm only listing games I've played and are sitting in my hard-drive right now.

    Plucky Kid Detective by Anna Anthropy.

    SKEW by Ben Lehman actually has a Master of Ceremonies (facilitator) role and a separate GM role, which is introduced halfway through the game and may change hands repeatedly before the final act.

    Clover, also by Ben Lehman, is you count the Dad player as a de facto GM.

    Murderous Ghosts by Vincent Baker is longer than 20 pages, but the part you're actually supposed to read before you start playing is just a couple of those.

    The playtest document for Freebooting Venus is 22 pages, but some familiarity with Apocalypse World is recommended.

    Also by Vincent, Amazon, found in Worlds Without Master #11 (OK, I haven't played this - plus, there are more than one GMs).

    Swords Without Master has the interesting Overplayer role, which is very powerful in ways and very weak in others, compared to a more traditional GM role. The base rules take 20 pages in Worlds Without Master #3, if you leave out The Advanced Game (10 more pages). Or there's the City of Fire and Coin free document - 23 pages including character sheets.

    I have a 2013 free demo of Golden Sky Stories which is a 22 pages booklet, including a scenario.

    \n I'd say Amidst Endless Quiet by Ben Lehman would count. It's not explicitly GMed, and it got cited in the Favortie GMless thread, but I would argue that the Elios role in the game is a pretty strong GM role (as a unique role in the group with an incredible amount of spotlight control and literally all of the mechanical interaction). As for length, the game is the front and back of a single page.

    That's the one that jumps to mind that hasn't been mentioned yet.*
