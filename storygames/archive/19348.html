\n For many years, I've been developing a fantasy world for gaming purposes. I started it in tandem with Blade & Crown, but neither requires the other. And I've been thinking for a long time of making the wiki for my fantasy game-world public. Getting paid to develop a deep, well-maintained, self-consistent game-world? Yes, please.

I can't figure out how to try to fund it, though. I'm tempted to make it a Kickstarter to explicitly fund me to work on it for a couple years. Once funded, I'd upload what I already have (currently about 210 MB), then after a couple years, make everything created up to then CC or public domain. Patreon makes sense, too, but I don't know how to structure it in a way that seems fair -- per month? Per article? (But some articles are short and some are really long.) Per MB of data? (But that makes images literally worth way more than words.)

What do y'all think?

    \n Hm, guess I'd like to aim for something surer than just "see what happens".

    And yeah, it seems like the best way to be successful in RPGs is... to already be successful. But hopefully I'm starting to get my name and abilities out there. Blade & Crown has been out for over a year now, and I've been blogging steadily since then. I hope to finish the first adventure for B&C soon, and I've had tons of setting material on my personal website for more than a decade. So while I may not be a big name, I've been around for a while.

    So far as I've seen, this would be a whole new thing. Kind of like Mark Rosenfelder's Almeopedia, but geared more specifically for gaming and, eventually, downloadable. And unlike a lot of Kickstarter projects, it'd be usable from day 1. (As soon as I could get it uploaded, basically.)
