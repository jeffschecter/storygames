    I've heard some of these, and found some great stuff on publishing, some amusing stuff on community, and a few tidbits of how to do actual design of game rules. Can anyone call out any of these as being particularly useful on that last front?

    Thanks!
    \nYeah, I'm not particularly interested in publishing (yet), and there's community stuff everywhere. Sometimes there is discussion about the Big Model and Forge terminology, which I feel often gets bogged down in semantics. Then there is discussion about how to fix armor penetration in some 90s game, or reskin or hack something in order to make the game more like a popular favorite (FATE, AW, etc). But rarely do I see or hear discussion about the philosophy behind specific rules choices: what does every little rule mean in a grand scheme of things and that sort of stuff. It's hard to explain, I guess I should start a new thread about it.

    But anyway, what David said. Something for us who crave for rules discussion rather than publishing or community stuff.

    Sometimes there is discussion about the Big Model and Forge terminology, which I feel often gets bogged down in semantics.
    \nYeah, after spending 2 years reporting on Forge diaspora theory I reached the conclusion that no one actually agrees on these terms. It's just that some folks haven't yet realized how much they disagree...

    Mark Rosewater over at Wizards writes Making Magic, and he has just started a podcast as well and if they are half as good as his articles you'll want to listen to them. Making Magic is the first place I direct prospective game designers because it is both accessible and contains a wealth of analysis and practice.

    There are certainly a number of TED talks game designers ought to listen to. Is that too far afield? Or would you like me to link some of those in?
