\n I was thinking that before the game, each player writes a story element (or several) on an index card and shuffles it into a pile. The cards are drawn and interpreted as usual.

Has anyone done this before? How did it work out? Are there any caveats?

    \n We do something similar, but not exactly the same.

    First, we played a bunch (about 10 sessions) and got to know each other... and got a solid baseline of what the game contained/was about.

    I've asked my players to add 1 card to each of our 5 thematic decks. I suggested a couple of things that the best elements from the original game:

    1) Imply a relationship
    2) Avoid clich\xe9s
    3) Are evocative of the setting, possibly using slightly archaic phrasing or descriptive words with very specific meanings

    BUT... the rule is:

    Don't write something just to win the 'I know how to write a card that sounds good' game. Do it to put something that you want to see into the game.
