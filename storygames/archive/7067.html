    \n I can recommend Fastaval. It is a fixpoint in my Calendar each year. Takes place each year during Easter in a school in the suburbs of \xc5rhus.

    Very active community that provides the games (scenarios). No tradition for non-scandinavian guests, but I will be happy to help change that.

    Due to the long duration (four days), there is a very relaxed atmosphere. The big finale is the closing banquet Sunday evening with good food, entertainment and prizes for the best games.

    \n Hey, I can answer this non-larp stuff.

    Ropecon (in Espoo, Finland) is easily the best Nordic tabletop convention, he said with convinction. Not only do we boast the best indie array (heck, it's on par with anything else in Europe, most likely), but there's also the largest number of participants (easily over 3000, I understand) interested in all sorts of gaming. (I recommend the annual boardgame design competition, whether as a participant or judge; this year is all about games you can play in under ten minutes.) I know not what Jukka is saying about not getting hands-on experience; perhaps he just doesn't visit us in room 25, so he's stuck with the dull types for the whole convention. It's also cheap, as you can sleep on the premises in specially reserved rooms.

    Damn, this reminds me: I should ask whether those british people have time for Ropecon this year. They were entertaining last time, but I'm sure a repeat experience could be better.

    \n I've been to all the festivals in Norway, and Holmcon is by far the best if you want modern games, relaxed atmosphere and inspired discussions. This years RPP-slam was wonderful! We had 1 hour to make games, and then all of us got to play 3 of them and voting for the best. Until now HomCon has been held in the private home of Matthijs Holter. He is talking about renting a hotel next year though. I'll participate whatever he decides.

    I would love to go to Fastaval too, at least once, as I have heard a lot about it. A lot of the scenarios made for that festival is top of the line; experimental, exciting and fun (I've tried several of them at HolmCon: Matthijs is a great man for bringing new games into Norway).

    RopeCon would also be an experience I'd like to have, but further down the list. First I would like to visit LinCon in Sweden; a small and traditional one, but one I've heard a lot of nice things about.
