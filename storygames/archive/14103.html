    \n I have used Scribus a little bit and I do appreciate the price tag of zero. It has a great deal of power, once you manage to wrap your head around the program. That said, most of my reasons for doing so are as Rafu states; I value free software and I want to add a "made with free software" tag for marketing purposes.

    I personally vote for the thing. It has it's flaws, but I think it can give a quality product and I personally enjoy the process of learning by doing.

    Oh, if you want to learn the thing, start by designing a character sheet. If you can get that done, you are _set_ for any other layout work you might have to do. Oy.

    \n I've used Scribus, OpenOffice, Microsoft Publisher and PagePlus, but never InDesign. I am not skilled at layout, but even my non-ambitious projects were nightmares with Scribus. PagePlus is clunky; Publisher isn't as advanced as some of the other layout programs -- but it's more intuitive. I would recommend just using OpenOffice or Word unless you have to use a layout program.

    If you do use Scribus, go for the 'developmental' release. It's not particularly buggy and it does work better. I was banging my head against a problem in stable Scribus (unfortunately I can't remember what it was) and it was fixed in developmental Scribus.

    \n I have done my games with TeX. It is very easy produce easy to read stuff that way, the defaults are always reasonable. (Those of you who bought my game While the World Ends can see what it looks like with some minor tweaking.)

    I like that I can just write my text, and insert tags for the pictures (which mostly say, "I'd like to put this picture somewhere near this text block."), and then generate the document. And TeX will automagically flow the text, and insert the pictures where it is reasonable to do so. The same thing goes for tables and the grey boxes I use for examples and stuff.

    But it is a lot of work to do fancy stuff with TeX. The further you go from the math/science-text, the more you'll have to learn. I mostly use google, someone else has usually solved the same problem before and has posted the solution online, just to cut'n'paste and tweak.
