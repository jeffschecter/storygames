The other discussion about formats, sizes and whatnot got me thinking about something I was considering a while back.

Several years ago, a version of Werewolf came out that had a hole, like a tear, through the cover. I didn't like the game much, but I thought the idea of the cover was really nifty. Just a bit more interesting than just a picture and a logo. Sure it was gimmicky as hell, but y'know, it got me thinking.

What if you actually use odd shaped paper in a game's system? Like, I dunno, holes in the page that you can see certain things through and stuff like that. The nearest thing one I can think of right now is the Click/Lock thingies in Capes. I love that stuff, and love that you can cut those little things out and put them together. Although the cost of producing them would almost certainly be horrible, I mean, I know Tony's not price-gouging with the Click/Lock thingies on IPR and they're really damned expensive.

So I was thinking about perhaps some kind of sheet where the back part can be folded over the front and partly obscures some of the front. Imagine a sheet of A4 or letter or whatever, in a landscape orientation. Imagine folding the left third of the page over so that covers half of what's left. You'd have a character sheet that's kinda square when it's "closed", and regular A4 when it's "open", but you have 2/3rds of the front page concealed when the sheet is "closed". I have no idea quite how this would be used, but I find the idea appealing.




Say, for example, there was a system in which everyone had a character, but each scene had only one character as the Protagonist, and all the other characters present in the scene are Supporting Cast. You can use the "open" side for when the character's a Protagonist and the smaller, different, "Closed" side for when they are Supporting Cast.

Anything else like that about that I've forgotten or never heard of? Are such things just gimmicky and rubbish, or could something like that actually be useful?
