    \n Thanks, again, for giving it a go, guys. I've (FINALLY) gotten a change to listen to all of your recording RE GLASS; and I was taking notes for a while;... but, yeah.

    I definitely need to be more explicit about how to use the System Test Document. It sounded like y'all scanned the TOC, saw "Character Creation" and jumped to that... and got buried in confusion. Which I would expect because the whole book layout (again, just for the STD) is hyper front-loaded. In essence, the whole STD is a Glossary that steadily builds on previous terms. So, yeah, if you didn't know the basic action sequence or what single, double, and quad costs meant... yeah, tough row to hoe, if you start at p.35!

    It also helps if you've had exposure to Champions or HERO System (I can't recall if GURPs has the equivalent Ability Modifiers or not). The model of the mechanics is virtually identical, though highly streamlined for action-LARP-specific play. Further, No one would have had the cheek to try to take One-Legged x2 AND Ponderous:
    * One-Legged x2 = Can't walk (or stand, jump, or run).
    * Ponderous = Walk slow.

    Obviously, Ponderous doesn't "actually increase the risk to the character or limit the character" that is already no-legged; so no Exp would have been granted for buying Ponderous. (Heh... Ponderous is even used in the example in the RAW of a disingenuous Defect purchase! LOL)

    Also, being no-legged will make you an SC (as it says) and so you could only use Stare Attacks (which would have been prohibitively expensive in Exp). But hey, it's a concept!

    Suffice it to say that I have every intention of building the book with more of a "start simple, build in detail" approach. But that will mean a LOT of repetition and cross-referencing, and I don't want to get to that level of verbiage when I'm still uncertain if all of the rules will stay in. (I'm looking REAL hard at YOU, Scope.)

    I'll happily answer the most pressing questions you might have still bugging you.

    David

    \n [To Arpie, about the resource management handling.]

    Health is the only "speed-handling stat" that you'd ever track--folks are hitting you with Weapons and (rarely) calling out numbers, and they (tend to) cause Wounds (i.e., hit Armor, then Health).

    Drains are generally more rare, and mostly they are used by an enemy that isn't trying to actively hit you with a Weapon (pay attention to their costs--most Abilities that Drain are bought down with limitations). The practical outcome in combat is that it's very rare for Drain to impact speedy melee. It's kind of more like something folks sort out once the violence is ended (e.g., "Yeah, man, did you notice that Drain 2 the Lich hit you with, before you use Reserve to Restore Alacrity?").

    And there's no 'soak Abilities' for Psyche, so it's just a stat. No defense, one offense (Scar). When you hear folks calling out "Shock!" in actual melee, then the tendency is for players to run into the woods!

    So, yes, one must keep three "resources" in one's head to play fairly and react at the precise time when your character's situation would demand it. BUT... It's LARP. We KNOW folks miss math, don't feel hits, etc. Anyone with more than three weekends of LARP would just float past that vagueness. If shit gets totally pear-shaped, a GM calls a Hold and a short discussion ensues, followed by a restart (NOTE: Holy, cow! I didn't write Restart procedures!!)

    And you might also notice that the physical resource has a lot of 'angles' from which it can be managed or bolstered... but the mental is more limited... and the emotional is bare-assed. I think that's something worth grokking....

    ANYhow. "I'm just tryin' to help!"

    \n Thanks again for the feedback!

    I'm not sure what I can do to reduce GM Fiat, as the whole system is predicated on GMs totally customizing the genre and power levels, possibly even Packages and Templates, and only rarely letting players create abilities from scratch, with careful perusal of those with Warnings. In this way, it's basically like Hero System--one can do anything; but one rarely plays with anything available to anyone.

    GLASS can never be a "fire and forget" system for LARP. A particular GLASS Game Type can be such, though; and it will resemble nothing so much as a bog-standard, points-buy, pick-list RPG (e.g., classes, races, professions, canonical abilities). Some game types might even be GMless (e.g., genres with no NPCs and no "line crawls" or similar prepared content--for example, a basic WWII war game type that's 100% PvP).

    So I guess the best way I could make GLASS "your kind of fun" is to say, "OK, wait for complete GLASS-based game type rules to come out; and just buy the one or two genres that you'd play." And then grab shotGLASS (for free) so that you have the basic rules reference for procedures of play and effects (the game type will tell you what you can buy and how and with how many points; but, because my current CC license forbids reprinting any rule content in shotGLASS or GLASS, the game type won't have that basic stuff in it).

    They should be available... before the end of the decade. :}

    \n RE actual legless players walking on hands, and limbless players inchworming around.... Um... MAYBE I'll address that, in the discussion of Defects. But frankly, my inclination is to say that such a player would be at far too great a risk, in actual play (viz the Safe Characters rules, in general--they're built to protect BOTH disabled players AND the able players who might not notice someone below waist level.)

    As for a PC that "simulates" being an arm-walker or inchworm... I'll give that some thought. It's pretty hard for verisimilitude, to have someone be "legless" but have no wheelchair and so they are standing there saying, "I'm actually on the ground, but that's unsafe to do all the time, so I'm standing here."

    So, yeah... not Ponderous. Ponderous is, literally, for behemoths, zombies, etc--things that WALK, but do so very slowly.

    In closing, it's a well-known rule of LARP design: no rule system can contain cheating or system-abusing players. Whether GMs are in the loop or not, players have to self-regulate (and regulate each other) constantly in LARP. Hell, if your LARPs whole rule set was contained by the rules for hide and seek, there'd still be some players going out of bounds, or picking a hiding place that only a very tall person can reach, or whatever. The beauty of LARP is that it self-regulates so well--cheaters and abusers eventually are known by the whole troupe, and IGNORED if they insist upon misengaging the rules (e.g., ignoring hits in combat: opponent just stops fighting, say, "I'm done with your cheating" and walks away; that happens often enough, no one engages with the cheater and they get bored and quit).
