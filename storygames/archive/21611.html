So it looks like this:

5e inventory sheet

and you download it here

Forget about weights. Clear your mind. This system replaces that with item slots of six different sizes. Tiny, small, ration, medium, big, and heavy.

  • Tiny for things you put in your pouch
  • Small for things you put in your bag
  • Medium for things you hang from your belt
  • Big for things you strap onto your bag

Rations are twice as big as small things. They're the only thing that's that size. Heavy is also an unusual size, only a few normal gear items count as that heavy, and they're listed on the sheet.

  1. List your strength, and \u2153 and \u2154 of it too.
  2. Check your encumbrance budget. One check mark tells you three things: your penalty (no penalty, just a little slower, or a lot slower and disadvantage on all strength, dex and con rolls), your current gear weight, and how many circles you can mark.
  3. Mark that many circles. You've already paid for them with the check mark, so just go ahead. The backpack costs a double circle, mark both. Armor can cost 0, 1, 2, 3 or 4 depending on type.
  4. Fill in your gear in the slots that you\u2019ve gotten by marking circles.

Source code is here, improve it!

    The reason I don\u2019t do the same is because this is a part of the game that I want to teach the casuals. (Unlike the charop stuff where I\u2019m happy if they stay with The Searcher forever.)

    CW gory:

    The first OSR game I played in, the DM waved all of the encumbrance stuff and one guy put all the rib cages from every fallen foe in his pocket. He\u2026 did something with that later. That was the day I realized that yes encumbrance limits have their place. Before that day I had never seen it abused.

    I care about the inventory stuff because that\u2019s something that the character would care about. They would rifle through their backpack looking for that flask of oil.

    That\u2019s also why I like a list \u2013 a packing manifesto? Better than the square reddit slots or Tetris pieces of some of the other solutions I\u2019ve looked at. When you\u2019re writing a list you get away from the feeling of packing tiny little toy equipment.     Started playtesting the sheet from this thread\u2019s OP today!

    Very well received!

    Some problems, it did require explanation for sure.

    • The whole \u201cyou don\u2019t need to care about precise weights for the small, medium and heavy things\u201d was unclear and at first one player started listing in pound weights beside the boxes until I stopped him.
    • Also the fact that it\u2019s actually not super clear how many stones you\u2019re actually carrying. The backpack itself 2 stone, the tied-to-backpack 3 stone, then one stone for every three items on your body/belt/sheath etc, then pouches two per stone\u2026 That could be made visually clearer.
    • Also one PC could carry 11 stone with great difficulty, 6 stone with some difficulty and 3 stone easily. The way this sheet is set up, it was hard for him to get below 6 stone. Since there\u2019s pretty much five stones that are hard to move right there w/ the backpack&tied-to-backpack.

    It\u2019s still way more usable than anything we\u2019ve tried before, including that reddit one. But I hope some inspiration for a version 2 comes along soon.\u2665

    I still like the versions with the symbols better than the version with the chains, and the chains better than the original swapping guide.

    It\u2019s painful to throw out so much work. But sometimes you just stumble upon an easier solution.

    The symbols were meant as a clearer way of doing the same things as the chains version.
    The chains version as a clearer, more explicit way of doing the swapping.
    The swapping became necessary because my original idea didn\u2019t take in mind that people would want to micromanage and optimize to the degree that they immediately started doing.

    But the new version goes back to my original idea and instead of doing swapping, just adds more flexible slots. People want to buy a big and a medium? Now they can, a combo deal. People want to carry things in the ration compartment of the backpack? Now there\u2019s a space there for them to list those items. No more swapping, unlocking or buying slots. Just mark the circles you want and that\u2019s the space you have.

    I really love how this latest version makes it visual how the slots just become heavier and heavier. A circle can carry three medium things easily but only one really heavy thing.

    Forget about weights. Clear your mind. This system replaces that with item slots of five different sizes. Tiny (a.k.a. \u201ccoin-sized\u201d), Small, Medium, Big, and full Stone.

    Typically small things (like tinderboxes) go in your backpack, medium things (like weapons) you strap on yourself, and big thigs (like tents) you strap on your backpack. In case you\u2019re still worried, there\u2019s a guide printed on the sheet itself.

    I\u2019m going to walk through it with an example. Sarah, the rogue.
    She notes her character\u2019s weight \u2013 this isn\u2019t going to matter for her encumbrance, but it\u2019s good to know in case someone has to carry her body out of the dungeon \u2013 and her Strength score.

    Sarah noting her Strength

    Then she adds a third and two thirds.

    More specific encumbrance levels

    She wants to be unencumbered for this mission so she marks the left box.

    Left box

    It\u2019s a three, so that means she marks three circles. She is happy that her leather armor doesn\u2019t weigh anything.

    Pro tip: always take the backpack at this stage. It has the most flexibility when you want to customize later, and it\u2019s a good frame to strap things to.

    Three circles.

    She can start listing her gear. She has thirty small item slots and three medium slots. (She could\u2019ve also chosen a big slot instead of the three medium slot, or if she had been willing to go up to the next encumbrance level, she\u2019d get six circles to mark, for example spending three of them in one go to get on five big slots, and one more on another set of three medium slots. But she likes to do it this way.)

    Only list gear in slots you\u2019ve gotten access to by marking them.

    Sarah\u2019s fave gear.

    She\u2019s happy with the medium slots, since she loves weapons. But that backpack looks pretty sparse, and she has some bigger things she\u2019d like to take along.

    She can unlock a row for big things by crossing out ten small things. She does that twice. For the first she scratches out ten from the \u201cration corner\u201d, and for the second she crosses out six from the \u201cration corner\u201d and four from the \u201citem section\u201d. It doesn\u2019t really matter \u2013 the backpack is thirty small item slots, it\u2019s just that twenty of those slots take up less place on the sheet because you usually put rations there.

    Two new big slots.

    That\u2019s nice! Rope and bedroll strapped on there!

    The two big slots, filled.

    Finally, she adds a small pouch. Small pouches can carry 50 tiny things like coins, candles, nails and gems and take up a small item slot. Medium pouches can carry 250 tiny things and take up a medium slot.

    (Secret tip #1: If you want an old-school, PHB style 300 coin pouch, you can fill in both, one medium and one small. But don\u2019t do that, be happy with the pouches that are on here, that\u2019s gonna be easier.)

    (Secret tip #2: If you need more pouches, use sticky notes or draw them on the back.)

    (Secret tip #3: You can put small things inside pouches. Draw a filled triangle on the line. Small things take up 50 tiny slots so one completely fills up a small pouch, or you can fit up to five in a medium pouch. So inside a medium pouch you might carry your small magical component pouch, alongside 200 gems&coins, for example.)

    She also adds two rations. Rations are the only exception to the otherwise neat tiny/small/medium/big/full-stone system, but it\u2019s worth it because they\u2019re so delicious. A ration takes up two small slots. That\u2019s why there\u2019s a \u201cration corner\u201d of the backpack, with double-slots, she just circles two of those to indicate that she has yet to eat them. Or put Xs there or whatever you want.

    Done!

    She has two empty small item slots and place for 50 coins or gems, in case she finds any on her mission. If she eats the food she\u2019ll get even more room.

    PS: reversing

    If she later loses the rope, or ditches it, she can get ten small item slots back. Do that right away, your item slots can be life or death!\u2665

    It\u2019s interesting to see how this misguided evolution could take place\u2026

    the first version was made just assuming you\u2019d be able to carry it all. It took eight stones worth of gear and divided it up cozily. Adding some common sense presets (or so I thought). To make gear shopping faster. A backpack full of small items, wearing some medium items, strapping on some big items\u2026 but just a couple of test characters proved that version to be pretty untenable.

    I was like \u201cenh, people can just swap around if they really want to micromanage\u201d. Turns out everyone wants to do that. So then came some iterations of the \u201cswapping guide\u201d version. Some of these are lost to history, like a version that hade the 1 and 2 stones indicator for the big things on one side and the 3 stone indicator on the other. Or that didn\u2019t have clear stones indicators in the first place.

    But, you know, asking people to read the fine print is\u2026 well, they won\u2019t. The visuals need to be more clear and self-evident. Hence the \u201cchains\u201d version that we played with for many months. Worked really well.

    I intended the \u201csymbols\u201d version to be a small iteration on the \u201cchains\u201d version. One problem with the \u201cchains\u201d version was that it wasn\u2019t clear that you\u2019d unlock chained slots one at a time. And we found ourselves adding hand drawn circles to unlocked slots since you might want to have some empty slots around. The \u201csymbols\u201d version fixed that by making sure every unlockable slot had its own prerequisite and that that prerequisite applied to one slot, also, you\u2019d mark it so you could empty it, refill it, empty it, refill it etc as you found and lost things. Also, in play, it had become clear that if your encumbrance levels are at 3, 6 and 9, there\u2019s no need to sweat the difference between 4, 5 and 6. Once you were over 3 you might as well go to 6. In terms of slots if not in terms of gear.

    I made the \u201csymbols\u201d version yesterday but this morning I was thinking there are actually very few circles. I\u2019ll just give you some more choices on how to spend your circles and then you don\u2019t have to go to sub-currencies of \u201ctriangles\u201d and \u201cstars\u201d and \u201cpentagons\u201d. Easier. And that\u2019s how this current version got made. The second most common reasons for people to swap is that they have a slightly awkward configuration of medium vs big, so I added a combo box, and the most common reason for people to swap is that they aren\u2019t happy with how much room there is in their backpack. So I made it easier to just put things there, no need to swap out. The entire \u201cchain\u201d idea was so disconnected, in hindsight.

    \nThe latest version is closer to the first version than any other version. Sometimes you just go on a side trail
