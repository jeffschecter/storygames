\n I'm getting ready for a game of Dogs in the Vineyard, and one thing that's really not clicking is how and when to use relationships.

* I understand that it makes sense to leave relationship dice unassigned, then assign them whenever you wish.

What are good moments for players to do this? What are cool examples of doing this? Any interesting tricks?

* Prepping a Town

Do you work in some of the PCs' relationships, like take an NPC and make them so-and-so's mother? How do you choose when to do this to best effect? Or do you let the players decide?

What happens after you've been through a few Towns and all the relationships have already been "used". How can you (or should you) justify bringing those relations into the story again?

(Revisiting the same town sometime later sounds like a fun possibility...)

* Relationships to places

What are these good for? Are they useless once you move on to a different town? How do you leverage these for fun play?

Finally, the book kind of suggests that relationships are Flags, telling you things the players want conflicts to be about. But it doesn't then outright say, "go and do X", where X might be "include each relationship in your Town", or "to frame a significant conflict, choose a PC's relationship to a place, and set the action there".

EDIT: The thing that jumps out at me is that the text does NOT tell you to do any of the above. The reason that "sticks out" to my eye is because the rest of the text is SO good at telling you exactly what to do, how to do it, and when to do for best effect. So, I'm not sure if the text is missing something or if I'm missing something.

So, how do you use relationships to their best effect in Dogs?

    \n For most games I present the players with a list of NPCs to choose relationships with and encourage them to leave plenty of NPC dice unassigned. I found the best time to assign NPC dice is during a conflict, especially with that NPC. If you are having a conflict with an NPC, you are likely invested in them. Worst situation were when players assigned NPC dice upon meeting with an NPC without "getting to know them" and then regretting it later. Although I try to increase the importance of an NPC when players assign dice to them.

    \nAnd personally, when prepping a town I mark certain of my NPCs as blood relations and let the players choose who before they enter the town. Like this: "OK, You are heading towards the Blackwater Hills Branch. One of you has a cousin in this town, and somebody else has an aunt. Who wants 'em?"

    \n We only ever play one-shots of Dogs - i.e. no campaign story arcs - so I like to use the Initiation scenes as a Prologue to the Town and I introduce the importants NPCs in those scenes.

    I find that by doing that I can give the Dogs some insight into the important NPCs - so that they don't just hit 'em cold - and thus give the players some hooks that they can build into their character conception. It's much cooler to play out the scene from the Relationship with the Steward that helped you to learn how to break a mustang, than to just write 'Relationship, Steward, Father, 2d4'.

    It also allows me to foreshadow stuff that is going to come up later on if need be.
