Anyhoo, I got this game for Christmas and I'd like to know something before I crack into it. Somewhere along the way, maybe at a con or something, I picked up an idea about Lacuna, that it's best if you play it the first time knowing absolutely nothing about it. I don't know if this is actually true, or, honestly, if I even heard it or not. But it this point, it's firmly lodged in my brain as "fact".

So that's the question, then. Is it true? Is it monumentally better to play Lacuna that first time in a blissful state of pre-knowledge?

And if so, given that my opportunities to play it first will be *severely* limited, should I just go ahead and read -- and subsequently attempt to run -- this storied story game?

Anything else I need to know? Seriously, I haven't even read any reviews for fear of spoilers...
