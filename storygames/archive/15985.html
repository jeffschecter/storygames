    \n Whatever system you use, if they love the blood'n'guts, give them gory and ridiculous critical hit descriptions. Overload everything with pure, sugary entertainment value, explosive domino effects, etc. Do this for one session, and then encourage them to narrate their own criticals. It will also loosen them up for the whole concept of trying crazy, imaginative things and generally kicking ass with their characters.

    Posted By: DeliveratorMy resistance to Wushu: I get that it's very player-empowering, but does it have GM-side mechanics? Scene framing rules, token economies for NPCs, Principles/Agenda/Moves, and the like? I can't really run games without those sorts of tools.
    \nYou should give it a read since it is a freebie and only like five pages long. It doesn't use the vocabulary that you mention. But, the rules of cinema apply here. You break the story down into acts and challenge the players with mooks/environmental/social encounters that have a threat level or a Nemesis which is similar to a PC in power and design. If you need more ideas/structure check out the Wushu Skidoo articles as well. (Wushu and freebies)

    Another good one that would stretch their wings would be Ghost/Echo. Another freebie that weighs in at only two pages. It has enough rules where preparedness pays off, but you have still have to make hard choices between success and safety.
