If you're like me you have (almost) as good a time making and finding game tricks, toys and custom game props and tools that make the game sessions easier and fun!

I'm looking for thoughts and PICTURES of tricks and tools that you've made, found, thought of making, or things you've found very useful (or modified) from things made by companies.

I'm talking about things you've made by hand. Things found in Value-Village and thrift stores, things found in hardware store sections, things found in kitchen supplies, office supply stores, used book store things, etc... etc...
Neat "found items" or other things that can be used double-duty for gaming fun!

Fun things that could inspire or be made by other GMs and groups, if possible. Suggestions for "how to guides" on making things that you like using for gaming fun! From battle-grid counters for NPCs, initiative keepers, condition keepers, battle-mats, miniatures, action/hero points, easy terrain, etc... etc... anything that is unique that you've used or seen used for game fun.

Things to help make a game toolbox/kit for things to help play be more fun or go smoothly...
Even your game table setup, back-pack materials, etc...
I've seen all the "ultimate game table" images and such - those are great, but NOT practical to anyone without the MONEY or place to put them... So I'm talking more about things you can make/find for cheap and ALSO tuck away under the couch or pack in a backpack easy!


I'll take some photos of things I use or have considered making and post later.
I'd like to see other people's "kits" for what they use to game with!! For inspiration on my own.

    \nMine fits right in with this...

    For Montsegur 1244, you're supposed to use a heretic figure (the book says use a penny, or whatever) to track which act you're playing in, and a Cathar cross to mark who initiated the current act (for this one you're supposed to use a yellow cathar cross printed out from the M1244 page; I couldn't find a cross on that page).

    Using a penny to mark something as evocative as the heretic figure seemed lame to me, and I got lucky when walking in my neighborhood: I stumbled across a bunch of free junk set outside someone's house. I rifled through it and found a ceramic monk figure, took him home, bashed off his head, and mounted it on a small piece of wood. This is going to be my heretic figure for the game:



    This is going to be my Cathar cross. It's not yellow, but it's roughly square, which the Cathar cross is and the Catholic &c. crosses aren't. It's also burnt (I took some wood, bashed it apart, nailed it together, and set it on fire), which fits nicely with the theme and mood of the game, I think:
