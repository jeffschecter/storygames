iago
November 2006 edited November 2006 in Directed Promotion
I was at MACE this past weekend, slinging around some promotional postcards on IPR's behalf. Gratifyingly, I saw several of them get examined closely and then tucked into folks' bags/binders/what-have-you.

I also had a few IPR-tangenting conversations with folks around the con which have echoed ones I've heard elsewhere. There seem to be two kinds of comments that are sticking out for me, one generic, one specific, and they go like this:

Generic: Variations on the theme of...
"Man, the only place I look for games these days is IPR."

Specific:
"I would totally buy __________, if only it was for sale on IPR."

________ so far includes me hearing at least one incidence of; my spotty memory suggests to me that it may be multiples on all of these:
  • Agon
  • Perfect
  • My Life With Master
  • 1001 Nights
So, story-gamers, tell me -- what other games might you buy, if only they were on sale at IPR?

EDIT: So, rather than deciding that I'm going for some sort of passive-aggressive peer-pressure angle on these games, please trust that I'm openly trying to gain insight into what products aren't reaching a portion of their potential audience. I am the sort of guy who sees something that works and genuinely wants to try to bring it to help other people out. Consider that I have recently worked with Chad Underkoffler talking about potentially getting his stuff on IPR; I'm doing this as a positive-minded, good-hearted advocate, and I'd appreciate not being assumed to be some sort of a dick absent of actual evidence of dickery in my intentions.
