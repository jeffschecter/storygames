\n So, there's a dynamic that comes about in all my games of Shock.

I've played in a single session two player game, a six player single session con game, and a game with ever-changing cast/players and multiple sessions.

Here's the dynamic:

People spend the first 1-2 scenes (of the game, not each) getting comfortable with the fact that their success doesn't hinge on the other's failure.

Then the system stops being a tool for opposition, and instead becomes a tool for approval. It's like fanmail, but fanmail as the base mechanic of the game, essentially.

If I make badass stakes, you spend your dice on d10s instead of d4s, because you want to see me succeed. The same happens if your stakes are cool. Minutiae become a way to collaboratively craft a scene, as well as give props.

The dice turn into a way of saying "this is a cool direction to take the story" and "I'm actually more keen to go in a different direction, dude". Even though there is dice rolling, it FEELS like freeform.

Anyways, I really like this. It is WAY more a social mechanic than a "hard" mechanic. I'm not sure if this makes sense to you, or if you've had this experience, but I really like it.
