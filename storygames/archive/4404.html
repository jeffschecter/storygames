\n In Solipsist, which I am busily working on, one of my key game concepts is struggling for the right name, and I'm looking for suggestions. I've already asked lots of people, including most of Collective Endeavour, but I can always use more ideas!

In Solipsist the characters struggle to balance consensus reality (what is outside them) with their own reality (which is the world they would vanish into if they let themselves go). If they start to lean towards their own reality they gain points of X, which measure how much their reality is tearing away from the rest of reality.

In the first draft these points were called Rifts, which I thought was perfect, because it sounded like a rip, and a gap, and made it feel as if the substance of reality was a surface which could be torn and could have gaps in it. However it was pointed out that Rifts was rather an in-use term

Next I went with Tears, which has a nice feel of something active ... but lots of people misread it, not Tears as in tearing paper, but Tears, as in things you cry! OOops!

So now I need yet another word, and not one that sounds rude (e.g. crack, crevasse, canyon) I've suggested Rips, Rents, Gaps .... at the moment Rent seems to be on top, but I still don't know if I am 100% happy with that ... but then I've not been 100% happy since I stopped using Rift!

Any suggestions?
