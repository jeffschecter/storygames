\n Just thought about how something as simple as this can be a game changer:

Combat ensues, the DM calls or the initiative roll. She knows in what side of the location she will place their monsters and where will the players be. But the exact locations are often left at whim of players or GM, even when based on the scene framing or player feedback. This small ruling is meant to give players who go first an opportunity to also get a small positioning advantage and also, give the other players something to do while they think of their tactical approach.

So basically, players roll initiative, then the one who rolled the last places their miniature (or miniatures if she's the DM). Next goes whoever rolled the next higher value, trying to get the best from the previous players positioning.

On a surprise round whoever got surprised places their minis first and then the other side places theirs in reverse initiative order.

Was this already somewhere in the DM manual or in this forum and I'm just remembering it now? Does it look like it could generate some toxic play? Has anybody played using something like this?

    \n Personally, I'd have them place the minis in initiative order rather than reverse, making things a bit more chaotic.

    If you do it with reverse order, the player who goes first gets to see the whole battle map, place his mini, and then go, effectively amounting to getting a free move in addition to their turn. The player who is going last, in addition to having to wait for everyone else to place their mini and take their turn is likely making a poor decision on where to stand, as the other side is going to be able to pick more optimal positions compared to them. Going last sucks enough as it is, and imo, placing your mini first is an additional drawback, not a perk.

    \n I agree with that.

    Picking last is better if there will still be a number of viable spaces to choose from, since you have more information when you place.

    Picking first is better if you are going to run out of good choices fairly quickly and are just going to be taking what's left by the end.

    Who you think deserves that advantage depends on if you are looking for an additional reward for rolling high, or compensation for going last. Both are reasonable.

    Now, I would still go with the guy with highest init picking first in either case. Partly because of the free move that I mentioned previously that placing and then taking the turn gives him, but also because it just makes sense to me that the guy acting purely on instinct has less of an awareness of the battlefield, but I could see an argument for either way depending on what you personally thing the init roll represents.

    I would likely give the option to choose if they place first or second to the side that successfully pulled off an ambush though, regardless of post-surprise round init rolls.


    Of course, another option entirely to resolve starting positions is old-school marching orders, with the GM placing the enemies based off whatever the game's particular agenda is.
