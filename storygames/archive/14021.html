    \n I ordered a few samples from MagCloud. They're okay, but nothing to get excited about. Photo print quality is great; however, the paper is very white, which doesn't look so good with text pages. Also, printed text gives the impression of being a little bit blurry. So if you're doing a fashion mag, cool; if you're doing a text-heavy gaming mag, it'll work, but it's not ideal.

    \n In contrast, I printed a game (Gun Thief) using Ka-Blam, which is a POD comic publisher.

    Things which I liked: they were super friendly, their price for low page counts is great, their turn-around time is great, the quality was generally great.
    Things which I didn't like: there was some color-streaking on the cover (which, to be fair, was an all-black cover).

    I'd highly recommend this format to others, should they be looking to publish a game bigger than a Scoutbook but smaller than the common 100-page 6x9.

    \n\nCalithena \n\n\n
    \n
    \n\nApril 2011 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n Hi all,
    \n
    \nI am happy to answer questions about lulu POD for Fight On!. I have some experience with Amazon Createspace as well. Lulu has more formatting options (createspace does not have 8.5 x 11, significantly) but if you pay $40 for Createspace Prime production costs are significantly lower there.
    \n
    \nWe are up to about 1000 copies of issue 1 in distribution, with the numbers out there gradually tailing down as one approaches the current issue (#11), but note that we keep our print prices very low because we are a fanzine. I make a little money off the PDFs but that is to some degree an accident - we price the PDFs high on purpose to encourage print purchases. We also use that money to run contests, like this one:
    \n
    \nhttp://story-games.com/forums/comments.php?DiscussionID=14030&page;=1#Item_7
    \n
    \nI think it is doable if you have a large-ish community of interest and good contributors who want to support that community, as we do. As a business model, I am not sure. If I charged twenty bucks per issue I could pay myself and the writers and artists something, but not so much, and I'm really not sure that even big supporters would pay that much. I do think that FO!'s essentially nonprofit model would work to get stuff out there for any 'hot' medium-sized RPG community, like the Forge was a few years ago say, or maybe around Exalted or something like that assuming IP stuff was kosher. But I am not sure how viable it is as an actual business unless you do printing and fulfillment yourself, etc., which brings costs way down, but then you are working a full-time job to make a graduate student/fast food type income, so I don't know.
    \n
    \nIf you have something you love and want to support it is fun though, if you like laying things out yourself anyway.
