\n A few weeks ago, Rafael Chandler allowed me to use his Disciple 12 rules (the one used in Dread : First Book of Pandemonium) to write a small heroic-fantasy-monster-bashing rpg.

I really enjoy playing Dread and I always thought the game's rules were perfect for some over the top Fantasy action.

That's how Gates & Gorgons came to be

What is it ?

A fantasy rpg with a solid set of rules that is easy to learn, teach and play. Oh wait, you don't have to teach it, go directly to the "play" part. If you really want a slogan we might call it a "traditionnal game with an Indie game feel" but that feels kinda stupid.

So what do you get ?
\n
    \n
  • Grumpy dwarves, overweight Halflings, shapeshifters, elves and ratmen walking their Path (Warrior, Thief or Wizard) in search of fortune and glory.
    • \n
    \n
  • Some Fantasy equipment with weapons, magical objects and, uh well, that's all you need right ?
    • \n
    \n
  • The soon to be critically acclaimed G&G Bloodbath MapTM for the miniature's minded.
    • \n
    \n
  • 19 Monsters ! Monsters talents ! Monster magic ! Everything to make the adventurer's life miserable and ease the burden of the GM.
\n
    \n
  • A scenario generator
    • \n
    \n
  • Optionnal rules for handling Relationships, mass Combat and Ships !
    • \n
    \n
  • And of course a character sheet
    • \n
\n"So what do I do with it?"

Read the rules, grab some friends, make some characters, use the scenario generator. You can play a Gates & Gorgons game in less time you need to say "4th edition" (well, 2 hours according to playtest, calling your friends and cooking time included).

It's all here !
