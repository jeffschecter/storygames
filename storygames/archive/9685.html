\nMonkeyDome is the Imagination Sweatshop\u2019s \u201cGame in a Jiffy\u201d for JiffyCon 2009. Last year it was Trial and Terror. This year it\u2019s MonkeyDome. This is becoming a bit of a habit for us. It\u2019s a \u201cGame in a Jiffy\u201d because it was created entirely from conception to publication during the week prior to the June 6th, 2009, JiffyCon where it was distributed for free. We have since corrected some typos and added a chapter that was originally left out due to time constraints, and now you can download it via the links above!
\n
\nWhat is MonkeyDome, you ask?
\n
\nMonkeyDome is a schizophrenic game of shifting tones and harsh lessons in the cruel world of the future where life is ruled by Grim Violence or Zany Action, and either may strike at any moment. Sometimes you just got to swing that shovel to see if you can end a man with a sickening crunch, or knock him out with \u201cDong!\u201d
\n
\nTogether you and your friends will be making Survivors who are pitted against the cr\xe8me-de-la-cr\xe8me of postapocalyptic horrors, cannibals, and marauding hordes for their space in this ravaged world. Survivors who\u2019ve learned harsh lessons and seen the worst life has to offer. Survivors who do whatever it takes to stay alive. Survivors occasionally played by Brendan Fraser.

    \n Making this game (and subsequently becoming obsessed with modding it) blew my mind and left a gaping exit wound in my paradigm.* And now I can't look at roleplaying the same way again, and I can't fucking shut-up about it. I love it when something done on a lark has that kind of an effect.





    *No guarantee that reading or even playing the game will have the same effect on you. Results may vary. Availability of paradigm-shifting experience subject to state and local laws. Void where prohibited. Offer expires December 31st, 2009.

    \n Working on this nearly broke me.

    I learned Indesign in a few hours earlier that week.

    Friday at 2am we finish writing.

    I lay out everything Friday from 2-9am.

    Then we go to the printer but as I am about to pass out Eppy and Jim call me because there are printing and editing problems.

    I make the changes and email them to the printer as time ticks away faster and faster.

    We get everything in on time.

    Later that same day we spent 5-6 hours traveling to Jiffycon.

    I can't wait to do it again! Seriously!
