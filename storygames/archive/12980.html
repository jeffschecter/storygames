\n As part of my latest project I'm committing to paper a technique I've been using for a while to introduce NPCs- ask a question of a player about the NPC. It's a good fit for the current project (Jianghu/wuxia/everybody knows everybody and has a history) but I like it as a general technique. Like many occasions when called upon to make a list, I seem to be struggling to remember all the good examples I thought of in the bath.

Here's what I have so far- please give me some more questions or suggest cunning revisions to the existing ones.

Why do you trust/distrust him?
Where have you met him before?
Why does he despise/admire you?
Why is his appearance good/bad news?
What reason does he have to want to kill you?
Why does his presence bring back unpleasant memories?
Why are you glad/unhappy to see him?
What secret does he know about you?
Why do you fear him?
How does his arrival make the quest easier/harder/more complicated?
What rumours have you heard about him?
Why are you now in extreme danger?

    \n [Tangent]

    Sometimes my players freak out when put on the spot, so I'd break down things like these into two parts:
    "You know he wants you dead."
    And then, maybe a scene later,
    "So - why *does* he want you dead, anyway?"
    After they've had some time to think about it.

    [And, back to your regularly scheduled thread]

    How about -

    "You've worked with him before"..."What did you do together?"
    "He's impressed you before"..."What did he do to earn your respect?"
    "You used to be close"..."How did you two know each other?" or "What happened that drove you apart?"

    That's a great starting list by the way, I think I'll copy it for my own games.

    \n I find that in groups larger then 4, it can sometimes get tedious to try to outdo the last person's answers (not that that's required, but it's only natural to want to do something great). For that reason, I'd suggest throwing in curve-ball options. Encourage the players to state why they love the NPC, or why the NPC loves them. Imagine the dynamic of being in a party/group/swaray mostly made up of people Dr. Mephisto hates... yet one member has always held a fondness for the mad coot and the two are old friends.

    Mephisto hates me because I destroyed his precious device
    Mephisto wants me dead because I killed his young love
    Mephisto is jealous of my awesome hair style
    Mephisto and I have been blood brothers since our youth. I'm was going to be the best man at his wedding to the now dead young love.

    Awesome.

    - Frank Hablawi

    \n One I used in a game once was:

    "What favor did you get from him that you still owe him for?"

    It was an Amber quiz question and so anything could have been the subject of the devil's deal. Got some neat stuff going because each player thought they were the only one who got that question. When the man's name was dropped, they all caught a breath, noticed each other doing the same, then they all started worrying and scheming.

    \n I like this stuff a lot. One game that does it real well is Apocalypse World. I think the GM has a general rule to ask questions like this, but they are also built into chargen in the form of the History rules. In our last game, the Gunlugger had as one of his History choices to decide which one of the other characters left him bleeding. He chose me, and narrated how he was bleeding out on the ground in front of my workshop and I just closed the flaps and left him there. Then Colin as GM turns to me and asks me "why'd you leave him bleeding?" That set me back on my heels for a sec, until I looked at my own history rules for my Savvy-Head, which has me reduce my history stat with one PC because "I got better things to do." So, I left him bleeding cause he wasn't any of my concern and I had more important things to do. Worked for me.
