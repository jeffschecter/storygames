\n So, thanks to the recent discussion about turning SotC in a better pickup game and general awesomeness of system and the fans here, I'm now seriously itching to run a game with it. And because I long for the familiar charm of the "future" and some of the players don't know Shadowrun yet (!!!11!one!!!), I decided it's time for Spirit of the Sixth Age.

Now, chances are, somebody has already thought of that and I fully expect a complete conversion to turn up two minutes after posting that, even though a quick google search didn't turn up anything except an episode of 2d6 feet in a random direction (Which I'm listening to now.), so don't disappoint me. ^_^

But, anyway, in the unlikely case that the internet has not already produced what I want to read now, here are a couple musings by a SotC noob:

For a generic Shadowrun gam, I'd probably have the following phases in character creation:

Background: Basic Stuff, where do you come from, and so on. Not much change there.
The Fall/The Shadows: Nobody is born a Shadowrunner and there's a story there. Where you born in the gutter and started crawling upwards? Did you start out in the glittering castles of AAA and then fell? Got backstabbed by a coworker? Framed for a crime you didn't commit? Whatever it is, this phase explains why you started running.
The Run people heard about: In Shadowrun, reputation might not be everything, but it sure is important. So here it's all about the one run people remember you for. What happened? Was it your first one, showing you as a natural through huge success? Did you totally fuck it up and now nobody wants to work with you now?
Followers: This is again the basic Guest Role part. Think up situations where you met and worked with/for/against/next to one of the other characters in their Big Runs. Maybe you where the Security Guard he shot in the ass, maybe you where the one that drove him home afterwards or whatever.

Now, the rules should easily cover most of the stuff in Shadowrun, especially with a couple of the other conversion to steal liberally from. (Like some stunt for Mysteries to make ranged attacks for magicians, ...) Maybe have some "Cyber" Skill that covers cyber-repair, hacking and so on? Dunno, not sure.

Anybody tried that yet? Any comments or ideas?

    \n Well, moving SR over to SotC would require some fundamental changes in focus. The most obvious would be that the technical specifications of cyberware would become pretty moot. The gadget rules already undermine a techie-specific focus, much to the chagrin of the gearhead(s) in my own play group. So that'd be something to pay attention to. Personally, I would LOVE to move SR away from gear and tech specs, so that's a-okay by me.

    So, yeah, I'd say Gear = Aspect (or stunt, if you're using the Gadget rules from SotC and need a multifaceted piece of Gear. I'm not sure how I'd differentiate it). Each thing or integrated system would be an Aspect. I would also tightly restrict this, again to force focus back on characters and not on gear. Some additional thoughts: Out of your 10 total traits, I'd say no more than 3x Gear (unless you open up more of them with a Stunt, but that creates kind of a chicken-and-egg problem during character creation), at least 2x Aspects tied to other characters in your crew, a minimum 1x Aspect related to your character's relationship with magic, maybe 1x Aspect related to your character's race if non-human, etc. Not to tie them all up or anything; just giving character creation some shape.

    Second, I'm pretty sure five stunts would be totally fine for a SR character...assuming the stunts are broad in their application. Also, there's that change in focus thing again -- instead of modeling everything a Runner can do, you're modeling the things the runner can do that actually matter to the story at hand. In my game at least, the stunts would be fundamental expressions of the character as well.

    \n This borders on what I call "Fast FATE" - where you make a pickup game out of any old rpg you have on your shelf by doing the following steps:

    1. Go through the five phases of chargen (Background, Time of Trouble, Novel, Guest star, Guest star).
    2. Players pick one Aspect for each phase, while the gm examines the Novels for villians and adventure hooks and makes appropriate notes.
    3. Players select 10 skills from the source rpg's skills/abilities to make a skill pyramid (One skill at +4, two at +3, etc.). Be sure to include Might and Resolve from SOTC if the source rpg doesn't have the equivalent. Non-selected skills should default to +0 for regular skills and -2 for skills deemed 'technical' like surgery or flying helicopters.
    4. Game on! using SOTC rules ignoring Stunts and Stress Tracks (go straight to Consequences).

    Faster! Furiouser! Funner!

    \n Well, let's see. As a noob it's hard to get a feel for how broad or narrow a Stunt is "supposed to be", but here are a couple of "groups" I could think of:

    Magic: In Shadowrun, there are a couple of things most mges are supposed to be able to do, the biggest of which is Astral Perception, ie. seeing spirits and other magical critters, reading the aura of people to determine their emotinal state, health, whether somebody has magical talent or cyberware implanted. Some are also able to Astrally Project, ie. seperate their soul from their body to go take a look around. Some can cast spells (Of which a mage can typically learn a bunch) and/or summon spirits. And then there are Adepts, who use their magic to kick ass physically and who work differently again.

    Now, I could see a basic "Mage" package that covers Astral Perception (Which I could see working a bit like a combination of Psychic and Hand Reading, ie. use Mysteris/Magic instead of Empathy or Investigation in specific circumstances.) and allows one to get other magical Stunts. From there on, I could see a further Stunt for Astral Projection, some sort of summonable Companion Stunt to cover summoned Spirits and Elementals and so on.

    That still leaves me with the biggest Headache: Spells. They generally can do many different things, from frying people to reading their minds or create water. Damage spells are easy enough (Using Mystery instead of Guns) as are various defense spells (Mysteries instead of Athletics/Weapon/Fists), but how to put that into stunts without completely overpowering things OR making the things so narrow that one can only make half a mage?

    And Cyberware... Puh. But I actually think many Aspects/Skills work quite nicely there. Enhanced Strength? Superb Might+a couple of those stunts and so on.
