\n I've had some requests to post about this, so here it goes: what I can remember of the Dungeon World game I never thought I'd play.

My best man had been plotting something for a while, but when we left for the strip club with 24 beers for 4 people (I never knew there were B.Y.O.B. strip clubs) I thought that was it. Turns out that wasn't it. After many beers and lap dances (and my groomsmen using the phrase "knock him around" way too much) they pulled me over to a table and revealed the surprise: a copy of Dungeon World Basic (the Red Book), all the latest character sheets, and 2d6. They grabbed some dollar bills and asked a stripper to play with us.

Luckily she turned out to be pretty cool. I apologized for my nerdy friends, she said it was cool, all her friends played magic, and we got down to it.

Characters were assigned pretty randomly and we didn't do any setup: we assigned stats on the fly, you had the first option for each gear choice, and all other choices were made on the fly. Two of my groomsmen took the Cleric and Wizard to make sure we didn't have to teach spells to a new person, Sarah (the stripper) took the Thief, and we were off (I think some of my other friends took classes as well, maybe.)

In an effort to use as few dollar bills as possible, and hazily remembering something in the book about starting with impending action, I told them this:

"You've managed to infiltrate the goblin caves thus far unnoticed, but the ritual continues apace. Four goblins stand around the summoning circle, chanting their guttural goblin language. You have the drop on them as you peek around a bend in the passageway leading to the ritual cave. What do you do?"

(Or at least that's what I thought in my head. Given the amount of bare flesh and beer that night, it was probably more like "There's a summoning circle and on it are some\u2026 goblins! They're summoning\u2026 something! Something bad! What do you do about it?")

Sarah took the lead, led the charge, and before long the goblins were dead. After a backstab took out the first one they made an attack but a the players acted as a team, covered each others' backs, and before long it was done. The goblins were dead, but the weak point between the planes remained. We still had a few minutes left in the song, so Sarah was sticking around. She asked what she could do about the summoning circle and I asked what race she was. She looked at her choices, decided on an elf, and that gave me an opportunity: "Oh, an elf? This is old elven magic here, it looks like they were reaching out to the Green Realms. You have elven blood, you might have some influence on it\u2026"

(In the original Drunk: "You're an elf? They're all nature-y and stuff. And this is a portal to a crazy plant plane! So yeah, you've got some influence on it.")

A hasty ritual and some danger defied they were in control of the portal. The song ended and our Strip Club Heroes headed off to their planar adventures, likely never to be seen again. (Until someone else's Bachelor's Party, I guess).
