\n On a previous thread we all went on about how awesome Car Wars was. I just got my box set eBay purchase and I'm psyched. Except that now I remember there are some things about Car Wars that aren't so awesome. I'm wondering what a really great Car Wars game would look like, and I'm pretty sure it's not the game I played in 7th grade.

Things that I found awesome about Car Wars:
- Big rigs duking it out with wolf packs of armored motorcycles
- Spectacular crashes
- There was a pre-made solo adventure booklet they published about a road trip across the southwestern US that I really enjoyed
- A Mad Max road trip campaign that I played in where there were meaningful tactical decisions and resource management

Things I found not awesome about Car Wars:
- Most of the arena battles I fought
- Spending an hour of game time doing nothing but accelerating towards the fight
- Multiple failed attempts at a mega-size arena battle
- Multiple attempts at an arena battle campaign or league

Some ideas that spring to the top of my mind:
- Say yes or face the arena (or the highway)
- Might be a good game for ensemble play (particularly owing to its high death toll on characters)
- Recently watched a reality TV/documentary about Ice Road Truckers in northern Canada that would make a cool Car Wars campaign

    \n Arena battles were pretty fun for us when we played, but I think part of that is because we had more time and patience back then for undirected mayhem.

    What I really found fun the few times we did it were track races (I think it was called 'Dueltrack'), where the rules were such that you had to get around the track a few laps to win. You were still sniping at each other, but you were also jockeying for position and trying to outrun the other guys. Fun.

    By extension, I think a cross-country rally race would be fun, with layovers for repair/refueling allowing for some drama between the teams.

    This is all just situation-based stuff, of course, without any major modification to the rules or play procedures. I'm sure there are plenty of things you could do there to ramp up the fun too, but nothing comes to mind right now.

    \n We never realized that Car Wars WASN'T an RPG. We totally RPG'd it the entire time we played...with a GM and missions and adventures and everything.
    \n
    \nMy main character was Red Ryan named after the Han Solo equivalent from a Star Wars knockoff novel I'd read...although he looked more like King Smoit than Han Solo. My gunner was Jack Fox...I didn't create him until after I'd worked my way up to a car big enough to need a Gunner.
    \n
    \nI started out in the cheapy league...the one where they give you a car for free and if you suvive you get to keep it. Worked my way up through the arena league until I was renown enough to start getting jobs for road missions...deliver this package, escort this person, clean out that bike gang. By then I had 2 cars...my tricked out ueber arena car of death...and my optimized for road work long range cruiser of death.
    \n
    \nEventually we ran through the Solo Adventure "Convoy"...which we ran as a regular adventure with a GM...cuz...we had one, so why not. I started up my own trucking company New Wave Van Lines and hired on most of the NPCs from Convoy to be my employees. I had an awesome rig with a external sound system in the shape of a giant boom box mounted on it. The rig was painted violet and neon green and we'd roar through town pumping out 80s pop hits at 1000dbs. I think the NPC truck driver's name was Templeton Smythe...I'd ride escort in my car. Later we got the gas powered metal armored car rules and I had a Pick Up truck named Death Blossom (heh). It had some obscene number of those six shooter rocket packs mounted on it which IIRC you could fire one at a time or all at once. I basically could do enough damage in a single shot to obliterate any vehicle on the road...then I was done save for a measy MG or 2. But with a Gas Engine I could accelerate right up on some dude, shrugging off most damage with my metal composite armor...and nail him at point blank range. It was a one trick pony, but in a convoy...I dropped the hammer on lots of baddies with it.
    \n
    \nBy the time we were done I had a fully armored warehouse defended with VMG towers and AT gun turrets. A 10 wheet truck with autocannons in the turret and a ramplate, a cargo helicopter escorted by a gunship, a little grasshopper car/copter which could be carried in the back of the rig for special surprises...skills so high I could basically turn my car in a series of 90d slalom turns at 100 mph without wrecking and shoot the tires off of a motorcycle from across the board...and so many millions in cash that I was buying hydrofoil boats to race as a hobby. Oh yeah...Dragon Magazine published Tank and APC rules for Car Wars...yeah...I had me one of them 8 wheeled APCs...it could steamroller just about anything...
    \n
    \nWe must have played that campaign off and on for 5+ years. I bet if I pulled the box set off my shelf I still have the blue print of the warehouse and my drawing of the truck...
    \n
    \nI was awesome...

    \n Most awesome Car Wars thing I ever saw was a guy at Dragon*Con who ran a cool game of it. This was a guy who had several diorama sections of highway and some painted up matchboxes with some mods that ran a game of

    SPY HUNTER!

    for anyone who came up to his table in open gaming. One player at a time, controlling the spy hunter car until it died. As you ran down the road, he would remove the last board piece, randomize the "population" of the upcoming board, and lay it down to create a constantly rolling highway.     \n Car Wars is fun, especially when RP elements creep into the mix. We did a big arena battle last night - 10 vehicles altogether - and to spice it up for myself I added some totally extraneous motivation and a meta-narrative conceit for my driver.

    He's been searching for his brother's killer for months now, and has narrowed down his leads. One of the other duelists in this match has to be the one. All he has to do is watch for the sign - left to be determined after the fact - that certain signature move, or the Ace of Spades card they keep on the dashboard, or the distinctive racing uniform the driver wears.

    Rule 1) If my driver wins the match, all other drivers taken out, then the last driver he takes out is the killer. He'll run them down without mercy. Vengeance is satisfied!

    Rule 2) If my driver gets killed, then the car that takes him out is driven by his brother's killer. He only realizes this at the last minute. Tragedy!

    It was great fun, and I found it informed some of the tactical decisions I made in the larger game.

    Hm. John, how about:

    \n
      \n
    • Your rival dies: +1
      • \n
    • You kill (actually kill, not just participate in the damage, and he can't have run away) your rival: +1
      • \n
    • You die: -3
      • \n
    • Your friend kills his rival after you damaged the jerk: +2
      • \n
    • Your friend kills his rival: +1
      • \n
    • You kill your friend's rival: +1
      • \n
    \n

    Total points per team first. That determines the winning team. Then rank that team to discover who came in 2nd or 3rd or whatever.

    \n

    These absolutely stack. So if I help my friend kill his rival right after I damaged him, I get 3 points for that and my friend gets 1.

    \n

    Rivals are not symmetrical. My rival is the Humungus. The Humungus' rival is Stumpy Pete, who's on my team.

    \n

    These numbers might not be quite right. The biggest benefit is supposed to be for team play: damage your friend's rivals, then let them take the kill. But you want to be the last guy to damage the rival in question before your friend kills that rival, so you might go over and deprive him of those points, hogging them for yourself to the detriment of the team.

    \n

    Myself, I generally prefer the "Tuners with machine guns" aesthetic. But they can be rival gangs with sleek R-Types, too. Everyone doesn't have to be in bondage gear.
