    Sound Professionals make a good T-stick single point stero mic:

    http://www.soundprofessionals.com/cgi-bin/gold/item/SP-SPSM-13

    Sony also has a model like this for about 20 bucks less, but with cheaper components.

    I use one of these on my minidisc to get ambiences and meetings. I recommend stereo because it will capture a wide range of sources and then let you use the natural panorama of the sound to help determine one source from another.

    This may be more prosumer than you have in mind.
