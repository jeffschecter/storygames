\n When you have an item and you think it should help you wish success, tell the GM that you're going to use it. Roll with advantage. If either d20 comes up a 1, however, the item is "used up": worn out, in need of a recharge, played out, etc. It won't bring you any further benefit until you take action to restock or repair it.

This counts for more abstract items, too, like a notarized document from the constable. You can wave it around and make life easier, but eventually it's going to wear thin. Roll a 1 and people are going to stop caring about it.

    \n In a similar vein, THE BLACK HACK has a usage die mechanic.

    Whenever you use an expendable item, roll its usage die. If it comes up a 1 or 2, reduce the size of the usage die. If you roll a 1 or 2 on a d4, the item is spent.

    This prevents you from having to track torches and rations and arrows and stuff. I realize it's a little different than what you're aiming for.
