\nAn email, on Friday: "I'm going to run Amber, mother***kers. Amber, if Zelazny had published each chapter as a serial in a pulp magazine and needed to pulp it up to get it to sell. Amber inspired by the books, not emulating the DRPG.

This is an Amber with ray guns, planes that flap their own wings, clockwork-driven trump machines, a steam-driven monstrosity called Morgenstern, and growing fleets of zeppelins with Unicorn and Silver Rose emblems on the side.

Saturday: a chargen party and barbecue. I remained bemused that I'm running Amber again after all this time.

Next weekend: Nine Princes in Pulp

    \n"Camel Cigarettes and the fresh taste of Colgate's tooth-whitening Paste are proud to present our heroes in... The Moon Bridge of Tir na-Nog'th!"

    Session One is up here: random average: Nine Princes in Pulp: Session One, which is a blog and commentable (hint).

    The blog-post version also has a link to the wiki version, which is a bit better formatted, I think.

    Wiki Stuff Here: http://random.average-bear.com/FATE/NinePrincesInPulp

    The short version:

    1. People jumping from Zeppelin tops onto floating clockwork Ghostwheel.
    2. People leaping off Ghostwheel into open space and figuring out how they'll survive NEXT round, because there's MORE IMPORTANT things to do now.
    3. Moonriders attacking at night, over the Tir, on GIANT AIRBORNE MANTAS AND ORNITHOPTERS.
    4. Tanks of hydrogen used as missile weapons.
    5. Planes brought down with small arms fire -- QUICKLY.

    Pattern probability manipulation used to Declare scene aspects...

    Engineering used as an attack skill...

    ...

    I mean... Pattern use, Sorcery, Trumping, gunplay, zeppelins, explosions, and impossible feats of physical daring, over the ghostly city in the sky... that's "Pulp Amber" right there.

    And every round went faster, and smoother, and was full of fun.

    What. the. HELL is not to love?
