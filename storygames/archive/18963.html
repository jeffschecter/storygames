Hi all,

You may have heard about \u2018replays\u2019 before. You may have read the one in the back of the Fiasco rulebook or Ewen\u2019s \u2018The Broken Window\u2019
for Golden Sky Stories.

These are transcripts of actual games designed both to convey what the game is like and to entertain.

When I started out with story games a couple of years ago, my biggest question was \u2018okay, I can read the rulebook but how does it actually work?\u2019 I found the replay in Fiasco really helpful and wanted there to be more of them. Since the beginning of the year, I set myself the project to invite players, play a series of games and then write up the transcripts and then release them, first as pdfs and then (hopefully) as free ebooks.

Now that the games are all played and the transcripts nearly completed, I\u2019ll be starting to release them on a period basis (one every week) starting in a couple of weeks. Right now, however, I\u2019d like to share with this community the first of the set. It\u2019s the game Love in the Time of Sei\xf0 (not at all coincidentally the first story game I ever played) and the replay is called Norse Crown.

I do hope you read and enjoy.
http://epistolaryrichard.wordpress.com/2013/10/31/story-game-replay-love-in-the-time-of-seid-norse-crown/

These replays include not only what was said by the players, but also some notes by the players as to their thinking at the time. This is intended to help beginners understand what dots the players are connecting when they introduce new elements to the story. If members of this community think that first-time readers would benefit from further annotations or commentary on what\u2019s going on then - or if you have any other thoughts about this project - do please suggest them to me.

Richard

    Y'know, I'd theoretically love to do this, but I'm afeared that I won't be able to record everything enough to get all the important details down.
    \nAs mentioned by others, I recorded them all on a voice recorder. Yeah it takes a lot if time to write up (I'm still working on the 5 hour Forsooth game) but you can do as Jason did for Fiasco and transcribe the key moments while summarising the less critical scenes.

    I figure that there's no harm recording most games (where the surroundings suit) as you can always overwrite the ones that aren't so memorable.
