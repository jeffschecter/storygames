    \nHouses of the Blooded is merely vaguely gesturing into that direction. Unfortunately I'm not aware of any truly good roleplaying games of this nature. As long as e.g. King of Dragon Pass is better than any rpg contenders, there's little point to playing the latter.

    This is, of course, a fine reason to create such a game. D&D does it, sort of, as you mention, but it's a distant and optional end-game thing.

    \n Oh yeah, it definitely can be done! I've no doubt of that at all. It's just that, well, I can't name a single game that would be actually acceptable on the matter. The one that is perhaps the most explicitly "about" domain management is Birthright, and that unfortunately sucks donkey balls in the exact same ways all AD&D material tends to - doesn't beat King of Dragon Pass, which I apparently just now decided is the measuring stick in this matter.

    It's a good topic, I could easily see e.g. myself looking into it at some point. In fact, I'm sure that somebody comes soon and tells us about some small press game in development that does exactly this.
