\n So after Snyder leaked the very cool cover he designed for his 44 ashcan, I thought I'd follow through with a peek at what I'm doing for the Acts of Evil ashcan.

The books are tape bound, 94 pages, with matte black cardstock covers. And I'm splatter painting them, and embossing the titling by hand.

Here's a scan of my prototype:



That's two colors of green acrylic paint, done as a fairly controlled splatter via a secret method (yep, loading paint to a toothbrush and scraping the bristles with a popsicle stick), and pewter colored embossing.

That scan doesn't do it justice. Here are a couple of detail shots:



The color accuracy isn't perfect on this one either. You can really get lost in the green on the actual thing if you're not careful.

Paul

    \n Chris, yep. I dunno what it's called, but I think of it as "reverse stencilling."

    The embossing is done by applying an adherent to the paper (which I'm doing with a rubber stamp, but they make pens you can draw onto the paper with), sprinkling the embossing powder onto it, tapping away the excess, and then applying heat. The embossing powder melts.

    You know how embossed text feels on a business card? That's the same stuff.

    Paul
