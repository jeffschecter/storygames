DRYH Conversion - Magical Girls

I want to call this adaptation Destroy Everything You Touch.

So, the idea here is primarily to build a 'teamwork' overlay for DRYH...and add sailor outfits.

We change the terms and economies around a bit... (I'm still not really grokking Hope and Despair, so this is about the dice traits only)

Discipline

One character is the leader of the group. We call her the Princess of Power, and she has special mechanics. Her Discipline dice are white. Her Discipline has normal behavior.

The other characters in her magical girl team each have a special colour for their Discipline. This reflects their 'totem colour', which is repeated in their special attacks, outfit, and so on. A team-mate can choose between two things when her Discipline dominates; either eliminate one of her own Distress (below) dice, or remove a different character's Distress die, and replace it with a Discipline die in her own colour. As a result, a character can carry several colours of Discipline dice.

If you roll a Discipline die that's not your own colour, discard it after the roll. You don't have to roll these off-colour dice; you can just keep them around if you like.

(The tuxedo guy has something to do with Discipline and failure. But what?)

Distress

Distress is analogous to Exhaustion. It represents the psychological weight of mundane concerns. When Distress breaks its cap, the character must immerse herself in normal life for some time; she doesn't have the inner fire that allows her to transform and use her powers, and her Discipline drops to 1.

Love

Love is analogous to Madness. It is linked to some relationship that's part of the character's bedrock; it's important to her. When Love dominates, it damages the relationship somehow (though not necessarily by hurting either character); when it runs out of responses, the relationship is dissolved permanently.

Love is a bad term for this.

    I'm chewing on the dice loaning part, and I love the concept. I especially like the use of color as something that really reinforces the theme. That said, I think the question regarding the leader _feels_ like it should tie into the dice sharing. The original mechanic is designed to be a steadily ratcheting tension which might be mitigated, but which always threatens to snap.
