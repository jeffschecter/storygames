    Playing a character based on the Arch Deluxe or the McAfrica would be hilarious.

    Also, re: kinsey-- when we played it, we used a d6. If you rolled a 6, you had to determine your own place on the kinsey meter, and then choose the functional opposite. Unless you were a 3. Then you claimed to have no sexual interest in anyone, because it would demean the person you were interested in to be viewed as a sex object.

    I also heard of a "hipster variant" in which you use a d6: 1-2 is 2, 3-4 is 3, 5-6 is 4. This keeps anyone from being able to claim they are "Totally straight" or "Super gay."

    \n God, we just got back from our first full session of Balsamic Fist. Fantastic stuff.

    I'll post a full play report later. Mr Teapot, I didn't know those rules for the ninth amendment. For Ed McMervyn's kicker, we interpreted that amendment as "rights that are not specifically enumerated".

    So, as he was grinding pepper, his pepper grinder broke, and then he found another pepper grinder, but that was broken too. And he yelled "Jesus! Don't I have a right to ground black pepper?". It was a great moment, really illustrating that the middle classes have real problems too.

    Jason, I'm using the ashcan version Kory Curtis sold to me at a games convention. He'd just released Limbo And Libido, the first supplement to Limbo Fever, and kindly asked me to playtest this game. It's got a different cover from the one you posted. If anyone has this original version, and can scan the cover, I'd be grateful.
