    \n I've been thinking about how to do "wings" and "murder" in a way that doesn't smell like angels and demons. So...

    You are these guys...



    You have really pissed off this tyrant...



    Fortunately, you've run into this moron...



    I want the game to be about how you manipulate (and assist) the moron into murdering the tyrant for you without being discovered doing so (and without opening yourself up to exploitation by other kingdoms). Importantly, all the while, the moron has to think the whole thing is his idea.

    In this case, the moron is an NPC, as is the tyrant. The players are the hawkmen. (Maybe, still thinking about it.) I'm interested in any game that tries to do something similar.

    \n I wonder if you could adapt the explicit argumentation mechanics from Burning Wheel's Duel of Wits into something more... nefarious. Perhaps replacing the explicit argument moves with successfully planting "clues" for the moron to find, creating encounters to alter his intentions? You would keep a method for tracking someone's will regarding a situation (previously Body of Argument) and a neat mechanic for various sides to manipulate the targeted moron (previously the audience).

    I don't recall the rules very well, but AD&D had a mechanic for sufficiently-magical artifacts to influence the characters who wielded them. It appears that this has survived into v3.5, at least somewhat: http://www.dandwiki.com/wiki/SRD:Intelligent_Items#Item_Ego, but it seems to be reduced to an saving throw. It may be only my memory that made it more grand in the first edition.

    \n Obviously, whatever system you pick, you're going to need a tight focus on who your moron is and what he wants, so that you'll have enough interesting handles to manipulate him with. It's a con artist setup, basically: the more you know about your mark and the stronger your mark's desires, the easier it is to get him to sell himself on your scheme.

    That suggests some kind of FATE game to me, where said moron has a bunch of Aspects that you can compel directly or appeal to via clever arrangement of the situation (declarations, assessments, etc.).


    Edit: also, I understand the Leverage RPG is good for capers and cons (I haven't read it yet, but I'm dying to); you might want to check it out, too.
