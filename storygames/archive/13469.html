\n
\n
\n It\u2019s that time of year again. Snow in the air, Christmas music on the radio, and the aroma of great gaming just over the horizon! Dreamation 2011 will be held at the Morristown Hyatt in Morristown, NJ from Thursday, February 24 to Sunday, February 27, 2011. Will you be there? Do you dare to have that much fun?
\n
\nThere will be an Indie Games Explosion at Dreamation. Dozens and dozens of the most blisteringly awesome independent games will be run at the con. Plus, all attendees have a shot at a coveted starring role in the sure-to-be Internet smash \u201cOne Cool Thing I Saw At Dreamation 2011.\u201d
\n
\nHowever, the Explosion needs your help! We need you to come to the con and play games. We need you to come to the con and RUN games! Here\u2019s how:
\n
\nCome and play
\nIf you just want to come to Morristown and have a blast, here\u2019s how you can help make the Explosion great:
\n
\n1) Go to the registration page and pre-register for the con.
\n2) Watch this thread for announcements of what events people are planning to run.
\n3) When the official schedule is up, around February 14-16, register for the games you want to play.
\n4) Come to Morristown and bring your A-game!
\n
\nCome and run
\nThe nitroglycerin at the heart of every Indie Games Explosion are all the great folks who come out with a game in hand, ready to rock the house. If you\u2019ve got a game session in your soul, burning to get out, here\u2019s how you can help:
\n
\n1) Post your event description here, or on your blog, or wherever the kids are chattering these days. Get people excited about your game.
\n2) Send an e-mail to the IGE gmail account: indiegamesexplosion At that point, Kat will send you a Google document that contains all the information we need from you in order to officially register the event. Fill it out and return it BEFORE January 14, 2011.
\n3) If you run at least three events, you will receive a GM badge to the convention. At some point in January, we will send you a code you can use on the registration page.
\n4) When the official schedule is up, around February 14-16, register for the games you want to play.
\n5) Come to Morristown and revel!
\n
\nA Note on Small Events
\nGames sessions for 4 players are a bit of a sticky situation. On the one hand, they take up an entire table for an entire gaming slot while only entertaining a few people. On the other hand, some of the best indie games are only built for 4 and create less fun for everybody if expanded. It\u2019s a pickle.
\n
\nHere\u2019s my proposal for this year. I\u2019m going to ask you to consider running sessions for 5 or 6 players. By \u201cconsider,\u201d I don\u2019t mean \u201cyou must\u201d or \u201cyou should\u201d or \u201cyou will be shunned unless.\u201d I simply mean \u201cthink about it.\u201d Step away from the Internet for a moment, look at your game shelf, and ask yourself if you have a 5 or 6 player game you\u2019ve been wanting to run. Can you stretch your abilities in that direction? Do you want to? Can you run Game A for 6 players in addition to running Game B for 4 players?
\n
\nI can\u2019t answer those questions. I\u2019d just like you to answer them for yourself and your own situation. If the answer is \u201cI want to run a bunch of 4 player games,\u201d then that\u2019s fine and we want you on-board for the Explosion.
\n
\nPlaytests
\nDreamation is a great place for games-in-development to get put through their paces. Not only is it easy to get people you don\u2019t know to sign up for the game you\u2019re working on, but some of the sharpest game designers will be there looking for new games to test.
\n
\nWe\u2019ve tried a number of different things in the past. We\u2019re going back to the 2009 rules. If your game isn\u2019t done, but you think it is at a point where it can probably entertain people for 3 to 4 hours, then register it as a regular event. Just please include the word \u201cPlaytest\u201d in your event description.
\n
\nIf your game isn\u2019t done, and you don\u2019t know whether it works at all, it\u2019s probably not ready to run as a regular event. However, don\u2019t lose hope! Post about it here and see if people are interested. It\u2019s easy to arrange an informal playtest session at Dreamation, where a bunch of people meet in the lobby or a hotel room and give your game a workout.
\n
\nAssorted Other Stuff
\nWe usually hold an Indie Games Roundtable on Sunday to discuss games in development and other matters. Attendance at the Roundtable seemed down last year. Is it still worthwhile? Would a different time work better? How can it be better? Is there anyone willing to facilitate it?
\n
\nIn years past, we used to hold an Indie Games Party on Saturday night. It was a lot of fun, but as the community has grown, so has the workload of running the party. If anyone is up to the job, let me know and we\u2019ll do what we can to help out.
\n
\nI\u2019m posting this on Story Games, but there\u2019s probably other places on the Internet that might want to get involved. If you know of such a place, please invite them to participate. The more, the merrier.
\n
\nNone of the above is set in stone. If you\u2019ve got suggestions, I want to hear them. Kat and I will be doing our best to monitor these threads for questions and the like. However, if we haven\u2019t responded in timely fashion, please e-mail the indiegamesexplosion address with a link to the question. Thanks for your help.
\n
\nSee you in February!

    \n Brian, the Morristown stop on the NJ Transit train* is about an 8-minute walk from the con hotel. Take a right out of the station parking lot, walk to the big triangle intersection, take the left up the hill, then take your first right and go one short block (past a sloping entrance road to a parking garage or something). Turn right onto the very long street and walk until you see the Hyatt on your right. (This route may seem circuitous with the near U-turn at the top of the hill, but is actually much faster and easier than the directions the hotel will give you.)

    *1-hr ride from Penn Station, ~$12 IIRC

    \n
    \nYou are a group of ordinary people who are in an extraordinary circumstance. You are stranded in a remote location with no way of contacting the outside world. Your resources are limited and it\u2019s clear that time is against you. How will you survive? You can pool resources to try to stave off the inevitable.
