Hi all. We are organizing a game jam in the Seattle area. So first of all, stay tuned for info if you are in the area and want to participate.

And second of all, we would be happy (and eager) to recieve any helpful hints. None of the folks organizing this have ever done this kind of thing before (I don\u2019t think...).

We are a playtest group that works on gmLess and gmed games. Mostly one shots or short arcs.

Our goal is to lower the bar for entry into the world of game design for people who might be interested but haven\u2019t gotten over the hump and tried yet.

One thing we are looking for is a list of game mechanisms etc. we are hoping to provide something like a toolkit, or maybe a heap of spare parts that people can use to get started in the game design process.

Any other helpful hints or materials are also more than welcome.

We are all ears!

Thanks very much,
Davey.

    Sounds like an interesting idea--an RPG design game jam. One obvious category is dice mechanics: pools, d20+stat, etc. Another might be various other deciders like cards or jenga towers. There are things you can give people to get the started but...

    I think the general idea of a game jam is to see what the participants come up with, rather than tie them down to given mechanics. The experience comes from the collision of all those mechanics rather than the mechanics themselves.

    Maybe if you want to attract true newbies to the RPG design game, you could provide a super free-form, rules-lite system. Then ask them to hack it about: add mechanics for what they'd like the game to be. They could twist it and chop it up and do whatever they like with it--even scrap the core mechanic in favour of something else. But with that tiny seed, it may give participants something to build on, avoiding the "blank paper" creative block phase?
