A Random Encounter

Christian_Griffen
June 2006 edited June 2006 in Story Games
Last week on the train during my commute to work, I was (as usual) scribbling notes on my notepad about various RP design things. It so happened that I was writing things about a fantasy heartbreaker idea, which were more recognizable as RP elements than most of my other scribbles. So the guy next to me--a big guy with shades who looked like the quintessential biker--asked me if I'm designing an RPG. Turns out he was a DM for 15 years before going into the service. Now he's not playing anymore.

Why? He hated the amount of work he had to put in as a DM. Now he plays Everquest. No preparation, just log in and play. DMing was too much trouble and not enough reward.

I found this interesting as a random data point regarding RPGs and what drives people to and from them.
