I wanted to throw an offer out there for those guest users to feel free to join these forums.

Unlike other forums, this Vanilla forum software is different. There may be a few benefits you're not aware of:

* You can change your default forum style to one of over 10 views. Find a style that fits your reading style.
* About half of the discussion topics and posts aren't available for viewing unless you have an account and are signed in: Stuff like game design, focused game discussion, forum info, blogspace discussion, etc.
* There's a live chat feature available upon login
* Jason Morningstar provides back massages to all new members
* There's community projects from time to time to be involved in

That's about it. Feel free to remain a Guest, if you just want to peek. If you wish to participate or just read and lurk, sign on up! (update: As usual, please allow 12-24 hours for approval, although usually someone will spot your application and approve you much faster than that)

Just wanted to throw that out to any guest users who are on the fence in regards to signing up.

NOTE: You have to click on a "Tems of Agreement" (the standard faire) link for the site when registering: When you click on the link, the Terms of Agreement open in a new window. Some people report problems due to the fact that it's a popup window. Please bear through it (use Internet Explorer if you need to). Thanks!

-Andy

    February 2008 edited February 2008
    Will do.

    For the time being: There's "Closed" instead of "Locked". I don't think there are many Closed threads, that's mainly for announcements and stuff. There's maybe 2-3 where I say "OK, calm down everyone, Do Not Post X"... and some retard citizen posts X well after that time (so it's not just cross-posting). Those threads where tensions are high, Internets Drama is rampaging, etc. Those I close.

    Sinks are usually for fun threads that grow to big, and wear themselves out. "Sink" makes it so that you can't "Bump" the thread, but can still post to it if you're really interested.

    They're also for threads where folks step over some line that was drawn, and it looks like nothing good will further come out of it. I don't like Closing/Locking threads, because those threads are usually like faucets, draining the bile from folks. So I just make it so that no matter how passionately folks rant or post on them, they won't bump back on to the top, and are destined to be forgotten. If by some crazy miracle they get back on track, I'll unsink it, but usually at that point it's too late. It's like "Closed", but without that abrupt, unfair pause that says "I Have Spoken! No One Shall Post!" I dunno, it's softer IMO.

    -Andy
