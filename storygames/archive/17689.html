The Coven has made its decision. The use of magic in public is punishable by death - there are no exceptions. Those who would risk exposing the community of magic-users must be dealt with swiftly. Jack Tennyson, Madeleine Khaw, Alexander Dabila, and Marcus Fax must die. The problem? Other than having an order out for their deaths, the four of them have no idea who the Coven are or any clue that there are others out there with magical abilities. Unfortunately for them, none of that matters to the Finders who have been sent to carry out the Coven\u2019s decree. So it is with a grim fascination that the four have found themselves bound to chairs in an abandoned warehouse as they await their executions. Their captors will return any moment. What will they do?
-Introduction from The Legerdemain Betrayers


Hey fellow StoryGamers,

You can check out The Legerdemain Betrayers at http://spiffup.org/betrayers It is currently on sale for $2.99 for the first week. It is a 42 page, full-color, PDF, immediate download.

Thanks for your time,
-Daniel

    So, I thought it might be cool to go into some fun facts in regards to the game: I got my start in gaming in more story-focused, homebrew freeform games. The original version of The Legerdemain Betrayers is actually a Harry Potter-themed online game of that sort. It was about the Ministry of Magic going after this small group of wizards and witches who had banded together thinking they were the only people in the world with magical abilities. Another funny thing about that is the game used a system the group helped to created called "Wands". The core concept is still in there - outsiders to a magic-using community on the run - but the game is much different now. Not to mention the mechanics are completely different.

    Speaking of the mechanics, the game's playing card-based system has evolved quite a bit from the way it originally worked. My first game release, God-king, actually started out as an exercise in trying to improve upon the system used in Betrayers'. That little offshoot project turned into something completely different when the concept of God-king kind of organically formed from the mechanics - the system used in Betrayers is very different from the one in God-king. In terms of creation, God-king is chronologically the second game I have created.

    In the end, the system used in The Legerdemain Betrayers was inspired by card magic, Magic: The Gathering (of course), and, interestingly enough, fighting games. One of the core mechanics in Betrayers was directly inspired by a common mechanic in fighting games where you gain the ability to do special moves when you build up your "special meter" by successfully performing normal moves. The opposite mechanic is also in the game - an Ultra move that is built up by taking hits - in Betrayers this happens when you fail conflicts.

    None of this is strictly relevant to enjoying the game, but hopefully you all might enjoy some tidbits of Betrayers' design history. I am very happy to finally get this concept out there as it got its start way back in 2008 and has seen quite a lot of changes since that time.

    Thanks for listening to me ramble a bit!
    -Daniel

    \n@Mike_Sands: Glad you feel that way! I was hoping that the book's setting and characters oozed with a bit of coolness. I would love to know what you think after giving it a go.

    @snej: Thank you for the kind comment. Book design is actually something I wish I could do more often so these games are a great outlet for that purpose. I definitely agree that having something that's nice to look at helps to enhance the experience.

    Also, thanks for finding those errors! I have just sent out an email to everyone who has ordered the game with information about how to obtain the corrected PDF.

    As for the worsening side effects that result from the overuse of magic: yes the Narrator just keeps track of how many times a character has used his or her magical abilities and then ups the ante. The more severe effects are mainly meant to affect those who perform magic multiple times within a short time span.
