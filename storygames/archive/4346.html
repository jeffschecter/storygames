\n
\n It's times like these where you have to ask yourself: "Isn't it about time I punched Tories in the face with clockwork gauntlets?"

The Sons of Liberty beta playtest is on, and runs through October 31st. I'm looking for brave souls to try a night of madcap clockpunk revolutionary action, and a few even braver souls to try their hand at the game's multi-session Campaign Mode.

Ask yourself:
Do you have what it takes to answer Paul Revere's call to arms?
Do you have what it takes to set sail with Captain Fanny Campbell?
Do you have what it takes to don your power armor alongside Alexander Hamilton?
Do you have what it takes to get in the back seat of Abigail Adams' horseless carriage?

If you feel the siren song of Liberty, download the playtest edition now.

Keep in mind: this is no bare-bones rules summary full of holes and questions. This is a 106-page booklet with art by Jake Richmond and Anna Kreider. It provides rules for all three ways to play the game: Battle Mode, Campaign Mode, and Versus Mode. It details seventeen Patriots and twenty-three Tories. It has mechanical horses, clockwork cyborgs, and solar-powered frickin' laser beams.

It details a game that is impossible to play without shouting.

It very well be more anachronistic heroics and madcap fun than you're able to handle.

If you can bear to open yourself to this assault of gonzo revisionist-history insanity, today is your opportunity. Join the playtest; fight the Tory; and win the Revolutionary War all over again... with style.
