\n Here's the transcript of a real internet conversation. Josh has played d20 (Arcana Unearthed and D&D) and lots of 7th Sea. He's thinking of writing his own setting as he's writing a novel but he'll also be gaming in it.

Stoughton:
Dude, you HAVE TO play some of these indie games - or for god's sake read them - otherwise you won't see how much the indie games have moved the social "technology" of RPGs forward. These mechanics are for YOU.
Josh:
Oh yeah? Like what?

Obviously I answered Josh, but what should I have said?

    It's too easy - it's such a geek thing - to set yourself up to dislike something. And it's easy to look for flaws in how the mechanics aren't just for you. Bypass all that and just offer unspecific enthusiasm. And rely on the social glue to get a game going rather than selling points in a game.
