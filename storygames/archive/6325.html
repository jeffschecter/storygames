\n So in Medical Hospital you've got situations that are player authored. I need some help coming up with the best possible examples. Here's the text right now:\n
Situations should be concise but open-ended, and \u2013 most importantly \u2013 end with an exclamation point! Each will be revisited over the course of a session, so these really set the tone of your game. Tense, dramatic situations? Silly, over the top ones? Talk about where you want the session to go. There\u2019s no reason you can\u2019t vary the tone from session to session.

Perhaps you are angling for a game stuffed with personal drama and romance:

My lover can\u2019t find out!
Lost letter!
Betrayal!

Maybe it\u2019s time for action and adventure:

Hostages in the ER!
Wired to a time bomb!
We stay here, we die!

What about absurd mayhem:

Rats - everywhere!
The ghost of Trauma-3!
Clown College mass casualty incident!

Or gritty hospital drama:

We\u2019re contaminated - nobody leaves!
We can\u2019t afford to save her!
It\u2019s not my job!
\nSo what I need form you, Story Games, are better examples than the ones I've come up with. You've seen Scrubs and Casualty, so help me out with some compelling, punchy, exciting situations that fit the above categories in five words or less. Go! Best suggestions receive free balloon angioplasty.

    \nPerhaps you are angling for a game stuffed with personal drama and romance:

    -My mentor has a drug addiction and is using hospital meds to supply it
    (My mentor has an addiction)
    -My sister has an incurable cancer and can't afford pain medication
    (Sister can't afford pain meds)
    -There's an unconscious patient waiting for surgery who knows a secret that could ruin my carreer
    (Unconsious patient knows my secret!)

    Maybe it\u2019s time for action and adventure:

    -Someone tripped the "Level 4 pathogen" containment system, we have 6 hours to get out before the hospital is automatically "sterilized"
    (Level 4 Containmnet System Activated!!!)
    -A turff war between gangs errupts in the hospital. Seven members of one gang are trying to finish off a drive-by from earlier that day
    (Gang war in the ER!)
    -The FBI shows up and says there's a group of terrorists hidden somewhere in the building
    (FBI locks down the hospital)

    What about absurd mayhem:

    -A batch of medicine was impropperly formulated at a pharmicutical plant and turned several patients into homicidal maniaces
    (Medicine turns patients homicidal!)
    -An earthquake hits the city and all the floors are jumbled up. The doctors need to rescue the most critical patients before the aftershocks hit
    (An earthquake shreds the building!)
    -A group of diseased monkies escape from the research lab and are wreaking havoc in the rest of the hospital
    (Diseased monkies escape research lab!)

    Or gritty hospital drama:

    -The spouse I'm about to divorce is brought in after a horrendous car wreck that will probably leave him/her paralyzed for life
    (X-spouse needs lifesaving surgery)
    -Budget cutbacks will force the supervisor to fire the doctor with the worst patient survival record unless someone volunteers to go
    (Budget cuts! One must go.)
    -You meet the man/woman of your dreams...who has an excruciating terminal illness
    (Terminal patient is my soulmate)

    Hope those (revised ideas) are helpful, Jason!

    \n From the emotional standpoint, harder situations are those that are close to home. Of course having a spouse or kid with a terrible disease is devastating to anybody, doctors included. What is often a bit more specific to doctors is dealing with patients in difficult situations that are of similar age of himself (herself), or of his spouse or kids. A child with cancer, a teenager with HIV, a young adult with an unusual but incurable disease.

    Too close to home!
    This could have been me! (Or, my wife, daughter, etc...).

    It is very hard too when you do everything for a patient who ends having a bad outcome and either you second guess yourself if you could have done a better job or the family makes unjustified accusations.

    It was all my fault!
    What have you done?!

    It is very hard sometimes when the family or a family member stays distant during the course of the patient and jumps in the middle or towards the end without understanding how much hard work has been put in trying to figure things out or treat a difficult disease and try to question everything that has been done and the quality of the people and the treatment or even question how much you care about the patient.

    Bring a real doctor!
    You are killing her!
    Can you please leave now?!
