\n A friend of mine sent me this text:\n
Nathan and I have been talking. We'd like to play a rpg run by you, in which the players are: Me, Nathan & Josh. The premise is that Josh is an immortal monk who is always right. Nathan & I are cheap replacable thugs who ruthlessly protect Joshua's life.
-El Kierro
\nThis might be a bit of a joke, but I don't game that much with these guys so I might get them to play it. So my question is: What game do we play?

I need something VERY rules-light. And Nathan would usually rather draw than play a game, and all of us are musicians to one degree or another. I was thinking it might encourage them to play if there was at least some drawing involved. I could say they only have the equipment they can draw, and if they sing a song about whatever their character is doing, they get some extra dice. Or something? Does that sound feasible?

    \n There is only one game for this, and that game is Under the Bed. Why? Because the esteemable Ben Lehman has specifically written this game as a mod for UtB. It goes by the name of Journeying West and allows you to play the story of the immortal monk Xu\xe1nz\xe0ng on his journey to retrieve the holy Tripitaka writs of Buddhism for the education of the poor Chinese people. On this journey Tripitaka is accompanied by foul-mouthed, replacable thugs, such as Monkey King and the ever-lovely Pigsy. Monk Xu\xe1nz\xe0ng is, of course, always right, but also not able to directly protect himself except in exceptional circumstances.

    Hmm... after writing that, it seems obvious to me that Johnstone is pulling my leg. What are the odds? I mean, Ben actually sells this thing, or at least used to. I guess it's remotely possible that your friends just happen to like classical Chinese literature, too.
