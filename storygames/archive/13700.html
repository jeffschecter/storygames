\n Interesting find in this Winter Comiket's wave of interesting indie-made RPGs (there are other cool ones of note, but this one caught my "weird meter" attention for obvious reasons):



I know little about this game, other than that it's called "Families", and it's a game about housewives and househusbands. But instead of like Maid where their role is just trapping for bizarre hijinx/adventures, it appears to be a "honobono"/"feelgood" game of simple people stories. Sorta close to "Yotsuba&" or "Azumanga Daioh". You get some kind of a random "mission" which you have to overcome within five days.

There's nothing in English, but there's a bit of data:

    \nHmmm. I used Google, then Excite.jp, but both seem pretty spot on (be it gramattically hilarious) that the "problems" are totally all mundane.

    In order from Top Scenario to Bottom:

    * The yearly town Cherry Blossom Viewing festival just can't get off the ground due to lack of interest. Fix!
    * A member of the family disappears, find them! There is no pre-set reason for this or solution , it could be anything. But the "bad ending" scenario is "their body is found"
    * A cliff wall ("Miyuki Cliff") near town is eroding. Do something! (there are notes that this is an abnormal scenario)
    * There's a gloomy girl. Go find out what is up with her! (text says that the reasons are normal/banal/'serious', that there is no ESP, Magic, conspiracies, etc)
    * There's an abandoned lot with lots of cats. The cats are spreading garbage and breaking things. Fix!

    that sort of thing.

    \nI seem to recall seeing that name, although nothing really specific about it. I've got no clue what Fiasco is... I honestly haven't really been in game buying mode for a while now, just focused on personal projects and enjoying what I've got.

    I'm guessing it's a tough sort of thing to come up with, regardless of whether it's a game or anime. For example, I just finished watchine NieA under 7 and while I dig what was done with Lain, they (for me) dropped the ball with NieA. It seemed like they were going for an Azumanga sort of thing, then it looked like there was a point and then it just sorta...ended. The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya seems to sorta be a blend of going somewhere while having an awful lot of apparent pointlessness and "slice of life" stuff.

    I dunno. For me it's sorta hard to articulate, but I can point to Azumanga and say, "I'd like to see a game that lets me tell and participate in stories like that." Panty Explosion might be offered up as a way of doing Melancholy of Haruhi (I don't know, maybe it wouldn't be offered up), but it seems to be geared more towards something different than that Azumanga-ish sort of thing which I also think is in Melancholy.

    \n Raspberry Heaven is the name of the ending theme of the anime adaptation of Azumanga Daioh, and I felt a nice summation of what I wanted my game to ultimately be about.

    Well, Fiasco has been getting a lot of attention, and it gets a big thumbs-up from me (I've made four playsets for it after all), but it's certain mechanical elements (especially the setup) that's inspiring me rather than its setting or tone (which derives from Coen Brothers movies).

    Azumanga Daioh is a definitive work in what has become something of a genre of its own: 4-panel[1] slice of life manga about Japanese schoolgirls. There are a good number of others, like Sunshine Sketch, Lucky Star, Sketchbook, GA, K-On!, Uraban!, etc. Writing my own slice of life 4-panel webcomic, I can tell you it's not easy, and I'm not surprised to see stuff like NieA_7 sometimes have trouble pulling it off.

    [1]4-panel or yon-koma manga is basically the Japanese version of newspaper comic strips. It's probably not a coincidence that the cover of Families has four pictures of characters arranged vertically. Sazae-san was originally a 4-panel manga, and its anime adaptation is like the Japanese version of the Simpsons, if the Simpsons had run for 40+ years so far instead of 20+.
