    \n I'll be there Thursday afternoon and leave after the roundtable.

    I have no roommate for Thursday night, by the way. I do have roommates for Friday and Saturday nights. If someone wants a bed for Thursday and is willing to split the hotel cost with me, whisper me.

    I am hoping "Indie by Storm" will be all the rage this year. It should be a blast for the players! If you want to try out some indie games, swing by my table at 8pm Thursday or 2pm Friday and Saturday. I will accommodate as many people as possible and Roach and carry both tolerate a lot of players...

    My first choices for events to play in are:
    * D&D 4E, Living Forgotten Realms, "For Crown and Kingdom" (Friday 9am)
    * Swords Without Master, "The Curse of the Fire Apes" (Friday 8pm)
    * Luke's secret bargaining game experiment (Friday midnight)
    * Ticket to Ride (Saturday midnight)
    * Pandemic (Sunday 10am)
    * Indie Roundtable (Sunday 2pm)

    I'm pretty proud of my "balanced" game schedule! Some D&D, a new indie game I haven't played before (SwM), a weird game experiment, two board games (Ticket and Pandemic), a discussion panel (the Roundtable), plus I'm running three slots of games. I also left Saturday morning totally open (up late Friday) and gave myself an extra hour on Sunday (10am start, and it's a board game slot that I don't mind blowing off if I'm really dragging).

    Also note that I have all of Saturday night wide open. I'd like to find some "pick up" gaming there. If anyone has any awesome offers, let me know. I'm also willing to run a game in that 8pm-midnight slot. I could run full sessions of any of the games on my "Indie by Storm" list. I could run D&D Metrocalypse: the Shakespeare and Elizabeth version. I could play some board games with people. I could show people Solis' "Happy Birthday, Robot," which is 45-minutes quick and plays well with alcohol. Make offers! Corner me at con and make more offers!

    Also, people, please say hi. And pardon me if I peek at your badge. I have a terrible memory for names. I don't mind an interruption for a quick "hello" even if I'm GMing or playing (can't have a conversation, though, obviously). Really, say hi!
