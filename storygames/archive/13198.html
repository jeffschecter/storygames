    \n OK, I'm thinking of trying this out for something very much inspired by Moorcock's Eternal Champions:

    - Each week would be a different sphere of the Multiverse, with the group using the mechanic to show if it where in thrall to chaos or law (or poss in balance), adding facts as appropriate.
    - Players would rotate the roles of Eternal Champion, Companion, Villain and Doomed Lover (each with a alignment related story-agenda).
    - At the end of the session each Player and Sphere's fate would be sealed and would be set to Chaos, Balance or Law.

    \n I could see something like this being used in Dawn of Worlds, where the players are gods creating a fantasy world. At the start of each turn, they roll two dice to accumulate points that can be spent of various effects. The two sides could then be opposing alignments, so you may encounter situations where you really need a higher die roll, but that requires you to break your principles and go with the "evil" dice. With a few tweaks to the main rules, these alignments could be made to matter. For example, certain actions might only be available to you if you have more points in "good" than in "evil".

    \n I have played Dawn of Worlds. It never has any conflict between good and evil. Everyone seems to want to play evil. It just seems easier to get at a gamist ideal when you have no concern for morality. I think that it would require more to make Dawn of Worlds fun than adding this mechanic. Harnessing this mechanic to force decisions between good and evil in a way that meant something would be great, but I don't see Dawn of Worlds having anything to support a real decision at this point.
