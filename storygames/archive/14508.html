\n A friend has started a new Facebook group called #LoveARG.

He describes it thus:
"This is group for those who would be interested in making the best Love ARG in the world. We will aim to make a game that is about the beauty of the world and other people, based on bringing people together in a world of love.

This will not be for the typical geeky ARG players and will mainly be based on physical media.

Join in if you think you can bring something to the mix. We look forward to getting to know you better."

This seems to be very much in the same area as Jeepform, but also looks to the kind of intimacy that you get in Breaking the Ice. I thought some Story Gamers might be interested in participating.

    I want to point out something before we get too far into the woods here:

    \n
    \n

    This seems to be very much in the same area as Jeepform, but also looks to the kind of intimacy that you get in Breaking the Ice. I thought some Story Gamers might be interested in participating.

    \n
    \n

    Why? And I mean that literally? Where would you possibly get the impression of any of that? Certainly not on the FB page, which is as devoid of content as the box I keep morality in. Not in the thread, since neither Graham nor GB have actually said anything other than "Wouldn't it be cool if ...?" and even failed to put anything after the ellipses. There's literally nothing over there except ... Oh, wait, they actually added some text:

    \n
    \n

    This is group for those who would be interested in making the best love game in the world. We will aim to make a game that is about the beauty of the world and other people, based on bringing people together in a world of love.

    \n
    \n

    >

    \n
    \n

    Through a series of puzzle and clues, two people will be brought together through a journey of physical objects and each others' histories to a conclusion, where only their combined history can make sense of the result.

    \n
    \n

    Its like the output of some demented Markov generator, trained only on ground-up hippie, and I say that as an unrepentant "hippie-game gamer." Aside from treating the phrases "Jeepform" and "Breaking the Ice" like jargon and peppering the line with an SEO's single-minded-but-actual-knowledge-free intensity, how does that even make any sense?

    \n

    I love Story Games as a community, not the least reason because the vast bulk of the time there's a hard-nosed practicality of "give us something to look at, then we'll talk" in a lot of the 'new idea' threads, which is just as it should be. You need to bring something to the community that makes people actually want to get involved and get interested beyond moon-eyed fawning. But there's none of that. Someone gets to be That Guy(tm) who points out its not there; I guess its my turn in the barrel.

    \n

    Dudes, there's nothing to care about here. If you want us to care, if you want us to be engaged, if you want to elicit the interest and feedback from folks who aren't sipping the Kool-Aid with pinkie in the air, you've got to actually have something to elicit feedback about. Start there. Come back when you've got something worth looking at (and given the folks who hang out here, we'll settle for even a half-page outline of how you envision play proceeding, target audience, and writing style; we're not greedy).

    \n

    I feel like the guy at the bank who says, "Sorry, your loan application is not going through because your business plan is crocked." But our time (well, my time, anyway) is valuable. I can be thinking about game design ideas with some meat rather than distracted by this.

    \n

    Come back when you have some meat. If you ever have some meat.
