\n I played four games of Zombie Cinema in three days last weekend. Here's a tweak to the rules we used in one of them:

In the first round of scenes, the zombie marker starts off the board. No conflicts can occur in the first round. At the beginning of the second round, place the zombie marker on the zombie start place. Play proceeds normally.

--Paul

    \n You only pass the round marker during ties, when the zombie moves. Otherwise it stays where it is, so it might end up being kept by the starting player for the whole game.

    The early version of the rules of the game didn't have the round marker moving at all, but that caused the zombies to be a bit too brutal - you'd get these situations where the zombies move two steps at once when the last player of the round manages to get a tie in a conflict on his turn. Moving the marker around means that the zombie will only move on its own accord after a full round of not moving.
