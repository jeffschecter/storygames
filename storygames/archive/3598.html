\n In Bone White, Blood Red, there are six bead colors, and each one means something.

Well, actually, they mean a lot of somethings.

At the May Ides of Gaming, where BWBR was playtested, it became obvious pretty quickly that having Spider Grandmother read off a long list of words wasn't going to work.

So, instead: These.



















Do these work for you at conveying the meaning of each color of bead, without having read any sort of long explanation as to what they mean?

    \n These are a really cool idea. I think it would be cool to keep these around as inspiration, even if you didn't need the reminder. They're a good snapshot of what the game is about, and also I think a good way of getting into the mindset of the game.

    Are the font sizes relevant? (Aside from the main title). I'm not sure, from looking at them, but it would be cool to have the major points of each colour be in the larger fonts. Something like "Coin", for example, which covers two concepts (Money and Church) that aren't neccisarily linked in people's minds, would benefit from having those seperate concepts stand out. At the moment you've got "wealth" "tithe" and "Crusifix", and I wonder if you shouldn't have "Church" "Greed" and maybe "Wealth" or "Holiness" instead. I guess the idea is to be able to glance at the things to get a sense of what they cover, so having the kind of extreme poles stand out might make them quicker to read.
