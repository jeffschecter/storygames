\n Once upon a time, I read a blog post about how "missing isn't fun", that touted the proponents of always knowing you would hit, or on a larger scale, knowing ahead of time that your character would succeed, and turning the question of the story to "what did it cost you to get there?" and "what questions are caused by success?" There were little diagrams with comic-style arrows, and an overall well-worded essay on the topic, and I desperately want to find it again, but my google-fu has repeatedly failed on countless attempts.

I'm fairly confident that it was authored by a story game designer, but I could be wrong.

Does anyone know what I'm talking about? Do you recognize the post I'm thinking of? Maybe this is a needle-in-a-haystack, but I've had a half-dozen attempts to find it when a discussion I was privvy to broached the subject, and it really bothers me that I can't track it down.
