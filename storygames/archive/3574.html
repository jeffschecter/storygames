Posted By: Ryan MacklinThis reminds me of the for-YouTube (and similar services) commercial that I keep dreaming about directing for Dogs: two people in period clothing & Coats getting into an argument, going through the stages of escalation, and ending with one drawing down on the other, who looks back in fear. In between each escalation is a quick cut to a black screen with white lettering saying "Escalate", in ever-increasing font. It would end with a cut to black again, saying "Dogs in the Vineyard" and some tagline.

I'm probably never going to get around to making it, nor the one for Don't Rest Your Head, Spirit of the Century, Truth & Justice, or the other indie games I love so much. But I keep having neat ideas for 20-30 second internet video spots. While this isn't exactly what we're talking about it, it's in a similar "get their attention quickly and get them interested in learning more" space.
\nSee, this sounds to me like an awesome thing to do some random time slot at GenCon. Seriously: my stupid midrange digital camera can take video. I can dump that file into my laptop. iMovie is a fantastic editing program, and free. The only problem being, I'm not going to GenCon.

But that's no reason that other folks couldn't do this. Seriously: what do you need, a couple cheap-ass costumes that can double as cosplay for a day, an empty room and/or park area, and maybe a rolled-up backdrop if you got really energetic about it? This is easily within reach for indiefolk, and would only really require three or four designers getting together and 'starring' in each other's videos.
