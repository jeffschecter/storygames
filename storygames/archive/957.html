[TSoY] Getting the game

Doyce
July 2006 edited July 2006 in Actual Play
So here we go: been very very thin on gaming for the last year, and I get together four other folks who want to (or are willing to) spend a night with a game none of us have really played, but which we want to learn. I've read TSoY over a half dozen times -- it's time to put that sucker on the table and really get it.

Enter TSoY. Enter John Harper's "Freebooters" scenario, with four pregen characters.

The Awesome
Gull Trecher, fighting the witch of Rope Hill, Bloodies and Breaks her, but is himself Broken and out of Vigor points. Player looks the situation over, gives to the witch's goal of "witchify him up with LOVE" and refuels his vigor pool with the hot magical monkey rubble sex that ensues. Awesome. Player takes the loss in the fight, buys off Key of Bloodlust to buy Key of Masochist and Key of Renown, then goes and brags up the whole thing to a group of guys who'll try to kick his ass for consorting with a witch. This player hadn't never even HEARD of the game before we started play. He rocked it... got something like 4 or 5 advances in the course of 3.5 hours of play.

Wester's player using Secret of Contacts to establish that he was the long-departed lover the big bad voodoo mama, ending the evening not with a big fight, but makeup sex.

Canter's player using the macguffin to 'unlock' the binding spells on the girls in the brothel. Key of Conscience, baby.

Ending the night with no rules-questions, not from myself or anyone else. Personally checking the rules twice the whole night, and looking at the one-page 'conflict cheat sheet' not at all. High contact, low downtime.

---

The System

Intuitive, Smooth, Crunchy, Game-y, Story-driving, Bad Ass. Clinton made the fantasy game I wish I'd always played.

    I have to agree. We just wrapped up a two-shot, and I never felt so good. Per my blog:

    Shadow of Yesterday has been really really fun. Shadow of Yesterday gives me what few game exeriences ever have: a sense of constant direction for my character. It's a really neat process:

    1. Scene is framed.
    2. I ask myself, "Should I be in this scene?"
    3. I look at my character sheet. Are there any keys or secrets that fit, are there any characters that I want to become involved in, or are there any secrets or keys that I want to work towards in-game continuity for collecting?
    4. If yes, I add myself to the scene and start using abilities, getting XP, etc. That's immediate gratification that I love.
    5. If no, then I sit back, watch other spotlight coolness, and throw some chips in when it gets good.

    Also, when I want to have a scene framed around me, I can ask for it after a quick look, using nearly the exact same technique, but I also add any Keys I bought off, to see if I can riff of some roleplay of a changed perspective in the character.

    Is it time for "TSOY rocks this much" stories?

    Last night my guy, an arrogant young sorcerer, approached another player character, the ruler of Feng Du, the city we're hanging out in. He's in mourning because he just killed his uncle in a duel, and he's a mess, and he's peace-bound his sword with a Key of the Vow. And I say "Hey, you know that bad-ass geomancer who has been vexing us since the campaign began? Yao the Unclean? He's coming to your uncle's funeral, and I think I can take him out for good. I want your permission, because there will be some ... collateral damage." And what the hell, right? He's grieving. He hates himself. He waves me off with a "I won't stop you."

    Well, I've got the Key of the Mission related to this Yao guy, so I'm ready to do anything to ruin him. I've also go the Key of Unrequited Love for my brother, who hates all sorcerers, including me, but who hates Yao the most. So I figure it's a win-win, right? And I get busy. Turns out I have to make a few deals, a few promises, and basically sell my soul and the entire city of Feng Du to this snake demon lady from the Hell of Chains. No big deal, you have to break a few eggs to make lemonade, right?

    Chaos ensues at the funeral, the sky rains iron, the dead rise, the earth cracks open. My magic succeeds way too well. And the ruler of Feng Du (played by Steve) prays like he's never prayed before, and throws down and wins, protecting his family and the sanctity of his uncle's corpse. But he can do no more, and has to watch helplessly, with his peace-bound sword, as the forces of darkness rip his beloved home town a new one.

    Perhaps best of all, there's this girl named Wei-Wei, right? She's my sister. She's my brother's sister (another player character), too, and he's got the Key of the Guardian for her. And the final player has the Key of Love for her. So who do the demons go straight for and drag off to the Hell of Chains? Aw, yeah.

    Was the XP flying? Yes it was. Did I take out Yao the Unclean? Yes I did. I expected a medal for my heroic efforts at eliminating a hated nemesis, but what I got was a lost sister, a punch in the head, and a trip to prison. Awesome!
