\nRecent threads have gotten me inspired. Let's play a game based on the first three books of classic Traveller!

Here's what I'm thinking:\n
    \n
  • This will be basically, "Let the dice fall as they may, and then we try to come up with the coolest explanation for what those rolls mean in the fiction. (Here I'm thinking particularly about the quirky, scientifically dubious world generation system.)
    • \n
  • You need access to a copy of classic Traveller little black books 1, 2, and 3. Nothing else will be considered part of "the rules." You are still welcome to mine other gaming material, astrophysics texts, and science fictions for creative inspiration.
    • \n
  • Author stance, to the extent that the rules allow it. There will be little to no opportunity for "role-playing" your character. Also, I'm thinking no secrets between players.
    • \n
  • If you roll up your character and he dies during chargen, or he just sucks, you still have to post him, and must wait 24 hours of real time before making the next one. This is part of the game!
    • \n
  • I have NO IDEA what sort of campaign this will be until everyone has a character and pieced together the most interesting setup. Mercenary adventurers? Desperate merchants? Scout surveying? Who knows! (No big ship-to-ship combats, though. See the next point.)
    • \n
  • There are certain areas potential areas of Traveller I have no interest in dealing with here. If you want to gearhead out a sweet-ass spaceship, goodbye. If you want to futz with calculating delta-V to perform an orbital slingshot, goodbye. If you're wondering how they eat and breathe and other science stuff, goodbye. (Wait. Actually, figuring out how eight billion people on a medieval vacuum world manage to survive is exactly the sort of fun I'm hoping for.)
    • \n
  • The action should hopefully revolve around moving from place to place on the map. Because it's, you know... Traveller, not Guys Stuck on a Planet.
    • \n
  • We'll slyly transmogrify Traveller's skill system into a conflict resolution system. Mostly, for the sake of keeping back-and-forth exchanges to a minimum.
    • \n
  • I'm not quite sure where we'll do this. I don't know if Story Games will work. I guess if necessary I'll make a place for it.
    • \n
  • I'll be, um, "referee". I'll be freely delegating GM-tasks freeform style and kibbitzing about world-building decisions.
    • \n
  • I foresee something like this: When your character arrives at a world for the first time, I break down the UWP code into plain English, and then we chat about what sort of planet this might be. After we agree that it's "awesome," you do your stuff on that planet and head off the next place.
    • \n
\nNow possibly this will end up a disaster of expecting emergent story. And the result might not even be much of a "Role-Playing Game" at all. But I think it will at least be a fun exercise in collaborative world-building. And no great time commitment will be expected of anyone.

I have already rolled up a starting subsector. By hand.

Who's in?

    \n I'm super flexible about rate of posting. We'll rely on the tried-and-true technique of nagging if somebody's holding up the show. I figure every day or three is fine for doing things, but you might want to check in more to participate in the kibitzing, which will probably go at speed of Internet discussion. Probably no reason to require everyone to be in a single "party" either, except to pool resources. So then latecomers and unlucky rollers can author in their arrival as seems cool.

    Basically, I don't expect anyone to put any more effort into this than they would otherwise put into participation on Story Games. Spending five hours writing up a character bio by yourself will probably be a waste. Exchanging fifteen posts over the course of several days to collaboratively come up with your character's reason for Travelling is more the idea.

    I want this to work out more like an ongoing forum discussion and less like every play-by-email I've ever tried to participate in.
