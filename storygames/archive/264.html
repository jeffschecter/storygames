    Workshops can be good or bad depending entirely on the people within them.

    The biggest issue you need to resolve is "What are the ground rules for the workshop" (How much feedback is requested, how is it handled, is it round robin, assigned, or voluntary.)

    The second thing is you have to /give up/ your own biases in part.

    That is you have to be willing to go into a workshop, and provide feedback that's useful to the person's design. EVEN if you prefer different designs. And as a recipient of feedback you have to be able to understand that not everyone will like your writing/product/format and accept that

    Some of the workshops I've been in had problems with people "I don't like X" and because they didn't like X couldn't produce any feedback--so either the workshop needs to state up front the dropping of biases, or it needs to find a way to distill everyone interested to the same basic design concepts.

    (The above was actually a writing workshop, where someone didn't like relationship issues in their "entertainment" )

    The reason I bring this up, is my one attempt to workshop for RPG's had several people from fairly classical design of RPG's, and one person who wanted something very different (no protaganists IIRC for example) now there is nothing WRONG with that one person having a different desire of design at all. I provided feedback as best I could even though the game was something I /never/ would likely play. But I tried to provide it within the frame of what the other member wanted. Not everyone did so, and this causes workshopping to break down.
