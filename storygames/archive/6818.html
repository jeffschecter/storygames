\n Wednesday night I had a bit of a cold, no enthusiasm, and I almost canceled my game.

But I stuck it out, went and played... and the fact that I was GMing didn't stop the game from being awesome.

In A Wicked Age makes rising action, twists, and so on so easy I can't even believe it.

Rather than saying "meh, Wednesday's game kind of was OK" I can say: My players really flexed some creative muscles there. I think it's fair to say we played a game about a woman in an abusive relationship fighting against a corrupt and oppressive society to save life of her child. Also, a game about callous gods (spirits?) and their callous worshippers. How cool is that?

    Posted By: jenskotWhat about iaWA system, rules, structure, or text makes rising action and twists easy for you?
    The best interests setup phase means that the situation can't stay stable... and it can mean that as certain points resolve allegiances can shift. So that makes twists happen several times. Also, there's enough unpredictability that you can't count on even a disadvantaged PC going down, but it sure brings you into the moment.

    The one-to-three round structure of the rolling and the end-consequence that can come up (dice damage) means both sides really care about the dice every time. Also, the fact that the GM doesn't say a thing in conflicts between players meant that even if I was off my game that didn't impact their excitement in rolling it out. In D&D the players would be looking at me every time they rolled to describe / decide the outcome.

    Finally, the fact that dice can't be refreshed mid-chapter makes traits an unpredictable timebomb in the story; that timebomb is the losers getting weaker, and (often) that means more desperate. But at the same time, low dice = on the owe list, so reversals are possible as the giant gets brought to his knees.
