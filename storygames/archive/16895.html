      \n When I first played Fiasco, I was really nervous because I'm often plagued by creative paralysis - what scene would be best right now? I have to make it amazing and relevant and thematic and it's got to be the best possible scene that it can be, it has to set up future actions, etc.

      I had these worries right up until the moment I made my first scene in the game. I didn't know what the BEST scene would be, but I had an idea for a scene.       I can understand why people are nervous about their own creative paralysis when they read about the game, or read about / watch other people playing the game, but it's not until you actually sit down and play it that you realize how little of a problem it actually is.

      My reflection on the comments is that it seems to depend on which side of the spectrum people approach this from. Coming from an rpg background, Fiasco seems easy! Coming from a mainstream (ie Monopoly, Yahtzee) background, it can seem hopelessly complex. Half full, half empty.

      I've seen friends who love, love, love board games get held back by when asked to role play because they don't want to be on put on the spot. Despite having seen them come up with amazing ideas in other contexts! It's a shame.
