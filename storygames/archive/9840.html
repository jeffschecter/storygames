    \n As a solo game designer, I object to the definition of solo being "one GM and one player". Let's nip that one in the bud right there. After all, if that's solo, then what are the Fighting Fantasy book, zero-player game?

    Solo games are a bit neglected, I think. I'll be the first to pimp my game, How to Host a Dungeon. It's a solo game where you create a dungeon and watch it grow organically.

    Jackson Tegu's The Smoke Dream is a solo game with a magical-realism feel to it, and is quite excellent.

    There are several series of solo fantasy adventure books, like the old Fighting Fantasy and Tunnels & Trolls books. Many of them are very good. There was also a solo Car Wars adventure in the same vein, called Convoy.

    I'm probably forgetting some stuff.
