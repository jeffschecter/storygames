    Posted By: AndySo a conversation point: What's the secret to writing an in-character narrative, and keeping it interesting? Tips? Tricks?
    \nAt the risk of being flippant, is it any different from writing in general?

    Perhaps it's unique in the fact that its a blend of fiction and memoir (the recounting of something that happened only in a collective imagination?). But looking at Mike's excellent piece, I do not see anything unique about it. It has all the calling cards of well written fiction. It's got a gripping beginning. Engaging lines. Good description. little, if any, time wasted not revealing plot or character. A compelling metaphor that binds the piece together. Granted, I've only scanned and skimmed because my eyes can't take that much italicized text.

    But it seems to me the secret to writing an in-character narrative is the same as writing in general. Using the tricks of the trade and practice, practice, practice (also practice).
