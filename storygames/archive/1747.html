    I recently got a copy of Fantasy Flight's Warrior Knights game (apparently an update of an old game from 1985) and tried it out on Saturday. It was cool, a lot of fun with lots of options and player interaction but without getting complicated. Once you have the rules down (which look kinda complex but are actually really easy once you start playing) the game flows very well. The expeditions feel a touch tacked on and the space on the board could instead have been given over as a place to put Laws that have been voted into affect in the Assembly but, other than that, I have no complaints. It looks good and is of good quality, nice card counters and decent plastic miniatures and it can take anything from 3 to 6 players, although I get the feeling that the more players the more fun the game as it would force more player vs player interactions.

    You mentioned Shadows Over Camelot in your opening post and I can't recommend that one highly enough! It's a high quality game with good looks and pieces (and the picts, saxons and siege engines could certainly be painted up should someone feel that way inclined). I also love the co-operative nature of the game. It's all about the players vs the game and centres around resource management. There's difficult choices to make about which quests to attempt and which ones to let slide and the tension really builds up towards the end game when the siege engines begin to pile up. I've had great fun the 4 or 5 times that I've played it.

    Carcassonne was also my first thought - our FLGS has a guarantee on it that i you don't like it they'll take it back, no questions asked, they are that confident of its playability and appeal. One person once took advantage of that, and there were special circumstances. Along similar threads, Ticket to Ride and/or Thurn and Taxis are both strategic route laying games, good for teaching topography and quite fun and challenging without being hard.

    Otherwise: dare I say it, lots of people have loved the D&D board game. It has that sort of Warhammer Quest feel to it, which character expansion, even though it is limited, and little minis you can paint, cardboard trees which you can replace with ones you made yourself etc. etc. It has a couple of expansions which have proved popular as well. Though I know we loath them, the Clix games, especially Heroclix, do have a place here I'd say. Again, minis which you can collect (!), repaint or whatever, plus a nice squared map layout to keep the game quite straight forward but with potential for change.

    High end: Risk 2210 if you want something a little more advanced. Not that complex, but a few rules to juggle at the same time and strategic choices about when to reveal cards and when to spend resources. Has a limited number of turns, so is nicely enclosed for someone who doesn't do well with huge numbers of choices. Dad might not find so much here unless he wants to make his own commanders, but could find the game itself more to his liking.
