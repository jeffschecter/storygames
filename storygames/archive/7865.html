\n My brain is kind of fried when it comes to game design currently, but I've been playing a ton of video games-- much to Shreyas' annoyance at me staying up until 3 AM. We just finished Persona 3 FES, which was completely awesome, and I really wish there were an RPG that could kind of model the themes and feel of the game.

Don't tell me to play Misery Bubblegum! I know it's about high school students. Or Panty Explosion. I'm looking for a game with themes of personal responsibility, coming to terms with the inevitable, loss, and personal autonomy vs. free will. I mean, come on, it's a dating sim where at night, you shoot yourself in the head with a nightmare gun to evoke a terrible tarot-based creature from your psyche to battle the evil that humanity has been calling for to end the world because existence is so bleak. And then in the morning-- oh crap! It's finals!

Anyone play this and have RPG recommendations for me? Or feel like writing something I'm too lazy to write? Because either would be awesome.

    \n *cough* Uhm, yeah. I've been working on precisely this.

    But it's not even close to done...

    This is what I have formated right this second. There's a whole slew of rules about using your Persona and the tokens that go along with them that I still have to put in here, but this at least shows the background and what I'm trying to do with it.

    I think it captures some of the ideas you're talking about, while using Panty Explosion as the base system. The rule-set should eventually have rules about dealing with your Persona and growing into it -- including something like that specific moment in the game where almost everybody's Persona "matures" as they learn to deal with their internal Shadows, etc.

    I'd love to say that it'll be done shortly, but October's turning into a total nightmare schedule-wise.

    Anyway, just wanted to say you're not the only one who finished P3 and went "damn, I want to play that as a story-game."
