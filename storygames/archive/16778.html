\n Hi, everyone! A friend of mine asked me to run an Avatar (as in the Last Airbender) game for him. I'm considering something powered by the Apocalypse, but I realized I don't know very much about the show! I've seen a few episodes and intend to watch more, and understand the broad concept, but I'm curious about how ya'll view a few things. This might help me hack together that'll scratch his itch, I suppose!

In Avatar...
what qualities do all the heroes share in some amount?
what do the characters do all the time?
what are examples of things individual characters do all the time, but not everyone?
what differentiates and distinguishes characters from each other? Their culture and training, I'm sure, for one, but what else?
how do non-benders relate to and interact with benders? How do they use their own unique skills (and what sorts of things are those skills)?

More questions'll come up, sure, but right now I'm trying to figure out the stats, basic moves, and playbooks and I think these questions lead towards that. I'll research on my own, of course, watching more and reading up on the subject. All help is super-appreciated!

Thanks!

- Alex

    \n A thought I had while watching Korra coming from an RPG background (possible spoilers):

    If you look at all the different bending types they all have higher level forms that you may be or can learn. If you're a fire bender you can learn lightning. If you're a water bender you may be a healer or a blood bender. If you're an earth bender you can learn metal bending. And it seems that the avatar doesn't have access to any of these, though he does get soul bending, which is pretty cool.

    So Korra starts and I'm like: awesome, non-avatar air bender. We'll get to see some cool new bending form. But no. We get an avatar doing healing and a blood bender basically doing soul bending.
