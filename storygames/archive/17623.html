    \n When you establish a scene, you're starting it; choosing where it takes place, what it's about, and who's in it... usually. You describe toward something that is oftentimes rife with conflict(but not always). You're setting the scene. Directing it and acting for your character. When it becomes likely that the scene's coming to a head, another player hands you a die, and takes over describing the outcome, either positive or negative.

    When you to chose to resolve a scene, the other players set the scene and jerk your character into conflict. You get to choose and describe the outcome of the conflict.

    I think.

    \n Yeah, Nathan's got it. You never have complete control over any scene. You can either choose how it begins or how it ends, and even then, collaborating with the other players is always going to be better.

    Usually when you establish you're not going to start out by saying "and I'm getting shot at the end of this scene" but if that's what you want from the story, say that and talk it over with the other players. Then you can determine whether you're establishing or actually resolving.

    You can also have the same outcome mean different things depending on the die and the situation- getting shot sounds bad for your character (black die) but what if taking the bullet is the only way to save someone precious, and that's exactly what he wants? Sounds like that scene went well for him (white die).
