    \n Three is enough. The Lady and Cyrus are normally in play, you can add Kale and have a sort of love triangle (esp if Kale is a girl). Naomi as the cock-blocker of the romance is amusing, too.

    You can actually have a good time with both Natasha and Cyrus as NPCs, and have the PCs be Kale, Naomi and Snargle. Then you run the game similar to Rosencrantz and Gildernstern Are Dead, they're the protagonists, but not exactly the stars. It's quite amusing that way.

    \n I played it with 3. I gave the players a few options for how to take it, and they decided they liked the feel of playing the crew and having the passengers be the NPCs, so we had Snargle, Kale, and Cyrus. There was some discovery of puzzling out who the Lady was and such, I would definitely run it like that again. Essentially, I guess I'm saying that the Lady and Cyrus don't need to both be in play.

    However, with the Lady and Cyrus already established, I'd personally add Kale. Naomi would work as well, but I'm not so sure that Snargle is tied into them enough for him to function as a third.
