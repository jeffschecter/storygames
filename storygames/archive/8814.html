Sea Dracula at Arcon

\n

\n
\n
\n
\n
\n I went to Arcon at my old alma mater over the weekend. As the guy who named the convention I try to go every year and have only missed one due to illness. It's mostly undergrads which makes me feel like an old man but a lot of my old college buddies go too which turns it into a bit of a reunion.

One of the kids was running Sea Dracula, though, and I had to sign up. This game is insane and awesome. It is a great convention or party game and when I described it to one of my friends later she was thrilled and said something to the effect of "there needs to be more games like this."

Dances done: the Charleston, the hammer dance, the Numa Numa dance, a lap dance (!), the Truffle Shuffle, and more.

I think myself and one other person in the room got the reference the Sea Dracula Oath was making. Everyone else just thought it was silly and random and was kind of confused by it. I was ten when that song was popular, and I think everyone in the room was about ten years younger than me.

Insane and fun, though 45 seconds of hammer dancing killed my knees for the weekend and 45 seconds of dancing to "I'm a Barbie Girl" killed my ego.
