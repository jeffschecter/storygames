    \n Hey! There's some stuff that should have been said long before now, and I'm sad that I haven't said it.

    There are some people who deserve a bunch of credit for Go Play NW, and I'm not sure they've gotten it. I think a lot of people don't know who's on the team, and their contributions have gone a bit unrecognized.

    First of all, Ping has joined our team this year as project manager. She is personally responsible for helping us find our awesome venue this year and is helping us get organized so that we can have a successful Go Play NW every year.

    Also, Phil LaRose is the treasurer and has been keeping track of our bank account and updating the Go Play Web site and doing a dozen other of the important jobs that make Go Play go.

    I'd also like to thank John Harper, who despite not being on the official team this year has created an awesome T-shirt and continues to promote Go Play like gangbusters, as well as Brandon Amancio, who was on the team last year and did a tremendous amount of unrecognized legwork to get us a venue for the convention.

    This is shaping up to be an incredibly awesome year with our new venue and Friday night feast. We\u2019re looking forward tremendously to seeing everyone in June!

    \n Last night we got together and created the game scheduling and sign-up boards for Go Play NW. There was a lot of planning discussion, so to provide the best information for the pre-scheduled games, we printed out the discussion threads and posted them on the sign-up board. We did this because some games have changing numbers of players, additional GM's, double tables and so on. We didn't want to be in charge of interpreting what the GMs wanted. GMs should take care to clearly communicate with their players so everyone understands who's signed up, and who is an alternate for each game.

    Like previous years, there will be plenty of day-of scheduling and open games. We'll make sure that nobody is left without a game!

    The spread of games is really great. It's going to be a very exciting convention!

    Cheers,
    Tony, Phil, and Ping
