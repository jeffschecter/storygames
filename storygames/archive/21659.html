\n Hi all, first time poster here. Hope I'm not cloning a thread but a quick search didn't yield anything.

I'm really interested in games and systems which deal with collapsing or failing civilisations, as in: what causes them to fail, is it even possible to stop the process, what stories emerge from people watching as the systems they've relied on their whole lives stop functioning, what meaning can be salvaged from a rapidly collapsing culture?

Does anyone know of any story games that talk about those sorts of things? I know of "Downfall", where a society is founded at the start of the game with a central flaw as its foundational principle. Any others?

    \nWhile the World Ends, Zombie Cinema, Escape from Tentacle City are all examples of story games that deal with an apocalyptic milieu. This style of game is generally rather removed from the macro-level societal concern you outline: most story games are about human drama, so it's less about why the Roman empire collapses or how it happens, but rather about how human beings react to the sudden disruption in their lives.

    It's an interesting topic, and I'd like to be able to point at a kind of game that actually went deep into the sociological aspects - some sort of wargame, perhaps - but I can't really name any worthy of the name. It's a solid topic, I'd be interested in playing something where you build a society and then destroy it, myself.

    It is interesting that the best tabletop game about this that I can name off-hand might well be How to Host a Dungeon, the dungeon-building "story game" about dwarves delving too deep and all that. If nothing else, at least it's got the right scope for our purposes, which can't be said of most roleplaying games that are hopelessly mired in a personal scope. I don't really feel that a game telling about desperate people trying to survive the collapse can capture the higher-order concerns.

    \n Here\u2019s some of the top of my head. Some could be very close what your looking for (e.g. Polaris); others will work if you actively make the potential fall of civilization the major focus of the theme, or adjust the scope/size involved (e.g. have a town/city represent a larger part of civilization or civilization as a whole) or similar minor tweaks.

    -Polaris

    -Children of the Fall (currently being crowd funded on Indiegogo)

    -Microscope (if you want it to)

    -The Quite Year (if you want it to)

    -The Deep Forest (if you want it to)

    -Three Dooms (if you want it to)

    -Follow (if you choose the right scinerio and you want it to; the fall of civilization isn\u2019t an explicit theme of the game, but could be made to work if stressed in play)

    \nBurning Empires is about humanity's interstellar civilization falling to invading aliens, planet by planet. A campaign is about an individual planet, from alien reconaissance and infiltration to final landing operations, from the POV of various humans (who just might save the day). It's based on the popular Burning Wheel RPG and a kick-ass comic. (It's all sitting on my shelf unused, though.)

    \nKingdom by @Ben_Robbins also feels quite appropriate, if you set it up accordingly. Granted, collapse in such a game would be a concrete possibility on the horizon, but not a given.

    In Dialect, you play a society that is bound to be lost eventually, as no other speakers of their language remain. It doesn't deal with the dynamics of collapse per se as much as it does with themes of establishing a community, setting it apart and - especially - legacy: how we came to be "a thing" and what little will be remembered.
