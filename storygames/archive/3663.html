    \nWell, I was mostly kidding. (You do know I publish DS, right?) I like the 64-pagers too, you know.

    As for DS, it is the only full-scale sci-fi setting built on d20 Modern and powered by d20 Future. (That's part of the elevator pitch.) It's a "firm-science" setting. We have a technical advisor we vet all the science stuff with. Everything in the setting is theoretically possible (including our take on psionics, "Red Truth"). The setting was designed to permit a number of different sci-fi sub-genres, e.g., bug hunt, space opera, Space Western, alien relic hunting, etc. Most people say it (at least the OQL book) has a strong Firefly vibe. HR and our upcoming mega-adventure Shadow War, I think, are gleefully steeped in Battle Beyond the Stars.

    Although it is d20, the mechanics are, by and large, very clearly segregated from the setting material. I think it would be a simple matter to run an adventure or campaign using a different ruleset. I could even see SG-style games in the setting, e.g., DitV in the Eotian faction camps, Burning Empires as part of a vaasi invasion, etc. You can also download a ton of freebie support off our website, including extensive previews of all the books.
