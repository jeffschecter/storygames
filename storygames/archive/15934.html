\n Played a little bit of an irregular, ongoing Dungeon World game I have going with two players playing Eldar and Sparrow, kind of a Fafhrd/Mouser fighter/thief combo, currently fighting their way out of a zombie-infested Waterdeep. This is the first time I've played with the new 1.1 rules. Everything went well as usual but I realized that I just don't understand how Bonds are supposed to work.

In the new rules, your Bond score is equal to the number of bonds you have with the other player (max 3), yeah? In addition to guiding roleplaying the relationships between characters, you can roll +Bond to Aid another character. Finally, you can "resolve" a Bond to mark XP and start a new one.

In this game, Eldar has the Bond "I have sworn to protect Sparrow," and Sparrow has "Eldar has my back when things go wrong." (Sparrow's Bond is from the Redbook edition.) These are nice, complementary Bonds, if a little boring.

Where I'm having trouble is when and how these Bonds actually matter for gameplay. It seems like neither is likely to ever be "resolved" - they aren't phrased like keys, so there's no clear fictional trigger for resolution. There doesn't appear to be any other way of gaining XP for acting on a Bond. The Bond score never increases unless they add new Bonds, but there don't seem to be any rules for adding new Bonds except in the event that a Bond is resolved (meaning that your Bond score at character creation will stay the same for the whole campaign).

Help! Do we just need to write more interesting and dynamic Bonds? Is this just a side-effect of playing a two-player campaign? Am I missing something about how Bonds are supposed to work?

    \n Felan! Don't panic. This is all great feedback for Adam and Sage. You are right, the Beta 1.1 rules are a little thin on the ground in the guidance of using / writing / resolving bonds. I agree that they need to have a BW style belief 'trigger' or narrative based resolution, though most of them can be simply reversed as a resolution trigger.
    \n
    \n[cross posted] Hans gives excellent advice! Especially about GM agency in 'pushing' on bonds.
    \n
    \nYour PC's for instance might descide that when Eldar fails to protect Sparrow that their bond is resolved and worthy of a new one, or that an aditional bond be written? The rules don't seem to put a cap on Bonds at the moment, so they seem to be a good XP mine if you can write good bonds and justifiably resolve them (with the consent of the group) within the narrative.
    \n
    \nSo although the Bonds set at Chargen is a fixed modifier, it can lower as Bonds get resolved and no new ones get added, or increase as new Bonds become apparant between the PCs.
    \n
    \nAs an optional rule in one or two player games, you might like to consider the idea from AW in the advanced Fuckery Section of giving NPCs bond, therefore increasing the player's workload as they track their various bonds, but increasing the narrative (and XP) potential in low player number games.

    \n We have a system for new Bonds, you'll see it in Beta 2. Basically:

    When you write a new bond, choose another character, first. Then, pick something relevant to the session you've just finished - maybe a place you traveled together or an object you discovered. Lastly, choose a thought or belief your character holds that ties the two together and an action, something you're going to do about it. You'll end up with something like this;

    - Avon proved himself a coward in the Dungeons of the Xax'takar, he is a dangerous liability to the party.
    - Mouse's quick thinking saved me from the White Dragon we faced. I owe her a boon.
    - Xotoq won the Bone-and-Whispers Axe through trickery! It will be mine, I swear it.
    - Valeria's kindness to the Gnomes of the Vale has swayed my heart. I will find a way to prove to her love.
