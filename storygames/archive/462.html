    Y'know, this line's been ringing in my brain since I read it. So I'm gonna respond.

    Luke: I am not leaving you unsupported on this issue because I think you're absolutely right and the cause is noble, but I'm too lazy and complacent to stir myself off my couch to take action.

    I'm leaving you unsupported because I feel that you are dead wrong.

    You're going after the Moderators on the basis that their responsibility to respect you and your concerns is of the same caliber as your responsibility to respect them and their concerns. It assumes that you are, in the context of RPG.Net, peers.

    That is explicitly not how that board is organized. The Mods are not answerable to you, or to Paul, or even to the community as a whole. They aren't supposed to be. If RPG.Net were meant to be a democracy, with the elected Mods trying to implement the will of the people, then your protest would make sense. But it's not, and nobody has ever claimed that it is. It's an oligarchy, in which the Mods do what they think is best, period. They're in charge. The board functions with them in charge. It ain't broken.

    I see the "civil disobedience" posters making the argument that RPG.Net is in fact a democracy, but that the Mods have somehow usurped power that belongs rightfully to the people. They're treating it as a broken democracy rather than a functioning oligarchy. In doing that they are not driving it toward being a functioning democracy. They're just driving it toward being a broken oligarchy.

    I tend to assume that such folks (you included, Luke) are just confused and passionate. You think that democracy is keen (it is!) and that therefore oligarchy is evil (it isn't). Your hearts are in the right place, but you're misdirecting your energy and zeal. So I sit back and hope that you'll burn yourselves out before you do too much harm.

    But, hypothetically ... if you didn't? If you guys were actually to start making headway in your fight to break RPG.Net? I wouldn't be lazing on my couch. I would be fighting against you.
