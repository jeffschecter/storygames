\n Is ability even the right word here?

Next, what sounds better:

For\u2014is used for proactive actions. (e.g. you want to to climb a wall)
Against\u2014is used for reactive actions. (e.g. you are trying to avoid a boulder falling on you)
With\u2014is used for creative actions. (e.g. you are casting a spell)

For\u2014are actions you initiate.
Against\u2014are actions you respond with.
With\u2014are actions you have a specific talent in.

For\u2014proactive actions where you control the situation.
Against\u2014reactive actions where someone or something controls the situation.
With\u2014creative actions where you want to alter the situation.

Which reads the best to you?

One of the interesting dilemmas coming from the context is for \u2018With\u2019. The the first sentence, this seems like it would only be used unique individuals (magic-user or cleric, in a dungeon crawl). But the second sentence is broader\u2014could be use for a magic-user casting a spell, but also a for a fighter with a favored weapon.

Any changes that could make it more clear and concise?

    \n At character creation you get 7 points to put into the three abilities (maximum of 4 points in any score).

    Example: For: 3 Against: 2 With: 2

    I climb the wall (use For). I dodge chunks of wall falling on me (use Against). I cast (x) spell on the wall to create a magical doorway or alcove (use With).

    You pick 6 traits (there is a big list, varies a bit by genre). If you can use your trait add +5 to ability roll (For, Against, With).

    Example: Explore; Decipher, Camp, Monster Lore, Cast Spells, Diplomacy

    You are searching for a secret door in a dungeon, use For 3+5 for Explore and roll d20 higher than the Difficulty number.

    You are casting magic missile, use With 2+5 for Cast Spells roll d20 over Difficulty number\u2014in this case is the spell level+monster HD.
