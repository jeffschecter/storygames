\n Today I started a game of Apocalypse World with a few friends; two of them had never really played story games before. The game went well, but that's not why I'm posting. There came the moment when I told them that I didn't have anything prepared, that we were going to come up with it here and now. Their eyes shined, and we were off.
\n
\nI want to organize a public space for introducing people to GM-full games that are filled to the brim with these sort of experiences: Microscope, Fiasco, Polaris, etc. I know Ben Robbins has been doing this sort of thing in Seattle, but I'd like to openly ask you:
\n
\nHave you organized an open story game street team?
\nWhat did you do to publicize it?
\nWhat kind of facilitation and leadership do you bring to the event?
\nWhat else should I, and anyone interested in doing something similar, know before diving it?

    \n We have a sister meetup group at Story Games Olympia.


    I know there is something going on down in Portland.


    I was talking to some folks at PAX. Apparently what we need is a Bellingham meetup group so we get that solid, I-5 PNW action.

    Ethan, you should check out cons in your area and advertise a bit on here and rpg.net. I had never heard of meetup.com until I saw it on a flyer, but now I think it is a great platform. Also, you'd be surprised at how many people who have never played rpg's can get into Story Games.

    \n Nathan Paoletta, Dev Purkayastha, and I started SG Boston in 2006-2007, running random pick-up games every week. The number of participants varied from 4-25, slowly increasing over time, but it eventually settled into a fairly stable group of a dozen regulars before I left to move to Seattle. That was cool, but it kinda subverted the original plan of playing a lot of different games with a lot of different people. SG Boston is still cruising along, so I hear, but I really have no idea what they're up to these days.
