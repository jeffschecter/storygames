    \n English language RPGs I purchased in the past six months which aren't already listed in this thread, or "to be released from Kickstarter", which I would heartily recommend:

    Anima Prime - I'm a huge fan of classic Final Fantasy, and Christian and I have similar tastes, so this was simply a must-buy. Having said that, I have not played it yet, so I can't confirm the awesomeness. Hoping to have it run for me at GPNW.

    Panty Explosion Perfect - A smart, fast storytelling game of Japanese schoolgirls. Either mundane, or with (default) psychic powers. The best part is that the entire game is written in a graphical format. You don't actually have to Read The Rules, you can read the explanation comics and totally understand it!
    See this: Link to the rules-manga.
    I have played the previous version of PE, the new one strips down the rules to very simple roots, which makes it a little Too Simple (so I thought at first glance), but really spins up the story and communication aspects strongly, and thus keeps the game strong.

    ...hmmm... I can't think of any others at this time, will repost if I do.

    \n I know I'm a bit late as well, but here's my input:

    First, it's hard to know what to recommend without knowing what you like, Sebastian. But that's fine, because then we get to talk about what WE like, which is a favorite pastime of most people. And it's also fine, because I figure this thread is sort of addressing anyone who may want to buy a new game, not just Sebastian specifically.

    So with that in mind:

    All the Cel*Style books are beautiful and fun to play, and Jake's teach-the-game comics in PE and GxB are brilliant. Like, change the face of game design, brilliant. But you've seen them, so you know that. And if the genre's aren't your thing, that's cool; I WILL, though, point to Jake's series of writeup/illustrations on how to play Panty Explosion in other genres and properties. (scroll down for more! If you like Star wars, X-men, Harry Potter, Warhammer, or Naruto, it might interest you!)

    Regarding Micrscope: it's a slick, fun and well-designed game. It can be more impersonal than some RPGs, to be sure, but here's a blog post I wrote about telling more personal, emotionally affecting stories with the game, and a bit of what made that work.

    Perfect is superb. You get to be defiant and edgy and cool, and you get to try and break the edgy defiance of others, and you get to see passionate characters suffering for their beliefs, and occasionally triumph! (Not that this trumps your privately expressed reasons, of course, Sebastian!)

    Smallville is a great game even if you don't know the show. Most people I know who've bought/read/played it are either "meh, Smallville" or "ugh, Smallville." But it's great for running ANY sort of TV soap opera game, especially with superpowers. It IS big, though, with a lot of rules clockwork, no mistake. haven't played Leverage. I hear its simpler, not to mention differently focused. Again, don't need to know the show, just need to enjoy "competent people have jobs/capers/adventures, with crazy complications."

    Apocalypse World is Apocalypse World. Everyone's talking about it. What's great about it for me is, you've got sharp and evocative character types with all kinds of badassery, but they're enmeshed in really human circumstances surrounded by messy human wants and needs. And the rules are great. And everything else is great, really. And yes, its eminently hackable, and the AW book will let you enjoy all the rad hacks out there, like Dungeon World and Monsterhearts.

    And last but not least, since self-promotion seems kosher in the thread: I wrote a game called The Dreaming Crucible, which is about troubled youngsters going into Faerie to face their greatest pain and fears and be transformed, for good or ill. It can cover the range of "greatest fears and pain" from "but nobody understaaands me" (like in The Labyrinth) to "I was abused by a close family member" (like in a recent AP). It's designed to skew about two thirds toward the trauma side, generally, and depends on what a set of players brings to the game. I'm very proud of the game and the handmade book, and Erin Kelso's gorgeous artwork!

    peace,
    -Joel
