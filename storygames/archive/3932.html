Posted By: MarhaultPosted By: Joshua BishopRobyDude, word. Screw hit points; combat should whittle down resolve.
\nHit Points could be said to represent resolve already. Unless anyone believes someone can receive the worst wound hit from an axe a dozen times over and survive, just because they have lots of "experience".
It's all in the narration.

I don't think I made myself clear, and I don't want to further threadjack the Two-Weapon Fighting thread. So.

Hit points do not represent a character's resolve. They're a counter for how long a character can stay in play. Sure, you could narrate however you like, but I don't know about you, but my resolve fluctuates a lot without being hit with an axe.

Using resolve instead of hit points would mean that feints, intimidation, being outnumbered, and the badguy revealing terrible things about your friends would all reduce your resolve... as well as being hit with an axe. Your resolve could increase when you are encouraged, when the tide of battle swings your way, when you suddenly realize you love the woman you're fighting for, or when said woman throws the Sword of Destiny into your hand.

Resolve would track, not your ability to stay in the fight, but your desire to fight in the first place.

I mean, like, priests sitting back there and healing you up as you get beat on with an axe? Has anybody ever considered how really fucking creepy that is, with big bloody gashes on your body closing up again even as you keep fighting? How screwed up is that? Instead, imagine the priest in the back chanting holy prayers, giving you the moral strength to keep fighting -- that makes so much more sense to me, and also seems a whole lot more fun.

    Posted By: Quintin StoneIsn't this kind of what Star Wars d20 does with their Wounds/Vitality system?
    \nUSED to do, what with the new Saga Edition and all. And it's similar. Your vitality is the stuff that powers both your "parrying" abilities (you have a separate Defense stat that is analogous to "dodging") in a fight and your Force abilities. Whenever your opponent gets past your Defense, you lose Vitality. Whenever you use a Force ability you lose Vitality. When you run out of Vitality, you are open to losing Hit Points.

    Personally, I think it works really well, but perhaps the system should be opened up a little to include other factors besides "parrying" and "magic." Obviously Obi-wan lost the will to finish off Anakin at the end of their fight. Why? Pity caused a lose of Vitality (or resolve). So, this isn't a new idea, but one that perhaps hasn't been developed to it's potential.

    \n What if it worked like this.

    You start the game with a Resolve at Zero (0). But wait, you're John McClain and the terrorists have your wife.
    So you lay down "Love my wife" and "I'm a cop" and maybe some other stuff. Now your Resolve is 20 (I'm making stuff up here).

    They shoot at you, you lose your shoes, run out of ammo. Your resolve is now 8. But you beat some of them up, heard them on the radio threatening your wife, you've found some bad-ass ordinance, just scaled an elevator shaft, and walked over broken glass. Now your Resolve is 60! You're almost ready to face down the bad guys.

    I'm getting a sort of reverse D&D here. You're not trying to conserve hit points and spells so you'll have enough resources to fight the evil wizard. You're trying to build up your Resolve to some level (80? 100? Depends on the story?) at which point, you face off against the terrorist leader and save the day. Or to bring in more suspense, maybe you need 100 resolve to overcome the obstacles. But, you face off against the big bad guy at 80. You've got to get 20 resolve in that last fight or your finished!
