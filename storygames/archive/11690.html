\n I'm looking for help, so I'm holding a contest. This has been posted over on The Forge too.

I'm looking for sample scenarios (called Frames\u2014read this?). They're 7 lines long (see below).

Best entry goes into the book and wins a PDF.

EDIT (by request)

Frame Components

Setting: Where is the game set, who does it follow and what does it look like?
Adversary: Who is the bad guy that wants you killed?
Gore Threshold: Low = gory, high = tame (between 3-7)
Connection: How the characters know each other?
Drop-Off: Where does it all start (not in medias res, please)?
Objective: Where does it all end (location)?
Checkpoints: 3 tasks. Each is themed. 1st: Covert. 2nd: Overt. 3rd: Access\u2014how to get access to the Objective.

Submissions by the 18th of March.

[The purpose of this contest is to provide me with a new perspective on my own design, to push me out of my own restrictions and to expose the game to the community]

    \nWe Are John Connor

    Setting: Alternates between the present day, big-haired 1984 and the post-war hell of 2029.
    Adversary: Skynet and its machines, from clunky early HKs through to advanced TXs and beyond.
    Gore Threshold: 5, with an emphasis on collateral damage - bystanders, law enforcement and lots of buildings getting smashed.
    Connection: Skynet has extensive files - characters are connected by what they will do.
    Drop-Off: At least two separate time periods.
    Objective: United in one time period.
    Checkpoints: A bunker in the desert, a factory in the city, and a suburban home - not in that order, necessarily, and the time frames aren't specified. Combine these in any order with 'blowing up', 'destroying a hidden robot factory inside', and 'travelling in time from'.

    Additional tweaks: To better represent the risk and reward of becoming more like the machines, felonies include 'Going metal' - becoming as cold and ruthless as a terminator.

    \n I'm going to do a bunch for games I have been playing in or running lately. A lot of these setup questions are very similar to the things that get generated by my game, Misspent Youth, so I have a font of examples (including one from Emily Care Boss's Sign in Stranger from MaulCon III and one from the weekly Apocalypse World game Vincent Baker is running for us). First, I'll start with:

    PROMETHEUS FUCKS SHIT UP (based on a MY game played by Buddha Davis, Daniel Eison, Courtny Hopen, Abel Vargas, and me at Dreamation 2010)
    Setting: Far-future dystopia, poor live in "Gehenna," the trash dump of the floating city Tantalus, peopled by "gods" (rich people with tech that each take on a different god's persona). The poor are "sacrificed" to the gods, who test out tech on them.
    Adversary: The Gods of Tantalus
    Gore Threshold: 6
    Connection: A street gang that rolled a drunk "god," Bima.
    Drop-off: Bima comes to the gang's hangout to get his holy water and scimitar-like thumbnails back.
    Objective: Tantalus
    Checkpoints: Rolling Bima again, bargaining with the dragon, the undercity (the craggy/rocky inverted mountain that Tantalus sits atop)
