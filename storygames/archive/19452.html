    \n\nHarlequin \n\n\n
    \n
    \n\nMay 2014 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n Pretty epic.
    I continue to be surprised by players every year.

    I started a really awesome game of Warrior Poet, because I had four players who just let themselves free creatively, without fear that anyone would would judge them. There is little to no self-doubt at Nerdly, it seems. That set the tone of the weekend for me.

    The new campgrounds was way bigger, better, and awesome-er. Several LARPS used the new space very well, and even the table-tops were played outdoors Sunday morning on account of the great weather and location.

    Nerdly attracted NEW people yet again. While most were friends of friends, we did have one gentleman who brought his child with him all the way from out West!

    Also, we didn't realize until too late that the bathrooms in the individual camps (A,B,C,D) were unisex, and only the main one (near the Mess) was divided into Men/Women, but everyone dealt with it.

    I didn't get to game with half the people I wanted to, but that's the way it go. SO MUCH TO DO!
