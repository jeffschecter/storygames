    \n Alexander had an extra surgery, it seems. Here's the latest:\n
    Posted By: fierinferno@LiveJournal
    Things are a little different from what I thought yesterday, but the most important thing is that Alex is doing really well. He does have chest tubes in, but they're drainage tubes, not breathing tubes, so he's mostly okay. He's recovering really well, and he's ready to get out of the ICU into a private room now. The nurses are just waiting for a room on the 6th floor to open up so they can move him. Then a few more days and he'll be out of there.

    The surgery went well, although they did find some other stuff they removed, and we're waiting on biopsy information to find out how serious that was, or could be.

    He had an epidural, so he's in no pain. The only problem is that his clavicle hurts like hell. It's a combination of them messing with the shunt from his chemo, being less than gentle where they originally went in, and just soreness from the surgery. He's in a lot of pain in that area, but other than that he's okay.

    If you want to get in touch with him he should have his cell phone and internet access back by tomorrow afternoon, maybe sooner. He'll be on the 6th floor of St Jo's, again, by tomorrow. Right now he's on the 4th floor in the Cardio Thorasic ICU. If we're really lucky, he'll be home during the weekend. He'll probably have to wait until Monday so the docs can all clear him for discharge, though.

    Thanks again. If you have any questions or concerns you can contact him by tomorrow, or me any time. Just let me know if you need my phone number and I'll keep you in the loop as I am.
