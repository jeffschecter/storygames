    Posted By: GrahamJason (and other moderators): Will you enforce this? You'll have to enforce it for people you agree with, as well as people you don't.
    \nI can't speak for the other moderators but this is my perspective:

    - We need to create a system to encourage community self-moderation. Which will in effect transform moderators into rule designers and rule enforcers rather than traditional moderators.

    - The moderators work 40-80 hour work weeks while Story Games is a non-profit community service. The moderators can't and won't read every thread or every post. So we will need a system where the rules are clear and the community proactively contacts the moderators when rules need to be enforced.

    - When rules are broken, rules will be enforced regardless who broke the rules. This is very important since the moderators have so many friends on Story Games, it's hard to be unbiased. Which is why encouraging the community to help is crucial. If my best friend is breaking the rules and a stranger notifies me, then my best friend gets a time out or whatever enforcement is required.

    - Slow Down is only one of over 20+ techniques we've identified to improve Story Games. But just like game design, we need to roll out changes slowly to properly test which changes causes which effects and adjust as needed.

I want the original poster to be able to exercise a little ruling in that opening post within reason*, without always having to call on the mods, and I want the readers to respect that.

    If original posters start to misuse this rule, we'll re-evaluate.

    (*within reason: I've seen two cases years ago where an OP basically started a vague thread (not specific about the kind of reply they wanted), then basically "moderated" it by insulting repliers they didn't like, bullying them off the thread, etc; I came down on it pretty hard when it happened, and ultimately both of those posters were eventually banned anyway.     \n A problem I see with the way "Slow Down" was presented and described here: if you present it as an exception, something that should be used only in the most horrible threads, or when touching abominable subjects, and you even say "please don't use it just to try, use only in case of extreme necessity"... well, who will take on himself the responsibility of saying to the other people in the thread "you caused this thread to become a mess, now you have forced me to take this extreme and deadly measure", and who will say to the entire forum "I don't believe that you will be able to talk about this without dogpiling me, so I am using this insulting measure against you all"?

    Who will open himself (or herself) to the accusation of abusing that rule?

    Used like this, the rule is useful only to trolls, or people who don't care about their reputation in the forum. The people who would really need to invoke the rule... will be dissuaded by the heavy peer pressure that doing so will heap on his/her head. And if some poor naive user will try to use it... it will be a trollfest of every troll in the forum screaming at the way they are being "silenced".

    The "slow down" rule is useful only if it's a total free choice, that has no need to be motivated in any way. ONLY IN THIS MANNER it will not be an accusation to other people and will not risk causing bad feedback on the user.

    "this thread is in slow down because I don't want to read too many post"
    "this thread is in slow down, I have my motives"
    "this thread is in slow down".
    In this way the rule work.

    "this thread is in slow down because I don't trust you" will never work.
