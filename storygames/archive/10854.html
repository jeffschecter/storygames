    \n I am a huge fan and have read the book several times and played more than 60 episodes. I am wondering what could change or improve in the new edition.

    The book is well written, reviewed and revised. There is only one glaring error where the two final sentences in one page are repeated in the next. Not much to improve year.

    The designer has hinted that conflict will be called scene resolution and I think this is better.

    The examples of play are adequate and plenty.

    The examples used in the beginning of the explanation of conflict are not great since they are more bangs than conflicts per se.

    I think more examples of edges, connections and issues would be great.

    I am wondering if there will be any mechanical changes since there is absolutely nothing that I would change.

    Could there be a producer-less variant?

I wonder if the designer will get rid of the word "stake(s)" as it can have a negative impact in the community as I perceive it. I also wonder if they will expand in exploring the possibilities of the protagonists' "issues". The issue is essentially described as what the character is about but the issue is so much more. It can be "what adventure are you interested in playing". It can be as deep and as personal as desired or it could be "plot oriented": ie "my issue is to find my estranged brother" (who left years ago and was last seen on a pirate ship).
