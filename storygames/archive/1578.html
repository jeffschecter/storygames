eruditus
October 2006 edited October 2006 in Actual Play
So one of the main themes to my game is Ressurrection - bringing those things that are dead back to life.

Sorcerers only bind the dead things and make them do their bidding. And the dead, demons, always seek to come back but never show themselves to the ignorant. I also have levied a heavy social stigma against bringing back the dead - both physically and metaphorically.

Any suggestions how the Sorcerers of Mu can make demons into something alive and well, mechanically speaking?

As an AP example: The players decided that one of the villainous sorcerers has a demon bound to him to elevate his status and prop up his ego. That demon is Faith. Faith is long departed from the blasted world. Now it seeks to capitalize on the ignornace of Marr'd's denizens (this is a very cynical game - a good low point to start with) and seed the world with followers of one cause or another. Faith is an object demon, bound to a set of vestments - the hair of slaves woven into a matted shawl adorned with ancient patches sporting uncipherable runes - N.A.S.A.

If the players and characters were to bring faith back into the world how would that goal be demonstrated mechanically? Would the demon need spawn? What happens to the bound demon when it's influence gains acceptance? Should the player decide to banish the demon at an opportune time would the demon remain here free to flower? What should that point be?

Your thoughts and suggestions are much appreciated.

- Don

    Hi eruditus,

    Taking your Faith example into consideration, I think that if you're looking for mechanical ways for players to restore Faith to Maar'd (and consequently do away with the evil one's demon) then you might look again at Sorcerer's Banishing rules.

    Specifically, I'd note that:
    • As others (eg. Fred) have noted, Faith should have a massive Power.
    • That also means it most likely has a pretty high Will, Binding Strength, etc.
    • Ergo, Banishing Faith (ie. restoring Faith to life -- it's the same thing) will not be easy.
    • This is where the Sorcerer rules for helping and rollover successes come into play.
    • Put simply, the PCs are going to have to sweat out some very significant accomplishments of restoring faith to NPCs (or other PCs) before they have enough accumulated successes to feed into the banishing and really bring Faith back.

    That's how I'd handle it in-game, anyway. What I like about it is that it really turns around the concept of "Banishing", making it into something much more restorative and good. And that synchronizes nicely with the fact that Banishing someone else's demon actually allows you to increase Humanity, and with Mu's overall Humanity definition.

    So, yeah. That's how I'd do it.
