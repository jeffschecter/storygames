\n I wrote a one page dungeon for use with @John_Harper's Breakers (World of Dungeons: Turbo). You can get it here.

It's a mash-up of London's Natural History Museum (a gorgeous building, if you've never seen it) and the eponymous fire swamp. The whale is the new full blue whale skeleton that hangs in the entrance hall, brought to unlife by necromantic power to roam the swamp and eat lizardmen.

More generally, we've really been enjoying playing Breakers - we've played two sessions so far and the mixture of "straight to the action" (from the Dungeon World / PbtA roots), exciting places and intrinsic humour is a great mix for our group.

For the first session I used "The Sunken Spires" by etirocke, jumbled up with London's Tate Modern. This mostly meant that I added a (tongue in cheek) layer of contemporary art over the dungeon, rather than changing its structure: So the hypnotic moths had wings pattern with Mondrians, etc. It began with a rickety mine lift situated on the Turbine Hall's floor, which is actually somewhat plausible given real exhibits that have been there.

I'd love to hear what you think of the fire swamp, and also to hear about your Breakers' play experiences.

    \n I've no idea if the dream-like fantasy genre here is because of the scenario or the game, but either way, I love it. In fact, that's totally the sort of scenario I could see running in my 19th century mystery archeology D&D, or my dreamlands D&D. I love how the real interleaves with the fantastic; it's a technique I've worked with myself, I'm slowly converting Helsinki into a fantasy adventure location for my desk drawer.

    So yeah, that adventure works for me.
