\n I've put our group's Pulp Hero on hold when it became (sadly) clearly that in order to get new blood into the group, I'd need to use d20 to get them in the door. Not that I dislike d20, but I like a lot of games and diffderent styles.
\n
\nI wanted to do something different than the two other d20 games and then a thought hit me: start them at level 10, and keep them there so I wouldn't have to worry about the headaches of high level play that had derailed my last two games. Once I came to that conclusion, a more political game, set in a fantasy version of the real-world, began to take shjape.
\n
\nAnyway, I've wikified the original notes. We had a character generation session and then I set up the first game as follows
\n
\n"You're on a mountainside in south-central France. Looming several hundred yeards away, a hundred feet above yiur head, is a large cave that's been burrowed in the mountainside. The air reeks of brimstone. An acrid yellow smoke wafts out of the cave, produced by the red dragon that the king has sent you to kill."
\n
\nThis description received the appropriate response. A great way to kick a campaign.
