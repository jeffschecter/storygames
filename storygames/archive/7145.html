    \n Oh, not many. I'm rotten at actually telling anybody what I'm doing. I'm now trying to learn, though, by reporting at my blog and such. Better learn to be loud about myself if I want anybody to actually buy this at Gencon.

    The short history of the game is that I designed it around 2006 Christmas, published it in Finnish in summer 2007 and now am going to Gencon with the English version. Finns seem to like the game, although in the interest of full disclosure I should reveal that it only got two stars out of five from the premier Finnish rpg magazine due to the fact that characters actually very rarely shoot zombies in the head to any appreciable effect in the game. I've distributed the Finnish version and various translations to interested people during the last year, so there are some actual play reports in English as well, mostly at the Forge. I'll see if I can dig any up before I go to sleep.

    \n Ah, most excellent. I found a Forge thread from last year, when I was still developing the game for the Finnish publication. I detail the theoretical underpinnings of the game there pretty well for those interested in that sort of stuff. There's also a pretty interesting report I gave after the Finnish publication on the topic of making your own boardgame in a 100 copy print run. Some of those lessons were directly applicable to this larger English-language print run, some were set aside in favor of higher production values.

    There are also some actual play reports I found by the quick and dirty method. There might be a few more out there, I'm not sure.
    Love Boat Under Siege
    Yikes! Zombies!
    No brain, no pain!
    This time with vampires
    Most fun I've had in a while
    Hmm... looking at those, the game comes across as terribly comedic. When I play it with the local teenagers it's all about cynical social critique, desperate terror and emptiness at the end of the road. Must be something in the water around here.
