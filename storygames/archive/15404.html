    \n I'll second Jerry. I would never use them again.

    It took them two months to send me a prototype deck. I had to harangue them over the phone several times (and I live in the UK, so that was costly). When I did receive the product, the cards were:

    * misaligned (off-center)
    * flimsy
    * washy

    They didn't understand their own RIP and the resulting colouration was awful. If I were you, I'd consider PrintMaster.com. Much better quality, but they don't offer custom tuck boxes. If you want custom tuck boxes, get them from SuperiorPOD and order your cards with PrintMaster.Com. That is, don't use SuperiorPOD unless you have to.

    \n Once i got my digital cutter i said goodbye to using a service. now that i have my own laser capable of printing on the thickest of card, print shops can kiss my grits.

    I had a problem with my print guy once, i just told myself to knock it off.

    So this post is not idle gloating here is my little guide to DIY POD

    Digital cutters are cheap. the cheapest is the silhouette cameo, followed by the Click n cut zing. i'm wanting to upgrade to the zing because it handles thicker card stock better. if you take the time to learn how to use it a digital cutter can produce any paper based game competent, be that cards, stickers, standees if you can design it to work as paper you can make it.

    As for getting a good printer if you do enough research and are careful you can find great printers for dirt cheap on craigslist, there is way more supply of used printers than there is demand. also look into toner refills and generic consumables, so long as you google before you buy you can find quite a bit of good information on what to use and what to avoid, look for forums, not websites that advertise printers.
