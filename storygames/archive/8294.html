\nparse: to examine in a minute way : analyze critically.

What I mean is that we have common states for games:

"What are you playing these days?"
"What games did you recently buy?"
"What games are you reading now?"
"What's on your list to buy?"
"What games are you designing now?"

What I'm thinking here is, "What games do you have, which you are reading and breaking down with the intention of running or playing them soon?"

EDIT (a few days later): Oh, also, if you're parsing something, rather than just a shortlist of games you're reading, I'd totally love to hear your thoughts on what about the game is currently in your headspace (something you're trying to sort out, a rule or setting element that's stuck on you, etc), how you're reading it, etc.

For me, these days I tend to pick up a game, and due to limited reading time, I go through it for a month or two (less time for a smaller game, of course) before racking it up for a campaign in one of my groups. Usually I "parse" 2-4 games at the same time, coming up with nascent situations, ideas for campaigns, and getting familiar with the rules. I usually do my reading for a few minutes a night (usually no more than 20 these days) before bed. At some point, I decide which one I want to run/play *next*, and focus on that game.

So, currently, here's what I'm parsing through, with the intent to run:

HELLAS, aiming for a game with local friends by the end of the year.
*Currently going over the ship rules, thinking of a way to get them into play without getting bogged down too much. Also, thinking of a solid situation to start with after the Big Setting Bang of "Sparta Blows Up".

Vampire the Requiem, having been fired up by replaying the Vampire Bloodlines PC Game (which I believe is The Best Western-Style RPG for the console/pc ever, although I have not played Fallout 3 yet), and recent threads here where people recommended me some books (which I then ordered). Planning on running this for my bi-weekly group, either before or after our upcoming Castle Falkenstein game.
*Currently thinking of a situation, I think I have it (characters are all members of or tied to The Invictus, which in Raleigh/Durham are actually the closest thing that there is to "good guys" in vampire culture, as opposed to The Invictus in other areas). Also going over the rules to see what's changed since Masquerade. Setting is far more open, and its ripe for the GM to sandbox in, but the possibilities can be quite overwhelming. I am very excited to Go Goth again, as it's been fucking ages since I've done some solid WoD gaming.

Empire of Dust, which is simply a fun read. The constant tie-ins to the Dune universe can be a little jarring at times, but I plan on playing this in the next 2-3 months.
*I'm just starting to break this in. I got through the character powers/backgrounds section, which I love (especially the "asterisk'd" backgrounds, which is an AWESOME mechanic to inject Story into the game and characters). Now I'm about to begin learning how the rules work.

And you? Whatcha reading, what are you thinking about it, and when do you plan on putting it into action?

-Andy

    \n I'm parsing 4th edition. A group of friends are playing the game and have invited me to join in. I currently play Magic with them and have fun, and I know they approach 4th in a way that I find completely alien to RPGs, so I'd be interested in playing with them. But, it's been years since I've read an instruction manual as long as D&D, and I'm having trouble sticking with it. Also, it's really really involved in terms of just the quantity of rules. But the biggest hurdle for me is how I interpret the game, which is by and large a tale of genocide and racism. So on the one hand I really want to play with my friend, experience their playstyle, and experience what I consider, so far, to be top-notch rules. On the other hand, the concept or premise of the game is horrifying to me, and those rules, they might be brilliant, but there are just so many of them. I'm a hundred and fifty pages in and they keep going.

    And, as always, Play Unsafe is on my mind. I'm in the middle of a game of a horror-themed Breaking the Ice with my partner, and PU offers me a lot of views or techniques when it comes to that game. So, you know, rereading it, seeing the implications, that kind of thing. I tend to read it pretty much every time I'm gearing up to play a new game where the techniques would be useful.
