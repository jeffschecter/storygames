    \n Also, a "review" has a shred of meaning only if you can manage to PLAY a game for at least one FULL CICLE of its game experience.
    In D&D5 it would be a full career from level 1 to 20.
    In Dungeon World it would be until the end of a campaign front.
    In Dog Eat Dog it would be a 2-3 hours session.

    Maybe the review doesn't need to be so in-depth as a proper game design analysts, but it would still take a lot of time. How do you propose that someone could accomplish such things in the span of 1 week goes beyond me.

    Just reading a rulebook and looking at its art has next to no value, unless you are not reviewing a GAME but an editorial artefact.

    Hiring a large pool of reviewers that offer you 1 article every one or two months is the most sensible solution.

    \n Where?

    Do people like non-gameplay reviews or do they have to put out with it because it's the standard.

    To me, it's like reviewing a board or a video game by reading the manual or doing the tutorial.

    If roleplaying games are to be considered "games", then it's about the synergy/emergence that is created within the system (which the players are a part of). Anyone who had playtested their own game knows that what says on paper doesn't have to be like that in practice because that synergy is hard to predict.

    Just because a lot of reviewers do this, it doesn't mean it's the right thing.

    \n I guess I need to think about this more.

    To be clear, I'm not looking for someone who can take an RPG and review it 1 week later. I'm looking for someone who can write 1 review or article a week.

    So, for instance, you could review Fiasco one week, Apocalypse World the next, meanwhile your group has been playing Dungeon World for the past few weeks and then the next week you review that.

    I review board games, which are very different, and produce about 1.5 reviews a week. I require 4-5 plays typically before I will review something. It usually takes about a month from when I get a game before I review it.

    I'm looking for something similar for an RPG writer. Articles, as opposed to reviews, would be fine as well.

    \n I can see different appeals in most of the formats mentioned. A thorough critique based on extended play would be invaluable for readers who are already interested but want the full scoop; a quick summary of one or two noteworthy features would be great for readers just looking to familiarize themselves with the array of games out there. I'd be interested to see which review-reading needs are already well-served by other channels and which aren't.

    As for feasibility, it's hard for me to imagine your boardgame criteria -- 4-5 recent plays per review -- working out for RPGs. However, if we have a Fiasco review based on 4-5 plays over the past two years, an AW review based on a mini-campaign from last year, and a Dungeon World review from a one-shot last week, I could see that working.

    Personally, of the games I've played, 50% were one-shots, 25% were campaigns, and 25% were in-between. I'd guess that this is fairly typical of the "play a ton of different games" roleplayer.
