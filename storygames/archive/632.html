After reading several threads here, I wrote this up, sort of as it occured to me.


Creating a Character

1) Determine the character's fundament - A character's fundament is basically any ideas expressed by answering several "fundamental questions" (asked by the GM, related to the genre, setting, and situation of the game). Basically, the fundamental questions are a way for the GM to say what is important for the game, and the fundamental answers are a way for the players to decide what is important to them from within that.

2) Write down two "charged" traits - A trait can be anything that describes the character, including skills, attitudes, relationships, etc.



Conflict Resolution
When two or more characters are in conflict over something, use the following steps to resolve who gets what they want.

1) Set the stakes - decide what is at stake in the conflict; who gets what, should they win the conflict.

2) Describe the actions - describe what your character is doing to to accomplish their goals.

3) Determine success or failure - Flip a coin. If the result is heads, the player achieves their stakes, and their opponent doesn't. If the result is tails, the player loses their stakes, and their opponent achieves theirs.

3a) If you succeeded on the flip and the conflict situation is related to the character's fundament, then you can "charge*" one of the character's traits by ignoring the success result and re-flipping the coin. The new result counts. The charged trait must somehow be applicable to the situation.

3b) If you failed on the flip and the conflict situation is related to the character's fundament, then you can re-flip the coin if you "discharge" one of your "charged" traits. The discharged trait must somehow be applicable to the situation.

4) The player narrates the success or failure, constrained by the stakes determined above.


*"Charging" a trait can involve creating an entirely new trait. It doesn't necessarily mean that the character just learned a new skill, but it does mean that some new part of the character was revealed. Could be he just never used the skill before, or the relationship never mattered before.



So, what do you guys think? It's obviously very simple, but I think that it does a couple of neat things (trait charging/discharging, the fundament).
