    \n I absolutely love rolling for random effects. Critical hit/miss tables, random monster and treasure tables, plot twists, etc.

    My favourite example: near the climax of a steampunk in space game, a moon-ankheg swallowed one of our PCs. Her stalwart NPC bodyguard rolled a crit, and then rolled "severe neck wound, x3 damage" on the critical hit table, finishing the monster and in doing so, slicing open its throat and freeing the PC. Fantastic.

    \n I can remember lots of moments where just rolling to see how well someone did something was fun.

    For instance, I remember when we had a non-conflict scene about a Prince training with his old Master at Arms. (Both were PCs.) We decided to roll for their "fight", and the old Master won when the Prince really screwed something up. This turned into a huge character moment, highlighting the Prince's feelings of failure, and heavily informed how the game went forward from that point on.

    \n The Mythic Game Master Emulator has a nifty mechanic to add surprises and twists whenever you roll double numbers (with some restrictions involving a "chaos factor", which I won't get into). You first roll for a "Focus", which could be a "Positive NPC" or "Negative PC" among other things, so you never know what twist will be thrown at you plot wise, or if it's just going to be something that adds to the atmosphere (which would be the "Ambiguous Event" entry). After that you roll for the Action and Subject keywords which should act as inspiration to define the event.

    \n It will not come as a surprise that I love the random chargen and random events in Maid RPG. It gives you the most bizarre characters, and then in play lets the GM or players call up random events to make all kinds of insane things happen. It's exhausting fun trying to keep up with having ninjas attack, the mansion turn into a giant robot, a portal open up to another world, and a witch turn the master into a squirrel in the space of 10 minutes.

    It wound up being an important influence on Magical Burst, and in general these days I seldom make an RPG without at least a couple of D66 charts. (Even Dragon World, my Apocalypse World hack, has a couple.)

    \n Monster species generation using The Metamorphica! Or, you know, using the book to mutate things. But I have more fun making monsters with it.

    Once in the game, I'd say one of my favorite non-combat things to roll is definitely Open Your Brain or Gaze Into The Abyss. I don't really consider them to be conflict, and I love the weirdness it generates. Success, failure, or partial, I love it. I've Gazed and met with the Infernal's demonic patron, which is amazing because I was a Hollow who was a former demon myself. I've sent players into great danger by Opening Their Brain, or given them apocalyptic visions (of the second apocalypse I guess - The Re-apocalypse?) about my tech cult. I just love these moves, and they aren't even fight-y!
