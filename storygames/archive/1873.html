    When I get home. I work for a defence company and cameras are not permitted here. My digital camera is a bit poo also, but I'll see if I can nab Claire's.

    EDIT: A lot of people have expressed concern over the fact that it's 80 pages. Don't worry, at least 10 pages of that are pure artwork and each of the 11 chapters has a page that starts it with very little. The book is divided into 4 sections: The Basics, Advanced BattleLore, The World Of BattleLore and Appendices. Each of the sections is colour coded for easy reference. Plus there's a list of tables and sidebars in the contents menu and even a bit in the intro that says "If you've played Memoir, just read the following paragraphs and then play the first scenario". All the text is big and easy to see and there are tons of example images.

    This is what I want, not a 300 page rulebook.

  • Chris Gardiner
    December 2006
    I got a chance to play a couple of games of this today. It's faaaaaaaantastic.

    It's really quick, and the turn system of using command cards to order just a portion of your troops at a time is brilliant - it means that rather than waiting for your opponent to move his entire army, the battle is resolved as a series of swift, colourful, exciting skirmishes.

    The rules are really simple, but the way morale, retreats, pursuits, terrain, and the twiddles on the command cards work are really satisfying. And I've not even got to play with any of the Lore rules, yet.

    I had ridiculously high hopes for this game, and I'm *still* impressed. It's the wargame I've been looking for for a very long time.
