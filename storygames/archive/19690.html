    \n Indeed, @Nathan_H. I think the word "game" is being used broadly and the word "story" is being used narrowly in the OP.

    One view of "story" holds that every character represents a logical or emotional point of view related to a topic within the author's own head, and therefore the entire story really represents nothing more than the author working their own way through an internal conversation. "Dramatica" takes this view, for instance.

    If you view stories in this light, it's pretty obvious that a game in which multiple people inject their own topics will never map to a story structure - except by luck - without mechanical intervention or social contrivance.

    Fortunately, there are other ways to view stories, and other ways to structure games.
