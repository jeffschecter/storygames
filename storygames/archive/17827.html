    \n Right, Hans pretty much has my rant down pat. I\u2019ve been spoiled by the Geeknights guys. They cram information into a well-rehearsed, hour-long presentation. If you want to ask them questions, they have a forum and email addresses. You sit down, they start, and an hour later you know a lot more than you did. Brilliant.

    This requires preparation, which is especially hard if you have multiple presenters coming to the con from different geographies. Anyway, this is my personal preference after wasting a lot of valuable con time in poorly presented panels.

    \u201cBut\u201d, you say, \u201cwhat if I want to host a discussion with those present? Y\u2019know, really open it up to people.\u201d This can be good, but consider preparing some comments to deliver at the beginning. It can help focus the conversation, ensure the panel gets off to a smooth start, and if the audience starts to derail when you open it up, at least the panel won\u2019t be a total loss. See Meguey Baker\u2019s panel at PAX Prime 2012 on sex in gaming for a great \u201cfrontload and glossary\u201d approach.

    If you do go the part prepped, part open route, try delivering your prepped bit to someone else. Then let them ask questions. Then incorporate the answers to the questions into your prepped comments. The more times you do this, the more efficiently everyone's con time will be spent. You can take the discussion farther, faster. Any question you can't answer optimally in this fashion, is one that a crowd will unlikely be able to answer well on the fly.

    More prep means more work, but it makes for a better panel. If you plan on riffing with some cool peeps the whole hour, let me know ahead of time so I can avoid your panel and just buy you a beer later.

    I'ma put together a presentation on role-playing techniques.

    \n Hey y'all!

    So, here's the deal:

    If you want to run games at Indie Hurricane, go to the main page and create an account (little "register" button under Login in the upper left corner). Then from the main page go to the scheduling page and log in (if you're logged into the main site you'll still need to log in afresh on the schedule site).

    Click "Submit a Proposal" and, well, write a proposal! Click on the pink Indie Hurricane Track if you want to run it in the Indie Hurricane space. It's helpful if the proposal includes a sense of what the experience of play is like, and other expectation-setting info like intended age of audience (mature, teen, all ages, etc). The short description is what will appear in the program book, so keep that in mind.

    I schedule the Indie Hurricane games in set time-blocks to minimize schedule conflicts and overlaps, and give us all lunch and dinner breaks at the same time. The breakdown is:

    MORNING 10AM-1PM

    LUNCH BREAK

    AFTERNOON 2PM-6PM

    DINNER BREAK

    EVENING 7PM-MIDNIGHT

    Games that are shorter than the timeslot are fine; I'll schedule them to begin with the rest of the block. Request otherwise if you'd like! Also, it's possible to fit truly short events it's possible to fit multiples within a block, e.g. two 2-hour games in the Afternoon block.

    I'm going to schedule panels, by default, in the morning block, primarily at noon. If you'd like a panel at a different time let's talk, but I'd like to keep things so that panels conflict with scheduled games to a minimal amount.

    As for panel topics, that's up to you! If people submit 'em, I'll schedule 'em! This thread is a good place to bump skulls about what topics people are excited to collaborate on.

    If you have any questions or difficulties, ask away. The con is deceptively close, so I'd like to see event proposals go up right away.
