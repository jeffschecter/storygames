    I assumed the nature pix would be of minimal interest to you guys who do "gritty" games, but someone here might like them.

    I suppose you know that all images taken by US government agencies like NASA* are automatically public domain? Google "NASA photos" and you'll get a number of starting points. Gimp-Savvy.com (http://gimp-savvy.com/PHOTO-ARCHIVE/) also gives 27,000 NASA, National Oceanic and Atmosphere Administration, and US Fish and Wildlife Service pix. Another portal is here: http://www.firstgov.gov/Topics/Graphics.shtml.

    There's also PDPhotos (http://pdphoto.org/), which is a collection of public domain pix. I found it by Googling "public domain photos", and there's some other good stuff when you do that too. Of course you're way ahead of me there, I'm sure.

    Oh, and stock.xchg (http://www.sxc.hu/), which I've used for photo reference for art in the past. Almost a quarter of a million free stock photos there now.

    Wikimedia Commons (check also http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_domain_image_resources, which links to a number of large sites), Creative Commons, probably Library of Congress - there's a ton of good stuff out there with no strings attached. There's even a search engine for free/copyleft pix: http://yotophoto.com/.

    Shooting your own stock, though, is a lot of fun if you can do that for the image you want.

    * though probably not US government agencies like, say, the CIA.
