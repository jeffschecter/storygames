    \n I can't remember a lot of the bangs I used in the past but I'll give it a shot.

    One of my favorites was the character's stumbling across a large stone door in the ground (like the hatch in Lost). No idea what was inside - I Mountain Witch tricked the hell out of it to excellent effect.

    Another solid one I stole from someone off the internet is a pear tree. Ask the players what happens when they eat the pears; it's really that simple.

    Lastly, the characters come across an archway on the hillside. Adjacent to archway is a line of gray ash extending as far as the eye can see on both sides (like a wall). The ash was said to form a wall in that anyone who crosses or who goes through the archway without first giving up that which they cherish most - would suffer greatly. There was a basin near the archway to offer up what you cherished most.

    \n One technique I've used successfully in several games is to have the Witch or a minion make reference to all of the characters in the group...save one. When used at the right time, it can really sow the seeds of discord within the group.

    In the WW2 Nazi castle game, it was dogtag numbers written on a chalkboard in the radio room. In the Apocalypse Now game, it was a Vietnamese woman calling out their nicknames over a hidden loudspeaker in the jungle. But you could just as easily use names carved into headstones in a graveyard or beautifully done ink drawings on rice paper.

    Remember that when you are running this game as a con scenario, you need to skip to the awesome. I typically don't even use chapters. When your trust is gone it is GONE.

    \n From one game I ran:
    (AP over on the Forge here)

    These aren't bangs, they're encounters, but they set up play into which you can throw some good bangs.

    First encounter:
    The ronin encounter a wolfpack. The pack leader can speak, and orders the ronin to turn back, for the way is closed to them. The wolf's first words are 'which of you is the leader of your band?' to force some kind of response to that issue.

    Second encounter (from an AP by Ron Edwards):
    Some tengu stalk the ronin\u2019s camp at night, and since the ronin all look alike to them, they ask the sentry if he or she is the one who "made the deal." Another ronin hears this exchange.

    Third encounter:
    The ronin are lost in a maze of tangled roots and dark trees. A forest sprite offers to lead the Ronin to the mountain path, but only if he is given a token of the most personal significance by each ronin.

    Fourth encounter:
    Blind Man\u2019s Cottage: The blind man lives alone in a small cottage. He will invite the ronin to take tea with him and spend the night under his roof. He tells them the Witch can corrupt the weak, and any who are too weak will inevitably betray their fellows. But at night his head detaches and flies off to feed on human flesh!

    Those were the only planned encounters I used - as you can see in the AP, past this point the players were driving everything. Below is cut'n'pasted from my wrap-up post:

    "My experience was this:
    * importance of GM prep and guidance in Act 1: high
    * importance of the above in Act 2: fair
    * importance of the above in Act 3 and 4: low to nil

    The only other advice I have for GMs in the endgame is to keep clearing space around the characters for them to resolve their own conflicts. I could have kept throwing bad guys at the ronin all day but the clear focus of interest for everyone was the ronin's relationships. Adversity in the very final stages should be applied carefully, so as not to upstage the interactions between the ronin."

    Hope that's of use!

    \n I have six pages of Mountain Witch notes and ideas culled from various threads over the years; too large (and loosely attributed) to post but I'm happy to email a copy to anyone who wants one. Email me at cdr at telemancy.com for a copy.

    Abilities I've seen (by no means comprehensive): Spider-like climbing, Bow, Doctor, Dutch Knowledge, Iaijutsu, Limitless Sorrow, Implacable Dignity, Tengu Blood, Tough as Nails, Priest, Sorcerer.

    Tim had a great article at http://www.story-games.com/forums/comments.php?DiscussionID=2150 on how to squeeze a game into 5 hours.

    My prop notes for my 2006 DunDraCon run "Cherry Blossom Bloodbath" are at http://www.indie-rpgs.com/forum/index.php?topic=18873.0

    I had great success using the 5 demo characters plus I added a sixth, Dragon Zodiac, and seated the players so enemy was across, allies two away on either side, to remember easily.

    Here's my run list, although depending on time and what curves the players throw using their fates, I'll drop or shuffle or add as needed, especially in the later parts.

    \t1.Partial 3.Regular 4.Critical 5.Double
    Weak\tchapter\t out
    Able\tflesh\t chapter out
    Strong\tflesh\t chapter permanent out

    Act 1: Introduction (1 chapter)
    Act 2: Building Tension (1-2 chapters)
    Act 3: Fates Revealed (1-2 chapters)
    Act 4: Conclusion (1 chapter)
    Epilogues

    Night before @ inn, intro characters, each showing an item significant to fate. Free food, sake, women. A Geisha begs one ronin to return her brother (family debt). Shogun's emissary.

    Dawn. Praying (or not) at shrine at base of Mt. Fuji. Monk (only survivor of a previous expedition) mentions another band making the attempt (linked to fate) with a gunman.

    Snow coming, a mother begs them to save her little girl from an Ogre (Able), which will delay them. (Mother reluctantly in league with Ogre.)

    Ogre's belt has items each ronin has lost at some time in their lives, that belonged to someone important to you. Who was it that used to to own the item and how do you suppose the item got to where it is now?

    Wolves (weak) at battlefield, which can be avoided or fought. Surviving brother, wounded, warns of forest of confusion and leader who betrayed them to the wolves and escaped.

    Waiting out snow storm in temple. Shrine offerings linked to fate. Shrine maiden, duellist Tengu. Dreams foreshadowing fate,

    Night watch. Tengu (weak) "Are you the one that made the deal?"

    Remains of an army that died in avalanche

    Another band making the attempt (linked to fate), gunman.

    Cave shortcut with giant spider (strong)

    Yasha, giant vampire bats (weak) on narrow stairway hewn from rock

    Avalanche

    Gate with two heads on poles, which someone recognizes

    Blood drinking forest with spiders and zombies (p103, weak)

    Gates guarded by two Shishi, lion-dogs (strong)

    Magical cat (weak or able) luring to Giant's cave (strong).

    Magical crow gives a key with note "I trust our deal is now concluded."

    Dreams and dreameaters (Baku, p101, any)

    Master of spear guarding rickety rope & bamboo bridge over chasm: No swords shall pass. Willing to let them pass if they throw their swords into the chasm. (The sword is the soul of the samurai.)

    Archers on walls (weak)

    Ogre ambush (Able)

    Arriving at the mansion, where they are greeted and offered food, hot springs, a night's rest. All the servants are children, commanded by mute dolls Tokuturo-kun and Otoku-chan (p106, weak).

    Shogun's emissary.

    Garden of Regret, and Yurei (ghost, p107, any).

    Reflecting Pool, in which each can see the face of one they have deeply wronged. Koi as souls of those who drowned themselves in shame. Duelist challenge in the snow.

    Yuki-Ona, the snow maiden (Able or Strong, p106). Servants are children of war spirited away.

    O-Yanma, Mountain Witch (Strong). 2 Nio (p104, strong)
