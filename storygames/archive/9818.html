\n I would like some public domain maps, which I can publish in a book. Not just maps of areas, but plans of houses and large buildings.

I'm personally interested in maps from the early 20th century but, really, if you've got anything interesting*, post it here.

Not Creative Commons, please! I want stuff I can freely publish. (Don't argue about this one, because the argument will go on forever. Just stick to public domain stuff.)

    \n Try the Library of Congress American Memory collection. Be sure to look within multiformat collections. I did a keyword search for "architectural drawing" and found loads of kickass stuff.

    Sanborn fire protection maps are fantastic primary sources for this sort of thing, and many American libraries have local atlases in their electronic collections. Here are some from Missouri. The pre-1923 stuff is public domain. If you need a neighborhood, or higher level view of strteets or factories, these are pure gold.
