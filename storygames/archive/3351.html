\n So I'm working on this idea for a game.

And in the game, each player builds a story about her character in response to questions from the other players (not necessarily characters).

It's a game about telling stories.

But the characters themselves don't direct relate, although the mechanics make each story a collaborative effort.

So it's not a story game, per se, because it seems to me that most story games presume players taking roles of characters which then interact with each other.

But it's a game about stories. And so I'm not sure what it is, really. It's not an RPG, but it's a game in which you play roles...

I guess I'm asking if anyone knows of games along those lines. Maybe I just need to write the darn thing up so you can see it.

    \n I'm currently working on a campaign (currently unconnected to a game/system, although Scion, Changeling, and GURPS In Nomine are all possibilities) in which the "main" characters only have the very barest of interactions, and the players take on different minor roles in different portions of the "big story" that includes everyone. It's highly outre' and could definitely use some grounding by people talking about "ranges" on the scale from "everyone together all the time" to "totally independent stories" and how they moved in one direction or another.
