\n So I still check Praxis fairly often (now that I'm back, in general) and I'm noticing that it's sort of dead. As in, "so dead, even thread originators don't come back to read replies."

Perhaps--especially with The Forge getting winterized--Praxis should be folded back into Story Games? Benefits include one log in, more eyes on new ideas, a centralized archive (i.e., for search). I can think of no downsides--we already have "design threads" cropping up in here; and one can ignore design threads as readily as D&D or jeepform threads (i.e., scan on by, nothing to see here). It might be a bit of a bitch to pull in the existing posts, or it might be trivial (I don't know, with Vanilla).

    Posted By: J. WaltonEven when Praxis was more active, it wasn't always especially useful, and that was historically also true of the Indie Game Design and First Thoughts forums at the Forge.
    \nBased on my experience on Praxis (and to a lesser extent in the later years of the Forge), I think that "first thoughts" type forums are poisonous. They basically encourage people to start easy-to-digest posts which attract a lot of attention from people who want to be helpful, but then leave the helpful people in the lurch when the OP almost inevitably doesn't follow up. This causes the helpful people to burn out. I'm not yet completely convinced that forums are unworkable as a basis for a game design community, but the ways that have been tried haven't been super effective. Even if there was a good way to do a game design forum (which might be an interesting discussion topic), I'm skeptical that the S-G forum culture, with all the snarkiness, etc., would be a good match.

    \n Yeah, that was basically it.

    Story Games the Community started up. Got new people. Kept its focus on gameplay and games. And of course, since it was kind of an offshoot from an earlier Forge, there was some design talk here and there (mostly people finishing up some work or other and saying "Here's some files, if you're interested give it a shot!"

    After a few years, there was suddenly an influx of "designers". People whose "Join" message wasn't so much "Hey I like (game X) and want to talk about it", but rather pretty much 75% of the Join messages for 2-3 months were "I'm working on a game design and I want some feedback".

    The threads about gaming simply choked the main page. There was a point where over half the threads on the top page were basically people posting the same game design shit they do on Livejournal or other personal blog, but just here in front of other people. And not even a lot of "Hey can someone help me on this specific thing?" (which has always been inobtrusive), it was more "Here's another post where I talk about my aim for this game I'm working on: (INSERT WEB LINK)." Basically, not looking for feedback but looking for lots of attention. Or perhaps using the forum to talk about the design they eventually want to get around to starting instead of actually Starting it ("Resistance").

    And many of these Designers weren't participating at all in the forum in threads about gameplay or whatever, they were simply posting a public blog-like-thing. It became parasitic, which... "meh"... but on top of that it was drowning out the real game and play discussion which was always the heart of this place. This place started to look like RPGNet Tangency. Or worse, the RPGNet game design forum. (zing!)

    Since this forum doesn't have a mode for splitting out groups of categories into sub-pages (Note: Vanilla 2.0 does!) in the same database, letting the discussion continue here wasn't a viable option.

    For about a full year after Jason and I diagnosed the problem, the solution was "Go to the Forge or somewhere else."
    The response was, "But I like the people here!"
    To which my reply was "...so? Again, take it somewhere else."

    But after a while, I got many, many pleas in whispers and emails to allow game design to be discussed here, and from people who were pretty active in the actual gameplay/gaming threads. Many of them just didn't like the atmosphere or environment at the Forge, or had a series of unfortunate interactions which led them to avoid the place.

    Since I could split the DB and create a totally unmoderated zero-work zero-upkeep area for design discussion, I did so. Basically stating from the beginning that it was no longer my responsibility to keep it active or alive, it was up to the people who wanted to do the design discussion. It sort of went that way in the beginning, but over time it gradually died. Part of that was because of other, better places to discuss design (G+). Part of it was that as the Forge closed down various sub-forums, it started a mini-renaissance there again of more activity.

    So, in the current form of S-G, we won't see bottom-up game design threads here. If you want to post a playtest, this is the place. If you need help with a very specific thing ("Hey, anyone know good books on Mesopotamian culture? I need some reference materials." "Hey, this rule doesn't work, can anyone suggest an alternate?" etc) I've allowed it to happen here. But we're not sliding back to the days of a hundred people all talking past each other in design threads created here because they couldn't get enough eyes on their blog.

    (cont)

    \n Now, having said that, we're not too far away from an upgrade to Vanilla 2.0. When that happens, I've been thinking about creating a game design space again, since Vanilla in 2.0 can actually be split into sub-forums: The game play/talk of S-G proper categories in one area, and the game design and publishing categories in another. One database. More inter-connected. But still, not all meshed together with Design and Play threads in one place.

    However, please see this bit from Jason:\n
    Posted By: Jason MorningstarYou guys should talk about how to engineer a good game design forum. That is a worthy goal.
    \nSo, very recently, the "mod staff" (got to think of a better word) were inspired by John Stavropolous' simple but elegant idea:
    * We have specific goals for Story Games. Success conditions. People we want to "play" here. Behaviors we want to encourage, and behaviors we want to discourage.
    So why don't we look at the forums as if they were a game that we are in the process of perpetually designing?

    BOOM: Off we went. A lot of things have changed in the background, and we're working on a lot of new tools, a few rules, and new behaviors to make Story Games a more ideal place: For our ideals, anyway, not Everyone's ideals.



    ...So why doesn't someone try that for a game design discussion area? Maybe one person grabs a few trusted friends, and determines what kinds of behaviors they do and do not want to see in such a community, what the goals are, what a successful site looks like, etc.

    I'm so not interested in that: I'm not interested in design that much, and if I do dust some off and start again, I've got (better to say "built up over the years", it's not like they fell in my lap) a group of friends that would provide me that feedback. So it's not a need for me.

    But if it's a need for others, I'd be happy to post a link to someone's forum for game design off-site if I like the look/feel of it, and offload it to that site entirely.
    Alternately, if some stable human being comes along and emails me saying "Hey, I got three friends together and we've got some fresh ideas for a sustainable game design forum (here they are): Can we Have/Run Praxis so that we can tear it down and rebuild it to be this kind of place?"
    ...if the ideas sounded cool and again those folks had a track record of being stable, and they had a small group of people willing to make it happen, I'd totally turn over the keys to Praxis to a discrete admin team (or be inclined to re-merge* it after the Vanilla 2 S-G upgrade happens).

    ...but no one's been doing that, in the X years it's been open. Everyone's waiting for Someone Else to make that first move.

    -Andy

    * Re "merge", it's more like lock the old forum and archive it, start over fresh on the new S-G site. Merging/splicing the two databases back together isn't going to happen, for technical/time reasons.

Bricks are not actually made of gold. Pies are actually made of gold." if you are good at becoming incoherent, do so now - have fun with it "My gosh, brother Maynard said coquettishly, pies my five for another round! Midas milks the dog after market."

      \nAdvance the clock whenever the players miss any deadline set by themselves or by the Computer. At any time, on a missed roll, the Computer may add new rules or deadlines.

      \nAt 3:00 the walls start to shake, and any players who are still alive receive a condition (i.e. shattered). At 6:00 players receive another condition. At 9:00 players receive another condition. At midnight all players die.

If the player then successfully makes the change requests, then the computer congratulates them in even more masqueraded voices than were originally used, and that player is declared to be a Winner. Winners are uploaded to the "Winner's Lounge" via an extremely painful process.

      \n I like game design stuff, but not on a level of "should I have six attributes or eight?" or "should my sand-elves know blood magic?" or "what about rolling Connected instead of Heartless in Emotional Rescue Phase?" Fine-tuning stuff like that for someone else is deadly dull when you haven't even played the game and that's why most design threads anywhere don't have much responses. Discussion should be on a level of creative agendas, where everyone can participate and have some general insights and the designer can walk away with a clarified idea of what he wants to design. Examples: "tell me about great fear mechanics", "what makes investigation games tick?" and "pros and cons of asymmetrical design". Of course, that sort of stuff doesn't really need to be tagged as "design" at all...
