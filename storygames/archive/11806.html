\n Sometimes they Win is a game where you kill your friends (in character of course). I posted up the early alpha version on my web site At the Table for people to look at and play. I will be playtesting it in May at Camp Nerdly but would love reviews from those who read it or play it before then.

The idea came to me in a nightmare. Players play themselves with normal everyday concerns except that at least one of them has gone off the deep end and beguins to kill the other players. There are rules for the attacker, the victim and the potential victims. The game focuses on the killer chasing down and hurting the victim until the killing moment where the victim finally has a chance to turn the tables and kill their persuer. The act of doing so however drives them over the edge and they become the killer.

I have a few problems that I would like others to look at. The first is the potential victims/former victims. I want to make sure that those players don't feel left out while the chase is going on but I also don't want to focus on them too much as the chase is the focus of play. Watch the PV/FVs in play and see if there are ways to increse their involvement.

The second problem is, "Why am I killing my friends!?!" question. For my part I don't care but it would be nice to have some fictional reason to base the game off of. The quote that I put at the start of the game sums it up for me, that being "Monsters are real, and so are ghosts, they live inside us and sometimes they win." I don't want to go too far from that idea so I am looking for a way to make that jibe.

The last problem is the dice. In my head the dice should work out so that if the person being chased allows themselves to be horribly injured they have a good shot at survival while if they try and stay whole they are asking for death. Let me know how this works in play! Also let me know if I should add numerical weight to the injuries that the attacker and victim recieve.

Thanks for your input!

J.B. Mannon
