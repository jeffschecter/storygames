    \n It feels like you got the same idea as "samber\xe4ttande" here in Sweden. Samber\xe4ttande is roughly translated into "collaborative storytelling", but the Swedish language can take several words to form new words, and it has a lot of homonyms. The word "sam" can come from samarbete (collaboration) but it can also come from samh\xf6righet (togetherness). Ber\xe4ttande means narration.

    So the word samber\xe4ttande is "the feeling of togetherness while narrating in a collaborative manner". It describes well, at least, what I feel when playing collaborative storytelling.

    I liked idea of "structured playtime". That's what I design games for these days.
