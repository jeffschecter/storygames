Eric Provost
February 2006 edited February 2006 in Story Games

After the bit of awesome help I got here in relabeling "Creepiness" I thought I'd weigh in with another, slightly more cumbersome problem.

In the Infected players regularly author locations, places they'd like their characters to go to relax or arm up or otherwise add to the story. Each location has it's four statistics and one can author "sub-locations" to alter those statistics. Sub-locations are kind of like nested locations. They exist as part of the larger location that they're authored to modifiy.

For example;
A Location of Jim Bob's Army Navy might be authored to have a good supply of firearms. Another player might then author a sub-location within Jim Bob's Army Navy called The Back Room and then author it's statistics so as to make Crazy Weapons (explosives, rocket launchers, KatanaChucks, etc) available.

It's one of the niftiest features of the game. But the issue is the lableing. Some rules affect the 'base' location (Jim Bob's in this case), some rules affect the specific sub-location (The Back Room),and some rules affect the entire area (the location and all it's sub-locations).

So, what I'd like to do is to re-label the Base Locations, Sub-Locations, and Areas, into something a little more palatable. Something that's maybe a bit more thematic and maybe something that slides off the tongue a little easier. As there are cards in play that need these labels, something with fewer letters would be a wonderful bonus too.

Any ideas, brainstorming, or spitballing would be appreciated.

-Eric

    I've been totally shooting from the hip. So I read the PDF and that helped quite a bit!

    I think Location is the key element to name here. Let's assume for the moment that most Areas will be buildings (although naturally the rules allow for different kinds of Color).

    "Area" is easily understandable: it's the whole of Jim Bob's Army Navy. It's the building, with all the rooms in it.

    "Sub-Location" is easily understandable: it's a Back Room or a Lavatory or the Basement. It's a room or a section of the building.

    "Location" is... both? But different? I think once you get the name for this, the others will fall into place.

    It feels kind of like Location should be the room via which you enter the building. When I author Jim Bob's Army Navy, let's say I frame a scene of breaking into the sales floor through the front display window.

    Maybe a good term would be "Threshold." It's the place where you cross from Everywhere Else into this Area (Site, Location, Map). It is the first room where you realize the rules are different here. Sub-Locations (Chambers, Cells, Rooms) are what you encounter beyond the Threshold.

    So at this point I'm fond of Site / Threshold / Chamber.
