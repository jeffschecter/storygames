  • timfire
    December 2006
    Actually, there was a brief report of a eight person game a while ago. It seemed to go well.

    I don't think having repeating Fates would be all that bad, since different players often come up with very different concepts for the same Fate. In lieu of having two decks, I would suggest you break the group into groups of 3 and 4. Deal one group normally. After that's settled, have them return the cards to the deck and re-shuffle. Then deal the second group the same way. There you go.

    Be sure to post about it if it does happen! I would to get a couple reports of large games to see how it handles. In general, tMW is one of a minority of games that works better with more players. But right now I can't say whether or not there's a drop-off point where more begins to detract from play.
  • timfire
    December 2006
    Sorry, another note---is this intended to be a one-shot, or a mini-campaign? I ask because I've noticed with more players, there's more content floating around, so it takes longer to resolve everything. For one-shots, I think it's easier if you try to keep the number of players on the low end. Large one-shots are still possible, just harder. If it's intended for a mini-campaign, than it's no problem.
