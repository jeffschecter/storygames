\n Last week at the Italian "Lucca Comics and Games" convention debuted four new italian translations of Indie Games:
- Polaris (Janus Design)
- Non Perdere il Senno (Don't Lose Your Mind, Janus Design)
- Annalise (Narrattiva)
- Sporchi Segreti (Dirty Secrets, Narrattiva)

    \n\nrenatoram \n\n\n
    \n
    \n\nNovember 2009 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n I think there is not yet really an "average" indie game in Italy. We and the Narrattiva people, and now the Coyotes, have basically created the indie scene/public/whatever out of thin air a cople of years ago.
    \n
    \nObviously there was someone who knew these games before, but we were a very small minority (just check how many books us indie games sold to italy 3 years ago...).
    \n
    \nI think there is something going on, though, in general. The numbers of several things are a bit weird and do not scale with respect to the size of the US (population wise). The Italian top selling comics are 96 pages, black and white, and sell hundreds of thousands of copies (I think Dylan Dog is still selling something like 400k copies/month).
    \n
    \nLucca Comics and Games it the biggest Italian fair/con and encompasses boardgames, role-playing games, comics (manga, us comics and european ones), and recently even videogames.... I'm sure it's small with respect to GenCon, and this year 140.000 (that's 140thousand) tickets were sold over the 4 days. The town of Lucca has 80k inhabitants, for reference
    \n
    \nWe've just run out of the second reprint of DRYH, so I don't have the precise numbers but that's several hundred copies sold in a county that up to 2 years ago did not know indie games at all. I think we sold more than a hundred copies of Polaris there in the 4 days of fair, and so on (again, no hard numbers 'cause I was not in the selling part of the booth, I demoed almost constantly). We do very small print runs because we have the option, and because we don't really have the money to do bigger ones, but we are fully expecting to continue selling these books for years: I don't think DRYH sales have really slowed, for example.
    \n
    \nInterNosCon, that was born as something only slightly bigger than a family-and-friends-minicon had 80 attendees this year, and next year will have 300 places (for people that pay in advance).
    \n
    \nI don't know, but something is going on

    \n That would clearly be outside of our agreement with Ben, but on the other hand I guess if Ben wanted to do a new edition in that format it would not be too much work adapting our layout (and/or finding an agreement with Tazio, who did some additional illustrations).
    \n

As an impact on one's pocket, 30 eur in Italy is more or less the same as 30 usd in the US.
