\n We had a lot of fun playing InSpectres as a Sherlock Holmes'ian mystery (the Case of the Peripatetic Pharaoh) but we wanted to try using the same method for other genres.

The result: InSpectres in Spaaace. Cosmic revelation and exploration of the vast unknown, a la 2001, Ringworld, Contact.

The Dauntless: Mission to Telos-3



The first game was a win so we're going to take the Dauntless out for another spin tonight.

    \n It was the other way around: we played InSpectres doing British yarn mysteries and had a really good time, and were brainstorming other ways to use the "players define the truth" method. The "cosmic mysteries" genre jumped out at us.

    Whenever we played Shock we always had a great time brainstorming the shock itself -- actually more than playing out what happened with the protagonists. The InSpectres in Spaace! games exploit that fun for the whole game: the ideas that emerged from organic collaboration are surprisingly good. And no prep

    Posted By: MortalityJust out of curiosity, I'm now assuming 'in spaaace' is some kind of cultural reference that's missed me?
    \nI believe it's from the Muppet Show's Pigs in Space.

    Once upon a time, I plotted a histogram of the number of Google hits for "spaaace" through something ridiculus like "spaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaace". I think "spaaaaace" was the most common, but the numbers slowly start increasing when you reach the far end of the Long Tail.

    It's also funny to see Google offer suggestions like: You searched for "spaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaace". Did you mean "spaaaaaaaaace"?
