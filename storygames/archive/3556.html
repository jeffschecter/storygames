\n So in the next two months (perhaps as early as two weeks, at Go Play SE) I plan to run Legends of Alyria. It is awesome in its combustible hippieness, and I really, really like the thought behind a lot of the rules. Like, frex, it's easier to win conflicts if you do Evil Things (which reinforces the good-vs-evil struggle the author stands behind).

But I need help. Clock Hour Names are not doing it for me, and I need to make an adjustment so that I can run it/play without going nuts. I've read the book twice but can not remember what Terce, Lauds, and Matins are, which is higher, etc.

I started thinking of a Dark Sun conversion briefly (more on that in a sec) and came to a conclusion that will work for the game and keep its sensibilities, but I need a little help from someone who's played the game.

Here's the scale, from highest to lowest, and the die roll on a 1d10 it corresponds to:
Sext 10
Terce/None 8-9
Prime/Vespers 5-7
Lauds/Compline 3-4
Matins 2
Devils Hour 1
Note that the abilities and virtue go only from Sext to Matins, Sext being the closest to "Good", and Matins being the closest to "Evil", Prime/Vespers being "Neutral", with Devil's Hour being outside that scale as a Big Bad Failure.

I'm good with pictures and ideas, so I was thinking of the following scale instead of the one that's confusing the hell out of me:
UNICORN
something goodish
THE CITADEL (or the SEA OF MIST)
something badish
DRAGON
DEVIL'S HOUR

So what can I use to fill in the blanks? And is there something better for "Neutral"? Some that I was thinking of using:
The Keepers (for goodish)
Blessed (for goodish)
The Misbegotten (for baddish)
Outsiders (for baddish)

Any thoughts?

    \n Once upon a time, Alyria used moon faces instead of Clocks. The lowest phase was "New" and the highest was "Full". Instead of Devil's Hour, you had the Weeping Moon, which is a red moon. I explain why this changed over here. That being said, when I play Alyria, I still use Moon Phases.

    So, here are the correspondences:

    Sext---Full
    Terce/None---Gibbous
    Prime/Vespers---Half Moon
    Lauds/Compline---Crescent
    Matins 2---New Moon
    Devils Hour---Weeping Moon

    This way, the more "good" a Trait is, the brighter it is.

    Posted By: AndyWhat happens when two PCs want to team up in a conflict against a third character?
    \nNo bonus or anything. Treat each pairing as a separate conflict, but narrate each exchange together.

    Example: the classic combat example, right? Let's see. Have to have a good Alyria vibe for this.... I know! A battle on a catwalk in the Web in the middle of a lightning storm! Yeah, that's the ticket. A Digger Paladin and a warrior of the Noble Houses are facing off against a dragon cultist. For simplicity, they're all using Force.

    You would define the Digger Paladin vs. dragon cultist as one conflict and the Noble warrior vs. dragon cultist as another conflict. Then roll the first exchange in each conflict. Figure out your winner and loser in each exchange, then narrate the whole thing together.

    I don't think that I discuss this in the book. I'll cop to that being an error on my part.

    Also, while you're here, do note that a character doesn't have to be present to be in conflict. Minions, weapons, and the like are all treated as extensions of the character. So, if teh Evil D00d sent his henchmen to look for Our Hero, you should still use the Evil D00d's Attributes and Traits, because it's really a conflict between Our Hero and the Evil D00d. The henchmen are irrelevant.

    Seth Ben-Ezra
