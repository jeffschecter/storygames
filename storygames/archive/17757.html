\n Hi all,

My default game format is the 4-10 session run. I set out to run that many, and pretty much we keep to it. This is good - I don't have the patience for anything more, certainly not as a GM, barely even as a player. But what do you call one of those runs?\n
    \n
  • A "campaign"? - sounds too grandiose, and at odds with what most people would expect
  • A "mini-campaign"? - cumbersome, and sounds belittling; this form is at least the equal of long-term play, on aesthetic, moral and spiritual grounds
  • A "run"? - I've not heard many people use that word for it
  • A "story"? - not bad, but suggests a linear plotline which is the thing I hate most in the world
\nAnyone got any comments on those options? Any better ideas?


Rob

    \n My full-length campaigns are usually around five sessions. I can take a campaign that usually takes around a year to play and narrow it down to five sessions (four hours each). I don't believe in the "20 minutes of fun for 4 hours", but in hard scene framing and always making something happen, usually parallel to other situations.

    So I would call it a campaign. It's not about how long it takes, but how much has happened.

    \n I can't suggest a better name, but what I can say is: it's an interesting format, possibly the "best" one, and sure the longest I'm interested in doing since I gave up on D&D3.
    Also:
    \u2014 both "short" (5 eps.) and "long" (9 eps.) PTA "seasons" are part of it.
    \u2014 there's a huge difference between a 4-sessions game and a 10-sessions one. I sure have done many 4-sessions games and very few 10-sessions ones. 10 sessions feels like a really long campaign to me these days.
    \u2014 I can think of some games which are made exactly for 4 sessions of play, including The Mountain Witch and Grey Ranks.
    \u2014 in my mind/play habits, 2-4 sessions games are a thing, while 5+ sessions games are another (Apocalypse World, Sorcerer, etc.). The latter are often shortened to just 4 sessions out of necessity (except PTA, which I always play in the 5-eps. mode).

    Edited to add:
    I guess that would have been one of the key differences with those old style wargame campaigns though: they would generally be designed with some sort of end-state in mind.

    It's also kinda intriguing to think of what you're suggesting as being somewhat similar to the old style D&D adventure modules/dungeons. One of those would likely be played through and completed in that 4-10 sessions you were tallking about, especially the older ones which are mostly site looting/exploration with a little social mongering added in. Both The Keep on the Borderlands and The Isle of Dread, those two old well-known D&D modules almost certainly would be (largely) completable within that sort of time frame of 4-10 sessions.
