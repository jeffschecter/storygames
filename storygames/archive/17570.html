\n A lot of the basic Monsterhearts moves give mechanical outcomes without telling you what actually happens in the fiction. An obvious example:

"When you turn someone on, roll with hot. On a 10 up, take a String against them. [...]"

Now, I know that this was a careful and deliberate choice on Joe's part.

What I'm curious about is:

When you play, how do you handle this?

If I turn your character on with a 10+, and gain a String on them... what happens in the fiction?

Is it completely up to you, as the move's description indicates--you could just say, "pshaw!" and walk away, even though the String tells us that, yeah, I got to you--or do you, in practice, place certain limitations or certain expectations on how you can respond?

How does your group handle this? How do you wish your group handled it, if that's different?

    \n I dont think there needs to be any story consequence of turning someone on, and no imposed response either. For me it signifies that the character is turned on, distracted, affected by the person making the move, but that doesnt mean it activates one of the 7-9 responses. Its produced the desired effect of turning them on, and left them vulnerable which is that its intended to do.

    \n When it gets used on my characters, either my own PC's or NPC's if I'm MC, I generally try to have them respond appropriately. They either blush and stammer or make rude comments, depending on their personality and the situation.

    When I use it on others, I often end up spending the string I get from it immediately afterward in order to get them to do something for me, so that process of string-based bartering, and the results from that, often overlaps their characters response.

    \n When I'm MCing, I always ask the player whose character got turned on to describe the reaction visible on their character's face - "Like, are you looking at him, or do you look away...? What is your mouth doing, what are your eyes doing, are you blushing?"

    I feel like that's an on-genre treatment of the String transferal.

    And then when i'm playing, i want to chime in with details about that, too.

    The each-of-you-lose-Strings-on-one-another result for shut someone down is trickier, but i still like to give it a fictional nod if possible.
