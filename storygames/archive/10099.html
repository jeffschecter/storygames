\n I've been thinking about universal magic systems recently (like the ones in Mage and Ars Magica), and most of them seem to be powered primarily by the will or stamina of the caster. Sure, there are things (like Tass and Vis) that give an external power boost, but most of what you're doing and how you're doing it comes from your character. You can't do magic without it.

Are there any fairly universal magic systems (ones that cover a multitude of different magic types and effects) where the magic is contained in objects, rituals, and actions rather than within the character? Where anyone who has the right ritual and ingredients has the power? I can't think of any games like that right now.

    \n Mage 2nd Ed. could easily work for this. Foci + Ritual + Tass use as per a specific paradigm and presto! universal magic system that meets your requirements. Have the cost in Tass equal the total sum of necessary spheres. No one has spheres as stats just arete. Difficultly can equal the highest sphere.
    \n
    \nLike so:
    \nExample Spell
    \nPrime: 000 Mind: 0 Correspondence: 0000
    \nCost in Tass: 8
    \nDifficulty: 4
    \nRitual: Do the hooky poky
    \nFocus: Consecrated dancing shoes
    \n
    \nI roll my Arete, assuming that I can preform the necessary ritual with the ritual objects, pay the cost in Tass (things to be sacrificed) and if four of my dice show 7 or higher than it works. Yay!
    \n
    \nAlternatively, any game that allows you to narrate cool powers on the fly (Dogs and SotC, IIRC; there are many others) always already has a universal magic system.

    \n Play Mage, make Avatars into spirit NPCs, and have each player hand his Avatar's charsheet to the player to his left. Power now comes from those NPCs. Or play Wraith, emphasizing the power you get by letting your Shadow loose, which is the same basic thing.

    Unknown Armies has its ritual magic system, the name of which is escaping me, but all you need is the knowledge and the ingredients, as specified. The rituals tend to be less powerful than the foomancy powers, but that's not a strict requirement.

    Sorcerer has already been mentioned, but it's this, 100%. If it's not general enough for you, add a few demon powers.

    Call of Cthulhu was also mentioned, and it too is this, 100%. Any random schmuck with a couple of CON to invest in the project, knowing the ritual, can make an Elder Sign or open a gate to Pluto; any random (evil) schmuck willing to manipulate a string of successively younger lovers can live forever in their bodies. I do love that game!

    Wasn't there a "thread magic" system in Earthdawn that had elements of this? I forget. Too many rules in that game.

    AD&D and D&D3 rogues can operate magical items, but not create them. A mystic rogue campaign might actually be a lot of fun.
