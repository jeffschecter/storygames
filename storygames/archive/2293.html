My Epic Dreamation Saga

James_Nostack
January 2007 edited January 2007 in Meetups / Conventions
Wow, what an amazing convention! Believe me, if you didn't make it to Dreamation 2007, you don't know what you're missing. And neither do I. I was there, but I didn't attend! Let's break it down, SG-style! Raise a glass, gentlemen, to the tale of StoryGaming Sisyphus!

Kicker - "It is Saturday! After meaning to go to Dreamation for two years, today is the day! You have mastered many games, and now is your chance to meet on-line friends, conquer worlds, and destroy Vincent and Joshua in MechaTon, becoming the greatest of all MechaTon players. It's time to leave the apartment! What happens now?"

Bang 1 - Last week your wallet was stolen; you're an unemployed student, so money's tight. Do you go to Dreamation or not? Leads to . . . Hell yes I'm going! Throwing money around on entertainment expenses is exactly what I need to distract me from my woes.

Bang 2 - Saturday is also the day of massive anti-war rallies. Do you participate in pointless protests against a foreign policy you despise, or do you sit around playing silly games? Leads to . . . heading to Dreamation anyway, to wage imaginary wars instead of protesting a real one. (There will be plenty of time to protest the Iraq war over the next decade.) The primitive Tarkut ptimanya will be ground into Lego-dust!

Bang 3 - This weekend is also your mother's birthday. Do you go home and enjoy the comforts of family, or socialize with unfamiliar white men? Leads to . . . I spent time with my mom last year. This is the year of Dreamation! RAAAAAGH!!!!! The people of Marr'd will weep tears of blood!

Bang 4 - Now you're actually in motion! You have 40 minutes to get to the Port Authority Bus Terminal, which normally takes 20 minutes. But your subway train breaks down at Prince Street. Leads to . . . running into traffic on Broadway to catch a cab. Raise my "Stationary Student" aspect from primed to threatened.

Bang 5 - Your cab driver screws up, and you end up arriving at the bus station seconds from its departure. It's true he messed up, but you should have left earlier--do you tip? Leads to . . . tipping the cabbie. Gain a point of Humanity.

Bang 6 - Hey! It's the Port Authority Bus Terminal! You sprint up four flights of stairs and for some reason, a Bowling Alley to catch your bus. And it pulls away just as you are in view of the gate. You've just missed the morning session, which you really wanted to make in order to get the most for your limited funds. Do you cry and give up? Leads to . . . a cost-benefit analysis of how to spend the weekend, concluding that I have come this far, and must keep going, but I'll have to game like a madman to make this worthwhile, missing the morning session. Stake 3 tokens of Hope.

Bang 7 - After waiting an hour, you get on the proper bus, and arrive! The promised land! You've ignored wars, filial obligations, financial constraints, transit demons, and the horror of the Port Authority to get here! It's time for Story Games, bitches! And then you meet Judd at the booth, who confesses that virtually all the SG slots filled up during pre-registration. All of your expectations are shot. Do you pay $40 to spend eight hours scavenging among pick-up games, or do you head back to NYC to take part in any protest rallies goin' on. Leads to . . . turning around and heading back to the bus station.

I will spare you the other details of my journey--boarding the bus, and then being told there's no space, so getting delayed; then the next bus gets in an accident. But, suffice to say, by noon I am back at the Port Authority, four hours after first arriving . . . ready to start my day.

Still, because I didn't make it to 2005 or 2006, it's still the best Dreamation ever. I look forward to actually getting into the convention next year, meeting people, and actually playing these stupid games. Small steps.
