\n This might provoke "umm.. why?" but I think it will help me at the table.

In A Wicked Age, you assign d12/d8, d10/d6, d6/d4 to every NPC. So ultimately, there are only 6 kinds of NPCs. To save me time at the table and make it easier to pick NPC stats, I've come up with "totem animals" that describe each. That way I can just pull from the prepped pile and have a "done" NPC at any second depending on what kind of NPC I want.

Asp
d12 d8 Action
d10 d6 Maneuvering
d6 d4 Self-protection

Wolf
d12 d8 Action
d6 d4 Maneuvering
d10 d6 Self-protection

Hawk
d10 d6 Action
d12 d8 Maneuvering
d6 d4 Self-protection

Crow
d6 d4 Action
d12 d8 Maneuvering
d10 d6 Self-protection

Bear
d10 d6 Action
d6 d4 Maneuvering
d12 d8 Self-protection

Rat
d6 d4 Action
d10 d6 Maneuvering
d12 d8 Self-protection

Edit: Changed Hawk to Asp

    \n Handy to have some pre-written, as GM you might as well fill in some sheets beforehand etc.

    My concern would be avoiding any self-inflicted-bias from labels, e.g., to play ever bear as bear-like, rat as rat-like, after all sometimes there's a bear that makes the mistake of thinking of itself as a hawk or is really a turtle. Though less evocative/snazzy, noting that 12/8 > 10/6 > 6/4, it's basically also A/M/SP, A/SP/M etc. in terms of which stats are ranked first for an NPC.
