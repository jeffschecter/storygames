    \n Oh, here's something geeky that I also loved:

    Statting yourself out in Aberrant. Total fantasy-dream trip. No other supers game does it as good as Aberrant does, for this reason:

    In Aberrant, you stat your dude (read: yourself) out normally, with of course the regular caveats for it being an RPG ("Sure, I'm good with guns").

    Then, you have The Incident where you become a superhero.

    You stat yourself out further, thinking of how cool it would be to even have a "1" in Mega-Strength or Telekinesis.

    It's a bitch when trying to jump into play quickly (cause it's two character generation systems, back to back), but it's great for seeing what it would be like to be Yourself, but More Awesome.

      \n I really like the little goblinesque critters from the original Fiend Folio - the mite (aka koboldkin), the meazel, the snyad (aka pestie), the dark creepers and dark stalkers. All of them had very evocative art - most if not all were done by the same artist, who also put some of them in the background of another unrelated picture or two. They just made me wonder, who are these weird little guys, what's their story, what're their lives about? Dwarves and elves and even orcs and goblins get long writeups with dry histories and basic relationships and so forth, but these little guys seemed to be part of that extra level of background detail that brings it alive - like the weird things you might notice going on in the background or in a quick little scene in films like Labyrinth or MirrorMask.

      Also from that book, I liked the weird undead - the coffer corpse, the crypt thing, the eye of fear and flame, the death knight, the skeleton warrior. And of course the slaad. Oh, the slaad. The Fiend Folio had some of the stupidest monsters in any first edition book - trilloch, anyone? - but also some of the coolest.
