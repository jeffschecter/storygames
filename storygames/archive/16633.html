\n We played this last night, and it was a heap of fun! For those who don't know, it's a mashup of In a Wicked Age with Archipelago, made by some person [Edit: Jamie Fridstrom] on this forum who I don't remember (help me out here?). I've not played either game by themselves, so I couldn't say that this is better than either, but it's certainly a fun game in its own right. The situation that comes out of the Oracles is detailed and kinky and slightly uncomfortable, and then the system in play is just robust enough to provide unexpected outcomes and tough decisions.

One thing I found really interesting, coming off the back of some previous discussions here, was how the fact that we were consciously playing a mashup of two games, with some rough edges and unclear procedures, meant that we felt very free about improvising rules and procedures as we needed to. We passed some characters around the table, we interpreted the resolution system pretty broadly, and we improvised some scene framing rules. It felt good and kind of exploratory and dangerous.

A thing I'm really interested in exploring further is what exactly we were doing in terms of GMly stuff around the table - who was providing adversity, who was framing scenes, who was provoking more from the characters. We were doing all that stuff, but it was complicated and not explicit, and sometimes it worked and sometimes it didn't.
