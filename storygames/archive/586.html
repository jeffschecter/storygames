    One of my favorite topics, if you read my reviews at rpgnow.com. :)

    The requirements of printer-friendliness and screen-friendliness are very different. A PDF that is used on a laptop or PDA at the gaming table can benefit greatly from hypertext links to related segments. One that's printed out just has a lot of silly-looking underlining. A printer-friendly PDF has the page numbers at the bottom of the page and they may not match the page numbers of the PDF file itself. A screen-friendly PDF can also take advantage of mouse-over notes to remind people about one thing or another. With a screen PDF, you are basically building a gaming tool, not just organizing a text.

    "I asked RPGnet":http://forum.rpg.net/showthread.php?p=5675903#post5675903 and what I gleaned is that electronic versions of games are:

    * For some people, the games are primarily printed out, then used, so optimize for that (probably PDF)
    * For some people, the games are primarily read on a computer, so optimize for that (perhaps HTML)

    And my _guess_ is that someone who primarily does one of these does not do the other! So perhaps you ideally have a PDF geared towards being printed, and some other format (PDF, HTML, or otherwise) that is geared towards screen reading. Assuming you have the time to do it both ways...
