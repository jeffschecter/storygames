    \n I suggested this to Daniel on his LJ, but others might like to know about it as well: Fundamental Modeling Concepts has a number of diagramming models that are particularly useful for software systems. Their use of petri nets could be very useful in gaming to describe high-level processes or sequences in a more visually accessible way than the traditional UML sequence diagram. OmniGraffle (OmniGroup's "Visio"-like tool on the Macintosh) has a "stencil" site called Graffletopia where one can get a stencil to work with FMC diagrams, and that's what I use in my day-to-day-work, mostly (that and UML).

    I'm not sure if there's an FMC Visio stencil/library/template whatever available, but I would presume there is somewhere.
