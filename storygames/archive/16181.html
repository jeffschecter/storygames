    \n In Dreaming Crucible, there are three player roles: the Heroine, the Light Faery, and the Dark Faery.

    The Heroine player controls the story's protagonist.
    The Light Faery controls all of the helpful elements of the world of faery, but especially a supporting companion.
    The Dark Faery controls all of the dangerous and malicious elements of the world of faery, but especially a creepy antagonist.

    So while each player has a character that they control, only one of those characters is the protagonist, and the other two players also have extra-character responsibilities.

    Posted By: HarlequinSpione. With 5 players, you get two principles? Something like that.
    \nNo. In Spione there are no PCs and no NPCs (even if it coild be said after all that the two Pincipals are a sort of NPCs, in a manner of speaking). There is no character ownership and no protected protagonism. The players play all the characters, and the sum of principals, relationships and the spider web for both principals is bigger than the number of players.

    Two games that can be played with only one protagonist if the time is short (at conventions, for example) are Annalise and Polaris, even if it's not the standard way of playing them.
