How can I ask for dietary requirements while dissuading people from giving them?

\n
\n
\n
\n\nGraham \n\n\n
\n
\n\nOctober 2009 \n edited October 2009 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n An interesting point, which arose while inviting a new player to our gaming group.

I wanted to know if there was anything the guy wouldn't eat. However, I don't like the phrase "specific dietary requirements". After all, I'm cooking for him, and that's a nice thing. I don't want people to "require" things from me.

Additionally, I'd quite like to dissuade people from giving me requirements. If someone is allergic to something, of course, then I need to know. And if they really don't like something, then fine. But if they have a whole list of stuff, then, well, no, eat what you're given. And, equally, if they're something like a vegetarian who eats fish and chicken but not shellfish and by the way they really don't like tomatoes, I don't particularly want to cook for them.

How can I ask the question?

Graham

    Posted By: Colin_FredericksI only ask, "Are you allergic to anything?" If they're vegetarian or whatnot, they'll tell me the second I mention I'm cooking.
    \nYeah, by the time they're in their twenties, most people with allergies learn to mention them up front when someone else is doing the cooking. The more serious/overwhelming the allergy, the faster they are to mention it; vegetarians and vegans are also trained to bring it up when someone else says "hey, I'll make dinner for you."

    So just saying that you're cooking dinner and telling them what you're making will get you the information you want without any fuss. Although if you want to hedge your bet, you can throw in a "...you're okay with that, right? Not allergic or anything?" and be assured that no one's going to fumble frantically for an epipen before dessert is served.
