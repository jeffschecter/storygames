\n
\n
\n
\n\nGraham \n\n\n
\n
\n\nDecember 2010 \n edited December 2010 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n Recently, I've been playing games with my father. So far, we've played Fox and Geese, Mancala and Surakarka. Can you recommend me any other games we should play?

I'm mainly talking about non-roleplaying games, here, so I hope you'll forgive the digression, although simple storytelling games (e.g. Happy Birthday Robot) might be good.

I'm most interested in two-player games. I'm also, but not exclusively, interested in ancient games, like Fox and Geese.

If they're not ancient, they should feel "classic" in some way. I realise that's rather indefinable, but Jenga, for example, has a classic feel about it: it's simple but gives rise to complex play.

Any ideas? Tell me a little about games you recommend and why they're good.

Graham

    \n +1 on go (google for "baduk", the Korean name, for tons of stuff online). Simple rules, and the wicked thing is, it scales nicely--you can play on a 9x9 board and it takes less than half an hour, and it's the same game as on the full-size 19x19. Except totally not the same game. Oh, and the handicapping is very tidy, so people of widely divergent skills can enjoy a close game.
    \n
    \nEdit: another good "classic-feeling" one that is quick to learn and play is Hive. The heavy bakelite tile-pieces are very nice-feeling and satisfying to thonk down on the table.
