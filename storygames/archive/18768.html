\n Hi folks,



Imp of the Perverse is an monster-hunting adventure game set in the gothic 1830's America of Edgar Allen Poe. You play characters who are seeking to deal with their own Perversity by hunting down those who have already given in to theirs, and transformed into literal monsters and creatures of the night.

Here's the direct PDF download.

This game has been in the works for, geez, years, but it started coming together in it's present form about a year ago. This draft is totally playable, though maybe a little rough around some edges. It's gone through a couple rounds of internal playtesting, and I'd love to get some feedback from a wider sphere of interested folks.

A couple key conceits of the game:

- the default mode of the play is episodic, mission- and party-based play, so
- it's built to accommodate fluctuating attendance and single-sessions of play
- there's a GM role (the Editor)
- the key tension of the game is whether your character will give in to their Perversity and embrace their Imp - it's not deterministic that this will happen, but it's always there, tempting you. If/when a character does give in, then they actually take on the Editor role for the next Chapter of play, and prep their former character as the new antagonist for the group. The former Editor makes a new character to join the group, and so on.
- it uses a fairly simple d6-based die pool system that pulls a lot of information out of each roll, and rolls matter in terms of affecting both your characters immediate prospects and their long-term development over time

There's some more details at my playtest page as well. And of course I'm happy to talk about it/answer questions.

Thanks!

    \n Not a specific procedure at this point. I'm thinking in any such situation there's probably someone who actively wants to become the Editor, so basically whoever volunteers. I don't know if there needs to be a hard mechanism, beyond something like "in the event that you simply cannot decide who should be the next Editor, whoever hit 0 Perversity first, or dice off for it if need be."

    So far in playtest, there's a group sense of whoever is closest to going over, and players seem to "wait" for that to resolve, if that makes sense. That might just be my local play culture, though.
