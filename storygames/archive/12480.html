[Society of Dreamers] first session - how kosher was that?

\n
\n
\n
\n\nIna \n\n\n
\n
\n\nJuly 2010 \n edited July 2010 in Actual Play
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n There were three of us: vh, Juliusz (nid) and me. Two of us have played Archipelago (and Last Train out of Warsaw based on it) before, we've also tried a few games form "Norwegian Style". However, we're still not sure whether we get what Norwegian style actually is. Plus, we've customized a couple of things, rather spontaneously. Anyway, here's what we did.
\n
\nFirst, an important thing \u2013 I\u2019ve decided to omit the introductory part with clapping hands and making noise. We always play in the same cafe and we don't want to be thrown out of there. After a quick brainstorming we choose not to play in the 1800\u2019s but to choose late 60's in the United States.
\n
\nCharacters we drew:
\n1) American Jew + Soviet spy + old as the Wandering Jew (finally interpreted as the Wandering Jew himself) + women (interpreted as sexual preference not gender, of course) \u2013 vh
\n2) Ethiopian + senator (interpreted not as job but as his nickname in the voodoo cult) + eternally young + frigid \u2013 Juliusz
\n3) Russian + leader of the youth New Age cult + teenager + male \u2013 me (Ina)
\n
\nSide note: the characters we\u2019ve created were a bit more unconventional than the example with Polish carpenter given in the book. Maybe that\u2019s the reason why our play was concentrated more on the characters and the Society itself, with the mnemosites laid somehow aside, as you will see below.
\n
\nPregame (childhood):
\nThe Russian:
\nI wanted my character to be brought in the States by parents being dissidents from Soviet Union. The scene was set in a funfair and after short play we reached the climax: the character\u2019s parents were shot during the roller coaster ride.
\nThe Senator:
\nThe middle of war in Ethiopia. Ten years old kid caught by soldiers and mistaken for a partisan is interrogated. He pretends to be courageous claiming he won\u2019t tell anything (actually, there hardly is something he could tell). But finally he repeats what he heard from his parents and gets a cookie. And here\u2019s something supercool I noticed much later: the scene ends like this \u2013 somebody throws _a_cat_ (a dead one?) right in the character\u2019s face.
\nThe Wandering Jew
\nThat was quick but strong. It\u2019s the first year of a new century. The character is 3 years old. Soldiers bend over him and wonder whether to kill him or not. Finally they decide not to do so \u2013 well, they have the order to kill newborns and this kid seems to be somewhat older.
\n
\nPregame (youth)
\nFor all characters it was the moment of the first contact with mnemosites.
\nThe Senator
\nDuring his long stay in the jungle was bitten by a white crocodile. While recovering he had a vision of big, cat like, yellow eyes gazing at him.
\n
\nSide note: after the session it occurred to me that this first notion about mnemosites strongly influenced our further ideas and from this point we were leaning towards the image of mnemosites as the beasts.
\n
\nNext to facts we\u2019ve discovered in this act: mnemosites groan and you can hear that not only in a dream (discovered when put to jail after driving), they are contagious like a disease (here we had a scene with Jews in the Temple getting infected one by one \u2013 it looks like a kind of trance; I really enjoyed that scene).
\n
\nSide note: here in his AP Matthijs concentrated on the pregame when it comes to describing the fiction. That makes me feel that the pregame is particularly important part but I also think that in our case it took just too long.
\n
\nExposition
\nThe society was founded in San Francisco in 1967 by the Senator. It\u2019s main aim is to protect form the mnemosites.
\n
\nSide note: the problem with in-character. Juliusz solved it by narrating most of his scenes from the perspective of a survivor who records a video tape (also the foundation of the society scene). Vh\u2019s Jew was really grumpy because grumbling is the only thing vh can do in-character as he claims. Maybe the lack of ritualisation and may out-of-character elements changed our perspective form internal and mental to a more social one.
\n
\nExposition and weaving
\nHere we had a couple of not really connected scenes. We paid no attention to the chronological order. Meetings of New Age cult, assisting the death of one of the Dreamers, hunting in the realm of dreams and the Society members who try to escape from the danger by committing suicide.
\n
\nSide note: the problem with dream. We incorporated a dream into a scene not only when the board suggested so. We\u2019ve decided to use a dream in two cases and in two ways:
\n1) when it\u2019s chosen by Ouija \u2013 I think it\u2019s like an exploration scene and the dream is the point of the scene itself; here we use the special rules;
\n2) when it\u2019s not chosen by the board but one of players decides to use a dream as a way to achieve other goal (e.g. hunting scene); here we narrate the scene in a regular way, even if it includes a contact with a Dreamer.
\nIs that kosher?
\n
\nOne more side note: I feel that our game was nor about mnemosites, neither about the Society and its members. Most of the scenes included only one character (and just one included all three of them). It seems to me that we were concentrated mainly on social aspects of the discoveries of the Society.
\nTo clarify that I have to mention the cut scenes our play was full of. Starting from the second act at least once per scene somebody came up with a cultural allusion or context. Examples: we made a man with a man with an \u2018x\u2019 on his forehead (Charles Manson) and Valerie Solanas members of the Society who assisted the death of a Dreamer. We had also the scene with the Senator talking about his lost journal. The fact connected with mnemosites here was that they reproduce when described and read. And here we just couldn\u2019t resist and had to add some cut scenes: first one with a Norwegian game designer who designs a game that concentrates on generating mnemosites (sorry!) and second one with a French professor of study of religions who lends his students a strange journal and asks them to make copies.
\nThat was super fun but, again, is that kosher?
\n
\nEnding
\nFinally we ended up with the last meeting of a few members who survived. It\u2019s Russian\u2019s funeral. The Senator and Wandering Jew meet for the last time. They know it wasn\u2019t death by natural causes. They\u2019ve seen a huge hole in Russian\u2019s chest and they know what emerged from it. A strange cat strolling down the pavement. Shot.
\nPlus the epilogue \u2013 the Senator\u2019s last recording, he shoots himself; the Wandering Jew somewhere in Russia, hiding a mysterious board.
\n
\nTo sum up:
\nWe\u2019ve started at 5 p.m., had a 15 minutes break about 8 and finished at 9 p.m. We\u2019ve tried to take our time and not to rush and that was ok. We omitted most of the ritual elements, now I think I regret it because I really liked the fun with oujia (though Juliusz says it\u2019s just a gimmick I feel it has an influence on the pace and character of the game). There should be more in-character play, but the cut scenes were great, however out-of-character, they were worth it. The feeling of danger gave us a bit \u201c44\u201d-like effect. Oh, and one thing we had problem with \u2013 the competences of instructor slightly change form phase to phase and it was sometimes difficult to keep that in mind. But maybe it\u2019s just because it was our first session. Definitely, I want to play one more time and try to do it more \u201cby the book\u201d.
\n
\nOh, and please forgive me grammar mistakes, if there are any. English is not my first language.

    \n Incredibly cool writeup! I've wanted to play Society of Dreamers in a different setting for a LONG time - in fact, I was thinking about trying the 60's last weekend. Now I want to even more.

    Since you're asking, I'll just say for the record that everything you do with any of my games is kosher as far as I'm concerned. I write the games, I try to say how I and other people have played them successfully, and then people do things I'd never thought of with them, which is fun. There is no RIGHT way. I'll tell you how I get fun and interesting play, though. Feel free to ignore everything I say and do whatever you want with the game.\n

    \n Hi Matthijs,

    Thanks for answers!

    About the whole kosher thing: for me, it boils down to this. Designer knows which way of playing his game is fun, and how the ruleset supports that. First time (of first few times) I play the game, I want to follow the rules as closely as possible, and reconstruct that way of playing. Issue of "the right way" is nothing more than another way of saying that the game is designed to deliver particular experience, and playing by the book allows that. Of course, I have no problems with hijacking the game, but first I'd like to know how exactly it works. I kinda believe in author's intention and all that stuff.

    Some Norwegian Style games (Until We Sink, In the Belly of the Whale) pose some problems here: it seems to me that there is sort of "background knowledge", concerning number of techniques and things to do / to not do while playing these two games, and that "background knowledge" is very important for the experience generated by those games. It seems to me, that quasi-LARP techniques, like gesticulation, or particular approach, like points from "Nordic Dreaming" part of The Society of Dreamers .pdf, are very important here. Multiforming is an excellent example - I can imagine that playing In the Belly of the Whale using multiform is very different from playing it, so to say, tabletop.

    But, that's unnecessary digression. Going into details...\n
