If I made myself as a character right now, I would have the following RPG flaws:

- Rules Lawyer: I recently leveled up so now at least I wait till after the game to complain.

- Rage: flies into rage when I am or think I am being Railroaded

- Rage - Part 2: flies into rage when the game isn't about the players or players' participation is greatly minimized to show off "cool NPCs".

- Perversion: In silly games... I tend to take silly into the realm of perversion.

- Sleep: falls asleep during single combats that takes multiple hours to resolve.

- Min Max: must min max point buy systems.

- Pregens Weakness: when pre-generated characters are available, must take the "weakest" one.

- "Special": can't play a straight up "normal" character, if there is a psionic, shapeshifter, or monster character... you know that's the one I made!

- Straight Face: can't keep it!

What are your RPG flaws?

    Who Turned This Into a Chick Flick?: I have real trouble ending sessions on cliffhangers in the middle of the action, or immediately following scenes that are purely action oriented. I need a personal, social interaction scene that allows me to emotionally react in game after the action to cement it as part of the story.     Where Were We Again? Despite the fact that the gaming hangovers of PTGD (above) make me think hard about the game in the days following the game, I will often remember the emotional intensity of the previous session over the specifics of what happened. That means that a recap is generally necessary before we play the next session. If it wasn't in the recap, I might not remember it at all.

    Don't Give Me Your Jedi Mind Trick Crap: I will react violently to plots where my character is mind controlled unless I lead the plot in. There's some bad game experiences that just never go away.
