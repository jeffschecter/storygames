\n A lone ronin struggles up the mountain. Powdery snow lies on top of smooth, unforgiving, unwelcoming ice. A stolen iron rod is the only thing that keeps him from sliding back down, down to the edge of the snow where he left his clothes as an offering to appease the Winter Storm.

A lone shack, like a guard tower inches closer, and three faces come from it. They were once men, but have been corrupted by the mountain. They swarm the ronin, knocking him to his back where the ice sends new pain into his numbing body. As he struggles to whip his makeshift weapon, the nearest attacker sinks his teeth into the ronin's frozen ear, and with a crunch, the ronin blacks out.

    \n I'm playing Witch: The Road to Lindisfarne with my World of Dungeons campaign group. We've reached the Absolution scene, where we decide if the witch is to be burned or not. Sir Hayden, whose gotten crap the whole game for being a coward, ties up the witch to be burned. Hayden then draws his as-yet unblooded sword and slices open the bonds he had just tied, setting Eloise, the accused witch, free.

    Berrick, Hayden's spiteful squire, seeing his master finally make a bold and decisive move, decides that Hayden must know what he's doing and Eloise must be innocent. He hands over the baby he had been hiding from Eloise. Eloise slices open the baby's throat, draws a burning pentagram with its blood and curses all of Lindisfarne with the black plague!

    Dammit, Hayden!

    \n Malandros, a game of rogues and strivers in 1880s Rio.
    PCs in italics.

    Posing as an investor, political fixer Meredith uncovers the secret keeping the rival candidate's wealthy backers sweet - knockdown prices on prime seafront land, once the market and half the parish is knocked down too.

    At a smart-set party, his boss's daughter Rosa does her best to land a wealthy, dim husband in the person of Pedro, elder scion of a Minas Gerais dairy baron. It seems to be working.

    Old fisherman Jose confronts his niece Aranha about her criminal activities (she runs a street gang) and implores her to smarten up and go straight. She says no. He pleads with her to at least leave his sons out of it. She says no. He decides to enlist help on the matter and (possibly intentionally) gets her fired from her day job.

    On the beach at Praia Beijaflor, Aranha confronts Sem Parar, leader of the gang from Sao Francisco parish, who recently tried collecting protection money on her turf. Aranha beats Sem Parar half to death, and some of his gang come over to her.

    Early next morning, there's outrage as Meredith's leaked story hits the front pages.

    \n Two crooked Al Amarjan cops decide to kidnap the voodoo economist PC and harness his magical powers to inflate their bank accounts. When one tries to attack parapsychologist graduate student, she realizes that he is in fact a tulpa, a fictitious being that took this form based on the imaginations of those around him. She thinks really hard about Danny Glover in _Lethal Weapon_, despite the PC, player and GM never having seen the movie. One successful roll later and the cop changes into an honest, by-the-books cop who apprehends his former compatriot and takes him down to the station.

    \n Dark Heresy 1492 (Solar System hack).

    GM: In the morning, the bishop knocks on your chamber door to wake you. What are you doing?
    PC Tuscan noblewoman, secretly a vampire: Praying.
    Other player, OOC: Preying on whom?

    Fusilli, the skeptic: I'll believe it when I see it, Friar. Seeing is believing.
    Brother Bernardo: That's what St. Paul said.
    Fusilli: Yes, and look how he ended up.

    The PCs discover an occult symbol seemingly burned into solid rock on a cliff face.
    Bernardo: How do you explain this, with your science?
    Fusilli: I use my chemical reagents and stuff to examine it. (fails roll, then spends to add a bonus die and passes).
    GM: It's magnesium.
    Fusilli: You see? Science explains everything.
    Bernardo: I suppose it also explains the flock of crows that's been following us for the last several hours?
    Fusilli: Of course it does. Crows love magnesium. Especially in the winter.

    \n Introducing new gamers to oD&D.

    After the party had lowered the dwarf down a deep shaft on a rope for reconnaissance purposes, the fighter's player announces apropo of nothing:

    "I want to take a shit on the dwarf!"
    "Sure. Roll 1d20, on a 20 your turd hits the dwarf. On a 1 you loose your footing and tumble into the shaft."
    [1]
    "You slip and fall some 100 feet into the darkness. The shallow cave-pool is not sufficient to break your fall and you die instantly."

    Important lessons were learnt that, yes, you can do anything. But no, you might not survive it.

    \n Oh man. Just killed our first PC in Black Stars Rise. We had a great horror movie setpiece where three PCs were in an apartment watching webfootage of the building's foyer, when the footage cut-impossibly-to outside their apartment, showing them entering (3 minutes earlier) and an inky black entity watching them in the hallway.

    The soon-to-die PC looked out the door's peephole, saw nothing but inky blackness and realised something was looking back in at him. The thing outside started disintegrating the door to get in, then realised it could corrode the electrical wiring in the wall, plunging the room into darkness. Cue failed Keep It Together rolls, failed Take a Risk rolls to get out onto the fire escape, and massively failed Suffer Harm rolls to the Detective trying to protect the Doctor who was carrying the footage.

    So good. But, man, the Detective's player is so bummed out by it.

    \n Bubblegumshoe Playtest: We've only done character generation as yet. One thing characters have is an arc, defined as a goal they're working towards that's not a mystery. One Sleuth wants to move out and live on her own -- she's all of sixteen, after all! One wants to have the school get rid of the Vice Principal. The New Kid in town wants to be the acknowledged King of the Road! And the tough gangbanger? He wants to learn how to knit a sweater for his mother.

    Call of Cthulhu: Masks of Nyarlathotep: One of the characters is a Russian woman who used to be a countess, currently pregnant with the child of an Outer God. She's very ambivalent about this, but still hopeful, as she's been promised by cultists that her child will become the Tsar of all of Russia. Traveling with the group is an NPC Russian man, who used to be a count and has been her friend since childhood. He knows the situation, as does one other member of the group.

    The rest of the group... well, everyone now knows she's pregnant. And, in the midst of fighting cultists and monsters, trying to figure out what's really going on and save the world, Father O'Connor has now decided that the really important thing is that the Count and Countess must get married. Obviously, the Count's the father. No one has denied this. And a wedding is just what everyone needs! They're in the a Russian neighborhood in Shanghai, in the French Concession (assuming a) I'm reading a map correctly and b) my source of information on Shanghai in the 1920s is correct), and this will make everyone's day! The good father would be happy to perform the ceremony.

    The Countess hesitantly explained that she wants a Russian Orthodox priest, not an Irish-American Jesuit priest, to perform the wedding. This dismays Father O'Connor not a whit! He understands completely -- obviously, they had intended to marry all along. But, they needed a priest of their own sect to perform the wedding, and until they got to Shanghai, this simply wasn't possible. Well, now it is, and he will arrange all the details!
