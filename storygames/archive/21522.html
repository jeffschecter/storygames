\n How does conditions works and affect the player/npc?
EXAMPLE: 1 of my players tried to shut down the bully in front of the whole class.
Result? 6 or less.
He got a voice crack while saying it and all the class started to laugh gaining 1 string on Rey Ross <--- the player

I said that everyone (npc and players in the class) gets a string on Rey Ross because that happened. Should have i changed something? Instead of the strings i had to give a condition to Rey Ross?

Another question

The backstory of the Vampire says:
Someone once saved your unlife. They gain 2 strings on you.

What does that mean?

    \n I read a Condition to mean that the character is perceived as exuding that particular characteristic until the Condition is removed or logically no longer applies. If another character acts against a character with a Condition and (in the opinion of the MC and/or group) takes advantage of it, they gain a +1 to their roll.

    In your example, I would apply a condition such as 'ineffectual', 'wimp', 'all bark and no bite' or something similar. If another character tries to (for example) Shut Down the character with the Condition and does so by daring them to stand up or shut up, I would class that as giving a +1.

    If the character with the 'Wimp' Condition later successfully causes a bully Harm (for example), I would count the Condition as successfully removed.

    That's my interpretation, anyhow.

    \n Just going by the examples in the book (and aiming for somewhat realistic and elegant book-keeping), I would recommend one of the following for your MC Reaction in that situation (your hard move):

    * Give the bully a String on the PC. (To focus the action on the PC's relationship with the bully.)
    * Give the PC a Condition. (To focus the action on how this makes the PC look in front of the whole class.)

    That second option is easier to track, simpler, and generally better than giving a whole crew of PCs and NPCs each a String. It also feels more in line with the examples in the rules.

    Sidenote: Unlike 1st Edition, in Monsterhearts 2 it's much more ambiguous how NPCs might take advantage of Conditions. You can consider porting in the rules from 1st Edition, or you can just really commit to making a big deal of that Condition moving forward - treat it narratively, in other words, rather than mechanically.

    That usually works best, although with my group I had some trouble with that when certain Conditions reflected personal relationships and status rather than a wider reputation (as in the school).

    enricoboschin said:


    The backstory of the Vampire says:
    Someone once saved your unlife. They gain 2 strings on you.

    What does that mean?

    \nIt's kind of a joke, in a way. A creature or person who has "returned from the dead", as a vampire has, is called "undead". So, here, it simply means that 'someone once saved your [life].' However, the term "unlife" is used, instead, to indicate that this happened after your character turned into a vampire (who, strictly speaking, doesn't have a normal "life" anymore).
