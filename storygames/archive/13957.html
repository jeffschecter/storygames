      Posted By: TheDarkness73So exactly what is new in 3E?
      \nThere is a lot. I'm just going to list most of the changes instead of explaining them, because frankly some of this stuff needs the text in the books to explain it (and in my opinion, more text than is actually in the book):

      Realm and Spirit are out, replaced by Persona (used for using your Estate in a metaphorical way, instead of the literal one Domain does) and Treasure (used for acting through and enhancing Anchors).

      The timing of events in miraculous combat is more strictly defined, and there is a new system for performing mundane actions, which overlaps with the lower levels of Aspect miracles.

      Nobles are no longer immune to direct miracles, and you need to spend an action and some MP to activate your Auctoritas. This ties into the slightly changed Wounds system, and the new Bonds and Afflictions (which sort of brings Fate's Aspects or Weapons of the Gods' Chi Conditions into Nobilis). There is also an "anti-Auctoritas" called Divine Mantle you can activate to make your mundane actions more effective and miracles harder to oppose. Penetration has been renamed to Strike, and Miracle Points are refreshed much more often now.

      Chancel and Imperator creation, as a mechanical process of "pick from this list of advantages and disadvantages", are both gone. They still exist, but mostly as color chosen by the group. That said, there are now rules for the miracles Imperators use to change the world.

      Dynasty Points are out, as are direct awarding of Character Points as XP-- any mechanical advancement to a PC must be done in-game, as part of the new Projects system.

      There is an optional lifepath system for character creation.

      There's much more detail on setting things only lightly mentioned in previous editions, like the Locust Court and Ananda's Chancel Cityback, what the Spirit World the Imperators and Excrucians fight in is like, and new things entirely like the Zu and Actuals.

      A lot of these changes have nested effects within one another-- for instance, Bonds and Afflictions tie into the damage system, mortal actions, Treasure/Anchor rules, and can enhance some miracles. It's not easily summarized.\n

      \n So, the five person Nobilis convention game I'm writing is slowly coming together.

      Basic premise: The Chancel has been invaded by an Excrucian incursion; this was a coordinated strike that had help from within. Banes have taken root in each of the Sieges of the Chancel [I'm defining a "Siege" as a region of the Chancel traditionally held by one of the Nobles, their seat of power]. Excrucian shards are moving through the Sieges. The Temple defenses which protect the body of the Imperator Ghislaine are weakening, so PCs are on the clock to resolve this problem. Gates into and out of the Chancel have been closed and regular gates and conventional passages between Sieges in the Chancel are on the fritz, but hidden passages are accessible. PCs begin at a central point, probably at the Temple in the heart of the Chancel. Each of the Nobles is missing a memory of whom they were prior to being Ennobled - I'm calling this an Echo (sort of like a mnemonic version of a Horcrux (from Harry Potter), but much less violent and can be in any shape or form). PCs need to find and consume/absorb their stolen Echoes to fill in their missing memories and and be restored to full strength. The Echoes are located in each of the Sieges, which have become darker, corrupted versions of what they once were and should be. The Banes and Excrucians inhabiting the Sieges need to be wiped out to restore the Imperator before the corrupted Chancel infects and consumes her body.

      The body of the Imperator Ghislaine is in a dream-like state while her spirit is away fighting The Excrucians in the Spirit World \u2013 her Powers and the Chancel Erus are charged with her protection. The Erus helps to slowly dispense exposition, put the PCs on the clock, and answer questions whenever possible. This NPC should be likable.

      The Incursion involved a coordinated Excrucian Rite that was performed at the same time in each of the Sieges of the Chancel (every Siege should have a potential culprit/Excrucian ally/cultist \u2013 their rituals were coordinated with their allies in the Cult in each of the Sieges to bring the Chancel to its knees). The reveal in the last Siege that the Powers reach is that there's actually a sixth Siege, and a sixth Noble - The Power of Memory; an NPC traitor Power that was one of Familia, but for reasons not yet determined joined the Excrucian cult, coordinated the rituals and attempted to wipe the memories of the PCs, but failed, instead she took the memories of their lives before they were Ennobled, and removed all memories of her, and that she and her Siege ever existed. She'll be backed by her new Excrucian master, who is still being determined.

      Impressions, suggestions, obvious pitfalls?
