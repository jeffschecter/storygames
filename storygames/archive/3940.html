\n In Lacuna, you have a Heart Rate. It starts at 70 (75 if you're a girl, which, on average, you're probably not) and increases throughout the game.

When you reach a maximum, which ranges from 161 to 199, you're in trouble.

When you're in your target range, which ranges from 83-123 to 100-150, you have a special ability, which means you can roll any number of dice for success.

You succeed if your dice total 11 or more. However, you must also add the dice total to your heart rate, taking it closer to, or over, the maximum.

Now, in a recent review thread, Thor makes the interesting point that you need never roll more than 11 dice. They'll all roll at least 1, so rolling 11 dice means you automatically roll at least 11.

This got me thinking.

In practice, of course, you'd never roll 11 dice. If you want to succeed, you might as well roll 10 dice: the only way you'll fail is if you roll 10 1s, and the odds against are astronomical (1 in 60 million, I make it).

So I thought I'd throw this open for statistical kicking around. Every possible number of dice will have a probability of success and there'll be some number of dice where the chance of success is, say, 99% or more, and it'd be silly to roll any more.

Also, each number of dice will have an expected amount to increase your heart rate by: with 10 dice, it's 35.

I don't know if it's possible to calculate an optimal number of dice to roll. If it were, it'd be cool.

But it'd be possible to design a table, which, for any given number of dice, would tell you the probability of succeeding and the expected amount by which they'd increase your heart rate. Think of the possibilities! Simply by playing with Excel for an afternoon, you could kill all the enjoyment of guessing how many dice to roll!

So, yes, I wanted to throw it out there. Unless someone who's better at statistics gets to it first, I'll probably fiddle around with some statistics later.
