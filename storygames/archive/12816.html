    \n OK, I've read through RPGirl 2010 and it is full of good stuff. Beyond its unique perspective on the hobby (both female and international), there's a ton of practical information in this issue - hints and tips for freelancers, artists, working with an editor, running a good game. There's a fantastic interview conducted by a ten-year-old gamer nerd girl. Ping explains why Go Play Northwest is so awesome. Kira Scott interviews Jessica Hammer. The Norwegian and Italian scenes. Tons and tons of interesting articles, seriously.

    \n
    \n Thanks so much, Jason! It was a labor of love by a lot of people. Can't thank the editors, artists and layout gurus enough.

    And the folks who contributed articles come from all walks of the hobby: some bright-eyed, bushy-tailed storygamer types like Kira and Ping, cheek by jowl with dyed-in-the-wool traditional writers, artists and designers like Liz Danforth and Jen Seiden Schoonover. Plus folks doing their very own thing, like Kat Jones and her college group who threw out the rules and went full-throttle freeform, and Francesca Garello and her Italian online narrative rp community that transforms their play into novels now in print.

    It's been a rare privilege to get to work with all these great folks.
