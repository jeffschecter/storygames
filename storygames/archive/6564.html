\n OK, so I've managed to gel some ideas for the mercenary pilots game I was rattling on about in this thread. I find I'm getting stuck as to where to focus my efforts. I seem to be dealing with two things, the core mechanic for dogfighting/conflict in the game and the overall structure of a session and where the mechanics fit in. I feel like I should have one hammered out before the other, but at the moment, I'm putting both together at the same time and it seems...inefficient.

My question to the experienced designers, do you find it easier to let the mechanics inform the structure of the session, or go from the top down, session structure to mechanics. To make a simpler analogy, do you design the engine to fit the car, or the car to fit the engine?

    \n Core mechanic, all the way. Sit with a friend and noodle with it until it feels right. Then start doing things you think should fit into a session while using the core mechanic to resolve the situations that arise from play. Take notes on how the session runs. Try to align what you want to happen with what's actually happening.

    \n I concur. First get the mechanic you want working the way you want it to.
    \n
    \nThen you can analyze the process that you were actually doing during the moments when it was working...and use that to hang the structure of the game on.
    \n
    \nIt doesn't hurt to go in with a vague notion of the sort of process you're thinking of as a guide, but IME its easier to alter the process of play around the needs of the engine, then to force the engine to fit a given structure.
