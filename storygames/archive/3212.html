\n (This started as a Game You Always Wanted but kinda migrated to little ideadom.)

I would like a single-session game that would simulate Battle Royale for a medium-to-large group.

(For those who don't know, Battle Royale is a Japanese novel / manga / film. A random high school class is kidnapped to an island. There, they fight until only one student remains. It's a big circus in the future fascist Japan.)

The game would be something where you could choose/roll student alignments / races / genders and then have some kinda interaction mechanics that would govern how easy it is for students to trust or fight each other. By alignments, I mean the Breakfast Club kinda stuff--jocks, delinquents, nerds, druggies, goths, etc.

It could use a Riddle of Steelish mechanic where when two characters encounter each other, they simultaneously throw a die -- red die = conflict, white die = diplomacy. So there's tension to encounters.

It could have a MLWMish endgame where if enough trust is built up among enough students, they can take down the system. Or, if you like, you can play for the solo win.

It'd have quick NPC-generation rules, and with rules for jumping back in if your character gets wasted.

Maybe it could have an American Idol frame, with running commentary from the game master, popularity awards, interviews, side battles between uninvolved family members. That might be lasersharking it though.

    This would also be a good way to keep players of dead characters involved. Perhaps you lose their player powers when your character dies, but gain some GMlike powers to create scenes and such via outside commentary?
    \nWe kind of do this in Classroom Deathmatch. All players, dead or alive, have the ability to frame scenes and introduce conflicts. Each player also has the responsibility of describing the Successful or failed actions of at least one other player. so even if your character is dead or currently out of the scene there is still plenty of stuff for you to do.

    Of course, character death is only a minor stumbling block. When your character does die (and he or she will almost certainly die at some point during the Classroom Deathmatch) you'll simply draw a new characterfrom the character pile and jump right in.
