\n I was trying to come up with a list of some one-liners/words of wisdom about SG type play and have the following:

Story is created in play.
Is it fun?
Create opportunity and provide feedback.
Player\u2019s actions matter, reinforce this to create immersion
Story is the culmination of player\u2019s goals.
Continuity fosters reality
Supply redundant and convergent paths
Drive the story with all you have.


what are some of yours that make you a better player, ref, or pundit?

    \n One I thought of just recently, appropriated from Thomas Edison:

    "Great gaming is 1% inspiration, 99% communication."

    This is related to the "be obvious, don't try too hard to be awesome" stuff upthread. A lot of people who have performance anxiety surrounding RPGs would benefit from my maxim, I think. You don't have to be brilliant to roleplay effectively, especially if you've got good situation-generating tools from your game. You just need to be a good listener. (Which is what "make each other awesome" is referring to as well, IME.)

    \n Don't paint, tell.

    Sometimes trying to make the forest seem scary and ominous by describing the deep shadows, muffled sounds, and low-lying fog is a waste of time.

    Just say "The forest is scary and ominous." Then describe the place, having cued the players how to interpret the description.

    This is another instance of "be obvious" aka "don't try so hard".

    \n Definitely aims to address spotlight issues.

    In the past it was easy for me to have a fantastic time as a player, get really invested in what\u2019s happening, want to accomplish a lot, and end up taking more than my share\u2019s worth of time. I was definitely a spotlight hog back in the day and I\u2019m now very careful to only take my fair portion.

    Groups are often filled with different people, some assertive and direct while others patient and subtle. It\u2019s easy for the assertive people to run over everyone else. It happens in conversations all the time. RPGs are essentially conversations. I find that sometimes people who are less assertive have amazing things to say so I want to make sure they have their time and space to say it.

    Making sure everyone else has had as much spotlight time as myself is an easy way to make sure I don\u2019t have too much spotlight time :-)
