\n I want to try my hand at running a game again and would like to give some PbtA variant a try, but for various reasons (Living in exile, not knowing any local gamers, the non-local gamers I know not being into online play, ...) it will probably have to be a one-shot when I'm back home for a visit soon.

Now, I realize many/most PbtA games shine best with at least a few sessions. Characters grow, conflicts snowball, relationships tangle up, etc. But from what I can tell many people are also playing them at conventions, so One-Shot play should be possible. In fact, some of the games I read (Urban Shadows and Night Witches) have explicit "So you want to run a this as a One-Shot" advice, which is great.

But I figure I'd tap the collective Story Games knowledge and see wether there were any PbtA games that are more suited to non-campaign play while still highlighting what's nifty about PbtA.

So, given me never having run AW or one of its descendants and neither of my prospective players having played in any of them, what game would you recommend we check out first and why?

    \n As far as I can see, no well-known/popular PbtA games are great for one-shot play, as written.

    All require some thought, design, and player skill to work in a one-shot setting (and many, many people do successfully manage it). However, the game's not going to deliver a satisfying one-shot for you... you have to bring the skills to do it yourself.

    If you feel comfortable running a one-shot in general, then chances are that using PbtA rules to do so is going to work great. If not... they're not going to help you do so.

    One suggestion:

    Tell people that what you're going to play isn't going to "wrap up" neatly. It will be an exploration into a new game (and perhaps GM style/technique), and it will be fun, but it will end just as it gets going.

    If everyone's on board with that, you're good to go! And, who knows, if you all have so much fun that you want to continue... you can discuss turning it into a longer-term game after the session.

    \n The ones that I've found work perfectly for a one-shot are:

    - Monsterhearts
    - Monster of the Week
    - Cartel
    - Bluebeard's Bride
    - Dungeon World
    - The Warren

    If you wanted to go further afield (away from hacks that rely on the 'roll 2d6' move structure), then you have:

    - Undying
    - Soth
    - Dream Askew
    - Superhuman

    I've had great one-shot experiences with all of those.

    \n Also, I completely agree with telling your players that the story of your session is not likely to wrap up neatly.

    Here are some things I regularly do in one-shots:

    - limit the available playbooks to select from
    - base initial scenes on the histories and bonds that players select for their playbooks
    - ignore advancement and experience. Instead, I give players a free advance about halfway through

    And a recent tip I read:

    - do 'End of Session' moves about halfway through your one-shot, and do new 'Start of Session' moves.
