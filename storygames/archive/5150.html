\n As I mentioned elsewhere, my Committee game didn't get off the ground. On the plus side, we played some fun games, including: Diamant, a neat German game of bluffing and chance with a fun Indiana Jones motif; Coloretto, a fun twist on the typical trick-making games; and Word Blur an interesting charades mod, where you search for word clues in a sea of 900 small chits. All three are recommended.

    \n\nPaulB \n\n\n
    \n
    \n\nDecember 2007 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n Lacuna, for the first time. An incredible amount of fun packed into a very small space: Two players finished two missions in about 2.5 hours, with 15 minutes of making characters in the beginning.

    There are a couple rules issues I would have liked to have seen tightened up in the text, but otherwise it ran amazingly well. By far my favorite of the indie-style little minigames, it now sits at the same OMFG-you-must-play-this level as Burning Empires and SotC.

    I'm curious about long-term Lacuna play. I suspect the Commendation Points thing is just barely enough bildungsroman juice to keep the players on the hook for a good long time -- 2-3 missions per night doesn't suck, either. I'm already brainstorming new Static Sheets for future pull-out-and-play nights.

    p.
