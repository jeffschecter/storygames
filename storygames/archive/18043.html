\n One of the items introduced in my game is the Dark Tarot. There are only six such decks in existence, and two of them happen to be in Redbank, one in the possession of the group's Infernal/Hollow.

The mechanic: there is a physical deck of cards. If you use it, you draw a card, and each card has a move. Essentially, roll Dark: on a 10+, something horrible happens, but you get to target it. On a 7-9, something horrible happens, on a miss, something really horrible happens.

The only card I have so far is the Death card:

On a 10+, choose an NPC with no strings on you. They will die this session.
On a 7-9, the MC will choose an NPC, they will die this session, or you can voluntarily choose the fail result.
On a miss, you will die this session*

*Which can be mitigated normally, of course.

Any other ideas for cards? They don't have to be limited to normal Tarot Major Arcana.

    \n When I ran World of Dungeons, I had the Deck of Many Things show up. The way I did it was this:

    Draw a card from the physical deck. Then roll +0*.

    On a 10+, interpret the card as you would a Tarot card to describe the beneficial effect it gives you.
    On a 7-9, interpret the card as you would a Tarot card to describe the beneficial effect it gives you but it comes with a cost, complication or compromise
    On a 6 or below, the GM interprets the card to describe the terrible curse it lays upon you.


    *No WoD stat seemed to apply but maybe a Monsterhearts one would.


    The idea was to make the deck unpredictable and potentially gamebreaking (by letting the player be open ended in their descriptions). We wound up with one warrior who was invisible except when wet (Partial Success, the dungeon was half flooded, so he didn't get much use) and a PC trapped as a face in the wall (Failure on the "Dungeon" card).
