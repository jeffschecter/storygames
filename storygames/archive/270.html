What the hell am I doing?

TonyLB
March 2006 edited March 2006 in Story Games

I hate playtesting. I hate playtesting. I hate playtesting.

It's a sadistic torture device created to mangle the ego and body of a game designer. Some fucker out there made this thing. Just another person I need to add to the list of "Track down and kill when I have the time."

Specifically, tonight, I am having the second playtest of Misery Bubblegum. Of course, it's really the first playtest for this set of rules, which has nothing in common (except a name) with the set that was used for the first playtest.

And I'm sitting here frantically trying to do, of all things, prep-work. Which would be possible if I'd played the game, and knew what sort of patterns it fell into. But which is just an exercise in futility at this stage in the rules. And yet, I am compelled: what if I don't prepare, and everything goes wrong because of that? What if I do prepare, and everything goes wrong because of that? What if I prepare wrong?

See, I know the game is going to go wrong. The point is for it to go wrong, so that I can fix the bits that don't work.

But I want it to go right. I want the one-in-a-million moment when it all comes together, and I know I have a winner, and all of the problems are little ones, barely speed-bumps on our reckless hurtling toward fun.

I want the twelfth (or whatever) playtest. I just don't want to have to experience the playtests two through eleven. Oh God how much I don't want that.

You know how, when you get a really bad stomach bug, the nausea itself is a killing, debilitating force. How you look forward to vomiting, because when you're done having your freakin' guts turned inside out for ten or twenty minutes there will be a blessed few minutes when you are not yet nauseous again.

I get that, and I sit in bed hating myself. I hate myself for giving in, for accepting that vomiting and agony is my fate, and being grateful for the tiny respites that my torturer, my body, offers as a way to make my later suffering even worse. I hate myself for beginning to be glad that I can vomit, because it honestly could be worse.

God, I hate myself so much right now. I'm beginning to be glad that I can playtest this thing, because not knowing whether I've got a working idea is worse. It's actually worse than playtesting. Worse than playtesting. Yes, I'm going to take my ideas and watch them go terribly, horribly awry. And I'm going to subject my friends to the utter bullshit which is my broken mockery of a system. And it would be worse if I couldn't. Doing this is better than not.

God, I hate playtesting. Hate, hate, hate!
