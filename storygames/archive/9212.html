    \n We are really enjoying it; since we aren't sure how long we'll play for, and since new plot material is easy to come by, I made a conscious choice prior to Episode 4 to reveal as much as possible to the players. That's why there were so many revelations about the NPCs, e.g. the Reverend's involvement with army deserters, Mr. Clay's sexuality, the mystery of Captain Josef's 'death' and so on.
    \n
    \nTony, who has had no prior experience of Hot War and had almost no preconceptions about the game, is playing his character in a very challenging way; he's not dallying with the dark side, he's embracing it wholeheartedly and his character, John, is prepared to do anything to achieve his hidden agendas. This is particularly interesting, as during the game creation session, it seemed much more like Dave's character, Major Connors, was going to be the really corrupt one, whereas Tony's seemed like a good man forced to make hard choices. There's a real gulf between the experience scene John Szilinski started with and where he is now: a murderous black marketeer making deals with inhuman entities...
    \n
    \nEpisode 4 was absolutley cracking; Tony got a taste of what the consequences of John's choices might be, when he spent half the episode languishing in jail, but the real capper was the final two scenes. First John got co-opted into helping his unit ambush the forces sent by the British to shut down the Factory, then in the next scene, Andrew is put in charge of those forces and discovers that Captain Josef (whom he knows nothing else about) is dead. I'm on tenterhooks to see what happens next week: will Tony go through with the ambush, since out-of-character he knows that Dave's character has a good chance of surviving it? Will Dave find an in-character way of sharing what they both know out-of-character? Dave was telling me that an ex-roleplaying companion of ours has been following our exploits and wishing he didn't now live in Scotland, since he's gagging to get into the game!
