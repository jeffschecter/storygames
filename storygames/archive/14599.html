\n From the Forge Provisional Dictionary:

Blood Opera
Play in which character generation focuses on potentially irreconcilable differences among at least some of the characters, and in which scenario generation is designed to put as much pressure on these differences (and therefore on unexpected alliances as possible). Notable for high mortality rates among characters. An example of Situation. Term coined by Ralph Mazza, Jake Norwood, and Ron Edwards.

I think this is sort of what In a Wicked Age does. What other games do you know that fall into this category? What game mechanics are best used for this kind of game?

A little bit of background: I've played a game of IAWA recently and I noticed a few things that irked me. Now I'm trying to find out what I can do about it, or how other games do it differently. If all fails, I might write my own little Blood Opera.

    \n I agree with Jonathan, blood opera is more a play style than a set of mechanics. It's much like a standard soap opera, in that everyone is very het up and emotional; the 'blood' part comes from everyone having access to deadly force with little to restrain their actions.

    Dust Devils and Cold City (and Hot War to a lesser extent) are other games where the default assumption is that the PCs are in opposition to each other.

    Neil: I agree, but wouldn't it be nice if we had a game with some procedural rules on scenario creation + a set of conflict rules that can do PvP properly?

    For example, Legends Of Alyria has Storymapping and can do PC vs. PC play perfectly well, but the conflict mechanics are extremely deadly.

    "What's at stake? You die or I die."

    \n I agree! Building complex, compelling, gameable situations is something some good gaming technology could help with.

    I find it works best when I pregenerate the characters. When I set up blood operas, I tend to have lots of ties between characters that are based on blood and sex. I also try to have two or three issues that have to be dealt with, each of which has two or three different positions that people can take. For instance, on the issue "Should we continue to give service to the Imperial overlords?" people can take positions of "Yes, as we become rich while doing the service," "Yes, as revolt would be too costly," and "No, we should regain our independence." You then have different characters take those different positions. With a few issues, you can make sure that no-one entirely agrees with anyone about everything. Throw in a few personal issues ("My best ally in council stole my wife.") and let the fun ensue!
