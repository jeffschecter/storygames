\n I'm pondering running an adventure that crosses-over the Young Ladies of Quality set-up with the Space 1889 setting.

One interesting aspect of the YLQ rules I want to use are the special abilities by nationality. Right now, those are:\n
Nationality
You may be American, British, French, German, Swiss, or Italian.
American girls get some benefit when attempting rash, bold, or dangerous things
British girls get some benefit in withstanding hardship or enduring duress
French girls get some benefit in dissembling and social exchanges
German girls get some benefit in physical exertion and weathering illness
Swiss girls get some benefit in making and using mechanical equipment
Italian girls get some benefit in languages and history
This benefit shall be one additional mark in the appropriate circumstances
\nWhat sorts of things could I add for Martian YLQs ( Hill, Canal, High, etc)?

What about girls of other nationalities or culture groups/backgrounds?

    \n This is an excellent idea!

    Canal Martian ladies might get a benefit when engaged in art or in cultural discourse (due to the stagnant, introverted and involved nature of their civilization).
    Hill Martian ladies might get a benefit when enduring privation (particularly thirst) or when handling animals (since most hill clans are nomads and live closely with one breed or other of Martian animal).
    There are no 'Ladies' among the High Martians, 'of Quality' or otherwise!
    ...
    OK, if you insist, and merely for completeness; how about High Martian 'ladies' getting a benefit when using their claws to fight and when eating meat raw. ::Gulp!::

    Belgians and Japanese are nationalities featured prominently in the 1889 setting. I'll have to think a bit to come up with suggestions for appropriate 'cultural stereotypes' for them. You might include Venusian lizard-ladies of quality; but that's almost as far afield as High Martian ones...

    Good luck with this, it sounds like it will be a blast!

    \n Glad you like it! I admit, I'm currently doing a horrible disservice to the games tha are inspiring this, by crash building an uber-light system to use with it, rather than either of the native systems.

    [To put this idea into context, I'm going to the local game con in a couple months and taking my 11 yera old daughter. Last year, she ended up in the kids room, playing what turned into an all-girls game of The Princes' Kingdom. I wanted to do something that would be fun, I'd enjoy running, and which would hopefully give Thren a chance to meet other gamer-girls around her age to hang out with over the weekend.]

    As for the High Martians? Well, I admit, I found it a bit far-fetched, too. OTOH, maybe the sly WormPriests have convinced Hattabranx to send one of his many grand-daughters off as a spy. Time in the school would be perfect cover for learning more about the Red Men from Earth. And, if the grand-daughter happens to become too friendly, well, Hattabranx has many granchildren ( mwahahahahaha!). Sort of like Ming the Merciless' daughter crossed with a Slytherin student, I think.

    I got nothin' for the lizard people, mostly because I'd been thinking of Mars, and it seems awfully dry for them. Maybe lizard folk have some different sort of gender ( maybe that's getting a wee bit Star Trek-y...)

    Any thoughts on folks from the rest of Earth? Indians? Native Americans? CowGirls of Quality?

    BTW- I think you really nailed the Martian types and I'll probably use those pretty much directly.
