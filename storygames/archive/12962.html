\n Good news: I may have found a regular gaming group for the first time in more than a year, and at least one person in it is open to the possibility of indie RPGs.
\n
\nBad news: I am dead broke. Really can't afford anything that's not a necessity broke. And I don't own any of the many indie RPGs I've played.
\n
\nSo I ask y'all--what indie RPGs out there are available as free PDFs? Ashcans are fine, so long as they aren't too dramatically different from the final version. I'm guessing this group is going to be more into action/adventure/monster bashing kinds of games, but really, I'll take a look at anything you suggest.
\n
\nI know Lady Blackbird's out there, and I think that could be a good first-time/getting to know you game. (I've only met one of the three possible players in this group before). What next?

    I'm guessing this group is going to be more into action/adventure/monster bashing kinds of games
    \nTry out Hell for Leather (the Free edition). It's bloody, full of action and uses a quirky dice mechanic. I've tried it with "traditional" gamers and with non-gamers, and it has worked really well. The game prompts people to add stuff to the fiction based on how actively they are contributing, making sure everyone gets a turn. You don't need to do any preparation before the game, it takes around two to three hours to play and no one has to be the GM. You can get it here for free.
