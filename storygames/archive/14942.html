\n So, I've long wanted to role-play with my kids, in a slightly more formalized way than just playing make-believe, for some time.
They've also wanted to play, but I've said I would run something when they could read. And now they can read enough to get by.
They are 7 and 6. They've heard Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone (nothing else yet), read by me, and the book on cd.
They really love it, and while I am not as enthusiastic, I think it could be a good starting place, playing young wizards at hogwarts, solving mysteries, learning spells, and winning points for Gryffindor.
I'd like to have some lite-rules, but not a lot. Maybe a handful of stats (represented by poly dice? they covet my dice).
Some spells they learn in her fake Latin, maybe written on cards.
Maybe some mechanics for conflict/combat resolution
Anyone already done this? No sense reinventing the wheel.

    \n Well I started with a very lite game.
    Three stats - Study, Sport, Magic.
    The kids got to choose which die (d4, d6, d8) they put on each stat.
    So far we've made characters and had a simple adventure, my daughter jumped right in, my son is having too much fun listening to her story, to think to do anything himself (today anyway)
    I've been basically dividing challenges into Easy (d4), Average (d6) and Hard (d8) though I have room for some others (Heroic d10, and Miraculous d12).
    They roll against the die, and failures usually just mean that. I plan to have a Bludge (fumble) and Brilliant! (success) like Broomstix.
    They haven't learned any spells yet, but that is coming. Along with gaining skill in classes they take.
    The Harry Potter Lexicon is proving a useful tool for language and spells, and such.
