PDF Lovers - how do you print, bind, etc., your games?

\n
\n
\n
\n\nM Eryesen \n\n\n
\n
\n\nJanuary 2008 \n edited January 2008 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n I've been getting more and more into PDF games rather than waiting for something to arrive by shipping. The local stores pretty much only carry d20/other trad/industry games.
\n
\nI got myself a decent laser printer and have been thinking more about what I'm going to do about my books.
\n
\nFor my copy of 9 Worlds I bought a while back, I printed it, took a manilla file folder, chopped out the bottom and a fixed it to the pages with 9 binder clips. It looks ghetto but it's been tough as nails (and it's cool to hold a book with metal binding).
\n
\nI know some people take their PDF and have it printed and coil bound. Other people always reduce the size and make digest sized books regardless of the original PDF.
\n
\nAnyone have any other ideas? What do you do with PDFs you buy? Just view them on screen? Always have a laptop at play? Something else?

    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n Duplex print onto either A4 or A5. I use 100 gsm, I'm not quite as fancy as Neil ;^)

    Large A4 books (over 80 pages, say) go into a 4-ring binder. For example, I bought Legends of Shaintar (which, despite having a really cheesy name is actually a damn fine product) which is a 256 page PDF and that gets it's own binder.

    Small books (say, less than 80 pages) usually get comb-bound. Unless they're part of a bigger thing, like I have a ring binder of Savage Worlds Toolkit books which all go together.

    I try to avoid, wherever possible, buying PDFs which are not A4/Letter. They're too much of an arseache to bind. I'll get the hardcopy or not buy the product if I don't want it enough to justify the expense of the hardcopy. This generally means that most of the indie games I own are hardcopy. I need a hardcopy at hand to play a game, so buying a PDF I can't print and bind easily is pointless.

    \n A friend of mine owns a gigantic color laser printer he got at a massive discount from the hospital where he works. I actually did print out Burning Empires on that beast (digest-size, though).

    For B&W printouts of medium page count PDFs, I print them on the sly on the office laser printer where I work. Usually in digest-size, three-hole-punched and bound tight with thin ribbon; sometimes just stapled. OctaNe, Truth & Justice, etc.

    Small books I can print on my aging inkjet printer at home. In a Wicked Age, Wilderness of Mirrors, anything with a page-count under 50, or it will take all night.

    Finally, some things go to Kinkos for professional duplex printing, spiral binding, and color covers, if they're special and big enough to be unwieldy printed single-sided. Sons of Liberty, Agon, Dogs in the Vineyard; stuff like that.

    But I carry close to 2 Gigs of gaming PDFs on the JumpDrive on my keyring, so they're handy for easy reference. I've even bought PDFs of things I own in professional hardcopy, just to be able to carry them in my pocket.

    \n If it's a short game, I print out the pages and put them in plastic sheathes that go into a 3 ring binder. If it's a long game, I have the copy shop print it directly to a copier for 3 cents a page, then depending, either:

    a) get some office clips to hold it together
    b) hole punch it and toss it in the binder
    c) plastic sheathe it, if I really like the game and am feeling OCD. So far, the only long game to receive this treatment is the Zodiac Final Fantasy RPG.

    \n When we open our store, this is one of the services we are going to off our local customers.
    \n
    \nPrint and perfect bind their PDF game books. We have a small perfect binder so we can easily do one off book bindings, then trim trim it so it looks like any other perfect bound book we traditionally produce.
    \n
    \nThe only down side is, most PDF book publishers print a "cover" page, but not a back cover page. So not too sure how to make the back cover of the book look good. Maybe we'll be able to eventually talk more PDF publishers to offer a pre formated cover for print and binding of their PDF. In the meantime, I suppose I can make the back covers a flat color of the customers choice, or even duplicate the front cover onto the pack or put some other kind of artwork onto it.

    \n Nothing fancy for me, since I generally don't print large documents. If I'm buying a hefty rulebook then I'll get a published copy rather than PDF.

    Usually I pick up a few "display book" folders, the type with transparent sleeves bound into them, from the local supermarket; cheap, nasty items, made in Poland I believe, but entirely functional... and I'm not doing this so that I can show off what a pretty book I have.

    In a Wicked Age... I printed single page A4 on my Brother laser printer, whereas things like Blackmoor and Greyhawk usually get printed two pages to the page, just to cut down on paper usage. On the other hand, my various T\xe9kumel-related printouts take up way too much room for those folders, so they occupy a number of ring binders.

    \n I print mine booklet-style on legal paper, fold it in the middle, punch four tiny holes on the fold, and then thread some string through the holes--round and round and then I tie it off. It's pretty low tech, but it looks okay and seems fairly durable, especially if I put a cardstock cover on it before I sew it together.

    I haven't tried it on anything really big yet, though. I don't think it will work so well for that.
