\n Hi everyone! This is my first time here and as you may notice from the nickname I am from Italy. So hello :P

I'm here basically to spam about a little new game I wrote yesterday. Even if it's quite "young" as a game, it curiously has been playtested already a couple of times, this night, by a friend of mine.

The game is named 111 mph. Its original name was 180 Km/h, but translating it to english I changed also the notation. That's more or less the speed at which stabilizes a human body in free fall, according to wikipedia.

111 mph infact is a small rpg (I think I can call it a RPGpoem...) for 2 players about a parachutist whose parachute doesn't open (so now you understand why it's a fast game...). He cannot but think of his whole life, which is passing in front of his eyes for 5 minutes, before he splats to the ground.

One of the players is the parachutist, and will have 5 minutes to answer 40 questions asked by the other player. Every time he vacillates, he starts over (the timer will go on, obviously).

In the game experience I imagined the situation should be so frenetic that, after a while, the parachutist stops inventing things, to gain time, and starts answering with his personal facts. I don't know the correct term to describe this situation; in Italy we just say that when you merge this way with your character you're entering in the unsafe part of the game experience. I think that comes from a book by Graham Walmsley, but I'm not sure.

There's not much else to say: here's the download link (you'll be redirected by a black screen which is needed for the download counter to work): http://revanadler.altervista.org/trashmeetssteel/DN_180_eng.php

If you're interested in my other games (they all are in ITA, though, except for Brachininae...) here's the production page from my blog: http://trashmeetssteel.blogspot.com/p/produzioni.html

The number of questions in the game and the timing will probably be revised after several playtests, so if you play it please let me know about it
