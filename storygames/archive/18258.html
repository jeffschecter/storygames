\n Hi everyone,

First up, I'm a bit of a "long time lurker, first time poster". Hi there.

Secondly, apologies to anyone who has already seen this over on the UK Roleplayers Forum.

And, thirdly, apologies if I've posted this in the wrong section.

Okay - I'd like to ask something in relation to gaming (and something I'm working on for my Masters course - I'm making a resource guide to information resources about a topic of my own choosing, and I've chosen roleplaying games.)

What I would like to ask you is this: what are your "go to" sources of information about RPGs? (The term information is deliberately broad - it could be news, reviews of games, play advice, resources for a particular game such as scenarios and character sheets, discussion of game design, etc ... )

The format doesn't matter - so it could be an RPG discussion forum like this one or ukroleplayers, somebody's blog that you find highly reliable (again, it could be about play advice, or game design, or reviews of games), a podcast, a particular company's website, a Twitter feed, etc.

It could also be a source dedicated to a particular game (such as yog-sothoth.com for Cthulhu stuff or a website full of resources for one particular game), or gaming in general (for example a forum such as this one).

Enormous thanks in advance for any responses ...

Cheers,
Cam.

    On a side note: does anyone know the nomination process for game awards? A friend asked me the other day and I couldn't rightly say. I just kind of assumed it was certain games with community presence?
    \nDepends on which game awards you're looking for. For the Ennies and the Origins Awards, the publisher has to submit their work for consideration (and then the submissions are winnowed down to a shortlist which is voted on by fans). For the Diana Jones Award, the DJA committee decides on the nominations themselves.
