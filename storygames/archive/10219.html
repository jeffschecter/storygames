\n I unexpectedly ended up without a group this Saturday, and I was really jonesing for some play. I thought to myself: wouldn't it be nice if that whole Play Now! movement we tried to get going a couple of years ago had taken off?

I've had mixed success with online play. Some of the best games I've ever played were online, but they usually ran out of gas after a few sessions. There were invariably some scheduling conflicts, and this often arose from one of the great benefits of online play: getting to play with people who live nowhere near you. Long-term play is often impossible.

What if we did online games like Games On Demand? A few people hang out chatting online, maybe playing boardgames, until enough folks show up to play a one-shot? This idea is still in its infancy. Any thoughts?

By the way, if all you have to offer are reasons why this won't work, don't bother participating. I'm asking for only positive and enthusiastic participation in this thread.

    \nThere's this online freemium (pay for all the bells & whistles) game I've played: Puzzle Pirates.

    In it, there are "the game tables". You walk your little sprite up to the table, click it, and you're now on a menu wher you could join, leave, and start lots of card and other games in play - hearts, spades, poker, a 'drinking game', etc. Each game has a chat box. There are always people playing.

    If there was a site that did exactly that bit, but also had a voice channel, and a "run a tabletop game online with built-in utilities", I would spend so very very much time there that it would hurt me.
