    Posted By: Neko EwenSavage Worlds. [snip] None of the settings I've seen really grabbed me either, though I may be getting jaded and/or weird.
    \nYou might want to look at Low Life, Necessary Evil, and 50 Fathoms. Those're the ones that caught my attention as the most-novel. Plus, Low Life is all Andy Hopp, which is the next best thing to Brian Froud.

    My non-purchase non-regrets:
    In the spirit of the thread, i think, i'm not going to simply list every RPG i haven't bought, and don't intend to buy--it's a long list with, frex, most D20 System stuff on it (which, right there, makes for a long list). Rather, i'll focus here on specific games that i had some experience with and know aren't for me.

    D&D3[.5]E: When this first came out, i dismissed it. Then, due to extenuating circumstances, i played in a lengthy campaign (~2.5yrs). I discovered that a lot of my preconceptions were wrong--usually in good ways (that is, something i thought was wrong with it, wasn't), but sometimes in bad ways (discovering that one of the bits i thought was good, wasn't). And that many of these misconceptions were due to the horrible organization and writing in the D&D3E PH, rather than the rules themselves--once i got my hands on the D20SRD, i actually could read and understand the rules. That was in the first few sessions. I think damn near every game session after that, for well over two years of nearly-weekly gaming, i discovered yet another new thing about the system that at least bugged me, and oftentimes that drove my nuts. Of all the things that bug me about D&D3E, however, only one of them is inherent to D20 System, however--of course, that's the d20: it's just too flat and wide, relative to everything else, for satisfactory play. Too damn random. Nonetheless, there are easy fixes (mid20, ubiquitous Take 10, hero points) for that, unlike for most of the other problems i have with D&D3E. I'm still firmly in the "Wow! state-of-the-art RPG design--for '93" camp in my overall opinion, nonetheless. While i'll play other D20 System games, there are just too many things in D&D3E that i can't stand. And i never have bought it, and don't regret it for a minute. Even if someone were to "force" me to run a fantasy D20 System game, i've got True20, Iron Heroes, Blue Rose, Arcana Unearthed, and Everquest D20, all of which i consider vastly superior to D&D3[.5]E in one or more ways.

    I was a huge fan of the old Werewolf, and a moderate fan of the old Mage (i liked the first edition, and each subsequent edition moved further and further from what i liked about it--so i never bought any subsequent editions). So i was really looking forward to the nWoD games. So, I bought the new Werewolf, because i could get that and the nWoD core book for something like $30, total, from somebody. I dunno--i keep trying to read it, and just can't. I make it another few pages, and get bored. Compared to the original Werewolf, which i pretty much sat down and read in one marathon session (with some eating and sleeping thrown in), because i couldn't stop. So, given that, the fact that gold (the color) really bugs me, the fact that every previous iteration of Mage moved it further and further from what i wanted, and the reviews i've read seem to indicated that this trend has continued with the newest version, i don't think i regret not buying Mage: the Awakening, despite loving Mage: the Ascension (at least at first).
