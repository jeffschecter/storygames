    Speaking for HQ, this is the discussion of the Heortling Duck on one hand, and the discussion of Arkat as Troll on the other. Basically it's unusual, but possible in HQ for somebody to be brought up in a culture that's unusual for the species. A common exception is a Duck raised in a Heortling village. The duck gets the duck species, but the Heortling cultural package.

    Further it's theoretically possible that a character has spent his life split between more than one culture, and has learned both - like my real life friend Sean who grew up in both Paris and in Madison here in the US. He is truely bicultural (having him surprise French people with his perfect unaccented French when they're being rude to a supposed American is very fun).

    More common, however, is to have the "Traveler" keyword, where one knows the languages and cultural traditions of other places, but is not truely poly-cultural. I suggest that a Traveler Secret would be awesome in TSOY (if it doesn't already exist).

    Now, as to species, though it seems absurd to assume that one can change species... that's exactly what Arkat did in Glorantha. At one point be decided to become a troll. This requires a heroquest - basically a great magic ritual - but it can be done.

    Now there's some argument about whether or not Arkat physically transformed into a troll, or whether he was simply culturally accepted as a troll. But that's all Glorantha stuff. For Near, or another world, it's as plausible as you want to make it. I like to think that Arkat actually changed into a Troll - that's much more magical. But for your game, you're going to have to decide for yourself whether this is possible. If you do, of course, you're deciding to allow the character to change what is likely something that is very core to his existence. I think that's cool, but it's pretty wooly.

    Mike
