\n Hi folks,

I got inspired by Nerdwerds playbook post and tried to come up with something that fills in another role in AW. I'm pretty sure its missing something to differentiate it from a hardholder and a solace more clearly, so if you have ideas/feedback, please fire away! The text underneath the name is more descriptive than evocative at the moment. Alternate title was "The Noble" or "The Duchess." I'm sort of picturing a fallen lord of a crumbling castle taken over by a hardholder's force because of its strategic value, or a noble farmer who's property has been invaded by a gang and she's doing the best she can to keep it running and maintain her place in the chaos.

The Blueblood
You\u2019re not in charge, probably, but you\u2019ve got something they want. A mansion or a farm, the deed to a lab full of hi-tech parts, maybe in your basement there\u2019s a gaping hole where the psychic maelstrom comes pouring out. And you know how to leverage promises and obligations in order to keep yourself safe.

MOVES
You get two Noble moves.

Proper Etiquette: Roll+sharp to manipulate instead of +hot.

Entitled: When buying something costing you at least 1-barter, either goods or services, roll+sharp. On a 10+, you receive it for free as is your due. On a 7-9, get a 1-barter discount or something else thrown in to sweeten the deal. On a miss, you\u2019ve offended and may be charged extra or denied sale.

The Gauntlet: When your position is threatened or when you\u2019re disrespected, you may request a champion from those players present. Anyone who steps up to protect you may mark experience. or maybe Fair Maiden?

Noblesse Oblige: When you administer to the poor or the helpless (NPCs) or offer them free access to something from your domain, roll+sharp. On a 10+ hold 3, on a 7-9, hold 1. Spend your hold 1 for 1 to have them do you favors, like run errands or carry messages, or spend 3 hold to ask someone to do something more dangerous.

We Live in Forever: Threatening you in your domain is acting under fire.

    \n It seems like the core of the character concept is someone who trades on their reputation, yeah? People treat them differently, like maybe they are a remnant of the Golden Age themselves. Maybe like the Touchstone is a symbol of hope and a better tomorrow, this character is a symbol of sadness for a yesterday lost. Which makes it hard for people to treat them like a normal person. It could work as a supplement or expansion for the Hardholder, Hocus, and Maestro D' pretty well, too.
