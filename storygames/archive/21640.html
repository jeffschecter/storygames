    \n I mean, I still love GenCon for actually playing games. I generally hang out in the Games on Demand area and play something in literally every slot of the convention that I'm not using to get food or run games for friends. I went to Origins last year for the first time, and did the same thing \u2014\xa0Games on Demand area the whole time. It means I'm not ever playing the big popular games, but that's not what I go to gaming conventions for anyway \u2014\xa0it's usually to discover new indies I wouldn't have come across on my own, or to finally play indies I've been curious about.

    \n So, the only gaming con I've had trouble finding games to play is a specific very small gaming con, and I've handled that by running games at it. (It has plenty of games for its size, but the ones I want go fast.)

    Dreamation, DexCon, Metatopia, Gen Con, and Origins before the date shift (I have no opinion on Origins after the date shift as we've not gotten back there because so many conventions and only so many days in the year) -- I've generally had no trouble filling my schedule to overflowing. I don't have a lot of time to make Games on Demand at Gen Con because, despite promising myself I'll leave Huge Spaces for exactly that... well, there's all these tasty looking games on the schedule, and I put them into my wish list, and... I get some subset of them that I really want to play.

    I do a lot of gming when not at conventions. So, even when I do run a game or two at conventions (yeah, okay, I got talked into running a couple of sections of Clockwork: Dominion at Gen Con this year, which wasn't hard), I really, no fooling, absolutely am there TO PLAY!

    ...and shop. And talk to people in the spaces between games.
