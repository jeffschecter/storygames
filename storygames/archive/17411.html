    \n I'll play... As long as you promise to replace that awful Batman film with a proper Noir film like Kiss Kiss Bang Bang [rant](Bale can't act, Batman is not a ninja and the Batmobile is not a monster truck)[/rant] (Tim Burton FTW!)

    So. Anyway (Apocalyspe World Style)

    Agenda:
    Make the world and its people in shades of grey with featuires that reflect a disenchanted universe.

    Principles
    Keep the world small and intimate.
    Respond with rewards that come with a cost.
    Look at your NPCs with an eye towards the skeletons in their closets.
    Occasionally, an NPC acts with no hidden agenda. These people shine brightly and sometimes fade quickly.
    Look at your NPC's through the lens of their vices...The ones that can bring them down.
    There is no right. Just less wrong.

    \n StuMc - love those principles - especially the "Look at your NPCs with an eye towards the skeletons in their closets. "
    The "Respond with rewards that come with a cost" is also pretty good - now you have what you wanted, did you really want all the baggage it comes with?

    The aim isn't the violence of AW, but to always make the NPCs objects of suspicion and doubt... who'd side are they really on?
    And in good Noir the characters are always in doubt. If i use those principles, then add the regular (make move, misdirect, never speak name, name everyone, fan of the characters, offscreen) then that is a pretty solid set of principles.

    The follow up for this is the fronts... creating fronts is what really builds the colourful world of apocalypse world. The hacks I like (dungeon world, the sprawl) have different rules and setups for fronts (campaign & dungeon, corporations and sites), which leads to the question - what is the collective grouping in Noir? I'm leaning towards a "mystery" or "enigma". A thing which arouses suspense and curiosity because of facts concealed...

    Things like Violence (a murder mystery), Greed (Hiest), Lust (cheating husband etc)... Hmm, maybe the deadly sins might be the defining feature of the mystery....

    \n@Rickard. Awesome. I've got this series on DVD and I love it. "The batmansignal" is particularly amusing.

    @Dionysus I've been thinking about a "Noir World" game all day and I reckon you need a special "Flaw Move for each character. What I would do is make the move fictionally bad but mechanically irresistible.

    eg.
    Carouse. Your character is fond of bars and parties and won't often pass up an opportunity to partake.
    Roll plus temperance (or vice or flaw or whatever)

    On a 10+ No problem, you get drunk, fall down, get back up again and somehow wake up alone in your own bed with your integrity intact. Mark experience and take +1 forward.

    On a 7-9, Mark experience or take +1 forward and then choose two
    Your reputation is intact, you don't gain a condition that may impact your personal or professional life.
    You don't blow a wad of cash on a night you can barely remember.
    You don't wake up in a strange place and/or with a strange person.

    Most of the time, people are going to be tempted by the XP IMO nicely modeling the way people succumb to vices.

    As for fronts, I like "Curiosities" as Noir stories often balance the depressing inevitability of the world with a strong element of discovery. (I'm assuming a detective type of game)

    The categories of Curiosities could then be related to the seven deadly sins with subcategories under each. They could even be used to drive the creation of vices like Gluttony leading to Carouse above.
