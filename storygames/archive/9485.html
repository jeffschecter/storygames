How do you plan to adapt oracle games?

\n
\n
\n
\n\nSeth Drebitko \n\n\n
\n
\n\nJune 2009 \n edited June 2009 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n I was curious in what ways people planned to implement Oracle games into their product lines (if people have them) or even just their hobby in general? I personally am still tweaking out my \u201cStory Game System\u201d and the oracle games have given me some great ideas to adding on to my services I plan. The way I want to fit oracle games in would be:
\n
\n\u201cStory Game System\u201d core rules for free with expanded print edition.
\nSupplements would come in 4 flavors
\n
\nShort Stories \uf0df (the oracle game) super cheapo with some smattering of lexicon words to evoke a concept, and some rules to bridge them to the story game system. This would be an ultra cheapo thing like around a dollar if even that. The short story would include attached document of cards that can be printed out and have individual lexicon words or phrases that when combined like a deck with other short stories could be shuffled and dealt out to create unique oracle games.
\n
\nNovellas \uf0df More detail on the setting in the form of a detailed adventure that incorporates the lexicon information from the short story.
\n
\nNovels \uf0df Loads of detail expanding on the base concepts as this will be a full campaign, detailing a bit beyond the lexicon.
\n
\nSagas \uf0df This would just be a bundle of the above.
\n
\n Any way with all the buzz about this little micro games I thought I would see what other people had in mind as far as what to do with them.
\n
