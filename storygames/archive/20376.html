\n What stuff should we watch, read, play, etc?

1. Feel free to respond here in-thread to another link posted.

a. If you're looking to start a conversation, perhaps take your comment and create a new thread with it in the Stuff to Watch category.

b. Otherwise, to prevent too many comments in the thread, number your response (first response: [1]; second response [2]; third response [3]). If you see that you're the second person to post, "Wow, that looks cool!" to a post here, then put a "[2]" at the top of your post. Three posts ([3]) is the limit: The fourth person to post should just take their comment and create a new thread, to carry the discussion over there further.

Why?

Because these threads are great: We can link to little things being talked about on the web in general, on blogs, or other forums that might be noteworthy, without having to dedicate a new thread to each and every one (although that, too, is fine if you want to discuss the item further or "open up a discussion").

Responding to these posts with a comment or two is understandable (other times there might be no comments, just because the link was self-explanatory: nothing to say, lots of heads nodding in agreement). But we don't want to create a huge discussion in the middle of the thread: Some folks get a lot out of these threads, and don't want to look back to see the "10 latest posts" just to see that it's a heated discussion of something from a previous page.

Go!

    \nMasks: A New Generation is on Kickstarter. It's a PbtA game about teen superheroes and it has some nice incremental developments -- instead of trading Strings, the characters swap Labels reflecting their self-image. It's a nice idea that I immediately want to tinker with. Also, the MC agenda has a section where there are specific Moves for each of the playbooks -- I'm not sure if that's brilliant or excessive, but it's interesting.

    \n I am working on a 2-players game called Maelstrom Dreamers - with an AW style setting, and totally different mechanics (I've borrowed from Paul Taliesin \u201cAs the Worm Turns\u201d and added a final resolution mechanic based on dreams - real dreams).
    The game is played in short, very simple sessions of three scenes, and it could run for quite some time (basically you're waiting for one player, the Dreamer, having a dream in real life between sessions). So it has quite a peculiar pace...

    I am in a closed-beta testing phase, but if anyone is interested please drop me an email or a message here and I can provide by mail a copy of the game (I am fine with curious readers but definitely prefer people interested in testing).

    \nWrath of the Autarch is now on kickstarter! It's an episodic kingdom building game that features troupe based play, no gamemaster, and an asymmetric competition between one player (who takes on the side of the Empire of the Autarch) and the other players (who build a society int he Empire's shadow).

    If you like stuff like Ars Magic, Birthright, or Crusader Kings, this might be up your alley!

    \nMalandros is now on Kickstarter.

    It's a kind of laid-back slice-of-life game with periodic outbursts of drama or violence. It's set in 19th century Rio de Janeiro and powered by DramaSystem - with some mechanics lifted from elsewhere too and a couple I might have invented. One of the playtesters said the game creates stories like a telenovela and he is probably right.
