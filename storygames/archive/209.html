GM Earning His Power

Adam_Dray
February 2006 edited February 2006 in Story Games
In games with a GM, why not force a GM to do certain things to earn some of his GM powers.

This would work cool with Flags. Every time the GM introduces something that tweaks a player's Flag, the GM gets a point that can be used to do more cool stuff to the players. (Notice that this works for games without a GM, too.)

Instead of "Mother, May I?" abilities, now you have "Daddy, Do Me!" abilities.

You need to make sure that if he can't earn powers, the game doesn't stall, though. That means giving players all the power to push the story forward.

  • Eric Provost

    I had something like that in my Villains of Safinubi heartbreaker. Players authored people, places, or things that they wanted to see show up on their character sheet. The GM would then earn currency for making his own NPCs cooler every time one of those player-authored flags showed up. With no flags, the GM got no currency. With no currency the players would always walk right over the GM's NPCs. It seemed to work out fairly well in the few playtests we had.

    -Eric
