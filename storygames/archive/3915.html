    \n I'm actually the eponymous bitch not allowed at the table, so the comment kind of hurt my feelings in particular. It was a nice little reminder that the world is still very segregated into men vs. women for some people (of both types). I certainly wouldn't support a woman saying "no pigs at the table" either. I think it was actually really interesting to see what a nerve the comment touched. A lot of women have felt excluded by such attitudes, and a lot of men have felt their "them" time attacked.

    In general, I think a lot of misogynist attitudes are tolerated in gamer culture (to use a sweeping term) that have been driven out of most other areas of life. A lot of people are willing to give geeks an "uncomfortable around women" pass, but fuck that. It's 2007; being "uncomfortable" around women is just as worthy of derision as being "uncomfortable" around black people. They should grow up and accept that people are more than their chromosomes.

    \n The disconnect in that thread is pretty obvious, in retrospect. Two things are clear:

    1. What Bob said was misogynist, and
    2. Bob may be the nicest guy alive. Or not. Nobody knows.

    Very, very few people actually implicitly agreed that what Bob said was cool. But a lot of people reacted against the knee-jerk reactions that Bob's misogynist statement necessarily meant that Bob was the Great Satan, since there were a million possible reasons why he said what he said in the way he said it, and we were not given enough context to properly judge Bob. Some of those people responded to the fire-and-brimstone comments against Bob's person by vehemently defending not only him as a person, but also the statement, in an excellent example of Internet message boards communication strategies.

    Rinse and repeat for 107 pages.
