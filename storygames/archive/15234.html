\n I realized that the game text that most helped me was not a game at all but Play Unsafe. That other thread specifically said 'game' so I didnt list it there, but it needs props.

My gaming has been on another level since reading it. Which is interesting because we sometimes neg overarching advice on these forums - there isnt supposed to be one-size-fits-all-games advice. But for me, anyway, this was it.

The attitude in Play Unsafe is viral. If I model don't-shoot-down and yes-and, it's catching. At least in the session itself, and maybe beyond...

Anyhow, cheers to Graham.

    \nI always associated it with " Do The Obvious" from Play Unsafe.

    Which really sounded stupid to me as a catch-phrase, but made more sense with further description.

    "Do the Obvious" hinges on the fact that The Obvious isn't obvious to everyone equally. It's actually very personal.

    Given a situation with a whole lot of varying information, multiple people will, not shockingly once considered, come to different conclusions on the next event in ways ranging from subtely different to radically divergent.

    Don't Try to be Awesome ties in with Do the Obvious. DTTBA says don't wait or plan or build up a whole Rube Goldberg-esque series of conditionals, because the effort isn't worth the payout. DTO says you'll almost always be right with less effort and more immediately.

    \n Okay, I think this is my favorite paragraph:\n
    In games, whether we mean to or not, we often shoot others' ideas down:

    Player: "I search for another entrance!"
    GM: "No, there's only the main door."

    Player 1: "I force you to kneel in front of me."
    Player 2: "I break free and stand up."

    GM: "She moves towards you seductively."
    Player: "I push her away."

    It's hard to define "shooting ideas down" exactly, but you'll know it when you see it.
    \nBut lots of other good stuff: go obvious, 'yes, and/but', 'let your guard down', 'create a mystery and solve it', 'get to the action' ... I'd forgotten how many gems are in here.
