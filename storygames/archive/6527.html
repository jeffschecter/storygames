    \n I'm trying to do this in as few words as possible, so please interpret the words broadly

    0. The Fool: Beginnings
    1. The Magician: Masculinity, Experience, Creation, Mastery
    2. The High Priestess: Femininity, Intuition, Subconscious Mind
    3. The Empress: Maternal Authority
    4. The Emperor: Paternal Authority
    5. The Hierophant: Spiritual Authority
    6. The Lovers: Love
    7. The Chariot: Paths
    8. Strength: Strength
    9. The Hermit: Wisdom
    10. The Wheel of Fortune: Destiny, Chance
    11. Justice: Justice
    12. The Hanged Man: Asceticism, Enlightenment
    13. Death: Death
    14. Temperance: Synthesis
    15. The Devil: the Id
    16. The Tower: Crisis
    17. The Star: Achievement
    18. The Moon: Hidden Strength, Subconcious
    19. The Sun: Glory, Life
    20. Judgement: Endings, Resurrection
    21. The World: Everything

    \n Yeah, I know. Like I said, interpret broadly. I think "change, evolution, something ends and something new begins" can all be inferred from "death."

    I say, within the context of a story, consider letting Death actually mean death (of what? A person? A relationship? A company? A dream?) because it keeps the stakes much higher. I think this is why you see it on TV all the time; the writers are intentionally taking the more dramatic reading.
