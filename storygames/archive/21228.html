\n As mentioned before, I'm looking for freeform/semi-larp games to be played with 10-12 teenagers. Game sessions will be 2 and 3 hours.

Another idea that's on the table is a Superhero Wedding.

Characters
- Marrying superhero couple
- Superheroes in disguise (ca. 2/3 of players; looking normal, hiding secret identity & power)
- Ordinary people (ca. 1/3 of players; lovers, press)
- 1 Superhero Villain

Game play
Each player gets a character card with specific information. Facilitator guides through the scenes. Scenes are played out in-character.

Scenes
- Greeting: marrying superhero couple reveals themself, welcomes all guests
- Partners: lovers reveal themselves, choose their partner
- Jealousy: one of the characters (superhero) chooses a love interest among the couples, creating jealousy/rivalry.
- Revelation: press characters gets to reveal the identity of one other character.
- Attack: Villain chooses victim and attacks (i.e. transform oxygen around victim), the closest superheor next to victim may use superpower (i.e. slow time)
- Wish: each (surviving) character writes a wish for the rest of the wedding, wraps the paper and places it in a bowl. Facilitator randomly picks 3 wishes, paraphrases appropriate events for the rest of the wedding
- Goodbye: all guests say goodbye and leave the wedding.


Any ideas on this? Does this sound workable/interesting? Any other similar or useable games?
