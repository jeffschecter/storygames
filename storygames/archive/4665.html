    \n "go play" is already trademarked in three trademark classes, including entertainment.

    I'm not very familiar with IP law, and not that anyone is terribly serious about this issue, but as I understand it, this is how these things play out:

    If you're a mom and pop store operating in a small region using an unregistered mark (McDonald's for example) and a large international company (McDonald's for example) comes along and registers the mark with the USPTO, you get to continue your use of the mark, but only in your current region of operation and only for the very specific use to which it was previously put. I don't think you even have to oppose the trademark application to maintain those rights.

    It gets trickier when you are a small outfit on the web using your mark internationally, but I believe the result is essentially the same. If M$ filed a tm application for "go play" (ignoring the existing registered marks for the moment) you could continue to use "go play" in the same manner that you had previously used it. If you expanded your use of the mark, however, M$ would be within their rights to come down on you.

    Practically speaking, what M$ would do is come down on you anyway, and file an infringement suit against go-play.org or Rob or whomever, and allege infringement. That would suck, because defaulting on that might lead to a judgment against the defendants (whoever they would properly be in this case). And if you truly wanted to defend the suit, you'd need to find many many dollars to do so. The likely result would be some release of any rights to the mark in exchange for a dismissal of the action.

    \n It's not about use, it's about copyright. We didn't attempt to copyright the phrase. But as others have pointed out, other companies have already used the phrase, and may have copyrighted it. Or they may not have. Microsoft also may not have copyrighted it, making this whole discussion entirely moot.
    \n
    \nAnd maybe they have. I still kinda doubt they're going to attempt anything, even if they we do end up on their radar. People intentionally use legitimately copyrighted phrases and images all the time on the internet, and the big corps don't bother going after them all, because there's no central organization to bodyslam. Same with this. They may send out a hundred C&D orders to specific individuals "making money" off the Go Play phrase and logo, but that's not going to stop the majority of people. It won't stop the small, off-the-radar Go Play events. It won't make me, or anyone else, stop using the logo in our icons. It won't stop people not sent the C&D order from opening up their own cafepress stores to sell Go Play merchandise.
    \n
    \n...and I think Microsoft knows this. It's not worthwhile, even for them. It's like trying to swat a million flies, who all happen to be minding their own business.
