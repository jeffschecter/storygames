    \n So, why'd you choose the famous hands from Michelangelo's The Creation of Adam? It's a rather controversial painting with a lot of really cool theories behind it.

    My favorite interpretation (from John Ortberg, in God Is Closer Than You Think) is that God is eager to reach out to Adam, who -- already created and alive -- cannot be bothered to lift a finger to meet Him. Also, there's a good deal of evidence that the big shape that God is flying around in is a depiction of the brain.

    So, do these ideas play into your thoughts of Sufficiently Advanced?

    \n My main interpretation of that image was "stretching towards divinity." I had no idea that there was such controversy around it. The whole point of Sufficiently Advanced is that it deals with entire civilizations of people who have almost godlike powers, and what sorts of things they'd do, so it seemed like a good link to me. I was also riffing off the cover, which has the universe inside a person.

    Posted By: Adam DrayMy favorite interpretation (from John Ortberg, inGod Is Closer Than You Think) is that God is eager to reach out to Adam, who -- already created and alive -- cannot be bothered to lift a finger to meet Him.
    \nOK, I'm guilty now: thread drift, here I come....

    I'd always thought (or maybe I was taught decades ago) that the symbolism indicates that we are near to--but never can touch--Divinity. And even if we could bridge that narrow divide--less than a finger's width--we'd touch merely a fingertip (neither are reaching to grasp or embrace, no?).

    But looking more closely... there does seem to be a lack of urgency--Adam could strain, could raise off his elbow. But is that "can't be bothered" (Brit for "apathy" if I recall correctly) or is it human frailty? Adam certainly isn't looking at God in a lackadaisical manner. Amused, maybe. Lovingly, possibly. Apathetic, unconcerned...? Not in the face, not to my eyes. I'd need more than a languid pose and limp finger to ascribe apathy rather than, say, weakness. Although it *is* interesting to ponder the hand angles of each in addition to the relative strain: God's is opened outwards, almost upturned, and straining; while Adam's is palm-down and languid. Unfortunately, I don't know enough about body language and gesture language during the Renaissance to make a definite call on the import of all this (there was *extensive* conscious and unconscious gestural languages in the courts of that time!).

    OK, back to praise for the cool banner ad (which I like a LOT!), if the Colin doesn't want this thread to become a "rambler."

    \n RE the mini version (if I may): If at all possible, I'd stick with using the same title font that you use on the book cover and your web site (and the big banner, ya?). For me, type is just as valuable for recognition and evoking a tone as anything in the background; don't treat it like it can be trivially swapped out if it become unwieldy in some circumstances. If the original font is too wide (or has too-thin strokes, at smaller scales) then I'd try to bring it into a Multiple Master font editor and tweak it to make a a "Narrow" or "Demi" version which still retains the character of the original font.

    But that's just me... it's still very smart and evocative. I particularly like how the horizontal flare of the sun is used tto underscore most of "Sufficiently."
