\n Hey guys!

I'm looking for feedback from this awesome community!

Screenplay is a game that lets you narrate a movie with your friends.
It's a zero-prep, GMless story game that plays in 2-3 hours. There are no dice - you just print out a deck of cards from the PDF.

Download the Zombie Movie deck here, or from the website.
Print it out, cut out the cards, and you can play! The rules are pretty simple.


* I want to know what you like about it, because I've worked on it loads and I could use the applause.
* I want to know what doesn't work for you, because I've worked on it loads, and I want it to be as good as it can be. The rules, the art, the layout - nothing it too small or too big! If I can change it, I will, if I can't, I'll learn!
* If you want to playtest it and let me know how it goes, that would be REALLY helpful. I've playtested it loads, but it's only been run once without me present, and I want to make sure it runs smooth!

Planned future decks include Superhero Movies, Costume Dramas, and The Slenderman. You can mix decks together to create funky new genres!
It's an ideal convention game, it's easy-to-grasp for people who are new to roleplaying, and it teaches some story-making and GMing skills.

Thanks very much, and Happy Valentines!

- Ed

    I hadn't actually heard of zombie cinema before, so thanks!

    In brief, here's the differences: Screenplay is designed to play any film genre, and to be able to mix them together (think Pride and Prejudice and Zombies). Also, the style of play is academically different - no dice, no game board etc.

    Practically though, the pitch is the same - that is, as long as I'm pitching it as a zombie movie game. The solution would be to make the free promo deck something other than the zombie deck (to clarify - I'm planning to give one deck away for free. That was going to be the zombie deck, but perhaps I should look for another universally appealing genre).
