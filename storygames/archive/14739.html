    \n Perry Bible Fellowship should make for some very interesting playsets.

    More seriously, the rules of Fiasco are really easy to grasp and explain, so that part's not a problem, but it seems to me that not having all the tables immediately visible to the players could make things tough during the setup, and will also slow things down quite a bit at the tilt and the aftermath. Also, when I've played Fiasco, a lot of the magic of is in the interplay and exchange of ideas at the table. That part, allowed to happen organically in play-by-post, will take forever. That's not to say it won't work, but I think it'll be a very different experience.

    \n Check out the Fiasco PBF contest that was held over on RPGGeek. You can see a number of different playset's games (or at least the beginnings of them). Also check out this thread in which a bunch of the people involved give advice on doing Fiasco by PbF. The companion has some good advice as well. After playing online, one thing I discovered is that you can't play around with the timeline as much as you could in a normal game. A jump or two backwards or forwards in the timeline is OK but because of the slower pace it is harder to keep the timeline straight. Linear timelines are obviously the easiest. Also, I'd strongly recommend having whoever is not involved in determining the tilt write up a short synopsis of the game thus far so that everyone can review what has gone on in the past weeks of play.
