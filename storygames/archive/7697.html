    \n I hope to finish GLASS this year. I am allowing myself until February, if needed--basically, I just want it out before the convention and outdoor LARP seasons start. It's awesome because it's Hero for Live Action Gaming with real-time combat. Basically, an OGL + Living Worlds for LARPs, to allow for character portability between games (and cons).

    I WILL finish ASCII @HACK, as it should be fully tested soon (cross fingers). It's awesome because its a story-game approach to running a roguelike game, with only one Adventurer trying to survive the machinations of up to four Opposition players (each of whom wants to win by being the one whose Obstacle kills the Adventurer before the Adventurer finishes the Final Zone). Basically, two-hour-or-so, gamist games with a LOT of player agency (like, 100%).

    \nTransantiago (bodhisattvas on the subway at the end of the world) and Agonia (crusaderpunk campaign setting for Agon) are almost the same boat as Agora. Alpha playtesting will probably start in the next couple months, conditions willing, and go on until I've got a beta. Then I'll release some hard copies of the beta for free, along with a downloadable version, as I did with Geiger Counter. Though, unlike Geiger, the beta (second draft) of these two games might just be the final, since they're mechanically solid right now (Transantiago is dead simple and Agonia is based on a well-tested game). I'm mainly focused on making sure the rules are communicated clearly.

    I'm working on a few other things too, but nothing that'll be anywhere close to finished by GenCon 2009 (though I'll be sticking to Dreamation and JiffyCon(s), most likely).
