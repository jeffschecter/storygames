\n I'll start. David Beckham. He looks boyishly charming, crisp and dashing, and has the image of a humanitarian and family man. But he possesses massive wealth, global fame, a cultlike base of fans, a dangerously fit and oversexed body, and mystical power over the cosmic energies.

Obviously: villain. He's perhaps two months away from his Moon Laser and then we're all good and fucked. At the heart of his power is a global conspiracy centered around a cult of personality. It would take a motley counter-conspiracy of various talents to infiltrate his shadowy network. And once you're on the inside - well, who will believe that he's doing anything wrong? He's got a perfect record. You'll have to take him down from the inside.

In other words: we can take celebrities and famous people, and drift them into prototypes for villains in whatever games we may run.

    \nSamuel Franklin Cody, American expatriate, philanderer and charlatan, wildly popular in Europe for his wild west show by 1900. Unrelated to a certain even more famous Bill Cody but prone to wearing buckskin and dramatic facial hair. Charming and harmless. Married to two women at once.

    Then, weirdly: Cody invents the war kite, becomes a balloonist and aeronaut, and then is the first person to fly in the UK, in an airplane of his own design. Shadowy contracts with the Crown throughout this change of careers, and Cody dies in a mysterious crash almost exactly one year before the start of the Great War.

    Obviously: Villain. I mean, look at the guy. C'mon, what spells evil genius more than war kites?
