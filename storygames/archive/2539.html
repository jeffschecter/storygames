\n One of those alternate reality games is going on right now like the ones for A.I. and HALO from a few years ago.

This one is for the new Nine Inch Nails album due in April. This one is set in 2022 and has government conspiracies, terrorist attacks with dirty bombs, and mass, drug-induced visions of the hand of God reaching down from the sky.

Maybe not exactly story-gaming as such, but I can put my mind in the same kind of place as I go through this stuff. And it's certainly inspiring to me: my wife and I decided tonight to start playing a one-on-one, post-apocalyptic game on our commute home from work each night (45 minutes). I'm stoked!

Tell me if anyone thinks this is wildly off-topic for the site.

    \nTo watch how information spreads from hour to hour, and follow the different forum\xe2\u20ac\u2122s prejudices and (partially constructed) animosity towards each other is mysterious stuff. As a reporter, I\xe2\u20ac\u2122ve been read by ten thousands of people daily the past couple of years, but to me, this process today has been much more fascinating. Both because I care much more for role playing games than most of the articles I used to write, and because the people who\xe2\u20ac\u2122ve been responding feel the same kind of love for our hobby.
    \n
    \nPersonally, I\xe2\u20ac\u2122d like to see us all just get along, but that might not be fully reflected in the article.     \nThe difficulty of conveying humour even across the barriers of relatively similar cultures also comes to mind when reading some of the comments the article has received.

    \nPersonally, I\xe2\u20ac\u2122ve mostly been paying attention to the Norwegian LARP and RPG-boards, only briefly glancing at the larger Anglo American debate over the past few years. That the Forge was one of his big issues, I didn\xe2\u20ac\u2122t really know until doing a little research a week ago. My motivation for doing the interview was simply; I\xe2\u20ac\u2122m in the neighbourhood, so it would be fun to meet a role playing Uruguayan.     \n It's moments like this one when I wonder wether the cultural differences between me and other users are really that big or wether it's just me being strange. (Or insensitive or something.)

    Because when clicked on that link, I expected... Oh, I don't know what I expected, but looking at the picture I don't really see it as very racist. And then I thought a bit more about it and realized that I've reacted in the same way in similar situations. Often, I'd see people get angry about something in art or whatever, that they call racist, or sexist, and while I can see where they are coming from, but at the same time I don't understand what's there to get all angry about.

    Like, when some S-G users talked about Conan covers on one of the Blogs and in a way ripped these covers a new one for being sexist. I read their comments and looked at the pictures and found myself agreeing that the pictures were extremly cliched and stereotyped. But I wouldn't call them sexist in the "This is offensive and should be stopped" sense, just like I couldn't call the Jabba pic racist in this sense. It's, like, I don't know... In my mind, there's a difference between using stereotypes with the intention to offend and out of an hatred of a specific group of people "(Like saying "All blacks are criminals" or whatever.) and using stereotypes to evoke the mood of a specific genre. I'd have no problem with labeling the former racist, but the latter? Meh, not really. I think, in my head, racism has a lot, maybe even everything to do with intention and the actual believes of the person. Calling a picture racist, or the artist for making the picture, is extremly problematic to me. After all, I could sit down and draw a picture of a guy in SS-uniform being hailed as a hero while in the background, jews, homosexuals and a whole lot of other people are burned alive. But would that make me a racist? And if I ran a pulp game where the bad guy was a classical Fu Manchu-type villain?

    ...

    To me, racism is hurtfull, offensive and bad in practically any way. If I had three wishes, the first one would be spent towards removing the various Isms that plague this world. But if a picture like "Jabba the Hun" can be racist, or the covers of Conan can be sexist, does that mean these pictures should equally disappear? Should should it be impssible to draw these pictures, for whatever reasons? Should writing something like SS in the Vineyard be impossible?

    Nah, I don't think so. People can be racist and somebody who draws a picture like Jabba the Hun might very well be a racist, who knows. But I don't think a picture, taken by itself, can be racist. Cliched, sure. Stereptyped, yeah. Used by a racist as expression for his racism, defenitly. But not racist itself.
