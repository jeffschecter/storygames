\n So I was chatting to Ben earlier today, and in between being unnecessarily obtuse about Moldavy D&D, he said something about a PBP game where everyone plays a character posting on a forum. Then I didn't think about it, until now.

What I'm thinking is that I'd set up a whole seperate forum, which is ostensibly the discussion forum for the class of Professor Abalone's school of magic - the most prestigious magic college in Europe, catering to international students. Professor Abalone herself never posts to the forum, as she's above that kind of thing, but I'd post as her TA, Reginald Fink.

Anyone can join the forum, and post about any subject they like, as a character in the game. Maybe some stories would develop, or something.

Anyway, setting this up would be a bit of work, so I wanted to see if there was any interest before I started. I think it would be a fun experiment in what, (if I become rich and famous) I'll call "Forum LARP".

    \nPROFESSOR ABALONE'S COLLEGE OF MAGIC OPEN TO ALL APPLICANTS
    For Immediate Release

    For the first time in its 84-year history, the Dr Runchibald F Abalone Institute of Thaumaturgy, Alchemy and Sewing Machine Repair--popularly known as Professor Abalone's College of Magic--is open to unsolicited applications from the general public.

    "Dr Abalone's founding policy, which we've always strived to maintain, was to base our admissions exclusively on referrals from the top hermetic prep schools from around the world," said Bose Kaufner, Dean of the Institute. "But the fact is that shrinking class sizes at those fine institutions have led inevitably to a decline in enrollment here, while the quality of public education has only risen. The Board of Trustees and I decided that this was the time to bring us into the twenty-first century. Also, there aren't as many sewing machine-based prep schools as you'd think."

    While the Institute expects to welcome mostly applicants who completed secondary school in 2007 or 2008, applications are also open to nontraditonal students. Forms are available by visiting or writing the Admissions House at 1200 Witley Ln, Effingham Half-Hundred, Surrey GU2 E78; or by visiting the Institute's Web site at http://www.abalone.ac.uk.

    "We're very pleased to be opening this new door to the something something, blast it, you wrote the card too small," noted Dr Abalone himself. "I think those thrice-cursed students have put petroleum jelly on the lenses of my spectacles again. Where have you hidden my tea?"

    The Institute of Thaumaturgy and Alchemy was founded in 1924 by Abalone and a contingent of professors he recruited from other schools as a reaction to what they felt were declining standards of wizardly education. After financial difficulties during World War II, the Insitute joined forces with the Berthe Santino Ten-Week Course in Sewing Machine Repair to boost numbers and began admitting female students for the first time. The Institute made headlines in 1998 when a group of advanced students summoned award-winning demon Belasc\xe6l Blister-the-Bone and utilized him to keep the Thaumalympic Torch lit for one leg of its journey to Nagano, Japan. All resulting property damage was subsequently repaired, and the vast majority of lawsuits were dropped.
