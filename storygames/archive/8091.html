\n OK, I'm listening to Sons of Kryos GenCon thing, and Robin Laws (?) just said, "Hunter S. Thompson RPG."

Ho. Lee. Schmidt. Two (well, three or four, frankly) of my favorite things, combined. And I have to start a thread.

Brainstorm, pitch a game concept, muse about situations and thematic focus. I think there's something to this notion--gonzo journalist(s) and hippies and lunatic cops and Nixon (gotta have Nixon). Drugs, hallucinations, bizarre encounters with straight-edges, high-strung editors, goofy-assed photographers.

Is there enough there? I'd DEFINITELY need to "re-read for RPG" to see what the hooks for actual play might be. Or maybe there's already a game that can take me there as written?

    \n For this, I'd use a hack of Ryan Macklin's Mythender.

    You are all gonzo journalists seeking the deep Truth in a crazy world full of Tear and Loathing. You might be covering rock and roll, politics, war, sports, you name it. You are endowed with mythic powers that grow as you draw nearer to the Truth. At the same time, you become more and more part of the story yourself. In time, you pass into myth. Your character may no longer be played and instead becomes the target for a new generation of gonzo mythenders.
