\n
\n Here's a thought for reflection: What if there was a per-day limit on the number of responses to a thread?
\n
\nThis might have the following effects:
\n
\n- When the thread was full, people would have to cool down for a day before posting again, making for more reflection and thought.
\n- People would post longer and fewer posts, so as not to fill up the quota.
\n- People would start more spin-off threads, for better or worse.
\n- More people would have the chance to actually read the whole thread, since it wouldn't reach 100+ posts in the space of one day.
\n
\nI'd love to see this idea tested somewhere, at some point.

    Otherwise, my personal moderation style at this point, for the amount of users we currently have, and the high level of self-control and maturity that we've seen, is something like this: If there's a fire, let it burn.     \n I don't see that particular thing is really a huge problem around here to begin with.

    I have found, however, that voluntarily limiting myself to checking in on any online discussion only once or twice a day seems to have a positive effect on my contributions. There are very few conversations being had anywhere that can't wait twelve hours for me to reply to something.

    Rapid flurries of posting also have the side-effect that people who have other things to do than slavishly keep up with your discussion are effectively shut out of the process. "Slow the fuck down, let somebody else get a few words in, and think" is always a good rule of thumb.
