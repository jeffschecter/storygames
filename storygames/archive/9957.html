\n The town of Husk lies near the center of a rocky badlands. To the north and west lie mountains, and to the far south the badlands give way to scrub and grasslands. To the east, more badlands.

Husk is named for the strange, ancient ship that crashed there long ago. This ship provides certain natural benefits that outweigh the creepiness.

But I have no idea what they are. Any suggestions?

    \n I think what I like most about the ship of Husk is that no single person's vision of it is the same. My father sees a silver and marble statue who sings soft secrets on the wind that tell him what the weather will be like tomorrow. My mother sees a cast-off skin of a large cosmic snake and she can sometimes break off a piece of and use that skin to make the quilts she sells to gunfighter to protect them from all but the most righteous of bullets. She also says it leaks poison from deep sacs, but she has not yet brought any back - she fears to anger its progenitor, wherever in the sky he may dance.

    I hide what I see. I tell people I see a mist of purple clouds that is struck through with lightning and floating golden orbs with ruins written upon them. They nod at this and pity me a bit, for it seems I get no benefit from my interface with the Husk. In truth, that is the vision of the Husk an old traveler told me as he died in a hospice I worked in as a boy. He was carried away, he claimed in his last moments, by one of those orbs.

    I lie because I have the worst vision of all. You see, I do not see the Husk at all. I pray I am not the victim of some prophesy or worse, the one person in the world forsaken by the Husk and its gifts.

    \n I ended up going with the following:

    Features of Husk

    1. Protection from winds
    2. Rain shadow / runoff (the ship itself is tall and cool, and condensation runs through it from the other side and runs down the inner side in rivulets)
    3. Crater (oasis where the water gathers from the ship's runoff)
    4. Natural fortification.
    5. "We dream of a time before Wasters like us ever found the ship, when the ones who lived here thought it might run again, before its engines cooled, before birds used it as a place to roost and moss grew on its hull." (Paraphrasing Judd)
    6. The people of Husk have heard of Wingsfall and Shadowspan, to the east and south, but no Waster has been to those places in living memory. A few Knocks made the journey from Shadowspan to here years back, but they said it's not worth it - they say those places were just like Husk, but with more murder.
    7. It used to be that pieces of the ship could do amzing things, but it's been centuries since the whole ship was gutted for parts. Now all that remains is the husk.
