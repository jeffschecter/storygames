    I'm starting to podcast for work and what I do may be relevant - I record the body of classes I teach (usually about 40 minutes of an hour-long slot) and edit those down to 30 minutes after sweetening them in Audacity and eliminating all the "uuuuh" and jackass rabbit trails I go down.

    Then I record a bunch of 2-3 minute "hot tips" that cover individual points within that material. These I syndicate, with pointers to the longer session. So in game terms this would be an actual play post with short clips on how scene framing works, what narration is, the game's die mechanic, etc.

    I think staging an instructive actual play scene might be productive - there's no need for the session to be "real" if the goal is to demonstrate concepts. OTOH if you want to give your listener a feel for a certain vibe or social dynamic, this wouldn't work. Hmm.
