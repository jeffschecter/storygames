\n(This thread is a split from the Gen Con 2010 discussion to talk about IGE and GOD)

In preparing for Gen Con, I've seen a lot of folks mentioning Games on Demand and Indie Games Explosion, saying that's where the fun is at the con. I agree with that, but I've found it hard to point to anything online that properly promotes or explains the events for new folks. I'm interested in talking about changing that dynamic, and possibly building the brand of these events a bit.

I'm not entirely clear on the history and ownership (such as it is) of IGE and GOD. I know the events have been going on since 2006 or so, and have happened at Gen Con and other cons. I know that Kat and Michael have taken a strong lead in both, and host the IGE website on Livejournal. The GOD site is being generously hosted by Tony, and both events are being organized this year by Scott. Clearly the intention is that this is a grassroots effort, and lots of other folks have been vocal advocates. It's even a hit in Australia!

So what else can/should we do? I'm a web developer, so my first inclination is to put up a site with a stronger presence (its own domain, some "about" content, an archive of previous events, etc.). Would it be useful to put up an indiegamesexplosion.com domain, backed by Wordpress or something similar, so that we could distribute content management? Are these events worth promoting and building into a more substantial brand?

    \n no buzz kill, I appreciate the feedback!

    Mostly I'm just kicking ideas around, with the goal of creating a stronger indie community at the conventions. My low-end goal is really just to score a more convenient location near the convention hall and maybe have the freedom to put up a few signs.     Also, as a publisher, I think that creating space for people to play our games is critical to seeing sales at the booths. We can do that with scheduled events, but I think it's generally agreed that the informal collaboration between GOD folks and the booths has been very useful on both ends.
