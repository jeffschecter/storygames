  • Lxndr
    March 2006
    In order of shelf:

    Shangri-la - This will hopefully wind up being a dream game that doesn't suck. At the moment in mothballs, 'cause the path I was leading it down was making it suck (as a dream game), and then it got cannibalized for a product called Ensemble, which is currently cooking so doesn't belong here. So I kinda need to re-start it anyway. I'm not sure if I'm capable of writing it yet, but I will keep dragging it out, tinkering with it, and putting it back.

    Mudsylvania - Most likely a d20-based game, this is supposed to be the game I was hoping Everquest D20 would be - full of the strange artifacts that come from MUD/MMO play. Death by violence being a minor inconvenience, people appearing and disappearing, monsters also spawning and dying only to spawn again, bizarre changes to the code, levels and whatnot being externally verifiable entities, etc. There would be no "out of game" - the tactic I want to take is the perception of people who actually live in a universe where this is the reality.

    Earth, Too - I'm still trying to figure out what I want the players to DO, but I think this is worthwhile to explore. The basic idea is, instead of going to heaven when you die, you are born again in another universe, where everyone lives their second life, without losing their memories of their first life (or at least not entirely). The idea came to me in a philosophy class a decade ago, and the inherent theme is supposed to be "Will this world be better, or worse, than our first try?"

    Shore Leave - An STL interstellar game, where each session is a stop at a given port, and between sessions you're frozen. The idea is that society on these ports grows much faster than you, and your fellow PCs are the only constants in your life. I want recurring visits to these ports, and a technological plateau, so it's not the tech changes that you'll be dealing with, just the social changes as generations rise and fall while you sleep.

    Still Life - A sorcerer mini-supplement set on Mars after the Earth suddenly just goes away, with no explanation as to what happened. Demons are the ghosts of lost Earth.

    A few others:

    An unnamed cinematic fight system for fantasy RPG, sort of like Wushu, but adding a tactical side. And one that more closely emulates the type of battle scenes shot in the Lord of the Rings first movie. It's sort of outlined right now. Sort of.

    Toll Bridge: A game set in the middle of nowhere. The players play misfits who have been exiled to the only bridge in the middle of nowhere. Their duty is to extract tolls from passing caravans, but their desire is to not only extract the required goals, but also line their own pockets. The players compete with each other trying to be the one who extracts the most tolls in order to have their exiles lifted, but they also try to expose each other for lining their pockets. A secondary part to this game may include the fight system listed above, and the players also have to protect the caravans.

    Started but on the back burner:

    Dubuque Street: The bar hopping RPG game of fighting the insanity that is set in Iowa City. The Deadwood, Sport's Column, and the Dublin Underground form the core for the Sanity of the Universe, and within tunnels that connect the bars sits the Sacred Keg of Sanity that is all that protects the universe from going insane. The forces of insanity attempt to take control by destroying the Big Three bars by building new bars. The players are students who have found out about this epic struggle and attempt to stop their favorite drinking establishments from being destroyed. Have a playable draft, but not really working on it. It needs a system added for threats to the bars and some other things ironed out.
