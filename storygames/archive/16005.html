\n A few weeks ago, I posted a thread calling for help, then later some questions about possible replacement systems.

Well, I need to follow up I suppose - it was an awesome success

We had our session where we all sat down and talked about what we wanted from the game and how we could get it. It was a really good session where we all got out our reasons for playing and what we were hoping to get out of our sessions Had some interested requests.

We came down to a decision to switch systems, and "translate" the characters from World of Darkness into The One Ring.

Our wants:
An "old school" feel of a series of adventures/encounters
Sandbox/West Marches
No "meta plot" + Character driven "story"
No Levels.
Potentially Lethal/ Always repercussions
Able to influence the dice "when it matters".

I could probably talk a lot about each of those wants, but after our session this week where we actually played we could see a dramatic change

I am not sure how closely we are RAW for the one ring, but we played in the following way:

The players were narrating what they were doing, and when they describe doing something outside the accepted capabilities of their characters - or interesting - they pull out the dice, which determine how the scenes continues.

The "accepted capabilities" was hard to gauge in WoD, but in ToR we found the Distinctions and Favored Abilities let the players have a good feel of when they could "just talk" and when they had to "roll the bones".

They wanted a trope and so went to the Tavern looking for interesting things. After a few failed courtesy and song rolls the players described themselves as a bunch of drunken louts... until one player decided to use a Lore roll to find some potential hints - and after an awesome success decided that she had met someone who was in awe of her repitation as a wise-one... So we ended up looking for historical treasure in ancient ruins (The NPC became Henri of Jonesford)

The game just flowed this time - the mechanic of Hope and the fellowship rules really made the players come together - the more "gamer" ones were playing the system of hope and distinctions for XP, but the result almost like watching an adventure movie or something The characters were acting like interesting characters in a dangerous situation without the players having to step out or think more meta as they have with FATE.

The traveled through a dark forest, risking hope and encountering dangers on the path (which they neatly sidestepped though clever logic). and into an old-school dungeon crawl - the Tomb of the High King - where they are facing magical traps and puzzles, and everyone was joyously adding details and practically jumping to get the next word in and have their characters doing/saying things.

Honestly was one of the better sessions i've had... and especially after the earlier horror of a session this was most welcome

To everyone who gave advice, pointed me in new directions, and pointed out potential flaws in how I was GM'ing - THANK YOU!!!

Dionysus aka KJ.

    \n Yeah. The One Ring feels very 'low fantasy, low magic' setting. The setting we are using is pretty similar, but a little higher on the fantasy side (goblins/fae and strange things)

    The only thing we are really changing is the "colour" of the descriptions, but the mechanics are unchanged.

    We really loved the ability to "spend hope" after rolling to push those rolls that just didn't make it... It made everyone feel a little more "heroic" than in WoD. But still know that they would be paying for it later.
