  • Jason_Morningstar
    February 2006
    OK, take it easy. I appreciate your enthusiasm! Honest to God I do.

    I'm not taking pre-orders because I want to be absolutely confident on the quality of this, my first product. I'm not selling something I don't have. This is all new to me and I am not going to let anybody down. Future products, once I'm cozy with my printer? You bet pre-orders.

    We have Paypal ready on the site, tested and good to go. Once we get our books, you guys can have the game in a week, complete with a horrible "first 100 copies" surprise.

    After exploring a couple of options with traditional printers who balked at the volume, we had our cards done at Kinkos. They did a great job with some fairly precise cuts and we got 5280 cards printed and cut (110 sets of 40 for our initial print run of 100 books, plus an eight-card "bonus pak" we'll either use for promotion or sell seperately or both) for $76.00. It isn't a great solution but it works for us, for now. If the game is a success we'll try a real printer with higher volume.

    I'll ask Steve, who picked up the order, what the Kinkos people liked. I'm betting MURUB - COPULATE WITH THIS PERSON. That's a perennial favorite.

    The cheapass games solution is zipper bags.

    4ml Reclosable Bags

    Using just the company on this link, one case of 1,000 of a 2x3" 4mil plastic bag (smallest dimension, medium thickness) cost $33 plus shipping. After shipping and bad bags (there's always some bad in full cases) I'd guess about a nickel per game. For bags of half thickness (look too flimsy to me), it's half the price. The 6mil version are twice the price.

    As I've got the custom-card flu myself, I can easily imagine myself putting a little rubber band around the suckers, then dropping them in these little baggies. Vi-O-La.

    -Eric
