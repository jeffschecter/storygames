    Okay, so after chatting with these folks about indie games for a few days, the guy who runs the BBS said "you want a subforum to talk about this stuff more?" So now I'm running a new sub-forum. I didn't want to get into the whole "what is an indie game?" discussion, because it becomes even more complicated if you move beyond the Anglo-American scene, so I suggested this:

    "Progressive TRPG Design and Play Methods: For Discussing the Whole World's Most Fresh TRPG Play Methods, Promoting Mutual Understanding Between Sino-Foreign Roleplaying Culture, Slowly Developing the Future of Roleplaying"

    So I'm hoping some of the Chinese folks (including HK, Taiwan, Singapore, Malaysia, etc.) will also throw down and talk about what they're doing in their own groups that may be new and different, and there won't be this kind of heirarchical "learn from the Americans" thing going on. Wish me luck.

    White settlers w/religious motives = yep.
    100s killed in Salem = nope. 14 women, 6 men. Mostly it was petty infighting, and the stories of a 9 yr old and 11 yr old girl that set it off.
    Natives fought and demonized as 'other' = yep.
    Law of the Gun = kinda. Cowboys were actually mostly sentimental lonely guys with good hearts who loved storytelling. Cattle barons were more nasty, and they tended to stay put more.
    Quilts given for strength = kinda. They were made and given for everything from pure practicality to great celebration of life events (marriage and birth, etc). The strength gained was that they kept you warm and reminded you of people who cared about you.
