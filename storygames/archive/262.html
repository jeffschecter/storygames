Summary: Mark is using the Risk Godstorm map in his upcoming Fudge (FATE?) fantasy game. Andy throws up the horns in approval.

Personally, I've never done this. Maybe I've not been thinking outside the box enough, or just haven't found the right resources, but looking around at all those new awesome German board games and the like, I figure that with all their little resource components and maps, that there's something cool that can be pulled into an RPG.

Anyway, I was hoping for two types of responses:

1) Specific cases where you pulled materials from other games (board, card, video )manuals/maps/etc)) into your RPG. Anyone done this?

2) Specific ideas of specific games and materials to pull from, and where/how to use such materials in your game.

Thoughts?

-Andy

    I went absolutely bananas with the homebrew electropunk game I ran - since it mixed fiction (Wells' War of the Worlds) with fact (they worked for Teddy friggin' Roosevelt) we really had free range - there were fictional characters from film (Hiram Gerome from The Wind and the Lion), real people fictionalized (Ota Benga), and characters both plausible and implausible we created from scratch. I borrowed the idea of author-stance-granting cards from TORG and we collectively wrote up a bunch that were genre-appropriate, like "I'm an American!" and "Just keep pounding on it!". Those cards became the core of the game in many ways.
