I think I'm into pregens these days. Let's look at _Crime & Punishment_ for example; the game comes with a fixed set of detectives within the standard archetypes (the cynical one, the rookie, the guy who's only 5 days from retirement, the cop on the edge). But if your game is mainly going to play with archetypes - if the main gameplay is about *how* those are used, rather than building those character from scratch - then yeah, pregens are great.

But maybe you can also come up with, say, twenty NPCs with situation hooks attached: *Brother Isiah is proud of his wealth, and thinks he's above what his community needs. Sister Anastasia is a desired by four different men of different social standings, but is too proud for all of them. Steward Malcolm needs to control people in order to feel like he's no longer a victim.* So for a certain kind of game, you could possibly have those seeds there, good to go.

And perhaps your system has an elaborate system for creating equipment with some kind of tactical use and emotional import. *My Partner's Gunsword: 4d8 in duels, 3d4 in relationships, 5d6 is mass combat." Perhaps you simply need an army of evocative items that you do what you want? The same argument holds for games with freeform trait systems - the possibility of suggesting trait lists is also there.

If your game has a crunchy subsystem or two, you can precreate some of that content with it and seemingly lower the handling time & performance profile of your system in-play, particularly if the game is about the manipulation of those fictional objects, rather than their unique creation.
