\n What components do you typically include with games you bring for Games on Demand?

For example, my Fiasco kit includes:
- d6's, 12 blue, 12 red
- 5 sharpies
- healthy stack of index cards
- play mat
- reference tables
- a handful of playsets

My Marvel Heoric Roleplaying kit includes:
- lots of dice, including around 8,234 d8's (almost enough)
- poker chips
- datafiles
- character stand ups
- breakout
- cheat sheets
- 6 pens
- a pile of post-it notes

    \n You need 20 dice in two colors for Fiasco as well.

    For most games that are not intended for a two hours session, I try to cut corners and front-load to get to the good stuff while teaching about what's cool in the game. often this means ignoring some rules or bending others.

    For Dungeon World I rely on the players for funny dice, which are needed in small quantities. I have a stock dungeon I run, so info for that (map, monsters), lots of playbooks (or laminated dry-erase playbooks), and I also use custom treasure cards I made up.

    For Durance the kit includes custom character cards (with different colored borders for rank and convict/Authority symbols, so I can say "pick two characters, different colors, different symbols"), a laminated planet and colony sheet we can dry erase on, and a printout of all the planets and colonies + the tie tables and handouts.

    \n -I carry around a big stack of pre-gens, and quick reference sheets (for those games not in pdf), even several hacks that I like, all tab separated, in a large 3 ring binder.
    -In the binder I also have all of the GM'ing notes for various scenarios, several pictures of NPC's, Maps, and several blank sheets of card stock (well some have grid lines on them just in case), and any other notes.
    -All of this stuff is in laminated sleeves so that I can reuse them.
    -Scotch tape to hold the laminated sheets together to make a map.
    -I keep all of the Fiasco Playsets, books, and any other content I need on my old tablet.
    -A stack of dry erase markers of various colors.
    -Napkins and alcohol pads in case the dry erase ink will not come off.
    -My large plano box organized full of dice.
    -A large, and heavy, bag of glass beads, and poker chips of various styles.
    -Circular labels for the poker chips.
    -A stack of laminated index cards.
    -A stack of laminated index cards that are folded over to use as name cards, or miniatures if needed.
    -A deck of cards
    -My medicine, Excedrin migraine, an energy drink, bottle of water (to hydrate after all that caffeine), gum, and a few power bars.
    -All goes in to large a backpack that is ready to go.

    I travel around a lot to various game day events at stores, small cons, and peoples houses doing demos for all sorts of games. There is material in there for at least 20+ different games. Everytime I run or play a game I copy the characters and take notes on the scenario, then add it my binder as a possible demo. I also run a bi-weekly group online that does games on demand, although lately we have elected to play longer games.

    \n Just an aside...

    If anyone has a boardgame they aren't using anymore, (preferably something like the 6 segment folding RISK boards work very very well) you can put a layer of adhesive whiteboard for very cheaps. Bang! Instant 24 x 32 whiteboard. Or adhesive chalkboard works too, but chalk isn't as fine point.

    Also, in my kit is a digital voice recorder (which I sometimes tell players about, but mostly it's just for improving my side of things). And a digital camera to snap a shot of the whiteboard / players / other stuff worth remembering.

    \n I'm only running a few games, so everything fits easily in a laptop bag.

    Basics: standard big ol' bag of dice, cubes of 36 d6s (white, black, blue, red, and green), mechanical pencils galore, and a 11" x 17" pad of lined graph paper.

    Apocalypse World: book, one full set of printed out playbooks.

    Technoir: book, character sheets, a few sets of pregens, player's guides, and six copies of every Transmission.

    Wushu: 3 x 5 notecards in several colors, poker chips.

    Slingtown(in development): mc guide, four player's guides, two copies of each of the twelve playbooks.

    Fortunately enough, my fiance's a machine at folding and stapling
