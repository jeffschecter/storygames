\n So, there's another thread on here about the prototypical flashlight dropper player. This concept highly amused me, so I made a game with that as a start condition. Here's the JPG of the game. I challenged myself to make it in 3 hours, so ya know, it's rough. Let me know what you think.

Edit: I chose a weird dice mechanic, just to play around with things. It might be super horrible, I don't know.

    \n This is very slick, for a one-page game. It's got an adventure design "chapter", an interesting mechanical take (Terror versus Determination is very cool for a horror game), and a great starting situation.

    I like the idea of having a single "Flashlight Power" number, and rolling above it when you're pushed by Terror or below it when you're propelled by Determination. And giving up one point allows for automatic success. (Useful in character vs. character situations, for conflicts.)

    I don't know if you need an additional mechanic for your actions hurting the others: after all, having someone "on your team" whose number is very low is pretty bad by itself!
