\n Over in the Stuff to Watch: January thread I mentioned my open challenge to all of myself and my fellow game designers, write one game per month and put it out in some form. I'm calling this challenge Games Galore 2012 and a few other designers have already mentioned that they would be interested. I'm starting this thread to give us a place to talk about our progress on our various games. This is an open invitation, if you read this later in the year don't feel like you need to catch up, just join in! Feel free to post about the games you are working on and comment on the games others are working on. I will try and keep an up to date list of the games here including blog posts about them and links to threads where they are being discussed. Help me out by posting any needed links in the thread please. Heres to an awesome year of game designing goodness!

    \n In a surprise reversal, I wrote up Fevered Pace, a cyberpunk game of speed, debt, and love, for this month. BECT will likely reappear next month.

    Edited to add: Made some last, last minute fixes. I forgot constitution, rotating ledgers, and of course mortality:\n
    Sometimes a character will die. Such is the dangerous and unpredictable world of Fevered Pace. But death doesn't end the story. When a character dies, the player distributes her debts among her loved ones. Then the player chooses one of those loved ones to create as a new character, building on the debts and loves of the deceased one and adding new debts and loves.

    \n Having recently jumped back into WH40k and thinking about how tough it is as a newish player to get into I got into a playtest of Jason Morningstar's Task Force Roach. Playing TFR gave me some great ideas on ways to take it's simple mechanics back into a theme I know and love. The result is a new game I'm calling Galaxy Aflame. It has elements that I took from many different sources including a deck building element in place of traditional army building. It is still very much a playtest and I encourage anyone who plays to post about their experiences with it.

    \n This month's game is Mimicry World, the last in a trilogy of Apocalypse World hacks I've been working on - this one is MC-full rather than being MC-less (like the last two). In Mimicry World you play a mimic, a being who was pushed out of its original form and must inhabit other forms to exist - so you could be a bear who used to be a tree, or a cloud who used to be a computer. Ultimately you want to change the world around you in order to reach the lofty realms of the True Forms, and either join with your chosen ideal or replace it. But these forms have fronts in the mundane world, which cause no end of trouble.

    Mimicry World takes the Apocalypse World system and adds some elements (many of which are from Homeworld Project) to give smoother pacing and more support for the MC. You can find it on my Games Galore page.

    \n This month's game is Magic's End, building from an idea I had a few years ago during the RPG Solitaire competition. In Magic's End you play an Archmage in a world where magic and destiny has made your life simple. But as the gods leave and magic leaks out of the world, you are faced with the need to use your remaining magic wisely, to change and discover who you will be in a world without magic or destiny. You can find it on my Games Galore page.

    I'm planning to finish up Brimstone Emissaries to the City of Thorns (BECT) for next month, which will neatly bring things into a full circle if I can succeed.

    \n
    \n This month's game is Brimstone Emissaries in the City of Thorns (BECT), which I had planned to be my game in January and I've been working on and off on all year. It borrows the notion of moves from Apocalypse World, but takes it in a very different direction. In BECT, you have maps of the moves you can take. Instead of the moves being unpredictable, you instead have different options depending on what you have tried recently. You can find it on my Games Galore page.
