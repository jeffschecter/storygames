\n So, I don't know if this is a 'fix' or a 'home rule'. I may find out that it matters.

I love games where Social conflicts can end up changing my character's mind or make me fall in love and I love having it apply to NPCs and PCs alike. It takes me to new places and makes Social skills meaningful.

However, I recently ran a con session of TSOY and one player expressed a particular disinterest in anything affecting his character under the skin. We shifted gears with the game and still had a decent session. There was an honest discussion of mixed expectations right there at the situation and we were able to work things out.

I wonder, though, for those individuals who have problems with this dissonance between rules affecting physics vs. the mind, if they could be brought to more fun (as I imagine it above) if it were ran a little differently.

What if, when a Social Conflict was taking place that involved the changing of a character's mind (both PC and NPC) in some fashion, the goal was to have a Key given to the character?

Instead of Stakes: (Will she love me?) it could be Stakes: (Will she now have the Key of Love?) Then, the player could choose to act on these impulses and be rewarded for it. Even tastier, betrayal could be met with a Key buy-off.

I'm weak when it comes to Social Contract issues. I expect there to be some concern about that. I'm just wondering that if it were presented in this new light, could anyone see problems with it?

I'll go ahead and state one: You haven't really won anything if the PC or NPC chooses not to cash in on the Key. If only you could compel Keys like Aspects in Fate!

Thoughts?

    \n I am currently stealing this idea for my Tribe 8 TSOY hack.

    My current (rough) notes look like this:

    1. When you are defeated in a conflict that has gone to bringing down the pain the consequences can attack your Keys. This can either happen from being destroyed or from giving when the consequences are on the table.

    2. You can have a Key "shut off" -- in this case the key no longer gives you Xp. You can turn the Key back on by winning a conflict appropriate to rejuvenating the key. (Such as losing the Key of Love when you become convinced your lover is cheating on you, but regaining it after having a conflict in which you learn that she was set up.) Note: Should the PC be able to buy off the key and still get XP? -- possibly, as that means the other was ultimately successful in permanently destroying the key. Note 2: Is it possible to target only specific parts of a key for shut off? Like, target only the buy off option? Or target only the mid level option? That might be interesting.

    3. You can have a new key placed upon you. This key is as a normal key but doesn't cost you XP to buy and it does not have a buy off condition. To gain a buy off condition you must win a conflict in which you have stakes and situation fitting to moving towards your character learning to let go. (For example if you are seduced and gain the key of love, but later you win a conflict and catch the person that seduced you setting you up to be killed, you gain the ability to buy off.) Note that this does not force you to act in accord with the new key -- you may, for example, kill the man that seduced you even though you love him. It just rewards you if you chose to follow along with it.

    4. You can have a key transmuted. In this case you have one key replaced with another, for example the Key of Love being replaced with the Key of Unrequited Love if someone wins a contest against you and takes your lover. You can then use that key as normal (including buy off), or you can transmute it back by winning a fitting conflict in the future.

    In general conflicts to change, remove, or unfreeze keys should not have so simple and direct of stakes as "I want to end my fear of snakes now." It should play out in a dramatic fashion and have real risk to the character if it fails. Some keys may be harder to shake than others, and may require some creativity to get rid of. In all cases keys can only be modded in Bringing Down the Pain, not in a normal contest.

    Option: A normal contest can modify a Key for a scene, but not permanently.

    PCs can do this to NPCs too. Minor NPCs may be able to be modded without BDtP, but named NPCs can only be modded permanently in BDtP, just as they can only be killed in BDtP.

    Note: Keys from conflicts only come from the result of real conflicts. You can't conflict yourself to get a free key. If the group thinks someone is trying to get a free key they have the right to instantly change the consequences to something nastier.

    Note 2: You can have keys shut off, but you can't just lose one off your sheet forever.

    For Tribe 8 TSOY, this kind of thing is the perfect option for Synthesis based Secrets. Either only those with a secret can do this (such as Magdalites using Lust to give someone the Key of Lust), or the secrets give a weapon bonus (up to +3) for conflicts in which the consequences involve giving another character a key. Other Secrets (like Joanite Devotion) could give armor (same value) to resist having your keys messed with.


    P.S. Yes, I talk to myself when I make notes.
