\n So, I'm pulling together a game finally. And it's a bleeding awesome game! Here is the pitch:

MINOTAUR

So, imagine a cross between House of Leaves and Foster's Home for Imaginary Friends. Maybe stir some Mi\xc3\xa9ville and Gorey in there. You're
in this mansion, right. It's so big that you've gotten lost. It's lonely and strange and you don't know where the door is, and what's worse is you heard it lock when you came in.

The house is full of mysteries.


So, I need some things to put in the house. I already have a couple. I'm stealing a bit from Ursula Vernon's Gearworld, and I know that the mansion has its own peculiar wildlife and a few other inhabitants - the birds-of-the-maze, who herald the approach of the Minotaur; Alphabeth, who can read any written thing; Tractor Duncan, who turns into a tractor at night; etc.

But I need more! More locations, more things, weird objects, puzzles to be found scattered across the house. MORE!

    \n The big room at the centre, with a tree and a fountain, which spins slowly and has no gravity. There are birds, you can just jump up and touch them.
    \n
    \nThe tiny point of light. It's in a dark place, and as you approach it, you see there's something inside. You can see the back of your own head. However, there are some subtle changes; you can't be sure it's you.
    \n
    \nThree books that say the same thing in different languages. Two of the languages are knowable, one is not. When you compare the two you understand, you will see that there are small differences in what they describe. Put together with the third, the differences between the books will form a narrative. The two knowable books only form part of it.
    \n
    \nA snake that only exists in nighttime. It lays an egg at night. It's reborn every sunset from its own egg, complete with all its memories. During the day it exists only as a spirit, a point of observation, at a specific - or random? - location in the house. On the inside of the shell are inscriptions, describing what the snake spirit has seen during the day.
    \n
    \nAn argument that, when you enter a corridor, takes possession of you and makes you reenact it.

    \n A chandelier where each flame is a tiny firey being, trapped inside the glass. (That sort of thing can be a game-ender under the wrong circumstances; big mansions tend to go up in flames pretty fast.)

    In one area the floorboards creak in tune. If you play around on them, making a song, a terrible ogre will come and listen happily to the music. When you stop, he demands you play more. If you don't, he threatens you with violence. If you still don't, he tries to pull you apart.

    A Scooby-Doo hallway. (If you've never seen the show, you go into one door and come out a different one in the hallway at random, though groups usually stay together.)

    A cackling madwoman collects rainwater from the roofs into a giant kettle. She'll let you have a drink of precious water if you'll bring her things. The room is full of frogs; if you try to take water, she'll show you one as an example of what happens to naughty little children.
