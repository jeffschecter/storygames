    \n Many of the old shadowrun adventure modules adhere to this idea. They were a series of interrelated places with motivated characters in them. Each situation had a chance of leading players to one of the other situations, but none were set in stone, and there were no guarantees that you would see all or even most of the situations in your adventure.

    \n Do you remember the names of the ones you're thinking of? As far back as I can remember (late SR1 era) the Shadowrun adventures have all been interlocked situations with a section called Debugging or something like that. The point of the section was to give the GM things to do when the players wandered off the beaten path and needed a nudge to get them back on it.
