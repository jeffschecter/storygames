    BeePeeGee said:

    Arcbipelago uses ritualized phrases like "try a different way". So, these could be used without resolution mechanics.

    Archipelago definitely has resolution mechanics, though, in the "That might not be so easy" mechanics.


    Other games that might be relevant:

    Swords Without Master has rules for who can say what, but it deliberately, explicitly leaves out resolution. You could end a Perilous phase right away, or keep it going, as you prefer. Things are resolved when they are interesting to be resolved, or difficult and unresolved if it is interesting for them to be difficult and unresolved.

    The Skeletons doesn't address who kills the intruders, or any other actions you take in the tomb. Those are all left up to narration and to players to decide in a way that fits with their emerging memories and story.

    The Melon Child of Radnor doesn't have any resolution mechanics, IIRC. So do a few of Jason's other little free games, like Thola or The Great Belt.
