\n I've had two ideas in my mind, and I'm looking for a way to combine them. One is a setting that takes place in a very swampy forest, the type of place you'd expect to find witches, strange herbs, ruins, and things that bite off your head from above or drag you into the murky depths below. The other is a setting in which ghettoized lower classes become more like cyborgs as their bodies are piece-by-piece sacrificed to the divine by the upper classes. The "magic" in this game would mainly be along the lines of radical bioengineering and robotics.

I'm thinking Aztec culture might be a fit for some of this. My knowledge about Aztec culture though is on the level of what you might learn in high school world history, though, i.e., not much. I can spell Quetzalcoatl and generally describe what happened when the Spanish came, but that's about it right now. I've got some studying to do, I guess.

But if I can find a real-life setting that fits the swamp-overrun city setting, then I want to study up on it as well.

    \n Rome in the middle ages had problems with sinking into the swamps and mosquitos everywhere and all that. It was falling into disrepair during that time though. Usually a city in a swampy environment is defined by fighting to keep the swamp out. Rome could fit the ghettoized lower class too, the rich people would be living on the hills.
    Often cities are build into swamp regions because of trade. In river deltas for example. New Orleans would be one and Bangkok. Swimming markets and houses on stilts are something to keep in mind. Also aboveground gravesites.

    \n Given the whole "sacrificing body parts of the lower classes to the gods" thing, I think it might be interesting to avoid the exotic culture aspect altogether. Culture-wise, make them as close to "western culture" as you can get while adding that cultural element. That way you can focus on just the stuff that is different.

    Also, it helps avoid issues of potential racism and cultural appropriation, which would be emphasized with the heavy negativity of the sacrifice part.

    Just a thought.
