\n Hello!

Most of you probably already know about my wife Meguey's campaign to print the second edition of her game, but if you don't, it's here: A Thousand and One Nights: a game of enticing stories. She's trying to raise enough now to commission a dozen new pieces from the illustrator, Claudia Cangini, to include as pre-made courtiers for convention or LARP games.

But what I'm here to say is, food! One of the reward levels features a booklet of recipes collected and adapted by me, customized for you, and I'm excited about it, and it's not getting quite all the love I think it deserves. Here's the writeup:\n
The Sultan's Own Chef

A Thousand and One Nights is a sensual game. As the Sultan's Own Chef, you have a unique opportunity to make your game night into an occasion to remember. This reward includes a booklet of fitting recipes, collected and adapted by Vincent Baker, to create several delightful feasts or one true extravaganza. Shown here: Persian rice with saffron, pistachios and sultanas; butter beans with herbs and golden tomatoes; butternut squash with caramelized small onions and almonds; and a salad of artichokes, oranges and black olives.

\nBy default, the recipes will be vegetarian & vegan, and I'll add meat dishes if you want, or make sure to include only gluten-free dishes, or whatever you need. One of the things I'm doing is giving the principles of the dish, then a specific recipe, then suggested variations, so that you can easily adapt the dishes yourself. You can see what I'm talking about here, for example: Persian Rice (pdf). This is the way I cook myself, and I'm having a lot of fun thinking about the recipes this way.

Check it out if you want to! Food!

-Vincent
