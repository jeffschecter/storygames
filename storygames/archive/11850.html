    \n I've got it and I've put it to some use already. There's one entry for each letter of the alphabet, like books for "b", gems for "g", undead for "u", weird for "w". Each entry has a bunch of flavor text and some random tables.

    The tables are a deliciously mixed bag. Some are best used during adventure prep, or as a source of ideas. Some are perfect to roll out during play. The list of random books, for example, could be used any time. The artwork is particularly good, however. Every page is lavishly illustrated. That's worth the price of admission alone.

    \n It's definitely worth the 10 bucks (what, is that like half a pound Sterling now?) for the art alone. The content is reminiscent of the early AD&D modules, which I loved.

    But for someone coming at it, say from a D&D 3.5 standpoint and who doesn't care for old school D&D, you might find less value out of it.

    The tables are tables, but for me the best use is to ignore the rolling and have the cool content right at hand when you want to describe something in the dungeon. Sort of a D&D cheat sheet.
