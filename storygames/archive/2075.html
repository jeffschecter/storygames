    Aww, I miss seasons. When I lived in Alaska the winter solstice really meant something, because you'd be losing 5+ minutes of daylight every day for a while, and when that trend reversed it was worth celebrating. There is a cherished tradition of hiking up Flattop mountain, an easy summer hike right outside of town, and a couple of hundred people would get up there on the summer solstice and have a party to say goodbye to the light. For the winter solstice the crowd was a lot smaller but proportionally more awesome - we'd haul wood up and have a bonfire in the bitter cold and inky darkness. I miss that.
