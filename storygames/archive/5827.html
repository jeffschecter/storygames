\n In my current group* we are considering changing from Burning Wheel to The Shadow of Yesterday. One of thwe stumbling blocks for me is that I love the Circles-mechanic in BW - especially that a new-found contact may be opposed to the PC. This has already created quite a few memorable and important NPCs and events in the game fiction. There's a feeling of s huge, unexplored social network and I'd love to recreate that in TSOY.
But how to do it?
The Secret of Contacts goes some of the way, but I miss the chance of failure.
Does anyone have any suggestions?
Best
Peter

* We are playing a fantasy campaign inspired partly by A Game of Thrones and partly by Danish history in the Middle Ages ...

    \n Peter, be wary that TSoY works best with strictly "human" conflict. If you're campaign includes a lot of monsters, then BDtP can become quite tedious. You must avoid setting the monsters' intent as "wants to kill the PCs."

    Anyhow, I play in a TSoY game every Tuesday night, and in a BW game every Thursday, and the speed of them turns out to be about the same. We've used more aggressive scene framing in our TSoY game, so it might feel like more has "happened," but not really.

    BDtP and Fight! take about the same time to resolve in my experience.

    Good luck either way,

    Yoki

    \n Alternative which I found works best is this:

    You buy your friends with your own experience points. Ie. anything that the npc needs to roll is bought with your levels.
    Notice that most npc:s never need to roll anything and thus can be cheap. If the npc is leaves the game, points come
    back.

    Perhaps you can also introduce a roll to determine if you get to introduce someone.

    Why? Because if you are able to introduce something that has stats to the game without hitting
    your keys in progress you are getting basically free levels with a die roll. Which seems to break
    the whole fun keybashing thing for my group.
