    \nMouse Guard would work. Why?
    \n
    \n- You can fail individual actions without failing the overall mission as failure really means succeeding but with consequences, twists, and compromises.
    \n
    \n- The extended conflict mechanic supports chases, mass battles, scout missions, and whatever else you need pretty well. If you want to succeed, you will need team work and it pays to get prepared before you jump in (find allies, buy or build tools).

    \n Blowback is mission-based and explicitly plays in three phases: mission prep, the mission, and the ramifications of the mission. It also has it's own spin on the idea that failure means success with consequences\u2014 only instead of it making the next part of the plan more difficult (although it does that too, through the dice mechanic) the focus is on how far you're willing to go in order to get that success. What are you willing to sacrifice? Who are you willing to disappoint? What kind of person are you willing to become?

    \n Bliss Stage is also super great at this.

    1. You have a certain number of fixed mission objectives that you set for the mission.
    2. You have a fixed number of resources, measured in Fudge dice. If you roll those resources, you're putting them at risk.
    3. Each time you attempt an objective, you roll and assign dice to specific categories (keeping your shit together, achieving the objective, protecting your resources, etc.).
    4. When you assign dice, + is success, blank is some progress, - is bad things (it's Otherkind/AW dice, essentially).
    5. The GM can threaten specific categories or objectives to make you assign more dice to those (need ++ to be a +, etc.)
    6. New objectives can be uncovered or improvised during the mission... but now you have to roll again for that objective.

    It's super great and really, really deserves to be hacked to do more things than just Bliss Stage, because it's a fantastic set of mechanics.
