\n I'm happy to report that I'll be DMing a 4e conversion of Queen of Spiders for some of my employees at a weekly lunch game. Some of you might know that QoS was named as the top adventure of all time by Dungeon magazine, and it's no small stretch, combining the rock solid Against the Giants arc with the sauce Underdark goodness of the D series.

For me, there is the additional delight of reading through the original adventures with a nostalgic eye. Some of it is just, well, magical. On the other hand, some stuff, looking back now, is pretty entertainingly laughable, e.g., \n

Giants' Bags

There are numerous occasions when bags and chests are searched by the party. The contents of these containers may be detetmined randomly by using the table hereafter. The number of items in the bag or container is simply determined by rolling 5d4 to obtain a random number of items between 5 and 20. A roll for each item is then made on the Contents Table (following). If any item would be duplicated, roll again to select a replacement.

Giant's Bag Contents Table

1d100 Item in Bag or Other Container
01-03 old axe blade for use as hand chopper
04-17 boulders, small (1-4)
18-19 bowl and spoon, tin or pewter, battered
20-21 brass items, various and sundry, bent
22-23 caItrops, iron, large (1-6)
24-25 cheese, hard, slightly moldy, and stinky
26-30 cloak, shabby, wool or hide
31-32 comb and hairpins, bone
33-40 cooking pot, iron
41-43 horn, drinking
44-47 knife, skinning
48-53 Iinens, various, soiled. patched
54-60 meat, haunch of
61-64 money. copper (100-400 pieces)
65-67 money, silver (20-80 pieces)
68-69 money, gold (10-40 pieces)
70-76 pelt, fur, worthless and mangy
77-83 rope, 10(FM)-120(FM) coil, very strong
84-85 salt, small bag or box of
86-90 sandals, old
91-98 skin, water or wine, fulI
99-00 teeth or tusks, animaI, no ivory value (1-8)
\nThis is weird on just so many levels. For any given giant bag, you're looking at as many as 20-40 rolls, and there's like 50 giants in the steading. All just so I can tell the PCs there's some moldy cheese in a giant's bag?

What's even weirder is that I'm quite certain this (and tables like it) didn't even give me pause back in the day.

    \n That noun-comma-adjective style reminds me of the labels you seen in army clothing. "Jacket, wool, lined, men's", etc. I can imagine your more organized humanoid races, like hobgoblins, labeling all of their possessions like that.

    "The wretched halfling slave has a tag affixed to his iron slave-collar reading 'Slave, Halfling, Used'."

    DM: "You don't have salt, show me your character sheet!"
    Me: "Sure I do. I have like fifteen bags from those giants. Come get some, giant slugs!"

    \n Anyone else remember a piece of software called TableMaster? It was a simple programming language, vaguely BASIC-like, that was totally optimized for writing programs that rolled randomly on complicated tables. It came with many example tables, some of them useful, some just silly.

    I loved that thing to death, back in the day.
