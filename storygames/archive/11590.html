    \n I wouldn't do Foundation as a team-based mission-structured game, frankly. I mean, what's the point of gaming the glory that is Foundation if you can't play
    • Ignorant warlords in the age of decline?
    • Keen agents of Second Foundation, burdened with hidden knowledge and psionic powers?
    • Space Traders of the First Foundation, oblivious but independent-minded?
    • A robot?
    • The Mule?
    \nThe above reminds me of In a Wicked Age. Also, I could see Shock:, with psychohistory, planet-wide urbanization, robots and galactic empire as the shocks. Wouldn't be able to do it justice in a one session of either of those, though.

    If team-based is crucial, then I guess I'd go for Traveller. Out of left field, eh?

    \n Shock: is a good choice!

    The Foundation series was actually a big influence on Burning Empires. You can make Hober Mallow, the Mule, and psychologists from the Second Foundation straight out of the book. Psychohistory is a skill in the game. We imagined that the Iron Empires were the result of the Seldon Plan's inability to cope with both the Mule and the vaylen.

    It's not a perfect fit, as the game focuses on the conflict between humanity and the vaylen. But I don't think it would be too challenging to get it to do what you want.
