\n The RPG = Role Playing Girl 2009 zine is now available in print and pdf format.

This was a labor of love, and a real education to put together. I'm fond of saying "people talk about how to 'get women into gaming' but they don't look around and realize that there *are* tons of women involved." But I had no idea of how ignorant I was about women who were currently or had been active in writing and publishing games. My hat is off to Lisa Stevens, current CEO of Paizo, whose credits include helping found that company, Wizards of the Coast and on back to Lion Rampant Games. And folks like Julia Martin who has been writing for D&D for two decades now. As well as Lee Gold, philker extraordinaire, and founder of Alarums and Excursions and Liz Danforth who is just a powerhouse of energy working on integrating games into the curriculum at libraries nationwide. Truly master geeks.

Meg Baker and I wrote articles for the zine. Meg talks about teaching Storming the Wizard's Tower to kids in her son's school, with reflections on the girls' experiences. I wrote about my experiences being an independent publisher, and talk a lot about what being at the Forge booth meant to me. You can see the contents here.

We're working on gathering interested folks for another in 2010. We had contributers from New Zealand and editors from near Detroit (Thanks, Danielle!) and the UK (Go Christina!). If you'd like to contribute or help, or know someone who might, please let me know!
