\n Hey, I've been asked to write a Valentine's Day gift guide for Graffiti about romantic games or ones that would be good to play for couples around the holiday. I've heard of Shooting the Moon and know a little bit about it, but was wondering if you all had any other suggestions, either RPGs or board/card games you've come across that would fit the bill.

I only have enough space for about 3-4 games, with how to purchase info and a short description. Any suggestions would be appreciated. The article is due today or Monday, but shouldn't take me long to write.

Thanks in advance,

    \n Yes, that's right. Breaking The Ice is an indie classic. You can't not put it in.

    I think the other must-have is Paul Czege's Bacchanal. It's about finding your companion while you're caught up in Bacchanalian celebrations. It's...how do I put this?...for mature readers.

    As a counterpoint, how about Beast Hunters? That's a two-player game, where one player is a sort of tribal hunter and the other puts his challenges in his/her way. Not romantic, so much, as a good game for two people to play together.

    As a quirky one, It Was A Mutual Decision is a game about a break-up. I think you split into two teams, boys and girls.

    \n Hey Michael,

    I'm trying to put myself in your shoes and I honestly can't say whether I'd include Bacchanal in the Valentine's Day article or not. It plays great with two players. It's intense. It's explicit. It's intimate and thrilling and voyeuristic. But there's definitely the potential for dark turnings of events. If the Graffiti audience likes their entertainment on the edge, then yeah. But otherwise, my recommendation is Vincent Baker's Matchmaker.

    Here's our actual play report from a couple of years ago:

    it was a double shot of my baby's love
