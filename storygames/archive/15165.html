\n This is a question for (Sage or Adam, obviously, and) anyone in the Adventurer's Guild:
\n
\nWhich rules would you recommend I swap to from the Hack version, the "Full, Print" rules or the Red Book? Is there a significant difference?
\nPlease bear in mind that this comes on the heels of my previous first-ever DW session, so there are five players (it is also probably worth noting that the only character whose class is not represented in the Redbook or a separate playbook is the Ranger, and also that he appears by all accounts to love being a Ranger).
\n
\nAs I see it, I really have four directions I can go here:
\n
\n
\nA: I transition to Red Book rules whole cloth, and as the Ranger to be a Cleric (I don't like this idea, since only one player has to be put out, and that seems shitty).
\n
\nB: I transition to Red Book rules, but allow the Ranger version from the "Full" rules (but I don't know how compatible the class is with the Red Book, and also he'll be the only player without a proper playbook).
\n
\nC: I transition to Red Book rules, and attempt to edit the Ranger found in the "Full" rules to be compatible (though I don't feel qualified to do that yet, as I am not aware of what changes that might entail in order to maintain a modicum of "balance" and harmony with Red Book).
\n
\n I transition whole cloth to the "Full" rules and ignore the Red Book entirely (not sure if this is a good idea since they are an older version of the rules. Are there as many internal consistencies as there are in Hack? Also there is the issue of a lack of proper playbooks, so lots of paper per player...
\n
\n
\nSo...what do we think?

    \n I use a collaboration of all the rulesets. Thr principles and GM moves stay the same. The front and monster design stay the same. Its just the player moves that are a little different. Its no big deal. Just remember to alllow the moves to evolve from the fiction, rather than trying to fit a particular move into a scene. The mechanical effect is simply a way of interpreting the move into the story. I say go with the characters you've got, the playbooks you've got and develop fronts as per the rules. If you are hesitant about introducing a player move, run it by the group before play and ask them what they think. I haven't had a bad experience yet with this mishmash. Untill the full rules are complete, it works for us. :)
