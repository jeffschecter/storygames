    \n Your tray table is going to be a tight fit. When I played, I found it helped my enjoyment to take a minute and update the fiction in my head with each drawn card - don't get lazy! Make sure you have a pencil and a separate copy of the game sheet to play with (gotten from the web site). If you can print it out half size, so much the better given the tray table size.

    You are in for a treat.

    \n
    \n
    \n The fiction really is dream-like, in that you have to interpret it yourself. A lot of it won't seem to make sense, but if you get into it, you can weave interesting bits together.

    Games can be short or long, depending on how your cards are shuffled. So playing one game won't necessarily give you the whole experience. It's perfectly possible that it just ends abruptly without you having had to figure much out, if your Heart is out in the open all the time. But if it gets caught in a prism, or taken by a character in the game, you might have to work hard on getting it back. That means one game may be very different from another.

    \n Lisa turned over and handled the cards, I read the entries, and we talked a bit about the fiction together. There wasn't really that much fiction going on, other than some imagery and trying to tie some of the events together. Lisa was quite smitten with the descriptions and names as they were, though, considering it a very romantic game. Also an "amazing game with great potential" that nevertheless "is somewhat frustrating," at least throughout our first game (which we finished earlier today). In short, while there are so many things that could happen, most of the time we're told what we can't do because we don't have the right item or companion with us; Lisa suggested upping the number of items you can carry at once to make this less of an issue. Still, though, overall a very good experience playing together.
