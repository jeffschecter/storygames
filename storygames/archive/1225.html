      RED STRINGS
      By Jason Morningstar

      Virginia, 1862. The capitol of the Confederacy. Richmond College is a hotbed of rebellion - against a rebellion.

It formed a language impenetrable to casual - and not so casual - eavesdroppers. The terms were transmitted from one cell to another. Now the entire Richmond underground whispers about viscosity and membranes.

      The movement is growing, but the price for a single mistake is high. Treason means an undignified death at the end of a rope. And spies are everywhere. Who can you trust? What risks will you take? Are you prepared to give your life for Mr. Lincoln, and the cause of liberty?

      THE THINGS ON YOUR SHEET

      Your character has...

      ...a Name.

      ... a Flavor - sweet, salty, sour, or bitter. This is your general demeanor, and will give you an advantage in recruiting like-minded people. You are encouraged to use your flavor as roleplaying shorthand.

      ...a Membrane, which is both his personal background and protective social layer. Maybe he is a professor at Richmond College. Maybe he is a student, or a groundskeeper, or a dormitory matron and chaperone. Maybe he is a doctor or nurse at the makeshift hospital that has been set up on the grounds. Maybe he is a slave. Write down your character's most important milieu - good examples would be "Richmond College faculty", "Richmond Presbyterian church", or "13th Regiment, Virginia militia". This is the group to which you will turn for aid in your most dire need, and who may well turn their back on you and watch you hang. Their status in society is irrelevant, but their importance to your character should be paramount.

      ... Vacuoles, which are people from within the membrane who can and will aid your character in times of trouble. Write down three - they need not be detailed or fleshed out in advance.

      ... Six potential sympathizers, to whom you have extended discrete Tendrils. If an NPC is written down under tendrils, they are a likely bet due to Union sympathies, family ties, potential for blackmail, or other reasons. When trying to recruit people under tendrils, instead of rolling a single die, the agent and sympathizer each roll two dice and discard the lower.

      ... a Cell, which is represented by the plasm diagram on the upper right of the character sheet. Your character occupies the center of the cell, the seven position. The character's of the players to your right and left at the table take the one and two positions. Any sympathizer you recruit can be added to your cell in any of the four remaining positions. Using them as agents to recruit others may be to your advantage.

      ... Plasm markers, to indicate covert cells that you have successfully initiated, as well as the value of the sympathizer you have recruited.

      The four techniques for recruiting sympathizers:

      Pliability refers to a character's use of pressure and overt intimidation.
