\n Ariele Agostini is one of the guys from Janus Design who translated Polaris, Don't Rest Your Head and now Spirit of the Century into Italian. He's also a teacher to children from 3 to 5 years of age. He kindly offered to play Happy Birthday, Robot! with his class and send me the resulting story.


Happy Birthday, Robot!
Robot is sad because he thinks he's the only robot left.
Robot is wrong but he doesn't know it.
There was a huge war between robots but he's the only good one.
So other robots came and to try to destroy him.
Robot destroys'em all but is again sad and lonely.
Robot knows how to build other robots, but couldn't because he had no pieces.
The other robots are now all destroyed and junk.
Robot rebuilds them because he knows how to do it.
Now the other robots are good too, and robot has many friends!


This is my slight edit of Ariele's translation from Italian into English, so the wording might be a little clunky. Well, actually, no that wording is pretty typical for a Robot story! :P It's nice to know that even though the game is designed for the "clever ten-year-old" audience, a very young kid with adult guidance can play, too.
