    Okay. First, yes, there are people who are totally into PC death on a die roll, or "Figure out my puzzle, as a player, or die!" provided the GM in question is actually decent at coming up with fair puzzles.     \n Since Jason's got most of what he wanted out of this thread (and it sounds like it turned out great), let me take just a second to talk about traps:

    A trap is just a fictional thing, right? The GM portrays the fantastic world, which is filled with traps, magical and mundane. So in the most basic sense, a trap is just a fictional thing that GM knows about that may be sprung. It could even be arrived at in the moment - Steve misses a roll, so Jason thinks "oh yeah, there's probably some sewer goblin traps around, maybe that can be my move" (so long as it hasn't already been established through Trap Sense or other moves that there aren't any traps).

    If the GM has planned those out, they could totally have custom moves associated with them. The custom moves and hacking the game chapter is the last I'm writing, which is happening now, and that seems more like what's being asked with regards to "making traps."

    The sewer log flume? That could be a big deal, with it's own custom moves and such, or it could be just a way to know what basic moves to make - probably separating them (the flume throws people down different side-pipes), maybe deal damage (getting crushed against walls), or use up their resources (rations washed away or inedible). You could even get a little more creative just with the basic GM moves: put someone in a spot by washing them into a sleeping albino sewer gator's lair, or show signs of doom as the sewer water washes back and carries mutilated body parts with it.

    So, to stefoid's question: a trap isn't any more or less pointless than any other challenge the GM portrays. All of the GM's moves are designed to create more interesting situations, a trap is just a reason to make moves.

    \n I just worked up a Dungeon World monster using the Beta 2 monster creation rules and I like it!

    WATER MONSTER
    Tail-hand (d8+1 damage, +strangle, +silent, +ignores armor), 16 HP, 1 armor (thick fur)
    Close, reach

    Special qualities: Tail-hand, Stealthy, Intelligent, Construct, Disturbing

    Instinct: Ambush and murder, Parley from safety

    * * *
    Near the Hill of the Raccoon lives the terrible Water Monster. It is a twisted aberration, half-human and half-monkey but larger than either, stinking of tannin and decay and slick with green moss, with a tail that terminates in a weird grasping hand.

    Water Monster lurks in thick swampy forest, using its tail-hand to snatch small animals to devour, but its greatest delight is human flesh. It will gladly negotiate safe passage (and even serve as guide and protector through its domain) in exchange for fresh eyes, teeth or nails - Water Monster's favorite morsels. More than one lost wanderer has been forced to make a terrible sacrifice. If offered a ready supply of fresh human meat, Water Monster might even become a loyal companion.

    Water Monster has a memory stretching back to the Age of Magic and remembers its creator, Princess Deer, and her long-abandoned tower now swallowed by the jungle.

    * * *\n
    CREATION NOTES

    I want a monster based on the Aztec beast Ahuizotl, bane of Mapacht\xe9pec, offspring of Lady Mazotl.

    Water Monster's biggest threat is ambush and murder. Its instinct is to trick and devour.
    It is a solitary hunter: +solitary, d8+1 damage, 16 HP
    Big as a cart: +large, +close, +reach, HP bump, damage bump
    Thick fur, +1 armor
    Known for trickery (+stealthy, custom trip/tangle move for tail)
    Known for strange adaptation (tail-hand, custom move)
    Armaments are small and weak (-die size)
    Armaments keep others at bay +reach
    Smart as a human +intelligent
    Magical construct +construct
    Appearance +disturbing

      The Nuckelavee
      "If crops were blighted by sea-gust or mildew, if livestock fell over high rocks that skirt the shores, or if an epidemic raged among men, or among the lower animals, Nuckelavee was the cause of all. His breath was venom, falling like blight on vegetable, and with deadly disease on animal life."

      The Nuckelavee is a creature of abject terror, and spoken of with bated breath - From the few survivors descriptions of the Nuckelavee, we learn that its head is similar to that of a man only "ten times larger". It has an incredibly wide mouth that juts out like a pig's snout and a single red eye that burns with a red flame. Hairless, its body is also skinless, its entire surface appearing like raw and living flesh. It's said that its thick, black blood can be seen coursing through his veins, as its sinewy muscles writhed with every movement it makes. Its long ape-like arms hang down to the ground and from its gaping mouth spews a foul, black reek.

      All in all, not a pleasant sight to encounter on some lonely stretch of coastline.


      What is the biggest threat it poses?
      *Spreads pestilence and decay with its foul breath
      *sow the seed of doubt, despair and fear into the hearts of others
      *slaughter with abandon
      *feed off the terror it creates

      What does it want that causes problems for others?
      This is its instinct: An Undying hatred and spite for all mankind
      How does it usually hunt or fight?
      All by its lonesome: Solitary, d10 damage, 12 HP
      How big is it?
      As big as a cart: Large, Close, Reach, +4 HP[16], +1 damage[d10+1]

      Leathers or think hide: 1 armor + Permanent magical protection: Magical

      Unrelenting strength: +2 damage, forceful [1d10+3]
      Unnatural endurance: +4 HP[20]
