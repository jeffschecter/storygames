\n OK, thanks to everybody who contributed an element in part one. Time for part two! We're writing an adventure for roleplaying.

The adventure is divided into three Episodes, each of which has five Chapters. I've arranged the elements you provided in a semi-logical way, and now we can build the Chapters out one by one. Here's how it works:

1. Chapters must be authored in sequence. If someone has done chapter One and someone else has done chapter Two, you must do chapter Three. All three Episodes can be worked on simultaneously, though, so add a chapter to the one you like best.

2. Number and title your chapter if it needs one. Include all the elements provided for your chapter. Add new elements sparingly, but reincorporate when appropriate. Upon their first appearance, name characters and add color. Aim for a few hundred words.

3. Honor previous chapters and use details provided (names, etc). As before, play it straight - avoid the weak choice of silly humor.

4. Only author a single chapter! I'll change this if we don't get a solid response.

OK, let's go!

    \nEPISODE TWO: THE PEARL AND THE DINOSAUR

    CHAPTER ONE: THE CROCODILE PENS OF ABDUL ALEEM

    The jungles of Puerto Pincesa sweltered in the summer, and even moreso when there were a hundred crocodiles nipping at your feet! Junius Bird wiped the sweat off of the stubble on his granite chin and wiped it on his faded fedora, for luck. He had been captured after tracking the rogue Japanese scientist Dr. Epoch to the fabled crocodile pens of the crazed Arab named Abdul Aleem.

    Dr. Epoch's men had deposited him with a laugh on a little island that was maybe three corpses wide in the middle of the swampy den. The hungry crocodiles took turns lunging up at their pro-offered meal, and the brave adventurer kept them at bay only with swift kicks from his favorite brown leather riding boots. He looked over again at the mad scientist who had turned his attention to a complicated-looking device that was set next to an altar built into the rocky prominence. This was an X-Ray machine that could actually see through the rock to what was beyond! Other uniformed soldiers stood by with pickaxes and shovels, ready to dig into the perfect spot in the rock.

    Above the scientific layout was a network of bamboo and palm fronds to shield this work from prying eyes who might be flying by. Junius glanced over at his kukri, the heavy curved knife given to him by a grateful Nepalese Colonel. The pitted and razor-sharp knife was sitting in it's leather sling on a small table next to one of the Japanese guards, who was also looking at the altar. And then suddenly a roar and a blast of water on Junius' exposed legs as another crocodile lunged at him. Junius looked down at the crocodiles in the water and at the land about ten yards away, did some quick calculations in his mind, and jumped!

    Heads turned in amazement and water splashed as Junius quickly ran towards dry land, using crocodile heads as stepping stones. He ran straight over and punched the soldier guarding his knife in his face, breaking the soldier's glasses and causing him to drop his rifle.

    Dr. Epoch screamed, "Bird! Get him!!" as Junius grabbed his favorite kukri knife and swiped at one of the bamboo supports. It sliced right in two, and the entire latticework of bamboo and palm leaves groaned loudly before crashing down on the Japanese.

    A few errant rifle shots followed Junius Bird as he ran up a rocky outcropping to safety. Looking back, he could see that some of the palm leaves had fallen partly in the swampy water. And crocodiles were starting to make their way onto dry land. There were more rifle shots and some screams as he ran off to find his seaplane and get some support.

    EPISODE ONE, CHAPTER ONE: THE SNOWS OF KOLYMA

    Kolyma, Russian Far East: 70,000 prisoners work in subhuman conditions mining gold. It is, literally, a hell on earth. And from this hell one man has to be saved \u2013 Viktor Kovalyanov, brilliant optical physicist and inventor of the Hyperboloid device.

    The action begins as the player characters fight their way out of a subterranean gold mine with the weak and fragile physicist in tow. Cool stuff:

    Hand-pulled cars on crude tracks snaking everywhere
    Hardened Soviet criminals waiting for an excuse to riot
    Dynamite! And 100 kilos of gold.
    Desperate political prisoners eager to be rescued \u2013 or for a crust of bread or a whispered word home
    Brutal NKVD guards and soldiers, who will themselves be Gulag-bound if a prisoner escapes
    Eduard Petrovich Berzin, Commandant and cruel enforcer, who will dog the PCs and swear vengeance upon them.

    Once out of the gold mine, it's into the howling subarctic gale! Cool stuff:

    A cat-and-mouse chase through the frozen tiaga.
    Crashing through a frozen river!
    Berzin's armored Rolls Royce and a truck full of NKVD troops in bearskin coats
    A timely assist from Yukaghir natives
    The physicist collapses
    On the frozen lake \u2013 the plane won't start! The severe cold has turned the motor oil into sludge!

    Assuming they escape, with or without Kovalyanov, the chapter should probably end with them en route back to Manhattan, USA. In fact, the whole thing could be framed as a flashback.

    \nEPISODE ONE, CHAPTER TWO: BAGUETTE OF DEATH

    The chapter starts as the player characters are traveling back to Manhattan in style in a French dirigible. There is a cocktail party being thrown onboard for either Viktor Kovalyanov (if he is present) or his noble assistant, Lev Sergeivitch Termen as the Hyperboloid is bring brought in a briefcase to America. Cool stuff:

    A Cagoulard hit-man with a walking stick hiding a silenced pistol
    A beautiful actress, dressed to the nines
    A smoke bomb causing havoc on board the dirigible
    A fistfight outside in the blistering airstream
    A stolen briefcase containing the Hyperboloid
    Parachute chase to the liner steaming below.
    A dead pilot and jammed dirigible controls

    Presuming the characters follow the hit-man down to the Q-ship disguised as an ocean liner. More cool stuff:

    French baddies armed with MAS machine-carbines spitting lead
    Hidden gun turrets rake the skies
    A desperate chase for the briefcase
    A man's dying admission, "Dr. Epoch put me up to it!"
    A baker's dozen of Marseilles sailors, armed with long knives
    Strange man locked up in a stateroom with "Mayflower" tattooed on his arm
    A submersible escape pod

    The chapter should probably end with the party entering Manhattan, either with or without the Hyperboloid
