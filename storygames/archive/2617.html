\n I'm crossposting my plea, because I am a selfish, stinky jerk who needs help.
\n
\nSo I'm in Texas with my Significant Other's family for spring break. Tomorrow afternoon, we leave to drive to a beach house in Galveston where, her 16 year-old brother assures me, there is almost nothing to do. Two of his friends are coming with us, and have made plans to bring instruments and video games to amuse themselves. He's heard me go on about Games before, so he suggested that he and I and one of his friends (according to him, the other one wouldn't be interested) play some MERP.
\n
\nI had never even heard of MERP before, but it's apparently all he and his friends play. I may still try to play it with them, but from what the S.O. says, it's everything about D&D-alikes that I don't find fun. That, and the way her brother argues how unlike D&D it is, has convinced me that I should run a game or two of something we can all find fun, and that might expose us to a wider range of gaming experience.
\n
\nBig Problem: My awesome-o RPG library is back in Maryland. I am the king of planning ahead.
\n
\nSo, I'm looking for games that I can get before I go beyond the reach of the internet, glorious source of answers that it is.
\n
\nSome tenative (as in, I realize I might be wrong and please tell me if I am) requirements:
\n
\n1) Electronic. Obviously, I can't wait for shipping, and I don't know what's available in the area. A pdf can be downloaded and printed out (or stored on a laptop if it's too long) in nothing flat. Obviously, any product sold on RPGnow or Drivethru would be fine.
\n2) Not already owned by me. I don't own a laptop, and having both the print and pdf versions of a book doesn't really appeal to me. Also, there's a lot of great games out there that I don't own yet, and I'm not above using this as an impetus to get some more. I won't give you a list, but I will say that (unfortunately) Mountain Witch and With Great Power are both out.
\n3) Targeted appeal. I haven't played with them yet, so I can't be overly specific, but I know that these guys like the way they play, chart consultation and all. I want to challenge them, but I worry they might snap back if I push too hard. I don't want anything too 'out there.' Interpret as loosely as you like.
\n4) Fast. The phrase is 'low barrier to entry'. My most recent fave game is Artesia, at least in part for it's long and immensely entertaining character generation system, but that seems like it might not be suited for the lazy days situation. I don't want the game to feel like work at any point.
\n
\nThere are already a few games simple enough that I've memorized them, and thus will be 'brought with' by definition. WUSHU is the big one that I'm definitely trying to fit in. The startup edition of Inspectres is fairly straightforward, though I'm sad to not remember cool dice, weird agents and how to handle traits given by testimonials. Finally, the S.O. says I have to try starting a game of Kazekami Kyoko Kills Kublai Khan on the car-ride down.
\n
\nPossibilities I have noticed and am considering beyond those:
\n
\n* Roanoke. I love WUSHU intensely, and it sounds awesome. Maybe a bit dark and 'weird' for the target audience, but at 5$, I may buy it anyway and take my chances.
\n* Contenders. I have heard this is a great, broadly appealing pick up game, with the ability to appeal to both sides of the brain.
\n* Spirit of the Century. People can't stop gushing about it, and I've been planning on buying it for a while. I suppose I'd rather have a print than pdf edition, but I could have fun with both later, and only have fun with the latter this week.
\n
\nBut, to get to the point, I would really appreciate the help, perspective and brilliance of the fandom. So please, if you have any ideas, please name them, briefly explain why they are suited to my situation, and tell me where I can get them.
\n
\nIf everything works out, I'll report my purchases in these threads, and even give an AP report when I get home in two weeks.

    \n If you own Primetime Adventures, you probably know the rules, which means you don't need electronic delivery. While PTA doesn't fit under the "targeted appeal" section of your wishlist, you can pitch it to them as Middle Earth as an ongoing television series. Although they like the charts, they might want to try something different. PTA is pretty darn fast. If you don't have PTA, you can order it at http://www.dog-eared-designs.com/pta-buy.html and Matt will get the PDF for you on the Forge's bookshelf. You'll have to start up a bookshelf account and wait for Matt to throw the switch to get the file.

    If you want a dungeon-crawl like experience and can get your hands on a lot of d20s (about ten might do you good), try Donjon. RPGnow has it for $6.75 It comes with a low-level adventure, too. It has some charts they might like to reference and is a pretty quick game to play.

    \nThe Shadow of Yesterday is what you are looking for! Freely available, I assume you don't own it, targeted appeal, and fast, fast, fast.

    If you use the existing setting (Near), it's ready to go. If you de-couple the system from the setting, like if your guys want to play in Middle Earth or whatever, it works for pretty much anything if you prepare Secrets and Keys for the setting you want in advance. It's "traditional" enough that they will know which end is up, there is plenty of crunch if you want it, but it does fun things with authority and flag-setting that makes it 1000% awesome.

    Based on your initial post, The Roach (while I of course urge you to pick up a copy to bathe in the undiluted genius of the author) absolutely isn't what you want.

    \nSpirit of the Century character creation is downright awesome, and I want to say that the PDF+PoD bundle is only $5 more than the PoD by itself. Plus, pretty easy.

    But when it comes to your Targeted Appeal, you might get some more snap-back if you're running a game you're not familiar with. I've seen people try to consult SotC when doing bribery attempts who didn't accept my somewhat shaky answer of "uh, we could look it up later, just roll." That "look it up in the book" reaction ground the game to a halt, and liberal application of clockwork ninjas was required to save it.

    I'm going to be contrary and suggest three entirely different options.

    \n

    if they are hardcore about tables, detailed combat, and crunchiness: GURPS Lite. It's free, easy to pick up, and the side benefit is you can sneak in stuff like conflict resolution vs. task resolution or letting them define details on critical success for knowledge rolls.

    \n

    if they aren't so hardcore, and you're familiar with octaNe: Blood and Steel. It's free, and you could do a Middle Earth setting in it very easily.

    \n

    if they aren't hardcore and you want something you can learn quickly: the demo version of vs. Monsters, or the deluxe version if you haven't bought it yet. You could easily change it to vs. Sauron if need be.
