\n I've been thinking about my playing habits over the past few years.
\n
\nI am lucky to have a reliable group of story games enthusiasts. Only three of us, though, but for most games, that's enough. We've recruited others for certain games, but none of them stuck like the rest of us three "core" members. No matter, for most games, it works. Though often we are wanting for a fourth...
\n
\nBut I digress. We've been playing to a rythm of 1 game session per week,approimately. We've skipped several, and added a few mid-week.
\n
\nMostly, we've been trying out games we'd never played before. We'd play them once, either as a one-shot or as a short campaign (which would usually finish before the end). And then we'd put the game down, and pick up another for the next week.
\n
\nAnd that's great! Of the thirteen games we've played together in the past 12 months, there were nine which I've had never played before! That's very neat.
\n
\nHowever, I realize we've often gone for quantity over quality. Many games were not given a fair chance to shine at our table. Others provided a good experience, but we still put them aside for another time.
\n
\nAnd I still have over a dozen unplayed games on my shelf (more counting what I have on my hard drive).
\n
\nSo I decided, for one thing, to not buy any new games for a while. (Actually, I said a year, but that's not very realistic...) Also, I'm going to try and play fewer games, each of them longer and more often. That means not trying as many new games (new games I already have) as last year, probably, but still a few. It would be way too frustrating not to.
\n
\nSo now I wonder how much my gaming habits vary from my fellow gamers. What are your gaming habits?

    \n I'm in two groups.Group one has four people. We play more or less weekly, and eat together, with a schedule of who provides food when. We also rotate choosing what to play and usually play the same game for 3-5 sessions. When it is your turn, you get buy-in (if anybody isn't happy with your choice you pick a different game) and GM or facilitate or whatever. Right now we're playing How We Came To Live Here and before that it was Diaspora, etc. The only game I can recall playing more than once is PTA, although I may be forgetting something.

    The other group has seven people. Also more or less weekly, usually down at least one participant - the rule is if two can't make it we either skip a week or do something else. We used to eat together but don't anymore, although we share snack-bringing duties. This group also rotates through games, but we have a more limited assortment - primarily TSOY and PTA these days, with the occasional experiment. We pitch short campaign ideas twice a year and our games tend to run eight or more sessions. This group has been together for like ten years.

    \n There's like 8 people here that I would consider part of our gaming group. However, three of them are absent most of the year and one is a complete casual player, of whom we don't expect any investment. So it boils down to four regulars. Depending on who shows up we have various games going now, and try to find something ongoing to play with the people present or kick off something new. At the moment we have a Vampire, BattleTech, WHFRP2 and D&D games going, with Burning Wheel, Apocalypse World, Mouse Guard, Rogue Trader and Cthulhu pending (with a chance of becoming regulars). In between we get to play stuff like Lady Blackbird, Ghost/Echo, Montsegur, my playtests and other one-shots. If there's no RPG game going for the people present, we usually end up playing one of our many boardgames (recently: Twilight Imperium, Game of Thrones, Catan, Hero Quest, Battlestar Galactica).

    This has been going on only for the past few months, when we managed to "get back" two of our absent gamers, and began to re-evaluate our gaming times, scheduling, etc. I made a roster of people present and we cross-reference to see what we should play. Before that it was far more sporadic and unorganized, with practically no games going. Long before that it was highschool and we had campaigns that lasted years that we would play for 12 hours at a time. Ah, youth.

    The approach of having many games running at once for different combinations of players has worked very well for us in terms of quantity. We've gamed a lot more in the past few months than in the past few years, probably. The problem is, we all want to settle down with something and play it for a while, but it's really hard to do with this setup. Plus, one of the irregulars will be moving away soon and one of our core group will probably go to work abroad, too. So...

    \n I get my game on roughly twice a month with a regular game on the last Thursday of the month and another one on a Sunday (whenever group scheduling allows). I pepper this with more spontaneous gaming when I can get it. I like minis gaming too, so I do that when I have the opportunity.

    I have a core group but there are several players on the periphery I invite when I run something I think they might like. As a result I game with about 7 or 8 people regularly.

    In between games, I pitch all kinds of ideas to the crew and see what gains any traction with them that I can turn into an actual game. When I get hooked on something, I read all kinds of related books and watch movies for ideas and inspiration.

    \n Our group gets together once a week (with a week off here and there for work-related travel, etc.) for a 3-4 hour session. We generally play a game for 12-16 sessions before reaching an ending, setting it aside, and starting a new game. The new game's usually in a different genre and mood entirely: we won't play two sci-fi games back to back, in other words, or two humorous games in a row, or two action-focused games in a row.

    And that's actually been our system for many years now, barring a few hiccups here and there when schedules got rearranged or no one felt like running anything; during those times we still get together and hang out, we just don't game. Sometimes we've added a session during the week, sometimes we've done a month of one-shots, but we always come back to playing one game, once a week, for 3-4 months.

    \n I've been gaming weekly with the same group, give or take a few people, for two years now. The schedule's pretty flexible: we play a game until it's over, however long that takes (our Apocalypse World game is starting to come to a close, and I think we've been playing since early winter). Then we "take a break," during which we usually have a weekly boardgame or one-shot night, and then someone (Vincent) has something they want to run, and our headspace is cleared up, and we're ready for the next long game.

    More recently (last three or four months?) we've been doing AW on alternating thursdays with Blowback, because everyone wanted to play both, Vincent needed a break in his GMing, and we didn't have the space to fit in two games a week. Blowback's over though, and now we're looking at doing the boardgame/one-shot night for a while on alternate thursdays. Unless we can find another player for Poison'd (3 people want to play, 3 don't).
