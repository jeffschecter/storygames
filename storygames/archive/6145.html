    \n The campsites are fairly nice, being spacious enough for several tents each. (Each site nominally holds eight campers, and depending of the tent configuration could certainly hold more.

    We were up on a hill last time, but there wasn't much wind. The campground is actually across the road from the water, but you could certainly walk to the beach.

    Nighttime got a bit cold, but this year we're slightly later so it should be warmer.

    We had a few gas stoves last time, but we also had a wood fire.

    Cambria is a mile down the road, complete with grocery store.

    We didn't interact much with non-Nerdly campers, as the part wasn't even close to full so people tended to spread out. Everyone I dealt with was nice though.

    \n There are also flush toilets and a couple of sinks to wash dishes at the campground, as well as a place to buy firewood if you run out. It was a chilly at night and there was a bit of fog in the morning, but t-shirt warm during the day. Bring a sun-hat. Each campsite has a firepit with grill that worked well for cooking meats/veggies for dinner, and there were a couple of propane-fueled campstoves for other cooking, too. There are some trees and the campsites were relatively flat; definitely enough space so no one had to sleep on a noticeable slope.

    --Nancy

    \n And, yes, showers.

    I really, really like this park. It's well-put-together, in a beautiful location, and relatively unknown, so uncrowded.

    Last year we did a slightly overcomplicated food plan; this year we're simplifying and making things more sit-chat-and-be-friendly rather than hey-everybody-it's-time-to-help-make-food. We'll have propane and firepits for cooking this year.
