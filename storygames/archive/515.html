    My brother and I were big bottle-rocket enthusiasts in our day. We made up a game that was essentially a live-action turn-based combat sim. we had 'rifles' that were tubes on sticks that the rockets shot out of. Each turn, you could take three big steps in any direction, end-facing was important, of course.

    Then came the combat phase. Load a bottle-rocket, light, aim and fire. (We had protective goggles or football helmets and long sleeves/pants. OK - not always.) Points were scored if you a) hit the other person, or b) got them to jump out of the way or move their feet. Five points if you scored a 'scorcher' - actually had the rocket explode on your near your target.

    Wildly popular with us - not so much with our mother.

    To our credit, we only had one incident where clothes ignited, and only a few minor burn patches.
