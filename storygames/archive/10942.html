\n I just submitted my playtest draft for Castle Bravo to Pelgrane Press. It's a Trail of Cthulhu adventure set in the 1950s that was loosely inspired by an idea that Judd Karlman had about an aircraft carrier that is circling a classified uncharted island in the Pacific. The pitch is this: You're all sailors and scientists aboard the USS Bairoko near the Bikini atoll in 1954 for secret atomic weapons testing. The bomb goes off, and things start to go sideways...

I ran it two weeks ago at Ubercon and had a ton of fun--it was the same weekend, in fact, that Jason Morningstar ran his TOC adventure at MACE called The Black Drop, also coming out soon from Pelgrane Press. It's an adventure Ripped from History! and Simon Rogers is looking for folks to playtest it. If you're interested, e-mail him at simon@dyingearth.com, subject "Castle Bravo Playtest."
