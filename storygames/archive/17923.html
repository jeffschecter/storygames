    \n When I first heard of Freemarket - a place where the life qualities are perfect, with no crime, no fight for surviving etc. - I thought it would be a cool experiment to see what happens if you put the mind of the people living today (the players) into a setting like that. My guess is that they probably want to destroy Freemarket, because they got thoughts like greed and envy, which also was confirmed when I heard my gaming group first attempt to play the game: they tried to destroy Freemarket.

    So when I finally got to play the game, I discovered that it was a trading game where you build a company up from scratch. Cool idea, and nice implementations, but not exactly what I thought.

    In Unknown Armies, I thought that you took sentences and tied them together to create an adventure. I was so disappointed when I discovered that all the sentences in the beginning of each chapter was explained later on in the chapters, that I sold the book. I later on did some reconsidering and came to a conclusion that it's still a great game, so I bought a new copy. I was disappointed with how In A Wicked Age handled it's oracles too, although we probably played the game wrong.

    My Life With Master - I thought that we were going to play imps in Dungeon Keeper that tried to suck up to the keeper. We weren't - we were in the end going to kill the keeper. Cool theme though, but I can honestly say that I didn't understand the game mechanics ... at all. I understood the purpose but not how it was designed. Guess it's a child of it's time, like many of the early Forge games.

    \n So there's Physical fallout, right? You don't get that just because someone has escalated to Physical action. You get Physical fallout when you "take the blow" in response to a Physical "raise".

    So you can totally have escalated to Guns, but when you "take the blow" of someone calling you a child-murdering bastard, you don't take d10 fallout, you take d4 fallout, immediately add that to the fallout pool, but don't roll it until the end.

    Fallout is not connected to the last Escalation that took place - > it's instead connected to the "raise" itself.

    \n No, David, you probably had it right in the first place. The missing piece is that you don't take Fallout dice when you Give. So if someone says they shoot you dead, you always have the chance to say, "No, wait, stop! I give." In this case you don't take any Fallout dice from the gun, just from any Blows you Took earlier in the conflict. Each time you take the Blow, you take Fallout dice relevant to the type of Raise that was made: if someone punches you, it's d8s, if they say something at you, it's d4s, etc.
