\n Hello all,
\n
\nI am Mike, one of the hosts of the Ninja vs Pirates podcast. You may remember us from our interview with Vincent Baker about his game Dogs In the Vineyard that has been posted about here on story-games forums. Season 2 is over. We are taking a short break before we begin releasing the interviews from season 3. I'd like to thank anyone that has been listening so far and let you know that if you don't see any podcasts over the next month it is only because Season 3 will start November 1st. We haven't disappeared, just taking a hiatus. Today we released a bonus episode of our second interview with Minimus's Ken Burnside (from our first episode in Season 2). We hadn't an opportunity to play his game before interviewing him in that episode and agree to do a follow-up after we got to play his game. There is some other information as well: this weekend, Mitch and I will be in Schenectedy, NY for Council of 5 Nations gaming convention. Mitch will be there all weekend, I will only be there saturday. But, we'll be playing games, Mitch will be running one or two and having fun.
\n
\nThanks again for listening. Check out the bonus episode with Ken Burnside.

    \n Hi again from the Ninja vs Pirates Podcast, an infrequent poster here at Story Games. We had another podcast released today which might interest those who are fans of DnD in some way. Castle Ravenloft with Mike Mearls We discuss with Mike (also a host on the DnD Insider podcast) the ins and outs of the latest released from Wizards, Castle Ravenloft (it's a cooperative board game).

    We also recently discussed a game from Robert Bohl called Misspent Youth. It's about teens fighting against the authority.

    Hopefully these interviews with interest some here. We've interviewed more story game posters, and story game designers this season than previous ones. It's weird how that worked out.

    Anyway, check out the interviews if anyone is interested. They each have some interesting things about some very cool games.

    Mike
    Ninja vs Pirates

    Edited to add: if anyone has any suggestions for future interviews, feel free to email us, or send me a message here.
