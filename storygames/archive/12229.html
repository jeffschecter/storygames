    \n Nathan, that looks gorgeous! Of course I must also pimp my own AW mashup: apocalyse D&D (http://story-games.com/praxis/comments.php?DiscussionID=152&page=1#Item_79). Apocalypse D&D is a loving mashup of AW and AD&D. The goal is to deliver old-school feeling dungeon play using AW-style game design to streamling game-mastering. Making the game master's job easier is, IMHO, the best way to improve the OD&D experience. AW does it very well.

    In Apoc D&D, each of the six primary stats is associated with a move:

    STR: go aggro
    INT: spout lore
    DEX: make your move
    WIS: discren realities
    CON: defy danger
    CHA: have a parlay

    These moves replace D&D-style combat with AW style resolution. Pretty much everything else comes straight out of AD&D.

    \n Several years back, thanks to an SG thread, I think, Justin Jacobsen and I teamed up to collaborate on a game called Fingers on the Firmament, a space fantasy game without space ships, where people travel through space by reaching out, grabbing the stars between their fingers, and pulling themselves through the void. The game was meant to be about scarcity, but primarily about a scarcity of other people and human contact, since space is really, really big and it's insanely unlikely that you'll ever encounter another living soul, even if there are millions of people out floating around the universe. So it's a game about loneliness and fear of the dark and what it means to be a community and the like, all amidst the beautiful backdrop of Hubble photographs and the lost temples and relics of ancient human communities in space, left empty for the past hundreds or thousands of years.

    Elizabeth shot this photo collage of the premise a while back:



    The opening of the game goes something like this:\n
    What follows is an account of what happened to Li Shen.

    It was the Heavenly Wolf, the Dog Star, Sirius, who has been the doom of many. Li Shen was looking out his bedroom window on the evening of December 20th, 1991. Then he saw it, rising dimly through the glare and smog of the Beijing skyline, barely visible above the horizon, naked of its usual companions: Mirzam, Wezen, Adhara, Aludra.

    He reached out and his fingertips brushed the star, candle-warm and crystalline. It was an impossible, beautiful gesture. The smudge of Sirius came to rest in a small crack between his cupped fingers. Curiously, the star remained suspended in the firmament like a petal in a pool of water. He tried to gently tug it free, but the star held fast. Shen, however, did not.

    And that is how it all began.
    \nIt's now going to be a AW hack, when I can get around to making it happen, because it's just so *perfect* for what the game wants to do.

    For example, the Psychic Maelstrom becomes the Darkness Between The Stars, the nameless silence which comes to ask you questions When You Are Lost and/or When You Are Alone. I think there's also going to be a fixed number of characters in the game, like 8-12. So you have to spend an advance to discover a new NPC, or otherwise have to rescue one in your travels and hope they're not insane for being alone with the Darkness for a decade or more.

    \n I was tinkering with some ideas for a Mutant playbook which never went anywhere (not having gotten to play the game in several weeks may be a factor), but there's a few snippets still stuck in my head that I'll throw out here.

    Choose one:
    cool+2 hard+1 hot-2 sharp+1 weird+1
    cool+1 hard+2 hot-2 sharp+1 weird+1
    cool+1 hard+1 hot-2 sharp+2 weird+1
    cool+1 hard+1 hot-2 sharp+1 weird+2

    eyes: Too many eyes, too few eyes, strangely colored eyes, asymmetrical eyes

    There were a few of their hypothetical special moves on the ecretsay ogblay, reproduced here for your benefit!

    Push Back: Whenever you fail a roll, get +1 ongoing that ends when you roll a 7+. This stacks with itself, so you\u2019ve got +2 ongoing after two failed rolls in a row, +3 after three failed rolls, etc.

    Blood Rage: Whenever you take harm, roll+harm-just-taken. On a 10+, get +1 ongoing until you heal any harm. On a 7-9, get +1 forward. On a miss, take 1 harm or -1 ongoing until you heal any harm, your choice.

    (I might switch the Blood Rage bonuses to only for hard rolls, not sure yet.)

    Vindicated: Whenever another player rolls to interfere with you, fill an experience circle.

    A fun alternative I just thought of for Vindicated: change that to "both of you fill an experience circle".

    \n I'd started to write up a lengthy post for a thread on rpg.net (this one, specifically #4) before running out to work, and totally forgot to post it or even finish it, so here's what I'd gotten so far. Context: The OP had asked for Human-only Post-Apocalypse games, and a commenter had suggested AP without the Weird stat.\n
    I wouldn't suggest removing Weird as a stat completely. That de-powers or removes The Brainer, The Hocus, and The Savvyhead. Instead, shift the Hocus into Hot, make necessary choices about how each of the character powers work (see below), and let Weird be the "mechanical"/"insightful" stat minus the Psychic Maelstrom-vibe. Clever might work as a name. That way, one could roll Clever to Gain Insight to a Situation at Hand (Opening your brain), subject to the roll and the fuckery of the MC. The MC could still ask for random details, but say that they come in a dream, or it's your brain making startlingly odd connections between events past and present, and a miss indicates that you totally made the wrong connection.

    Brainer move fixes:
    Unnatural lust transfixion-> Brains are sexy too: when seducing, roll+clever not roll+hot.
    Casual brain receptivity-> Mathematical social systems: when reading, roll+clever, not roll+sharp. You must be able to see your victim.
    Preternatural at-will brain attunement-> Nimble mind: +1clever (clever+3).
    Deep brain scan-> Greasing conversation: when you have time and intimacy with someone - physical intimacy like holding them in your arms, or emotional intimacy like a painful conversation - you can read them more deeply than normal. Roll+clever. On a 10+, hold 3. On a 7-9, hold 1. While you're reading them, spend your hold to ask their player questions, 1 for 1:
    :: (same list)
    Treat a miss as though you've gained insight to the situation at hand and missed the roll.
    In-brain puppet strings-> Puppy eyes: when you have time and intimacy with someone - again, physical or emotional - you can leverage your bond with them to get them to do what you want. On a 10+ hold 3. On a 7-9, hold 1. At your behest, given a request, you can spend your hold 1 for 1:
    :: they take a definite step toward your request, regardless of their personal safety or wishes
    :: they take -1 forward
    If they fulfuill your request, that counts for all your remaining hold. On a miss, both your Hx with them and their Hx with you is -1, to no benefit.
    Direct-brain whisper projection-> I don't know what to do with this one.

    \n Running cyberpunk using AW is really easy. Rename Brainer (call him Hacker or something), rename Maelstrom (go for Matrix, or Vurt, or whatever), instead of Apocalypse put Nanotechnology, or Snow Crash-style social issues or whatever you like. Rule of thumb: anything that is big and radically changes the world and lives of ordinary people works fine. Now when you're ready with bits of setting included in the book, move on to the first session stuff, everything else works as it would in AW. Been there, done that.

    I'd love to see A Song of Ice and Fire on AW engine. It would require some serious hacking (especially when it comes to Maelstrom), but role of PCs, easy NPCs management, Fronts - all that would emulate Martin's saga really good.

    \n I would argue that you can find scarcity in a lot of different settings, the main question is a scarcity of WHAT. After all, in AW there's no scarcity of guns or bullets. I mean, I'm not sure how to handle "abundance" in AW rules, but most RPG settings don't deal in abundance. Is there a scarcity of heirs? A scarcity of land? A scarcity of honorable civil officials? A scarcity of human blood to drink? All scarcity really means, right, is that people are getting desperate, which means dramatic and norm-breaking action is necessary and that's the basis of most heroic RPGs. I'm hard pressed to think of a heroic (in the broadest possible sense) RPG in which the PCs are not liminal figures of some kind -- bandits, outlaws, wandering brigands, minor lords, vampires, street samurai, etc. -- charged with acting outside the normal scope of human action and morality to deal with problems. If you're playing a game like that, scarcity is almost definitely a thing.

    Zac: thanks!

    First of all, I haven't tried it yet - I'm waiting for that chapter about customization of AW, and for the final version of the book as well. Most of what follows are cosmetic changes, and nothing more than conservative extension of AW rules, I think.

    To begin with, there are some basic things: scarcity, brutality, betrayal, fuckery, working really good in AW and present in Martin's books, right? Scarcity is also ambition, or envy, or fear, so no need to change anything here, I think.

    But, when I started thinking about combining A Song of Ice and Fire with AW, my motivation stem from NPCs management. In other games, I used about 5, maximum 10 NPCs during one session. By "using", I mean: they were individuals, had their own names, showed up and did their normal NPC stuff. In AW, I used 25+. Maybe because of good advices on how to use them (body parts as motivations and so on), maybe because it's really easy to manage them via Fronts. And I was like, wow, it was almost like in Martin's books.

    Now, let's go step by step. Setting. Maelstrom aside (I'll tell more about it in a moment), what would be counterpart of Apocalypse? I'd go for death of this (Robert Baratheon) or that (that last Targaryen) king. Which one, depends on how much the group would like to stay kosher about Martin's saga.

    Next, characters. Someone (I think it was somewhere here on sg) complained that in AW he'd like to play kind of macho character, but the game does not provide it, because if he'll fail few rolls, he's in such a deep shit, no hero at all, etc.. Great! That's what we're looking for. Think about Tyrion, or Jaime, or Eddard, Catelyn, Robb, well - Starks in general.

    Now, of course there's issue with characterbooks. My initial idea was to create new set of characterbooks, maybe starting with Houses - so, we have Stark, Lannister, Greyjoy, Baratheon, Tyrell, Targaryen, Martell, maybe Tully and Arryn - and adding few others - say, Night's Watch and maesters, and maybe septons.

    Most of basic moves are the same: going aggro, seducing, reading situation and person, working under fire, helping, seizing by force. Don't forget to use special barter moves from blog.

    The only problem is with opening brain to the maelstrom. I see two options here:
    1. change setting a little bit, by adding more magic (it seems to me that Martin's saga starts with very low level of supernatural effects and goes for more and more in following volumes - ressurections, dragons, Others, and so on). Using maelstrom = using magic. Details (are fireballs allowed? and so son) would depend on group and first session.
    2. interpret opening brain to the maelstrom as opening brain to the dreams (think about Brienne dreams in Feast of Crows) and augury (Cersei memories, ibidem) and weird Old Gods things (Starks are good in that, especially Bran), and maybe they are the same, who knows?

    I prefer second one, because it leaves more space for MC interpretation and sounds more fun. And if I'd like to play game about magic, I'd take Sorcerer or something.

    Now, special moves. Those which add +1 to stats are easy, rename them. Number of special moves, especially from Hocus, Hardholder and Operator books are fine, and so are those like Chopper's Pack Alpha. Procedure here would be like that: take as many as you can from AW, is necessary add some custom moves, especially those like custom battle moves, rename everything, if necessary change descriptions, and divide the moves among new characterbooks. And, of course, Maester would be an Angel. Sounds not so exciting, huh? It requires some work, but it's simple.

    Next, MC. I think that, actually, almost all MC part would work as it does in AW: Fronts, threats, first session stuff, MC moves, custom moves. One has to rewrite the crap part and create new list of names.

    It seems to me that MC Agenda and principles would be the same, with respect to proper names of course.
    And to MC moves, one should add things like:
    - marry them
    - force someone to promise to act against another character
    - present them consequences of their choices (meaning: what the consequences are for common people, those who don't play the game of thrones)
    - activate their family downside
    and maybe one or two more.

    Finally, the most interesting thing: issue of scale.

    Here, first, +1 to Kenny. When you separate them, of frame scenes for particular characters, not group as a whole, and generally when they're not playing in the same team, it kinda resembles Martin (among others, obviously).

    Next, there are things like announcing off-screen badness. Normally, they will interact with nobles and knights and the like, sometimes with groups of other people. But they got grasp of Big Consequences via off-screen badness and presenting consequences. Short cut-scenes (kind of Burning Empires-style Colour Scenes) would work fine.

    Beside that, moves are quite general: 'abstract' things like loyality, or leadership, or honour, are what could be seized by force, plotting often is done under fire, and so on.

    Makes sense? What do you think?

      \n Exactly. It's not that AW can't handle these sorts of things -- it's that you probably shouldn't have moves for them. If your setting is all about complicated intrigue, having a single Do Complicated Intrigue skill/move/whatever doesn't really make a lot of sense -- if it's your focus, you want it to be broken down into smaller pieces, to allow for greater complexity and development in the fiction. AW is set up with a pretty even level of granularity for all its proactive, in-session moves; some of the pre-session moves have a wider scope, but their purpose is to generate situation that will then get processed at that basic individual-character-does-stuff level. And all the in-session moves operate 'below' the scope at which the game generates meaning/theme/story/whatever, which makes them much more interesting building blocks for survival/scarcity stories than moves that directly address survival and scarcity.
