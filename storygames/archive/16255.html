\n Howdy, folks! Just wanted to drop a line here about a new game in public beta. You know how BANG! is kind of an emergent story game? BELLE OF THE BALL is kind of like that, too. Except instead of a showdown at high noon, it's more like the "Shindig" episode of Firefly. Hope you dig it. Here's the official announcement:




BELLE OF THE BALL card game is now in public beta. In BELLE, you invite and attract guests to a lavish party. Your goal is to create well-matched couples, win duels, please Belles, and create the most popular clique at the party.

    \nWell it is a free program mostly used for Magic the Gathering TCG. I have seen a few people play WOW on it too.

    To enter the cards manually is quite simple (in theory, I am computer illiterate, but *everyone* else says it is very simple) , based on text. You do not need any pictures.

    But tbh, I had to be walked through the installation step by step by a friend overseas, and it took me about 20X as long to fix any problems that it did my friend. I am hopeless with computers.

    There are some forums on MWS programming (mostly for M:tG), such as mtgsalvation.com has a few threads with updates and instructions.

    If you want I can look around for some webpages that give a walk-through for designing your own cards? I remember looking at a bunch of them last year when I was making a fan M:tG set.

    I will try to look for a good website or two (it might take a little while), though tbh, you might have to ask someone that is good with computers to help.

    There are some other programs too (I have zero experience with, but have heard they are also v good) Cockatrice; and Octagon(sp?) these are both very popular. I think they are also free (MWS is free, do not give them money it does not give you extra features, they stopped supporting it a long time ago).
