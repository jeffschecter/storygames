    \n Years from now, looking back on 2010, we'll consider it the beginning of the end of printed books being the chief source of income for most indie (and, eventually, mainstream) roleplaying publishers. Texts will be increasingly distributed for free electronically, with sold products being supplementary material, boxed set-style play components, prestige-format special edition printed versions, and other things in the webcomics / free apps model. Some folks selling higher numbers and with more firmly established methods (Luke, Vincent, etc.) will stay in printed books for significantly longer than the 100-200 copies-a-year folks.

    John Harper will win Best Free Game in the 2009 Indie Game Awards for the alpha/beta draft of Danger Patrol, sparking a huge discussion about games being "finished" and a return of the are-ashcans-games? debate. Or maybe people won't care. I'll believe it when I see it. Then again, who cares about Best Free Game?

    There will be more solo games around next year, following in the footsteps of How to Host a Dungeon and The Smoke Dream.

    Indie RPG designers will further embrace their inner desire to be board game / card game / video game designers.

    The next "GenCon darling" from the indie scene will not be a commercial product.

    \n Many more free "gamelike objects" will be created in the tradition of Ghost/Echo.
