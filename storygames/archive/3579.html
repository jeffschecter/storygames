\n In the spirit of Stat up characters in the new system, I'm hoping that people will give my situation-generator a whirl, and see whether it covers the types of things they'd like to push as GMs. Now the keys aren't going to define what can happen (what fun would it be to play in a game where the future was already defined?) so trying to find the one combination that MUST BE a given story and can be nothing else ... that isn't going to work. Best bet, for my money, is just to find the keys that would let the GM cash in most readily if he happened to GM a session where the story turned out that way.
\n
\nA full story (like a movie or something) will probably consist of several distinct situations (and would play as several ~1-hour sessions of Misery Bubblegum). Each situation is created by combining a Tension card and a Stakes card (as inspired by Cross-Product your way to situation).
\n
\nTENSION (GM draws a card when he introduces one of the listed story elements ... the GM's Countdown Clock is turn back a notch each time a player wins any stakes listed in the cards "Buy-In")
\n
\n
Rivalry
\n- An advantage an NPC or group has that a PC cannot attain
\n- A prize only one group may attain
\n- A vulnerability of an NPC or group
\nBUY-IN: Stakes of public victory
\n
\nObstacles
\n- The perfect opportunity thwarted at the last moment
\n- Time pressure
\n- The outcome of the last "solution" is the next problem
\nBUY-IN: Stakes of ???
\n
\nPursuit
\n- Danger in front, pursuers close behind
\n- A close call
\n- A chance to cover a lot of distance quickly
\nBUY-IN: Stakes of delaying or distracting pursuers
\n
\nOppression
\n- Rules get in the way of human needs
\n- What's important is obedience, whatever the orders
\n- Wildly disproportionate punishment
\nBUY-IN: Stakes of hiding something from the authorities
\n
\nMystery
\n- The truth obviously concealed
\n- A new truth that seemingly contradicts an old one
\n- One of these things just doesn't belong
\nBUY-IN: Stakes of finding evidence to support your theory
\n
\nInfighting
\n- Something or someone two groups are vying over
\n- Something two groups have in common
\n- A vital message not received
\nBUY-IN: Stakes of proving where you belong
\n
\nDisaster
\n- An unfocussed, general danger
\n- A temporary safe haven
\n- A dangerous gauntlet to be traversed
\nBUY-IN: Stakes of ???
\n
\n
\nAlso, if anyone has ideas for what story elements would encourage: Suspense, Entanglement (e.g. "You mistakenly get the McGuffin, and are pulled into the story against your will") or The Natural Order Disturbed (e.g. Mind-switch episodes) that would be great. Otherwise I'll just keep plugging away at them.
\n
\nSTAKES (GM draws a card when an NPC does one of the listed things)
\n
\n
Romance
\n- Conceals what they think of a PC
\n- Confides in a PC about their romantic feelings
\n- Acts selflessly
\n
\nRecognition
\n- Ignores a PC
\n- Praises someone
\n- Mistakes a PCs abilities or intentions
\n
\nAuthority
\n- Disregards a PCs wishes
\n- Issues commands
\n- Agonizes over their conflicting loyalties
\n
\nSafety
\n- Panics (only once per session per NPC)
\n- Threatens a PC
\n- Looks to a PC for rescue
\n
\nPrinciples
\n- Does the practical thing
\n- Hates him- or her-self for cowardice
\n- Explains how doing the right thing isn't possible
\n
\nFriendship
\n- Confides a secret to a PC
\n- Feels betrayed by a PC
\n- Isolates him- or her-self
\n
\nConfidence
\n- Relies on a PC
\n- Scorns help to act alone
\n- Protects a PC
\n
\n
\nHere's my best try at Star Wars, to prime the pump:
\n
\nObstacle + Safety (opening credits -> Jawas sell droids)
\nMystery + Confidence (Luke whining -> Mos Eisley)
\nPursuit + Principles (Canteena -> arrival at rebel base)
\nDisaster + Confidence (Attack on Death Star -> closing credits)
\n
\nI think that a GM who was running those sections with those Keys would rake in some serious card-draws.

    Otherwise I'm not sure what your Star Wars examples are showing us. Most of the things you mentioned are based on what I'd call "PC actions." (It's Luke's confidence that is important in the Death Star, not Biggs' or Grand Moff Tarkin's.)

    \n Kynn: Because the players are not under the GMs control, whereas the NPCs are.
    \n
    \nI can't simply decide to set up a situation where the PC will shine, any more than I can decide to create a beloved film. I can decide to make a film with lots of special effects, big explosions, sassy comic relief and a summer release date. Maybe it'll be beloved, maybe it won't, but you don't have to wonder "How do I make such a film?"
    \n
    \nMy goal here is to provide the GM with a solid formula that she can follow unilaterally. The formula may not produce the results desired, but it won't leave the GM wondering "Uh ... okay ... how do I do what I want to do?"
