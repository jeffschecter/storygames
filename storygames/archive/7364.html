Here is the list of games I either purchased (or got gratis for review purposes) at Gen Con 2008:

Annalise: stories of hunger, pain, and redemption, by Nathan D. Paoletta.
Bullseye v1.0 (ashcan), by Justin D. Jacobson.
Don't Lose Your Mind, by Benjamin Baugh and Fred Hicks.
Messiah (ashcan), by Chris Perrin.
Misspent Youth (ashcan), by Robert Bohl.
Poison'd: a pirate rpg, by D. Vincent Baker.
The Shab-Al-Hiri Roach, by Jason Morningstar.
Shock: Social Science Fiction v1.1, by Joshua A.C. Newman.
Shooting the Moon: all's fair in love and war, by Emily Care Boss.
Steal Away Jordan: Stories from American's Peculiar Institution, by Julia Bond Ellingboe.
Sons of Liberty, by Josh Roby.
Things We Think About Games, by Will Hindmarch, Jeff Tidball, et al.

Over the next few weeks/months, between work on S7S and a new game Fred Hicks and I came up with on the drive to Indy (I even got to playtest a bit of it at the con!), I'll be doing Underkoffler's Overviews on the above list of awesomeness.

(I'm thinking about doing these alphabetical by title... but also reshuffling so that the three ashcans get looked at first.)
