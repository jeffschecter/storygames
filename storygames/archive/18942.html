\n At last, it's here! Yes, Sci-Fi Beta Kappa, the game about delinquent aliens that a gamer buddy and I designed and which we've been demoing at various UK cons over the last year and a bit (sadly, we're too poor atm to do any non-UK ones ), finally hits the shelves on November 1st, and will be available to purchase at Indiecon (a major UK con in early November), price \xa39.

Folks not based in the UK can purchase it from DriveTHRU RPG, for whatever the equivalent of \xa39 is in local currency, from November 1st this year. Follow this link to see the cover of the Sci-Fi Beta Kappa book, so you'll know to look out for it .

    Will it still be available at Indiecon?
    \nYes, we're selling them on the Design Collective stand for \xa39, but if you want one, buy it as soon as you can because we could only afford to make the minimum print run of 25 copies in time for Indiecon, although we'll be re-printing throughout the year.\n
    My youngest brother played at the Concrete Cow. It was the session about an easter parade but the few Easter facts they knew were just enough for them to misinterpret everything.
    \nAh yes, I remember that game. IRC, Vuff (shapeshifter/multiple forms) became loads of 'ickle bunny rabbits, and when the aliens were stopped by the cops at one point and shot at when they ignored their command to pull over, one of the PCs used either power manipulation or teleporting to make the cop's bullets ricochet back and burst the police car's tyres. \n
    Is that the big buy in point? That you will willingly misinterpret the human culture things?
    \nIt's one of them. Forex, in an Xmas party scenario I ran at Indiecon, they got the idea about there being a big bird, but got it a big wrong and stole the football team's mascot, which just happened to be the last remaining bald eagle on Terra, and, discovering that Xmas was a sort of religious event and that such events were usually accompanied by sacrifices, sacrificed the bald eagle in the middle of Miguel Benitez Square on Xmas Day. In fact it was Dahod (doppelganger) who did the actual sacrificing, while making people believe he was the Dean. So yeah, pretty much. There are other selling points, which I'll go into here if anybody's interested, but I'm a wee bit reluctant to do so otherwise, not because I'm being coy but because I've been banging on about it for months now and I don't want to risk overkill.

    However, here's a ink to the current RPGGeek page (I'll put up a new one when 1e is officially out), where I've posted all the AP reports, some of which give you an insight into various aspects of the game and the overall design process.

    is this any different to the copy I bought earlier in the year??
    Brette:)
    \nYes and no. The original 1e was going to include some absolutely stunning artwork by someone we know on the roleplaying scene (i.e. one of the few that isn't either a programmer or a teacher), but she'd been having some family stuff to deal with and got further and further behind with the work, and eventually Dan and I took the decision (reluctantly, because we really like her work and it would have made a big difference to the book) to make the demo version 1e.

    However, there are a number of crucial differences. First, I've tidied up the back cover, which now has neater speech bubbles, and generally looks a lot more professional. Secondly, I've changed the typefaces, which are now UltraCondensedSansSerif and Optima, and I've centred the headers, hoovered up several typos and added an acknowledgements page. We were going to charge \xa311-12 for 1e, but since we don't have the artwork the insides are effectively b/w (although we've kept the clipart just to break the text up a bit) so we're charging \xa39 for it, or \xa37 to playtesters.

    So, if you have a copy of the ashcan version, should you buy a copy of 1e? Obviously I'm slightly biased, but my objective response is: as the rules are exactly the same, if you're not too fussed about the design, you needn't buy 1e. OTOH, as a result of all the tidying up I've done, overall it's much cleaner and slicker, so on that basis you might consider investing in a copy.
