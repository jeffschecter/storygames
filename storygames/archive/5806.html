    Posted By: C.W.RichesonThat's true, Ryan. What useful things have been produced for you?
    \n"Have been produced?" Not much, yet, in terms of tangible effects. For me, the biggest thing produced is a sense that there is room for my dissenting opinion here and that others share it, as well as the likely reaction I'll get when I actually start sharing that opinion.

    But, too many of us have spent too much time arguing (myself very included) and not enough time doing. That I'll agree with. That's why I phrased my response as "being produced" -- things are in the works. I just have to respect that the burden of articulation and discourse is on the dissenter here, so for my criticism of a work I have to do the following:
    * Find out where my copy of said work is (I'm kind of a local indie library for folks)
    * Go through it again to refresh myself
    * Go through any emails I may have had with the creator about it
    * Outline what I want to write
    * Write
    * Send it to some friends for peer-review & editing
    * Revise
    * Publish publicly

    If I do less, then I am irresponsible. Maybe this is why such movements always falter -- such public criticism is (a) long work, (b) hard work, and (c) thankless work. It takes a stubborn person who is willing to look like an asshole to everyone to get the job done.
