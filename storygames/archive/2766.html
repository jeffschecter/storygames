Several people have shown interest in seeing some of the boffer weapons that I have and will be bringing to Camp Nerdly with me.
Although my "supplier" is not the only one that makes these I have had excellent service from them. Most of the swords you see here I bought kits from Edhellen Armory and put them together myself which saved me about half the retail price. I had a little fiddling to do and took them apart once or twice but I think i have the process down now. I also had a sword I bought first as a template.
You can see that these come in a variety of lengths, both in blade and hilt/handle. I bought some varied parts from Edhellen to develope my own weapons. One I am particular fond of is this "Katana." Of course none of these weapons can be up to specs but the longer handle makes simulating kenjutsu and kendo techniques much easier.\xa0\xa0\xa0
\xa0
The katana is not my weapon of choice, however. I started the quest for some decent boffer weapons (without me having to break out the duct-tape) by looking for a no-dachi or great sword. I bought this one at Origins last year and look to make others like it by the years end.

    Alright, man, it's on. I might even learn how to make one out of that crap PVC that seems to be all the rage so I can have one that's balanced well. We used to make them of rattan with pipe insulation. When you were hit in the leg, you didn't usually hop around on the other foot \xe2\u20ac\u201d you usually took it and went down because you had to recover for a few minutes before fighting again. I actually got knocked out once (though I was back before I hit the ground \xe2\u20ac\u201d I guess that's "stunned"?).

    \n

    But, then I was 17 and indestructible.

    \n

    It turns out that that indestructibility wears off.

    \n

    So I dig the lighter weapons as long as they're balanced, aren't too flexy, and you've got to commit to a blow.
