    \n Assuming that all zombies are slow-moving, dull-witted creatures, they will naturally spread outward from their originator in the direction of most-food. My suggestion therefore is to map out the extent of zombification, and overlay a general population map. Since zombies will spread more in the direction of highest population where there are more brains to eat, start by placing your ideal point at the very centre of the cloud. Now consider the following: look at the population north of that spot and the population south. Which is higher, and by how much? Move your central pin towards the higher population by a distance equal to the percentage difference in populations, multiplied by the total distance to the edge of the plague area. Now, do the same with east and west. If you want to be really careful, repeat the process with the two diagonals and take an average of the results.

    Alas this method is pretty impractical too far into the zombie epidemic. For one thing, it only gives a general area to search in and won't tell you who or what you're looking for, but I'd suggest seeking the most rotten-smelling zombie in the poorest condition as they drop apart pretty quickly. Chances are if anyone's fighting back, patient zero will be totally dead by now. This method, however, is almost completely ineffective once the range of the outbreak extends beyond the major population area.

    -Ash

    \n I suspect that the zombie plague will propagate more or less at the same speed in all directions unless there are any major barriers such as very fast rivers or gorges, so once you know extent of the spread, which can be achieved by people twittering their post codes, or by helicopter flyover or by tracking fires by satellite image, then you can extrapolate back to the point of origin by finding the center of gravity of the shape of the outbreak.

    his can be done, as any junior school student will tell you, by tracing it on cardboard and then hanging in from a piece of string. Just extend the line across the shape, do it from another point and the intersection is the point of outbreak.

    In a less data rich society this might be achieved by outriders tracing the edge of the outbreak and taking into account the rate of spread and interpolating to get an estimate for a particular point in time.

    \n Each zombie contains viral cells that impede and alter the functions of the brain. You'll need to take samples from the brains of zombies in a wide range of geographical areas. The virus mutates quickly, like the common cold, and once the genomes of the samples are completely mapped, you should be able to trace the origins of the plague to within a relatively small region. Depending on the comprehensiveness of your survey, that is. A second or even third, more concentrated sample may be necessary to produce results with the desired degree of accuracy.

    Of course, in order to pull this off at all, you'll need live subjects (or at least their heads). Destroying the brain pan as one might generally do with these sorts of ghouls will splatter your valuable sample cells all over the place.
