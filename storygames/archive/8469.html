    \n Oh...I meant to bring this up last night.
    \n
    \nI was thinking about it...not sure, but maybe the crossover effect isn't the best option.
    \n
    \nConsider: winning flashbacks allows you to control characterization, both your own (by not giving you opponent the chance to redefine it) and your opponents. Winning the duel allows you to damage your opponents ability to fight and control who wins and who dies.
    \n
    \nBy allowing flashbacks to cross over like this (i.e. after you're done redefining your opponent now you can use flashbacks to cause damage) you may be giving a privilege of priority to flashbacks that disrupts the balance between the two sides.
    \n
    \nPerhaps it would be best to keep the two effects entirely seperate. When you win a flashback against someone who has been completely redefined, instead of it crossing over into damaging dice it instead could allow you to redeem one of your own traits.
    \n
    \nI think I am ---> but really I am ---> and now I'm striving to become

    \n Thinking about this some more, I like the idea of a player who's lost all his Qualities having to write another one.

    It's like: OK, my character is so messed up... but he can still hang on to THIS shred of dignity...

    ...and then we see if he does.

    If you don't like having only one Quality available to be damaged for the victor, then have the player write a new Quality whenever he's down to one--that way characters always have at least two Qualities that can get screwed up.
