    \n I played around with it for awhile. Here is a short list of pros and cons based on my limited experiences:

    Pros:

    Several apps rolled into one.
    PDF support...you can share rulebooks temporarily without allowing players to download the file.
    You can write on the pdfs.
    Integrated web browser that might allow web apps like flash dice rollers and card decks.

    Cons:

    Dice roller integration a little buggy
    Windows only (which is becoming less an issue due to emulator software)
    A little sluggish
    Still missing a lot of functionality

    I'm going to mess around with it a little more and give a more detailed report later.

    \n The web-browser feature is definitely a pro. We (I connected with Brennan for a while) tried to check out a flash die-roller, but that didn't work out very well. However, it did allow me to quickly bring him to a page where he was able to look at something I wanted him to see, and vice versa.

    The ability to write on the pdfs (or anything else for that matter) still needs some work, as font size and style is not editable. Still, allowing for .pdfs would allow multiple copies of a character sheet to be kept, and if they fix it so that the text size, at least, is editable, it will work well for that.


    Voice chat and video worked well enough with just us two; I don't have a webcamera, but I was able to see him clearly, and both video and audio were smooth for the most part, with occasional skips and freezes. There is a noticeable delay of about 1-2 seconds on the audio, but that may have as much to do with our respective connection speeds as anything else.

    I think Beta might be a tiny bit generous; It works as advertised, but there are a lot of little ease of use features it needs to make it so that it's not more frustrating than to use than useful. Overall, with a little more polish and complete implementation of the parts that seem incomplete, this will be a very good application; Possibly the best so far for online gaming.

    \n So, Wolfe and I tried it out for a little while. I think it has a lot of potential, but it's still definately a beta. Unfortunately, it crashed on me. I'm a big fan of stability in my VGTs.

    The pdf support is limited. You can't use forms. It appears that it loads pdfs as images...so you can't select text.

    The web support is also limited. You go to the same page, but you don't see what the other players see...for instance, you couldn't see someone else's result in an online die roller. There is an option to view someone else's screen, but it didn't work at all.

    I think I'm going to try out Ryan's online gaming application cocktail.
