\n So I'm working on my game which is called ''Fallen''. I haven't posted anything about it here yet, I'm a bit unsure if it's ok to just post general info about a game or what.
Anyway, my ''problem'' is this. I have some really nice art for the game, but it's from different artists and their styles and mediums don't match. Will this look really bad in the book or will noone really care as long as the art is good? The differences are a bit radical (traditional painting vs photorealistic digital) and I'm a bit unsure on the final product.
So, do you know of any games that have art like this, do you think this can pose a problem and if so, what can be done?

-George

    \n George, generally speaking, your art does not really need to match as long as no two pieces appear on the same spread. That is, if the reader is never going to be looking at both pieces at the same time, it doesn't matter if they're different. Those differences will not be apparent.

    The thing that needs to match is your art and the layout. Especially with disparate art, you will need to create a layout design that "matches" all of them. The layout will be on every spread, including those with art, so the layout and the art will be seen together and compared by the reader.
