\n Hi All,
\n
\nYou may remember some weeks ago Valamir started a thread regarding my game, Summerland, where he vented some frustration at assumptions made in game texts. The crux of the issue if I got it right was that I had made some unconscious assumptions about the experience of those readig the books. it wasn't that there was anything wrong with the rules, just that I hadn't been quite explicit enough so that a newbie could pick up exactly what I had intended to happen at the table, especially regarding setting difficulties for actions and conflicts. Valamir was making a general comment about a lot of games using mine as an example, and to be fair a lot of people didn't agree and this has never come up in other reviews, hence the rather sprawling thread that resulted.
\n
\nWell, I'm always looking to improve my games. Since then I have contacted Valamir off list and had a brief discussion about this issue, and as a result penned an essay that I hoped explained the process of setting difficulties in greater detail. Valamir kindly gave a read through and made some very useful suggestions, and we ended up with a piece that I think we were both happy with. So, I've stuck it up on my Lulu store as a free download. Please note this doesn't change any of the rules in the game, it just re-emphasises the rules and clears-up any assumptions for the reader. If you're happy with the game as is then fine, but if you want a little more detail, please feel free to download the essay.
\n
\nhttp://www.lulu.com/content/e-book/setting-difficulties-in-summerland/6879474
\n
\nThanks again to Valamir for all his help.
\n
\nI've also got my website up and running, www.fireruby.co.uk, some come on over and check it out.

\nCheers,
