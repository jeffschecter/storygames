\n Alright, folks. I need help with my homework.

I'm training to become an Orton-Gillingham Tutor, which is someone who tutors children with dyslexia. It's all multi-sensory teaching, so I get to do lots of hands on arts and crafts projects and fun stuff, as prep work.

My current assignment is that I need to find an interesting picture. It needs to be a picture that me and a grade 6 boy can dialog over for about a minute, after which I will ask him to write down the descriptive story we just discussed. The picture and dialog together act as a cue for a Composition exercise.

So... that's it. It's a classroom setting, so no gore, nipples, sexualized situations, gritty violence, etc. Find me an interesting picture that would be inspiring to a grade 6 boy. GO!



If no one comes up with anything better, I'm going to find a picture of spacemen firing lasers at each other and post that.

    \n RP,

    I really like the two that Daniel showed me, as well as the Star Wars band one.
    They're good because you can talk a little about them, or a lot about them. With the treehouse one, if there's a talkative kid, we can talk about the robots, how they're watering flowers, the giant chicken that the man rode in, why the man came to the house, the shape of the house, the skyline bridges in the background.... Or if the kid is quiet or struggling with verbal expression, we can just talk about the fact that it's a twisty treehouse in the woods.

    Almost all of them are really awesome.

    The second one that Alex D linked is great, but possibly too sexualized to use with a child. If I knew how to photoshop, I'd make the dress less transparent/more solidly white, and it would be perfect.

    In the end, I'm preparing a few Composition Cue pictures: The Star Wars rock band, the Twisty Treehouse, and a Redwall picture I found.
    Thanks all! Feel free to keep posting pictures, I love what I'm seeing.
