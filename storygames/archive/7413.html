\nHow To Host A Dungeon is so fun.

I tried it out last night and it's a blast. There's a certain level of abstraction and complexity that would benefit, I think, from visual description (video, maybe), but wherever I wasn't sure what to do I did the most awesome thing and it worked out pretty good. In many cases where I thought I'd found some stupid loophole, it turned out it had been planned for and I'd misread the instructions.

So I ended up with a small dungeon, but one that is full of danger and excitement! Magma spritis have driven a band of Ettin out of their lair and into the hoary old tombs of the long-lost dwarves! I don't even know what an Ettin is but they are bad-ass! There's a crypt full of evil ghosts beneath the castle! A boiling underground river! Plague caverns beneath Mt. Kraken! Making this was an exercise in joy.

    \n I'm about...I'd guess 1/4 - 1/3 or so of the way through the game.
    \n
    \nIn the Primordial age I discovered that deep underground there's a river with a big ass waterfall. A cave through which the waterfall passes containing some new form of plague (likely evolved over the ages in the damp of the waterfall cave). A series of natural caverns containing vast riches in gems and a mysterious cave of Fate.
    \n
    \nThen the Dwarves came sinking a mining shaft down to a vein of gold and following that vein nigh unto exhaustion. The vein crossed and recrossed the underground river so there are several cool entry points along the route. Unfortuneately for the Dwarves the gold vein passed right through the water fall plague cave and many dwarves died. A great tomb was raised beneath The Hall of Angmar. The dwarves built the city of lords at the bottom of their original shaft arranging it so the underground river flowed through its center and beneath this they built the Hall of Records containing all known dwarven knowledge. But they dug yet deeper still until their minings crossed a major fault line triggering a cataclysmic earthquake shattering their realm. 11 generations of clan Darfur lived and died in those halls and then passed away forever.
    \n
    \nGenerations later Hobgoblin miners moved into the old dwarf barracks near the surface repairing the earthquake damage and slowly expanding looking for left over ore. They discovered the Helm of Ang in the shattered ruins of the Hall of Angmar and desecrated the tomb to the plague victims. Meanwhile a two headed giant neanderthal...known as an Ettin...moved into the hall of records and foraged through the City Lords and collected much plunder. He discovered the Treasure Room which had been shattered open by the earthquake and defeated the Mimic who resided there. Farther away a clan of Kobolds occupied the old mining tunnels, including the waterfall room where the plague first struck...awakening the ghosts of dead dwarves who in their rage decimated the kobolds who still have yet to recover. Now a tribe of orcs have moved into the natural caverns which even the dwarves hadn't discovered, and fueled by the wealth of gems to be found there look poised to overrun the dungeon...if the prowling ankhegs don't keep them in check.
    \n
    \nMeanwhile on the surface a minor noble has erected Caer Carradock and a thriving agricultural community has sprung up on the banks of the cleft...a vast crevasse deeper than any canyon, opened by the earthquake and leading down into the shattered underworld below...
    \n
    \nThat was about 1 turn in the Primoridal Age, 11 turns in the Age of Civilization, and 5 turns in the Age of Monsters.

    \n Yes, I was playing Dwarf Fortress at the time I wrote How to Host a Dungeon, though the basic idea goes back a bit further than that.

    Brian, I use a HTHAD dungeon as the basis of my Basic D&D campaign. It works pretty well. Any D&D inspired game should work OK for it. Tunnels & Trolls and Labyrinth Lord come to mind. I want to try it for 4th edition D&D. Since 4th breaks things up into encounters rather than rooms, it might actually be easier to map.

    For games that aren't D&D inspired, I recommend chaning the names of the monsters and civilizations to something that matches your game world. I'd love to see a sci-fi adaptation.
