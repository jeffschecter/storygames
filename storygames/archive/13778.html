    Stargates
    Ultramen (transhuman star-faring group from Alastair Reynold's Revelation Space books)
    Melding Plague (Chasm City again)

    Laserswords (yes, they are beyond our capability but don't violate the known laws of physics)

    My objectives in coming up with Academic technologies for Human Contact was that they be plausible and based on extrapolations of their material engineering. My favorites are:

    \n
      \n
    • A parasail that undulates to generate thrust, sort of like a jellyfish, but pulsing front-to-back rather than top-down. It makes no sound and allows the pilot to change from prone to supine position in flight because it's attached at their center of gravity. Flying takes a running start or a jump off a cliff.
      • \n
    • A space suit that holds pressure mechanically, rather than with air pressure, allowing sweat to evaporate into space to keep the wearer cool (which, oddly, is one of the biggest problems in space). It's practically being outside in space. It's based on the Bio Suit.
      • \n
    • Omnifood. One 10cm x 10cm x 2cm bar gives you half the day's nutrition. It comes in Dry Crumbly Salty Yeast Flavor.
      • \n
    \n

    Things from other books I think are neat:

    \n
      \n
    • E-suits, from Chindi. It's a little harness you wear that makes a force field a millimeter from your skin or clothes. You explore space in a UNIVERSITY OF MICHIGAN t-shirt.
      • \n
    • The inflatable habitats in Deepness in the Sky. Once a starship (subluminal) arrives at a destination where it can trade with a civilization, everyone on board wakes up. There's not enough living space for everyone until they inflate their space stations. I don't know if it's deliberately based on Bigelow Aerospace's thing.
      • \n
    • AUM from the Illuminatus! trilogy. It's a notional drug that makes you embrace new ideas.
      • \n
    • Key 23 from The Invisibles. Another drug that makes you unable to distinguish words from their meanings. In a world where meaning forms the substrate of the universe, that's pretty big magic.
      • \n
    • Rama, from Rendezvous with Rama. As far as I can tell, after reading the first and part of the second book, it's an ecosystem designed to coast between stars, absorbing both kinetic and electromagnetic energy whenever it gets near one. Automated systems use the energy for maintenance checks, then go back into hybernation. Presumably, it will arrive at its destination at some point and (re?)construct the passengers from whole cloth.
      • \n
    • The monoliths from 2001. They're "cosmic Swiss Army knives", capable of controlling nervous systems, communicating and traveling interdimensionally, broadcasting radio, manufacturing stars, and who knows what else, at the will of whatever entities created them.
      • \n
