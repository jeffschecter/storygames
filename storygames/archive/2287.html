  • Penn42
    January 2007
    Dundracon is my favorite of the three bay area shows and is going on it's 32nd(?) year, although working with the staff this year has been more challenging than in the past. It always hovers around 1,800 - 2,000 people. The dealers room is pretty ok, and there will be some Story Game representation in the play areas.

    Kublacon is the biggest of the three shows in the area, with almost 3,000 people attending. The dealers room is larger, and has much more manufacturer support than Dundrcon or Conquest. I would say the staff does a great job in each of their respective areas, but seemingly have no process to allow them too coordinate between themselves. This can lead to a lot of behind the scenes fire-drilling. I think they are better at coming off organized though.

    Both shows are seriously pushed for space. Dundrcon lost the RPGA because they were unhappy at the space they were given, so thy took their ball, and formed their own little show that weekend elsewhere.This allows for a lot more "other Role-Playing." Kubla has a very large RPGA presence, and they take up a lot of room.

    Neither of the three shows in the area will give you the national type attention a Gencon would...but Kubla sure wants to be there some day.
