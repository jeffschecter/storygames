    I totally hear you. In that case, say a month after the con ends, I'd contact the con staff regarding the possibility of starting a game track.

    It might come down to: "Hey, good idea! You're now in charge of the Katsucon gaming track"... but if such a thing happens, you'll be able to work some magic into getting some folks really interested in gaming. I've found that whole scene to be quite cross-compatible with gaming.

    Ex: at a local SF con, they've got a big Furry-fan subculture that meets there I guess (ick, but yeah). Anyway, games like RPGA events and the like get solid participation... but the Ironclaw, Jadeclaw, and two other indie anthro games that are run consistently get full, usually overfull, tables. Anime events seem to go the same way, lots of interested folks.

    I'd say that if you, say, had an anime or manga that you liked, and listed events (with whatever system, with a disclaimer that folks new to rpgs are welcome) that were remotely similar to those manga or anime -=- Or even list those events as-is "Blade of the Immortal: With PTA!" "BLEACH: With CAPES!" -=- I'm sure you would get full tables.

    Ever been to an anime convention? There's surprisingly not all that much to do.
    There's a dealer's room, to buy anime which you already probably have at suggested retail price-prices.
    There's also the rare dealers of import games, manga, anime, figures and goods, which are hotpoints. Again, though, look-and-buy.
    There's an anime viewing room, to watch anime that you've already probably seen if you're up-to-date with BitTorrent and the like.
    There's an art gallery of dubious quality.
    There's the cosplayers, and the cosplay contest, again to watch.
    There's the room of professional manga and voice actors who get interviewed. Hum.
    There's various events like Dance Dance Revolution contests, Karaoke contests... but again, a lot of waiting and watching, not much do-ing.

    RPGs would go over like hellfire in hell. HOT hellfire, even. Finally, something to fucking do when you're tired of Watching, Waiting and Buying.

    I think i'td be worth it to tap that comunity. If you're already into manga/anime.

    -Andy
