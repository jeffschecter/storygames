    \n WOTC's DDI pretty much prints out all powers in card format along with second wind. You can take the rest and put it on a "placemat" (skills, racial/class features). Give out tokens for action points (and conditions like bloodied) cf Fiery Dragon's products.

    There's a one page D&D cheat sheet that is essentially a couple of key tables from the DM Screen (which you might as well turn inside out and face the players).

    I agree that a game should "be what it is up front" to avoid misleading players learning it. Learn by playing is fine if everyone is at roughly same stage or more advanced willing to be coaches (which is very tricky, I've seen too much coaching that is really telling the new player what to do and depriving them of fun of choices in a tactical game). I think it's better for people to make mistakes and learn to correct them (oh, you mean because I have trained acrobatics jumping down 20' not such a big deal for me as for other PC, cool).

    A nice teaching segue 4e uses anyway is the passive/active skill use. Push some information at Players under Passive checks (have a cheat sheet with all PC's passive checks on it) but make clear "that's without specifically trying", this shows importance of skills that the "powercard" approach can gloss over (e.g., Insight tells you the townspeople are nervous and Perception causes you to notice that there are no children in sight).

    \n I've never seen a premade D&D sheet that was actually any good. So I just make my own, using a simple word document as a template, that I then tweak to fit the individual character. So far I've gotten characters up through low Paragon level without needing more than two sheets of paper, with all the relevant information on there for every power.

    (Scribd.com seems to have messed up the formatting on the second one slightly, as the tabs are off and one line of the first column spills over onto the second. It doesn't do this in the original Word document, but I'm too lazy to convert to PDf just to upload as an example.)
