\n In this episode, I talk with Jason Morningstar of Bully Pulpit Games about Grey Ranks. Grey Ranks is a game about the 1944 Warsaw Uprising against the Nazis in World War II (not to be confused with the Warsaw Ghetto Uprising). In the game, you play teenage kids (roughly the equivalent of the Boy Scouts) to fight (to ultimate futility) to defeat the Nazis. I get very verbally fumbly at the end, discussing the status issue. Hope you enjoy the show.

    \n No GM, Rachel! You frame mission scenes for each other and build personal scenes for your own character. Each chapter has a character-based "leader", who gets to outline the overall objective for that chapter and make some tactical decisions, but everyone is pretty much on even footing.

    I've played carry and it is excellent. It's very specifically simulating Vietnam war fiction, though, so you're coming at it from a different angle, not so concerned with the history as much as with the tropes and themes - that's my impression, anyway. I love the collaborative creation of burdens in that game, which was influential for Grey Ranks as well.

    \n Hey William,

    I've talked to a number of Poles, including a reporter for Gazeta Krakowska, who really hammered on the sociopolitical aspects of the game, but I've never talked to someone who was there. I'd like to! In Poland the Uprising is a very sensitive subject, so I've gotten some indignation as well as some friendly interest and even fascination that a foreigner could know and care about it to such a degree. No feedback from scholars or armchair historians so far. I had to make some decisions about how and why things happened, and these are certainly debatable - the game has a stridently pro Home Army stance, for example, which I suppose historians could view as unsubtle and precious. I'll defend that as valid, though, and it works well for the game as a side bonus!

    I got some samples from Guild of Blades of playing cards and they are very good. My general feeling is that nicer cards for The Roach would be fantastic, and would probably be part of a complete overhaul of the game sometime in the future. You'll probably see us use GoB's service for another project first, though.
