    UR
    Main Entry: ur-
    Pronunciation: 'ur
    1. (prefix) original or primitive: "ur-form"
    2. (prefix) urinary and/or urogenital

    In the 23rd Century, the last humans boarded the Baculum and set course for Alpha Centauri. They left behind a crumbling globe pockmarked with smoking craters. They also left behind race riots, AIDS, bigotry, starvation, pogs, genocide, pollution, traffic, and TRL. They considered themselves fortunate. And so, they slumbered in deep hypercriothermostasis for years, until their artificially intelligent AI starship located a planet with an ecosystem that was pretty much kind of exactly identical to that of Earth, and they named it Ethra. There was one guy who said they should call it Htrae, but they buried him up to his neck and stoned him to death. Since they couldn't find any stones, they had to use mangos. Mangoes. Fuck it, whatever, they chucked fruit at the son of a bitch until he was dead, it took fucking forever. They were so irritated with each other that they decided that from this point on, they'd need laws to govern their conduct, and to determine the nature of their punishments. So, one guy was like, shit, I got it, we'll use the Code of Hammurabi. And so they did. And they girded their loins with the hides of beasts, and they forsook their technology, which had wrought so much grief and despair and terror and grief, and they lived as simple primitive people from a simpler, more primal era, and they swam in the yellow oceans of Ethra and they dwelled as had their ancestors. They were the people of Ur-Ethra.

    Ur-Ethra is powered by the astonishing D3 system, a flexible and completely modular freeform point-based system that can handle any game situation in any conceiveable -- shit, that doesn't look right... conceivable? game universe that could ever be imagined. Fast, flexible, cinematic, fun, and awesome, the D3 system lets you get right into the action. Unlike other systems that keep you holding it in, Ur-Ethra's dynamic emotion-driven conflict-resolution lets you cut loose with a hot stream of storytelling action.

    Get into Ur-Ethra. Pass into something new.

    -- Rafael
