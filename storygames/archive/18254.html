    You mean something like Kudos?
    \nNo. Definitely not like that.

    I don't want a "minus 1" option. If you disagree with a point, you need to reply explaining how and why you disagree. Likewise if you disapprove of the content of the post, you should explain why. A +1 can be taken as "I agree, nothing more to add"; -1 just leaves the reader to puzzle about the what and why.

    Allowing "cheap negatives" like -1's might also sour the tone of the forum. Explicit disagreement can be respectful; more respectful than silence, in some cases. A -1 is a cheap shot.

    Kudos also seems to keep a per-user tally. I'm not keen on any kind of scoring system for this, although UKRP has one and in practice I don't worry about it. I'm not asking for a way to directly score people; I'm asking for a way to acclaim their posts.

    \n Personally, I like the lack of +1 clutter BS here. We already have the ability to link a thread to social network sites if we want to. I don't think we need to add gadget clutter to every single post.

    I like the fact that if someone wants to agree with you, they need to make a whole post (whisper or public) to do so. It makes it special when it happens. It encourages meaningful communication rather than vague, unclear gestures. It encourages people to be more articulate in both their agreements and disagreements, which is a good thing in a medium of communication where people can easily not understand each others' intentions.

    When someone +1s something I post elsewhere, I don't know if it's because they agree, or because they thought it was funny, or because they sympathize or empathize, or what. They're just sending some sort of vague semi-positivity that carries about as much meaning as a lips-closed smile-nod to a stranger when they get into the elevator with you.

    If your "me too" doesn't really add to the conversation, then recording it through a +1 mechanism doesn't really add anything either.\n
    Rob, I'd like to 'thank' you for this thread, but can't find how to do so.
    \nYou just did. With your words. And then you went on to actually add to the conversation, because the lack of a +1 mechanism encouraged you to do so.

    And if you hadn't had anything to actually add, then nothing really would have been missed by the fact that you didn't have a chance to run a little +1 or Like counter up by one. Nobody here, including you and Rob, would really have been the poorer for it. Instead, we are the richer because you actually ended up writing a full post and adding new insight rather than just giving a lazy +1.
