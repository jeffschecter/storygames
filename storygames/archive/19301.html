    \n I think you need more "above the fold." I'd like to get a little more information before we get to the big Introduction banner.     \n What @FOVBen says. Images are big and the text is long and compact. For instance, 'the team', you could use bullets points with roles and links. For the images and texts, you could integrate them altogether in an image (even the text in the KS page would be in the image). It has no real impact on 'keyword', but it is a lot of work, in my opinion.

    Something I think you should really, but really add (and note that you not have done it either for Magician, which is very good page BTW): present me the rewards and the pledge levels.
    I went thru all this beautifully illustrated page to have to move up and read the rewards in that awful layout. And you have a lot of tiers.

    Represent the rewards with icons and put them in the center of the page. For instance, as I read it 'Home Grown Hero' is limited to people living in Korea? If so, put a 'label' with the information on it.

    I would put a production planning too. And add that information on the reward level ('early access' means a certain date).

    Otherwise, you are all set. Quite an impressive work.

    A little note: I have a KS starting this Friday and I'm doing something a little bit like you (PDF only) and I think it could serve you as an example.

    \n OP -- your KS page looks fun as hell. LOVE the art and I think when it comes to getting KS interest, that's a huge deal -- especially as it relates to superhero gaming.

    The video, though... hmm. It did nothing to sell the product. Gave me vague promises that pretty much every rpg ever promises. Provided no details.

Makes me feel connected to the creators, makes me want to be more of a patron then simply someone looking to pre-order a game.

    Pells, quick note on your KS -- you jump from $10 CAD to $40 CAD. Really? Nothing in between?
    \nNo, and three reasons:
    - I just hate (but really, really hate) all rewards that are not directly related to the core of the project. For instance, at 25$ I could have stickers, t-shirt, mugs, posters?
    - If I want to include them (let's say posters or T-shirts), I'll have to include them in my budget (and I'm not even talking about shipping), thus increasing my initial goal for something that is not relevant to the project, and has weak value to the backers. Who's going to buy that 25$ posters anyway?
    - The main reason KickStarter projects deliver late and off budget is the delivery of physical rewards. There is none in my project.

    I'm really looking for 'customers' here: I need DM, I need players ...

    \n You might see what you think of your layout if you move the first para of your introduction "above the fold." Having a little more of a sell before the reader invests in scrolling down seems like it would be a better hook.

    Right now you have
    video
    blurb:THE superhero RPG for the Apocalypse World system! Comic book stories and characters like you've always wanted them!
    banner
    video
    Intro: Comic book stories and characters like you've always wanted them! Worlds in Peril combines descriptive, creative and flexible powers with a narrative structure that encourages players to take control of their stories and build worlds together. Powered by the Apocalypse World system, Worlds in Peril can produce compelling super hero action in any world with little-to-no preparation. Play to find out what happens, be the hero you want to be!

    The Apocalypse World engine has already been hacked and adapted into various games, many of which have been successfully funded through kickstarter and crowdfunding.It's been adapted to the award-winning Dungeon World for dungeon crawling adventures, Tremulus for lovecraftian horror. Sagas of the Icelanders for playing Icelandic settlers. Monsterhearts for playing teenager monsters. Monster of the Week for monster hunting, and many others! We've spend the last year and a half designing, hacking, playtesting and writing to introduce superheroes into the already incredibly diverse and amazing Apocalypse World system.
