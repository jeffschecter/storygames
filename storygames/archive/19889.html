    \n How about you Colin, what's going on?

    As for me, I've been suuuuuuper busy with my new life in Japan. Mostly work, a lot of work, and more work (12-16 hour days etc). Things should stabilize within the next year or so (I work for a company that just went startup to public). It's exciting, but it casts a heavy shadow over my gaming life:

    Currently finishing up the last edits of Ryuutama for its English release.

    I get in gaming about once every two weeks, half the sessions in English, half in Japanese (or some sessions which are mixed Eng/Jpn), usually with friends and strangers (even mix) at a local gaming cafe in Tokyo (Cafe Daydream). It keeps my head screwed on straight. Mostly RPGs (5e, which is blowing my mind, I'm really loving it; plus other English and Japanese games), with some board games here or there (Risk Legacy, full campaign over the summer).

    Since work is kinda dominating my life, and I've been focusing on improving my Japanese (may have to attend classes to reach that next plateau) and trying to exercise, while maintaining a gaming life, I recently realized I may soon have to pretty much entirely give up electronic gaming (video games; mostly these days on PC/Steam). But it might be for the best, there's too many games - good games, even - to keep them all in my head.

    Next few weeks I've got lined up:

    * Finishing the Ryuutama corebook altogether

    * More 5E, sinking slowly into a homebrew world that borrows parts from 13th Age, other parts from Shin Megami Tensei

    * Various Japanese games, including Log Horizon

    * Starting a bi-weekly game club where we play 1-2 sessions of a small-press story game. I generated some interest in PTA, Archipelago, Freemarket and others; time to show them off.

    -Andy

    \n Actually I would have preferred to use Cortex+ Drama, my very favoritest superhero system of all time, but two of the very big group of players I needed to gather to do this reeeeallly hated it and several others were "meh" about it, but there you are. Fate Core is somewhat of a compromise choice because to make this work I need to develop a core of about 15 players or so and so I need a reaaally popular and easy to comprehend game.

    So the anthology. I just wrapped up a World of Darkness game set in a fictional city at different times in the 20th century:

    A Hunter game in late 1963
    A Vampire game in 1921
    A Changeling game in 1973

    (I still have a few more to do at some point: Promethean in 1936, Orpheus in 2000.)

    Each of these games had some crossover in terms of players so they all could get some thrill of seeing things they had learned in a prior game come up again. But they all also had some players who had not played in the anthology before so they could just respond naturalistically.

    In the current game I'm pushing the timeline much harder. Literally every chapter of the game I announce the in-character date and say that it's "189 days until Tropical Storm Jeremiah". That's the crisis that will end this section of the anthology and require all the characters to pull together to save people!
