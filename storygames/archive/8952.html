    \n For Dragonlance:

    Stolen from: Dragonlance Nexus

    Otik's Spiced Fried Potatoes

    Entrees

    Ingredients

    * 3-5 Med to Large Potatos (depending on how many are to be served)
    * 1 Stick of Butter
    * 1 Medium Onion cut in large pieces
    * 2 Cloves of Garlic crushed
    * 1-2 Teaspoons Ground Cayenne Pepper
    * 1-2 Teaspoons Ground Black Pepper
    * Salt to taste

    Directions

    Scrub potatoes, and remove any eyes. Chop into 1/2-inch cubes with skins. Microwave until they start to soften while you prepare other ingredients.

    Melt butter in frying pan (traditionally an iron skillet), and heat until sizzling. Add cayenne and black peppers to butter and stir.

    Add onion and garlic to pan and cook until onions are starting to turn clear.

    Add the potatoes and stir to coat them in the butter, then salt to taste. Cook until potatoes are nice and crisp.

    Serves 3-4 commoners or 1 hungry warrior.

    Note - For the full effect, serve in the pan with a large hunk of bread and cheese and a big tankard of strong ale.

    \n Poison'd food can be really delicious! Pirates didn't eat navy rations, they put to shore to hunt or raided fishing boats or villages. They also had access to all kinds of spices - spices were a big part of the international trade that they preyed upon. They probably ate more weevilly tack and salt pork than, like, I do, but what's the use being a pirate if you might as well be a navy sailor?

    Make a paste of garlic, anchovies, sugar, hot pepper, black pepper, and other spices if you like*. Smear it on whatever you're going to cook.

    Get a little oil hot in a dutch oven. Drop in your smeared meat, including any liquid on the plate. Add water to halfway up the meat, not to cover it. Simmer, lidded, until done, and if it's not fish, turn it over a couple times while it's cooking. Fish will be done already. Finish it with chopped fresh herbs, or not.

    You can have boiled greens (with, sure, salt pork) and whatever else - oh man how about some roast sweet potatoes? - too, to make it an occasion. All with serious bread to sop up the goodness, and dark and stormies (ginger beer, lime juice, rum) to make you happy. Yummo!

    * It turns out that the spice mix in a jar called "pickling spice" is a perfectly appropriate mix and suitable for birds or pork. If you're cooking goat, you'll want something with more guts, pickling spice is too bright for goat. And if you're cooking fish, just go with garlic-anchovies-sugar-hot pepper-black pepper, plus maaaaaybe some coriander seeds.

    For Dogs in the Vineyard, have your pork and beans with whole wheat griddlecakes.

    For the Wicked Age, roast or stew a duck with many, many garlic cloves and garlic scapes, and have it with bulgur pilaf and a bread-tasting beer. Be all like "dang, people ate food just like this in the first cities in the world. So yum."

    Weapons of the Gods: nothing better than a pot of tea made from a tea brick (or medallion). Make sure to grind and roast the tea before brewing, to kill any mold that might have built up during shipping. Be careful to pour gently and not to pour the dregs into somebody's cup, as the tea powder will be there.

    Castle Falkenstein: potted shrimp (cooked shrimp covered in melted, clarified butter with a bit of spices, then put someplace cool to let the butter solidify), flavoured with mace and parsley, or whatever other spices you prefer. It keeps pretty well, especially with modern refridgeration, so you can make it a couple of days before and pull it out for the game. Break open the butter to eat the shrimp.
