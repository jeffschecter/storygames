\n Hi. I don't post here very often but I do lurk fairly frequently.

Biblio the card game

Is a custom card game where all the cards display images of various book covers. It's kind of like Supertrumps (I think) except that when two players play cards against each other they have to use the themes/ideas/plot/content of the book they have to argue for a victory over the opposing players book card. So essentially it's a game of semi-structured yelling about books you may or may not know anything about for fun. How to determine a victorious argument??

I'm not really into rules sets so I'm after suggestions on possible ideas or engines to make this playable.

    \n When I was a student with no money and lots of free time, we used to play "whose car is coolest?"

    You watch traffic and the first car that goes past belongs to the first player. The second car to go past belongs to the second player, and so on. When every player has a car, the player with the coolest car gets a point. Then you start again.

    It works so long as no one knows anything about cars. As soon as someone busts out "Oh, but the engine in the '86 model is shit" the game goes downhill.

    It gets more fun when you play "whose car is the worst". Everyone cheering when a dude on a broken-down scooter comes trundling past is funny.

    I guess justifying purly subjective judgements with spurious logic and furious debate is a fun thing to do?

    Posted By: David ArtmanPosted By: bevedogIt just occurred to me that you will have a massive intellectual property problem with book covers as a major game element. Might have to go with just titles and info about the book, perhaps color-coded?
    Just have to stay in the public domain, is all (restricting you to a mere tens of thousands of great works). But, yes, there's game potential in just titles and authors.

    Generally speaking, in the USA, it's not public domain unless it was published prior to 1923. So yeah, Shakespeare is in the public domain, but all the book covers slapped on the Bard since 1923 are still copyrighted. And since The Great Gatsby is often thought of as the book with the first really memorable cover, and that was published in 1925, there just won't be public domain book covers, I'm afraid.

    I don't want to be a downer--this sounds like a game I would buy--but if part of the fun is recognizable book covers, I fear it won't work.

    (Also, while I have dealt with copyright in my job as a librarian, I'm not a lawyer, and if someone can show me I'm wrong that would be cool.)
