\n Well, that was educational, if somewhat disappointing.

I\u2019ve signed up for the annual game design contest, Game Chef, this year: write a game on one theme with four ingredients over nine days. I decided to try running through one of the past contests as practice and went right back to the start, to 2002. The ingredients were:

- Africa
- Art
- Court
- Numerology

I had intended to run through this in the nine days while I was up in Iceland on holiday, but work interfered and I ended up working for most of it and a whole load of other work crap rained down when I got back. That meant the game has had to keep being bumped off of my priority list.

Combine that with my tendency to over research subjects and the game has taken far longer than the nine days, although admittedly, most of that is spread out around actual work and a lot of travel.

Anyway, I have work commitments and other gaming projects I\u2019ve promised people I\u2019ll complete so I\u2019m reluctantly conceding my game Mother Sahra done. It\u2019s not finished, but it\u2019s about 80% there. I at least managed to learn a lot about my own work patterns and the realistic amount of time I can devote to the contest (next to nothing).

I\u2019ll try to come back to this and finish it when I can, but for the moment, here it is in all its under-cooked glory.

    \n Hey man!

    Good on your for attempting Game Chef. It's a tough thing to do, creating a full RPG in just a few weeks. I entered (and won) in 2011 with Forsooth!

    I find that the most important thing I did to help Forsooth! succeed was to shy away from setting and use my time and book space to create an interesting rules system that reflects the themes. I see that your book is a ton of (good) theme, but then just a page and a half of mechanics! If you want to do well in Game Chef, you need a fully playable game. If you keep that in mind for next year, you'll be in great shape.
