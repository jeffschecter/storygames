\n I'd love a thread that put together all of the various games that are currently being developed or playtested. It'd make it easy for me to find them when I'm choosing games to run.

So, this is an experiment: let's see if people use it / find it useful.


* Tell us about your game in one post. If you want to link to another thread, post or document, go for it.


If you're responding, I feel like some of the principles from the 'Stuff to Watch' thread might work well here:

* Number your response (first response: [1]; second response [2]; third response [3]). If you see that you're the second person to post, "Wow, that looks cool!" to a post here, then put a "[2]" at the top of your post.

* The fourth person to post should just take their comment and create a new thread (or talk on an existing thread about the game), to carry the discussion over there further.

    I have three games I'm currently working on right now

    Touch of Evil is a gmless game of lovecraftian horror. It mashes up many elements from Cthlhu Dark and Stealing Cthulhu, Montsegur 1244, PsiRun and some other sources of inspiration (such as KULT and Unknown Armies) to deliver what I hope will be a disturbing experience.
    It's still in development and not yet playable :P

    King of the City is a gmless game of "power play" between 2 and 4 players that uses Chess and Chess2 rules as a basic frame to tell stories of influent individuals and their retinues viciously competing for the ultimate control of The City.
    Development is complete, in-house playtesting has been done, now I really need esternal/blind testing.
    Ask me if you would like to be a tester

    FateLess is a gmless hack of Fate Core, allowing 2 to 5 players to play open-ended adventures. It uses its mechanics to summon a feeling that is as close as possible to that of a "traditional rpg" (you don't play as a part-time GM, you play as your PC with an invisible/homoeopathic GM taking care of the rest). The focus is on individual and independent PCs with their own personal goals, rather than PCs in a united group (they might band together, but they can as easily part ways or be adversaries).
    Development is complete, in-house playtesting has been done, now I really need esternal/blind testing.
    LINK for more info, and ask me if you would like to be a tester

    \n I just released my Core Rules edition of "DayTrippers" (Surreal Sci Fi OSR RPG) and I am about to release the first two adventure modules for it. More coming.
    http://www.rpgnow.com/product/142287/DayTrippers

    Also this month: I'm still playtesting "ScenePlay" (collaborative narrative card game) and I expect that to be released later this year.

    ETA: Those modules have dropped...

    Less Little is an introductory module for 1-2 players. The tone is dreamlike and humorous with some mildly adult situations, and the plot is firmly guided. This module is recommended for beginning players and campaigns with a comical touch.
    http://www.rpgnow.com/product/142329/Less-Little

    Vidome 123 is a module for 2 or more players. The tone is dark and spooky, with serious threats from inscrutable foes (both human and alien), and the plot is open-ended. This module is recommended for DayTrippers with at least one previous mission under their belt.
    http://www.rpgnow.com/product/142330/Vidome-123\ufeff

    Both modules make use of a simple resolution system and include conversion tables for common dice ranges, making them system-agnostic.

    \nMagical Fury
    A dark magical girl RPG, kind of a cousin to Magical Burst but much simpler. It's a Powered by the Apocalypse game in the same sense as The Sundered Land, and meant to play very quickly. Combat is cut down to a few die rolls to determine the outcome of the battle, and a bigger portion of the rules is various moves dealing with the characters trying to affect the world.

    Raspberry Heaven
    A heartwarming slice of life high school girls RPG (inspired by Azumanga Daioh, Hidamari Sketch, and other 4-panel manga) I've been trying to figure out how to design for a while now. I'm nearly ready to playtest a new version inspired by J. Walton's "Restless," which is more freeform and runs on a series of scenes on cards.

    AnimeCon
    A freeform RPG (rules-wise inspired by Remodel and Amidst Endless Quiet) for serious stories about people going to an anime convention.

    \n I've got one game that's ready to go before playtesters (I think - haven't really done this before!)...

    Tall Pines is my love letter to the TV series Twin Peaks, in the form of a surrealist story game. When you play, you\u2019ll tell the story of a tragic murder in a small town. The victim of this murder is someone that most people in town loved, admired, and assumed to be basically good. As the story unfolds and the mystery is revealed, however, the town\u2019s opinion of the victim is likely to change. Through play, you\u2019ll discover why the victim was killed, what the people in the town are hiding, and what dark forces are at play in the seemingly bucolic town. You\u2019ll also develop your own language of symbolism and metaphor to drive the game forward!

    This game is card-based, and plays fairly similarly to Jason Morningstar's Carolina Death Crawl (a big inspiration!). It is GMless/full and there is no character monogamy. There's also a token currency/feedback mechanic fed by the emotional beats that the game's scenes evoke.

    Hit me up if you'd like to check this out, or if you have questions - would love to get some feedback!

    \nWithin My Clutches is my supervillain snafu game. It's been playtested thoroughly, but I have a brand new tweak I need to test. A small thing, but I've been slow writing it up. Playtester interest would get me over the hump!

    About the game: take turns playing your villain, GMing, and NPC guest starring in others' scenes. Spend GM fanmail to direct others' bonus scenes; spend Villain fanmail to add scenes for your villain's master plan.

    Within a scene, play is pretty freeform until the roll at the end.

    As a Villain, try to minimize the amount of trouble you get in while pursuing your neurotic Wants; you can always buy success on your die roll, but for the price of over-extending and creating Distractions. In the endgame, roll against Distractions to see if you can hold onto your successes, achieve your master plan, both, or neither. There's some simple strategy involved, but it's mostly luck.

    The point is to tell fun supervillain stories. They usually produce a lot of laughs, but some pathos and tragedy isn't uncommon as well.

    \n I'll be running final playtests on two games at Kapcon this weekend.

    Soth
    This is my game about cultists in small-town USA trying to summon a dark god. If you're familiar with Call of Cthulhu, it's the mirror version of that game. You play the bad guys who've gone insane, and you're dealing with the pesky investigators trying to ruin your plans.

    The last few runs of the game have felt fairly tight and given me specific things to rewrite. This weekend *fingers crossed* I'll be checking that it all works the way I want it to.

    I'm hoping to start putting this into layout in about two weeks.


    Left Coast
    This is a game about science-fiction authors in California dealing with the pressures of writing a novel, the needy people in their lives, and the subtle emergence of weird conspiracies and paranormal events that seemed to have been ripped out of their stories.

    I've created a quickstart version of the game design to create short stories. This one feels pretty tight to me: I'm going to ship it as soon as I can (but that may not be for another couple of months).

    \n I am developing AfroFuture, a game about music and social justice. Here is the pitch.\n
    Inspired by P-Funk mythology and Ishmael Reed\u2019s fiction, AfroFuture is a tabletop role-playing game about the war for the narrative of human history. In our corner, representing art and freedom, is the Mothership: a transdimensional soul machine crewed by historical luminaries and culture heroes born from your favorite songs. In that corner, representing patriarchy and dystopia, is the Man: a mysterious entity wrought from the evil in every human heart.
    He\u2019s recruited the past\u2019s worst villains\u2014from Andrew Jackson to Bal Thackeray to Vivian James\u2014to command his forces and to suborn the will of the people. His machinations are already underway, not only in the present, past, and future, but also in the parallel worlds represented by your favorite RPGs. It is up to you, as AfroFuture operatives, to bring the noise and the funk to any place, any time, and any RPG system which suffers the Man\u2019s depredations.

    AfroFuture gives us a creative, cathartic, and galvanizing means of exploring frustration with the social ills which weaken our hobby. Music figures prominently in the game: every player character has a song about them, and the fiction interfaces with music playing at your real-world table. If you want a setting which places minority experience front and center, this game will hook you up.
    \nIt's crowdfunding via the Different Play Patreon here, and I'm answering questions about it in this thread.
