    I'm using SimpleMu, on the Foundry MUSH.

    It logs to text file, it has a DitV specific object that handles conflicts, conflict res, fallout and has info pages that bring up fallout etc (coded by Josh BR). It clearly differentiates IC from OOC in the same window (which is important if you want people to learn the procedures of play, because it parses the OOC in the middle of the IC action), allows me to both say and pose (speak or do an action) allows me to page other players privately, and if I wanted to take advantage of some of SimpleMu's options, I could also change the display options for each of the other players input, or set triggers to respond to specific actions in game. I can C&P into the text entry, and I can call up historical input that I've put in.

      OK, Matt and I are reviving this idea of a browser-based online tabletop, and we're planning to put together a proof-of-concept consisting of a chat client (OOC/IC) and die roller (based on Graham's one).

      Later on, all going well, we can fancy it up with features like a simple whiteboard, built-in active character sheets and the ability to pass around tokens and cards.

      And now, your questions pre-answered!

      Q: Why browser-based?
      A: Because browsers are platform-independent and (effectively) zero-install.

      Q: Won't this be really laggy and suck?
      A: That's what the proof-of-concept is to establish. Great strides have been made in real-time data exchange between servers and browsers, under the name Comet. It should be do-able.

      Since Matt and I are twelve time zones apart, we'll be able to tell quite quickly if it's laggy.

      At the moment I am not envisaging different authority levels.

      My aim is to produce something which has the generic tools necessary for playing indie-style tabletop games online, but doesn't encode any rules - just like your actual dice, for example, don't. So everyone will be able to do everything, but if the particular game you're playing has a GM, everyone else will simply refrain from doing certain things that (in that game) are considered the prerogative of the GM. Just like you wouldn't roll a D20 if the game you were playing only used D6s.

      The "in-character" channel will have the ability to switch characters. Again, everyone will be able to do this, but in games where players only play one character, they simply won't use that feature.
