This is the "Laser Princess" thing for you folks who don't want to click the link.

I'm doing it. I'm building a campaign world, based on this idea. I don't see any reason to use a new game system, though, so I'm considering using something like FATE or Heroquest to run it. (Aspects or Keywords would replace "classes" from Adventurers and Abbatoirs)

Here's the world's creation myth:

It Is As If there were, at a time so long ago that it is legend even
to the Last God, ten thousand worlds, hanging in the firmament like
bright jewels. Each was the sole creation of a God, who loved it and
cared for it and protected it.

It Is As If a beast came, teeth and mouth and gullet, devouring,
tearing, shredding, world after world, god after god, hunger without
limit, ferocity without end.

Or As If a fire came, leaping from world to world, despoiling,
consuming, an inferno to which hell itself is but a candle.

Or As If a war came, a great clashing of arms, setting god upon god,
with worlds used as weapons, a conflict to shake the very foundations
of the sky.

It Is As If, when all was done, the merest shreds of the worlds
remained, tattered fragments floating in an empty void, and one
wounded god.

Or As If, before the end came, One came to each world, a thief,
stealing away one piece from each world, hiding it from the cataclysm.

Or As If, in the midst of it, each god gave unto One of their number,
a piece of his world, for safekeeping, should the Worst occur.

It Is As If, when all that was left was remnants, the Last God took
them, and knitted them together to make a patchwork world. The
patches are the Domains, the nations great and small. The threads are
the borderlands, the grey nothingnesses that lie between the Domains.
The knots are the Cusps, the great fonts of power where magic flows
like water.

When I'm done writing it up, I'll have a TiddlyWiki document that will contain gameworld information, character creation instructions, and links to online resources that I can distribute to players.

This isn't a playtest; I just want to run a game. The Skype Nine Worlds game is going to be coming to a close, and I want to get another voice chat game going, either in Skype or Ventrilo.

More later.

    Here's the list of "Classes" that I worked up:

    The following Class Aspects do not use magic. Nonmagical Class Aspects generally have a wide variety of skills that can be purchased.

    Knight - An armored warrior, skilled with horse and lance, sword and shield, a protector and enforcer
    Prince or Princess - A young scion of a noble house.
    Ranger - Master of the wild lands
    Rogue - Sneaky outlaw

    The following Class Aspects use magic. Magical Class Aspects generally have few skills, but have some special magical ability.

    Sage - Uses words of power to command nearly anything
    Prophet - Sees what only fate can see
    Summoner - Calls demons and elementals to do his bidding
    Wizard - Adept with the engineering of crystal artifacts
    Priest - Devotee of the Lost God
    Greenweaver - Friend of the Forests
    Nihilomancer - Uses the Void to destroy what he will

    Here's the list of "Descriptors"

    Arcane - Derives mystic defense from a secret name
    Bestial - Takes on an aspect of a savage beast
    Dark - Corrupt servant of the demons
    Doomed - Cursed and blessed by knowledge of dark fate
    Fey - Ethereally fair or disgustingly foul, creatures of the forest
    Laser - Attuned to the crystals and able to use their power
    Mighty - Powerfully built and immensely strong
    Mysterious - Hooded and cloaked, few know their power
    Noble - Associated with the noble houses
    Sky - Born of the sky, they make it their home
    Wandering - Survivor of a land taken by the void

    The first two steps of making a character is to choose one of each. (some combinations are not allowed, so the full space of possibilities is around 88 combinations)

    Sometimes they overlap. A prince wouldn't get much benefit out of being Noble. A Laser Wizard similarly makes little sense.

    Sometimes they're contradictory. Many of the descriptors have to do with attuning and internalizing one of the sources of power in the world, whereas the associated classes are about externalizing and manipulating the power.

    For example, the Dark Ones are those who have allowed themselves to be mastered by demons, whereas summoners bind and control demons.
