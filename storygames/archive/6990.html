    \n There are plenty of bugs and it's awful laggy. But let's have some perspective. There are dozens of companies (I've talked to at least a half-dozen of them) who are trying to create their own casual virtual worlds right now. And right under their noses, Google has been able to create a Beta (or Alpha, really) of a virtual world that runs in a browser with no client download. And I can already see the structure they have built to support microtransactions and user-generated content. Plus it plugs into the Google systems like GMail and the like for ease of use.

    It's quite a technical achievement, and as a virtual worlds proponent I'm pretty excited about the possibilities!

    \n I also am confident enough that Google has some big enough brains that in a year or so this might be something pretty cool. But that doesn't keep it from being a dog right now. If virtual worlds were a new _idea_ then sure, I'd be all excited about the possibilities, but they're not. This is just an implementation, and I'll be the first to admit that one of Google's great strengths is implementing on existing ideas, and from that perspective this is probably going to be something pretty cool eventually. But I kind of must underscore "eventually".

    -Rob D.

    PS - That said, I'll fully concede that there are others trying to do this who suck even more, as hard as that may be to imagine. Curse of an interesting idea's transformation into new hotness.

    Posted By: BrendanPosted By: Caesar_Xa virtual world thatruns in a browser with no client download.
    \nExcept that... there is a client, and you have to download it, which is why it only works on Windows?

    With much respect, that's splitting hairs, really. The point is that the experience is usable straight from the browser, without needing to go to another app to play in the world. I think their concept is that most users have the browser open most of the time, so why not let them multitask using that tool?

    I think the reason it works only in Windows is that they used that as their baseline OS to develop on. Once they get it more stable, it shouldn't be difficult to port it to Mac. Just a matter of time and resources.
