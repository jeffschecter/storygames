\n Really basic narrative-control hack for White Wolf games, goes like this:

Roll your dice pool as normal. Calculate your successes as normal. If you roll 10s, don't re-roll those dice, roll new ones. Let's say your dice pool is 5, and you roll 1, 2, 5, 7, 10. Don't say "one success" and re-roll the 10. Roll an extra die. It comes up a 9. Groovy. 1, 2, 5, 7, 9, 10.

Pass all the 1s to your GM. Set aside the 10s in front of yourself.

If you failed, GM gets narrative control, and is free to do bad things to you. If you succeeded, you get narrative control, and can do what you like. Note that you don't have to narrate success or failure based on what the dice say. The catch: you can only describe your outcome in its basic form: "I unlock the door" or "you fall between the roofs".

Now, if you succeeded, the GM can add a detail if there are any 1s on the table. One detail costs a 1. Use "yes, but" , or FANBOYS (from Houses of the Blooded) if you like. Then, if you have 10s, you can add a detail by setting aside a 10. Go back and forth, 1 die each, until all the 10s and 1s are gone. Shouldn't take too long.

As the player, feel free to add details that would normally be the realm of the GM in a trad game. That's the point.

Hooray.

    \n Nice, it's rare enough that it adds some spice, but common enough that it should happen more than once a session. Also, if you're super skilled in an area, you're more likely to both develop "yes, but" and your own badass details, which is good since you've (hopefully) made yourself really good in that area because you want it to be your turf and you're really interested in it.
