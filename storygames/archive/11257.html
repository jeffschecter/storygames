\n My game experience in 2009 was lacking. I was able to get in a few games of D&D 4e, which was fun, but never seemed to scratch my gaming itch. After looking around all the new RPG goodies delivered during Christmas, I had the sinking feeling that I was amassing a collection of games that I would never have the chance to play... Then I came up with a neat idea.
\n
\nI know enough gamers in my area to put together a one-shot or something, but most players are simply too busy to do a sustainable campaign or weekly gaming. Instead I'm going to do a once-a-month one shot of every RPG I own (including some free ones). My picks, a mix of traditional and story games, are listed below:
\n
\nMouse Guard (probably run the Delivering Mail mission in the book)
\nLady Blackbird
\nWarhammer FRP 3e
\nShotgun Diaries
\nMutants and Masterminds
\nBurning Wheel (Probably run the sword)
\nSavage Worlds
\nD&D 3.5
\nD&D 4e
\nStar Wars Saga Edition
\nCall of Chtulu
\nA wild card pick (maybe Dogs in the Vineyard or Apocalypse World)
\n
\nMy questions for you all:
\n
\nAny good one-off scenarios for some of these games that you're aware of? Any suggestions for the monthly order in which I play (There is a lot of fantasy in there so a mix would be good)? Any games that should be added or knocked off the list?
