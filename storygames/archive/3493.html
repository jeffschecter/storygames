I said:I was planning to organize a low-key Bay Area camp-con to offset the [current planned] lack of NBP2 in the fall, but then I looked around and realized that all the area state parks, at least, have camping areas that are full on all weekends well into September.
Caesar_X replied:I think this sounds awesome, but we might want to wait until late Sept or Oct to do this. The parks go from crazy busy to easy-to-get-into. I went to Yosemite a few years back in early Nov and there felt like there were like 12 people in the whole park!
\nIn principle: Yes! Late September to October is the ideal time.
In practice: this year, such a thing will probably not be instigated by the currently designated NBP ringleaders (myself, JBR, Paul Tevis). Josh and Paul are busy August to mid-September with con season, and Nancy and I are busy mid-September to late-October with getting married and going to Japan. Past late October, we miss the good camping season, because things get Cold and Wet and then we can't snigger at the people freezing at Camp Nerdly while we chill in the sun drinking margaritas and gin.

An August "Not going to GenCon, but wish you were?" Bay Area camp con was supposed to tide us [me] over, but, as mentioned, I didn't get a move on soon enough to secure camping.

Uh, there is some chance that I could still set it up for the weekend after Labor Day. It's the week after ConQuest, but that may not be such a big deal, and a lot of campsites mysteriously free up space the moment Labor Day is past. Is there sufficient interest?

    \n Expected price range: $30-40 per person, which includes campsite Friday and Saturday night, extensive sandwich-makings for lunch Saturday and Sunday, and assorted simple breakfasts. Dinners will basically be bring your own grillables, and I'll make sure that either the firepits have grill spots or to bring a charcoal grill.

    Non-player family has been a troublesome element for both Camp Nerdly and Nerdly Beach Party. It has yet to work out well. To make matters worse, camping is unlikely to involve well-segregated space conducive to 'officially adult' areas or the like. Unless this ends up being a lot more people than I'm expecting, I'm inclined to dodge the issue and close the event to non-participants. I know that doesn't work out best for everybody, but one goal is to keep this low-impact.

    Assuming there are enough all-weekend people to go forward, though, there's probably room for some to just show up for a day. I'm thinking either Big Basin or Portola Redwoods state parks, both of which are easy day trips from the valley.
