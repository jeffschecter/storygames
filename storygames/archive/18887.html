    It screws people up when the real people don't live up to the Princess' fantasy. So she can make them live up to it. I dunno how you'd necessarily reflect that, but...

Maybe you have to Hold Steady when the Princess is in the scene, if you want to contradict her image of you. She's controlling. (Maybe take a cue from some of the Queen's schtick?)

    \n My biggest concern is that this skin relies on regressive gender roles. Monsterhearts is all about queering up its genre, and so The Princess needs to be all about queering up its fairy tales if it's to truly play to the spirit of the game. "The Princess" is a gendered title, which is fine if you can imagine someone saying "I'm going to play a boy Princess, I think." But then it goes on to talk about the noble prince, the helpful brother, and the wicked stepmother/matriarch. And now I don't see the possibility of boy Princesses, I see a cisnormative and heteronormative set of expectations and roles. It needs to be queered up.

    What is the sex move supposed to be doing? I get the first half (which squicks me but is appropriate, perhaps), but I don't understand what story the second half is supposed to be telling. It sounds like the princess refusing sex provokes anger and fear, but having sex provokes remorse and self-judgment. This means that the Princess is strongly discouraged from ever approaching sexual situations, regardless of her intent or boundaries. Unless I'm misunderstanding how the move would play out: that sounds boring. Maybe alternately: having sex could represent a step away from living in a fairy tale and a step toward living in the real adult world. Mechanically, this could involve changing one of The Archetypes to reflect something grounded in the real world.
