\n
\n
\n I've been coming up with diagrams for Universalis, and plan to do the same for Primetime Adventures. I'm thinking about what I like from different systems.
\n
\nPrimetime Adventures does a great job of structuring play.
\nUniversalis does a great job of defining things in the world in terms of whether it's important to define them.
\nD&D does a great job of setting up a common visual baseline in a scene.
\nPDQ does a great job in making a common scale and a core mechanic that resolves quickly.
\nStar Wars Saga Edition does a great job of making skills into a common language about who can do what.
\n
\nAlso, the one that doesn't fit with the rest:
\nd20 in its infinite varieties almost does a great job of having characters have rules built around their character concept (feats), but the levelling mechanics frequently trip over these (i.e. "builds" and linear advancement rather than gaining feats ad-hoc).
\n
\nanyone have things they really think a game does well, and which would definitely be part of their ultimate RPG?

    \n Dogs/Afraid does a great job of harnassing character change (in terms of what's on the character sheet) into play both in terms of changes relating to play events and being continuous in play, keeping specifics of change under player control even when the dice dictated the outcome of play.

    Dogs/Afraid and Burning Wheel do great job of breaking the connection of "character must succeed at actions" and "character becomes more proficient/player controlling character gains resources to affect play".

    Burning Wheel does a great job of a GM-based system distributing creative content authority to players (e.g., circles tests).

    Universalis/Pool do a great job of breaking the "player must win conflicts in the fiction in order to gain authority in the system".

    Pool does a great job of saying "you must have set-backs before big success, you cannot go from success to success to success with same player or character starring always" (mapping to that story/dramatic sensibility of structure).
