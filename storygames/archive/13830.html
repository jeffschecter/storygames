\n I'm one of those people who writes up ideas in notebooks with the intention of compiling disjointed fragments and gems of wisdom into complete coherent systems later. I'm sure there are other people around here like that, because we've seen some great discussions about writing in compendiums (compendia?).

I was recently unpacking some boxes after moving house late last year and I found some of my old notebooks. They had pages and pages of absolute crap...but stuff that I thought was really good at the time. But in each of the notebooks, there were a coupe of fragments that I looked at and thought "Why haven't I used this yet??? THIS IS AWESOME!!"...quickly followed by "This is still incomplete and I haven't come up with a game idea that can use it".

Examples:
* A simple trait based live-roleplaying system (I haven't run a good live game in years, and that's why this hasn't seen the light of day).
* A simple step-die system for miniatures which I wrote up in the early 90s (most people I've payed miniatures with have only wanted to play with established rules sets, so this has basically been neglected and forgotten).
* A few twisted forms of magic that could be applied to a setting (I just haven't found the right setting or genre for these rules to emulate).
* etc...

So I'm basically wondering who else has had these moments.

What have you found among your old ideas that would be awesome to use, but you just haven't had the opportunity to use them?
