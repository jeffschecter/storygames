\n Hi everybody!

I'm coordinating Indie Hurricane at GameStorm 16 in March 2014, and I'd like you input on how we can put on the best show possible.

The thing is, I'm considering changing a few things, and that's what I would like your input on. But before I get to the details of that, there are the groups people whose GameStorm experience I'm considering:

-The people running the games.
-The people playing in those games.
-The people who are new to these games.

I'd also like to point out that the people running the games tend to be community insiders who are already well established in this crowd.

I think each of those groups has different needs to one degree or another.

The basic format of Indie Hurricane breaks down into three gaming slots: Morning (3 hours), Afternoon (4 hours), and Evening (4 hours), with hour long meal breaks in between. That much probably won't change.

Here is what we have been doing:
Morning - pre-scheduled games
Afternoon - pre-scheduled games and open play pitch circle
Evening - pre-scheduled games and open play pitch circle
Panels - ??? (sometimes we have 'em, sometimes we don't)
Special Events - do their own thing regardless of the rest of the schedule

(The open gaming pitch circle is a less formal thing, where a handful of people pitch games they'd like to run, and the big group of players pitch which game they'd like to play based on the pitches made.)

We have had a great time with that over the past years! But, I notice a few things here. This set up is best for the people running games, because they basically get to do whatever they want. This is significantly less good for those playing games, because we leave them to choose between the security of the pre-scheduled games and the selection of the pitch circle. Many IH participants have expressed a kind of "buyer's remorse" on account of that choice, and that has been my experience too (doubly so when it seems like the hot new playtests wind up being pitched instead of scheduled). Someone new to this would probably wind up in the pitch circle on account of the selection. Panels have been hit an miss for IH, partly because we talk about our gaming thoughts all the time, and partly because you often finding yourself choosing between playing a game for a few hours or attending a panel for only one hour. Finally, we have the booth in the deals room, where we have some locally made indie games for sale. Pretty cool for those in the know, but a little mysterious for those not in the know.

To summarize, this set up is best for people running games who are also established members of the community. It's okay for those playing the games, but only partially accessible to those who are new (which means they are both unfamiliar with these games and are community outsiders).

I would like to make things a little bit more even. So here is what I've been toying with.

Mornings: Panel + "Speed Dating"
Afternoon: Open Gaming Pitch Circle. No Pre-Scheduled Games.
Evening: Pre-Scheduled Games. No Pitch Circle.
Night: Socializing in the Lounge.
Special Event: Big giant Nordic Freeform for like 30 people in a Morning slot.

Some of this needs explaining.

Speed Dating: Demo four games in two hours (30 min each). Four local designers run their own games that are for sale in the IH booth. Twelve players or so split into four groups and rotate through those demos. This is great for advertising games that may be unknown to people not in the loop, with a low time commitment, and trying lots of things very quickly. It will probably assist with sales, it's accessible to new people, and you can also attend the panel. But, it's may not have much to offer well established individuals, and it may be kind of a pain for the people running those demos back to back. Let me re-emphasize with this option one no longer needs to choose between attending a panel and playing a game.

Socializing in the Lounge: This is still in the works, but we may have a big ol' space with food and drink (I'm hoping to get alcohol happening here) where we can hang out on site. This would be a great place to have those awesome convention conversations, GameStorm staff/volunteers could attend more easily, and there is no coordination required (games all end at different times, different groups go to different places, etc.).

And of course, what I expect may the the most controversial point, is separating out the pre-scheduled games and the open gaming pitch circle. My aim with this is to not leave anyone feeling like they made "the wrong" choice with their IH experience. However, this is less ideal for, say, out-of-towners who maybe don't know until very late when they will be attending, and may be banking on the flexibility we've had in years past. Another option may be to just didtch pre-registered games all together, and just be awesome about announcing what is on the pitch circle menu in advance.

I want to talk panels, and how we can make it worth it for us, but I think I'll save that topic for a little later.

One more announcement: (unless disaster intervenes) our Indie Hurricane Special Guest this year is Paul Czege! So excited!

So what do you think about all of this? I want to keep the flexibility and "improv-like" nature of IH, but making this event accessible for indie-curious folks and people who do not benefit from being an indie-insider is very important to me as well. I totally want to hear from people who have attended GameStorm/IH in the past, those who have considered attending but haven't, and those who intend to attend in the future, those who have attended gaming conventions in general, and everybody.

Thank you all so much!

Tayler

    \n I think it's going to be nearly impossible to get people to list their "pitch circle" games in advance, but good luck with herding all us cats! I often choose what I'm going to run at the last minute, based on how I'm feeling and what else is going on.

    Honestly, I'm really used to the combined 4-5 methods that we tend to use at indie events in the PacificNW:

    1. Pre-reg before the con
    2. Sign-up on sheets of paper at the con
    3. Pitch circle
    4. The Donut (which we use at GoPlayNW and less at Gamestorm)
    5. Pitch/Donut organizers facilitate a game for anyone left out

    That seems totally normal to me now, but I understand if it's a bit confusing to people coming and expecting a standard con experience. But what we have to offer is explicitly NOT the standard con experience, but something better!

    An important thing to remember, though: it's critical that the people volunteering their time (and money) to organize most of the games (1) feel comfortable, (2) feel supported, and (3) have a good time, because otherwise the Indie Hurricane is less of a success and fewer people will want to spend their limited time and funds to attend next year. Right now, every year I've been at GameStorm has been better than the last (and I pretty much only participate in the Indie Hurricane), but if I go and have a less good time, I may think twice about making the effort to come the next year.

    Essentially: by all mean please help us think better about newcomers and those not yet plugged in, but let's not sabatoge the great community and experience we've created in an attempt to seek out a larger following. Honestly, the Indie Hurricane at GameStorm already feels close the maximum size it can be to operate in the same space that they've assigned to us for the last two years. If it gets bigger, we'll need a bigger space to run it too!

    \n Other thoughts:
    \u201360 people (which means 10-12 pitches) is the hard limit for pitches
    \u2013Don't stand in a circle, Pitchers stand in a line addressing the crowd
    \u2013Gather all the facilitators before you start pitching, you need 1 pitch /5 people present
    \u2013Pitchers say: their name, their game (for the love of god, only pitch one game!), a 30 second pitch, and how many players they can take
    \u2013After pitching, have all the facilitators spread out, and raise their hand, showing fingers for how many players they can take. Players migrate to their facilitator, and we shuffle folks around till everyone has a game.

    \n Ok guys,

    At long last the GameStorm scheduling system is kind of up. It's time to start submitting games!

    I'd like things to stick to the three slot schedule as much as possible. 10am-1pm, 2pm-6pm, 7pm-11pm. Open Story Gaming in the afternoon and evening on each day of the con.

    For pre-scheduled games, just go to www.gamestorm.org and register/propose events. Be sure to fill out all of the fields, and in the notes at the very bottom mention which times you'd like to run your game. Be sure to indicate your level of flexibility (if multiple times will do, or if only one will). On account of the scheduling system shenanigans this might get a little crazy, so please give me too much information rather than too little.

    For Open Story Gaming, all you have to do is get in contact with me and let me know what time(s) work for you. If you know what you want to run, say so. It's okay if you change your mind later.

    I'd like to do a kind of intro track in the afternoons on Friday, Saturday and Sunday. Fiasco, Monsterhearts, Dungeon World, or some other indie mega hit. if you are interested in that, get in touch with me or just go ahead and propose it.

    I've been toying with a way to rapidly demo games in the mornings, but I'd need a few inspired GMs for that. There is no way it could happen without a certain minimum support, so please get in touch with me if you are interested in that.

    If you run 14 hours of games, next years admission is free! You also earn "GM Bucks" which you can exchange for mostly insignificant but sometimes kind of cool swag.

    If you are interested in volunteering at the Indie Hurricane booth, also get in touch with me. I could use a few more volunteers. Volunteer 12 hours and your admission is free!

    Finally, I'm scheduling one large-ish and one medium-ish event, which are Nordic and American Freeform games. Before and After Silence, and Play With Intent. I think some of you may be interested in that. Still working on the rooms for that.

    You can reach me by emailing indiehurricane@gamestorm.org. I sometimes miss PMs on here, so that's not the best way.

    If you've got questions, let's have 'em.

    Thanks guys! I hope you're all excited for this!

    Tayler
