    \n hey dave.

    last night i played session 5 of our 1-on-1 DRYH game. i am GM. both of us are really, really enjoying ourselves,
    there is very little "jumpy" scene framing, the "camera" follows our heroine very closely; the story we are telling is very intense
    and spooky - i believe this is mostly because the person that i'm playing with, Amelia, is totally unwavering in her
    portrayal of Valerie; Valerie is a normal person who lives in a normal world and so therefore there must be a rational
    reason that she vomited up an exact duplicate of herself the day that she moved to a new city. Valerie fled the
    apartment, got ensnared in other situations...

    DRYH is working for us because Amelia and myself are able to structure the narrative in a way that pleases both of
    us, it's most likely that we'll have just one more session. There was a bit of a showdown last night...

    i keep digressing. I sense that we are kind of free-forming-with-a-safety-net, since we roll seldom and are more
    focused on what's going on socially than tossing handfuls of pain on the table. we have diverged from the
    pun-laden characters of the text, but have drawn great inspiration from the weirdness of the setting.
    i mean, the five questions are just amazing to play a one-on-one game using.
    it's so intense, everything matters, it's great.

    so, yeah. i recommend.
