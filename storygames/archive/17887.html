    Western is just a genre, but most people understand the basics. So it's pretty easy to use a system you are familiar with,

    I've played Old West with both Best Friends and Archipelago and had lots of fun.

    I bet Lady Blackbird and AW-engine would be good choices as well. I could imagine a really cool game of mistrust and backstabbing using The Mountain Witch (hmmm....)

    Really depends on what stories are you hoping to tell. What are you looking for beyond cowboys and PvP?
    \nLooking for a lot of the flavor tropes of Westerns built into the system:

    - The Archetypes (Preacher, Prospector, Whore, Black Bart, Sheriff, Drunk, Doctor, etc.)
    - Occupations and Activities (Gold Prospecting, Poker, Cattle Rustling, Train Robberies, etc)

    But also looking for some dynamic social mechanics (akin to AW or MH)

    I'm almost thinking about playing AW but flavoring my campaign as a desolate Western.

    What do you like about role-playing games, and what don't you like?
    \n- I don't like when there's so much crunch you end up completely out of character and just doing math

    - I like when the crunch is light and acts as a randomizing element of the story I'm telling with my gaming group, my focus is always on that collaboratively story piece, I enjoy the numbers and stats when they create that fluid mechanic of adding to the story but in surprising ways

    - I like when each player has an equal share in the story we are telling, equal even to me if its a GM game.

    - If I am a GM/MC I love being forced to improv and play on the fly, hence why I love my players to have a lot of control in the story, it forces me to have to think fast.

    - I don't like anything scripted or on rails, a game should give me a basic skeleton and some inspiration but leave the juicy parts of the story up in the air for my group to play out.

    - I like games that can be a one-shot, but if we enjoy ourselves can easily continue into a campaign
