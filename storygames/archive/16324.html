On Saturday, May 19th, there's going to be a very special party. It'll be a release party for Monsterhearts, taking place at Endgame Oakland, from 10am until 7pm. I'm super excited about it!

There are going to be two gaming sessions, I believe. I'll be running games in each. Jackson Tegu will be coming down with me, from Olympia. I believe he'll be running a game, and I know that Carl Rigney will be there running games. If you're a Monsterhearts MC, you can sign up to run some games as well! I believe that Chris (who owns that fine store) is still looking for additional MCs for the event.

This is my first trip to California! I'm very excited.

More details about the event can be found here.

For those who are going: afterwards, we should all go out for drinks and dinner. I hear that there's a magical place called "The Trappist." What is it? Will it be the right place? Will it be able to hold a semi-large reservation on a Saturday night?

    \n On reflection, the Infernal is an interesting GM challenge. You really need to be thinking about how to use the Dark Power's Strings, since the sine qua non of Infernal-ness is that the Infernal's player is constantly giving those away. I wish there were a better mechanism for the Infernal's player to ask for a String to be spent. I'm thinking that letting an Infernal bargain with another PC - especially a Fae or something - would be a good way to reduce the GM's burden in keeping up with the Dark Power, who amounts to a substantial fraction of the Infernal's contribution to the fiction.

    \n Four MCs (Joe, Jackson, Carl, and Alan H) ran 2 games each for around 31 players total (not counting MCs), and Sunday Jackson ran a bonus game for 3 more. Joe revealed a printed copy of The Blood of Misty Harbour 3-part adventure, which promptly sold out. Tina's Tamales were delicious, but only available from 11:30-1:30.

    Eric L proved the Serpentine worked well for Dragons, and GHOST HORSE monster rock touring van made an off-screen cameo in my afternoon game, and was the focus of Joe's morning game I believe.

    Joe's afternoon game was for the "Actual People, Actual Play" crew who came up from LA, and after Endgame closed at 7 they
    recorded a podcast (which I'm looking forward to very much) with Joe, then joined Eric and I for Le Cheval, where five of seven ordered the claypot rice and Joe rescued and repurposed a mylar helium balloon. Ten or so had earlier gone to Breads of India, and we rejoined at The Trappist, taking over their back room, drinking fine beer, looking up, and thrilling to Morgan E's Star Wars Saga Epic.

    It was superfun. The book looks great, and Endgame had plenty on hand to sell (along with Perfect Unrevised).

    Joe and Jackson will be at Kublacon Memorial Day weekend, and I'll be there running as much Monsterhearts in Open Gaming as I can manage.

    \n At The Trappist last night, Jonathan raised a glass and said it best -

    "To teenagers, and other monsters."

    These three games over the last two days have left me happy and exhausted. So much pain, anger, too-short sweetness and overdue catharsis hit those tables that the mist is still, even now, dispersing.

    There were dozens of lesser evils sided with, and several greater; parties were attended, classrooms were escaped from, bullies were confronted, clothes were fumbled out of.

    There were too many stand-out moments to recount. There was nothing that remained pure, but there was one sort-of unhappy werewolf girl who blew every roll in the game except the one that mattered - and even that sweetness (picture them after, with feathers in each of their hair from the torn pillow, lying exhausted on the bed of the mom and dad of whoever's throwing this party) had a bitter aftertaste.

    Thanks, Joe. Thanks, Carl, and Endgame. Thanks, Joe & Morgan & Monica & Monte & Sev & Karen & John & Colin & June & Ethan & Chris. This was really something special.
