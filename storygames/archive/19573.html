    I'm sure there are many connections between the two scenes.
    \nActually, there's very little. We're aware of the thriving Australian con circuit for theatre-style larps (or freeforms as they call them) but there's very little cross-Tasman traffic. We don't see Australians at Chimera or Hydra, and there's not a culture of kiwis going the other way to try their cons (despite their good reputations). Our authors don't talk or exchange games. I'm aware of one kiwi larp being run at an Australian con (Ryan Paddy's "Black Hart of Camelot" at SydCon 2011). I'm not aware of any Australian games being run here (and yes, I do track these things).

    As for reasons, I've hunted for info on the Australian freeform scene as part of my programme to acquire international games and see how other people do it. But they're largely invisible outside the cons, and its difficult to track down their authors. They don't have a forum, they don't publish their work, and so as far as the net is concerned they don't exist. Its frustrating; we have better contacts with the UK and Intercon larp cultures than we do with our nearest neighbour.

    (And wearing my official NZLARPS hat, I'm happy to provide information on the New Zealand larp scene, or connect you with authors. Or you could just go directly to Diatribe and ask there)
