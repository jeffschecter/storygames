\n I'm Jenn from the Jennisodes and I've created an awesome storytelling roleplaying game called "Project Ninja Panda Taco". We have put together a fabulous kickstarter with some great videos showing the artwork and an example of how a turn of play works.

In this game you get to play a Mastermind trying to take over the world and a Minion would loves to collect shiny toolbelt items. To take over the world you must complete your three step plan and thwart your nemesis. This is a fast paced, low prep, super fun and collaborative game that will make you laugh out loud and use funny voices. Project Ninja Panda Taco is designed for 4 to 6 players and a game lasts about 2 hours. The team consists of Daniel Solis doing layout, Brian Patterson doing art, and John Adamus doing the editing. Check it out and let me know what you think!

    \n I'd be more than happy to discuss the breakdown of the campaign and my thoughts on reaching the goal.

    I started off with my project knowing I wanted a full coler, hard cover book that is approximately 8"x8" and 32 pages. This size and type of book needs to be customized which brought up the cost. I decided to go with a full print run of the book at 1,000 copies. Ordering a smaller print run or going Print On Demand were not cost effective.

    I also wanted to pay the people working on the project fair rates. Project Ninja Panda Taco has a lot of art and layout that goes into it. I also hired an editor who helped during the design process and the writing. Brian Patterson, Daniel Solis and John Adamus are fabulous people and I want to do right by them.

    Another big issue I've seen with other kickstarters is shipping. I've seen books arrive with bent corners or damaged packaging. While I can't hand deliver every book I plan on using better packaging so that the books arrive in pristine condition. Adding up the printing, art, layout, editing and packaging the breakdown is around $8500.

    Once I added up these costs I had to estimate the costs of the different pledge levels. Lastly I had to add in marketing (ad banners), taxes, the kickstarter and Amazon fees (~5% each) and contingency.

    While a $12,500 goal may seem high and ambitious I felt I could do it based on the game itself and my involvement in the community. Watching how well other kickstarters in the community have done I want my project to succeed and create a beautiful amazing product and give back to the awesome people who have worked so hard on it. Just like my podcast, this game is a labor of love but I also have to look at it from a business standpoint and where all the costs factor into it.

    This whole process has been an interesting journey and I'm excited to see it finish successfully! Playing a Mastermind taking over the world is a ton of fun.
