\n I usually write an AP to point out which elements of a particular game work well and which rules are a bit obscure to me and thus cause problems when incorporating into play. But this time it\u2019s only to share the love and what I try to say here is: go and play \u201cLove in the Time of Sei\xf0\u201d because you will have a great time.
\n
\nI facilitated \u201cLove in the Time of Sei\xf0\u201d at one of the conventions (Inne Sfery) in Wroc\u0142aw, Poland last weekend as one of the games on demand. I played it with complete strangers, so it was even more exciting as everything they did was a huge surprise to me. It took us three hours (including explaining the rules).
\n
\nFirst, it\u2019s great that despite the setting being determined, there are a lot of things to come up with along the play, for example how exactly the magic of Sei\xf0kona works. So we started with pinpointing the sei\xf0 and we assigned it to the stars and reading future and recalling past form them. (Oh, imagine the great colour scene with Sei\xf0kona wearing long dress not embroidered with stars but made of real stars!) Then we created a story incorporating some Slavic myths into the Nordic legend by adding the fern flower which is said to blossom only once a year. We made it a symbol of the kingdom and it was forbidden to cut the flower. So the Earl obviously did it. After this we could immediately switch to the King\u2019s hall to see the crown falling down from his head and right to the flashback with him being crowned by Sei\xf0kona. But as this might not be quite so easy! it turned out that Sei\xf0kona somehow made a mistake pronouncing the crowning formula. The effect was terrifying \u2013 without her the King had no power and authority. So she became his lover to save the kingdom. But the effort of the crowing formula exhausted her making her unable to perform it once again. So she knew that to anoint the new king new sei\xf0kona would be needed. But the Princess cannot become one because she lost her virginity with the Kinght. Sounds great, doesn\u2019t it? Then we had even more twists and ended up with the marriage of the Princess and the Eral (being an incarnation of or being blessed by one of the Kings who betrayed his country) and losing the power of sei\xf0 forever. It gave us, the players, great feeling that the old time of some myths and legends is over.
\n
\nIt\u2019s perfect game to teach people how the multiform really works. We had no problem switching between scenes in character and the out of character negotiations. Phrases and cards really support that way of playing. And what\u2019s even more important to me, they help players to create the story together. By controlling themes (they work even better than the elements in Archipelago II to my mind) and events all participants stay concentrated also when not in spotlight. Plus it enhances players\u2019 creativity giving them motifs to get inspired.
\n
\nIt also seems to me that there is a lot of material we just didn\u2019t manage to use at all. Some questions were not answered, some were not even asked, some themes were not exposed. I can imagine playing that game again and having a completely different story. And I\u2019m going to check if it\u2019s so soon!
