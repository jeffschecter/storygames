\n I'm GMing several players (7 or 8 depending on the day) in a 4e d20 campaign.
\nI did the usual, "give me your character's history" spiel.
\n
\nOnly one player gave me a history. So, rather than bog down play at the table exploring this guy's history, I suggested doing a bit of play-by-email between sessions.
\n
\nI'm not interested in using the 4e rules for pbem. (4e is for table top mini-combat, and for this it works great.)
\n
\nI have a decent kicker from him, which I've gone ahead and build up quite a bit of backstory.
\n
\nSo, what system would you recommend for this pbem?
\n
\nI started with the IAWA mechanic-- and rather than explain the rules, I just sort of go to the dice when I think there is conflict. He picked up on the nature of negotiations quickly.
\n
\nHere's the issue, IAWA kind of stalls out when there is no urge to get on the Owe list, and much of the back story is already filled out by me, so that is a bit of a constraint. Plus, I have some implied Best Interests, from him, given his kicker, but nothing is super clear. (His kicker is mostly a mystery--his guy has missing memories and such).
\n
\nI'd like to try Sorcerer, but that would require defining humanity and demons-- which is more than I think he's ready to deal with.
\n
\nSuggestions?

    \n I'd avoid combats entirely, and try to just talk out history without any "play." If you do want to actually play out the backstory, stick to 4e but avoid all combat situations. You know the character won the fight, right, because he's still alive in present-day. You might use skill challenges for other stuff but, really, these sound like players who don't want a lot of hassle for their fun. Keep it simple. Just talk out their awesome backstory.

    \n Are you asking how to resolve the changing backstory/get going or what system/if any to use to resolve events?

    On the second, it likely should be a largely narrative system that doesn't give any gains measured in the 4e scale so it doesn't throw him out of whack with rest of Players in that venue. The Pool from James West might be simple way to handle that; each "session" he can reintepret his pool stats riffing off his 4e stats for "what traits are featured this time". http://www.randomordercreations.com/rpg.html
