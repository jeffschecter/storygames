\n I love CAPES, and i started from there to design a hack, based on a french comic taking place in WWI with some proto super-heros.

There are some substancials modifications to make to the original system, so here are my concerns

- in this setting, there is no asymetric "drives", nor good vs evil. Instead of this, each PC have mixed motivation (ie "good" AND "bad") . I'm not sure it would work as well as with antagonists drives.

- Drives should be differents but i can't see clearly where's the border between Traits and Drives. For exemple, "Patriotism" should be a Drive in this context, but it's usually a Trait

There are some more important modifications i'd want to add in order to give to the player more tools to build a story, a skeleton.

a) Meta-plots:


pre-made meta-plot could be the equivalent to a scenario: a set of "thematic directions": non-human characters, that could be used by all the players during all the game (but it's not clear how it would be introduced) are some ideas :

- the where/who/when (etc...) of the scene would have to be created throught non-human characters sheets
- When opening a new scene, some of these Non-human Characters would have to be re-used to give a sense of continuity
- Players could have to pay a tax (or earn a gain) depending if they introduce the same / close / independant element. The more there are scene, the higher the would be the cost.


b) "solids" consequences

Let's say you win the goal "Take Paris" or "Kill the Kaiser". In CAPES, you will narrate this a fact. In this case, it's easy to figure out. But sometimes it can be tricky to define where are the limits for the narrator about what he can state. There is also an another problem: a lot of event produce a lot of consequences:

- not easy to remember
- and that could lead to others consequences.

When a goal/event is over, players have to create the consequences as a non-human character. The "winner" would have more control on this process, the the other players should be able to create minor consequences.

The consequences are "remanent" and can have effect during various pages or scene (some traits can be more remanent than others). During the game, the player could also "feed" the consequences", or let them loose strenght.

For exemple, "fall of Paris" is an important event, that should have consequences during at least the next pages. To ensure that, we create a "consequences non-human character". The winner give a 6 to "Chaos and disorder", a 5 to "temptation for surrender", etc...

During the next pages, this Non-human PC is still there, and every player can use it anytime. during the next page, all the Traits are loosing 1 point (unless someone have feeded one of them winning a related event)

As usual, sorry for my english & welcome to your ideas...
