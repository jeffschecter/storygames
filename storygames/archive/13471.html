What do you write when you sign your games?

\n
\n
\n
\n\nGraham \n\n\n
\n
\n\nDecember 2010 \n edited December 2010 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n This is a completely trivial question, of course, but what do you write when someone asks you to sign your game?

Let's take the person's name and your name for granted.

For Play Unsafe, I write:

This is Control. Please report.

Enjoy your games.

and explain that the "Control" line is what I say in Lacuna, which is the game I reference in the introduction.

For my Cthulhu scenarios, I need three different things to write, one for each scenario. So I tend to write:

Go mad and die!
Go crazy!
Drive yourself insane!

Also, when I was signing 50 limited editions in the Pelgrane offices, I wrote the following on one copy:

Help! I am trapped in the Pelgrane offices. Please alert the authorities.

What do you write?

    \n For most of the Dresden books I sign, on Book One I sign on signature facing the inside cover:

    [Name, verified],
    Only you can be trusted
    with this tome of power!
    - Ryan Macklin '10

    Which will be '11 next year. A habit I got from watching Fred Hicks sign books.

    For Book Two, I sign next to my first credit on the credits page "Behind you!" and the signature.

    That was the format the seven of us started sticking to at Origins, that we would write something bigger on Book One and smaller on Book Two, unless we only got one book to sign.

    For Finis, I always did something custom, a line as though from the book

    [Name, verified],
    You decide to [custom line]...
    - Ryan Macklin

    I also once signed as Mike Mearls, as a joke. By the book owner's request.

    - Ryan

    Edit: If I like you, I'll threaten the use of other body parts for use with signing.
