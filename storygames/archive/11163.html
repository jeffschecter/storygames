\n So I've recently had a minor financial windfall (holiday bonus at my job) and while it's still entirely possible that I may need to spend this on essential items for life, I'm hoping to be able to put some of it toward fun crap. And I realized: I haven't bought any new RPGs for like a year! Partially because I've been buying more board games, partially because I've been running the same RPG with two friends since spring (and I have been buying supplements for that). But anyway, I think it may be time to rectify this, but I've been kind of out of the loop, haven't been hanging around S-G as much as I used to.

So what's hot and awesome these days? What should I be excited about? Who should I give some of my money to immediately?

    \n My picks from this year's loot are as follows, especially focusing on titles that to me seem like ones you might not have considered yet:\n
      \n
    • S/lay w/ me and Trollbabe from Ron Edwards are both excellent titles in different ways. SwM is the smallest viable sword & sorcery game I've seen - very experimental and quirky, sure to provoke ideas; two-player game, very intimate. Trollbabe is the premier choice for an introductory vanilla narrativist game, with the sort of hippy Nordic fantasy setting I can get behind.
      • \n
    • James Raggi makes artistic, original old school D&D adventure modules, starting this year. They're cheap and very ambitious, making for excellent reading for the ideas even if you don't run them as is. This year's fare includes heavy metal fantasy, historical comedy of manners and a traphouse dungeon.
      • \n
    • MSG\u2122 is an under-appreciated game of cynical, idealistic corporate cyberpunk. I especially like the scenario-based design: half of the book consists of ready-made scenarios you just plug and play in an episodic manner.
      • \n
    • Misery Bubblegum is a nigh-impenetrable text, but I think the game itself shows great promise. The cards are pretty, too, so it's justified as a boardgame purchase, too.
      • \n
    • Time & Temp is an original, under-publicized game of time travel adventure - reminds me of Dr. Who, Quantum Leap and other time travel tv shows. The well-considered, focused simulationistic design is a breath of fresh air in a market dominated by generic, unfocused simulationism.
      • \n
    \nAlso, my own World of Near is starting to seem like an overlooked gem - I think it's the best book of its kind out there right now, but nobody seems to be reading it. I'm subjective about it, obviously, and I'm starting to see in hindsight that the book is pretty complex and dense, perhaps too much so for the average audience not obsessed with the world of Near.
