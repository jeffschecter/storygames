    \n I'm not sure how do it, but we have yet to see a game that really addresses the core of the postmodern cyberpunk uncanny: the fractured and disposable identity. Cyberpunk RPGs are still full of beaming Golden Age heroes, Buck Rogering their around the slums and space. These new rules for me would emphasis the frailty and interdependent and intercorporeal character of existence. The flexibility and modularity of the technology would entail radical ruptures in personal and collective identities. I would ask, were are the contradictions and excesses of capital, were is the suffering, where are broken moments and uncanny alien operations of these spiritual machines? EP is too neat in that nauseating twee transhuman way. For all the historical tragedy in EP, it doesn't really seem that anyone really suffers. It's all too shiny and operatic.

    \n Eclipse Phase (EP) so needs this! It is awesome fictional background, but even though I love Shadowrun the EP rules don't inspire me at all.

    When I think of rules for this, I can't help thinking of the simplicity and snowballing of Apocalypse World. I also think keeping the core stats away from the sleeve is the way to keep the characters consistent across changing appearance. I started a hack, but got distracted on other things. Here is the only move I made (and it needs some work):

    When you resleeve into a new morph, roll+Morph Complexity.
    - 12+ best morph ever, -1 stress.
    - 10-11 minor integration issues take -1 forward.
    - 7-9 some integration issues, -1 ongoing for one day.
    - on a miss -1 ongoing for one day plus choose one:
    - Major Integration issues, -1 ongoing until resleeved instead of one day;
    - Alienation issues, +2 stress; or
    - Continuity issues, reduce stress maximum by 1.

    Flat +3
    Other biomorph +2
    Synth +1
    Swarm +0
    Combat models -1 on above
