    \n Well, I'd only met two of the others about 15 minutes before we started playing, so I don't really know their background, and for full disclosure, one of those two is the one who had expressed some reservations on the RPGG thread. The fellow who had played Fiasco before has played other games before -- He GM'ed a game of InSpectres at the previous year's con, so I knew him a bit, and the other player has a fair bit of role playing experience, including some Norwegian LARP (easy to do when you live in Norway :-) ).

    As for me, I have a whole bunch of D&D experience, starting with basic D&D and stopping just as AD&D 2E was released. After a 20+ year hiatus, I've since played a season plus another pitch session of Primetime Adventures, and that previously mentioned InSpectres game from last year. But I've read a ton, and I think I had a good idea of what to expect.

    In the scene where my character got kidnapped, I went into the scene totally thinking I had the upper hand. But they grabbed a black die, and my upper hand ended up tied behind my back. I very clearly remember thinking to myself, "Hey, that's not right! No, wait: suck it up, and roll with it." So, yeah, that'll happen, and you and your group will need to work it out.

    But I think we all were pretty OK with that. The group issue we faced was more story pacing. There was one specific confrontation that was going to occur, and it nearly occurred three or four times, but things in the story kept getting in the way. Some in the group felt that we should have just let it happen, and move on. I felt that the interruptions fit the genre pretty well, and they nicely escalated the tension in the story until the turning point occurred for the confronter. It's at a more foundational level than scene to scene issues, and I think it's more due to being new to each other, rather than being new to Fiasco.
