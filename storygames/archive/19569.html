\n Hey everyone I have been lurking around here for a while, too nervous to join in on any discussion. I take an epicurean approach to gaming recently I invited some of my improv buddies over to play One Last Job.

This game is a great genre emulator. Just reading the rules I could see the awesome melodramatic heist movie stories unfolding in my head. There are so many parts of the game that are totally diceless. Nine times out of ten failing a roll is more fun and interesting than succeeding.

I think my favorite part of the game is how it starts. The GM sets the stage for the life of a master thief after (s)he has retired. One of the players who is inspired by the setup jumps in as the thief. The Player and GM then play out a scene of someone approaching that thief to come out of retirement. The play then remarks (s)he will need a specialist to help with the job and sets the sage for a new character to be recruited. So the character creation process is actually a sereis of active scenes. Skills and abilities are decided in a similar fashion. They are created in the moment by the party doing flashbacks to demonstrate a character's skill.

I recorded an actual play of our experience with One Last Job for my RPG comedy show. Here is the link to the first part of the session.
