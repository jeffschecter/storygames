    \n I similarly ran a game in a theme park, but I was the employee of the theme park, it was a live action game and we actually used the park. Two of the other players were members of the theme park's security team, so the game's presence was acknowledged by anyone who could have been a potential threat to it's livelihood. In fact, the reverse occurred, and we got a number of the security guards to improvise and occasionally even "capture and interrogate" players/characters who's actions we too overt. The premise was that somewhere in the park was a demon feeding on fear, everyone expected it to be on one of the scary rides....but the demon was actually in the lost-and-found area, feeding on the fears of children who had lost their parents.

    Fifty players, twenty security guards, ten other park employees who knew what was happening and who were in on the act...all scattered among a few thousand innocent civilians for a session that lasted eight hours. It was awesome, you just can't create that vibe around a table.

    If we're just talking regular table-top based on a real-world location, that's most of my games. It makes research easy for GM and players.

    \n I try to set as many of my games as I can in my local area. I've played Pittsburgh 1,000 years ago, Pittsburgh 400 years from now, colonial Pittsburgh and modern Pittsburgh in various games at various times. I've even played western Pennsylvanian mice.

    The thing I love about locally-set games is the way it weaves story into your everyday landscape. One of my greatest moments in gaming came when my brother told me that he'd gone down to Station Square, and realized that the Ferryman would live in a wigwam built into the very structure he was walking through. That's the kind of effect I really aim for.

    \n Most of my games are location based, especially the historic ones. Also, when I go to conventions and run Trail of Cthulhu demos, I like to run a game set in that location. So I've got games for Indianapolis and Montreuil (near Paris).

    I also like to run games where I live in Southwark so I've done:
    - GURPS Goblins (The Royal School of Magic)
    - Time & Temp (Shakespeare at the Globe)
    - Buffy (Victorian setting)
    and others I can't think of at the moment.
