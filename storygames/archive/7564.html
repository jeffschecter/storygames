    \n It's one of the techniques right there in the section on Mythos Madness, page 77 if you have the book. They suggest, when a character goes bonkers, having the player of the character in question leave the room for a second, and decided something that's now true or untrue, unbeknowst to the player.

    So, for example, investigator sees a big horrible Mythos creature, rolls some dice, and snaps big. The player gets to leave the room, and then when he comes back, and they all run away, all the other players look at the player with concern, and ask if he's all right. He says he's fine, and they give him some strange looks, and drop the subject.

    Later, when he gets home, he discovers many of the pictures of himself also have this other woman he doesn't recognize. Friends ask him how "Sarah" is doing, and give him really weird looks when he asks who Sarah is. Finally, the police come around and ask him formally about the whereabouts of his wife.

    He probably begins to realize that he didn't just see that monster posing malevolently as the GM described, but rather, doing something so traumatic that he blocked out the relevant memories out of his mind.

    Basically, you retroactively change some fact about the character, or even the setting ("There's no such thing as squirrels" is an example straight from the book), and the player doesn't know. It's not a technique for everyone, certainly, but there's that great moment, as a player, when you walk back into the room, and know that something you think you know is wrong, and you have no idea what.
