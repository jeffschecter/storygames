\n The Kickstarter for Within the Devil's Reach is now live!

Hey guys, I wanted to let you know that I am putting together a book with three Dungeon World adventures set in the backwater town of Devil's Reach. The three adventures in Within the Devil's Reach are meant to be introductory adventures you can play through seporately or chain them together. Within the Devil's Reach is going to feature some great art from Storn Cook and awesome maps from Dyson Logos. If you want to know more about it I am posting some articles on my site about it.

Is anyone else thinking about writing adventures for Dungeon World? Is there anybody else crazy enough to try and tackle writing a campaign for Dungeon World?

    \n Is each plane like a dungeon? I usually think of planes as wide spanning things that have dozens if not hundereds of fronts with their own impending doomsa going on at any given time but that might not be how Planescape planes were presented (never looked into anything to do with Planescape).

    There is something different about setting up a lot of little boulders that, with a little work, a GM can turn into an adventure and setting up one boulder that will roll and roll through adventure after adventure that require very little tweeking on the part of the GM. Granted I can see both as valuable but there is a definate difference.

    \n\nFebruary 2012 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n I'll explain it both places as a few different people have asked.

    Within the Devil's Reach is a single book with three adventures in it. Devil's Reach is planned to release in August so that I have time to make any changes based on the changes Sage and Adam make to Dungeon World in that time. The PDF of Devil's Reach will be going out to backers as soon as it is ready as well as a revised PDF with any needed changes at the time the books ship.

    Gears of the Worm God is a series of adventure books, including Within the Devil's Reach that will come out over the next year. Each of these books will be playtested before they go out and the backers will get their copies as soon as they are ready. All of the adventures in the Gears books are meant to be played as a campaign but each one can be pulled out and played by itself. The books will give guidence on using each adventure as it's own, seporate thing.
