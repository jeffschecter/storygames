\n The thread about teaching core story game techniques and all the Lady Blackbird outreach stuff got me thinking.

There are lots of people who'll be at GPNW who know particular games or interesting styles of play really, really well. But there will also be lots of people who are newer to story games, or who have played some story games but never touched others. Maybe they played lots of Wicked Age but never tried InSpectres or Polaris or Capes -- those are very different styles of play.

What if we came up with some flag on the schedule where people who know some systems really well can volunteer to not just run the game, but intentionally run/play the game with people who have no clue how that particular game/style works? I'm not talking about showcasing hot new games, but getting to learn some cool story fu by playing with people who know the secret crane techniques that go with particular staple story games. Hang out the "play and learn" shingle.

The flag would make it clear that the emphasis of the session was on beginners (in that game or style), to make it more welcoming and get everyone in same frame of mind.

Crazy? Unnecessary? Of course it's only a useful idea if anyone wanted to do this.

Myself, I think it could be super cool... NINJA cool.

    For those skill-building micro-workshops, the format would basically be:
    Introduction (1 minute)
    Statement of Purpose: Why is this important, and when? (2 minutes)
    Several good techniques, demoed (4 minutes)
    Several good techniques, discussed (4 minutes)
    Practice game (4-8 minutes)
    Wrapup (1 minute)
    So, like... roughly 20-25 minutes all told, with the chance to participate in technique demos, discuss approaches, and learn a new roleplaying poem / learning game / teaching game.
