\n I purchased Juggernaut at GenCon 2015 and have read the cards to understand the rules and possible content that could come up in play.

But I am concerned that there is a lacuna in the rules as stated because I assume that running the LARP effectively may rest on a foundation of experience or technique I may not currently possess.

I've decades of GM experience and play experience with a number of story games. But running LARPs are a new territory for me. I've read of some of the innovation in Jeepform but I have no direct experience with most of them.

Can anyone who has experience with Juggernaut (play or facilitating) offer advice on which LARP or GM techniques are most appropriate to apply when facilitating? Or if there is are excellent LARP facilitator resources I should become familiar with to prepare myself?

Thank you.

    \n Copied here:

    "First of all, you'll be fine. It isn't really that hard, and you get to play, too. Your main role is to organize the game, conduct an initial briefing and safety talk, and make sure everybody is OK when the game is over. During play it is self-paced and everyone's motivations are clear. I think it is a good transitional game for tabletop-comfortable people into live action.

    As facilitator, as you play you;ll want to keep a meta-level eye on everyone to make sure they are doing OK, and maybe goose the game if things are getting boring - in JUGGERNAUT that's easy; just press the button.

    If you are interested in Larp, live action, freeform stuff, check out Lizzie Stark's blog Leaving Mundaina. But again, you've got this! \ufeff"
