\n In Europe, in the early 19th century, a little-known group was formed. The members called themselves the Society of Dreamers. The Society believed that there were autonomous life forms living in people\u2019s dreams. They called these creatures mnemosites.

In this game, you and your friends play the Society of Dreamers, seeking to uncover the nature of the mnemosites with tools of science and mysticism. You will witness the childhood and youth of the members; the forming of the Society; and their investigation and \u2013 possibly \u2013 understanding of the mnemosite phenomenon.

The game is written for 2-5 players and takes an evening to play. There\u2019s no game master, and no need to prepare anything (beyond reading the rules).

    \nPlay progresses through four acts:
    - The pregame, in which we learn about the characters' lives before they formed the Society;
    - The exposition, in which the Society learns more about the mnemosites;
    - The weaving, in which the fates of the Society and the mnemosites are inextricably connected;
    - The ending, in which we see what happens when the Society faces the mnemosites, and what turns their lives took afterwards.

    Much of the game uses a scene structure where one player acts as instructor, while the others use a ouija-board-like mechanism to decide what type of scene will be played out.

    During the game, facts about the mnemosites will be generated - usually one per scene. In the weaving phase, those are re-incorporated into the story. Nobody, neither the players nor the characters, know the nature of the mnemosites before the game begins. This exploration is what the game revolves around.

    The stories are often of delusion, beauty, madness, surrealism and the inner world bleeding into the outside.

    [EDIT] Oh, and yes, I did include two short bonus games in the back of the book: "Five Characters in Search of an Identity" and "The Orc in the Well". They're both quick & fun; the first one is pretty existential, and the last one is often surprisingly poetic.
