    \n I assume from the existing suits that this is a fantasy setting?

    Typical tarot decks usually have very simple symbols for the minor arcana, are you sure you want dragons in there? Also, how keen are you on getting the traditional symbolisms of the tarot correct? It's a lot more complex than many people think.

    Other common suits in various tarot decks I've seen include spears, shields, rods, staves, pentacles, stars, cups, chalices, moons, discs and wands. The commonest in the decks I've seen are swords and cups. Of the other two suits, one will usually be disk-like and the other long and rod-like, hence the variety of round and long, straight symbols.

    You've broken quite firmly from tradition with Dragons, which throws me for a loop a bit. I suppose you could have Unicorns, being a common 'opposite' for dragons. The difficulty with both dragons and unicorns is going to be that it's difficult to represent them in small, iconic form due to their complexity, especially when you compare them to swords and particularly to moons which will likely be no more than a circle or crescent. This could make your suits look odd when placed side by side.

    As for symbols, let me know when you've decided which suits to go for and I might be able to help.

    \n The Tarot suits symbolize, among other things, the four Greek elements (cups=water, wands=air, etc. IIRC) This helps make the cards more evocative. You might consider something similar. Maybe clich\xe9 RPG stats -- STR, DEX, INT, WIS?

    Symbolically, it seems you've got suits that are aggressive/masculine, fiery/acquisitive, and celestial/feminine. You might want an earthy suit, maybe a gemstone? (Come to think of it, you've then got suits for the four clich\xe9 races: men=sword, elves=moon, orcs=dragon, dwarves=gem.)

    You could make dragons work as a suit icon if you pick a relatively simple symbol to use, like a slit-pupiled eye, or a claw. For instance, the Decktet (a really fabulous new Tarot-like card deck) has "Wyrms" as one of its suits, whose icon is a curled-up lizard in the form of a green spiraled circle:
