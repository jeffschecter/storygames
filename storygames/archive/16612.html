    \n I've only played Itras by once, but I think that was enough to convince me it's a great game.

    The surreal setting and the card mechanic form a lovely union: the Chance Cards may ask you to rewind time and have something happen in an opposite way, change your characters gender, talk to your shadow while time has stopped, or change roles with each other for the rest of a scene, sending your role one step to the right (the role of game master not excluded).

    I guess the game can be described as Archipelago II but with a GM instead of phrases to guide play, no destiny points, a given (very cool) setting and setting-specific Fate Cards.

    \n It is also EXCELLENT for experimenting with new techniques. Due to the fertile setting and the surrealist nature of the game, it's very easy to get inspired to try new things, and the game is pretty much impossible to break.

    One tip for new players: Don't try to get too weird right away - it's good to have some sort of stable consensus reality to begin with.
