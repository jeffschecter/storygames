\nHi StoryGamers,

Well, my game is finally complete and I've released it out into the wild so I wanted you guys to be among the first to know. I really love this site for all the discussions that pop up on it. I've found a lot of interesting things here ever since joining, so I first want to say thanks for giving me something to read instead of do work!

On to the main attraction: God-king is a roleplaying game that focuses heavily on creating an exciting story experience for a group of three to five players and a Narrator. The resolution mechanics are playing-card-based and most of the rules are designed to push the story in interesting directions. One of the more interesting things about the game is that each player has to take on a specific role that makes them explicitly integral to the adventure (only one player can, respectively, heal others, be the group translator, most experienced warrior, etc.). Everyone relies on each other for some important task but this idea of having to work together in order to have any chance of surviving is tested heartily because everyone has their own motives for joining the adventure.

There's a lot more to God-king - importantly, the actual story that frames the events - however I do not want to yammer on too much. Hopefully you all will be willing to give it a try. I am excited to hear what everyone has to say about the game.

You can get it at my website: http://spiffup.org/godking for $5 USD as a digital download (full color, 40 pages, PDF, immediate download).

Please let me know if you have any questions or comments about the game. I will be more than happy to respond to you.

Thank you for taking the time to check out the game,
-Daniel

    \n Thank you, Jonathan! I appreciated your comments in regards to the previous drafts.

    Probably the biggest changes are in the Journey and Narrator sections. The "Journey" section, which houses most of the story and adventure details, has a new sub-section regarding the "end-game" of the journey, so-to-speak. The descriptions of the locations and inhabitants of the world were also cleaned up and modified to include some important details that I believe were not in the playtest version. The Narrator section is now a lot clearer than it was before and covers important aspects like choosing which roles are appropriate, as a Narrator, to offer to the group when you have less than five players. The game also includes flavor text in the form of journal-type quotes that help to reveal more about the world - these are peppered throughout the book. Finally, Knowledges (a character's skills, basically) were given a sub-section in the Mechanics chapter with rules in regards to how they can modify your Hand. Aside from those, the text was simply cleaned up and revised in some smaller places that were not initially fixed.

    For the most part, the game plays the same way as it did before and the basic text that was in the previous Playtest Draft was just fixed up a bit and added on to. The new sections server to add more depth to the story and mechanics of the game and also help to clarify the Narrating process. Oh, I nearly forgot, the book layout and design is completely different from the Playtest Drafts.

    I think that about covers it. I don't have a copy of the playtest draft handy at the moment, but I'm pretty sure my memory serves me right.

    If you have any other questions, do feel free to ask them.

    \n It slows down based on how many cards of a certain type you have versus the type of scene that you feel like framing. It happened to me when I played it for the Mom's Basement podcast.
