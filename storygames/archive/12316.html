\n I mean, I know that in Dogs, that advice is aimed at the GM.

But, are there games (or play approaches, or whatever) that specifically suggest to the player "accept what the GM says, or roll against it"?
I strikes me that I've never felt, as a player, like that advice was directed at me.

As a player, I've always felt like my options were "roll the dice, or figure out how to get what you want without rolling".

So, yeah: is "roll dice or say yes" directed at players too?
In some games? In all of them?
Implications?

    \n I'd like to think that it's directed at everyone at the table. It might have something to do with what precedes the decision. If the GM (or player) is asking a question, like "How do you want to enter the fort?" or "Do you want to enter the fort?" then it does not apply.

    When they state a fact, then you agree to it or you make a contest out of it. "The cute little daughter of the sheriff convinces you to marry her." or "My character blows open the mouth of the cave with dynamite." then it's time for everyone at the table to nod their heads or pick up some dice, most often accompanied by "The hell you do!"

    Some games (Dogs in the Vineyard, Misspent Youth) really benefit from getting a (playfully) angry reaction to kick-start the dice. However, some games lend themselves to a more subtle language.

    For me, the broader lesson is that Actions taken by the GM or the Players should have an impact, which should be measurable. Make bold assertions and take action. The balance to that is that [b]everyone[/b] needs to agree and support it, or offer up strong opposition to it. Lukewarm Actions and Oppositions just fall flat and waste time.

    So yeah, directed at the players and the GM.
