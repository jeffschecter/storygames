\n Hello!

I played a LOT of D&D last year. Probably some of the best D&D I've ever played. I took a lot of inspiration from Eero's threads about the game, and he was good enough to answer a lot of my questions. I also read bunches of Jack Vance, and I feel like I FINALLY understood the aesthetic a lot of early D&D modules were trying to achieve.

Mostly I ran modules, some classics, as well as some new modules. I especially liked Raggi's "The Tower of the Stargazer". I also wrote a few adventures of my own.

Now, I've written up some of my most successful adventures into modules. The Cosmic Trader is the first of these. It's about a strange jeweler's shop in a small town, which turns out to be the gateway to a much larger and even stranger adventure beyond. It's a short adventure - 11 pages, and about one session's worth of stuff to explore. It's perfect for dropping into a sandbox style game, where the players can either pick up the hook, or leave it for as long as they like. All for just $3.

I hope you'll go check it out, and tell me what you think!
