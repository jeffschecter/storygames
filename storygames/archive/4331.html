\n The recent thread on "Raising the Stakes" got me interested in what's new in the D&D house-rule scene, which used to be my bread and butter. There's always a good mix of people out there coming up with fringe, interesting stuff. Some jumping from link to link found me Ars Ludi, which is amazing, like "holy crap, I am now running a D&D game" amazing. From there, I found Jonathan Drain's d20 Source, which is pretty good.

What I'm looking for now are excellent web resources in this same vein. If you read a smart D&D blog, send it my way! Wikis and other web sites are great, too! If you have an example of excellent adventure writing, I want to see that. If I have to pay for it, that's ok!

I'm specifically looking for D&D stuff. d20 Modern or other d20 stuff is much less interesting, although d20 fantasy stuff might be promising. What do you guys know about that I'm missing?

    Posted By: Caesar SlaadArs Ludi is interesting. I've heard the idea of the virtual roll before... but funny how the principles that it informs are the very same principles that I was being chided over when I spoke up in support of the "roleplaying bonuses" mentioned in the Grimm podcast regarding SotC.
    \nIf it makes you feel any better Alan, while I liked some of the stuff I saw at Ars Ludi, I didn't like the virtual roll idea at all. I think you're exactly right that it works on the same principles as "bonus for roleplaying," and I still think it's problematic at best. Furthermore, the possibility of an extremely low result despite high levels of skill is an inescapable consequence of d20's core mechanic; if you don't like that, it's probably better to address the issue directly by, e.g., using the 3d6 variant rule, rather than fixing every individual instance of it that annoys you. Or if you'd rather, at least apply the virtual roll idea consistently by having it apply to the fighter's attack based on the player's description as well.

    Personally, when I run D&D3.5 I use this home rule by the Order of the Stick guy for social conflict stuff, and I push for the same when I play.

    \n My absolute favorite monster for E6 is the Elf.
    \n
    \nWhy? Because someone pointed out to me that E6 explains why Elves haven't conquered the universe. Sure, they might have 30 feats, but they're 6th level mortals like the rest of us.
    \n
    \nBesides that, well, I think E6 does mooks very well also, as this thread on RPG.net demonstrates quite nicely:
    \n
    \nhttp://forum.rpg.net/showthread.php?t=354950
    \n
    \nBesides those... An Aboleth in E6 is really, really scary. Especially in a tanklike central pool in a oppressively quiet half-submerged underground city full of dessicated skum that's been cut off from the surface for thousands of years.
    \n
    \nManticores are also a nice pick; I find them one of my favorite monsters in D&D; period.
