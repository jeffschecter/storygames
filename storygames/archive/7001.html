    \n Plot/flex printing offers the best quality (you can submit vector images and the design is actually cut and applied), but the level of detail you are describing is going to require digital printing, where they transfer the whole design I think. Not sure if it could even be silkscreened.

    Only tangentially related, but extremely cool and game-worthy: Spreadshirt offers flex printing you can write on and erase, so you could make a character sheet shirt. I've used them for flex printing and they are great.

    \n Why not CafePress?\n
    Posted By: Ben LehmanIt's not going to be, well, legible at a distance. About 12 point font, I think.
    Then it had better be a sans serif, heavy-stroke font--12 points won't generally, digital silk screen (from my, admittedly somewhat dated, experience). See how big you can make it and still fit the space allotted by the printer. CafePress takes PNG, which can be vector-based, FWIW (i.e. don't have to pixellate to a raster format; should be saved in PNG as actual font strokes). For that matter, be sure your back logo/graphic/titles is also stroked/vector, when saved to PNG (i.e. forget about the JPG option at CP).
