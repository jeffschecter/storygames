\n If you were running a con game and wanted to do a quick exploration of the rules, but also have a lasting mark on your character for your experience (ala Dogs), what would you do?

Now, admittedly, I'm stealing a portion of this from Judd. But! I wish to expand on it. And here's how I'd imagine it going.

Have a collection of Keys and Secrets on cards. Give them to the players to look at. Ask them to choose a Secret and a Key based on this:

Choose a Secret that you want your character to have. We're going to have a scene where you try to learn it. However, since you might not get it, I want you to also have a Key in mind that will relate to how you feel having not learned that Secret if you were unsuccessful. That is, either you are successful and get the Secret, or you develop a strong mindset that originates from your experience of trying but not succeeding.

Does that make sense? Would it be unfun? I have doubts, but I thought I'd share.

    Posted By: DanielSolisFor those of us who are spring chickens, could someone explain Dogs' style initiation?
    \nDaniel: You do this thing where each character gets one scene. Their first scene as dogs, and it's their initiation. The player decides a sentence, like "I hope I was able to save that guy", or "I hope I am able to get this bitter person back to the light". Then the GM makes this contest about that very thing, filling in all the details.

    For the example of "I hope I am able to save that guy", it might be another kid in camp who accidentally dropped his gun and shot himself, or got dysentery, or got mauled by a bear, and you are trying to save him. You give it your all, putting as much into the conflict as you want. It's effectively an Intro Mission where the player learns the basics of the rules.

    Then at the end, whether or not the character fails, she gets a new trait either way: In the above example, she would get "I was able to save that person" or "My hands work miracles" or "I know about healing"; and if she failed she might get "I wasn't able to save that guy", or "Horace's death weighs on my shoulders", or "Scared of blood" or "I can't heal people". Either way something goes on the sheet.
