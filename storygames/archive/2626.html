\n I got my first chance to play Agon yesterday evening. Unfortunately time was limited, so we didn't get to complete the quest, but hopefully we'll get to that tonight or tomorrow, and once we do I'll try to post an actual play report. Anyway, a couple simple questions popped up during the play. I checked the FAQ at the wiki, but didn't see answers to these particular questions.
\n
\n1) In a simple contest that is against an existing NPC, how do you determine the Glory reward for the highest-rolling protagonist? I'm assuming you don't base it on how much Strife was used to create the NPC - that'd be an awful lot, potentially. And an NPC built using the rules in the book won't necessarily have a pool that corresponds to the table of dice bought up from 2d6 with Strife - for example, an NPC can easily have a name die of d4 and an ability of d10, which has no equivalent on the Glory table.
\n
\n2) When creating a new hero, the book says to give them a number of free ability levels equal to half the number of quests the previous hero completed. Should this be rounded up or down? And, more importantly, are these free ability levels in addition to the 2 that every starting character gets, or do you just get a number equal to half your completed quests?
\n

    I don't have a facile answer for 1 ("Find the level on the chart for the appropriate total dice sides" is fairly close, though).

    \n

    But for 2, I think that you shouldn't add those inherited levels to the 2 that a starting-from-scratch hero gets; those (in my interpretation) represent the levels that he inherited from his predecessor. We assume that never-played predecessors are respectable but not exceptional.

    \nThe way I handled it was to "normalize" the NPC's pool by stepping the dice sizes towards each other. In the d4 + d10 example above, you can bring the d4 up to d6 and the d10 down to d8, giving you d6 + d8. You're right though that you get the same result by totaling the dice sides.
