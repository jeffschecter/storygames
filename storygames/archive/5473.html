\n Hi all,

I made a french translation of IAWA's oracles. The PDF can be downloaded here :
oracles french translation

I have my own reasons for the translation choices but I welcome any advice you can give me : choice of words, error on the meaning of a phrase, litterary value, etc...

What gave me the most difficulties was to try to keep the gender ambiguity of some characters. Some translation notes mention it, but I also included an advice explaining this problem and I advise people on feeling free to question the implied gender of most of the characters.

Specificaly, one oracle was very difficult to translate :
In the God-Kings of War oracle :
10 of Hearts : "a tender of war-bulls, shaved-headed and fearless."

I have problems with the word "tender". Does the sentence imply a man, a woman ? What does a "tender" do ? Is it an employee, a healer, some kind of servant, a waitress ? If you can offer me some advice regarding the meaning of this sentence, I'd be gratefull.

Thanks all,
Antoine
