\n I've read the PDQ core rules and love them.

I'm going to be running some "demo" games at my FLGS. SotC, Thousand Suns, and possibly Donjon. If I end up buying ZoZ, I'll probably try to run it, too.

Now... if no one plays, or if it's the only time I ever play that game, is it going to be worth the $30? I mean in that, "well, damn, I really loved reading that rule book" kind of a way. It seems like with the extensive discussion of fairy tales, I would like it even just for the read... but it's hard to say.

And as for actual play...

Has anyone actually played? Good? Bad?

    \n Currently we're playing session 4 of probably about 6 or 7.

    Its been very fun. I was surprised at how evocative the character sheet and doing the "once upon a time" previous session summaries are. They really get folks into the play mindset.

    We've had a few questions about magic and its cost/catch. The book explains it, but individual magic traits always need a little thinking to make them work just right.

    I also had to adjust my GMing style to give folks more stuff to fail at, so they could gain more learning points, and advance. I tend to want to see my players succeed, but in some cases, making their characters fail was more fun for them than triumphing after a struggle.

    The damage trait/story hook is great especially if your players want more narrative control.

    For our group, the game has been a blast, and very happy/upbeat. I'd play it again in a heartbeat, and I'd certainly run it for a demo.

    The only thing I'm not really sure about is if it works well long term. I'm guessing that higher "level" characters are going to probably get a bit tired, since the game is really driven by a heavy failure/light success need, and it may be hard for really advanced characters to fail at much.

    Jeff
