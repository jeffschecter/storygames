\n Has anyone else here read Cathy's Book? I'm reading it now, and... I just want to make more stuff like this.

Cathy's Book is an ARG (alternate reality game) with the book as entry point. While reading, you'll find phone numbers and references to stuff on the 'net. Calling those numbers and visiting the sites will give you more insight into the fictional events.

And there are handouts. OMGZ. An envelope full of old newspaper clippings, cards, a paper napkin... cool stuff.

    \n Amazon has Cathy's Book: If Found Call 650-266-8233 by Sean Stewart and Jordan Weisman in hardcover for only $6 or paperback for $10. I didn't know about the sequel Cathy's Key: If Found 650-266-8202 until this thread, so many thanks to Matthjis for mentioning it.

    Sean Stewart's an amazing writer but apparently Alternate Reality Games pay much better than writing midlist meaning of life thrillers. His non-trilogy Resurrection Man, The Night Watch, and Galveston were a huge influence in how I think about magic in RPGs in a modern setting, at least for those parts of my brain not already in thrall to Tim Powers. Sadly out of print, but not hard to find.

    And I totally want Sweet Agatha, from the snippets I've heard.

    Cory Doctorow's latest (and excellent) book about teen hackers vs. the Department of Homeland Security, Little Brother starts off with an ARG called Harajuku Fun Madness that sounds like big fun, if it weren't fictional. There's a pretty good description of how ARGs work in chapter 1. Like all his books, it's also available free online under a creative commons license.
