\n What is Delve, I hear you ask? It's an adventure game that delivers classic dungeon crawl action blended with new-school narrative control. No more wandering in circles, erasing holes in your graph paper, or wasted hours lost on tedious secret door checks. A dungeon isn't a place, it's a plot, and Delve lets you get right to the action by dispensing with the map altogether.

Best of all, the Delve Adventurer's Guide, which fits on the front and back of three sheets of paper, is absolutely free at parenthesispress.com/delve, along with a free one-page starter dungeon (the Warrens of Woe), and you can get the Delve Gamemaster's Guide at Distribly for just $5. Grab your sword and kick down that door!

    \n That's where the narrative control via the two colors of dice come in: the players have a pretty good chance to actually decide details for themselves. I'm of the belief that players are in the long run better at choosing the things that interest them than somebody else would be. Do you want this dungeon to be an undead bash? Then you're going to say the monster's a zombie when you get the narration. Are you questing for a magic sword? Then you can find it when the treasure's yours to determine. (Those last two actually happened in my playtest sessions at Gen Con and Metatopia last year.) Once players got over the [very shallow] learning curve, they were wholly engaged.
