\n When you play a rodent, in a game, do you ever adopt rodent-like mannerisms?

For example, if you're playing Mouse Guard, or a Redwall hack for Ironclaw, or Big Ears, Small Mouse, do you find yourself emphasizing your front teeth, or sniffing a lot, or making quick little movements, or cocking your head, or talking in a squeaky voice?

What if that was part of the game's rules? Do you think it would pretty much kill any chance of a serious game? Or do you think you'd get used to it?

    Posted By: JohnstoneWhen I played Mouse Guard we didn't add any mouse-y stuff to our role-playing, no. We were well aware of being little mice, but we still acted like people.
    \nJohnstone won embodiment every session, I think.

    The thing about Mouse Guard is that the dualism of Mouse Nature is a big part of the game, mechanically. I think I'd only ever "be mousey" in that game if I was trying to show that my character was losing grip on what made him part of civilization. You know, looking at a 6-Nature mouse with a bunch of checks, I'd just be nervous and jumpy and afraid of stuff but not necessarily like, being squeaky and stuff. It's just not really part of the milieu.

    Maybe in another game, maybe not. I don't tend to enjoy "silly" roleplaying and that's how, I think, this would feel.
