Classes cuing other classes to be awesome.

\n

\n
\n While working on a little comedic D&D game, I had this image of a fighter clumsily stumbling into a trap that only the thief can disarm or the thief blundering into a regiment of kobolds that only the fighter can defeat in combat. So, I came up with this little engine for D&D-style classes to cue other classes to be awesome.

How to Play
You'll need a supply of Fudge dice, but d6s will work, too. On your turn, roll 3dF as many times as you want, collecting as many blanks as you can.

Danger
If you get four or more +s or four or more -s, you must stop rolling because you are now in danger. You get get danger points based on your class.

Fighter:
For each -, get a Magical danger point.
For each +, get a Finesse danger point.

Thief:
For each -, get a Force danger point.
For each +, get a Magical danger point.

Mage:
For each -, get a Finesse danger point.
For each +, get a Force danger point.

Magical dangers include getting cursed, releasing mystical energies, and changing the physics in a local area.
Finesse dangers include triggering traps, sneaking past sleeping guards, and outwitting puzzles.
Force dangers include a regiment of kobolds and generally any threat of violence.

Removing Danger
If you did not get into danger and currently do not have any danger points, you can erase another player's danger points. The kind of danger you erase is based on your class.

Fighter: Erase 2 Force danger points for each blank. Erase 1 Force danger point for each + or -.
Thief: Erase 2 Finesse danger points for each blank. Erase 1 Finesse danger point for each + or -.
Mage: Erase 2 Magical danger points for each blank. Erase 1 Magical danger point for each + or -

Experience
Earn 1XP for each danger point you erase from another player.

I dunno what you spend XP on, though. Maybe erasing your own danger points? Maybe increasing the threshold for getting into danger? Anyhoo, it's a little idea. Just thought I'd share.

    \n To better answer your question, do the Mage or Fighter have four or more +/-? I think if everyone is in danger, then that probably means some kind of total party kill since you can't rescue anyone in that state.

    Assuming that is not the case...

    Dirk the thief plucks a diamond from an ancient statue's eye socket.

    Dirk's player rolls to see if Dirk gets in danger from this act. He 3dF and gets Blank, Blank, +. Not a bad roll. He notes this down on a piece of paper and rolls again, hoping to ride that luck some more. He rolls again, gets + + +. Aww. That means Dirk is now in some kind of magical danger worth four magical danger points. Dirk's player (or the GM) interprets it thusly.

    Dirk's body glows for a moment before he is sucked inside the diamond! The diamond falls to the ground, at the feet of Krump the Fighter and Raul the Mage. A tiny image of Dirk can be seen inside the diamond, pounding at it's unbreakable walls.

    Because this is a magical danger, Raul steps into the spotlight. Raul's player describes his actions.

    Raul climbs up the statue and replaces the diamond. He holds his hand there over the socket, mumbling arcane incantations to free Dirk.

    Raul's player rolls a few times and gets a total of four blanks, 3 -s and 1 +. With those four blanks, he erases the four magical danger points from Dirk.

    Dirk pops out of the diamond and lands on his face, but only his ego is bruised. Well, also his face.

    So I just ran this solo to see how it works. It definitely needs additional players but it's still pretty awesome

    Quest: Getting there over land - Find the Infinite Staircase
    XP needed: 2

    High Desert Grasslands
    Monk\t\tblank - -
    Trickster\tblank blank blank
    Battle Mage\tblank + blank

    Monk gains 2 Finesse danger
    Trickster gains 3 blanks
    Battle Mage gains 2 blanks and 1 Finesse danger

    High Desert Grasslands
    Monk\t\t+ blank -
    Trickster\t+ + -
    Battle Mage\t+ - +

    Monk gets 1 blank, 1 Magical and 1 Finesse danger
    Trickster gets 2 Force and 1 Magical danger
    Battle Mage gets 2 Finesse and 1 Force danger

    High Desert Grasslands
    Monk\t\t- + blank
    Trickster\t+ + blank
    Battle Mage\tblank - -

    Both the Monk and Trickster are now in danger. So only the Battlemage can rescue
    The Monk is in Finesse danger
    The Trickster is in Force danger

    The Trickster is waylaid by sand demons and the Monk is going to be blinded by the swirling sands
    The Battlemage elects to spend 2 Blanks to cast a simmering shield of blades on the Trickster (-1 Force)
    The Trickster is no longer in danger and spends 2 Blanks on the Monk (-2 Finesse) by reciting the old story of how Coyote calmed the sand storms and brought rain

    Both the Trickster and the Battlemage gain 1 XP for helping the Monk

    High Desert Grasslands
    Monk\t\tblank blank -

    Battle Mage\tblank blank blank

    The Monk has a total of 7 blanks. 6 of these convert to 1 XP. This can be spent to advance the goal or kept to end a Danger scene, losing on you own terms

    The Trickster is back in danger again. Force. Realizing that they had been tricked by the Stories the sand demons waylay!!

    The Monk and the Battlemage can help. The Monk recites chants of how they came to be unravelling the sand demons and returning them to the earth. Monk spends 1 Blank and removes on Force from the Trickster.

    Monk gains 1 XP. The Battlemage and the Monk have had enough. They elect to spend 1 XP each to reach the goal.

    Goal 2 XP - 2XP spent

    Arrival at the Infinite Staircase
    Quest - Canyonlands seek out the SpiderWomen
    Object - Patterns

    So some additional rules
    1) For each scene you roll and then describe what's going on if there is no danger
    2) Mark this on the map. Players create the map as they go. Hexes are pretty useful but freeform is cool too.
    3) Placenames. Name all the things as you head towards the goal


    This game could be run GM-less, with a GM, and/or with cards/tables. I am playing around with all those.

    \n One of the other things I added in along with the map is the following (thanks to Dictionary of Mu)

    Storybook or Story Cards
    On either the cards or a hand bound book record the challenges that your characters have faced. Each player takes a turn writing in the book. Here is an example from the sample adventure

    While crossing the land of the Broken Grasses the Sand Demons attacked!!

    All Sand Demons attack (Force) from hidden places. Some Sand Demons whip up sand and rocks that can be dodged (Finesse). A few Sand Demons steal pieces of your dreams (Magical) for their children. All Sand Demons are mesmerized by stories because the Sun stole their speech. Some Sand Demons can be undone by Words.

    Creatures, people, and animals usually follow the All, Some, and Few pattern. The next set of ASF arise from how the characters interact with the Challenge. Not all Challenges will fill in all three Dangers. And the second set of ASF might only have one entry until next time.

    Be sure to include things the players add. The player of the Trickster included a brief story to calm the Sand Demons so in the Storybook add a note

    An old story of how Coyote calmed the sand storms and brought rain

    Objects/Items
    Creating object or items that are found is done by the group. Basically what gets everyone excited and what leads to more questing/exploring.

    Each Object/Item needs:
    A brief description
    An immediate use - involves sacrificing the object for a temporary gain
    A longer term use - involves a possible use in the future

    Astrolabe
    A disc of bronze craved with rusted arcane symbols
    I: If overland sacrifice to reduce your Finesse Danger by 2
    L: Airship requires this to fly

    \n Turns out James Wallis had similar thoughts for an unpublished game called Cop Show. Basically, you describe three things your character is good at. Then the next player begins with three things that are the exact opposite of what the previous character was good at, followed by three positive traits.\n
    The Captain: [smarmy smile] I\u2019m the Captain. I\u2019m charming, friendly and get on well with everyone I meet, especially the ladies (wink, wink).

    The Scientist: [groans as he realises the other player has just shafted him] So I\u2019m not charming, not friendly and don\u2019t get on well with people, especially not with the ladies. But I\u2019m a great scientist, sober, precise and know what I\u2019m talking about.

    The Engineer: So I\u2019m never sober, my estimates are way off, and I don\u2019t know I\u2019m talking about. But that\u2019s okay, I\u2019m not a scientist, I\u2019m an engineer. I can fix the engines and build things.

    The Doctor: [In character already] Damn it, I\u2019m a doctor not a brick-layer. But remember that what I do keeps the crew alive.

    The Captain: Erm.. Does that mean that what I do results in a lot of the crew dying?

    The GM weeps.
    \nThe system in this thread is pretty generic and abstract so far, so you could swap out "Finesse Danger" and "Force Danger" with more personalized problems like "Awkward interaction with subordinates" and "The ship is about to explode."
