\n One of my players loves 3e gnomes and bards, and I've been trying to think of how to work that into my RBH-like game.

Here's things that are cool about gnomes:

Zest for Life
- Devil-may-care attitude
- Love of food and nature
- Daring against bigger opponents

Love of art
- Appreciate beauty
- Inventive
- Expressive

Disdain needless violence
- Mercantile
- Prefers deception
- Illusion magic

So I'm trying to think of a RBH class for a gnome. So far I'm thinking - lots of the abilities help this concept (especially Illusions/Enchantment from Magus and Chalk Door/Contacts/Disguise from the Ninja), but none really capture what I'm looking for in the character: The devil-may-care daring hero with the gift of the gab and a natural artistry. Any thoughts?

    \n This one's probably not that good for RBH proper but I like it:

    Gift of the Gab
    You can always get someone to start talking, even if it's just a little bit. On your turn you can temporarily halt combat, usually saying "Wait!" or "Money!" The battle will stop for a moment as your opponent hears you out, warily. If they don't like what they hear combat might start right up again. If you can wrangle it, however, combat ends peaceably.
