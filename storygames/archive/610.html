Kenny

You are a teenage truant, bored with life and desperate for a change.

Tonight, you are at a biker rally at the Lost and Found, a dive bar on the edge of town. You are here looking for excitement.

The Cards

At the start of the game, you will be dealt two hands of cards: a hand of Black Fives and a hand of Red Fives. Keep these hands separate.

Your aim is to get rid of the Fives and get as many other Black and Red cards as possible.

Throughout the game, Aces are always low.

Love

Red cards represent Love.

You can get Love cards from someone by Making Love to them. You, Kenny, will generally want to make love to older, glamorous women.

To make love to another (female) character:

1. Give her any one of your Love cards.
2. She gives you any one of your Love cards.
3. If the card you gave her is higher than the one she gave you, the love-making has gone wrong. You must stop Making Love and decide exactly what went wrong.
4. If the card you gave her is lower or equal to the one she gave you, you reach First Base with her.
5. If you reached First Base, repeat this process to attempt Second Base, Third Base and hit a Home Run.

At the end of Making Love, you both keep all the cards you were given. So you keep her cards and she keeps yours.

After Making Love, you must wait 5 minutes before trying again.

Rock

Black cards represent Rock. You, Kenny, can claim Rock cards from other players as follows:

Also, if you are the first person to die tragically, claim 1 Rock card from everyone in the room.

You can also win Rock cards by Drinking Contests and Fighting.

Drinking and Fighting

When you have a Drinking Contest or a Fight with another player:

1. The other player draws one of your Rock cards, at random.
2. You draw one of the other playerÃ¢â‚¬â„¢s Rock cards, at random.
3. If your card (which the other player drew) is higher, you win. If it is lower, you lose. If they are equal, draw again.

Whoever wins keeps all the cards that were drawn.

Afterwards, decide with the other player exactly what happened. If a biker wins a fight with another biker, perhaps he smashed him over the head with a bottle; if a teenage girl wins a fight with a biker, she probably kicked him in the balls.

Bikes

There will be three races tonight: one at sunset, one at midnight and one at dawn.

If you want to compete, you will need a bike. Someone may be willing to lend you one.

In addition, you know how to repair, modify and even sabotage bikes. This means that you can add blue 'Modification' cards to bikes or add them on; and you can remove red 'Damage' cards or add them on. Doing any of these takes two minutes, during which you must kneel beside the bike.

The End Of The Game

At the end of the game, discard all Fives from your hand. Then count how many Love cards and Rock cards you have.

The player with the most Love cards rides out of town to a new life. They may choose one other character to take with them. They have been saved by Love.

The player with the most Rock cards dies gloriously in a bike crash. They have been saved by Rock.

Everyone else is condemned to spend the rest of their lives in this God-forsaken town.

    All right, I think I know how to do the bike races.

    To start, everyone racing must play one Rock card (given to the GM). Whoever plays the highest card starts in the lead, whoever plays the second-highest starts second and so on. The racers stand in a line to indicate this.

    Everyone not racing is a spectator.

    Then, there's five rounds.

    Each round, the bikers write on a piece of paper what they're trying to do. For example: "Drive really fast", "Force Jimmy off the road", "Overtake on the inside". The GM writes something about the road conditions on a piece of paper: "Sudden curve", "Oil on road".

    Everyone shows their piece of paper at the same time. The spectators then decide what actually happens: Does Jimmy go off the road? Does anyone crash?

    In particular, the spectators decide who overtakes who. If someone overtakes, they change their position in the line to indicate this. A racer may only overtake one other racer per round.

    Repeat until the race is won.

    To use a bike modification, you write it on your paper as part of what you want to do. For example, if you've got the "Blades On Wheels" modification, you might write "Use Blades to force Jimmy off the road". When the spectators decide what happens, they're encouraged to think of modifications as really cool.

    Damage works in a similar way. If you have a damage card on your bike, you must write that damage on your card at some point. For example, if you have the damage "Dodgy exhaust", you should write "Exhaust gives out cloud of smoke" at some point during the race.

    Something like that?
