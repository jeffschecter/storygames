\n Following on from Simon's post The Creator Owned Publishing (COP) Model and Control.

It has been identified that many owner creators would be quite happy to not undertake themselves all the stages necessary to game publication (Writing, Playtesting, Editing, Art, Layout, Print Buying, Marketing, Sales, Mangement).

So my questions now are:
1) to what extent does the lack of interest, skill or time in activities other than writing prevent your game from being published in what you consider a reasonable time scale, or even at all?

2) And then, what kinds of solutions to this problem are acceptable to you?

    \n 1) I'm borked by not having the time (whole weekends) or money (camp site or other venue rental) to run real play tests. I have only been able to do "sparring" tests, and I don't think they stress the system enough.

    2) I'd happily let others playtest (and have begged for as much). I am not yet willing to pay people to play test, though I might in time (pay for venue and food, mostly--not actual hired play testers).
