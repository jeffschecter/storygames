    \n Here are mine (good list, too! I didn't have to come up with any new ones):

    Let me break it down, too.
    ----------
    - Hanging out
    I'm a console geek, who reads Japanese and watches J-Dramas with my wife. If it weren't for group exercise classes and work, I'd probably not leave the house much or hang out if it weren't to gather to play games.

    - Acting
    I love getting into character, "taking the stage", and watching others really get dramatic. It's like a play laid out in front of you.

    - Telling stories
    This is more the result of what happens in play, but yeah I like the stories that develop in the game, either through the story ("Kazuichi then threw away his crown!") or through the game ("Then Jason had his character be, like, "I throw away my crown!" and didn't even challenge Amy back!")

    - Escaping
    Escapism. It's why I run fun stories, or cool fantasy/SF stories, or escapable horror stories: It's fun to escape from life and finances and work and stuff.

    - Feeling powerful
    Perhaps an arm of Escapism: I love feeling powerful in a game: I like making badasses. If I have a totally weak character, then rest assured they're good at SOMETHING. My weak-ass CoC investigator is a black-belt in Zoology and Stealth. My female thief is badass with throwing weapons and charming men. My socially inept brute is just a weapons and poetry expert.

    - Exploring fictional worlds
    Natch. Escapism. Exploring worlds, AND making them up as we go along.

    - Problem / puzzle solving as a team
    This usually comes up in those critical moments, but it's still great to have everyone leaning forward, thinking together through the problem.

    -Andy

    \n It's way easier to say why I DON'T game - escapism. But here is my top 5 THIS month

    - Telling stories - I love sitting back and seeing things unfold in exciting ways - sometimes surprising and sometimes predictable but almost always entertaining.
    - Competition - I like pitting my mind against others in a constructive environment
    - Challenging people - I like to see people squirm and tick and react. I learn about them and myself.
    - Exploring unsafe ideas in a safe place - Being able to see people at their worst means we are far better prepared for life.
    - Creating characters - I dig the fiddly aspects of character creation and finding unique ways to use the rules to make characters

    Wow, I pasted the list here and weeded out my best answers. Then I trimmed the natural seven down to five. Then I was going to reorder them and they were already in my basic order of importance Yay!

    - Don
