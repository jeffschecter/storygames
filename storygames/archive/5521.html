\n Normally I'd just tuck this into "January February Things to Watch", but it was so cool that I had to bust it out for more eyes:

From Rob D:

"If you have a character sheet with checkboxes (like for health, or willpower, or whatever) put a piece of scotch tape over the boxes. You can now fill them in with dry erase marker and wipe them clean without needing to put the whole sheet in clear plastic. It's a silly thing, but that is the price of Theraflu."

Slick trick! I'll need to remember that. And find some dry erase markers with a thinner writing area than my thigh, but still.

    \n
    \n It's cheaper, and when I'm dealing with a character sheet I'm willing to lose, such as at a con, it's much more convenient then getting things out of the sleeves and so on. Additionally, when you have data that changes rarely, but which you still want to track, like inventory, it can be easier to do that with pencil. Also, this ends up working very well on non-standard sheet sizes.

    And it packs thinner too!

    -Rob D.
