\n So I have submitted a game for a Ronnie. It was an interesting process trying to design a game in 24 hours. I will also be submitting it for the Solo Game Challenge. I realize now that I have had some sleep that it seems to have a good foundation, but it is really a mess. I am working on cleaning it up now, but that is not wihat I am actually here to talk about.

This game will be run on random encounter tables. During the initial 24 hours of design I was running low on creativity and put off filling in some of the tables. I am now here to invite you all to help me to fill them.

Picture an aging wizard on a flight into darkness from foes that are overpowering, trying to reach the Stone Circle at Graystead to cast a ritual to save the world. What locations do you pass through on your way? Think about locations that would be good for confrontations against opposing Knights, ninja-witches, goblins, and walking nightmares. These should be locations that Frodo and crew could have passed through on their way to Rivendell if they had taken a different route in a different story in a different world. They could be locations that Rand Al'Thor and crew could have passed through on their initial race out of the Two Rivers.

Challenge: post examples of places that could have shown up in your favorite "escape the forces of darkness" chase story in fantasy literature. Something evocative but relatively unconstrained.

Some examples from me:

On the High Road at midnight
The ferry at Hillshire on a foggy morning
A stand of pines on Bedron\u2019s Ridge in a rainstorm
The Cherry Hill Inn, upstairs coridor, just before dawn
Farmer Gable\u2019s Farm, just after supper time
Elderberry Wells, with two gypsey wagons encamped 100 yards away

Thanks in advance.
