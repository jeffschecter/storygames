[DitV] Jedi mod: Passions

Vaxalon
March 2006 edited March 2006 in Story Games
Here's a little essay about passions with respect to Jedi that I want
you guys to have a look at, see if it jives for you. Comments are
encouraged.

Passions are dangerous for Jedi. At one time, they distract him from
following the Force, cloud his reasoning, and provide a tempting
source of power.

Every Jedi has passions. When that stakes of a conflict are WHETHER the Jedi acts on a passion, the dice fall against him, that is, his opponent can roll those dice on his side to try to convince him to act in accordance with it. When the Jedi IS acting on a passion, however, the Jedi may use those dice on HIS side, to see whether he succeeds. Doing so, however, makes the passion stronger, and the Jedi slips further under its control; as those passions become darker, the Jedi slips into the Dark Side.

Fears are negative passions, a desire and fixation that an event not come to pass.
Lures are positive passions, a desire and fixation that an event come to pass.

If your passion is at the level of "Fear" or "Lure" then it has d4's.

Fear becomes Anger when one blames another for one's fear. Anger is fear turned outwards and personalized.
Lure becomes Envy when one blames another for being unable to achieve one's desires. Envy is lust turned outwards and personalized.

If your passion is at the level of "Anger" or "Envy" then it has d6's.

Anger and Envy become Hatred when they are given expression. Hatred is passion made real through word or deed.

If your passion is at the level of "Hatred" then it has d8's.

Hatred which is fulfilled becomes Dark Taint.

For example, a Jedi who has a Lure to join the Jedi Council, which becomes a Hatred of Mace Windu, gains a Dark Taint die when he kills Mace Windu and takes his place on the Council.

Dark Taint dice are d10's, and are no longer passions. Instead, they are added (once) to the dice whenever passion dice are rolled. When a Jedi acquires a Dark Taint die, the GM gives him a new passion at d4. This new passion is almost always a Lure to dominate or control something; at first, they are small things, but each new passion added by the GM will be more grandiose than the one before. Thus, as a Jedi slips further into the Dark Side, he becomes easier to manipulate, and more powerful.

I'm not sure precisely how it's going to work yet, but passions will likely replace relationships in the Jedi game, and instilling them (or reinforcing them) will be the subject of conflicts.

Passions can be decremented by using experience to lower the die size of the passion. A passion that is at d4 and is decremented in this way is removed, and is replaced by a new one of the player's choice. A Jedi always has four passions.


These rules are not fully formed yet. There's pieces missing. This is a good start, I think.

    OK. But I think you're going to have to go further here than simple passion dice. This is going to wind up rebuilding Town Creation, for one thing.

    Here's what I'd do:

    Those Passions are what you want to make every story be about, at least to some significant degree.     Also, since you want an escalation mechanic here, this is how I think it should work:

    I'm the Player. You're the GM.

    It starts off where you've got a d4 on some Passion of mine. You roll it against me, then give it to me to be used in the next scene or later. I roll it when I activate that Passion for myself.     When you have my Passion dice, it's your job to make me want them. When I have them, it's your job to make me use them.
