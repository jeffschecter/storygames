Ask the Blind Man

Doyce
June 2006 edited June 2006 in Story Games
[originally on my blog, today]

So, there's this old rhyme.

One bright day in the middle of the night,
Two dead men got up to fight,
Back to back they faced each other,
Drew their swords and shot each other.

A deaf policeman heard the noise,
And came and shot those two dead boys,
If you don't believe this story's true,
Ask the blind man; he saw it too.
I keep thinking...

"You could run a game where this would all be possible."

and

"It could be serious."

and then I get creeped out.

---

Then I get to thinking about how to do it, and with what. TSoY has that one race, you know? Those guys who don't really die until everyone who knew them is dead? You know the ones. Them. They could be the dead boys.

And then I sort of wander off into extra-senses and pre- and post-cognition and summoning of ghosts and giving them form...

Qek. Those are the guys. The Qek.

... and the then there's the whole Solar/Lunar eclipse thing that happens when someone Transcends... "if it's at Night, the Sun eclipses the Moon", and that gets us "one bright day in the middle of the night" -- the whole story starts when someone Transcends... and that brings... somehow, that brings two dead boys back.

Like... hmm. They used to be totally forgotten -- everyone that knew them was dead, but suddenly everyone that knew them is NOT dead, somehow. And they're back. Duelling.

Then you mix in a little Dark City and some ur-machines under the streets of a half-ruined city....

Why? I dunno, I just like it.

... and there you go.

I'm trying to figure out the 'drew their swords and shot each other' without thinking of Final Fantasy IX (which I didn't even play), but it's hard.
