The Dinner Game

DanielSolis
October 2006 edited October 2006 in Story Games
I've just listened to The Dinner Game, the latest episode of Escape Pod, a scifi/fantasy short story anthology podcast. The premise of is a man and a woman playing a storygame while having dinner at a hotel restaurant.

This, and a recent episodes of Doctor Who, have gotten me imagining a game played at a semi-formal dinner setting. No dice, nor character sheet, nor anything else that wouldn't normally be found at a dinner gathering.

The game is played through conversation and is competitive in nature, prone to twists and turns that would spur an "oh snap!" in a more casual environment. The primary system interface is tapping a fork against a glass as if calling for a toast. Other game props include napkins, forks, spoons, knives and other common dinner utensils. If the game is played at a rectangular table, the players at either end may have special roles that the rest of the dinner guests do not. Depending on the formality of the dinner, each course may indicate a different segment of the game. The whole game can be played from beginning to end within the span of the meal.

The last bit that I'm imagining will probably be the most difficult to implement: You could play this game at a restaurant without anyone outside of the game noticing that you're doing anything out of the ordinary.

And so the eternal question presents itself: Has this been done before?
