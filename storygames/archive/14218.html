    \nScott: Recently some folks have been throwing around GNS jargon pretty much randomly. I wish they wouldn't! Honestly, I kinda wish people would stop using those terms unless they really, really need to, because they very often get in the way of actual communication rather than assisting in it. GNS doesn't actually exist as a real life thing! It's just a perspective that can be used to analyze play. A useful one in many situations, sure, but it shouldn't be confused with "the way things are."

    A short and dirty version:

    Narrativism (Story Now): Focused on exploring a premise, usually in the form of a question, like "How much are you willing to sacrifice to get what you want?"
    Simulationism (Right to Dream): Focused on exploring the fiction, seeing what its like, reaffirming or altering it, etc.
    Gamism (Step on Up): Focused on exploring being challenged and overcoming those challenges.

    Note that you can do all of these things (explore premise, explore fiction, explore challenge) in the same game, moment to moment. So it's not like if you explore challenge it's suddenly Gamism. It's about tendencies that a group of players show over time, not just moment to moment. Consequently, you can't really use it to analyze one particular circumstance, a particular player, or a particular game. It doesn't give you enough information about the overall trends!

    And remember, GNS is an analytical model. It doesn't really exist! You could analyze roleplaying in a host of other ways that would be just as useful or even more useful, depending on what you were trying to do or understand.

    And now back to your regularly scheduled squabbling...
