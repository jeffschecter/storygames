    Hard to put into a nice bullet form:

    The entire barter economy is based on the good word of the people involved, as not every transaction is a one-for-one trade. Most of the time, you get a chicken from a farmer and you owe them a little bit. You help them build a wall the next month and that's more than the chicken was worth, so they owe you now. And so on. The only reason anybody gives you anything is if they believe that you'll live up to your end of the bargain eventually. A man's good word then is his credit rating of today.

    So: Breaking your word, refusing to recompense appropriately, etc?
