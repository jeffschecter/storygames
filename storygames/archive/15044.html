\nDND for 8 year old boys...\n
\n
For my son's 8th birthday party he convinced me to run Dungeons and Dragons for four 2nd graders... I decided to take actual characters made in the DND Insider and simplify them us that the boys could have fun without much of a learning curve.
\n
\n
\nYou can download the high resolution files here.

    Sadly they emphasize one of the things I so dislike about D&D - they're completely useless after 3 sessions when you level up and 50% of those stats change. Oh well.



    \nNo worries,move the stats of the world instead of the character stats.
    I know how to design around it for other games.
    And I know how I'd hack D&D to work for static, rather than escalating characters. (In fact, 4th ed D&D doesn't really need levelling. You could play 1st level characters indefinitely and they would work fine, as long as you're not trying to do a zero-to-hero campaign.)

    I'm just saying that for D&D, rules-as-written, these sheets become useless after about 3 sessions, which is sad.

    In Savage Worlds, for example, you would have, maybe, 1 stat change after like 4 sessions. Or a couple edges added. Easy peasy small stuff that you could change with just the occasional scratch out here and there.

    But with a weekly campaign of D&D, you'd have to remake the sheets completely every month. And I think that's sad.

    All that said, these sheets are awesomesauce.

    I particularly like how the female characters are amuzing and look fun to play without being a bunch of tween sluts-in-training. The pally girl's armor covers her protectively. Only the necro girl and the mino are showing midriff. And the minotaur and warforged girls are wonderfully atypical of female characters.
