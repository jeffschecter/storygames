    Person 42 / whatever: See, the problem is that people think they can talk about theory as if it's without context. When really, any discussion of theory is embedded in the theories that come before. So trying to say something is never just trying to say something. It has to be trying to say something either in accord with, or in defiance of, how things have been said before. That's why I don't bother listening to anyone until they've addressed the complaints and concerns I've raised in every single theory thread of the past ten years. Because until they discuss that, they're just ignoring the obvious context. It's an insult to me, and to all right-minded people. I do not think that addressing ten years worth of discussion (a mere million or two posts) is too high a bar to present to someone who wants to talk about how RPGs work.
