Why DON'T most indie games have "demo versions"?

Levi Kornelsen
July 2006 edited July 2006 in Story Games
I've just thought to wonder if there's actually an answer to this. Many top-end games have free starter rules, or intro adventues, or various things like that... But Indie and small-press games, for the most part, don't. Now, thus far, I've been prepping such stuff as a cool way to get early interest, develop an audience, and so on.

But now I'm wondering if there's an actual *reason* that this isn't done. Has it been shown to be ineffective, not worth the time? Or something? Or it it just something most small game writers don't feel would be helpful to them?

    Okay, so the basic consensus seems to be that it's probably beneficial to make demo versions, but for many people, and many games, it's not all that easy, for three basic reasons:

    1-Lack of people/hours to do the work; one-man operations are common.

    2-Some games aren't easily divisible; plenty of small-press games are simply too unified.

    3-Most people don't have experience at it or a body of practical advice on doing it *well*.

    ...Of those, #1 and #2 are very real roadblocks, though Jess has suggested the obvious solution to #1. But #3 is something that could change - something we could think about, research, and find ways to serve.

    July 2006 edited July 2006
    TonyLB has a Capes Lite version. How has that worked out for you, Tony?

    Welll ... it's done what it was intended to do, but I don't think it was intended to do the exact things you might be expecting. The Capes Lite demo rules serve several different purposes:
      [li]First, yes, it helps people who rightly think they might be excited about the game to get
    really excited about the game.[/li]
    [li]Second, and more importantly, it helps people who wrongly think they might be excited about the game to learn that it's not for them. Don't wanna be sellin' to those folks anyway.[/li]
    [li]Third, and growing in importance as time goes on, it lets me point people to some of the rules and say "Figure it out yer own damn self." When I get someone saying "I know nothing about your system except that it has no GM, and therefore it must all fall apart, you idiot!" I say "Okay, when you've read the Lite rules and the Flash Demo come back and talk to me and I'll explain how those rules work to prevent the things you're worried about." Most of the time they don't come back, and that is a great and powerful relief.[/li]

    If you're asking strictly "How has it worked out in terms of selling games?" ... it's probably a wash. I expect I sell about the same amount of games because of the demo as I lose because of them. But I'm a lot happier with the folks who purchase than I would be otherwise.
