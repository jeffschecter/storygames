The SG Names Project: Submissions are Now Closed

Andy
July 2006 edited July 2006 in Play Advice
Hi all, since the other thread reached well over 300 posts, I'm starting a new thread to keep us focused and moving forward. Please abandon that thread and post to this one if you have something to say. Also, normally side chatter is totally cool, but for this thread only I ask that we keep it to a minimum, and focus on directed pragmatic questions, concerns, answers, and discussion on the lists (just so we can get that much further). Feel free to start individual threads to hash out specific sets, as Joshua BishopRoby did for the porn stars set.

Jason and I decided that the October 31st deadline was just too far away, and we grew stagnant. But GenCon is too close, and rushing to print it now would in time for GenCon woiuld mean we'd have some big glaring holes.

So now it's time to go back and cool-ify the stuff we already have, divvy up and hammer out the stuff we don't have, and come up with new stuff to throw in.

To Recap:

The rules are simple: The minimum requirements for a list is 40 names: 20 male, 20 female. After that, you can add whatever you want (like more names), as long as it's in groups of 20. If you want to add other cool cultural or background information, put it in, but in groups of 20 (see the Egyptian list for "Concepts", the Angels list for "Titles", the Aztec list for "Food and Drink", the 1001 Nights list for tons of awesome ideas outside of names). The priority is names and new name lists, but if you take a look at the existing names' lists and think of 20 new names to put in, or 20 surnames, or 20 Major Events, or 20 weapons, etc, feel free to submit just that part to Jason and he'll add it to one of the existing lists.

Here is a link to the Template, which is just "how to submit your stuff to Jason so he doesn't kill himself reformatting it". Send them to Jason at jmstar at gmail dot com.

After August 20th, the whole thing will be compiled, laid out, art and illustration added, and it will be put on sale at Lulu: All profits will go to the Carter Center. Because charity fucking rocks. Everyone who contributed to the project will get credit.

The next post on this discussion will be the current names list, and what we're definitely looking to add (the "holes" we want to fill).

As I mentioned above, feel free to contribute more to an existing list if you have ideas for 20 Things (or 20 More Names). Also, another thing to consider is regions and history: Some regions we only have historical names and not modern ones; some regions we have modern names and not historic ones. That might be an area to look for for more easy name lists.

Finally, don't hesitate to get folks off of Story-Games Proper to help. I'm thinking friends and aquaintences, not random people. For example, for the Japan list I have above, I don't have historic names (Samurai, Heian), and I want more "things lists" (I'm working on 20 Weapons, but things like 20 foods, etc) so I will probably have my friends on the Japanese International Gaming Guild list throw out some ideas. If you have others help, credit them in your email to Jason so that they will be named in the book as well.

That's all. I'll post reminders weekly to get people moving. And check back to the beginning of this thread (specifically the Next Post), as we'll be updating it every few days with What We Have and What We Need (rather than having the confusion of tracking down new lists by post. But you can always head over to Jason's site to see the latest pdfs.)

Thanks!

    Here's the ad information, stickied for eternity:

    $25 gets you a 1.5" by 4.5" ad connected with a list you specify, typically on the second page of the set but at the layout guy's discretion. This is the size of the sample I posted earlier.

    $50 gets you a 8.5" by 5.5" ad, wherever we want to put it, most likely in the back of the book in a group, but maybe clustered in the middle or something.

    Your ad will be inserted exactly as you give it to us. If you lack the ability to produce an ad, we'll be glad to just add some text you provide us in a distinctive font inside a border, but it won't look good or fancy. Consider asking a designer friend to help you out.

    Submit a camera-ready greyscale .tif file, ideally at 600 dpi, either as an attachment or a URL we can grab, along with a copy of the electronic receipt from The Carter Center indicating your donation of the desired amount (or more!). If you buy a small ad, also indicate which list you'd like it to be associated with if you have a preference. There will be a maximum of one ad per list, so first come, first served. Lists may change between now and layout, but we'll all work together to make sure your ad is as awesome and useful as possible. Feel free to buy an "ad" that promotes something other than a game - like, say, story-games.com for example, or your favorite podcast or whatever. If your ad will make the book look stupid, we'll reject it.

    The deadline for ads (and everything else) is 20 August.
