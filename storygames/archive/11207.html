"BEVERLY HILLS, Calif. \u2013 Threats on Facebook, name-calling, security guard escorts \u2014 tempers are running high around schools these days in this normally sedate enclave of ostentatious wealth."
...
Facebook pages have sprung up on both sides, with police investigating one posting that called for "machine gun machetes" to be used against those who favor ending permits. Disciplinary action against the 12-year-old boy who posted the statement was referred to school administrators..."

MACHINE. GUN. MACHETES.

Somebody hire this kid. We have the concept sketch of the next Drizzt Do'Urden on our hands!

So, how would you stat them in a system or three?

    \n for MADCorp:

    Machine gun machete (gadget reliability:10 edged+1/gun medium/medium ammo:3 nasty)

    The information in the brackets there is, in order, a restrictive item class tag, probability on a d20 that it won't break when abused, damage type/alternate damage type, reach/alternate reach ("medium" with a gun is different from "medium" with a contact weapon), the number of times you can attack with it before reloading, and a descriptive tag with a few mechanical implications.

    \n "Machine-gun machete" is a technique which allows repeated uses of the machete attack with great speed. OBVIOUSLY not actual machine gun speed. That's not realistic. More like: make 6d6 machete attacks consecutively, where two subsequent successful attacks gain you another instant attack after the second attack but before the what-was-the-third attack, so the instant attack and third-attack together count towards the creation of new instant attacks unless you have effects in play doing otherwise. Of course activating your Limit Break power on a High Critical breaks the rest of your combat chain, because you can never follow a shift to a BreakEnder Maneuver with a instant attack, for obvious game-balance reasons. So this is a trade-off.
