\n I've invited people round for games and food tomorrow. Since it's August, I assumed many would be on holiday or something, so I asked lots of people. It turns out about 14 people are coming. We'll have enough for three games at once. Superb.

I'll run Lacuna and I think someone wants to run Dogs.

Anyway! Enough about the games. I'm also cooking. I'm cooking coq au vin, for two reasons:

a. It's best when left overnight. So I've half-cooked it today and I'll finish it off tomorrow. And that just takes twenty minutes of gentle simmering. Easy.
b. It was pretty cheap: 20 quid to feed 14 people.
c. It allows me to make infantile jokes about my coq.

With the last point in mind, here's some pictures of the coq in progress, as I cooked it today.

    \n Pretty bloody good.
    \n
    \nApparently the handle is hollow and filled with sand, which gives the knife a remarkable lightness and great balance. The centre of gravity is right where the handle and blade meet, meaning that the whole thing is really easy to use.
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \nIt's incredibly sharp. I've had this one for a little over two years and never sharpened it and it's still amazingly sharp. I'm careful not to use it for stuff like opening plastic packets and stuff like that, which has probably helped. The first few times I cleaned it I almost ended up with two half sponges. I need to figure out how to sharpen it at some point before too long though.
    \n
    \nIt never seems to get slippery when it's wet, but if you get grease on it from chicken or something it does. I have short fingers though, so YMMV etc etc.
    \n
    \nThey aren't cheap, to the point where I probably never would have bought one myself. This one was a gift, and I am very pleased with it! I have a couple of Taylor's Eye Witness ones, which are also excellent (made in Sheffield from razor blade steel) but I'm not sure that those are available outside the UK and they don't seem to hold their edge quite as well.

    \n And now it's time for coq.

    As you remember, I'd half-cooked the coq the day before. So, now, I simmer it for 20 more minutes, and it's done. I soak couscous in boiling water for 10 minutes and that's done too. Really, really easy stuff. It takes half an hour out of the day to feed ten people: all the hard work was done yesterday and even that wasn't hard.

    Steve refuses to eat my coq: apparently, it's cooked in wine, and he doesn't like wine. Funny: I always find a bit of wine helps coq go down easier. I make him sausages instead.
