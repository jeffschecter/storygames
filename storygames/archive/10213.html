\n Random Dungeon

I am dodging writing my thesis and journal paper so I decided to roll up a random dungeon and go explore it with a character. I've grabbed material from Fight On! 1-5, Judges Guild 14, Old School Encounters Reference, The Strategic Review, for rules I have the 3 ODD books, White Box Sword and Sorcery, and for reference Labyrinth Lord. There are some house rules too.

If you want to join in on the fun let me know. If you want to use your own system please feel free or you can use mine as well.

The character name (Abulafia):
Silent Dog

Motivation for dungeon crawling (Fight On! 5):
16 - Seeks the subterranean Black Rock River, hoping to forget a
shameful past deed.

Alignment: yeah right

Class: Fighter

STR 15
INT 10
WIS 7
DEX 12
CON 10
CHR 15

Hit Dice d8
Money 120 gp

Equipment
Backpack (40 lb capacity)

In the backpack:
crowbar
flint & steel
12 flasks of oil
Iron rations for 8 days
12 iron spikes
torches (8)
jar of healing salve

Attached to backpack
bedroll

On belt
daggers
handaxe
light hammer

Over the body
Rope and lantern
Waterskins

Across back
spear
backpack

Armor:
Coat of Plates AC 6ish

Gold: 16

According to Dungeons and Dragons Vol 3 each dungeon expedition takes about a week. So hopefully I've outfitted correctly here. One of the house rules involves the abstraction of tracking provisions and light sources. These two items go from High to Out based on the outcomes of die rolls at certain intervals. This way you can choose to push how long you are down in the dark. There is an odd obsession with weight and placement of equipment so I kept it, maybe it will be fun. I have a custom character sheet that is filled out now.

    \n Random Dungeon - The Upper Works

    So for the upper works I hacked the Judges Guild 14 publication so I could have my own table.

    I decided that this dungeon is set into a dry canyon of red sandstone with scrubby brush and stunted trees. The canyon is deep for nightfall comes quickly.

    From the tables:
    We have a ruin, partially covered in cinders, the ruin is crumbled and decayed, and there is some sort of mechanical keeper

    The ruins are a city, of houses and temples, the temples are shrines and possibly artifact in nature

    Some possible Oracles for the upper works (Abulafia):
    A small room under the foundation, its doorway bricked shut, the prison of a dreadful and malicious spirit.
    A fearsome storm, with thunder and driving winds.
    A reader of omens and caster of auguries, with grim news.
    A necromancer who steals the knowledge of the dying.

    The mechanical keeper sounds just perfect for Vincent Baker's The Monster Machine in Fight On
    Nameless
    Made of: Metal and Shadow (LVL d8, DC 0, Speed 15)
    Metal Attack (Impale) - 1d8 dmg, 2d8 on a natural 20
    Shadow Frighten - save vs spell or flee
    Shadow Stealth - Surprises on 4 in 6, Circle (ignores shield and DEX bonuses)
    ODD stats - HD: 6d6; AC: 0, #ATT: 1

    Ummm yuck, I don't want to run into that. There are other things running around but we will deal with that later.


    A map of sorts is next.

    The main goal here is for my character to find the way down into the dungeon.
