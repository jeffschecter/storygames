    \n I suppose that the de-rigeur answer would be FATE; however, I still think that's a very useful answer and it's the first one that popped into mind. I think the notion of assigning Aspects to scenes,whether physical, virtual, or narrative would dovetail into "Snow Crash" quite nicely, and I have a strong suspicion that the FATE mechanics would flex between "normal human", "augmented physical human", and "OMG yer an avatar!" power levels quite nicely.

    I also think that Aspect assigning and tagging would represent a really elegant interface between the various arenas in the background (i.e. actions in the virtual world can be used to locate, assign, and tag Aspects in the physical world).

    \n Settle down there, buddy. I already bought Shock:, so you don't gotta sell me.
    That said, I wouldn't want to use Shock: to play "Snow Crash the RPG" and here's why:

    I think one of the coolest things from Shock:, one of the things I'm most looking forward to when I finally get a chance to play it, is collaborative world building. Hard to do that and also say, "Hey guys, we're doing Snow Crash the RPG tonight." Plus, while Snow Crash has some interesting Issues and Shocks in it, that... isn't really what I like about the book. Okay, that's a lie. I love that about the book. I love talking about burbclaves and corporate owned and driven culture and crazy religious cults. It's cool shit that also makes me THINK. But it's not what sucks me in while I'm reading it. While I'm reading it I'm all about gattling gun duels on the Raft, and wild motorcycle chases through the Metaverse, and Y.T.'s crazy highway surfing escapades. And I'm not really sure I'd want to try to do that stuff with Shock:. I might be wrong. Shock: might be awesome for this stuff. I don't know because I haven't played yet, but from reading it, it doesn't seem, to me, like it's the system I'd want to use for that feel and game.
