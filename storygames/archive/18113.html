\n So I've begun running a game of Fantasy Flight's Star Wars: Edge of the Empire RPG (I picked up the beta book off their website in Dec). I ran the first session last wednesday and it went very well. Afterwards it was pointed out to me "we never rolled initiative". It seems I've been running Apocalypse World games too much. I'm wondering if anyone else has (intentionally or not) done something similar?

Also, looking for some feedback and ideas for some Dread style questions to spring on my players to put some flesh on their numbery bones.

So far I have these:

-Wookiee Bounty Hunter/Tech
What is the worse decision you've ever made while on Booster Blue?

When did the nightmares start?

-Human "Face" Smugglar/Thief
How is your bunk decorated?

There is a grave you visit every time you are on your homeworld. Who's grave is it, and how did they die?

-Bothan Doctor/Tech
There are tons of strange things in the galaxy. What is the strangest thing you've seen, have you told anyone, would anyone believe you?

What have you been meaning to do but never got around to?

-Rodian Pilot/Smugglar (owner of the ship, a Gthroc 720 Transport named the Gamera)
What is that thing, and why do you have it?

Why haven't you replaced those uncomfortable chairs?

    What is that thing, and why do you have it?
    \nI don't know about your players, but for me, "thing" is too broad for a quick response question. If it was me you were asking, you'd get a much better response if you said it was a weapon or a nicknack or a decoration on my clothing or something with a little more of a prompt to it.

    \n Then how about adding some interesting story hook to it?

    Examples:

    "What is that thing you have, and why is [nasty smuggler Hutt] desperately looking for it?"

    or

    "What is that thing you have that you desperately wish you could get rid of?"

    or

    "What is that thing you have, and why can't you ever sell it in public, though you wish you could?

    or

    "What is that thing you have, and how did it come to be the last of its kind?"

    \n I think it's wise to go for the more "asking provocative questions" direction with this rather than actually mechanizing the "story-gaminess," like trying to bolt on Beliefs from BW or something like that. I've seen it done but it's usually pretty messy, because the emergent interactions that such flagging systems are built on just aren't there. Whereas what you're doing is simply using a set of principled techniques, non-numerical, to build player investment. Rock on!
