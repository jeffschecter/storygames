Finally the book has arrived for Piledrivers & Powerbombs!
Arising from the ashes of a brutal birthday bash (thread on CE), the Chokeslam of Darkness edition has been unleashed in a bonfire night bonanza.

PILEDRIVERS AND POWERBOMBS: CHOKESLAM OF DARKNESS EDITION (\xa39.99 GBP)

A no-holds-barred RPG of ferocious feuds and gargantuan grudges!

PnP requires no preparation time whatsoever - grab your buddies, create a wrestler and you're away. Card-play and role-play are fast and furious. Fight fair like a true hero - or cheat your way to victory.

Dare to step into the ring, strive to become...
The Undisputed Champion of the Universe!

Also on IPR.

+++

Any of you story gamers reckon you can run a game with more than 12 players?

    \n Well, JP made an entire party play the game the other day. And by the sounds of it, we enjoyed it. I certainly did.

    Cool bits? Specific scenes are a mechanic. My caveman-themed wrestler had a training scene dodging raptors in a cheap animatronic Vegas version of Jurassic Park.

    Actual wrestling is resolved, cleverly, as a group. There's tag teams. There's theme music. And lots of cheese and showmanship.

    I've not yet played a serious game of P&P, but I'd really like to. Despite traits like 'Testicular Fortitude', there's all the usual potential for character you'd expect from a JoeP game.

    \n Big thumbs up to Joe for actually going through with this - I admit that I was slightly scheptical about a party with games involved, but it worked great - and I was entirely sober all the way through as I was driving me and my wife home again afterwards.

    P&P has similarities with Contenders - the fixed scenes, the low buy-in and little/zero prep - it works but feels lighter than Contenders. Contenders can be emotionally deep while P&P was (from the short time we played that night) more grunting big-arms-thing, over-the-top crazy/silly. With swastikas, nudge-nudge-jokes and indeed a lot of shouting. Heavy cocktail, perhaps, but in the right company quite entertaining.

    Wrestling does very little for me - in fact I'm not even sure I really know what it is, but that was no hindrance. At all.

    Per
