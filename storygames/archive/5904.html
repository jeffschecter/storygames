\n
\n

\n\nMark_Causey \n\n\n
\n
\n\nMarch 2008 \n edited March 2008 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n I often have some off-the-wall ideas that I pitch for gaming. I spend a lot of time doing auditions for each system because system really matters for the results.

However, you can't always find the perfect system. Sometimes you have to shoehorn a game into a system and see what the void points to during the game for patching. This is often useful for nascent game design, in my opinion. However, that isn't the point of this thread.

A go-to game is a system that you use to often take care of this shoehorning. Right now I have some pitches in my head (not campaigns, but con sessions or one-to-three session arcs) that I have auditioned for quite a while and am just not finding anything for. I have often said that you can do almost anything in The Shadow of Yesterday (and often have, to good use). However, I'm wondering if there are others out there that you use that might be more interesting or have less problems with on my current pitches. I'll be glad to go over my current pitches if necessary, but what I really want is this:

What's your Go-To Gaming System? What have you used it for that it wasn't designed for? How well did it work out?

    \n Yes, I left the die rolling the same. And I forgot to list the games I'm running for my family:

    Usagi Yojimbo
    Star Wars

    I have two daughters aged 13 and 10. The 10 year old needs to work on her basic computation skills. So adding the d6s, subtracting the opposing roll, multiplying by the weapon factor, subtracting armor, then subtracting from hit points, and after the fight recovering 1/2 the hitpoints lost, is all good to me!

    The biggest hacks I've made to the system were grafting Pow and Sanity from CoC for playing CoC and Delta Green. For the Star Wars game I beefed up the Experience Pool and renamed it Force Points. Instead of just giving a bonus die to a roll, spending a Force Point lets you maximize a roll. I typically give out 2 Force points per PC at the start of a session, and award Force points when I 'compel' one of their Traits or when the bad guys use a Force Point. Taking 'Strong with the Force' as a Trait lets you start each session with Force Points equal to the dice in the Trait.

    Posted By: Mark CauseyThat's two for OtE. Can anyone tell me about its hackability?
    \nMore on this:

    Freeform traits rated in d6s, high granularity (2-5 dice is the entire scale), roll to beat a target number or opposing roll, two forms of die influence - bonus/penalty dice that let you take the best/worst set, and additional dice that just add to or subtract from your roll. That's pretty much it, minus individual permutations for bodily damage and whatnot that should be pretty easy to extrapolate from the Thundarr link above.

    So you can flexibly represent just about anything, and have an immediate context to understand what small and large situational modifiers look like without needing a chart.

    Pretty much, when I have any hack for *any* system that I'm pondering, I port the concept into OtE first to see what happens. Little bugger is damn resilient like that.
