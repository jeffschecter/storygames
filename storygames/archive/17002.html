Modern Myths Comics & Games brings Geek Culture to Mamaroneck, NY!

Modern Myths, Inc., an independent specialty retailer of comic books, graphic novels, and hobby games, announced the signing of a lease for a new storefront at 822 Mamaroneck Avenue, Mamaroneck, NY. The store will feature the cutting edge of \u201cgeek culture\u201d, with new releases and back issue comic books, a full line of trade paperback and graphic novels, and a complete line of hobby games, including European board games like Settlers of Catan and role-playing games like Dungeons & Dragons, as well as related merchandise and accessories.

\u201cMamaroneck is the perfect place for our expansion,\u201d said Jim Crocker, President of Modern Myths, Inc. and General Manager of the new shop. \u201cFrom our very first visit, we were really impressed with the vibrant local business environment. The presence of other independent specialty retailers, including two great local toy stores, felt like a perfect fit given the unique pop culture products we've carried for a decade now.\u201d

Modern Myths won't be just a place to buy books and games, however. The spacious new shop will feature social meeting space for casual games, organized tournaments, artist and author signings, art and design classes, even Dungeons & Dragons birthday parties. \u201cWe're well aware we're competing with dozens of other businesses, including sporting events, video games, and even cable TV for that precious bit of fun time everyone has,\u201d notes Crocker. \u201cWe're hoping that, by making Modern Myths more than just a store, but a community in its own right, we'll give both longtime fans and curious newcomers a reason not just to stop by, but to stay a while and have some fun together.\u201d

Modern Myths' Mamaroneck location is currently under construction, and Crocker expects to be ready to open for business in September: \u201cWe're planning on being open a few weeks to get to know our new customers and make sure we're up to speed, so that we can host a really spectacular Grand Opening party in late October. This is the best birthday present for our tenth anniversary as a company that I could imagine.\u201d

Modern Myths, Inc. was founded in 2002 in Northampton, MA as Modern Myths, LLC,. The Northampton shop will celebrate its tenth anniversary in business on Friday, September 21, 2012.

RPGs in the new shop, as we do in Northampton. The Mamaroneck store will of course be an easy train ride up from NYC, and there's a pizza place RIGHT NEXT DOOR. I'll be crazy-busy the next 6 month to a year, but GenCon will be my last trip where I'm not solely worried about the new shop, so I plan to have a great time. I'll post up tidbits from time to time, hopefully enough to keep you all appraised without spamming. If you want to follow the blow-by-blow, I'll be posting a lot pf pics and little essays about opening on FB and the store's own blog, so head over there.

    \n There will be plenty of pics once we get the power on (Conn Edison requires me to FAX them records to set us up. Seriously?) and we get in there and start setting up. Thanks for all the encouragement, I'll let you all know how it's going as it comes together.

    Jason, We've Dem comic art classes, and I really want to come up with a model that works for game design seminars or classes, too. I envision it as being something like an extended Con seminar; when we do events that have a teaching/discussion component they always go over way better than just a plain old book signing, so that's what we really want to do when we're up and running.

    Any of you design folks who end up in NYC, we'd of course love to try and set something up. This early in the game I can't make any promises of Earth-shattering turnout or anything, but we're happy to cover your train ticket and a lunch or dinner.
