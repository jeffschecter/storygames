    TI does have its own RPG, for what it's worth.

    \n

    To be fair, that RPG does make me bleed from the eyes, even though the background info is full of wonder and win.

    \n

    Were I going to make a stab at it, I might use Universalis, but I'm more than marginally perverse. It does, however, handle conflicts at a multitude of scales without a problem. I also might be tempted to use Capes, for a similar reason and to inject a bit of the air of competition back into the structure; while it's explicitly textual in-setting, having it be explicitly textual in the mechanical level certainly has some appeal.

    \n

    If for some reason I felt like doing an all-character-level game with TI -- which seems wasteful to me, but oh well -- I might well try It's Complicated ... just because. I'm not sure how that'd work at all, but I'm intrigued. Wushu might be just fine as well, depending on what kind of dynamicism you're looking for.

    \n

    Really, we need to know more of what you're looking for to give you any reasonable answer. What scale? What dynamic? Indie game design has a vast reach on both those axes, much unlike more traditional game designs, so you have to comensurately give us more to narrow down with.

    \n Hey all thanks for the directions given so far!

    What im looking for is a game focused on social/diplomatic conflict and zoomed in on the characters and their issues, against a background of inter galactic war. A dose of spy/sabotage topped off with a hint of deadly violence. I like the idea of serialized play of PTA but think i could port that over to any other indie sys quite easily.

    I was aware of the Twilight Imperium RPG, but im not interested in Trad.games and i like the way that the boardgame doesnt give you all the details, but still gives us enough to give us all of the "feeling". It seems the right amount of detail is given by the boardgame to fit in any type of story the way we want that twilight universe to be.

    I don't like: Skill lists! (dont ask) Excessive Crunch! (weapon damage tables, etc. etc.)
    I Like: Focus of rules on Story!
    I Like: to have the charactes paint the background (the way the war unfolds as well as details) the players need Lotso narrative power.
    I Like: personalized dramas.


    I have limited internet access so it might not respond as fast as others but be assured im very happy with all your great ideas!

    cheers!

    \n Hello Timeless,

    \nI am actually testing an RPG game for Richard Hatch (from BSG not Survivor) right now. It is based on a new movie he is working on called the Great War of Magellan. http://greatwarofmagellan.com
    \n
    \nThe universe he builds is very similar to the Twilight Universe but everyone is adapted or modified humans. Basic gist is...
    \n
    \nOld race of humans colonized the Clouds of Magellan and started many colonies. The great race vanishes and the human races each lose contact with each other and evolve differently according to their own world. Old race comes back and a great war starts again.
    \n
    \nThe mechanics are different to me from most rpgs I have played (rolling for a middle score rather than a high or a low) but so far it plays well. This could be easily adapted to the Twilight universe just requiring the races to be made (variables changes, bonuses, deficiencies put into place etc.) There are already about a dozen different human styles with their own abilities including low tech, high tech etc so adapting these to the races of Twilight should be a weekend task for a good crew.
    \n
    \nThere are still hit bonuses, weapon bonuses etc but if each player has their character sheet done right that and dice should be all you need to play.
    \n
    \nPolitics, Technology, Action, Objectives, Racial Diversity, Ancient races and History are all important in play just like with TI so I feel adapting it would be a fun if not simple thing to do. The game is still in the Alpha/Beta stages but so far it plays well.
    \n
