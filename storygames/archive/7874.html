    \n Friar Rush
    A goblin clergyman eating a book. There is an enthusiastic glee to the destruction and the strange little man may even be scattering papers far and wide. He squats inhumanly before a stained glass window of a holy man eating a bible (an old symbol of knowledge) and has clearly misinterpreted it. The goblin is not dumb, just an exiled creature of faerie that is easily confused by human customs, trying to fit in to the monastery to which he was banished.

    He is, perhaps, a bit too enthusiastic and just a tad bit sinister, is that why he was banished from fairyland? How do his fellow monks regard his behavior? Does the stalwart abbot share Friar Rush's secret, hiding his misadventures out of a desire to learn the secrets of faerie, compassion, a fear of shame or all three? Is Friar Rush a beloved troublemaker or a problematic curse? How many of the other monks share one opinion or the other or vacillate between the two of them? Has anyone seen this spectacle? A child taken in by the clerical order, perhaps? A group of fellow monks? If so, are they aghast or amused or angered?

    One thing I can say for sure, Friar Rush has a love of cakes and jam and it shows.

    I'd like to see a portrait of my "tomte" (may be translated with "goblin", but not as you see it). The "tomte" is a race of small (30-50 cm) humanoids with pink skin. They are inhabitants of my fantasy world Fabula, and are among the most powerful humanoids of the world, however small and unassuming they seems. They don't wear much clothes, and they love their white hair to be long and flowing, and wear their nails long and curly (no need for practical fingers with such powers). Most of the tomten lives in great mythical trees, hundreds of fathoms high, imbued with mystical powers tomten thrives on (babies grow up sucking the treesap).

    Amongst them we also count the "mossman". the mossman is a tomte dedicated to the element of earth. These tomten cloth themselves in their element and wanders far and wide. They look like small heaps of moss and heather. They stand still and observe happenings in the world, but when they move it is with astonishing speed. The mossman blend in perfectly with the forest floor, until he opens his big, black eyes.

    Ooh. Charcoal. That's a tough one. I fear charcoal almost as much as I fear watercolor.
    \n
    \n
    \nHere's a technique my old art teacher showed us (maybe you're already familiar with it) that made working with charcoal a bit more fun for me.
    \n
    \nTake a paper towel (preferably one of the really rugged types you find at schools in the dispensers),
    \n
    \nRoll the paper in such a way to make a sharp tip.
    \nRub the tip on the charcoal.
    \nDraw with tip.
    \n
    \nIf you want dark fine lines, you rub really hard on the charcoal and draw only short bits.
    \nIf you want gradated shades, use the side of the paper.

    \n The Empress Zahara Zhan, the Dreambreaker. Eclipse and Solar Circle Sorceress.

    Her blonde hair and blue eyes are all that remain of how she once looked, and she has cut her hair to be short and spiky. Her eyes are haunted, wary. So perhaps nothing is the same at all.

    Elegant traceries of Orichalcum glint throughout her skin in swirling golden tattoos. Few know that these marks were once the scars of a week of twisted torture, reclaimed and formed into something greater, something beautiful. The irony of the scars and the golden leaf-and-vine inscribed collar that sits, inert, on her neck is apparent when one realizes that she has been known as the foremost torturer of the age, creator of the original Essence-blocking collars. In a way, she appreciates this.

    She wears a dress made of the deep red of true love, accented with last night's sunset. A simple gold tiara rests on her head, and an equally simple pendant hangs below the collar on her neck. She carries an enormous blue Jade daiklave, and a large bell and four water-enchanted swords hover around her protectively.

    \n Here are four characters I'd love to see drawn by someone in a more realistic style (my own style is a lot manga-like).

    Earth Father Terranus, God of Earth, Nature and Stability
    (middle/old aged man; reptilean features; celt culture; father)

    Sky Mother Aeolya, Godess of Air, Creativity and Change
    (middle aged beautiful woman; avian features; persian/gypsy culture; mother)

    Blue Scholar Aquarium, God of Water, Science and Reason
    (young man; fish features; mayan culture; son)

    Red Warrioress Pyra, Godess of Fire, War and Passion
    (young woman; feline features, chinese/japanese culture; daughter)

    See, they are Gods, so they should look magnificent and proud. Their features should be human, but with some features indicating the type of creatures they created (more human than anthropomorphic animal). Their garbs should have some influences from the real world cultures I listed, but the artist can take as many liberties he feels like (it is a fantasy setting after all). I'm more interested in the feel and color than in the historical accuracy.

    \n A Couple of Steampunk concepts:

    The Widow Greene

His cast iron hide is fashioned to look like that of a Victorian business man, but his size and proportions do not help disguise him. He takes to wearing a large trenchcoat and wide-brimmed hat to minimize just how much he stands out in polite company.

      \n Aztec conquistador post-apocolyptic punk quester young person. In the shambles left by Cort\xe9s, Pagualtzon, at ten and four years, tenuously holds onto his status as a noble, but he's quickly losing everything. The old upper class is no more, so if people treat him at best like a trusted message runner or favored trader, at least keeps him eating for another day. And everything else has gone to shit anyway. With luck, that bastard Cort\xe9s will return to reforge the empire he fell. Pagualtzon plans to meet him on the way. He will lead Cort\xe9s to the new empire's first enemies, the Centzon Totochtin, a group of dishonorable bandits who claim to be representatives of the gods of anarchy. If he can, he will become a conquistador.

      \n I gots to get me in on this. So, let's see. . .

      Dox Proteum is a treasured celebrity and socialite in the Ideal, the virtual world to which humanity has increasingly retreated as technology has eased more and more of the physical burden of human life. Now a priesthood of the denial of crude flesh enforces the orthodoxy of virtual living, and everyone but the gutter-trash are jacked in all the time, new generations never even knowing the outside world. In this mutable universe a person can "be" anything they envision.

      Dox's trademark appearance is that of a lean man with a wry aqualine face, befitting his status as an intellectual and personality. His half-lidded eyes might sometimes betray that he has started to weary of this shallow hedonistic life, and entertain heretical thoughts of life in the world of matter. His virtual hair falls in long cascades colored at his whim, and his rectangular floating tinted spectacles are more an expressive prop than visual aid. He has the body of a slim sex god, sheathed in futuristic and suave clothing.

      Bonus points for drawing Dox in his before/after states, "after" being his true body--pot-bellied, dessicated, long stringy hair--emerging from his pod dripping with fluid and trailing wires. Or possibly wandering the real-world streets as a rag-shrouded fugitive, unable to survive on his own.
