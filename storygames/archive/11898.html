\n Well, Rustin Simons has caused me to burp out another stupid pun, and i'm going to offer it up to be done a thing about. it's like this.

joepub is a game designer who posts to these boards.

there is a concept in role-playing games called a "dungeon crawl", wherein a party of well-equipped adventurers parade methodically through a series of encounters, killing monsters and taking their stuff.

there is a concept in towns with more than one pub called a "pub crawl", wherein a party of well-inebriated
celebrants parade enthusiastically from one drinking establishment to the next.


so.

i challenge you, dear reader, to:

1. create a dungeon complex (which is entirely open to interpretation) wherein a group of adventurers (or whomever, really) can parade through, methodically or no, going from encounter to encounter...

2. which is titled Joe Pub Crawl...

3. and engages, to various degrees, one or several or joe's games, a sampling of which are titled Perfect, Ribbon Drive, The Night It Died, Heart of Ashes, or any other project he's had a hand in...

4. and can be played using either its own internal system or another indie story-game which is currently on my shelf. (list below)

bonus points will be awarded if...
* it is fun.
* it explains why it will be fun, so that i might convince friends to play it with me.
* it is funny.
* it can be played within a single session.
* joe likes it too.
* it incorporates other things that joe likes (list below)
* it has illustrations.
* you are tony dowler or ralph mazza.

OK REALLY!

& i shall promise two immediacies and two eventualities to the winner:
upon being declared the winner, i shall review their Joe Pub Crawl favorably & post that to RPG.net or a reasonable website of their choice, and i shall arrange for a photograph to be taken of their printed-out winning entry with me holding it.

eventually (like, by summer) i shall mail to the winner 1 pretty visual thing (which will be a game-thing, of some kind) and 1 pretty audio thing (which will be a cd of music that i have made).

there is only one way to find out if these prizes are going to be good, and that's to win the competition.

IMPORTANT NOTE: this is practice. don't freak out if it's not exactly right. this is joyful. make & enjoy making, the fact that there will be a product produced is besides the point.

deadline: MIDNIGHT ON SUNDAY THE 18TH OF APRIL 2010.

post to this thread to declare entry.
