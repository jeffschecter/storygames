Posted By: McdaldnoI've seen tears stream in 5 (I think) Ribbon Drive games so far.

It's emotionally provoking in a few ways: (1) it pushes towards real drama over big drama. Sometimes that makes for stories that echo hard experiences we've had. (2) There's lots of moments of "boring," just like the game that Jenskot's OP describes. Having lulls in the action allow you to just connect with your character, and hear their thoughts. (3) The game is very immersive, and the mechanics are very light and require very little handling time. There's nothing mediating conversations. (4) The game isn't about succeeding at a task; it's about exploring an outlook. When you aren't getting swept away by the success/failure paradigm, you have more energy to put towards empathizing and resonanting.
