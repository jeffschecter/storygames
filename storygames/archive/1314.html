    September 2006
    Well, Judd, these are exciting days for Andy in GamingLand. Here's what's up:

    -1) The work on Tenra Bansho Zero is coming to a critical stage. The translations are to be completed this month. Then the REAL work begins on shuffling them all into the game. Coming up with new rules and new game advice. Getting Microfiction and Other Related Writing written for the thing so that it's understandable/playable for non-Japanese.A dn playtesting the fuck out of it in record time to make sure it all does what it says it does. Then layout begins at the end of Fall / early Winter.

    0) The new dining room table, aka the new gaming table, arrives next week from Furnitureland South. This is the most significant purchase we've made since we moved into the new house last year, and it was motivated equally by Gaming as by Eating. It is a thing of beauty, and there will be pictures.

    1) My bi-weekly experimental gaming group (read: Mostly indie games) is starting the first "actual full-on gaming session" of Burning Wheel next week (though we did play about 15 minutes last session). We are all lords of a thieves' guild of Lankhmar, and the opening scene was "You are in the Guildmaster's quarters. The Guildmaster lays dead at your feet. And now someone is knocking at the door behind you." This will be a 5 session game, each session protraying one full day (night) in succession. Like 24, but with a day per session.

    After this, the jury's out on what's next. Probably Tenra Bansho, as we see what works and what needs work.

    2) Upcoming Events: NC Gameday in September (Sat), where I plan to bring TBZ but will first see what else is being run. Then Camp Nerdly in October, and finally MACE in November, where I'll be running TBZ at both. Inside there is also helping judge the MACE Game Chef competition, which is pretty much like baby Game Chef; easy, light and fun.

    3) My weekly 'more traditional' group will finish up our current Time Cop PTA game in 3 weeks. Then we will decide what to do next. This is really exciting because:

    A) Folks (me too) are interested in trying something more crunchy/tactical for a while. So we're gonna have to decide on a ruleset. Much of me is leaning towards D&D 3.0, as it's tactical, but not quite "requires minis". We would do Burning Wheel or Riddle of Steel, but we want a system where it's not too much of a learning curve (as one of the players is very much the "social gamer", and while she is a supergenius at psychology, Java development and research, for some reason game rules just never stick for her), and also ALL of us have experience with good ol d20.

    There was some interest in using HeroQuest rules, but I think too much of the rulebook is tied to Glorantha to be of much use to us. I'm kinda eyeing for when Mythic Russia will be out, to see if it scratches the itch of "HeroQuest, but a little distanced from Glorantha".

    Mark, the usual quiet one of the group, told me that he's thinking that he wants to try running something. Out of the blue. This made me flip out, because it shows me that he's really excited about gaming, and because I'm the de facto GM:

    Jeremie is concerned that his English isn't good enough to run a good adventure. Which isn't really true, but enough of a concern of his that he's afraid. But he's awesome and theatrical as a player.
    Beth is the social gamer: She expressed interest in running something at some time, but she's really busy with job, child, and other distractions. But Alan and I have encouraged her to think about it, and she isn't completely opposed.
    Alan is the Other GM, but tends to do the whole trad roleplaying thing (Roll behind the screen; When we fail our dice roll, but it Needs to Happen (ie Finding The Clue) we do anyways; there's 'one solution' to 'the problem'; etc) which sometimes the others get bothered with. I'd love him to run again as I'm kinda tired these days, but he's busy as hell with his new job, coding on the side, and leaving the country every other month on business/SVG conference trips.
    And Me.

    So right now Mark and I are thinking that we go to something like d20, FATE, Storyteller, HeroQuest or whatever and have everyone get "the book" to get familiar with the game. From that point, I'll GM a small "story arc" or something in the setting we choose (we may come up with our own again for player buy-in). Then after a few sessions I'll turn it over to Mark for the next part and help him out (as it's the first time he'd ever be GM-ing).

    Anyway, that was really fucking exciting, to have someone else in my group who had never GMed before step up and want to give it a shot. I want to be careful with choice of system and all to make sure we're all satisfied and that it goes well for him.

    -Andy
