\n Ok, I'm totally new to this. I've never designed a game before and I'm nervous even posting this. I just discovered this contest today. I don't even know if I'll finish by Sunday but I'll give it a go. My game's called The Keys of Absence. It uses Sight B, Sound A, and Concept B.

Here's the grab'em blurb:
You\xe2\u20ac\u2122re dead. But your still here on Earth. Your memories of your life are empty but for one thing: you remember doing little to \xe2\u20ac\u0153follow that dream\xe2\u20ac\x9d. Not much more than baby steps. Rack your brains all you want. You won\xe2\u20ac\u2122t remember that dream or what kept you from it. That\xe2\u20ac\u2122s why your still here. Not enough spirit to break out of this place. And soon what little\xe2\u20ac\u2122s left of you will just disappear like morning haze. However, there\xe2\u20ac\u2122s one last hope. Find a breather who\xe2\u20ac\u2122s like you used be. Get in his head, find his dream, and set it free. Maybe you\xe2\u20ac\u2122ll find yourself in there. Just look out for the keys.

In a nutshell, a ghost of someone who failed to achieve it\xe2\u20ac\u2122s dream gets one last shot at the golden ring before it\xe2\u20ac\u2122s spirit disappears forever. The ghost enters the psyche/mind of a living person and hunts down that person\xe2\u20ac\u2122s dream. But something in the living person\xe2\u20ac\u2122s mind doesn\xe2\u20ac\u2122t want that dream found.

Three things I've learned so far: 1) I don't know what the hell I'm doing; 2) I'm having some trouble putting my ideas into words; 3) I type really slow.

I'll post more when I have something that makes sense.

    The Key of Absence: A Game of Senses and Symbols

    In a nutshell, a ghost of someone who failed to achieve his/her dream gets one last shot at the golden ring before it\xe2\u20ac\u2122s spirit disappears forever. The ghost enters the subconscious of a living person and hunts down that person\xe2\u20ac\u2122s dream. But something in the living person\xe2\u20ac\u2122s mind doesn\xe2\u20ac\u2122t want that dream found. This game uses Sight B, Sound A, and Concept B (which I will point out throughout this document).

    Basic game structure

    Each turn one player acts as the judge and decides who gains control of a scene. The player to the judge\xe2\u20ac\u2122s left plays either the Ghost, and the player to her right plays the Hold (the breather\xe2\u20ac\u2122s block to his dream). Each turn the other players join with either the ghost or the host based on a randomizer used playing card suits. The game ends when the breather is fully inspired (Concept or demoralized, or if the Ghost finds 3 keys and fades away.

    \xc2\xb7\tBlank paper or index cards (preferred)
    \xc2\xb7\t3 to 7 keys (depending on the number of players)
    \xc2\xb7\tA vessel from which to draw the index cards and keys (a hat, a bag, etc.)
    \xc2\xb7\tPencils or pens
    \xc2\xb7\tPlaying cards
    \xc2\xb7\t3-8 people
    \xc2\xb7\tThe Key of Absence (the sound file for Sound A)

    Set up

    Each player creates three phrases describing a visual image, one image inferring something mundane, one joyful, and one despairing. Write each on an index card. Label each card or use a different color for each type of image. Each player also gets some index cards and describes one sound on each card. The only sounds excluded are those made by the human voice. Don\xe2\u20ac\u2122t show other players your sounds.
    No. of Sounds Cards
    4-5 players\xe2\u20ac\u201dfour cards each
    6-7 players\xe2\u20ac\u201dthree cards each
    8 players\xe2\u20ac\u201dtwo cards each

    Players may choose to write one sound on up to two image card. Only two sounds can be used in this way. At least one image card will have no sound attached.

    Print three copies of the key photo (Sight , if possible (see notes). Write the word \xe2\u20ac\u02dckey\xe2\u20ac\u2122 on some index cards. Place the image cards and the keys in your \xe2\u20ac\u02dchat\xe2\u20ac\u2122.

    Before Judging

    The Judge pulls a card from the \xe2\u20ac\u02dchat\xe2\u20ac\u2122 and reads the image written on it but doesn\xe2\u20ac\u2122t show it to anyone. The Ghost turns over a card from the deck.     Suit

    scene is part of the Ghost\xe2\u20ac\u2122s deferred dream

All the players then choose the sound card they hold that they feel best \xe2\u20ac\u02dcpowers\xe2\u20ac\u2122 their side of the struggle and places it face down. If a club or diamond is in play, players affected by it who haven\xe2\u20ac\u2122t yet chosen a side must do so now

    Judging

    When the players are ready the Judge tells them to reveal their sounds. She decides which sound prevails in the scene (similar to Apples to Apples). The Judge may only consider the situation of the current scene, disregarding any previous results. However, she chooses the winner as she sees fit. If a player plays \xe2\u20ac\u02dcthe distant sound of church bells\xe2\u20ac\u2122 as his sound, and it happens to be one the Judge\xe2\u20ac\u2122s favorite sounds, she may choose it simply for that reason. The Judge need not explain herself.

    The only exception to this is if a player chooses a sound written on the image card. That sound trumps all others. The Judge declares the player of the trump sound the winner. A player only gets one trump win per game. In the unlikely event two players both have a trump sound the Judge is free to choose from the remaining sounds in play.

    \n After Judging

    The player who holds the winning sound narrates the effect of the sound on the scene and inspires or corrupts the breathers Vision:

    A Vision of adjective inspirational or transformational noun to verb (the) noun/phrase and verb the Key of noun.

    If the player is part of the Ghost he inspires hope or joy by adding a word derived from the scene to one of the empty spots in breathers Vision, or changing a word corrupted by the Hold. Add the words from the end of the sentence to the beginning. Also, the Ghost may instead choose to remove a Key if he wins any scene when a spade is in play.

    If the player is the part of the Hold he removes hope by changing a verb, adjective, or the type of Key to another word based on the scene just played. He may only add a word if there are none for him to corrupt.

    If the player who won the scene used a trump sound draw a circle around the winning sound. Winning sounds can no longer be used when playing as or joining with the Ghost, and those cards remains face up on the table. The player gains the benefit of adding one additional sensory element for each face up card. So, if a player has 2 cards face up he may add three sensory elements. He may add them all at once or add them as play moves around the table. Unfortunately, he won\xe2\u20ac\u2122t get the benefit of this on the next turn, as the winner of the scene becomes the next Judge.

    Play continues in this way until the players complete a fully realized Vision of Hope or a fully realized Vision of Despair, or three keys are drawn.

    End game

    Play the sound (A) file, which is The Key of Absence, once players meet one of the conditions mentioned above. Put all the image cards back in the \xe2\u20ac\u02dchat\xe2\u20ac\u2122 and remove any remaining keys. All players choose an image card from the \xe2\u20ac\u02dchat\xe2\u20ac\u2122.

    If The Key of Absence ends the game: Using the image on the card as a starting point, write the Ghost\xe2\u20ac\u2122s dream\xe2\u20ac\u201dusing things revealed in play\xe2\u20ac\u201das it\xe2\u20ac\u2122s memory returns in the moment the Ghost fades . . .

    If the Vision of Despair ends the game: Using the image on the card as a starting point, write how the Hold ultimately defeats the Ghost and expels it from the breather.

    If the Vision of Hope end the game: Using the image on the card as a starting point, write how the breather achieves the vision and how the Ghost, watching the realization unfold, moves on using the spirit it\xe2\u20ac\u2122s gained.

    Take no more than 5 minutes, and use only one index card while writing. Players take turns sharing what they wrote.
