    \n I had a great time too. Played in John's Clancy-world game of The Regiment, a little Parisian melody of carnage followed by a late night blood opera session of Saga of the Icelanders. The next day I ran my somewhat raunchy hack of Lady Blackbird to an amazing group of players. It being the first time I'd really ever run anything for any Story Gamers, I admit I started out with a small case of nerves, but things took off nicely and it turned out to be a smooth flight.

    Thanks, all. Really fun weekend.

    \n I got to play Kingdom with Mike, Abi and Jamie. Mighty KBQX, 10 watts of throbbing television power, serving the Greater Albuquerque community! It's a playtest and I'm supposed to be paying attention how the rules work and stuff, but most of the time they just had me in stitches.

    Before we started, Mike mentioned something about being rusty, not role-playing enough, and then immediately proceeded to rock it as the visionary but completely inexperienced new owner of the station, filled with grand ideas to "strategize!" and "monetize!" by moving the station to this thing called the Internet or (dum-dum-dum) basic cable! But he was also touchingly unwilling to abandon his childhood friend and hanger-on, Baker, even when it completely jeopardizes the station.

    I loved that Abi's producer character was all power skirt-suits and hardball office politics on the outside, but secretly she had a school girl's undying love and faith in her kept man, a struggling stand-up comic who she really believed in with all her heart! She was also constantly embezzling from the station to fund her beautiful home and their decadent lifestyle together. But when she decided to abandon Albuquerque and follow her lover's dream to New York, Jamie's formerly sincere and trusting production assistant lost his innocence and blackmailed her with evidence of her embezzling so she wouldn't leave and let the station collapse. She didn't show up for their flight and she couldn't explain why she couldn't go with him, Casablanca-style\u2026

    Oh, and I got to call our viewing audience a pack of idiotic, inbred harpies on live television. Fantasy come true.
