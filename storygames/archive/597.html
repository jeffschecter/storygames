    April 2006 edited April 2006
    Larry, you were obviously never a Rifts player.

    *Ahem*

    Human being have Hit Points and S.D.C. (Structural Damage Capacity).

    When you take normal damage, you subtract points from your S.D.C. first, before proceeding to your Hit Points.

    Objects have S.D.C. only. No Hit Points.

    Now, things like tanks, giant robots, supernatural creatures, and most P.C.s who don't want to die have M.D.C. (Mega-Damage Capacity). They cannot be damaged by things that only deal normal damage. They can only be damaged by attacks that deal Mega-Damage (M.D.).

    In the case of a giant robot stepping on a human, 1 M.D. is roughly equivilent to 100 S.D.C. damage (which is redundant, but no one ever just says S.D.). So even a small amount of Mega-Damage will make unprotected humans go squish.

    So, to summerize, when you implied that the "hit points" of the Laser Penguins "wouldn't be expressed in MegaDamage," the term you were obviously reaching for was actually "M.D.C." or "Mega-Damage Capacity."
