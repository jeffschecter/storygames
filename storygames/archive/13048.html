\n This is a play set for Jason Morningstar's Fiasco RPG that takes place around the night of October 30th in Detroit, Michigan. The city goes up in flames...during a vampire apocalypse.

I intended this to be a bit darker and more serious than most other Fiasco playsets already out there...more straight-up horror, and less zaniness. Take a look and let me know if you see anything that seems flat, particularly good, or too out of place for Detroit or the intended story direction.

    \n OK, I read this set. It is a little too heavily-themed for my taste, but that's just preference - I think Devil's Night alone is interesting enough without the vampires. I don't see why it wouldn't work as written, though. I worry a little about the relationships, because many are very specific and may not play well with other likely combinations. My advice from the upcoming Fiasco Companion on this issue:\n
    It's possible you'll have a playset that relies on certain assumptions, and breaking these assumptions can lead to Relationships that can't possibly mesh. A good example of this is Lucky Strike, which has the default assumption that each player's character will be an American soldier. Change that up by adding a nurse, and things go a little haywire:


    Relationship: Replacement Increments: Officer's Aide and Supply Clerk
    Relationship: Brothers-In-Arms: Separated From Their Unit
    Relationship: The Black Market: Army CID Investigator and Fixer
    Relationship: Special cases: Nurse and Soldier

    So the nurse is probably a fixer, since it is unlikely she's moonlighting with CID. But is she also the officer's aide? Or the supply clerk? And that makes the CID investigator separated from his unit somehow ... it begins to get weird, fast. Maybe your group loves to roll with that sort of thing, but it can be awkward.

    While a little discretion when putting together a playset may alleviate this sort of tangle, sometimes it is really great to include the oddball Relationships. In this case, my advice is to do what I did in Lucky Strike - sequester the ones that might break things into one list of six, and flag it with "use caution". That way everyone can work to make the oddball choice fit into the overall Setup by being a little more thoughtful about how they choose to connect to that Relationship.

    \n I have now played Devil's Night twice, with different groups. I think both games went very smoothly.

    To address the relationships issue, I'm going to add a bit more to The Score, explaining or proposing that a vampire might pretend to be normal, and hold a regular job like a TV cameraman.

    I made a point of asking whether anyone had a problem with the stalker/pursued relationship classified as Romance, but no one did. I'll probably just rename the category to 'Romance and Obsession' or something.

    And 'Twins' will be renamed 'Identical Twins' in the next version.
