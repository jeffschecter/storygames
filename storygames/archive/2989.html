\n I was reading a book about making zines (yeah, irony) and one of the formats it talked about was the minibook, a 32 page zine made from a single sheet of letter-sized paper. Super cool, thought I, so I made a couple and perfected an indesign template.

Here's my first try. Contact me if you'd like the .indd file to make your own - they are fun and I think the format has potential. You can cram about 3,500 words in there. There's some labor involved but you can't beat the paper cost. If you print out my little book, share your experience - was it hard? Was it worth it? If one came as part of a game .pdf package, would you bother?

    Posted By: Jason MorningstarIf one came as part of a game .pdf package, would you bother?
    \nIn all honesty? Probably not.

    Firstly, the type is very small, I found myself straining to read it. Part of being myopic and also having hey fever right now.

    Secondly, it took me 2 goes of printing to get the thing to line up right on A4. I should have thought better about it before I started really, but there you go.

    Thirdly, my stapler hates me.

    As an object it's pretty neat, and fun and I like looking at and owning it. But the same text just laid out neatly as two columns of regular size text on a sheet of A4 or Letter is actually more useful to me to be honest, easier to read, easier to store and far easier to prepare.

    \n The heck with staples, bind the book. All you need is some string or dental floss, a pushpin, and some tape (something stronger than clear scotch tape) to cover the binding. Start at step 5 at http://www.diyplanner.com/node/442 . There's something like a dozen steps, but it's totally easy and the pages will seem to stay together better than stapling. Use the tape to cover the spine of the book to get a nice reinforced spine (as seen in the image at http://www.readinga-z.com/ ). The first time I tried binding a small folio like this it took about four minutes.

    \n Hah, I finally got a chnace to play around with pocketmods a bit and now have my own Pocket-Mod copy of Wushu Open Reloaded:



    This is the first prototype ('nother picture here and here.) and it shows, but I'm pretty satisfied. (Especially considering that it was made around midnight, watching a funny Japanese Zombie movie at the same time. Stoned.)

    First thing I did was up the font size of the file after I realized that every normal page would become a single pocketmod page. Wushu Open Reloaded covers six pocketmods (With the last page going into the seventh, but I didn't print that.), so after printing them out, cutting away the borders (Badly.) and folding them (Badly.) I punched a hole through each one and made two rings out of paperclips to hold them all together. (Even in the Wushu red-and-black. Yay!)

    The whole thing is nicely readable, though the font resize did mess up the layout a bit, and it fits nicely into my pocket. It also seems like it would withstand a bit of wear and tear. (And it took me about half an hour making it and cost me nothing, so it's not like it would be much of a loss.) Nifty little pocketbook.

    There are things that could be done better, though:

    - The folding sucks, which means that a few individual 'mods look really, really sloppy and don't feel as nice to read/browse as others. It also means that some of the holes I punched didn't hole all the inner folds, instead just cutting half-circles out of some of them.

    - Next time, I'll redo the layout a bit making the font a bit smaller, so that I don't have to leave out the last pages. There are also a few breaks in the text that I could leave out to save space.

    - This thing really needs a nice cover. ^_^

    - The two "open pages" per pocketmod feel kinda sloppy, like you made a mistake when folding. Can't see a way to change it as it's just a part of pocketmods folding scheme, but maybe I could glue them together or somehow incorporate them into the books as a whole. (Pockets for Character sheets or something...)

    - A better way to hold the thing together. The former-paperclips are funtional and hold together will, but they're ugly and I'd prefer something that makes the thing feel more booklike. (Originally I'd planned to make the paperclip into a spiral and even had a nice one bent already, but it didn't really work.) Not really sure how to do that yet, though, at least not short of making a real book cover and gluing/stiching/stapling the mods in. Another idea would be to re-do the layout so that the frot and backs of the inner 'mods will be blank and gluing them together.

    --------------------------------------

    Another thing I plan to to is create something of an "eight page wushu" so that it fits in one pocketmod and then print a bunch of character sheets on the other side. So you could read the rules, re-fold it and then have a place for the characters and notes on the other side. (Of course, Wushu is kinda rules-light for a cheatsheet. Probably works better for "heavier" games. ^_^)

    \n The way I've done saddle-stitching (that's staple-binding for layfolk) is to open up a stapler so that when you trigger it, the staple just shoots out into the air. What you get is an unbent staple that you can use for measuring where to punch little holes into the spine with a push pin. Once you've punched those little holes, stick the "arms" of the staple into them so that they are on the inside of the book while the flat part is on the outside. (Looking at a staplebound magazine for reference helps a lot.) Fold the arms by hand and you've got yourself a nicely staple-bound little book. I made mockups like this all the time in school and still do at work on occasion.

(Looking at a staplebound magazine for reference helps a lot.) Fold the arms by hand and you've got yourself a nicely staple-bound little book. I made mockups like this all the time in school and still do at work on occasion.

    \n A little FYI, folks: You can use a modern HP PostScript Printer Definition (PPD) file from Adobe as your Print To File "printer" to get a PS file which you then distill (Acrobat Distiller) to PDF.

    Why would you do that? Because it has Booklet Printing and Multi-Page-Up print options. Fiddle around with the right values, and you can get ANY program to spit out a mini book, of just about any scale (half page, quarter page, ninth page, etc--just make your page template suit the ACTUAL end dimensions once it's printed Multi-Up and Booklet). It's the simplest thing in the word to do a basic half-page (the default booklet setting), and that doesn't even have to be typeset scaled (the print driver just halves everything and landscapes the page).

    Only limitation is 32 sheets per bundle (per signature, technically) and so you might have a practical page limit of 128 pages (each sheet is two-up, duplex, and so has four pages on it, total).

    \n OK, so two things spring to mind regarding minibooks and RPGs:
    \n
    \n1. If you had a game that could be played with only a deck of cards, a minibook could probably be inserted into the pack such that all you needed to play could be placed in your shirt pocket. In this case, the minibook could be either a character sheet or the rules. The pocketmod, listed above, could be cool with the rules on one side and the character sheet on the other - this way you print and fold a bunch, hand them out for players to read, then they unfold and write up their character. That would be awesome swag/demo/autographed item at a convention.
    \n
    \n2. There is a board game called "Imperial" that uses a rondel mechanic. Imagine a pie with an action on each slice. You start on one slice and can go around the pie to your next action. The catch is that the first couple moves are free and subsequent moves cost you a resource - the effect being that it limits the frequency with which you take each type of action. I could see using a minibook like a rolladex with each page having an aspect or action or type of scene. You manipulate the story by flipping pages (if you reach the end, go to the beginning) - the first couple are free and if you want to flip more pages it will cost you "pacing" points (which you get by good role playing).
