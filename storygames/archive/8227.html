      This seems like a reasonable request.
      I do wonder, however, why you consider the comparison inappropriate (i don't think a comparison can really be "incorrect" as such)?

      i haven't read much of Mouse Guard yet so I would hesitate myself to say whether I think it's an appropriate comparison or not, but it does seem to be an inevitable one. Even if the mechanical systems didn't share certain aspects, nearly anyone who produces creative works (or, sometimes, even non-creative works) has their latest work discussed in comparison to past works. It happens with movies, novels, music, paintings, and I've certainly seen it happen with RPGs written/designed by the same person, even for games much different from each other than (as I've been led to understand) Mouse Guard is from BW or BE.

      But I do totally think it's fair to request people to try discussing the game on its own terms, as a discrete entity. Once I have my physical copy and have more thoroughly read it (and hopefully played it) I plan on taking part in that conversation, for sure.

Gwendolyn is worried, both because the events it describes might be true and because it's causing mice to lose confidence in Lockhaven or even openly mock guardmice. Your patrol has been assigned to investigate its origins and get to the bottom of things.

      The Tragic Tale of Caroline
      (to a tune somewhere between "O For a Thousand Tongues to Sing" and "How Can I Keep from Singing?")

      Far down the road
      In Woodruff's Grove
      Beneath a rosy pine
      Was born a childe
      Both brave and mild
      They call'd her Caroline

      She grew up strong
      With will of iron
      And when it fell her time
      She gave her heart
      Full to the Guard
      For such was Caroline

      Silent they stole
      to Woodruff's Grove
      With intents serpentine
      Those wicked stoats
      Cut all their throats
      Except for Caroline

      "You vicious knaves
      Shall meet your graves!
      By all means take your time,
      For soon the Guard
      Will hit you hard!"
      Or so said Caroline

      They took her then
      Back to their den
      And gave their dark decree
      "There's no patrol
      That's quite so bold
      As to return for thee"

      The days they pass'd
      First slow, then fast
      The snow fell all around
      But none there came
      And so the flame
      Of Caroline burn'd out

      Now there's no road
      to Woodruff's Grove
      And weasels roam the pines
      For still the Guard
      With hearts so hard
      Comes not for Caroline
