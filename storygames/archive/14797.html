    The dice are paper! I got set up to make paper toys and figured I may as well make paper dice. Here is my experience with the dice so far.

    Durability
    They can stand being shaken in the same dice bag as normal dice, even being tossed or thrown. these were printed with ink jet, any products I offer would be either offset printed onto coated stock or quality color copies making the die surfaces more durable and semi gloss.

    Balance
    Currently refining the balance, it's technical but i'm confident these dice will be more balanced then any of the rounded corner dice you are using.

    looks
    unless you are using a colored stock (a great option) the edge of the paper will always be white, with this design I had the color fade around the edges to make the white paper edge work with the color of the die. as these are printed there are no real limit to how fancy the die faces can be.

    Assembly
    Just fold and insert tabs into slots, the edges come scored.

    Price
    CHEAP not sure what the final cost will be but i'm shooting for 12-16 1/2 inch dice for around 5 bucks, not sure how to handle custom jobs for individuals at the moment but i will offer the most popular dice when I relaunch papercrafted.com

    so anyone interested? i will take a few to PAX Prime if you want to see them in person.
