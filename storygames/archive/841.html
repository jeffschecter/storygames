    Half-Owlbear

    Description: Half-Owlbears are an awkward, strange-looking race that was never meant to be. The progeny of an owlbear and some hapless star-crossed mate, half-owlbears resemble great shaggy feathered lumps, their bulk disguising powerful musculature and a bestial cunning.

    Stat Bonuses and Penalties

    The Half-Owlbear's stat modifiers are derived from its subspecies.

    The Owlbear-Humanoid The most common kind of Half-Owlbear, the Owlbear-Humanoid traces one half of its genetics to one of the other humanoid races of Xui-Grom. Human, goblin, feyij, or xaoctl, owlbear-humanoids all look more or less the same, with less feathers and fur and a leaner build. Unsurprisingly, Owlbear-Humanoids have the easiest time integrating with civilized society. Owlbear-Humanoids receive two extra stones which may be invested in any of: Wisdom, Strength, or Stamina.

    The Owlbear-Bear The unfortunate result of a cross of Owlbear and Bear produces this subspecies, which has an intellect that is barely capable of using language and tools. However, the Owlbear-bear is the largest and strongest of all the Half-owlbear subspecies, with hardly any feathers, no vestigial wings, and powerful legs and arms. Owlbear-bears receive three extra stones to split between Strength and Stamina, but cannot have an Intellect greater than three stones. Owlbear-Bears can Rage and Berserk in combat as if they had the Berserker Paradigm (Owlbear-Bear Berserkers get a +2 to their roll).

    The Owlbear-Owl The Owlbear-Owl is the rarest of the three subspecies, due to the understandable difficulty of a full-grown Owlbear mating with an owl. However, the offspring of such an unlikely union is the only subspecies that retains its ability to fly. Its bones are hollow, resulting in a somewhat frailer frame, but the Owlbear-Owl retains the wisdom of its three-quarters owl heritage. Many heroes have been drawn from these unlikely ranks. Owlbear-Owls receive two extra stones to split between Intellect and Wisdom; their Strength cannot be greater than four stones. Owlbear-Owls have the Flight racial power, and cannot wear metal armor.

    Paradigm Restrictions:

    Half-Owlbears are often found on the fringes of society, and as such cannot take any paradigms, job or specialty, that features a high status, such as Laser Princess, Noble Knight, Holy Cleric, Rock Paladin, or Scheming Guildsman.

      Also, oddly enough, I have come up with an attribute system for the Magestrike game. Which is, of course, the cornerstone of any good roleplaying game.

      You have attributes rated from 1 to 100. Normal humans are "5".
      The Attributes are:

      Power
      Agility
      Endurance
      Insight
      Acuity
      and Spirit

      You get a rating in them from one to one-hundred. Then, you consult a chart to see where your ability lies on a scale.
      1-15 d4
      15-40 d4 d6
      41-60 d4 d6 d8
      61-80 d4 d6 d8 d10
      81-90 d4 d6 d8 d10 d12
      91-100 d4 d6 d8 d10 d12 d20

      These are the dice that you roll for Every Roll. The rolls are almost always opposed. If you get doubles, then the width of all doubles gets added to the highest number rolled.
      Ex: I have a Wisdom Acuity of 85. I pick up my handful of dice and I roll 3, 3, 8, 3 and 7. My result is '11': 8, plus 3 for the three 1s.

      This is the most important and fundamental part of the game. Without attributes there is chaos.

      Furthermore, the dice are named:
      It is not a d4, it is Exacting Form
      It is not a d6, is it Cubitron
      It is not a d8, it is Twin Pharaoh
      It is not a d10, it is Cold Comet
      It is not a d12, it is Honeyed Disciple
      It is not a d20, it is Magestriker

      I think I will write this game as a series of koans.

      -Andy
