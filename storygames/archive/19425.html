    \n Bah, wrongFor most adventurers, supplies are going to take up whatever slot size the gear list says they do. Most of the stuff that doesn't show up on the gear list is unlikely to matter in the field. Equipment does not equal supplies. Equipment is listed in the master gear list, supplies are noted on page 36. The short answer is that supplies take up one slot, are consumed after use, and provide +1D.

    I made a svg of the skill help and supply relationships a while back. Solid lines are helps, dashed lines are supplies. Skills with a * on them don't appear in the game but are referenced as help or as providing supplies.



    I didn't put a default image size on it, so it'll end up pretty huge if you view it straight.

    \n Yes, that work chain for bonus dice all the way from roots to healing is the way it would go. The way I'd say it is that if you don't have scavenged roots for the alchemist you can still have the roots (in and amongst your 40 lbs of random stuff), you just don't get any kind of bonus for them. Necessary Tools for Skill on page 34 and Tools on page 36 eludes to the difference between Tools (a thing you need to do a skill with no penalty) and Supplies (a thing that gives you a +1D bonus), and how they interact differently with your travelling gear.

    EDIT:
    Also check out Supplies on page 36 (right below Tools). That should answer any remaining questions. I updated my post above to reflect the fact that I completely forgot about the Supplies heading.
