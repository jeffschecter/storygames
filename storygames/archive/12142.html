\n Another thread here is about how certain movies have inspired your gaming. What about the opposite? I mean, most games (many games?) now have a sort of "Inspirations" section describing what movies and books inspired the writing of a game. Eclipse Phase in particular has a really exhaustive list of inspirations. Are there any films that you've watched purely because you saw them listed in such a section of a game you like / were getting in the mood to play? If so, what did they change about your attitude towards said game or the playing thereof?

I read Down and Out in the Magic Kingdom, for example, because it was listed as a primary idea-generator for FreeMarket. Reading the book completely opened up the game for me - I "got" it after I read the book. How about you?

    \n Almost all the sources listed across the various Sorcerer supplements. I would have never known that I actually liked fantasy fiction if it wasn't for the stuff in Sorcerer & Sword. In particular Wagner's Kane stories really blew me away (for Sorcerer & Sword) and James Ellroy's L.A. Quartet (for Sorcerer's Soul).

    The one exception was my experience with Wicked City from Demon Cops. It's listed as THE reference for the game but when I watched it I was very confused. The basic setup matches the Demon Cops ideas but the follow through has very little to do with the setup. Indeed it carries a very gross and anti-sex message that I'm a little surprised Ron would be so fond of. So when I talked to Ron about it, I discovered that he got slightly confused when writing up the bibliography and mixed up the setup of Wicked City with the follow through of another film on the list. Very amusing. In any event I don't recommend Wicked City at all.

    \n When I write games, I usually do so in order to "play through" my favourite movies. I know that's not what you're asking, but from a designer standpoint... Ribbon Drive was written because I saw Wristcutters and Everything Is Illuminated and I wanted to live through those stories. My other games follow a similar pattern.

    I've often watched movies and then immediately wanted to play a certain game that I felt embodied that movie. Burn After Reading and Fiasco, for example.

    \n I think it's a pretty common brain-damage among gamers (at least, myself and mine) that when absorbing other media, the constant question lurking in the dungeons at the back of the brain is "How could I make this work as an RPG?"

    I know I get that a lot when I'm watching a movie or a game. Pretty much anything interesting inspires me in my gaming-life.

    \nDark Knight made me rethink Narrativism, which in turn has changed the way I approach design. Does that count?

    The relentless, every-single-scene approach that movie took to addressing its chief argument - is murder ever justified [which segued into "what is the nature of humanity"] - made me see how front-and-center theme could be.

    The Guy Ritchie movies make/made me very excited about designing a True Crime game of some sort, one where factions played a huge role. That could be a part of why I eventually got in on my design La Familia - I had a string of design attempts that focused on the PC-as-group-member (or as Organization Man, if you're into sociology).

    Dragonslayer felt like better-than-D&D, realized in film. It's got Peter McNichol of Ally McBeal and Numb3rs fame. Willow had a similar effect ^_^ Go, Mad Martigan! You see these archetypes up on the screen, you think about your D&D experiences (mine, anyway), and think Garsh, where'd I go wrong, and how can I go right?
