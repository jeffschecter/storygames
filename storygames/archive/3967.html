\n Hello out there in indie land.

Some folks may have noticed a distinct lack of Mischa at this year's Gen Con. For that, I apologize. I really wanted to go this year; I've never attended Gen Con, and I really need a dose of teh aw3smoe. If you number amongst the dozens who cried yourself to sleep for not seeing me, here's my situation.

My girlfriend and I moved back to New Orleans from Austin after the storm just about three weeks ago, and I'm currently jobless. I mean hey,I had a choice of do I afford con or do I afford to move home? Based on the date of the convention, I didn't pre-reg, since I had no idea where I'd be or what my situation was. I didn't even look at booking a flight in advance- who knows where in the world I would be living?

For the last two weeks, I considered a few crazy schemes to get my monkey ass up to Indianapolis, including hawking print copies from the first run of 1984 Prime to be hand-delivered at Gen Con, to outright begging on S-G ("If everyone drops five bucks into the hat..."), to didj street performance, to taking the train or ridesharing with possible crazy strangers from my LGS, to saying the hell with it and charging the lot to my credit card. I even took some one-off computer repair tasks, hoping to make a few shekels enough to afford travel. Obviously, none of these panned out and I had to live vicariously through yall- mostly Judson and Marc.

What about you? If you didn't make it this year, why? Break our hearts!

MDK

    \n My reason is dumb. I just didn't enjoy the actual GenCon part of GenCon. Most of the people I met and rocked out with after-hours were selling games all day, or part of booth operations. I couldn't handle wandering the exhibitor hall by myself, and so I decided I didn't really want to go back unless something were seriously different about the lay of the land (i.e. I had close gaming buddies who I was attending with who weren't selling games, or I had a game of my own to sell).

    Money may have been part of my excuse before, but I just bought a Wii, so that's a load of BS. It just comes down to social anxiety.
