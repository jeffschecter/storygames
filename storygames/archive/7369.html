    \n Hmmm. Here's the thing about WGP: The conflicts have to be able to escalate back and forth from talking to punching. If you can think of four ways conflict can can exist in Sailor Moon (I haven't seen the series, so I don't know), then it might work. But I tend to see that once a fight starts in an anime or manga, it rarely drifts to another mode of conflict. So it might be hard.

    Like, if I were doing "Blade of the Immortal", I might use "fearsome words/shouting", "weapon attacks", "manuvering" and "..." (something else, I can't think of anything now). That might work. But it also might make things messy. Haven't tried.

    If I were doing Revolutionary Girl Utena, I'd use "social", "willpower/psychology", "status", and "martial"... but the thing is, in Utena it never escalates through one or two of these. It would produce really weird effects, I think.

    So in my opinion, it might be a hard conversion. You can give it a shot, though. The "dude gets ass kicked, then comes back for more" is definitely similar to anime and manga story arcs, but beyond that I think you'll have a hard time deciding what the suits should be in a conflict.

    -Andy

    \n I'm quite fond of both Prime Time Adventures and With Great Power... Either one could be used for a good anime game, but With Great Power... specifically invokes the kind of feeling you only get from really, really violent animes... and comic books, of course.

    If you like animes and manga like Fist of the North Star or even moodier episodes of Ranma 1/2, then your group will probably find With Great Power... to their liking. Otherwise, I'd stick with Prime Time Adventures. It supports a wider ranger of emotions and problem solving than With Great Power... (which practically requires a fight every couple of turns to get things resolved.)

    \nWell, actually, you made me think more about this. You need to have, at any time, the option of at least 4 "different actions" to go along with the suits. But you're right, you could use them for 4 different social things like "being nice; being mean; appealing to status; appealing to friendship". However, it's good to remember that the core of WGP is that it expected to keep those ranges open as much as possible: In other words, that you could talk to the enemy in one frame, and throw a bus at them in the next. To pull that back and make 4 different shades of the same kind of activity (phsyical, social, etc)... well, it probably CAN be done. But it might be complicated. And one has no leg to stand on with blaming WGP if such a hack fails.

    It might be worth giving a shot, though. Hmmmm.
