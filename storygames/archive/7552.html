\n Ah, the sweetness of banal insights.

We started our Archipelago campaign yesterday - myself, Monica, Ole Peder, Morten, Steinar and Erling. Everyone'd actually made characters without knowing what system we'd wind up using, and there was a lot of mail to go through and info to assemble into R-maps and the like - so I was halfway through preparing for yesterday's game when I discovered that uhhh... I'd planned for a GM'ed campaign, and Archipelago has no GM. And I had no character.

I decided to test what it would like to facilitate a GM-less game, and participated without playing a character. As all players can do in Archipelago, I created destiny points for other characters, ran NPCs in their stories, called out "Draw a card!", suggested actions, described environment... I was very much in the game. However, it didn't really click; I wasn't there in the fiction, I didn't smell the smells and taste the tastes. I assumed everyone felt the same - like the session was okay, but not really performing yet.

I got up and started cooking dinner. Meanwhile, I listened to the others - not their words, but their voices. People were laughing, talking in weird voices, getting into character, doing stuff that sounded fun. It sounded like a table I'd really like to sit at. And after a while I got it.

The game is simply much more fun if you actually have a character to play. Doh!

Next time I'll try that.
