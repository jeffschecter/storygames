    \n Well then!

    The DayTrippers GameMasters Guide came out in mid-May. This expansion to the DayTrippers Core Rules supports old-school style play in the same surreal sci-fi multiverse that began as a thread here on SG.

    DayTrippers is a blend of modern and traditional game techniques that deliberately goes beyond most people's conceptions of "OSR" while remaining true to OSR standards such as "rulings not rules" and "player creativity not character skills". It's a trad game with narrativist elements.

    You can play DayTrippers as a collaborative group game with a "GM-Lite" configuration (Core Rules only), or as a heavily-prepped campaign with an Auteur GM (using both books). Same milieu, two different playstyles. I've asked around, and no one seems to have done that before. Which I found weird, so I figured it was about time. Check it out below...

    DayTrippers Website
    DayTrippers Core Rules
    DayTrippers GameMasters Guide
    DayTrippers G+ Community

    PDF only right now; PODs are in the works.

    \n Here's something totally dumb I wrote on impulse in the middle of the night: Point Man, a game that is shamelessly Dread without a GM. The idea is that, by removing the GM from the story, the players go from reactive to proactive, thus shifting the genre from slasher horror to that kind of investigative/military/"dug too deep" sort of story where the only way out is through.
