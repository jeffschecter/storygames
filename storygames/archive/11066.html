\n Hey,

People who like violence,

I need some terms for this fighty game I'm working on. It's about gangs that square off in the badlands, like Necromunda or Mordheim or Malifaux.
Ideally, I'd like the game to use setting-neutral terms, so that you could play port it to Necromunda or Mordheim or Malifaux.

You have a character! That character has some Tags, which are like tendencies or stuff they are good at or whatever.
Some examples include: Command, Grit, Precise, Weird...

During character creation, you buy Powers and Items for your character.
The Powers and Items have Tags as well.
If you have none of the Tags of the Power/Item, it costs more. If you have all of them, it costs less.

I'm trying to figure out the Tags that relate to weapon skills and fighting skills.
I thought about dividing the Tags into things like: Precision Weapons, Heavy Weapons...
Then I realized it'd be way better to divide the Tags into the combat functions, like blowin' up terrain, killin' a single dude, crowd control, etc.

So, what are those combat functions? and what are some good single word Tags to describe 'em?
The game is a story games hippy-ified tactical minis game. Sort of.
It's about gangs (of 2-12), who fight in ruins and terrain-heavy places.

So far I've identified:

1.) Blowin' up terrain, using explosives (Tag might be: DEMOLITIONS)

2.) Crowd control, using big indiscriminating weapons to hit lots of enemies (no idea for Tags)

3.) Shooting as a diversion, for cover fire, to bring dudes out of cover, in general - not for the purpose of actually killing (no idea for Tags)

4.) Snipering, assassination, target strikes (Tag might be: TARGETED)

5.) Charging into close combat, assault (Tag might be: ASSAULT)

Thoughts?

    \nGood point, Wordman!

    The examples you give are too finnicky for my taste, and imply some stuff about gameplay that doesn't jive with me (the importance of concealability in a Necromuna-esque fight game?), but in general I'm totally on board with what you're saying, and want to make it happen.

    I've added "Stealth" as a tag, to reflect the up close and quiet kind of kill.

    In general, my lean towards type of attack (Assault, Barrage, Direct Fire, Sharpshooting) and style of attack (Dangerous, Tactical, etc) is an attempt to create just what you're talking about... tags that reflect situation and fighting style, making them matter.

    The trick is that the tags themselves carry no mechanical weight, in play, per se...
    They modify the cost of a Power or Item during chargen - if you have the tags that the Power or Item has, then it's cheaper for you.
    Also, they imply a cluster of effects. Generally, most things with the Dangerous tag will impose a Health risk for failed actions and give you a higher bonus...
    Finally, some Powers reference "the number of powers you have with the X tag" or some-such.

    \n Obligatory grumbling:
    Assault describes an attack (offensive operation,) and much of dedicated assault gear (offensive hand grenades, as one example) are built with the assumption that the user will not always have the benefit of hard cover or stable/stationary position. It is exceedingly difficult to be on the offensive while standing still. It is still possible to use offensive language while standing still, as a large number of stand-up comics will attest.

    Assault rifles, for example, can be fired from the hip, and (usually, especially around the time of their innovation) be fired full auto to suppress enemy combatants while you seek cover. Battle rifles, with larger rounds and more recoil, serve this function less well.

    Assault to describe Close Quarters Combat is a Warhammer-ism. The only place I find that conflation is in the name of the H&K CAWS (Close Assault Weapon System, an automatic shotgun.)

    "Assault" will be readily understood by your public, which is your goal. I would however chose "Close" or "Personal," but I am biased against Warhammer-isms. Current military jargon doesn't (to my knowledge) make a distinction between CQC with melee weapons (knives, etc.) and short-range firearms (shotguns, submachine guns, pistols.)
