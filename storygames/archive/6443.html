\n OK, this all started from reading Vincent Baker's big discussion about the unwelcome on his anyway blog.

Have a pitch meeting and make characters first, one person refers to themselves as 'we' and doesn't make a character, they're the audience (think theatre, not TV). Everybody writes a page with karma, issue, and strengths, it's all Color and all public. Each player reads their page out loud following the token rules below. Mention a few things you're likely to call 'Lame' on if there's anything you can think of, and what you think is Cool and ways to get you to say Damn.

First scene starts:
Audience starts the scene 'we see...' including at least one player character.

Start talking, let others talk as appropriate, jot down stuff you think is worth jotting down if you're the sort. You are narrating in first or second person but you can control the external world with this ("I see a man in black heading towards me." "We see someone sneaking up on the man in black from behind." "I'm totally oblivious as a second person sneaks up behind me.")

If someone jumps in on you and says X: Y
Risky: What you want is already clear, so someone (could be anybody) has to say something bad that could happen (like 'you could fail', 'someone could see you' or 'but they don't make out like the audience was hoping'). Normally, EDIT: you and the person who said 'Risky' each roll a die, highest roll wins, you win a tie. However, if you want, you can Back Down. This means you get a white token and you nullify what you said in the first place.

Cool: You get a cool-blue token, because you made the situation cooler.

Damn: You get a devil-red token, because you made the situation worse.

Lame: You have two options. Back Off, which means the person who called Lame gets a white Lame token, or Own It, in which case you take the white Lame token yourself.

Fucked Up*: You get a red and a blue token. (Edit: *Said in tortured admiration)

Done: Scene's over.

Scene bidding
You can't bid if you have a white token. You can bid for scenes with red or blue, hidden bid, high bid is spent, everyone else keeps theirs. If no one bids, or everybody has a white token you need to go back to the pitch meeting, write more premise, and hope someone comes up with some cool and damn so somebody can ditch their white, or someone wants to bid.

Token Economy:
If you have a white token you have to use 1 red and 1 blue token to nullify it as soon as you're able.
You can spend a red token to add 1 to your roll (before the roll, as many times as you like) when someone else says Risky on you. (Fire it up)
You can spend a blue token to add 1 to your roll (before the roll, as many times as you like) when you say Risky. (Cool it off)

So basically you never want to be lame, you want to add "Damn" so you can get what you want while avoiding risks, and being cool gives you more power to make what others don't want happen (and therefore make them likely to say Damn). You never want Lame. Blocking with Lame is itself Lame, but possibly worth it, backing off from risks is Lame.

Higher-order interactions (saying Damn while someone else says Risky)
That's what's interesting to me. I think this is a major body-language game so I don't think it would work well online.

Why I took the time to write it down
Because I'm trying to think of a way to set stakes but stay immersed, still be able to prevent something lame without being a jerk about it (if you acquiesce to my 'Lame' I take it, if you don't you're owning it).

What I want to know
Is this playable?

    \nI can't tell. I think seeing an example of play might help...for example, I have no idea what "fucked up" actually means in this context. Is it "That's fucked up" in admiration, "That's fucked up" as a complaint, or "YOU fucked up" as in you botched terribly, or "You ARE fucked up" as in major damage/injury/setback, or...?

    One thing I can put out there right now: every set of poker chips I've ever seen in my local grocery store is red, blue, and white. Replace the green chips in your suggested rules with white chips, and you've just made getting the materials to use this system much, much easier. It ain't like green = lame has some kind of appealing metaphor in the first place (which I'm guessing is the reason you're using red and blue, so as to get the cutesy "heat it up"/"cool it down" jargon), whereas with white you can maybe associate it with blandness.

So shouldn't A be spending blue tokens to subtract 1 from the roll? Or is there some opposed dice mechanic that got skipped?

    \n I see your point - but I think it Edit: could hold up: Cool, Damn, and Fucked Up are all 'fan mail' - all of those are good things. Cool and Damn cross over so that to get what you want you need to frustrate someone else (game must put players in tension).

    Lame never economically punishes the person you call it on unless they choose it - it's volunteering for economic punishment yourself to keep something out of the fiction. Like Challenges in universalis, I think it's going to be the least-used rule. But it needs to be there because there's nothing that says "take a moment and reflect to see if you have collective agreement that what was just said becomes part of the fiction"
