    \n This is probably another thread, really, but I've yet to find a virtual tabletop which I actually liked. I thing there's perhaps something very philosophically wrong about the virtual tabletop which no amount of sufficiently advanced technology will fix, short of fully-tangible-holographic-virtual-reality type stuff.

    More on topic, I'm certainly up for trying online play in various forms. I am, however, beholden somewhat to my natural sleep cycle as a UK resident. Simon, this is good news for you, but bad news for Americans.

    My go-to place for this sort of thing is #indierpgs, the IRC channel on the MagicStar network, but sadly that place is now highly depopulated. The only remedy for that, though, is to keep asking people to come along anyway and be patient. It costs nothing to be idling in that channel, and maybe one day there'll be enough people paying attention at the same time that a game could start.

    There's the Play Now! movement as well, which exists somewhere as a mailing list group or some such, but I never really got a handle on how that worked.

    In general I think the internet is just to ethereal a medium for the "regular group meeting regularly" social paradigm to be functional, to the extent that it is even functional in real life.
