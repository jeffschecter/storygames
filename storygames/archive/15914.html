\n I used to play computer games in my copy books. This was back when Commodore 64s were saturating Santa's to-buy list. Everyone wanted one. Most had one. 'Member those days?

Well, I didn't have a C64, but I had a twin brother.

I'd draw Bad Guy HQ in my homework notebook, populate it with bad guys (either monsters or Nazis, or both) and give my twin brother an ammo sheet. After that, Mum would hear "pchoo-pchoo-kaboom" noises for about an hour while Jay and I would doodle the massacre of a hundred bogeymen. There were rules and stuff, but mostly doodling.

I like doodling.

That's why I wrote Chronicles of Skin. It mixes storytelling with doodling. While it's not as doodleheavy as my childhood, it uses images in an interesting way. Anyhoo, it's on sale now from IPR. If you're a doodler, I think it would be a shame not to at least know about it. You can find it here.

    \n Hey Guillaume, thanks for your appreciation! Nathan, I'm surprised you haven't received yours yet. Let me know.

    Rules Answers

    1) If a character dies during open play (before the tragedy phase), the player in charge of that character immediately receives a token and discards the character card from play. It doesn't matter how a character dies\u2014it could be the result of an event, puppet or the player narrating for his own character.

    2) "Dead characters are not included in the tragedy," means that the player is not asked whether the character succumbs or escapes. In the tragedy phase, each player in turn is asked whether they will succumb or escape the tragedy. If the character is already dead, the character, in a sense, has already succumbed. In other words, you can't get a free token when your character dies during play and then get another free token for succumbing during the tragedy.

    3) Tragedy, very importantly, does not mean death. Only mark a tombstone if the character dies (and is removed from play).

    4) Not unless you want to. When you draw a card for a new NPC, you're doing two things: giving the character a name and allowing reincorporation in follow up scenes. I don't use the rank and culture for improvised NPCs because it slows down play. But you could if you wanted!

    5) Thanks for looking at the FAQ. The puppet and event rules that you see in the first edition print were written for the Chronicle chapter. Somehow, I never rewrote them when I pasted them into the Sketch chapter. So, in the Sketch version, spending a token is optional, but you can spend as many as you like (more than one, if you prefer).

    6) Yes, that is possible. The Scribe chooses which culture is affected by the tragedy. However, remember that the tragedy will also be aimed at the characters in the story. That is, you, as the Scribe, will be asking each player whether their soldier will succumb or escape the tragedy. When you tell a story about the characters of one culture causing the tragedy the other, the Glyph of War will take some thinking about (as this is supposed to be an impending threat for the characters). In summary, yes you can do that, but I find that it rarely comes up in play, and when it does, it feels natural. My advice, don't push for it, but if it happens, cool. When drawing tombstones that are generated by the tragedy pile, always use the tombstones for the culture that was affected by the tragedy.

    Thanks for these questions. Let me know if that's clear and then I can use these answers to populate my FAQ.
