    \n I've been privately scolded for not linking.
    so for those who haven't heard about it, I'll point you to the place where I heard about it (though there are quite a few of other reviews and sites out there talking about it as well.)
    I saw it on Play This Thing!
    http://playthisthing.com/path

    As far as what I think makes it cool and interesting, well... I'm still working that out. So far I've only played with Robin, though once I'm done with my beginning of the day web stuffs, I think I'll try Rose (planning on working my way through the girls from youngest to oldest).

    One thing that's interesting to me so far (and which possibly makes it an odd thing to discuss on Story Games) is that there's no real story. Or at least not an explicit one. Everything is so goddamn surreal and symbolic that it's almost completely up to the player to assemble any story there might be in his or her imagination. I mean, for my part, I'm fairly convinced that absolutely nothing in the game is literal or real. The whole thing seems to be a metaphor for something (some people seem to think that something is probably "Life" and I'm not sure I disagree).

    The game is Slow. This shouldn't be a surprise, given that the designers specifically call it a Slow Game (in caps like that too, though I've never heard that term applied to anything else before. maybe they coined it? i don't know.). This can be kind of frustrating sometimes (but I think that's mostly due to what I'm used to with games) but when I'm able to just chill out and get into the experience, it's also kind of cool.

    The game does an absolutely fucking fantastic job with atmosphere, in my opinion. My favorite example of this is what happens if you run while in the woods. Normally the camera sits behind your girl. You can see her whole body and straight in front of her. If you run, the camera pulls back and up, meaning that you can barely see in front of her at all, but mostly are seeing what's behind her. Plus, as you continue to run, the screen darkens with weird patterns and you start to hear the girl's heartbeat. It does a great job of capturing the feeling of running headlong from some unseen predator, heedless of what you might be running toward.

    I first played it on Thursday, I think. I did a safe run with Robin and followed The Rule. I stayed on the path, went right to Grandma's house. And it still creeped me the hell out. The next day I did a "successful" run with Robin, though it took me two tries (be warned that even if you save the game in the forest, when you start again you'll start from the red room. it'll save the items you found, but you can't pick up exactly where you left off). I've found that, unlike many more traditional video games that I like, I'm not feeling compelled to keeping playing this until I'm done with it. So I've been liking to fire it up, play it for like an hour, then leave it and come back the next day or later on. That might just be me, though.

    So, that's what I think so far.
    I'd like to hear your thoughts, Andy, once you've played it. I may have more to say once I've been through it with another girl or two.

    \n Another thing...
    it's weird to observe my ingrained video game expectations and tendencies, and it's also weird to see them in The Path. in particular, it's really doesn't seem to be a game about things like "scores" and yet, after completing each run the game will tell you how run down how many objects you found, how many rooms you unlocked, etc. and give you a "grade." this is odd enough as it is, but i find it even more odd to observe my own annoyance at the fact that (since if i save and quit before finishing a run it will remember the objects i found but won't list them in the "score" for the next run) i'm not getting the score i think i should if it takes me more than one session to finish with a girl.

    \n I haven't played much more since the last time I posted, but I read a few reviews today, and some of the comments with those reviews. I saw a lot of complaints about the graphics and people generally saying that it looked "unpolished" and I found myself flabbergasted. Do that many people ONLY play major commercial releases from huge publishers? Every time I saw a comment like that I wanted to scream, "It's a $10 game basically made by TWO people!"
    Cuz, for my part, I love the feel of it. I mean, there are a few graphics issues that annoy me (first and foremost of those being the large number of things that your avatar can just walk right through - for me, major clipping is the easiest way, graphically, to break my immersion in a video game), but by and large, I think the overall art and style of the game is fantastic for what it's trying to do.\n
