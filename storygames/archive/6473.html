    \n Hey there Matthijs:

    Sorry i didn't get to chat you up sooner, i was at nerdly \u2013deep in the trees\u2013 away from the internet. Here's the story on Agatha:

    \u2022 everything paul says is totally true, ashcan last year, nicely received, although i didn't hear a lot from people who picked it up so i can't really speak to their play experience. I can say that i've played the hell out of the thing and it sings like a bird in the spring. I'm super please with how it's turned out. It's very easy to use and gets interesting play. It's a total gateway device to other storytelling games.
    \u2022 The document is totally done at this time, i'm just fund raising to get the thing to the printer. Money has been tight.

    I'll have copies in my hand in the early summer but for safety's sake i'm going to only promise i'll have it at gencon (and IPR, at the same time). I'll be pimping it pretty hard out at indy.

    If you (or anyone else for that matter) have any other questions feel free to ask here, or to email me at kevin dot allen dot jr@gmail dot com

    You can also check out www.sweetagatha.com for updates, extras, and developments. As it gets closer to August that site will be coming alive with a lot more info and fun mysterious stuff.

    hope this helps clear up any confusion

    EDIT- I should also say that that blog post is from December 06, and a lot has changed since then. The spirit of the game is the same but the play, contents and usage of Sweet Agatha is vastly different now. Go to the above website for a more current description.
