Hi Again,

\n

As a follow-up to the train thread, I need some more info. I'm a first-time GenCon attendee, and I'm looking for a hotel that's about a 10-minute walk to the con. The roomies and I agree that some actual decompression time between the overpacked (sleeping 4!) room and the overpacked con would be a good thing.

\n

The only one I've found that appears (at first blush) to be in that geographical window is the University Suites and Conference Centre. Any previous attendess stay here? Was it nice? Can we do better for the price? Is there something else that's maybe less luxurious, just as close, but cheaper?

\n

Thanks,

    \n Sorry to tell you the University Suites are much more than a ten minute walk. Double that and you are closer to the actual transit time. There is a bus route, however, that can get you close, but I do not believe it runs late and its times are not necessarily the most convenient.
    \n
    \nAll the other close in hotels are booked solid, except those right next to the con. I stayed at the Embassy Suites last year and it is just fine. Quirky as it used to be a rail station and still has train cars inside. It is also the LARP headquarters so if you are big into LARPs you should consider staying there so you can have a quick costume change upstairs. The Embassy Suites, through the GenCon room block, ran $150/night for their standard 2 bed room.
    \n
    \nHope it helps.

    \n We've got a double-double at the Sheraton Indianapolis City Centre Hotel, which looks to be four or five blocks from the convention center. I haven't been there before, but the room seems to be of the large suite size variety, and should hold four people comfortably at around $190 a night. I got my reservations in the first day, but I wouldn't be surprised if there weren't still some rooms available.

    Last year, we were all the way the hell on the other side of town, across the river, which seriously impinged upon my late-night enjoyment. This year, the only thing that will keep me from wringing every drop of gaming goodness out of my time is my capacity for lack of sleep. Or whatever that last sentence means, in English.

    \n The Embassy Suites have very nice rooms, an ideal location, and a free breakfast buffet.

    However, the rooms are all (mostly?) arranged around a massive central atrium, and during the con people like to play games and socialize in the lobby and cafeteria type area. The upshot of this is that there'll be neat stuff going on downstairs from your room, but you'll get to listen to that neat stuff until about 3 a.m. even if you'd rather sleep.
