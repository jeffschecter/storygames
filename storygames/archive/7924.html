\nEmerald City Gamefest is having its third annual free game day on Saturday November 15 at the Northgate Community Center, 10510 5th Ave. NE, Seattle, between 11AM and 7PM. As usual, we are expecting a mix of trad and indie, role-playing, board games and miniatures, hard-core fans and new gamers.

GeekGirlsRule and Ogremarco have agreed to host another Story Game Lounge for people who prefer to drop in and run or play a story game without scheduling first. But we already know we'll also have scheduled games of The Bridge System (formerly Two-Die), Universalis, InSpectres, and Knights of the Road, Knights of the Rail. Ogre and GeekGirl are bringing tons of other indie games, and may even record podcast segments.

There are also prize draws, guest artists and authors, and more. It costs absolutely nothing, so please drop by and try a new game or a trusted classic. Better yet, register a game too, and run something for a few hours!

    \n Hi, Jackson!

    If you just want to play, then you don't need to register at all: just show up on November 15! Andif you want to sign up to run a game, just send an e-mail to registration@emeraldcitygamefest.org with the following information:

    1. Type of event (board game, card game, miniatures game, roleplaying game, other game).
    2. The title of your event or name of the game you will be running.
    3. The game system being used in your event (if different from above).
    4. Your name.
    5. An e-mail address where we can reach you (this information will not be made public).
    6. The maximum number of players you can accommodate.
    7. How long your event will run (don't forget to count set-up time).
    8. A brief description of your event (we reserve the right to edit descriptions if they are more than a paragraph or two).

    You are welcome to submit a link to a site giving more information about your game if you wish.

    @kev: Hi! Yes, Edmund and I are now in Eureka, California but we promised to honour all previous commitments, including Emerald City Gamefest. I'm keeping a couple of vacation days so I can be there for the event, and I really look forward to seeing everybody. :-)
