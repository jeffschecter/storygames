\n Long. Bear with me.

Crossposted from the Children of Children recruitment and planning thread at RPG.net, concerning a game starring the kids of the Final Act crew. Added context in italics.

"I have drawings of the Pilots, which are awesome, and I will post them as soon as I get a scanner. I'm also doing pics of each pilot with their Anchor - so far I have Ariana (Smith, by Josh) and Casey (Lin, by Keenan), and Archer's next (Archer Lin from the one-shot, all grown up).

However, in these drawings, Jon Smith (by Anchor #2, "Nathan Sinclair") is black (well, half black - looks like a young Obama with an "Oh shit I think I am in love with Casey Lin but do not know how to tell her" expression). So is Ariana's Anchor Jessica, for that matter. (Jon' player's response: "Unacceptable! ...I demand dreadlocks and shades.")

I have 3 questions for you regarding that:

1 - Does this overly boggle your perception of all of them?
2 - At what point did I say that Sara's Anchor was white (or Asian, or Semetic, or Latino?)
3 - You did realize that by nature of the low income prereq, Treasure Island Job Corps (where the game is set) students are mostly black (followed by white and Latino students with a substantial minority of Chinese exchange students)?

For extra credit, state in your own words why absolutely everyone thought that the Innocent Sweetheart template illo represented Sara Smith in particular, or indeed, that Sara was white (I usually have her and Derek explicitly black in the one-shot). (On the other hand, she's explicitly an older version of that girl in this game.) Use examples."

Looking back, I realized that four out of six of Shiro Nezze's illos are of white guys (and that's assuming that the Seasoned Vet is Anna - that illo could either be white or Asian, but because of surname Lin...).

This bothers me a great deal - I mean, statistically, one in six being black is about right, but no Latinos strikes a dissonant chord with me, not to mention that AFAICT the first playtests took place in Oakland, for crissake.

Part of this is a desire to work racial tension into a heavily I Will Not Abandon You game - given that we quickly decided on "Can we build a better civilization?" as a Hope, this felt appropriate.

I've requested, 'frex, that Anna and a Chinese Laura's gang was an ethnic Chinese one for this game's purposes, and that an Eager Young Soldier pilot descended from Laura and Keenan be half-chinese as well so I could play on that.

Realizing that I had named Keenan's anchor after Yukiko, a crush of mine from elementary school (last name changed to Tendo to protect the innocent), also suggested to me that we could play on Keenan's alleged fixation for Asian women.

Jon Derek Smith is black, her sister is white, and you can beleive that Josh and anchor Nathan's opinions on the matter will come up.

What are your thoughts on this?

The Anchors are, of course, are shuffled according to the childhoods of the players; if I need to define them for some reason ("...I didn't start thinking about anyone that way until I was 20, and we still know each other!"), Anchor # 1 is Yukiko Tsudonimu (Nisei, Kind) ~_^ Anchor 2# is Nathan Totalfabrication (Black or third-gen Russian Jewish as needed, Driven), and Meredith Makinthisup (Originally a white redhead, but occasionally black: Kind).
