I just finished my first draft of my hack for World of Dungeons called World of Fallout and would love to hear what you clever people think of it.
Having read most of the other World of Dungeons-thread got me really inspired so I decided to try it out myself.
I have yet to playtest it (which I guess is a pretty big no-no) but I intend to try and GM it as soon as I possibly can.
(The idea here is to make it into one of those tidy little pdf's that are super easy to share, but until this has been properly balanced and looked through, i'll wait with that)

Check it out here!

Cheers!

    \n I've often thought about how it would be neat to use the Apocalypse World engine (or even just Apocalypse World straight up) to run Fallout, so I'm really curious to hear about how playtesting goes. It's got enough additional moves and gear and stuff that it actually feels more like a light first draft of "Fallout World" than "World of Dungeons - Fallout" to my mind. You may end up wanting to cut some stuff and leave more open to interpretation if you want to go with the "rules fit on a page and a half" approach.

    (Just for example: As a Fallout fan, I appreciate addiction and radiation being specifically called out as particular risks in the setting, and I'd find it cool to see basic moves for them in a full-on AW hack. In something really short, though, you can totally get away with the short description you have for each special stat, where it briefly mentions using endurance to resist drugs/radiation poisoning.)
