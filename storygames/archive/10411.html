    \n Guys, this is Story Games. So consider the important part of the stat blocks...\n
    Bear: These massive blah blah weigh boring etc and stand whatever tall. They are fighty blah blah. The brown bear\u2019s statistics are totally generic, we admit.
    \nvs
    Owlbear: An owlbear\u2019s coat ranges in color from brown-black to yellowish brown; its beak is a dull ivory color. A full-grown male can stand as tall as 8 feet and weigh up to 1,500 pounds. Adventurers who have survived encounters with the creature often speak of the bestial madness they glimpsed in its red-rimmed eyes.
    \nOK, so the bear is color. It has a height and a weight and some tactics. The owlbear is guys. It has (sing along if you know the words) bestial madness in its red-rimmed eyes. Adventurers have survived encounters with it, so it has a story. We need to have time to explore the chickub-hood traumas that put the BM in its RRE, and address the attendant premise (maybe "How does the chickub's story shape or limit the growth of the owlbear he/she becomes?"). Once we ascribe a rich inner life to the owlbear, and Myth grows up around it, we'll be looking for a significant arc to its biography. It can't just randomly get eaten by a bear, unless the bear is symbolic of its heroic flaw. (Anyone have the 3e MM2 with the "Symbolic" template handy?) In short, the owlbear needs to live through the fight so it can be reincorporated.

    That doesn't mean that the owlbear is the sole victor, though. The bear can actually win too, because the adventurers will find a pretty intimidating owlbear pellet if the owlbear wins its stakes of eating the bear \u2014 since the bear's goal was to keep the adventurers out of its territory, it gets its intent but not its preferred means.

    \n Anyone who decides this on the basis of information published after 1975 is doing it wrong. Supplement I: Greyhawk contains the first mention of the Owlbear:

    OWLBEAR
    2 claws, 1 bite
    1-6 claw****, 1-12 bite
    **** = hug on score of 18 or better causes 2-16 points of additional damage
    No. Appearing 2-5
    Armor Class 5
    Move in Inches 12
    Hit Dice 5
    % in Lair 40%
    Type or Amount of Treasure C

    Creatures of horrid visage and disposition. Owl Bears will attack
    whatever they see and fight to the death. They deliver a "hug" just as a Werebear, for
    example, as well as great damage from beak, tooth, and claw. A large male will stand 8'
    tall, weigh 1,500 pounds, and have claws over 2" long. Bodies are furry, tending towards
    feathers over the cranial region, and the skin is very thick.

    ===========
    Now, in comparison . . . there are no stats for Bears (grizzly or otherwise) in Dungeons & Dragons. The closest you'll find is "Large Insects or Animals" in Monsters & Treasure of the Little Brown Books.

    LARGE INSECTS OR ANIMALS
    (attack types and specific damage not listed)
    No. Appearing 2-16
    Armor Class Variable
    Move in Inches Variable
    Hit Dice Variable
    % in Lair Variable
    Type or Amount of Treasure Variable

    This category includes giant ants and prehistoric
    monsters. Armor Class can be anything from 8 to 2. Hit Dice should range
    from 2 to anywhere near 20, let us say, for a Tyrannasaurus Rex. Also included
    in this group are the optionally usable "Martian" animals such as Apts, Banths,
    Thoats, etc. If the referee is not personally familiar with the various monsters included
    in this category the participants of the campaign can be polled to decide
    all characteristics. Damage caused by hits should range between 2-4 dice (2-24
    points).

    =======
    From what I can tell, the first time a Bear gets any kind of stats is in Tom Moldvay's Basic Rules, published in 1981. So for the first seven years, if you wanted stats for Grizzly Bears you had to make them up - presumably by using the Owl Bear as a starting point and then going through a process very much like this.

    In other words, carry on . . .
