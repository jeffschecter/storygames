    \n for Perfect (my game about Victorian-Dystopic criminals)
    ----------------
    1 How To Play book (~50 pages)
    3-4 Character Creation books (~15 pages)
    1 Alternate Settings book, detailing two alternate settings for the game (~15 pages)
    1 fold-out 22x17 poster/play-sheet that listed pertinent game information (mechanical options, lists of names, etc) + killer artwork
    16 character sheets
    4 pre-made characters
    2 black d6s, 3 white d6s


    for Heart of Ashes (my dark fantasy game about children)
    ----------------
    1 How To Play book (~30 pages)
    15-20 character sheets
    a deck of Powers and Keys (roughly 50 cards)
    a deck of Lures (roughly 40 cards)
    a deck of Monsters/Factions/Situations (roughly 40 cards, corresponding to the Lures)
    2 full-colour maps of the game world
    12 white d6, 24 red d6

    \n I'm very fond of packaging. How to Host a Dungeon comes in a semi-transparent folder:


    Once I realized that the game really needed to be loose pages, packaging became a real problem. I hunted around stationary stores until I found the folders nice and cheap, then ordered 100 of them. I had the printer make stickers to put on the front. Total cost is just about exactly $1 per package. This packaging also lets me include sheets of tracking paper in the game package.

    The same company that makes these folders make a bigger, sturdier 2" thick folder. I've got it in the back of my mind for a future project with multiple boards, cards, and the like.

    Large stationary stores and party supply stores are great sources for packaging ideas. An hour at our local party supply store pretty much blew my mind for the variety of papers, containers, and crazy papercraft items they had available for cheap.

    \nFor Sufficiently Advanced:
    One book for setting, full-color with illustrations.
    One book for system, don't really need full color.
    A pamphlet full of useful things: pregen PCs and NPCs, the briefing and mission generators (with examples), interesting cities and stations, and so forth.
    Blank character sheets.
    Several laminated copies of the cheat sheet.
    Some sort of marker to act as a physical representation of Twists.
    Five pairs of ten-sided dice.

    For Console:
    Several "short version" copies of the game engine to hand out to players. Reference stuff on the back.
    Cardstock stand-up copies of the various magic types. One for the Black Mage, one for the White Mage...
    One long version of the game engine with examples, details, and options.
    The CPU's reference book for play, with monsters, equipment, and statuses.
    A booklet for use in planning, with the standard conventions of the genre, era and setting suggestions, etc.
    Five pairs of percentile dice.
    Initiative whiteboard with marker.
    Combat whiteboard with markers, divided into two sides and front/back rows as tradition demands. Ooh ooh - we could make it magnetic and include a bunch of character and monster magnets!
    A soundtrack straight off OCRemix.

    \n Funnily enough, I am starting to put together the stuff for an Uml\xe4ut: Game of Metal boxed set.

    My main reservation is confusing people by having two products: One of which is "just" a book, and the other of which is has bunch of stuff with it. But really, it's all about collating the set. Current plan is:

    A box, big enough to hold it all.
    A copy of the rulebook.
    Some plastic counters shaped like guitars to represent Cash (in gold and silver)
    Four sets of Style cards.
    A CD of AWESOME METAL to listen to while you play the game (which also has the PDF and a the Band sheets and stuff on).
    4 rules reference sheets.
    Some air.

    \n A lot of the design of Dungeon Construction Kit has been driven by how to make the game in an affordable package. Right now, it sits at:

    Rules booklet (16 pages)
    One encounter book (42 pages)

    Event card deck (app 18 cards)
    Treasure cards (app 24)
    Final Encounter cards (12)
    Item cards (18)

    Dungeon map tiles (app 22, 6" x 6")
    Hallway/Door/Spacer tiles (varying sizes)

    I'd love to get the cards in a 2.75" by 4.2" package; that will give me a larger font and more room for flavor text. But that all depends on the bottom line, and I may end up going with the standard 2.5" by 3.5".

    I'm just starting to really zero in on the package for the tentatively named Wilderness Construction Kit, and it looks fairly different.
