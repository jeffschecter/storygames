    \n This thread and that one about the Forge closing reminded me of something I posted there a long time ago, inspired by an idea posted by sombody with the nick Aapov. I tried it succesfully on a few 3:16 sessions we played with my pals here. It's a few tables the GM can use to prepare for the game, along with those provided by the book. These tables help the GM state what the troopers will be told in debriefing and their orders... and what of those things go wrong in the mission. Instant tons of fun, just add players and mix under enemy fire:

    Logistics

    Insertion
    1) Far from enemy, enemy unaware
    2) Close to enemy, enemy unaware
    3) Far from enemy, enemy aware
    4) Close to enemy, enemy aware
    5) Enemy in movement to your position
    6) Enemy in movement away from your position

    Equipment
    1) Insufficient equipment and ammo, get your own on the road
    2) Minimal standard equipment, count your rounds and make them count
    3) Standard full equipment
    4) Specific additional equipment relevant for the mission
    5) Heavy Equipment and additional gadgets
    6) Last generation equipment and reinforcements available

    Extraction
    1) Team will have to get out of the zone by their own
    2) Extraction in safe zone far from target in 1d6 hours
    3) Extraction in unsafe zone far from target in 1d6 hours
    4) Extraction in safe zone close to target as soon as is called in
    5) Extraction on the target as soon as is called in
    6) Reinforcements will arrive in 1d6 x 10 min. Hold the perimeter.

    Intelligence

    Objective
    1) Sabotage to instalation or enemy vehicle
    2) Assassination of an specific individual
    3) Hit and Run
    4) Recon
    5) Ambush
    6) Infiltration to obtain specific information.


    Opposition numbers
    1) Isolated unit
    2) One squad
    3) One platoon
    4) One company
    5) One Brigade
    6) One division


    Opposition strength
    1) Insufficient equipment and ammo
    2) Light opposition, maybe grenades and a machine-gun nest
    3) anti-tank equipment and support vehicles
    4) tanks and artillery support
    5) aereal support, tanks, artillery and reinforcements coming
    6) all of the above and the last advances in warfare


    Ok, it works like this: Roll 1d6 for each aspect of the mission, some stuff you tell the players, some not. You can even tell them everything and then roll another d6 in secret to see what goes wrong. Here's an example: *rolls some dice and interpretates the result* "Your team will be dropped in regular pods, three clicks from the target, you will have the surprise element so keep it stealth until you get a clear visual on them. You will have with full equipment and optic camo for this. The objective is an enemy factory. Hit and run, no heroics today. Intel says opposition is gonna be light from 3:00 to 6:00 for some blah blah reason. The team has to get out on their own, traveling 7 hours by night to the northwest and then following a river to the nearest campbase."

    Now I'll roll to se what goes wrong (bwahahahahaaaa!)... I rolled a one, that's insertion; so now I roll again to see how bad is it. A 5 may mean the transport is shot down on route, so they have to get to the target on their own and beg they make it before 5:00. I could also re-roll in the same table, so a 5 would mean that yes, your unit is deployed far from the enemy, but unbeknownst to you there's an enemy patrol on their way to your landing zone.

    You can roll the 'what goes wrong?' dice more than once and even do it along the session just cuz, you know, everything can go wrong
    I didn't specify much on the tables on purpose, since I think this can also work for that WWII setting, modern combat, etc.Hope this helps, have fun.

    \n OK here is a link to my 3:16 Deathwatch draft. This is very deliberately an attempt to write something that uses 3:16 rules and Deathwatch fiction, so you will probably need both of these books to run it. We've played half a campaign of this so far, and I expect to play a lot more. Some corrections I have on paper not in this yet, but I will update at some point.

    And this is a draft version of the Butchers & Thieves document we use to play 3:16 D&D. Played 5 playtests of this and I'm happy, but just worked out how I want the rolls to work as a single roll for attack and damage and how I need to re-write experience in the last few days so I'm planning to re-write it.

    We started playing Butchers & Thieves for three reasons. Firstly, we played Dungeon World wrong and our Halfling Fighter killed 10 goblins in a single move and oh boy did we ever want more of that. Secondly, I wanted a way to work up move ideas for horrifying adventurers for my my humanoid hack of Apocalypse World, which is why the races are called Sunlovers (humans) and the classes are called Butchers (fighters) (but you could easily change them back). And thirdly, someone on a thread wanted ultra-fast fantasy combats and I told them hacked 3:16 could deliver so I wanted to prove it.
