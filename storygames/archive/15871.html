What is it?

The contest started here based on this.\n
The idea of the contest is to ask readers to create one dungeon level in an edition-less format (ex: you can name monsters but you don\u2019t provide stats for them) using the template and submit it for the contest. We\u2019d judge the entries based on criteria such as \u2018most evocative setting\u2019, \u2018Funniest entry\u2019, \u2018Most creative use of a Trap\u201d and so on. Once we\u2019ve named winners, we\u2019ll be compiling a FREE PDF of the winners/runner-ups and releasing it to the community at large.

    \n There are at least three really great things about the One Page dungeons:
    \n
    \n1. Easy as hell to prep. Read one page, digest for maybe 5 minutes, and boom.
    \n
    \n2. Super low social footprint. One of the things I hate about Dungeons & Dragons is that it never fucking ends: published modules have like 60-80+ rooms, successful campaigns have zillions of NPC's, the whole thing is a multi-year investment that sometimes runs into serious scheduling problems and accrues huge opportunity costs. But a one-page dungeon is a single night's entertainment.
    \n
    \n3. Brevity is the soul of interpretation. Um, in this room are 8 skeletons playing in a band. What? What's that all about? There might be some notes, but in the end I'm making something up as a sort of "seeded impro."
    \n
    \nYou lose out on things with One Pagers, of course. Most of them aren't very "deep" in terms of complexity of interaction or long-range evolution, like the Caverns of Thracia where players are pitted against an extremely "sensitive" setting. But hey, that's the nature of the beast.
