About the game
Pickets & Blinds is a competitive storytelling game where each player tells their own story about regular suburban American citizens who is driven to commit murder. It's a dark, sometimes funny, often poignant look at average folks who get involved with extreme circumstances. Everyone starts out with their stories existing separately but by the end of the game peoples actions have influenced each other and the stories find ways to knit together. This all happens completely organically, and behind the curtain (it's like pulp fiction or independence day, lots of unrelated characters affecting each other). FACTS:

\u2022 Games take about 2-3 hours
\u2022 No GM
\u2022 3-6 players (works with 7, but everyone gets less spotlight time, and your stories are more hurried)
\u2022 Simple, playing card based mechanic (it's pretty much "memory")
\u2022 Over 500 unique and flavorful story "leads" (nuggets of color that you drop into your scenes)
\u2022 50 page, perfect bound, full color glossy magazine (print version)
\u2022 26 page, bookmarked and linked, screen ready PDF

    On Magcloud-
    I was enamored with magcloud when I first read about it. I haven't ever been very satisfied with print on demand services, I tend to make smaller games, and I feel like physically very few indie games stand out from each other (in that all the books are sort of the same size/format). Magcloud was an interesting answer to this (the recent addition of perfect binding sealed the deal). The issue is that as your game gets longer the less reasonable magcloud's costs become. At 20\xa2 a page you can approach the unprofitable sales point pretty quickly. On the other hand the printing is superb, the magazines are solid, nicely glossy, the color is spot on what you send them, issues are printed and sent to you very fast, free proof, and the user interface is super easy. It's not for everybody or every product, but if it works for you it's really tight.

    \n I ordered a copy of this from MagCloud five days ago when I read the announcement. It arrived in my mailbox in Minnesota yesterday. Fast!

    I haven't started reading yet, but I can report on the book. It's very nice quality.

    In transit, the top of the spine got banged up just a little, but I've honestly bought books from retail that were worse. There's also a dent in the cover that I assume came in the post. It's nit-picky of me, but I'm trying for completeness.

    When the book is laying face-up, the cover curls open a bit but it's just about as hot and humid as it ever gets in Minnesota, so that might be the culprit even though it's been in the AC for fifteen hours now. (It doesn't curl open when placed face-down, so it might not be humidity.)

    The grey page-background color prints notably different on the cover and interior stocks. This is barely perceptible at the front cover and quite obvious at the back. Kevin, is it like that in the copies you saw? If you cared to do something about it, how hard would it be to compensate for?

    Overall, I'm excited about MagCloud and hope to see more stuff coming from them.
