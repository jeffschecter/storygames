    I read it last year and enjoyed it quite a bit. My take-away wasn't so much the theory itself, but Koster's process of carefully defining "fun" for himself. Seeing the work he does of splitting various enjoyments (like schadenfreude) off from "fun" as tangential to his own focus is what I think is most worthwhile about the book. What he does gives focus to his sense of purpose as a game designer.
