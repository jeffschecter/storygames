    \n Yeah... I mean, you gotta do what you think is prudent at this point, but... (and take this with a good dose of IANAL)

    - the name "Stranglehold" itself shouldn't be trademarkable because it's a common word
    - that's why their trademark is actually "John Woo's Stranglehold"
    - nothing else about the product markets anywhere similarly like that video game; no shared genre, references, etc.
    - as xternal says, there is therefore no purposeful nor accidental harm to them or benefit to you

    Now, you might have to go to court to defend yourself, and that can be costly, so I can see why you'd rather not do that. But their claim is very thin and I can't see it holding up to even a judge's purview (it'd most likely get dismissed/summary judged).

    Posted By: joepubAre you saying this is a part of Indie Game publishing? I mean, as opposed to other game publishing or other publishing in general? If so, how?
    \nI am not Jim, but my take would be that it's part of indie game publishing because of the relative size of the company filing for C&D and the company publishing the game. A larger company like Midway know it will strike terror into the hearts of men when they send a C&D, because no matter how frivolous the lawsuit would be the recipient will know that it's less costly to just change the name than even the plane ticket to the court where the suit was being filed, much less legal costs. And if the case wasn't immediately thrown out, the indie publisher may as well fold since they couldn't possibly fight a company with that sized coffers available for legal actions.

    It makes me really freaking mad. Jim, my condolences. Hopefully it doesn't put too much of a dent in your plans or profits.
