\n What's out there that needs playtesting, that would actually be of use to someone? Something that someone's actively working on and needs feedback?

Firstly, it'd be a one-shot session, after dinner. So I'd like something that would suit a single evening and wouldn't feel like the start to a campaign.

Secondly, I don't want to plough through lots of rules. Something with less than 20 pages (ish) to read.

Thirdly, no issue games (so not "Silence Makes Me A Victim") and nothing grim (not "Grey Ranks"). Nothing wrong with those games, but I don't want to play them after dinner.

Fourthly, some sort of grabby setting. My two initial ideas are Meg Baker's game "Miss Schiffer's Young Ladies Of Quality" (posh girls going on adventures) or Seth Ben-Ezra's game "Dirty Secrets" (noir), so that gives you some idea. Maybe Remi Treuer's "Young Adult RPG", which is grabby in a non-setting way.

Fifthly, something that's not just a first draft (because I'd like it to largely functional) but not a finished, unalterable product (because I'd like the playtest to be of some use).

Sixthly, quirky dark humour is always good. I think of My Life With Master as having quirky dark humour. Or Shab Al-Hiri Roach. That sort of thing.

Seventhly, don't suggest your own game, because then I'll get suggestions of games that at least one other person likes. If you do suggest your own game, in this thread, you implicitly give me permission to mock it mercilessly.

Any ideas? I'd really appreciate some.

Graham

    \n Allow me to pimp 1st Quest a little more. My favorite part of it is the world-creation stuff (no surprise to those who know me, I'm a setting-creation whore). It asks you very cool questions like "what is the danger facing the world" and "what will happen if you don't stop it" that are a hell of a lot of fun. We wound up playing a game where we were being oppressed by the secret geometry behind the world at a boarding school for truants. It was a huge inspiration for my game.
