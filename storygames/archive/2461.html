  • Colin_Fredericks

    The brilliant (and sadly unpublished) 6ix-se7en, by Tim Sweeney, went this way for its entire run. It was a media-based game, with each campaign based around a particular album. This was true both in-game and out-of-game. Those who figured out the album, looked for anagrams, and exploited it, had a serious advantage when it came to manipulating events, though not necessarily at succeeding at their original goals. Given that the special powers in that game revolved entirely around invoking various songs, movies, games, and other mass-media threads, it made perfect sense.
