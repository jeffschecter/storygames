\n
\n
\n
\n\nGraham \n\n\n
\n
\n\nNovember 2009 \n edited November 2009 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n So, I'm writing another book about play, like Play Unsafe. It's going well. This time, it draws on my acting training, plus some interesting story structure stuff.

Originally, I'd planned to cover everything in one book. However, my little Trail of Cthulhu scenario sold well as a 5 dollar PDF. So I'm wondering whether to release the whole thing as two inexpensive PDFs: one for the acting stuff, one for the story structure.

What do you think? It would probably be two PDFs, both costing 4 or 5 dollars. Eventually, I might combine the two, and release them as a book, selling the combined PDF for the total price of the little PDFs. Have I missed any pitfalls? Are bitesize PDFs a cool thing or an irritation? Is there a better way of doing it?

Any advice or comments appreciated.

Graham

    \nPretending myself completely ignorant of the existence of capitalism or monetary interest... what are your publishing goals?

    A single, unified book will be a more prestigious product, something you can more easily point to and say "I made that."
    A set of smaller PDFs allow readers to pick-and-choose what they want to learn about, giving them more choice and power.
    And so on.

    I wonder how many readers will be interested in buying both, over simply choosing the one they are most interested in, though I'd wager that people will likely be interested in both if they are interested in one.

    \n I would have thought that with the availability of PDF creation software and sites such as Lulu that any print/PDF product would allow a range of possibilities?

    How about...
    1: Printed book with both parts but without PDF (say, \xa310+p&p), as well as...
    2: Full print + PDF (say, \xa315+p&p), as well as...
    3: Full PDF without print (say, \xa39), and lastly...
    4: Two separate PDFs of each section (for \xa35) that can be bought separately or together.

    I don't imagine it being that difficult to create these four methods of selling your product and it gives a great level of choice (and price points) for the consumer. The prices are simply an example.
