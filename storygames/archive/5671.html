Give me a title for my murder mystery game

\n
\n
\n
\n\nGraham \n\n\n
\n
\n\nFebruary 2008 \n edited February 2008 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n The title of this game is driving me mad.

See, here's how it works. In the game, there's a big relationship map. And, in the game, you make each relationship worse and worse, until it provides a reason to murder.

So, my first title was A Reason To Murder. Not bad, but too low key. Then it became A Need To Kill. Better, not perfect.

But that's the sort of title I want. I want to say: "This relationship is so bad, that it's [Insert Title Of Game]".

Here are some ideas. Which strike you as good?\n
  • A Need To Kill
    • \n
  • No Choice But Murder
    • \n
  • It Drove Them To Kill
\nAlternatively, any other suggestions?

One quick point: I don't want it to sound like a police procedural. So "A Motive For Murder" is obvious, but not right.

I'm tearing my hair out, here, so I'd really appreciate a deluge of ideas.

Graham

    \nA Time to Kill is a John Grisham novel. It's a very American style legal defense thriller that I think of whenever I see A Need to Kill. And that's the only reason I have hesitation about A Need to Kill as the title. It makes me think the game is intended to be like John Grisham novels, or if not, then at least in the legal defense thriller genre. It's prominent enough an association for me that I don't think of A View to a Kill. (Which would be an issue too, but I don't think of it.) I do think naming a book/movie/game in this murder/mystery genre is particularly difficult because of the sheer amount of writers and series that have plowed the field the past 80 years or whatever. And I'm not surprised to see that writers these past thirty years or so tend to stake a claim to a titling scheme that they then leverage for many many books.

    Graham,

    Back when In a Wicked Age... was Art, Grace & Guts I sent Vincent an unsolicited email arguing for a title change to Anthology, with the possible subtitle of "the game of short fantasy." My rationale being that it was clear the creative product of the game is the roleplaying equivalent of a thematically powerful shared world anthology of short stories. The title suggestion didn't stick (though I believe he's calling the game's guts "the Anthology Engine"). And maybe a similar perspective is in order for this game. Is playing the game like creating an anthology of similarly authored murder mysteries? Perhaps then, a title like:

    The Raven Press Anthology of Murder

    And write it in the voice of the anthology's very English editor.

    Paul

No Way Out doesn't strike me as great but Dead End has some more obligeing connotations. What things other than Murder can you think of which inspire the same sort of feelings?

      Are you looking to sell this game, or just title this game? I think it's fine as a title, but as a brand identity the first thing it makes me think is, "Hm, I didn't read those books, this must be a licensed property or something ... that I'd be unfamiliar with."

      \nThat would point towards things like "A Reason For Murder" or "A Need To Kill", then, I think. And, ironically, towards "The Inspector Chapel Mysteries", which really gives a very good sense of what the game's about (although, as you point out, there's another problem with that).

      Thanks.
