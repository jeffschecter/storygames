    \n What other people have said! Reading a person isn't mind reading, but if you actually ask, IC, the question you want answered, "Are they telling the truth?" will usually suffice.

    One bit of advice I remember Vincent giving in the development blog ran along the lines of, "They can ask any question, but you only have to answer one of the ones on the list" which can be a helpful way of framing it. So if Shadow asked "Why is he so eager to have me stay?" you might interpret that as "what does he intend to do?" and say "Yeah, he's licking his lips when he thinks you aren't watching, and his stomach growls a bit. You're pretty sure he's gonna chop you up as soon as you're under and eat your young, supple flesh." Stuff like that. (Don't forget, disturbing revelations at every opportunity.)

    And also as others have said... if somebody's looking for information, there's plenty of your moves that you can use to just hand it to them. Announce future badness, Tell them the possible consequences and ask, etc. Giving the PCs the right knowledge is a great way to keep their lives not boring.

    \n One of the things about AW that takes a while to get use to is that, when someone asks you a question, it's your job to really answer it as best as you can, in a pretty thorough and deep fashion. You're not supposed to stick strickly to the wording of the question or try to hold secrets back from them. Your really just lay it all out there.

    Like, if she read the doc and asked "Is he telling the truth?" As MC I would have said, "No, actually, he really wants to eat you," but described the scene more, like Ben did above, and make it clear how she gets that sense by looking at him. You really can't do the 'mysterious GM' thing and have secrets gradually emerge through play. They made the roll, so you have to fucking tell them what's going on. This doesn't mean they can ask any question -- that's an expanded move -- but the standard questions are still really, really good in most cases.

    Posted By: DionysusIs he telling the truth - yes, the injuries were bad, she needs rest
    \nIt may just be a language/phrasing issue but you almost make it sound like you were giving an objective evaluation of the truth of the statement -- instead of giving an answer about whether the person themselves was being honest. To me this question is about honesty -- are they being deceptive, or are they being honest -- not about whether what they're saying is actually true or not.

    And of course 'partially' is a different answer than 'yes' -- so in this case, if the Doctor is both honestly concerned about the injuries AND also scheming agains the character you can just say 'he's being honest about that, but there's more going on.' At which point the player has something to follow up on, if they are really serious about getting to the bottom of things.

    But I don't feel that AW is, for example, like Dogs in the Vineyard, where the GM has to go out of their way to provide additional information or answers to questions the players didn't or were not able to ask. While you're not supposed to hold back secrets, you aren't supposed to volunteer them either. You can certainly volunteer that they exist, in general, but if the players want to know they have to go after it, and that means making rolls, and that means more opportunities for things to go horribly wrong.

    \n As to how much or how little you're supposed to reveal, I have to disagree with Daniel (shocking!). As MC, you're looking for opportunities to reveal the world. You want to paint the picture of your fucked up little apocalypse every chance you get. If the guy wants to eat them, and they read him and ask questions, I am definitely going to say that he wants to eat them even if they don't think to ask about that specifically. That's part of "putting your bloody fingerprints" all over the game.

    "I wonder if this is the guy working with Dremmer. I say, 'At least that fuck Dremmer isn't here.' What's he really feeling?"
    "He wants to kill you and eat you. He's not really paying attention to what you're saying. He can't wait to taste your flesh."
    "Jesus, fuck, this guy's a cannibal? Shit!"

    Don't be all nit-picky and tight-lipped. 'Two questions for the price of one' is not a problem, ever. Say more than you think you should. Digress occasionally on some interesting thing. Be expansive. Answer more than they asked. Cover everything in apocalyptica every chance you get. It's not like you're going to run out.
