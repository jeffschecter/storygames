The Sci-fi thread started me think'n about board games, like the Star Trek III one I mentioned in the thread. Are there any board games or otherwise that you wish were RPGs and what would you do to make them one?

Mine: About the same time Star Trek III came out, another game call The Last Starfighter: The Tunnel Chase was out. You flew your starfighter around an asteroid avoiding walls while blasting the other players ships up. I remember having a ton o' fun with the game, but along the way I lost the rules, and then just found them recently online again. (http://www.boardgamegeek.com/game/3881)

What I'd do: I'd steal some of the ideas in Jennifer Schoonover's Game Chef entry Mississippi Steel and make the tunnel chase only part of the game. I'd reduce the damage capacity of the ships to have the tunnel chases end quicker, but then the characters would have a back life outside of the tunnels where they were space gladiators fighting over fame, fortune, and that new hot Y5600mdXXX sex robot. There would be bars, fist fights, sponsorships, and then the races.

    Actually, Colin, that makes perfect sense.

    There was a trading element to character creation in an Amber DRPG game that I did years and years ago... Clinton may remember it, I did it at a housecon at his place.

    Every player started out with a deck of cards. Number cards were attributes, face cards were powers. If you wanted to get better at anything compared to bog-standard Amberite abilities, you had to trade cards to do it. That way everyone started out with some clues about what the other players could do, but noone had the total picture... all they knew was what cards they had traded to whom.

    Hm. Here's an idea for a story element trading game:

    There's eleven of any particular token type in the game.

    These could be, for example, Create, Destroy, Change, Preserve; Fight, Talk, Sneak, Make; or what have you.

    Under some circumstance (dunno what that might be, every scene?) you can pull a token at random from the pot.

    If you can get ALL ELEVEN, you can buy something totally game-changing.
    If you get six (that is, more than everyone else put together) you can buy something major.
    If you have more than any ONE other player does, you can buy something minor.
    If you have any at all, you can buy something incidental.

    Manifesto! (a game I proposed for the 2005 game chef contest, failed to write then, but drafted later) transforms board games into the conflict resolution mechanism for an rpg of sorts. You get any go-round-the track-and-collect-cards type board game from a thrift shop, your closet, whatever, and detourne it by adding additional cards, scratching out or changing the title and logo, etc. The distance your piece travels on the board each turn detemines whether your character successfully deals with issues that arise in their life and the cards you collect are raw material/ideas for the manifesto players draft by the end of the game.

  • NickWedig
    April 2006
    Psychonauts! It's a platforming game for the PS2, but it has really entertaining situations and characters, and I think would provide a different sort of roleplaying experience from what I've found in other games.

    The last couple of levels in the game worked in a really interesting and cool way: as a Psychonaut, you can enter people mental landscapes, which are always surreal and cool looking. In the later levels, you enter the minds of a variety of insane characters and, through interacting with aspects of their personality, cure them of their mental problems. Their mental issues become externalized into various mental figments. Thus Edgar, the artist, is unable to paint anything except bullfights and is unable to control his rage. In his mental landscape, his rage is a giant red bull, and you eventually discover the obsession with bullfighting and the rage both come from anger after someone stole his girlfriend. His exgirlfirend, in his mind, becomes a beautiful, distant and mysterious woman who is imprisoned off in the sky. Edgar has to build a house of cards to reach her, but the house is repeatedly knocked down by the bull. The guy who stole edgar's girl is abstracted into a handsome bullfighter who won't face the bull. you have to help Edgar quell his anger by dealing with the mental constructs in his mental realm. In another level, you help a bipolar actress with a hyperactive self-criticizing side by defeating her mental critic, who appears as a phantom haunting the theatre that is her mind.

    This interests me as a way of addressing personal issues by externalizing them. I made some notes a few months ago sketching out a Psychonauts inspired rpg, but stalled out in design.
