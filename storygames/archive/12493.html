    \n
    \n Produce at least two sizes of PDF, if that's the format (A4, or somewhat smaller for iPads and KindleDX, and A5 or smaller for e-ink readers), with very, very minimal margins, single column text, no background image under the text.
    \n
    \nMost of the readers nowadays are 6 inches (Kindle2, Bokeen, Astak/Endless Ideas, Sony, Samsung, etc etc): A5 is already too big for that without zoom (sucky) or reflow (likely sucky or suckier).
    \nRemember that PDF is a photo of the page, more or less, so the ideal would be a pdf that is designed to fit a 6inch screen (800x600) to the millimeter (90 x 120 mm (3.54 x 4.72 inches)).
    \n
    \nIn the same vein, line-art b/n illustrations are generally better, better yet if in vector in the PDF, because they benefit from the high resolution (dpi wise) of the eink screens. But gradiends are fine: I've read manga on my BeBook Mini and they look great (if a bit small). Be careful with color: it gets rendered fine, but sometimes going from color to 16 gray levels does weird stuff to colors that are very different that become very similar greys.
    \n
    \nA last thing: if at all possible produce your pdf (in the layout program) in the most linear way possible. Some pdfs reflow pretty beautifully, some become ungodly messes. A reflowed pdf maintains fonts but *will* screw up with absolutely positioned stuff, layers and so on.
    \nI've seen pdfs where the ornated initials end up at the bottom of the page, the chapter titles (being graphics) disappear, or end up in strange places in the text, and so on.
    \n
    \nBottom line: either produce a pdf that will be readable *without* either zoom or reflow, or a pdf that will reflow gracefully (if not beautifully). Otherwise, it's just a print book that's not been printed (yet), not an ebook...
