    \n What timespan are we talking about? I usually run InSpectres on conventions, and that takes me 45 around minutes, but I'm following a very strict structure when doing that, where I introduce the rules as we play.

    Try searching for nanogames, where they are usually written on only one page. I haven't played them myself so I can't say how much time they demand.

    I'm currently looking for playtesters for my own This Is Pulp (60+ people has already tried it). The rules are three pages long, the game takes around 70 minutes to run, including collaborative setup and explaining the rules. I structured the game from the experience I got from running InSpectres. That game introduces the rules as you play, and we started to play when another team, that started at the same time and ran InSpectres, was half-way through character creation. I noticed that this game takes around 10 minutes for beginners to understand, and 20 minutes for experienced gamers.

    Check out Queen of Thorns by Eric Boyd. It's a nice little game with a very evocative mood that runs in under an hour. It's for the GM plus 1-3 players and runs great with a single player, so it's nice for an on-demand kind of thing.

    By default it's an evil queen, fairy-tale forest setting, but it can be reskinned on the fly too.

    \n How short is very short?

    At a library presentation in two weeks, I think I'm going to use Ben Lehman's SKEW (really an excellent game) and Vincent Baker's justly famous Murderous Ghosts.

    Giovanni Micolucci's Cartooner tends to run pretty short, as does Ben Lehman's Clover. And so does Epidiah Ravachol's Vast & Starlit, I'm told, though I haven't played it.

    A single "adventure" in Fables of Camelot is expected to last <1 hour, with a breakpoint in the middle to allow for players leaving or joining.

    I'm also focusing on short duration in my own designs, especially <a href="https://sites.google.com/site/sitodirafu/Home/i-miei-giochi/enter-the-avenger">Enter the Avenger and The Little Candy House (not yet available in English).
