\n With not being able to go to Gen Con, I'm looking for an alternative to my yearly gaming trip and looks like NecronomiCon in Tampa will be it. I'm talking to Rich Rogers of Canon Puncture to see if he can make it down from Jacksonville as well, and we'll see who else we can rope in.
\n
\nThe Gaming schedule is being posted to the Necro Gaming website, and of note (and this surprised me in a good way when I saw the announcements come in via Twitter), there'll be scheduled games of The Mountain Witch, Dogs in the Vineyard & My Life With Master alongside Savage Worlds, WoD, etc, which is just awesome. Once I know for sure I'll be going, I'll see about scheduling some other indie games as well.
\n
\nSo, Floridians, wanna jump in the Necro bus?

    \n Concerning Necronomicon, The Call of Cthulhu game being run at noon on Saturday is well worth signing up for. I've known John for 20 years, and his CoC is his go to game. One of his scenarios, "A Method to Madness" was recently published by Chaosium in "Terrors from Beyond".

    When I lived in Florida, Necro was the con to go to in October. Have fun!
