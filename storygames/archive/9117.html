\n The E6 SRD is supposed to be a subset of the d20 rules, reorganized with some appendices containing 3e's clunkiest rules.

These problems aren't anything new; the point is that the stuff that I didn't farm off to appendices makes for an excellent game.

With that game I will \n
  • develop an implied setting.
  • Run a West Marches campaign for in Toronto/GTA and online.
  • Show everybody and their dog how to quickly prepare great, huge sandbox campaigns.
\nBut I'm stalling as I work on the SRD, mostly because it feels like there's a mountain of work between this document and the point where I'm doing those things.

    \n Well, as much as anything the appendices are an organizational tool for me, so I know exactly what I've changed. 3.5e has a lot of interrelations.

    I do have all this other stuff I want to do to tweak it - replace the turn undead rules, spontaneous casters, tweak hit points, give action points, revise several subsystems. I could go down this road pretty damn far, to the point of completely changing the dice mechanics, movement scale, unify game currencies, and so on. But that would take E6 away from 3.5e and I don't want to make the players re-learn the game.

    So the Appendices are me keeping myself honest about not changing the rules of 3.5e.

    The mountain of work isn't just the appendices, though. There's also a lot of formatting and form-filling writing of sections ("Barbarians are great big hairy dudes - or chicks - that run fast and fight a lot. But get them into a spelling bee and they won't know what to do!") The reorganization of the core text is a lot of work, and to make the text structured in the compartmentalized way I want I've had to make some changes to terms that rattle through several other sections. As a result, what I see as "the beating heart of E6" is a game with max ranks, no multiclassing, and goblins and orcs instead of dwarves and halflings. The math of the system is preserved, but I wonder if I'm hurting the game's usefulness to 3e-o-philes.

    \n The important thing for a fan project is that you complete it to your own satisfaction and for your own edification and those of your peers. It's not the same as a commercial project. Don't worry about who you are going to "reach" or whether the purpose of the project is in some way worthwhile other than the worth you give to it.

    I mean, I did a fan.supplement for Trinity that was based on William Faulkner and there ain't NOBODY who gives a shit about that except me, but I felt great satisfaction when I completed it and I was very happy with how it came out.
