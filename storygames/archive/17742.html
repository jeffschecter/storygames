\n I love the cards in the game Dixit, but not so much the actual game itself. It's fun, but those cards are so evocative and rich that I can't help but think there's some hack or incorporation of them into another gaming system. My design mind is in its infancy so I can't quite figure out what to do with them, but I have a feeling some of you clever people may already have an idea for their other uses. The only thing I can think of its some sort of Oracle using the cards, like a GM emulator?

    \n Here is little idea, not based on the cards themselves, but on Dixit system.

    In rpg/storygames without GM, there is no judge to evaluate how good is your "role play". You could vote or spend token, but it's not very satisfacting : players could just vote for themselves, or make deals. From a "gamist" point of view, there's no incentive.

    Using the Dixit votation system - or a close version - could bring a well regulated way to do it. here it is:

    Each player vote for the "best role player", even himself. The most voted win, BUT all the player who have voted for him as well (or just a little less points).
