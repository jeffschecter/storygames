\n Jason asked me for this in another thread

Last night, everyone left but Sarah and Me (who live here), and Jared (who slept for two hours before coming over and didn't feel like going home). Jared was really hurting for some play, and since it's weird for me to play with just one other person, I made doe eyes at Sarah and apparently rolled a 20. I offered a one-shot using a mixture of the framing cards I designed for The Libertines (but decided against) and a really, really slim-and-trim version of FATE that I just kind of pulled out of the air as we played.

First off, the FATE-for-dummies variant I used:

Each character got 10 Aspects and 10 Fate Points. I think 10 FP is too much, so I might try 5 next time. I had them write down Health and Composure with 5 boxes each, but we never used them. Instead, we just used one roll to determine the winner of conflicts.

Fate Points acted as usual. +1 for just spending one. +2 for invoking an Aspect (they stacked the hell out of their aspects). Declare facts. I let them declare pretty much whatever they wanted to as long as it was awesome.

Now, the framing cards:

I bought a bunch of blank playing card-ish stock from a teacher supply store. Each card had a roman numeral on the bottom and a word or short phrase on the top:

1 = Setting (The Moors, The Graveyard, The Ruined Abbey, etc)
2 = Time (Midnight, Dawn, etc.)
3 = Stuff that adds mood but possibly has no impact on the story (Black, A Gnarled Tree, Wolves Howling, An Inexplicable Stench, Drought, etc.)...they actually all had major impact on the story we played.
4 = Character (A Stranger, A Boy, The Priest, The Innkeeper, etc.) There are also cards that modify these: (...who nobody listens to, ...who is extremely obese, etc.)
5 = Situation (Fire!, Someone Disappears, An Execution, A Body is Found, etc.)

Each player drew a hand of ten cards. We rolled initiative for the first player. Sarah won (as usual) and put down a 1: The School. Jared put down a 2: Sunset. We took turns until we put down the following numbers of cards: 1 2 3 3 3 4 4 4 5, to get this situation:

The School...Sunset...Red...A Thunderstorm...An Inexplicable Stench...A Boy (who is mute, who is nervous)...A Stranger (who has just returned after many years)...Villagers...A Body is Found

...added characters, stirred a little and placed over fire.

I have a little more work to do on the cards, and then I'll probably put them up as a PDF somewhere.

    Posted By: JDCorleyHow did you come up with 1 2 3 3 3 4 4 4 5?
    One setting, one time, three flavor/color details, three characters (just because that seemed like enough for this scene), and one situation.

    Three is the magic number for details. More can be silly, less is just boring.

    I haven't formalized the number of characters per scene, and I don't know that I will. Maybe it will just end when the person who's turn it is puts down a situation card.\n
