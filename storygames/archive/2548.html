    \n Dan, if you're in a metropolitain area, which it sounds like you are, you are almost by definition surrounded by gamers -- current gamers, lapsed gamers, potential gamers, the gamut. You just don't know who they are. All you need is a way to identify them.

    Findplay, if it gets functional, is a good start. So are local gaming conventions, as well as related conventions. Take a look at board game conventions or SCA groups and the like. I guarantee you there are some roleplayers among them. And then there's a little shindig near you called DragonCon. Start identifying gamers, gathering their contact information, and then start putting together little one-shots. Pick a nice, neutral location like a park on a weekend and get together a handful of folks to try Roach. Then the next month (or the next week, or whatever), get a slightly different group of people together and play, I dunno, Octane or something. Repeat as necessary. Eventually you'll be in a position where no matter what you want to play, you'll know a few guys who'd be interested in playing it.

    If you get really energetic, you can put up a website to help gamers network and before you know it, people that you introduced are off gaming on their own.

    This is one of those things where your biggest enemy is overcoming the initial inertia to start. Once the ball gets rolling, however, gamers start coming out of the woodwork like crazy. I've been consciously and intentionally networking gamers for just over one year, now, and I have more gaming opportunities than I can fit into a week.

    \n For a long time I wished that there was a store in Portland that carried Indie games, or a community that played them.
    \n
    \nA while back someone started an Portland Indie Games yahoo group. There wasn't much activity on it for some time, then one of the members (hi Matt!) invited a few other members (myself included) down to the pub and we spent a few hours talking about the games we all want to play and how we can make Portland a better place for Indie games. Matt ltached onto the idea that he could use the group to pitch a different game every month. I wasn't too suprised to find that once someone offered to facilitate a game there would be plenty of people willing to play it. His first go was Roach back in Jan, which went really well. Travis Brown latched onto the idea that out lack kluster loal show Gamestorm could be repurposed as a 3 day indie game event. He's organizing a booth, scheduling games and panels, setting up demos and getting people excited for what has traditionally been a dull con. For my part, I've decided to organize an almost monthly game night at my home where I invite over 10-14 people and we play games. The first time I did this was in Jan. We had Beast Hunters and Panty Explosion, and the whole thing went pretty well. We're trying it again this Sat with Capes, Sign in Stranger and The CrossRoads of Eternity. Should be fun.
    \n
    \nI think when Colin, Mathew, Travis and I first sat down at the pub we thought putting together and encouraging an Indie games community in Portland would be a lot of work. Turns out it was complete cake, and players have come out of the woodwork. The one thing we are still missing though is a local store that carries the games we want to play, but even without that we've been able to build a nice community.
