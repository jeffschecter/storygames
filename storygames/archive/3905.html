\n The Ashcan Front is ready for prime time at GenCon.

Check out this booth "menu" -- a nice PDF that provides a sneak peek to our 11 games appearing for the first time at GenCon 2007. This is an exciting line up of games.

There are some really astounding covers in the catalog. I can't wait to see these games in the flesh. Paul's Acts of Evil texture looks amazing. Matt nailed it with the Galactic titling, which I bitched at him about months ago. Nice! And, Kevin's Sweet Agatha cover just blows me away. So alluring. Nice fucking work, Kevin!

    \n And, I've also got to add these pics of the Ashcan Front booth shelving. My brother, Dave, built these. His work is superb. It is awesome. Yes, Graham, actually awesome. His craftsmanship fills me with awe. He's quite talented. The shelf is far and away the most attractive thing I've seen at GenCon for this kind of thing.

    It's constructed from maple solid wood and maple plywood veneer, with small flourishes of ebony wood.

    Check it out:



    Here is the shelf stocked with some books from my shelf (sorry, no ashcan books just yet).



    Here's a close-up view of the shelf corner. Dave inset decorative corner pegs of ebony wood into the maple. The effect is very nice. A bit of flourish, but not over-the-top.



    And, here's the full shelf again. Dave and I designed it to hold a variety of sizes from half-digest (like The Blossoms Are Falling ... -- YES I OWN IT ALREADY THANKYOUVERYMUCH!) up to full letter size (like HeroQuest). Dust Devils is 6x9, and you can see if fits nicely on the top shelf, which is smaller.

    The only downside I can see so far is the slight shadow from the top and the shelf. I think this won't be much problem in the convention hall, which is well lit.
