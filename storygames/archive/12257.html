\n John and I were just discussing these today, though his suggestions were a little different than mine here.

Go Play
(already a classic: nothing substitutes for play; go do it)

No Blame
(when something weird/bad happens, don't focus on blaming yourself, the other players, the game, or the designer; talk about it, fix it, and/or move on)

Be Attentive
(be sincere, listen, and engage the game on its own terms)

Story Now
(another classic: cut right to the meat of things instead of fucking around with preparations)

Try Again
(sometimes it doesn't work the first time; back it up and do it better or differently)

    \n Right now they're just cute little two-word phrases with a set of familiar symbols that make them easy to remember.

    They can also be, like, a shorthand way of talking about positivity and mindfulness with your group. Like, "Hey, I feel like we sometimes have issues being attentive." Or "Can we stop it with the blame and figure out how to move forward?" Or even during play, "Yo, can we try that again? It felt weird to me."

    There's no real agenda here, just sharing some thoughts. I'm not really attached to the jargon and realize that I'm slaughtering Ron's use of Story Now a bit. I'm more interested in the overall concepts: general principles for approaching play with a positive attitude and some tools for avoiding common social issues.

    \n Just for the sake of comparison, here's how I've been using these:

    \xa0 Begin or resume in-character play.

    \xa0 Stop the game entirely, to eat, go home, or discuss social matters.

    \xa0 Stop in-character play to address something about the game itself, presumably briefly.

    \xa0 Summarize some fictional events to get to the next interesting part.

    \xa0 Undo a specified amount of the fiction.

    I think the FFW button is the most universally obvious. The rest may be seen in many different ways.
