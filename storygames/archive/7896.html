\n For many years now, I've been playing other people's games by the book. Insisting on following the rules, to see what they would do. In hindsight, I've been like an apprentice, learning from strict mentors through the games they designed, as if they were sitting on my shoulder while I tried to fathom their visions and run their games. Some were idiots, some were brilliant, and I've learned from all of them. Sometimes I was an idiot, sometimes I was brilliant. But what strikes me now is how many times I've played a game not to enjoy it, but just to see what it does.

I'll probably be doing that for the rest of my life. Learning techniques, picking up skills and ideas, trying them out, believing in them for a while, throwing them away when they're no longer useful. Returning to the core sometimes, other times going out to explore again.
