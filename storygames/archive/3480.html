\n I am noodling around about White Wolf's new game, Scion (helmets on, brain damage imminent!) as potentially my next campaign after Project Tenebrous, future winner of the R-Map contest, comes to an end.

Scion, for those of you who don't frequent rpg.net and therefore aren't sick to death of hearing about it, is a non-World of Darkness game using a souped-up version of the Storyteller system that encourages more stunts and throws around more dice. It has some similarities to Exalted, but is set in the modern day, with the characters as the offspring of the gods of various pantheons. It's pretty stylish, it has centaur motorcycles and dwarves that turn to stone in the sunlight and things explode and there's magic.

I was a bit inspired by how great the Tenebrous r-map came out without even any significant attention on my part, and intend for the game to have a much more wide, and deep, relationship map, with unexpected relationships popping up here and there, and the players taking on NPC roles on a semi-regular basis. I may also have some inconsistent narration and basically all the junk that I love.

I read about someone's (it may have even been a story-gamer's!) Cyberpunk 2020 campaign called 99 Problems..., about a rapper's entourage and their adventures in a teenage wasteland, I mean their adventures in the future. I immediately thought the format for this game would be like a really stylish television show, with each problem actually numbered. So someone would come in with a subpoena that conflicts with a flight to Tokyo and there would be a freeze-frame on the subpoena and there would be a graphic that said "PROBLEM 8", or a guy suddenly popping up in a restaurant with mini-guns and yelling and starting to fire, there would be a freeze-frame and a graphic that said "PROBLEM 19", and the show would be over after 99 problems. I have no idea if this is what that person's game was like. But that's what I thought it SHOULD be like.

Anyway, I was trying to think of a good name for the campaign that would suggest a certain kind of format, like Lexicon is played through entries in a dictionary, and my idea for "99 Problems" would be to divide the game into problems. What should I do for a game of modern-day children of the gods having high-flying, face-punching adventures and the ripples of their activities and the magical world they're in spreading ever-outwards in a relationship map that would even, could such a thing be imagined, be better than my Tenebrous r-map?

    \n I decided not to go with a number in the title, but instead to have one of the in-character organizations, a group obsessed with recording everything that happens on this world or in any other, assign a completely bizarre numbering and organizational scheme, in order to reflect my goal that the relationship map for this game be only about 1000 times more fucking insane than my award-winning map for the Tenebrous game, and also to have the first chapter be called "40 Days, 40 Nights" and be about (among other things) the impending corruption of the 40 Valkyrie, one per night.

    The name of the actual game is "What the Crows Saw" and although it's so very not ready yet, you can see the bits I'm using to make it at:

    thecrows.scarymonsters.net

    It is currently a little like an unfinished ARG.

    "speak a little softer
    work a little louder
    shoot less with more care
    sing a little sweeter
    and love a little longer
    and soon you will be there"
