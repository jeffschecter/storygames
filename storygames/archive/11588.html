\n

Shock:Human Contact went really well at Dreamation. Thanks to the dozen or so players for your creativity and ability to throw and take a fictional punch. You made some worlds that needed help, and we started to see some neat ideas about socioeconomic systems that might work. We blew up some heads, we fomented revolution, we kidnapped some leaders, and we had some really weird sex. You guys make great partners.

\n

As expected, I've got a few copies left over. I'd love to see them in the hands of people who will be into it. I've only got a few of this limited edition, so I'm not going to set up a Buy Now button, but you can PayPal $9 + $4 s&h in the US or +$6 s&h elsewhere in the world to orders@glyphpress.com .

\n

If you like Ian M. Banks' Culture novels, Ursula LeGuin's Hainish stories, Asimov's Foundation trilogy, or heck, Star Trek (while wishing the show went into the intercultural parts it claims to be about), you'll be into this. The supplement does interesting things to Shock:, not the least of which is that it gives players enough hope to not destroy the world at the end. It also includes a new set of rules for interprotagonist conflict and a "mind control" subsystem that works for seduction, brainwashing, and lying.

\n

It's a Shock: supplement, so you'll need a copy of the book to play.
