\n You are hereby invited to submit a game or scenario for Fastaval 2010, March 31 \u2013 April 5, \xc5rhus, Denmark. At first we need a synopsis \u2013 a short description of your idea. After the deadline we will select which games are set up at the con, and you will receive more information.

Fastaval is interested in the broad spectrum of rollplaying games and scenarios from larps, story games to structured freeform. The games are selected so there is a divers program with different genres and style. All games on the program will participate in the Otto Awards where a jury will honour the best games in 6 different award categories.

The final contributions should be instructions for a game session. The instructions are not required to be translated to Danish (English or Norwegian, Swedish is fine), but it should be self explanatory so a game master or a group can host/play the game from the instruction alone.

You are not required to attend the con, to make a contribution, but we highly recommend it.

Send your synopsis to rollespil at fastaval dot dk containing the following information:
o Worktitle
o Contact information (name, address, email)
o Short description of contribution (one page at most)
o Expected play time

The synopsis deadline for internationals is Sunday the 25 October 2009. You will receive more information after the selection and participants with approved synopsis will be offered assistance with concept development, play test and production.

Final submission of the completed games deadline is Monday the 15. February 2010.

On behalf of Fastaval
Kristoffer Rudkjaer

    Presentation of scenarios Fastaval 2010

    Here follows a presentation of the scenarios that come to Fastaval 2010. As most may know, we got incredible synopsis of many years. Entire 61 to be precise. For these scenarios we have selected the following 31. We are very pleased to both engage in the development process and to see the final scenarios.

    - Klaus Meier Olsen, on behalf of the scenario responsible

    "Salvation" by Simon Steen Hansen: A grim and violent Western thriller about five lawless who discovers an abandoned mining town cruel secret and must make up their own dark past.

    "The seven deadly sins" of Sanne Harder, Tina Arbj\xf8rn, Cicely Balling, Eva Fog Regitze Illum, Helene Willer and Maja Crown: An anthology of short stories about the seven deadly sins, where the stories are woven together by place in the same city.

    "Dungeon" by Lars Andresen: a group A player takes a real dungeon in Assist's graveyard and goes on adventures. An affectionate satire of 80's dungeon-crawler.

    "The Slave Trader from Ascalon" by Johannes Busted Larsen: A frantic action-narrative to the system Red Box Hack, where both roles should fight against snake demons and their own past. It is rock 'n' roll!

    "Fucking Mental!" by Kristoffer Rudkj\xe6r and Kristoffer Apollo: Kitchen sink realism, hooligans and macho culture of the '90s England. There must drink cider, fuck easy birds and smashing some West Ham fans.

    "Zodiac" by Simon James Pettitt: 12 gods shall judge of the dead. World is created when the sentences are written in a chronicle and God is slowly becoming more and more human.

    "Empire 40K" by Peter Fallesen, Ren\xe9 Toft, Kristian Bach Petersen, Elias Helfer, Greis Morten Pedersen (eds), Mads Brynnum, Kristoffer Rudkj\xe6r (ed.) and Martin Svendsen: An anthology of short stories set in the Warhammer universe 40Ks. Novelettes explores the dystopian universe and mankind's destiny in a future where only the war is back.

    "As sent from heaven" by Andreas Liberoth Wadum and Rasmus Boldt: Five angels help a man with love on a trip from Copenhagen to Langeland. A comedy about faith, hope and love. And Bodega Heavens Hound.

    "When the Chinese come," by Marie Oscilowski: The Chinese troops in Tibet with an iron grip and trying to brutally crack people's resistance in this intense personal drama.

    "Villa Clich\xe9lla" by Milton Felice Brambati Lund and Anders Bo Wood: A gyserpastiche that still manages to scare people. Expect nerd-bitch quarterbacks and stupid cheerleaders, whose death is closely linked to how much they are cliches.

    "Magician: Impossible" by Kristian Bach Petersen: A fantasy comedy that makes up the magic as boring rules and makes it magical and exciting again. It has never been cooler to throw a fireball.

    "Previous occupant" by Tobias Wrigstad and Frederik Berg \xd8stergaard: Two stories of thirty years between them, is told in parallel, creating a thriller that mixed-type jeep with a brutal murder in a hotel room.

    "Quis custodiet Ipsos custodes?" by Peter Fallesen: A society critical drama set on a volcanic island, where five lifestyle gurus losing more and more of themselves in their encounter with a genuine guru.

    "Passion Fruit" by Nathan Hook: Indie meetings more classic scenario writing in this little drama about love, where players have great influence on the story.

    "Gears claims" by Carsten Andreasen and Martin Horn Pedersen: A meeting of scientists and workers in a steampunk drama with a political agenda.

    "Cat" by Lars Hansen Blichfeldt: Two parallel stories about love and death, shows the consequences of both choices and coincidences in this jeep-type drama where an ordinary cat means the difference between life and death.

    "Magicide" by Louise Floor Frellsen: A scenario where players are forced to see the past through another's eyes, set in a fantasy world where magic is forbidden and magical creatures are being persecuted.

    "Ascalondynastiet" afTroels Ken Pedersen and Morten Greis Pedersen: An epic chronicle of the country Ascalon from a young king sits on the throne for him, old and gray, looks out over his country. Expect major policy and potent epic in this scenario of double length.

    "Epifani" by Michael Sonne: A science fiction scenario set in an isolated spaceship, where players must seek recognition of their situation while they lose more and more power over their own actions.

    "Farmers Maze" by Regitze Illum and Malik Hyltoft: An investigation scenario set in a science fiction world of "No home. Thousand Stars" where you both play the suspects and those who must solve the crime.

    "Vase" by Mikkel B\xe6kgaard: Social realism and supernatural horror meet each other in this scenario, where a retired social worker investigating a young girl's mysterious death.

    "Innocent" by Mikael Erik N\xe6sby: David Riis returns in this game looking horror tackle, where players have to create eerie, aided by scenario tools.

    "The boy and the City" by Anne angle and Niels Ladefoged Rasmussen: A magical telling realistic scenario about a boy whose life forms a whole city's fate.

    "Three kidney shock to romantic love" of Frikard Elleman: Three small indie-inspired short stories about the nature of love and the price it demands.

    "Sense and Sensibility" by Anna West Erling: An adaptation, in which Jane Austen's famous story is mixed up with the jeep-form and players who prefer the story in different directions.

    "Resan / The Journey" by Fredrik Axelson: A road movie set in a bleak post-apocalyptic world. The scenario mixes jeep-form, theatrical elements and heavy role playing Jutland, where the key is all that is not being said.

    "What's your worth?" by Charles Bo Nielsen and Lasse E. Oszadlik: A classic confined space / mind fuck live scenario for losers. Think "Executioners" with an undertone of social criticism.

    "Ghost" by Troels Barkholdt: A live scenario plays with fiction and player roles. A murder must be committed, but who the murderer is, provides the players themselves.

    "Farewell" by Max Miller: An oppressive personal drama about grief, sat at the funeral of a loved one, where the past slowly comes to the surface.

    "A rat race" by Klaus Meier Olsen: A swindler comedy set in the Warhammer world where five Skaven actually should prepare for the invasion of the Empire, but are too busy to stab each other in the back.

    "What to do about Tam Lin" by Emily Care Boss and Julia Ellingboe: A little life scenario of what happens to a child with both Alfe and human blood. The scenario draws on Celtic myths and mixes them with the jeep-type.
