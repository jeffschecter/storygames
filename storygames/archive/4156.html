\n So, last night while idly pondering Best Friends and Do, as well as Shock:'s issues you care about, it occurred to me that there's a neat variant on the "I hate X because..." and "I want to learn X from..."

Rather than defining their character by your character. Define their character by some element of yourself you consider important. Then do that for everybody.

"Xavier is like me, because of his dogged persistence"

Fun because it lets people in on how you perceive yourself, and gives somebody's character something you find interesting to show you from another angle. Dangerous because it opens you up for critique...

    \n What I find interesting -- as a feature to be worked in, not a bug -- is inconsistency. A very simple example:

    Bob says: "Xavier is like me, because he's strong."
    Joe says: "Xavier is like me, because he's weak."

    There's something here that could be really interesting.
