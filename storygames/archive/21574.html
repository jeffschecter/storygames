    vgunn said:


    Explain Waiting to me.

    Would Defending/Dodging fall under Combat?

    \n
    Waiting rolls is whenever time passes. Are you lucky or will monsters come?

    Combat: not every dungeoneer can fight. Some cast spells, others just carry the chests and water. When the kobolds shoot them, they make Dodging rolls to not die.

    These three are the only ones I've noticed every character use. And about half of them have used first aid so that's my honorary fourth.

    Searching I'm not into because I want that as a player skill, not a character skill. Same goes for bargaining and exploring and navigating. And make camp, preparing, reiterate resolutions, I don't really want dice to make them to fail.

    And sorcery is like combat: essential to some roles, not others.

    \n I'm going to cut in some different directions than I see above:

    Dungeoneering: (how good are you at navigating and manipulating the physical structures of the dungeon?)

    Interpersonal: (how good are you at dealing with people and monsters you encounter; this covers combat and diplomacy; they're all ways of dealing with people).

    I'm more fuzzy on a third; I could see Perception (but that can be rolled into dungeoneering/interpersonal if everything you see is either part of the dungeon or a person), Self (how good are you at maintaining control over yourself; this will handle will checks, self-control, and other internal conflicts), or Luck.
