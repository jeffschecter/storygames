\n Okay guys, so I've got a magic system based on speaking Korean but I want to make sure that players will be able to cast any kind of spell they can think of. So you can either just post some spells you think are really cool from other games, or you can look at which categories I have and come up with something that won't fit into any category or "type" of magic. So far I have these:


1) illusionist (anything to do with the five senses)
2) healer
3) clairvoyant/divination (finding out information)
4) telekinetic (floating/flying, moving things around)
5) buffer (augmenting someone in someway or the reverse)
6) dispeller (getting rid of spells)
7) puppet master (compulsions, commands)
8) time master (anything to do with time)
9) striker (hitting things)
10) summoner (calling/compelling/conjuring)

Anything I'm missing?

    \n Well each category has grammar patterns and/or vocab associated with it, since the game is designed to learn a language. So it would go like this:

    1) illusionist (simple statements - is/is not, five senses)
    2) healer (asking for things)
    3) clairvoyant (questions)
    4) telekinetic (directions)
    5) buffer (adverbs/adjectives)
    6) dispeller (negatives)
    7) puppet master (commands, compulsions)
    8) time traveler (tenses, numbers)
    9) striker (human body, location)
    10) transmuter/controller (passive verbs)


    So sneak and defensive stuff I think could go under buffer/augmenter (make Kyle sneaky/make Kyle's skin hard ) or illusion (make it so he can't be seen or heard) that kind of thing. I've actually got a system for wards and shields as well where players have to say tongue twisters to put them up since they'll most likely be done at the start of a game session.

    Striker would include fireballs and such, you'd say something like 'I throw magical fire at him' or 'I send a bolt of lightning at his hand'.

    I don't have anything for curses or geases, that's for sure. That would be a pretty hard sentence but good for teaching conditionals I suppose.
