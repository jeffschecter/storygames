\n So... this post is here for two reasons.

One, on the very off chance that there's anyone here in my geographical vicinity (eastern Norway), I'm having a role-playing salon on friday, and we'll be playing Shock: - see this post. You're welcome to join us if you're willing and able.

Second, has anyone else tried this? It's basically just an open invitation for anyone to come and play a specific game at a specific time. I've done this a few times - with Mridangam, Downtown (local game), and Lacuna (amazing game!). Others have done it, too - it's an open thing, so anyone can set a time and game and invite people, as long as it's open for everyone. We've been drawing crowds of... around 5 people.

    \n Yes, I started up what I called the Melbourne Roleplaying Salon at the beginning of last year. We are a group of people who didn't know each other well beforehand, who meet up once a fortnight to play 4-8 session runs of different games. The ideal is that we play games that are new to us or try things we haven't done before. We've had playing of games people never got to play before, we've had first time GMing, and lots of fun.

    It hasn't had as much discussion and review as I initally envisaged, but it's working ok. My main problem is that I keep getting sick on game nights and not making it.

    \n The lack of open gaming is the snake in our (Raleigh/Durham's) gaming paradise. When I've had this going in other locations, it's been wonderful, and a great way to play new games, meet new people, and start new groups.

    I imagine a large part of it is the growing age of the people I play with. In Seattle, for example, I had no responsibilities besides work, and so I could schedule accordingly. Now I have lots of responsibilities and so do most people I know, which gets in the way. The monthly meetups I tried to run here didn't work, but I think the biggest parts of that was lack of location and lack of salesmanship by me.

    Anyway, I have no solution, but this is my holy grail of gaming for our area.

    \n Space and location are problems in NYC.
    \n
    \nWe occasionally organize boardgame nights at thinkcoffee but the space wouldn't work for RPGs. Space is limited to 30 people.
    \n
    \nWe occasionally rent space at Martial Arts and Theater studios for RPG 1shots and short campaigns. But we have to charge at least $7-8 per person for 4 hours to cover the rent. Space is limited to 45 people.
    \n
    \nWe occasionally rent space at art galleries or community centers for all day gaming. But we have to charge at least $15 per person for 11 hours to cover the rent. Space is limited to 90 people. We're running one of these events on March 11th.
    \n
    \nI think if we switched our focus from biweekly gaming events and put more energy purely into all day open gaming events, we would do a much better job growing or bringing together the local community.
