\n I'm a tabletop gamer who (like @Jason_Morningstar, @Emily_Care and others) has recently become a larpwright and larper par excellence.

This transformation happened partially due to Fastaval in Denmark, but definitely due to Intercon as well.

Intercon is a multi-genre parlor larp convention in its 16th year that runs out of an excellent Radisson Suites hotel in Chelmsford, MA every March.
This year, it's March 1-3.

The way the convention works is:
--You bid a game that would work well as a short parlor larp (2-6 hours, 5-30 players).
--After they accept your bid, sometime in late October the sign-up goes live and larpers sign up for it.
--Then you've got almost 5 months of blessed e-mail contact with your prospective players in which you establish their characters and run logistics, so that everyone's got some interesting, invested backstory and even a kick-ass costume to boot
--You run it with a wonderful group of players.

Anyone who, for example, enjoyed Jason's run of Jiituomas' The Tribunal or who are interested in jeepform, etc., this is your con.

This thread is for folks thinking about going to talk about it, or to pitch games that they're probably going to run.
