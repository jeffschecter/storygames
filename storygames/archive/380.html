Is it Conflict Resolution or Something Else?

Jasper
March 2006 edited March 2006 in Story Games
I'm playtesting a game (Utopia) that has an interesting resolution scheme. It may be a form of conflict resolution, but maybe it's different enough to gets its own label.

It goes like this.
  • A conflict is initiated by a PC making a decision or taking an action that might have major repurcussions.
  • That action/decision is not in question. In either case, a long-term hope is also named, such that the decision or action is hoped to advance that cause in some way.
  • The resolution mechanics are invoked. They answer the question, "What are the consequences of your action?" If you roll low, then your action will backfire and actually harm you, mostly in terms of the long-term hope. If you roll well, you'll take a big step towards it.
  • Knowing the roll result, the context of the roll, and the named hope, a narrator describes something which makes the roll result come true. The exact form of good or bad consequences is up to him, however.
Here's an example.

My character has an audience with a foreign king. I say that he greets the king and makes a fine speech, praising his domain. Everyone acknowledges that this occurs (there is a way to challenge it). Someone says that this should be a conflict, and I agree. I say that my goal is to get the king's help in a war. Here's what someone might narrate, based on different roll results.

Kinda good roll: The king is impressed, and gives you some gifts. He also promises to send a retinue to escort you through the rest of his domain.

Bad roll: The king gives gifts to you and makes promises, but actually orders his leiutenant to murder you in your beds.

Really good roll: The king pledges to support your war effort, and immediately musters 200 of his best men.


Is this conflit resolution? We're naming a kind of stakes up front, but only in vague terms. What we fill in, with narration, are the actual consequences, as well as how we get them (unlike a lot of Conflict Res). My character's actions are already fixed before resolution is invoked, unlike Conflict or Task resolution (at least as I know them).

Obviously, classifying the scheme won't change it, but I'm curious what people think. And I want to know if it seems cool or problematic too, of course.
