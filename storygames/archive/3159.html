\n This is actually a rather long AP and I'll be posting it in sections to a certain degree. AP on con's Forum. this is the game mentioned in my thread about doing atheory panel.
\n
\nPolaris is played with each player taking a turn as you go around the table. The person that sits across form you plays the Mistaken for you controlling as adversity that comes against you. Those to either side play the Moons that are neutral judges of what happens and play any characters that are not against you. the thread on the con forum has some more details about how the game works.
\n
\nCraig played Giauzar
\nJon played Canis Major. Played Scutum, the Shield.
\nMe as Equaleus.
\nThat is how we sat around the table.
\n
\nWe started with Craig playing Giauzar, me as his Mistaken, with Jon and Scutum's player as the Moons.
\nThere was no set story or plot and just work things out as we play.
\n
\nBut hope was not yet lost for Giauzar still heard the song of the stars.
\nAnd so it was...
\nthat Giauzar ventured forth in the Spring to fight of the Mistaken with a group of fellow knights. this is a dangerous time for the People as all but the Knights stand frozen before the rising of the sun. A single Mistaken that appears in form like one of the insect steeds of the people approaches them along the side of the canyon wall but Giauzar sneaks up apon it and slays it but he then sees a large army of these creatures lead by a demon knight riding atop one of them approaching from further along the canyon.
\nand that is how it came to pass.
\n
\nEveryone takes a turn with their character being the main character of the story with the other players taking the roles of mistaken and moons depending on how they sit around the table.
\nThe key phrase "And so it was..." is used to start a scene with a character. "and that is how it came to pass." is used to accept everything that has happened and end a scene. I'll be using those to mark when one player starts his turn and when his turn ends ends.
\n
\nBut hope was not yet lost for Scutum still heard the song of the stars.
\nAnd so it was...
\nthat Scutum, a week before the Spring came and Giauzar ventured into the wastes to fight the Mistaken, took part in tournament of the People. Jon sets the stage here since he is Scutums Mistaken. Scutum and Jon's character Canus Major are enemies ut we don't yet know why. Jon says the tournament is to see who is the strongest, fastest, and tallest of the People. Canus and Scutum are in a wrestling match to see who is the strongest. Scutum is the largest and strongest of the two so he has an advantage in this. They trade feet and hold him overhead but Canus rips off Scutums lion cloth during this to embarrass him. Scutum calls forth his starlight shield preserve his dignity.
\nand that is how it happened.
\n
\nall of the Knights have weapons of starlight that they can call forth though some carry preferment weapons with them instead.
\nWe have the idea of making Equuleus (Means of the Horse) the demon that Giauzar is fighting I set my character one year in the past.
\n
\nAnd so it was...
\nthat in the Spring one year before Giauzar rode into the wastes Equuleus rides out with his friends Scutum and Canus Minor (brother of Canus Major) at the start of the spring to fight the Mistaken. They find a great lord of the demons and a host of lesser demons at his command. Scutum calls forth his starlight sword and shield and does battle with the lesser Mistaken while the demon lord rides toward Canus Minor to kill him. Equuleus must choose between the saving Scutum or Canus Minor and chooses to save Scutum. Canus Minor is beheaded in a single blow while Equuleus and Scutum kill or set to flight most of the demon's host. Scutum strikes down kill the demon lord but only if Equuleus is blinded. And that is how it happened that Scutum cut down the demon lord moments before it struck down Equuleus but the foul blood of the demon spills upon Equuleus and blinds him.
\n
\nJon plays his character Canus Major next setting the scene himself rather then letting his Mistaken (Scutum's player) do it for him since he has an idea that ties in with the 2 starlight swords he wields.
\n
\nAnd so it was...
\nat the funeral for his brother Canus Minor Canus Major blames and berates Equuleus for the death of his brother and laments that they could not even bring back Minor's head for a proper burial. Canus proclaims that Equalies is no Knight his brother's star shall be forever dimiished because of his incomplete burial. Admist the mourners for Canus Minor Canus Major rips off his brothers arm and proclaims that he shall have it forged into a second starlight sword. To try and molify Canus Major the priest tells him that if he wins glories with his brothers sword his brother's star will shine the brighter for it. Canus Major punches the priest in the face and goes to kill him only to be stopped by Scutum.
\nAnd that is how Canus Major came to wield 2 starlight swords and the rivalry between him and Scutum was created.
\n
\nWe now return to Giauzar in the present time.
\nAnd so it was...
\nGiauzar now fights man to man with the demon knight of the Mistaken while his companions try to hold off the horde of demon steeds that pour forth form the Mistake. The demon knight wears 2 heads at his belt (One is Canus Minors.) and threatens to add Giauzar's head as well before calling forth his blade of blackness and smoke that lets forth a horrid wail as he charges. Giauzar calls forth his sword of starlight that sings in a pure tone, charging to meet the demon lord. He cuts off the demons sword arm and turns to face him for a second pass. The demon lord rips off one of the legs form his mount and uses it as a spear to renew the attack against Giauzar only to lose his other arm.
\nand that is how it happened.
\n
\nAnd so it was...
\nDuring Scutum and Canus Major's fight (1 week before present) at the tournament Canus Major is thrown aside by Scutum and as he lies on the ground picks up a large rock and attacks Scutum with it. Scutum seeing this sticks back at Canus with his starlight shield that he was using to preserve his dignity crushing Canus's face with it.
\nWhile somewhat disgraced Scutum is awarded the trophy for being the strongest this year. As Scutum is leaving the tournament yard he sees Giauzar preparing for his journey into the wastes of the Mistake. He goes to see Canus major in the doctors tent to apologize for the injury he dealt him. He sees that Canus is having his face patched up with the light of the hated sun for as the doctors says \xe2\u20ac\u0153It it not beautiful?\xe2\u20ac\x9d and \xe2\u20ac\u0153Does it not give us light so we do not dwell in darkness?\xe2\u20ac\x9d. But the knights know that the sun is the cause of the People destruction and the herald of the Mistaken's attacks and hate it for that. Canus is angry and will not be consoled calling both Scutum and himself false knights and unworthy for having acted so lowly in the fight. Canus says that there is a true Knight of the Order of the Starts and you passed him on your way here, think about that.
\nand that is how it happened.

    \n And so it was...
    \nEquuleus (1 year before present) returns home to mourn the fact that 2 great knights have been lost to the cause, one to death and himself to blindness. But his faithful servant Savik(Really a servant of the Mistaken) tells him of a doctor in another Remnant that can restore his sight and he will lead him there. Following Savik Equuleus is led deep into the wastes where he meets with one of the Mistaken and he learns how to restore his sight but only if he swears to call the Mistaken master and serve him. And so it was that by blood he lost his sight and blood he will regain it but it must be the blood of the innocent.
    \nPerhaps it was some evil sorcerer or the weakness in the knights heart but we find Equuleus lead by his servant Savik in the bed chambers of the sleeping Su'hail that very night. Su'hail who is the most beautiful in appearance, song, and innocence amongst the People. Equuleus is at first dismayed at what he is about to do but tells himself that one life is a small price to pay compared to how many shall be lost if the People lose the service of a knight as great as he. Calling forth his starlight sword Equuleus kills Su'hail but only if this alerts Prince Rigel to his presence. And so it was that Prince Rigel sees that Equuleus killed his daughter, the most beautiful of the People, but Equuleus tells him that a great evil has been removed from here and leaves Rigel to his mourning.
    \nAnd that is how it happened having done this horrible crime that Equuleus went to meet his new master in the wastes.
    \n
    \nAnd so it was...
    \nCanus Major and Equuleus have been no more then a week apart since the funereal we come across him being called before Prince Rigel. With the sound of the mourner's songs being sung for the innocent Su'hail around them Prince Rigel beseeches Canus to find why Equuleus had killed his daughter and to kill Equuleus if it is possible. Canus still felling the lose of his brother and blaming Equuleus vows to kill Equuleus. Going to his friend Giauzar Canus begs him to come as well and help in the search. While they did not find Equuleus in the first week since Equuleus is with his new master in the wastes they do find his servant Savik. Canus tortures Savik to get answers from him but receives none. Giauzar finding this to have happened saw that Canus was mad and forced Canus to swear that he would not kill Equuleus if he did not have evidence that Equuleus deserved death.
    \nAnd that is how it happened.

The first was to take some blood from Giauzar to heal his own wounds with and secondly to blind Giauzar with his own blood. But Giauzar was not so easily overcome and prevented the demon from blinding him but the demon bite him cruelly and by that blood regained his arms. Calling forth a demon steed for himself and for Giauzar he said \xe2\u20ac\u0153Come brave Knight, let us both remount and make this a real fight.\xe2\u20ac\x9d. And having said that he remounted his own steed and caused the heads apon his belt to sing as to bring down the cavern on all who fought within.

    \nAnd so it was...
    \nthat Equuleus returned form the wastes and his new master's services went to Scutum's house. In great distress Equuleus begged Scutum to protect his life with his own for he had done great wrongs and feared that some may wish to do him ill in revenge for it. On having Scutum's promise he swore that he loved him as a brother and declared \xe2\u20ac\u0153Let us be done with such sorrowful thoughts and make merry while we can.\xe2\u20ac\x9d. Heading out they drank and made merry until Equuleus servant Savik found them. Savik told Equuleus that his master demanded his presence. This angered Equuleus greatly and he yelled at this servant to let them be and when Savik pressed further Equuleus struck him a blow that threw Savik to the ground. \xe2\u20ac\u0153Go tell my master that I will see him when I am ready to do so.\xe2\u20ac\x9d
    \nEquuleus would not answer any question put to him on this matter by Scutum and so Scutum left Equuleus to his drink alone.
    \nhaving his merry mood ruined and his companion left him Equuleus put off the unpleasant task no longer and went into the wastes to meet his master. On meeting this lord of the Mistaken Equuleus called him by his name Price Rigel and asked what service was needed now.
    \nAnd that was how it happened.
    \n
    \nJon had the idea to make Prince Rigel the lord of the Mistaken at this point. It took me completely by surprise that Prince Rigel had set this whole thing up from the start with Equuleus's blindness, the murder of his daughter, and corrupting Canus with thoughts of vengeance.
    \nAlso at this point my Knight had lost all of his Zeal. Zeal measures a Knight's devoutness to his cause and is a powerful in game resource. A knight may lose some when ever he betrays the People or from other acts unworthy of a knight. A knight with no Zeal can fall and become one of the Mistaken and that is what happened here. Equuleus official became the Demon Knight that Giauzar is fighting.
