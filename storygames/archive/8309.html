    \n I was born my mother's Antagonist. She wept when I turned thirteen and was given my Fulcrum, knowing it was now my destiny to oppose her at every turn. I told her it was her fault - it was her own ambition that drove her to become a Protagonist. She was young when she made the choice, of course; and when the Keepers of the Minutiae told her of the dangers and potential consequences of Protagonization, she laughed them off.

    My early Antagonism was basic, crude; I cringe when thinking about it. Pushing her off a cliff, leaving her hanging on by her fingernails. Sending black-clad men with guns after her. Now it is more subtle, more cunning. I have manipulated her over the decades. Now, we live together again. We can sit in the same room, saying nothing. She knows she must spring to action herself, lest I force her; the fear of my Antagonism lives in her, making her forever restless. A single glance from me is enough to wake up the Antagonist inside her.

    \n This is similar to an idea I've been toying with for a while. Only instead of a game book, said troglodytes would have found books of song lyrics, and compiled them into a holy book of "psalms." Song lyrics get all kinds of weird when you just have them in text in front of you, devoid of musical meaning. I figured their deity would probably be female, due to all the references to 'she' and 'her' in songs.

    I should chase that idea down and compile an appropriate book of psalms.

    As for the game they dig out of my debris:

    There is a certain elite called the Masters. They are probably descended from the weird shaman who could first read the holy text. To these elites, the rest of the populace are assigned as servants - their females are given the honorary title of Maid, their males Butler. Both are given a sacred combination of colours, to wear as a part of their uniforms.

    In the lower castes, female births are prized - one cannot have more than one Butler in a Master's household, and so there are fears about what to do with extra sons. Some Second Sons join the ranks of Maids, for blending into the facade of femaleness is an honorable goal.
