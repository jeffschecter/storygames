\n I don't know if this has ever been discussed here before:

Kevin Smith has never played D&D, but that didn't stop him from trying to DM a homebrewed session, with Jason Mewes, Scott Mosier, and Malcolm Ingram as the players; based on Smith's vague second-hand understanding of what rpg play is like.

The scenario is lewd and childish, as you would expect.

It's funny to see Kevin Smith encounter rudimentary stuff for the first time, like How Dice Work (he hadn't anticipated 3 guys each rolling 1d6-- might result in a tie).

My favorite part is when Mewes suggests rolling to determine Intelligence, and Smith argues that a high number won't make Mewes any smarter.

Actually, all my favorite parts involve inane dice rolls. "Let's roll to make your character stop talking in that voice. You sound like Christian Bale as Batman!"

    \n I'm pretty sure Kevin Smith hasn't come up around here before, or I probably would have noticed.

    This episode is funny, and also slightly painful because Kev does just about EVERYTHING a horrible GM does.

    It's also funny hearing Mewes, the only one who actually plays D&D, actually trying to play D&D, while everybody else is just recording a comedy podcast.

    A couple times on Jay & Bob Get Old, they talk about Mewes painting 40K orc armies while he's a junkie living in hotel rooms and such, which is also pretty surreal.
