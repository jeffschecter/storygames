\n We've kit-bashed The Shadow of Yesterday a little (a very little) to play a superheroes game, set before and during the American Civil War.

So it's August, 1856, and we're on Last Island, in the Gulf of Mexico south of New Orleans, in a hurricane. There's a five-foot storm surge and the entire island is being literally washed away, and we're trying to save 140 people trapped there, including an evil conjure-woman slave, an Irish "aristocrat" whose words are supernaturally convincing, and Chinese twins who are, respectively, a human magnet and a human shaped charge. It's off the chain.

Steve's playing a guy who cannot be physically injured. He gets mauled and just heals back up. He's a mean drunk, a stubborn old dude with the Key of Addiction (prayer). And at one point, he's trying to fight his way through the hurricane to the rapidly-collapsing resort hotel, so that he can locate the evil guy causing the hurricane and convince him to stop. But the storm's in the way! He makes a Athletics check and fails, because he's got wicked penalty dice and the storm is pretty skilled at blowing things down. So Steve Brings Down the Pain and gets in this massive conflict with the hurricane - if I recall correctly he was opposing it parallel-style using Sway, screaming at it and telling it it was going to let him through. It was a cool moment.
