\n As part of the development process for Sunshine (1970s SF + Cold War + cyberpunk, for want of a better description - you can find a more detailed, multi-paragraph description here), I've set up a dedicated feedback forum over at the general CGS forums. In addition, anyone who wants to can get access to the game files (plus some artwork) through Dropbox. And yes, the Alt1977 adverts by Alex Varanese were (and are) a big influence on the game!
\n
\nThe forum aspect ensures that development is fully open and in public, hopefully generating some good feedback. Using Dropbox to disseminate game information will (hopefully) ensure that people interested in the progress of the game design will always be able to access, and be alerted to, new developments, new documents, and new pieces of art.
\n
\nIt's my hope that this very open form of design will give dividends in terms of making a better game and making people feel part of the entire process.
\n
\nYou can find the development forum right here. And if you would like to check out the latest Sunshine files, please don't hesitate to email me at malcolm[at]contestedground[dot]co[dot]uk and I'll add you to the Dropbox folder.
\n
\nCheers
\nMalcolm

    Posted By: vulpinoidI've already got space on my bookshelf for this one.
    \nCheers Michael, that's great to hear! The plan (at the moment) is to produce the game as three digest/A5 sized books, bound up by a 'belly band'. The first (and longest) book will be in full colour and contain all of the system elements. The artwork in this will be artefacts relating to the wider world. The second book will also be in full colour, but will be an in-game visitors guide to Fulcrum City. So, the entire booklet will be adverts and so forth from places and people that exist in-game.For example, the two page map of the city will be presented as a flyer for a local taxi company. The third booklet will be black & white and will have the primer sheet, game creation sheet, character sheets, agenda cards, and some sample game situations.

    That's the plan, at least.\n
