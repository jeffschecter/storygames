    \nVisualcomplexity.com has lots of examples of different network maps done different ways, and usually links to the software used if it's available. If those are too crazy, I stick to graphviz.

    I wish Touchgraph's stuff were freely available.

    There's lots of dead links at Netvis resources, but that might lead to a few good programs with a little poking. But Jason's probably right about laying out by hand. For large networks, software is really good at doing the initial layout with a minimum of crossed lines, which you can then redraw for beauty & effect.

    \n Well, Graphviz is a good tool, albeit with a steep learning curve (OSX has a good gui for it).

    Otherwise, OpenOffice Draw supports connector arrows that stick to the objects: handy for R-maps and flowcharts.

    If you are more graphically inclined, Inkscape also has connectors (albeit a bit less smart) and is (imo) very handy and more comfortable than OOO.
