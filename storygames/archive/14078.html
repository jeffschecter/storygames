\n My minilarp group ripped off Fiasco for our latest game.
\n
\nWe're five people in \xc5rhus, Denmark, who meet about once a month to make and play small larps. We spend one evening designing a game from scratch and one to play it. It's scaled like a parlor larp, but in the immersive scandinavian freeform tradition. We also have a strong narrativist agenda of letting the story flow on its own. And no, it's not Jeepform. Though it shares many traits with that way of playing.
\n
\nWe used a modified version of the setup method and a three act structure instead of the regular two parter. It turned out really well, we managed to have a wonderful hybrid of story game and larp.
\nThe idea of establishing relationships first, characters second is a great shortcut to a quick start and dramatic conflicts. Prep was a couple of hours and actual play time was a little shorter than that, but we had great fun all the way. We told the story of the last chaotic monthly meeting in a doomed commune that had grown apart. Of course it ended badly, we based it on Fiasco!
