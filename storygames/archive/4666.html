    \n I recently took a break from running a long series of connected traditional campaigns with GURPS to take some time to do some shorter less traditional games with as many different people as possible. Just today I played a mob of tiny goblins known collectively as The Grimmikins when my wife ran The Zorcerer of Zo for our weekly game.
    \n
    \nA few weeks ago we gave Elf's a try, and laughed until we were sick at a friend's every other Saturday game. I've never seen someone kill so many opponents by accident and happenstance. Dumb Luck makes me happy conceptually and with it's results.
    \n
    \nI have Carry, PTA, and Don't rest your head on order at my comic/game shop and it's a hard decision as to which one I want to pick up first as I'm busily saving money for AmberCon Northwest next weekend.
    \n
    \nI'm looking forward to running some exciting things at AmberCon. A version of our homegrown system with an emotional mechanic bolted on where a Panzer crew in the last days of the second world war attempt to flee from the eastern front to the western front to surrender to the Americans, thus avoiding execution or gulag. I took the idea from a story in Garth Ennis' War Story, which is a great comic I believe is in a collection now and I highly recommend.
    \n
    \nThe Second game I'm running is going to be a very experimental blending blending of diceless and competetive narrative where the players will attempt to outbid each other with consequences and currency to gain narration of a scene. The plot of the game is that historical figures awaken in a private facility where they've been stolen from time. What really excites me about this is that I don't know what characters anyone is bringing. I have fantasies in my head of Ben Franklin, Ghengis Khan, and Marie Curie coming face to face and trying to figure out what to do.
    \n
    \nLastly I'm running a game I call "The Professionals." There's a briefcase full of McGuffin worth a lot of money and several hired guns are after it. Each character is played by a player and represents a particular action movie star and the stats represent their tropes. It's a second version, the first one was run several years ago using GURPS, and while it was a fun romp, I felt the system held back the action movie style pacing I was looking for. This time around I going with that same home grown system I mentioned above (a little bit similar to pdq I found out today) and heavily relying on stakes to give scope to the conflicts. I think that instead of roll to hit, roll to defend, repeat, that "Ok I want to vault through the transom and beat the hell out of him" vs. "I want to plug him through the door when I hear him in the hall" works a lot better for that fast kinetic feel I'm looking for. I'm really hoping that people get up out of their seats, scream and holler, and do little victory dances.
    \n
    \nAs to podcasts, I think that the Livejournal post I did earlier today can answer that quite well. Here's the link http://ogremarco.livejournal.com/499763.html and feel free to say hello while you're there or even add me if you like.
    \n
    \nThanks for your welcomes,
    \nOgre

    \nPs. go ahead and call me Clint if you must, but most people call me Ogre.
