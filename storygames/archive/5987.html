\n I volunteer at the Penny Arcade Expo in Seattle, WA every year. Last year, there were several story games folks around, and some talk of trying to have more of a presence at the convention this year. There's likely to be a turnstile attendance of over 50,000 people this year, and I think it would be awesome for us to spread the story games love.

Penny Arcade is now putting out a call for people to run gaming-related panels at this year's convention, and I think this would be a great way for interested parties to get involved and maybe get some info about the hobby out to a relatively new audience. If you're interested in running a game design/roleplaying/story games panel this year, please visit the submission page and see if you can get something going!

    Strong indie presence is totally welcome, but don't expect to be the darlings of this show (like at Gen Con).

    I think a 4-day series of panels would be good for the soul. Hell, there's not much else for us to do! Pick up games can happen post panel at the open gaming tables. It worked for us last year.

    And as Ben pointed out, we need to structure our panels for Pax differently than we would for Gen Con (or other cons). We need to figure out how to put D&D in the title.
