I like games where...

Eric Provost
February 2006 edited February 2006 in Story Games

While I was at work today I started thinking about the differences in what kind of games I like vs. what kind of games the guys I play with like. It was mostly an "I'm ok, you're ok" kind of self-reassuring thought, but it reminded me of the "Write the games that you want to play" position.

Naturally, I started thinking a bit too deeply about the games that I wanna play. I thought it would be a fun thing to sit down and write out some bullet statements about what kinds of things I like about gaming, and therefore I'd like to see spring forth in my designs. I figured I'd do it on my blog, but then it occurred to me that this would be a nifty thing to have everyone jump in on.

While it's impossible to be complete, here's a few bullets about games I like, and therefore would like to design.

  • I like it when a game inspires me to do or say something I never would have thought of when I sat down to it.
  • I like a game that invites me to think deeply and be extra creative without actually requiring me to do so all the time.
  • I like to kill things and take their stuff.
  • I love GM prep-work when I know all my prep will be exciting and entertaining. Fruitful would be a better word, I suppose.
  • I like games that give you that satisfied feeling over the short term (a few hours) and over the long term (a few... months?).
  • I like games where supeficial but fun scenes, like killing an army of baddies, will lead to, or fuel, some deeper scenes. More meaningful scenes. Like trying to decide what to do with your POW baddies.
  • I like convention games. Or rather, I like games at conventions that are constructed so that a handful of people who never met each other before can sit down and bang out some fun in a 4-hour block.

That's all that's coming to my noggin' right now. At least, those are the important ones that come to mind right now.

I'd like to hear what you like.
