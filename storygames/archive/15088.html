\n I've been preparing to run Last Train Out Of Warsaw (by Jason Morningstar).

The game says it's best with 6 players, but I think I'll only have 3.

I started wondering, "What if I played one of the characters, instead of just GMing?" That would make it a 4P game.

Any thoughts or experiential advice to offer?

I need to take another look through the book with this idea in mind, which I haven't done yet.

    \n It has been run GMless! It works fine, since the bones behind it are Archipelago II. Think hard about what elements are important thematically and make sure everybody owns one or more of them. You lose the suspense and surprise, which is the entire reason there's a GM.

    More characters is better; with 3 people I think I might have everyone play two, personally.

    \n Yes, all the setting element cards should be owned by someone at the table, and that person should be heavily invested in their element/s.

    The game is a literal railroad, and there are interesting developments in each chapter that are surprising and filled with excitement that a GM can spring on players with color and panache. No GM means this stuff either needs to be openly shared or you need to assign a rotating mini-GM for each scene.

    I would GM for three people who each play two characters myself. One of the two will likely emerge as their focus, and that's fine.

    I would carefully assign each player 2 characters that are not as likely to interact. If they do end up interacting, play half the exchange yourself or ask the player to hand off on of his characters to another player.

    That's just me - I know for a fact the game works fine GMless as well.
