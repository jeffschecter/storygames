In fact, the Dogs usually participate in something like ten to twenty conflicts over the course of a given town. Early conflicts are usually of the find-out-what's-happening variety. Later conflicts are usually of the accept-my-authority-and-judgement variety. It's the GM's job to lay out what's happening in the town, yes, but it's the GM's job to do that in play, the effect of which is to make play last more than one conflict. That's what I'm looking for, here.

    Now, in typing the first paragraph of this comment, I may have stumbled across a possibility. Perhaps, instead of situation creation generating three relatively discrete objectives, it generated one Primary Objective and two or three Supplementary Objectives. In order to accomplish the Primary Objective, the Patriots must complete some of the Intermediate Objectives, with the assumption that the Supplementary Objectives lead into the Primary Objective in some way.

    So you'd get something like this (these are pulled off the auto-generating webpage):

    Primary: The Sons of Liberty must misinform a street gang in New Hampshire to prevent the British from smuggling in a city alderman!
    Supplementary 1. The Sons of Liberty must install a brother mason after the British build Tories!
    Supplementary 2. The Sons of Liberty must spy on a city alderman while the British remove a Liberty Tree!
    Supplementary 3. The Sons of Liberty must smuggle a court while the British install Quakers!

    So the overall goal is to surrepticiously co-opt a street gang in New Hampshire so that they'll screw up the British plans to get this city alderman into town. In order to do that, they've got to plant a spy into some Tory organization that the British have set up, then do some surveillance on the alderman in question while the British are occupied chopping down a Liberty Tree, and they need to get a court into town while the British are doing something with Quakers. A little creative interpretation by the players in set up may create some situation where the British are trying to gain support in a New Hampshire town or city by getting one of their guys elected alderman. They're drumming up local support (the Tory organization) and kicking down opposition (the Tree) and reaching out for allies (the Quakers). The Patriot's goal is to incite a street gang to running the British-sympathizing alderman out of town, and to do so they must gather information from the Tory organization, spy on the alderman himself, and get a thuggish underworld court into town to convince the street gang that the alderman in question is responsible for the murder of Joseph Lightfingers, their fondly-remembered former leader.

    The specifics can be toyed with, but perhaps the Patriots must complete at least two of the Supplementary Objectives in order to attempt the Primary. Completing all three gets you a little bonus, maybe. The important part being that this generates a more complex situation with more objectives that the players can play with and complete, and ties it all together to an overarching "plot."
