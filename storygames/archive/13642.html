\n After over a year of work Of Sky & Sea is now ready! I am really excited to be bringing you all a full board game as an independant production. When I started out on Game Chef '09 I had no idea that OS&S would be what came out. Before this I had never concidered doing a board game and didn't even know producing an indie board game was possible.

Looking back at the last year and change, I have been really lucky. From finding The Game Crafter (http://thegamecrafter.com) or Gary Simpson (OS&S's amazing artist) bringing you all this game would have never happened. Beyond that all of the love and support of my family and friends has been invaluable!

Come check out Of Sky & Sea! \xa0https://www.thegamecrafter.com/games/of-sky--sea

And check out At the Table Games blog. http://atthetablegames.wordpress.com

    \n
    \n The first copy of OS&S was purchased and recieved by my wife's best friend Amanda. She recieved her copy within a week of ordering it which made me very happy! She is bringing it by on Saturday so I can sign it and we can get a couple of play-thu's in. Amanda, my mother and father-in-law have never played before so it should be fun getting them into it. This will also be my first game with the new board (my original version with 10 spaces is now horribly marked up from the changes I made. And it will be my first chance to play with the finalized rat token (no real gameplay difference but it will be nice to see the whole set.

    A word to collecters: after the March 17th deadline I am going to be makeing some changes to the componentry that comes in OS&S. None of the changes will alter gameplay but I will be adding in cards with the OS&S logo and website that fans can hand out to interested friends and I will be adding the website to the bottom of the rules so that players will know where to send friends looking for the game. I mention this becouse a co-worker wanted to make sure he got his hands on a "unaltered" first edition.

    \n\nFebruary 2011 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n Well I am very pleased with the components that came with the game and with how fast they got it out the door. The one thing I wish they would do to help me as a designer out is to ask the customers if they wish to receive emails. I have no way to contact my customers to see how their experience was. I would also like to be able to offer discount codes but there is no way for me to do this. The last thing I am hoping for some day soon is full color boxes that would allow me to sell the game in some of my local game stores.
