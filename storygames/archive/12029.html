    Posted By: Jason MorningstarBarely related - if anyone can briefly summarize the core "AP sucks" argument for reference, that'd also be great.
    \nWell my take on it is two-fold:
    1) Actual play reports as theory tools are bad - because they are pretty poor translations of what is happening during, well, actual play. There is a strong tendency, both on part of the writer and the reader, to re-envision the dynamics of play into something more literary (whether it is the "story of the session" or the "lesson learned", or what not).
    2) Actual play reports are hazardous advertisement - because they work significantly better for some games rather than others.

    This is not to say that actual play reports can't be enjoyable, just that they seem to be taken far too seriously.

    The preceding view is offered for comparison purposes only. If you wish to discuss it please take it to another thread. Thank you.

    - Mendel

    \n There's probably a side topic here on the intents behind a bite-sized AP thread.

    The obvious one is to lower the threshold of investment, on the sides of both the posters as well as of any prospective readers, so that we get more instances of participation. But maybe a tertiary intent (actual or only perceived, it doesn't matter), is "all you cats who want to simply effuse on your gaming moments of awesome, you do that *here* so we can easily avoid you. You're not expected here to provide constructive observations on the mechanics or methods of play."

    And now then the full-sized APs have conversely a higher bar to reach and are therefore less attractive to provide for some; we either lack the confidence we can meet the higher bar, or we want to mostly just geek out at length but feel that the audience for our contribution is so narrow it does belong more to the provinces of blogs.
