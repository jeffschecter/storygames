    \n I'm actually not sure I agree with Tod about the, as you put it, AW singularity. I think we're seeing a lot less direct hacks of the game that try to use the same exact frame, but I also think that a lot of the language of AW is finding itself integrated into even unrelated projects - namely, clearly stated fictional triggers and encoded Principles of play that are treated as rules rather than advice. These ideas weren't unique or invented by PbtA, but are hallmarks of the system that I think we're seeing show up in other games even as we move away from straight hacks.
    This thread might not be the best place for further discussion of that, but I wanted to bring up a different side. I totally agree with the rest of his observations.

    I know that I've seen a bit of a push for games that can be played in a smaller amount of time than a standard 3/4 hour session, from Lady Windimere's Fan in minutes to 2-hour games.

    There were a couple surges from contests like Game Chef and Golden Cobra too, bringing in unconventional presentations of games and more freeform stuff. Whether this stuff counts depends on your definition of "released."

    As for a list of stuff from 2014, that's really hard. Do you count a released public beta as a release? Simple posted game stuff that's complete, like Tod's Being Eero thing here? Only games officially published through avenues like DrivethruRPG or the like?
    Good starting points would be DriveThruRPG, or perhaps looking through the successful 2013 RPG Kickstarters and seeing what's been delivered in 2014.

    \n Oh my goodness please stop using Jeepform to refer to anything but, you know, Jeepform. It refers to games designed by an artistic collective called Vi \xe5ker jeep, most of whom live in Sweden, and all of whom have moved on.

    American Freeform is a new term for short form, semi-live games emerging from the North American scene that are influenced by, among other things, Nordic larp, Danish scenario play, focused tabletop roleplaying and American parlor larp. I don't know if it will catch on as a term or not but it is definitely a thing.

    Long cons seem to be happening with more or less success at larger regional cons (again, in North America). Outside that context I don't see them going on.
