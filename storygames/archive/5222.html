    Posted By: HituroThe main argument against it is that the game itself is only about 40 pages long as it is, so 2 pages is a fairly significant investment.
    *blink blink*

    OK, I'll have to take your word for it, because most POD and small press printers I've checked run about 2\xa2 to 8\xa2 per page (i.e. a nickel to a quarter in MSRP increase). I figure most purveyors of small-book, indie games would consider original design notes to be well worth an extra coin at the register.

    Furthermore, if you are looking at more "serious" web press runs, then you haven't hit a full signature at 40 pages yet anyway (signatures are usually multiples of 16). 48 is the closest multiple to 40, so you'd likely end up with 6 blank pages even after you add the 2 extra. (Note: some web presses have 8-page signatures, in which case adding two pages would, in fact, overrun a 5-signature book setup. Thus, as ever, YMMV....)

    Anyhow, glad you're going with consensus on it. Your readers will thank you (so long as you... well, to be frank... do a better job of scanning them and adjusting their contrast and levels to make them more legible).

    \n Ahh sorry, I didn't mean investement in terms of cost, I meant in terms of the proportion of the book devoted to it. I'm looking at it from the point of view of the reader, who will be thinking, what did I get when I paid for this game, did it devote space to things I wanted, like an index, or examples of play, or sample characters, or did it waste it on fluff like blank pages, and adds and so forth.

    \n Having scanned a fair bit of paper in my time, I can assure you that tweaking in PShop or GIMP can virtually eliminate paper texture, wrinkles, and even the rules (blue lines). Then again, you might like the rough-and-ready look, if it fits other book aesthetics and doesn't look "tacked on."

    Or, you could always just transcribe them into text, including strike-outs if you want to preserve the organic feel without carrying along the pencil mess. It's not like you have doodles or sketches or tables or graphs in there, to give it additional cache as "the original artifact." It's just headings and paragraphs and some strike-out.

    Good luck with whatever course you take!
