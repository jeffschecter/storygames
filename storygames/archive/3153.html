    \n The Ransom Model has worked well for Greg Stolze and I in the past, but its success was built upon his name recognition and some heavy marketing through forum sigs, LJ posts, a promotional web site, and art previews. The art, as it turns out, was the biggest money-maker. I'd recommend posting as much distinctive art as possible, preferably in a central location like a blog. Greg fosters community involvement through mailing lists. Um... Oh! Sending out review copies to podcasts and gaming blogs might also help, but I've never done it personally.

    \n Reviews/podcasts are handy, both for the direct promo when it comes out and as an ongoing resource for further promo, e.g. giving nice quotes from known figures like Ken Hite and a link so potential customers can read stuff that'll incline them toward buying. The downside of reviews is that they're substantially outside the publisher's control - you don't know if or when they'll appear (though some reviewers - including Chris! - are more dependable than others).

    \n While I don't have any numbers to go with for this (since I haven't started pre-ordering quite yet), Fred Hicks heavily suggested doing pre-release previews to generate buzz & show people what they'll be able to order in the future. The first preview post on our blogs generated a decent number of comments, though that's dwindled down in later posts. I can't tell if that means people are still interested but just aren't voicing it.
