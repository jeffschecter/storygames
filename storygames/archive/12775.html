    \n Still reading! Halfway in.

    What I like about it is, well, that it's about me and my friends and the worst players I've ever met, when I was... probably fourteen. I still remember my female half-elf Leetah Malnuthion, with giant boobs, having sex with my best friend's character Akira Bandylegs, a dwarf. And I remember people having their characters attacking everything in sight, including furniture (this happens in the book). And the DM letting the fighty character survive through all odds, just to keep the player in the game. And playing with the weirdest people just because they were gamers, because that was the only way to play. (I'm sure other people felt the same way about me, though). Etc.

    The book has been criticized for portraying gamers as dysfunctional and socially inept. Well, when I was in my teens, that was my world. And probably a lot of other kids' world, too; I don't know many people who remember their early teens as a smooth ride of social mastery and joy. The things we do... man.

    Of course, in this book, everything is condensed and pushed to the limit. Still, most of the (real-world) characters are believable... except maybe the DM with his cardboard-dragon wheelchair.
