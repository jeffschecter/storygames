\n Basically, #f1re is a twitterized version of the game [f1 re: esc] ("fire escape")
I wrote a couple of years back.

#f1re is a twitter game. 1) A tweet starts it. 2) There are 2 ways to answer \u2013 play or cheer.
#f1re is a twitter game. Cheer by replying to a #f1re tweet with \u201cRT @user Winner! #f1re\u201d
#f1re is a twitter game. Play by answering a tweet, and make a challenge in the same tweet.
#f1re is a twitter game. Include #f1re in your tweet when playing or cheering.
#f1re is a twitter game. You score points for cheers you get, until I call it.

I'm curious what kind of things cmoe out from such a tiny structure. So I want to try it out!

You can find it by searching for #f1re on twitter.
