\n Hey, you've heard of GM emulators, but what if you want to GM and don't have any players? And what if you want a real GMing challenge, to test your mad GM skills? I've got you covered:
(And before you think I'm a hater, I'd like to point out you can totally make me with this.)

Name:
Look:
greasy hair, bald spot, unfashionable hair, shaved head, unkempt hair
overweight body, skinny body, skinny-with-a-belly body
sad eyes, small eyes, glasses
goatee, soul patch, five o'clock shadow, mutton chops, baby face, acne face

Stats:
Assign +2, +1, 0, and -1 to these four traits:
Uncool, Sharp, Hot, Weird

The moves:
When you ask them what they do, roll +Sharp.
On a 10+, choose 3
On a 7-9, choose 1
- they recognize they're being railroaded
- they go off the rails
- they stab their buddy
- they stab a key ally NPC
- they shrug and say "I don't know"
- they look up from their smartphone and ask "What? Where am I again?"
On a miss, they do something cool that doesn't fuck up your prep

When something bad happens to their character, roll +Uncool.
On a 10+, choose 3
On a 7-9, choose 1
- they whine like a dog. they take +1 forward
- they try to talk you out of it (see below)
- they 'go aggro' - change your mind or they quit
- your relationship with them takes 1 harm
On a miss, they're cool with it.

When they try to talk you into or out of something, roll +Hot.
On a 10+, choose 3.
On a 7-9, choose 1.
If you do it, your relationship with them heals 1 harm
If you don't do it, they get +1 XP.
If you don't do it, your relationship with them takes 1 harm
If you don't do it, you take +1 forward.
On a miss, you get to talk them into something.

When their character is not in the scene, roll +Weird.
On a 10+, choose 2
On a 7-9, choose 1
- they leave the room to get a snack or go to the bathroom or something
- they try talking you into cutting back to them (see above)
- they engage (real or imagined) narrative authority mechanics to get attention and fuck up your story. If your game has real narrative authority mechanics, suck it up and take it. If they're imagined, this counts as talking you into something. (see above)
- they talk about how much more fun they'd be having if they were playing a different system
On a miss, they pay attention and contribute welcome insights.
