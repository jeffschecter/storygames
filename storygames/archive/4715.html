    Posted By: shreyasSo, dude, what is the goal of your games acquisition here? Are you looking to justown more stuff,in which case I think I know where to point you, or are you looking toplay more games?Because if it's the latter then I think you have enough on your plate already.
    \nIt's consumption, but not mindlessly so.

    As someone who is looking at gaming across the entire spectrum, I need input from time to time to see where folks are going, mechanically, so I can test my model. It's all for SCIENCE!

    If my only desire was to play more games, I'd just be putting together my own systems, since I can do that readily. What I want is to see what other people are doing, and looking around the edges. (As such, SotC is off the list because I've already analyzed FATE - unless there are substantial differences from the core.)

    Lastly, I don't see the problem in spending good money to support a community that I derive a lot of value from.

    \n I'd like to recommend:\n
      \n
    • Full Light, Full Steam because it's well conceived and well written, with very clear explanations and lots of good ideas for creating stories that fit the play group and are diametrically opposed to railroading.
      • \n
    • The Committee for the Exploration of Mysteries, another well-written game. It's GM-less, and slightly competitive but story comes first. It's likeBaron Munchausen meets Polaris.
      • \n
    • Wilderness of Mirrors, a light-weight engine to create spy thrillers in the Mission Impossible style (original TV show) or even Ocean's Eleven-style heist stories. It can also be used as an overlay on another system.
