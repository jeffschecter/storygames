\n Holy crap, it's the battle of Shiloh!

The player characters are pressed into service with the 17th Kentucky, fighting on the Union's left wing, up against the Tennessee river!!

ZOMG zombies!

Good times. We gave each unit two abilities (Fight and Morale) and played each "turn" in the battle as an hour, and had roleplaying bits in between them. Once the zombies showed up the tactical battle was pretty much over - night was falling. One cool moment - superhero Hamish Bean, a character with the ability to duplicate himself, took up the cannons abandoned by the Confederate Hodgson's Battery and fought off one zombie horde by himselves.

    \n Well blue and grey, Union and Confederate. PCs had their own smaller counters and moved independently. We more or less hacked together a simple set of wargame rules and replayed the first day of the battle of Shiloh. It was pretty cool to have the actual units in their actual positions, and departing from there.

    Two of the PCs were from Kentucky, and there were Kentucky units fighting on both sides. At one point the two rival brigades met and joined forces against some zombies, which was pretty fun.

    The roleplaying centered around a couple of threads - there were some bad guys who had manipulated both sides into position for a needlessly bloody battle, because they were harvesting souls for some later evil purpose. These bad guys had strong ties from previous encounters to the PCs in various ways. So that was going on, and there was also some nonsense with another superhero named Electa, one of the "fatal five" of the Order of the Eastern Star and the paramour of another PC. Somebody blew up the soul-harvesting machine in the middle of the battle, causing confused souls to return to their bodies and try to fight on. It was all very Jerry Bruckheimer.

    EDIT: Thanks, Steve. There were also simple rules for movement and supporting fire and so forth.
