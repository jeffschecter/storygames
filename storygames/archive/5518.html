    \n It's got a whole chapter on GM advice. The first part is about different ways to run the game and create Situation as a GM. In it, it goes into structured ways for quick GM-prep, or to run with practically no GM-prep. The advice is basically "Cliche is okay" and "Keep the Fate Points flowing by hitting aspects".

    There are ways for players to generate Situation here, too. Characters with high Knowledge skills will help the GM fill in the blanks. And of course they'll be hitting aspects, too.

    \n Situation's sourced from the players who show up to the table that night; the advice hammers on the idea of using the stuff on their character sheet to set up your situation. Then it hits you with "How to keep things pulpy" which ... well, I think it's more than just "cliches are good", but it starts there and runs through a bunch of notions how to keep things hopping.
