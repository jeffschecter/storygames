\n What adjectives do you use the most when you're trying to describe a scene to your players? it's easy to say "theres a hideous displacer beast there", or "a dark corridor extends in front of you" but to frame an scene at it's best you usually need to describe sounds, smells and emotions more than just the actual things/beings that are the source of those. Which words you felt had the best impact on your players?

    \n When I GM, my go-to description is "as if".

    There are small grooves in the rock, as if something really sharp or really heavy was dragged across it.

    There's an uneven hole in the door, as if something was yanked out of it, or thrust through it with incredible force.

    The torch light isn't reaching you, as if there was something dark in between you and it, covering or sucking up the light.

    I know these aren't adjectives, but I've had more luck framing scenes from concrete environmental details than from "grisly" or "harrowing".
