\n Ok, so I'm not ashamed to say that whenever I see a great custom move posted online I want to drop what I'm doing and go play AW right then, just so that I can use it. Unfortunately, I simply don't see enough custom moves posted online to feed my hunger.

So, I'm proposing a game. Here's how it works, make a post in which you name one or more custom moves. No mechanics, just a name. Then, look to a post above yours, pick a named move, and write mechanics for it. (Don't worry I won't be totally embarrassed if no one plays - I will, however, be totally thrilled if we make a cool list of cool custom moves to use)

Lets seed the pot (don't leave me hanging):

When you are abruptly awoken from stasis, roll+...
When you make a play for first base, roll+...
When you try to get off the junk, roll+...
When you try to speak their language, roll+...

And, go...

      \nWhen you dream... seems like a minigame to me, so that's what I did. It breaks the 'line' because I think the experience of having a dream in a game should not.make.sense the way the rest of the game makes sense.

      When you dream... you're not just sleeping. That's weird.

      Ask the other players to each give you an impression of something from before the Apocalypse (basically a visual image with an emotion like 'A sad horse with a tophat'). Each of the players says what their impression was, writes it down, and doesn't repeat it. With your eyes closed and your head leaned back or laid down, tell the table about your dream. Other players, give them a thumbs up if your element is included, MC give a thumbs up if it sounds 'dreamlike' to you. When the dream is done open your eyes and look at the other players. If everybody is giving you a thumbs up, mark experience. If not, you open your brain to the psychic maelstrom.
