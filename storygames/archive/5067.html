    \n The only session of Beast Hunters I played involved my character digging into a giant monsters back and trying to rip out the spine, and another character dove into the beasts mouth and fought back out through the chest or throat.

    The setting was a primitive tribe o Beast Hunters, but we sought out the Fire People, something a player made up on the spot, and they were essentially Spaniards with guns, so I'm not sure if it was the official setting or not.

    It turned out very Exalted. Or rather, it turned out to be big action with over the top events, which is something that Exalted tries for as well.

    Outside of the tattoos (which we didn't use) I don't think adhering to the setting is a big deal. An isolated culture where beast hunting is important seems to be important. But I could be wrong.

    I think you'll be fine with Exalted. I think you'll be better if you change the setting to whatever makes you go "yeah, awesome!" whether it's Exalted or making up something new (heck, make it up as you play!)
