\n hello story-folks.
\ni'm looking for essays and articles which are concise and clear, not meandering threads in which ideas originally rose.
\nif you could post links directly to them here, i think that would be very useful for all of us new folks.
\nperhaps someone would like to host these links so future newcomers can find them with ease?
\nto begin, i'll include some essays that M. J. Young wrote in the Places to Go , People To Be e-zine.
\n
\nthis text is from M. J.'s email to me:
\n
\nIf you found the Applied Theory article helpful, you might find the three-article series at Places to Go, People to Be useful as well. The editors there asked for someone to summarize the essential theory from The Forge discussions, and as I had previously done a series for them (on Law and Enforcement in Imaginary Realms) it was simple to arrange to do this one. The series title is Theory 101, and there are three subtitles in it:
\n
\nhttp://ptgptb.org/0026/theory101-01.html System and the Shared Imagined Space explains how all role playing games work fundamentally, as social contracts in which we implicitly agree concerning who is permitted to say what. It talks about how to adjust those rules for different approaches to play.
\n
\nhttp://ptgptb.org/0027/theory101-02.html The Impossible Thing Before Breakfast is about the tension between "the referee controls the story" and "the players control the main characters in the story". It details major approaches to referee styles, and how these fit into credibility.
\n
\nhttp://ptgptb.org/0028/theory101-03.html Creative Agenda explains gamism, narrativism, and simulationism, why they are incompatible, and what drives each.
\n
\n-----
\nthank you all. other thoughts on this topic would be welcome as well.
