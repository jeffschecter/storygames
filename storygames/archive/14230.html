Will you concentrate on the lever, the tilt or the elbow the face of an opponent? So the player interpretation becomes the dominant aspect, and games can end wildly differently in terms of who is accused of cheating.

    \n Just ran into a couple of players playing a pickup game of HHH in my local lounge room. And they invited me to play which I happily agreed to in the interests of an AP example.* I played "green" in this case as the other players had established colours and I didn't want to go "pink" and risk being called a "girl".
    \n
    \nMy estimate of 30 seconds was about right. 20 to load and 10 to eat them all. Loading time could easily be used in other discussion or running another RPG, but eating time was a little hectic. The third scoring stage was much slower, but this was mostly to do with maths ability which an experienced group could reduce.
    \n
    \nNote that these players were not playing standard 20 white ball. They use 16 white (the others had "gone missing") and called them "goodies" and 8 metal ball bearings from another game that they called "baddies". This is an interesting alternative as although the players didn't distinguish during the eating phase, the game could be played more carefully and slowly if say white needed to be maximized and metal minimized.
    \n
    \nNote that 24 balls seems an upper maximum for loading capacity, and in a three player game I was at a significant disadvantage in being the oldest player of having to load two "magazines". I recommend limiting magazine use to one per player and therefore 6 balls per player for fairness.
    \n
    \nI can hear that the blaming phase is starting so I better get out and intercede. Any more HHH AP?
