\n So, I really don't use chat all that much anymore. However, other folks do. Right now, the best we've got is the little "Start Chat!" applet for Yaplet on the left. The Yaplet app has a limitation of 12 people, though (but I don't think it's ever really hit that limit before).

Anyway, my goal for a while has been to find a chat tool with the following:
1) Extremely easy to join for new people. ie Browser-based.
2) Possibility of moderation.
3) Must be persistent (like, I could start a Yahoo IM Chat, which would rock and meets all my criteria... but the chat ends when the last person leaves. And you have to be invited, you can't just join whenever)

and hopefully having the following:
1) Ease of communication/use
2) Allows guest accounts so that not every person has to register to participate

My experiments have shown that:
* chat/chatroom technology really hasn't improved all that much in the last 8 years.
-- IRC is still king.
-- Though at least there are better web-based IRC clients.
* I like Campfire
-- But campfire is really a product for businesses, and most of its features, while cool, are overkill
-- And it costs a lot
-- But I'd easily pay a lot of cash to have a codebase that looked/felt like Campfire that I could install on my own server.

So right now, I'm thinking of the following:
1) Registering an IRC channel somewhere (if not just outright setting up my own irc server), and linking to a good graphical IRC webclient like Mibbit (http://www.mibbit.com/).
2) Collecting the folks who actually get a lot out of a chat feature, and asking them to chip in to collectively buy a Campfire account
3) Leaving stuff as it is (the Yaplet link on the left), cause it's free, easy and good enough for now. In another 1-2 years, check back to see if there's anything new and cool out there to take its place (like the Campfire source, or the equivalent).

For now, if people want to play around with Campfire, here it is:
http://story-games.campfirenow.com/c6461 . Only 4 people can join at once, though, so if you play around for a bit, make sure to Leave so that others have a chance to poke around.

Anyone have insight into other products, services, etc that meet the criteria above?

Thanks!
-Andy

    \n I have no idea whether it would stand up to the torrent of users that is storygames, but I've had good experiences with PFC, an extension for Vanilla that adds a pfhfreechat chatroom, complete with a Vanilla database support. (So that people don't have to enter their name again when entering the chatroom. They are automatically connected using their normal SG account.)

    I've installed in on Story-Games Austria and so far, it has worked well, although there have never been more then ... 3.5 Users online at a time. I've also not looked deeper into what features (If any) PFC offers for moderation and stuff. (I know that the admin of the forum is also automatically a moderator in PC, but that's about it.)

    Might be worth looking into, as it's free and, from where I'm standing, quite good.
