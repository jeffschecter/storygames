\n Got around to finally playing Tremulus this weekend.

Overall I liked it but there are a number of let-downs with the system, including most playbook moves are rather mundane and don't inspire me to play that class like AW-original and great hacks of AW like Dungeon World and Monsterhearts. Perhaps the biggest let down are lore moves which are, for the most part, very dull and matter of fact. Given this takes place in a supernatural world of cosmic horror, Lore moves -- the thematic concept of which is made of awesome -- are totally lacking.

Has anyone put forth alternative ideas for Lore moves? I've looked through all the Tremulus threads here on SG already. (Also, I would totally bring over Graham W's Insane Moves from his own purist Lovecraft hack.)

    \n Poke Around allows only discovery minor items or major items that are up to the Keeper. I was envisioning Lore moves as allowing more drastic inclusions/changes to the narrative.

    I think the problem with Tremulous is a distinct issue with gluing Lovecraftian style narratives to the type of narrative that *World style mechanics leans towards. Lovecraftian focus tends towards mundane characters caught up in something beyond their understanding. In AW, the characters in AW are unique badasses and larger than life, and all NPCs are put in crosshairs. In Lovecraft, anyone and everyone unfortunate enough to get caught up in the horrors and madness is put in the crosshairs including the characters. The authors of Tremulous dealt with this conflict by making the Playbooks more like simple stereotypes and all the player moves generally mundane. If you read the section on Advancement, it's assumed that most characters would just die or go mad and players would just grab another playbook. The playbooks are strongly deemphasized as characters are seen as disposable. It's also telling that the most innovative part of Tremulous is the Playsets, which makes the town and the horrors more richly detailed and important than the characters.

    In order to deal with this conflict between Lovecraftian storytelling and the type *World leans towards, I think Tremulous needs a more profound shift either in the type of narrative or the mechanics. If you are determined to tell a story where the Characters are nothing more than ants to be stepped on by the elder gods, then the Playbooks should be drastically simplified where Stats are simplified and you get one unique Move and that's it, or gotten rid of completely (more in line with Cthulhu Dark or Dread). If you are determined to use Playbooks the way AW does, then the Characters deserve to be more badasses with more interesting moves and more ability to affect the narrative and win the day (but not without a cost of course).

    \n Player moves:
    - go somewhere you shouldn't
    - do something you shouldn't
    - trust someone you shouldn't
    - split up
    - make a lot of noise

    Monster move:
    When face to face with the horror of the unknown, say which thing you hope you won't do:
    * get murdered
    * flee
    * freeze
    Roll+survival, on a 10+ great, you don't do that one; pick another that you do instead.
    On a 7-9, too bad, you do that one, but you get to say how.
    On a miss, nope, it's that one and the GM says how.

    \n WEAK SAUCE WALTON

    Professor
    Dangerous-1, Lore+2, Resolute+1.
    Professor Moves:
    * Impress with credentials.
    * Produce a theory.
    * Research a subject.

    Detective
    Dangerous+1, Lore-1, Resolute+2
    Detective Moves:
    * Cut a deal.
    * Find a clue.
    * Pull a gun.

    Cultist
    Dangerous+2, Lore+1, Resolute-1
    Cultist Moves:
    * Intimidate with threats.
    * Recite cult teachings.
    * Summon fellow cultists.

    When you are attacked by a monster, roll+dangerous. On a 10+, you are unharmed. On a 7-9, you can flee or you can stay and suffer the consequences. On a miss, you fall victim.

    When you face the horror, roll+resolute. On a 10+, you are shaken but resolved to act! Take +1 forward to react. On a 7-9, you lose control for an instant before you come to your senses. On a miss, you go insane until the horror passes, after which you are permanently scarred by mental disorder.

    When you perform an occult ritual, roll+lore. On a 10+, it works! On a 7-9, it works but requires a sacrifice. On a miss, it makes things worse.

    ---

    Mysteries are kind of like a reverse of threat countdowns, so it's not that strange of an idea.

    And I agree that the basic moves in tremulus are a misstep, although "successful" is a matter of who is setting the goalposts.
