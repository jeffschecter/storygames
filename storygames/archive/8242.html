Using Name Cards

\n
\n
\n No not, "my name is..." tags worn by the players (though I've done that).

Instead at tonight's session of AFMBE, I noticed a little quirk of how the GM was improvising. As is good habit, she kept a list of pre-made NPC names for use on the fly. She also had a notebook with blank pages, and a stack of index cards for passing notes. I watched her, when an NPC became noteworthy, flip to the name list, make a choice, then flip to a blank page and record said name with lines of information. And it occurred to me how, when GM'ing myself that step between the name list and making notes had inordinately detrimental effect on my pace. Then I looked at the index cards and had an idea.

Rather than a list of names, why not instead write the names, one each, on the top of otherwise blank index cards? When an NPC needs to be fleshed out, grab a name-card from the stack and write any notes on that. Build up the stack of active names as play progresses, and spare the tendency of scribbled descriptions of town guards filling up the margins.

    \n You could even take it a step further, write a few personality traits on each name card ahead of time. You can always cross them out if a name really fits an NPC but a pre-written personality trait just doesn't fit. On the other hand, you have the opportunity to wind up with a personality trait that might be a bit unexpected.

    I like the idea of coding the names by culture using color of cards. If you could get cards in two shades of each color, you could also code gender while still making it easy.
