    \n I'm glad everyone seemed to enjoy the passport. I helped Fred put it together, designing the logo and helping to recruit booths outside the post-Forge community, but he definitely deserves most of the credit. I hope somebody, somewhere counted how many passports were actually turned in fully stamped, because that would be a nice figure to know.

    Next year, I have a few thoughts for making it work better.

    1. Do a better job of reaching out to non-Forge indie games people earlier. I think we might have been able to get several other booths involved (Pagan, Apophis, etc.) given more time. Key20 and IPR-affiliated companies is certainly a start, but we could definitely make it a more widespread thing. We could also have done a better job of promoting other artists, like Keith, who've done work on indie games.

    2. Allow people to keep their stamped passports afterwards, since it's basically a list of game companies that they could buy from or learn more about after the con is over. Maybe there's just a inserted half-sheet where they fill out their contact info and have a booth member sign off that they've gotten all the stamps. So they stick that half-sheet in the box and keep the stamped passport.

    3. Figure out a better way to present the map, especially if we're going to have more booths involved next year.

    4. Like John said, see if we can get it mentioned in the con booklet, as an activity con visitors can take part in.

    5. Like Justin said, add in a stamp for attending one of the various indie games seminars featuring Luke, Jared, Paul Tevis, Sons of Kryos, etc.

    \n See, I had a pretty different experience with it, and that's probably because of how I used it. When I had random Joe/Josephine come by, I asked them if they had a passport. If they didn't, I offered them one, explained it, told them what they might get, and directed them to the various booths. I sent many people away with passports and I know some of them at least went on to check out some of the other booths. So I wasn't waiting for people to come with passports, I was giving them out.

    Some of the people who came to get our stamp were booth participants, but far more people who walked away with our stamp did so not having brought the passport in the first place.

    The passport was a great idea, and not only because I won first pick.
