    \n Top 3

    - Adventurers just out of prison, looking for redemption! : I'm a sucker for a good redemption story. I like this one better than "Evil characters realize they are the "bad guys" and turn on their own!" because in the first case, the adventurers are not necessarily evil. And in in the second case, the trigger is pretty unclear : how and why did they realize they were the bad guys.

    - Adventurers just sent to prison, fighting to survive! : I like it as a social commentary on the carceral system. Part of me would like to change "adventurers" to simply "convicts" and set it to modern times. But I could also see it as the way the game above would start. It could even be part of character generation! We see why the character was sent to prison, and how he fared, and then we play through the release and his rehabilitation into society (or redemption).

    - Gunslinger Paladins returning home to lay judgment, find peace, and confront childhood rivals! : I like this one, it focuses on the end of the Hero's Journey, the return home. You find out if you were right to leave, how much you've changed and others have changed, resolve any regrets. It's very interesting. Also, I'm a fan of Dogs in the Vineyard, so this is a no-brainer.


    - Heroes who take the powers of the monsters they defeat by consuming their flesh... but can you defeat a dragon? - I'm a vegetarian, for one thing. And this takes it a bit far : kill sentient beings to steal their strength is really not appropriate behavior. Of course, I recall the Lunars in Exalted do pretty much that, and I used to think it was neat. I don't know. Plus, it seems the big conflict is going to be : can we defeat a dragon. It doesn't grab my interest that much.

    - Monsters hoping to becoming adventurers aren't accepted into the prestigious school of adventure excellency due to racism\u2026 now it's time to show them why they call you monsters! : ...yeah, that's pretty much the opposite response to racism I'd like to play. Rather that showing why they call me a monster, I'd like to show them who the real monsters are. I've had a dream!

    - Swashbuckling Rogues competing to tell the best war story over copious amounts of alcohol... play out these epic battles in flashback as each character tells their story! : Not bad. In fact, I first put this in my top 3. Then I though, this is just like Baron Munchaussen, but more limited. Or maybe I'm not seeing it's potential. Three Musketeers is awesome, but far less over-the-top than many other works of literature.

    \n My top three:
    * A family of grief stricken, rage-full dwarves on a quest to take back their home. This one has drama and action stamped all over it.

    * Adventurers just out of prison, looking for redemption. Same thing: a game that lets you bash heads and break skulls, with some sweet interludes of "why are we doing all this stuff? what for?" followed by more skull-smashing.

It's not that I don't really like it, it's just that based on that single sentence, I'm not really sure what the game is about.
