\n Justin and Adam both sent their Duel PDFs to me, two Film Noir games.

Then I had a brilliant idea: they should make the two games, together, into an ashcan for GenCon! Two games in one! And I nearly mailed them with my brilliant idea.

But then I thought: maybe Gen Con is too soon. I wouldn't recommend them to publish their game at Gen Con, would I? Just like I wouldn't encourage any Game Chef entrants to publish at Gen Con. It's just too soon to playtest and redraft it thoroughly.

(Obviously, if you're a brilliant game designer, you're probably be an exception. But my rule of thumb, as a consumer, is that I'm suspicious about buying a game at GenCon that was in that year's Game Chef.)

And I remembered the Ashcan idea: 90% chance of awesome. For your game to have a 90% chance of producing awesome play, you'd have to do a bit of playtesting and revision, surely?

So what's the rule of thumb? Generally, is it there enough time to turn a Game Chef game into an ashcan of sufficient standard to sell at GenCon? What about a game that's just been written? Is it too late?

If I've missed something important in previous ashcan threads, do point me to it. I'm mainly asking to clarify my concept of how finished an ashcan should be.

Graham
