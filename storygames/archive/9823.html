\n Me and a couple of friends played our first game of Lacuna when we were up in Vegas for the weekend. It was tons of fun, and I thought it was the highlight of the trip (also the cheapest thing there). I got inspired, so I wrote it up as a short story which I've posted here: [CLASSIFIED]

I had read through the book twice, but otherwise did no prep. The static rules drove the game at a satisfying pace (helped by the fact that I had the static gaining conditions: mention the spidermen, mention the hostile personality's name, and argue with the team leader, and that happened a lot because one player saw the cover illustration and was convinced the spidermen were behind everything, and another player kept arguing with the team leader's decisions).

Afterwords, one of the players compared it to a Neil Gaiman novel, which I thought was interesting. Another player described it as the most interesting and confusing game session he'd ever played in.

Needless to say, I recommend the game to anyone interested in horror, thrill, or fun RPGs.
