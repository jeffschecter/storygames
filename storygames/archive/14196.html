\n I did get my Human Contact book but not in time for this semesters game. I was thinking how to use Shock both as a teaching tool and for creating cool sci-fi stories. So I did an experiment:

I gave the group a Shock.

Shock: Travel to Mars and establish a Colony

I then asked them for the Issues and Owners. Then we started with the Minutiae. In order to tie into the astronomy unit I asked the group the following questions:
What are seasons like on Mars, length of year, travel time to mars, what stars can you see, how do you keep time, info on the planet, and so on.
This information about Mars then gets worked into the Scenes, Issues, Protag/Antag. Basically we are using Shock as a way to demonstrate knowledge, look at integration of new information, and push social issues related to science


Part 2: Issues, Tagonists, Minutiae, first round of scenes

The Issues are really cool and the Tagonists are set at cross purposes!

    \n A teacher friend of mine did something with Shock: and middle school pupils (7th graders, I think). They didn't play the game, but she explained them the shock/issues thing, had groups of children propose shocks, each of them pick an issue and figure out a protagonist, and then had them write short stories from that. I hope she'll muster the time to write down a proper "actual play" report of the experiment sometimes.
