    \nMaeve Shaw seems pretty solid. Her traits and tags seem like they'll come up a lot and be useful in plenty of Lady Blackbird-ish situations, and her Keys tie her into the setting a bit, with a relationship with Vance. Plus the sky squid thing is cool.

    Deangelo seems like a problem as a character. Points for a clockwork doctor, but his traits and tags seem very focused; like he'd be only useful in figuring stuff out.

    His doctor skills probably won't come up much, since the nature of the game mechanics mean that the other PCs don't really need "healing" like they might in some games. And his Secret that lets him use random things as deadly weapons a) doesn't seem in keeping with his "wise doctor" shtick, and b) may not be that useful, since none of his traits and tags can be used for fighting.

    Also, unlike Maeve, his Keys don't tie him to any of the existing characters.

    Maybe he'd work better if he was part of Lady Blackbird's entourage, and had a couple extra skillsets, like interfacing directly with Empire hardware (a la R2D2), or mimicking and decrypting Empire codes and signals.

    Just some thoughts; it's a flexible enough game that the character would probably be fine in the hands of a clever player.

    \n I like your new characters. Here's one I did for my daughter's 12th birthday party ~ 4 years ago. The character is an explorer.

    Seraphina Wright

    TRAITS

    Explorer
    Curious, Aware, Nimble, Hardy, Ancient Lore, Languages, Ruins, Monsters, Myths, Maps

    Bold
    Brave, Daring, Heroic, Rescue, Falling, Fire, Reckless, Explosions, Escapes, Outnumbered, [Underdog]

    Crew
    Gunnery, Aim, Cargo, Supplies, First Aid, The Owl, [Boarding Action], [Maintenance], [Damage Control], [Observation], [Signals]

    KEYS

    The Key of the Traveler
    You love exploring new places and meeting new people. Hit your key when you share an interesting detail about a person, place, or thing or when you go somewhere exciting and new.
    Buyoff: Pass up the opportunity to see something new.

    The Key of the Broker
    You like to make deals and trade favors. Hit your key when you bargain, make a new contact, or exchange a favor. Buyoff: Cut yourself off from your network of contacts.

    The Key of the Vow
    You have a vow of personal behavior that you have sworn not to
    break. Hit your key when your vow significantly impacts your
    decisions. Buyoff: Break your vow.associated responsibilities and privileges. Requires: Promotion in the organization or excellent forgeries of papers indicating
    the same.

    SECRETS

    The Secret of the Explorer
    You\u2019ve been all over the Blue, seen a lot of strange things. Once per session, you can re-roll a failure when you\u2019re dealing with local customs or strange places. Requires: You\u2019ve traveled from one
    side of the Blue to the other.

    The Secret of the Sky Song
    You know how to call sky squid. Requires: You\u2019ve trained with a master of the Sky Song or have been dream-linked to a sky squid.

    \n I made this guy a few years ago. (FYI, I think this was before I saw the Lady Blackbird Companion, so my versions of some of the stuff which happens to overlap with it are a little different . . . )

    Edgar Bell
    Grandson of Baron Whitehorn, officer cadet on the Hand of Sorrow, addict, artist, layabout

    TRAITS

    Black Sheep of a Noble House
    Educated in the Imperial Navy, History, Science, Gossip,
    House Whitehorn, [Etiquette], [Connections], [Wealth]

    Artist
    Poetry, Painting, Cabarets, Rejects, Revolutionaries,
    Stimulants, [Fame], [Patron]

    Snob
    Fashion, Spirits, Status, Fads, Impress, One-up, [Gifted],
    [Unflappable], [Intimidate]

    Half-voidblood [use one tag at a time]
    Invisibility, Vacuum, Make Weightless, Pass Through,
    [Erase mind], [Counterspell], [Disintegrate]

    KEYS

    Key of the Rebel
    You do not respect authority. Hit your key when you show
    contempt to someone in a position of power over you.
    Buyoff: Accept somebody as your leader

    Key of the Traitor
    You reject the Empire. Hit your key when you publically
    do harm to the Empire.
    Buyoff: Accept your noble birth-right

    Key of the Addict
    You abuse various elixirs, powders, and pills. Hit your key
    when your addiction causes you problems or is important in
    a scene.
    Buyoff: Kick the habit

    SECRETS

    Secret of Near Miss
    Once per session you may reroll a failure that involves
    avoiding physical harm.

    Secret of the Dilettante
    Once per session, you can duplicate any non-magical action
    performed by one of the other characters in a scene.
