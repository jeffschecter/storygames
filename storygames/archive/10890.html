\n So, I just signed up to D&D Insider.

My current experience with D&D 4th Edition: I've played it a handful of times, read downloaded PDFs of the core books (+PHB2, FRCG), and am currently writing up Polaris as a 4th edition setting (with full races and classes). Subscribing to the Insider for 3 months ($24) is the first money I've spent on D&D 4th.

It's pretty mind-boggling how much they are giving you.
The D&D Compendium lists all of the Races, Classes, Powers, Skills and Feats (ie, everything the player needs) as well as Traps (300) and Monsters (~3,000).
Dungeon and Dragon magazines are both there, which include Adventures (and a bunch of stuff that also appears automatically in the Compendium).
There's a Monster Builder, and a Character Builder, with the ability to generate a one-click character.

In short, if you have a printer, you don't need to ever buy a book.
Well, not true, I suppose... you need to learn how to play from somewhere, but if someone teaches you (or you download a PDF of the PHB), then the D&D Insider is the only purchase you ever need to make.

It feels like there's a lot of resources here, they're all linked up nicely (Stuff from Dragon also appears in the Compendium), and some of them are super useful.

...just wanted to share how impressed I was.
Now, if they have a free SRD somewhere as well, then the D&D Insider is literally the only purchase one ever needs to make.
Is such a thing in existence?

    \n As far as I know, Wizards dropped the SRD and OGL licences completely for 4E and went in another direction. This hit the supplement/hack market pretty hard, because with 3.x, the rules were free for use and anyone could write and publish a splatbook for the game, publish it under OGL and pop a D&D-compatible icon on the front cover.

    The relevant passage from wiki:
    "With the release of the fourth edition, Wizards of the Coast has introduced its Game System License, which represents a significant restriction compared with the very open policies embodied by the OGL. In part as a response to this, some publishers (such as Paizo Publishing with its Pathfinder Roleplaying Game) who previously produced materials in support of the D&D product line, have made the decision to continue supporting the 3rd Edition rules, thereby competing directly with Wizards of the Coast. Others, such as Kenzer & Company, are returning to the practice of publishing unlicensed supplements, stressing that copyright law does not allow Wizards of the Coast to restrict third-party usage."

    So, no, I don't think there is a SRD, or ever will be.

    \n As a 4E DM, I think D&D Insider is glorious. The Compendium is a rules database Done Right. I have never not been able to find the exact thing I was looking for with a quick compendium search, and it's great for quickly browsing possibilities for monsters or magic items.

    The Monster and Character builder tools are comprehensive, high-quality, and supremely easy to use. The Character Builder let me whip out eight pregens for a teaching game in about half an hour. The Monster Builder lets me do one-click level adjustments to preexisting monsters if I want to fine-tune them for an encounter, and prints out very nice looking cards as well.

    Dragon and Dungeon are usually decent reads for bus rides, but aren't quite essential. The Mearls stuff is pretty good, I guess.

    My only niggles are that the Builder tools are Windows-only. In fact, the Monster Builder is the only thing that keeps Windows running in my house. Also, the print functionality from the Compendium is terrible; WoTC has set the print stylesheet to be ugly ugly ugly, so if I want to print stuff from it, I have to slipstream my own stylesheet in.

    \n As to WoTC's money - they are making more from the on line fees big time.

    Say you buy $100 in books at a store. The manufacturer sold it to a distributor for a 60% discount (probably more like 55% for WoTC) so the manufacturer gets $45. You have three big off set press books printed in huge print runs so maybe they get them for $5 a book. They are now left with $30. They have a lot of overhead in maintaining the supply chain of paper products. So some of that $30 is used up. Let's say $6 worth. Leaving $24...

    The CEO looks at the bottem line. $24 where you have to hustle to sell books or $24 where you have to keep a supply of new online material flowing (which you would have to be doing anyway for paper products). I fully understand why they've done this even though I wonder if it is degrading the brand.

    D+D is not splashed big at the major cons like it used to be. Maybe paper products only speak to older players (who don't buy as much) and on line to young players (who do buy) I don't know. Best not to get too nostalgic I guess.

    \n I agree that D&D Insider is a great deal particularly for a DM.

    Posted By: Daniel H.
    Joe, when I saw you paid $24 for three months of DDI access, it seemed awfully high (roughly the same as the PH off Amazon).
    \nYeah,
    their rates are $9.95/month, $7.95/month (3 months), and $5.95/month (1 year).

    Here is the utility of the products, for me:

    So I could say to myself "Alright, I want to run a con game where the PCs are all Tieflings and Drow, and the adventure is in the underdark against their more sinister kin. I want a lv. 7 Tiefling Warlock, a lv. 7 Tiefling Warlord, a lv. 7 Drow Wizard, a lv. 7 Drow Rogue. Oh, and looking down you realize that you need a Defender, so you throw in a lv. 7 Tiefling Swordmage. And, in the event that there is a 6th player, hand them a lv. 7 Tiefling Avenger (3 strikers, one party! THEMATIC STATEMENT!)
    Total time taken: 10-15 minutes.

    Now I want to write this adventure up! I know that it'll have bad guy Drow and Tieflings (kin against kin!). I also know there's such a thing as a Drider.
    I turn to the compendium.
    Searching "underdark" gets me only 2 responses: Duergar Scout (lv.4) and Duergar Flesheater (lv.11). Neither feel quite right.
    Searching monsters lv5-11, with darkvision, from core rulebooks, who are minions... gets me 12 results. I marvel that there's a monster called "Vampire Spawn Fleshripper".
    I look at Legion Devil Guard, loathsome Chanter, Servile Ghost and Vile Pet.
    I then do a search for the same, except replace minion with Brute Elite, and I find out there's a lv. 11 named The Thing in the Hold. And one called a Blood Elemental.
    Finally, I do a search for "drow" under Creatures, and get a whack of results, selecting: Drow Shadowhunter, Drow Cleric of Lolth, Chosen Mezzodemon and finally Drow Spiderguard. Oh, and there should be tieflings in this dungeon! Search "tiefling" creatures, get a list of results, choose Tiefling Heretic and Karak the Damned.
    Finally, I need a final boss fight!
    Searching "drow" again gives me nothing like what I want. So instead I search Devils (lv6-11) and find a Pain Devil (lv9). Perfect.

    I can easily tweak the levels of these monsters in Monster Builder to make them more appropriate to my party, and construct my encounters:
    1. THE GATES OF THE HOLD. 2 Drider Fanglords (at the gates, can be negotiated with by the drow characters)
    2. THE DARK CORRIDOR. Drow Shadowhunter, 2 Drow Spiderguard, and a Chosen Mezzodemon.
    3. THE TEMPLE HALL. Drow Cleric of Lolth, 3 Loathsome Chanters, 2 Tiefling Heretics.
    3 THE INNER HOLD. Drow Spiderguard, and a Blood Elemental (with a skill challenge to prevent The Thing in the Hold from breaking free)
    4. THE LORD'S PRIVATE QUARTERS. The lord of this hold (Pain Devil), with his Vile Pet and his lover (Kravak the Damned).

    Now I write a bit of backstory. There was this Drow hold, and everything was lovely. And then the lord of this hold fell in love with a Tiefling, named Kravak. Kravak and some of his Tiefling follows joined the hold. Others were repulsed by the idea of living in a den of spiders and villainy, and a rift was formed with their leader Kravak. These Tieflings (A Warlock, a Warlord and a Swordmage) found two Drow (a Wizard and a Rogue) who'd been exiled by their lord for trying to stand up to him and argue a foolish course of action. They also want revenge. Now, these renegades return to the drow hold to destroy their led-astray former masters. They need to pass through the gates of the hold, and will surely encounter additional foes in the adjourning chambers. They'll pass through the Temple of Lolth, into the heart of the chamber, and finally find those two lovers and slay them both. Hurray!

    Total time: ~45 minutes. I did all the searching and encounter constructing while writing this post, and it took me 20 minutes. I just need to tweak the monsters in the Monster Builder, scaling their levels so that they are appropriate.     \n Oh, what's important about that whole process:
    The heroes are constructed from about 6 different books (PHB, PHB2, Forgotten Realms, Martial Power, Divine Power, Arcane Power).
    The monsters are taken from about 6 sources (Monster Manual, Monster Manual 2, Exemplars of Evil, some other ones).
    The monsters are a pastiche of levels: 3,4,6,11,12,13.

    To have designed this by hand would have been quite unwieldy.
    Character creation would have taken hours (Or, more realistically, I would have restricted my sources and limited options).
    I'd never have found all of those monsters.
    I'd not have been able to tweak them to be more-or-less level appropriate.

    Not only was it faster, but it enabled me to work with whatever material I felt like.
