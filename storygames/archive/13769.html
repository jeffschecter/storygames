\n Howdy y'all.
\n
\nI was looking for some recommendations for no-prep and little-prep RP games.
\n
\nI pulled together a group of players last week for some "Saga of the Icelanders". That game was literally 5 minutes of prep - sit down, pick a sheet and a couple of moves, then get straight into the game.
\n
\nI am looking for some other games that are like that - where the players can sit down and pretty much start playing without too much "study" required. Also that there isn't too much need for the GM to prepare a plot or story before.
\n
\nAnother game that we'll be trying next week is Fiasco, which is specifically gm-less and a pick up game.
\n
\nAnything else out there?
\n
\nK

    \n Let me add my game Ganakagok to the list of no-prep games. I should stipulate that it's slightly longer form, though, with complete games requiring on the order of 3 to 5 hours. It's a game of dramatic hope and heartbreak in a fantastical ice-world of eternal night. The game begins at the same point each time it is played: in a world that is ruled by the Stars, the Sun is about to rise. The order of things as you knew it is about to change forever. What do you do?

    \n A couple that haven't been mentioned yet:

    A Penny for my Thoughts, which is GMless, zero prep, and fast to play
    Puppetland, which requires a GM but no prep beyond a one-sentence premise description, and takes precisely one hour to play (maybe, if you're very slow, there's as much as half an hour of stuff you do before the game itself starts, so budget 90 minutes)

    Both of these games are excellent. The play of Penny is structured to create rules mastery during the session, and you've already mastered the rules of Puppetland.

    \nJason provided great written derivatives. But note that the default "island" setting in the rules is just a suggestion.

    We've played Archipelago a number of times as:
    -Inhabited asteroid belt
    -Nationalistic 19th century basecamps in the South Pole
    -A large valley in Gold Rush-era Northern California
    -Encampments in a post-apocalypic Midwest town

    Each of these ideas came out of a 10-15 minute discussion at the start of the session. All were one-shots and all were fun!
