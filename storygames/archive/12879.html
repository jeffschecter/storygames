    \n I've run a couple of auctions recently to sell off the vast majority of my Shadowrun and Exalted collections. (Combined, this needed three different boxes to ship, so was a pretty large collection.) Books which survived this culling fell into one of two categories: collector's editions (gilt edged or what long time Shadowrunners would know as a BABY) and boxed sets.

    Prior to this I culled a huge amount of stuff in order to move away from Colorado. From a ton of AD&D stuff, I kept only the original Deities & Demigods and the Manual of the Planes (which I still have). I also kept the original Battletech house books and my Traveller black box. I miss quite a bit of stuff I gave up now and again (Star Frontiers, Top Secret, Gamma World, Paranoia... sniff). Most of it I have since managed to get electronically. Some of it not (particularly Time and Time Again and Lords of Creation, not to mention a cherry version of the board game Shogun).

    I did keep a large supply of Dragon magazines (from #68 to about #130) during this move, thinking them crucial and worth shipping. They sat on a shelf until moved again, then again. When another move came, I found them a better home with a friend of mine. What helped to make this choice was buying the Dragon CD-ROM, which had them all electronically (I've even accessed these once or twice since).

    Going forward, I'm not buying hard-copies of much (I made the mistake of asking Quicken to tell me how much I've spent at drive-thru rpg a while back... I've been busy). I tend to get stuff that has limited runs (if they sold a leather bound D&D4, for example, I'd be all over that) or things that don't translate electronically (I just got my copy of Freemarket, for example).

    \n I feel great about my recent culling! I was sad to see some of it go, but I hope it all goes to good homes.

    Old Stuff I Got Rid Of
    Long list, but you can see a picture of it here.

    Old Stuff I Kept
    D&D Basic Set (Mentzer Red Box)
    D&D 4E core books
    Planescape 2E boxed set
    Forgotten Realms 3E and Unapproachable East sourcebooks
    Mutants Down Under and Mystic China (Palladium) sourcebooks
    GURPS China, GURPS Steam-tech, GURPS Castle Falkenstein and GURPS Ottoman Empire

    Why I Kept These Books
    I kept the old D&D box so I'd have a simple and beloved edition of D&D to play.
    I kept D&D 4E because some friends from my old group occasionally make noises about want to play it.
    I kept the others because they are great sources of inspiration for games.

    Stuff I Kept That I Never Considered Selling Anyway
    Mouse Guard
    Savage Worlds
    Burning Wheel (five rulebooks + Blossoms and Jihad)
    Dogs in the Vineyard
    InSpectres
    Mountain Witch
    Poison'd
    Dust Devils
    Montsegur
    Love in the Time of Seid
    Spirit of the Century
    Weapons of the Gods
    Fiasco
    Diaspora
    + a pile of printed and bound PDF games

    My Future Plans to Prevent Giant Piles of Unplayed Games
    For the last year or so I've tried to only buy games I think I'll play. PDFs are great for this, because I can buy or download something for cheap (usually) and have it printed and coil-bound at Kinko's for six bucks, and, for some reason, that stack doesn't "count" for me in the same way a big shelf full of gaming books does.

    * * *

    As you can see, the pile of things I never considered selling is mostly indie/story/new-school games.

    So this culling process is clearly the documentation of a major transformation in what games I play.

    \n For me, I think I told my story a few times. But in late 2000 I came back to America after being in Japan for a number of years. Brought with me 3 cats, 1 wife and no career. I had to start over from scratch, basically. They were fun times, but definitely very frugal.

    Anyway, I happened to end up in NC, where my folks lived, and to basically clean up my folks' house, clean up my life a bit, and make some money in the progress, I proceeded to sell just about everything I had accumulated: Mostly books, games, etc. The thrill of basically slimming down my life caught on, and I basically from that point on had an unwritten rule that my games wouldn't take up more than "two shelves" before I started selling again. In truth, it wasn't a real rule or anything, but it sure felt right. I've gone over at times, in fact right now I'm kind of at 2.5 shelves (but I've got some multiple copies of various games, plus that includes the stuff I plan on selling off soon).

    At the time, I ended up selling everything I had, and made a few thousand. Everything from classic D&D to WoD to Fading Suns to Shadowrun. The only things I kept at that time were:

    A set of Planescape stuff, some signed by Tony DiTerlizzi
    A set of Blue Planet books
    Sengoku and a few supplements
    Fudge
    D&D 3e PHB
    BESM 2nd Edition
    Dark Sun set
    1 copy of AD&D 2e, still in a weird faux-pleather tome cover thing I bought at a GenCon way back when. This was the book that turned me from a casual RPGer to "heads over heels in love with The Game".
    A copy of The End
    D&D Rules Cyclopedia
    D&D Mystara: Kingdom of Nithia
    Some L5R books
    Some Tribe 8 books
    Star Wars d6 Revised and Expanded
    Continuum
    And a few various AD&D items

    Cut to almost ten years later, from that original set here's what I have left:

    Planescape
    Blue Planet
    Sengoku
    That AD&D 2e PHB in its cover
    The End
    D&D Mystara: Kingdom of Nithia (I love the illustrations of egyptian fantasy warriors, great stuff)
    Continuum

    I will likely actually be getting rid of Blue Planet and Sengoku at some point, maybe even some of the Planescape stuff as well. My interests have changed too much, there's likely no chance of using it again... The only memento I'll be likely keeping is that copy of the 2e PHB...

    Anyway, I really dig living with less. If nothing else, it's much easier to clean, and I don't have to buy nearly as many bookshelves and stuff.

    \n I used Noble Knight for the culling referenced in the thread title. I would recommend it enthusiastically.

    Aaron gave me a quote via email after I sent him a list of games with a very vague description of their condition (good vs fair). He also sent me FedEx labels, so I didn't have to pay shipping costs (a big deal, when talking about boxes filled with giant hardback books).
