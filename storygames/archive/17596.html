\n Choose your own adventures? In real time? While the author's writing?

Crazy premise isn't it? That's what anonkun.com is about.

http://anonkun.com/

We have a community of writers and readers who want to tell the writer what to write. People make polls, and mouth off to each other as the story's happening. The writer basically acts like a DM.

We have a variety of stories already, come check us out.

http://anonkun.com/stories

    \n Huh, that's a pretty clever idea. The site is well-made as well, kudos to the developer. I feel like this sort of thing should have legs - and evidently this is an extension of some sort of on-going 4chan phenomenon? The role of the author seems interesting, and voting on story direction is a low-commitment way to participate, suitable for almost anybody.

    Skimming a few stories gives me the impression that these are mostly pretty deep into memetic Internet culture. Also, it clearly takes some degree of skill for the author to craft meaningful and interesting choices. I wonder if there are some particularly brilliant stories out there in this format that might be held up as good examples of what the form can achieve?
