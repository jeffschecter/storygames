    \n Yeah, it'd be nice if Scribus really could manage to bump off Quark and InDesign from my palette of layout programs. Practically speaking, I don't really consider it when starting a new project. If I didn't have other options at hand, though, I'd probably use it and curse it.

    Posted By: Keith FyansI have a few of questions:

    - What would peeps here recommend for an RPG writer to use for book layout?
    - What would be the costs?
    - What would the down-sides be to using an educational copy of which doesn't allow for commercial use (would anyone care)?
    \nIf you're the RPG writer, you might consider using a separate graphic designer as your layout-guy. The costs would depend on the length of the work and your connections; I wouldn't be entirely surprised if you could wrangle somebody to do it for free - full professional prices wouldn't likely make any sort of sense in this sort of project, anyway.

    If you're planning to do the layout yourself, I don't find the following course entirely bad:\n
      \n
    1. Create your first work in Open Office. Make this a short to mid-length book with a limited amount of graphic bling; you'll learn the limitations of the program without killing yourself with it.
      2. \n
    2. After getting frustrated by the amount of work, switch to Scribus for your second project. Learn good layout workflows and professional typography.
      3. \n
    3. After getting tired with the lacking features and instability of Scribus, buy InDesign or QuarkXPress for your third project. Apparently student licenses make this a deal even if you're not taking in actual professional income with your layout skills and software package.
      4. \n

    Don't buy InDesign unless you're a professional layout designer, getting seriously into layout or laying out lots of books. Even with the student discount, it's too much. You do not need it to lay out your first project. Use a cheap version first, play around, and trade up if you need to.

    Scribus is an option, but it's pretty crap. I think Serif Page Plus is good for $10.
