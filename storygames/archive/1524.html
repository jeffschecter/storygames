The first bit of art for the Infected arrives.

Eric Provost
October 2006 edited October 2006 in Directed Promotion

Hiya all,

I'm in the home stretch of commissioning my first bit of art for a game. Check it out;

It was done by Claudia Cangini and I have got to tell you she was a pleasure to work with. I've never had the opportunity to work with an artist before and I was certainly nervous about it. And I told her so when we were first chatting about the game. But she was very professional, terribly helpful, and apparently just a little bit psychic.

This is what I gave her to work with;

Martyr: A woman sits in a throne. She looks sad and off-camera. Her flesh is pierced in many places by spears, held by off-scene attackers. The spears pierce her heart, her womb, and her head. Her hand loosely holds the spear in her heart.

See how her left hand has three fingers sticking up? Yeah, I totally imagined that. Specifically, three fingers. But I never mentioned it to Claudia. I told her that, within the outline I gave her, she was totally free to make shit up. And she came up with stuff that was already in my head.

Check out Claudia's Deviant Art site at http://claudiacangini.deviantart.com/ . I look forward to working with her again in the future.

-Eric

  • Eric Provost
    October 2006

    Good questions!

    There were a lot of influences for the Infected. The most obvious ones are monster movies. Especially zombie movies. When I first came up with the idea, Jason Morningstar loaned me a great big stack o' zombie movies. And I spent one whole Saturday doing nothing but watching them. But, the more I worked on it, the more I discovered that the whole turning into a monster thing applied to a whole lot of monster movies, werewolves and vampires and radioactive mutants included.

    The game and system influences include everything that Jason's doing, mostly because I get the opportunity to playtest with him pretty often, as well as The Mountain Witch and Trollbabe. Also influencing the mechanisms was my desire to have a game designed specifically to run at conventions. Something that would fit nicely into that 6-player 4-hour slot that's so common at the bigger conventions.

    Mechanically unique? Well, I didn't set out looking for any uniqueness, but I think I managed to find a bit along the way. The conflict resolution system, the way the dice flow, isn't unique at all. In fact, it's really just a re-imaging of the Trollbabe system, cobbled together with some inspiration from the game formerly known as AG&G. The use of the Motivation Cards to immediately spark the players' imaginations does come to mind though. I don't think anyone's doing anything like that right now. It works pretty well to bring a player from zero idea about what their character is about right into knowing all about what's wrong with them in no time flat. I think it's pretty damned awesome.

    To give you an idea, just take a peek again at that picture up there. Now imagine that it's one of several cards in a deck, and that's the one you were delt. It's face down, so only you know what it is. The game asks you to consider the image of that card, to let it represent something that's totally fucked up in your life. The figure or figures on your card might represent your character, or someone important to them. You're free to interpret the symbols of the card however you like. Now that you've got an idea of what's wrong in your character's life, get a picture in your head for how they're going to fix that wrong. Got it? Awesome. Now, spend the whole session trying to fix that wrongness.

    And, oh yeah, there's fucking monsters on the loose that are going to naturally be messing with every little thing you do. Trying to reconsile with your mother after years of hatred? Hard to do when she can blame you for letting your sister get eaten by flesh eating zombies. Even harder when your dad, who used to be on your side IS a zombie now.

    -Eric
