\nFastaval 2012 will be held from April 4-8, 2012 somewhere in Denmark. It will be followed by an RPG research summit in Helsinki, Finland as well as Solmukohta 2012, the big larp summit.

Frederik Jensen, Frederik Berg \xd8stergaard, Tobias Wrigstad, Olle Jensen, Anne Westerling, Nathan Hook, and many more European RPG writers / larpwrights can be found there.

U.S. American Fastaval alumni include: Emily Care Boss, John H. Kim, Bill White, Julia and Chris Ellingboe, Kat Jones, Matt Beisler, myself, Jared Sorensen and Luke Crane (I may be missing a few).

My Metropolis larp scenario, which has run at InterCon and MaxiCon here in the U.S. has been accepted, so now I really have to decide if I'm going to go this year.

Anyone in this forum interested in attending?

(For those with any doubts: it really is the best role-playing I've ever seen at a con, along with the best booze)

    \n 75% chance of me showing up. It would be my first Fastaval ever.

    My gaming interests are pretty much on the other side of the spectrum from what can be had on Fastaval - I prefer small games where the players bring their own Awesome with them, rather than detailed games where a GM has prepared the Awesome in advance.

    \n Simon, thanks for the kind words,

    I also love the Indie Room / Indie Gaming Lounge at GothCon. But I have been running games there for five years straight. It is familiar, comfortable and something I can do very well by now, or so I'd like to think. But I'm stagnating, and I'm not learning new things any more. This irks me a bit, I want to see new things so I can make the Indie Room even better in the future.

    Also, the old guard has been dominating the Indie Room, it is time to give the new talents room to grow. With WRNU taking over the management from Arvidos the Indie Room has every chance to transform into something new, even better. 2012 is the perfect time to take a year off and see new things.

    I'm not telling you to go, or to stay, do as your heart bids you. But I'm planning for Fastaval, and it feels good.
