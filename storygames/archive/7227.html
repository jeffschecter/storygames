\n Has anyone played PTA with this many? I made an open call to friends for some roleplaying, hoping that maybe half would be able to make it. Instead almost everyone could. I know at least three want to play PTA as the top pick out of the options I gave (and three more are interested), so it's looking likely.

I was thinking this was on the high side after some stories, but the book suggests having a season that closely matches the number of players, so since there's a 9 episode season option, I figure the player number could at least go that high. Also Firefly has 9 core characters, so it should work story wise. But we might only be doing a pilot (I'm not sure if all 9 of us will be able to meet regularly).

So, experiences with a lot of people in PTA? How did it go? Any tips on helping it run smoothly?

As an aside, I put 9 in parentheses because one person who can't make it is interested in making it at some future point. I figuring adding him in will be easy, since the first episode will be the pilot, it won't be hard to fill in a gap (well, hopefully) and at least mechanically will work out just fine. But, can you run PTA with inconsistent players? Like I have 8 this time, 7 the next, 9 after that, 6 for episode 3, etc....

    \n I've never played a game of PTA with so many players, but I do have some thoughts:

    1. Be careful. The conflict resolution mechanic could get messy in situation involving more than 2 or 3 characters. (Maybe there's a trick to it, though?)

    2. You could make a large group PTA game more fun for everyone by creating a fairly limited setting. Take an isolated location like a shipwreck on an island or a small space station, and populate it only sparsely (if at all). With the majority of the people existing in your setting being PCs, any scene you come up with will involve multiple players and be relevant enough to everyone else to keep them interested. Contrast that with a story spread out over several continents, where one character's actions could hardly affect or concern any other PCs!

    Hope that helps!

    \n Ran PTA with nine (!!!) players at Game Storm earlier this year. (We came up with "Funkula", a show about a nine-piece funk band that fights vampires.)

    It ran just fine with nine players. Granted, one player was pretty tired, so he wasn't able to contribute much. (Ever a danger with convention games.) Everyone else contributed about in-line with their screen presence for the episode. The 1s mostly observed and pitched in from time-to-time, the 2s did a moderate amount of roleplaying and contributing, and our spotlight player was on fire.

    So I'd say, go ahead and try it. Just makes sure that 1.) you pitch a show that can handle a large ensemble cast, and 2.) you skip the pilot. With a large group, you need strong screen presence roles right away, so that each player knows how much they're expected to contribute in any given episode.

    And remember that PTA has "audience participation" rules in the back, so that people without characters can contribute to the game. If someone knows that they won't make it to most games, you can see if they would prefer an "audience" role.
