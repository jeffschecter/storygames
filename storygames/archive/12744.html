\n Since, Jason's original thread has descended into a parody of itself, I wanted to start a new thread to bring together the issues that are popping up in the indie blogosphere and am hoping that those folks who were at GenCon can help those of us who weren't (like me) get a better handle on how the community seems to be doing and what are the major issues facing indie RPG fans and designer/publishers this year.

As someone who wasn't at GenCon, it seems like the fallout this year -- at least so far -- has mostly been about frustrations with existing publishing practices (the cost of booths, concerns about IPR and distro in general, looking for new ways for individual design-publishers to collaborate better). Like a lot of our previous controversies (the "why isn't the community supporting me by buying my game?" meltdown of 2006-2007, for example), I think this one has been a long time in coming, following the exodus from the combined Forge-IPR booth after 2007-8 (?). Are these the continued ripples of that or the beginnings of some larger change? Hard to know yet, perhaps.

For examples of this general trend, there's:
-- a few threads about booth stuff on SG: Selling at GenCon, the First-Timers Area, and the GPA program
-- some conversations about sales #s in the Design Matters thread
-- Chris Chinn providing a wider historical perspective over here
-- Elizabeth over here (EDIT: check out Rob's response, too) and followup
-- Vincent and friends over here and conversations mentioned here

Another major concern seems to be Games on Demand and in general better integrating GoD/IGE into the overall indie games experience at GenCon. This has also been a perennial topic, even back when Kat Miller was running it every year and things seemed somewhat more organized. I would venture to suggest that the indie publishing community loves the idea of strengthening both on-site and future sales through supporting play at conventions -- in addition to enjoying playing games with each other -- but hasn't ever really settled on a model that feels really optimal. Honestly, this may be something that is always under inspection and revision, just like publishing / distro / marketing / convention methods, in an attempt to keep making it better or suit current needs / trends.

Threads on GoD are popping up:
-- here on SG

There's also the annual discussion of the ENnies and indie games' role in awards, but I'm not sure we've really said anything new there this year. Am I wrong?

In some ways, it's interesting that none of these conversations seem to happening on The Forge, at least yet. Another sign of shifting things, perhaps, or maybe people are waiting to post more thoughtful responses when they get a chance.

What about other folks? What themes are you hearing or pondering? What conversations did you seem to have more than once?

A small request: can we try to talk about the conversations that are happening without trying to argue with folks or blame them for having these conversations or concerns? I realize a lot of this stuff is potentially controversial and involves money and status and all that crap. But it would be nice to be able to check the pulse of the community on various issues without having this degenerate into a mess. Thanks.
