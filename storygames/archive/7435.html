\n A bunch of people get thrown in a lifeboat in the nineteenth century. Things are looking bad. There's nothing to eat but some mint cake and the ship's Boy.

There are two classes of people: Those with some energy to get things done, and the poor souls too exhausted to do anything but lay around and drain your resources. I'm not sure what to call either group

Right now they are Castaways and Wretches, but are passable I'm unsatisfied with both terms. Survivor is right out!

So, in the rules, it says things like: "At dawn you can either draw an Event card or allow a Wretch to die" or "Lower a Castaway\u2019s Degeneracy by one point".

Any suggestions for better terms, lazyweb? Thanks!

    \n Well, this is your game and all so I might be getting it completely wrong. All of the following is simply from my standpoint of me thinking about playing your game and what I might want out of it.
    \n
    \nI personally think that implying that the Wretched don't have souls is not a good idea. If your game is by any chance about exploring what it's like to be in a me-or-them type of situation or about making a statement about the value of human life or about making a statement about whether it's better to sacrifice yourself in order to help others vs. sacrificing others to help yourself, then it would probably be advisable to even make the Wretches as human-like as possible. Incredibly unhelpfull humans, maybe even despicable, but humans. By imposing such a strong valued nomenclature on both parties your taking any kind of statement out of the hands of the players.
    \n
    \nHowever, you're an accomplished game desinger and so you've probably already thought of all this.
    \n
    \nIf you want to set up the game with such a strong judgement, however, you could even go so far as to call the Wretched "Cattle", "Provisions", "Rations", "Ballast", "Freight" or other de-humanizing things. Calling people who eat their kin "Souls" however, doesn't feel right to me at all. "Soul" generally implies something good or sacred. And I see nothing good or sacred in eating other people, even (or especially) if it is for the sake of saving yourself. But maybe that's just me. Something more aggressive would be a better fit, I guess. Something like "Driver" (or "Officer", as has already been suggested) or "Driven" (driven to survive no matter what, that is). You could go with the probably somewhat-out-of-place "Warrior", or the more modern version "Fighter". "Driven" might just be my favorite for the active party and "Cattle" for the passive party.

    \nYou're the malingerer, Mr. Karlman!

    Sven, the game sets up a deliberate death spiral, so the logical thing to do is to dispatch the useless hangers-on as soon as is practical and convert them into food (and water; there's water in the liver!). The beautiful thing I've seen emerge again and again in playtesting, though, is that quite often there will be a player who isn't at all interested in the logical solution. These people will doggedly hang onto their humanity and go down like heroes, losing by every objective measure but winning the moral battle by staying human. I love this. I think it makes the game much more fun. One of the victory conditions rewards this, actually - if nobody survives, the first player whose Castaway died wins.
