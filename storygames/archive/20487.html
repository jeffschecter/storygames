    \n Thank you for your kind comment!

    Well, on the call subject, I wanted to do a table for "background flavor" which included the exact ones that you're talking about: Wilderness, the dead, eldritch horrors... and then I couldn't think on anything else. So I left it blank and let the players choose whatever they liked. I guess that depending on which effects they want to achieve, they'll have to pick a different entity, but also it will carry a specific kind of drawbacks (In short, I think it will be easier to deal with that in-game than on the rulebook)

    On the second question, I wanted it to fit my specific campaign, but I didn't wanted to implement Final Fantasy quirks on the rules. I made something more general for a fantasy world where everyone can learn some magic; but much grittier than a videogame (no phoenix downs or massive-damaging summons; in my campaign I want to enforce the "cold war" aspect of infiltrating into the enemy lines and plotting the assasination of a supernatural entity).

    But I have this Final Fantasy-related idea that I might implement: every character that is wielding their favored weapon can decide during combat to land a LIMIT BREAK: you sacrifice 1d6 HP to add a third d6 to your attack roll; then you choose the higher two results. The player describes which kind of technique does this involve in-game.
