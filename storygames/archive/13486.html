\n I'm talking about: The Magnificent Seven, The Fellowship of the Ring, Aliens, RED, The Dirty Dozen, Firefly, Ocean's 11, Predators, Band of Brothers, Star Wars, Young Guns, Toy Story 2, Uncommon Valor, U.S. Marshalls, The Losers, Jason and the Argonauts, Pitch Black, even the A-Team.

In every group there seem to be members that have similar roles (even stereotypes). Sometimes these members only have a single defining role and sometimes they are a bit more complex, filling numerous roles at once.

Here are a few I've come up with:

Leader The guy who calls the shots
Veteran Guy with years of experience
Rookie No experience, trying to prove himself
Loyal The guy the leader can count on to back him up
Outsider Doesn't have the trust of the rest of the group
Joker Doesn't take things seriously, goofs off
Mysterious The quiet guy that no one really knows much about
Professional Stays on task, gets the job done
Wanna-be leader Always questions the leaders authority
Coward When the going gets tough, so does he
The Whiner When the situation is bleak, he'll make sure you know how bleak it is
Peacemaker Friend to everyone, tries to calm inter-group conflicts
Rogue Is in it for himself, not the group
Heavy The big guy
Spiritual The guy who has more faith in a higher power than in the group itself
Wise The teacher or sage of the group
Greedy Always looking to pick up a few extra bucks along the way
Hot head Easily angered, unpredictable
Lone Wolf Prefers to work alone
Brain Uses brains instead of brawn

I know there are more. If you can think of examples, let me know. Thanks.

    \nThe Healer Worries about the (physical and emotional) well-being of the group, most often the medic of sorts
    The Social Beast If it takes diplomacy, seduction, fast-talk, social manipulation of any sort, this is your guy
    Planner Always practical, always down-to-earth, always quick to improvise new plans, but more comfortable when given time to prepare and calculate paths and procedures
    Scape-goat The Wolverine type ("I'm the best at what I do, but what I do isn't pretty"), willing to do the dirty work everyone else is unwilling or unable to do

    \n The Hot-Shot: I can do it myself, I don't need anyone's help, I'm the best on the team. Basically, kind of a big deal.

    The Psycho: Charges right in and sows confusion and mayhem, often thinks they are in charge. Sun Tzu said that.

    The Drunk: It might not be drink, but he's been so scarred by the jobs he's done, he can't go in without a filthy bottle of cheap scrumpy in his belly. They've got more *bleep bleep bleep* than they have the likes of me.

    The Dummy: Maybe you can outsmart him. Maybe. But you can't outsmart boolet.

    The Gadgeteer: A planner, the sort of person with 10 smaller cases inside their suitcase, and the right tool for every situation. Even if it's just more gun.

    The Professional: Blokes who bludgeon their wives to death with golf trophies have feelings - by contrast, professionals have standards.

    The Cheat: He turns the other side against each other rather than fighting them directly. The second worst thing that will happen to you today.

    \nMuscle: "Don't ask me, I'm just the brawn." He's not just the tough guy. He doesn't want it any other way.

    Bastard: Everyone hates him (with good reason), but he's just too important for the job.

    The Mentor: He's not just a grizzled veteran, he likes sharing his experiences with the young and naive. He can be rough and cynical, but he means well. (Might or might not die before the end.)

    The Rival: He wants what you want, he needs what you need, he knows what you need to know. He wants to see you fall, but in a fair fight and by being superior to you.

    Bootlicker: He's too weak, coward, opportunist or stupid to survive on his own, so he is always trying to be on the good side of those able to (sometimes a little too much for their liking). Might turn the traitor or the mole with ease.

    The fool: He is not the smartest pea in the pot. In fact, he is way too dense and close-minded, and his ideas could lead to certain catastrophe. Everyone knows that... except him. (He's just convinced no one else understands his geniality.)

    The Diva: He's here for the glory, the fame and the attention. He's more concerned about looking good and not loosing face, than about getting the job done. He's good at what he does most of the time, but when he fails he's easy to anger when someone criticizes him.
