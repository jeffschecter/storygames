    1) No stereotypes, none of the races are definitevly good, evil, etc. So, if you meet a goblin, you won't know if they are friend or foe til you get to know them. Also, all nations give bonuses (even humans). But, each nation has its own distinct "feel."

    The thing that struck me about Lanasia was the idea of all these political entities and the inter-relations between them. So...

    Imagine a matrix with all the major nations across the top and sides. Each square represents how two nations relate.

    What role do the players have in affecting this matrix? What if every player had a marker saying where they lived on this matrix? What if they could change what was in a square through their actions? What if their enemies were trying to change that value in a different direction? What would they do?

    Or imagine a matrix with pretty generic terms ("hate", "mistrust", "alliance"). Now the players get to fill in all or some of the matrix squares with a beleif that reflects the term already there, like "you can't trust anyone form Aerie, butter to just kill 'em", or "they embody what we fear about ourselves", or "our cities are built on the blood of their sacrifice".

    Or what if the races were all really reflections of magical stock, but don't know it. If a nation's relaitons with its neighbors are hostile, the people of that race morph into a more hostile form, until they aren't the same race they were.

    I dont' know if that helps or not...
