    \n You will find a great deal of the feel of Traveller in TS. That said, the print version I got early had some very confusing typos in it. Snag some errata on their site. You can also find a basics document there to test drive it.

    Overall, the setting is geared to that large, Imperial space setting - a large and sprawling government keeps the trains moving on time, but needs some help in that from the occasional adventurer... One of my friends who is normally very critical of games loved the system.

    There is also an add-in book that has three novellas from H Beam Piper and the TS stats for running the setting.

    \n As part of playing Classic 'story-gameized' Traveller at Dexcon, we used the TS planet creation rules to whip up a few planets to round out the galaxy. That process definitely had the feel of Traveller, but was easier to use as the different charts for atmosphere, planet size (or whatever) etc., weren't linked to the previous rolls. It still required some thought to figure out why a planet high in population and agriculture had a poisonous atmosphere but that was part of the fun!

    \n I own and have read TS. I'd love to play it.

    Compared to Traveller:
    Can't die during chargen. Actually TS chargen reminds me of Space:1889. After species, homeworld and stats, you pick previous occupations to get your skills.
    2d12 gives a wider range of results than 2d6.
    TS has Aspects and FATE tokens - relabeled Hooks and and something else... Action points?
    TS has social conflict rules.

    The game design is more structured than Starblazer Adventures, but less traditional than Traveller.
