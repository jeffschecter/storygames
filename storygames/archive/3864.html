How about you? I was curious if there was some strong electrical force that puts gaming usually on Thursdays (when "the best" TV is on, which I religiously skip).

Sometimes, local friends and I throw down on Friday night, Saturday or Sunday afternoon, but that is not regular.

    \n I'll throw in the longest possible answer to this question:
    \n
    \nMy group always schedules ad-hoc. We schedule over email and pick a day that's best for the players who are playing. The group is about 8-10 people but each game is typically 3-4 players. We play with the same group of friends (it's not an open club), but not necessarily the exact same people at every game if that makes sense. Not everyone can play all the time and not everyone is interested in every game i.e. some people only want to play D&D;, but not SotC and vice-versa and that's ok. For some reason, an above average number in our group have flexible or non-standard work hours so we play pretty much any day of the week, though we play more consistently on the weekends for those with more traditional schedules.
    \n
    \nThe guy who started this arranged it this way because we weren't in college anymore and had complicated lives, jobs and families so if you waited until everyone's schedule lined up, you'd only game once every 2 months. There's also this debatable notion that you should only game when everyone really wants play, not just because it's on the calendar and everyone is used to showing up (but that's another discussion). Anyway, the solution was to have campaigns that were more flexible so people who want to play a lot (once, twice a week) can and those who can only manage once a month can as well and those who suddenly find themselves with tons of time (vacation, unemployment, spouse-out-of-town) can play to their hearts content. Our group has 1 or 2 main campaigns and anyone who wants to run or play something else can just throw it out and see if anyone else is interested. In general, I think there's enough gaming to go around with occasional peaks and valleys.

    \n I'd be not at all surprised to find out a preponderance of Thurs regulars. IME, there're way fewer regularly-scheduled events of other sorts on Thurs--which is part of why "the good TV" is scheduled then. Fri night & weekends people are, typically, completely free, so they go out, or spend the weekend doing things, like short trips. Social/group/community things tend to be Sun-Wed evenings, because turn-out on Thurs, while not as poor as Fri, is too low. That leaves Thurs as the night that's not quite the weekend (so it's "ok" to not go out--whether going out for you means partying like a rockstar or spending time with family), and not as likely to have your knitting club or dance practice or whatever. At least that's been my experience with a *lot* of people, not just my gaming friends. And since gaming typically involves scheduling a half dozen or so people, which is many fewer than most of the other weeknight activities, it becomes the most flexible, and thus gets the 'last choice' night, which is Thurs.

    Similarly, the local college is huge (40000), yet if you want anything more intellectually stimulating than a foreign film, the weekend is right out, and Thurs is extremely unlikely--because they get low turnout if they schedule something then. It was always extremely frustrating to me when i was in college, because i had classes Mon-Thurs nights, so i could never go to any of the guest lectures or any of that stuff, and on Fri and Sat nights, there was nothing to do but drink, catch a movie, or hang with friends.

    So, there's my pop-sociology analysis of why i'd *expect* Thurs evening to be the most popular gaming day.

    For me, the previous group was Wed night. Current group is weekends, usually Sat or Sun evening, sometimes Fri night. I'd really much prefer a weeknight because my experience has been that way too many people do things like go out of town on weekends. So while we can't run late on a weeknight, neither do we have to skip a week because too many people are out of town (none of the people i game with are the sort to travel for business). But, unfortunately, half the group is dancing the other 4 nights of the week (and Sat and Sun afternoons). And, for everyone in the group but me, gaming is lower on the priority list than a lot of other things--it's more about something to do with friends, than it is about the gaming--so getting a less-interrupted night of the week isn't likely to happen.

    Back in college, it was our regular weekend thing--though i can't remember now if it was Fri or Sat night. I think Sat, because i think i remember starting earlier than we would've been able to on Fridays.\n

    Posted By: Skylawoodelf wrote:I just need to get more of them interested in GMing, or interested in GMless RPGs, so that it actually works.
    \nIt's my first day posting on SG and I have already achieved synergy! Inthis other post of mine, I talk about the one-shot as the gateway drug to gaming and to get your players to GM, start aRun Clubwhere everyone runs a one-shot and everyone plays in everyone else's game.     Yeah, saw that post. And I tend to agree that that's a perfect match for the one-shot game. I haven't yet convinced anyone else in the group, however. I'll keep working on them, and putting forth games i think the given player would (1) be comfortable running and (2) be good at running. The one thing working against that is that a fair number of people i've dealt with are only comfortable running a game after they're very familiar with it. So the last thing they want to GM is a game that's new to the whole group, much less to them.

    The only thing i've found better than the one-shot for "breaking in" new GMs, is troupe-style play. Because then hte proto-GM doesn't have to create characters or setting, just plot. And not only the GM, but also the rest of the players are already familiar with the characters, the setting, the rules, the mood, etc. The only thing that's new is the new GM. And we're currently running a system and setting that're designed for and perfectly matched to troupe-style play, respectively. We've set up the game to be conducive, now i just need to get someone else to actually step up and GM.
