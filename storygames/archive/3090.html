    \n Don: I did try staggering the diamonds, but it came out looking like crap. It was just a grid with some off-sized elements (which looked accidental), tilted 45 degrees. The top circles got staggered at first because I increased their size, and then I decided it looked better this way.

    Joe: Huh. It does look sort of diner-esque. I hadn't noticed. (Other people's comments about the 50's "ultramodern" vibe are definitely part of what I was going for.) What sort of visual elements would you normally associate with high technology, keeping in mind that aircars and spaceships are practically verboten in this game?

    Daniel: The colors in that image are slightly more yellow than they appeared in the original piece; they definitely had a more gold look there. I'm going to be using gold as a motif throughout the book - the page borders are identical to the character sheet borders, and I'm going to be telling my artists to try to work in a little gold when possible. The advice section will actually talk about why gold is a theme - how gold doesn't tarnish, how technology makes it cheaply available in almost every civilization in the game, how the purest gold isn't the strongest, and general references to a "golden age."

    \n Colin ran a session of his new rules recently and I had a blast, even though science fiction is not really my thing. I never played the previous version, but this new one is diceless. If I recall correctly, you earn points by by playing to your Core Values, and spend them to activate Themes. For instance, my agent character had the theme "Super Spy," which I activated to pull off some cool stunts during a daring rescue of our captured teammate. The resolution system is just comparing skill pool totals, but with a neat trick: instead of binary resolution, you compare the totals and demand concessions from your opponent based on your scores. It's cool.

    \n Woah, how did this thread get back on the front page? I was just cruising through looking for inspiration for NaGaDeMon and wondered if I had accidentally clicked the "My Threads" link.

    Anyway, yeah, Sufficiently Advanced worked out really well for me. It's been my most popular game, even beating out Console (and let me tell you, I had groupies for Console).

    I'm building toward the Beta version of SA2. As Scrape mentioned, we did a 2nd Edition playtest recently at Modern Myths, and it worked fairly well. There are definitely still a lot of changes that need to be made, and a lot of writing that will probably have to wait until the spring, but it's progressing. As usual, updates can be found on the forums at http://suffadv.wikidot.com/ (Run AdBlock when you go there; Wikidot has ads these days.) My biggest question right now is going to be how to handle the sheer size of the game; 240+ pages is a lot. I was already getting comments on the last version about having to read too much setting and tech to grok the game.

    --Colin

    P.S.: Detail item - you currently earn the points that Scrape is talking about by screwing your character over, rather than playing to your Core Values. However, I need to do something better with CVs anyway... Hmm.
