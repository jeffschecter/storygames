Congratulations to Dixit by Jean-Louis Roubira and artist Marie Cardouat!

The Spiel de Jahres is the most prestigious game award in Europe and, really, the whole world. Generally the award goes to cool board games and card games. To my knowledge, this is the first time such a story-driven game has won the award. Pretty awesome and relevant to this forum, I think.

    \n Dixit is a really solid party game. It's vaguely reminiscent of Balderdash, in that everyone at the table is trying to both simultaneously deceive each other and pick the correct card. But it also has the best part of Apples to Apples in that you just throw in a card rather than have to write some definition or whatever, which really slows things down and requires a whole other set of skills.

    Basically, the "Storyteller" picks one card from his hand, puts it facedown, and describes it using a sound, word, phrase, or sentence. Everyone else then looks at their hand and tries to pick the card that would best fit that description as well. The cards are then shuffled and flipped over, and all the players (non-storytellers) vote on which one is the original card played by the storyteller.

    Scoring goes like this:
    - If all or none of the players picked the storyteller's card, the storyteller scores nothing and the players all score 2 points (meaning that the storyteller tries to be somewhat vague in their description, but not make it too difficult that no one gets it)
    - If only some of the players pick the right card, then they and the storyteller get 3 points
    - Players also get 1 point if any other player pick their card instead

    You play through the deck of incredibly beautiful and somewhat disturbing cards, and whoever has the most points wins!

    \n We played it for the first time tonight, and pretty much agreed that it was very fun and interesting. One thing, is that it is quite hard when it's your turn to actually decide which of your six cards to use, and then what to "say" (the "sentence") about that card. Otherwise, we liked every element of it.

    Inside Joke for folks that have the game: I dig bizarre/ironic humor. Anyway, some of our "sentences" were rather esoteric. It just so happened that the last round I was the judge/sayer. I looked at my deck, picked one. My sentence was:
    "The Thermometer Bleeds Like a Snake"
    I collected everyone's cards, but made sure to shuffle them so that my card was on the bottom and would be revealed last. Then I started turning them over, one by one, watching their faces as they intently tried to determine which one was my card. Then the last one was revealed, which was, of course, a picture of a thermometer, with the mercury part actually a snake made of blood, bleeding all over the place. They didn't stop laughing for like a minute. You can only pull that joke once per group playing the game for the first time, tho. :-)
