  • shreyas
    March 2006

    Adequacy and optimality are distinct. Wiki is enough for Lexicon, but it's also not ideal.

    What do we know about Lexicon?

    • Individual authors write entries
    • Entries are linked to one another
    • An entry (except for the seeds) is always named and thereby created by someone other than its author.

    So, what I want is a robust system for linking articles together (which wikis often lack), without the free-for-all editing chaos of wiki.

    I've used Mediawiki for my Lexicon, but I had to work with the added disadvantage of needing a wiki that's already coded to work with Hebrew (You would not believe the incompatabilities between left-to-right and right-to-left formatting...). It worked fine, but the wiki did seem overly cluttered for what we needed. I've been much more comfortable tech-wise on the simpler wikis I've played Lexicons (in English) on. PMwiki seems to be a favorite.

    So my advice is to go with Mediawiki if you need a foreign language that's not easily supported by something simpler, and avoid it otherwise.
