\n I'm accustomed to, when running a game at the table, having folks share a book. I run off copies of particularly important passages or rules for reference, and make sure everyone gets at least one copy of those, for ease of play. No controversy there, I imagine.

In the context of GMing an online/PbP game:

What're folks' best practices for sharing rules? I feel uncomfortable putting up the whole PDF on the group gdrive, for obvious reasons: it's a cool game, I'd like to support the creator, etc. I can chop pieces out of the PDF, but by the time I excise all the bits they ought to have access to, if more than they absolutely need, I've given them the majority of the book anyway.

I'm considering using GDrive's Advanced Share option, which lets you prohibit downloading, printing, copying, etc. but... everything can be circumvented.

What have you done, or what would you do?
