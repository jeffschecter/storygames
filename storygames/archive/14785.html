    \n Eons ago (read 1990s), I used to run double and triple blind events at my local convention. Two groups would be in different rooms, competing with one another and in one case, not even knowing the other group existed. We ran a triple blind star trek, double-blind gettsburg, double-blind fantasy, double-blind cyberpunk, the list goes on. The key is to have some kind of tool that allows communication between the rooms. Cellphones are great for this now, but not ubiquitous in 1993.

    We also did two games of fiasco side by side and then reviewed each other's games afterward.

    But not exactly what you are talking about.

    I could see IAWA working this way, where one (random) person rotates out of a table and goes to another at the table, implying the two tables are in the same city. Might require a little fudging of the relationship map here and there.

    \n In college, we played a game of runequest with 2 gms and about 5 players apiece. Games were run at the same time but in adjacent rooms. The world we played in did not rotate and so half of the planet was always light while the other was always dark. Recently, a mysterious wall of fog encircled the border and the peoples of the light and the peoples of the dark each suspected or blamed the other of causing it (in preparation for attack!). Each GM ran a game based on the factions of one side of the world. When you died, you handed in your character sheet and switched to the other game. The two GMs then got together and discussed world level events and AP between sessions.

    \nGame idea: Cops and Robbers

    You play an organized crime syndicate in the style of The Wire. One group is in jail giving orders and receiving gifts and one is in the streets running business. Two players could simply communicate with each other about what to do. You could share a relationship map and trade off months. A third group plays the police, trying to keep up. They could be affected by the bribes you place and have to clean up the crime scenes you create. Once they get close enough the police could call a dual session \u201ccrackdown\u201d. Suddenly the jailed group gets more players.

    You could start with separate relationship maps, but then they start to get more and more connections as the police get informants and such.

    \n In a way (sorta, kinda, if you squint) medium to large larps do this all the time.

    They have multiple sets of things going on, but any given person at any given moment is only really interacting with 1 or 2 of those things.

    The huge difference being that everything is happening as part of the same event at the same time.

    The sense I'm getting from the discussion is that people are interested in asynchronous interaction with the same world at different times.

    Oh - another sort of version of this would be a West Marches type game. Yeah, it's kinda sorta just one campaign, but if the players involved generally fell into a couple groups (like the Thursday adventurers and the Saturday adventurers) then you could end up with the same sort of thing happening. Put out the same hooks for both groups. If one group clears out the Karshok Temple, then the Karshok Temple is cleared.

    And you could run a Super West Marches campaign with some fellow GMs, so that all your stuff was happening in the same world at the same time. Which would be pretty neat.
