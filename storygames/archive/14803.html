    \n I think it would be cool to use the trope of D&D Players Can't Tell Reality From The Game.

    So the playset would include stuff like:

    NEED: to maintain celibacy (because you're a cleric)
    NEED: to cast a spell and jump off the Two Towers
    RELATIONSHIP: alcoholic parent and child
    RELATIONSHIP: teacher and student

    without specifying in the playset whether these things refer to "Debbie" (high school student) or "Black Leaf" (evil wizard's apprentice).
    Tom "Robbie" Hanks or Tom "Pardeux the Cleric" Hanks?

    Let the players sort-out which things are true at which level of fiction,
    or encourage them to play it from a delusional-player's standpoint, where they leave what's real unspecified in play.


    Likewise,
    I'd substitute "Fundamentalist parents" with "NEED: to escape angry religious villagers with pitchforks and torches"-- and leave it ambiguous [in the playset] whether that describes NPCs trying to burn D&D PCs/ or Players' Parents burning all the Monster Manuals at Waldenbooks.
