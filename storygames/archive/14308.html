\n Based on the response I'm getting in this thread there seems to be a pent-up demand for people playtesting games (in addition to the public pitches there are also some more in whispers). I've got some free time and a desire to try to expand the universe of people I've gamed with, so I'm going to try to kill two birds with one stone: Would anyone out there like to playtest some RPGs over Skype with me? It only takes a handful of people getting their schedules lined up to form critical mass for a game. Some people are understandably nervous about gaming with people they haven't met before, or gaming over Skype if they haven't done it before, but I'd encourage people to think of this like a convention game: there's no need for long-term commitments, but it's an opportunity to try a new game and build contacts with people you might like to game with again.

Here are the constraints I'm working with:
I live in the US Pacific time zone, but my schedule is pretty flexible. I have regular commitments every Monday and Thursday night, but I can make a lot of other times work.
I find that three hour sessions over Skype tend to be the best.
There are plenty of games that work fine with just Skype and Googledocs, so I don't like to bother with any virtual tabletops. For games that use dice, we can just read our results off to each other (I personally like the tactile experience of rolling real dice in games that use them).
I'm planning to record the sessions so I can make the audio available to the designer, since it's potentially very useful playtest info.
I'd like to have the option of podcasting the session, but won't do it if any participants are uncomfortable with the idea.

If this sounds like something you'd like to try, let me know (either with a public reply, a whisper, or e-mail to dan.maruschak at gmail.com), and let me know what kind of schedule availability you have (please be specific) so I can try to get a session organized. I'm assuming that I'll take the GM or facilitator role, but if someone else has a game they want to run I'd be open to considering it.
