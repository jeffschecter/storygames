    \nR276: Contract Work; "Heavy Hitters". See R191. Saturday, 2:00PM - 6:00PM; One Round.
    \n
    \nD919: The Independent Publishing POTLUCK FOOD FEST!. Come and join this POTLUCK FOOD FEST where you can meet game designers, or just hangout with fans of Indie games. Plenty of good times and good people! Saturday, 6:00PM - 8:00PM; One Session; Fun, All Ages.
    \n
    \nD921: Breaking into the Gaming Industry. Join our panel of experienced gaming industry mavens as they explain how things look in 2007 and how you can get your game from concept to market. Various company representatives will be on hand to compare points of view. Saturday, 7:00PM - 8:00PM; One Session; Serious, All Ages.
    \n
    \nD922: Women in Gaming. Where did all these female gamers come from? Gaming used to be (mostly) a guy thing. How did that change, and what do women bring to the table, so to speak, that men might not? Saturday, 8:00PM - 9:00PM; One Session; Serious, All Ages.
    \n
    \nD923: Podcasting. Join Perfect Score Productions for a discussion about podcasting. This talented crew will take you through the ins and outs of starting your own, and talk about their experiences. Saturday, 9:00PM - 10:00PM; One Session; Serious, All Ages.
    \n
    \nD926: The Independent Publishing Roundtable. Everything you ever wanted to know about designing, publishing, and promoting your own role playing game, but were afraid to ask. Come join us and we'll discuss everything from game design goals, play testing, printing techniques and cutting-edge theory to sales and marketing. Creating your own RPG has never been easier than it is right now! Fresh faces and old hands equally welcome. Sunday, 10:00AM - 12:00PM; One Session; Serious, All Ages.

    \nI may also play a pickup game of WEGS and/or Spirit of the Century.

    \n Here's what I just signed up for:

    R240: 1st Quest; "Dragon Knights" by Judd Karlman. An INDEPENDENTLY PUBLISHED GAME from Adept Press - Part of the Indie Games Explosion! A child from mundane earth, his best friend, a dragon hatchling, a wizard's apprentice and a fallen knight must save the world as dragons re-awaken. Friday, 8:00PM - 12:00AM

    R265: Carry; "The Burden of War" by Nathan Paoletta. An INDEPENDENTLY PUBLISHED GAME from Hamsterprophet Productions - Part of the Indie Games Explosion! This is a short-form game that follows the story of a squad of U.S. Marines in the Vietnam war. The players take on the roles of soldiers from this squad, and create the weights and issues that they bring with them into the war. As the unit falls apart under both external attack and internal strife, how will you shape the legacy and memories that you will leave behind? Saturday, 9:00AM - 1:00PM

    R281: Sorceror; "Dictionary of Mu" by Judd Karlman. An INDEPENDENTLY PUBLISHED GAME from Adept Press - Part of the Indie Games Explosion! Imagine you could summon a piece of history in a dark world where the dunes are stained red with the blood of past slaughters. Imagine it could give you power and glory. Imagine it wanted something in return. How far would you go? Are you a hero who will cultivate the hope of a dying land, or a villain who will choke out its last dying breaths? Come play this Sorceror mini-supplement inspired by equal parts Howard's Conan, Burroughs' Barsoom and the Bible! Saturday, 2:00PM - 6:00PM

    R298: Spirit of the Century; "Rex Rich and the Warlord of Mars" by Bill White. An INDEPENDENTLY PUBLISHED GAME from Evil Hat Productions - Part of the Indie Games Explosion! In our last episode, wealthy aviation entrepreneur Rex Rich discovered a strange alien artifact amid the lunar ruins where Rex landed in a rocket built by teen rocket scientist Snap Redstone. Now back on Earth, the fluted silver torus is attracting unwanted attention from Rex's old enemies the Futurians and their new bug-eyed allies. Will Rex and his friends be able to unravel the mystery of the artifact before it's too late? Saturday, 8:00PM - 12:00AM

    D926: The Independent Publishing Roundtable. Everything you ever wanted to know about designing, publishing, and promoting your own role playing game, but were afraid to ask. Come join us and we'll discuss everything from game design goals, play testing, printing techniques and cutting-edge theory to sales and marketing. Creating your own RPG has never been easier than it is right now! Fresh faces and old hands equally welcome. Sunday, 10:00AM - 12:00PM

    \n Okay, cool. Here's what I'm running:
    \n
    \nR179: Unistat System; "Dungeons & Dragons - Unistat Style" by Andrew Morris. An INDEPENDENTLY PUBLISHED GAME from Plague Monkey Games - Part of the Indie Games Explosion! Unlike games that promise both dragons and dungeons but never deliver, this game will have at least one dungeon and one dragon. Everything else is up to you. And yes, you'll be cool enough to kill the dragon. Or befriend it. Or enslave it. Or whatever you want. Wednesday, 10:00PM - 2:00AM; One Round; All Materials Provided. Beginners Welcome; Fun, All Ages.
    \n
    \nR196: Unistat System; "Out in the Black" by Andrew Morris. An INDEPENDENTLY PUBLISHED GAME from Plague Monkey Games - Part of the Indie Games Explosion! Adventure around the 'verse as characters from Joss Whedon's Firefly series. Familiarity with the series is recommended but not required. Note: the Unistat System will be used, not the published Serenity RPG. Thursday, 2:00PM - 6:00PM; One Round; All Materials Provided. Beginners Welcome; Serious, All Ages. See Also: R234.
    \n
    \nR205: Unistat System; "Rhadamani Steel" by Andrew Morris. An INDEPENDENTLY PUBLISHED GAME from Plague Monkey Games - Part of the Indie Games Explosion! A serpentine blade, an empire that must be defended, an enemy that cannot be stopped. Stand your ground, blademaster...you are all that is left. Thursday, 8:00PM - 12:00AM; One Round; All Materials Provided. Beginners Welcome; Serious, 18 & Over ONLY.
    \n
    \nR234: Unistat System; "Out in the Black". See R196. Friday, 2:00PM - 6:00PM; One Round.
    \n
    \nL156: "The Anvil of Creation" by Andrew Morris. A DEXCON 10 EXCLUSIVE! This is a heavily social and politcal game, based loosely on the Sci-Fi Channel series, "The Lost Room." Players take on the roles of Collectors, people who have found objects with strange powers. These items are known as artifacts. The last leader of the collectors has died, and the powerful artifact he possesed - the Anvil of Creation - has yet to be found. Some want to solve the mystery of his death, others want to conceal it. Some just want to take his position as leader of the Collectors, while others have more sinister motives. Saturday, 9:00AM - 1:00PM; One Round; All Materials Provided. Beginners Welcome; Serious, 18 & Over ONLY.
    \n
    \nR326: Unistat System; "Sunday Burnout" by Andrew Morris. An INDEPENDENTLY PUBLISHED GAME from Plague Monkey Games - Part of the Indie Games Explosion! You're tired, worn out, drained of creativity...but you still want to get some awesome game on, right? Sign up for this session of Unistat, and we'll muster up our collective strength and tell an exciting story with cool characters doing amazing things. Convert your end-of-con weariness into a blast of gaming happiness to take with your on your trip home. Sunday, 2:00PM - 6:00PM; One Round; All Materials Provided. Beginners Welcome; Fun, All Ages.
    \n
    \nAnd here's what I'm playing for sure:
    \n
    \nR182: Capes; "Nobody Can Save You Now!" by Tony Lower-Basch. An INDEPENDENTLY PUBLISHED GAME from Muse of Fire Studio - Part of the Indie Games Explosion! A malevolent villain has a plot to hold all of Boost City for ransom! The superheroic defenders of the city have the power to stop him, but can they overcome the conflicts within their own team in order to work together? Who is this villain, who are the heroes, what are their problems and what are their strengths? You must decide, and live with your decisions. Will Boost City survive? Thursday, 9:00AM - 1:00PM; One Round; All Materials Provided. Beginners Welcome; Fun, Under 18 Requires Parental Clearance. See Also: R225.
    \n
    \nB475: The Double Exposure $100 Summer Spades Championship. See B363. Thursday, 12:00AM - 2:00AM; Session 1B of 4. See Also: B638, B686. Next Session: B737.
    \n
    \nR290: Capes; "Adventure Lies Ahead...And Down!" by Tony Lower-Basch. An INDEPENDENTLY PUBLISHED GAME from Muse of Fire Studio - Part of the Indie Games Explosion! Take the over-the-top Capes! system into the depths of a fantasy dungeon. Assault the evil legions of Harro the Black, before he manages to resurrect the dried bones of the ancient Dragon God and cause a new Age of Chaos! Saturday, 8:00PM - 12:00AM; One Round; All Materials Provided. Beginners Welcome; Fun, Under 18 Requires Parental Clearance.
    \n
    \nB820: The Double Exposure $250 David Stern Memorial Summer Poker Championship. Double Exposure's traditional Poker tournament. Played as No Limit Texas Hold 'Em, tables will be pared down as necessary. The grand prize is a $250 Gift Certificate for the DEXCON 10 Dealers' Room! NOTE: THERE IS NO GAMBLING AT DEXCON 10; ALL TRANSACTIONS ARE IN CHIPS WITH NO VALUE OUTSIDE OF THE GAME. Saturday, 12:00AM - 4:00AM; One Session; All Materials Provided. Knowledge Necessary; Serious, 18 & Over ONLY.
