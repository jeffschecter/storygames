\n I have posted a report of a solo playtest of Pocket Danger Patrol on my blog, The Stronghold Rebuilt.

I ran two characters, and used a combination of the Threat-O-Matic and the Mythic GME to create the initial stuff. Other questions pretty much answered themselves as the game played, making it very suitable for solo play, if lacking the fertile imaginations you get with multiple players.

A few comments and questions:

I assume that the scope of a Complication is determined on a common-sense basis. In my game I assigned them to a Threat (so any attack on the Threat picked up the dice), a character (so anything the character did got the dice) or an arbitrary area (so, in my game, only actions taken outside the Mansion were affected by The Storm). I also moved a complication that one character had onto a Threat when that character was taken out by the Threat.

I assumed that if a Complication took less than three hits they were ignored and didn't accumulate as bonus dice like they do on a Threat.

I also assumed that all Complications ceased to exist once all Threats were eliminated, although they could provide extra difficulty or colour for a following scene.

On to Abilities. Can a character attempt an action with an Ability in which they have a score of zero? I assumed that they could - it's still worth doing to get the damage bonus for a weakness off any other dice you roll.

Finally I used a protected threat - one you could only attack once one or more other threats were eliminated. I didn't seem to be a game-breaker. The miniature gamer in me can almost see a points system for Threats developing, with Threats with countdowns or that are protected costing more than basic threats
