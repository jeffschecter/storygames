\n There's been some talk about highlighted stats and how they're kind of like Keys.

And, yeah, they're *kind of* like Keys, you know, if you squint. But they're pretty different, too.

* Players don't choose their own highlighted stats.
* They change from session to session, NOT based on in-game choices (e.g. the Buyoff).
* They're much more general (types of actions), not specifics (like when you have Key of Love for Lady Blackbird, or want to get married, or whatever)

I've played a bit of Apocalypse World, and I can see that, yeah, in some situations, you have a certain stat highlighted, so you might be more tempted to do a certain thing. "Ok, I WILL use this move this time around, because I have it highlighted. I could use that extra advance."

But what about from the MC's side of the screen?

Keys allow me, as GM/MC to prepare specific situations and make cool stuff and interesting choices pop up in play. I even have my systematic method for generating entire scenes simply from a list of players' Keys.

When you MC, do you keep track of highlights and somehow use that to inform the game? If so, how? What kinds of choices have you made based around highlights?

Or is it just on the player's side that they come into play? (There is nothing in the AW book, after all, which suggest the MC should ever even consider the players' highlighted stats when choosing moves, etc.)

    Posted By: JuddI don't pay attention to the players' character sheets at all except for the NPC's and places they provide for me for my MC's moves.
    \nDitto. I've run both AW (a lot) & DW (once), and I've never thought a bit about the highlighted stats once I've highlighted them. To my mind, they are highlighted specifically so the player is tempted to take action using moves that use those stats. Highlighting them is more than enough influence on the situation; anything else is a waste and heavy-handed to boot.

    People talk about how they're like Keys, but I think they're much more like earning tests in Burning Wheel: you take action, you get experience. In BW this can happen with any skill, depending on the test you need, and in AW/DW this can happen with any stat, depending on which ones get highlighted that session. Now obviously the advancement & reward cycles in BW & AW/DW are like night and day different, but in this one respect how you earn "experience" is very similar. It's tied to taking action. I suppose that's true with Keys as well, but Keys narrow it down, fictionally, quite a bit more.

    \n Keeping track of highlighted stats in AW works a bit differently than in DW, because each stat is associated with a certain type of activity, whereas in DW activities are split between stats. Additionally, highlighted stats do not change character goals, so they do not indicate what characters are going to do just how they are going to go about doing it.

    Since activities are split between stats in DW they can indicate which characters are going to be rutted. For example a character with strength and dexterity highlighted would be at a disadvantage compared to someone with strength and wisdom, generally speaking.

    \n I've been thinking about the highlighted stats lately. One nice thing about them is that the group can act as a moderating factor on characters who are advancing faster than others. If the fighter has STR and DEX highlighted, they will likely power through the XP and advance faster than the rest of the party. If they get a level ahead of the other players, the party can highlight WIS and CHA next session. It doesn't nerf the character's effectiveness but it does (likely) change the rate of XP gain and encourage the player to make use of their spotlight time differently.

    Thinking also of my long wait for Diablo 3, it would be awesome to have an "XP Shrine" in the dungeon with a custom move that let you highlight an additional stat for the rest of the session. In fact, if I do another Dungeon Starter, I think I'm going to call it "D is for Diablo" and do a Diablo hack...

    \n That highlighted stats in Apocalypse World are very different from Keys in Solar System/TSoY from the MC/SG point of view follows from the fact that the AW Master of Ceremonies role is completely unlike the SS Story Guide role, and one point where the two especially differ is preparation: the Story Guide prepares for each session of the game during the downtime, while the Master of Ceremonies only goes through a prep process once for the whole game campaign (a process which is 2/3 play and 1/3 pre/after-play prep: compiling Fronts).
    Thus, it would be an unfair expectation if we demanded that highlighted Stats, like Keys, help the MC through a kind of session-prepping process which MCs aren't supposed to engage in. But, if we really try to read AW as a whole through the lens of a SS/TSoY metaphor, in actuality they do: in "Key" terms all AW PCs have the same set Keys assigned to them (the Key of Cool, the Key of Hard, and so on), though not all of them will be active at the same time during play, and as a MC you plan your game (campaign) so that it hits them all.

    \n I like to have little arty cards or even just simple post-its on bold display in front of each player. These show the characters name, a picture (if they have gone to the trouble of making / finding one) and the two highlighted stats for the session. Like HOT! or COOL! or WEIRD! in brightly coloured tabs.
    \n
    \nOn the laminated move sheets I also have the stats particular to each move highlighted and (colour co-ordinated too)
    \nHOT - Red
    \nHARD - Yellow
    \nCOOL - Blue
    \nWEIRD - Green
    \nSHARP - Purple
    \nThat way its a rather obvious reminder to me as MC (and the other players) as to what stats (and therefore moves) could trigger experience. Not that I play to 'put them in a spot' which would likely cause the use of a highlighted stat, but maybe I think about it....

    \n I'm not getting how they're like fanmail. Fanmail is a reward for what you already did. Higlighting stats is the potential for a reward, should you choose to do what I wanted. Also, once you've highlighted my stat, I can use that stat and gain my XP as many times this session as I want (assuming I can justify it within the fiction), but you can decide not to give me fanmail for doing a behavior that got me fanmail from you before. Did I just make the same point twice? Almost. But the point itself stands: I can't really see how fanmail is even close to being as similar to Highlights as Keys are. I mean, Keys are the first thing that popped to mind when I originally read about highlighted stats, simply because certain types of behaviors are governed by certain stats, and only those types of behaviors will net you XP (for this session, at least).
