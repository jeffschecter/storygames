    \n\nAugust 2010 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n Lots of GC goodness this year - my fifth one, holy crap! Jared told me once that odd years are better than even ones, and this has def held true for me. The general energy this year was much more...mature, I think is the best way to put it. People were there to have a good time, spend money and play games. Things felt more respectful and polite than I have been accustomed to expecting. I had very few "OMG WHAT IS WRONG WITH YOU" moments, and managed to eat much better, due to a combination of going farther afield for food and a couple of new/newish places right by the convention center. I also went to sleep when I was tired, which made everything else better.

    I met a lot of cool new people this year! Both meet-the-real-person-behind-the-internet-handle, and meet-a-totally-new-person.

    Gamewise, I played a lot! Board/card games: finally played Dominion, liked it but it's not a must-buy for me. Got a bunch of board game demos and made my Gen Con purchase of Infinite Cities. Talked up and played Joe Prince's Pro Wrasslin' Battle Royale card game, and figured out how to fix it for a group of 8 (elimination match, not one-pin win!). Played some other stuff with some folks.

    RPGwise, I played in a really solid game of The Dance and the Dawn, ran a small game of Dread House, and facilitated a good "this-is-how-this-game-works" short game of Remember Tomorrow, all at Games on Demand. I played Eppy's forthcoming Swords Without Master, which was tots rad and you should buy it when it comes out. And the highlight was absolutely the aforementioned Mountain Witch/Annalise mashup. It was one of the best games of Annalise I've played, and probably the best one-session game of it, by far. So that was really nice.

    Design Matters had our best year ever (still finishing the paperwork, but damn!).

    Overall, I'm very pleased, just tiiiiired and still recovering from the pre-show-prep/travel/show/travel/post-show cycle.
