\n Aiming to host a Halloween party later this month, I thought incorporating a LARP would be novel (and maybe turn some of my non-gaming friends onto the idea more regularly). I was going to use the straightforward zombie-themed game Shelter In Place, but unfortunately on review I doubt it'll work in the space restrictions of my home. I'd appreciate suggestions.

Criteria:
*Succinct, I've not much time to study it.
*Thematically casual, though still horror-themed.
*Comparatively fast-play, no more than a couple hours.
*No more than three modoset-sized rooms required.
*Roles for 6-12 players.

    \n Wouldn't a larpified Werewolf game fit the bill rather precisely, it being conventionally horror-themed and all? It's good in that the base concept is tabletop, so you have quite a lot of flexibility in choosing how much to "garnish" it with larp elements like werewolf masks and whatnot. It's also simple enough to hack it together for a casual use like this. The game being a robust little logic exercise at heart doesn't hurt, either - it can accomodate both thespian types and people who want the game to have a bit of strategy to it.

    \nThat's not a bad idea, except it just so happens some of the people I might invite have specifically mastered Werewolf to the point of playing it like speed-Chess. And come to think of it, I don't relish the player-elimination aspect.

    Your suggestion does however spark a notion of mixing elements from Shelter In Place with Werewolf for a genteel parlor game where the setting is a gilded-age banquet where one of the guests is secretly a vampire. The vampire's goal is to isolate other guests and turn them, the humans' goal is to figure out who the vampire is. Use a simple resolution system so confrontations take a bit of manuevering, add a layer of back-stabbing driven by social ambition and it might just do.

    \n Werewolf/Mafia has issues (at least for me) in terms of how player elimination works, and no real "roleplaying" elements. (Some people manage to pull some in, but they work at odds with the procedures of the game.)

    However, there is a small group boardgame version of Mafia called "The Resistance", which solves the first issue (no players get eliminated). Recasting it as a LARP (with, for example, the chosen group going into a separate room) could bring in some roleplaying elements and potentially make for a really interesting experience.
