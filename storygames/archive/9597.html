\n So this morning when I came into work I was talking with a co-worker about my reviews of some Call of Cthulhu RPG supplements. At some point the conversation turned to the apparent remake of the 80s Cold War-paranoia movie Red Dawn and the question of who would be the enemy in the remake (since Russia/Soviets teaming up with South Americans really doesn't make sense any more).

I Twittered about the conversation and several people thought I was talking about a Cthulhu/Red Dawn mashup, which got me thinking, how would I do that? I keep imagining teens with mullets wearing letterman jackets and firing AK-47s at Cthulhu while shouting "Woverines!"

So what kinds of elements would be in a mashup like this? Any thoughts?

    \n Camp is fine, in small doses. But I'll remind folks that (a) Red Dawn was FAR from intentionally silly (Patrick Swayze took three more years to become utterly silly, with Dirty Dancing, and thereby back-port silliness to all of his earlier works) and (b) HP Lovecraft didn't laugh at all, after about 1930. Army of Darkness got its camp from a legacy of silly horror flicks and the budget of a porn flick. I see no parallels.

    But it's your fun to have, so go get campy if ya feel the need! Me, I'm sticking with a Grey Ranks port and a box of Kleenex. Russian-backed Cubans just don't compare, for outright terror and horror, to Nyralathotep feeding on fresh teenager brains....

    \n 80s-centric imaages = things to which the characters cling that somehow get them through the (bump in the) night. The harder or more risky it is to hold on to, the greater the benefit. Lettermen jackets stand out, as do mullets (maybe the norm is to be totally unkempt, or have a shaved head, and what do you do when the last town barber gets eaten by Nyarlathotep?). The first person to shout "Wolverines!" in a scene gets a bonus (and the attention of more gaping eyes and groping tentacles). It has to be obvious. So waving a tattered US flag, yes, clutching your dead boyfriend's class ring, not so much. Now, brandishing the ring...
