    \n Adam is right that I should give a list of what I think is the difference - and upon reflection, I honestly can't give one that works for me! AD&D 2E was the second game I played upon being exposed to RPGs at all, and I had little game experiences to then go on, nor a sense of what the game was like outside of my own gaming group.

    But what the hell. Here's a few things:

    - lots of undiscovered crunchy corners with fictional consequences

    - discovering "new" tactics for ourselves for the first time, e.g. fun with greek fire, narrow hallways and a very fast thief as bait

    - a DM that really wanted to recreate this awesome novel he wanted to write (but to his credit: the game was like sandbox + threads in the best way, rather than a railroad)

    - players that were driven by recreating an awesome character from that novel they read

    On the latter two points: I definitely see that aspirations towards genre fantasy is part of what's going on. Perhaps part of this is starting with a bit more setting prep, rather than going in from general points of light. The DM and/or group should come in with a fleshed out world with an epic Front somewhere in the background, with a few Heritage moves to match perhaps.

    \n I started playing D&D; during the 2E era (let's see, 1994 and I was 10) and only later got to play 1E under an older DM. Also, I was introduced to the Dragonlance novels at the same time as D&D.; My experience was that the art, the DM advice, the settings, and the novels of the 2E era encouraged an epic, high-fantasy type of game in which the PCs were heroes who would save the world. Those were the kinds of stories my friends and I were trying to create in junior high. But the nature of the system (actually changed very little from 1E) and our general juvenility conspired to wreck most of those stories before they came to fruition. Only later on did I get to play an old school game and understand what D&D; was like before 2E and Dragonlance and us kids came along.
    \n
    \nThere is a lot more I could say about the differences between 1E and 2E, but instead I'll just comment that Tony DiTerlizzi is still my favorite D&D; artist ever.

    \n You know, I bought a bunch of the 2E books and really liked how they refined it from 1E, but I don't believe I ever actually played a game with that edition. We did play a few games of Planescape but I'm pretty sure we were using 1E rules.

    Anyhow, I just want to third or fourth character kits as an important part of what 2E was, and I wouldn't even bother to chime in just for that, except that I want to point out that I feel the mini-playbook approach that Sage and Adam have already begun is very much in the spirit of character kits. You're still playing whatever class you started as, but with the mini playbook (character kit) you get access to a few extra moves (nonweapon proficiencies and such) that add color and distinctiveness to your character. They're a good way to evoke a different setting or culture without having to go to the trouble of creating a whole new class playbook from the ground up.
