6-Bang, Appendix to Power 19

Josh Roby
March 2006 edited March 2006 in Story Games
Okay, so Andy gave words to what has been bothering me:
I'm wondering if I should work with Tony to maybe make another Power X, where the questions and responses are much more open to outsider feedback and could generate more interest. Cause, in most cases (even my own!), people post the Power 19 because they want to talk about their game. But the Power 19 are probably a bad tool for this, because they're all show and tell, and little on discussion-generating elements.

I do love me the Power 19, but mostly for my own purposes -- it helps me understand my own design, and figure out where I want to go with it. It's pretty tepid, though, as a conversation starter.

But there are always open questions on any design, and designers always have niggling questions that they want to answer, and those can lead to some constructive conversation. What I'm wondering is if there are a handful (say, 6) of types of questions that can prompt that discussion.

I'm thinking things like:
1) Ask a question about the game's currency.
2) Ask a question about packaging and marketing.
3) Ask a question about player investment in the events of the game.

So were I doing this for, say, Web of Shadows, I'd ask:
1) Is 10 tokens too many for a full pool, or will players spend their pools down into the 4-7 range and then stay there for most of the game anyway?
2) How can I possibly package this monstrosity made of strings and index cards?
3) With players playing a different character each scene, will they really get invested in the game, or will it be a mental exercise for them?
And so on.

So I open it up to the noble minds here: what kinds of questions are that extra 'oomph' step into discussion?

    Power 19 talk about your firm ideas and strong parts of the game. While discussion appears when you talk about the fuzzy parts and points not yet solved.

    Power 19 is the first exercise, but then you need to have a look at the whole thing from some distance to detect the holes in the tapestry and expose them to public discussion.

    Perhaps questions like:
    Which is the rule-set I hesitated more before including it in the game? Why?
    Which part of the currency economy I untrust more?
    I would like the players to do this and that... which are the rules I think may not help or even be a little hindrance for them?

    It is hard to think on this terms about your creation, but you need to know which are the weakest points to work on them.
