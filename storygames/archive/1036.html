    Meg,

    The game was a lot of fun. The thing I found most fascinating about this game is that all of the actual mechanics take place within the story, and there are post-story effects on the Courtiers. Pre-story, it's just narration. The actual characters, the Courtiers, are a kind of interlude between the very involved and emotionally intense stories. Pretty cool.

    The stories we made were excellent, and definitely seemed like they came right out of a storybook. It's amazing how easy it is to create one of these, and it is sort of a collaborative synthesis of every story the players at the table have ever heard.

    It was really fun, and made stories that were interesting not just to look back on, but to be involved in. It has the same effect Dogs has: everyone knows the Arabian Nights stories, so it's easy to know what you should be doing and "buy in."

    I would have liked to play longer, to get a better feel for the court scenes and the interactions there; I don't think we played with those as much as we might have with more time. Though it was totally fun even with the court being minor...and maybe it's never really as important as the stories within the story?

    I think that extra layer helped a lot to free our creativity. The stories don't directly affect the PCs so there was less shyness. Now, of course, it's silly that we should need that extra layer. We were still affecting the sub-story characters and our main PCs are equally fictional...but psychologically there's a difference. Maybe because there's less investment in the sub-story characters, I think, but it's also probably just gamer baggage: Oh no! don't dare to affect someone else's holy, enshrined character through - gasp - PLAY!

    Anyway, what I'm saying (again) is that it's a good game.
