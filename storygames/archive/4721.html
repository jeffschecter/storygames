\n Just an brief initial idea at the moment, but I'll welcome some ideas to be thrown into the pot.

You're an ordinary Christian, faced with being true to your faith, while life throws temptations at you. In Send Your Light, you must cope with these temptations, without straying along the path to sin.

Send Your Light is a storytelling game consisting of a 55-card deck, comprising Character Cards and Temptation Cards. The finished product will be sold online and in Christian stores across the country.

It's not an entirely stupid idea, of course. Christian products sell very well and "Christian story game" isn't a bad niche to occupy.

And, well, Joe did ask for "alternatives to a standardized and mundane marketing and publishing market", so it's his fault.

    \n With a 110 card deck, you'll be able to move into a higher price point and WWJD?/SyL will be a physically larger boxed game, moving it away from the idea that 55 card decks should be priced around the same price point as a deck of regular playing cards. (I'm not certain if this is a concern in Christian stores.) Also with a 110 card deck, there are additional cards for more variety in play. The downside with the larger deck (and indeed, any game with cards) is you're going to have to hire an artist to create artwork for all those cards -- this will increase your manufacturing costs. A way to produce WWJD?/SyL cheaper is to reuse card art on several cards, much like how Bang! reuses art on Missed! cards, which makes up the bulk of the game's deck.

A way to produce WWJD?/SyL cheaper is to reuse card art on several cards, much like how Bang! reuses art on Missed! cards, which makes up the bulk of the game's deck.
    An alternate approach to take on artwork would be to do what the sort-of collectible card game Mystic Domination did: they used images of classic artwork from the public domain. Since there are centuries and centuries of Christian-themed artwork out there, that could save some money. Of course, you'd still need art direction and layout, but there should still be some savings.
