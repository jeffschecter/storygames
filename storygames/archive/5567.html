    Wow, those numbers are pretty interesting. Similarly, I had the following results on a couple of reviews written within a few weeks of each other:
    RPG.net - 12/31/07 - 1140 reads
    theRPGsite - 1/22/08 - 191 reads

    I guess that it's a question of what those 'reads' mean to a publisher. If one in 200 readers bought my book (and yeah, I'm totally pulling that number out of thin air), then the RPG.net review netted me 5 or 6 sales, whereas the Pundit review scored me a single copy.

    Wait! Actually, I can just look at my Lulu sales for the two-week period immediately following those reviews:
    12/31/07 - 1/14/08: 12 sales
    1/22/08 - 2/5/08: 8 sales

    Okay, I have no idea if there's any correlation at all here. Damn, I'd love to know how many people read the review then bought the copy of the book. Mainly because the Pundit review required me to print a copy from Lulu and ship it to South America (pricey).

    You know, this thread is weaving like a pop starlet on her first day out of rehab. And I'm to blame. But what the hell.

    I guess I'm interested in the mechanics and the process of game reviews because it's a relatively inexpensive way of getting some good advertising for your game. You spend a hundred bucks on printing and shipping, and you can reach a lot of people, even if you work with Lulu (and therefore spend five to fifteen dollars, or more, on the physical book when you order a copy for yourself).

    Seems like RPG.net is consistently a good way to reach a large audience, but I'm really curious about overlap. Is the Gaming Report audience just a smaller subset of the RPG.net crowd, or is it a different animal altogether? I know that theRPGsite (or is it "the theRPGsite"?) crew consists of numerous exiles from RPG.net, so I know that the overlap isn't one hundred percent there.
