\n
\n I was waiting for the bus with my girlfriend, and as she laughed I noticed she had a bit of a bump around her gums right where vampire fangs would come out, so I asked if she were one. She responded by saying she eats garlic, has a reflection and goes out in the day. I pointed out that vampires would naturally want to spread as much misinformation as possible about their weaknesses, so naturally anything covered in stories would be untrue. So she threw out that vampires are killed by olives (she's quite seriously allergic to them).

I figured that would make a really cool take on vampires. Leave that they suck blood. Ditch all the other stuff (because it's all some variation or other on the traditional beliefs, some movies say they're not allergic to garlic, others say they are, some say silver does nothing, others say wood does nothing, etc. they all though stick around with one thing or another that still works - usually sunlight). Having to identify vampires by them not eating mediterannean food, using palmolive soap, etc. would be quite the interesting change to a supernatural investigation game.

    \n Years ago I did a paper on Vampire mythologies and found some of the most interesting vampire myths never made it to Hollywood. For example, one superstition said if you drop mustard seeds while being pursued by a vampire, the vamp had to stop and count all of the seeds before s/he could continue pursuit. Like an undead OCD.

    A sprig of Hawthorne had pretty much the same effect. Drop one on the ground and the vamp had to stop and pick it up, buying you a few valuable seconds.
