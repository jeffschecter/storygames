\n I am fascinated by Scientology, but not enough to join those crazy kids and their hijinks.

There are many similarities to it and the world's most popular fantasy role-playing game(Wizard & Warlocks).
What would've L. Ron thought of E. Gary?

I could see it like a strange Pendragon-like game, but instead of medieval glory, you acquire greater modern celebrity.

    \n We had a great PTA game where the TV show was all about a cult ("The Apex Foundation") and we played various members / prospective members of the cult. I played an outsider / blogger / investigative journalist who wanted to get herself recruited so she could write a tell-all from the inside but ended up going super native. One of us played the son of the guy who started the cult, another played one of the psychologists that did the recruiting, etc.

    I think PTA was great for helping us frame how we thought about the cult in an arc. We never did get to finish that campaign for real life reasons, but I loved where it was going.

    Point is, Scientology is great color/theming you can apply to many games, but it'll work particularly well in ones that support story-focused play. After all, it's not about the Thetans, it's about the crazy people that believe in them.
