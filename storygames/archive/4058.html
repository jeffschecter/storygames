\n I'm going to try out TSoY with some friends. Looking over the Keys, it seems like some would be easier to bring into play than others. For example, if you have Keys pointing to another PC, opportunities for those will occur more 'organically' than if they pointed to an NPC ('How does Helmutt's boyfriend show up in every other scene? Doesn't he have a day job back at the palace?"). Does the game lend itself to easy balance, or is it an effort to make sure people get chances to express their Keys and thereby advance?

    \n You definitely have to work at it as a group - Keys you can trigger yourself are no problem, but those requiring specific circumstances or actions by others are harder. Reviewing everybody's Keys before play helps, and so does some transparency about player goals. "I'm really looking for a chance to buy of Conscience" is useful information to share. The GM should be working to incorporate Key-rific situations for everyone, every session, too. I usually try to pick one for each player, maybe one that has been a little neglected or is hard to hit.

    \n My experiences seem to suggest that it can be important to have a healthy "diet" of Keys that draws from all Four Key Groups:

    - Keys you can hit yourself, whenever you decide to.
    - Keys that point at other PCs.
    - Keys that point at major secondary characters, factions, or objectives.
    - Keys that only come up occasionally.

    Of these, I think the players hold most of the responsibility for the first two, while the GM can choose to focus more on the last two. This divvies up the responsibility for hitting Keys into something that's more manageable.
