Prince of Darkness Games is conjunction with Boxninja is proud to announce that Hell 4 Leather is now available!



A RPG of Bloody Revenge on Devil\u2019s Night!

Inspired by tales of revenge as diverse as: Hamlet, Kill Bill, The Crow and with a hint of Ghost Rider \u2013 Hell 4 Leather roars like a motorbike on full throttle.

The game features a liberating style of scene-based free-style role-playing brought to a focus with decisive card mechanics.

Tarot Cards are used by The Fool, a vengeful revenant intent on killing off the rest of the Devil's Dozen before the rooster crows.

Major Arcana denote the Major Characters, the Scenes to play, and the various Boons that advantage both The Fool and those who had him killed.

An awesome layout by Boxninja's Gregor Hutton (3:16) makes this game a thing of true beauty.

Hell 4 Leather available now from PoD Games!

Players: 3 \u2013 6 (No GM required)
Playing Time: 2 \u2013 4 hours
System: Tarot Deck (playing cards can be used as a substitute)
Genre: Revenge / Outlaw Bikers / Occult
Format: Full Colour 6 Page booklet
Price: \xa36.66 (full colour booklet and PDF).

Hell 4 Leather somehow won the \u201cTwo Games One Name\u201d competition. A fun-filled extravaganza organized by Mr Nathan D Paoletta (hamsterprophet.wordpress.com/).
H4L was paired with Sebastian Hickey\u2018s Hell For Leather \u2013 a game where murder equals extra dice! (http://www.cobwebgames.com)

So why should you, the avid storygamer care about H4L?
Well there are some interesting card mechanics which don't feature in many RPGs - if you liked the rhine cards in Conspiracy X, you'll get a kick out of H4L's death checks!
It's a pretty unusual format - everything you need on 6 (full colour) pages.
Also possibly the most hippy way of resolving minor conflicts ever...
Go on go on go on.

Cheers for looking - please share any questions you have.
Joe

    \n Actually, Hell for Leather and Hell Bent for Leather are completely different phrases with very different meanings. The former means a long, arduous journey, as in 'it's been hell on the leather in the soles of my shoes', therefore it's 'hell for leather'. On the other hand, 'Hell Bent for Leather' in the old West, described an animal that had become more trouble than it was worth, and was a hair's breadth away from being killed for meat and raw materials, hence it was 'hell bent on becoming leather'. The latter phrase appears in the song 'Rawhide', incidentally.
