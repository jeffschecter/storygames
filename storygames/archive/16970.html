\n I am trying to compile a reading list of awesome books to inspire me and help me run even better games. I am really looking for books which are about deconstructing stories, theatre, drama, psychology etc. rather than traditional gaming books or novels.

My current To Read List looks like this:

1. Joseph Campbell - The Hero with 1000 Faces
2. Christopher Vogler - Writer's Journey: Mythic Structure for Writers
3. Augusto Boal - The Rainbow of Desire: The Boal Method of Theatre and Therapy
4. Clarissa Pinkola Estes - Women Who Run With Wolves
5. Daniel Goleman - Emotional Intelligence

I haven't even started reading most of these but a number of these come highly recommended so I know they are going to be good.

Just wondered if anyone else could recommend me something in the same sort of style?

Thank you!

    \n I'm an actor and the this is the best acting book I've ever read:

    http://www.amazon.com/Practical-Handbook-Actor-Melissa-Bruder/dp/0394744128/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&qid=1343931521&sr=8-1&keywords=practical+handbook+for+the+actor

    It's written by six students who studied with David Mamet and WIlliam H. Macy back in the 80's. I could see this challenging a lot of ideas about role playing in general. The book calls for actors to only focus on simple actions and everything else should be disregarded (i.e. the "how" of acting - character etc). I don't agree with 100% of what is said but still think this is probably the clearest and most helpful acting book I've read.

    A similar book is "True and False" by David Mamet:

    http://www.amazon.com/True-False-Heresy-Common-Sense/dp/0679772642/ref=sr_1_4?ie=UTF8&qid=1343932130&sr=8-4&keywords=david+mamet
