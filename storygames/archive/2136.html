    It's hard to "cannibalize" because it's a whole new flavor of the game, but my favorite d20 stuff in the last few years has been Blue Rose/True20 and Iron Heroes (the latter because of all the classes and their cool combat-focused abilities). Iron Heroes makes d20 into a very tactical Feng Shui game ("I climb that wall to get better aim!" "I knock over the cart and stand behind it for the defense bonus!" etc).

    If you can find it, THE END is a slick game, and was one of the early adopters of d20 for a gritty modern game. I totally did not think that d20 was the right choice for this game, but the author really worked the system to make it fit. Low HP, realistic feats and stuff. If you wanted to play a d20-based game that felt like, say, Twilight 2000, this would definitely be the system to use. Things to cannibalize might be the magic system, HP progression and character classes for a modern grit/horror game.

    Posted By: rossI am thinking of restarting a D&D 3.0 campaign that fizzled a few years ago. In preperation I am looking at what I think are the best d20/OGL products that I can cannibalize if need be.

    So far mine are BESMd20 and Nyambe, what are yours?
    First off, I'd make the switch to 3.5 if you haven't already done so. Early-revision-griping aside, it's a definite improvement, and it opens up the current collection of supplements. Of the stuff from WotC, the Spell Compendium is a must-have. The Complete books and PHB2 add lots of fun options, and Book of Nine Swords is very cool. If you're a tweaker, you'll also want to look at Unearthed Arcana, which is a collection of optional rules. (You can preview most of them here.)

    As for d20/OGL, I echo the recommendations for Malhavoc's Iron Heroes. Admittedly, it does have a lot of warts, but they're warts on a gloriously beautiful product (and the IH fan community has helped mitigate most of them). IH takes D&D's focus on heavy tactical combat and makes it fun. The skills chapter is also pure gold, IMO, and is very easily integrated into standard D&D.

    My next recommendation would be Arcana Unearthed/Evolved, another Malhavoc product line. It's a refreshing take on D&D that's also easily compatible.

    Beyond that, I'd want to know what kind of game you plan to run.
