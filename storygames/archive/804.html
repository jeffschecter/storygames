Say you have game of students at a university that can only hold back the encroaching madness of Cthulhu by weaving protection spells into debate. It has broad skill groups that everyone gets to take a priority ranking on:


Skill - Rank
Research A
Elocution B
Improvisation C
Charisma D

The above character is better at Research than Elocution, which is better than Improvisation, etc. These ranks are solid numbers, say A = 4, B = 3, and so on. These numbers would indicate something like number of dice to roll or cards to pull.

But! During each story arc/session, you could narrate whatever you wanted, given social contract, by spending a point from the category. You would use the new total for further tests in that arc, but they would be reset each time. However, when you spent that point, you would gain a trait based off of the narration you delivered, and it would give a bonus from there on out when appropriate.

Did I steal this without realizing it? (Like I did with Crispin Glover?)

I don't know if my other question will make sense, but:

What kind of response would this provoke? As a player, what tactics would you see played out?
