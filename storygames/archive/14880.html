\nHammerCon III is taking place on Nov 4-5 in Hamilton, Ontario, Canada, and following on last year's success with our Cthulhu Cup tournament event, we're looking for a repeat!
\n
\nReferences: Last year's call for scenario authors on SG, Last year's call for scenario authors on Yog-Sothoth.com, scenario description in last year's convention program (see page 24)
\n
\nBackground on HammerCon: Now in its third consecutive year, our convention is a community organized and volunteer-based event, with a focus on celebrating play rather than trade. Despite this, we always aim for professional production quality in what we do. Our approach to convention organization, focusing on the actual gaming and taking an active approach to polishing each event on our roster to maximize the play experience, has been extremely well-received by our attendees and is a big factor in our pretty rapid growth curve.
\n
\nCthulhu Cup: The tournament is a multi-table event where several groups of investigators play through an original scenario set in the Cthulhu mythos, competing with the other teams to find and draw conclusions from clues, advancing the plot, characterizing "in-character", "in-theme", and "in-period", and creating the most enjoyable play experience for the table. The winning team's participants receives a prize package and the Cthulhu Cup.
\n
\nYour mission, should you choose to accept it, is to write an original scenario based around traditional CoC investigation activities, written for a convention format (roughly 3-4 hours in length) with highly entertaining and interesting characters. The writer we select to write this scenario for us will get full writer's credit and shared copyright to the edited text, a copy of their printed and bound scenario, and an honourarium of $100 CAD.
\n
\nWe want a scenario in which anyone can participate in regardless of their prior experience with the Mythos or Mythos-based games. The ruleset you use should ideally be either Chaosium's Call of Cthulhu 6th edition, Graham Walmsley's Cthulhu Dark, or some other well-known, ubiquitous and/or easy to learn ruleset. We prefer a Purist scenario over Pulp (but also prefer an excellent Pulp scenario over a mediocre Purist one).
\n
\nWe'd need your synopsis by August 28th at the latest, from which we'll pick our writer and notify by September 4th. Your final manuscript would be due on October 10th.
\n
\nWe'd like to make the scenario available under a Creative Commons International Share-Alike Attribution license.
\n
\nIf you're interested in writing for us or have any questions, please email events@hammercon.ca for more details or comment in this thread.
\n
\nIf you're interested in attending, please visit http://www.hammercon.ca for more details, newsletter and signup info. You can also join us on Facebook and Twitter.

    \n Graham, thanks for your kind words!
    \n
    \nWe're actually quite excited about the notion of using Cthulhu Dark for the Cup, and hope we get at least a few proposals using it. The ruleset is very minimal and easy to teach / use, and therefore very suitable for a convention game with time constraints. Some of its rules also invoke themes that seem more Purist (in terms of actual Lovecraft literature) than good ol' Call of Cthulhu. Of course, the increased degree of player authorship I'm perceiving in the system means that certain types of pre-written scenario triggers aren't going to work quite right, so it seems to me that the scenario actually has to be written explicitly for Cthulhu Dark in order to work with it without relying on the Keeper drifting the scenario. Obviously, Keeper drifting is something we want to avoid as much as possible given the competitive nature of a tournament game.
    \n
    \nI'd be overjoyed if you submitted a proposal this year, Graham (hint hint!).

    . Not really! There's still plenty of room for the GM or the scenario to throw up constraints, just like in BRP. The only things to keep an eye on are (1) the deadliness of pushing on rolls until you get a success - CD investigators will not endure too many three-dice rolls, and (2) the fact that you never get to fight monsters, which shouldn't be a problem in a Purist framework anyway.

    \n Deadline is quickly approaching! Anyone who wants to be considered for this year's Cthulhu Cup scenario authorship, please submit your proposals by August 28th 11:59pm EST at the latest to events@hammercon.ca

    Some clarifications:

    We will consider scenarios set in any time period, as long as it captures the Purist feel.
    There are no word-count requirements or restrictions for either the proposal or final submission. Naturally, the proposal length should be sufficient to give us a good idea of what's cool and interesting about your scenario. A synopsis of a few paragraphs accompanied by a list of major NPCs and the player characters would serve well, for example. The final submission should be sufficient to provide 3-4 hours worth of playtime with ample guidance to the Keeper. Last year's submission worked out to 17,000 words.
    We lay no claim to copyright of your proposal or final submission. Copyright of the final print product will be shared between you and Hammer Games, with neither party able to license it for exclusive use to a third party without mutual approval, but either party able to share it through the Creative Commons International Share-Alike Attribution license.
    If you are selected as the author, you will be responsible for submitting your final manuscript by October 10th, and upon receipt you will receive a $100 CAD honourarium (via PayPal or other mutually agreed-upon method). After the convention (Nov 5th) we will send you a print copy of the scenario by mail, as well as the printable files in PDF format. Also, you may get eaten first, but no promises.
