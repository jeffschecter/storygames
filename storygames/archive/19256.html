\n I have a few projects in mind for anyone interested in\n
    \n
  • Gaming over Hangouts
  • Exploring puppetry
\nProject number one is a 4 session series of games, all played in that most marvelous of systems: Puppetland.
We would need:
5 total people(including me)
5 one hour sessions (one for set-up, 4 for play) (same players each time)
I'd like play to extend over a year. Each session will sum up the most important thing that happened that season, playing through Spring, Summer, Fall, and then ending in Winter.
In this scenario, I'd like to encourage players to make or find puppets to use, but we should go through character creation first.

Project number two is a playtest, of a new ViewScream scenario.
We would need:
4 people exactly each session (I may play, I may just facilitate)
As many sessions as interest provides (different players each time?)
Games usually last 90 minutes, including all explaining of the rules and technology.
In this scenario, it is not necessary to have a puppet ready to go, only to be willing to play off of a puppet. Some playtests on either side of this issue would be helpful.

    \n I goofed this one up. I tried to write for a middle ground between horror and comedy.
    What I'm doing for the next iteration is to write to the extreme, and let the players wiggle from there.

    So I'm editing a "G" version, for Sesame Street style play, and then a more surreal horror version, "Pan's Labrynth" "Dark Crystal" David Bowie's Labrynth.

    Also, this crew was all puppet. I'd like to experiment with humans in the mix.

    As always, if anyone wants to be part of a play run, let me know.

    \n I feel strangely conflicted/compelled by this concept. The masked performer, the avatar, the uncanny valley, doll-play. I need to satisfy my curiosity! Can I be a part of a play run? At the very least I'd love to be apart of your trying to intermix humans and puppets in play - I've always wanted to see what performing against a puppet was like.

    Though, what counts as a puppet? A wooden spoon with twine hair and felt-tip smile? A portrait on card with a moveable mouth? A hand shaped and flapped to form a mouth? Or are you felt-only purists? Oh, oh, oh oh! Could I have a puppet on-screen with me alongside - like a ventriloquism thing - effectively having two PCs? It'd great to have a puppet/human;master/servant routine in a more slapstick game.

    Have you tried playing trad games with puppets? It would be amazing to do a dungeon crawl with over-acting puppets! Or or or Dogs in the Vineyard with broody pistol-toting puppets!
