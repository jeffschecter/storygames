    I agree with Rafial, as far as supporting the widest number of devices, .epub is good place to start. While a .pdf is perfect for preserving the exact static layout of a document, this very characteristic can be problematic on many reader devices, especially ones with smaller screens like mobile phones. The advantage of using a dedicated ebook format like .epub rather than .pdf is that it supports text reflow - allowing the text be readjusted to appropriately fill the device's screen. For this reason, plain old .html files can be useful as well; even .txt files can work, though you loose the advantage of formatting (bold, italics, headers, etc).

    \n

    A good, free, open source utility is Calibre. It not only functions as an ebook library manager, but also allows you to convert between various ebook format, including .epub.

    \n

    Recently, I converted several of Clinton's creative commons games (TSoY, The Face of Angles and Inuma) to epub for personal use on my iPhone. I use the excellent and free Stanza app as my reader. I took the route of marking up the text in Markdown, then running the result through Calibre to get my .epub files - though you could output to whatever format that works best for your device.

    \n

    If you decided to give it a try, feel free to whisper me an questions you might run into about Calibre, and I'll do my best to help you out.
