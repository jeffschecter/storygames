\n Below are the rules for creating Vampires and Victims in ... and cast no shadow, as they stand now. What I'm wondering about is...

1. Are these rules clear and understandable? Is there anything you'd specifically want more explanation of?

2. Should I put in more examples? Is there anything in particular you'd like to see an example of?

Thanks in advance!

Making a Vampire

Vampires have three Traits:

Beast - physical capability
Mastery - social capability
Darkness - supernatural capability

Divide 13 points among the three traits as you choose, putting at least one point in each.

Now, choose five Keywords for your vampire. Each keyword is attached to a trait, and answers a question that relates to that trait. The questions are:

Beast: What animal do you have characteristics of? OR In what physical way are you superior to humans?

Mastery: How do you control your victims?

Darkness: What do you control or do that's supernatural?

Example: If you were trying to make a vampire with powers like Dracula's, you might choose:

Beast: wolf, bat
Mastery: mesmerism
Darkness: turn into animals, insubstantiality

If you wanted to have an Anne Rice sort of vampire, you might choose:

Beast: speed, strength
Mastery: seduction
Darkness: telepathy, telekinesis

You have to have at least one keyword for each trait.

Next, you choose Weaknesses. This works just like choosing Keywords, except that the questions are different:

Beast: What physical things harm or repel you?

Mastery: Who are you powerless to control? OR Where can you not go?

Darkness: What prevents you from using your supernatural abilities? OR What can reveal you for what you really are?

Again, you have to have at least one weakness for each trait.


Making a Victim

Victims have three Traits:

Prowess - physical capability
Will - social / mental capability
Innocence - protection from the supernatural

Divide up 7 points among these as you choose, putting at least one point in each.

Now, choose five Keywords for your victim. Each keyword is associated with a trait, and answers a question about that trait. The questions are:

Prowess: What can you do that most others can't?
Will: How do you resist others' attempts to control you?
Innocence: What protects you from the supernatural?

You have to have at least one keyword for each trait.

Next, choose five weaknesses for your victim. This works just like choosing keywords, except that the questions are different:

Prowess: What puts you at risk, but you do it anyway?
Will: What makes you vulnerable to others' control? (Thanks to Johnstone Metzger for helping with this one!)
Innocence: What tempts you to do things you know are wrong?

You have to have at least one weakness for each trait.

    Posted By: JohnstoneSeems fairly clear.

    If your Beast and Mastery traits can be supernatural powers, what's the point of Darkness? Miscellaneous supernatural capabilities?
    \nWell... yes, that's part of it. The other part is that you can't use your Beast or Mastery to do anything that's outright supernatural -- you can do those things that humans can do, only better.

    For example, in order for Dracula to turn into a bat, he *needs* that "transform into animals" keyword on Darkness, because humans just can't do that. Without it, he could use his "bat" keyword to hear really well, or to climb the side of a castle (even upside down, since a human could in theory do that, if he/she were strong enough), but he can't use it to fly or turn into a bat without having a Darkness keyword to let him. Similarly, he could use his "wolf" keyword to run fast, to leap, to smell things better than any human could -- because those are things that humans do, he just does them better.

    On the "miscellaneous powers" side, Darkness is your chance to really go crazy. Want to have a shadow that can reach out and do things physically, like Nosferatu? That's a Darkness power. Want to turn into mist, like Dracula? Or seemingly teleport around as people run away from you, like Jerry Dandrige in "Fright Night"? Any of those can be Darkness powers.\n
    Some Victim examples would be good. What kind of traits make you want to be controlled? "I love the monarchy"? A fear of violence, or responsibility?
    \nThose are possibilities. Others would be things like, "I need the attention of men" (Lucy in Dracula), "I'm desperate for a friend" (Laura in Carmilla),

    Hmm. Thinking about it, though, those could also come under Innocence's "What tempts you to do things you know are wrong?" I think I need to come up with a different question for one or both... probably Will. Maybe something like "What makes you vulnerable to control?" (Different from *wanting*, since it could be a failing, rather than something active.) That could be things like "sheltered life", "overconfidence", "needs a father figure", "is socially isolated", "afraid of physical threats", etc.
