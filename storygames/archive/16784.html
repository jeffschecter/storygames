    \n One thing I did with the minis I use for 3:16 that makes it easier for larger groups is I bought big d10s in a color that matched each mini. We roll together, then line the dice up in order rolled and everyone can see visually who's next and what range they are at. Because I wanted to do this I planned my mini purchase carefully to cover the colors I could get dice in.

    \n I'd also look for super-cheap HeroClix figures, because it was pan-genre: the same booster could give you an "alien" (a monster), a "space trooper" (an Atlantean), and a dwarf steam engineer.

    I think you could find whole cases of Rebellion for... yep, about $100, which will net you 240 figures. BOUND to get a matched set of both "space marines" and a variety of "aliens." Painted. Snap off the Clix base and re-base, if you like (they ain't worth nothing, if they aren't LE/Super-Rare--but double-check on eBay for any about which you aren't sure, like a high-value fig that you only find one of out of the 240!).

    \n I looked around for a while for some good cheap minis for a game of 3:16 and I ended up getting some unpainted, unassembled plastic Warhammer 40K space marines that were on Ebay. They were pulled from a larger set that the seller had parted out so they were pretty affordable. I think I ended up spending around $17 total for 10 marines and I just put some different colored stickers on their bases to help differentiate them.

    It would have been nice if they were painted but I didn't have the time or skill to give it a try before the game.
