  • RobMuadib

    My Game, T:COTEC, still in development has alot of shared ownership elements. Covering not only character's (Personae in my game parlance) but virtually every element of the game world (Narrative Environment in my game language) I cover some of this breakdown in this entry at my design journal, Wild Musings.

    Anyway, Personae are of one of 3 types, Company, Shared, & Proprietor. Basically a shared Personae is one that someone has created and introduced into the Narrative Environment, and makes available for use by the other players. While proprietor entities are the "property" of a specific player, and there are special rules regarding what can happen to that Persona without the Proprietor's permission.

    There is also an option for a player to "rent" a Proprietary Persona for a Scene. So this sharing of PC's is a big feature of the game. Eliminating the 1 to 1 character assumption in traditional RPG's. THe game also features distributed GM Duties, unlike in a regular RPG as well. So there is alot distance built into the basic system design, to move the focus toward the collaborative world creation & communal fantasy that is the thrust of the game.

    Oh, just thought of another instance of Co-ownership, well actually potential co-ownership. IIRC Pendragon allowed you to play sucessive generations of a character's family, and the old Birthright supplement for D&D did something similar.

    In Aria, there were a couple of approaches that although shown as single ownership didn't have to be. You had the Perpetual Genealogies where you could play members of the same family, or it could be extended to say a Noble house, or Royal Dynasty, or even Merchant House, or even a particular race/culture in the game through Aria's Interactive History mechanics. Another interesting take on that was the idea of Eternal Presences, basically semi-mythic figures that would show up through-out a particular peoples history, say something like Merlin, or other mythic "shepherd" figures.

    Another interesting take on this was what they called Individual Presence, but Archetypal Presence is a better name. Basicaly you would play the same character archetype within a race/cultures/nations history. So you might play a great General archetype, or Wartime Leader, or conqueror, etc. That showed up in a peoples history again and again over time. Neat ideas.

    Anyway, had to step away from the keyboard for a while and lost the rest of my train of thought. But that should give you some ideas of how the 1 to 1 correspondence between character's have/can be dvidied.

    This leads me into thoughts for another post. But basically, I find that using, "Third Person" characters for focus sets the stage for "Secondary Narrative" play. That is, by freeing up the single character, protragonist focus, you can engage in less story focused play, perhaps engaging "explorative vignette" play, where the idea is to experience the reality of a character or character type, a role within the Narrative Environment. Also it frees you up for "Toy" play, basically, the players can simpley engage in Setpieces, usually battles, using the unique, flavorful units that make up that NE. This is of couse the direction I am taking with my game. I would say that "Loose Coupliing" (To steal a term) of character would seem to be counter to a good "Narraivist" focused game. But I could be wrong.
