    \n\nHaakon \n\n\n

    \n\nAugust 2007 \n edited August 2007
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n HolmCon is in Holmestrand, about an hour drive outside Oslo and has been an important testing ground for both foreign indie-games and self-produced indie-games for the Norwegian gaming society (or rather the environment around the rollespill.net forums). Among the games played has been DiTV, PtA, Universalis, Shock, Nicotine Girls, etc.). Among the self-produced games we have tried are Magnus J's RISK winners (Norwegian game-chef-like competition) Until We Sink and In the Belly of the Whale (not yet translated) as well as Matthijs' We All Had Names and Zombieporno. Roleplaying-poetry were also introduced at HolmCon. So it has been a breeding-ground for new games and concepts as well as introduction to the international (ok US) indie-scene.

    \n Well, FWIW, ENWorld Chicago Gameday has had a growing indie contingent over the past few years.

    Last one: SotC, Primitive
    Before that: Agon, DRYH, SotC
    Before that: Hero's Banner (run by Mr. Koppang), Burning Empires
    Before that: Burning Wheel, Dread

    And, while not "indie" in the Story-games sense, Bill Corrie (i.e., HinterWelt Enterprises) regularly runs his Iridium system games, which are creator-owned and chock full o' old-school goodness.

    Pretty good for a mostly d20 fansite.
