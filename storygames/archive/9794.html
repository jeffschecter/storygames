\n Hi. I don't post here so much anymore (due to time mostly. I still have love in my heart for all of you), but since my contest came up in the July thread I thought I'd provide details. Specifically I'd like to state that the contest is open to everyone.
\n
\n!!! Jake's Awesome Fantasy Game Contest !!!
\n
\nI want to start a design contest. Joel and I discovered a game called Mythweaver:Reckoning on RPGNOW. Here's the link:
\n
\nhttp://www.rpgnow.com/product_info.php?products_id=63114.
\n
\nWhile the game is actually pretty good, the copy in the RPGNOW listing is pretty awful. It's kind of everything I hate about generic fantasy games. But... At the same time, maybe these are parts that could make an awesome whole? Here's the idea:
\n
\nChallenge: Build an awesome fantasy game. Your game must meet these requirements.
\n
\n-Must be 48 pages.
\n-Must include a distinctive core mechanic.
\n-Must include complete rules for character creation.
\n-Must include complete rules for magic.
\n-Must include a flexible skill system.
\n-May include guidelines for resolving actions during the game.
\n-Must cost just $3.00
\n-Must be made available for sale on RPGNOW
\n
\nDeadline: Last day of August! We'll play the games at the August Go Play SE PDX, or I'll play the games in my spare time.
\n
\nPrize: I'll do the art for your game. At least a cover and 4 illustrations. I'll provide publishing support as well, if needed.
\n
\nProgress? This thread will act as a home for the contest. You can post questions, ideas and drafts here.
\n
\nWho can participate? You can!
\n
\nWho Judges? Me. I'm looking for creativity, originality and innovation. Solid design is a bonus as well. I'll be choosing whichever game i like the best, and my tastes are what will guide me.
\n
\n
\nMore on the requirements:
\n
\n-Must be 48 pages: I mean 48 pages as a finished product. With art and layout and stuff. Since this is a design contest I doubt anyone will be taking their game that far before the deadline (but I can hope, because I love finished products). So what I'm looking for is a text that will be about 48 pages when/if it becomes a published game. Does that make sense? So aim for a 48 page publication, not 48 pages of text!
\n
\n-Must include a distinctive core mechanic: Distinctive meaning whatever you think it means.
\n
\n-Must include complete rules for character creation: Or character generation and distribution, really. Don't feel tied to any traditional character creation ideas.
\n
\n-Must include complete rules for magic: Or powers. Have fun with that.
\n
\n-Must include a flexible skill system: The neat thing here is that there's no requirement for a combat system of any kind.
\n
\n-May include guidelines for resolving actions during the game: Please.
\n
\n-Must cost just $3.00: See below.
\n
\n-Must be made available for sale on RPGNOW: This is not actually a requirement. I just think it would be fun to flood RPGNOW with a bunch of cool, cheap fantasy games.
\n
\nThe official thread for the contest is on Go Play PDX There's a lot more info there.

    \n (cross posted on the main thread listed above)
    \nEnter
    \nPandemic ~ Age of man
    \n
    \n Imagine a pulpy fantasy setting where there is no magic but instead psionics (ooh aah) and tesla punk style awesome. For ages the various fantasy races of [insert cool planet name] have existed in a gritty corrupt world and now after a mysterious meteor shower a new plague has stricken the land\u2026man. A strange illness [insert strange illness name] has left many crippled and sickened but those who have survived the ghastly virus have become wretched pink beasts calling themselves humans.
    \n Upon first awakening most are crazed versions of their former selves until human cultists slip them away to strange meme pools where they are implanted with memories.
    \n
    \n The game will use binomial dice (0\u2019s and 1\u2019s) where the player roles a number of dice equal to their total modifiers and such. Before rolling a difficulty level is determined ranging from impossible to probable (or something like that) in two main areas quality and speed much like the ore system. The player then spends their binomial dice to raise the difficulties for example I spend some of my dice to go from awful quality to good.
    \n The game will also use resources pools similar to the pool system in which characters can add flair to their actions, take temporary narrative control, or anything else I think of.
    \n
    \nSo I am as they say\u2026in.

    \n Hi. We're one month into the contest, and right at it's middle point. Up until now I've been kind of vague about a few things, so now is the time for me to get specific.

    Contest deadline: 11:59 PM (Pacific time), August 31st 2009. No games will be accepted after this time. I'd encourage all of you to send your games in sooner then that if possible.

    Acceptable formats: Anything is really fine. I'm expecting to receive most of the games as either Word or PDF files or Google Docs. Posting your content online is fine. Just make sure I know where to go to read it. Please do not send me scanned, hand written pages. I won't be able to read them. If you're going the extra mile and uploading your game to RPGNOW, make sure you send me a comp copy.

    How to send me a copy of your game: You can email me at jake@atarashigames.com. You can attach the file to the email, or include a link in the email. You can also post a link on the official thread at the Goplaypdx forum (link below). You can also upload files to the Goplaypdx Downloads page (link below). Please do not post links on the Story-games forum, my blog or anywhere else. I may not notice them.

    What I will do with the games: I will read every game submitted. I will play as many of them as possible. At this point I don't anticipate being able to play every game, so I'll be focusing on games that I feel need to be played in order for me to understand them.

    My judging criteria: As I've said before, I will be judging using my own personal tastes as a guide. I value originality, creativity, unique experiences. More then any of that, I value the unexpected. The game that wins will likely do so because it captures my heart completely by showing me something I've never seen before. Or maybe it won't? maybe I'll be won over by a nice, solid, traditional design?

    Of course, I'll also be using the contests requirements as a guideline. I'm not just looking to see how closely these games followed the requirements, but also in what interesting ways the designers worked around them. These requirements were chosen because the represent much of what I typically don't like in a a game, so I'm very curious to see what you all have come up with.

    As noted by several people, those of you who know me and share my tastes may have a better chance of winning this contest. That's just the way it is. I'm a big believer in designing for specific audiences. That said, I think the best way to win this contest is to show me something I've never seen before.

    Awards: I'll be giving out a Grand Prize Award and two special category awards. The Grand Prize Award prize is the art package. The special category awards won't include a prize. Each of the three awards will come with a special graphic that the winners can display on their site, blog, or game (or have tattooed on their arm or printed on their underwear).

    Additionally, I'll be providing written feedback for each game.

    Time frame: I plan to announce winners on September 26th. there may be delays if I recieve more games then I'm anticipating receiving, so let's keep that as a tentative date.


    Questions?

    Contact Jake: jake@atarashigames.com

      \n You pick the first two elements from a list, and make up a third one.

      Say your class is "Spooky Troll Goat-Shaman". Spooky gives you some abilities (you can cast spells, you get a bonus to Influence rolls whenever being spooky would help with that, and you get your choice of small, creepy creature as a familiar) and Troll gives you some other abilities (you get more hit points, you can talk to trees). "Goat-Shaman" is something you made up, and it gives you a bonus to whatever you and the rest of your group agrees that a Goat-Shaman would be good at.

      \n On Friday, I started a game called Urban Fantasy. It won't be part of this contest, but I'm glad I started working on it.

      It's a game about normal people realizing there's more to their city. Characters and setting are developed in play. Characters change after conflicts, win or lose.

      The dice system involves rolling handfuls of six-siders, and finding dice melds (combos) that match the patterns related to their skills. Like you might have a (freeform) skill "defend myself" and you might have written a power under it: "flying kick." You associated some dice meld and dice power to "flying kick," like "3K-push 2" (use three-of-a-kind to activate the "push 2" power.

      Conflicts involve a dice tug of war. Conflicts start with a d20 set at "10" and one side pulls toward 20 and the other side pulls toward 1. "Push 2" really means "push the conflict die 2 positions toward my goal" (football style, so your goal is what your opponent is defending). Any time after the third back-and-forth in the system, either player may pick up the conflict die and roll it. If it's between the die's "tug" value and your opponent's goal post, you win. So if it was 15 when you picked it up, and you are defending goal 20, and your opponent is defending goal 1, you need to roll 1-14 to win.

      There are a dozen or so dice powers. They do things like mess with your opponent's dice pool, reroll certain dice in your own pool, add dice to your pool, and of course push the "football" around the field.

      I spent a lot of time writing about head space and ritual, actually. This is intended to evoke a magical quality during play.

      It's GMless. Each locale in the setting is written on a card. Every scene, players add "tension" dice to sites. The dice on that card are the starting difficulty of challenges in the next scene there (then the tension resets to zero dice). Plus each player gets one die to give to the player or his opposition (there's a sort of temp GM role per scene). At the end of the scene, the player rolls the tension dice and makes dice melds out of it to purchase a new power.

      Annalise was a huge inspiration, especially regarding character creation, emergent setting creation, and structured storytelling. Play steps through phases like in Annalise. There's a "normal life" phase, then a phase where characters encounter something magical and must win a conflict to understand it. Then they explore the magical world and get embroiled in its trouble. Finally, once their troubles are resolved, they return home (forever changed). I borrowed the idea of Claims, sorta, too. They show up as aspects on site cards.

      In summary:
      * urban fantasy
      * formal story structure for urban fantasy
      * character creation during play
      * setting creation during play
      * freeform character aspects, skills
      * "make your own" character powers using a toolkit
      * unique (?) "goal post" conflict resolution system
      * handfuls of six-siders!
