    \n Hi, I wrote Spectrum, and just released it last month. It's great to see a discussion thread started about it by someone else.
    I created Spectrum because I wasn't satisfied with how other games handled time travel. I wanted mechanics for characters to be able to do really weird things with time travel, like alter scenes already in play, meet themselves, call on their past or future selves for help, remember things that haven't happened yet, and play events out of sequence.

    I've given a brief synopsis of the rules on Praxis. I have submitted it to RPGnet for review, but I would love to find other people interested in reviewing it. I'd be happy to answer any other questions about the game. Thanks for your interest!

    \n Sorry to leave you hanging, L.Trenti MrVALIS, I thought I'd replied to this already.

    Unfortunately all I can say is "soon". I still want to make a few minor revisions to the text before I make a print version (adding more examples, and clarifying the costs of high tech abilities). That part is easy. What is really holding me back are the color pages.

    Spectrum has 7 full-page, single-color illustations. When I've printed hard copies locally, I print the illustration pages separately, and assemble the books by hand before having them bound. There has to be a better way to do this for print-on-demand, but the only ways I have found are:

    -Print the whole book as color, which makes it much more expensive than it needs to be.
    -Print the whole book in black and white, which would be fine, except that the color spectrum is the central metaphor of the game, and the book would seem really lacking without it.

    The best option seems to be to make hard copies available in black and white, and include a PDF with those pages in color. I will likely have this available by the end of February.

    Do you know of any print-on-demand options for a black and white book with only a few color pages?
