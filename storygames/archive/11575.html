\n Random thought, just tossing it out there.

Step 1: Each player finishes the statement "I'm in love with ______" and fills in the blank with a simple character concept, perhaps including a name.

Examples:
I'm in love with a zombie.
I'm in love with the town barber.
I'm in love with a superhero.
I'm in love with the assassin sent to kill me.
I'm in love with a good-hearted cowboy.

Step 2: Each player volunteers to play one of the character concepts described above, thus creating some kinda lovey-webby-thingie.

    \n It seems like this could build a situation in which the characters weren't connected. I think I'd want to include some way to help/encourage the use of what's already been authored, or easily identify where two love relationships could be competitive or reciprocal.

    Vaguely related: In The Roach you establish "I love you" with the character of the player on your right and "I hate you" with the character on your left. This kick starts the game with intense, contradictory, lopsided relationships.
