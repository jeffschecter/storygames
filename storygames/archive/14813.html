\n I'd like to hear your suggestions for neat one-shot/con-game set-ups. I'm generally thinking of system (even genre)-neutral ideas that might lead to an interesting play experience for groups, particularly con-game strangers, and multiple final resolutions and/or routes towards them.
\n
\nFeel free to avoid, or embrace, cliche as appropriate dependent on your tastes. And to name / conceal your sources, and points of final resolution.
\n
\n(Credit where its due - this thread got me thinking about one-shots again, and how I should get out of the ordinary with my con games, and, obviously, this)
\n
\nHere are a few to get you started:
\n
\nHarry Brown - The PCs are elderly retired adventurers / soldiers / wizards who are living the quiet life until one of their own is killed by some upstarts and they have to take bloody revenge on them.
\n
\nBlood Meridian - The PCs are clueless, desperate mercenaries hopping over the border to kill some orcs / Mexicans / etc for the bounties on their heads
\n
\nAnswering Cthulhu - The PCs are fanatical heretics who just need to ensure that their apocalyptic ritual succeeds tomorrow night.

    \nLost Mindfucky thing like Lacuna or DRYH - inexplicable stuff happens to the PC's, and just as they think they know what's going on, a new inexplicable thing happens that smashes their hypothesis. The GM has a "transcendent signified" / "objective reality" in mind where it all makes sense, but may change it on the way, taking cues from the players. "It's all just a dream" or "We're all in virtual reality" are *not* acceptable cop-outs. Can be spliced with most of the suggestions above.

    \nSkinwalkers: In a remote location, the military is testing new telepresense technology. The players are soldiers that are jacked in to the bodies of killer androids and are conducting combat drills. During one such mission they pick up sounds of alarm and small arms fire coming from their bunker followed by a ground shaking explosion. The players black out. When they wake up, they realize that they're still in the bodies of the androids.

    Let's see if white text works...
    What the soldiers were not told is that telepresense doesn't work for military application. Signals can be jammed, or even worse, hacked. The military is actually conducting testing in mind transference. When the androids return to base, they find that there has been a fire fight and that their bodies are missing. Low of power and firepower, can the players discover the truth, rescue themselves, and defeat the enemy?

    \nDude, where's my horse?/Wanted - players wake up unable to remember what they did... the last 5 years. Among their equipment is a chest with several locks (one for each player) and once they open it, they find a wanted ad with their faces. Dead or alive for the kidnapping of princess XY. Oh, and among other trinkets, they find the head of the princess.

    Ok, this is serving me for a campaign actually but you can rush the end for a one shot.

    Insert escape music here - place all players in a city for a random reason connected with a chain of powerful / almighty / dangerous NPCs present in the city. Add a little kid with something everybody wants... that could also tear the whole city in pieces at midnight if it's not returned to an specific loaction in the city. Have the child randomly bump into each player's character and drop the uber NPCs to keep the chase going. Rinse and repeat until midnight.
