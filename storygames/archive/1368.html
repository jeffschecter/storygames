One of my favorite games ever was the (sadly unpublished) 6ix-7even, by my friend Tim Sweeney. It was a very interesting media-based game, and I think every campaign of it had an album - not as a soundtrack during the game, but as inspiration. If you figured out the connection between the soundtrack and the campaign, you had a LOT of leverage with that game's secrets; if not, you weren't screwed.

I was listening to Bad Religion today and heard one of my favorite lines, which made me think of this. There are single lines in Bad Religion that I could build a session, a campaign, hell a whole game out of. Some of my favorites, from different songs on The Process of Belief:

"There's no such thing as hell, but you can make it if you try."
"There is no such thing as human debris."

    So here are my thoughts.

    Definites

    * Each participant can submit up to three songs in total.
    * A participant's songs are all considered together, as a unit.
    * They must provide all of the lyrics.
    * Participant *highlights* three phrases across all of his submitted lyrical material. If it's a single song, you'd highlight three phrases within it. If it's a set of three songs, you'd highlight one phrase per song. These phrases must be used in the design (and one of them must be the title of the game).

    Possibly

    * Design is done 24-hour RPG style (but maybe 72-hour, or maybe not-at-all, this one I keep having multiple opinions about)
    * Poems and other short lyrical forms may be considered valid substitutions for songs. (Have three haiku you love? Bring it on.)

    This is just my initial brainstorm
