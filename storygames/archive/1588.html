A little toy for anyone making a Firefly/Serenity heartbreaker, I'll release this out into the black.

If I were going to do a Firefly/Serenity game, the character sheet would be a wanted poster. The "stats" would be a list of past crimes. The current bounty on your head would be mechanically relevant as a "level" or "CR" and also as a sort of "trouble" rating that measures the level of obstacles that you'll face with each job. Your known accomplices are the other members of your crew. Your aliases are the roles you play during a con job.

Just tossin' it out there.

    Dev and I were thinking about this a bit just now: Basically, the game is told as much as possible in the form of wanted posters. There's maybe 5-10 pages of system/setting, but the whole rest of the book is page after page of wanted posters of criminals from all walks of life and evilness. Henchmen, vigilantes, masterminds, and low-lifes.

    This is a cross between the Lore Pages from Weapons of Gods and the D&D Monstrous Manual. You flip through the book to find your character AND your targets. The big twist is that each poster has a list of known accomplices, so the whole book is a vast criminal underground network.
