Okay, time to get down to business and make this thing. I'm making it live in a thread because this is a non-commercial project, so there's no reason not to share!

\n

Let's start with the characters:

\n

There are two player teams.

\n

The first team is the Research Team. The Research Team consists of the Sorceror, the Sorceror's Child, and the Apprentice. The Sorceror doesn't go out into the field. S/He stays at home and catalogues the demons that the Child and the Apprentice capture. The Child and Apprentice are opposite sexes. They go in the field, sometimes together and sometimes separately, to capture demons.

\n

The second team is the Nefarious Team. There are two members of opposite sexes. The Nefarious Team's goal is to collect as many demons as possible, for them to use in their plot to take over the world.

\n

The Research Team may gain members by befriending other researchers in the course of their adventures.

\n

The Nefarious Team works alone and does not expand, but new Nefarious Teams may be added; they all work for the same shadowy organization.

    To capture demons, you need a device called a Demon Ring.

    \n

    This is a metal ring about the size of a dessert plate, marked with ninety-nine names of God and enclosing a network of wires arranged in a sacred geometric formula designed to trap spirits in its interstices. It's a little like a Hermetic dream-catcher.

    \n

    The best Demon Rings are made of gold, but less precious metals are usable too. Cheap student rings are stamped from steel.

    \n

    To release a captured demon, one obscures one of the names of God with a special chalk made with bone ash, or disrupts the wire network to release all of them simultaneously.

    How you catch demons:

    \n

    Every city in the Hundred Islands is protected by an Arx Sigillata - a Citadel of Seals. Arces Sigillatae are a group of towers, connected by beams of light made with sun-and-mirrors or beacon lanterns. They are the opposite of Demon Rings; their purpose is to repel demons rather than capture them.

    \n

    However, it's impossible to make a perfect Arx, and so every city's defences are breached by a few types of minor demons. These demons possess the local wildlife, turning them monstrous and strange.

    \n

    To generate an area's demons, you need to decide what kind of terrain the area has, and make a list of ten wild animals that live in the area. Call this list that area's Table D. Then roll d12. The GM should do this job and conceal the demon properties from the players until they discover them by interaction.

    \n

    Table A:
    \nIf A or B tell you that a monster type appears that you haven't seen before, youy will need to roll on Tables C and D to generate that monster type and enter it into your Codex Timorosus.

    \n

    1-4: Monster type 1.
    \n5-8: Monster type 2.
    \n9-11: Monster type 3.
    \n12: Roll again and see Table B.

    \n

    Table B:
    \n1: Two type 1 monsters appear!
    \n2: Two type 2 monsters appear!
    \n3: Two type 3 monsters appear!
    \n4: Two type 4 monsters appear!
    \n5-8: Monster type 4.
    \n9: Two monsters of different types!
    \n10: A monster from a neighboring region!
    \n11-12: A rare monster! Roll twice on Table C for its monstrous properties.

    \n

    Table C:
    \nEach of these entries will eventually refer to a list of demon moves/traits you can simply tack onto an animal in order to monsterize it, see below for examples.

    \n

    1: The monster is neotenic or incomplete. It lacks maturity or some essential part or organ.
    \n2: The monster is carnal and bloated with fecundity.
    \n3: The monster is ravenous.
    \n4: The monster is greedy.
    \n5: The monster is possessed of an emotional imbalance.
    \n6: The monster performs dark magics.
    \n7: The monster is wrathful. Its body is hypertrophied, clawed, and armoured.
    \n8: The monster warps the mind or senses.
    \n9: The monster seems kind but is secretly wicked.
    \n10: Roll twice, ignoring any 10s, and choose 5 demon moves instead of 3.

    \n

    Moves:
    \nRegular animals have 3 moves. Dunno what they are yet, but they should look a lot like standard Pokemon normal-type moves: Peck, Dash, etc etc.

    \n

    Demons also get 3 demon moves, or 5 if you rolled a 10. They look like below:

    \n

    Spawn:
    \nThe monster creates a baby monster that attacks you. At all times, half-formed larvae or glistening eggs drip from its hindquarters.

    \n

    Shriek:
    \nThe monster lets out a piercing scream. Its mouth is distended and enormous.

    \n

    Betray:
    \nThe opponent monster-catcher decides what the demon does this turn.
