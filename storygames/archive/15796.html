\n Let's talk about inspirational music for Fiasco. Name a playset, name a song that tells a story from that playset, and name an artist or genre that'd make nice, unobtrusive, evocative background music for a session. No right answers as mileage varies widely. Your Boom Town is not my Boom Town. Links encouraged.

    Once in a game of Fiasco using the Transatlantic playset, when one character (a grizzled old private detective whose family was killed by capitalists) successfully seduced another character (the player said no, but the character said yes thanks to a lack of black dice), I threw on "My Heart Will Go On" by Celine Dion, causing a brief, contemplative interlude.     \n John Lurie' work on Get Shorty provides a pretty fantastic playlist for a variety of more comedic toned games.

    Stink in particular is a solid theme song. I don't have specific playsets memorized, but it would work for fringe elements with connections to jazz in the 40's or 50's America, and starting in the 60's as more and more of a throwback when being upbeat was cool. Almost certainly wants a metropolitan setting, but at least needs something cosmopolitan (like Transatlantic).
