\n Just out of curiosity, do you cheat often on your dice rolls when you play a solo game?

For my part, I try not, but in my last How to host a dungeon game, I have cheated a few time when it was too tempting to favor a more interesting situation.
I cheated two time on random monster moves to prevent cool monsters from being removed. I cheated also one time on the special feature table and one time on the new group table. But I dint cheat on important dice rolls, like when the adventurer delved into the dungeon or in monsters group encounters.

Once you cheat, do you start cheating more often? Me, I think I still try to keep my cheating minimal once I have done it once (if I care about the game).

    \n I avoid cheating. I've cheated once in the past 7 years, and it felt like a knife. I did it because one player kept blowing rolls on dailies in 4E, and this was like the 5th blown roll in a row and it missed the defense by one. So I said it was a hit when it wasn't. This is clearly I sign I need to institute a house rule to allow players rerolls or roll bonuses, perhaps with Aspects, so I will be doing that so I won't feel the compulsion again.

    I never cheat in solo games though. Absolutely no reason to. I can fine tune everything to fit the single player. It's much easier. I also pick systems (Nobilis, HeroQuest, and WoD being favorites) that don't have high whiff factors or require character optimization.
