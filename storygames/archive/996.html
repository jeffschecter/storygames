Interesting blog post here: http://jayloomis.livejournal.com/2562.html

The point is that boardgames are being revitalised but facing less hostility in the process than rpgs get, plus that boardgames are easy to pick up and play in a way rpgs still generally aren't.

Similar stuff can be said for skirmish wargames, not the revitalising bit (though it's a healthy hobby) but that people can pick up the rules and get playing quickly and that quite complex and highly simulationist game experiences can be generated using generally very light (by rpg standards) rulesets.

Which suggests that we (in the wider sense) may be missing a trick. Particularly sim gamers like me, who historically have gone for levels of crunch that equally if not more simmy people in the skirmish wargames hobby have felt no real need for.

    I'm a hybrid Boardgame-RPG designer, and have spent a lot of time thinking about the relative strengths of both boardgames and RPGs.

    I read a great comment someplace that said D&D (the generic RPG) is 20 minutes of fun wrapped in 4 hours of game.

    I think a lot of (older) RPGs invest effort in making the game more detailed, realistic, or modular -- often at the expense of making it fun. RPGs can learn from boardgames to think about what the players are actually doing at the table. Are they spending a lot of time doing math? Looking up rules in books? Waiting while someone else does math or looks up rules? Listening to other people roleplaying (sometimes badly)? How often do they get to take their "turn" and how often do they have to watch other people taking their turns? How often do they get to make interesting decisions, roll dice, etc.

    I found listening to these podcasts of a group in the UK playing D&D to be verly enlightening:
    http://www.podcast.net/show/29233

    Whenever one of the players seems confused about the rules, whenever the roleplaying seems very awkward (or boring) and generally whenever you feel the urge to hit the fast-forward button -- that's something to make note of.

    (If anyone else has podcasts of actual gameplay, I'd love to hear it!)
