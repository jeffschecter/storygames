\n talking to Filip about Unsung got me thinking: why not use Polaris to play Cop Dramas?

here are some quick notes

please tell me what you think!

**************************************************************************************************************************

The People: the people of the city
The Knights: the cops (who have sworn to uphold the law)

Everything is worse than it was before, and people blame:
- foreigners
- new technology
- neglect for religion
- capitalism
- ...

The Mistake: The Zone (harbour?)
The Sun: modern culture
The Mistaken: criminals (gangs, organized crime, bad cops...)
The South: Latin America, Asia, North Africa...
The Battle: the cops fight crime on the city streets, but in the end they fall to drugs, corruption, apathy...

Novice = Rookie
Knight of the Order of the Stars = Police Officer
Aide to Senator Altair = Aide to Senator Johnson
Starlight Sword = Police Sidearm
Lore of Demons = Criminology
Lore of the Stars = Streetwise
Lore of the Outer Wastes = Foreign culture/language

    \n Ben,

    Does the existential threat NEED to affect an entire people? If the Boston PD falls to corruption, in-fighting and gang dealings, it's not like everyone can just apply to transfer to NYPD. Sure, some people might, but by and large, everyone will be left in their web of corruption.

    And more importantly... if ethics and justice fail here, who's to say that they'll work elsewhere. The existential threat is different for these cops. If they fail here, the entire civilization doesn't fall... but it might as well. Because they've delved down into the heart of the matter and realized that things are rotten at their core. Boston, New York, San Francisco, Portland, Kelowna, Edgewater, New Denver... Things aren't going to be magical and textbook anywhere.

    The existential threat is idealism, specifically about justice and the human spirit, and what lies at the center of a person who's life is surrounded by violence.

    The threat is that if the thin blue line gets murky and grey, then who's to say that good guys exist?
