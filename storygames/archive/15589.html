\n The year 2112.
You are aboard the spaceship Mega III, a slow cargo hauler with no modern infotainment-systems aboard, and no money to buy such high-brow shit.

The crew
- of five is desperately seeking something new to do, while jetting slowly through the eternity of space.
> The Captain: tight with resources, no non-vital use of on-board computers
> Grimes: "fortunate" die-roller
> First mate K\xf6lher: not happy with zero-g dice floating, would like to try out a magnetic rpg
- Lula Lovelly: excited about the new magnetic rpg Demolitions and Dragsters

Is there a rpg retailer out there?
How will the crew react to the shoddy saleswoman?
What old/new games will they take with them?
How will they fail/succeed to play them?
How will it affect on-board life?

Rules
- Do it as a narrative.
- Make use of posts already in the thread.
- Base drama on details and narrow descriptions.
- Use names, as they appear.

    \nC'mon, we need a random generator if this is going to be any fun.

    The Captain crossed his arms and shook his head slowly. No can do he said, any non vital use of computer power lead to excess head generation and excess heat generation had been the death of countless sloppy cargo haulers in the last decades. Plus, he hated the smell of sweaty crew. First mate K\xf6hler was not happy. How could they pretend they were on some planet when their dice would float around flaunting the fact they were stuck in zero-g for endless months to come? Add to that the fact that the best way to roll them they had come up with yet was shaking them in a cup of pudding till they stuck before reading, not only was it messy but seeing what side was up was ambigious more often than not. Just rolling them in a cupped hand and then holding them against a wall worked well till they discovered Grimes had a tendency to get just the numbers he needed.
