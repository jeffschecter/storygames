    \n Here are my notes from the thing:

    \xe2\u20ac\xa2\tWriting
    o\tDocs and spreadsheets to share edits and share reviews.
    o\tShare calculations, probabilities, etc. with spreadsheets
    o\tRelease early, iterate often
    o\tCharts added to Google Spreadsheets
    o\tHave to be online to use these things for now
    o\tPreview to specific individuals or publicly
    o\tRPG Playtest google group.
    o\tKeeps earlier revisions
    o\tGtalk on Windows has a voice talk feature
    \xe2\u20ac\xa2\tPromotion
    o\tGoogle Analytics
    \xef\u201a\xa7\tJavascript on a website will show you who's linking to it, what search terms, what they're clicking on, etc.
    \xef\u201a\xa7\tUseful when you talk to other game designers when exchange links.
    o\tRead Google's rules for inclusion in the index; if you break them you'll get kicked out.
    o\tMake a page about why you would want someone else's product. "If it's not your bag for this reason, if for this, try that," etc.
    o\tHave a blog
    \xef\u201a\xa7\tMake sure it's indexed
    \xef\u201a\xa7\tGet an RSS feed
    \xef\u201a\xa7\tTrack your audience
    \xef\u201a\xa7\tInteract with audience
    \xef\u201a\xa7\tWarn/ban people
    \xef\u201a\xa7\tReach out to people who blog about your games
    \xef\u201a\xa7\tBlogsearch with alerts. Tweak the search, then get RSS feed of that
    \xe2\u20ac\xa2\tSales
    o\tMake it as buyable as it can be.
    o\tCheckout is similar to but not the same as Paypal
    o\tCreate Checkout "Buy now" button. Google charges their CC. Through the whole of 2008, they will eat the fees. 2% to $0.20, less than Paypal, likely to remain so
    o\tAdWords
    \xef\u201a\xa7\tSet a cap budget and a budget per item for click cost
    \xef\u201a\xa7\tWhoever pays highest goes highest only on click
    \xef\u201a\xa7\t$1 advertising = $10 free checkout fees
    \xef\u201a\xa7\tDetects clickfraud
    \xef\u201a\xa7\tDon't bother unless you're not front page
    \xe2\u20ac\xa2\tGoogle Product Search (formerly froogle)
    o\tIntegrated with Checkout
    o\tUpload catalog to Google Base and you're in

    \n Hi Alexander,
    \n
    \nIf that's fairly accurate notes, here's some other stuff I think Designers might find useful. Feel free to steal. A lot of it will be gmail based as gmail has replaced my previous favorite mail client Evolution. Almost everything except the organization tips you can do with whatever you use for email.
    \n
    \nSecure Web based email
    \n[ulist]
    \n[*]Log in by using https://mail.google.com . This will mean that not only is the passing of your account name and password encrypted, but your whole gmail session is encrypted.
    \n[*]This isn't work safe. There is a way for your workplace to workaround SSL, while still making it appear that the SSL is intact if you don't check the certificate.
    \n[/ulist]
    \nMultiple Personalities
    \n[ulist]
    \n[*]Gmail allows you to control multiple gmail accounts from one account. You can set up different accounts like idonttrustyou@gmail.com , mygamecompanyname@gmail.com and have them come to myrealemailaddress@gmail.com . This is part of my strategy that keeps me from receiving any spam.
    \n[*] You can also send out email from myrealemailaddress@gmail.com so they actually appear to be from any of your other email addresses.
    \n[*]Gmail automagically sends reply email pretending as if from the address it was recieved from. For instance if you send something to the theory from the closet email address and I reply from my central address it will look like it came from the theory from the closet address. (unless the reciever checks the headers, which will show your main address, this is necessary to prevent spaming.)
    \n[*]Gmail will also let you access Pop3 accounts from gmail. I haven't played with this... yet.
    \n[/ulist]
    \nInternet Omnipresence
    \n[ulist]
    \n[*]Google Alerts will report to you what google webspiders find on the internet. This means you don't have to constantly search the info is sent to you by email.
    \n[*]You need to have Google Alerts set to watch your; game names, and business name. Perhaps also your real name.
    \n[*]In gmail click on the my services link in the top left corner. On the page that comes up click alerts.
    \n[*]You want to enter your search term thusly: "my game name", or "my business name" . The quotation marks tell Google that you want an exact match on the term as entered, minus capitalization. Google ignores capitalization for search terms. This will save you sifting through a bunch of garbage you aren't interested in. Also you might want alerts to look for www.mywebsitename.
    \n[*]Command line options. Use: /backlinks my website name. For example: /backlinks story-games . /pdf "my game name" for you pdf sellers. Also alerts yourpdfname.pdf couldn't hurt for keeping an eye open.
    \n[*]Doing all this means you won't have to monitor a bunch of websites to see if folks are talking about your game, google will let you know with enough speed that you can get involved in the conversation if you want. I think the biggest delay I've experienced is three days from when it's mentioned.(unless it's knife fight, or now the forge, there needs to be an index only once a day/hour/week option for robots.txt. Write that on one of those hallway dry erase boards Alexander?)
    \n[/ulist]
    \nOrganization
    \n[ulist]
    \n[*]Now you have all this info coming into your gmail account. You need to learn about Filters and Labels.
    \n[*]Labels allow you to create labels to organize your email in. For instance I have a theory from the closet label. I mark email with this label so it's easy to find and look at on a macro level. You might want a label for myfirstgame, mysecondgame, and mywebsite. You make labels by clicking on settings in the upper right corner of your gmail page, and then labels.
    \n[*]Filters allow you to do different actions with incoming mail. I mainly use this to attach Labels, and to auto archive some things. For instance: I have my emails from netflix marked with a netflix label, and then archived so I don't have to see them, but they're very easy to look at if I have a problem, or to delete later when they build up. I have email from my theory from the closet address marked with a theory from the closet label and then starred so it stands out.
    \n[/ulist]
    \n
    \nI look forward to seeing your presentation after it's typed up... I'm a minor google hacker, so I'm interested.

    \nI could swear that I had to manually enable this. In any case, if you find it's not automatically replying using the address the email came in (or if you want to change that), you can go to the "Settings" page, click in on the "Accounts" tab, and go to "When I receive a message sent to one of my addresses."

    Email from my other domains gets forwarded to my Gmail account, and Gmail handles that pretty well. I'm pleased with how Gmail does mail overall, and I used to swear I'd never give up using PINE.\n
    You want to enter your search term thusly
    \nIncidentally, if you end up naming your product after a common term, you'll be sifting through a lot of chaff. If I want to find out if people are talking about my podcast, Master Plan, I have a royal pain of a time finding stuff about me versus, well, everyone else in the world who is using that term. Even if you limit it by adding other keywords, like "podcast" in my case, will you still be drowning in a see of misinformation while potentially missing relevant content. Now, don't let that dissuade you from naming your product whatever you want, just be sure you know what you're in for.
