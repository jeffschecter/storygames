\n Hi, I have a question and I hope it hasn't already been asked (couldn't find anything with the search engine of the boards)...
\n
\nWhat game or game hack would you recommend for a group who is searching for a game system that could work well with the Transformers setting. I'm especially searching for a system that could work as a celebration of the movies' color, and helps/rewards for creating flashy stunts and action.
\nI doubt that moral issues should be a central part of such a game apart from a simple good vs. evil dualism.
\n
\nShock: could for instance be interesting if one sees it from the transformers' perspective of having lost one's planet and found a new, inhabitable yet already inhabited one, so something akin to a diaspora shock. But it makes the story drift into complex issues, which is not the movies' point... although it would be interesting, in my opinion. Much more interesting if you take their situation and put in humans instead of Transformers, so a human diaspora after Earth's destruction to an alien inhabited world where the human species splits into two factions, one of invaders and one of protectors of the new world.
\nFarewell to the Transformers' color.
\n
\nPrimetime Adventures could be used, but it helps little with the action part.
\nAny other ideas?
\nThanks in advance.

    \n Okay, so this is going to sound crazy and I throw it out alot but Dogs in the Vineyard with the Town Creation part hacked to shit.

    The reasons are these: First, I always felt Transformers was about relationships and what fighting for what you thought was right would cost you. Second, it scales well and handles cinematic action perfectly. Thirdly, Traits 4d10 I'm a Fucking Big Rig. Finally, Escalation seems to work pretty well too.

    You'd need to make sure that the initial situations put alot of pressure on the crew and change the whole demonic influence bit. The colour is what needs changing and this isn't a insignificant hack but it can be done.

    This won't work if you have more than three players.

    Otherwise, PTA or FATE.

    I don't think I'd recommend Shock: for this. It's pretty much the opposite game. None of the players will let "Good vs. Evil" go unquestioned, cuz that's where all the fun is. Shock: is really character-hostile, and you won't be able to count on your heroes remaining uncompromisingly good while eventually pulling through and winning. Your fellow players won't let you. The system's all about moral compromise as the ground changes under your feet.

    \n

    I think I'd go for Solar System, myself. Come up with Secrets that fit transformations, keep the keys, and use the color from the cartoons.

    \n My 2 cents

    You should have players make 1 human and 1 transformer. The human shouldn't be around when the transformer is around, and there should always be at least 1 human around. Humans should be able to have a positive effect on a transformer's abilities. They can also accomplish tasks impossible for a giant robot. However, they're obviously very squishy. Humans should also have the final say (transformers often defer to the humans in the fiction).
