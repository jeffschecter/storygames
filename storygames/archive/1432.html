[GAMA Survey] Motivational Factors in Gaming

GB_Steve
September 2006 edited September 2006 in Play Advice
The GAMA survey in the Wargamer gives Discovery Narrative and Creative Control and the two stastically significant factors in gaming. On the next page is says that what they call "Pleasurable Immersion" is not a factor for Fantasy Role-Players.

These factors were defined as follows from text responses to the survey:
The Discovery Narrative, having an interest in the game's narrative elements that allow a level of wish fulfillment.
The underlying motivational components of this factor were:
the game's story
explore places I normally couldn't
interesting characters in the game
do something I normally can't
wondering what came next

Creative Control, enjoying the ability to manipulate gaming elements.
The underlying motivational components of this factor were:
creating game components
modifying rules, scenarios
design, create part of the game

Pleasurable Immersion, feeling present in the game to the exclusion of other thoughts, worries.
The underlying motivational components of this factor were:
reduce stress
playing, not watching
feel immersed
really getting into the game

Other factors mentioned were The Challenge of Playing, having a sense of accomplishment over a hard task being overcome and completed; The Exciting Alternative, seeing game play as a stimulating way to spend time, divert from less interesting tasks; Competition with Peers, having some skills and abilities to compare with others, prove self as better; Catalyst for Socializing, playing the game as a reason to spend time with others;

Obviously this survey is probably not a survey of the kinds of gamers who come here but I found it interesting that creative control rated so highly given that in many RPGs this is very limited or even denied.
