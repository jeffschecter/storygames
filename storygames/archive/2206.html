    Posted By: shreyasAnyone actually use those q-workshop dice? I remember seeing them at GenCon and thinking that these are not something that I could read at arm's length in anything less than perfect lighting.
    I have some Q-Workshop Fudge Dice and they're ok. Someone used some regular QW polys in a game I played at Dragonmeet and yeah, from more than 30 cm away they're pretty, but unreadable.

    I also bought some of Anne Stokes' (no relation that I know of!) QW dice for Claire as an anniversary present. They're fairly readable once you get the hang of them.

    It's also worth pointing out that when you get QW dice (at least all the ones I've had) they smell rather... unpleasant. I assume that this is some kind of lubricant used in their construction, but it's very hard to get off without taking the ink off them too. Claire described the smell as "3 day old disco knickers", which I think is pretty evocative.

    On a more pleasant but not much more useful to Jason note, em4 do all kinds of plain polydice in all sizes, but tend to only do multiples of 5. Plus, y'know, they're in the UK and shipping would probably be horrible.

    Hi Jason,

    You'll likely find Gamestation competitive for prices. I haven't ordered from them personally but when I did some searching because I was designing a game that used a lot of colored dice they had some of the best deals I could find on single opaque dice. They responded fairly quickly to my email with questions, and from what I understand they do a lot of conventions. They also discount by bulk. Some dice are small discounts for ten or more, and some dice can get to 50% off on orders of 1000. I understand you are likely the first, not the second, just wanted to add all the data points I have.
