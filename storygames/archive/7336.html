    \n I got a few:
    \n
    \n- Try to catch any Cruxshadows or Voltaire show you can go to.
    \n
    \n- Maximize your efforts, but grabbing a convention book right up front. It's got the schedules for everything and its good for making sure you get to do all the things you can. A lot of panels will often cross times with other things you want to do.
    \n
    \n- Don't stress yourself off with getting to the Dealer rooms. They bring extras of everything. I usually wait until the last day to buy anything. Works well every year.
    \n
    \n- Be nice to everyone. But i think that's a general rule.
    \n
    \nHope that helps you. I'll be there too. Who's knows if we may run into each other.

    \n Oh, advice...
    * Take the minimum gaming gear you can, in your backpack. It gets REAL tiring lugging a bunch of books you use once, if at all, all con.
    * Bring your own drinks. Cons are notoriously expensive, and Atlanta pricing just makes that worse.
    * Leave the hotels to eat. It's a ton cheaper, and the walk will do you good.
    * Speaking of which: comfortable walking shoes.
    * Business/contact cards. It's a ton easier to trade cards than write down info.
