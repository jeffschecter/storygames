\n Over at Grognardia, James Maliszewski is talking about Arduin's XP system:\n
...I do find it interesting to see some of things that Dave Hargrave did think worthier of granting XP than acquiring treasure. Here's just a few:

* Death (with successful revival)
* Being sole survivor of an expedition
* Doing spells of tremendous magical import
* Being cursed
* Being point man
* Being expedition leader
* Coming within one point of dying
* Being rear guard
\nWow, that list is almost like some hippy Keys or something.

Just wanted to share.

    \n Emperor's Choice has republished the first three volumes of the Arduin Trilogy with a new layout and organization, which is still a strange reading experience. It's best approached as a toolkit of stuff an OD&D; DM might find useful (I have, e.g. prices for intelligent swords) rather than a game per se: insights into play style are definitely there but are as cryptic and koan-like as in the original three little books of D&D.;
    \n
    \nDave Arneson's First Fantasy Campaign (1977) also had rules where you got XP for spending treasure on your PC's special interests, generating event hooks for the DM in the process.

    \n Also in 1977 there was an article in The Dragon called "Orgies, Inc." that had similar rules for earning XP for blowing treasure on stuff that mattered to your character, like philanthropy, sacrificing to a god or demon, or (of course) throwing an orgy. This post at The Mule Abides has a quote from Arneson's FFC, a rundown of the Orgies, Inc. guidelines plus the awesome Trampier illustration that went with 'em.
