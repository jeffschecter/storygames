\n From the always reliable and versatile oil bottle to the intelligent living-armor, what was the weapon, item of mcGuffin you liked the most? Oil bottles are still my favorite, since our Pathfinder GM allowed us to use it in so many different situations. The first time, we spilled it over a senile beholder and drop a torch on it. That was on level 1. We're on level 4 and it seems that when we go back to the dungeon we will still find it burning (GM stated magical fires burn for ages). The last time I tied powder charge to the bottle to get rid of a zombie problem. Not to mention you can always pour some on you for some bonus to your escape artist skill...

    \n Right now, it's a little toy we've invented that we call Dark Tiles. They're carryable two dimensional energy absorption devices that suck up light, sound or any other kind of energy. The user is perpetually shrouded in a dark haze that makes it easier to hide in darkness and sneak around. As an unintended off shoot of the technology, when they touch, they form a 4 dimensional pocket dimension and the user is drawn into it, vanishing along with the tiles from ordinary space. When the user separates the tiles, they are returned to normal space.

    They come in extremely handy. My players have had immeasurable fun exploring their use and debating possible uses.

    \n In Nobilis, I once played the power of Dreams, who had the Sword of Nightmares, an obsidian-blade whose surface reflected the worst fears of whoever viewed it. It was explicitly hinted to have potential not yet unlocked, but honestly that one schtick (a sword, so good at fighting, and linked to people's fears) was enough combined with Nobilis 3e's Treasure rules. He didn't use it much but when he DID draw it forth it felt so badass.

    In an old D&D 3.5 game, I had a warlock with an eternal wand of servant horde. Eternal Wands had per-day uses, rather than charges, and Servant Horde was a spell that got you something like 1d6+level unseen servants for level hours, so in the wand's case, my warlock was basically never without a small army of unseen servants at his beck and call. We made more jokes about his fully furnished portable hole ("Looks like it's time to get in my hole again, guys") but that wand was nice, in a sense of luxury and style.

    Running Everway, I once had the characters acquire a ruby key from a man of dubious morality. Used in any door, it transformed that doorway into a portal to an extradimensional harem, staffed with succubi bound to respect and obey the owner of the key and any of their invited guests. There was a back door in the harem that the key's owner was advised not to go through. The characters were way too classy to use the item's obvious intended purpose, but it made a handy extra-dimensional stowing space and means of smuggling.

    \n I was playing a minotaur warrior and had received the First Silver Sword, which had been crafted by a titan and given to the githyanki, with which they copied to make their own silver swords.

    Once I had the sword, every so often the githyanki would send strike teams to attack me and take back the sword. The GM described their entrance as cutting open holes in the air to cross between the planes. After I killed a few, some of them started to flee, so I decided that since my sword was sharper than theirs, I could do the same thing and chase them down. Except I didn't have as much practice cutting holes between the planes, so I ended up in Tartarus.

    At one of the games of mythender I ran at GenCon, one of the players used his own dismembered head to beat Odin to death.

    \n My all-time best long-term roleplaying experience featured a number of intelligent swords, two of which are still remembered by everyone in the group. Both were extremely opinionated. The Sword of Justice had no patience at all for stealth (it boomed "JUSTICE!" in a baritone voice every time it was drawn) and disagreed with most of our actions (it being all about law and order and the group, as a rule, not being so into that). Many Things Specific was very talkative, equally opinionated, but mysterious with a very sensitive ego that needed to be coddled.

    Sort of had to be there.

    \n Once in our Anima Campaign I put the players in the main villain's armory, and let them come up with whatever item they wanted, from magical items to crude fireweapons. One choosed a full plate armor that once done and with the visor down, it summoned a war horse and showed the PC visions of blood and rage on an ancient battleground, allowing him to go into a berserker rage. Another player got a sword made of flesh and bone, which could extend to trap opponents and pull them closer. They also got living armors, psychic crystals, healing armors... and got cursed by all of these items, slowly becoming undeads attached to such hellish items.

    Later one of the players made me paid for that, when he GMed another campaign where I ended up using an armor infused with the soul of the best friend of one of the main bad guys. Yeah, it was that bad guy the one who had killed his best friend and attached him to the armor. Friendship is magic, you know.

    I went with my buddy and made a new character, and made fun of the "you start as 5th levels, but you get to buy magic items for XP". So I bought some cool one-use items and a cursed dagger, which wasn't exactly intelligent, but compared to the group he at least with in. So my buddy played the antagonistic, wise-cracking, trouble-making rusty dagger who made a simple oiling by the fireside seem lewd.

    The GM (and the players!) loved it! I loved to play with my buddy, but the adventure really wasn't worth it. The dagger, was really fun though.
