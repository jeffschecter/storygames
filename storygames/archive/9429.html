\n So, the lady and I were drifting off to sleep last night, and as so often happens, I'm geeking about a game or a novel or some other nerdery, and the lady says to me: "You know what would be cool? A competitive game about new gods, growing up and pushing the old gods out of their place. You know, because there's only so much worship and 'stuff' to go around."

When pressed, she revealed that by "stuff" she meant something similar to what pre-4th D&Ders might call "domains", or Scion players would know as "purviews"; ie., those areas in which a god has influence.

Based on her idea of a competitive RPG in that area, I tossed out a stock-market mechanic by which players would compete for a limited number of domains, and would have to fight over -something- in order to earn new points to use in the stock-market. Players could therefore -choose- to be cooperative and not all go for the same domains, but if the number of domains was limited, and the resources were limited, then eventually they would be forced to go for each others' throats. Also it would make for more fun.

I also thought it would be interesting to have some kind of oracle-system, whereby a GM could quickly toss off "OK, Thor and Hermes are both in love with the same woman, the daughter of a priestess of Isis... go!" and the players could then play in an attempt to win... well, whatever the currency of the game ends up being, by whatever means it ends up being earned.

That said, I tossed this off this morning:

Game of the Gods

What is the game about?
Jealous Gods toying with mortals for their amusement.

How does the game do that?
Domains of influence established by players for the setting (or if there is a setting?) with PCs able to bid on areas of influence; more influence in an area makes it more expensive, however (stock market mechanic).

More points earned by clever use of domains to mess with mortals? Stolen from other players? Mortal worship (sites of control, shrines, temples, sacrifices, etc.)?

Why is this fun?
Competitive, tactical resource management without crunchy charts-and-graph-paper rules, flexible for more- or less-narrative control. Oracle system to encourage quick "into-the-action" play and competition.

    \n Logos,

    Good point. Between last night and this morning, I lost the "gods warring amongst themselves" vibe that Erin's idea initially had. From our discussion, she actually meant more competition between the players as lesser gods, but I really like the idea of trying to topple the very thrones of the universe. Zeus and his kids toppling the Titans would make a good template.

    So:

    What is the game about?
    Jealous Gods wresting, cajoling, seducing, and downright stealing the worship of mortals from each other to fuel their own advancement in the divine heirarchy.

    How does the game do that?
    Domains of influence established by players for the setting (or if there is a setting?) with PCs able to bid on areas of influence; more influence in an area makes it more expensive, however (stock market mechanic). Gods can then use their domains to provide reward or wreak havoc in the mortal world, which either allows them to -earn- "ambrosia" or deprive the other players of it -- ambrosia being the "stuff" of mortal worship which gives gods their power (and allows them to buy into the market).

    Ambrosia should be a limited resource. Acts of generosity or reward can inspire loving worship. Acts of terror can either inspire fearful sacrifice or simply be used to drive worship away from another god. Domains should also be limited. The smaller the available amount of ambrosia and the fewer domains, the less "godly" the gods will be, and the more cutthroat their battles.

    Why is this fun?
    Competitive, tactical resource management without crunchy charts-and-graph-paper rules, flexible for more- or less-narrative control. Oracle system to encourage quick "into-the-action" play and competition.

    ***

    A new thought: perhaps what the god does to mortals will earn them different kinds of ambrosia. Loving worship gives "influence" ambrosia, which can be spent on improving domains OR on influencing other gods; fearful worship gives "pain" ambrosia, which can be spent improving domains OR on directly attacking other gods.

    Godly conflict can then take four basic forms:
    1) Steal or destroy another god's worship (kill, coerce, or seduce their followers);
    2) Encourage your followers to wage war against the followers of another god (like 1, but less-direct);
    3) Use the stock market to "steal" another god's domains (gradually supplanting the god of a given domain in your pantheon);
    4) Direct confrontation, either through Influence or Pain (spend raw god-juice/ambrosia in directed challenge).

    Edit for clarity
