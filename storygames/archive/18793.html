\n Hello, story gamers and friends,

Months ago Game Chef 2013 concluded. A winner was declared for each participating language. In the case of English, our winner was Ashok Desai, with a brilliant satirical game called Paper Tigers.

At that point, the winner for each language community was translated into a shared tongue and then entered into a final bloody cage-match to determine a World Champion. And then... we dropped the ball, me and all the other members of the shadowy international judging cabal. A few months slid by while we did other things.

Finally, months later, we have an announcement. A Game Chef 2013 World Champion has been declared. And you can find out who it is...

...here.

    \n Paper Tigers looks like a lot of fun. With only a little bit of reworking it would make for an excellent classroom game for a college poli sci class or a high school civics class. With a little more rework, it would work in a history class too - Roman senatorial elections or something like that.

    But as a classroom exercise, I would reduce the freeform "set any scene you want" part and structure that in a way that would lead to less blank slate syndrome among those timid with roleplay. I know my wife would look at that part and shy away.

    I'll see if I can get my wife interested in using this for her 6th and 7th grade social studies students.
