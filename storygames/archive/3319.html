\n In my near-future sci-fi game of youthful rebellion, Misspent Youth, characters' traits exist in pairs. One version of the trait is the pure, youthful one. The other is one that is twisted, darkened, and rendered cynical as a result of taking The Authority's aid to win an otherwise-lost conflict. Here are some examples:

Spoiled/Selfish
Altruism/Manipulative
Fast/Efficient
"I'm a rabble-rousing community leader."/"This town's going to listen to me if they know what's good for them."

Right now the game terms I'm using for these are Free and Sold, but I'm not really happy with those. Free is okay, if a bit cliche, but Sold is way way supercliche, and also doesn't quite get the meaning I'm going for. Ideally I'd like to use Commodified, since that is absolutely the right explicit and connotative meaning I'm going for. The problem is it's a clunky, blah word. Another thing is it might be nice to be able to use the game term as a verb, since the way they get these traits flipped is by The Authority offering them the chance to flip it. If the word is easily verbable, I can then say something like, "The Authority may offer to X the Trait."

Any ideas? Right now I'm thinking Pure/Twisted.
