\n I just finished reading over Itras By and I couldn't help but think the Chance Cards could A) Be used outside the system most definitely and Could be used almost in the way Mythic GM Emulator works. The cards aren't exactly perfect for GM-less or making a game GM-full. The cards have the ability to distribute control of a game's direction outside of a single GM and Itras By itself encourages the players to establish what they see and experience. I'm thinking of incorporating the cards into an AW or MH session at some point and see how it effects the gameplay.

    \n I love those whimsy cards. Thinking they could do a lot to supplement GM duties in a game. Maybe...you're playing a three hour session of AW, set a timer that goes off every 20 minutes. When it goes off, one player pulls a Whimsy card and must interpret how that fits in to the current situation. I was reading a thread from awhile back where @lumpley explained how to distribute the MC duties of building fronts to make AW MC-ful, I think these cards would add an element of fun disruptiveness.

    Player A is running a front, Player B gets to pull a Whimsy Card and as a result Player A's front is changed in someway. My personal preference is that I love anything that drops the unexpected into the middle of the game and makes me have to think on my toes. One of the big reasons I despise all the homework that goes into a 4e session. Thank you immensely for these Whimsy Cards, they will definitely be getting used by me.

    \n The Whimsy Cards are quite powerful and I suspect they could introduce too much chaos if used too often. They're a good fit for the dreamlike Surrealism of Itras By, but in something like AW the result might be more like post-apocalyptic Fluxx.

    The original directions for the cards say to have each player draw one at the start of the session, which they can play at any time. I don't think you get a replacement after using yours. In the one game I played them in (RuneQuest) one usage saved a character from certain death and another introduced a major plot twist. Strong mojo!

    For more frequent usage, I could envision cards that are more vague and ambiguous, nudging the tone of play or suggesting new possibilities. For instance, you could use Tarot cards (or Everway cards!) or throw an I Ching hexagram. Those Dixit cards are pretty cool too, to draw in another current thread
