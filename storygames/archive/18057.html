\n Hopefully this is simple and i just missed something. There are a couple instances I can find where Hold is listed as like 1+1 (instead of 2 or whatever). Take the Saavyhead's 'Bonefeel' move for instance:

Bonefeel: at the beginning of the session, roll+weird. On a 10+,
hold 1+1. On a 7\u20139, hold 1. At any time, either you or the MC can
spend your hold to have you already be there, with the proper
tools and knowledge, with or without any clear explanation
why. If your hold was 1+1, take +1forward now. On a miss, the
MC holds 1, and can spend it to have you already be there, but
somehow pinned, caught or trapped.

Is there a specific game reason this is listed this way, rather than just holding 2? It seems to indicate that you and the MC each get one hold, perhaps? It says '..either you or the MC can spend your hold..' - does 'your' in this sentence mean 'the player's hold' or 'the player's personal one point or the MC's one point, respectively' or something else? I kinda think it is just separating them one for the player and one for the MC, but i wasn't really able to find anywhere this is spelled out.

    \n In this case, "1+1" means hold 1 and get a bonus when you spend it. In other words, it means 1 hold with +1 forward. Not 1 hold + 1 hold.

    If that's confusing you, replace "hold 1+1" with "hold 1*". Or try something like this, which is equivalent:

    "On a 10+, hold a green token. On a 7\u20139, hold a yellow token. At any time, either you or the MC can spend your token to have you already be there, with the proper tools and knowledge, with or without any clear explanation why. If your token was green, take +1forward now."

    If green and yellow don't work for you, you can use any other distinction you want. "Hold a dime" and "hold a nickel", for example.

    \n You still only hold one (1) total, but also take +1forward at the start of the session. when you spend it.

    So the outcomes are:

    You and the MC hold 1 collectively also take +1forward now;

    You and the MC hold 1 collectively;

    The MC holds 1 but not in a good way.
