\n I have bashed out a play-testable version of Each Star Could Be An Ally, a hot-blooded mecha roleplaying game where you must save not only Earth, but forge alliances with the myriad lost children of Earth, to fight the ravenous Von Neuman Locusts.

Playtest docs so far are at my Cambridge University Roleplaying Society page- and I'm jonesing to playtest.

We (and by "We" I mean "Any interested party") could play over ToothyChat fairly easily: Friday Afternoon (pacific time) is best for me, but I'm available in the afternoon, you know, whenever.

    \n At this point, yes, a BS hack. I don't think I've misrepresented myself in that way on my other posts, no

    I'm also going to try to get a physical game going as well, with some Berkeley friends.

    There is also a fair deal of maneuvering on a strategic map, which I haven't yet posted: the strength of the Objectives and of your allies is determined by the combined Might of units of the various forces arrayed on the map (3 Might = 30 Flow prerequisite or effective +3 Drive to that ally's fleet weapons.)

    The Vonners move in different ways than the others (they must move half of thier forces to an adjacent sector, while humans and allies can move in any way so long as 1 Might remains left behind), which ought to prove interesting. The Vonners also build much, much faster...
