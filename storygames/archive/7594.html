\nThe Guild of Blades Retail Group will be opening its first store in about 3 weeks. Pending any delays due to the city and the usual red tape anyways. \n

    \nThe Alcove was a great store. And my first as well. It was an odd duck of a game store, that had all the indie and small press stuff from the day. By virtue of that it was owned by Greenfield Hobby Distributors, who used the store as a means to help sell their slowing selling stuff and close outs. So its selection was something rather impressive compared to most game stores.
    \n
    \nHituro,
    \n
    \nYes, we print the product at our cost to inventory on our shelves. The publisher has no money vested in us selling the product through our Retailer or Wholesale programs. I can't say that we'll take and sell everything, but we're here to support small publishers and even if, for some reason, we felt a title was such that we didn't feel there was space for us to print up to inventory in the store, we still plan to add those kinds of titles to a binder with info for said titles and we can still POD print it on the spot for a customer and sell it to them. And let them browse through the binder for the odd and hard to find stuff. But here at the beginning, most any game that seems playable and presentable, we'll sell.

    \n\nOctober 2008 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n Well, traffic in the store has been pretty light yet. But then, we're still going about organizing product, putting up signs, etc. Had a rush on POD orders recently and the work to keep up on those has slowed down completing our original vision for the completed store configuration. We haven't done much in the way of local marketing as of yet, but are making prepearations to have a grand opening/ribbon cutting ceremony (the town's major comes on out to do the ribbon cutting) and will be getting the local media on board. A whole bunch of other local marketing program will be kick starting over the next couple of weeks.
    \n
    \nWe are grossly behind on getting product printed and shelves from those manufacturers that have joinned our retail program. Its something we hope to get completely caught up on in this coming week.
    \n
    \nAs for online, while doing the above, we have begun a ground level reworking of our online store code, moving everything into a flexible database system (where we had begun previously with only a partially databased system and coding individual product pages manually...and quickly figured out that was going to take forever. lol). The backbone to the new store is going to organize and retail our entire store's inventory including all varieties of new games, used games, use video games, used fiction, new fiction and other books from our POD clients (we will be stocking and selling used video games, hobby games and fiction in store in an attempt to remain diverse and fairly non dependant on distributors), specialty POD merchandise, art prints, etc. All of it will be online and it will be spread out over three to four domains (we recently bought createPODbooks.com and createPODcards.com) with the way the product is organized varying based on which site you are on. In general, we expect we'll have the basic framework and most of the products live on these new sites, backed by this new code base, within about 3-4 weeks.
    \n
    \nFeedback from the store thus far seems to be that the local love that we can professionally print and bind their PDF books. Though we've had to engage a couple on the copyright issues when insisting that they show valid proof of purchase, license, or ownership of the copyright before we can legally print it. lol.
