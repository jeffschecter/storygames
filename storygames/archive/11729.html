\n Now, I'm in no way saying I'll run this, ever. But I'd like to see a con like this, and maybe I'll pick up the idea in a few years or something.

I looked at the programme for an upcoming con, and it struck me that while I'd have loved to see the debate between two of my fave designers, I kind of know what they'll be talking about and what viewpoints they'll bring to the table. Not everything, of course, and I'd _love_ to see the talk - but it'd probably be well within the bounds of our current gaming cultures.

What I'd like to see is something like the book "Second Person" in con format. Knutepunkt is sometimes like that - I'd like to see it even more. I'd like a place where someone held an interactive lecture on Borges' labyrinthine fiction, someone held an IF workshop, someone ran a 4-hour ARG, someone talked about anthropological studies on storytelling - somewhere that wasn't about indie or nordic designers talking about their recent games, but about people from other disciplines broadening our horizons.

That would be cool, I think. Would you go to a con like that?
