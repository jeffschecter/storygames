\n I consider myself a big board game, RPG and soccer fan. I have a played a very good variety of soccer-themed boardgames and recently paricipated in a PTA series based around a soccer club. The game was great and it sparked me the interest in finding or designing a soccer specific RPG.

I have been playing RPGs since 1993 and Indie games since 2007. I have played: PTA, Ribbon Drive, Breaking the Ice and Dirty Secrets. Have thoroughly read Universalis, Trollbabe, Best Friends, TSOY, The Pool, Lady Blackbird, Grey Ranks, SotC and partially Capes.

I seriously doubt there is much interest in a soccer-themed RPG and therefore I think I would have to come up with it myself.


I would like it to be a GM-ful game just because these are much more enjoyable to me. I would like a game to be based on the characters stories and motivations but I would like them to directly impact on the players' and team's performance. I was thinking that at the end of each session, there would be the MATCH where everything that happened would somewhat have an expression on the pitch and be relevant to the final score. I would really like to see a mechanic linking players actions, choices, successes and failures directly impact on the Match.

In case of victory, the player (not necessarily soccer player character) that contributed the most would be the MAN OF THE MATCH and would have benefits tat would translate into the game, probably next session. In case of defeat, the player that contributed the least would take one for the team.

I would like to have a game that could play as a one shot - A Match so to speak, or that could be played over several sessions - A Season. Or even many seasons - A History (or Dinasty). I would like the game not to be very crunchy since I would like it to be easy to pick up and play. Difficulty level like PTA, Ribbon Drive, Best Friends would be great.

Any suggestions on how I could attempt to achieve these goals?

Thank you.

    \n These are not impossible goals, and your desire for a soccer-based (or should I say proper football/futbol-based) game is shared by some of us. At least I think it'd be cool.

    The first question that comes to my mind is this: would the players be representing individuals? Or would each person take control of a team, and play against each other? If they are individuals, then how do you represent the actions of those team members who end up being NPCs?
    Also, how would you want events and motivations outside of a match influence the events inside a match?

    My initial thoughts for this fall upon one of my favorite games, Danger Patrol. It would be simple enough to divide the abilities into on-field and off-field related aspects, and Danger Patrol does allow for a fairly smooth transition between action and interlude. The action scenes could be matches, the interludes being interviews and PR opportunities, and the suspense scenes could represent their normal lives and outside events which may later influence the next game.

    This would work particularly if you run each session much like a sport-oriented Japanese anime. I know there's been a few soccer-related series, though none have really made their way to the US, but the way series such as Prince of Tennis or Slam Dunk are structured would work just as well for a soccer-related series, whether centered on high school, Nationals, or even the World Cup.

    \n The characters would be individuals associated with The Club. Players, manager, owner, agents, doctor, nurse, physical therapist or even significant others, offspring, sibblings, etc. Even the characters that are not football players or managers, would have their actions affect the outcome of The Match.

    The Match would be played at the end of the session and would last about 5-10 minutes. It could work as a summary or reflection of the session and probably give a starting point to the next session.

    In a way, The Match would be building up the whole session. For example, an impulsive act during the session could make The Club play more offensively or more aggressive or with an attacking formation (4-2-4) during the match. Failed actions during the session could lead to injuries during practices or unfavorable sports court decisions leading to suspensions. Cautious choices could lead to conservative play during The Match, etc...

    The game would still be about the characters: jealousy, intrigue, overcoming adversity, growth, etc... The Club would be the background, synthesis, thermometer and end-result of all the choices, decisions, actions, successes and failures.

    \n We are back playing a soccer-themed campaign. The solution was simple. We are using Archipelago as a system for conflict resolution but it would have worked just as well with PTA.

    Instead of leaving The Match to the end of the session, after each conflict, we cut to the future and describe a highlight of the match. Yes, and... and yes, but... will have positive developments for the team and no, and... no, but... and help is needed are good for the opposition. At the end of the session, we wrap up the match. This way, the match represents how the characters did that week.
