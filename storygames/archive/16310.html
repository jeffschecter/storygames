    \n These are my favorite 2 player games in which the theme is very strongly implemented:
    Agricola (Farming)
    Jambo or Race for the Galaxy or Eminent Domain (if she's into strategic card games)
    Twilight Struggle (Cold War, lots of strategy, lots of history)
    Arkham Horror or Pandemic or Ghost Stories (if she's into cooperative games)
    Tales of the Arabian Nights (almost all story but so much fun)
    Mr. Jack (deduction)
    Battleground: Fantasy Warfare or BattleLore or Commands and Colors: Ancients (if she's into wargames)
    High Frontier (hard science space missions)

    \n I really like Horus Heresy. It's got theme up in all its holes. I said this about it, on a website once:

    This game is bursting with replayability (6 missions, any of which are deep enough to play multiple times), and full of high-level strategy with a good bit of tactics. It\u2019s got handfuls of great minis\u2013bits & pieces galore. If you\u2019re like me, and you like the fiction & fluff of Warhammer 40K but think the tabletop game is sorely lacking in the game design department, this may very well scratch your itch. I could play it again and again, and it\u2019s always a good battle that makes you think both in broad strokes and action-to-action. Fantasy Flight and Jeff Tidball knocked this one out of the park.

    \n Hints on what sorts of games you have liked in the past would help.

    Dominion is an excellent non-collectible card based game and plays well with 2-4 (it plays unusually well with 3 - I find it much more challenging to find games to do that, than to find games that work well with 2)

    Do you like abstract competitive (chess, checkers) type games? Gobblet is one of my favorites, of that sort. Pentago is also good.

    Do you like historical games? 9 Men's Morris is a classic for good reason (though it's arguably "solved" by modern computers, but not in a human-easy way). Shut The Box is good fun. And Captain's Mistress, which is basically Connect 4, is also good.

    Do you like war games? Blood Bowl is a miniatures-based footbal sim game. It's long to play, but quite fun. And of course, there's all sorts of other stuff by Citadel and others.

    Ticket to Ride is an excellent board game. It plays decently with 2 players, though it's better with 3 or 4. Great one to have on hand when friends come over.

    Any sorts of hints you could give about preferences would be helpful. It's harder to hit the target when you're shooting in the dark.

    \n My wife really like "Jambo". But it's a little dry on the theme for me.
    "Dominion" is a good choice, but not very theme-y when you get down to it. But again, the wife loves that game. There's a bunch of Dominion-ish games that have come out recently that supposedly do the theme aspect better, but are considered to be lesser games (not that they are bad, just that Dominion is that good). Things like Ascension or Eminent Domain. Maybe Thunderstone or Nightfall.

    Personally, I can't say enough wonderful things about "Mr. Jack," but my wife can't wrap her head around deduction/hide yourself games. But to me it captures the theme remarkably well and is a fantastic battle of wits.

    There's a couple of lighter co-ops I can recommend for 2 players, with decent theme:
    "Forbidden Island" is a good choice is you want a lighter Pandemic.
    Also "Castle Panic".
    "Ghost Stories" feels way too mechanical to me for it to be theme-y.

    Theres always Magic or Pokemon, I guess. Grab a few starter decks and just play those. Or any of the living card games that suits your theme from Fantasy Flight, I suppose.

    Pimping my own horn, this is the two-player game that's sort of theme-y I came up with for the "Stage One" contest that Mr. Walton was running.Fall of the Titans. Which I'm always looking for outside playtesters.
