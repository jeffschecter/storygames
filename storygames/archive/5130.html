\n I was having a fit of nostalgia the other day. Star Frontiers was the first game I cut my teeth on, and I was reflecting that out of all the times I was a player, I picked:

Human (x1)
Vruusk (x10+)
Other : never.

Back in those days, I thought the Dralasite were gross, and didn't want to play a slow-moving race (Vruusk + Exoskeleton = He fires bullets at you, then runs alongside them to sign them with a pen in-flight as they tumble towards you). Yazarian never really appealed to me too much.

Best moment: Me and my dad, together, took out a Sathar mothership. It involved a hoverbike and gauss rifles.

Any other Star Frontiers players from way back when? Any nostalgia for the days of hot turquoise jumpsuits and teased permed hair?

-Andy

    \n I was always the GM. I just reopened my old magenta Knight Hawks box and rifled through the old books in there. A lot of the rules are still there for Star Frontiers and Alpha Dawn and Knight Hawks and all that. I also found my old map I drew of a city, New Hope, Triad, Cassidine. It has a monorail and some kind of video rental store and other mundane crap. I think a lot of that stuff got used in play, too. I remember players wanting to do stuff that wasn't on the map and getting frustrated and making them stay within the grid.

    It's on a folded sheet of that enormous 1"-grid paper from The Armory. Maybe I'll scan it and post it.
