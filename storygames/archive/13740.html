\n So, crowd-source me this:

Would you print out, cut-to-size, put-into-sleeves-with-backing a set of cards to playtest a game?

Assume that the hypothetical game that i'm speaking of would appeal to you personally,
regardless how likely you are to like a game that i might make.

I'd love to know the whys, too.

(For bonus points, you can also express the high-water mark of prep you would be willing to craft in order to playtest a game; perhaps that will inspire others to make games with many difficult moving parts.)

    \n I would do this for up to about 75 cards. I know this because I've done that exact procedure hundreds of times to prep for Magic tournaments, back when I played somewhat seriously. I'd also be willing to build a handful of custom dice (no more than about a half-dozen, say), paste up a board-game board as long as it was no bigger than four sheets of letter-sized paper, cut out no more than about two dozen more complex shapes from paper/board/foam/fabric, or sew up to around six feet of stitches in ordinary fabric - again, there's precedent for all of these activities in my playtesting history.

    If you can design a game that wants me to crochet up to about one hat and/or one scarf, I guess I'd do that too.
