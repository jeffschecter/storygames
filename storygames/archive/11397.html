    \n Oh, just some interesting reference stuff.

    So there's many ways to write "KAGEMATSU" in Japanese. Well, "MATSU" is standard (\u677e, pine). However, for the KAGE part there are a lot of kanji that can make that part up, including words like "Shadow", \u201dShade", "Reflection", "Yin", "Fan".

    The one I picked was based on the name of a real Ukiyo-e artist of old (a very minor one at that) named Utagawa Kagematsu. His "KAGE" means "scenic".

    Anyway, here's some links to calligraphy written by Utagawa Kagematsu:

    http://members3.jcom.home.ne.jp/daido/morimoto.html

    And here's some ukiyo-e illustrations for books (basically samurai tales) that Kagematsu did:
    http://www.wul.waseda.ac.jp/kotenseki/search.php?cndbn=%89%cc%90%ec+%8c%69%8f%bc
    (click on the pic twice, then the link that says "HTML")

    Just for folks that really wanted to dip into the lore that can spawn from something as simple as "How should this name be written in Chinese characters?"
