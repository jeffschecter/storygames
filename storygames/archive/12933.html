    Game Creation: Given this description of game play I'd like to facilitate, how do I design to that experience?

    Posted By: ElizabethOther sites specifically about game design fell out of fashion. People came here. Despite the admonition that Story Games is about play, lots of the emergent SG culture became about design, partially due to the rather laid-back modding of the site.
    \nActually, it goes back further than that. This was originally the place for Forge folks to talk about gaming in general non-theory ways, exactly like the old "Forge Birthday Forum". So even here, early on, while there was a stated goal of "gaming discussion", there was still lots of idle chatter, marketing talk, design discussions, etc. And there were even actual entire design contests within the site for the first few years.

    It wasn't until later that I started trying to find a way to split off the "from the ground up" ("Hey guys, can someone help me with this dice resolution mechanic?" "Hey, here's my game idea, what do you think?") threads from the forum, simply because about three or four years in, they started drowning out the other conversations. Due to a clear limitation of the forum (which I think I just came up with an idea for a solution, huh. Probably too late to be helpful, but will throw it by Dev later*), the discussions couldn't be easily "moved to the side".

    I didn't want to just say "no more design". Well, actually, I kinda did, but enough people from all walks of life kept asking about it, so I figured it could use its own space rather than me saying "stop that" each time it came up.

    I do think that idle talk about marketing and "what motivates you to design?" and the like is "design focused", and on an aggregated level probably makes the site seem design focused. I'm not ready to throw that bathwater out yet, though, even though it would be the Final Move to make this place Play Only. If the site gets as overrun with marketing/idle-designer-talk as it was a year or so ago with "help me with my game" posts, then yeah I'll aim at those next. But for now, it seems to be a thing that comes and goes in waves, usually around major cons. So I'm keeping an eye on those threads, which are a majority of the 20-some threads counted.
