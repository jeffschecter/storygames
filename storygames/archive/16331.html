    >Examine secretary

    Tim looks over at you through horn-rimmed glasses. "I'd say good morning, but it's about two hours too late for that. Lemme guess: another late night?"

    "Lemme guess, another night at home with your mother?"

    "At least my mother still lets me in her house."

    "Not my fault she can't take a joke."

    "What was that? I couldn't hear it over you boiling over, Ms. Pot."

    You grumble indistinctly. Lousy goddamn secretaries and their lousy goddamn sass. You're getting too old for this shit.

    >Inventory

    You have:

    A wallet
    A pack of cigarettes, nearly empty.
    A book of matches, actually empty.

    Equipped weapon: Revolver

    >Examine matchbook

    Nothing special. Just a matchbook you picked up while visiting Fawlty O'Laugic's bar downtown. I mean, it's not like a random matchbook found on someone's person could be a real solid piece of evidence for anything.

    Something about that reminds you of lampshades for some reason. There's probably a witty remark there somewhere.

    >Call bookie

    Finally, something approaching a plan. And that plan is apparently hassling people who owe you money.

    You dial up Jack. The phone rings seven or so times before he answers.

    "Hey. Hey! You there? Got those two-hundred clams yet ya skeezebal-"

    "What? Oh, oh God...listen Coque, I'm kinda in a jam here. I, uh...I kinda ran my mouth off when I shouldna. You gotta come here, they're not listening to reason OH GOD NOT THE SHINS! AAAAAUUUGGH!"

    The lines cuts out.

    >Examine revolver

    Aw yeah, this baby is top of the line, real piece of craftsmanship. You'd bet your life on this gun, which you've done again and again and still remain standing.

    By which you mean it's an old piece of crap you picked up at a pawn shop when you got into the PI business. Still, the old .38 has always had your back.

    You've named it Chekhov for reason's you can't put a finger on. Just felt appropriate.
