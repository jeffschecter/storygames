\n So the player who is the Sgt. leveled up after our first session (the session rocked by the way) and gained rank quite easily due to his now 7 NFA. Now being a Lt. he gained another Strength (he of course used his first up) and it looks as though he can easily rank up again next mission (70%) chance.

I'm not sure how the other players feel but it seems as though having a Captain by the Second/Third session seems a bit whacked. The missions are going to evolve drastically and rank separation will spread rather quickly. I was looking forward to exploring the gritty mess of the missions and taking a moral pounding from your Superior Officers before accelerating too quickly but it seems as though politics and large scale operations will enter play rather soon.

Quite simply, am I worrying about nothing? Have others yet to experience players quickly becoming officers while other grunts are left behind? Is it still satisfying for everyone involved?

Lastly, a quick question: Do you roll for rank before or after leveling up in between Missions?

    Still, it's possible to advance to the officer ranks pretty fast, assuming no one interferes (which is a pretty big assumption). Some grunts just go for rank every chance they get.     This dynamic is part of the game. How the players deal with it is one of the cooler emergent bits of play.

    \n The order from the book is strictly...

    Gain Level
    Gain Automatic Improvement
    Make a Development Roll
    Test for Demotion

    As for the grunts...they're getting their free improvements after every mission for their grenades weapons right? If I were gaming it... and I were the Trooper... I'd Force Weakness on Studd Boy early on a planet to get that off his sheet. I'd then either run out of an encounter (change range beyond Far) or use my own Weakness to leave him in a hot Threat-filled encounter on his own. Sure, he'll take out a Threat Token every turn, but probably get killed in the process if he can't change range enough. If he burns a Strength... good. I can bug out of the next encounter too, or let my PC die rather than use a Strength to save him. Then I'd dice off with the other Trooper for the field promotion. Ouch. It depends on the dice, really. A high FA means you'll probably kill, but it doesn't say whether you get killed doing so, the dice rolls decide that.

    (Oh, and you all get to Level in the end if you play the campaign long enough.)
