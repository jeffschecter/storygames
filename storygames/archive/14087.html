\n Story gamers, I don't think I have much time left.

Rat-man mutants are invading my small mountain village. They're vicious and quite angry. I tried to reason with some at first, but they charged through my front door and attempted to grab me.

I fled to the basement. It doesn't have any windows or exterior access, which means that the only entrance is through the thick wooden door at the top of the wooden stairs. I can hear those ferocious creatures skittering about through my house, occasionally trying to gnaw their way through the door before getting distracted. I barricaded it with everything I could find - spare lumber, an ironing board, a few cinder blocks. It's obviously not much.

Here's what I have: my iPhone (on which I'm typing this), the washer & dryer (as well as all the laundry room supplies you'd expect), a dry cellar containing some canned goods and some spare mason jars, a table saw and some basic construction tools, spare lumber, cinder blocks, an assortment of industrial strength cleaners, several feet of spare PVC piping, three sleeping bags, a tent, a tarp, and my little sister's ballet gear (my family ran out of storage space and asked me to store it here).

My fiancee Kaleigh just called me. She saw the rat-people and fled into the bedroom. She locked herself inside of an old-fashioned treasure chest-style trunk, because she didn't think she'd be safe in the closet. Not the best choice, but what's done is done. She said the rats are trying to claw and chew their way through the chest, but haven't managed much purchase yet. They also tried to pick it up, maybe to toss it down the stairs or something gruesome like that. But the only managed to tip it sideways - meaning they must be relatively weak. She's worried they'll chew through, or that she'll run out of oxygen if they don't. In the chest with her is her cell phone, several blouses, and a ziploc baggie full of narcotics that I'm not supposed to tell anyone about.

Things are dire. I saw them tear one of my neighbours in two, before they charged my house. Me and Kaleigh don't have much time. I don't know where I found the time to make this detailed post. Help!

What should we do?

    \n First of all, Ratmen typically agress only when they feel threatened. Understanding their reason for attacking might be key to survival here. I'm not going to lecture you as you don't have much time. So, I'll just hit a few salient points:

    Ratman agression is typically short lived. If you can rout or wait out the Ratmen, I believe you will be safe. The first few hours are vital to your survival.

    Ratmen with an impaired sense of smell, in most cases, will not attack. Find a way to deaden their senses and you are one step closer. Zinc Sulfate, as found in fertilizers is a good choice.

    Ratmen are effectively colorblind and have blurry vision at best. Extreme light will give them pause in their attack, so turn on all lights and keep any flashlights close to shine them. For god's sake, do not turn off the lights! They already know you are there.

    Ratmen are hard wired to be susceptible to attacks to their rear. Though they are not fatal, a wound to the rear is often more effective than a fatal wound to the chest or head. Ratmen typically fight among themselves until one has taken a bite on the other's rump at which time the wounded submits and the victor asserts his domiance.

    Finally, Ratmen are highly motivated by avoidance behaviors and once they have sumbitted in an area will not likely return.

    So, my suggestion is to blow some fertilizer (if you have some) or alternatively some laundry detergent through the PVC pipes at the attacking Ratmen. If you can rout them from a room, it is likely safe to assume you have won that room back (due to classic avoidance behavior). If some sneak past the fertlizer or detergent then try to blind them and smack, punch or stab them in the rear. This will send them running for easier prey.

    Good luck. I've only had to do this once and while I was mostly successful, I really hate typing with one hand.
