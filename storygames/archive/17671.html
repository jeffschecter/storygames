\n Was re-reading the the book the other day and thought it would be interesting to hack the system and turn it into an inner city high school. With many of the big city schools upping the ante in police-like security could be a very interesting re-interpretation. The Governor is now The Principal. The Dimber Damber becomes Mr. or Ms. Popular. The power struggle in a public high school could be epic. Re-work the Planet Survey and Colony Descriptions as Travelogue Descriptions of Cities and Blurbs About the High Schools from their websites.

    Oh man, why did you move the student council away from the Emancipist position? That was perfect.

    My only concern is that there's no fluidity between sides of the ladder. The Principal will never be a burnout and vice versa.
    \nStudent council members can't become Principal, that's the only hitch. They are the perfect analog for Emancipists in that they are the "sell-outs" to the establishment. That's the one part of the ladder that's giving me headaches. Everything else fits really well IMO, but that bottom rung of The Faculty.

    I'm also seeing the ladder as two separate but equal paths. While the Principal can never become a burn-out, he can end up as a custodian. Instead of two paths that connect, I'm looking at them as two rival paths to power. A popular kid in a high school can be many times more influential than a principal. I also see a Russian Revolution re-skinning in Durance too.
