\n I thought it would be fun to do a game where you stack up rulebooks from your old collection of RPGs and try to squeeze the rules you like out of them, while trying to choke back the rules your fellow GMs were trying to get from their own piles of nostalgia.
\n
[url=http://www.geocities.com/Area51/Quasar/9229/gam/canib.html]http://www.geocities.com/Area51/Quasar/9229/gam/canib.html[/url]\n
\n
\nI can't see it being a great game, but I think it's a step in addressing several growing problems for me in the world of tabletop rpgs.
\nNamely:
\n
\n1. Too many GMs (good ones, too) and not enough players
\n2. Looking around my squalid apartment, I see a lot of old rpg books that I like parts of, but consider most of the rest of the book crap.
\n(I don't really need all this stuff, but once you get locked into a serious collection, the tendency is to push it as far as you can.)
\n3. I like to see what's out there, so I end up in a lot of lousy rpg groups with even worse GMs. I need to hurt back, sometimes.
\n
\nAnyway, it's free. Enjoy
