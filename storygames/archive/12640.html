\n Start in the city of Tyr, ruled over by its tyrannical sorcerer-king, Kalak. Set up your characters' factions, grudges, opposition, oppression.

Then: as per canon KALAK GETS SPEAR TO THE HEAD. The game takes place in more or less real time as soon as he falls. The party tries to survive in the anarchy that follows while pursuing their own goals:

* seek revenge against your former masters
* hunt down the Templar hold outs
* payback against crooked business partners
* rescue your colleagues within the Veiled Alliance
* raid the Nobles' armories
* sack the Ziggurat

The con/one-shot version can take place within the first day; a campaign may play out a fortnight, perhaps leading up to Nibenay's templars arriving to make a treaty with whatever new government they find. (Or maybe to go all Fortinbras on the situtation, depending.) There is clearly a deadline.

I can easily imagine tracking the group's time hour by hour as they make the most of a chaotic situation. They may spend an hour in a open combat with a group of Templars, then spend two hours trying to rally support in the Warrens. I'd lay the map of Tyr before the players and ask them: what do you want? How will you get it? As DM, I'd set up some possible set pieces but mainly respond to player-driven challenges.

Perhaps, in sandbox style: after one group has played through it, allow other groups play through the scenario while these previously authored events are happening at the same time, providing a kind of baseline (but alterable) canon.

(Bonus: if you don't like D&D-rules, I think you can figure out some ways to play it out with other rules.)
