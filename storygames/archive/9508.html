\n I'm helping an author working on an encyclopedia of horror, who's wondering if there are current indie games where you play a vampire. Naturally, he knows about White Wolf's Vampire line, but wondered if there are indie games as well. Before there was World of Darkness, there were a handful of other games where you could play a vampire. Since then, it seems like it's cornered the market on playing a vampire.

I come to the font of indie wisdom, humbly.

Jae Walker

    \n The number of indie games where you CAN play a Vampire is not calculable. Think about it: Sorcerer (the demon is a parasite inside you, the need is drinking blood), Universalis, PTA, The Pool, and the list go on and on...

    If you need a few specific titles, maybe it's better to concentrate on the games where you HAVE to play a Vampire.

    Interestingly, "Annalise" is one of them, even if it is the story of the vampire's possible victims: being GM-less, every player sometimes has to play the vampire against another player...

    \n I'm interested in this question, and I'd like to elicit a bit more feedback. I hope this is in-topic for the original question.

    I'm writing pre-gen characters for Principia. One of them is Machiavelli. He has a fact that says "I am a vampire" and a drive that says "I must find a cure for the affliction of vampirism". The fact has no mechanical effect except to define something in the fiction. The drive gives you resources whenever it comes up in the story, and can eventually be discarded for a reward. It works like a key in TSOY.

    Here's my question. When you get a character sheet with this information on it, do you know how to play this character, or does it make you say WTF? Do you need a rulebook to tell you what a vampire is in this world? How much information do you need before you can play this character?

    And in a larger sense, since we agree there's room for vampires in almost any game - what happens when you decide there is a vampire in your game? Does it change how people play?

    Posted By: tony dowler...Do you need a rulebook to tell you what a vampire is in this world? How much information do you need before you can play this character?

    And in a larger sense, since we agree there's room for vampires in almost any game - what happens when you decide there is a vampire in your game? Does it change how people play?
    This is what I was trying to get at when I gave the examples of Afraid and MLWM. To me, one of the most attractive things about post-sim-by-default games is the wide range of possible roles for mechanics. Does a "vampire game" have to be one where the mechanics concern themselves with drinking blood, or does it suffice that the fiction talks about drinking blood (the sine qua non of vampires, right?), leaving the mechanics to deal with loneliness or otherness? I think that the latter, broader answer is more interesting.
