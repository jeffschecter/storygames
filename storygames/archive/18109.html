    \n Tangentially related, David Webber's book Path of the Fury contains a duo of a sentient ship and its pilot. It might be a good read for inspiration if your in the mood for pulpy spaceship, revenge story action in a similar vein.

    I've always wanted to design a game where the PC's played a ship/pilot team. Complimenting each others skills and talents. Building a realtionship and psyches perhaps, becoming irrevocably intertwined till they become two faces of the same entity. The biggest hurdle is how to make the ship an important player in times and places where a giganto-huge ship isn't practical. You can always fall back on accessing security systems and an "operator" roll, holo-projections of the ships A.I. Cortana style, android bodies or drones and that sort of stuff. But I've never seriously tackled it.

    \n The comments here really got me thinking and I just want to expand and expound on much of what has been said; not to "dispense pearls of wisdom" but as a sounding board to clarify my own thoughts (I often need a wall of text to create an echo).
    "Are there any role playing systems in which this is reflected, where the ship is essentially a member of the party?" (Again, I apologize if this was meant as a short-answer trivia question about marrying system (mechanics) to setting (fiction).

    I think it's largely system agnostic. My first thought was also JDColey's "anthropomorphizing". Any ship can have stats but the relationships make it a Character. Can the Millennium Falcon run an Imperial Blockade? Maybe. Can Han Solo pilot a ship through said Blockade? Possibly. Can Solo pilot the Falcon through an Imperial Blockade? Hellz, yeah! (That There, Been Done).

    The relationship may also have a negative side. Enterprise/Kirk ("She takes and TAKES!" or "Nevermind me. Get my ship out of here!" Needy/Demanding, Self-sacrifice) or Enterprise/Scotty ("He called the Enterprise a garbage skow!"
    Prideful/Defensive?). In my old Traveller game, a character Mustered Out with a standard decommissioned Scout class ship. Upon being dubbed the "Led Zeppelin" it instantly became a Character. Best. Ship. Ever!
    Today, I would ask my players what Trait did the Led Zeppelin gain this session. Probably things like "What Black Hole?", and "Zombie After-Burner!" (long story).

    As Player-Character, a ship might need to be organic (Lexx) or have a true A.I.(Red Dwarf) but if the setting didn't allow for these, I would suggest a para-phrasing of Clarke: Any sufficiently advanced Computer is indistinguishable from sapience (see "Turing test" to show how close we are now). Likewise, any sufficiently advanced stat block is indistinguishable from a character sheet. Problems of planet-side or indoor scenarios are solved with a representation or Icon: a hologram or robotic body come to mind.

    Hell, now I want to be the ship!

    Also, Traveller, Book 9: Robots and The Kinunir.
