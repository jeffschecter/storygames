    As I have understand it, a oppositional conflict is where the two goals are clearly ...um... opposed, and if one side wins the other must lose. "A wants to kill B. B does not want to get killed" kinda thing. A either does, or does not, kill B. There is no way for both of the goals to be true, or both false, at the same time.

    Orthogonal conflict have goals which interfere with each other (otherwise there would be no conflict at all), but are not directly opposed. "A wants to kill B. B wants to kill A right back". In this case, you can end up with A dead, B dead, both dead, or neither dead. Either goal can be true or false, regardless of the state of the other.

    As I asked Ron, you could assume that a orthogonal conflict can be reduced down into two or more oppositional conflicts with implict counter-goals; my example could be broken down into: "A wants to kill B. B does not want to get killed" + "B wants to kill A. A does not want to get killed", but in doing so, a lot of the natural timing & sublety could be lost.

    In the context of the stakes thread this is what the terms mean:

    Oppositional Conflict: Character A is trying to achieve something and all Character B wants to do is prevent that from happening. There are only two outcomes. Either Character A gets what he wants or he doesn't. Example: Amy goes to pull the gem out of the scary Aztec Idol and Bob goes to stop her. Either Amy gets the gem out of the idol or she doesn't.

    Orthogonal Conflict: Character A is trying to achieve something and Character B is trying to achieve something and those two somethings can interfere and impact each other. There are four possible outcomes. Character A and Character B both succeed. Character A and Character B both fail. Character A succeeds and Character B fails. Character A fails and Character B succeeds. Example: Amy is trying to get the gem out of the scary Aztec Idol and in an effort to stop her Bob tries to shoot her. Possible outcomes:

    Amy gets shot but manages to stagger and get the gem anyway.
    Amy gets shot and collapses failing to get the gem.
    Amy dodges the bullet but in doing so gives up her claim on the gem.
    Amy dodges the bullet and snatches the gem in the process.

    Does that help?
