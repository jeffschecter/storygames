\n Remember the Monsters Manuals from 2nd Edition AD&D;? You know the ones which were hole punched so you could add new creatures to your Monster Manual ringbinder with the fantasy monster on the front? Yeah, that one. What kind of game would work well for loose leaf publishing? I can imagine a collection of free story games released on a loose leaf format. Get the ones you're interested in and keep them together in one handy ringbinder. How do you feel about loose leaf publishing and have you ever considered it for anything you've written?

    \n For me, it's pretty simple: extensible games and loose-leaf binders go hand-in-hand.

    "Modular" is a form of extensible--here's the core game, and here's optional rules to toggle on or off by putting them into the binder--and so is a "customizable" game--here's the basic Ability, and here's several variations on it for different genres, with different Advantages and Limitations to suit.

    Less extensible (as I mean it) games are those which generate additional or supplemental content as a part of play. For instance, DitV could have been loose-leaf-bound, with the intent that new towns be added in the appropriate place, or even hacks with different escalation and fallout charts. But that's not REALLY "extending" the DitV game so much as it's either playing it normally and keeping somewhat-superfluous records (towns inserts) or hacking it away from its intent and keeping the reference in the same place in the "real" game (i.e. it's not a generic system, even if it drifts readily--unlike Universalis, which could be loose-leaf-bound for inclusion of Rules Gimmicks).

    But that's just my 2\xa2 about the theory of Why Loose Leaf. In my actual experience, loose-leaf-bound books aren't printed on heavy enough paper and, as such, fall to pieces with normal referential use in regular gaming (which makes the expense of using sheet protectors for every page more understandable).
    -----
    Speaking of "on-demand rules," that's my plan for GLASS Game Type definitions: a GM toggles in the online database ONLY the rules the game will allow, and then a player wanting a Type-specific printout of shotGLASS (the free PDF of JUST the base rules) can just click a link and the database "builds" a new book out of ONLY the active rules and permitted power levels. It's doable... but it takes some coding.
