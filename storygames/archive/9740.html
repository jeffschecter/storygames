\n So there's a lot of new folks here, and it's been a long while since we did this last.

There's a Category, like "Actual Play", "Play Advice" etc called "The Best of Story Games". These threads represent, if nothing else, the *best* (in terms of "Excellence") that the site has to offer.

It's those conversations that gave you advice to bring back to the table and use. It's the ones filled with ideas that blew your mind in some way.

Many of you have Bookmarked various threads because there was something awesome that you got from them. Those are perfect candidates.

So here's how to submit them:

1) Post to this thread.

2) In your post, drop up to six direct links to those threads you want to nominate.

3) With each thread, drop a sentence or so about why you like that thread, what it did for you. Can be as brief as you need, or detailed as you want.

After a few weeks, I'll trawl through these threads, pick the 20 or so that (based on your comments and such) fit my idea of Best Of. They'll get thrown into that category. Not all of your suggestions will make it, but we can always come back and revisit those threads in a later "Best Of - Off".

Unlike previous submissions for Best Of, there is no time range. The thread could have been from The Other Day, or Three Days After the Site went Live. Any time period is A-OK.


Ex of how to post in this thread:

"Here's my submissions:

http://www.story-games.com/forums/comments.php?DiscussionID=7789&page=1#Item_58
Generating Culturally Rich NPCs. I love the initial idea, and even used it a few times in my games. It totally worked.

http://www.story-games.com/forums/comments.php?DiscussionID=2982&page=1#Item_47
Shows how to make games more collaborative. Solid advice.

(and so on)"

Thanks!

-Andy

    \nMethodical and Mechanical RPG Design
    I'm not actually sure that the thread was good, it had a lot of a train-wreck moments in it. But even so, if you read through it you can see two conflicting and extreme examples of the "Mechanics over all" and the "Method over all" stances.

    More Game Designer Challenges
    People giving other designers design challenge, a community! (Maybe find the original one too?)

    What's Story?
    "What's Story?" I think people too often forget this, they're playing and creating "Story Games", and to know what a story is, or what you count as one matters, and of course, usually they don't mean "Story", but "Good story".

    Big GenCon Stakes Discussion
    GenCon 06 Stakes discussion, real theory discussion of worth here.

    Flow?
    Using real research from other fields to discuss RPG theory, with people from different viewpoints weighing on Immersion, including Immersion in the mechanics!

    Why do you design games?
    A thread I started, people giving their personal reasons on why they design games.
