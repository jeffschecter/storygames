    Wow, guys. Way to totally fail at awesomise. Let me show you how this is done.

    \n

    Dark Water is a polite euphemism. Everyone knows what it really is\xe2\u20ac\u201dthe blood of the Dweller in the Deep. In years past and forgotten, the first king of Octopon fought a creature of the waters in defence of his new city. It tore out his eye and wounded him mortally, and in his agony he cursed it with immortality. Too gravely wounded to heal itself, the creature sunk into the deepest watery abyss, clinging on to the edge of life by the thinnest margin. There it began to decompose and change, and the blood flowing out of its gashes became the dark water, black as ink and flowering with oil-slick colours. Itself immortal, the dark water is just alive enough to move about with angry, vengeful deliberateness.

    \n

    To this day the dark water recognizes the scent of the royal blood of Mer.

    \n From the series bible:\n
    THE IMBIBERS are a group of specially trained children who drink Dark Water. They becoming all knowing for a short span of time and then turn to stone to take a place of honor in their tribe\xe2\u20ac\u2122s sanctuary.
    \nWhich is rad in and of itself. But why would the blood of the Dweller grant this knowledge?

    Because long ago, the Dweller tunneled away from the painful light and gnawed out a home in the living heart of Mer. Dark, warm caves of soft coral riddle the planet, opening to the principle seas in deep rifts, which in their time birthed all creatures who today swim and crawl and fly. The Dweller (as he came to be called) consumed the power of that place and grew mighty, but the First King of Octupon, with all living things as his allies, defeated him before he could consume the planet's surface biosphere. But having linked his life to that Mer itself, the Dweller could no longer be killed. Instead, using dark and forgotten magics the King unmade him, extracting from the Dweller the thirteen principles which allowed him life, form, and the ability which truly made him a dark god, plundered from the world's heart: Making and remaking life. These principles crystallized into appropriate forms, which were hidden from the ever-grasping tounges and fingers of the dead god, who yet lurks in his dark, sterile tunnels, fanning his hatred for the life, emerging only to futilely consume anything he stumbles across.

    Ehn. Too much?
