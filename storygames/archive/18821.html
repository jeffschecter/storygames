\n Despite all the recent developments in VTTs, everything is still very much d&d-centric and we still have to mess around quite a bit to find a a way to play other games. What solutions have you discovered that allowed you to play stuff like Fiasco, In A Wicked Age, Marvel Heroic Roleplaying or Technoir?

A lot of the difficulties come from the fact that we don't yet have virtual dice that are customizable or that can be picked up and placed around the table. Other than that, shared character sheets are still a pain to do in something like google docs.

    \n There's a ton of people playing story games on Google Hangouts these days.

    For Fiasco, create a new Google Drive document from Template and look for "Fiasco Table": you'll still need to roll dice somewhere, but you only do that a couple of times in a game, so it's not a big problem; everything else fits nicely on the shared document.

    \n I know Quinn Murphy has played Marvel Heroic several times On Air, and I believe they were using some solution that kept dice and current scene distinctions visible as overlays of the hangout windows.
    Here is an example.

    Also, even a simple "Lower Third" with the name and portrait of your character can help.

    Oh, and let's not forget, Viewscream by Raphael Chandler has been written specifically to be played on video conference.
    http://www.rafaelchandler.com/blogger/?p=521

    ...and one of the Sundered Lands games by Vincent Baker, the "Doomed Pilgrim" one, again, is written to be played on Google Plus (or facebook, or similar platform).

    \n Roll20 seems very cool. Even though it is set up for D&D, it's simplicity allows for any kind of game. I like its simple drawing tools quite a bit and since it is a browser program, it is cross Mac-PC with no fuss (a big sticking point with my particular group).

    \n For more free form games (especially ones with multiple custom decks, dominoes, etc),
    Taebl (http://www.taebl.com) is my go-to VTT.

    For something quick and easy that only needs shared dice and labels, I like the
    Catch Your Hare dice roller. (http://catchyourhare.com/diceroller/)
