\n So I'm playing these great games of IAWA downtown in Toronto, and I think they're going very well. We've got vampires with helpful husbands, a murdered-baby devil-boy who is growing up into some kind of Rat - Satyr, child abuse, treachery within armies, and murders when mages gather.

But here's the thing.

When I go home from these, I have to spend 20 minutes walking, then 10 minutes waiting for a bus, then an hour on the bus, then a half hour walking home.

The bus, mind you, makes me nauseated every time, and I can't sleep for an hour afterwards. Blame Toronto's crappy transit system.

I hate nausea, and I hate the cold (which is only getting worse). I also hate worrying about whether I can 9:40 and be home before midnight, or if I miss the bus I have to be up well past midnight.

So when I leave our games, my good feelings get 2 hours of bad feelings attached to them.

    \n You have my sympathies. I hate hate hate being at the mercy of public transit, especially when the system sucks. It's why I ride my bike everywhere, even most of the winter here in Montreal.

    I know it may be a long shot, but you might seriously want to consider adding a bike as part of your commute. Not for the whole trip, but for cutting out some decent chunks of it. You ride to and from the bus stops (and possibly even to a different bus stop that has better, faster routes) and maybe cut 30 minutes from your overall commute. Plus, the exercise will probably help cut down on the nausea. This is assuming you can take your bike on the bus, of course.

    Just a thought. Might not work for you, but a lot of people who haven't ridden much in the city find it kind of intimidating before realizing how easy and convenient (and fun and healthy and all that blah blah) it can be when they actually give it a try.
