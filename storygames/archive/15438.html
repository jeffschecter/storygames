    \n Speaking of fire...

    Our tiefling warlord just hit paragon and (unknown to me until last night) he has Resist Fire 10.

    When he led the initial charge against the berzerkers, he ate their entire combined plasma output. All four hit, each doing 3d6+6, and I think there was a critical in there too.

    He wasn't even bloodied.

    Cue lots of movie-trailer shots of him calmly striding slow-motion through the white-hot storm, his eyes blazing anger hotter than all those megawatt plasma beams.

    \n How We Came To Live Here / Dread

    The Outside chief has sent some of the members of the village out into the wilderness to make contact with another vilalge. He-binds-us-together looks at the night sky and sees He-eats-the-sun a massive, ebony six armed creature climbing from star to star towards the full moon. The creature begins to envelop the moon plunging the world into darkness. Thinking fast, he throws a rock which draws Eats-the-sun's attention who swings down from the stars to consume this little morsel.

    Meanwhile, He-flies-with-ravens (Ghost society) and She-feeds-the-wolves (Bone society), who have been involved in a magical game of cat and mouse of curses and counter-curses, have fallen in love. They edge away from the group and decide to add to the power of the moon by combining their power in a passionate act of lovemaking. The moon waxes so brightly as the two magicians experience climax that Eats-the-sun is driven away back into the darkness. They return to the group, moonlight trickling down the inside of She-feeds-the-wolves legs, and both know that the result of this pairing will be a truly puissant progeny.

    \n Monsterhearts -

    The Chosen goes to her favorite doctor with a bullet in her leg. The doctor (mostly) patches her up, but tells her he's really going to have to report this, what with the murder last night and everything. So she (a 16-year old girl) turns him (a dude in his forties) on, by turning on the tears and showing some skin ('look at where he hurt me last night!').
    She rolls 7-9, so I say he gives himself to her. ("Ha ha," I say, "You didn't get a string on him.") They start making out.
    She pulls away before it goes too far. He apologizes, says he doesn't know what came over him.
    She then aces a roll to manipulate. "Don't report this, and you don't have to worry - I won't tell anyone this happened, not even your wife."

    \n Dungeon World. We're deep under Witch Island, battered and swamped by the vicious animated corpses of drowning victims, the avatar of the evil god has brought the darkness and it is looking bad for the party. Hard moves are falling like twelve-sided rain.

    Samu the thief has never been a church-going man, but in his hour of desperate need he calls upon our god, who is a god that appreciates suffering a little bit. He tries to strike a bargain - his life for the lives of his two faltering friends. To get our god's attention he cuts his hand and does enough damage (accidentally!) to kill himself. Last Breath move fails, he's gone. Did his prayer and sacrifice work?

    At that moment my cleric levels up, takes protection from evil, successfully casts it with the +1 from Samu's posthumous bond move, and the two survivors crawl out of that hell-hole. So I guess the god heard Samu after all!

    \n Two games of Durance on New Year's Eve.

    The first was about what happens when you ignore the native ecosystem and revolved around The Abbott, an enterprising woman named Sela Geambe whose oath changed from "I will never let my girls come to harm" to "I will never let compassion sway me" when she became Dimber Damber more or less by surprise, after her girls ... came to harm.

    The second was all about infidelity and what that does to a closed, pressure-cooker community. The Judge Advocate and his wife were both cheating on each other - him with an Emancipist servant and her with the Dimber Damber - and really no good came of it. it was headed for a bloody showdown when we were interrupted by the desiccated remains of Dick Clark and had to stop.
