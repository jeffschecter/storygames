\n Complete this sentence for me.

I played Witch for the first time on Saturday. I played a most naive and trusting Berrick who nevertheless had the guts on the shores of the causeway to stand up to Hayden and defend Elouise... unfortunately, my tactless horror at discovering Hayden's past resulted in him committing suicide by poison. Meanwhile, Armond turned out to be the ghost of a dead monk who had been haunting us for our journey, and Elhouise walked free... and was indeed innocent! I had a great time.

I know Witch is a pretty high bar to set! I guess you could answer this question in one of two ways (or both at once if you're clever). Firstly, I would like more games that are structurally similar to Witch - so diceless, GM-less, narrative-focused games, that can be played in a few hours, with no-prep. Secondly, I enjoyed the themes of Witch and am interested in games dealing with similar things.

I already know about Montsegur 1244. What else is there?

Hopefully this thread will be a useful reference for others

    \n TFVotS follows the 3 acts plus introductions and codas structure of Witch very closely, but is thematically different: the players are largely left to create their own motivations for their characters (with some hints from the Roles and Agendas), so there is no central plot linking them at the start of play. The only certainty is that The Selene will meet with disaster in the final act of the game.
