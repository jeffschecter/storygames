    \n It is a Weakness. So, it allows you to lose on your terms, and the reason that you lose will be your Hatred For Home.

    Up to you how and when you use it once it is available, and the nature of that Hatred For Home is up to you as well as how it came about. (Note that if it is your only available Weakness then you must use it if someone uses Force Weakness on you.)

    What does it do in the fiction? I can't answer that for you. It's not going to come up until someone is at Level 17 or 18. And by then, I reckon whoever has it will know what they want to do with it.

    Posted By: DInDenverIt feels like there is a big spotlight shining on this weakness. But you are saying its just like any other weakness. Why is there special rules (when you can take it, that is has to be there, etc.) for it then?
    \nWell, yeah, there is a big spotlight on it, sure. But it just works mechanically like any other weakness. The only difference is that it must be the final weakness for someone at the table. And what does that mean for your character? And how will that weakness, if used, affect the story, your character and the game? Do you die when you use it? It's on your terms how you lose, right?

    When you reach the highest levels then your story is starting to come to an end, and at that point the rules say: up to now you could pick anything for a weakness (be it Cowardice, Greed, Lack of Moral Fibre, Pride, Optimistic, Fanatical Belief, Blood Lust, whatever), but the next one available has to be about losing because of a Hatred For Home.

    It's a spike in the fiction.

    I've heard some people say they'll use it to describe something unpleasant on Terra that makes them lose on some planet out there, and as a result they'll never want to go home -- they'll just give up on Terra. Others say they'll lose because of the Hatred For Home that's brewed over the course of the game and then they'll head home to take bloody revenge. I think you'll only know when you're going to use it. And it's a bit presumptious to speculate too much before you're there. After all, it might be a different character that gets that Weakness, and when you're there you might use it in a way you'd never have guessed.

    As for why it's there? It's to bring into sharp focus the Trooper's feelings for Terra. But it's a long way off. Don't think too hard.

    \n No worries, but the book is there to help. Read the role-playing advice on p.15. That's really there to help you.

    It's perfectly fine for you to say "This is what the Weakness is." It's also perfectly fine to listen to suggestions from the group and pick one of their suggestions, or something sparked by their suggestions. It's a group effort, but it's the player's decision about what their character actually does. It doesn't mean you have to carry all the weight.

    When you see your first Strength and Weakness used in play, you'll see what they do and what sort of stuff your group is bringing in.

    Oh, and if you're GMing, you need not worry at all. The GM never gets them and just gets to help the players with theirs if they want. The GM instead gets to just blow shit up as a multitude of (soon-to-be-extinct) alien races.

    \nIn the game you're a soldier following order defending your home from galactic enemies.

    \n
    \nThen you learn that "defending your home" means committing mass genocide...for pretty much no reason what-so-ever.
    \n
    \nThen you learn that the only reason the 3:16 even exists is to get pschopathic trouble makers like you off the planet.
    \n
    \nThen you learn that the folks back home you're out here "defending"...they don't want you back.
    \n
    \n
    \nYou won't have any trouble at all figuring out what "Hatred for home" means in your fiction once you've played enough for your players to start coming to the above realizations. Its not a mechanical thing...its a thematic thing. And it ain't at all a subtle one.
