\n The first external ready playtest rules is now available.
\n
\nBlood Red Sands is a competitive RPG set in a savage world of dark sorceries and sword wielding heros. In Blood Red Sands players will take turns playing heros on a quest to defeat one of the 5 Witch Kings of Abalone, ancient sorcerers who slew the gods and have now ruled for over a millenia harvesting souls to fuel their magic. The other players will play various factions with agendas of their own, fighting or allying with the hero and other factions in order to score the most Victory Points. One by one the heroes will fall or be retired from the saga until only one remains. That hero will face the Witch King (played by the player who scored the most Victory Points) in the final ordeal -- a knock down drag out fight to the finish to determine which player wins the game and who is vanquished.

    \n Hey Lance, posting questions in this thread is fine by me (I did get your email which I'll respond to).
    \n
    \nFor those looking to playtest, 4-5 players is the sweet spot. It could scale larger but then you get the usual issues of games taking progressively longer.
    \n
    \n3 players might work, or there might be the usual 3 player problem of 2 against 1. I haven't tested that.
    \n
    \n2 players...well, you could give the dice mechanics a good work out with two players, but I think most of the narrative interest would disappear.
