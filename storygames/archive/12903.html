\n Dread House is available! In print and PDF!




Dread House is a quick and easy board game/rpg for kids and adults to play together. They play a group of teens who dare each other to spend the night in a spooky haunted house. The players have to pull from a Tower of tumbling blocks, such as Jenga, to prove their kids are brave enough to stay until dawn. If the Tower falls, the character runs out of the house and that player gets to be one of the real monsters that haunt the house.

It's now available in print and pdf through the Dig a Thousand Holes website and the un-store, and as a pdf from RPGNow/DriveThruRPG. It will be available soon via IPR.



The print version of the game includes a map of the Dread House (drawn by Tony Dowler of How to Host a Dungeon design and Parsely illustration fame), rules book, Spooky Deck, Item Deck and six Monster Cards, as well as character and monster sheets, and tokens for play. To play you also need a few tokens and a Jenga or other tumbling block tower. The PDF is included for free with purchase of the physical edition. Some assembly required for the PDF.

    Posted By: David ArtmanBecoming a monster--if it has mechanical play impact (as I'd assume it does)--is a great idea.
    \nWhy thank you.

    It does have mechanical impact. While you're the monster you get a few powers you can use on your turn. The powers are different depending on which monster you are. The ghost, for example, can possess people. The mummy can put a curse on them. The powers are designed to really ramp up the number of pulls people are making so the tower can get back to a wobbly state as soon as possible.\n
    Posted By: wundergeekThis looks nifty. What would you say the recommended age group would be?
    \nI'd say ages 8 and up. But people have played with younger, so what do I know?

    We were really careful about making rules with enough depth to hold the attention of an adult gamer. That was important to me. I wanted a game I'd want to play. But we were just as careful about making the core of the game simple and robust enough that younger players could safely ignore rules they don't quite understand or can't remember. As long as they're describing spooky stuff and pulling blocks on their turn, the game will keep chugging along.

    Posted By: Epidiah RavacholI'll have to check that out! What's the die for?

    Another set that fits the mood nicely is the Nightmare Before Christmas one.

    \nEvery turn you roll the die. It has spooky images instead of numbers.
    The haunted house: remove any block
    Witches hat: remove orange and black block
    Spider: pick another player to remove a block
    Tombstone: remove a black block
    Bat: Remove an orange block
    Pumpkin: you don't have to remove a block and play reverses direction
