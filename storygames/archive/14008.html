\n This thread topic has been bugging me for a while, and, after another failed attempt with my own game World vs. Hero, I figured I'd write about it (blog article HERE) and see what others had to say.

Can two (or more) players of a competitive storytelling game express events through the same character - a first-person narrator - without becoming stymied by disagreements about what the narrator would say and how he or she would say it?

Games will vary, and I'm aware that the 1st-person mode is much easier in cooperative game play, but I'd really like to know if it can still work to engage, surprise, and reward players of games with more competitive rules.

Thanks!

    \n While the World Ends is also competitive, in that one team of players will win in the end.

    There is a shared narrative in the 1st-person in that while each player has one personal PC, but all NPCs are played collectively. I'm not sure what counts as 1st-person, but you play PCs and NPCs in the same way, if your group usually play in 1st person WTWE will be 1st person, if you prefer playing in 3rd person WTWE will be 3rd person.
