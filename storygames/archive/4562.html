\n So, I feel kinda lame asking this, but I've been flicking arounf IPR for a while now, and I just can't make up my mind.

I'm buying a print copy of TSoY, but because I'm having it shipped to Japan, it makes sense to buy at least one other book to get the best use of the shipping cost. Now, ideally I'd be buying The Mountain Witch, but it's out of stock. Word is it's coming back in stock sometime soon, but I'm hoping to have a gaming weekend in the next few weeks, and I'd love to have a game to play for it.

So, what should I go for? What's good for a potentially large group of players (say, four to six, including me), mostly new to indie games, or gaming in general (everyone but me and Emma), and looking for some light fun? I'd prefer to stick to a more "classic" game, something that I'm going to get a lot of use out of. I can already hear people warming up to say "PTA", but I'm not sure that's right for me. I'm a bit squicked out by fanmail-type mechanics. I'm strongly looking at Dust Devils, which has been recommended highly, and Dogs, which I know is great, but is probably better for a smaller, more intimate group.

Should I just go for PDF Mountain Witch, and damn the shipping? Or is there some other perfect game for me?

    \n Hmm.

    Hmmm.

    Thanks for the advice, everyone. I get crippling buyer's remorse, so I tend to really agonise over purchasing decisions. Which is pretty dumb, I know.

    I'm not sure about Spirit of the Century. It's a bit more crunchy than I'm interested in right now. I wanted to get TSoY because it's a game I really admire, and even if I don't play it right away, it's a game I'm always going to enjoy having. Agon is an interesting call. It's not something I would have considered, but I think it could be pretty fun. Strategic isn't a problem, but crunchy probably is.

    Regarding fanmail, this is probably worth a whole other thread, but in brief I'm really uncomfortable judging my friends' contributions to the game. I'm always really conscious of which moments don't get fanmail, and I really hate the feeling of "playing it up for the crowd". I have the same issue with bonus dice in Sorcerer, and Stunting in Exalted (though they're all kinda different). There are a bunch of other things too, but I think that's the biggest reason.

    I think I really had my heart set on The Mountain Witch, which means I may end up just getting the PDF. How much printing will I need to do to play the game? I'm gonna need to make cards, right?
