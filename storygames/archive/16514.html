    \n I've played with 6 players, and I found it harder to keep focused. We had a good time, but in my opinion, getting to play with a smaller group is more enjoyable. Could you split into two groups?

    It also takes much longer to play each round with more players. Depending on how much time you have to play, it will be easier to let each person have a turn being the Lens with fewer.

    \nThe last time I played Microscope there were six of us (three veterans since playtest, one with some experience, and two total newbs), and it took something like twelve hours (spaced out over a few weeks) to give everyone a shot at being Lens. We didn't have a serious problem with people sitting idle, but I think it did dilute the core Microscope-ness a bit; there were so many ideas hitting the table that too many of them wound up not getting followed up on.

    I'd definitely recommend splitting into two groups. Four does seem to be the sweet spot, but I think three would be fine.

    \n Yep, what everyone else said is quite true: 7 players is a lot. I wouldn't recommend it. I've heard anecdotal reports of Microscope games that large that worked but I'm mystified how.

    If you do split into two tables but want to keep your games connected, you can try picking one history idea together and then compare notes after you play to see how each group took the idea and ran with it. You can even start with the same beginning and ending bookends, so your games are almost like alternate histories of each other.
