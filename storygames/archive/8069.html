\n I was browsing the "archives" of this forum, and saw a bunch of attempts to play Dogs in the Vineyard with cards.

It seemed like an interesting challenge, and I know I often want to play a game or another but don't have a pile of dice on me, so being able to play Dogs with cards seems like a good idea.

I put together a set of rules that, as far I can see, while fairly simple, exhibit all the important mechanical twists and bits Dogs throws at you. How does this look to you?

Dogs with Cards

Anyone want to give it a spin?

    \n Hmmm, looking at it from my own perspective:

    * The adjustment to Stats is twice as significant as in regular Dogs. I don't see this causing an issue with Stat reduction, but it might cause an issue with people raising Stats.

    An easy fix might be: your four Stats, added together, may never exceed 9. If your Stats already add up to 9, when you increase a Stat you must decrease another Stat.

    I'm not sure how important increasing Stats in Dogs is anyway--the other Fallout options are much more interesting. So maybe increasing Stats should be restricted to regaining lost points?

    * It might be interesting to increase the value of face cards, or distinguish between different face cards.

    For instance:

    -Face cards are worth the same number of points but retain their hierarchy: for example, you can't See a King with a Jack.

    -Face cards have different point values: Jack = 11, Queen = 12, King = 13, or Jack = 12, Queen = 14, King = 16. The advantage to this is that higher Raises can be Reversed (see below). The downside is having to remember numbers. I don't like that much.

    * One interesting thing I realized about Dogs rules while doing this is that any Raise of 11 or more (a 6 or higher if using cards) canNOT be Reversed. So, you know when you're making that you're "safe" when you're making such a Raise.
