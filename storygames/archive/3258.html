    \nI think that the pervasive use of GM comes from two factors. First it's used in GURPS, which as a "generic" game, seems to make that term generic. But, second, it goes back much farther than that. Game Master predates RPGs from wargames (heck, did HG Wells use it?), and, in fact, DM is a modification of GM as the title of the role for the first RPG. As such, all such titles are descendants of GM, or forms of GM.

    \nMy reason is simple: my first game was GURPS, so "GM" was imprinted on my gaming language first.

    I used to say that if (and now that's a when, not and if I ever wrote an RPG, I wouldn't use a term other than GM. I'm actually going back on that for at least one project I'm on, because I want to emphasize the role of the GM in the horror game I'm working on. The term I'm using is "The Evil," because the GM plays the Evil that half-possesses the players. Though, given my tendency to dip a little bit into the tongue-in-cheek, there might be a sidebar or note saying "Honestly, even though I use the title 'The Evil', I call this role the 'GM' when I'm actually running the game. This is because I want to constantly remind you in the text what sort of game this is and what sort of GM you need to be. Call it what you want, as long as you're being Evil."

    Or something like that, which is my acknowledgment of both (a) that gaming language is a big deal to us and calling the role something else only for its own sake isn't really effective and (b) but by using another term in the text, you're reminded that this isn't a "traditional GM" role -- by which I mean has loosely defined expected behaviors and actions outside of Rule Zero.

    "Honestly, though, I kind of suspect that calling everything "GM" makes it harder to acknowledge the different responsibilities different games require of their Alpha Player."

    Totally agreed... but I don't necessarily see that as a problem in this very particular instance. When I refer to "My GM" in conversation with another person, unless I'm specifically there to discuss all the glorious details of his particular role, then I'm not going to bother attempting to accurately portray him in some system-and-game-specific light - especially if I'm talking to a casual or non-gamer. I'll call him "My GM" and move on to the actual subject of whatever conversation is at hand. MY purpose is not to acknowledge his responsibilities, in exactly the same light as when I say "when I was in my car last night" I'm not intending to acknowledge my car's gas mileage, safety features, and handling performance when compared to those of other cars.

    Frankly, I suspect if I were to try and casually use "Hollyhock God" or some such weirdness in a conversation with my friends, it would on;y delay th flow of the conversation more when I had to stop and actually explain what I mean. I find that "GM" is a simple enough term to get across my points, and their individual interpretations of that term are not my concern.

    Now, should the subject of that conversation actually pertain to the actual details of that GM's role in contrast with those of another, then more specific terms will of course be applied.
