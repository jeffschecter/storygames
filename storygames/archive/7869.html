    Heya Paul,

    \n

    I get no sensation of making music, being a participant in the songs, or performing in any way shape or form. The guitar-shaped controller is nifty and lots of fun, but there's no sensation of actually playing an instrument. The soundtrack is fun, and it's neat how my screw-ups can affect the quality of the music, but there's no illusion that I'm actually making those sounds happen.

    \n

    I enjoy the challenge of pushing the right buttons at the right time. I dig the reward of hearing the song "played" well when I manage to hit all the buttons at the right time. I also enjoy the added challenge of the co-op mode where my wife and I can share the screw-ups and successes together.

    \n

    I suppose, in a nutshell, it's like this: The music is both a clue for overcoming the challenge of the game and it is a reward for that accomplishment. Without the challenge, the music is just music.

    \n I see it both ways, depending on my mood. Sometimes I just pick up the controller and go all air-guitar with it, jumping around and doing Pete Townshend windmills and rocking out like I've got ten thousand panty-flinging groupies hanging on every note. Other times I crank up the difficulty, sit down, and try to beat the HELL out of a song with singleminded precision.

    The nice thing about the game (and Rock Band, for that matter) is that it works either way: it's goofy karaoke fun in the privacy of your own home, and it's a challenging coordination puzzle game. If you're not having fun playing it one way, you can just go ahead and play it the other way and probably have a good time.
