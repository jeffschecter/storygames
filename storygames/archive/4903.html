\n Milton Keynes RPG Club are holding another of their games days on 8 March 2008.
\n
\nWHO?
\nWe're Milton Keynes RPG Club. Find out more about us at http://www.mk-rpg.org.uk.
\n
\nWHEN?
\nSaturday 8 March 2008. Doors open at 9.00pm, first games start at 10.00am. We'll finish at 11.00pm.
\n
\n(We'll be doing another on 27 September 2008 at the same times.)
\n
\nWHAT?
\nAny roleplaying or board game. You want to play it, bring it along and run it. Previously we've had on offer Amber, Call of Cthulhu, Cold City, Dogs in the Vineyard, GURPS Banestorm, Gwenthia, HeroQuest, Mythic Russia, Primetime Adventures, Savage Worlds, Tekumel, Transhuman Space, True20, Vampire, and some more that I've forgotten. Latest updates will be posted on our website.
\n
\nA full list of games on offer is on the Concrete Cow games page.
\n
\nWHO'LL BE THERE?
\nMike Mason will be our Guest of Honour at Concrete Cow 08. He's been a great friend of Concrete Cow in the past, helping out behind the scenes. He's also the Manager of Black Industries (the RPG arm of Games Workshop) and the man behind the Kult of Keepers (that group of mad people who run games of Call of Cthulhu). He's currently working on Dark Heresy, the RPG set in the Warhammer 40,000 universe. That'll be released in February 08, just in time for Concrete Cow 08! Mike will be chained to a table all day and forced to run games of Dark Heresy.
\n
\nRepresentatives of the Kult of Keepers will be in attendance so expect plenty of top quality Call of Cthulhu.
\n
\nMany members of the Collective Endeavour, a coterie of UK Indie RPG designers, will be coming along too. That means you can expect all sorts of indie RPG goodness, if that's the sort of thing that floats your boat. Last time we had games of Cold City, Covenant, Dead of Night, and Primetime Adventures, but what will be happening this time is anyone's guess. See the Concrete Cow games page for news.
\n
\nAnd, of course, there's all the fantastic games you'll bring along and run. If you let us know what you're planning to run, we'll put it on the Concrete Cow games page.
\n
\nWHERE?
\n
\nThe Old Bath House, Wolverton, in the north-west corner of Milton Keynes. The full address is
\n
\nThe Old Bath House
\n205 Stratford Road
\nWolverton
\nMilton Keynes
\nMK12 5RL
\n
\nMaps are available on MK Web, Google Maps, and Streetmap.co.uk.
\n
\nDirections for drivers are on the directions page.
\n
\nRegular trains to Milton Keynes run from London Euston station. The nearest station to the con is Wolverton (one stop north of Milton Keynes Central), about five minutes' walk away.
\n
\nHOW MUCH?
\n\xa35.00, payable on the door. This'll cover the cost of hiring the hall.
\n
\nFor more information, visit our Yahoo group or email concrete-cow-owner@yahoogroups.com

    \n An update for Concrete Cow 08! Loz Whitaker has agreed to come along as a Guest of Honour.
    \n
    \nLoz Whitaker is an old friend of Concrete Cow. He's long been a leading light in the Hawkmoon/Eternal Champion community, is a major force behind the open-source world of Gwenthia, and organiser of the fabulous Continuum conventions. He also seems to be writing about half of Mongoose's current publishing output! He's the author of the Elric game and the Elric Companion. He's written the Games Master's Handbook and Land of the Samurai for RuneQuest, the Gloranthan supplements Dragonewts and Dara Happa Stirs, the Lurker's Guide to Starports for Babylon 5, Cimmeria for Conan, and Castle Brass for Hawkmoon. He'll be chained to a table and forced to run games all day, but he's complaining of being spoilt for choice of what to do!
    \n
    \nDon't forget that Mike Mason will also be there, running games of Dark Heresy and Warhammer Fantasy Roleplay, along with representatives of the Kult of Keepers, some of the Collective Endeavour, and many more!
    \n
    \nSo, come to Sunny Milton Keynes on 8 March for all your gaming goodness!

    \n Only about a month to go until Concrete Cow on 8 March in Milton Keynes. Here's a couple of updates.
    \n
    \nDespite the news that Black Industries will be closing down soon, Mike Mason has confirmed that he'll be turning up as our Guest of Honour at Concrete Cow. He'll be bringing along some games of Dark Heresy. If you want to play in a Dark Heresy game with an employee of Black Industries, this could be your last chance.
    \n
    \nThe trader House Atreides will be along with all sorts of RPGs and stuff in an attempt to part you from your money.
    \n
    \nLoz Whitaker, our other Guest of Honour, will be running a game of Planetary, a modern day superhero game.
    \n
    \n
    Planetary - 21st Century Threads
    \n
    \n2007 and it is a quiet time for The Planetary Foundation. Excavations continue into the secret history of the world but nothing has been heard of The Four for over two years. Whilst the multiverse is never safe, for the time being reality is unthreatened.
    \n
    \nWhat's intriguing Elijah Snow and the Planetary Field Team (Jakita Wagner, The Drummer and new addition, Luther Arkwright) is the appearance in New York of four of the wealthiest individuals in the world: Gil Bates, creator of the Portals operating system; Warren Dining, Wall Street investor with an uncanny ability to predict the global economic markets; Ms Anna Hark, president of the Hark Corporation and Guardian of the Rising Sun; and Karl Albrecht, retail mogul known as 'All the World's Grocer'. Intel suggests that these four billionaires are gathering for some form of summit - but no one knows why or what the repercussions might be.
    \n
    \nPlanetary's job is to find out what these four disparate individuals need to discuss so urgently - and why have they arranged to arrive in New York in complete secrecy (so secret, that even the CIA, FBI and STORM are unaware of the conference)?
    \n
    \nPlanetary - 21st Century Threads is a scenario based on Warren Ellis's Planetary graphic novel series. Each character is possessed of superhuman capablities and, together, they are Archaeologists of the Impossible, delving into the secret history of the world in order to preserve both humanity and reality from threat. For those unfamiliar with the graphic novels, a summary can be found at http://home.earthlink.net/~rkkman/frames/. In essence, think League of Extraordinary Gentlemen meets The X-Files, Marvel and DC superhero universes, with Michael Moorcock and Neil Gaiman as the godparents. As this is a convention scenario, expect a few extra spins on the traditional Planetary themes, too.
    \n
    \nThe game system being used is Chaosium's Basic Roleplaying and the scenario is for up to 4 players.
    \n

    \n Less than two weeks to go before 8 March and the cornucopia of gaming delights that is Concrete Cow! Here are a couple of updates.
    \n
    \nFirst, confirmation of the games being offered by our Guests of Honour:
    \n
    \nLoz Whitaker, using Chaosium BR: Planetary - 21st Century Threads
    \n
    \nA scenario based on Warren Ellis's 'Planetary' graphic novel series. Each character is possessed of superhuman capabilities and, together, they are Archaeologists of the Impossible, delving into the secret history of the world in order to preserve both humanity and reality from threat. In essence, think League of Extraordinary Gentlemen meets The X-Files, Marvel and DC superhero universes, with Michael Moorcock and Neil Gaiman as the godparents. As this is a convention scenario, expect a few extra spins on the traditional Planetary themes, too.
    \n
    \nMike Mason, using Call of Cthulhu: When the Wind Blows:
    \n
    \nNo Blurb. No Blurb. No Blurb. Dare to play?
    \n
    \nMike Mason, using Dark Heresy: The House of Dust & Ashes
    \n
    \nAs Acolytes of the Holy Inquisition, you will face many tests, many dangers and you may come to question just what you are fighting for; are you truly arm of the Emperor, or simply pawns in the great game? A Rogue Trader's estate brought to auction is just the thing to draw out all a cobweb of shadows and heresy, and you shall be there with the net to catch all.
    \n
    \nLoz Whitaker, in Gwenthia: One Tree Hill
    \n
    \nA scenario set in the shared world of Gwenthia, as showcased at previous Concrete Cows. The characters are the esteemed crew of 'Not Here, Not Now' - a rag-tag assortment of Tharuxi[1] ne'er do wells who have been living on the wrong side of the Tharuxi Code for just a little too long.
    \nThe scenario uses the Mongoose RuneQuest game engine as its base, coupled with unique rules for magic. The scenario is for up to six players and no prior knowledge of Gwenthia is required.
    \n[*]Think Rastafarian steam-punk mechanics who always travel as a troupe and worship a myriad of highly potent alien ancestors, and you're on the right track...
    \n
    \nYes, Mike Mason will be at Concrete Cow as a Guest of Honour, despite the imminent closure of Black Industries. Come along if you want to grab one of the last few chances to play with official Black Industries staff, or just commiserate with him and buy him a pint.
    \n
    \nThere are loads of other great games on offer. If you're thinking of offering a game, let me know and I'll put it on the website.
    \n
    \nLook forward to seeing you all in sunny Milton Keynes on the 8th!
    \n
