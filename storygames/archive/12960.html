    \n Well, my off-the-cuff response would be to ask if you think all your players have enough 'buy in' and/or are involved enough in all that stuff you can make fronts out of. I mean, if everybody is on board with the setting as established (even the new guy) and you have juicy conflicts that they'll be happy to get tangled up in, then I'd say go nuts and jump in. On the other hand if you just plain want to take it slow and crock-pot some stuff, that might be a nice change of pace.
