\n Hi all,

I know some of you may be interested in Glorantha and in particular games in the Hero Wars family.

I am a co-author of The Coming Storm for Chaosium which is out in PDF now and print later for Heroquest: Glorantha.

This is the first of two volumes in the Red Cow series. This first book is a complete setting guide to the Red Cow clan, their neighbours, and their tribe, as well as nearby enemies the Telmori and the ghouls of The Woods of the Dead.

In this book we present enough material for you to use this as a sandbox setting. The heart of the material is over 65 NPCs (all illustrated) and their relationships. There is struggle here against not only against external threats, but internally for the future of the clan.

This is in essence a sandbox setting.

The second volume contains a year-by-year campaign from 1618-1625. Think of the Great Pendragon Campaign for format (each year is presented with events, the reactions of NPCs to those events, and a couple of episodes based around those events). If you are strictly sandbox the material here may be useful as a 'ticking clock' to prevent your sandbox turning into quicksand, but there is also a playable campaign for those who prefer that approach.

Feel free to ask me any questions about the product. I'll try to check in regularly.

Ian

    \n Yes, Moon Design Publications stepped in to save Chaosium after problems caused by the CoC 7e Kickstarter. In essence the 80s IP of Chaosium is back in one place, by a circuitous route. So yes, they are using the Chaosium label for this work.

    Jeff was a co-author and contributed to the material. So it is canon. In fact canon was subtly adjusted when the Guide to Glorantha was written to ensure that ideas we had for this book were part of the established canon.
