\n On Clyde Rhoer's thread....\n
Posted By: Joshua BishopRobySpecifically, Clyde, situation is how all the stuff in the game is interrelatedin such a waythat the ensuing gameplay will be interesting. I mean, you can make a situation that's a town full of happy people without a care in the world, but if there's nothing for the characters to do, that's just a crappy situation. A 'better' situation is a town full of unhappy, desperate people who want or need the characters to do things, preferably to other people in the town. Or in non-Dogs examples, a pile of gold in a perfectly safe cave just outside of the city walls is a boring situation; put a dragon on top of the pile of gold and orcs guarding the tunnels down to it, and maybe some traps and gelatinous cubes, and now you have an engaging situation.
\nBoldface mine. \n
Posted By: John HarperJBR is right. But to his dragon/gold example I would add:and a party of adventurers that need a huge pile of money, now.You kinda need that bit or something like it.
\nItalics his.

These two bits got together and broke my brain. Dungeon adventure as caper film! Think about it: you've got guys each with their own function or "character class" with niche protection, the occasional fight here and there, the big score at the end. Planning, information gathering.

Hmmm...
