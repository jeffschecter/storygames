\n Yes indeed, it's yet another 3:16 thread. It's mostly going to be an actual play thread, despite the title, but I wanted to share a disturbing observation first.

I played in what was probably the last 3:16 game that John ran at GenCon (though it's quite possible he ran more on Sunday..) and before I got up from the table at sometime after midnight, I was feeling pretty nauseous. This turns into a full on stomach bug which had me throwing up by Sunday night and missing work on Monday, plus still feeling puny all day Tuesday. This by itself isn't enough.

I ran it again last Monday. I'll talk more about that in a moment. By the time I was running one of the players over to his barracks, and then all week until finally Friday morning I've been feeling like refried ass. Stomach ache, general body aches, fatigue, nausea.. The works, plus a couple new ones.

I'm planning on running it again this Monday. I'll know by Tuesday if my hypothesis is correct.


So, on to the actual play. The game was thrown together last minute, because we're ending a D&D module. We were planning to go on to a second, but with various delays (to include the aforementioned missed day of work; no gaming either that day) and others coming up, it seemed unwise to commit to a module I may not finish before my trip to the sandbox. So I woo'd them with the promise of a shoot-em-up game that could go from character creation to completed mission in 2-3 hours. When they took me up on it, I realized I hadn't actually prepared for them to say yes, so I printed character sheets (more on that at the end of the post), dug out some Pente beads to use for Threat Tokens, and drew three boxes on my battle mat and labeled 'em Close Near and Far, and settled in to play.

First, there was a lot of table talk... Waiting for me to get everything ready got everyone distracted, so I got their attention by standing up and shouting "Alright maggots, listen up!"

We went through the character creation mostly from memory, and came up with the following 3 Troopers.

SGT Dallas, with a reputation for being Loose Lipped.
FA/NFA: 5/5
Slug Rifle as his primary weapon

CPL Sunny, with a reputation for being Not-So-Sunny.
FA/NFA: 6/4
E-Cannon as his primary weapon (duh.)

TPR Chaz, with a reputation for being Blood Thirsty.
FA/NFA: 6/4
Slug Rifle as his primary weapon

I barely sketched in anything about the color and setting aside from reading the basic setting background, and reading them off their kits and their Orders. I didn't tell them before-hand that their rank was based off of FA and NFA, so they didn't make their choices based on rank.

The planet I rolled up was planet Goya, a rock so rad-hot it glowed on the night side. Their platoon was tasked with moving to and securing a location that the limited sensor scans suggested had particularly pure radioactives, so that a team of egg-heads could come down and test it to see if it was high enough quality for munitions.
The aliens they would meet up with would have AA 5 (highest FA+lowest NFA)/2, 12 Threat Tokens, and were of the type Furred Creatures with the Special of Rapid Movement.

LT Murphy was your typical 3:16 lieutenant; No confidence in his platoon, with a particular low-grade animosity toward the PCs Squad (Bandaleros). Because I wasn't really feeling the mood, his response to interruptions was a sad attempt at a cold stare. CPL Sunny's stare was warmer, in that "I'd like to see your head in a microwave" variety of warm. For next session, I think I'm going to have him be moved to another platoon, and replace him with an idealistic young LT who got a massive field commission with very little experience, because in my experience, that sort are much more annoying. The briefing was sketchy, due to my poor plann- I mean, radioactivity on the planet interfering with detailed scans.

The drop-ship pilot was a direct rip-off from John's game, except less sass, and more ice-queen. SGT Dallas tried to hit on her. CPL Sunny continued to show his animosity toward officers by glaring at her until it became clear that she had no problems taking off with him standing there, not strapped in. So she drops out of the bay and hits atmo so hard that several of the NPC Troopers, and TPR Chaz all lose their lunch, then kicks them out the back without stopping, letting them hit and roll as training dictated. The NPC Squad took off immediately while SGT Dallas took stock of his men, and got a fix on their objective.

The first encounter was as they were en route. On the dominance roll, all three Troopers succeeded, and I failed, hence they ambushed the aliens. I described them as massive, shaggy hulks that ran on all fours. I was running low on creativity. They blew the hell out of my 6 Token encounter in short order. I think only one person took a hit.

Their next encounter came on the objective, where they found the other squad being surrounded by what turned out to be only 4 aliens. As they assaulted with grenades (aliens won Dominance, so set the range at close) they managed to blow themselves up pretty well, and killed off the remnants of the other squad in the process, along with 4 total aliens. No casualties, but I believe CPL Sunny may have used his armor here.

    They secured the objective, and finally called in. The eggheads were sent down, and the squad waited. And waited. And waited... Some 40 minutes passed, and they called back in. The eggheads had broken atmo 30 minutes previously, and should have already been on-site. The ship did a scan, and managed to detect a faint SOS, and sent the squad to respond. On the way there, I described them seeing a firefight on the horizon (just color, to make it clear that they weren't the only troops on the planet) and when they arrived at the location, they found several dead aliens, and a single dead trooper. They debated carrying him with them, but decided to just mark a waypoint, and continue on. Chaz pried into the corpse's armor to lift his dog tags.

    They arrive at the crash-site to find it being attacked by several aliens, just as it's defensive shields fail. Chaz and SGT Dallas make for the ship to use as cover and to check on the eggheads (NFA checks, I believe). CPL Sunny lays down suppressive fire, though he ends up not hitting a thing as the aliens scatter, allowing the SGT and TPR to make the ship... Then they swarm back in for a round, then move to far range, and flee. Again, grenades along with the aliens nearly manage to kill CPL Sunny. If he didn't use his armor in encounter 2, he did here.

    Once Sunny is patched up, they take stock of the ship. They got there in time to save the eggheads and the pilot, though a panicky egghead nearly shoots Chaz as he's dragging the badly wounded CPL back in. SGT Dallas smashes him down, knocks him out cold, then radios in again. They're expecting, with the damage to the ship and all the specialized equipment, that the mission is gonna be scrubbed, and they're getting out.

    No such luck. They're to escort the eggheads and the pilot back to the original objective so they can take samples. On the way back, they grab the corpse they'd seen before. They get to the objective, and the eggheads start their thing. The pilot (another LT) tries to start giving orders, but he's backed down by the SGT. Then the aliens attack. They keep at Far range this time, using their previously established speed to make darting attacks from all directions, while keeping the majority of their number at range. SGT Dallas tries to call E-Vac and fails while Chaz goes to town with his slug rifle. In the end, Chaz cancels once to keep CPL Sunny from dying, then uses his Strength Flashback to save them all, wiping out the remainder of the aliens in a berzerk charge with his bare hands.

    SGT Dallas gives a "full" debrief, spinning every event to make his squad look better, and Chaz definitely comes out looking like the hero (which, by kills and final Threat Token removal, he was)

    After the debrief and detox, the Troopers get back to their quarters. Chaz strips immediately to shower (SGTs orders: Troops before leaders), but before he can get there, there comes a tinny fanfare, and "Troopers, Post!" from a speaker in the wall, under which there is a small slot.

    "Attention to Orders. The following personnel are awarded the Campaign Badge, First Award, for actions on the Planet Goya..." SGT Dallas dutifully takes his medals. CPL Sunny ignores his so they're dropped onto the floor. Chaz grabs his up, except for the Crimson Sword, which SGT Dallas stabs into his naked chest. Chaz takes a shower with the sword still sticking out of his pec, and we fade to black.

    Chaz levels due to kills (28), and raises FA to a 7.
    SGT Dallas wins the roll, and raises FA to 6.
    Chaz declines to buck for rank, and all three attempt to requisition, and fail.

    Oddly, all three choose to advance their weapons at Near range. I would have expected them to go either Close or Far, as they had very difficult times of getting into or staying at Near range for most encounters.

    \n Comments:

    Neil, who played CPL Sunny, had the worst dice-luck ever. He got one decent kill (17 kills) in the first encounter, and another single kill (1) with grenades in the 3rd encounter. He consistently rolled high, failing more encounters than he succeeded in, and lost both the roll-off for level advancement and for requisitioning. Twice, he had to be bailed out by his comrades to keep from dying. (once with a cancel, once with a Flashback)

    Still, I believe even with my lackluster GMing, that everyone enjoyed the game. Everyone expressed interest in playing again, and for people mostly familiar with D&D, they were definitely impressed that I was able to deliver (if only JUST) on my promise that we could go from character creation to completed mission, with no pre-generated mission, in 2-3 hours.

    Some concerns:

    When looking at weapons for requisition, I was reminded of something that puzzled me in John's game. The Powerblade. It's so cool thematically.. But from a tactical standpoint, there is NO reason to ever take this weapon, unless something's wrong with the book as printed. It does more damage than the other Close-range weapon, the power-claw, but the power-claw can be switched automatically. So there's a good reason to pick the Powerblade over the Power Claw. But the Powerblade does the exact same damage at close range as the Shotgun, but the Shotgun can also be fired at Near. So anyone smart would pick the shotgun over the Powerblade. If the Powerblade is able to be switched to automatically, like the other hand-to-hand weapons, then there's no reason anyone would pick the power claw. It just seems a pity, because I WANT to pick the Powerblade, but I'd be better served going with either a Powerclaw or a Shotgun.

    The Character sheet. This is probably my own obtuseness, but I couldn't get the downloaded character sheet to print properly from the site. I wanted the nice two-per-sheet that John had at GenCon, but when I tried that, it printed it extra-small, and still only one-per-sheet. I know there's a way to do it, but I couldn't figure it out. So I made due, and recently made my own character sheet .pdf. It's a letter-sized document, with 2 of the sheet from the book on it, with one small modification: I added a box for combat drugs, so that we won't have to keep drawing it in. I'll be using it for my own group, but if Gregor doesn't mind, I'll toss up a link to it so others can use it too.

    And that's that. I know it's lengthy, but I can't ever seem to get a decently short actual-play post without leaving out gobs of cool/useful/informative stuff.

The TPRs are hard-pressed to keep at range, and once, SGT Dallas has to cancel to avoid TPR Chess being blown to smithereens by her comrades grenades. His cancel maneuver involves throwing his grenade to knock TPR Chaz's grenade wide, so that no one is hurt. First round that all three succeed, the cat people shake it off, their armor badly damaged, and keep coming. Finally they take them down.

    LT Young joins them once the plateau is secure, and they once again repeat their maneuver to get the rest of the way down the mountain. Once more, Chess misthrows, and is rescued by SGT Dallas. They come to a chasm leading down into the acid sea that they must cross, and so SGT Dallas ties a rope around his waist, and attempts the 30ft jump, hoping the lower gravity allows him to coast across. He fails, and activates his combat drugs to give him an extra boost...

    And dies as the drugs surge through his system, stopping his heart instantly.

    30 minutes later, after they've hauled him back up, attempted to resuscitate him, and had a death certificate number called down from the Aspiron, he suddenly starts spasming and twitching, cursing violently and randomly. He used a Weakness flashback (my reading of the rules seemed to allow this) of when he was a teen, and recreated crystal meth from a recipe he found on the internet, and overdosed. He'll probably be dealing with the consequences of the death certificate for the next couple of sessions.

    Once SGT Dallas had recovered, they continued on to where the rest of the platoon was being attacked on the LZ by an elite band of felinoids in real MandelBrite armor, using TEF issued weapons. After SGT Dallas' squad joined the fight, they managed to wipe them out, and then inspected their armor and weapons. LT Young called it up, and was not pleased by the response. The main body landed, and the assault of the city was on. The Youngbloods were put on point, and lead the way into the city.

    Shortly before reaching the city, they found a felinoid war shrine, decorated with felinoid and human skulls. The human skulls were not even close to recent. SGT Ortiz was duly impressed, and they moved on, slightly disturbed. Once they reached the city, the desperate defenders attacked, and even with their armor, were quickly destroyed.


    Comments:
