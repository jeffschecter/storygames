\n So I started a Sorcerer game and read up on some of the online notes and Ron Edwards's annotations on Sorcerer and the bit about playing demons like cartoon characters really clicked with me and helped me get demons in a way I didn't before. So we're playing a modern day, one-on-one game with classical demons from medieval woodcuttings, and Carly has decided that her demon, Vetis, has a Desire for acquisition. And I am pumped about playing this guy. At the moment he just seems strange and wacky and a bit annoying, like he has a cell phone and is fascinated with downloading apps, and he went grocery shopping and now her fridge and freezer is totally STUFFED with raw meat, and they visited a friend of hers and that scene hasn't resolved yet but he's totally going to try to pocket some of her shit. But I totally see him eventually going somewhere sinister and terrible, like he'll buy the apartment building she lives and evict the inhabitants and then start buying people and chaining them up in the basement. He might start a business and hire her friends, but I don't know what kind of business it would be yet.

Well, I guess the obvious business would be acquisitions.

    \n Demons as cartoons is really important.

    I'm GMing the game Hans is talking about. It took me 2 or 3 sessions to finally find his demon (a passer named Kennedy)'s voice. When I finally did the idea I had seemed super stupid. Kennedy is Hans's PC's connection to the art world. She's basically his agent. I finally stumbled on the idea that she wants to make a reality show about struggling artists and plans to rig things so that Hans's character (Kelly) comes out on top. My first thought was: no, that's dumb. Then I remembered the cartoon thing and ran with it.

    All of a sudden Kennedy is all over Kelly's life. She's pushing him to make "dramatic" decisions for the sake of the "narrative" she's building around him for the audience. At one point Kennedy moved into Kelly's basement and there was this scene where Kelly went down to visit her and discovered that she had this huge bank of monitors set up down there and that she'd installed cameras all over his life and was just obsessively editing footage.

    Yeah, it was great.

    \n Yeah, I wish I knew why the "demons are cartoons" thing was such a huge key to unlocking Sorcerer play for me so I could articulate it. But it makes them way more fun, and it also occasionally startles me. Like where we're at in the game now, Vetis is running around the sorcerer's friend's apartment stealing things and he totally dominated her on a Will roll to get him to stop (complete victory) so obviously he just keeps going and stealing EVERYTHING he can. Books, spoons, an apple. And I totally didn't see the scene going this way where the sorcerer is trying to comfort her grieving friend and her demon is running around ransacking the place. He's not doing it to piss her off, he's doing it because that's what he is.

    I guess they key was in realizing that they don't have to be malicious or subversive in order to cause problems. They just have to have recognizable motivations that they take way, way too far.
