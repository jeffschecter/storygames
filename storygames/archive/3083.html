    \n I agree that aces high is most intuitive for people generally. However, I can see reasons for non-intuitive results.

    I've been pondering a game based on Wen Spencer's "A Brother's Price", which is set in a world where only 5% of live births are men. So there are very large families with many sisters married to a single husband. Menfolk are kept well-protected as valuable reproductive resources, while the large families are generally fine with losing a sister or two. I thought that the card mechanics should be somewhat non-intuitive -- the sequence at the top is Jack, King, Queen, for example. And aces are low, reflecting the society which doesn't value loners.
