    \n In a nutshell: A GM-less game where the characters are survivors of a nuclear apocoplypse. Characters try to define their lives and the lives of the other players while trying to survive in a hopeless environment. My poject is this: what would an Existentiallist RPG play like? I had originally modled it after JP Sartre's No Exit and The Stranger, which I find to be very accessable from a gaming point of view.

    What is Survivors about? Defining yourself in the context of others. Existential philosophy.
    What do the characters do? Try to survive in a nuclear wasteland while attempting to realize themselves. In order to be "myself," others have to play along-- I cannot lead if nobody follows. So, I must coerce by various means their cooperation in my identity.
    What do the players do? Define the scenes and situations, overcome survival challenges, and inflict traits on other players.

    Why GM-less? One of the core tennets of Existentiallist thought is that there is no meaning to anything except what we give it. Having a GM means there will invariably be plot, structure and all the things that come with that. By removing the GM, I hope to make an uncaring and random world, one without meaning, and no plot other than what the players put into it.

    Why a nuclear wasteland? All of those things that go with the world, the "I am THIS" has just gotten blown to hell. The only thing left in a hostile world is the interaction between these characters and the identities they give to themselves.

    What is the system like? The system itself is undergoing a bit of revision, but the core of it is The Tree and the Trait Track.
    The Tree is filled out at the beginning of the game, and it has three parts. The Trunk is a one word or phrase definition of who you want to be: Mother, Tyrant, Leader, Rebel, whatever. The Roots are four traits that you must gain to become The Trunk (eg, a Mother could choose Nurturing, Protective, Caring and Wise.) There is one Branch for each other player, and each has 4 traits that they need to gain for you to become your Trunk. So, our Mother example may need a Daughter, a Husband, and a Threat-- each with four traits.
    The Trait Track is a list of the traits that the character has accumliated, which defines their Angst. Angst makes getting new traits more difficult, and makes survival chacks harder.

    It's been on the back burner for about six months now, so I've got a bit of revision before it's in playtest shape, but I should be able to get a preliminary document up and running before too long.
