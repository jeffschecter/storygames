\n So I'm sitting here at Julia's and we're about to go get breakfast. Julia's ashcans have been sitting outside, on a table: she'd just put acrylic on them.

(They are bloody gorgeous, by the way. Each has an individual cover and then they're hand-covered with...I don't know...nice stuff...and then they've got acrylic on them...and they're hand-bound with red wool that looks like netting. Both Julia and her husband have been working on them.)

There was a huge and sudden shower of rain, which makes Julia scream: "Oh no!". We rush outside to rescue the ashcans.

When we get outside, the layer of acrylic is successfully repelling the rainstorm. The drips of water are running straight off the ashcans.

So, Tales of the Fisherman's Wife is waterproof. This strikes me as nicely appropriate. Mind you, it'd also have been cool if they'd looked as though they'd been underwater.

Graham
