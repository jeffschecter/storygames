\n So back in January, when Shreyas Sampat's (late, lamented) MANual of Style was still updating, he threw out what must have seemed like an innocent piece of advice for men with my build:\n
"If you have small shoulders, then do all that stuff I warned top-heavy guys not to do: wear structured tops with details around the shoulders. You could find one of those gradient-dyed tees that\u2019s more saturated at the top and more muted at the bottom. Avoid things that draw attention to the center. Or you can do pushups. Shoulders are one of the easiest, fastest parts of the body to build muscle on, and it hardly takes any time at all."
\nThat sentence (emphasis mine) reminded me of something linked the previous summer by another game designer I admire, Mr. Daniel Solis: the 100 Pushups workout plan. That looked simple enough, right? I thought I could probably give it a try. And Shreyas said it would hardly take any time at all!

It actually took twice as long as the workout plan prescribes, but yesterday I hit my goal of 100 pushups (in a row!). I twittered about it with alacrity and enthusiasm, including this exchange:\n
Me: "@danielsolis Hey, was it you that linked to the 100 Pushups site years ago on your LJ? I just hit my goal, and if so I owe you a drink!"
\n
Daniel: "@BrendanAdkins I'll need one at GenCon! "
\n
Me: "@danielsolis Tempting. But drinks from me turn into full meals at GPNW..."
\n
Daniel: "@BrendanAdkins Alas, I can only swing two trips this summer: GenCon and my honeymoon. Maybe next year, when you do 200 pushups. "
\n
Me: "@danielsolis 'Daniel Solis will come to GPNW 2011 if I do 200 pushups.' That's a binding contract now, sir."
\nSo I've got a new goal, which is good. But I'm just the tiniest bit concerned. What if his side of the contract isn't quite as I interpreted it? What if he's only pledging to attend the con of choice of the first nerd who can do 200 pushups to summon him? Oh crap! Those Nerdly punks are going to get him first, aren't they! Damn it! I bet at Gen Con all they do is pushups!

All right, gamers, you're on. Who wants to race me to 200?

    \n Yeaaahhhh, push-ups aren't gonna be very good for bulking up (as another "Army buddy" here). They'll make your triceps and chest pretty well defined, but they're not gonna do much for your shoulders, and they're not gonna make any muscles very large. Weights are what you want to go for on that (as mentioned above 8-12 reps with the last 2 being really hard is a good rubric, and it can be good to go up in weight and down in repetitions between sets). Push-ups are mostly good for endurance.

    If you *do* want to get more out of pushups (they're nice and portable) you can do a few things with them:
    * Move your hands around - close in will work out inner chest more, far apart will work out your triceps and shoulders more
    * Elevate your feet - remember when Starbuck is doing pushups with her feet on the bed in Battlestar Galactica? That puts more weight on your arms and makes push ups way harder. If you're feeling really crazy, brace your feet on a wall and do hand-stand push ups!
    * Those "perfect push up" gizmos on cable TV are actually pretty good. If you don't have or want those, though, holding on to some sort of elevated handle (like a pair of dumb bells on the ground) lets you lower your chest more, which makes the push ups harder
    * Those crazy push ups where you clap after pushing up. That gives your muscles "explosive power" and are wicked hard

    Sorry, I spend way too much time doing push ups, and not enough time gaming

Also, I have no idea how such advice needs to be changed for ladies! But yeah, the rowing machine ought to be working the back of your shoulders/your back.

    If anybody wants to follow the original piece of advice referenced in this thread (broadening your shoulders) you'll want to do things like lift weights over your head (like "raise the roof" - that's the military press) or lift up a bar from your waist to your chest, keeping your elbows high, or lift dumbells from your waist to eye level keeping your elbow straight (both in front of you and to the side).

    Oh, and it's a good idea to be aware of what muscles you're working out and to do it in 'groups'. Like, instead of working out everything every time you go to the gym, have a "chest and tricep day", a "back and bicep day" and a "legs and shoulders day". But you might change that if you can only make it to the gym a couple times a week or something. The key point is balance. Even if you have a specific goal like 'tone my arms' or whatever, it's good to work everything out.

    \n Pushups can be fun, but you're not going to gain a lot of bulk from them. You will probably gain some muscle, though. The harder you can make them, the better for that. And just doing them slowly and with good form--perfect form--goes a LONG way to make it harder. Doing a 100 sloppy, really fast pushups is probably like doing 20 slow "perfect" pushups.

    When I can't get to a gym, I like to do a few things:

    1. Elevate the feet, like above.
    2. Lift one leg off the ground, and as high as you can (aiming for perpendicular to the ground, although you probably won't get that far). Do a set of pushups, then switch to the other leg.
    3. Get a good backpack, and fill it with water bottles, sand/dirt, heavy books, whatever.

    You can get a bit of a more serious workout that way.

    But putting on bulk has as much to do with what you eat as with what you exercise, so that's a whole different story.

    One last comment:

    Exercise is no different for girls/women than it is for guys. We don't have "different needs", or different effects from different types of exercise.

    There isn't really a difference between "toning" and "building", for example. That's a marketing thing, or some kind of misunderstanding.

    One thing to watch out for: your wrists! You can hurt or damage them. If it hurts, having some kind of handles to grip (or doing pushups on your knuckles) is a good alternative.
