    \n Robin,

    When you play AD&D, it's likely that you are using information that, over the years, you have built structures in your brain that serve as super-optimized searches and calculators specifically relevant to playing AD&D. I suppose my original intent was more towards finding the well-designed game that least requires such retrieval structures in the first place. But if you feel a game is uniquely geared towards creating such structures -- so much so that an introspective player might never realize they were lacking -- then maybe that should count as well.

    I find it interesting that there were many suggestions and, except for one instance, each was unique. Does this only reflect different gaming experiences, or does it reflect some kind of floor? (Or maybe just the way people answer open-ended questions in this kind of forum.)

    \n Very light and clever games I've really liked:

    * Lacuna. Love the "succeed whenever you want but you're running your own timer down" element. Great for players, maybe tough for an inexperienced GM. Requires a very firm hand on the pacing to make it work right.

    * Dust Devils. The fact that the point of the game is to reach your own character's final scene is liberating in lots of interesting ways.

    * Zombie Cinema. Only got to play this once, just a couple days ago, but I really enjoyed the social dynamic of competing to escape at the expense of other characters, the fact that players can decide to be either supportive audience members or get involved in scenes, and the super-quick character-making bit. Sweet little package, pure "story game."

    * InSpectres. The whole confessional concept is a killer app. I'd love to see more games that mess with the player role that way. Considered a fantasy-type hack, where the confessional becomes dreams/visions/omens experienced by the various characters. I love how hackable the core concept is.

    Those games have their hooks deepest into me at the clever/light level.

    No idea what you're getting at with your second question. Is BW the gruff cop who's too old for this shit? Or the angry all-business guy? Or The Chosen One?
