    \nYeah it was interesting: every time I do this talk (over the years) I check to see how much exposure the crowd has already had with GMless games. More recently lots of people had played once or twice but wanted to know more. But this group almost no one had played one or really knew what they were about. I try to adjust my talk to fit.

    \n A great talk, Ben. I love the enthusiasm - it's contagious!

    One thing I always think about in this context:

    People are often mystified by the concept, and imagine some wild, raw, unbounded cloud of creative energy. It's daunting!

    If I'm talking to gamers, I always say:

    "You know how, when you roleplay, in some games it's all talking and making stuff up and you rarely roll the dice... but in other games, it's consulting rules and rolling dice and selecting game moves? It's just the same in GMless games. There are different kinds. Some are pretty loose; others are strict about when you talk and what you can say."
