I will facilitate an event for teenagers and I'm looking for suitable freeform/semi-larp game recommendations. There will be
- 2 adult facilitators

- 3x 3hour time-slots throughout a week.

    \n That's an interesting situation you've got there - a fruitful frame, you should be able to fit a nice little mini-campaign of something wonderful in there. I would write my own game for this thing, were I in your stead. Would you like to brainstorm/crowdsource something, or would you rather hunt down the perfect game for this? Larp-ish things aren't difficult to design as long as you stick to the conventional, but there's some work involved in writing up the characters. The advantage would be getting to tailor your subject matter and style and such for the situation.

    Also, a relevant general question: when you say larp, do you mean something relatively stately, with players talking to each other and maybe playing rock-paper-scissors, or would athletic elements fit in as well? Boffer swordfighting is a larp favourite in particular around here, but I understand that there's plenty of local cultures where it'd be very of out of place.

    Another pin-down question: do you have an idea of what sort of creative emphasis the players would enjoy? Competitive play, atmospheric thespian thing, open-ended creativity?

    And one last pin-down: what's the venue like? If the game was something more larp-ish, would there be e.g. multiple rooms to conduct the game in?

    The reason I'm asking about the details is that I'm hoping to jog somebody into realizing that they've got the perfect game for you. I might have something, for instance, but then again they might be shitty recommendations after all. I'll think on it.

    \n A trimmed-up Werewolf game would make for a good, simple larp, yeah. A bit of work, though - I'm not aware of a ready-made version. It would basically revolve around individualized character motivations and rules for secret murder and investigating such. I would personally enjoy "blowing up" the boardgame's premise a lot - like, nobody knows how many werewolves there even are, or if the whole thing's just a witch hunt cooked up for somebody's benefit.

    Another semi-simple idea would be to do a Diplomacy larp. Roughly similar amount of work overall in prep, I'd say.

    More usefully, I believe that Shreyas Sampat's Mist-Robed Gate could make a good chassis for this. I am not entirely happy with its accessibility (there is a bit of a learning curve with the rules), but provided that he facilitators get the rules and the players get the genre, it would fit the time-frame, number of players and other constraints well. (The milieu could be turned e.g. European easily enough, too, if that part doesn't work.)

    Another game that similarly hits the design constraints (time-frame, larp-y, large number of players, short-campaign potential) yet causes similar challenges in accessibility is my own Fellows of the Julenius Archive. I wouldn't mention it here weren't ready-made designs for this space somewhat sparse, as it has a double fault in being set in and being about Finland, and also being more suited to experienced roleplayers than newcomers. Still, it would work fine if one put in the work to adapt it to the local milieu and did a good job in facilitating it.

    Both of those are available for free at their respective websites, I think, so perhaps you'll take a look and tell us what you think? Not necessarily because those solve your problem (I would like a game for teens to be more immediately intuitive), but because we might get some clues as to what direction to think about. I've got the feeling that a surprisingly well-fitting game is probably out there, I just can't think of it right this moment.

    In general, though, there should be more games in this design space; I like it, it deserves more attention. I imagine that many people have stuff for it in their private files, but at least in my own case it's too Finnish (as in, I can't be bothered to translate my old con scenarios), too adult (negative horror stuff, cerebral wargamey things) or too sporty (boffer games and such). Surely somebody's made a simple and immediately grabbing game for this purpose...

    \n 12 players, 3 hours, simple? If you want to try a normal theatre-style larp, then "Down and out in Middenburg" might work. It's about fantasy beggars, has short character sheets, and nothing more complicated than paper-scissors-rock. Alternatively, you could look at the stuff by Freeform Games: they have easily-graspable themes, simple characters, no disturbing content, and really good advice for GM's.

    If you're after something mechanically simpler but more complex thematicly, you could try The Tribunal. Though that might not end up especially "life-positive" (or it might; only one way to find out).
