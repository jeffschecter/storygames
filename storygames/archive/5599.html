    \n So is the intent here to be a fun, in-play way of setting up situations for the Dogs to show up in?

    ...'cause that sounds awesome.

    I'm imagining this thing where you just set up a town with no real problems, but with people and relationships. Then the GM in some way introduces an issue.. a question of faith, and how people react to that question of faith eventually leads to an escalation of sin. At some point, you freeze-frame the town, and write it up as a town for DitV.. then you play it to it's conclusion without the Dogs ever showing up, and put that into the part of "what happens if the Dogs never came?"

    Then you put this up for some group to play Dogs in... maybe your group, maybe someone else's. Then you take the aftermath of the Dogs showing up, and if there's a town left, you play "The Vineyard" again, playing on the decisions made by the Dogs to create another situation, etc.

    \n I just had another idea concerning this hack: use Polaris

    frame scenes where townspeople are tempted to break the rules of the community (just like Polaris has scenes where knights are tempted to break the rules of their order)

    at the end of the scene, roll experience

    if the character fails, instead of having them "advance", have the town "advance" on the hierarchy of sins

    everything's fine --> someone breaks the rules --> injustice --> sin --> etc.

    once the town has advanced to a certain level, you can frame scenes where someone is tempted to move it one step further up the hierarchy

    so if you've narrated injustice, now you can frame scenes where characters are tempted to sin, and so on

    maybe include some kind of randomizer too, to determine who's the victim of the injustice/sin/etc.

    aren't I brilliant?

    The issue here is that Town Creation is a really powerful tool. I think you can modify it in some interesting ways here.

    \n
      \n
    1. The Obvious. Make it so that Town Creation generates relationships between townsfolk. Then play those relationships.
      2. \n
    2. The Less Obvious. Make the Dogs represent a hierarchy of sin, themselves. You folks are going to work things out! Maybe there will be blood, maybe there will be grudging acceptance of someone else's child, whatever. But the Dogs come in, with their Pride ( ->...) and their own issues with each other and they've got pistols to prove their point. So use Town Creation to make a pack of Dogs who have stolen, gotten each other pregnant, insulted each other, etc. But always for reasons you could understand.
      3. \n
    \n

    Whatever happens, make sure your Relationship dice are a big part.
