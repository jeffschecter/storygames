    \nStars Without Number

    Matt is creating a great game. There are all these converging vectors of pressure on our characters, and the tension of "what do you do?" is way more palpable than they have been in any other game I've been in, except for maybe Thor O's BW game. What do we act on? Loyalty? Morality? Greed? Fear?

    We're bounty hunters who got caught up in a situation where our bounty was wanted by this corporation called Microzenith. And whatever, except Microzenith wanted her dead (she had some company secrets or something, we're still not sure). She'd holed up with some low-time gang in the undercity, and we had to figure out which way we go. The bounty was given to us by Barry, who we have a working relationship with and who hasn't done any wrong by us. So do we follow through? Tangling with the gang would be nasty. We're not incompetent but we're also just three bounty hunters with guns and there was at least a dozen of them. Or do we throw in with Microzenith? In this situation, they are the ones who have the monopoly on both money and power. However, there's a difference between turning someone in for a bounty and even killing them in the process, and turning someone over to a corporation that you absolutely KNOW is going to murder them once you turn them over.

    It was a rough situation, and we were learning towards blowing off Barry to help this woman get off the planet, but then Microzenith weighed in and leaned on us. It's one thing to know that we're working in opposition to them, and another thing to know that THEY know. So we backed off with our hands in the air. After the gang leader got killed in a gunfight with Microzenith security shortly after our meeting with them, there was little to keep the bounty hunters around. They left, and felt shitty about it.

    And I want to get at how the desperation and powerlessness of our characters made me feel and think more deeply about the decisions I've made in any game since Thor O's Burning Wheel game went on hiatus, but man it was awesome. Having our characters be like, "Man... I want to help this girl, but I don't want to die," made me connect to them. They really were, at 1st level, just normal people with guns.

    I'm unbelievably excited to advance to higher levels and see how that changes things.

    First session. We (a skeleton crew of Monday Ladies Game Night) got through setting and char gen and a couple of scenes. Making character for this game is immensely fun. The players rolled dice for every detail. The GM (me) rolled dice for almost everything for the Master. And we got....

We are at the Toblerone Estate, a yellow and sky blue palatial estate with an outdoor pool (and an alligator) and a torture room. The Master is a 12 year old boy named Crowley Valentine, a sickly, evil emporer with brown hair and gold eyes. His favorite maid type is a Lolita. Stress explosion: binge eating.

    The maids: Amina, age 30, though she's been a vampire for 2 years. She's a sexy heroine with stalker damage who likes them young (due to a random event, she took this as a perversion). Her clothes are vermillion, her eyes blue, her hair is cream. Stress explosion: teasing. Maid roots: loyalty. Maid power: trespass. She did not make a very good first impression on the other maids, and Ahmad stole her underwear and fed them to the alligator because she insisted that she change into her uniform in his room. Ahmad wanted her to change in the labyrinth outside while Crowley took in the fresh air and drank his bitters (don't ask). She carries a knife. She redeemed herself with the master when he was attacked by ninjas. She killed one, saved Crowley, and completed her task of restocking and cleaning the torture chamber.

    Ahmad, age 14. A sexy Lolita maid with elf ears and a spade tattooed on his chest (make way for the yaoi plot). His maid uniform is yellow, he has purple eyes, and red hair. Maid roots: admirer of maids. Stress explosion: gambling. Maid power: chance meeting. Weapon: pole arm. He doesn't like Amina very much, and was very disappointed when Crowley had him shadow the butler for the day. He is an admirer of maids, not butlers.

On the plus side, all the maids managed to set aside their squabbles and work together to defend the master against the ninjas. Good for them. They all got 2 favor for their teamwork.     \nMurderous Ghosts

    I went into this a little dubious. I wasn't sure if a two-player choose your own adventure could be creepy. I am now quite, quite sure it can be.

    I was the victim and my wife was the ghosts.

    I was, of course, spelunking in the subbasement of an abandoned factory. It was full of rotten and decaying items of daily life: dishes, clothes, toys, furniture, and so on. Water dripped down heavily from the darkness above. Several pools of rusty water had formed. Behind some partitions were crude medical examination tables with straps to hold patients down. There was an Allied propaganda poster from WWII on the wall. I wandered into a padded room, beyond which was an observation room behind what was likely once a two-way mirror. The water was almost gushing in here. In the observation room there were drawers filled with finger bones, dried cartilage, finger nails and that's all I saw before I turned to leave. But the door to the main room wouldn't open. The handle was slick with condensation and would not turn, almost as if something on the other side were turning it in the other direction. I put all my force into it and the door flew open, tossing me onto the floor. Standing in the open doorway was a creature made of water. By his dress it was clear he was a surgeon of some sort. I stayed motionless on the ground as he walked past me and into the observation room. He sat down, drew out a clipboard and began to make the motions of note taking. He had almost a bored expression. I slowly arose and inched toward the door. The ghost looked at me and gestured me into the center of the room. I bolted and made it back into the main room, slamming the door behind me. I took off running in the direction I came and was stopped by two more ghostly figures, these both made out of an amber dimness. It was a Japanese mother and her daughter holding hands. The daughter's other hand was missing three fingers. I tried to run, but was stopped by a tugging on my pant leg. There was another amber figure, a boy, who could not lift himself up.
