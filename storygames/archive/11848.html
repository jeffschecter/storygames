\n Milton Keynes RPG Club are holding another of their games days on 11 September 2010.
\n
\nWHO?
\nWe're Milton Keynes RPG Club. Find out more about us at http://www.mk-rpg.org.uk.
\n
\nWHEN?
\nSaturday 11 September 2010. Doors open at 9.00am, first games start at 10.00am. We'll finish at 11.00pm.
\n
\nWHAT?
\nAny roleplaying or board game. You want to play it, bring it along and run it. Previously we've had on offer 3:16, Amber, Call of Cthulhu, Cold City, Dead of Night, Dogs in the Vineyard, GURPS, Gwenthia, HeroQuest, Hollow Earth Expedition, Hot War, Landston, Mouse Guard, Mythic Russia, Primetime Adventures, Savage Worlds, Spycraft, Tekumel, Trail of Cthulhu, Transhuman Space, True20, Vampire, and some more that I've forgotten. Latest updates will be posted on the Concrete Cow website.
\n
\nA full list of games on offer is on the Concrete Cow games page.
\n
\nWHO'LL BE THERE?
\nWe'll be inviting some Guests of Honour. More details when we have them!
\n
\nWe should have some traders again. Stay tuned for when we get confirmation!
\n
\nMembers of the Concrete Cowncil, MKRPG's convention gaming arm, will be in attendance to run all sorts of funky games.
\n
\nAnd, of course, there's all the fantastic games you'll bring along and run. If you let us know what you're planning to run, we'll put it on the Concrete Cow games page.
\n
\n(Note that for child protection reasons, under-16s will need to be accompanied by an adult at Concrete Cow.)
\n
\nWHERE?
\n
\nThe Old Bath House, Wolverton, in the north-west corner of Milton Keynes. The full address is
\n
\nThe Old Bath House
\n205 Stratford Road
\nWolverton
\nMilton Keynes
\nMK12 5RL
\n
\nMaps are available on MK Web, Google Maps, and Streetmap.co.uk.
\n
\nDirections for drivers are on the directions page.
\n
\nRegular trains to Milton Keynes run from London Euston station. The nearest station to the con is Wolverton (one stop north of Milton Keynes Central), about five minutes' walk away.
\n
\nHOW MUCH?
\n\xa35.00, payable on the door. This'll cover the cost of hiring the hall.
\n
\nFor more information, visit our Yahoo group, email concrete-cow-owner@yahoogroups.com, or ask here!

    \n The very same: the vibrant, thronging metropolis that is Milton Keynes. In fact, I live close to the industrial estate where the climax to The Concrete Jungle take place.
    \n
    \nThe concrete cows are an infamous feature of Milton Keynes, erected soon after the town was created in the 1970s as overspill for London. They're widely known throughout the UK, so it seemed to be an obvious thing to name the con after.

    \n I'm pleased our Guest of Honour for Concrete Cow 10\xbd, Andy Kenrick! For those who don't know him, Andy is the editor of White Dwarf magazine, member of the Collective Endeavour, and the man behind Steampower Publishing, the publishers of Dead of Night 2, the game that revels in the best traditions of horror movies.
    \n
    \nConcrete Cow is also the first con that Andy will attend since the launch of Dead of Night 2, so we'll be hosting a (belated) Launch Party for the game! We're still working out the details, but rest assured that Andy will be chained to a table, running games of Dead of Night when he's not answering questions about what's going on in Games Workshop.
    \n
    \nAnd we still need more games for the Concrete Cow games page, so let us know what you'll be offering.
    \n
    \nSee you on 11 September!

    \n Less than a week to go to Concrete Cow 10\xbd on 11 September, and time to remind you all of the wonderful things that will be happening!
    \n
    \nFirst, there's games! Lots of games! Read the list and quiver in anticipation of all the fun you'll have playing all those games! But there's still time to add your masterpiece to the list and drum up a bit of interest before the event. (In fact, one was added while I was writing this post!)
    \n
    \nThen, there's traders! Leisure Games will be there with lots of shiny books and boxes, and Reapers Revenge will be there with lots of shiny polyhedra (and some not-so-shiny second-hand games). Bob the Diceman also says that he's got stacks of Fudge dice available, which appear to be rarer than hens' teeth at the moment.
    \n
    \nAnd there's lunch! The Verandah Talk restaurant, just over the road from the Old Bath House, are once again offering us a special deal on their lunch buffet. Great food, and bizarre non-alcoholic cocktails.
    \n
    \nAnd a Guest of Honour! Andy Kenrick will be there, running games of Dead of Night 2 all day (literally).
    \n
    \nOh, and there'll also be rooms full of gamers having a great time all day. You know you want to come along and join us!
    \n
    \nCome along to Concrete Cow, for the most* fun you can have with your clothes on!
    \n
    \nNeil.
    \n
    \n(* comparison does not include things that aren't Concrete Cow)
