\n If you're a fan of learning about the history of D&D, or a fan of the OSR, you should take a look at the Ryth Chronicle: a detailed account and record of a long-term D&D campaign from the earliest days of the hobby. (One of the players takes credit for inventing the "20-digit die" by writing all 20 numbers on a icosahedron - Gary Gygax and his crew used dice marked 1-10 and then a coin flip or other randomizer to turn into a proper d20.)

If you get a chance to read it, post your thoughts and observations here.

The most striking thing to me, aside from the clear love and dedication to the project showing in these pages, is the typical number of players in any given "expedition" - often from 8-15 players on any given dungeon delve.
