\n Hopefully, a bunch of you are familiar with The Long Halloween (a.k.a. the best Batman story ever [Frank Millar fans, shut up!]). If not, you should read it.

I have this overwhelming desire to run something that's basically Scream + Gossip Girl + The Long Halloween, set in Gotham city about 5 years after Dark Victory, when Robin's a bit older, but Gotham is still in transition from the gangsters of Year One to the crazy masked villains that appear later. Here's my basic premise:

Gotham Academy is a preparatory school for the children of Gotham City's wealthiest and most famous citizens. Even current Wayne Enterprises chairman, Bruce Wayne, is among the school's prestigious alumni. But this year, things at "The G.A." are different. It began in September, when Luciano Falcone -- the young scion of a formerly notorious crime family that has since "gone legit" -- was killed over the Labor Day weekend. The murderer used a .22 caliber pistol and left a UAW pamphlet at the scene. While the police have emphasized that the killer is probably involved in a work-related dispute with Falcone Industries, the rumor on the street (and in the school hallways) is that Luciano was dispatched by the notorious "Holiday Killer," though all previous suspects in that serial murder case are now dead. Which of Gotham's young elite will be the next victim, with Halloween arriving soon?

Characters:
- Luciano's siblings
- children of other Batman characters and villains?
- other kids at Gotham Academy
- maybe Robin, going undercover as an academy student?

NPCs:
- Bruce Wayne / The Batman
- Commissioner Gordon
- the detective assigned to the case

Basically, at least one character (major or minor) will die every school holiday until the next September, when the Holiday Killer will be revealed or there will be a climactic confrontation of some sort. I'm imagining each session of play will probably last two "months" of time in the game world, showing two separate killings. Play would take place over six sessions, then.

I want the game to develop in the way of an exciting mystery, with lots of clues and red herrings and the like, but I don't want the GM to decide what's really going on and for the players to gradually figure that out. In fact, it would be great if there was a great deal of ambiguity in the end about who the Holiday Killer actually was, while still finding a fitting end to the series. The mystery should be emergent from various character contributions, but still not be all over the place. Have we figured out how to do that yet?

Seems like the Gumshoe system really isn't what I'm looking for. Maybe a modified version of PTA? Not sure how that would handle characters constantly being killed or if it would helpfully lead us to a satisfying conclusion. Also, I'm worried that screen presence might get in the way of the story instead of supporting it. Like, if the events of play clearly tell us who the next bit of play should be about, then if that character's screen presence is low in the next episode, it's weird.

Help?

    \n I'm not that familiar with Over the Edge or Nemesis, but I feel like Unknown Armies, Over the Edge, and Sorcerer all have a lot of mechanics that we'd never really end up using. I was thinking more along the lines of starting with something really sparse, like PTA or Shadows, and building it up from there. Shadows would be interesting because you could have one die represent what your character wants to have happen and other represent what the Holiday Killer wants, then roll them both to see what occurs. That way, you would gradually define what Holiday's motives were as you went along, without them always being 100% successful. And if the desires of Holiday started becoming too disunified, perhaps you could decide that there were multiple Holiday Killers.

    I totally agree that, more important than the system, the various characters and locations and pacing and color are the most significant parts. Seems like having players define NPCs or "sets / locations" could be critical. A relationship map would be cool, but I'd also want it to evolve and develop over the course of play, not be static from the beginning. Like, what if we discover that Luciano Falcone's father wasn't actually a member of the Falcone crime family! That changes everything! Is there a way to work those kinds of revelations into a relationship map or the mechanics of the game? Or should they just emerge from the events of play?

    Your point about suspects and victims is interesting. Ideally, one way that suspects are narrowed down is that some of them will get killed. I'm not sure if PCs should be totally immune to that either, though clearly killing more than a couple is probably not necessary. Perhaps player-defined NPCs (which they may or may not play during earlier scenes) could serve as back-up PCs in case primary characters get killed? So a single player should never lose all their NPCs or, if they do, their PC might be safe. I don't know, something like that might work.
