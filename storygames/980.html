  • Eric Provost
    July 2006
    Oh! I can do that for you Matt...

    To get into a scheduled game, you need a ticket. Tickets are generally $1.50 for every two hours that the game is scheduled to run for. You can either buy tickets with the specific game printed on them (which gives you dibs on getting into that game) or you can buy Generic Tickets. That is, tickets with no games printed on them. They're good for any game but don't give you dibs. Each GM will collect the appropriate number of Genero Tickies from you, meaning 1 for each 2 hour block the game takes up.

    So, like Eric said, if you can't buy the specific ticket 'cuz the game is already sold out, you just put a couple generics in your pocket and show up. If there's a seat you can use your tickets to play. If you can't, well those tickets are just as good for running across the hall to someone else's game too.

    -Eric
