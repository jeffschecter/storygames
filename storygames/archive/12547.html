    \n OK, we played!

    So much fun. All my monster thinking was for naught, Joel came up with the excellent Loon Man, a murderous giant loon that tormented our village, which was on an island in a lake. I'd read about how the Hopi would put corn meal around the village to ward off some evil, so I wanted to incorporate that somehow. Two of the external threats the players authored were Loon Man and The Water is Rising, so I put those together in my first scene as Outside GM. The ring of corn meal kept Loon Man out of the village, but the rising waters were washing away the magical protection. Loon Man got in, and changed himself into a handsome stranger. After an initial conflict I spent all my victory dice to make him a friend of She Changes Minds, Shane's heroic Sweet Water clan ingenue.

    One of my favorite moments came when I recalled that I could personify anything. So when He Hunts Clouds, Joel's dude, decided to climb the imposing mountain called Bald Face, I described how Bald Face frowned down at him and shook his head and sent a cascade of rocks falling from his wrinkles.

    The game's situation seems to write itself - we established that the Outside Chief, His Nose Is Broken, was somehow weak, but didn't know the specifics. Once I had him shoveling corn meal and relying on a sorcerer for the village's protection, it all fell into place. Really cool.

    \n I find this setting so creatively fertile -- a great mix of familiarity with a perspective that's distinctly different, yet relatable. It's really easy to be a neat character with lots of colorful trappings and yet be clearly human. I've played very non-epic games, with fairly "regular people" concerns (social, food, weather), and it's felt more like anthropology than fantasy. Less escapist somehow. On the other hand, I guess it wouldn't be hard to play it like Ganakagok either, resolving giant cataclysms.

    This is one of those designs that I'd play Sim with one group and Narr with another group.

    I was never clear on how much Brennan had a specific vision for how this "should" play, or wanted to leave it customizable. I brought this up in a pretty detailed editing pass I did on an early 2009 draft, but that never turned into a discussion. I'm guessing "customizable" but I haven't seen the final edition.

    Loon Man sounds awesome, as does Bald Face, actually. Great stuff! A weak Outside Chief always causes much drama.

    David, I intended to leave it customizable. In my designs, I like to leave room for each group's individual taste. I've had games of HWCTLH that were crazy over-the-top supernatural, with flying witches riding clouds of owls, and others where all of the drama occurred between characters in the village and there was little if any supernatural influences. It all depends on where the groups' interest turns. The game naturally focuses on whatever the players are collectively interested in, due to the scene framing mechanics.
