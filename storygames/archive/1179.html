This is really interesting, and it would be sad for people not to see it because it's buried in Matt Snyders latest blog post (which everyone should take a look at anyway. But I digress). Anyway...
Jared A. Sorenson wrote:
GenCon has a few "big" RPG companies:

Hero Games
Green Ronin
White Wolf
Wizards

That's pretty much it. And aside from Wizard, they're not especially big.

Without the books for The Forge, Lulu and WDBC, there'd be precious little RPG representation.

Solution 1: take over GenCon (create an area akin to WotC's area filled with indie game goodness).

Solution 2: steal the RPG aspect of gaming away from GenCon... or more to the point, move it to a new con (NextGenCon...haha). I spoke with someone about this yesterday. The ideas that sprungeth foreth...

Set up regional RPG cons across the country at different times of the year. These are for the people who get it. The "indie scene" (FAIAP let's call this "The Forge") has a booth at GenCon but it's smaller and concentrates on "What's new." It, in effect, becomes the showcase for the indie RPG cons and as a way to get people NOT in the choir to start singing.

Meanwhile, the new indie cons set up as non-profit entities so that booth space costs remain low and lots and lots of people can attend, set up shop and rock out for the weekend. Forge Midwest with a dealer's hall.

DISCUSS, MOTHERS.
