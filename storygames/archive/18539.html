So, my little troupe is also interested in Perfect, Unrevised. Similar but different problem: the rulebook here says it is only really suitable for 2-4 players.

Is that a hard and fast rule, or will it just mean a slower game? I'm not too concerned about people being stuck as audience for much of the time, but is it essentially unplayable? Would I be better off splitting into 2 groups, or merely facilitating (which I don't mind doing but, shucks, I always have to GM)?

    \n I'd personally split into a game of 2 and a game of 3. A game of Perfect Unrevised for 5 involves lots of downtime. If you are all good at being engaged audience members and like being audience members, then a game of 5 will totes work for you.

    \n Oh! Also! If you're looking to have a larger group play a game about passionate thought-criminals in a dystopian world, consider checking out the Game Chef 2013 English Runner-Up, Finding Haven.     Not trying to discourage you from playing my game, obviously! I'm just thinking about cool options for larger groups than Perfect is built for.
