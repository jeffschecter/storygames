    \n My experience with roleplaying games is that most of the time "overpowered" is something a player is, and only rarely it's the game crunch. In the type of game that Monsterhearts is, I would expect "stealing the show" to happen thanks to player skills (good narrative flow, lots of ideas, no hesitation in jumping in, knows how to use the rules) more than the specific crunch they've got. Of course you've probably closed off this avenue of inquiry already, knowing your group.

    \n Well, I wouldn't want the skin in my game, if it's the one I'm thinking of. It skeeves me out a little, but that might be just me.

    While what Eero says has sometimes been true in my experience also, I could envisage some moves effectively enabling a player to do preferential scene framing, or inserting their character into other scenes, grabbing extra spotlight (presuming a game like *W where it is supposed to be distributed somewhat equitably). Still needs player initiative, though, and I'm not sure if the Succubus has those anyway.

    In regards the Succubus being mechanically powerful, as an example, their core move lets them offer a voucher good for one 10+ auto-success to another character, gaining a String in the bargain, and marking XP, at no cost or risk to themselves- no roll required or price to be paid. That feels like three (kind of unferal) moves right there, but no, you still get to pick two more.

    I know the MC is supposed to be a fan of the characters, but in my opinion the designer is too much of a fan of this Skin.

    \n I see what you are saying. However, all players still have access to Turning Someone On, Shutting Someone Down and Lashing Out Physically. As means to generate Strings. You don't need Skin specific String generation moves to gain Strings on people. Generating Strings in build into many of the Basic Moves.

    A Vampire with Hypnotic can screw with you, they can generate drama which is a good thing (most likely not for your character but great as a player) but it will precipitate action of some sort, which is at the heart of MH anyways, but it's not like they can kill you or anything since you have control over that aspect. And once they have played their Hypnotizing card, even a player who is unaware of the Move to begin with is then aware and good luck pulling that same card a second time. Telling someone to jump off a building is tantamount to declaring war on that person with everything it entails and all the delicious dramariffic fallout that will follow. And the most fun I've had in MH is when players goals are at odds with each other. When the PC's unite to a common goal the systems pretty much ensures that nothing will stand in their way. At least not for long.

    And while yes, having to keep a String in reserve to mitigate a power sucks and does make the String not fulfill it's purpose in game it still serves a role in game. That of denying the Vampire control over you.

    But really, we've strayed from the original topic of the Succubus and it's power level and moves. I'll agree that Hypnotic is powerful but it's power can be pretty easily mitigated by PCs. NPCs are another matter entirely but they are stolen cars anyways so don't really matter except in how they affect the players and their relations to them. The move can generate drama and conflict which is great IMO. An adversarial group of players may not be to your liking though, and that's okay, to each their own.
