    Who tells the story?

    A onexone encounter isn't just about getting it on â€“ it's interactive erotic fiction. This means that each participant gets to build a story and experience it at the same time.

    Not all roleplay has to be this way. For example, one person could just do all the talking while the other person listens. This isn't necessarily a bad thing, it's a bit like reading fanfic, or watching live performance art.

    However, this author believes that it's more fun when two people are building the story together, each adding elements to complement, surprise and titillate the other participant. Does that remind you of something?

    Of course, the problem with two people trying to tell the same story is that it's quite hard to get into each other's rhythm and come up with stuff that 'fits' with the other person's vision of what should be going on in the story. It's like the difference between a rehearsed song and a jam session.

    The onexone rules are there to help you to build this story together. How does that work?

    They allow each person to give little â€œcuesâ€ to the other, that lets them know whether they're enjoying each other's contribution.

    They let you send clear messages about when to slow down (or speed up) the intimacy of the encounter, without stepping outside the story itself. This is a big deal, especially when you're with someone you haven't played with much before.

    Once you've found another person to play with (either through finding someone's profile and contacting them, or by finding someone you already know who wants to play with you, you should work out some details with each other before you play through an encounter.

    Basic organising stuff

    Before you even decide what sort of story you'll be playing through, you need to check each other's diaries. The things you need to know straight away are:

    When you'll both be free to play;
    How much time you can play for;
    Whether you just want a single play â€œsessionâ€ or whether you are interested in playing over several sessions with breaks in between (for example, once a week for three weeks)
    What you'll be using to talk to each other. For example, a private chatroom, instant messenger or (for slower, more considered stories) email or a private webforum.

    This isn't really any different from setting up a meeting, in real life â€“ which also means that you need to be polite and turn up on time, or at least let the other person know if you're going to be late (or, if you need to change plans.)

    Preparing the encounter

    This is the really fun bit. It's like picking which movie you want to go see together. Unless you've both got very narrow preferences (from your profiles) for the type of roleplay you enjoy, you need to pick the type of game you want to play in this particular encounter.

    Why choose in advance? Like nearly any other activity, a bit of planning is really useful if you're going to enjoy yourselves properly. You don't have to do this days in advance, but it's really important to know before you play what sort of situation and comfort levels you're playing with. The time you spend on this will pay itself back with a better roleplay experience.

    Things you really need to know in advance include:

    The basic situation you'll be playing out. This includes the setting, and any major story or roleplay elements that you both want to introduce
    Whether you prefer to play from a fairly 'fixed' script, or whether it's more fun to add lots of new elements as you go along. Anything you do need 'fixed' should also be agreed before play.
    Anything that you do not want to explore in this encounter. This is not the same as just checking each other's profile. For example, you may sometimes be willing to roleplay certain types of activity, but that doesn't mean you want to do it every time. Checking this now could save a lot of disappointment (or grief) later!
    Any codes you want to use. The most common example of a code is a â€œsafeâ€ word or phrase that means â€œplease stop, I am not comfortableâ€.
