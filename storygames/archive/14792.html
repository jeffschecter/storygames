in NOBODY HAS FACES everyone has lost their identity.

\n

The PCs might still have theirs, but for NPCs, everyone is the same featureless waxy generic-body, a mass produced mannequin with no face and no distinguishing mannerisms, vocal traits, and so on. There are three kinds of body: man, woman, child.

\n

As the GM you may only describe the NPCs by what they are currently doing or wearing. "The man by the door," is fine, "the child with a hat," is fine, "the woman you saw earlier," is not fine because there is no way you can verify whether that is actually the woman you saw earlier or not.

\n

GMs, have NPCs change their clothes often and be sure that you keep track of who's who, but do not give reliable personally-identifying information to the players. When PCs encounter the same person a second time, it should be in a different place and circumstance than the first. NPCs constantly move and take one another's place. Stores don't have shopkeepers; they employ many members of staff.
