With a little help from Gambler's General Store, who do custom-printed poker chips. I bought a hundred, at 17 cents apiece or so. A recent Serenity game where the GM used bullet casings for Plot Points has slowly turned me into a prop-a-holic. I could see getting sets of black, grey and white chips for BWr, and so on...

These replica Chinese coins would also make for good markers of some sort.

    \n You know, I realized during our recent Contenders game that one of the cooler things about using props (also poker chips in that instant) is that not only do the people involved get to push something around physically, but the other players also get to visualize it better. That is, instead of me scribbling on my character sheet, they SEE me handing over a chip or accepting one from the GM. It's more involved in a subtle but neat way.
