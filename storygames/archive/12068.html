\n Nice post by Seth Godin about micro magazines.

Relevant to our hobby I think from several angles:

-Free self-contained games like Lady Blackbird and Archipelago
-Gaming fan magazines like Push and Fight On!
-More scholarly publications like the yearly Knutepunkt book

Thoughts?

How would you like to take your content and use it to create a micro magazine?

    \n Seth's post is really interesting, but also really vague. He starts out by asking if anyone reads Newsweek. The answer is yes. 2 million people read Newsweek. I'm one of them. It gives me a bunch of political coverage I can't get anywhere else. The problem is, when they don't have anything political I want to read this week, I don't buy the magazine. Eventually Newsweek will probably disappear, and I won't have anywhere to go for the stuff that I get out of my Newsweek.

    So I think that Seth's saying there's a place for something digital and micro-sized to replace Newsweek and its ilk--something that people are going to pay money for. How is this different from a blog? A blog can't afford to fund investigative journalism. A blogger probably can't spend a whole year travelling with some presidential campaigner. There are things you can do with professional staff and a budget that you can't do with a blog.

    I'm not sure what that has to do with gamers... but then again, there's Fight On! I'd have to read a dozen different blogs and slog through 10x the volume to get the value I get out of one issue of Fight On! Actually, I do read all those blogs, and I STILL buy Fight On! So FO is definitely giving me something I can't get for free by reading blogs.

    \n It is the future, one way or another. Vogue, like all magazines is a dinosaur slated for extinction. But it won't be pdfs - it will be multimedia for devices like the iPad.

    The key difference is that it will be Push media - something you'll subscribe to (for a fee or not) - so that you will get it when it's fresh and hot.

    And everybody wants ads. When you buy a car magazine, or a computer magazine, or a fashion magazine, or a photography magazine, you're buying it as much or more for the ads as for whatever pathetic "content" it has.

    But the real sea change will be the elimination of the distinction between content and advertising. Everything will be product placement.
