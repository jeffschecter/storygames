\n I'm playtesting Microscope Union and I'm looking for more people to give it a try:\n
Union is a spin-off of the Microscope rules, redesigned to explore a family history and all the triumphs and tragedies as each generation creates the next. But like Microscope, you already know the ending: you know who the final descendent is, the outcome of all these unions, then you go back and see all the lives that came before and made that person who they are.
\nUnion should work for any setting where people have families and ancestors. You could do legacy superheroes, generations of settlers taming the frontier, regal bloodlines a la Aragorn son of Arathorn, etc.

I'm specifically looking for people who will play and give feedback, not just read it. It's based on Microscope, so you need to know how to play Microscope to participate. Email union-playtest + lamemage.com or whisper me your email in this thread. Also let me know the last few games you've played, other than Microscope or Kingdom.

    \n It wasn't the first name I came up with but it really is a perfect fit.

    In the game, the family history is very overtly a series of unions that create the end character: there are two individuals, people with their own lives and achievements, but at some point they come together and make the next generation. Without the union of those two individuals the next generation would not happen.

    Who they are, how they come together and what happens to them afterwards are what you explore in play. Each union is part of the big picture, leading down the family tree, but each life is also it\u2019s own story. In their own time, they are the main characters of their own lives.

    It\u2019s also worth noting that not all parents are parents by blood. The game encompasses fosters parents, adoption, etc.

    \n I agree that Union is a good word for it. What might trip people up momentarily is that the word has other connotations ("states of the union" or "workers union" for instance) which are fairly likely to arise in the minds of anyone who's played Kingdom (and perhaps to a lesser extent, Microscope). Other words (like "Legacy" or "Influence" for instance) carry the "formative" aspect, but less of the "conjoined" aspect, of the word "Union".

    It's kind of a tricky semantic problem.
