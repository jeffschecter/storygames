\n I've just published a free PDF preview of my forthcoming game Radiance. [Edit to add new description]
\n
\nRadiance is a narrative game with a focus on primordial storytelling. You and your friends will create vibrant exciting tales where the activities within the story push the narrative forward. You'll take a primitive stance on creating stories to immerse yourself in ways that are both natural and original. And while you're listening to your friends' narration, you'll be able to introduce disturbances which can change the plot of the story in fun and unexpected ways. Easy to learn rules and no character creation means you'll be ready to play in a few minutes

    \n The activities within the story reveal the characters. As in real life, if you want to bestow character to something, you look at what it is doing.
    \n
    \nRadiance is primitive because its based on animism and contains ritualistic behaviour. The first of which is experience through the notion that anything can be a character and the belief that world has precedence over persons. The latter through the lighting of candles at specific moments to signify the crossing of metaphysical thresholds (much in the way that shamans enter trances with the aid of dancing, sleep deprivation, drumming, or hallucinogens or people become married by saying I do and signing a piece of paper).
    \n
    \nDuring the game you will narrate activities in the story. You'll be able to consult with your audience (the other narrators) and if all is going as it should be, you won't have to roll a single dice. But if they want to introduce a disturbance to the story, something that will distract, impede, halt, corrupt or divert the progress of the activity you're narrating, you'll have the choice of accepting the disturbance or rolling dice to narrate how the activity overcame it. Once you're through narrating the activity, its time for the person to your left to decide the next activity in the story and narrate it.
