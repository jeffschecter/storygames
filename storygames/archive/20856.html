\n I have some traditional RPGs that have lost their appeal or that I simply don't have time for. I don't really want to sell them being that I don't think I could recoup enough to make it worth it. I'm interested in giving the games away to a girl or boy who wants to get into the hobby; a girl would be really cool! Ideally, this would be someone who wouldn't have the means to purchase the RPG books that I would gift them. I would like to do this without being intrusive in any way; basically, just giving the game to their parents or mailing it to them. Has anyone done this and how did they go about it? Is their a way to know the right person is getting it and someone isn't just taking advantage of the situation? Any advice would be greatly appreciated Thanks

    \n An idea specifically for Jeff: bring your book parcel to your gaming club event with a prepared cover letter (something where you describe how you want to give them to some youngsters likely to be interested - basically what you wrote here). Put the parcel on a side table, and point it out to your co-gamers as they come in, in case they know of a good destination. It is likely that some of them have appropriate young relatives who would enjoy the books. Give the parcel to whoever indicates such, and let them take the books the rest of the way. If you have lots of books, just bring them out in several parcels over time, no need to burden a single recipient with a massive pile.

    (I personally wouldn't worry about somebody taking advantage of you in this scenario: presumably the people you game with aren't scummy enough to lie to your face about what they intend to do with your books. I could see that scenario in an open online call for recipients, but not in this sort of face-to-face situation.)

    A lateral idea that might work for your purposes: gift the books to a library. That way many children get to use them, and arguably they will be better utilized, as it is likelier that somebody who really appreciates the given game will find it. Obviously you'll want to make sure that the library has space for the books on their shelves so they don't end up in storage. (Also, I hear that some American libraries maybe don't stock rpgs because they originate in Satan worship or something, but I'm sure you'd know about that better than me. Finnish libraries love to have rpgs, historically they've collectively been my biggest customer.)

    \n With American libraries, you really need to talk to people in person. More often than not, donated books end up as part of the Library Book Sale or Book Sale table, rather than be entered into the collection and placed on shelves.

    Not so much an issue of their Clearly Satanic Nature as an issue of libraries being horribly underfunded.
