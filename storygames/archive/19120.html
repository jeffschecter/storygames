    I guess I was just asking what it is that makes you prefer, whether hypothetically or only sometimes or whatever, 'Rad gaming over Indie gaming. What does Trad do well or better?
    If one defines "indie" as meta-heavy, emo, non-challenge-based, down to earth, shared-GMing gaming, and one defines "trad" as "not indie", then clearly "trad" is better for no-meta, light-hearted, challenge-based, fantastical, "just play your character" gaming.

    And yes, there are plenty of times when I'm in the mood for that, and play a game that supports it.

    Warning: your favorite indie game may be a trad game.

    On the other hand, if one defines "trad" as "from a major publisher", then no, there is nothing in particular that those games do better in play. They do tend to have better artwork, though.

    I think the distinction between Trad and Indie is a bit pointless as game types.
    Not if you're a publisher. For most publishers it helps to know what audience you're aiming your book at, and where and how to reach them. Achieving crossover is very difficult, because if there is too much emphasis on collaborative mechanics, emotional content and hard choices etc. a lot of 'trad' gamers will dismiss it as being too indie, whereas if there are pages and page of character gen and lots of spell and equipment lists and so forth, a lot of 'indie' gamers will say 'Uh, it's too trad'. Sad but true. The one standout exception to this is FATE- which has garnered support across the spectrum- and to a lesser extent PDQ.
