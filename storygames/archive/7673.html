\n Ryan's thread, about wanting a pick-up game to play with cards, made me think of The Pool, using cards.

The original version is on James West's website. This version, with cards, will make more sense if you read that first.

1. Take 15 cards each, as your pool, keeping them face down. Put the rest of the deck on the table.

2. Describe your character in a few sentences.

3. Define traits for your character, by saying them out loud. You may add bonuses to any trait:

* For a one card bonus, deal one card on the table in front of you, face-down, to represent the trait and its bonus.
* For a two card bonus, deal two cards on the table, and discard two more cards.
* For a three card bonus, deal three cards on the table, and discard six more cards.

You'll have to remember the traits and which bonus cards go with which trait. It won't be too hard.

3. To resolve conflicts:

a. The GM deals 1, 2 or 3 cards in front of you.
b. If you can bring in a Trait, add the bonus cards for that Trait, keeping them face-down.
c. You can also gamble up to 9 cards from your pool, dealing them on top of the other cards, face-down.
d. Turn the cards over. You need a Jack, Queen or King, somewhere in the pile, to win.
e. If you used a Trait bonus, deal yourself new bonus cards from the deck for that Trait. For example, if you used a two card Trait bonus, deal yourself two new cards.
f. If you gambled cards, you lose them if you lost the conflict; and you get the same number of cards back if you won the conflict.

4. When you win, you may either deal a new card into your pool, or give a monologue of victory.


I think that'd work.

Graham
