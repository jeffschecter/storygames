    \n What with the other discussion on the board about designing in public, and making blogs a part of it, how about some blogs discussing the design of the biggest game in our hobby? Over at WoTC's site, the D&D4 design team are all keeping blogs; the ones I think are the most regular and interesting are Mike Mearls and Dave Noonan's.     \n Marcus Kings' Titan Games is an interesting store because he sells so much more than game stuff: used CDs, DVDs, video games, coins, etc. The decisions that he would make about stock levels are, therefore, not what a "dedicated" game store would do.     Posted By: Adam JuryMarcus Kings' Titan Games is an interesting store because he sells so much more than game stuff: used CDs, DVDs, video games, coins, etc. The decisions that he would make about stock levels are, therefore, not what a "dedicated" game store would do. Something else he didn't mention in that post, but I've seen him mention several times elsewhere -- his main [only?] gaming competitor in town discounts all RPG titles 20% off, all the time, so Marcus loses a lot of sales of RPG product to him.
    \nHe may as well strip his stock down just to the things that might appeal to people not yet in the RPG market - given that he actually has some of those coming into his store.
