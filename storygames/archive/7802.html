The "Screw all of y'all anyway" Pledge

\n
\n
\n
\n\nTonyLB \n\n\n
\n
\n\nOctober 2008 \n edited October 2008 in Directed Promotion
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n By Gosh and Golly, we've got folks putting up things like the "Intellectual Property Availability Pledge" and making noise about it, and ... well ... I don't wanna take their pledge (which they say is all their thread should be for). I wanna take my own pledge.
\n
\nI pledge to totally ignore anyone who tells me how I should design, produce, market, sell, promote or play my games. I pledge to wilfully and capriciously fly in the face of advice any damn time I feel like doing something different. I pledge that if at any time I no longer want to keep providing people with new copies of my games, they will vanish from availability with a stunning and complete suddenness. I pledge to selfishly pursue my own preferences and visions, even (in fact especially) if it makes other people unhappy that I am doing so.
\n
\nI pledge, in short, to make the games I want to make, and sell them the way I want to sell them, no matter who yowls about it, or what arguments they raise for why my desires in the matter shouldn't count.
\n
\nMoreover, when other folks make and sell games in ways that aren't the way I would do it, I pledge to respond with a hearty "Fuck YEAH!", and my unconditional support for their right to do so.
\n
\n
\nBy posting to this thread you totally do NOT commit to taking the pledge. Go right ahead and tell me how wrong I am. Just remember, I have pledged a solemn pledge not to give a fig.

    Posted By: TonyLBI wanna take my own pledge.
    \nMe too!

    I pledge to let anybody who wants to make my work constantly available do so. It's all open for remix; knock yourselves out. I don't swear to be the one ensuring that it's available; if I don't feel like maintaining it at some point? I won't. (As a side note on that? Mr. Kim; thank you for maintaining the availability of Perfect 20. You rock, and rock hard)

    I also pledge that I will attempt to fail informatively, when I fail, will try to explain odd decisions if asked, and will attempt to explain any successes I may have. I won't be ashamed to request the same; nor will I be angered if those requests are denied either by silence or directly. I will lsten to any advice others have for me. And I'll try to respond with respect. Which does not mean that I will actually act on any damn thing anyone else says.

    I will attempt to respect the opinion and desires of any creator, with regard to their creations. And that includes players and fans as creators just as much as it includes the designers. Which may mean that at some point, you, designer, might not like what I have to say or what I may choose to do with regard to your material. I won't take pleasure in such a conflict of desires, should it occur. But I refuse to pretend that such things are simple, one-way equations.

    That is all.
