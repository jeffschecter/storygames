There is a temple in the center of a universe of open skies. It is surrounded by hundreds of tiny little planets, each containing a unique culture. People on these worlds write letters to the temple asking for help with their problems. Players take on the roles of monks from the temple who, letters in-hand, fly to each of these worlds on a pilgrimage helping as best they can.
\nI put out a call for letters a few months ago, figuring the best way to get a variety of voices in the letters was to actually have several creative people write them! Unsurprisingly, you all provided some really awesome letters, some of which can be read here. Alas, I often hear from letter-writers who have cool ideas, but have no time to actually write them out fully. Well, I still wanna hear 'em!

I'd like to gather up all the little ideas that you don't think you'll get a chance to develop into full letters. All I need is a brief synopsis of a fantastical problem for which someone would write to temple for help. For example:\n
  • A god of war is hopelessly in love with the goddess of beauty, so he needs the pilgrims to play Cyrano.
    • \n
  • The temple gets a letter from a bill-collector, requesting repayment for damages caused a previous group of pilgrims.
    • \n
  • A dwindling tribe of nomadic dinosaurs is being hunted by a comet that destroys every world they live on. It's Battlestar Galactica meets Land of the Lost!
\nSee, it's easy-peasy. Just post your idea and I'll do my best to develop it into a full letter. You'll of course get credit when Do is finally published next year.

    \n Personally, I want to see more situations that problematize the Temple at the Center of the Sky's position in the universe and the freewheeling authority its pilgrims claim. My postcolonialist soul screams against this benevolent autocracy of outside interlopers!

    I also think it'd be cool to have more letters directly addressed to specific pilgrims, since each monk has to be from somewhere, right? Surely, if a world has a problem, their first thought is going to be to send for the monk that's actually from their world, assuming that a native monk will better understand the local conditions. It would be pretty cool to see how a monk reacts to problems on their homeworld too, since they are more invested in the place and things suddenly become more personal. It'd be a good equivalent of a Spotlight Episode in PTA for a character.
