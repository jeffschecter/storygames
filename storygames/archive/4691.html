    \n After our Burning Wheel game fizzled (just didn't feel right), it was my turn to run something. Everyone expects dystopian madness in my games on some level; what they didn't expect was for me to find a pure channel for my immersive style of gaming. Lacuna has seamlessly blended a deceptively simple set of mechanics for rising tension and integrated these mechanics into an engaging, surreal dreamworld.

    The immersion factor in Lacuna is ultra-high; it produces the kind of emotional resonance in our gaming group that has the players actually excited about filling out paperwork. I've actually asked all my Agents to file a brief report; what I got was a deep glimpse into the voice of the characters, and by extension the players themselves. The minimal layering between player and character is intriguing; it eventually becomes impossible to tell when you're in or out of character, and that makes Lacuna succeed in its' main mission: it doesn't let you know you're playing. The players were playing for an hour before they knew it; and everyone was completely impressed and our skeptic gamist in the group was pleasantly surprised at his effectiveness in combat, and got way more into the paperwork than I would have guessed.

    \n My experience as a player in Lacuna is that the less I know about the setting, the more enjoyable the game is, because we players are discovering the setting through play. We began the game on our first mission with some kind of memory lapse about what's going on. To preserve this secrecy, the rule book is divided into sections, some of which are off limits to players. As we've progressed in the game, I've cycled through total bewilderment, to believing I understood I knew what's going on, to expecting everything that has occured to be revealed as a dream or memory...And, with regards to play, I'm not sure if the GM is making everything up as he goes along, or if we're making our way through a planned set of encounters. I'm really enjoying the game! I've posted a bit about it at Virtual Play, Episode 13. There's also a link to the complete audiofile.
