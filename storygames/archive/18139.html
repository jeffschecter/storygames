    \n It will really depend on the convention & the people running games. Hopefully they'll be really great to you!

    Some questions you can ask beforehand, to the organizer or the person running whichever game you're interested in:
    "Will you be teaching the system at the table?" (ideal answer: yes!)
    "Do I need to bring my own character?" (ideal answer: no, we'll create them together!)
    "Are new players welcome?" (ideal answer: most definitely!)

    Sometimes D&D or Pathfinder games will be catered to players with lots of experience and system mastery already. Often indie games will be catered towards the whole spectrum of experience.

    \n I don't think you'd ever be "in the way", and most gamers love to meet new people and introduce them to the stuff they love. I'd just let them know you are new and want to have fun and see where they put you. The people who are running the event, or the store staff, are in a great position to match-make.

    Know that anybody who gives you a hard time or is a dick to you is an outlier you don't want to game with anyway.
