    \n It's an energy thing, and it's hard to pin down. When I get the feeling that letting someone else establish will do any of the following:

    a) bring forth a great idea they're itching to deliver
    b) make them think about how to work their interests into my character's plot
    c) let them know it's totally okay to throw some ideas out there, and that we'll run with them and make them great

    Then I will totally chose to Resolve, especially if I think I've got a good idea to establish.

    If I think there's a good possibility, on the other hand, that any of these will happen:

    d) the spotlight will burn a shy contributor
    e) someone will get cut off or talked over by another player who just hasn't realized that they're not the only person at the table
    f) someone is already using other player's scenes to push unwelcome content

    Then I will chose to Establish instead, because it's not hard to control the possible outcomes with a good strong Establish.

    \n If I am playing with new players, I try to see if there is one option the newer players are not selecting as much and choose that one -- to make sure we've got some variety and they see how it's done. If both options are already getting selected a fair amount, I usually opt for Establishing about 75% of the time, Resolving the 25% of the time when I don't immediately have an idea for a scene.

    My rule of thumb is don't hold up the energy of the game deliberating on Establishing vs. Resolving -- if you can't come up with something, chances are somebody else will.

    I never really decide to Establish or Resolve for strategic reasons (i.e., to make sure I am the one selecting a die color), but I know others who do, so it makes for a good mix.
