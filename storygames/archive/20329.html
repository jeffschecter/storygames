\n I feel like a bit of technological neophyte for not learning this earlier, but I'm probably not the only one, so I thought I'd post about it:

On most Google or iPhone devices, you can draw a card or roll dice.

Just activate the Siri or Google feature and say, "pick a card". The device will give you a random card.

Say, "roll a die", and it will give you a single d6 roll.

Say, "roll dice", and it will give you a 2d6 roll.

I don't know how random the output is, or how it's generated, but it gives you an easy way to play 2d6 games (like any PbtA games) in a pinch.

    \n Back in the Nordic / Turku "immersion" marshlands of the 90's and 00's, we were so down on anything with a glowing screen at the gaming table.

    Candles, paper... that was the ticket.

    That really set the tone for my first gaming decade. The other reason was "Well, I'm doing this because I want to get away from all the lan-parties and Counter-Strike dorks, to do something that's really FTF".

    But now phones are so ubiquitous and tablets, too. I don't really put the kibosh on my players using phones and tablets for their characters and similar things.
    One of them wanted me to bring up the region map on a tablet but I nixed that. So there's a little bit of luddite still in me.

    Intellectually, I've realized that the game I'm playing now isn't as "fragile" as those 00's super moody horror improv sessions were. But I haven't internalized it. It still bugs me when they bring up their devices (but I want to get away from the DM = boss role & responsibility mindset). And actually... I kinda want to some day bring the two styles together. Cozy lighting, no glowing screens, long stretches of in-character stuff (whether it be Finch-style trap finding or Laws-style dialogue) still built upon a solid OSR-D&D chassi.
