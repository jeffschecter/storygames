\n Over on my journal (and, now, in a fair few other places), I'm distributing a game by online meme. That is, there's a download button, and then a discussion of the fact that the distribution IS a meme, and "wants" to be copied. The game, Microcosm, is eighteen 5.5 x 8.5 pages, and includes a ground-up "how to play" for new people. Now, the game is basically a highly reingineered version of "Token Effort" - the system from In Spaaace!, with a few grafts from Cat, and a collaborative relationship-map method of building situation.

It needs to be slimmer and catchier. So I'm grinding and rewriting down to 12 pages, breaking up as many of the dull text blocks as possible, adding little icons, coloring up the relationship maps, moving to plainer language, and so on - with any luck, I'll be done that first grind today, and I'll post up a copy of the revised version here.

I'm sure it can get even keener than I'm making it now; in this case, the criteria for "keen" is "extremely attractive and accessible". I want it to be incredibly easy for new people to read, and I want it to make those of them that might be inclined to play an RPG feel like they can do so immediately.

And I really, really, want to work on the delivery system. Right now, You'd most likely only distribute the meme further if you know me, are a pretty hardcore gamer, or are interested in self-publishing or memes themselves. And there, I'm somewhat baffled. I've been seriously leaning toward building one of those blog quizzes that gives you a result for you, and the result is a situation you might like to play in, with a link to the rules.

But I really don't know. At this point, "trying goofy ideas" is pretty much par for the course. So if you have any, hit me.

    \n Levi, the picture is cool, but there's no call to action on it. If you put some text that said something like, "Have you experienced Microcosm?" or whatever, it would probably get more clicks. And if it was more the size of a banner ad (flat and long), it might be less obtrusive for someone to add to their blog or sig.\n
    And I really, really, want to work on the delivery system. Right now, You'd most likely only distribute the meme further if you know me, are a pretty hardcore gamer, or are interested in self-publishing or memes themselves.
    \nI totally agree with you here. If you look at some of the other successful viral things on the web (SETI@HOME, et al) there is a backend connected to it. So in other words, there is some connection and incentive to passing the meme around. even a simple automated counter could be golden. Imagine if someone's blog got updated (via your server) to say, "I've turned xx people on to Microcosm...are you one of them?"

    I've only taken a real quick look at your content, and I'm totally excited that you are doing this. But it's pretty dense. I can't imagine a non-hardcore gamer getting through more than a few pages of it. Here's another idea; what if you made the core rules very simple(like 3-4 pages tops) and then directed them to your site/LJ/wiki to get more content? Give them just enough sweetness to get them interested but not overwhelmed.
