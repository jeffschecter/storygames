    \nOnce we are into our new space we'll be able to offer basic POD printing for books, POD cards, custom die cut stickers, art prints, etc. I've done some pricing and expect for your standard 8.5" x 11" books we would be somewhere between Lulu pricing and that of Avalon Innovations. We won't be able to do hard cover books, just perfect bound.
    \n
    \nFor 6" x 9" paperbacks I expect we'll be able to be a bit more aggressive. Probably clocking in at a penny a page plus $1.50 for cover and binding. But we're still working on finalizing our pricing for books.
    \n
    \nOnce I have the new machines to you with, I will be experimenting with mini book printing. I have several game lines that would go well in those formats. If I can perfect those kinds of bindings, mini books will be something we offer for POD printings.
    \n
    \nWe also won't have the distribution venues in with Ingrams like LSI does or with Amazon like Lulu does. Though I expect if we started to generate enough book printing business, trying to establish those connections is something I would pursue.
    \n
    \nWe will be offering drop ship services, but I doubt we're ever going to do true POD. More likely we would require minimum print runs of like 10 books and up to 18-27 books for mini books. We would warehouse that small quantity on hand to utilize as the inventory to ship when drop ship orders were given to us. Depending on what was being printed, we would have options for in store and online retailing as well.

    \n I'm currently in the process of setting up my novel City of Masks through CreateSpace. It's been pretty straightforward so far; I'm waiting for the proof to reach me.

    The reason I went with them rather than Lulu is that, because CreateSpace is owned by Amazon, you can get straight into Amazon's catalog for free with your own ISBN and imprint. Lulu charges you for access to distribution and it looks complicated and non-non-US-publisher-friendly.

    I'm not sure where you get "Lulu is significantly cheaper":

    Lulu: $4.53 base cost + 0.02 per page; 20% commission.
    CreateSpace: $3.15 base cost + 0.02 per page; 20% commission through their store or 30% through Amazon.

    Ah, wait on, Lulu's 20% is of profit while CreateSpace's is of the total price. Lulu's bulk discount also cuts in sooner and appears to be more generous, though it's hard to tell as they don't seem to set it out conveniently in one place.

    I may go with a dual setup, since there's nothing to stop me doing so.

    EDIT: Except the fact that I chose 8.5" x 5.5" as my size at CreateSpace, and Lulu don't print that size. D'oh!

    \nYes, I am very familiar with Rapid Pod and Ken and his history. Don't worry, we have no intentions of following in their footsteps. lol. Also, you have to remember, often what got said and what was true there were often two different things. While it was spun that they were simply doing so much business they couldn't keep up with orders, that seems to ring false when you consider that a number of times they got up on RPG.net and other gaming sites throwing desperate rediculously low priced offers out there to try and drum up business. Having worked with a lot of old machinery myself, my own personal working theory is products were sometimes delayed because of machines going down and them not having the funds to replace or repair them. Really, as a business if you are doing so much business you can't keep up with demand, its easy to get a line of credit or other capital funding. In that case you just go out and buy or lease new machines and add staff to handle the increased demand. Too much business are "problems" you want to have. And if you planned in advance for that event, you stroll onwards and keep on growing. Only if you have no plan, then you find yourself trying to operate the business by the seat of your pants and are as likely to run it into the ground as grow it.
    \n
    \nThe price I listed above was only for 6" x 9" novel printing, essentially, though I guess someone could format an RPG at that size for the price benefits. Generally speaking that size is meant for trade paperbacks. 8.5" x 11" would cost us a good 40% more, so I expect our pricing on those would also run 40-50% more. But like I said, we're still working on finalizing our pricing for some of these services.
    \n
    \nThe best way to visualize the store that we are opening is take your average Kinkos and your average game store and slap them together, then shrink the overall space some. So the retail end of our business will have a goodly number of revenue streams and won't be reliant on just book printing, card printing, local copy and print services, B&M game sales, etc. You won't ever see an fire sales there. The occasional targeted promotion to pick up more customers and drive branding, sure, but no acts of desperation to drive cash flow. lol.

    I loaded my revised file on both sites the same afternoon, one after the other, and watched them race.

    The result... depends on your interpretation. CreateSpace's proof arrived first by quite a margin, but it had exactly the same error as the first proof I got from them, which they'd subsequently corrected - not a good look. The fourth, hopefully also corrected, proof from them is on its way to me currently, but the Lulu one reached me today, and is fine. (Which means my book is now for sale, plug, plug.)

    \n Wow, it's been two months, nearly, but I have written that article comparing and contrasting Lulu and CreateSpace now (pdf).

    I included the local printer I used, Zenith, for a third point of comparison with Lulu and CreateSpace. Quick summary (from my blog post):

    * CreateSpace is overall the best for my purposes: simple but functional website, free access to Amazon, price per unit comparable to Lulu. However, the only printing fault I had was from them, and their customer service isn't as good as the other two, mainly because it's so indirect.
    * Zenith gave excellent customer service and were cost-effective for bulk orders for me, because they're local. Their website needs serious work, though.
    * Lulu, for my purposes, weren't worthwhile because the high cost of shipping to New Zealand negated the advantage of their excellent bulk discounts. If they print locally to you, though, it would be worth using them as well as CreateSpace.
