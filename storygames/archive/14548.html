\n This is a brainstorming thread, for a technique I think can be cool.

So usually, ritual phrases are spoken by the players. "When a players says , happens."

What if some phrases were spoken by characters? Here are two examples:

When a character says "There's snow in the air", one of the other characters present will die soon.
When a character says to another "I love you", that character must have sex with someone else the same day.

Wanna think up some others?

    \nWhen a character says "I've heard of you," they & their addressee will be revealed to have a shared past in this or the next scene.

    When a character asks for something to be given back, more unfairness will be piled on them, decided by the player with the fewest tokens.

    When a character says "There's no such thing as goblins!" one of the goblins will swap one of their items with a random item from table B.

    When Big Chuck says "Get back in the truck," then there will be a fire soon after, and either Big Chuck will have someone new on his trail or his wounds will reveal themselves to be worse than they appeared.

    When Amanda says "Let's stay out a little longer," the dawn die is re-rolled, but one of the other kids must take a sleepy token.

    This is fun. There are a lot of things we can do with it!
