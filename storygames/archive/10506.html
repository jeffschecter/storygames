\n I just got back from Italy where I rocked out with the Italians behind Janus Designs, some of whom hang out around here. They organized up a little game day where, to my great surprise and delight, all my games got played. I facilitated a game of Fiasco and a chapter of Grey Ranks, and a game of The Roach was played in Italiano - I think it went well; there was a lot of screaming. Everybody was extraordinarily welcoming and kind, and I'm very excited about the connections we made.

It's always really interesting to be a part of a different culture of play. In the case of the folks I played with in Chivasso, there was a much greater emphasis on fellowship over "productivity", and it shocked me to realize how hard we push at our local house-cons - squeezing in as much gaming as possible at the expense of simple socialization. This was a great lesson for me, and now I want to host a Relaxicon where sharing meals and enjoying each others company as friends has equal billing with whatever games get played. This may be a good topic for its own thread.

    \n It's funny you mention it, Jason, because I was just telling my girlfriend about the home-con that I hosted here at our house over the weekend and that for the first time, I felt that the socializing part of the con was equal in importance - and enjoyment - to the gaming. And a big part of that was the delicious meal that Johannes and Anne prepared for us yesterday evening - just sitting around the dinner table, sharing a great, tasty meal and talking about this and that after having played great games was extremely enjoyable. It isn't rocket science but it's one hell of an important realization for me. Roleplaying, after all, is a social THING (couldn't think of a better word - tired after three days of gaming) and oftentime quiate intimate. Sharing a meal helps strengthen the bonds we've created.

    \n For our part (I'm the guy at 0:25) I can say we had great fun: Jason (and Autumn) were a pleasant and fun company.
    \nSpeaking of the gaming side of things I'd say it went very well. We played a game of Fiasco with 4 out of 5 of the players roleplaying in a second language, and 3 out of 5 who never read the game.
    \n
    \nWe had fun, we had a very sad and melancholic story in a western town with an ex-robber Sheriff, a crime-lord banker, a not-very-heroic deputy and his cheating but loving wife. The language barrier meant a slower game, but it worked and I'm very happy for that. Jason confirmed that our not-so-perfect language did not impair the enjoyement for him too. I hope to find some time to write a longer AP report soon.
    \n
    \nThe (short) game of The Roach was very (very) loud and wild, and piqued my interest to play it again: it seems to really have good potential as a party game too.
    \n
    \nI know my friends were happy with the Gray Ranks game.
    \n
    \nThe "relaxicon" aspect I guess came pretty much naturally: it's not like we planned to have time to play and enough time to socialize, and stuff. I guess it's just the way we'd do this. Also, having Jason and Autumn experience some good Italian food was one of our priorities.
