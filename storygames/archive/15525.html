    \n They're very much like Keys. They give you XP when you hit the triggers. You spend the XP on upgrades and unlockable things.

    I guess the big thing with Milestones is they're aimed at a significant decision point, some kind of major choice you must make as your hero. Once you make that choice, you get 10 XP and close out the Milestone.

    Each Event has some Milestones you can go after, or you can stick with your hero's personal Milestones, or you can mix and match. The Event Milestones help to create the storyline as much as the prewritten scenes and set pieces do.

    \n I am going to assume that a lot of players who are interested in this game want a solid dose of genre emulation. Sure, they will want to do it their way, but overarchingly, the game should provide the event structure. Milestones, from what I gather, are a great way to do this.     \n Okay, it's out! So I can talk about Milestones more now.

    Milestones are very, very interesting.

    As I mentioned above, I've identified several types of Milestones:

    Personal Milestones. You're a drunk. You're having a nervous breakdown because the love of your life was murdered by Bullseye in a church. You crave the spotlight. You are looking for love. You stab people in the back.

    Power-Related Milestones. Oh my god, you're a filthy mutant. The corporation that built your cybernetics wants it back. You are a werewolf by night and can't control your urges.

    Team-Related Milestones. Nobody on the team respects you. You want to be the leader of the team SO BAD. You are the leader of the team. The team has money problems. The government runs the team. Your old enemy is running the team.

    All of the above are created and selected by the player.

    There's another kind of Milestone, created by the GM but still selected by the player: the Event-Related Milestone. Shapeshifting aliens are taking over. SHIELD is acting shady. A mysterious figure is behind the event. The Kingpin appears dead and everyone wants a piece of the kingdom.

    It can be remarkably specific. In fact, I feel the more specific the Milestone triggers are, the clearer the dramatic direction of the character is.

    The mini-Event in the main book has 3 Milestones. Multiple players can take them, or nobody can take them. I think there might be some mileage in having unique Milestones that only one person can select, too.

    Milestones are structured similarly to Keys, but I see them as very different in function. Milestones are a pacing mechanism - if you finish an Event and have done none of them, that's okay. You aren't missing out on anything like you are missing out on the endgame of TSOY. Instead, here's how I think of Milestone levels:

    1 XP triggers: This is a small thing that your character does, related to the issue they're dealing with. It is something the other characters may immediately react to, but which doesn't really "go anywhere". You only ever get 1 XP from a Scene from this level of Milestone. I see this as something where the audience chuckles and goes "welp, that's Wolverine" or whoever the character is. It's just a simple character note.

    3 XP triggers: This is a larger thing your character does - something the other characters may need to base a scene around, confront you about, or talk to each other about. Oh my god, it's actually serious. You only ever get 3 XP from a Scene from this level of Milestone.

    10 XP triggers. You can only hit them once in an Act. This is something that changes the whole of the game going forward for you. This is a major reveal that the audience sits up and takes notice of. It is absolutely unmissable and multiple scenes, confrontations and even conflicts can come from it.

    I'll post a bunch of sample Milestones I came up with to help plan my Smallville game (Smallville has no XP so I couldn't actually use them).

    \nGeraldine "Jer" Parchman:

    Heartbroke Human: She fell in love with a mutant and threw her life away trying to help him. It didn't work out and he dumped her.

    1 XP Make a snide observation about love or a couple.

    3 XP: Ruin a relationship by telling someone a hard truth or secret about the other person.

    10 XP: Fall in love again, or join the Friends of Humanity.

    Note that the 10 XP level gives two clear options for what her next Milestone might be.

    In the current Marvel game, you don't have to chain together Milestones like this. In fact, once you resolve a Milestone, you don't have to pick another until the next Event if you don't want to. But I feel that it gives a strong direction. We know what Jer's next problem is going to be:

    Half-Hearted Hater: She joined the Friends of Humanity out of bitterness over her mutant-broken heart.

    1 XP: when you describe mutants as traitorous, backstabbing, or betrayers.

    3 XP: When you out a closeted mutant online or in the media.

    10 XP: When you betray the Friends to the X-Men, or establish a secret identity to work against the Friends.

    Or maybe if I wanted her to be more of a real hater, I could make it:

    10 XP: When a Friends action you support causes the death of a mutant or when you are given a high position in the Friends.

    \n Yeah, exactly. You choose your Milestones anew at the beginning of each Event, or (if you want) when you resolve a previous Milestone. You can pick the same Milestone again if you want - Bendis' Daredevil (hand on heart) was all about different ways of fucking with his secret identity - but you aren't required to and you can always move on if you want to try a different point of view.

    This is really important, because to me the key problem with comics continuity isn't that someone will get a power slightly different from how it was in 1968 or whatever, people are pretty forgiving about that (more or less). It's that over the years different writers see different characters in different ways, and have them play different roles in different stories, and eventually you get boxed into what a character would or wouldn't do, or you end up doing the same shit over and over, because that's all you can imagine the character doing. This is why reboots/reimaginings are so tempting in comics, because the iconic, broadly drawn character is compelling, but the writer wants is to do something new with it. So basically this setup is how the comics should do it, but they don't do it as well or as often as you will.

    \n I almost forgot, I had a first draft of a post written before the game came out and I realized, oh, uh, yeah, NDA. So here it is!

    Here's a set of Milestones I wrote for one of my Marvel universe characters (who previously existed only in the Smallville system) and my reasoning for each level. It's not quite like Keys. Keys are pretty unique. This is not that.

    SHIELD Agent Johann Amadeus Sutherland

    Milestone: SHIELD's Least Wanted - After the death of his partner (and a whole squad, and a lot of embarrassing lawsuits) in an ill-advised attempt to bring down the Kingpin, Agent Sutherland is jammed into the least desirable positions and given the worst missions Colonel Fury can think of.

    You pick a Milestone based on the situation you're in at the start of the event. Sometimes it's related to your personal life, sometimes to a professional problem, sometimes to the event. This is a good example of a professional problem or a problem related to the hero's calling.

    J.A. wants to do good but he really fucked up bad. So he's on his own, gathering his own assets from the other heroes (PCs) he can find and chasing down any lead he can - normally on his own - to redeem himself in the eyes of his superiors and himself. So here's the Milestones I worked up:

    Gain 1 XP when you tell another hero you're a Customs agent.

    A 1 XP Milestone is something that you semi-routinely do that adds some flavor to a scene, gives a little juice to a relationship to another PC, etc. Essentially it creates the possibility of approximately a scene or two in the future. \n
      \n
    • "Hey, that Sutherland guy's not with Customs!" "What gives, J.A.?"
      • \n
    • "Look guys, I have to level with you. I'm not really a Customs agent."
      • \n
    • "That's classified, Customs doesn't need to know about that." "I thought you were Customs!"
    • "You lied to me! How could you lie to me?! You prick!"
    • "Fuck SHIELD, right JA, I bet you Customs guys are really tired of SHIELD."
    \nAnd so on.

    Gain 3 XP when you get caught omitting crucial intel from a report, or when your SHIELD-related traits are shutdown because they won't back you up.

    Basically a 3 XP Milestone is something that happens less often, adds significant complications or dangers to a scene or relationship or creates several scenes or problems that have to be resolved, a sequence of issues and consequences. Nevertheless it's still within "normal" for the character you've made in the situation you're in. It's like the problem/situation you wanted your character to be in, the central part of it when you envisioned how your character might change or struggle. When this happens you're right on target with that aspect of your character's situation/in the thick of it.\n
      \n
    • "Oh my god, didn't you tell them it was mutant terrorists riding dinosaurs?! I guess we're really on our own now..."
    • "Agent Sutherland, you really thought you could partner with the Punisher and we wouldn't know?"
    • "What part of 'SHIELD takes no role in the mutant issue' do you not understand, Sutherland?"
    • "Er, hello, Colonel Fury, fancy meeting you here."
    • "You don't believe the building is surrounded with 100 SHIELD agents with laser rifles? How about a half dozen boy scouts with slingshots?"
    \nAnd so on.

    Gain 10 XP and delete the Milestone when you must either temporarily take command of SHIELD or when you are brought up on charges of treason by SHIELD.

    Write your 10 XP Milestone to be related to the Next Big Thing that you are going to do - really, the next Milestone in the arena (personal, professional, spiritual, etc.) that you want to have to deal with. Basically they are game-changers. Everything is different not just for you but for everyone in your life after the 10 XP Milestone. If you are ever going to go back to the way things were, guess what? It's time to write a new Milestone all about trying to get back to how things were.\n
      \n
    • "Everyone else is dead, Agent Sutherland, not to mention you're the only one who seems to know what a Norn Stone is. What are your orders?"
    • "Hello, I'm your court appointed defense attorney, Franklin Nelson. You can call me Foggy."
    • "The Valkyries are Oscar Mike, mount up and follow, you heard Director Sutherland, this is Ragnarok!"
    • "This plea offer isn't really what you'd call an offer, because you can refuse an offer."
    • "Did you see the front page of the Bugle today?"
    \nAnd that's how you write Milestones for your character.

    \n Event Milestones work. Character Milestones are a net negative and the game would be better off without them.

    1) The primary benefit is to provide bullet point ideas on how to play the character like the comics. Problem is:
    a) the majority of players will already be Marvel Fans and know far better how to play the characters than these brief snippets anyway.
    b) for those that aren't the brief snippets are really insufficient to be of much use.
    c) far too many of them are just out right bad...either mechanically flawed or narratively questionable. Three separate games, three different players spent half the game turning to the other players and announcing "I'm not Daredevil" and marking XP.     2) But beyond that, XPs are a completely uninteresting and unnecessary currency. The only thing XPs do that is of any interest whatsoever is to allow you to rejigger the Sole, Buddy, and Team dice. That would have been FAR better handled like Challenging from Smallville. Challenge your Team stat and get triple dice for 1 roll and the right to reorder the dice once per session. Done. Far more fictionally interesting and no extra fiddly currency needed.


    I'm a fan of keys...but character milestones might be the absolute worst implementation of them I've yet seen...mechanically flawed, potentially culturally offensive, and entirely unneeded...

    Here's a damn near mandatory hack. Scrap XPs altogether, eliminate all character Milestones altogether. Reward Plot Points for Event Milestones (gee...get PLOT points for hitting the PLOT triggers in an event...that kind of makes sense). Seriously...they're APPALLING.

    \n I like Milestones, kind of the same way I liked Nature/Demeanor in the old WOD - they provide an idea of your character's motivations and goals, as well as what would really be a significant change of direction for the character.

    As for people abusing the Milestones to get XP left and right, there's already a limit built in, not just in how many times per Scene you can use it, but also these two paragraphs:

    You may hit the 10 XP trigger only once per Act and once you hit this trigger you\u2019ve reached your Milestone. You may now close out the Milestone and choose a new one. If it makes sense within the story, you can choose the same Milestone again, ready to pursue it in the next Act.

    Earning XP
    As a player, it\u2019s up to you to keep track of your Milestones. Alert the Watcher to them when there\u2019s a lot going on and things get chaotic\u2014the Watcher can\u2019t keep track of them for you, although it\u2019s generally a good idea for the Watcher to have each hero\u2019s Milestones written down in a list in the Event notes. When
    something happens that lets you hit a trigger for XP, speak up and announce it to the table. Then, assuming you\u2019re not way off your mark, make a note of the XP you just earned.

    So, in the example of the player of Captain America promoting Luke Cage to lead the Avengers, and then firing him, wouldn't work because A) They could only use the 10XP Milestone once per act, and They would be closing out that Milestone anyway (Cap just gave up his leadership role to Luke Cage, so can't actually kick him off since Cap is no longer the leader...at least not without some good in-story reason for Cap to actually keep that Milestone), and would need to choose a new Milestone.
