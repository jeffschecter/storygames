\n Inspired by the recent posts about gamers and fashion, Shreyas and I have embarked on a new project!

The MANual of Style is a blog that, as Joe MacDonald said, sets out to teach fashion through tutorials the way that one might teach InDesign. It will start with the basic fundamentals-- skincare, silhouettes, how to shop-- and move through developing your own personal style. Tutorials will be posted on Tuesdays and Thursdays, with specific products featured on Saturdays for breakdown and analysis. We've got some pretty big plans, including multimedia tutorials (Flash paper dolls to help demonstrate what kind of glasses frames work for what kind of face, for example). Shreyas' audience is definitely hip gamer types, so it won't bear much, if any, resemblance to Put This On stylistically.

Shreyas is doing the writing, I'm just doing the graphic design and administration, perhaps with the occasional "This is what a girl thinks" butt-in. I might do a post later on how to take a good profile picture for Facebook/online dating sites/etc, since most girls already know those tricks, but I don't think many guys do. It might be too much of a digression, though. Anyway. We're hoping the site generates a lot of discussion, and Shreyas is excited about doing some Q&A.

The first post goes up tomorrow.

      \n It is a quite defensible web-design decision to leave the font size up to the end-user and his display device. Technically there is nothing preventing a web browser from making a font-size adjustment permanent in the given user's settings, so that you wouldn't have to adjust it every time you visit the site. As different users use different screens and viewing conditions, it's really rather difficult to say that some font size is too large or too small objectively - the best we can do is to make layouts that conform to browsers rendering the fonts in different sizes, leaving it at that.
