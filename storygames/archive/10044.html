\n I'm going to run Geiger Counter at a local con tomorrow, and I suddenly realised that I'd like to put together a style sheet for it. My pitch is pretty simple:\n
"The Hollywood Murders"

Early 1950s Los Angeles. Detectives, gangsters, movie stars and a vicious murderer spreading terror through the city.
\nI'm thinking it's a cross between LA Confidential, Sweet Smell of Success, Kiss Me Deadly and Halloween. I was hoping to pick your brains about stuff to add into the style sheets. I figured I'd do a brain dump of what I had so far, and add to it throughout the course of the day ...

Setting Details
Hollywood studio offices and backlot, police station, a private detective's office, mansions in the hills, the freeway, ...

Menace Dice
Unstoppable, More than one of them, Hides in the Shadows, Silent as the Night ...

Character Goals
Nothing yet ...

Character Concepts
Starlet, PI, vice cop, tabloid journalist, drunkard, busboy, jazz musician, studio exec, femme fatale, Deputy mayor (or some other mid-level local politician susceptible to or fighting against corruption), a Stranger in town, mob enforcer, war veteran ...

Character Names
The Story Games name project (below) ...

Survival Dice
A brief description of why the character's life is worth fighting for ...

Advantage Dice
Badge, gun, movie star good looks, Moxie, Connections, Your Fedora, Voice-over, suitcase full of money (some of it covered in blood) ...

Special Conditions
Suspected - the cops are either hunting you or interrogating you in connection with the killings. BUYOFF: provide evidence that exonorates you

Marked for Death - This replaces 'Infected'. Any character in a scene with a character who has this condition rolls a single die at the end of the scene. On a 1 or 2, they are Marked for Death too and are now specifically targeted by the killer. BUYOFF: No idea, actually.

On a Bender - You're all hopped up on booze and barbiturates.You can't move from your location by yourself.

Blackmailed - One of the other characters has some salacious dirt on you.

That's what I've got so far. You got any ideas or 1950s Hollywood trivia to share?
