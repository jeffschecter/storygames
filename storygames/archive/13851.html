    \n I just feel crap now that it's over, because I want it to last forever. Other than that...

    I went to the Grand Mechanism/"The Art Director Must Die", which was an amazing larp impro game centering around the power hierarchy in a theatre. We got to recite Shakespeare, which I've never done before, and now I understand why it's such a big deal.

    Workshops on improvisation and actions. Intense scenes of quarrels and death in a cozy loft with Danish spring sunlight coming in the windows.

    Drunkenness at the parties. A guy dressed up as a faerie (or fairy) Hitler. The Norwegian ritual phrase "womba" transmuted to the Finnish "wombalainen".

    I learned that I want to be an actor in my next life. Although a friend told me that in alternate universes we're all doing a million other things, so that's okay.

    And oh, BY THE WAY, I launched Playground magazine! With awesomeness! Champagne! The greatest people ever! And stuff!

    \n It was fun and intense, and now I am bit tired, but at least there only two months to Fastaval, and then we go again.

    I learned about ethics and roleplaying - or rather I saw how the latest games have started roleplayers reflect on the ethics of manipulating players (not just at Knudepunkt, but also in Playground Magazine (article by Peter Fallesen) and the planB-blog) - and I saw people discuss the difficulties of creating games about totalitarian regimes/nazis involving German players.

    I got to talk about Fastaval, and saw the confused faces of the Israeli players, when I talked about our scenario-tradition, and I met up with people from the larpfactories, and there was a reception for the cool new magazine. Hurray for Playground Magazine. Don't forget to get your subscription at playgroundmagazine.net and meet us at Fastaval at the Grand Bazaar.

    And there was a lot more, and drinking, and cool books.

    \n The Knudepunkt-threads over at Norwegian laivforum.net (in English): http://laivforum.net/forums/14-Knutepunkt

    My post from the "stuff I liked"-thread:

    I liked...

    * The hotel! It was spacious and nice, had a sauna, good food and areas for play and lectures.
    * Personally achieving a good mix between doing and listening.
    * All the good people! It's fantastic to spend a weekend with so many people who share my interests from all over the world.
    * The organizers were very helpful and service-minded and seemed, well, organized.
    * Three books! Wow!
    * Fantasiforbundet and Martin Nielsen organizing the trip for around 40 (?) Norwegians. Nice bunch! Strengthened a few friendships, got some new.
    * The starting game: a good way to break the ice and become warm before the convention proper
    * The Tribunal: a cool, short larp with semi-political content. Lot's of good role playing going on, high intensity.
    * Child's play: a bit of physical warm up in the morning was good
    * The walkabout project: Very interesting project in the grey area between performance and larp, presented in an understandable way
    * The pub: socializin', getting to know people, finding good contacts for the Playground news section
    * Loosing the loosing game before it even started, and having time for the Playground release party instead
    * Morning energizer: again; nice to warm up before the day's program items started
    * Willfull action: nice little improvisational practice, good to exercise those muscles
    * Manipulating for good: only saw the first half. Many good presentations and an interesting problem to be addressed
    * This is your dream: zoning out and into the dream without safety nets in the form of many formalized rules and meta techniques. A highly fragile illusion which worked for me.
    * Jeep in the forest: very cool project of "larp embedding", and a good format with questions from the audience along the way
    * A Doomsday's Eve: One of the best presentations I saw, with video and insightful commentary by Christer. An apocalyptic larp where the world was brought to the brink of nuclear war.

    \nThis is your dream
    The Walkabout project is Finnish. Johanna MacDonald and Aarni Korpela ran it. They're doing something in between performance and larp, exploring that "grey area" (this is just from my recollection of their presentation). The projects have been based on the archetypes from the major arcana in the tarot "even though it's a bit cheesy it gives some kind of framework to the audience/participants/players". This summer they will hold their third installation/performance/whatever in Helsinki, "The Lovers' Match-Making Agency. For all kinds of encounters and relationships From jogging partners to soulmates From seconds to a lifetime From ridiculous to sublime", based on the card "The Lovers". I consider going. http://walkabout.happeningfish.com/ will supposedly be up soon.

    The "This is your dream" game was based on the installation Hermitii, based on the Hermit card, which had to do with the tarot card of the same name and was about withdrawal and reflection. The "game" itself consisted of an introduction where we were asked to "perceive the world as if a dream", there were not many set rules and "no wrong way of playing it". There was a sort of ritual countdown/intro with closing of eyes and Johanna singing a song about The Sandman, then people started "dreaming". A lot of the players generated entire dreamlike narratives where everyday objects were viewed as something else. Personally I played it more as a deriv\xe9, something in this style: http://grant-morrison.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=195&Itemid=83 (see Exercise 1 and 2 under \u201cmagical consciousness\u201d).

    We moved amongst the other con goers, marked with yellow bands around our arms to indicate we were playing. The illusion was very fragile, it was very easy to go \u201coff-game\u201d in your head. But I enjoyed it quite a lot, especially a few interactions with non-players and fellow players were cool.

    Matthijs might add some more details, as he also participated.

    Jeep in the forest
    A group of jeepers created two larps-within-a-larp. They had a group of fanatical Christians at a Viking larp in Denmark. They were a bit separate from the other players, and had some pretty hardcore play amongst themselves. Then they had sort of a third larp where they moved away from the main larp altogether every couple of days. In this larp they played the characters (more or less) from the Viking larp, but in a modern day setting (a traditional Swedish celebration of Midsummer\u2019s Eve). They had different rules covering how to act within the different settings, for instance \u201cevery kindness in one setting has to be repaid by unkindness in the other and vice versa\u201d. Again, Matthijs\u2019 might want to provide further details. I\u2019m unsure whether the larp(s) is documented online. Try to ask Tobias Wrigstad for further details (he was part of the group). Maybe something will turn up on http://jeepen.org
