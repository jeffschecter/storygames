The Average row I want to make each answer as equal as possible. Below the Average row I want more weight toward "Yes, and" but still be equally distributed as much as possible. Above the Average row I want more weight toward "No, and" but still be equally distributed as much as possible. In all cases I want the slimmest chance of "Yes, but" and "No, but" because no matter how impossible a task is there is always a chance of success and no matter how sure something is to succeed there is the slightest chance of failure.

Please, look it over and let me know what needs changing. I want to keep the 1D10 option as it works well with Mythic and for a lot of supplements I use.

    \n Sure.

    By the way, you are using 1d100 (not 2d10, that would be adding the two dice).

    Personally, I prefer a different distribution. I feel, before rolling, there should really be a chance of succeeding with "Yes". If the only option would be "Yes but..." as GM (or GM Emulator) I would say: "no, you can't succeed in what you want like this, you have to make concessions.". This is a bit of a personal design choice.

    Personally, I find 1d10 more elegant.
    If you want to use 1d100, you might as well stick to the Mythic GME table. Just use the original table (with exceptional Yes/No) or modify it like below:

    i.e. 50/50 with Chaos Rank 5 has the following distribution: 10 - 50 - 91
    So you already have:
    1-10: Yes and...
    91-100: No and...
    Use the top/bottom percentage (+10%, -10%) in the middle range (here at 50%) and you have your distribution:
    11-40: Yes (50-10=40)
    40-50: Yes but..
    51-60: No but... (50+10=60)
    61-90: No

    Another example (Very Likely at Chaos 5): 16 - 85 - 97
    Here the top percentage is 16% and the bottom percentage is 4% (97 to 100, incl. 100):
    1-16: Yes and...
    17-69: Yes (85-16= 69)
    70-85: Yes but...
    86-89: No but... (85+4=89)
    89-96: No
    97-100: No and...

    You might calculate this on the fly or prepare it in advance for the whole Mythic GME table.
    Or just use Chaos Factor 5 and use it as a basis for your own table.

    I would call a positive reaction to near sure thing, s.th. like "Oh Sure", "Casual Affirmation" or "As a matter of course".
