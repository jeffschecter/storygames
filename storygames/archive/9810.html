    Posted By: Eero TuovinenTell us more about the IceKey, will you? I'm interested in keyboards, the keyboard being my primary tool in almost everything I do. I've been considering switching from whatever happens to be under my fingers to some sort of fancy board, such asDas Keyboardor some other weird luxury item. Could try out Dvorak while I'm at it, too.
    \n

    The IceKey keyboard is nothing more than a keyboard purposed originally for the Mac. I became interested in it because a few years back I was suffering a little from tendonitis, and I definitely noticed that some keyboards had a lighter touch than others: The throwaway keybaords that came with work PCs required a lot more force on the keys than other models. So I asked around on various forums and at work, got about 5 recommendations, and had a cookoff: IceKey won. Great "light touch" keyboard. Allows me to type faster with less chance of tendonitis-style stuff.

    Note that the new default Mac keyboard (from the last 1-2 years) is also really slick, and super-light resistance, but there's something about it that didn't quite work for me. I think it was that the keys were a tiny bit too cramped together for me. But other than that, it might be worth a shot; and yeah, it totally works with PCs/Linux/etc.

    I tried Dvorak about 10 years ago when I heard about them. Even learned to type Dvorak. But in the end, there's two factors:
    1) Even after using nothing but Dvorak for about 3 weeks, I couldn't get up to my regular speed.
    2) Dvorak is also built upon the assumption that you're gonna camp on the cramped "home row". So it, too is kinda flawed. I'd love to see a Dvorak-QUERTY hybrid that put the important keys towards the center of the keyboard and spiraled out from there, rather than the home row.

    Also, as an aside, if you're into ergonomics, and suffer from tendonitis, there's no better mouse on this earth than the kinda pricey (but worth every cent) Quill Mouse aka Aero2Bic Mouse. Lame name, awesome product.


    I never got carpal, but I got really bad tendonitis in my mouse hand (combination of too much gaming at night and too much PC work during the day). It got so bad I was actually considering getting out of PC work. But this mouse cured it in like 2 days of use.

    It was pricey (about $100 US), so I held a cookoff, testing about 4 various Ergonomic mice I found on various areas of the internet, including upright mice, joystick-looking mice, trackballs, etc but ended up settling on the Quill/Aerobic Mouse and returning the rest. The only thing that it's hard with is very fine mouse manipulation. Like, when I'm playing FPSes or doing fine art work in Photoshop, I tend to flip back to a regular mouse for an hour or two. But at work (10 hour days in front of PC), I have no problem with it, and it was a lifesaver.

    Anyway, just wanted to throw that out there. I'm really into ergonomics, especially in the modern office.

    -Andy

      In some ways the lack of a number pad is actually a feature.
