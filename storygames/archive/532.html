Designing openly: Should I?

rumble
April 2006 edited April 2006 in Story Games
I'm not an indie designer. I never have been. I don't think I want to be.
I usually play my cards pretty close to the chest.
I only accidentally got published, and my publisher wants to invest in my next design, which is as-yet incomplete.

I expect to have it done before the end of the year.

As with Dead of Night, I'm pretty skeptical that I'm creating anything ground-breaking. It's just my approach to the elements of the game that permit a little innovation to shine through.

However, to drum up interest before it's released, I'm wondering whether it's actually worth revealing the design process. Would it be better to do this in a blog, on the company site, or in a public forum such as this? Does it really cultivate a fan base, or does it just spill your ideas out without return?

    I don't mind listening, but I don't want anyone thinking I'm stealing their ideas, any more than I want anyone running off and publishing a game based on my ideas before I'm good and ready to publish.

    I've had this presented to me as a "risk" before and I really can't disagree more. First of all, you can't stop other people from using your game mechanics or using a very similar setting to yours; another person can all but replicate your entire game and not break any laws. The thing of it is, they're really not going to siphon off a lot of sales, anyway. Game design is such a low-margin and low-volume endeavor that it's not worth the trouble of plaigarizing.

    If your vision is already strong and clear, there's no harm in designing in public. It's probably a good idea, as it will get attention.

    But if what you have right now is mainly a cool idea, and you want feedback on the idea - you run a very real risk of getting a [i]lot[/i] of other, sometimes better ideas from other people, enough to get you so confused about what ideas to use that you can end up doing nothing instead.

    It's happened to me, and I've seen it happen.
