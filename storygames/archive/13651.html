    \n Magfest is a video game, arcade, console, music, nerd convention that costs 45 dollars, or less with group discount. It occurs every year in January, in Alexandria, VA. It's always a TON of fun and I've always had a great time. They have tons of table top game tournaments, movies, a LAN room, live music, panels, conferences with people such as John St Jon (Duke Nukem). I ran into Pete just by wandering around. He was sitting there doing nothing and had cards for Danger Patrol sitting on a table, I asked about it, and he got EXTREMELY excited. He told me all about it, so we gathered some other people and started playing. I was a two-fisted warrior named BroManDudeBoy, this is all besides the point. MAGfest is amazing and very very fun. I found out this year that their limit is 3,000 people, so you should register as soon as you can, I'd recommend registering a few months before January 2012, just to secure your spot.
    \n
    \nIf you would like to see/learn more, you can just go here, www.magfest.org

    \n I'm the Pete that Molly's talking about. I ran two back-to-back sessions of Danger Patrol (over a course of, I believe, 6 straight hours) for two groups of 11 different people total.

    When I finished, I explained, broadly, the concept of "story games," and directed interested people to this community, to learn more and so forth. Aside from that, Molly was way into Danger Patrol, and I know John Harper is on these forums. I figured the best way to get really into a game is to talk to the developer himself!

    Hey Molly. Glad you enjoyed the games and tracked down these forums. It's a cool place.

    On the topic of MAGfest, I think it's a good venue for indie developers to introduce their stuff to people. I would say the con is about 80% video game focused, and the tabletop presence is largely board games and D&D. However, every single person I encountered in the tabletop area was also ravenous for new games. There is also the huge advantage of the con being 24 hours, so you don't have to worry about fighting over table space or fitting into rigid time blocks. Just get some people together, find some space, and play!

    I highly recommend a strong story game presence at this con. You'll find some new players. Also, it's fun!

    EDIT: Also, if you're interested in pushing your games out to a convention crowd, my friends Rym and Scott of Geeknights "fame" do several panels about story games and RPG's that are beyond D&D and the usual suspects. Give 'em a shout out and they might help you plug your game.
