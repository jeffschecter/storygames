\n So we're playing D&D (3.5/PF mashup, taking all the best bits), and I'd been recently reading Play Unsafe. I suddenly realized why I had only rarely found that spark of awesome while gaming, and it was because I wasn't making myself vulnerable. My character in our online game is Drak, a Duergar Barbarian/Warblade, and is superficially modeled after Riddick from Pitch Black, but despite all of his badass abilities, I wasn't feeling super-stoked about him until recently. I now understand fully why that is.

Being badass is not about having laser eyes and rocket-punching capability (sorry, Voltron). Being badass is about making the right choice when it's someone's ass on the line. Being badass is about coming through in the clutch. And in order to do all that, your character has to actually give a fuck about something. See, we like to do backstories and all, which is good fun. However, 99% of the time, none of these contain any weaknesses.

Our group is in one of those seedy towns full of thieves and ne'er-do-wells - a classic hive of scum and villainy. In this city, which is near a large swamp, the Swamp Elves are traded as slaves. In the course of the adventure, our NPC friend "bought" one such slave (she is completely free to do as she pleases, but he has kept the deed of ownership in order to avoid potential issues within the city), because we need a guide and she is some sort of divine spellcaster as well (Spirit Shaman?). The DM described her as similar to Tia Dalma from the Pirates of the Carribean films. Almost immediately, I thought that she would be a good matchup for Riddick. I mean, WWVDD, amirite? So I decided he would fall in love with her.

So fast forward to the last couple of days in this game, and we are being stopped at a fort along the river while heading into the swamp (on a quest for milady, actually). They tell us we're good to go, but our Swamp Elf needs to be...questioned for an hour or two. So now I'm like "Crap, I hope this doesn't ruin anyone else's fun (you know, by getting them killed or anything), but these fuckers are NOT taking my girlfriend 'out for a spin.'"

\nSorry, because probably all (and certainly most) of you have come to this realization long before I found it and picked it up, dusted it off and wrote a post about it. The crux of it is this: incorporate clear vulnerabilities into your characters. Make them things you'd really care about. And then don't cry foul when the GM fucks with them. Instead, use that fuckery as your opportunity to show how badass you (and by extension, your character) really are.
