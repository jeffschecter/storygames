\n So, the other day I bought Mouse Guard: Fall 1152, and it turned out to be one of the best comics I've ever read. Today I was at Barnes & Noble and wound up picking up the first volume of Bizenghast, and I'm digging it too to far. That got me thinking: What "indie" (or at least not totally mainstream) comics might make for good story games?

I can only think of one off the top of my head right now:

Dramacon, by Svetlana Chmakova, is about a girl named Christine who goes to her first anime con and develops a crush on a guy there, and it then proceeds to live up to its name. I don't know that it would be a game for me, but I could totally see a game about trying to make a connection with someone (while overcoming baggage) who you only see at a yearly convention.

    \nIron Empires, of course...

    Vertigo isn't very "indie" by some definitions, but I am (slowly) working on a game that counts DMZ as a significant influence.

    Girl Genius supposedly has an RPG in the works, although it's been several years now, and it's never been anything more than "in the works." You could probably do a pretty good job of it with Spirit of the Century, or another steampunk/pulp game.

    The Homeless Channel would be an interesting setting for Primetime Adventures.
