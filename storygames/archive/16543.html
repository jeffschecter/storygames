\n I played Monsterhearts for the first time yesterday and it was a cool game session. It got me thinking - AW is so hackable, has anybody used it for superheroes?
\n
\nSomething like the playbooks would be types - like 'Gadgeteer', 'Brick', 'Brawler', 'Side-kick', 'Blaster', 'Villain' etc. The moves would be centred around an aspect of their power type.
\n
\nBefore I go and reinvent the wheel - has anyone done any work on this?

    \n Along the same lines, even if it wasn't a mutant hack (Mutanthearts, LOL, love it) I wouldn't define it by their powers (anyone can have any power in comics), but instead by their origin or personal issue (normally the same thing). Superheroes are the embodiment of their personal issues. So you have the Lost Alien, the Mastermind, the Self-Made Man, the Accidental Hero, the Hated Outcast, etc.

    \nJDCorely: Along those lines, I've long thought the thematic Concepts from Darkpages would make excellent playbooks for a dark Apocalypse World supers-hack. Cribbed from the GenCon sketchbook:\n
      \n
    • The Adept: Those who esoteric knowledge to shape the world around them. (Dr. Fate, Dr. Strange, Mr. Fantastic, Iron Man)
      • \n
    • The Beast: Those who represent the duality of man; savage instinct vs calculating reason. (Beast, Wolverine, Hulk)
      • \n
    • The Construct: Artificial beings struggling with their identity and humanity. (Vision, Frankenstein, Human Torch I)
      • \n
    • The Elemental: Those connected to and master of a primal force. (Storm, Human Torch II, Static)
      • \n
    • The Freak: Those outcast, exiled and misunderstood for their gifts. (any mutant really)
      • \n
    • The Ghost: Lost souls caught between the worlds of life and death. (The Spectre, Lady Death)
      • \n
    • The Outsider: Those from another place, civilization, time... (Superman, Martian Manhunter, Captain America, Namor)
      • \n
    • The Vampire: Those with hunger to feed on others. (Blade)
      • \n
    • The Vigilante: Those that walk the line between justice and vengeance. (Batman, Daredevil, Punisher)
