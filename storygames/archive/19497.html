    \n\nJune 2014 \n

    \n
    \n The main technique I use with Primetime Adventures is to explicitly say that player performance and creativity matters and push people to do it better. I do this with jokes, a lot of the time "My god, we are getting killed in the ratings, I sure hope this next scene is a fuckin corker. It's your turn." or "Zombies?! That's the worst idea I've ever heard. What if, instead, we did a show with........... (leans back in chair, looks at ceiling) ............zombies. You all run with that, I need to grab some more cocaine, I mean coffee."

    The main problem that PTA tends to run into is that people aren't normally eager or willing to say "no, let's do this instead" or "I don't like this that much", and the fanmail mechanic is explicitly about positive feedback only (this is not a flaw, see Facebook and Twitter), so if people aren't really thrilled with someone else's contribution it leads to a mediocre experience even for the contributor who might have really loved their own idea.
