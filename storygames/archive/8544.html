\n The dance group Groove Armada are selling their new album using a rather interesting sharing technique. You get the first track free. If you share that with 20 friends (or, rather, if the track propagates 20 times through the website), you get the second. And so on.

We don't make much use of things like this. I've seen John Wick selling very cheap PDFs, to stimulate play, and presumably people buying the full book. There's also ransom models.But, given the range of techniques available, we're using very few of them.

What techniques, like this, could we use to sell games? What techniques have you seen used elsewhere, to sell things which aren't games, that we could steal?

No essays, please, but short, thoughtful posts very welcome.

Graham

    \n 1. Craft a game line with some interesting supplements.
    2. Sell the main book with tracking PGP keys.
    3. Whenever the buyer posts an actual play signed with the PGP key, give him a free (or a significant discount to purchase a) PDF supplement and give all the players a discount (to purchase the main book).
    4. Rinse and repeat.
    5. Profit and Fame.
