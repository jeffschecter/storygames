    It's good to know people at least care about this.

    This seems to be a dispute between folks who don't want any restrictions on the re-use of their materials at all and those that want a clear and binding agreement. The most liberal of the Creative Commons licenses set clear, sensible guidelines for use and re-use and I support that in principle. One nice thing is that you can always allow an individual to do more than the blanket license allows, so if Nathan wants to use my game as toilet paper and sell it, all he has to do is ask and I will give him three thumbs up.

    But ultimately the IP guidelines Kevin chooses aren't going to make much difference for entries in the Gelatinous Cube Challenge. It's a tempest in a teapot. I'll participate no matter what and I'm not worried about it at all.

    The lesson here, as far as I'm concerned, is that if you want to have a contest or encourage people to contribute some stuff to some thing, just spell out from the beginning what status their work will be accorded. If that's "let your freak flag fly", awesome. If it's "I'll own it and say I wrote it", that's awesome too. Then people can choose to participate or not without acrimony.
