    \n For me...

    Write it like a story, like you were telling me down the pub. Say "Our group got together, and this is who we were; and we did this, and it was fun; and then there was this die roll, and that was weird; and we didn't know what to do about this".

    I'd want to know about the group and the game fiction and the mechanics and how all those three interrelated.

    \n Well for me (and my site) it's you guys. I'd like to involve a number of people and actually start getting some traffic (truth be known).

    I'd like to illicit three reactions:
    1. Wow, I learned something about that game and Story Games in general.
    2. Wow, I'd like you guys to playtest my game to offer me input and thoughtful recource.
    3. My experience with this game was similar/different in some intriguing way so let's talk about it.

    And if someone can follow any one of these up with "hey that was a really cool story" then it's a plus.
    - Don
