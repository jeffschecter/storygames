So, in (dis)honor of Palladium I want to play Rifts.

But I can't. I just can't do it. I have a copy of the Ninth Printing of the original MRB sitting here, and even the thought of playing this game from the book makes my soul squirm in the same uncomfortable way that looking at detailed pictures of nasty anthropoids does. The kitchen sink of system and setting, the ludicrous terms, the total combat focusÃ¢â‚¬Â¦ they just don't work for my anymore.

And yet there is nostalgia And there is the fact that giant robots fighting dragons fighting insects from outerspace fighting psionic warriors driven insane by the mad-science implants put into their brains is cool. And there is the lingering idea that RIFTS is bad because of what was done to it, poor abused child, and not because of its essential nature.

So I am thinking of doing what I can, and playing NOT RIFTS. NOT RIFTS is a game that takes cogs from the broken body of the clattering RIFTS mecha and uses them to rebuild something faster, leaner, and sharper with a new and working engine. The versions of NOT RIFTS I am currently thinking of playing include:

Big Rifts, Small Mouth: A group of inexplicably powerful and cute but alienated teens from a Coalition town are brought together by an invasion of insects from another dimension! They decide to use their gifts with cybernetics and transforming magic to fight back and keep their community alive. Can they stop the bug-menace while dealing with their own angst, coming of age, and endless sexual and romantic tension?

Prime Time Rifters: A man driven mad by the power of his bionic-psionic implants, a woman sworn to the defense of the true and right at the cost of her own identity, a rogue scholar who has in her head and breaking-down digital library the knowing of things forbidden, and the unutterably charming mystic and hypnotist who never cared about anything until it was too late: together they are the R-Team, the last hope of the dispossessed and disenfranchised, who fight against the Coalition and their own pasts!

Sorcerifts: We live in the age of garbage, the ragged refuse of remainders of ages more glorious. Each of is dying by inches, faced with the horror of finding our place in a dying world. There is the Juicer, warrior without peer, who had a heart attack this morning and is now realizing that his five years are up and his implants are killing him. The Glitterboy, last scion of a dead legion, whose armor is all that is left of his life, but the price of whose ownership is destroying him. The Shifter who ruled his commune as a God-King, until he broke his taboo and is now hunted by the demon prince who once made him a power among men. And the Coalition General who has just been given proof that his own pogrom was responsible for murdering his own daughter. But can they walk away from their power and pride when the past, which will not die, comes roaring back to destroy what they care most about?

Burning Rifts: Welcome to the brutal world of the frontier, out past the places where Juicers and Deadboys rule the world. Here a man's worth is in his steel, a woman's value in her wits. The Vampire Kingdoms have taken over now, and town after town has fallen into the stupor of their Tenebrous Dream, a haze state that reduces these once proud people to a state of thralldom. But you've woken up, or maybe were lucky enough never to have been taken, and now you have to figure out what you believe, and what price you are willing to pay for it. (With rules from Under a Serpent Sun and Jihad, of course.)

So, any other ideas for NOT RIFTS?
