    \n The Hunterian Museum, 35 Lincoln's Inn Fields. Little known and spectacular. Where else can you see the sawn-off face of a smallpox victim? A giant's skeleton? Conjoined twins in jars? Ok, Philadephia, but how about a rooster with a human tooth growing out of its head? Seriously, the Hunterian is really fun.

    \n The Wellcome Foundation has something called Skeletons on the south end of Camden. And if you're going to the Hunterian you might as well have a look at the John Soanes on the other side of the square and the British Museum only 10 minutes walk away.

    Camden has a decentish market and the Devonshire Arms, a goth place if that's your kind of thing.

    Gaming in Clapham on Wednesday, that's pretty geeky. Our host's wife is a nutrionist who has written a cook book (but not a veggie one).

    A day trip to Scotland is crazy talk. It's about an 8 hour drive to Edinburgh. You can fly up getting the 8.45 from Stanstead (1 hour out of London) arrive at 9.55 in Edinburgh, plus another hour to get into town. You'd get the 21.15 back from Edinburgh and arrive in London at 22.30 (plus another hour on the train). That would set you back about \xa3260 for two plus transfer at either end, another \xa340 or so. So for $600 you could have about 9 hours in Scotland, mug Gregor for a copy of 3:16 and do a bit of sightseeing.

    You can easily do Stonehenge in a day. We bought a combined rail/bus ticket which got us to Salisbury and the bus to the Stones. Winchester is about the same distance. Bath is only two hours by train.

    Brighton is a nice day trip to the sea with it's funny Lanes, the Pavilion and another groovy little musuem of stuff. There's a nudist beach but in October it's likely to be a bit chilly.

    \n For edible vegetarian food, go to Indian restaurants. Indian restaurants are generally good and generally do good vegetable curries.

    A day trip to Scotland is indeed crazy talk, but if you stop overnight, it's quite doable. A cheap hotel for a night should be fairly...um...cheap.

    Oh, yes, John's right, we've got Concrete Cow on the 27th, which is a lovely little convention. It's in Milton Keynes, a short train ride north of London. Do come if you can. Really, I recommend it, definitely worth a day of your holiday. (If you do want to go, I can go with you on the train and show how to get there.)

    Go up to Leisure Games in Finchley. From Camden, it's a few stops up the Northern Line.

    Windsor is a day trip and also near me, in Berkshire.

    Come and play games with us one Wednesday. Or both Wednesdays. I'll probably make you play A Taste For Murder.

    \n I set myself the task of limiting recommendations to places within comfortable walking distance of Camden station. Here you go:

    Drummond Street is the place to go for veggie indian meals. 8/10 of the restaurants on the strip are veggie, and it's dead cheap. 1.0 miles
    Mega City Comics ~200metres. Used to be my local many moons ago. Fair selection, friendly atmosphere, cool clientele.
    If it's not raining, walk down camden canal. There is a nice pub about 15 minutes up west, towards...
    Regents park, further down the canal. Enjoy a royal park!
    1.1 miles - Hampstead Comedy Club plays on Saturdays in the Washington, a nice pub in belsize park
    Primrose Hill is great for views, and no distance away. While you're there, enjoy Manna, one of the best vegetarian restaurants I've been to. Pricier than Eat and Two Veg, but better, I think.

    Further afield....

    One of my friends acts in the London Dungeon. You know, running around as a bloodied strumpet. She says it's good fun. I might be able to get reduced price/ no queue tickets (it's very popular)
    Do a London walking tour, especially one around the city (financial district) on a weekend. Or just wander around yourself.
