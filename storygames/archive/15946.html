\n Hey guys, Andy K here.

So, I often see "I lost my post!/S-G ate my post!/etc" here. Unfortunately, this site doesn't have the ajax scripting that will save your posts for you (and some of that won't work if your browser crashes anyway). Everyone should know that if you post after a pause the site says something about authentication, but all your post info is saved right there so you just need to click "submit" again and it's fine (if you don't see your post info and it's asking you to log in from scratch again, it's because you don't have "Stay Logged In" checked; thanks Dave B for pointing that out). Another thing that may happen, if you have a connection problem and the page goes blank after submitting, all you need to do is hit Reload/F5/Command-R and it will attempt to resubmit your post.

In my case, my biggest reason for losing posts is that I've got this otherwise great 5-button Microsoft mouse, but if my thumb grazes the thumb button, it will automatically go Back a page (pinky button goes Forward a page, but that's not so easy to accidentally press), and nothing recovers that post data. Much swearing ensues.

AAAAAAANYWAY: Okay, having gotten that out of the way, I've been playing around with a solution that works for All The Above Cases. And I recommend it enough to make it an "Official Recommendation for Y'all(tm)": LAZARUS. Works on Google Chrome, Firefox, Safari. Here's the link:

http://lazarus.interclue.com/download
(you can download it directly from the official Google Chrome extensions page as well)

Basically, as you type in the "comments" field you'll see a little grey ankh in the top right of the form. From that point on, even if your browser crashes, you accidentally click "Back", you accidentally close your browser, etc, you can still bring back your post info.

-------

In fact, see that line right there? I typed in all the text above the line, and then I:
* Hit back button, then forward button (data populated again)
* Closed this window with Ctrl-W, reopened, started a new thread (clicked on the ankh and the data was there, and populated all fields again for me)
* Killed all Chrome processes, reopened chrome, did the steps immediately above
...and the data returned. No problems.

So yeah, if you've ever lost your post data here or on other forums, I highly recommend giving this free and light extension a try on all computers you normally post on. It already saved my butt in the five days or so I've been using it.

Thanks!
-Andy

ps: I am not affiliated with the developers of Lazarus in any way, other than in the fact that I want to kiss them on the mouth.
