    \n I was in a playtest of the game run at Go Play NW. The session had its issues in terms of the structure & mechanics, but I found the character generation to be extremely compelling in terms of creating characters who were both completely constrained by some very clear genre expectations and were nonetheless fully three-dimensional.

    I feel like the cover gives a very 'rah rah punk smash' take on the characters (reinforced by the blurb text) that doesn't quite get across the fact that you are playing vulnerable, emotionally interdependant teenagers who have more going on than just anger. I think that Bret's comment further up-thread is illustrative -- the cover suggested to him he should have been playing more aggressive, more angry. It's not that I'm saying 'hey your game is actually really mellow', but I guess the one-note-smash-the-state thing feels like it's falling short in terms of promoting what I, personally, liked in the game. That said, I think what I got out of the game may not be its greatest strength or its real focus -- and this is a cover we are talking about here, after all. So grains of salt all around, I just wish there was a bit more there that would get across to the potential buyer that this is not a game where you just play cardboard cut-outs in an excuse to fantasize about breaking shit. Right now the cover gives me the impression this is a game where the characters are stereotypical angry punks.

    \nGot it.

    So here's the thing: I think that the game text and the rules will push the kind of play you prefer and that the art and cover and layout won't interfere with that. In point of fact, while you could play the game in pure-smash mode, I think that the aesthetic is actually a bit of a useful/helpful/fun misdirect (that's the wrong word but hopefully context will make what I mean clear). Come for the kicking dudes in the face, stay for fucking over your friends and betraying your ideals in order to save the day, as it were.

    In short, I get your concern. I suspect it won't be a problem, but I'll remain alive to it. If the APs that come in (please, O spaghetti monster, let there be APs coming in) are all of the "we fucked shit up and had no introspection" variety, then I will know that maybe I should sell some of the other elements of the game more strongly than I am, either in the art or the text. So, thank you. Sincerely.
