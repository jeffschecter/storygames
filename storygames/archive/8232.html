\n
\nWhat did Celanawe won?
\n
\nIn some hippy game...Celanawe's player would have just established that his opponents were indeed the theives who stole his axe...but this is a BW design so eff that choice straight away.
\n

\n
\nBut that seems rather disatisfying since game text examples should be understood to represent examples of GOOD play unless otherwise stated.
\n

\nThoughts?

Its possible for a GM to narrate a player's failure in a way that violates any number of things, ruins any number of things, etc, etc, etc. That only works in so much as the GM isn't ruling from context, and isn't listening to what people at the table are saying to him.
