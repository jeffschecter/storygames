    \n I once played in a game of Fudge where our characters had to switch genres every 15-30 minutes, so we started out having a McGuffin in some noir universe, then suddenly found ourselves in capes having to save Metro City from the villains after the McGuffin, then we had to sing (badly) about wanting the McGuffin, then we were piloting a starship towards it, and so forth.

    I remember the GM making the potentially rocky transitions somewhat plausible and smooth, and the constant quest for the same object lent our characters some continuity. It would be an experiment I'd love to try again.

    Also on another note: trying to simulate Tout va bien (1972) or any of Godard's Dziga Vertov era work via a role-playing game seems also like a game design challenge. But having an interplay of ideas with characters shifting into the picture every now and then would make for a fun, stimulating evening as long as a solid social contract underlying the game rules was in play.
