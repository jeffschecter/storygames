    \nDragons at Dawn is Dan Boggs' researched effort to reconstruct rules for how Dave Arneson was running the game that became Dungeons & Dragons before Dave ever taught it to Gary Gygax.

    It's based on Dave's publications, articles, interviews, and forum posts.

    It's pretty cool to see the influence of Gary Gygax laid so bare, in lots of tiny ways, by the rules that differ from the way D&D has always been. It's not an utterly perfect text, but it's pretty good for something that aims to be an early-70s RPG, and after some important fixes in the weeks after its release the text seems pretty well stabilized now.

    Paul
