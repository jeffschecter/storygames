\n So for the first session of my Wednesday group's "NIGHT WATCH: Seattle" game (using With Great Power), I needed to create a handful of background NPCs to give the setting some teeth, and then just let the players come up with the rest from there.

I basically went from one end to the other, thinking that this was going to be kind of an ecclectic, multicultural set of names for most of the Others in the background. I drew on American, French, Italian, Czech, Chechneyan, Sengalese, North Indian, and Russain. There was only one character who I didn't pick straight out of the names list, actually.

The cool thing was, since I was going through and drawing names out of a hat, and looking for cool ones, I ended up including lots of inspiring POC, female etc characters without even trying to multiculture-it-up. That side-effect rocked.

Here was the names that I ended up using:

NIGHT WATCH
Boss of the Seattle Night Watch: Mark Murdock aka Mukhammad
Trainer/Light Other-Incubus: Dominic Kucera
Teenage Seer: Nicole Parsh

DAY WATCH
Boss of the Seattle Day Watch: Celine Pichette
High Vampire City Councilman: Alexander Robertson
?? Viviana Falcone (sister)
?? Paulo Falcone (brother)

DARK OTHER
Shape Changing Combat Mage/Insect Colony: Angela Oakes

PEOPLE
Federal Agent Will Dansky
Federal Agent Sara Castro

THE INQUISITION
Light Other/Healer Inquisitor: Edison Huang

EXTRA
Diner: Annie Starr's Wharf-Side Diner (contains: Good Pie)

THE NAME I DIDN'T PULL FROM THE BOOK:
Grace Park, ex-girlfriend and love interest of one of the PCs.

Anyway, the cool thing was as I was going across the names, I was getting inspirations as to where to put them on the relationship map. When I came across Celine Pichette, that screamed to me "Evil Ex-Canadian Leader of the Day Watch". When I stumbled across Mukhammad, I knew that he would be (like Geser/Boris Ignovitch from the Night Watch books) The Boss of the Night Watch, with an old name from old Africa which few people know, and a modern name that he uses to fit into human society.

So... success? Success! It was valuable to my game within a week of buying it.

The actual game rocked too, but that's another story.

-Andy
