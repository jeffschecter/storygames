\n My socks: they have been rocked. Now where can I get more?
\n
\nStory games, indie games, whatever you want to call them. Games focused on the narrative recently burst into my life and completely blew me away. I've played all of one entire session of Eric J. Boyd's "Committee for the Exploration of Mysteries" and I know I'm hooked. Not that the dozen or so RPG podcasts in my iTunes were a dead giveaway or anything.
\n
\nSo here's where it gets less exciting: I haven't found many local players, let alone experienced players. I have the few people that I played Committee with and while none of us are lacking in enthusiasm, we are all lacking in experience. Specifically, we're lacking in narrative skills like scene framing and such. So, we're looking to get out there and meet some people and learn some of your story-fu as well as see what some of these other games are like.
\n
\nI've hit up the FLGS and unfortunately, I get blank stares when I mention anything other than D&D, Warhammer and Vampire.
\n
\nSo, I'm looking to you good folks to point us in the right direction of cons, meetups and the like to check out. On the list so far are DragonCon (because we have friends in Atlanta) and Dexcon 11(because it's close by and coming up soon).
\n
\nIs there anything to look specifically for at either of these cons? Are there any others in the New York/Philadelphia/DC strip that I should look at attending?
\n
\nLastly, as the complete con-noob that I am I'm wondering how one goes about signing up for games at these sorts of things.
\n
\nAs for what games we're looking for, I'm down for anything from the crunchier, more traditional games to the most out there, let's-tell-a-story and explore concepts hippie stuff as long as there's a focus on supporting creation of the story and the characters.
\n
\nThanks!
\n- Kevin

    \n Hey Kevin, Dexcon and Dreamation are chocolate-covered unicorns of quality gaming. If you are interested in this kind of game and you live on the eastern seaboard, you should try to get to these cons. You can sign up for games in advance after you register, and there is a ton of pick-up gaming going on as well. There are also active groups of players in New York city, Philadelphia, and other places you mentioned. Hopefully they will pop up to provide some local info for you. In addition, a lot of people are running and playing games on-line, via chat or virtual tabletop, so that's also an option.

    To speak to your larger implied question of skill, man, go play some games! Try Jared Sorenson's InSpectres, which is pretty hard to mess up. Try Dogs in the Vineyard, following the game's rules and advice. In my opinion, the nice thing about many of these games is that they are written with the assumption that you are actually going to follow the rules, and the rules therefore actually help you play better. They may be different from what you are used to, but you have all the skill you need - it isn't rocket science.

    \n Kevin, I just so happen to be a story game kinda guy and live in Philadelphia as well! If you like, you can get in touch with me at mposullivan1978 at gmail dot com. I'm always up for hanging out with some new people and maybe getting in some gaming.

    Philadelphia actually does have a pretty stellar gaming scene, from Don Corcoran just about being one of the nicest guys on the planet, to the biiiig group of players in the West Philly scene, and with all kinds of stops in between. I've never in my life found a better town for gaming.

    Also, if you're a convention guy, you cannot go wrong with the Double Exposure events. Dreamation and Dexcon are great conventions that really center on sitting down and playing games. You won't find much in the way of big booths and industry types trying to get their product down your throat. You'll also find that the Double Exposure events are something akin to the unofficial home of the indie gaming scene. Tons of designers will run their first open playtests of their games there. Honestly, they're a lot like a LARP version of the story-games forums. I've made some great friends because of those events.

    Oh, by the way, welcome to the forums, man!
