    \n I've gone from 4 hr being standard to a 3hr, tops, session length. As in, 3 hours (3.5 at a push) now feels optimal to me. I keep thinking I can push it a little further, and that if we could get meaningful sessions out of 2 hours or less I might get more gaming in (2 hrs on a weeknight is much easier for me to cope with after a day at work)... and we might even get more interest in RPGs (if they're the length of a movie, say).

    While I've zipped through, say, 3-player Fiasco games in just over an hour, I think there must be some emergent best practice (beyond aggressive scene framing and PACE at the forefront of the GMs mind) that'll lead to these shorter sessions being possible. Rules complexity needn't be a problem - I ran Burning Wheel last year at a con with only 1 of the 5 players having ever experienced it before and fit into a 3-hour slot. When I playtested the scenario with my home group (admittedly, with only 2 players) we were done in under 2 hours - I actually had to pad it out and add an encounter to make it fit the con session length.

    Having people show up on time or early is key. I mentally add about a half hour to our start times to make up for that. So, far it's worked out. One of my players is always exactly ten minutes early and the others tend to drift in within the half hour and leave within the half hour after play.

    I find starting firmly in the afternoon helps. So, not at five or six but at two, say. This means you're neither booking them for the afternoon nor the evening, but rather 2-6. Mostly, we're students or work evenings so this seems like a good time for us.
