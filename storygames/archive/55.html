    When I was on my last train ride, seating was 'as per availablilty', meaning to say there were no assigned seats. You should just be able to grab a section or area or group of seats so you can play. In most overnight trains, (at least in my experience) there are sections in the coach cars where in the center, the seats faced each other and there was a table in the middle.

    You might want to contact Amtark to see if that is the sort of train you would be on. When I rode, I was on a single level train with that sort of seating until I got to Chicago. Then I was on a double decker train that had lounges on the lower levels for either just resting and/or smoking that had nice seats and plenty of tables. Also, the lounge car had nice swivel seats that had tables in the middle.

    But that was back in 1998 so I don't know if it is the same. When I looked at the Amtrak website it mentioned nothing about how the trains were laid out so you might need to contact a rep.
