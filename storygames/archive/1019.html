  • Adam_Dray
    August 2006
    I like the tennis analogy. If you go out to play tennis with someone, regardless of how seriously you're playing, you expect a few things. If you're playing a competitive game, you want him to really push you and make you run hard and earn your victory. If you're playing a friendly game, you want him to avoid those cutthroat behaviors and hit the ball into the middle of your court so you can get to it and return it; the fun is in sustainable back-and-forth action.

    At the very least, you want the other player to return the ball. If he keeps whacking it out of bounds and you spend all your time chasing it, the game is no fun. If you hit the ball over the net and he misses it every time, that's no fun either. You also hope he doesn't get pissed off and throw his racket at you.
