\n I'm about to start a SotC game set in India in the heyday of the British Empire, around 1840-ish. One aesthetic I'm adopting is keeping things historically plausible, so we're not having Gadgets and Artefacts. Most of the changes to SotC as written are minor, but Guns are quite different.
\n
\nIn the 1840s, all guns are single-shot muzzle loaders*. That means that reload times for guns are significant. What's the best way to incorporate this in the game?
\n
\nMy first thought was to simply say characters have to spend an action or two reloading a gun. Does that sound fair? Does anyone have any other suggestions? On the one hand, I want to reflect that guns aren't as effective then as they are later; on the other hand, I don't want to make Guns a completely useless skill.
\n
\nOther changes needed: Guns can't be used for suppressive fire block actions, the Rain of Lead stunt doesn't exist, and Fast Reload needs changing, but I'm not sure how yet.
\n
\nThanks,
\n
\nNeil.
\n
\n* what revolvers there are don't seem to be reliable enough for serious use.

    \n Are you still making the player characters larger than life? Spirit of the Century is geared towards very powerful PCs who can pull off stunts that would be unbelievable in any "normal" setting - with stunts like Ricochet, Faster Than A Leopard, and Right Place, Right Time I wouldn't find it out of place for the characters to be able to reload a single shot between rounds as part of the combat round. Personally, I think I'd just leave it the way it is.

    However, if that still seems weird to you, then I think demiurgeastaroth is headed in the right direction. Maybe you need to make a reload maneuver to reload between shots, but the Fast Reload stunt allows you to avoid that and shoot every turn. In exchange, the guns do a little bit more damage, such as 1 or 2 additional stress on a successful hit to make them worthwhile to use, but to balance that maybe you have a -1 to your roll because they are inaccurate. If you do that, then you'll end up with what I personally envision as the way they were used then (and this could be totally wrong) - average characters use the guns for one good shot before dropping them and rushing in with swords and fists. That makes the Fast Reload stunt really powerful, so maybe chain it after a new stunt that negates the -1 penalty to Guns rolls.

    If you went this way then non-gun characters will use them on occasion but not really focus on them at all, but Gun skill characters will be able to pick up a bit more damage with one stunt and be able to reload without a maneuver with 2 stunts. Does that seem balanced to you?
