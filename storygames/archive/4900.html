\n Really, I swear I had some kind of dream about Red Box D&D last night. So I'm sitting around in the basement of the house I grew up in, with about eight pre-teen girls. I think they're supposed to be my sister's friends or something. For some reason, they are eager to learn how to play the role-playing games. I consider breaking out a quality indie game, but for some reason we're going to play red box Dungeons & Dragons. They show me the character sheets from when they tried to figure the chargen by themselves, but the stats are like in the 80 to 90 range, so it's clear they need some expert assistance. I declare we'll just roll up new characters.

But wait... I can find the Dungeon Master's book, but somebody has moved my Player's book, and I can't find it anywhere. How can we roll up characters without the rulebook?! A friend of mine is hanging around and suggests we make AD&D2 characters. No! I will do no such thing! Another friend offers me his rulebook. He'll pulled all those little booklets apart and bound them all in a big fat comb binding. But his Basic set is the fourth edition, and my stuff is for the third edition, and those are totally different!!! The dream ends with this crisis unresolved.

Fuck, my subconscious is weird.
