As the title says. Here's a couple thoughts I've thunk:

We should have a full IGE event schedule in pamphlet form available at the sales table, and hand them out to passerby and with sales. This would have a dual purpose: both to centralize all of the IGE events and make it easy to pick what you want to play, and to give links to product websites (for long-term sales). Maybe there could be a "Interested in this game? Check out X and Y as well!" feature in there. And a prominent mention of Jennifer Rodger's booth, because why not?

Also, and related: an organized-but-not-official playtest track. I don't think registering official 4-hour playtest sessions is really the best idea, given the amount of published games being run and everybody's limited time. But maybe we could create a series of unofficial 2-hour slots that people can plan for and commit too, which run alongside the official events. These could be on a "meet at the IPR booth and find somewhere to play" basis, and could be listed in the afore-mentioned "program" alongside the official game slots.

So, there's some ideas to talk about, without anyone having to commit to enacting them right now at this moment.
