\n So, we got Lady Blackbird on the front page of Boing Boing, and they've talked about Mouse Guard, My Life With Master, and Spirit of the Century before. Cool, right?

The comments on the post are pretty good too, with at least a little interest in "this, but with a different setting." Breenan already posted a comment with a link to a list of LB hacks and I have a similar post awaiting moderation.

Hans mentioned Freemarket, and how that should be right up the BB crowd's ally, and I agree. I actually submitted a link to it before (before their new socially reviewed submissions) but it didn't get posted. With the new submission system it might be worth another try, but we have to be careful not to abuse the site, or people will get fed up with RPG stories. Maybe when the game actually ships? Or GenCon?

I'd also be curious to see if we could get a post about gaming onto the site. I'm not sure how many people reading that article are actually going to play unless there's some thoughtful articles telling them why play is cool/useful/whatever.

I'm also curious to see how much traffic this actually generates. I'm pretty sure John has analytics for the LB site, would you mind sharing some general idea of how much traffic this generates for you? Anyone contact you through email?

For my part, I'm seeing a bit of a trickle down effect (my site is linked from the post and I have one of those LB hacks), but nothing that I can really identify yet (edit: I take that back, I've seen a around 10 referrals from BoingBoing, which is enough to be noticeable in my normal traffic, but not nearly as big as posting a link to something useful here). I already have slightly more visits than an average day, but I also posted something new last night, so it's hard to call the actual effect.

    \n I've also seen a small but beneficial effect. I'm getting about 3-4x normal traffic, about half of which is from Boing Boing, plus slightly higher than usual referrals from John's blog. Most of that seems to be going straight to my main page, which is most likely people clicking the link from the BB article. Traffic to my Old Mesilla page (which is linked from John's blog, which was linked from the comments) is completely average. Story games, as usual, produced a handful of hits.

    In comparison, when I first posted Old Mesilla and linked it from the Stuff to Watch thread, I got roughly 10x normal traffic, and some consistent follow through visitors.

    If I would have thought this through, I would have put something a lot more interesting on my front page today.

    Anyway, summary: the trickle down to other Hackbird games seems minimal, but people apparently find my name interesting enough to click on a link of it.
