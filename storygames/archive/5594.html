    \n I found a pair of dice exactly as you describe (pipped, blank to 5) in a jar at my FLGS, among hundreds of other dice. Therefore, my answer to your question is "by dumb luck". :-)

    Or, you could take a couple of white dice with black pips, a black marker, and connect the pips of the 'six' as to make a square, and look at it as a '0' (I remember a web page on how to make your own fudge dice from plain pipped d6es, connecting the pips into '+', '\u2013', and '0').
