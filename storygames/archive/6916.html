\n So, for ages now I've been thinking it would be neat to make cards to use in place of Fudge dice. It would be a relatively simple matter to make 81 cards representing the -4 to +4 spread that Fudge dice represent. (I found that printable perforated business cards worked well for making item cards for Maid RPG by the way).

But just putting the same probability spread on cards wouldn't really accomplish much. I want to come up with something more interesting to justify using the cards. The possibilities that occurred to me so far are:

* Symbols that can form combos for special effects.
* Markings for generating different numbers or other things with the cards.
* Some kind of meta-story thingy.

    \n One option for the idea of cards with a descriptor. Make two decks, draw one card from each. Now each minimal deck is 9 cards (ranging from -2 to +2). Try and divy the descriptors up such that you should always be able to combine them. Note that you will only ever have to combine a positive and negative descriptor for the marginal result range of -1 to +1. Now make each deck 36 cards, and now you have 16 combinations of descriptors for a +4 (and with 4 copies, you can even just draw off the deck and reshuffle say when only half the deck is left or something and not have the draws get too predictable (with a single sequence of 81 -4 to +4 cards, as soon as the +4 card is drawn, we know we won't draw another, and almost anyone can count the -3 and +3 cards).

    Hmm, with the two decks, you could even have some interesting strategy with a hand of say 6 cards (3 from each deck). Do you play your two +2s (one from each deck) for a single +4, or do you play them with one of your -1s to gain a couple marginal successes instead of one major success and one failure?

    \n Well fudge is an excellent game with the kind of attention to detail regarding failure you could only expect from a fan of Sergo Aragone's Groo.
    \n
    \nBut I don't recall seeing a FUDGE deck, yet, with hippy principals:
    \n
    \nThat is:
    \nrewarding failure with both pain and story control (or other benefits)
    \nencouraging players to set resoultions to their attempts at a task beyond success and failure*
    \n
    \n*Fudge has that neat scaling effect and a range of failure-to-success options already.
    \nSpeaking of Which:
    \n
    \n-4 (play this card on yourself to describe one long-reaching effect of your actions, but someone else gets to describe how you suffer as a result of the immediate)
    \n-3 (play this card on yourself to describe one lasting benefit you receive, but someone else gets to describe how you suffer as a result of this task)
    \n-2 (play this card on yourself to describe how your task comes out, but if it's a failure, you must describe your character's fumble)
    \n-1 (play this card on yourself to describe one immediate result of your task, but, if it's a failure, you must describe your character's fumble)
    \n0 (there are no benefits or penalties for playing this card on yourself)
    \n+1 (if you play this card on your character, you must describe a minor side effect of your actions, good or bad)
    \n+2 (if you play this card on your character, someone else gets to describe a minor side effect of your actions, good or bad)
    \n+3 (if you play this card on your character, pick someone else to describe an immediate major side effect of your actions, good or bad)
    \n+4 (if you play this card on your character, someone else gets to describe a lasting drawback you suffer as a side effect)
    \n
    \nNeeds some tweaking, as always, but I think it's a sound idea.
