\n Well, it appears I borked the lolcat thread. (A joke that needs to be explained is a bad joke. BAAD joke! No biscuit.)

In an attempt to make up for it, here's a silly little idea.

What about a game where the various snowclones and other catchphrases associated with the lolcat phenomenon were ritual phrases with mechanical weight? Think about it:

"I can has X?"
"I has a X."
"Someone stoled/eated/ my X."
"I'm in your X, Ying your Z."
"Do not want!"
"Halp!"
"Adjective cat is adjective".
"I iz X."
"Invisible X!"
"Oh noes!"


Kthx.

    \n Thanks for the link to Lolcode, Clyde, it was - lolworthy.

    Yes, a card game. Perhaps along the lines of Fish (or Go Fish, as the verbose Americans call it).

    "I can has queenz?"
    "I has a queen."
    (or, if you don't): "Invisible queenz."

    But with an exchange rule, like Settlers of Cat-an (heh):

    "I can has queenz?"
    "I has a queen. I can has threez?"

    So you then need to decide if that's a swap you want to do:

    "Kthx" (if you do)
    "Do not want!" (if you don't)

    You need to declare if you're going to draw a card from the deck, or "bukket":

    "I'm in ur bukket, drawing ur cardz."

    And declare that you have collected a set, as you lay it down:

    "O hai, I gotted your queens."

    Of course, when you win, you declare:

    "Winner cat is winner."

    To which the other players must respond:

    "O noes!"
