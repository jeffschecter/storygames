\nIt's here.

They're FATE dice that use a 'pulse' to represent +1, and a 'flatline' to represent -1.

You know, I'm my own worst enemy. I waited several months before executing on this because I was so concerned with the details, and the fact that even now somewhere in the back of my mind I can't make a big deal about dice. They're just dice, right? Who gets fired up about dice?

I think the biggest part to these Kickstarters is the mental game. It really is true that the worst that can happen is you FAIL. It costs nothing but potential pride and sanity. And that is an awesome thing.

In related news, I can now confirm that at least one Dresden Files campaign was killed because of Diablo III. DAMN YOU DIABLOOOO!!! *shakes fist vigorously*.

    \n Yeah, I wish the price could be lower, but the major culprit is shipping, which is ridiculously high on the low end, and ridiculously low on the high end. To be specific, it's a flat $6.90 out of every pledge, for 1 to 100 dice, anywhere in the world. That's why there's no single die option. With shipping included it would have been over $10.

    Then again, it's not like I lose anything by putting that option up. Hmm...

    Oh, and check this out. Notice the spike today (June 19th)? While I can't conclusively say such was the case, the only thing I did for the Kickstarter was go to three game stores on Long Island (Ravenblood, Brothers Grim, Men at Arms) and leave business card sized flyers advertising the project.

    Now if this spike WAS a result of that, then physical space advertising is far more important than I initially suspected. I'm planning to hit The Compleat Strategist, Twenty Sided Store, and Forbidden Planet tomorrow, and DexCon in July, but if any of you know good game stores, events, or clubs I should contact to send flyers to, PLEASE tell me
