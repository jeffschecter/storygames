\n Hello,

You are invited to take part in a game design contest this January:

RPG Solitaire Challenge

The theme is to create one-person role playing games. That is, one-player games with no GM. It can be done! How to Host a Dungeon is a lovely example. It is standard in war games and card games (of course) to write games that one person can play. The challenge presented by role playing games is unique, because of how integral the social interaction is to them. (Frex, how awkward it can be when two characters you play have to interact.) But telling stories, and creating worlds, exploring characters and having emotional experiences are all things we can do on our own. The types of games that could support this kind of play range from war game-esque simulations to Once-Upon-A-Time or tarot based story wanderings, to amazing embodied, live-form games where you play a role that perhaps only you witness. There is a lot of room to experiment here, so now's the time!

This will be a fun, low-pressure event, with an emphasis on helping each other design, and giving interesting feedback about the games written. There will be specific challenges established by Judges to give some creative constraints. The Challenges will be announced on January 1, when the contest opens.

There will be prizes awarded: Judges will post about their favorite games from the contest during February!

The contest will begin on 1/1/11 and end on 1/11/11*. Hope to see you there.

All the best,
Emily
http://rpgsolitairechallenge.blogspot.com/

*Jan 1, 2011 through Jan 11, 2011

    \n Hi Em,

    This challenge looks awesome! Thanks for setting it up.

    One question: it says in the rules that judging will only be based on a text file, but what about games for which graphic elements are essential to the game? For example, in thinking about one-player games like Jackson's "Smoke Dream" or player-against-the-board games like Reiner Knizia's "Lord of the Rings" or the new "Castle Ravenloft," a lot of those games use random card draws or a boardgame board as a way of providing structure for solo play. If I participate, it would most likely involve something along those lines, like the Snow Queen game (Quicktime demo) I was experimenting with. But I'm having trouble thinking about how to describe or represent a game board or the like in a text file. For cards, I suppose I could just write a description of each card, but that still seems like a less-than-idea way to present the game.

    Any suggestions? I'm not asking for a rules change -- clearly you made that decision intentionally -- just advice on how to submit a game where the visual elements or form factor are part of the core gameplay.

    \n I've posted more information about the Birds of a Feather and Golden Crow Awards at the RPG Solitaire Challenge blog. These awards are open to all the contestants, even if you don't finish your game, and are also open to everyone else who isn't competing!

    You win the Birds of a Feather Award by reading the blog posts or threads of contestants and posting comments and questions there. The Golden Crow Award will be given to all those who comment substantively on all of the open development threads. If you win the Golden Crow, you'll receive a prize through the mail. I talked about them more over at the RPG Crossroads forum.

    (The secret here is that this is one of the best parts of these contests. The real prize is in the doing.)
