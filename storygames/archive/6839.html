\n Well, it\u2019s been a while coming, but I finally released an electronic version of Covenant, my game of failing conspiracies.

In case you don't know the game, here the pitch: What if you were a member of a secret conspiracy who spent millennia preparing for the apocalypse, but it didn't happen? How would you reassess your life? What would you do when your duty and your convictions pulled you in different directions. Where do you go when everything you believed was a lie?

More detail, including examples and resources, at the Covenant webpage

You can currently download the Covenant PDF at RPGNow for a $10 price tag. This is half what the book costs, so you\u2019re getting plenty of goodness for your e-buck (but I would say that).

The PDF also contains all the errata and a few extra tweaks. Nothing major was added, but if you own the original book, drop me and email (matt / at / realms / co / uk) and I\u2019ll hook you up with a PDF.

    \n Covenant is a fantastically fun game.

    Motifs and Conventions are a great idea for bringing mood and tone to a game. The character sheet has a magnificent visual hierarchy that tells you "start here, threaten this, end here" visually. The game has enough setting to ground you, and enough freedom to make it fit your vision.

    Last game we played, we were a society cell stirring riots in an attempt to disrupt the Messiah's message from being delivered to the people of Paris, thus redeeming Apocalypse. We had a sexy riot cop, and a brothel owner, and a psychotic avant-garde artist who used human sacrifices in his art, and a shootout, and a daughter almost losing faith in her father, and underworld dealings, and a suave martini lounge, and... it was grand.

    I would be buying this if I didn't already own it.
