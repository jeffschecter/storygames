\n I want to start a playtesting contest. What does that mean? I'm not sure.

It seems we have some cool things fall by the wayside because getting feedback is more difficult in our cornucopia of design. I got my ashcan into about 40 pairs of hands last year and getting feedback has been like pulling teeth. I want to help folks get encouraged, instead of discouraged. I want to try to work on helping folks with ashcans this year with a contest, which if it works out I'll run every year, till I don't. What kind of prize can I give to encourage folks to get off their duffs and find something interesting to give feedback on? What kind of criteria can I use to judge performance? Help?

    \n How about a long-term contest, going for say, 2-6 months.

    For every game that someone playtests and gives feeback for, the contestant gets a point.
    The designer gets to decide if feedback was actually given and if they want their game involved in the contest.

    The winner is the one with the most points at the end of the 2-6 months.
    Ties are broken in a fun way that I'm sure someone will figure out.

    The prize is a copy of the final released version of all the games that were playtested by the contestant.
    They will be awarded as they are made available.

    Posted By: Clyde L. RhoerIt seems to be that you're not motivated by the idea of a contest, because you already receive benefits from playtesting
    It's not that I'm not motivated by the idea of a contest, but that the suggestions in this thread so far cover the entire space I could come up with \u2014 a beauty contest, basically. It's not clear to me how interesting that is. Maybe it would be fun to add a rubric? Like, a point for the best rewrite suggestion, a point for the funniest rules exploit, a point for the best writeup of mechanics 1...5 in play, a point for the report that makes the designer laugh, and a point for the one that makes him cry tears of hot, bitter shame? That gives the playtesters a shot at getting better, too.

    Let me go back one step, Clyde, on your IIEE: You say you've made up your mind to do a contest, to get people testing. Would it be too much of a rewrite to do an event instead, with the same goal in mind? Like, a Playtesting Week, where participants get some cool reward as detailed in my original posting? That would get some people out of the woodwork, I wager, on both sides. As a designer, I have a game that's been tested once each internally and externally, and I'm just wrapping up a rewrite incorporating all the feedback. I don't know how interested I'd be in freeform judging a contest, but I'd be less hesitant if I had a rubric to stick to, and even less hesitant if I knew it would cost me just the price of making up a dozen hard copies and mailing them to my testers, with a little "bonus" of some kind (or just mailing out the bonus, for that matter). And as a playtester, I'd be psyched to participate in the greatest accumulation of AP reports we'll see all year, swapping stories with everyone else about the crazy, under-the-radar game I got to try out.
