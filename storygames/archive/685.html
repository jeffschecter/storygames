Do you hang with your gaming group when not gaming?

Andy
May 2006 edited May 2006 in Story Games
I was just curious as to the social relationships and stuff going on out there.

Here's my regular groups:
1: Weekly: Mark, Beth, Alan, Jeremie

2: Bi-Weekly: Mark (Causey), Scott, Tim and Rick

3: Random-ish: Clinton, Jason, Mark, Remi, Lisa, Eric, Jeff, Jonathan, a few others

In my regular group, I'm pretty close with Alan and Scott, so we hang out about once or twice a month outside of gaming, usually dinners or movies or DVDs or that kind of thing.

A lot of folks in my regular groups (1 + 2) have a lot of the same interests, but it's not often stuff you can "do together" (like Interactive fiction, coding, personal management, etc). My weekly, group, however are kind of cinema enthusiasts, so when something new and fun comes out (major or indie), a lot of us often meet up on the weekend to see a matinee. We also do things like go to the occasional free music fest (Jeremie is a big bluegrass musician, and is known locally as "That French Professor who can play some killer bluegrass") in the area and the like. Sometimes we all go out to dinner.

The Bi-Weekly group we usually meet just for gaming, it's a more hardcore crowd (we basically play all sorts of indie or small-press games for 3 sessions then move to the next game).

Overall, I'm comfortable with how that's going. I'd be up for meeting the Randomish local folks more, though, as they're cool people and all, but it's often hard enough to coordinate schedules for gaming much less stuff outside of gaming. To help bridge those cool things to do, though, I set up a Yahoogroup where we can post about cool local events that we might be interested in. Also, we're planning on doing a rythm games day at some point (DDR, Guitar Hero, Taiko no Tatsujin, etc).

So, overall, I'm pretty happy with the level of personal involvement I have with my gamer friends. How about you folks?

-Andy
