    \n I'm actually a little bit flabbergasted that anyone would need a tutor for playing games. Game time is used productively when you're having fun and learning what you like and don't like.     \nA gaming tutor isn't a good idea because it isn't enough. One on one gaming is a very different experience to a larger group experience, and therefore one on one tutorials aren't going to get across the techniques needed to engage in a gaming group effectively.

    Therefore, what you would need to rent out is an entire gaming group. I would recommend a team of three people that could play these games with you, in a simulation of an actual gaming environment. You would learn not only techniques for playing the game, but social techniques needed to facillitate a successful gaming group. These three individuals would allow you to learn in an immersion environment (Not the gaming technique, the educational equivalent, like French Immersion). You would be literally playing games, but with the overall goal of improving your skills in handling rules, engaging with other people at the table, reading social cues and learning how to channel the flow of ideas and concepts in a group environment.

    \n Hiring a tutor for anything is, in my mind, perfectly fine. However, I will present alternate strategies that might work better for you.

    1. Reading. There are a lot of role-playing books out there on how to make the hobby more awesome. I recommend Play Unsafe as a starting point, but there are loads of other books as well. Also, many game blogs.
    2. Observing. Sit in on other games with the intention of serious (engaged) observation. Take notes. Come to conclusions based on what you've seen. Develop theories.
    3. Interview. Close to tutoring, you can ask others specifically for what they look for in games and what they've seen that has sparked their passions. A few good interviews can clarify much of what you'd learn from a tutor.
    4. Classwork. Take an actual class in acting, go to an improvisational group, watch movies from various genres and deconstruct them. Let's face it; if you're hiring a tutor, they would assign you much of this anyway.

    It's not so much that you shouldn't hire a tutor, as that is just one way to the knowledge you're looking for. I highly recommend you sketch out your precise goals and then evaluate what the best path to meeting them is.
