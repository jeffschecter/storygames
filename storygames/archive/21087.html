    \n I love the Dramasystem (minus procedural mechanics) and find it one of the most underrated game systems at the moment. I blame it on these three factors that it is underrated:
    1. The Hillfolk setting (bronze age) is not so intriguing to a large audience.
    2. The layout looks beautiful yet is hard to read (esp. as PDF).
    3. There are tons of settings in the book(s) yet almost no ready-to-play playsets (only for Hillfolk and Malandros, as far as I know).

    Personally, I don't find DramaSystem really suitable for drop-in/out style play. Right at the beginning, you invest quite a bit of time in building dramatic poles between the characters (and within themselves). Then you play out the manifesting drama gradually over session(s).
    Choosing these characters that totally matter to each other (like brothers, lovers, sworn enemies) is in my opinion the most important factor for the success of a Dramasystem game.
    I cannot see this possibly playing out with drop in/out play.
