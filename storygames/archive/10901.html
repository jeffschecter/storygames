    Posted By: Marshall BurnsChargen rules are overrated! Just give the character what makes sense to describe who they are.
    \nNo way! Character generation rules are What Makes A Game A Role-playing Game.

    Well ... maybe that's too much. But still.

    You can definitely see how the characters in Lady Blackbird aren't just a random band of adventurers. They're charged up with intrapersonal conflicts. Plus the game starts in the middle of a tense, dramatic situation.

    So you could make chargen rules for Lady Hackbird (whatever) ... but you'd need more than just stuff like three Keys, five Traits, etc. You'd have to make those intrapersonal conflicts and dramatic starting situations part of the mechanics.

    For example: One (1) Trait must revolve positively around another PC. One (1) Trait must revolve negatively around another PC. One (1) Trait must explain the character's past. Etc.

    You could do the whole thing yourself, like Jason did for his Bloody Forks of the Ohio hack (which I really dig, and have put into the long, long queue of games my group is gonna play). But with chargen rules that captured that stuff, it could become a group activity that would be part of the game.

    I'm not saying Blackbird and its descendants NEEDS this, but if someone wanted to do it for their own game, there's probably a way to do it right.

    \n This thread makes me happy.

    I won't harsh the flow by coming down with the Word on what a generic LB might be. Too many interesting thoughts coalescing already.

    About Conditions:
    A condition is a fictional situation that constrains what you're allowed to say about your character. It's a cue to tell the GM and players to pay attention to that thing and use it as material for the developing fiction. Gaming is just us saying stuff to each other, yeah? So you're like, "What do I say now?" and you look down and go, "Oh, I'm angry. I'll go be angry at someone then. 'Snargle! Why is my bridge full of chicken feathers?!'"

    For the GM, the conditions represent permissions. "They throw manacles on you and toss you back in the brig," would be a fight normally, but no, you're Trapped, so there's permission. And I mean permission to say a thing is true, not some kind of required mechanical thing like "When someone is Trapped for two rounds without removing the Trapped condition, move them to the brig." Gah.

    That's how I use them. There are other ways.

    \n So, I sort of did this, because it looked like nobody else had published their efforts. A generic Lady Hackbird with some character generation.

    I also rearranged it a bit for "fluency play" (and so it's easier to play over Skype or IRC) - there's a basic version I called "meat"--which fits on a page--and then you can layer additional rules up on top as you go.

    I also took out some stuff (keys and secrets) with a much slimmer "flags" rule in its place - but I put it on the story games wiki, so you can put that stuff right back in as optional / advanced rules if you want.

    And just last week I finally got a chance to playtest. I'm happy to say it worked! I didn't kill the magic!

    I called it "Blackbird Pie".

    And here it is:

    http://www.story-games.com/codex/index.php?title=Blackbird_Pie
