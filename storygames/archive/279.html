Part the first, I've stopped trying to make real discussion involving GNS theory as I have yet to grasp it. Maybe one day I will, but this is not that time. But, I am using terms in a way that I think matches their definition and is meaningful. If it comes out that I should use other words, just say so and I'll fix things.

Part the second:

I've finally gotten to play some games recently with stakes setting, and after going over it in my head, I think I realized a pattern: I was competing with it! Stakes that everyone found to be reasonable but didn't get excited over rubbed me wrong, and thus I'd step on up and up the ante! And I'd know that I'd gotten what I wanted when people would look at me and ask (verbally or silently) you'd risk that with your character?

Am I just a fun stakes-setting kind of guy, or more in touch with how stakes should be set?

    Mark:

    I'm not sure I'd call what you're doing Gamism. 'Step on Up' implies a personal risk on the same level as competitive sports and the like - it's about your self-esteem and your ego. You risk your abilities to play the game for the chance to prove yourself to your peers (or to yourself). Which is an altogether different thing than pushing the stakes to the max because that's what makes it extra cool and meaningful for you.

    The difference is in the payoff. So, what's the payoff like in your group, and for you, when you do that? Is it something like, "Yeah, up with the stakes. Check it out, I'm the most badass emo storyteller *evar*!" Or is it something like, "Yeah, up with the stakes. Look at how cool and badass and emo this story is now!" Or is it something else?
