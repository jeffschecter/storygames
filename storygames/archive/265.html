Generation

Josh Roby
February 2006 edited February 2006 in Story Games
An RPG that takes place on a generation ship carrying a large number of settlers to colonize another world. Play begins by determining the length of the voyage in terms of sessions. Each session is a different generation; players play their own or each other's children. Each session/episode/situation that the PCs play through determines the character of the colony to be founded. So one session where they deal with labor disputes determines whether or not slavery will be legal; another session where a cult begins calling for the downfall of the government determines the religious landscape of the colony.

    Sounds like a fun ARIA that works.

    Actually, it sounds a little like an independently-made but widely loved/respected Japanese RPG from a few years back for the PSX called "Ore no Shikabane wo Koete Yuke" (Step Over my Dead Body and Continue Forth"). It was set in a fantasty Japanese/Chinese setting, and you played the cursed bloodline of a family, and its current male or female heir: Every few "turns" your character died and your descendent became the current heir (you could manage the bloodline as well). As the game progressed you set up institutions and the like that your descendents could draw upon and develop. The game never evolved too much (like Civilization with Technologies, it stayed in that same setting and all), and it was teleological: Eventually your descendents had to rise up and strike down the demons that cursed your family.

    But it got a lot of rep and is still a huge underground hit because of those aspects that you describe in your session breakdown above (not single protagonists for a long period of time, etc). I think, done well, such a game could rock the house. It would be a lot of work to write well, though.

    -Andy
