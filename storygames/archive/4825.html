Have you ever had Alexander Hamilton wind up your clockwork power armor, jump out of Thomas Paine's ornithopter, and land in the middle of the Battle of Yorktown to punch General Cornwallis in the face?

No?

Well... would you like to?

The Sons of Liberty Preorder is now open. Take on the role of the Founding Fathers to kick ass and take names for truth, justice, and the American way in the only Roleplaying Game of Freedom and Badassery.

The game's fast-paced card mechanics ensure high-action madness and revolutionary heroics. If you are playing Benjamin Franklin and you aren't swinging an electrified kite over your head to clear the streets of redcoats, then you are playing it wrong.

View the game's Patriot Sheet, Tory Play Aid, and Objectives Sheet.

Physical Specifications\n
  • 144 pages
    • \n
  • 6" x 9" format
    • \n
  • Softcover
    • \n
  • Perfect Bound
    • \n
Play Sons of Liberty in three different modes: Battle Mode, Campaign Mode, and Versus Mode.\n
  • In Battle Mode, you and three or more friends play
    through one secret \u201cbattle\u201d of the Sons of Liberty. One player takes on the role of the Tories, while everyone else picks a Patriot figure to portray. A single battle takes between two and three hours to play.
    • \n
  • In Campaign Mode, you and your friends string together a series of battles to tell the whole, secret story of the American Revolution. Each battle features different Patriot figures, and the role of Tory player is traded to a new player each time. Scheduling is simple since the group of players need not be the same for each battle \u2013 if Jim can\u2019t make it one evening, there\u2019s just one less Patriot and more badassery for everybody else.
    • \n
  • In Versus Mode, you and one other player can each take on the role of one of the Sons of Liberty after the Revolution has been won. Once comrades in arms who founded a nation, the task of actually building that nation often set these men and women at odds. It was their impassioned struggles with each other that formed the nation we know today.
\nRead the Sons of Liberty Design Blog at Ludanta Retero.
Sons of Liberty will be released on President's Day, February 15th, Election Year 2008 at OrcCon. Preorder and receive the Full PDF Preview in January in addition to the softcover in February!

The Sons of Liberty preorder is $25 and is available through Kallisti Press website.

    \n Sophie, SoL is pretty much nothing like FLFS -- even if its fictional content makes it a prequel (pre-ether, so using solar-powered light cannon; one of the Tory figures is a female military officer leading the Morrigan Corbie, etc).

    SoL is a very fast-paced, very confrontational, very one-upsmanship oriented game. It takes a lot of the tys from the -punk genres and uses them as fodder for telling way, way larger-than-life stories of figures who have become larger-than-life legends.

    FLFS is all about character; SoL is all about badassery.

    (( Ooh -- FLFS is like the League of Extraordinary Gentlemen graphic novel. SoL is like the movie version, complete with the anachronistic car. )

    \nSons of Liberty is Jungle Speed: the Roleplaying Game.

    This statement is not completely true, but it brings out similar feelings in me when I play.

    An example. In our in-office campaign, someone just fired The Shot Heard Round The World. Except it was in Providence, Rhode Island. And it was fired by George Washington, who was astride a flying mechanical horse. Oh yeah, and the shot was from a flank-mounted rocket launcher on said horse.

    Another example. Ben Franklin needs to make his getaway from the British. He leaps off a building, arms outspread. Kites shoot out from launchers up his sleeves, and he flies away to safety.

    If that sort of thing bothers you, then you're probably not going to enjoy this game.

    If there's a tiny voice inside you that said, "Awesome!", then you might enjoy this game.

    Seth Ben-Ezra
    Great Wolf
