    Feedback suggests that the game is very friendly to non-gamers. It was always an aim to make the game easy to play for non-gamers. At cons, for example, with players who've never played before or seen the book, I have spent 30 mins with players as they create their bands and play the first couple of scenes, then observed and answered questions for the next hour, then sat on my hands for the next hour and said nothing and the game has run completely smoothly. So much so that I actually left the game I started at Conception in January and went to the bar. Half an hour later I came back and the player hadn't really noticed that I was gone. I'd left them a copy of the Cheat Sheet to refer to and that was all they needed.

    Play has a simple structure. Players each create a Metal Band, then take it in turn to "Rock Out"; setting a scene for their Band, resolving any conflict which occurs and narrating the outcomes.

    The basic mechanics are a simple card draw. Your band's Ego, Hope and Fanbase scores along with the type of scene you're playing will determine how many cards each player draws, and the player with the most black cards wins the conflict at hand (because black cards are more Metal than red ones, right?). Player with the highest card (regardless of who wins the conflict) get to narrate the outcome, so there are times when you "win" but don't get to describe how, and times when you "lose" but get to decide exactly what happens.

    By default, you play for 3 hours and then trigger the endgame. Once that's over, you'll know your bands' fates and will describe what happens to them in an epilogue.

    (Anyone who's played Contenders will be right at home here. The game started out as a Contenders hack, and while quite different in play and feel, it still shows it's origins fairly clearly.)

    I'm happy to answer any other questions anyone might have, but I hope that shows you a bit more of what the game is about. You can see a sample here if you wantto see what the game looks like.
