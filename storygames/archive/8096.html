\n Arising from the "What are you working on?" thread, in which:\n
Posted By: HituroPosted By: MikeRM
Myon-blog novelGu, which is kind of Charlie Stross on Ecstasy or something, needs only about three or four more posts to finish; it's been languishing because I've had other projects which are more immediate and which are receiving more interest from other people. I'm not sure I'm going to bother to print it just yet.
\nI'm fascinated by the concept of an on-blog novel, which people can read as it's written (as opposed to the short-form fan fiction that most people write online). Does it work well for you? Tell me more (possibly in a whisper or another thread)

It has worked pretty well, because I can do a post of 1500-odd words and it's a chapter. A 1500-word post is surprisingly easy to write (I find; YMMV), and because you're in a blog mindset it's a stimulus to write regularly. And it also discourages spending a lot of time revising, since it's easier to post new stuff than to revise old stuff (or rather, your RSS audience won't see the revisions, so I restricted myself to a couple of small needed retcons).

If blogs had existed when I started my previous novel, City of Masks, I would certainly have done it as a blog because it's in journal-entry format (with a couple of letters for variety).

The actual Gu novel is a bit unusual and hasn't attracted a big audience, despite my blatant linkbaiting with mentions of Cory Doctorow (does the guy no longer egosurf, or is he deliberately ignoring me?). But in principle, I think it's a good approach to writing in general - I've also got a non-fiction book out of one of my other blogs (I have four at the moment).

I use the comments to give something like a director's commentary track (particularly appropriate since Gu is presented as a kind of documentary film).

I've also thought of writing a play-by-blog storygame in which multiple bloggers collectively build a story of a small colonial town through their "journal entries".

Does anyone have any other thoughts on creative uses for blogs? It's a very new medium - as I say, when I started my previous novel in about 1997, it didn't exist - so I'm sure there's all kinds of cutting-edge stuff that people are doing or could be doing.

    \n Yeah, I've recently begun an LJ community (why I chose a community, I'm not quite certain anymore...) for posting my NaNoWriMo novel (http://community.livejournal.com/nnwm_tgk/). One thing that irks me is that there doesn't appear to be a way to read top to bottom. Newer stuff is at the top, as is normal in most blogs, and this might be preferable if I'd started posting when I started writing, and people following along could read each chapter as it came. As it is, scrolling down to the first chapter, reading to the bottom, scrolling up to the next and reading back down, etc. is kind of a hassle.
    \n
    \nI've never even considered futzing with RSS feeds. I like the idea, but I see no benefit in learning about them at this time, so I haven't. I read only LJ, and avoid all the other social networking spots. I'm not incredibly interested in game design or theory blogs either, so I have no reason to go to various other sites, or to aggregate posts from more than one location.
    \n
    \nFor serial reading, I like the webcomic method, though obviously pictures aren't necessary, but having to load every page individually if you're reading from the beginning can be annoying, too, most especially if you're on a somewhat slow connection.
