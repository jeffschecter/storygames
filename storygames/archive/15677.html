    \n Here they are!

    (A slightly abridged version of the email I sent to McDaldno)

    Today was my day of service! No kids, no classes, the house wasn't about to fall apart or burn down, so I had some time...

    No kids, no classes, no (significant) housework. I could get to work with the Purposeful Acts...

    ...except, no camera (I had planned to document each one) and no car (they had to be close to home, I guess).

    Well, they didn't have to be close to home, I thought - I've got the internet! But, money's tight all over, and I didn't really have that much of an excuse not to get out on a beautiful day and DO something, rather than just give money online. So, a compromise. Two donations, two projects outside. This, I could do.

    I decided on a concentric effort, distant to close, to spread my efforts around.

    First, distant. It had to be something global, or at least large-scale. I give regularly at my parish, but for this I had to do something I wouldn't normally do. For me, this would be self-directed targeted giving. About an hour's research had kiboshed some of my first thoughts, but let me to water & sanitation issues. Really dead-basic 'you need this to live' stuff. So, I made a $10 donation to WaterAid.org. They seemed to be the most comprehensive global water-issue group.

    Next, closer. I'm a big fan of public libraries. A repository of information and knowledge, available free, is a good, good thing. I live in a slightly-less-wealthy part of a very wealthy (overall) small town in Boone County, Indiana. The rest of Boone County is mostly rural, and poor. We have a great library. Lebanon, the county seat, has a pretty good one, but they can always use help. Their webpage doesn't have an online donation function, so I called them up and spoke to Yvonne, who said they would gladly take a check. She also told me that donations like that are very welcome, as they allow them to do projects outside of the primary budget. Into the mail went a $10 check.

    Third, even closer. The neighborhood I live in is bounded on the east side by a sidewalk that a lot of middle-and high-school kids use to walk home, or to nearby shops. Our street department does a very good job, but they're always stretched thin. I could use the exercise, so out I went with a big trash bag and walked up and down a half-mile stretch, picking up litter. It wasn't as bad as I expected it to be, but I did get no small amount of candy wrappers, cans and cigarette butts off the grass and walk. Next time I do that, I'm getting one of those grabby-stabby-stick things. Ow, my back.

    Last, very close. My next door neighbor, a single mom with two teenage kids, has a big, sweet yellow lab. I cleaned up the poop in her yard. Simple, effective, and having had dogs from my earliest memories on, getting a break from Poop Patrol duties is a good thing.

    And those are the Purposeful Acts! UserClone, I have no contact info for you, so I asked McDaldno to contact you and arrange for your copies of Perfect and The Grotesque.

    (This was fun! The challenge of thinking up these acts led to a lot of creative, investigative possibilities. 'What needs doing around here, that I can do?' is a very interesting question...)
