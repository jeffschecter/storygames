\n Hey there!

Currently, the system has the following mode.
Roll $Skill + $Attribute d10, keep $Attribute dice.

Attributes are very valuable:
1) you get dice
2) you get to keep more dice
3) they count on a lot of different rolls

Skills are much more limited:
1) you get dice on specific rolls

So it shouldn't surprise anyone that Attributes have a cost factor of x4, while Skills only have x1.

What if we made Attributes a bit cheaper, but attached the "you get to keep more dice" aspect to the Skills instead?
Of course Skills would need to be a bit more expensive then. And we'd have a problem with untrained rolls.

Attribute rolls would still be: roll $Attribute dice, keep $Attribute dice

Your thoughts?

    \n I think if you break it down this way, it doesn't really "harm" the game balance. But it does say something about how the world works. Essentially, this is saying it doesn't matter how much natural talent you have, if you are not well trained. Whereas the previous system means that characters with natural talent are boosted by training in specific skills.
    I don't "feel" like one model is better than another, so it is down to preference or feel of the setting at that point.
    Bear in mind that your Rings are affected by Attributes, so don't discount it to much if you make that change.
