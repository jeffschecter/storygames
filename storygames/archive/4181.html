\n I have, from near the beginning, broken my game's (Stories from the Shelter) manual into two major sections: the Players' Section and the Narrator's Guidelines. Problem is, while the entirety of the manual is open reading for all players, only the first section is important for starting play. I'm afraid the current section titles might discourage the casual player from reading the Narrator's Guidelines, even though the manual's description of what's included in the sections encourages its reading. Anyone have any suggestions on alternate section titles?

    \n Let me add a suggestion: don't ditch completely the "generic names" of the sections in favour of the "flavoured ones". Instead, put a generic, explanatory subtitle under them.

    For example:

    At the doorway
    Playing the game


    Deep in the Life
    How to run the game
    (or "tips and tricks" or or whatever)

    Because yes, flavoured headings are good for setting the tone. But don't generate "game speak": you risk the pitfall of game argot in WW games
