\n I snagged the texts from the What is a RPG? thread. I dumped them into separate text files names by title, author, and year. All the texts were pulled in the R software package. Several R packages were used to mine the texts.

Corpus - a collection of texts
Text Document Matrix (tdm) - Bag of words. A mathematical matrix that describes the frequency of terms that occur in a collection of documents.

Words the occur more then 100 times in the corpus
[1] "action" "adventur" "charact" "creat" "game" "play" "player" "role"
[9] "roleplay" "rule" "stori" "world"

Fifteen of the most used words in the corpus
game charact player play roleplay stori rule role adventur world creat
714 611 562 298 255 251 152 132 130 127 118
action make storytel set
107 94 84 83


So we might want to ask what words are associated with some of our top 15 most used words.

findAssocs(tdm, "game", 0.6)

    That said, there's nothing much that jumps out here, the association of D&D, levels etc with challenges, rather than say the pursuit of a goal in a story game, which is a much a challenge.

    \n@catty_big Well, it should be for everyone who is interested! So ask away!! I'll do my best to answer your questions.

    What this says:
    When this group (which is heavily bias and still needs to be corrected) of 110 authors write about What is a RPG the word game is associated with player, characters, and play fairly frequently.

    The point
    Reading through the other thread I saw people talking about what it might mean if we write about What is a RPG in a certain way. So I wondered is there a certain way we write about this.

    This initial data analysis shows that we do talk about what is a RPG in a certain (perhaps limited) way.

    The big cluster dendrogram shows how each What is a RPG related to each other. So texts that have similar word frequency and association of words group together.
