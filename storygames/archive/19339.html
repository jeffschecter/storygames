    \n Risus one-shot, because the table was too crowded for Star Wars FAE. The genre: Cold War thriller set in the Future as seen from 1964

    On the moon, an American secretary desperately attempt to help the vaguely-carrot shaped alien Visitor broadcast a message of peace to Earth before World War III breaks out.

    Sadly, the secretary botches her roll. The alien's message is broadcast--but only to televisions currently tuned to children's programming.

    In their lunar rover, the Soviet spy and the Swiss trade delegate to the lunar colonies try to help Premier Sergei Krushchev get in touch with President Barry Goldwater. Unfortunately, their message is blocked. The last sound they hear from Moscow is the high pitched whine of the phone melting.

    Looking up, they see the flashes of nuclear detonations winking across the Earth...

    \n Post-Modern Masks of Nyarlathotep: the PCs are at the Stumbling Tiger, which two nights a week is Shanghai's best gay nightclub.

    Jimmy Wright finds a charming Chinese bartender who speaks with a perfect Received Pronunciation accent. He asks him if he ever met a crazy woman reporter named Jackson Elias.

    "No speak English," says the bartender, leaving.

    A pretty young drag queen at the end of the bar notices. "Say, were you looking for Jack Brady? I know something about him."

    "You do? What?"

    Beat.

    "Well, you could buy a girl a drink."

    Sheepishly, Jimmy orders a drink for her.

    \n Tenra Bansho hack: PC's army is hexcrawling to the north, circling a forest on fire only to find a flamethrower power armor on the other side.

    While the rear of the army and the cooker engage in a battle against an undead hog -ok, half of it actually- they were scavenging for food (they had just killed it but everything that dies in the forest comes back as a zombie in almost no time), the 300 men of the barbarian division engage with the power armor. Some end up with some burns but they have it mostly under control until the last roll of the battle that ends up killing... one of them. As the pyromaniac in the armor dies, he laughs and detonates the machine, but Que Monito, the only midget barbarian that fights unarmed, pushes the armor away from their fellow barbarians, saving them at the cost of his life.

    A couple of hexes later, the army finds a necromancer who is using the souls of all the fallen to fight them. Inside a sort of lam on his hand, he taunts them showing them the soul of Que Monito. The barbarian division jumps to the battle completely enraged.

    \n Apocalypse World play-by-post.

    Brainer PC Silica, after a few dalliances with a girl named K2, decides she needs to break up with her. That she was only sleeping with K2 because she was mourning the lost of Footie, her best friend who was shot in a recent gunfight. After a painful back-and-forth, K2 begs Silica to let her stay, admitting she needs to be around her, she can't explain it, she just wants her, whatever she needs to be.

    Of course, Silica has the Gear of "receptivity drugs", which the player requested if it could be her own body fluids. How could I deny such a request? K2 is tagged and has become addicted to Silica. She thinks it's sexual attraction / love. But truth be told, Silica is a disease.

    \n In the most recent Eternal Lies session, folks wanted to get from Point A to Point B, but all of the motorized vehicles had been commandeered by the military. I didn't expect this to be a problem, as they had contacts and money for bribes, as well as the skills to steal a vehicle. Really, no big.

    Martin: Everyone! Let me work!

    Lillian: Thank Kali for competent pimps.

    Okay, I figured we were going for "My friend here will have sex with you if you'll give us all a lift to Point B." Not the approach I expected, but perfectly viable. Martin spoke the local language, so he'd be doing the talking. If the captain preferred guys, well, so did Martin, so he could improvise, and anyway, it was getting late, and Point B was where a Core Clue was, so I wasn't about to add extra complications this time.

    As it turned out, I didn't have to. Martin's player cheerfully did it. What I expected to be a simple, straightforward sex-for-transportation deal turned into --

    Martin: So, my friend here read a silly desert romance novel and wants a fine Italian captain to pretend he's kidnapping her and have sex with her and then drive her to an infantry column so it can pretend to rescue her because her uncle here is going to send her to school when he gets back -- no, it's fine, her uncle is totally behind this plan because he also wants her to have some fun.

    Once Vito, pretending to be Lillian's uncle (and who also spoke Italian) finished mentally facepalming, he explained that she had a thing for one of those, er, jazz musician types, and he'd rather her first time was with a fine Italian man who knew how to treat her. And, Lillian's player spent a point of flattery, and...

    Well, that's one guy who's going to be telling stories about weird American women for a long time.
