    BTW, I played D&F this weekend, and it was fun as hell. It's a board gamey game that plays out like a half-RPG half-board game.

    I say of it, "It feels like one of those funny Steve Jackson Games games, but it's actually fun and playable. The fun and hilarity comes from the players, not the cards (ie Munchkin, Chez Geek etc) so you never get that "use the card twice and its no longer funny" effect".

    In our last week's game, the heroes:

    Fell into the mouth of a giant sleeping viper
    Fell into debt with the Dark Elven Crossing Guard Guild
    Drowned in hallucinations from psychadelic mushrooms
    Fell into a spiked pit guarded by a ferocious minotaur
    Fell in love with Disco, orchestrated by a Flying Succubus Olivia Newton John
    among other things

    ...I can't recall the other drowning encounters at the moment...

    -Andy

    Getting out of debt in the dark city of the dark elves was tough. I think Eric shanked an old lady dark elf for her grocery money, which was really cold.

    I'm really proud of D&F's simultaneous death spiral and awesome spiral, in which badly rolled characters get mercilessly crushed and replaced (that's why it's called a characters sheet), and guys with dumb luck accumulate treasure and level up and are generally boss. It's every designer's worst nightmare and it is totally fun.
