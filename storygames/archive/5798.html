    \n I, too, love the picture.

    This seems like the thread in which to relate a little anecdote from my history:

    Three or four years ago, before I discovered indie gaming, I felt the desire to chat about RPGs on the Internet. I was yearning for the kind of panel discussions I got into at gaming and science fiction conventions, and there was no convention coming up on the calendar. Sooo, I hit the 'net:

    For whatever reason, my Google Fu turned up TheRPGSite first, so I went there and lurked, reading posts for a couple of hours. Then I shut down the computer in disgust, thinking "My God, if that's the attitude and general level of civility of gamers online, I want nothing to do with it."

    I was burned so bad that it took me a couple of weeks before the desire to chat about RPGs overcame my disgust, and I searched again. This time, I found RPGnet, and digging deeper, the Forge. RPGnet seemed a lot more civil than TheRPGSite, and the Forge was fascinating: Here were people who made actual, literate posts. They were cryptic as Hell, but they were coherent. People were polite, for the most part, and helpful in pointing out where to start learning the weird jargon. Eventually, from the Forge I followed links and found Story Games for Everybody, and other sites from the 'diaspora'. Happiness ensued.

    The point of this story is; what would my reaction to Story Games as a community have been if I had just walked in two or three days ago? What has the reaction been of other guests, just discovering the site for the first time? How many accidental casualties have there been; people who might have had useful contributions, whom we will now never even meet?

    --
    DainXB
