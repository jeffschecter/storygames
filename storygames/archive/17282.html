    \n Useful post relevant to this discussion. http://ryanmacklin.com/2011/03/ways-to-afford-an-editor/

    1) Do you need it proofread, or do you need developmental editing?
    2) Is that you cannot afford to pay expensive prices for proofreading, or that you can't afford anything?
    3) If you are planning on using Kickstarter, that could/fund this.
    4) If not, you can conside barter with someone else. You do something, possibly even editing, for them in exchange for proofreading.

    Hope this helps.

    \nEero mentioned Villages too. I had a look at it and I'm in the middle of setting up my account. \n
    Can you pitch your game, to see if there are people potentially interested in it? What's is about? What do the players do? etc.
    \nThe setting is somewhere between DRYH's Mad City and Paris during the French Revolution. It was inspired by Victor Hugo's "Les Miserables", Charles Dicken's "A Tale of Two Cities" and the old TSR "Planescape: Torment" crpg. Oh, and Ralph Mazza and Mike Holme's "Universalis" was also an inspiration for how I designed the system.

    Here's two passages from my game that describe what the game is about:

    1.0 What You Do.
    De\ufb01ance is a game of cooperative storytelling where participants are player narrators role-playing rebels. You build stories by creating a city setting, deciding on a problem needing to be solved, and describing the actions of your rebel. Good stories unfold by fostering a balance between narration and role-playing.

    4.0 Theme.
    The underlying theme that runs through almost all stories built from playing De\ufb01ance is corruption. The people and institutes that govern and rule the city are corrupt, but so are the rebels role-played by the player narrators. Rebels are deeply flawed characters. For all their talk of freedom or equality, they still hunger for power [...]

    With corruption a theme at the heart of every story, you might assume that De\ufb01ance is about power corrupting, but this is not true. It is instead about how powers allows the already corrupted to extend the harm they can do to others. The actions of rebels cause a great deal of misery and suffering to their families and neighbours. From this angle, it becomes clear that the difference between rebels and corrupt authorities is only a matter of degrees.\n

    \nI wish I had helped others in the past. I guess I've always been hesitant to do so because I've not felt that I have the skill to be of any use. About the only thing I have any background in is playing and running games and as much as I'd like to do playtests for other people's games, I can't because I don't have a gaming group. That just leaves proofreading and editing, and as I just mentioned, I don't feel too confident in my ability to do either. But I am willing to give them a try if someone does need help and they don't mind my lack of experience.
