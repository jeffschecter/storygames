    When you take some of Toyota's dirty opium, roll+Hard. On a 10+, no problem, either your stuff was clean or your body fought it off. 7-9, not too bad, you're only afflicted til the drugs are out of your system. 6-, you're Lovesick for Toyota and her stuff.
    \nThough I feel like Weird should be your stat when tripping out on a psychedelic and maybe a "success" roll while on drugs could actually cause lots of trouble. Thinking if you're on meth or bath salts, super paranoid and almost psychotic, then a successful move to read a sitch could actually be your fucked up mind interpreting something that isn't there. A failed roll would be you coming to your senses and not getting caught up in your paranoia.

    \n I kinda have a drug. More like a chemical.

    I had this machine shop where the techno-cult had its main base, and my Maestro D' was lured there. In there, it was full of all sorts of mysterious fumes. I'd prepared this as a threat move for the Techno-Cult front:
    "Any character exerting themselves physically in [the machine shop] forcibly rolls opens their brain to the Psychic Maelstrom."
    The Maestro D' succumbed to the 7-9, thankfully. Got to really establish just how dangerous this techno-cult could be.

    And yeah, my move for Toyota that huckabees just pointed out. I picked Hard cuz I know the dude very likely to be targeted by it is pretty decent at Hard. Weird could totally work overall as well.

    \n In my Town of Gaga game, there was a drug called "city dope" or nastier names that was extracted from the heart of the dead city. It was some sort of mind-controlling technology that the city was using to try to get itself rebuilt, not understanding that a giant high-tech city can't be rebuilt by a handful of crazed survivors.

    When characters used it, it started a countdown clock starting with a pleasant high, then +1 forward for any action related to exploring or restoring the city, then dreams and obsessions about the city... you get the idea. I didn't take the clock all the way to seizures and death because I thought it was more interesting to play out addiction and obsession without the threat of automatic death hanging over it. After all, heroin is plenty bad in the real world without killing everybody who uses it.

    City Dope also acted as a threat front among the Gaga citizens, I think an Affliction, acting like a cult centered on the dead city as more and more people became obsessive city-builders.

    \n Nice! Copied here:\n
    When you try to get off the junk, roll+hard.
    On a 10+, hold five. On 7-9, hold two. Hold may be spent one-for-one:
    - you can choose not to shoot up this week and still not take harm
    - take +1 forward to help someone else try to get off the junk
    - take +1 forward in violence against your source

    On a miss, you give up and party with your junkie friends. Mark -1 barter and you and everyone you shoot up with takes +1 Hx with each other.
