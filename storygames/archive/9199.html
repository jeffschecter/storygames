    \n Judd,

    I think our grid was...

    Issue: DIY/Non-establishmentarian movement
    Issue: Apathy
    Issue: Intentional Communities
    Shock: Immersive, Overlaid Virtual Reality
    Shock: Alien Overlords

    Earth was held hostage by apathy and technological immersion long before the alien overlords arrived.
    Well, overlord, I should say. The extra-dimensional fungus was technically a single being, iterated through many temporary bodies.
    It decided that Earth would function better if its systems were standardized and re-structured.
    It took the liberty of initiating this process for us.
    We didn't care one way or the other.
    After all, the overlords gave us our current virtual reality overlays, which we love and utilize daily (but would never become dependent on! Psssh!)
    The overlord/s (The Benefactors) had amazing technologies (some related to matter reorganization, some virtual reality...), and would give them out along with their orders for the restructuring of our world.

    It's just... well, The Benefactors are an extradimensional fungal shamble, with alien views and technologies.
    Their orders make no sense to us, because we make no sense to them.
    And so we were often appointed to jobs we couldn't reasonably do, asked to perform tasks that were daunting and/or impossible.

    But, despite our apathy, we were quick to work together. After all, we wanted to cash in on favours down the road... when the Benefactors sent us an order.

    After being displaced (communities were often moved or re-organized), some people would snap or become hostile.
    There was a small but growing "fuck the orders, I'm going to live where I want to live", DIY, anti-establishmentarian movement.
    That's part of the DIY/Non-establishmentarian movement.
    The other part was that our previous corporations, etc broke down with the Benefactors frequent and bizarre restructurings. As a result, it was necessitated that we do a lot more as small groups, in small communities, with the resources at hand (which now included all of the Benefactors lovely technologies!)

    So:
    Almost everyone is a virtual reality junky, and quite apathetic.
    Thus, no one really minded when we were taken over.
    The Benefactors are a fungal mass that are trying their best to systematize and perfect our societies.
    We're given crazy orders by the Benefactors, who don't really "get" humans that well.
    We band together to complete the orders, but out of fear of consequence, not initiative (we're apathetic but essentially obligated)
    Those who actually have the initiative to go out in the world and do stuff can really get a lot done, as people are pretty malleable to suggestion and command nowadays.
    We have cool technologies!
    Did I mention virtual realities?

    \n Josh,

    It ended like we spent three hours making this up, and our actual play lasted 1-2 scenes each. We were tired, and some of us were even loopy tired.
    None of us had it as a top priority to play characters that evening.
    It was clear that our joy was in the manufacture of this setting.

    And so we didn't really play in it, but we were all still really pleased with the session.

    I was a social worker from this underpriviledged neighbourhood with lots of gang violence.
    We were ordered by the Benefactors to leave, and new people were ordered to demolish our old home and construct a new community.
    I decided that I missed the bad, ole' streets... and contacted some of my previous clients and suggested we take back our old 'hood.
    By force if necessary.
    My one scene was when me and these two sketched-up thugs stroll into our old neighborhood to find it replaced by a lots-of-volunteer-tended-public-green-space, lively-social-support-systems-evidently-at-work, family-friendly hippy haven... and are totally freaked out and decide that we need to take some drastic action to restore it to its former... whatever.
