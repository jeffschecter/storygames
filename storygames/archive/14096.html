    \n I like it when people have powers that don't fit neatly with their role, but they make them work. Superhero shows are all up on the "your power makes your internal conflict external" thing, but I like the idea of the people just getting these powers at random, and people are left with an ability that doesn't suit them at all.

    Like, maybe Carol's power isn't something that makes her great at fighting? Or does "the tough one" mean she's the fighter?

    Empathy? She can feel what other people are feeling, and she uses that to be a great trainer?
    Precognition? She can see possible futures, up to five minutes ahead?

    Or do you want some "big deal" power?

    My favorite super power of all time is the dude from Powers who has super memory. They ask him about some obscure piece of graffiti, and he's like "Yes, I've seen that twice. Once was in this location at this time, and the other one was over there at this other specific time." It was cool!

    I also like Nathan from "Misfits" and his

    Posted By: JDCorleyWhat I'm not sure about with Adam is what he's after in general.
    \nAdam's the friend of one of the Leads, so one thing to think about is -- what is Mikael after, what kind of guy is he, who would be his friend?

    I'd have to think that Adam's going to act at least a bit like his friend does. He's going to come from a similar background, he's going to like a lot of the same things, he's going to have a similar sense of humor, similar values, etc.: if Mikael's a punk, then Adam's going to be someone who can mix well with punks. More anarchic and freethinking and less autocratic and calculating, or more defiant/idealistic and less conformist/capitalist. So first, I'd look at what Mikael cares about most -- if he's all about fighting The Man, then whatever Adam's goals are should at least include a little bit of fighting The Man, too. There's room for them to disagree on the methods, but they should at least share some common ground. You shouldn't be wondering "Why are you two friends, anyway?" every time the two of them appear in a scene: even if it's a total put-on by Adam, he has to at least present a convincing appearance of friendship. (And if it IS a total put-on, you might want to get the player to buy in on that up front so you can both go heavy on the subtext and really enjoy the ensuing backstabbery.)

    Then there's Adam's relationship to Minerva, which sounds at least partially antagonistic (people who disappear typically don't want to be caught). Maybe Adam's going to be a wedge between Mikael and Minerva, with Mikael being totally "I trust you, man" but having to balance that against Minerva's desire to stay off-camera? So again, looking at what Minerva cares about, it might be good to have Adam hold some opposing viewpoints, or to be undecided but leaning against whatever Minerva's values are. That friction between Minerva and Adam is like a two-for-one special, Mikael has to have an opinion about it one way or the other, and boom, now Minerva and Mikael have issues they need to deal with. Relationship drama all over the place.

    Not knowing what the Leads care about, it's hard for me to come up with a solid concept for Adam. But maybe there are so many mutants in Adam's life not because he's ambivalent about them, but because he's infatuated with them: they're an oppressed minority, they have a lot of visual flair (they bring their own special effects!), and they're very secretive, which makes them fascinating and exotic and worth filming. Maybe he wants to be part of that scene, to document it and demystify it and make them (and him) celebrities? Or maybe he's one of those guys who's convinced that the news doesn't report what's really happening, and that he'll get access to the real stories by tagging along with the X-Men? Maybe he feels powerless (any kid who wants to shoot documentaries for a living probably does), and sees mutants as a resource that could help him accomplish significant social change?

      \n Leslie's dream is humans and mutants getting along peacefully, blah blah blah...but why? Because he's seen the worst of it first-hand: his brother had an X-gene, and was strung up by a mob for it. Carol and Emma, they see anti-mutant prejudice and think about how to resist it: all Leslie thinks about is dead children swinging from lampposts.

      Mutants who are on the edge of slipping into crime or terrorism or who just plain don't get why it's so important that mutants not be merely tolerated but truly accepted -- those are the ones he most wants to get to, before they slip into a life that leads inexorably to anti-mutant riots and innocents getting caught in the crossfire. If Carol's teaching the kids how to defend themselves and Emma's teaching them whatever it is that Emma teaches them, Leslie's the one who wants them to learn context and to see the bigger picture. For him, it's all about doing the right thing at the right time to promote peace and acceptance and prevent bloodshed and prejudice.

      And don't fool yourself, he may come off as a touchy-feely peace-and-love milquetoast, but the man is a fucking iron-willed powerhouse. He's got friends and informants keeping tabs on things all over the world, and he's a planner. Even Emma can't keep track of all the balls he's got up in the air, although she'll probably pretend she does. Leslie understands psychology, history, politics, and can read data and interpret polls like a pro.

      Basically, he's who Professor X wanted to be, if he hadn't been wrapped up in his own penny-ante telepathic bullshit all day. This team is his idea, and if it works out it'll be the fulcrum upon which he's going to put a very long lever and move the whole damn world.

      If he's a mutant (I could see going either way on that), I'd give him an offbeat power that isn't covered by any of the Leads. Technopathy or Teleportation or Duplication or one of the Control powers, maybe; something that he won't use casually, but keeps up his sleeve for an emergency or as a hidden resource. Oh, and something to block Telepathy, no matter what (Gear or an Ability), to explain why he can disagree with Emma without being made into a sock puppet.

      \n Hey, so if you're interested, I did a little playset/flashback thing for Carol's past. Here's the character packets. Basically this side story is how Carol got drummed out of the Army on their Don't Ask Don't Tell-a-like mutant policy.

      Yes, it does use the events of X2. Fuck that movie, god it's so bad.

      Anyway.

      Afghanistan, Three Years Ago

      The events are basically:

      * Nightcrawler attempts to assassinate the President. The Army goes on high alert, and tensions in the ranks explode as mutants are singled out by other soldiers.

      * Taliban-backed insurgents take this opportunity, when things are in disarray, to attack. In their attack, they free a mutant from a local temporary detention center. He was mistreated by the town the PCs are protecting and comes back for personal revenge.

      * Isolated and cut off from support, they have to get civilians out of the valley in order to get them to a hospital (and also call for help), under attack from IEDs and rockets.

      It's relatively short, and emphasizes my mission-based/leader-based hack for Smallville rather than Wedges, but there are enough tensions over the mutant issue (and the fact that one just tried to assassinate the President) that there should be a bit of back and forth too.

      \n Cool, glad you like it. Here's a summary of the Distinctions I wrote up for the prequel. I didn't do three full Triggers for each, some only have one or two.

      Wheelman - Daddy ran the whiskey in a big block Dodge. If it's got wheels, from a Humvee to a Six-by, you can drive it, hang it off the edge, build it up, break it down, fix it, and squeeze every ounce out of it.

      Triggers:
      * Earn a plot point when you Reveal that your vehicle is breaking down.
      * Add a d6 to Trouble to Reroll any die in a pool when you are behind the wheel.


      Big Mouth - Ha ha ha, Christ! Roll this when getting people's cooperation, confidence, intimidating your opponents, or having a good time.

      Triggers:
      * Increase your Angry or Afraid stress pool and add a d6 to Trouble as you wind up your opponent.
      * Earn a plot point when your big mouth gets you into trouble you can't handle alone.

      Combat Medic - Lay still, stay down. Roll to help patients, diagnose and treat trauma, and stay cool under fire.

      Triggers:
      * Earn a Plot Point when you Choose to enter harm's way to treat a patient.
      * Remove a d6 from Trouble when you Choose to treat an enemy combatant or prisoner.

      Lateral Thinker - Guns make you stupid. Duct tape makes you smart. Roll when you are trying to bypass, subvert, or trick opposition rather than overcome resistance directly.

      Triggers:
      * Spend a Plot Point to Reveal you anticipated the opposition's action.
      * Increase your Angry or Exhausted pool and add a d6 to Trouble - you knew this would piss them off.

      Tech-Head - Cover me, I'm going for the data. Roll when dealing with electronics, computers or other gadgetry.

      Trigger:
      * Spend a Plot Point to Reveal that you have a gadget that will help in this situation.

      Streetwise - Heroin has ruled here for a thousand years. Roll when gathering information, performing or dealing with underground, illegal or shady activities.

      Triggers:
      * Earn a Plot Point when you Reveal you owe someone a shady favor.
      * Increase your Insecure or Afraid pool and add a d6 to Trouble by using street tactics.

      Furtive - I have something to hide. Roll this when you are doing something stealthily, trying not to be noticed, or trying to trick someone without suspicion.

      Trigger:
      * Add a d6 to Trouble to Reroll your Furtive die

      \n If you need something more prosaic, then drop the Hell angle (if we've learned anything from the comics, it's that X-Men + Mephisto = DUMB) and make Earl's deal with some antagonist you were already planning on introducing eventually. Earl cut a deal with them, planning some kind of Fiasco-esque screwjob that would really only profit himself, but died before he could pull it off and/or fuck things up even worse. Now he's haunting the band because he feels guilty about leaving them on the hook for what he did, but has absolutely no clue how to fix it.

      The band's going to have to figure out what Earl promised, who he promised it to, and how they're going to get out of this mess. And Earl? He's not going to be able to move on unless he can earn their forgiveness for what he did to them.

      I figure this angle lets you make Earl's story something that the band has to care about: they can't just shrug and say, "Fuck Earl, he's dead, it ain't our problem." And then you can use Earl as either an ally or an antagonist depending on what the scene actually needs. (Earl's not a planner, but he is a schemer; if he can eke out something for himself out of this mess, he will. Or Earl's really trying to help, but he's so confused and so bad at planning that he's just making things worse. Or he's doing his best to protect and care for the people he now realizes were the closest thing he ever had to a family.)
