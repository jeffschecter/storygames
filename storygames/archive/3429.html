\n My wife's at the American Library Association conference this weekend. She's a young adult librarian and has run some gaming-themed programs, so she was sort of stoked to check out the Wizards of the Coast booth in the vendor area. She found it, and they were pushing their young adult fantasy fiction hard. "Where's your stuff for gamers?" Autumn asked.

"Oh yeah," said the WotC rep, "we still make that stuff."

    \n I have heard from a somewhat reliable source that WotC have plans to stop making true pen & paper RPGs altogether by the end of the decade. My friend is not directly affiliated with the industry, but does have a lot of inside contact. I've watched him say things like this through the years and he's managed to be right most of the time, but it should still be taken with a grain of salt.
    \n
    \nIt's obvious that if that is their plan, however, they could pull it off pretty easily. Dungeon & Dragons has become a pretty major identity in the toy and hobby industry. The sales of things like the "miniatures" game (if you want to call cheap, plastic, pre-painted lumps of things that vaguely resemble a human figure a "miniature"), software, and fiction books with the brand have all been progressively overshadowing the RPG for quite a while now.
