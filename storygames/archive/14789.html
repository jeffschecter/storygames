\n A small town on the edge of nowhere.

A secretive cabal of wizards who protect it with their spells from the dangers that lurk in the isolated countryside nearby.

They occasionally take in apprentices.

Just now, a huge demonic force gated in and attacked the wizards' tower. The old pointy-beards took them down, but died in the process.

You are the apprentices. Go.

***

System stuff: Double-max 1st level HP. Everyone must be a specialist wizard, no other classes allowed, and no repeating specialties. Most importantly, your Vancian spell-casting chart denotes encounter powers, not daily ones.

    \n Ok, so if you don't want Burning Wheel, than I'd say 3e, specifically the Pathfinder flavor. Lots of good stuff for the specialists in there, and all the new class info is in the various SRDs floating around out there.
    \n
    \n2e I like specifically because if you use a number of the optional rules, Wizards are not good at being self reliant, and need others to keep them alive. Pathfinder/3e's biggest flaw is the Wizard eventually makes a mockery of everyone else, which in this case is not actually a flaw, but is instead an asset.
