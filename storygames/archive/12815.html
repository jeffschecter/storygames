\n After playing Action Castle at Brennan's place on Saturday, last night I wound up running it for four people unfamiliar with Parsely... from memory. It worked really well, actually. I was able to remember accurately where all the items were and what they had to be used for, as well as the layout of the map. I even remembered some funny details, like how climbing the tall tree takes "a looooooong time." The main problem was that I didn't always know exactly what to say in response to the players trying moves that I knew were incorrect. Generally I just made stuff up and it worked fine. "The Guard isn't interested in talking," etc. Perhaps more problematically, I wasn't sure which actions were supposed to get them killed as opposed to simply not making progress. They did get killed by the Troll once, by trying to fight him bare-fisted, but I think maybe either the Guard or the Ghost should have killed them at another point based on what they were doing. Overall, though, it was really fun and I'm definitely looking forward to running it more, buying more scenarios, and making up my own. Am planning on running Action Castle for my advisory of 9th-graders at work sometime this year, maybe early in the year as a group bonding activity.

Matt

    Posted By: skinnyghost "I'm sorry, I don't know how to XXX"
    \nI always like "I don't see an [thing just explicitly mentioned in the text] here." Anything you don't want the players fiddling with just wasn't programmed as an object in the game. The mention of the princess's bed? Pure color. You can't sit on the bed, burn it or even look at it, because as far as the game is concerned, there's no bed there.

    It's frustrating to the players, but it also channels them toward productive ends, so it serves two purposes.

    Posted By: skinnyghostGET POLE - OUT - SOUTH - GET ROSE - WEST - CLIMB TREE (etc)
    \nThat's all wrong. You want (let's see): Get pole - exit- get rose - south - use pole on pond - N - N - climb tree - get branch - down - E - give fish to troll - E - hit guard with branch - take key - E - take candle - W - D - light lamp - D - light candle - take crown - up (possibly twice) - up - unlock door with key - up [or enter] - give rose to princess - give crown to princess - D (possibly twice) - E - E - sit on throne.

    I think that's it, from memory.

    Which specific commands you need actually depends on who's doing the parsing. When my friend ran Jungle Adventure he was much more brutal in the "Lost in the Jungle" stuff than Jared was when I saw him run it. The players still got through and mapped the jungle fine, using old-school Zork "dropping items in the maze of twisty passages" type maneuvers.
