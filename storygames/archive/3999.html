\n This GenCon was my first GenCon. And, wow, it unbelievably rocked beyond anything I had expected, and if you need further proof, you can see me gushing in just about every post GenCon thread here.

While at the con, I was thinking "Next time is Origins, and maybe GenCon every two or three years." But I'm STILL coming down from the gaming high. I know Origins is more RPG centric, but I have a hard time of it living up to the GenCon dealer room, various story game/indie RPG demos, and last and certainly not least, the quality games and people to be found in Games on Demand and Embassy Suites (Sweets?)

So what's generally a better gaming experience, GenCon or Origins? Where is my vacation time and money better spent? Specifically from a story gaming perspective.

I have no doubts I'll get to Origins at some point just to experience it, so I'm looking for "You should go because its awesome" not "You should go because you haven't been."

    \n Yeah, some in the pods, some in the Hyatt. Note that the only other option here would be to put RPGs in one of the halls somehow. But that would be a bad thing. Just because they have the big halls dedicated to boardgames and minis doesn't mean that it's really so heavily boardgamey. Recall that Origins predates RPGs (as does GenCon, interestingly). As such RPGs are always said to be an "invader" to Origins. But that sentiment died in actuality about fifteen years ago, and people just still talk about it. Now what remains is just the classic tendency for gamers to cordon themselves off into smaller groups, lamenting that their group is shrinking due to losses to the other groups (when, in fact, this isn't true, and losses are due to other factors entirely).
    \n
    \nThey're "game conventions." Not RPG conventions, or boardgame conventions. Maybe they're even "hobby game conventions" as Origins stipulates. I dunno. All I can say is that there are more RPGs played at Origins than any other convention that I can think of except for GenCon. So saying that it's not a RPG convention... I'm not sure what point there is to that.
