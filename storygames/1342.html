    Wow, this is a good thread! Trying not to repeat some of this, I'd really like to learn how to *play* some of the great games out there, from the designers themselves. The idea of "classes" strikes me as a fantastic one. I'd love to take a class in how to play Dogs in the Vineyard from Vincent, for example. I've read the game, I've got what I think is a decent handle on it, but I think it would be very fruitful to have the designer "run" a game demo either here or on the Forge, walking through each part of actual play, with the student(s) asking questions. I think it might be a valuable long-term resource.

    Beyond that, "classes" in the various subjects discussed would also be awesome. I note there are a few threads along this lines already, like the "book format" discussion John Harper started. To incent would-be teachers into agreeing, I would point out that it could be very informative for them to take a look at *how* they do what they do so well; I know a lot of people that work on instinct, but I suspect even that is transmissable.

    Other than that, I want to learn:
    What to Do Next from Clinton.
    How to Know When I'm Awesome from Remi.
    How to Known When I'm Finished Designing a Game from Vincent and Luke.
    How to Know When It's Really Not Working from all of you.
