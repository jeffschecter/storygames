\n Apologies for being a bit Scandocentric here, but for those who read Norwegian (I know there are a few here who do), there's a great thread by Kaia Aardal who's currently playing a X-mas calender RPG with her two sons (ages 7 and 5). Every day they play for a little under an hour, with a high degree of player involvement and control. "What world do you want to go to today?" "Star Wars! And tomorrow, we go to the Titanic! No, to Warhammerland!" One of the characters is called Winnie the Pooh. Legolas was part of today's session, apparently.

    Rare Slave Manuscripts Tell Stories of Escape
    In A Slave No More, historian David W. Blight showcases the emancipation narratives of two men, one from Alabama and one from Virginia. Manuscripts written by Wallace Turnage and John Washington, and genealogical information compiled by Blight, combine to tell the stories of their lives as slaves and their harrowing flights to freedom.

    Family Memories of Men Who Were Slaves
    We continue our conversation about A Slave No More and the men whose stories it tells with three of John Washington's descendants: granddaughter Ruth Washington, great-granddaughter Barbara Anne Hinksman, and great-great-granddaughter Maureen Ramos.

It's only his unfortunate lack of immortality that kept him from writing about lolcats, parkour, and WWII. So, trying to create a virtual "Shakespeare-land" in Merrie Old Englande seemed like a bad idea to start with, since it leaves out a lot of favorites -- unless you have separate annexes for ancient Rome, Denmark, Venice, and Scotland.

      \n OK, this is console talk. But I've been a big fan of Counter-Strike from way back when.

      Anyway, I just installed Steam again, and what do I find? A zombie mod on its way on the 28th, and better than that, a mod for HL2 called "The Hidden". It is fucking fantastic! Basically, 2-9 people are the members of this British swat unit called IRIS, and the last player is The Hidden. The IRIS members get guns and shotguns and stuff. And the hidden has only a knife. Thing is, he is invisible, and you can only see him (a vague outline, fainter than "Predator", more like a mist) when he moves.
