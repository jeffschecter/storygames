    \n I love Sleep is Death, but I've never played it as a Player, just as a GM (about ten times). I still say it's an awesome tool, and the only reason it blipped and dried up so fast is because everyone found it pretty hard to match up and play with friends.

    In its earlier incarnation (it might be fixed now) it was pretty impossible to connect without you and the other using 3rd party VPN tools to create a private tunnel. Things might have gotten better in an update since then though.

    One thing I terribly suggest for your first game: Change your INI to allow for 120 seconds instead of 30. You can always hit "done" if you get finished in 30 secs anwyay, but honestly 30 secs is too fast for your first few times. Even when I got good at it, I was calling for minute-spaced turns as GM.

    There was a website where you sign in and it paired you up super easy-peasy. Only problem was that the waiting list for GMs was ZERO (you GM a game, boom you are instantly paired) but the Player line (not an ordered line, mind; you had to refresh the page and wait for an opening; even hitting F5 LITERALLY every second (!!!), someone would still be faster than you) was always huge.

    Let me dust it off, see if it got better about matchmaking.

    EDIT: sidtube was the awesome site with all the SiD content; but it looks like it's down now (just two years? that's balls).

    Also, did some research, looks like the "Hamachi" (now "LogMeIn") free VPN product is what will help you and your friend connect easily to each other even through NAT'ed routers.
    https://secure.logmein.com

    -Andy
