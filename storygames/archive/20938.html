\n Hi all

I'm currently funding the 2nd Edition of my game Monkey, The Roleplaying Game of the Journey to the West.

Monkey the roleplaying game of the Journey to the West on Kickstarter.com

This is a story-telling game set in a mythical China, where out of grace Immortals seek redemption by escorting a vulnerable, mortal Monk to distant India. Braving many dangers along the way, to recover scrolls of Buddhist scripture, that are missing from the Chinese canon.The road through the wilderness is dangerous since all of Demon-kind has resolved to stop the pilgrims and the eat the Monk's flesh!

The game's simple rules, which use standard playing cards, allow for any sort of action that you can imagine, from fast paced kung-fu to heated debates.

The main rule book contains all that you need to play, except two packs of standard playing cards (one for the players and one for the referee) and your imagination.

The kickstarter is to pay for the new art that the book(s) require, since the first edition was completely made up from clip art except the cover (which is also being redone), and the development costs for the new and expanded text.

At time of writing, a week on from its initial launch, the main rule book has been funded , along with two first two stretch goals. These were: If you see Buddha on the Road an additional adventure in the main book, which will be the basis of a Quick Start Book as well, and an additional chapter called Defenders of the Dragon Empire detailing how to create and use mortal wuxia heroes with the game.

Currently we are funding the next two stretch goals: The Ministry of Thunder a book of five linked adventures, involving the celestial of the title whose agents hunt down supernatural criminals on Earth, and the Monkey Companion a collection of short adventures and articles that expand the game further.

    \n Hi Rafu,

    I can understand this, given the fact that the Kickstarter at this point is actively advertising the Stretch Goal which is a standalone book of adventures, but the actual game as written doesn't require you to use pre-written adventures.

    Breifly the main rulebook assumes that within the story frame work of the Journey to the West the Narrator and players will create and improvise the story as they go along.

    I did this post to explain how this works:

    23 Days of Monkey, Day 14 Playing the Journey to the West

    BUT alot of folk have told me they want working examples, ie.writen up adventures, to show how the setting works. Most of them have come across the story quickly via one of the films or TV series. So I've included a couple of quick one session scripted adventures in the main rulebook to help get people started.

    How I approach adventures is dealt with in this post, along with how the players can grab narrative control.

    23 Days of Monkey, Day 20 Journeying West

    Hope that helps.
