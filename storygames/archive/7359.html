I'm about half-way through unWritten. The text keeps referring it to as "a game of Film and Literature" and talks about stories in those mediums. I'm curious as to what exactly that means.     \n Thanks for the question Jesse. I wanted to address it here in case anyone else had the same question, which is rather important to unWritten.

    unWritten leads its players through an explicit narrative arc through the use of the Journey Questions, without relying on a specific theme or motif. The games premise, motif, theme, etc. is developed at the beginning of the game, similar to PTA. However, the protagonist's story arc follows a literary structure that is intrinsic to the way that most books and movies are written. Additionally, each protagonist is, explicitly, the lead character in his or her story while still being a support player in the story of the other protagonists. So you have multiple stories being told, each with a single protagonist telling his or her own story.

    Does this answer your question?
