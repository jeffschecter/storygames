    \n A fantasy heartbreaker is a very specific thing. It is a game that:\n
      \n
    • Has been developed by someone with no real experience of games outside a single system, generally
    • This system is (often) DnD
    • The author thinks to have created the end-all-be-all game that fixes all the "broken stuff" in the system he knows, generally reinventing the wheel and rediscovering stuff that has been common in other games for years ("My game has no classes, and no levels, wooot!").
    • The game (And that is the critical part) contains at least one or two really good ideas, buried under all the other stuff.
    • The author, not knowing any better, pours TONS of money in the project, printing thousands of copies that will have no market whatsoever.
    \nWhat makes it a heartbreaker are the last two bullet points: the fact that a good idea is lost, forgotten, among a pile of cruft. And, well the fact that someone wasted a lot of money.
