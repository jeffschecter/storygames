\n I know I'm the last guy to figure this out, but it has made me so excited I just have to say it out loud.

I've struggled often with PTA's series premise stage. Though it has worked to get the game off the ground, I often sacrifice my own interest in the premise (as producer) to just get a game off and rolling. I have players who don't game much and 'just want to get to the playing part'.

Not only do IAWA do the setting and character for you, but by picking either from Blood and Sex, a Nest of Vipers, etc., you also pick tone.

So, rather than making Oracles on our favorite settings, you could play with the differing tonal aspects of each setting. When does this setting employ Comedy? Tragedy? Romance? Mystery?

In today's game, I want to play high fantasy with a dark murder-mystery tone. Next week we'll go back to comedy, or the romantic soap opera, or whatever.

I've noticed this on tv shows, where a serious show will have a silly episode just to let some pressure off (Angel's 'Smile Time' puppet show episode comes to mind).

Anyway, IAWA already does this for itself (except for the comic tone that some sword and sorcery has...but maybe VB meant to keep the focus on serious tones).

Anyway, I've realized now that a good PTA episode lies just an oracle away. Setting, character, and tone, all set up really quickly with lots of prompts and ready-made ideas. And as a producer, I can make it a game I enjoy by creating the overall Oracle group, or picking the Oracle group I want to go with. Or collaborating on making it with the rest of the players on a different night, so that we only have to do it once!
