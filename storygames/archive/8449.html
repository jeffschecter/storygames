    \n Sure, Adam. When I say "Star Wars, I mean the original trilogy. Always and ever. If you pass me on the street and you hear me say the words 'Star Wars" that is what I mean. If I say them in my sleep, that is what I mean. I am very firm on this.

    If I need to further differentiate within the trilogy, then of course I'll use individual names of movies. 'Cause as you say, there's a lot of difference.




    I'm not sure how to describe the requisite "Essential Starwarsness" a game would need. I'll think on that. Meanwhile, let me go over my list:

    Baron Munchausen: Essentially a bragging game, right? "She's the ship that made the kessel run in 8 parsecs," anyone? Also, you're telling stories but from the stance of storyteller, rather than actor, after the fact instead of in the moment. You could totally do a Star Wars game in this vein, in a seedy space bar as grizzled veterans and cocky heroes regale each other with inflated tales of their exploits.

    Best Friends: I initially thought, this is petty rivalries and bickering, as far from the adventure mode of Star Wars as possible, right? then I thought the words "petty rivalries and bickering" again and went, "Holy Shit! Luke and Han and Leia are playing Best Friends for like, the entirety of New Hope!"

    Marvel Superheroes: So this one, I thought, it's pretty keyed to Marvel Comics color, right? But then I though about all the big, bombastic Feats and chart results and realized that while you'd have to rewrite the Powers as Starwars-ish Force abilities and action stunts, the ol' FASERIP would do Star Wars action pretty well.



    Bliss Stage: this one, however, never redeemed itself. It's all about survivalk of trauma and vulnerable, desperate relationships and existential questions. Not Star Wars. Except maybe the 'brother and sister in a love triangle" thing. :P
