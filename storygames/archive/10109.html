Labyrinths & Lycanthropes: Duck Roll City

\n
\n
\n
\n\nRich Stokes \n\n\n
\n
\n\nAugust 2009 \n edited August 2009 in Actual Play
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n There are two things that running Joe "Contenders" Prince's new game L&L; on Monday taught me.
\n
\nFirst is that I can't spell Labyrinths and the second is that whenever you put a blank sheet of paper in front of a group of people and give them pens, someone always draws a comedy penis. I was out of the room while a lot of the map was created, but sure enough, there was an archipelago drawn when I returned, consisting of islands shaped like classic schoolyard knobs. Some wag had named this land "The Paedo Isles" and thus a grand adventure was born.
\n
\nCharacter creation left us with a motley assortment of adventurers:
\n
\nA magical healing Ponycorn called Yayy (With pannier mounted + 1 Healing ice-cream dispensers)
\nPrincess Boris, the ninja were-gnome
\nWaitrose, arch-demon of groceries (with a Trolley of +1 Dash)
\nDurgen Splurgen, Nordic wolf-boy
\nX-40: shoving robot - Playing the Bard.
\n
\nAt this point disaster truck, and Claire was summoned away from the table to collect a swine-flu addled relative from the other end of the country, but the rest of us decided to soldier on heartlessly.
\n
\nA location on the map was chosen, the oddly named "Duck Roll City" which had a picture of a duck with wheels next to it. It was quickly decided that the big boss of Duck Roll City was Lord Turducken, who's specialities were Hack & Slash and Sense. Three level 3 character entering their first dungeon made him level 6.
\n
\nMonster creation was fruitful, and netted us a selection of critters with names I cannot possibly post on the internet because of their general gutter-mindedness, plus a level 7 magic "Blast" monster called a Trevor, a level 3 "Shoot" Miffed Duck and a level 1 "Hack" monster called "ARGH! FUCKING ANTS!!!"
\n
\nThe players entered Duck Roll City and were immediately set upon by merchants selling cheap Hoi-sin and orange sauce. After enquiring upon the freshness of the goods, Waitrose was told that they were frozen and imported from Ice-Land, home of his sworn enemies, the Mumms. This kicked things off nicely, and a fight ensued which wrecked the market and left many baddies with unmentionable names dead or dying.
\n
\nPlay continued, with many enemies succumbing to Waitrose's Blast and Boris' Sneak Attacks. Even the harmless group of NPCs who just wanted a favour were mercilessly butchered for their stuff. Eventually even Lord Turducken was laid low by the party.
\n
\nPlans were made to visit the next Labyrinth, but alas: the hour was late and it was time to go home.
\n
\nOverall, this game was a blast, but the learning-curve seemed a little steep. To be fair, that's mainly because, (being exceptionally busy and lazy) I'd played this briefly about a year ago, and hadn't actually read the book until I started running the game. If I'd taken the time to read the book properly ahead of time we'd have found things a lot easier. Oh well, that's what I get for being lazy.
