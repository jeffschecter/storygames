    \n I always hide the sheet from the players. Way more fun that way

    When you say, "Tell the truth", are you talking about the "Always say what your proposed mystery and arcs demand" and "Always say what honesty demands"? I interpret those as meaning I need to give the hunters the information they've earned through their actions (or their failure to act).

    So I interpret "demand" to mean what is demanded when we're playing moment by moment, rather than releasing the information all at once.

    \n Yup, definitely make the characters work to reveal the mystery!

    One principle I've found useful in MotW mystery creation: keep dropping bodies (or otherwise having more incidents of monster mayhem -- the monster carrying people off can be just as good). It does a lot to keep the players from turtling (i.e. holing up because the threat seems too great) and does a lot to keep them from getting stuck because each new scene will (or should) offer new avenues of investigation.

    In campaign play, mix it up with subtler threats, but for one-shots or beginning games, I'd stick to repeated overt manifestations of the threat.
