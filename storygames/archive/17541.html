\n This happens to me sometimes:

I want to post an idea or thought, and then I get afraid that I might provoke people and get into a huge internet fight, filled with bad emotions and making everyone look like idiots. I have deleted a lot of such posts over time.

However, some of them contain ideas that I would like to examine. I would like, somehow, to create a specific social and mental space for these ideas, where they are divorced from both my and others' identities. I want the idea to exist separately, so that if people attack it, they don't attack me; and so that the idea is not seen as my attack on anyone. I would like us to be able to examine the idea as if it came from nobody, and was addressed to nobody; as if it was a strange animal that just walked into our camp, and we'd find out together if we had any use for it.

Examples of hypotheses that could be posted (I don't believe in or support any of these personally, AFAIK, they're just examples): "Story games promote a slave morality". "Our community makes people more sporty." "The reason men do X is Y."

The first thing I'm thinking is an anonymous forum. I've been wanting to set up something like that for a long time. However, anonymous fora might have a lot of other interesting effects/problems...

    \n Creating an anonymous forum involves, well, the issues of creating a new forum. There's no point to doing that if the user-base you want is here.

    Starting a thread anonymously is as simple as enlisting the help of another user, who's going to write something to the effect of: "I'm just a messenger, here, quoting a post from a user who'd like to stay anonymous."
    The problem would then be, how to allow anonymous participation? The trick can get old pretty fast, plus you'd completely lose the option to use whispers.
