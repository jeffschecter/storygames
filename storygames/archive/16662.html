    \n So, I like the idea of a quickplay zombie game and this looks promising. A few hopefully constructive comments:

    1. The threat level seems like it will be too high to me. Even in round one your scene threat is going to be at least 3 (two times the round number plus one for the minimum number of PCs involved), probably higher. You have to spend that many action points to act. You'll be out of action points pretty damn quick unless you ruthlessly focus on stuff where you have a negative modifier. (That may be deliberate, of course, but it looks like being severely limiting.)

    2. It could use a proof-read. I spotted a few things:
    - You say the threat level is calculated by multiplying the round by two - I guess you mean the round number? Could be a bit clearer.
    - Though it's implicit that they can, you don't actually say whether a player can act more than once in a scene.
    - You refer to the scenario ending when the threat pool is reduced to zero, but the threat pool is based on the round number and could presumably go up indefinitely. Perhaps you meant "round" rather than "scenario", but if so you're repeating yourself. from an earlier para.
    - You refer to ability points increasing on a refresh, should say action points.

    3. I'd say it would be helpful to include some guidance on how to set up and play the game. I don't mean mechanically or procedurally, just, like, how to frame a good scene, maybe some examples of how play works, that kind of thing.

    Good luck with it!
