\n I've been hitting the gym hard, running on an Elliptical for an hour, burning 600+ calories and... reading RPGs.
\n
\nWARNING: This post is iPhone specific. If you don't care about iPhones, or the gym, this thread may not be interesting.
\n
\n
\nHow?
\n
\nI use GoodReader.
\n
\nSpecifically it lets me grab a PDF, strip out the layout, turn 2 columns into 1 column, change the font size & color, remove all graphics, and then automatically scroll the page at any speed I desire so I can run while reading hands free.
\n
\nAmazing! I didn't know it could do all these wonderful things... I found out by tapping random buttons.
\n
\n
\nWhich buttons? Just 3:
\n
\n Remove all images, layout, and formatting (except line breaks) without left/right scrolling and with the font size of your choice.
\n
\n Autoscroll your text to read without touching the screen.
\n
\n Choose your font sizes, colors, and scrolling speeds.
\n
\n
\nWhat else?
\n
\n Crop excess margins.
\n
\n After zooming to your desired width, enable Horizontal Scroll Lock so you can only scroll up and down. Great if you want to read a PDF while keeping its graphical layout intact but only want to read 1 column at a time.
\n
\n Add notes to your PDF with popup notes and text highlights.
\n
\n Email yourself a summary of all your notes (annotations).
\n
\n Search your notes and see a summary of all your notes.
\n
\n Bookmark information to reference later.
\n
\n Switch between day and night modes (different screen brightness, font, color, and background settings of your choice).
\n
\n
\nThis is all fantastically useful to me. It's not uncommon for me to run, read, listen to podcasts, check email, and occasionally make forum posts back and forth or at the same time. I'll often get ideas while running, so I type in notes directly into GoodReader or switch to NoteMaster (which syncs with Google Docs).
\n
\nReading 40+ pages of RPG rules while getting my daily exercise is a huge time saver!

    \n So, here's some interesting data: I messed around with Goodreader for a bit, found a great balance between large font size and auto-scroll speed (in the "text view" mode). I also got on a *gasp!* elliptical machine!

    Turns out that Reading your RPGs on Goodreader, while it might cause you to lose focus a bit (Re John's observations of -20%; I think it can be countered if you make a conscious effort to keep pushing yourself, maybe? Might be hard to do that AND focus on the read, though), it does one wonderful thing:

    I used to love elliptical machines because it kept me away from the IRON, which was heavy and raw and scary. Now I embrace the IRON, and yet I'm still out of shape and flabby so I totally need to up my game, hence I am now working out more and using the ellipticals more. B

    ut one of the reasons I didn't want to go back to the elliptical (even over running) for a long time was that the passage of time felt sooooo slooooow. I would give it my all for what seemed to be an eternity, only to find out that only three minutes passed. Watching the little TVs while working out helped a little, but most gym TV is stupid.

    Turns out that Goodreader-ing my RPGs makes time pass quickly. I dug into a dozen or so pages of Anima Prime, and next thing I know 20 minutes are gone, POOF, and I'd burned 200 calories.

    Thanks John: You friggin win the internet. I am now an Elliptical-usin' Goodreader RPG readin' fool.
