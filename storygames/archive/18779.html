\n So, there's this whole community of people who play these tabletop RPGs with the qualities that we like.

The community isn't just These Forums, nor are these forums the "center" of that movement. It's a movement scattered across the internet and meatspace, a social expansion. Lots of activity happens here, but lots also happens on G+, the public posts of private bloggers, and so on.

There's lots of folks who browse these forums just for the unique "Stuff to Watch" threads, which are awesome. But we want to also move the awesome from being limited to these forums and help people be passively aware of what's interesting these days. Rather than actively having to follow several forums, G+ communities, blogs, etc, we're hoping to help people sit back and get pinged on various interesting things that happened over the past few weeks.

Some of it will be commercial, like new games or crowdsourced projects that people find interesting, some of it will be discussions of interest across the internet (including threads here, G+, etc). It'll be simple and short. All of us modstaff (me, Kira, John S, Jason, Bethany, James) contributed to it in some way, it represents a lot of eyes and ideas. We've been thinking about it for months, and working on it for weeks, and it's ready.

It's an email newsletter, and it'll be in your inbox in a few hours. This is a heads up.

To get momentum on this, we've used an abridged list of emails pulled from the user list here, including both folks active and inactive here.
Even as we get 5-10 new human users here a week, there are many who have left for various reasons. To that end, we've made sure that along with the standard "stupid-simple one-click opt-out button" at the bottom, we're also going to put a bolded link at the TOP of the email as well for the first two "issues". If you never want to see the newsletter again, just click on that button, you'll be unsubscribed, you'll never be contacted again.
Apologies in advance for any tension that creates: We want to bridge in the people who came and left as they weren't interested in forums as their news/content delivery system (but still interested in the scene), and this was the best way to get started. If you left because of other issues/stressors, our deepest apologies, just click on that one button and you'll never get another update from us or this site.

We'll have details once the first one goes out for how to subscribe, for example if you have more than one email address and want to use another one vs the one you have registered here. We're using Mailchimp at the backend, and we've found them extremely professional and easy to work with (particularly with hassle-free subscriptions and unsubscriptions, and deeper concerns about privacy/identity).

Thanks again, everyone!
