Blade & Crown: 80s realism + modern indie design

\n
\n
\n
\n\nJiawen \n\n\n
\n
\n\nSeptember 2012 \n edited September 2012 in Directed Promotion
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nBlade & Crown is my fantasy RPG that balances 80s realism and indie narrativism. It's a gritty, somewhat crunchy system, with lots of detail, twelve characteristics, a skill list, etc. But it's also strongly influenced by modern, indie games. It has pretty unified mechanics (you almost always roll the dice the same way), and there are lots of active enticements for players to take control of the narrative, put their characters into adventurous situations and help immerse everyone in the game. B&C is available as a PDF on DriveThruRPG, and a print edition should be available soon.

    \n Hi Jiawen, your game looks cool. Looking through your website, it looks as if the game doesn't really have a built-in premise, like dungeon adventuring or what not, but that you create characters with beliefs and goals and then try to accomplish those, similar to games like Burning Wheel and The Riddle of Steel. Does that sound right? Are those the types of games you'd consider Blade & Crown to be similar to in its approach?

    \n Yes, it's definitely a general-purpose RPG. It tends to work best with settings that are early medieval in feel, but it can work with other settings, too; it'd be great if I could get the license to adapt it for Miyazaki Hayao's Valley of the Wind, for example. The game is hopefully driven by characters' goals (and personalities, and destinies, and whatever else players use Traits to represent), though that's flexible. Burning Wheel and Riddle of Steel are certainly games in the same vein, yes.
