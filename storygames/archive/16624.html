\n Amercia, Land of the Fief, Home of the Grave. Each state is a kingdom, each county a fief, the dead are honoured with elaborate tombs, each larger than the other, the words of the ancestors are taken as gospel.

In Burlington County NJ, they live by the words of Alice Paul, "Mr. President how long must women wait to get their liberty? Let us have the fights we deserve." It's pretty rough.

In Greenville County SC they espouse John B Watson as their spiritual leader, 'Give me .. infants ... I\u2019ll ... take everyone at random and train him to become any type of specialist I ... select\u2014doctor, lawyer, artist, merchant-chief and, yes, even beggar-man and thief ...'. Occupations are assigned at birth by the Baron. It's exciting.

And in Yellowstone County MT, it's Plenty Coup's wisdom that is most prized, "The ground on which we stand is scared ground", all the houses, people, even the livestock, wears stilts. It's teetering.

    New York County, NY, of all it's distinguished deceased, elevates the words of George William Curtis above all - "A man's county is not a certain area of land, of mountains, rivers, and woods, but it is a principal; and patriotism is loyalty to that principal." Educators run the infrastructure of the fief. Even after graduation, adults are organized into homerooms and their life is strictly regimented by a bell-system, including 55 minutes of exercise a day, which generally involves throwing rubber balls at unpopular people. It's instructional.
