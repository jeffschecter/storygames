\n At Gen Con, I ran a D&D; 3.5 hack using Keys (Shadow of Yesterday), Bonds (Dungeon World), Stickers, and more.
\n
\nThe Keys were printed on sticky labels. Peel off the ones you want and stick them on your character sheet.
\n
\nI also redesigned the character sheet to make it easier for new players and put focus on character relationships.
\n
\nAs an example, check out the Dwarf Paladin booklet. It's a fast read, 8 pages, large type.

    \n Thanks Jason / Deliverator / Ludanto / Andy / Whiteknife / Judd!
    \n
    \nThe booklets were binded with a transparent plastic cover and hard stock back cover. Character sheets were printed on heavy card stock with a paper texture that matched the printed texture. Players didn't realize that the printed texture was digital and thought that was just the paper itself!
    \n
    \nI handed each player a transparent goblet filled with red "blood" droplets. They could see their goblets fill and empty as their hit points ebbed and flowed. A player near death would often hold up their goblet to signal\u2026 "help!"
    \n
    \nI built 3D terrain on the fly mid game using scissors, clay, grids printed on card stock, and lots of 2 sided tape. Players could attach their minis to the side of walls as the climbed towers. It was pretty wild.
    \n
    \nPlayers wore fake beards for all the Dwarf characters (5 out of 6)!
    \n
    \nIt was wild.
    \n
    \nIron GM itself was amazing. It felt like being at a Rock Concert meets Competitive Sport except we were playing D&D;! Lou, Rone, and Ed did an outstanding job putting together a stellar event.
    \n
    \nI unfortunately had an ever bombarding string of terrible luck and awful experiences that ruined the event for me. I've decided to remain mostly positive about the whole thing but if you are interested in details, whisper me. But my experience was in the minority and not the fault of the organizers. Most people had a tremendous amount of fun.
    \n
    \nTim (ogreteeth on Story Games) of Dice + Food + Lodging (fun podcast, worth giving a listen) took 2nd place. In my eyes, Tim was in 1st place! Several GMs backed out of the event (some because of a registration mixup, a few because they had last minute reservations due to the pressure) and the organizers asked if any players would like to step up and run. Tim stepped up. And with no preparation, on a moment's notice, kicked major ass. He deserves major props!
