Food: What do you eat when gaming?

Andy
March 2006 edited March 2006 in Story Games
I noticed that there's a lot of gamecraft-oriented threads popping up, so I wanted to bring discussion back onto the common level.

So, how does food play into your gaming experience?

Here's how it works for me:

My weekly Thursday group at Beth's house: I eat dinner beforehand, gaming starts at 7:30-8:00, and from there there's a lot of crap candy/junk food which we eat during the night. I've gained a lot of weight in the past five years, and unfortunately I think at least part of that is due to poor choices every Thursday. For some reason, eating crap throughout the night doesn't register on the radar. However, I'm looking for ways around that. Namely, I've come up with:

Self Control | Coffee (light/no sugar) | Veggies | Being more conscious of my eating habits (though it's not at my house usually, so it's hard to remember sometimes)

Every other week's Wednesday group at My house: Starts at 7:00, and since I get off work at 6:00 (others get off work at 5:00), I usually end up eating dinner in front of the others (who usually ate). It's never made much of a problem. And after that, I rarely eat snacks and the like, just water/coffee.

So, half of me is wondering how food habits in your gaming group works, and any dynamics it creates.

The other half is looking for advice on changing an external enviornment to encourage at least self-control, if not to encourage others to eat a little better, too.

-Andy

    Okay, so like, my wife and I are total foodies and more than once drop-in guests that stay for dinner have asked in astonishment, "Do you eat like this, like, all the time?" So... we're kind of weird. Here's what we usually do:

    1) Finger Food at the table -- canapes, fresh-baked bread of various kinds, veggies and dip, dried and fresh fruit, et cetera.

    2) Dim Sum & Friends -- home-made, Andy, home-made. Don't trust those dimsum places in the stripmalls. So we steam/fry/bake a variety of different tasty bits. The advantage of making dim sum at home is that the same ingredient (one carrot, shredded) can be used in three different dishes. It's relatively cheap to turn out six or so different items.

    3) Dinner before, after, or during a break. Occasionally we potluck; other times we just cook up a coordinated meal.

    4) Deserts, usually baking as we play so it's warm and fresh and to die for when we break for desert.

    5) While we've ditched the chips and candy for the most part, the gallons of soda have remained. It's just not gaming unless I'm jumpy from caffeine.

    On the other hand, in my sunday group without my wife, I usually hit up Taco Bell on the way there.

    Usually my groups eat dinner together, either before game or durring a long break. The communal aspect of sharing food is pretty important to our social structure, I think. Games where we don't do a meal together never go as well. (Odd, but true. I only realized it myself recently.)
