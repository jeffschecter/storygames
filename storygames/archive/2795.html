\n I have a story idea for a game that I'd like to run, and I'm at the stage where I need to pick and/or design a system to support the idea.

Basic elevator pitch:

You have two selves: an archetypical fantasy hero trying to save a dying land, AND a townsperson trying to deal with life's troubles in Smalltown, USA. Which one is the dream, and which is reality?

Think The Maxx meets Twin Peaks. The game would switch back and forth between worlds, where victories or defeats in one world echo into the other. At the start of the game, the characters' two sides wouldn't necessarily be aware of each other, but as the game progresses the two worlds would naturally collide. In the fantasy world, the characters would be larger-than-life heroes that face tangible metaphors for the problems plaguing them in the real world. In the Smalltown USA world, the game would focus on relationships between characters and other townspeople, with an added crisis or wierdness (like the death of Laura Palmer in Twin Peaks) lingering in the background.

Anyone have any suggestions for an existing system I could use for this idea? Or thoughts on what the gameplay should focus on (in case I end up designing rules from scratch)?
