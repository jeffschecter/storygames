\n Interview with Peter Adkison on the Iron GM podcast. Listen here. Interview starts at 6:25.
\n
\nFor those who don't know Peter, he started Wizards of the Coast, herald D&D; 3E & Magic the Gathering, and owns Gen Con.
\n
\nPeter and Rone (the interviewer) discuss:
\n
\n- Indie game are great for lazy GMs!
\n- Forge & Gaming Outpost
\n- My Life With Master
\n- Grey Ranks
\n- Burning Wheel
\n- Dogs in the Vineyard
\n- inSpectres
\n- Gaming online
\n
\nI ran the Dogs in the Vineyard game Rone talks about!
\n
\nI'm super excited for Iron GM. GM spots are full but there are a few player spots open.
\n
\nPeter's enthusiasm is quite infectious.
\n
\nRock,
\nJohn

    Posted By: JDCorleyPosted By: jdfristromIt's not obvious. Wizards kind of defines trad, eh?
    \nWut, but plenty of trad (ugh, that word) game designers like non-trad (ugh) games? Never met one that didn't? Seems obvious to me?
    The last few Gen Cons I've run Dogs in the Vineyard for many full time and freelance employees at Paizo, Wizards, and White Wolf. Most of who had never played games like My Life With Master, Dogs, Inspectres, or Grey Ranks. I've met many professionals who have only ever played D&D, only played with the same 4-5 people the entire lives at home, and if they go to conventions, it is usually limited to Gen Con or Origins. Taking it to extremes, some of these employees barely even play D&D. On the flip side, I've gamed with Mike Mearls who loves a huge variety of games and has lots of experience. It depends on the person although in my personal experience someone like Peter is rare. \n
