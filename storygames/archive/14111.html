    \n Do you think that it would work to capture the feel of the genre? I tried to design the game in a way that captured that cinematic heist flavor.
    \n
    \nAesthetically, you roll 2 dice like on a craps table, the GM presents challenges in the form of chips, graphic design of the book, etc.
    \n
    \nMechanically, you are pushing for lucky 7s.
    \n
    \nDo you think that the framework is strong enough to support a traditional style of play AND a narrative style?

    \n Ah. Yes. It felt like an outline. So now I see your approach.

    If you're trying to emulate that kind of film, the mechanics/structure need to reflect an ACT 2.

    So, in ACT 1, the GM reveals "some" of the information about the vault. But in ACT 2, is allowed to change some of those numbers (not all of them) as well as one critical ability that the players couldn't have planned for (perhaps from a fixed list of 11 abilities).

    I think rolling a 3 or 11 should do something, just not as extreme as a 2 or 12.

    And right now, I didn't see a lot of room for the witty banter that really makes these films so successful. But maybe I was rushing through it.

    Is that helpful?
