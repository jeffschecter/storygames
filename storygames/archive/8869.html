\n It seems there have been a few shots at this in the past, but no real shared tools have emerged.

Of late I've been glomming onto PS like a gutter wino to his bottle of Barefoot merlot, and I had an epiphany that, oh man, I could run Burning Planescape! A game all about player priorities expressed through character beliefs in a setting where character beliefs can change the nature of existence? That's chocolate and peanut butter, son.

So, has anyone tried this at the table? The unfortunate thing, despite the oh-shit-I-want-to-play-this factor, is that it seems like a prohibitive amount of work. The best tack seems to just give players only racial options from the BW book, and make sure BIT focus on PS themes, and go from there to see how it works in play.

Oh, and if I'm missing any threads or online resources on this, please share. The above are all I could find.

Do those rules make at least rough sense, so that 6/10 groups might handle things in more or less the same way? If not, you may want to consider starting hacking the rules collaboratively, with your group, ahead of any serious test of the mechanics in question. When you promise your group that they get to "change the nature of existence" and then don't let them, they don't have fun. It's the thing that they'll want to do most, the way you were supposed to make them awesome, and if it's broken, they will be annoyed.

    \n Heya Hansel,

    I'd hit up the posts on the BW wiki about first time play. If your whole group is new to BW then I would recommend reading pgs. 1 -77 of the main book (up to where it says play now) and then run the Sword for your group. Just stick with the rules on pgs 1-77. This way you have throw away characters and people can try out the system to see if they like it.

    There is loads of advice on the wiki for first timers. Once you figure out if BW will work for you and your group then proceed forward with the PS thingy.

    This is stolen from a poster over on the BW forums but it can help draw in your players. These questions need to be answered as a group before your PS game begins.

    "What's the Big Picture? What's going on in this setting that makes it ripe for adventure. What's changing, evolving, declining?

    What's the world's culture? What are the cultural analogs? Analogs can be taken from historical earth, current events or fantasy works.

    What's the conflict in which the characters are involved? What are the sides? What's wrong?

    What physical place does this conflict take place in? What ecology, environment, place?

    What's the name of the most important place in this setting? Not the capital or any dumb shit like that, but THE PLACE where all the action goes down?

    What's the name of a faraway place that folks talk about, dream about or mutter under their breath about?

    Who are the antagonists? Who is opposing the goals of the characters?

    Imagine all of the characters are standing a room/ruin/field with the antagonists or their minions. What do the antagonists want from that meeting? What do the characters want from that meeting?

    What type of magic exists in this world?

    What character stocks are in play in this world? Which are restricted and why?

    What cultural traits apply to the characters of this game world? Pick three character traits for each culture.

    What's your Resources cycle? 1 month, seasonal, 6 months, annual? What's the game world's currency? Who collects the taxes? What do people do for work? What's the major economy?

    Material world: What weapons and armor are available? Are some weapons and armor restricted to certain cultures or character stocks? What property is available? Are resources and gear otherwise restricted?"

    \n I have not tried it but I have played plenty of Burning Wheel in this past couple of years.

    Alright, in the ole Planescape boxed set there was this bit about changing reality through adventuring, reality bending based on their beliefs, thereby altering the outer realms. I dug that. So, two ideas.

    #1

    So, in BW we've got Beliefs. Beliefs can become Traits through ye old trait vote. I'd like it if traits created through trait votes could somehow, through collaborative effort in between sessions could maybe somehow change reality.

    #2

    Or, in a more dramatic manner, maybe there is some way to spend artha to change reality. Maybe you can alter reality based on the character's beliefs somehow by sending an extraordinary amount of Artha, like a setting mini-epiphany.

    I wouldn't bother creating lifepaths or any of that nonsense; I don't think it is necessary. You can do plenty with traits.

    Hope that helps.

    \nYes! Thats a good point. A few more, of the top of my head:

    Because it's reward-based, players aren't forced to do anything, just encouraged. So, ignoring the story possibilities set up by the specific Outer Plane gets you nothing. Fighting against it (struggling against that belief) gets you rewarded, and conforming to the Plane gets you rewarded even more.


    If you rewrite a belief to match the Plane's belief, of course you get rewarded with double Artha - one for each Belief, even though they are the same. I guess you could almost get the same effect if you rewrite your beliefs to be the exact opposite of the Plane's, then you get rewarded for pushing yours and struggling against the planes, but it won't be an even 1:1 ratio every time.

    There are also traits in Burning Empires and later products (Blossoms, Magic Burner IIRC), that give Artha awards for specific behaviors. Something else to draw some inspiration from.
