    Okay, the down-side: They simply don't buy the mantra of "System Matters." They are, by conscious focus of the site not talking about any system other than d20. It's just never going to happen. And d20 is the cause of many of their problems. It just is. And they don't want to hear it, because ... well, basically "Hear, o Roleplayers, d20 is thy system, and there is no system but d20!"

    So, I've waded in there before. It feels like wearing a very constrictive set of badly tailored clothes. I can still walk around and talk and stuff, but I can't move freely. There are things, many things, that I cannot do without ripping the clothes.

    So what's the up-side? Huge, vast numbers of people who have (among their other interests) a keen interest in the craft of running and playing games well. If you have things to say about that craft (perhaps derived from your theory, but not actually talking about theory) and you can fit it in the tight clothes, you can have really good discussions.

    Hey gang. I've been a member here for several months but I've had about zero time to peruse the site and post thanks to life being busy. I was pointed to this discussion by my pal Mark Causey who I know is very active here. Since I'm a Mod at ENWorld I have a perspective on the place to offer.

    First up, ENWorld is just NOT for everybody and that's a plain and simple truth. Some people don't like that it's d20 focused. Some people don't like the rules there (particularly they don't like that they can't flame somebody they think needs flaming or they can't use the colorful language that would violate the "Grandma Rule"). Some people don't like the culture there.

    But I think it is in error to believe that non-d20 talk isn't welcome. I'm not even playing much d20 these days and was just today posting in a thread about the Warhammer FRP system. Nobody should be getting on your case about posting a thread that doesn't involve D&D or d20. And if they do, report their post and myself or one of the other mods will correct that because ENWorld is not about shouting down anybody who doesn't play your favorite system, whatever it happens to be.

    Furthermore, a lot of what gaming is about doesn't really have all that much to do with the system. I see threads all the time that discuss campaign, adventure and story ideas that don't use any technical terms at all. That is a huge resource for anybody that wants to bounce ideas off a large community. There are almost always over 1,000 people logged on there at any given moment and the quality of discourse on most topics is fairly good I think. So if you're having difficulty working out a plotline or are just hunting for ideas that involve zombie werewolves then you can post a thread on it and get tons of feedback without even discussing system.

    As to the ENnies, I'm not involved with them at all. Denise is a great lady, strong and organized and I applaud what she's done with the awards. I also personally know most of the judges and just got finished hanging out with them at GenCon and they are all great people (and you have not LIVED until you sit up until 4:00 AM listening to Teflon Billy's story about how he, back in the day, told Peter Adkinson, "This whole Magic: The Gathering thing? Yeah, that's never going to take off."). I am very pleased that the awards are not d20 only. In fact, for the last couple of years, some of the big winners have not been d20. Warhammer FRP won Game of the Year last year and there were lots of awards given out to other non d20 games this year including the new Shadowrun.

    As a final note, a new website has been set up by Morrus (who runs ENWorld) that is virtually unmoderated and largely off topic but is almost wholly populated by gamers. It's called Circvs Maximvs and can be found here: http://www.enworld.org/circvs/
    If you want to hang with gamers but not be bound by the same rules as at ENWorld then it's a great place.

    If anybody has any further questions they'd like to address directly to me as an ENWorld Mod or long time poster there, feel free. And I hope to see some of you at the September NC Game Day. I saw that Mark had posted about it here and you guys are always welcome.

    Man, it's cool to see Rel post here. SG is the new, hip place to be!

    Anyway, I'm a long-time denizen of ENWorld; my post count is up around 3200 or something. I also organize the ENWorld Chicago Gameday.

    In general, I think it's a great place to hang out, and I've met tons of great people via the site. I really started posting there when I was getting tired of the volatile nature of the RPG.net forums. ENWorld is well-moderated and not as prone to the flaming arguments you see on RPG.net and other boards. Some people think this makes ENWorld a little mild, but I generally like it.

    As for the d20 focus, well, the site was born out of Eric Noah's 3e rumors site; D&D/d20 is its main reason for being. That said, there's a lot of love for Castles & Crusades, True20, WFRP, HERO, and older editions of D&D, too.

    However, TonyLB has it right. The big impediment with ENWorld is that most people there will argue 'till they're blue in the face that pretty much everything in Forge (and related) theory is totally, utterly wrong. "Does 3.5 take away too much power from the DM?" is a frighteningly common thread topic. "Famous" ENWorlders like Teflon Billy, e.g., has a .sig quote that basically says, "Show me a Forge game and I'll show you me walking away from the table." He's a great guy, but it saddens me. At ENWorld, System Doesn't Matter, the DM is God, Immersion is King, and "role-play, not roll-play" can be said with a straight face.

    Consequently, I've found it harder and harder to participate there the more I learn about Forge theory and indie RPGs. More and more of the play I see described there turns me off. And when I try to talk about it, people respond like I'm from Mars.

    Granted, 100% of the community isn't like this, but enough are that I notice it.
