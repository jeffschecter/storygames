I'm kicking a little idea around right now, and I'd like some help on three points.

First, the idea. The working title is Something Stupid. My goal is an intense game built around petty criminals in bad circumstances. The PCs (criminals) have a task, in which someone does something stupid: takes a hostage, kills/maims a hostage, kills the wrong person, loses the boss' drugs, whatever. They are stuck together, nervous and armed as tension pushes them to the inevitable conclusion. This game will probably be played in one session, and wil likely not have a GM.

The first point I'd like some help on is genre reference. The first movie that comes to mind is "Resivoir Dogs," where the events of the final shootout come spiraling down from Mr. White taking a cop hostage. The second is James Hollywood, inspiration behind the new film "Alpha Dog." He takes a hostage (the brother of a guy that owed him money,) and then is left holding the "WTF" bag with his cohort. So far, I've also found "Freakonomics" by Levitt and Dubner-- the chapter "Why do crack dealers live with their mothers?"-- to be helpful understanding the boss (and a hell of a lot more, too. Great book, highly recomended.) Also on the real-world side, Wikipedia is always helpful, but when isn't it?
Any other sources I should be looking at? I may be limiting the setting to street gangs to keep the "petty criminals over their heads" vibe.

The second point I would like help on is: what games would be good to run this with? I've got some mechanics/system ideas flowing onto the paper, but I would also like to be able to reference some rulesets or elements of rulesets that have already trod this ground.

As always, any assistance is greatly appreciated.
