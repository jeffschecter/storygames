\n Only a few hours remain to pick up Adventurer Conqueror King and Domains of War at a bargain price on Kickstarter

anyway......

So, I'm thinking of doing a free little red ACKS-compatible supplement - Dungeonista ! - almost an anti-domain game, to answer the question - in OD&D - why are the villagers angry ?

The answer lies in the Secret History of the Empire and the legacy of a doomed Dwarven dissident.

What is Dungeonista!

- you level collectively - not as individuals but as a cell, by 9th Level - you set up a Commune. Teamwork is the key.

- XP is gained by spreading the word (a bit like Clerics congregations), storming strongholds, liberating the oppressed (Freedom for Constructs!), disrupting the trade in sentient monster parts, publically defying patriachy (Adventurer, Conqueror, Queen!), redistributing wealth (adating the rules for carousing) and the control of the means of production.

- new classes, Elven Antagonist ('What do I oppose - Everything!'), Dwarven Sappers (Kaboom, there goes Castle Greyhawk), Riot-Maidens and Disenchanters (rebel skalds inciting revolution - 'Kick out the Djinns , Peasant Farmers !')

- book includes seeds for Shadowrun style scenarios of intrusion, infiltration, extraction, assault.

- artwork - Socialist Realist Poster-style parodies of fantasy art with Ancient Roman graffiti and May '68 slogans?

Viva the Dungeonistas ! The Commune must be built !

(overuse of exclamation marks is urgent & key)

Your thoughts, comrades ?

..and specifically - I want to keep the party together working as a team are there any great indie-mechanics that I can port into a D&D style game to encourage this, and maybe also build internal tensions?

    \n This! sounds! amazingly! neat! -- why hasn't someone done this before?!

    A possible mechanism to encourage the party to stay together is a shared pool of resources (points, dice, bonuses, gerbils, whatever) that can only be used when the party is working together as a collective on a joint task. Given how revolutions often start out on the ropes and slowly gain momentum, maybe that pool is charged by your failures, sort of like Thematic Batteries from Full Light, Full Steam, except on the campaign level instead of the adventure level.

    You know, if it wasn't going to be AQK compatible, using a system like the Story Engine, where scenes are resolved by a single die roll that all the characters present contribute to would be kind of appropriate to the theme.
