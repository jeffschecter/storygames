\n I was reading a great article about shapes in game design and came upon the following:\n
  • Circle: innocence, youth, energy,
    • \n
  • Square: maturity, stability, balance, stubbornness
    • \n
  • Triangle: aggression, masculinity, force
\nSo I started to think about this in different medias. Speech balloons are normally round, information to the reader are in square boxes and if someone shouts, it's in a pointy speech balloon. And then I started to think about Zombie Cinema, where the player tokens are round figures, the boxes on the board are ... well, boxes, and the zombie token is a pyramid. Is this a coincident? It can't be, right?

For me, I'm going to be more aware about shapes when designing character sheets, choosing tokens for different game mechanics (destructive tokens should be triangular, and helpful tokens round) and how the text blocks combined with images will appear in the game book.

    \nEero: Cool, then I know. I mostly made this thread to ask about that.

    How you move on the game board in Zombie Cinema is a little zig-zaggy, which also resembles the triangular shape ("pointy"). I wonder how different the feel of the board would've been, if the tokens moved around in form of a circle or a square. I'm not saying people will say the game is harmonic just because tokens travels around in circles, but to quote something Mark Rosewater once wrote about aesthetics: "Lesson #2 - People sense what they do not consciously notice."
