    Hey Caeser, let me try to break some down:

    http://wiki.d-addicts.com/static/images/2/2f/JikouKisetsuChart.jpg

    So there's little boxes here and there: The box that encompasses the funny hair dude, and the three on the left, is basically "foot policeman".
    The bottom two are Detectives. The girl on the left is in the "Information Department" (dispatch?), and the dude on the bottom left, I can't make out the kanji in that pic but it looks like he's in the yakuza investigation group. So the boxes here denote job/group roles.

    In this one:
    http://wiki.d-addicts.com/static/images/thumb/e/e6/800px-Haken_no_Hinkaku_chart.jpg

    The big blue box represents "S & F Industries". The blue box within that represents the "Marketing Group". The dudes on the left are people not in the company. Ex: The woman on the top left is the owner of a bar, the one underneath is her son, a Flamenco Guitarist. The reason that many arrows point towards the chick in the middle is because there's a lot of characters here, and the one in the middle is the "main character". So while she has initial relationships with the others, they only detailed a few of them (like the dude next to her: His relationship to her is "Woah" (unclear the meaning, I think it's a combo of "she's strong/accomplished" and "maybe headstrong"), and Her relationship to him is "I can't rely on him!").
    Each character box lists the character name in green, the actor in gold, and in black what their position or role in the company (or their job, for the ones on the left) is.

    http://wiki.d-addicts.com/static/images/7/7c/Cast_kekkon.jpg
    In this one, the dude in the middle/right is the main dude (the show is called "The man who can't get married", and that's The Man the title refers to). They ise red and blue to distinguish the sexes, as there's a lot of war-of-the-sexes style undertones in the show. The woman to the left of him is the eventual love(ish) interest. The top of each box is a brief description of the character's job or role, then their name in big letters, then the actor's name in parentheses.

    http://wiki.d-addicts.com/static/images/9/98/Cast_of_Saigo_no_Nightingale.jpg
    Unfortunately, I've not seen this drama, nor do I know anything about it, save that it's about VERY young army nurses in WW2. But from the pictures and descriptions, the four girls in the middle are the military nurses for some sort of extended campiagn. The woman on the far right is one of them (the leftmost of the four girls) in modern times (so it's probably one of those "old lady recalls story of youth" tales). The other characters are nondescript, just the character name then the actor name in parentheses.

    http://wiki.d-addicts.com/static/images/6/66/Sailor_Fuku_to_Kikanjuu_chart.jpg
    This is about a girl who finds out that she's the last of the line of a Yakuza gang (called "Sailor Uniform plus Submachinegun"). The dudes in red names are with the Medaka gang, the ones in green are the Heiguchi gang (which they fight), the ones in blue are police officers, and the ones on the left don't really have "gang affiliation". Interesting thing here, aside from use of color, is that the four ones on the right, they're all members of the gang now, but the description to the right of their picture describes what they Used to be. From the top left, clockwise: Used to be a detective, used to be a shut-in/otaku, used to be the leader of a street gang, user to be a computer tech.

    http://wiki.d-addicts.com/static/images/d/de/Furin_Kazan-Correlation_Chart.jpg
    This one makes my eyes bleed from the small text. But each "color area" is another clan in the feudal era: Takeda, Uesugi, Imagawa, etc. Unfortunately, I'm not too familiar with that era of Japanese history. The red lines show blood relationships. The thick blue double-line shows marriages, and the dotted line is "other".

    ^_^

    It's OmniGraffle.

    The lines are...

    • X -(blue)-> Y : X loves Y (Birds doesn't know which boy to pick)
    • X -(blue dotted)-> Y : Birds thinks it'd be funny if X were to love Y
    • X -(purple)-> Y : X controls Y
    • X <-(green)-> Y : X has a secret with Y (There are actually a lot more of these than I could even think to put on the chart)
    • X -(orange)-> Y : X is allied with Y

    The colours and shapes are for Exalted splats...

    • Gold circles are Solar, blue Lunar
    • Stars are Sidereals and pentagons Terrestrial (colourcoded for Caste)
    • Tombstones are Abyssals
    • Spiky things are dinosaurs
    • Temples-with-towers are gods
    • Jelly jar labels are mortals

    Finally, this is for an Exalted game I'm playing in, where I finally started to lose track of who was what where, and our ST gave us a similar chart that describes matters in the time of the last Solar generation (not my bandwidth, so not linking), which got me going.
