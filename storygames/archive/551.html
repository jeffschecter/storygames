My head is just filled with all kinds of little ideas. Let's flush and see what floats.

I keep going back to a dungeon idea. I don't know why, really. You'd think the concept would be all mined out. Har. Har. But I'm leaning towards something like Gauntlet: The Third Encounter for the Atari Lynx:

http://www.ataritimes.com/lynx/reviews/gauntlet3.html

In that one, you get to choose from several characters: Valkyrie, Wizard, Pirate, Gunfighter, Samurai, Android, Nerd, and Punkrocker. As you can see, the character types are kind of thrown together without much rhyme or reason. Every so often someone posts an idea for making a dungeon game where the whole world is a dungeon and there is no surface world to deal with, but those conversations tend to run into stuff like figuring out a realistic or at least logical ecology for a world that is totally underground. I plan to chuck logic into the garbage disposal with the grotty old potato peels. Where it belongs.

But I also keep thinking of a dungeon card game which would be similar to several other dungeon card games out there with the difference that you don't need a large table to lay out the cards to create the maze because the maze element is handled in a more abstract way. Possibly with several piles like Solitaire. In fact you could play it solitaire or with a group
