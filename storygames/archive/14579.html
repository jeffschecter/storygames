    \n Oh, how can we forget the tale of Miyamoto Musashi, thought to be one of the greatest swordsmen who ever lived, in his climactic battle against longtime rival Sasaki Kojiro, "The Demon of the West" at Funajima?

    Legend has all these factors that Miyamoto Musashi carefully calculated in his favor (like tides, sun position etc) but the truth is that Musashi was pretty much a barefoot drunk who wandered from place to place in a drunken stupor at around that age, only becoming coherent when it was time to fight or teach (later he sobered up a bit and started writing more).

    The day of the fight his squire comes to shake him awake, cause he totally overslept after drinking all night and was late by several hours. Musashi stumbles out of the inn, the squire boats him out to the island, and on his way he takes one of the oars and carves it up into a bokken (a wooden sword). He stumbles onto the shore, proceeds to beat the well-armed and prepared Sasaki to death with a piece of wood, stumbles back into the boat and has the squire boat him back to town for more drinking.

    They made a trilogy of movies in the 50s Based Upon his life (Samurai I, II and III). The third movie was the duel described above. But the movie isn't historically accurate, otherwise it would have ended in 12 minutes.

    \n The obvious, used any number of places (several times during the Lord of the Rings movies, for example) to show that the warrior is Cooler Than You: cutting the guy's head off.

    When on foot, confronting the charging knight: killing the horse. (Has several variations, such as Mal's shooting of the horse the villain is using as cover.)

    Using the weapon you were just stabbed with to kill the guy that stabbed you. Bonus points if you cut off his head.

    Viggo Mortensen using his sword to deflect an incoming knife on the first take. (Maybe that's for PtA warriors.)

    Secondary move to emphasize that yes, he really is dead. (Riddick snaps the hilt off the knife he just embedded into Bad Guy's skull. Bruce Lee does the little twisting the off-camera feet move.)

    The "machinegun circle at your feet to fall through the floor move". Probably perfected by Nemesis.

    Wolverine doing the "two claws on either side of the face and threatening to extend the third" to get information.

    Indy shooting the guy with the scimitar.

    Sitching up your own wounds with needle and thread (First Blood, also one of the Blade movies, I think).

    Nuking the site out of spite, as your dying act (Predator).

    Using other warriors as a distraction, while you bring the "flying sword of Damocles from the flank" trick. (Sif vs. the Destroyer in the Thor movie, as an example.)
