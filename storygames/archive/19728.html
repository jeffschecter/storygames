    \n Huh, I might have to paint up a few squads of WW1 French soldiers and take this to my local Historical Wargames Shop in my city. It would make a great convention game with some nicely painted miniatures and a well terrained board and some neat artillery bursts to throw around the battlefield. After trying a few run throughs solo I could see that this would be a fun to do with several players all rolling for their squads in a "push". It's fast, simple yet engaging and one page of rules to be printed out and given to each player it could tell a great story.

    If I get that involved I'll post some pictures. I'll at least bring it to my local shop and give it a run through a few times.

    \n If someone actually plays this with minis, I want to see pictures! I've just been drawing little maps on post-it notes.

    I can definitely say I am anti-war, and this was inspired by listening to some history podcasts about WWI. (Hardcore History and When Diplomacy Fails for those interested.) I don't mean to make light of the terrible sacrifices of the men who fought at Verdun, but if my game makes people think about the tremendous courage it takes to jump out of a trench and run into machine gun fire, I am content.

    I also realized the PDF version doesn't make it explicitly clear the main game is a solo affair, although a number of hacks tell you how to play with multiple people.
