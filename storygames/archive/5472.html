\n I'm compiling the book "N\xf8rwegian Style", which is a collection of Norwegian RPGs that have been translated to English. It's pretty much the Norwegian answer to what the No Press Anthology did some years back, and I'd like to hear more about people's experiences with that book as a reference point.

So. Did you ever hear about the No Press Anthology? Did you buy the book? Did you play any of the games?

If you were involved in the project, what did you think of it? What worked? What didn't? What would you do differently?

    \n I think most of the authors post here semi-regularly. Nopress site for those who are unaware of the book.

    For creators it was a great project to test the waters of publishing. For a consumer it was a nice mix of different tastes, and I reckon quite a good intro to this style of games. It was in Leisure Game's top Indie sellers and found its way to plenty of gameshops via Key20.

    Luke did an amazing job of holding disparate elements together. So having a strong project manager is definitely key.
