\n In a Wicked Age and Red Box Hack: Are they compatible? Or would it be like running GameBoy ROMs on a WINE instance running on a RHEL 4 box, which itself is a VMWare instance on a blade server running a fiber-channel SAN connection through multipath Brocade switches to yet another GameBoy?

Since these are the only two RPGs of note, I was thinkinig of perhaps combining them somehow.

However, what about something different: Like, using the Red Box Hack rules system, and then adding the elements of the I Owe list and the like? Perhaps making character sheets a mashup of RBH abilities and the like, and the 6 IAWA stats?

Perhaps the easiest method to combine the two is to use RBX as the backbone, and simply use the IAWA Oracles, I Owe list and the like. Thoughts?

-Andy

    \n You know, guys, that's kind of a dick move.

    There are two roleplaying games in the world and that's enough for anybody - why do you have to go and try to mash them together? Can't you leave well enough alone? Red Box Hack players are perfectly happy playing - you guessed it - Red Box Hack. I think what you're really trying to do here is get more people playing In A Wicked Age, which is fine if you are into it I guess.

    But this stinks like evangelism to me! Like the first slippery step down a staircase of misery for guys who just want to rock out with their favorite game.
