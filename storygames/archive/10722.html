\n OK. So, I have this scenario in mind: It is post WWII but pre Korean War America. There are a group of people who have, through various techniques, hit the pinnacle of human development. They fight crime at the street level. Some of them are gadgeteers. Sometimes, they try to ferret out Communist cells.

The swing is that they have a superman whose powers are devouring him. He has the option of passing this power off before it kills him or possibly dying in a horrific and highly explosive manner.

I want a game that will give that feel. Think the Minutemen from Watchmen before things got really ugly.

Suggestions?

    \nFeng Shui would also be a good system, it's explicitly designed for cinematic action games, and has some good rules for corrupting powers that you could hack pretty easily for something like this.

    Mist-Robed Gate would be perfect if you wanted to do it as a one-session movie-style game where the characters have conflicts with each other, not so much if you want a multi-episode 'team' campaign.

    A lot of this depends on just how loose you want your combat to be, and how cinematic you're looking to go. UA is a good system that HEAVILY emphasises the psychological effects of PC's actions at the expense of any real tactical rules, while FS is probably the opposite end of that spectrum, lots about combat, few concrete rules to enforce mental trauma on PCs. Love 'em both, it just depends on the game you want to run.

    -Jim Crocker
