    \n Oh hey, my game The Dreaming Crucible now has downloadable play aids!

    The main one is the game cards, which basically function as your "character sheet." Now you can download and print instead of having to photocopy your one set of them! Hello, 21st century!





    The second aid is a roadmap of play, which lists the main principles of play and the duties of the main player roles, as well as delineating the major play procedures. I'm keen for feedback from folks who've read or played the game, especially if you use the sheet to help tach the game. it's definitely an alpha, and I'd like to refine its usefulness.

    Peace,
    -Joel

    \n Pelgrane are doing a preorder for Ashen Stars, which is Robin Laws' science fiction take on GUMSHOE.

    It's interesting (in my view) because they're using it specifically to fund a full-colour printed edition. And also because it's a two-stage preorder. There was a "Pre-pre-order" last year, which included a text copy, and now there's another one. So it's worth keeping an eye on.

    Ashen Stars itself is beautifully written. It's the first GUMSHOE product with a witty setting: much of the text is genuinely, subtly funny. Nevertheless, it's grimy, bloody science fiction. It's a nice blend.

    The art is also good. Here is an insect with guns.

    \n So, today Magic: the Gathering released its newest set - New Phyrexia.

    And I am giddy excited about that, so I hacked my own game (Perfect Unrevised) to play stories in that setting. It's a beautiful fit, actually: instead of playing criminals in a dystopian steampunk world, who risk brainwashing and reconditioning at the hands of inspectors... you play resistance fighters in a dystopian biopunk world, who risk body restructuring and infection at the hands of the minions of Phyrexia.

    The game's called Compleat, and it's linked here. It requires an understanding of Perfect Unrevised to play it. And I'm offering a $2-off coupon for Perfect right now, in case anyone is interested in Compleat but doesn't own Perfect yet. Deets in the link.

    \n Digital Images of Yale\u2019s Vast Cultural Collections Now Available for Free

    Published: May 10, 2011

    George Stubbs, Zebra, 1763, oil on canvas, Yale Center for British Art, Paul Mellon Collection. From the collections of Yale University.

    New Haven, Conn. \u2014 Scholars, artists and other individuals around the world will enjoy free access to online images of millions of objects housed in Yale's museums, archives, and libraries thanks to a new "Open Access" policy that the University announced today. Yale is the first Ivy League university to make its collections accessible in this fashion, and already more than 250,000 images are available through a newly developed collective catalog.

    The goal of the new policy is to make high quality digital images of Yale's vast cultural heritage collections in the public domain openly and freely available.

      \n I just released episode 28 of the Designer vs. Reality podcast which is a playtest of Marc Majcher's game Skin Men. Designer vs. Reality is my Actual Play podcast that features playtests of games in development by independent designers. The concept of the podcast is the give AP podcast fans a way to follow games in development, to give the designers of the games valuable playtest data, and to give other designers a chance to observe actual play of games that may still have design issues (where you might see some things you wouldn't in published, polished games).
