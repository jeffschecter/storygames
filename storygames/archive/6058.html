\nRinCon '08 will be held Halloween Weekend (Oct 31 through Nov 2nd) at the Tucson Convention Center.

Here's why you should go.

RinCon '08 is being run by the same people that run the Ides of Gaming monthly gaming event. As far as role-playing games go, we're pretty big into indie games. I'm very interested in the Story Games scene and I'm the one that's organizing the RPG track at RinCon '08. We're going to have a strong indie RPG and story game focus in our RPG events: \n
    \n
  • Just about half of the scheduled games at the anniversary Ides event were role-playing game events. Five of the six were non-mainstream games. At regular monthly events, indie games and story games are played more often than mainstream games.
  • Some of the regulars at our game days include SG's own JDCorley, Kynn, and Peter Aronson, so there should be some awesome people to game with.
  • There is a good group of independent game designers here in Tucson; one of our announced guests is from a prototyping and development group. There's going to be a big focus on independent game development and indie games.
\nWe're also tying the convention into the cultural events around Tucson. On Halloween night, the downtown area becomes a huge costumed street party (Nightmare on Congress Street). The last day of the convention is also the day of the All Souls Procession, a massive street parade that is similar to Mexico's Dia de los Muertos holiday. Over ten thousand participants gather on the streets of downtown Tucson for this amazing event. We have also been working closely with the Tucson Visitor's Bureau and will be featuring some of the many local attractions in and around Tucson for our attendees from outside of town, such as the popular haunted ghost town event Nightfall at Old Tucson Studios.

With our debut, we're trying to pull in people from all over the state of Arizona, from as far east as El Paso and Albuquerque, and from as far west as the San Diego area.

The people that are organizing this event aren't just some guys that like to play games. We're the people behind the Pulp Gamer suite of podcasts and The Game's The Thing podcast (a/k/a the Savage Worlds podcast). We're tied into major game distributors, retailers worldwide and local, and local gaming groups. We're game developers both professional and amateur. We're organizers that have run game conventions, game days, and gaming events. We've been planning RinCon '08 for a year and we know it's going to be great.

You should come. It's going to be totally awesome.

You can find more information about RinCon at RinConGames.com.
