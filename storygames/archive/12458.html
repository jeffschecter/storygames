    \n Here's a post I made from rpghaven on the nominations:

    There are several awards that are so easy and have such an obvious answer that any judge who doesn't send in their ballot right now should be disqualified from voting in them:

    Best Adventure from this list is the Armitage Files, by 1,000 percent.

    Best Accessory is Gaming Paper

    The Art awards should be taken someplace quiet and shot.

    Best Blog is One Geek To Another.

    Best Cartography is the Dungeon of Terror "boxed set".

    Best Electronic book from that list is the Great City Player's Guide (though this is a ridiculously under-nominated category, so much so that anyone associated with the enterprise should be ashamed and apologize to everyone they meet for the rest of their lives, forever)

    Best Free Product is Lady Blackbird - probably this one would win Best Game if the awards were arranged right, sure as hell it's better than most of the nominees.

    Anyway, Best Game, from that list, is Dragon Age Set 1.

    Best Monster/Adversary is Rustlers and Townsfolk

    Best Production Values is a category that should not exist, it is a malformed and diseased offspring masquerading as its parent, real artistic import, deserving only a quick death.

    There are some categories with strong competition. Best Rules is a good example. Wild Talents, BASH and Diaspora are all good choices. The remainder of the category must be some kind of sick joke. Best Setting is another example: Goblin Markets, Day after Ragnarok, and Kerberos Club are all brilliant. And the Best Writing category is good from top to bottom. Product of the Year is also an excellent category.
