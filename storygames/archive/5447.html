\n Just a little idea I was thinking about with regard to Dogs towns. The GM is instructed (i paraphrase) to create towns which challenge the players morally. But it's not always easy to come up with interesting and difficult ones. So, let's all describe moral choices which we think are difficult.

Note: Please don't take sides on your dilemma, just state it.

1. A great author dies, with firm instructions to his son that his last manuscript should be burned, even though the son, having read it, thinks it contains some of his father's most transcendent writing. Should his wishes be respected, or should it be published anyway?

    \n An African country is wracked by civil war, which is really a nice way of saying criminality and gangsterism have destroyed law and order.

    European businessmen who own the mining and mineral rights in said country loan the properly elected government enough money to hire mercenaries to restore law and order. Those same businessmen then supply said mercenaries, whose primary mission is to secure areas where oil and mineral mining are profitable.

    Right or wrong?

    On a similar tangent, the tyrannical dictator of a country on or near the African continent wages a homicidal campaign against first his own family, then the people of his own country. Some would say he is not the worst dictator they have ever seen, but all would call his regime despicable. European businessmen who want mining and mineral concessions in this country hire mercenaries to overthrow him, restore the government in exile, and then hold proper democratic elections.

    Right or wrong?

    \n How about work ethics?
    \n
    \nA rich person commissions an Artist to make a picture of him. The rich man has no idea about art and little true taste himself. The artist's Muse is idle, so his work is mediocre. Would he charge the original amount of money agreed, even while he knows the quality of his art is poor and plain?
    \n
    \nA soldier is badly hurted during a battle. If he isn't operated immediatly, he'll probably die. If he is, he'll most likelly become crippled. He isn't capable of making the choice by himself at the moment. What would his superior decide? What would the doctor do (considering that if he survives he'll probably live in pain for the rest of his life because of his injuries)?
