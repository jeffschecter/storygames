\n So I'm playing IAWA, chatting with my players about some house rule Vincent posted, and it got me thinking...

How cool would it be if instead of "We will always use this house rule" or "We will never use this house rule" we did a "Call the game" kind of thing. Like, the GM gets to call the game, like the dealer gets to call the hand on a poker night.

So sometimes we could call "Dragons" which means "After you draw the oracle change 1 word to Dragon or Draconic"
or we could call "Oaths" and use the promise-keeping-incentive Vincent posted before
Or we could call "Polaris!" and pick a Heart, New Moon, Full Moon, and Mistaken, and set up our characters' best interests with that in mind.

    Posted By: fnord3125is there any reason this kind of thing would be especially suited to IaWA?
    Off the top of my head:
    1) The rules are few enough that it's easy to keep it all straight.
    This avoids: the every-night-a-different-rule D&D game I ran when I was 17
    2) The game isn't so tightly written around 1 premise.
    This avoids: "I was all psyched to play a caged yeti in My Life With Master and now I'm playing a teenage drug-addicted prostitute and the Master is my dealer. This sucks."
    3) A combination of 1 & 2 means there's a lot of value to calling games that aren't technically rules changes; just slight drifts in terms of setup. So you can stay in familiar territory in the ways you want to.
