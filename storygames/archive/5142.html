    \n Trail of Cthulhu's coming out soon. That's looking really good.

    If you must get one now, then it's difficult: The Book Of Unremitting Horror is the best for the atmosphere and setting details and also includes two sample adventures that really show what the system can do. But it doesn't actually have the rules in, so you'd need something like Fear Itself, too.

    Seriously, though, Trail of Cthulhu is looking great. I think I'd recommend waiting for that. Not long now.

    \n Oh, Matthijs, I forgot. You can pre-order Trail of Cthulhu. Do that. Seriously, do that.

    You asked for the book that showcases the mechanics, and so on, at their best. In my opinion, the mechanics work best with an atmospheric setting. Fear Itself does that pretty well, especially if you're using it with the Book Of Unremitting Horror. But, really, nothing matches Cthulhu for atmospheric mystery*.

    \n I've got both Esoterrorists and Fear Itself, and have Trail of Cthulhu on order, but note that as of this moment, while I have EsoT on the shortlist of games to play in the upcoming months, I still have not played any incarnation of the system yet.

    Looking through the books, I think Fear Itself is a better product in general, and is really ripe for both doing Everyman investigators, bringing their own fears and angst into the investigation... and yet, I'll probably hang my hat on Esoterrorists, because it can let me run and play Delta Green with 100% no problem, mods, or editing, and I fucking love Delta Green.

    \n Disclosure: I am the publisher, talking as an enthusiastic GM/player.
    \n
    \nIf you players like pulling together as a team of competent investigators, and with less mechanical emphasis on character traits, Esoterrorist. In actual play for me reliably gives the quite good. I've GM'ed a couple of times and player once.
    \n
    \nIf you want in-depth characters, normal people facing terrible horrors, then I suggest Fear Itself. In actual play for me, ranges from OK to exceptional rpg experience. GM'ed a couple of times.
    \n
    \nBOUH is background for both or either. I've played the d20 version.
    \n
    \nTrail of Cthulhu is so tailored to the Mythos you should get one of the others if the Mythos doesn't interest you. I've played a few times. It does include new general GUMSHOE rules which can be used in the other books, and we'll add those for Eso and Fear on the website early next year.
    \n
    \nPDFs are available.
    \n
    \nMCB isn't four-colour. It's a bit more Heroes/Powers, but Top 10 did spring to mind in terms of the buddy cop thing.
