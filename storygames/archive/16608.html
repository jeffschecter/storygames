\n I love World Building.
\nThere are some guides on the web on how to world build, for instance Patricia C. Wrede\u2019s Fantasy World Building Questions (http://www.sfwa.org/2009/08/fantasy-worldbuilding-questions/) and 30 Days of WorldBuilding (http://www.web-writer.net/fantasy/days/index.html).
\nThese are both \u201ctop-down\u201d world building methods (got that from some other world building site), and quite inspirational. HOWEVER, I find myself preferring more scattershot methods.
\n
\nThis thread has two purposes:
\n1) Having fun with world building.
\n2) Compiling a list of scattershot slash down-up approaches to world building.
\nThe rules for the thread are as follows:
\nPost 5 + challenges/questions/hints to world builders
\nOr
\nAnswer previous challenges. You yourself choose which and how many to answer. You can answer based on a world you\u2019ve made, one you\u2019re working on, or even someone else\u2019s. You can also answer your own challenges.
\n
\nOr
\nDo both.
\n
\n(I might publish the final challenges in some sort of blog post at some point.)
\n
\nHere are some challenges slash slash for starters\u2026
\n
\n* Draw a creature from your world and post it here. It can be a character, a monster an NPC or whatever. What the heck, it can be a mountain for all we know. Or a photo from our own world identical to something in yours.
\n
\n* Who\u2019s an outsider in your setting? Why? What does it mean to be an outsider? Limit yourself to 300 words.
\n
\n* Describe something sacred. You can use as many characters as you like, but you\u2019re not allowed to explain anything about the setting\u2019s cosmology directly.
\n
\n* Write three words in one of the languages of your setting. Explain their meaning.
\n
\n* Write five keywords about the mood you wish to convey.
\n
\n* Use at most 1000 characters on a mood piece about your setting.
\n
\n* Show us your map.
\n
\n* In five sentences \u2013 show how religion functions in daily life.
\n
\n* Write a timeline.
\n
\n* Decide upon the one single element in your setting you\u2019re the least happy with. Remove it from the setting description, and tell us afterwards.
\n
\n* What\u2019s the most meager resource in the setting?
\n* Mention three movies/books/comics/RPGs or the like one could be inspired by if one wanted to write/play in your setting.
\n
\n* Find a photo using Google Image Search which matches your setting, and link to it here.
\n* Kill your darlings.
\n
\n* Do a playtest in the setting, no matter how incomplete it might be.
\n
\n* Invite a few friends. Tell them about your setting. Accept their input. Tell us what you\u2019re going to use.
\n
\n* What\u2019s the worst profanity one can use in your setting? Why?
\n
\n* Is there a God?
\n
\n* What on your timeline would you most want to witness as it unfolds?
\n
\n* What on your timeline would you avoid at all costs?
\n
\n* Create a character for any RPG which might fit in with your setting and post it here.
\n
\n* Who would you rather be in your setting?
