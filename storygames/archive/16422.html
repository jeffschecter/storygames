\nI'm running another game of Lady Blackbird for a group of first timers. I've actually run LB quite a few times. I, as a GM, want to try adding a little extra flare to the game. Nothing that actually gets in the way of the players developing the setting, characters, and narrative.
\n
\nI decided something simple would be a big map of the Wild Blue.
\n
\n1) is this ok? (legally)
\n
\n2) My thoughts were to take it to an Office store (max, depot, staples, whatever is closest) and have them put it on a 36x48 sheet. Big enough to look nice on a table, but not so much get in the way. Thoughts?
\n
\n3) I was also going to make a little gaming token of the Owl. Just a little quarter size token to move on the map. (this was pretty much just a statement....no question here. Move on to #4!)
\n
\n4) Should I add the text descriptions of the planets onto the map?
\n
\n5) Any other ideas for play aids? I just want to add a little jazz to this gaming session.
\n
\n
\nOn a side note: I got a couple sets of q-workshops steampunk dice. I'm going to give them out as prizes for the character who collects the most xp and the character who overcomes the most obstacles. Just a lil bit of fun to help motivate the players to keep hitting keys and complicating things for themselves!

    \n So I really need to stop prepping for this. I'm just so excited. Please comment on this list of questions I have come up with to ask the players randomly. Of course things are going to rapidly change during play, but I wanted to have a reference to come back to.
    \n
    \nLady Blackbird
    \n1.\tHow did you fall in love with Uriah Flint?
    \n2.\tThere are 8 Noble houses, Pick one of the other 7 and tell me about them.
    \n3.\tTell me about your relationship with Count Carlowe and why he chases you.
    \n4.\tWhat is Stormblood?
    \n5.\tHave your views about the different classes (nobles and commoners) changed? If so, how?
    \n6.\tWho are you in love with?
    \nNaomi Bishop
    \n1.\tInteresting, you serve a noble, Lady Blackbird, but you\u2019re an \u201cex-slave.\u201d So then, who are the slavers?
    \n2.\tYou recognize someone from your past as a pit-fighter. Who is it? What secret does s/he know about you? (Refer to names on page 2)
    \n3.\tThere are two political factions: The Empire and the Free Worlds. What separates them?
    \n4.\tWhat is on the light side of Nightport?
    \n5.\tHow did you become the Lady\u2019s Bodyguard?
    \n6.\tSomething terrible and life changing happened to you in the pits. What happened?
    \nSnargle
    \n1.\tThere are goblins. What\u2019s everyone else? What else is out there? What role do goblins fill in this world?
    \n2.\tYou have a history with the Trade Union. What is your connection to them?
    \n3.\tYou\u2019ve been to the lower depths before. Why? What was it like?
    \n4.\tOut of all the cities on Haven, why this one?
    \n5.\tWho is your contact to find the secret of the True Course?
    \n6.\tThere is a starship docked next to the Owl. Describe it. Who does it belong to? What is it doing here?
    \n
    \nKale Arkam
    \n1.\tThis is a world of magic\u2026.Scholars believe that the star is made from pure Essence\u2014the strange energy that sorcerers channel for their magic. Is this true? What role does magic have in the Wild Blue?
    \n2.\tWhat are the mysteries of the Sky Squid? What is the song of the Sky Squid?
    \n3.\tYou once stole something of importance. What was it? Who from?
    \n4.\tWhat created such a deep bond between you and the Captain?
    \n5.\tIs there a connection between magic and technology? If so, what?
    \n6.\tYou are driven to see the Lady to her destination. Why?
    \nCyrus Vance
    \n1.\tYou are \u201cWarpblood.\u201d Is that a half-race thing? A ritual done to you? A euphemism for something else entirely?
    \n2.\tYou\u2019re not just going to let Captain Hollas get away with what he did, are you? What was it that he did again?
    \n3.\tWhat do you smuggle?
    \n4.\tHow are you going to pay for the fuel?
    \n5.\tA major event caused you to become an outcast from the Empire. What was it?
    \n6.\tDo you still love the Lady? Why or Why not?

    \n Hey Matthew, are you running this for the SW Suburban RPG meetup in Plainfield, IL? 'Cause I'm one of the attendees.

    I just played LB this weekend at Forge Midwest, and I ran it once a few years ago.

    My suggestion would be not to bother with asking any questions up front, just start playing. Hold on to those questions and just let them come up organically in play. E.g., the person playing Lady Blackbird in our game this weekend specifically held off on deciding what stormblood was until very late in the game. She waiting for the right moment, and then came up with something awesome that make the final scene totally epic. It was cool!

    The setup of the game is very familiar-feeling (it's Star Wars), so I find it's best to just start playing and let players bring the awesome.

    \nI know LB practically runs itself, I just want to be well prepared. I was planning on asking those questions intermittently just to keep the pacing right. I really want to jam the entire game into that one session, so I have to keep things moving (yet let things play out organically as they need to). The plans were to just introduce the concepts in the game guide itself and nothing more. I think after running this game several times I've found the perfect balance to keep the game moving. The first time I ran the game, I just let things go. The game did run itself, but too fast and not as fun as I'd hope. The second time, I added too much to the game and stifled the players. The last few times, I found that asking these very, very specific leading questions keeps the game heading in the right direction. Hence, a list that will help me.
