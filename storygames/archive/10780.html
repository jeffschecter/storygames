\n I hear scattered remarks about people's games-in-progress around here and RPG.net, but just because someone says they're "working on a game" doesn't mean it'll be done soon/ever.

So, I guess what I'd like to hear is what games people are working on/finished with that are coming out (or planning to come out with) within, let's say the next year or so.

Tentative dates, as well as "this game is awesome because it's about X and you do Y in Z and it's just so great, you don't even know!" remarks are welcome as well.

Come on, what do we have to look forward to? I want to watch some previews, but for RPGs.

    I'm currently play-testing Lost Chronicles, a game in which the group collaboratively creates a body of mythology and folklore for a world through a series of standalone yet interconnected 'tales'. The main conceit is that they are a group of scholar/priests/shamen/whatever who are rediscovering the lost lore of their world by re-telling the acts and trials of various gods and heroes. All the while, identifying answers these tales provide to explain how their world came to be the way it is.

    Essentially you create a mythic history with the breath of say, the Silmarillion, one related story at a time.

    \nTokyo Rain should be out around Spring of next year.

    This is an officially licensed game based on the John Rain novels by NYT bestselling author Barry Eisler. For those not familiar (shame on you!) John Rain is an assassin who specializes in death by natural causes. But his is a complicated character. He's not a soulless killing machine, and his "work" weighs on him. Unsurprisingly, I'm using Barry's own writing for the game's tagline: "Killing is the easy part."

    Gamewise, it's a two-player competitive story game with a meaty strategic element designed to be played in a few hours. Playtesting is essentially done. I've just started working on the draft. Layout is being done by the incomparable Fred Hicks. More stuff to announce soon.

    \n\nRich Stokes \n\n\n
    \n
    \n\nNovember 2009 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n Uml\xe4ut lets each player create and control their own metal band. You come up with a name and style you like and play out various scenes from their lives and career. It's a GM-less, competitive game. Bands are rated at the end by being the most famous or most harmonious; some bands will be wildly successful but end up hating each other and split up, while others will meet only moderate success but be happy playing together for years. Gigs are played out over a series of hands of cards, with each band trying to accumulate the most Glory before then end of the Gig.
    \n
    \nIf Contenders is Raging Bull or Rocky, I guess this is Anvil or Spinal Tap.
