\n There are lots of really strange images floating around the internet. There are even blogs of pretty much nothing but, and to me those seem like a fertile place to look for ideas, albeit very strange ones.

To play this forum game, you make up and post some kind of RPG idea (a character concept, adventure seed, an idea for a new game, etc.) based on the picture that the poster above you posted, along with a WTF image for the next person to do the same with.

Some places you might look for pictures:
Awkward Family Photos
Boing Boing Offworld (look for the "One Shot" posts)
FAIL Blog (takes a little hunting to find stuff that's WTF rather than just FAIL and/or hilarious)
LPCover Lover
Picture Is Unrelated (some NSFW stuff)
Safebooru's 'what' Tag (anime/manga insanity)

Braver souls can delve into 4chan, DeviantArt, etc., and people with lots of time to kill might go through Flickr or Bibliodyssey.

Regardless, you can simply download an image, upload it to imgur, and copy the HTML code into your post.

    \nShetrit Yadidi - A Game About Retail Sales (From) Hell

    Inspired by "The Bottle Imp" by Robert Louis Stevenson. From the Wikipedia page...\n
    The story is about a working class native of Hawai\u02bbi, Keawe, who buys a strange bottle from a sad, elderly gentleman who credits the bottle with his wealth and fortune, and promises the imp in the bottle will also grant Keawe his every wish and desire.

    Of course, there is a catch \u2014 the bottle must be sold at a loss, i.e. for less than its owner originally paid, or else it will simply return to him. The currency used in the transaction must also be in coin (not paper currency or check). The bottle may not be thrown or given away. If an owner of the bottle dies without having sold it in the prescribed manner, that person's soul will burn for eternity in Hell.
    \nThe game is about a series of owners of the small retail store "Shetrit Yadidi", the ownership conditions of which are the same as the aforementioned Bottle.

    So, three player game:
    The Shopkeeper - "The Flesh" - The protagonist. Must sell everything in the store, or the store itself, before he dies. Otherwise the devil collects his soul.
    The Landlord - "The Devil". Provides adversity in-game, tries to prevent the Shopkeeper from selling inventory or selling the store to someone else.
    "The World" - Neutral player. Plays everyone/thing else.

    Each player picks a role. You play out the story of a protagonist until he manages to sell the store to some other hapless dupe, at which point roles rotate to the left. Game ends when either all inventory is sold or someone ends up burning in hell.
