    \n ClariceB and Eric Finley both have ideas where the players must act, as if they were actors on stage, for the game to go properly, rather than sit back and talk calmly.

    The smudging of line between player and actor/character (in Clarice's game, you play yourself in a fictional world connected to our own, like Narnia) may be hard to pull off with all players, but is awesome.

    \n Group Omega has a crazy retirement home black comedy game brewing called "...and then you die".

    As rossum said in his elevator pitch:
    "Think of it as Meatloaf and Medications; It's like the opposite of a typical RPG- there's no "getting more powerful" or "kicking more ass." At the end of your life, you want to be on top when one of you die. Plus it uses dominoes. "
