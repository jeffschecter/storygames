\n I'm pretty sure we've already had a thread like this, but even if I managed to dig it up, it wouldn't be up to date, would it?

I'm interested in story games (in the broadest sense) designed for two players, including those designed for two and up and those designed for up to two. I'm especially interested in non-obvious ones I might be overlooking.

The reason I'm asking is, I have a large set of very specific conditions I'm trying to slot a game into (basically, continuing an ongoing campaign, but switching systems) of which the only tangible, objective one is "2 players". I'm currently in the process of digging through my (sizable and quite comprehensive) collection of games looking for just the right one, but I figure I might easily be overlooking some promising candidate.

    Mars Colony
    Shooting the Moon
    Showdown
    Sweet Agatha
    Tales of the Fisherman\u2019s Wife
    Thy Vernal Chieftains

    In your specific situation I would also look into games that are merely accidentally fit for two players. These could range from Paranoia to Dread to 4th edition D&D depending on other requirements.
