\n Well, not my personal hands and feet, obviously.

These hands and feet:



that I picked up yesterday in the Works (home of cheap books and random crafty things). I saw them and thought, hmm, they could be used in an RPG.

But how could I use them? They come in differing sizes and colours, and all can be held in your own hand. They're made from flexible foam. You could bid with them or use them as tokens or something else...

What would you use them for?

    \n I'd use them for a thiefy game. Take a mixture of hands and feet (the greater the ratio of hands to feet, the greater a thief you are).

    Drop them from a height over a map of a room.

    If a hand lands on a valuable item, you steal it. If a foot lands on a trap or something that would make noise, you've attracted attention.

    Posted By: MattPosted By: majcherDefinitely attacking/defending tokens for a bidding mechanic in a punchy-kicky kung fu game.
    \nOoooh. My open red palm technique beats your blue ice foot technique!

    Also, could do combat by dropping the hands and feet on a a suitable diagram of the opponent.

    Yes! PCs have two skills, punchy and kicky, both divided into attack and defense. Each PC gets a color. During conflict you get an amount of little hands or feet equal to your skill in that quadrant. All the hands and feet are dropped from a ladder onto a lifesized diagram. Any attacks that land on the diagram can be blocked by any defenses that land on the diagram. Any left over attacks hit. Any left over defenses become extra counter attacks.

    Big hands and feet are special maneuvers. Haven't worked that part out yet.
