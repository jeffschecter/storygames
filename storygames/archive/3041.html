\n Hey guys,

I'd like to share with you my new beta-stage LARP game.

It's called The Internet.

Everyone needs one mace and one d20 to play.
Write some words on your mace. "Brain damage", "Trad", or "GNS" all work.
Now everyone stands around and pointlessly swings their maces around above their heads, seeing who they can hit with their words.

Last man standing gets to make some important statement incorporating whatever words were on his mace.
This important statement will be referenced in future sessions of The Internet.

Oh, and also, you should spend a good deal of time spitting on the d20 while playing The Internet.




Note: I figured I'd just share my visionary new game here instead of derailing an otherwise batfuck retarded thread elsewhere.

    \n Andy, on the subject of waves:

    I've noted that Story Games has a distinct tide to it.

    There's a wave of good discussions, then a wave of frenzied posting where not a lot of discussion happens but lots of lists get made, then a wave of people fighting each other, then a wave of everyone being passive aggressive and making comments like "well, now that Matt Synder's shut up, we can get back to..." and then after the passive aggression people start back at square one.

    good discussions are awesome.
    big lists of Essential Stuff are sometimes awesome.
    the fighty bits can occassionally produce something good or shake me out of my existing mindset.

    the passive aggressive stage is always bullshit and I hate it. This stage includes: a handful of backlash threads, a handful of people making stupid catty comments in other threads ("well, I would say awesome, but that word is now banned"), a handful of people swearing off these boards forever (some who I sincerely miss), and a handful of conversation kills.

    Shreyas always makes a point of saying "way to kill the thread just as we were starting to converse", when the passive aggressive phase begins. And... I tend to disagree with him DURING the fight, but totally appreciate his presence during the post-conflict cattiness.

    Note One: The waves of fights and ensuing bitchy passive aggression have included:
    -Story Games is too polite; designer insincerity; quality standards
    -Story Games is too mean; dealing with problem children within a community
    -Jargon vs. Understanding; barriers to entry

    Note Two: Of course I'm not blaming Matt Snyder in the above example. It's just one person I know has really fuelled a few fights on this board. There are tons of others too. (Matt, if you're reading this, please don't dispute it. It's true. Nothing personal.)

    \n A rough translation of what I posted last weekend on my Russian rpg-blog:

    The best game for modelling a forum discussion is not a LARP but a forum-based game. It should be a kind of dungeon crawling but in the style of kill monsters post answers and take their stuff scorn their ideas. You earn XP automatically as you post. When you have enough, you become a Moderator (name level). Your reputation is HP based and is damaged by others' attacks. You can choose between different classes:\n
      \n
    • barbarian: rarely has an opinion, but tells it without hesitation or side consideration, and even without asking. damn self-confident, resistant to reputation damage due to its virtual absense.
    • bard: does nothing, but inspires others for jokes, nice conversations, contests and other stuff.
    • cleric: helps newcomers, insensibly dragging them to his/her point of view. acts like a peace-maker. turns or rebukes spammers.
    • druid: throws neutral facts into discussion while keeping neutral position, proving him/herself to be well-read, well-lanned and able to google. can temporarily assume a shape of an adept of any theory to understand it and answer without being attacked by the enemies of that theory.
    • fighter: shows his/her broad skills in most forum debates, boosts his/her XP with external features (references, reputation, karma).
    • monk: unrivaled champion of generic discussions where no practical knowledge is needed ("would you allow dragon-riding paladins in your game?", "how do you understand alignment?", "omg have you seen the news?")
    • paladin: sacred defender of one and only point of view, from which he/she can be moved only by a bug in forum software.
    • ranger: ambidexterity allows to flood the forums with his/her posts twice as efficient. hides in the sight of a moderator. good saving throws for reaction, bad for changing one's mind.
    • rogue: attacks the weakest spots with dirty tricks, lies about facts, cuts the quote to his/her favour, bullshits, escapes destiny.
    • wizard: can add forum features, a friend of administrator, writes articles, draws pictures and otherwise tames familiars.
    \nAdventure examples:\n
      \n
    • Forum Newcomer: a first-level barbarian arrives, everyone makes fun of him/her, fighting among themselves and grinning to one another.
    • Lycantrope Quest: a new mind-boggling theory that makes people who had contact with it to say weird shit against their will.
    • Save the Princess: a first-level ranger girl after having posted nude photos in her blog and thus winning the hearts of many forum folks, arrives at a forum and is being attacked by a bunch of blood-thursty paladins. A Dragon Moderator gave her two warnings: one more and she'll be banned. How shall the poor girl defend herself against rampaging paladins without provoking Dragon's move?
    • Holy War: a group of courageous folks go to cleanse a forum full of ideological opponents and a high-level monster boss: a moderator!
    • New Universal Game System: a small group of unsophisticated gamers make a new system that is perfect for any type of play and not only this, it also fixes all the bugs of D&D, GURPS and Storytelling! They post an unfinished document full of msitypigns and insist on using a different thinking pattern they cannot explain but refer to in every post, and they don't speak Forge Moon Talk. Will they get trampled to the dirt or will they manage to win the hearts of others and get a new subforum at the Forge and RPG.net?
    \nI realise some points here make allusions to the things unknown to you, but I still hope it's somewhat funny. You can kick me if it's not.
