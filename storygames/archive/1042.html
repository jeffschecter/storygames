    Ken, Vim and Savvy sound nicely Victorian English to me. And, you're right, they have that street-putting-on-airs sound. Mike's right, though, they do kind of mean the same thing. (The spelling of Savvy is fine).

    Knack doesn't sound quite right to me. It's the right sort of street English, but sounds much too mundane. I've got a similar objection to Craft. Magic in Victorian England wouldn't be thought to be a craft: it'd be mystical.

    Veil's nice. I also really liked "Potence" and "Ley".

    Actually, I've just thought of something. Victorian England was a God-fearing society, so anything magic would naturally be related to God or the Devil. So you could go for a word related to religious power in some way: either on the God side (piety, favour, virtue) or on the Devil side (heresy, pact, possession). I rather like "Pact".
