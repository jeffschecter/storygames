\n So this is kind of weird: I dreamt up a game last night. In my dream, I was playing with Lukas, Ryan and Joe McD in a small dark room. I assume it was late night after a convention (I'm assuming this whole part is Go Play NW-inspired). After the dream, I was in a half-sleepy daze and tried to figure out how that game worked. Then I fell asleep again. So I'm not sure now how much of the game was in the dream and how much of it was hazy puzzling-together afterwards, but it was still cool, so I'm sharing it.

Here's the dream, with rule-explanations mixed in:

We're in that darkened room. I tell everyone to close their eyes. I'm GMing, and I'm describing things. They have to keep their eyes closed until, suddenly, I scream--and conflict resolution starts. A metronome (!) is kicked off and I toss down cards in front of people. They have to match them by searching through their own stacks (with eyes open now , trying frantically to find the right ones (is this Jungle Speed slipping in?), but every other tick of the metronome I toss in another card so they have to fight hard to keep up. There's something going on where they pass their matches to the other players who then have to match those, too, so it's possible to team up on a fellow player and make them fail. Apparently only the one who fails gets killed (or whatnot). In my dream, Lukas loses and I yell at him to run, and he runs out of the room. Later he comes back with a mask and knife and stabs me. But it's all staged! To scare Ryan and Joe. (I assume the inspiration there was Scream.) And then the game ends, and the whole game was really just set up for that false attack at the end. Some other stuff happened, but I forgot those parts.

So now I have this dreamed-up framework for a horror game. I'm not sure it's an RPG, at least it seems like the RPG elements fall away once the cards hit the table because everything turned into chaos at that point. But maybe that can be tweaked, somehow. I'll consider writing this up in a more technical way when I figure out how that can all hang together. It seems like there's actually some sort of interesting core there that could be exploited.

Anyway. My first dream design
