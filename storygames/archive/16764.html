    \n OK, so, uhm\u2026 Fiasco, of course. Penny, of course. 1001 Nights. Mouse Guard with pre-gens (it's full of those). Fables of Camelot. One-shot, pilot-only PTA, and "prep" (pitch) has to be considered part of play (i.e. keep it short and fun). Murderous Ghosts. Montsegur 1244. Love in the Time of Sei\xf0. Maybe Dirty Secrets in its Short Story mode? In a Wicked Age (playing it again later is optional, but recommended). Poison'd (you may or may not hit mechanical "the end" within time limit, but just cut it by the clock) (chargen is quick and fun). Maybe Psi*Run with convention-play rules to keep it short (chargen is quick and fun)? It's Complicated. Microscope.\n
    As for number of people: 2-15ish? Ideally, the games are suitable to run themselves without a strong GM as I might be bouncing between groups rather than running a game if we get a big turn-out.
    \nSo, you can give them copies of Penny, or pair them off and hand out Murderous Ghosts booklet, but I don't think there's much else you can have them play "by the book" w/out participating: you've got to teach by example. Once you got the ball rolling, though, it can spread fast enough.

    \nRemember Tomorrow deserves a strong mention, because it has the express advantage of bringing unrelated, very different characters near and sometimes into one another. It can help coalesce strangers with different ideas into a consistent narrative. Plus it's GMless. Set them up and walk away, come back to clarify and check in.

    After one playthrough, you can easily see how to convert it to Old Japan, The Age of Sail, Space Heresy, or a million other genres.

    Also, Mist Robed Gate and Zombie Cinema are very approachable, because they take movies and film as in in-road. The mechanics are mostly to get people focused and stay out of the way of the story. They are both GMless too, so you could move between multiple tables if need be.

    \n I think The Final Girl would be worth considering, if your co-workers are into slasher movies (or into trying a game that's based on slasher movies). It's no-prep, has very few rules, an innovative way of creating a cast of characters and then rotating through which ones you play on a scene-by-scene basis, and finishes up in less than 2 hours, in my experience.

    I personally think Remember Tomorrow would not be a good choice, unless you're experienced with facilitating it.

    Actually, generally, I would say you should pick something you are comfortable teaching and playing, or go with something that has literal pick-up-and-play-ability. I consider The Final Girl to be the latter, along with Zombie Cinema and maybe Lady Blackbird, if you're comfortable with GMing generally.

    \nKaleidoscope, Jackson Tegu's hack of Microscope (where you create an unwatchable foreign film) is great. It jettisons some of Microscope's structure (you don't play scenes), and it refines a couple things in beautiful ways. Simple, low-demand, low-intensity, fun.

    Fiasco uses lots of dice, and it can be tricky sometimes to figure out how all the seed ideas coalesce. But play is dead-simple, and often hilariously funny.

    Norwegian Style has several strong contenders.

    Keep It Sunny plays in 45 minutes, is good for 3-5 players, uses only pennies and a handful of cards. It builds on strong genre conventions (there's a plot summary that each episode follows: The Argument, Scheme, Counter-Scheme, Short Scenes, It All Collapses). Limitations: episodes begin with a cold start, it's based on a show about douchebags (which some find hilarious and others find a turn-off).

    Jenga Dread is a fantastic idea with the right facilitator.

    Mist-Robed Gate is a good idea if you come in with pre-generated characters and beautiful props. It can be hard to "build the situation" from a cold start, though, so I wouldn't play this with new players unless I'd already baked something tight in advance. Mist-Robed Gate might be too intense for some, thematically, as a "first game." But for others, it'd sing.

    GxB is cute and simple and has pre-generated characters so that you can start immediately. Good choice.
