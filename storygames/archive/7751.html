\n I am proud (actually I'm thrilled) to announce that Duty & Honour is now available
\n
\nDesigned by Neil Gow and illustrated by Peter Frain, Duty & Honour is a game of adventure and romance in Wellington\u2019s army during the Peninsular War. Inspired by the adventures of Richard Sharpe and Matthew Hervey amongst others, you take the role of soldiers and officers, fighting their way across Portugal and Spain. Can you defeat the French Major who has sullied your honour? Will you succumb to the machinations of the wicked Sergeant? Will the heaving busom of Lady Haversham seduce you into betraying your brother? All these adventures and more are possible, with Duty & Honour.
\n
\nThe game features the Mission system which allows players to define their own objectives, set their own rewards and create meaningful relationships within the game. Missions, which can be military, personal or even romantic, shape the gameplay, the advancement of the characters and the direction of your campaign.
\n
\nYou can read more about the game, check out some of the playtest APs and download game resources at http://www.omnihedron.co.uk/dutyandhonour
\n
\nYou can become a fan of Duty & Honour on Facebook at http://www.new.facebook.com/pages/Duty-Honour/27681967199
\n
\nThe game was designed as part of Collective Endeavour and the game forums exist at http://www.collective-endeavour.com
\n
\nThe game will also be supported by free .pdf releases of the Duty & Honour Almanac providing new background material, NPCs and Missions for the game.
\n
\n
\n
\nDuty & Honour is a 128 page book, perfect bound 7.5 x9.7 inch b&w; interior, colour cover
\n
\nIt will be officially released at Furnace Convention in Sheffield, UK on Saturday October 18th 2008. However, I have some copies available for pre-release.

    \n Heaving bosoms notwithstanding*, I'm immensely excited that Duty & Honour will shortly see the light of day.

    Neil is a very modest, self-effacing chap**, so I will say that D&H is a game that combines roleplaying, tactics and strategy most excellently. And: it is a military game where you can play characters of vastly differing rank and still have a fun time that doesn't devolve into "Corporal Jones, go do all the dirty work that I don't want to do while I just sit here and drink this rather fine claret!" "Bloody officers!" remarks Corporal Jones.

    Also, just a over a year ago, Neil came up to the Play Collective booth at Gen Con, slightly concerned about discussing his games design in public. I don't think he need ever have worried. Through taking on board critique and advice, engaging in thorough playtesting and harbouring a desire to produce the best possible game he could, all the while staying true to his original vision of creating something that would give the action-packed feel of the Sharpe novels, Neil has produced an exceptionally fine first published game.

    Now I just need to wait for the printed version to drop through my letterbox.

    Cheers
    Malcolm

    *Not that I'm averse, you understand. But a time and a place for everything.
    **Truly remarkable when you consider he's from Newcastle.
