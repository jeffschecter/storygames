\n

\n
\n This weekend I'm going to be running a one-shot of Nine Worlds. I've got some ideas on how I want to approach it, but I was wondering if anyone here has any advice.
\n
\nMy intention is to:
\n
\n1. Encourage players to create at least some Muses that tie into each other's characters.
\n2. Draw up a relationship map of all the NPCs implied by those Muses.
\n3. Ask each player what they want to do to start achieving their Muses, and frame the first scenes accordingly.
\n
\nThe session should be about four hours long.
\nI'll be GMing, and I've never run Nine Worlds before.
\n
\nThere are four players - all experienced GMs, so I have no worries about designing good Muses or proactive play. The players range from "familiar with the rules and run Nine Worlds before", to "unfamiliar with the setting".
\n
\nSome related threads that I've found include:
\n
\nVaxalon and Valamir offer advice on setting up a game.
\n
\nSimplifying the setting, and prepping for a two or three session game.
\n
\nAny help or thoughts would be appreciated!

    \n Doing chargen with the players is smart, Steve. For most one-shots, I would have pregens, but for 9W, it's really good to do creation together. I would change your "encourage" in item 1 to "require."

    You might want to go in with a situation set up, to get the ball rolling. Something like, Prometheus is missing and you're the only people who know where he went. The veil that hides Earth is going to fail unless he returns. Go!

    \n What John said.

    For a bit of fun, you could try doing the technique Daniel created in this thread for Dust Devils. He makes a list of motivations, then each player picks from the list. It's spontaneous and relatively quick. Ideal for a one-shot. For Nine Worlds, make a list of Muses that people can take. For example, stuff like:

    List 1
    In love with the Martian Legion Commander's wife.
    Wants to kill Eros
    Transporting stolen keys to the Ninth Gate to Apollo.
    Find Atlantis and assassinate its king.

    List 2
    Needs to infiltrate the Aquarians inner circle.
    Start a civil war on Luna.
    Free father from Uranus prison camp.

    Etc.

    This isn't an especially coherent list -- it's just examples I came up from the top of my head. With a bit cleaner list, you're off to the races for a good game.

    \n The game went fantastically! I cannot recommend Nine Worlds highly enough. It's basically no-prep at any stage. The Muses create rock-solid ideas for conflicts, the resolution system and rewards are intuitive, and the setting is totally customisable by the group. I've been recommending this to everybody.
    \n
    \nOur thread, analysing the game, is here.
    \n
    \nJohn, the technique you suggest for a 'situation set-up' is a good one. I read out brief summaries of all the worlds, and we hooked onto the Twilight Fever / Apollo-and-Hades-have-cut-a-deal material. Out of that we got characters:
    \n
    \n- Asariel, the dead son of Apollo. Died of the Fever, escaped from Hades as a shade, is coming to warn Apollo.
    \n- Makarios, an adventuring traveller transporting Asariel to Sol.
    \n- Caros, a double-agent working in Sol's underworld. His girlfriend is dying of the fever and needs medical treatment.
    \n- Dr Daedalus, an honoured physician searching for a cure to the Twilight Fever, and absolutely loyal to Apollo.
    \n
    \nMy favourite NPC of the game was the Twilight Fever (Power 9).
    \n
    \nMatt, you've got an unbelievably good game here. I have some ideas about its presentation that I'd like to pitch to you, and I'll email you over a scan of the handout I made for how Points get distributed.
