    \n I bought the paper version. From the reading looks like fun though getting control aka becoming gm can happen only after the lots of sessions. Players must first get enough points. Other fun things:
    \n- gm has its own character in the game,
    \n- the scenario is semi structured - gm chooses the agenda (killing player character, subvert or assasinate world leader, gain a new resource, raise the controller's control level) of the adventure and the goal of the players is to stop the agenda of the gm,
    \n- gm has somewhat limited resources set by his conspiracy and there rules are for this
    \n- players are awarded for succumbing for weaknesses and uncovering new info on conspiracy which can probably lead the game in interesting directions
    \n
    \nThere are quite a lot of rules for a small book it is (hit location chart, modifiers in combat, many specific skills, etc.) and the mechanic is more or less mainstream besides the control and conspiracy thing which may be or not your cup of tea. Also, there's this conflict between refeering the game and playing the conspiracy and its leader. There could be similar troubles like in Burning Empires - setting apart these two roles.
    \n
    \nAnyway, the game looks interesting.
