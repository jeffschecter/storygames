\n Bear with me SG, I promise I won't make a new thread every time I finish an art piece, but this one is special.
Last night I stayed up in hopes of finishing my game(the one for the contest with Jarrod), ''Bitches ain't shit: A game of lovecraftian horror''. Alas instead I ended up refreshing my email account in hopes of hearing back from a multitude of artists, concerning the cover for my main game, Van Dread. Things weren't looking so good, partly because of my low, low budget.
I started working on a piece in photoshop, which ended up beeing the cover for my game. Six hours later I was looking at the screen in disbelief at what I accomplished. I have never done anything remotely similar in style in my life! Since SG is my online home, I wanted to share it with my SG buddies. Here's the still unfinished cover for Van Dread:




In order to make this thread worth the bandwith load on SG's servers, let's hear it from you:
What have you accomplished while working on a game that you never thought you could? Be it untapped skills or the creation of something that made you really proud of yourself.

    Posted By: Jonathan WaltonCan you get even more insane? Or are you already at SAN 0 so it doesn't really matter what horrible things you encounter?
    \nThere's a sanity mechanic. All characters have a derangement (they have been diagnosed since they are in fact incarcerated), but they can choose if they are truly insane or not(since a character could have been incarcerated because he was talking about monsters that he knows exist, yet everyone else doesn't). Medication helps keep the effects under check.
    I'm thinking that every character is a ticking bomb. If they get stressed out too much, they snap and either kill themselves or go on a murder spree(according to their diagnosed derangement. Effects may vary).

    Would it highjack the threat too much to ask for a bit more detail. Maybe bits about the system? We could also start a new thread for that...

    I don't mind, I was already kind of feeling bad about posting a thread solely to post my cover artwork.
    I'm using a hacked version of Fate, along with some nifty mechanics(including the wounding mechanic Paul suggested in another thread). Characters are essentially paranormal investigators, only with a more hands-on approach. They use guns, artifacts(enchanted items) and spells(heavily based on components and ritualism) to achieve their goals.
    Main inspirations are John Constantine comics and a bit of BPRD. In essence, I want to make a game that's scary. Sure you can shoot shit and act tough, but you're scared shitless while doing it.
