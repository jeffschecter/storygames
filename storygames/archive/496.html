One colloquialism I've seen on various fora is using violence to the male sex to reflect some kind of interpersonal friction going on. Such as: "Next person who mentions Exalted gets cock-punched." I think this is funny and probably harmless, but I also feel like it's probably ultimately exclusionary. I mean, when's the last time someone said "Next person who mentions D20 gets breast-thwacked"? Not so much. So I suggest reaching for metaphors of pure, non-sexualized violence if you need, such as: "Next person who mentions Dogs in the Vineyard gets run over with my car." See, isn't that better? But this is probably a minor thing - there's a good chance that no one is offended, but also it's something that may make a community more inclusive. Maybe.

The "Muy Macho" thing: I don't feel the maleness is, itself, the point. What other metaphors work?

I actually feel I've seen lots of good poker play that taps into some of what's going on.

  • I like stakes to be high, and I play as good as I can in order to win. Of course, I do not cheat.
  • I will do everything to win, and I need you to play as good as you can as well.
    ** Note: Good poker players often complain that inexperienced casual players often "mess up" their guesses/calculations by not playing "hard" enough. I can explain this later if needed.
  • You might take all my chips: but by the rules of poker, that's okay. That we're playing poker in the first place means we're both taking each other's chips.
    ** Many poker players are upset when they lose their chips, and act like tools. I say they are bad poker players.
  • I'm willing to go all in an any time, and will do so in order to push you out and, hence, get closer to winning. You have to be willing to do the same.
  • Poker is AWESOME.

Any takers?
