    Posted By: Graham WThere's arealrisk that this ends up as an indie love-in, where we only review games positively, and then nobody will take the reviews seriously.
    \nYeah. But I'm going to turn that on its head.

    I think we should only review games that are actually worth reviewing. If the game is good, tell people about it! Good for the readers to know about good games, and good for the publisher to get the word-of-mouth.

    If it's bad, don't review it. Almost all reviews, no matter how bad, lead to more sales, and IMO, a bad game doesn't deserve that people spend their time helping the publisher sell it. If you have to talk about it, let the designer know what's wrong with the game so he/she can fix it for later - that helps both designer and customer.

    \n Couldn't agree more that "bad" reviews are just as important as "good" reviews. Some of this is a matter of perspective. So, Christopher is a "reviewer". He gets games to review.     And this seems like a fine place to drop a funny anecdote about that "miserable bastard" Christopher Richeson. Christopher reviewed one of my products and gave it a tough but fair (and very extensive) review. Christopher chimed in with an (admittedly) very silly layout error in that same book. That book: the four-times-ENnie-nominated Helios Rising. But I will say this: Christopher is one of the top 3 reviewers in this business ... precisely because he is a miserable bastard. His opinion means something; he has credibility.
