    \n I treat IndieGoGo with suspicion and sometimes silent derision for any situation other than "The creator comes from a nation where Kickstarter is unavailable for use", with one exception: Fixed Funding campaign (read: "If we don't make the goal, we don't keep the money")

    "Flexible Funding" (the option most people use, where you get to keep the money even if the goal is not met) means that you're throwing money at someone with no obligation by them to do anything to return it if the goal is not met. I've seen it used too many times for situations where:
    * The creator tried to raise money on KS, failed. Rather than regroup, rethink, pause, etc, the creator immediately starts an IndieGoGo campaign. The goal is not met, the creator keeps the money (several thousand dollars or more), and in most cases the project never materializes.
    -- One single exception I saw was a project called "The Other Brothers", a video game platformer. They eventually developed it for iOS, but apparently have not sent the rewards to any of their backers, who are starting to write angry comments.
    * The creator suffered one or a string of failures on Kickstarter (including things like "Kickstarter doesn't approve the project for some reason"), and moved to IndieGoGo to Just Get At That Money. Project never appears to proceed from there.
    * To "just get money", with no creative project. "I want to study for a semester in Europe/Asia/etc; and - ew - I don't want to get a job here or there to fund it, so please give me money! As a reward, I'll take selfies of me having fun." I noticed this trend when I searched for "Japan" on Kickstarter (a bunch of culture, music, game, theater, writing projects) and on IndieGoGo (hundreds of folks around college age simply raising donations for overseas study or vacation; which horrified me given all the time I spent raising the money (and working) to make it happen in my own case).

    The above two situations (the project ones, not the skuzzy ones) I've seen happen over and over again so many times that I just can't take IGG seriously, not while "Flexible Funding" exists as an option on their site.

    There are very few IndieGoGo projects that I'll back at the Flexible Funding level. I'll take more risks on Fixed Funding (ala "Kickstarter"), or where it's clear that the creator is in a country that Kickstarter wasn't available in.

    -Andy
