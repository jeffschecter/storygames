    \n Aaron,

    Thanks for the question. When you describe your character using a piece of gear or relying on the current assistance or past advice of an association, then you roll the number of dice equal to your Gear or Associations rating. In addition, if the gear or association you are using is the one in your special descriptor, then you can choose to roll one descriptor die as well (make sure it's a different color than your other dice so you remember which one it is).

    So when Bric Taylor is using a crow bar to bash someone, then you roll 2d6 for his Gear. But if Bric is using his rosary beads (his Gear descriptor) to hang from the bottom of a biplane, then you roll 2d6 for his Gear, plus you can roll 1d6 for his descriptor.

    Hopefully that helps. Feel free to hit me with any other questions.

    Cheers,

    Eric
