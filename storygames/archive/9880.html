\n Good day to you all, I'd like to plug my latest RPG offering: Labyrinths & Lycanthropes!



A game like no other - an RPG of Loot and Legends!

It's a kick ass comedy dungeon bash featuring rather splendid cover art from Mr Remi Treuer. And some weird beasties from Gregor Hutton.

Choose from the only three classes you'll ever need: Fighter, Ninja or Mage. Or combine classes to play a Fighter-Mage, a Mage-Ninja or even a Fighter-Ninja-Mage. The combinations are literally virtually endless (until exhausted).

Beware though, in the dread labyrinths you will likely encounter: giant sharks, chicken berserkers, dapper skellingtons, penguin necromancers, and well dressed orcs. Not to mention whatever your own twisted imagination cooks up\u2026

L&L is the fantasy RPG you have been waiting for - possibly. It\u2019s a leaner meaner dungeon bash stripped down to bare essentials. You can be up and running in under 20 minutes. A rotating GM mechanic and random Labyrinth generation puts the game into this RPG!

Available from Prince of Darkness Games and coming soon to IPR.

Play badass adventurers - kill monsters, take their stuff, go up levels - you know you want to...

If you like Dungeon Squad or Drowning and Falling L&L will be right up your back alley.

Cheers for looking
Joe

    \n Hi guys

    Tristan - the big difference is that L&L is not an OD&D clone or retro game! It's a parody of such games - it's a hell of a lot faster. Combat and magic are far more streamlined - no spell lists it's more freeform. And the emphasis is on comedy - making other players laugh is more powerful in game than any particular build options.

    \n I've played it three times now (twice at RopeCon in Finland and once at GameWang) and run it once (at GenCon with Tom Russell, John Wilson and err someone else...). It's great for lazy GMs like me. You flip cards down on to a table to make a dungeon, the GM secretly places a couple of false endings and the big bad guy, and you're off.

    If you like bawdy, goofy dungeon-bashing fun then it's great. Elfs, Drowning and Falling, etc.

    The random monsters are pretty cool too as the GM can allocate ones you've all made up pre-game or add in some on the fly (Furlings! Nakedlings! Gelatinous Infections...). Their strength is regulated by the party's levels and treasure is regulated by how much treasure you have already.

    The GM does have a vested interest in seeing you hosed too (since it levels his own character up) but it's all good natured fun.

    Yeah, I think it's ace! Oh, also the ranges did influence 3:16. Absolutely!
