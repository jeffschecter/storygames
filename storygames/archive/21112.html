Mythos Mixtape

\n
Dice
\n
Adjective scale & dice from Fudge
\n
Character generation
\n
Professions / clich\xe9s from WaRP (Over the Edge), relationship map, desires, bonds, DP:s from Dramasystem. (You don't need the full player count you'd need from Dramasystem.)
\n
Investigating
\n
"Describe where you search it" from Old School Primer
\n
Talking
\n
Petitioner/Granter from Unframed
\n
Fighting system
\n
Initiative, combat & health system from Kutulu. Hack: instead of counting pool successes for wounds, count blank dice in the 4dF roll you used for the attack/counter-attack
\n
Effects of mythos contact on humans (instead of "Insanity")
\n
Just lift the entire chapter 3 from Kutulu
\n
Keeper-side prep
\n
Mythos sandbox from Silent Legions + other OSR tools. Think in terms of "Aspects" from Fate when you write your prep. + Hack: See "time checks" below.
\n
\n

Time checks

\nFor every location, and by location "The warehouse if the PCs go there at night" might be very different from "The warehouse if the PCs go there at daytime", come up with five random events and a time amount. All five events should be such that they put serious wrench in the gears. Two of the events should be pretty ordinary, and one of the three rare events should be very dangerous. A good default time span is one hour.

Work fractally, i.e. you might have a list for "any time, anywhere on Earth" that you can, if you want to, override with "On the streets of Paris, at night" that you can, if you want to, override with "On Rue de la Ferronnerie at night".

Example of "ordinary" wrenches: A black cat starts crying out loudly, attracting attention to the PCs, neighbours will wake up and go to the PCs. A nosy police officer starts questioning the PCs, possibly arresting them. For the unusual wrenches, here's where you put in some attacks from the yog-sothothery du jour. Byakhees attack, maybe. Remember that this might create a time gap depending on when you are in the Kutulu perception cycle for that particular mythos aspect.

Use this format for the lists: [Ordinary blank], [Ordinary plus], [Unusual blank], [Unsual plus], [Unusual and very dangerous minus]

Now, whenever the designated time span passes in the fiction, roll 4dF. It's fine if the keeper does this (do it openly), and does this without calling undue attention to it (explain in advance what this roll means and how it works). The idea is that the keeper is talking in diegetical terms about how time is passing while the players look anxiously at the dice. "You go to bed for the night [while rolling the nightly check, which comes up +\u2610\u2610-, i.e. no wrench]. The next day..." or, "You go to bed for the night [rolls ----, i.e. Byakhee attack, cause for a time gap if players are in phase 1 with Byakhee] and it's ice cold, you reach for the blanket but your hands touch only dew-sogged grass. You're lying naked in a field under the starry sky."

If there are three minuses, look at the last die to see which of the ordinary events happen. If all four dice show the same result, use the face shown to see which of the unusual event happens.

You might also co-assign significant advances in the enemies' plans to the unusual wrenches. They get closer on whatever clock you've set up behind the screen. But, that doesn't mean there shouldn't also be a wrench; don't just put "there's a blood moon that night", something must happen that the PCs might have to deal with. However, the tables are written down (behind the screen). If the PCs have taken precautions against a particular event, let them benefit fully from those precautions. By "wrench" I mean something that *might* be a wrench, depending on what the PCs are doing.

(Alternative: Use cards, that you flip over, with meanings assigned behind the screen. A tarot deck might be very flavorful if you use the Kult mythos instead of Cthulhu's. About 5/6, 6/7 or so of the cards should be "blank" or just the passingest of fluff. Some scent, a passing feeling... (Because this is combined random table and encounter check in one.))

Do keep the lists alive, change things on them depending on what happens in the fiction. If you have plenty of ideas (maybe you found online or in another game or in books) you can have a separate big list to pull from and put into these smaller lists.

Note: the time checks don't replace the people and beings that are location keyed. In D&D terms: stock the "dungeon" normally (with receptionists and CEOs and socialites and thugs and cops and priests and byakhees and cultists and what have you), the time checks are the "wandering monster check" on top of that.\n

Examples

\n
\n
Character
\n
"Dr Alice Jones is a great scholar, mediocre fencer, superb chef, and a poor friend. She's torn between the freedom of solitude, and the connection to others."
\n
Group
\n
Alice has lost touch with her gregarious sister Mary, but now they've met for the first time in about two years, in order to look for their missing father.
\n
Investigating
\n
"I look in the wardrobe." "It's empty. The paint still smells fresh."
\n
Talking
\n
"We're looking for a Ms. Whately. Can we see her, please?"
\n
Fighting
\n
Alice rolled a sub-poor fencing result [---\u2610] against a cultist who rolled a great [\u2610+-\u2610] result. The cultist was the one of the two who succeed. Alice takes one wound for every blank face the cultist rolled, so two.
\n
Mythos effect on mind
\n
"The tiny, little creature slowly grasps at you with its enormous hands. You're like a grain of sand between its finger and thumb."
\n
\n

Advice

\n
    \n
  • Prep widely; don't think "funnel to revelation", think "world filled with many mysteries that go deep".
    • \n
  • Don't use a meta resource such as drama tokens, fudge points, fate points, inspiration, or gumshoe points.
    • \n
  • Only use dice for time checks and for things that carry physical risk to the character, such as fights. For non-dangerous situations, either go to player skill or use the listed value, i.e. a "poor friend" won't have contact in the new city (don't make this call subjectively, use some sorta system, principle or keeper-facing rule for it). In 5e terms, "passive abilities" \u2014 compare the character's scores to the scores listed in your prep. It's the ultimate "Let It Ride" from BW: a permanent roll of all blanks.
    • \n
  • Don't use the mountain witch trick for this particular game. Maintain identifying stance. Stay IC always. Players, don't say "Alice goes down to the first floor." Say "I go down to the first floor."
    • \n
  • Never mention numbers.The spirit of the system is for it to be invisible at the table, but based on hard principles behind the screen. It's a sandbox game, complete with maps, prep, random encounters, "prepped hard landscape with player agency w/o any prepped outcomes or plot" that, to anyone listening in, looks like a nineties freeform game.
    • \n
\n
"It's impossible to tell CA from transcript." Yes. Make it so.

Therefore, do tell the players beforehand how the game is set up. Perhaps show them some prep (for a different scenario), tell them how the time checks work etc.
