\n I'm one of those people that likes to game with BGM going, and I make playlists for all my games. 3:16 has gotten in my face like a pissed off drill instructor and directed me to make a playlist. I tried to get the music to follow a narrative of sorts as well, with some quieter tracks in the middle after the high-powered start, then more intensity and finally a winding down to sort of a requiem at the end. Here's what I have so far:

The Gate, Enigma - Uses a sample of Carmina Burana, which is just the right sort of epic, bombastic tone I want to strike.
Marching Off to War, Motorhead - "You'll never understand if you weren't there"
Entrenched, Bolt Thrower - ""Violated - breached are all defensive lines, the enemy has victory in sight"
Hy Pro Glow, Anthrax - "How many ways can you try, how many ways can you die"
Granite Wall, Bolt Thrower - "Mourning the tragic loss, life cut short, this broken column, immortal"
Futile Escape, Aliens Soundtrack
Lento, Alien 3 Soundtrack
Death Dance, Alien 3 Soundtrack
Veteran of the Psychic Wars, Blue Oyster Cult - "I have used up all my weapons and my armor is destroyed, wounds are all I'm made of"
Heavy Duty/Defenders of the Faith, Judas Priest - "Let's all join forces, rule with an iron hand"
Anti-Tank (Dead Armour), Bolt Thrower - My favorite song on Those Once Loyal "The kill rate ratio rising, one hundred to one"
Disposable Heroes, Metallica - "Barking of machine-gun fire does nothing to me now"
Flashback, Ministry - "I'm gonna laugh like a motherfucker"
Those Once Loyal, Bolt Thrower - "To those once loyal now wreathed in crimson"
When Cannons Fade, Bolt Thrower - "I close my eyes and even now the distant memory remains of the last laments to be played"
One, Metallica - "Darkness, imprisoning me, all that I see, absolute horror"
Lullaby Elegy, Alien 3 Soundtrack

Bolt Thrower weighs the list down heavily, but I'm okay with that since BT's themes and imagery match up with 3:16 perfectly. Those Once Loyal is a fantastic album and the tracks just nail the 3:16 vibe. The Alien 3 soundtrack is suitably creepy (for downtime or those moments right before the ambush hits). I need to put more instrumental stuff in so more soundtracks for that. It's about an hour's worth of music but I can easily fill it out with another hour with the stuff I have. I'm going to lay in a few tracks by Sepultura and Napalm Death too. And more Motorhead!

So, what's on (or will be on) your 3:16 playlist (and why - nice to hear why you chose certain tracks too)?
