    \n I'm thankful for the chance to make my own podcast with two great friends. Mucho thanks to Chris Perrin and Chris Norwood, my buddy co-hosts. If it weren't for our gamer resolutions for 2007, Canon Puncture wouldn't exist. I'm especially thankful for all of Perrin's passion and hard work, his vaunted "planning" and "process", concepts I still find a bit foreign. This podcast may not be the shining jewel of role-playing game podcasts (yet), but through it, I've met many inspirational creators and had a bunch of interesting conversations.

    I'm thankful for Judd Karlson IMing me one Sunday out of the blue while he was working at the library and bored. As a result, we've started a friendship that's led to me getting another gamer buddy along with some awesome gaming (as in, it has created awe in me at the amount of fun and engagement I've enjoyed) and plain old school gamer hanging out.

    I'm thankful for swapping insults with Ryan Macklin and then interviewing him for Canon Puncture, finding out he's a pompous ass, but my kind of guy. This led to some fun podcast chatting, gossip, infrequent gaming (my fault) and the single best session I've played in forever.

    I'm thankful for meeting so many intelligent and fun people at GenCon. Story Games forum denizens rock.
