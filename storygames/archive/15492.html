    \n Hey Davey: What both Roger AND Paul said: There's not a ton in the same field of "GMless story-telling games with bidding", but if you remove the "with bidding", you have Microscope, or the interesting (sometimes confusing due to its stance on conflict, but every game I've played in has been great fun) shock: social science fiction. Jason Morningstar doesn't pimp his own games for modesty reasons, but his games Fiasco and Love in the Time of Sei\xf0 might fill this niche.

    Let us know what criticisms you had, that will help us narrow it down. But at a glance, if you don't mind "no bidding", Microscope might be a solid fit: It straddles the "group world creation" and "roleplaying" fields in much the same way Universalis does.

    EDIT: Nice call, Paul, re Archipelago II (and free from the web to boot! this is a great game as well!). However, if you liked the structure of Universalis, the structure of Microscope might be more form-fitting: ArchII is pretty light in comparison.
