Posted By: EricRegarding the We Owe list and one-dimensional characters not coming back, I pointed out that it's only a disincentive if I want to play that character again. If I just want to compete and win, it's not a relevant deterrent. Now, maybe that's a _feature_, since I have then effectively volunteered to take the "villain of the week" role in perpetuity.
\nThat's half right. The owe list gives you two things: recurrence, and bonus dice when you want them. If you never get onto the owe list, at the end of the session you'll be at the mercy of the people who got onto it early and often. You're volunteering to play not just the villain of the week, but an ineffectual one.

If you want to play a serious villain of the week, get onto the owe list several times early in the session, then scratch yourself back off of it for the advantage dice before the session ends. This is the strongest single-session tactical play, and it has the benefit of making for an interesting, threatening villain instead of a one-dimensional one.\n
...clearly, the optimal first move in IAWA is something like "as the clock strikes noon, my Far Reaching plans come to fruition and I gain immortal mastery of reality."
\nThat's not a legal move in IaWA at all, any more than it would be in D&D. In IaWA, like in D&D, you describe what your character does, you don't ever say what happens.

It might help you stick to legal moves if you limit yourself to "I do" constructions. "I walk down to the marketplace" or "I fight my way to the throne room" or "I shout magic words at heaven." Strictly speaking, IaWA doesn't demand 1st person - you could say "my guy Marku walks down to the marketplace" instead - but just be sure you're always talking about what your character does, not what happens to or around him. Even "I gain" or "I hurt you" or the famous "I convince you" go too far - you're saying the outcome you hope for, not what your character actually does. Try "I reach out and take," "I punch," and "I say" instead.

The other players' legit response to "my far reaching plans come to fruition and I gain immortal mastery of reality" isn't "oh no I stop you!" but "you do? How? These alleged far reaching plans, they were what? Why do you think they come to fruition now?" Or even, if you're being particularly egregious, "come on Eric, back to the game."

    \n Getting your characters onto the owe list is a thing you have to figure out how to do, it won't always happen by itself. The trick to it starts at character creation. If you create a character whose best forms apply directly to her winning her best interests, she won't make the owe list. You have to make a character whose best interests won't follow straight from her best forms.

    So let's say you've got your grizzled warrior character. Best forms: Directly, With Violence. If you say "it's in my best interests to crush my enemies," you're effectively writing yourself off the owe list. If you say "it's in my best interests to win the heart of the queen," though, you're setting yourself up to go on the owe list a lot.

    \n IAWA gives you a broad spectrum of choice for what kind of character you want to play. You can play the guy whose strengths align with his interests. It's fun. You can also play the guy whose strengths don't align well with his interests. This is also fun. On top of those, you can play a guy who is very driven to achieve his goals or you might play a guy who is his own worst enemy and just doesn't act in his own best interest at all. Both fun.

    The game design doesn't ask you to make one of those character types as the one right way to play. You get to play lots of different characters as you build your series of chapters, so the system lets you try out all kinds of different roles as you go.
