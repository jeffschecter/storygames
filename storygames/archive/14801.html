    \n Um, that's all really good advice, though, isn't it?

    1.) Start by exposing yourself to a variety of systems.
    2.) If you want to make a new game, see if existing systems can be drifted to do what you want.
    3.) Design exists as part of a feedback loop with actual play. Design through play, not prior to it.
    4.) If you decide to publish, start small, and start local.
    5.) After putting out something small and learning from that experience, assess your next steps based on community excitement.
    6.) You can either seek out an established publisher or go about self publishing.

    Like, the video is absolutely clueless, but it also captures all of our community's best practices, does it not?
