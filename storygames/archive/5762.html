\n I looked at previews and read some discussions about the book, and when I finally received my copy and read it, I was quite pleased. A lot of this is stuff that I've been working through personally, but the book puts it into words and ties it to other similar techniques. I like the authorial voice; I think it would make a very pleasant (if short) book-on-tape... The sidebar examples are really useful material, and they make the techniques and methods being discussed stand out. One of my pre-reading concerns was that the book would be iSUPERMACHO! about everything. Not that I'm opposed to that presentation, but the way it turned out is my friendlier, in my opinion.

My favorite bits: The Tell Stories chapter was full of ideas that I can see myself using in the very next game I play. I also dug the first chapter (Play) as a pep-talk... you know "Hey, chill. This is a lot easier than it might seem." I like that.

I've got some minor issues with the book (mostly insignificant, really), but I'm glad to have spent my money on it. I'm hoping to absorb it handily and then start incorporating the impro advice when game play triggers them. So we'll see!

    \n Graham,

    I think the Play chapter is important because that stuff doesn't get said much. I think my favorite part (due to personal experiences) is the parts that say "don't try to be clever." I've played in a lot of games where there was one person who tried desperately to be funny, and it just ended up being awkward for everyone else.

    Criticisms... well, the look of the book isn't great. It definitely seems self published (which isn't necessarily a bad thing...). One thing that threw me off aesthetically was the chapter opening pictures. I like them, but I would rather have seen a blank page so that each chapter began on a facing page. Uh... if that makes any sense; I'm having a hard time describing it. Blank pages are usually objectionable, but in this case, it would have made the break between sections clearer. I have a hard time criticizing the text - it's just a bunch of "try this and see if you like it" type stuff, and it's presented in an inoffensive manner.

    Anyway, I think it's overall a good book with very few obvious problems and any problems there are are mostly subjective anyway. It's not perfect, but it's clear and does what it means to do. It's hard to find fault with that.
