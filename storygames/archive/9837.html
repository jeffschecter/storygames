\n Hey, so this is a thread to give short, like Twitter-length posts talking about good roleplaying sessions you've had recently that were great stuff, but not enough to write an AP about or anything. I sometimes feel like discussing roleplaying online always leans towards the crazy awesome or the terrible, with little acknowledgment of what normally happens when folks come to the table. So this is a thread for brief shout-outs to normal, solid play.

For example, today was my final session with StoryGames Boston (since I'm moving to Seattle) and the second session of a 2-shot of The Mountain Witch. The first session was okay, but this session was really solid. Only two characters survived. There were some cool visual effects where different parts of the ghost city were only visible in flashes when clouds passed overhead. Chuck's character became the new mountain witch at the end, a very fitting ending. After that we played a one-lap race of Formula De with six racers and only three finished without crashing into the stands. Then we went for beers and food at one of our favorite local hangouts down the street. All in all, a great celebration of what SGBoston has been in the three years since we put it together in 2006. Yay!

    \n Hey Jonathan! Welcome to Seattle!

    Here's what I played at Go Play NW and don't have time to write an AP for. I'm sorry there are so many exclamation points:

    Wilhelm ran some OD&D (Swords & Wizardry). We fought goblins! We made a map! I found treasure in an acid pool using a 10 foot pole and four large sacks!

    I ran Principia for seven people and it worked! Invisible Lucrezia Borgia stabbed Ludovico and Caterina Sforza in the back. Then Caterina rose from the dead because she'd previously been bitten by vampire Machiavelli! Florence wasn't destroyed, but a new Inquisition was launched.

    We played Microscope. None of us could remember the story of the Lost City of Kandor, so we made one up. Political intrigue and scientific hubris destroyed Krypton. Later, Earth was sucked into a black hole. There was no Superman. Microscope is a new kind of game, and it deserves all your love.

    Ogre ran a game where we were the entourage for a 1999 Christian Rock Band called Millennium Crater. I got to see a whole bunch of the Story Gamers strut their Story Gamer stuff, and loved every minute of it.

    I ran Car Wars for some old-school grognards (and Sage). It was good. Sage wrecked, I think three of his own cars, and who knows how many others.

    Anima Prime is a tight, tight game. Anime Hells Angels attacked our train and we burned them with fire. Then we did a super combo team move on the Emperor! The game has been playtested a lot, and it shows.

    I got to run Principia again! Michelangelo, Lucerzia Borgia, and Machiavelli decided to use Leonardo's flying machine to get over the walls and negotiate with / assassinate Ludovico Sforza.

    We played Committee for the Exploration of Dungeons with Eric Boyd. It was fun to watch a bombastic wizard, a dodgy Dwarf and an untrusty burglar trade barbs and brags at the Inn.

    \n OK, here's some Normal Awesome Tweets from Go Play NW:

    I played a Dwarf in Swords and Wizardry. I got props for employing a Manchester accent rather than the typical Scottish. We hightailed it with a load of treasure, and I never had to swing my axe once!

He received a brain-boost from conniving commie villain Doctor Steel (played by Alan Barclay), and rose from the rubble a vengeful and erudite Marxist!

    \n Fiasco at GPNW: I had a lot of fun playing Tyler Wright, a really obtuse screw-up who makes terrible choices. I usually play competent characters and "straight men." I'm delighted when they face difficult situations and choices, and even if things go horribly wrong for them. But I'm usually framing scenes or encouraging others to frame scenes to that end, not having my character's choices lead directly to mishap.

    Recent Burning Wheel: We've only had one short session. I hope to do a lengthier post later about some of the scenes and charcters. For now I'll say I'm really excited about the potential of the situation we've created. It's 886 AD Eire in Tirath, a viking-ruled village. We've got both Norse and Irish who are Christians or pagans, rebellious and acquiescent factions, and with wild trouble-filled connections, familial and otherwise, between all the characters. It's a powder keg on one level, but the connections mean it will likely go off in slow-motion and with great pathos.

    \n Did some story gaming with my wife for the first time this week - she's never played before. We forget how many preconceptions and learned behaviors we have until we play with someone who has never played before. Very fresh and very cool.

    One brief scene:

    Protagonist gets summoned by a wizard who was hoping to make a pact with a demon. Protagonist offers to carry out the task instead, in exchange for future aid. The wizard wants a certain woman to fall madly in love with him, but when asked what love means to him fumbles for an answer. Protagonist says...

    "That is the biggest tragedy in the world. How can I give you what you want if you don't know what love is?"

    Good stuff.

    \nDudes, I love this kind of thing: Bite Size Actual Play (maybe someone needs to create a Twitter Group or something for bite-sized AP awesomes). But yeah, if this kinda thread became a regular or monthly thing, I would be very happy.

    Anyway, from my TBZ game last week:

    Ex-strategist swordsmaster (who, unlike most such characters, specialized in Huge Anime Katana *as well as* Huge Anime Gun (first time I'd ever seen it, actually) meets antagonist "General Dragon-Arm" at the mountain ridge, overlooking a cliff (which in turn overlooks a battlefield). Dragon Arm begins the speech before the final showdown. Before he can get more than a sentence out,

    PL "I run towards him, grab him, and push us both over the cliff."

    GM (me) "???"

    PL "Yeah, remember, my character is embracing this "No-Thought" strategy. This is the culmination"

    GM "You might die, though. Is that OK with you?"

    PL: "Yep."

    GM "... ...uh oh. General Dragon Arm is at full health. His Dead Box is unchecked, so he can soak all the damage from the fall, so he can walk away. Critically hurt, but otherwise without a scratch. You need to hit him *twice* - the fall plus Something Else, before he will die."

    PL "..."

    PL "..."

    PL "... ... ... ok, as we fall, swinging swords at each other in freefall, I kick myself off of him, drifting up about 12 feet mid-flight. Then I shoot him."

    General Dragon-Arm's exceptional military career came to an unforseen, sudden and epic end.
