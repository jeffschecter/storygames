    \n A GM-less dungeon crawl.

    In the first phase of the game, you talk to the locals about the dungeon. I mention how I go to the tavern, and the barkeep tells me that the dungeon used to belong to an evil sorcerer. So, I put the "Evil Sorcerer's Lair" cards in the middle of the table. The next player talks about how he goes to the local wizard, who tells him that before the evil sorcerer moved in, the dungeon had actually contained a temple to a forgotten god, so she puts the "Ruined Temple" cards in the middle of the table. When we've finished all that, we shuffle the cards, and we have a random dungeon to explore.

    \n This will likely be stupidly specific. I want a game where...

    - good guys are responsible for making bad guys look threatening

    - bad guys are responsible for making good guys look sympathetic

    - the GM has flags (beliefs, aspects, keys)

    - players don't create flags before play, they create them in play

    - flags are updated in real time

    - players not in a scene have things to do that matter

    - random strangers that walk by and observe your game can jump in and do something

    - what you describe mechanically matters

    - rules aren't just prescriptive but also descriptive

    - players compel themselves into doing things against their character's interest in a way that isn't overly meta and doesn't involve the GM

    - every conflict changes your character

    - conflicts can start as resolving a single action but explode into a series of back and forth actions with momentum swinging back and forth

    - in a conflict where one side is clearly about to lose, there is a chance for a sudden momentum shift

    - GM doesn't roll anything

    - everyone creates the world and initial situation together and world creation doesn't stop when the game starts

    - NPCs have minimum stats and are reflections of player flags

    - you can fail forward towards success, accumulating positive momentum as you fail

    - failure is as fun or more fun that winning

    - you are rewarded for giving up in conflicts

    - lots of tools for creating random characters, scenarios, situations, and anything else you have to prep

    - rules scale, you can read just enough to start playing in 15 minutes and add complexity as you feel comfortable

    - GM screen that summarizes all the rules you would normally look up

    - handouts with summaries of how to pay and make characters

    - character sheet has room for the other PCs' names and relevant info or there is a shared group character sheet

    - more team work and helping or interfering rules that aren't just... here is a +2

    - clear communication tools that aren't overly aggressive to point to out what you don't like in a game and highlight what you do like

    - failing rolls create choices... do I sacrifice X to push, do I escalate, do I switch tactics

    - refreshing resources, like healing, is a huge part of the game and an opportunity to add to the fiction... to heal I don't just rest but I must actively do this exciting or entertaining thing...

    - you don't have to list everyone you know on your character sheet or everything you own, instead you can roll or tap a resource to do "character creation" on the fly

    - when you use a resource, like traits, in a conflict, you risk them in some way

    - when you roll, complications are as or more likely than success or failure

    - when you have an amazing success, you get something extra

    - different types of conflicts feel different

    - other players give you traits based on how you roleplay

    I'm sure there is way more! Apologies for the long list!

      Posted By: Marc MajcherI want a game that has the immersiveness and intimacy and spontaneity and personal connection of a good tabletop RPG, but that can be played in small chunks here and there, asynchronously, without the players needing to be physically present. If it handled players dropping in or out, or playing/contributing at different rates, super bonus.
      \nSo... A West Marches style player/character drop-in/drop-out game with an emphasis on social interaction game-play elements and a between-session (or even above/beyond meta-session) online / email / forum aspect?

      "Welcome to BIRTHRIGHT REDUX. The Tuesday meet-up will be for exploration and dungeon crawling. The Thursday meet-up will be for in person court intrigue and character drama scenes. The online play forums are for political maneuvering, arcane research and large scale warfare. Tactical miniatures based combat will either take place during a Tuesday / Thursday meet-up or a special session. Each player will be running between two and five inter-related characters belonging to the same House or Guild."
