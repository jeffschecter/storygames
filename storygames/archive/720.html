Roleplaying and Weak Ties

kleenestar
May 2006 edited May 2006 in Story Games
So, there's this guy Granovetter who wrote an article called The Strength of Weak Ties, which has pretty much blown the mind of everyone in the social software community, including me.

For those who don't feel like diving into the PDF, Granovetter argues that 'weak ties' (acquaintances) are actually more powerful for social networking and many practical benefits than 'strong ties' (family, close friends) are. His idea is that people that you have weak ties with are much more likely to be in a different social community than you, and be able to access and leverage resources that you can't.

One of my core arguments about Why Games Matter has to do with weak ties, and how games help us form meaningful social relationships with people we wouldn't otherwise come into contact with.

My own group has been a powerful illustration of this, I think. We come from many different communities, even though we have some underlying similarities (college-educated, for example), and we've been able to use our real-life resources to help each other in some pretty significant ways. For example, when one member of the group had dental problems but no insurance, we found a low-cost way to get him to see a dentist. Just last week, one member of the group found a job for another's boyfriend. And more than one person in the group has been introduced to their significant other (and in one case, spouse) through this particular social connection. Now, admittedly, many of us also became close friends through our gaming - but not every member of the group who's helped another has been close with that particular member.

So, basically, I'm curious about your own experiences, and whether your own gaming groups have been a source of good 'weak tie' connections for you! What do you think?
