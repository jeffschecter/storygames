\n So, I've been working on turning Polaris into a setting for D&D 4th Edition.

This project, that I've titled Kingdom of Ice, is going to involve writing new classes (currently: Librarian, Muse, Consul, Martyr, Knight), power sets for those classes, some rules additions (Zeal, Weariness, Moons).

And Kaleigh, my girlfriend, has been working on some art for the project.
The first piece is done!

    Posted By: jackson tegu'Cause if you don't have to justify your projects tome, then who do you have left to justify your projects to?
    \nHere it is:
    I like the concept of D&D 4th Edition.
    But, it's a thematic train-wreck. Dragonborn and gnomes and elves and dwarves and invokers and shamans and druids and warlocks? WTF.

    So, imagine an iteration of D&D 4th with a bit of premise and thematic coherency built in.
    Imagine a set of classes, powers and adventures that together told a story.

    Imagine that story was about a librarian, a foreign ambassador and a senator joining a group of knights as they venture into the wasteland to track down demons. Imagine that story was about people, possessed of great power and knowledge, trying to save a city dying of its own mistakes.

    But, all of that without losing: cool powers, tactical fights, fantastic heroes, and high fantasy flavor.

    That's my goal.
    Also, making 4th Ed powers is hella fun.
