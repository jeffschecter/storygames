\n I ran a session of The Final Girl at Arcon, a small gaming convention at my alma mater SUNY Oswego. It was real nice, everyone in the game were people who had played with me the previous year and one new player. I pitched an idea that I'd had for Dexcon but hadn't had a chance to run - a theater on the opening night of Twilight gets attacked by 30 Days of Night-style vampires. So it was clearly tongue-in-cheek, and one of the players was even a Twilight fan but got way into it.

So we've made characters, a collection of theater staff and Twilight fans, and I sort of muse, "I wish I'd made Stephanie Meyer as a character," and everyone says, "DO IT." She pretty much died right away when the vampires were introduced in the middle of the movie and got dragged into the darkness at the back of the theater.

I got super attached to my character, Vanessa the shrieking Twilight fan. I played her as being so excited about the Twilight movie that she could not stop crying during all the opening scenes. However once the shit hit the fan she ducked into a Black and Decker store by the theater and was chopping up vampires with a cordless circular saw. Everyone else in the game hated her so eventually the vampires got her, but only after she had stacked up a dozen or so vampires that had tried to chase her into the store.

Other notables were Jordan, the wannabe vampire who screamed "MAKE ME ONE OF YOU!" before having his entire head bit off by a vampire, and Michael, Vanessa's boyfriend who had been forced to come to the movie, closing his eyes and being thankful for the sweet release of death after the vampire baseball scene in the movie.

The Final Girl was Jody of Team Jacob, who managed to escape the bloodbath that claimed her brother and mother by being small and sneaky and slipping by the vampires when a dozen descended on Vanessa to take her out. So even though Vanessa died she died as a heroine. I really liked twisting her around from the overemotional character that everyone hated to the hardcore hero of the game.

Jody was found by a cop wandering the streets of town, covered in blood. He asked her what had happened and was she okay, and she just laughed and howled.

This was my favorite The Final Girl game to date.

    \n Jamey, there were a couple of other players who absolutely hated Vanessa and used Rivalries to take her down. She had a lot of staying power but having two other players make it their mission to kill you makes your survival odds pretty slim.

    I used the experience rules and they were just right. Enough to give you better odds but not enough to throw the game in favor of the characters who have experience.

    I found that they were actually surprisingly liberal in this session. If they could save someone, they did, and if they could screw someone they did that too. Having the scene end after one character dies makes people more free with their cards I think.
