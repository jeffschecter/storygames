\n I just posted this on my blog:\n
So, I keep thinking about my play at Go Play NW with Beast Hunters. And I still want to run a Final Fantasy VII game.

Sadly, most of my players couldn't care less about Final Fantasy VII and anime tropes in general. Maybe I should work it up as a con game instead.

I mean come on! Beast tattoos could so totally be Enemy Skill Materia.

Gah, this idea is one of many that's eating my brain.

(I will need to ponder how I handle Limit Breaks. Maybe that's how you damage, instead of calling it "I'll do damage" now -- but then it doesn't connect with how damaged you are.)
\nThe vibe I got out of playing Beast Hunters at Go Play NW was one of "Dude, be awesome, do badass shit! The crazier, the better!" I had a moment where I was thinking about the visuals from FF7: Advent Children. And it all clicked: Beast Hunters is so a great choice for a Final Fantasy VII game (or, at least a combat could easily be a high-energy anime combat, and FF7 is something near and dear to my heart).

So, for those fans of FF7, what would you see as crucial in a game to keep it feeling like an FF7 game? Do you care about Limit Breaks? Item & MP management? Summons? Materia? What totally rocked for you that you'd love to see in an RPG hack?

    \n I think the trick is finding the stuff that FFVII does, but not as well as it could have because of the medium in which it was produced.     \n I think, as a twist on what Daniel is saying, that you should find what you want to get out of playing FFVII *with your friends* and focus on that. Because one of the main things you can't do on the console is, obviously, to play with other people and make shit up together. So by that very token, you're already exploring venues you can't do with the console game.

    What I liked about Final Fantasy games are the kickass moves, teamwork, and awesome boss fights in cool settings. Those you can definitely do with BH. I'd put a greater focus on the characters working together, maybe make up powers that allow bonuses for teaming up and the likes.

    I might also consider, to make it more action RPG like, to directly link Maneuvers and Strikes. Seeing that you will have the ability to heal after a short time (usually in the FF series after every few fights, what with save sphere refreshes and the likes), I'd say give people free Strikes whenever they make an Offensive Maneuver. So you're almost always doing damage, like in the game.
