    I know that many people congregate on other forums, Twitter, Reddit, Discord, and other similar media, but I have less knowledge of gaming communities on those platforms.

    In practice, people who are "indie game enthusiasts" tend to try to find each other. In conventions or meetup groups, there's often someone who starts to organize others around this shared interest. (Game conventions that feature a lot of indie games tend to be known for that, for example.)

    My city's local Meetup group has a lot of "come try out a new game" events, although I'm rarely available to make it to those, sadly. They are good a opportunity to try out some different games, though!

    I have a small group of friends which was formed specifically to play a variety of indie games (there's such tremendous variety that trying out the "classics" as well as new fare is incredibly rewarding!), and we go through maybe 5-10 games a year. We chat about games, too, all the time, since we're always keeping a list of games we want to try. Some are old classics and others are "the new hotness".

    I feel like I bring up podcasts way too often on here, but I’ve found the majority of my favorite indie games by listening to people play them on various shows. One Shot is particularly good – they run a new game system every few weeks, almost all of them indies. You can also check out interview shows like +1 Forward or Modifier, where they talk directly to indie designers. There are lots of others.

    Between that and casual chats with friends & acquaintances at the local game store (“Oh you like X? Have you played Y?”), I feel like I’ve come across more indie games than I could ever play in my life. Oh, and cons. Even if you don’t attend, browsing a gaming convention event listing will trawl up tons of new games. And if you do go out to a con, well, that solves your “find people to play with” problem, at least for a day or two.

    I do the posting to theindierpgpipeline.blogspot.com (which of course started as a joke) as to where I find the games in the first place I have a feed of all new postings to DriveThruRPG, itchio's physical game category, kicktraq, this forum and @Teylen 's excellent crowdfunding round-up. If I wanted to go even further I would probably look through the Metatopia listings and The Gauntlet's schedule to see what's in development, but I figure that those games there will ultimately hit one of the existing venues above if the designer wants to release them.

    As to how , in amongst the >100 games released a month I find stuff I might be interested in playing, I produce a podcast by two friends who are big gamers James Mullen & Lloyd Gyan who pick out the new games that interest them the most each month. I also follow Rob Carnel on Pluspora and a variety of podcasts including OneShot, Misdirected Mark, +1 Forward, Farrier's Bellows. I also check out whatever games are played at conventions and games played listings from Games on Demand to get a feel as to what folk are running.
