\n I think we need a Story Games PAX team. Some dudes who are into running and selling the gaming public on indie games. The PAX crowd is already very much pro-small publisher / creator-owned games and I think if we had some folks there who were into pushing a little (handing out promo materials, running games, showing those D&D entrenched dudes that, yes, games can be about more than just rolling dice) we could get some serious attention on the subject.

I'm going - are you? What do you think?

    Or is this something that's qualitatively different between PAX and PAX East? I would believe that too.

    \n No story-gamer culture, I'm pretty sure there were only a handful of people at PAX last year that already knew about, say, Mouse Guard, which Luke demoed a lot. However, there was a nearly packed auditorium for a panel on "Games Beyond D&D" with a lot of people sounds pretty excited when they heard about stuff like Dread or Burning Wheel. And it may have been the largest Action Castle game yet.

    So yeah, lots of people interested in new cool stuff, but not many preconceived notions about story games. My impression was there was a lot more openness to indie games than, say, GenCon.
