    \n

    \n
    \n I'm going to try to get all of these in shape to run or at least playtest at cons this year:\n
      \n
    • for Spirit of the Century: HUB CITY CONFIDENTIAL starring MAX MENSCH in "Little Girl Lost": Join private eye Max Mensch and his friends daring newspaper reporter Lucy Lovelace, high-wire construction worker Skyscraper Steele, and cabby-on-the-spot Hack Hooper as they try to uncover the mystery behind the abduction of young Ophelia Orfino in order to clear wealthy entrepreneur Rex Rich of a heinous crime!
      • \n
    • for Trail of Cthulhu: The Babalon Working: California, 1952. Jack Parsons is dead, blown apart in an accidental garage-lab explosion. He was a strange character with strange ideas -- a visionary rocket scientist with occultist inclinations who was in personal contact with Aleister Crowley. Great stuff for a science fiction writer! You hardly knew him, but nonetheless you have managed to get an invitation to his memorial service out in the California desert. Little do you realize that you are about to become embroiled in a secret war for the soul of humanity itself! In this game, the PCs include 20th century sf writers Philip K. Dick and Robert A Heinlein as well as mystery writer/editor Tony Boucher and Bob's wife Virginia Heinlein.
      • \n
    • The New World: An allohistorical colonization game informed by post-colonialism and ethnohistory; it's Anti-Civilization III, the role-playing game. The game dispenses with the conception of Old World inscribing itself upon the blank slate of the history-less New World, proceeding instead from the notion of two old worlds colliding and a new one forming in the space between them. You play someone from either side of that gap caught up in the New World.
      • \n
    • A Truth Universally Acknowledged: My game for Austen RPG scholarly panel project we've been talking about; the name will probably change but the idea is to hack the "jeepforged" version of Ganakagok and run it at Dexcon over the summer.
      • \n
    \nThat's enough, right? If I get half of them done I'll be happy.
