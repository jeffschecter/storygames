\n Like, the forum at the Forge. I really miss it.

Is it just me or does anyone get the same thing?

    \n Isn't the First Thoughts forum just a more directed version of Indie Game Design? I thought it was re-imaged to get down to brass tacks; the "Here's my specific idea for this specific game, can you tell me if this thing will work or not?", and not the "HAY GUYS I want to make a fantasy game with elves and realistic arrow damage. Also, all elves are red. Do you guys like this?" style nonspecific fluff stuff with no directed questions or content. Doesn't it serve the same function?

    There's Game Design Help here, but I made that a member's only topic. I want people to be free to bring up their directed questions/help requests every now and again, but I don't want to turn this into a Forge-style game design help powerhouse. I want more threads on play than design, in the end. I don't mind some design threads, and in fact a bunch of games were built in part here; but in the end I want there to be, y'know, like "Play/Running/GMing/Enthusiasm" having a "majority sharehold of stock in Story Games".

    \n Wasn't the growing signal-to-noise ratio the reason for the Forge losing that forum? I imagine it's inescapable.

    Rather than jump from one forum to another, I've recently decided I need to abuse the relationships I've formed at conventions and directly ask the designers whose work I appreciate for a quick review of a (clearly stated) element of my work in progress. Fair warning to you poor fools who greet me by my first name. My own work is too far along to introduce in a post that stays shy of the ramblers I FF through myself.

    Of course, this relies on getting well past the "woodshedding" phases and having some actual content to present but First Ideas should be fine for that. Once the game is too complex to fit in First Ideas, get it to people you trust for detailed inspection and thrashing.

    I was watching the M dot Strange film school videos and one of my favorite suggestions was to stay far away from the internet with an immature idea. Your friends and acquaintances should be your audience at that stage. If they offer you level, objective criticism, great. If they praise you, you've got the inflated ego to survive a few trolls. If they beat you down, you develop a thick skin. Just keep going.

    \n Russell hit it. I equate this to two issues (not specific to the Forge):
    First, It got too popular. I suspect a smaller audience is far easier to listen to then a larger one. I have though about running a design symposium where you go for answers and you get only a handful of people that can actually respond to posts. This way the feedback is directed and informed.

    The danger is that these people may get their heads stuck in one place and talk from a single theory or idea instead of maybe approaching things from a broad, accepting and experimental stance.

    Second, everyone wants Vincent or Clinton to answer their posts. I can only assume that although flattering and interesting, answering forums posts are not their full time job and could get overwhelming quickly (if not jaded) and two it would be hard to get new blood into the mix. A symposium - a council, so to speak - would have to rotate through different people and maybe have a process by which you're admitted. On one hand I like to be among the noise throwing out my ideas and maybe even see them being used. On the other hand I don't want to be from someone I don't know for him to tell me that I should scrap my game and make it d20

    It's a fine balancing act that I think every popular forum eventually degrades to. I like the idea of a community that you have to be invited into, personally. Especially if you have to be invited in person!!! That would be so hot! Please don't misunderstand I am not saying that's what THIS should be. I just think that the environment would be cool to mingle in.

    - Don
