    \n Okay, so what kind of game are you craving at the moment?

    \n Apocalypse World is a game about turbulent badasses in a post-apocalyptic community.
    Dungeon World is a game about adventuring heroes banding together to fight dungeon monsters.
    Monsterhearts is a game about angsty teenage monsters reeling in the wake of puberty.

    Saga of the Icelanders is about epic Icelandic drama, spanning seasons and even generations.
    The Regiment is about the tough lives of a military squadron, both in and between battles.
    Monster of the Week is about hellboy-esque monster hunters.
    Tremulus is about lovecraftian investigation adventure.

    ***

    Apocalypse World & Saga of the Icelanders strike me as similar in that they're about a community of people being both badass and human.

    Dungeon World & Monster of the Week & maybe Tremulus strike me as similar in that they focus the AW-engine toward adventure gaming.

    Monsterhearts is the most navel-gaze-y, and focuses on personal horror / body horror.

    ***

    If you want a text that's punchy and full of flavour, I think the best is Apocalypse World.
    If you want a text that's more approachable & teaching-oriented, I'd recommend my own Monsterhearts.

    I've not read Dungeon World, but I hear it does a great job of teaching itself as well.

    \n I adore Monsterhearts so much. It does such a good job explaining the rules of the game, and makes quite a few improvements on the already great base game. Far and away, it's been the most consistently fun game I've played in the last couple of years. Everyone who's ever tried it enjoys themselves, and the concepts have always been really easy to get across in play. And NO ONE I've played with liked the genre before they played it. If you were to back me into a corner and say what the best one I've played was, it would be Monsterhearts.

    That being said, as mixed as my results with the base game has been when I've run it, they're mostly mistakes I hope to correct in the future. My times as a player of AW have always been exciting and visceral and a hell of a lot of fun. That being said, I think the game is a bit trickier than other people find it. But I've also had some people tell me they had no luck at all running Monsterhearts, so YMMV.

    Dungeon World is amazing in it's own way. I love the game to pieces, but it doesn't feel like the same thing as the other two. It's very much a sort of Super Dungeons & Dragons that somehow manages to be exactly like Dungeons & Dragons should, but never actually feels like when you play a 'real' edition of D&D. It bends the resolution in it's own way, and feels like it's doing something very different from Apocalypse World and Monsterhearts, something both like and unlike traditional adventure gaming. I love it, but it feels like it's own creature entirely.

    \n Dungeon World has been pretty huge for me, it takes all my years of D&D knowledge and sinks it into a framework that's finally consistently fun to play around with. Highly recommended if you have a D&D background lurking!

    Regardless, I'd just go with whatever game hooks you the most on its flavor, the one you think you can read the most of yourself into. For me that happens to be Dungeon World, but Mcdaldno's setup of what the games are about is pretty much spot-on. Monsterhearts reads really well too, but I've had trouble getting people around here to play it, there are a lot more classic D&D types.

    Ultimately, though, Apocalypse World itself explains its own system really well. From what I've read not everyone likes the style of writing, so your mileage may vary? If you're solely wanting to read a game to get a sense of all the versions of it, the original is setup remarkably well for that.
