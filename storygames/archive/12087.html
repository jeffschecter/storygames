    \n Now I have never played Fastlane, but I know a lot about it from reading AP to discussing it with you at some length (albeit all this a while ago). I feel strongly that the favors are important to the game, and actually the part of the game that draws me towards it. I think Dave is right, and it just needs a new format - something easier on the paperwork. Now what I'm thinking about is something rather well... um... extensive in that it may change the play of the game or such - not sure. Here is what is coming to mind.
    \n
    \nIf you establish themes for the game you could collect these favors under them on an index card or such, not defining the favor except by the fact that it connects their PC to said PC/NPC under a particular theme. Then when called into play the details could be hammered out. So I'm seeing the themes as extension of the character here, each character has their own set of index card theme tracker board thing-a-ma-bobs. So in a James Bond game you have the character theme revenge and then a name under that card that connects that other PC/NPC to them through that personal story theme. This is all vague, but if you want to discuss the idea further we can, or if its not in the vein of how you see the game we couid not
    \n
    \nRegardless I think the favors are too important to lose and I hope you find what you need to clarify and rebuild the second edition!
