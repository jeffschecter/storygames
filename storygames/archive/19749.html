\n My friend David Guyll, who authors the blog "Points of Light" wrote an awesome rules lite narrative horror game called Fright Night.

It's simple to learn, and emulates everything from slasher flicks, to supernatural horror, to lovecraftian weirdness.

The coolest part is that everything is randomly generated, so not even the GM knows what's going to happen. It really captures the feel of being in a horror movie.

You can read a rough draft of the game here.

And if you really dig it, there's a kickstarter here.

    \n Hmmm...those are interesting possibilities.

    The game is basically designed for tense and suspenseful one shot games, so it seems like it would pair nicely with with those sorts scenarios, particularly given the randomness of in-game events and the three act structure of Fright Night sessions.

    The author's name is David Guyll, and he's all about re-skinning and jury rigging systems. He'd love any feedback the gaming community has to offer.
