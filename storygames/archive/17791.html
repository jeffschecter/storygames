What stuff should we watch, read, play?

1.Feel free to respond here in-thread to another link posted.

a. If you're looking to start a conversation, perhaps take your comment and create a new thread with it in the Stuff to Watch category.

b.Otherwise, to prevent too many comments in the thread, number your response (first response: [1]; second response [2]; third response [3]). If you see that you're the second person to post, "Wow, that looks cool!" to a post here, then put a "[2]" at the top of your post. Three posts ([3]) is the limit: The fourth person to post should just take their comment and create a new thread, to carry the discussion over there further.

Why?

Because these threads are great: We can link to little things being talked about on the web in general, on blogs, or other forums that might be noteworthy, without having to dedicate a new thread to each and every one (although that, too, is fine if you want to discuss the item further or "open up a discussion").

Responding to these posts with a comment or two is understandable (other times there might be no comments, just cause the link was self-explanatory: nothing to say, lots of heads nodding in agreement). But we don't want to create a huge discussion in the middle of the thread: Some folks get a lot out of these threads, and don't want to look back to see the "10 latest posts" just to see that it's a heated discussion of something from a previous page. Cool? Cool.

    \n [Ghost City Raiders 2]

    It's a part of the post apocalyptic "Walkabout" setting that I've been developing. It's a quasi-minis game where players control a single mini and tell the story of that miniature as a scavenger in the ruined cities of a shattered world where the spirits of myth and legend roam the landscape. A player then advances that mini through scenarios and more often than not, they don't need to fight one another to win a scenario. Characters can win victory points through scavenging, through specific scenario objectives, or through specific personal objectives unique to the character. I'll be developing new characters, scenario ideas, mini campaigns (with two or three interconnected scenarios), and backstory events for the game over the course of the year. A few more story driven elements to determine scenarios are also in the works.

    There was a bit of development behind the idea over the course of November (as a NaGaDeMon project), with the whole idea of the game being a complete sub-skirmish scale narrative wargame that could theoretically fit in a little tin box (or someone's back pocket).

    So, short answer...Yes, it's a mini's game...Yes, it develops characters and tells stories...Yes, it's genre bending...and Yes, I'll be developing a website for it.

    Thanks for the interest.

    \n As an experiment, Anders Nygaard and I are setting up an anonymous roleplaying forum at http://imagonem.net/cgi-bin/index.html.

    "DeRole is an experiment to see what happens when people from Story Games and rollespill.net discuss games anonymously. The specific context of the experiment is to see what happens when we are divorced from our perceived status and reputation in the community.
    The rules are:
    - Do not divulge your identity, or ask others for theirs.
    - Do not situate yourself (e. g. don't write "I'm a white gay male", "I'm a game designer" or similar), nor ask others for their background.
    - Reply to thoughts and ideas, not people. Try not to imagine what the people you're discussing with are like, or who they are.
    - Be aware that this is not a safe space. We want people to behave civilly, but can not guarantee that individuals will not be hurt, intentionally or unintentionally."

    Feel free to check it out. No idea how it will work out, or how long it will last. Will I be there? WHO KNOWS! It's anonymous!

    \n [A Terrible Idea 3]
    $20 for a game from Jackson Tegu, Tracy Barnett and Renee Knipe, including shipping anywhere in the world? That's a pretty good friggin deal. Actually, the shipping part is probably the crux of the "shooting selves in foot" bit more than "no PDF": Not sure they're in the US, but will assume so: I'm in France, I donate $20 equivalent to the charity, and they send me a copy of the book for a total of (looks it up on USPS.com ...) $16.95. So, $17 basically. Luckily, the $20 (not $3) is in the hands of the charity, so Mission Accomplished and all.

    And hey: It's their choice of format, so if they want to limit donations to charities for a physical book, it's up to them. Personally, I miss zines and zine culture.
    And hey^2: Each game is 1 page. Many people have scanners, with OCR even. Just sayin', what's to stop you from handing out PDF copies to your local friends or close internet friends if they in turn volunteer to spend, say, $10 on one of those charities? They chose to go underground, you can choose to go underground-er-er.
    But would insist (if it were me) that folks receiving the PDF prove they donated to the charity beforehand, to keep the spirit (if not the form) of the thing going.

    -Andy
