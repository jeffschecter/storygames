  • Mark_Causey
    October 2006
    Reserve spells are pretty cool, but they've been enhanced by reserve feats. So long as you have them prepared (or are able to cast them if you're a spontaneous caster) you get an effect, e.g. having certain electricity spells prepared means you can use a standard action to let forth an electrical blast of defined damage and range. The reserve feats mean that you get an effect and its power is based off of the highest spell in that category that you can still cast.

    Standard, Move and Full round actions are no longer the entire list. There is now Immediate and Swift. Immediate means you can take the action any time during the initiative cycle (not affecting your other actions for the round or your initiative) and Swift is an action you can take along with a Standard and a Move action in your round.

    I haven't played it in over a year. This is crazy, but I still flip through them when I get to the store.

Player's Handbook II and Complete Mage also feature 'Channeled' spells, which are spells where the spellcasting time varies between swift and two entire rounds - but the longer you keep casting the more powerful the spell is.
