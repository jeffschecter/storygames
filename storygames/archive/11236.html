\n I've been talking about it over on #playnow for quite a while and I figure it's time to do it or shut up about it. Do what? Run a game.

As I've mentioned, one of my preferred genres is the western played straight, preferably Italian-style. So I've borrowed some tried-and-true Italo-Western tropes and a page from Apocalypse World and put something together that I'm interested in running over a session or three for a few players. We would meet in the #playnow channel and then port over to something specific to the game.

I went back and forth on a ruleset for this thing and finally decided to go with something semi-freeform. No character sheets and such, but a dice-resolution mechanic (the steal from Apocalypse World) for when challenges come up. Italo-Western heroes and villains all tend to have the same skills when they aren't sharing the exact same names \u2014 Django, I'm looking at you \u2014 so I feel this'll work. I'm sure everyone will tell me if it doesn't.

Which leaves time. Weekends are best. During the week I'm generally on in the mornings and not so much the afternoons/evenings. Depending on how we schedule things I may be able to work around that and get some PM gaming happening M-F, but....

And that's it. The town of Split Rock, somewhere along the Texas/Mexico border, awaits. The sun is blasting down hard on the shale and even the plants wish they could find shade. There's a blood price for wanted men. They say it's a dollar a bullet.
