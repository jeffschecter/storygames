\n For my DungeonMaker game, I'm gonna want a bunch of different, interconnectable dungeon tiles. I was contemplating drawing them all myself, but I thought why not share the fun and make a new community project. So, let's draw some dungeon tiles! When we're all done, I'll put them all together in a free .pdf and make them available under the creative commons attribution 3.0 license, which means you get credited for your work but anyone can pretty much do whatever they want with it otherwise. Submitting tiles means you're agreeing to have your tiles published, licensed, and used according to that license.

How to Contribute
1) Download one or more of the following template pages: DungeonTiles01 DungeonTiles02 DungeonTiles03 and either print it out or load it up into photoshop or inkscape or whatever if you're that kind of talented. Or make your own template page, see below.
2) Draw us some dungeon tiles, following the guidelines below. Print your name legibly at the bottom of the template page outside the bottom bold line. Title it if you like.
3) Scan your template page back in and email it to me as a .pdf, .jpg, .png, or (if you must) .tif. my email address is dave dot younce at that one google-run email system, that is to say, gmail.com. Include the words "Dungeon Tiles" in the subject line please. Or post them in this thread if that's easier.
4) Repeat as necessary! Let's set the deadline for tile submission as May 5th.

Guidelines
1) Make your walls meet the guidelines on the template at each intersection of 6x6 squares. Don't draw anything that crosses the 6x6 square boundaries except walls at the points shown - otherwise it will mess with the ability of users to mix & match tiles. That said, within a given 6x6 tile, feel free to draw doors or walls that further subdivide the square, bars, portcullises, ledges, ladders, traps, stairs, statues, altars, pools, spiderwebs, summoning circles, furniture, traps, and whatever else inspires you; just keep it contained and don't cross the boundaries. Cavernous shapes are fine as long as your tile entrances and exits are where the guidelines leave them.
2) Use "standard" dungeon map symbols, or make your own where necessary, but hopefully it should be clear to a person encountering your map for the first time what everything is.
3) One way to approach the template is to draw it as an actual megadungeon level, with one or more stairs up, one or more stairs down, and distinctly themed areas.
4) Where possible, avoid writing too much in the drawing area. Sometimes you'll probably have to, though.
5) 'Dead Space' between walls should be black or crosshatched to indicate it as such.
6) Use of color is fine, but we expect most submissions will be black & white.

Finished tiles might look kind of like these:


Making your own Template page(s)
Feel free to make new template pages and incorporate new features on them such as underground waterways, lava flows, multi-level tiles, or other unusual types of tile entrance/exit. DungeonTilesDots has just dots on the 6x6 border lines (If you really need to make your own page with no dots, make sure your graph squares are the same size as everyone else's - please use the graph paper generator here, putting 0.25 in for the minimum border, leaving weight at .50, 3 squares per inch, and grid color light grey). If you are adding a new connection/terrain type, make sure it can mix and match well by making the spaces it occupies when it crosses tile borders in the same place or places every time (eg always in the middle 2 squares of a border).

I'm excited to see your tiles!
