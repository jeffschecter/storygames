\n So in a few recent podcasts (Voice of the Revolution, SoK) there has been some discussion of how the Knight Realms LARP looked pretty cool.

I was a boffer larper for 8 years, but i haven't participated in one in a while, and i've been searching for a new place to play. Today's warm weather got me thinking... It would be pretty cool if a crew of us story game folk rolled up on knight realms this summer for a weekend of in-character fantasy adventure. Is there any interest in this?

Looking at their website www.knightrealms.com, they don't really edit their rules very well (or at all perhaps) but they very heavily emphasis how Role-play intensive they are, and their production values are out of sight. Check out the photos section, some of that stuff looks really good.

come on don't you want to dress up like a loon and hang out in the woods? Then fight some orcs? How can you turn that down?

    \n Huh. I may or may not be down for this. But I can certainly lend weapons. I have: (1) black wizard's staff, with a red sphere at the top, held in place by a metal claw, with silver runes on the shaft (it's all foam and totally safe), (1) awesome black and blue scimitar, with artwork on it, (1) latex short sword, (1) small latex mace, and (0-6) open cell foam throwing daggers. I also have (1) black wizard's robe, with gold designs around the edges and (1) green cloak. Both are suitable for someone at least 5' 9", but taller would be better.
    \n
    \nIf it turns out I don't go, these are all up for use, so long as someone (Kevin, I'm thinking you or Frank, assuming you drag him along) is willing to swing by my place and pick them up and (even more importantly) return them. If I do go, I'll bring everything along, and folks can borrow whatever I don't use personally.
    \n
    \nI also happen to have the somewhat hard to find 5/8 inch pipe insulation required for most boffer LARP weapons. Most stores only carry the 3/8 inch stuff, which doesn't work out so well as padding. Anyway, I haven't done a boffer LARP in some time, so I'd be happy to get rid of this stuff -- it's just taking up space these days. With the foam, some PVC pipe, and some duct tape, you can make your own weapons to size.
