\n I just wrote up an actual play report (here) that got me thinking about alternative mechanics for Werewolf: The Apocalypse. Essentially I have one of these "Exalted/Shadowrun" scenarios where there is a lush setting, an engaging situation and a crunchy/slow mechanic that I don't feel does justice to the game.

Carl Rigney recently ran Don't Lose Your Edge, a Don't Rest Your Head port of Shadowrun. I haven't heard any details yet but if Carl ran it I can be pretty sure it rocked. Considering that I'm wondering if DRYH could be used or Werewolf. Here's my simple premise.

Setting: Our world, 1999 and the world is about to end. I mean like any minute now.
Situation: Garou are the worlds last hope for salvation and they have just discovered that they have been deceived, the Weaver is the cause of the Wrym's corruption and not the other way around. Now, against all odds they must fight off the Weaver before the world is locked in stasis, destroying all life.
Mechanics: Using the DrYH mechanics, dice have new names:\n
  • Discipline = Willpower (I think this name is kind of weak, probably needs something else, but it represents the difference between a mortal (1 die) and a Garou (3 dice).
    • \n
  • Madness = Rage (it is very fitting that "Rage" dominating would cause a frenzy as the results are berserk (essentially fight) or fox (flight). Taking other forms would require a minimum number of madness dice and each werewolf would have a Rage ability that they would define (I'm still thinking about what these would be)
    • \n
  • Exhaustion = Gnosis. This would be the ability that powers gifts, but I'm not sure the way it scales makes sense (i.e. unable to decrease it). Personally it seems more fitting to use the scaling exhaustion mechanic for rage (representing that it is hard to calm down once you're full or rage) but that means either swapping pools or swapping components of the way the pools work and that seems dangerous.
\nAny thoughts on DRYH for Werewolf?

    \n Keep Discipline the same... it's self control and self mastery. It plays a nice counter-point to the other forces.

    Rage is a good map for Madness.

    But...

    The mechanics for exaustion seem to fit Range better. It builds up, gets stronger, makes you superhuman, but eventually you'll pop your lid.

    Rage would accumulate just like Exhaustion. Shape changing works through this, replacing talents. Minor uses are the humanoid and near-human shape changes, or wolfy stuff while remaining mostly human. Major uses involve changing your shape completely. When you crash, it's death frenzy for you.

    For madness, I'm thinking the analog should be something which combines spirit magic, social and spiritual obligation, taboo, honor... melding Gnosis with Renown.

    Call is Gnosis for ease of use, and use it for doing werewolfy spirit magic (have a Gift which defines your particular potencies). When it dominates, you come under the sway of spiritual obligation, causing you to meet an Obligation. Obligations are like responses, and you have two types- Taboo (something you can not do) and Gaesa (something you must do). When you can accept no more Obligations (you can accept 2+ you Renown score... see below), one of your Discipline dice becomes a permanent Gnosis die- you're losing touch with your pack, and becoming more like a spirit. When it replaces your Discipline completely, then you pass from the world completely.

    I'm also seeing Coins of the Man and Coins of the Beast.

    For Renown, you have as much as you have Scars. More Renown means you can shoulder more obligations to the people or the spirit.

    \n This seems to be working out. These are the ideas that have gelled with me the most.

    Discipline - Called whatever, it doesn't seem very interesting. Just sticking with Discipline will work. Perhaps I can mine the W:tA books for something else. Other thoughts right now are Gaia's Blessing, Garou, and Renown.

    Madness - Rage. I know the idea of it building (like exhaustion) is attractive, but think the fight/flight mechanic works better. Need to go into Crinos (war form), you've got to roll at least 3 Rage dice. If Rage dominates, you go fight or flight. I think that is solid. Your particular rage talent is something that is still a blank, however. Going wolf is just part of the setting, so we might still want to add something to differentiate the players. Perhaps the madness talent could correspond to the moon (Ragabash could have some stealth / sabotage type talent, Philidox some lie/justice detection, Galliard the ability to convince people, etc). Frenzy to much and you become a monster. Solid.

    Exhaustion \u2013 Gnosis - Your spiritual tie. Each use of gifts/talking to spirits/passing into the Umbra makes you closer to the spiritual world, but drift too far and you loose touch with the real world. It seems perfect for say, Scion (Legend rising forces you to the Overworld) but still not sold on it in Werewolf. The crux of the problem here is that in W:tA there is really only one way a garou will usually self destruct and that is through rage. In general being more connected with the spirits is seen as a good thing. What do you guys think about the above taint idea as a replacement? Taint of the Wyrm is pretty brutal indeed, possibly too brutal. Maybe just fuse the ideas and say that the spirits have become tainted so the closer you get to them, the more tainted you become?

    Pain - Corruption. All that is fucked up with the world around you, ranging from the two guys who just saw you change and instead of running will pull out their buck knives and come for you (Corruption 1) to a pack of elder Black Spiral Dancers lead by an legendary abomination (Corruption 10).

    I also really like Bailywolfs coins idea. I hadn't thought about that, but I think coins of man and coins of beast are perfect.

    Discipline because... whatever. It works (though, there's something to be said for making this base pool the line's morality stat... humanity for Vamps, Harmony for Wolves, Wisdom for Mages).

    Hrm...

    Lets do that.

    Wisdom is then the Discipline analog. It gets eroded by Hubris, the Madness analog.

    Mystery is the Exhaustion analog. You use it for subtle magic. It builds up, making you more arcane, bringing you into closer proximity to the Supernal (and the abyss)- a crash is a convulsive mystical revelation which gives you a taste of true reality, and then flings you back down into the mud of reality.

    Then we have Hubris used for doing vulgar magic. Works just like Madness- used for fat crackling obvious power. Want to animate the Statue of Liberty? Here you go. Responses for Hubris are Dominate or Paradox- either impose your will, and take control, or suffer some kind of external reality backlash. Basically, be a dick, or take one on the chin.

    If you wanted to map things tighter, from the Splat+Splat then Path gives you the mundane skills you use like Exhaustion talents, and Order gives you the Arcana which you use like Madness talents.

    The Coins would obviously be Supernal and Abyssal.

    \n Okay, playing with the exhaustion as rage idea. At the start, a garou has no rage. He gets into a conflict and calls upon his rage to make him more powerful. This works and has the added danger of making him even madder (rage dominating). Each time he leaves his rage unchecked (i.e. doesn't take a period of rest (or maybe something else more difficult to find in WOD) the rage will grow. It starts as a slow simmer and ramps up (as rage becomes more likely to dominate the more dice you have) to a boil.

    So, how does the player represent changing form? Strictly narrative? Minimum number of Rage dice? Making a Major use of rage?

    And what happens when they "crash"? Is that a berserk frenzy? That doesn't seem to remove the character the way exhaustion should. Do the enter the "Thrall of the Wyrm" and attack their allies? Or do they have a some kind of condition (heart attack, stroke, chest cavity exploding) that debilitates them long term? Maybe they just become wild and run off into the woods?

    That begs the question of a rage talent. Here are some ideas but to be fun I think they would need to be either much broader or more flexible.

    Minor use of rage - Changing forms, using wolf senses.
    Major use of rage - Superhuman levels of strength or speed
