\n Tonight, we played Mortal Coil. I haven't played before, but it was wonderfully fun.

We decided we were children in wartime Jersey. John played a Boy Scout who hated the war; Steve played the boy who told tales; I played a girl who liked guns; and Alex didn't play a child at all, but an elderly witch.

The questions on setting creation were excellent. We decided the magic should be like an English fairytale: fairies, trolls and so forth. I decided that my character had "Gun Magic", although we only began to establish what that meant. Finally, we decided that magic should be "happy and bubbly".

At the beginning of the game, John's character was given a Hitler Youth uniform and told to put it on, which he did. When he walked through the woods, my character (quite naturally) shot him. We then found Alex's witch in a cave in the woods and ended up on the South Cliffs, where a water spirit reared out of the water and scared us away.

We ended up staying out all night. This was fine for me, because I was an orphan. However, Steve's character was locked in the attic as a punishment. John's character was caught, as he crept in at dawn, by his saccharine-voiced elder sister.

We only just got started, but the game was promising. The only problem was that the "happy and bubbly" magic never seemed happy and bubbly. Every time I described flowers growing and birds singing, it just sounded sinister. Perhaps it's me.

Graham
