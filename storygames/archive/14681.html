\n Playing again at a convention this summer, and I have two questions for anyone interested.

1) Deal scenes. Would it help to mark those on the character sheet, along with some check boxes?

It's like, Van the Deadly (Held PC) goes to the Iron Fangs (Faction) for a Deal scene. What motivation is there for Van to actually hold up his end of the Deal? The roll's over, the improvement either happened or it didn't. So I'm thinking:
Van's player can establish that he's accomplished his end of the Deal later, in another scene perhaps. He can give someone a Negative Condition, if that was the Deal, or he can use a Success to "establish a fact."
-or-
Another player can grab the Iron Fangs and push a Face Off with Van for squelching.

So maybe something like

Accomplished Deals Burned
[ ] ___________________________________________ [ ]
[ ] ___________________________________________ [ ]
[ ] ___________________________________________ [ ]


2) Face-Off scenes.

What if both controller and player are happy just getting what they want, and not rolling the dice? Is there a kharmic strike option? I mean, it's not in the mechanics as written, I don't think, but have you been tempted? Would it spoil the tone?

Or maybe it's a sign that the stakes have been framed poorly? Like, "if they're framed correctly, both sides will really fight hard." Or "if you're willing to give in to your opponent's stakes, they aren't high enough" ?

    I don't know that it needs mechanics. Sure, the PC already got his roll, but the Faction already got its INF. It can just be assumed that the PC accomplished his end of the deal, unless it involves screwing with another PC directly, somebody frames the PC into the mission in media res, or its established in the fiction that the PC blew it off. And in that case, if the PC doesn't uphold his end of the deal, it just makes it easier for somebody to send the Faction after the PC for Face-Off, which, really, is what you want.
