\n A couple of years ago, some friends of mine ran a Universalis game of that title that was awesome--Harry Potter wire-fu adventure.

I've been re-reading the Harry Potter books getting ready for the finale, and I've been reminded how much I love the stories. So if you were going to run something Harry Potter, how would you do it? What system would you use? Would you use canon characters, or customize?

I've been thinking that you could do some awesome HP using SotC. Spells in Harry Potter seem to work an aweful lot like maneuvers.

    \n I was in that Universalis game. It was amazing. Best Uni game ever.

    I don't think I'd use the canon characters. Maybe do the class that graduated before Harry came to Hogwarts. I'm sure they had lots of great adventures, too (and we'll probably get to read all about them, unless the publisher is insane).

    When I ran an HP-like game series, I used the system from John Tynes' Puppetland. It's incredibly simple, captured the childlike quality of things, and was good for the non-gamers I was playing with. Sadly, it seems to be gone from the Internet now.

    If I had to do it again, I'd use TSOY -- specifically Judd's forthcoming TSOY-based game, 1st Quest, which is built to run stories just like that. I think it will be available at GenCon.

    Jason: I think you indirectly raise a good point. What elements stand out in the Harry Potter books as being the important ones you want to carry over? In your case, you wanted to emphasize the boarding school feel, and focused on a summer camp as an Americanized equivalent.

    \n

    On the other hand, if you're just interested in teens with magic and don't feel the need to maintain the scary away-from-home aspect of the Potter books, you could have an honors class/gifted program in an ordinary school, or a "wizard's magnet school" that the kids get bussed to. This allows you to merge Potter with the American "teen witch" genre, like "Sabrina the Teen-Aged Witch". I've been thinking Trollbabe would be a good ruleset to run a teen witch game.

    \n Shreyas forgot to mention Ashes of Shaolin, the collaborative fiction thing we did with Thomas Robertson and Josh Kashinsky. The premise was:

    Harry Potter meets Volcano High School, all under the auspices of an anachronistic wuxia setting.

    Shaolin Temple has been burned by an invading army from the tribal regions of the north. Its warrior monks have been scattered to the four winds and eight directions, making a living as they can. Some have renounced their vows and settled down into a relatively normal life. Others have been adopted into non-militant monasteries. Some have founded the secret Heaven & Earth Societies, the "Triads" that work to undermine the rule of the invader government.

    A generation has passed and stories have begun to circulate once more, especially now that the daughters, sons, and adopted students of the Shaolin disapora have been called to attend a new academy being built on Zu Shan mountain, far to the West beyond the empire's grasp, one that will train them in the forgotten arts of the flying swordsmen...

    My friend Rafe recorded himself reading the first 10 chapters aloud. Hmm, I'll have to see if I can get a copy of that.
