    We seriously need an academy for GMing.

    Or perhaps, games that better tell the GM what they're supposed to be doing. There's a million different ways to game, in a fun way, as much as there's a million possible games you can play with a ball- as long as everyone can get on the same page.

    Typically the players have their own ideas, the GM has her or his own ideas, and most groups assume they've all come to do the same thing without confirmation.

    Of course he can't explain what a "track" is, because odds are pretty good he lacks the tools to communicate it to the players, or pick up whatever the players are also looking for. He has a nebulous idea, unbacked by experience, instilled by many games that have told him if he masters task resolution a game will magically form, as much as mixing organic chemicals together spontaneously creates life.

    Aside from ramming someone through 5 years of theory, or getting them to grasp Flags (before input, or Social Contract), I usually rather just have someone play Inspectres... then work from there.
