\n So, I am working through some specs on Iridium V2 and I am reviewing some armor systems.So far, I have (Feel free to correct or add to):

D20: Abstract, makes you harder to be hit (decreases chance of opponent hitting you), armor lasts forever

Palladium: Middlin concrete, protects for points of damage

Iridium: Concrete, Ablative, heavier armor makes you easier to be hit

oWoD: Concrete, subtracted damage successes but limited your dodge IIRC.

I guess we could add DR to D20 as well, so there might be more of a concrete aspect to it.

So, other armors? Looking for as comprehensive a list as possible.

Thanks,
Bill

    \n Top Secret: Converted lethal damage to bashing damage. My favorite for ease, logic, and playability, but only works in modern soft armor settings. Each location covered reduced dex? I forget but there was a real incentive to use vests rather than jackets and the like.
    \nShadowrun 2-3e: reduces difficulty of using Endurance to soak damage.
    \nSLA Industries: Subtractive but countered by Penetration of the weapon used. Penetration was usually too high to make armor work, but the concept was sound.
    \nBattlelords 23C: Ablative and Subtractive, but much more the former. Subtractive would vary based on type of weapon and armour. A good gear bunny gamist design.

    \n RuneQuest: Armor abstorbs damage to a specific location. Optional rules allow for soft and hard armor types to differ in resistance to blunt trauma as opposed to piercing attacks. Armor provides encumberance, and modifiers to skills. Shields and weapons also have armor points for parrying but are ablative.
    Stormbringer: Armor absorbs damage, but the value is rolled each time, so it may end up giving you nothing, it may end up giving you a lot. No hit locations. IIRC armor may be degraded.
    Hero Wars: Armor is an edge which reduces the Action Points lost when losing in an extended contest. Weapons have an edge which increases the AP loss to yhour opponent.
    HeroQuest: Armor and weapons are an augment to skills used in combat but a penalty to others. i.e. with chain + shield + sword I get a +8 to my Sword and Shield Fighting Skill. (Note that many people chose to ignore this rule in favor of treating significant armor or weapons as an ability otherwise treating it as chrome).

    \n Mekton 2/Zeta:

    Ablative armor is cheap, gives extra hp basically.
    Normal armor blocks X damage, then decreases X by 1.
    Forcefields block X damage, more than that and they break completely.

    Human sized armor made you easier to hit if it was heavy.
    Mecha sized armor it really depended (armor = weight, weight makes you slower, but you could spend points otherwise, etc.)

    Iron Heroes:

    Armor blocks a random amount of damage, the die size being dependent on how heavy the armor is (1d4, 1d6, etc.), Armor does not make you easier to hit. Shields make you harder to hit.
