\n So tomorrow I'm probably going to get together with two of my friends to game, and we're probably going to play an RPG. I've been running an nWoD campaign, but at the moment I kind of feel like doing something else, something that *I* can play too, and something that isn't too serious. Does anyone have any good suggestions for such a game? If you could please only suggest things that I can get online either for free or as an inexpensive pdf that would be awesome. Cuz I can't really get a hold of a new physical in one day.
Oh, and well... I own some GMless stuff myself already that would be suitable for one shots, but some of it doesn't seem particularly light and non-serious in its normal mode. So if anyone wants to offer suggestions for how I could turn them into such a thing, that would be cool too. Those are: Contenders, Polaris, Grey Ranks, Dirty Secrets, Best Friends, the Committee for the Exploration of Mysteries, and Shock. and a couple others that i don't think are particularly suitable for just 3 people.

help me out, smart SG peoples!

    \n I'm tooting my own horn here, but Coming of Age is exactly what you're asking for, with no need to modify or pull the game out of its comfort zone. It is a light, one shot game geared for 3 - 6 players. It takes about 3 hours to play, including character creation, and all the rules fit on the character sheet, and the closest thing it has to a GM is that is flows better if one player has read through the rules in advance and helps to keep things going while he or she is playing.

    That link is to a pdf of the free playtest rules, just use pace 1 (pace got eliminated in the later versions of the rules, because they always used pace 1). Or you could send me a private message with an email and I'll send you a newer copy with the Scions of the Superspy setting (you play James Bond's illegitimate kids who have to save the world because their deadbeat dad screwed up).

    - Mendel S.
