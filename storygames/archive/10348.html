\n Riff with me.

So Dominion is this fantastic new card game where play consists of building your own deck of cards from various draw piles. Each turn, you get a new hand of cards from your deck, and they give you things that you can do to draw new cards from draw piles into your deck. Most of the 'things you can do' are things like: draw more cards from your deck into your hand, add new cards from draw piles into your deck, play more cards. Very simple building blocks, very complex results. It is a fantastic, lean, brilliant engine for play.

How do we rip off this fantastic engine for story gaming?

One thought:

Instead of buying victory point cards and totaling them up at the end, you can buy damage cards and apply them to badguys (or call them resolution cards and apply them to conflicts). You can also buy, say, weapon cards that allow you to buy larger damage cards (and think TSoY here, with a universal 'harm' that's not just seeping wounds).

But I can tell this isn't the best implementation. I've only had the chance to play Dominion a handful of times; maybe I just need to play it again (and again and again) to get better perspective...

    \n Josh, I actually have the beginnings of a write up of an RPG that is largely a reskin of Dominion.
    \n
    \nThe premise is a fantasy wild west boom town ala Deadwood where instead of miners looking for gold in the black hills their Delvers looking for ancient treasure in a newly discovered ancient ruin.
    \n
    \nThe game is played in two parts with one part being the goings on in the town with vaguely Otherkindish mechanics and where you select the cards that will be part of dungeon play. The dungeon play is a game of Dominion to see who can grab the most loot.
    \n
    \nMy most recent write up has strayed away from pure Dominion mechanics but originally it was almost just relabelling the cards.
    \n
    \nI won't have a chance to work on it in detail until I get Blood Red Sands and Robots out the door, but if you want to collaborate to develop it further, I'd be open. We share similar sensibilities in letting board gameyness intrude in our RPG designs.

    \n Ideas.

    What's in your deck tells you true things about your character. Discard pile's face down. When you draw cards or whatever you also have to mill (discard without seeing) a bunch of cards, and other players via some mechanism can add cards to your discard pile without you knowing what they are. They get shuffled back in, you might see 'em next time around, you might not...

    Cursed artifact: put 2x the following card in your discard pile. "+3 to attack and you get a cookie. If you draw 2+ of these cards at once you are immediately transformed into a demon."

    A group deck. Add cards to it via your character's actions (and char creation!). Add cards in secret: "did you just give us a +2 or a Betrayal??".

    \n I'm not too interested in making dungeon-crawls (other than to peruse the rulebook fondly, and then never actually play), but I'll just generally riff on this subject and say that I can scarcely play board games that have this level of detail without trying to devise internal plot justification for everything.

    Example: drawing Victory cards means your hand fills up with cards that are useless in the moment - why? Every good lord visits his territories at some point or another; drawing that Province card means you're going on a tour of the countryside, to keep up appearances, thus having to neglect more interesting business for the moment.
