    \n I have hacked a system that I have seen in a sort of "choose-your-own-adventure" comic (I believe it was from 2000AD Diceman 1 - I found it here: http://www.abandonia.com/en/gamebooks#2000AD:+Diceman ).

    The idea behind it was the faster you go it is more difficult to hit you or catch-up to you, but it was harder for you to hit anything or avoid obstacles. What I have done is to use the abstracted speed as a modifier to rolls. I have used it in few different systems like GURPS, Savage Worlds and a Polish RPG - Neuroshima. Any system where you can apply modifiers works for that.

    You choose your speed rating, let's say 4. If you need to avoid obstacle, take a tight turn or shoot the traffic lights you take a -4 to your rolls. It also means that if someone wants to shoot at you or ram you they also take a -4 roll. At the end of the turn you get a number of points equal to your speed (or some number (vechicle grade or something) + your speed). Race to let's say 40 points (or get over 15 points against the other guy to loose him).

    I kinda improvised the rest. Like drawn cards for each "leg" of the race to see if there is any obstacle etc.
