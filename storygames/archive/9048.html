    \n Vincent Baker is hosting some really interesting discussions about the relationship between fiction and physical-world-stuff in RPGs on his blog. In particular, this conversation leads up to his argument for fiction affecting real-world stuff (such as escalation in Dogs in the Vineyard, where a purely fictional thing has an effect on your character sheet).

      \n Want to publish ebooks in multiple formats, including ones for portable devices?

      I uploaded my Word file to Smashwords (it also takes RTF) and it autoconverted it to about 8 different formats - html, pdf, .mobi (Kindle), Epub (Stanza reader on iPhone), RTF, LRF (Sony Reader), Palm doc and plaintext.

      You can set your books as free, charge what you like or let the reader decide. The site takes 15%. You can also let readers have substantial amounts of preview (they actually suggest 50% of the total text).

      You need a fairly plain Word or RTF file. Charts and images may shift around and tables probably won't work, because it's autoconversion and they don't charge you for it.
