\n In Italy, Narrattiva just released the italian version of Murderous Ghosts.
Me and my wife were eager to try it.
My wife, Francesca, was the MC, and I was the other player.
We enjoyed it but... let's see.
I don't know if the problem is just in the italian version, but the guys that published it in Italy are usually really good in translations, expecially with baker's works that are definitely not easy to read.

Anyway, I found myself running a lot of times, as I tried usually to escape from barely anything.
When everything that moves is a ghost trying to kill you, stretching out rotten limbs towards you, I think it's difficult to set up a nice conversation.
Francesca, as the MC, found a page saying "as far as you can judge, the actions of the other player will realistically let him to survive and escape from the ghost?"
To be more precise: In the story, I discovered that ghosts were able to grab and use tools; a superannuated, skinny, spectral nurse was trying to pierce me with a huge old syringe, screaming obscenities. I was running through a corridor. Suddenly, I opened a door in her face trying to stop at least the syringe (by sticking it into the door). It worked, and I escaped the ghost.
But, according to the manual, Francesca should have said "I don't think it will help. You block the syringe with the door, but the ghost passes through the wood, and chokes you to death" or "In my opinion, the ghost is quicker than you, and easily avoid the door, drives the syringe in your right eyes and pushes it into your brain. You're dead."
The only discriminating factors seemed to be her judgement, and her idea of realism (but how the hell it is possible to determine when the deeds of a ghost are realistic?).
Another suggestion for the MC was to do something "if it scares you". Damn, she could have said "dying in a horrible way is scary! you're dead and gone, no way!"

I'm sure that we missed something or we played wrong.
I cannot believe that the MC can perform an action or a "move" that allows him to instantly kill the other player "because he says so".
That's not scary, that's deeply frustrating.
Can anyone help us?
