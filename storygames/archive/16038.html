      \n I made my first game purchase since I was a teenage recently, I ordered two Ron Edwards books, because I want to see how he presented both Bangs! and his glossary.

      I kept my ODD red box (and blue box) dice for over twenty years before giving them away recently.

      Though now I feel that I can justify purchasing indie games because I want to release a free game, and want to see how other people present their work, even if it is another genre.

      Also since now I only kept Harn Master, as my rules, I should probably get a game that does not freak out d20 players. But Idk what that is, as currently I am drawn to some unconventional games.

      I mostly run whatever game people want to play, though the last few years I stopped playing and focused on collaborative writing. I have a reputation of being a better ideas person than a player, though I think it is just sometimes hard for me to convey what my character is thinking or feeling. As I am one of those people that lets the PC make a decision I would never do.

      I am a big fan of karma and narrative resolution and my preferred style of DM is without rule-books, and just use a DM screen as the rule-book. Something like D&D or V:tM, I would not consider even opening a rule book to play, unless it was to go through a list of spells or something. I do not personally ascribe to DM Fiat, and believe Social Contract is the defining rule of any system.

      Though I am a pretty lax player, and try to avoid arguments where possible. Though I ask that there is some consistency and played without personal bias (I have had a few very bad experience with RP), though on the internet would be a very sensible way to play in my opinion as you are already writing notes during the course of a game.

      Cheers,
      Snake_Eyes

      \nHi Victorian,

      Just to clarify a few things:
      The GM doesn't create any questions. You are allowed to leave a question blank if you want at the beginning. If you do, then you or the other players can fill it in later during the game.
      Psi*Run doesn't run off pre-planned adventures; it's played spontaneously. As GM, I do two things before the game: I give you a setting where you'll start, and I write a few ominous details down on a Chaser Card, to be used later.
      Last, don't try to create your character before the game. It's wouldn't break the game, but creating characters together with the group right before you start is the way to go.

      You're more than welcome to join us tonight; add me on G+. See you later.
