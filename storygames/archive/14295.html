\n I'm in a regular Skype gaming group that I formed with a few other designers in order to playtest each others' games. (I've also been releasing audio of the sessions on my podcast Designer vs. Reality, for anyone who's interesting in following games in development or in exercising their playtest analysis skills). We may be having a lull where none of us have any games that are ready for testing but we'd still like to playtest some games. Are there any designers out there who have games that are ready for some external playtesting?

Here are our likely constraints:
It's a group of three people (Nolan, Leo, and me)
We play over Skype without any virtual tabletops (although we use Googledocs for sharing written info)
Our sessions last about three hours
I'll probably record and podcast the session

We may need a game to play by Monday night, so I'm open to suggestions (not making any promises, though -- we might be able to get one of our games ready).

    \n Which one? Heh. The "maiming" track has been eliminated, with "crippled," "mangled," and "amputated" being occasional add-ons to grievous, severe, and severe wounds (respectively). Limited resurrection is in. First aid kits (and all other kits) use the Distant Threat mechanic to run out of stuff rather than having a set number of uses (but with modifiers that mean you usually get at least 10 uses out of it). There's a new Trauma Track that fits checkboxes and explanations for everything but the ASDs on one letter-sized sheet (including that laundry list of ailments and afflictions). I still have to do the grunt work of actually writing up the new damage tables to reflect stuff. And laying them out in a format that's actually, like, usable.

    Aside from guns getting changed from having 2/20 instakills to 1/20 like everything else, the system is still just as deadly and unforgiving. Hopefully it's easier to use, though.
