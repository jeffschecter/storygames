      I also have a "War" board game I have been working/playing with.

      It is what I call a "Persistent World" board game. The things that happen in one game will translate over into the next game. This plays out similarly to Mechaton, and uses rules similar to Marvel Heroic. At the start of every "Campaign" the deck of cards are secretly sorted in a very specific way to create a series of events that are prescripted for the next 1-5 games into small decks that are revealed during play. The decks for the next games are already sorted and secretly placed in an envelope to be revealed for the next game. The cards and score cards that players earn go into a dossier file that can be saved for the next game.

      The game is really neat with the way players take on different roles that completely change the way that they interact with the game. Just because it is about a war does not mean it is about miniatures combat, many times you will resolve things through diplomacy, deals with other players, or manipulation of the economy.

      The part I have to figure out is the mechanics that create the story for all the things that are going on. My group can do it without rules since they are veteran story gamers, but I need something a lot more concrete for a board game.
