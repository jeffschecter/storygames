\n First, The Rending Box. (All prices approximate, because they're actually converted from pounds).\n
  • Box $1.50. The major expense, but worth it.
  • Spraypaint for box about 25c. Easy. I'd never done it before and it looked fine.
  • Dice about 10c each.
  • Booklets printed on my home printer. About 50c.
  • I sold 20, at Dragonmeet, for $15 each.
\n


Next, the much cheaper Cthulhu Apocalypse.\n
  • Booklets about 50c. Printed on home printer. Stapled with a long-armed stapler (really cheap on Amazon) in the centre.
  • Green card covers, for booklets, about 25c each. The version in the picture is a prototype and doesn't have these. The final versions looked much better.
  • Card wrapping, about 25c.
  • I've sold about 25 of these at Dragonmeet for about $15
\n

Finally, the Purist adventures. In fact, I used a printer for these (Collective Copies, they were great). But they're the same idea as Cthulhu Apocalypse and could be constructed cheaply the same way. They're printed booklets with nail varnish dripped on the front. We've sold about 75 of each booklets in all.

    \n Fair point. The nail polish costs nothing: a couple of dollars between 25 books.
