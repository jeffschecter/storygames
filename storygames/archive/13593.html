\n Not really sure what category this goes under. So I am a caver, actually a cave scientist now. I've always been fascinated with dungeons and dungeon crawls in all their forms in RPGs. So what I have here is my gear pack for 5 days underground as we play (errr I mean work) in this cave.

So here is the pic of the gear


The yellow bag is the gear bag where all this stuff will go. The black duffel is already half full at this point. The Burning Wheel book is for scale and it's an awesome game. The upper right hand corner contains the caving gear (ascending and repelling eq, knee pads etc...)

Also included is 5 days of food, 3L of water (we have to find water sources to refill), clothes, and the pee and pooh bags (pack in, pack out).

With science gear this all totals somewhere around 20 kilos maybe a little more.

    \n So in Underworld and Wilderness the typically dungeon crawl lasts one week. We are 2 days shy.

    So the ropes are already in place so we don't have to carry those. Imagine if you did and were exploring as dungeoneers.
    The first drop is 10m the second drop is 50m, and there are other unknown drops and ascents where we are going. So 300-500m of rope would be a conservative estimate if you were carry it all in. The ropes are 11mm static line and this gets damn heavy. The ropes would weigh in at an additional 25-50 kilos.

    Now further imagine armor, weapons, and other adventuring gear. You get the picture. I really doubt anyone would go in a full plate harness.

    Oh yeah the temp is 21 C with 97% humidity.

    When I get back I'll try and dungeonfy the cave where we are going so you can see all the drops and imagine monsters lurking the New Mexico desert.

    \n We have some pretty great caves out here in AZ, and whenever I go into one I also think about dungeonizing it.

    Mostly if we hit a real cave like a bunch of period-accurate dungeon-divers might, we'd go in wildly overloaded (imagining big woolen bedrolls, shitty medieval-era gear like ropes and mmmmaybe pitons if we were inventive, oily torches), haul in food that's so stale/rotten you'll be sick for days, and pretty much fuck ourselves over the first time we have to run away from something. Twisted ankles and broken limbs at a minimum. Lots of unexpected falls because, frankly, there's just no way to throw decent light if you can't strap it to your head.

    I've done some urban exploration (urbex) as well, which is probably closer to the exploring-the-long-forgotten-temple model, and it's not much better. You can maneuver a little easier but there's all kinds of sharp shit, you still can't carry enough pre-20th century gear, there's still not enough light. And, wow, the disorientation of never quite knowing where your "north" is! No biggie with compasses or a strong GPS signal, but even those are dicey at best today. Magic: it'd have to be magic lights, magic orienteering tools, magical food.

    Whenever I've actually indulged my players in an old-fashioned dungeon delve I always keep it to day trips. Getting unexpectedly stuck inside something overnight isn't a disaster, but by the third day you're going to be in serious trouble.

    Posted By: ffilzReal caves a nothing like D&D dungeons or caves (D&D caves are probably closer to Star Trek caves than real caves...).
    Oh, I dunno... some of the Underdark stuff (and Dungeoneer's Survival Guide) tried to reflect more natural cave formations.

    Thing is... dungeon <> cave. The former in constructed, not naturally forming (to be pedantic). Perhaps few modules used natural caves, or only had one corner of the dungeon impinge onto a natural system. But it's Dungeons and Dragons, not Caves and Colossi.

    All that said... more natural cave systems would make AWESOME dungeons, if evocatively presented (by the author and the GM). They might be a bit of a nightmare to map in any manner other than abstractly (i.e. a flowchart, not a topo map), but it would be awesome to have all the light occlusion, shifting shadows, twists and turns, tight tunnels, rock shifts, water systems, etc, etc, etc feeding into every encounter or exploration!

    If only someone in the RPG community was an expert on cave systems, to make such a cool, system-agnostic module/supplement!

    \n I found that Wielizka Salt Mine was remarkably dungeon-like - ten foot wide straight corridors, big rectangular rooms, etc - because the salt is easy to cut out and forms in big blocks; also it's air-conditioned and electrically pumped (apparently under medieval conditions it was fiendishly hot and perpetually in danger of flooding, which isn't part of my mental picture for dungeoncrawling). The medieval silver mine I visited was much more strange - twisty, winding, low passages, no rooms, often just crawlspaces. Even though it also had been dug out by human hands (as I assume is true of D&D; dungeons), the hardness of the rock and the low tech of the mining tools made it turn out labyrinthine and cave-like.
    \n
    \nI was immensely jazzed to be in these underground spaces & would love to do some caving not too far from NYC if anyone is down for it when the weather is better. Also there's urban spelunking for the bank-robbery side of things.
