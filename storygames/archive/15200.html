    \n I believe Tori has the numbers on new folks, but it was somewhere in the teens.

    I was happy that I got to run InSpectres. A guy walked up to the Story Games open play table, and picked up InSpectres and said something like, "I used to play D&D, and read about this game 10 years ago, and have been wanting to play it ever since." He lit up when I said, "Ok, want to play it now?" We had a good time, and despite some initial nervousness on his part, he did as well. I hope he looks around the internet for games & community, now.

    I also played Silver & White, despite Jackson's reservations, and had a blast. I got genuine chills during that game--suprise and fear, and that's never happened for me in a roleplaying/story game before. I'd never played with the crew (Orion, Tori, someone else I can't remember her name), but we meshed quickly and what came of it was special.

    The only other game I played was My Daughter, the Queen of France, though I had to leave early and didn't get the full experience. It worked OK in a crowded con setting, and we had one "new to story games" player, though she didn't mesh as well, since we were all going monotone in scenes (per the rules) and she couldn't quite bring herself to restrain her emotion. I had more fun playing other people's characters in scenes rather than my own, but chalk that up to only playing like 4 scenes. Let's play this before the year is out, Harry.

    And that's all I played! Brenda and I went to a few panels, and they ranged from mediocre (geek culture, feminism, and race) to great (Ink-Stained Amazons). It was sort of a unique con experience for me, as it was my first non-gaming centric con, and it was my wife's first con in general. She was a bit overwhelmed at first, and Saturday was kinda difficult for her, but Sunday was a blast, and she stepped up and had a great time with InSpectres.

    I picked up some fun art, as well:









    And oh! I got to visit Card Kingdom. Guardian Games wants to be that place so badly.

    \n Hello,

    The con as a whole went really, really well. I spent it mostly just kicking it with the Northwest Story Games crew in the gaming room. However, my girlfriend attended panels in every single time slot and she made a very keen and I think important observation. She said the whole thing felt less like a convention and more like a conference. The difference being that a convention is a celebratory environment while a conference is about sharing and promoting actionable ideas.

    That really, really shifted focus on the contents of the panel discussions. Which is a good thing! But it made creating a story game presence as an out reach thing really, really difficult because people we usually not able to commit to 3 to 4 hours for a single game. The result was that while I had an awesome time meeting and playing with people I usually only deal with online, I didn't really feel that we connected with the rest of the con.

    By contrast there was a guy over in the corner doing a really smart thing with D&D4e. I don't know if he was officially supported by WotC or a game store or what, but what he was doing was running little mini-adventures in 1 hour slots. You picked up a character, you threw down a miniatures a couple of encounters later you were done. And that's all you really need to do to show case the game. It made me realize just how poorly "Story Games" scale. It made me start thinking about games that might fit that format. Which is probably worth its own thread so I won't go into details just now.

    So, overall a success, but much food for thought.

    Jesse
