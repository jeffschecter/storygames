\n Lately I have been wanting to design a rules lite superhero game. The following is a beta idea for a method of creating Powers using Keywords as loose restrictions.
\n
\nThere exists 40 Keywords. During Character Creation you either roll up two or choose two. I suppose you could even roll up one and then choose the other. This is likely based on an agreement between the players at the table.
\n
\nRolling for Keywords isn't as painfully random as it sounds. You roll two sets of 1D100, pick one result for the X-Axis and the other result is used for the Y-Axis. Then you do it once more. This gives you a small amount of control over what you receive.
\n[align=center][/align]
\nKeywords form the core of each character's Power. Once a player has his Keywords he expected to detail his Theme. Theme details what your Keywords mean for your specific Character.
\n
\nOnce you have your Theme, you create 3 Techniques. Techniques are representations of what your character knows how to do with his Powers. Think of Green Lantern and the way he learns to do different things with his power ring... Heroes begin with great power but they only unlock their true strength by using and understanding their power. As the game progresses... The players will get chances to acquire new Techniques at the behest of the GM.
\n
\nTechniques are encouraged to be narrowly defined but are only as mechanically detailed as the players at the table agree is necessary.
\nThis means that if your group agrees on it, you may have to define how many feet you can teleport, whether or not you can teleport to places you've never seen before, if you can teleport others with you, etc...
\n
\nMy question is... does there exist too much creative freedom here for this to be a viable option for creating powers?
\n
\nAny suggestions, observations, questions, comments, criticisms and such things are welcome. As long as they pertain to the topic, of course.
\n
\nSuggestions regarding more/different Keywords are both welcomed and appreciated. =D I do realize that some of the Keywords are insanely vague while others overlap... The overlapping part is fine, in my opinion, because the origin of the power may yet be very different.

    \n Examples...
    \n
    \n[align=center]Themes[/align]
    \nDuke has the Teleport and Shadow Keywords.\n
    \n
    Duke has access to the Shadow Dimension. A world that mirrors our own but where shadows are living entities called Shadowkind. Each Shadowkind is connected to a specific person. Duke is able to enter this dimension and move throughout it as he pleases. This allows him to reappear in the Material World in different locations.
    \n
    \nHugo has the Create and Machine Keywords.\n
    \n
    Hugo has a robot factory hive inside of his body. This machine can build robotic bugs that he can control mentally.
    \n
    \nTo reinforce what Theme does for the game...
    \n
    \nQuord has the Create and Machine Keywords.\n
    \n
    Quord has access to a pocket dimension where he keeps all sorts of hi-tech weapons including mega energy cannons and missile launchers. With a command word he can summon these weapons to his grip.
    \n
    \n
    \nRoy Gulvin
    \nOccupation: Mercenary
    \nWeapons: Katana, Various Small Firearms
    \nKeywords: Sound, Speed
    \nTheme: Roy is capable of nullifying sound waves and moving at super speeds.
    \n
    \n[align=center]Techniques[/align]Silence Is Golden\n
    \n
    Roy may project a field of complete silence around his body. Any noise that occurs within this field will be nullified completely as well as any noise that enters it. This field is invisible and very malleable, he can choose to cover only specific parts of his body. With some effort Roy can also project this aura at a distance to cover objects no larger than 4\u2019x4\u2019.
    \n
    \nAt the Speed of Sound\n
    \n
    Roy is capable of moving almost as fast as sound, about 900\u2019 per second. However, he is only able to do this in short bursts. This power also allows Roy to react with the alacrity required to operate at speeds of 900\u2019/sec. The aura generated by his Powers protects his body and clothing from friction.
    \n
    \nSonic Boom Snap\n
    \n
    By centering his speed bursts directly into his hand, Roy can move his fist so fast that he creates a minor sonic boom. This concussion of sound is strong enough to deafen normal humans and harm any who come in contact with the sound waves. Roy is completely protected from the sonic boom because of his Silence Aura.
    \n
    \n
    \nMana Hart, AKA Aikida
    \nOccupation: News Reporter
    \nWeapon of Choice: Ancient Kung-Fu Mastery
    \nKeywords: Air, Time
    \nTheme: Aikida is a descendant of an ancient line of supernatural martial artist. The legacy of the Hart clan is the ability to manipulate the immortal force known only as the Ageless Winds.
    \n
    \n[align=center]Techniques[/align]Temporal Wind Lance\n
    \n
    Aikida is capable of gathering the ambient energies of the Ageless Winds to create a sky blue spiraling shaft of power. This energy shaft may emanate from any of her limbs and is capable of piercing or bludgeoning. Using the bludgeoning power she can actually launch herself high into the air.
    \n
    \nFast Forward\n
    \n
    By summoning the Ageless Winds, Aikida can accelerate the rate at which she moves exponentially. She may also use this effect on objects and beings she touches.
    \n
    \nForm of the Ageless Winds\n
    \n
    By attuning herself with the Ageless Winds, Aikida's body takes on its properties. This allows her to move short distances instantly while remaining intangible.
    \n
    \n
    \nDmitri Aronofsky AKA Dr. Meteor
    \nOccupation: Meteorologist
    \nWeapon of Choice: Natural Propensity Towards Brawling
    \nKeywords: Metal, Fire
    \nTheme: Dr. Meteor has strange space metal fused to his flesh that he can control psychically. The metal that suffuses his body can take many shapes, it also contains a fiery core that can be summoned.
    \n[align=center]
    \nTechniques[/align]Space Steel Shift\n
    \n
    Dr. Meteor can alter the shape of the metal that surrounds his torso. He can create any shape as long as the size of these creations doesn't exceed 3' x 3'.
    \n
    \nLava Core\n
    \n
    Dr. Meteor is capable of covering his metal ally in flames hot enough to melt most common metals. He may also fling these flames.
    \n
    \nFiery Constructs\n
    \n
    Dr. Meteor is capable of hurling parts of his space steel by first melting it. These parts automatically form specific structures that are controlled by Dr. Meteor psychically. These creations cannot exceed the size of a 5' man.

    \n I've gotten strong responses from the other two forums I've posted this on... but I am still wondering about something.
    \n
    \nI was thinking about how I can make this system a little better or perhaps more functional... Talislanta came to mind.
    \n
    \nWhat if Techniques were built around Action Words? This would limit the precise mechanics of each Technique.
    \n
    \nFor those who don't know... The 'Modes' of Talislanta 4e are; Alter, Attack, Conjure, Defend, Heal, Illusion, Influence, Move, Reveal, Summon, Transform and Ward.
    \n
    \nSo what do y'all think?

    \n If I roll randomly, I get Time/Restoration (depending on which way I choose), then Magnetic/Form. I must admit, I'm not sure what to do with this. Nothing's leaping to mind.

    Perhaps it's a freak result. I'll try again. Adaptation/Animal and Speed/Kinetic. OK, that's better. I can transform myself into a variety of fast animals: leopards, eagles, horses.

    Still, it's not giving me instant fun. I like the idea of a table, but I think you should refine it so it only gives cool stuff. Seriously, roll again and again, and every time you get something boring, change the table.

    Apart from that, it's hard to know what to suggest. We could go in the usual direction, which means you answer the following three questions: what's your game about? What do the players do? What do the characters do?

    And I think I'd need to know about the central mechanic before knowing whether Action Words are a good idea.

    \nI've had three or four people use the random selection method and they have all come up with fun sounding characters, if that makes you feel any better. =P That said, manual selection is entirely allowed and included as a potential option. In fact... I think the encouraged option should be, 'Select one Keyword and roll for the other.'
    \n
    \nI did mention in the first post that new/additional suggestions for Keywords would be helpful. For instance, Time is not really a favorite of mine.
    \n
    \nI think part of it is that you have to be self-motivated and really interested in the superhero genre. That said, I'm sure that applies to every RPG that revolves around a specific genre... its just that with anything that allows free form player creation (PDQ, Risus, Donjon, etc...) it is doubly true. In a way this lays more responsibilities onto the player... which can be perceived as good or bad. I only created the system this way because that's what I wanted out of a game. I wanted that free form style of player created abilities only made specifically for superheroes.
    \n
    \n
    Apart from that, it's hard to know what to suggest. We could go in the usual direction, which means you answer the following three questions: what's your game about? What do the players do? What do the characters do?
    \n
    \nThe game is about a bit above street level superheroes doing what superheroes do; being super heroic. The game is meant to be powered by a light set of rules that allow for free form creation and interpretation with advice designed to promote the previously mentioned ideas.
    \nPlayers are encouraged to invest as much creative thinking as possible in the designing and application of their heroes' powers. The mechanics are simple enough to where one isn't required to plan a lot as far as the dice and how they are thrown is concerned. Its more about coming up with great ways that the characters can impact the game fiction, especially as far as powers are concerned.
    \nI don't think I'm capable of writing a game (or, even the basics of a game) that isn't about what characters do. So my answer to the 3rd question is the same as my first response. The game is about characters and what they do, primarily what the PCs do.
    \nThe included (admittedly sparse) setting has the common people aware of super powered individuals. All the super powered folks are aware of what they can do off the bat. The event that causes the outbreak of strangely evolving humans is the same event that creates super zombies with powers similar to the living super folks plus the thirst for human flesh. Think zombie apocalypse crossed with Batman only Batman can teleport and drain electricity.
    \n
    \n
    I think I'd need to know about the central mechanic before knowing whether Action Words are a good idea.
    \n
    \nYou can see here at the Forge how the mechanics turned out. You'll see how badly my mechanics were bashed initially, as well. -_-
    \nWhat you need to know as related to Techniques is that mechanically they are free to interpretation for the most part based on how detailed the group wishes things to be. Originally I intended to include mechanical suggestions for the application of powers... but then I got to thinking about this 'Action Word' idea.
