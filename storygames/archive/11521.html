\n So the gaming group is getting ready for part II of the Grinding Gear. We are using a slightly house ruled version of the LWBs. I've been using some GM fiat and handwaving when the players do something that isn't covered by the rules. So for part II I want something different.

What are some good ways to specify who has narration rights in ODD? I am looking for ideas. I know a simple one is when successful on a roll the player narrates using the established fiction and the ref embellishes. What else might be fun?



Possible useful or useless information follows-
My default FRG is Burning Wheel but I don't want my ODD game to be houseruled into BW, that seems silly.

The resolution systems we are using so far are:
Combat - the d20 thing outcomes are either alive or dead
Saving Throws
Delver Skills such as Hear Noise, Move Silently, etc..

Things the players want to be rewarded for (I asked them):
"rewarded for game objectives -- beating opponents and figuring out puzzles in this game. I like it when the GM finds ways to reward a clever idea or important action that a player takes."

"In old-skool dungeon crawls, I want rewards for cleverness and bravery and working as a team. Except when I'm the thief, in which case I want to bogart all the swag"

Thanks!!,
Ara

    \n We talking about old D&D here? By default, the players say what their characters say, think, feel and do, the GM says everything else. I have no idea what would happen if you change that. But a common thing is to let players narrate their own successes and failures, with coordination from the GM, yes.
