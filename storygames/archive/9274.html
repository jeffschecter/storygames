\n Last week I saw a performance of 7 neo-benshi film interpretations. Basically, sections of movies screened with the performer voicing replacement dialogue (as in woody allen's what's up tiger lilly), or commentary in place of the dialogue, a new soundtrack, etc.

That plus a game I've been playing that draws loosely on a genre got me thinking. Instead of playing a game inspired by genre X or source Y- take an original film, book, whatever and the game is to comment on/ alter/ shorten/ re-voice that original instead in a way that pays homage to or makes fun of it or a combo.

Not exactly sure how- if there should be a set of tools etc but it seems worth trying. Anyone ever done something like this?

    \n andy, I like the idea of a commentator player, perhaps rotating, that could provide perspective while other players are focusing on their characters

    some additional examples of this kind of thing

    1. play an old school D&D module using a system that itself is a kind of commentary/ homage/ parody > like hackmaster or drowning & falling
    2. play an old school module using an indie rpg like, I don't know, my life with master for Queen of the demon web pits or Village of hommlet with dogs in the vineyard
    3. 'play' a film or youtube video with each player assigned a main character and bit characters and getting a set number of rewinds/ pauses to think of dialogue to insert in lieu of the film dialogue
    4. play a randomly chosen pulp story with players able to re-arrange or rip out pages, role-play scenes, assign dice or cards to important characters, plot points, villains etc
