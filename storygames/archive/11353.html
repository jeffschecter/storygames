\n
\n To put it simply,

If I have a girl over at my house, and it's 2am, and we've been drinking coffee or tea or hot chocolate, and talking for a few hours, and are sitting perhaps too close on the couch, and there's great chemistry... and it'd be inappropriate to start making out, because she's not my girlfriend, but it'd be dumb to just say goodnight and part ways... and she hears that I play roleplaying games (or perhaps already knew this) and says "want to play one right now?"

In that situation, what game do I play with her?

Breaking the Ice seems so abundantly inappropriate. It'd be like using a chainsaw to chop vegetables. The secretly-we're-both-flirting-we're-just-not-admitting-it element is already there in this hypothetical situation, and to introduce Breaking the Ice seems... redundant?

Anyways, so, what is the perfect game to play in this situation?

    \nThis is good.

    For what it's worth, this thread has given me a few:
    Ghost/Echo
    Sweet Agatha

    And some possibilities:
    Annalise
    Breaking the Ice
    Shooting the Moon

    What's true about the first two is that they are very simple, and both spend a lot of time (a) pouring over evocative details, and (b) constructing something from them, all while being mechanically simple and never requiring a giant leap of faith.

    Giant leap of faith here means doing anything in preparation for anything else, or taking any creative risk where the creative reward isn't immediately obvious. Examples include: character creation, determining goals, making mechanical choices that have lasting mechanical effects.

      Posted By: deadlytoquealso your choice of wording was very ambiguous.
      \nVery intentional.

      There's a category of social situations (namely, "it's 2am and you're staying up talking to a beautiful girl who is interested in playing a roleplaying game with you, though she's never played one before") and I'm interested in having a response that's different from "well, that's not really possible right now."

      It doesn't matter whether the person in that situation is me, or a friend of mine, with a girlfriend or without one.

      The general idea is:
      Hey, it's 2am. You're sitting with a gorgeous and intelligent girl. You aren't going to start kissing her. You're going to play a roleplaying game with her.

      I can say with some confidence that there are many people who've kicked themselves while saying "well, I don't really know what we could play, right now," or who've pulled out the wrong game and subsequently had a lukewarm experience (and gave a lukewarm introduction).

      The suggestions I've heard so far, that I like:
      Sweet Agatha
      Ghost/Echo
      Psi Run
      Shooting the Moon
      Annalise
      Crow Game
      Nicotine Girls
      Trollbabe

      That's a pretty solid list.
      And now I feel like a more socially capable person.
