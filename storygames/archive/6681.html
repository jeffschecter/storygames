    \n I have thought about it, yes, flying over and participating. My economy has not allowed for it, yet.

    I reckon a trip over there would involve both lectures, workshops and actual gameplay. I do prefer gaming in my own language, of course, but I reckon my English is good enough to make it a great event.

    On my agenda for a workshop: both classical and modern roleplaying games
    - classical: dynamic methods in GMing
    - classical: scenarios by the building-block method
    - classical: elemental game-design within the classical paradigm
    - modern: minimal effort, maximum fun; the philosophy of simplistic design
    - modern: do you care? humanistic game-design in a new millenium
    - modern: how to develop as a designer; thematic consistency and artistic challenges
    - mix: presentation and play of Norwegian RPGs:
    Until We Sink (M. Jacobson)
    Draug, We All Had Names and ZombiePorn (Matthijs)
    Muu, Fabula and Autumn of Life (me)
    - and more ...

    I know Matthijs would bring his own agenda to such a workshop, themes both tangential to mine, and unique themes pertaining to GMing and design, and to the presentation of Norwegian games (he has great knowledge there).

    I would expect to learn a lot myself while presenting my ideas for a new audience. And I would be excited about actually talking to some of you guys. I enjoy brainstorming and creative work with others.
