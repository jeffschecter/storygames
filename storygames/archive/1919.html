I'm considering running a play-by-post game, especially since I'm going to be moving in a few months and will miss my old gaming buddies. Part of the reason I haven't started one yet is that I want to find a system and/or setting that would be well-suited to the somewhat asynchronous nature of such games.

Amber, Nobilis, and Continuum have already crossed my mind, and seem like ok possibilities - all three involve varying degrees of temporal oddity, and have pretty simple systems.

Any other suggestions? Feel free to pimp your own stuff if you think it would work.

    Pro: Dogs in the Vineyard works great over PbP. Nobilis works very well. Unisystem games like Buffy work well, too. PTA and Wushu both work, but they're definitely more fun face-to-face.

    Con: I haven't had much luck with PbP of World of Darkness games unless they're really heavily drifted towards diceless freeform roleplaying.

    I *think* Mortal Coil would work well, but I haven't scraped together the money to buy it yet. And I'd love to run Trollbabe as a PbP game, but I haven't had the chance yet.

    I'm prepping to run a PbP game of Spirit of the Century now, but it seems doable so far.

    The Mountain Witch. Slick and simple resolution system, and short-form play.
