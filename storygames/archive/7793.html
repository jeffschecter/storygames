\n So in response to some things I said about being pissed about the lack of long games, one of my friends convinced me to run a weekly 4e game, starting Tuesday the 14th with character creation.

I'm using the 4e rules, but I'm doing some creative stuff with it to make it something I really, really dig.

I'm testing out what I think of as 4e E6, which is to say E12.
Everything I'm going to use is getting a makeover. Races, monsters, and so on - 4e art sucks, so I'm just boiling them down to stats.
I'm not shying away from racism or the morality of war as issues in the game.
There's a little splash of Battlestar Galactica in there, with a "pilot episode" we'll all work together on at the first session.
Unambiguously evil forces are at work from several directions, some of which are probably better as allies than enemies.
There's a huge, dangerous environment to explore, and the nicest people out there are all sworn to kill you.

Desperation and violence are the order of the day.

    \n The players build PCs from the Sovereignty, an expansive monarchy encompassing many lesser Principalities. There is no rival to the power of the Sovereign, which stretches from the cold lands to the southern sea, dominating the entire world.

    All the races are treated as "human" despite obvious stat differences.

    The Devic are the original founders of the Sovereignty. Devics practice magic of Reason and the Virtues.
    Sidereals , a people closely related to the Devic, were first to be annexed. Sidereals intermarry with the Devics in many houses of the Council, and the two races are nearly identical in appearance. Sidereals excel in the magics of art and passion.
    Various Principalean peoples fell in the next age where the Sidereals were united under the Devics. They practise various lesser forms of magic.
    The Coast People are unwashed masses that hang around the edges of true civilization. Coast People engage in ancestor worship and other backwards practises.

    \n The Sovereign Dynasty was a great credit to its peoples, because... it cleared out a space for humanity, pushing off or exterminating the monstrous Brutes of the north and the Cryptics of the south. The Sovereignty has existed, sometimes schismatically but always surviving, for nine hundred years. The Sovereign is dead, and the succession is in doubt, because by rules of succession the next Sovereign would be a Sidereal.

    This has sparked a civil war that has reached to every corner of the Sovereignty.

    \n The story centers around Fort Daunt, in the northern reaches of the empire, near the city of Wake. A magical battle occurs there as one of the major Siderean factions arrives to claim the city. During this battle, something happens.

    Suddenly, the entire world goes dark, the earth lurches, everyone falls, and then dirt, bits of grass, water, and foliage rain down over the whole area. Both sides retreat somewhat, and make a truce. As dawn rises they find they're in a very different world... but later find the ruins of Wake. This is the same world, thousands of years later. The lands are awash with savages jabbering in senseless tongues, monsters that bear faint resemblance to the siege beasts of the Sovereignty, and little trace that anyone even remembers the Dynasty.

    Point of light, check.
    Dangerous world, check.
    Endless supply of hostiles, check.
    Tension among friendlies, check.
    Reason to unite against the rest of the world, check.
    D&D-compatible premise, check.
