\n Where I'm coming from: I'm a long-time player of The Shadow of Yesterday (TSoY). It has been my favorite RPG for a while and at Gen Con this year, I heard about Fate Core from some of my friends who told me to check it out. I bought it and consumed the rulebook. I've been debating about trying to run a game using Fate Core but have a major concern about how the systems for advancement affect the play.

I really like Keys in TSoY as a way of allowing players to determine what sorts of things lead to character advancement. Fate's advancement seems to be more authoritarian: the Story Guide just kind of tells people when they've hit a "milestone" and then all of the characters advance.

I'm wondering if anyone has enough experience with Fate Core to explain how this "feels" in play.

My concern is that the characters will be less driven because the players realize that they are rewarded for reaching the Story Guide's "milestones" rather than pursuing their characters' Keys and so they try to play into what they perceive will get them advances (even if it's counter to what the characters might do normally).

    \n Okay, so first of all Fate Core works fine when characters don't significantly pursue individual, overarching goals, but instead work together as a team to achieve objectives provided to them by the GM, so take everything I say as an option. Fate Core is EXTREMELY modular - in a way, you're asking me "how do supernatural abilities work in GURPS" and there's probably 10 answers, all valid and printed and supported and used by players of GURPS, and there's tons of GURPS games where there aren't supernatural abilities at all.

    So let's assume you're organizing a Fate Core group and you want to do some kind of fantasy thing where you're pursuing individual goals for long periods.

    Steps on the way to those goals then become the milestones in the game that makes advancement happen.

    Every session you get a minor milestone, every time you accomplish something significant in pursuit of your goal you get a significant milestone and if there's some catastrophic thing that happens that's a major milestone.

    Now note that advancement in Fate Core is not a really significant part of gameplay, since characters start out fitted for the genre and situation they're in, and aren't merely aimed at greatness in a wider world by a XP curve as in TSOY or D&D.

    \n Yeah...and definitely do look closely at what each of the milestones are.

    Minor Milestones are really just your character adjusting to their changing circumstances or the stress of the situation. They don't actually advance the character.

    Significant Milestones are your character becoming generally more adept at one thing or another as part of an ongoing situation, a broader change as a result of broader changed circumstances (though this may involve them not being so adept at something that's not as central to their ongoing, current problem.)

    And of course at Major Milestones the whole campaign is changing to accommodate a straight-up powerup, a character who has been through the fires and come out on the other side.
