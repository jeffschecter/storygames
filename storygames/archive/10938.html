\n The idea to do an accountability group came up in another thread. The idea for this thread is that you write down what you are working on and what you've got done this week. At the end of you post you write what you plan to get done in the next week.

People can comment, give support, give advice, etc. The only limit is that no one has to take any advice given.

The purpose of the thread it to give us all a weekly deadline to get something done. I've done this in the past and found it very useful. I'll try posting this thread once a week - so after the week let the old thread die and switch to the new thread. This way it won't get too long.

Anyone can use the thread but I hope that some people will use it regularly so we can form a primary group feel.

Chris Engle/Hamster Press

    Just sayin'....

    I'd like to be in, but my problems won't be solved by a "write something this week" incentive or a buddy. UNLESS that buddy is willing to actually hack on the system, make characters trying to break it, or actually organize a few folks in his or her area to do live combat tests.

    \n We need to determin if this is an acceptable thread. Here is what is written in the title of this catagory.

    Game Design Help
    Category description This is the category for folks to use who are working on a game and need specific, directed help. Ask specific questions, aim for specific feedback.

    Game design help seems to be the most specific topic on this forum but the description says "specific directed help" Accountability check in may not meet that criteria. I think saying what you're doing and what you plan to get done is specific but the "help" gotten from others is just their publically seeing what you're planning. That is not a specific piece of feedback.

    From what you said, the Praxis site is for working on games ground up. I'm not certain we are talking about that either in this type of thread. Some people may be starting at the beginning but I suspect many aren't. I know I'm not. My games are close to end stage. I would see this more as being about being public with where you are and saying what you will do next so it wouldn't matter where a person was at in development.

    We need input from the administrators of the forum on whither this is kosher. This kind of thread is like a self help group.

    If it is deamed okay then to Ryan I'd say - Sure! Writing is writing but that's just my opinion and I have absolutely no authority.

    \n Hey all, I recreated the thread over here:

    http://story-games.com/praxis/comments.php?DiscussionID=27

    And my reaction was posted over here: http://story-games.com/forums/comments.php?DiscussionID=10940&page=1#Comment_242736

    In effect, there's really nothing that warrants an argument about where things go, Praxis is still finding its legs and all.

    But basically, the split comes down to "I want Story Games to be more focused on playing games and stuff in that field; I want Praxis to be more focused on designing and publishing games and stuff in that field." In the latter camp go things like Marketing, Building (websites, presence, materials, connections, expansions, and so on).

    The "design from the bottom up" distinction was just in relation to one item that I thought might be difficult to split; House rules for existing games (like, the Red Box Hack 2.0 Classes Thread, where there's a guy posting a bunch of homebrew classes for an existing game, getting feedback etc). While this is an act of creation as powerful as unique game creation, I wanted to keep those threads on S-G, since they relate to the tabletop play of games that are already available. And split the "this is an entirely new game, not a mod or houserule for an existing game" stuff over on Praxis.

    But this thread is basically aimed at people who are in the process of writing or have designed or written something, or are (as Chris is) looking to build further upon previous works.

    Make sense?
