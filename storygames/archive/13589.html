\n Tonight, we did character creation for Diaspora. If you don't know, Diaspora is FATE-based and a bit like Traveller. It has a five-stage character creation system, in which you narrate each phase of your character's life, until you arrive at the present day.

So, at the start, we joked around about Traveller. Simon said he wanted his character to level up. I said something about dying during character creation.

Now, often, I like to take a joke and see if it works in practice. After all, jokes are as good a source of inspiration as any. Sometimes it works and sometimes it doesn't (c.f. the time I narrated a Deck of Many Things into Polaris).

So, during character creation, I decided my character was Level Two. This meant a Level Two crew member (Level Ones are unskilled, you see, whereas Level Twos have skills).

Also, I narrated my character dying. In the third phase of character creation, during which there's meant to be a conflict, my character died in a spaceship crash. Her corpse was taken somewhere and incinerated. I didn't really know where this was going. Perhaps, I thought, I could be rebuilt as an android or become some form of technological ghost.

However, Simon suggested I could be the ship's computer. So that's what happened. My character, formerly a dead smuggler, was reconstructed as an artificial intelligence and now controls our ship.

(She still smuggles stuff.)
