    \n I like the pointy swooshes framing the eye.

    The central details are all too small to read. Scale this down to use in any other context and they become mere texture. The "&" doesn't read as an "&" at all. Either ditch it or make it WAY larger.

    The Fear Flame typeface is okay if you're going for slightly creepy / distressed, but if that's your aesthetic, you should apply it to the central image elements too (e.g. make those pointy swooshes with rough lines rather than smooth ones).

    Those are my first thoughts.

    If you wanted a less technical reaction: some of the elements have some potential, but it ain't there yet.

    \n I agree with David on all counts. The &-sign is particularly an issue; either remove the round pupil element and draw a rounder & sign that in itself strikes a balance between "pupil" and "&-sign", or rethink the whole idea. The way it is, you might as well remove the & for all the difference it makes and not have a conjunction at all. (That's a serious option, too - nothing says that your logo needs to scan word for word the same as the title.)

    The eye design is striking, but I might just like it better if you didn't try to put it in the middle of the title. Putting your crest element (what's the English designer-ese name for the picture element of a logo display?) on the side and arranging the logo next to it is a tried and true arrangement, for instance.

    Also, make that whole eye ensemble like 15% smaller, I bet it'll make for a tighter whole. That and distressing its lines to match the type face should help achieve an unitary look. Right now it looks pretty loose, just a bit less like a logo and more like a display of some sort.
