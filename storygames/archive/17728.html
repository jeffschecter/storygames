    \n I came to Story Games and the whole theory behind it this year or at least made a bigger afford to learn and engage it. So that is something new for me that most here would consider old.

    I also kept playing The Dark Eye through the year, always one of my favorite games and it has been for 17 years. Still new things to do and background to explore.
    I revisited Vampire: The Masquerade and remembered why I stopped playing it. Still a reminder of the good things in that game as well.
    The trappings of the Noir genre also were a theme in my activities this year, inspired by movies. I used them in A Dirty World (which is one of my new favorite systems) and for Call of Cthulhu. Not a new genre and look and I often return to it, having a game that does it well will encourage me to do it more.

    \n\nDecember 2012 \n edited December 2012
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n I'm currently in a contest where I'm supposed to write articles or rules for a traditional neofuturistic roleplaying game (it's a kind of crowd sourcing). To be able to write some addiction rules (which came out more like personality- and experience rules), I had to go through a bunch of already written game texts. And something happened. I normally dislike reading a game that longer than ten pages, but all the information that entered my head started to build something. Together with my written rules, pictures started to take form in my mind that showed me how it was to live in that world.

    I haven't read a fictional book for a decade, but I suddenly realised why I did start reading books in the first place.

    I still dislike reading more than ten pages for a game. I also believe that the pictures that entered my mind is just fluff and nothing that I really can build a scenario around. But the game text will probably make me go back to reading books, so I can experience my mind painting pictures once again out from written words.

    \n Under the theory that anything actually published is "old", I finally got to play Kagematsu just last weekend and was and am super excited about it. I could pretty much tell it was one of the best designs I'd read and playing bore that impression out.

    I want to see scores more designs with highly constrained scenes and goals that for all that are still full of wonder and leave you on the edge of your seat about the outcome. More secret information and non-symmetrical player role games that aren't strictly GM'd games too. While it's gotten its kudos, Kagematsu is one of several RPGS that I feel should be a hugely more influential game than it appears to be so far.

    Also not old in anything other than feel, I played a ton of Dungeon Crawl Classics this year and LUUURVE it.
