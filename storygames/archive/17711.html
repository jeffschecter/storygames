    \n Maitresinh, in response to your question on how Dungeon World and Tremulus differ from Apocalypse World, a lot of people have highlighted the focus of the fiction (naturally) as being different. For example, in Apocalypse World, you're following around capable, hot, bad asses. The focus is on the messy relationships between the characters and the scarcities that create friction among the PC's and the NPC's. In Dungeon World, your following around capable, adventurous, bad asses. The focus is on exploring the dungeon and surviving the encounters you have in that dungeon. The rules are tailored to each focus. The two different focuses seem to create very different experiences for different people.

    Check out Hexabolic's Dungeon Girls! It's a great rendition of World of Dungeons. If you read some of the latest posts, Hexabolic details some of his house rules - which I really really really like.

    \n I don't think it's productive to look for a 'core' or 'essence' of Apocalypse World or anything else. AW serves diverse users diversely, to paraphrase genre theory. Instead, think of AW and its various hacks and adaptations as an ongoing conversation between different systems and people, emphasizing different elements depending on the context.

    \n@Felan, that's an interesting point. TerrorForm (my current project) has some thematic and genre similarities to AW, but there are also some big differences. Those differences have me focusing on different parts of the rules to highlight the genre elements that I want to highlight. It's very cool that this set of rules allows that. Many different sets of rules are flexible, but I don't know that I've encountered one that's as flexible as AW.

    Still, I do think there is a "core" as it pertains to the construction of moves and the like. Yes, the genre will serve to highlight different aspects of the rules, but the rolling of dice, the cascading moves, all of that has to remain at the heart of what's going on or you're using a different set of rules altogether. Not a bad thing, just a thing.

    \nThe only AW-"powered" that i really know is Dungeon World.

    But before, just a bit of background.

    The fact is i've read AW about two years before it was released in french (just now). The very first time i heard about it, i just didn't saw the point. Then, i had a closer look to the playbooks and i immediatly saw how it work throught the moves. And it was really all that i was expecting, the kind of games who'd bring me back to RPG.

    i started talking about it with the future translator (who has already made a non official translation) during the Vieux Pots contest. We both agree the system had a potential for newbies and non (or formers) gamers, if it were in a box, with all the stugg ready to play. Finally, we ended working together on the boxed version of Dungeon World in french (http://fr.ulule.com/dungeon-world-vf/)

    So, from my point of view, the main difference between AW and DW is that DW has a more pre-established structure than AW (ie: something closer to a scenario), throught the Dungeon itself (places and what's there). In the french version, we've tried to give it a format close the fronts (ie: not a "literary" format as for a normal scenario)

    I've also had a close look on Tremulus before the ransom, thanks to Sean, and i think it have the same kind of alteration (i love the hazard track). I heard told about a metaplot system in monsterheart, but i don't know about it.
