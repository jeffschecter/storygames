Polaris Conflict Cards

DanielSolis
October 2006 edited October 2006 in Story Games
Have I mentioned how much I love having a "little ideas" forum? I'm a whole friggin' galaxy of little ideas. :P Anyhoo... So I'm terrible at remembering rules. Seriously. Take a look at some of my board games to see how bad I am at remembering them.

The conflict flowchart of Polaris is super-keen, but not exactly the most evocative thing to reference in the middle of a game. I had a little idea to help the flow of play: Conflict Cards. These are a small set of cards with the conflict phrases written on them. On the tops and bottoms are easily recognizable symbols. Each player has a full set of cards, with duplicates if necessary.

Let's say someone places the first conflict card. Now normally you'd check the flowchart to see which responses are available to you (or you'd know by rote, if you're a mentat). With conflict cards, all you have to do is check the symbol at the bottom of the card that was just put down on the table. Next, you check the symbols at the top of the cards you have in your hand. If the symbols match, you can use the card in your hand as a response. If they don't match, you can't.

Just to be super fancy about it, the cards can have cool illustrations like in the book, too. Yup!
