    \n I played at Gamed on Demand at GenCon last year. The storylines slipped between a beautiful young socialite, diving into a conspiracy, a hacker going on a VR quest to hunt down or confront an AI (the descriptions of the virtual realms I remember were celestial), and a working grunt out to make a buck, fighting for his life on the streets. I'm paraphrasing the plots, but that's what I took away from it.

    My favorite scene was mostly all dictated by a friend from NYC. Moment by moment narration brought us into the beautiful young woman's life as it changed irrevocably, and left us hanging waiting for more.

    I had a work shift elsewhere, so I didn't get to finish the game. It felt like parallel stories, all brief insights into the larger cyberpunk world, waiting to be interlocked through our demands and deals.
