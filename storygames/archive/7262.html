\n Hey there,

Ready to get your jeep on? Under my Skin is done!!! This is the sequel to Breaking the Ice and Shooting the Moon in the relationship game series I've been working on, Three Quick Games about the Human Heart. From a couple going on their first dates, to a love triangle, now we have a web of tangled hearts as a social circle fall in love with one another's partners and friends. Rob B. totally gets to make fun of me for finishing my girlie game before the more staid and serious Sign in Stranger.



The main version is a live action jeep-style game complete with a story structured in acts or stages; and intense, sexy situations and scenes where you get to whisper temptations (or admonitions) into someone's ear as they contemplate romantic betrayal. There is also a tabletop version in development included in the game. We'll do some final testing on that at GenCon I suspect.

To whet your appetite, I've put a sneak preview online at the Black & Green website. We'll have it at the Playcollective, Booth 2039 at GenCon in full-blooded color. And we'll definitely play UmS in the jeep form Games on Demand track.

See you there!

all the best,
Emily

    \n Hi Moreno!

    It is all but done. The system works and just needs some more playtesting to tweak the numbers and make sure to balance the dice. It's included in the game in a preview version as a short section in a chapter that gives other variations on the game such as different jeep techniques to use, and ways to play with no gm.

    The tabletop version is an addendum to the regular version of the game. You take all of the character creation and some of the scene structure and adding in a few more aspects to the characters, and a diced resolution system that makes you choose between respecting the lines of your committed relationship vs. getting what you desire with a new love. As it gets revised I will post the table top section of the rules in pdf for free on my website and incorporate any revisions into the next edition of the game. I suspect the changes will be minor, but if they amount to anything, I will put them into a small pamphlet format illustrated with Elizabeth Shoemaker's delightful photos. I am curious to see if people will want a separate pen'n'paper edition.
