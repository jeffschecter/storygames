\n We're playing Apocalypse World at the moment. One thing that particularly excites me is using real world locations and, also, the fact that I can look them up on Google Maps.

We decided to set the game in an encampment on Clapham Common. It's a market encampment, surrounded by corrugated iron fencing. Long Pond, which you can see on the map, is used to grow soy.

Once, the encampment was attacked by kids with AK-47s. Their base, it turned out, was in Wood Green. See that large building in the bottom right corner? That's actually Hollywood Green cinema, which, for our game, became the kids' headquarters.

At the end of the last game, the characters were in a helicopter, flying from Clapham Common to Cliveden, in Berkshire. Look at the route they'll fly: along the M4, then up the Thames. Cliveden itself is a beautiful stately home (you need to scroll up and to the left a bit to see it): see how it's easily defendable from the West, because of the Thames, but you're fucked from any other side?

I love real-world locations.

Graham
