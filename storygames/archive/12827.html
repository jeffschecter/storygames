\n One of our players didn't show up last night and we ended up playing a 3 player game of Universalis that went pretty well and I thought I'd share our tech:

- we used cosketch as a shared whiteboard. cosketch doesn't seem to work very well under IE, but it works under Firefox and mostly works with Chrome (except for the eraser - to erase text with Chrome I would click on the text with the text tool and then hit the delete key) So that's where we wrote all our components and traits.

- we used catchyourhare.com as our die roller, thanks Graham

The most useful gimmicks:
- Events are free, so you can freely narrate as much as you want. To balance the economy a little, we dropped to d8's for complications - just big enough to still make a profit on your complication.
- Instead of bidding for scenes, we took turns. Player A, Player B, Player C, repeat.
- We refreshed every three scenes, only 3 coins each. (I think this was a little low, but we survived.)
- We had a really mild "character ownership" gimmick - you could pay one coin for the fact that you own a character - so if someone tried to control it for you you'd have double-force power to challenge.

Things we never did, not because we ruled them away, but just because:
- created master / sub components
- bought extra dice for complications; we only bought traits

We got through a tenets phase and seven scenes in under two hours. And, when we ran out of time at the end, and wanted to go to bed, but were really only halfway through the story, one of the players just quickly summarized the rest of the plot, and the other two made amendments ("What about Harriet? We introduced her tesla coil experiment, aren't we going to use it to stop the aliens?" "Oh, sure, Dirk must walk into a transformer and get zapped and be freed from the alien influence, so she'll grab her tesla coil and zap all the infected until the invasion is neutralized.") until we were all happy.

What tech do y'all use to play remotely?
