I was going to write a review of this Rev Eng entry, but I didn't get round to it. Besides, this might start a discussion with Mark, who wrote the game, and I didn't want to clog up the main review thread with it.

So, I read through the game, and the concept was totally fantastic: the best I read in this competition. I love the idea of the vengeful clock and the switch, halfway through the game, where it becomes nightmarish.

Mark, could I ask some questions about it? As I understand it, before the nightmare stage, there's six attacks. In each attack, a scene is set, and the guests describe some actions; then the attack takes place and there might or might not be an Admission.

Firstly, it seems very short. There's only six attacks and they seem, pretty much, to be a mechanical thing. Is the expectation that setting the scene will take a while, with the guests describing what they're doing, in a sort of free play thing?

Secondly, it seems unevenly divided. If there's four players, two players will get one scene, while the other two will get two scenes. Is that right?

I'm not sure about the admissions. There's only one or two of them per player. I worry that it just lead to a couple of "revelations" about each character: for example, they poisoned their wife, they've always hated the way they look. It's OK, but I'm not sure it'll really build into a story: particularly as we only get one admission from each character before moving on to the next and there's only six in total. Have I misunderstood something?

I also had a thought about the end section. You say that the first character's punishment is the worst; the second's is slightly less bad; and so on. It seems to take the tension down, rather than up.

How would feel about switching it round: so the first character gets a nasty punishment, but then the next one is even worse, until the last punishment is indescribably hellish? Then there's one character left, who gets away unharmed.

Anyway, good luck with the game: it's a superb premise.

Graham

    Graham,

    Thank you for your kind words and your interest in the design!

    In your polite manner, you have hit all the places where I think the design is weak. On to your questions:

    1) I think the game will play quickly in practice, and as written I think it will play out very much like a casual card game instead of a "true" role-playing game. My design path focussed on the endgame, mechanics for getting there, and then I sort of handwaved the in-between stuff. Fred Hicks noted this in his review as well. The game is rather "flat" right now, and could use some way of putting a direction to the "free play" portions.

    2) Yes, it is unevenly divided. But there are auctions for the rights to attack, so the players getting the extra attacks will have to pay for them with cards.

    3) I think you've nailed the way the admissions will probably play out. The game is going to be short as presently written. I had initially thought that maybe the game could actually be played on the clock, and that it would take an hour to play. I left myself with no playtesting time, so I didn't know whether that would work.

    4) Your idea for the end section is so obviously right I have nothing to add. Brilliant.

    Unfortunately, I think the game will be an orphan for a while, since I have plenty on my plate in Real Life as well as a separate WIP to give some love to. Thanks again for commenting on it!
