    Character generation is easy as hell. I cannot imagine it bogging down.

    I'm not sure if there is actaully verbiage covering this, but I'd suggest agreeing in advance on some color. Last time we played I brought a big list of Norse names and said "Norse feel, lusers." But of course it will evolve in play.

    Once you have your oracle fragments, it will all fall together in an exciting way, because each of the fragments bleeds story. Just consciously tie them together and don't be afraid to let one character fulfil multiple conditions if that seems cool - the old fur trapper can also be the warrior queen's father. Make sure everybody is throwing in ideas.

    Personally I'd be more concerned about resolving conflicts, which I still can't completely internalize. Play out a few yourself and try to get confident with that.

    And don't forget to add people to the owe list, and to use your owes.

    Try to play more than a single episode, because it gets way better after characters have some history.
