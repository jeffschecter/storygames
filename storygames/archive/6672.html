\n So something put thoughts of good ol' Wraith: the Oblivion in my head the other day (specifically an episode of Pseudopod featuring a story by Rich Dansky). I liked that game. A lot. It probably wasn't quite my favorite of the Old WoD, but it was up there, and I was disappointed when it went away. I was shocked enough (though pleased) at an nWoD version of Changeling, and I don't really expect to see similar treatment of Wraith anytime soon.

Besides, I'm mature enough these days to admit that what I liked about it wasn't the GAME part of the game. It was the setting, the theme, the mood. The fact that the PCs are ghosts for fuck's sake! oWoD mechanics were pretty crap. the new stuff is better, i think, but even if i wanted to mess around trying to do some kind of conversion... well, that sounds like more trouble than it's worth.

so i thought i'd ask the good, smart, crazy people over here on SG what system they would use to run a Wraith-style game.
and as i was thinking that, i thought...

Maybe Polaris?
As long as everyone is willing to play a game where it is a given that all the PCs will eventually succumb to Oblivion, it seems like this could work, and possibly quite well. the system is certainly flexible in terms of powers and abilities and all that crap. it leaves things fluid and focused on the story, which is where a game based around a bunch of dead people should probably be, if you ask me. plus, one of the trickier but also more interesting parts about Wraith was the Shadow. No problems there if you do it through Polaris's system. The Mistaken can handle that role just fine. In fact, the Mistaken player role probably could/should be renamed to the Shadow in that case.

Anyway, I'm curious to hear what anyone else thinks. Think the Polaris idea is crazy-talk? If so, or even if not, what system would work well for doing a Wraith-y game?

    \n One of the problems I had with Wraith (and it's been forever since I've read it so if I have this wrong please correct me) is that there was basically only one way the game could end: Oblivion. So in addition to succumbing to Oblivion (probably the equivalent of the Demon outcome) I would want some other out, some other kind of conclusion. I don't know what though.

    \n Maybe it was just me, but I always saw Wraith as the most genuinely hopeful and concretely human of the oWoD games. Sure, the world of the Shadowlands sucked, but it was a second chance for you to cope with the issues of your life, and character creation pushed you inexorably towards picking those issues, and Transcendence awaited if you could resolve them. That fundamental hope at the core of Wraith makes limiting character resolution to Spectre-dom and Oblivion seem an unsatisfying adaptation.

    Of course if you just liked the doom and gloom and hating yourself, that works fine.

    \n also, re: Transcendence v. Oblivion...
    it's only as players, from outside the game, that you can know Transcendence is a good thing, or Oblivion a bad thing, or that the two are at all different. the Wraiths have no way of knowing, just like real, living humans have no way to know conclusively what lies beyond death. we have our ideas and out beliefs, but we can't really know until we get there.
    So i guess to a certain extent, if you did Wraith via Polaris, there's no problem saying that the end of the Wraith's story can be with Transcendence. As a player you can describe it that way, but the Wraith doesn't have any way to know that it's going to be what he hopes it is. In the same way, a player might decide that when his Knight dies at the end of a Polaris story he goes off to some kind of heaven or paradise

    A more general thought: a game like this probably should take place at the end of the oWoD Wraith metaplot, when Stygia is being attacked by spectres? this would allow the game to keep the same them of a collapsing civilization that Polaris has.
