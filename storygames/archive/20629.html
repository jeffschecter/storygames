    \n If your game has an interesting setting start by selling it. If the interesting part is what it does, describe it using more emotions, as in the emotions it will produce in the players. Any reference you can use to give us an idea of how game plays like is welcome. It's also important to mention what the players will be doing in the game. Your pitch isn't too bad actually, you just need to add a couple of things.

    For example, using your description above and rearranging it to sound more convincing, it could be: "X game is a tabletop rpg with fluid gameplay focused on giving an action movie feel, Kill Bill style, without massive rules and yet with the right amount of complexity. Players would be pushing their luck to be able to achieve N goals, which will put everyone at each other throats at the climax of the game. Friendship, Honor or Ambition, which will prevail?"

    That's my two cents, after that , I would also like to know where to go and how to get attention; I've got and idea of where and how in my country, but not on the internet.

    \n Not speaking as a moderator, but as always, never listen to JDCorley about anything.

    My suggestion is to playtest for other people with no expectation of reciprocity. Basically embrace mutualism, make friends, ideally meet people face to face and play with them, and the people you connect with become your pool of playtesters over time. That playtesting for others sharpens your own design skills is an added bonus.

    Don't "sell it" or "pitch it", build a community of people who know and trust you.
