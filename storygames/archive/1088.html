Card Sharps and Blunts

MikeRM
August 2006 edited August 2006 in Story Games
So yesterday I was having a huge one-person brainstorm about ways to generate random selections out of playing card suits. I came up with this odd one, which I'm not planning on using, but I thought I'd toss it out there in case anyone else wants it.

It uses the appearance of the symbols on the cards, and pits "sharps" against "blunts". A "sharp" is a pointy bit, like the top of the spade, the bottom of the heart, or all four sides of the diamond. A "blunt" is a rounded bit, like the bottom left and right of the spade, the top left and right of the heart, or the three lobes of the club. So diamonds have 4 sharps, spades and hearts both have a sharp and two blunts, and clubs have three blunts. The overall ratio of sharps to blunts in an entire pack of cards is 6:7.

I have no idea what you'd do with this, but anyone who can think of something is very welcome to it.
