\n Hi! I'm about to start up a "publisher" along with 2 other friends. We're from Argentina, but our idea is to broaden our readership. So, the plan is to start publishing both in Spanish and English, and that's why we need some help from English native speakers. We want a name that sounds interesting to speakers of both languages, and we've been considering these 4 alternatives:
(they are all related to the name of a blog we are running, "runas explosivas")
--RX
--Runa
--Runica (spelled RUH-nih-cah)
--Futhark

Which one sounds better or more interesting to you?

    \n Of these, Runica is the one that sounds the most appealing. RX is an english abbreviation for a prescription, medication prescribed for a doctor (I don't know how you get from there to RX, it just is). Runa is short and ambiguously close to Luna; Futhark sounds like a cat hacking up a hairball. Runica on the other hand is something I could picture being like Runica Publishing, Runica LLC, Runica 2nd Edition.

    \n I suspect that "runika" might look funny and flawed to some European multilingual people, as it combines a Scandinavian concept with a Latinate word suffix and Scandinavian spelling - sort of lots going on there. Same issue that American "heavy metal umlaut" gets when introduced in countries that actually use umlauts in regular writing.

    That being said, "Runik" might be more balanced in this regard; it's an English adjective written in a way suggestive of Nordic influence. Less ambiguous. Might be too Nordic for the Argentinians - but then again, Khimus has apparently been considering "Futhark", too

    I personally like "Runica", though - it's a reasonable combination of Southern and Northern European influences, and doesn't sound linguistically weird.
