\n So "Fantasy F*cking Vietnam" has always been an expression that has fascinated me. It's typical connontation is tied to old-skool dungeon crawling where PC's can expect to inch forth through trap filled dungeons. But I wanted to flip the expression a bit - what would a setting look like that was slightly more on the "Vietnam War" side of the equation, and a smidgen less fantasy? I'm talking about enchanted Flak jackets and M16's, Dwarf tunnel rats, Elven rainbow-streamed napalm dropped from above onto triple canopy jungles teeming with orcs. Whats going on here? How does it look? How should I combine these two elements? What is this universe? and what system should I use? (OSR? something simple right?)

    \n Hmm, my first question when I saw this was: what is the magic level? It's one thing for the wizards to zeppelin over a target zone to bombard it with meteor swarms. Quite another for casters to be more utility focused, less godly in their casting repertoire. Setting wise, I think it would play out quite well using a World of Warcraft type setting. Somewhat steampunk with gnomes piloting gyrocopters to drop the PCs off in a hotzone. At least, that's the first thing that came to my mind. I'll think some more on it and get back to you. Those were first impressions.
