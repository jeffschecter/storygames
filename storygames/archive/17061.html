    \n Once I was going to try running my persistent gaming bugbear: Pathfinder. About once a year, I decide this is the year I'll learn to love 3rd edition D&D, and since I got a lot of pathfinder stuff from various people for free, I usually use that. So, we spent a few days making characters, and we started and we got to the first combat with goblins in the dungeon. Basically, people moved into position, and then just rolled dice at each other and nothing was happening I was really bored. So I took fifteen minutes to print out my Dungeon World stuff, everybody made their character in five minutes, and then we were off.

    One of the things I did in that session was when one of the characters failed a roll to cast a spell, I turned them into a toad. When they tried to fix it, they turned into a duck. I treated this more as a minor inconvience than anything else, but I like how moves let spells have crazy misfire effects without a table.
