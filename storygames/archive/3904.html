\n Hi,

I've been developing unWritten, my game, in relative secret for the past few years now. Mostly cause I'm shy and I suck at the whole online/blog/forum thing. sorry. Anyway, I'm at a point where I need some outside playtesters for the game. You can get it here...

latest playtest [edit]

... I'm in the process of re-editing it myself right now, but its good to go for playtesting!

unWritten
1) unWritten is a gmless, settingless game that walks the players through setting, premise, and character creation. It heavily relies on player collaboration.

2) The mechanics are a variant of Otherkind (by Vincent Baker) with a miniscule pinch of Shock!

3) The players have 2 roles, the setting player (rotating GM) and the acting player (which can include the setting players character.)

4) Scene framing and the types of conflicts one pushes is determined by the use of the Hero's Journey or the 3 act structure (but the 3 act structure isn't yet ready. so it isn't in the playtest.)

I've already playtested it several times with great results, my group has thoroughly enjoyed it. Additionally, Joshua BishopRoby, Judson Nestor, and Mark Vallianatos have also played it, apparently with great success. I haven't heard back from Josh or Judson yet, but Mark has given me some feedback that I'm dealing with now. But, the changes aren't in the current playtest just yet.

I'll be playtesting it with another group next Sunday (the 18th?) and I'll be running it 3 times during Gateway in LA.

The main thing I'm concerned about right now is the fact that, I'm not sure if what my group is doing is actually in the text, though like I said, JBR, JN, and MV had fun with it...

So, I'd appreciate any help I can get.

Thanks Alex
