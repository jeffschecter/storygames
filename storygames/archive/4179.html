\n After digesting the great feedback from Jason's playtest, I have some fixes in mind for some aspects of Psi Run. What do you think of these ideas?
\n
\n
We all really wanted some mechanical adversity! Outside of narrative there was no meaning to the arrival of the chasers.
\n
\n
\nOkay. Chase: 4-6 Chasers do not move / 1-3 Chasers move one scene closer. If the Chasers catch up to the Runners, then Chase is replaced by Capture: 5-6 No Runners captured / 3-4 one Runner captured (GM's choice) / 1-2 All Runners captured. Any Captured Runner(s) replace Capture with Gone: 4-6 Character is still around / 1-3 Character is gone (for some reason no longer in play, but the character can have a Crossroads scene)
\n
\n
What about injury \xe2\u20ac\u201c also color, short of death?
\n
\n
\n1) Take a penalty die (drop the highest die) for the duration of the injury.
\n
\n2) Converting long-term and permanent injuries ("Scars") into disappointing elements in the Crossroads scene; making the Scar somehow frustrate a character's goals in the epilogue.
\n
\nI think this would ramp things up. Let me know!
\n-Chris

    \n Hey Chris,
    \n
    \nI'm not completely familiar with the whole of PSI-run's mechanics, so this idea may not even work. I thought of using something similar to the damage tracks found in the original version of The Mountain Witch. I think this track takes away some of the 'just color' of injuries. It uses Vincent's rule found over on the playtest thread:
    \n
    \n
    5. For dice, we rolled an extra die and dropped the lowest. Being hurt meant you lost that spare die. I forgot all about the "you get bonus dice for inserting yourself into others' memories" rule. Being hurt again never happened, but if it had we'd roll an extra die and drop the highest.
    \n
    \nSAFETY Category:
    \n5-6-Unharmed
    \n4----Flesh Wound - Take 1 Hurt. This injury automatically heals at the end of the scene.
    \n3----Conditional Injury - Take 1 Hurt. This is Hurt is conditional, meaning the damage remains until some special action is taken to heal it.
    \n2----Permanent Injury - Take 1 Hurt. This injury cannot be healed, it remains for the duration of the game.
    \n1----Taken Out - The character is taken out of the adventure. This could mean death, but the character could also be simply incapacitated, or captured.
    \n
    \nIf a character is taken out but lives he may, after at least 1 whole scene, roll 3d6 on the Goal Category to recover:
    \n
    \n3 Successes - The character recovers 100%.
    \n2 Successes, 1 Failure - The character recovers, suffering only a Flesh Wound for his troubles.
    \n1 Success, 2 Failures - The character recovers, but suffers some Permanent Injury.
    \n3 Failures - The character dies and is removed completely from the game.
    \n
    \nI'm guessing with this track Hurts would be cumulative some how.
    \nNo Hurts, roll an extra die, drop lowest.
    \n1 Hurt, lose the extra die.
    \n2 Hurts, roll an extra die, drop highest.
    \n3 Hurts, the character suffers a Conditional Injury (unconscious, must be resuscitated) or maybe Taken Out (incapacitated in some way).
    \n
    \nWell, just an idea.
    \n-Michael
