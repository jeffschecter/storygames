\n First, to explain... The following happened at the Pirate Jenny booth at GenCon:

Some Dude: (deadpan) So, where are your pirate games?
Me: Umm... we... don't have any.
Some Dude: (annoyed) Dammit. I just came from Privateer Press, and they don't have any either!
Me: Uh, okay... [halfhearted pitch on our booth]
Some Dude: Well, actually, I do roleplay and I'm looking for games I can play with my wife.
Danielle: !!! [starts pitching Kagematsu]
Some Dude: -- [interrupts Danielle] Nope. Japan.
Me and Danielle: ???
Some Dude: Well, I uh. Kind of overdid it. So now she won't play anything with Japan.
Danielle: [tries to explain gendered mechanics]
Some Dude: No. She won't play it. [picks up With Great Power] What's this?
Me and Danielle: ... (seriously, most literal game cover ever)
Me: [elevator pitch for WGP...]
Some Dude: Nope. Japan.
Me and Danielle: ?!?!?!?
Some Dude: My wife says superheroes are set in Japan.
Me: Okay, sir. Please let us know if you have any more questions.
Me and Danielle: [start conversations with other customers.


So afterwards, we started laughing our asses off when we were coming up with other indie games to dismiss as "Nope. Japan!". We came up with:

Ganakagok: it's on an island - Japan!
Dogs: samurai of the desert - Japan!
Mountain Witch: Duh.
Bliss Stage: Japan.
Ben Lehman: Japan.

So, S-G. What other games can we arbitrarily connect to Japan and thus dismiss from the indie gaming catalog forever?
