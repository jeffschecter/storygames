    \n I'd been thinking of grabbing the Klingon ( the John M. Ford style ones, not later canonical ones) adventures and simply using them, openly, as the situation starter, a bit like Malcolm Craig had suggested doing with an Open-style Cold City game.

    Basically, the players are fully aware of the secrets and so on, but the characters are not. Part of what the players do is then try to mix things up so those cross-purposes and secrets clash.

    Someone else had also suggested the Triangle campaign, since it seems to be a sort of intergalactic neutral zone.

    Anyway, I was thinking of doing that because the players can then start from pre-made characters and situation, then the GM acts more like a simple enablor/mediator rather than the more ful-scale GM of Yore. I also wanted to do these as one-shots rather than a campaign, so speeding up play is a big deal for what I had in mind.

    \n That sounds like a great plan. Many of the FASA adventures I've seen have loads of backstory and setup stuff and its a real burden to try and shoehorn it into play in a GMful way - using them as collective tools sounds like a great way to get value out of the awesome ideas in these adventures but do it on better terms.

    My idea of using the Keep for PTA was driven by a different but complementary idea - that the Keep is a legendary-in-the-hobby setting with heaps of living beings in physical relationships but no agendas of any kind, anywhere. (Okay, with one or two exceptions if you read between the lines.) PTA on the other hand gives all agendas, all the time. Seems like a perfect fit like no other D&D adventure setting/indie game that I can think of.

    Posted By: ArpieThat sounds like a great idea, and a great way to re-ignite some interest in those old modules, some of which were pretty fun.
    \nHonestly, it really has mostly to do with having little time, and not really wanting to get involved with a campaign straight off, plus being likely to try to recruit a play group of folks I may not know all that well.

    In that sort of context, throwing everything out in the open, and then building from that makes a good fit, even if it isn't very traditional. Heck, even if the module has "events which must occur", you can, as a group, look at that more like the direction things might have gone until the players ( and sometimes their characters) started mucking with it.

    Alternately, I suppose you might decide to shove things in that direction anyway; a sort of group agreement to follow the rails to a point, before veering off.

    If it does work out, though, you're right, it would be a good way to re-purpose those old modules. I actually think it might work better for non-site modules like the trek stuff, or even some of the old pulpy stuff.

    Hell, might even work for some of those old WW modules. Just let the players pick amongst the NPCs, rather than having them try to wedge in appropriate characters with limited info. Besides, who wouldn't want to play vampire-Al Capone?
