\n Hey all, RinCon 2010 is coming up sooner than you think, on October 8-10 at the Tucson Convention Center.

Last year we had a blast, RPGs were huge, this year it's going to be even huger!

Registration is open and it's only $20 if you register before August 25.

I am going to be the RPG director - the game signup database is not live yet, but we hope it will be soon.

Here's the schedule for gaming slots:

Fri 2-6
Fri 6-10
Fri 10-2

Sat 10-2
Sat 2-6
Sat 6-10
Sat 10-2

Sun 10-2
Sun 2-6

I can already tell you that on Sunday from 10-noon, we are having our in-convention GM's Conference, which was really well-attended last year and very good.

Last year we had RIDICULOUS amounts of overflow at our two Call of Cthulhu events, so I'm hoping to have a "Lovecraftian table" with Call of Cthulhu, Trail of Cthulhu, Penny For My Thoughts' Cthulhu-esque scenario, and so on.

Last year we had great success with a simultaneous three-table Primetime Adventures scenario, with audience members "changing channels" by walking from table to table, I want to do this again, maybe even with more tables than three.

I would love to have an indie games track (I normally almost do this myself every year), I would love to see at least 1 table going at all times.

I want to have a "Play With The Creator" track, if you're a creator and you're coming, let's talk about this.

I don't have any RPG-type games on demand, but we do have a boardgame/cardgame library, so there will be space for that if you want to do a Games on Demand segment. Post in this thread or send me an e-mail if you want to.

If you GM an event that actually happens, you get at least 1 RinCoin, each is worth $5 at any vendor in the Con, or which can be redeemed for materials at our prize table. As an enthusiast GM I have never gone to a RinCon and not left with a net profit. Yes, that's right. We will pay you, the GM (or game organizer, for GM-less games), to come and run games at RinCon.

Post questions here, whisper me with ideas here or shoot me an e-mail at jasondcorley at yahoo dot com. (I will have a RinCon address soon for official stuff, but it seems silly for me to worry about that with you folk.)

We have a smoking deal with the hotel, they are really cool to us, so if you're coming, you can get a good deal. If you can't afford even the smoking deal at the hotel, e-mail me, we'll find something.

See you there!

    \n SHIT JUST GOT REAL - (tm) LeoGeo2, pwnage.tv

    Okay, so before I give the BIG FUCKING NEWS, let me explain what an amazing, smoking deal RinCon is for GMs already:

    Each game slot, as you can see, is 4 hours long. To us, that means you, the GM, are a volunteer providing 4 hours of programming. Thus you are entitled to 2 RinCoins for each game session that actually happens. What is a RinCoin? Well, it's a little plastic coin that can be redeemed at any vendor at the con for $5. Yes, that's right, if you preregister for RinCon ($20), and run 2, count them, 2 RPGs, your attendance will be completely comped. Not a discount, not a "special GM's rate", fuck that, you will be comped into the con like a fucking VIP. If you run more than 2 sessions, you will walk away from RinCon with a profit.

    And furthermore, RinCoins can be used to purchase books at our prize table. Last year I walked away with a $40 White Wolf book for 2 RinCoins, so they can be worth even more than five dollars.

    But that's not all, that's not good enough for me, because I'm not going to rest until we have the best RPG setup ever. I've set up three "tracks" for game experiences that are going to be featured at RinCon:

    * Play with the Creator. This includes not just what it did last year, but, if I can swing it, will also including playing a game with the creator also playing, not organizing or running the game.

    * Lovecraftiana. Chaosium has been a big supporter of RinCon, and as I noted above, last year, we had a ridiculous overflow of Call of Cthulhu players. This table won't exclusively be Call of Cthulhu, though, I intend to run a Cthulhutech game, I want someone to do the Penny Cthulhu scenario, and also Trail of Cthulhu and maybe even Pokethulu! Anything even remotely Lovecraftian will work at this table.

    * Classic Gaming. That's from 1977-1992, the first 15 years of the hobby. Dig out those crates and fish around in the shed, show these young kids how it was done back in the day.

    If you run a game on any of these tracks (and I'm working on adding more tracks, these are the ones I'm confident I can fill tables with), you will get three RinCoins instead of two. Oh my god, do you know how fucking insane that is? Run a Call of Cthulhu scenario and you're almost all the way comped. More than comped if you get a good prize at the prize table!

    This is completely crazy. Take advantage of this before they realize I'm giving away the farm this year!

    Also, game registration/creation should be up and running on the website soon.
