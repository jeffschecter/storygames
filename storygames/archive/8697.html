\n In Prep
1\tTalk about what the group will look like within the setting. What kind of characters will they be? What kind of relationships will they have?
2\tYou get 6 points on a skill list. Assign them.
3\tTalk about where/when you met/ will meet each other character. Not ever "the whole group" at once.
4\tYou get 4 more points for the skill list.
5\tTalk about your character's goals.
6\tYou get 2 points for each other player. Assign them to a freeform trait, for that character. That is, you are deciding for that character what his next 2 points are spent in, with no artificial limits from the skill list. It stays on your character sheet, not theirs.

In Play
*\tWhen you use skills, you roll white dice. When someone else uses the trait you assigned them, you may give them 2 black dice.
*\tBlack dice are mechanically more potent than white dice.
*\tRather than boosting stats, the reward mechanism could force another player to rewrite their trait for you, but having no influence on what they can write.

\tWhat I'm thinking of is a game where instead of "guess the GM" being an emergent thing, "guess the group" is a mechanically enforced/encouraged thing.
\tThe gradual release of skill points is supposed to be a thing that encourages a meshed feeling between the players.
\tThe fact that you assign skills to your self, but traits to others is intended to get you more heavily invested in the other guy's dude, rather than your own, maybe.
\tThe potency of trait dice is, you know, obviously meant to encourage giving the other players what they want.
\tThe reward mechanism idea is included so that you have some control over changing things when an idea is getting stale.

Just a little idea. Is there something like this already?

    \n I love how in threads like this almost invariably someone says "in the game I'm currently working on..."

    Anyway, in the game I'm currently working on, Fiasco, you build initial relationships as part of play, and extrapolate characters from those relationships. So when you sit down to play, you have no idea who your guy will be, but after building relationships, you have to figure it out collaboratively. This reminds me of that, a bit. In my experience it's a great way to develop a cohesive feeling and a sense of shared characters and elements. Anything that gives me some influence over your guy is going to push in this direction, I think.

    \n It's definitely an interesting start to what I think is the next big collaborative thing I'm going to try out with my group. After having family drama plots fall on their face for campaign after campaign, I did a game where everyone was invested in the same family drama from day one moment one and it has taken off like a fucking rocket. Sure, in action games everyone needs a niche that is theirs and theirs alone, but few in my group are engaged enough audience members to care about anything that doesn't have something at stake directly for them.

Why suddenly throw them away and have people faced with a wide open vista? At that point in character creation you should have a much narrower focus than you did at the start.

    I wanted characters made of traits, but having the traits be in the hands of other players. Players with a good idea of the character you wanted to play, but not necessarily giving it to you precisely the way you want. The chargen is mostly trad, minus the traits part. The gameplay could even be mostly trad too, but the mechanical bonus of doing "the awesome" that your friends request might encourage that kind of play in a manner that suggesting it in the sidebars won't.

    \n The advantage of skill lists is that you get to work out what sorts of things the characters will be doing, sometimes prior to character creation even starting. If I tell you "this is a game about courtly samurai, the skill list is poetry, flower arranging, cutting people's heads off with swords, and translating barbaric languages", that gives everyone a very clear picture of what's going on in the game.

    If you then say "oh and you can just add in whatever skills you want", then people go "wait wut is going on in this gaem".
