\n I'm looking for moral dilemmas that can be used as a plot hook in story games or RPGs. I find them the most promising seeds for developing a story. And I love that they force the PC to make a choice with consequences, thus challenging them to choose what the character really believes in.

The obvious example would be for example the super-hero who can either save his girl-friend or a bus full of school-children.

Here are some things I ideas that come to my mind:
General categories
- Protect family/stay home VS save world/go on mission
- Corruption (money/safety) VS integrity (risk/threat)
- Idealism (do the right thing, be yourself) VS pragmatism (get rich, benefit)
- Uncomfortable truth VS comforting (tormenting?) lie
Job offers
- Accept new job & relocate vs stay with partner/family
- idealistic dream job vs boring job with great pay
- Tempting job offer with some shady/grey areas
Relationship
- Sexy affair or loyal to wife?
- Admit to partner infidelity or lie
- Enter opportunistic relationship (for money, influence, position...) without love
- Cancel romantic holiday for important business trip
Terrible secret
- Do you really want to know the terrible truth? (ignorance is bliss)
- Your best friend/family member has committed a crime. Do go to the police?
- Do you endure torture to keep a secret (and friends/family/allies safe)?
- Do you sell a secret for the right price?
Things we do for power
- Are you willing to kill someone?
- Are you willing to betray your family/friends/allies?
- Are you willing to loose face in public?
- Are you willing to change (physical or sanity) forever?

See also on TV Tropes (moral dilemma):
- Sadistic Choice: Having to choose between two immoral actions.
- If You Kill Him, You Will Be Just Like Him: Having to choose between killing a villain or sparing him.
- To Be Lawful or Good: Having to choose between the law and personal morals.
- Friend or Idol Decision: Having to choose between someone you care about and your mission.
- Genocide Dilemma: Having to choose between destroying the evil enemy race or letting them wreak havoc.

    \n I think you're restricting yourself if you see these as pairs that go together. Almost any of these can be paired up in dilemmas:

    Corruption vs. losing face
    Following the law vs. important business trip

    Etc. Basically, take any two things the character cares about and find a way to force them to choose between them. Or take two things they really don't want to happen. As an aid to finding things most peaople care about (besides the things written on their sheet or established in the fiction), I find that the five spheres of morality offer a good guide: http://www.nytimes.com/2008/01/13/magazine/13Psychology-t.html?_r=2&pagewanted=print

    Simon: I really like the NY Times text with the "five spheres of morality": harm, fairness, community (or group loyalty), authority and purity .

    I also remembered the Hillfolk (DramaSystem) Dramatic Poles. They work here pretty well.

    Another thing I remember are the Apocalypse World PC-NPC-PC triangles. Basically, you introduce an NPC that brings two players (or their goals/ambitions/desires) into conflict.

    I guess another approach could be:
    What could be to find mutually exlusive character drives/desires/motivations/goals/values.
    Then, it would be interesting at why are these goals exclusive? Possible reasons:
    - Geographical/time limitation (can only be at one place at the same time)
    - Limitation of resources (can only have one thing)
    - Doing one thing destoys the other
    - Contradicting values

    \n Question: are you looking for a list of dilemmas for the purposes of running a game with your players? Or are you looking for a list because you need it for some kind of game design goal?

    I ask because I would approach each differently. For the purposes of running a game I would crib off something like a personality test for a character. Let the players tell you what's important. Then put those things at odds.

    For design purposes however, I think it's a bit trickier. I quite like the list you already have, but I've been wracking my brain thinking, "there's gotta be a better way than listing it all out." What you need is a dilemma engine.

    OK so here's me going out on a big limb trying to give an answer that encapsulates both intents...
    What do we as people care about? Nouns! We care about people (some, probably not all). We care about things (from iPads to the more abstract; more later). And we care about places (where you met your SO, where you went to karate class, the city/town/house you love). Last, but not least, we need to take a look at 'things' more closely. I think it would be worth it to split the category across the material and abstract. The latter covers stuff like philosophies, beliefs, personal codes of honor etc. So, why sketch out the obvious?

    I present to you NiC- Nouns in Conflict:

    Noun 1 is in Conflict with Noun 2 because of Monkey Wrench

    For Nouns:
    Roll d4: 1- person(s) 2- Place 3-Thing (Material) 4-Thing (Abstract)

    For Monkey Wrench, Pick 1:
    Limitations - Beyond your scope/out of your control; Not enough (finite resources/time); too much (large distances, widespread panic/disease)
    Change - Into something new/different
    Violence - General destructive forces; Nouns perpetrating against one another or being set upon by external forces
    Uncertainty - fear; unclear effect; shift in (ethical, social, cultural, or physical) topography

    Things (Abstract):
    Cultural - Ethics, traditions, demands
    Personal - Ethics, vulnerabilities/insecurities, pride [accomplishments, things you like about you], goals/desires
    Societal - Norms/contracts, standing, laws/principles

    Hopefully this will be useful to you for the purposes of generating lists for design and lists for actual play. I recognize this is both monstrous and vague, but I'm hoping the NiC system will help spark some thought as you try to resolve why the Nouns are in Conflict.
