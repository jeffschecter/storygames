    \n I doubt it really needs another thread. Basically, although Jason and I have been playing TSOY weekly for more than a year, we've managed to drift a number of rules. The way Harm should work is that there are six boxes and any kind of harm (Vigor, Instinct, or Reason) can fill one of those boxes. You should check off the box and make a note of the associated pool, so you heal it with pool points later. If you have a 2 (vigor) Harm already, and you take a 2 (Reason) Harm, it becomes a 3 (Reason) Harm instead.

    What we were doing was tracking Harm by Pool type, so you had a column for each. You could be beaten to hell in Vigor, but still up for a good Reason fight. In the previous example, you'd have a 2 Harm in both your Vigor and Reason columns, and still have a long way to go to be broken. It's a lot more complicated and draws out BDtP more than it should. I'm amazed it took me this long to figure it out- but then the rules are pretty casual in places, so it's not hard to misinterpret them.
