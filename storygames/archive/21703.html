\nEdit: They were more open to using a custom xp curve. That's a good solution if the entire campaign is going to be for two, but it doesn't handle drop in drop out as well as the following does:

So this is the scaling idea that my players rejected. So fwiw it might be a bad idea.
Thanks to Scarlet Heroes that prompted me to think of the idea of changing the rules rather than changing the modules. But my solution is very different from theirs.

The idea is that for each player you are short a nice party of four, you add one shadow. This is perfect for when you have two players, then they have a shadow each.

For each shadow a PC has, it means:

You get an extra full turn per round. If you're using rolled initiative, roll an extra dice. If you do "tapping" inititative, you get an extra card. Etc.

You get an extra pool of HP. Whenever you take damage or are healed, you choose which pool gets hurt or healed. Each pool has its own death saves. One pool might be 0 & dying for example and then it gets a healing word and the death saves for that pool is cleared.
You have one of the pools and your shadow has one of the pools. So you'll say "I take that damage on my shadow" or "I'll take that damage myself". And for things that can group heal, or group hand out temp HP, the shadow can be one of the targets and you can be another. If either pool dies, does not matter which, you lose a shadow and you get the remaining pool. If either pool is unconscious, you only get one turn per round (hello death spiral) since you don't have a shadow.

The turns per rounds is still you. You get grappled, then you are grappled. Your shadow isn't running around separately from you. The shadow isn't an extra character. The shadow is only making you faster (more turns) & more durable (more HP). Diegetically, the shadow means that fate is smiling on you for a while.

The shadow eats XP. Every shadow in the party saps away a full share. So if there are two players and two shadows, and the party gets 100xp, that means each of the two PCs get 25xp each. The shadow doesn't have its own stats. It has the same max hp as the PC it's shadowing.

    But it's also stronger than a full extra character. If you take damage from a fireball, it's not you and the shadow both taking damage, it's one of them. (This works differently than healing.) This is also a knob tweakable in playtesting. Because I haven't tested this at all yet.
