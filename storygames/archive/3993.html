\n Hi all, in need a little bit o'help.
\n
\nStarting late September I want to GM a Call of Cthulhu campaign. I love the Delta Green fiction and like DG rulebooks, although the obsession with technicalities around secret agencies (and subagencies, and departments, and guns etc...) was putting me off a bit (so I was more and more inclined to GM a 1927 Arkham game). Plus I don't like the BRP system and therefore decided to settle down with d20, although that would not be my optimal system pick - but I guess I am too lazy for a more demanding conversion of something else and I'm not sure FATE or PTA would handle it well. (Though I got very enthusiastic about some of the ideas for CoC and handling sanity, floating around on this forum and the Forge and I think they could be implemented in a d20 game.)
\n
\nEnter Cold City. I encountered the mention of it on this forum recently again and although I am still not impressed much with the preview of the basic rulebook, the preview version of the Cold City Companion got me going. Now, before I buy it I would love to hear opinions on the following:
\n
\n- Is it suitable for a Mythos-themed game? If yes, is it suitable for Delta Green without a lot of fuss?
\n- Can it be used to model CoC sanity, or at least some effects of insanity, as part of its mechanics?
\n
\nThank you very much,
\nMichal

    \n To the first question, yes. Cold City should do a good job as a CoC/DG system.

    For the second, all conflicts can leave you with consequences on a character. This mechanic could easily be used to model the sanity death spiral, although as presented it is more general.

    Fair warning: I haven't actually run Cold City yet, but I did just reread my copy the other day.

    \n I think the game would suffer if the competing entities do not have huge and irrevocably different characters and agendas, as different as the Cold War US and Soviet Union. On a player level, the differences between these nationalities are easy to grasp intuitively based on stereotypes we carry anyway. So in re-working them, I'd make the differences really enormous.

    The claustrophobia of post-war Berlin as a setting is also a little thematic - if your game is free-wheeling, you'll lose this. Sort of up to you as to whether that's important, but I really like it.

    \n Chipping in to this discussion really late (travelling around with a rucksack really does have its disadvantages at times!), I'd say that the advice so far has been pretty solid. Cold City does rely fairly heavily on the environment of Berlin during the Cold War. However, ou could very easily have agency agendas as opposed to the blanket 'national agendas'. And, to be fair, this was always the intention. National agendas can be handed down to the character from a government, an agency, a military goup, etc. As long as they in some way represents the interests of that national group.

    As for other places? I'm unsure about that one. As Jason said, the environment of Berlin in 1950 is very much part of the game as a whole. There are other locations I could think of transferring it to, although they aren't actually during the Cold War. Constantinople in the post-WW1 period was divided by the Allies and full of competing factions (the British, French, immigrant White Russians fleeing the Revolution, Turkish Nationalists and so forth). Cold City may work very well in that environment. Another Cold War setting would have to have an equally strong level of input from all sides in the conflict.
