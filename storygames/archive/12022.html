    \n Well, one of the greatest evening geek hangouts that I know of is Ground Control, in downtown Portland off of Burnside, in the Chinatown area. I can't remember the exact address, but it's on 4th and Couch street. Bar + Classic Arcade games = Win. (Actually pretty close to the downtown train station so make sure to pick up a street map).

    Also, just up the street from there is one of the largest, privately owned bookstores in the U.S., Powell's Books (11th and Burnside). Many, many floors of wonderful reading, and long hours. I am able to go there in the morning, and not surface again until they close, and still be fantastically entertained. And they do have a gaming section (mostly standard RPGs and some card games, but there are plenty of gems in the mix, as well as a lot of old-school games). And their Sci-Fi/Fantasy section is heaven.

    \n Nothing in Portland is more delicious to nerd out on than food carts.

    For breakfast: waffles at Moxie Rx on N Mississippi.

    For lunch: khao man gai at Nong's Khao Man Gai, SW 10th and Alder, coffee at Spella Caffe at 9th and Alder.

    For dinner: poutine at Potato Champion, SE 12th and Hawthorne. Or pizza at Pyro Pizza, same location. Or both, and a fried pie from Whiffies for dessert.

    Speaking of dessert, Voodoo Doughnut is rapidly overtaking Powell's as Portland's best known tourist attraction.

    Lastly, when at Ground Kontrol, go east on Couch for a block and visit the 24 Hour Church of Elvis.

    \n It's to my shame that I've lived in Portland for almost a year but haven't been to one food cart.

    It's not nerdy, but go to the Horse Brass Pub and get a 20oz of Fred Strong and some Bangers & Mash. Or that sausage-with-a-hardboiled-egg-inside thing. mmm. It's my favorite bar in Portland.

    Dark Horse (10956 Southeast Main Street Milwaukie, OR 97222-7644) and Oni Press (1305 SE Martin Luther King Blvd., Ste. A Portland, OR 97214) are in Portland.

    \n PLEASE PLEASE go to Floating World Comics, they are awesome and are just down the street from Ground Kontrol. You could do Powell's, Ground Kontrol, Floating World Comics, and Voodoo Donuts in the same 2-3 hours as they are all close to each other.

    MOVIE MADNESS! It's the best video store I've ever been to. It's in SE on Belmont. The selection is astounding and one can geek out on all the movie memorabilia.

    One would be remiss to not go to a theatre-pub to see a movie for three bucks and have a beer and pizza. I recommend the Bagdad on Hawthorne or the Kennedy School in NE. As a bonus, the Kennedy School is just an awesome place to go to for food or just looking at the weird paintings.

    There's a Black Velvet museum, I can't remember exactly where, maybe on Powell in SE.

    You can do the touristy thing and do a tour of the Shanghai Tunnels. I've never done it myself.

    If you're a coffee geek, well there's Northwest Coffeehouse in NW, and Crema in SE. TONS others of course, but those are my favorites. Chance of Rain on Hawthorne has a nice staff and vibe if you want to chill a bit.

    For music, there's tons of places, but I like The Doug Fir and the Crystal Ballroom. One of my good friends runs The Parlour in SE if you're looking for a smaller place. Mention me, and you can probably get in cheaper? I don't know.

    Ah hell, I miss that place.
