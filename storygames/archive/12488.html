\n Just thinking out loud...

What would it take to organize a global game weekend? An Asynchronous convention, but held on locally-synchronous weekend. 7 PM, wherever you are, or all weekend meeting up virtually with friends across oceans.

I'm thinking across multiple channels, so you'd have IRC/Skype/distributed games, as well as local meetups and house cons. Maybe some of the house cons could arrange audio/video so that there was a non-local seat at one table, with a player Skyping in or whatever. Like a robot head sitting there.

I know this has been done on a small scale. What if we ramped it up?

When would you do this? What time during the year would you be most likely to get ready availability? What would make this fun and interesting? How could you tie it all together?

    \n Thanks for your thoughts, guys. I know people who regularly have a remote player (the robot head), and it seems to work fine, time differences aside (they play trans-Atlantic). I've played with a robot head GM and it worked well.

    I guess what I'm imagining is a regular, normal old house con, but with parallel events all over the world on the same weekend. Game locally, face to face, in your own language. If anybody wants to hook up via robot head, that's totally great, but that wouldn't be the focus of the global event. Internet-based games (a la iCon) would also be happening that weekend. Maybe there would be a dedicated forum for coordinating, match-making, and sharing stories. There could also be some light programming, like "everybody try to run a session of Danger Patrol called Atomic Queen of the Uranian Blast Pits" or something.

    Again, I'm just daydreaming at this point. Assuming it were to happen, given the shape of theyear, when would make sense to do something like this? Not in the summer, obviously.
