Sell me on Contenders

Gaerik
December 2006 edited December 2006 in Story Games
There's been quite the buzz going around about this game since GenCon. I don't get the appeal of the game and want to give people the chance to explain it to me. I'm having the same reaction to Contenders that I originally had to Dogs in the Vineyard. The subject matter just doesn't thrill me. I'm not a huge boxing or westerns fan. However, I am now a fan of DitV and I'd like to be interested in Contenders... but I'm not at this point. So, given that boxing doesn't really interest me that much (I don't dislike it... I'm just ambivalent), why should I go buy Contenders? Sell me on it. Please.
  • Jason_Morningstar
    December 2006
    I love Contenders and I think boxing is both stupid and boring.

    The cool thing about Contenders is that it distills the drama of competition very nicely - it's a sweaty, brutal, tactical fight that you can really sink your teeth into every time, and that is leavened by interstitial scenes that make the fighting matter. So you've got premise to address, you've got these sharp, gamey fights with moves and tactics to agonize over, and it's extremely light and the handling time is low. Every decision is important, and it's very easy to pick up and play.

    I know what you mean - boxing is a big big turn-off for me as well. But Contenders turned out to be among the three best playing experiences I've had - ever. And I've played great, great sessions of Sorcerer, PTA, TSOY and Dogs. It's about boxing, yes, but then again, it isn't. It's about stories about people. Good stories. Great stories. No prep. No GM. No hassle.

    Why? Firstly, the game works big time, just like when you find the perfect tool for a task. It works. As one of the other players in my group, Joe Murphy, said after a couple of sessions: "This game helps make art happen". It does. Contenders is a tweak on My Life With Master, which may be just as great a game, but unfortunately I haven't played MLWM - yet.

    Actual play: [Contenders] This Must Be The Place

    If you're human, I can't see how Contenders can fail.
