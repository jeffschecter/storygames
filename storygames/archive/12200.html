    Anyway, Lucca started as a comic-only fair. And, at first, it was about serious, cultural, appreciation of classic (old) comics, conferences, really high-brow. (and sometimes, when I can't even BREATHE in the middle of 50.000 visitors, I really miss these times... sigh!)

    \n Speaking in all seriousness, Lucca does seem like an intriguing event - and an amazing city of course; I've only ever read about the renaissance communes of Italy, it would no doubt be well and proper to view these grounds by my own eyes. Closest to a pilgrimage that a humanist gets, I imagine, when you consider that the place is just a hop and a skip away from Florence. Definitely a serious target for leisure travel.

    So yeah, I guess one really can't argue with A.C. and The Morningstar on this matter. I'm personally still figuring out the purposes and framework of my international conventioneering, though, so adding Lucca on a short notice for this year isn't really feasible. Now that I know more about the event and have it in my calendar, though, it'll be easy to plan for it in 2011 assuming this Spiel thing goes well and I decide to start treating European conventions in general as a place to be. I'm not a very travel-prone person by nature, so this whole idea of going to the other side of the continent for a convention is something I likely wouldn't consider if I didn't have this whole publishing endeavour going. Not that I don't enjoy playing games with new people and seeing the sights, but a starving designer has to look at the bottom line, too.

    I sure sound defensive, but then I have some reason to be: I'm usually the first in line when it comes to claiming that European roleplayers should look more beyond the borders and work towards a more international scene in general. It's nice to see that the Italians don't need any hand-holding in this regard
