\n One of the neat things in Empire of Dust is the Banter mechanic: as a combat action, you can narrate a piece of dialogue, possibly earning yourself some useful combat resources. How does this work in play? What are some other games that do this sort of thing?

I'm looking for an appropriate but unobtrusive D&D 4E hack to add a Banter mechanic without breaking the existing subsystems, so suggestions there are nice too.

    \n In Spirit of the Century, and presumably other FATE games, you can use a social skill to do a Maneuver instead of attacking. This can result in your opponent getting an Aspect, such as 'Flustered' or 'Shamed', that you or another player can use on another action to get a bonus to your attack. It's pretty open ended.

    I think the basic question you have to answer is whether Banter is something a player does instead of attacking, or something they can do in addition to their main action. In the first case, you need to have the results of the Banter be fairly powerful to compensate for losing your attack. In the latter case, you probably want some other limiting mechanism so that people don't feel the need to Banter each and every turn. I'm not that familiar with 4e; maybe Banter replaces the Move action or something of the sort?

    \n I have done this sort of thing of a Mutants and Masterminds game, and Savage Worlds.

    It is simple in a d20 game, we just rolled 2 d10's to resolve a conflict. Every time someone went beyond " I attack him" with their description they earned an extra d10 to roll, and could more and more with added descriptors. They could even earn them after the roll to save up for the next time. They would would then take the two highest die to resolve. They could also hold them back to earn Hero Points, usually about three dice.
    You can also make skill checks on opponent, or stunt a power on the fly, to place various status effects on an opponent.

    In SW they could just earn additional Wild Dice.
    You can also do tricks in SW.

    \n Another method you might consider is the (Jest?) skill from Reign:
    \n
    \nThe player gives the GM a list of one-liners that NPCs could use. The GM tries (but is not obligated) to work those into the session. Then I know these lines are probably coming sometime in the session and I have a witty reply prepared.
    \n
    \nFor example, one of my prepped lines is "Well I never...!" The GM will try to make some NPC say that. And then I've had time to think about my riposte. "And with hair like that, you never will!" or "Maybe you should, honey-- It's good for the skin!" or whatever.
    \n
    \nYou can tie you rewards to that system. Something to think about, anyway. Cheers!
