\n When I read about a new game, sometimes I get this feeling for how cool it's going to be to play. I feel how the systems might interact, I get the sense that something will emerge, something new and wonderful. This time, it's going to be just right. Just perfect, and new, and interesting, and the way roleplaying should be.

Sometimes I buy the game. Sometimes I read it. Sometimes I play it. Occasionally, something like that dream emerges; not necessarily because of the game.

Sometimes, back in the day, I'd spend years chasing that dream. Last time I did it was a six-session campaign of Burning Empires a year or two ago, because I did believe that it would be magic if we managed to play it just right.

I think this dream is one of the things that keeps the hobby/art going. We want to believe, and we do believe, and we're not really all that disappointed when this game doesn't deliver, because there's always the next game, which will be better. Maybe even perfect, for real, this time.

    \n I still dream!

    Less about the perfect game, more about specific techniques or experiences.

    I'm always looking for games:

    - with better communication tools that help everyone get on the same page
    - that do what a great GM does without the GM
    - that teaches you how to roleplay well without lots of experience
    - whose rules teach you how to play without first reading the entire book
    - that emulate specific super hero stories
    - that emulate specific cyberpunk stories
    - that emulates my favorite TV shows (like the Wire)
    - that can be played in 30 minutes or 1 hour or 2 hours
    - that can be played online with similar engagement as person to person games
    - that emulate specific TV or Movie dramatic rules
    - that don't require prep yet don't feel like everything is made up in the moment with little weight
    - that can be played with 1 or 100 people

    But I don't need 1 game to do all these things. And some games do them but it's always exciting when new games improve on these techniques and goals.

    I don't believe in the single perfect game though. I think it's because I play with so many different people who want so many different things.

    And in general I enjoy games more when I go in with no expectations.

    But I still dream!

    \n I have played in perfect games.

    They are once-in-a-lifetime alchemy where the right players in the right mood are provoked exactly right by the ruleset and magic happens.

    You can't tell when they're coming and you can't replicate them. In fact, trying to replicate them can be depressing. All you can do is play lots of great games with lots of great people and wait for the stars to align.
