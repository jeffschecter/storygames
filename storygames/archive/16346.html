    \n It's tricky, because when you're selling a nerd shirt, you want to make your money back. You need to sell ALL THE SHIRTS so you need to pick a color that's more or less easy for anyone to wear and like. Black is the easiest shirt to do this with. Red, blue, yellow, green, etc are all awesome colors, but it's way trickier to sell a shirt in a single color vs selling one in a non-color like black. Also, in a weird way, colored shirts can be non-inclusive because it's like, okay, awesome, your INSERT GAME TITLE HERE shirt is in a pale mustard. With my skin tone? Not happening. Ever.

    Posted By: skinnyghostIt's tricky, because when you're selling a nerd shirt, you want to make your money back. You need to sell ALL THE SHIRTS so you need to pick a color that's more or less easy for anyone to wear and like. Black is the easiest shirt to do this with. Red, blue, yellow, green, etc are all awesome colors, but it's way trickier to sell a shirt in a single color vs selling one in a non-color like black. Also, in a weird way, colored shirts can be non-inclusive because it's like, okay, awesome, your INSERT GAME TITLE HERE shirt is in a pale mustard. With my skin tone? Not happening. Ever.
    \nThere are lots of ways to do shirts, and some are better at covering your costs than others. Spreadshirt (or the like) gives you a lot of flexibility in terms of sizes and colors, but they're expensive. We've been working with a local T-shirt artist which cuts our costs in half, letting us sell the shirts for less than we could on Spreadshirt. The trick is knowing ahead of time roughly how many shirts of each size you need, so you don't end up with stock you can't sell. That's where a pre-order or Kickstarter comes in. If you pick a design (and ink color) that works with a lot of shirt colors, buyers can pick whatever shirt color and size they want.
