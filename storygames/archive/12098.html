    \nIt's an hack of "My Life With Master" where the Master is the Egomaniac Girl From Hell. At INC'10 the players decided to go REALLY unsafe and played themselves as her "minions"...

    This image says it all...



    Ah, at the end of the second "the best thing" Clip, Ron is screaming "Parpuzio!" It's a word used our forum for the kind of role-playing where "the GM is God" and the rules are only "suggestions" that he can ignore at will (disregarding even roll results). There were a lot of in-jokes about Parpuzio at the Con (the t-shirt with "Parpuzio marks you" with a Pictures of Ezio's tattoo was one of them) and Ron found it very funny

    \n
    \n Hi,
    \nI'm Alessandro Piroddi... but online I go by the name of "Hasimir".
    \nNice to meet ya all
    \n
    \n@ Jesse
    \n@ Noclue
    \nThe game I was testing is named Beyond All Reason and it's a veeery early draft of mine ... it seems to kinda work (at least it hasn't blown up in my face, yet ^_^) but it's still in the very first stages of development.
    \n
    \nIf I had to describe it in just a few words I'll say that it's kinda like Montsegur meets Bacchanal meets Hellraiser
    \nIt's a game meant to conjure the desperate run through one hellish night, where your Protagonist tryes to go Beyond All Reason to reach dawn and see his dearest hope come true.
    \nOnly one will succeed, all the others will meet nefarious destinies.
    \n
    \nI wanted it to be very toy-ous ... very boardgame-y in it's layout and components.
    \nSo you have the sephirotic three of life that is used to keep track of the 5 phases of the game (from Malkuth to Kether), and that influences each phase in a different way depending on which Sephira the players go.
    \nThey can also draw on darker powers and access the corresponding Qlippoth, with very bad long term consequences.
    \n
    \nIt also uses Major Arcana tarot cards to both establish a sort of "initiative order" (based on the tarot number, like Fool=0 and World=21), and as part of the actual game to establish evocative NPCs.
    \n
    \nIf anyone is interested I'll go in more details, but perhaps in a different topik
    \nComments and suggestions are VERY welcome, and I'm also going to say THANK YOU to Frederik for the very useful chat

    Posted By: MatthijsYes, that's just f'ing crazy. What the hell happened to make Italy such a centre of indieness?
    \nWell, for InterNosCon the merit is of Claudia Cangini and Michele Gelli of Narrattiva, that every year organize the convention, and were the first (at least here) to think to organize a convention where good food and good wine (and nice locations) were integral to the gaming experience.

    Italy really isn't a real "center of indieness". Indie games arrived in Italy only 2-3 years ago (the first ones were published by Claudia and Michele, too) and were greeted with a lot of hostility from "traditional gamers" and a lot of polemics, and still sell a lot less than in the USA. But from the beginning our indie gaming forum, www.gentechegioca.it was dedicated to making people meet in "real life" to play. So maybe because of the shorter distance between Italian cities, there are a lot of "micro-conventions" that people organize at their home or in private places. So people did know each other and got better at playing (the improvement of the quality of play from the last InternosCon to this one was incredible...) and more secure about trying to organize games of their own.

    Maybe the philosophy could be compressed into: "first: play better, and enjoy playing: you will not need to search for players".

    InterNosCon is really organized, too: there is no place for people who go to "take a look", you have to register not only for the three-day convention, but for every scheduled game. It's not a convention to sell manuals or to proselytize, it's a convention for people who want to play some specific games using the time in the most efficient way. This means, for example, that knowing already in advance who will play with you in a specific slot, you can organize the game in the best possible way. For my "Annalise" game Sunday morning, for example, I was able to organize with the players a different, earlier starting time, we already did the character by forum and we already did play the first phase of the game - where every player simply describe a scene - by forum too. (if you can read Italian, this is the thread with the first phase and this is the actual play thread) So at the Hotel we were ready to start the second phase without having to worry about doing all that or explaining rules. My other games were organized in the same way: we already created the characters both for 1001 Nights and Dogs in the Vineyard (and that gave me the luxury to be able to create a city tailored to these specific characters)
