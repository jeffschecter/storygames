    \n Some suggestions:

    1. D&D. Or "Totemic D&D", if you like. Just use as little as you can get away with and make up a story.

    2. Panty Explosion. Best of all, you don't have to refer to it by name.

    3. Tri-Stat. Since you don't have an Attributes list, just don't use any. Allocate 20 points among Body, Mind, and Soul. Make up some skills if you want to and hand out some skill points; about 10 ought to do it. Attack rolls are based on your related Stat+skill. Defense rolls are the same, minus 2. Forget about Health points and just narrate the outcomes.

    4. Theatrix.

    -Grant

    \n I threw out some options and let them choose. They chose to play a game of mine I call "Winter Crucible", which is a sort of cross between the Pool and TSoY.

    It was a fun game, although it went completely off... well, there were no tracks to depart from, but there wasn't...

    Well, the setup for the game involves Keys and a tangled web of relationships. So, from the get-go, the characters were at each other's throats and the whole thing was a mess (a sticky, fun mess, mind you), with violence, double-crossing, etc. I think it was mostly because it was and we were tired.

    Nevertheless, it was a good evening, and I consider it a successful first playtest of Winter Crucible. Indeed, you don't have to try too hard with Keys: I needn't have worried. A bunch of character with a few Keys each and the game ran itself. I'm looking forward to a more serious game using a similar ruleset--it could be a lot of fun.

    Thanks for all the advice!
