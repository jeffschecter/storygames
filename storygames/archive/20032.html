\n Hi, I'm looking for some help critiquing this player's sheet. The rules in the hub and spokes are organized by topic, but I've found that when I'm in play, especially as a new player, I need to know what decisions and actions I can take at that given moment. So, I've condensed the rules and reorganized them according to time and from the perspective of the player. This way, when you're resolving a test, you can look through the guide to find the point that you're at, and any information about artha to spend, exceptions to account for, or just basic steps to follow will be right there. Hopefully this will make it easier to grok the system as a whole. It was definitely helpful for me.

However, I'm sure I've made some errors in there, so I'd like some more experienced eyes to look it over. The sentences in red are ones that I'm especially unsure about.

I'm also open to feedback on other information I should add. The sheet assumes that you've read the actual book, so it doesn't bother with definitions and things like that.

    \n Yeah, after a second look, I agree with CarpeGuitarrem. I have a lot of experience teaching BW, including to people new to RPGs (my one-on-one campaign with a new player lasted around 50 sessions!), and I have to say this is simply Too Much. In particular, you should really separate out the GM-facing stuff from the player-facing. Also, you need to introduce the more advanced levels later or on a separate sheet. I've actually had some success just telling people what to mark for advancement for the first session or two, and then getting into detail about how advancement works when they get curious.

    What players really need to know is: what base pool to roll, if they get any bonuses from FoRKing, Help, or simply good fictional positioning, and if they're spending any Artha either before or after the roll. Though I do like that you tell them their job is to fight for their Beliefs\u2014that's important!!! On the other hand, it's also good to tell them, hey, you know, it's also okay if mid-session you realize that the price is too high for one of your Beliefs and you end up betraying it. You get rewarded the same either way.

    Setting the Ob and Failure consequences, handing out Artha, even going over emotional attributes and Steel\u2014that's all really for the GM to do.
