Notes Around A Game Written For My Almost Five-Year Old

Blue
February 2006 edited February 2006 in Story Games
My son, The Indigo Kid, is typically awake when our regular PTA group gets together and has repeatedly expressed an interest in playing. For that, and several other reasons, I have been trying to come up with a game to play with him. Below, I have some extended brainstorming around such a game. I would appreciate any thoughts or suggestions anyone has. For background, some of the design considerations are encouraging skills (math, art, storytelling), teaching consequences, and shifting his mindset from 'heroes fight' to 'heroes help' (and sometimes they fight).

Quick Kid in the Virtual World

The world is defined collaboratively by:
(1) Drawing the map. Whenever the character moves to a new location in the world, the players work collaboratively to define that location and to add it graphically to the map.

(2) Picking the Allies and Opposition. Mechanics (Proposed):
(1) A conflict resolution roll pits the total of one or more dice plus modifiers against one or more dice plus modifiers. The highest total wins. Note that a conflict or an NPC can be defeated without having all their stones depleted.

(2) Each side starts with one die. They may add an additional die for each stone they remove from their character sheet (in the case of PCs) or from their pool (in the case of NPCs).

(3) PCs will potentially have stones in each cell of their sigil. I propose having seven stones distributed through the cells.
