\n I'm really enjoying reading through Paul Czege's very interesting game Thy Vernal Chieftans, and I got this really inspiring idea - to take a pic of the items I'm planning to use for tokens when I play the game. It found it pretty fascinating that I had several of the items Paul calls for in the text - or at least reasonable symbolic substitutes.
\n
\nInstead of a chunk of silver I'm using a silver ring etched with Celtic knotwork. My clear quartz is not smooth and polished but it is still cool. The small bone comes from an old Lakota choker I once had. The bloodstone is a nice green one with just enough red in it to provide the symbolic resonance the game calls for. I do not have any dark pearls, so as a substitute I'm using a hematite cabochon because I think it has a very similar vibe. I have several pieces of sodalite, but the one in the pic is the only one that has any green in it. All my others are very blue. I have no bits of polished black walnut, and I haven't chosen a substitute for that token yet. I'm still thinking of something that is appropriate to use. I might end up using either a bit of driftwood or maybe a small chunk of Connemara marble. In any case, there's nothing in the pic to represent that.
\n
\nSo, I'm sure there are other folks excited about the game who are already going around scrounging for things to use as tokens. If you are, take a pic of yours and post it in this thread. I'd love to see what other folks are coming up with.

    So I suppose I need to confess that my own set of special tokens is also a work in progress? (And your set is more complete than mine!)



    My own magic token is also a ring. Danielle and I got promise rings for each other a year or so before we got engaged. They're sterling silver and darkly cut with the word pietas.

    My food token is a resin sheep knuckle, stained to look like bone. It's part of a set of five I bought from MacGregor Historic Games a few years ago.

    And regarding your substitutions, I think driftwood is an excellent alternate token for remembrance. Another good one might be a piece of fossilized wood.

    Also, I like your hematite, and damn if I don't know that a suitably large dark pearl is going to be a challenge to acquire, but I should say that secrets is symbolically represented by a pearl because it's an oyster's secret.
