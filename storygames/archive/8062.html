    \n I've tried playing Dogs where we hid our die pools from each other. Biggest changes were it took away a lot of the tension in escalating (less pressure since you don't know your situation), made reversing the blow much more frequent which made the players less willing to take fallout on purpose and made conflicts shorter, disconnected traits from the perception of potency (you don't know if the trait rolled was the trait used or how it affects play later). From an immersion perspective, for some of the players it made it easier for them to immerse. The dice were out of sight, out of mind. For others, they became more distracted wondering what they were dealing with. We also only played 1 game this way. Practice will likely generate different results over time.
