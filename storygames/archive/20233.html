\n Hi Story Gamers,

I'm running a bunch of games at London Anime and Gaming Con this weekend, and I wondered if any people here had any suggestion for games that I should take with me.

My criteria are:
-Fast: The games should be playable within an hour, including any setup.
-Free: I would like short, free games, ideally a page or two that I can print at home.
-Friendly: A lot of the people who come to the convention aren't roleplayers, and so the games need to be accessible to new players.

I'm going to be bringing Lasers & Feelings, Roll for Shoes, Everyone is John, Doctor Magnethands, and a bunch of things from the Technical Grimoire 200-word RPG challenge ( http://schirduans.com/david/2015/04/200-word-rpg-challenge.html ).

There'll be a few other things too, but the ones mentioned above give you an idea what I'm looking for.

If people want to discuss instead / as well:
What do you folks find makes a good, light, convention game?
Are there any games that look like they would work in that environment, but don't?
Do you think GMed or GMless games are equally at home in that situation?
What is your position on /strategy for prepping for convention games?

Thanks,
Purple

    \n Dang, I was gonna go with Lasers and Feelings, so I guess I'll just trot out John Harper's other darling and mention Lady Blackbird.

    Lasers and Feelings is perfect though; It's rules minimal - to the point where there's very little to learn and even less room for argument, but it gives you plenty of control over who your character is. It has 'enough' setting while still allowing everyone to really build up the setting on the fly.

    I think GM'd games work better for conventions, because having one person who really knows what they are doing and is confident in it is a huge asset in this environment.
