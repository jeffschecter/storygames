\n Hi folks, I thought I'd remove my promotion to the right sub forum.
\n
\nAs some of you might know, there is something of an indie movement in Germany. Most of the guys came across the forge sometime and bounced their ideas around in a german discussion forum now called [url=http:www.tanelorn.net]Tanelorn[/url]. And, inspired by Game Chef and other competitions, they made their own challenge.
\n
\nIn the 2006 Challenge, J\xf6rg D\xfcnne wrote a story game named Western City in a scant 72h. To me, it looks like the lovechild of Primetime Adventures, Universalis, WuShu and 7th Sea; which is funny because J\xf6rg was introduced to Universalis only after the fact. Western City didn't even make it to the finals in the challenge, but is still the only game from the challenges to reach publication. It has seen a lot of independent playtesting, and J\xf6rg managed to get a lot of people to give solid feedback, most of which made it back into the game.
\n
\nThe game has a prep phase, ritual phrases, is GM-less, scene framing rights are won by bidding coins, which you can also use to buy facts and additional mooks. This sounds like a bad pastiche, but in truth it's like Long Island Ice Tea: It sounds like too much of everything, but put together the right way, it makes for a rock solid, fun game.
\n
\nThe Western genre is underrepresented in role-playing games; apart from the american western, there's also the european spaghetti western, and unlike superhero games, it transcends geekiness.
\n
\nAs is said in the foreword of the game, you can use it to critically reflect upon the hard themes that are tied into the historical epoch of the genre \u2013 racism, slavery, genocide \u2013 but it is built to be played by using and possibly reinterpeting clich\xe9.
\n
\nAs Anunnaki pointed out in the other thread about RedBrick, Western City is close to release. I leave it up to him to come with art previews, but let me link to the game's Splash Page, where you can have a peek at the cover at least. I've seen some of the layout previews, and the awesome work of Kathy Schad gives that game a pretty - no, beautiful face.
\n
\nTanelorn now hosts the Western City Forum, which is purely in german. So, for the first time in history, it's the small group of german speakers who has direct access to an indie game before the english speaking audience does. Here you'll find actual play and playtest reports, and there's place for rules questions and stuff \u2013 not much there yet, but still something for an unreleased game.
\n
\nWestern City is not bound to the Western genre at all \u2013 this is just a shorthand used to get to play quicly. Adaptations discussed are Clancy City and one to model indiana jones-like stories. So, should you have a problem with the genre, you could still give the game a look.
\n
\n
\nI'm happy to answer questions to my best effort.

    \n
    \nThere are no the ritual phrases like in Polaris. You can see the veto as a key sentence.
    \n
    \nDirect from the book:
    \n[quote]
    \nVetoes
    \nIf a player does not a agree with what someone else is describing, he or she signals refusal by throwing a chip into the pot and speaking the key sentence:
    \n
    \n"Not today\u2014and not in my town!"
    \n
    \nThis means the player is calling on his right to veto. Ideally, players should now come up with some sort of agreement and then continue playing. However, if they cannot agree on a tradeoff, bidding is called for to settle the dispute and decide how the scene plays out. Once someone has successfully enforced his veto against some else's description, the vetoed scene or description is banned for the duration of the session and may not be re-introduced during this game session (not even in similar form).[/quote]

    \n"Welcome to Western City, Greenhorns!"
    \n
    \n\u201cThis town has everything you'd expect to find way out here in the West. It's the place to be if you wanna have yourselves some good ol\u2019 dirty fun\u2014our little sinkhole, as we cowboys say. Here we can blow off some steam after the long cattle trails and spend our hard earned dough. There\u2019s paved streets, whitewashed churches, arms dealers, undertakers, and some odd folk. But what's best is, there's saloons, casinos, and brothels aplenty.
    \nThe railroad connects uptown, and the trader\u2019s special offers have greenhorns as well as old hands flocking to this place. For a fistful of dollars, you can trade everything near the paddle steamer wharf down on Green River.
    \nYou'll find preachers, gamblers, loose women, desperados, and God knows what other riff-raff. No one knows how big this goddamned town is, exactly, and to tell the truth, no one cares enough to find out. \u2018Coz 'round here, we like to mind our own business\u2014that\u2019s tough sometimes, as our business is often robbing others of theirs. Beyond the town limits, you've got notorious Fort O'Hare, home to the formidable Seventh Cavalry. Bunch of brave boys they are; had to restore calm more than once here in the city and in the hinterlands.
    \nThat\u2019s where those darn Injun tribes live. All over the place, too, them pesky redskins. You have them Crows, them Pumas, them Elks, them Beavers, them Bisons, and a helluva lot of other smaller tribes even your own humble guide here doesn't know about. Always looking for trouble, them redskins; that much I knows for sure, though.
    \nBut big ol\u2019 Dark Forest here and the craggy Gold Mountains are just what those Indian rascals and all the other scalawags needin' for a hideout from the soldiers and the marshals\u2014not to mention them cussed Fever Swamps down south. Those swamps, boys, there's mosquitoes big as my thumb there. Suck a full-grown stock-bull dry in a single day\u2014just like that. No kiddin'!
    \nMy cousin Joe, he tells me about weather magic and redskins turning into animals. Honestly, more than one red we caught lit off without a trace. And it's not like we aced them, believe me; the sarge couldn't keep a secret between himself and a bottle of Dead Rat Whisky.
    \n
    \nSmell that? You'll find the rarest smells in the world here, with all them immigrant folk and their outlandish cookin'. Best food this side of the Great River here, they say. Crooked traders offer slaves, too, and over yonder, the Silver Inc. factories, that's a union stronghold right there. Accept it, pardners, there's nothing' you all might want out in the West, you won't find here or right by. How about you treat me another Whisky now, eh? Or I'll tell the Sheriff you been pissin' in his boots the other day!\u201d

    \n Let me add some details regarding the art preview. The extras 'sheet' is the size of a playing card, and the game includes a selection of extras to use directly, again reducing prep time (usually, people make up their own extras).
    \n
    \nThe 'character sheet' is letter sized \u2013 it has a list of this sessions' goals, the friends, enemies and acquaintances of this character, and notes on the left side. The right side describes the characters mechanical representation: Characters have their 'cave' \u2013 akin to a personal set in PtA, and a hubris which you can play against them. Then you have three attributes and up to five abilities and a hobby \u2013 these are self defined, but the game brings an extensive list of sample skills. You can have special items which give you a bonus on certain stuff (my professional poker player had his 'hand of cards' as a special item, which I explained as being a representation of the luck he has when playing), and on the bottom of the right side is a players 'heart', which tracks the effect of lost conflicts. I forgot how you regain heart, but I vaguely remember it involves going back to your cave and doing stuff (almost like refreshment scenes).

    \n Jason:
    \nYes, my decription comes off as racist. But if you see a typical Spagetti Western, the peopel will talk so. I didnt made the movies, i just take them as inspiration.
    \n
    \nBut i sense the problem and wrote an Introduction:
    \n
    \nIntroduction
    \n"This Game is aimed at creating the feeling of a typical Spaghetti Western, including stereotypes about hard men, real women and ethnic minorities. Indians are either noble savages or a threat, Chinese own laundries and Mexicans are either lazy or bandits. I do not assume these stereotypes to be real, they are part of the genre. Nothing in Western City prevents you from tackling a story about the actual situation of Native Americans in the West or from playing characters, that deliberately break away from the stereotypes. It`s a game, fiction not reality. When playing let your inspiration be the limit, not any real or imagined stereotype."
    \n
    \nTo the heart regaining:
    \n
    \nRegaining Heart
    \nAll heroes and extras involved in a scene regain two points of Heart after the scene is finished. Absent characters regain 4 points of Heart for each scene during which they are absent. Their Heart can never be higher than at the beginning of the game (that is 8 for heroes and 4 for extras). Special monsters and big baddies may have more Heart than that.
    \n
    \nHeroes and extras start each adventure with their Heart points fully replenished.
    \n
    \nDuring a fight:
    \n
    \nMake a patch on it and stop crying
    \n
    \nOnce per fight, players may replenish one point of Heart by first aid, downing a tincture, applying herbs to the wound, call down the support of the saints or Great Spirit, or another healing action. This does not take a whole sequence.
    \n
    \nA character with either the Doctor or Surgeon profession may also try rolling for Bandaging or similar skills, and thus heal Heart up to a maximum of half the damage caused by hits during one sequence.
    \n
    \nExample:
    \n
    \nAspirax got badly roughed up and then thrown into the horse trough by Knut's thugs. He only has 4 points of Heart left Alex describes how Aspirax dripping wet climbs out of the trough, wipes the blood from the corner of his mouth, and takes a hearty draft from his bottle of Dead Rat Whisky.
    \n
    \n
    \nThe whisky gains Aspirax back one point of Heart, and full of Dutch (or Irish, in this case) courage he returns to the brawl, breath reeking of cheap whisky.
