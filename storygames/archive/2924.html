\n So, I've been toying with retooling my big ol' game world and releasing it as setting material. My wife is especially interested in this, and I think it would be fun. She was going to do a lot of the creative work, and have me handle system. My own fantasy system is old and creaky, so I don't think I'll reuse it when there are so many other good systems out there for fantasy now.

I'd definitely want to do up Burning Wheel rules for it, and probably d20 as well (it's out there and it's huge) if 4e isn't out by then.

My question is this, should I do more? What other systems would be cool to see the document set up for? I was thinking Shadow of Yesterday, but there might be some more people think are good.

    \n I'll second DitV here, beyond my obviously biased recommendation of Fate v3. I've had really good success with it as a generic system - the biggest things you need to think about when adapting it to a certain genre or setting are:

    * defining the levels of escalation in conflict
    * fine-tuning the character creation archetypes and "initiation"
    * altering town creation to something more appropriate to the themes of your game

    Different sort of game hacking than usual, but well worth the effort, I think.

    \n What it is like is a pretty bog-standard fantasy world, albeit with absolute good and evil stripped out. It's pretty low fantasy, with the history of the world being that tyrant magicians were overthrown about 500 years ago, and pretty much all written knowledge people could get their hands on was destroyed. There are fewer creature theats, and far more people threats in the world, and the original system was pretty gritty and lethal.

    What I'd want to do in the setting material is add a lot of depth to the world, and make it a richer, more living setting. I'd depart from a lot of the cliched stuff I originally included, and the stuff I want to publish would take a closer look at a few of the interesting sections of the world. I think an up-close treatment will help bring it to life and avoid some of the standard fantasy novel boringness.

    I can elaborate more later if folks are interested.

    \n Yeah, a Mortal Coil refit would be cool.

    So settings are cool. Some of my favorite books are setting material. However, what I like to see is how does a story progress within the setting. What sorts of tales will come of it? What drives the game and plot along? This is my biggest struggle with Artesia and even basic BW (thus I incorporated The Book of Dooms into a narrative process). So my answer becomes, the system that does that for you while hitting all your settings thematic components.
