    \n I will be running a playtest of Acts of Evil. I've made a change to give players some room in scenes to discover and express their characters with less anxiety about mechanical consequences. This might be the last rule refinement I make to the game, but I need to see what players beyond my local playtest do with it. So I've put together a scenario with player characters in two Terrenes: the art community of the Impressionists in Paris in ~1878; and at Price Castle (a WalMart-esque store) during its escalating and somewhat surreal rivalry with G-Mart across the street.

    I will also be running a playtest of Marshall Burns' game, The Rustbelt, which I'm representing for him at The Ashcan Front. Marshall is putting together a crazy playtest scenario with a saxophonist, a singer he loves, a club owner who wants her too, the spoils of a jewel heist, and a couple of ne'er-do-wells with their own obligations and issues.

    I was thinking of just floating the games at the Embassy Suites in the evenings, but if you really need to play in one of them, get in touch.
