I've mentioned it a little bit here and there, and I thought some of you folks might be interested in my currently running Kickstarter: Belly of the Beast.

While it is designed with a bit more of a traditional bent, the theme focuses on the counterbalance of human ferality vs survival, and frames the characters' lives in a pretty terrible setting (they're literally swallowed by a world-eating monster).

As always I appreciate the help I've received here over the years and am happy to answer any questions. Thanks!

    \n I've had it go both ways in my playtest campaigns, and received similar feedback from external groups. The GM is there to try to coax out difficult situations which force the players into harder and harder predicaments. In order to gain the mechanical benefit of Instinct Dice, they have to behave instinctively (in turn making them more and more feral / base).

    So, I've seen several groups completely shatter after going down the greed rabbit hole for example, but I've also seen enormous amounts of self-sacrifice even for very small group benefits. I think it sort of crystallizes the survival dungeon delve / horror-adventure genre well.

    I feel like I'm not answering your question very well

    \n Closed-mindedness essentially - they're loyal to their clan or their stronghold or their friends even when it isn't beneficial to them. So it is more like intense focus / stubbornness rather than "succumbing." Edit, to expand: It has come up generally in play as some kind of prejudice.

    The most common is when scavengers have to try to negotiate with (or on behalf of) other groups, and one of the PCs can succumb to Loyalty and essentially uber-intimidate their opponents or bolster their allies with morale.
