    \n Shouldn't be--that's a fuck-up in imposition. Page creep (what hat's called) is supposed to be accounted for as folds (sets of 2-up, duplex pages) are imposed into signatures (bundles of pages that are later bound in order), so that when the pages are face cut (removing the outward curvature of a stack of folded sheets) there's no creep.

    Ask Lulu if they can confirm the "pages per signature" setting is consistent with the paper size that they are using. Odds are the printer "thinks" it's doing, say, 8 folds per signature when its really doing 16 (or whatever). Odd though... really shouldn't be possible to "break" that at the imposition stage of their process.
