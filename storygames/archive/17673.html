\n A couple of years back, Chris Chinn created the Same Page Tool. I recently discovered this and am quite excited about it - it seems like a really concise way to agree how you will play an rpg game or campaign.

I've taken Chris's original version, combined it with some stuff from the Milton Keynes RPG club version, and put it up here.

Thanks to Shiru Kuro on rpg.net for the last question, which of course is crucial (but usually not asked). That thread is here.

Have any of you used the SPT? What have the results been?

    \n My experiences so far are inconclusive but positive. In my BW and Vampire games, where I'm GM, it's provoked some discussion. In my Pathfinder game, where I'm a player, it's at least provoked some confusion. The Pathfinder GM was quite surprised when I produced it - he'd never seen anything like that before.

    We're still struggling to understand each other in Pathfinder - the problem is perhaps understanding the strength of my preferences (how extreme I am on the "must be player-driven" front). That's not something a sheet like this can get across, not if you've never heard of playing like that before.

    Maybe the most important role of the SPT is to open up discussion - just being at the table, it reminds people that the way we play the game is a thing and can be negotiated.
