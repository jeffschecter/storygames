    I'll join the chorus in terms of thinking that this should be more biting satire and less Benny Hill. And that part of that would be too yank the Mission Impossible/World of Darkness vibe.     The rummy resolution seems a little off, though. Part of it is the dual difficulties - the set the GM lays and the arbitrary difficulty number - and I wonder about why the GM is laying sets in the first place. As players we can't influence his hand at all, and it may get very difficult to out-play the GM.

    Hard and fast suggestions: The GM starts with his 6+players cards. When you come up to a challenge, draw a card blind from the GM's hand and lay your set face down. The GM can lay his set whenever he wants - earlier he has less of an idea about matching set to difficulty, but later he might not have the cards. Once everyone has laid their sets, there's a showdown. Right after revealing cards but just before resolving the situation, team spirit can come into play. Sets that beat the GM win.

    Perhaps a rule for single card sets - a penalty, perhaps? And special cases that allow further draws. Both of those notions are probably the result of recently learning Canasta.

    Ah, excellent question, George. The lesbians are a sort of mistrusted ally: a bit like the way the British Secret Service thinks about the CIA.

    Thank you very much for the feedback. I hardly know where to start.

    Joe, thanks for your post, it's made me think; Joshua, since you ask, I'm straight. Chris, I do appreciate your comments, although I don't really want to take the discussion down that route.

    The rummy suggestions are useful. I think I've made a mistake with having both a difficulty number and a hand for the GM. And I've given the GM too many cards. And Judson's right that I need a rule for single cards. I'll need to think again about that.

    What I'm really interested in, and undecided about, is whether to darken the humour. The original idea of the setting is that every gay or straight cliche is true: gay men really do recruit; straight men really can be turned gay after a couple of beers; you really can recognise gay men by looking at them. All this made a world so ridiculous that I felt it could only be treated as a cartoon.

    What would be the satire option? It's a society, similar to ours, where straights and gays are segregated; where straights aggressively defend their territory; gay men are probably ghettoised, if you want it really dark; and where gays react to the segregation by proving to the straights that they're not as straight as they think they are. If anyone does have any suggestions about how to do the satire angle, do let me have them, because I'm not sure about it.

    It's interesting. It would be a massive change in the game: I couldn't have groups called "Pink Fist" and "Rainbow Warriors".

    Perhaps there's a sort of Paranoia-esque or Judge Dredd-ish middle ground. That's the sort of territory I'd like to aim for. And there might also be a dial option too: the group decides whether it wants cartoon or satirical.

    Any suggestions are very welcome.

    Graham

South Park is a good model here: the way it tackles every offensive subject, in no depth whatsoever, portrays everything in stereotypes, and you can't get offended because it's disrespectful towards everyone.
