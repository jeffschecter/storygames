\n Sorry about the wide posting (I'm kinda spamming all Tenra-related sites). I need help making some English Manga recommendations, and so I'm asking around.

I'm basically looking for recommendations of manga to go with Tenra Bansho, so I was thinking "historical-ish", "preferably asian themed", etc. I can think of a hundred movie, anime or J-drama recs, but racking my brains on actual manga recs.

I'm looking for manga that fit these criteria:

* Available in English, in book form.
* Has awesome character drama and at least some action.
* Not titilating bullshit, nor a vehicle to express artist's panty/tit fetish (see "Bastard!!", "Tenjo Tenge" - A real shame on the latter, the manga might have been good otherwise)

From there, I'm looking for:

* Anything manga-ish. Doesn't have to be from Japan. Korean or Chinese Manwa is totally OK.
* Doesn't have to focus on historic Japan. Western history (Vinland Saga) or even contemporary (Eden) is fine, but the further it drifts from historical the less I can use it.

This is particularly difficult for me. I am a manga-eating monster. But all the manga I have or love to death are:
1) Comedy
2) Slice-of-life
3) Biographic (Saibara Rieko, Darling wa Gaikokujin, etc)
4) Feel-good/Heartwarming

The ones I already have are:
Blade of the Immortal
Shigurui (unfortunately only available as scanlation, so disqualified kinda)
Ooku: The Inner Chambers ( http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/\u014coku:_The_Inner_Chambers )
Maaaaaaaaaybe Vagabond
Sumomomo Momomo (contemporary, and kind of gag-y, but its serious bits are awesome and dramatic)
Basilisk (but more than this, I'd rec the movie version "Shinobi Heart Under Blade", and yet, I'm only looking for manga recs...)
I'd rec Twelve Kingdoms, but the manga is simply an adaptation of the much better anime, and not even a good adaptation.

Thanks!

    \n OK, so some quick comments.

    First, thanks for all the recs! This is great stuff. I was able to put a lot of these to use.

    OK, I wasn't thinking of commenting on each manga rec, but here goes, in order as listed in the thread:

    Naruto: Surprisingly YES. I tend not to like the JUMP-style manga treadmill (you think the supplement treadmill in RPGs is bad? We ain't got shit on Shonen JUMP's manga treadmill of endless fight subplots which have ruined countless manga since 1980). But it's solid, has great story elements buried under and around the fights, etc. Alexander Neuman also totally sold me on watching Naruto on Hulu as well.

    Dororo/Mushishi - Good calls. I used to read Mushishi when it came out, it was serialized in "Afternoon", which I had a subscription to (even in the US) for years: It's the same mangabon that featured Blade of the Immortal, Genshiken, BLAME!, EDEN, Rinshi!! Ekoda-chan, The Pigeon Bride, Historie, Vinland Saga and others.
    On it's own, Mushishi tends to get kinda repetitive/bland after a long while, but it really is a striking, innovative and definitely laid-back story.

    Shaman Warrior: Yep. I have a volume of it, it's a great Manhua. I plan on looking into even more Korean series soon.
    Vagabond: Yes and No. Good story. Hell, great story even. But slooooooow.

    Rafu: My next post will be about "Bastard!!", since that warrants Special Attention. :-)

    Buddha: Excellent manga, but not really for this rec here. It's a great historical look at the Buddha, and a classic manga at that. Nothing really ahistoric or action-y about it, though. I still regard it as a high recommendation, though.

    Lone Wolf: Pretty solid rec.
    Sayonara: Interesting, a shinsengumi tale. Looks like a companion piece to the movie "When the Last Sword is Drawn" (on Netflix)
    Not afamiliar with Phoenix, will look into that.
    Tekkonkinkreet: Not sure for this project, but it has been added to my next "buying used manga in bulk from Japan" order list.
    Berserk is OK, too long maybe.
    And yeah, Vinland Saga is good. I even like the companion comedy manga about the main character's sister.
    Rurouni Kenshin: Can you believe that I've never seen an episode, read an issue? Need to correct that.
    Gasaraki: The setup is awesome, and honestly as a visual piece it's a great reference for what in the game are called "Kimen Armours", armours (mecha) that are mass-produced instead of "crafted" by sorcerer-blacksmiths. But after all that awesome with pan-dimensional Noh theatre dancing... whatta letdown in the end...

    Carl: Twelve Kingdoms is in fact the greatest anime ever created. I have wrestled people on the conviction of that stance!
    And yeah, the novels are decent/okay. For all that great setting she created, the novels (in Japanese) are basically a vehicle for her to throw out bizarre, ancient Chinese pictographs for terms no longer ever used at the reader until their eyes glaze over.
    Lady Snowblood: Sweet catch.
    Not familiar with the others, but looking into them (Kurogane, Kaze Hikaru). Am familiar with Otogi Zoshi, and for all I love the Heian period and works ballsy enough to use it for a setting... Snoozefest.

    Mikes's Recs: All interesting. Not familiar with Arata, Shin Angyo Onshi, but will look into it.

    And good call on Inuyasha, at least one volume anyway. There are a class of characters called "Ayakashi" in the game, which are essentally like Inuyasha or Vampire Princess Miyu.

    3x3 Eyes: Interesting, haven't looked back in years.

    Trigun: Also good call. It's got a lot of style over substance, but sometimes games like that are fun to run and play too. Trigun Maximum is cool as well, in like an Emo-version of Trigun.

    Here are my slice of life/feelgood manga recommendations.

    So, my current favorite all around is "Sore Demo Machi Wa Mawatte-iru". It is hands-down the best combination of feelgood and slice-of-life that I know of, perhaps second only to Azumanga Daioh. The only problem is that it's not available in English... the good news is this: The anime is coming out this fall season in Japan:
    ( http://www.tbs.co.jp/anime/soremachi/
    What that means is the same thing it meant for Sumomomo-Momomo and other series I like: The anime may very well suck: Not a problem.
    Sucky anime or not, if a manga becomes an anime, a US publisher almost always picks up the rights to release the manga, since it has a "track record of success". This is even likelier since the anime is running on Tokyo Broadcasting Station (TBS), like the "NBC of Japan", and not "Anime Satellite Channel Number 138" or something.
    So it's just a matter of time until this manga, again my current favorite manga *of all time*, gets translated officially into English. A few years, sure, but not "never" like other series I like.

    Available in English:

    LOVE ROMA (one of my all-time faves, and usually #1 when I recommend "feelgood manga" to someone)
    Fruits Basket
    Azumanga Daioh
    Genshiken
    Yotsuba-to

    Japanese:
    Saint Young Men (Jesus and Buddha share an apartment in Tokyo) (you may be able to find scanlations of the first 12 chapters online, if you look hard enough)
