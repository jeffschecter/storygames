    \n Are we talking cover colors, or interior colors?

    Yeah, the Lulu software has trouble parsing a CMYK JPEG or whatever for the cover, in my experience. I've never done an interior-color project before with them, so I have no idea if the PDF you supply has to have stuff in CMYK. I would certainly *never* put together a print project with a color interior using RGB stuff unless I was only expecting the PDF to be read on-screen.

    Anyway, covers with Lulu *are* going to be a lot different between your screen and your paper. It's the nature of things. Screen displays gain brightness by *adding colored light* together (starting from black). Paper displays gain darkness by *adding colored ink* together (starting from white).

    When I was doing the cover for Ryan Macklin's Finis book, the first proof -- which had a cover tinted with a blue wash -- came out looking very purple, despite the blueness of on-screen. So I went super heavy into the Cyan direction. Ryan saw the screen image and said, whoa, way too cyan. I said trust me. Then he got the proof and said, "Yep, that's pretty much perfect."

    It's not a "guaranteed fix", certainly, but I always try to print out my color cover images on my inkjet at home. The way the image turns out on paper can be informative, and at the very least you can compare your home printing colors to the cover colors on your first proof and get a sense of how much and what kind of color drift there is between your screen, your printer, and Lulu's printer, and then do some approximate adjustments to try to get what you supply Lulu into a form that looks right on the page.

    I also always do my cover design in CMYK mode in photoshop and *then* turn it into an RGB image for upload to Lulu. CMYK simply can't produce certain colors that RGB can -- they're too bright, only possible with added light, not layered ink. When I look at your SA cover design I think, Wow, that's a very lit-up screen image. I'd expect a lot of those colors to go immediately duller if you ran it through Image->Mode->CMYK and then back to RGB again. And *that* will be closer to what you should expect.
