\n I finished Deus Ex: Human Revolution, and greatly enjoyed it. I also reread Remember Tomorrow recently. Could I bring these two games together?

Advantages: firstly, they both have a tremendous amount of Orange. It's lovely. And there's a good structure in the world of DX:HR to work with, and a framework for addressing some great posthuman cyberpunk themes. If you find other players who are genuinely interested in effectively creating thematic fanfic.

Disadvantages: I think that RT's default style is more of that perfect Gibson vibe, and its shades of silicon gray are different from the neo-renaissance/pre-collapse world of DX:HR. More importantly: supposing I found some players who were interested in this game due to their knowledge of Deus Ex, there's a risk of getting a mismatch of expectation.

The Deus Ex games have some rich tactical play because of the alternative ways they offer the player a solution to their conflicts - crazy cyberware, run-and-gun, sneak and shock, or just sneak away in a pacifistic fashion. This is all still a reflection of the challenge in using the facts of the fiction to create a solution. I'm uncertain that Remember Tomorrow can provide moments of that kind of tactical, problem-solving experience, but I'd like to hear how you'd approach it.

(For those who haven't read it yet: Remember Tomorrow is GM-less with shared character roles, and is about the interweaving storylines that result as the PCs seek out their goals and face off against each other and ominous Factions.)

    \n Yeah, I'd second Tech Noir if you want tactical.

    Played our first RT and I'm really interested in the concept of using RT for other interwoven story type games. In our case this would be an interwoven story version of Eclipse Phase which I think will work with reflavoured tables.

    Was thinking about starting a thread on using RT with some sort of simple Fiasco Playset model. We love Fiasco, but want a game where you can advocate a little more. There are a couple of samples for this on Box Ninja and complex stories like Neal Stephenson's Baroque Cycle or The Difference Engine strike me as doable with RT.

    \n I will take the Tech Noir suggestion and read through the PDF when I can.

    Still, I do have an idea of how I'd adapt with RT. Perhaps my idea isn't "tactical" so much as immersing in an occasional challenge in a fictionally grounded way. I'm not sure if this is simply a kind of Face Off scene, or if it's a kind of Colour scene really, perhaps with a rules tweak where the players choose Outcomes for each other. Let me describe what I'm thinking of:

    Controller: "Your argument is terribly convincing, and the Terra Nova initiate is leading you out of the building. He walks you out into a rather dark alleyway; you can bearly make out some rusted fire-escapes above you, and the pounding of the rain is muffling any sound." {hinting

    Bancroft: "I flip on my Deep Scanning optics and quickly flip my gaze upwards. Any guards or heat up there?"

    Controller: "It's crawling with them on everything but the rooftop. It's dense with guards. The initiate nudges you in the ribs to quit stalling." {leading from the fiction with what the Initiate

    Bancroft: "Pointless if what I need is in the basement. Next time he reaches for me, I make skin-contact and activate my taser-touch. Once he's fried, I grab the ident card and swipe myself into the lab below."

    Controller: "And with that, you're in. Trade Outcomes? Maybe Burned Out for Dangerous?"

    ETA: I'm in fact thinking this kind of exchange is, indeed, either a Colour or Face/Off scene, depending on how much the Controller wants to Resist at the time.
