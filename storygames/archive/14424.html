I'm currently in the process of designing a roleplaying game that is designed to go on a tee shirt. That is to say a tee shirt which contains on it all the information and equipment required to play the game printed upon it. Ideally the tee shirt should be something cool to wear and the game should be so easy and engaging that strangers in the street will come up to you and start playing - no explanation beyond the tee shirt needed.

I've tossed the idea around a bit on IRC, twitter and other places, but I thought I'd poll the esteemed interlocutors here. I think at the moment I'm focussing on a few main questions: is this the kind of project that people who aren't me find appealing? What's required in making the game itself such that it's as easy to play as I'd like, it make the tee itself appealing as an article of clothing and it uses the tee shirt as a tee shirt and not as a funny shaped bit of paper.

(For reference, some conversation on the matter has already occurred here and I've talked about it a bit here.

Any and all input welcome - especially if it begins: "Who on Earth would want a product like this Stuart? Go back to using well worn mechanics and riffing off others ideas."

Stuart.

    \n A) You'd need a fairly simple set of rules, and you'd need the shirt to be integral instead of gimmicky. I'm not sure how to do that for a "tabletop" rpg, though it's totally easy for a LARP. Have the t-shirt be the character's sheet, especially if the shirt relays information to other players about how they can interact with the wearer.

    Steve Jackson Games published a d20 supplement t-shirt back in the day. The front was a brief little D&d thing (a spell), and the back was the Open Game License, making it all official.
