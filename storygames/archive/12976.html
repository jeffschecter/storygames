    \n In our game, the maelstrom is the result of a psychic alien virus and was itself the cause of the apocalypse: either it opened your brain or you failed to thrive.

    Thematically the maelstrom is built on desire. It wants to please you! But it's also codependent. It needs to please you, and if it can't or if it misinterprets your desires, things go wrong. So in the game I am constantly asking these probing, occasionally obsequious questions and then misinterpreting the answers.

    The maelstrom is a front in my game. There's a population of ...people, constructs, whatever that live in it and are terribly needy and lonely. There is also a countdown leading up to a local merging of the maelstrom and reality. Third front is the hocus' own cult, which is addicted to the maelstrom.

    Visually we started by describing great billowing violet clouds, lightning storms, ooga booga. As the countdown runs down, it's just starting to look like normal reality. Albeit populated by dead relatives, long lost lovers, etc.

    \n We haven't gotten that far, but I think all 3 of the players in my game have opened their brains once. The savvyhead saw a lot of platonic solids. One of them spoken to him in the voice of his dead adoptive father and gave him advice (he rolled a 10+) on the things he was having trouble with.

    When the gunlugger opened her brain (and missed, big time) she saw, and talked to, the gang leader who'd enslaved her as a child, looking as he was after she brutally murdered him to escape. She also collapsed on the ground, in a seizure.

    The driver tried opening his brain once too, and also blew it pretty big, again resulting in a seizure. He did it while surrounded, in the real world, by a horde of mutant, psychic rats. So that had an effect.

    Damn, it's been 3 weeks since we last played... I can't right now recall whether it was the driver, in the above situation, or the savvyhead in another situation, who opened his brain and talked to the first girl he lost his virginity to, but she was the first human to appear in the maelstrom that may not be dead (we don't know where she is.) At first I wrote in her notes that she was dead (because I was thinking the maelstrom might be filled with dead people) but I crossed that out. I want to leave it open. I think it's also possible the "people" they've been talking to in the maelstrom are really just aspects of their own personality and subconscious mind. At any rate, every character's experience of it has been very different and personal.

The MC told the player to do something he'd really regret, and he responded with having Daimyo rape the cult leader.

    \nWhen my operator Nero opened his brain, it was in the sacred chambers of a water cult, and it seemed like the big pool they had started to swirl while the rest of the world pretty much disappeared.     \n Our Maelstrom is not very well-defined yet; it mostly manifests as sounds, especially old recordings of music. Only one character has chosen to access it, the Hocus. He keeps hitting his rolls, so there's been little blowback. So far he:

    1) Used augury during his art scene's revel to pull out a piece of "real" old-time music, a plaintive piece for strings, and 'play' it for everyone. It was nowhere near orgiastic enough for the crowd, and mutated into a much harsher blast of sound.

    2) Opened his brain to try and find a sniper. It's the only time he's gotten anything visual, an aerial view of the past few minutes run backwards.

    3) Opened his brain with his "acolyte", taking her with him into the Maelstrom. He heard her mother's voice singing a lullaby, which prompted her to confess how bad certain things had gotten at home.

    4) Used augury again, to take a Skinner's drum performance during a revel and insert it into the Maelstrom, globally. I think I just decided that the Skinner has no memory of his performance now, but everyone else keeps dreaming about it.

    \n The Maelstrom in my Gamma Road game is always filled with Symbols, and it lets us get a little deeper into the mind spaces of the Player characters. Therefore each person's perception is different, and based on a particular deep seated mental theme.

    For JC, our resident Operator, in the Maelstrom he's always a little boy, looking up to his father for advice, talking to him about current problems while reliving events from the past. Sometimes the Dad gives him an important clue, sometimes (when there's a failure) he brings pain and misery.

    For Goldie, our transgendered Chopper, the Maelstrom always has Goldie's deeply buried longings for motherhood. Either it's a good place, where Goldie interacts with her (idealized) children and gains important insights on the world, or a bad place, where Goldie is tortured by stillborn monsters and the blood of all the sons and daughters she's killed in the wastes.

    For Lemieux, the Savvyhead and more recently turned Battlebabe, the Maelstrom was the dead, specifically the people he killed, or the people he didn't save from death. That really started coming about after he used the Maelstrom to help him figure out how to boost a CB radio's range, and as a result (or a 7-9) accidentally created "Channel 41", which if you turn to, will allow you to hear the dead speak.

    A lot of this is deeply personal, and I view it as explicit permission to focus on dream sequences, strange time, and really gonzo crazy imagery. I like using it to contrast how gritty and real the world is, by showing the Maelstrom as a place of dreams and nightmares, and the deepest part of the character's psyche. By asking the players questions, they get to craft the Maelstrom and get into what their characters are all about.

    I also like our interpretation because the Maelstrom is an excuse to see the softer side of the characters. The world is so harsh and unforgiving that you don't see a lot of tenderness or self-reflection in the wastes, the Maelstrom makes it ok to deal with this stuff, as it's in a place that is not quite real.

    \n The sun is sick. It never moves in the sky and long fiery tentacles claw at the horizon. When we open our brain a small black sun can be seen behind the yellow sun, controlling it with black tendrills. It hates humanity and is dead set on eradicating it and even enslaving humans to do its will. We have a custom move which is staring into the sun without opening your brain and it gives insight into a weakness in humanity and a bonus to corresponding stat. A miss gives a penalty to cool until the character gains some insight into the human condition.

    My brainer Sundown used a strange cabled antenna in Icks Coil (a tower held together by psychic energy from Ick) to shoot his soul through the fiery sun and severing one of the black tendrils of the black sun to save a newborn child from corruption.

    The gunner Buddy has a combat suit that seems to interface with the psychic mealstrom, when he opens his brain the world looks like Tron/The Matrix. Also it's dark, a very positive thing in a world without night!

    The driver Marlon Jag sees the road of his live. On a success it never branches or ends and his soul is at ease.

    \n My central conceit for the maelstrom is that it's a place of temporal indeterminacy, where different times co-exist and interact, which in practice means forebodings of the future and some pretty massive shadows of the past from the dead city that the settlement is built on the outskirts of. Moreover, it's kind of a spiritual tar pit so it's got the animistic spirits of roads and cities and wars in there, along with animal spirits and the broken-down old gods and the dead. Common theme is that it's broken and everything hungers for people's love and blood and attention.

    tl;dr: A mashup of Donnie Darko and Dictionary of Mu, plus some leftovers from Werewolf: the Apocalypse. But it's really varied from person to person.

    EDIT: I'd like to make the maelstrom more interactive. I love the idea of people encountering stuff from their own lives and psyches there, that's a good technique.

    \n In our first game, the Psychic Maelstrom was partially this network created by super high tech that was currently falling apart. The Maelstrom was dying and we had to fix it.     \n Actually, serious question: are you guys deciding what the psychic maelstrom is as the MC and barfing it forth?

    In my games I've just asked the players what the psychic maelstrom is like for them the first time they open their brains, and operate from there. If the different characters interpret the maelstrom differently, though, it makes it hard to be like, "Oh, this is what it is," and instead leave it as this nebulous, strange thing.

    I'm thinking that barfing forth apocalyptica and telling them might be a better idea though.

    \n I've been thinking about this a fair bit, since doing two short AW sessions at PAX.

    Here's what I want to do with the maelstrom, the next time I am MCing and it fits:

    The psychic maelstrom is all of the good left behind by others. No matter what, opening your mind to it feels nice, like a thousand tiny hugs from half-completed strangers. It's where the good goes when a person dies.

    It's curious about you. It asks you questions in order to figure out how you'll fit into it, when you die. It asks you questions in order to anticipate you better. Because it loves you, and wants you comfortable when you come home for keeps.

    It is recklessly and hopelessly positive. It is incapable of seeing problems, and so asking it to help you learn more about a vicious bike gang will go strangely... It'll do its best to help, it'll show you all of their virtues and their best moments, it'll barf forth optimistica. That's it.

    And then the rest of the world will be the apocalypse world. People getting sick off the bamboo shoots they failed to boil correctly, thugs who mount crude spikes onto their human-hide jackets, angels in it for the barter and nothing more. The maelstrom will love it all, because love is all it knows how to do.
