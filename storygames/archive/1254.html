Every now and then on RPGnet there's a thread about "what movie/game/comic/show/whatever would you want to see as an RPG?" -- the last one (which I failed to find with my apparently weak search fu) was over 100 pages IIRC. I'm curious though, how the Story Games crowd would respond to this question.

What things from other media would you want to see (or design yourself) as a story game?


The one that I've been obsessing over lately is an anime (and the series of novels it's based on) called The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya. Some minor spoilers (for the early episodes/chapters) follow, because otherwise I can't explain why I think it'd make a neat game.

At first Haruhi seems like an eccentric high school girl. Instead of introducing herself to the class and says, "I have no interest in normal humans. If you are an alien, have special powers, or are from the future, come see me. That is all." As a child, she realized the mundaneness of her existance, and ever since has been trying to find something truly unique and enjoyable.

What she doesn't realize -- what the people around her who know are afraid to let her find out -- is that she seems to have unconscious but nearly absolute power over the universe. Espers, aliens, and time travellers really do exist, and one of each shows up in the club (the SOS Squad) she founds, but none of these work quite like you'd expect. Over the course of the series, the SOS Squad members find themselves trying to lead normal lives while doing anything they can to keep Haruhi from getting bored and dissatisfied enough to leave their world behind and make a new one more to her liking. In doing so they have to keep her grounded in a relatively rational paradigm, because if they tell her that espers are real, there's a good chance that they'll suddenly become an everyday occurrence.

I'm not sure exactly what kind of RPG this would turn into. A little bit of My Life With Master and a little bit of BESM, maybe.

    October 2006
    Collaborative mythology creation. A game where the players could all sit down, and through play, create a rich and deep body of mythology that would be their own. You could start at the beginning with 'And the Sky took the Ocean for his wife...' and work forward to "and so the age ended as the last of the sky-people sailed into the western sun, leaving man his own destiny". Or you could alternately jump around somewhere in the middle filling in the important events as you saw fit. The inspirations for this are works such as the 'Simarilion', 'The Elder Scrolls' series of games, and various real-world mythic cycles.

    As far as execution, I'm thinking using the basic structure of Polaris' gm-less, negotiated narration; Cosmos; designated narrative roles (Heart, Moons, Mistaken) and working from there makes the most sense to me. Taking a page from Universalis' tenants probably wouldn't hurt either. The only problem area I can see is providing tools, structure and advice that helps to quickly introduce clear conflict and adversity into the myth cycle as soon as possible, so that it doesn't run flat.

    Damn, now I want to write this.
