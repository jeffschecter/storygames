\n What if we had a distributed housecon that lasted all weekend? We'd do something Friday night, have a couple of games on Saturday, and a couple of games on Sunday. I'm thinking that somebody with space and the desire would host each day - so we'd hang out at Andy's on Friday night, then game all day at my place Saturday, and all go over to Clinton's on Sunday. Meals would be casual and communal - everybody piles into a local shop, that sort of thing.

Cool things - minimal overhead/impact for any one host, people could drop in or out by day/session, lots of gaming.

Potential problems - scheduling, communication, making sure you had a critical mass at each session.

Please tell me if that sounds fun or practical, comment on how to structure such a beast if so, and point out hidden pitfalls to the model.

    \n Some pitfalls I could see:

    One is a sense of preparedness that the Saturday & Sunday hosts will have to deal with. They won't have the day or two before to finish any prep, so all three places will have to effectively be ready by Friday night (unless they opt to not engage on days before they're hosting).

    Also, it's easy to have food and beverages bought for three days store in one location.

    When I did MacKlon last year, it spanned three days -- Friday night, Saturday, and an impromptu Sunday thing, with Saturday also spilling into my neighbor's place. But everything was easily accessible in my house, so people kept coming back for food & beverages, making my kitchen a natural meeting point. You might or might not have that, but it's probably organic and not a big deal.

    \n A couple of ideas about this, Jason, in no particular order:
    * The gaming slots would probably have to have a bit different schedule than a "normal" con, because of travel times. Everything's either 10 minutes or 40 minutes away, in the Triangle; so having a final night clot end at midnight and the first slot the next day start at nine means some folks don't get a full night's sleep. Sure, that's true of most cons. But still....

    * Public spaces and conventions centers have some things that some homes lack: seating for multiple groups of 4 to 6 people; easy-to-access, on-demand refreshments; more than two toilets; parking--in general, support for comfortable gatherings of more than 10 people. Therefore, I would definitely try to hit up the richest gamers in the area. That said, I know a couple that does monthly board game nights that host upwards of 30 people (by having a LOT of folding chairs and tables, and using every square foot of "public" space). Perhaps the "con committee" could get a van and rent some tables and chairs and transport them nightly?

    * Did you consider having the house cons going in all three houses, all weekend? Bigger, better, bolder, themed (i.e. each house would focus on one third of the interests for the con overall). Of course, then it's really just three simultaneous house cons with narrower themes, I reckon.

    * If you were thinking LARP, this would be easy and PREFECT for a weekend-long game. Then, you've got the whole property to use, and less of a demand for table space.

    Sounds like fun, though. re you thinking public invites or private con? (The former has its own associated issues, as anyone who's thrown a kegger can attest.)
