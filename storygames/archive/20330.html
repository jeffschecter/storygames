\n I kinda want more!
So far I've used strong magnets, deep pits, treetops, knee-high water, sticky floor, piles of bones. We ran a running fights in the bronze tubes of B4, that was incredible.
More ideas are muddy floor, darkness, big crates, chandelier, tables and beds\u2026

I kinda looking for CTTCH "cool things that could happen" from Feng Shui 1, but instead of specific actions I want actionable things, items, conditions and obstacles. Things that make fighting easier, harder, or neither but above all makes it different, but less in a condition sense \u201dboy, it sure is windy up here" and more in a super practical "let's break a window", "let's flip a table" sense.

I want to come up with a way to be better at making these stuff up (I put them in rooms at prep time) because you can never have enough.
Maybe some sort of categories, like "big blocks" (crates, furniture) \u201dalternatives to the ground\u201d (treetops, chandeliers, rope ladders, bronze tubes), \u201dhazards\u201d (spiky walls, deep pits). More?

I especially want to get away from the top down, \u201dchess board" view of fights and instead tumble it up completely. I played a game with a really excellent Dungeon World GM and it was amazing, a two-story fight with golems in a burning building.

I think this sort of stuff is useful for any game where there's a lot of fighting. I'm gonna use it for D&D

    \n I had a really interesting fight on a revolving space station (spinning to generate gravity), particularly since people could launch themselves in effective zero-G in the shaft through the middle of the station.

    I think a big part of this is just rewarding creative actions, and remembering to take creative actions on the part of the opponents, as well. Counting squares on a grid, having to pick just the right combat maneuver, and penalties and complications surrounding anything except attacking... these all push players away from this kind of action.

    \n Things I've used:
    Giant rolling boulders
    A "bottomless" pit, inspired by the one at Carlsbad. Not that my players figured out that there was actually a bottom.
    Creeping, bloodsucking fungus: harmless if you're walking through, but slowly covers everything that stays still. (Bonus: breaking down doors means the fungus can spread further. Intact locks become important.)
    A magical pillar that turns things that touch it to stone.
    Stairs. Always lots of fun on stairs.
    Slippery floors next to shallow pools of water.
    Waterfalls.
    A bridge over troubled lava.
    A throne room populated by a court that's been turned to stone.

    Things I want to use:
    A fragile environment (paper? crystal? glass?) that gets damaged in a fight.
    The edge of a cliff.

    \n Also, anything that allows you to use it as a swing (vines, chandeliers, torture equipment) or a slide (bannisters, elephant trunks).

    An enraged or mad "neutral" element, like a frenzied bear, carnivorous plant, or trapped demon toad in a fragile cage. Particularly if everyone knows what it is they'll go after if they're loose...

    Things that are hanging and can be cut down. (Particularly heavy things.)

    Things that are lighter-than-air and can be cut loose to float up (balloons, gaseous floating creatures, magically-propelled platforms or floating orbs, puffer fish).

    Passageways covered with stuff which makes it impossible to see through but easy to pass through (or attack through).

    Mirrors. (Allowing unusual lines-of-sight.)

    \n I've got a sort of a James Bond game in my desk drawer, and the conceit is basically to focus on delivering big setpiece fights the way they do in the movies, with a concete feel for the space, time and terrain, just like you're talking about. Unsurprisingly the scenery for the adventures is lifted from James Bond movies. My point is, you could simply go through some movies and pull stuff for your own use.

    An arbitrary example off the top of my head: in the latest Bond movie 007 makes a quick visit to Shanghai (I think - some Chinese place) and accosts his lead in a gentlemen's club or casino or something, and the place has an atrium with a bridge over an oriental sand garden, and there's an alligator living down below. Obviously Bond ends up fighting his way out, so he gets to fight on this small inside-a-house bridge setpiece, complete with a grizzly fate awaiting underneath for whoever drops down there to join the alligator.

    It might work for you to grab some action movies and plain steal their action setpieces, there's a lot of stuff like that to be had. It is unfortunate that not all are so striking, so perhaps one would do best by being a bit choosy about which movies to look at in this regard. Should be doable to build up a list by asking people to list movies with memorable action setpieces. Then you just watch them (or even just read about them), jot down the ideas, and hey presto.

    \n I like dianamic locations aspects for fighting, if that's the right term.
    They start off small and if not delt with snowball.
    E.g the troublesome lava rises and belches bubles of gas, igniting in flumes of yellow and green flame. Dark smoke is left to obscure the bridge.

    1. The flames rise melting the ice in rivulets of running water streaming across the stone floor.
    2. The kerosene floats in meandering arms of flame as water droplets fall like rain.
    3. The ice cracks and growns.
    4. Shards of ice fall from above.
    5. Locked in the ice wall can be seen the menacing eye of some other worldly creature.
