    \n Yeah, I've had exactly the same problem. There is a work around though and it only took me about 10 minutes to sort it out. Convert your postcode to a (N,W) map reference at this website and enter that as 1.2345,6.7890. You may have to play around with it to get it right but I managed to get it to literally just down the road. It is frustrating though when you should be able to just bung in a post code.

To move the pin North and/or East ADD to the numbers and to move them South and/or West, SUBTRACT from the numbers. Clear as mud?     \n Ha! I know how you feel. It takes some playing around with. Hopefully there will be a more user-friendly fix. Glad to see you made it though. Now, where are the rest of us Londoners?
    \nJohn
    \n(Edit: I have just added this to the Nearby gamers forum and thought I'd add it here.)
    \n
    \nHi all, After much grief and a lot of playing around, here's the method (albeit a bit of a hassle) for getting your location in London pinned. Cut and paste this map ref into your location field, 51.50800, -0.12900 It's for Trafalger square.
    \n
    \nTo move your pin UP or Northwards modify the first set of numbers by making them larger. Start out by modifying the 50800 figure until you get the right location on the X axis. Off course, to move DOWN, or South, simply decrease this number.
    \n
    \nTo move LEFT, or west, make the second number larger (as with the X axis), and of course to go RIGHT, or East, make it smaller. Of course, this number is a negative, but don't worry, just increase or decrease the numbers as necessary.
    \n
    \nGet one axis right first, then move onto the next. It takes some time and error, but is surprisingly fast once you're comfortable with throwing the pin around. Good luck and lets have some more Londoners on the map, eh?
