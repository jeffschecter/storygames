\nWell; I've got a concept hacked out on this link.

In short, I want to combine some theories about neanderthal culture and behaviour with wikipedia's list of cognitive biases and slap on some urban fantasy.

So maybe I could pick your brains for some Archipelago elements to go with this thing; what would make cool Fates for these characters? What would be important places in their world? What kind of connections would they make? What elements should players own? Any parts of the concept that needs changing before you would want to play it?

    \n Thanks! Rounding it out might be "Food" or "foraging", or maybe just a basket category for "nature". How to make new connections - social maneuvering within the group - could also make a good ownership element, overlapping but not necessarily the same as Tradition...

    I think I'll want to try this once with some very specific ownerships, and once with some very general ones. For the stormtrooper playset I mapped out almost everything, and added equipment for a sense of scarcity - for this one, it might be better to leave fates and connections to the players, with just a list of tribal archetypes (young warrior, shaman, grandmother etc) to get them started.

    Once you have called the locations, players tend to stick to them, cycling between them as needed. That may not work well when I want a story about a nomadic group. Maybe make one location "on the move", and dictate that locations are redefined every time the moving location has been in play?
