    \n You could use a big piece of card and score it. You'd need a very sharp knife and, then, cut halfway through the card without cutting all the way through.

    Alternatively, you could stick bits of card together with bookbinder's tape.

    Finally, you could use spray adhesive when gluing new pages to card. Attach one edge of the sprayed page to the card, then use a ruler to lay the rest of the page down on to the card. Keep tension in the paper, as you lay it, to avoid bubbles.

    \n Instead of scoring the card with a sharp knife, you could flip a butter knife knife around to the dull side and create a crease that way.

    You could take 3 or 4 hard plastic page protectors and slide in your pictures back to back. Then you use bookbinder's tape or something similar (duct tape) to tape them together.

    \n Take three clipboards (or four, whatever) - the hard ones, I think they might actually be made of masonite. Get two sets of little hinges for each joint. Drill holes to match the hinges and use short 3/16" bolts and nuts to bolt em up. Alternately, use double the clipboards and put them back-to-back before through-bolting. Then you can clip different sheets on both sides, and glue or tape permanent info directly onto the clipboard surface.

    \n I don't have a camera handy at the moment but it's three 8.5x11 landscape panels with 2 sides.

    On the side facing the players, two of the panels feature the world's map and the third panel show the natural order chart (the chart that shows silhouettes of the common animals in the territories and how big they are relative to your small mouse).

    On the side facing the GM, I have...

    - The Mission: creating missions, prologue, gm turn, player turn, rewards, missing sessions, losing characters, example names
    - Resolution: dice basics, skill ratings, skill obstacles, bonuses, penalties, ties, teamwork, beginners luck, compromise in conflicts, natural order, recovery
    - Seasons: duties, weather, wilderness, animal details by season
    - The Territories: wilderness details, major towns and cities
    - Territory Denizens: index of mice types and weasels
    - Abilities & Skills: factors, obstacles, special conditions

    All of the above includes page reference numbers. It's an expanded index with rules notes organized in the order of the book itself rather than alphabetically. I didn't include any rules already on the character or conflict sheets.

    I ran Mouse Guard in public last night with a mix of old and new people and it worked well. There is plenty I need to change on the GM screen information wise and the visual design is slapped together. But it's a good start. I'll revise it again after I use it a few times and print it on a borderless printer when I'm done. I also took a page out of Luke's book and laminated the conflict sheets for use with dry/wet erase markers or grease pencils.

    I tried using the conflict cards from the Burning Wheel wiki but I found using the standard conflict sheet was faster. Maybe I'm just not used to the cards yet?

    \n I just purchased some tri-fold restaurant menus from a restaurant supply store. These are the sort where you can slip in standard letter sized sheets. They have clear plastic, with the plastic held together by stitched vinyl with brass metal reinforcements at the edge. They run about $4 each with a minimum order quantity of 10, and you get to pick the color of the stitched vinyl.
    \n
    \nWhile they may not have pretty artwork for the players on the other side, I can put whatever reference materials I want into them. You could always buy some artwork to put into the outward facing sides.
    \n
    \nMany game sites already have reference sheets available for download.
