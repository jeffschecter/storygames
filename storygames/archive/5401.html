Greetings Owlbears! The time to register for Camp Nerdly 2 is now.

\n

How to register:
\n1. Read the Nerdly Code.
\n2. Nod a lot at the screen and give it two-thumbs up.
\n3. Go to the registration page and click on a button. Yup, it's more expensive this year, but look what you're getting!
\n4. Don't forget to tell us the names of everyone you're buying bunk space for.
\n5. Keep an eyeball on that wiki, and think about all the fun you'll be having!

\n

Where the heck is Camp Nerdly taking place?
\nWhy, it's the same place it was last year: Camp Orenda of Prince William Forest Park.

    Good question Tony.

    \n

    First, I draw your attention to item #4 in the Nerdly Code:

    \n
    \n

    Games and the outdoors are great for young people, and they are encouraged to come. However, people under 16 must be under the supervision of their parents or a guardian at all time for safety's sake, and to help everyone have fun. They are still considered campers, and should follow the Code.

    \n
    \n

    Owlbear campers under the age of 16 are considered Jr. Owlbears. They're not permitted in the Castle (except in an emergency) and they're not permitted to play with the fire. If you want to go off and play a game without your own Jr. Owlbears tagging along, then you'll need to arrange supervision with another adult.

    \n

    It's worth noting that we feel this is a very important rule at Camp Nerdly. Parents with Jr. Owlbears who cannot or will not follow the rules will be asked to leave the camp.

    \n

    That being said, I'd like to personally encourage everyone to consider all the attendees of Camp Nerdly as potential players. For my own part, my Red Box Hack games will be open to any Owlbear age 13 and up, and my Battletech games will be open to anyone who can add and subtract without using their toes. If there are Jr. Owlbears who are interested in some kind of paper-airplane workshop, then I'll be prepared to facilitate that at the same time I'm wrangling the Battletech players.

    \n

    Does that cover all your concerns?
