\n In honor of the somnolent Fred Thompson, who just announced his retirement from the presidential race, I present:

Don't Rest Your Fred: a game of shaky candidates in crappy little states
Or, What Happens When You Keep Switching Between Daily Kos and Story-Games

Premise: You're a candidate for president, the fringier the better.

Answer the usual questions: who are you, what were you before you became a candidate, why do you want to be president, etc.

Also decide what your special line of Bullshit is, and who your backers are -- they will be your assets and your Achilles' heel.

Four pools of dice:
Challenge The GM gives these out when something goes wrong -- you get heckled at a pancake house, or the cow you're trying to milk pees on you, or your opponent publishes your old love letters to somebody's wife. When these dice prevail, the GM gets a Coin of Debt. When the GM spends it to hurt you, you get a Coin of Donation to use later in a tough spot.

Charisma These are the dice you use for being a basic, sensible politician. When they dominate, you handle the situation well and get the media to take you seriously.

Bullshit Sometimes charisma isn't enough, and you have to go for the risky gesture or rhetorical flight of fancy. If it goes well, the campaign gets a little stranger but it doesn't hurt your rep. If Bullshit prevails, check off a Response (either Counterattack or Dodge the Question). If you run out of Responses, one of your Charisma dice becomes a permanent Bullshit die to represent the public not believing you any more.

Baggage The other way to shift the odds in your favor is to call in a few favors from your backers -- your Church, crazed warmongers, 9/11 Truthers, sleazy businessmen. These guys don't go away when you're done with them; they continue to hang around, lending their Baggage dice to you whether you want them or not. When Baggage dice prevail, you "win ugly", reminding the public of all the things they don't like about you. If your Baggage reaches 6, your campaign goes into a meltdown and you need to take a break from campaigning and fire your manager and press guy.

The game is structured by states, and each state should have its own theme: Iowa is cold and full of farmers and hunters, South Carolina is diverse and cooky and full of Evangelical types, Nevada... well, Nevada. You know.

I haven't figured out how to decide who wins a given primary, and how to handle debates (candidate vs. candidate, or candidate vs. Chris Matthews?)

Suggestions and Actual Play experiences are welcome.
