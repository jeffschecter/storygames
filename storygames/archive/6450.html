\n Are fewer people going to conventions this year? I've been hearing that the hospitality business has been hit hard because people are not travelling due to higher gas prices, economic squeeze, and so on. Is this impacting the small indie conventions people are running? We've definitely seen fewer signups for Go Play NW this year. It's unclear if fewer people are coming, or if they're just waiting till the last minute to sign up (that's no big deal, a small-medium convention is just as fun as a big one as far as we're concerned). Am I seeing a trend?

    \n

    \n
    \nIn any case, I'm curious... let's say that con attendance dropped by 50% just to speculate... Then what? Basically why are you asking the question?

    Posted By: Mike HolmesIn any case, I'm curious... let's say that con attendance dropped by 50% just to speculate... Then what? Basically why are you asking the question?
    \nIt's a practical question to help with convention planning and also curiosity about the factors that drive it. With Go Play NW just a few weeks away, we're trying to optimize our use of space and resources. The size of the con isn't that important, but our expectations about size are important, because they drive our decisions. It sounds like the economy isn't a huge factor right now, but it's clearly having some impact.
