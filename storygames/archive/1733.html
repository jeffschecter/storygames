Man, we are having a ball with the world we've made up, but people hate hate hate the labels I'm putting on things. And they're right. The labels suck.

I basically set out to do to PokÃ©mon what Neon Genesis Evangelion did to giant robots: Take it seriously, make it sick, fuck with people's heads.

So, here's the deal: Gods started reappearing (not the old ones ... new ones, humans don't even have names for) and causing havoc. Lots of solutions were attempted, mostly in vain. But someone (nobody's telling who ... or how ... yet) figured out how to capture spiritual entities. Or maybe they made them. Or ... something.

Anyway. There are these kids, and they're nominally in-charge of these spiritual things that they keep caged most of the time. Then, when rogue gods start causing trouble, we whip out our little pocket horrors and have combat. So there's issues of slavery and abuse, causeless war, all that good stuff. Pikachu, I choose you!

Currently, the agency that's doing all this is the "God/Angel Intercept Agency", or G.A.I.A. Everyone hates the name, and won't use it.

We don't have a name for the gods. We don't have a name for the spirits as a class of beings. We don't have a name for the handler/spirit relationship, or (really) for the handlers. We don't have a naming schema for individual spirits (I love NGE's use of "Unit Zero," "Unit One," "Unit Two" for the way that it applies an inherent and interpretable hierarchy on the three pilots, but trying a similar thing in our game didn't have the same zest).

I need names that will make people say "Oh, that rocks! NERV. I just like saying it. Our organization has NERV. The NERV of some people!" I need something that we'll make up excuses to use, rather than avoiding at all costs. Help us Obi-Wan Story-Games, you're our only hope!

    My favorite real-life unnerving organization is NR, Naval Reactors, of which almost certainly none of you has heard. It's a joint Energy Department-Defense Department activity with roots going back to the bad old Cold War days of the Atomic Energy Commission. As I understand it, it's run by a Navy admiral, so when he doesn't want to take orders from the Secretary of Energy, he says, "sorrry, sir, but Navy regulations prevent us from doing that," and when he doesn't want to take orders from the Secretary of Defense, he says "I'm sorry, sir, but Energy Department regulations prevent us from doing that." NR's primary job is to design and build the power plants for US Navy nuclear submarines. But that puts them in contact with the nuclear weapons complex (e.g. Los Alamos, an Energy Department lab nowadays), the military, and the frickin' sea floor. If Atlantis and R'lyeh exist, they know about it.

    There's also JSOC, Joint Special Operations Command, which nominally runs training for US Special Operations Common (SOCOM) but is widely known to conduct high-stakes special operations involving teams like Delta Force (counterterrorism) and Lincoln Gold (nuclear weapons containment): Apparently, even in a domestic US mission, while FBI would have overall command, JSOC would retain absolute control of its own personnel at the assault site, e.g. where the hostages were held or the nuclear bomb was hidden.

    That said, the real world solution to a problem of this magnitude would probably be a JIATF, Joint Interagency Task Force, or a CTF, Coalition Task Force: That's an organization primarily based on the US military -- the only people on the planet with the money, global communications, and supply network to handle this kind of problem -- but with detailees and plug-ins from civilian US agencies like Energy and CIA (hence "interagency") and, if it's a CTF, from foreign allies as well.

    My personal belief is that you want something aggressively bland juxtaposed with something surreally creepy, to highlight the creepiness. The organization's HQ should be a repurposed Air National Guard base, all run down and jury-rigged rather than shiny and high-tech, and the name should be something like "CTF Asura" or "Project Herod" or simply "Intercept." As in, "We're from Intercept, Sheriff. We need you to pull your people back right now or we're not responsible for their safety."
