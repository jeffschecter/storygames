\nDATE Saturday, October 11, 2008
LOCATION Alexandria, Virginia
EVENT NAME Ice Station Nerdly II
QUICK DESCRIPTION A day of games, food, and fun at my home.

Greetings, all!

Some of you may know me from such mini-cons as the DC Area meetup and Ice Station Nerdly (and Camp Nerdly 01 and 02). Anyway, I was thinking it was about time to try and hold another day of fun and gaming at my place in Alexandria, VA. After discussing it with She-Who-Must-Be-Appeased (AKA my wife Mona), we thought Columbus Day Weekend (Saturday, Oct 11) would be a good day. (If there are any cons that weekend in the area, let me know and I'll reschedule.)

We can pick people up from the Huntington Metro Station, and we will be supplying food and drink (though more snacks are always welcome!)

The event will likely start at 2 PM, and run until whenever. We managed to play 6 games (3 in the afternoon and 3 in the evening, if I recall) last time. It was crowded, but fun. Please let us know if you want to run a game, so I can put together a schedule.

Be warned that we probably can't host more than about 15 players, so get your RSVP in quick! Also, we have two extremely cute dogs who will be SO excited to see you and get dog hair all over you!

I'd also like to extend a special invite to any game designers who'd like to use this mini-con to playtest a game. We'd be happy to help you out, I'm sure.

    \n Jason has just asked me to change the game he wants me to playtest to "FIASCO," which he describes as follows:

    THINGS CAN GO WRONG, FAST

    Perhaps you know this. Maybe some dude from youth group talked you into boosting a case of motor oil, but now your cousin is dead in a swamp and you killed him. Maybe you and your girlfriend figured you could scare your wife into a divorce, but things went pear-shaped and now a gang of cranked-up Mexicans with latex gloves and a pit bull are looking for you.

    It seemed like such a good idea at the time.

    WHAT THE GAME IS ABOUT

    Fiasco is inspired by stories of small time capers gone disastrously wrong \u2013 films like Blood Simple, Burn After Reading, and A Simple Plan. You\u2019ll play ordinary people with extraordinary ambition and precious little common sense. There will be big dreams and inept execution. It won\u2019t go well for them, to put it mildly, but it\u2019ll be fun to watch in a train wreck sort of way.

      Posted By: IgnotusMy only regret is that the marines of Rescue 91:E didn't shoot the treacherous bastard lieutenant who sold his subordinates out to the Ape-Men for a case of fine ape cigars.
      \nDon't you worry.. At some point I will run 3:16 again, but my only regret was that the treacherous bastard lieutenant who sold his subordinates out to the Ape-Men for a case of fine ape cigars, didn't get a chance to sell out the marines of Rescue 91:E, for a new shiny golden arm..

      I forgot that I had my camera.. I should post those eh?

      Dirty Secrets was fun, latter my very tired brain, thought up some more connecting events.. "It was Jin's gun that Barbara used.. he sold it to her.." that will only be meaningful to 4 of ya..

      3:16 was a lot more fun then I expected. I was very hard on the Troopers..
      The First planet, as a hot jungle, with cigar smoking Ape-Men. My AA was 7 with Rage.
      Next planet, had dense neon cloud cover, with Floaty Spidery bagged things.. AA of 8, with Exploding Bodys! That was so much fun! I should have had them find Flamers on the Ape-Planet.. There they lost the Sargent..

      Everyone had weakness, one Trooper had a strength. Maybe I should have had both Troopers with one.. Hmmm.
      I gave them development roll before the drop. While on planet they found a downed scout ship, witch gave them another opportunity for a development roll.. If I had not be improvising the entire game, should have done something here that set them up for a fall on the next planet..

      Overall a very fun time..

      Latter after 3:16, myself, Mona and Sam got a game of Carassonne in.. A very good simple tile-placement game.
      and after that Mona asked me and Sam to try Notre Dame. Plays like Puerto Rico, that it uses wood blocks. Feels lighter then Puerto Rico and I didn't feel like I could be Locked out.

      Over all a very good night of gaming. Jeff and Mona are awesome.

      Nick
