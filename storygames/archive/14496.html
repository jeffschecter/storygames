\n There were 22 nominees, here are the 5 contenders: \n
Catacombs, a board-game by Ryan Amos, Marc Kelsey and Aron West, published by Sands of Time Games

The Dresden Files RPG by the Dresden Files RPG Team, published by Evil Hat Productions

Escape from the Aliens in Outer Space, a board-game by Mario Porpora, Pietro Righi Riva, Luca Francesco Rossi and Nicol\xf2 Tedeschi, published by Cranio Creations

Fiasco, an RPG by Jason Morningstar, published by Bully Pulpit Games

Freemarket, an RPG by Luke Crane and Jared A. Sorensen, published by Sorencrane MRCZ
\nABOUT THE AWARD\n
The Diana Jones Award for Excellence in Gaming was founded and first awarded in 2001. It is presented annually to the person, product, company, event or any other thing that has, in the opinion of its mostly anonymous committee of games industry luminaries, best demonstrated the quality of \u2018excellence\u2019 in the world of hobby-gaming in the previous year. The winner of the Award receives the Diana Jones trophy.

The short-list and eventual winner are chosen by the Diana Jones Committee, a mostly anonymous group of games-industry alumni and illuminati, known to include designers, publishers, cartoonists, and those content to rest on their laurels.
\nMore info: http://www.dianajonesaward.org or http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diana_Jones_Award

    \n That's tough competition. I would find it very difficult to pick a winner. All are great games!

    I would put Escape from the Aliens in Outer Space at the bottom of the list (which is by no means an insult).

    In terms of innovation, I would have to put Catacombs, Fiasco, and Freemarket on the top of the list.

    Popularity would go to either Catacombs or The Dresden Files.

    I personally would want Freemarket to win. It's the underdog, probably the most innovative, definitely the most risk taking, transgressive, and I feel under rated and under appreciated.

    If I had to pick based on past Diana Jones award trends, I would say Catacombs. With Fiasco a close second.

    Tough!

    \n I'd say Catacombs is semi-innovative with its whole finger-flicking dungeon-crawl madness where there is no randomness and you flick tokens against each other. It won the Boardgame Geek innovation award but I've played similar lesser known finger-flicking fantasy themed games. It hasn't changed the boardgame world the way say Settlers or Dominion did.

    In terms of pure "wow" ideas, I'd give it to Freemarket, no question. Especially thematically.

    But in terms of impact, I would say none of them... yet. Fiasco can eventually get there but it takes time. I know Fiasco feels everywhere but most gamers I know have never heard of it. Dresden Files has more impact in terms of awareness. Boardgame Geek won last year but that had several years to grow into a game changer. I think Fiasco will be a game changer.

    \n After researching the quite amusing history of the (in)Diana Jones Award, I'm quite surprised that Escape would appear on that list. I come from the boardgame side of the hobby niche, and I had never heard of it. I doubt that it will win, given there are a few "more mainstream" pure boardgames out that seem to do similar things. Not to mention that the last two pure board games that won had huge to moderate cross-over appeal: Ticket to Ride and Dominion.
    \n
    \nAside from "excellence" I'm not sure how else to take a guess on what would be a winner. Both Catacombs and Fiasco would be my personal choices with regards to having the most potential to cross over from a niche product into something more. But I'm not sure how much weight that carries. I would venture a case that it's a RPG's turn this year.
