\n The ninth semi-annual National Capital Area Gameday will be on the 8th and 9th of October, 2011. The event will be held at the AFT building, at the corner of New Jersey Ave and F St NW (555 New Jersey Ave, N.W., Washington DC, 20001), two blocks from the Union Station stop on Metro\u2019s Red Line. Join fellow adventure, storytelling, and roleplaying gamers in the nation\u2019s capital for two fun-filled days of games. The event is free to all participants thanks to the generous support of the gameday organizers.

Doors open at 9:30. There will be two sessions each day. The first session will begin promptly at 10am and end at 2pm. The second session will begin at 3:30 pm and end at 7:30 pm. Morning GMs should plan on arriving no later than 9:30am so that they are ready to start at 10am.

Following the last session on Saturday, there will be a group dinner as usual, although the location has not been finalized. RSVP by September 16th if you plan on attending the dinner.

Gamemasters/moderators/facilitators/hosts will be able to start posting games on the 2nd of September. Player registration will commence at 7pm on the 9th of September on this website. If you'd like to run a game, please email admin@dcgameday.org after completing the registration process so you can be added to the system as an event host.

For more information and GM/Player registration, please check out our *new* website at: DCGameday.org

    \n DC Gameday IX was fun! I got to play Gamma World (my dude was a pile of sentient cockroaches operating a suit of power armor), and run a game of Storming the Wizard's Tower set in the Planescape universe (the party fought evil honey-badgers in Sigil, stormed an actual wizard's tower - he wasn't home - and went to a demon's birthday party).

    \n\njoshnumbersbad \n\n\n
    \n
    \n\nOctober 2011 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n We played the Business Casual playset. Think "The Office."
    \n
    \nSetup: It was a three-player table. Two of us had played before, but the other guy who'd played before mostly played-by-forum. There was one element in the Relationships section that we had trouble with, it was an element that wasn't in the same format as the others. Otherwise, things went smoothly, and we quickly figured out that our players were father & son at the family business (a paper company, as a nod to the series) and a shiftless cousin who was the janitor.
    \n
    \nPlay: Things were very much PG for the first half of the game. We established our characters' personalities and roles at the business, and there were some hijinks here and there, but it was mostly pretty pedestrian. Mention was made of one character's casual drug use, but that was probably the most extreme thing that happened. When we got to the tilt, we considered using the soft tilt table because of the nature of the TV shows that inspired the playset and because our particular game seemed suited to that. But since the game was a playtest, we decided to do the less intuitive thing and go for the standard tilt table. All hell broke loose. Suddenly there were ski masks, assaults with baseball bats, people getting shoved off the roof, comas, disinheritance, and someone got smothered with a pillow embroidered with a motivational phrase ("Quality: It Starts With You"). I was surprised at how well the change of tone in Act Two went over -- we were enjoying the game during Act One, but there was a lot more laughter and glee during the carnage of the second act.
