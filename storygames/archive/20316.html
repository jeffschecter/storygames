\n OK, after running Advanced Wizards and Wizards for the better part of two years, my brain is now going to the complete opposite end of the spectrum. I'm pretty seriously considering running a 5E game with no casters.

This would leave eight class options: Barbarian (Totem or Berserker), Fighter (Champion or Battlemaster), Monk (Open Hand or Shadow), Rogue (Thief or Assassin). Still a good spread.

I'd probably leave the race options open; even though some of them get some innate spells, there's nothing that would make a character feel like a caster.

What I want to know is, what's a good classic module to use to run this with? Something that already has that "sword and sorcery" vibe to it, where magic is kind of exotic and weird and something only bad guys or maybe the occasional helpful sage uses, but certainly never the protagonists. Suggestions welcome!

    \n The OSR generally has this sort of flavour, I see more stuff along this vein than ones where the adventurers go to Ye Olde Magic Shoppe to prep for a routine dragon hunt. Original TSR adventures often fail aesthetically in this particular way.

    Titles that have particularly scratched my own itches in this regard have included XRP's Advanced Adventures such as
    "The Sarcophagus Legion",
    "The Conqueror Worm",
    "The Barrow Mounds of Gravemoor",
    "White Dragon Run",
    "The Frozen Wave Satsuma".
    (Not all XRP stuff counts here - their line also has lots of high fantasy AD&D flavour!) From smaller publishers, "Charnel Crypt of the Sightless Serpent" oozes this feel.
    "Ice Tower of the Salka",
    "Insidious",
    "The Pearl of Pirate's Cove",
    "The Purple Worm Graveyard",
    "The Screaming Temple",
    "Skull Mountain",
    "Tomb of the Iron God",
    "The Thing in the Valley" and
    "Wheel of Evil" also represent.
    Also, most of the LotFP stuff counts here, I desist from listing them simply because they're practically all fully compatible with letting Solomon Kane get in there to investigate.

    (If you're wondering about the esoteric nature of that list, it just represents what stuff I happen to have in my own library. They're all OSR stuff from a few years back, and all of those are legit adventures - I've refereed most of them at least in passing.)

    \n Just as Eero said, the classic TSR modules often fail aesthetically but some of them has a strong s&s vibe. I second that X1 - Isle of Dread is a very good idea, and I also suggest I1 - Dwellers of the Forbidden City and B4 - The Lost City (influenced by Conan's Red Nails).

    If you can find them, Gabor Lux ('Melan') modules are especially HQ and fine on genre terms, but sometimes a bit planetary romanceish or science fantasyish. Search the early Fight On magazines for them!
