    \n Yeh, Gifts are interesting. They're definitely a social feedback mechanism, and the veto is something that will have different standings with different groups. Basically, Unsung is very "Never Getting Hurt" as opposed to "I Will Not Abandon You" in that you can't ever force someone to allow their character to get hurt, but you can offer to do so.

    Unsung is also definitely aimed at upfront lets-play-characters-with-issues-in-harsh-situations play (aka "facestabby"). I think, if you're interested in that kind of play, the veto thing won't be a problem; if you're not, the game won't really have a lot of meaning in it. This is one of the things I mention in my thoughts on Unsung from a couple of years ago. It was a big inspiration for carry, but I decided to take a stronger stance.

    \n It's probably assumed that you want to play in order to torture your character - rather than fend off all attacks and have him live a perfect life.

    The veto rule probably isn't really for the player, it's for the one giving the gift. Because of the veto, they have to think about the gift they give, craft it so it will fit and wont be thrown away. It's like when you buy someone a birthday present - you try and buy something they wont just stick in a cupboard somewhere and forget about. It's more about prompting creativity in the gift giver, than in protecting the gift reciever. That'd be my estimate, anyway.
