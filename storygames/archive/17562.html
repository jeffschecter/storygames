\n I have been working on my own custom Skin for Monsterhearts and would love some feedback. Here is the Visitor: https://docs.google.com/file/d/0B0Ut5mF-spZyOEhtb1ZzclRZR1U/edit

I was inspired by the Queen, which I consider to be one of the most interesting Skins out there. Skins like Vampire and Witch and Werewolf are very fun, but they are really driven by horror archetypes. The Queen is (IMHO) the only Skin that is really driven by social archetypes. He or she can have a variety of Origins (whether supernatural or not), but his or her social position remains "center of power." Since Monsterhearts is about teenage social standing as much as it is about scary monsters, I thought it would be fun to add in another Skin driven primarily by the social side of things.

Like the Queen, the Visitor has many possible Origins -- some are crazy b-movie plots, others are relatively mundane. The Visitor can be everything from an extraterrestial to a foreign exchange student to a parallel-earth doppelganger to a spy. But whichever Origin you choose, the Visitor's social position remains "outsider torn between two worlds."

And, just a few things I know I'll get asked:
* I have no artistic abilities, so this is not formatted like other Monsterhearts skins, and it does not have a picture. (Any artistic people want to assist?)
* I have not playtested the Visitor.
* Compared to other Skins, the Visitor's Achievement options give one additional "Take another Visitor move" and one fewer "Take a move from another Skin." This is intentional, since the Visitor has several other ways of acquiring other Skin moves, sometimes at the price of losing a Visitor move; the Achievements help make sure the Visitor can keep some Visitor moves in play!

Any feedback would be appreciated!

    \n I like that this has a mix of normal and extranormal origins, like the Queen. That's awesome. I agree that in these kinds of stories, the difference between a foreign exchange student and an actual alien is negligible. They're just outsiders. But maybe you should have the player "select all that apply" from that list - they could be an alien posing as a foreign exchange student or new guy or whatever.

    I like the Agendas, but I'd take out Return as an agenda - because it can be completed and because successfully completing that agenda takes the character out of the game. For the same reason, you don't want something like "Find Your Lost ___" as an agenda, because once you accomplish it, it's done. The other agendas are great because they aren't a lightswitch that can be flipped. Learning, hiding, and healing can go on indefinitely.

    Likewise, I'd rework the Darkest Self so it didn't point so strongly to the possibility of taking the character out of the story.

    I think the Lost Love concept is a big fat arrow constantly pointing off screen. It doesn't help - I'd remove it entirely. I'd replace New Love with Anchor. Your Anchor is the person you have found in this new place that makes you feel a little comfortable. I'd treat the Anchor like the Lover for the Mortal - you always have exactly one, but whom it is can change. That way, the Anchor can be more vague. It can be someone who reminds you of your other place/time/whatever. Or it can be someone who has made you feel welcome and accepted, even if it's only them that accepts you. (And being Monsterhearts, that acceptance and welcoming is likely to get screwed up, which is why the Anchor should be able to be transferred). Or some of both. I think the sex move already does enough to connect the Anchor to being a lover - I don't think you need to connect it more explicitly than that.

    Reporting Back could be expanded to include comfort as well as orders, maybe. It still fits that being overheard results in a String - you're exposing a source of neediness.
