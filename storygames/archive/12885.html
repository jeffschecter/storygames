\n PAX is this weekend and I plan on bringing some games to demo. Most of those are games that are pretty much geared for short play, or for which I have a clear idea of how demoing works. Freemarket is the big unknown. I really want to show off the game, but I'm a little unsure as to what parts are best to show off.

So, besides wearing my awesome Freemarket shirt and sewing my CHOIRS patch on my gaming bag, how can I prepare to demo this game?

    \n Grab one of the sample characters and run a challenge:

    Betty: Mick-E wants to do the race NOW. Shaping challenge to get an edge over the amazon.
    Ellroy: Smersh comes back from his stay in the medical bays and he wants revenge. You need to lose him in the crowd (ghosting) or fight (wetwork)!
    Khenbish: Tam's stove is on the fritz and the freemers are lining up for his dumplings. It's going to be a recycle challenge to repair that stove.
    Qais: Thin slice news of Douglas' attempted assassination and get people to start wearing air filtration devices.

    Or just grab something else from the characters' memories.

    I ran a bunch of short demos of FreeMarket (one or two challenges each), some solo some multi-user. It's not hard at all.

    \n Keep in mind that it can take a long time to explain the concepts about the game to people. It's not as easy as "The world has gone to shit, you're some cool guys in the rubble" or "you're a band of merry adventurers out for gold and stuff". I mean, it's fairly simple in a big-picture way but people get confused, in my experience. In a four hour demo slot at GPNW we spent the first hour just discussing what the game was "about".

    Actual chargen and play isn't nearly as strenuous.

    \n FreeMarket is a space station near Saturn, home to over 80,000 genetically modified, cybernetically enhanced humans.

    You are one of them.

    There are no laws, there is no money, death is temporary and all your basic human needs are taken care of by the station. But there's not enough room to give everyone exactly what they want. If you want more than what you have, you'll have to earn it through cooperation, negotiation, trading, giving gifts, and making friends.

    Or: shut up, sit down and I'll show you.
