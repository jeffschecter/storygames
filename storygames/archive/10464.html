\n I was wondering if the following would be a good generic dice rolling mechanic...
\n
\nAttributes are ranked from 1 to 5.
\nEach Rank is associated with a die from D4 (1) to D12 (5).
\n(just like Savage Worlds in a way)
\n
\nSkills are ranked the same way.
\n
\nPowers are ranked in four Grades; Greater, Major, Minor and Lesser.
\nEach Rank opens up new options for that Power. For instance, Lesser teleportation allows one to teleport to a location no farther than a few hundred feet away and only a location he knows while Greater teleportation allows one to teleport himself and a great number of others miles away
\n
\nActions that require resolving are done like so...
\n
\n1) Choose one applicable Attribute, Skill and Power. If no Skill or Power applies, no biggie. You just won't be as proficient.
\n
\n2) You roll the appropriate Rank Die for both Attribute and Skill (if a Skill applies). Powers add a +1 per Grade if one is applied.
\n
\n3) Dice Explode if the maximum value is rolled. Allows one to roll next Rank Die up. These dice can explode as well. When D12s explode, the value is increased by 1 instead.
\n
\nI wonder if the range of numbers is too small... or if the mechanic just isn't interesting enough. The rest of the game intends to be pretty rules lite where Skills are created by the players.
\n
\nAny observations, comments, questions, criticisms and what-not are welcome.

    So yeah, I'm kind of intrigued by it, but I can't help but be intrigued by the math (this is me thinking out loud; I'm sure you've thought about it already ^_^):

    If you have a Rank 1 ability, you have about a 0.5% chance of exploding into an 18-total, and a chance to roll a d10; if you have a Rank 5 ability, you have about a 0.6% chance of getting a 24-total, and a chance to roll another d12. It sounds like being better at things, a lot of the time, is a significant advantage, but about 8% of the time, someone with Rank 1 will beat someone who's Rank 5 on a single die roll.

    On the subject of powers, I think letting multiple major characters possess the same kind of power would be wonky, unless it's the kind of super power that's pretty common (different characters with different levels of super strength wouldn't be too weird for me, but different characters with, say, different levels of teleportation seems a little esoteric unless they're wielding very different power sources).

    \n Hey, MacLeod!

    Glad to hear you're trying to get around that big problem of Powers systems that whiteknife mentioned - Mutants and Masterminds is just one example of a supers RPG with this problem. Oddly enough, Donjon, of all things, could offer up some good advice in this regard - in that freeform power-creation system, you're forced to come up with something that can be quantified in some way beyond "yes:has power/no:lack power".

    No straight-up fire immunity, for example, but fire resistance is A-OK. If you're making a game that features humans suddenly developing magic/super/whatev powers, then it's all right to design something that isn't "comprehensive" - i.e. doesn't need to include the possibility of magic fire demons to be complete.

    \n I have been thinking about a different way to setup Powers...
    \nThe following is the beta idea for the Key Word Power Creation System (KWPCS).
    \n(what? acronyms are a staple of super hero comics!)
    \n
    \nAlright, so there exists 40 Key Words. During Character Creation you either roll up two or choose two.
    \nRolling them isn't as painful as it sounds. You roll 2D20, and pick one for the X axis and the other for the Y axis. Then you do it once more. Gives you a small amount of control over what you receive.
    \n
    \n[align=center][/align]
    \nOnce you have your Key Words, you are allowed to create 3 Aspects (perhaps a better term should be used, Extensions maybe?). These Aspects must relate in some way to your Key Words, either one or both. Aspects must be narrowly defined but are only as detailed as the players at the table agree is necessary.
    \nThis means that if your group agrees on it, you may have to define how many feet you can teleport, whether or not you can teleport to places you've never seen before, if you can teleport others with you, etc...
    \n
    \nEveryone has a Skill called Power Control that serves as a way to channel your Powers and make your rolls better.
    \n
    \nIts a rough idea, but how does it look folks? Or, should I create a new thread for this topic altogether?
    \n
    \nSuggestions for better or more Key Words is also welcome. =D

    \n Here is the mechanic "in motion" so to speak.
    \n
    \nRoy Gulvin
    \nOccupation: Mercenary
    \nWeapons: Katana, Various Small Firearms
    \nKeywords: Sound, Speed
    \n
    \n[align=center]Extensions[/align]
    \nSilence Is Golden\n
    \n
    Roy may project a field of complete silence around his body. Any noise that occurs within this field will be nullified completely as well as any noise that enters it. This field is invisible and very malleable, he can choose to cover only specific parts of his body. With some effort Roy can also project this aura at a distance to cover objects no larger than 4\u2019x4\u2019.
    \n
    \nAt the Speed of Sound\n
    \n
    Roy is capable of moving almost as fast sound, about 900\u2019 per second. However, he is only able to do this in short bursts. This power also allows Roy to react with the alacrity required to operate at 900\u2019/sec. The aura generated by his Powers protects his body and clothing from friction.
    \n
    \nSonic Boom Snap\n
    \n
    By centering his speed bursts directly into his hand, Roy can move his fist so fast that he creates a minor sonic boom. This concussion of sound is strong enough to deafen normal humans and harm any who come in contact with the sound waves. Roy is completely protected from the sonic boom because of his Silence Aura. Area attack and damage dealt is equal to \xbd of the Power Control Effort.
