    \n psytrooper - this isn't a common indie game feature. So, don't worry, #notallindiegames

    In Undying, death happens when you make the "Fight" move and the winner decides you die (or GM, in a tie) or when you are outmatched by the prey and the GM makes the "Overwhelm a PC" move and you can't spend enough blood. The Fight case is the more common case.

    I think part of what makes "Fight" special is that even though your character is really powerful, in that moment, all their power is left to the fates and, in that moment, they triumph or they perish. Coming from a trad background myself, I can totally understand how losing control of your character this way could be a tough sell. All I can say is, it's exhilarating!

    To make the sell to your friends easier, maybe try a one shot? In a one shot, you don't have as much to lose. I think once you have some fun playing with Meddle and Fight, you'll want to keep playing!

    \n A) Depends on your take on things -- PVP is a big part of what makes Undying fun! My recommendation: don't inhibit PvP unless it becomes a problem at the table (i.e. folks getting upset or monopolizing on time). You can (and should) interfere with your PCs by using your NPCs to Meddle with them, call in old debts, or offer them new debts. That can include taking sides with one PC against another PC. Otherwise, just let it happen, grab some popcorn, and watch the fireworks!

    The Handling PC Death section (pg 46) says "If a PC is destroyed, the player may introduce a new character, or with the GM\u2019s permission take control of a NPC. To introduce a new character, follow the same steps used to create your previous character. If you take charge of a NPC, you retain their established debts, status, and grudges, but otherwise create the character as normal."
