\n With GenCon getting closer and closer, I've begun to think about what I want to do when I go. This will be my first time at the convention, and while I've been to DragonCon before, GenCon seems to be much larger in scope. I'm planning on checking out a lot of stuff (especially the dealer hall)...

Anyway, before I digress too much, here's the purpose of this thread: My wife and I want to play some games at GenCon. And chances are, even though we don't know each other, we would be interested in playing with you. Yes, you! And I figure I'm not the only one. So I thought I would start a thread about it. I honestly don't know if this will go anywhere, but at the very least, it will let me ask around and see if there is any way to get some games together.

I'd like to play Dread (in fact I made a thread about this a while back), Dust Devils, and Nine Worlds. I'd probably also be into a large variety of other games, including but not limited to With Great Power, Primetime Adventures and The Dictionary of Mu. Honestly, though, I just would like to play something; I don't get a chance to play a wide variety of stuff, and though I certainly have fun with my game groups, I would really like to play with some folks I don't know well but who I'm sure are cool, friendly people.

Anyone else up for some games?

    \n Hey Chris, I'd love to play with you guys! That said, here's my advice: Come with some games prepped to run. That way you are 100% guaranteed to be ready to have a game, regardless of who you meet and what's going on. There are many people who want to play games, and if you take a little initiative, you won't be disappointed. The Games on Demand area during the day is stuffed with greatness, and people game all night as well.

    \n A lot of us are going to be wandering near/at the Games on Demand booth. Stop by!

    Ewen Cluney and me will be with Jerry Grayson and others at the Khepera Publishing booth (I'll be leaving here and there often to get to GoD), pimping Maid RPG, a very silly game. We'd be happy to throw down at any point.

    I'm also going to the RPGNet Meetup: I expect it to be a geek-out, but I think we can squeeze some gaming in if we try.

    There's also the Embassy gaming thing. Personally, I'm going to drift between that area and GoD at night (if the tables aren't being used by other events at those times), and maybe folks' rooms. I'm mainly in it for the talking at night (I game hard during the day, looking for beer and chat at night), but I'm always happy to throw down some light prep fun.

    And I second Jason's rec. The Best Way to get a game going with online buddies is to bring something ready to play. Seriously. Nothing kills a gaming meetup and turns it into a talk-a-thon more than 6 people showing up, each person looking expectantly to the person to their left.

    -Andy
