    \n Wow, future. Ok, it's 2015.
    Hrm...there's probably a ton of cool new games out. Judging by the crop of recent years, there are at minimum ten new games that I'm playing or want to play.

    I'm almost certainly still playing Burning Wheel and Mouse Guard, Apocalypse World (in one of its million incarnations, Knife and Candle is rocking my world.) and some variant of D&D and probably Storming the Wizard's Tower.

    Doing one-shots and short games of Montsegur 1244, Human Contact, Fiasco, In a Wicked Age... on the side. Or some cool new game that came out.

    I have published at least one game in the meanwhile. Not sure which one.

    \n Game wise, probably something new or a hack of something old and playtests of friend's games.

    I'd love to play a wrestling game that meets my probably too high expectations. A modern game where we play every day people. A modern horror game. A civil war game set in the past or future. More cyberpunk. A superhero game where we play normal people first and what we do and how we behave determines the type of powers we develop later.

    My ideal gaming life is GMing more and playing 2-3 times a week with different groups and having a Saturday or Sunday game day where multiple groups are simultaneously running multiple games at my apartment.

    I'd also draw more. Small things like sketches of PCs and NPCs.

    \n The game we're playing is less exciting than how we play it. When we're gathered around the table, all of us beautiful and clever, there is an amazing feeling of gravity to what it is that we're doing.

    The way we throw dialogue back and forth, play with conflicts... well, let's just say that the room drips with sexual tension. We are held captive by one another, in turns. We want this spell to last as long as possible. It's art, in the most human and profound of senses.

    (Probably it's Apocalypse World.)
