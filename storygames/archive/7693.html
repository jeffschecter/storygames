\n So I ran my first game of 3:16 last night, and it rawked. I won't bore you with details, since we kept it rather simple, me having just had time to give the rules a once-over before starting play. Anyway:
\n
\nThe Idea
\nThis is for handling the initiative in conflicts. It worked beautifully. Grab a piece of paper and make two rows on it (as in rectangles, wide enough to fit a die). Above the one, write "Success" and above the other, write "Failure".
\nNow, make sure that every player has their own uniquely-coloured (I write it with a 'u' for you, Gregor) D10 for initiative. The aliens also have their own (mine is green and translucent). When everyone roll for Dominace, have them put their die in the Success row if they're successful, and in the other if they're not. Have them arrange the dice in the row, highest to lowest. So if you roll a high success, you put the die towards the front of the row, so as to give space behind it for the people with lower successes. It's really easy to do this everyone at once, and not as chaotic as it might sound.
\nAnd there you have a simple initiative order. I use coloured plastic miniatures from a Swedish board game for the characters on the range map, and the players have dice of the same colour as their character, so it's really easy to look at who's at what range and acts at what time (very important for grenade use). I'm thinking of modifying my range map to have these two rows on it, so I can make do with one paper.
\n
\nThe Question
\nIs switching to a weapon, apart from hand-to-hand and grenades, a full action?
