\n Priorities are keeping me from being an active participant in the online gaming community. Specifically, when I have time to post about gaming, I instead write my game. I don't have time to be promoting it to the community and write at the same time.

Is this bad? Am I going to miss out on important advertisement space? Is it easy to switch gears from developer to salesman, or would I be better served by scheduling myself some time every week to remind people what I'm doing?

Anyone else felt this way?

Is this the wrong topic? It seemed related to promotion but it's more about asking for promotion advice.

    \n I feel your pain. Work on your game now. When it's mostly written or gotten to a useable draft stage, you can start talking about it. You'll still have time to garner interest while you playtest and tweak. In fact, if you've got specific questions or concerns when you get done, you can ashcan it or post about that.

    \n Promotion needn't be anything complex, too. Half of promotion is establishing name recognition-- you just need folks to know the name when they see the book or thread.

    Also, networking is key--if at all possible, get other folks talking about your game. Playtests are useful for rules-refining, but they're also great for getting your toehold of mindshsare.
