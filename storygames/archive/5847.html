\n There are too many new games to keep up with. Cool games are all over the place - but who knows about them? I'd like someone to point me towards cool stuff.

Half-formed commie idea coming up.

The Committee for the Advancement of Interesting Games (CAIG) has as its goal to find interesting games and spotlight them. "Interesting" is important: This is for showing new, cool ideas, application of previously untested ideas, innovative use of ideas etc.

The structure is very simple: Five people, a blog and a mailing list.

If you've made an interesting game, or read about someone else's, you send a mail to the CAIG. The CAIG reads and tests as many games as they can, and discusses them. Every three months, a new batch of games is recommended on the blog and mailing list.

All publication is completely up to the designers. Whether the designer has a full-color hardback book in print, or the game is simply a free PDF on their web page, is up to them. The CAIG can put a link (to a web page, file, LULU storefront etc) on their blog and quarterly mail.
