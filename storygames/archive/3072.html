\nTrying to figure out who all is coming to this con from So Cal/U.S. southwest area. I will look for you if you will be there (and some of you know me by sight or rep there). Please post here if you will make it.
\n
\nAs I was going through events today, I noticed something that surprised me.
\n
\nWhat has happened since a group of business-savvy fans purchased the con from the previous couldn't-give-a-damn owners, the rpg side of the con, under the very awesome supervision of indie-rpg aficionado and pod-cast expert Paul Tevis, has transformed itself into a kind of indie-RPG haven for the West Coast.
\n
\nYes, there are still plenty of D&D;/RPGA events (about one hundred, actually) but indie games abound and, just from what's scheduled, Strategicon actually has more indie-rpg events listed than Gen Con Indy. (That provoked a WTF? from me though, I realize, from the amount of folks that show up at Gen Con Indy, there will be plenty of Open Gaming indie-rpg events). I was just kind of startled to notice the schedules comparing favorably a local con to the hobby's Mothership.
\n
\nSome indie highlights from the upcoming Memorial Day con:
\n- John Wick will be there to playtest his Houses of the Blooded as well as run indie-fave Cat and Discordia.
\n- Joshua BishopRoby is running his own Full Light, Full Steam rpg which has proven to be quite popular.
\n- Sorcerer[/b], Don't Rest Your Head, Dogs in the Vineyard all have their sessions. There are multiple incarnations of Spirit of the Century (serving as the game engine for Star Wars and Cthulhu pulp) as well as Heroquest and octaNe used for Doctor Who. There's also a couple of cool "taste test" slots of various indie-rpgs such as Fulminata, Puppetland, Schauermarchen, Mazes & Minotaurs, Wuthering Heights, Empire of Satanis (sorry, I had to juxtapose those), etc.
\n[/ulist]
\nOutside of indie-games, there's new games like Scion, Patrick Kapera running his own Spycraft 2.0, Hollow Earth Expedition, Shadowrun 4th, Paranoia XP plus plenty of favorites such as Call of Cthulhu, GURPS, Buffy the Vampire Slayer, Hero 5th, Stargate D20 and Savage Worlds. Also, beloved nostalgia games like Red Box D&D;, Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (yes, the Palladium version!) and even Toon.
\n
\nAnyway, I'm excited about running and playing there and I hope to see you. The more folks that participate will only help continue the trend of attracting good GMs and kick-ass games. Anyway, here's the link: www.strategicon.net
