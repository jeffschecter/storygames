    \n Take a look at this thread: http://www.story-games.com/forums/discussion/20760/fame-social-status-mechanic

    I'd suggest the Standing rules from The One Ring. A value from 0 to 6 telling you how much influence, respect, admiration a character enjoys in his community. Standing helps in social encounters with NPCs but the more interesting thing is that it gives your character a say in the wider history of their homeland. The higher your standing, the bigger your influence on changes and developments at home (the game reserves the so called Year's End Phase for this).

    \n For a different angle on this, look at Masks; It's a binary "Who has influence over you" function, since the PCs are teenaged superheroes, it's basically a marker for "whose opinion impacts you". The exciting part comes from all the stuff that triggers off someone having influence over you and how you choose to deal with it.

    \n My favorite influence mechanics are those where the players/player characters give each other power, like the board game Diplomacy. Actually having or not having influence is so much better than other players evaluating how influential your character should be in theory.
