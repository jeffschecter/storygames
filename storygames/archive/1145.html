    1) What is the best thing we can learn from MMORPGs?

    More people are interested in "Roleplaying" and even "Traditional Genre Roleplaying (D&D)" than actually play Roleplaying games (including D&D). This should be a serious wake-up call to the RPG industry / hobby. Time for thinking about why the average person wouldn't play an RPG, but would play WoW.

    * Don't have to organize a group of people to meet in the same physical place
    * Can be played whenever it's conventient
    * No prep work require of any Dungeon Master
    * No (or minimal) requirement to learn the rules of the game
    * No social stigma about playing video games (or at least much less than RPGs)
    * Easy to buy in the mall, install on your computer and get playing right away

    2) What is the best thing we can learn from CCGs?

    * The Game part of RPG and competitive play often gets overlooked.
    * The rules are often much tighter, and don't require a Dungeon Master / Referee
    * Artwork is appreciated, if it shows up as part of the game (instead of just in the rulebook) it is appreciated, and can build a sense of immersion

    3) What is the best thing we can learn from Reality Television?

    * There is no starring character in a Reality Television show (not like a book, movie, etc)
    * People are aware that there isn't a single path through the game/story to victory.
    * The interaction between the people is more interesting that the overall narrative of the game.

    4) What is the best thing we can learn from Collectible Miniatures Games (a.k.a. Clicky Figs)?

    * Just like CCGs...
    * Artwork is appreciated, if it shows up as part of the game (instead of just in the rulebook) it is appreciated, and can build a sense of immersion
    * Not everyone who would have miniatures in their game is interested in the parallel hobby of painting miniatures

    5) What is the best thing we can learn from the Fine Arts?

    * Originality is appreciated
    * Skillful execution is appreciated
    * Learn from the classics

    Okay everyone, here are some brainstorming questions regarding game elements and game design for story games in relation to other entertainment media. Please limit your responses to one item per question.

    As game designers/or players:


    1) What is the best thing we can learn from MMORPGs?

    Crafting. People can spend hours trying to make their own stuff... and never seem to get bored. It's about homemade rewards. Third edition D&D has attempted to address this phenomena but I think it falls short. I think a good tabletop solution for crafting has yet to be found.


    2) What is the best thing we can learn from CCGs?

    Stackable Mechanics. The best thing about CCGs is the way a single card can be one thing when regarded alone and another thing entirely when coupled with a partner. Guild Wars is a computer game that tries to adapt this concept to an RPG application. Another computer RPG, Chrono Trigger (SNES) had characters who could utilize special attacks only when they worked together. Stackable Mechanics. These can be a powerful addition to any story game.


    3) What is the best thing we can learn from Reality Television?

    Immunity Challenges. I haven't watched many Reality TV shows but I am a huge comic book fan and I have been watching "Who Wants to be a Superhero?" it has immunity challenges and I know that Survivor does too and I am sure many others. Story Telling games are not elimination games, so the Immunity Challenge as such won't work. But conceptually the idea is good. Give the players a challenge and reward them for accomplishing this challenge which will give them some special edge that will only be useful in the next (bigger, badder) encounter. It's a model for structuring adventure scenarios that could teach us a lot about motivating players to accomplish a particular goal.


    4) What is the best thing we can learn from Collectible Miniatures Games (a.k.a. Clicky Figs)?

    Use Miniatures. Miniatures are intrinsic to our hobby. I think the popularity of Miniatures games proves how important the things are to any gaming experience. This gaming model is based upon a CCG marketing concept and has many of the same strengths and weaknesses. So, beyond repeating what was said about Stackable Mechanics (that also exists in the CMG) I just want to emphasize that I feel any story telling game needs to have a mechanic for dealing with miniature figures. People like them.


    5) What is the best thing we can learn from the Fine Arts?

    Create for Yourself. The best art is a reflection of the artist's soul. As human beings we all have things in common and as such it is entirely possible that your vision will touch someone. Write create and build the game that YOU want to play. This then is your art. The more personal, the more passionate you are about it, the more those people you reach out to who can appreciate your vision will be able to say, "Wow, this really speaks to me."
