    Youthful Supers Saving The City

    The Broadcast
    Before the Broadcast, supers were few and far between. Even the biggest cities maybe only had two or three superpowered individuals, and they were lucky if any of them were considered heroes. No one was really sure how they got their powers, even the supers themselves didn\u2019t always know. Some believed themselves to be aliens, others believed they had been exposed to exotic radiations, and a few even claimed to be gods. But eventually their deeds, whether good or bad, were overshadowed by endless publicity events and paparazzi reports about their relationships and personal problems. The people had no real heroes who would save them from trouble, and little recourse against the villains who became wealthier by selling their stories after being prosecuted.
    This balance of power came to an end a few years ago with the first Broadcast. Some heard it in-between certain FM radio stations, others saw it flicker on and off during a commercial break, and a few even accidentally streamed it onto their digital music players. Those who experienced it were almost exclusively adolescents and young adults, and they heard a call to action. It warned them of the dangers threatening their communities, and filled the emptiness and apathy in their hearts with inspiring messages. While its origins remain unknown, and rumors run rampant about what could be responsible, those who do hear the Broadcast are special \u2013 with discipline and a strong spirit, they can use it to perform superhuman feats.
    Power is now in the hands of the city\u2019s youth, the Receivers. Of course, the grown-ups won\u2019t like it, and will try to stop them from doing what they think is right. The old-guard heroes and villains aren\u2019t too happy about it either, and may try to bring their adventures to a bitter end.
    YOU have heard the Broadcast. What are YOU going to do, eh?

    Supplies
    You will need lots of blank paper, writing utensils, a rap-sheet for each player, a Trouble token for each player (such as a poker chip or action figure), and a bag filled with five white and five black D6 dice per player.

    Characters
    Give your characters names. Not the names the grownups call them by, but the names their friends call them by, one or two words that really describe who they are and what they stand for. Razor Mike, Overheat, Winona O\u2019Wisp, Terra Biter, etc. Making it related to your Signal or Noise would make a lot of sense, but sometimes, tags don\u2019t make sense.
    Your character has a two-word Signal, which is a power, special ability, or unique talent. Your character uses the Signal to help people and oppose the bad guys. Examples include: Fiery Blast, Magic Teleportation, Distant Sight, Super Size, Stretching Arms, etc.
    Your character has a one-word Noise, which is a weakness, disadvantage, or significant social complication. Your character gets in trouble because of the Noise. Examples include: Scared, Greedy, Smelly, Curious, Insomniac, Embarrassed, etc.
    Your character is friends with the other characters \u2013 you are all Receivers who work as a team.

    The Broadcast
    Starting with the youngest player and moving clockwise, take turns making D66 rolls on the Broadcast Generator. Amplifiers and Modulators are optional, so you can choose to roll on one, the other, both, or neither at all, depending on what your team wants. Once you have at least one Spectrum, Frequency, and Transmission, with their associated Events, Locations, Persons, and Things (as determined by previous rolls), write down the complete Broadcast at the top of a sheet of paper. This is the starting scenario for your team, the Broadcast that they hear today that spurs them into action! Is a power-hungry CEO evicting children from an orphanage? Is a washed-up superhero threatening your friends downtown? The Broadcast tells you what\u2019s going on, and its up to you and your team to decide how to resolve it!

    The Story
    Starting with the youngest player and moving clockwise, take turns being the Broadcaster. When it is your turn being the Broadcaster, the other players will take the role of the Interference \u2013 adversaries, the authorities, the bad guys, or the disasters that oppose the Receivers and their adventures.
    The Broadcaster starts their turn by taking five random dice from the bag, and shows them to the rest of the group:
    If you have more White dice, your Receiver has the Advantage, and will get to describe doing something cool or getting out of Trouble, removing the Trouble token from his rap sheet.
    If you have more Black dice, your Receiver will be in Trouble, and the Interference will get to describe the bad things that happen or the trouble it causes the Receiver. The Broadcaster puts his Trouble token on his rap sheet.

    The Broadcaster then rolls the dice:
    If the Receiver has the Advantage, total the two highest White dice \u2013 that is how many words you can use to write a sentence about your Receiver\u2019s actions; then take the highest Black die (if any) \u2013 that is how many words the Interference can use to write a sentence about reacting to, but not escaping from, your Receiver\u2019s actions.
    If the Receiver is in Trouble, total the two highest Black dice \u2013 that is how many words the Interference can use to write a sentence about causing disasters and getting in the way of the Receiver.; then take the highest White die (if any) \u2013 that is how many words you can use to write a sentence about your Receiver reacting to, though not escaping from, this problem.

    When the sentences are done, the Broadcaster takes one of the highest dice, value side-up \u2013 if the Receiver had the Advantage, take the highest White die; if the Receiver got into Trouble, take the highest Black die instead. It is now the next player\u2019s turn as Broadcaster. Once everyone has had a turn, that is the end of the round, and the next round starts once again with the youngest player\u2019s turn.

    Receiver Free Words:
    Character Name
    Character\u2019s Signal
    Just Then
    Meanwhile

    Interference Free Words:
    Broadcast\u2019s Amplifier, Frequency, and Modulator
    Character\u2019s Noise
    However
    Suddenly

    Using Your Dice
    As you collect dice, you Receiver and the rest of the team will be inching ever closer to the end of their adventure. Once any Receiver gains at least five dice of one color, the story will be finished at the end of the round. There are ways to extend your playing time, however.
    During another player\u2019s turn as Broadcaster, you can take one of your collected White dice and place it in the center of the table, value side-up. You can then add this many words at the end of the Broadcaster\u2019s sentence, with the free word \u201cAnd,\u201d describing something beneficial.
    Alternatively, you could take one of your collected Black dice, and place it in the center of the table, value side-up. Then add this many words at the end of the Broadcaster\u2019s sentence, with the free word \u201cBut,\u201d describing something detrimental.
    Now, here\u2019s the trick. Any dice used to add \u201cAnds\u201d and \u201cButs\u201d to a Broadcaster\u2019s sentence cannot be of a higher value than the highest value die of the same color already used, and there cannot be any duplicate values of the same color. So, if the first person to use a White die to add an \u201cAnd\u201d has a value of 4, no one will be able to use a White 5 or 6 later on, and once 1, 2, and 3 have been used up, no further White dice can be used.

    Ending the Game
    The Story ends when any player gets at least five dice of one color, and the last player gets a change to take their turn. After this last round, each Player gets to write an Epilogue sentence with a number of words equal to their two highest collected dice (whether White or Black).
    Total up the White and Black dice collected by each player, excluding those spent on \u201cAnds\u201d and \u201cButs.\u201d If they have a greater number of White dice, the Receivers were ultimately victorious, and their Epilogues should describe their success, whether a final blow against the villain, being praised by friends, or getting a reward. If the Black dice were greater, then the Receivers were defeated, and must describe their shame, their escape from the final battle, or the humiliation they suffer from their upset friends.

    Summing Up
    Each player will then add up the values of their collected White and Black dice (see, there\u2019s a reason you need the value side-up!)
    If the White die result is higher, your Receiver becomes stronger from his experience, and adds a new, one-word Signal, or adds an additional word to an existing Signal.
    If the Black die result is higher, your Receiver becomes stronger from his experience, but at a cost, adding a new, one-word Signal, but also adding a new, one-word Noise.
    If the White and Black total is even, your Receiver is able to refine his abilities, changing one word from either a Signal or Noise, but not adding any words.
    Add these totals to your rap-sheet.

    Becoming a Hero or Villain
    When the player decides that his Receiver has grown up enough to move on to bigger and better things, compare the final White and Black totals.
    If your White total is higher, your Receiver loses his connection to the Broadcast, but keeps his powers through heroic acts, becoming a full-fledged superhero.
    If your Black total is higher, your Receiver loses his connection to the Broadcast, but maintains his powers through dastardly deeds, becoming a full-fledged supervillain.
    If the totals are even, your Receiver vanishes from the public eye. Describe what happens to him!

    How many dice need to be in the bag?
    \nFive black and five white D6s per player. And because I didn't explicitly state it before, the dice you end up not keeping (since you will take one of the dice after rolling) go back in the bag. Similar to Do: Pilgrims of the Flying Temple, where you return the stones to the bag that you decide not to keep.
