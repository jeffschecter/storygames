    \nAlias is pretty much Sexy Deadly the TV show.

    Sydney Bristow is a naturally-gifted young woman trained to be a highly capable operative. At first, because she thinks she is serving her country; and then after the betrayal, to destroy the man she hates.

    She sexy, but not in a drop-dead gorgeous way. More in a "I'd kick your ass as soon as kiss you" sort of way. Plus she almost always gets into one hot outfit to infiltrate some Swiss bank vault or hip Berlin nightclub each episode.

    She's deadly, but not unfeeling like a Terminator. She does what she needs to get the job done, and typically takes no joy in the pain she causes. That being said, she's not immune to feeling revenge.

    Alias typically doesn't use flashbacks. But they do a great job of using quiet scenes in-between the "jobs" to let Sydney come back to reality and reflect on the life she is leading. And sometimes those scenes make her seem painfully human.

    Sydney's relationships with her hard-driving but loyal father, her ex-KGB manipulative mother and her handler/boyfriend are complex and allow the show to explore different areas of her personality.

    If you haven't watched the first season of this show, you really should.

Underworld, Ultraviolet, Aeon Flux, and the list goes on. As a result most of them suck as movies as well. (The other thing that happens is that some of the successful iterations are reduced to supporting character roll, and so don't quite get to be what they should. Like, say, Trinity from the Matrix. I always thought that movie would have been better if she'd been the One.)

    Faster Pussy Cat, Kill, Kill is an interesting case. Varla was sexy deadly, but was as much the male fear of female violence as anything human, so even though I have to give her full props for hardcore, I can't quite give her full props for being an actual human.

    The TV shows that quickly spring to mind have already been mentioned. There are also a handful of animated females who get at the role for me. John already touched on Eboshi. I'd also add Lady Oscar from The Rose of Versailles. She's sexy, if androgynous, a killer swordswoman, and through the series has to deal with both her gender and her class as a double one-two punch that has fucked her out of her ability to be happy. The way the series ends is troubling, but not necessarily bad. Motoko Kusanagi from Ghost in the Shell also edges into this category, though the whole humanity issue is rather heavy handed at stake. (I have some memory of really liking Iria, but its been forever since I've watched it, so who knows?)

    Comic books have some of the best examples, the Catwomen, Black Widows, Electras, and so forth. Almost all of them have problems in their history, but almost all of them also have at least one run where they're allowed to be awesome, deadly, and still human. I'd also add Miss Misery from the Sleeper series. Any chick that can seduce a CIA super spy by kicking a guy to death in front of him is something sexy deadly, and the end of her story is certainly about her humanity -- though not always in the best of ways. And in fantasy land, Artesia is deadly sexy and human, and has a lot of orgies too.

      \n So, I'll explain a little bit about this part of the editing process. We have 11 archetypes in the game, each one gets a specific special power that can be used once per game. The archetype description is there to help you create your Lady, although ultimately the special power is the only limitation on your character that needs to be justified.

      If I do the writing correctly, the reader will read the description of the archetype, form an idea of it, then look at the examples and see both a) a bang-on example that he already knows and confirms his idea, and b) another example that doesn't fit and makes him say "oh, you can do that too?" Above and beyond that, it would also be nice to have people discover good movies/other media by checking out something on the list they haven't heard of, but that's just icing.

      Some examples are easy to line up to archetypes. The archetype Perfect is the chick who was created to be a killing machine. Major Kusanagi (cyborg) and (La Femme) Nikita (special training) are excellent examples. So is Selene from Underworld - 'cause she was made into a vampire and that dude her boss trained her to be bad-ass. But you could play a possessed chick (Exorcist, Prince of Darkness) and say Satan made her that way.

      A character like Alice from Resident Evil is hard for me to use. She could be Perfect, 'cause she wakes up in a lab, and she kicks some ass. She could be Black Widow, 'cause she has some bad flashbacks and isn't that her boyfriend? She could be the Underdog, 'cause she's pretty tough.

      My personal definition of Deadly is pretty lax - if one killing is good enough or the law, it's good enough for me. I don't think Jean Grey ever kills anybody... Dark Phoenix, on the other hand, is absolutely what this game is about. She could fit into several different archetypes - The Gorgon, if you want to emphasize her fearsomeness and telepathic blitzkrieg; Firestarter if you want to play her as a wild card; and you could make a case for Undying, Perfect, Elite, or Black Widow. Actually, I think maybe I'll list Dark Phoenix as an example under the Gorgon archetype, 'cause that one is harder to find good examples for.

      Storm? Huh, I dunno. She's not Elite if she loses her powers. Probably Underdog. Does she have some sort of martial ethic at all? If so, she could be Sphynx.
