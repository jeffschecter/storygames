  • Alex F
    September 2006
    Posted By: Neko EwenWhat Do You Think you Dislike About Non-Traditional Gaming?
    In this thread (p18) I was struck by this succintly rendered account of a kind of Sim preference.
    It's usually claimed these games are democratic for taking power away from the dictator and distributing it among the whole group. As stated, that's accurate. It's not accurate to say, as is also claimed, that thereby the GM becomes a player. On the contrary, all players become GMs. And that means that, with respect to the game world, the dictatorship has become, not a democracy at all, but an oligarchy.

    Because what has been disempowered more than ever before is the game world. It has turned into this unreal, potentially infinitely malleable foil for a collective ego trip. Where in sim games parameters are provided that make parts of the game world inviolate and which the GM is supposed merely to administrate, it now has no or little substance and logical consistency left by itself.
    It's weird because it's not really my bag, but I can see the appeal. Most people seem to be down with the constraints->creativity thing, and I guess "setting as constraint, rules as malleable" can work if "setting as malleable, rules as constraint does". I think Plume implies a similar idea upthread (setting consistency trumps rules consistency).

    • Arminius

      The discussion probably should continue (and is) over at theRPGsite but on this point I want to back Marco. Introducing the whole "taking advantage of a date" concept is rhetorically dangerous because it invites one party or the other to bring in a social hot-button that acts as a "get of this discussion free" card. "You('re saying that I) condone rape/racism/genocide!" are all good ways to end a conversation fast. However, if we were talking about, say, barrelling into the catcher at home plate during a neighborhood softball game, the importance of respecting unspoken assumptions (if you don't want to be a jerk) would be obvious, I hope. Or to give an example that's closer to the thing that's triggering the "don't go there!" language:

      There are cultures where female virginity is very important. In many of these cultures, due to local poverty and lack of medical/dental care, men from America are very attractive. (Note they may be immigrants from the same culture, or more likely children of immigrants who retain the language.) It's not uncommon at all for men from America to visit these cultures, take advantage of young women's lack of sophistication, flatter and seduce them--not necessarily lying, but behaving in a way that, in the local culture, coming from an ostensibly "good boy", could only be construed as implying intent to marry. Then they abandon them, causing psychological pain and social ramifications (e.g., no longer being seen as a suitable mate for local boys). No explicit rules are being broken. But the Americans also have some idea of the local culture, know what buttons to bush to lead a woman on, and are aware of the consequences of abandonment. They just don't care--at least, not enough to seriously examine their intentions, and probably not enough, if they're honest with themselves, to be forthright.
