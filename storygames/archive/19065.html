    \n I've seen the author comment elsewhere that the "nine bonus dice" line is the mistake. It should be 27.

    That said, I use a house rule because I noticed that with the minimum players (3) it would require ALL their dice to solve all three mysteries which means everyone's dead guaranteed. That's mitigated slightly by the fact that there are items that get you clues and bring dice into the economy but they are very few and it doesn't seem like the cost of buying an item outweighs the dice loss for buying a clue in that situation.

    So, I make the cost of buying the clues equal to 1/2 the players rounded down. So a clue costs one die in a 3 player game, 2 dice in 4 or 5 player game and 3 dice in at 6 or more player game.

    Jesse
