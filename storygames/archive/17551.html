\n Hi guys,

I know there are some Hero Wars and HeroQuest fans on this list, as well as some broader fans of Glorantha. So I thought I would point out that Moon Designs has a kickstarter for their new Guide To Glorantha.

http://www.kickstarter.com/projects/224590870/the-guide-to-glorantha

In full disclosure, I have been involved with this project as a reviewer. It's ready to go, it just needs help covering the costs of the print runs. It is not Hero Wars focused, instead its intended to be the encylopedia of Glorantha for anyone interested in that game world. It builds on some of the classic world guide products such as Genertela: Crucible of the Hero Wars, but includes Palmetela and the Islands, has been extensively reworked from Greg's archives and enhanced and updated for current understanding and playability. Jeff went to a lot of effort to define the 'look and feel' for the art so that it was consistent, made sense, and kept to a late bronze age and early iron age feel.

http://moondesignpublications.com/page/look-glorantha

The resulting art is the finest vision there has been of Glorantha

A lot of work has been done on rationalizing outlier elements like the West, and that holistic approach has pulled together the patchwork of Glorantha into a whole.

I've backed it, I hope some of you want to too.

Glorantha is one of my handful of gaming weaknesses, and a systemless setting work is exactly what I've been desiring for it. I don't usually want to have real books on my bookshelf, but I'll make an exception for Glorantha books.

    The reward levels on that Kickstarter page are pretty confusing, though, what with the various maps that might or might not be part of given reward levels. Like, should I get the "High Priest" at $150 if I want everything in hardcover, or are the mystery maps at "Rune Lord" worth the extra $50? Or is MoLaD at $500 actually worth the extra $300 for the maps? Takes some doing to separate the products from the prestige money-sinks; probably would be much easier to just wait for this stuff to come out for ordinary purchase.

    \n I think I've figured out the mystery wrapped inside a conundrum that is the Kickstarter game Moon Design is offering here. Basically what you want as a Glorantha fan is the $150 "high priest" package that includes both books in hardcover. One could make an argument for the "Dwarf" pdf-only package at $50, but at least for me it seems that if I'm going to pay that much (or rather, engage in Internet shoppery at all - I buy stuff very rarely), I might as well pay some more to have paper books. And if I'm getting books on paper, I might as well get ones that look good and are durable. The other levels and knick-knacks on offer here, on the other hand, are luxury items with relatively little practical significance; I did consider the paper maps at length for +$100, but decided that they're unlikely to be as useful as an atlas in book form.

    So yeah, I think that I'm going to make an exception here and participate in this Kickstarter. Now I'll just need to wait for the offerings to stop shifting around just in case something comes about and changes my evaluation.

    (And yes, I am old-fashioned enough to think that there must be easier ways to shop than Kickstarter. People seem to like the game show elements, though.)
