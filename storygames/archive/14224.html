    The GM role often makes someone the primary provider of many elements of a game, maybe even the sole provider of some of those elements -- but FUN is not one of those elements. Saying anything else isn't just misrepresenting what GMs do, it's misrepresenting what players do.

    Posted By: Accounting for TasteSure, there's probably something that driveseveryincorrect perception in this world, but I don't think that's an argument for letting them go uncorrected.
    \nWhether there are any specific *games* that hold the GM as the "provider of fun" I'm not 100% sure of that assumption.

    What I am 100% sure of is that there is definitely a cultural trend within the hobby where that is absolutely the case. I have been at the table where all of the following has happened.

    1) A die roll has gone bad and a player has turned to the GM or resorted to passive-aggressive behavior directed at the GM with the clear expectations that the rules will be fudged or a re-roll allowed or some combination of the two because the player is unhappy with the result and expects the GM to "fix it".

    2) A player is uncomfortable with another players' words or actions and turns to the GM expecting him to address it.

    3) A player refuses to engage the scenario despite given every opportunity to do so and then blames the GM for the not providing appropriate content.

    1 & 3 in combination are particularly insidious because THAT player is unhappy because the GM not only fails to provide specific oportunities but ALSO specific outcomes usually relating to the player's particular character "schtick" or "archetype".

    Are these expectations of specific games? No, probably not (though I have seen published material that promote these habits). I have encountered these behaviors enough to call them a cultural trend through out the hobby.

    \nThose texts are from 10-40 years old. Things have evolved significantly since then and even then it's impossible to say what percentage of the hobby actually followed that advice.     That said, I sure do love story games, or indie games, or whatever we call them. And I love the players even more. Seriously: you put out a PDF of a fun game and within a day people have read it, edited it, and helped you make it better. It's amazing. And there are so many great games too!
