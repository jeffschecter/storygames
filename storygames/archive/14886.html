\n Yours truly will be running the Singapore Sling transmission at FanExpo in two weeks from now. We just playtested it in a similar 4-hour format a couple of days ago (sold a copy to one of the players as a result, too - you're welcome, Jeremy!). I learned that providing half-baked pre-gens to save some initial setup time could be very useful in a con setting. I'll be using the saved time to better explain the resolution system up front, as that got a little confusing to do in-play.

(As a side note, I wouldn't recommend this approach for an ongoing home-game - making the characters, building their relationships, kitting them up with gear while tagging Connections for Favours is awesome, and a great start for a 3-5+ session game to get everyone invested. But at cons, players are more receptive to pick-up-and-play something more front-loaded, and often even see it as a fun challenge to play a character someone else created.)

SO. What I'm asking from y'all is to write up a character for me to use in the game. I could write them all up myself with minimal effort, but I'm concerned that by doing that, I'll be filtering out some really awesome character concepts subconsciously and becoming invested in the characters as being played a certain way - the game will be more "mine" and less "theirs", and that's not as cool.

Anyone can play this character creation game regardless if they own Technoir or not. The player's guide with all the instructions is publicly available here. Follow the instructions, with the following differences:\n
    \n
  • Unless you own Technoir with the Transmission and know who the Connections for Singapore Sling are, don't select any Connections.
    • \n
  • If you do own Technoir, pick 2 out of the 6 Connections, leaving the last slot blank for the player to fill in at the table. Feel free to pick a "suggested" relationship as well if you like, or leave it blank.
    • \n
  • Don't select any relationships with other Protagonists. They'll be doing this before play, too.
    • \n
  • Do not use any Connection Favours. This will be done right before play to seed the plot map.
    • \n
  • Only purchase up to 8 Kreds worth of key objects integral to the character concept. Cyberarm with claws for a street samurai, or specs / headjack for a hacker is fine, while a kevlar vest for an investigator maybe not so much, unless you have a really cool idea to justify it.
    • \n
  • When you "write a short description", also provide a clear idea of the overall character concept, not just their physical appearance/heritage/mannerisms. Anything that helps a player see what's really cool about the character is helpful, here.
    • \n
\nIf you're into the genre enough to have good sources of inspirations and ideas to draw upon, this will take maybe 10-20 minutes out of your day including the writeup. From the pool of suggestions, I will select 6-8 to bring to the table, of which 4 will be selected for play by the players.

I really appreciate your help! Thanks!
