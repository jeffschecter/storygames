What Don't You Understand (about Gaming)?

James_Nostack
December 2006 edited December 2006 in Play Advice
So in the "Everybody's too Consensus-y Groupthink-y" thread (http://www.story-games.com/forums/comments.php?DiscussionID=1951&page=2#Comment_45681) some dudes are like, "Hey we don't get satisfying arguments anymore."

And--though I've stayed out of the discussion mostly--I'm like, "Well, okay, what do you want to argue about?"

Which is another way of saying: "What don't you (you, anybody) understand about gaming yet?" Because odds are, nobody else really understands it either. I used to have a professor who said, "Make the easy questions hard, because really they are hard." And I think that applies.

So--I'm curious. This is a thread for anyone, from n00b's to gaming to old-timers, to ask a question that they don't understand about gaming. And then, people can split that off into other threads. But mainly I'm curious to see what's on people's minds.

For me: "I don't understand what a role-playing game is. How is it different than playing with toy soldiers? How is it different from collaborating on a novel? Is Mechaton, as Vincent, Emily and NinJ are playing it, a role-playing game? Is Dungeons & Dragons? (where's the difference)? Is Universalis a role-playing game? How is it different than collaborating on writing a novel?" (I have a theoretical answer, but that's best saved for a full thread devoted to this. This thread is just, 'What don't you understand?')

For me, also: "What makes a good setting?"

And for me also, "What makes a rich character?"

    Why does Panty Explosion require a GM (Superintendant)?

    It dosent. You can play Panty Explosion perfectly well without a Superintendenmt. So why dosen't it say so in the book? Well... We had intended to present the game with several different options, one of which was playing without a Superintendent. We never really got around to it, although we'll likely put up some information on that on our site at some point. Maybe.

    As to why PE ended up with a GM, I can't really say for sure. It just sort of evolved that way. Both Matt and I mostly play in gM-less games, so I guess thats kind of weird. I think PE is pretty GM light though. Ideally, the Superintendent is just the player in charge of the Demon. The responsibilty of creating a narrative lies as much with the Students as it does the Superintendent. More if your playing it "right" (the way I play it). Students choose their own Agendas, their goals that they want to follow through the game. They choose their relationships. They are responsible for narrating each others sucesses and failures. They can create scenes, and define elements of scenes created by the Superintendent. But the Superintendent, in a real way, is still calling the shots.

    Getting rid of the Superintedent is pretty easy though. In fact, if you simply make the Demon dice somthing that everyone can use to create conflicts and antagonistic situations then the need for the Superintendent disapears entirely. you'll have to sit down before the game and design your school together, but thats supposed to be something you do anyway, and you don't really need the Superintendent to guide you through it.

    If I ever go back and do a major revision of Panty Explosion I'll probably make changes along those lines.

    Does that answer your question in anty way what-so-ever?

    Jake
