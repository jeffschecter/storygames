\nCut from this thread, when I realized that this was starting to become a side-discussion:\n
In my older gaming groups in high school and stuff, we would often give a collective present to the GM: Usually it was a book or game resource for the game we were playing at the time. Frex, my friends chipped in and bought Steve, my high school DM, the Dark Sun boxed set the year it came out (together we played AD&D 2e for a few years, usually bi-weekly or so). That kind of thing.

Nowadays, though, the game sessions feel far more like collectives of friends, without a single regular GM (save me for my weekly group). I gave a new player to our monthly D&D 3e game a set of polyhedrals (as he was new to gaming, didn't have a set and was borrowing ours, and I had some extra tubes from an order I made to Koplow Games), but that's about it. In my weekly group, our GM is often Me. Instead, we often buy gifts (usually food, beer or random book/game) to The Person Whose House We Game At Regularly.
\nHow about you? XMas/B-Day presents for "The GM"? presents for other players? Presents for anyone? Why or why not?

-Andy

    \n All of us in the group GM, so there's no "The GM." The place regularly shifts between my home and my neighbor's, so there's no one host. In the gaming culture I was brought up in, we either pooled money, regardless of who is host or the GM, for food or we dealt with food for ourselves -- everyone is always on the same level.

    That said, when I go to conventions with a decent dealer's room or hall, I shop for post-con gifts for my gaming group friends. Typically, this is only at GenCon (and formerly GenCon SoCal), so it's more of a "Xmas in August" sort of thing. Since this is the first year without a GenCon SoCal, I bought Xmas gifts from another source: I gave my Mechaton-playing friends some custom Lego minifigs & weapons from BrickArms.com.
