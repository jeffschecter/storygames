\n Hi,

I want to run a game for my group, instead of always being a player, but I have a couple of problems. One is that I haven't run a game probably since around 1990 or so, so I need the game to be reeeeeally simple so that I can concentrate on running a good game rather than looking up rules. The second thing is that I want to do a story game, with a group who have mixed experience: I have played Universalis, FATE based games and idly read a number of 'indie' games another player is an indie game veteran, another has played a couple and the third none at all.

I thought maybe I would like to try something horror/zombie based or perhaps something a little weird, such as Don't Rest Your Head but I'm not too set on a specific genre. What do you think would be a good one to get my head round? I am particularly looking for ultra-simple rules and interesting ways story-telling is encouraged, having had fun playing Once Upon a Time. My first thought was to use The Pool, but I think maybe I'm looking for something with a background or genre already set so it's easy for me to spell out what the game is about quickly. I'm looking for anything that will help me run a game without worrying about too many things at once.

Any ideas would be very gratefully received.

    \n I used Lady Blackbird as my tool to get myself into running story games - it's very easy, and you already know about The Pool, which is one of the roots of Lady Blackbird. And LB is free!
    And the Keys promote interesting and different takes at the story depending on how the player takes it. It's one of my absolute favorite games.
    LINK TO LADY BLACKBIRD

    Something I've used more recently for easing some of my group into doing more GM-y things was playing Fiasco. Everyone acts as a GM to some degree, letting you ease into framing provocative scenes, but you're not being looked to by everyone to respond to their actions. You get some experience, but without the heat on you (or at least the heat is evenly distributed). Plus, the rules really get out of the way - it's a hell of a lot of improv gaming rather than rules interference.
    You could probably find a good Horror playset, and they introduced a Zombie playset add-on not too long ago.
    LINK TO FIASCO'S WEBSITE

    These aren't particularly creative solutions (they're both really popular here and in general), but hope you find something you like!

    EDIT: Zombie Cinema was also on my list of potential recommendations, but I haven't actually played. So that could totally work as well.

    \nDon't Rest Your Head requires a GM who can do two things well: run an impromptu action adventure, and base the specifics of that adventure off prompts and flags given to you by the players (ie, what they write on their sheet).

    It's not a hard game to run, but it does require those two skills. You gotta take a bunch of creative tidbits from the character sheets and weave them into a weird, surreal action-chase-spooky adventure.

    Zombie Cinema and Lady Blackbird are both good suggestions, too.

    \n I'd suggest talking to the other players in your group who have some SG experience and find out what they'd be excited to play (perhaps using some of the up-thread suggestions as examples). Since a SG is a much more collaborative effort than a traditional RPG, having these guys/gals actively contributing will take most of the GM load off you (and encourage your other players to contribute more too). IMO this will give you more breathing space than focusing on super-lightweight rules.

    Sometimes super-light rules require skilled players to engage with in a meaningful way and can feel flat compared with a traditional RPG if people try to engage with them on a "system mastery" basis. Obviously don't try to run any system that you're uncomfortable with but there is some middle ground in terms of the ideal rules (and this varies from group to group and individual to individual).
