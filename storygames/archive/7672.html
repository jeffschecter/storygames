\n So I was in the bookstore the other day and they had two awesome-looking old games that looked like a lot of fun. I've heard various good things about them, but I don't really know how well they play, and I'm a bit hesitant to plunk down the cash. The two games that they had were a mostly complete boxed set of Marvel Superheroes (minus the dice, but including some extra adventures and other stuff) for $80, and a still shrink-wrapped box set of the originial James Bond rpg for $25. They also had the first three still shrink-wrapped boxed adventures for James Bond which apparently go for a little more cash.

They both sound awesome, but I'm leaning towards the James Bond one, as a fun 2-player game (1 gm, 1 player.)

What do you think of theses?

    Posted By: David ArtmanGet James Bond now, and the adventures (which are GREAt at capturing the flow and scenes of the movies without being TOO railroady). Go. Get up and GO! You're gonna miss out....
    \nIt's interesting the way the James Bond RPG was, even all these years ago, firmly against railroading and "rule zero"

    From page 5:
    "the GM creates the scripts, presents the situation to the players, portrays all the secondary characters, and act as the final arbiter on the rules of the game - that is, the rules all have agreed to play by"

    The GM is not the final arbiter of the rules: the rules must the ones everybody agreed to play by...

    next page:

    "the James Bond roleplaying game is not like most role playing games available today. Those games are designed to make the GM all-powerful and give the NPC a much greater advantage over player characters. [...]. this game, however, is designed in the favor of the players. After all, what good is being James Bond if the game says you fail rather than succeed [...] While the GM will be required to give players every advantage, he cannot change the roll of the dice or hold neon signs to show the players the next correct move to make"

    So much for ""old style gamin", eh? ;-)
