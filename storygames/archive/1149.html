THE DRAGONLANCE THREAD

To the next level. I must admit, I'm actually wanting to get to Dark Sun BAD, but first I want to try a few other settings out, to see what emerges (and to whet my own appetite, so sorry about the agenda there).

PLANESCAPE.

Perhaps the most awesome, higher conflict driven setting for old AD&D 2e Out-of-the-Box. And yet, I never seemed to get around to playing it but once or twice? SIGIL, a city beyond time and space, floating in the center of the known/unknown universe, the Outer Planes. Basically the point of the Tenth Dimension: All possibile Universes merging on one point. Factions. Otherworldliness. Doorways to other planes.

...is it even possible to Awesome-O-Fy this setting? Make it cooler, possibly easier to ease into even?

I'm interested in seeing what folks come up with. Cause I'm almost blank.

    Here's my ancient webpage doing just this thing: Post Sigil Planescape.

    It's all setting, but it's my start at an attempt to:
    • Push the PCs into Hero status by getting rid of all the named, meta-plotesque NPCs.
    • Softening the boundaries between planes would offer more chances to push your beliefs around.
    • Societies replace sects and factions, in order that the PCs can easily start their own.
    I loved the old Planescape stuff. But yes, when I ran it, it boiled down to the typical "hook + adventure flowchart + maps" that was at odds with the "belief shapes the world, now GO!" attitude of the text.

    There's another semi-Planescape-inspired game I was working on that focused on the strengths of D&D. It could easily be transplanted into Planescape:
    • Four awful primal gods have dominated the multiverse. Sigil is the last bastion for hope, held together by the bravery of the survivors and the mysterious power of the Lady of Pain
    • These four primal gods are:
      • Life: At its most depraved and overabundant. Entire planes have given over to orgiastic cults. Others are seething masses of life, with no purpose save expansion.
      • Death: As violence and confusion. Death caused by war, famine, disease, murder, torture.
      • Decay: Rot, dust and forgetfulness. The eventual state of decay is the cessation of the entire multiverse.
      • Undeath: The weakest of the four, its aspects divided. There are those who blindly revel in the domination of Undeath, creating entire undying civiliations. The minority, however, realize that undeath requires life to begin and sustain it, and have formed an uneasy alliance with the living of Sigil in order to retake the multiverse for balance.
    • Characters all share the same overarching goal: To fight back against the madness of the Four. But their personal goals may be glory, wealth, power, ethical.
    • Conflicts are direct battles against the Four and the more subtle battles towards personal goals.
    • PCs may start as epic heroes, or advance from struggling footsoldiers to faction leaders.
    • Of course, characters can start as or become undead.
    • Instead of a strictly medieval/renaissance culture, Sigil's people and technology come from the entire multiverse. Stone-age barbarians wield machineguns. Egyptian mummies pack vampiric laser pistols. Androids learn to harness elemental magic.

    I disagree about the Planescape setting being solid as-is. The idea behind it is great, but the setting petered out pretty quickly with the supplement churn. Planes of Chaos is great, but by the time you get to Planes of Conflict and the Guide to the Elemental Planes, it winds up being pretty dull. I think a redesign would have to modify the setting, or simply take the first boxed set as a starting point and let the players develop the setting as the game is played out.

    As for the system, it would need to be overhauled. If you keep with d20, it would be served best by focusing on a combat-driven game or a bit of modification:
    • New social and philosophical feats.
    • Expand the reward system (if d20 doesn't handle it already, I can't remember) to provide XP for winning social and idealogical conflicts
    • Emphasized & possibly expand on the interaction between "talking" skills. Diplomacy, bluff, etc. Allow Player Characters to be affected by the results of failing these checks, rather than just the NPCs and monsters.
    • Introduced skills/abilities that easily and explicitly handle "belief = power". Or keep them as-is, since your PC's level is already a direct measure of power, which means that you are able to influence the multiverse by having higher HP, better abilities, etc.
    If I went with a whole new system, well, that'd be a whole project in and of itself. And this is more an "Awesome-o-Fied ideas" thread.
