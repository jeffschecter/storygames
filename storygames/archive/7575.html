\n There's this game I'm working on called American Wizards, and it's got this Corruption mechanic that's somewhere between Sorcerer's Humanity and Misspent Youth's Selling Out.

See, your primary source of effectiveness are these traits, which are dice pools named as adjectives and selected from a fixed list, paired with their Corrupt version. The idea with the Corrupt version is that it's the same thing, more or less, but taken across a certain line, into a realm where it becomes harmful, negligent, wasteful, manipulative, and/or self-destructive. But still useful, in more or less the same ways that the original version is useful.

The ones that I can't think of Corrupted versions for are:

Endearing
Graceful
Receptive
Resilient
Suave

And, to give you an idea of what I'm going for, here's the ones I've got so far:

Affluent / Opulent
Alert / Paranoid
Assertive / Belligerent
Attractive / Vain
Audacious / Insolent
Brave / Foolhardy
Calculating / Cold
Calm / Relaxed
Careful / Anxious
Composed / Aloof
Confident / Arrogant
Cultured / Snobbish
Cunning / Conniving
Driven / Compelled
Flexible / Tractable
Frank / Callous
Hip / Trendy
Intellectual / Pedantic
Nonchalant / Flippant
Open-minded / Credulous
Patient / Passive
Punctilious / Finicky
Quick / Hasty
Resolute / Stubborn
Ruthless / Cruel
Sensitive / Delicate
Shrewd / Cagey
Subtle / Obtuse
Thorough / Obsessive
Thrifty / Stingy
Vigorous / Brutish
Witty / Facetious

Any ideas?

    \nOk, as someone who self-identifies as a hipster, I am appalled at how many people don't understand the difference between hip and trendy. (Yes, two people is enough to appall me.) Jack Kerouac was hip. Kids in the mall are trendy.

    Nonchalant = casually unconcerned; Flippant = marked by disrespectful casualness.

    Being calm is considered ideal by wizards; being relaxed is foolish. A wizard can't afford to relax. He must be constantly vigilant, ready to act and respond impeccably when the abstract forces he's been stirring up like a hornet's nest decide to do something unexpected.

    Really, I'm surprised that nobody asked about Ruthless being a good thing.


    I think I'm decided on a few of these.
    Resilient / Numb is fantastic.
    Suave / Manipulative is fantastic.
    And I'm going with Graceful / Rehearsed. Been thinking about it, and, see, there's 4 arenas of conflict in the game: the physical, the mental, the social, and the abstract. I think it's a mistake to limit a trait to any one of those. I'd rather take the approach that, if the player is clever enough, almost any trait can be used in the given situation.

    This is also why I'm considering Receptive / Consuming. I didn't mean "receptive" as in "open-minded," just as in "capable of receiving." In all manners of reception, whether physical (like catching a bullet), mental (like comprehending a complicated thesis), social (like picking up someone's body language), or abstract (like channeling a force).

    I'm having second thoughts about Endearing / Cloying. I don't think I want it to mean nice & cute, necessarily (although that would be an option). I think that Gandalf is endearing, as was Benjamin Franklin (also a wizard in the game's canon). Captivating is a possibility here, but I'm not sure yet.
