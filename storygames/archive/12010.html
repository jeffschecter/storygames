\n Was just toying with this little idea, possibly for a competitive space exploration RPG. Don't know of any space exploration focused RPGs.

- At the beginning players secretly choose 1 of the 10 missions. They cannot be chosen multiple times.
- During play there are trophies that will change hands based on accomplishing goals, but there's always 1 fewer trophy than number of players (ie. musical trophies)
- The end of the game occurs leaving each player at a major story cliff-hanger
- At the end of the game, all players except 1 will be holding a trophy. The trophies are ranked, so each player has a victory rank at end of game.
- Your power to narrate your own ending depends on a) Your trophy rank and b) your mission type

For example: 5 players. There are 4 trophies, ranked 1-4. The end of the game occurs when the last player arrives at his major cliff hanger. Now you look at the trophies to see how everyone's story get narrated:

Player with no trophy: Failure -- everyone else narrates the 4 elements of his ending
Player with 4th trophy: Minor success -- can narrate 1 ending element, but the rest is up to 3 others
3rd trophy: Success with sacrifice -- can narrate half of your ending, rest is narrated by 2 other players
2nd trophy: Major success -- narrate most of your ending, with 1 player able to add one element to it
1st trophy: Total victory -- narrate your own ending

The type of mission gives a few limitations that must be followed during the narration of the ending.

I was also thinking maybe you start with the guy who has no tropies and work forward. That way the "losing" players get a chance to squeeze in the best stuff they can before the total victor gets to narrate the grand finale. It will provide him with a challenge to get the kind of ending he wants while still maintaining whatever narrative consistency he can with the endings previously narrated.

-- John M.
