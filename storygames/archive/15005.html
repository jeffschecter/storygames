\n This is a playset I am working on for a local convention in November and would love some feedback. It started as a cthulhu playset but it evolved into more of a small town horror theme. Ossipee is a real New Hampshire town with all kinds a weird stories related to it so most of the names are real places which I thought was cool.


The Score

Setting in brief
Founded on Indian land Ossipee is a quite little New Hampshire town nestled against Archers pond deep in the states interior. If you drove by it you may not even notice it and most would assume nothing much happens in Ossipee. The residents would disagree.Things happen in Ossipee, things no one speaks of. During the day the town a fa\xe7ade of normalcy prevails; except for the occasional knowing look between two people. At night the air thickens with the unspoken rising from behind the veil. Warnings abound of places you don\u2019t go, people you don\u2019t visit, and things you must never say.

    \n "To Get out of town" might be a problematic Need, as it can cause a character to want to remove themselves fromt he story (no fun for anyone) or it requires others to block that player from acting on their goals (better but not great). The other "Get Out" entries are really nice, though.

    This playset might do well with a customized Tilt table, that amps up the supernatural or horror in some way. Maybe the Tilt gives aspects of the horror in the town.


    The Town set of locations feel a little less exciting than the others. I'd really like to have them be interesting and a little bit more grabby. Still some down to earth color, but as they are now, they don't feel like a choice I'd see anyone taking. Maybe a brief note on a few places, pointing out why they're important to the locals? Or split them into other categories somehow?


    I like the playset. I hope to see it in action in November.
