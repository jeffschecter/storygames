\n What stuff should we watch, read, play?

1.Feel free to respond here in-thread to another link posted.

a. If you're looking to start a conversation, perhaps take your comment and create a new thread with it in the Stuff to Watch category.

b.Otherwise, to prevent too many comments in the thread, number your response (first response: [1]; second response [2]; third response [3]). If you see that you're the second person to post, "Wow, that looks cool!" to a post here, then put a "[2]" at the top of your post. Three posts ([3]) is the limit: The fourth person to post should just take their comment and create a new thread, to carry the discussion over there further.

Why?

Because these threads are great: We can link to little things being talked about on the web in general, on blogs, or other forums that might be noteworthy, without having to dedicate a new thread to each and every one (although that, too, is fine if you want to discuss the item further or "open up a discussion").

Responding to these posts with a comment or two is understandable (other times there might be no comments, just cause the link was self-explanatory: nothing to say, lots of heads nodding in agreement). But we don't want to create a huge discussion in the middle of the thread: Some folks get a lot out of these threads, and don't want to look back to see the "10 latest posts" just to see that it's a heated discussion of something from a previous page. Cool? Cool.

    \n Robin Laws Hillfolk game has reached it's last few hours.

    Hillfolk, "a game of epic personal interaction ..., brings new dramatic insight to character-driven roleplaying." While the original game setting is about Iron Age people, there are now many alternate settings proposed. Stretch goals contain things like the game going open source, Gumshoe going open source, a large amount of alternate settings, game advise, a second full-size book and (as both books have reached their maximum size), the "Pitch of the Month club", where there will be a campaign/setting pitch coming out each month, a bit like with Fiasco playbooks.

    \n I'll talk about the awesome significance of this later (maybe on my blog or something), but one of my favorite takes on the gaming scene is Naoko Kanazawa's "Piyopu Seikatsu" manga, basically her take on RPGs, the scene, etc, and lots of info from a woman's point of view.

    Anyway, I obliquely asked her through Twitter to consider making this two-manga series available at some point on J-Comi (the new free online comic reader site), and interestingly enough, she did! She was bemused that people would be interested in it, but I hold that it's one of the best takes on what we do, ever. Funny, historic, irreverent, and informative.

    http://www.j-comi.jp/book/comic/43781

    It's currently in Moonspeak, so only Japanese readers will get anything out of it for now, but maybe someone will do a fan translation of it...

    -Andy

    \n [Tempus/Assassin's Creed 1]\n
    Working on a game/hack inspired by Assassin's Creed and some other stuff. Here's most of the first draft.
    \nYou are just too cool. I'm confident that's scientifically provable.

    What an intriguing way to explore the concepts in Assassin's Creed, by mashing up these storygame elements! I've only had a chance for a quick skim, but I think I can see how the disparate pieces are going to come together. I've not played Bliss Stage, but I understand it has a 'other world mission' success mechanic. Is it your intention that only some characters will be wired up to the machine? And X invoking Real (world) Challenges- do the Bliss Stage mechanics have any suitable 7-9 type hard choice triggers? I'll have to re-read my copy of Bliss Stage tonight...

    Did you start from Assassin's Creed and cherrypick game elements to mashup and hack to suit? Or did it start with another inspiration- e.g. by wanting to do, I don't know, something more constrained around the Microscope process?

    \n [Tempus 2]

    Glad you dig it. Bliss Stage is based partially on Otherkind Dice and has "7-9" results on blanks (it uses Fudge dice, which are assigned to various objectives after rolling). The hack originally came to me as a Bliss Stage mod, thinking about Assassin's Creed but also Inception, Cloud Atlas, and Pastwatch. The Microscope elements became obvious once I started drafting it, as did the core of the game being +workspace (in setting agendas you have to pursue incrementally).
