\n These are the rules of a forum game; I don't want to play it in this thread, but I'd love to get feedback on the rules - I think they're solid but I may have missed something.

F1 Re: Esc is a game for people who are bored at work with just enough time to read a forum. The goal is to keep your creative juices flowing, shrug off the negativity, and escape from the grind.

No Homework accumulates in this game. Even if there\u2019s a game in progress, all you need to do is read the Seed and the most recent Challenge. As the game goes on, there\u2019s still never an obligation to "catch up" on the thread, or even to Claim a Prize.

The Seed starts the game; after pasting these rules, the OP writes 100 words and then issues a Challenge in 20 words or less. From then on each post should Answer, Cheer, or Claim a Prize.

Answer by writing a response to the latest challenge in 100 words or less. Don't Answer your own Challenge. Try to work in the same spirit as the seed, but there is no wrong way to answer a challenge. In the same post, issue a new challenge in 20 words or less. This challenge doesn't name a specific person, just what you want someone to make.

Cheer by quoting an Answer written since your last post and declare it a Winner. If you've never posted you can declare any Answer a Winner. Spectators and lurkers are welcome to declare winners even if they don\u2019t post themselves!

Claim a Prize after someone has declared you a Winner. You Claim a Prize by writing, in 120 words or less, on any topic you want (but not issue a Challenge). Again, try to work in the same spirit as the seed, and again, there\u2019s no wrong way to do it.

Crossposting with Honor If you find you have cross-posted with someone else, and your challenge appears below theirs, then your challenge is considered honorary \u2013 it can only be answered by a winner if they choose to answer it when they Claim a Prize. If you see this it\u2019s a good idea to edit your post and show that your challenge is with honor.

    \n Ryan - Goals depend on the challenges; essentially the Seed says "here's a theme" and everybody else plays with it in a low-key way, shaping it by writing Answers and shaping it further by new challenges. So the goal really is "hey, look at what I did!" and "hey, do something cool like this!" Writing in a vacuum is no fun and writing anything long for a contest is hard. I just want people to play in the sandbox and encourage them to say they like each other's castles. The Seed is basically helping people find their way to the sandbox.

    Jason - here's an example Seed:

    With over a thousand stars and hundreds of planets, the Pleiades star cluster is one of the busiest regions of space imaginable, and as the only region home to intelligent life, it is undoubtably the most interesting. At least nine distinct alien civilizations have inhabited the Pleiades, including the Alcyone, Electra, Maia, Merope, Taygeta, Celaeno, Asterope, and the lost civilizations of the Atlas and Pleione. Expanding outward from their golden star of Hume, humans are by far the \u2018youngest\u2019 interstellar civilization of the Pleiades, and yet they may hold the key to its most ancient secrets.

    I challenge you to describe the least humanlike of the interstellar civilizations of the Pleiades!
