    \n Well, it has rules for how to run the game now, and they're pretty darn good. Basically, the Imprint (the setting that you're playing in, for the uninitiated) has stats that parallel the character's stats, and the Imprint itself can take Pain like a character.

    \n So when a character (any character, protagonist or adversary) takes too much pain, you do a roll vs. their Archetype, and if the pain wins, the character "takes a hit" that reduces their Archetype number by the margin of failure. Once you hit 0 in your Archetype, your character is taken out. Which is actually really exciting, because it means that you get to reincarnate the character. Reincarnation is really interesting, b/c you basically get to choose which of the standard comic book methods for bringing a character back you want. New writer? New spin on the basic concept? Turns out there's some plot twist that brings him back? Whatever!

    So, adversarys take pain when they lose rolls, and could potentially be reincarnated. Protagonists have Bonds that they can shift their pain onto (i.e. Spidey having a heart-to-heart with Aunt May, etc) to stave off the roll; adversarys, instead of having Bonds, lose pain whenever their linked protagonist takes a hit. So, your character giving in to pain helps your adversary. It's very neat.
