    \n I was sure I posted this before.

    If running at a convention or another one-shot, have a pool of characters at the center of the table, with the ability to change characters, perhaps every scene, but adding 1d3/1d6 Static for every scene that had passed.

    Anyway, the more important to me idea was something that to me was always obvious: "Angelic Intervention" counts the transformation of you and others' organs to robotic (that is, angelic). If you see someone's hand as turning, or your own, it could frame a new scene/conflict.

    Here is a question I would like to see raised, without me providing an answer: When you "lost" an organ to the Angelic Host, do you maintain free will in regards to that organ, do you still control it? To what extent? Perhaps a conflict could be between you and the Mainframe regarding an action you/organ wants to take and the other does not, like saving a human sinner or striking them down (such as your wife, for loving you).
