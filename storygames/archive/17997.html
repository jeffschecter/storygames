    \n Cool, Doc.

    Some of the oracle cards for The Quiet Year posit other outside groups in the environment. With "a new planetary colony" as the starting premise, there are a few ways to best make sense of those outsider groups that are mentioned in the cards.

    The first is that they might be peoples indigenous to the planet.
    The second is that other colony ships might have landed here previously, but fallen out of contact or favour.
    The third is that they might be colonial groups sent forward by other nations / interests / planets.

    I don't think any hacking of the deck is required in order to play a planetary colony setting. All you need to do is replace the phrase "The Jackals" with "our long and problematic journey through space," and leave room in your framing for groups outside the community.

    I think that Contempt and the way conflict in the community is framed/staged makes The Quiet Year a good game to explore the moral and logistical quandaries that colonization/settling create, whether this planet has indigenous life or not.
