\n I suffer terribly from gaming ADD, by which I mean I rarely can keep myself focused on the game I'm GMing. I always come upon a shiny new game that I can't get my mind off of and it usually derails my group's current game. Does anyone else have this problem? How do you keep yourself focused and interested in what you're currently running?

    \n So I'm not sure I entirely understand. Is the problem that you can't help bringing your new shiny to the game and holding it in front of everyone's faces while they're trying to play a different game, or is it that while you're playing, you can't keep your mind in the game because you're thinking about that game that's at home?

    If it's the former, maybe restrict yourself to bringing only the relevant game books. If it's the latter, maybe you shouldn't GM. If this same behaviour exhibits itself in other aspects of your life, maybe you do have real ADD.

    \n I once had a regular DM who suffered from this condition, but he also had a group that was very insistent to run a long campaign and to always stay with the same characters.
    \n
    \nThe compromise that resulted was that the ADD DM constantly revised his current storyline to include features and mechanics from the new-shiny games. It really was a lot of fun, and it helped to keep him involved/interested in the current game. Of course the obvious downside was that with all the revisions, the poor man hardly had any free time to enjoy other activities.

    \n I don't have real ADD, I'm assured of that. The problem is really both of your examples Eryops. The problem is I find a new game online that looks killer, buy the PDF usually and then read it in my free time. When we get back together for our weekly game night I find myself really wanting to play that new game I just bought. I find my interest has waned in what we were playing before (which was likely also a shiny new game at one point).

    Meanwhile, my group would prefer longer term campaigns (6 sessions) but I have a hard time waiting 2 months to play that next new game.

    \n Get a second group. I got tired of waiting to run all the games I wanted to run, so I organized a group (via this very website) that would give me access to new games, particularly the fiddly little small-press games I kept ordering from the Internet. Now I play semi-regularly with my old group and focus on long-term stuff (usually more trad games), and then play semi-regularly with my new group, and focus on one-shots and short-form stuff (usually SGs, playtests, and indie games).
