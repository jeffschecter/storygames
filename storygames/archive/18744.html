    \n You could pretty easily replace Hx with Strings. It'd turn the game into post-apocalyptic relationship drama. I'm pretty in favour of that.

    1. Remove Hx.

    2. Any move that would give you +1Hx or -1Hx with someone instead gives you a String on them.

    3. Consider changing the Seduce or Manipulate a PC 10+ result to "If they don't do it, take a String on them - you've got power over them whether it's now or later."

    4. For Read a Person, consider changing the 10+ to "either hold 3, or hold 1 and gain a String on them."

    5. Rework some Skinner moves and Angel moves to be about Strings. Rework the Hoarder's Hoard moves to be an exchange of Strings.

    \n Good question! I helped run a longcon at GenCon for AW set in an icy wilderness. We used the following custom move to give Hx a bit more umph, heavily drawing on Strings:

    When you offer someone tenderness, care, or resources that keep the ice at bay, take one hold over them and take +1 HX with them. Spend the hold at any time to:

    - offer them an XP to do what you want.
    - make them act under fire to continue a course of action.
    - aid/interfere with their action as if you hit a 10+ on your roll.

    When you spend your hold, they take +1 HX with you.

    \n One thing I do with Debts (which are a cousin of Strings) in my urban-fantasy hack Urban Shadows is simply introduce an MC move Give Someone a Debt/String. Then you as MC can also control the flow of Strings whenever it seems appropriate as a soft/hard move.

    "The Angel just healed you and didn't ask for payment yet. Give them a String on you."

    "The Chopper called his gang off of you before they could kick your head into the dirt. Give them a String on you."

    "You just saved Joe's Girl life, and plenty of people saw you do it. Take a String on Joe."

    This does add some work to the MC's already busy job, but I have found that once you get into the habit it comes very easily and is fun to do.

    Mcdaldno and Mark T's ideas should still be used, no question! They mechanize Strings perfectly with those procedures. Just don't forget you can have a part in the String flow as well.

    My two cents.
