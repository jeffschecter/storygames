\n This was a little gem of a game released in 2004 and already gone. It's hard to find copies, even of the PDF, though it was released for free by Gold Leaf Games.
\n
\nAnyway. My point is, even though this may not be a Polaris-level Story Game with capitals and such, it is a really neat indie RPG, and I'm offering the free PDF to anyone who wants it.
\n
\nThe hook? It's a game made to be played over internet chat, and I really, really want to get a group together. Email me at hcotterson@gmail.com if you want the PDF, no strings attached, and hit me up if the game looks interesting and you want to play. I will, of course, GM.

    \n There were some other bits done for Unaris too. Sample characters and I think a couple of adventures. I want to say there was a map too or something. I've got this stuff sitting at home on the hard drive.

    If you don't have the stuff hansel, I'm perfectly happy to upload it to something like DivShare including the game pdf. Since everything was offered for free (including the game) from the website I don't mind reposting it so that other folks can benefit.

    As I recall, Robin Laws or Jonathan Tweet experimented with a sort of hack mechanic a while ago. It's an older book that collects a number of mini-games together. I'm not quite sure how well a similar hack would work for actual face-to-face play, but I thought it would be an interesting bit to see at the table. In some ways you've got the hack thing going when players are able to spend a resource and dictate facts about the world; hacking would be like taking it up to the next level.

    I really liked what I saw in CoU and really hoped it would manage to make some in-roads. Maybe it was another one of those "ahead of its time" sort of games.
