    \n I downloaded it today, because A) it piqued my curiosity, and it was really cheap. Plus, it struck me as frighteningly similar in tone and presentation to John Harper's DangerPatrol.
    I've only skimmed the rules and everything so far, but here's the lowdown that I've been able to gather:

    1: GM-less. It kinda sounds like everyone takes a turn framing a scene, setting up what's at stake, and everyone joins in to resolve it (didn't read too much on this, but it reminded me of Fiasco).
    2: Abilities are measured in specific sized dice, rolled and totaled (mm...maybe similar to Cortex+), and then compared to a random D20 roll. Difficulties subtract from the players' total. Thing that struck me as different, with normal abilities, you roll 1D12, +ability die vs. the D20.
    3: Characters have a list of details and hooks that tie him into the action. Didn't see how they worked, but they felt very DangerPatrol-ish.

    Couldn't get much else with my brief skimming, but I'll be printing out a copy at work, and taking a closer look later this week.
