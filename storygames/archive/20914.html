I may eventually publish this game in some form, but I hereby happily place the initial rules in the public domain.

A while ago I presented a game to my playtesting group which had players each coin three major facts about the world, inspired by cards such as "An important substance", "These things don't mix", or "Everything is as planned". Then we narrated scenes which, one by one, demonstrated that the facts established in the setup were all false -- that they were surface appearances (believed by, say, 95% of people) but not how things actually were. As you might expect, attempting to negate 100% of the setup facts resulted in a pretty convoluted story; not all the players were up for the degree of mental gymnastics it required. (Part of the issue here was that we played with 6 people, so there were 18 facts to negate.) Personally I thought it was great.

I've been thinking that softening the 'negate everything' mechanic or mixing it lightly in to another game would be a good idea, and eventually I came up with the following.
