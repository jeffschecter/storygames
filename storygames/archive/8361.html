\n I see people using this term a lot in theory discussions, but it's very hard to figure out exactly what it means from context, and there aren't really any good definitions.

I think you know what word I'm talking about - it begins with "f" and ends with "uck". It's often used when talking about Sorcerer, for example, as in "driving with fucks" and "the F-map".

It's obviously a hardcore theorists' term, but when people use it I sometimes find myself questioning whether it's based on actual experience, or just something they came up with on their own. Single-handedly.

    \nAh! But that is the brilliance of my anti-jargon plan. If you don't mean that particular permutation of the concept, then you don't say it!

    See, when you say "fuck" now, I have no idea what you mean. How could I possibly? The word has been around for hundreds of years and has been used as every major part of speech in dozens of different sub communities. It has become so very specialized in its application in the sphere of any individual group that it is really impossible to know what someone is actually meaning when they say "fuck."

    But if you dejargonify it, then everything becomes clear! The fact that you never say "I never got over the fact that I'm allowed to use language my parents wouldn't approve of and even though I left the house 20 years ago it still makes me feel like I'm a cool young rebel to use profane language that no one really cares about anymore" keeps me from thinking you mean "I never got over the fact that I'm allowed to use language my parents wouldn't approve of and even though I left the house 20 years ago it still makes me feel like I'm a cool young rebel to use profane language that no one really cares about anymore." But if you just say "fuck" I might think you mean "I never got over the fact that I'm allowed to use language my parents wouldn't approve of and even though I left the house 20 years ago it still makes me feel like I'm a cool young rebel to use profane language that no one really cares about anymore" where really you mean "Brand you ass looks so hot in those jeans that it gives me a boner, however I'm very uncomfortable with the homo-eroticism of those feelings so I'm sublimating it into an ironic statement instead."

    (For the rest of this post I'm going to shorten "I never got over the fact that I'm allowed to use language my parents wouldn't approve of and even though I left the house 20 years ago it still makes me feel like I'm a cool young rebel to use profane language that no one really cares about anymore" to the acronym "INGOTOFUCKTHEBASTARDASSHOLESATTHERCAA" as that is much easier to type.)

    My anti-jargon campaign is also clearly superior to JACN's "cocknobblery" for the same reason. If you slam your hand in a door and yell either FUCK or COCKNOBBLERY then what you're really saying is "I am surprised and in pain and am responding with a verbal declaration of such emotion in order to release some of the sudden surge of nervous energy in order to produce a countering surge of endorphins to numb the sudden pain, also at the same time I am trying to get attention from others so that they will give me sympathy for my pain or perhaps aid for my injury." Now, if you would just say that instead, it would make everything much clearer.

    I mean, imagine if you lie down in bed with your SO and you say to him "Want to cocknobble?" and he thinks you are saying the above definition? He will be very surprised and wonder how you hurt yourself, and you will get no deep dicking that night. Or even worse, imagine if you yell "Cocknobblery!" after hitting your hand on a job sight and the guys you're working with now think you want to put your penis into their body holes?

    Really, the use of plain language will make everything clearer for everyone. So next time you're in sharp pain and surprise, do not waste our time by yelling "fuck" instead make a clear and assertive deceleration of the emotional, physical, and social response you are having and how you wish us to respond.

    \nThe substantive form would be "smurffi", adjective/qualitative "smurffi" and verb "smurffata".
