\n Hot on the heels of GenCon's awesome Games on Demand, it's time to rock some PAX indie gaming.

Thanks to the great folks at PAX and some awesome sponsorship from Gamma Ray Games we'll have some space set aside in the gaming area. Indie RPGs On Demand is 306 (on this map). We'll have 6 tables set up for us. So, let's rock this.\n

Getting People There

\nFirst off, Gamma Ray will be stearing people who want to play down to us. Their booth will be elsewhere, a few floors up. Let's make sure that we steer people interested in the games back to Gamma Ray. I'll try to get as exact directions to them as I can ahead of time.

Last year we did some cards. They worked alright, but maybe not the best. Is it worth doing them again? I still have some from last year but sadly the location has changed.\n

Keeping Our Tables

\nThe tables are set aside for us but, ya know, PAX is a bit of a madhouse. I think our best bet is to make our tables feel set aside as much as possible. There's a couple of ways to do that:

First off, we can sacrifice one table as a greeter table. This has the upside of giving us a central location to show off what's available, greet people, and get them into games. On the downside it leaves only 5 (maybe 5.5) tables left for gaming.

PAX has also offered that if we can get them the design for a table tent that can be easily printed they'll see what they can do about that. Something that clearly says "Reserved" "Indie Games" and maybe "Gamma Ray" and "On Demand" too would be great.

The best way to keep those tables ours of course is to keep games happening at them. If you're at PAX and can run a game, it'd be great if you could stop by and see if there's demand. I'll do what I can to make the demo space a good hangout space for those offering to run games.\n

What Else?

\nSo, that's the current state. What haven't we covered? Who's going to run games?

      \n "Come play right now", right outside the room, sets up unmanageable expectations. People are going to read the sign, walk in, and find everyone is already gaming. Unless you want to keep breaking up existing games every time someone walks in the door it won't work. Even the "on demand" part is misleading, but a less so.

      I think a better option is something like:

      "new games start every 2 hours"

      (or whatever the interval is). Interested people can plan accordingly and you'll get a big kickoff (GPNW pickup donut style) rather than one's and two's trickling in and out, not finding somewhere to get in and wandering away disappointed.

      Less chaos. People are already pushing their boundaries and trying something new, so eliminate as much uncertainty & fear as possible. Chaos = bad.
