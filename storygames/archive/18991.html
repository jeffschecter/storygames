    \n Here's a bit from me (a playtester).

    As a story game, this is great. It provides enough prompting to inspire you, but also enough space for you to interpret the characters in different ways. It plays for long enough to comfortably fill a gaming evening with a pizza.

    As a historical game, it's also satisfying. It's got those little details which give life to the setting, but it doesn't overwhelm you. You don't actually have to know much about history to play it, but if you do, it'll enrich the game.

    Historical games always make me ask: will I enjoy playing a woman in this game? Are stereotypes the only roles available to women? The answer is: yes, you will enjoy it, and no, they're not. Take a look at the character sheets.

    (Btw, each character starts with a promise - which is essentially their early motivation - but it can be changed at will when you decide the character now cares about something else).
