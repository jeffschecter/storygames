    \n Booksurge is not a traditional POD service, but like a more expensive version of Lulu with less creator control. They charge you several hundred dollars ($300 if you have print-ready files) to set up your product at Amazon, where Lulu is free. Also, unlike Lulu, you can't choose how to price your products, but get a flat percentage (33%) of the list price. They also offer fewer print sizes and binding options than Lulu. You can still buy your own product at wholesale prices from them, but their printing costs are not really competitive, especially when compared to short-run and traditional POD services (Publishers Graphics, Fidlar Doubleday, etc.), which don't expect to also do distribution for you.

    \n Lighting Source, for a decent number of years now, has sort of had a stance that they only want to do business with established publishers, not independent authors. I don't know if they have this policy anymore, but a few years ago they had a policy of not doing business with any publisher with less than 10 titles in their catalog for POD printing.
    \n
    \nTheir reasons are fairly straight forward. Established publishers largely know what they are doing when it comes to putting a book together for print. With independent authors, and especially with folks putting forth their first book for print, its a turkey shoot at how prepared they are for that process. To illustrate, we recently had a client who was publishing his first book and he didn't know how to get us a single PDF document. He didn't know how save his Word documents as PDFs and for some reason he seemed incapable of merging his 45 different Word documents into a single document. So what he sent us was 45 different Word documents. We had to work with him with his fonts and then practically had to 'build' his books for him to make it ready for print. Lightning Source doesn't want to deal with that sort of customer. That's what companies like LULU exist for, to provide a web interface for the customer to stumble through so as to get rid of the human customer service hand holding. In truth, his book was such a mess, I almost declined the job as well and was going to point him at a couple indie game forums where I know folks could walk him through the process. But since he was also on a short deadline, we dealt with it and slapped his book together for him. lol.
    \n
    \nA large outfit like Lightning Source is set up with a turnkey POD printing setup, but it quickly gets encumbered if it has to deal with file review, file prep and customer service issues much. Given the shier volume of books they print, they have to focus on their core compentency, which is the actual printing.

    \nI haven't used Booksurge but I have used CreateSpace, which is also owned by Amazon, also gets you into their catalogue, and doesn't charge a huge upfront fee (though you do need to pay for a proof to be sent to you).

    Their customer service is very much at arm's length, which was a problem as they screwed up something on my order (the same thing three times, and the third one, in Amazon's Look Inside the Book, never got resolved).

    I would cautiously recommend them if getting into Amazon's catalogue is a big priority for you, but if you are doing small runs Lulu is better to deal with, has better bulk discounts, better customer service and has also sold more books for me (I presume because more people have a Lulu account than a CreateSpace account). If you decide to go with both, make sure your size is one they both handle, and be prepared to do two cover files because their specs are different.
