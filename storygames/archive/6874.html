\n Reverse Dungeon - Encounter Ideas needed

I'm looking to make many many "encounters" to have in my hip pocket for a new campaign I'm running. I'm looking for your inspiration and ideas.
Short, sweat and simple but COOL ideas.

Here's my one "need" ... my PCs are all playing the monsterous races in D&D fantasy... a "reverse dungeon" type setting. We have a:
- Goblin Paladin/Thief
- Gnoll Ranger
- Hobgoblin Wizard
- Bugbear Cleric
- Minotaur Warlock

I'm NOT looking for stat blocks or D&D specific ideas. Just cool medieval fantasy encounters.
The group plans to go "out into the country" ... OUT of the dungeons, so it'd be great to have ideas on how/what they'd encounter for little outposts, towns, villages, farms, and other normal human type locations, stuff with farmers, homesteaders and such (think of how adventurers go into dungeons, the HOMES of these monster... I want to do that in reverse!). But I also want to make them challenging and fun.
For Example:
The PCs stumble onto the small shack of Grizzly Adams - who happens to have a big bear pet!! etc...

I'm also looking for other neat wilderness and other encounters.


Any help?

thanks
-kev-

    \n The group finds one of the points of intersection between the Feywild and the mundane world that the Eladrin use to pass between the two.

    \n I might be off topic but an idea I would find very compelling for this reverse dungeon premise would be to make a parallel with the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and their missionaries. I mean I always had a tender spot for those guys in their twenties, going two by two in the streets of the Old World, very polite, very neat, very patient and trying to convince the average gnostic European denizens that "yeah Jesus latest apparition was in America". Transposed with your "monster" PC group concept, it could become something funny like a "goblin Peace Corps" where PCs try their best to show they're here to help but obviously face prejudices if not down right animosity.

    my two cents...

    \n So there's this gateway, right, that some monsters and allied mortals are keeping open so that other monsters can freely pass between this plane and their home plane. After all, most monsters that are more or less stuck in this plane as second class citizens, living on the outskirts of society, only gaining employment when a wizard stops by wanting to populate his dungeon or a warlord wants to sack a city. It's very much like being an illegal immigrant trying to make it as a day laborer. It's really tough. But it's nice to be able to send treasure back home to support your family struggling under the oppression of some demon lord or another, right? Or just to head home every once in a while to see how everyone's doing. And, to do that, somebody has to keep this portal open.

    However, recently, the passage home has come under repeated attacks from wandering mercenary bands of mortals. So you're being called back because, if the Keep falls, how the hell are you gonna get back to the Shadowfell for the holidays? Irontooth says they seem to be coming up from Winterhaven, so you're gonna start with hiding by the side of the road and jumping anyone who gets close. Maybe, after a few encounters, you'll try storming the town itself, going alley by alley and putting a stop to this nonsense.

    \n The party climbs out of the underworld somewhere near (or in) a town that has just seen a terrible crime committed. Everyone is outraged and wants to see the "monsters" who did this brought to justice at any cost.

    However, the crime was actually committed by some humans (maybe the Mayor of the town), who are now fueling these "monster" rumours.

    Will the PCs try to fight off the would be heroes? Or will they try to confront the humans and see if they will listen to reason; try to expose the real criminals?

    Possible twist: The PCs have somehow lost the entrance to the underworld that they climbed out of. They need to get back there... to safety. But only the humans in the village know where it is. (Perhaps it is in the village itself.)
