\n I want to play a multi generation campaign were:

1) You play characters in a powerful situation like nobles. They marry & have children. Then they do stuff and take decisions that will shape their children.
2) The children raise to power and a new situation and a new relation map is created. The children & their traits shape this new situation & relation map.
3) Now we kind of turn the table around and the players play the children of their former characters children. They confront their parents (shaped by the players former actions), raise to power, marry, have children and have to deal with new problems that will have repercussions on their own children.
4) End campaign or repeat step 2.

So I think I need a game were conflict generate fallout that will shape the children. Those fallout will later be use to create the next generation set-up.
Any suggestion of what to play or what to hack? :-/

I guess that I will also need something to deal with pacing.

    \n Games like Houses of the Blooded (and I'm sure there are others, but I've only played that one) are focused on nobility, land management, territorial expansion, political power, and, of course, inevitable doom. Par for the course, those nobles will make plenty of mistakes and enemies the whole time, leading to a rich tapestry of exploits for the children who seek to unseat them.

    Bonus is that you'd create a campaign map just by playing, complete with temples, ruins, palaces, warzones, etc. Plus, it advances time pretty well.

    Downside is there's a lot of fiction to swallow or discard, and it's relatively easy for characters to get killed off until the players grasp what's going on, so everyone surviving into adult-hood seems unlikely at first attempt.

    How long (in real time, number of sessions) do you plan on taking for each step?

    \n House of the Blooded seem very interesting. I was also told to check Hellas & Reign.

    Well the lenght of the campaign is a big question. I think I would like a session telling the character story before they had children and a other one when they choose their spouse and their early years of marriage. Then I would like to jump in time and play some session were their children have different age. So, I think that I would let years fly between each session.

    Hero's Banner: yeah maybe I am only complicating things and that I don't really need a system to deal with having fallout from conflicts shaping the children, etc. and I could just use Hero's Banner.

    What I would like, I think, is to have conflicts were there would alway be a stake about the children. Like a secondary stake that maybe you could sacrifice to gain your main stake. Like win this conflict vs your brother, but your first son will now get attached to him and will receive one of his traits.
