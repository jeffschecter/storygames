\n We've had a lot of threads about what was really cool and popular at this year's GenCon. Time to balance out all this yang energy with a little yin. To whit:

What brought the awesome, but was, to your knowledge, overlooked?

Don't point out that indie games are a very small market, and thus all count as sleepers. This is not clever; you will be viewed with disdain.

Did anybody see a game over at Simply Fun called Handy? It's sort of like Twister for your fingers. Two players turn over cards each indicating a finger. Then they have to support styrofoam balls between their hands, each using the indicated finger. Then flip more cards and repeat. Simple, but highly novel. I thought it would make a cool mechanic for a game about trust and betrayal.

    \n"Sleeper Hit" is a set phrase. It means something that was really good, but that wasn't commercially successful. In film, these are sometimes called critical success. It also sometimes gets used to mean something that was really good, but had such a tiny print run that almost nobody has seen it. In the world of videogames, you've had recent titles like Shadow of the Colossus, Ico, Psychonauts, or Beyond Good & Evil.

    \n Last year Mob Justice came out as an ashcan and got OK sales. This year it was out in a really slick re-worked version, with great art, and it doesn't really get any love at all. Even though it has now been polished for a year and made "just right".

    I figure if people start reading and playing that game they'll love it, but I'm at a loss to understand why it slipped under the radar. Maybe it was the year of the Faery or Pulp, rather than the year of the Mobster?

    I know we had a big thread on Passages, which was in a similar situation, so I reckon there are a few games out there in the same boat.

    So, um, I reckon Mob Justice could be a sleeper that people might start to "get" over the next year.
