    I'm really a diceless kind of guy, but some of those make me drool at the thought of all the design possibilities....

    In another thread which I can't just now locate, we mentioned dominoes. Thinking about this tonight, I realized some of the things you could do with dominoes as a substitute for dice - some of which you can't do with dice. Dominoes are of course based on the possible rolls of 2d6, and the Chinese ones are still just that, but the Western dominoes add a "blank" - which makes them 2d7. So if you needed a d7 roll for some reason (7 being an evocative number in many contexts), you can draw a domino and take, say, the left half.

    Also quite common are domino sets that go from blank to 9, which gives you 2d10 (or 2d9 if you remove the blanks before you start, or d9 if you just take one half as above). You could even use them for percentiles, but you'd have to toss a coin or otherwise have some way of determining which side you read as 10s and which as units.

    And if you needed some other weird random number generated, like 13s or something, you could make your own set of dominoes to do it with.

    Koplow has d20 numbered 0-9 twice. Of course they're pre-inked. Unfortunately, Koplow dice have a poor pattern in my mind. Originally, the 0-9 twice D20s had each pair on opposite sides. The Koplow dice have some pairs adjacent. Back in college, I was getting suspicious of the pair of Koplow dice I was using, and made myself a 10x10 grid and then proceeded to roll the pair 1000 times. The distribution came up rather uneven. Then I noticed that 7 which was one of the numbers that came up way more than it should have, was also one of the pairs right next to each other. My Koplow dice were carefully quarantined after that.

    I wish it was still easy to get decent 0-9 twice dice because I'd rather ban the use of d10s. I just don't trust their "non-roundness."
