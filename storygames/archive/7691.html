\nPsi Run session on September 17, 2008
\n
\nSo, I(SteveEG) belong to the The Toronto Area Gamers(TAG) Meetup group. I posted my first ever [PSI=RUN] session, ten days before I planned to run it. There was good respond to the game until I decided to record it as a PodCast, then I had people opting out!(even some small complaints) In the end I got 2 players; Sara, Stephen and Me(the GM). Luckily, three is the minimum number needed to play and a smaller group made for a more intimate game and less discussion; so the game moved very smoothly.
\n
\nWe found a quite place in the Hub; a University Caf\xe9. I set up the ICrecorder in a cardboard box with styrofoam and black crape paper over it, this left just the silver, side mics visible and placed a folded black T-shirt under it(less distracting and soften the table noise; dice!). Then we got down to business right away with character design, some game rules and concept discussion.
\n
\nWe decided to go with the very standard modern setting and the Men In Black ( MIB minus the Sci-fi gadgets ) as Chasers working for a massive and diabolical, Multi-National Corporation. ( Later revealed to be GEN-TEK )
\n
\nThe \u201cCRASH\u201d scene was an Avalanche on a treacherous mountain road smashing in to a large, black Semi-trailer Truck (18 Wheeler) causing it to careen over the embankment and bounce, tumble and roll down the ravine. The wrecked (under carriage is ripped away) Semi comes to rest at the bottom of the ravine near the snowy, wet, half frozen lake. It is spilling diesel fuel and sparks are flying from the broken engine!
\n
\nThe Runners find themselves hanging over the fuel by bungy-cords attached to the crushed roof of the Trailer. The back doors are torn away with the undercarriage and it miraculously sheared their chains without harming them. They are Free! Free at last. But, Wait! The Chasers may be near, they must RUN!
\n
\nI'm sorry, not going to write out the whole story. If you want more please listen to the Podcast! ;0)
\n

    \nChanges and Rule tweaks
    \nI did my own Character Sheets and a Large Category Sheet for dice placement, which I used colour highlighters on going ( BAD to GOOD ) red, orange, yellow, blue, and green for more visual representation of the categories results. I also laminated it.
    \n
    \nI changed the Chase and Capture Categories widening them a bit and giving the chasers more bite I think!
    \n
    \nCHASE
    \n1 : Chasers move Two Scenes closer to the Runners. RED (highlighter colour)
    \n2,3 : Chasers move One Scene closer. YELLOW
    \n4,5 : Chasers make no progress. BLUE
    \n6 : Chasers get stalled & fall back One Scene. GREEN
    \n
    \nOR
    \n
    \nCAPTURE
    \n1 : All Runners Captured. RED (highlighter colour)
    \n2 : Half of the Runners Captured. ORANGE
    \n3,4: One Runner Captured. YELLOW
    \n5,6: Runners Evade Capture. GREEN
    \n
    \nMy players like the changes and thought that PSI: Brainstorm should only come up on a 1 and have another sub-category result for 2.
    \nHhmmm... Maybe:
    \nPSI: 2: Mind Surge. People Critically Injured or Killed and Things get Badly Damaged or explode!
    \nHa! That could be fun, not that the existing categories ever stopped me from killing or blowing things up.
    \n
    \nOh I almost forgot I changed the Crossroad ( Epilogue ) choices to:
    \n
    \nHome, Turn the Tables, The Mission, Hiding, A New Family, Face to Face, On the Run, A Discovery and Lost!

    \nThe Characters that my players came up with were:
    \n
    \nPlayer: Stephen -- Character name after reveal: Jeremy -- In the Crossroads he chose: Hiding
    \n
    \nMiddle Aged, Biracial(Black/White) Man with green eyes and curly hair closely cropped on the sides. Reasonably fit, Well dressed but casually so; designer shirt, jeans, expensive shoes & watch.
    \n
    \n1. Why do I finish other people's sentences?
    \n( Never Answered )
    \n
    \n2. Why has no one rescued me?
    \nWe formed a PSI power group and all the other members were murdered by those that fear us! (the chasers/MIB)
    \n
    \n3. Why is Blue my favourite colour?
    \nBlue men group a Secret Society of Psi; blue represents the group. A poem(by Sara) is the mark of membership (pass phrase?)
    \n
    \n"Blue is the colour of running water.
    \nBlue is the colour of the limitless sky.
    \nBlue is the where the wild wonder.
    \nLook for the Blue door to let your mind fly!"
    \n
    \nWhich was just too cool, Go Sara!
    \n
    \n
    \nPlayer: Sara -- Character name after reveal: Elizabeth -- In the Crossroads she chose: The Mission
    \n
    \nI am ordinary, not extraordinary from what I can tell. I don't look different from other women I pass on the streets or not materially different. I seem to be of average height & weight. It doesn't feel like my hair has been washed in a few days, and my clothing is wrinkled. I think I am an office worker or something less exciting. I have no pictures of children, though I think I'm young to have them. No rings. I don't like the mousy brownness of my hair or the pale almost grey, non-exciting shade of my eyes... the only thing that sticks out about me is the seemingly painstaking measures of averageness!
    \n
    \n1. How do I end up in far away places?
    \n( Never Answered in game, but it was set as the psi-power question some sort of transportation ability. I do describe her Brainstorm on the podcast in the last scene. ) [ You'll have to go listen if you just have to know! lol ;0) ]
    \n
    \n2. Why is "3" the answer to everything?
    \n( Never Answered in game, although on the end of podcast, I think; Stephen alludes to how he might of Answered. )
    \n
    \n3. Did Jeremy get my message?
    \nJeremy is the player Stephen's Character and has a voice/text?(with pics) message from Elizabeth who is Sara's Character!
