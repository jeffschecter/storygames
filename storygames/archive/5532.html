    \n I haven't (yet) but these lines made me say, "Fuck, no" to Published by Lulu:\n
    Published By Lulu License Agreement (emphasis mine):1. You are granting publishing rights to Lulu to act as a publisher on your behalf to retailers and wholesalers globally.
    ...
    3. Lulu will assign an ISBN to the title and Lulu.com will be listed as the publisher in all bibliographic feeds.
    \nSorry, but I've got too much ego for that, not to mention I don't like the exclusivity presumption here:\n
    Published By Lulu License Agreement (emphasis mine):11. You may not make available to the public a duplicate project with a lower Lulu Marketplace price while the original is active in Distribution.
    \nSounds to me like my "convention discount" sales just got kaboshed. It also sounds like it wouldn't be possible to offer a retailer discount (i.e. 20-30% off MSRP, for registered retailers... but maybe Lulu already has "two-tier" pricing models worked out?). No... I'd prefer my PRINTER to have just a BIT less rights to my intellectual property (i.e. NONE).

    For the same $100, I'd do Published By You. ESPECIALLY, if this is for Scorn: T2BOP--you've got a "product line" going now; why shoot your "brand recognition" in the foot by giving even the impression that Lulu is the publisher?

    But, as always, IANAL;
    David
