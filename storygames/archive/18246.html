\n
\n Ran Apocalypse World for the first time (My first time GMing a non-DnD game). There were a few hiccups (like I felt that the Maestro D' wasn't part of enough PC-NPC-PC triangles or have any real reason to leave her establishments) but the biggest is one is snowmobiles.

The apocalypse is that climate change has created a new ice age in the north and vast deserts in the south. Cities are encrusted almost permanently with ice (I assume there's a brief spring/summer). Obviously motorcycles wouldn't work, so I replaced motorcycle gangs with snowmobile gangs.

The trouble is that while it's easy to make a motorcycle scary, whenever I try to barf forth apocalyptica with snowmobiles "... in the distance you hear the dark howl of their snowmobiles..." the players usually burst out laughing. I like a few laughs in my games, but Dremmer's gang is supposed to be scary or at least threatening dammit!

How can I make them treat snowmobile gangs a little more seriously?

    \n Snowmobiles sound like chainsaws.

    While motorcycles need to stick to well-trod trails in order to travel with any speed, snowscapes tend to be smooth enough that a snowmobile can travel in all directions and with a greater degree of visibility. It's thus harder to get to safety when you hear them approaching.

    They are sturdier, making it easier for a rider to shoot while mounted.

    A snowmobile can easily tow the bodies of its rider's recent kills. Trophies? Food source? Either way, Dremmer gets a lot scarier when he's got some carcasses dragging behind him.

    \n Motor cycles come with a certain "cool" factor built in; from dirt bikes to low riders; suicide machines to choppers. It's no wonder the jet skis of Water World didn't quite have that Mad Max feel. But what if motor cycle gangs rode "motorized velocipedes" or "self-propelled Dandy Horses".

    \n I been at a post apolyptic larp in the north of Sweden with snowmobiles, and they where fucking scary.

    Imagine this. It is -20c/-4F outside. That is so cold that you skin burns. The snow is 1m/3 feet deep, and covered with a crust and neither brakes nor carries your weight. Walking over it is a random sequence of the crust braking and you sinking thigh deep into the snow, and the crust carrying your weight, this time. Imaging trying to run during those conditions. Even walk. It is exhausting. At those temperatures getting out of breath or breathing heavily actually damages your lungs. Even if you don't have asthma, breathing that cold air can trigger asthma attacks is you start breathing to fast. Frostbite is something that will happen if you can retreat into the warmth into regular temperatures. Perhaps just a blister. But every time you get a frostbite, that patch of you will have a lower resistance to new frost bite for the rest of your life.

    During those conditions having snowmobiles roar, like chainsaws and surround you is fucking terrifying. On grounds where a motorcycle or a car can move with ease, so can you. You can walk, you can run, you can hide.

    On snow, where a snowmobile can move, you can't walk, you can't run and you can't hide because of the tracks you leave behind.

    You against people on motorcycles is a kick ass scene. You still have agency. You against people on snowmobiles are hopelessness and despair. You don\xe4t have any agency in that situation. You will die if you can't make it back into the warmt.

    \n A lot of the people driving around with "snow-scooters" or "snow-trackers" here in Norway makes their vehicles as tough and menacing-looking as any motorcycles you find out there. And they gives you a hell of advantage in the snow. Being on foot in the snow is meaningless, but even on skis or snowshoes you are hopelessly slow compared to a snow-scooter.

    When it's snowing you have a chance at hiding when on skis, as your tracks are harder to follow. If it's downhill you may have a chance at holding them on a distance, on skis, but forget speeding down a hill on snowshoes. In the forest though, you may have a sporting chance on snowshoes; it is hard to turn with speed on a snow-scooter, so going around threes is a bit hard to do. But then; in a post-apocalyptic landscape of frost there will be damn few threes around ...

    Once the laughter has set in, it may be hard to turn, but you should be able to do it by using some of the excellent advice in this thread. Good luck!
