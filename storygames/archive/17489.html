    \n Rise, ancient thread of music!

    I'm a big Metal fan. Last year for the northern California cons, I ran a very rules-light Fate game called The Spirit of Metal. Players got to make a mortal slug who becomes an over-the-top metal adventurer inspired by a band or sub-genre of their choosing. So you could be Den of Earth from the HM animated film, a guitar playing lightning mage, Nathan Explosion, or Eddie the Iron Maiden mascot, etc. Use of song lyrics and album titles for Aspects was heavily encouraged.

    Everything was created at the table, including a metal realm and mini adventure for each character. Each run became an anthology of adventures where everyone got to play a part, but with each act focusing on an individual hero. It was awesomely challenging and fun to run. Only about a quarter of the players were actual metal-heads, but most everybody got the gist of it. I'd help people choose a them and a band, if they needed it.

    My favorite session had one player who came with pages of notes he'd been taking on song lyrics in prep for the game. He played Eddie the Iron Maiden mascot, and every time we switched worlds he would change his look and weaponry to fit the new world. Pure F'ing Awesome.

    And yes, We also made a custom playlist for each session with an album by each band shuffled while we played. It was great fun.
