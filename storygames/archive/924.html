Mommy, Where do Monsters Come From?

Andy
July 2006 edited July 2006 in Story Games
OK. Schlock Fantasy world. Could be Magestrike! ULTIMUS, Forgotten Realms, Burning Wheel, etc. I'm curious as to new ideas for where monsters come from, like in 1-4 sentences.

Example: Here's where monsters come from in Final Fantasy X (the CRPG):

Humans have this essence, which must be Sent to (heaven analogue) by a priest via this "Sending Ritual". When people (perhaps animals, too, not sure) die without a proper Sending, their essence floats around the world like blobs of living-but-unconscious energy. As it floats away, that energy becomes darker and darker (angrier/hateful/etc). Eventually, they coallesce (sp?) into living forms, and those forms are Monsters. That also explains why Monsters attack living things at random.

Another one, at random (more "rational"):

In this fantasy world, monsters aren't "out there", rather they clearly have roots in animals and people (stuff like displacer beasts, gnolls, owlbears, etc). In this world, magic has a radiative effect that can cause bizarre evolutionary spikes in small communities of animals (or primitive people). Monsters are the results of that effect. Way-out-there monsters like Xorn or Gelatinous Cubes were originally regular animals that got hit with the magic effect, evolved, got hit again, evolved, and got hit again; Xorn were originally chipmunks. Gelatinous Cubes were originally bears.

Another one:

Monsters are just like animals. Monsters always have been, and always will be. There is no explanation for them.

Another one ("mythic"):

In this fantasy world, the Gods often walk the earth. One of the Gods, the God of Dark Creation or whatever, frequently travels the world and mindlessly creates bizarre living creatures without thinking of the consequences. Result? Monsters.

Care to come up with a couple? Looking for creative ones, "rational" ones, bizarre ones, etc.

    Every creature posseses unto itself an essence that is shaped by its pyschic states throughout its life. That is, intense emotions and thought patterns etch into this essence, which surrounds the souls of creatures. When that creature dies, the soul attempts to leave this dimension and moves to the next; however, the large amounts of energy weigh it down and anchor it to this world. If it can't reach the gravity of this dimension, it returns, shedding some of its energy and joins a newborn creature. This cycle continues until it can escape this dimension and move on to the next and accomplishes this by shedding the heaviest of these energies which are classified as Sin Energies, or sinergy.

    When, in an attempt to find a more 'humane' method of executing criminals, scientists discover this release of energy on the death of creatures with their execution method, it soon is developed as a technology not fully understood. Energy is ever abundant and the world enters a golden age, but one that is corrupt in the center, as the very electricity the world thrives on is suffused with sin.

    When these souls, unready for the next dimension, arrive in their new home, Others recognize that the cycle has been warped and erect a barrier between dimensions, making it harder for these souls and all souls to enter the next dimension.

    First order Monsters:
    Human souls virtually destroyed by these sin sucking machines return to this dimension, and either enter newborns or seek out their energy in the world's electric matrix. Also, souls that were ready to move on but unable to do so return, confused and upset. Thus:

    Ghouls:Corrupted newborns exhibit signs of congenital defects that science is unable to cure; symptoms include red eyes, necrotic flesh, and the inability to process cooked meats.
    Ghosts (in the Machine): Ghosts begin to haunt their old homes and lives, seeking to understand their situation, or destroy that which they can never have again. Some ghosts discover the scent of their sinergy, and seek it out the energy.

    Second order Monsters:
    Refinements in the technology allow scientists to take out a person's sinergy without killing them, either by leaving that last little bit or by forcing the soul to remain in the body.
    Zombies: Homeless people, kindapped individuals, and anyone that won't go noticed are taken to energy plants owned by evil dictators. Once sucked of their energies, they are tasked with the jobs that no one wants and seem to be complacent to it all; their souls were anchored to the body but are empty.
    Vampires: Special projects create individuals with that last little bit, and it forces the soul to starve for more energy. These vampires are fed either by their regeneration beds (coffins) or find their own energy by attacking others and leeching their energy.
    Empaths: Rare is the occasion that the electric field in certain core reactors goes a little wonky and separates the soul from its sinergy for but a moment. These individuals find that as they reprocess the energy of their lives they are given special insight into others and can read their sinergy with direct contact or, if powerful enough, at a distance.

    Third order Monsters:
    The next dimension, upset at the problem with the cycle and unable to resolve it on their end decide to come back to their previous lives and affect some change. There are two mindsets on how to accomplish this.
    Lycanthropes: Violent overthrow and destruction of the technology will be necessary to force humanity to stop corrupting the cycle. This will probably cause humanity to fall to a stone age if completed, but it will be neccessary to get things right again. Enlightened souls from the next dimension feel compressed by taking over just one being and absorb another for whatever characteristics it deems necessary. It shifts between these forms as necessary to acquire the abilities it needs.
    Changelings: Violence will only meet with more souls being sent too soon and worsen the issue. Change from within will be necessary. Englightened souls from the next dimension feel compressed by taking over just one being and use their ability to manipulate sinergy to change forms as necessary.
