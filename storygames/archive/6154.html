    \n You know Ryan, I'm all for people playing what makes them happy. I'm not going to cast judgment or look down on people who have a play style different than my own.

    That said, I feel that it is necessary to refute your idiotic claims point by point.

    1. Red Box Hack is not genre-specific. If you can't port it to the setting of your choice, you are an utter loser. Just last weekend we were rocking out with a game set in a space hospital being swallowed by a dying star. It was full of pathos. The Naga was our anaesthesiologist.

    2. Red Box Hack may be "unfinished", but that is only because Eric Provost, the author and designer, wants it to be the best game it can be. And it will certainly be more than 30 pages long, if you know what I mean.

    3. Setting stakes backwards makes no sense. Why do people feel the need to go and change things around just to score points on the Internet? "Oh, look at me, I'm edgy, I set my stakes backwards and play a game that has sex in it."

    You Wicked Age zealots really get on my nerves. Just cool it and play what you like without dumping on the vast majority of gamers, who play and love Red Box Hack.
