\n Hey, persons,

So, people get excited sometimes about the fact that The Shadow of Yesterday is out there under a Creative Commons license. But, the fact is, the newest edition of it isn't, really. There's copies of the ruleset in a few places on the net, and no version of the setting stuff. Even all that stuff is under a non-commercial version of the license, which isn't cool. I keep meaning to get around to it, but getting the entire text online is an ordeal for one person.

So I'm asking all of you. I spent the evening getting The Shadow of Yesterday wiki ready for anyone to enter the text of the game. (You have to register, and that sucks, but spam's a pain.) If you've got some enjoyment out of the game, and have a few minutes, please take a moment to enter some of the text, either from this site, or from the book, for the setting stuff.

After everything's entered, I'll revive this for a call for proofing. Once the whole text is up, then I will engage step 2 of the wiki - adding new content and making The Shadow of Yesterday a living document.

Thank you in advance for being spectacular, and helping The Shadow of Yesterday finally get its text where it should be.

    Last time I'm going to turn this into a "look at TSOY" thread, I promise, but: this morning, after a tremendous amount of thought, I changed the license of TSOY from a Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike license to a CC Attribution license. What this means in practical terms is that you are allowed to re-use TSOY for any purpose, without using the same license terms as I did. You still have to attribute me for the original work, but that's all. I, of course, take reuse of the license as a kindness, but I decided to quit requiring it.

    Jason,

    \n

    I've been very, very burnt out on publishing, as you well know. The major reason for this is that it became a commercial activity for me. However, I don't want to make trade goods. I convert my intelligence into sellable product for eight or so hours a day already. I want to make art, and art is a gift.

    \n

    So, anyway, with my thinking on gift culture and moral responsibility, I decided I didn't want to hold back part of the gift of TSOY any longer. I almost put it in the public domain, but that concept is US-centric, and doesn't necessarily apply in other countries. One of the most exciting things to happen for TSOY in the last few years is the emergence of translations, and I want to encourage that by having a copyright that works well in other countries. (I would be a liar if I didn't also say that of course I want attribution. The true currency of the indie RPG hobby is reputation, not money.)

    \n

    I believe heavily in the idea that sharing your ideas is a thing that benefits others, and so the ShareAlike licenses have always appealed to me. When TSOY was under them, it meant that in order to use TSOY, you had to share your ideas for it, too, and that would seem like it introduces even more goodness into the world. However, you can't force what is, essentially, a moral decision, and I think it actually counteracts what my goal is: to give a gift to others. To add strings to that gift makes it not a gift.

    The CC-Attribution license, has, as I found out when I researched it recently, some particulars about this. For derivative works - that is, when you're not just re-publishing the text as written - one should include my name, and the name of "The Shadow of Yesterday" somewhere in the copyright text. Here's an example, for an RPG that uses the same system and some text:\n

    Copyright 2007 Jim Dangly.

    System based "The Shadow of Yesterday" by Clinton R. Nixon.
    \nThe other part is if you have contributing authors, my name would have to be with them, no less prominent than the rest.

    I, of course, would not be a big stickler about this stuff, but that's how the license reads.

I'm obviously starting with d20, since it is what I publish mostly, but I would also like to support the small-press open systems as well, such as FATE or TSoY.

    I am not familiar enough with the system yet so as to tackle doing a rules appendix for the Solar System, but maybe someone who is well-versed in TSoY could help me out? I can send you a free copy of the main product for inspiration and the d20 rules appendix for possible ideas, so you could do a TSoY take on the release as well. The main product is for sale, while all rules appendixes are offered free.
