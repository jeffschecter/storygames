\n Two friends and I played Lady Blackbird last Wednesday evening. Lady Blackbird, Snargle, and Arkam were all NPCs; Jeff played Captain Vance and Scott took up Naomi Bishop. After breaking out of their brig and taking out some Imperial marines (Naomi Bishop is a killing machine!), they directed Snargle and Arkam to disable the Hand of Sorrow's engines while they sought out Lady Blackbird herself. I decided Milady had been recognized as a noble and thus was being held in the Executive Officer's quarters awaiting some gentle but persuasive interrogation. The PCs broke in just as Lady Blackbird was about to attempt fly over to the Owl using her weather magic (questionable). Vance teleported her there instead, and moments later more marines approached. Injured and angry, the protagonists barred the door, then tore through the decking (Bishop's Secret) and escaped to a lower deck, covering their escape with one of the XO's expensive oriental rugs. They had also widened the hole in the hull created by Blackbird during her attempted escape, making it look like they had jumped out into the Wild Blue.

Sneaking through the Hand of Sorrow, they made it to the hangar deck, where they could see the Owl lashed to the imperial cruiser by several anchor chains, as well as a chain catwalk about a 100 m long connecting them. They could make out Snargle and Arkam on the Owl, working furiously to loose it and get underway. As they sprinted across the catwalk, marines began shooting from the hangar deck above, and the Owl's gun started turning toward them! Lady Blackbird was attempting to strafe the marines, but being unfamiliar with gunnery she cut the catwalk instead. Bishop slid down the collapsing chain bridge onto the hull of the Owl, but Vance, caught on the other side of the break, swung away from the smaller ship, hanging on to the severed bridge for dear life. When the end of the bridge swung back into view, Vance was nowhere to be seen, presumed dead.

Snargle, daredevil goblin pilot that he is, snatched up the controls of the already moving Owl and careened around the hull of the much more massive Hand of Sorrow. There was Vance, perched on one of the imperial cruiser's bristling gun emplacements. They picked him up, and tore into the Wild Blue as the lumbering Hand of Sorrow, her engines sabotaged, slowly listed starboard and slid into the noxious reaches of the Lower Depths...

We had a blast, and the mashup system of TSoY and The Pool played really well. Initially, it seemed like all their tags would make the players unstoppable, but it didn't turn out that way. As the stakes mounted and their pool dwindled, things started to go wrong, and complications abounded. I'm sure that's the game design intent, so well done on that score.

One beef I have is with the Captain's name; as cool as "Cyrus Vance" sounds, it's also a real person. Maybe change it?
