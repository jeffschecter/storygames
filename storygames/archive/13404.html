    \n OK!

    Horse Fever is a new board game from Cranio Creations. You play evil people fixing horse races in the thirties. It's got really charming, funny color layered over a game that includes betting, tactical decision making and a bunch of horse races. I brought a copy home from Lucca so I'm not sure how available it is yet in various places.

    Cool things:

    * Many emergent and non-obvious strategic choices throughout
    * The awesome color
    * Three different modes of play, including a party game and a family game
    * There's a Fiasco tie-in playset (only in Italian now but we're working on that)

    Not so cool things:

    * I lost really bad to Steve last night
    * The cards and components are internationalized using shared iconography, but many require reference so there's a ton of flipping through the manual to see what your latest evil trick actually does. It's like if every character in Bang! only had its special power in the rules rather than on the card. A minor annoyance.

    \n Horses are rated by odds (1:2 down to 1:7) and there's a race track consisting of a series of spaces ending in a finish line. Once races have been fixed (with beneficial and harmful cards placed face down on various horses) and wagers have been placed, the race occurs over phases. In each phase you draw a card, which tells you how many spaces each horse moves, by odds. This is very clever, because it is engineered to favor the 1:2 and hurt the 1:7, and you have to factor that into your betting. Individual cards might say "this horse moves 4 in the first phase" or "this horse always wins a photo finish" or "If in first place, this horse moves zero". There's also a tiny bit of randomness added to the racing with a couple of dice that move one or two horses an extra space or two.

    In practice it is pretty tense and surprisingly fun.
