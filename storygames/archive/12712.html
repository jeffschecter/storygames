\n I'm running Dread for my friend's birthday. (You can see our last game here.) This time, the session based on movies like [Rec] or Dark Water. The players are couples living in an apartment building that gets locked down for some reason.

The players thought it would be cool if random blocks in the tower were marked with a description of an inexplicably creepy scene. If you drew that block, then that's something that your character sees. Stuff like:

This room is bigger than it should be.
A whisper comes from the walls.
You see footsteps on the ceiling.
The furniture is suddenly rearranged.
You hear a call for help.

Any other ideas?

    \n Yeah, I played it on Friday. It went pretty well. They started off with some initial animosities towards each other, but eventually grouped up to follow a trail of blood leading upstairs to a vacant floor.

    Upstairs, the hallway was longer than it should be, but they continued on to one of the rooms that shouldn't be there.

    The reporter took pictures that revealed people in anachronistic dress and static over their faces, all gathered in a circle.

    First, they found a pristine empty room with bright daylight shining through the windows. They were actually way creeped out by this room and didn't go in.

    Across the hall, they entered a room that was night time, with lots of furniture covered in tarps. They peeked underneath the tarps to find a stack of bodies. Indeed, they realized all the "furniture" here were dead bodies. Well, one was almost dead. A small child with no eyes and an indistinct bloody orifice in the lower half of its face grabbed at one of their legs. Meanwhile, another PC ran over to the window to use the fire escape, only to find that the city outside was from at least 50 years ago.

    Eventually they escaped to the hallway again. One went downstairs to the lobby to try running out the front door. The rest went to the daylight room.

    Through the window of the daylight room, they could see the same group of people in the reporter's photos. They looked like normal people, but in the same anachronistic clothing. All in a circle watching someone with a giant pair of scissors cutting a red ribbon. They were watching the grand opening of the Tower, the 100-year-old apartment building they all lived in.

    Meanwhile, the one who went downstairs shoulders down both lobby doors. (She pulled a block for each door and finally the tower came down!) She exits the front door to find herself surrounded by glass and steel buildings covered in neon lights. Behind her is a pile of rubble where the Tower Apartments once stood.

    That's how the game ended, with each PC lost in the wrong time.
