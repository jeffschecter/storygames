    \n I have a long-standing tradition of really, really intending to do NaNoWriMo each year but then having something come up every November that sucks up all my free time. Some years it's something unpleasant (getting really sick, frantically scrambling for enough cash to keep from getting evicted); other years it's something wonderful (moving across the continent to be with the one I love, getting totally wrapped up in a brand-new hobby).

    This year is no different, as we're just about to kick off on a new online collaborative writing/freeform RPG project.

    I might never actually participate in NaNoWriMo, but it certainly seems to keep my Novembers interesting.

      Posted By: WolfeCongratulations Edward and Shane!
      \nThanks! Good to know that you were watching over my shoulder. I had decided to finish it off today, but when I was so close last night I couldn't resist making a mad dash for the finish line--and I'm so glad to be done now.

      It was way more work than fun for me this year. I won last year too, but ended up with something I was a lot happier with. Last year was fantasy, which came quite easily to me. This year I wanted to do sci-fi set far in the future, like DUNE or something (and I think that might have worked fine), but at the last minute I switched to a sci-fi techno thriller set within the next two-hundred years--because I really enjoy reading those when they are well done. The problem is that they are _hard_ to write, because I kept running into scientific problems that I didn't know how to solve. I'd always come up with last minute solutions that would do for a work-around, but I'm sure that there are plot holes that you could fly a rocket through--which I don't feel was true of my fantasy book from last year. It largely became a screed about my atheism and politics. It will need some heavy revision before I could show it to someone without hanging my head in shame. Major characters completely disappeared without explanation!

      Still, I would say it was a positive experience for one reason especially: last year I wanted to run away from my keyboard and I haven't looked at that manuscript since then. This year, even though I want to distance myself from what I just wrote, I still feel the urge to write and write often--in fact, I was wondering just as I woke up this morning how I would be able to get through my day without writing some fiction since it's become my routine. I kind of hope that next year I won't even do NanoWriMo, because I'll be too busy writing already--that would be the best possible outcome. We'll see!

      Anyway, thanks guys for rooting for me and for each other and congratulations to everyone who made it. I was cheering for Wolfe and Neko even as I saw them cross the finish line. I hope the rest of you can pull out a win as well!

      Thanks everybody!
      --Shane
