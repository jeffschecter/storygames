    \n Interesting! I'm a professional musician, so I'd be happy to chip in if I can.

    This seems like a pretty good start, assuming you're willing to be a bit tongue-in-cheek.

    I like your idea of using "NOTES" as a term for points spent to take actions.

    Maybe "groove" is something which refers to your various status effects? So your "groove" can be strengthened, slowed, weakened, etc.

    It's worth thinking about whether your musical metaphor is specific to any particular time period, culture, or musical genre. That will affect the terms you'll want to use pretty strongly, since every musical genre has different concerns, and often different terminology to go with that.

    (Mechanical abilities = techniques sounds pretty solid to me. I'd keep that.)

    You might want to look into some more high-brow musical theory for more terms, as well. For example, terms like phrase, antecedent, cantus firmus, retrograde, contrafact, etc, might serve you well, depending on how obscure you're willing to get.

    \n Also a trained musican (although not professional), I agree with Paul that Points == Notes, and I also see "Groove" as a good candidate for Positive Status in the sense of "flowing along, no impedance". The opposite of that (Negative Status) might be "Glitch".

    But you really do need to define the musical genre/period to give a better idea of which words may be acceptable and which not so much. Even specific instruments in some cases ("shred" is specific to the guitar, obviously). In general, I don't really know if I am writing metaphorical stats for a Musician or for Music itself.

    That said, here are some more ideas for your given words:
    Perfect Defense = Crescendo
    Okay Defense = Power Chord
    Failed Defense = Staccato
    Positive Status = Groove
    Negative Status = Glitch
    Positive Status Effects = Steady, Rhythmic, Shakin'
    Negative Status Effects = Erratic, Offbeat, Undanceable

    And some for not-given words:
    Speed/Pace = Tempo
    Mood/Feeling = Timbre
    Happiness/Joy = Major Chord
    Sadness/Sorrow = Minor Chord
    Uncertainty/Change = Augmented Chord
    Weirdness/Unease = Diminished Chord
    Warning/Alert/Alarm = Trill
    Stealth/Sneaking = Pizzicato
    Spotlight/Monologue = Solo
    Return to Location or Retcon = Coda
    Recurring NPC/Concept/Front = Motif, Refrain
    Collective/Mass/Area Defense = Wall of Sound
    Confusing/Confounding/Disrupting Attack = Polyrhythm

    \n AsIf's right on the money.

    You have to narrow things down a little bit, unless you're consciously trying to lasershark this thing.

    One type of game might speak of shredding, riffs, and things being pitchy (ahem, ahem), another might talk about retrograde canons, the cantus firmus, fugue, species of counterpoint and mode, and a third might talk about leimotifs, exposition, atonality, and recapitulation, while another might be all about pocket, groove, bounce, blue notes, and the wailing interval.

    I suppose a possible "genre" might be "basic music education", in which you might simply review basic elements which are taught in basic music classes, like solfege, scales, chords, keys, introductions, repeats, and codas, and so on.

    I could even see an "orchestra"-themed game where your stats are things like Brass, Strings, Percussion, Chorus.

    It all depends why and what for, in other words.

    \n That sounds awesome.

    Shounen fighting anime tend to have a very flashy aspect to them. Move names are always dynamic, colorful, and easily imaginable. Asif's technique of Color Noun-Color Noun-Base Noun works really well, and you can swap one of the Color Nouns in for an Adjective. Make as much sense as you want--sometimes, it's totally hodgepodge, other times there is some attempt to make sense. Being hyperbolic is a common technique.

    The main point is to tag a signature move with an easy-to-remember name that evokes images. Chromatic Tempo Riff! Thunder Scale Chorus! Infinite Harmony Rhythm!

    Thinking about music and shonen always brings up Soul Eater for me. The main male protagonist uses piano music to focus his power with the other protagonist to devastating effect. (resonating their soul wavelengths, a concept I really like for some reason)

    Anyways. I'm not trying to attach a full setting to the game mechanics. That said, I'm still pursuing the perfect set of overly stylized kewl terminology if anyone wants to make more suggestions.

    Finalized Terms

    Health = Harmony
    Damage = Dissonance
    Rounds = Measures
    Turn = Beats
    Points spent to take actions = Notes
    Narrative Abilities = Riffs
    Mechanical Abilities = Techniques

    Non-Finalized Terms

    Perfect Defense = Counter
    Okay Defense = Defend
    Failed Defense = Hit
    Total value of a strike/reaction = Effort
    Opponents in combat = Enemy
    Special achievements in combat = Goal
    Non-sentient threats to PCs in combat = Hazard
    Defensive maneuver, opposite of strike = Reaction
    Action taken to resolve a Goal, defeat an Enemy or remove a Hazard = Strike

    General Status = Conditions
    Positive Status = Boons
    Negative Status = Banes
    Positive Status Effects = Strengthened, Quickened, Protected
    Negative Status Effects = Slowed, Exposed, Suffering, Weakened

    Combat Scene = Battle
    Character Scene = Scene
    Free Form Scene = Filler
    Out of combat rolls to resolve actions/scenes = Task Rolls
    Task Roll; rating required to generate 1 success = Difficulty
    Successes generated by a Task Roll = Success
