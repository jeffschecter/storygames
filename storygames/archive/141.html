  • ffilz

    I guess my question would be why you want to separate spiritual from mental and social if it isn't religious?

    When I have encountered people with issues over the tying of spiritual to religious, it's been folks who adamantly don't want to associate with some bad experiences with religion, but yet they want to access the feelings people get from religious experience. So basically, they view "religion" as the elements of religion that they see as "bad" and something else as the elements that are "good". And since spiritual is a religious word, it's "bad" also.

    This is something I've never understood, but then I didn't grow up with bad religious experiences. So to me, it would almost be like someone deciding that since they had been fed terrible food as a kid, that all cooking is terrible. And when someone serves something they like, and talks about the joy of cooking it, they cover their ears and yell "I'm not listening, I'm not listening" because cooking is bad, but tasty food is good.

    Frank
