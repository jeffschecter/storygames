\n I am in the process of working on interiors for John Wick's ([I]7th Sea, Legend of the 5 Rings game designer)[/I] House of the Blooded. Which I also had the opportunity to be part of the playtest of the rules and setting. It is a great game.

Playing a game you are to illustrate, makes it a ton of fun. Working with someone like John, is even more fun on top of it. I'm to do 13 illustrations for each of the 13 chapters. Because we did NOT want to show DIRECTLY what the ven look like (the culture that HotB is about), we are doing it as a series of archaeologist sketches and notes. Show indirectly through a cameo and a wall painting, what the ven are... and hopefully, leaving a lot to the imagination of each table that plays the game.

Coming up with the notes is a hoot. Because we can be so academic... and because we are purposively jumping to wrong conclusions.. which you can discover when you play the game.

John has given me permission to post some of my art as I go along on this assignment. So I'm sharing a bit of current passion. I hope it intrigues... and oh yeah, one more note... the runes are an alphabet I made up. The key will be in the book.

    Posted By: Saint&SinnerI love the way the Ven are suggested but not defined. Do you end up defining Ven culture in the game?
    \nPretty much. I put as much as we know about the ven in the game, but what we don't know far and away eclipses what we do know. So, I made a decision not to make things up, but report on the ven as faithfully as I could.

    The game is about ven mythology rather than ven history. Like playing a Greek myth game vs. a Greek history game. That really frees up a lot for the players, giving them plenty of room for exploration.

    \n\nJohn Wick \n\n\n
    \n
    \n\nMay 2008 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n My goal with the art--something Storn jumped all over--was to give an impression of the ven without actually defining them. The different playtest groups have all embraced different visions of the ven. One group saw them through the Conan lens: they saw the ven as Hyperborian nobles. Another saw them more like Moorcock's Dancers at the End of Time. Still another thought they looked more like fantastic Victorians. Each group defined the ven for themselves, and that was half the fun of running the game.

    Because of that, I didn't want the art to illustrate the ven, but give suggestions as to who they may have been. Storn loved the idea and his magnificent art helped it along.
