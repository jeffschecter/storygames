\n I have posted this at lumpley's forums and have gotten no reply in the last couple of weeks, so I'm trying Story Games to get some advice. I have been furiously hacking Apocalypse World for my own Conan game (I have seen SimonC's 'World of Conan' too).

The sticking point I've had is coming up with a Weird move to replace, "Open your brain".

I have created a move called, "Swear an Oath". The character can swear an oath about some objective they are passionate about completing.
examples:

"I'll varnish the floor with your brains!"
"By Mitra, I won't let you die today!"
"Bel's Balls, it won't slip through my fingers again!"
"I'll dine with Crom the day a scurvy Barachan gets the best of me!"

Roll+Weird ('Wyrd' in my hack). On a 10+ hold 1+1. On a 7-9 hold 1. The character may spend his hold in any effort to see his oath through. On a 10+ they are also invigorated by making the oath and get +1 forward now. On a miss they are mocked by the gods; take -1 forward and deduct an experience point, if any.

So... how is this move? Thoughts? Suggestions? Alternative ideas?

(It may be important to note that since there is no psychic maelstrom in the Hyborian age, I made Augury the same as the original 'Open your brain' move, but it is only available to Cultists, Sages, Sorcerers, and Witches).

Thanks

    \n

    First of all - moves don't have explicit misses. Miss is for the MC, not for the character!

    \n

    When you 'spend your hold' you need to explicitly say what spending your hold does. Your text could meaning anything from the delightfully abstract (and completely bizarre) "You can spend this hold on any move where you have hold, as long as it's for your oath," to "Spend your hold, 1 for 1, to help or interfere with yourself. On a 10+, help/interfere as with a 10+; on 7-9 help/interfere likewise," which again is bizarre but a little more sensible.

    \n

    The 'invigorated by making an oath' thing is kinda strange too.

    \n

    Here's how I'd do it:

    \n

    When you swear an oath to someone, write it down. At the beginning of each session, roll+Wyrd. On a hit, the gods smile on your dedication. On a 7-9, you can roll+Wyrd instead of Hot and Sharp, when your goal is in sight; on a 10+ you can roll+Wyrd instead of Cool, too.

    \n

    When you break or abandon an oath, you get +1 Forsworn (starts at 0) and become an oathbreaker. While you're an oathbreaker, you take -1 forward. At the start of each session, roll+Forsworn; on a 10+, you can't roll+Wyrd this session. On a miss, stop being an oathbreaker.

    \n

    EDIT

    \n

    When you fulfill an oath you swore, cross it out and mark experience.

    \n The thing that's tripping ME up here is that Conan would presumably be a low-Wyrd character, yet you'd think he'd be SUPER AWESOME at swearing oaths. My preference would be to still make the Basic Move for Wyrd be about the interacting with the Uncanny in a risky and unsettling way. the Oath move is invoking the Gods, but not in the uncanny "the Abyss also gazes back" way that open Your Brain does.

    Maybe "When you converse with Strange Spirits or Old Gods, roll +Wyrd..." it wouldn't come up as much (ie you couldn't just do it whenevs, you'd have to find a situation where there's Spirits or Gods), but it would be more in keeping with how Wyrd would most likely be used in Conan. Are there any more general-purpose ways that people get their Wyrd on in Conan's stories? Maybe "When you speak of old superstitions or unsettling legends, roll +Wyrd..."

    Shreyas' Move writeup is really hot in and of itself, though. But it's only good if your Wyrd is higher than Hot, Sharp or Cool, which again underscores the disconnect with Conan and Oaths.

    There are probably some reasonable other oath bonuses, like, hold 3/1, spend 1-for-1 when your goal's in sight to get +1 on a roll.

    \n

    The reason I say, "when your goal's in sight," is because it compels the characters to action; they have to do a reasonable bit of legwork to get into the place where their oath means something, but then it actually means quite a bit. So instead of having degenerate situations where dudes just swear oaths and then don't do anything about it, you get dudes pursuing their oaths wholeheartedly and marking xp for it (I consider that whole suite of three moves as a unit; they don't work right without each other).

    \nPerhaps: When you swear an oath before an audience, take -1 ongoing with the audience until your oath is complete as they know what you now must do. Once the oath is complete, hold 3. Holds may be spent, 1 for 1, to take +1 forward with the audience.

    The idea being that if you swear an oath to one person they have expectations and once you fulfill that oath you have considerable sway over them. On the other hand, if you, for example, swear an oath before the kings court, you could return victorious and use your good name to curry favors among members of the court. The -1 ongoing only hurts you if you swear an oath and then stick around with out following up on your oath, I'm guessing that won't happen a lot.
