    \n The longest we had someone as their Darkest Self in the Monsterhearts games we've played was a Witch who was Dark for most of a session.

    She played it as going mad with power and made a number of very very terrible decisions regarding the summoning of demons that drove much of the rest of the season.

    It definitely brought the awesome.

    The playbook I've seen people struggle with constantly is the fae. I'm really interested in hearing how people handled fae Darkest Selves, as they have always seemed to cause their players significant confusion.

    \n We played a one shot where the Mortal turned the Infernal dark pretty near the beginning and nothing really snapped them out of it, but it didn't seem to change much, I'm not sure that that doesn't say more about how the Infernal was being played than it does about the game itself though.

    Having played as Fae and Mortal I can see how going dark self for any length of time would be tough to be interesting with. The Fae option I didn't have to try, but I agree with the advice that it seems quite a vague darkness. The Mortal when played it went dark near game end and it was largely meltdown time. It fitted with the one shot format we were playing but I'm not sure how it would sit with happening as part of a multi-session game.

    \nI've mentioned this before, but when the Hollow in our intermittent game goes Darkest, she pretty much tends to stay that way for the entire rest of the session because our group finds it a little challenging to find someone/thing that can meet the conditions to snap out of it.

    It brought ridiculous amounts of awesome, in a gonzo way (most notably, when she was being questioned by police about the day's insane outbreaks of violence at the school, looked at her reflection in the one-way mirror and started bashing her head against it), but it surely was difficult to talk her down.
