\n Andy's told me that Game Chef will take place late this year, rather than early in the year, as it usually does.

So, I'm going to steal the time slot, and run Little Game Chef from Friday 20th to Sunday 29th March. Ingredients announced on Friday 20th, deadline for entries Sunday 29th. Like Game Chef, but smaller scale: which will be nice, because Game Chef's pretty big these days.

There's two other judges: I'll let them out themselves if they like.

I know what the theme's going to be. It's a theory word. In the early days of Game Chef, the themes were "Simulationist" and "Gamist"...and...this is a similar theory word, which I think would be useful to write some games around.

There'll probably be a length restriction. I'm rather tempted by John Harper's two-page thing, actually, although that would be pretty restrictive.

(Edited to add: there'll also be four ingredients, in the normal Game Chef style, which will be general words).

Any questions, exhortations to do things a particular way, opinions on the length restriction, covert abuse or shouts of "awesome"?

Graham

    \n That's useful feedback so far, thank you.

    It'll be a theory word, plus four ingredients, which will be unrelated words.

    The particular theory word I'm thinking of is the reason I want to do the competition. Undoubtedly, using a theory word will put some people off; equally undoubtedly, whatever theory word I choose, it'll put people off. (Imagine if I chose "Participationism", for example, which is exactly the sort of thing I might choose. Many people would just have no desire to write a game around that).

    And, if some people are put off by theory, that's kinda fine. There'll be other competitions: Game Chef proper will be along, later in the year. I'd prefer a small number of entries, by people who are keen on the theory word, than casting the net wider.

    On limits: I think that, if we include design as part of the judging criteria, then a two page limit is the way to go. That way, you could squeeze thousands of words into two pages, but you won't win. If we exclude design, judging purely on game text, a word limit sounds better.

    I'm undecided between the two. Usually, I'd exclude design, but having seen John Harper's thing, I'm quite keen to see what a well-designed two pages would be like.

    Graham

Other than this point I favour minimalism, I never really understood the modern set-up with feedback groups and whatnot.
