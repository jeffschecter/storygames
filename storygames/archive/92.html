    Anyway, a translation:
    Do you mean by sound or actual letters? I'll assume phonetically for those without the right fonts!
    Note, E is "eh", A is "Ah", just like Esperanto.

    Why is this game different from all other games?
    Ma nishtana hamischak haze mikall sh'ar hamischakim?

    1) In all other games we roll either when it matters or when it doesn't. In this game why only when it matters?
    Shebecall hamischakim anu megalgelim keshe meshane vekesheze lo. Bamischak haze rack kesheze lo.

    2) In all other games, we play characters of any sort; in this game, why only one kind?
    Shebecall hamischakim anu meshachkim shear demuyot. Bamischak haze rack sug echad.

    3) In all other games we do not get authority to choose our direction even once; in this game why do we get it twice over?
    Shebecall hamischakim ein anu mekablin zchut bchira afilu pa'am achat. Vebamischak haze shtei pa'amim.

    4) In all other games, we play in any manner; on this night why do we sit around the table together and follow what is in the book?
    Shebecall hamischakim anu meshackim becall derech. Bamischak haze culanu mesachkim bederech achat.
    (This doesn't follow the 4th line in the Hagadda)
