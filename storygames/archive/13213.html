\n Another discussion of "real world tools/things that may help with game sessions" vibe (notebooks, book carriers, etc).

So recently, I've been getting a lot of mileage out of my furoshiki(s). It's basically a wrapping cloth, imagine a thick cloth about 45cm (18 inches) to a side, or more.

Here's Wikipedia's take: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Furoshiki

Here's a pic of one of my faves, and some dice/fake money (used in one of the games I run a lot) on it to show size: (kanji/hansi furoshiki)

And very relevant, a pic guide to various ways to wrap a furoshiki cloth, all extremely simple:
http://jordiferente.files.wordpress.com/2010/05/060403-5.jpg


I commonly use the "Otsukai Tsutsumi" for books and dice, sometimes the "Suika Tsutsumi" for big piles of dice.

The cool thing about it is that you can drop it on the table, it becomes a centerpiece for the game. And cleanup is a snap, as you just throw everything (dice, pencils, chips, etc) into the center and do the Suika-Tsusumi to wrap it up and take it away.

I also used it at GenCon this year when GM-ing on the last session I ran: Basically, we had to move our game because of me forgetting my badge and the staff members (who I had had conversations with every day until then) kinda flipping out and making us move our game to another common room: The game I was running had all sorts of dice, coins, etc. I threw it all in the middle, wrapped it up, carried it to the new room, dropped it down, and we were playing again in just a minute or two.

Anyway, it sounds like there's kind of a mini wrapping-cloth/furoshiki boom going on. I found some gaming uses for them. :-)

-Andy
