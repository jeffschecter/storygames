\nChampions of ZED is the first and only comprehensive Sandbox edition of the world\u2019s first fantasy RolePlaying game! Champions of ZED weaves together the scattered rules and intentions of original game authors Dave Arneson and Gary Gygax, including revisions drawn from the long lost Beyond This Point Be Dragons manuscript.
\nCoZ is built from the ground up for sanbox play, following the methods of the original game. It hearkens to a time when players built their fantasy worlds together, one hex at a time, when vanilla settings from far off publishing houses simply did not exist; when a handful of dice and creative minds crafted and conquered worlds of their own.

    \n Hey Geordi,
    \n
    \nTook me a while to get back to this board....
    \n
    \n1. There are a few points of crossover between D@D and CoZ - the rules for getting lost frex, but for the most part the games are quite distinct. D@D as you know focuses on the uniques aspects of play going on in Blackmoor. CoZ is very much OD&D, fleshed out with the information left us by both Gygax and Arneson.
    \n
    \n2. Only in the combat system, which is the offspring of my "OD&D with CHAINMAIL" write up. CHAINMAIL is, of course not OGL material firstly, and secondly has to be substantially houseruled to make work with OD&D anyway. Anyone familiar with CM will feel right at home when running one of the three combat methods given in CoZ. I use the "Fighting Capability" statistic and break it down between "Fantastic" and "Mundane" opponents as seen in CM and as Gygax instructed in the Strategic Review FAQ.
    \n
    \n3. Around 175 pp without illus
