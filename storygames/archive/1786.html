    On the bio-tech side: I've wanted to see a bio-fantasy setting since I visited the museum and saw an exhibit of ancient Egyptan artifacts and Dale Chihuly organic-shaped glass sculptures. That's a mind trip if there ever was one.

    I can see the various classic D&D races actually being bio-magical self-augmentation. If you're human, you can undergo a procedure to become an orc. An orc can become a dwarf. Elves into orcs, etc. The idea being that though you could theoretically do whatever the heck you want, there are definite trends and fashions that persist throughout society despite the unlimited options. Eh, maybe not.

    The one thing that would be cool about this setting is that everything is grown. For whatever reason, perhaps limited mineral resources, all technology might be an outgrowth of advanced animal husbandry and breeding. If you need a tool more advanced than a hammer or a blade, there's a creature that can be bred for that purpose.

    Counting to Infinity is, in both modern and ancient senses of the term, awesome.

    Minx, you have nailed many things that I think are needful for this game.

    The background that I've thought of is as follows:

    In the distant past, we colonized the galaxy, and came up with wild and crazy science and technological shit that was Indistinguishable From Magic. The problem was, we didn't evolve with it.

    So much for that spiral arm of the galaxy.

    This made a lot of people very angry and was widely regarded as a bad move. This made a lot of Luddites even more angry, angry enough that power-hungry Evil Dudes seized control of the Terran Republic "on the Luddites behalf" and used thier Schweet Toys to take everyone else's Schweet Toys. They sat on the "most dangerous" ones, pretended not to use the inconspicious stuff, and conspiciously destroyed the flashy stuff.

    The remaining scientists researching, say, memetics or string theory or nanotech were either recruited to do nothing or driven underground, harried, attacked by missile raids, attacked by plasma cannons, attacked by slamhounds, blasted off the Net, subjected to memetic assault... after 1000 years, most of them that lived that long are old loons with 4 parts mystical bullshit to 1 part Science, but who can blame them?

    But your character knows things. Like - the end of the universe is very fucking nigh. And you and a bunch of buddies are going to stop it.

    With the power of Science.
