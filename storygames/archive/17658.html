\n Inspired by this great thread "Historical events that might make interesting games" created by @vulpinoid, I thought I would create a thread specifically on historical personalities which could make great NPCs, player characters, or whose life could inspire an entire game.

Like the other thread, I'm NOT looking for the obvious (in)famous people like Hitler, Churchill, Ganghis Khan, Wild Bill Hickok, etc. More slightly lesser known personalities whether tragic or heroic or just plain weird.

To start it off here are a few I thought of:

Julius and Ethel Rosenberg - a couple who were Soviet spies living in America. They were executed in 1953, which was the only execution of civilians for espionage in United States history.

David Karr - mysterious businessman and possible American/Soviet double agent. Great short film made by his grandson

Joan Pujol Garcia - British double agent nicknamed Garbo who fed tons of bad information to Germany during WWII. Without his help D-Day may have been a failure. Pujol had the distinction of being one of the few people \u2013 if there were any others \u2013 during World War II to receive decorations from both sides, gaining both an Iron Cross from the Germans and an MBE from the British. NPR article

In the modern day there is John McAfee, whose life is pretty much exactly a Fiasco game: House hidden away in the jungles of Belize, hiring gang members as body guards, loads of firearms, young prostitutes, huge fan of using the "bath salt" drug MDPV rectally, on a quest to find a super perv powder drug, other scam business ventures like "observational yoga", fighting dogs, and then to top it all off running from the police when his neighbor is murdered.

I love the idea of a game about fire brigades in London in the late 1600's - firemen rushing from the tavern to a fire and back to the tavern for one last pint before returning home to their families.

    \n I would submit Ludwing Wittgenstein one of the most important philosophers but also a guy with some interesting problems and an interesting life. Might be as influential in his field als Churchill in his, but still often overlooked. Could show up in weird places too and give some insight, weird characters out. Also his earlier teachings about language can seem rather creepy when applied directly. If a GM can pull of a character talking that way it really is a nice quirk.

    Gottfried Benn was german expressionist poet and medical doctor. Who again had an interesting biography and wrote some morbid stuff. He was a pathologist and having him show up in that function during a mystery game could be a cool nod.

    \nEmily "Mickey" Hahn, 20th century American writer. She studied mining engineering in college, drove across the country with a girlfriend in a Model T, went to Europe for a while and then walked through the African jungle, where she was nearly arrested for espionage by the Belgians. She wound up during the 1930s in Shanghai, where she "never meant to become an opium addict." She interviewed Madame Kai-Shek, married a British officer, was interned during World War II, and finally made it home to the U.S. where she continued writing for The New Yorker. Also, she had a pet monkey. I love her.

    \n Speaking of awesome woman writers for The New Yorker, Lois Long. She basically was the the platonic ideal strong willed flapper woman of the roaring 20s. Under the penname of Lipstick, she chronicled her nightly escapades across New York City speakeasies drinking, dining, and dancing writing with loads of wicked sexual innuendo and humor which was quite "unbecoming a lady" for the day. She would come into the office early in the morning still drunk, still in her evening dress, and not having slept after a night partying. Another story from here:\n
    One evening when managing editor Ralph Ingersoll stopped by the office after regular working hours, he was dismayed to find the magazine\u2019s star cartoonist, Peter Arno, lying naked on a sofa with \u201cTables for Two\u201d columnist, Lois Long. Years later Long said, \u201cArno and I may have been married to one another by then, I can\u2019t remember.\u201d On second thought, she added, \u201cMaybe we began drinking and forgot that we were married and had an apartment to go to.\u201d
    \nOne of her famous quotes is "Tomorrow we may die, so let's get drunk and make love."
