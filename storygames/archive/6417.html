Hey, ever'one, Shock: was out of stock for almost three months there due to printer humbuggery. I just got new copies in, though, and they look spectacular. You can get it from me or from IPR if you've been waiting for it. If you've ordered from me in the last two months and have been waiting for your books, they're going out today (in about ten minutes, actually) and should be at your doorstep in a couple of days if you're in the US.

\n

I'm quickly accruing a lot of printer stories. If anyone is thinking about publishing on paper and needs advice, I'm very happy to share what I've learned.

    I've played Shock: at the dawn of civilization (we called it Rock: and there might be more to it, but the game will take tweaking to get it to do what I want. We can talk about that elsewhere, I guess). The important thing is that history is crashing into the protagonists. That might be plague brought to your people by Europeans along with their horses and guns, or it might be the first encounters between Homo Sapiens and Homo Neanderthalis. Maybe it's the advent of iron among the Etruscans or the effects of the Ghost Shirt (let's say they work!) collapsing American expansion. Or, hell, MK-ULTRA on mid-20th century politics.

    \n

    I actually want to do some alternate history stuff with it. Maybe at Nerdly!

    \n

    Basically, if you can imagine it showing up in a science fiction magazine, it should work fine. Put it at a turning point in history, name the most obvious cause of that turning point, and go from there!
