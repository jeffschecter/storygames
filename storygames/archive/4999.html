\n Some friends and I are working on an anthology of Norwegian games translated to English. It's looking good, and hopefully it'll be on Lulu this summer.

As a sister project, we've set up a blog of free games. Some of these will be in the book, but possibly in a different form; some games will only be in the book.

There's a lot of good stuff there now, and more to come. It's all been tested and revised. Sometimes even (my God) edited. Don't expect fancy layout, however.

N\xf8rwegian Style. If you want to know what\u2019s going on in Norwegian gaming, but nobody ever actually taught you Norwegian.

    \n "N\xf8rwegian Style" (note the "\xf8" instead of an "o") is a book we're putting together, an anthology of Norwegian indie RPGs. It's also the name of a blog where we publish games in PDF format.

    The name does imply that there is some sort of shared design/gaming style - which is, as with all these things, true and untrue.

    A lot of games on the site, and in the book, are meant for an evening's play, not for campaigns. They're often GM-less (or GM-ful). Many don't use numbers or dice. Many rely on cooperation, not competition.

    However, for each of these there are exceptions. Pervo is the most GM-heavy game of all - it's essentially a GM's revenge over players as a class of people. Zombieporno (which will be in the book) is a competitive, tactical game with numbers and dice all over.

    Does that answer your question? Have you checked out some of the games on the site?

    \n Good question - I love the Fastaval / Otto thing - but no, there isn't. We've tried to drum up some action there, but there's no central drive. There's a prize for "best con scenario" at Arcon, but it's very poorly publicized (their web page doesn't say who won, for example), and we haven't been able to invest a lot of prestige in the competition.

    What we're getting now, however, is a tradition centered around the R.I.S.K. competition (at the forum rollespill.net) and HolmCon (a festival at my house). Games are designed for R.I.S.K. in february, then played and refined at HolmCon, and sometimes presented to a larger public at Arcon.
