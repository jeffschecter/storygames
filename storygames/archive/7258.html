\n I was waffling on posting this to LJ or not, but I figured I'd share. It's a fluff promo piece of little substance. Goofy lonely fun.

So someone over on RPGNet today was saying how they like GMing with random tables. And I was like, "If you like random tables, you'll love this...", and I proceeded to ask the OP to roll a bunch, like a fuckload, of dice and post the results of each number. Unfortunately, the OP left for the day, so there's no reply to that thread.

What I didn't mention was that it was for Maid RPG. And just cause I'm excited (perhaps high from only about 3 hours of sleep), have a few spare moments, and am antsy to give this a shot, I'll post it here.

So, for the first two people (for now) who reply with their rolls, I'll do them up and post the results, MAID-style

=====
In all the below, when I say "roll 1d6 four times", I don't mean for you to add them together (1+4+4+6= "15"), but rather just list the results: "1,4,4,6".
(maid)
1) Roll 1d6 twice
2) Roll 1d6 six times
3) Roll 1d6 eight times
4) Roll 1d6 Twice
5) Roll 1d6 twice
6) Roll 1d6 once
7) Roll 1d6 twice
8) Roll 1d6 twice
8.5) Stats: As Ben indicates below: Roll 2d6 6 times, and add the results together (2-12): "10,3,6,7,11,5" etc.

Then, take a break, then
(costume and items)
9) Roll 1d6 twice
10) Roll 1d6 three times
11) Roll 1d6 three times

Take a break, and roll:
(genre and mansion)
12) Roll a 1d10, once. If a 0, roll again until you get a non-0.
13) Roll 1d6 once
14) Roll 1d6 four times
15) Roll 1d6 once
16) Roll 1d6 four times

One more rest, then
(events, weather))
17) Roll 1d6 four times
18) Roll 1d6 four times
19) And what the hell, cap it off by rolling 1d6 twice more.

Go for it, and I'll tell you your results when I have a sec.

    \n Jason:
    (I just realized I didn't ask for stat rolls, but that's ok, it's not the meat of what's happening here).

    Your Maid is:
    DOUBLE PURE. She is an exemplar of maidenly virtue, purity, cleanliness, perhaps fragility.

    Her maid outfit is Gray
    Her hair is Orange
    Her eyes are Purple
    She wears a large ribbon in her hair, an accessory which is special to her
    She is a neat freak
    She is a vampire(!)

    Reason she became a maid: Who knows? She might not even realize why she became a maid.
    Stress Explosion is Complaining: When you take too much damage (usually socially), you will fall into a habit of complaining to the master and other maids.
    Your maid power is lockpicking: You can enter any room whenever you feel like it.
    Your traumatic event (optional) is that you were kidnapped.
    Your maid weapon (optional) is a machinegun.

    Your mansion is located in the middle of a wild jungle!
    The mood of this setting is "Action/Slapstick". Physical comedy is the way of this session.
    The mansion has an Otherworld Gate.
    The mansion has an Indoor Pool
    The mansion isn't so much a mansion, as a collection of grassy huts. Cleaning's a bitch.
    The theme colors of the mansion are Vermillion and Pink. Perhaps it's a scenic vacation resort/jungle adventure themed village in the wilds? Beatiful red sunsets, pink baboon butts(?).

    You have an extra maid outfit: It's a Magician outfit. You can dress up like a D&D sorcerer/magic user and cast magical attacks on people, at a cost of a few stress points.
    You have found two items within the mansion! They are:
    Hot Springs Resort Tickets. A free pass to a local hot springs resort. What a lovely vacation!
    A Secret Construction Plant. Underneath the mansion, you have a secret room with clones of yourself in jars of liquid. If you "die", you can "come back to life" a few minutes later.

    Here's some things that happen during the scenario:

    Action Random Events:
    The mansion master accidentally walks in on one of the maids changing her clothes!
    Mechanization: One of the maids who has a stress explosion somehow is turned into an android (is this resort built upon a secret science lab? That's my impression from the above).

    Jungle/Wild Kingdom Scenario Events:
    In the midst of heavy jungle rain, a girl staggers up to the mansion... she seems to want to ask something of the master...
    An epidemic! The master falls ill with a tropical disease!

    Weather report: Strong UV rays. Better stay indoors.

    -----------------------------
    Thoughts:

    I'm totally sold on the maid concept. A super-fragile (ego) maid who is actually a vampire, who comes and goes into any room she wishes.
    I have to squint to make the jungle-laboratory thing work. But it could be a little Isle of Doctor Moreau action there. Also, I think I'd aim it for horror over action.

    \n Jacob:

    Your Maid is
    Sexy and
    Pure
    (pure is described above. Sexy implies charming, glamorous, coquettish, etc)
    Her Maid Uniform is Green
    Her Hair is Orange
    Her Eyes are Sky Blue

    She has a secret job! In private, she is actually an assassin
    She has an injury: She has burns somewhere on her body or face
    She is generally a quiet person
    She became a maid in order to pay back a favor to someone (perhaps the family of the master?)
    When she stresses out, she immediately turns to gambling.
    Your maid power is Passionate Gaze: You can quickly ingratiate yourself with the master.
    Her trauma (optional) is that she betrayed a close friend.
    Her maid weapon (optional) is a halberd.

    The mansion takes place in the Old West... Since that's close enough to the Edo period (9), let's roll with that chart instead. Ha! I exercise my "roll or choose" rule as GM!
    So, the mansion is in the old Edo period. Samurai and kimonos and isolation and shogunate and stuff.
    Oh, and the setting is Dark! (gritty)
    The mansion colors are purple and pink. The purple could be the royal heraldry on the family flag, and the pink? Well, pink in Japan is almost always associated with sex. Could be part of a brothel filled with heart-of-gold prostitutes, or perhaps be near the red light district?
    Oh wait, that 6 tells us that the Mansion is actually a Shinto Shrine. A sexy shrine? :-) Maybe there's a lot of children around, with their pink faces.
    The mansion has a self-destruct switch. Apparently a common feature of old shinto shrines, cause you never know...
    It also has an indoor pool. Perhaps a reflecting pool or kami-infused pool which brings health?

    Your maid has an optional costume: A Western style wedding dress!! Perhaps it was given to her by a western traveler or missionary? You can instantly be married to someone, in effect seducing them.
    Some things you find in the shrine include:
    The Finest Maid Uniform in the World. This exquisite maid uniform is woven from thread so fine, that it is invisible to the uncultured naked eye. If you walk around town wearing this outfit, people are so blinded by the beauty of this gown that they stop and gawk at you, or look nervously away for fear of breaching station. It was rumored to have previously belonged to an emperor!
    A Decisive Coin: A sinister-looking coin. Flip it to settle fights between maids.

    Some things that happen at the shrine:
    Dark Events:
    Pleasure mixed with Pain: Any time that a maid with the highest Will score takes Stress damage, she gets that amount in Favor (the currency of appealing to the Master)
    Dark Heart Revealed! The maid with the lowest luck rolls a new special quality on the Criminal Tendancies table. Perhaps because of her crime she ran away to become a shrine maiden?

    Old Edo Events:
    A fire starts like mad! If the maids can't put it out, the shrine will be lost!
    1-6 merchant vigilantes come to punish you for a deal gone bad!

    The Weather report:
    Sudden downpour. You better hurry up and get that laundry in.

    -------
    Thoughts:
    Maid is classic and interesting. Halberd actually works for the setting (naginata).
    I love the idea of maids protecting a shrine. The master might actually be the kami in the shrine! Plus, "Dark". Sweet.

    \n The book outlines three styles of play:
    Scenario-Based (the GM has a specific thing if mind, either of his/her own devising or from a pre-written scenario)
    Favor Race (the players compete to serve the Master and come out with the most Favor)
    Random (roll random stuff and run with it)

    Oh, and if you really feel the need to roll up attributes, roll 2d6 and divide by 3 (round down) six times.
