\n In the Stuff to Watch thread Kevin posted a link to Wordle, a service that makes word clouds out of an text you give it. The two examples he gave were the PHP and the DMG and the differences between the two clouds where so obvious and interesting, I thought this would deserve its own thread. The basic idea is: Take some game text, run it through Wordle and post what comes out.

    \n I processed some more of my own games:\n\n\n
    Hellball Architects of Aztlan Sex/Magic









    \nObservations:

    I wrote Hellball for the MACE Iron Game Chef, Architects of Aztlan for Reversed-Engineer, and Sex/Magic for this year's Game Chef.

    I use the word "can" too much.

    Some of these games (like Hellball) seem fun to play from the most common words. Some of these games (like S/M) seem more like instruction manuals. Our games should seem fun to play from a brief skim of the pages, just from most common words.
