    \n
    \n Creativity is inherently frightening. It takes a certain amount of ego to present something and say "Look what I did! Behold its awesomeness!" This is why, so often, the primary hallmark of the great artist is not talent, but chutzpah. So it's no wonder you're anxious.
    \n
    \nWhen you run these games for the first time, you will probably do something incorrectly, as far as the rules are concerned. However, if you and your players are having a good time, that doesn't much matter. You may notice it afterwards and maybe make things even better by doing it more correctly next time. Or you may find that you and your players like it better the way you did it, and leave it that way. The truth of the matter is that most of the enjoyment of playing any game is interacting with the other players (thank you Robin Laws for pointing this out).
    \n
    \nI sometimes have anxiety fits at the prospect of running the roleplaying game I designed myself. No one knows the rules better than I, and yet I don't always run the game correctly. But my players almost always seem to enjoy themselves anyway. And for me, that's the whole point.

    \n My advice for the pitch session:

    Never EVER say "whatever you want", that is poison.
    Yell, "I HATE YOUR IDEA" at least once for an idea that is clearly brilliant, then introduce that same idea in a speculative "here's what I thought of..." tone, using the exact same words.
    Go from general show concept -> characters -> things we want to see the characters do (includes conflicts) -> specific show content.
    Mumble something about how it's only a five hundred dollar fine from the Guild if you murder a writer.
    Stop in the middle of the pitch session and say "This is a DISASTER, make it a hundred times better by the time I get back" and go get some coffee from up the street.

    And so on.

    Posted By: fnord3125WoD wasn't something I, personally, was thinking of when I think about games I'm especially worried about running... but if you have any advice on nWod, especially Changeling or Promethean, I'd certainly be interested in hearing it.
    \nI haven't run Changeling, but I've been playing it for the last 8 months, so I have some ideas. I think the key thing, and its not that well explained in the text, is to remember that although the *really bad* Faeries are the Antagonists, and offstage, that most Faerie story type creatures (pookas, ogres, things under bridges, undines, imps etc.) are the player characters and they are really, really, not human. In many ways they are far less human than Vampires and the like, because their minds have been so twisted and broken by their experiences that they really don't know what is truth and what is fiction any more.

    A Changeling on low-clarity can't tell the difference between visions, the unreality of the hedge, and the normal world. They are like a paranoid schizophrenic, except the voices can beat them up and make them kidnap children to sell them to the goblins for better dreams ... even one on high clarity still isn't always clear on what is real or not, and their system of morality, such as it is, bears little relation to normal human morality (e.g. killing someone is far better than locking them up). I'd run tabletop Changeling with a constant eye on that dreamlike unreality, and bearing in minds that the normal PCs are creepy monsters out of grimm's tales.

    If you can (and this may be my preference of course), I'd also avoid the pseudo-medieval pageantry of the courts to some degree. It's easy to turn the whole thing into a politics game (like Nobilis where no one ever goes outside), that misses the fact that its quite a bleak horror setting.

    \n "more than human", "less than human"... at doing something. Meaning: a TASK.

    The rolls in MLWM are all about CONFLICTS. The tasks you do to win are only pre-roll and post-roll narration.

    Do you want to do steal something in a house, and you are "more than human" in the way you can turn invisible, inaudible, enter anyplace, steal anything, if there isn't a cat around?

    Well, if you want to do villainy (steal from that house) you still have to roll for the conflict (villainy). If you win the roll, you narrate what you do to steal. You can even use your "more than human" ability if you want.

    If you lose the roll... inside the house there is a cat.
