\n Chronicles of Skin features cards and doodles. People ask about both. There are free, illustrated rules online which explain the use of the cards, so I\u2019ll restrain this thread to the latter topic. Doodles. What do they look like in actual play?



This game was played at the UK Games Expo in May 2012 with myself and four virgin skinners: Tony, Steve, Hannah and John. I\u2019ll give two examples of play. First, a trait inversion (a character at odds with their culture\u2019s trait).

In the last story/scene, we meet \u201cBrynyn,\u201d at odds with \u201cBeautiful.\u201d We know the Croen take pride in their beauty, so this presents an interesting inversion\u2014Steve introduces Brynyn as a one-eyed, slovenly, disgusting reprobate. Of course, he goes Richard III on everyone\u2019s ass and takes control of the kingdom. It\u2019s interesting to see the betrayal and self-loathing inferred from a simple volte-face. You\u2019ll see the result of his betrayal illustrated at the bottom left of the skin, at Cross Sea. Look at all those Croen tombstones.

Down at the bottom right, near the Grand Abattoir of Byzen, you\u2019ll see a bugle. During the scene there were deliberations about the fate of the Iho nation. In the distance, a war horn was blown, announcing the impending massacre. Hannah doodled the bugle to signify that event. Had we been playing using the full Chronicle rules, she could have used that doodle to get an extra token.

If you\u2019ve played a game of Chronicles of Skin, why not show off your art and explain some of the story? I\u2019m particularly interested in Grand Events, trait inversions and long standing characters.
