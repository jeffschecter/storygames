    \n Yeah, the Games on Demand area requires Generic Tix and the like, all you have to do is say hi to the designated greeter and get shuffled into a game.

    The Embassy Suites at Night thing is different. Anyone is welcome. We sort of made the cafeteria/dining area "our place" like locusts on a juicy field. We have no more right to be there than others. But it's a great place to meet people. Many people play in that area, or split off and go somewhere to play ("Where's John Harper?" "Oh, he, Droz, Keith and Julie went to play Conspiracy of Shadows in Keith's room, I think it's 512 or something."). Unless it gets PACKED, I usually just stay there.

    So, we won't know you, and you won't know us. There's no handshake. Just look for people playing or talking. Ask questions. Say "hi". That's how it all gets started. Albeit, looking for signs like Go-Play stickers, copies of Dogs in the Vineyard, or Ron Edwards standing on a chair helps people narrow down their searches. 80% or so of the people there will be Us or Affiliates, tho.

    Stop on by!

    -Andy
