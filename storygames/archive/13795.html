    \n I'm in total agreement with you about that feeling, Kenny. I'm a more recent convert to the whole Story Game and narrative game thing, but I took to it like a fish to water.     I had a bugger of a time with one player when I ran Warhammer Fantasy 2E game, where I had pretty much ignored a majority of the Warhammer fiction, and was running it instead more as a general urban fantasy. But this player kept saying "But the Sigmarites would then..." or "They can't do that there..." etc.
    Or like Amber. Not a bad system (though I'm not really a fan of diceless systems), but everything was so rooted in this complex series of books and fiction, that as a player, I had NO CLUE what the hell was expected of me.     Posted By: DionysusI HATE with a passion those details and the fact that they exist for a game makes me not want to play that game.
    Why do you hate it so? I propose that the answer is somewhat close to the fact that classical role-playing games of old (Dungeons, Vampire, etc.) were loaded with setting info and heavy with rules. That often makes for a game that is hard to lift into functional fiction.

    I do surmise that you still long for something beyond that, in the classical games (correct me if I'm wrong). You may have had some rare moments of pure magic in them, where you and the character managed to elegantly suft a wave of choices and consequences, choices and consequences, c & c, c & c ... until the game settled, beautifully, beyond the narrative horizon.

    To get there again, you may want to try out classical games of more recent design, with lighter mechanics, more conscious dialogue-techniques and setting-info given in a much more effective and inspiring way. I hope someone will be able to point toward such a game in English ...

    \nI guess I don't see a problem? I mean, yes, you seem frustrated, you are not having as much fun as you imagine you might in some other group in some other place in some other time playing some other game, but you don't know that, you might go to that group, that place, that time, and play that game, and it might be 1,000 percent more horrible an experience than you're having now.

    So the evaluation you have to make is:

    * Is playing this game, with this group of people, in this place and time, more enjoyable for me than the alternative (reading a book, watching TV, listening to music, organizing a new group, etc.)?

    If yes, then you take what you can out of the group and enjoy it.

    If no, don't do it anymore, do whatever the other thing is.

    There's nothing wrong with your friends or how they want to play, so changing that is off the table. You can only change yourself or your situation.
