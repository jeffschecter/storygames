\n I need some help with my 2 Games, 1 Name entry.
I'm doing Love In Winter (restriction: Fairy-tale Fantasy Setting).
I'm playing pretty fast/loose with my restriction.
Here it goes:

Goblins and Dragons spill into this world. Specifically, Russia. They quickly conquer it.
Russia is now Goblin Country. The goblins have adopted communist trappings, built weird machines that hybridize goblin magic and roughly WWII era Russian technology. (two reference points: Tannhauser: Operation Novgorod, Red Blok).

So, here's how this works:
There are magical force fields, possibly the dragons are their beacons, so you can't nuke or fly over Goblin Country.
There are billions of goblins, so you can't just march troopers in there.
There are dragons, so you can't just send in tanks or infantry-demolishers.
Russia is really big, so there's lots of terrain to cover.

And yet:
the US military wants to be doing recon work, probably looking for the shield generators.
there might be survivors to rescue.
we need to figure out how to eject these creatures from our world.

And we just realized that children, pure of heart, cannot be harmed by the goblins!
In fact, if they close their eyes, the goblins can't even come within a certain range of them (meters equal to their Purity Rating).
So, the US Military does the only logical thing. Trains pure-hearted children for recon work and dumps them in Goblin Country.

Okay, so...
The kids get separated momentarily, while in Goblin Country.
The goblins run up and kidnap Jacob (Lucy's boyfriend!).
Jacob closes his eyes. The goblins don't go flying several meters back, because... ...Jacob isn't pure of heart any more!

So, the rest of the kids watch Jacob get dragged off, kicking and screaming.
These kids are:
Lucy (12 years old, was dating Jacob. Kissed him once.)
Andreas (11 years old, madly in love with Lucy.)
[no clue]
[no clue]

The game is a hodge-podge mix of semi-tactical minis game, fairy tale story, and shortform adventure game (a la Blackbird).
I need to know... who are those other kids?
They all need hooks that make purity loss possible.
The basic game is: Your friend is kidnapped. You're stuck in Goblin Country. Get out without losing your pure-heartedness. Oh, and failing to help people out will lose you pure-heartedness.
