    Just FYI, this link shows the proper way to engage in something while showing disdain for it. Apply to gaming:

    http://www.theonion.com/content/node/33176

    EDIT (from the Onion link, revised):
    "A few weeks ago, my friend Curtis organized a Dungeons and Dragons event for his friends. Can you imagine anything more tacky and blase? But contrary to what you might think, I was more than game for it. I even bought a set of old "crayon dice" so I could fit in with these common folk. I got killed by an orc, but I loved it. The people there were so into it, some of them actually did little dances when they got a critical strike. There was this one guy I called "One-Fist," because after every attack hit, he'd pump his fist in the air like some blue-collar Billy Idol. Never in my life have I had such a great time participating in base geek culture while simultaneously sneering at it from a distance."

    -Andy

      \n The total charge for name tags plus renting space at the hotel for Forge Midwest 2007 will be $1100 plus tax.

      This is more than last year, as expected in the nicer hotel in the more expensive area.

      Unfortunately, the current list of confirmed participants is somewhere around 30 people, with only a couple people otherwise telling me privately they're coming.

      Obviously, when we do the math, that comes out to about $36 per person, possibly more once we figure in taxes and fees to the total bill.

      This is more than I'd hoped to charge people, particularly since I expected around 50 participants. So, if you haven't entered confirmation that you're coming on evite.com, please do so: Click here to RSVP

      We paid just under $20 per person last year, folks. It really was extraordinary fun. I hate to see participation slide and cost go up! But, I still think it's worth the money to have this event. So, this is fair warning that your price tag on Saturday morning will be as high as $36.
