\n I figure we have enough people and meet often enough to call ourselves a scene now, so rather than just have a thread about Go Play, I thought I'd expand the scope of things a bit seeing that everyone was directed here.
\n
\nBut yes: Today was Go Play Brisbane, which happens every six months or so. If you happen to live in Brisbane and haven't heard about it, you should look here. If you were there, you should post in this thread.
\n
\nQuick summary: There were five games played and a bunch more on offer. The games actually run were Siege, A Penny For My Thoughts, The Beast of Limfjord, Devil Die, and Happy Birthday Robot. Games on offer but without enough players to be run were Amber, Archipelago II, and Don't Rest Your Head, with copies of Bliss Stage, Inspectres, and Shock: floating around the place.
\n
\nOddly, we seem to mostly do playtests, but I suppose that reflects the large number of people designing stuff and most of it's pretty far along, so it's all good. Every game seemed to go well.
\n
\n
\nHalf-arsed recap over (feel free to use the thread for reminiscing and feedback on them though). Let us speak of the Future.
\n
\n1. GenCon Oz was cancelled yesterday. AndyIndieCon* is in the works to take its timeslot - what new and exciting things shall we be doing for that? Or shall we just go for another Go Play?
\n
\n* May not be final name.
\n
\n2. Once every six months seems like a fairly long time between gaming, seeing that I got the strong impression that most of us don't have a regular indie games group. We should really trade some contact information or something and organise games.
\n
\nTo get us started - my email is aikynaro at the gmail dot com. I was the person offering Archipelago.
\n
\n(Also: Gavin (I think your name was ... the guy playing Hatchet) - I was wracking my brains on the train home trying to work out how I know you from elsewhere, because I'm sure I do. You're a NaNoer, right?)
