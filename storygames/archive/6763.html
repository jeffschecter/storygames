\n In honor of the date, let's talk about spicing up an ongoing game with a horror one shot.

If you wanted to inject a single horror session into a running campaign, what techniques would you use to make it work? Are there things to twiddle in a particular set of rules? Are there situations specific to a certain set of characters or a setting that you can play with to make this rock?

To be specific, I am talking about keeping the same set of characters in the same setting, with the same rules. Don't patch the characters into a different game, don't jump to a different place or time and try to tie it back in to the campaign later. Just scary up your dungeon crawl, space opera or super slugfest.

    \n Generally, I've found that horror scenes are about power and subtlety. If your characters are normally in charge of things- the baddest asses in town, never backing down from a conflict- you flip the situation and make them truly powerless. If the characters rely on powerful magic, they slowly realize it doesn't work here, against them. If they are relying on the vacuum of space and their energy shields to keep a safe distance, they'll panic when it is suddenly standing right next to them, whispering in their ear.

    The point about subtlety is just that small, almost inconsequential details often have a bigger impact than big flashy terrors. If it's a raging demon in the middle of your dungeon, ringed in flames, roaring for the PCs blood, they'll probably assume it's just another monster they have to deal with. If it's a the shadow of a child, moving slowly down a hallway in complete silence, they'll have no idea what to do with it- they don't even know if it's a threat. But maybe they shouldn't wait around to find out...

    \n In last week's TSoY session, a bunch of people got drugged (thanks, Steve!). So we're all Fort Apache in a Costco, barricaded against the zombie hordes, and all of a sudden:

    1. Half the people are unconscious and need to be taken care of and
    2. The awake half are groggy and out of it and
    3. The zombies are breaking through and
    4. We were fucking drugged. By a living person and
    5. We don't know who.

    That was a scary moment.
