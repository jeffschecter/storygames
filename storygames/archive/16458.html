\n hi,
\n
\nI know some folks here run play-by-post games and play via IRC, so I wanted to post a heads up that myself and a small group are in the planning stages for a web-based magazine about text-based games. Currently we're mostly mud and IF people so it'd be cool to get some fresh perspectives, and I thought, hey Story Games.
\n
\nWe're defining our scope broadly at the moment, so it includes play-by-post, IRC RP, muds, interactive fiction, some browser games such as Echo Bazaar, gamebooks, and also general issues of design and game development applicable to text-based games.
\n
\nIn brief we want to put these genres, which up to now have mostly been hanging out in their own spots, into one place and see what happens.
\n
\nIf you're curious the development forum is at http://www.imaginary-realities.com/phpbb/ ,
\n
\nand there's an ongoing summary of the discussion in progress at http://www.imaginary-realities.com/mw/ , if you want a quick overview.

    Posted By: Mathalus</cite>George, I clicked through, but the pages look a little sparse. Tell us a little about some plans and stuff. What do you need?
    \n
    \n
    \nThe content is going to be about text-based games, so mostly about muds, interactive fiction, gamebooks, play-by-post and so on, but also including articles on design and play relevant to text-based games; for example an article about the design challenges of random magical items like those in Diablo (for more on this see the scope summary).
    \n
    \nWe're leaning toward a quarterly web publication with stuff like short and feature articles, interviews, reviews, and embedded games. The ideas page gives more detail.
    \n
    \nThough we're aiming for a popular tone for both players and developers, we want to include some more academic content too.
    \n
    \nI think we could use more help in a few ways:
    \n
    \n* contributions to the scope discussion, like ideas for content and advice on web publishing (the core group is mostly new to this).
    \n
    \n* pointers to more types of games and sites to check out -- we're pretty light on play-by-post and other relevant RPG sites right now (I started a list at http://www.imaginary-realities.com/phpbb/viewtopic.php?f=2&t;=9).
    \n
    \n* contacts from people who might like to contribute content -- articles, art, graphic design.
