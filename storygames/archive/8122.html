\n This has been on my mind for a few days. I decided that in the next version of my homebrew (very PDQ-like) I wanted degrees of success to get a little more feedback from the dice.

2d6 + Stat vs. Target Number, like PDQ. PCs are the only ones that roll.

Roll Greater than or equal to the Target Number = Success
- With two 6es: Critical Success
- With a single 6: Major Success
- With a single 1: Minor Success
None of the above or a 6 and a 1: Success.

Roll Less than the Target Number = Failure
- With a single 6: Minor Failure
- With a single 1: Major Failure
- With two 1s: Critical Failure
None of the above or a 1 and a 6: Failure

High-definition (Major/Minor/Critical) is about 50% likely, low definition (Success/Failure) is about 50% likely.

    \n Those are pretty easy odds to work out.

    You've got a 1 in 36 chance of getting two sixes.
    You've got an 8 in 36 chance of getting one six and no ones.
    You've got an 8 in 36 chance of getting one one and no sixes.
    You've got a 1 in 36 chance of getting two ones.

    \n Largely I'm looking at the rollover-qua-Sorcerer idea, where a Critical success gives a +3 to the next roll, a Major a +2, a Success a +1, and a Minor no change (but you do succeed at your action). So it's about momentum.

    There are 36 possibilities in 2d6, 20 of them include a 1 or a 6. 2 of them include both. So if you take the 2 out you've got 18 out of 36. I think that's all I have to worry about.
