    I'd like to back Andy up on this. The Power 19 can be a great design tool for some. However, what's great for getting thoughts ordered and understanding where you want to go with a game is not necessarily great for communicating with potential players, reviewers, or people you are asking for help. What these people want is as concise an overview of the game as possible, followed by a very short recap of data needed to understand the question you're about to ask, followed by your question.

    The Big Three works great for summaries, if you restrict each answer to one sentence. If you've done the Power 19 for yourself, you should be able to answer the Big Three, since the Power 19 is an elaboration of the Big Three.
