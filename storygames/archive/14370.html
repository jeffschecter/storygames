\n We played Kagematsu last night (excellent as always, never had a bad game) and we all thought\u2026 could you hack this to play Game of Thrones?

Brainstorming\u2026

Threat = Outside hordes bringing war to your kingdom. Possibly civil war?

The Promise = Becoming King!

The Players = Heirs to the King or people who could rule in the King's place.

Kagematsu = The current King? The people of the kingdom?

Innocence = ?

Charm = ?

Love = ?

Pity = ?

Fear = ?

But Kagematsu doesn't seem competitive enough. What if instead of having separate character sheets with separate lists of Affections and Desperations you can aim for\u2026 you all shared 1 giant sheet of Affections! And as Affections are accomplished, they become unavailable to competing players!

    \n I could see Innocence and Charm be any of the following\u2026
    \n
    \n- Wealth (supplies, armies)
    \n- Knowledge (secrets, spies, ravens)
    \n- Reputation (blood, lineage, titles)
    \n- Strength (fighting, will)
    \n
    \nIt seems like Fear could easily be Loyalty. The more successful you are in winning over the people, the more loyal they are, the more likely they will stand by you and fight the oncoming Threat in the end and the less likely it will all turn into civil war or treachery.
    \n
    \nThe Boardgame has an interesting mechanic where you can bid to be the current ruler of the Iron Throne. The ruler chooses the order people play their turns which can be beneficial if you are going for the same affections on a shared sheet. It's also fun to play the ruler as I imagine there would be plenty of "bend the knee" proclamations. Perhaps whoever has the highest Loyalty has the Iron Throne. Kagematsu already states the player with the most Fear goes first. Perhaps the person with the least Loyalty can gain some sort of "underdog" benefit.
    \n
    \nI'm not sure what the equivalent of Pity and Love would by. Maybe Pity = Fear and Love = Respect, Hope, Belief.

    \n In Game of Thrones, it's never as simple as winning favors from one faction. There should be multiple progress tracks, with separate love/pity scores for each, and the winner is the first to get two Promises. I've started working on this. For four players, here are the factions, and what you need them to Promise you:
    Lannisters - financial support
    Targaryens - legitimacy support
    Starks - popular support
    Baratheons - military support

    Are those off? Can anyone suggest better ideas for ingredients needed to hold the throne?

    You play one of these, so the others will be seeking favors from you. So you spend as much time playing Kagematsu as playing a village woman, roughly. I guess that's not really Kagematsu any more...

    I also need terms for Love vs Pity that means "I'm working with you because I genuinely value you going forward and would like us to be on the same side" vs "I'm working with you because you are a convenient tool for me at the moment and I'm happy to have you think I'm an ally, but I'd turn on you in a second". The best I have is Commitment vs Convenience, but I'd like terms that can be used to more directly ask, "so how do you feel about them from that scene?" the way Love and Pity do. Ideas?

    \n Specific supports seems to matter more than specific factions.

    There are multiple sources, for example, of financial support. What's important is that you secure one of them. The Lannisters (in the show so far) are the most prominent financial player but there are several other factions offering significant financial power or to a lesser extent you can buy the support you need with say, dragon eggs.

    Keeping in line with Kagematsu, winning specific Affections can represent gaining different levels of support from financial, to legitimacy, to military.

    Of course there are a lot of aspects of Kagematsu that don't support Game of Thrones well. For example, betrayals. An affection you win now can be turned against you later. Winning a faction or specific type of support is only the beginning... keeping hold of them is a different story!
