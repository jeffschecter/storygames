A "Book Club", but with RPGs?

\n
\n
\n
\n\nAndy \n\n\n
\n
\n\nDecember 2010 \n edited December 2010 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n This is something I've been kinda thinking about for a while. Kind of like a book club ("Let's read this book and then talk about it later!"), but with RPGs.

So, basically, an RPG is chosen. I'd say "voted", but given everyone's interests I'd say that it'd probably work best if the ringleader gather ideas in private, and then simply Decide on the game to run: This would likely have the intended effect of getting folks to try to play stuff that they'd otherwise not consider as a first choice.

Anyway, then from there the folks in the club (which means "whomever wants to show up") have 4-6 weeks to play 1-2 sessions of that game. Folks play (preferably in person with their local groups, perhaps organizing for online play etc), then report the successes and failures they had playing the game, group discussion, and so on.

Focusing on less expensive games (ie so that folks don't have to run out and buy something expensive), with maybe something Bigger thrown in every few months.

Even if folks were interested, one of the harder things I think would be encouraging them to stick with it even when the game is not something they'd be inclined to play otherwise (for example, say that the first two rounds were hippie light games, then the third round was something Older, or Crunchy, etc). I guess if the stated sign-up goal was not only to play and experience all sorts of "story-focused RPGs", but also to "try various types of games, perhaps stepping outside your comfort zone", it might work better.

Anyway, just a little idea I've had on my mind for a few months. Of course, I start thinking this as I pick up my second weekly long-term game and see my free time dry up due to work and other obligations, but still. Something I've been thinking about for the new year...

-Andy
