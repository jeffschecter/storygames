\n Looking for people's opinions of Tent Cards at tabletop games (especially convention games). What kind of character information do you like to have on them? Do you use both sides? Is it to inform everyone else whom that person is playing or to inform the player what their most important stats are, or both? Do you like having pictures of the character on the card?
Have you used them (or seen them used) in innovative ways?

    \n For D&D 5 where you have things like Passive Perception and the DM needs to refer to that all the time, I just have them write it in red in the corner of the card.

    Some DMs grab the tent cards at the start of combat and use them to put people in initiative order. Some drop an "initiative ruler," marked 1-30 or whatever, onto the table and have players drop their tent cards onto the right position. Instant initiative.

    Technically, tent cards have four (or more) configurations. They have a front and back, plus you can invert them at the fold and get two more. I'm sure you could do something neat with that.
