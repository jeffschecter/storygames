    \n Hey Nick,

    I had a bit of an epiphany about HTHAD about halfway through my first play, and it basically amounts to: Lighten up! I spent a bunch of time worrying about whether I was doing it "right" or exactly how the rules are supposed to work. And then I realized that it doesn't actually matter. I'm playing it to have fun mucking around in a dungeon. I don't need the same social contract common ground that RPG rules are normally supposed to provide. If I bend the rules, or interpret them creatively because I think that outcome is more fun, I'm doing it right! It was tremendously liberating. So just pick a way of handling encounters that works for you, and have fun with it.

    \n Cool dungeon! Albert's got it right; there's no really wrong way to do it. That said, there are a few tricks to use.

    When your Dwarves encounter primordial monsters, each monster kills one Dwarf, before getting killed itself. If the Dwarves encounter an ancient Wyrm, the Wyrm takes 1 of their white beads, and then they leave it alone after that. These rules are in the Primoridal section, not the Civilization section, which is why they are easy to miss.

    The size of the beads does make a difference, although you should be able to play at any size. Big beads will get crowded after a while. Different size sheets of paper work too. We played on a huge sheet about 2 feet square at Gen Con, and it worked great!
