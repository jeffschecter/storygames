\n There seems to be a growing number of RPGs suited to short play sessions; Gun Thief, S/lay with Me, Mud Dragons, and so on. We noticed last year that some players took a few sessions at Go Play NW to play 2-3 short games as opposed to the usual one long one.

I'm curious, is there something we as con organizers can do to help make a better play space for short games? As a con go-er, would you be likely to set aside a gaming slot to play these? What would make that a more or less attractive opportunity? Game facilitators and designers, what do you think would support your games best in a con setting?

    \n Something we did at Nerdly one year was add a deliberately short slot to the schedule without much comment. People filled it with short games, relaxing, extended meals, and so forth. There was no down side.

    In terms of support for short games, some (like In the Belly of the Whale) require a bit of arts-and-crafts prep, so announcing it and making it clear that short games are what it is for might be encouraging.

    \n We used to have 2 and 4 hour time slots. This coming Recess we are going back to only 4 hour slots.

    The 2 hour time slots were mainly due to board and card games. But after 2 hours, most people stuck with the same group and either played the same game multiple times or played a different short game with the same people. So we are going back to 4 hour slots but encouraging people to run multiple games in the same slot.

    \n The past year or two I've simply taken a normal spot and let people know that we'd be played several short form games. I just set expectations that the games may last 15 minutes up to an hour and that we'd get several play sessions in. People seemed to get that pretty easily.

    Personally, I'm not so sure I'd use a 6 or 8 hour slot for running short form. That can be a lot of games!
