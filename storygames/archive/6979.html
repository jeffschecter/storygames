\n Since other folks have been posting D&D4E-related cutekidness, I thought I'd add my own.

My daughter and I have been discussing the illustrations in the new 4th Edition D&D Player's Handbook for some days now; you may be interested to know that the female character pictured on p. 296 [SIC: pg. 297, actually] is, apparently, Snow White. (She hid her crown so that the bad guys would not know it was her).

A few nights ago, post-bath, my daughter escaped from her towel and began flipping through the Player's Handbook. I have transcribed as many of her comments as I could. For those of you who own a copy, I have provided page references:

p. 38: "That's Princess Callie -- I never thought she would be here!... [and] this is her prince."
p. 40: "And that's Princess Callie again."
p. 48: "Princess Christy and Prince Tonkin."
p. 50: "Tiefling man and Tiefling lady."
p. 51: "That's Princess Kaylee who got knocked over by the dragon."
p. 60: "A prince who didn't get married."
p. 105: "Oh oh oh, I know who that is. That's Snow White!.... Someone tried to put a spell on her; that's why she's green."
p. 125: "Complicated."
p. 129: "A Tiefling lady with curly horns, right?"
p. 143: "That looks like a wizard. He has braids in his beard, so he's a wizard."
p. 190: "Did the skeleton got chopped and hurt its arm?"
p. 195: "Oh, another prince who didn't get married. Do you know why there are so many princes who didn't get married? Maybe because the author wanted that."

    \n Snow White, it turns out, is AWESOME. After she became Queen, she started consulting the Magic Mirror about important questions, like who in the kingdom needed help, or where bandits were hiding out, and then proceeded to ride out with her husband the Prince (Prince-Consort to a Queen-Regnant, technically, it sounds like) and her children, Princess Callie and Prince Jackson. For a few weeks I was getting daily requests to tell new stories about them, and even now, when my daughter asks for a story, I make sure to ask, "is this a story about when Snow White was Queen, or about a different Queen?"
