"We regret to inform you that our credit card database was hacked"

Rich Stokes
January 2007 edited January 2007 in Story Games
I just got this email from RPGNow.

I've been paying them by Paypal for quite some time, and the last card I entered into their database expired over two years ago, but even so, I have to say that I don't exactly feel warm and fuzzy about this...
Dear Customer,

We regret to inform you that our credit card database at RPGShop.com and RPGNow.com was hacked. We have the obligation to notify you (our customers have chosen to save their credit card data with us) of the breach in security. While this may not mean your account has been compromised, we strongly urge you to notify your credit card company and make arrangements for a new card to be sent to you just in case. Only customers who have stored their credit card numbers with us are affected. This does not affect customers of DriveThruRPG.com

We have now removed all credit card data from our servers and no longer support the option to save your credit card with us. We're sorry for the troubles but the Internet is just full of too many hackers to block them all. FYI, the source of this hack was a Brazilian spammer who happened to get access to the server so the risk is not as high as if it was in the hands of a Russian Mafia - but we don't want to take any chances. The actual breach occurred on the RPGShop.com server (not the RPGNow.com server) but since they share the same checkout system the card would have been stored on the RPGShop.com server.

Again, if you're getting this email, that means you have chosen to save your credit card with us in the past. The expiration date of that card is in the subject of this message. We no longer save cards so this cannot happen in the future. IMMEDIATE ACTION IS REQUIRED ON YOUR PART!

We deeply apologize for the hassles this will cause you. If you have any questions, please do not reply to this email - rather send a message to "webmaster@rpgnow.com"

RPGNow.com
