\n Hello, Story Games! I have just arrived in the beautiful town of Northwich Spa, in the English Lake District, for my holiday.

Could you tell me if there is anything I should be wary of? For example, the streets are littered with dead bodies. How soon does typhoid and cholera set in? How long can I survive without food and water, if I have to? Is there anywhere particular I should sleep, or can I choose anywhere? What should I be concentrating on immediately?

Many thanks for all your help. I'm determined to enjoy my holiday, no matter what!

    \n Hi Graham!

    Do you have power? I assume if the outbreak is localized you do. If so, clear out the bodies, have a bonfire, and live it up. There should be plenty of food around in the many local dwellings, as well as comfortable beds.

    You don't have much to worry about from the corpses. Organisms traditionally associated with transmission from cadavers include bloodborne viruses (HIV, hepatitis B, hepatitis C, human T-lymphotropic virus 1), enteric bacteria (Salmonella, Shigella, Campylobacter, Yersinia, Vibrio cholerae, Vibrio vulnificus, Escherichia coli, Leptospira), viruses (rotavirus, norovirus, hepatitis A virus and enteric adenovirus), parasites (Giardia, Cryptosporidium) and airborne agents (Mycobacterium tuberculosis). So follow infectious disease protocol and burn them in a big pile.

    I don't want to be a downer, but is there any chance that the British Army is going to ... sanitize ... the area? If so, enjoying your holiday is the last thing you should be doing. Escape is paramount. They'll cut the utilities before they move in anyway, so the local situation will deteriorate rapidly. Be gone before that happens, or die for the greater good.

    A public health cordon, enforced by the military, is going to necessarily be porous. There will probably be electronic monitors and FLIR-equipped aircraft, so minimize your heat signature and stick to wooded areas as you approach the likely line of defense. If the British military is competent (an open question) they'll anticipate this and be watching waterways closely. It may be necessary to kill - don't hesitate if it comes to that. If you acquire a MOPP suit or similar protective gear, I'd suggest taking it and carefully hiding or destroying it - if they think you are "disguised", it will sow confusion and fear among your pursuers and slow them down. In contrast, wearing a hazardous material suit will just slow you down and offer little protective cover.

    What happens next depends on whether they are specifically looking for you, and if you are indeed infected, so there are too many variables to really pontificate at this point.

    Have a nice holiday!

    \n The recent wildfires in California consumed miles of forest and houses in a matter of days because of the high winds. In some cases people had less than an hour or two warning before their homes were destroyed.

    A steady rain or damp conditions could prevent it from spreading quickly, and whether you are in a village of primarily stone buildings versus wooden structures could have an effect too. Winds can also carry embers pretty far, which again, depending on how damp/dry everything is and what kind of surface they land on, you could have a lot of different fires starting up in different areas, and at the very least have structures smoldering and putting off large amounts of acrid smoke for days.

    If you are in a large modern city, I would think the spread of fire would be less of a threat, though the collapse of buildings due to their structures weakening could have all sorts of effect, from clouds of debris to busted water lines to interruptions in power.
