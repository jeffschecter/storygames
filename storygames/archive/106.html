Hey fellow gamers,

So, an interesting argument/issue came up over last week in regards to a certain PDF publisher and his use of advertising. Essentially, he is running a series of ads featuring busty women in bikinis to sell PDFs containing x number of spells/artifacts/etc for D20. Follow the livejournal posts to see the play of comments:

First, Nicole Lindros of Green Ronin: http://iamnikchick.livejournal.com/

Then, Michelle Lyons, formerly of Wizards of the Coast (and editor of the Eldritch Ass Kicking rpg!): http://anaka.livejournal.com/

And then my latest LJ about this issue: http://nathanhill.livejournal.com/

What makes this bad for me... is that I wrote a little artifact supplement for The Le Games, and this particular gimmicky marketing is something that I don't want to be associated with. I am going to contact him and let him know how I feel about the issue (politely), but I'm also interested in other opinions.

Watcha think?

    I've had to throttle back the imaginations of the artists for Verge. They're talented chaps, but I had to make it clear that I wanted women who looked effective. I want players to open my book, see the pictures therein, and say, "I want to play her," not just ogle her boobies.

    There's also this genre style convention -- I don't know where it came from -- that draws the women with disproportionately long legs, large breasts, and waspishly thin waists. I don't want that in my game. Players might deal with sex in the game but the game isn't about sex, it's about relationships and risk. I want pictures of people in relationships and people taking risks. One of the drawings my guys did is a young daughter clinging to her mother's back on a motorcycle, running away from Bad People. It's the awesome.
