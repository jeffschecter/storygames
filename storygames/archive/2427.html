When you purchase a Doom Key, it will have an object in the game world. This object is usually filled by an NPC or an NPC Organization. This NPC or Organization will now pursue your character (is now revealed to have been pursuing your character) and will seek some level of harm against you. You will also gain a new Pool called Doom. This is determined by the current level of your Doom, as represented by the threat level of your new antagonist. When you purchase your Doom Key, your Doom level will start at 1.

For example, a player is playing Montana Smith, a famous parapsychologist and adventurer. Montanaâ€™s player decided that a chance meeting with Nazis would make for a great story and adversary and picks up a Doom Key for free:

Doom of the Nazis
The Nazis pursue Montana Smith, and wish him, and any who aid or befriend him, harm. Nazis may now announce that they intend to kill Montana Smith.
Gain 1 XP every time the Nazis hurt you.
Gain 2 XP every time the Nazis strike a minor blow against you (kill your hirelings, disrupt your life, destroy your property, bring your reputation into question).
Gain 5 XP every time the Nazis strike a major blow against you (abduct your friends, torture you, permanently injure you, destroy your reputation).
Buyoff: Give up your Doom Pool to the Nazis.

The player also gets a Doom Pool ranked at 1. The player can use the Pool like any other Pool, but in addition to other Pool usages (e.g. a player could spend a point of Vigor and a point of Doom on a roll). The description of the results should either involve the object of the Doom Key or the roll should be against the object of the Key.

At any time thereafter, the Pool may be increased normally. Unlike other Pools, it has a maximum size of 6. As you increase the Pool, so should the description of the NPC or Organization increasing their efforts against you. At every odd level size of the Pool (1, 3, 5), the NPC or Organization gains a weapon with deals +1 harm to you (either by addition or separation â€“ three +1 harm weapons, a +2 weapon and a +1 weapon, or a +3 weapon).

Giving up your Doom Pool

Have the GM frame a scenario where your character and any other characters that need to be involved are confronting the object of your Doom. Announce that you are giving up your Doom Pool. The GM now has this Pool to use in the conflict. The stakes are set for you: Does the NPC get the harm/revenge that they want, or do you force them to give it up? Win or lose, your Key is now bought off along with the normal 10 XP.
