\n I remember MCing some great Apocalypse World but at some point, really wishing I had an initiative system to juggle multiple actions (particularly when players where going up against each other or had incompatible desire).

    Posted By: DevP
    How do you approach questions of initiative - of which actions happen "first" - in MCing either of these games?
    \nI just try to follow the fiction. Who seems like they're going first? Is someone saying, "Before he does that, I want to do this!", as a way of interfering with an action? If someone is saying that, either they can do it first or they can't, and I decide as MC based on my Agenda & Principles (unless, following from the fiction, doing that is making some kind of move--most likely Act Under Fire/Defy Danger). If they're trying to interfere with a PC, then the Hx/Bond move nicely takes care of that.

    NPCs act when it makes sense that they would given what I know about them and the situation (or make up about them according to my A&P), or when the PCs fail a roll.

    It really is about following the fiction, but that does take practice.

    \n I've never really felt the need for initiative, but maybe it would help to have an example, since it really comes from the fiction?

    Generally it seems like the first player to declare an action makes it first (with other players helping or interfering as they like), assuming play feels fairly "open" and freewheeling. When things get tense, I feel like it's my job as GM to follow the fiction and decide who should go next, fictionally. If people are in different locations, often I follow the action like a movie, letting one group make a few moves or do non-move things, before swinging back to the other group. If everyone's all together, I do the "take turns but not like taking turns" thing. That gets harder in larger groups, but ideally I would avoid playing AW/DW with more than 3-4 players anyway.

    I could say that both must first Act Under Fire to pre-empt the other, which is something of using a roll for situational initiative? But in some fo the cases above, it's more of a meta question.

    \n In both those cases, I'd go with whoever said their action first or -- if both actions kinda emerged from player dialog at once -- whose "turn" it was (who had less of the spotlight so far, thus: the person the camera swings to).     \n Part of "to do it, do it" is about declaring actions. So you can't declare an action and then have it not happen, unless the player decides not to actually do it.

    So, in your first example, the Savvyhead makes their seduce move first. Then I might turn to the Angel and say: "Do you want to interfere? Like rip the mutant from the Saavyhead's arms and kick them into a ravine?" And they might attempt to interfere, stopping the seduction. But then the Saavyhead could also interfere in the Angel's attempt to kick them into a ravine. Everything can happen, it's not "One player gets to make a make a move and the other player can't do anything about it, and loses their turn."

    P.S. Hans is also right that fictional positioning like "who's standing where" can also matter, if that's already been established. But sometimes it hasn't and it feels a bit fake to invent it on the fly. I was assuming the latter, since it wasn't spelled out in the example.

    \n Everyone gets more specific about what's happening until the order of actions is obvious. That covers 90% of the cases for me.

    Failing that, go to a move. Something act under fire-y should serve, but AW has that really cool chapter about making custom moves to fit your game, so I'd dig in there and craft something I really like. Making custom moves is fun and good for the game.

    When the chaos of battle is about to envelop you, roll+cool. On a 10+, hold 3. On a 7-9, hold 2. Spend your hold 1-for-1 on the following as the battle plays out:
    - Get your bearings and develop a clear, accurate picture of just exactly what the fuck is going on
    - Interrupt someone else's action with your own
    - Coordinate with someone else to time something just right

    I dunno. Something better than that, but there's a quick stab at it.

    \n Player says: I rush in, swinging my sword like a maniac!
    GM says, to herself: Hey, self, that sounds like he's Hacking and Slashing!
    GM says, to the Player: Are you Hacking and Slashing?
    Player says: Oh, shit, no, I'm trying to keep them from attacking Avon!
    GM says: Ah, so you're Defending. Cool! Roll some dice.

    This covers both "If you do it, do it" - which is the part about the character doing a thing and the player rolling dice against a move and it covers "to do it, do it." wherein the Player wants to accomplish a thing, so he's having his character do the thing. The GM is the move-detector, the chime that sounds when a move is necessary.

    It's something I still fuck up in AW all the time - I let people do it without doing it.
