I posted this over on the MH forums, but traffic seems a bit slow, so I figured I'd boost the signal here.
I've drafted up three new playbooks, just sort of inspired, but I could definitely use some feedback on them.

The first is The Doc.
This is your basic mad scientist Frankenstien type.

The next is The Cursed.
It's someone with terrible luck, at the bottom of the totem pole, who can't do anything right...

Finally is The Nightmare.
This is a child of dreams and nightmares, who tries to give people their desires... or their fears.

Thanks in advance everyone!
Hope maybe ya'll like them!

    \n I think for the Doc you need to cut back the "sure, but..." to ONLY things that require a sacrifice/problem on their part. Time is not an issue, for example, unless it's "too long for goal X". Also, what if you roll an 11, shouldn't the answer to that just be "sure"?

    Here are some options that would be what I'm taklnig about:

    * You need a rare material and only (name other playbook here) has access to it.
    * You spend so much time on your project that something goes wrong in another part of your life (grades drop, etc.)
    * It will cost far more money than you have.
    * Someone important to you gains a string on you - guilt over your abandonment
    * A rival gets wind of the project and finishes it first

    Things that actually are drawbacks. There's no "time" mechanic or "resources" mechanic in Monsterhearts so saying those are drains is not a drawback.

    Also, no Darker Self should be able to get out of with a "realization". You need to concretely DO something. I would suggest: destroy all previous inventions to this point and burn all your notes.
