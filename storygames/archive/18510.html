\n One year ago, in June 2012, the Swedish RPG Svavelvinter (Winter of Brimstone) was released by Fria Ligan, a Stockholm-based games studio. Based on Erik Granstr\xf6m's The Fifth Conflux series of novels, with its peculiar style of Renaissance/Babylonian fantasy, Svavelvinter RPG was a big hit - the first print run sold out in three months and it was awarded Best RPG of 2012 by both the Fenix RPG magazine and the Boningen games blog.

Now we give a taste of the game to an international audience. A compact quick-start rules document and the complete introductory scenario from the full game, The last days of Arhem, is available for download, free of charge. The zip file also contains complete character sheets for the premade characters used in the scenario.

So what kind of game is Svavelvinter? Ruleswise the main sources of inspiration are story-driven games like Burning Wheel, FATE, Solar System, Lady Blackbird and Apocalypse World. The game lets you choose between two specific styles of play - \u201ccharacter driven\u201d, that supports a Story Now agenda, and \u201cclassical\u201d, which is focused on more traditional adventuring.

The full 352-page game (so far only available in Swedish) also contains a complete map-based strategic conflict rules system called the Game of Shadows, as well as a comprehensive system for making and fulfilling grand prophecies that can guide the story arcs of whole campaigns.

You can download the quick-start set and read more about the game and the Tracorian setting here.
