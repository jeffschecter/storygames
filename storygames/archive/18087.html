\n So the first place I looked was Apocalypse World, but that seems to be about a different kind of story than I mean to run, but the engine is brilliant, and I would love to play the game like that. On the other hand, a more free form way to do it would be FATE, or I could go to other roots and use Savage Worlds or GURPS. The thing is I don't really want it to be so mechanical as the last two, and I was hoping to do it with something a little fresh.

Ooh! Now there is an idea: Take the idea of Fallout and mix it up some by putting the tropes into a fantasy setting. A city that saves itself by locking itself in a demiplane for a hundred years to ride out a great catastrophe opens its gates to send forth a party of adventurers? Maybe I can get the guys to buy that.

You see, that is the problem. I have to run this on Saturday for this game group of kids half my age I only played with a couple times.

Anyway, sorry for the disjointed riffing, but I have to get my head around this.

    \n This is one elevator pitch for a fantasy game called The Shadow of Yesterday:

    It has been 300 years since the SKYFIRE, a catastrophe that decimated the world. A giant asteroid ripped into the planet, forcing a part of it to separate into a large moon. Only luck and large mountain chains saved Near from the shockwave that obliterated most of the world. It still remains to be seen whether the broken world can sustain life indefinitely or whether the biosphere is doomed to perish as the atmosphere leaks into space or the continental plates realign catastrophically.

    Although the lands known as Near were largely protected from the immediate impact, the Year of Shadow that immediately followed decimated the agriculture, leading to nine tenths of the population perishing. The remaining people struggle with a lost culture and changed world, surviving at a subsistence level.

    So if you're interested in mixing postapocalypse and fantasy, that's pretty much the thing.

    Regarding playing Fallout straight, I would use either Solar System, Apocalypse World or FATE myself, depending on the particular goals. They're all pretty flexible toolbox systems that require the GM to be at home with the system, though.

    \n Again not sure what you want out of Fallout, but have you heard about "Flatpack: Fix the Future" by Filamena Young? From the DTRGP game description:\n
    Have you heard anyone say that the future's not so bright, and thought, "But I have lightbulbs?" If so, Flatpack is the game for you! Until someone tells us otherwise, we're saying that Flatpack: Fix the Future is the first game in the smash Hyperoptimistic Postapocalyptic genre of gaming. Flatpack is a game about building a new society using pre-apocalypse technology that you don't always trust or understand, and solving terrible problems creatively.
    \nYou play kids who have just got out of a Vault and are sent out to search the outside world for for 'flatpacks' (ready made modules that expand into city parts). IIRC the author tried to integrate puzzles into game mechanics and the game seems to stress that combat is not the first solution you should be thinking of, or even a good solution most of the time.

    There's a free demo adventure available for download:

    http://rpg.drivethrustuff.com/product/97997/Yes,-but-is-it-Art,-a-Demo-Adventure-for-Flatpack

    (My impressions of the game are based on this demo, as I haven't bought the game books yet).

    \n Okay, Emergence is an awesome take on it. Thanks for that. I expect to use several things from it. I think, however I am going to continue on the path I have started Hacking together certain parts of Dungeon World (World of Dungeons) derivatives "Streets of Marionburg" and "Star Worlds: Streets of Mos Eisley". Star Worlds has a few bit of Old School Hack in there that I really like, such as spending Awesome Points (called Karma in Retropocalypse) to build experience.

    I'm fixing to run Fallout as a group of dwellers that go out into the world for whatever reason, but, like Fallout 3, I'm going to set the first episode inside the Vault. I love some of the stuff in Emergence, there, that I do not have, like the idea of intercharacter bonds and reasons to leave the safety of the Vault. My Vault is going to be more small town sized than single family sized. That is the plan so far.
