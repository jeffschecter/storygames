\n I have this idea I want to run. Paris suburbian fantasy. The characters are men and women longing for change, carrying small secrets of magic and wonder. A night of violence and rage is coming. How will it end? A little bit of Nobilis, a little bit of Unknown Armies. One-shot game, one or two nights of playtime.
\n
\nAnyway, I was planning to eventually run a full Changeling: The Lost Campaign with Solar System, so I thought why not try out the system right now?
\n
\n- How does SS work for one-shots? The mechanics really seems based on long-term play.
\n- How would SS handle this particular setting, anything useful or complicated to look out for?
\n- Anything in else in particular I should keep in mind, seeing as it is the first time I GM Solar System?
\nI'm not new to indie RPGs at all, so that part is not a problem. :)

    \n One-shots are possible. I've only made them work to the game's potential when I've had system expertise within the group combined with a rather long session. A part of this might be that I'm not too fond of a one-shot that is accomplished by having precreated characters, situation and even preplanned scene framing. It's not as exciting as flying without an autopilot and seeing what the group comes up with; the latter just often takes 8 to 12 hours to run through a reasonable, movie-sized story. More if there are more than three players.

    The new TSoY book includes an appropriate one-shot scenario, too, as a model of how I'd prepare for such. The key conceit is that the Keys are prechosen to match the scenario, but the players still get to create their own characters; they just distribute the Keys between themselves as they best see fit.
