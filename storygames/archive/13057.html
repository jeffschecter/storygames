    \n I might not be alive.

    A couple of years after AD&D 2E came out I was going through a really shitty period in my life -- unemployed, practically homeless, severely depressed, the works. Somehow I got hold of a 2E Player's Handbook and it totally rekindled my childhood/early teen love of gaming and fantasy. I think I burned through the local library's entire SF/fantasy section that winter. It may sound melodramatic, but I'm convinced that the sense of wonder and possibility and hope that those books reawakened in me actually kept me from killing myself.

    \n
    Posted By: ffilzI have a hard time conceiving RPGs not being invented...
    \nYeah, same here. What's interesting to me is imagining when and how they'd have come about if D&D had never existed.

    I suspect that some form of computer-based interactive fiction was inevitable -- though without RPGs, it wouldn't have started as Colossal Cave/Zork/etc. style text adventures. Most likely it would have started as porn before branching out to a more mainstream audience. Non-porn interactive fiction would probably stick to the "adventure-y" genres, stories involving lots of action and problem-solving and not much in the way of dialog, simply because coding realistic, non-scripted dialog is pretty much impossible. I imagine survival horror, post-apocalyptic, and "Oh no, you're trapped inside a computer!" themes would have been popular.

    Eventually, someone would get the bright idea to stick interactive fiction on this new-fangled internet thing everyone's all abuzz about, allowing multiple users to play the same story. That whole dialog problem would finally go away because players could talk to each other. Essentially, we'd have skipped MUDs and gone straight to MUSHes, although probably with more of an emphasis on everyone logging in at a scheduled time to progress through a storyline than the sort of "in-character chat rooms" that I remember RP MUSHes being.

    Multiple users playing characters in the same story would open a huge can of worms. What happens when one player types "PUSH LEVER", and you're, like, "No way, man, I'm gonna PULL LEVER!"? If only there was some way to figure out whose character was stronger, so we could determine which way the lever went. Hmm. Maybe we could assign some kind of number to each character to represent their Strongness or Lever Manipulation Skill or something and then have the computer compare those numbers to see who gets what they want in a lever-moving conflict.

    Or when instead of "TIE ROPE TO RAIL. CLIMB DOWN ROPE" you want to tie the rope to Bob's character and lower him slowly down into the pit, but not so far that whatever's down there can grab him. Or whatever. Users would come to demand a level of sophistication and responsiveness that just wouldn't be feasible to code, so eventually someone would get the bright idea to have a human handle those kinds of scenarios. A "GM", if you will.
