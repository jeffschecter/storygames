AND SO BEGINS ANOTHER ADVENTURE OF DOCTOR WHO

This is a game about an unrecorded adventure of the Matt Smith Doctor that will be run as a play by forum game for 4 to 8 players. In it you will create a Doctor Who story and probably save the world. The alien menace is unknown at the start of the game. The players will make it up as they play.

Unlike in many role play games, our players are not limited to saying what their character does. Each turn the players will describe what all of the players do in a scene. The means everyone is in effect the Doctor, the companions and everyone else in the game. Each player champions one character but in the end the goal is to tell a good story rather than to make your character win. This is a competitive game though. As player make stuff up they earn victory points. At the end of the show the player with the highest total wins.

HOW TO PLAY THE GAME

It is helpful to imagine this game like a card game. There are location cards laid out in a row on a table. Each character card sits next to the location they are in. On the side of the table are a series of plot cards the guide the game through a story. Lastly each player has a hand of 5 action cards that they use to make things happen.

Each turn a player lays down an action card and makes up a little story about what happens in that scene. In this game you will do this by writing a description of a scene (please keep these to five paragraphs or less). If the description is fun players won\u2019t challenge it but if you put words into their character\u2019s mouth that they don\u2019t like they may challenge the scene. To challenge a player jumps in and says \u201cwhat really happened\u201d. A simple die roll determines what action happens. Each player gets one challenge a turn. It is likely that there will be few dice rolls in the game until the story moves to a climax.

If no one challenges an action, the 1st player wins the turn and gets the victory points from the action card. If a challenger wins, they get the victory points. After the turn the first player gets a new action card so players always have 5 cards.

Instead of playing an action card, the active player may instead pick a plot card and use it as the inspiration for their turn. Plot cards start off giving few victory points but they go up as the story progresses. They are about pivotal points so players will likely fight over who wins them. The game ends when the last plot card is played.

Turns end with people moving their character to new locations, or not. It this game though we will ignore movement. The people present are the ones you say are present.

After the end of the game each player gets to make one last statement about what happens to their character after the game ends.

Those are the rules. They are pretty simple. The action or plot card tells you what the scene is about. You make up the details. All you need to do is make it fun!

A FEW MINOR RULES

It goes without saying that the Doctor, Amy and Rory cannot die in the game. Everyone else though is fair game. If you character is killed, you are not out of the game. You continue to make up scenes as before \u2013 just through other characters.

As you can tell, this is a story telling game so the players have a lot of power to make things happen. You may also have noticed that there is no game master. This is true, but only a little. I will act as your game host. My job is to help keep the game moving but in the end I have only as much power as the rest of you have.

The turn order will be preset so players may jump in and make a resolve turns quickly. Please allow a day to pass between actions to give everyone a chance to make a challenge. If a challenge is made it is okay to jump in immediately if you want to challenge challenges. In this way we can move through turns very quickly. It is possible the game will only take a couple of weeks to play.

I will roll for replacement cards and send them out to you in a private email.

HOW DICE ROLLS ARE DONE

Challenges are resolved using a single die roll. I am going to trust your honor to tell the truth what you die roll is. The challenging player rolls 1d6. If they roll an even number, they win. If they roll an odd number the person they are challenging wins.
