    \n Wow, any kind of strict power-based system seems like an absolute disaster for a setting like Wild Cards. I mean okay, it's been like ten years since I read any of the books, but wasn't the cool part how everyone had totally bugfuck insane abilities? Exactly not the sort of thing you would model with some sort of 'buy X power with X modifiers and Y disadvantages' system? I mean, I think M&M is a very slick game, but no way in hell would I want to use it to play Wild Cards, compared to an alternative like Capes or another CR-style game where I can describe my super powers however the hell I like without worrying about whether or not it's technically allowed to have the Teleport ability chain off Blast (10).

    \n
    \n
    \n Wild Cards will be done as a campaign setting for Mutants & Masterminds, not as a stand alone game. The series is getting a re-launch in 2008 and that's one of the things that makes the license attractive. The first book, Inside Straight, is a fresh starting point for the series and brings it to the current day. You can bet that Tor will be making the most of George R.R. Martin's name in selling the new books and of course we will too. Since we're also doing the A Song of Ice and Fire RPG, we should be able to make the most of Martin's fanbase.
