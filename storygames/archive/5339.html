\n Polaris as a modern spy game? The hell you say!\n
Forget all that mythic tragedy beneath the fading stars stuff: I wanted to try Polaris as a modern spy game. It seemed perfect for the job, since the key conflict phrases looked like they would work really well for rapidly spawning the kinds of twists and revelations that are the meat of espionage. Flexible, but with a rules structure that fostered collaborative discovery. I was anticipating exchanges like:

\u201cI open the safe and find the stolen files. Go me!\u201d
\u201cBut Only If you also find evidence that your partner was working for the other side.\u201d
\u201cOkay, But Only If he did it against his will \u2014 he was blackmailed or something.\u201d
\u201cSure, And Furthermore you find information he gave them about you to set up that assassination attempt you escaped. Willing or not, he sold you out too.\u201d
\u201cDamn! It Shall Not Come To Pass!\u201d

    \nExcept for the terminology changes I mentioned we really ran it mostly by the book. It really does click onto the spy genre without much modification.

    I skipped over it in the post but naturally we changed the starting aspects -- Office: "Agent of Northstar", Ability: "Spycraft", and Asset: "Pistol" (if you wanted it).\n
    Posted By: Ryan MacklinWhat did you for the Zeal/Weariness/Experience/Tragic Fate deal? Are the spies fated to betray Northstar? What are the conditions you use for checking for Experience?
    \nAll characters still move from idealism to cynicism/world-weariness using the normal Zeal/Weariness rules. We're only one session in, so we haven't really had to tackle veteran-hood or anything like that yet, but I think the dark revelation in the spy genre is less about switching sides than it is about realizing that both sides are just as bad, that "we" aren't any different from "them." That's probably the Aspect that we'll apply when someone hits veteran ("Our side is no different than theirs").

    Experience checks are for all the normal reasons (callousness, cynicism, despair, etc) but also for being ruthless about the job, putting the mission above your morals/humanity. Anya (Ping) already scored a check for turning in her would-be love.

    \n Oh, hey, for a spy game, let's try this. An experience check is:

    Sympathy for an enemy agent, nation, or citizen.
    Antipathy towards an allied agent, nation, or citizen
    Antipathy towards an agent or citzien of the your own nation, or antipathy towards your own government.
    Apathy
    Callousness
    Cynicism
    Doubt
    Despair

    Note that it is totally possible for the Mistaken to force a situation where the Protagonist is necessarily going to get an experience check. Check these out.

    1) Your partner is revealed as a double agent. No matter what reaction you have, you get an experience check (because he is both an enemy agent and a domestic agent at the same time.)
    2) Your own government orders you liquidated. Bam. Antipathy for agents of your own government.
    3) An enemy agent asks for assistance in putting down a domestic terrorist group. Either you're sympathetic to an enemy agent, or to the enemy's civilians.

    The Mistaken should not feel bad about setting up these "no right answers" situations.
