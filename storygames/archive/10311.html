    Posted By: JanCould you please point out what the Paul Czege principle is?
    \nI think it's the one which states, in brief: "It's kinda lame if you put your character into a bind, and then you have the power/agency to pull them back out again without help" (that is, you can do it yourself, without outside help from others; we see lots of games where others (like GM) can put you in a bind where you have to get out. I don't think I've seen one where you can put yourself ain a bind and others have to help you out: Maybe Breaking The Ice?).

    Which John's not violating (I'd love to see the tickets issues for Czege Violations), because he tries to put them in a problem situation. Presumably he needs help/support of others to get his character out. Or, he TRIES to put his character in a trouble spot, and only others like the GM can solidify it ("Yep, that happens, and you're in trouble now").

    I like it too.
    But, usually only if my character gets to get out of it AWESOMELY.
    If it's a string of bad situations my character gets himself in, which she can't get out of, or gets out of by fate/luck/whim/help, I'm not as interested.

    -Andy

    \n Tongue in cheeck ~ the moment poor Sir Edric became a PC's avatar he immediately gained a world of complication to his life, every time you create a PC you've given them grief.

    I find that I like to suggest issues/obstacles that "fit with my character's story". E.G., An actual example (as I recall it, it was fast freeform play so details hazy): I'm a good cleric, young, new to city and I have made a friend of a copyist in temple. I have fun suggesting that a demon is trying to ensare my friend and either I recognize this and save him - heroic fellow that I am, or fail to and have a friend who's now (unknown to my PC) an evil cultist who will one day betray me. Either way, fits with my idea of the sort of story for my PC. Having set that up, I try like dickens to save my friend.

    Flags, Beliefs and other mechanisms like that are "authoring trouble" for your PC in that it's understood that "Aspect - Ladies Man" is pretty much asking to earn Fate Points for letting the wrong word slip while kissing persuasive lipts etc.

    That said, I've never done this as "win a conflict to define trouble", rather "create something that means trouble, suggest trouble, roleplay into trouble", then use game rules for conflicts to defeat trouble.

    \n John,

    Do you see the difference between being a collaborator in providing antagonism for your character and simply "wimping out" when your character is in the crunch?

    It's only the later that I have a problem with. It's fine if you say to the GM, "You know what would totally suck for my character right now? If his wife came home," and the GM's eyes lighting up and going, "Dude, you're totally right. Your wife totally comes home!" The point being that AFTER that's establish, you say, "You know what he would do? He'd lie to her face." And you pick up dice and roll.

    What's lame is when you do this: "You know what he'd do? He'd lie to her face. But I bet she'd totally see right through it." Wait what? Your dude doesn't even get a CHANCE!? It's lame when GM's do it. It's equally lame when player's do it.

    Jesse
