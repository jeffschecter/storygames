    \n Hi Will! Thanks for asking.

    OK, Night Witches is about the 588th Night Bomber Regiment, which was an all-female regiment that operated on the eastern front throughout the Great Patriotic War. They were absolute badasses, and fought sexism and Soviet idiocy as much as they fought the Germans. Stalin was sort of pressured into allowing female units in the first place, and they got the worst of everything across the board - they were flying twenty year old biplanes, flying harrassment sorties right on the front lines, as many as ten a night. It was outrageously dangerous and they developed hair-raising techniques for completing their missions. I read a bunch of secondary sources, but I realized it'd make a great game when I started reading first-person accounts of what they went through and what was on their minds - these were mostly 18-20 year olds. They had young adult issues related to family and relationships, they had issues of self-worth within the Soviet scheme of things, they had something to prove, and they were flying their crappy planes and getting killed nightly. They were watching friends die, then returning for barnyard mixers with neighboring all-male regiments. It's a very dramatic, tragic, odd story.

    So the game's very focused - a small tight game. Three players, each with a pilot and a navigator (who exists to support the unfolding tale of another pilot). The other constant and shared role is the Regimental commander, Major Bershanskaya, who debriefs flight crews after every mission. You learn about missions in retrospect, through the lens of the Major's debriefing, and there's a little card mechanic to guide the narrative. The meat of the game takes place on the ground, as characters respond to mission events and move their personal stories and that of the regiment forward*. Six scenes per duty station, six duty stations that tell the tale of the Great Patriotic War, from training at Engels Aerodrome through the Don counteroffensive, Stalingrad, Operation Bagraton and on to Berlin.

    It's still pretty early in development but ultimately it'll be a simple, small game. I'm excited about Night Witches and really enjoying working on it.

    *I should hasten to add that these are actual stories - "Lidya Smichov's Strange Romance", for example, that you title before play and discover as you go along. The Regiment has one, too, like "The 588th: Ferocious Sisters" or "The 588th: Gallant Socialists".

    \n You know, I almost regret getting interested in this, because I don't want to become the Carroll O'Connor of roleplaying, but you can't deny inspiration. It's a cool story, and here's what I'm seeing:

    * A women's story (it can be a feminist story if you want, and I think it will tend toward that in play)
    * A Russo-Soviet story (and like Grey Ranks I think players will learn something along the way)
    * An ass-kicking adventure story (biplanes! Nazis! Daring deeds against impossible odds!!)
    * An anti-war story (War sucks, bad. Seriously. You don't know how bad until you're flying over fucking Stalingrad without a parachute)

    So there's not really a child soldier angle at all, they were all eager volunteers and skilled specialists.

    Also, missions are not part of play - you get interrogated when you return, and we only discover what happened in retrospect, as a segue into the lives of the pilots. Anyway, it's not even remotely baked. Lots of "cool solution to this problem goes here" in the manuscript right now.
