\n I playtested my Jedi Blackbird hack last week and it was tons of fun. I love how easy it is to hack and run Lady B! A couple of questions came up, though, and I was wondering how other people would answer them...

1) Say you're playing in a group without a PC Snargle and the Captain wants to get all jiggy with the piloting. Without Snargle, no PC has piloting traits or tags. What do you do?

In our case, we had previously established that Snargle was along as an NPC and so when our captain ordered evasive maneuvers, we let the captain use Snargle's traits with his own pool dice. It didn't feel right to penalize the group or to remove cool skyship maneuver scenes because there's no Snargle. But I was sorely tempted to say that when things are put in an NPC's hands that they're put into the GM's hands, and then conditions and escalation can flow from that.

2) We decided that players could trade XP for dice if they got excited about something and wanted to supercharge their pool. Has anyone experimented with eliminating the XP / pool dice divide, and just awarding pool dice for hitting keys and then charging pool dice for advances?

    \n Here's an example from our Jedi Blackbird playtest.

    The module has a straight-laced Jedi Knight named Colonel Vaud Hurkoon and a ne'er-do-well junker scav named Franter Nord. The backstory is that the Jedi are sending an expedition to Franter's distant homeworld and they've paroled Franter so he can serve them as a guide. Vaud needs to refresh, but we're running low on time, so we have to be efficient.

    Vaud: My refresh scene is about when I first met Franter. I pulled him out of the spice mines of Kessel.

    GM: Did you have to bribe the warden?

    Vaud: Yes.

    GM: Do you have to bring Franter back to the mines? Did you purchase his freedom or is he more like a library book?

    Vaud: (thinks for awhile). Yes. He has to go back and serve his sentence.

    GM: What's at stake if you don't?

    Vaud: My good name as a Jedi.

    Franter: (profanity). Do I know about this?

    Vaud: No.

    Franter (grabs for the advance list and buys the Secret of Vanishing...)

    \n The thing with conditions is a separate thing from who describes the failure, as Zac pointed out.

    But, yeah, Jamie, sorry, I should have said, "LB doesn't specify who narrates what." The group gets to decide how they do it (any way is fine for LB). So players can narrate their failures if that works for you -- it's not required, though. The only thing specified is that players get to say how they get a condition.

    So, the GM chooses the condition, "Oh, so I think this makes you Angry, yeah?" and the player fills in the details. "Oh, yeah... I think I'll blame Snargle! Stupid goblin flipping the shacklin' boat all around while I'm trying to work!"
