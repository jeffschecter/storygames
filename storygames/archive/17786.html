\n Happy New Year! Time to let go of old burdens so you can take on new ones!

WHALE CHEF is a game design festival where you pass on the burden of an old "white whale" game design project (some impossible game you've been thinking about or working on forever, but haven't been able to get any real traction on). You lay down your game design burden, down by the riverside, and some other game designer picks it up, just as you yourself will pick up someone else's burden and -- here's the important part -- finish a short, playable draft of it in a few days.

Rather than taking up space here or elsewhere, I'm going to be conducting this mini-event on a dedicated Google Plus community. Please sign up for an account there if you are interested and don't already have one. If you're interested in participating, please join the community and further details will be posted shortly.

Thanks!

    \n When 'taking over' someone's game idea, are you suppose to be finishing the exact pitch or use it as inspiration to tweak with the initial concept? I can see people unwilling to give up their projects if someone comes along and tries to interpret whatever mess they made in a .Doc file. The stranger may not be able to satisfy such a demand from the original writer and usually anyone who really cares about a project that has been lingering for years will not do this because of that potential for the rules.

    I'm mainly trying to say be a little clearer on the 'picking up someone's burden' piece since there are many ways to do that and the author willing to drop said burden is more likely to join when they know how it will be treated. Love the concept, It is just too bad I don't have any projects that are wasting away.
