\n Green Ronin, in association with Firefly Games, will be publishing Faery's Tale Deluxe this summer. The 96-page deluxe edition will contain the original game plus 32 pages of new material, including adventures by Colin Chapman & Robin D. Laws, additional artwork, bibliographies & a filmography, all errata, new creatures, optional dice rules, a new Essence power, and more.

We're finalizing the deluxe draft for layout now, so if you have requests or suggestions for the ultimate version of Faery's Tale, post away.

    Posted By: Jason MorningstarI'm interested in your arrangement with Green Ronin. Can you discuss the terms? Who's doing what, and who's getting what as a result? How is it beneficial, and what were the trade-offs for you?
    \nI can't talk much about the details of the publishing deal itself, but the primary benefit to me is tapping into Green Ronin's much stronger market penetration, particularly in distribution & the book trade. And Green Ronin adds a product to its lineup that requires a lot less internal staff time than creating a new one, and one that lets the company connect to an audience of younger or introductory gamers. Plus, it's a proven product with an existing fanbase & some degree of critical acclaim.

    On a bigger scale, another benefit to Firefly Games is this puts me in a position to start getting out of the physical inventory end of things - all the time-consuming stuff of ordering new print runs, storing product, tracking inventory, packing & shipping direct print orders, & so forth. Since I run Firefly Games as a sideline to begin with, everything that takes me away from writing or otherwise producing games has a big effect on my release schedule, and the more of that I can start delegating through publishing deals, the more games & supplements I can produce.
