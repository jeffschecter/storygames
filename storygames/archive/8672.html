    \n I've got this post in my head that I need to work out in the next week or so. I'm playing Persona 4 now, and having a lot of fun: And between Persona 3 and 4, I see an awesome role-playing-game type of fun to be had.

    But the problem is that, for all their awesome trappings, they are one-player games. That's the way the world is set up. Making it a world for more than one (significant) player means coming to the point where you may have to unravel everything that makes that game special. Certainly it's the case of Persona, of Final Fantasy X, and other single-player console/PC games that I love.

    But there might be some ideas behind Solid Methods to break up a single-player console game experience into a multi-player RPG...
