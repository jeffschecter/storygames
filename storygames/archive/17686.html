    \n Basically it means the core engine has lower Refresh rates and a lower number of stunts which are more generically but specifically defined. There are also some jargon changes that collapse some definitions into each other. (There are no supernatural powers in the core itself.) Other than that the system is unchanged.

    \n Biggest change for me is now on a failure the player has an option to succeed with a cost, or on a tie succeed with a minor cost. Since this occurs before you spend fate there should be more options for complicated successes instead of the binary pass/fail or the need to always spend fate to succeed. Which is pretty sweet.

    \n The substantive changes IMO are how aspects are dealt with. Rather than Manouvers, Assessments and Declarations, it's boiled down to "Create Advantage" which is much cleaner as a mechanic. Same thing for clarification of the Tag vs Invoke mechanics.

    Colin -- since you qualify complicated successes, I should assume the "cost" is not simply more FP, right?
    \nYep they are full on "yes, and..." or "yes, but..." that the GM creates to complicate the success.

    So frex is the player misses a roll to jump roof to roof over an alley the GM could say you lose your sword on the landing as a cost or for a minor cost that you take a few moments getting to your feet allowing your pursers to close in a bit...

    Or the player can miss the jump taking some stress or spend fate to succeed.
