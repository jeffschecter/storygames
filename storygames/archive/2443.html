    February 2007 edited February 2007
    I've had the translation issue (and turning a translation down) as well.

    The way I think of it is (roughly) this: Someone comes up to me saying "I can help your book reach new consumers." That is almost certainly true. I don't think they're lying to me. The question is "Can you help my book reach enough new consumers to pay for having a whole 'nother person drawing profit from the same customer-base"?

    IPR, as a for-instance, can. My intuition (backed with facts and math that are real, but so dodgy that I refuse to share them!) is that if I went to selling the books directly, I would lose more gross profit than the margin that IPR charges me. They are creating the money that pays them.

    The traditional chains though? Can they produce enough extra money to pay a share to gaming stores and distributor warehouses and publisher offices and all that? Off of the new people they can bring to my book? Across broad swaths of the industry, the answer seems to be "No, they can't." Doesn't mean that will always be the answer, but it seems to be the rule of thumb so far.

    Well and IPR does the shipping and warehousing. I mean that's what makes Ben and Vincent have to do no work. So even if they didn't make for new customers, it'd be worth it just for that. Put another way, the only thing that Mongoose would do more than IPR is their marketing - which they've admitted in their estimate would be no better than the IPR/Vincent marketing. But would produce far, far less profit for Vincent.

    Here's the thing. The market for a RPG is quite finite in a period of time. There simply are only X people out there willing to buy any particular RPG at a time. And it's not hard to attract the majority of them. If, in fact, Vincent sells to that other 1000 people, that's 1000 people out of his revenue stream that might eventually buy at the higher profit margin. What incentive does he have to burn through his market at one tenth the profitability?

    And that all assumes that he would retain all rights and that the product would be in no way damaged by the Mongoose handling. Much safer to go with the plan he's on now.

    Now, all that said, would there never be a reason to go with Mongoose? I think that under certain limited circumstances that, sure, Mongoose would be the right choice. A D20 supplement, for instance, might be best coming from Mongoose. If you need capital, and they can provide it (though the start up capital for a game these days is just so little that this is would almost require the liscencing or something mentioned above to be sensible). I don't think that anyone is saying that Mongoose could never be the right choice. Merely that one should consider everything as Vincent did before making a decision.
