\n Hi guys,

I need your help with player stats.

I\u2019m writing a game for Nathan Paoletta\u2019s Two Games One Name competition called Hell for Leather. It\u2019s heavily influenced by Stephen King\u2019s \u2018The Running Man\u2019 (the book, not the film\u2026 NOT the film).

You play a team of contestants on an international game show called The Gauntlet. You\u2019re dropped off in the woods at night with a pack of supplies and a bag of cash. After 6 hours, they\u2019re going to send out the hunters. Your name will appear on every network on the globe. Every hour you survive, your family gets $5,000.

The game uses physical resolution (as per the competition requirements). If you\u2019re curious about that, you could look at the blog.

PCs are going to have three sets of stats. They\u2019re going to work like a see-saw (so having a high score in one will negatively affect the other). I need to figure out what sorts of pairings would be useful and interesting in a game about violence and pursuit.

Before I go any further, I\u2019d like to say that I want this game to explore consequences of redemption. In other words, I want this game to ask the question, \u2018how far are you willing to go for your freedom?\u2019 I imagine the PCs getting into hairy situations with the hungry public (who win cash if they betray the contestants to the network), and in resolving these conflicts I imagine a pretty high cruelty count.

Here are my pairings so far:

Defiance vs. Evasion
Empathy vs. Logic
? vs. ?

Defiance is for confrontation, Evasion is for evading, Empathy is the catch-all social bit, Logic is for ruthlessness, and also technology. What am I missing here? If you were playing a game about getting away, what pair of motivating stats would you expect?

Sebastian.

    \n Hi Joe,

    I considered your suggestion and ran a play test with a friend of mine. First we used Fight, Flight, Thought, Action, Trickery & Heart. There was a bit of cross-over confusion when Trickery would be used instead of Heart, and the segregation of those two diluted both. The same issue cropped up with Action and Fight.

    After that, we tried it without just the four attributes. Fight for confrontations, defiance and physical prowess, Flight for sneaking, hiding and getting away, Head for hacking, smarts and using technology, and Heart for insight, deception and manipulation. I think I'm happy with just the 4 stats. They cover everything in as much detail as is needed, I reckon.

    However, the naming is less suited than I'd like. Part of the flavour of the game will come from these stats, so I'd like them to evoke turmoil, tension and violence.

    Overt v Covert, Murder v Theft, War v Retreat

    Apathy v Empathy, Forge v Con, Brains v Charms
