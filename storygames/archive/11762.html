\n I'm not sure I really have a coherent topic here, but I'll do my best...
\n
\nI've been playing a bit of Microlite20 with my friends recently, and I noted that one of our adventuring parties is pretty much made entirely of Rogues and Mages. After some consideration, I know why this is on a metagame level - everybody wants to either throw awesome magical energy around, or be lithe and fast and sneaky... even if half of them don't actually act anything like that in play.
\n
\nAnywho, this got me thinking about the possibility of a game specifically designed for playing sneaky burglars and mystical wizards. Probably with a distinct line drawn between the two. Sadly, while I love the general idea, I can't think of any games that spring out at me as being perfect for such, and really wouldn't know where to start making my own, as the general idea is so vague.
\n
\nAnybody got two cents (or more) to add?

    \n Not to sound like a broken record, but Exalted might work for this. The setting has a distinct role for Scavenger Lords, quick-witted opportunists with a bit of magical knowledge who go into First Age Ruins and steal valuable stuff from amidst guardian robots and demons. The system is functional, if not spectacular. Using shreyas' Radiant (which you can find searching on this board) for it might well produce the desired results.

    \n This sounds like a neat group to of characters to either do an espionage game - wizard and theives infiltrating the castle weeks before the big army arrives
    or a hard boiled cop game with both the wizards as cops and the thieves as well thieves.

    As for a system, you need something that rewards stealth and secrecy as well as explosions and massive power slinging. And that has a good/robust/interesting magic system. Nothing comes to mind that you wont have to hack, but I could see Swashbucklers of the Seven Skies/PDQ being most of the way there, or Wushu if you want that bigger than life feel. Lady Blackbird might also fit.
