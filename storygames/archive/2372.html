    It's a pretty primitive plot structure (basically "there and back again"), but Errantry makes an attempt. Like its source material (medieval quest stories), its fine structure is episodic (a series of encounters), and its overall structure is "heroes accumulate what they will need to fulfil their quest, do so, then must return home changed".

    Shooting the Moon and Breaking the Ice both have pretty tight plot structures, based around love and the attempts to get love.

    Burning Empires has a plot structure that's very defined, via the infection/invasion mechanics.

    The Apocalypse material for Conspiracy of Shadows adds some campaign level plot structure, and can be found on the wiki here: http://www.bobgoat.com/wiki/pmwiki.php/Main/Mechanics

    The Mountain Witch divides the game into acts, with guidelines for the things that happen in each act.

    Spycraft 2.0 actually has a bit of plot structure, as the game goes through regular phases, with rules differing specifically between phases.

    Then there are some games that lack the formal structure, but that have mechanics that imply a structure or bring a meta-level growth of structure about: Polaris and Hero's Banner both fall into this camp.
