\n Collective Consciousness will be held on Saturday, July 12, 2008, at All Fun & Games in Apex, NC. This free mini-con features indie and traditional RPGs. There will be two four-hour slots: an 11-3 session and a 5-9 session. Linky: CollectiveCon web site.

To sign up, please email collectivecon at yahoo dot com. You can also use this email to submit a session that you'd like to run.

Here are the sessions that will be featured at the con:

    \n Thanks, Jason.

    To everyone who came and participated - thanks! It was a lot of fun. I played my first story games (though someone told me that apparently all RPGs are story games, so I'm not sure which terms to use now). Anyhow, Jason ran a game of Medical Hospital, which was awesome and hilarious (Andy came up with this terrific name for a doctor from India, and Jeph was the only one who could pronounce it properly, which he did without effort). The index-card mechanic was a lot of fun, and I really appreciated the way that Jason was able to get us prepped and playing in such a short time.

    Then Jason ran a game of Dulse, which was a Game Chef entry, if I recall correctly. It was a game of love, betrayal, war, lust, sorcery, and secrets. I played an aging Duke, Jeph was Princess Dulse (is that in any way connected etymologically to dulcet or the Spanish word dulce?), Andy was a sorceress, and Shane was the Captain of the armies (who seemed so benevolent at first, but gradually revealed a treacherous side as we progressed through scenes of childbirth, murder, and marriage). It was a great game that I look forward to playing again.

    Mark Causey's D&D4E game looked like a blast, and everyone really seemed to be digging the Dogs in the Vineyard game that Jason was running (you really put your shoulder to the plow, man, three games in one day!).

    So thanks to everyone again, and I will definitely keep you posted as I start to plan the next one, hopefully later this year!

    - Rafael

    \n Yeah, good con, glad to put another face or two to the names here.

    D&D4E was interesting to try out--without the feat (?) cards, it would have been WAY overwhelming, playing one's first session starting at lvl 15. I think it borrows a lot from other mini game techniques, to speed play; and they seem to work well in this particular combination. It was interesting to see how Mark changes, when not running something more "nar" (last NC Game Day, I played in his IaWA)--he seemed more "bubbly," maybe(?). Good, fun, jocular group (sorry, some names got drank Saturday night during the sad Sydney AFL game). Clerics are now fun (surprising)....

    Raf's Sick Six System is as slick as the d12 source of it in Dread: TFBOP. Switching to d6s seems to "humanize" the results a bit more, with far lower potential score differentials between attack and defense. This is probably fitting for the con game's "social sci fi" genre or anything lacking in (much of) the supernatural. But the Stunts are still there, in all their mad, wild glory. Oddly, for the first half (or more) of play, I really let the character source and pic (a mish mash of someone in Stargate Atlantis with President Roslin in BSG, the pic) influence my thinking and mode of play. A lot of order issuing--or "advice," until I declared martial law and proclaimed myself C-in-C after a bad bridge hit--and hanging back when the action began, looking for a way to "talk it over and work things out" (with Wraiths and psychotic ex-bodyguard/-lovers). That, and my chosen low Attack meant I was not much involved in combat, other than as a possible target (ceramic armor helped there) and as a source of wildly missing .44 rounds. Then, I remembered... this game is about going balls-out with stunts (get more dice for descriptions, and stunts tend to skew high for results) and letting the dice--and body parts--fall where they may. Next combat, Laura is leaping onto the back of flying Wraith/scorpions, using their own stingers against each other, before slicing her "mount's" entire set of right wings off, to ride it safely back down to the group like a half-humanoid, bleeding, poisonous, fire-breathing maple tree seed. Then she's running up it's back, leaping off it toward the cave wall, and slamming her knife into the wall, seeking purchase in cracks and seams to avoid plunging into some kind of laser cage at the bottom (IIRC, Andy said, "Kratos, President of the Twelve Colonies" after that one). Yeah, she stopped being the BSG Roslin about the time the talking stopped and the carbines opened fire.

    As always, Raf did a great job of NPC portrayal and pushing players to provide narrative content (including our opposition--in a Gozer the Gozerian "Choose and Perish!" sort of way). We didn't portray the opposition, though--just initial seed ideas, rough sketches, or out-right larceny: I was asked "OK, you've landed on the surface, but--Dave--your poor, poor back-up bodyguard and pilot didn't make it: why?" Thinking slow, I reply, "Fuck, uh, OK... this screeching, airborne, aboriginal beast stooped out of the clouds to snatch him away as we got out of the shuttle." (And later, an engineered cave has an orrery... *ahem* thanks, Pitch Black!)

    Yep... and them's the beasts the Wraths experiment upon to make the winged-scorpion-firebreathing Wraiths above.

    Great times; looking forward to Trinoc*coN (two weeks!) and MACE with relish.. though I hope there's an NC Game Day between 'em, as it's about a three month con-less gap, other than the MegaCon of Funk, Dragon*Con.

    \n Hey all,
    \nIt was a really great event, and I am so glad I went. Played the 4E game, which I think really showed off what's different about this version. I'd never really played a game with miniatures or even more, miniature mechanics. Made for a really fun experience. Our first encounter was 6 on 5 and we found it a lot easier to push, pull, and slide the enemy off the side rather than dps them to death. Which I found a lot more fun than just hacking them. Despite how much fun I was having, I still felt jealous of the guys playing Medical Hospital. Every once in a while I'd hear something like "Doctor, you've cut his lung in half," "45 seconds left," "Doctor what should I do? He's going to die." All in urgent and very realistic sounding tones. Sounded like a lot of fun.
    \nAfternoon was Dogs in the Vine for me, which I really, really liked. Totally different than playing D&D. It's so collaborative and free-form. Once I got the hang of it, it had it's own momentum, with the help of our great GM, Morningstar. Again, the snippets of Battlestar Atlantica I heard were very, very intriguing. Not that I can remember exactly, but "You kill the Wraith Cylon as Colonel Oniel applauds softly in the gateroom, Appollo and Dr. Shepherd go on a date to mark the occassion." Sort of an amalgam of all the overheard things.
    \nAnyways, it was a blast, and I look forward to seeing any of you in the future. I leave tomorrow for a month long road trip, so my single post will have to stand for me for now. Later.
