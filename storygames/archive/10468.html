\n Pretty simple question - which do you think is a more exciting adversary for an introductory demo game of 3:16 - Carnage Amongst the Stars?

I'm running a demo of it soon and I can't decide that one, key aspect. Who to fire bullets at? Space-bugs (ala tyranids, zerg, etc) have a classic feel and there's lots of fun with squishing and claws and such. Mutant humans have a bonus squick-factor and I can throw in tentacles and corrupted vehicles and such. Robots are pretty cool, also, with flying drones and laser cannons and flailing hook-arms.

So, fellow nerds, what do you think?

    \n It's the GM's call what the aliens and the enemy world are, so I'd suggest not giving the players any input on that at all. For me? First out the gate I tend to pick the one that's the example in the book: Forest World, Dinosaurs, High AA and Ambush. Go with something you are super comfortable with would be my advice. That choice pops for me because I love Predator, Jurassic Park, The Lost World, etc. so describing the environment is so easy.

    A close second for me would be the Artificial Lifeformed Eel Cock-Beasts, High AA and Swarm, location variable (Waterworld for Leviathan, Rainworld or Poisonous Atmosphere for Aliens or whatever).

    If I'm running it a lot at cons then I just pick from the list based on how I'm feeling -- crossing off choices as I go (so I don't repeat over a con). I really don't have any pre-conceived ideas and I don't roll for them.

    Anyway, what one do you have a super-realized world in your head for? Go with that one.
