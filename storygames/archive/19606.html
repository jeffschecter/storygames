    \nActually, the REALLY crazy thing about Smallville's resolution system is that it usually doesn't resolve anything! In a character-vs-character conflict (be that PvP or PvNPC) it only kicks into gear when someone decides to PREVENT someone else from doing something, and the actor only gets their way if the spoiler decides to give in-- the dice can't actually ensure the actor gets their way or force the spoiler to relent, they just punish the spoiler with damage for getting in the way (or the actor, if they roll poorly).

    Smallville's resolution mechanic privileges hurt feelings and melodramatic reversals over decisive action, which is what makes it so brilliant at evoking its genre.

You could get a black die, win the argument/fight/deal, and a cutscene or flash-forward could show how bad this outcome was for you.
    \nThis is a conflict resolution: you just don't roll for it.

    ---

    Anyway, I've been wondering for some time now how you can play a game without conflict. One way of avoiding it is when you create something in a collaborative manner. You don't play in a competitive way; you don't create obstacles for each other; the only thing you do is building on each other's ideas. I made The Murder or Mr. Crow based on this. Sure, you roll but you don't do it because it's you against someone else.

    Another way of avoiding to play with conflicts is to play out to get a sensation out of something, possibly by exploring parts of the game, may it be the group, the premise, the setting or even the structure of the game.

    There should be more ways. I can't see them now but I hope I will.

    When thinking about games of this type, consider that "no conflict resolution" doesn't mean "no conflict." It would be fairly easy to play almost any situation without a conflict resolution mechanic; it just means that the mechanical focus of play will be elsewhere.

    Swords Without Master doesn't have direct conflict resolution and still has lots of swordfights.
    1001 Nights doesn't have much conflict resolution and is still about life or death struggle.
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
