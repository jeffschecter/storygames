    Posted By: Justin D. JacobsonPosted By: Malcolm SheppardI'm curious about sites like Your Games Now, which seem to go for the same segment as OBS.
    \nIf Fred's numbers are to be believed, it is a pimple on OBS's butt. I never bothered to sign up because I hadn't seen anything to the contrary, and I didn't want to be bothered with another round of uploading/formatting for ever product I release.

    In my experience, the ratios have been something like 3 or 5 to 1 with OBS to IPR in terms of *PDF* sales (I'm pulling those numbers sort of out of my gut -- I haven't sat down and crunched the numbers). But I get 20% more profit with each IPR sale, so I like providing my PDFs both locations. Plus, IPR's my *only* print outlet right now, outside of direct to consumer POD stuff on Lulu. I think IPR offers a good alternative solution to OBS, overall (grain of salt: i own 5% of IPR), but at this point I'm very much of the perspective that pairing IPR with at least one PDF-only outlet is a good way to cover most of the market (without looking into 3-tier distro).

    As far as how the OBS vs other PDF e-tailers goes, in a given month I might:

    Sell mid-teens to mid-twenties in PDFs (SOTC and DRYH combined) on OBS;
    Sell a handful of standalone PDFs through IPR;
    Sell a couple PDFs through Lulu;
    Sell a couple PDFs through e23;
    If I'm lucky, sell one PDF on YGN.

    I like a lot of YGN's philosophy, but their do-everything-by-committee perspective has really harmed them, I think. I'm a big fan of democracy in some things, but sometimes business could use a little autocracy in order to get its ass into gear.

    All the same, there's no up-front cost (other than some investment of time) to sign up with Lulu, e23, or YGN, while OBS requires you to spend some money to get in the door. So personally I'd recommend at least giving the smaller boys a shot; you might only make a handful of sales through their sites, but when you do, you'll be making a fatter margin than OBS. Consider the cut:

    An IPR sale means IPR gets 15% of the sale price.
    A Lulu sale means Lulu gets 20% of the sale price (IIRC).
    An e23 sale means e23 gets 20 or 25% of the sale price (I don't remember which; I want to say 20).
    A YGN sale means YGN gets at most 25% of the sale price (in months where there's surplus, cash gets distributed proportionally amongst the selling membership, so sometimes this can be a few percentage points less).
    A OBS sale means OBS gets 30 or 35% of the sale price. For the privilege of them getting 30%, you can't sell your PDFs anywhere else (an exclusive contract); if you go non-exclusive, it's 35%. But OBS's volume in the PDF market, even with utterly fucked navigation, is hard to ignore.
