\nWays to make much noise but not change shit:

Start a thread on a forum or post on your blog about how something sucks with no suggestions on how to change it or how to make it better. Bonus points if you aren't specific and don't name names in order to be polite/passive aggressive and paint an entire group of people who can't agree on anything with a wide brush.


Ways to bring and be the change with clear examples:

Start the Ashcan Front like Paul and Matt because you see a trend in game design among the people in your community that you'd like to see quashed.

Start an ENnie award winning podcast about games of all kinds that you enjoy like Paul Tevis because your love of gaming is filled to the brim with joy.

Write and play and playtest a game and publish it on a blog / in a book / in a pdf document / in a pamphlet like...like a whole lot of people because you have an idea that you think is fun or because you see something great in gaming that isn't being utilized or because just because.

Start a blog about how to make play better like Chris Cinn and write amazing articles about procedures that you use at your table because you have found techniques that work at the table in play. Bonus points to Chris for brining racial issues in gaming to the forefront.

Review dozens of games on RPG.net like C.W. Richeson because you have something to say.

Start a site about indie RPG's and commit to moderating a forum that helps people publish the game they've always wanted or realize that they don't have to spend tens of thousands of dollars to publish their book and change conventional wisdom about how games are distributed, played and written like Ron and Clinton.


And even better, the trump card, the card that beats all, the strategy that stands over all others and wins:

Don't do anything on the internet at all. Eff the internet.

Get together with your meatspace friends and game. Introduce buddies to a new game. Play an old game that everyone loves. Talk about it after the game over beverages. Daydream about the amazing shit you and your friends created at last night's game at work.

Bonus points for going online and talking about what worked and even more bonus points if you go online and talk about a session that did not work.
