Games About America

Josh Roby
May 2006 edited May 2006 in Story Games
Okay, so. Based on something Jonathan said about his "Dynasty Warriors for the American Revolution" game, where you play Paul Revere except you assassinate redcoats by throwing red-hot horseshoes at them or something, it got me to thinking. How many games out there are about America, or to be all hoity-toity about it, about the American experience? Cause there's all sorts of games that will let you play through European history and experience chivalry and noble power-plays, and there's all sorts of asian fetish stuff out there, but what's available that plunks you down and puts you through a series of American themes?

Now, sure, you can play World of Darkness set in America. I don't think any of the WoD games are really about America, though, with the possible exception of some portions of Mage.

There is Ben's Drifter's Escape, which certainly qualifies (Ben, can you talk briefly of how your game relates to America-in-general?).

octaNe might qualify. This is what I know about octaNe: it has a red car on the cover.

I don't know about Deadlands, having never played. How much of it is really about taming the wilderness and how much of it is squashing horror monsters? How much does the game relate the two?

ShadowRun and its predecessor Cyberpunk probably qualify in this category in a weird sort of way -- the cyberpunk genre is pretty solidly founded on worries of the American Dream gone wrong, especially early in the genre. But how prevalent is the megacorp's domination and how potent is it to the story, and how much of it is pretext for getting you into a dungeon that happens to be a skyscraper?

The best example that I can think of is Dogs, which is about as American as you can get, and plunks you down front-and-center with some pretty central American questions (so you want to go off and do your own thing, huh? okay, now you have to not totally destroy yourself and everything you love doing it -- good luck!). Dogs is the only game I've played that really made me feel like I was on the frontier, too.

What other games are out there that might qualify? What would a game that is "about America" look like?

    This is a great thread. Damn near everything that I wanted to say has been said except one thing: I think that the pulp to paperback explosion going (for pulp) from the 30s to the early 50s, and (for the paperbacks) from the 50s to the early 80s had such a huge effect on genre literature of a thousand different kinds (romance, science fiction, horror, etc.) that it was felt far outside America and far outside the "genres" that made up the forms. So games that explore a particular version of the "genre" (i.e. Adventure! is not just a pulp game, it's a pulp adventure action period piece game) that sets it in that pulp-to-paperback world is springing out of something really very important about American literature.
