\n Okay, here's the deal.

I like playing structured freeform or system-light tabletop RPGs. Mostly my own designs (Society of Dreamers, Archipelago, generic fantasy hack etc). I like games that are like Shock: without the dice.

Now I'd like some recommendations, preferably for something that can be played in one or a few sessions. Any tips?

    \n Ok, back on topic, you lot!

    I've enjoyed Don't Rest Your Head on many occasions- the setting in the book starts off great and devolves into a pun-soup that (actually, totally shockingly) is not really my thing. And the dice are interesting, but seem to always get really pushy and shout "do this one thing! this game supports this one thing!" over and over, which i've never been able to get around. But somehow, the concept of the game is really wonderful and it comes out in the non-dice mechanics... there are questions for character-creation that i find to be really great. The Daniel Wood school of DRYH has the GM take the character sheet and tease out a lot of previously unseen connections between the various things that the character's author put onto the page, and then bring those to life in play. Play that a few times, it makes for dark psychological intimacy, which i think is right up your alley.

    \n Mayfair's DC Heroes. The exponential charts are really funky, with a strength of 3 being twice as strong as a strength of 2 and so on. Imagine if Batman tops out Human strength at 5 and Superman is a 25 ...

    Everything is based on two 10 sided dice and using an X-Y axis chart to determine your difficulty number. Hitting higher than your number generates "column shifts" which cause you do do more damage, fly faster, intimidate more, etc.

    Oh, and you can punch someone in their Body, Mind or Spirit!
