\n [supers] Frankenstien a new Supers Rule system
I cross-posted this thread on RPGnet. RPGnet sometimes "forgets" about the non-traditional and (sometimes) more elegant "indie" and rule-lite game systems out there.

My question for topic is... If you were to begin building your own Super Hero rule system, looking back to ALL the previous Supers game systems, also looking at non-supers RPGs, also looking at board games and card games, and looking at the style of comic books and super hero cartoons and movies...

If you were to line up specific rules to reference when building a NEW super hero game system what are the best of the best rules that you could look at "modularly" to gather and build your new idea?


EXAMPLES:

Are "Hero Points" necessary for a supers game? If so, which game system(s) have the best options for Hero Point rules? Would you combine or borrow from multiple games? Are there non-supers games that include a form of "action, karma, drama" points that you'd look to?

Getting your hero or a villain "Knockbacked" is a common element of supers stories. What system handles knockback the best?

Supers have a high degree of "swing" in the way their stories are told in battles and action. Meaning, even a lesser powerful character can sometimes (with a lucky or well planned shot) take out a Bigger threat. What rule or die roll type do you feel captures this best and most elegantly?




In general, if you were to list specific rule points from your favorite games that would be fun to look at while building a NEW super hero RPG - what would they be? From the oldest systems to the newest? From super RPGs specific to non-supers games to other game types such as Video Games or Board Games, Miniature Games or Card Games?
