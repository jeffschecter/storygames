I've been working on Perseverant, a GMless, collaborative story game about survival. While the full game doc is about 50 pages, there's a three page quick start in the very back that makes it easy to get started quickly.

I'd love to get a Story Games forum-playtest of Perseverant going. I'll serve as a facilitator of sorts, using one of the starting scenarios in the back of the book (rather than going through the process of creating 10 locations and 10 challenges for us). If you have an interest in playing as a character, just hop in at any time (just name your character and give them their appropriate Traits).

Ideally, I'd want the first few people to post to connect their characters to each other with relationship cards (per the rules), however that might be a bit funky to do so with a freeform group playtest like this. As such, try to connect yourself to one of the previous posters' characters the first time you post.

I'm not entirely sure if this will work, but, heck, what's the damage if it doesn't? Hope you enjoy. Game document.

Basics of a character
Each character is made up of four descriptive traits
1. History
2. Method
3. Ideals
4. Drive

When posting, briefly list your four traits and any relationships to previous characters that you can think of.

Basic resolution

Describe how you're trying to overcome the challenge, noting up to three relevant "cards" (your Traits or Relationships). You roll 1+ the number of denoted cards d6. 4+ is a success. Success lowers the challenge's severity by an equal amount. You return cards to your hand for each success you rolled, and leave others out of your hand for each failure. Complete failure (no successes) elicits a Setback.
