    \n Oh my GOD Go Play NW was Incredible! No, Amazing! Perhaps even Monstrous?

    Anyway, it was my first, so I'm ridin' the wave, baby. I met a bunch of SG folks, so now I have names to faces! I love that. Makes online interaction SOOO much nicer. And I scored some cool new friendships, and strengthened old ones. It was a truly special weekend.

    And I got to play cool games! Wow, did I ever! I played my first game of Jungle Speed; I lost but I bloodied the enemy. I played Ribbon Drive with Joe o' the Pub, and it was moving and cool. I played the Upgrade! with Ryan and Jason, which was an awesome way to lose my Jeepginity. I had a wonderful Nicotine Girls game with Ogre, Michael Petersen, and Johnstone. It was sad-funny. or maybe funny-sad. And I had a hoot of a Capes game (Villains won!) with some peeps: Alan Barclay, Matthew SB, and, um, Doug, I think, who I injured in Jungle Speed. And finally, my own Spectre of the Beast was smashing, with awesome players who had a blast. Very gratifying. Thanks, Mickey, Kelli and. . .Stephen? Sorry dude, I can't remember. But thanks.

    Thanks to Ping, Phil and Tony. . .you were truly awesome and embraced the credo of No Gamer Left Behind. Way to facilitate!

    Peace,

    \n I want to quickly recap my weekend, so I shall:


    3:16, run by Matthew Klein. Awesome bug killing. Unfortunately I had to leave a bit early.

    The Shadow of Yesterday played at a nice light tone. Ratkin rule!

    I ran Steal Away Jordan, which went really well (if perhaps a little shorter than I'd envisioned at 2 hours) despite me being a little nervous on account of Jason Morningstar playing in it;

    I played Carry, and it went swimmingly. I really need to buy this game. Post-enlistment homosexual awakening + machismo as a cover + a past dealing with all kinds of other prejudice as a black man from Alabama. Awesome, and kinda scary.

    The Upgrade was fucking fantasmagoric. I want more Jeep. Really.

    Games from the Norwegian Style book= short form rock, + Chris Bennett's awesome small game the Believers, about the aliens coming and going, was the perfect, calm, disturbing way to start Sunday morning. I want to thank you all (shoulda said this at the con) who were playing with me for being cool with skipping the seance game on account of my uncomfortable-ness.

    Microscope, Ben Robbins' game in playtest, is really fucking cool. I will play it with you. We shall see what histories we create.

    I totally enjoyed Ogre Whiteside's Combat Diaries, but as the last game I played, on Sunday afternoon, my mind was frazzled as hell. I'd love to play it again clear-headed.

    \n I am apprehensive to gush about GPNW and how wondrous and radiant the weekend was. There are no ALLCAPS big enough to contain that enthusiasm! The organization and execution were exceptional. I like that the format well accommodated both people who presigned for most of their slots, and folks like me who left most open to fill at the con.

    I gamed with some new peeps that rocked to an insane degree. I shared several game slots with James Cunningham, so got to really appreciate his quietly awesome contributions. Johnstone provided empowering opposition in Principia, and added greatly to Carry. Tony Dower as the humorously inquisitive Cleric(named?) in Friday's dungeon delve and running a smooth 7(!) player Principia...Yikes! I finally got to game with Blake, sharing both a short Shadowrun game and a jaw dropping Beast Hunters battle. Jeremy Tidwell not only drank scotch with me Saturday night, but blew me away with brutal and poignant flashbacks in Beast Hunters! Ogre ran a brutally lovely 8(!!) player Carry game, with Matthew Klein as the hard-ass Psycho. Hans was in that game as well, and his squad scene addressing the sexuality burden was one of the high points. Rock. Joel's Capes game was challenging, rewarding and satisfying.

    Extra special props to Ping, James Brown and Eric Boyd. I didn't get the players for my Saturday Contenders game, and was adrift after helping the one player find another game to play. They took me into their Geiger Counter game, and it was a wild and wonderful experience.

    I managed to track down spot in a game with Johnzo, an insane riot of a Beast Hunters game. It is extra cool returning to hang with folks met in years past. I was pleased to play with Alan and Doug again. Brendan rocked a chilling(irony, dig) Savonarola in Saturday's Principia game. I didn't get to game with Macklin, but did enjoy hanging out over a bit of scotch Saturday night! It was also nice to sit and chat with Carl, though I didn't end up in a game with him this year either.

    Another missed connection was Chris B, who gets big thanks for giving over Repugnata in Friday's dungeon delve when he was bowing out.

    The worst thing: Waking up Monday morning and realizing that somehow the world had not been transformed over the weekend. Didn't anyone roll high enough to Transcend? Weren't myths ended all damn weekend long?
