\n I am pleased to announce that the voting booth for the 2010 ENnie Awards is now officially open! To vote click the link below:

2011 Judges Voting

This year, you will have the opportunity to vote for the 2011 ENnies Judges immediately after you vote for your favorite products. If you would rather vote separately, you can visit the 2011 Judges Voting Booth at any time at this link:

http://www.ennie-awards.com/voting/judgesballot.asp

    \n Eppy sent it in to the ENnies and the judges read, played and loved it. So when those judges spoke of it eloquently it carried a lot of weight and others checked it out.     \nWell just in case you're not disabused enough, as I pointed out in reply to your similar "I am suspicious that they even know which copy they're talking about" comment at Canon Puncture: when it comes to Dread and the ENnies judge candidates Kennon Bauman, Tracy Michienzi, Sean Parker, and James Surano (all fairly regular denizens of either or both ENWorld or Circvs Maximvs), I know that both Kennon and Tracy have run several games of Dread each, and I\u2019ve seen and/or played with all four of them in various Dread games over the past few years.

    And I\u2019d be willing to bet that more Dread has been run by the aformentioned ENWorlders and several others on ENWorld and Circvs Maximvs since Kevin Kulp raved about it on ENW, subsequently winning the 2006 ENnies Innovation award, than possibly any other community, including Story Games, at various regional cons and possibly even home games.
