Just some thoughts I had:

\n
    \n
  • It would be really cool to run a series of games using the same setting, but switching the game system every couple of sessions
    • \n
  • Each change in system could describe a different set of characters and a different environment
    • \n
\n

Example \u2013 Explore the Shadowrun world with:

\n
    \n
  • Primetime Adventures Pilot + a couple of episodes where we introduce the world and kinds of characters
    • \n
  • Burning Empires/Burning*/Mouseguard for an adventure/heist \u2013 played as Shadowrunners
    • \n
  • Some other system for a megacorporation pseudo-governmental political drama \u2013 played as executives
    • \n
  • Mortal Coil for an exploration of the magical side of the Sixth World \u2013 playing only magicians and/or non-human characters
    • \n
\n

Has anyone done something like this and how did it work out for you?

    \n An acquaintance of mine, twenty years ago about, mounted a large "mega campaign" here in our city. He advertised for players in local game shops and clubs. The event lasted for over a year and was divided into a small set of adventures. You signed up and submitted a preference list for the adventure slot you wanted, being provided with the game system to be used and an exceedingly basic summary (pitch, basically) of what the adventure would be about. My dim memory provides this list of game systems used:
    - Teenagers From Outer Space
    - Chivalry and Sorcery
    - Call of Cthulhu
    - Justice Incorporated
    - Champions
    - Classic Traveller
    - Aftermath
    - Space Opera

    There were a few others, I believe. The campaign inhabited the same setting and each adventure was loosely connected to the events of previous ones: there was an over-arcing story to the whole thing. I played in the Call of Cthulhu and Aftermath adventures, although I really wanted to be in the Chivalry and Sorcery and Space Opera ones.

    The GM who mounted the whole thing did it specifically to (a) widen his circle of gaming acquaintances, (b) try out game systems he'd wanted to play but hadn't yet, and (c) act as a sort of general promotion of the hobby (i.e. demonstrate to the community that there were more games to play than just D&D).

    \n I've played in a wushu game that switched from HERO system to GURPS mid-play. That was the first time I'd witness that GM admitted to any shortcoming in the GURPS system, as big chunks of what had made the game fun and interesting vanished in the switch. I suppose the lesson to be learned is make sure that you understand and play to the strengths of each system.

    \n I'd love to see this done with Exalted's setting, with maybe different games used for different types of characters, not just different foci. Some games that would work well for this are those which share some of the same influences as Exalted does, such as\n
    • In A Wicked Age...
    • Mist-Robed Gate
    • Wushu
    • Houses of the Blooded (particularly for dynastic chapters)
    • My Life With Master (for chapters involving mortals or gods as characters, or just anything along the theme of usurpation that runs through the setting).
    • Toon (particularly for Fair Folk chapters)
