\n These wars are just in my head.

I'm going to be releasing Skulduggery, a new slimline version of the Dying Earth ruleset, but with plug-in settings. Each setting comes with card-sized character sheets and GM notes. I'll be releasing supplementary settings. My problem is, I have no idea how to release it. I could just go for 8.5" x 11", but I'm quite tempted to try another format, and maybe even make the character cards available in a deck. What do you think?

    \n What does "card-sized" mean? 3.5x2.5"?

    Do you want people to be able to play the game the instant it arrives, or is a little arts-and-crafts (cutting out cards with scissors, etc) acceptable?

    How much material constitutes a setting, beyond character cards? Is it easily atomized into 120-word chunks? I ask because you might be able to make the whole thing a set of cards. That's a direction I'm heading with two projects. It takes care of your form factor problem and works with a card printer's economy of scale in a useful way.
