There's already a hint of resentment that can build up if you're one of the first voted out of the game (usually because those first few lynchings are based on nothing but grudges, eliminating good players, and whim). Adding money to the mix would really ramp up that element of the game.

    That said, I would LOVE to watch a "professional" Werewolf game, played like a professional poker game.

    \n Well, going with default Werewolf with just the Wolves and the Villagers, two ways to award the money:

    1. Award the money to all Villager players if the Villagers win (even Villagers that were killed before the game was won), or all Werewolf players if the Werewolves win (even to Werewolves that were caught and killed during the game). This would make for higher stakes but wouldn't significantly distort gameplay motivations - and there wouldn't be resentment at being killed early (although there might be resentment from surviving players at those killed early taking a share of the prize money).

    2. Alternatively, award money only to the surviving players who win. This would significantly distort the motivations for both the Players and the Werewolves (you have motivation to betray and/or kill those on your own side).

    \n I was part of a group that designed a game show version of Werewolf as an assignment for a game design workshop led by Katie Salen. As we had a couple of guys with money gaming (i.e. lottery, sports betting and stuff) design experience, we ended up with a bit more complicated game than what Thomas described earlier. The major changes were that audience participation was added (audience phone voting counted as one vote) along with optional limited insurances against killing (use of which lessened your potential winnings).

    I probably have notes somewhere, I'll dig them up if I can.

    BTW, nice to hear that someone, somewhere had the same kind of idea for a quiz show. And got it to go all the way.
