    \n Basically, anyone can create a Fiasco playset based on their favourite book/film/RPG setting etc., and I'm sure Jason Morningstar would say the more the merrier. Interestingly, at a con I attended recently, the facilitator had created a Dresden Files playset, and before starting the game she asked how many of us had played the RPG. Half the players hadn't played it but we said just try it and see what happens. Well we did and, guess what, it worked! Those who had played DF delightedly imported DF references, and the others just enjoyed the characters and narratives they'd created.
    \nSo, Go for it!!

    \n Leo I am delighted that Fiasco was your gateway drug, and I hope you grind out some playsets soon. Our policy is that Fiasco playsets are things anybody can make, and we encourage that.

    I really, really want people making them to consider running it by us first (info@bullypulpitgames.com) for consideration as a Playset of the Month. We'll keep putting them out as long as we get high quality submissions. If you do this, you retain ownership of your creative work and give us permission to edit, format and share it free of charge. Our standards are high so don't be hurt if we don't bite, but we'd love to at least take a look. If you throw this Moby Dick thing out on the Internet without letting us consider it for PotM I will throw you in a try-pot.

    One exception is anything related to a licensed property, which we won't even look at unless you are the license-holder. Then we'd love to! But don't bother sending us your Welcome Back Kotter tribute playset, OK?
