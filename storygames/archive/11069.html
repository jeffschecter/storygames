\n Chris Bennett had a fun idea and I buffed it out a little:

1920. War rages between the Soviet Union and Poland. A power-mad Lenin and his most trusted servant - a former agitator and newspaper publisher named Stalin- are ruling Russia and the captured territories through fear. They have built a horrifying weapon known as the Zaamurets, a giant armored train capable of annihilating an entire city in minutes. When the Death Train's blueprints are captured by the scrappy Polish intelligence service, Stalin starts a pursuit of the agents carrying them aboard a steamer on the Dniepr river in Byelorussia. A young Senator of the Polish Second Republic, Ludmilla Olszewska, is aboard the steamer and secretly puts the blueprints into the luggage of a traveling salesman. Although she is captured, the Death Train blueprints cannot be found, as the salesman & his companion, a tall British ex-diplomat, have escaped to the bitterly cold steppe on the Ukrainian border.

Through a series of mishaps, the pair end up in the hands of a farm boy named Lukasz Sidorowicz, who lives with his uncle and aunt and rides across the frozen steppe in his aerosan snow-racer. Lukasz's uncle and aunt are viciously murdered by Cossacks who are trying to recover the plans, and Lukasz & the salesmen meet with former Polish intelligence agent (and pre-war tutor to the Bolsheviks) Iwo Karwicka to try to return the plans to Ludmilla Olszewska's home, Kiev. After contracting an American pilot named Marian C. Cooper & his garrolous Spanish companion Chuy, they escape a Soviet blockade. But when they reach Kiev, they find that it was destroyed on June 13 - by the Death Train. They soon find themselves captured and put aboard the Zaamurets. Although they rescue Ludmilla Olszewska from the Death Train after a series of narrow escapes, Iwo Karwicka sacrifices himself and is slain by his former pupil, Stalin.

The good guys reach the secret Polish arifield at Zamosc, but the Russians are in hot pursuit with the Death Train, and plan to annihilate Poland's last hope of saving the besieged city of Lwow and the trapped government of the Second Republic. The Poles must quickly find a way to eliminate the Death Train before it destroys them as it did Kiev! Can a rag-tag band of surplus biplanes and their foreign pilots - the Ko\u015bciuszko Squadron - save the day? Will Lukasz Sidorowicz hit the one weak point on the Death Train with the single 100 pound bomb hanging beneath his ancient Breguet-14?"

It's a chocolate bacon of an idea but I get the feeling the story has already been told elsewhere. How would you make it game-worthy?

    \n It's hilarious. But, as Ben says, impossible as a narrativist game.

    However, it's eminently gameable as a gamist game. Do it up with awesome minis. Run it as a convention scenario. With that write-up you won't have trouble filling the game. Make sure every player has more than one character, or that you assign new characters as the set scenes progress, so if the British diplomat dies early, everyone stays engaged. Have fun genre-reinforcing rules. (Like the Cossacks have a terrible hit accuracy, except off camera.) The fun, as you say, will be in appreciating the color with a sort of double-vision.
