\n Previously on Story Games, Elliott Belser solicited players/students for his pay-for-service RPG club. There was much wailing and gnashing of teeth, but in the end it was deemed kosher by most, if not the best place to put the ad.

This led to comments about an explicit contract between players and GM.

Could you comment on the one I've posted below?

Edited to add: spot on comments. Contract amended.

THE BERKELEY ROLEPLAYING CLUB CONTRACT

THE PLAYERS MUST...
- Arrive with payment, or pay in advance.
- Arrive on time.
- Give 24 hours notice of absence for a refund.
- Make a good faith effort to follow the rules and regulations of the game, and all house rules.


AND THE GAME MASTER MUST IN RETURN...
- Arrive at least 15 minutes before the lesson begins to prepare.
- Have a lesson and scenario prepared in advance.
- Assure a space for every player that pays for a lesson at least 24 hours in advance.
- Provide beverages and food at a cost of $5.00 per person or less.
- Provide all materials, such as rulebooks, dice and character sheets.
- Provide a refund for unsatisfactory games, less the cost of food and drink, when asked.
- Only run games when at least two players are present.
- Warn a disruptive player once before banning them from the table without the chance of a refund, and ask them if there is any way that we can incorporate them into the game in a less disruptive manner.
- Make a good faith effort to follow the rules and regulations of the game, and all house rules.
- Brief and debrief all players.

THE PLAYERS MUST...
- Pay in full no later than the day of the game.
- Arrive on time.
- Give 24 hours notice of absence for a refund.
- Attempt to clearly set the stakes of all character actions by stating why the character wants to do something.
- Discuss, and if needed, veto other player's actions to ensure they make sense in the context of the game and it's setting.
- Follow all rules and regulations of the chosen game, except by mutual agreement to substitute house rules.

AND THE GAME MASTER MUST IN RETURN...
- Arrive at least 15 minutes before the lesson begins to prepare.
- Have a lesson and scenario prepared in advance.
- Assure a space for every player that pays for a lesson at least 24 hours in advance.
- Provide beverages and food at a cost of $5.00 per person or less.
- Provide all materials, such as rulebooks, dice and character sheets.
- Provide a refund for unsatisfactory games, less the cost of food and drink, when asked.
- Only run games when at least two players are present.
- Provide adequate story time and attention to all players.
- Warn a disruptive player once before banning them from the table without the chance of a refund, and ask them if there is any way that we can incorporate them into the game in a less disruptive manner.
- Assist players in clarifying unclear stakes by asking "how" an action with too-high stakes happens and "why" an action with too-low stakes was attempted (Example one: "why do you shut down the reactor?" "The lower heat signature will hide us from the pirates." Example two: "How do you save us from imminent death?" "I redeem the Prince of Nightmares by telling him I love him.")
- Say yes to or allow a dice roll for all character actions.
- Ask everyone at the end of a session if they had fun and why or why not.
- Follow all rules and regulations of the chosen game, except by mutual agreement to substitute house rules.

Some of this is no-duh, but needs to be there; some of it is a naked man in woad dancing on a harpsichord singing "Look at me! I'm a lesson in Narrativist gaming!" and both are needed for this.

Any other comments on it? It's still being revised at this point. Thank you in advance.

    \n Elliott, it's kind of weird seeing the social contract things mixed up with the financial contract. I don't mind signing up to "pay in full no later than the day of the game", but I'm worried I'll be in breach of contract if I fail to "Follow all rules and regulations of the chosen game".

    Graham: It was an accident! I just forgot to change my hit points!
    Elliott: Too late. You broke the contract, Walmsley. Get out.

    Seriously, it's a funny mix of important financial obligations (e.g. "Only run games when at least two players are present") and social obligations. Also, I think you'll freak new players out if you ask them to sign a document guaranteeing they'll "attempt to clearly set the stakes".
