    \n Once upon a time I was working on something called Sorcerer Unbound. I was able to break things down into "discussion topics" and address some of them piece meal but I could never get the thing to gel into a coherent "how to" style book. I, sadly, lack the ability to settle on a "target audience" and found myself thrashing between writing something to speak to imagined reader A only to have to go back and rewrite it because I didn't think it would be clear to imagined reader B. In a fit of frustration I dumped a lot of the discussion topics here on Story Games. They are archived here: http://bloodthornpress.wordpress.com/category/hacks-supplements/sorcerer/

    Christopher Kubasik's project Play Sorcerer is the one you contributed money to. Christopher is still working on it. I know him in real life and we've had many email exchanges and even some face-to-face meetings over it. In many ways, I'm much happier being Christopher's sounding board than I was trying to write the damn thing myself. Christopher is also much more qualified to speak to some of the more fundamental narrative issues than I am because he's a professional screenwriter. The down side of this is that he has many, many, MANY projects in the works which he has to juggle.

    Jesse

    \n Hey, hey all.

    Guy, shoot me with a whisper for you email. You should have gotten one. Andy, you too... if you want one delivered to a specific email address.

    Here's what my life is like right now:

    I have a three ring binder with about twelve tabs. Each tab is a different project for TV or FIlm or the Internet or Play Sorcerer.

    I set my timer for 45 minutes and work on that project... and then set the timer again and work on the next project, rolling through the project list.

    Some of the projects are doing re-writes for scripts already sold (I put Play Sorcerer in this category), others are original scripts I'm writing right now; others are brainstorming for scripts I want to write in the future; and still others are ideas for meetings at production companies or studios who like scripts I've written and want to see if I've got any pitches they want to buy.

    A year ago I wasn't having meetings at studios. Now I am. Things are busy -- and good! But I'm still working away on Play Sorcerer.

    We sold a few copies of the chapter at GenCon. (The chapter being for sale was more of a concern for Ron Edwards than for me; I think he wanted something to draw traffic at the booth. Or something.) The Boot Camp went really well. I was able to test certain concepts and phrasings and get immediate feedback. Most folks had "ah-ha!" moments. A good time.

    CK
