    \n In the absence of any other info, 1. has a better feel. "Dance" and "Wits" have parallel ending sounds; "Dance", "Blood", and "Wits" are all short one-syllable words. "Blood" and "Cun" share a vowel sound but ABB- is much worse than ABA-, plus "ning" just kinda hangs there, uselessly. Neither strikes me as particularly... striking. I agree with miedvied that "Blood", maybe in that position, feels like the culprit.

    Of course a better title would be "Blood Dance: The Cunninging" but I imagine you're saving that for the sequel.

    \n I suspect many respondents in this thread favor originality in their game concepts and are viewing "derivative" as a downside. Personally, I think a title that sounds like "A Song of Ice and Fire" is a great idea, given your goals to emulate it. "A Dance of Blood and Wits" is the best option on that score, I'd say.

    If you want a title that communicates what your specific game adds to the mix beyond Song of Ice and Fire, though, I'd definitely go for something else. "Blood", "Wits" and "Cunning" are all fairly expected and not very interest-grabbing in themselves. I'd prefer things like "A Dance of Jibes and Duels" if you're pushing the courtly angle, "A Dance of Grime and Viscera" if you're pushing the brutal combat angle, "The Dance of Low and High" if you're delving into the nobility vs smallfolk themes, "A Dance of One and Seven" if you're focused on the competing religious pantheons, etc.
