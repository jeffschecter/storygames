    \n I think this is a good question, but I not sure what you are asking for.

    When you have characters of different power levels they often have different levels of agency in the fiction. A God probably have more agency then a spirit, and the spirit might have more agency then a small child. Power levels are often about differences in agnecy in the fiction.

    But we also have a player level. If I understand your post correctly you want games where the players have azn equal agency, even if their characters have different power levels?

    Have I understood correctly?

    If I understand you correctly I think this can be done in many ways.

    One way of doing it narrative way is that letting both the God player and the Chil player have the same chance of narrating the outcome of the conflict. Lots an lots of Story Games do this.

    Another way of handling this that often done in Nordic larps is by letting the characters have different levels of agency but respecting the players expedience and creative agenda equally. Lets say you can either play enslaved, or you can play the slave owner. The slave will have much less agency in character, and the slave owner will have much more agency. In a conflict the enslaved is likely to loose. but out of character equal focus giving both the players playing enslaved and the players playing slave owner will have a great game expedience and their creative agenda respected. The slave expedience will be about the lack and struggle for agency. While the slave owners will be about having power and abusing that power. They are both protagonist both their stories will differ.
