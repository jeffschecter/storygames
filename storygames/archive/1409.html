Highly Structured Campaign

marknau
September 2006 edited September 2006 in Story Games
I'd like to make a highly structured campaign mechanic for my WIP, to wit:
1) Players decide how much time they want the campaign to take
2) This implies a certain amount of Progress Points overall that must be earned to reach completion
3) There is a Progress Points schedule that tells us what elements we should be including at various steps along the way, e.g.:
"This is the last mission of Act I, so any player who doesn't have a class 2 complication should get one tonight."
or "We're still in the middle of Act 2, so everyone increase a complication by 1 before we start tonight."
"We're in Act 2, so I can transform this motivation into a new one at +1"

Something like that. The idea being that the mechanics of the game recognize the overall structure, and the players steadily move through and are guided into doing the "right sorts of things" in order to make it all fit and work out right in a roughly predetermined time frame.

What sorts of ideas do people have for things that might show up on that overall schedule?

    So, like, before you run away with the spoon and this mechanic, I'd think about that - are you building a timing mechanic or a pacing mechanic? Which do you want?

    This sounds very like my WIP Errantry. I have something that was originally (or rather, unoriginally) referred to as "quest tokens", now "boons", which work pretty much as you describe.

    Things that might show up on your schedule:

    * Act 1: We learn where the characters come from, why they are doing what they are doing, and why they are doing it together. By end of Act 1 all major characters, all major conflicts and all major resources should have been seen and we should have a clear idea of the mission/goal.
    * Act 2: All of this is firmed up and explored through conflict and complication. New angles appear on the factors seen in Act 1.
    * Act 3: Things move to their denoument as the major players maneouver for advantage and come to grapple. Resolution and wind-down.

    This is almost certainly not what you were looking for but it's my best attempt. I'm slowly working on a wiki in this general space, you might find some more ideas there.

    At the 300 mark, we're starting act III
