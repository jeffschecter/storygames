    \n I'm not going this year, so I don't know if this is/will be in the program guide anywhere, but I always signed up for a game called "Duel II". You basically get four or five different character sheets with numbers you can rearrange and weapons/armor you can take. Then they use a computer program to run your characters in battles against other player characters. They produce a printout every 4(?) hours that describes your arena battle along with moves, dialogue, etc. You move up (or down) in rank depending on how you do, and there's a special arena combat at the end. You can adjust your characters skills and equipment if they aren't doing well, and you can replace characters that die. When I was stuck in a company booth all day and doing company events all evening, this was one of my favorite things to do since I didn't have to be there, and my friends could pick up the sheets for me as long as they knew my 'box number' for the printouts.

    \n There's a food court in the mall attached kitty-corner to the convention center. Quite often you can quickly get sort of decent food there without any hassle, which is not the typical Gen Con experience.

    I highly recommend Games on Demand, which is a lovely place to spend any downtime in your schedule or to drift over to when you are at wit's end. They'll put you in a game (the name is a tiny bit misleading!), it'll be surprising and fun, and everyone is friendly and enthusiastic.

    You can get almost anywhere without setting foot out of doors - for example that food court is right next to a weird sky tunnel leading to the Embassy Suites (which is usually a destination for late night pick-up gaming, although I'm not sure who has colonized it lately).

    \n Eat, sleep, shower. Hydrate, hydrate, hydrate. And, while it sounds obvious, don't forget to go to the bathroom frequently. Careful of heat and sun, and occasionally thunderstorms -- skywalks help a lot with these.

    I second the Games on Demand recommendation.

    Beyond that? There's a whole lot going on. There are tracks I never see -- film and anime and music. I go into board game areas and card game areas when I'm rejoining my spouse, but otherwise, generally avoid them (except for the occasional game of Dominion). For me, it's GoD, the dealers, and the games I've signed up for -- I used to do the auction. Well, not the auction itself, but the auction store, where there are often really good deals.

    For the dealers, have a plan. It can be "We will go up and down all the rows." For me, these days, it's "Okay, I must go here, here, and here." Inside the exhibit hall are the artists, and they're selling -- but also happy to talk about their work. I try to leave time for that.

    Remember to leave enough time to go between places. Figure about 20-30 minutes from Crowne Plaza to JW Marriott, maybe 5-10 minutes less from the convention center to JW Marriott. It may take less in practice, but at least that first time, give yourself extra time. Some of the hallways are confusing.

    Noodles & Company extend their hours, iirc, and a couple of other places do as well. Shake Shack, iirc, is 24 hours, but does tend to be quite crowded. If you want to do somewhere fancy-ish, make a reservation if you can. You may not need it if you get far enough from the convention center, but it won't hurt.
