    \n One of the problems Fastlane has already, due to the roulette wheel, is a too-strong assocation with modern settings since the wheel sets off all these associations in the head that dice do not. Setting with guns and helicopters, etc. I feel that encouraging this would be being bad to the game, like abusive. There's nothing wrong with using these settings in Fastlane, they are nifty and awesome settings, but it's not designed for them in particular, and I want to avoid pigeonholing the game even MORE by choosing a cover like that.

    This may be a pipe dream, a sacred cow that needs killed. But it just feels WRONG to have a cover that suggests a setting that strongly, in a game that is meant to be earnestly setting-free, and /theme-strong/.

    \n I'm not working on anything. For a while, it was mostly because I wasn't getting enough actual play to feel like I should be designing. Now, it's at least partly because I'm not feeling up to finishing anything. There are still projects sitting in my workfile that I care about and want to finish sometime, but who knows when I'll actually get to it.

    The Real Me, the highschool slice-of-life game formerly known as Heartbreaker, is stalled out on the shoals of me not being able to figure out how scene framing should work for it and nail down IIEE.

    Perfect Blossom, the distributed-character-ownership Magical Girl Apocalypse project, is stalled out because I've realized that it's more of an IDEA for a game than a GAME, and I can't seem to make the jump. Plus, writing anything coherent for it was really kicking my ass - I know what I want to have happen, but explaining it is hard.

    Shadow Galaxy Legend, my Epic Space Opera tSOY rebuild, fell down and went boom on the shoals of me not being able to explain adequately what was cool about Legend of the Galactic Heroes.

    In short: I suck.
