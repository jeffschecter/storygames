    \n Metamorphosis is intended to be permanent (although you can theoretically change skin to get out of it). It's the "Dark end" - whether dark out of pathos as you are forced to live in the swamp or dark because you seek revenge on the cruel world that turned you into this. That's why it's a very mechanically powerful move; you're now a tentacled horror that NPCs run from while screaming in terror (and the metamorphosis/corruption combination can be used for even more pathos - corrupting where you sleep is not optional).

    Cover skin is a widget that only comes into play choosing new moves. I should put it in the starting notes - it represents you both being like others and a layer of excuses, and plays into the consuming secret part of the skin.

    \n After some thought, the thing I think Masquerade lacks that is common in similar moves is an alternating choice based off of how the other character reacts. It may not need it, but I think I'm used to seeing it with other skins. Serpentine's "Big Reveal" comes to mind. And to a lesser extent the Sex Move for the Chosen. There are different mechanical consequences for either choice. Since a lot of the skin is about punishing the character for being different, it may be that offering a choice would be counterproductive. It may be that giving characters an incentive to only dislike the PC may be what the move should do. But I think the absence is what I picked up on.

    I'm not so sure I like Metamorphosis as a (semi-)permanent change. It seems like it would take a player out of the parts of the game that involve being a teenager. It's too much of an end for me. Even more so since it's something the Deep One could start out with.

    I think I'd also really like to see a gang advance in the list. I'd love the Deep One equivalent of an LGBTQ support group. Or maybe a network of online friends who are similarly alone in their own areas?

    \n Both those are deliberate decisions :-) But as deliberate design decisions that are twisting away from the baseline, those are the two parts that I think need serious playtesting.

    The best, mechanically, the Deep One can hope for when revealing themselves is to not be rejected out of hand. This is partly a game conceit that makes it different from the Serpentine's reveal and partly because my experience of Monsterhearts players is that we're an incredibly accepting bunch. For the Deep One the acceptance is always conditional with a chance of being taken back.

    People starting with Metamorphosis is possible - but at that point Metamorphosis is a pretty terrible move and the mechanical incentives are strongly against taking it*. The epic tragedy of Metamorphosis in the course of play, on the other hand, is something I want to see play out if the game is heading that way - as is the seeming inevitability and then the change of skins. It isn't something I expect every deep one to take, but is something that should IMO be there as much to hang over the character as a possibility as anything else.

    And the gang is something I've wavered on. The best I've got so far is "You have a network of Shoggoth-kin".

    * Is there a genuinely ugly skin anywhere? One that causes revulsion on sight as the central theme? Because if not there ought to be - and that would cover the point of a pre-metamorphosed Deep One.
