    \n Thanks, MKAdams. I realized modern plots can fit as well as fantasy tropes. Everyone else, your ideas are awesome as well. A few of my own...(I have a system of my own where I call gods/goddess Divinities).
    \n
    \nA cruel entity is imprisoned underneath a volcanic mountain range. The PCs are in charge of keeping it from the outside world. It is whispering to them, promising them power in exchange for being released. Its favors start with information and slowly evolve to greater interference in their affairs. However, accepting help means the prison grows weaker. Will they be able to find out before it\u2019s too late? Are the promises genuine or were they lies to get out of its prison?
    \n
    \nMany creatures have stories which trace their origins to being transformed by a Divinity. One of the animal species has gathered together and they demand to be changed back. There were not changed by the PCs. They tell a tale of woe designed to move them. However, their removal from the natural order will cause environmental harm. Is it more important to right an ancient wrong or to save the environment?
    \n
    \nOnce an eon, a creature who is able to increase the power of one divinity has been roaming the galaxy. The Great Hunt is on. Will the PCs partake of this event and if they find it, how will decide who receives the benefit for catching the creature?
    \n
    \nA group of divinities has decided to dispense with priests and other church officials. They are charging a set rate in exchange for various benefits. Followers start to worship them because they think that they have easier access to the divine. Are the divinities engaging in a social experiment or are others forcing them to sell their power to the highest bidder?

    \n There is a rumor among divinities, a strange and wondrous substance has been spotted among mortals. It is called Ambrosy, and the rumor states that its consumption gives mortals divine powers for a short period of time. After some investigations, the Ambrosy is tracked down to one Immortal who is selling it to mortals. What's the true agenda of the immortal? How will the divinities deal with the chaos of so many mortals suddenly gaining (even for a blink of time) reality altering powers? What's the side effect of using Ambrosy? What would happen if divinities consume it themselves?

    \n It's a modern, jaded epoch; and the gods are losing adherents. As such, there is almost nothing for them to do--no petitions or prayer to answer or ignore; no wars to prosecute or pervert--and they are developing ennui. To spur themselves out of this cycle of doom, they have challenged each other to entertain, amuse, or inspire each other. Each god's approach is unique, in keeping with its bailiwick.     Another (Iliad inspired) idea.

    A war among mortals keeps escalating, and soon the divinities are "forced" to choose a side. Some are being loyal to their subjects, others are in it because of personal gain, and even some are in it because of mere boredom and joy. (Remember that even "peaceful" Aphrodite entered the battle at some point.)
    \n...and, of course, the key to making this really work:

    The gods have the ability to grant the mortals powers that can wound, kill, or otherwise piss off or terminate other gods. (So, you go to convince the mortals not to fight, in all your godlike splendour, only to discover that your enemy has given their war chief a spear that could very well kill you.)
