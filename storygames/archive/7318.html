\n THIS THREAD IS EXCLUSIVELY FOR: Johnstone Metzger, Tad Kelson, Matt Weber, Thor, Christian Griffen, KT.

So, you've got your road trip: A bunch of strange souls in a heap that's falling apart (possibly held together by nothing more than the will of its occupants?), looking for anywhere but here. Resources are low but things always seem to sort themselves out in the long run.

Now, we hit play on the second song of Thor's playlist, as he passes out copies of the lyrics.

You will create your character while this song is playing. We pause the tape again after the song ends, while we finish creating our characters. Then, we discuss our characters.

The rule is: At least one Trait or Future is inspired by the song.

Create for your character three Traits: words or short phrases that summarize parts of your character's personality or experience.

Create for your character two Futures: Futures are goals or objectives that your character has. They see this road trip as a possible venue for addressing those goals or objectives.

One catch in Long Road Nowhere is this: You aren't allowed to talk about the future, in game. The only exception is what's listed in your Futures. So if you want to talk about finding a nice hotel to sleep in when you reach your destination, or finding a soulmate and settling down, you need to write that into a Future.

Alright. Hit play. Write a character. Then share.

    \n "Average" Sam, not Joe even if the name is
    \nJoseph Samuel Franklin
    \n
    \nMiddle aged, middle section spread, thinning and greying, losing the touch and not as concerned as he used to be.
    \nOnce he was going to be something, be someone.
    \nNow he is on the move, been a while now come to think of it, he muses, with dust covering him he waits to see what is over the next ridge, or across the oncoming bridge.
    \n
    \nAll around pretty average
    \n
    \n3 Traits
    \nA drinker, of all sorts of beverages, hot or cold, adult or anyone, caffeinated or not, it does not matter
    \nHas a fear of body fluids, especially blood
    \nNomad at heart
    \n
    \n2 Futures
    \nOne future is to find out which drink has the answer in it, the flask or the coffee cup
    \nThe other future is related to finding where his heart belongs, that one place that feels like home
