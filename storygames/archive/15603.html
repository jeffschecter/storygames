\n My first game this year is a game about mobsters who grow steadily more corrupt as the game goes on and slowly push away the people they love. I've got some really great ideas on how to make that part of the system sing but where I'm having issues is trying to figure out just what the heck mobsters do! What I have so far:

Intimidate an Innocent
Intimidate a Corrupted
Gamble with your money
Gamble with your health
Gamble with your life
Make a scene
Stay hidden
Steal
Drink

What else do mobsters do?

    \n Report a weapon "stolen". (Have your buddy do the same).
    File off a weapon's serial number.
    Bury a body.
    Beat the "Prisoner's Dilemma" by upholding a code of not snitching.
    Go to prison instead of talking.
    Make friends in prison.
    Learn how to be a better criminal in prison.
    Upkeep the organization's hierarchy in prison.
    Kill discretely in prison.
    Get out of prison.
    Be rewarded for not talking and going to prison.
    Find that you have no legimate options as an ex-con.

    Become increasingly callous to violence by committing more heinous acts.
    Become increasingly desperate as the law learns more about you.
    Become increasingly trapped in a situation that is illegal and dangerous, but staying in the situation is always, in the short term, in your own self-interest.
    Become increasingly reliant on the company of fellow criminals to feel normal.

    (Wouldn't it be interesting to play a gangster game with a mechanical Callousness rating that would require lengthier and lengthier narration of violent acts the higher your score increased? Could it be matched by a Desperation mechanic that dictated pacing?)

    \n Lamb of God's rather poetic chorus of "Violence begins to mend what was broken" comes to mind. (The song is "Omert\xe0" and it's excellent as metalcore goes.) Anyway, I was thinking that the signature fact of life for criminals is that they have no recourse to civil society when something goes wrong. If a promise is broken, you can't sue to recover. If your chattels are stolen, you can't call the police. You need to obtain satisfaction by threatening or executing violence, and your threats are made credible by the people who will back them up. If it's just you, even if you're the most notorious sociopath around, you'll eventually run into someone who rolls ten deep, and your run will end. So:

    Build up your gang power
    - Groom your peers
    - Kiss ass
    - Recruit (worth saying again in this context)
    - Subordinate
    - Be seen with your gang
    - Do "little favors"
    - Take one for the team (again)

    Feather a nest in "straight" society to enable non-omert\xe0 conflict resolution
    - Be seen in church
    - Philanthropy
    - Pay off cops

    Omert\xe0
    - Gather your posse
    - Exact revenge
    - Buy your way out of trouble
    - Be the bigger man
    - Accept an apology
    - Negotiate a tense peace
    - Dirt nap

    \n Lead an increasingly double life as you transition from thug to a higher-up with only financial ties to illegal activity
    Either become sophisticated or become garish and think you're sophisticated
    Feel increasingly untouchable while you, in fact, become more of a target
    Worry less about outsiders attacking you and more about insiders betraying you
    Watch your kids grow up, love your wife and parents
    Decide whether to bring your kids into the business or keep them out
    Deal with divorce and the chance of loosing your kids and also your wealth
    Get old and have ever-more medical problems
    Get tapped by associates in other cities to do "outside work" and "travel for business"
    Deal with your personal assistant
    Find positions for and clean up the messes made by your associate's children
    Develop overseas connections
    Interact with people from the home country
    Establish legit businesses
    Attend court proceedings
    Deal with lawyers
    Plan your exit strategy and spend time fantasizing about retirement
    Eat good food
    Throw temper tantrums and berate your underlings
