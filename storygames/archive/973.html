I'm looking to create a demo for Don't Rest Your Head. I'm figuring the idea here is to create a compact, 15-minute-or-less scenario that showcases enough of the setting and mechanics to tweak someone's interest. I've got a reasonably solid conceit: the PCs will be pregenerated characters based on famous missing people (Amelia Earhart, Jimmy Hoffa, and D.B.Cooper are on the shortlist), dealing with a strange threat to the Mad City itself.

So, that's where I'm starting from. To those of you who've crafted demos for your games -- what am I missing? What sorts of things need to be kept in mind when building a demo?
  • TonyLB
    July 2006
    How many mechanics do you have. Like ... seriously ... how many individual rules? My best bet? You'll probably be able to convey three.

    If three rules is all the rules you've got (Dogs in the Vineyard's demo gives pretty much all of the system that matters by saying "You get dice for these things (including escalating)," "You raise and see this way," and "How many dice you see with has import") then you are good to go.

    If your system has more than three rules (Capes, for instance, has significantly more) then what I would recommend for a demo is to make up a toy system that generates play that feels like your system feels, and that can be expressed in three rules, and to demo people that system.

    Well, they should immediately be able to do the things the game is about. If it's monster-killing, then yeah.

    I played a Cyberpunk demo where I spent the first ten minutes with my little japanese tough-as-nails biker girl trying to figure out a way to get out of a school. After ten minutes I bailed.

    I would totally have had buy-in if the very first thing the GM said to me was "You're riding your motorcycle away from the burning remnants of the school your parents tried to dump you in." I'd be like "Damn, YEAH! This is why I wanted to play this character," and you could throw any number of people trying to get me back under control and I'd give anything to keep doing what I wanted to be doing.

    If you haven't already, have a look at Tony's "10 Minute DitV Demo", which is the third post down in Demo How-To?.

    What I like about this is the piles of dice. There's no character sheet, no stats for the player to worry about. Just piles of dice which they roll at particular times. (I think the dice should be different colours). So essentially, the Dogs system (Traits, dice, statistics, relationship) has been slimmed down to this little encounter, which is a little microcosm of a Dogs game.
