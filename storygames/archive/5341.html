\n Ah, all this talk of maps has got me thinking.

I'm about to run a game set in a small ish town somewhere in the northern United States. I don't care exactly where. I'd like to present the players with a map of the town so that it can be a focus and inspiration.

Where can I find one which I can use? Online, preferably. Ideally free, but I'd pay for a cheap map I could print and use. The nicer looking the better. I've looked at just getting Google maps and pointing it to a likely looking town somewhere and jiggling it until to looks how I want it and that's OK ish, but I'm wondering if there isn't a better option.

    \n Sorry, missed some of that out didn't i?

    Modern.

    Smallish, I'm thinking probably around the 5K population. Say 3,000 - 10,000

    Not coastal. For some reason that doesn't really work for me. Probably because I live on the coast.

    Not really bothered about the social/economic status. whatever that happens to be for this town will become a feature.

    I guess I'm as much looking for inspiration as anything. The more I think about this, the more I want to experiment with the idea of using a map (and maybe a printout of the town's Wikipedia entry) as a starting point for the story. The seed, if you like.

    The game will be modern horror/supernatural investigation. In that the players will be creating a situation as we go along.

    I've been looking at Estherville and Algona, Iowa as examples. They're ok, (particularly Estherville's Meteor and Algona's "World's Biggest Cheeto") but I'm in no way tied to them. Also, maps of Iowa look really weird to me. I'm used to looking at maps of England, where the roads are all, y'know, wiggly!

    \n How about Morris, Minnesota - 5,000 souls, a university branch to bring in oddballs, a very, very typical midwestern layout (railroad tracks paralleling a downtown strip), grain elevators, mysterious Crystal Lake that only shows up on satellite maps, and a pair of dubious-looking cemeteries buffering the university from the town. Corn, soybean and hay country (probably lots of corn now). Minnesota's largest recorded earthquake happened here ... 13 violent crimes and no murders last year. Stop by for Prairie Pioneer Days in July and the Stevens County Fair in August. Outside the University it looks like a pretty conservative place.

    \n The small towns I grew up in often had "cartoon" or "caricature" maps available for tourists. These were bright, colorful illustrations of the town with oversized landmarks. Local businesses would pay to have their building drawn in along with a small ad.

    Googling "cartoon map" came up with this, to give you an example of what I'm talking about.

    Something you might try is finding a town you like and checking their website to see if they have some kind of tourist map to download. If you had the requisite Photoshop skillz, you could even add you're own stuff to it ("Happy Valley High School -- Home of the Fightin' Zombie Cheerleaders", etc.)
