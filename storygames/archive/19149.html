- What was/were your favorite game(s) released in 2013?
or alternatively
- What was/were your favorite game(s) played in 2013?

    \n Released? Probably Fate Accelerated Edition, though I'm feeling very fuzzy about what did and didn't come out last year. Also, there are a lot of games that came out that I've not read / played, so didn't think of listing. (Sagas of the Icelanders is definitely one of them.)

    Played? That would be most of them. I tend to be decent at selecting games I like at conventions. Highlights include:

    Matt Weber's Monsterwarts, at Dreamation. Every other game I played at Dreamation also rocked.
    Kevin Kulp's TimeWatch at Metatopia. I'm waiting with baited breath for the kickstarter.
    My Kerberos Club Fate game. I had wonderful players, and we ended the campaign when one moved away, hitting a proper, triple-marriage episode. I also started running Masks of Nyarlathotep for Call of Cthulhu and Eternal Lies for Trail of Cthulhu. Both are going well, due to a combination of my insane ideas about how to do game prep and the players.
    Fate Accelerated at Games on Demand at GenCon. Also, I got my dose of steampunk with Victoriana 3rd and Kerberos Club Savage Worlds. Victoriana 3rd did something very cool -- each character sheet existed in male and female version, and players could pick their gender. The point the sheets were making was: Yes, the world of Victoriana can be sexist, but you should never run a sexist game.
    The Build Your Own Larp in 24 hours from NELCO, where I was morale officer for the authors, proofreader, and last minute insertion to make the numbers. The larp is called The Night Queen Princess Fluffykins Passed, and it is an all-cat larp. It is an astonishingly good all-cat larp, dealing with mature issues that get trigger warnings -- public urination, euthanasia, forced sterilization, and drugs (such as catnip). The trigger warnings are tongue in cheek, sure, but they're also serious.

    Okay, this is sort of a top five list, for values of five that are variable. I'm stopping before I add several more.

    \n Fairy Tale Assassin League was my most successful game ever. I've sold over 400 copies mostly through stores.

    The ideas from it are jelling nicely into the game I want to stick with in future miniatures games. In retirement (in twenty years or so) I want to run pick up miniatures games. Got a great set of 15mm minis for sci fi games. The card story mechanic allows a combo of minis and story elements without a ton of rule books.

    \n\nFebruary 2014 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n The players at my games club are so firmly entrenched in D&D it's stupid, but during 2013 I managed to talk 4 of them into playing Mouse Guard and we had a scream.

    The number of laugh-out-loud moments have been too high to count, with twists for failure leading to the party's raft falling to bits and them being swept downstream, a bridge they built collapsing; plunging an old mouse (whom they insisted on pushing around in a wheelbarrow) to his doom, and the attempted capture of an owl resulting in members of the party being catapulted through the air.

    The party have also been paranoid about creatures coming back from the dead (don't ask!) and upon defeating a badger in combat, they cut off it's forelegs so that if it DID reanimate, it would have to push its face through the muck to pursue them.

    I can't remember having this much fun GM-ing a game.
