    With Great Polaris: The only way to gain the power you need to save the world is, step by step, to drive everything you love towards annihilation. Recovering Aspects? Uh ... why would we have rules for that?

    Breaking the Bed: You play the neglected stuffed animals of two young teens who are falling in love. Your goal is to encourage them to fall back upon their childish feelings in the face of the rigors of a mature relationship ... if you can infantilize them enough to break up their relationship, you win! If they end up having clumsy, tender adolescent sex it's all over for you, you might as well stamp "Garage Sale" on your plushy little foreheads.

    The Mountain Roach: The university sent you to a dry and arid mountain in what was once Mesopotamia. You have fellow professors, but obviously they did something to merit this hell-hole assignment. You can't trust them, but you need them in order to ascend to the peak and recover the famed Shab-al-Hiri Roach. I mean, you can hardly have sundowners and a "Halfway up the mountain formal" all by yourself!

    Burning Bacchanal, Revised: The "Duel of Cocks" system (new in this edition) brings mechanical structure to social arenas that have previously had to rely on fiat and player performance.

    Elfs in Capes!: Sure, the ability to fart the entire 1812 overture is fun, but do yuo deserve it? How will you prove yourself worthy of your abilities?

    OK, here's some more...

    Indie RPG + Mainstream RPG
    My Life With Rolemaster - Now with more tables!

    Indie RPG + Board Game
    LIFE With My Master - My Life With Master played using a Game of Life board

    Come to think of it, you could just mash the games up with themselves. Mix up the title, judiciously add some punctuation, and Voila!

    In the Dogs' Vineyard - You play a townsperson in a small isolated religious community. Your town is under the heel of the so-called "Watchdogs of God", who ruthlessly crush any deviation from cultural norms. You, however, don't agree with some part of your town's beliefs. How far will you go to protect your Pride? Just Talking? Sin? Even Sorcery?

    Wheel Burning - A no-holds-barred RPG about the intense lives of race car drivers. Use your Beliefs ("I am the greatest driver ever!") and Instincts ("When surprised, do a bootlegger's reverse") to gather Artha and fight your way to the finish line. Very popular, despite the strangely dissonant "Drive!" system used to resolve races.

    The Witch Mountain - You play a pair of unearthly children trying to make their way to Witch Mountain to find their parents. As they make progress, they have to decide whether or not to trust the people they meet on the road. Is the old man driving the RV a friend, or an agent of the greedy masterminds who want to kidnap you for their own purposes? Eventually the children may even question their trust for each other. The game relentlessly pushes them from one conflict to the next, until their terrible secret is revealed in the final confrontation with their "parents" on the slopes of Witch Mountain.

    Kill Satan For Puppies - They're just so cute! Who wouldn't you kill if a puppy asked you to?

    Do anagrams count?
