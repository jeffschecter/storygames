\n Hello all!
\n
\nJust got my copy of Monsterhearts on wednesday night from my Kickstarter contribution and finished reading it this afternoon. Here are my initial thoughts;
\n
\n*Easy easy book to read. I simply flew through this book faster than most rpg's. My familiarity with Apocalypse World no doubt had a hand to play in this but still, a good example of only what you need in there.
\n
\n*The section on Queer Content was a real eye-opener to me. Too often I as a player feel the entitlement of "my character, mine to define in every way" but the entry regarding how you don't get to decide what 'turns your character on' was excellent. This captures, in my opinion, a perfect sense of how teenage life sometimes slips out of our control even if we desperately wish it would not. Just for reference, I am a boring straight white dude and I don't know how this actually captures the struggle that young people with varying sexual orientations and identity go through, but I think that the respectful effort put forth is excellent. Well done! I look forward to embracing this style of play in future gaming and not letting myself get too wrapped up in deciding every facet of my characters so quickly any longer.
\n
\n*The Skins are all awesome. I felt the same giddy joy reading through these as I did when first reading through Apocalypse World. I easily pictured playing a melodramatic Mortal in love with an Infernal or a Witch who all the kids pick on and is sick and tired of being stepped on but can't break the cycle of feeling weak. You know, High School stuff. :)
\n
\n*The new Moves are all perfect to capture the feel and important content of a game like Monsterhearts. I can't wait to roll some dice and see how they play out.
\n
\nI have more good things to say but they just aren't coming to me right now. Nevertheless, great game Mr. McDaldno and thank you for making it. If you plan on being at GenCon this year and demo-ing or even running a game of Monsterhearts I would be extremely happy to participate, might even run it at Indie Games On Demand if there is a group interested! This is my first time attending and I'm driving in from Ontario to be there.

    Posted By: cczernia
    The one concern I had was the example in the back where the two girls start fighting and it ends with one going her darkest self. All I could imagine was the werewolf character killing the witch.
    \nLet's talk about this concern!

    I don't think the situation is as one-sided as you might expect.

    There are a lot of factors in the fiction that might complicate it - maybe the father is also a werewolf, and he's currently got a spirit connection going with the witch (his last "mate"). I mean, the Werewolf mechanics don't necessarily apply to the father, but one could assume that he is a werewolf and that his mythology mirrors that of his daughter.

    There are a lot of factors in the mechanics that might complicate it - the werewolf could screw up a roll, and you could turn their move on them. The witch also has agency in this situation. You should be asking "What do you do?" to her just as much as with the werewolf. Maybe she hexes the werewolf with a binding spell. Maybe she cunningly shoves the werewolf into the basement and locks the door shut.

    There's also a basic move called "run away" that the Witch can spring to at any moment. A good running away roll extricates her from physical danger.

    Finally, it's rare that a character is ever forced into death. When you take four harm, you can immediately erase it all by becoming your Darkest Self, or by losing all of your Strings. So the Witch can avert death in that situation pretty easily - things will just end up going pear-shaped for her.

    Situations like this aren't about "who would win in a fight." They're about "who can control their emotions and keep their shit together, amidst raging hormones and raging self-doubt." The mechanics of the game (hopefully, usually) ensure that the latter is what the scene is actually about.

    And, hey: if the Witch gets ripped apart in that scene, it's going to be awesome. She sleeps with a creepy dad, and gets rended apart by his werewolf daughter. Bloody revenge opera black metal awesomeness.
