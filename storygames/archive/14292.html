\n
\nConsensus about Cthulhutech seems to be "background and artwork are brilliant, rules are kind of shitty."
\nConsensus about Apocalypse World seems to be "rules are absolutely awesome."
\n
\nHow come nobody tried to do a AW hack for the Ctech background ? Until now that is.
\n
\npdf file: advisor.pdf
\n
\nMy rules to hack the thing is : the less changes, the better. Right now the changes are :
\n
\n\u2022 minor name changes : "weird" becomes "other", "barter" becomes "budget", "surplus" becomes "giri" or "asset", "gig" becomes "objectives", "gang" becomes "unit", "disease" becomes "taint", "followers" becomes "contacts", "augury" becomes "breach", "insight" becomes "intel" and I let go of hungry and famine for now but might find something more appropriate.
\n
\n\u2022\xa0the Shock countdown : works exactly like the Harm countdown but for psychological damage. Shock is a tag that things or actions have, and work like harm, except it's always area (everybody who see it takes shock). Stability works like armor for your mind. Has anyone come across a better way to handle mental breakdown in AW?
\n
\n\u2022 added some tags : mech (works like gangs- err, units with size and all but you have to pilot it instead of giving it order), behemoth (a size tag bigger than large)...
\n
\nSo far I've done the Ashcroft Advisor (see above), and I'm polishing up the Major and the Engel pilot. My goal is to map everything 1:1 if possible and change only fluff if I can. So far the Advisor is exactly 1:1 the Hocus, the Major is more or less the Hardholder (had to change an option here and there) and the Engel pilot is the Driver with a big Eva instead of a bunch of cars (I had to retool the vehicle rules a bit).
\n
\nI'll post them here as soon as they're done.
\n
\nThoughts, reactions, ideas, corrections, insults ?

    Posted By: Joe McGuffinGreg, this looks kind of amazing.

    How many playbooks are you looking at? It's been a while since I looked through CT, but I'm drawing a blank on notable archetypes after Tager, Nazzadi, sorceror, and maybe arcanotechnician.

    \nSince there are few differences between Nazzadi and Human I figured out I'd just put an option about races in playbooks (like "Human, Nazzadi or Xenomix", with "Whites" being thrown in for the traditionalist or - if I do one - the zoner).

    So far, the full list would be : the Major (Hardholder), the Fifth Engel (Driver with a twist), the Tager (Gunlugger with a twist), the Traditionalist (=sorcerer with bells and candle, the Brainer), the A-tech (Savvyhead), the Ronin (Battlebabe), the Squad Leader (Chopper), the Avantgardist (Skinner), The Advisor (Hocus), the Healer (Angel) and the Agent (Operator, but for a NEG agency - or even organized crimes).

    It's not that I'm taking CT archetype and rewrite them for AW, it's that I'm mapping AW playbook with the CT universe \n
    Posted By: J. WaltonOne thought: currently, the core Fifth Engel rule is kinda uninteresting, currently; +2 to most moves when in your mech? One of the cool things about the Driver is that their stats reflect which vehicle they're in, which doesn't happen here. Seems like a wasted opportunity, since mechs could definitely use some differentiation besides just their weapons. Vehicle weaknesses are also really cool and would seem to make sense for giant monster-fighting mechs.
    \nYeah, the Engel mechanics are a bit bland, but in the end, vehicles are mostly +2 power, +1 style with a few weaknesses. There also the fact that contrary to the Driver who changes rides, the Fifth Engel as one and only one Engel - having the *option* of piloting another one or even a replacement is a special Move.

    I decided I'd ditch the weaknesses to swap them for gang cues (savage, unruly and the like) to represent the fact that the Engel is alive, Taking "unruly" for example is a big thing : it means the Engel is now a full NPC listed as a thread and all. Same as savage : when the MC can make a move, he can describe the Engel ditching free from your hand and EATING THE ABOMINATION YOU'VE BEEN FIGHTING (and deal shock as well).

    But all these are in beta, of course, and any suggestions are a plus. I'm not that happy with the Engel mechanic as is, I'll try to figure something out for the final booklet release.

    Thanks you all for your feedback!

    \n The thing is, I didn't feel I had to. The roles are just so well fitted. I wanted to do a hack but the more I thought about it, the more it felt like a simple reskin would fit. I and it it does ! The themes of Cthulhutech and those of AW fit so well when you think about it. For instance, in the C-Tech rulebook:
    \n
    \n"ARCHETYPES
    \n(...)by helping steer Characters into familiar roles, and creating supporting cast that fit many other roles, you'll be one step closer to that anime feel."
    \n
    \nThe playbooks are big archetypes that suit a C-Tech sort of story when twisted accordingly. Oh, and
    \n
    \n"HOPE VS. HOPELESSNESS
    \n(...)from the anime side of the equation, you have the constant influence of hope, and from the eldritch horror side you have the eventual understanding that there is no hope."
    \n
    \nScarcity and despair, but still, the PC can make a hell of a difference by the rules of AW. Check. Or
    \n
    \n"SOAP OPERA
    \n(...)All kind of crazy soap operas occur in anime and your game should not be different"
    \n
    \nThat's just an abstract version of "create PC-NPC-PC triangle" in a way.
    \n
    \nI feel the rules are a perfect fit for the background, so just a reskin was enough for me. But still, hacking the Tager or the Engel was fun! So yeah, I'll probably do a hack, because it's fun, but I'd rather play now, because it's more fun. So when I'm done with my first AW campaign and we've had our fun with the C-Tech reskin, I'll do one.

    \n I admit that C-tech background never clicked to me until I read JDCordley's excellent post. My main beef with the game was the blatant disconnect between the themes exposed p. 248 and the rules that do nothing to lead the game towards them, and in places even prevent these themes from emerging through gameplay.
    \n
    \nAnd as it happens, from what I saw at the table, AW handles these themes far, far better, as in: "actually do something with them".
    \n
    \nThe rest to me is pretty much fluff, and that fluff I changed thouroughtly. I also refocused the character's playbooks to match the whole "your character is unique" schtick AW has. Hence, the tager is (supposedly) the last one, the Engel is the last of the first generation, the arcanotech is the next Ashcroft, etc. But I didn't feel I had to change the whole thing. If it ain't broken, don't fix it.
    \n
    \nI must insist that the prettiness of the sheets are only about 20% my doing. Skyller's ctech sheets are great and I, hum, "borrowed" a lot, the same with the C-tech art and art directions, both spot on.
