Upcoming 40K RPG Has a Little "System Does Matter"

J_Walton
August 2006 edited August 2006 in Story Games
This may be old news to many of you, but I just read up about the new Warhammer 40K Role Play property that Games Workshop and Green Ronin are developing. Their plans for this line are both really interesting and also rather frustrating.

They are releasing the 320p full-color hardcover corebook for the line in 3 different flavors, tailored for 3 different types of games: inquisition, smuggling, and killing things. So far it looks like players who are interested in more than one flavor of play will have to buy another 320p corebook (which blows), but it's interesting that GW/Green Ronin seems to have decided that one set of "universal" rules isn't going to deliver the right kind of play experience for people wanting to explore dramatically different styles of play. They've already announced that the inquisition version of the game will have a lot of different mechanics for morality and emotional anguish that will probably not figure prominently in the smuggling and killing things versions.

Personally, I kinda think that, in 320 pages, they would have had plenty of room (OVER 100 PAGES A PIECE!) for all 3 variant games, or that they could have released 3 much shorter, tighter, and more focused books, if that's what they wanted to do. Still, I'll be interested to see how this bizarre frankenstein mixture of what have traditionally been mainstream (huge, "universal" tomes) and indie (short, focused booklets) publishing formats works out for them.
