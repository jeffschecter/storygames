    \n I've noticed this about authors who spend a lot of time in one fictional world -- the world is almost always developed way beyond what was merely necessary to populate the protagonists' journeys. I think to get really into this sort of project, the fictional events have to seem real to you, and that includes the fictional place seeming real.

    Anyone who loves the long, immersive roleplay campaign above all others knows what I'm talking about!

    I am slightly surprised to see how much Tolkein turned his world-building into drawings, but then I think back to when I was more into world-building, and I occasionally did sketches of important lords' citadels too.
