    \n Well, given a couple of regular players in my group were away this week, we played with me GMing and two players, and that worked just fine. I'd recommend going for it.

    More players is probably a little easier on a GM, because there are more combinations of characters to consider when setting scenes. With fewer people, it's a bit more work to put things together and keep it moving. On the other hand, the story is more naturally continuous with less cuts from one situation to another.

    \n Technically, as I understand it, the way it works with unique items that are particular strengths is that you can negotiate for the loser of the challenge to mark it off his sheet, but the winner can't write it on his sheet. He can take it, though, and use it during the chapter. If he appears in another chapter he can then use the option of writing a new particular strength on his sheet -- and pick the unique item, as you said. (Unless the original character gets to it first. Check the order of names on the owe list before you negotiate!)

    An alternative is to negotiate for control of the item just for the current chapter: The loser doesn't have to mark it off his sheet, it just gets taken and used by the winner temporarily. That's negotiating for something that happens in the fiction, not something 'mechanical' in the rules. When (or if) the original owner of the item returns in a later chapter, his item has found it's way back into his hands, since it never left his character sheet. (Example: Elric never seems to lose Stormbringer for very long, no matter how hard he might try...)

    \n Always follow the internal logic of the particular strength. Is it the kind of thing that can change hands? Then allow it to change hands.
