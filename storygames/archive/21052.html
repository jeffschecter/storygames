Being possibly the last human on Earth with no "social media" accounts, I find it increasingly difficult to learn of new small press/self-published role-playing games that people are kickstarting, otherwise crowdfunding or just - you know - publishing. Most of the time, I discover them when they just happen to be mentioned here on SG or on blogs I read. Since "boosting the signal" is so important to make small presses & self-publishers be successful, wouldn't it be nice if we kept a thread going just for this purpose?
Furthermore, I suggest re-starting this thread monthly, or maybe quarterly - to keep the new stuff from being preceded by a zillion no-more-relevant posts a year from now, when a new or returning user first clicks on the thread.

TL;DR = Any new games for sale, for free or being crowdfunded? Please post a line here.

    The Imposters is an anthology of seven tabletop and live-action roleplaying games from seven different designers.
    \nWow (immediately impulse-backed)! Looks like it's anthology book time, down to a common undercurrent of retro-fashionable in the KS videos, even! In fact...

    Despite it being already included in @Teylen's very comprehensive collection, I can't help but give a shout out to a project I personally have a stake in:

    Crescendo Giocoso is an anthology of small-scale, "chamber" larp scenarios by Italian authors.

    It's a big (400-page) and gorgeous (I know 'cause I had a proof in my hands ten days ago) book which is bound to make role-playing look cool to the non-geeky friends who'll happen to peruse it on your coffee table. It includes a dozen (or more, in case stretch goals are met!) game scenarios ranging from 2 to 30 players, half of them by the very talented Oscar Biffi, our project leader. I'm just a co-author to one of the scenarios, but I feel really proud to be part of it (in case you're wondering, I'm the guy in a top-hat).
    These games are part of a distinctively Italian tradition which owes something to Forge and post-Forge English-language story-games, Vampire larps, British freeform, Danish Fastaval scenarios, Swedish Jeepform, Finnish chamber larps, "black box" games, "American freeform" and more, but is definitely a beast of its own. "Character sheets" amounting to biographies and detailed personality descriptions are often provided, rule-sets always kept to a strictly functional minimum, theatrics and costuming optional.
    Possibly interesting to some in the Story Games crowd is that Oscar has chosen, for this anthology, to break with the tradition of host-organizer figures in Italian larp, and instead required all scenarios to be rewritten as a set of instructions addressed to players: these are "GM-less" larps, possibly the very first ones ever designed in Italy.
