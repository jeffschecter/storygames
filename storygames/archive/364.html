Ok, as quick a rundown as I can manage:

The group plays a team of time travelers. In this vision of time travel, in order to avoid any one person gaining access to all the various skills necessary to time travel (due to some early close-calls with mad scientists), the skills (called Functions) are distributed - one for each member of the team, called a Pod. The pod travels back in time on a bubble of hypercurved 5d-space (hence the name).

The functions are:

Connect: This character moves the Speaker, the objects of the Maker, or the forces of the Mover into and out of the timeline.

Speak: This character enters the timeline in order to interact with the people of the time.

Know: The Know function senses the exact location of the disturbance in the timeline, and to detect whether the actions taken by the Pod have been successful.

See: The Seer records every aspect of the PodÃ¢â‚¬â„¢s mission, both within Pod and in the timeline.

Make: Makers fabricate objects that may be required to fulfill a repair to the timeline, including any costumes required by the Speaker.

You'll notice that there are two important functions missing - traveling through space and time. That's because those two functions are distributed between all of the members of the pod, and all of the members must concur before a move in space and time is accomplished.

There's one last job, that of the Engineer, who acts like the Operator in The Matrix - he/she stays behind in the 'present' to ground the Pod (which actually never moves from the spot) in their reality and monitor the situation. This is the 'GM', only without any authorial control - basically a referee.

A traitor is someone who, through their own hubris or some other reasoning, decides that they can do what everyone else has failed at - learn and take control of all the Functions and master time. Since this historically and inevitably causes havoc, traitors are put to death when they're discovered.

All of this is prologue.

The game, as envisioned, takes place in two sessions separated by two weeks. In the first session, the Pod plays out a time-repair mission, hunting down a traitor. At the end of the first session, they lose contact with the Engineer, and failsafe back to the present to find it completely devastated. Only the emergency hyperbolic field around the facility has kept it from succumbing to a massive change in the timeline. A last transmission from the Engineer confirms that the traitor is not only alive and well, but is in the Pod. One of the players is the traitor, and they've not only not fixed the past, but made it worse.

In the second session, the Pod has to go back and figure out who among them is the traitor, how they sabotaged the past, and fix it.

Ok. Got all that? Now, here's my problem:

I have no idea how to frame this game's mechanics. The whole idea was (originally) that the players would, using a token-bid mechanic, create an official narrative in the first session, working together. Using any leftover tokens, however, they could make (or appear to make) amendments to this official log, so that when they come back in the second session, things are not quite as they were in the first. However, since two weeks of real time have passed and the players are only allowed to take cursory notes (on one side of a 3x5 card), the idea is that they either will or won't remember the way things 'really' happened - but if they do remember and can convince everyone that something has changed, they're one step closer to finding the traitor.

So in the second session, the players aren't acting in concert anymore, they're in competition - using a twisted version of the bid process to create a metanarrative on top of the original one, where they implicate one character or another as the traitor. "It turns out that the circuit board that Bodi Made was faulty - on purpose - and it caused Flight 11 to miss the World Trade Center!"

But I just ... can't ... get my hands around this fucking thing. Every angle that I take, it just slips away from me. I'm hoping that maybe a question or a comment or a suggestion will crystalize this for me in time. I'm not married to the token system, necessarily - so if that looks like the problem, I can ditch it.

Help me, Obi Wan - you're my only hope!
