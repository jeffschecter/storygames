\n My group has been playing a lot of The Quiet Year recently. We really love the collaborative map-building element. We've played the Deep Forest version as well as making our own oracles to play with, and even a hack which uses Tarot cards instead of playing cards.

So I wonder if anyone can recommend any other games which feature maps as a core element?

Edit to add: We've played and loved Archipelago too.

    \nLabyrinths & Lycanthropes comes to mind, and World of Near introduces certain mapping techniques to The Shadow of Yesterday. How to Host a Dungeon is, obviously, all about the mapping.

    More generally, I find that map-making is relatively easy to introduce to various games. I'm getting a lot out of it in S/lay w/ Me recently, for example, even as it's not in the rules formally.

    \nEden has a centrally featured collaboratively-drawn map.\n
    All the players work together to create the Garden, drawing cartographical or symbolic versions of each land on a shared map. The Eden you make is new every time you play, so no two will ever be quite alike. You\u2019ll update the map as you tell your story, so by the end of the game, your map becomes a record of the changes your characters made to the landscape of Eden.
    \nhttp://lessthanthreegames.com/eden/index.html
