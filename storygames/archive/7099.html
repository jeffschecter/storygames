\n I'm going to be running a campaign about degenerate (and sometimes brilliant) noblemen who fight monsters for kicks. Think Lord Byron meets Van Helsing. Or John Wilmot, Earl of Rutherford meets Cugel the Clever...or Marquis de Sade...get the idea?

I want the addictions, afflictions, madness, and ennui of the characters, as well as their wealth and reputations to play a large part in the emerging fiction. I want the mechanics to reflect this. I've considered writing a homebrew system, but I don't want to reinvent the wheel. Intuition is telling me that FATE or TSoY could handle it, and a friend suggested looking into Heroquest.

Any opinions? Any advice on how to hack existing systems to do what I want?

    \n You're right about TSOY/Solar System. It is really good at addressing human-centric issues like addiction and obsession, and capably handles wealth and status differentials through Secrets. It's not as good at dealing with challenges outside human scale, so a lot of its utility would depend on the tone you chose.
