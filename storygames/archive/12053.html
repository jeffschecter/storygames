\n I'm laying out the Example of Play chapter for Happy Birthday, Robot! and I wanted to see how another set of eyes reacts at first glance. (Click to embiggen.)



A page from the Example of Play chapter. A lot of things are happening here and I'm really trying to make them as organized as possible. I've got pure text flowing for those "I learn by reading" learners. I've got images of the actual play area with example players for the "I learn by watching" learners. Captions below each image reiterate what's happening in the main text, too.

The examples are partly written in the second-person tense, as if the text were a teacher sitting in on a three-player game and directing the flow of play.

The second-person tense is also how I'm getting around the he/she/they pronoun problem. The text uses a three-player game as an example, so there can be one male player, one female player and then "you" as a third player.

I'm also tinkering with a video tutorial, which you can see a little bit of here, but that's beside the point. Is this layout working? Too much going on?

    Posted By: DanielSolisHowever, the leading is actually less than 1.5x the point size.
    It's not 1.5 times the point size, but the space from x-height to baseline should be 1.5 times the x-height.

    This is still just a guideline, but for Univers, that gives me 10/12 (rounded) and for Caslon that gives me 10/11 (also rounded). I guess for most sans fonts, a good text leading is 120%, while for a serif face 110% is pretty good. If there's a super high x-height, like those ITC fonts from the 1970s, you'll need to add enough lead to make King George go mad. Also keep in mind that the wider your text area, the more leading you'll need. I wish there were a real formula, but it really just comes down to eyeballs in the end.
