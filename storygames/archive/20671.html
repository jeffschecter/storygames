\n I'm off to Stockholm this weekend to play with some other GMs/facilitators from GothCon, since we're tired of never getting to play with each other at conventions due to running games for other groups all the time.

We'll be playing for a weekend and I'd love to bring some slick, hitherto unplayed (by us) game suitable for a one-shot. Drama is appreciated, though some silliness is acceptable. Hit me with your tips! what should I get?

Requirements:
* Availible as a PDF
* Free or cheap
* Playable as a one-shot without pregenerated characters and a prepared scenario
* Suitable for five players in total (including the GM if there is one)
* Fun!

Games in English are fine, and games in Swedish, French, German, Italian, Spanish or Portuguese are especially interesting. Hit me!

    \n My unpublished supervillain game Within My Clutches is usually high on silliness with a side of drama, but it's totally possible to play it with the drama turned up and the silliness turned down. I usually use pre-gens in con slots to save time, but if your group is decisive, you'd have time to make characters. There's an endgame, and if you want to get to it you'll probably need a good 3.5 hrs.

    It is definitely fun and I'm happy to send you the materials for free. None of them are pretty, though -- utilitarian layout, no art.

    The premise is the classic overreaching Marvel-style villain, getting themself in trouble despite their great powers because of some neurotic neediness.

    Villains take turns. GM and NPC tasks are shared.

    Interested?

    \n I have some games available on DriveThruRPG which fit the bill.

    The Final Voyage of the Selene: a guided narrative space opera freeform. During the course of 3 acts, bookended by a prologue and epilogue, up to six characters will explore their agendas and motivations, while the players are aware that their actions may be the cause of the disaster that engulfs The Selene and their characters may not get out alive.

    Monster Force Terra: a light storygame using the Apocalypse World Engine to tales of giant monsters who terrorize the Earth but also just happen to save the human race from an even worse threat in the process! You take on the roles of the kaiju in this game, going about your own business of seeking food, shelter and a mate, when disaster strikes! In getting back to the normal order of your lives, you inadvertently find yourselves becoming the heroes, though you may never be aware of what you've done. Play your monster as a pawn in the story, using them to achieve the goals that you want, by using them to stomp, steal or investigate the story's obstacles.

    Play Dead #1-4: a collection of four very short games with minimal rules and maximum fun. In 101 Zombies, you clear out the undead from a strategic site; Pyramid is a game of exploration & betrayal in the quest for a valuable treasure; Mystery of Life is a game of black humour for 4 players, where an angel & devil investigate a death and bring their evidence to trial; finally, Sawmill is a horror movie scenario where the characters must learn to co-operate against their tormentor or die.

    \n I'm spent! It was a nice weekend. We played both Follow and The Final Voyage of the Selene (as well as Montsegur 1244 and Dream Askew) and I had a lot of fun. Unfortunately we didn't play Within My Clutches, but I'll see if I can get a game of that going separately.

    Follow is great! I loved it, and so did they. Simple mechanics that on first glance seem pretty bland yielded a lot of good story. We played a group of monks trying to cure a disease but fighting against church dogma and people's superstitions. We failed. I'll try to write something about this and maybe make a new thread.

    The Final Voyage of the Selene was a lot of fun, too, though it felt a bit rough around the edges sometimes. Some agendas felt more appropriate and interesting than others, and the flavor text read aloud didn't feel like it had much to do with what was happening in the story. But there was shenanigans, some drama, and some great reveals and twists. Would play again!
