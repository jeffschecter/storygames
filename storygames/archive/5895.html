\n Hey people


I played MLWM for the first time yesterday on Skype. My planned game ended sooner than expected, so I rounded up some folks with Play Now, and off we went.

Playing through Skype is almost like playing face-to-face. We shared character sheets and such on a blog. I rolled all the dice, but we could have just as well used an online dice-roller (web-page or IRC).

There were three of us in the end: Filip, Kamil, and myself. The game went surprisingly smoothly, considering Kamil had never read the rules, and I was the only one with the actual book. Oh and did I mention none of us had ever played it before?

I won\u2019t go through the whole story, but we had a blast. Both minions actually had a happy ending, integrating into the town\u2019s society after killing the master. Filip joked about how crazy it was that the first happy end he\u2019s had in months on end was with MLWM.

It was a surprise, but not a problem at all, because A/ having the possibility of a happy ending means the minions actually have something to lose if it goes wrong, and B/ plenty of nasty, nasty things still happened during the game.

MLWM is very well suited to one-shot play. For the record, we had Fear=2, Reason=3, and each minion started out with Love=1 for both of his connections. The game lasted 6 hours, and that includes going through all the rules, creating the setting and characters, figuring out what the hell we were doing, and playing through the whole game.

Since this was our first try, we stuck with the default setting (actually, 19th century Paris, but close enough). But the replay potential seems huge to me. These types of stories can be set in dozens of interesting and disturbing settings. Hospital? Secret police? Family? The only thing I\u2019ll concentrate on next time is making sure the master is disturbing to the players themselves.

So, folks, I heartily recommend this game, and hope to see you on Play Now soon. You can sign up here, and you can put your Skype contact info and game preferences in the Database.


Cheers!

JC
