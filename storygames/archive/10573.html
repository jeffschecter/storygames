    \n So the Indie hipster invasion of Montreal is off the ground. I was fortunate enough to bully my way into an impromptu intro session to Mouse Guard that was really enjoyable. Got to meet Robert, Vincent, Luke and Joshua in the flesh and throw some dice. It will be a nice foundation for my first Burning Wheel game tomorrow, which immediately follows my first 4e game!     \n I slept my sorry homecourt ass in on Sunday and missed the first panel, which was too bad. I caught the second and I'm going to be an asshole here and say that I am kind of angered at the two guys who were actually deep into designing games (deep enough to be having playtests with observers at the con) who bailed on the game design seminar because they were hungry. Grab a fucking sandwich and come back! Lame. I've heard podcasts of these sessions and they are really rigorous. I'm not sure if I'm in agreement on some of the philosophical assumptions behind them and I don't like the "tough" style of discourse, but the end result is effective and if you aren't willing to take advantage of these designers rare presence in town, then you are missing a major opportunity to improve your game. Plus, I wanted to see it live!
    \n
    \nAs it was, we had a pretty interesting discussion about what is kit-bashing and what is designing and actual game. Really interesting and the hour seemed about 20 minutes long. I really loved Luke's metaphor about the currency system in D&D; being this beautiful, elaborate, baroque clockwork.
    \n
    \nDraconis is a bit humble and we've gone through some difficult times and face some struggles still, so we really appreciate you guys making the effort to come up. It seems like you had a good time and you definitely got some people interested in your games. I think we can do more to make visits like yours way more effective, both for you and for the community. But just your coming was like a giant shot of gaming adrenaline in the neck. So thanks to Luke, Robert, Vincent and Joshua from all of us in Montreal.
    \n
    \nIf anyone is interested in some more in-depth and varied discussion about the con, both at the organization and gameplay levels, we are getting quite into it over at the general Roleplaying sub-forum on Midnight's Lair:
