\nLast week I wrote on my blog about roleplaying in the world of Rogue Trooper. The big problem I faced wasn't the setting's usability as a roleplaying environment, because war stories are great for providing drama, but with finding a system that adequately reflected the themes and general tone. Today it occurred to me \u2014 maybe because I'm slow \u2014 that perhaps 3:16 might be the resolution to this conundrum.

But is it? I point out in my blog entry that I was worried about making the game just an exercise in "shoot 'em all and let Gawd sort 'em out," so I'm not sure if the sheer level of carnage 3:16 depicts is appropriate. Do you think it could be tweaked to fit or should I be looking somewhere else for the solution?

    \n Well, as I wrote in the original blog entry, there are a couple of ways to approach this, the first being a squad of GIs. Just tweak the origin story a little bit so that not all the GIs were wiped out in the Quartz Zone Massacre and proceed from there into the hellworld of endless battle that is Nu Earth. But I'm not sure I'd want to just have them wade hip-deep in blood like a bunch of blue Rambos. Certainly there's some meaningful play to be wrung from the situation these guys face.

    The second approach is to see the war from the perspective of the human soldiers. Maybe spec ops, maybe just regular grunts. Use the SF setting to tell stories with application to the real world. But again, finding the right system is important. I like the simplicity of 3:16, but as I admit it might not be the right fit for what I see being done.
