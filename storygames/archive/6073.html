    \n So, here's the problem with a Stuff to Watch forum:

    * I like the monthly threads. Because sometimes there's an article on botany or space travel or legos which is tangential to gaming, but might apply to 1-2 people. The monthly threads are great for that sort of thing. Post, Blam, Move On. I'd not want to see the forums clogged with links to cool things, it'll make this place smell like RPGNet Tangency. Especially when there's an article that people think is cool, and gaming related, but don't really have a good comment/reply to. The Month-Threads are a perfect environment to hold those discussions, I think.

    * And yet, I feel the pain of a 10-post discussion happening in one of those threads. I mean, I myself don't really care too much (20 + posts is my, "C'mon guys, let's take it to a new thread" barrier I think), but I know how it bothers folks.

    So the other day I was contemplating a counter-rule, and perhaps implementing it in a social way (adding it to the rules, setting a precedent, etc), which goes something like this:

    * In the monthly threads, if a link gets three replies, the fourth person who wants to reply (or fifth or so, given cross-posting) is obliged to create a new thread on that discussion.
    ** And to facilitate that, a nice rule of thumb that some people might want to do (to see what happens, mostly, I might revoke it later) is to number their response. Like, if Jason sees that he's the second person to comment on Posted Link A, then he could write "2" or "[2]" or something at the top of his reply. And he's under no obligation to tell Replier #1 to go back and number her post, etc. Just a little internal rule to adopt, to see if it works or not.

    How about something like that?

    -Andy
