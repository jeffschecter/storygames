\n In Society of Dreamers, there will be two bonus games in the back of the book: "The Orc in The Well" and "Five Characters in Search of an Identity". They're fun, quick and interesting, and since there was some extra space left, I decided to throw them in.

I'm sure this isn't the first time someone's done something like this, but I can't think of any examples. Anyone?

    \n "My Uncle the Baron" is a very quick-playing and simple game of ecalation. One player begins by telling his fellows one thing about his noble and accomplished relation: "My uncle the Baron once crossed the ocean on the back of a turtle." Then the other players take turns escalating, in some sense, on that claim ("My aunt the Duchess crosses the ocean on a turtle's back every Friday to bask in the affection of her American subjects.") until either they can't think of something, the thing they think of doesn't really count as an escalation ("Uh, my uncle the Baron... two turtles?"), or they violate the rules of propriety ( "My uncle the Baron crosses the ocean on a saucy Duchess..."). That's it! I've played a few times and it's always enjoyable.
