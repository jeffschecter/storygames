    I'd like to see the development of some key ideas and terminology that has come out of the Forge into a set of introductions so jargon-free that they can given to rpg virgins. Why? To allow them to see what they might get from them. Introductions to each hit a particular sweet spot, a kind of person, a kind of issue.

    I can imagine one inspired by Paul Czege's
    agenda for mind invasion to jazz your politics/cultural studies buddy.

    Or a pithy restatement of Vincent + Meg Baker, and Charles Wotton's comments on how making your own entertainment falls into a category with biking to work, making your own muesli etc to toss to your self-sufficient DIY friend who's already tossed their TV.

    This kind of thing would work great as a threaded conversation between people who want to make something that will work and connect, and not worry overly about whether this describes all games x, or whether y makes it not a game at all (which is why a small easy-going forum is especially ideal): you can start a thread with a bunch of related links, or with a question "How to sell the concept of rpgs to teachers?" and contributors can put together their heads to iterate to a few solid paras, a mission statement, a 1-page essay, or a pithy line.

    One outcome would be providing a resource (cheat sheets) for people like me, who spend some time around friends who may find their hobby interesting, given the right hook. Another is that by refining and simplifying claims and terminology you end up with material that can find an online home beyond the rpg-sphere. I'm thinking audio snippets that can be thrown into a 'New fiction' podcast. That kind of stuff.

    Call it the Cookhouse, PR centre, Place for Making Stuff Simple.

    Alex Fradera
