Vaxalon
March 2006 edited March 2006 in Story Games
How would it change the game, if you rolled fallout when you received it, so that when someone is knocked out of a fight you know it right away?

The way I'm envisioning it, you roll fallout as the dice arrive, and then just keep track of whatever is your highest fallout number as you go along. As soon as you hit the 12 mark, you know you're injured; roll your body against your fallout score to avoid unconsciousness; otherwise just lose a die from an attribute to represent injury. If your fallout score hits 16, or if you fail to see the fallout at 12-15, then you're out of the fight.

It seems to me that it would make combat more deadly, which may or may not be a problem.

    On immediate reflection, I think the "Fallout Now!" option is not a good one, for the very reason you cite.

    Instead, what's needed is careful attention to stakes, and second, a willingness to narrate the end of a conflict creatively.

    Let's look at all the lightsaber duels we see in Eps. 4-6.

    Episode 4:

    Darth Vader vs. Obi-Wan
    What's at stake? Darth Vader starts the conflict. The stakes? "I am the master now." Did he win or lose? I think he lost, when Obi-wan discorporates rather than be struck down. Darth certainly didn't gloat over his "victory"

    Episode 5:

    Darth Vader vs. Luke Skywalker
    What is at stake? Convince Luke to join Vader against the Emperor. Darth loses, but Luke takes significant fallout in the form of a few d4's in "Darth Vader is my Father" relationship.

    Episode 6:

    Emperor vs. Luke Skywalker
    What is at stake? Convince Luke to strike down Vader and take his place. Vader is an "improvised posession" in this conflict, a mere tool of the Emperor. The Emperor loses, and in fact takes major fallout. The fact that the lightsaber was being used against Vader didn't change the fact that at the end, two tens came up on the Emperor's side.
