    \n This is different, but I'm recommending D&D 4th ed. if you like challenge games as opposed to this story creation stuff. I've actually played it more at this point as a 1-on-1 than a rpg, and it works better this way, at least if you're the sort of jerk I am and refuse to drift the game into something resembling a roleplaying game. It has little to complain about as a miniature skirmish game, however, and having the game be 1-on-1 enables an interesting drift wherein the players negotiate for any fictional rules-calls they want to see happen - there's still flexibility in the rules where you want it, but they're solid enough to allow balls-to-the-wall trying to kill each other, too. Resembles Descent more than a little.
