    \n Uh, okay. Well, Heroics was different from the other playsets I've done in that there was a lot of fictional material to go through. For "Knights of Light" I had The Guild and not much else, and for "The Big Anime Con" I had over a decade of attending cons in real life to draw on, but for Heroics there were three movies and a few comics to draw on. I got the idea for Heroics a while back when I saw Super in a theater, and when I watched Defendor during my push to finish it off I knew that "a jar of angry wasps" just had to be an Object. A lot of it was going through the relevant Wikipedia articles (and apparently Kick-Ass has an ongoing comic sequel) and plucking ideas.

    It was also the first playset I did after the Fiasco Companion came out, so I had its advice in mind, which is why the elements in it are by and large shorter than in my other playsets. I don't know when I'll be able to try it out, but I do worry about the balance between the "dumb superheroes" side of things and the "evil motherfucking organized crime guys" side.

    In general I like stories about people who dream about fantastical stuff but live in the real world. Fandom in general has this amazing energy that can be beautiful or awful. Great ambition and poor impulse control.
