\n Hey, Game designers with things you're planning to get out this summer, is the 4E hoopla at GenCon this year making you think about a different release date?

I've been mulling over the thought that it would be wiser for folks to release at Origins for several reasons, no 4E hoopla to stand in the shadow of, a few weeks of people buying and playing to get the buzz really cooking, and more face time and gaming at Origins.

So, what are your thoughts?

Oh, and this in no way is a selfish push to get more folks at Origins, just because I and Mickey happen to be planning on attending (with fingers crossed, hoping that we can afford it).

    \n No and Yes.

    Consideration of "impact of Sales" or somesuch if I bring and sell Tenra Bansho or Another Japanese Game at GenCon due to 4E? Not at all. Either people will bring the money to buy and play my games too, or else they'll be the kind of folks who are interested in something other than 4E.

    Consideration of what will happen after GenCon? I want to aim to sell my games at GenCon, at least have them prepared and ready to roll in August. Because if I don't and D&D 4E comes out, I will buy 4E and probably be playing it so hard that I will slack on finishing up my games; and will have to push deadlines back another year or something.

    So basically, I want to be done before 4E, because when it comes out I imagine I'll be playing the shit out of it. But at the same time, it's not stopping me from rethinking a release date for my own stuff: Before, after or during the Mad 4E Rush.

    -Andy

    \n It did make me rethink our summer schedule. I had assumed WotC would release 4E at GenCon, so I originally planned to release our new A Song of Ice and Fire game in June. I figured hitting in the lull between editions would be good for an alternate fantasy game. Then WotC announced they were launching in May (later changed to June). Since there was no way to get the game out earlier than 4E and still give it enough time for playtesting and polish, I instead opted to make it our featured GenCon release instead. Launching in the face of 4E is not ideal, but I suspect there will be enough people looking for something different to make it work.
