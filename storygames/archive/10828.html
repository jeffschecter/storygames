\n Hi! I have an idea in mind for an RPG that follows one family through multiple generations. I'm imagining a set-up where each scene is a slice-of-life vignette for the family; the next scene takes place five years later, and so on. In this way, one session of play covers 30-60 years. The idea is to show how large-scale historical events affects individual families in a personal way.

Does anyone know of an RPG that does something like that? I've been out of the "indie RPG" loop for a while. I vaguely recall that Hero's Banner has a system wherein each hero follows the legend of the previous one, but that's the only example I can think of, offhand.

Thanks in advance!

    \n So, not much I know that will do the 5-year-leap thing, but you can easily bring that into any existing game you want, heck even D&D ("It's 5 years later, boost yourselves a few levels").

    But in terms of true multi-generational without hard "5 year" or other limits, I think the best games that does it is In A Wicked Age. Every single game I've played of it (that is "a game" being "a few actual games, spread across one or several gaming sessions") has been multi-generational: One game following the next by 1-5 years, or 15-30 years. One game I played had about 6 "games" in it over 4 sessions, and within it we saw at least two characters go from children to grandparents.

    -Andy
