\n (Partly rehashed from the meetup sticky thread and private conversations:)

I was talking off-list to a few people and I'm now bouncing the idea to create an Indie Tour 2008 t-shirt! This started with the three I'm able to attend here in the Pacific Northwest (ConQuest NW Story Lounge in February, Indie GameStorm in March, and Go Play NW in summer), plus a couple more where I hope to introduce indie/story games (Dragonflight, Emerald City Gamefest) if people are interested. My husband also suggested a logo showing the various mascots playing in a band.

Now I'm thinking it might be great to add all the "tour dates" we can confirm for all 2008 hippie game and go-play events. A few people here have expressed an interest. If enough groups want to participate, I can start throwing something together; I'm thinking of drafting a strawman version -- so people who are more talented than me can then feel inspired to show me how it's done by improving it! :-)

    Posted By: scottdunphyWhat is the criteria to qualify?
    \n(1) I'm keeping it to events taking place in 2008, just as an arbitrary but easily remembered cut-off date;

    (2) I didn't include any recurring dates, like our weekly Monday Night Short-Shots, because they wouldn't work well with the "tour dates" approach;

    (3) I've only included events where there was an attempt to create dedicated indie representation. Many of these are piggy-backed onto a wider convention: ConQuest NW Story Games Lounge, Go Play GameStorm, GenCon Games On Demand, Indie Track Ropecon, etc., so it doesn't have to be indie-only.

    Why not get together with other SG/indie GMs at Genghis Con and start the "Indie Horde" or something like that, as a rallying point for anyone interested in SG games? It doesn't necessarily have to be an official track, it could be as informal as the old "Secret Masters of Gaming" at DunDraCon that used to represent by all wearing hawaiian shirts. :-) You could have something as simple as an indie/SG get-together dinner and keep a list of all indie games in the program for interested gamers.\n
