    \n Hans,
    \n
    \nIn this case, game-story structures are a collection of tools that a group of players may use to facilitate telling and thinking about certain kinds of stories within the storyjam framework.
    \n
    \nI'm thinking things like: story-specific recommended narrative methods like historical flashbacks, confessionals, or RVT (requested-vulnerability threshold) Chips; pertinent illustrations and other multimodal media; scene environment and framing cards; useful character options for the story; info cards (nonfiction) that may play into or inform the story (moving into educational possibilities)
    \n
    \nAnd most importantly, Question Themes which the designer recommends around the story, which are (as you know) used to "spotlight" scenes. Since these form the structural skeleton of the story, and since they are expandable at player discretion, these form a really good way to flexibly inform the kinds of topics that the group is jamming about.

    \n
    \n The term storyjam in this context was coined by Willem Larsen.
    \n
    \nI really appreciated some of his thoughts and goals, and started using it to describe the fairly experimental kind of story gaming that we were doing down here in Eugene, OR.
    \n
    \nI don't have any links, since I'm still figuring out how to talk about this. I hope to e-publish an introduction to Storyjamming sometime in the next year.
    \n
    \nFrom what I've seen, this system is most effective at allowing players to collaborate and flexibly tell personally relevant (and vulnerable) stories. It excels at longer, multi-session stories in which characters transform, gain, and lose in powerful ways.
    \n
    \nAs you can see, I don't really know how to talk about or pitch this, yet. I kind of see it as a universal system for character-based story gaming. Probably shines most for games with at least some serious or vulnerable elements. I know that it's my favorite system right now, but am still trying to figure out how to talk about it with the "uninitiated".
