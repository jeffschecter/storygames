    This is very cool, but I'm not sure how these numbers are compiled.

    For example, Story Games shows up as a site with one (1) active member, one (total) members, and one post.
    \nVanilla forums (like Story Games) don't make this kind of information public by default. But most forums do. Generally this is at the bottom of the forums home page.

    Only a few forums show and "Active Member stat" (and these probably use different definitions of what it means to be active) so this should be taken with a grain of salt.

    There are plugins for Vanilla forums that will display this info. But for now the only stat I have for Story Games is the "rounded" number of Threads in each category added up manually.

    \n Yeah, I checked them because I wanted to join in on the flamewa^W friendly and intelligent discussions. Turns out it was all PBP, all the time. I've always wondered if not IRC or a similar medium would be more perfect for RP than PBP.

    Guys, there are also tons and tons of PBP sites that are completely made up of people who don't know the similarity between that and TRPGs. Like there are at least twenty sites where people play wild horses (this was pre the MLP craze), or characters from Glee or other tv shows, or even original characters in weird universes. I wanted to join one of these horse sites and I lurked and read a lot of the threads for hours and hours and then it was dawn and I fell asleep and I forgot it the next day. They also do it on Tumblr.

    I'd say the difference between such a site's posts and a Pathfinder PBP is like 2%. No die rolls. Some have GMs, some don't. Most of the rules are about diegetic limitations like "no mare / mare relationship in the game", or "in this game it's an alternate universe where Rachel and Quinn are brothers" and things like that.
