    \n I want to find something to use smiley and/or emoticon dice for characters' reactions or some such.

    I want to do something with poker dice, but I have no idea what.

    Chessex makes a ton of different dice for generating D&D-style encounters. Weather, Monsters, Treasure, Traps, etc.

    Flying Buffalo makes trap dice, monster dice, and pizza dice.

    Koplow makes dice with just about everything, including mathematical operators, units of measure, geometric shapes, rock-paper-scissors, d30s with the alphabet, "double dice" (tiny die inside of a transparent die) for d6/10/d12/20, six-sided dice with colored pips, body parts dice, wooden dice with each side a different color, etc.

    Steve Jackson makes Proteus.

    Posted By: WirebrainI've collected some strange dice, two of the weirdest were a set of "Rock, Paper, Scissors" on large D12s.
    Nothing strange about those, I can tell you as a former WOD LARPer who played with folks who could totally predict your throws (like, 90% of the time). There's actually a skill to RPS; and those dice would strip the skilled folks' ability to pattern-predict.\n
    Posted By: Neko EwenKoplow makes dice with just about everything
    ...including in foreign scripts/glyphs. Nothing says "l33t" (or I ch33t) like using Chinese dice to roll dice pools in Sick Six System (*ahem* yes, he knows who he is, the flaming Nippophile!).
    ----
    I actually have and use a Fumble die, which shows icons for things like slipping and falling, broken weapon, stuck weapon, dazed, etc. It's neat-o. Could be used as an encounter randomizer in the upcoming "Falling and Drowning" expansion: "Falling, Drowning, Slipping, and Generally Screwed."
    ----
    The venerable d30 is also under-represented. And as a single-source die, it's sort of rockin'; it generates these ranges, with simple division: 1-2, 1-3, 1-5, 1-6, 1-10, 1-15, 1-30. There's GOTTA be a game waiting for that range of die results. (My own Three Stooges RPG, circa 1986, used it in simple "roll over" test, like a d20 against a DC or an opposed roll+stat--VERY volatile, which sort of fit the 3S theme.)
    ----
    Anyone ever thought of a good use for the "hyper dice": a d6 inside another, clear d6? I recall one of the threads on one of these sites talking around that notion some....
