\n We just posted this on the Playground Magazine blog:

----

Dear Playground readers,

This last year has been interesting, fun, challenging and troublesome. We\u2019ve got three issues out, and the fourth one is being done as I\u2019m writing this.

And it will be our final issue.

Anyone who\u2019s worked with a magazine will know what challenges it poses to keep things running, especially financially; and if you\u2019ve been involved in any project based on voluntary work, you know how hard it is to keep some of the key spots filled in. Sadly, we haven\u2019t been able to reach a critical mass of subscribers, and if we continue longer than this, we will, in essence, have to pay for the magazine out of our own pockets. (Which is, to some extent, happening already).

It\u2019s still possible, for a while, to buy back issues; you can even buy all four for the price of a one-year subscription. We\u2019ve taken down the subscription page, so people won\u2019t be misled \u2013 but just contact us at subscriptions@playgroundmagazine.net for more information.

We have tried to be fair in all our dealings, and will continue to try. If, after receiving #4, you feel you haven\u2019t received your money\u2019s worth, please let us know at contact@playgroundmagazine.net and we\u2019ll sort things out.

As I said, it\u2019s been an interesting year. I, personally, believe this magazine, or something like it, should exist. However, we \u2013 the current team of editors and designers \u2013 won\u2019t be the ones to make it happen. If you think you are, I hope you do make it happen! Let us know if there\u2019s anything you want to know from us, at contact@playgroundmagazine.net.

Yours,

Matthijs Holter
Soon-to-be-ex-Chief Editor of Playground Magazine

    \n Many in our era have dreamed up and implemented 'zines, only to find them nearly impossible to sustain.

    Take Alan Moore's excellent Dodgem Logic, which had extremely high quality curatorial, artistic and political import for a 'zine about Northampton, UK and well, other stuff.

    But even with an international audience, the 'zine wound up being financed by whatever extra money Moore had on hand, and after Issue 8 he had to throw in the towel after never breaking even once. But maybe that was also because he was paying his writers fair wages.

    It remains to be seen if enough underground magazines can develop a synergy strategy to keep themselves going (i.e. underground comics! edgy RPGs! deep thought about computer games! non-inane discussions of Doctor Who! all in one issue) without being co-opted by one force or another.

    I'm glad that such a magazine existed, and we'll try to spur revivals whenever we can.

    \n I still believe there's a market for a magazine like this. What we did was basically the old "I wanna make a mag! Who's in? Let's go!". I tried getting people on board who could do marketing, but that never worked out - people dropped out, stopped working on things, etc. Without proper marketing, you never get your stuff out there.

    At some point (definitely at Solmukohta 2012) I'll put together my thoughts on what happened, what went right, what went wrong, and what is needed to make something like this work in the future.

    \n If you had a U.S.-side partnership to keep down costs in the U.S., I think you'd have done much better (meaning: I would have bought it, and I imagine people like me would've).

    I don't know how it is in the rest of the world, but people in the U.S. very rarely buy things internationally. I have never done it. Most people have never done it. I imagine this is different in many other parts of the world, but I may be wrong.

    Seriously, I think it's just: people in the U.S. don't buy things if they're shipping from overseas. I look back & wish I had bought Playground, but I also look back and remember why: shipping from overseas? That's expensive & weird & conversion rates & what? I know: super lazy. But hey, this is the U.S., and we're very accustomed to our convenience.
