    \n If I were looking for "general freeform best practices", I'd start with Play With Intent. I've seen The Upgrade mentioned as a newcomer-friendly trove of useful techniques -- it's not generic, but one could jump off from it pretty easily, I think.

    As for "means to generate settings, characters, relationships, plots, conflicts etc as well as conflict resolve mechanics", I have never seen any such thing in an explicitly freeform game. If you find one, I'd love to know! Fiasco might fit, but you already mentioned it, so I assume it's not what you're looking for. How do its set-up procedures differ from what you want?
