\n I bought some garnet crystals today at a stone collectors' fair. They look pretty much like this:



They're irregular. You can roll them, and they land on one side - although sometimes they make weird little jumps or roll back, probably because of their weird weight distribution. They're unmarked.

Here's a challenge: How could these dice be used in a game - without marking them in any way, reducing them to mere tokens etc? In other words, what function can you find/design for garnet dice so that no other object could fulfil the same function?

    \n Targetted but somewhat random location generators. Roll them across the table and, wherever they land, that's the result. Good way to generate star maps, for instance. Then again, you could do this with normal dice too, they'd just be easier to aim.

    Test of skill: roll a garnet along the table toward the target. Proximity to the center = degree of success.

    Test of luck: place garnet into cup full of regular dice. Throw all dice toward the target. Number of garnets within circle = degree of success.

    \n Players stack them, with a timer to race against. Difficult challenges require a higher stack of garnets.

    You have a few slightly different types of garnets, and some are easier to stack - less lopsided, more polished, whatever. Characters have proficiencies in certain skills. I might have Swordsmanship 3, which allows me to use 3 red garnets in place of the wonkier purple garnets when attempting swordsmanship contests. I might have deficits too - Crippled 1 forces me to use a VERY lopsided or rounded blue garnet in place of one of the purple ones.
