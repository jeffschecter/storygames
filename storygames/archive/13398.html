    Posted By: RyWhat's the big difference between Barbarians and Sellswords?

    Also, what about priestesses, harlots, and nobles?

    (/love your work, btw. I'd love to be able to play some kind of AW-IAWA hybrid)
    \nGood questions.

    Sellswords lead a band of dudes. They're choppers without the bike (and they get a bigger gang instead).

    Barbarians don't lead a band of dudes. They're gunluggers without the guns (and they get better stats instead, and a different selection of moves).

    Priestesses, harlots, and nobles? I had a harlot playbook all written up, but it was basically the skinner but less interesting. I couldn't figure out a way to make it balance with the others. In the end I rolled it into the slave.

    A "Priestess" playbook based on the hocus would be cool. The difficulty I had was that Howard is really ambiguous about religion in his stories - Gods are possibly all imaginary, or possibly not. It would be quite tricky to write a playbook that captured that, I think. It could be quite cool though.

    Nobles seem like a less interesting version of the tyrant. "Noble", I figure, is just a thing that may or may not be true of your character, regardless of what playbook you've chosen.\n
    Posted By: Paul T.This is very, very cool! Nicely done.

    \n It's worth making it clear that this hack is about Conan specifically, rather than Sword and Sorcery generally. It'd be pretty different if I were to make a Fafhrd and Grey Mouser hack. An Elric hack would be sweet though (and I believe a friend of mine is already noodling with one where you get to roll +doom, and rolling 12+ is scary as hell).

    Ryan, I guess the litmus test for me is "How excited would you be to play this character?" So yeah, there are a few stout Bossonians and battle-hardened Aquilonians in the stories, but they mostly act as a foil for Conan; they exist to make Conan look cooler. I think that's a good role for NPCs.
