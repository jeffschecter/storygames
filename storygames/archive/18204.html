\n Hey!

I want to show you guys at this comedy video I made to promote my first game!

The video is actually just a lure to ask for some feedback on the game - I had some great feedback on here a couple of months ago, and I was hoping to get more people's 2 cents/pence.

Screenplay is a free game in which you write a movie by mixing two genres together. How about Costume Drama with Superheroes? or a Zombie Movie with Supernatural Horror elements?
It's GMless, it takes about 2 and a half hours to play, and it's completely zero-prep.
It plays a little like Fiasco, though it was originally inspired by Dogs In the Vineyard and Cuthulhu Dark - I don't think you'd really recognize those influences anymore, though.

So, if I may, your thoughts:

Does the game appeal to you?
Does it strike you as something different?
Are the rules easy to understand?

If anybody plays it, I'd frickin' love to hear about your game!

Hope this is something interesting for you!

    \n I really like the videos - they helped make the game come alive for me.

    A couple of thoughts on the cards. At first I was going to recommend making them larger to include more space for notes but then I thought a better idea might be to make them Avery label format, and reformatting them landscape so you can stick them at the top of an index card (if you don't have labels, print them on paper and get out the Pritt Stick - you could call this the Blue Peter round...).

    Speaking as a boardgame veteran used to such terminology, for me it would be more clear if you said the game had three phases - character, story and epilogue - each of which has one or more rounds. The character "round" actually includes 3 rounds (ie complete sweeps around the table) - introduction, flashback and goal - and I struggled to make sense of the rules as written (this might be my own baggage, but I think games have developed more detailed breakdowns like that for good reasons).

    Am I right in thinking that the cards you deal out at the start are the only cards used in the game? And that at the start of each story round, all the cards are shuffled and put into a pile? It isn't clear to me.

    Turn order: it's always fun to have a silly rule at the start to say who should go first. I guess you've already considered how always going clockwise will tend to create one type of story as opposed to mixing it up each time. Either way, I think you should make turn order more explicit in the text.

    Finally, I think you could do worse than to include a short list of things you can/are encouraged to do, eg. DO incorporate other player's characters, their flashbacks, etc. Otherwise, I think there's a risk that n00b players will start telling parallel stories and come unstuck.

You lay out the cards while explaining why there are seven of them. Then cut to the presentation phase, go through each round with one player, pointing out the rules and that other players are taking their turn during the round before you move on. And so on. In the story phase, introduce more and more examples as you go, like that any other player may feel free to involve other characters in the story. Don't explain everything during the first round in the story phase.

    This would give me a clearer view of how to play your game.

    \n Thanks Rickard!
    I've been living in Liverpool for university, but I'm actually from Yorkshire. My accent is closer to a southern English accent than northern, though, for no apparent reason .

    The bulk of the game takes place in the Story Phase. In each player's turn, they begin the scene, then two other players each add a section of narration to end it. The epilogue and character rounds are relatively quick - but the story rounds take a longer.
    The game does run exceptionally quick though - it often runs at just over 2 hours, though it feels like a pretty full and intense roleplaying session.

    I like your idea for the video a lot - I've come to the conclusion that the new video will need to show the game being played more - and you're plan is a lovely way of doing that. I'll be filming the new video tomorrow!
