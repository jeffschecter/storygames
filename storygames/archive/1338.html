    Hey Jason,

    I'm unwrapping my snarky-bat. Because I love.

    Aren't all keys "player keys"? I mean, they're all rewards to the player for particular behaviors on the part of the player. If the character does something, it's only because the player narrated them doing that something.

    I think what you've got here are meta-keys. They stand (for the most part) outside the narrative actions of your character.

    Are they even keys? TSOY's keys are rewards I set for myself. If I choose the Key of the Mission, then I get my reward when I guide the narration around my character towards that mission. Your keys are rewards that one player sets for another, not for themselves. It's a trivial and silly point, and doesn't mean that what you're creating isn't goodness, but worth noting.

    So, what I'm wondering is, what are you trying to get at here? Some form of Gamer's Preschool? How about the Key of Sharing? Gain 1 XP when you give me a piece of your gum. Gain 2 XP when you bring enough for the whole class.

    Seriously though, you did make me think of an "I'm gonna make you awesome"-style Key;

    Key of the lackey

    Your character owes allegience to another PC. Gain 1 XP when they are in the spotlight, looking awesome. Gain 3 XP when you use your spotlight time to focus on them.

    For my part: I would be very, very uncomfortable about participating in a game where participants were encouraged to assign each other personal growth goals, and then make judgement calls on how the various participants are behaving.

    Specifically, it would not feel like I was spending an evening with mature friends interacting with one another as grown-ups; it would feel like I was in primary school again, with a set list of positive behaviours that I get gold star for from Teacher/the GM.     As for the Key of Kindness - if a player isn't treating the other people in the group with respect, there's no point pussyfooting around with semi-in-game measures to solve what is essentially an out-of-game problem with rudeness and insensitivity. Someone who doesn't respect you and is unkind to you is, by definition, Not Your Friend. Don't game with them.
