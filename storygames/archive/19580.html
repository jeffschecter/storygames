\n All magic spells are limited in time.

To perform magic, you just need to know the right spell, and say the words at the right time.

The right words are always on the same format. All known spells follow it.

First, you name who established the spell. For some spells, everything about them but their name is lost in the shrouds of history. As long as people cast the spell, however, their name will be remembered.

Second, you name the price they paid to establish the spell. This is often one or more persons.

Third, you say what the spell does.

If you perform those steps at the right time, it always work. You don't have to pay the price; that was for creating the spell and leaving it open for anyone to cast.



The right time is often very limiting. Many spells only work on a particular date each year. The more useful ones depend on the phases of the moon, e.g. can be used each full moon. I've heard of one that could be used any wednesday. It's probably not that strong, though.

    \n I love them!

    I imagine magicians as keeping these enormous lists of spells, great spellbooks, along with reference charts for julian-gregorian calendar conversion, the chinese calendar, phases of the moon, old farmer's timekeeping schemes, navigation charts etc to know which spells they had available a given day. Like, once you get into the community, it's not about finding spells - it's about building up an array of useful spells, that you can use at useful times.



    King Rakai Garung established a contract for which he paid five prize bulls. I invoke it to weave the clouds and rain around me into any shape I wish.
    (This spell can only be used on the day of Kliwon; one of the days in the five-day Javanese calendar.)

    The poor man Zhu Wudi of Anhuin established a contract for which he paid the lives of his three eldest children. I invoke it now to cause this river to flood unnaturally for several years.
    (This spell can only be used during the Chinese New Year's celebration after the year of the Horse.)

    Bodhmall established a contract for which she paid the largest tree of the forest. I invoke it to turn into a fox until the sun next rises.
    (This spell can be used on the seventh day of each month, following the Gaulish Caligny calendar.)
