    \n Someone started a game for one of the Ronnies contests at The Forge years back called Krasnoarmeets that attempted to combine gritty war stories about Red Army soldiers on a deadly yet dull front with more dirty-hippy storygame elements. It might be worth looking into that.

    I noticed it because the author was another minis fan, and was planning to use the game with minis, despite it not having the usually expected minis use rules.

    It was built to be very trench-horror-ish, with folks in a unit gelling as a team, characters dealing with their comrades slowly being knocked off, and what impact that has on everyone in the group. IIRC, the author also suggested that likely, most or even all characters were doomed to die eventually in the war, as the setting eentually leads into a huge offensive in which the units the game were based on were essentially fed into a meat grinder series of assaults against the retreating Wertmacht and its allies.

    Yup, Gritty McGrimness right there. I personally thought it sounded terribly interesting.

    \n I agree that for lightweight tactical combat that scales up nicely, Savage Worlds is excellent. The core system is well tuned for WWII level tech, and I'm sure the Weird War bood fleshes that out even better.

    The system is easy to hack at, if you want to bring in aspects from other games, in case you find some other mechanics that help reinforce the Band of Brothers feel.

    Personally, I don't like the "buy skills that cost less when they're levelled less than the related stat" stuff. So I hacked out the skill buying and replaced it with a Stat + Job chart (for which I got the idea from Leverage's Stat+Job rolls). You roll the Stat and Job dice and take the highest (which replaces the wild die roll).

    So far the system is working well for non-combat challenges. We'll have a combat next session, and I expect it to go well too (I used to enjoy Great Rail Wars back in the day, which Savage Worlds is based on).
