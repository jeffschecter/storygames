    I like Pace for diceless play with younger folks. I haven't yet had time to actually try this, so I'm really just blowing smoke, but it has a few elements that recommend it:

    1. It's got a simple numbers-comparison to start with: you get two descriptor words and seven points to allocate between. For a beginning player you can just use a 4/3 split.
    2. It's got resource pips to increase result values for conflicts whether or not your descriptor words apply, making it possible to add the currency management stuff later.
    3. You earn these points by taking falls and losing conflicts, which can be introduced as appropriate depending on the mentality of your players.
    4. Once you add in the resource currency manipulation, you can move on to more elaborate mechanics like pooling into a team effort and deficit spending, blots, etc.

    So it has the nice layer-cake approach, so you can introduce rules live into a running game as the players become comfortable with the ones already explained. So when a player runs out of pips you can remind them that they can choose to lose to earn more, or go into debt.

    I imagine that the mechanics have a lot of great props you can use, too. Since players have two descriptive words, you can write them on bracelets and thread tokens on a necklace, etc etc.

    Lots of this is dependent on the whims and phases that many kids go through. I think every six year old I ever knew manifested an inherent sense of total munchkinism throughout everyday life, insisting on being the biggest/best/most at everything and anything.
