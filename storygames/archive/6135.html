\n I enjoyed running this little PTA session so much that I had to share.

So I'd decided to run Prime Time Adventures at Conpulsion and do my standard zero prep. Hooray for Indie games that need to organisation!

I get three players, Debbie, Adam and Graham. It's the morning slot so people are a bit zonked and I have the tail end of a sore throat. Not ideal situations.

So we start pitching ideas back and forth. Zooming in from a general feel "dark comedy" via a couple of diversions into pseudo reality-tv, and end up with the following:

It's a show about a German exchange student who comes to England and stays with a family of cultists. It's a dark comedy drama.

This takes us about fifteen minutes, followed by a further half hour fleshing out our characters: Gunter, our naive exchange student who just wants to be accepted. Terror (or Trevor) Black, the family's son, who just wants to be normal. Christiana Black, the matriarch and leader of the cult, owner of a foul temper. Plus attendant family members, little sister, Vengeance with her knives and the downtrodden Brummie father. Plus hangers on in the way of the school exchange counsellor with her "wheel of feeling" and a social worker nemesis.

It worked so well. It was all in the wonderful contrasts between the everyday and the weird, I just love this kind of juxtaposition stuff. Lots of moments of ha, ha, ha! Ouch...

A selection of scenes:

Gunter in discussion with the counsellor. "I have problems with some words, but it's all okay, Ja? They are going to have a December-ment?" Counsellor: "Well, yes, it is coming up for Christmas."

Terror and Gunter playing guitar hero, with Terror slowly turning up the volume to drown out something horrific going on in the basement. Only to have Christiana come up and bawl the kids out because of the noise.

The office of Sinsiter Uncle Aphonse, a lawyer, forcing the family to "owe him Gunter" in exchange for making some trouble with the police and social services go away.

    \n This was my first game of PTA despite having owned the game for over a year - mainly because I've always felt that the game would need more time than is available at a con. How completely wrong I was. We managed to do series creation and two episodes in three hours - which is pretty amazing.
    \n
    \nI very much enjoyed the game, but never really clicked with Terror. I'm not sure if that was because of his low screen presence or if he just a slightly dull character. It's a pity that we didn't manage to find time to play his spotlight episode too - that would have been interesting. If we were doing the set-up again, I would have be tempted to take the little sister Vengeance as my protagonist.
    \n
    \nBut yeah, great game, thanks for running it.

    Posted By: Alvin FrewerWow that is amazing! The pitch session only being 15 minutes probably helped. They've always lasted about an hour for me. Was there anything in particular you guys did to help speed up the pace?
    \nThat everybody was aware it was a con game and pushed hard for that fifteen minutes helped. I've also done enough of this kind of prep (it's similar to Covenant's in some ways) to spot the tells of "that's cool" or "that's shit" that people give off.

    Each player got two scene requests per episode, and the scenes zoomed in on the conflicts pretty nicely. Again being a con game helped, as there's the expectation of pace. We reached the end of budget quite naturally at the right points, as it happened.
