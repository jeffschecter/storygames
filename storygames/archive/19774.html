\n Hey friends,

The Golden Cobra is almost ready to slither into view!

The Golden Cobra Challenge is a contest for writing freeform games. Whitney Beltr\xe1n, Emily Care Boss, Evan Torner and I are the judges. We'll announce the contest constraints on 1 October (Next Wednesday!) and the winners at Metatopia on 7 November.

There's an FAQ as well, but I'm happy to answer questions and hope you'll consider participating.

    \n This looks like a very nice contest! I like the website and the aesthetics and such. Jeep's very much not my thing as a designer (although I have done it when I've had to), but I hope you'll get a lot of contestants.

    The definition of the term "freeform" intrigues me here. Any thoughts on how you came about that? Is that how the word is used in your neck of the woods?

    Around here the word "freeform" has meant a game, particularly tabletop, that has minimal explicit mechanical rules (and is very likely to substitute those with an auteur GM). This must have been the case since like early '90s at least; I remember it having been well established in Finnish rpg discourse by the mid-decade, and by '00s it showed up as a routine descriptor in con session adverts and the like. The common name of a specific cultural tradition of roleplaying starting in that era, "Nordic freeform", exemplifies this sense of the term.

    Admittedly there's no real good word for "midway between tabletop and larp" except Jeepform, and that's a bit specific to the Swedish tradition by that name, and less of a technical description. I've sometimes called it "hybrid medium" in discourse myself, but as far as I know there's no really widely used term for this around. I guess "freeform" is as good as any, even if it confuses some - I genuinely thought that you were planning a non-mechanical rpg design event here, rather than a larp-hybridizing one, for example.

    \n Thanks, Eero!

    Freeform is a bit of a semantic rat nest. As the FAQ states, we're pretty ecumenical and welcome whatever you define as freeform. It's a problem-based competition (as you'll see when we reveal the constraints), so our goal is to solve the problem, and we're hoping that multiple traditions will weigh in. Jeepform counts, Nordic semi-live, any of the national flavors (UK, Australian, American), even online freeform. The constraints will preclude some of these, most likely. My hope is that every year we can focus on a different "problem", so if this year is not a good fit for your brand of freeform, perhaps next year's will be.

    \n The contest constraints have been announced. In order to be considered for a Golden Cobra, your game must:

    Be playable from start to finish in two hours or less, by people who are not you.

    Be playable by a variable but small number of participants, ideally a wide range like 2-8.

    Be playable in a public space, like an open lounge in a busy hallway.

    Here are the optional ingredients if you crave additional creative constraints:

    Chord, Light, Solution, Bear and Minute

    Good luck! Have fun! You have until 30 October to submit your game.
