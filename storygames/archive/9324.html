\n In another thread, I joked that some things Pelgrane was saying sounded like D&D haiku. They didn't follow haiku rules though. He whispered me a clever little D&D haiku that was much better. I decided to start this new thread to prolong the silliness.

You know the basic rules (5, 7, 5). It's also nice if you can get in a seasonal or time reference. Better, have a word, mark, or place that cuts the poem in two and juxtaposes the two parts of the poem in some way that is interesting to compare.

Share your gaming haiku. Try to make it obvious (or irrelevant) what game the poem describes.


My last torch dies out
Seven trolls growl in the night--
Total party kill

    \n Interesting side-note, it's a common misconception that haikus must have 5/7/5 syllables. This is rather an error in translation; the 5/7/5 actually refers to something that only applies in Japanese and languages like it, not syllables as they are understood in Western languages. However I gotta say, I much prefer a Haiku to follow the 5/7/5 syllable rule. You can't translate the actual rules into English, so I prefer a close fit to no rules at all.

    Anyway, useless side-notes over, let's see...

    The Hallway is Green
    My Uniform is Orange
    Send a Fresh Clone Please

    -Ash

    Posted By: Mel WhiteSweet Rain drains away
    Soaking into dry brown dust
    Snow is still and pure

    Recordedduring a "Blossoms Are Falling" game at Dexcon.
    \nWhat made me happy about this haiku was that both Sweet Rain and Snow were the names of characters; as the evil vizier-type, I wanted the son of the lord not to marry the innocent but common Sweet Rain because of my belief that it would somehow defame Lady Snow, the lord's wife. Since I had command of the lord's elite guard, the haiku was simultaneously unbridled snobbery and veiled threat.
