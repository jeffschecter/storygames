\n I'm putting together some quick play scenarios to use to cons and such for Revolucion, a game of revolution, revolt, and generally sticking it to the man. I meant to run it at GPNW, but due to the amount of other awesome, the time I had, and the fact that I didn't have something planned I didn't get to it. I could use more ideas, so tell me:

You're a revolutionary, standing for your rhetoric, sticking it to the regime, with the people behind you. Who are you overthrowing?

Fictional, historic, just crazy out there, suggest anything.

    \n My target is the Internet - I mean it, and not out of some misplaced Luddism.

    No, my revolutionary extropian technologist believes that the form of the internet is inherently flawed, not merely architecturally but also in the way that it inspires dysfunctional social interaction, isolation, and its very fake form of human interconnectedness. My revolutionary understands that destroying the internet could have catastrophic consequences for the world, but only when it is removed could a better technosocial system (of my own devising) take it's place.

    Can you imagine a future global network based on cooperation instead of hate? Based on real creative individuals rather than protocorporate microfame chasers? True marketplaces instead of spamhives? Led by virtuous social engineers instead of anonymous griefers and black-hats and spyware and censors? My revolutionary can imagine it, and will do whatever it takes to create the future.
