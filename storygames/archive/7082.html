    \n Sure thing!

    Misspent Youth's tagline is: "Teenage rebellion in a fucked-up future." That gives you a pretty good idea of what the game's like, but I'll expound because I'm prone to logorrhea.

    Misspent Youth is a science fiction game about friendship and rebellion. You play 12-to-17-year-old kids whose world is being fucked up by a bad guy. As players, you make the bad guy first, then the world, then your characters. The game has a craps-like resolution mechanic and one of the ways the game is about friendship is that all conflicts are group conflicts. The game also has a firm scene structure which produces stories that follow the three-act dramatic structure.

    Flavor-wise, think Oceans 11 meets Snow Crash meets Avatar: The Last Airbender. Kinda. The other (voluminous) mood/vibe references can be found at the game's site link, above.

      \n It's the scene pacing I was referring to. Part of the issue with the scenes not lacking an endpoint or going on too long is that you've got to keep scenes light on characters-- if there's no reason for a character to be there, that character shouldn't be in the scene. When a ton of characters are mulling about without the ability to declare new personality traits, it kind of drags down the pace and makes scene ending feel sluggish and awkward. When people don't feel like they've done anything, it doesn't feel like the scene should end.

      There's a big chapter called "Inside the Scene" which covers dos and dont's for when it's your scene, when it's NOT your scene, and elegant/easy ideas to declare multiple relationships later in the game, when you're always crossing a ton of people every turn.

Sometimes I feel like roleplaying, but only have a certain amount of energy-- and I want to invest that energy into character stuff, not fidgety negotiation tactics.

      \n Re: Acts of Evil

      I have been informed privately that it's important to announce when you're not releasing a game, and that not making announcements when you won't be releasing a game is not sufficient for communicating a game release won't happen. So yes, awesome playtesting and feedback over the past year has slashed away at the game's design issues. And I think I may have solved my last thorny design issue in a phone conversation with Ron a week ago. (Or rather, he may have solved it for me. But dammit I was an important part of that fucking conversation, as it was me who packaged the issue for solving.)

      So yeah, my head is definitely on fire. But I've become convinced the game needs a different voice, so I'm doing a complete rewrite of it from the ground up. (A lot of guys ignore the voice, and that's about standards.) And Ron's suggested fix to the last design issue needs full testing.

      So, to be clear, the release date for Acts of Evil is: when all has been made ready.

      Paul
