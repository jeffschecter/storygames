    OK, I'll bite:

    FROM THE 1976 GAME CHEF COMPETITION...

    Bunnies and Burrows
    Required ingredients:
    Includes non-human protagonists, humans are the enemy, or both.
    Based on a media property.

    Optional ingredients:
    Rules for social interaction - storytelling, persuasion, gambling, and sex.
    Rules for carrying, eating, and bartering heads of lettuce.

    Bunnies and Burrows hits both required and optional ingredients, so it earns high scores for compliance. Although the system is a little crazy (it was obviously not thoroughly playtested), it is in many ways state of the art, far surpassing Boot Hill in innovative features. For example, it introduces the concept of a combat results matrix with individual moves by the paired fighters! Also - it is level-based, but characters have levels in each of their attributes. There are detailed rules for smelling things. 8/10.
