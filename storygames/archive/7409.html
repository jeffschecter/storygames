\n I just can't stop loving this game, but as cool as the sword and sorcery setting of it is, I'd love to try something else that fits.

I ran In a Wicked Age during Macklon, and a lot of fun was had. I used one of the supers oracles, and well, they were just flat. Not enough characters, not enough situation, too bland, and not many potential conflicts. It was like pulling teeth for the five of us to just come up with enough initial characters to get the game going. I had to create more NPCs during the game than I usually do, and since the new ones weren't tied into the inital batch of characters, no one cared as much about them. I could've redrawn the cards, but I wanted to push the system (and the oracle) to see if it would break. It almost did.

Luckily, I had a great group of players who managed to turn lemons into super-powered lemonade. But that won't always be the case, and when I play IAWA as written, the oracles just leap off of the page.

So, my question is: What oracles on Abulafia are good, and what ones suck that you've had experience with?

I'm eyeing the Battlestar one with great excitement as we speak...

    \n Might not be quite what you want, but at a recent con, I used the default Oracles... but set in the modern day. Some of the supernatural elements had to be either filed off or kept "unseen/unconfirmed" (i.e. superstition more than supernatural), but I worked fine to generate a setting, initial scene(s), and plenty of characters. You might also have to tweak terms of rank (king -> CEO).

    Most of the themed Oracles that I've checked out (admittedly, not many) seem to be "over-colored," so to speak: impossible to drift, too-tightly coupled to the source material, already "done" out of the box, or leaving only "minor" characters for PCs in the shadow the some Uber character that's treated like a force of nature or a set piece.

    \n The best way to do something with the Conan Oracle, (or any Oracle), is to let the Owe List determine which character is 'Conan'. The guy who keeps showing up is the main character -- thats what IaWA is designed to model. The character who keeps coming back because he faces tough challenges, the character who keeps being brought back because the players think he's interesting, he is the story's 'Conan'.

    Of course, if you assume that the title character of the fiction that inspired the Oracle is out there in the world somewhere, that's OK too: Conan is somewhere in Hyboria, Batman is somewhere in Gotham City -- but this story is about other people who live in that world too. If it turns out that a Stygian sorcerer with a death-wish becomes the main character, or if the other incorruptible cop with the Gotham PD (besides Jim Gordon) becomes the main character, how does the existence of Conan or Batman (or Gordon, for that matter) overshadow their drama?

    This is an issue with any RPG set in a pre-existing fictional universe. Does the existence of Elminster and Drizzt overshadow PCs in the Forgotten Realms setting? Usually not, IMHO. The established fiction is backdrop, and so are the established fictional characters.
