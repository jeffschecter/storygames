\n It's been mentioned somewhere (probably many places) that the reason so many RPGs have detailed combat mechanics is that's where your character can die so the most rules are needed. This of course assumes that type of game, but given that type it's a pretty good explanation. Saving throws were thrown in as a second chance to survive when you were already supposed to be dead, but it rather sucks having them tied to character level.

So instead, have a single universal stat that has a saving throw attached to it: Karma (or whatever, but it's a suitable choice). Depending on how you roleplay your character, it goes up and down. Increases would come from stuff like giving enemies a proper burial, or at least not looting them (unless they've got something that's clearly not theirs, such as a legendary sword), being generally selfless, rushing in to save innocent people (making you more at risk of dying), giving up treasure that's acquired instead of hoarding it, etc. Doing the opposite would cause it to drop. Having an interesting character makes it go up too, and having a boring or dull character would have it go down. Any time your character would die (either by being hacked to death, falling down a chasm, or being poisened, or anything else) you can roll against your Karma (roll under percentile would work nicely), and if you make it you get plot immunity from dying. Your liver fights off the poison, the felling blow just knocked you out rather than hitting a vital and killing you, and you managed to grab on to something while falling.

I figure it could be attached to most systems, like for instance ORE could have it where you get dice in Karma, and if you get a set, you survive.
