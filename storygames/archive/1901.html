      I'm under 50% done (approximately) on a game I'm calling Set Apart. It uses the Sorcerer game engine.
      Instead of a sorcerer, you are creating a Holy Man/Woman (Prophet, Herald, Messiah, Zealot, Oracle/Soothsayer, Savior, Metatron, etc.) and a founder of a nascent religion. You are creating a hero, an exceptional individual who has to make tough, sometimes painful choices for yourself, your followers, and ultimately, the rest of the world. You are roleplaying Jesus Christ, Abraham, Mohammed, Moses, Mahatma Gandhiâ€¦or, quite possibly, Charles Manson, Jim Jones, David Koresh, Marshal Applewhite, or Osama bin Laden.

      You create your God (or Pantheon), your character, determine your scores, derive your Followers score, and set on about the life-long goal of establishing your religion. In the modern world. You deal with your Followers, skeptics, militant non-believers, the demands of your God(s), and your loved ones and friends. Because it's based on Sorcerer, there will be actual demons involved, as well as angels. (Depending on the Deity you serve, it may be hard to tell the difference!) You have the option to make Reliquaries, sacred objects, sacred lands, and or sacred holidays. (Yes, there are rough mechanics for making official holidays!)

      The main focus is on your character. The character is the center of the cult, and his or her actions directly determine the success or failure of the faith. (Although there are rules for whipping up a new character from the same faith if the original character eats it during play.) There are miracles, and the price your character will pay for requesting them. There are rules for characters who worship really out there deities. All of these rules are, of course, derived from the Sorcerer books.

      It will need playtesting, more than anything. If I push ahead to get this into print, it will need layout and art. It will also need Ron's approval, before I go any further. If not, it'll stay a little homebrew variant.

      Beast Hunters is currently being laid out. Game and art are all done. I guess that puts it in the 95% or so range. That's the game about tribal warriors who hunt down powerful monsters and use their preys' blood to inscribe magical tattoos into their skin. The rules are focused on letting you do that while using as much of your creativity as possible to address the challenges in your character's path.

      Sagas is my other mention-worthy project. It's a dramatic story game that incorporates scene framing, conflict webs, story flags, etc., into the mechanics. The conflict resolution system is focused on allowing the players to prioritize on either winning the stakes or impacting the other player's character, including changing their traits. Then there are rewards for playing out changed traits, hitting story flags, and so on. There are generic traits, and then you pick for each scenario or campaign which specializations you want to have available (e.g., violence, sex, intrigue, warfare, magic), which introduces specialization traits, specific rewards, different impacts from conflict resolution, etc. It's like large-scale flagging and story-specific resolution rolled together, I guess. I got most of the rules in various notebooks, but need to type them in so I can start testing it. I guess that means I'm at 10-15% or so, given that a lot might change throughout playtesting. Plus I plan to write lots and lots of example scenarios of increasing complexity for players to try out as they learn more of the rules.
