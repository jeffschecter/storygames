    \n The "Day Pass" and the "One Day Badge" are confusingly two separate things.

    The Day Pass is only $5 and is primarily meant to cover the Exhibit Hall, Art Show, Auction, and Open Gaming.

    The One Day Badge is $25 or $35 (it may depend on the specific day) and lets you into all events.

    \n Yea, I don't think anyone got a GM badge for running GoD, but peeps paid to play. Not sure how that works. Maybe a first year thing?

    Chris! Your washed out blackjack dealer was awesome in our Fiasco game!

    I think Games on Demand was really great for it's first Origins year! We had anywhere between 4 and 6 tables running games in all 4 slots, and people would stop by and see what was going on and watch the games for awhile too. There was a good combo of noobs (most of my fiasco players had never played before, and apocalypse world had 4 noobs) and experts (my first apocalypse world game had 2 people who had played before, and one who hadn't). The room was spacious, and it was pretty much ours all weekend cause only a few games ended up being run in there. People would use it as a meeting place to run other pickup games off hours too. Both the up and downside was that it was right next to big bar on 2!

    I think the signup sheets worked ok, and someone had put up a dry erase board as well, but I'd like to work something out better for next time. Also someone on facebook had recommended having more available slots to play, like an evening slot, which was a great idea and I think we could easily organize that for next year now that we're becoming familiar with faces and names and maybe we could wrangle a few more committed GMs so we could rotate out. I think keeping the facebook page open for comments and ideas will be good for next year.

    So Jim, since you're our pointman to GAMA... will you be returning next year? What should we be thinking about for next year? Is it possible to get GM badges next time? I'll be glad to help out again! It was a total blast. All you suckas that didn't show should definitely be there next year.

    \n Thanks for all those notes, Kira! I'm really curious how GoD might be improved and honed for future conventions, from Gen Con onward. Like, what's better than a dry-erase board for what we want to do? Or, rather, what didn't the dry-erase board do that it should have done?

    One thing that I thought the slots did that was interesting was create spectators. People would come in late and actually, quietly (for the most part) check out games they weren't a part of. "What's this?" they'd ask. That's great! It makes actual play into a kind of demo for more than the 3-5 people playing. When it works. But the GoD room seemed to be a go-to place for gamers looking to check out games in action. I want that to be true at Gen Con, too.

    I'll be spending a lot of Gen Con in the GoD room, too, so I'm eager to hear what I can do in a few weeks to learn some lessons from this past weekend. So keep the notes coming, y'all!

    Cheers,
    Will
