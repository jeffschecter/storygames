\n It seems like there's lots of talk about gaming online with skype, chat, wave, whatever. I'm curious how much of gaming is going on remotely vs in person. I'm talking about pen-n-paper RPGs, not MMORPGs or video games.

I've got no axe to grind or agenda, I'm just curious what's going on in the world.

    So percentages are cool, but how much time playing do they represent?

      \n 100% at the table.

      In the past, when I'd discovered RPGs but hadn't yet found people to play them with, I played a couple play-by-post games that, typically, fizzled and weren't that great. I played in a fairly long Planescape pbp that really had its moments, but in the end I shoved off.

      Oh, I've also played a 4-session game of Code of Unaris, which is meant to be (and was) played over chat.

      Anyway, now that I'm gaming weekly, I don't have the time or the inclination to game online. If I were absolutely stranded in a desert somewhere, I might try video skype gaming, but I think I'd feel dirty--I'd feel like I should devote my energy to investing in people around me and trying out games with them, which is what I'd probably do first.
