THE RAIN
- a roleplaying poem by Tomas HV M\xf8rkrid

You may be 6-13 players of this RP-poem.
You need chairs (or floorspace) in front of a window (every player must have a view of the outside).
The weather must be rainy (a heavy torrent is best).

Sit down before the window. Sit as close as possible.
Each and every player should be within easy reach of the hands of several co-players.
One of you read these instructions for all.
When at the end of the instructions, you commence play without delay.
One of you must take the time while playing, and stop the gameplay at 15 minutes.

- The phases and the game
During the two phases of the game; talk and silence, you will go through life, experiencing love and breakups as if the whole game lasted a lifetime. And when the game ends, you will narrate your own death.

- The talk phase
You may talk normally on any topic you deem interesting. Try to engage in calm dialog with the others.
Whenever anyone takes your hand, you are in love with that person, and the two of you are spouses/sweethearts.
If you hold hands with two men/women, you have a lover on the side (the last to take your hand).

- The silence phase
When anyone say these words: It is raining outside, you will all be quiet for a little while, staring at the rain.
You may let go of the hand of your spouse/sweetheart/lover during the silence, and thus breaking up with him/her.

Go through the two phases again and again, until the timekeeper announce the end.

- The end
When the timekeeper announces that the time is up, you will stand up one by one, and describe how you die. Make sure to tell your co-players if you dies as a happy or miserable soul. Do not embellish. Make it short, and sit down to listen to the deaths of the others.

When all have told how they died, the game is over.

Take care and have fun!

    \n I love rainy days and I love this game, Tomas. And reading it brought up a question about social mores.

    Is it more "normal" or "acceptable" (not really saying this right) for (Northern) Europeans to hold each others hands outside of a romantic relationship? Even in the hippie-dippy SF Bay Area, most people will flinch or hesitate when asked to hold onto someone's hand if they aren't close family or lovers. Unless it's a situation like helping someone or comforting someone who is in pain or sad.

    So for instance, if I suggested at an open gaming table that everyone hold hands, I'd get a lot of uneasy looks and hesitation and probably joking comments. It's seen as pretty intimate here.

    \n Thanks Chris!

    Holding hands is seen as kind of intimate here too, apart for the everyday handshake, of course. That is the reason I use it in this game. The fact is that we react to touch, and even the mild reaction created by the hand-holding may be used to unlock some deeper levels in us while playing this game.

    A for the looks and hesitation and joking comments: it all depends on two things:

    1 - the players: who are they? How mature are they? Do they have it in them to play a game of serious make-believe?

    2 - your way of presenting the element: do it with confidence and a calm voice! Make no fuzz about it, and it will most likely be a natural part of the game.

    Good luck!
