\n I've been slowly selling off or giving away stuff that I've accumulated throughout the years, and during that sell off, I stumbled across a box of games. It's full of old games like the Floating Vagabond, Consulting Detective, Red Box D&D, and other stuff. But perhaps the most interesting find was Space Infantry by Daniel Douglas Hutto. It sat in my donate pile for the last few months until tonight, when I thought I'd offer it up to the first person that wants it.

I remember playing the game, but looking the rule book over, I can't for the life of me figure out exactly how that was done. I'm willing to give this away to whoever wants it, but I thought it would be fun if the person who wants it:

1. Plays it once.
2. Tries to play it as it would have been played in 1982.
3. Writes an actual play report about it.
4. Sends it off to someone else to do the same.

Or whatever.

I'll pay for postage in the US, and maybe elsewhere if it's not too much. It'd be cool if there is competition about why you should be the first to receive it and I'll pick my favorite answer, but that's up to whoever wants it. Anyway, it's available to whoever wants it. I'll send it out at the end of Jan.

    \n It's a military space game where the pcs explore star systems, run into aliens, blow things up, fight the bad guys with extensive charts and percentages, build and blow up ships in space battles. There are rules for creating worlds and aliens. Here's the basic back cover blurb:\n
    Space Infantry has numerous simple rules which describe important aspects of adventure in space. We at D&R GAME DESIGN are always working to expand and improve the game.
    \nFrom Darkshire.net:\n
    1st ed by Daniel Douglas Hutto, Roger Allen Esnard (1982) D&R Game Design
    A spacefaring military sci-fi RPG, where PC's are space cadets in military service.
    \nRPG.net Review
