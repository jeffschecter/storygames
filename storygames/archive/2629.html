\n There are quite a few games where you see war from the soldiers' point of view. From Carry and Grey Ranks through Recon to Afghanistan d20.
\n
\nHowever, I can't think of any games offhand that are about war as seen from a civilian's point of view. Victims, bystanders, profiteers. I'm pretty sure there must be such games... can anyone think of any titles?
\n
\nAlso, why do you suppose it's this way? I have a few thoughts myself, but would be interested to hear from others.

    \n I'm not sure Grey Ranks counts as a game about soldiers. You can certainly play it as a game about victims, anyway - the degree to which actual combat figures in the narrative, and the overall approach to the war as a scene framing device, is 100% up to each group. And in the end, all the characters are victims - a happy ending is extraordinarily difficult to achieve.

    I'd suggest that the reason we don't see more games with a focus on war's non-combatant victims is that most games are power fantasies.

    \nI wrote a game a few years ago called Far Apart (html/text and PDF versions) that's half way there. Each player controls a British soldier at Ypres during World War I, but also his girlfriend at home in a munitions factory. The focus of the game is the sacrifices both persons has to make to see each other again after the war. I think the stories of the civilians would be more interesting than the soldiers', mainly because their situation is easier to understand.
    \n
    \nI haven't playtested the game, and to my knowledge it hasn't been played by anyone else.
