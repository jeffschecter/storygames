\n So I had this concept, and I thought it would be soooo coool.\n
The person who draws the oracle then replace 1 character or object with a dragon.
Example:

The Unquiet Past
KD: The exposure by erosion of a long-buried door.
JS: A knowledge-mad sorceress, delving into ancient secrets.
8D: A long-dead queen (replace with long-dead dragon), still trying to defend her realm.
QD: The fey and unfriendly guardians of an enchanted glade.
\nTurns out that doesn't work. (it took me 3 sessions of playtesting to figure this out)

Here's why: While the person is reading the oracle, everyone is thinking about what the dragon could be. Then they're jumping in with ideas, totally naturally, about what the dragon could be. That puts the facts of the fictional world up for debate, and slows down the game in this really noticeable way.

The moment the Oracle is drawn is a very special moment. The whole table has to jump in and very quickly go from loose ideas (oracle elements) to these very concrete setting elements. We have to all start agreeing about what we're imagining in order to have momentum to get into character generation and then scenes, because if we don't we're going to be continuously asking permission to make assertions about the world.

A "debate what we're doing tonight" mentality is, IME, killer for In A Wicked Age. The Oracle has to provide a strong hand on the tiller.
