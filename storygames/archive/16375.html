    \n Hello! The pocket edition isn't really "released" yet, but you can take a look at it here: http://dangerpatrol.com/dangerpatrol_pocket.pdf.

    I needed a quick-play version of the game that didn't require per-printed materials, so I whipped that up. Turns out I'm beginning to like it more than the Beta version.

    The "cards" referred to in the pocket version are simply index cards or scrap papers that you write stuff on. Not pre-made cards like the Beta had.

    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n The conditions weren't removed, per se, they just didn't make it in. I started writing a compact version of the game on an index card, just for kicks, and that's what I ended up with. We playtested it at Gamestorm, and it turned out to be pretty fun. It wasn't meant to replace the Beta version, so I wasn't writing it with changes in mind. Instead, I started from nothing, and asked myself, "Okay, what's the bare minimum I can include here and still get Danger Patrol?"

    \n I must say, I am completely impressed with how much information can be squeezed into two small pages. I'm starting to wonder if I would have been able to play this pocket version if I had not previously had the experience of running the full beta. I'm sure there are things that I am implying that aren't actually part of the text due to the condensed nature.
    \n
    \nPosted By: John Harper "Okay, what's the bare minimum I can include here and still get Danger Patrol?"
    \n
    \nWell it is clearly still Danger Patrol. And it is a bare minimum of rules, which I believe makes it even more Danger Patrol than Danger Patrol was. If that even makes sense. I just feel the nature of the beast is that of a quick pick up and play game, and going through the 30 page document was a bit more than I would have liked to have done. So this is a much better version!
    \n
    \nI'm still wondering what happend to the Gamma version that was teased using some Lady Blackbird like mechanics. I can see the influence of some of the mechanics I had read about though.
    \n
    \n
    \nAll-in-all I think there are some vast improvements here, but a couple of things I would have liked to see carried over (I'm sure they just didn't make it in due to space limitations).
