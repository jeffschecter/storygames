    So who can come? Like...just SG folks? Or is John Q. Gamer welcome as well? If I invite all my gamer friends, I'd expect anywhere from none to a couple of dozen to be interested. Is bringing your own horde bad form?

    Oh, and here's a random thought on organization. Put folks doing the same tasks together in a cabin. Like, "...Cabin 4: the Wolf (Food preparation); Cabin 5: the Rat (Firewood and trash collection); Cabin 6: the Monkey (Finance, planning, logistics, and administration)..."

    The upside of this is that it groups folks together by task (obviously), which can be helpful, since it makes coordinating schedules easier. And this way, the food planning folks don't have to disturb non-food planning folks when they get up early to make breakfast, for example. Another bonus is that it's a nice ice-breaker for attendees who don't know one another. Instead of, "Oh, hey, you're some guy who bunked down in the same cabin as me," it's, "Oh, you're on trash duty as well? Cool, let's sit down and talk about the details now." The big downside to this is that it could potentially split up groups that came together -- families, couples, buddies, etc. -- unless they coordinate their participation ahead of time.

    For events, I'd like to see more organization than at CN0, since there will be so many more attendees. My thought on this is to have five tracks, with one or two events at any time in each track. The first track would be "New Hotness" for the newer indie releases -- Agon, SotC, Don't Rest Your Head, Contenders, Primitive, etc. The second would be "Gold Standards," for the classic indie hits -- Burning Wheel, DitV, Sorcerer, PTA, etc. Third would be "Old School," for traditional games -- D&D, WoD, Champions, etc. Fourth would be "The Workshop," for games currently in playtest. Fifth would be "Out of the Box," for non-RPG games -- Jungle Speed, Memoir 44, Monopoly, poker, etc.

    There could also be a sixth track that's undefined.

    With 70 people, even having a dozen games running at the same time will not provide space for everyone to be in a game. Of course, some timeslots will be slower than others, and perhaps everyone attending won't be playing games. But still, that's a lot of people to accomodate.

    By having these tracks, we can make sure all types of games get equal opportunity.
