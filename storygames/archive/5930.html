    \n It is not really Solipsism, I realise, because of the acknowledgment of things outside yourself and the possibility of their independent existence. More accurately only those Solipsists which have Ascended (i.e. already left play) are true Solipsists, because they *are* the only things which exist in their reality.

    As explained on the history page on the solipsist website the name came out of the original 24 minute RPG contest, where 'solipsistic' was one of my words out of the hat

    \n Well the key is that your primary motivation is all about you. You should always me acting for yourself, and you cannot do anything serious without changing the world towards how you feel reality should be ... or more key, how you feel your place in the world should be. So you may admit the possibility that there are minds other than yours in the world (with their own desires), but yours always have to be more important.

    The juggling act for the player is how to do that without getting what you want and vanishing into your own narcissistic reality. If you like its more like your character is *almost* a true Solipsist, having tasted the power that reality is willing to offer totally unshakable self-obsession, but you are still on the edge, not quite sure that its right to vanish into your own reality.

    \n I have bad news for your Solipsism, Guy. I exist. (Though perhaps I'm only saying that to spread doubt of course...)

    In the game your character can become a truly solipsistic (and there are mechanics there to do so). When that happens nothing but your character's vision will exist: no other PCs,no other minds, in fact nothing that is not part of your vision of how the world is (and it can be a place that is cruel and harsh to you, for sure). But then you're no longer a player character in the game, and why should your character care as there is no longer a game. For some characters I think this is where they will end up, and the players may see that as fitting or not. Some might even go the other way and lose their ability to change reality as that can happen too, they just become part of someone else's imagination.

    \n Oh, David might have had an earlier version of rules up for playtesting but subsequently removed it. He does the website himself so he'd be the one to answer that. The character sheet has the equations at the bottom if you are familiar with the way it works. Basically there is a difficulty which is made easier by your Obsessions, then made harder by your Limitations, with the GM having the option to spend Shadow tokens to further oppose you. You can then spend Infestation to reach 0 or having expended all those you can also "push" further. Depending on whether the final difficulty is negative, exactly zero or positive determines who gets to say what happens and which mechanical values change as a result.
