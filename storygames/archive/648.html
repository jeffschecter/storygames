    To my mind, any particular instance of Freeform has rules, but they're implemented in a different way than in RPG's.

    1> Freeform rules are generally considered, adopted, modified, or rejected on an individual level, rather than on a larger scale, that is to say, a particular play group considers and adopts the rule "We Will Not Abandon You" rather than an entire suite of rules such as "GURPS" or a subset such as "GURPS detailed combat".

    2> Freeform rules are generally more open to interpretation, nuance, and GM/player fiat. Group consensus more explicitly trumps established rules in freeform play than in an RPG.

    3> Freeform rules are often less formally stated.
