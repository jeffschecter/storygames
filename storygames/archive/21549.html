    \n I'm reading this as "RPGs with asymmetrical roles, but near-equal creative responsibilities and strong textual guidance". Off the top of my head...

    Vincent Baker's other games - all of them.

    Those games by @Epidiah_Ravachol which are related to AW, but not in the super obvious "roll 2d6+stat" way -- I'm thinking of Wolfspell and The Dread Geas of Duke Vulku especially.

    You've gotta love The Mountain Witch by Tim @timfire Kleinart. A new edition is in the works.

    Some or some other version of The Shadow of Yesterday by Clinton Nixon/Dreisbach (you could probably go with @Eero_Tuovinen 's The Solar System + the World of Near supplement).

    Probably @Ben_Lehman 's Bliss Stage and The Drifter's Escape, two games I can't recommend highly enough.

    On Mighty Thews, by @Simon_C arryer, and (tentatively, as I haven't played it yet) The Bloody-handed Name of Bronze by @Joshua A.C. Newman

    You might also be interested in The Clay that Woke by @PaulCzege

    Tentatively (see above) Malandros and Other Borders.

    Possibly Primetime Adventures by @Matt Wilson or even The Pool by James V. West (OK, I'm dropping the "clear guidance" part here, but still...).

    Most definitely Sorcerer and Trollbabe, both by Ron Edwards. In fact, familiarity with AW might be useful in understanding and playing those.

    ...but, again, perhaps that's not why you like AW & its children, like, at all. I'll scrap together a different list then.

    \n It's hard to say what it is, exactly, that you like about "PbtA" games, without some more detail.

    Is it: a) a GMed type of story game with b) a focus on the characters, c) some clear ways for the players to contribute to emergent story (via mechanics design), and d) clear ways for the GM to prep and/or contribute to their stories, in a structure that supports all that?

    If so, I'd recommend some of the following, grouped loosely by category...

    Vincent's games that are similar in type and style: particularly Dogs in the Vineyard and Poison'd, and also possibly Storming the Wizard's Tower (if you can find it) and In a Wicked Age... (it's much less "traditional", in some ways - but I think you might like it).

    You may like Vincent's "series of prompts" games, perhaps - The King is Dead, In Dreaming Avalon, and Firebrands. These are a bit different, though.

    Going back in time:

    The Shadow of Yesterday, or Eero's version of it as The Solar System. Lady Blackbird and its variations (as well as the sequel, Magister Lor, share the "Key" technology of TSoY).

    For people who are not averse to more "crunchy" gaming, Burning Wheel and Mouse Guard (the latter is "lighter" in mechanical weight, but still fairly rules-heavy compared to the typical PbtA game).

    Lots of other "Forge classics" games may suit this category well, too, like Dust Devils, My Life with Master, The Mountain Witch, and With Great Power...

    Possibly Polaris, depending on whether you enjoy its quirks or not (it's a beautiful game! but it's also one of a kind).

    Grey Ranks is a game I've wanted to play for a long time, another "Forge classic" or "Indie favourite".

    Other "maybes" in this category: 3:16 and Misspent Youth.

    Some of my games fit into this category quite clearly, like The Bureau and Thus began the adventures of Eowyn... They explain my approach to using Keys for GM prep pretty succinctly, and therefore provide good tools for any other Key-based games (many of my favourite PbtA moves, by the way, are really Keys).

    I hear great things about Mutant: Year Zero and Tales of the Loop (they share mechanics, as far as I understand), but I haven't played either myself.

    \n Well for my part, the key to PbTA is that it is a fairly traditional Gygaxian game (GM and players), with a lightweight single-mechanic which uses a Fortune in the Middle approach (http://big-model.info/wiki/Fortune-in-the-middle).

    Hero Wars descended game are an obvious choice, such as the current incarnation of the HeroQuest RPG. but also, as others have mentioned TSOY/The Solar System. Also there are antecedents that Hero Wars drew on, such as Prince Valiant and Over The Edge
