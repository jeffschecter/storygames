\n Someone in another thread mentioned rpgsolo.com/, which I have found to be a neat tool to play with. Basically, it's part of the Mythic GM Emulator built into a notepad. You ask yes/no questions and then roll on them.

I want to use it to create a small squad/force/party/group of characters with interesting relationships.

So, I might ask:

Is the leader a male?
Then I roll "50/50" (or "Somewhat Likely"...whatever makes sense)
If the answer is "no", then the leader is a female.
Is she an alien?
Is she pilot of the ship?

And I use this method to get to a team of people for a starship crew or a dungeon adventuring party or a skirmish squad. It can have really interesting results in terms of teams that I would not have put together if I had chosen them.

What I would love is some yes/no questions that would really make the relationships interesting. Crews of people that squabble, fall in love, backstabbing...jealousy.

Does X get along with Y?
What other yes/no questions can you think of that I could employ to get interesting relationships?

    \n "Did X and Y grow up together?"
    "Did X and Y date the same person at one point?"
    "Did X break Y's heart in the past?"

    I personally like mythic complex questions a bit better, though. They have that extra bit of randomness with the Subject/Action lists which spice things up considerably.

    Like, for example: "What do X and Y share in common?"

    I got "Imprison. Illness."

    Maybe they were in a mental hospital together at some point (maybe they were both patients, or one was an orderly). Or, maybe they were jailbirds, and they both got addicted to some drug, or caught a venereal disease while doing time.

    Or, maybe they were both part of a former squad of CDC disease containment clean up team...

    \n What kind of questions render interesting relations? I think what you should look for is questions that give something no matter what you ask. Asking about if X or Y is brothers would be uninteresting if it said "no", so what you could do is always implying things. Here follows some example.

    AGENDAS
    - Have I betrayed you yet?
    - Did I manage to kill someone you cared about?

    ESTABLISHING FACTS ABOUT ANOTHER
    - Do I know about your secret?
    - Are your enemy also mine?

    \n I can see this working with character creation but for actual game play it might be a little lacking. Given that this is a solo game maybe you could write out two events - one from the point of view of "screw the character" and another from the point of view of "the character succeeds" and do a 50/50 roll to see what happens next. The story could grow incrementally from there. It would keep with you yes/no dichotomy.
