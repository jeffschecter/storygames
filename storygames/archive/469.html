I miss random character generation. I like the challenge of finding a character through play. I miss working to feel confortable in someone alien to my experience.

So I am trying to have a random chargen system in my game.

Am I crazy?

    Thems, mighty big claims... The best game ever written was the Yellow Dragon Inn, and it had random character generation also. (Edit: And it was played on an old dial up BBS that we connected to with our C64s!) I just found a copy (dot matrix baby) happily tucked away between the pages of the Floating Vagabond... And I thought I had lost everything in the great hard drive throwaway.

    My beef with random attributes was that you could end up with a character who was incompetent in all regards. I did like the solution to random stats that were in the old Space Gamer games like Battle Born. You rolled to see which stats your character was going to have, and in every roll, you'd have a character that would be good at something.

    So, I'd say a random chargen system would be good as long as it randomly creates good characters.
