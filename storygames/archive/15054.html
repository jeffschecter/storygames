\n Hey look! Bully Pulpit Games now has a wiki!

Our current goal is to make it a one-stop shop for Fiasco playsets, much like Bryant Durrell's comprehensive listing but with all the playsets hosted locally and available for download. We've already had lots of help getting things running from our friends Bryant, David Artman, Tim Rodriguez and Pete Douglas. We'd like your help as well!

If you are the author of an existing playset and would be willing to let us add it to the collection, we need you to 1) give us permission and 2) consider helping us by adding your work to the site yourself. If you're interested, contact us at info@bullypulpitgames.com.

For everyone else, we're looking for enthusiastic people with Mediawiki and/or organization skills who would like to give us a hand with ongoing maintenance and additions as more playsets come out. If that's you, drop us a line!

We're also open to suggestions about the best ways to make this a valuable resource for the Fiasco community. What would you like to see?

    \n I'll take the first round of questions/suggestion....

    RSS - You can get an Atom feed off the Recent Changes page, but that might be too much info for someone just wanting new pages... so get tthe Atom feed of the New Pages special page:
    (page: http://www.bullypulpitgames.com/wiki/index.php?title=Special:NewPages )
    (feed: http://www.bullypulpitgames.com/wiki/index.php?title=Special:NewPages&feed=atom&hideliu=&hidepatrolled=&hidebots=&hideredirs=1&limit=50&namespace=0 )

    Tweak the limit, if you like.

    Playset Categories - Category structure is ongoing; and it can be the biggest bugbear of managing a wiki. The POTM Category, however, is listed as the second link on the Playsets page (after the Featured PS link); how could it be more prominent for you? Should it be on the sidebar navigation (Steve?)?

    Is a "Printed" Category even relevant, given that the BPG Category includes them (by default, being BPG PSes)? Easy enough to make a "Playset/Printed" SubCategory and retag the six (eight?) that are in-print. Just can't see who would benefit (also not sure if Jason and Steve want those available freely; nor the printed Tilt and Aftermath tables--that's kind of, like, 90% of the rulebooks).

    Art - That's just time and effort; doable (and I've asked for that to be standard, for playset top pages). I am not sure, however, if the Playsets page should have thumbnails for EVERY playset--could make that page very unweildy/large to download. 25KB per thumbnail, given the one there already... that could get ugly fast.

    But, yes, each PS should have the cover art on its top page. Yes, the Fiasco top page should have some art--I don't have source files for the printed books (nor a scanner). Jason? Steve?

    \n And, yes, we are aware that not all Fan PSs have the "Playset/Fan" Category on them--automated input issues, to be resolved ASAP.

    If someone wants to take charge of that cleanup: every PS gets the "Playset" cat; then either the "Playset/BPG" or "Playset/Fan" cat. These are pulled by SemanticWiki to the playsets main page.

    Those of you who want to put in your own PSs (once made authors on the wiki), please use the Using Templates page to get started--read it all, lest ye break semantic code! :-)

    If you see a problem in a template page, let me know (here, there, email, whatever) and I'll fix it (if you can't figure out how to do so). I *think* I have covered every contingent--the templates are built with a "delete if not needed" approach, rather than "add what's needed" approach, to make it easier on newbies and provide consistency across PSs.

    Yay! Months in the making, and now the baby is going to leave the nest (and probably become bedraggled and confused! Gotta love wiki management...).
