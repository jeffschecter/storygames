Playing Card Based Resolution Systems

Flynn
January 2007 edited January 2007 in Game Design Help
I'm interested in surveying games (even non-roleplaying games, like board games that have some element besides just card-play) that use playing cards as a mechanic in play, especially for resolution. I'm interested in exposing myself to a wealth of new ideas / approaches.

The ones I've found so far -

Games that use playing cards as a central resolution mechanic:
Primetime Adventures
With Great Power...
Dust Devils
Dragonlance Saga (customized playing cards)

Games that use cards less centrally or non-playing cards:
Everway (uses custom tarot cards)
Deadlands (playing cards used for character abilities / initiative)
Savage Worlds (playing cards used for initiative)
2300 AD (playing cards used for NPC personalities)

I appreciate any suggestions in identifying additional games, or other resources, I might examine for further insight / ideas. My primary interest is roleplaying games using standard playing cards for resolution mechanics, but other related links or recommendations are welcome. Thanks.

    Hi Flynn,

    I'm working on a playing card mechanic for 'The Naked City' that gives each player a hand throughout the game and lets them play cards along with relevant narration to resolve conflicts. Each suit is a different type of communication/action (i.e. Hearts - emotion) and players can "trump" each others suits. After glancing at Sons of Liberty I can see some similarities.

    But if you are looking for super-simple, you can't beat Prime Time Adventures or Contenders.
