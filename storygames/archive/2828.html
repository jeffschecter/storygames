\n Lot gets sent down to Gamorrah, a world that was inherintly evil through and through from its very beginning to its near end. Lot carries its destruction, and he'll probably die along with it. The survival of both Gamorrah and Lot depend on his finding or creating a thriving goodness in that world. He turns his attention to the teaching a group of the most childlike beings he could find. They're probably creations of a witch or something. Things get episodic. He reaches for lessons (charms?) they may grasp; they have adventures in which each lesson is proven valuable or worthless to them. Time is limited (maybe the world is about to achieve space-travel or something). Moreover, teaching his pupils to be nice is a good way to for them to get killed, the world being what it is. If he fails to change the world in a meaningful way, which of his own lessons will he break to get himself out alive (or will he try to save his flawed pupils instead)?

I dunno. Which rpg already out would be best for exploring this sort of idea?

    \n I don't know about what game that is currently out there, but this gives me all sorts of scenario ideas for my 24 hour espionage game in which you play fallen angels. Are you trying to find good people in Gomorrah, to stop the oncoming rain of fire? Or are you bringing it on to punish the sinners? Or are you enticing the sinners to sin, because you're really working for Hell? Or, as an agent of Hell, are you trying to hide the sins of the Gomorrans, so that they won't be slaughtered (and can keep sinning)? Lots of angelic intrigue potential around that story.
