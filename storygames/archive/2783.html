If you're keeping up with Anders Sandberg's blog, in the hope that somewhere between posts about 3D imaging of information spaces and ones discussing the social ramifications of human augmentation he'll get back to talking about roleplaying games, you may have seen this post.

\n

Superg\xc3\xa4nget by Tobias Rades\xc3\xa4ter is a superhero game in Swedish, presented entirely in web-comic format, in a style something between European children's comic and Scott McCloud's Understanding Comics.

\n

I just came by to share the awesome.

    \n\nJonas Ferry \n\n\n
    \n
    \n\nApril 2007 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n I played the game with Tobias and two other guys this weekend.
    \n
    \nOne fun part of the game is that you play yourself, as a super hero. I played the "Super Nerd", with a fake Klingon forehead, Star Trek uniform, Dracula cape and functional Han Solo blaster. One of his super powers was the incredible knowledge collected in blogs and forums on the internet, which he used to find weak spots on super villains.
    \n
    \nAs I rolled for the super power I got to invent a weird blogger debating that blasters couldn't ignite exhaust fumes from plasma-driven spaceships. The Super Nerd proved it possible, ending an internet debate. Nothing less than a super hero could do that!
    \n
    \nAlso, you have to draw the super-hero costume on your character sheet with crayons.
    \n
    \nI hope for a translation as well, as I think many people would enjoy the game. But I know Tobias's first step is to print it in Swedish.
