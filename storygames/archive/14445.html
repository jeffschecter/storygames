A guy I follow on twitter for his board games recommendations/discussion was participating in a webinar and mentioned a bunch of stuff about using improve/RPG rules in office environments to increase productive collaboration. Sounded like it might tie in to the more "open-ended"

    \n The Quiet Year would be ideal for this:

    1.) It's about learning to collaborate and work together,
    2.) Its rules introduce new vocabulary and ways of thinking about communication,
    3.) It explores how groups are impacted by decisions they weren't involved in,
    4.) You get to be post-collapse survivalists and defend your village from wolves and plague.

    I can email out the playtest copy to anyone who asks for it.
    It's developed to the point of being an ashcan, I suppose: thorough text, been playtested about 9 times (one blind), plays consistently.

    \n In my office, we've played "My Uncle the Baron", and I've facilitated some freeform with a storyboard as output - both of those were fun and helpful.

    I've written a game about offices, with several less-than-subtle suggestions for running successful meetings (invite the smallest possible number of people, don't invite people who are on tilt...) in the mechanics, right alongside an absurd premise that gives players some emotional distance. That said, failure is the more enjoyable and likely outcome, and it's a dark black comedy, so it might not be office-appropriate.
