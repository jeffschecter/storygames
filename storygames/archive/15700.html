\n Hi All,

I was re-reading the My Life With Master rules and I was struck by the relatively weak role of the GM in the game; all of the prep is collective, and the only truly GM-specific task seems to be scene framing.

If you devolved the responsibility for scene-framing onto the players collectively, couldn't you play it without a GM?

If anyone has tried this, or has any advice, please let me know!

Best,

Zach

    \n I've run My Life with Master A LOT. I would absolutely not do away with the GM.

    The Master is a very big hammer and it needs to be wielded mercilessly. Similarly the towns people need to be full of loathing and contempt for the PCs. And those More Than/Less Than Humans need to be targeted hard.

    That's hard to do while shepherding your own minion.

    Jesse

    \n I play the Master as if he were my own PC with my own goals and agendas and a full compliment of minions at my disposal. I would say the master is present and active in about 1/3 of all scenes. I make sure the minions are constantly sent to do things. Usually focusing on these points:

    1) Something that is impossible for the minion to do because of his Less than Human.
    2) Something that the minion is extra qualified to do because of his More than Human but absolutely opposed to his own interests.
    3) Something that complicates the minion or another minion's Connections.

    And then all of that is peppered with the Master constantly promising but then withholding or conditionally applying whatever the minion emotionally needs. For example, I remember one PC was actually being ultra effective at serving the Master's demands. So I made sure the Master attributed credit to just about any other minion other than him.

    GMing My Life with Master is extremely proactive.

    Jesse

    P.S. Just remembered another favorite tactic of mine as the Master. Make sure success on Task A actually hinders the Master on Task B then blame the Minion for it. For example, send the Minion off to kidnap the Mayor's daughter but then come up with a reason as to why the Mayor's daughter needs to NOT be kidnapped for another part of the Master's plan to work. When the minion comes back with the kidnapped daughter get angry and ask them why they would do something so stupid?
