    \n Yes, I've played all of the Marquee Press games (Legendary Lives, Lost Souls, etc). The system in Khaotic is a variation on that found in the other games, with one-sided rolls (the GM doesn't have to roll dice) and levels of success based on roll quality that determine everything from basic task outcomes to damage dealt in combat. All of this works well (as it does in the other games).

    The problem here is the basic premise. Unless you have very well-adjusted players, you can pretty much be assured that your group will spend more time fighting for control of the vessel in the alternate universe than pursuing any kind of real goal. This can be fun for a while, though it quickly grows very tiring, IME.

    I think you'd be best off running Khaotic one-shots or interspersing the 'epsiodes' of your Khaotic campaign amongst other game sessions to alleviate the aforementioned problem.
