\n Back in the Game Chef event, all the entries in my feedback group rocked hard. Aidley's Together rocked me "double hard", because I too had been thinking a little of a "Massive Multiplayer Offline Tabletop Story-Game". It's a touch Guild Wars, a touch RPGA, a touch LEGO (when you look at how to build, deconstruct, and rebuild your character). It handles things like social and combat conflicts with the same system in kind of a do-it-yourself and still crunchy way.

BTW, you can find the G-C copy here:
http://game-chef.com/workshop/uploads/Together.pdf

Since I've been thinking about throwing down in the next two weeks over here in NC, I was wondering if Jack had done anything new with it?

Also, have you put any more thought into it, or thought about things that would best be changed?

Have you had a chance to run it?

    \n Wow. Nothing like being put on the spot is there?

    No, I've not done anything with it; nor, to be honest, am I terribly likely to. There are other games in my head that I'm more interested in; and the truth is that I don't find very much time to work on games these days.

    Mechanically, it works - more or less. The tests we did were fun, but not exceptional. It's a bit of a hodge podge of ideas, mechanically equal but freestyled character design (very much inspired by Capes) is a big fave of mine as is Character Change as more significant than Character advancement. To be honest I think each of the systems, ideas and features in it needs a good shake down and putting back together in a more coherent and well realised form. Another game I was working on which shares some features with it, Supers Hollow (highly incomplete doc) did it better but has flaws of its own.

    Conceptually? I'm less sure. The basic flaw is that in order to strip it down so that characters can be freely swapped between any game I took away all the bits that make it really interesting to play in the world. You're basically floating free with no long term aims, goals or solutions. An unstable world is, I think, ultimately an uninteresting world. Perhaps an Amber-esque infinite series of worlds would form a better backdrop to the style of gaming.

    I'm very keen on "Open" games still, but I think the sweet spot for them probably lies in a "group" of about 12 to 20 itinerant players than in attempting to push the concept out further.
