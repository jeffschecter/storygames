Leisure Games London reprazents the indie scene...!

rrr23
April 2006 edited April 2006 in Story Games
So I was wandering around a gaming con today. Turns out it's much more of a wargaming / miniatures thang, which I'm not that into, but nevermind. I watched a few games, didn't play anything; browsed a few stalls, didn't buy anything and had pretty much decided I was coming away empty handed and having not really done much.

But anyway Leisure Games had a stall. They're a pretty decent gaming store in North London. So I wandered over and had a look, not expecting much.

Standard rpg store stuff of course, but then in one section I spot... ooh Inspectres!

Cool I think, they've got an indie game. I wonder how that snuck in?

Then I notice.... Conspiracy of Shadows and Capes... and I'm getting more interested. Turns out they had quite a solid selection of indie titles amongst the standard stuff. MLWM, Sorcerer, and all it's supplements, Burning Wheel, With Great Power and others. And not only that but they had multiple copies of many of them.

I ended up with Kill Puppies For Satan (Annotated Edition), Burning Wheel: Monster Burner, The Sorcerer's Soul, and the nice happy feeling that I'd got something fun and interesting out of the day. That's the first time I've seen these games in a retailer over here, and personally I think it was a good thing. I know the retail avenue isn't the be-all and end all of sales any more, but I do think it has the opportunity to introduce smaller games to a new audience.

w00t!

Drew

  • PaulCzege
    April 2006
    Angus Abranson absolutely reprazents. A couple of years ago he contacted me out of the blue. (Half Meme Press has only directly solicited a couple of retailers in its history, and Leisure Games wasn't one of them.) Now, two or three times a year he contacts me directly to order a big box of copies of My Life with Master. (Last time he got some Bacchanals too.) As a retailer with a brick and mortar storefront, he gets a 50% per unit discount from me. But otherwise he pays for his order up front, via PayPal. And he pays shipping. (Those are my retailer terms.) He has me ship to a U.S. address, a freight forwarder. I presume he's consolidating his U.S. orders to control overseas shipping costs. I can't guess what his profit margin per book is, but it has to be less than what he's making on the titles of traditional RPG publishers. I've sold lots of books to retailers, but I'm unaware of any retailer who pays more attention, puts more effort, and better accommodates costs than Angus does to have that shelf of indie games for his customers.

    Paul
