\n Can you think of any specific time periods or events that haven't really been touched on by roleplaying products?

Sure someone may have generated up an obscure game for the period...something that never reached the mainstream, or was never considered "the new indie hotness"...but if you don't know about that obscure game than it's a valid answer for this question. I you've got an idea for a system that might be an easy hack for this historical event, feel free to share that as well.

Answers I'm NOT looking for... Either of the World Wars, the Vietnam War, the Sengoku Period of Japan, Ancient Rome or Greece, Biblical Events...these subjects have been done many times over.

The kind of answer I'm looking for is The Great Emu War (which might make a fun 3:16 Hack).

    \nThe Glasgow Ice Cream Wars of the 1980s killed at least six people.

    The Maria Reynolds/Alexander Hamilton affair/blackmail/con job, a complete mess of lies and secrets involving famous dead guys.

    It's 1979 and you're working for NORAD. All of a sudden, the radar goes crazy and starts telling you that the Russians have launched their missiles. Do you retaliate and launch as well or do you hope and trust that it's a glitch? (Spoiler: in the real world, it was a false alarm, or we wouldn't still be here. But in a game, who knows?)

    Prescott Bush's plot to overthrow FDR and install a fascist government.

    Jack Parsons and L. Ron Hubbard and various science fiction authors in California in the late 40s, doing Crowleyan magick and inventing rocket science and all kinds of crazy shit. (I think there's a Trail of Cthulhu scenario along these lines, but we could stand some more stuff.)

    The whole Men Who Stare at Goats/MKULTRA/First Earth Battalion weirdness scene could easily have some cool gaming potential in a variety of ways (with or without assuming the psychic powers are real.)

    Sealand or any other micronation of dubious legal standing. (Might make a decent Dramasystem pitch? You're fighting over how to keep your tiny, disrespected nation running while fighting for international recognition.)

    Centralia, PA, the everburning. Useful as a backdrop for your modern horror games, or as a game about the drama and legal battles of the holdouts still living there, misguidedly fighting the man.

    Any of these historical superweapons could be used to transpose Star Wars into historical periods.

    \n The Spanish Civil War and the Russian Revolution and interwar-Germany

    Games deal with these time periods, but rarely specifically with the events, which were full of drama and often terribly odd. And the games are usually full of Cthulhu, airships, and ancient tombs. Or gangsters.

    Not ancient Rome or Greece, but Alexander's successor states in Persia and India haven't been covered TOO well, as far as I know. And I'd love a game that specifically covered the Anabasis. The period of great migrations after the fall of Rome would also be FASCINATING. Practically any medieval RPG could do it, but probably wouldn't deal with the culture clashes and the knowledge that there's another, even nastier group of nomads on your tail.


    1837 Farmer's Revolt in Upper Canada.
    1903 Colorado Labour Wars
    Rhodesian Counterinsurgency/Second Chimurenga
    The Opium Wars
    Post WWII Nazi hunting
    The Battleship Potempkin (might be better as a board game)
    Mutinies at Spithead and Nore
    Watergate (you're political operatives trying to ensure the re-election of Nixon!)
    The Pax Mongolia, and the Khanates that replaced it.


    And I'd love to play the Regiment in WWI.

    \n A very specific moment in musical history: It's December 21st, 1908, and Schoenberg's Second String Quartet is being premiered in Vienna, Austria. Somewhere during the third or fourth movement. We've started, not quite comfortably, but at least in somewhat familiar territory, in a version of F-sharp minor that we might not like, but do recognize. Now, the soprano is singing, "I feel a breeze from another planet..." and the world goes fucking nuts - there's no harmony, no order, nothing to hold on to. We're fully into atonal territory here. I mean, it's not like we're petty bourgeosie, afraid of change - we wouldn't be here, at this concert, if we didn't want to be challenged, right? But this goes too far. People were already muttering during the third movement, but by this point it's starting to get rowdy. That lady in the awful fox fur collar starts sobbing into her hands, some fellow that looks like an engineering professor stands up and starts shouting. There's a riot... at a concert... during a string quartet. Arnold Schoenberg has finally walked into the wilderness, and taken us with him. Folks are arrested, there are court cases, Schoenberg himself testifies...
