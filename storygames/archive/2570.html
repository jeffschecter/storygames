    \n 'Cuz the Middle Ages are fun. But! It's only a one-shot, so really the setting isn't going to matter too much. No one is expected to do any research, and the setting's color is just that: color, not an end in itself.
    \n
    \n"Germany" and "1250 AD" are simply vague signposts. Somewhere during this period:
    \n
    \n* Holy Roman Emperor Frederick II dies; the succession is unclear, and the Empire erupts into civil war.
    \n* The Holy Roman Empire was at war against the Pope for control of Sicily and Tuscany.
    \n* The Mongols conquer Russia, and aim for the Near East until they bog down fighting the cult of the assassins.
    \n* The Teutonic Knights and the Poles were trying to suppress Slavic pagans in Eastern Europe.
    \n* The Spaniards were beginning to make the first stirrings of the Reconquista against the Moors.
    \n* The Cathar heresy was in its last days, pummeled by the French monarchy and the Inquisition.
    \n* Byzantium is an occupied city, conquered by traitor knights and ruled by Venetian merchant-princes.
    \n* The Crusades are sort of on/off throughout this time.
    \n* Many groups were sorely oppressed: Jews, Muslims, Roma, women, heretics, you name it...
    \n
    \nSo, it's simply a matter of D&D-ing; this stuff up. Throw in some giants in Scandinavia, some pixies, some ghouls, a clay golem or two controlled by Jewish mystics, a pit fiend (the pit fiend, really), toss some dragons into the Swiss Alps, turn the sword handed down by Charlemagne into a flame tongue, gargoyles guarding the cathedral, etc.
    \n
    \n(Again: I don't anticipate any of this arising in the course of play--I just want, like, dungeons and knights and stuff, in a setting that's interesting to design yet unobtrusive in play. But it's out there, should everyone say, "Hey let's do this." I suppose we could use a generic setting instead, but the setting sold me on the game rather than vice-versa.)
