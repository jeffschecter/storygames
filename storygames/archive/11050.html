    \n Read through this game last night. Not played it yet ...

    I really like the setting; a train leaving Warsaw amidst the German/Russian attack on Poland in WWII. The railroaded nature of the game is good, as a track for building our own story, on the skeleton of the "real" one. The ten scenes gives a nice feeling of a chaotic country falling apart, and the train traveling through the chaos with nothing more to commend itself than the resources of it's ad-hoc staff. These scenes also provide the players with the dramatic frames for their interplay, feeding the game with a multitude of interesting challenges.

    The pre-generated characters are all nicely formed, and I suspect; with the essential elements to support the drama in effective ways. The character-elements are divided into three; the YOU HAVE (resources), the WE ALL WONDER (character-guidelines, openings for developments) and the AND WE KNOW (predicted future, fate). This is really good! It empowers the players in several ways, giving them ample stuff to participate in the overall narration with their own individual agendas, securing an element of conflict and surprise to the game.

    I am even more interested in playing this game now, and will try to do so with in the next weeks. Wish me luck!

    BTW: Jason: I did not find any map in the PDF from Lulu ...

    \n Thanks, Tomas. I wish you every success! I really appreciate your observations and can't wait to hear how it goes. The pre-generated characters follow the model in Archipelago II, where they would have been built collaboratively by everyone at the table ("We all know" is your session destiny point). For this adventure I wanted really focused characters that were strongly tied to the setting and provided additional backstory, so I made them all up. You could easily cross out one or two "We all wonder" questions and replace them with your own if you wanted to make the characters your own.

    The map forms the cover of the printed book, so it hadn't occurred to me that Lulu wouldn't include it in the pdf. Here it is.
