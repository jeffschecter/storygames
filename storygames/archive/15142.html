\n I'm working on a hack for Apocalypse World that is set in our contemporary cities: no changes, nothing supernatural or weird, just the ordinary, everyday world we are familiar with. The PCs will all be chancers and criminals or anybody who isn't afraid to work on the wrong side of the law in order to make enough scratch to survive and prosper.
\n
\nWhat I really need are lots of suggestions for the playbooks: I'm working on 12 of them and its a lot to do on my own, so any contributions, ideas or just general feedback are very gratefully received. I'm particularly interested in list of names and descriptions to choose from but mostly I need unique moves. Here's a precis of the 12 playbooks in development:
\n
\nThe Bastion - There are sharks circling this 'hood, ready to eat it whole and spit out the bones; maybe if you give them what they want, they\u2019ll leave some of it alone.
\nThe Blur - It can\u2019t have been you, because officially, you don\u2019t even exist.
\nThe Fallen - You took an oath to uphold the law, but you had your fingers crossed behind your back.
\nThe Feelgood - If you can smoke it, sniff or stick it into yourself, you sell it to those who need it.
\nThe Go-Between - Everyone\u2019s looking for something; you bring together those with a demand and those who can supply.
\nThe Heavy - You provide manual labour on the wrong side of the law: moving crates, guarding doors, breaking arms.
\nThe Ice - You\u2019re a killer.
\nThe Lever - Once you know what they\u2019re most afraid of, you\u2019ve got them in the palm of your hand.
\nThe Merchant - It\u2019s all about buying low and selling high; the commodity doesn\u2019t matter, only the money.
\nThe Mover - When it positively, absolutely has to fucking get there tomorrow.
\nThe Thief - All property is theft, so on balance you\u2019re not really doing anything wrong.
\nThe Trickster - Black is white, up is down, hot is cold... all it takes is a word to change the world.
\n
\nMore info about this hack is on my website.

    \n
    \nName: being respected, being reliable, being connected.
    \nHush: being discreet, being subtle, being close-mouthed.
    \nBrass: being bold, being determined, being pragmatic.
    \nWise: being prepared, being cunning, being aware.

    \nDebt: how much someone owes you (replaces Hx)
    \n

    \n
    \n
    \n This project is still live and on-going: the latest developments relate to the in-game economy (e.g. the money and property that the characters own) which I've basically scrapped. No, really.
    \n
    \nOK, there is still some dough in the hack, but its mostly an abstract quality used as a tracker of a character's overall wealth: what really counts on a day to day basis is whether or not a character is making a living.
    \n
    \nInstead of an entry for Look, every playbook will soon have an entry for Loot, which describes the circumstances under which it is assumed their character is making a living, e.g. the Feelgood's living depends on keeping at least three users supplied with drugs, whereas the Bastion's depends on protecting the people in their 'hood from going down or out.
    \n
    \nIf a PC's livelihood is threatened, then they can only spend token amounts of money and can't afford to support themselves until their livelihood is restored. Besides having their living threatened directly by interrupting the revenue stream given by their Loot, it can also be threatened if they spend a large sum of money, are down, etc.
    \n
    \nThis has neccessitated more than just an additional entry for each playbook: many of the moves have had to be changed to reflect the new role of dough, so expect updates to just about all the playbooks over the next week, plus one all-new playbook. Having had some fruitful discussions on this topic over on the AW forums, it struck me that one very obvious playbook was missing from the set... the Shark is swimming this way soon. :-D
