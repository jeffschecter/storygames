\n An idea I've been playing around with for a few years, playing giant monsters who battle it out in human cities around the world but might just be saving it in the process, took a big leap forward today. I've begun a very basic AW hack with some stripped down rules: there's still a lot more to be done, but see what you think. Does the one stat method here work? What do you think of the moves in the two sample playbooks? And the rage rule?

    \n As I intimated, I've wrestled with this idea on and off for a few years, but the simplicity of this version has really charmed me: I've always envisioned this as a true tale of monsters, acting on their instincts but unwittingly having a positive impact on human affairs, e.g. they just happen to stomp on an alien invasion or eat a toxic waste spill. There's something about basing everything on their Size that I find really appealing, it makes the monsters seem more like a force of nature.

    The idea of "What does it want?!" is to prompt the players to name something specific the monster wants to achieve, even if the monster itself is incapable of expressing this desire, so not just a city that it favours, but an organisation like the Royal Navy or a rock group and its fan club, etc. So, whilst the monster acts instinctively, you, the player, direct its behaviour at one remove, so that it achieves its goals 'unintentionally': that is, the intent is the player's rather than the monster's, but the monster reaps the benefits of its actions.

    \n I've been working on a rough 1 to 4 scale for damage: so the basic damage a monster dishes out is 1, which is also the amount it takes them to kill a human being or destroy a small vehicle. If a monster takes time to grab something to hit with, it does 2 damage, which is enough to demolish small buildings and armoured vehicles; larger buildings or heavily armoured vehicles (such as battleships) take 3 damage to destroy and 4 damage is reserved for more conceptual entities (like armies or cities) or truly huge and dangerous targets (like other monsters).

    Almost any weapon that could conceivably harm a monster is only going to do 1 damage to it, so artillery, missiles, lasers, poison gas, etc. Anything that can do more than 1 damage to a monster would certainly be doing horrible amounts of collateral damage or would destroy itself in the process, i.e. crashing a jet fighter into a monster. To do more than 2 damage surely requires some kind of special move with its own restrictions or requirements, like only being effective in water or in space, but I prefer fronts with special moves other than merely raised damage: in the one game I've MCed so far, the three-headed space dragon attacking Earth could regenerate damage whilst in space and I can easily imagine other threats that entrap or coerce the monsters rather than trying to kill them outright.
