    \n In a game of fae royalty, mixed with industrial-age england, my character had the edge "Cannot see Glamours", meaning that he always saw the Fae and their environs exactly as they truly were, rather than as the hyper-beautiful, aetheric beings everyone else saw.

    He wouldn't sit down on or even touch any furniture in a fae's home for reasons he never shared with anyone, though his distaste when the subject came up was... palpable.

    He also couldn't quite hide his revulsion when he discovered one of the other characters was sleeping with a fae lord.

    He went to great lengths to conceal this 'ability', (rightly) suspecting that it would get him killed if it ever came out.

    \n Not perhaps as creative as the cyborg zombie monkey example, but one of the really great edge/issue combinations I saw in the PTA game that I ran was a character with Control as his issue and "I knew this was coming" as his edge. So the player would be able to improvise these wildly implausible back-up contingency plans that he had of course come up with in advance, worthy of House of Games and the like, and then have them succeed flawlessly while, in the bigger picture, totally f-ing everything up, so as to hit his issue the hardest. So his super-secret back-up plan might mean that he wins a duel, but succeeds in making the PCs political pariahs and the situation infinitely worse.
