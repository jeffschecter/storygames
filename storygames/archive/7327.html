\n I can't quite bear to resurrect the thread since it's been a month and a half, but this is a direct follow-up to D&D Mini's and Check out John Harper's awesome tokens.

I spent the last few days using the Wizards art gallery to which Judd linked, along with a $10 Martha Stewart craft punch, a $9 box of 1" metal fender washers and my little Canon photo printer, to make a few dozen token minis. They look cool as hell and the washers give them a really solid feel. I also took Erik's suggestion and made them double-sided, with a red overlay on the back to indicate bloodied status.

However, even after drying overnight, the Mod Podge (hard coat variety, as per Jason) is still pretty tacky--enough that it sticks slightly to paper, and sticks REALLY hard to the other tokens if I stack them or even toss them in a pile. Mod Podge sticks better to Mod Podge than to metal, unsurprisingly.

Has anybody else had a problem like this? Any suggestions? Am I just going to have to make some kind of custom foam-block cutout tray to store them?

    \n Ok, response from my GF:

    First things first: what glue type did you use to stick the paper to the base? She explains that some combinations of glue and varnish don't go well, and cause it to not harden, or to make white halos.

    2nd: you have to apply it very thin, and then wait VERY patiently. That kind of finish can take from 24 to 72 hours to harden. Don't worry.

    3rd: that specific kind of varnish is not marketed here. Please check if it is water based or not. That's very important, especially regarding the interaction with the glue.

    4th: if you want a thicker coat... you'll have to lather, rinse, repeat: apply several thin coats waiting the whole hardening time.

    That's it for now, now go find some late 19th century cherubs to stick to your dresser!

    \n Update on the finish: waiting a few days reduced the tackiness but did not eliminate it; same goes (better but still not eliminated) for the spray-on matte finish. Basically, if I stack these guys for an hour they stick together, and if I were to leave them stacked for a month I'm sure at least a few would end up damaged when I separated them.

    So I spent a couple days wishing I had some kind of perfectly round one-inch nonstick separators to put between these guys when I stack them in my Alea hardshell case along with all the magnets I got (to which the washers stick very nicely). Yes, two days... before realizing I still had half a box of washers left over. To which the coating doesn't stick in the slightest.
