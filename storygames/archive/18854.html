\n I have an unusual request: I'm looking for relatively small games that can be played in maybe a couple of hours (or at least reach a reasonable stopping point in that time) suitable for a group of somewhere between six and eight people, several of which will be kids between the ages of 11 and 13. This is way outside my realm of familiarity, so I'm turning to you folks for advice.

For context, every year during October I host a small haunted house. It's a month-long project with the builders getting together on the weekends or early weeknights and working long hours in to the night. This year we also have some of the neighborhood kids as volunteers, which has changed the group dynamic a bit. They aren't well-suited to the kind of hours and intensity we usually put in, and they don't know us very well, so we're taking a more relaxed schedule this year and spending some time in the evenings hanging out and playing games. I'd like to offer up a few storygames, but I'm not entirely sure there's anything suitable. I have my eye on Haunted and possibly Psi*Run, but that's it so far.

Any thoughts?

    \n I would look at making a targeted scenario using Lasers & Feelings by John Harper. The game was really easy to run even for a dozen people at once, it was very forgiving of people just getting up and leaving or showing up partway through, and a good game of it is about an hour and a half to two hours at most, so it's easier on the attention span than many games. It's very simple to learn so youth or inexperience won't get in the way, and while its default tone is crazy space adventures (eg classic Trek, Farscape), it wouldn't be hard to do it as a spookier episode of the show, with the ship powered down, or making a big deal of Captain Darcy's psychic entity in his head, or an Alien-esque scenario (minus the horrible gore and elements that turn it to an R rating).

    Of course, even in a spookier scenario, it's still sci-fi, and as such might not be appropriate for a haunted house deal. I suggest it for its simplicity, its ease with which it can handle large groups, and how it is very forgiving of lower attention spans. Just seemed like the perfect game with regard to "big group with kids".
    Maybe it could be hacked into a more appropriate setting. I dunno. Good luck, this is a cool event you're doing!

    Oh, and here's a link to it: Lasers & Feelings PDF

    I also totally agree with Adam that Parsely games are fantastic for this. Along with Action Castle, Pumpkin Town seems very suited to the context.

    \n Eight people is a lot for most tabletop games. One option is a larp of some sort. I have some for slightly larger groups. One option for 8 people is the Hamlet Parlor Larp, available at the Shifting Forest Storyworks site:

    http://www.shiftingforest.com/

    Most of the others of the series are too dark for 11-13 year olds, but this one should be fine, I think.
