Eric_J_Boyd

So lo and behold I have an email this morning directing me to RPG Laboratory an offshoot of 1km1kt. It looks like a potential central location for gaming development blogs, a place to share playtest drafts, etc. The idea seems like a great one, especially for those (like me) who are relatively unknown and working on their first game. My concern, though, is whether a critical mass of folks will actually make use of the site and create enough traffic to it that we get the exchange of ideas that would help everyone's game development.

So do you think you'll be using it? If so, how will it fit into what you're already doing to market and develop your games?

    Heya,

    IMO, for a site like that to work it's got to have more that space for blogs and rough draft PDFs. It needs tools of some kind. Probablity generators, copyright free map generators, forms to fill out stuff like the Big Three or the Power 19, links and contacts for POD printers, a news section from places like IPR, OneBookShelf, LuLu, etc., links to artists and a small place for them to display samples of their work w/prices, links to copy-right free artwork, and an article section where verterans who have published several games can offer pointers and relate their stories to newer designers. Without all that stuff, you won't attract people from places like The Forge, RPGnet, or RPGtalk. At least, that's what I think.
