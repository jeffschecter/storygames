    Posted By: RafaelWhen you're thinking about buying a new game:

    How important is word of mouth to you?

    Whose mouth?
    \nWhen I hear about a new game, today I almost always ignore it until I hear one of these things:
    - something about it that grab me (the situation or the setting, or some nifty new system usually)
    - somebody whose opinion on games who I have learned to trust and can write well write about it
    - somebody whose craft I trust wrote it

    The first one is not sufficent, today, to make me buy something, but it's enough to make a mental note of follow any thread about that game to learn more. The second and the third are usually enough to make me buy a game, if there is nothing about it that make me think that I will not be able to play it.

    This is rather different from last year, when I did buy almost anything that got good word-of-mouth or a good review anywhere. I don't regret the amount of books I brought and I could not play - I was still "learning about the territory" and it was a fast way of doing it - but now I am much more cautious.

    The people I trust are, I think, the "usual suspects" that can move a lot of copies with a single actual play thread: Ron, Vincent, and some others (the "others" vary in effect: sometimes they can write an actual play report that get me fired up wanting to play that game RIGHT NOW, other times not. Vincent and Ron have the ability to get that effect every time, if they like very much the game. They know how to write about games. I wrote their names because I think they are in everybody's list, but I don't want to write the other's names to avoid forgetting about somebody or embarassing others)\n

    Do you find that word of mouth prepares you adequately for the book or PDF that you've purchased?
    \nNo, if by "word of mouth" you mean the quick citations on story-games or rpg.net. The ones where somebody ask for the "best game to play Romans in Chicago in the '30" and everybody list their favorite game. I don't even notices these at all these days. A single detailed actual play thread is much better than tens of quick postings.\n

    Do you read reviews as well?
    \nAlmost never. The people I trust don't write reviews anymore, it seems, and the people who DO write reviews usually hasn't still earned my trust (there are always exceptions)

    \n If a game solves a design problem I have, or contains a design innovation that I don't feel like I could have come up with, I tend to jump on it. But the really best way to have me buy a game is to have me hear someone talk excitedly about it and explain it to me. Podcasts are a good way, but real life or Skype encounters are better.
