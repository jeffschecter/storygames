    Posted By: RyPlease don't hold back in explaining what the games are. I don't know why I should be excited about Ingenero. Is it my dream game?
    \nAll about Ingenero

    Im reading 'messages that stick' at the moment, based on a recommendation from someone in this forum. How to communicate the core idea in one sentence or so. Its unbelievably hard.

    So far I have this which is too wordy, but it the best I can do right now.

    Ingenero is a little system with a big engine. Its primary purpose is to propel the characters towards dramatic action.

    Ingenero is for action-themed play in any setting \u2013 players describe their characters in terms of actions they can take, rather than skills or equipment they possess.

    The most important feature of the system is goal-oriented play for pacing and focus. This is achieved using two alternating phases \u2013 the first deals entirely with story development, character decisions and consequences, and the second zooms in on those decisive moments of achievement or failure when the characters advance closely on their goals.

    An important principle is adhering to the rules of drama rather than the rules of physics or probability. Characters always get a chance to at least compete for their goals \u2013 they are never completely knocked out of a conflict prematurely.

    A key aim of the system is cinematic conflict resolution. Conflicts are resolved using descriptive, competing actions which can be used to target other characters, maneuver for advantage, and even aid or interfere with another characters actions. Resolve complex situations with ease and style!

    \n Well, it's like this:

    -- Push: New Thinking about Roleplaying was published in 2006, after 2 years of work, and was meant to be an annual journal (ha!)
    -- I attempted to organize Push 2 c. 2007-2008, but got totally overwhelmed, despite having solid drafts from Tim Kleinart, Eero Tuovinen, Bill White, and maybe John Kim (I forget exactly, but they were good).
    -- Magic Missile will be published in 2011, five years after Push, and though I initially planned to co-edit with Ryan Macklin (who sparked the idea to do another anthology of articles and short games), he had to drop back to focus on other work.

    It's possible I might head up another edited volume in the future, but don't keep your fingers crossed on a release date. Trying to wrangle one of these things on your own is a monumental task and there's a reason folks like Luke Crane and Fred Hicks surround themselves with a sizable support staff, allowing them to get a product out most every year. Otherwise, nothing doing.

    Honestly, I'm thinking more and more about the potential of presenting this same type of material as a series of booklets, each containing one or two articles or short games, because those are much easier to organize and quicker to turn around, but I'm less sure about the commercial potential of such things. Perhaps they would work better as PDFs or an electronic-only journal? Still, Matt Snyder seemed to be increasingly stressed out managing Daedalus, so I'm wary of that as well. I'm keeping an eye on Nathan's plans to publish his short-form work partially to see if there's a model there that I can steal.

    In any event, I have enough to do this year already, with both Magic Missile and Game Chef 2011.

    \n "Jump the Shark"

    A story telling gambling game about a bunch of writers for the B-Movie Channel forced to write outlandish Sci Fi scripts.

    The game comes with story cards that guide the players through the scenes of a really bad movie. With each scene players throw a token into the pot and make the scene worse. As each writer adds to the scene the chance they have of winning the pot goes down but only the last writer and the caller have a shot at the pot (and the call can be stolen). Adding good scenes is rewarded with extra tokens. At the end of the game the movie is written and the player with the most tokens wins.

    Plays in 20 to 30 minutes for 3 to 6 players.

    I'm putting this out for Gen Con - but I'm running it at Origins as well. I feel good about this game so I'm going to make a 1000 of them and get them out to stores. I can sell them through distribution at a profit because of the labor I can do in house to make the things.

    Chris Engle
