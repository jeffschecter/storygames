\n Two artists that make me think, "Why hasn't someone designed an RPG illustrated with their work?"

Cheval Noire - The Art of Aya Kato
\u201cFor me, the beauty of Japanese art is in its two dimensions \u2013 its deepness and the beauty of the outlines. I often find inspiration from books or poetic language and my pieces extend from there. I like to think the passion I have for my work is expressed through the images I produce and would love to collaborate with people all over the world \u2013 in music, illustration and fashion.\u201d - Aya Kato

The Art of Caroline Young.
"Lam Oi Char changed my life. My mother had encouraged me to paint as a teenager in Hong Kong where I was born and raised. But it was not until I began studying watercolor wit Lam Oi Char that I gained the confidence to succeed as an artist, "says Young. From her teacher Caroline Young learned the traditional forms of Chinese art and filled her silken rural landscapes. She chose as her medium Chinese watercolors, acrylic and gouache. Caroline mixes her own colors to achieve unique and vibrant tones, unavailable in commercially prepared paints."
