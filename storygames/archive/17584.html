\n A friend of mine asked me if there are any swashbuckler story game out there. Could someone point me at
1) a specifically swashbuckler game where narration rights are shared (which doesn't mean there can't be a GM!)
or
2) a game you think could really shine with a swashbuckler setting, and again, where narration is shared ?

Swashbucklers of the seven skies, I already know, but it's a little bit too much traditional for him (to establish his criteria).

I would also like you to explain one or two things that make this game great for the swashbuckler genre.

Thanks a lot.

    \nElizabethan World has shared narration in the Apocalypse World sense.

    What's really great about this game is the sense of historical location. In swashbuckly movies, there's often a sense of time and place underlying the whole thing. Elizabethan World is one of the few RPGs to give you that time and place, and what a time and place it is.

    Lady Blackbird seems like a pretty obvious choice here.

    \nI hadn't thought about it until now, but Lady Blackbird really would make for a nice swashbuckling game. The system itself is tight and lends itself to cinematic narration and action. The tags, keys and secrets are a nice fit as well.

    Feels like you could whip up character cards for all the main characters in The Three Musketeers (for example) pretty easily.

    Snargle is the new Porthos!
