    The things I've learned from people here have deeply shaped me as a designer and player. A few notable cases:

    - I've learned that games can be pseudo-anthropological descriptions of non-existant cultures (from Shreyas Sampat, cf. every game he's ever written).
    - I've learned that rules do matter, but not as much as your working relationship with your fellow players (from the Western Mass Crew: Adventures in Improvised System).
    - I've learned that roleplaying can handle genres that are non-narrative, or narrative in non-traditional ways (from Neel Krishnaswami, Lexicon).
    - I've learned that gender politics and issues of social empowerment make themselves known in rules, not just in setting and character (from Mo Turkington, Push vs. Pull).
    - I've learned that play is made more awesome by listening to your fellow players and supporting them (from Selene Tan, Adventures in Ritual Language).
    - I've learned that roleplaying is ultimately about the relationship between the players, projected onto the relationship between the characters or other game content (from Emily Care Boss, cf. every game she's ever written).
    - I've learned that, together, we can create beautiful & terrible things that would have been impossible on our own (from all the people who worked on Push vol 1; and from Thomas Robertson, Shreyas Sampat, & Selene Tan's work on the Four Nations project).
    - I've learned that being able to communicate with your audience clearly and powerfully is worth its weight in gold (from Vincent Baker, cf. Dogs in the Vineyard, Theory: Hardcore, anyway)
    - I've learned that being in love with what you're doing and putting your whole heart into your work is pure, unadulterated awesome (from Luke Crane, cf. seeing the dude in action)
    - I've learned that a guy with a cool idea can make a community devoted to producing more cool ideas (from Clinton R. Nixon, Ron Edwards, & Andy Kitkowski cf. the Forge and Story Games).

    Holy crap, I hardly know where to begin. I think I'm going to avoid the big names. Mostly because I think that most everyone here knows that Ron, Vincent, and Luke, and a host of other big thinkers have taught us all quite a bit, and I think everyone really knows where to find that stuff. So, lemmie focus on a short list of the most important things that I've learned from people that you may not have expected.

    • I've learned from Jason Morningstar how to enjoy a GM-less game about copy repairmen.
    • I've learned from Remi Treuer how to make someone else awesome by stabbing myself in the eye. And how to love it.
    • I've learned from all the awesome folks who came by my tables at conventions just how to refine a game presentation into just a few minutes time, how to really get right to the point of awesome every time, and how rediculously cool it can be to play a story game with half a dozen people you've never met before in your life.
