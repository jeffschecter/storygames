\n Jason asked me why I didn't put a random encounter table into Play Unsafe. I think he was joking, but, irritatingly, I also think he's right. So I'm thinking of tables, which I'll put on the web as a supplement to the book. Can you help with them?

Firstly, I've got a random status change table. When you think a scene needs something, roll on the table:

1. Lower your status, suddenly (break down, cry, beg).
2. Lower their status, suddenly (humiliate them, hit them).
3. Lower your status, gradually, as the scene plays out.
4. Raise your status, suddenly.
5. Raise their status, suddenly.
6. Raise their status, gradually, as the scene plays out.

Secondly, here's a random emotion change table. Again, when you think a scene needs something, roll on the table. Act out the emotion immediately and justify it later.

1. Envy
2. Sadness
3. Fright
4. Anger
5. Happiness
6. Confidence

So...what I need from you guys...

Firstly, what other tables should I have?

Secondly, I'd like a general "random element" table. It'll contain random things to put in your scene and justify. For example:

1. A letter
2. A weapon
3. A body

...and so on. Can you complete the table for me? What else can I have in there? Bear in mind that, as far as is possible, I'd like it to be non-period-specific.

Any help appreciated, as always.
Graham

    \n Write down all of the player's traits, skills, whatever; and then assign each one a number. There's your table, and whatever you roll you simply emphasize that ability in the scene or location, no matter what. If it's Rock-climbing, there better be something to climb or if it's Sweet-talk there should be someone to influence.

    \n Being able to suggest tables/entires depends for me on "how they are to be used".

    For example, some of these (the status one in particular at start) would combine well with/into the Universal NCP Emulator
    http://conjecturegames.110mb.com/index.htm

    And generally work well with Mythic GM Emulator tables etc. for solo play.

    Some of the random tables might work well as "cards" (a nod to Ryan S.). E.G., some event (any time, or if anyone thinks action lagging, or if anyone thinks story lagging even if action not) call for a draw from deck, some cards are "this happens" and some are "play the character(s) you control this way (sudden change in emotion)". Some of those are indeed things from Play Unsafe as in "do the obvious". Take something like Green Ronin's "Deck of Many Things", or a Tarot Deck and set various themed meanings to the cards along these lines.

    I think generators add particular value in high-free-form "pick up" games. Vincent B.'s In a Wicked Age's use of generator for situation elements being an example. These events add a spark to be creative from when there isn't a large canon or pre-authored GM story, and prevent every game being about everyone's "favourite tropes" (e.g., it's always robots, or it's always corrupt power themes).

    Posted By: jessecoombsWrite down all of the player's traits, skills, whatever; and then assign each one a number. There's your table, and whatever you roll you simply emphasize that ability in the scene or location, no matter what.
    \nBrilliant... far better than a "character-agnostic" situation generator, to me. Pinging character traits and abilities is the point, right? Otherwise, you could get some fancy, intriguing situation in which the PCs only option is "hit it with sword until it is no longer a problem." Furthermore, this makes all those seemingly pointless "background" abilities suddenly significant (ex: "Profession: Fishing" gets pinged, so somehow, one must have to catch an "important" fish).
