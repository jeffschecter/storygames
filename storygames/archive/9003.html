\n (cross-posted on Facebook)

I hate naming things. I have a habit of being lazy, giving something a working title, and then never finishing the project because I'd have to give it a title. Even in art school, I never really NAMED my pieces, so much as described them. I did come up with a pretty good title for Thou Art But A Warrior, but frankly I think it exhausted all of my titling mojo for about the next ten years.

So here's the deal. I'm working on writing the alpha draft of a Firefly game. I managed to fix the conflict system and am now totally motivated to finish the damn alpha draft, except the lack of a name is KILLING ME.

See, right now I'm calling it Into The Black. Which I really, really like, but is really, really unusable. If you're going to file the serial numbers off of a TV show for a game, it doesn't do to use a lyric from the show's theme as your title. So help me, Story-Games-kenobi! You're my only hope!

If YOU were writing a game that did character-driven Firefly-style drama, what would YOU call it? And remember, the goal is NOT copyright infringey.

Suggestions?

    \n I'd suggest reading a lot of American Civil War correspondence (I'd suggest this in general, because it is awesome). A lot of the diction and meter in the show seems strongly inspired by the colloquialisms of the mid-19th century, and you might be able to bend some peculiar turn of phrase to your own ends.

    One of my favorites, for paroled Confederate POWs who agreed to go west and fight Indians rather than die of typhus, was "galvanized Yankees".

    I just love the way even common folk wrote: "We left the beautiful village of Green Ville and its citizens about 3 o'clock p. m. to meditate on the vicissitudes of war and the penalties of treason."

    "O, how exhilarating to the spirits and how romantic the thought to be almost constantly in the saddle for 48 hours at a time, without sleep, to ride all day under a burning sun the heat at 90 or 100 degrees and by the aid of foraging and jayhawking make three days rations answer for six, and be shot at in the bargain! "

    Excuse the prolixity of this letter and believe me,

    Yours Respectfully,

    MORNINGSTAR

    \n Oh hey, another cool Civil War era slang term is Jayhawk. Most people think of vicious pro-Union thugs in Kansas, but any under-the-radar guerrilla bands were called Jayhawkers, and living off pillage (rather than forage) was jayhawking no matter who did it. So you know, Browncoats, Jayhawks, &c &c.
