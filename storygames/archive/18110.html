\nReady! Fight! is a roleplaying game with a single purpose: to replicate the experience of side-scrolling fighting games. It\u2019s meant to be generic, not adhering to any particular game, and giving just enough information for house rules to expand on any one element to recreate your favorite game.

What\u2019s more, it can be run with or without a GM.

This game was talked about last summer in another thread. Erik Battle was kind enough to lend his art talents to the project, and I was looking for someone to do the layout, since I'm not quite as good at that as I thought I was. This was meant to be a free project from the beginning, however, and if anyone is interested in doing some layout work for the credit/fun of it all, I'll add you to the credits.

You can download the draft here.

Enjoy!

    \n Tells you under rolling, though you do have to read into it a bit. And not featuring some form of initiative was a glaring oversight.

    On the very first action, both players allocate their dice. Then, lets add a rule: roll your attack dice against your opponent's to see who goes first in the bout.

    Whoever goes first rolls their attack dice (having selected a technique first). Defender rolls as well (if they have any dice). Once the action has resolved, move to the other player's action.

    Thanks for pointing that out. I'll have to remember to include outlines for taking action in future projects.

    \n Ryan,

    Have you seen Seth's game 'Showdown'? It's more story game than your project, but the way you pick sets of dice secretly to determine what happens is pretty cool, and something like that could work well for this game.

    For instance, is there a way to indicate your choice of actions/maneuvers by placing something in your closed fist, or on the table, covered by your hand?

    Then both players can simultaneously reveal their choices, without having any option to change their strategy. (Although maybe some special move or maneuver would allow them to under certain circumstances.)

    For instance, you could palm all your "attack dice" in your left hand, and your "defense dice" in your right, and then reveal at the same time as your opponent.
