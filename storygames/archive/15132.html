\n So over in this thread, Marshall Miller suggested a move I really liked, but thought could be improved. Here is my version:
\n
\n
\nWhen your move directly relates to your Bond, you may roll+Bond instead of the normal stat. If you do:
\nOn a 10+, you and the target of that Bond gain XP. On a 7-9, you gain XP. On a 6-, you may increase or decrease your Bond with them by 1.
\nThese effects are in addition to the normal effects of the move.
\n
\nWhat do you guys think? Does this add anything? Is it basically something you've already been doing? Do I ask too many questions? How many licks DOES it take...?

    \n I like the idea of tying Bonds to XP, but this move adds extra fiddliness without bringing things back to the fiction.

    What I mean is, in Apocalypse World, moves start in the fiction and then, with their outcomes, place you back into the fiction. With this, you start from the fiction but don't go back into the fiction (you do go back into the fiction from the results of the regular move you're layering the Bond move on top of, but then it feels like the Bond move is a little toothless: there's no hard-wired fictional outcome from playing to your Bonds).

    Taking this idea further, maybe each Bond has a reinforce move and a buyoff move, so something happens when you fictionally reinforce your Bond, and something big happens when you buy off your Bond (i.e., doing something to sever ties with your Bond or change the nature of your Bond completely).

    Playing AW, I've often wished the Hx questions had more tooth in the game long-term, but thinking about it again, I'm not sure if it needs it: relationships change naturally through play, using the moves you already have. I've only played DW once, though, so maybe it's different here?

This move is about incentivizing the style of play I'd like to see, which is one where the Bonds mean something concrete to the players, and therefore come up in the fiction frequently.     \n My thinking is: The bonds are good for establishing some initial relationships but after that the text of the bonds don't have mechanical support. They simply describe why you have a fluctuating ability to aid or interfere with a given PC. That's a shame because they're evocative and take the place of back-stories while still giving the players some agency in the matter of their character's history by allowing them to choose which bonds they want and with whom.

    A move that supports the text of the bonds with XP would reward you for playing to them but, A) the demands of the session will probably make them about as frequent as XP for alignment, the text of the bond will stagnate quickly because they relate to the past rather than the present/future, and C) they do not start out written as fiction-based triggers (thus relying on the GM to affirm that you legitimately hit your bond).

    Taking these issues into account, you would probably need an additional move to allow you to change them periodically and/or with the fiction. I think it would be tempting to rewrite bond text similar to Beliefs/Keys (from BW and TSoY/LB, respectively) so that they did have fictional triggers; however, that might clash with the mechanical purpose of bonds which describe your ability to aid or interfere with a specific PC. Bonds-as-keys could work (Alignments are basically keys) but they would not always explain why you were better or worse at aiding or interfering with that person.

    It also seems possible to have a textless bond with another player, one where that player has said at the end of a session that your character knows them better but for which you did not choose a bond at the beginning of the game. You mark +1 bond but there is no text. Perhaps the best solution is to simply rename bonds "relationships" and have the relationship give you the bonus to your bond with that PC. That way the relationships get entered into the fiction but are separated from the mechanics (if that makes any sense). Sorry if it seems like I'm rambling.
