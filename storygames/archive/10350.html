Dear Vancouver,

Lately, you've been hurting for a public, low-key, drop-in story gaming event. I've decided to make one for you. It's called Story Games Thursday, and I'm inviting all of your residents to check it out. The idea is that people can show up at Lugz Coffee on Thursdays (~6:30pm), grab some good eats & drinks, meet other people interested in story games, and then sit down and play a game.

The first SGT takes place on September 17th, and it's every Thursday after that. Hopefully lots of your residents (& people from out of town) will stop in on a regular basis.

Love,
Joe.

PS. Unrelatedly, I just wanted to apologize in advance about Riot 2010.

    \n Yah yah yah!

    A quick summary of how the event went down:
    We had a total of 11 people show up to the coffeeshop, arriving between 6:30 and 7:00.

    We played Action Castle as a big group, then broke into two groups around 7:30.

    Adam ran a game of 3:16 Carnage Amongst The Stars (kill the mutants!), and I ran a game of Misery Bubblegum (travelling circus high school!).

    Around 9:00, both games wrapped up and we broke into different groups - I ran Penny For My Thoughts (with 5 players, we got through 2/3 memories for everyone) and Nathan ran Lady Blackbird.

    We wrapped up at 11pm, which cut our Penny For My Thoughts game short (but in a way that was totally okay, because we'd all just finished our second memory).

    The host (Jark) was really enthusiastic about our group, and excited that Lugz Coffee (2525 Main Street) is going to keep hosting the event (every Thursday!)

    All the games were amazingly fun. I met 7 new people (2 of which I knew from the internet already, sort of).

    \n Okay, for those of you who want to do something like this in your town, Things That I Noticed that I Think Made It Work!:
    \n
    \n1. Joe is a really friendly guy!
    \n2. Lugz was really happy to have us. So, location, location, location! The space was fairly quite, their was enough space and tables for all.
    \n3. The games need to be really easy to run and play. Like, to the point that you just need to add people and stir. Dogs is probably too much.
    \n4. The big group game at the start totally made it. It was simple, low-pressure, fun and it got everyone involved.
    \n5. Short games or running the games speedily seemed to work. It was like the speed-dating of gaming. So, maybe have two two hour slots and a pre- and -post half-hour activity planed.
    \n6. The fact that Adam pretty much brought his whole game group and SO was definitely critical in my mind. So, encourage people to bring their friends and regular groups down. Especially non-gamers. See #3
    \n7. Having little fliers to hand to people who are curious is really, really handy. You don't have to stop and talk to them and they can learn about what it's about without having to talk to you, if they're shy or in a hurry.
