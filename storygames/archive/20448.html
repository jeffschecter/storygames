\n I love the way that psychic powers are handled in Apocalypse World. I picked up Dungeon World and I was a little bummed that they decided to go with spell lists instead of more freeform moves like Open Your Brain.

Are there other PBtA games or hacks with magic systems that are closer in spirit to the way Apocalypse World handles psychics?

    \n The Wizard Ritual move is one of my favorite magic implementations:

    "When you draw on a place of power to create a magical effect, tell the GM what you\u2019re trying to achieve. Ritual effects are always possible, but the GM will give you one to four of the following conditions:

    It\u2019s going to take days/weeks/months
    First you must ____
    You\u2019ll need help from ____
    It will require a lot of money
    The best you can do is a lesser version, unreliable and limited
    You and your allies will risk danger from ____
    You\u2019ll have to disenchant ____ to do it"

    Monster of the Week has Cast a Spell as a basic move, and follows a similar format.

    The main drawback of that style of implementation is that they rely on very careful arbitration by the GM.

    \n Here's the text of the make a wish move from Troublemakers, my kids' fiction hack-in-progress:

    When you\u2019re desperate and place all your faith in the unknown to save the day, spend 1 will and roll+dreams. On a hit, good fortune comes your way and you can specify that the very thing you need appears or occurs just when you need it; on a 10+, choose one, on a 7-9, choose two:
    \u2022\tThis draws the attention of witnesses who weren\u2019t supposed to see this.
    \u2022\tThe power you were calling on is used up; neither you nor the other kids can do this again until that power is restored.
    \u2022\tThere\u2019s a price to be paid and you lose something unexpected.
    \u2022\tYou let something loose that is not supposed to be here.
    \u2022\tThis really takes it out of you: you pass out and can only be brought around by an adult.

    Lizzie grips the lucky charm pendant around her neck and wishes the shadow creatures to go away; on a hit, the charm shines with brilliant white light and the shadows melt away, but on a 10+, Lizzie\u2019s own shadow also fades out; how will she explain that to her family and can she get it back? On a 7-9, as well as losing her shadow, the pendant itself breaks and must be repaired before it can be used to drive back the shadow creatures again.

    Whenever you get a hit on this move and were focusing your wish on or through a specific object, you can make a charm if you spend 1 more will: that object now becomes a charm that has that specific power and can be used to have this same effect again, indefinitely, without spending will.

    You can spend 1 will after rolling to make a wish, in order to add +1 to your total, just as with any other move that requires a roll.



    And here's some explanatory text from the MC's chapter:

    When a kid makes a wish, they are acknowledging their own desperation and faith: they are giving up on solving their own problems, instead relying on something invisible & intangible to save the day for them. Making a wish is something that should never be done lightly:
    \u2022\tMost kids have dreams as one of their lowest stats, so if their problem can be solved by pulling a stunt, getting a clue, telling a lie, etc, then they have more chance of succeeding that way.
    \u2022\tYou have to pay the fee of 1 will: if you have no will, then you can\u2019t make a wish.
    \u2022\tThere are always consequences when you make a wish and they are often very serious: it might sometimes be better to miss on any other move than to get a 7-9 result on making a wish.
    If, even after taking all this into consideration, you still want to make a wish, roll+dreams but be clear on what you\u2019re getting: a wish doesn\u2019t change reality, it just makes things happen in the here and now, often in a way that seems almost plausible in hindsight.

    Nicky is cornered by the escaped prisoners in a dead-end shaft, deep in the old abandoned mine where they stashed their loot before they were caught: there\u2019s a rock wall behind her and three tough, desperate thugs in front of her. Nicky is good in a scuffle, but three-against-one isn\u2019t good odds; as they close in on her, she crouches down into a ball, whispering an old, half-remembered nursery rhyme in the dark, a memory of her grandmother...

    Nicky\u2019s player spends 1 will, announcing her intent to make a wish and rolls+dreams, getting a final total of 8: it\u2019s a hit, but there will be two consequences. The MC narrates how the crumbling rock of the shaft suddenly collapses, dropping a pile of rubble between Nicky and the convicts, while simultaneously the floor opens up underneath her and she lands in an underground stream with a huge splash. Nicky says that she passes out as the currents sweep her away, so it will need an adult to rescue her and bring her back to consciousness; also, she loses her grip on her precious flick knife, which sinks like a stone, so she will be without it for the rest of this session.

    \n This is a special move from the Gadgeteer origin from Just Heroes:

    You are always ready to try out some new Experiment which will push back the boundaries of knowledge: when you build a large apparatus to produce any effect you can imagine (including giving a subject the equivalent of any style move, permanently), roll+genius. On a hit, it works as you predicted it would; on a 10+, choose one; on a 7-9, choose two:
    - It drains power: the city is blacked out whenever you use it.
    - It unleashes strange energies: a powerful new supervillain is created as a result.
    - It rips open space-time: the city is invaded by weird beings.
    - It has terrible side-effects: anyone who uses it gains an affliction of the MC\u2019s choice.
    - It only works once: that must have been a fluke, because you can never do this again.

    On a miss, it fails to work as expected but you still choose two from the options above.

    If you advance this move, on a 12+, choose none.

    \n The third-party Mage class for Dungeon World is a non-Vancian caster with fictional limitations on the kinds of effects you can produce, based on different "spell foci" chosen by the player.

    It's part of the Alternate Playbooks collection, but it's offered individually for free: http://www.drivethrurpg.com/product/108623/Dungeon-World-Alternative-Playbooks

    Worth noting is that the creator thinks it's unbalanced. In his words:\n
    The Mage is one of the most popular playbooks I have ever written, but it has its flaws - the class is too open ended, and is generally capable of solving problems on a larger scope than any other class can really confront. My solution was to pare down the class into multiple different classes, each built around a single Spell Focus. http://www.drivethrurpg.com/product/127049/Dungeon-World-Mage-Bundle-BUNDLE?src=list_view

    \n A magic thing I've been playing with, sort of an expansion of the DW Ritual move.

    Mystic art
    You practice an eldritch craft. Name the source of your power: (choose one and describe it)
    -a dark bargain
    -the wisdom of the ancients
    -communion with a higher power
    -the land and her spirits
    -the tutelage of a great magician

    The practice of your craft requires... (choose two methods)
    ...the speaking of incantations
    ...the weaving of arcane hand signs
    ...the aid of a mystic fetish like a staff, ring or book
    ...knowledge of a thing\u2019s true name
    ...the drawing of runes
    ...the aid of a skilled apprentice

    Your craft is concerned with\u2026 (choose two spheres)
    ...things that grow from the earth
    ...the minds of living things
    ...the working of weather
    ...the speaking of prophecy
    ...the bestowing of curses
    ...the mending of wounds
    ...the construction of wards
    ...the transmutation of stone and mineral
    ...the calling of spirits (of nature, of the dead, from the seven hells, from the weird beyond)

    When you construct a magical ritual related to one of your spheres, tell the GM what you wish to achieve. Simple, subtle magic should be easily accomplished with standard methods. For more ambitious acts of craft, the GM may demand any of the following:

    -First you must ____
    -It will take days/weeks/months
    -It will require the use of one or more additional methods
    -It will require one or more exotic reagents
    -The best you can do is a lesser version
    -You will risk danger from _____

    \n First of all, the explanation: Dungeon World uses spell lists because Dungeon World is EXPLICITLY emulating D&D. That's why it has other nonsense like 3-18 ability scores too. D&D has spell lists, therefore, Dungeon World has spell lists.

    For a game that doesn't, Monster of the Week uses a semi-freeform magic system, with a completely freeform "Big Magic" system stacked on top of it. Here's the whole thing (I assume that since this is in a publicly available PDF, I'm not breaking any rules by posting it here):

    Use Magic
    When you use magic, say what you\u2019re trying to achieve and how you do the spell, then roll +Weird. On a 10+, the magic works without issues: choose your effect. On a 7-9, it works imperfectly: choose your effect and a glitch. The Keeper will decide what effect the glitch has. advanced: On a 12+ the Keeper will offer you some added benefit.

    Effects
    \u2022 Inflict harm (1-harm ignore-armour magic obvious).
    \u2022 Enchant a weapon. It gets +1 harm and +magic.
    \u2022 Do one thing that is beyond human limitations.
    \u2022 Bar a place or portal to a specific person or a type of creature.
    \u2022 Trap a specific person, minion, or monster.
    \u2022 Banish a spirit or curse from the person, object, or place it inhabits.
    \u2022 Summon a monster into the world.
    \u2022 Communicate with something that you do not share a language with.
    \u2022 Observe another place or time.
    \u2022 Heal 1-harm from an injury, or cure a disease, or neutralize a poison.

    Glitches
    \u2022 The effect is weakened.
    \u2022 The effect is of short duration.
    \u2022 You take 1-harm ignore-armour.
    \u2022 The magic draws immediate, unwelcome attention.

    The Keeper may say that...
    \u2022 The spell requires weird materials.
    \u2022 The spell will take 10 seconds, 30 seconds, or 1 minute to cast.
    \u2022 The spell requires ritual chanting and gestures.
    \u2022 The spell requires you to draw arcane symbols.
    \u2022 You need one or two people to help cast the spell.
    \u2022 You need to refer to a tome of magic for the details.

    Big Magic
    Use this when you want more than the Use Magic effects. Tell the Keeper what you want to do.

    The Keeper may require:
    \u2022 You need to spend a lot of time (days or weeks) researching the magic ritual.
    \u2022 You need to experiment with the spell \u2013 there will be lots of failures before you get it right.
    \u2022 You need some rare and weird ingredients and supplies.
    \u2022 The spell will take a long time (hours or days) to cast.
    \u2022 You need a lot of people (2, 3, 7, 13, or more) to help.
    \u2022 The spell needs to be cast at a particular place and/or time.
    \u2022 You need to use magic as part of the ritual, perhaps to summon a monster, communicate with something, or bar the portal you opened.
    \u2022 It will have a specific side-effect or danger.
    If you meet the requirements, then the magic takes effect.
