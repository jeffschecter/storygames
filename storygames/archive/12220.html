    \n Hee! A Spooky Stories/Breaking The Ice mashup would work well with Josh Roby's suggestion of "how we first met" as the theme. Okay, this is sloppy and inelegant, but here's a rough idea:\n
    A game for any number of players.
    Players tell the story of how a couple first met.
    The "bride" and "groom" are the central characters of the story, but no player has ownership of either one.
    At the end of the game, you'll discover whether the bride or the groom pops the question.

    Prep:
    Players each write a noun, an adjective and a verb on separate slips of paper.
    Those slips are gathered in a pile in the middle of the table.

    Play:
    Each turn, two lines of the story are told. One line focuses on the bride's actions, the other on the groom's actions.
    To begin, the players roll a white die and a groom die, representing the bride and groom respectively.
    The higher die gets the first line of the turn.
    The line may include words drawn from the pile.
    The number of words drawn may not exceed the die result.

    Scoring:
    When a word is used in a story, draw a hashmark on that slip of paper.
    Put the slip of paper back into the pile.
    Whoever draws the third hashmark on a word gets to keep it and removes it from the pile permanently.
    Over time, the bride and group start gathering lots of slips of paper.

    Victory:
    The game is over when the pile is empty.
    Whoever has the most slips of paper pops the question.
    \nA lot of this could be streamlined, trimmed down.

    \n Worked on a prototype during lunch today. Got a chinese take out box design roughed out to be printable on 8.5x11 cardstock, with the board printed on the inside of the box.

    The box is tiny when folded, 1.25"x1.25" at the base, 1.75"x1.75" at the top and 2" tall. When open and laying flat, the printed board is 4.5"x4.5" - though it could have been a fraction larger, with individual squares a little over 1/2" wide. I didn't do a traditional checkerboard pattern. Instead, I colored the corner squares a dark color (in this case red) and the five Maid squares at each corner colored a lighter hue.

    At this size, for the elegance required for a wedding favor, the box is too small to print the rules on or in the box; though if I were to make a version only for casual gifting, then I'd consider printing the rules on the box's outside.

    The challenge now is to find suitable playing pieces. I'm thinking small wood craft pieces could work; that would allow me to stain them using nice colors.

    Once I get a proper prototype fabricated, I'll post pics. Right now, all I have is the box in B&W on orange file folder cardstock (it's all I had on hand).

    \n As promised, here are pics of the Procession wedding favor prototype I cobbled up.

    A few details about its construction:

    The takeout box is printed on 110lb cardstock.
    The maids are 3/8" wood screwhole buttons.
    The brides are made with a 1" shaker peg (with the dowel end trimmed off) glued to the top of an inverted wood flower pot (3/4' Ttall I think).
    All the pieces are painted with typical craft acrylic paint. The buttons were dip painted in diluted paint for more even coat.
