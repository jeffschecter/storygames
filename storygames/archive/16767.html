\n So I finally had the extra cash to pick up the marvel book. I'm in the process of producing my own box set for the game and wanted to make the best use of those slick data files that really have no purpose looking so good considering they are not functional table components! The big problem is the heroes can advance and then players have to use an ugly pen and paper sheet.

To that I say HELL NO! not good enough! Go directly to jail, do not pass go, do not collect 200! Quoting monopoly cards is as close as I come to cursing.

Think about your earliest memories of accomplishing something in a structured environment. you got something back then, thats right, you got a sticker! When you spend exp you should get a sticker!

papercrafted.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/MHR-advancement-stickers.pdf

So I made these classy stickers that cover about 80-90% of advancement options. the idea is that each little box will perfectly cover over a thing on the sheet or fit snugly in the wide margin on the left side of the sheet.

But I have yet to actually play this game so i want to ask experienced players, what options am i missing? what options are most popular and need more stickers? what options need fewer? Thanks!

    \n It's an interesting idea, but I'm not sure if its practical. Those are a lot of stickers that would have to be cut out, and they're pretty small, too. In particular, the stickers for new Limits, SFXs, and Specialties are probably too small to write in the new details (primarily the Limits and SFX, though, since those can be a couple sentences, as opposed to a few words).

    I will admit that I, too, find the datafiles to be slick-looking, but limited in function, with a lot of wasted space on the left of the sheet, and no real room to modify things.
    I've been slowly tinkering with my own layout for a datafile in InDesign (though it's taken the back-burner in the last couple of weeks, along with my Mighty Marvel Watcher Screen...), and I was planning on painstakingly retyping all of the current and future datafiles, leaving space for additions, advancements, etc. Maybe once this month is over, I'll be able to get back on that again.
