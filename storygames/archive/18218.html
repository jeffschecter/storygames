\n So I created a game called ViewScream, which is a varp (a video-augmented role-playing game) -- a low-prep hybrid of story game and larp.

It's made specifically for play via video-chat programs like G+ Hangout. It's about four officers (Medical, Bridge, Weapons, and Engineering) aboard a damaged starship, in great peril. They're cut off from each other, and can only communicate via viewscreens throughout the ship.

It usually ends badly!

Features nine adventures, including Cancel Christmas by Jason Morningstar, Vermilion Letters by Jack Shear, and War Room by Kerra Bolton.

    \n Yeah, exactly -- you assign success or failure to your solutions before play begins, then you innocently inquire, "Do you want me to try running the pathogen through the biomolecular centrifuge?" -- knowing damn well that this isn't going to work. Or maybe it will! Depends on what you chose.

    Cancel Christmas is the only one that's played on a timer -- it's exactly 1 hour.

    The other 8 adventures require video, and there's no timer (but most sessions last about 90 minutes).
