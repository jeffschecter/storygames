    \n I'm gonna go a bit out on a limb and suggest a few artists that you might not think connect to Sword and Sorcery...

    well... me. Check out Catspaw here: Catspaw

    It might be a bit more romaticized S&S.... but I love the genre and this is my modern take on it.

    Virgil Findlay is a B&W artist of the 30s and 40s...a google should bring up plenty. A bit dated. But fun.

    Neil Adams Tarzan covers for Ballantine are quite good.

    Hal Foster's Prince Valiant... a bit of a surprise... but his artwork is so good, so evocative, even if it is a bit squeeky clean.

    Barry Windsor Smith's run on Conan in the early 70s. His backgrounds are worth the price of the Dark Horse Graphic Novel reprints alone. His Freebooter's is really quite good too!!!

    Michael Whelan's Elric covers for Daw certainly nail down both Sword and SORCERY almost better than anyone.

    Brom's Dark Sun stuff. Leather fetishism to the max.
