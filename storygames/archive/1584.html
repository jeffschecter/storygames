I was just browsing through some old bookmarks of photography sites and the like, and as usual many of the more evocative images turned my thoughts in the direction of roleplaying -- whether as settings, characters, or whatever. I'm sure I'm not the only gamer who has found these sorts of resources useful, so I thought I'd start up a thread about useful (and ideally online) reference sites. For me, I mostly look to references for help with colour and setting, but of course there are all sorts of sites out there that could serve as inspiration for different aspects of the gaming experience. Actual gaming reference sites are cool too, but the more obscure the better -- I'm hoping we can all come away from this a little bookmark-richer.

Anyways, here's a few:

Modern Ruins - Photographic Essays by Shaun O'Boyle - This is the site that prompted this post. I found it ages ago but it had since escaped my mind. A collection of colour and black-and-white photographs of modern ruins: abandoned asylums, steel factories, hospitals and trainyards, any of which would make fantastic material for a straightforward ghost story, or something far beyond.

LeastWanted photostream on Flickr.com - In my opinion, the best single gallery Flickr has to offer. An extremely extensive collection of mugshots from throughout the US, most between the turn of the century and the late 1970s. The context adds instant pathos, and it turns out that when photographers spend every day taking portraits of people, they get pretty damn good at it.

They Fight Crime! - I think I linked an uglier version of this on another thread at some point. A random character-concept generator that creates punchy, pulpy crime-fighting duos. Endlessly suggestive. At some point I had a plan to write rules for generating Feng Shui characters based on They Fight Crime descriptors, but it never quite came together.
