Hey, folks! Like last year, I'll be running the Indie Bazaar at Dreamation. Let's talk about what it is and how it works!

\n
    \n
  1. You bring a game you've made. It can be in any form, so long as, I dunno, it doesn't take up the whole table or something else antisocial.
    2. \n
  2. You tell us how much it costs for people to buy.
    3. \n
  3. You explain about your game to everyone else participating in the Bazaar. You'll be best at pitching it, but everyone should know what it is.
    4. \n
  4. You listen to everyone else's pitch for their game so you can return the favor.
    5. \n
  5. You hang out in the booth for a couple of hours a day, telling people about games (especially yours), and sell games.
    6. \n
  6. You help us cash out at the end of the day before dinner, when you get 100% of your take.
    7. \n
\n

I'm going to bring the big black tablecloth and the bookstands that I made to Jason Morningstar's spec last year. Those things are really great. Jason, if you're coming, please bring them again!

\n

I'll also have my Square, which we'll use as a cash register with my iPad. If you have a Square, bring it, too. Redundancy has bailed us out of more than one weirdness.

\n

If you sell stuff through IPR, please don't forget to do Darren the courtesy of telling him that you're bringing your games so he doesn't have to carry stuff that you'll be selling anyway!

\n

I'm looking forward to seeing what you guys are bringing this year!

    \n Hey, everyone!

    I have gotten in touch with IPR to rep them officially at several East Coast cons, including the Dreamation complex, and hopefully Pax and maybe the Comic Cons, depending on how this trial run works. I am an Indie Press fan of long standing as well, so I'm looking forward to attending my first Deamation. Anyone who wants us to carry their stuff via Modern Myths, AND/or put it into IPR after the show, please feel free to drop me a line at modern.myths.ny at gmail and we'll talk about the details. Hopefully some of the MA self-publishers we deal with (including Josh!) will be along to confirm that we're reasonable people to deal with. : )

    This is all somewhat last-minute and still coming together, but I have solid commitments from both IPR and just got an email from Dreamation that we're good to go. I'm very excited to be attending!

    Per a discussion we had last year, I will look into what's needed to make sure we're all compliant with the applicable New Jersey sales tax regulations. It may be that small-volume sales like you guys are doing fall under a minimum, but since I need to find out about being a con vendor as an out-of-state business, I will let you all know!

    This is especially exciting for me, because it's the first semi-official bit of business for the new shop we're in the planning stages of here in Westchester, just north of NYC. More details as I confirm some numbers.

    Thanks, Joshua!

    -Jim Crocker, President
    Modern Myths, Inc.
    www.modern-myths.com
