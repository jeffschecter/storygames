    And how! Check out this message from the management guy I made contact with earlier in this process:

    http://drivingblind.livejournal.com/194436.html

    The point of all of this is pretty simple math, but it's good to have it laid out:

    * Form relationships with people the company, and don't blame the people when shit goes wrong.
    * Always budget twice as much time as you think it will take. Something will come along that eats up that time.
    * It's not whether or not printing problems will arise, it's how extensive they'll be.
    * When reporting a problem, lead in with as extensive a detailing of the problem as possible. This means that the first contact you make will give rise to as complete a solution as possible, as rapidly as possible.
    * Don't panic. This is perfectly normal.

    Anecdotally, I believe all of those points to be true pretty much no matter the company you're dealing with.
