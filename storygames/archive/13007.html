\n In response to David Pigeon's design challenge from this thread, I have created Warm and Fuzzy, a story game for the whole family.

In fact, I designed it with younger kids and their parents in mind. With Warm and Fuzzy, you can create stories similar to those you might find in kids' books aimed at toddlers or beginning readers.

The mechanics are very simple. Players pick and roll Story Blocks -customized dice with story elements on them- and narrate short sentences including those rolled elements. It's just about that easy.

The most complicated part of the game, right now, is the bit of DIY needed to create the Story Blocks - the custom dice used during play. Though I do provide two different methods for creating them.

Now here's where the DIY can become worth while. I took some inspiration from Fiasco when I designed the Story Blocks concept for Warm and Fuzzy. The Story Blocks sets are themed and modular. This means that you can create vastly different stories simply by using a different set of Story Blocks. You can even create your own Story Blocks theme sets.

The game is still unplaytested, but I hope to remedy that personally soon; and you can help out by playing the game and posting your stories and comments about the game.
