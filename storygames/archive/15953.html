\n I'm going out on a limb and guessing it's ok to introduce myself here. My name's Joachim, or just Joe. I've been into tabletop RPGs since I was 16. I was interested in them as early as 12, but it was all rather foreign and beyond me at that point, and I didn't know anybody else who could guide me into the scene, so I had to make do with computer games and playing pretend (which is, really, the purest form of RPG, some might say).
\n
\nI've also been into music my whole life, starting on the drums at the age of 6, and progressing to piano and guitar, and for the past 6 years, electronic music performance and production has been my thing. So it was a natural step for me to combine the two big creative impulses in my life, with this result: http://soundcloud.com/rpgambience So if anybody is interested in some music or location soundbeds, or really anything audio, feel free to drop me a line! I work for free, as a token of gratitude and good faith to those online communities that have supported (and continue to support) me in my life as a gamer. Also, it's just something I love doing.
\n
\nSystems I've played or tried out are: WoD, AD&D, Kobolds Ate My Baby, Conan, and D20 Modern. I've also done a ton of freeform play-by-post writing, and that's really what turned me into a tabletop gamer. I've read a ton of pdfs and books for other games, but haven't managed to get a group in gear for them yet. Things on the to-do list are: ORE, Swashbuckler, Heavy Gear, Risus, Everway, The Pool, and many more.
\n
\nI've also been working on my own little truculent ogre of a homebrew project for the past year and a half ish. You can find some scruffy rough drafts and half-ideas up here: http://rubbermancer.wordpress.com/the-broken-realms/
\n
\nAaaand that's me in a nutshell!

    \nJenskot, a location soundbed (or "soundscape") is an audio track of ambiance, designed to simulate the sound of a place, real or imagined. It's great having the sound of an in-world area on the speakers in the background at the gaming table.
