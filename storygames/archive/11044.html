\n Heya all,

I'm discontinuing Death's Door. It's had a good long run, and I've gotten what I wanted out of it as a designer and a publisher. Rather than carry the stock as a cost into another fiscal year, I'm selling off what's left, and if it's not gone by Dec 31, it's getting destroyed.

Which is to say: If you want it, get it now, because you'll not otherwise find it.

It's been marked down to $8.00 USD everywhere I sell it. IPR has about a dozen left, and I have a few at Blank Shield Press as well. I'd appreciate it if you go through IPR by preference; Ideally that stock will just sell out, and they won't need to ship anything back to me.


thanks,
James

    \n Moreno and Hans: the reasons for it not being in a .pdf or on Lulu are basically the same: I'm very happy with Death's Door as a game that I wrote and published five years ago. If I were to republish it in any form, I wouldn't be happy with it as a game that I am publishing today. The market is different, I'm different.

    I'm still somewhat amazed by how solidly I was able to nail my design goals for presentation and format, but even at the time, I recognized that I was deliberately presenting something that did not appeal to a typical RPG aesthetic, indie or otherwise. Trying to release it now without significantly reworking the layout and presentation would be like continuing to try selling vaccuum tube radios when everyone else has moved to (and past) the transistor.

    And I don't have the time to rework it for today's market.


    thanks,
    James

    \n Jesse, immortality is free for the taking when I'm dead: all my writing is being explicitly released into the public domain in my will.

    In the meantime, I could just take the existing game, .pdf it and release it - but it would be frakkin' huge, because the image files are optimized for printing, not download, and the format would be crap for reading on a screen. So I'd have to rework it to make it a .pdf that I'd be willing to attach my name as a publisher to.

    I've no particular objection to .pdf release - Brick Battles is available as a free .pdf as well as a little booklet. (except that the little booklets are sitting at my shelf at home, instead of available at IPR, for no good reason.)
