    10.\tA fleet of airships, ready for war, manned by the nameless damned.
    11.\tA martial artist, once powerful, blindly seeks vengeance.
    12.\tA dark lord, megalomaniacal, served by fiends of elemental power.
    13.\tA library, the largest on the continent, serving as rebel headquarters.
    14.\tA caravan, thick with many races, famous for its myriad chocobos.
    15.\tA lost ship of legend returns via the prayers of the downtrodden.
    16.\tA brash prince seeks his kidnapped parents at the expense of his people.
    17.\tA forest, dark and old, concealing a pure lake and skittish spirits.
    18.\tA massive train, no longer bound to tracks, takes the willing and the dead.
    19.\tA coliseum, rich with history and gambling, home to gladiators and loners.

    \n FFX and Final Fantasy Legend 3 for the gameboy belong with 6-8.

    Final Fantasy Legend 1, 2, and Final Fantasy Adventure for the gameboy fit with the FF4 group.

    I'd say FF9 is right on the line. Magic has replaced technology but it has a very technology vibe to me (black mages on an assembly line).

    But that said, I wouldn't want to put all in one or the other, because there were a few times that FF6 outdid its predecessors in moments of classic fantasy, and certainly some of the situations were very strong (poisoning of Cyan's castle, the interlude where you can turn Raiden into Odin, and the Esper World come to mind).

    \nUbiquitous
    1) An airship speeds through the sky, its captain charged with a dangerous mission.
    2) An isolated community of mages finds itself besieged by forces from the outside world.
    3) Plumbing ancient ruins, explorers find a powerful relic of an advanced society.
    4) A throng of monsters are released from their prison, terrorizing the countryside.
    5) At the bridge, the party makes their stand.
    6) An earthquake reopens the path to a long-hidden land.
    7) A daring rescue is already underway.
    8) A crystal, conduit of great magic, is stolen from its rightful keeper.
    9) A vast and lonely tower, sealed for generations, is breached.
    10) The path to escape leads over the waterfall.
    11) Dark acolytes vie for the confidence of their great master.
    12) A dragon guards the pass.
    13) Monsters await beneath the ruins.

    1 was inspired by the opening of FF4 but plays on pretty much every end sequence. 2 is from the same idea but it's also a recurring theme. I'm getting 3 from the fact that ancient civilizations are always more advanced than modern ones. 4 is from FF6 where the portal opens, or where the earth revolts against what's happened and releases the 8 dragons, or the backstory of FF1. 5 is inspired by the scene at the drawbridge in FF4. I'm practically certain I remember 6 happening in FF4. 7 happens a lot in FF4. 8 happens a lot in FF4. 9 is from FF1. 10 is from FF4 (octopus fight) and FF6 (escape from enemy camp). 11 is from FF4 where the fiends are trying to be Golbez's favorite and FF6 where the generals are trying to influence Gestahl. 12 is FF4 and right here I think writing sources is too boring but I might ask about some of them from others later.

    Resistance
    1) An empire marches on, its troops conquering city after city.
    2) A resistance movement strikes back against the conquerers, lead by a young woman thrust to the forefront by fate.
    3) Those in power are not what they seem - they have been replaced by dopplegangers and fill the ranks with their minions.
    4) Beneath the city, sewers provide a conduit into the enemy castle.
    5) A madman has seized great magical power and rules through fear.
    6) The king's strongest knight turns away from his lord, joining the forces that oppose him.
    7) New allies are discovered, though they too are beseiged.
    8) The plan has already been betrayed to the enemy.
    9) A hero is imprisoned and beaten for choosing the right path.

    Technology and the Balance
    1) Great spirits are forced into servitude by strange, new magic.
    2) The forces of nature strike back against the defilers.
    3) A cosmic event presages doom, or a new opportunity for the cunning.

    Love
    1) Love and duty are at war in the heart of a powerful warrior.
    2) Kids run through the city corner.
    3) A ghost visits one who yearns, giving crucial advice and bolstering their heart.

    Uncategorized
    1) A tense meeting between two ships - exchange, and treachery.
    1) A poisoned monarch slumbers - the kingdom shudders from attacks without and treachery within.
    2) Ancient technology is awakened, using deadly force in an attempt to save its long-dead masters.
    3) A hypnotized thrall struggles against his bonds, but continues to do his master's bidding and assaults his former friends.
    32) A man discovers his strange heritage and finds himself torn between two worlds.

    \n Hey, I know this is a small point to make, but the Final Fantasy Legend and Final Fantasy Adventure games were actually not a part of the Final Fantasy series. They just had a name change and some small plot alterations to fit in with what was Square's most popular USA series. FFL1-3 was actually the start of the SaGa series(Which is why the first two were so mechanically unique at the time. Weapon degradation in a jrpg?), while FFA was the first game in the Mana series.

    With that said, I'll take off my obscure video game knowledge hat... I think these elements are great, and a lot of them cut to the juicy core of what makes Final Fantasy what it is.

    \n Romancing SaGa 1-3 (the SNES era games) are really good. Absurdly so. You can find a translation for the third one on the net. They're nonlinear jrpgs. After the intro 'quest', you're really just given an open world to explore and quest in at your leisure. There's no obvious "main quest path" that you have to follow like in most such nonlinear games, just lots and lots of optional quests. You can pick up the Romancing SaGa 1 remake for the PS2(Being how unpopular it was, for like 10 bucks). The graphics are offputting though. Realistic graphics combined with superdeformed characters is uncomfortable to look at.

    SaGa Frontier felt rushed to me. It returned to a many-worlds setting like that of FFL, though(Sadly you don't kill god in the end). SaGa Frontier 2 is indeed much better.

    Unlimited Saga was panned by all but like 3 people. I still want to try it, because its main point of comparison was tabletop rpgs.
