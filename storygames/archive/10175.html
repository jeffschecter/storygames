\n It's come to my attention that there is a debate over what constitutes indie, and what the significance of indie is.
Clearly, I can help solve this problem.

Indie is when you write your own game, do your own layout, edit internally, do your own art, create your own imprint, publish your own game, bind your own books, and sell your own product.

There are a lot of diluted, half-indie horrorshows out there. Games which contract out editing services, or that have people contributing writing who will not get a cut of the final sales. Clearly, we need some kind of measure to ensure that games which are actually indie are praised and hoisted above all other games.

An award show is in order, to celebrate true indie status in print media. Enter The Anarcho-Indie Craftsman Awards!

Categories available for nomination:

Best True Indie
To qualify, the game must be:
self written (in its entirety)
self edited (or unedited)
laid out by oneself (or not laid out at all)
containing only your own art (or no art at all)
self published
printed and bound by yourself (either at home, or in a print centre that you gained access to)
sold direct to customers only (or never sold)
no outsourced playtesting

Best Half-Breed Indie
Any game with one of the above missing.

Best Barely Indie
Any game with two of the above missing.

It's totally appropriate to argue that others have nominated something in the wrong category, and justify why that is so.

    \nself written (in its entirety)
    \nself edited (or unedited)
    \nlaid out by oneself (or not laid out at all)
    \ncontaining only your own art (or no art at all)
    \nself published
    \nprinted and bound by yourself (either at home, or in a print centre that you gained access to)
    \nsold direct to customers only (or never sold)
    \nno outsourced playtesting
    \n
    \nBest Half-Breed Indie
    \nAny game with one of the above missing.
    \n
    \nBest Barely Indie
    \nAny game with two of the above missing.
    \n
    \n
    \nWow! By these criteria my games are half indie because I sometimes use an outside proof reader and my Gen Con release (Thunder Hamsters and the Temple of Cheese) barely indie because I paid an artist for original work. Even though I do everything else myself. Does an Indie designer really have to do everything? Seems a bit extreme.
    \n
    \nChris Engle Hamster Press
