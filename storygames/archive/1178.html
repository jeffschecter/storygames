In the Nixon Principle thread I mentioned that I was debating whether to run My Life with Vader or My Life with Sauron at Furnace 06 in October. Vaxalon then commented:
Posted By: VaxalonMy Life with Sauron is just too good. I can just see the Witch-King of Angmar sneaking around Rohan trying to do something nice for Eowyn, and then bango... on the field of Pellinor Sauron tells him, "Go kill King Theoden, and anyone who gets in your way."
... So I think I have little choice but to go ahead with it! I'm wondering how best to handle it though, as I've never ran a convention game of MLWM (although I've ran a couple of short games of MLWM now). Should I make pre-gens for all the players, or just generate Sauron up in advance?

I'm thinking that Sauron would be as follows:

Fear 5, Reason 2-3
Needs: slaves, hobbits, to destroy the kingdoms of men
Wants: the One Ring!
Outsiders: umm ... not sure about this one! Who would Sauron's outsiders be? The wizards? Those gribbly elder gods alluded to in the Silmarillion?

But who could the minions be? I'm thinking the Witch King of Angmar, Saruman, Gollum (indirectly), maybe Shelob (it'd be fun!), the King of Gondor and the Balrog.

Help me make this a con game of epic (if somewhat silly!) proportions!

    That would be the Istari or Wizards, Josh. I think, yes, Saruman, is "the" outsider here. And Gandalf, Radagast and the other two as well. Though only Saruman is likely to fall here, that doesn't mean that Sauron isn't out to prove to the rest that his master, Melkor, had the right idea. That is was unfair of Illuvatar to make Middle Earth and then give it to the elves and men, and not them.

    The "Elder Gods" themselves are unchanging and stuck in the West, so there's nothing to prove to them.

    The wraiths as minions...I dunno. I understand that this is supposed to be somewhat silly, but that seems to be pushing it a bit far. The wraiths have all lost their humanity, that's the point of a wraith.

    Gollum is, of course, the perfect example. Compelled by the ring and it's master, but not yet completely devoid of humanity. Bilbo Baggins, too. Frodo? I think Frodo as a minion has legs. What about the other members of the Fellowship? Boromir, anyone?

    The orc in question is called Gothmog, and it's interesting because he's an ICE creation, IIRC. Named for the most powerful of the Balrogs. Again, these are creatures made by the dark lord, orcs, so their "humanity" was torn from them when Morgoth twisted the elves into being orcs. So... again I think that's pushing it.

    The Mouth of Sauron is a good candidate, I think. Not quite a wraith (though he has lost so much humanity, it's said that even he's forgotten his own name).

    How about Easterlings, Men of Khand, and the men of Umbar? They're guilty of following Sauron of their own will.

    I dunno, I think that the best option would be to run the Fellowship as the minions. But, then I probably am taking this all waaay too seriously.
