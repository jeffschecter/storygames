The discussion on rules for GMs made me think of this.

I think I'd like to write a game in which the GM's preparation for the session/story is like character generation normally is.

I guess Dogs kind of has this already, but it's not quite what I have in mind: I'm looking for more crunchy, more "choose one of these types, get these things to use in the story" kind of stuff.

Sound cool? Anyone got any ideas how to actually do it?

    Mike, I just thought of another game that has pretty structured rules for GM prep, and it actually occurs at the same time as char gen. The game is Fastlane by Alexander Cherry of Twisted Confessions.

    Fastlane does a great job of coming up with game ideas during the combined char gen and GM prep. You should check it out. While it's not quite the "select one of these" that you mention it rifs off of the players selections. When the players spend points on certain aspects, you as a GM get a corresponding amount of points to spend on the setting - mainly NPCs and factions. It is very cool.
