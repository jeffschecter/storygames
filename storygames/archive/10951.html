\n In games, people will set up conflicts for your character. In some cases, there is a pure feeling of relish. "You are really screwing with my character. This will be great to watch." In other cases, it's "You are really screwing with my character, you bastard." In the latter case, you might even be annoyed with the source of the adversity.
\n
\nI like both types of conflict, but I prefer the second. For example, I was playing in a Dr Who game at IndieCon. One of the characters was a ringer. As a player I knew this fairly early on, but didn't act on it, as my character would not have done so.
\n
\nThe ringer did things I considered to be in the first category. "Ha! You are are so screwing with everyone, and don't I know it!" But then, in the midst of another conflict, that player signalled to the GM and clocked my character over the head with a pipe. It was a big surprise. This was definitely in the second category. I was momentarily annoyed, but I enjoyed the conflict much more as a result. In other words, I particularlyl like a bit of player competitiveness/conflict mixed in with character conflict. However, this is a little more dangerous, as it can lead to players becoming seriously annoyed.
\n
\nWhich type of conflict do you prefer, assuming you even accept the distinction?
