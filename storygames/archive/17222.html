    \n I have one hack where I use either a d6 or d6+d10 depending on the move (with the later having four outcome bands 16+, 15-13, 12-10, and 9-).

    I have another where I use d6+d12, and the three outcomes bands (hit, mid, and miss) change over the course of play (each hit makes the miss more likely and vice-verse).

    In both cases, having two different dice lets me incorporate effects like using the d6 to indicate the level of harm inflicted or the number of followers gained by the moves.

    I think this is one of the most interesting places to hack AW, right up there with getting rid of the MC.

    \n Cool story game discussion, bro. This thread is relevant to me.

    I was messing with a thing that looked kind of like this:

    BRAWL (Roll+Fight)
    One a Full Success, keep 3. On a Half Success, keep 1. Choose:
    - You bash your enemy and inflict damage
    - You gain ground on your enemy, take +1 forward
    - You knock your enemy off balance, they take -1 forward
    - You avoid taking a counter-attack in the process

    So, what you have is three options where you accomplish something and the effects take place, and one option which allows you to avoid a negative side-effect. If you don't select the danger avoidance option, it's assumed to take place. On a Full Success, you can take the danger avoidance option and two of the positive benefits, or you can take all three of the positive benefits but not the danger avoidance option. On a Half Success, you can take just the danger avoidance option but essentially accomplish nothing but save your own hide. Otherwise, you can take one of the positive outcomes and not the danger avoidance option, accomplishing something but not avoiding the danger as well.

    \n If you wanted a bit more gradated system you could easily replace the 2d6 with the usual FATE dice roll of 4dF (FUDGE Dice). Add/subtract the dice roll with the stat.
    +1 or more success with number of holds (or success move options) equal to the positive result (+2 = 2 holds, etc.)
    0 is either a miss with no ill effects or the MC can propose a hard choice if they want
    -1 or less is a definite miss and means the MC get's holds (or MC move options) equal to the negative result (12 = 2 MC holds, etc.)

    The bell curve is a bit tighter (which happens whenever you add more dice) so it's a bit harder to get a "stronger hit". Also, unless you cap the total, it does allow for players on a high roll to choose 4 options or more, which sorta makes the advanced 12+ moves defunct. You'd need to make few changes to moves that don't use the "choose options" method like Going Aggro and Manipulate but that wouldn't be too hard, and adding options might actually make those moves a bit more interesting. Overall it wouldn't change the game drastically but may add a bit more flavor.

    That said, I rather like the simplicity of AW's 2d6 strong hit/weak hit/miss as it works with the overall flavor of the game where the dice are secondary to the fiction.

    \n You can also add subtle gradations, instead of just going with the most blatant ones. Here's a (crude) example:

    Let's say I'm writing an AW hack for Vampire (I am not, I improed all this just now).

    The stats are aware, controlled, dangerous, and majestic. There is a move for being social:

    When you sway someone with your influence, roll+majestic. On a 10+, they find you compelling and will accommodate you to the best of their ability, only asking for compensation if you want them to do something that could get them in trouble. On a 7-9, they are interested, but require further convincing. The MC will tell you what. On a miss, hard move, natch. So, three possible outcomes, right? The fiction after will be tailored to fit the fiction before, of course, as always.

    But wait, you are a vampire aren't you? So hey, how about anytime you want to use violence against someone, you can take a big fat +2 to your roll? No hurry, you can decide after you roll if you want. How about creepy, invasive hypnotic mind control? Physical or psychic violence, it's all good for a +2. Man! Fuckin' vampires, amirite?

    So there. Now you have five distinct outcomes (not six, a miss is the same either way), instead of three. It's just the gradations between some are slightly more subtle, and it hasn't become full-blown Otherkind Dice.

    \n Here's the thing that I think is important. 6- is not miss. It's GM makes a move against the player from their list of GM moves. There's a WORLD of difference.

    6-, where the GM makes a move against the player, is fun. It's engaging. It's something interesting happening.

    Miss, on the other hand, is the lack of something happening. It's boring.

    When you have more than 3 outcomes, it makes is much more difficult to focus on interesting failures that add to the fiction.

    When you have a difference between miss and critical miss, critical miss is interesting but rare and miss is boring but common.

    I find that the simple 2d6 mechanic, followed by 3 possible results - all of which add to the fiction in some meaningful way, helps emphasize that every roll represents risk and that every outcome is meaningful.

    Rather than thinking of these rolls as crunchie task checks I think it is good to think of them as forking points for the story. Maybe if there were 5 outcomes *but* IMO it is more efficient to simply have moves snowball and hey presto you have another 3 outcomes etc

    This may well be more of an issue with DW. I don't think its moves are quite as polished as AW.     When you go aggro on someone, roll+hard. On a 10+, they have to choose: force your hand and suck it up, or cave and do what you want. On a 7\u20139, they can instead choose 1:
    \u2022 get the hell out of your way
    \u2022 barricade themselves securely in
    \u2022 give you something they think you want
    \u2022 back off calmly, hands where you can see
    \u2022 tell you what you want to know (or what you want to hear)

    This drives the story forward in lots of exciting ways!
