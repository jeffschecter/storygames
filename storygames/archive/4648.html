\n OK, you guys each get one point of mojo just for being you - here you go, enjoy. But one point won't get you very far - you need lots! You can earn more points for making the scene delightful, engaging, or powerful.

What are the things that earn you those extra points? Some ideas:

* Reincorporating something
* Making somebody else awesome
* Doing something surprising or truthful
* Creating a fun new person or thing

I'm just thinking out loud. What else is worth a point at your table?

    \n * Going beyond the next obvious thing: whether that be to do the obvious thing plus a stinger, or to bypass the obvious thing in order to make something genius.
    \n
    \nExample: I've started reading a wierd manga called "Sgt. Frog". It starts out as follows. An older sister is trying to roust her younger brother out of bed. Knowing that he's an alien buff, she points at a random section of wall in his room and shouts "Look! It's a real live alien!" There is a pause ... and then a holographic sheet peels away revealing a small and very surprised alien. "How ... h-h-h-how did you know?"
    \n
    \nI have seen the "Look, it's Elvis!" gag thousands of times. This is the first time I have ever seen that stinger. Damn I found it funny. A lot of really good moments in RPGs happen just like that.

    \n So I'm imagining a piece of paper with a bunch of circles on it. Each has a label, and before each scene (there are only six, so they are pretty big), everybody gets to put a coin on something that they, in turn, think will be fun. Until, say, nine circles are filled:

    One for acting on your character\xe2\u20ac\u2122s Need (Tech)
    One for acting on your character\xe2\u20ac\u2122s Type (Tech)
    One for getting your money\xe2\u20ac\u2122s worth as a paying customer (Client)
    One for doing something obvious
    One for doing something honest
    One for doing something unexpected
    One for doing something devious
    One for injecting energy and excitement into the game
    One for supporting another player
    One for messing with another player
    One for building on an idea
    One for changing status
    One for creating a dilemma
    One for reincorporating something
    One for explaining an aspect of photocopier technology in great detail
    One for creating bureaucratic entanglement
    One for introducing a new character

    Your friends toss you a coin when you hit that particular fun thing for the first time. Obviously you want everybody to be doing all of these all the time, but a coin on "do something obvious" is a great reminder that that can be a powerful technique (thanks, Graham). I worry that "Inject excitement!" sends the wrong message, though, because that can't very well be a mechanical action. I think the list needs refinement, and I invite commentary and suggestions.

    I like this idea in general because it's visual short-hand for "We like these nine things in our game the best right now!"

    \n Tears.

    Many, many times, what starts as a d20 Modern Supercops game turns into a Euro-LARP, with players so in character that they burst into tears when we find their wife dead at the crime scene. We had one player in a Battletech game who became so distraught over his best friend's death that we spent an entire session in mourning, and then he (both in game and out) disappeared. Keep in mind that the player whose character died was pleased to no end at how the situation resolved; he immediately grabbed an NPC which he'd played as in some scenes, and went to each PC asking them to tell him their favorite stories about the character. This took us about three sessions to work through, and the PC had only been there for four sessions to begin with.

    So yeah, we give XP for nervous breakdowns.
