    \n I was back in St. Louis this past weekend and demoed this for my Mom. I was surprised how quickly she picked this up and rocked out. She liked the fact that name/location/job generation was random, but that you had some say in the matter. And after explaining how the cards worked, she was creating characters like Gloria Grinder, Scheming Newspaper Reporter. Motivation? "She wants to become the editor-in-chief, but she's a girl in a guy's world!" Situation? "Train Wreck!"

    She said it was just like making her own mystery story. Who knew my Mom could bring down the awesome like that?

    Posted By: DanielSolisMy current hangup with the generator is not with the scene-framing itself, it's how the individual scenes affect each other, building into a cohesive "Act."
    \nThat is my current challenge at the moment as well. I think the Stake-Meter mechanic is a cool idea. I'm just trying to find a way to make it feel "natural". But it might be just as easy to set the Stakes at 1-3 (where 1 is talking and 3 is violent) and let them go up and down during play. But I have two issues there:

    1. I'm working with a nine scene structure with three 3-scene Acts. That doesn't give much room for moving up and down a lot.
    2. I'm not sure I like telling the players that "this scene needs to be mellow/violent". Because you might watch a scene/conflict develop that gets nasty, and the next scene the players could be wanting a follow-up that is even more bloody. I want this to be a cool shove in the right direction, but not break the flow of the story they are creating together.

    But I am doing something with the three-Act structure. Currently I'm placing all the cards down at once in such a way that each card touches at least one other card. Cards which touch only one card are considered to be "Isolated". And cards which are surrounded by four other cards are considered "Trapped". My current thinking is that cards cannot be Trapped in the first Act, and they cannot be Isolated in the third act. I'm trying to push the narrative towards a certain type of ending, and that is one way I hope to accomplish it.
