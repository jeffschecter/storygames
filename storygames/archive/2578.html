    \n I layed it as a one shot at Gencon. It rocks. Hard. The grunting is actually tons of fun.
    \n
    \nI would go to great, great lengths to explain to people that you cannot TALK during the game. You can't step out of character and explain what your character is trying to say. Yes, I know it seems obvious, but in the game I played there was one player who just did not get this and kept trying to explain to everyone what her character was trying to say! So really, if you think there is any confusion at all, go out of your way to explain how the game is supposed to work. Nothing ruins it faster then one of the players saying "my character is trying to tell you that you need to follow her".

    \n Hey Chris, thanks for picking up the game and extra special thanks for the length you went to get and play it. To answer the question first put out there...

    Yeah you can describe to the GM what you are doing. "I'm going to go hide behind the tree" if your telling your GM, totally fair. If you're telling the tribe, point at the tree, cover your eyes, grunt like a great ape, whatever you gotta do but then you cant talk.

    What Jake says above is totally legit. Really emphasize that the no talking thing is important, I've found the best way to really drive this point home to the players is to explain to them that it's really a lot of fun. I like to have a simple problem solving scene to start so people can get comfortable with each other and the language thing.

    If you have any other questions please feel free to ask them here, i'll be glad to answer. But i would also love to hear other people's input as to how they play the game as well. I'm really no more an authority than anyone else who has played the game and read the book. Everyone's conclusions are worth a lot here.

    \n Actually, I think the best advice is what Chris is already doing (having seen his draft write-up): he's very clearly noted the non-traditional PC-to-PC communication style of the game.

    While I respect folks' comments re: "tell them it's really a lot of fun", I think the best time to let players know about this constraint is up front when they are considering the game, and not after they've committed to the game.

    Not everyone is going to enjoy the no talking thing.
