    \nThe main reason people use a shield in games I've run is this:

    When in combat, there are a limited number of left-hand/right-hand combinations. For close combat, you can have Sword/Sword, Sword/Shield or Spear/Shield. Using a Shield means you get to add the Shield Ability die into the mix. It's only ever used for defence, but the thing to remember is that Swords always have 2d6, they never get better. Sure, you can crank up your Sword ability die size, but you can also use that Sword Ability die defensively along with your Shield die and your Shield Ability die in an emergency if you need to.

    The weapon combinations ARE pretty balanced though, and using (say) Swords/Sword or Sword/Shield and having a rubbish Shield ability early in the game is perfectly valid and works well exactly as you describe. But later on (once you've earned some Advances) you're going to benefit from higher die sizes in Shield, because it means you can both attack and defend effectively on the same turn.

    Using the example of a fairly typical combat focused "early level" or starting PC, they'll typically have something like:

    D6 name
    D10 Sword
    D8 Shield

    Lets say they're using Sword/Sword and going for full-on attack. They'll get:

    Attack: d6 (name) d10 (Sword Ability) 2d6 (Sword in right hand) d6 (Die moved over from left hand sword)
    Defence: d6 (remaining die from left hand sword)

    In full-on defence, they get:

    Defence: d6 (name) d10 (Sword Ability) 2d6 (Sword in left hand) d6 (Die moved over from right hand sword)
    Attack: d6 (remaining die from right hand sword)

    If they're using Sword/Shield, for full attack they'll have:

    Attack: d6 (name) d10 (Sword Ability) 2d6 (Sword in right hand)
    Defence: d8 (Shield Ability) d8 (Shield)

    So a d6 less. In full defence, they'd be rolling:

    Attack: d6 (Remaining die from Sword)
    Defence: d8 (Shield Ability) d8 (Shield) d6 (Die moved over from Sword) d6 (name) d10 (Sword Ability)

    Quite a bit more.

    \n You have a handle on it, everybody.

    You get to use one battle die during an exchange. You can use any other abilities as creative abilities for extra dice. If a creative ability happens to be a weapon ability, ranges and such don't matter. You don't even have to be holding the weapon. Just say how that ability helps you, is all.

    So... what the heck is the Shield ability good for? The answer is: two things. One of which isn't explicitly in the book. :-)

    1. Shield is the only "dump stat" in the game. It's kind of a positive dump stat, though. Basically, you can pretty much assume that you won't ever have to use Shield in a contest. It might come up in some odd situation, but it'll be rare. This means that using Shield for extra creative ability dice (and impairing it) isn't a big deal, in terms of weakening you for future contests. Shield is a great catch-all source for extra dice in battle when you need them. Cranking your Shield up to d10 gives you three pretty good helping dice (d10, d8, d6) at pretty much zero risk.

    2. You use the Shield ability to defend other people. The book doesn't explain this, which is totally my fault. Every ability in Agon has its own "special thing" right? This is the special thing for Shield. Here's what you do:

    - If you're in the same range band as someone you want to protect, you can give them your Shield ability die to add to their left hand.
    - You can only do this if you're wielding a shield.
    - Doing this does not impair your Shield ability.
    - You can certainly charge an Oath (or two) for this.

    And yes, two or more heroes can do this for each other, forming a shield wall.

    If you're an archer type, call in some Oaths to make someone position with you and give their Shield die to cover you while you shoot.

    \n About Shield bashing:
    I would treat a shield bash as an advantage contest. If it works, you get an advantage die, probably because you just knocked the opponent down. Every kind of special move can be handled this way.

    However, a zero range left hand attack with a Shield does seem reasonable. It won't be unpenalized, since left hand attacks take a -2. Still, I don't think it will cause any problems if you want to let the player try it out.

    Regarding weapons:
    The starting weapons that you choose are just that, starting weapons. After the game begins, you can find more weapons and carry them with you. Heroes can lose their weapons, too, like when Poseidon swamps your ship and sends all your belongings to the bottom of the sea. Assuming they lose the contest. :-)
