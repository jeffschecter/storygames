\n So Catalyst Games recently announced a new retro-scifi RPG that's inspired in part by "the wonderful stream of Indy games over the last few years," and since this year weekend just happens to be the 100th anniversary of Indy, I thought it would be appropriate to commemorate it with a little design contest.



Here's the rules:

1. Design a game (rpg, board game, card game, chat game, minis game, whatever kind of game you like).
2. Have your game involve motorsports (F1, NASCAR, Le Mans, rally racing, motorcycles, old school dirt track, illegal streetracing, monster trucks, whatever).
3. Keep the rules to 2 pages (letter).
4. You can also have up to 2 more sheets worth of play materials (maps, cards, character sheets, play guides, whatever).
5. Post a link to your game here.
6. I'll read them all and maybe even play some of them.
7. The winner gets a free copy of the Magic Missile anthology I'm publishing later this year. Plus some Hotweels cars. Dude!

I'll accept the first 8 games that cross the finish line. Everybody else fails to qualify for the main race. Sorry!

    FULL BORE

    \n

    ... is a game of hardcore Rallycross where you get just what you always wished you had in a racing game: when you fall behind, it's time to start shooting! You build your track out of tape on a tabletop and your cars and dashboards of Lego, then slide the corners and get the other guys to crash worse!

    \n

    (The function of guns in Full Bore is the same as shells in Mario Kart: to give you opportunities to catch up. Pulling ahead gives you points for either victory or repairs, while falling behind means that you get to shoot forward. On the flip side of that, getting shot at gives you an option: you can take damage to your car where you like OR you can turn your car to dodge, putting you in a worse position to make the upcoming corner; that is, you can take damage to your position in the race.)

    \n

    Thanks, Nerdly racers for helping me solidify the acceleration rules! After I left, I played a full race with my cousin in Richmond and managed to write down the rest. The game feels 90% done, (which usually means I have a lot of work left).
