Excerpt from from Sean Willis' email:

\n
\n

The characters \u2013 my Naga (with a third eye) \u2018Black Manda\u2019 and her friends a butch-looking bear (a mighty grip) \u2018Buck\u2019 and a Longrunner (guild contacts) \u2018Dodger\u2019

\n

I was told by the GM that my character was being haunting by visions of a ghostly Naga \u2013 so I went to see the Wise Auld Woman of the mountains (A Mystic) to find out how to rid myself of the apparition. On the way we fought with the infamous Full Metal Goblins (in the image) \u2013 who looked tough but could hardly move \u2013 who blocked the pass leading to the WAW\u2019s hut. Buck left the leader floundering on his back and we did a runner as the two other goblins (mooks) cowered.

\n

The WAW explained that the ghost was my Grandad who had used \u2018The Words\u2019 to magic the WHOLE capital city of the old Naga empire (minus the inhabitants) into a long scroll during a bad night on the ale. Then lost the scrollcase and fled to distant lands in shame. Some Naga priests thought it was a punishment from the gods - the confusion and bickering resulted in the empire splintering into factions.

\n

So to put Grandad to rest and give my character some peace, the characters would have to retrieve the scroll from under a bed in the dread port of Gullhaven (Grandad had found it there after years of searching) next session\u2026\u2026..and return the city to the Nagas \u2026\u2026..it all sounds too easy.

    \n Thanks guys, we had great fun. The bizarre confrontation with the over-encumbered Goblins turned into a duel of wits insults (we all rolled good Eloquence scores) with the Bear showing off (using his heavy staff to bat small rocks at the mook Goblins) to secure Awesome tokens before walloping the Goblin leader flat on his back in melee. See, they shouldn't have laughed at our Longrunner (he's Size +2, so the player visualized him as a tall, gangly Ninja, a bit awkward).

    We're playing again this week so I'll post what happens - I'll try and put a bit more mechanics into the next AP now that I've read the rules (when we played, only the GM had studied them).
