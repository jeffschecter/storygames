    B2 Keep on the Borderlands came with the Basic Sheet in 1980-ish. It was designed for levels 1-3 and features a cave complex (The Caves of Chaos) with several tribes of humanoids. It also includes details on the Keep itself, with some stats on notes on the inhabitants of the keep, as well as a number of wilderness encounters in the area.

    It is interesting for the time in that it included a rumor table, providing a reason for conversation with keep inhabitants, as well as, notes for what could happens as the humanoids learn from experience or if the heroes 'emptied' one of the areas of the caves. Looking at it 25+ years later, there is actually a lot of story potential here.


    S3 Expedition to the Barrier Peaks on the other hand was designed for Advanced Dungeons and Dragons, Character levels 8-12. The cover declares it to be the official Advanced Dungeons and Dragons Tournament scenario at Origins II. There is a Dual-Class Fighter/Magic-User among the 'official' pre-gen characters. The module has some brief notes about where in Greyhawk the adventure occurs, but otherwise focuses on the 'dungeon' itself. In short, sometime in the past, part of a human spaceship from another universe crash-landed on Greyhawk. Recently, an earthquake uncovered the upper airlock and the robot workers have been discharging unwanted "goods" - in other words, the story hook that brings the PCs to the adventure.

    S3 is notable in that it introduces then-new monsters to the game, it has rules for figuring out technological items, and the PCs, if they survive, can come out with some pretty buff hi-tech gear. However, unlike B2, there is not a lot of story here - mostly the 'fun' factor of standard fantasy characters coming to terms with Sci Fi technology - and maybe a bit of the mystery of how did this "spaceship" get here.

    Posted By: RichDNever heard if it got off the ground, but there was a Burning Wheel version of Keep on the Borderlands where you played the orcs in the Caves of Chaos trying to regroup and rebuild before another group of those damn adventurers showed up.
    \nKublai, iirc, proposed this on the BW forums (or maybe RPG.net). I decided to run it (using BW) for the last Chicago Gameday we had back in November.

    Basically, the PCs were leaders of the various monster factions in the caves, but I whittled it down to two opposing orc tribe leaders, a hobgoblin commander, a kobold sorcerer-king, and an enslaved gnoll ranger. It was a battle of wills between the ones who wanted to sack the Keep, and the ones who were in thrall to the Temple of Evil Chaos, which did not want to mess with the Keep. I botched many of the rules, but in all it was pretty fun. I set up a bunch of conflicting beliefs, had some stats ready for various big players (the Temple, the guards and leaders of the Keep, various Cave denizens), and just let the players go nuts.

    There's tons of info on running BW games like this on the BW forums. There's even a fan-compiled downloadable guide on their wiki.

    \n Some French gamers are collectively designing a dungeon in tribute to Gygax. Since the Keep on the Borderlands is often pointed to as an example of old school "series of disconnected rooms with no logical relationship to one another" and such (deservedly or not), I'd Story Game it up in similar fashion: everyone designs a room of the dungeon separately, and then they all fit into one big complex of random monsters and crazy death traps. For a smaller group, maybe you play a game like 1000 Blank White Cards, where each player makes a host of random dungeon encounters, which are shuffled into the deck and dealt out as a series of rooms the PCs have to deal with.

    \nARCANE GYGAXIAN
    \n
    \nENTRY: CANTRIPS (2)
    \nYou can cast these spells whenever you want:
    \nArcane Mark: Inscribes a permanent personal rune (visible or invisible).
    \nDetect Magic: Detects spells and magic items within 60 ft. while concentrating.
    \nGhost Sound: Figment sounds from a point originating in line of sight. Each new sound requires a new casting.
    \nLight: Object shines like a torch, goes out like a torch later.
    \nMage Hand: 5-pound telekinesis while concentrating.
    \nPrestidigitation: Performs minor tricks.
    \nRead Magic: Read scrolls and spellbooks.
    \nFrost: You can chill drinks and give cold handshakes. Babies cry when you do this.
    \n
    \nHANDY TRICKS (2)
    \nAlso, you can spend 1 Conviction and an action to do any of these tricks:
    \nFLOATING DISK: Creates 3-ft.-diameter horizontal disk that holds 500 lbs worth of stuff. It lasts until you rest.
    \nFEATHER FALL: Make Objects or creatures fall slowly, like a feather.
    \nFLYJUMP: Fly for the rest of a battle, or to fly to a particular spot you can see if not in battle.
    \nTONGUES: Speak a language you don\u2019t already speak. When you go to sleep you forget the language.
    \n
    \nSLEEP AND WAKE UP (3)
    \nSpend 1 Conviction to make an Insight action against the target\u2019s Will. If they fail they fall asleep immediately. They get a +3 bonus if they\u2019re hostile (but not if you sneak up on them).
    \nSpend 1 Conviction to \u2018charge\u2019 a metal item, like a weapon. When it\u2019s next touched it deals 2 damage to the person who touches it.
    \n
    \nFIREBALL (3)
    \nSpend 1 Conviction and make an attack \u2013 this attack counts against everyone in an arena; if they don\u2019t successfully defend, they take 1 damage. You can spend 1 Conviction to miss someone who is in the Arena.
    \n
    \n
    \nDIVINE GYGAXIAN
    \n
    \nENTRY: PRAYERS (1)
    \nYou can, as an action, do any of these:
    \nCreate Water: Creates gallons of pure water.
    \nLight: Object shines like a torch and goes out like a torch.
    \nPurify Food and Drink: Purifies food or water.
    \nResistance: Subject uses Ranked defense for magic until the end of the battle.
    \nGentle Repose: Preserves one corpse.
    \n
    \nDIVINELY INSPIRED (2)
    \nBless Water: Turns regular water into holy water.
    \nCure: Cures 1 damage for 1 Conviction at a time on someone you touch. Max 1 point per action.
    \nShield: Spend 1 Conviction with an action to grant +2 defensive aura to an ally you touch. Lasts until the end of the battle.
    \nCreate Food and Water: Feeds three humans (or one horse) for 1 Conviction
    \n
    \nGREAT HEALER (3)
    \nYou can do all of these:
    \nSpend 2 Conviction to dispel a magical penalty or repairs 2 stat penalty.
    \nSpend 5 Conviction to cures all diseases affecting subject.
    \nCures 1 damage per point of Conviction, multiple Conviction can be used. It can be done to anyone you can see.
    \nStops poison from harming subject while in your company.
    \nRelease one or more creatures from paralysis or slow effect.
    \n
