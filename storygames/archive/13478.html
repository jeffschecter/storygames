    \n Interesting discussion on compels. I agree that compels don't work well as "a thumb on the scales" for character decisions of the type Jesse describes, but I suspect that's not what they're meant for. I think the logic of compels makes more sense from a plot-progress rather than a character-dynamic POV. For example, compels that push characters into sidetracks or detours along the direction they're already trying to go are less problematic than compels that try to dictate which "story branch" a character wants to take. Another way to go would be things that cause players to use a suboptimal style of interaction to achieve what they want, or to limit their effectiveness in certain arenas (e.g. compelling a character to walk into a situation without a gun, or to skip the research on an unusual monster). They're probably also useful for encouraging specific combos of characters to face particular situations (e.g. compel a character with high social skills to be busy doing something else so the tough guy has to take the lead on a negotiation). I'm not an expert, though, I found compels to be one of the harder systems to get a handle on when I GMed SOTC. I think it's a system that seems simpler on the page than it is in practice.
