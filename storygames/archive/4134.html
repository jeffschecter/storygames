\n So, I GMmed a Truth & Justice game this weekend, and I had a blast.

Here's the actual report.

I'll just summarize the points I made:\n
  • I never figured that snacks that were served during play would actually be one of the main contributing factors to success. I mean, it kept the game flowing, no one worried about getting hungry and wanting to stop the game, no one suffered from post high-carb meal brain death. Really can't thank my wife often enough for figuring that out.
  • I really need to work on my organization while GMming. I'm a messy guy.
  • Chargen took longer than expected. Need to factor that in next time.
  • Speaking of chargen, 40% of the stuff that was in it didn't seem to important for some of the players. Mind you, it was a different 40% for one person, and a different 40% for another.
  • How does one accommodate the needs of what Robin D. Laws calls a "Tactician" in a game where the most planning I did was to figure out maybe 3 stock characters, a skeleton of a plot, and about 12 pages of mook sheets (basically forms that I could fill up quickly and make up up to dozens and dozens of faceless mooks at one time)? 'Cause I really don't want to work for my prep, and I don't want the guy to look so unhappy when I threw another curve ball at him. Never mind that he was 1 hour 15 minutes late. And it was fun to surround him with zombies. Who were ninjas.
  • Oh, yeah. I GMmed the game, on a dare from one of my players, while wearing a sarong. I don't know if that affected my GMming performance or not. It should be noted that, for almost 26 years of my life, I had no idea how to wear that darned thing, and it was only last week I figured it out, which was more or less like, "Hey! I'm a man now, I can figure this shit out without depending on other people!"

    \n Cool report. Thanks!

    I've seen a few different problems with tacticians at times. Because it's hard to give them a good reward other than "good thinking, here's some sweet bonuses", but then, you don't want things to go exactly according to plan (because that's not what the genre is about, IMHO?), but even still you don't want to just nerf the tactician and take away the smart stuff they came up with.

    What I'd guess I'd do is work out a system with the tactician (with their consent). For example: good ideas get a bonus to rolls, and a successful roll means a successful plan. If a plan is going well the GM may still invoke a complication or new problem, but they basically get to take their previous success as a bonus to use later as an instant "Aha! I thought of this all along..." moment. So their goal won't be to be clever in order to prevent trouble later on, but to be clever to earn a bonus to use as a vital last-minute success.

    \nThat's very close to what I eventually worked out in the game; it wasn't very clear then, but it's clearer now, and I'll want to tell Khalil about it and see how he handles it.

    Basically, they tell me how they prep a particular situation, and may ask me a particular question. Usually, I ask them to use one of their Qualities or Powers in order for them to get what they want, and if they make it, they get it without a hitch. If it doesn't work, I tell them more or less the same thing, but I include a complication (the information was incomplete, the doohickey doesn't work, what they do just disables the primary system and only slows it down for a while, their entry is less than quiet and people notice).
