\n I was thinking about this new comics publisher, radical comics, and how they have all sorts of new talent and a very high budget and it really helps give an awesome product. Anyways, It got me thinking: what would you do if you had a ton of money to put into a tabletop RPG? (Lets say a movie level budget, so millions of dollars here.)
Do you think your game would be much better? The same?
Do you think simply being able to work (with yourself or with others if you so chose) would drastically improve your game?
Would you spend the money on advertising to make the game seen by the "mainstream" people? (we'll assume that you don't have to make the money back necessarily, perhaps you got some sort of huge inheritance that must be spent on making an RPG or something).
Would you hire major artists, your own minis line or five, or whatever?
Just a hypothetical here. I'd be interested to see what would happen if indie RPGs suddenly became "massively high budget with few restrictions" RPGs.

    \n I'd say that basically all of it would go to marketing. Actually, I'd probably skip the design phase altogether and just bring in some excellent games and designers that have already been made. Some creative editing, professional copy-editing and new art, too. Whatever's left after that would go to innovative marketing towards new market segments. This would probably be the best choice for the rpg culture, and I believe that if I were told to specifically waste the money in this rpg project (as opposed to just spending a smart amount and keeping the rest), this'd probably be the best way to make some of it back as well. Spending it all on creating games would just result in more losses of the same type companies in the business suffer now.

    Alternatively, use the money to finance indie operations. It'd take decades to run out, and in the long term it would replenish itself anyway. Probably a better investment financially.

    \n I would spend a fraction of the money on actual games. Instead, I'd come up with a concept, put together a bunch of art, and figure out some kind of toyline related to it. I'd make it somethng that I knew could be picked up and expanded upon as an animated feature or feature film by another company with even more money.

Then I'd slap those ideas together with re-used artwork and give them away as promotional items to increase the sales of the toy line and make it more valuable as a property to be bought up for a film, or books, or lunch boxes, or whatever.

    For those of you old enought to remember clipping out proof-of-purchase seals from Star Wars action figures to send off and get Boba Fett, it'd basically work like that, but with a variety of games rather than an action figure.

    Of course, all of this is based on a goal of making a wider public, especially kids, more familiar with the concepts of rpgs/s-gs and related games, while also encouraging them to make their own hacks once they've encountered the concept of "Play-Pretend as game/structured activity". It wouldn't ever be to make The Game .

I would buy a decent building like a community center, move my HUGE gaming collection there, fill in the gaps with ancient games bought at auction by my minions, and supply the club with amazing furnishings catered to the geek lifestyle.

    I would then supply membership grants to selected creators and gamers. I would build a pod-casting wing with a decent studio and start a podcast released from there and nationally syndicated on AM talk radio (late nights). I would then allow gamers to purchase memberships to the Club.

I will hop on the talk-show circuit and build the rep that comes with having an elite status in something.     Finally, I would make sure to have some revenue production through products, dues, and investments in the name of the Club to keep it self-sustaining. Some of those funds would be reserved for scholarships and a annual Miss Gaming Pageant, the winner of which I will add to my harem of Gamer Grrls.

    \n With a million dollars for an RPG project, I would:
    \n
    \n1) Contract with a freelance IT consultant who specializes in EDI system to get me a simplified EDI system that I could use to sync into the EDI system at the major chains.
    \n
    \n2) Hire a really talented artist full time.
    \n
    \n3) Hire two programmers to begin working on the IT infrastructure for the game. Including a virtual table top that could link into the massive shared world environment that we would be supporting for the game.
    \n
    \n4) Put the first few core rules and box sets into production and made ready for sale before the game was launched. Get some small dollar store editions of the core books made and set up meetings with the various dollar chains I would want to distribute them through.
    \n
    \n5) Print a million fully playable introductory game books and distribute them through as many hobby game and comic stores as possibly and through every game con that would take them. Tie some rare support merchandise to a pre order program run through the local game stores. Free stuff would include small stuff like some free game books, t-shirts, but un all the way up to a small number of limited life sized statues of characters and critters from the game.
    \n
    \n6) Put together a paid event staff that would work part time organizing and running games at the highest profile retail stores around the nation. About 50 such people would be set up to work about 12-14 hours from friday evening through Sunday.
    \n
    \n7) Hire a staffer that would be dedicated to support logistics. Supporting printings and shipping and supporting the above mentioned demo and convention staff.
    \n
    \n8) Set up my own version of an OGL specific to the game and set up several different publisher participation packages meant to allow for small levels of envolvement like publishing modules or PDF support materials to larger projects involving development of full worlds and settings that would connect to and be a part of the larger online shared world setting.
    \n
    \nAll of that would run on a budget of about $750,000 for the first year. After that the game would have to start generatings its own operating capital and profits in order to be sustainable with that sort of structure. Un, yeah, I've put this plan to paper long before this thread. Just been waiting on the disposable $750K to get started. :)
