    \n Dead of Night is a quick, rules-light system that does horror very well. It can do any kind of horror story, really. It's good stuff. Some things about it are quite traditional: it's got a GM who, more or less, takes you through an adventure. Some things aren't traditional: you can spend Survival Points to narrate things.

    Zombie Cinema does zombies. As you progress through the game, you move a pawn on the board, and the zombie take over the world. PCs die and it's cool. That's good too.

    Trail of Cthulhu is good for Purist Lovecraft stuff. In fact, it splits play into two modes: Purist and Pulp. It's much more traditional than the other games. It's pretty good and, I think, the best of the Gumshoe series.

    If you want a single game to do all your horror stuff, I'd say Dead of Night.

    Graham
