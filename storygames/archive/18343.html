    \n I think that RPGs do this to an extent already with their list of stats and skills. Would, for example, AD&D's alignment mechanic count for partial credit at least?

    Here's my attempt, by the way. I thought that maybe I wanted to limit the use of violence in say, Cyberpunk (or D&D). What if an arbitrary mechanic were to be introduced such that if a character goes past a number of kills, his head explodes? It could be implemented in a stat similar to hit points or some other gauge.

    Make that limit 1 kill, and the game could get very interesting. Without the availability of violence, they would have to rely on other resources, and you'd end up with something like a stealth game, perhaps.

    To justify it in the fiction, maybe all characters in these variations of Cyberpunk start off as ex-cons released on probation (and the bomb in their brain is their behavior modification stimulus). In the case of D&D, maybe it's justified through some divine curse against murderers.
