Archaia Entertainment, publisher of the Dark Crystal comic book, has announced plans to release a role-playing game based on the Jim Henson classic late in 2012.
\n
\n
\n
\u201cIt will be fairly simple, all-ages appropriate, and available for younger players,\u201d Smylie explained. The game will be designed by Luke Crane, who also created the Mouse Guard RPG for Archaia and the award-winning Burning Wheel.
\n
\n
\n
A sequel to the Jim Henson and Frank Oz film is reportedly in the works (see \u201c\u2018Dark Crystal\u2019 Sequel Set\u201d).

    \n I talked it over with my wife (the puppeteer) last night and (aside from wanting this game NOW) she thinks they wouldn't have used servos on the Gelflings if they had them but they would have used softer, more manipulatable, rubber. So it was a technical glitch in her mind to.

    I don't think Henson wanted to make the Gelflings boring, they were just trying to break away from the cloth muppet design that they were known for. The Fraggles were still clearly muppets, most of the Dark Crystal puppets (aside from the pod people and the dog) were decidely not muppets. If they had moved the Gelflings around more as they spoke you wouldn't have noticed the flatness as much. In a show we did a few years ago the puppet I was opperating had the mouth control ring fall out in mid show. I compensated by having him more more when speaking (a silent move trick). No one noticed him mouth stopped moving.

    Chris Engle
