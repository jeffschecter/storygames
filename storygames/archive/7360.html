    Hey Chris, sure thing!

    \n

    I'll try to explain via example. You're playing a character, Stumpy Pete. You know two things about Stumpy Pete. One, Stumpy Pete won't admit he has a peg leg. That's because, two, he still has nightmares about losing it.

    \n

    These are Pete's Oddity and Dysfunction, which are spaces on the gameboard connected by a line. You always have a Dysfunction because of an Oddity.

    \n

    Now, say that Susie is playing Antigone Spit, and it's her turn. That means she adds a line - Antigone Spit never takes a bath because she is afraid of washing off her skin. Her line crosses Stumpy Pete's line, which means that in her scene, she needs to give a 'relationship update' about how she feels about him.

    \n

    So, maybe in her scene, Antigone Spit falls off a railroad car, and out of all her friends that are present, she calls out to Stumpy Pete: "Pete, help!" Susie explains to the other players/viewing audience: Antigone Spit trusts Stumpy Pete more than anyone else.

    \n

    But it's her scene, so we can't find out how Stumpy Pete feels about her yet. It has to be Pete's turn for that, and only if he crosses her line. We better cut off here. Cliffhanger!!!

    \n

    That's it in a nutshell. There are a few other rules and details, but I hope that gives you an idea.

    \n Hi Josh!

    In one-shot games, no. When you go to a new gameboard, everything that has been established continues as history, but does not show up on the gameboard for that session, and guidance for the resolution scenes at the end of a gameboard is pretty loose; the only rule is that no new personality information is ADDED to the board, so fixing things is perfectly kosher.

    In short, the ability to resolve or overcome their quirks is there for a longer-form game, but not necessarily part of shorter games.

    \n Chris, not remotely. The demo I did involved a businessman and an electrician. Oddities are anything you don't want people to know about your character, and Dysfunctions are the way they affect your personality in an outward way. You could have a character with the Oddity "Insomnia" and the attached Dysfunction "Can't concentrate."
