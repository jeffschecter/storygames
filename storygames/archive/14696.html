\n I'm struggling with this last bit of hacking Apocalypse World into Deadwood. I've got except a replacement for "opening your brain".

I replaced Weird with Will (which works well for all the play books I converted) and decided I wanted to replace the move "Open your brain" with "Make an earnest plea" believing that in Deadwood, showing you're need is a very risky move. It may gain someone's sympathy, but it may also be showing them a weakness to exploit.

Unfortunately every time I write it, it ends up looking a) too complicated and b) too much like seduce/manipulate. Here's is what I've got so far. I'd appreciate any suggestions you've got.

MAKE AN EARNEST PLEA

When you make an earnest plea, showing your honest need, roll+will. For NPCs: on a hit, the believe in your cause. On a 7-9 they will help you so long as it doesn\u2019t put them in danger. On a 10+ they will endanger themselves for you. For PCs, on a hit you both get +1 Hx with the other. You understand each other better. On a 10+ they get hold 2. On a 7-9 the hold 1. On a miss, you\u2019ve exposed yourself and they have an advantage on you. Any action you take they can choose to make you do something under fire.

A PC can spend hold to:
\u2022 mark experience when trying to help you
\u2022 take +1 on an action that benefits your plea

Compare with:

SEDUCE OR MANIPULATE

When you try to seduce or manipulate someone, tell them what you want and roll+hot. For NPCs: on a hit, they ask you to promise something first, and do it if you promise. On a 10+, whether you keep your promise is up to you, later. On a 7\u20139, they need some concrete assurance right now. For PCs: on a 10+, both. On a 7\u20139, choose 1:
\u2022 if they do it, they mark experience
\u2022 if they refuse, it\u2019s acting under fire
What they do then is up to them.


Other thoughts I had were on a miss you take -1 going forward as you've exposed yourself are vulnerable.

Alternately the move could be scrapped and changed to "Intimidate" but I think that is just "going agrro".

    \n Ain't that so?
    When you make an appeal to the crowd, roll+(insert stat here). On a 10+, the crowd is behind you and the truth will out, hold 3. On a 7-9, someone comes forward but their fate is now tied to yours, hold 1. On a miss, the crowd is not impressed, take -1 forward.

    Holds can be spent, 1 for 1, to:
    -take +1 forward
    -Ask and the crowd will provide
    -You can do no wrong, the crowd is behind you

    People often do or say things for the benefit of the crowd and gamble on the results - they make accusations, they ask who did this thing, they provoke on the stage of public perception - they call people out. Parts of the move seem really right, I'm sure it could use some tweaking to work better.
