    \n Just to disagree with Judd, who I do have more fun than, I am going to list my unreasonably honest responses. I've listed some problems, and some things I can do about it (if I can):

    - I think people who don't state things as opinions, when in fact they are opinions, are rude, selfish, and probably not people I want to spend time with.
    - I think people in the geek community are simultaneously too tolerant of unsociable behavior, and full of an overinflated, Ayn-Randian sense of self-opinion. I need to learn that I can't change that, and just figure out who I can be friendly with.
    - I think there are too many people who try to discourage people who have a passion to create roleplaying games. Too often I hear people's enthusiasm crushed, rudely and callously. In my opinion, people who do this are (in decreasing order of charitableness): mistakenly believing they're helping, overly-fussy about some kind of indie "brand," stupid, or are venally or pathologically worried about their games or reputations being damaged by association with this "dummy" who's asking for help. Perhaps some combination of these, I don't know. And, yes, the (edit) stupid are not as bad as the self-interested, in my opinion.
