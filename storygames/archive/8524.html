\n Hi all,

Different crowd here from some fora, so possibly some different answers to this one.

What practical tools do you use to help your actual play? I'm thinking here of actual play with games which span more than one play session, and which may require some degree of prep by some participants.

In my group we use wikis a lot, we didn't always but we've found them a very useful tool. The current game is at http://cursushonorum.pbwiki.com/ and there's a link there to other games we've run and may come back to. The wiki helps keep track of who's who, acts as a record of actual play (which is useful when someone can't make it, which given we're all adults with jobs happens a fair bit) and since we prefer traditional GM/player structures helps save prep time by collating the game's history in one central location.

So, what tools do you use? I'm interested in any practical suggestions, for example Jason Morningstar in his Big Store game circulated a briefing pack before the game and part way through a relationship map both of which struck me as really good ideas for that game.

    \n I have never used a wiki for my games, but I've been in a number of groups that had pre-wiki websites that were pretty much equivalent as sources of knowledge. These included campaign diaries, who's who lists, definitions of common terms, house rules and so forth.

    They are a great resource, and also a wonderful way to keep an archive of past games and then look back at them in a nostalgic sense. I have one old one still online at http://www.teuton.org/~stranger/Tanaloth/index.html

    \n I use a Google Group for my bi-weekly group. Mostly we just fire emails to prepare for the next session, but sometimes we store characters sheets and NPC/game setting sheets on it for reference. I'm not really into Wikis, because it's just another thing I have to be aware of, and rarely end up using them.

    \n We use a vanilla forum like this one for some amount of prep, sorting out the dates and post-game comments. We have used a recorder sometimes. It's particularly useful for playtests.

    I use character generators where I can to cut down on prep time, especially in games with complicated stat blocks. I also use the GUMSHOE one for pre-gens (NPCs are easy) - it should be out sometime this year.

    We use CC for mapping if it's complicated or 4e.

    \n I took a view a couple of years back that I no longer wish to play anything requiring (or even notably benefitting from) a character generator. I miss out on some cool games that way, but tehre's so many cool games I can afford to.

    I'm increasingly of the view that if my prep involves stuff beyond writing down names, descriptions and NPC goals then for me the game isn't right.
