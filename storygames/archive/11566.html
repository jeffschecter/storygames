    \n I'd be happy to read RPG books in public, if I ever actually felt like reading RPG books. But there's two reasons I don't feel like that right now: first, I've got a stack of books waiting to be read, all of which look interesting and entertaining, and so I'm going to read one of them because it's just a better use of my time.     \nMan, I hope you told her she wasn't really having fun, and recommended some indie games.

    I'm a bit nervous about reading RPG books in public. People might think I'm a geek. It's bad enough reading Lovecraft. Also, it's not like I want to evangelise about RPGs or anything. I don't feel a duty to make RPGs mainstream or bring them out of the closet or anything.

    It depends on the book, too. Breaking The Ice is fine. Poison'd is fine. But I once read Mongoose: Traveller on a train and felt like a total, total geek, because it's a big shiny book that looks like a game book.

    Don't get me wrong, it's a cool, fun idea, but I think my nervousness will get the better of me.

    \n For me, I want to present the best face on being a gaming nerd. That means preferring gaming books with covers that aren't so illustrative. I want stuff more evocative, like Primetime Adventures second edition's rabbit-eared TV. I call it the "subway test," I want to see/produce covers that fit with the environment we live in, while still drawing attention for being exciting, unique, and hey-what's-that-you're-reading-worthy.

    I also have a problem with reading gaming books at all. I need to get on that. I'm in cafes all the fucking time.
