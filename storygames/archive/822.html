    So I dug out Dragon 214 and read "Ecology of the Owlbear." Here's the interesting tidbits:

    • Owlbears can turn their heads an 270 degrees, just like an owl. Their eyeballs do not rotate in their sockets though.
    • Owlbears have primate-like swivelling forearms, so they can properly crush their prey.
    • Owlbear ears are slightly assymetrical in height, so their ability to locate things by sound is awesome.
    • Variant owlbears include Actic Owlbears (as seen in the owlbear trophy hunter photos) and Flying Owlbears.
    • Owlbears hock up pellets of undigested material, like bones.
    • Owlbear "cubs" hatch from eggs. They are not, however, milk-fed and therefore owlbears are not mammals.

    This last leads me to propose a new childrens toy. Cute adowwable widdle plush owlbear cubs, that come packaged in big plastic eggs. They could have spring-loaded arms that give hugs! All the kids will want one! "Owlbears" will be the next Cabbage Patch Kids!
