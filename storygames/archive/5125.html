    \nRoma Map and it is included with the PDF. Generally, lots of info over on the Roma Site site should you want to browse.
    \n
    \nThe biggest thing was mapping the Iridium Class and Progression to True20. Also, Matthew and I went back and forth a lot on the magic. Just the nature of Free Form magic in Iridium (stat tests, possibility of damage and the general nature of forming your spells as your own concepts) did not initially flow smoothly. I had a bit of a naive understanding of how to shoe horn it into True20 and Matthew needed to meet me half way on understanding just what the Free Form system was like. The foes, of course with Matthew heading the effort, went smoothly. Weapons and equipment took some support from me since Iridium really does not care too much about handedness of a weapon. Skills and feats were smooth and once we were in play test a few more came out that would be good adds.
    \n
    \nOver all, I am pretty pleased with how it came out.

    \nAvalon Printing. Fairly resonable for a POD printer. We had a number of options but ended up with this one as the most durable and usable. Lance is a bit slow in communicating though, You have to stay on top of him. That is how it is with a lot of POD printers though and Lance had the advantage of knowing exactly what I was talking about. Some other printers I have worked with you need to walk them through the sometimes strange requirements of our games.

    Posted By: Jason MorningstarIs Avalon printing the large format map you are offering as well? If you don't mind, what are the printing specs on that - paper weight and so on? I'd love to offer things in that format, so any information would be welcome.

    Who did the cartography? It looks great.

    \nYes, Avalaon will be handling the map. In Roma Iridium the map is an endsheet and is duotone. However, as with a typical pod printer, Avalon cannot do true duotone so they are going to print it full color. One of the important reasons to get a proof. It is on 28 lb paper and is 11x17. Adding it to the book is going to run about 65 cents but should be well worth it. I always like big maps and something like this is very useful.

    Clayton Bunce did the original cartography from my reference maps. Unfortunately, he did not follow them precisely and with this round I went in and corrected a few points. The interior maps are my original reference maps.
