\n So, can we make a game out of this day where four different fandoms coincide? The Glorious 25th of May and Towel Day always coincide and this year it is the 30th anniversary of the release of Star Wars -- A New Hope and the release date of Pirates of the Caribbean 3.

What I propose is something with the Empire and Rebels opposed, with a third group composed of Pirates, and a fourth group of Watchmen all duking it out in space (or at least on a space station). Each character gets a one-phrase description and one "super power" such as "confuse people enough to get away", "be so naive my plans actually work", or "I'm so cynical I tarnish the world". Conflict is resolved by single FATE die roll, and you're not allowed to kill anyone except in extreme circumstances "Luke, I am your...aarrrrgh!".

Other ideas?

--Nancy

    \n Star Wars + Pirates + Hitchhiker = 4 different fandoms?

    edit: Ahh! Lilac! Pratchett! Right.

    Well, since the 25th of May (from Terry Pratchett's novel I Forgot the Name, But Not the Content, of that Book) is all about remembering fallen heroes, and all the characters of the other three sources are dead (One takes place ~200 years ago, one takes place 'long long' ago, and one ends with...uhhh, no wait, forget I said anything), then clearly, we need be telling stories of their shared valhalla-esque afterlife, where they are set to battle for our amusement. Riverworld, but in space.

    Benefits of their being dead #1: Everyone has supernatural kung-fu powers.
