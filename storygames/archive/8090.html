\n We're proud to present the Collective Endeavour Journal. This short collection contains a selection of what we hope you\u2019ll agree are excellent and exciting articles, relating to a variety of games. In these pages, you\u2019ll find new rules, new setting elements, new characters, and above all, new ideas for use with some of the games we publish.

Hot War - SALISBURY PLAIN, gets a write up and some of London\u2019s villains get covered in FIRMS & HATCHETMEN. (Malcolm Craig and Andrew Kenrick)
Solipsist - SHADOW MASTERY gives GMs some ideas to help make Shadow manifestations that bit more creepy. (David Donachie)
3:16 - New weapons! New objectives! New aliens! New planets! DISPOSABLE HEROES has it all. (Gregor Hutton)
Duty & Honour - I DEMAND SATISFACTION introduces players and GMs to the wonderful world of duelling, with new mechanics, traits and NPCs. (Neil Gow)
Pulp! - DIRK HEMLOCK AND THE BLACKFEL INCIDENT is a quickstart scenario for Pulp! - a miniatures game of two-fisted action - and contains all the rules needed to play! (Matt Machell)
Dead of Night - Two Fright Sheets to help your games get going - QUARRY HILL BUNKER and GARTWOOD ROYAL HOSPITAL. (Andrew Kenrick and Malcolm Craig)
Contenders - Use everyone\u2019s favourite boxing game either to tell stories about Wargamers with TOY SOLDIERS, or as a campaign framework with PRETENDERS. (Rich Stokes and Joe Prince)
Mob Justice - An ideal starting scenario is presented in BROTHERS IN ARMS. (Iain McAllister)



The Journal will be launched at Dragonmeet at the end of November! This 88 page trade paperback will cost \xa36 and be available from our booth in the main trade hall. The PDF will be available free of charge to anyone who wants it! Give us your email address at the con and we'll email you the PDF the following day.

It'll go on sale shortly afterwards from Lulu.com and obviously we'll be stocking it on our stall at other cons throughout the year (Conception, Games Expo, Furnace etc). The PDF will also be available for download free of charge at the same time from a variety of places (Lulu, RPGNow etc).

Please stop by the booth or website and say hi!

Rich Stokes - Editor
http://www.collective-endeavour.com

    Posted By: WolfeLooks interesting. I'll definitely have to pick up at least the .pdf for the 3:16 stuff, if nothing else. It'll also be nice to look through stuff related to Hot War, which I spoke very briefly with Malcolm about at GenCon, but I didn't have time to really dig into it.

    \nThe article on Salisbury Plain by Andrew really is very good. I'm keen to have a game where the characters venture out towards that area. I thinkmy own Hot War article is pretty nifty as well. Hopefully it will spur interest and encourage people to find out more for themselves.

    Overall, the Journal is an excellent piece of work. The way that everyone pulled together to produce it has been wonderful. With a strong lead from Rich, drawing on the expertise that various individuals have, it all came together quickly and relatively easily. The way that people produced work for the games of others helps illustrate how much of a mutualistic thing the Journal is.

    At six quid, there's value in it for everybody.

    Cheers
