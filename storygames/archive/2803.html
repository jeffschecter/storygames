\n One of my first posts on the forge was about a spy game that I am still writing. At that time we were working on a mechanism that changed things from success to catasrophy avoidance. I refferred to it as a game where the spies could not fail. Tony chimed in and said that was one of the coolest things and that I should write that game. It was great advice except I don't know how to make that game fun.

With that said I was thining about 8, my game chef game, and how the same mechanism could be used for a caper game. the players roll dice like normal but they succeed at everything that doesn't involve ending the game before the fun is over.

For whatever reason they are inder a clock so the high die of every roll moves the clock forward and any rolll of 1 goes into the stack when the stack collapses they are on to you and things get bigger.

I think that I would change things so that the players would roll a number of dice and choose what they wanted to keep. that way a "better" character would get a better choice of how things went.

Damn you Tony LB your curse haunts me still.
