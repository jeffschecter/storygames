    Posted By: Elizabeththe ransomer would then have to refund everything individually, possibly eating the paypal fees
    \nThe big deal here not being the Paypal fees: Paypal gets a cut both ways (they send to you, then you send back to them). Hopefully you can ask your donators to, if the fund doesn't go through, to accept the Paypal cut on their return funds so that you aren't dinged twice (ie having to give them back MORE money so that, after Paypal's return-cut, they end up receiving the exact amount they paid to you).

    Personally, I think that's fine: It shows dedication to the cause. You won't set totally unrealistic and unmeetable goals (see Eric Gibson/West End Games) for fundable-items, because you know that you will get dinged on payments/returned payments. Accepting the Paypal Cut shows that you're committed to making it happen. Plus, if you're like $5 short at the end, you can announce a 48-hour extension or something.

    BUT, the part that I think is problematic, is that if you receive hundreds of donations from users all over the place: That's a lot of man-hours counting, confirming, managing and returning (plus management and confirmation on that end) of the funds. Several if not dozens of hours of management, spreadsheets and confirmation emails. That would be a goddamn mess, if all you want to do is work on your project and leave that management crap to someone else.

    -Andy

    \n It's scary that folks going to Fundable won't know about the weird drama that took place by visiting the site. (I should know better, being all web2.0 and all.)

    I think some good advice is to not stake more money than you can stand to lose on a site you haven't vetted throughly, and at least do some quick googling before giving CC info to a site. The fact that other people use it != vetting. For me: using a credit card to try a $X pledge is okay, because I'm pretty comfortable in my CC's anti-fraud protection and if I'm weirdly out $X that's a surivable loss, but on the other hand if I was really counting on the $X000 coming in from the pledge, I'd be more careful.
