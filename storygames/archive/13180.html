    When I'm GMing I feel uncertain when I think the game might fail to enchant the players, that they're going to be bored and that everyone will rather be doing something else. I feel lost when we're playing a crunch-heavy game and I have no idea which rules I'm supposed to be using in a certain instance. I feel insecure when I'm not in the mood and I need to act or narrate a serious and emotional scene.

    When I'm playing in general, I feel uncertain when I don't know if my contribution is going to rock with the others, when our agendas start to clash. I feel lost when I enter a game that has been going on for a while and I have to deal with all this inside knowledge and jokes and I have nothing to work with.

    \n The only time I feel uncertain as a GM is when the system abandons me, when there's some moment of resolution within the game where either authority gets totally confusing and/or nonexistent, or there's some big authority-credibility gap. Example: the extent of the "I win this conflict" power in 3:16. Fighting bugs on a planet, works great! Fighting with another character...dunno! The game builds and builds toward intercharacter conflict and then the floor drops out from under you. (Just one example pulled out of my head at the moment; I'm not bagging on 3:16. But after lots of SG threads featuring unspecific allegations and vague references, I feel like I'm better off backing up stuff I say with specific examples.)

    As a player, hm. I feel uncertain when it's not clear to me what my risk/reward is for doing something mechanical. Or when I'm playing with a new (to me) GM, and I'm not sure where our creative instincts intersect. Example: I'm all about high-stakes situation changes but you're all about no-win moral choices, so when you make me choose which of two things I'm going to lose at and I don't perceive any real situational change on the thing I'm going to keep, that bums me out. Makes me feel like I'm being tested for the "right" answer rather than being open to whatever answer I give.
