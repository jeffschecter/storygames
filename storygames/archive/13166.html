\n The original Mouse Guard book has sold out!
\n
\nDavid Peterson (Mouse Guard artist and writer) announced via Twitter that:
\n
\n"Mouse Guard RPG boxed set includes the main book w/ minor corrections, a supplement w/ new rules for mounts, additional weapons, &adventures;"
\n
\nAnd Luke and David announced the details at a NY Comic Con seminar but I wasn't there and don't see any recaps online yet.
\n
\nMy understanding (and I may be misremembering) is that it will include a rule book, 32-page supplement with all-new adventures, weapons, locations, and mount rules, a GM screen, updated character & GM sheets (which I helped with!), decks of conflict cards, condition cards, a fancy map, custom dice, some sort of pawn or miniature, and more!
\n
\nAt 10 10 10 there were preview character sheets which were sweet!

    out later this year

    \n
    and with plans to reprint, we thought we would offer a deluxe package as well that is full of stuff fans of the game asked for
    \n
    \n
    \n
    a softcover edition of the 320-page Mouse Guard Roleplaying Game, a pad of character sheets, a pad of GM sheets, a folding GM screen with handy reference charts, a map of the territories, 5 mouse pawns (which I carved the master for) to mark locations on the map, and a few more items I'd like to talk about in more detail
    \n
    \n
    \n
    new rules including riding mounts, 'weapons' for conflicts, & three new locations: Flintrust, Grasslake, and Sandmason. The pre-made characters from the main RPG book have been advanced, Celanawe has been added to Kenzie's patrol, and an entire new patrol of characters from the Mouse Guard comics has been made into template characters. With all of that AND three new missions
    \n
    \n
    \n
    we designed special dice that are included in this set(x10). The failures are represented by a snake eating it's own tail, the successes are crossed swords, and the '6' is replaced by the Black Axe

    \n
    \n
    Three decks of 12 Action Cards each, 15 Condition Cards, & 21 Weapon Cards
    \n
    \n
    \n
    The plan is to release the supplement booklet as a .pdf purchase so that players who already have the original RPG hardcover are not forced to buy the boxed set in order to take advantage of the new rules

    \n It's a matter for each person to decide, Hans, sure. And online will ALWAYS be cheaper, if there's a three-tier distribution model behind the product (volume discounting possible because wholesale cheaper than retail).

    Here's the MGBS pricing at SciFiGenre:
    Suggested: \t$64.95
    Our Price: \t$51.95
    You Save: \t$13.00 (20% off)

    $13 is a chunk of cash--half way (or more) to another indie (book-only) product or about a quarter the way to another boxed set game.

    Troll And Toad is only $2 cheaper ($49.99); I can not find the Amazon pricing.

    So, how THAT for a compromise: order from a FLGS that's online discounting (and any that aren't are probably doomed). Support a local game store AND have your low-low Internet pricing!
