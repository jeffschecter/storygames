\n Are there any games that have the GM hold onto all the character sheets; leaving the players with their hands free?
Like a group character sheet. Six to a page or something like that; a cast sheet.
Are there games that have two different character sheets, one for the player and one for the GM?
What about games that have a sheet for your character and the other player characters? Part of the sheet would have your character information, and the other part would have information about the other characters. A place to keep track of how they've related to you during the game.
I hope I'm explaining this clearly enough?

    \n There are many different types of "Player aid" character sheets out there that have spaces for additional bits of information like this, but not many games that make it part of the mechanics.

    I know 7th Sea had a Gossip sheet to track favor/dislike with other players and NPC's.
    Kingdom of Nothing has the rest of the players define your back story, and the GM is the only one that holds it.
    Smallville has stats that represent the relationships that you have with other PCs and NPCs.

    I am sure there are a few more, but all I can think of right now.

    \n I GM'ed some sessions of D&D and two campaigns of Unknown Armies with all the sheets and dice rolls behind the screen with me. Worked great and did what we were trying to achieve at the time, as the players naturally concentrated on amazing ideas and descriptions of their actions as the only way that felt they could impact on the story. So I'd say you can do this with many games as they currently exist.

    \n As Ry said -- there is a great deal of various player aids. The most common one being probably a gm reference sheet to keep together basic information about characters. It works well I'm games such as Kagematsu or Ganakagok. Gm writes down only ideas (places and objects of a particular value for a character in Kagematsu or fears in Ganakagok for example) and not stats (these are only on character sheets). Such a reference sheet helps gm to frame and flesh out scenes.
    \n
    \nAlso, in Ganakagok there are sheets that do not belong to anyone in particular, ie. a map of the world and a map of connections.
