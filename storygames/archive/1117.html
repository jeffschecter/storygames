    Okay ... alternate viewpoint. Go-Play and Games-on-Demand are not philosophies that anyone needs to be convinced of. They're so wise that, once said, they are self-evident ... like going up to a friend who is in a panic attack and reminding them "You need to breathe."

    I don't think that we need a "tract" on what GoD is about. Maybe (and I stress maybe) we need a little inspirational brochure to get people revved up to do some Games-on-Demand stuff in their own area, like at a game-store or a library. But apart from rousing the spirit, the strength of the message is that everybody agrees with it ... so there's no excuse not to act.

    Basically ... yeah, YAY Go-Play! I'm glad that it's taking off! Now let's not bog it down with a lot of stuff which isn't what it's about.
