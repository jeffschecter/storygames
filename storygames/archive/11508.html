\n I am pleased to announce that the fourth Guided Play Scenario for Annalise is now available!
\n
\nGuided Play Scenarios are short, free supplements for Annalise that give the group an established setting, situation and cast of characters with which to play the game. They are intended mostly for one-shot, introductory or convention play, but are also suitable for a longer-term game, depending the groups level of investment in the material.
\n
\nAt The Crossroads of Sanctuary and Salvation takes place in 1947 the town of Sanctuary, the picture-perfect model for small town living in the American South. The Mayor will tell you they call it Sanctuary because folks know how to keep their problems behind closed doors. All of that changed one humid summer day when a mysterious traveling salesman named Quentin Drake came to town. He always has what you need. Don\u2019t worry about the cost; you needn\u2019t always pay in cash.
\n
\nHow will you cope with that which threatens your way of life?
\n
\nThis dark mid-century America setting was written by the erudite Jesse Burneko. He developed a nice twist on Secret-setting derived from Spione that's pretty neat, in addition to writing up one of those "ew, so creepy!" kind of settings. Thank Jesse!
\n
\nArt, as always, by Jennifer Rodgers.

    \n Thanks for setting this up Nathan.

    I've been at OrcCon all weekend so I didn't get a chance to respond here until now.

    Oddly enough, my initial notes for the scenario were WAY creepier. I dialed them back a bit, not because I necessarily wanted them to be less creepy but I realized they were tied a little too closely to how I personally would play the scenario. I loosed things up a bit to allow more interpretation from play group to play group.

    If anyone does play this scenario I'd be curious to know how it went.
