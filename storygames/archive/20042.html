\n For those who know the TV show Entourage - what would be a good system or mechanics?


The way I see it, the main mechanic would be a trade off between relationship vs career goals.
You charm people, build relationships. Then you "burn" relationships, betray them, jeopardize their trust, to reach your career goals (get a director on board, ditch another; sell a script etc).
Then, you try to make up for it, regain their friendship, show remorse.
The character "Ari Gold" is a perfect example of a "Munchkin relationship burner".


What system or game mechanic could be suitable for mapping this dynamic?

    \n There are two that jump out at me immediately:

    Firstly, Capes come over which despite the name is primarily concerned with the issues involving characters which interest the players at the table. If everyone at the table is come together to see and tell a story about these characters in the trade-offs they make between relationships and career goals, then those of the kind of Conflicts that they will introduce and that will see action. You can hone that a bit by choosing different Drives for the characters rather than the ones which sort of focus on whether a character is heroic or villainous, but that is the easiest the potential hacks. Literally everything else works just fine.

    Alternately, there is MSG, which is literally about working for a dystopian company in deciding whether or not the trade-offs of personal life and relationships against the overwhelming demands of the Company are worth it \u2013 and incidentally backstabbing and manipulating the people around you to get ahead. Replace of very reified Company with a more abstract Pressures of the Career and, again, you've got a game pretty much straight up set up to do exactly what you want.

    Of the two, MSG is more GM-full, though the role of the Company rotates around the table so no one person is in control of it the whole game. Capes is explicitly GM-less which is part of why it is so awesome.

    So far, Smallville Cortex Plus Dramatic Roleplaying seems to be the best option.
    Still, I'd like to have a game with real relationship economy, where you can build relationships (charm, invite to dinner, have sex, do a favor...) and also sacrifice relationships for profit.
    \nMonsterHearts Strings work similarly to that, but are built up in more awkward-teenager-with-little-emotional-insight ways. They are a currency to measure entwinement and emotional leverage that you build (generally) through making manipulative Moves (like Turn Someone On) and spend to gain advantage when acting against them.

    Smallville absolutely lets you build up a strong relationship over time then burn it (with the triple-for-a-roll, then reduce-it-and-rewrite mechanic)- but it's the strength of your relationship to/feelings for them you are burning. Of course, if it's a vicious enough knife-twisting, they may well burn their relationship right back at you on the same roll.
