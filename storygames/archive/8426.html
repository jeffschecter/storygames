\n I've put together an Artist's Style Guide for Do: Pilgrims of the Flying Temple. This is a document I'll send out to any prospective artists for the game. It lays out the details on the universe of Do, the visual design for the Pilgrims, the clothes they wear and a whole smorgasbord of reference for human beings in flight.

I don't know how common it is for game designers to create reference documents for artists to use in their work, but it's something I thought would be supremely helpful for three reasons:

1) Gathering and organizing all this reference material forced me to clearly articulate the visual aesthetic for my game.
2) Searching for this reference clued me in to a handful of illustrators whom I may initially contact for estmates.
3) Once artists are hired, they won't have to work from a blank slate.

I wanted to put together the kind of reference that I always wished I got at the beginning of a new project. Kind of a mood board for the whole book. I've also converted the pages to jpegs that can be viewed on this flickr set.

\xa0

Any game designers out there looking for artists or layout people, I hope this document gives you an idea of the kind of input that is really valuable.

    \n Blue, I think we could do that in this thread, no? My process was basically a couple years of casual browsing and web-surfing, bookmarking any interesting illustrators and images with a "Do" tag on delicious. When it came time to put this document together, I just re-visited all those links and downloaded the images. I then grouped them generally according to images of the setting, of the characters, their clothes, their posture and some textures I'd be using later in the book. I layed out the document in InDesign.

    \n Oh, Josh reminds me - we had art direction on Grey Ranks, too, of course - I gave Jeff some specific instructions, because every illustration was tied to a moment in the ongoing play example that runs throughout the text. Probably a paragraph per illo (before you ask, I have no idea where this is). But how those instructions were actualized was pretty hands off once I'd approved his pencils. We only struggled with one of the ten illustrations, going back and forth quite a bit before giving up and taking a different tack. ANyway, each of these had a descriptive paragraph, which included mood, details I really wanted, and in some cases my idea about how to frame it.

    ILLO ONE (KOTWICA)
    Janek and Jadwiga, caught in the act beneath a statue of Zawisza, the Black Knight.

    ILLO TWO (CHECKPOINT)
    At the Czerniakowska street checkpoint, Janek's forged papers almost cost a German soldier his life.

    ILLO THREE (FIREFIGHT)
    Janek learns a hard lesson outside Number 25 Szucha Street - Gestapo headquarters.

    ILLO FOUR (AMBUSH)
    One way to get a rifle: Janek and Pelikan in a Gibalskiego Street apartment, Wola.

    ILLO FIVE (FLAG)
    The crew celebrates outside the liberated Adria Hotel.

    ILLO SIX (BUNKER)
    Irka and Pelikan escape an artillery barrage beneath the Parish House of the Church of the Redeemer, 37 Marszalkowska Street.

    ILLO SEVEN (TANK)
    A tank crew uses human shields on Filtrowa Street in Ochota.

    ILLO EIGHT (KISS)
    A furtive rendezvous: Irka and Pelikan's last kiss, in Irka's ruined bedroom at number 12 Elekcyjna Street, Wola.

    ILLO NINE (EXECUTION)
    Irka and Pelikan are executed by Waffen-SS Brigade RONA Ukrainian auxiliaries.

    ILLO TEN (GRAVE)
    Janek's above-ground grave, Krasinski Square, Old Town.
