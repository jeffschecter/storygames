\n Though they are badass games, I don't really see a monster manual working for Prime Time Adventures or Fiasco. I'm racking my brain trying to think of a game for which such a sourcebook would be useful. Naturally, D&D and Pathfinder and such games all use monster manuals.

But are there any indie games out there for which one could publish a creature-compendium type book? This would, naturally, require a system that's open-for-publishing in some way. Like, with the Books of Pandemonium, if anyone wanted to publish supplements for the game, I'd be like, okay, sure. That's the kind of game license I'm looking for. No lawyerese required.

Tall order. Hoping that you guys can help me on this one!

    It certainly narrows down the list a bunch. I'd re-factor the whole post/query as "Which indie/story games have open or CC-licensed rules AND would benefit from pre-generated opposition?"

    Otherwise, your "monster manual" will have to be system-agnostic... at which point, it doesn't matter if it is inspired by an indie/story game or not.     Prebuilt setting structures or monsters in Universalis also might be a good idea. "Hey, I just wanna have fuckin' vampires in this game, I don't want to spend two hours debating what they're like".
    Interesting.... Problematic... but interesting.

    On the plus side, one could make them as just lists of Facts, which is pretty damned system-agnostic, even if you're "thinking of Universalis" when you write them.

    Biggest negative I can see: "Vampire" as a Fact is worth 1 Coin. "Lives off blood; immune to normal weapons; mistform; batform; hypnotism" is 5 Coins. And there's meaningful differences, there--a character with only "Vampire" is relatively easy to destroy; one with five Facts from a template is much harder (if I recall correctly. One DOES have to knock off every fact of the template, not just the "has the template" Fact on the PC, right?).

    What might be Really Neat, though, is to actually make full settings. As in "we want to do Castle Ravenloft in Uni" and you get Add Ons, Tenets, templates, and maybe even a few "main NPCs". So all the "how valuable is Vampire, for us" stuff goes away, as a player conversation, because the setting establishes it.
