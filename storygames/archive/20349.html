\n You know when people say they're "working on a game"? What does that actually mean? It's not like lifting crates, or writing tedious and routine business letters, or papering a wall, is it? Well, I've learnt over the last few weeks that the expression 'working on a game' means exactly that. Don't get me wrong, I love working on my games, but this is the first time I've ever had to (a) do a huge amount of research, and (b) edit down the results of that research (which in most cases - these are character sheets we're talking about, but eventually there'll be a section summarising the background to the game - came to about a dozen closely written pages).

It's been very hard work in fact, and I've put it off more times than I care to remember. However, one positive side-effect has been that I've begun to feel like a professional game designer, rather than some dippy hippy putting together a piece of ephemeral nonsense. I'm quite proud of all the research and editing I've done, and I think the game stands a chance of becoming, if not a timeless classic, at least a solid gaming artefact. We have yet to see how it stands up under playtesting, clearly, but I'm quietly confident on that front.

So, the lesson? Put in the hard yards and you (and the book-buying public) will be rewarded. Make it so folks!
