\n I'm working on a game called Rain. The premise: the world (our own earth) was poisoned. Nature responded by flooding it (so much for rainbows...); for a day it rained so hard the world dramatically changed. People died in the downpour simply for lack of air. Communication and transportation crumbled. Technology died as well. That day was dubbed the Deluge. Ever since then it hasn't stopped raining (though the intensity of the rain is much less). From out of the Rain stepped monsters. A dragon now claims Boston; New York is overrun by ghosts (echoes of people long dead); Miami is a swamp, and DC is crawling with gargoyles. No one knows what has happened anywhere else (the game is set on the east coast of the US, but it could easily be run in other places). It has been three generations since the Deluge and people are now reaching out to each other. Expeditions are sent to the ruins of our cities to find survivors and reclaim artifacts of our past. Human magic has also been born of the Rain.

Enough backstory; I need help: does anyone know a good font? Odd question, I know, but I've literally spent hours online looking and haven't found what i'm looking for. Which is: something reminiscent of a fantasy-setting (that's the thrust of the game - fantasy, not post-apocalyptic) but also with a nod to the Rain. I don't want anything cheesy (most water-based fonts that I've seen are kinda cheesy). And it needs to be easy to read. A tall order to be sure, but if anyone can point me in a direction that would be a great help.

Thanks!

    ... Ah, but no, more seriously: I usually point people to "A Few Good Fonts" when trying to pick a typeface that's actually going to be used for titles and headings. Novelty fonts are fine to use here and there, but I worry about how they stand up over the course of an entire manuscript. For instance, Oh Shit is probably visually appropriate for what you have in mind, but I'm not sure it's readable enough to use for anything more frequent than (quite large) chapter headings. (That one was found by just looking for "fantasy fonts" on fontspace.com, which includes some other visual takes on classic styles and popular logos.)

    \n I would suggest looking at horror fonts instead when it comes to finding one suitable for the title page. Some are sort of bloody, which can also be taken as somewhat liquid. Also, I think horror and post-apoc can be quite close when it comes to the mood (zombies are both, for example).

    When it comes to headings, just keep it to a simple sans serif (definition). It takes more attention if you have something fancy pantsy than sets the mood to choose a font that has a really specific style. It's fun to see a weird font the first couple of pages, but then it's just tiresome.
