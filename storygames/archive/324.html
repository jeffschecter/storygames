Expected and Possible Mastery

Bruce Baugh
March 2006 edited March 2006 in Story Games
This is a spinoff of another thread, because the more I thought about it, the more I thought, "I'll bet there's an unchecked assumption here."

Given a universe of semi-connected game design and play styles which is very wide, and given a conceptual framework in which you feel there's a place for pretty much everything, or at least for a whole lot of it...

How much of that universe do you feel you ought to be able to design for and play in? Do you think of yourself as someone whose tastes and realm of conceptual flourishing is a lil' piece of the whole, someone who is or ought to be capable of striding across the whole map, or somewhere in between? Do you feel that you have something to offer everyone, or lots of folks, or do you see yourself as best addressing a fairly narrow set of listeners?

What do you feel about your situation now, what do you feel you (or anyone else with the right clues) is capable of, and what do you feel you ought to be doing to bridge the gap, if there is one?

Personally, I find my focus contracting with time, for a variety of reasons, some positive and some negative. But I think that expansive visions are important and worthwhile too, and above all I'm fascinated by others' self-definition on my way to a sharper definition for myself. I hope none of this sounds perjorative - if it does, holler and I'll fix it if I can.

    I'm going to answer by corrolary.

    I'm a prose author. I write short stories and I write novels. I've sold little bits and pieces, and I hope to sell more in the future. But with that as my stated goal, I don't only read prose fiction. I read poetry, I read plays, I read nonfiction, too. And I occasionally take a hand at writing these other things, too. Not because it's my primary goal, but because the experience of reading, writing, and simply experiencing them can inform what I am really and truly interested in.

    On a more pragmatic note, I've written games for my wife; they fit her desires and habits of play very very well. They're only an adequate "fit" for me. But if I only ever wrote what I liked, I wouldn't be able to do that for her.

My tastes are all over the map, and I want each of my games to stand alone, complete in themselves, hitting the target I've chosen for them. Some of my games will have more to offer to more people than others; others will have something very cool to offer a few people; others will offer a broad range to many people.

    What do you feel about your situation now, what do you feel you (or anyone else with the right clues) is capable of, and what do you feel you ought to be doing to bridge the gap, if there is one?

    I feel that, right now, I'm taking my ranging shots - OK, so this game hits this conceptual area and audience, and now that game should hit another one. I feel like, once I have my ratios set arights and my trajectories calculated, I will be capable of fulfilling any goals that I find personally interesting and valuable to design for. The calibration process, however, is a long one that will take much work before I'm satisfied that I can hit the mark.
