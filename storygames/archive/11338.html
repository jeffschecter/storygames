\n There have been comments on the Gaming Austen thread about the significance of roleplaying being mentioned in academic journals. Well, as a point of contrast, here's something I'm more geeked about than I'd be for a mention in an academic journal.

A week or so ago I received an email from an instructor at the University of Colorado Denver informing me that he'd made My Life with Master assigned reading for his course in using games to teach for the university's Department of Instructional Design.

My Life with Master is a required college course text.

Paul

    \n Thank you all for the congratulations.

    Thor, here's what he had to say about why he chose My Life with Master:\n
      "This semester, I was invited to teach a course in the instructional technology and design department on using games to teach. And while I assume many of the students are interested in using videogames to teach, my approach to this topic is to get them playing board and pen and paper games to bring them up to speed on many of the concepts of game design and use of games to teach.

      Which brings me to My Life With Master. I have assigned this game to my class as an example of simulating something in RPG form. There are a number of reason I chose MLWM. For one, the rules are beautifully written and fun to read! For another, I think that you have taken this common narrative trope\u2014the minion\u2014and turned it into a game system that models it well."

      And in a subsequent email:

      "Really, it's a wonderful piece of work and I think it is a great way to get these students thinking about the connections between role playing and learning."
    \nVince, unfortunately for that plan, though a few of the students ordered the book, most opted for the pdf.
