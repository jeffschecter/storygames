    \n I've played it once. It works.

    1) It moves slower than regular InSpectres. I ran it in a 4 hours con slot and did not actually achieve the end-game condition specified in the rules. So, I think Unspeakable probably would run best in a 2 to 3 session run.

    2) Unless the players are totally on board with the Lovecraft tone the game runs much more towards the pulpy version of Lovecraft. I had people wrestling nightguants in mid-air and summoning for things from the abyss left and right.

    3) Given that it didn't descend into the commical, per se. Not the way InSpectres does (and is supposed to). It was jut very over-the-top.

    \n Actually, our group had a really fantastic experience with Unspeakable. We wound up telling a story that scared me so much, I didn't go to bed until 1 because I DIDN'T WANT TO TURN THE LIGHTS OFF.

    As for the style of story, I think that might be a group thing. We had no elder gods or weird summonings, just a lot of weird and eerie shit and unnatural geometry.

    I highly recommend it, with the caveat that there needs to be a group discussion beforehand to make sure everyone's expectations are on the same page.
    ~Anna
