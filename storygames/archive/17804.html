    \n I'll start.

    My favourite, as of this moment, is MERP 2nd Ed.

    Picture: https://www.dropbox.com/s/zu7r7vrjoraymmt/Merp_1986a.jpg

    The hobbits are cartoony, yes, but there is loving attention to detail. It's quite static, with little action. It looks like a game of journeys, not just of fights. The characters are realistic, not overblown - they look like they could struggle and bleed, care about things, and maybe love as well.

    This game cover makes me want to explore Middle Earth. It makes me want to run a game of characters going on a journey.

    A flaw? Sausage-fest.

    \n Kata Kumbas is an 80's game, taking place in an alternative fantastic medieval Italy with a heavy touch of mediterranean folklore. The tone is picaresque and the art really supports the atmosphere. Magic is very much based on fictional results rather than straight mechanic effects.

    Kata Kumbas is greek for "about the Caves" and is the etymological root for "Catacombs"

    It has many many nice touches and I invite you to check out the pdf of the original game, now impossible to find on the market.

    As a kid I was into D&D and I didn't appreciate much KK, but I can now see that it was a very nuanced game for a more mature audience. Go to page 39 to see the delightfully illustrated tables of initial random equipment. The game is proudly unbalanced! You could get a sword and armor or a rabbit or something whimsical such as a necklace of wolf teeth, dog fur or a monkey! As a kid this was frustrating, but for an adult gamer this is pure gold for improv and color.

    The character creation system asks you to choose a class before rolling the dice for your stats. This creates wonderfully maladjusted characters that have to rely on their smarts to survive, but it also makes them very human and loveable. You get the ignorant lazy wizard that barely graduated from the arcane college and has to bluff his way for a living, or the articulate thoughtful warrior that can't hold a two handed sword and needs a servant to reload his crossbow, or the huge priest that is better at preaching with his hands than by the book.

    Look at page 57 for the results of spirit summoning. No attempt at balance. Full of flavour, sometimes silly, sometimes deadly and relying on the GM for handling the fictional powers of the summoned creatures.

    Page 85 shows what you can buy in the bazaar instead of showing a price list. It's all highly reusable stuff that could be lifted straight for your favorite medieval setting.

    Wonderful stuff.

    James, Nobilis 2E is one of the greatest RPG products ever put together. So much for you to be proud of there. The Game of Powers cover with Ophelia is also amazing.
    \nOh, so very true. Nobilis 2E is one of the absolute favourite books I own. I recall I was visiting London for some reason, and popped into Orcs Nest. As is my wont, I was browsing the shelves and was struck by this big white book sticking out two or three inches from the rest. I admired the magnificent cover and gorgeous binding, puzzling over what the game inside could be and though they wouldn't open the shrinkwrap, I couldn't leave the store without it.

    Aside from the cover, so much of the rest of the design is equally awe-inspiring. So much so it led me to put the game on the pedestal of 'I musn't sully this by playing it wrong', rather than bringing it to my gaming table (admittedly I wasn't in a regular group at the time). I believe I am not the only one so affected- though the gorgeous physical artifact is only part of the cause. Unfortunately, I've still not run it (or 3e for that matter).

      \n Geist is a New World of Darkness game about people who had a brush with death and came back because a Ghost made a pact with them to use their body part time. They are changed and have methaphysical keys to power. Those keys can be combined with certain "Realms" they unlock to make your own supernatural effect in the game. It is a bit between Wraith and Orpheus (if you are more familiar with the old world of darkness.) A bit like Monster and Other Childish things in as you have a very powerful other character that hangs around with you, only this time the human is acting out the powerful characters wishes and compulsions for a taste at immortality or at least life. The people who took the bargain can now see Ghosts and Ghosts can see them, which causes problems of course.
      Good game with some balancing problems ruleswise. The corebook and the "Book of the Dead" detailling the underworld were the only sourcebooks in the line. If you can stand the New World of Darkness it is worth a look. You need to like Faustian / Ghost stories of course.

      \n Geist is a very broken mechanically, but conceptually pure amazing World of Darkness game that is DYING for a remake with a more 'indie' style system overhaul. It's like Beatlejuice Persona Ghostbusters, but takes itself mostly seriously. You play someone who died, and then made a deal with a ghost called a Geist that brought you back to life, and gives you cool powers. It's mostly about helping spirits pass on, and kicking the asses of spirits who won't. There's tons of cool ideas in it, but it's...well...really wonky ruleswise. In a lot of ways. Also too many powers even for a WoD game. Prettiest book they ever made, though.
