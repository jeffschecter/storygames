    Posted By: Troy_CostisickYay! We won!

    So...Chad, you wanna give us some insight on what you were thinking during this process? For instance, what did you like about it? What was fun for you? What brought it to an end? What did you get out of it, and what did you learn?
    \nI initially posted a line of pseudo-poetry, with the thought that we might be able to get some Exquisite Corpse, Chinese Whispers/Telephone, or Nomic action going... which we did.

    I have no idea why I put a number in front of the entry.

    The first rule seems to be "repeat the last word or two of the previous entry."

    I liked that new rules seemed to come up in play. I pushed a bunch of them (DECADE, FORK, FOUL, CAP), but then Colin brought in COLLAPSE. That was awesome.

    I also liked that this can be played asynchronously -- you can walk away from it and come back, if it's still running.
    Untangling the points earned situation may be intriguing if you go away too long, though.

    What brought it to an end was CAPping the last active thread.

    An emergent rule/aspect of gameplay, then, seems to be that a game cannot FORK or CAP (end) until after the first DECADE.

    More as I ponder it, but everyone, feel free to chip in.

    CU

    Posted By: Ryan StoughtonIn my mind there was a rule "each post costs a point after round 1."
    \nIntriguing. I didn't have that rule in mind.

    I did have a rule of "cannot post until someone else does again" (though I DID, in the "gamemaster/referee" role).

    Back to "what was learned?" -- I think a few things are jumping out at me (all DUH! moments):
    * This reflects the process of learning an unfamiliar game.
    * This shows how simple rules can create complexity.
    * This shows how new rules/house-rules come up.
    * This shows how misunderstanding of inadequately explained rules impact gameplay.
    * The monetization of the game via DECADE at 10 posts changed the nature of the game.
    * People present early on seemed to have more buy-in than people coming in late.

    As a last aside, when DECADE came to me, I was thinking of praying the rosary.

      \n Here are some of my reactions:

      I had no good idea of how the points worked - whether they were accumulated naturally or assigned by Chad. I figured I might be able to count posts and figure it out, but that would have been a pain. So I ignored them and figured that someone would eventually correct me.

      I tried introducing Collapse for several reasons. 1: I could easily see the whole thing turning into twenty different Forked stories. 2: It's one of the ways stories, roads, and even poems work - elements from before connect with elements later on. 3: It's something you can't do with Computer Science type Forks (unless I'm remembering wrong).

      I'm really glad it didn't turn into a big game of Mao.

      It needs a visual aid.
