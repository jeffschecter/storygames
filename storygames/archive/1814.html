    Do a Topic search for "improv" to see some previous focused threads here at S-G.

    All three of us in my Monday group have taken improv classes and one is a local company member who performs all the time. Basically it blew our minds. It's huge in terms of actual play. But be careful not to conflate "stakes" in improvisation with stakes in gaming, because they aren't really the same thing in my opinion. Same with the orientation of conflict and agreement.

    In terms of design, I think I've learned a lot of interesting things from improvisation. The biggest, the one that is already explicitly plugged into my next game, is mechanically rewarding reincorporation.

    November 2006 edited November 2006
    I studied theatre for about 10 years, and in that time had a vast improv life, studied in school and applied in practice. There were about four solid years in there where I had at least one improv show (sometimes as many as three) that went live on stage on a weekly basis. Some were improv comedy, some serial dramas, some "soaps". They had a vast impact on my understanding of human dynamics and the choreography of social groups. They are also, incidentally, what brought me back to gaming.

    Because of the timing involved (there was that wide body of theatre experience followed by a wide body of play experience, followed by a much smaller body of design experience, the interim of which spans ten years) it's hard to pick out what specifically one has done to the other, however I'm certain that experience informs everything I do in game design, game play and frequently, in life.
