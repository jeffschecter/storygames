    \n Hi, Neko. Fair enough.

    Dread is only available via Lulu (though if you see me in person at a con or something, I'll be happy to sell you a copy). It's a large book -- 280 pages, 8.5 x 11.

    The cover art has been described as lurid, adolescent, and sexist. People have said that the profanity in the game's text is unnecessary and offensive. The game's interior art has been criticised for its reliance on grotesquerie, deformity, and women with tentacles where sex organs ought to be.

    And these are its good points.

    p.s. MSF High looks really fun. But that page reminds me of a pet peeve of mine. The text on a game's Lulu page should include a hyperlink to the publisher's web site. The few lines of text permitted by Lulu aren't really enough to help me make a decision about a purchase.

    \n In addition to the numerous interesting games mentioned so far, here are a few others:

    Colin Frederick's Sufficiently Advanced & Princess Game
    Bret Gillan's Pundit's & Pollsters
    John O'Brien's Caper!
    Kirt Dankmeyer's Unsung
    and, of course, the RPGpundit's Forward ...to Adventure!

    (These are all games I've acquired, excepting the RPGPundit's - I'm not afraid to say it's name, but I draw the line at caring.)

    And we're still only scratching the surface. There are hundreds of RPGs on lulu and the run the gamut of quality and type, but there is also a raw vein of madcap creativity there that you will not find anywhere else. It's pretty amazing, and pretty scary.

    -Rob D.
