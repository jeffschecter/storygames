    Posted By: northerainIn all fairness, the ''beeing cool'' rule also exists in the Exalted games and works pretty well(it also worked pretty well in my old game, ''Fallen''), so I don't see what the problem is.
    \nYeah, I only played Exalted a few times (save one mini-campaign) and it was all 1E.

    I read the arguments against the Cool Rule. They make sense (save the "poseur" bits: that's just an impotent cynical critic being an impotent cynical critic).

    But if you've played the game, the arguments don't stand. They simply don't stand. If you've played the game, you'll see it work: People aren't always scrambling for them. And when they're rewarded, that player gets the momentary rush of "I've contributed something and was recognized for it". I've played with about 60 people in 6 years, and have never seen a situation arise where (items 4 and 5 have been leveled on RPGnet against the Cool Die in other reviews):

    1) People constantly aim for the cool die in that "grubbing for power" powergaming way.
    2) People ruin it by being "too cool, all the time". Milking the cool.
    3) People acting like posers.
    4) People feeling guilt for not getting the cool die, when they thought it was cool.
    5) People getting into this whole esoteric brain freeze about how John normally describes things cooler than Randy does, so how do we reward them fairly? Also the rewarding is all up to the GM/players, so if they're not "wired right" they might give out cool dice disprportionately, and Oh My God the subjectivity of it all!!!" (screamed while leaping out of a window)

    Again, though, it's impotent criticism leveled at a game read and never tried. It's in the same way I can look at a D&D 3.5 E handbook and say "No one who plays this game possibly roleplays (plays a role in character), because there's no rules for it in here. Most of the book is powerz and featz and combat attacks, manuvers, poisons, treasure, etc. Therefore no one acts in character in this game, instead focusing on the combat." A misnomer, to be sure, grounded in an interpretation based on reading the game and assuming play, rather than watching/playing for oneself.

    Listening to a description of how the game Katamari Damacy works sounds like its the most retarded idea in the world, and totally unfun. Reading the rulebook, I can't imagine being entertained by this PS2 game for more then three minutes. But all those imagined criticisms crumble to ash when you give it a shot, and find yourself 6 hours later like "Dammit, I'm gonna GET YOU, Brown Crayon!".

    There's anime like that, books like that, movies like that, and apparently RPGs like that.

    -Andy
