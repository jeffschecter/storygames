    \n Currently:

    My bi-weekly group: We've been playing some one-shots to clear the palate, including a Savage Worlds Conan-themed game last week (yep, killed us a goddess, imprisoned some Turkish pirates). Next week we will start With Great Power.
    * However, there is a clause: I expect my copy of REIGN to come in sometime this week. If it sings to me, and I can get through some of it, we will ditch WGP for Reign for a few weeks first.

    My Weekly Group: Next few weeks we will be playing a RISUS game inspired by Vampire Hunter D and Mythic Russia: The year is THE FUTURE, and in the post-apoc wilderness of Russia, demon hunters track down lone creatures. The first session totally worked out well, and this game is giving me more ideas for a rewrite of my stalled "The Ghost Killers" game. I'm just about ready for a rewrite and replay.

    Before that, we played some Tenra Bansho, which was kind of painful, honestly. I found out the following:
    1) My weekly group is already distractable.
    2) However, given focus, they are awesome roleplayers, down to a (wo)man.
    3) Tenra is an awesome game for a GM and 2-4 players. In fact, the group resources pretty much indicate that 4 players is the "max".
    4) With 5 players, things get strained but if you can maintain focus, it can be fun. But it definitely takes longer.
    5) With 5 players in my weekly group, running Tenra is an exercise in herding cats.

    Other:

    In June (Sat June 16th), I'm throwing a housecon. I'm organizing it now. It will focus on laid-back gaming, and mostly playtesting of area designers' designs (and perhaps an actual game or two of Something Already Published). Expecting about 10-13 people, dividing up into 3 groups for play. EDIT: As an aside, if you're in the Triangle NC around that time, whisper me!

    In July, some of us are going to Nerdly/GoPlay South-East (which is actually Tennessee). I'm really looking forward to that. It sounds like a lake house, no slasher,just laid back relaxed gaming with Andrew and folks.

    In August, it has been decided that I am going to GenCon. In fact, I'm going to soon start organizing SHADOW GENCON. SHADOW GENCON. Is basically going to be a con within a con: There's usually that gaming in the cafe of the hotel after hours. But what about DURING hours? I will try to volunteer at Kat's gaming thingy, but in the interim I want to contact other friends who are not running a booth, and organize laid-back schedule-free gaming in the open gaming areas. More on that soon.

    -Andy

    \n I'm 4 sessions in to running a Tribe 8 game, which really began to gel last night. We started out using the Everway rules, but a couple of the players felt that they were (at least psychologically) not very empowered by the rules, so last night we switched to an unholy frankenstein system of OTE character description and task resolution overlayed with Mortal Coil's Passions, Magic Tokens, and Power Tokens. Everyone liked the switch, despite the fact that the two fighty characters (ex-Joanite Templar and ex-Yagan Reaver) are much less badass under the new rules. We're playing the metaplot and just finished "Enemy of my Enemy" last night.

    Ran a Mortal Coil one-shot a couple of weeks ago and enjoyed it, and folks want to do another one soon.

    Played a Revised Mazes and Minotaurs one-shot last Sunday and it was a blast. For those who haven't sen this, it's a true labor of love. The premise of the project is that rather than being influenced by Tolkien and the Middle Ages, the first RPG was influenced by Greek Myths and the ancient world. The game texts are full of funny takes on D&D and gaming history, and the game itself is very playable and fun in an Old School, Basic D&D way. Plus, it looks great and is totally free.

    T8 should keep us occupied for the rest of the summer, but if I can swing a longer running Mazes and Minotaurs game, I'll be all over it.

    John

    \n The Thule Files, my ongoing Spirit of the Century campaign of fighting Nazi Cthulhoid occult conspiracies, is just starting up again tomorrow after a few weeks off, and I'm psyched. Everyone has now got the hang of the system enough that I just need to turn up to a session with a few antagonists, a general situation and an opening scene (always in medias res and always opening the the line "Thirty seconds ago, X seemed like a good idea...") and I can count on players to pump in all the energy they can.

    Next Tuesday I start running a new weekly PtA game, spun off from a one-shot I did a while back, called Mooks. The premise is that the prestigious Mooks recruitment agency -- purveyors of quality henchmen to evil overlords, mad scientists and supervillains the world over -- has just opened its door to a fly-on-the-wall TV crew, who will follow the lives of the men and women who make up the organisation, their clients and the staff they place. When I ran the one-off, the Venture Brothers hadn't aired; now it's difficult to imagine this game without its influence.

    I'll be doing some playtesting over the next few weekends, with Lost Days of Memories and Madness this weekend and, possibly, Dirty Secrets the week after.

    Last Tuesday I ran the Unknown Armies one-shot, Jailbreak, with a full complement of nine players. I pitched it as halfway between a tabletop game and a LARP -- I used props, didn't allow a gaming table and, having the use of a big room, encouraged people to move around and act things out. It was one of the most manic GMing experiences of my life and an absolute blast.

    Oh, and my friend Robin is going to start running his Cold City campaign a week on Saturday. I'm really looking forward to this. I played in a one-shot that Malcolm Craig ran at the last Concrete Cow, and I'm completely sold on the game.

    It's a full life! No wonder I feel tired the whole time.

      \n Mark Causey asked for enlightenment re Zombies in the Vineyard. ZomV is my adaptation of Dogs in the Vineyard mechanics to a modern setting with PCs being ordinary people facing the end of the world; I've run it three times so far and hope to do so at GoPlay NW. Here's the summary description I used for Kublacon:

      "When there's no more room in Hell the dead will walk the earth!" The thing I like best about zombie movies is seeing what desperate people will do under pressure, and whether there's some core of human decency that's more important to preserve than mere survival, or if staying alive no matter what is the only thing that matters.

      Plus brains being eaten and stuff.
      System based on DitV will be taught.

      Check out Have Games Will Travel podcast 77 to hear what it's like.

      \n Quick addendum: I'm using SotC to run a Nine Princes in Pulp game on roughly biweekly basis. We have did chargen a week ago and played the first big fight scene this weekend. Only did the one big fight, however:

      Five PCs.
      One named NPC enemy.
      Two groups of enemy mooks.
      Two groups of allied mooks.
      Roughly 28 zones on the map (over half of them aerial, about 15 of which saw heavy use.)
      About five declares and maneuvers (in five exchanges) to add environmental aspects and the like.

      That was our 'training wheels' fight. Yes it took awhile, but every exchange got faster and faster, and in the middle of the third exchange, one of the really not-rulesy people commented "you know, this is taking some time, but I'm having FUN."

      So... yeah. A good addition to my schedule.
