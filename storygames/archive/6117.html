\n Well, it's been more than a year in the works, but I can finally announce it:

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

BLUE DEVIL GAMES TO PUBLISH JOHN RAIN GAME

Leading Publisher Will Bring Barry Eisler\u2019s Best-Selling Assassin to the Game Table.

PLANTATION, FL \u2013 March 25, 2008 \u2013 Blue Devil Games and Barry Eisler have entered into a licensing agreement to publish table-top role-playing game products based on the author\u2019s award-winning novels featuring assassin John Rain.

\u201cRole-playing games are about creating stories,\u201d says game designer Justin D. Jacobson. \u201cMore than just gripping stories, intriguing characters, and realistic depictions of the art of combat, the John Rain novels get right in your face and demand: When you pull the trigger, how will it change you? These qualities make John Rain the ultimate source material for an exciting and thought-provoking game.\u201d

\u201cI've long thought that a \u2018natural causes\u2019 assassin like John Rain would be a great foundation for a terrific RPG, and I'm excited to be working with Justin and BDG to make it happen,\u201d says Eisler. Eisler\u2019s Requiem for an Assassin launched at #19 on the New York Times Best Seller List in 2007. Like its predecessors, Requiem depicts the life-and-death escapades of John Rain, the assassin who specializes in death by \u201cnatural causes\u201d. Eisler\u2019s seventh novel is slated for release in early 2009.

In Tokyo Rain, players to take the role of an assassin or his prey, building their own cat-and-mouse story, culminating in a showdown with their characters\u2019 very lives at stake. But, in the words of John Rain: Killing is the easy part. Tokyo Rain will feature a foreword by Eisler and will be available at major American booksellers and on-line by the end of 2008. Tokyo Rain will be a stand-alone game with an original system, tailored to suit the material. More information will be released shortly with details of the game and related products. Information is also available at www.johnraingame.com.

About Barry Eisler
Barry Eisler worked in the CIA\u2019s Directorate of Operations, then lived in Japan, where he earned his black belt at the Kodokan International Judo Center. The Rain books, featuring half-Japanese, half-American assassin John Rain, have won the Barry and Gumshoe Awards; have been included in numerous \u201cBest Of\u201d lists; have been translated into twenty languages; and are the basis for a forthcoming film. www.barryeisler.com

About Blue Devil Games
The solo enterprise of award-winning game designer Justin D. Jacobson, Blue Devil Games publishes \u201cfiendishly clever\u201d table-top games in both print and electronic format. Feature products include the forthcoming Tokyo Rain and the five-time ENnie-nominated Dawning Star science-fiction setting. For more information visit www.bluedevilgames.com.

    Posted By: Jason MorningstarWow, congratulations Justin! That's an exciting development. Also: Is this Bullseye? Different thing? More details!
    \nSomething new to emulate the books. I've been doing a lot of noodling in the past few months, and I'll be doing some DevLog posts of course as I go along. For starters, it will be a head-to-head, competitive game. As I say in the Overview: One of you is predator; one of you is prey; and only one of you can win.

    Obviously, nothing is set in stone yet, but some game elements I've already got in my sights are: BE's location-based combat rules (i.e., Firefight!); SotC's use of zones; Sons of Liberty's battle modes; Riddle of Steel's strategic combat.
