\n I'm looking for advice and feedback.

I'm taking this model to a few smaller conventions this Spring/Early summer.

Bring a bag of games that I can run and am super-excited about.
Let players choose which game or games they want to see.
Play through an hour of the game in question, highlighting why I think it's awesome, what I believe it does really well.
Encourage them to play it more, go buy it, read up about it, etc.
Move on to another game and repeat.

The last few times I brought folders, like fancy printed menus. Each game got a page, with "back of the box" type text.
This worked okay.

The last time I took a framed poster. It also worked okay.

I'm no expert in human factors or design or making things look nice and friendly. So I'm here hoping someone will have an opinion or suggestion on what they thing would work.

    \n Rad idea! Layout rec: left-align each title and description with the cover picture. Bring the italic subtitle right up under the title (right now it's confusing about whether the italics refer the the title below, because of proximity). Either bold the titles or make the subtitles a discernibly smaller point size.

    Also, maybe put the go-play logo on the bottom of the poster. Keep the Games On Demand title on top, and then some white space or a subhead ("You Pick, We Play" or something less lame, perhaps). That way the info you actually want people to read will be higher on the poster. The logo will still stand out at the bottom, I think.
