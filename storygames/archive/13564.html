    \nApocalypse World has a set of Agendas (3).
    From those Agendas there spring a bunch of Principles (between 10-18 or so).
    Those Principles lead you to a bunch of MC Moves, which you use to explore the Stakes for your Fronts (MC Created).

    \n Right now Minions, Inc. is more of a setting for Apocalypse World (like Dark Sun, Greyhawk, or Forgotten Realms is a setting for D&D). There are some new rules and tweaks as to how things work, but fundamentally it is just Apocalypse World.

    Kevin: For copyright reasons I could not just cut and paste pages 108 - 150 of the Apocalypse World book, but you should assume that they are actually a part of the Minions, Inc. rules. Those pages include the chapters: Master of Ceremonies, The 1st Session, and Fronts.

    To answer your broader questions, I will try to give you my perspective as to how I intended the game to be played.

    Game Setup
    1) Have the players choose their playbooks, but don't have them make their characters yet.
    2) As a group go through the Employer Creation steps together. All of the choices about the employer are made by the players, rather than by the MC solely. Basically, this is the process that will create the setup for your game.
    3) Once the employer has been built, the players can now create their characters and the MC can flesh out the Employer, his specific goals, and what he needs them to do. You may find that a lot of this process has already been done just through the natural brainstorming that comes from making an employer.

    Playing the Game
    There is a lot of flexibility in how this part goes and it is all going to be dependent on your Employer and your group's style. Here are some styles of play:
    \u2022 Day in the Life - What is daily life like in the lair? Think Venture Bros with 21 & 24 as the Main Characters
    \u2022 Minion Impossible - The Henchmen take on a more "elite" role and are sent out to do missions for the employer.
    \u2022 Employer Directed - The Employer goes out to do things and drags his minions along and then sends them to do things as necessary (typically by screaming things like, "Kill him!", "Defend me!", or "Bring me the girl!"
    \u2022 Employer Management - The Employer is an idiot and needs his Minions to keep him alive (so they stay employed) or he is competent and deadly and they need to secretly thwart him (so they can stay employed and not dead)
    \u2022 Some combination of all of these

    One of the big decisions here though is tone. Is the Employer silly and cartoony (the Monarch - Venture Bros., Dr. Drakken - Kim Possible), or is he deadly and menacing (Apocalypse - DC, Any Bond Villain, Kingpin - Marvel, etc ...)

    Remember: Vincent says it best. Your agenda as the MC is:
    \u2022 Make Minions, Inc. seem real.
    \u2022 Make the players\u2019 characters\u2019 lives not boring.
    \u2022 Play to find out what happens.

    From my point of view the Minions main goal (probably) is to not die
    \u2022 Don't get killed on the job
    \u2022 Don't get in the hero's way if you can avoid it (they can't)
    \u2022 Don't piss off your employer so he doesn't try to kill them.

    Play the game. Have fun with it. If it doesn't make sense, ignore it (and then tell me about it.)

    I hope that this helps.
