\n A couple of weeks ago, I saw some gamers playing Apples to Apples in between games. It was great to fill breaks because you can deal in and deal out players pretty much whenever you like. Are there any story games out there where you can add or remove players at will? (Microscope strikes me as a possibility)

EDIT: To clarify, I'm looking for games where you can add players mid-session - i.e. a new player walks past the table mid-game, you say come and join us and you include him, equally players can walk off at any time. Useful for cons and the like.

    \n I've had to wrestle with this in convention environment; the ideal introductory RPG is one where you can just keep a game rolling and draw people in and have them drop out fluidly, without specific starting and stopping times. The requirements are that players can pick it up quickly when they come in, the game adapts to a wide range of player numbers ("2-10" has been my testing requirement) and the game holds interest for longer periods of time as well (if for no other reason, then because the GM is going to go crazy if the game just keeps repeating the same 15 minute loop all day long).

    The two games I've identified as ideal (forgoing specialized solutions like Parsely that forego too much of the ordinary rpg structure) are old school D&D (go with lethal, quick treasure, and keep real-time track of xp so players can compare and compete among themselves) and Fables of Camelot, a game we specifically designed for the purpose a couple years back with Sami Koponen.

    Microscope would work, too, although you'd need to add a bit of procedure about how established information keeps being transmitted to new players. I would suggest adding some steps or moves where new players get to point at established elements and have other players recite interesting lore about them. This could get interesting when the information gets more and more vague, being second- or third-hand as the players who originally established it have been long gone from the game. And of course it wouldn't hurt for something to get completely forgotten, only to be replayed, and then re-established as a player from earlier in the game comes back - that's history for you, conflicting accounts and all.

    Are you looking for suggestions for single session play or long term play? For single session, The Quiet Year would be pretty easy to do this with (as demonstrated in the forum game happening right here).
    \nWhile the drop-in/drop-out thing is working in that forum game, generally I think that The Quiet Year is a poor choice for this kind of social arrangement. The game relies on ritualistic elements, the text asks to be read aloud at the start of the game, context is vital for making sense of most Quiet Year maps, and so much of the communication in the game consists of reading context clues.

    (but as stated, it's working in the forum game! I'm kind of surprised by how well it's working.)

    \n Action Castle and other Parsely games work just fine with people walking by, throwing in a command, then walking away when they get bored. Easy.

    Do: Pilgrims of the Flying Temple will let you play several short letters in an hour or two, and character creation is dead simple, so people can easily join for a brief time and then leave when a letter is complete. (I'm not sure if entering/leaving mid letter would cause problems with the stone's math or otherwise break things, but I'm leaning toward it being okay as well.)
