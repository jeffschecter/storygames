You Know What They Say: Once you go Indie...

Josh Roby
September 2006 edited September 2006 in Story Games
So there's, you know, talk in other corners of the internet, about "indie" sales versus "mainstream" sales and how indie sales are a drop in the bucket to mainstream and blah de blah blah blah. Now, that we will not be getting accurate sales figures, um, ever, is sort of a foregone conclusion. However, I'm wondering about a trend that I'm seeing -- or hallucinating.

See, I stopped buying "mainstream" games a while ago. I mean, like, stopped dead. Last one was Blue Rose, years ago. Now, I know there are folks who straddle the fence, but I'm curious:

Since you bought and played your first indie game...
1) ...have you continued to buy the same amount of mainstream game books? More mainstream game books? Fewer mainstream game books? No mainstream books?
2) ...have you continued to buy the same number of game books total? More game books total? Fewer game books total? No game books at all?
3) ...have you continued to spend the same amount of money on RPGs? More money? Less money? No money at all?

I leave the indie/mainstream divide to the standards of the surveyee. While I'd certainly like to hear explication, kindly give one of the provided answers first, then explicate, like so:

1) I buy no mainstream books. I browse the mainstream titles, but it's more like watching a trainwreck than shopping. The thought of purchase doesn't even occur to me.
2) I buy more game books total. I cannot get enough, and there are too many games coming out to play!
3) I spend more money total. Now, this may also have something to do with me having money to spend, but indie gaming has also changed my spending priorities. I'd rather get an indie game than go to the movies, for instance. Not so with mainstream games.

Obviously, if you've never bought and played any indie games, your data is not relevant to the question. Not that the totally non-indie crowd isn't good enough for this survey or anything, but I'm curious if indie games are replacing mainstream games for others like they are for me. Who knows, maybe I'm just a freak.

So tell me, story-games: once you go indie, is there any going back?
