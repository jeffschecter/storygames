Gay Snap

Graham
December 2006 edited December 2006 in Game Design Help
Here's an alternative Snap-based mechanic for The Gay Recruitment Squad Wants YOU! It's meant to be lighter and more fun than the previous rummy-based mechanic.

Does it make sense? Can you see any flaws or ambiguities? Does it sound fun?
Each player gets dealt a card, which is their Skill Card. Each player also has a pack of cards, which they hold face down. The GM doesn't have either a Skill Card or a pack of cards.

Then, starting with the player to the left of the GM and going round, each player throws the top card of their pack, face up, into the centre of the table.

If this card has the same number as your Skill Card, you can claim it, by putting your hand on it. If you claim the card before anyone else does, you win your challenge.

However, the GM will also try to claim the card. If he claims the card first, you lose your challenge.

Also, if another player has the same number on his Skill Card as you, he will try to claim the card. Only one of you may win the challenge: whoever claims the card first wins.

Carry on dealing cards, going round the table, until everyone has won or lost their challenge.

Assisting

Rather than taking on a challenge yourself, you can choose to assist someone else in their challenge.

If you do this, you don't get a Skill Card, but you are allowed to claim cards on behalf of the person who you are assisting. Of course, they can claim cards too.

Chanting

If you successfully complete your Regimental Chant before the challenge, you can claim a card on behalf of any other player. Also, they may claim a card on your behalf.

Only one player may attempt a Regimental Chant before the challenge. If a player attempts a Regimental Chant and fails, no-one gets the benefit of chanting; similarly, if two players attempt a Regimental Chant, everyone loses the benefit of chanting.
Any good? Any tweaks to suggest?

Graham
