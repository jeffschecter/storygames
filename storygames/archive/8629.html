\n So, I'm interested in how my efforts to market this game worked. All I know is that I sold 36 copies in 30 days, with a very large percentage of buyers (maybe all, for all I know) coming from S-G. I am pretty sure that many of my sales were due to general interest in Jared's contest, and not because of anything about Out There itself.

My efforts:
I put actual play threads here, on the Forge, EnWorld, and RPGnet, and worked out an independent playtest post on RPGnet.
I linked from all these to a very simple web page I made, which included: 1) brief, basic game color, 2) list of core play dynamics, 3) cover image, 4) PayPal button, 5) ordering notes (file size etc.).
With each order I fulfilled (via email), I provided a link to my blog's Out There page, and requested, essentailly, "If you write about this game, tell me!" (I hope this wasn't annoying. I was succinct...) To date, no replies.

I'm mainly starting this thread to help me learn more about marketing RPGs. Feel free to wander off the specific question in the thread title if it's within that spirit.

Thanks,
-David

    \n First I thought I hadn't seen a picture of it - but then I remembered I had; I even wrote a forum post about the contest games where I included the Out There cover.

    Usually, things that grab me are that one sentence, that one evocative picture, or that one designer name; I bought The Five mostly because of the cool layout + the pictures of dominoes, and Thy Vernal Chieftains because it's by Paul Czege (and a solo effort, which I liked).

    There was nothing about the cover that grabbed me. It's dark, but not in a noir way, not in a cool b/w way, and not in a goth way - just a lot of black. I can't make out what the greenish textures are, and from the quick glances I gave the cover, they gave me the (subliminal/subconscious) impression that the image was smudgy or unclear. These one-second fleeting impressions are everything in marketing.

    The concept was never entirely clear to me. Paranormal investigation or something, yeah? I've played some games like that, and they always seem to go for a silly and light-hearted feel.

    The title doesn't stand out from the crowd. "Out There" is probably a good description of the game, but it doesn't put images - or, more importantly, questions - in my hed. "Thy Vernal Chieftains" makes me go "what's Vernal? and why 'thy'?"

    So, all in all: The cover didn't do it for me, and there was nothing else to grab me when I browsed some random threads.

    Posted By: David BergMatthijs, thanks for the cover & title critique. As for the concept being unclear, would you mind explaining what you mean specifically?
    \nHm, I guess I was being unclear. Sorry about that.

    What I mean, I think, is that even though the concept itself is clear enough - paranormal investigation - it doesn't stand out from other concepts. I vaguely remember thinking "couldn't I just do this with PTA?" I don't think I ever read the details you mention (no prep etc).

    So, quite honestly, it looks like I never actually got to the point where I read how the game plays. I stopped at "bland cover, familiar concept", and never went past that.

    I know that sucks! But I'm being brutally honest. There are way too many cool games out there now - I probably have a dozen games on my shelf that I haven't tried yet, and there are free games on the net I want to try out. New stuff is being thrown out all the time. Doesn't matter if your game plays well; if it doesn't make me sit up and notice before I even know what it's about, chances are I'll forget it as soon as I hear about it.

    I would have noticed covers with images like the ones below, for example - even though some of them are really crap, they have personality, and personality is more than enough reason for me to spend a buck.
