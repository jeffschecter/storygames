I've been using Storygames to quiz people on little design ideas as they happen - I hope nobody finds this too irritating. It's useful as hell for me, anyway.

So if you are inclined to help me out, let's come up with some killer dualities for the key qualities of opposing but concordant Norse-flavored Gods. Both choices must encompass a range of good and bad possibilities. The one I'm working with right now is Peace and Freedom - both possibly good, possibly bad, and in a Norse world-view pretty much opposed. Ideally I'd like to come up with a bunch of strong possibilities and let the players choose which one they want their game to be about. Any pairs spring to mind?

    The Viking virtues were thought, in general, to be: Honesty, Hospitality, Courage, Loyalty, Cleverness, and Luck. Inverting them gives you some neat vices: Dishonesty, Inhospitality, Cowardice, Disloyalty, Gullibility, and Ill-Fortune. (I did this for an optional Virtue & Vice system in Dead Inside.)

    Now, the problem is that the way the vices are phrased is overly negative, and you want a range of good/bad factors for each. But a lot of the examples above can be adapted into this, or the vice names can be altered to things like: Politeness, Practicality, Cautiousness, Independence, Trust, and Preparation.
