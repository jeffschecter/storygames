    \n Thanks, and I'll go about answering questions now.

    So, the fourth one... this is gonna take awhile to explain, but hopefully it'll prove worthwhile. I make layers, but in order to make layers("layers")in MSPaint, you've got to craft an image and use the color white, as a sort of "green screen" material, that the program then recognizes as transparent. So, I started off making these really small fake transparent images using this "the color white as transparent" method. The little white squares I'd use, just evolved into little white triangles and sometimes little white cockeyed squares. I work in layers, but they're incredible time consuming layers. But yes, there's a bit of method and math to the fourth image. I make up my own rules while doing art. I think ya have to make up a method. I'm sure game designers have a personal method, that they play with(or against).

    I use MSPaint because I am too backwards to use GIMP or Photoshop. It serves it's purpose, and I'm sure the crazy amount of time it takes plays into my enjoyment of using it.

    The second one actually has less of a basis then the newer ones. It's all based off of glitches with the program really. It's pushing against the function of the thing.

    Some of these images are several years apart, so my techniques and habits have changed.

    This one
    uses a similar techinque. I start with an outline of a shape, fill it in, then outline another shape. The interplay of the bucket tool is what gives it it's quality.

    \n Thank you Christopher(really, thanks), but I am very slow to understand the need for tools beyond first developing the needs. I'm mostly a purpose should define the tools first kinda guy.
    It breaks down something like this; me>purpose>tool. I mean, it happens the other way certainly, but I make an attempt to fight it. The machines will win, and it's no sense being mashed about their gears. Like, I think the newer version of MSPaint is nice for having an intuitive zoom function, but in order to make a perfectly diagonal line, it's much harder then in the version of Paint in XP. So, I use the Paint version in XP to make the frameworks, then I open them up in Windows 7. That's my level of technological rigidity. Before I was less anal-retentive, I would just freehand(mouse?)stuff. I should maybe go back to that at some point?

    Who knew one could have opinions on various versions of Paint?
    I like that it's an everyman kinda tool. I'm not painting anything life-like so I don't need as many bells and whistles, but if you have bells and whistles, I'll take them. Actual bells and whistles.
    It looks like an image crafted with a computer program. It's not trying to look like an oil painting.

    So, in short, I am surprisingly attached to MSPaint.
