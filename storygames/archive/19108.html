\n Spun off from the Ars Magica thread:\n
I really want to try Ben Robbins Kingdom in the Ars Magica setting. It seems like a game to jump directly of the good parts of an Ars Magica campaign.
\n@CoveredInFish we did almost exactly that at Go Play NW this year. Me, @tony_dowler, @SevenSidedDie and Doug-whose-username-I-can't-find made an Ars Magica-esque conclave as our Kingdom: the Covenant of Librum Nox. It was supposed to mean "Book of Night" but we might have muffed our Latinese.

It was pretty fantastic. We had a library-labyrinth at the heart of our enclave. Only the wisest mages could pass its hidden wards and find their way to the innermost chambers (and the most arcane tomes). We were low-key most of the game, keeping our magic hidden from the world around us. We wanted no part in the petty affairs of kings, but all the land around us was in danger of getting pillaged by the armies of feuding Dukes and warlords.

Our final Crossroad was whether we should abandon our low profile and awaken the giant that slept beneath the mountain and use it to destroy the armies. It was our ancient duty to ensure that the mountain giant slumbered unseen -- a legend from myth only -- but hey, drastic times, drastic measures.

Prediction: if we awaken the giant, we won't be able to control it. Good times.
