    \n Hey Rob, I'm really excited about your podcast. In fact, I'm going spend my pre-superbowl time listening to all of your excellent interviews, but I have one question / suggestion. Right now, your format seems to be "interview PERSON about GAME THEY WROTE" and I think that tends to reinforce the existing bias towards prioritizing game design and publication among the indie games community. Is there any chance of you interviewing "indie insurgents" without games to sell? Like, I would pay money to hear an interview with John Kim or Don Corcoran or Carrie Bernstein or Matthijs Holter or Mo Turkington or Brand Robins or Jess Hammer or that Jenskot guy who keeps posting in this thread just to name a few.

    Posted By: Jonathan WaltonThat's totally cool. Maybe that's another podcast or something. Just something to think about, since I can't think of many podcasts that don't focus on designers and publishers.
    \nWell, there's Sons of Kryos. I think that its dominance in that area is one of the reasons I didn't go that way. Also I think the audience I'm making my show for most primarily is other game designers, who could learn from the experiences of the people I'm interviewing. I know I've already had someone thank me for asking about how to choose a printer because he's found the responses very helpful to him.

    Anyway, one of the benefits of not having a mission statement in every episode is that the theme/purpose/whatever is only implied rather than explicit. It allows me to turn on a dime and once I have my identity pretty well established I can engage in the kinds of experiments you suggest here.

    Thanks again.
