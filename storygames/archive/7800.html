\n After reading all the crazy stuff about Matt and IP, I figured I'd open up a bit and give a real candid view of why I find Matt's decision to (possibly) permanently remove his works from public hands to be fundamentally destructive. I struggle a lot with the issue of legacy. If I could bottle every important experience I've ever had and distribute it to others I would.

One of those experiences was discovering The Forge. I am still in this hobby because of the ideas developed at The Forge. I would not be, otherwise. But it did a lot more than enable my continued participation in this "fringe" insignificant hobby. The concepts developed there trickled out into other aspects my life. At the time of encountering The Forge I pretty much hated fiction. The concepts at The Forge completely defined my view of fiction such that I could love it again. Sorcerer in particularly redefined my concepts of "fantasy fiction" and "hardboiled detective" novels. By redefining my view of fiction and my relationship with fiction, over time I've had a rather fundamental shift in how I view humanity as a whole. And of course any shift in your view on humanity is going to affect how you interact with the world in general.

So yes, encountering The Forge and the ideas there has had a pretty radical impact on my life beyond "gaming." I'm a happier, more confident, more positive and more creative person because of it. So, the idea that someone who was in a similar disgruntled, unhappy mindset as I was back then and DOESN"T find that experience, scares the shit out of me. If I could bottle the experiences that lead to that paradigm shift and pass it out at conventions I would. The closest I come to that is running the games I enjoy most in the manner I enjoy most at conventions so that I know there is always a place local to me where maybe that cynical gamer might stumble across my table, "looking for something different" and actually find it.

However, I also know there is no one thing that may inspire that light bulb. I mean the initial light bulb for me actually went off when reading a game that had nothing to do with The Forge: Story Engine. The way things were presented in that book really began the wheels turning in the right direction that lead to the insights necessary for me to play Sorcerer effective. So I realize that in the absence of being able to bottle my experience I need access to as many different approaches as possible. TSOY will speak to someone while Dirty Secrets will speak to someone else.

So when Matt says, "my stuff will no longer be available" all I can think of is the handful (and that's probably all it will be) of people for whom Dust Devils or Nine Worlds would have been *it*, the thing that spoke to them the way Story Engine & Sorcerer spoke to me. But because they got there too late it won't happen. A handful of people who never get the jolt I did and continue down the path of cynical unhappiness or as Ron sometimes puts it, "fun someday."

Yes, I realize this is the "But think of the CHILDREN!" argument and that is rather flawed. But it's still how I *feel*. It's an emotional response, not necessarily a rational one. I also believe it has some merit as an academic ethic.

So, you may now proceed to think I'm an angst ridden nut ball. But thank you for listening.

Jesse
