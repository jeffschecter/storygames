    \n Matthijs, here's a basic description of the kinds of articles I want for Push. This may change somewhat after Push 2 is done and I have a chance to reassess:

    \xe2\u20ac\xa2 How We Play (Macro) - This type of article describes a large community or (multi)national style of roleplaying: Nordic larp, Jeepform, Japanese roleplay, online freeform, fanfic, MMORGs, cosplay, etc.

    \xe2\u20ac\xa2 How We Play (Micro) - This type of article describes lessons learned and techniques developed by individual groups during play or designers during the design/play process. These could range all over the place, covering just about anything.

    \xe2\u20ac\xa2 How We Play (Meta) - This type of article describes recent or historical developments in design and play, analyzing trends, assigning meaning, projecting into the future. Discussions need to be backed up by strong sources and examples, not just empty theorizing.

    \xe2\u20ac\xa2 How We Play (Transcript) - This is not an article at all, but an actual transcript (edited for clarity) of real, honest-to-God actual play. Online play transcripts are the easiest, but recording and transcribing tabletop works too. Finding a way to document larp play would be terrific, but difficult. Same with MMORGs and other things. We don't have any of these for Push 2, unfortunately, so I may end up finding a transcript of something I've played in to include. I have a few candidates I'm sorting through, but if people have stuff they want me to look at, I'll see what I can do, even though it's kinda last minute for Push 2.

    I'm also still interested in publishing a limited number of short games in each issue. Push 2 has Eero's memory palace game and Bill's marketing game, which are both super awesome and very different from what we typically think of as roleplaying games.

    So, yeah, Matthijs, your proposed concept certain fits the Micro category. I'd love to hear more about it... in a couple more months

    Chris (Caesar), I haven't done much layout for the book yet, so I'm not sure about page count and price point. I do know that we're sticking to the same 7.5" x 7.5" square format with sidebar commentary. I imagine volume 2 will end up being somewhat thicker. This may raise the price if I stick with Lulu, though if I can get a reasonable, small print run (say 100-200 copies) done somewhere, the price might not go up as much. We'll see.

    I have the cover art in, so maybe I'll layout a mock cover soon, but I think the contributors who've sent in drafts really deserve redlines first. The cover for volume 2 is exceptional and continues the tradition of Push experimenting with non-traditional roleplaying art. The last one was an action shot involving a bucket (non-traditional subject matter). This one is non-traditional from a formalistic perspective (Bethany Culp is amazing).

    Hmm... most excited by...

    Honestly, while Rob's article is way cool, I'm most excited about the articles on online freeform (forum-based) play, that is, the edited version of Sarah Kahn's interview with Thomas Robertson and Madeline Klink's article on being a star in Harry Potter fanfic/roleplaying communities. I feel like that's a set of roleplaying interests that we don't interact with very much and I'm really hoping we can build some bridges there.
