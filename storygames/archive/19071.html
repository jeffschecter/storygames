\n The Google Plus is overflowing with this year's Epimas announcement, but I couldn't see anything here on SG.

It is live now at epimas.wordpress.com/

Epimas is awesome. You buy games in PDF format as gifts to friends, and you also get copies for yourself so you can come prepared and ready to play when you meet.

Games participating this year:

Anima Prime by Christian Griffen
Bulldogs! By Brennan Taylor
carry by Nathan Paoletta
The Daughters of Verona by Wilhelm Person
Dirty Secrets by Seth Ben-Ezra
Dust Devils Revenged by Matt Snyder
Faith by Daniel Hodges
Fortune\u2019s Fool by Pantheon Press
Full Moon by Paul Stefko
Grey Ranks by Jason Morningstar
Heroine by Josh T Jordan
Misericord(e) by Emily Care Boss
Misspent Youth by Robert Bohl
Montsegur 1244 by Frederik Jensen
Our Last Best Hope by Mark Diaz Truman
Pirateas by Frida Ullenius
Poison\u2019d by Vincent Baker
Psi*Run by Meguey Baker
Spark RPG by Jason Pitre
Thou Art But a Warrior by Anna Kreider

And if you get in on it, even at the lowest level, you'll also get this neat bundle of games.

Burning Opera by Giacomo Vicenzi
Hot Guys Making Out by Ben Lehman
Nanoworld by Marshall Miller
Revenant by Paul Stefko
Spin the Beetle by Vincent Baker
Superhero Bakery by Jason Morningstar
The Vast & Starlit Library by Epidiah Ravachol

After having been busy on my own projects the last couple of years I have lost touch with the scene in general.

But I recognize Montsegur 1244 in the list, it is an absolutely fantastic game, although not very full of X-mas spirit. :-) I can't recommend it highly enough. It is an important game about faith.

Pirateas was translated into English specifically for Epimas, and it is not available elsewhere. If you like friendly storytelling games about the stories told by tea drinking pirates you should take a look at it.

Epimas is the only way to get the full version of The Daughters of Verona, a storygame of Shakespearean comedy, in digital format. The printed version of the game is as expensive as it is brilliant. :-)
