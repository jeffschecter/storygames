\n I've been working on a library of tweaked scenarios for Hell for Leather, and this is the first one, called War of the Bard.



It's about six grievously wounded heroes and their race to save the world. Here's the blurb:

One hundred heroes were sent to the mountain lair of Torc\xe0n Olc to read from the Book of Undoing.

Only six survived.

Now, as the great volcanoes boil behind them, they must bring the knowledge home.

Jason submitted some really lovely artwork. There's also an experiment with audio commentary. Sorry about the accent.

    \n Here's the second book for the library, called Flight of the Huguenots (by Eoin Corrigan).



    It's about four persecuted Catholics during the St. Bartholemew's Day Massacre in 16th Century Paris. Here's the blurb:

    Thousands of worshippers are being massacred for their virtue and Paris reeks with the stink of burnt flesh.

    Four friends cower in a shadowy side street, desperate to flee the religious pogrom.

    As the mob descends upon them, they must flee the country to find their freedom.

    There's more audio commentary here, though this time it's only a couple of minutes long.

    In an age of religious persecution, four Christians have had enough!

    Will you burn the Eternal City to the ground or will you die a martyr\u2019s death? Either way, glory awaits!

    The audio commentary is flaky. German and Irish accents!
