So I've been banging together this Ludolab thing. There are about 30 people signed up for the site, but as Joao said on the fora there: "Ok, now what?".

Which is a damn great question, and I've been barking around for an answer before finally disseminating (and asking for help disseminating) the word about the place to all and sundry. What I've come up with is a series of workshop 'courses', based around the following three themes:

I. Bring Your Own Design (Exchange). Everyone in the course brings their own idea to the table and everyone comments on all of the other designs. Then there are a series of structured activities to shake up each person's sacred cows and to encourage cross-pollenation.

II. Come As You Are (Tabula Rasa). Everyone in the cohort brings absolutely nothing to the table with them, and they develop a game from the concept up. Everyone is working on their own design, and commentary is required all-around at every exercise.

III. Frankengame. Everyone in the cohort brings nothing to the table, and they work on one concept, shaping a single project. Exercises in each round help shape a particular aspect of the game, and try to dislodge new ideas.

IV - VI. Worldbuilding (I - III).

Are these viable ideas? Are there too many?

I'm investigating a lot of workshop material trying to suss out a decent curriculum for workshopping. What I'd like to ask you folks is what sorts of things would you want to see covered in a game design course? What parts of game design were the hardest for you to come to grips with on your own?

I'm aiming for starting the first cohorts two weeks after Game Chef ends this year, to give myself some breathing room (I'm Game Cheffing like a BIATCH this year), and to give it a chance to get some airtime without running over Game Chef, either.

Finally, I'm thinking of setting up the whole thing as part of a ransom system. In order to participate in a cohort, you put $X ($10 - 15) in the pot for the cohort. At the end of the cohort, the folks who haven't dropped out split the pot between them, to encourage people to keep up with it. (I won't claim any of the funds myself this time. If it's at all successful, maybe down the road.)

Will this work, or am I crazy? Or am I crazy and this just might work?
