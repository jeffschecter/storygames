\n Some friends of mine and I are going to play a game of Monsterhearts in a few days. I'm still pretty new to story games (and gaming in general, to be honest) and one of the things I think I have the most trouble with is smoothly stepping in to the mindset of whoever I might be playing. All the skins/potential characters in Monsterhearts seem to boil down to their skewed relationships to power. From the forum discussions I've read so far it seems like all the sexual and social dysfunction intended to bubble up over the course of the game can be laid out in terms of heavily imbalanced power differentials, and the struggle to gain control and the upper hand.
\n
\nLuckily, I happen to have a lot of books about imbalances of power. Here's what I've been rereading from my shelves so far:
\n
\nLolita (Vladmir Nabokov), Les Liaisons Dangereuses (Choderlos de Laclos), three Robert Greene books (48 Laws of Power, The Art of Seduction, 33 Strategies of War - pretty trashy, but surprisingly useful for creating characters who adhere to different systems of morality and want different things from the people around them than you), Veronica (Mary Gaitskill), Philosophy in the Boudoir (Marquis de Sade), short stories by Angela Carter and Kelly Link, as well as a wide assortment of comics, horror manga/manhwa, folktales, mythology, and urban legends just to have direct imagery and archetypes to draw from should I need them. I've also been rewatching Buffy with a friend of mine who somehow managed to make it through twenty eight years of life having never heard of the show before, so that's been fun and rewarding.
\n
\nDoes anyone have any recommendations for a quick read/viewing of media they've found helpful for getting in the mood for Monsterhearts? I read the "let's fight over source material" thread on another forum and found it helpful, but now I'm looking for things that are more about psychological atmosphere and tug-of-war over social and romantic capital than direct references.

    \nIf you were to turn to a single piece of media to get into a Monsterhearts headspace, I'd recommend Ginger Snaps. Monsterhood as a metaphor for puberty, weird and uncomfortable power dynamics, conflicting loyalties and priorities, and a beautiful mix of mundane and supernatural problems. The way they treat lycanthropy as a metaphor for menstruation and sexual awakening? They strike the perfect note of being unsubtle without being ham-handed.\n

    If so, check out the movie Disco Pigs. Two almost-twins-almost-lovers struggle to find a balance between their delusional retreatist wonderland and the cold real world. Every moment feels like things could go really wrong, and that someone could lose their grip.

    Posted By: Ignotus
    I gotta say, the more I think of monsterhearts as the buffy rpg, the more excited I am about it.
    \nIf you like geeking out about this sort of thing (esp. figuring out what moves different media characters would have), then let me share with you my recent revelation about Xander. See, for months, I wonder what Xander's deal was. I thought at first that he was a Mortal, and then I tinkered around with a "best friend"-centric version of The Mortal. Then I explored the notion that he was a Hollow (as his whole deal is feeling like he has no meaning or role in the group). Finally, in a conversation with Josh Mannon, I realized what Xander's deal is.

    Xander is a Queen. He starts with The Clique (they're armed) and The Shield. His whole deal is being involved in the toughest gang around, even though he's clearly cut from a different cloth than they are. Instead of leading the clique like the average Queen, he plays them against his neediness. His out-of-Skin advances are Down the Rabbit Hole from The Mortal and Growing Pains from The Chosen.

    \nThat is a really, really interesting read on Xander's archetype. I totally agree that the "kick his ass" moment at the end of Season 2 was one of Xander's worst over the course of the show. However, on my personal list of "Alexander Harris' Top 5 Fuck Ups" I think it's sort of an outlier in the sense that in that moment he let a personal vendetta take over his judgment and his normal setting of "loyalty to the gang". The others (for me) almost all fall within that one episode about the hyena gang - cruel, targeted bullying, violence, and attempted sexual assault. I don't get the same feeling as much for the swim team and zombie jock episodes because it felt like in the context of those two groups Xander was pulled along in the current of the gangs without being an instigator of destruction or really even condoning the group's behavior (in "Go Fish" I think his role is pretty neutral as an undercover agent for the Scoobies, and in "The Zeppo" he protests most of O' Toole's decisions) but in "The Pack" you really see him embody the worst aspects of his character - and it totally works as The Queen.
