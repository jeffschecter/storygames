    Posted By: Ben LehmanNo one mistake my unhappiness with the way I talk as an insult to my fine interviewers or anything other than pure, uncut self consciousness, okay?
    \nHere's the deal as a podcaster: Honestly, say your ums, uhs, and everything.

    Seriously, say it as naturally as you can. Now, if I can (and am willing to) edit out some of those, awesome, but I would rather leave them in than editing them out in a way that makes you sound unnatural. As a podcaster I've become pretty sensitive to such vocal artifacts, but when it comes to guest doing it, I don't see a problem.

    It's when the hosts don't *and* when the host is talking directly to you than to a co-host that is sounds the most distracting to my ear. Guests are talking to a host, so it's more natural.

    Truthfully, I don't edit "ums" and "uhs" to make people sound better. That's a nice effect, and I wouldn't do it if it made people sound worse, but I primarily edit for time (and because I'm an obsessive editor). I have a rule that no Master Plan ep will go over 30 minutes (and broke that once begrudgingly), and I can usually get around 3 or 4 minutes trimmed from an interview by removing false sentence starts and things like that.

    But *everyone* speaks on the mic exactly like you're talking about, Ben. Natural speaking is like constantly uttering a rough draft -- it's always raw, unpolished, and -- just like a rough draft -- unable to satisfy the creator.
