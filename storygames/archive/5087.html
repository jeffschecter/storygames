\n In the game I'm writing right now (Quilt, a severe d20 hack) characters start at different levels (usually 3 to 5) based on how good their stats are. I'd like to do a Spirit of the Century background with various stages based on what level they get to, i.e.
\n
\nFor each level you have, write a paragraph about a previous adventure your character has gone on.
\n
\nLevel 1: Backstory
\nWhere do you come from? Did you know any of the other PCs? What were you brought up to become?
\n
\nLevel 2: First Adventure
\nDid you find adventure or did it find you? Who was your patron or mentor? What happened to them? If you didn't have a mentor or patron, how did you make it through your first adventure? Were any other PCs involved? How did this adventure change you?
\n
\nLevel 3: Trouble
\nLevel 4: More Trouble
\nLevel 5: Enemies
\nLevel 6: Allies
\n
\n
\nI don't think I"m the first one through this way - can someone give me guidance?

    \n I'm doing something similar with Van Dread.
    I kept Phase 1 as initial background, growing up, family, friends, etc
    Phase 2 is your first paranormal mission
    Phase 3 is your last paranormal mission that landed you in the asylum.

    I haven't been able to come up with more phases, but I'm working on it. Do you have to include alot of details, or are you ok with more freeform stuff? For example I'm using a mechanic where the player describes an object and spins a little tale around it and about that phase of his life.

    \n I've got some notes on an epic fantasy SOTC hack, where I integrate world & NPC building in with character generation. My five main strokes are:

    Phase 1: The person behind the legend
    Phase 2: Your Legend
    Phase 3 & 4: Guest-Starring
    Phase 5: The Quest Yet Unfinished

    In each one, I have questions about lands and NPCs.

    I threw up the notes (which are very much in note-form) online at: http://docs.google.com/Doc?id=dfn8n37p_60dj55txg7

    \n I'm doing something similar for the FATEhack I'm running at Dreamation (playtest next Saturday!), except instead of "phases" I presume all the characters begin the game together at a Council of Elrond-type deal and ask questions about how they got there. Players answer the questions and then select Aspects from among them; as they do so, they'll be adding world details and (perhaps unwittingly) giving me as GM grist for my own Aspect mill.

    1. Why have you come before Mindaemos, the King of Endysion? Has he summoned you into his presence, or have you come seeking audience on your own behalf? Has another sent you as a messenger? An emissary? Do you bear gifts to the King? Do you desire that he mete out justice, or mercy?

    2. Which of all his treasures do you desire? An enchanted sword that hangs amid his trophies of war? A map from among the trove of ancient scrolls he has collected from across the Isles? A bejewelled rod crafted in his workshops? Or his daughter, the Princess Filomela, cloistered within his palace?

    3. Why do you fear Dosdaemon, the Court Magician of Endysion? Are you leery of his origins in the Benighted Lands? Are you suspicious of his ambitions? Does he seem to exert an unhealthy influence upon the King?

    4. What is the name of the place from which you have come, and where is it near? Do you come from one of these places, or somewhere else?

    The Andalgon. A pastoral region of shepherds and farmers on the large island called Natyros, well-watered by rivers draining from the Aroballa Magys. Its chief city is Onalys, the City of Bridges.
    Aroballa Magys. The great forest land, home of the brown elves (minalvas). This area is the heart of the large island called Natyros. It is a cascade of serried hills, with cliffs and thickly wooded tablelands overlooking the Andalgon.
    Elandthal. A small island near unto Endysion. The mariners who live there still revere Madez, the old goddess of the sea, and do her honor in the ancient way.
    Sundalvallas. The lands of the silver elves (sundalvas). Rugged, mountainous, cloud-topped islands upon which castles are built to command the lowlands and from which silver elves clad for war occasionally descend to scour the land of mortal presence.
    Valienalvion. The island of the high elves (valienalvas). A mist-hidden isle somewhere amid the Sunlit Sea, it is supposed to hold airy white towers of delicate and filigreed aspect, wherein high elven scholars contemplate the heavens.
    Omimallas. The lands of the strangers; known also as Mierallas, the Benighted Lands, since the Fire of Analla cannot be seen from these cold barrens upon the far shores of the Sunlit Sea.
    Chenys. The isle of Chenys in the Benighted Lands is a wild, raw-edged land of soaring mountains and tangled forests, inhabited by a proud and strong-willed people. They are ruled by many petty kings whom they elect at need and who are often no better than bandit-chiefs. The druids of Chenys exert a moderating influence on the excesses of the Chenysi.

    5. Who are your people? Are you a Oer, that half-elven race of men whose ancestors came out of the Benighted Lands in time past memory, and were changed by what they beheld? A fair-haired and wild-hearted Lan who\u2019s come from across the sea to settle on the shores of the Camallan Isles? Or are you a dark elf (droghalvar) of black and bitter mien out of Omimallas? Are you a stone-skinned giant from the Munquilanvar or \u201cQuickstone Mountains\u201d? Or a diminutive minalvar\u2014a brown elf of the forest, with merry wits and antic disposition? Or are your people none of these?

    6. When in your life was your destiny foretold? Who spoke of it, and what was said? Did your mother proclaim at your birth that you would reclaim your dead father\u2019s patrimony? Did the witch who floated to shore in a boat of feathers when you were a boy touch you upon the head and prophesy your willing death at the hands of one who would love you?

    7. What have you brought with you to Endysion? A message from the ruler of your city? A riddle spoken by the old hermit you met in your travels? Nothing but your strong right arm? A company of archers, to war against the Silver Elves?

    8. What obstacles did you overcome on your journey? Pirates at sea? The resistance of your family? Your own doubts and fears?

    9. What has troubled you in your dreams of late? Did you dream of dragons mere nights ago? Did you hear mocking laughter as you wandered through a labyrinth of stone? Did you see stars fall from the night sky, or a procession of hooded torch-bearers descend silently down a spiral stair?

    10. What is your name? Oerish names have a vaguely Greek sound to them (Mindaemos, Dosdaemon) that you should strive for as well. Male names: Anaros, Babales, Caphryon, Diadaman, Ethalos, Ganathos, Inamon, Luthos, Malamon, Nanares, Ophanon, Pharyos, Rhoparithes, Sanalyon, Termagan, Umaros, Vinamos, Xeles. Female names are similar, but end in \u201c-a,\u201d \u201c-ia,\u201d or \u201c-ana,\u201d so Anaria, Babala, Caphryana, Diadama, Ethalia, etc.

    \n I just thought of this: Why not set some background stakes around some tasks - things that could have gone either way, and whether or not your character succeeded they still ended up here, but where those events shaped the character's perspective. I.E. - I fell in love with a girl, but did I woo her or lose her?
