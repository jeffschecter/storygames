    \n I love the Gaean Reach! Cruising the Galaxy in a well-appointed space yacht and touching down on a quaintly parochial backwater planet to enjoy the local color, or searching for habitable worlds in the Beyond in a one-man locator, or serving aboard a tramp freighter plying the space lanes--the Reach is a vast space in which each human being pursues his or her own singular lurulu.*

    I'd make a Handbook of the Planets similar in spirit to the tried-and-true Traveller world generation system, but colored by Vance's darkly whimsical perspective so as to produce oddball worlds and cultures, some charming and mannered, others brusque to the point of menace. If you liked, you could "drill down" to create places like Big Planet and Tschai, Planet of Adventure, as congeries of contrasting and possibly inimical societies and ecosystems. The game would support a picaresque and peripatetic narrative, a heavily romanticized sort of space opera.



    * a "special word from the language of myth," according to Vance. It seems to refer to the object of one's as-yet unrequited yearning, especially if that object is glimpsed but dimly and imperfectly by its pursuer.

    \n Three words: OH. HECK. YEAH.

    I would say that the GR game should allow color to be color and mechanize aspects of the cultures only when it serves a true-game purpose. Each planet should have some huge thematic issue that an interloper can go awry of, or clash with in some way. Any "rules" for crafting worlds should be very high-level and involve establishing those oddities of the world that make it a place of adventure. No charts of types an such unless they are there to show possibilities, not dictate and constrain.

    Also, an Effectuator or two couldn't go amiss, nor would a crew of delightful tramps ferrying pilgrims and cargo about the worlds of the Reach.

    \n I'm cool with lots of Maid-like tables, but as long as they are color-builders as opposed to that old sci-fi game saw of:

    "roll for local law level. This figure provides a modifier for social developments for TL4+ societies..."

    until you snore.

    Traveller did this to the point that worlds started out as these clinical and boring marbles you had to struggle to make fun or adventurous (of course, this was the game that thought adventure came from "shady guy hires you to (roll d6):" tables. MANY sci-fi games make this mistake. Borrow a page from Diaposora, I say.

    Most worlds in the GR are thematic more than well-documented. Color should rule. Now, charts for that color should be fast and loose, but vague and open to radical interpretation and willing misreading...

    \n I think the Story Arc mechanic from Ashen Stars is a must for the Gaean Reach. In space there are no goals, you have to bring your own. I'd like to see more about plotting and dramatic arcs. For example, depending on where your character is in its Story Arc gives it more or less plot immunity. You shouldn't die if you're just at the start but you can if it's your big scene. Things like that. A bit of PTA basically.

    Rules-wise I'm much more interested in the stuff surrounding the character than how one might resolve a test. Test resolution, whilst necessary is old hat. There are 14 million ways of doing it, most of them equivalent and fine. Obviously for such a game you'd want SPACEresolution but that's really just colour.

    So, for me, the big things are about plot and dramatic arcs, generating content on the fly, involving players in the creative process, all without being too tied down by resource management (such as FATE points in Dresden Files which rubs me up the wrong way YMMV).

    Also rules for running with or without a GM. Mortal Coil function fine under both regimes but there are differences. How can play be directed without the visible hand of the GM, who is the ultimate arbiter (if one is really needed) etc.

    Finally, how do you play this in a distributed fashion? How do you play over Skype, perhaps one GM per planet? How can player generated content be saved for others to use? Cloudsource the Gaean Reach!
