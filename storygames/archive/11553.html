    \n A collaboration of amateur designers and writers is exactly what we are doing with bySwarm. We just wrapped up our setting concept submissions on Saturday, and site members can submit reviews of the submitted concepts this week. The reviews are really a measure of how interested you are in contributing and what you think of the general concept. It's a pretty diverse set of RPG setting concepts. If you are interested in collaborating on a large project to potentially break into the industry, this is a great opportunity.

    \n Hey,

    I really like developing settings, situations and thematic content for games. I excel at describing the game that I want to play. I can write fairly well. I have some professional training in both communication and hands-on teaching, and can bring that expertise to a project.

    I suck at designing systems which do what I say they do. I don't have a head for math, I can't focus on detail-oriented work (especially when we're talking about refining material that I've been working with for a year, like late-stage game development), and I don't have an intuitive sense of mechanical crunch.

    I'd love to work with people who wanted to take my ideas and make them into tangible things.

    Thematically/premise-wise, I want to design games about:
    real people learning to see themselves in a new light, coming of age stories, cutesy fantasy, bizarre & dark wonderland mystery, resource-starved community building.

    Mechanically, these are the games I'm interested in:
    focused designs, narrativism-supporting systems, games that prioritize goals and beliefs (Keys, Beliefs, Agendas, etc), simple games.

    See past work: Perfect, Ribbon Drive
    See current projects: Heart of Ashes
