\n I\u2019m playing The Quiet Year with two friends of mine via play by forum on Gente che Gioca (an Italian forum).

During my turn as an action I start a new project. Fabio has a Contempt token and decides to use it for acting selfishly and says a part of the community that is working on another project has words of fire on the new project and threatens to keep the benefits of their project for themselves.

Martino, the other player, tell us he thought the use of the Contempt token should be used during the turn of the active player (instead that was mine, not Fabio\u2019s turn).

So the question is: when can you use Contempt tokens in order to act selfishly or to diffuse past tension?

    \n Well, I think the intent is that the whole Selfishly jazz is about the active player's own turn, and the Diffusing Past Tensions is about what happens of others. Now, that said, I think you might have just stumbled on a very entertaining drift that may bear delicious, dysfunctional fruit, but assuming I'm right about the intent of things you may want to discuss and decide actively amongst yourselves if that's a drift you want to go with.

    \n I would agree with Martino = Contempt Tokens do not allow you to speak out of turn. You can discard them during someone else's turn if they diffuse tension, and you can spend them while taking your own action to indicate/justify its selfishness.
