\n Unfortunately I am crap at coming up with ideas, so I turn to the community for inspiration. I'm thinking of post-apocalyptic scenarios - a modern setting, but the apoc itself is less relevant. Maybe it's zombehs, maybe a plague, maybe the economy bottomed out and everything went to pot. Now the protagonists are wandering around and looting and setting up safehouses and forming relationships with each other.

What are some common/uncommon events that could happen? Examples: wandering (hit a bandit-staged roadblock, find a stash of stuff, encounter a new character, weather turns bad) or chilling (supplies are running low, floodwaters rise, foreboding dreams). Think Oregon Trail, Night of the Living Dead, Sim City, Roadwarrior. If anyone can recommend a sourcebook or gamebook with a nice list of Things That Can Happen To Travellers/Settlers that would be awesome - most RPGs focus on random encounters instead of the vagaries of fate and luck.

    \n Think about things that the players need and remember the rule of 3's.

    The rule of 3's states that you're in trouble if you go:
    3 minutes without air
    3 hours without shelter (think hot, cold, wind, hail)
    3 days without water or sleep
    3 weeks without food

    \n If you're having trouble coming up with plot hooks, go back to the basics:

    Why do you want to play in a post apoc setting?
    What are the things that draw you to the setting?
    What influences (movies and such) made you interested in it?
    What are you hoping to accomplish by setting the game in a post apocalyptic world that you can't accomplish in a different setting?

    Hopefully at least some of those questions should lead you toward ideas.

    Another direction to look would be the players and their characters:
    What kinds of scenes have particular players enjoyed in previous games?
    What things get the players excited when they hear about the game you want to run?
    What are the dynamics of the party - are there interesting conflicts and hooks there? If there aren't, could there be?

    And, to take that to more of an extreme, you could ask your players directly exactly the types of things they want to see happen.

    If none of this works, search through the various post apoc threads on RPG.net. There's lots of them, and lots of ideas there. But I would encourage you to start with your own and your players' interests first.
