\n How do you make a good character sheet for your game? Can this be done in Word (or a similar easily accessed program) or do I need a design program. What makes a sheet useful at teaching about the game?

The game I'm trying to design a sheet for has a variable number of Traits (some preset for genre, others created by the characters), some Traits will have a short descriptor Aspect that can be tagged for a bonus and Passions (which drive character development). The game is simple enough that it could be used nearly freeform or a LARP. If I design the sheet so it would fit on a blank index card is there a way to print it using Word etc?


A sample character:

Petra (20 points)

Apparently loyal assassin with a shadowy past; although he looks human, he is really a demon bound in human form to serve the crown. He is patient in setting up the assassinations and will do anything necessary to win in a fight. The Rune Blade contains another demon, very bloodthirsty and destructive.

Traits
Strength (7) - Stronger than he looks
Warfare (6) - Ambush tactics
Endurance (1)
Psyche (1)
Assassin (5) - Patient and deadly
Items (2) - Blood and Souls for the Rune Blade
Stuff (-2) - Bound to the King's will

Passions
Find a way to be released from my bondage (5)

Note: When on assassination run usually has an effective Trait of 18 if attacking with Sword (Warfare 6 + Assassin 5 + Rune Sword 2 + Bonus for Most Specific Trait 1 + 4 Aspect Bonuses)

    \n Oh, and its diceless. The resolution chart would be cool to fit on the sheet as well as what you can spend Stuff on.

    Spending Good Stuff\n
      \n
    • Buy a Trait (either develop in story or with back-story, work with GM to develop story hooks associated with it)
    • Create/Claim/Contest an Item, Position, Ally, Enemy, Birth or Shadow
    • Buy off Bad Stuff Trait
    • Declare a Fact or Set a Scene (Subject to GM approval)
    • Influence Conflict
      • \n
    \nFor conflict total everything together and compare final Traits to get results:\n
      \n
    • x2 = Fast Fight. Taken Out (At their mercy. If live, you have a Consequence that requires either the Winners permission or Good Stuff and a Story to eliminate.)
    • less than x2 = Normal Fight. Lose with Consequence (at least a Session and Good Stuff or Story to eliminate). Winner Concedes a Minor Detail.
    • Tie = Slow Fight. Each may bring in complementary or secondary Traits to break Tie. Lose with Consequence (at least a Session to eliminate). Winner Concedes a Major Detail.

    \n Whatever word processor you use (I just checked OpenOffice) has a setting for printing Avery 5388, which is pre-perfed index card material in single sheets. You could buy the crazy expensive cardstock from Avery, or you could use the template to print to plain paper and clue it to cardstock. Or you could write on index cards with a pencil or something.

    \n Scott,

    I once used MS Paint to design a character sheet (Mage Blade), and on two other occasions, have used MS Paint to create copies of a character sheet for existing games that I did not have as convenient digital files. (Gamma World 4th Ed and Star Wars D6, respectively)

    I also sometimes use Notepad to keep character data, and have printed it out on occasion.

    I do not recommend this program, but it does allow for pixel by pixel editing if you're a stickler for exact spacing et al.

    I have also seen Excel (or any other spreadsheet program) used to good effect.

    Personally, I'll probably use some layout program, whatever seems most cost effective once the time comes to create the finalized product for my games.

    It's even possible you could use PowerPoint, though I think Word/Works might still be better.

    \n A lot of printers that can print photo-film can print directly to index cards. It's something that you just need to test, and then set up the paper size yourself, but if it works, it's incredibly convenient _and_ cheap.

    Honestly, Word tends to be fine. I'll use more graphics-heavy programs sometimes for the sheer fun of it, but Word can very easily make attractive, easily readable character sheets.

    \n I hate to say it (as the program is so cheesey in nature), but PrintShop Pro works fine for me. Its kind of a text-cad-lite. I don't need to spend a huge amount of time learning InDesign or Scribus, and it outputs to PDF. You can pick up an older version for $10-$20 bucks at the local Staples or Office Despot.

    I wouldn't want to put anything of length together with it, but for 1 or 2 pages its fine.
