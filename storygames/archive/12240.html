    \n Hmm, a few thoughts. PDQ does allow characters to become very competent in particular areas by combining Qualities. You should also look at giving them challenges outside these comfort zones. It doesn't allow levels of success though, to show that they're getting better still, which is why I tried adding the Mastery optional rule.

    The basic premise of the MODs and target numbers is that you have an even chance of beating someone with the same Rank as yourself. I think that's sound.

    Fundamentally, to challenge a character in their area of competence you need a conflict situation with a significant opponent.

    PS - folks commenting on S7S - remember that it's a substantial reworking of the system.

    \nWell, I look at Qualities quite in the same vein as I look at Aspects from SotC or Beliefs and Instincts from Burning Wheel. They are big flags waving showing me what the players are interested in. Especially with the idea of a Quality being downgraded first in a conflict leads to a story hook. So to put up conflicts that do not involve their Qualities on purpose seems to be a bit at odds with how I GM.

    \n You could try a variant where, rather than adding all their MODs, each helper who has a relevant Quality contributes a flat +2. (Or Upshift, but that could get into a multi-dice extravaganza very quickly.)

That sort of approach might actually do a lot to make a game feel darker/grittier/more grounded.
