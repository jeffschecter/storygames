    \n I bought a ticket! Darren, I can help out at the booth and demo some games.

    I want to submit a panel proposal as well, since that will make it worthwhile in the eyes of my institution. I'm thinking about something like this, give me feedback:

    Games in the Ivory Tower: Making Sense of the Scholarship of Gaming\n
    The academic study of games, gaming, and gamers is a burgeoning field, attracting investigators from a wide variety of disciplines--computer science, cultural studies, communication, and even an interdisciplinary "game studies" field. Graduate programs in game design, digital storytelling, serious games, and related areas are in high demand. This presentation provides an overview of who is studying what kinds of games where and how, with an eye towards identifying topics and opportunities for graduate study. It will help students thinking about pursuing an advanced degree and be of interest to gamers who'd like know what the academics who study games are saying about their hobby.
