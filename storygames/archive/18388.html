    \n I don't have my book in front of me (which means everything after this next parenthesy is suspect), but here's a quote from Thor I scrounged from the Burning Wheel forums:\n
    A GM can do some really punishing things to a character with no Resources. Fail that Lifestyle Maintenance test and have no Resources to tax? You develop a terrible cough. +1 Ob to all Forte tests for the duration of the cycle.
    \nSo, essentially, the default failure is that you tax Resources which basically lowers your Resources attribute. (Look it up.) If there are no Resources to tax - if the attribute is 0 - the failure consequence can be anything appropriate, just like any other test.

    \n This is a little off topic but, since I gather you're a new Burner, keep in mind that Burning Wheel is a game wherein it's often more fun to fail than to succeed. Failure drives the game in new and unexpected directions. In fact, given the way advancement works you have to fail to progress. So embrace it - it's part of the fun!

    \n Making a Lifestyle test using only your Resources (which represent what you have already acquired, your banking accounts, what people owe you) is exactly like living off retirement. Therefore the money is going to run out eventually.

    So what do you the player do IRL? You go to work. What do you do when playing BW? Check your skills, Relationships, Reputations and Affiliations and figure out which can be used to make money (cash dice or funds, depending on what the GM gives you). And make it a Belief! "I'm personally responsible for the upkeep of Highcaptain's Hold. I will ensure its upkeep for this season by (insert appropriate method here)."

    Have an Affiliation with the guard as a guard captain? Make money by taking tolls or hunting bandits. Have an Affiliation with the local mafia? Go out to shake down local businesses. Royal prince? Ride out onto your lands and ensure the nobles tithe to you what they owe you this season. Have skill in Estate Management? Use it to ensure your ancestral properties yield enough stuff this season. No Estate Management, but high Circles? Find yourself a seneschal who can manage the land for you. High Haggling? Get involved in trade with neighbouring outposts, castles, towns...
