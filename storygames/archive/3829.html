Tickboxes for Paper

\n
\n
\n
\n\nJosh Roby \n\n\n

\n\nAugust 2007 \n edited August 2007 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n Okay, I keep coming across this problem as I design and I see the same problem as others design.

In the internet age, we're all very used to tickboxes and clickable switches in our media. All we do is click and change the state of the tickbox. It's on, now it's off, now it's on again. Or extend this to a row of clickable switches, and we can indicate fluctuations: now it's 3 boxes, now it's up to 5, now it's down to 2, then it's up to 4 again. We get really, really used to being able to do this sort of thing, and then we go to the game table and we try and reproduce it on paper. We end up scratching out what we've written, or erasing a hole into the paper, or whatever else. It's suboptimal at best.

So, the question is: what's a clever way to indicate a fluctuating on/off state on paper? I'm sure there's some design trick out there that can surmount this problem. Alternately, what are some tactile manipulables that can be used (poker chips, paperclips along edges of paper, dice-as-tokens), and how can they be used effectively?
