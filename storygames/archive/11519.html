\n Tell us about the game you want to play but will never design, either because you don't design RPGs, can't be bothered to design this one, or think you don't have the time/skill/what-have-you. Say what the game would be about or what you'd be doing in it or something like that. Have you had anything close to this? Do you want to see a heartbreaker for a game that didn't go so well?

You may also reply to people, and tell them about games you did not design that meet their needs in your opinion (but please, say why). If you choose to create one of these games, say so.

The purpose of this thread is to serve as inspiration for anyone who wants to make a new RPG, and to let everyone know what you want to play, but can't. If it catches on, maybe we can sticky this?

    \n I want* a game that plays like IAWA but is designed for multiple or volatile groups to share the creation of the game fiction without invalidating each other.

    I imagine it would do this via some kind of evolving artifact (like a map or a Dictionary or something) that could be copied and given to new players to convey what's important to them about the established fiction. This artifact would have to be bound up with play itself, so it wasn't an optional add-on that players could ignore (i.e. players wouldn't be tempted by short-term convenience to ignore the thing that improves long-term play).

    I think this mechanic could conceivably also help improve the fiction-to-mechanics thing (right pointing arrows), which would be a nice bonus.

    * Did I say want? I mean desperately yearn for.

    \n Yesterday, while editing the latest episode of my new podcast (an episode on the topic of GM-less games and why I don't like games with GM's), I started thinking about a GM-less dungeon crawl.

    In The Fifth World, I have a beginning round where each character describes something they want to achieve, and the obstacles in their way. The description of the obstacles then translates into how much they'll need to do to get their goal. I can see crossing this with something like How to Host a Dungeon, where you have an initial phase in town researching the dungeon. Each scary tale you hear from the locals adds more dangers to the dungeon, but the more you scale up the danger, the more treasure you can find down there.

    Then you go down and have to clear out all the deadly traps and horrible monsters you heard about--and naturally, some of the sinister surprises that you didn't hear about. If you wanted to take to heart what I said in the episode of same podcast before that, you could have two possible outcomes for each encounter: overcoming it, or finding some new, previously unsuspected trap or monster down there. You could do this randomly without falling into the pit of just a mish-mash of nonsense, too. If you're building the dungeon in the first phase in steps\u2014so, first the dwarves dug too greedily and too deep, and then the wizard built his evil tower on top\u2014then you could have the "abandoned dwarfhold" set of random encounters, and the "evil wizard's tower" set of random encounters, and first you've got to work through some number of evil wizard tower encounters before you reach the abandoned dwarfhome encounters.

    Done right, I think you could have a game as fun as D&D, maybe a whole lot simpler, and certainly requiring a lot less work. And I'd personally love to see a good counterpoint to the argument that a dungeon delve needs a GM.

    \n Okay, what I'd like to see is a game that combines LARP with ARG. It would be tied together by a website, but orbited by an enormous amount of 'clue' sites. You play a character that appears to be you in human terms, but can look wildly different to those with the 'sight'.

    It'd have vampires, faeries, death cults, immortals (Highlanders?), men in black, liches, red caps, mummies, vengeful hunters of supernatural baddies, aliens, werewolves, ressurection cults, ghosts, serial killers and supernatural versions of real-life things like gang-bangers, motorcycle gangs, mafia, political cabals, conspiracies, terrorists and so on.

    Downtime play would be like some giant veiled version of the Illuminati: New World Order card game, but even wackier. Online play would be like conspiracy ARGs where you find clue sites and work your way to in-game secrets. Live Action play would be all about court intrigues and back room dealing. Humanity would be treated like a resource, but one made out of dynamite. These groups aren't hiding because they fear humanity, but because they need to shape mankind's ideas and ideals to make the groups the most powerful and controlling.

    The central hub website would house password protected forums and blogs for each and every conspiracy; your allegiances would dictate which and how many you could read. Real-life news events would affect play (possibly retroactively) and tabloids would be treated as fact in play. There'd be card games, novels, television shows, webspaces and even religious organizations involved.

    Oh, and I could play it, but be the only one changing identities as I please and only I'd be able to read all the conspiracies.

    [Whew! Actually, I've been dreaming about this game since the nineties and it just keeps on growing.]
