\n I have a few games that both fascinate and frighten me. Not necessarily because of content or system, but sometimes because of both! I'm talking about games I want to play but don't for some reason/s. Not bad scary, but TOO GOOD scary, if that makes sense.

So what three games are you looking at but hesitant to play?

My top 3 likely would be:

* Burning Wheel.
This game intimidates me, pure and simple. I love some of the ideas, I bought the books based on a demo of a Duel of Wills/Wits, but have yet to run an actual session because there is SO much to the game and I really have no idea where to start. I want to play, but the thought of trying to learn this game and THEN teach it to others... yeah, it has me whimpering in a corner.

* Grey Ranks.
Wow, this is a heavy, uncomfortable game. Brilliant, but I'm just not ready for this level of game. I have read the book and want to play, but life insurance commercials on television make me get misty eyed. How the hell can I handle something like this? The fact is, I can't, at least not right now.

*Aces & Eights.
Remember when I talked about how Burning Wheel was a big and complicated game? Oh dear lord, A&E is such a monster! Again, looks hella fun, but just trying to figure out where to start gives me fits. I mean, you have a target overlay to figure out when you shoot someone depending on your die roll and drawing a card from a deck. Combat breaks down into a fraction of a second per move. There are rules for the percentage of cattle lost on a cattle drive. The book is beautiful, and the Kenzer guys obviously love this genre, but it simply is too much game. And yes, there is such a thing.

So how's about you?

ME

Add the rest in as you need (there's actually a section at the end of the Spokes part of the book that says, in essence, 'go play now with what you know, come back when you need something special').

    It takes a lot of really difficult things working in synergy to do it correctly. I've played and loved The Princes Kingdom and also child PCs in other games... but each time I am so worried, going in, that I'll botch it up.

I want to try this, but I'm afraid to face the fact that I'm a gamer first, and an intense actor second. I'm not sure if I just need to stretch my comfort zone a bit with this, or if it's just not for me.

      Bacchanal, Breaking the Ice, etc       - I think romantic and sexual themes can add a lot of depth to a game, I'm just not very good at actually implementing them in games. I'm happily married and have a family, so presumably I've been romantic and sexual at some point in my life but I just don't have the grammar (for lack of a better term) to create such situations out of whole cloth like I can a swordfight or a tense political debate.

      I balk at playing Bacchanal for a couple reasons I think. One is more a matter of group makeup than anything else. My regular group has just never done this kind of gaming before, and erotic situations are not the usual topic of the day in out of game chat. So, awkward. The other, and I think Graham talked about this in "Play Unsafe" is fear of revealing something and having it be rejected. You show a lot about yourself by being more engaged in some parts of a game than others, or making one choice over another. Sex has so much emotional content attached to it I think the fear of putting ones ideas out there for judgement, even indirectly in a game, is kinda scary.

      Burning Wheel - This game just intimidates me based on sheer volume and fiddliness. I hear stories about how an experienced person can run a 20 person combat as easily as a 2 person one, but I just can't make the leap. My friends and I make characters, think about a campaign, stare at the books sitting there in a pile, then break out a board game. I really want to try, but I just can't...do...it.

      So, there you have it. Do I turn my Indie Gamer badge in here, or at the door? :P
