\nThen the second game we wanted to try the other side of the coin and played "Today Jared dies." Jared was the free word, and the initial intent was to quickly throw together a kind of American Beauty type game, but it shifted into a teen prom horror story instead.
\n
\nIt is amazing to see how much the insertion of one or two words through a sentence can radically alter it's meaning!
\n
\nSo what other versions has anyone tried? With much success?

    We used http://catchyourhare.com/diceroller/ as we do for most games. The Skype window was where we kept track of the story turn by turn.

    We kept labels for each player on the dice roller page, so that we knew what position we were "seated" in. Also, we used the labels to show how many H or T we had at any given moment.

    Also, that program has Fudge dice built in, so, piece of cake.

    \n The first game took a little over an hour, with three people. Skype didn't really help with one of the players, as he's much more inclined to sit silently and think, rather than talk it out, and without a visual connection, it made things drag.

    The second game took maybe thirty to fourty-five minutes.

    But we played again with four people today, and it's probably the best one so far. I was talking about it to a couple we were hanging out with, and they seemed interested, so I whipped out my palm (it has a Fudge dice roller on it) and took down the story on a paper bag we had just been given for buying some candy. I'm going to take a scan of the bag tonight and post it somewhere. I mean, how funny is that?

    "I have this brown paper bag and some coins. Want to play a game?"

    The game we played helped me articulate a point I was trying to make about trying to tell a story, but using dice to decide who had what kind of authority. And one of the players helped me show the other two what changes when someone has a high degree of buy-in. And also, we had a good time, so win all around.

    It played in thirty minutes, and could have gone longer, but we were at a fair ground and I kind of pushed myself into 10 coins so we could get to the conclusion and talk about how it went. We ended with 10, 6, 5, and 3 coins.

    Posted By: HarlequinI kind of pushed myself into 10 coins so we could get to the conclusion and talk about how it went.
    \nThat is one aspect of the game that's been shining in each play. You feel a sense that the story is coming to a conclusion, and it will, but you have a just enough control to decide whether it happens now or one round later if you need a little more time.

    If you need a shorter game, you can also just set the time limit to be 7 or 8 coins. That's what I did at Dreamation to play through a 1 hour slot. It worked quite well.
