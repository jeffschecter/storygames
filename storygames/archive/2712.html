\n So, you're probably all out doing Game Chef right now.

And you're all sharing your creative endeavours, and it's probably wonderful.

I opted not to do Game Chef because a local contest is going on this month, and I wanted to support my local scene, yo.

The contest is this: design a board game by March 31st, based on one of these three arcade games: Robotron, Joust, Space Invaders.

And since there is no online forum for discussing this comp, and since Story Games is a ghost town right now, I decided that I'd share with you how badass my board game concept is.

It's called Vulture Lords!

Setting pitch:

For untold centuries, the Vulture Lords kept the earth in fear. These immortal evils have been reborn a thousand times, and their will had always remained unchanged: destroy mankind and plague the earth with vulture-riding demons.

Now, though, things have changed - for the Vulture Lords fight amongst themselves. For the past three centuries, they have fought for dominion over the wastelands. All of the Lords undying, they would come to life again after every fall from the skies. The only way that a Vulture Lord will be able to win control of the wastelands will be if he kills his enemies enough times that they simply abandon their claims and their homeland. And so, the war amongst the immortals began. And now, it is almost over.

Game mechanics:

There's a 4 square by 4 square board. This is the Wastelands.
Every player (2-4 players) plays a Vulture Lord. You need to kill an enemy 5 times to knock him out of the game (there's only so much abuse a guy can take, right?)

There is height in this game too. It goes from 1 to 5, and all Vulture Lords start at Height 1.

During your turn, you draw 3 cards. Playing a card allows you to Move (changing direction, ie turning 90 degrees, costs a point of movement), Flap (raise your height), Dive (lower your height), and sometimes do other nifty things.

To kill an enemy, simply be in the same square as he, but at a Height 1 higher. Wicked.

If you enter a square at the same height as another dude, you both drop by 1, and you "push" him into an adjacent square. Thus maxing out at 5 doesn't instil any magical "safety zone".

Spawn:

Some cards are spawn cards. Because no undead lord fights a war without summoning his friends from the pits of [wherever]. You play a Spawn card and place it in a Spawn location of your choice. The five Spawns are: Bounder, Hunter, Shadow Lord, Pterodactyl, and Ostrich Knight (that's right... you can lure human knights into the fray. They remain landlocked and are essentially sitting prey.)

Once in play, you can play your cards on a Spawn instead of yourself. Anyone can play cards on any Spawn - they have no allegiances.

Killing any enemy - a Spawn or a Vulture Lord - gets you an extra card. Thus, Ostrich Knights are free cards, waiting to be taken.

So, yeah. That's my game.
You fly around killing each other multiple times, until you are the final undying lord of the wasteland, ready to beset mankind with your terrifying, awful power!

Kirk Mitchell is doing all the card illustrations for me, as well as designing the board.

    \n Komradebob, I've actually got a flat, 2-d board.

    You take your Vulture Lord card, and stand it on top of a height marker.

    Height markers are little board game pieces that are about 1-2 inches tall, and have a slit in the top for resting a card in.

    When you climb to height two, you just stack two height markers and place your card on top, in the little slit.




    Troy - I don't really know, just yet. I've looked at websites before, for this kind of thing... because an RPG I'm working on uses several highly game-y physical components, and I liked the idea of offering a boxed set.

    Most board game producers have a minimum order of either 500 or 5,000. I've never found a board game producer able to do production at less than 500 units (although they did offer proto-type ordering, it's just that this was exorbitantly expensive and wouldn't work as an actual production cost).


    Jason, Remi, Kevin - Thanks guys. After the competition, I'm going to talk to Kirk about the possibility of developing this game further - getting some full colour art, finding printers, etc. It's JUST THAT SWEET, man.

    \n Wicked guys.

    Since the competition deadline is Saturday at 6:30, we'll likely have the game finished by Saturday at 6:20.

    Let me tell you the components now, so that you can start amassing them, and the rules/card templates/board (just a ledger sized sheet of paper, printed one-side) will be coming your way in a matter of days.

    Equipment needed:
    board (I will send, then print it onto a Ledger sized sheet of glossy paper.)
    cards (I will send, then print them onto Avery perforated business card sheets.)
    rulebook (I will send, then print onto letter sized regular paper.)
    Height markers and bases
    Life counters and small holding trays

    For Height markers, you want some kind of fairly thin, stackable, 1-2 inch tall marker. If you take the lids of kids' felt pens, your example, these work great. There's a store down the street from where I live that sells absolutely random stuff, and they happened to have several buckets of surplus medical supplies, including a bucket of syringe caps (don't worry - not used).These syringe caps proved to be PERFECT, and they're what I use.

    You'll want to put a vertical slit in the top of each marker, only going down a fraction of an inch. Should this be the top Height Marker on the stack, you'll be resting a Vulture Lord or Spawn card in that little slit. (I tend to make a vertical slit and then a much smaller diagonal slit, so that the card can more easily slide into place.)

    Most height markers (kids felt pens for example) are very wobbly. You'll want to find some kind of base for them - a weight that will prevent them from toppling over mid-game, even if stacked 5 high. Bases should be easily detachable from the markers themselves, and not too wide if possible. I have this weird plastic cap thing that I got at the random stuff store.

    Everyone has 4 Bonus Lives, just like in Joust. These are represented by small Life Counters, resting in your holding tray. If you happen to own Battlelore, feel free to use their dishes and [mana points?] for this. I got little plastic dishes and small white buttons for this purpose.

    So, my legion of Vulture Lord fanatics - assemble these things, and I'll have the game to you by Saturday at the latest.

    Ryan - I'll email you in a few days, if I get serious serious about production.

    \n Game feedback:

    Some people really loved Vulture Lords!, and some people didn't like the tactical aspect of it.

    The game works best when you are into tactics, but also into taking it light and having fun. It's often a poor tactical option to Spawn dudes, but the game is fun when you say "what the hell, let's throw down two more Bounders."

    My stands turned out to be flimsy, and considering that there was an overhead fan they often blew over. Note to self: sturdier height markers.

    We gave away swag to people - a logo sticker and 2 cards in every bag. That was pretty neat.

    We definitely didn't win (there was a team from Relic there, with professionally illustrated, coloured and produced playing cards. God damn!), but we had fun and people dug our game.

    All in all, it was pretty kick ass, and we're interested in developing it further. Anyone interested in trying a playtest, I can send you some PDFs of stuff.
