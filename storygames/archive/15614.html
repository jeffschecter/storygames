\n Hello all,

So I've been working on a sci-fi fighter pilot game for sometime, and my friends are really digging the direction I am going in. However, I need some advice. I am working on a mechanical system in which you weigh your priorities in a hierarchical fashion based on your rolls. The simplest way it could work is as follows:

There are two factors in a conflict:
a.) Do I get what I want?
b.) What will I sacrifice to achieve this.

Say I roll 2d6 and my results are 5 and 1. I have to choose how to prioritize the above factors. If I favor getting what I want, I will assign the "5" to factor a and the "1" to factor b. Consequently, I would suffer a bit.

On the other hand, if I favor staying safe, I might assign the "5" to factor b and the "1" to factor a. I won't suffer, but I probably won't get what I want either.

I feel like I want one (or two) more factor(s) to complete this basic system, but I'm not sure what other elements need to be prioritized in a conflict. The only stipulation I can see is that the system work for physical/mental/social conflicts (with minor tweaks in and out for the three conflict arenas I just mentioned).

If anyone can think of other "priorities," that would be FANTASTIC.

Thank you all,
~Klaus
