Unfortunately the MG isn\u2019t nocturnal and tends to snooze on the job but its sight and reputation is usually enough to frighten away the portside thieves and beggars.

    \n I think it's because everyone's been anxious to GM their session. We'd been playing Instant Game one-shots and wanted to focus on one game without too much crunch and with a bit more story than our last campaign using Forward to Adventure!

    This last session was totally combat-orientated - Dave used the city erupting from the ground to split the characters into different starting arenas with ultimately gave each a chance to shine - Tony played the longrunner this time and used your ideas about the improvised weapons (they worked fine) and Buck - the most combat-talented character - was chief mook-slayer. My naga fought the heir on the roof off a building, he took damage and fled to the floor below and hid - Manda used 3rd eye (yet again to find him) - showed off and won the fight. Dodger and Manda then rejoined a wounded Buck. Now that the city was re-built completely the remaining undead dissolved into the ground.* We ended the session with the heroes at the gates of the empty city and the grandfather's ghost appearing one last time to thank Manda. As the trio trudged back to Little Narport to get their wounds dressed/chill out etc they passed nagas heading back to Nagapolis.

    *Dave said afterwards he considered having Valare and the Guild coming to the rescue but decided against it as that would've shifted the focus away from the trio.
