\n Idea for a mechanic: a stat that also behaves like its own resource. The number fluxuates up and down depending on the success of checks made with that stat. Some quick examples I've sketched out:


When you encounter something aberrant, eldritch, or horrifying, roll +sanity. On a miss, your sanity is reduced by 1.

When you hack the matrix, roll +ice. On a success, you accomplish your goal. On a partial success, choose: your ice is reduced by 1, or you fail. On a miss, it's both.

When you take damage, roll +constitution. On a miss, your con is reduced by 1. The GM will say whether your injury is temporary, ongoing, or permanent. If you reach -3 con, make a death roll.

When the villagers farm their fields and hunt for game, roll +food. On a success, you have enough food for the season. On a critical success, mark 1 stock. On a miss, choose 1: erase 1 stock, suffer 1 harm, or reduce the village's food by 1.


Any systems that incorporate this? Any players with experience using it?

    \n Sure, there are a number of games that do this.

    Some of the "stats" in Apocalypse World and Monsterhearts come to mind (e.g. the Angel in Monsterhearts).

    The Shadow of Yesterday has "Pools", which are effectively attributes, but you can spend and replenish them.

    Hit points are effectively an attribute which fluctuates up and down, if you think about it.

    I once designed a game called Winter Crucible, where players had four stats, and used them to overcome obstacles. Each stat suggested a different approach ("Disciplined", "Daring", etc.). Every roll you made would decrease or increase a certain stat, so that, for example, screwing up several Disciplined rolls would keep decreasing your Disciplined stat and increase your Daring stat, instead.

    I only got to playtest it once, but it worked well in practice.

    In a Wicked Age... does this, as well. Stats can be "damaged" in conflicts, and act as hit points. You can redistribute fresh dice to your stats instead of increasing your abilities every time you play a character once again.

    \n All Outta Bubblegum! Unfortunately, looks like the original site is no longer up, but it's preserved in this G+ post which credits the original authors.

    It's a roll-under/roll-over stat, as in Lasers and Feelings, where you roll over your current amount of bubblegum sticks (yes, you start with sticks of bubblegum as your character stat) to do awesome action hero stuff, and you roll under or equal to your bubblegum sticks to do mundane stuff.

    At any time, you can spend a stick of bubblegum to automatically succeed at doing something awesome. You also lose a bubblegum stick if you fail at a non-combat roll.

    Once you're all outta bubblegum, you become "a nearly unstoppable ball of bubblegum-less fury". Also, you are completely perplexed by mundane challenges and tasks, like opening doors.



    It's kinda hilarious, but the core mechanic behind it is really neat, and you could easily adapt it to any number of settings, such as vampires (roll over your current Vitae to do something with vampiric powers, roll under or equal to your Vitae to do something normal and human, like connecting with someone), werewolves (roll over your current Control to do something with werewolf fury, roll under or equal to do something human), punk rebels (doing something in line with The System vs. being a rebel), etc., etc.

    \n Bubblegum sounds hilarious. Characters get progessively stumped by mundane tasks even as they evolve into hulks of fury. It's like Manly Guys Doing Manly Things the game.

    I had an idea for a similar mechanic for a Cthulhu-esque game, your stat being Sanity. Roll under for Reason, to accomplish ordinary feats like fighting or negotiating, and roll over for Madness to attempt to grapple with the eldritch unknown. Various events, including failures, reduce the stat - the character degenerates and becomes progessively more obsessed with the occult - and eventually spiral to 0, where they die, commit suicide, or are abducted by horrendous forces. (I also liked the idea that if Sanity ever reached 7, the character would decide that all this space monster nonsense couldn't possibly be real, and refuse to participate further in the mystery.)

    \n Yeah, I had this idea for a system somewhat loosely based on WW.

    You have the body, mind and feeling attributes. Plus whatever skills.

    When you test a skill, you roll the skill + 1 as long as you aren't considered tapped out of an attribute that would be considered key to the skill use.

    And you can also choose to spend from an associated attribute to increase your roll. This could be on the offense/active or the defense/passive. This is how you become tapped out, btw.

    Simple and solves the problem common to some trad games of attributes having a disproportionately high mechanical value when compared to skills.
