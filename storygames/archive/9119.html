    Posted By: MJGrahamI had similar thoughts about using the word sedition, but I couldn't think of an alternative which meant both rebellion and incitement. I'd like to use revolution or uprising, but the game is really about what goes on before people take up arms against an oppressor.
    \nSay, "A storytelling game of incitement and rebellion", then. Don't make the reader (prospective buyer!) work for it. "Fighting back" is too general - any game could be about that. You might also want to see if you can give a clue to era/setting while also keeping the strapline short.

    As to graphics - I like the basic silhouette concept. I like the dark sides suggesting emerging from a tunnel or an alley between buildings. Struggling from darkness into light - or fire... Another way you might try is to have that as the middle portion, with bands of a different colour across the top and bottom (bit of a long shot though).

    Make the title a bit bigger. Move the strapline down. Do identify the publisher or author on the front cover. Don't, however, use Uncial or whatever Celtic-themed font you used originally, unless it's part of a definite logo.

    Your original version is not bad, actually - you could just make it a bit more contrasty, alter the strapline text and sort out the publisher credit.

    Posted By: ValamirI'm curious...why "quasi historical 18th century"...why not actual historical 18th century?
    \nPart of creating the setting was to consider how things might be from the perspective of the people within the setting and not from our modern viewpoint. Miracles, curses, blessed items, divine judgment, angelic retribution, people becoming deformed and disfigured by their own moral failings or the the harsh and uncaring situation life presents to them... make the setting unreal to our modern eyes. The term fantasy is laden with connotations of elves and wizards, dragons and arcane artifacts, epic journeys and mighty warriors, none of which are present in my game.
