    We definitely did. Well, in that instance I was actually being totally useless and failing every time I tried to hit something, but other people thoroughly beat up the baddies.

    The nice thing about not having the hp/damage is that, since it's supposed to be for kids, it lets you tailor whatever damage/violence/etc to being more age-appropriate, and the bad guy can escape shouting "curse you you meddling kids!" or what have you instead of gruesomely dying at 0 hitpoints. The emphasis can be more on other ways a kid would solve a problem instead of how heartily you beat the crap out of something. OR, you can choose to emphasize how heartily you beat the crap out of something, if that's your thing.

    \n Just a note: Mike Krahulik was never an avid RPG player until recently, when he was introduced to D&D 4E. It's quite possible that he doesn't really have much exposure to "Story Games", post-Forge indie RPGs, etc. Thus the question about damage and HP.

The Penny Arcade guys are possibly some of the most influential people on the Internet. Show them that there's more to RPGs than D&D, and they might just show everyone else.
