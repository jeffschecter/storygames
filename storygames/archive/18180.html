When you use the phrase "That won't be quite so easy" in Archipelago, you get to draw a resolution card to see what happens. These cards have different results ("Yes, but..." "No, and..." and similar) that are interpreted by one of the players.

Sometimes this breaks the flow, especially with the more concrete cards ("You need help from someone else to make this happen"). In a playset I'm working on, ALL the cards have concrete effects - someone loses or gains a resource, a good or bad plan/inspiration comes to someone, etc. People need some time to think, which can be a little frustrating since they know people are waiting to see what happens in the middle of a conflict/exciting scene.

I'm looking for ideas to make this more fun. Possibly ways of eliminating the problem; ways of making it not feel like a problem; ways of changing the explanation of the rules or perhaps the wording of the phrase so it won't be perceived as a problem.

Do you have any ideas or suggestions?

    \n Do you think "That might not be that simple" demands less time to think than "Try a different way"?

    ---

    I don't like the "That might not be that simple" rules, because they can have a tendency for the other to look for conflicts in the situation. It also takes control over my character - from freeforming narrating to suddenly having to adjust to a game mechanic. What the rule does is for someone else to narrate the outcome, which I can see the point with. When we played Archipelago, we sometimes created an obstacle by using Try a different way or Describe that in detail. I think that is enough, to be honest.

    The person who invoke "Try a different way" should include something that needs to be fulfilled in the new description - "Include how you're breaking your promise", "I don't want to end up in jail" - or a consequence of the action if the current players chooses to go through with it: "You will be known as a traitor to the kingdom". This creates the same thing - another player has control over the outcome - but it still includes the current players decision.
