\n Last year, while living on the road, I took photos, attempting to illustrate Ben Lehman's "The Drifter's Escape".

I liked those pictures, but felt they were too scenic/sunny/outdoorsy.
And the images didn't include enough people-- to inhabit the raw edge of americana.


Then, the weekend before last, I attended an art installation-- which is still available for viewing:

CREATIVE TIME PRESENTS
MIKE
NELSON
A PSYCHIC VACUUM
SEPTEMBER 8 - OCTOBER 28, 2007
FRIDAYS, SATURDAYS, AND SUNDAYS 12-6 PM
-FREE-
THE OLD ESSEX STREET MARKET
117 DELANCEY STREET @ ESSEX STREET, SOUTHEAST CORNER, NEW YORK CITY
CLICK TO ENTER


The artwork consists of a maze of beat-down dusty little rooms, bare lightbulbs or cheapo flourescent, peeling paint,
worthless salvaged nobody artifacts, and several tons of sand.

I was reminded of The Drifter's Escape, so last weekend I pre-empted a scheduled PSI-RUN playtest & told the players to
instead come in costume as Grapes of Wrath okies, biker burnouts, shady hitch-hikers, etc.

We explored the maze, fooled around, slipped in and out of character, got locked in and nearly had to kick down a heavy door.

I don't think Ben is still in need of illustrations, but I got a nice sense of having 'finished' last year's shoot.
I have only pulled stills from the first few minutes of footage, so I can't post any samples yet.

But I wanted to post this now, to recommend the art installation to you all.

    Also, I forgot to mention that afterward-- we went from the installation to Brooklyn, to attend
    Indian Larry's Grease Monkey Block Party.

    No sooner had we removed costumes and put-away the camera-- then we found ourselves amid hundreds of
    seriously tuff outlaw bikers, huuuuge nypd harley cops,
    Manson-like bar bands, and.... male supermodel Tyson? ...and a contingent of Hassidic jews!
    The change of venue was an interesting juxtaposition of fiction and reality.
