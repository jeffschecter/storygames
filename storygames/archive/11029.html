\n I'd like to get some nice D4s engraved with some funky symbols on them. Problem is: minimum order 1000 dice.

I was talking to Dawn at Special Dice, who was very, very nice, and she advised me to go for a bag of blank D4s, and to use stickers for the graphics.

Sounds cheap (bad) and cheap (good). Anyone have any thoughts on the stickers vs. engraving see-saw that is my transient, mental crossroad this morning? Like, how do you get those tiny, weeny little stickers to print on transparent, sticky paper, and who does it for you, and does it look horrible? Any photos of good/bad sticker jobs from your own collection?

Or can you recommend a place to get affordable, custom, engraved D4s?

    \n Steve,

    I have thought about getting it done.

    You can get blank 4ds easily, or large blanks, and if you're luck you can sometimes find blanks with a lip around the edge which lets you put stickers on more easily.

    (examples of the D6 can be seen here:
    http://www.the-education-shop.co.uk/acatalog/BLANK_DICE.html)

    Getting labels done seems hard: paper ones will look ok, until you actually use them and they go crappy from hand grease. So getting plastic laminated stickers is the way to go. That's expensive, so a bit of lateral thinking is in order. 2 approaches are possible, although I have tried neither.

    The first is to print, using a laser printer (or whatever) onto decal paper. People do this all the time in the custom Lego minifig world. The results can be highly detailed, which is great for lego figs. Probably too delicate for dice though, given that they're thrown about and slammed on the table all the time. Varnishing them might mitigate that, but I;'m not the person to ask, and it sounds like a lot of work to be honest. You don't need a highly detailed decal on a die though, people can read simple designs more easily (try reading most Q-Workshop dice in poor light).

    The second is a bit more practical if you want to make a lot of them. Go to http://www.vistaprint.co.uk/ or your local equivalent if you're outside the UK. Look at Window Decals. These are basically colour printed plastic sheets which are sticky on one side and can be transparent. Fiddle with the details, but you'll probably have to do a test run before you get the sizing just right, but you can design labels to cut out and stick on the dice.

    Of course, I've never done it, but it seems like it ought to work!
