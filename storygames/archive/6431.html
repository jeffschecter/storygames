\n Hello all,

I have this idea about assigning character roles in a TSOY game based on Dark*Matter or X-Files via how you were 'hired' - if you were a Witness, an Internal Hire, or a Field Agent (something supernatural unto yourself). Witnesses tend to be naive to the true nature of the organization, Internal Hires are much more jaded, and Field Agents used to be hunted themselves and their loyalties are highly questionable and can change over time. However, there are alternate views of these employees: Witnesses are very new to this world and don't have too much influence, Internal Hires are taught from the beginning and have lots of influence, and Field Agents have prejudices to deal with, but some connections, too. Finally, if you look at it in terms of supernatural power, you'd have Witnesses in the middle, being exposed but new, Internal Hires as sub-par, suffering from their banal surroundings, and Field Agents with high power.

That is:\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n
LoyaltyInfluenceSupernatural
Witnesses + -- [ ]
Internal Hires [ ] + --
Field Agents -- [ ] +
\nSo, you can label any type of agent on any of three views with the faces of a Fudge Die.

Anyone know what I can do with this?

    \n Yeah, I screwed up the Witnesses line. It's fixed now.

    I was thinking more along the lines of this: In certain situations, after determining success or failure on your roll, look at which die type is in majority; this is the overriding motivator to your success or failure. For example, if you were arguing with a rival faction member who saw something they shouldn't have and you're trying to Sway them to back off, you could:

    Succeed, with Pluses: You bring them to becoming loyal to you.
    Succeed, with Minuses: You call on your superiors, who pressure them politically.
    Succeed, with Zeroes: You alter their minds with a gadget you recovered from your stint in Atlantis.

    The key point here is this: WHEN would this be appropriate? I'm not trying to solve a problem, but I'd love to see where this would fit in and add to the fun.

    Maybe, also, the player could define their own categories of Plus, Minus and Zero?
