\n I just wrapped a game of Durance via Google hangouts, which is a fantastic, useful tool. You can enable "with extras", which you definitely want to do. Here's a screenshot:



"With extras" you've got voice and video, text chat, and file sharing. If you share Google Docs you can edit them as you play, in the view window. It's pretty simple and nice, a good utilitarian toolset. In that image you see a simple "play mat" I built using Google Draw. The game went OK, and we had some weird drops and latency, but that sort of thing will only get better as time goes by.

    \n Nice screen shot Jason!

    We've used Google Hangouts + Google Docs with good success. Specifically we ran a game where 2 people were over my house and 2 people were remote.

    If you use Google Spreadsheets, you can mimic dice rolling. Although I found given the video feature and that you can look people in the eye, I really didn't need to see their dice rolls, just know their results.

    With the G+ Hangouts API, you can write an RPG designed specifically to be used with this platform: https://developers.google.com/+/hangouts/

    G+ Hangouts was the very first time I've ever considered, "wow online roleplaying may be something I actually want to do, rather than something I settle for." In the future all of Story Games, regardless of physical location, will be able to play and design games together.

    It will be fun to GM with voice modulators!

    \n Yeah, I played a couple sessions of 3:16 with Mikael Anderson and Darcy Burgess about 6 months ago, and then played in the above-screenshotted Durance game. Hangouts has come a long, long way in a relatively short time. The camera angle is definitely the killer app, as Jason points out. The ability to look at Jason, Rafael and Alejandro during play and see their expressions and reactions was great. In retrospect, I'm disappointed I didn't have a camera, especially considering that I occasionally had to take unannounced breaks from the game, so seeing my presence or lack thereof would probably have helped smooth those over a bit.
    \n
    \nAbout the only thing I wish would have been better, and they're likely to be user experience things rather than technical, is being able to edit the playboard (for some reason, I couldn't) and easier scanning through the linked material. Having the guide available right through the Hangout was invaluable. If I'd not been a last-minute addition to the game because Graham was forced to drop out, I could probably have oriented myself in the book and completely eliminated my issues there.
    \n
    \nAlso, I love that my remark about Inter-sibling attempted cannibalism is in this screenshot.
    \n
    \nAdditionally, looking at the text chat, it occurs to me that a whisper feature would be useful, if it doesn't already exist. I was tending to use the text as a way to comment and question without interrupting the scene-in-progress, and sometimes it may be appropriate to do that to specific players, rather than the group at large.

    \n Jason brings up a point, about persistence, that is important. The chat log in Hangouts isn't persistent, unfortunately, but one thing that it does have going for it is the synergy with GDocs. I'm not specifically familiar with Google Draw, which Jason used for the playmat, but I do know that it is still sitting in my GDocs list, exactly as we left it.
    \n
    \nPossibly even more interesting, so is the 3:16 range map I used with Darcy and Mikael... along with the revision history of every time we added, removed, moved, or renamed a piece. Depending on what the play resource is, you could almost replay a game with the revision history.
    \n
    \nProbably the areas where a dedicated virtual table-top trumps G+ Hangouts is in certain ease-of-use features, such as map scaling, and some specific game support functions like character sheets, die rollers, etc. But with at least a couple people working on VTT plugins for Hangouts, I think those advantages will be seen too soon enough.

    \n Hello everyone, I am the developer of the Tabletop Forge app and thought I would stop by to say hi.     \n Hi Scott,

    What Joshuha and I are building is similar, though we're taking different approaches to it. Once Google releases the API from beta we'll be able to add in all the "With extras" features that Jason mentioned. I personally think having all those tools in one window along with video and audio of players around the world is going to revolutionize the way we all play and open the door for a lot of new players and people who just can't find enough players.

    I am working on building a game agnostic system with a simple interface. It has a map with moveable tokens and a die roller along with a system for taking notes that persist from session to session. I'm adding more things as I go but I want to keep it simple and not specialized to any one system.
