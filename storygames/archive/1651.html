    I played with ideas like this in my Character Bonds game hack. The problem with its reward system is that it isnt strong enough to override the game's built-in reward system. In other words, you get all those XP for doing cool goal-oriented things that have meaning to you, then you level up and get better and killing shit and taking its stuff and the system still encourages the strategic (and fun!) minmaxing that is the leveling-up process.

    I found that my Character Bonds system worked well for a few sessions (and a few levels) but then the level treadmill took over and players became more concerned with kicking ass and taking names than addressing premise. The Character Bonds system became just another tool in their Gamist CA.

    That said, I think your Wyrd system is superior to Character Bonds. It closes the loop. Players complete Wyrds and gain a bonus from them and immediately get to write a new one (a slot becomes open for one anyway). I would require a player to keep his Wyrd slots full, just as Sorcerer requires the player to write a Kicker. Giving the DM a Doom point also escalates Situation: you have succeeded, but now things are more grave -- what do you do about it? I would give the DM a lot more Doom points for a finished Wyrd! Perhaps 1d6 or 1d8.

    Wyrd Failure is a bit off for me. Who decides that a PC has failed? Maybe you didn't defeat the goblin warlock with your magic this time, but you plan to face him again! Can a player immediately take that Wyrd again, since there's an open slot?

    Here's an idea for long campaign play: Doom Quests. Start your big bad behind-the-scenes guy ("boss") at one or two levels about the average party member. As the PCs complete Wyrds, the boss earns 200 XP. When the PC fails a Wyrd, the boss earns 1000 XP. As in most epic games, the boss is always out of reach, controlling things behind the scenes. Let the players know about the boss' XP totals so they feel a sense of urgency. When he gains a level, tell them what class he takes. Explain it game terms: "Reports filter down about your arch-enemy, Fanton Risth Ren. It seems he's been attacking village assemblies and holding them in mass, then slaughtering them one by one." (Fanton must be 13th level to use the 7th level spell.)
