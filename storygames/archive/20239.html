\n Hello all,

I've been tinkering with a game called Terminal and would like to find play testers who are not being led by the game's creator (me). The game is still unfinished, perhaps rough around the edges, but the mechanics are all there (or so I think).

In the game, players take on the role of a terminally ill character who keeps a journal. My design goal is to make a safe space to provoke frank but sensitive conversation about divisive social topics.

I dropped a pdf on my OneDrive:
http://1drv.ms/1S0eA2z

I'd be very pleased if your group wants to give it a shot. I'd also be pleased if anyone would like to provide feedback of any kind.

Thanks in advance,

    \n Dice: the Chessex sets of 36 work for me. But since you mentioned it, the specific dice requirement seems to be the largest stumbling block for potential players. I bring my own and share (inexpensive journals, too...sometimes even extra pencils, you get the idea). This doesn't always help. Some still scoff and wander off. Others look on with concern at the piles of dice and start asking questions until hey find a nice way to tell me why their not interested.

    My sweet spot: I like a group of four players and a moderator (4+M). But I'm a gaming table voyeur and don't contribute much. Three outgoing people (2+M) could probably fill the void. Anything more than five is a crowd IMO. Your experience will almost certainly vary.

    \n BeePeeGee,
    I understand your concern. However, I want the dice to feel like poker chips, to be wagered (invested?) during play. In this case the poker chips come in incomparable colors (value wise) and also express probability. Gambling them means something to the story telling.

    Have you tried using the dice mat? It might make the dice feel more intuitive and less onerous.

    While I am pointing out my own critics' arguments: what do you think of the mandatory in-character journaling? This has been common stumbling block among potential players.

    Rickard,
    This game is a heartbreaker. I have been grinding on it for a few years. Months seems like a time constant I can get behind for a growth formula.

    To both of you, thanks for the interest. Feel free to hit me up with the "why" of any or all of it. I am happy to pull any thread you find worthy of posting about.

    \n BeePeeGee,

    I can't see a way around the number of dice. Exactly what colors are assigned to each attribute, sure. But the game is designed around differentiable dice pools gambled and then rolled all at once. Fast paced resolution.

    I acknowledge the dice as a possible barrier to entry for some.

    I'm curious, though. Do the procedural descriptions make sense? Can you visualize exactly how to execute them? In my mind (huge qualifier there), it's a pretty straightforward party-ready, single-session roleplaying game. But can it be executed as written? I am not certain.

    Appreciate your time,
    Eddie
