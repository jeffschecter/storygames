\n So Simon over at Beyond Belief games. Wrote an awesome game called "Barbarians of Lemuria" (and a bunch of other indie games, I just happen to love BoL the most).

It's now licensed in French. And the French, as they are wont to do, have created tons of awesome new art for the game.

BEHOLD.

The game is laid out already, kinda spartan-like.

Simon doesn't really have the time to go back through the thing and replace the original art with the new art.

But he commented that, if someone wants a free copy of the game and to test out their layout chops, he'll let you do it for him.

I love the game, and am interested in practicing some InDesign skills, but I simply do not have the time right now for another side-project. If no one else picks it up in a few months, though, I totally will do it to completion.

But anyway, if that sounds like fun to you, check it out. Here's the thread at RPGNet:

http://forum.rpg.net/showthread.php?t=446024

I can't find Simon's contact info on the Beyond Belief website, but you can IM him from RPGNet ("BeyondBelief").

    Posted By: Tim GrayI strongly suspect that Flaming Cobra won't take it until it's polished up a bit. Not sure whether that's the most appropriate channel for BoL anyway - what do others think?
    \nFlaming Cobra do have a penchant for republishing games that are already successful in their own right, and the ones that aren't tend to be very lavishly illustrated, so I'd say that it's highly likely you'd need a polished product to get anywhere with them. On the other hand, BoL has been getting some good web coverage and that is likely to count in your favour. FC aren't daft, they only want to publish books that they are reasonably certain will sell, and both excellent art and good provenance are strong selling points. Whichever way you look at it, it doesn't hurt to discuss things with them. If there's one thing about Mongoose Publishing, they are very approachable. I would recommend looking at all options available before making a firm decision though.

    Didn't know Cubicle 7 were accepting submissions though. I may have to talk to them myself.

    Oh, regarding size, have you looked at Crown Quarto? Can't remember the exact dimensions but it's about the same size as an old-fashioned school jotter book. Personally, it's one of my favourite book sizes. You've got plenty of width for a double-column spread, but the size of the book is much more manageable than letter size.

    Posted By: Tim GrayLightning Source's biggest hardback seems to be 7x10in - is that quarto?
    \nWell, approximately. It's about half an inch out in either direction, slightly taller but slightly narrower than Quarto. As for the lack of letter size, A4 is very similar so you could probably convert to that without too much fiddling around. Assuming you can get A4 of course. I generally feel that A4 converts to Letter a tiny bit better than Letter converts to A4, but it's pretty subjective.

    Incidentally, for low-cost layout solutions, keep an eye on computer magazine coverdisks. I got my copy of Quark from one of those, and it does everything that I really need with no license restrictions. Just make sure you check that commercial usage is OK.
