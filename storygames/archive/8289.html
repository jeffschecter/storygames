\nBeyond man\u2019s small blue world a vast cosmos awaits. What wonders of the universe are yet to be discovered? Is space more grand or terrible than we even imagine? Are we alone?

I've finished InSpace and it's ready for download. It takes the InSpectres' "players define the answer to the mystery" system and applies it to cosmic mysteries and the exploration of limitless space. Basically InSpectres meets 2001: A Space Odyssey.

InSpace: an InSpectres supplement

The supplement is free, but you'll need InSpectres to play of course.

Thanks to Shock: by Joshua A.C. Newman for proving you can tackle big sci fi in small games, Geiger Counter by Jonathan Walton for showing our crew that shared authority can be fun, and of course to Jared A. Sorensen for making InSpectres in the first place and turning detective work on its head.

Kudos to the brave crew of the DAUNTLESS (Mike \u201cStark\u201d Frost, Jem \u201cConrad\u201d Lewis, Ching-Ping \u201cKessler\u201d Lin, and Stephen \u201cDuchamps\u201d Scholz) and our other InSpectres gamers from the Third Eye Detectives, the Copernicus Agency, and the Gentlemen of the Royale Arms (Greg Gorden, Chris Haddad, Robert Haskell, and Kevin Lewis).

If you want actual play of the DAUNTLESS space missions, check them out yonder: http://www.story-games.com/forums/comments.php?DiscussionID=7599

    \nWe definitely played InSpace a lot, and not just that, but InSpectres in general with a bunch of different franchises.     Posted By: IgnotusAny suggestions for movies or tv shows to watch with the group before sitting down to play?
    \nI suspect there are a lot more "mysteries of the cosmos" books than movies -- blame Star Wars and Star Trek. They tilted movie sci fi in the other direction. TV is also hard because it leans towards episodic instead of big changes.

    I was thinking about movie examples and it hit me that the original Planet of the Apes actually works as an (atypical) InSpace game. No really: start with the raw premise that you've crash landed on a strange planet and know nothing else, then let the answer unfold from that. You can almost see the facts as they are revealed by the players.
