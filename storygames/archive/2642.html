\n Weird thing, and it may only just be me, but I was wondering if someone had some advice.

So, I'll look at my shelf every once in a while, and think "What do I want to play next month?" Inevitably, all of my PDF games get forgotten, and it's only later that I remember that I've got like 20-30 games in that format. Even, like, Dust Devils, and Dust Devils is one of my prime "fall back games" when a planned game session falls through!

So, when thinking about what to play in the upcoming weeks/months, how do you remember what's in your PDF repertoire? Any tricks for organization?

-Andy

    \n Living overseas leads me to have a lot of games in PDF format, to reduce shipping costs when possible.
    I have some of them (the small ones) printed and binded together with a coil, with a list of the games on the cover. The others are printed and binded one by one. All of them in the same self as the real game-books.
    When possible I have printed the covers in full color. I agree they help to convey the feeling to play the game.

    On my disk, there is a directory for each game, or for each publisher if I have more than one game from the same publisher. I have other directories for demos, little free stuff, downloaded web pages, etc. But sometimes I also use links with the names of games or relevant stuff in other directories when necessary to easily find things when searching by different criteria. Basically, I'm using the file-system as a tagging system. It is not perfect, but it is easy.
