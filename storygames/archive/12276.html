\n After the recent discussion about playtesting I thought I'd start a thread for publishers to solicit playtests.
\n
\nTo post, describe the game or supplement you want playtested and its current state, the pre-requisites for playtesters, your contact details and the timeline.
\n
\nInclude the number of sessions of play required, how many players are needed and whether the playtest includes pregenerated characters (if appropriate)
\n
\nIf you get enough playtesters, edit your post to say so, or else put a cut-off date.

    Wanted: Playtesters for two new short one-shot Purist adventures for Trail of Cthulhu.

    \n Wanted: Playtesters for short- to medium-length run of Star Lost.

    Star Lost is a space-opera RPG set in the present. The setting is primarily our local bubble of the galaxy, with the opportunity to play on Earth as well.

    The Galactic Union is on the cusp of war with the Thalock Protectorate, and Earth lies on the line between them.

    Players take on the roles of teenaged and young-adult runaways and street dwellers who have been secretly recruited into the paramilitary arm of the Galactic Union's Interplanetary Defense Council.

    As officers of the Police and Protection Corps, the players may be detectives, soldiers, diplomats and police \u2014 often all four at the same time. They may find themselves schmoozing at a state dinner, digging through alley garbage in the seediest part of town, or dodging blaster fire behind enemy lines.


    Star Lost is in the final stages of editing and layout and is slated for release in early 2011.

    What I need from you is commentary on\n
      \n
    • how well the manual reads
    • how well the rules work
      • \n
    \nIn recompense, I can offer complimentary copies of the pdf upon publication, with the possiblity of a bound edition for especially insightful help.

    There is still time for polishing things before publication, but playtest reports should be submitted before November. If you're interested, contact me at qhudspeth at hudspethgames.com and I'll get a document to you.

    Wanted: Playtesters for "Defiance: A Discourse on Civic Tyranny"

    Defiance is about insurrectionists in an fictional setting that resembles an 18th century European city on the verge of a violent uprising.

    As members of a small revolutionary cabal you may have to murder, steal, counterfeit, forge, intimidate, lie, incite, sabotage, torture and poison for the sake of liberty and justice.


    Defiance is in the final stages of editing and proofreading. You will need a roleplaying group of about 3 to 6 players.You will also need approximately 20 to 30 beads of one colour and 20 to 30 beads of another colour depending on the size of your group.


    Just like Quentin above, I need to know how well the book reads and how well the rules work.

    Playtester will receive a credit and free PDF copies of the completed game. Stealing Borrowing once more from Quentin's post, physical copies of the book will be given for especially insightful help.

    Playtest reports should be sent to me before the end of August. If you are interested in playtesting Defiance, please e-mail mj-graham at hotmail.co.uk.

    \nEdit: Playtesting is now closed

    Wanted: Playtesters for Ashen Stars (GUMSHOE Space)

    System: This is a stand-alone game using the GUMSHOE system.

    Premise: Ashen Stars places its central characters in a conflict between altruism and selfishness. This is reflected in its setting, pitting the idealism of a fallen utopian order against the harsh realities of a post-war environment.

    Who are the PCs: The PCs freelance law enforcement officers called lasers contracting their services to a central authority weakened by stellar war. They investigate kipdnappings, deliver cargoes, chase fugitives, collect debts, but they must keep careful track of their reputation, or miss out on future work.

    For 4-6 players. You do not need to be familiar with GUMSHOE to participate.

    Feedback by 1st August.

    Please email simon@dyingearth.com if you are interested.
