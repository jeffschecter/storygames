Posted By: grey.spiralI found it more difficult to find players for Old School gaming than I did for Forge-type stuff.
Not at all surprising! Classical RPGs tend to be rules-heavy, and that makes it a field of interest for relatively few souls. Modern RPGs is generally much easier to learn and play, with less emphasis on the game-bit, and so it has a better potential amongst non-gamers.

I believe that the modern games of today, and new games in the classical form (with a GM), have a greater chance of enticing a broader public. I believe they may be more open invitations to the engaging practice of dialogue based, creative interaction.

- How do we muster the potential presented in modern RPGs?
- Any thoughts?

    \nI disagree. What's important is that the rewards of the play experience are commensurate with the investment of creative and social effort required before play can happen. Many traditional games with broken social architectures fail this test. So one solution is to scale back the up front investment, making it match the actual reward magnitude of the play experience. But an equally reasonable, if more challenging solution is to optimize the rewards of play to match the game's necessary up front creative and social investment.

    The initial question again, anew;

    - Which rpg's of today have potential to entertain a (relatively) substantial part of the populace, if given the chance?

    MInd you; I am asking these questions to unearth possibilities, possibly in spite of a consensus that boardgames have lost out to computer games by now. The "what if" of my question take into consideration that the future is hard to predict, and as long as we make great games there is still a glimmer of hope ... :-)
