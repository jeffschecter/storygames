    \n You link to it above, but so people know explicitly what it is:
    This is the mission statement & model for our design group, Master Mines.
    In short: a Game Chef-like support network of designers that comment and contributes to everyone else's stuff. The goal is something like the activity of Game Chef, but in a form that can be sustained for longer periods.

    For me, a key element of this form is The Ping -- where if you aren't actively posting or commenting on the group in some length of time, others in the group ask you what's up and if there's anything that can be done to help out. I talk about this idea more on the site, so I won't go into depth here.

    \n How's this for a stab at a format for posts?

    I am: Mike Reeves-McMillan
    Located in: New Zealand
    Can offer: Editing (former book editor and technical writer, MA in English)
    Would like: Playtesting, general ideas-bouncing-off and critique/questioning to make my games better, maybe art (George/Northerain, do you have a website with any of your art?)
    Games and status:
    - Errantry (link is to PDF), a TSOY adaptation for tales of medieval chivalry, with "GM" functions split up and rotating among the players, intelligent-random NPC generation (playing card draw), and an overall "quest points" mechanic to provide a higher-level arc to the basically episodic structure. Needs playtesting.
    - City of Masks (link is to PDF), a competitive/collaborative game based in the setting of my (as-yet-unpublished) novel of the same name, a Renaissance city where everyone wears masks and your mask is your identity. Playing-card-based chargen, domino resolution system, win conditions, no GM. Has had one playtest and a revision, needs more.
    - The Pentasystem/Underground Railroad (link is to blog posts), my current project. Started out as a generic "adventure" system and is rapidly heading towards being about characters exercising their agency by getting behind social changes that are going on and pushing them in a particular direction. System is based on a mashup of TSOY, FATE, FLFS and Dogs, but with D6s and some other tweaks. Will probably be GMless or, if not quite, then almost. The Underground Railroad setting (intended to be the first of several) is steampunk fantasy with built-in themes of class, race, gender, religion, worker exploitation and postcolonialism if you want to go after them.
