1.

The doors of the elevator opened before her, revealing her destination: the last place she had expected to find herself today. She smelled sex and candy, and muttered aloud as she opened the door to her long-empty office: "Hmm. This surely is a dream."

Her twin sister, Candy, was sitting in the chair behind her desk, a nervous look on her face.

The last time Alice had seen Candy had been eight years ago when they fished her corpse out of the Hudson river - a corpse which, inexplicably, could not later be found at the morgue.

There was someone or something stretched out on the floor in front of the desk. The parts of the body seemed mis-matched; limbs were misshapen, wounds gaping, organs missing. The eyes, gaping open and unmoving, were those of a fly--Alice could see her surprised reflection in each of their facets.

Alice had seen something like this before in a dream she had the night her sister Candy's body was fished out of the river. [...] She'd visited a museum of curiosities in the basement of a teaching hospital, a dream that had seemed more real than any dream she'd ever had before.

Candy was pulling her stockings back on as Alice, utterly speechless, took in this phantasmagoric scene. A retinoscope, for conducting eye examinations, inlaid with gold and pearl, rested on the desk.

    \n2.

    "What do you think, Sis?" smiled Candy, clipping her second stocking into place. "Is seeing really believing?"

    Finally finding her voice, Alice managed to say, "Hi Sis, I'm so happy to see that you're still alive. Now what in the name of the 9th circle of Hell is going on here?"

    "What does it look like?" Candy shrugged. "I knew you didn't use this office much anymore... so I'm taking advantage of it."

    Hearing her sister's bossy tone reminded Alice that on that dark day when they found Candy's body, deep inside Alice's secret self she had finally felt free.

    Alice responded by crouching into her legs and leaping to the window.

    "I wouldn't do that if I were you, Sis, trust me. You open the window and the increased oxygen content will wake him up."

    Alice looked out the window and saw that the Mafia car was still waiting in the street outside; the burly goons were leaning on their car, smoking. Alice knew now that she would never get the thing she came for out of the office without Candy noticing. She was fairly sure that the goons, whoever they were, wouldn't get paid unless they delivered her back to the scene of the crime ("unhurt and unmolested", Alice had overheard that line clearly enough, even if she wasn't too certain of any other details).

    \n3.

    Alice moved her gaze back into the office, focusing her eyes on the black statuette that her sister (if that thing really was her sister) was holding. Alice thought to herself, "If I'm going to grab the Artefact it's now or never," and rushed forward to snatch it from Candy's fingers.

    Its eyes opened.

    Candy grinned wide and pushed the statuette into Alice's arms, relinquishing it willingly, even as the girl tried to backpedal, her eyes full of fear. It felt both cold and warm at the same time, both lifeless and very much alive. Moreover, it appeared to be telepathically communicating with her.

    \u201cAutoscopy acquired.\u201d

    Candy grinned wide and, without the slightest hesitation, in a single, well-rehearsed, smooth stroke, slit her own throat.

    \n4.

    As awareness returned, Alice saw a hundred images of herself. How strange. The moment of confusion passed when she saw her own lips move from 100 slightly different angles and heard her own voice say, "Hey, sis, enjoy your new body! Ciao, baby!"

    As Alice watched a hundred Candies stroll out of her office with the statuette, now inert, in their hands, she became aware of pain radiating throughout this new body - it felt like she'd been beaten half to death, and the wounds throbbed.

    Buzzing. She could hear it, just outside. Worse yet, Alice smelled rotting meat--and it was delicious.

    She propped herself up on one arm and looked: the cabinet behind her desk - the one she'd always planned to fill with Project Nero files, but never got around to actually doing... The smell was coming from there.

    \n5.

    Alice painfully dragged herself to the cabinet and opened the door. Inside, there was a rotting pig carcass wrapped in a plastic bag (how had she not noticed this delicious aroma earlier?).

    As she consumed the carcass, she gradually felt herself becoming human again, but for one thing.

    Her consciousness.

    \n6.

    "Alice" was inspecting her new body: apparently a 30-something male, dressed in a rumpled business suit, and rapidly healing before her eyes, when a swarm of flies, moving as a unit, attacked the window, cracking it.

    Insect sacs were spilling out of her body without organs. Before her eyes--which seemed remarkably human now--Alice saw the sacs burst forth with streams of flies. The flies joined with the ones just now breaking in through the window, and together they formed into... something amazing...

    The face-shaped swarm spoke with the buzzing of a thousand thousand flies. "They are coming. You must move fast." Black sparkle of space brought a thousand thousand thoughts of flies lamenting the removal of their sacred objects.

    Flies, flies, and more flies swarmed into the room, filling the air, rushing into Alice's mouth and ears, threatening to choke her, as she stumbled for the door... a vague sense of direction, like a homing beacon, was guiding her towards the Artefact which held her soul.

    \n7.

    Having lost their patience waiting outside, the Mafia goons had decided to come upstairs to investigate. The lead goon's face lit up with surprise at first, and then melted into cautious recognition.

    "Finster, what the hell are you doing up here?"

    The goon was holding a .50 caliber Desert Eagle in his right hand. He raised it to point at Alice (whom he thought of as "Finster", the fellow had gotten close with Candy, against the express orders of the boss). Seeing that Finster's shirt and pants were splattered with pig's blood, he hesitated instead of pulling the trigger.

    \n8.

    Alice had seen this goon on the car ride to her office--of course--but unbeknownst to him they'd met many years before at a Bachelor Party. His nickname was Rocky.

    Alice said: \u201cIt\u2019s your last night of freedom, Rocky \u2013 let\u2019s enjoy ourselves!\u201d

    Rocky reacted with a peculiar mixture of horror and delight. He allowed himself to be absorbed almost bodily into Alice/Finster's embrace.

    The other two goons, seeing Rocky hugging a strange man covered in blood, jumped back in alarm and confusion, drawing their own pistols.

    A bouquet of flowers fell to the ground as one of them drew his pistol.
