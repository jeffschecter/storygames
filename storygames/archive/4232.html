    \n I started with Moldvay and then went quickly to AD&D, so I dunno about Mentzer's BD&D (though I do have a copy of RC). I do know that, by the book, surviving past step 1 isn't all that easy. It's a lot more feasible in 3.x.

    That said, I could easily see the four steps above presented in a supplement, for those that want such a campaign structure. It'd be a lot more interesting than yet another campaign setting, that's for sure.

    If you change your medium to resemble another, more popular competing medium to acquire its consumers, you inevitably argue its merits, not your own.
