    \n Even before reading your post I wanted to suggest Lamentations otFP.
    Pros:
    -it is totally oldschool, while at the same time streamlined and updated
    -it is easy to make characters and play, as opposed to some newer editions or convoluted oldschool games trapped in their own lingo
    -it has quite a lot of cool advice and some awesome adventures (if you aren't using just the free core pdfs)
    -Ragi is unapologetic about challenging the players, and killing them mercilessly if the screw up, it's very step-on-up

    Another suggestion would be Dungeonslayers.
    -it's completely free, with a handful of expansions for it
    -it's simple and quite fast
    -it evokes a lot of the basic old D&D tropes and stuff without being D&D

    Now, both are awesome, but I think LotFP is better suited to more serious, darker, almost Call of Cthulu-esque gaming, even if there's nothing in the mechanics to suggest that outright, so if you want something less serious, Dungeonslayers is probably better for you.
