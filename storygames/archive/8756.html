So after a number of months of hemming and hawing about whether I would enjoy 3:16, I finally bit the bullet and played in Matthew Klien's game last night. And it pretty much rocked.

But while we were playing, I couldn't help thinking about these Space Marines turning into the Band of Brothers fighting in Nazi Germany, or Chesty Puller's Marines island-hopping in the Pacific.

So you WW2 fans out there, give me your great ideas on how to hack this game.

Obviously the weapons need to be renamed. And I would prefer to change Armor and Drugs into something else. But I don't get the feeling that much of the game really needs to be changed at all to fit what I am thinking of.

My only preferences would be to keep it general enough to be easily used in any part of the world during WW2. And to keep the tone light.

What should I change?

What should I keep?

    \n Oh, oh, missed this.

    I'm actually supposed to be talking to ....errr.... someone ... in a few weeks about a Band of Brothers-style of game. Umm, yeah, anyway... we'll see what happens. I think it's do-able. The one concern I do have is that these are other people you're killing. But I guess it never hurt Medal of Honor.

    ...one change that I'm putting in the fantasy version (almost done... oops) is that you can pick at character generation whether you want a re-roll on FA or NFA. You can't change it later on, but some people get a reroll on FA, while others choose it as NFA. Of course, if the character dies your new character can have a different re-roll choice.

    I really like Band of Brothers. To get that gritty feel you'd need more PC death I think, to end up with an ensemble cast over the campaign. Of course, "dead" characters might not actually be dead. They'd get a final flat roll or something, based on the AA. Higher than or equal to the AA - alive but invalided home. Less than the AA Shit! Actually dead.
