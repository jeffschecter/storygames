    Posted By: nemomemeMonsterhearts: Ben R managed to make playing a Vampire look fun and interesting and made moves that nudged our Chosen towards her Darkest Self. Our Mortal went there too. Teen angst galore.

    Oh, and we all learned a little something about Thorton Wilder'sOur Town.
    \nGood things come to those who pick random characters. I never would have picked vampire normally, but it worked perfectly to highlight other people's issues. The Monsterhearts template is pretty much perversely built to attract people but then keep them at arm's length, so that was a great fit. Picking the Chosen as the person who saved me rather than going a more obvious Chosen vs Vampire route also really paid off.

    I could see playing a lot more with those characters and really milking and twisting the relationships. I dug Jackson emancipating Casey (the Mortal) with some emerging bad-assery as a replacement for her unsatisfactory romances, and Christian just grabbed Carmen's (the Chosen) self-doubt and ran with it. Lovely. I definitely wanted to see more about Rowan the Fae's "stolen gift," and what would happen to Rowan if he couldn't fulfill his goal of getting in on with a true love (any true love, mind you). I could see serious trouble around episode 3.

    Also, homecoming dance with the Our Town graveyard in the background: creepy.

    \n For posterity, these were the games:

    Slot 1:
    GRG1: Old School Small World Hack (Brendan)
    S&C: Burning Wheel Trouble in Hochen (Wilhelm)
    APT1: Monsterhearts (Joe)

    Impromptu Dinner Gaming
    GRG1: Zombie Dice (Jon/Seth?)
    GRG2: Dixit (me)

    Slot 2:
    GRG1: Gamma World (Jon)
    S&C: Thunderstone / a rail game I can't remember (Wilhelm)
    APT1: Venetian Tragedy (Matthew)
    APT2: Drifter's Escape (Susan)

    We did lose some cohesion by hosting games in three different buildings -- the dinner run was a janky affair -- but the games themselves really benefited from less noise pollution. And the afterparty in the apartment was lots of fun. Ordinarily when you get 17 people in a room there will be three separate conversations but I see our story games training has made us very good at taking turns talking.

    As for the con-crud, it was potent but not persistent and I'm back at work today.

    Edit to add: Next time I do this I'll write up some stuff about how to cope with the really crappy parking in my hood.
