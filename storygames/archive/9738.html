    Posted By: deadlytoqueI thought it might be good to ask the people here if they have any information to share with Gnome Stew's readers
    \nThere is a basic "optimal" strategy of playing-the-numbers here. It goes like this:

    1. Always start social, and use your crappy dice here early; take lots of social fallout, it's good for you.
    2. In conjunction with #1, escalate yourself only after you've run through your crap rolls. The GM will often do it for you.
    3. Use social fallout to build a big trait (many d4's); then later change the trait to d6's, then d8's.

    Note that playing to the optimal strategy is completely in tune with the setup for play. But be aware that building a one-spell-johnny (Deadly Shot 8d8) is actually also an invitation for the GM to be a bastard to you, and throw stuff at you going "Is this bad enough to shoot? How about this?".

    \n Also be aware that while Levi's optimal strategy does work, there is some danger in it. It is possible to start taking lots of d4 social fallout, get invested, get escalated, and end up taking a few dice of higher fallout and end up on death's door. At that point all those d4s of fallout do count against you in the healing conflicts, and can result in you dying because the GM can just suck back a billion d4 based taking the blows.

    I shit you not, I have seen this happen more than once.
