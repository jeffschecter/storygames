\n We've created a new tradition here in Columbus, when the 25th of December rolls around we additionally celebrate Saturnalia, complete with a Lord of Misrule, funny hats, spiced wine, too much food, and holiday themed gaming!

This year, my friend Evan wrote a hack of Fiasco that he cleverly named Clusterf*ck, about four pages of rules that poked fun of all the tropes of those indie games we all love. There were aspects from fate, and index cards and sharpies, lots of agency everywhere, oh and double the sexyness. We had a blast playing high school alchemysts during the 2nd American Revolution, but most of us just wanted to make a friend or be kissed for the first time.

What holiday games did you play? Planning any for the new year?

    \n We played Shooting the Moon, set in a Macy's during the run-up to Christmas. The Beloved was Kristen, head elf, whose dream was to have a perfect holiday season. The Suitors were Trixie, a short, larcenous Santa's Helper (Santa being her uncle), and Darla, a dour pre-med student volunteering at the charity-sponsored gift-wrapping booth. They were vying for a New Year's Eve date with Kristen. Stern store management! Stern charity management! Whiny children! Whinier parents! Carolers in period costume! Drunk Santa! Drunk college girls wanting picture with drunk Santa! Hostage crisis with deranged, fired, drunk Santa!

    We all wanted to know more about the secret life of one of the NPCs, Bill, the store handyman, who we discovered was more comfortable as Willa. Need to cover up a shoplifting dye explosion? Willa's got the make-up. Need to charm a couple cops when the Christmas party gets too rowdy? Willa's got the moves. The world needs more Willa.

    \n We played a "Bad Santa"-inspired Freeasco set in a crappy mall in Burbank. (Do people here know about Freeasco? Caroline, one of the Seattle Story Games regulars, came up with a way to quickly generate a mini-playset for Fiasco on the fly. It's quite cool and actually deserves its own thread.) Santa didn't end up being in the Freeasco, but there was lots of behind-the-scenes badness with drug-dealing elves, age-inappropriate seduction, and a mysterious left-behind present that the mall security guard was afraid might be a bomb but which turned out to only be a human head.
