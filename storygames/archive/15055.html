Mouse Guard Summer 1153

The patrol is investigating a gopher hole and encounter a steep grade. Halberd-wielding Edmund has affixed a torch to his polearm and is on point. The grade begins to grow muddy and the patrol has to make rolls to keep their footing. Edmund fails and begins to slide down the steep incline. Thinking quickly, he turns onto his back, using his shield like a sled. So, now he's flying down this incline backwards and upside down with his halberd in front of him like a lance and his torch sputtering as he gains speed.

The tunnel levels out and the halberd tip plants into the ground, extinguishing his torch and vaulting Edmund through the darkness who deftly rights himself as he flies through the air and lands in a defensive crouch with his shield raised towards the massive dark tunnel ahead.

The best part? When the patrol caught up with him, Edmund, being a sober, humble soldier didn't even mention it.

    \n Lady Blackbird, second session
    \n
    \nHaving escaped Nightsong's bounty hunters, the party navigated the Remnants to find Uriah Flint's fortress destroyed by a mysterious enemy, many of his pirates seeming to have killed themselves. They search the fortress and find a distraught Uriah(Cyrus shoots him in some confusion, which seriously upsets Lady Blackbird). It seems a few of Uriah's crew were taken instead of killed, including some of Uriah's closest mates. He gives up his title to convince Cyrus to help him rescue his allies. Following signal trails, they find a hidden rock on the outskirts of the blue, inhabited by squid-people, who are found to be mutated people who can sing the songs of the sky squid. The crew, having been captured to be ritual sacrifices, are rescued and the squid-people's ritual completes, summoning an enormous sky squid which, without other sacrifices, devoured a great deal of squid people while The Owl makes its escape.
    \n

    \n Dungeon World, playtesting my "Wreck of the Indigo Galleon" module.

    The party slays the decatarch of the octopus folk before it can sacrifice a captured band of pirates and trigger the Great Miration. Then Thalion the Wizard suffers a mortal wound and the Guardian of the Black Gate says that if he wants to live he must replace the decatarch!

    Thalion returns to this realm and draws a mob of enraged octopus folk towards the party and the prisoners, who are all busy looting the cave's treasure chamber. In the ensuing melee, Thalion's emerald lancing magic missiles kill many of the prisoners -- and also Randy the fighter! But Randy boxcars his Last Breath roll. He is too beautiful to die, and escapes with a gnarly scar that only makes him more compelling.

    Randy and Wesley the cleric and a few of the sacrifices manage to escape just as Thalion triggers the Great Migration. As the octopus folk ascend aboard their god-ark, the treacherous wizard activates his Carouse move and surrenders to the timeless alien ecstasies of the Far Realm.

    \n Just had session 3 of an Apocalypse World game; I'm the MC:

    [first, a session 2 recap] Rue the Gunlugger stepped on a hypo looking for a place to camp for the night, so he goes back into town and finds the local junkie, Mimi, and sticks a gun in her face and tells her not to do it again. She backs down, cries a little, and he decides it'd be pretty fucked up to hurt her. He leaves her with her boyfriend from the Mudflats and goes on his way.
    He doesn't end up getting sick or anything from the needle, so he puts a bandaid on it and forgets about it.
    Cue the boyfriend, Bar, getting really pissed and going to tell his farming buddies "this asshole Rue tried to kill me!"

    So I hand Rue a little love letter I wrote - - to paraphrase, he rolled +sharp really badly and the farmers grabbed him, but he Acted Under Fire and grabbed his machete, and they all backed off. He put his blade in Bar's head, and then shot both of the others as they ran away.
    The gunfire attracted some Mudflats folks and Diamond, the local chopper.
    After a bit of a misunderstanding, the Mudflatters attacked Diamond and crew, fended them off, and then Boo the Angel showed up and said she'd be happy to patch everyone up.
    The Mudflatters were really happy about that - they kind of get shit on by the hill people, and when Boo managed to completely revive Bar and heal his injuries, they decided to call it a truce.
    This was our first actual fight scene, and our first use of Seize by Force - Diamond used this a couple of times to go toe to toe with the Mudflatters alone. We still haven't used Read a Sitch or Read a Person, but that's partly from them not being super curious about the Basic Moves sheet and relying a lot on me for how the system works (which is fine; this is their first time playing AW).

    Oh, and our first advance happened today! Diamond the Chopper is gonna think about it for a bit before he chooses, but it's exciting.

    EDIT: Oh, and the best line of the night goes to Rue the Gunlugger.
    Rue: Ha, those assholes are all up in the tunnel, and they're trapped.
    MC: So what do you do?
    Rue: I got my rifle, right? I'm gonna go up to the mouth of the tunnel and start shooting.
    MC: You do remember that Boo, the Angel, is up there, yeah?
    Rue: Oh, I remember. [longish pause] Wouldn't want to shoot him, now would we?
    MC: Would we?
    Rue: Nah. I won't hurt him. I just keep an eye on the tunnel.

    \n [Sunday Skypers 3]
    In a nutshell, used the DRYH game mechanics set in the Warhammer Fantasy universe (first book, I believe.) It's a generally grim and nasty setting with filth and grime turned up to 11. The central point that bound the story together is a strange inscribed stone tablet that has a corrupting/chaos influence on the people who have handled it (the PCs, of course.) Add in a ton of fear from a warmongering church, too much ambition, and too little impulse control. In some ways, it played like a fantasy version of Paranoia with everyone being a part of a secret society, having a 'mutation', and a secret desire to mess with the stone for better or worse. I'm sure the church would have us all tortured, executed, burned, and our ashes scattered if they knew we were playing with the magical equivalent of a dirty bomb. [Yes, this was 'fun' and a lot of the hurt inflicted we did to ourselves.] :-)

    \n [Sunday Skypers 5]
    Excellent fit in my opinion. From the GM's pitch, the setting was inspired by the earlier editions of the rules and the Enemy Within campaign. He sold the grim and desperate parts pretty well. The three dice pools (discipline, grim, chaos) represented your primary mundane skill, your level of injury/fatigue/desperation, and your chaos taint/power. Without going on about DRYH mechanics too much, the more dice (discipline, exhaustion, madness) you have in your pool influences how the outcome is narrated.

    \n Last night my Pathfinder group completed their first adventure, the classic D&D 3.0 module The Sunless Citadel. They killed the evil druid, chopped down the evil tree and put the possessed adventurers they were supposed to rescue out of their misery. The most interesting and hilarious stuff came afterwards, as they cleared out the rest of the dungeon. Our dragon-obsessed fire sorceror decided to free Calcryx, the kobolds' unwilling white dragon whelp mascot. He's already got CHA 20 got a 24-hour +5 from the Ashardalon Shrine, so he easily convinces Meepo the kobold keeper of the dragon to join the party and free the dragon. The dragon flies back to the surface and Meepo scurries after it, while the party deals with some abysmally low climb checks (including two back-to-back critical fails). When they finally make it to the top, they find the bottom half of Meepo lying in a pool of blood, and Calcryx soars overhead, screeching a thank-you. The fighter, who spent several adventures hanging out getting drunk with Meepo due to real-world player absence, took a dragon-shaped bottle-stopper from the corpse as a token of remembrance. Then, back to town for rewards, petty larceny and a big party at the local tavern. I was stifling delighted laughter the whole time.
