    \n Yay, four!

    Okay, so here's the deal. In this hypothetical game, everything that's important (characters, sets, props, organizations, ideals) is statted out on an index card. On that index card, that thing has three Resources and three Relationships. Resources are basically anything that might be a Dogs trait: some characteristic of the thing that might be useful in the course of the game. Relationships are connections to specific other things ("supports George W. Bush"), not generalities ("likes Americans"). Presumably this hypothetical game assigns some dice to them at some point, but we're not going to worry about that yet. We're just going to list three Resources and three Relationships. Stupid example:\n
    Tony the Tiger
    Resources:
    Blindingly Orange
    Disturbingly Large
    "Tastes grrrrrrreat!"
    Relationships:
    Loves Frosted Flakes
    Works for Kellogg
    Best Friend, Little Timmy
    \nWe're going to start with each of you making your Player Character. Let's assume a sort of generic fantasy sword-n-sorcery kinda setting, since it's easy.

    So please, all four of you, each "make a character" by listing off three Resources and two Relationships. Leave the last Relationship blank for now.

    \n Alright, awesome!

    So now, if we were at a tabletop, what we'd do is take blank index cards and write the names of the relationships across the tops. Then we'd pass one to the player on our right and one to the player on our left. Those players then assign three resources and three relationships to the new index cards.

    Since we're on a forum, the basic idea will be the same but the implementation will be slightly different. No index cards, after all. So we'll use the order that you guys signed up: Mike, Ryan, Caesar, and Rich. Your first relationship will go to the player 'below' you and your second relationship will go to the player 'above' you.

    So:
    Mike gets to create Rich's Magic Guild and Ryan's God of Money.
    Ryan gets to create Mike's beer and Caesar's Jun the moneylender.
    Caesar gets to create Ryan's father and Rich's Alfonse the Grey.
    Rich gets to create Caesar's father and Mike's long-suffering horse.

    So we need one round of each of you picking one of those things and giving it three resources and three relationships. One of those relationships must go back to the element that spawned it (Rich's Magic Guild must have a relationship to Rich's PC). One of those relationships must be to a different player character. One of those relationships must be to a new setting element.

    That new setting element will get its own virtual index cards and be passed to the next guy in the player list. You'll again have two things to choose from for round three.

    In round three, one relationship must go back to the element that spawned it. One relationship must be to another established setting element. One relationship must be to another player character.

    I'll explain rounds four and so on when we get there.

    Right now, if everybody could do round two: pick one of the elements that you got from your neighbor and stat it up. Make sure one of the relationships is to a different player character. One should go back to its origin. The other two relationships relationship should be to a completely new element.

    Readysetgo!

      Round Three (nearly identical to Round Two):
      Select one of the three elements you have. Create it with three relationships, one relationship going back to the element that spawned it and the other to an already created element. The last relationship goes to a new element, which passes to the left.

      Round Four
      Select one of the three elements you have. Create it with three relationships, one going back to origin, the other two going to existing elements.

      Round Five
      Select one of the two elements you have. Create it with three relationships, one going back to origin, the other two going to existing elements.

      Round Six
      Create the last setting element with three relationships, one going back to origin, the other two going to existing elements.
