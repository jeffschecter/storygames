    judson had asked
    * AD&D before Paranoia as a pick up?
    Yeah, my husband says it's strange, too, but while I enjoy reading Paranoia, and I enjoy watching it played, I can't stand playing or running it. We've all got our preferences, and something in Paranoia tweaks me. It just isn't FUN to me if I'm involved with it. Which isn't to say that I won't use it to influence other games I'm running (especially Kult) but experience has shown me that I should spend my time with things I find fun. As for using AD&D, I'm third generation gamer; I dream Umber Hulk stats, and tried to use THAC0 as a way of determining what kind of car to get. Besides, it's flexible enough that I can go the humourous route (Thief-Paladins of "Big Mac Daddy" and corporate kobolds), the horror route ("How many elfs can you fit into a chest?") and/or the classic route without any real preparation (which brings ut to the point of the thread, I think.)
    * What kind of house ruling have you been doing to Dogs without being familiar enough to run it off the cuff?
    You ever been in that position where you (mis)hear something and your brain translates it and then decides to work from there? For example, there's this power (I think it was from SJG's In Nomine) where you can transfer yourself from plant to plant. I'm the kind of person who, of course, combined this with the classic Swamp Thing in Gotham, and figured that meant an angel could (with appropriate modifiers) transfer to someone's intestinal flora, with, um, results as per Earth physics. ("Boom.") I kind of didn't read all of the rules of Dogs in such a way as I think the author intended, and while I certainly mean to someday ask the questions, in the meantime, we've been using our "broken" model and, of course, then fixing it "our way," and expanding it.

    You know. Tinkering.
