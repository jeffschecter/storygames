\n I'm starting this thread because I feel like TSOY needs an endless amount of praise heaped on it. I find myself thinking up cool ideas for games and being like, "Man, that would be an awesome piece of cake in TSOY."

Hip-hop meets Akira Kurosawa meets John Woo? TSOY (eventually I hope to put out a little for-free hack of this one)
Cosmic steampunk? TSOY
Star Trek: 1966? TSOY

It's so fiddley - I feel like I could literally do anything action adventure-y with it. The combination of Keys and BDTP are the peanut butter and jelly of an awesome action game. Also: CREATIVE COMMONS'ED.

Man, I don't know if threads like these are kosher here, but my love for TSOY cannot - nay - WILL NOT be contained.

    \n The I shall bring down the pain on you! Players can downshift from broad, conflict-y conflict to granular, task-y conflict in situations of supreme importance to their characters. This is super important because it is the only way to permanently remove named NPCs, and because only players can do it - not the GM. It's a very elegant and exciting way to handle situations the players care a lot about, and usually involves high stakes and permanent changes to the game. Last night Steve entered Bringing Down the Pain against Colonel James Pettigrew, a southern cavalry officer who rides with Paul Revere's magic spurs (yeah, that's right fool, Paul Revere's magic spurs.) And they fought, and Steve lost bad, and he ended up with a permanent spur-print in the side of his (invulnerable, super) character's face. It was a big scene and Steve's guy, Harwood, was burning resources right and left to stay in the fight.

    Posted By: jdrakehI gather from various individuals that there a few not insignificant changes between the two (I own 1st edition but haven't yet obtained a copy of 2nd edition to verify these claims personally).
    \nBigtime. 1st edition (which I ran for about six months) was "roll 2d6, add skill, and consult Unisystem-like chart for result". Skills were divided into "groups" like Artistic, Martial, etc.

    2nd Edition runs on what Clinton dubbed "The Solar System", which is "Fudge Dice, no chart consulting, no skill groups". Also, the damage for BDTP was completely different (personally, I like the feel of the first edition damage system more, but as-is it could be totally twunked into non-fun easily).

If anybody was developing a project for TSOY 1e and had this happen, I suspect that they won't risk having it happen a second time (however unlikely it may be).

    Also, if you guys don't mind, I'd like to comment on the two editions. The change had three real parts:

    - A change from 2d6 to 3 Fudge dice, and a change in the ability scale from 1-10 to 0-4 Mathematically, the odds are almost exactly the same as in the first edition, but it allowed me to remove a chart.       \n Christopher,

      The pool discussion threatens to widen the scope of this thread. Here's my quick answer that I haven't thought completely through: if you want to talk more about it, let's start a new thread, here or on my forum at the Forge.

      Pools are what make up a person in the game. They are prerequisites for person-hood. In TSOY, without Vigor, Instinct, and Reason, you are not a person. This holds for adventure swords and sorcery, but what about other settings? I could see a game of TSOY set in, say, the Crusades, that used God, Man, and the Devil as pools. Or, a fairly hard sci-fi game with Vision, Craft, and Intuition as pools.

      I'm pretty sure more pools won't work. Two might work pretty well, though - but I haven't tried it.

      Posted By: jdrakehAh, I didn't bother to check and see if the CC license was a non-commerical one.Thiswould explain the lack of third-party publisher support. Because itisa money thing. This is one of the reasons that games released under the OGL see a metric ton of third-party support and games released under a non-commercial CC Share-Alike license see almost none. Most publishers want to get paid for the time and money that they devote to a given work. And that, despite sentiments that it shouldn't be the case, is.
      Ehm, only the text of the book is licensed under a non-commercial CC-license, AFAIK. The basic Solar System and even parts of the setting are licensed under CC-licenses that are still share-alike but allow commercial use.

      That said, I think the two big reasons for a "lack" of commercial third party support are: (1) The commercially usable versions are hard to find, so most people think its only availabe for non-commercial use and (2) many people balk at the idea of using something licensed as Share Alike, thus making their work available in an open format/under an open license, because that would mean that other people could "steal their work" or something. (That making something open can actually be a good thing is something that's often forgotten, though.)

      Personally, I disagree, even though I can see where they are coming from. But I think it's defenitly a factor in people using TSOY/Solar System for their own games.

      \n Nate, Eero Tuovinen has posted out some rules regarding that. At the center of this is the Secret of Envy, which allows you to give Gift Dice away as Penalty Dice.       And it's easier to provide gift dice through supplemental actions than through a complete BDTP.
