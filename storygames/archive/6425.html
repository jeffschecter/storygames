\n I'm seeking: audience-friendly RPGs. That could include:

- RPGs that are inherently fun to watch for a non-participating audience
- RPGs that have features to allow participation from an irregular, non-commited audience

(For my purposes, you can assume there are players who are not audience.)

Any suggestions?

    \n We're seeing a lot of suggestions on games that can involve audience participation. But that was only one of the two proposals. I'm interested if there are any RPGs that can be said to be more entertaining for audience members as just that... passive audience members?
    \n
    \nWhich are good to watch?
    \n
    \nI'm actually of the opinion that RPGs generally make terrible fiction, and that they're only really consumable as a direct participant. But I'm willing to be proven wrong if somebody has a counter-example. Mickey suggests IAWA, and I can see that one being distinctly superior to some other games out there. Though I don't know if I'd stick around and watch a game of it played.
    \n
    \nDon't get me wrong, I often observe play. But it's rarely for the fiction, and really more to check out the player interactions with each other and the mechanics. More like research. I assume that most people won't share that proclivity.
