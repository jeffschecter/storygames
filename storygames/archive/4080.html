\n When the internet get's me down, I start a thread somewhere about Githyanki.

Silver word wielding, bony, Liche-Queen Worshipping, 10,000 year old race-war having mofo's remind me why I game.

I will attempt to weave Githyanki into as many games as I can think of before a meeting on my schedule.

Reign
Players are Githyanki Generals, taking down a Prime Material Plane world with flying V's of Red Dragons and brutal Warlocks as Githzarai enter the plane to claim their own chunk. Its ON!

Dogs in the Vineyard
Players are the Liche Queen's champions, traveling across the Astral Plane with Silver Swords in hand, administering her unholy justice.

Conspiracy of Shadows
Players are a cabal of Githyanki soldiers who know their timing to feed the Liche Queen is coming and the conspiracy is their entire nation.

Burning Wheel
Intrigue and brutality in the courts of T'unnarath, players navigate the courts of the Liche Queen where worlds hang in the balance even as priests from Vecna come to the palace, proposing a marriage.

Sorcerer
Humanity is realizing that other creatures are in fact sentient beasts with demons as silver swords, dragon steeds and psionic powers given by their long ago

Anyone can join!

Cleanse yourself in the astral pools of the Githyanki!

Take an idea and run with it or make your own.

    \n Sweet, I've got a little rant about the Githyanki to share.
    \n
    \nSo, these dudes are all, like, warrior-monks who are ruled by the undead Lich-Queen, and they live in their flying castle-citadels in the Astral Plane. Umm, okay, but... where do they get their food? What do they do with their shit? How do they build these citadels anyway? Isn't the Astral Plane empty?
    \n
    \nOh, right. They build their citadels on the giant inert bodies of dead giant gods! Okay, sure. Isn't the dead god body an optional rule? Anyway, okay, I can see them having a biosphere thing on their dead god body, without pooping all over the Astral Plane. And sure, they can just get supplies during raids of the Prime Material Plane - they brought their stone with them when they originally immigrated to the Astral Plane.
    \n
    \nYeah, I can get down with all that, sure. But do they ALL have to be this warrior-monk thing? Every single one wields a silver sword and fights the githzerai and stuff? Don't they need, like, peasants, or farmers - uh, I guess they would be more like gardeners, really, gardening their food off the dead god. Or what? Do they just take shifts doing that? Hey Szaryff, it's your turn to spend six months growing food at the citadel, I get to wield that sword now.
    \n
    \nHuh. I guess I'm down with that. Whatever the crazy Lich-Queen wants her society to be, I guess. Still, there's one thing that still bugs me. I can't quite find a way around this one. I mean, DnD has got some pretty ridiculous world-building in it, but I think this one takes the cake.
    \n
    \nIt says right there in the book: time does not exist in the Astral Plane.
    \n
    \nTime. does NOT. exist.
    \nin the Astral Plane.
    \n
    \nSo, a] why is their Queen undead? Nobody gets old in the Astral Plane! and b] where do the githyanki grow up? heal their wounds? breed?
    \n
    \nCause they ain't doing that stuff where they're supposed to be living!

    Posted By: johnstonewhere do they get their food?

    Posted By: johnstonedo they ALL have to be this warrior-monk thing?
    \nJust the ones your average D&D player is likely to meet. According to the source material, the Githyanki are profoundly against enslavement, so you're not going to find Githyanki serfs. Given that Githyanki don't need to eat or sleep, and the Astral Plane is connected to all points in the Prime Material Plane they could conceivably persist as an exclusively raider culture. So it's possible that Githyanki that don't look like they're going to cut it as warrior-monks are killed as children.

    Posted By: Ben JohnsonAny other plane, but most commonly the Prime Material Plane. Githyanki have outposts all over the place. Also, I believe githyanki hatch from eggs, so clutches can be hidden just about anywhere that would allow for protected hatching.
    \nAlso, some dead gods are surrounded by zones where time works normally, if I recall my second edition guide to the Astral Plane correctly. One interesting thing about these is that the Githyanki who have been around for the longest time need to avoid these (and other planes, where time works normally), since once you enter an such an area, time doesn't just start again for you: it catches up. So if you spend 100 years on the astral, and then hop over to the prime material plane (without the proper magical protection), you age 100 years instantly. Whoops!

    (Which is a potential reason to seek Lichdom, so if some series of events warrants you having to travel to another plane of existence, your life won't hinge on nobody thinking of hitting you with a dispel magic.)

      Githyanki Sorcerer Style

      Everyone starts off with the same demon, the Liche-Queen. Their binding scene is how they presented themselves to her and swore their lives to her. Yes, you can leave her service by Banishing her.

      When you hit Humanity 0, the Liche Queen comes to claim your soul and devour everything that you are.

      Humanity is empathy.

      Demons can be any of the trappings of Githyanki power that they bring with them from the astral plane:
      Silver swords, red dragons, hollow-eyed warlocks, bloodthirsty legions

      Oddly, most descriptors are parts of your character that deviate from normal Githyanki-ness. These are the parts that you are questioning.

      Stamina:

      Tired of Violence: You have seen so many worlds crushed before the Githyanki legions and your mastery of the blade is legendary but you tire of the blood.

      Cruelty: You aren't sure anymore if you are cruel because the Liche Queen demands it or if only because you enjoy it.

      Sick: You have been stricken with some kind of virus from one of the many worlds that you have crushed. It is killing you, even now.


      Will

      Love: You have gained a love of something apart from he Githyanki people or their wondrous queen, who gave up her own life to lead you all for eternity. This feeling has eclipsed your love of her, eclipsed your hatred of illithid and githzarai...what won't you do for it?

      More Gith: You have gained a trait that you know the other Githyanki would hate you for having and you are hiding it behind the facade of a sterling representation of what you believe a Githyanki should be.

      Guilt: You have destroyed countless beautiful worlds and believe that you and your people deserve to be next.

      Lore:
      This is the part of Githyanki culture that is the primary fuel to your character's ability to martial your people's resources from beyond.

      General: You are a leader, ushering the legions into unsuspecting worlds.

      Warlock: You have been taught arcane wonders from Red Dragon tutors for use in pacifying the planes.

      Psionic: Your mind keeps you linked to the Astral Plane and keeps you balanced when a world burns.
