Fred Hicks and Rob Donoghue. Rob Donoghue and Fred Hicks. Together they made FATE and Spirit of the Century, and are working on Dresden Files. One has the other's back, and vice-versa. Just about anything you see one of their names' on, you'll see the other's name on.

I'm embarrassed to say that often it's REALLY hard to tell the two of them apart, as their works and interests collide so often that I often think of them as "one entity".

Here's what I know:

Fred wrote Don't Rest Your Head.
Fred often goes by the handle "Iago" or the equivalent here and there on the web.
Fred's LJ handle is "Drivingblind"

Rob often goes by his own name here and there on the web.
Rob's LJ handle is "rob_donoghue"

Both are awesome, terrifica guys. In a hobby industry framed by How Much of a Jerk You Can Be, they are very unjerkful. Er, yeah that'sa real word, look it up.
I'm pretty sure that one of them killed a hooker in Reno, but I'm not sure who.

So, for those in the know, how would one tell Rob apart from Fred? Fred from Rob?
Who would win in a fight?
Who could make a more tastier fajita burrito?

    ... That said, here's my more serious take on telling us apart:

    * I'm considerably less patient than Rob (this is part of why I have spat out a number of micro-game designs, but Rob has done the majority portion of the heavy lifting on our larger projects like the free version of Fate and Spirit of the Century)

    * I'm much more likely (at least in the past) to explode in ill-advised flames of hellfire due to "posting angry". I've been doing my best to follow Rob's example here in more recent months. I think other people were charitable in calling me "excitable", but no charity's necessary: I can be a real dick, while Rob's a consistently great guy.

    * I tend to get laser-focused and obsessive. Rob takes a broader approach, working on several things at once. This means that I might power through one single thing very rapidly, while Rob can take on a large workload and make sure it all moves forward in smaller increments. While these two styles can clash on larger projects, we've had a good four or five years to figure out where they clash and where they team up to fight crime, and we've been fighting more crime than clashing as the years progress.

    * Rob is in general far more insightful than I am (just look at his latest livejournal posts to get an idea of what I'm talking about here). I might take an established concept and run it into the ground using twelve different appliations of it, but I would casually and easily credit Rob as the guy who breaks new conceptual ground in the Evil Hat team.

    * I'm more of an online marketeer/company-presence guy. This may be why some of you feel you've got more of a handle on me than Rob, unless you've met us in person. If you've met us in person, you know why Rob is easily at least half of Evil Hat by himself. Rob has people skills I just don't. Not that he'd ever agree with me on that point.

    There's more, but really in the end it comes down to this: Evil Hat would never have happened without Rob. He's the one who put in the elbow grease to get Fate from a little homebrew I was running Amber with into something that was available for human public consumption. No design I've done has *ever* survived first contact with him, and the revisions I make in the face of that always produce a better game. While I may be the "face" of the Hat at times, he's most definitely the heart.

    Or to put it this way. In the Fastball Special that we are, he's Colossus and I'm Wolverine. Sure, I can rip that Sentinel apart if it gets near me, but I've got no range, no reach, and no kinetic power without Rob winding up and giving me a good, healthy toss.
