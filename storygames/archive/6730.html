\n Okay, so I have Mortal Coil and am itching to play, but then I see that it requires four different colored tokens! Yikes, I only have a set of poker chips with three colors and a bag of glass beads (one color). So, I'm considering using the poker chips and then perhaps pennies or some other coin for the 4th token (either action or power) or making my way to the craft store (I mean taking my fiancee on a shopping trip for yarn) and picking up some other bead colors. But I'm sure there are other options as well. So, what have you used, or what would you use, for games that use four or more different tokens?

    \n If only one kind of token is required (like for DRYH's coins of despair/hope) I like Japanese 5-yen coins, or in a pinch the flimsier aluminum 1-yen coins. For 3 or more kinds of tokens, I like clay poker chips. I bought a set of 3 colors, then bought extra colors at the estimable Endgame although they're readily available online as well. They're heavy, but have a much nicer feel than plastic chips. I found it very useful when I ran The Mountain Witch to use a different color for each Ronin's trust, or in Agon to use a color for each hero's oaths owed him, plus white for strife/glory and red for 5 strife/glory. That way I can start with a big pile of strife in front of me and over the course of the game it turns into big piles of glory divided among the heroes.

    For Mortal Coil I used white chips for action, red for passion, blue for power, green for magic, and if playing with high-magic, black to represent either 5 or 10 magic, just to keep the number of chips down.

    If weight is a concern (like at conventions I fly to) I have some teensy colored translucent plastic discs,
    but they're not as visually interesting. They're excellent for Bliss Stage to mark intact/damaged/destroyed
    equipment with green/yellow/red, though.
