  • Mo
    May 2006
    On the medium:

    Slow. As. Death. One thing that the slowness pushes you to, though, is to really examine the conflict at hand. Because you have to invest more in every single transaction, every other single transaction has an elevated importance. While you wait for the other person to respond there' s a strong anticipatory pull, that while sometimes frustrating, when playing with a cohesive social dynamic can do really good things for play.

    On the comparative quality of the conflicts:

    As the only person that's played Dogs with Brand in BitV and in real life, I can honestly say that the quality of conflicts is very similar. In general, because Brand is so descriptive, so verbal, and has such a knack for delivering pain, (most of) his players have a hightened sense of investment into the interaction. What may be different is the concentration fo quality conflict. Becaise of the medium, every transaction leads that way rather than just every transaction of the conflict.

    On freeplay:

    I love freeplay, especially in the early stages of a game. I socket heavily through character, and getting into "the seat" is critical to my enjoyment of the game. Once the game gets going, and I have a strong context for character, I need less and less freeplay (but still would rather have a little in every game). For some reason, I really like having a little freeplay at the end of any serial game, which often makes Brand roll his eyes in our regular sessions.

    I almost said that this is because our games have the emotional volume turned way up and freeplay allows me to relax after the game, but that must be a lie, especially since some of our most emotional stuff comes out of freeplay. I think maybe it's because I have to work with active skills to find my enjoyment in many systems, and find freeplay can let me relax into the immersion seat and complicate the world. That said, I've never had much desire for freeplay in Dogs, and that's likely because I find it pretty intuitive, and have to fight it less. However in this session, it was necessary because not everybody got a chance to get in and get their hands dirty IC.
