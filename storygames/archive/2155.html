    January 2007
    cut and pasted from our local groups message board (with permission)

    Ok, here is a zippy quick summary of the next campaign:

    There is a magical pendant that records the 'essence' of every person who owns it. Every player is one such person (with one exception if someone wants to be the modern ally). In the present day, there is a man who discovered how to 'download' those essences, esentially returning your characters to life at the exact moment they last had contact with the pendant. This man used your characters for his own less than noble ends and, eventually, you all left his service with the help of an ally at UNC-CH (to be determined in more detail).

    Now, the strange magic that brought you all back also makes you weird magnets, so you all have joined together and made the most of that--I'm thinking maybe running a paranormal PI-service, perhaps just sticking together so that there are more of you to deal with the weirdness that comes your way.

    So far, here are ideas people have mentioned really liking:

    Katie: German alchemist
    Roy: Indian War-era pistolero
    Stan: very tentatively, a ritual magician
    SBR: 1500's German mercenary who has gone native in biker culture

    I continue to lean more and more toward True20 with a Mutants & Masterminds (sort of like Champions for d20) hack so that you guys can create your characters with a point-build system rather than a level-based one. That allows for some big differences in what your characters will be good at. The superpower system from M&M might also be a nice way to build in some low-level weirdness into your characters. The system isn't strictly linear in advancement, either, so we wouldn't run into the issue of everything having to be bigger and meaner.

    Other random things:

    Not all the weirdness in the world has to do directly with the pendant, although I plan for some recurring weirdness to happen around that.

    I am all about designing short arcs around fun bits of NC color: things like going to the State Fair, Halloween in downtown CH, heading into the stacks of Duke's nice gothic library for research, scary things happening in RTP because of corporate experimentation, and so on. I have a book about NC hauntings and plan to use it;). Please feel free to throw out other things that just sound fun to you.

    The weirdness is not obvious in the world except to you, who seem to attract it. Most everyone else in the game world thinks they live in the same triangle we players live in.
