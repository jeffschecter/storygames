\n Hi everyone!

Please, allow me to brag a bit about something that costed us a lot of work but came out really nice.
Narrattiva is the italian distributor of My Life with Master and this year, at our booth at Lucca Comics and Games Convention, we brought a peculiar "promotional item".

It was the oversized version of our edition of MLwM: a little thing that, once open, is 140 x 100 cm.




As we hoped, many people got curious about our "little book" and it was an awesome booth prop
Also, demoing the game under its hulking presence felt really appropriate.

    \n @ Jonn:
    We could discuss this argument at length but I have to wake up for work in less than 5 hours foe work so I hope you\u2019ll excuse the shortness of my answer.

    I\u2019d say italian gaming scene is small, asphictic and totally dominated by D&D.

    As for trends we\u2019re releasing games quite unusual for Italy since 2006 (PtA, DitV, MLwM, Spione and the Jeep Doubt) and there\u2019s another group called Janus Design doing the same (Don\u2019t Rest your Heas, Esoterrorist) and we often collaborate.
    We\u2019re struggling to make this games known, especially demoing at cons, shops and clubs and keeping an online presence with a mailing list and a forum where people can share experiences about this games and have advice about rules.

    A passionate community of people who loved the new concepts in this games is slowly building itself from scratch. It\u2019s people eager to try more games and discuss about them.
    Now it seems all our work is starting to pay off as other gamers are starting to note this games.

    We think this kind of games could also reach a wider/different public than the usual one and are also working towards this end by bringing our games in different venues (libraries, culture festivals, theaters).
    This is not easy as RPG games in Italy are mainly seen as a childish/geekish/not-cool activity, but we know our books are ubercool so we\u2019re not giving up! ^__^

    Sorry again for the fast reply!

    @ Paul:
    You're WAY too kind!

    Best!
