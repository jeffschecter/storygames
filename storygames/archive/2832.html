\n Hello folks
\n
\nMy group (six of us) are going away to a nice cottage in North Yorkshire for a weekend in May to get some nice, uninterrupted gaming going on. We're arriving on Friday night and leaving late Sunday. Our timetable is as follows:
\n
\nFriday Night: 'Duty and Honour' - a homebrew game based on the 'Sharpe' novels and TV series, modded from Pendragon. (Ran by me!)
\nSaturday AM: 'D&D meets WWZ' - something light to wake us up, jumping straight to the end of an epic campaign, 18th level 3e characters stand between a world of undead and civilisation. Saturday morning cartoon fun.
\nSaturday PM: 'Thrilling Tales' - Spirit of the Century, I believe the gallant heroes will be locking horns with the Immortal Purveyor of All Things Wicked, Zo Kath Ra, and his pygmy horde.
\nSaturday Night: Drunken Diplomacy - the classic board game with a few little drinkees thrown in too
\nSunday AM: Sightseeing trip out to see a 'tall ship' harboured in a nearby bay
\nSunday PM: Pendragon - the continuation of our ongoing 'The Great Pendragon Campaign' ... campaign. The Knights of Salisbury stand fast against the Saxon horde...but for how long?
\n
\nA little bit of everything thrown in there. We've got the travel and sleeping arrangements sorted. We've got the food, snacks and sundries sorted. We're even taking a load of fruit etc. so that we can keep the energy levels up too. Characters for the games are being pregen'd at the moment. It all seems to be running smooth.
\n
\nMy questions are
\n
\n1. Have we missed something?
\n2. Can anyone suggest something that we could add that would make the event even sweeter?
\n
\nThanks in advance
\nNeil

    \n Careful not to try to do too much - make sure you build in some non-gaming time, or be prepared to drop some games if you overrun or run out of time. We had a few weekends like this too (which we dubbed AndrewCon) and inevitably tried to cram too many games in, which led to disappointment and frustration!

    Excellent choice of settings tho! I hope its not too bleak, windy and lonely!

    \n http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiends_of_the_Eastern_Front
    \n
    \n"A diary found with an unearthed skeleton casts new light on a lost piece of history from World War II. It belongs to a young German soldier who was stationed on the Eastern Front who fought alongside a group of Romanian soldiers who always fight at night. Their true nature is soon revealed and when they change sides all Hell breaks loose."
    \n
    \nCracking little old school 2000 AD series about a guy reporting about his experiences as a German soldier fighting alongside Romanian troops. The twist at the end scared the living crap out of me when I was a kid. So when I saw the BOOK of this, I just had to grab it. It (apparently, according to the author - not read it yet, still elbow deep in the Sharpe Companion!) takes the premise of the story and spins it out across WW2 wholesale. There's a new book coming out soon that deals with Japanese vampires and American troops as well.
