\nThis is my friend Karla. I\u2019ve known her for years, since I lived in Alabama. She\u2019s one of the sweetest, kindest, most inspirational people I know: she\u2019s battled depression and become stronger for it, she\u2019s raising her daughter on her own (and doing a fantastic job), she works hard for a church-run daycare and loves what she does. She doesn\u2019t spend much money on herself, and she loves life more than most people I know.

A month or so ago, Karla was diagnosed with Retinitis Pigmentosa. If you\u2019re not familiar with this genetic disorder, it\u2019s a condition in which pigment builds up in your eye, clouding the receptors, and makes RP sufferers blind somewhere in their 40s or 50s. There is no cure, and the only things that might delay blindness into her 50s are high doses of vitamin A, and wearing big dark sunglasses whenever she goes outside.

Karla doesn\u2019t do much for herself, as I said: she\u2019s a single mom working at a church daycare and all of her money and energy go into her little girl. She's struggling to stay positive, and making a list of things she wants to do and see with her daughter before her vision worsens\u2014 many of which are nearly impossible in her current situation. On top of everything else, RP is genetic, and Karla is waiting to find out whether the same fate awaits her little girl. This breaks my heart, and I want to do something for them both.

The Sight For Sore Eyes Benefit Bundle is a group of games and supplements donated specifically for Karla. At the very least, it will help her buy a pair of quality sunglasses that can delay her blindness for up to ten years; hopefully we raise enough to cross one of the more modest activities off of her to-do list.
