    \n Cool. Also, I think, worth combining with Simon Carryer's ideas about how individuals and subgroups are imperfectly representative of their societies. I mean, GENERALLY speaking, members of X group might not mourn, but surely there are some rebels or weirdos who do, right?

    The Carryer-inspired list I use in the Planarch Codex (which can be applied to individuals, groups, traditions, artifacts, etc.) is:
    - X upholds a cultural principle, at great cost
    - X twists a principle in a new direction
    - X breaks, defies, or rebels against a principle
    - X is deeply affected or marked by a principle

    To call this population of strangers in the midst of which we live "society" is such a usurpation that even sociologists wonder if they should abandon a concept that was, for a century, their bread and butter. Such networks sometimes condense into a milieu, where nothing is shared but codes, and where nothing is played out except the incessant recomposition of identity.

    \n Challenge the second:

    When making aliens, portray more about their culture and thinking and social lives and affects than their morphology or shape or biology.

    Physiological differences are often played up because they are easy and obvious, but I think that was I was trying to get at with the two figures on the cover: Are they Drones? Animates? Labyrinthines? Hosts? Why are they identical? Why is one the left hiding? Are they women?

    Making aliens just a little bit radically alien is more intimate, more challenging I think.

      \n So, this (and the list of norms/fundamentals) gave me some ideas for a thing I've been writing recently, which needed more procedural-type rules, as opposed to lots of examples, but basically its for making alien cultures. I decided to highlight four techniques: addition, exaggeration, omission, and substitution. The 6i stuff is all using omission to make alien cultures.

      Here's what I wrote for that section. There's four different applications with 2-3 examples each:

      Omission
      Conceiving a functional society after removing an aspect that is fundamental to human existence is one of the more difficult ways of creating an alien society. There is a lack, one that we would find intolerable, and yet the members of this society do not see that lack and exist in spite of it.

      Categorical Omission
      One or more of the fundamental divisions among human beings is an alien concept to this society.
      \xb7\tCognitive distinctions are trinary\u2014as in past, present, and future, or red, blue, and yellow\u2014instead of binary.
      \xb7\tThe members of this society can transfer memories, both visual and muscle memories, and so age is an irrelevant category, save for how much time it means one has left to live. Elders are not respected for their wisdom, those who have more memories are.
      \xb7\tThough sexual reproduction is maintained, genders are not. Any member of this society can impregnate any other member.

      Conceptual Omission
      Remove a non-categorical idea that human society either requires or is stuck with.
      \xb7\tIn this society, people have no faces. They identify each other mostly by voice or through recognition of physical mannerisms, all of which they are acutely aware of.
      \xb7\tThe members of this society cannot feel positive emotions in isolation. Happiness and joy can only be created through group psychic feedback loops that require at least two people. Because of this, they have no concept of exploiting others for personal gain.
      \xb7\tThis culture has no sense of permanent aesthetics. Its people value acoustic effects but not music, desire spontaneous culinary experiences instead of familiar flavour profiles, and consider the visual sense good only for information, not entertainment.

      Emotional Omission
      One emotional aspect of humanity is missing. This could be love, hate, fear, or desire, whatever you can think of.
      \xb7\tThe members of this culture feel emotions less strongly, in general, than humans. They make all decisions based on calculated logic.
      \xb7\tThere are no celebrations of displays of joy in this culture. These people know the satisfaction of success and a job well done, but not euphoria or giddiness.
      \xb7\tThis culture has no war and no exploitation. Resources are shared as equitably as can be managed. But how do they defend themselves from outside threats?

      Lack of Predation
      This society truly is at the top of the food chain, not even prey to parasites, bacteria, or viruses. This society exists without threats, or without being a threat.
      \xb7\tAdvanced technology means everyone is provided with what they need to live. What do the people in this society do when they do not need to work to survive?
      \xb7\tThe members of this society are trapped inside indestructable bodies. They cannot harm each other, nor can they be harmed by any force they yet know of.

      \n I also tried writing some example cultures based on that list of human universals, which I'm not using anywhere, so I might as well post them here, too.

      Lack of Aesthetics
      This is an example of removing a concept just enough to make it alien, as opposed to removing it altogether. This culture seems to have no aesthetics. They cannot see or sight is a different experience for them, but they do not derive entertainment from visual input, they use it only to gather information or not at all. Nor do they have a sense of touch as we know it.

      They have an acute sense of hearing, though. They have sonar and can feel vibrations. They are not interested in music or repetitive performance, but value acoustic qualities and spontaneous aural and vibratory effects and the qualities they endow.

      Their material culture is attuned to pleasurable vibrations and has highly-advanced acoustics, but looks like garbage. They want to play with the fuzz pedal while the guitar feeds back, they don\u2019t want to strum chords.

      Another example would be a culture like one example above, where they have a sense of smell and taste and desire pleasant culinary experiences, but want them to be new and unfamiliar. In fact, there is no real "difference of taste" within this culture\u2014what pleases one member will please another, but familiarity will remove this pleasure. Thus, no aesthetics.

      And to expand on the Lack of Age Distinctions example:
      This culture can transfer memories, chemically. This is usually done by two individuals trading taking the time to trade memories with each other. The more leisure time one has, and the more persuasive one is, the more memories one collects. The accumulated wisdom of old age is not respected, but the accumulation of memories is. One might have more memories because one is older, but also the elites of this society might be chosen because they are good at storing, using, and distributing memories efficiently. Age is just a measure of how much time left you have to live.
      But what kinds of memories can they share? Here are some ideas:
      \xb7\tTransferring visual memory is an easy concept to grasp. Everybody nowadays has cameras and the internet\u2014just imagine if you didn\u2019t need that stuff and you could just give people the images you remember straight up, with no technological mediation.
      \xb7\tIf muscle memory can be transferred, then developed skills in arts and crafts become diffused throughout the society. Learning to ride a bike or shred on guitar is a community service, because you can share that with everybody else. It also becomes less valued\u2014nobody is admiring you because you are good at some physical activity.
      \xb7\tIf recognition memory can be shared, you will be able to recognize people and places that others have been to, even if you cannot picture them in your mind. This would end up being a fairly tight-knit society, because even members who had never met before would recognize each other through the memories of people they knew in common.

      \n This one's really creepy, you might not want to read it.

      Biological and social mother are never the same person:
      This one is easy to do: the biological mother is consumed by the offspring at birth. It must have a social mother that is not its biological mother. How does this work?

      There are two (or three) sexes/genders. The main two are nurturer and inseminator. A nurturer produces fruit/milk that nourishes others and primarily the young. These act as social mothers for offspring. The second sex is the inseminators. Instead of producing fruit/milk, most of that energy goes back into improved physique, plus they produce sperm or something like it. They can inseminate any other member of the species, willingly or unwillingly. Either they inseminate captured members of other communities (of this species) or willing members of their own community. The DNA in the sperm combines with the DNA in the inseminated host, so it's not like a parasite wasps laying eggs in some other species.

      It is like spiders, though. Once inseminated, the offspring grows inside the inseminated, feeds off it and devours it as it is born. There are probably multiple offspring born at a time, which are then raised by nurturers, which pick out specific ones to raise and imprint with their own genetically-influenced behaviours. Offspring are usually born either inseminator or nurturer, but can also be born a third sex, the intersex, that does not produce sperm or milk/fruit, but instead channels all that energy into making itself bigger and whatnot. So it is even more well-equipped to be inseminated, because the offspring will have more to eat.

      Why would some members of the community be willingly inseminated, if it means their death? Being inseminated guarantees your genes being passed on in a future generation. Helping a sibling inseminator is a way for a nurturer to guarantee their (her I guess?) genes get passed on to a new generation, or once you are old and have tired and have passed on accumulated knowledge, it can be a self-sacrificial way to secure reproduction for your genes. It could be a ploy for nurturers in a society with no siblings who have not been selected for insemination to injure themselves, diminishing their utility for anything but insemination, but its is also possible that they might then just be fed to new offspring. Unless they don\u2019t cannibalize after birth? Let\u2019s go with that one, this species is creepy enough already.

      A society could be "patriarchal" where the inseminators run everything, handle politics and war and generally use force to get what they want. They might use insemination as a punishment, although that would end up being detrimental in the long run, since the genes of those being punished would then be passed on to a new generation.

      A society could be a "matriarchal" community of nurturers who keep inseminators like dogs or bulls, and breed them with other inseminators or captured/aged nurturers, but always maintaining control of social organization.

      Perhaps this species has some kind of chemical cycle, when a being start to go into ______: a state of desiring to be inseminated.

      It would be an intense thing (kind of like menopause or puberty), something everyone has mixed and wildly varying feelings about. There are probably all kinds of off-colour jokes and proverbs about this ______ that happens.

      Some people welcome it with open arms (like people who answer powerfully to the ring of the biological clock), only to regret it after the chemical change has passed. Others become reclusive, resisting this urge, or maybe even feeling angry at their bodies.

      Everyone would have strong feelings about this, one way or another: perhaps certain groups look down on those who have a desire to be inseminated (while secretly harbouring the same desires, perhaps).

      No one knows (yet) what exactly triggers ________, although there are many theories and old wives' tales. If the insemination doesn't occur (or the pregnancy fails), the ______ goes away after a while, with the being returning to their normal self later.
