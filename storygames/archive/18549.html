    \n Monsters have stats? Who says?

    All monsters need are Hit Points and damage values. And even those are optional.


    When I've run it, it was always very loose with monsters. A monster never rolls to attack, instead the giant undead hermit crab swings its claw at a PC who rolls to avoid being hit. A monster only gets a HP total if the PCs decide to go with direct damage dealing ways, in which case they get a big pile of HPs if they're fighting alone. Tiny swarms die in a hit or so, possibly multiple on a good roll. Narratively dealing with a monster by, e.g. trapping it in a locked chamber or finding a hidden weakness have thus far been more effective than straight damage.

    I've only played with 1st level PCs, and even a d6 for damage makes the monsters plenty scary. No reason thus far to make monsters dish out more damage (or less for that matter). You could always have monsters that don't deal damage at all, but instead dish out corrupting diseases and curses and the like, where the PC would need to then roll Con or Wis or something to resist the evil magic effects.


    In the long run, this may disadvantage fighter type characters, but it's never been a big deal for the short runs of games I've been playing (a session or two at most).

    \n Yep, I do the same.

    Anything that's human-scale, 4hp. Gives a 50% chance to one-shot them with a normal weapon. They do 1d6+1 damage. That covers 90% of my monsters.

    Elites, warriors, magical stuff: 2d6 damage. Maybe 2 armor. Tough monsters should be very rare. Increase damage instead, to make them more risky to face, not a slog to finish off.

    Demons, major magic: 3d6 damage. I might tell you to roll at -2 if you oppose it where it's strongest.

    Pretty much anything from Basic D&D will work. And DW monsters, of course.

    \n It depends on the group. I have a bunch of third graders and seventh graders for whom the story expectations tilt toward the heroic, not super-deadly dungeon delving. I do tend to deal damage on 6-, but I also keep an eye on character HP so I don't inadvertently slaughter them. I may not kill them without player permission, but I'll tear them up, scar or maim them, damage their stuff, ruin their reputations, etc. There are certain situations, like the Dungeon Kids troll battle, where death is on the line. I honestly don't know how I'd have handled character death in that situation, given that the fiction was mighty deadly with little opportunity for fudging.

    When Valavanora made the Last Breath move and snake-eyed it, I could have killed her, but instead we came up with the Death-marked Druid idea, and there will be serious fictional consequences from that result that will become obvious once the party reaches the Druid Lands.

    I prefer to escalate the stakes or the situation sharply on a 6-. Sometimes that's dealing damage, sometimes it's about just making things dramatically worse. Roryb's take on making battles feel more cinematic and exciting is my main compass point. I'm not opposed to killing characters, but for this group, I've made it explicit that Death won't happen unless the player wills it so.

Letting the characters know that the shells were more valuable undamaged. So they had to come up with ways of avoiding straight up bashing and gave them more misuses to their to-hits. (They pulled off legs and wrapped them in cloaks etc. shell values I realized afterwards were too high, but ah well. 1d6 x 10 (x20 if undamaged) silver for babies & 5d6x10 silver (x20) for the mama spider. Wayyyy to high. It will be interesting to see what they do with all that coin though, and they felt well rewarded. The players were super happy.
