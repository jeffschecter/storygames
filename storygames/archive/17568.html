    \n Yes, but the GM can generally make a move on their turn and they can, if they deem it fitting, make a hard move without a soft move preceding it. There's not really a "no hard moves without a soft move" rule. It's not done often because you're a fan of the characters and you're more interested in playing to find out and making their lives interesting than in hitting them out of the blue (fuck with, not fuck over right?). So, here's Harum Scarum saying, if you miss this move the GM gets a free hand to smack you with his hardest stuff without foreshadowing some time in the future. That's a pretty interesting fictional choice. I'm trying to figure out if it's an intentional choice and what in Elizabethan fiction it is emulating.

    And if not, why does the GM need to hold +1?

    \n Hmm, I don't think I ever play hard moves without soft moves I definitely don't in combat (which is where I see harum scarum being played). Some examples I would use:

    As you advance towards the guard you hear the fizzing of a matchcord behind the curtain, what do you do? (no hold)
    Advancing towards the guard as you pass the curtain, there's a loud bang and it feels like you've just been kicked by a mule, roll 2d8.b

    Being attacked by 3 watchmen:
    Two of the watchmen lunge whilst the other circles behind you, what do you do. (no hold)
    The two watchmen in front of you prepare to lunge keeping your attention as the other circles to you rear and trips you to the floor / knock you out / stabs you in the back.

    With the "6 or less from an NPC" comment, what I mean is when a Player is in combat with an NPC and they roll a six, you get to do stuff to the Player. You can get to do that if you have 1 hold.

    If you don't like these, more than happy to listen to suggestions

    \n Because the Mc in apocalypse world doesn't need a 6 or less to make a Move. And the MCs moves follow directly from the fiction. They don't have to worry about getting hold on me. Like if the MC says you're in an exposed place and a dude with a high powered rifle and a decent scope would totally have you dead to rights, are you really not going to look around to see what you should be on the lookout for? Do you not care what your enemy's true position is? Cuz if you don't do something, I think the mc has established potential harm and they can just deal it. So, you're going to look around or take cover or something and that will trigger a move, with a possible 6 or less result.

    If the GM has no hold play as normal suggests hold is irrelevant. Either having hold matters or it doesn't.
