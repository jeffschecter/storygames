    \n I thought of acts of evil too.

    a bunch of german-style board games suggest ways to have players make choices about different ways to gather resources, empower themselves and disrupt opponents.

    if you want to avoid resource heavy game play, I'd also suggest looking sons of liberty which has two sides rather than every player being on their own- but it is a fast paced, card game-based story game of political conflict.
    http://joshroby.com/SoL

    for reading inspiration, seutonius' the twelve caesars is good on the scheming, rise and fall of roman emperors. http://penelope.uchicago.edu/Thayer/E/Roman/Texts/Suetonius/12Caesars/home.html

    good luck!

    \n Hmm, no idea if this would work... but what about hacking Burning Empires?
    It is very competive and has many mechanics for the personal aims and relationships vs greater political goals-themes which make Song of Ice and Fire great, too.
    The main thing to do (and I don't know if it can be done) would be to change it from a game with two factions (player/humans vs GM/vaylen) to a multi-faction, GMless game where each player forms his own faction (but is able to make temporary alliances with others).
