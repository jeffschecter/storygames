I'm currently working on my game for the ReversedEngineer-challenge. I find it very interesting to work on the basis of the sheet given by Meguey Baker.

I've recently finished writing a novel too, an that novel totally renewed the setting of my FRPG Fabula (published -99), based on the elements given in the drawings by an English woman I found on the net. She had intelligent birds in her drawings, and so I had to write them into the setting, although no bird had given evidence of intelligence before this.

This spring I also wrote one of my greatest RPG-designs ever, based on the method invented by another games designer. I've planned a series of nine RPGs based on that method.

It seems like outside input is a great way of setting off a project, at least to me. To have my own ideas meet up with the ideas of others, opens up exciting new portals in the creative process.

Anyone else have experiences with this?

    I haven't done full-on collaborative work myself, but my father wrote a very successful series of sporting non-fiction books with a collaborator and they managed to work together for something like 20-25 years without an argument. He claimed that the secret was that they agreed to a 50/50 split of the rewards upfront and stuck to it without further discussion.

    In other words, they had their social contract worked out. Since they were both men who stuck to their commitments, there was no problem.

    Easy to say, probably a lot harder to do.
