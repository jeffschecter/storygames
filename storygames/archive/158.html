Have you ever played in a game without Cool Special Powers?

Andy
February 2006 edited February 2006 in Story Games
I was just thinking back upon my life of some 18 years' worth of gaming or so. I can't, for the life of me, recall a game where I had played in a normal setting, without:

Cool powers (supers, psychic, magic, unreal kung fu, etc)
No cool powers, but definitely weird/unreal elements like Vampires, zombies, etc.

The closest I came was to suggesting a game of PTA where we were all people in the Lowrider community with various personal issues, but that's as close as I got.

Has anyone played a game of "regulars" in a "regular world"? I'll even allow in dramatic events (giant earthquakes, end of the world, historic events like ancient Rome, Greece, or 1800s... but without magic, lasers, etc) as "kosher", as long as no Cool Powerz are involved.

Just curious what people would use to play such a game?

Edit: I just remembered: Dust Devils. I've run about 3 games of Dust Devils, each one without cool powerz and the like. That's as close as I've ever gotten to "real world gaming".

    Y'know, I have another theory about the import of special powers.

    If I see a real-world story (like, say, an Pump Up the Volume]) in which the bad guy is discovered in the end, the good guy is celebrated, and an important moral victory is established ... I find it hard to believe. I distance myself from it, in that moment. I say "Ah ... they were lying to me. This movie isn't actually about the real world."

    And I do not consider myself a cynic. I'm a firm believer in the ascendancy of hope, of justice, of good over evil. I think that's the nature of the real world.

    But my experience is that, in the real world, hopes and dreams get stepped on. They get crushed, and battered, and bent. Their strength isn't in a rousing finale and a happy ending, it's in standing up and going on. Hope has a quiet, sad, uncathartic kind of strength. It makes for quiet, sad, uncathartic kinds of stories.

    So here's my theory: The wacko powers, the secret agents who never get shot (no matter how many bullets fly their way), the fantasy kingdoms and all that are a flag. They are "Once upon a time, a long way from here." They let you say "This is a story of a world that produces rousing stories with a big finish ... not the world you know."
