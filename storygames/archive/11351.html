\n So, I'm really hoping that this doesn't devolve into a flamewar, but I think there's good ground for discussion here.

Roleplaying games are notorious for publishing hyper-sexualized images of women in chainmail bikinis and compromising poses. The threads about Maid have recently brought up hyper-sexualized images (among other issues). Arguably, men often get the same treatment in art... hulking Conan-esque warriors who are always in scantily-clad muscle shots, like the cover of Men's Fitness or something.

And if that's your perfect ideal of sexuality, awesome. The world is so your oyster.
But if it's not... and you find those images crass and empty and trite... or worse things like oppressive and victimizing...

then I beg you: show me an image from a roleplaying game that you find genuinely sexy (or sensual, or steamy, or hot, or deeply romantic).

Don't post it in this thread, instead link it... and quickly describe what's in it. The reason I say that is to avoid posting images others may find offensive. I don't want to bar people from participating. So if you want to post a picture with a naked woman, just post the link and say "female nudity involved" or something. Cool!

By "image from a roleplaying game", I mean a pretty diverse set of things. Cover art, fan art, character art.... it's all good. Any art that was created to fuel collective storytelling counts.

AND! Explain a bit about what you find so sexy, or why it's sexy to you, or your reactions to it, or others reactions to it, or anything juicy like that.
Yes!

    \n I'll start.

    I'm a really big fan of some of the early art that was used for Pathfinder adventures. They consistently featured powerful, badass chicks who looked so self-assured and focused and alive with personality. They're consistently hot without that being "the point", or the reason they are there. They are there to kick your ass, and they are gorgeous.

    My favourite being this paladin: http://paizo.com/image/content/RiseOfTheRunelords/Pathfinder7_Paladin.jpg
    Yeah. Self-assured, a visionary, a leader, powerful, hot. I've never seen someone look hotter in full platemail.
    She wears it so well.

    This lady here: http://paizo.com/image/product/catalog/PZO/PZO9019_500.jpeg
    She's got her weapon drawn, and yet looks bemused, like she's taunting someone.
    She's hot, but also looks like she could slice me to ribbons in a second, amidst twirling fabrics and flashing steel.
    She looks like she would be able to slice me to pieces without losing her composure.
    The low-cut shirt is pretty cleavage heavy, but in my opinion its tasteful. Her clothing is appropriate to how she kicks ass. She's owning that outfit.

    And this scantily-clad barbarian warrior chick: http://paizo.com/image/content/CrimsonThrone/PZO9010-Barbarian.jpg
    So, a lot more exposed skin in this one, but I'm buying it, because she's a barbarian.
    What's awesome is that her stomach is littered with scars, as is her face. Every patch of skin that she's showing is a testament to the fact that she's here to fight.
    She's ready to kill.
    She's a warrior. That's the first thing you know about her.
    And she's snarling, and sexy, and has the most amazing hair ever, and I want her to maul me.

    There are lots of "hot chicks" in the Pathfinder line, and some are way closer to the cheesecake standard... but in my opinion, these characters look like confident, powerful, gorgeous women. I am not fantasizing about them as objects, but as people. Their stories immediately bubble up in my imagination.

    They are all confident, and self-aware, and capable, and hot. Both physically and in their implied personalities. They're the women I'd want as lovers, or as friends, or as mothers, and definitely as people who're watching my back in a fight.

    You can still pull all the typical "fantasy art" critiques out, and attack the fact that they're just posing and not doing, or that they're decked out in a thousand layers of armour and weapon, or that their swords are gigantic and... whatever. The point is that they are fantasy characters who manage to be women and warriors, and be genuine to both, and be amazing.

    \n Sexy art in games... sure a difficult proposition. I don't think I read roleplaying games in that way at all, so I never get to the stage where I'd decide whether some art is sexy or not. In fact, when I notice sexuality in rpg art, it's almost always because it's being blatantly plastered over the thing. This is almost without exception an inappropriate context considering the text and the purpose of the text, so I find it annoying. Thus the only "sexy" art I can even remember at this point involves all those offensive soft porn book covers that a certain sort of rpg publisher uses to catch attention in a bookstore.

    I imagine that this'd be different if I had a more ground-up attitude at the whole notion of reading roleplaying games. Less focus on the over-all picture and more on the fluff and the fiction and on what the book has to give to me as an object of imagination, instead of an object of rules and procedures. I imagine that this has to do with general grounding in fiction, too - I rarely catch any new ideas on fiction from rpg books, which might be why I don't pay much attention to the specifics of illustration and other passive aesthetics in them.

    Thinking about it, I guess my only picks for specifically sexy rpg art would all simply involve games I read and fantasized over before growing unbearably jaded - thus games like AD&D 2nd edition and Cyberpunk 2020 come to mind as titles that had some fetching erotic imagery in among the other stuff back at the time. In retrospect I doubt that they're special in any way compared to other games, it's just that I haven't evaluated rpg art from the personally sexy point of view through the last decade. Simple nostalgia, that.

    Then again - what is sexy, exactly? If we'll accept a less immediately carnal definition, I suppose I could claim that Misery Bubblegum has a pretty sexy illustrative style. I'd use the term sympathetic myself, but the important thing is that the art attracts the eye and inspires via the use of attractive pictures of people, isn't it? From this more relaxed viewpoint I'd also mention Breaking the Ice, Dead of Night, S/lay w/ Me, Trollbabe and Sweet Agatha as games that have included sexy art in a way that has drawn my attention.
