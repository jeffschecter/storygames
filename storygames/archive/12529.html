\nEpisode 29 UnSpeakable Inspecting
\n
\nThis week we discuss UnSeakable, a Lovecraftian rules variant for the InSpectres roleplaying came. We talk about how effective this rules variant is for creating Lovecraft inspired stories, the differences between UnSpeakable and core InSpectres, and the GM's role when a significant amount of narrative authority is invested in the players.

    \n Regarding the tendency of your plays of Unspeakable to (in my view) "go gonzo": I don't see anything in the text that would either encourage or discourage this. I think has to be an up front agreement amongst the players, who then need to work together to ensure the agreement is kept. When I read Ben Robbins' InSpace AP posts (InSpace being another InSpectres supplement designed for exploring the mysteries of the cosmos), they definitely seem to avoid gonzo, again without anything in InSpace's text that reins in the players in that regard. So it can be done.
