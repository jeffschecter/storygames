\n I think about games like alcoholic drinks. You know, people will describe a wine as "full-bodied but a weak finish" or "a delicate aroma" or things like that? Yeah, games are like that, too.

And then there's beer. Beer is seasonal. Certain beers are summer beers, while others are winter beers, and so on. Summer beers tend to be lighter (like an IPA), while winter beers are heavier (like an oatmeal stout).

Or something like that.

So, let's assign seasons to some storygames. It'll be fun! (Or maybe I'm just a nerd.)

What games correspond well with a particular season?

    \nBliss Stage is all autumn for me; it's the end of our world it depicts, after all.

    Looking at larger trends, it seems to me that games with imminent, destructive passion - blood operas - are autumn games. Like Dust Devils. Games that hinge on the dramatic tension provided by loose and as-yet unfitted elements, such as The Mountain Witch, are wintery, abiding their time. Games that are all about exploration and seeing new sights, such as Trollbabe, are spring. Summer games have the widest range of focus of all, with a solid, semi-stable situation in evidence, such as Capes. Or to say it another way: spring games focus on beginnings, autumn games on endings, winter games on secrets and summer games on existence.
