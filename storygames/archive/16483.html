\nI'm currently in a place where it's virtually impossible to get a gaming group together, so my options for games is limited to PbF or with my SO. We only have a couple of boardgames with us, an internet connection, and a desire to play some story-telling games. From what I've read about Fiasco, it would be a great fit for us, but I've never seen anyone discuss a 2-player variant, nor does it seem like it would work particularly well. Microscope also looks interesting, but a bit heavy on the scene rules and details. We are also more inclined towards games where we influence persistent characters.
\n
\nTo the point: Archipelago II looks like it would work fine with two-players, but is there enough creative back-and-forth with such a small group? Some people have said that they have tried it with two people, but I haven't been able to find a writeup of such a session. Does anyone want to chime in? Are there any suggested changes to the game for playing with two people? I think increasing the number of destiny points we write for each other would be a good idea - anything else?

    \n I've played 2-player with my son, who's ten. It works fine as written (but then, I would say that, having written the game). It's a bit intense - you have to pay attention the whole time. And you might want to allow more than one Fate card per player for the session, just in case someone needs a bit of extra input. I suggest having small breaks between turns to give you some breathing space. Also, wine is often great (although I didn't use that particular technique when playing with my son).

    (One of my absolutely favorite two-player sessions was with "Society of Dreamers", playing with my wife. Intense, atmospheric and just really nice. "Catherine", however, just ended up horribly sad.)

    \n Two-player Archipelago sounds totally do-able. One kink might be the limited ownership of elements, so double up on those so there are four.

    If you wanted to try two-player Fiasco, set it up as a four-player game and own two initially-unconnected characters each. I've never tried it but it'd probably work all right.
