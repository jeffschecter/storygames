    \n Hi, Evlyn! Welcome to SG!

    I think the idea is implicit in the game-as-written that yes, you can (and perhaps should) give secret missions to the players beforehand, either at character creation (representing personal objectives or secret plots) or right as the mission begins (either before or after the players rough out the mission details).

    That said, if you're not finding you need the Trust Dice, then there's no reason to use them. I've only run WoM once, and although I did give my PCs secret missions (about halfway through, just as alliances were forming, I specifically created secret missions to drive wedges in the group), the players found they weren't calling on them very often.

    Trust Dice are there to create an atmosphere of distrust. Wick specifically references Alias. James Bond, too, never knows who's going to stab him in the back. If you want the PCs to be able to trust each other implicitly (like the excellent comic series Queen & Country by Greg Rucka) then don't worry about Trust.

    On the other hand, if your players are aware of the Trust mechanic, and you don't use it for several sessions with the same spies, and then suddenly one day you hand three dice to one of their number, alarm bells will go off, big time.

    I think WoM could be easily hacked to run without a game master. All the GM really provides is the initial mission and then someone to introduce complications. My experience was that the highly-experienced team tended to break up to accomplish the things in their area of specialty anyway, so there's no reason that the player of an "idle" character couldn't just take over the GM hat while waiting for their turn. All you need to do is make sure that everyone feels they are getting sufficient face time.

    \n Oh, two more things:

    1) It might be interesting after the first session to allow the players to pick their own secret missions for future games and contact you about them privately. If you chum the metaphorical waters, your players will act like hungry sharks.

    2) If you are finding that your players have enough missions dice that they don't need Trust Dice, but you want to use Trust, just increase the time-based penalties. Maybe a -5 to outcomes every 20 minutes is leaving your group with too much power. Make it -7, and see what happens. They'll be begging for trust dice soon enough.
