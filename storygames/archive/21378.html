The world is falling to pieces all around us! But our Community is safe inside these walls, thanks to the foresight of our wise Leader. We\u2019ll build up our stores and bolster our defenses for as long as we can, in the hope that we can keep the dreaded Outsiders permanently at bay\u2026

The Enclave is a collaborative Storygame. Over the course of an hour or two, you and your friends will construct a narrative about a Community of heroic (or delusional) isolationists, who construct for themselves an entrenched bastion against (what they perceive to be) the hostile forces of the outer world. As they bolster up the defenses of their outpost, they will encounter unexpected misfortunes and betrayals, and fight bravely (or foolishly) against threats from the Outsiders whose world they have abandoned, until a final day of reckoning arrives.

(I solicited feedback on an early draft of this here a couple of years ago; curious to know what folks might think of the finished version.)

    \n Sorry about the previews! Trying to get a fix for that from the folks at DriveThru; will update you when it happens.

    The mechanics of the game are a bit of a mishmash of elements from map-building games like The Quiet Year and more traditional RPGs. There's a brief character generation phase at the outset for the story's main protagonists, but players can switch perspectives at will - they don't need to stick with a single character for the whole game.

    Once that's done, each turn consists of rolling on a table of minimally described plot elements, locating that event on a slowly evolving map of the Enclave, and then briefly describing what happens there. After 25 turns of this (which usually clocks in at around 2-3 hours of gameplay, depending on how chatty the players are) one player gets to develop a slightly longer "denouement."

    \n I've been testing it on and off for over two years - most of it was good to go on first conception, but the mechanism whereby narrative prompts got delivered once per turn took an enormous amount of tweaking to get (I think) just right.

    The only respect in which it really resembles trad RPGs is in its use of "character sheets," and in the fact that gameplay involves a separate (very truncated) character generation phase.

    It's both collaborative and GM-less, though I've worked quite hard to make it the sort of thing that a more experienced player can walk other players through fairly authoritatively without railroading the actual story.

    \n It depends on the group, of course, but there was never any awkwardness among the people I playtested it with. I suppose such a thing could occur at a convention or some other venue where players weren't previously acquainted. But in all of my tests, players were quite careful to preserve a few fortune tokens so they could vote on changing the outcome, and the denouement was always a lively, fairly fun process of negotiation.
