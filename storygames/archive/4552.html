\n I'm curious as to whether a game exists that includes mechanics for intentional failure as part of the story narrative.

I've found that there are two ways of treating failure in the games:

1. It is a random chance event that is an unlikely outcome of attempting an action, which generally does not have long-term consequences. Typically, encounters are scaled in such a way that success is difficult, but doable. If you fail, it's a temporary derailment from the march of progress. The gm must provide the illusion of danger without killing off all the pcs; after all, what's the fun of that? I'd say that this is generally the case for traditional rpgs like D20, Storyteller, Gurps, etc.

2. It is a real possibility that is planned for in the mechanics, with specific mechanical side effects. This is much more rare; for example, in Polaris, if you fail to succeed at a die roll to resolve a conflict, you gain certain mechanical side effects which progress the character or refresh certain resources. Again, this a randomly selected outcome, chiefly influenced by the roll of the dice.

Does anyone know of a system where people can choose whether to succeed or fail in advance, with each of these options being a viable alternative? I'd love to find a game where I could go into a session and say, "This session, I am going to fail miserably. I will lose things and sacrifice." By choosing to lose, I would gain the resources I need to enable later victory, but at a terrific cost.

I'm looking for a specific "feel" to this system and how the narration would work that I can't quite describe, so I'd like to hear as many options as possible. Anyone heard of something like this?

    \n I don't know that the standard d20/WoD/GURPS rules assume that a total-party-kill isn't fun. I think that's a bolt-on for specific groups (mine included)...

    To answer your question, any resource allocation game (or subgame, to a lesser degree) can do this - you just don't allocate any more than the minimum resources to the thing you want to fail at and that will give you more for what you want to succeed at later. That is, actually, what resource allocation means.

    Posted By: Ru Baraba DomDoes anyone know of a system where people can choose whether to succeed or fail in advance, with each of these options being a viable alternative? I'd love to find a game where I could go into a session and say, "This session, I am going to fail miserably. I will lose things and sacrifice."     \nThis is With Great Power: That plot advancer which says "You need to fail at least 3-5 conflicts in order to eventually succeed". Even knowing that going in to a conflict, it produces some pretty awesome results. Like, when the players hit the fourth of five milestones, they knew that they had to throw two more challenges before they could eventually take down the enemy.

    And even though the players were thinking, "ok, cool, we just need to throw the next two conflicts. Fail miserably. Lose things and sacrifice myself", there was still some open ground to NOT doing that: Specifically, we had two conflicts where the players just wanted to win them, even though losing would have advanced the plot. If I'm recalling correctly, one of the players had a Mary Jane style love interest, and it became clear that she was on the line (lose, and she gets caputred... with the implication that eventually you will track her down or whatever), and the player said, "No, fuck you, I'm going for the WIN!" and did.

    But, when squinting at the system, it sets you up for systematic, juicy, glorious failure. Hard, rough failure in order to have that much of a more spectacular final success.
