So, I was gonna bitch about this thing's sister thread on RPGnet, but, why?

I'm hacking Agon to make a Norse thing. I'm looking for mechanical thinking; do these weapons and mechanics make sense? Do they seem to be too much better than the comparable Greek mechanics? Etc.

I examined the weapons and made some adjustments to them; it's my hope that the following should be comparable to them. Are they?

New Weaponry for Ragnarok

Some weapons provide two dice. For all those weapons, put both dice in the hand that is using that weapon. One of these dice may be moved to the other hand. You may decide to combine or split these dice at any time before you roll.

Brawling—2d8—Range 0
You can brawl with one hand or two. If you are using brawling, the only weapon you can use in your left hand is brawling.

Broad Axe—1d8 1d4—Range 1
You hold a broad axe in one hand.

Great Sword—2d6—Range 1-2
You hold a great sword in your right hand.

Throwing Axe—Identical to the Greek javelin.

Longbow—Identical to the Greek bow.

In Ragnarok there is no shield. (It occurred to me that it is strictly inferior to defence via brawling.)

    I guess I included the quote to demonstrate why (I think) posters on RPG.net were getting bent out of shape: Spear and shield combat seems pretty quintessential to a lot of viking sagas and tales.     Your weapons ideas aren't bad, and I get the idea to make them different. Something I'd be interested in seeing might be ideas about attack maneuvers which change positioning -- both the idea of a "rush/push" which does damage but also pushes opponents back, and some kind of "grab/hook" which draws them closer. I could imagine linking shields --> push and spears --> grab, but you could do it with other weapons, eg. the axe could have a "hooking" component.

    Despite having run a bunch of trial Agon combats, I don't think I have the Actual Play chops to comment on specific numbers. From what I do know, the rest of your numbers look okay in that you have tried (I gather) to offset numbers vs the difficulty of closing, right?
