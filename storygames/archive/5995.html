\n So it appears that I'll be going to GameStorm at the end of the month. The question is, what games should I run? I don't know. Then I thought: Oh, I'll ask Story Games! So I am.

I want to run, maybe, three things? Here's my thoughts:

1) Games I have written.
Polaris: Knights trying and failing to save the greatest people of the world from their own Mistake and the complete annihilation it brings.
Bliss Stage: Post-apocolyptic teenagers having sex to power their giant robots.
The Drifter's Escape: It's basically "Blues Murder Ballad" the RPG.

2) Games other people have written.
1001 Nights: Arabian Nights style storytelling. It's fun.
Shock: It has a colon.
Shooting the Moon: God-damnit more people should play this game. Why don't more people play this game? Clearly because I don't run it at cons.
carry: never run this before, but it's a good idea to push myself.

3) Playtesting
\u5fcd: a game about an elite group of special forces agents trying, through a series of calculated strikes and terrorist actions, to undermine the military dictatorship that they helped create.
Adventures in the Land of a Thousand Kings: The game where You! (you the player) go to a magical fantasy world and have a magical fantasy adventure.

People who are going to be at GameStorm: Any thoughts?

yrs--
--Ben
