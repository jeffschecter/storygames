\n So, last year when I picked up Primetime Adventures, I was struck with the lack of knowledge of where to buy playing cards. I eventually ended up at Target and got a black & silver deck that they had (they didn't have any normal colored cards!). I moved recently and either lost the deck or its still buried somewhere. Came back to Target and got a two pack of bicycle cards with red and blue backs. In my mind, I'm also building up to 5 decks (or is it 6?) to eventually be able to run With Great Power....

So, I need variety, and preferably some cool themes (Kat Miller had a skull deck at GenCon that was the villain's core deck which was awesome). I've seen some licensed decks, like Superman Returns and similar. And while I might get one of those, I want more variety, especially something distinct without a license deal attached and movie screen shots in the middle of the cards. The bottom line is variety in the backs and a full 52 deck, but anything in addition to that criteria could be icing.

In another thread, someone linked Pagat which has a cool list of companies, but it ranges from small and sketchy looking websites, or sites with limited usefulness like Tarot focused stuff, and sites with collector cards that would be awesome if I had a $1,000 to throw away on some Victorian age Indian produced playing card deck.

So, where do you get your cards? Some local shop is great, and feel free to shine the love (especially if its in the bay area) on your favorite place for poker style playing cards, or even chain stores too, but I'm mostly thinking of reputable online shops that you've used.

    \n Guys, you're doing it all wrong. Let me show you how.

    First, find a carnival or arcade or Chuckie Cheese's with skeeball. If you're in Vancouver, totally go to the Kids Only Market.
    Then, spend roughly $5-10 dollars on skeeball. Hours of excitement and frustration ensue.
    Then, cash in your mound of prize tickets for miniature, plastic-encased cards at the prize counter.

    That way, your cards won't just be cards, they'll be precious memories of the experiences you went through to get them.
