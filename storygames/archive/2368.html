    You're thinking of PAGE - Phiadelphia Area Gaming Enthusiasts. Eruditus mentions them in the meet-up thread he started but I'll repeat it here. There are two groups that meet weekly, one in Center City at the Ethical Society Building on Sunday nights, the other group meets near Glenside PA (about 20 minute drive outside the city) they meet on Tuesdays. Since both groups pay rent there are membership dues, 5 dollars a visit or $15 per month. The first two meetings are free.

    They're all good people and Eru and I are both members - but we haven't been attending meetings in a while. Neither group has much in the way of indie-gaming, but that's not because they aren't open to it or don't have the space. If anyone wants to start a semi regular thing at the Sunday meeting Eru and I could probably play.

    There's another gaming group that meets on Penn's campus but I don't know much about them.
  • eruditus
    February 2007
    As a gaming community Philadelphia has a lot of quantity. I used to run 125 people Living Greyhawk events back in the day. ShoreCon WAS a big event. It's downward spiral happened around 9/11 (a week before the con) where it just lost steam and a lot of folk got burnt out over it. There are a dozen gaming related stores in a 1 hour radius, some better than others. PAGE is 60 members strong and has been around for over a decade. Penn gamers and EPGS are boardgaming communities that meet semi regularly.

    My boutique-con (PoliCon) happens annually in April and brings in about 50+ people. It's almost exclusively story games. There were two canned D&D games and my buddy Scott leshers runs a mean In Nomine game. Russ ran Dogs. Someone else ran Unistat. We had some minis games as well. About 18 games all in all.

    There is a scifi convention called PhilCon that spring and I are very involved in gaming at. That's in November.
