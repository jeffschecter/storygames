\n In this thread about alignment, it was (tangentially) mentioned that D&D's alignment system is an artifact of American thinking. That thread wasn't really the place to talk about if that idea was right or wrong. Nor, as it happens, is this thread.

Instead, the point of this thread is to imagine that:

1) D&D was created in some other country
2) That its designers just had to build a "two-axis" alignment system
3) The alignment system chosen inevitably reflected something about their national identity or politics or something (at the time the game was made, which, we assume to still be the 1970s).

...and then tell us what the alignment system they used was.

Added degree of difficulty: You may only make posts about a country that you have lived in for more than five years.

Additional degree of difficulty: If you have ever lived in America for more than six months, you cannot post at all. You are contaminated.
