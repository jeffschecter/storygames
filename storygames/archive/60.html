    2) If you haven't heard any feedback for two days, follow up on your own thread not with a "Hey, Anybody out there???" (which some do, to the chagrin of others), but rather look at your post again, see if you can pull out a more specific question, and ask that specific question in a post to your own thread:
    "Hey all, I was thinking a little more, and what i really need help with is this part right here: XYZ. Any ideas?"

    3) If someone posts to your thread, always consider their feedback and give a reply. if their reply is nothing more than a "Hey, cool!", then thank them and go to step 2, above, asking a more specific question.

    I went to check out your thread on the Forge. But I can't post a reply to it. Mostly because I can't figure out what the game is about. I mean, it looks like something is going on there, but I can't figure out what. See now, I can't force myself to dig deep into someone else's game and try to figure out what's going on and how to help them. So, if you can't catch my interest with your post and fire my imagination, then I can't really help you, right?

    Hell, before your post here I never even would have looked at your Forge thread. "Introductory scenarios in rpg books" sounds like a topic about the pros and cons of having into scenarios in an RPG book, as opposed to a request for help in your design. Topics like that make my eyes glaze over and my heart rate drop 'till I'm in a coma.

    It's been my experience while at the Forge that, to catch an eye and grab some inspiration, you've got to sell your idea. And you've got a very small amount of space to do that in. You've got to pull people in with your title, tell them all about your game and what will make it cool in four sentances or less, and then keep everyone focused with clear and concise paragraphs about your game. Then you've got to finish it up with some direct questions. Hard questions. Controversial questions. Shit that's going to make people think and take sides.

    So, what I'm saying is that, yeah... I've been in that boat too. I've posted plenty of shit at the Forge that I got zilch of a response too. But I came to realize that it was because I was boring people.

    My point is; I want to help you. They want to help you. There's a huge community out here that wants to help you. But dude, you've really got to earn it. You've got to entertain and inspire us to get into your game. You've got to make your PDF available as soon as you're asking questions about if anyone wants to help you with it. And please, pretty please, keep those intro PDFs for your game as slim as possible. There's nothing quite as dissapointing to me as getting psyched up to check out someone's new design and finding out that they're asking me to read a 60 page PDF with a wonky font.
