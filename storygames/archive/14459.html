    \n Mouse Guard Spring 1153

    The patrol continued their investigation of Matis the Master Chemist's death. Meanwhile, the city guard is investigating the chemists of Sprucetuck for a lack of scent provided for the scent border (which is actually the result of Clove, the tenderpaw, trying to make her rival Autumn look bad). The murder investigation seems to point to a chemist being involved. The patrol finds the damning evidence a bit too convenient. Through casting a wider net, they discover that Matis' son Norbert (a politically ambitious mouse) paid a runner to plant evidence. Upon confronting him (and the ensuing hanggliding chase through the spiral of Sprucetuck) the patrol brings him to justice and learns that Norbert had discovered that Matis was working with a ferret spy to obtain weasel musk for his super powerful scent formula. Norbert, worried about his political career, confronted his father and things got out of hand and he accidentally pushed him from the top of the Sprucetuck spiral. The head of the town guard, Leland, congratulates them on a job well done.

    Also, through some really good deceit rolls on the part of the surprisingly petty Clove, Autumn is demoted for her malfeasance. Next session, the beginning of Summer 1153!

    \n A week ago I had my second go at running an old school D&D game. My poison of choice is the Holmes edition and I ran a dungeon I'd constructed myself with some creative input from kesher over at the OD&D74 boards. I learned a lot, and had great fun! Best single part of the night might have been the look on the face of the guy whose character was the first to die (three were killed!) when I told him he had to tear up the character sheet, but it wasn't. And, the guy who was first to tear up his sheet (and got his second character killed as well) said he'd definitely play old school D&D again if offered.

    Story wise? The usual stuff. Dungeon. Goblins. Wolves. Orcs with aloe vera insides. Living roots that strangle you.

    \n Misspent Youth - finishing our Christian Vampires session.
    Along with the traditional way, our vampires can also drink through their fingernails, like blood-drawing needles. Creepy.
    The shriving ceremony of our Christian Vampire future - the supplicants - some human, some 'living vampires'; vampires who are infected but haven't died yet, which is what gives them their full expression - have the sin drunk from them from the Sisters, full vampires.

    One of the Sisters is onto us -
    Sister: "There was rebel activity right there where you do your bible study. You were so close to so much sin! Did you partake in any?"
    Sebastian (a vampire-hating human): "Well, it was knee deep in sin at that place. I didn't do it on purpose but I might have gotten some on me by accident."
    Sister: "Come and be cleansed, my child."
    Sebastian: "Um, sarcasm!?"
    Janella (a living vampire rebel who is secretly into Sebastian): (whispers) "Vampires don't *get* sarcasm."
    Sebastian (whispers) "You would know."
    Janella: "Jerk."

    Sebastian is enslaved by the vampires, rescued by Janella, and they burn down Second Chapel.

    \n Microscope:

    It turns out that the first encounter between humanity and the Vestas was between a Portuguese "Navigator" named Tomas Silva and a Vesta who is "of the Hunted" of his own race. Tomas and "The First" seemed to have some kind of symbiotic relationship; it's not entirely clear as of yet. Their contact was on a planet that was deep on the frontier of each of the respective species which the humans know as "Avalon."

    The idea that First Contact would be between a human and a faction/caste of aliens that are unwanted and even despised by the vast majority of their own race is really intriguing to me rather than the standard concept where the aliens are of a monolithic one-government entity.

    It looks like we're going to play two or three more sessions of this same history in quick succession over the rest of June.

    \n Dungeon Crawl Classics - we're each playing three zero-level guys, totally random and totally useless as adventurers. We've got a pair of Elven artisans, an astrologer, and a guy carrying a bag of human shit. We've got some rope, modeling clay, and a candle. Our most competent fighters are slain by animated skeletons about ten seconds in. We're near the end of the dungeon, having lost five guys (one to a centipede bite). And wouldn't you know it, there are more skeletons, including a magic skeleton who shoots lasers from his eyes. We are not ready for this nonsense! Our best remaining fighter, Elf Rider, ends up hanging from a burning bridge. Laser Eyes starts blasting at us from across a chasm. And it is at this moment that I look down on Moonshadow's character sheet and realize his one piece of equipment is a hand-held mirror.

    His STR may only be 4, and Laser Eyes may be across a chasm, but man, physics is physics!

    \n We're doing yet another Polaris setting hack. We've already done Aztecs vs Conquistadors, Fall of the Roman Republic, 1920s Empire City cops, and Battlestar Galactica (twice).

    What now? 1950s-esque America. The Mistake isn't Communism (too easy), it's self-righteous patriots trampling citizen's freedom to find The Enemy Within. McCarthyism, Un-American Activities witch-hunts, and all that post-911 stuff you hate so much.

    So who does that make our Knight analogs? Diligent FBI guys? Nope, that would only work if Communism was the Mistake. Our protagonists are that final line of defense against oppression: Journalists

    \n Love in the Time of Sequel (working title):

    \xcdsar's bad eye is still bleeding and filled with pus. His good eye is closed now, too, after he converted. He's on his knees outside the abbey, hammer in one hand, axe in the other. The creature in front of him is breathing; he can't see it, he's blind, but its breath is the smell of a cellar on a warm summer's night. \xcdsar is calm, a warrior. He hits with his axe, then the hammer. With his axe he holds, while the hammer pounds. The creature doesn't resist. \xcdsar climbs up, sits on top of its huge head. It launches into the air.

    - - -

    Fj\xf3la is hanging by her fingers from the abbey window in the dark, holding a bag of gold in her other hand. Below her, the moat is filled with something that moves. The dead nun Ughaine is looking out the window at her, her face black and swollen. She loosens Fj\xf3las fingers. The shield maiden grabs hold of the dead nun's arm with one hand. Her sword slips from its scabbard. Fj\xf3la drops the gold, grabs the sword by the blade. Fingers bleeding, she pummels the dead woman in the window with the sword hilt. Ughaine's face caves in.
