\n The seventh semi-annual National Capital Area Gameday will be held at the 9th floor of the AFT building, at the corner of New Jersey Ave and F St NW (555 New Jersey Ave, N.W., Washington DC, 20001), two blocks from the Union Station stop on Metro\u2019s Red Line.

Join fellow adventure, storytelling, and roleplaying gamers in the nation\u2019s capital for two fun-filled days of games. The event, held in conjunction with ENworld, one of the world\u2019s largest RPG fan communities, is free to all participants thanks to the generous support of the gameday organizers. Gamemasters/game hosts will be able to start posting games on September 10th; player signups will commence at 700pm Eastern, September 17th.

See the DC Gameday forum on ENWorld (registration required, free access) for more information.

DC Gameday on Facebook: http://www.facebook.com/pages/DC-Gameday/115555934924
DC Gameday on Twitter: http://twitter.com/DCGameDay

(This is a semi-annual event, held in March and October of each year.)

    \n Sam: I'll bump repeatedly.

    Jason: Some of the folks that live in Raleigh come up here for the DC Gameday, just as many of us go down there. Like NCGD, it's laid back, but much more communal in that we're all in a largish room, and there's a very cool dinner event on Satuday night that's a great way get to know folks you don't already know. Of course, my view of the tone at NCGD probably varies widely from yours, as I've only attended it a couple of times.

    Our Fall gameday is about the size of the Spring NC Gameday, and like that one, it's a two-day event. It typically runs between 40-60, and we might exceed 60 this year based on folks telling me they're coming.

    The range of games being played is getting wider every year -- there have been at least two to three events per day that were indie or small-press focused, some very successful, some mixed results. I'm inclined to say there's probably more story gaming up here than at the NCGD, but I'm working to increase the selection by always running something out of the regular games being run (and see Adam's post above). I've not been as successful there as I like, but to be blunt, I have you to thank for what I think will be a critical mass this year: we're expecting several sessions of Fiasco to be scheduled.

    More simply put: we have the potential for being a mini-Dreamation, but we're not there yet. Lots of games get offered, and some of us go out of our way to offer those that you typically won't see at a "trad" regional con (although the minute I typed that, I'm not comfortable with that kind of pigeon-holing).

    Sorry to ramble -- not sure if I'm answering the question well or not. I'd *love* to have you attend, if you could make it.

    EDIT: Rereading the above, it sounds like I'm being defensive and all that. Not my intent. Using meaning-laden (or, -devoid, depending upon who you talk to) generalized labels/terms to try and sum up a feel is a minefield.
