\n Within 24 hours, two buddies mentioned that they weren't gaming as much as they'd like. They were both dads and both solid candidates for Burning Wheel.

So, I invited them both to game once a week over Skype, as I'm in upstate N.Y., one player is in Florida and the other is in Maine.

We played with an online roller for a while but it seriously lagged and slowed the game down. Every time we got to a conflict, just when things should be amping up, we had to dial down and wait for the damned dice to roll. It was a pain in the ass.

So, we switched to an honor system, just rolling our own dice on our own desks.

Funny, thing, it isn't a matter of trust for me but a matter of enjoying each other's dice rolls. It is a palpable and visceral part of the game that is missing.

The game is going really well and I'm enjoying it far, far more than I thought I would but it just drove home how much fun it is to watch the dice fall where they may and take part in the cheering, groaning, etc.

    \nThe game is going really well and I'm enjoying it far, far more than I thought I would but it just drove home how much fun it is to watch the dice fall where they may and take part in the cheering, groaning, etc.

    So true. I've had at least two visceral moments of the dice themselves in the past month.

    In Zombies at the Door, where I rolled three threes for little Johnny, which sent him into the gaping maw of the Zombie horde. The other players, who had blithely piled on the side of the other character in the conflict (who now got to be that much farther from threat of the Zombies) now had to face the gut wrenching truth that they had killed little Johnny.

    Ah, the groans of guilt ridden agony. That was the most satisfying death of a characters I've had. So delicious. Wouldn't have missed it for the world.

    The other was playing Shooting the Moon with two confirmed non-dice players. We had fists full of the things 9, 12, 15 d6s. While rolling we just fell into the rhythm and kept going and going with these massive paws full of dice. Didn't want the moment to end.
