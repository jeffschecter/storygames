    \n Sorry it took me so long to get to answer this!\n
    Oh, why not? You're the one who writes your own secret, after all. It could even be something you're planning to tell the family, but haven't yet (e.g. your boyfriend proposed to you last night; you never told anyone that you were the one who killed the goldfish three years ago; etc).
    \nBelieve it or not, this would be (slightly) past my line for unsettling/scary in role-playing games. That's all!\n
    Not sure what you mean by realistic secrets, however. Can you explain?
    \nYeah, sorry, that was pretty opaque. I meant: rather than playing with your actual secrets, I advocate playing with fictional secrets that you imagine someone else may actually be keeping from their family in the real world, which - to me - tends to color the game political: it implies criticism of prevalent societal norms, values and models of relationship. Which is what turns me on.
