    \n Read Baker's treatise on game design in sundered land. The first principle of engineering, which is all about design, is first figure out what problem you are trying to solve, then design a solution that solves it. In game design terms, figure out what you want to get out of the game and then figure out what mechanics to use to get the game play you're looking for. The other way is the bass ackwards way.

    @Biest @CarpeGuitarrem Yeah, it\u2019s a very interesting period, and well worth looking at for settings and themes other than the traditional Lovecraftian ones (although those are fun too). It was the period after WWI when it really seemed as if anything was possible, particularly in terms of sexual mores and freedom of expression. Women over 30 were given the vote in the UK in 1918, with full suffrage coming a decade later; the class system began to break up, and the Labour Party made huge inroads politically.

    It was also at this time that a discernible drug culture emerged; although people had been using cocaine and opiates recreationally since the 19C, it wasn\u2019t until the early 20C that restrictive drug laws were introduced, thereby classing their users as criminal, which in turn created a drug sub-culture that continues to this day. Syncopated Jazz became fashionable and pervasive, breaking from the tradition of the heavily orchestrated and constrained music of previous centuries, and crucially it was a musical genre that originated in African-American culture, thus allowing African-Americans to exert an unprecedented influence on Anglo- and by extension world- culture, and paving the way for the enormous variety of musical styles we have today.

    In the US specifically, you had Prohibition, the overt and powerful gangsterism of people like Al Capone, and, as with the UK and other countries, an \u2018anything goes\u2019 mentality that was ultimately shattered by the global economic meltdown of the 1930s, which to those of a moralising disposition must have seemed like a Providential reckoning for all the cocaine-snorting, ukele-strumming Bright Young Things.

    \n Ok, I got me some ideas, so I\u2019ll do a \u2018barf forth\u2019 and see how folks react to them.

    But first, a possible problem. I wanted to set the game in New York, so there could be characters (mainly the Grey Hats) who weren\u2019t necessarily involved in the film industry (or Broadway, as I was thinking at the time), but would be living there anyway. But by the 1920s the film industry was already well established in Hollywood. Solution? Have every character be involved in the film industry in some way (which would become like a \u2018beacon\u2019 of immorality throughout the entire game), and let each character have a prologue where they say why/how they came to be there, using their character sheet (dreams/ambitions and worries/fears etc.) for inspiration.

    So, to the mechanics, and here I\u2019ve discovered something very interesting: I\u2019ve played and read about an awful of collaborative story games over the last couple of years, and looking over my notes I find that I\u2019ve pretty much taken all the mechanics I\u2019ve got from various other games. It might help orientate folks if I say which mechanic I\u2019ve taken from where. So\u2026

    1. From My Life with Master: each player distributes x number of points over three main stats: Fortitude, Ambition and Fear. Task resolution involves rolling against two of these (and maybe eventually some other stats). So forex, if a movie mogul is trying to get the Aspiring Starlet character to sleep with him, she rolls her FORT (ability to resist temptation) minus her AMB, against the mogul\u2019s FORT, plus or minus other hindrances/advantages that have built up in-game.
    2.\tFrom Hot War/Fiasco: establish relationships with one or more other PCs, for both roleplaying and mechanical advantage/disadvantage.
    3. From MLwM: establish connections with Grey Hats.
    4. From DramaSystem: collect points for [not sure] which can be spent on compelling other characters to do things for you.
    5. From Montsegur 1244, Witch: the Road to Lindisfarne and other similar games: questions that the characters answer through play.
    6. From Battlestar Galactica: if FORT dips below a set level (Zero points?) a PC can themselves become a Black Hat, FORT immediately goes up to max level, and they can attack other characters. If FORT goes above the max level, the PC becomes a White Hat and FORT stays at max. Can then help other characters.

    The above is similar to the BSG board game in that when cylons are revealed part way during the game they don\u2019t have to pretend not to be cylons anymore, and therefore can do greater damage. Not entirely sure it would work for Disgraceful though.

    I see FORT as the pivotal stat of the game, because it\u2019s how the PCs resist temptation, and it will go up and down during the game as they wrestle with their inner demons. I\u2019m thinking of making it increase arithmetically, but decrease exponentially, so that if say they do a good turn for someone it increases by 1, whereas if they do something bad, it decreases by 1 the first time, 2 the second, and 3 the third, etc.

    Game structure: as I\u2019ve put on the burb, I\u2019m intending to go for a lasagne-like structure, with play alternating between flavour scenes and ones where the PCs interact mechanically with the gameworld, various NPCs and each other:

    Act 0. Prologue (no points given out).
    Act 1
    a. Scenes that establish relationships and reveal questions etc.
    b. \u2018Combat\u2019 with Black Hat, or interaction with Grey Hat.
    Act 2
    a. More establishing scenes, which can include stuff from both 1a and 1 b.
    b. More combat (plus interaction with White Hat- solicit help from WH).
    Interval- OOC chat*.
    Act 3
    a. As 2a.
    b. As 2b.
    4. Pulling together strands- tilt?
    5. Epilogues.

    Possible characters:
    Pink Hat: Lily Fox (real name Bridget Maloney)- aspiring movie star, wants a lead role or to marry millionaire. Na\xefve. Sick mother.
    Red Hat: Jimmy Drake, disgruntled former shop/hotel employee)- wants to be script-writer. Angry at being dismissed. Impetuous, no self-control.
    Yellow Hat: Billy Kowalski- gossip writer, wants a scoop. Works for tabloid paper whose editor has no morals. Weak-willed, can\u2019t say no.
    Green Hat: Simeon \u2018Stookie\u2019 Michaels- cabbie, wants to own fleet of taxis. Jealous of his rich customers.

    Finally, I'm in the process of trying to re-invigorate my blog (which is code for finally getting round to doing something with it). In general, are folks happy with the approach I've adopted thus far, i.e. every now and then do a summary of where I am with the project, as I've done here, or would they prefer a link to my blog/website when it's ready so that interested parties can go and read about it it there? (Or both?).

    *A mechanic for releasing tension that I came up with for WtHi: players spend a few minutes OOC discussing how they think their characters have behaved.
