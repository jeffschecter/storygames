    \n I think there should be an element of risk involved.

    Like, you have the Limit pool, with a bunch of dice in it. There is some condition, or some trigger, which, if it happens, turns all the dice in there into something really really nasty for your character.

    So, you can kick ass and build up your Limit pool, but if your character is ever hit by [something], or is ever knocked unconscious, or ever travels through another dimension (or something else you could risk at certain points in the game), all those dice do something bad to you.

    You'd have to choose whether to bleed off your Limit pool with fallout, or risk carrying around a big Limit pool. You might get lucky, if you're lucky and smart enough... but if you don't, your fall will be a long one.

    \n I really like this idea.

    And yeah, I second the risk of Keeping Your Angst*. ("I need my angst. It keeps me sharp.") You can carry it, but if you build up too much, it blows up on you and you don't get to use it to your advantage. This would work best if you don't always have the choice whether to take fallout or not. If your opponent can sneak a good hit in there that MAKES you take extra fallout, it could be enough to push you over the edge (of your Limit), preferably (in my mind) causing you to even lose the conflict.

    Or you can be clever, saving a little bit of angst*, using it to help you get stuff done.


    *or call it Limit...
