    \n Crucial difference #1:
    Pick Durance if you want a one-shot game, which you'll have fun playing multiple times with different people, but will always wrap up in a single session.
    Pick Perfect if you want a game to play over multiple sessions. I guess it is occasionally possible that a game runs to completion in just one session, but I find it quite unlikely.

    \n From what I saw of browsing these two, I liked the style and vibe of Perfect. I liked the strong "story now" feel of Durance.

    I'm in awe of * World, Lady Blackbird, Microscope.

    I like tragedy and underdog stories--even ones that don't work out well--but not bleakness, hopelessness.

    Don't like heavily violent stuff. I don't want to delve into what the worst things are.
