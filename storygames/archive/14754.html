\n Hello all,

I posted a similar thread (on supers gaming) earlier... this is another idea I'm enjoying and looking to see if you have thoughts.

I'm having fun in my spare time thinking of how to build games and new game engines. I've been interested lately in supers game engines and also more simple game systems that could be used to inspire or teach younger players. Kids!

Though, I am of full belief that in playing games with kids, you don't need to be overly simple to have fun, to teach (a little), to tell neat even complex stories, or in any way "talk down to" kids. Thus... a game system focused on this type of more simple system CAN (even should!) be ELEGANT above all. Enough for older players (parents and siblings) to have fun with younger players as young as 3-4 years old and above.

One thought here I'm exploring is building a game that could be similar to the idea of "reading/telling a story" to kids as they go to bed - in place of the classic children's book before bedtime. Or even around the campfire - in place of ghost stories.
Additionally...
I'd love to have the game be built with a "campaign" level style that older game players could pick up and have fun with, perhaps building a campaign to mimic anything from campaigns based on Grimm Fairy Tales to campaigns based on movies such as Toy Story or Small Soldiers, etc...
Or even just a fun elegant system for one-shot "soda-n-pretzel" gaming! hehe


Any thoughts on game systems or mechanics to start on?

    \n This is something I've been thinking about with some work on a hack of my own FUBAR system...

    I wanted a simple system that teaches kids that sometimes you can't get what you want and that makes things interesting....with the bonus that if you don;t get what you want up front, it becomes even more valuable when you do end up successful later on.

    The tentative title for this kids version of FUBAR is "Oh No!". It still needs a lot of work though, and a decent bit of playtesting with my young nephews.
