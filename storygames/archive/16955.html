    \n Roleplaying is used in all sorts of professional training. Call centers use roleplaying to train people in dealing with customers. In law school I roleplayed through a client interview (in front of 70 other students and a prof ready to critique me), as well as in-court argument. First responders and military forces use roleplaying in various scenarios as part of their training. Various institutions often use different scenarios to evaluate their response to situations and students of various social fields use roleplaying to study the interaction of different institutions and situations. Sometimes they use fanciful situations in order to minimize the impact of pre-existing prejudices on various real-life subjects.

    Here's one from 2010 run at the Paterson school having to do with how various American institutions would cope with a True Blood-style vampire "outing".

    I know roleplaying is used in psychological treatment in many different areas, but don't know enough specifics to get into detail. Any decent psychology handbook should be able to give you more.
