[Rule of Three] - what flesh goes on these bones?

MikeRM
July 2006 edited July 2006 in Story Games
So I had a go at the 24 Hour RPG thing, just to see if I could. And I sort of could, but the result needed a lot of tidying after the 24 hours were up. But now I have a nice little game, 6 pages including a 1-page character chart, and I'm wondering - how should I flesh this out? I know it needs fleshing out, because of Reeves-McMillan's Third Law* if nothing else. But by that same law, I don't know what else people would want to see explained and what they can work out for themselves.

I want to resist writing a specific setting for it, for a number of reasons: laziness, lack of ideas, a preference for "settings-agnostic" universal game systems, and the presence in this one of a method for involving the players in generating the setting using a separate "pre-game". That's not to say I can't give more hints about how to do a setting. I suspect that more hints on story structure might be another good area.

Suggestions of other topics I could productively cover in more depth welcomed, indeed, sought. Also any general critique and suggestions. And if anyone wanted to playtest it - gratitude!

It's GM-less and diceful - you hippies have corrupted me, when I first started hanging out here I was all about diceless and GM-full. :-} Ah well.

    OK, I'm serious this time. Version 1.3 is up, thoroughly revised from earlier versions. It falls into several parts:

    - Pages 1-3 outline the Open Questions minigame which generates the setting and characters. Others are free to use this. Attribution appreciated.
    - Pages 4-7 are on character creation. I've completely revised the character creation system and the character chart (p. 14). It has nine aspects (skills, equipment, training, "drivers", relationships, background, attributes, insights and knowledge) in a 3 x 3 grid. You can invest up to 2 points per element in a number of elements which fall within these aspects (i.e. specific skills, etc.). By combining 3 elements in permitted ways, you get a number to compare against a d6 roll. You have "dark points" as well as ordinary points, which have narrative and character-development consequences later on.
    - Pages 8-10 are on Story in general, and again, feel free to use it, expand it, suggest how I should expand it, etc. I lay out a 3-act structure with suggestions for ways the characters can meet, situations/dilemmas that might form the basis for the action, ways of escalating the plot, and possible resolutions. The resolutions section is very sketchy as yet - if anyone can suggest a resource I'd appreciate it.
    - Pages 11-12 are on the mechanics of challenges.
    - Page 13 is on character change, including a mechanism allowing for Redemption or Fall of characters under defined circumstances.
    - And page 14, as previously indicated, is the character chart.

    Minimally formatted as yet, still with embryonic bits, but... I think to a point where I would like some serious critique. Is it playable? Would it attract you to play it? If not, why not? Which bits are non-obvious and need opening up? Like that.
