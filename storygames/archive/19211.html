    \n Scarily, I think there was (unofficial at least) a record for most consecutive hours spent gaming. It's the sort of thing clubs (primarily Uni ones) used to do in the 80's and 90's.     Ignoring that, sounds like you've got a full schedule, congrats. I've had the odd week where I accidentally ended up gaming 5 or 6 nights on the trot (when all the different groups and a Con/meet would all align) but I try hard to avoid it happening. I don't want to use up all the good will at home by mistake and after too much all grouped together (especially when some are running games), I just want the breathing space.

    \n I'm sure that there were weeks during the summer of '82* when I woke up, got online** and played asynchronous D&D, freeform or other RPGs for a couple hours until my peers were waking up at which point I'd ride my bike to someone's house (or The Dragon's Lair my FLGS of the time) and play games (which might include RPGs, board games, miniature war games and computer games) until dinner time after which I'd play D&D for three hours with my parents. And repeat that for each weekday. Then on Saturday, I'd have the same morning but my folks would drop me off at a game store where I would play with a different crowd from like noon to ten PM. And then on Sunday, my family would play a different D&D campaign, set in the same world as our after-dinner game, with one or two of my friends for several hours.

    I doubt it's a "record" but you'd have to get up pretty early to beat the obsession and free time that suburban kids can bring to the table. :-)

    * I'm just making that up, it could have been any summer within a couple years of that, but 82 seems most plausible...
    ** This means dialing into a BBS
