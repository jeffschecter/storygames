\n Ryzom is a Massive Online Role-Playing Game (MMORPG) that recently went open source.

All of their code is licensed under the AGPLv3 license, and most of their artwork is licensed under a CC-by-sa license.

That is a stunningly generous gift to the world. It consists of about 1,000,000 lines of code and 3 Gbytes of artwork that you are now free to use in your own projects, provided you follow the terms of the open source licenses.

Ryzom offers a 21 day free trial. So, check it out! You'll be impressed.

Here is an example screenshot:



The only artwork that Ryzom did not open source are the actual landscape of the major continent, the storyline and quests, and the specific skills and spells used by characters (the core game rules, though, are incorporated into the open sourced code). Even if you aren't into online gaming, you may have some use of their extensive artwork collection.

Personally, I plan on hacking on the source code and modifying/replacing the game rules to suit my own tastes. I am totally stoked!

    \n I agree. Most MMORPGs bore me to tears, and Ryzom has a lot of the same characteristics. Specifically, it has a lot of grinding to gain levels. I don't really mind that as much as the not being able to explore much before grinding. If I could explore a good part of the world independently of grinding to gain levels, then I wouldn't mind the grinding so much.

    But, as you say, the exciting part here is the ability to re-create the game to suit a different style of play. I think MMORPGs represent a huge potential in terms of what's possible, and that what's out there already in this domain has only scratched the surface. I want to explore some of the possibilities, and this gives me the opportunity to do that. I'm trying to finish up on my current projects so I can dive into this fully.
