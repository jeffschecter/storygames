Any interest in LARP scenario \u2018boxed sets\u2019?

\n

\n
\n So, from what I can tell there doesn\u2019t seem to be too big a market for high quality parlour-style LARP scenarios. There are a few nice-looking products out there, but the majority of LARP scenarios I can find available online are free offerings that are generally rough, relatively poorly formatted and put together, and usually fairly bare-bones when it comes to GM guidance about how to run them. It looks like this is usually because most scenarios are written for a specific group or crowd, and making a copy available online for use by others is an afterthought rather than the primary focus.

I kept this in mind when I was writing Blackened Hearts (released last month, thread here). I wanted to present a LARP scenario that was a bit more polished than a lot of other offerings, and provide a reasonably comprehensive guide on how to run the game, to make it easier for new GMs and players to get into it. While I wish I\u2019d had a bit more money to spend on layout and art, I think I succeeded in creating a product a cut above a lot of other scenarios that are currently around.

The response has been pretty positive and I\u2019ve gotten a lot more downloads than I was expecting. Not only that, but a mildly surprising number of people have been willing to toss me some actual money for a pay-what-you-want product.

One of the players from a playtest group suggested that it might be a good idea to put together a physical product, in addition to having PDFs available \u2013 something with all of the props, all of the name tags, everything, already put together so that a GM could run it out of the box without having to do much more than invite people and prepare a space \u2013 and said that they would almost certainly buy a set if I did.

I sort of dismissed this at the time, but I started thinking about it again recently. I don\u2019t think this sort of LARP scenario \u2018boxed set\u2019 is a thing at all, is it? I certainly haven\u2019t seen or found any, looking around. Does anyone think there could maybe be a market for something like this?

\u2018Murder mystery dinner\u2019 style products have been a thing for a really long time, but as a LARP player I\u2019ve found them to generally be a bit lacklustre. \u2018Escape Room in a Box\u2019 style games appear to be doing quite well recently as well, with a couple of different brands popping up.

Do you think there is a market for this sort of LARP scenario product? A box, complete with GM guidance, props, and everything else you need to run the game? Do you think it\u2019s something that would be worth pursuing, perhaps with a small Kickstarter?

    \n Having recently priced out the props I want to run Blackened Hearts (the coins and coin-bags and nametag holders, primarily), I suspect a good kit might wind up being a little pricey and a pain for you to put together, just because you'd have to source these kinds of things from a wholesaler and figure out how to print and package them. People I know who've been through the process of manufacturing a game have run into a lot of stumbles when they need custom gear. But it would be pretty convenient for people wanting to run the game, certainly. Seems like something you'd really want to do in conjunction with an established company that already has a relationship with toy manufacturers.

    One more example you might look to is Jay Treat's Strange Gravity, a kind of make-your-own-Star-Trek-LARP game that doesn't come with props, but does break down all the roles into well-designed printed cards, suitable for putting in nametag holders, and with all the necessary info printed on them. That's a step down from a LARP packaged with toys and props, and closer to deck-designed ones like Ghost Court and Juggernaut.

    \n Sure, I'll try, but I'm finding it a bit difficult to find the words.

    I roleplay because it's fun to put on a character and take part in interesting stories and have cool, exciting, funny things happen with fun interplay between characters.

    There are a lot of parlour/theatreform LARP scenarios that are deep explorations of heavily emotive content. They're interesting and I can understand why people enjoy them, but they're not the sort of thing I want to be immersed in.

    For example, look at this description for one of the scenarios in Crescendo Giocoso:\n
    In First they came, the players will step into the shoes of three opponents of the Third Reich to discover the pain and paranoia carved in the hearts of the German people by the persecutions of the regime. By reliving the characters\u2019 trials, they will have a chance to understand how they came to their predicament. What mistakes and atrocities did they condone to save their own lives? Were they part of the problem, as well?
    \nMore various bits snipped from other descriptions:\n
    What will happen when others strike at us with harrowing revelations about ourselves?

    The game mechanics aim to trigger a spiral of crushing paranoia ...

    ... a means to experience emotion, and emotion is a vessel for self-discovery ...

    ... a postmodern tragedy ...
    \nNone of these things sound very fun to me, at all. At best, they sound emotionally exhausting.
