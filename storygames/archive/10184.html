\n I showed up to Luke, Jared and Paul's seminar on game design at GenCon with some friends. Half of the time was spent working on an idea Paul threw out: a game about an extreme tattoo artist competition. The seminar was great, even with the suggestions of supplements for each celebrity you might want to tattoo (with posters, of course).

So, Friday night, we wrote the game.

So, Saturday night, we played the game, and had a great time doing it.

So, now it's on Story Games. Check out Tramp Stamp. It's not really Luke, Jared and Paul's fault. At least not directly.

    \n OK, I'd suggest that you use "die" for singular, not "dice."
    The And Then dice are never mentioned until we're told to roll them.
    When you speak about "if someone got a Real Life" you haven't yet defined it (you do so in the next bullet, and I notice you Don't Capitalize It in that one?).
    Looks like you have a virtuous spiral, WRT the traits getting increased for every die over 3. If that's intended, cool.
    I like how +Flash makes a way to "sabotage" another person, when you know you won't get to do the tattoo that day.
    Finally, for "If someone gets a Real Life, the person with the badass tattoo job has someone to keep them grounded. [I am guessing that's the person who got the Real Life, right?] They succeed in using their vices for the cause of awesome. Describe how your tattooing works out for you. [And, here, I'd guess that this person with a Real Life should be a part of that narration?]" I think a bit more verbiage would help, there.

    Hope this helps.

    \n Thanks for the feedback!

    The virtuous spiral is completely intentional, since as the stat goes up you're more likely to be a badass tattoo artists but also more likely to have horrible, horrible Life Scenes. When it comes time to tattoo, other players can level the playing field with -(Your Best Stat) or +Flash, so it all works out more or less. Not fine-tuned Dungeons and Dragons balance, but close enough to be fun in our experience.

    Anyway, there's a new version.
