\n Event registration for Go Play NW 2010 is now open!

www.goplaynw.org

Yes, that's our shiny new website with all the info. Details like:\n
  • We've got a brand new venue. Yes, everyone loved last year's space, but instead of resting on our laurels we did some soul-searching, followed by some good old-fashioned gumshoe detective work, and found someplace even better. More quiet private spaces, more big communal spaces -- just more spaces of every kind. Whatever kind of environment you like to game in, there's probably more of it here.
    • \n
  • 26 solid hours of gaming (and if you have the fortitude to game in every single session, you really do deserve a cookie, because you're a trooper)
    • \n
  • the Friday Night Feast, the golden opportunity to get to know all the people you're going to game with. Mingle, chat and eat some food. Do not miss it.
\nRegister early, and let us know you're in, because we really just can't wait to see who's coming.

Team GPNW

Go Play NW 2010
June 18-20

    \n Depending on how much exercise / travel you want, the Hugo House looks like it's just up Pike Street from the Convention Center stop, which means you could potentially stay in any part of the city on that central 71, 72, 73, 74 bus route, which is the main artery from the U District to downtown, through the East Lake neighborhood.

    Up on my side of town (U District, which would be like 20 minutes on the bus + 15 min walk to the stop from Hugo), there's the University Motel and, even cheaper, the hostel near the university that looks like a Shakespearean pub (and has great local beer on tap in the basement).

    The U District is almost as exciting as downtown and probably cheaper to hang out in, so something to consider.
