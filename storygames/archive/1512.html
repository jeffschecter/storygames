    Since it seems that everyone who wanted to weigh in did so, here was my original intent:

    "Do you care about this? Do you have something to fight with? Are you uninjured? If you're missing any of these, you'll just lose. Assuming you have all three, you and your foe compare several qualities. In this case: magic (the wand), speed/agility (the runner), strength (the weightlifter), weapons (the axe and claw), and toughness (that's a helm and shield there)." Note: In my head these would be binary qualities that you either have or don't. They could be pictures on the character sheet. To continue: "If you have more, you win. If you have less, you lose. If you're tied, you go back and forth until one of you runs, or you both get so tired you can't fight." (A very Oz sort of ending.)

    Now - what could be done to clear these meanings up? Obviously a key would be useful, but I'm trying to think of ways to do it in a purely graphical manner first. I know that the helm and shield gave some of my friends trouble (they just couldn't ID it), and some had trouble with the scales implying balance rather than comparison.

    Any suggestions?
