    \n At various times and for various audiences, I've used any or all of the following:

    * Remember D&D? Yeah, where you pretend to be an elf or whatever. That's basically it, except the games we're playing these days are usually more serious (this is my "elitist swine" pitch)

    * We take on roles, like in improv, except instead of just making everything up we turn it into a game (my "edge-case artistry" pitch)

    * Mostly we sit around and make up stories. Sometimes different people are responsible for different parts of the story (my "hand-wavey-you're-not-even-listening" pitch)

    * It's basically the same as playing Monopoly, except from the perspective of the shoe (my "that's funny but totally fucking weird, why would you do that?" pitch -- inevitably opens more conversation if they're even a little bit interested)

    * We pretend to be heroes, and one guy pretends to be the villain, and we roll dice to figure out who wins and loses (the one I gave my grandmother, which led her to -- I shit you not -- play Traveller with me when I was 15)

    * They're games like other games, except without boards or cards. Everyone imagines what's going on (the one I gave my fiancee).

    Except for the next-to-last one none of these has ever actually led to getting new people to play these games. But they've put the questions to rest.
