    \n I will be there; and I will likely be in the board/card/mini/pick-up area most of the con. I *might* track down a "conventional" game in a slot, but I tend to hang out with the VTES posse, and get meals and such with them, and they have their own schedule.

    More importantly--what should I BRING? I always over-pack, and end up wishing I hadn't hauled this, that, or the other. Plus the inevitable accumulation of new stuff--my back ain't what it used to be, and they day I need rolling luggage at a con is the day I check-in for rehab.

    I'm not even sure what the hawtness is, this year. I have a feeling some Descent will get played between VTES events--my co-con-goer is a HUGE junkie for it, these days. Personally, I got a Fiasco itch that needs scratching; and I'll have had the Adventure Burner for a month, so that might call for some fast-and-loose BW. Hero Clix, Slugfest, Steve Jackson, and Looney Labs check-ins are ubiquitous, of course; though I might not get stuck into the longer games on offing at their areas.

    MUST remember to BMOB. $12 LITs aren't in budget, this year.

    Where's Scott Acker?

    As for meetups and hangouts, I'm not totally adverse... but I'd rather meetup to play. D*C is My Con, the only one at which I do not GM most of the weekend; and further, my gamer group doesn't really do RPGs anymore; so I will want to pack in as much actual play as possible over the weekend. I won't even be eating out more than, say, once (as a "break from the madness," with the VTES crew). I can roll dice with one hand and stuff a burger with the other.

    I wonder if there's any cool LARPs this year--must read the booklet (received in the mail this week). I bet there will be steampunk. (Hmmm... Lady Blackbird LARP....)

    \nYunus: FUDGE dice are available everywhere. The Chessex booth will probably be the best bet, but I'd also just check some smaller vendors. I see them at every game store that I go to, so....

    Everyone Who's Interested: I'll be there from Friday night to Monday... some time. Mid-day, probably. My buddy and I have a room in the Marriott Marquis, so I'm totally free to do whatever, whenever (at past D*Cs, we've stayed with friends in ATL and had to leave when they did or pull an all-nighter).

    And as for "when, where"--I don't know, that's why I proposed a Google Group. I might be pulled into a LARP all Friday and Saturday night; I might finally get past first base with a friend I see there every year, and disappear all con; I might end up discovering some new (board/card/mini) game and being sucked in for hours.

    D*C is my One True Con--every other con that I attend, my schedule is packed with GM duties and desperate efforts to try a year's worth of new hawtness. I'm not planning a damned thing for D*C. Which makes some form of impromptu communications channel a good idea, to me. As long as folks tend to track along with the normal gaming slots (i.e. don't try to start a pick-up game a half hour into a slot, or with a half hour remaining in a slot) and provide at least some notice (i.e. more than "game in ten minutes, Y'ALL!!!!one1!!"), we should be able to get everything we want to play in and more.

    I have made a Google Group:
    Homepage: \t \thttp://groups.google.com/group/dragoncon-indie-2010
    Group email: \t \tdragoncon-indie-2010@googlegroups.com

    OK, we'll see how it goes--it's WIDE open, right now, and so we'll probably have to stay on top of spam for a bit... at least until everyone who's likely to join has joined, then I can tighten the membership restrictions.

    Use it or lose it--I will kill the group after D*C. DO NOT put anything unique there, or be sure to CC yourself if you do--I take no responsibility for lost creative works.

    Hope this works for y'all!
    David
