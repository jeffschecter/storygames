\n Hey all: I just got a heads up that the Indie RPG Awards were rolling around shortly. I looked over on the website, and holy crap! A lot of games came out in 2011 worth checking out, from many different walks of life!

Why was 2011 such a big year? Kickstarter? Collective Gaming Unconscious?

There's still a huge number that I haven't heard any (or many) play reports about. Maybe that's problematic, or maybe not?

http://www.rpg-awards.com/2011/registered_games.shtml

http://www.rpg-awards.com/2011/registered_supplements.shtml

I still haven't played Mystic Empyrean or Chronicles of Skin, though they are high on the list once things get a little less crazy.

Anyone else have thoughts on the entries? Anything you played and loved? Anything you haven't gotten around to playing yet?

-Andy

    \n Socio-Realism is a genre too, I'm not sure how we break out.

    Have played Beloved which is definitely the best game on the list!

    Have game mastered Crossroads, which I weren't too fond of it, really. But the book is shiny and free for me!

    Have bought and read Becoming Heroes and it got some cool ideas, but I don't know when I'll get around to playing it. It's not on the top of my list, and that list is long.

    Mystic Empyrean seems to have stolen some ideas directly out of my head and has a fantastic aesthetic. Will definitely have to play that at some point. The preview seems to leave out some of the crucial stuff (like, how to procedural generate the story and encounters in a GM-switching game), so I might have to buy the book.

    And Anima Prime may be the best stab I've seen at a genre dear to my heart. Might just cannibalize it for ideas rather than play it, though.
