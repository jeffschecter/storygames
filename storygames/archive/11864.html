\n Hey guys!

I made a screencast of my art work process, which you can see here: Video Link

I want to make more, but this one felt lacking because it has no direction.
I thought for the next one, I should have an art brief. But I didn't want to ''cheat'' by making my own brief. The honors go to you.

I will make (and screencast) 1 illustration. Instead of picking an art brief, let's make this collaborative yeah?
Each person after the first can add whatever he wants to the illustration, as well as use these statements:

1. ''yes, but''
2. ''yes, and''
3. ''but only if''

Let's try and keep the number of objects and characters within a reasonable range too.

Go!

    \n A dark goddess emerges from a shroud of mist; her long flowing hair seems alive. Arcane tattoos symbols cover her body (as most of it is covered by clothes).
    All who see her radiance are struck blind, but only if they are having their eyes pecked out by crows.
    But only if she is sensibly dressed, in clothing that covers her torso from shoulders to knees. So her face, her feet, and her arms (yes and she has more than 2 arms) are uncovered, showing them there symbols as tattoos.
    (as a side note, what exactly is sensibly dressed for a goddess? 'cause some of 'em seem pretty stoked to have necklaces of severed heads or skirts made of snakes...)
    Yes, but only if it's a parody of Botticelli's Birth of Venus. Yes but a parody that is incredibly subtle. This should be true, but NOT obvious.

    She comes bearing "gifts." Put them in her hands, or if you can figure out something better. If these gifts are new eyes, they should be polished stones, the eyes of flies, or pieces of coal. Or they could be guns, drugs, money, and record contracts. Make them fit the time period (modern, medieval, ancient India) which is up to you. The source material covers multiple dimensions and time periods, like Torg or Nobilis.

    The King of All Crows watches from the side. Either in the scene, or watching a vision of the scene. Since his minions are there. That's probably more than enough characters? How 'bout we limit the number of watchers to two. Three if you insist.

    Use a fairly limited colour palette. One dominant colour, one secondary colour, plus black, white, and grey.

    Can you maybe make a border around the image made of foreign-looking script? Like, runes or something, Armenian, whatever.

    Is there a moon or not? The rules say each person can add whatever he wants... It would be good to have a definite light source, I think.

    \n Your Photoshop method is a lot like mine:

    - Start with image
    - Add new layer on top
    - Check layer blends until one looks right
    - add texture layer on top
    - Check layer blends until one looks right
    - Add another texture or image
    - Check layer blends until one looks right
    - Erase stuff
    - Changer layer order
    - Touch stuff up
    - Keep adding layers and textures until it's done
    - Then go back and hide layers one by one to see if they are all necessary or if there is some magic combo hidden in it that I missed.

    That's just my interpretation from what I saw, maybe you have a more calculated approach, but I really like working without an exact idea of what it's going to look like. It's more of a discovery process than a creation process.

    Either way I enjoyed the video.
