\n I have been asked (multiple times) by Seth to create a thread on Story Games about the Game Publishers Association. I have been a member of the GPA since 1997, and most of that time has been serving on the Board of Directors as the GPA's webmaster.

The GPA is a trade organization that exists to benefit its members and the gaming industry. We have two main benefits that we are known for:

1.) The GPA-L mailing list. This is probably the main thing that many of our members rely on. Have a question about publishing your latest product? Wnat to know the best sources for game parts? Ask your question on the list and more than likely you'll end up with a number of answers from industry professionals. The information from this list could save you a lot of money.

2.) The GPA Booth Area. Conventions are expensive. However, they are the best way to be seen by customers and the industry. The GPA purchases booth space at GTS, Origins, and GenCon. Then we sell it back to our members, usually at a lower price and a better position on the floor than they could achieve on their own.

We also have the GPA Showcase, where you can put your products on display without the need of your own booth. Volunteers would sell your product and keep track of sales. Its possible to sign up for the Showcase show-by-show, or take advantage of the GPA Gold Program.

The GPA Gold Program is a service that allows members who wish to participate in convention showcases and co-ops to only pay for shipping once, to a single location, where their product will travel around the country, visiting six different conventions. We will keep an eye on inventory levels and send you after-con sales reports, so that you may send additional product should we run low on one or more of your titles. This allows us to pass on significant savings of both time and money to GPA members.

The following conventions are included in the 2008 Showcase Gold Program:

ConQuest Sacramento
ConQuest Las Vegas
Kublacon (CA)
Origins Show (Columbus, OH)
GenCon Indy
PAX (Seattle, WA)

    The Mailing List is paid for by the yearly membership dues. That is currently $75 for a General Membership and $35 for Associate Membership. Associate Membes get access to the Mailing List. In order to vote and get all the goodies the GPA offers, you must have a General Membership.

    April 18-20, 2008 \u2013 Conquest Vegas \u2013 Full $150 / Half $75
    April 20-23, 2008 \u2013 GAMA Trade Show (Las Vegas) \u2013 Corner $800 / Full $600 / Half $375
    June 25-29 2008 \u2013 Origins Show (Columbus, OH) \u2013 Corner $800 / Full $600 / Half $375
    August 14-17, 2008 \u2013 GenCon Indy \u2013 Corner $1560 / Full $1300 / Half $775
    August 29-31, 2008 \u2013 Penny Arcade Expo (PAX) \u2013 Full $1900 / Half $1000 / Quarter $500

    The GPA Showcase can vary from $20 (Conquest Sacramento) to $125 (PAX) We do not have a Showcase at GTS since that is an industry-only show and the only benefit you can get from it is by having a booth.

    The Gold Program for 2008 cost $475.

    \n FWIW, I am a member of the GPA and have been for several years now.

    To answer your question, Jason, the pool of industry professionals on the GPA list is quite different from the people at the Forge. If I wanted advice on how to get my book into Borders and B&N, I would ask on the GPA list. If I wanted recommendations for tuckbox manufacturers for my new card game, I would ask on the GPA list. If I wanted someone to warn me about the pitfalls of using a Chinese printers, I would ask on the GPA list.

    Now, if I wanted thoughts on how to market my small-press game, I would go to the Forge.

    As for the Showcase, the Forge does not do this, so I am unclear what you are referring to. If you are talking about IPR, they are two different beasts. I don't recall the buy-in for the showcase, but it is something like $25. They do not take a percentage of sales. Generally speaking, you will be lucky to hit double digits in sales. Mostly it is just to have a presence, so people walking by the booth see your cover.

    Well, if I remember the rules correctly, they don't want you selling your product unless you have a booth. They also don't want you camping out in the free gaming area and demoing it there.

    If you were using the GPA showcase, then you could easily direct people to it for sales. You can also schedule demos and tournaments for the price of a GM or Exhibitors badge.

    I've had some people follow me back to the booth after I had done a scheduled demo of Faeries Tale for Firefly Games. If you want to do some of your own selling you might also volunteer at the Showcase. But if you do, you want to be fair and help sell the other games too. Not just your own. We've had to smack one or two wrists in the past for steering people to their games only. It would also help you brush up on your "booth skills" without paying the full price of a booth.
