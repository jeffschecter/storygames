\n So I'm about to go play a game of Redbox D&D, at which I expect to have hoot loads of fun.

This weekend, I played a nice game of Burning Wheel which likewise brought the good.

I began to compare the two in my head, and suddenly I thought...

If your RPG was a car, what kind of car would it be?

Here's some to get you started:\n
    \n
  • White-box D&D: Ford Model T - first in mass production, set the standard.
  • Basic D&D: Ford Model A - cleaned up and re-imagined for a more competitive market.
  • Tunnels & Trolls: the Stanley Steamer - a different approach on the original, once very popular, but now only driven by enthusiasts.
  • REIGN: Toyota Prius - an unusual engine, mixing old and new technology, but a very nice ride
  • Warhammer Fantasy Roleplay 2 - the new Mini: a re-imagining of an old British favorite, retooled and upgraded to todays safety standards.
  • Burning Wheel: BMW Z4 - precision engineered, emphasizing power and control over luxury, it will give you an awesome ride, but expects skilled drivers.
  • Burning Empires: Tesla Roadster - uncompromising performance delivered via crazy technology.

    Dogs in the Vineyard - The water powered car - It can heal the world! Wizards of the Coast murdered Vincent Baker to try and surpress it!

    Polaris - The Solar Wing - Plays smooth and fast, at least until the last dying of the light.

    Spirit of the Century - The Skycar - 'nuff said.
