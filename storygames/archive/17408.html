    \n Two games leap to mind: Annalise, and Swords without Master.

    Both games make, effectively, a list of things that have come up, let you pare that list of uninteresting elements, and mechanically reward you, or mechanically require you, to reuse elements from that list. They differ in how they allocate responsibility for and authority over those elements.

    \n I'm glad someone mentioned Annalise. It's basically built around rewarding reincorporation, though not in the sense you'd normally think of "reward." It rewards other players, not the spotlight player, by letting them spend coins off their Claims ("I claim 'a knock at the door'!") to fuck with the spotlight player's outcomes.

    Technically, any game with freeform skills or traits (e.g., Dogs in the Vineyard) count, since those traits get introduced over and over. It's not quite what is implied by "reincorporation," but it kinda works that way.

    FATE tags are a kind of reward for reincorporation.

    \n The Shab Al-hiri Roach just has it mandated. You have a list of NPCs that must be brought into the game at specified periods. So if you embarrassed the Provost during the Welcome students scene, well, he'll be back later on and unhappy with your PC. If you chopped off and ate the left hand of the student body president, well, you'll have a one handed junior reappear a few scenes down the line. The effects you make on the NPCs will naturally return when the NPCs themselves do.
