\n I'm looking for a system that can emulate Battle Royale - ya know, a bunch of high school kids thrown on an island with random weapons and are forced to kill each other. This will be somewhat of an experiment for me, since I have never really ran a game where the sole purpose is for the players to eventually turn on each other.

What would people suggest? I'm leaning on the side of rules light but if a system really just shines for this - please feel free to put it out here.

    \nZombie Cinema does this just fine, for a GMless and ultralight treatment. For a more in-depth treatment, consider the unlicenced Battle Royale rpg, Classroom Deathmatch - it's basically the same exact thing under a different name. The system is lightweight (significantly more rules than ZC, but less than a traditional game) and modern, and strikes a balance between internal character traits and external relationship issues.

    \n Champions/Hero System:
    * 50-point normals
    * Heroic rules (i.e., gear is bought, not part of character build)
    * No Powers
    * Perks require GM Approval
    * No Disads that impact setting (i.e., DNPCs, Hunteds) but Physical and Psych Lims are permitted (good luck in battle, kid!)

    Heck, I think that could be fun as hell! Low Speeds and limited combat options will make it feel very fast and 'rules light' while still being simulationist.
