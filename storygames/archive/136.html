    Though the real thing this thread is making me feel is a profound sense of sadness for the mainstream of the industry. We are coming onto many of the big name games being at least a decade old. Feng Shui is 10 years this year, as are Witchcraft, Heavy Gear, Fading Suns, Everway, HKAT and Deadlands. Ars Magica, Shadowrun, Earthdawn, World of Darkness, Hero, and of course D&D are all far older... And 90% of the games we see are still laboring to be those games (and all too often failing).

    In those same 10 years computer games, board games, and CCGs have undergone big swings and changes, but much of our development seems to be stuck back sometime in 1995.

      Over 10 years... hmm.. We played so much basic D&D and then AD&D 2nd Edition back in the day. Those really colored my play, although I did some early juvenile game design back then... like a scifi game with a little bit of everything that was just a plain blast. But, we had fun with Deadlands and Dark Conspiracy too.

      Dark Conspiracy... here is a game with an interesting character generation system (at the time)... where you pick your jobs and build your character in an interesting way, but then the game just had some piss poor mechanics. And it never really answered the question, like what do you do? All I remember is that I would send the heroes into swamps to fight stuff, and they rode bikes in the adventures... just weird. Frustrating game...

      But I would still say AD&D 2E -- we did major rule chops and all sorts of random crap with that game... we broke rules, made new rules... As the years went out, we began to discover what the rules actually said.. and sometimes implemented them. Funny stuff, but great adventures and times with good friends.
