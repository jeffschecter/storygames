    \n Cut out all the mechanical talk from your pitch. You want to tell someone why the game will give them a compelling story first and foremost. Then, once you have their interest, you can tell them what the mechanics look like. But that's for after the elevator pitch.

    If I strip away mechanical talk from your pitch, I'm not left with much at all. It's a game about a single protagonist... who changes over the course of play. And some movies are cited, that seem to be linked through weird, impossible things happening to the protagonist. But I'm unclear on what that is about, exactly. There's not a lot to go on there. Tell us more about the sort of stories that the game tells. Tell us who the story is about, what they do and why we should care about them.

    One good technique is to end your blurb with a few questions that will be answered in play. "Will you negotiate the peace treaty before the interstellar warships destroy the earth?" "Will you find the traitor before they sabotage the mision?" etc. This creates uncertainty and engagement in the audience. They want to know the answer to the question, and they have to play to find out. (This is a trick stolen from comic books, which will often have a dangling question in the last panel of a page, to make you eager to read on.)

    Oh! Is that true? I wasn't sure about that. If so, then yeah, present it like that.

    I'd also like to know if the competition for narrative agency is a matter of:
    - Fate vs Fate (your first blurb made me think this)
    - Protag vs Team of Fates (your part about "dice pools that shift between the Fates and the Protagonist" made me think this)
    - everyone at the table vs everyone else (combining the above made me think this)

    I'd also like to know the object of this competition. Is it to get the protagonist to a given player's desired outcome? Or is it just for each player to be able to grab the reins when they'd like to (presumably when inspiration strikes)?

    If the Protag player isn't competing, what are they doing?

    The answers to these questions won't be the pitch, but I think they should help refine and focus it.
