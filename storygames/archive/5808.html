\n So, amidst all the talk going around here back and forth (myself very, very included), I thought I would pause for a moment and express something important:\n
Disagreeing with someone on the Internet, even so strongly that there may be no seeing eye-to-eye, doesn't mean a lack of respect for that person.
\nIt's easier to see that in person, but online all you see in the moment is someone disagreeing with your opinions -- sometimes politely, asking you to unpack more; sometimes harshly, saying things that they otherwise wouldn't. So, let's take a second and try something, if'n y'all don't mind.

I might disagree with Joshua A.C. Newman, but he was a bundle of fun energy at Dreamation. I'd like to hang with him again.

I might disagree with John Harper, but hell if I can't set my watch by the awesome ideas he drops on Story Games regularly.

I might disagree with Joe MacDonald, but I can't not respect the man who takes his pants off for convention games. The man practically invented American Jeepform with that move at Go Play NW last year.

Those are just three folks I want to point out as people I like and respect, even if we're in deep disagreement right now. I'd like this *sic* New New Honesty to not be about burning bridges, if I can help it -- and the Internet is like an accelerant in that respect.
