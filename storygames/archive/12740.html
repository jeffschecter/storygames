    \n I've been talking with one of the enforcers, he has provided me unofficial but insider information: room 213 will be tabletop HQ. No gaming here, but it will facilitate meetups through whiteboards and twitter (on projectors). If we have enough people to 'take over' a room we can probably get the HQ folks to funnel people interested in indie games to us.

    Rooms 201, 204-206, and 301-306 are tentatively slated as Tabletop Freeplay rooms. This may change. Sandstorm, one of the exhibitors, will likely have room 212 and be using that as their demo space. There will also be some gaming space in the Conference Center, across the street, but some of that will be given over to exhibitors. No word on what space the Dreaming has beyond their booth.

    This convention center map should give you a general idea where these rooms are, but the use of these rooms MAY CHANGE. 303 and 201 seem biggest and closest to the main escalators, maybe we should try to grab one of those?

    All of the tabletop space is for pretty much anything non-electric, not just RPGs. Tabletop HQ will check out boardgames. All open play rooms will be open 10am-2am (maybe 3am).

    Here's an excerpt from the enforcer's advice:\n
    So. Long story short. Pick a room from 301 to 306 as your rally point. Those are probably safest and least likely to be changed. In your post I would say something like "Ok, we're gonna go with Room 302 as our home base for indie RPGs, as long as the room is available." and leave it at that. But if the room you do pick is suddenly now a demo room, be prepared to be flexible. Come let us know in Rm. 213 about any indie RPG action you've got going on, and be sure to include the #paxttlfg in any of your Twitter posts -- those are the posts we'll be projecting onto a screen in our HQ for people to see.

    Hey, Joel, Joe, and anyone else who's running Shock:Human Contact!

    \n

    I just put together some Human Contact-specific sheets!

    \n

    Antagonist
    \nProtagonist

    \n

    At the bottom, you'll see "People" and "Identity" instead of Links. Use the Identity for things you believe and People for ... people. These are particularly important when using the seduction/mind control rules you've got. The reason People is separate is that, in the long game, you keep track of people. You can only have Protagonists that are people who already exist. Unlike what it says in the book, they don't need to have been Links or Antagonists first; they just need to have shown up and said something \u2014 if a Protag helps or is helped by someone, has sex with them, or shares food with them, they definitely need to go on the People list.

    \n

    If you want to play Alone (the "black ops anthropologist" version I did for PAX East), you'll need a colony sheet. You'll of course also need a grid.
