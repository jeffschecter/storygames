\n Hey all,

I've been working really hard on finishing Perfect. I'm now (almost) ready for what might be the final substantive round of playtesting. I am looking for people I can send the document to, to look at and run games with.

The deal:

I only want to send the document to people who are likely to read it and give me feedback on it.
It's roughly 20k words, so if you're only passingly interested, you should just hang tight and wait for me to sell it to you.
I'm looking for design & organizational feedback. The book is beyond "playtest directions" and is a solid draft.

I want people who are likely to organize 2-4 session games, with 3-4 players (themselves included).
I want people who will be able to record their play (rough notes, actual play summary, audio recordings, whatever!).
I want people who I can debrief with afterwards, over the phone.

Anyone who reads the document, gives me feedback, playtests a 2-4 session game, and gives me substantial feedback will be entered into a draw. The draw is for my Inspiration Pack, which includes a bunch of stuff that helped inspire Perfect and make it the game it is.

Inspiration Pack:
Dexter, Season One
Gotham by Gaslight
V for Vendetta (the movie)
A Clockwork Orange
Quills
One action figure, either V (for Vendetta) or The Batman (Gotham by Gaslight version)

That's 3 movies, 1 TV series season, 1 graphic novel, and 1 action figure.
That's like over $100 in stuff.
Because I like sharing.
No, because I like feedback.
I'm giving away that Inspiration Pack as an incentive to play the game promptly, and give me back good feedback.

I'll be drawing for the Inspiration Pack on February 30th, and sending the draft to the editor around then too (ie, feedback will only be meaningful if received by then).

So if you'd like me to send the document to you, toss me your email address.
Only do so if you are really likely to read through it and provide me with timely feedback, and likely to playtest it across 2-4 sessions.
Granted, of course, you might not be able to pull these things off... but you must have the intention!

Cheers!

    \nOh hey, mentioning what my game is about... that'd be a useful thing for this thread, hey?

    Perfect is a game about passionate criminals, in a Victorian-Dystopia, like Nineteen Eighty-Four meets A Clockwork Orange. The game is played out in spotlight scenes, which ask: What makes you a criminal? Will you get caught? If you do, will you withstand the inspectors' brainwashing? You have an antagonist player (like in Polaris or Shock), and its their job to push hard and provide your character\u2019s opposition, and help fuel their story.

    List of Inspirations:
    Dexter
    Gotham By Gaslight
    V for Vendetta
    Nineteen Eighty-Four
    A Clockwork Orange
    Shock: Social Science Fiction
    Robin Hood
    Brazil
    The Matrix
    THX 1138
    The Handmaiden's Tale
    steampunk, in general

    It can be a pretty intense, dark game. Criminals and inspectors, facing each other in the darkest of alleys, and the coldest of conditioning rooms, attempting to break one another.
