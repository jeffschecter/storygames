Followers of these paths take a series of bodhisattva vows, voluntary oaths to relieve the suffering of all beings.

    "One of those vows is that, basically, you have to kill if it will be of benefit to others," explains Nicholas Ribush, a former monk who heads the Lama Yeshe Archive. "If you don't, you are breaking your vows."

    So yea, sometimes Buddhists kill folks. I'm sure if you do enough research you'll even find a few Buddhist inspired madmen who did really horrible things in the name of belief.

    It's probably worth mentioning that "Buddhism" is not unitary by any means.

    There's a variety of Buddhism where even the greatest sinner can go to the Western Heaven if he chants the name of the Amidtabha Buddha.

    There's a kind of Buddhism where the greatest sins are murder and sacrilege (towards Buddhist stuff, natch).

    There's a kind of Buddhism where the whole problem with sin is the perception of sin and not-sin.

    Zen Buddhism has been used to help people fight better by not being afraid of death, maybe you could use that as a jumping-off place. Or, to address Shreyas' point, you could make a Dogs variant where the Buddhist "Dogs" go around visiting towns in trouble and helping them, but without actually gunning anyone down. Maybe the escalation ladder would include acts of severe self-sacrifice and selflessness at the risk of one's own life.
