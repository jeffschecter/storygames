    \n ACTION CASTLE is a game by Jared Sorenson that simulates an old text adventure game. Players take turns giving the GM (the computer) commands and the GM describes the game as it goes.

    YOU ARE STANDING IN A COTTAGE. THERE IS A FISHING POLE HERE. EXITS ARE OUT.

    Player 1: go out
    GM: The door is closed!
    Player 2: Open door.
    GM: The door is open.

    Etc. etc.

    It's a hilarious game and a lot of fun as an experiment in player-player and player-gm feedback and cooperation. It's especially fun at cons or in large groups of nerds as you get chains of cooperation and occasional griefers trying to "break" the game.

    \n Three things:

    It's SorensEn. With an E. Don't lump me in with those Norwegians!

    Parsely games must be played live. Otherwise, what's the point?

    The game appears to be bulletproof, provided the scenario is playtested and committed to memory (but you can cheat with crib notes and a map and it'll work fine). I've run it dozens and dozens of times with anywhere from 5 to 50+ players and it's always fun. An interesting note: it doesn't get boring at all for me. I'm continually surprised by the reactions and responses from players during the game. Partially because as live entertainment, the environment definitely plays a part.

    Posted By: RemiOr is the expectation that we will extrapolate from existing games and make our own in BASIC?
    When I was 7 or 8, on vacation, I played Zork on my grandfather's PC and decided that I could apply my rad C64 BASIC skills to write a game like that when I got back to school. I hardcoded a game with a few rooms, then realized that it was really repetitive, and making changes was hard. So I wrote a code generator which took a sort of awful, promiscuously-connected state diagram as an input, and output a badly-written, hardcoded BASIC program that embodied the game described by the state diagram, with a switch statement containing all possible verbs and nouns for each room.

    Arguably, this was not the best way to do it. I've never really considered trying again in the intervening 24 years. But all of a sudden, between "The Smoke Dream" and this, I'm getting an urge...

    \n I was thinking about doing it in a more Smoke Dreamy way, honestly. Embody the game state in a macroscale physical artifact. Use the player's imagination to generate a fiction richer than the output of the usual small FSM, based on an sequence of prompts that evolves based on the player's choices.

    The awesome thing about Action Castle, as I'm imagining it, isn't the nostalgia. It's that I, the player, wanting something new, can say "TALK TO BEAUTIFUL WOMAN" to the GM, and he can decide to honor my contribution even if that was just a color element (or if I introduced it de novo). Or he can be boring and say "There is no beautiful woman here," but whatever. She can join me as a companion - we didn't need to have pre-existing logic to handle that in the engine. Our differences and shared interests can become part of the story now, in addition to (or in place of!) the saga of my delving into the dungeon. Fun!
