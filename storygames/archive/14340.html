\n I said that i was going to write a new game every two months for 2011. I started out doing that pretty well. But then i got stuck on the second game: Ammo. For reasons beyond my control, and to honor the Queen, i've been stuck in a downward spiral of game design that can't seem to escape 'hacking' my own stupid game.

But, whatever.

Instead of 'revising' and 'fixing' my existing game, i've decided to just write 'sequels.' I'm not going to say i'm 'happy' with this version, just that i'm done learn'n from it. So i timidly offer to you my sequel to Ammo: Revenant War. This one's called Amm02: Deus Vs. Machina. Unexpectedly, and out of sheer curiosity, i'm actually asking you to take this seriously.

Seriously, i really am.

If you dig, please write me back here or ask for my email. If you hate the game please explain why. If you have any advice or stuff you'd like to add (especially ideas for cool Ammo) do all of the above.

Game is available here: a Link With more at metagate.wikidot.com/ammorpg.

    RPH [Regis Philban Hex] Rounds

    (+) - "Phone a Friend" - on a Triple Hit you can ask one other player next to you what order your shots should have been fired in. Re-arrange them to fit that.
    (0) - "Fifty Fifty" - On a Triple Miss you can use the SFX of a Triple Hit or Triple Ricochet, your choice.
    (-) - "Ask the Audience" - On a Triple Ricochet you can ask one other person nearby who is not playing the game if you win this fight or not. Whatever they answer you have to agree with and the results of the fight are finalized right then.

    \n Parsimonious Rounds

    (+) - "They want more bullets" On a triple hit, the target becomes ecstatic with the riddle of lead they're experiencing, and either force you to continue shooting them, or they begin to shoot themselves. They take double the damage you rolled.

    (0) - "They want more time with their family" On a triple miss, you've got to take them back to their house for them to spend a little time with their loved ones before you continue fighting. Really, is that so greedy?

    (-) - "They want more drink tickets" On a triple ricochet, you are made to feel sooooo guilty that you give over the three things that make your character cool to the enemy. This might be your hairstyle, maybe one of your guns, could be your backstory as an itinerant farm worker, the name that you picked for your ex boyfriend (even though he hasn't been introduced to the game yet.) Whatever. When you kill them, you can take the cool thing back, just like drink tickets.
