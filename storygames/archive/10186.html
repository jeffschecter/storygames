    Posted By: Saint&SinnerMy understanding is Dread is evocative but crunchy
    \nOh heck no. Dread is pretty much the opposite of crunchy. Check out this page, and look at the Pregen Characters to see how crunchy they are:

    http://www.neoplasticpress.com/dread-downloads.html

    There's a lot of what they call in the Japanese RPG world "Data": That is, there are lists of cool powers, spells, and special effects (of which you choose a small number, and write down what they do). But in terms of crunch, it basically comes down to this:

    Player: I try to track where the girl might have gone. My sense is 2.
    GM: That's two dice. Are you sure you don't want to do it "cool"?
    Player: Hmmmm. OK, Hammerwitch looks around the deserted motel room, and sees a few threads of clothing on the floor. She picks it up, and casually glances around the room; from door, to window, to the bathroom. Wait... the bathroom. She goes into the bathroom and notices that the window to the alley out back is cracked open, and is roughly... yeah... child sized. Holding the threads up to the window, she sees a few more threads hanging from the jam of the window. Hammerwitch kicks the door of the room open and runs around to the alley in the back, certain that this is the start of the trail...
    GM: Awesome. Take a Cool Die. So 3 dice total. Any spells you have that might help you?
    Player: Nope, all my stuff is combat-focused. What's the difficulty?
    GM: 7
    Player: (rolls 3d12; 1, 5, and 8): Got an 8.
    GM: Alright. A normal person would have no idea where the girl would have run off to, but you're no ordinary person. You survey the alley and, knowing the girl, you know it's likely that she went to the abandoned warehouse across the street to find a hole to crawl in. You've got her- It's just a matter of time.

    ...and that's about it. Roll some d12s, aim for target number. Just a few skills and stuff, no lists of modifiers, and wacky gruesome spells.

    -Andy

    Posted By: Saint&SinnerPosted By: Josh RobyFull Light, Full Steam is available insoftcoverandhardcoveron Lulu, as well.
    \nSomeone just recently recommended that based on something in the mechanics. I'm not that interested in the background though. Could you tell me more or point me tword an explanation of the mechanics so I can remember what this person thought was cool?

    The two core things about FLFS that make it really cool:

    The little one: The Aspects-like use of your character's thematic batteries rewards you for your life difficult early in the episode by letting you kick ass in the climax.

    The big one: The scrip-based use of thematic batteries rewards you for making the other protagonists relevant to the fiction. I've never seen a set of game procedures* do a better job of facilitating in-character banter and sharing of spotlight.

    FLFS deserves more attention than it gets. It does have some issues, but it also does some really neat things with player group dynamics that no other game does, and does them well.

    (*I hesitate to call them 'mechanics'. They are mechanics, but that word is too loaded with connotations of conflict resolution and fighty bits)
