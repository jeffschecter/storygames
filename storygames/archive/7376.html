    \n I've been thinking, with Dread, of having the players create a little pieced together notebook of information about the demons they are hunting. Since none of the creatures are familiar with any players I know, I think it would end up a cool little artifact; a book with sticky notes and information in different handwriting about all kinds of different demons that the players can also use as a reference throughout the game. Sort of like the dad's notebook in Supernatural of collected facts and information.

    It's not an artifact that you have to create while playing Dread, but I think it would be utterly cool and a natural part of play.

    \n Shreyas and I are working on a two-player game called The Salted Road. It's a travelogue game, where all of the places and people the Hero meet are created by the World out of sensory impressions from the person playing the Hero's life-- ie, the Hero's home could be made from the player's first love cobbled together with the house they grew up in. The problems in the places that the Hero travels to mirror problems that the Hero's player are having in real life, etc. All of these things from the Hero's life are created by drawing cards and answering the questions on them.

    Before you ever play The Salted Road, you need to get a journal-- choose it yourself. At the beginning of every game, the Hero and the World trade journals. The World records the answers to the Hero's questions, and their reactions to what happens; the Hero records their thoughts and feelings about what the World does during the game. At the end of the game, you get your journal back, filled with what the other person has learned about who you are.
