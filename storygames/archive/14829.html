    \n Credentials huh? Well, I advertise my services as science tutor through some tutoring websites so let's see how that experience translates.

    First off is Proof of Subject Knowledge. So there is no GMing degree or official certification, so you'll have to go by resume and experiences. I would want to see that the tutor has been playing the specific game or in the genre for years. I would want to see evidence of playing with different groups of people, preferably at cons or gaming clubs and the like. Testimonials from people the tutor has gamed would have some value.

    The company I tutor through has a policy of offering a refund on the first session if things just don't work out between the tutor and the student. I would think a game tutor would need to do the same because you really don't know if you can be taught by a person until he tries to teach you something.

    \n Get five referrals from no fewer than three different (ie more than 50% different composition) groups. When you call the referrals, ask them about their experiences with new fewer than three different game systems.

    If you have a game you want specific tutoring in, obviously require that at least one of your referrals has played with you in that game.

    If you have a specific technique you want polished up, you'd want to ask about that. Did the prospect work with funny voices and props? Did the prospect actively and creatively incorporate player input?
