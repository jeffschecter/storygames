    \n\niago \n\n\n
    \n
    \n\nJuly 2007 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n This is a great example of how building a fan army is a vital marketing function as an indie publisher.

    Burning Empires is a fantastic product -- it had to be, to get on the nomination list. But the Origins voting process was heavily stacked against it -- votes were all cast by "consumer" attendees of Origins.

    So Luke and company got their army in by dropship and went to town, getting out the vote, getting a little bit of an assist on that from the crew staffing the IPR booth as well, beat the streets, and encouraged fans of the game to put their votes in.

    It worked.

    Best of all, when Paul Tevis and I brought the Origins award and news over to Luke, it was because he had missed the awards ceremony... because he was running Burning Wheel.

    The room exploded.
