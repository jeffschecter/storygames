    Looks solidly PG to me, but I find the movie-rating system in question kind of unclear when applied to playsets -- the vast majority of what separates a PG movie from an R counterpart is the details of what is shown on camera, what dialogue is like, etc. Basically, stuff that is way smaller-scope than what is outlined in the playset, or for that matter most playsets I'm familiar with. Unless they include things like 'a visible penis!' or 'somebody swearing in an explicit manner, repeatedly', it's really going to be all in the delivery.

    There are lots of PG ways to treat a 'sex tape' object, but even a 'one night stand' relationship could easily be portrayed in a way that would garner an R rating. These things seem largely under control of the playgroup, making a playset rating seem like a sort of red herring.

    Also: looks like a cool playset!

    \nMPAA Ratings System is what I work off of, as (until recently) Fiasco had a mostly US audience. Lacking an international standard, and given that Bully Pulpit Games operates in the US and would be subject to legal action in the US, I think we'll stick with its standards.

    And I quote:\n
    PG: There may be some profanity and some depictions of violence or brief nudity. \u2026 There is no drug use content in a PG-rated motion picture.

    [Holy SHOT, this next one's a legal minefield!]
    PG-13: A PG-13 motion picture may go beyond the PG rating in theme, violence, nudity, sensuality, language, adult activities or other elements, but does not reach the restricted R category. \u2026 Any drug use will initially require at least a PG-13 rating. More than brief nudity will require at least a PG-13 rating, but such nudity in a PG-13 rated motion picture generally will not be sexually oriented. There may be depictions of violence in a PG-13 movie, but generally not both realistic and extreme or persistent violence. A motion picture\u2019s single use of one of the harsher sexually-derived words, though only as an expletive, initially requires at least a PG-13 rating. More than one such expletive requires an R rating, as must even one of those words used in a sexual context.

    R: An R-rated motion picture may include adult themes, adult activity, hard language, intense or persistent violence, sexually-oriented nudity, drug abuse or other elements\u2026
    \nSo that's your lens: if the playset text itself contains any of that stuff in the last paragraph, it's definitely R. Lacking any of that, it could be PG or G. Given the difficulty of drawing a line at the "PG-13" stage--the verbiage is even longer than I quoted, including some kind of subjective, 2/3s vote shit!--we can't be bothered to have that distinction.

    SO... if it stops at the level of the first paragraph above, it's PG (or G!). If it goes into the range of language and suggestiveness of the second and third paragraphs, it's arbitrarily R. Err on the side of protecting the little bastards.

    (Note that there are no G playsets at BPG wiki, yet! Get to it, you parental types!)\n
    G: A G-rated motion picture contains nothing in theme, language, nudity, sex, violence or other matters\u2026. Some snippets of language may go beyond polite conversation but they are common everyday expressions. No stronger words are present in G-rated motion pictures. Depictions of violence are minimal. No nudity, sex scenes or drug use are present in the motion picture.
