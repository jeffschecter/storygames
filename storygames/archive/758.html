  • Andy

    Very cool ideas all around. Some ideas, though, if one were to Official-ize it and all:

    1) I'd totally do the random groups thing. Choosing groups would be what you do when you own an RPG company and want to publish with friends by yourselves. That would lead to some folks getting excluded, or having far more hangups that it wouldn't even be challenging anymore (ie "4 dudes that hang out together in New England" vs "4 dudes that are scattered across Australia, Asia, Europe and America and have never worked together before". The latter team would have so many hangups it wouldn't be fair anymore).

    That sounds like a great idea, though. Teams would be cool. Take in teams, add more overall time to the contest, make the requirements/work constraints steeper (ie have a "min page requirement" or equivalent), and "selectively randomize" the groups: That is be cognizant of where people are located, and try to group them together by region when possible, otherwise split them up fairly ("Everyone gets an Australian/New Zelander", etc) so that one team doesn't have a huge aount of local leverage on another.

    Running with the original idea, I'd say that for this "team competition", I'd break the timelimit out of its normal shell to an extra week or two, and yes allow up to 4-5 people per team. Consider it a grand experiment before the competition returns to its normal 1 week 1 man gig.

    That would be the plan if, say, people wanted it to be an official Game-Chef thing and all. If someone just wants to host such a contest somewhere else by themselves, though, they're totally welcome to do that as well (I'm not sure if I'd have the time to participate myself in creation, though, though I might be able to help judge).

    -Andy
