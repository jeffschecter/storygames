I get to start GMing here shortly. What indie RPG should I take a look at, that we haven't played? High story, low/medium number crunching, run it for about six sessions before we decide whether to keep going/try something else.

...And if you know a good system to try to do something Army-of-Darkness-ish with, let me know.
\nBackground: she's played BW with me, and Mouseguard. Also IAWA, though not Dogs. Roach. Mountain Witch. SotC. Stuff like that. Her family tends to like stuff that's a little more trad in how it expresses characters.

I have a 7 month old, so I basically haven't played anything but Pilgrims of the Flying Temple and Castle Ravenloft in the last (feels like a) year. Therefore, I can't knowledgeably recommend stuff like Apocalypse World or Dungeon World or whatever the current hotness of trad-like indie gaming is.

Also, because of that, if you make a recommendation, please briefly explain why it might meet her needs, so that I can believably steal your work and present it as my own.

Many thanks.

    \n When I think of a game to run an Army of Darkness game in, I think of Savage Worlds. It lets you do simple tactical combat with 30 skeletons on the table and finish it in about 40 minutes, which is dang fast for a semi-mass combat.

    I think SW does a great job of giving you trad-style characterization-driven play and fast tactical combat without feeling slow and dumb and burdened by class/level nonsense.

    And it consists of a very lightweight and straightforward framework that is super easy to hack if any of the elements don't fit well with what you want the game to do. You can easily take things that you like from other good games and blend them in.

    For example, I liked the "roll stat+job and take the highest" mechanic from Leverage, so I replaced the normal "stats with skills under them" thing from SW with a Stat+Job grid (which also replaces the wild die). And so far, it's worked out extremely well.

    I've also taken some of the "choose 1" partial success concepts from Apocalypse World as ways to tweak some of the spells to have the flavor I want. The success and raise mechanics in SW work out similar enough to AW that the concepts are pretty interchangeable. And, of course, you can totally prep your threats the way AW fronts work.

    Of course, this recommendation is really only valid if you feel like having tactical combat. Which, to me, would be fun for an Army of Darkness game. But there are totally different ways to approach it. And if the idea of 30 skeleton miniatures on the table is not what you're getting at, then totally skip this recommendation. But if you do want to have a fun, lightweight, large tactical combat that doesn't grind the game to a halt for 3 hours, then Savage Worlds is a great way to do that.
