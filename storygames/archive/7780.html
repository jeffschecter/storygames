\n I'm looking at putting Play Unsafe on IPR. If you've published something through IPR, could you advise me on how many I should print, as a first batch, and who with?

Play Unsafe has sold well (350 copies) and still sells at a steady trickle (say, seven per month). IPR will attend conventions where I won't be present, too.

I gather the minimum number of copies is 30, but I'm wondering if I should go higher than that.

Any advice appreciated.

Graham

    \n The market may have changed some since I was selling Push, but I suggest starting with 40-50 copies, seeing how they do, and going from there. I don't think you'll have a hard time moving that many over the course of a year (you might do it a lot faster), and having to reorder things or having your game out of stock sucks. I wouldn't do 100 copies, say, until you get a sense of how fast they're moving. I sent batches of Push to IPR in 50-copy batches and sold through two of them before switching to my new publishing plan. Not sure if the tanking American/world economy should lead you to be more cautious or not.

    \n I think you'll get a number of orders from game shops now that you've sold a lot directly yourself. Certainly that's been my experience on BoxNinja books.

    So, from my POV you need to do two things: have enough books to last six months, and get the unit cost down as much as possible (since you'll get less money on a retail sale).

    If shops in the US order even semi-steadily then 30 copies will mean you re-order books and have to send them to the warehouse in only a few months from now. I'd say send 50 and see how it goes -- it could be 6 months worth. If it does better than that then you can adjust accordingly.

    If you hadn't sold so many already I'd say you could send more than that. But a short run of 50 copies from a digital printer and only one shipping bill is better than smaller runs and multiple shipping bills.

    And will you have a PDF on there? It's additional income in my experience and I've not noticed it killing print sales for me.
