    \n Grindhouse Dice:
    The starting Plot Points (one per player) are distributed as dice into the four Grindhouse Pools (SEXY, VIOLENT, RUDE and COOL). You must place at least one of the Grindhouse dice into Sexy, Violent and Rude pools. If you only have 2 players, you can choose either sexy or violent.

    Players may grab dice from the appropiate pool to spend on suitably sexy, violent, rude or cool actions.

    Instead of getting Plot Points during the course of play, dice are added to the Grindhouse. The game may end only when the required amount of Grindhouse Dice are accrued. Those dice are then channeled into the Grindhouse Pools for the next game.

    Suggested # of dice needed? Start out at least 5 per player (so 15-??).

    \n Woot! I saw Grindhouse this afternoon. After all, nothing says "Easter" better than a Rodriguez-Tarantino double-feature. I had a great time! It is most definitely octaNe material. Of the two movies, Rodriguez' offering (Planet Terror) was definitely the better. For one thing, it was more truly grindhouse material. Tarantino's Death Proof was fun and had great moments, but could easily have been trimmed of at least 20 minutes of material that added nothing to the movie. Still, both were octaNe fuel!

    And if anyone is wondering about the potential for sexism: although I went in there bracing myself for the worst, I was pleasantly surprised. Sure, all the women were definitely buxom babes, but they were all player characters, know what I mean? In fact, what sexual violence there was, was mostly directed towards men. Female characters end up "on top" in both movies.

    Now I need to get that soundtrack and to run a game ASAP!

    \n Just saw Grindhouse--it was one of the most entertaining theater experiences I've had in a long time. I'm sad to see so little love for the second feature. For those that didn't appreciate Deathproof, I'd say it helps if you've seen some of the films that Tarantino is paying homage: Roger Corman films like Faster Pussycat, Kill Kill and 70s car films like Steven Spielberg's Duel. (Or maybe it was just an uneven mess of pointless dialog and meaningless violence. *shrug*)

    I have no exposure to OctaNe, but I thought I'd chime in on Grindhouse and gaming in general. I noticed a recurring device in both features (especially Planet Terror). The writers would introduce a "set-up" (i.e. add something to the fictional space of the movie that will be important later) and IMMEDIATELY go for the payoff, usually within the same scene and a scene closely following.

    To help capture the feel of the movie, you could give players points they can spend to introduce a new story element into a scene (to either help themselves or hinder another character), but it must immediately pay off. (Is that similar to how plot points work in OctaNe?) Heck, you could possibly design an entire system around that mechanic.

    Another story-telling device I noticed was escalation: as the situation gets worse, the characters kick more and more ass. (A good example is the character Wray in Planet Terror.) Not sure how you would rule-ify that off the top of my head, but I think you could draw some interesting mechanics from that device.
