Why don't we talk about personal skills?

Nir Shiffer
March 2006 edited March 2006 in Story Games
I've been hanging on the outskirts of this crowd for several years now, and there's one thing that's always bothered me - we have here an aweful lot of smart, talented people with a keen interest in creating better gaming experiences, and yet no talk of personal skills.

I don't think there's any doubt that there exists such a thing as "better" in roleplaying (at least, I don't have this doubt. Feel free to disagree). It can be said that one GM is better then another (maybe only at certain things), or that a player improved over time. The application of the term itself is considerably more difficult and drags us into all sorts of quagmires, but I hope we can agree on the following:

Everyone strives toward improvement. No one says "I'm too good". There is the exception of those who don't strive toward improvement because they don't strive at all in the area of personal skills, but I have yet to see the case of someone actively striving "to stay put".

Mechanics recieve a lot of attention, and they are only one of three factors going into creating a play session.

Why isn't there any talk about improving personal skills? Why is there no talk about better methods of description, or other means of getting information from the GM to the players or between the players? Why no talk about improvisational techniques? Of using music and other atmosphere enhancers? The list goes on.

This is not a rant, or a taunt - I'd really like to know.

And unless someone can tell me why it's a bad idea (please tell me if you think this is bad idea) I hope to start opening some threads on the subject in the next few days.
