Anybody else thinks there's something totally wrong about this entry here:
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Storytelling_game

I mean, one might get over the fact that half the time they're describing a pretty normal role-playing game and then go on to say it's pretty different from a normal role-playing game, but then it ends with this incredible statement:

"White Wolf Game Studio's Storyteller System, which is used in World of Darkness role-playing games such as Vampire: The Masquerade and live-action games under the Mind's Eye Theatre imprint, is the best-known and most popular role-playing-style storytelling game."

Something doesn't add up, at least not me. So Storytelling Games supposedely are way different from normal RPGs and are a whole subgenre... and the best know ST Game is White Wolf's ST System? What's a normal RPG then: Universalis?
