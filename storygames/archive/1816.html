Anime Verbs and Adverbs/Nouns

Elliott Belser
November 2006 edited November 2006 in Story Games
So, my game designy friends are like, "Let's make a multi-genre RPG!" And I'm like, "How the hell am I supposed to focus on everything if I have ADHD?"

Then Guardians of Order goes SPLAT ARRGH DIE.

And a "Any genre you want, as long as it's anime" game looks appealing. Cuz me and all my friends love the big eyes, the small mouths, and the laser sharks and the drama.

In light of the most awesomest Verb for a game evar.

Anime characters don't always fight. They don't always love. They don't always outwit sugar addicted detectives while flipping out and killing people by writing thier names in the Death Note.

They do, however, always Exemplify beliefs! Beliefs which I'm going to make a key part of the system, oooooh yes.

Obviously, externally, and through every act.

The problem is, for adverbs?

I got... er... Physically. Intuitively. Rationally. Socially. Violently, which comes with it's own set of problems because it's not always a constant. Obviously and Covertly, which are probably variants on the other five. Any others?

I want to follow Vincent's latest Dangerous Ideas on this one - his Dangerous Idea being to Have stats and have those stats intertwine to have your conflict arena rolls.

I'm thinking four arenas or stats, and yin (covert) and yang (OVERT) aspects to each.

Consider this a cluster of darts. I'm done rambling.

Edited to add: By anime I mean "anime and manga," mmmkay?

    Kay.

    A friend of mine who is Very Very Into Indie Games came back from college and talked with me on this design.

    He suggested you make a character Background, and from that come the beliefs or Ideals. and from those come the Stats. I added "...and from those come the Powers and Traits." Like a tree branching upward on the character sheet. Or an explosion radiating from it's center.

    The Background? You write a short Background for your character, like in Heroquest. Then you use key events in it to come up with important Ideals for your character to follow and cool Descriptors of your characters abilities. I want there to be at least two Ideals that can butt heads, and when they do, the friction creates sparks that give you new abilities when you resolve the friction. Not XP - abilities. Directly.

    He also suggested the normal categories of stats - Physical, Mental, Social, Resources - but the addition of differing levels of descriptors for them. Some very different social descriptors would be "Strange mix of Cute and Seductive" and "Air of Sophistication." Descriptors in a stat have points. They are both dice pool and hit points. Use any two descriptors - yes, they can be different categories of descriptor - in a roll. If you use them and they aren't apropos, take the dice for them. If one is apropos as determined by the players and GM, take +1 die for it. If one is PERFECT, take +2 dice for it. These bonuses stack.

    I'd like to further add traits or powers under the descriptors that add additional "appropriateness bonuses" but I'm not sure how.

    A possible character with 7 descriptors and 24 points in them.

    Taken from the Seelie Court for her protection at the order of her mother, Queen Tatiana, just before the wolves of the Unseelie Lord of Nightmare Apeiron descended on Tatiana, the sidhe child who her adoptive parents, a lesbian couple named Rose and Sakura, would name Faye lived a fairly normal human life before she began to dream of life in the Faerie Realms. Now, Apeiron grows bolder and sends his lieutenants to shape nightmare creatures to siphon the dreams of mortals.     Ideal: I can't put my parents in danger, even if I need to lie to them to protect them.
    Ideal: Who were my birthparents, why do I have inhuman powers? I must find out who I am.

    Physical: I'm an atheletic girl who can get double SS on some DDR songs, on a good day. 4/4
    Mental: I dream of what may be - so I can stop it. 4/4 My parents being lesbians have taught me the value of an open mind. 3/3
