\n I've been falling in and out of phases where I really want to hack World of Dungeons into my perfect light-weight fantasy game. But I haven't been able to run it yet so I'm not 100% sure what effects my hacks might produce. Still, I know in my soul that I want something with just a little "more" to it. Not much, just a little.

Classes aren't packages of skills or abilities now, those are open to everyone. Instead, each class gives the character a free form special ability. Fighters have Feats, Thieves have Tactics, Clerics have Edicts, Wizards have Spells. When a PC is created they select a special ability then create two aspects related to it. The difference between having a special ability with the correct Aspect is that many actions could become narrative in nature instead of requiring a roll. Also, many may open up paths in the narrative that wouldn't have been possible otherwise. Finally, an Aspect could boost the probability of a good result for specific actions.

Feats are actions of martial superiority. Based on the aspects chosen Feats might allow whirlwind attacks, instant defeat of lesser foes, arrow catching, disarming foes.

Edicts are the will of one's deity made manifest into magical effects. What can be done is highly dependent on the character's deity.

Spells are pretty much what you'd expect. They can do anything. The big difference here is that I noted in the game text that failing to cast a Spell properly can be pretty bad... as opposed to a fighter failing to whirlwind or something, he just misses or blunders. Wizards might curse themselves, the land, friends or blow stuff up or get enslaved by a demon, etc. Technically, Edicts may carry a similar burden. Though they are easily lost by behaving opposite of your deity's strictures.

Tactics are... well, this one I'm struggling with a bit. Right now Tactics allow the shadowy, social PC to seduce, persuade, sabotage, flush out, interrogate, infiltrate. Ninja/spy, skill monkey stuff.

So what do you folks think? A free form special move system isn't too crazy of an idea for World of Dungeons considering how spirits and magic work in the original. Does it sound playable? Should I come up with a concrete list of Aspects for each special ability? Are Tactics too poorly defined?
