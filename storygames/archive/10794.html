    \n I don't get the hate either...

    Perhaps it's because for me (and the players I game with) laptops have become a natural part of living, life and fun. So they're not distracting and they don't draw eyes, etc...
    I'm not saying my group couldn't play without laptops - the game is still the same game. But they only add to my groups' gaming experiences. They're a tool like the old DM notebook or reference books or CD players, etc...
    We use out laptops for:

    - character creation (from Excel spreadsheet character builders to WotC's DDI)
    - players chronicling game events
    - playlist for great musics during game
    - instant information access (google, wikipedia, etc...)
    - instant imagery (from stored files to quick google-image searches)
    - PDF access/search
    - online rule search (WotC's Compendium, SRD, etc...)
    - calculators for experience points, etc...
    - quick printed props on the fly (copy/paste info or pix into a Word Doc and print without skipping a beat)
    - Wiki, Wiki, Wiki (I create a new Wiki Campaign Bible for EVERY campaign I run, for myself and players to add to - unbelievably better than any other resource I use)
    - "blue-booking" via wiki (this happens between games but it's handy to reference during)
    - video skype with distant players (only done this 3-4 times and wouldn't want to deal regularly but it works amazingly well)


    I don't like the idea of using a laptop as a GM Screen though... I'm not much a Screen guy anyway - but I can't imagine the keyboard taking up space in front of me. I usually have my laptop off to the side.
    I also know the limits of laptops and how searching for that "perfect song" CAN be a game hazard. So I usually consciously relegate my searches for info to a similar amount of time I'd search a book. Less than 1-2 minutes. Otherwise it IS a distraction - but NOT for the sake of it being a laptop alone... It's just taking up my time like any other source/tool that could be better spent getting back into game and going without the "perfect" thing.
