    \n Each player is given three to five cards, which read as follows:

    Side 1:

    We're gonna have a larp, and we're going to construct characters and relationships as we go. First, name your character and describe them as an adjective phrase then a noun phrase ("Curious bookseller" or "alcoholic sheriff" or "reckless catburglar". You are all guests at a high end charity fundraiser, sponsored by the mysterious Tithonus Society, so pick something cool or mysterious or appropriate for a fancy party. Write this info on your name badge (provided).

    When you encounter another character, you can decide what your relationship with them is. Write a one or two word description of the relationship. Then give them the card, and roleplay out meeting at the party.

    Each relationship card has six boxes. Each time you encounter the other person in the relationship, you have the option to escalate the relationship. "Escalate" in this case means "make more intense and dramatic". If you escalate, roleplay out a scene where the relationship becomes more prominent and more intense. More likely to spur you or the other player onto action. Then write a sentence detailing that escalation on the other side of the card, and give it to your relationship partner. Eventually, every relationship gets out of hand.

    If there are any conflicts, each player reveals their relationship card most relevant to the conflict. Whichever has the most checked boxes wins that conflict.


    Side 2:


    Character 1: ______________
    Character 2: ______________

    Hello, we have a relationship together! Each time you escalate this relationship, check off a box and write a detail about the relationship. Then give the card to your relationship partner. The ball is in their court, now.

    [ ] Base Relationship: _____________________
    [ ] Feelings intensify: ______________________
    [ ] A Dramatic Turn: _______________________
    [ ] A Secret is Revealed: ___________________
    [ ] Things become tense: ___________________
    [ ] Violent action ensues: ___________________



    (Actually, this might be a good system to frame as an amnesiac storyline: everyone awakens inside a strange place, with no memories at all. And the cards work as flashbacks.)

    \n There is a lot of supporting text to go with this, but it probably breaks the rules if you can't judge the entry by the card alone. The main idea is to keep the action focussed in one spot so spectators can enjoy it too. The "fey chorus" form a seperate group who pass comment from the sidelines and jolly things along. Conflict resolution is by audience approval.

    side 1
    Secret Garden\tSummary
    You are playing a part in a story. Whoever gave you this card is too. Decide with them who you and why you don\u2019t get along.
    The main drama occurs in the garden.
    You make assignations with players to meet in there because it is so secret. Let the head gardener know so he can tell you when it is free. Be prompt but be prepared to wait.
    If anything too dramatic goes on out here take it into the garden.
    For supporting cast, ask in the gallery.
    In the bar, in the gallery or outside of this venue everyone is out of character.

    side 2
    Secret Garden\tSummery
    The Garden is a magic place of secret assignations. Speak clearly and freely while you are here.
    Face the gallery. Step forward to speak your innermost thoughts.
    The fey are curious to know who you are and why you are here. Tell them.
    Tell them what is on your mind.
    They are fickle and quick to judge and you want them on your side.
    None here can harm you so long as the fey are on your side.

    I am still not sure about where's good for hosting documents. If someone gives me a little help I should be able to give you the fully formatted version. (two cards plus a four page set of instructions, surely that breaks the rules!)

    Andy (Not from America and nothing to do with Plague Monkeys)

    \n Here's my game, on the two sides of a playing card.

    __________________________________
    THE GRIEVE
    - a role-playing game for 7-9 persons
    Prep: tidy up your living room, white flowers
    and cloth, and coffee and cakes on the table,
    black clothes on you. You are the Dolores-
    family. The elder son; Edward (Eddie) died
    last week (25 years). He hanged himself.

    This game is set in your living room at his
    funeral wake. The suicide was a complete
    surprise to all of you, bar the siblings. The
    siblings are the ones who may start to talk
    of why he did it ...

    Start silent, with bowed heads. Stay in
    character through the whole game. What you
    say and do in the game, is your character.
    Set a timer for 3 hours. When it rings the
    game is over.

    Shake hands with all the players before and
    after the game.
    ___________________________________
    THE CHARACTERS
    - read all characters aloud, before chosing one
    of your own gender. You may change gender
    and name on characters to fit the players.

    Edward Sr (53): strict father, surgeon, no tears
    Melanie (47): bleak mother, housewife, tearful
    John (23): brother, bus-driver, married to Bea
    Elaine (19): sister, loved Eddie, writes poetry
    Grandpa (86): strict, former surgeon, no tears
    - Francine (25): shocked girlfriend of E. nurse
    Uncle Gazza (55): sad, drunk, unemployed
    Aunt Rose (50): sturdy, single, helps Melanie
    - Bea (23): secretary, lively, married to John

    NOTES
    - Edward Sr. is a chip of the old block:
    Grandpa; they are both strong and dominating
    - Melanie may foster a resentment towards
    her husband for having no time for his family.
    - Uncle Gazza has a really troubled relation-
    ship to his brother Edward Sr.
    - Francine was in love with Eddie, and may
    blame herself for not seeing his despair.
    ____________________________________

    To me, the point of a live action role-playing game (larp) is to set up a situation that may fuel interaction, and then to lean back and see the players explore it, turn it into something they care for, and evoke the drama of their characters. I hope this little game may be played like that.

    I've written a game I would find interesting myself, and leave it to you to use it, or not. Any comments are appreciated.

    \n I've played 13 til bords, and had a blast. I played the married son of the grandfather who hosted the celebration, and made an early speech were I accused him of abusing his children. The celebration went rapidly downhill from that point on.

    Contradictions are rare, but if they occur you have to make find out who lied, or who were confused. Or you stop play, and edit. Not a big problem though. I can't recall encountering it at all.

    Of course you may say things about other characters; that's one of the great challenges in such a game; to have someone come up to you, and start playing on something you've done, or something you share.

    EXAMPLE
    A woman may insinuate in conversation that you and she have an affair, with both being married (and you believed, up until now, that your marriage in this game was sound and happy). It's an exciting challenge, so how do you meet it? You play along, of course, and explore the affair, either as a willing participant, or as a man trying to entangle himself from this bad (but lustful) connection.

    SUCH A SCENE MAY PLAY OUT LIKE THIS:

    She: Hello Mark! (placing a hand on his arm)
    He: Hello! (smiling)
    She (whispers): Thanks for yesternight!
    He: Oh! Uh ... thanks!
    She: What do you think of the party?
    He: Oh, it's nice.
    She (whispers): Meet me under the apple-tree.
    She: Must mingle!
    He (whispers): I'll be there.
    - you split up, and meet under the apple-tree later ...

    OR LIKE THIS:

    She: Hello Mark! (placing a hand on his arm)
    He: Hello! (smiling)
    She (whispers): Thanks for yesternight!
    He: Oh! Uh ... ja, yesternight ... I would like to talk to you about that.
    She: What do you think of the party?
    He: Oh, it's nice.
    She (whispers): Meet me under the apple-tree.
    She: Must mingle!
    He (whispers): No, not here! Not this night. I'll see you at lunch tomorrow.
    She (pouting, whispers): Why? Don't you like the thrill any more?
    He (whispers): I can't talk now. Please. We'll talk tomorrow.
    She (whispers): Well ... I could talk to your wife, you know, and tell her something ...
    He (whispers): You would not dare!
    The host: Hey! Whispering tantalizing secrets? Let me in on it!
    - you seizes the opportunity, and leave her with the host.

    Having such challenges thrown at you is a thrill. It makes the game as unpredictable as life itself. ;-)
