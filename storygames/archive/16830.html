    \n I usually do something to help threaten one of the traits. Sometimes I'll just have the villain make weird threats, like delivering an ultimatum on TV or from the top of an imperiled civic monument. Or I'll try and capture a PC during an early fight (when I've got all the advantages) so I can gloat at them about my evil schemes. As the game progresses, I just pick a fights more and more and don't bother with enrichment, especially in one-shots and demo games at cons.

    But if you're trying to develop some depth of to your villains, I also recommend flashbacks, where we see the villain in brighter days and vignettes of normal people dealing with the villain's deprivations. I'll also do scenes where NPCs from players' threatened aspects worry and fret or otherwise react to the dangers and woes created by the villain's scheme (a technique I got, admittedly, from My Life with Master.)

    Another fun scene to do as an enrichment is to try and woo away one of those NPCs, like Octavius putting the moves on Aunt May.
