    \n As Cneph has linked to me saying previously, you can pick that option if only one person has a String.

    The text doesn't indicate one way or another whether you can pick it if no Strings will be lost. A similar case is whether or not you can pick the "lose all Strings on everybody" option for averting death if you've got no Strings at all.

    Feel free to interpret either way at your own table, just so long as you're consistent as a group. I'd recommend that if none of the relevant parties have any Strings, you can't pick an option that tells you to lose Strings.

    \n I play it as "if either person has a string you can pick that option, but if neither of you has a string you can't". For harm reset, I play it that if you have no strings, you can't pick "give up all strings" to reset harm, and if you're already your darkest self, you can't reset harm by becoming your darkest self. There are other valid interpretations, but I like those. I like the suggestion above about thinking of it as "you can't choose an option that doesn't change anything." It's all about change.

    Also, if you have no strings on ANYONE, you might as well die. In a pit. No one will care anyway.
