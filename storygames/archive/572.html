The Roleplayer's Toolkit

Christian_Griffen
April 2006 edited April 2006 in Play Advice
As I posted on RPGTalk, I'm thinking about creating a Roleplayer's Toolkit document that would collect the coolest techniques and tools developed in recent years in one place, in an easily accessible format.

My question is this: which tools and techniques do you think can be used for any type of game, independent of the rules used? For example, relationship maps can be useful for D&D games, freeform chat games, and even LARPs. Some will be more use for one style of play than others, but I'd still like to collect them for people to check them all out.

My short list, to kick this off, would be:
- Kickers
- Bangs
- Conflict Webs/Conflict Creation/Relationship Maps
- Flags (in various forms)
- Opening/Closing

I know that all the information on these things is out there, but it's spread out over books, threads, articles, and blog entries, and not easily accessible at all. The idea is not only to introduce the idea, but to explain it in simple terms and give lots of examples, also on how to use various tools in conjunction with each other.

So, any additions? I'm sure there's lots more.

    I was just throwing things out there, but yeah, how to organize stuff is going to be an issue. I've used traits as explicit flags, for example (with a rating not for efficiency but for how much the player wanted to use that trait in the game), whereas kickers are more specific. Like Josh said.

    As for Opening/Closing, I meant delineating the play time and space; see Polaris or Beast Hunters. I think that that's a technique that many people use implicitly ("Okay, we can bullshit for a while, but at exactly 5:00 we'll totally switch to in-game mode") but that can be much more stressed. It can also help to set the mood, if done right.
