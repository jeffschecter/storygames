\n Since History is a story, I was looking for suggestions for some History Tabletop Roleplaying games. I have seen historical supplements, add-ons, expansions and settings for non-historical RPG's, but what few I have seen all seem to be rather complex, hard-to-find, expensive or all three. I haven't seen any Roleplaying game *systems* specifically designed from the ground up to be historical- complete with their corresponding supplements. However, I am interested in both historical and non-historical systems with their corresponding historical supplements. There also seems to be some systems that are quasi or pseudo-historical in nature (Ars Magica comes to mind), however, I am especially interested in simpler, easy-to-obtain, inexpensive materials that are more historically accurate and factual.

Thank you

    Grey Ranks
    \nThis. A lesson in historical game design if there ever was one. It's a game about the Polish Resistance in the 1940s, as seen through the eyes of teenagers working with the partisans in Warsaw.

    Spione by Ron Edwards is about historical espionage in Berlin during the Cold War, while the upcoming Shahida is about the civil war in Lebanon.

    The Drifter's Escape by Ben Lehman is historical, inasmuch as the adventures of the titular drifter happen in real-world USA from the Civil War onwards.

    In a similar vein, Steal Away Jordan by Julia B. Ellingboe is about the history (and folk narratives) of slaves in the USA.

    Vincent Baker's Poison'd is pretty much about the real and historical pirates of the Caribbean sea.

    Gregor Vuga's Sagas of the Icelanders is about 10th Century Iceland.

    There are more\u2026
