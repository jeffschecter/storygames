\n Hi all,
Here in London there appears to be a lack of any unified gaming community and finding games or groups seems to be a very haphazard approach. I know this tends to be the norm with either fairly closed gaming groups meeting at private premises, or clubs that meet in more public venues, however there doesn't appear to be a community as such. This has seemed very strange to me for a variety of reasons, especially in the modern e-society, and has led me to NerdNYC and its affiliates in San Fransisco. The growing Nerd community, which caters to all gamers within a specific city seems an exciting and vibrant means of getting a larger group of disparate gamers together to facilitate gaming, as well as creating a sense of community via meet-ups and outings. I know there exists a multitude of gaming related forums and communities, but none seem to cater both across gaming genres nor dedicated to those gamers in London.

I'm trying to contact the NerdNYC creators with the proposal to spread the Nerd name across the pond to London...NerdLDN as it were. We have some excellent and very keen people on board and would like to discuss things with the 'old hands' and get permission to use their name. I've registered with NerdSF and have tried registering with NerdNYC, and while those at NerdSF have been very welcoming, I think having contact with the originators would be a real benefit.

Any help would be greatly appreciated and I can be directly contacted at johndavidanderson(at)hotmail.com
John

    \n To get this thread back to something salar can use, here's some advice I've been given:

    * Let it be okay to start out small. If your first event only has 5 people, cheer for those five people -- they're your foundation.
    * Think about those folks who are "sure things", and ask each of them to bring one person. See if those folks can bring one person. Let social networking be your friend.
    * Movies and the like are a good way to do some neutral gathering, give people a chance to meet & do something they were probably going to do already. Not every gathering has to involve a four-hour game slot.

    \n Guys, many thanks for the encouraging words. I've directed my co-conspirators to this thread as well in order to get their views. Hopefully they'll chime in as well. I don't see NerdLDN as a 'gaming club' per se, more a growing community of London clubs bringing all those disparate elements closer together, and therefore stronger. London is an exciting place to be gaming-wise with numerous conventions both large and small as well as numerous clubs and private groups. I would like to see NerdLDN as a one-stop shop complimenting all those elements, rather than stepping on anyone's toes. It could also act as unified source of information and advice for new nerds to help provide information about gaming clubs, conventions and like-minded groups within easy reach. At the moment it seems like there is a lot of information out there on the web regarding gaming, but for a new Nerd it can be both daunting and overwhelming as to where to start. All those who've provided contact details, many thanks, and I'll definitely be beating at your e-door. I will also get hold of Aaron brown and John/Jenskot to get their advice and permission to use the Nerd name.
    John
