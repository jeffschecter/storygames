    \n
    \n
    \n Darkpages is a game for 2 or 3 players. One person is the GM, essentially, and plays the Imprint and the antagonist characters. The other player or players create comic book characters based on horror, crime and "dark" comics. It's all about using the core metaphors of comic characters, and playing the game really does feel like creating a comic. The resolution is all about how much pain a character can take before giving up their core principles, and the characters can effect their imprint in the same way.

    I've been playing the various playtest version for 2 years now, and damn but I like it a lot. I started reading comics because of this game, in fact.

    \n So I played a short game of the Ashcan of Darkpages at Origins. It's a two player, one GM game. The game text is interesting as it is at the same time a treatise on a type of comicbook that I'm assuming Jared loves, and a game. The game sets up a character with conflicted values. I was playing a sorceror/magician who was trying to regain the soul of his best friend which he had accidentally sold to Beelzebub. It wasn't working out too well, and by the time we were done I was in for double or nothing, which mean that my characters soul was on the line also. Jared played a kind of avenging stalker type, who used to work for a newspaper but was fired for stalking the girl he was smitten by. He had cameras everywhere. I had fun with the game. The game has a framework to provide structure, but it doesn't bind you. I'm planning to grab the ashcan as I want to give it some playing and feedback. I'm not sure how much feedback it will need though, this one I think is at the very least at the 90% criteria.
