      \n Yes, Verge in the afternoon to take the overflow from AGON.

      Verge is in late playtest, but not quite ashcan, status. It's a cyberpunk game where the players get to connect all kinds of crazy things together to create their own setting. In the last playtest, one player created a normal joe who just wanted to bang a porn chick. Another player made a CEO of Buddhism, International and tried to corner the virtual porn market to make his company richer. The third player was a kung fu master, trying to restore dignity to the Buddhist ways, since the religion had sold out and gone commercial.

      Your world will be just as crazy, will have a cyberpunk feel, and will be as much fun to create as it is to play.

      The entire game is played on "network" (a sort of giant relationship map) that you draw on a giant piece of paper, shared by all the players. Your character is one node on that map. No separate character sheets.

      \n Well I was there, so I'll go first since no one else is.

      I played in my first game of Polaris and Shock. Both went really well.

      In Polaris I played the heart of Mirak, a knight whose senator father pressured and forced him against the head of the knights. And I played the mistaken of Mizar, a knight stuck in a love triangle between a fiance possessed by a demon, and a lover whose brother he was forced to kill.

      Cool game, it really took some getting used to just saying the result up front like you have to.

      In Shock I played as an antagonist the security forces of one nation, as the protagonist tried to grab up the technology of his enemies from across a battle line, and eventually ended up going to join up with the aliens. And I played the protagonist Shiva, who ended up sabotaging her work because she did not like the consequences of replicator technology escaping to the people.

      The thing I will do differently in Shock next time is to decide on conflict scope before making a protagonist. There was definitely some tension between me and the players around me as I went for little "personal" stories, and they aimed for bigger "global" stories. But otherwise it was a lot of fun.

      \n The highest compliments to everyone (especially our grascious hosts). It was well worth the 3 hour drive! The Philly crew got home around 3am talking gaming all the way. It just warms my soul.

I played a female satyr with mad bow skills and mean Orate. Nick and I so cheated (read: played the shit out of the game with newbies at the table - shame on us). (for the uninitiated this measn that Nick earned 52 Glory times his Fate multiplier of 2 while I gained 101 glory with a fate multiplier of 1) Of course in a one shot it didn't much matter that my story was pretty much relagated to that session only. In a campaign it would have been a bright burn toward oblivion.


      Ganakagok never disappoints. I just can't seem to get the timing right on it though and I just think I run it way slower than Bill does.
