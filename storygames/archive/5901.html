\n I was thumbing through the "Alternate Settings" section at the back of the most recent incarnation of Dust Devils over the weekend and was inspired. I think.

It stirkes me that the Devil in Dust Devils could be replaced with a Secret Shame or Deadly Sin for the knights and court of Camelot. Does the otherwise noble knight give into his Lust or Pride? And how does that lead to the ultimate downfall of the kingdom?

Has there been any discussion about porting Dust Devils to the days of Arthur and Camelot? A quick Google search this morning didn't yield me any satisfactory results.

    \n Hey David,

    A campaign scenario I've been toying with for years is based on the idea of viewing the crusades through the lens of Vietnam. That is, all the knights return from the crusades with dark secrets from life holding a city in the Holy Land: surreal drug experiences, the common practice of crusaders taking young "wives" from among the local population, horrific battles, terrorist-style retaliations by locals, etc. And these dark secrets the knights all know and share and protect would be a powerfully destabilizing force working beneath the surface of knightly society.

    I'd been thinking of using the Prince Valiant rules for it. But maybe Dust Devils would be better...

    \nI was thinking about this last night when I wasn't sleeping (I'm spending too much time not sleeping lately...). So be warned: The following is firmly in the category of brainstorming.

    Why couldn't a trick-taking game replace poker? Run everything the same up to the drawing of your hand - working with your devil gets you extra cards, working against loses you cards, etc. - then, instead of comparing your best poker hands, you play a quick five card round of trick-taking.

    The character who takes the most cards wins and the harm is equal to the difference in the number of tricks taken: Winner takes 5, loser takes 0 - 5 Harm; Winner takes 4, loser takes 1 - 3 Harm; Winner takes 3, loser takes 2 - 1 Harm.

    I don't know how the probabilities would work out, but the idea still feels right by the light of morning.

    \n The more I noodle on this the more I like the idea. I am really just stuck on Traits - one option is to have the characters choose two elements of the "code of chivalry" that they are particularly aligned with.

    In my mental model, I am replacing the Devil with the choice of one of the Seven Deadly Sins, tenatively referred to as the character's Shame. Past and present remain the same - the history of recent warfare parrallels nicely and I see no need to mess with it.

    The attributes are replaced with the Tarot suits, and instead of poker hands, you have a five-card hand to take tricks with.

    I am also considering tacking on a campaign character of "Camelot" that suffers when the player characters suffer. Possibly having at the end of a session to play a hand against the number of cards equal to the total level of suffering encountered by the players during the session - I'm stick working through the specifics.

    I welcome anyone to weigh in on these thoughts, particularly around the idea of traits. I would also be interested in hearing from Matt on what he considers "fair use" of his intellectual property/game engine/etc. I don't know if this would go any further than local use, but would like to know if advance what thoughts/guidelines Matt would have on me taking the idea beyond personal use.
