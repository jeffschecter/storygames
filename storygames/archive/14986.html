\n So I have a day off from grad. school finally and feeling very arty

Several days out from Amira's Way (more a waystation then a town) a vast ruin sprawls out over the broken tundra. A near constant cold rain and wet ground make this an unpleasant place to travel. The ruin has been explored in the past and at night your people tell tales of places deep within it. These tales are as follows:

So what I am looking for is placenames in this ruin. In some of the dungeon crawl games players attach names to the areas they explore (or hope to explore). I would like a list of these. An example from Bottle City - Trove of the Metal Kings

Ideally there would be a placename followed by what is likely to be found there (think challenged based play). If you want to be all hardcore about the placenames that's great. Traditionally placenames have a culturally encoded story and/or moral lesson. The placenames here could be stories and morals not yet told but a waiting for people to stumble across them.

Themeatcially - I'd like to see a mashup of Lamentations of the Flame Princess, Vornheim, and things intruding from the Gigastructure The waypoint and the people are from an array of different cultures (i.e. not just European). There is a native culture in place.

Goal:
Throughout the weekend I'll start drawing these places on an abstracted map as they get posted. I'll take photos every so often so people can see the map progress. If you want a partial type of area like say an entrance, an area on level 2, or some part of the upper works just make a note. I'd like to run this as a game over Google+ once the map is more or less up and running.

Note:
If you are feeling up to it and want to create tables like Colbat Reach go for it!

    \nThe Teeth
    The ceiling of what was once a huge basement has collapsed, leaving only the jagged metal wreckage of what could have been support columns jutting by the hundreds out of the remains at odd angles. Some say the entrance to a subterranean temple complex can be found under the rubble, but few are willing to disturb what now nests between the rusted teeth.

    \nArbuckle's Threnody
    Many years of rain, ice and wind have unevenly eroded this large sandstone block into a gnarled network of holes. When the wind hits it just right, the air moving through the holes produces an eerily human-like tone, said to be the ghostly voice of Arbuckle, an entertainer and explorer. Legend has it that the block once supported a large obelisk, which Arbuckle removed from the ruin to reassemble on his ostentatious estate. While transporting this, the final block, a freak accident left Arbuckle decapitated, and his greedy ghost jealous of any who remove treasure from the ruin.

    \n A mirror you can walk in.
    A broken glass reflection of a other dungeon location -
    1) crystal fungus growing on walls, burgeoning gem stones, eerie music, melancholia.
    2) something inverted in your reflection, shades creeping out of cracks chasing it, chasing you, don't speak too loud.
    3) ballroom or boudoir, strange candles, sad illusions, choose to exit as yourself or somewhere else as your reflection.
