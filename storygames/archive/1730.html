Name Change for The Fifth World

Brennan_Taylor
November 2006 edited November 2006 in Play Advice
So, I've been working (for a shamefully long time) on a game with the working title "The Fifth World." I got an e-mail today from a guy named Jason Godesky. Apparently, he's releasing a game in December also called The Fifth World, and wanted to see if I would be willing to change the name on mine. I don't want to get in a fight over the name, which is cool, but I'm not stuck on, and his game is coming out earlier than mine. I also don't want customer confusion over two games released in the same year with the same name.

So, I'll need to change the name of my Fifth World game to something else. I'm interested in ideas or feedback from anyone on Story Games about what to call it.

For those not familiar, my game is based on mythology from the American Southwest, and players portray characters in a nascent human civilization that has recently emerged from a hole in the ground into the Fifth World, currently populated by monsters. The themes of the game revolve around inside and outside behavior. Inside conduct is extremely conservative but serves to reinforce community bonds, and outside conduct is criminal and transgressive, but also necessary for ultimate success. The characters are the heroes of their people, and the goal is to become great leaders or inspirational figures without compromising yourself too much and becoming a human monster.
