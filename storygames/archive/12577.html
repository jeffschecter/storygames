    \n I tend to get rid of stuff after the campaign is done (just habit: I treat the paper at that point as garbage that can be tossed/recycled).

    However, a year or two ago Lisa and Eric P came over to game, and Lisa brought her character binder, which had her character for the game we were playing... and pretty much every character she ever played, for any game, ever. Old vampire/mage characters from the mid-90s, etc. I thought it was weird to keep that stuff for so long, but then when I thought about it, and how seeing something like that cna trigger the memories/stories about that game, I started thinking about this material like it was photographs: Snapshots in time to help you remember the fun or interesting things that happened.

    I started rethinking what I keep and what I toss. I won't keep all my game notes, but I might start holding on to character sheets and stuff. I need to find some good ways to organize this stuff.

    -Andy

    \n A lot depends on the game and the interesting things generated. I've got some characters from 1989 2E AD&D still written up somewhere. (I think because the stats were so damn good, and despite having two GM's witness him, never got to play him in a campaign.) I also have notes from all over the place. I've found maps from the early 90's, but in an effort to reduce the paper I keep and to not be crushed, I usually do go through and either compile important ideas into a file, or toss them if they aren't worth keeping.

    It's actually kind of freeing not to be trapped by old bits of paper.

    Although I've occasionally missed original sketches I thought I'd scanned and hadn't, I rarely miss game notes.

    \n I probably still have a lot of my old character sheets and whatnot; looking over at the bookshelf, I suspect that if I opened a couple of gaming books, some stuff from *coughmumble* decades ago would fall out. And there are some old notebooks and binders there which almost certainly have stuff in them. It's not so much that I'm a packrat (well, maybe a little), but more that I'm too lazy to locate and get rid of stuff like that.

    I figure the next time I move, I'll dedicate a day to looking through old papers One Last Time before stuffing them in a garbage bag and sending them on to the landfill. It should make a pleasant diversion from packing, and finally getting rid of that sort of useless crap (like old notes and papers from classes, floppy disks from old computer games, etc.) will make me feel virtuous.
