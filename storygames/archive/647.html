(1) If it's not obvious from your character's name what she can do, then it just doesn't really matter.

  • Silversteel Spellfire
  • Shintar Dead-Swift-Sword-Of-Night
  • Donobeer of Clan Fireangelus, Wielder of Ninety Psychic Thorns of Pain

(2) But a character can have many names! Including secret ones.

Donobeer is known also as: Donnie, Ravishing Rake of Nine Tomorrows.

(3) And a character may begin with no name at all - a man with no name - and reveal one over time.

    When acquiring Names of Import (such as after an Important Deed), our Hero Wars... errr... HeroQuest game would have a sequence of events like this:

    • GM: OK, so you slew the sheep-theif, by... ripping his neck open with a wood-axe?
    • One of players: Neckripper!
    • GM (not ready for expenditure of Hero Points just yet): Huh?
    • Other players (creative, brainstorming looks): [mumble] He-Who-Grinds-Axes? The Opener? The Necker? Woodchopper? Wood-cutting Bill? (Naaah, too bluesey a reference. -Wrong game, dude. -Yeah.)
    • GM (catches Power Gaming Wind, and likes it): Ah! I feel a True Name coming up.

    At which point some old non-playing character would announce the New Name aloud. Or, the player pining for a New Name would have had to roll dice to impress the coolness of the New Name on strangers she met.

    Both ways (the Empowered, True Name way - and the I-Grab-A-Name-To-See-If-It-Sticks variant) are fine story-changers.
