\n Who's up for it?
\n
\nThis goes for days when you have 3-4 hours to commit to writing, like weekends and if you're only quazi-employed. If you're working full time and still wanna write for an hour or two, 500 words is probably a safe bet. Some editing is allowed, sure, but mainly this is to get playtest drafts written so you can get on with the business of playing your game. Posting on internet forums doesn't count as part of your quota for the day, sorry.
\n
\nEvery day you make your quota, you get a gold star. Every time you get five (or ten) gold stars or when you finish your draft, you treat yourself to a semi-nice dinner or a new graphic novel or something.
\n
\nAnybody game? If so, post the rules of your pact here, for collective accountability, and start writing.
\n
\nP.S. I wrote 1200 words yesterday. Boo-yeah.

    \n So I missed a day due to mysterious excruciating back pain (my current suspect is worn out bed springs, which I "solved" by flipping the mattress over), but I got back into the swing of things with 627 words written down mostly on my lunch break at work today that nobody but myself will ever lay eyes upon. There's something liberating about making that decision. "I can just write, and don't have to worry about satisfying anybody but myself, and even satisfying myself is negotiable."

    I also discovered, if I'm writing by hand, those little folded paper books (ala the folding instructions for the ruleset for Zombie in my Pocket) are very easy, psychologically speaking, to write in. The smallness of the page makes me go "Yeah, I can totally fill this." whereas a full notebook page or an empty screen full of whitespace both just kind of sit there as a physical embodiment of writer's block.
