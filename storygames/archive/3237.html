\n I got to play two games of Contenders this weekend as pick up games at a small con in NC. And it rocked hard both times. Its a great game, even for me who doesn't like boxing, and the mechanics seem pretty darn cool.

But both sessions ended with total failure or all the boxers (Pain always greater than Hope). In the second session, I intentionally tried to work hard on my Hope to see if I could get a happy ending. But no dice (well the game doesn't use any so I figured the pun was appropriate...)

While I love the play, and the experience of the tragedy of the boxers life, it seems to me that one player who spirals down to into massive pain/no hope is essentially unbeatable. Definitely the seduction of the dark side.

So has anyone played contenders to a happy ending? for more than one player? and is it as gut wrenching as the downward spiral?

Jeff

    \n HI guys

    Cool post Jeff, thanks.

    I've had a couple of games where I've teetered on the brink and I think that's more gut wrenching than the downward spiral. But no happy endings yet. Next time, for sure.
    I reckon overall it's about a 1:3 ratio of good to bad endings. Ambivalent endings are fairly common too.
    However, this is not statistically precise. So people of story-games - post your Contenders data!

    Paul's experience highlights an important aspect of the game. There is a domino effect. Once one player has convinced himself things are hopeless he will probably turn antagonist and drag the rest of the Contenders down. Some players will turn antagonistic just for the hell of it!

    Here's some more thoughts and house rules from me on the Pain spiral matter.
    www.collective-endeavour.com/node/293

    Cheers
