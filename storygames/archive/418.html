For those complaining about the Power 19 not starting off discussions:

0) Provide us with a game text, please.
1) If your game was in its ideal state, what would be awesome about playing it?
2) How is your game, right now, failing to be awesome?
3) Why does that problem stop your game from being awesome?
4) Have you played it an it failed to be awesome, or just thinking about it know that it would?
5) Are there any other approachs that you've tried and failed?
6) Does your game not really have a problem, and you're just posting for social support or attention?

Any other additions?

yrs--
--Ben

P.S. Note that answering "yes" to the last one isn't really a problem, it just helps us provide you with exactly what you need.

    Actually, I disagree with that. When I run into an aspiring designer on the convention circuit, I start with the following question about the not-problems of the game ... the bits that should be great:

    What will your game do that's so incredibly cool that you're going crazy because you have to wait until the game is designed before you can play it and have this happen?

    If they can't answer that question then they drop off my radar in an instant ... passionless drones will waste my freakin' life trying to explain the whole system as if I care about the system. Dude ... I care about you and why you care about the system. The system itself can take a flying leap.

    But if they answer the question (and particularly if they answer it immediately, showing that they've already been doing the panting "I can't wait!" thing in their head) then I follow up with this question:

    Wow. That sounds hard.

    Yeah, yeah, it doesn't have a question mark. Bite me. It's an invitation. They invariably respond along the lines of "Damn man, it's so hard that designing it is like hitting myself in the balls with a pillowcase full of broken bricks. I need to find some way to do BLAH! and then I think the whole thing will come together, but I have absolutely no idea how to do that!"

    And then you discuss. That's my two cents on the matter.
