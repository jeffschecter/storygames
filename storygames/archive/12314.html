\n I doubt people would head halfway around the world to head to a game convention, but I'm sharing some ideas with everyone here anyway.

In 12 months time (the middle weekend in June), I'll be running a convention on an island in the middle of Sydney Harbour, in Australia.

The island is called Cockatoo Island, it's where the majority of the movie "X-Men Origins: Wolverine" was filmed; a disused naval base in the middle of the harbour which has recently been opened to the public as an event space and camping ground.

Details can be found here.

The games we'll be running are generally low-prep affairs, or at least games that don't require a dozen rule-books, elaborate maps, miniatures and other paraphernalia to be lugged onto the island (if you bring stuff like this, you'll have to carry it yourself, and the only transport to the island is a ferry service). The event will run over the course of a few days, with tents provided on the site, plenty of day time activities for those for don't want to camp on the island and a few night sessions on each of the two nights for those who want to get the most put of their experience. Still working out details like food, depending on how many people are interested in the concept.

If anyone might be interested in something a bit different, asks away on this thread, or whisper me.

    I was mostly thinking of the good old days in the 90s when there was a game convention on every long weekend.

    My wife said to me..."it's a long weekend, what game convention can we go to?"

    I had to respond "There isn't one...we'll have to organise one for next year"

    I'm not expecting this to be big, not like the 200-300 that most Sydney game conventions get (I'm only hoping for 20 to 40)...the price might rule out a few uni students anyway. It's mostly just a chance to play some games in an atmosphere a bit different to a regular home, library or community hall.
