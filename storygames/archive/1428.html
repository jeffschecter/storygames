  • Destriarch
    September 2006 edited September 2006
    I also feel a little overshadowed with the layout and art you put in Ash - that sheet always looked like one put together by someone who isn't very good at that stuff, and your game looks great.
    Don't put yourself down, the function of a character sheet is ultimately to disseminate information to the player in the most efficient manner as possible. If there's one thing I hate, it's searching through a character sheet looking for one tiny bit of information. Your sheet is clear from the start on what goes where and, although it is obviously designed with simplicity in mind anyway, nevertheless even if it contained a larger number of attributes and so on I think it would still be very quick and simple to navigate. I particularly liked the evokative borders you put around the three upper boxes. Prettiness is overrated. For example, have you ever seen the official character sheet for Deliria? It's a beautiful looking thing true enough, but it's in full colour and all of the attributes are represented by round iconic pictures. This wouldn't be a problem if those pictures clearly represented the attributes that they pertain to, but not only are they almost entirely nebulous to the uninitiated, the sheet is also in full colour with a pretty complex background. It doesn't photocopy well at all.

    I'll probably do a bit of a sheet redesign when I release DreamCatcher, mostly to thicken the lines up a bit and maybe to reduce the entire sheet to a smaller size so I can fit two on each page, but if it's ok with you I will also include the unadorned sheet as-is, as well as an overhauled version.

    Anyway, I'm glad you liked it! What I'm going to do when I expand upon it is include a more complete background on what goes on in the land of dreams and a number of agencies that the players can become a part of. That ought to provide a bit more direction. I wanted to do that with the game initially but I ran out of time so organisations like the DreamCatcher Society, along with the introductory fiction that I like to include with all of my games, will just have to wait for the final release. At least the game itself is complete, even if the background is a little hazy at present.

    Ash

  • lpsmith
    September 2006
    Yeah, with (potentially) only two voters per game, the variance can get pretty high. Some sort of standard can mitigate against this--saying "5 is average" helps, though I think people are probably shooting for this already. To expand on this a bit, we could say "1 is poor, 5 is average, 9 is great, 10 is perfection". Ideally, we'd also say what the average is *for*: all RPGs ever, or all RPGs entered into this contest? The latter would be ideal, but if you're only rating two, it's hard to do that. You'd have to at least skim a bunch of the others to get a sense of what the average would be.

    That being said, I think the typical score for the games will be 'above average', just like Lake Wobegon. But that's to be expected, I think, and not too bad.

    The other thing that can help is to rate more than 2 games. Kevin says above that this is allowable; it's only that we're required to rate two.
