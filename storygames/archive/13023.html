\n After I recovered from the sleep deprivation and mild insanity that always results from attending GenCon, it occured to me that GenCon would make a perfect playset. Think about it, tons of nerds with no sleep, lots of booze and/or caffeine, and easily-provoked nerd rage are just a fiasco waiting to happen. So I put together a playset, drafted some friends to help me test it, and after some minor tweaks I can happily say that it is ready to be released into the wild:

Some highlights from the game include:
* A fanblogger, who had his name legally changed to "Admiral Sulu", and Dave, a small time crook, kidnapped the celebrity guest of honor by drugging him and stuffing him in Admiral's van (vanity plates: NTRPRYZ). Unfortunately, during the scuffle, the celebrity guest of honor hit his head on the nightstand. So they took him to a veterinarian they knew and made her patch him up.

* Later on, Admiral and Dave went to get coffee at a diner, leaving the guest of honor drugged in the back. While they're inside, you see the van's engine start to smoke just as the celebrity guest of honor open the back of the van and wander off in a drugged stupor. Dave and Admiral don't notice anything until the van explodes, at which point Admiral freaks out and gets the cops to arrest him by showing them the photos he took on his iPhone of him and the drugged guest of honor.

* The cops release Admiral, having concluded that the photos were PhotoShopped, since they had gotten verification that the guest of honor was at the convention. Admiral raced back to the convention center to find the guest of honor, still dazed and confused, by the largest hentai booth at the convention looking at pornographic fan manga. He was still wearing his housecoat and pajamas from the night before, and had a 7-11 Big Gulp and a corn dog.

Good times.

So here for your Fiasco enjoyment is GenCon: The Worst Four Days In Gaming. You can find it here. I've also posted a link over on my blog.

Big thanks to Travis Farber and Nick Frankenhauser for helping to playtest, and to Jason Morningstar for helping me to make this as awful and terrible as I knew it could be.

    \n It depends on the category? I mean, the locations are taken 100% from real life. The needs are mostly based on stuff I've observed happening at GenCon - except for the crime stuff, natch - though some are exaggerated for effect. The objects are mostly mix of stuff I've seen and stuff that is only slightly exaggerated. For instance, I did see a discreetly placed rack of leather floggers, only it was at the big leather booth, not at a corset booth. And there were life-sized R2D2s, but I don't know if they contained any dead hobos. (Who knows. They might.) Oh, and the signed photo of Felica Day exists. Brennan Taylor told me that he stood in line to get a signed photo for his daughter, so I totally put it in.

    Anyway. I have a suspicion that this might be closer to reality than some other playsets.

    \n Embassy Suites--"By the elevator on the twentieth floor where you can see.. all... the way... down."

    Ugh. Now I'm getting flashbacks to the Flower for Mara game that I ran on the fifteenth floor of the Embassy Suites. I'm generally scared of heights anyways, but it was a really bad night. So I'm lugging my stuff up there, and I'm walking to the room, when I can feel the tug of the yawning void off to my right.

    It's beckoning me.

    And I'm afraid that, somehow, without really thinking about it, I'll accidentally jump off the balcony and only figure out what I was doing as I'm plummeting to my death.

    Even when I was in the room, I felt the void outside the door, like we were clinging to the edge of a cliff.

    And even worse, after the game, we were sitting in the lobby, debriefing and generally clinging to each other for comfort (a common reaction after playing A Flower for Mara, I admit), I felt like I was going to fall up through the skylight, twenty floors up.

    Um, yeah, that was a bad night.

    Also: this playset makes me very happy.

    Seth Ben-Ezra
    Great Wolf
