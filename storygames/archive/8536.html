\n I just moved to Vegas and a very cool cat has been running NeonCon for the last couple years. Meeting me sort of vicariously plugged him into the story/indie games community and he's looking to make indie games a big part of future cons. This left me with a mission to use my "contacts" in the community to see what needs to be done. Here I am! :-P

I'll give you guys the basic info about this particular con since I know it makes a bit of difference. My experience at NeonCon showed it to be a 4 day convention centered more around actual gaming than advertising new releases and the like. The owner has a ton of event experience and picked a good location and a good price of $45. The event was mostly RPGA stuff since that was the only thing the owner was familiar with a year ago. There were 300-400 people this year, but 90% of them were RPGAers. That number could easily be doubled with a few efforts to attract the other side of the gaming crowd.

I have a few ideas of my own, but I'd like to leave the floor open to whatever comes to mind from this particular gallery of peanuts.

1. What attracts you to a particular con?
2. What turns you away from another con?
3. What makes a con worth heading out of state for?
4. What do you look for as a gamer?
5. What do you look for as a designer or publisher?

That's the only questions I can think of right now. Feel free to add any pertinent ideas, advice or questions you have!
