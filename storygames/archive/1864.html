I'm blanking on games that have multiple players controlling individual player characters, or have different players in control of different aspects of shared characters. Anybody?

I'm reading Cormac McCarthy's The Road, which is unremittingly horrifying and great, and the protagonist is obsessed with staying alive, which means running and, when he can't run, fighting. It got me thinking - it'd be perfect for a post-apocalypse game to have characters cleanly divided between fight and flight, desperation and terror, and have each halve controlled by a different player. To add spice to the mix, there'll be a dial the dominant half can set - the farther it is adjusted toward fight or flight, the more effective the character will be at the expense of the opposite impulse. So if you set it all the way to flight, and get backed into a corner and have to fight, you're in trouble as you turn the character over to the other player.

Creating characters in this mode sounds fun to me - you've got two competing impulses, and each player will be creating traits and backstory that support their "side" of the character's personality.

One down-side to this would be circumstances where one player ends up controlling multiple shared characters. For example, if you have two players and two characters, and each takes flight for one character and flight for the other, any time one runs and the other takes a stand, one player will be left with nothing to do. I'm sure there are ways around this, but on the surface it seems inelegant.

    It Was A Mutual Decision has teams of players controlling each of the 2 characters involved in the story. I've only played it once, so can comment too much on how it works, but was an interesting experience from the point of view of collaboratively deciding the actions and impulses of one character.

    One thing that strikes me is that a game of Contenders (a one session one) might work (possibly) with a single shared character, each player taking a turn at framing a scene a taking the lead. Need to have a think about that more though.
