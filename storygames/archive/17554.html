    \n I have games that would benefit from playtesting, but not "run it for me" playtesting. My game Final Hour of a Storied Age seems to work very well in internal testing where I participate, but in order to make progress I need some external testing to confirm that the experience is coming from the game I designed rather than from my genre knowledge and any subtle role-modeling I may be doing as the perceived authority on how to play correctly. It's a pretty complete game with examples, etc., which I think has the paradoxical effect of scaring people off due to the page count.

    My game Getting There in Time is earlier in the development cycle. What I'd really like to do is experience the game as a player rather than the GM so I can better understand where some of the problems are creeping in, but since part of the game involves exploring a GM-prepped situation I can't get the full player experience with my pre-genned situations. Which means that until the GM-prep procedures are solid (which would require some more people to playtest the solo GM-prep procedures, and possibly some further development of those procedures by me if the results they're generating aren't "right") I can't usefully participate in a playtest like that. Theoretically I could GM more playtest sessions than I've already done, but I'm not confident I'd learn a lot more about the game by running more sessions with myself in the GM's seat so I hate to impose on people's time for a playtest of questionable utility. (Seeing a recording of an external group playing with one of my pre-genned situations might be informative, though).

    That said, I'm willing to participate in online playtests of other people's games, assuming there's something I can get interested about in the design. I used to do more online playtesting than I do now, but the pressure of always having to hunt down the games and organize the sessions took too much of my emotional energy for it to be sustainable.

    \n Pretty straightforward. Let's say I'm running the Hangout game. I click on 'lauch hangout', type in the names of people I want to invite, and you get a notification. You click on the notification, click on 'join hangout', and then this video-chat thingie pops up. You see/hear me, and vice versa.

    And there's other people, too (I've found that a total of 3-5 people works pretty good, never tried it with more players than that). And there's these other things you can add to the experience, which will let you show maps to the other players, or roll virtual dice by typing in commands, and then everyone can see the 'dice' you rolled.

    But the see/hear part is the good stuff. It's pretty stable, even if you're playing with people around the world.

    That's the basics!
