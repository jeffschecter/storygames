\n What started out as a fun little experiment has grown into a full blown challenge:

Design a Tabletop RPG, Setting, Hack, Expansion, or whatever in 200 words or less.

Email your submission to davidschirduan@gmail.com. Email it as simple plaintext or PDF. Once I\u2019ve got them all, we'll vote on them. I am sllwly collecting some fun prizes for the winners! Let me know if you'd like to contribute a prize to the competition!

Submissions due by May 1st!


View Current submissions and contest details here.

Good luck! Go Create!

This is a great chance to polish up a smaller rpg or supplement, or to see how much you can simplify and streamline an idea. I encourage everyone to participate!

    \n@brunobord thanks :-)

    You should use armor and shields as you'd use a weapon or another tool, when you think they would apply to the situation. Of course in combat: for example you're facing a monster, you pick a die for your sword, one for shield and one for armor. You mark the three boxes (one for each item) and have 3 extra dice to roll.
    In fiction, the extra dice make it easier for you to inflict harm: basically with the protection of your armor and shield you concentrate better on the attack, hence the higher chance to inflict damage on your opponent.
