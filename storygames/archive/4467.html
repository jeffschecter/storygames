\n Today, Richard Bartle announced via terranova.blogs.com a new, peer-reviewed journal. He said, "The International Journal of Role-Playing is 'a biannual international journal that covers all aspects of role-playing, irrespective of the medium, platform or intent'."

The journal's website is calling for papers with a submission deadline of 1 Jan 08.

From their site:\n
Potential topics include but are certainly not limited to the following:
\xe2\u20ac\xa2 Role playing games, e.g. frameworks, storytelling and graphics; art, design and creative industry
\xe2\u20ac\xa2 Role playing culture, psychology, media, economics, and sociology
\xe2\u20ac\xa2 Role playing technology, surveys, vocabulary, training and education
\xe2\u20ac\xa2 Other aspects of role playing and related research and development
\nBartle is one of the editors, and the review board consists of the following people:

Staffan Bjork
Sebastian Deterding

    \n I found this section of the submission guidelines to be interesting:

    Free text: Unique for academic journals, the IJRP also provides authors with the option of writing a piece of text where they are bound (sic) by any of the formal requirements of academic or industry writing, nor the regulations of the IJRP itself. This space is meant to be a free area where authors can note down their ideas, perspectives, visions in relation to their publication, such items that would normally not hold a place in a serious publication. For example, an academic article on gender in gaming might contain a free text section about the possible implications for games design, for games in the future (or the future of the particular field). Similarly, a games design article could have a free text on suggestions for use in research.
