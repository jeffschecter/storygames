\n Hey Folks,

So one of the things I want to do in the introduction to Magic Missile is try to talk about what the past 10+ years of indie design and play have been about. Yes, it's a ridiculous and possibly foolish task! I'm going to try anyway.

To try to hit the highlights, I'm going to structure it largely around the games listed in the Indie RPG Awards for various years. But in order to place the Forge / SG / IPR / GoPlay / etc. community in context, I want to also mention game designers that were doing similar things both BEFORE the Forge inspired this particular crowd of folks to come together and were doing similar stuff AT THE SAME TIME, just as part of different communities, with few if any connections to our particular swath of roleplaying.

Folks I'm already planning on mentioning include:

- Jonathan Tweet
- John Tynes
- Greg Stolze
- Greg Costikyan
- Ken Hite
- Robin Laws
- John Wick
- James Wallis (esp. New Style)
- Stefan O'Sullivan
- Ann Dupius
- Rebecca Sean Borgstrom / Jenna K. Moran

It's probably also good to remember folks who've engaged with this particular community on and off but mostly do their own thing (even more so than, say, Evil Hat), like Malcolm Sheppard and Gareth Hanrahan.

If I try to remember all the important folks on my own, I'm sure to miss some, so I wanted to draw on our collective brains a bit.

I'm mostly thinking of Anglo-American folks, since talking about international cross-over (Nordic, Japanese, and Italian, mostly) is going to be a separate section, but if you can think of important folks from Europe or elsewhere that I'm likely to forget, that would be great too.

Finally, I would also like to discuss influential games produced by this community that were never formally published and, as such, would be unlikely to have been nominated for the awards. Scarlet Wake comes to mind, but I'm sure there are others. So if you have thoughts on those too, send them along.

While it's going to be nearly impossible to come up with an OBJECTIVE list (and it's not meant to be a comprehensive list anyway, just hitting some highlights in a descriptive overview), I don't want my discussion to merely be playing favorites, covering the games that I or this community as a whole happen to like. So, where possible, I'm going to lean towards people who are following the kinds of publishing models that this community has championed or folks who seem to be doing similar kinds of structuralist analysis and experimentation, even if they're not really involved in the same conversations.

Thanks in advance!

    Posted By: J. WaltonDoes anybody remember who was pioneering the digest-sized format that people now associate with indie games? I know a lot of boxed sets came with digest sized booklets, but who were some of the folks putting out complete, stand-alone games in smaller sizes?
    \nKobolds Ate My Baby, Ninja Burger and especially Hero Wars. The latter was cause for something of a critical look into how retail stores stock and display games published in nonstandard formats, as it had been decades since a high-end game that was not parsed as a "joke game" had been published in digest format. (As Jason says it had been more common before the great production values jump beginning in late '80s made letter-size, preferably hardcover books the industry standard.)
