    \n Ah, y'all think small--must not be enough LARPers.

    PDF!?! Pfaugh, on your PDFs--I want a database driven, server-side hosted task (or conflict) resolution system which totally integrates my PC's stats and abilities with those of NPCs and other PCs in play.

    Want to cast a summon spell on another PC? Choose "Cast: Summon Person" and the Bluetooth detector pops-up a list of who's in range. I pick the PC, our iPhones test my Magic against their Will stat or whatever, and they get a notification which guides them to my general location using GPS. Obviously, "Cast Magic Missile" is bog-simple, so long as line of sight isn't a big deal in the system (the GPS might not be able to tell that there's no LOS to the Bluetooth-selected target): connect, roll, compare, inform victim, adjust stats on server. Hell, I could even make the phone take into consideration the movement sensor, to adjust "to hit" (or whatever) based on degree of agitation--user is most likely walking or running, if there's constant, rhythmic, somewhat-violent motion detected.

    Or, hell, fine--let's work in tabletop, removing the GPS and Bluetooth ranging focus and using networked iPhones to augment mapping software, "minis" movement, and secret notes. Want a different fog of war for each PC, based on race or position or prior-knowledge? Trivial. Want folks to move their own minis, while the game rules constrain them to fair play? Trivial. Need to pass some clue to someone without other player's knowledge? Trivial (can do so now with any TM phone, for what that's worth).

    Is all that worth doing, in a tabletop environment--prolly not, unless your group is BIG on tactics and at least somewhat competitive and secretive. The PC-based, projected D&D stuff I've seen is only BARELY better than hex mats and plastic minis, and I bet the up-front prep is far worse (but then again, we were rarely big on having "gorgeous maps" or "computer-managed range and rolls to hit" since we liked drawing with our dry erase, handling minis and dice, and doing things other than prep for D&D games).

    I will give others time to grok the power of social networking via the iPhone and its Bluetooth and GPS... I got an idea for a much more fun "game" to win: Match.com Live. >:^)
