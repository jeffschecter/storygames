I wish there was a Jodorowsky Story Game

James_Nostack
December 2006 edited December 2006 in Story Games
Alejandro Jodorowsky is a Chilean-born Frenchman who mainly makes crazy movies and, these days, funky surreal sci-fi comic books. He is awesomely crazy.

Once, the Divinity agreed to say to me in a lucid dream: "Your next film must be Dune." I had not read the novel. I lifted myself to a height of six o'clock in the morning and as an alcoholic who awaits the opening of the bar, I waited until someone opens the bookshop to buy the book. I read it of a feature without me stopping for drinking or eating. At midnight exactly, the very same day, I finish the reading. At one minute pass midnight I called from New York, Michel Seydoux in Paris... He would be the first of the seven samurai that it was necessary for me to have for the immense project. (edited to add: this is the most insanely great movie I've ever heard of. I wish it existed.)

Man, I wish there was a game that captured the Jodorowsky style of telling a story. I think there's some RPG about the Metabarons, one of his comics, but it kind of sounds like a franchise knock-off that doesn't really get down to business and reach the good stuff that makes this guy who he is.

    Posted By: Dirk RemmeckeI have to admit that I haven't detected any particular features in Jodorowky's works that make his way of telling a story remarkably different from other comic book authors.
    His storytelling (in the Metabarons series anyway) seems to be comprised of "drug-addled meandering extreme nonsense". If someone lined up 4 comics of any style, by any author, and said "one of these stories was written by Jodorowsky", I'd look for the most ridiculous, nonsensical, yet sensual one and that would be it.

    A true game based on his works would go like this:

    1) First, the GM would be called "The Jodorowsky". He would have unlimited fiat.
    2) Character generation would take about 20 seconds tops, because we wander from character to character so fast that anything more would get in the way of the story.
    3) Every player would have one stat, called "Importance".
    4) Every session would begin with the biggest group bang possible.

    It would look like this:

    The Jodorowsky: OK, so the Fengari Collective show up. Their ambassador says that they want to play Naked Explosion Space Chess for the fate of the universe. If they win, all the planets in human space will build engines to fly themselves into their suns. If they lose, they will give you a magic almond.
    Player One: Damn. So my guy is there, right?
    TJ: Yep.
    Player One: OK, I want to talk to them and try to nego...
    TJ: HOLD! Player Two, what are you going to do?
    Player Two: Have sex with a space vampire pirate. While weeping bright yellow tears of pure energy.
    TJ: Good. Player Three?
    Player Three: I think that my robotic concubine is going to down a glass of Catholic infant blood, then try to seduce the ambass...
    TJ: HOLD!
    TJ: OK. All players, roll your Influence.
    P1: 15
    P2: 22
    P3: 8
    TJ: OK, here's what happens.
    then the The Jodorowsky proceeds to tell the players exactly what happens in the story, changing scenes, starting and resolving conflicts and combats, playing all the actions of the player characters, as he sees fit, until the story is over. This can take 20 minutes to three hours. Players are encouraged to sit back comfortably. Wine is offered.
    P1: Wow, that was fascinating!
    P2: I particularly enjoyed the role of my character in your story.
    P3: My character... didn't even appear. But still, I really enjoyed your story.
    TJ: It was nothing. OK, next story. Roll up new characters.

    -Andy
