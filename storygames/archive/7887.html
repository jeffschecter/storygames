\n Can anyone point me to TSoY keys where players award other players, instead of getting the xp for themselves? I know Judd is working on something like this for 1st Quest, but I can't seem to FIND A COPY.

Alternatively, post your ideas in this thread.

Reverse Key of the Wise Friend
1xp: Have a discussion with another PC about what they're thinking, possibly while fishing.
2xp: The other PC takes your advice.
5xp: You stand by the PC even though they ignore your advice.
Buyoff: You don't stand by the PC and break off the friendship after they ignore your advice.

So for this one, I can sit down next to you by the river, listen to you talk, and then hand you an xp. If I give you advice, and you take it, I can hand you 2 more. If you ignore my advice but I stand by you anyway, I can give you 5 xp. But if you can turn me away from you, I'll give you your 10 XP; I hope you learn from this, but maybe you're just going your own way.

    \n I really like this idea.

    We've all had character concepts that kind of require everyone else to get on board with treating the character a certain way for it to work. For example the "Wolverine" type character, "the loner with a good heart" is notoriously popular, and notoriously disastrous. I can see this as a really cool way to flag how you want your character to work in the group.

    I think the buyoffs are the tricky part. Graham's "Reverse Key of the Casanova" is perfect, but he screwed up the "Reverse Key of the Bastard". The buyoff should be "Express genuine sympathy for a PC".

    There's a problem with how these are phrased though. They should be concrete actions that other characters can perform for XP. For example:

    Reverse Key of the Lovable Rogue
    1xp: Forgive this PC for a minor transgression
    3xp: Get this PC out of serious trouble.
    Buyoff: Be rescued by this PC.

    Or something. The buyoffs are still wonky.

    \n Here's my take on what a Judd-inspired "Key of the Casanova" would look like.

    Note: For me, the biggest sticking point with these is the phrasing--how they're written. Pretty much every version I've seen, not only in 1st Quest, but also all the examples above, are really hard to understand. Who gets XP when... who, huh? It's easy to get confused. So I'm being extra careful about the phrasing here.\n
    Key of the Casanova (Judd-style, at least as far as I understand his idea)

    Gain:

    1 XP each time you flirt with someone.
    2 XPs each time you successfully seduce someone.
    5 XPs each time you risk life and limb to pursue a romantic interest.

    Award another player:

    1 XP when they help you pursue a romantic interest.
    3 XPs if they pursue one of your romantic interests themselves.
    \nAnother thought I had was preparing the "reverse" elements in some form that the other players can see. In this case, you'd have the first (regular) part on your character sheet, and leave something like this on the table, for the other players to refer to:\n
    Key of the Casanova - [character name]

    Gain:

    1 XP when you help me pursue a romantic interest.
    3 XPs if you pursue one of my romantic interests yourself.
    \nI think careful use of pronouns (you, they, me, etc) is key in making such Keys (ahem) read clearly to everyone at the table.
