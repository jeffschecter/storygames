    The core elements of Sanctum: The Fallout are these.

    The Start of Game?

    Everyone in the given city, from the moment they awake, or the moment they stumble out of their home realize that they aren't really home after all. The game Sanctum asks, "What happens next?" and then leaves it to players, storytellers and the imagination. The start of game is really that simple. You wake up...."now what?"

    And because I want everyone, even those who need some creative assistance or plot ideas to have fun and be able to play, I'll offer plenty of suggestions, such as possible plots, stories, histories, explanations etc, but most importantly, how to get the players doing all the work for you...because the best games are player-plot driven, as far as I'm concerned. When the players are so busy with plot that you don't need to be involved in, then the game runs itself and you just sit back and watch it unfold. It's not linear, so people can relive memories from their past of before they were here in Sanctum, or they can foretell events that they actually make sure happen by driving the plot. (In ends and means, it's not a crime to figure out the outcome of what happens before you decide What happens...and this should make things really fun and fluid)

    There is no preset number of cities that must exist on or in the world...however, The world is defined BY its cities in that each city is set apart with a themed absolute.

    Each city's population shares a theme of sort. From ordinary people living ordinary lives, to that everyone in that city is a fictional historical figure. But there is no mixture of the two...it's ONE theme per city...It's what sets everything apart, what raises questions and what creates divides. Because of this, the game can change, adapt, evolve. If players get tired of playing the city they start with, the entire game could shift over to new characters in the same world, going from actually playing thier characters to seeing them involved as NPC's.

    A game could have one city and one city alone, or it could eventually link several cities, but they don't start out linked...and depending on the storytellers or where the players want to bring the game, they never even have to come into play, OR they could be the main focus.

    Because one city might be ordinary people from ordinary lives in different points of time but all from earth....but the next city might be monsterous creatures of Myth and Legend. Another City might be historical figures throughout earth's history. Another City might be fictional characters from legend and lore of earth. Or, it could be a city where there is no one left living, but spirits of those who once did.

    The things that bind Sanctum together is that no matter what elements are added, how sci-fi, how fictional, or how realistic the players and Storytellers intend to make it...Whether it is driven with Character Development (like the beta is going to be), or driven along by either player or storyteller generated plot, that each city is a theme that takes that theme and mashes everything known about the people within that city together into a pot-luck mixture of everything. (another thing to make sure players question is that this city is the closest thing to home that they have left in the world...it's at least familiar to them, and if they do find other cities, it might be fascinating for some, but overwhelming for others...which is going to potentially ensure that people stay living in their own cities...after all, it's familiar, right?)

    More Conditions of Sanctum?

    There is a solid count of people per city. There is a population and death DOES matter.
    for our beta game, it's 500. If a player or Storyteller creates an NPC, that NPC becomes a part of that population...it's as simple as having a notebook and writing the name down next to a number. When you are out of numbers, then that's it...after all, why should life be replaceable? This is modified for other conditions within the game; such as the theme is set far into a future in which cloning is easily done, magically created, etc. But, the ones who exist, still exist...and the ones who die, do die. I felt this makes murder and death an accountable action and gives more value to the content.
