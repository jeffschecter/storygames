    \n Hairy T is "a walk-up hole the wall" but "chock full o'stuff" indeed. Yes, realy it's owned by the cats with Leon as their agent. The people come and go, but the cats remain, lounging on the checkout counter looking up at you as come to pay as if to say, "yeah so?" Fair amount of used stuff as well.

    Pity about that Ryan, I would say it's the best LGS in downtown core for variety, then adding on all the comics and mini's (don't be shy, go past cash register into nook to the left). 401 Games across the street and up a block is okay for new releases of majors but I've never seen Burning Wheel proudly displayed in their "new release/just back in stock rack". The others with same breadth bit the dust (e.g., Worldhouse, which I would have put on the list as the "clean and friendly Hairy T" if it was still around). Long gone are the days of Mr. Gameways Ark (the House of Lords building on Yonge used to be a toy store, most of a floor set aside for RPGish type games etc, but that all happened long ago, and there are none now who remember it).

    Rob
