\nMACE 2009 Gaming Open GM Call has just gone out, and I was wondering if any of us locals want to try to maybe coordinate game slots, so as to offer a "Independent Story Games Track" to Ron (McClung, the gaming coordinator) as a fait accompli?

Basically, I'm just thinking that we try to organize our GM slots so there's minimal overlap, and then have some kind of "tag" to use in OGRe's Game/Scenario Title (and Description?) fields so that folks can find those of us in the Track. Something like:
Game/Scenario Title: [ISG] A Town In Sin
System: Dogs in the Vineyard
Description: [Independent Story Games] You and your fellow Dogs are making a routine tour of Laughing Gulch when....

I want to do at least four slots, and I intended to be there Friday evening slot to end-of-day Sunday (though I'll warn folks that Sunday afternoon gets SLOOOOOWWWW). I have no burning preference as to what I run, and I might do a slot of Are You A Werewolf? (or the Looneys' new game, Are You The Traitor?) as they are pretty much "indie" (though I wouldn't put the [ISG] tag on it, not being a story game, so much).

Anyone interested? I know Sci Fi Genre attends MACE and supports (some) indie publishers, so maybe we could get up with them (and a Triad game store, if y'all know of any) and make it into a sort of joint venture--or at the least warn them to have a few copies of the games we intend to run, as these games sell themselves and it would be good to say, "Oh, you like it? OK, go to the Dealer's Room and look for [insert your preferred FLGS here, if they have the game]."

Any thoughts or interest? At the VERY least, we can try not to so-overlap that we don't get any chance to play in each others' sessions.

    \n Andy, it seems like it can't hurt to do a bit of branding. It's a matter of a tag in the Game Name and a label in the Description, a moment's additional work. But it might, just MIGHT, make folks think, "Hmmm, are these 'those games' I've been hearing about?" Or it might make a non-(RPG)gamer think to check out a session, when they'd normally be doing Steve Jackson card games or pickup board games (descriptions matter!).

    It's the "folks here and there" I'm hoping to reach out to, without relying on just overhearing them (or them asking). You say there were a few folks here and there... how can you be sure they represented the totality of folks seeking out the indie hotness (or even a majority of them?). I'm just sayin'.

    Obviously, no one MUST self-identify with the tag and label (and I doubt those folks are in this thread, excepting maybe folks saying, "Why bother?"). Plus, I think it would be nice to showcase just how many published designers are right here in NC! (That said, it would be sweet if you designer folks ran at least ONE of your own games! Or even just ATTEND....)
