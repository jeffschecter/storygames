\n Kingdom needs playtesters. I've been working on it since before I released Microscope and playing it with a small cadre of brave souls since September, but now it's finally ready for external testing. Which means you.

So what is Kingdom?\n
Groups are stronger than individuals. A Kingdom can accomplish things no individual could.

\t\tBut the Kingdom makes demands on you too. If you\u2019re part of it, you\u2019re pressured to do what it thinks is right. If you believe in what the Kingdom is doing, you\u2019re fine. But if you don\u2019t, or if the Kingdom starts to become something other than what you want it to be, you might find yourself caught in the middle, pressured to do things you don\u2019t want to do.

\t\tThe question becomes: do you change the Kingdom or does the Kingdom change you?

What kind of Kingdoms can we play?

\t\tA "Kingdom" is the game term for an organization or community that the characters all belong to. Any kind of group works. Your Kingdom could be a hospital, the crew of a starship, French partisans, a viking clan, a Greek city-state, the Federation of Planets, the Mafia, a Wild West boom town, a newspaper, or Sunnybrook Elementary School.

\t\tJust about any tone or genre should work: anything from dudes with axes fighting to be chief, to concerned doctors arguing that the underprivileged deserve health care, to romantic comedy (yeah, I was surprised too, but it works).

\t\tWhat\u2019s the Kingdom for? What\u2019s it\u2019s purpose? What should it do? Our characters may not all see eye-to-eye. Then the trouble starts.
\nNo GM and no prep. 3-5 players. You can play one-shot or multiple sessions.

If you want to playtest, whisper me your email or contact me at kingdom-playtest at lamemage.com. Let me know the last few role-playing games your group played so I have a sense of what kind of demographic of testers I've got. I definitely want people who can play the game, not just read it.

And no, I would say it's not as weird as Microscope.

    \n Answering a whispered question:\n
    Is some kind of character/kingdom conflict necessary to play? Can all of us players be Kingdom-paragons and enjoy the game without conflicting our beliefs with the Kingdom's?
    \nThe rules will answer more fully, but basically it's entirely legit for the characters to be sympathetic to each other while the players simultaneously create situations to make each other's lives miserable. None of the characters have to be "bad people" or start off directly at odds, but the players may certainly have their characters go down roads they did not expect as the Kingdom develops.

    The rules are set up as much as possible to reflect real world dynamics in an interesting game way. The possible permutations that emerge from that are pretty vast.
