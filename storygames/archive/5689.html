\n So, one of the cool things about Lacuna is how fluid it is, and how individual games are from GM to GM.

One of the things Ive been thinking about lately is communication with Control. My group started out agreeing that Agents had to find an access point (telephone) to communicate with Control, but that soon turned out to be cumbersome. We settled on earpieces, ala Matrix.

How do you do it?

    \n In the first several missions, the agent only communicated with Control via a landline phone.

    In the last mission (run last Wed), Control was an older fellow and he was pretty chatty. It was the PCs first mission as team lead, so Control was on him most of the mission, giving tidbits, advice, making sardonic comments, asking him if he wanted to eject. It was different for the player, for sure. He ejected at 3 Static, so I think it influenced his fear level.

    \n I went with the fiction in the book.

    You need a telephone or similar device to hear Control talking to you. But you can send a telepathic "ping" to Control anytime you want, so players can roll for a requisition request at any point, but having an actual conversation takes a bit of work.

    Requisitioning a cellphone is a good one.

    I'm pretty strict about starting gear - the suit of clothes, the Lacuna lapel pin, nothing else.
