\n Hey there.
\n
\nNow, before I ask my question, let me tell you about polish rpg community. It's important.
\nOur community is huge, and we meet on many different websites. The biggest three sites are:
\n
\nPoltergeist - a site about anything bound with sf and fantasy genre. Movies, comics, books, games, stuff. But it's base always was, and always will be traditional rpg. Most registered members are players. Lately Polter starts to come out from the sf niche, and starts publicing rewievs of non fantastical genre of comic books, movies, and books. This is due to the unexpected boom of non-rpg readers coming on the site. Effect: 900.000 uniqe people at the website a month.
\nValkiria - a little smaller, but still huge site. Considered "rival" to Polter by some users on both sides.
\nGildia - also a known source of news for readers and gamers.
\n
\nIf you would like to advertise your game on any or all of these websites, all you have to do, is to write a mail to their owners. In 90% of cases, they will create a news article about your game. If you'll give them a copy for a review, they will do it gladly, and post again.
\n
\n* * *
\n
\nNow. I'm designing a free game. I want to advertise it worldwide, on german sites, on british sites, on italian sites, french, spanish, american... Everywhere. I spent some time looking for similar websites around europe, and I just couldn't find them. Now there can be two reasons for this: There are no sites such as those in poland (big in numbers of users), or they are not positioned very well in Google, or I'm just lame searcher.
\n
\nMy question is: You, people of Story Games, who come here just like me, from distant and exotic parts of the world, probably know such sites in your country. Why don't we create a base of such sites around the world? Sites, that we could use to advertise our games, to simply make more people play them? I would say, that we already have polish sites checked.
\n
\nI often wonder, why none (or very little at least) of your games were advertised on Poltergeist. If they were, it was always a fan initiative, not the authors. Why don't we advertise INDIE games on non-INDIE sites? It is RPG, is it not? Why do authors stick to the very few, international sites, and don't try direct advertisement? (This is not the main question of this thread. It's considered rethorical. So please, let's not change this thread into discussion about ways of advertisement, and stuff, okay? Whisper me, if you want to talk about that, and we'll create a proper topic.)
\n
\n* * *

    \n The main roleplaying site in hungarian is rpg.hu. Paying for a banner might be possible, but not what I'd do if I wanted to reach the site's visitors. Getting your game on the main site goes like this: someone who knows hungarian writes a review about your game and sends it in. I did that for Universalis for example. Not sure if this resulted in any copies sold, but overall awareness about the game grew from non-existent to "I think I heard about that somewhere." You might be able to get special offers and newly published games on the main page without a hungarian language review, if it's not too frequent.

    About numbers: The site has over 77k registered users but most accounts are of the one-post-and-forget type from when roleplaying was more popular here in Hungary. Articles on the main page have around 1k reads the month they were published. You should keep in mind that differences in wages, postal fees, language problems and higher barrier for online purchases (online transaction capable credit cards are less prevalent here) mean you'll probably get much less purchases from 1000 hungarian players who read an article about your game than from 1000 american ones. And I don't think that number is too high either.
