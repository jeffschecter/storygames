[AD&D Reprints] Why Erasing Dave Arneson from History is the "Natural" Choice

\n
\n
\n
\n\nJohnstone \n\n\n
\n
\n\nJanuary 2012 \n edited January 2012 in Stuff to Watch
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n In the January Stuff to Watch thread, there was some disappointment over the AD&D reprint, primarily because it is AD&D and not OD&D that is being reprinted. Personally, I am not really surprised that it is AD&D.

Joel responded to my comment with a question:\n
Posted By: JoelPosted By: Johnstone[AD&D Reprints 3]

AD&D was all Gary, so it's actually the natural choice here.
\nWait, why is it "natural"? Is there something so goddamned special about Gary that the best way to honor the roots of D&D is to pass over the products that Dave had input on?

I dunno if this is worth a whole new thread, so I suppose if you wanna answer it'll have to be whispers. But dammit, I am tired of seeing Dave Arneson shit on and ignored regarding the D&D legacy.

So, the answer:

This reprint is being done (ostensibly) to raise money for the Gygax Memorial Fund, and AD&D is the quintessential Gary Gygax.

It's not the Gary and Dave Memorial Fund, it's the charity dedicated to raising money to build a statue commemorating Gary Gygax as the "Father of Role-Playing Games," so it's natural to talk about how great Gary was and then celebrate him with the reprint of his magnum opus. It keeps everything tightly focused, plain, and simple. It communicates the minimum amount of information necessary and no more.

And it erases Dave Arneson.

Now, when I said it was "natural," I didn't say it was right.

On the one hand, should the people who want a Gary statue really feel obliged to honour Dave as well? "The Father of Role-Playing Games" is a bit rich for one guy, sure, but I mean, they're the ones paying for it and working to raise the money and whatnot, right? If you don't like it, you don't have to give them your money... oh, unless you buy the reprints, that is.

WotC, on the other hand, really has no excuse. Anybody interested in these books is going to notice the omission (or has noticed already), and none of them are going to believe Gary was the lone founder of rpgs. They'll buy it anyway, of course, and none of them are going to get it together to get a statue of Dave built, even one that proclaims him "the Mother of Role-Playing Games." Actually, maybe not such a bad gaff on their part, is it?

So yes, I understand why. And yes, I think it's bullshit, too. I agree with Joel.

Anyway, here's a thread! If you'd like to use this thread to complain about WotC, suggest possible ways to (better) memorialize Dave Arneson, or just demand reprints of those three little books that really started everything, by all means, be my guest.

    \n I've said it before, but I'll say it again. If you're trying to figure out what the credit line should read as next to "Creator of Roleplaying Games" you've got two options:

    (1) Dave Arneson, for creating the fundamental gameplay in his Blackmoor campaign
    (2) Dave Arneson and Gary Gygax, for co-authoring D&D

    You'll notice that neither one of these is "Gary Gygax all by himself". That's because there is no functional argument or logic by which Gary Gygax gets that title to himself.
