\n My brain has shut down on me and I need creative help.

I am writing a pseudo-Gloranthan Runequest crossover for the GURPS system. One of the features of the sub-Bronze-Age world of Glorantha that I want to introduce is body paint or tattoos that give protection. This will, in turn, allow characters to charge about naked if they wish, in the manner depicted on Greek vases and Roman bas-reliefs. No, probably not historically accurate but this is fantasy.

Now the skill to create and apply these protective warding patterns is a mix of artistic and religious. It can use any suitable paint or dye to create the ward.

The huge problem I have is that I cannot think of a suitable name for the skill or the artist. 'Protective Ward Artist' hardly screams Bronze Age culture, 'Body Painter' or 'Body Artist' suggests thin modern people wearing black taking themselves far too seriously.

Can anyone suggest anything, no matter how obvious or weird it seems? I'm happy to elaborate on the skill or game if necessary, but really all I want is a name that sounds a little bit mystical and a little bit artistic, describes someone painting or tattooing protective patterns, and won't look out of place on a list of pseudo-Bronze-Age skills or professions.

    \n Yes, woad did occur to me and it is certainly the sort of thing I'm looking to model. There are a couple of issues, however. Woad itself has become a cultural reference for the northern Celtic/Pictish people. It is also a particular plant.

    My body artist/ward creator/protective make-up artists will be able to use anything from blood to special inks, including woad and other plant dyes. The woad name might be a little too specific in a setting that encompasses a lot of Mediterranean-style cultures rather than northern European.

    EDIT: I can also just imagine the puns that would ensue. 'On the woad again', 'fetch the woad warrior', 'one for the woad'.... I would spare myself that pain.

    \n The problem is that GURPS uses descriptive skill names, and characters essentially run using skills (plus a few other abilities). That means that getting the name of the skill 'right' is important. Add to that the fact that the setting has a quite specific culture and flavour. It isn't exactly historical, but you can smell the history close by.

    A person with the skills to paint magical patterns on your body which make you immune damage (actually not immune, but every little helps) is going to have a pretty reverential title.

    I think I'm going with Hopeless_Wanderer's idea of 'Skin-Art Magician'. It is both descriptive and suggests both artistic talent and mystic power.
