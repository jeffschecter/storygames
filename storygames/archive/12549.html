\n So, I have an idea for a system, it draws on ideas from a system released under the OGL, and other ideas from a system released under CC. Now obviously I could just ignore it because of how copyright works, but say I wan't to respect the licenses, how am I to go about it? Release the system under both licenses simultaneously? Does that even work?

    \n You cannot copyright a system, only the text and wording. If your game only draws inspiration from those games and doesn't use bits of their text, you can publish it however you want. Just give a nod to them and say where you got the inspiration from, it's good form.

    \n To go into a little more detail, Robin, if the work is CC Attribution, you might be able to mix it with OGC - you 'attribute' the work in the Section 15 of the OGL.* If it's CC Anything Else, you can't mix it with the OGL because the two licences are incompatible (for example, CC Attribution-Noncommercial can't be used because OGC can be used for commercial purposes).

    But as Gregor said, game rules are are not copyright - only the expression of the rules. As long as it's your original expression (or you use the expression of ONE of the open games) you should be in the clear.

    *: I'm not a lawyer, and I'm not sure about this.
