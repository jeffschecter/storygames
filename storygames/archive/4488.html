\n Recently I purchase two sets of "Ubiquity Dice" along with my Hollow Earth Expedition core rulebook; when I got home, I shook my head amusedly at my penchant for collecting novelty dice, and stuck them in my desk drawer next to my bags of FVLMINATA tali and my Fudge dice, thinking, "when am I ever going to use these".

This weekend, I thought, "You know, it's about time that I thought about what rolegame I'd use to get my kids into gaming."

At first, I thought my copy of the D&D Cyclopedia was the right answer. But the, since we're reading Lloyd Alexander's Prydain Chronicles at bed time, it hit me... what about Prince Valiant? So I got out my copy of the game and started re-reading, and my heavens if it isn't (a) pretty much perfect for this job, and (b) also the grand-daddy of all story games.

And you know what else? It uses the same kind of dice pool system that Hollow Earth Expedition uses! Which means, rather than use the "fistful of coins" suggestion that the game officially requires (which I have never liked) I can use my pile of Ubiquity dice! Thank-you Exile Game Studio! (I may now special order a couple more tubes of these things, just to make sure I have a plentiful supply.)

It also occurs to me that I could use these dice with Burning Wheel and Burning Empires, except, of course, that they'd only be useful with Black Shade rolls...

What other new tools can you re-purpose to make old games better?

    \n Yeah, sorry I wasn't more clear about what they were. They're a very neat idea. So elegant, mathematically speaking. Now, I'm thinking that Luke should go through one of these custom dice places to make a custom set of Burning Wheel dice:

    \xe2\u20ac\xa2 Black six-sided dice that have 3 zeroes and 3 ones as faces

    \xe2\u20ac\xa2 Grey six-sided dice that have 2 zeroes and 4 ones as faces

    \xe2\u20ac\xa2 White dice that have 1 zero and 5 ones as faces

    He could sell them in tubes of 10 with 7 black dice, 2 grey dice, and one white die. Most tables could get by well with two or three tubes, or each player might want a single tube. (Players with Elven characters, would probably need a higher grey/white to black ratio.)

    The only downside to this is that you would only have one die = one trait going with this... part of the elegance of the U Dice is that since what you're talking about with 50-50 pools is powers of two, the D8 is a really elegant way to collapse numbers of traits up to 3 cubed... now if we have sixteen sided dice... of course, you could use D12s to hold more than one BW trait in them...

    Abzu... you listening? 8)

    (I notice that Burning Empires doesn't use trait shades, so the standard Ubiquity dice would work well for that.)
