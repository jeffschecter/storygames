    I read ZoZ cover-to-cover and I really liked it. It's a nice little intro not only to creating a campaign setting of wholecloth based on the lean-and-mean little PDQ engine, but it takes you through the entire actual play process, from the original 3-line intro blurb to the grand finale and happily-ever-after. It's like an applied demonstration of some of the advice in "Robin's Laws of Good Gamemastering." But it's not just a how-to book walking you through the specific steps of campaign creation; it also has just enough setting details to create mental pictures, with examples of NPC character sheets, etc. It made me want to run three new campaigns at once! :-)

    December 2006 edited December 2006
    If anyone is interested, we're going to be playing a ZoZ game tonight here in Seattle. My husband is running it; he posted the premise in an Actual Play thread here. The premise: A coalition of action figures and Devil Duckies have taken control of the Island of Forgotten Toys, having overthrown Good King Moonchaser. The PCs are Forgotten Toys who must save the island and the king from the junta!

    All the characters are toys that were obtained from a Dollar Store or a novelty shop. We wrapped them last night, and players get to pick a box, get a toy, decide whether they want to trade, then get the character sheets. And keep the toy afterwards. As soon as the game is done, we'll post the characters and pictures (wouldn't want to ruin the surprise for the players.)

    We hope other people will get some entertainment out of this game too! ;-)
