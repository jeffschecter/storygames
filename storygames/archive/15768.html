    Re: Traveller for Mass Effect

    Yup, I did a conversion and it works really well. I changed Social Status to Social Standing, which reflects how the person is seen (overall) by the galactic community. Quarians and Krogan have a lower Soc, obviously, but if you treat it as a general power and influence stat, you don't have much trouble.

    The main thing you want to do is create custom races with stat mods, and after that either specific point-buy lists, or custom career tables (several can be ported over directly). I have all of this written down somewhere, but my general hack is thus (using Mongoose Traveller):

    Races
    Asari: Automatically roll for Psi, +1 Dex, -1 Str
    Drell: Automatically roll for Psi, +1 Str, +1 Dex, -2 End
    Hanar: -4 Str, +2 Int, +2 Edu (If you have a better idea, I'd love it)
    Krogan: +2 Str, +2 End, -2 Int, -2 Edu, -2 Soc (unless intimidating someone, in which case+2)
    Quarian: +1 Int, +1 Edu, -2 End, -2 Soc (unless around other Quarians)
    Salarian: -2 Str, -2 End, +2 Int, +2 Edu
    Turian: Automatically roll for Psi
    Volus: -2 Str, -2 End, -2 Dex, +2 Edu, +2 Soc

    For spaceships, I use the Warp or Hyperspace Drive variant rules on page 109. You could easily rule the Mass Relays work as Hyperspace drives, and use Warp Drives to get around the galaxy (as per ME2).

    Other than that, the ammo slots work for heat sinks, or I have a variant rule where you can wait two turns in combat and your weapon will cool down. I make players specify which tech they're using, though.

    Medigel works by healing +3 points, divided however the player wants, and require a combat action to use. I'm pretty free with it, but my players don't often need to use it.

    So there's my quick & dirty hack. I hope it helps.

    \n The thing that Mass Effect didn't have much of (at least not in 1, I haven't gotten to 2 yet) was starship tactical conflict. It was all ground missions and such. Which is understandable, cause that's easier to do as squad-based stuff where it matters which characters are with you.

    But starships are fun.

    And a really good starship tactical game is Battlestations. It is designed with characters that level up and collect loot and stuff. It's basically a lightweight mission-focused tactical rpg.

    It's a lot of fun because the characters drive the ship action - you have to move around the ship and go to the various battlestation locations to turn, fire lasers, draw energy, repair sections, and stuff like that.

    You could add in stuff to round it out. Blending it with something like Savage Worlds would probably work well - maybe start with the Savage Worlds hack to make the characters, but then slot them in to the actions you do in Battlestations.

    If I wanted to do a space game, I'd definitely use Battlestations for the ship combat part.
