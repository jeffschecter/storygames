\n Hi. New here and new to story games in general. I am a big board-game fan, but have never really gotten into RPGs -- probably because most of my previous RPG encounters spent more time looking at rules and charts than actually playing a role (and in that case, I'd just as soon play a board game). But I was recently turned onto Fiasco and think it's quickly risen to the top of my list; this game has everything I'd want -- great theme, awesome replayability, and tons of creativity coming from the players.

Last week, I got to play my first game, using the Tales from Suburbia playset. I've put up a detailed session report here: http://boardgamegeek.com/thread/840184/cat-me-if-you-can-a-suburban-nightmare (it's too many characters, or I'd just post it here as well).

I am really loving this game. I just started a play-by-forum game using the Operation Zebra playset, and I'm hoping to get my second in-person game going this week. If anyone knows of any other opportunities to play this game online (or any other similar games), please let me know. Thanks!

P.S. I'm also working on a few Fiasco playsets -- one has a gothic setting, one is at a high-school reunion, and one is at an amusement park. I'll post them here if there's interest.

    \n I don't know of a "best way," which is why I'm trying the GDrive thing out. The wiki is kept on a pretty tight leash, as it's a business portal, so I don't think "live development" on it is encouraged (and wikis don't really handle edit collisions well, but GDrive should do fine). I could just as easily see it done via an email group, a forum (one element per post?), a G+ Group, etc.

    As for publishing: any way and every way. BPG decides what goes onto their site and how (and we wiki golems just convert PDFs over to the wiki as we find time).

    As for online play, it's really easy to use Hangouts (or any other video group chat) and Graham's Catch Your Hare die roller (as it allows one to move dice [black or white] anywhere and make labels [for chosen elements] anywhere):
    http://www.catchyourhare.com/diceroller/
