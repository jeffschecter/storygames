    \nCool, I'll see if I can dig some of them up. It's been years and years since I studied this, so I don't have sources easy to hand.

    From what I remember, it's also worth noting that the criticism is more about the way the reporter presented it than about the reality of the myths. For example the Blue Lady and Bloody Mary myths are really quite old and wide spread, and not particular to Miami or the kids described. Also, the spreading and continuity of the tales as presented didn't really exist, and was a pretty localized phenomena centred around one charismatic kid with a brilliant storytelling streak and not really a growing network of living stories in the mode presented by the article.

    (I personally suspect the truth lies somewhere in between. After all urban legends come from old things told again by a new teller, and that new version catching and spreading.)
