    \nMy concern is that it needs to be something that NOBODY in the group will be struggling with (least of all, me) - the experience is meant to be a fun/painless one rather than a crash-course in a confusing application.
    \n
    \nHere's my analogy: Photoshop is awesome and is my preferred tool for doing image editing and yet it is not the tool that I recommend to casual peeps looking to do simple cropping/basic color correction, etc. Regardless of how much I dig Photoshop, I realize the majority of the features are simply never going to be of any use to them and only exist as clutter. I never think to myself "oh sure, they'll only cut and paste for now but after a while they'll discover the other functions and love it!" It's just not true.

    \n really, there aren't any virtual gaming options that are painless. if there were, more people would be doing it.

    vassal is fairly easy. i'm not a programmer but i hacked together a version of the S-a-h Roach in an afternoon. vassal is a bit buggy but there are tons of modules premade that you can use just the pieces in the module that you need: note pads; dice rollers etc. maptools is a great piece of software but there are lots of issues with port-forwarding connectivity. playing with a handful of people usually means at least one night of trying to get someone's system to connect with the server.

    i'm a big fan of John's and would love to see Epic Table come to life but i've been waiting on a working beta for over a year now and will just keep using what i know until it comes to life.

    vtt gaming certainly offers you some growing pains but at the end of the day you get a bunch of gaming out of it and that's not the worst investment of your time.

    \n Neil, I totally understand where you're coming from. I should have said it right away but I was in a hurry: Vassal looks rather ugly out of the box, and overly complicated. But fear not! You don't need to do anything difficult, really. You don't need to know anything of programming even for creating modules, for the basic stuff.

    You have to launch the webstart, or otherwise install the program, and then download one module: the Bucket of Dice. Click on "files" and then downoad the .mod file.
    That should be enough for most games (unless you need miniatures. In that case I'd suggest maptool instead).

    My idea is to use Vassal only as a shared table, with no rules enforcing whatsoever, no turn tracking, nothing. It will only be there as a substitute of your table and dice.

    (screenshots of my module, made based on the Bucket of Dice, coming soon)

    Sorry for the huge image, it's on my double monitor; click to see the full size:



    The module has dice in the 4 colors, coins, and a "table" to place them with the two bowls drawn directly on. When Pain dominates you simply drag one coin from the toolbox on the Despair Bowl.

    There are also fancy moleskine character sheet that can be compiled using the text labels and the images from the toolbox (crosses, gray boxes to eliminate the responses you don't have, and so on) but in that regard Vassal is pretty clunky. I'd use a wiki, or something, to quickly compile the charsheets, and maybe later on use these taking my time to compile them.

    \n@Brennen: thanks

    @Darren
    So, you launched Vassal, clicked File -> Open Module and selected Bucket of Dice.

    Now you start a new game (online or offline, at your leisure), choose a table (I'm choosing Table ), and a side (say, the GM).

    You should now have a window named "A Bucket of Dice Controls". Click on the azure d6 button and the pane full of dice should open.
    Now click on the "Table" button on the right of the die button: the Table Window appears.

    Drag and Drop dice from the palette in the first window to the table. You can also select some of the dice on the table with your mouse and Clone them (with Ctrl-C) to speed up the process if you have some very large pool.

    Then select the same way, Right-Click and choose "Roll" (or CTRL-R).

    Should be all

    (yeah, I know, the GUI of Vassal is counterintuitive and rather ugly... but it works and has loads of boardgames/wargames for those interested... I never tried them, but I know people play WH40K with this)
