    \n What Jason said, basically. I'm seeing inbound links primarily from the PA site directly, along with an increase in visits coming from search engines (possibly because of the initial problem with Tycho's link). Then a lot of smaller bunches of traffic from local forums and listservs, where folks are echoing the link from PA. Those are great to see because some of the forums are focused on different gaming traditions (Shadowrun, for example) and yet the posters all sound really enthusiastic about playing our game.

    Normally our daily traffic is something like 100-200 visits, and yesterday it jumped to around 3500. Again, not a "Slashdot effect" situation, but still really exciting. We've seen an increase in sales as well, but the real value is having new folks talking about our game.

    I'm also glad to see that Jerry at least has had a chance to look over the book, and I hope he gets a chance to play it. Big thanks to you (Sage), Ryan, Joe and the others who helped bring Fiasco to PAX!

    \n A bit of a spike, yeah- mostly in terms of the print book. In the first part of October we'd sold 5 copies of the book at IPR and about 18 copies of the PDF through various Drivethrustuff storefronts. Since yesterday we've sold 14 copies of the book and 6 copies of the PDF in those same channels. So not much of an increase on electronic sales, but definitely on the print edition. Most likely that's because the "buy" links on our site go to the print product on IPR.

    Edit: My numbers there are a bit off, since I wasn't including IPR sales of PDFs: of those, we'd sold 9 in the first part of October and another 25 in the last two days. Now I'm starting to wonder if our PDF is under-priced...
