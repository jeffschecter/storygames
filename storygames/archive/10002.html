\n My game, The Dance and The Dawn, is finally coming out at this year, and will be showing up at the Design Matters booth!

In brief: "This game tells a fable of three Ladies who came upon the Ice Queen\u2019s court and danced with four Lords, all in the pursuit of the One True Love that could yield happiness. The conclusion of that fable is up to you." It's draws upon the symbolism of the chessboard to create the characters and the world. (More info: what the game is like and how it came to be.)

Here's an example of a character from the game. I'm hoping you can play along with the thread and try it out.

...

Lady Akal, formerly a Pirate Queen. Her chess piece is the black knight, and she is likely to be willful, driven, and reckless.

"In sashes and six seashells she's bejeweled;
each taken from the lands her bandits ruled."

* I once was a Queen of the Most Ferocious Pirates.
* I loved my horde of gold and gems.
* I lost my prized ship, the Windstar Prestige, to the royal navy.
* I wish to take back the seas.

This is what we know about her. If you had created her character, you might know more about her than this, but we're probably going to learn the most about her in play.

It is the second dance of the night, and she is dancing with Lord Jyn, who is represented by the white bishop. Right now, all we know is that Jyn is scholar, and is quite driven in his pursuit of knowledge. What we really want to find out as much as possible about Lord Jyn. Is he the key to finding a happy ending, or is there something darker lurking within him?

...

How this thread works: Lord Jyn (myself) will ask a question, and anyone can answer (in the voice of Lady Akal). If he likes your answer, Lady Akal may ask a question (provided by anyone on this thread).

Shall we dance?
