\n The idea behind Blowback is simple: it's about blacklisted spies stuck in your hometown.The game's heavy on the planning, preparation, and ability to be badass-- but really hits home the idea that the things that make someone a good international spy probably make them a really lousy friend. In short: taking down a virulent terrorist cell from the inside is easy, it's not disappointing your girlfriend that's hard.



I'm doing something different with this game, in that the document linked above only contains character creation and the first phase of a job. I need to know the rough amount of dice that the system would output, as well as any other problems, before I can commit the design for the second phase to paper. I'm looking for playtesters willing to play via IRC, Skype, or both, who'd be willing to play the game in phases. Playing online is slower anyway, so I think it'd be easy to get a good session out of the first phase alone. I've got all the procedures for the second phase mucking about in my brain, but until I know what things look like from the first part, I can't plug the numbers and the advice into the chapter. I want to make sure all the component parts work individually, so that it's easier to track problems to their source.

If you're interested, let me know! I missed GenCon this year so I'm itching to play with those of you I don't see regularly anyway. And even if you're not interested in online play, if the game strikes your fancy I'd be happy to answer your questions and listen to your thoughts.

    \n You mean, because you can't do something Without Incident?

    If you've got zero in something, you can't do it at all. If you have one in something, you have to Go For Broke. If you succeed with one die, you automatically get one Momentum, which means two dice when you roll next, etc. So it's actually easier to get Momentum when you've only got one die; if you've got a two in something, any time you roll one fail and one success you're not going to be gaining any Momentum. That's sort of what I was going for-- when you're not super good at something but you do it, you put your all into it, and the results are a little more wild and a little less consistent than if you're decent at something.
