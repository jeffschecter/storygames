\n I'm picturing a game where everyone sits around pretending to be people sitting around (wait, it gets better!) at the London Explorer's Club and detailing their daring exploits. It would be a collaborative storytelling game of BS and oneupsmanship, with each story getting more delighfully outlandish. Perhaps something like if Monty Python's Four Yorkshiremen skit was done by Sir Edmund Hillary, Nellie Bly, HM Stanley and Marco Polo.

Does that exist? Because I'd like to play it, please.

    \n[Woops, cross-posted with Mike and Andy.]

    I've played an intensely funny session of The Committee for the Expolration of Mysteries a couple of years ago (it's also on The Unstore in various formats).

    Basically, if I remember correctly, all the players interpret members of said committee, who just came back from an expedition and that gather to recount to the other members what happened. I remember that we were set to start our voyage from London to arrive somewhere in Africa, and we started from London to arrive somewhere in France. By air-balloon. While chased by members of an adversary committee on a Zeppelin. During a thunderstorm.

    It was crazy fun; if that's the kind of weird, showy and over-the-top stories you're looking for, I think you should check this game.

    \n Yes, I think you will enjoy Baron Munchausen. It still remains one of my favorite role playing games because it got people that would never roleplay to do so, where even something like Fiasco did not appeal.

    Here's a good house-rule: when someone shouts "Drink!" everyone drinks. Only provide enough libations so that everyone can be moderately drunk and no more. The game goes off the rails if someone gets too drunk.

    It might not be as important for your scenario but for the standard setting, coming up with suitably pompous and facetious titles was a very important part of the game, and gets everyone into the mood quicker than anything.
