\n I made something, so I'm figuring this is the place to put it. Do feel free to correct me if I'm wrong on that.

This wasn't made for the 200 Word RPG Challenge, although it was inspired by it. Not sure what I'll do with it (if anything), but I'd welcome any feedback, reactions, brickbats, anything else.

I've tested the card mechanic just a bit and it seems to be not broken, but I'd be interested in anyone else's perspective. Not sure "family" is the right container / theme either, but seems to work so far.

Anyway, thanks!
----------
Standard 54-card deck. Red is loving. Black is dark.

Shuffle face cards into their own deck -- the characters. (Other cards are events.) Turn two cards up: First, the head of the family. Second, their child.

Deal each player three event cards.

Tell the story of the family -- an oil dynasty, a fantasy kingdom, your suburban neighbors, whatever -- as follows:

On your turn, play a card from your hand to a family member, as long as it's at least as high as the highest event card already present there. If red, describe a loving event involving that character and another. If black, a dark one. Say why. Higher numbers means higher stakes.

Play an Ace to kill any character.

Play a Joker to discard all event cards from any character. Shuffle the Joker back in. Play again.

If you can't play, expose a new character, if any remain. Narrate their connection (dark or loving) to the family, then draw three cards.

For events, a change of color may be an upheaval. A change of suit in the same color may be a change in direction.

Play until events run out. Did dark characters meet dark ends? Did loving characters remain so?

    \n First of all I loved this game when I've read it.

    I'm very bad at remembering the name of suits so I usually got puzzled when a game uses them for different game aspects. L&D mainly use the colors, so i've found this very player friendly.

    We've tried the game out and it rocked.

    The picture of the face cards can give some inspiration for the character, which is good, because 'improvise something!' is usually a hard task.

    Also I think the mechanics are very suitable for hacking.

    We had a few questions though:
    1. When you expose a new character, their connection is based on the color of it's card or shall the player immediately put an event card next to it to do that? Is exposing a 'full action' in your turn?
    2. Can you introduce 'NPCs' without exposing a face card?

    \n Thanks for checking things out! Really interested to see any hacks.

    The mechanics are ones that have been kicking around in my head for some time and have made it into a couple of draft designs. The nice thing about doing something in 200 words is that it becomes a "finished" design very quickly and is easy to get out there. This may well become something more elaborate at some point, who knows...

    hamnacb, that's great to hear that you had a good time with it. Because the rules have to fit in 200 words there's obviously going to be some corner-cutting. My general assumption is that you don't do anything the rules don't say, so in exposing a new character you'd narrate their connection, draw three cards, but then *not* play one of those three. The connection could be based on the new character's color (that's how I've been rolling), but the rules don't explicitly say that.

    As to your #2, I don't think you could intro a new character without exposing a face card. Another thing the rules don't explicitly say is that the new cards don't have to be members of the family, they just have to be connected to a character already in play.

    Jeff_B_Slater, really interested to hear any questions or see any hacks you come up with. Thanks for looking in!

    One thing I'm interested to explore is whether "family" is the right theme for this. Not sure yet, but I haven't really done a deep dive on it. In a more fully fleshed-out design (i.e., one that doesn't have to fit into 200 words) a lot more might be possible.
