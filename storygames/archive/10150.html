\n This was the first time session for myself, Dave and Tony and the first RPGing we had done as a group since the end of our Hot War campaign; we were using the Alpha Playtest Kit (07.12.2009)
\n
\nMyself & Dave opted to select our characters at random; I played a Robot Commando called 'Captain Awesome!' and Dave was an Atomic Professor called 'Dr. What?', hence our tagline: "Awesome!", "What?", "Awesome!", "What?", etc.
\n
\nThe opening scene had giant insects threatening to overwhelm a Rocket City power plant and endangering traffic surrounding it; in the 'Previously Ons...', I added rescuing Princess Adastra from an exploding pesticide factory, while Dave had defused a mysterious doomsday weapon by cutting a red wire...
\n
\nAs the story unfolded, it turned out that Princess Adastra's father, King Stellios of Venus, a home appliance mogul, had been kidnapped by unknown agents and that an antropologist colleague of Dr. What's was also missing... though it turned out that Adastra had abducted her father and was working in collusion with the anthropologist, using a super-pheromone concoction to control the giant insects!
\n
\nDave and I shared another action scene, where we infiltrated an underground base hidden beneath the destroyed pesticide factory, which gave us some vital leads; after the next interlude, Captain Awesome was in a basement with two identical looking lab assistants trying to decide which one was the fake, while Dr. What tried to find the plane that had dropped a pheromone bomb on the power plant. It turned out that both the lab assistants were fakes (they were really ninja cyborg human-ant transformers) and that Dr. What had stumbled into a nest of Stygian Cult Assassins!
\n
\nWe all thorughly enjoyed the whole game and the control we had over the narrative worked perfectly, meshing well with the action scenes that followed the interlude scenes where we planted seeds of the story.
\n
\nOne issue I would note is that, due to our ability selections (I picked Damage Control Systems and Covering Fire, Dave chose Force Field and Studied the Schematics) we were nearly invulnerable to harm, no matter how many dnager dice we picked for up each roll; we both finished the session in the 'Vapourised' section of the Danger track, but the highrst hits we had were in 'Bashed' and we each had 6 power tokens! It felt like levlling up was almost going to be a formality, with no real feeling that we were in any danger of losing. Of course, this could be largely due to the fact that there were only two of us and we had picked quite passive, defensive abilities.
