    \n Thank you for this Vincent\u2665

    I ran the game for the first time last spring and it was a great game and we used another setting and another book of laws (supernatural/spiritual to the same extent as DitV but with a different moral code \u2014 pages 16\u201317 in Fortunes and Fates in the D&D2e Land of Fate box set). Things forbidden were things like taking slaves, public drunkenness, and religious prohibitions such as specific forms of worship being mandatory and others forbidden. So we got away from the sex&gender stuff. That isn't meant as a slight to anyone who has managed to create poignant and meaningful stories in DitV about those themes. We did this much more for the reason that our DitV game was an alternate weekday side story to another campaign than for any problems we would have with DitV itself. It also just happened to be easier for us, we have some weird group dynamic in that regard sometimes

    \n It's a bit ironic how so many people have throughout the years adapted the game to avoid playing in the original setting and now it gets pulled because of the setting. But I think I remember Vincent also criticizing DitV for having too many dice and a sequence of mechanics that didn't fit so well with the fiction.

    I love the game, I feel sad for a friend that probably will never be able to buy it, but I can understand how it may be difficult to go back to it for a new edition.

    By the way, our adaptation was for Judge Dredd and it worked beautifully. Thank you for a great game, Vincent.
