    \n Players play minor demons responsible for the smooth operation of Hell.

    GM plays their dark overlord and any necessary captive souls, as well as flipping the Attributes.

    The whole group collaboratively invents a Hell composed of 10 Attributes. Each Attribute must include WHY it makes this place Hellish, and also WHAT it is, concretely, in Hell's physical set-up.

    Play starts with the demons showing us their average day. Once the GM's ready, they interrupt by flipping an Attribute to its opposite. This means inventing a replacement attribute that's the opposite of the original. The lakes of blistering fire suddenly become lakes of soothing lotion. The demons flip out and discuss what this means for the damned souls and how this will ruin things. The dark overlord then calls them in to menace them and ask what they'll do about this.

    Once the demons have addressed it a bit, the GM flips the next Attribute.

    Continue play either until all 10 are flipped, or until Hell clearly isn't Hell any more and the demons give up.
