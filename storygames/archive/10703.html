Which of these are the least interesting?

\n
\n
\n
\n\nJason_Morningstar \n\n\n
\n
\n\nOctober 2009 \n edited October 2009 in Game Design Help
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n You are playing a dying cowboy on one last, epic ride. You've got some keepsakes with you, and otherwise you've got nothing but a sway-backed old nag, your hat and boots. You pick two keepsakes from the list, and they have a powerful effect, both mechanically and on the narrative. Which two of these are the least interesting to you as a player? I only have room for ten and need to cut a couple. What do I cut?

Grouch Bag: You\u2019ve got quite a little something put away in a dried bull\u2019s scrotum from Mexico, probably a mix of gold dollars, yellowbacks and Spanish silver. Who knew you were rich?

Conversation Piece: You keep it in a plain tin case - a queer little thing you picked up on your travels. You don\u2019t show it off much, it being so passing strange, but you always figured it\u2019d come in handy someways.

Barking Irons: A sweeter pair of pistols Colonel Colt never manufactured; these guns have seen you through. They are killing pieces and that is a proven fact.

Duds: Either a rifle coat, ten gallon hat, and boots or a decent sack coat, bowler and blood-red silk cravat. If you dress like a buckaroo people\u2019ll take you seriously. If you dress flash people\u2019ll figure you\u2019re either a banker or a vampire, but the ladies\u2019ll swoon either way.

Friends: You know people everywhere. Some of your friends might be Indians. Some might be bluestockings or less reputable ladies. All of them will help you in a pinch.

Daguerrotype: When you look at her picture, it makes you damn stubborn. If you\u2019re going to die you\u2019ll say when. Nothing\u2019s dropping you until you feel like dropping. Then you\u2019ll be together again.

Rabbit\u2019s Foot: It\u2019s a known fact that you are one fortunate son of a bitch, your current situation excepted. One dirty charm is to blame for your abundance of luck.

Tin Star: It meant something once, back in the mean old days when every polecat wanted to prove something at your expense and you were burying \u2018em more or less daily. Now, maybe not so much. But who knows?

Mine Crate: You panned out your pile with nothing to show for it but a mangy box full of blasting caps, sticks of dynamite weeping nitroglycerin, and sheafs of worthless stock certificates. Oh, well.

Book: It\u2019s probably a family bible but maybe not. Maybe it\u2019s a McGuffy Reader or a racy Spanish novel with pictures. Maybe it\u2019s the dictionary you used to settle bets up in Leadville. Maybe it\u2019s just a hollowed out container for something else.

President Cleveland: A finer range horse you will never find \u2013 sure footed, fast, smart as a whip and tough as a camel. You and the President have been to hell and beyond together, and it won\u2019t take two whoops and a holler before you\u2019ll ride him there again.

Scatter-Gun. A break-barrel Rossi, a gift from a better man in better times. The words carved on the stock give you comfort, and the notches on the other side give you a measure of respect - from a certain class of person, at least.
