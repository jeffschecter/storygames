    \n Just speaking from my experience, I know a lot of non-gamers who work in the video game business. But the non-gamers tend to be either non-creative (marketing, sales, etc.) or execs. The vast majority of engineers, artists, producers, designers and QA I know are "gamers" of one sort or another. And the interesting thing is that if you talk to them, you get a diverse set of game types and genres that they enjoy. A historical miniatures gamer gets to trade notes with a casual bubble popper gamer and a Starcraft or WOW gamer.

    I don't know many people who make games recreationally. But my guess would be that most of them are gamers of some sort. It's the passion for gaming that drives most creatives to make games.
