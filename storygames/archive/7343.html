    \n There's an absurdly fun improv game called sit/stand/lean - a three player game where someone must be in one of those three positions at all times. The goal is to make natural transitions and maintain the scene, so if somebody gets up, you must sit down, or maybe lean and force the stander to sit.

    I can see many uses for this, as a way to signal, as a way to show status or dominance, as a way to resolve conflicts.

    These are hopefully some little picture ideas for a "standing" blacktop game.
    I'm thinking players would cover their eyes once they're in the center circle or square(home), then when called apon, they'd move to a different place in that outlined area.
    ...or they'd just place a colored stone into the area they chose.
    I see each circle or square as representing something.
    Say you have four squares, one of those four squares represents something different than any other person's square.
    Maybe each one represents a element? or maybe an animal, or maybe part of a story.
    Then in that particular square or circle, each position has further meaning.
