\n Do y'all know the story of the WWII battleship Yamato? Towards the end of WWII, the Japanese built the two largest battleships (with the largest gun turrets) ever made: the Yamato, and her sister, the Musashi. But as the war progressed, it became apparent that the war wasn't going to be decided on the sea, but rather in the air. And it also became apparent that the United States was likely to win.

So on April 1st, 1945, the Japanese--in a show of pride and desperation--sent the Yamato on a daring suicide mission. The Yamato was to make a full frontal assault on the American navy positioned off the coast of Okinawa. The Yamato was to beach iself on the coast, effectively turning itself into an unsinkable gun turret.

But six days into its mission, while it was still over 200 miles from its destination, the Yamato was sunk by US aircraft. The Yamato was sacrificed, with nothing gained.
_________

For some reason I find this story very compelling. Awhile ago I got the idea to turn this into a game.

In the year 2345, the global government of Earth is at war with the United Galactic Colonies (or maybe some alien race, whatever). The government of Earth is losing, so it launches its one last hope of victory: the cosmic battleship Yamato, the largest cosmic battleship ever made. The plan is for the Yamato to enter enemy space and fire its uber-weapon, destroying the enemy homeworld (or whatever). But the trip through enemey territory won't be easy...

The players would play the officers of the escort ships sent to protect the Yamato. I decided to steal Andy's idea behind his game Tactica. The game will involve two kinds of scenes: Dramatic scenes and battle scenes. The drama scenes would involve your standard role-playing fare. The battle scenes, though, would involve full-on mini boardgame action. I'm not sure how Andy wanted his game to work, but mine would work like this: The drama scenes would fuel the battle scenes, and the battle scenes the drama scenes. But there's a twist...

In the drama scenes, various elements (like relationships, etc.) would be put at risk in order to fuel the battle scenes, via "action points". In the battle scenes, players would use the action points to do cool stuff to fend off enemy craft. But during the battle scenes, players would not only control their own ships, but would also share control of enemy craft (the game would be GM-less).

So here's the deal, you would receive "drama points" for inflicting damage as an enemy craft. Drama points would be used to further the drama elements, further the Yamato's mission, or possibly heal damage in general. And here's the twist with damage---the player who receives damage gets to decide how it's applied---towards his ship, his drama stuff, or the Yamato itself.

Are you seeing where this is going? People are always going to receive damage, but they get to decide how its applied, and how healing points are spents. What gets sacrificed and what gets spared?

The game would end when: 1) All the players are killed, 2) the Yamato is destroyed, or 3) the Yamato fulfills its mission (aka you rack up a certain number of points).
_______________

What do y'all think about the idea?
Help me think up some rules!

Thanks!

PS---I know there's an anime called Space Battle ship Yamato, there's no relation other than the influence of the real life ship.

    \n Random game ideas:

    --Drama points and damage points are cumulative for individual elements (drama stuff), respectively. Each element will have a maximum number of drama/damage points that it can take. If it reaches its maximum number of drama points first (that is, before damage is maxed out), then that element is "spared" or resolved (generally a happy outcome). If it maxes out on damage first, that element is destroyed (generally a negative outcome). I'm not sure if the maximums will be the same for all elements, or if some elements will be able to take more than others.

    --The Yamato, similarly, will have a max number of damage & drama points it can take. Drama points means success for the game. Damage means the game ends. The max numbers for the Yamato will be much greater than drama stuff.

    --Individual player ships will certainly have a max number of damage. I'm not sure what drama points would be good for, maybe repairing the ship (removing the ship's damage)?

    --When a player receives damage, they decide where the damage is applied (the Yamato, his ship, his drama stuff), but one of the *other players* narrates the outcome. Maybe the player that inflicts the damage gets to narrate? Narration can either occur immediately or during the next drama sequence.
