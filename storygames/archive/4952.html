    \n And those fliers in my hand? They say...\n
    BAN THE COLLECTIVE ENDEAVOUR!

    We ask for your support against the Collective Endeavour, who are flooding the market with cheap roleplaying games.

    Their games are a threat to established games we all love. Some of these games:

    o\tDo not have a GM.
    o\tAre about non-traditional subjects like wrestling and girlfriends.
    o\tAre glossy and use art to make them look \u201cprofessional\u201d.

    Yes, these games might be \u201cfun\u201d. But they ignore all the traditional roleplaying game values. They must be stopped.

    We also ask for your support against their roleplaying \u201cguide\u201d, Play Unsafe. This book claims it can make your roleplaying \u201cbetter\u201d. Please take a stand against this book with us.

    Help us stop them. We can do it together.

    Gamers Against The Collective Endeavour

    \n All of them together. And, yes, I think people got it.

    So, look, I know this sounds really silly. But here's the thing.

    Dragonmeet is the biggest London convention of the year. It opens at 10am and people queue from 9.30am. So, for half an hour on a cold winter morning, people are waiting in line.

    And nobody fliers that queue.

    I mean, come on. There's people. Standing there. And they're bored.

    So we knew we had to give fliers out. But we had to do it it a silly way, because people like being entertained. So we did a mock protest.

    And, well, we stood there with placards, and put fliers in their hands. And it was all rather silly, of course, but the fliers did point out that the games were a. cheap and b. fun.

    And people seemed rather receptive. They laughed as they read the fliers. We introduced ourselves and said "Will you support our campaign?" and "What are you here to play?". And when they said "Call of Cthulhu" we said "Great. That's just the kind of traditional game we support".

    Then we tore apart a copy of Contenders and everyone cheered.

    Graham

Anyway, I didn't realize that the store where I purchase my devil worshipping material is the ones who organise Dragonmeet and when I heard about the special guests, I was kicking myself for not having attended. I'm definitely going next year and if there are any more "special guests" like the ones that attended this year, I'll give them a piece of my mind. How dare they have better ideas than I do. What gives them then right to release quality prooducts that makes my forthcoming effort look like something squeezed out of lanced boil.
