\n Anima Prime is going to include rules for creating, running, and engaging factions. I'm thinking that the rules should be easily portable to other games, too, with a few minor modifications. I'm not going to include them in the alpha draft, because I want to test the regular rules thoroughly first before I add these. But I'm still working on this in parallel--that's just how my hopping mind works.

I've written up the first draft of the faction rules and thought I'd run it by you guys. They'll be much harder to playtest, because they're supposed to be long-term additions to the character level, not just played on their own in isolation. Though I guess they could, in a way.

Anyway, here's the ten-page draft:

AP Factions

Right now, I'm wondering:
1. Do the rules make sense without the AP rules? (That's important for feedback gathering.) If not, AP rules are here.
2. Is there any immediate flaw in the economy? For example, costs of engaging v. costs of developing factions etc.?
3. Does this seem too complex (or too simple) for its purpose?
4. Does the tie-in with goals and characters make sense?
