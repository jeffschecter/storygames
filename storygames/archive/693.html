    bq. Judson, what do you mean, an anthology rather than an index? What I'm thinking of doing is a wiki consisting mainly of links: not republishing the information but linking to it in some way. Is this "not cool"?

    What you're proposing is an index. My experience with wiki information collections of all kinds is that indices fall off my radar rapidly. That's what "del.icio.us":http://del.icio.us is for. I'd happily subscribe to the RSS feed from http://del.icio.us/graham_walmsley/rpg+publication.

    By an anthology, I mean exactly what you're proposing _not_ to do: republication. Especially on a wiki that leads, in my experience, to a much more heavily commented and cross linked site, and will generally be more useful with less investment of energy by the supplicant.

    Is it "not cool"? In the sense of bad form, or evil, no, it's not "not cool." In sense of lame, though, yes, it's not cool. For myself, I will turn from a collection of links to disparate forum threads to Google and never come back. A coherent document, with threads summarized into articles, and cross referenced as appropriate, I'll link to and come back to and contribute what little I know and as much formatting and linking as I can.

    I understand and laud the motivation of respecting people's rights to their publications on the Internet, even in public fora. I'd respect including links to materials whose authors hadn't granted republication rights. But I think that adding an off-site link to information that might reasonably be included as an article should be accompanied by a message to the original author requesting the right to republish.

    But hey, this is your idea and you're already investing into chasing it down. And right now, I'm just shouting criticism from the sidelines. Feel free to ignore me -- I just have ideas about how I'd like a publication wiki to be run, but it's your thing. I do have a marginal experience with wikis, and I will volunteer that expertise and some editorial time though, rather than just kibitz.

    4. However, it takes me an hour of clicking to find the best resources. A completely fantastic resource is Matt Snyder's PDF publishing, itself a PDF file, but it takes me ages to find that.

    Now, last night, I spent a couple of hours going through those PDF threads: the ones that, if I knew nothing about the subject, I'd want someone to point me towards. I compiled what I thought were the best resources into a Wiki page:
