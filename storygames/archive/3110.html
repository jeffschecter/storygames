MASTER EVENT LIST

DATE: Every Monday at 6:30pm
LOCATION: Metro Seattle Gamers clubhouse, 1600 W. Armory Way, Suite C-106, Seattle, WA
EVENT NAME: SGA Monday Night Short-Shots
QUICK DESCRIPTION: Seattle Gamers Assemble! (SGA) is a loose community of role-players centered around Seattle, Washington. SGA holds weekly meetings where members take turns running one-off or short-run games in indie, oldie, or oddball systems. Although GMs/hosts are not required to run story games, the vast majority of our games in the last several months have been SGs.
LINKS: the SGA Gaming Night Archive lists past and planned games; the wiki provides articles and information on SGA; the mailing list provides the on-going conversation.

    \n DATE: Saturday, July 14th, 4pm
    LOCATION: The Ennead, Portland, OR (email jake@atarashigames.com for directions)
    EVENT NAME GO PLAY! SE PORTLAND #5
    QUICK DESCRIPTION: This is Portland's monthly indie gaming event. Gamers from around Portland gather at the Ennead (home of Jake Richmond, Matt Schlotte, Charles Seaton and others) for dinner and gaming. We usually facilitate 2-4 games, with an average turnout of 12-16 players. This is a small local event, but we have a lot of room and are encouraging players from out of town to join us. The evening often includes pre-game sushi, a field trip to local store Empire Games and after game discussion. If you plan on being in Portland on July 14th make it a point to attend!

    Portland Indie Games Yahoo Group

      Here's the seminar I'm running:\n
        How To Have Fun with Indie RPGs
        Saturday the 14th, 3:00 p.m.-4:00 p.m.

        What's an ashcan? What's "actual play"? It's an RPG without a GM? Like other indie communities (music, comics, etc.), the creator-owned, self-published ("indie") RPG scene is both influential and sometimes impenetrable.
        In this seminar, Paul Czege, lifetime Michigander and the designer and publisher of the Diana Jones Award Winning RPG, My Life with Master, will explode the arcana of the community, the dynamics of its convention scene, and give you insights into selecting and finding fun with the unconventional games that are secretly influencing the traditional RPGs you're currently playing.

        In a Wicked Age
        Saturday the 14th, 5:00 p.m.-9:00 p.m.

        Pulp sword-and-sorcery roleplaying. The In A Wicked Age game system creates character arcs that feel like stories in a shared-world anthology. There will also be a IRC chat room available to act as a real time lobby for help and pick up games.
