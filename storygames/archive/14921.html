    \n Well, fair enough, I suppose.

    For me, though, I can't see a whole lot of "game of ambition and poor impulse control" in there. The characters are brave, intellectual, and accomplish great deeds while wrapped up in world-wide events outside their control. There's different timelines and descendants and ancestry and technological challenges and war and romance, while traveling throughout the world and visiting almost every continent. That said, certain events or moments (like the Bundok Site) certainly do turn into fiascos... the overall picture, though, I don't get that as a fiasco.

    Anyway, it's not terribly important. It just jumped out at me as one example that really didn't match the others, leading to some head-scratching.

    Now, the King of the Vagabonds as a Fiasco character, that I can get behind!
