    \n I could see using something like this, although it'd need a bit more bolstering in the mythological department to carry a gravity equivalent of the knights with their doom-filled fate and tangled relationship with the queen, king, Solaris knight, etc.

    I'm not sure I like having a choice of character class for chargen purposes, as it would rather strongly focus a player's attention on this particular matter; he would no longer be thinking of who his character is, but only whether he should be this class or that. With just one class of characters the class fades into the background and emphasizes the personal differences between the characters, while a Sun-monk would first and foremost be a Sun-monk.

    I know that this wouldn't work for most, but I could see using a few special character classes with a randomized class selection of some sort: roll a '1' and your character is a Sun-monk instead of a star-knight, something like that. I'd perhaps like it even more if this procedure was followed at the end of character creation instead of the more obvious beginning: first choose what your character is about, then find out what sort of background organization he hails from.
