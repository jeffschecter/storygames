\nOk, so the new store has been up for a while. Anyone here have some games and the companies that make them that would fit this bill?

    \n I don't know how obscure these are, but both are small-press/self-published games. I haven't played it, but it has received really good reviews (and I will be reviewing it soon), a game called GUBS about small creatures in a fairy-like setting. Very cute and humorous, fast card game. There also is Vampires' Quest by Headless Games. I just finished a review which will be posted in the next day or two. You are competing Vampires trying to claim victims. The artwork is very old-skool Dracula movie and the gameplay is pretty good too. You might check those out.

    If I think of any more I will post them. Hope that helps.

    \n It would be impossible to license, but Clash of the Lightsabers is sort of a forgotten (except by its fans) gem. A random licensing tie-in like that is not where you'd expect to find a decent little game.

    Here's an odd idea: no one (as far as I can tell), produces a French Suited Tarot deck using English designs/letters for the royal cards. These are used for point-trick games games such as French Tarot.

    Edit: Oh, and there's Mystick Domination (also see), which was a tarot-flavored collectible deck game, which alas went under. There were rumors that the rights to it have been taken over by FFG.

    \n There's a Japanese game publisher called Adventure Planning Service that has put out two card games, Meikyuu Conquest and Make You Gunfighters. I translated the text of the newest version of Meikyuu Conquest, and it looks like a fun little game.

    \n Make You Gunfighter is hella fun in the category of completely mindless filler where a signficant part of the joy comes from the requirement (actually in the rules) to drum your hands on the table to simulate a galloping sound when your gunfighter moves from one location to the next and the even greater requirement (not actually in the rules) to shout "BAN!" and make little finger guns as you gleefully gun down the other players (because "Ban" is apparently how the Japanese write "Bang" in Engrish).

    \n Hi David,
    \n
    \nYep, heard of I Drank What? We printed it for them and we sell it in our store. Largely seems to appeal to the gothic/alternative crowd thus far, but I see the game as having broader appeal than that. Temporarily out of stock (not something that normally happens since we POD print it), waiting on some newly revised rules they will be introducing on the new run.
    \n
    \nI hadn't heard of the new game yet, but sounds goods. We do have all the traditional games on stock (Zombieis, the Munchkin lines and other Steve Jackson card games, slugfest titles, Looney Labs, etc) and are striving to carry everything older we can get get our hands on. Like we picked up several cases of KODT Hack, Gother Than Thou, Abduction, Employee of the Month and so on.
    \n
    \nOh, and the newest cool little game we're printing and will have available for sale in a couple days, "Pixel Lincoln". We also have MMA Fight Night, Revenge of the B-Movie and a few others. We're working with a few of the manufacturers who printed cards with us this summer but used their own packaging to adapt versions where we can print and sell them using packaging available from our POD options. So over the next couple months we'll be bringing more indie card games in store and making them available on our new e-commerce site as well.
    \n
    \nI, of course, am looking for more. When someone ventures into our store or into the card games section on our website, I want the shier volume of unique card games available to stun the consumer with its vastness. That is essentially going to mean carrying just about every card game I can get my hands on, be they sold through our distributor, printed by our POD service, bought direct from the manufacturer and perhaps in some cases, buying old out of print IPs to rerelease them ourselves.
