    \n You reccord it in pie segments, and heal it in pie segments. So if you take two harm, you fill in two wedges. You heal to harm, you erase two wedges. The last three wedges have special effects that are noted in the rules, they make stuff harder for you. It's really just a thematic way of saying you have six health boxes/HP/Wounds.

    \n Just like Captain Thark said : you have six health level. Each harm suffered, you check out one health level. The first two levels are just bruises and flesh wounds and will recover with time on their own, health level n#3 will stay as is if you do nothing, and levels 4-6 will worsen without care (blood loss, infection, the work \u2014\xa0ie. the MC can use "hey, you know what ? Blood loss. Massive blood loss. Check out one more health segment" as a move).

    One more thing: does that mean that being hit by a knock on your face 6 times in a row mean you're dead? (harm+1 each)

    Yup.

    Well, if you do nothing and stand there while some guy \u2014 probably named Rolfball \u2014\xa0pounces your pretty uncovered face six times in a row, that is. I'm pretty sure that'd be pretty fatal in real life too (and probably qualify as a suicide).

    Most fights ends well before that, thanks to the way violence is handled in AW.

    Also, when you're supposed to fill any harm past 9 o'clock, you can always keep your clock at 9 and take a debility instead. So technically, somebody has to dock your pretty uncovered face 10 times for you to die.

    \n It was actually not used by the armed forces, which uses DefCon, but by the Bulletin of the Atomic Scientists at the University of Chicago. It's pretty cool.

    Also, the Homeland Security Advisory System ("threat level red, etc") was replaced by the National Terrorism Advisory System, which identifies threats to the U.S. as either Elevated or Imminent but doesn't have a general "atmosphere" reading associated with it.

    Also also, DefCon didn't technically measure how likely we were to be destroyed, but instead how ready/alert the US armed forces were ordered to be for action. In the days of mutual assured destruction (Eisenhower, Reagan), this was almost the same thing with respect to DefCon 1 ("nuclear war imminent") but during times with other strategic setups (Kennedy, Bush, today) it was not. Also, today, DefCon is only one of several Alert Conditions (LertCons) that measure things more specifically. So Force Protection Conditions (FPCON) measures threats against military installations and materiel and controls how secure they have to be (goes from FPCON NORMAL to FPCON ALPHA, BRAVO, etc.) and each unit in the armed forces have their own Readiness Condition (REDCON) that goes from 1 (ready to fight) to 4 (minimum alert).

    \n It's a trick on your eyes, I think.

    Without the clock face, you have basically six hit points.

    With the clock face, you basically have six hit points, but you also have a handle on how AW can treat the mostly mechanically equivalent bits of harm as in-your-head different. Looking at the filled out clock-spaces gives you a different oh-shit metric than the numeric value of your current suffered harm does.

    Not that AW relies that hard on that - I've played online games with numeric harm and it went swimmingly.
