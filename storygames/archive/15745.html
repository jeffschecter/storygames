    \n The preview of the core rules has me excited and hopeful. I thought Smallville had a lot of brilliant ideas but 3 of my gaming groups couldn't get past the text and found the rule books hard to use. Thankfully Leverage was much improved. And now looking at this preview, the text seems even clearer and easier to use! So great news! I was frustrated by Smallville because I thought it was a good game but couldn't convince others. I don't see that problem happening here.

    Also exciting, in the preview there is a side bar that says you will need:

    "A different kind of token, playing card, action figure, or index card to show when you\u2019ve taken your action"

    I'm curious to see how this plays out in the rules.

    And the instructions recommend using:

    Cheat sheets for the Watcher and players (downloaded from www.margaretweis.com

    I hope that means when the game launches cheat sheets will be available. In general I find cheat sheets invaluable when teaching new players how to play or persuading existing RPG players on giving a new game a try.

    \nI used to be a pretty big comic book fan myself, but most of my collection is in the form of Vertigo TPB collections.

    I'm not sure how I feel about Civil War yet. I've only just scratched the surface, but so far it looks promising. At the very least, I'm intrigued by the premise and am interested by the intersection of super heroes and politics.

    As for big events, I guess I'm neutral. I like to think that I prefer more "focused" stories, but I loved Secret Wars and Kingdom Come, which were both big events.

    I really enjoyed The Ultimates (for the first volume or two anyway) and have been digging the new Marvel movies, so I'd say The Avengers are my team of choice. I'm particularly partial to Thor. (Which may be the only Marvel comic I'm close to "up to date" on.)

    I can't think of any characters or teams that I DISLIKE. Anyone can be interesting when written well. However, I'm getting sort of bored of the X-Men.

    \n\nCamBanks \n\n\n
    \n
    \n\nFebruary 2012 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n Price points for books:

    Basic Game and all Event Supplements: $20, trade paperback
    Essentials Edition Event Books: $30, hardcover
    Premium Edition Event Books: $40, hardcover

    Datafiles will be printed in the book as 2-page spreads. We'll also be providing the blank datafile as a PDF for free together with the cheat sheets and some other material. PDFs are fully linked, but don't have any fancy audio or video stuff... this time around.

    We've shifted the dates on some of the later books; you can probably expect the Civil War Event Book to hit the market at the end of April. That's on account of needing to push harder on the Basic Game and move the CW book into Q2. Annihilation will be a Q3 event lineup, and Age of Apocalypse is Q4.

    Cheers,
    Cam
