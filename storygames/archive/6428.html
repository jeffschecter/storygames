\n Alright, this is it. I've not only completed my work on the full beta version of Anima Prime, I've also transfered the content to a drupal-based web site for direct online reading and discussion. And when I say "full beta version," I am talking about the complete game. It's over twice as long as the full Beast Hunters text (over 52,000 words--or in Mark Causey's words, "Holy crap it's huge!"). It's all Creative Commons, so feel free to share it all around. There's no art at this point, obviously, except for a sword graphic or two that I made in MS Paint (I kid you not). The game's laid out with page numbering, section indicators, index, etc.

For those who haven't heard about it, Anima Prime is my anime-inspired group RPG of spontaneous fun. The influences range from anime series (good ones like Avatar) to video games (most noticeably the Final Fantasy series, especially FFX). It's "anime-inspired" in the type of action conflicts and character scenes it supports. It's a "group RPG" at heart, contrary to Beast Hunters (a mainly 2-player game with the option for more), because it supports group interaction and PC combo actions and the likes. It's for "spontaneous fun" in that it provides lots of "seeds" during character and setting creation for spontaneous use in play, rather than planned-out adventures. Basically, it's the game that really supports my preferred way of playing in a group.

I've added a lot of stuff since the alpha version, including optional mechanics for items and for powers in slots (i.e., materia). Here's the direct download link for the 164-page PDF. The PDF uses a serif font for the main text because it's meant to be printed.

I'll write more about the game over on AnimaPrimeRPG.com soon, once I've recovered from the work I put into setting up the complete text on that site. That's for people who don't like reading PDFs (hi Callan), and because in drupal you can leave comments on every section, which I hope will make the feedback process easier. I might not release a final PDF version, but the site will always be updated and freely available, even when the game is released in book form.

    \n Alexandre, there's plenty of room for different games--as you can see from how different this is from, say, Bliss Stage or even Jake Richmond's also-Final-Fantasy-inspired Magical Land of Yeld.

    Lukas, yay! And thanks again for your feedback on the beta version, that helped clean it up.

    Levi, that means a lot coming from you. I've really enjoyed your work on techniques (Game Craft etc.) and your practical approach to thinking about RPGs; my playstyle essay goes in the same direction, albeit on a broader scale.

    And the essays can be read on their own, thanks to the drupal site:

    Playstyles, on dramatic, wish fulfillment, and challenge-oriented conflicts.
    Flags, on using sheet elements to say what you'd like to see in play (with much debt owed to Chris Chinn).
    Spontaneous Play, on using seeds, reincorporation, and conflict webs to play without a plan and yet with structure.
    Playing Over the Internet, on playing Anima Prime over post-based or chat-based interfaces.
