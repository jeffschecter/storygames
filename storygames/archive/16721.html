\n I've been noodling with a stripped-down fantasy AW Hack -- much lighter than Dungeon World, but I stopped with it to play with World of Dungeons instead. Part of what I was doing was working with the notion that the game would be played in pretty small nuggets of adventure. Microdungeons, etc. So I'm building a system for making it easy to come up with such things and I'm pretty happy with it so far. But now I'm thinking about sharing or publishing or whatever, the results and I'm considering utility for others.

Something like this.

What does such an artifact need to be useful?

I came up with a list of things to include before I made that doc above, it looked like:\n

Always answer the following:
- What's the situation?
- What lead to this?
- How might the PCs be hooked into it?

Always provide:
- A map or diagram
- One or more custom moves
- Evocative descriptions

Ideally, but optionally, include an illustration.
\nAnything I missed? Any other comments?

    \n As Marshall advises, his 'impressions' are mandatory for me now in prep. Whenever I get a cool vision or idea for a place / person / object in my head, or a player authors something memorable, I jot it down bullet point style under a heading called 'impressions', next to my fronts and dangers. As they get used in play they get crossed off and added to a front or the map / relationship map. If re-incorportated by any player it gets jotted down on its own card and placed in the play area to remind us of its signifcance.
