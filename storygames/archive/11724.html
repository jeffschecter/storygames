\n Charles Stross just posted this elevator pitch for an alternate-history novel his agent talked him out of writing in 2000 (he ended up writing the "Merchant Princes" series instead.) The concept may have been "too weird" for a then-fledgling novelist, but O man would it make a kick-ass campaign:\n
I'm going to cross the streams of The IPCRESS File and Heart of Darkness in a universe where the first world war ended in 1919 with allied tanks sitting in the wreckage of Berlin, and the decaying British empire went on to invent fascism in the 1940s. It's 1962, and two OSS agents are injected into British-dominated Europe to trace the underground railroad that is funneling abducted/brainwashed American scientists east. Our two spooks, "Wild" Bill Burroughs and his swivel-eyed Californian sidekick Philip K., follow the trail \u2014 by way of a sleazy S&M nightclub in Hamburg presided over by ageing queen Adi and his boyfriend Rudi Hess \u2014 to Ceylon, where in the guts of a hollowed-out mountain they confront the jackbooted, monocle-wearing Air Commodore Arthur Clarke and his program to build an atom-bomb powered space dreadnought.
\nI just realized that I've now wrecked it by giving away the plot, so you'll have to find players who don't read this site Plus, if the players are any good they'll certainly hijack the story into entirely unexpected directions, which is of course awesome, although it would be a shame not to be able to play a Fascist Arthur C. Clarke as an NPC archivillain. There are so many other possibilities, of course \u2014 like the young Jim Ballard, a sadistic doctor who stages car crashes for fun and wants to destroy the world in a holocaust of either water or wind (he can't make up his mind...) Oops, I gave away that idea too.

Please run this. It's too ambitious for me (I'm a total newb at GM'ing) but someone must do this idea justice (or bring it to justice...)
