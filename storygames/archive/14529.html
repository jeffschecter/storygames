\n In line with "Split decision" or the "Danger dice" mechanism, i wonder what could be a "violence brings (more) violence" dice mechanism.

The purpose is to emulate something like "no ending war", like inti-fada war, Afghan conflict, Algeria independance war... and/or emulate the idea that "violence is not the answer".

i imagine this:
- agressor attack.
- Then the other side gain, for his own attack, a "Rage bonus dice" based on the agressor succes.
- And so on...so that conflict may be more and more awfull for both parts.

Obviously, there should be a way to decrease the Rage level in order to give peace a chance.

Any thought ?

    \n This is a great idea. it would need to be coupled with some mechanical incentive for choosing not to escalate, I think.

    So, I can retaliate with greater force, or I can do something else (retaliate at the same level of force, retaliate at a reduced level of force, not retaliate at all, whatever makes sense in the context of the game) and enjoy some lateral reward, like enhanced social clout.

    So then the calculation is "if I hit him, he'll either hit me back twice as hard or totally humiliate me before the King" which is interesting and fun math to do. There's no way out of the bind - if you hit your enemy so hard he is incapable of retaliating, the King still frowns upon you. Actually this is an elegant check against escalating chaos, because the player faced with an opponent choosing retaliation that will result in utter destruction can, instead, roll over and use social capital to end the thing in his favor.

    \n i know DitV, and i was clearly inspired by it, but i think the purpose of VBV ( Violence brings Violence) is different, althougth it's can be rather close.

    I had in mind the actual Syrian conflict as a paradigm: how is started from almost nothing (some kids writing graffiti against government), each time with more and more violence as a response, leading to a civil war

    http://blogs.mediapart.fr/blog/agsulaiman/270411/les-debuts-de-la-revolution-en-syrie-les-enfants-martyrises-de-deraa

    Clearly in this case, it have reached a non-return point, which could be also a key concept for VBV: if you cross the line(s) - threshold - be ready for consequences (and it will be harder to turn back).

    By the way Jason, thanks for yours suggestions. Interesting ways to explore. I had also in mind so kind of lateral consequences, like more violence (or cross a threshold) leads to loose social support ( in the syrian case, loosing economic stability, international support, the police -then- the army, etc...).

    Regarding pure maths, VBV "as it" could also lead to "Blitzkrieg"-like strategies: you can win with violence only if you win quickly ( without giving time for response) but i'm not sure it's a good thing.
