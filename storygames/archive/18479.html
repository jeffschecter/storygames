    \n Thanks for the improvements Entrance would be better. And I wanted Compose a Satire to bring to mind the epic satires of Juvenal and Catullus, and the continual disparagements with the same taunts of Cicero. Also the gross slanders of Suetonius or Procopius. Even Caesar was taunted in the street for being the receiver in gay sex. But Perform a Satire would beat composing one.

    Plead a Case is intended for the second health track you get. Once you've gained four points of Notoriety (and it will happen) someone can launch a case against you with the end result of Exile or even death - i.e. out of the game. And getting back your social protection other than by pleading a case is very, very difficult.

    And yes, you use the same stat to attack someone as for one of the means of persuasion. While your ability to keep cool under fire (what Roman will admit to feeling fear?) is the same you use to turn an audience. But I haven't quite got the separation of those two.

    \n Make a speech would work I think, and I may be going too specific there.

    And the difference between Oratory and Rhetoric is that Oratory is almost entirely pre-planned. If you were up burning the candle from both ends it's Oratory - as it is if you published it in a book. Not a distinction I'd make in most other settings, but the Romans seemed very fond of pre-planned speeches (and ones with scurrilous accusations at that) and published slanders and I want the mechanics to encourage people to do this. So calling it out as its own stat, distinct from Rhetoric feels like the way to go. (I don't want to make it a move based on mere Rhetoric because the game should be politic heavy and I'm trying to spread around the functions).

    The other option is to drop Oratory, and replace it with something more like Libel or Defamation (I'm trying to stick with Latin-root words for the stats - but can't off the top of my head think of the qualities associated that are Latin derived). Whatever it would be would keep the Shutting Someone Down move.

    The big problem with Legionary is that I already have Imperator (who needs to be there) for the successful general. And to put it bluntly, Legionaries were plebs - and I'm trying to avoid direct subordinates. On the other hand there is room for Tribune as about the highest rank someone who started off as a Legionary can rise to (until you find the adoption papers with the ink still wet). There's also room for Tribune as "Young, ambitious scion". Actually this fits together nicely - either way they are outsiders (whether through having been adopted or through being young) with their main claim to fame being from the military and who don't have a secure personal powerbase - or even official leadership of their faction. Hmm... can I fold Tribune of the Plebs into this skin as well, making it the "Ambitious Outsider" skin? Or am I massively overloading this one poor skin? I think the latter :-(

    So I take it the game is about the upper echelons of Roman society, then? I can see how that could work well for a political game, but on the other hand, sometimes stories are more interesting when characters of varying status are involved. I'm sure you've seen the TV series, Rome, for example. There were common soldiers, servants, and slaves involved in the story right alongside Pompey and Caesar, and that Upstairs, Downstairs dynamic made the show more interesting.

    Also, what if Orate was a move?
    \nNot disagreeing in the slightest about the principle that having focus time on the servants made the show much more interesting.

    However. Tabletop RPGs are shows with only three or four stars. Which means that they have to be more focussed on a very small cast. And the Apocalypse World engine in specific is tightly focussed on a theme/genre - in this case politics and the attempt to become First Man in Rome. Were I writing a much looser Roman Life game I'd use a different engine (probably Fate Core) and with much less tightly focussed skills and approaches. The game has a working title of First Man In Rome for a reason.

    \n Plead a Case can go. Instead under Oratory I'm going with Write a Speech

    Write a Speech
    When someone delivers a speech you've spent time writing, roll Oratory. On a 10+ they carry one forward to whatever ends you intended the speech for and you can choose to be recognised as the author, have it thought to be them, or have it thought to be spontaneous.

    On a 7-9 pick 1.
    They take 1 forward to whatever ends the speech is intended for. (NPCs have Advantage).
    You are recognised as the author of the speech.
    The speech is thought to be spontaneous.

    (If delivering your own speech you are automatically thought to be its author).

    Is this strong enough?
