    \n Hey, I actually went through many "story games" in an effort to get a good spread of product for Finnish fans when I was at Gencon. I consequently read a lot of them, making me quite well-read on new releases. These are my recommendations; they're not all from 2008, but they're all games I encountered this year at Gencon:\n
      \n
    • How to Host a Dungeon is a very simple, very fun, very useful and very innovative little game. It's cheap, too, so I recommend it to anybody who needs to create dungeons or is interested in game design that pushes the envelope.
      • \n
    • 3:16 is easy to recommend if your group likes textured, largely freeform, light roleplaying with cool in-character rewards and a loose attitude to reward cycles. It's not a game that plays itself, but it has great potential for anybody wanting to savour the slow development of the campaign.
      • \n
    • Don't Lose Your Mind is excellent if you play Don't Rest Your Head or need crazy fantasy-horror superpowers for some other game.
      • \n
    • Dread: the 1st Book of Pandemonium is the best Gamist demon-hunting game I've encountered. It's like 3:16, except less hard-coded advancement, set in the modern world and 100% more brutality.
      • \n
    • In a Wicked Age and Poison'd are simply the best value games we got at Gencon, they're very small and cheap for the potential of the games themselves. If I had to sell 90% of my rpg collection, these are some of the few games that I'd keep without further thought.
      • \n
    • Mist-Robed Gate is infuriating and snotty, but also interesting and challenging. An obvious pick if you like wuxia.
      • \n
    • Urchin is a challenging and infuriating game like MRG above, except it's about people who don't wash regularly and see little green men.
      • \n
    \nThen there are Solar System and Zombie Cinema, which I'm biased to recommend, but which did get published this year.

    The above list focuses on wide-appeal gamer games that are unlikely to disappoint and likely to be worth the price of admission to most - there were other excellent games that came out this year that I heartily recommend, but something like Spione isn't everybody's cup of tea.

    \n Hot War was a highlight for me this year. I normally avoid post-holocaust games, but the sheer, unrelenting Britishness of the background, combined with the fine-tuned Cold City rules, really clicked with me. It's a good fit for fans of J. G. Ballard, Nigel Kneale and John Wyndham.

    I've only played one session of 3:16 so far, but I loved it, despite being very, very tired and propped up only by the life-giving energy of Red Bull. I can't wait to try a campaign of it.

    \n My highlights this year are:

    Hot War - Because it's just so wonderful a meshing of system and setting, both of which are great. Best session I've played all year was a Hot War game.

    Duty & Honour - Because, dammit, this genre was a gaping hole in the RPG market and now it's filled with a great game.

    3:16 - There is nothing to be said about this game which hasn't been said already. All the positive stuff is true, this game really is that good.

    IaWA - Is this considered a "done" game? I thought it was an Ashcan? Good fun though, but it'd benefit from proper, printed oracle decks.

    I haven't yet tried:

    Solipsist - Kinda like Mage, but without a lot of that annoying baggage and for much more personal stories. Slick looking too.

    Starblazer Adventures - I played a demo of this, but felt that it didn't really show off all the strengths of the system. I want to give it a proper go before I pass judgement, but so far, it's pretty nifty (although huge!)

    \nDread:TFBOP has been out for a while; the revised version, with new cover art and some more content, came out this year (right?). That said, it ROCKS the d12, there's a great supplement for it (and a fan supplement) and Spite will be coming out soon (*ahem* Full Disclosure--I'm attached to this project).

But not to someone studying Philosophy for a grade (there's some fundamental terminology misuse, I feel; but that has NO impact on what should be very rousing gameplay, though).

    IAWA delivers every time, with trad or indie gamers, short and long term. If you're only going to buy one, that's the one. It's going to win the iRPG Award for Best of 2008, mark my words.

    \n IAWA is a good game, and i've personally had a lot of fun with it, but it is not the be all and end all of gaming (or 2008) for that matter. I'm shocked no one has yet mentioned:

    Darkpages (yeah it's an ashcan, but it's pretty much totally complete in that form) is a game for a small group of people that read comics that matter and want to tell stories in that vein.

    Baron Munchausen. It was re-released this year, cleaned up, and compiled. It's is the grandfather (or maybe just father) of the form. It is also one of best party games ever.

    Empire of Dust. Do you like the fun you get out of D&D and the awesome characters you get out of Burning Wheel? Did you like Dune? or Coheed & Cambria? or The Road Warrior? Well mash all that together and you've got a truly incredible game that's super fun to play and has a story that's totally engrossing. Like TOTALLY ENGROSSING!

    Sweet Agatha. Yeah it's my own product, but what of it? You've never played a game or read a book like it before. It's a story game, it may in fact be the most STORY game around.
