\n Are you guys aware of any online archive of procedures and techniques for RPGs/SGs?
Do you think there is any value in making one?

I got started thinking in this direction by a recent thread about games where you can make up rules as you go along and I started thinking.. when you enter a scene you should ask yourself "what is this scene trying to discover?", then you should pick a procedure/technique appropriate to the answer.

    \n It would certainly be of most value, not only for everybody interesting in hacking/research but it can be the start of a completely new game in the lines of the topic mentioned. To start properly the compendium I'd suggest separate techniques and procedures into categories according to the stage of the game, like "world building", "character creation", "scene creation", "conflict resolution" and "ending the story". I'd also keep only the procedures and techniques that go along the lines of the principle mentioned in the other topic: Listen to other players and go with whatever they create

    (edited to avoid doublepost)

    I've lurking around a lot of places for a while and I haven't found a list of techniques and procedures. You can find advice on how to be a better GM, lots of indy games and advice on how to run them, but since each game has a particular procedure and technique for what it intends to do, there isn't and exhaustive list yet. I recall a study of common procedures in RPGs made ages ago, but it mentioned mostly stuff from commercial games and old school unknown games that went experimental and failed but at least did something interesting. Daunting task you have there, extracting the useful parts from all this mess...
