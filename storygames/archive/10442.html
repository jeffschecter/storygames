\nSo!

I recently got my copy of The Smoke Dream delivered to Mexico, I've played it once and I gotta tell you, it was awesom.

For starters:

The game took way longer then I had anticipated (granted it was a little slower because I was just getting a hold of the rules).

At first I tried to imagine complex fiction while drawing every card, I eventually stoped that and started just drawing cards, reading the write-ups and letting the imagination rest a little. When I got around to finishing the game and then remembered what happened, I was impressed to realize the memories I had did not involve the cards at all, but the characters and circumstances.

In my first game my heart was really easy to retreave.

Now I'm playing it for the second time:

This time it's become a lot more about experimentation for me; right now I'm trying to see what I can do with the ballroom.

Trying that has lead my to experiment a lot, I've already been to the bone yard (which was hard) and, right now, I'm trying to get a minor key back from the monster hoard stack... for which I'm trying to buy the lake (something tells me the lake is the way to get there) from the merchant king, who has it as an item for sale. My alternative is to bring the great wolf to him and see what happens then.

A little tip:

In order to "save my progress" I worked a little tick. I numbered the stacks on the game sheet (only the ones I have cards in) In that same order I stuffed the cards in said stacks into the book, one stack and then turn the page.

The rest of the deck I put in it's box, along with the out-of-game cards (jokers, the hollow lady and her heart) facing the oposite direction.

I hope I was clear... >.<


You guys have to try this game, it's freaking awesome!

    Posted By: Simon PetterssonFor those of us who are not familiar with the game, what kind of a game is it? Sounds like a solo game, is that right?
    \nThe others are obfuscating a bit more than necessary, I think.

    It's a solo card game, which carries a kindred spirit to old text adventure games (wander around in rooms with items and scripted NPCs).
    You're in a dreamscape, you're missing your heart, and only by navigating the dreamscape can you find your heart.

    Jackson stresses (repeatedly) that you don't shuffle the deck, meaning you encounter the same cards in the same order (albeit with a couple twists, mostly through 6's and 7's). This adds a tactical strategization element to the game, which is interesting.

    You only read the card text when you draw the card, and even then certain sections are forbidden to read without having a certain companion or item by your side. As a result, you're constantly being introduced to that which is new, through play.

    As a result... [small spoiler in this paragraph] I've heard mention of the wolf-blooded girl several times, but have yet to actually meet her, or learn anything about her, or to even read the "if the Wolf-Blooded Girl is your companion..." sections on those cards where I've seen her name appear. I'm super curious about who she is, what she'll do for me, and why she's ALL OVER THE FREAKIN' PLACE in these particular card descriptions. My next game, I'm going to be trying to locate her, as one of my top priorities.

    It's fun. It's amazing how the game quickly trains you to build goals for yourself, in this weird fruitful void sort of way.
    That said, it's a playtest and there are issues. The "6" cards really frustrate me, for example.

    \n Finally tried The Smoke Dream. A little weird at first. I think I was trying too hard. Eventually I just played the game, and the images came into my head naturally.

    I want to talk about stuff, but I don't want to spoil it.

    But this I can say: I soon discovered that the "win condition" of finding your heart is only a win condition if it's hard to do, which it usually isn't. It's usually more of a "lose condition", in that you do build gaols for yourself, as joepub said, and if you find your heart before then, you "lose". I was actually carrying around the clockwork heart, which is a burden, just so I could have another couple of shots at the Hollow Queen and the Venerable Unicorn.
