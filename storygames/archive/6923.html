\n So I am working on a game called Messiah for the Ashcan Front game. It's based on my Game Chef entry (also called Messiah) but turbo charged with a lot of feedback I got from my peer reviewers. I won't name names, but you know who are.

Anyway, I've been doing playtesting and what I have works...it's just well... the resolution mechanic concerns me. It's...I don't know disconnected from the rest of the game.

The general premise is that the Messiah (the true representative of whatever god is in power in the particular game setting) has come to break the world: perform miracles, make a few moral pronouncements, perhaps tear down some old institutions. The players disciples who should be all for the Messiah's cause, but in fact are secretly leading the old institutions. It is their job to contain the Messiah, twist the Messiah's words, and use the chaos of the Messiah to better their own position in the world.

So the current mechanic looks like this. Each player has stats and skills. Stats are rated D4-D12. Skills are rated 1-6. You roll a number of dice equal to your Skill rating of a type equal to your stat. If you roll higher than a 3, success.

Everything in Messiah is a contested roll, so margin of success is a good thing. The higher the margin of success, the more you get of what you want.

It works. It resolves conflicts. But there is nothing that screams Messiah breaking the world about it.

I wanted to toss the question out to this hallowed body for ideas about interesting things to do with dice rolls.

I have been reading Reign and I need to go back and look at Dogs with fresh eyes. I just wish there was something I could do to make the game have a more messiah-like feel. I'd take games, essays, ramblings, rants, RTFMs, anything.

    \n Actually, right now the point of the game isn't to play nice Disciples. It's to play ones that on the face of things are pro-Messiah, but beneath the surface are working cross purpose to his goals for their own ends. So the disciple powers are derived from the Messiah, but more importantly their ability to manipulate the Messiah behind the scenes is what the game models.

    There are four stats: Resources (the measure of what I can do and how many followers I have to do it)
    Infrastrucutre (the measure of how well I can defend against attack)
    Charisma (my ability to influence others)
    Establishment (my ability to recharge my benes)

    Skills are things like Medical and Cook and Cause Riot which allow you to build up your organization, Assassinate and Propaganda (your skills for causing harm to others).
