\n The third printing of Cold City, the game of hidden agendas trust and monster hunting in 1950's Berlin, is now available from the Contested Ground Studios shop and Indie Press Revolution.
\n
\nThis is not a revised version of the game, but some clarifications and additions have been made to the system text. These revolve around the areas of negative traits and the setting of stakes (the original stakes setting information was far, far too brief now that I look at it with fresh eyes). If you are the owner of a first or second printing of the game (which can be identified by looking at the credits page: if there is no mention of a printing version at the bottom of that page, then it's the first printing. Otherwise, it will say second or third printing), then you can download the revised sections for free from our website.
\n
\nIt's my hope and belief that these small changes benefit the game as a whole. The alternative use of negative traits is, I think a really god change, so I've got to thank Joshua Newman and Steve Dempsey for the suggestion. Its one of those crazy things that in all of the playtesting, it never came up as being sub par! Well, I think that the new way negative traits are handled is really good and shows the benefit of continuing to get feedback on your game and making improvements as and when necessary.
\n
\nIt's also my hope that by putting the revision up on our website for download, that those who own first and second printing will be able to check out the changes and see what they think as well.
\n
\nFinally, the recently released Cold City Companion, an additional volume of advice, intelligence agencies and sinister locations is also now available from our own shop, Indie Press Revolution and RPGnow.
\n
\nCheers
\nMalcolm
