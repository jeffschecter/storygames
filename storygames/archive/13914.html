    \n My fascination with STAPLE is with its similarity to the Comiket, the largest comic convention in the world, and the largest market for indie comics and magazines on the planet: So large that some of the artists make their livelihood from their Comiket sales, of comics basically loosely bound or stapled together for no more than 4-8 bucks (given 100yen = 1 dollar, not so true right now but in spirit that's the rate).

    Also, a convention with such true indie production that it takes the Post-Forge's scene, calls it a Sellout Pussy, and pushes it in the dirt face-down.

    If it's more than 10 bucks (usually 6-8 or less), You Fail.
    If you make other designers buy your game rather than just giving them a copy or swapping with theirs, You Fail.

    STAPLE seems to have that same scene/exchange at heart, which is why I'm so terribly interested in seeing it with my own eyes.

    Next year, I'm there (as a vendor or just attendee, I don't know yet).

    -Andy

    \n Andy et al,

    I would love to help organize an indie games meetup with basically that same idea (more than 10 bucks, refusing to trade = FAIL). Honestly, I think that would be much, much healthier and more sustainable for a large portion of folks wanting to be involved in game design and publishing, rather than the model that's grown out of the Forge (short run, around 20 bucks, 6x9 casebound, IPR and/or distro). The games would be shorter, the bar would be lower, the cost would be minimal, the focus on sales and making money would be minimized, and the distinction between "game designers" and the hoi polloi would be demolished. That's a (post)publishing model I could get behind.

    \n P.S. Okay, I just bought "1-barter.net" with the intention of creating an alternate method of indie games sales/distro somehow along the lines of STAPLE and Comiket, figuring we might be able to build off Vincent's preferences in regards to trading custom Apocalypse World playbooks. In my mind, there's a listing of available games, kinda like the Unstore, but the "buy" button says [spend 1-barter]. And contributing folks can post what they will accept as barter, though there might be some defaults like print booklets, PDFs, baked goods, etc. You could also use it for trading your used games for other stuff that you actually want, though there could be categories where you can sort listings to only see certain kinds of content. You could also theoretically trade services like editing, proofreading, artwork, layout, external play, etc.

    Sorry if this is hijacking your thread, Trey.
