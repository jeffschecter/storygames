    \nJoin JIGG, and post now! groups.yahoo.com/group/jigg
    (Japan International Gamers Guild)
    JIGG is primarily people in and around Tokyo/Chiba/Yokohama that meet up erratically for gaming events, news, etc.

    Also, Cam Eeles (of the Godzilla Gaming Podcast) is out over by Ebisu/Tokyo IIRC. Check out Yellow Sub in Akihabara (there are many; Go to the RPG one) when you get a chance and flip through some Japanese RPGs, see if any appeal to you: Might be a good way to get into studying the language. I highly recommend the brand new and VERY inexpensive (800 yen) Doublecross 3rd edition RPG. I'd also recommend Shinobigami for the same reasons, but there are tons of Kanji and it might be overwhelming.

    \n True, BUT: While the Shinjuku store has the largest selection of Western RPGs of any store in Japan, their collection of Japanese RPGs is far less than at Akiba (note: Akihabara's store has like Zero western RPGs that aren't rare/collectors items on display). Also, Akiba's store gets indie games (they work with doujin TRPG publishers at Comiket and the like, and buy then stock 20 copies of these games at-a-time) here and there, as well as release party promos, etc. While Shinjuku's store is the holy ground for English RPGs, Akihabara's store is the holy ground for Japanese RPGs.

    Plus, Akihabara is the location of "gohan/\u30b4\u30cf\u30f3", one of my favorite izakayas. (^.^)

    Also, it's a lot easier to direct people to who are new to Tokyo. The Akihabara store is basically "Take the main exit out to Electric Town/Follow EVERYBODY. Go straight a block, turn right, go straight a block, turn left, cross the street, walk 8 meters, you're there". The Shinjuku store you end up having to explain the whole difference between West and East Shinjuku, and direct people through that non-Euclydian rat-maze where that store sits. They have to sacrifice chickens and use divining rods to get there their first time (especially if they're not used to the Shinjuku East/West thing). But there's a comic store next door, so that's kinda cool.

    But yeah, I do agree with the sentiment somewhat. When I go to Akihabara, I tend to get in, nail some RPGs, run to LAOX, buy some console games, then Get The Fuck Out before the otaku-stink gets all over you. However, there are really great hole-in-the-wall indie fashion stores and the like (great places to buy cool indie-made T-shirts and the like, and not just because of the funny Engrish).

    -Andy

    \nCulture lesson one! (^.^)

    If you have more than 10,000, you'll always use "man", not "sen" with yen. So that would be 72\u4e07\u5186. Which makes more sense to folks since the 1-man bill is the highest paper currency used. Also, it's easy for Americans case we just think "take the number, in MAN, and that == the approximate price, in HUNDREDS of US dollars".

    -Andy
