So yesterday I was playing Legos with my 4.5 year old nephew. He built this cool thing and the following conversation ensued:

ME: What the heck is that?

HIM: It's a rescue unit.

ME: What does it rescue?

HIM: It's part of the vampire robot army.

ME: That is so cool.

HIM: They are robots ... and they drink blood!

So I'm throwing down the gauntlet - let's see some ideas for fun, functional RPGs that incorporate the vampire robot army, as well as the specialized rescue units that pull them out of trouble. Give me some color, back story, game mechanics, whatever strikes you based on the earth-shaking initial premise. Bonus points if he can run it for me, or i can run it for him.

    The robot shells protect the cybernetic brains encased inside. Due to faulty recirculation systems (the shells are rather old and steampunky) fresh, oxygenated blood must be introduced regularly.

    Also due to their dwindling numbers, the vampire robots cannot afford to lose the damaged shells, so rescue airlift units will recover them.

    I actually see this as a boardgame, like "Zombies!" Your overpowered tank robot mows down rows of helpless humans as it rolls through town, avoiding the military's anti-tank weapons. If you get smashed, you can call for evac and if successful you get to start over. If you fail, you're out and your brain withers in its smoking metal carcass.

    Ok, so you clearly need a couple of different armies against which the robot army wages its war--perhaps the snail people and the humans. Humans are a great source of blood but have sucky resources while the snail people have great resources (replacement parts) but no good blood. Parts go to keep rescued vampire robots on the battlefield and the rescue unit in the air. Blood keeps the whole army lubricated. If either falls below a certain number, things get desperate and the pilot really has to start working.

    The game centers around a day in the life of a vampire robot rescue unit pilot. Every challenge is rated according to how many pieces of the ship the pilot may lose in an encounter as opposed to how much blood, parts, and vampire robots it manages to rescue.

    So, a tough challenge may be "lose 1d6 pieces of ship, gain d8 parts, d4 rescued robots" or "lose d6 parts but get d8 units of blood and d6 urescued robots." The ship should have a defense number, a die they can roll to avoid damage--perhaps based on pilot skill. The more rescues they survive, the less likely they are to get shot down. Every part lost incurs a penalty on that total until the part is repaired.

    Every robot the ship rescues should give them a morale die (d4?) that they can use to bolster other rolls. So you might choose a tough rescue for the morale dice it will give you to make a supply raid against the snails. Robots rescued from snail battles don't have any blood, robots rescued from human battles are full of it.

    If a rescue unit crashes (certain number of parts lost + failure to get more parts than damage taken in a supply run), play can continue. Just start with a new pilot whose first job is to rescue the old pilot.

    Add color and voila! [sort of--still a lot of rough spots]
