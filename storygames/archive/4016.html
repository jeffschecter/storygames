Anyhow, I'm looking for some additional suggestions.
I know that there is a lot of other great Anime (Ninja Scroll, Rurouni Kenshinm, Ghost in the Shell) but right now I specifically looking for the stuff that fits the Bliss Stage mold.

    \n I prefer Rah Xephon to Evangelion. It's very much in the same mold, but it brings some of its own ideas to the table. Our teenage pilot hero character is conflicted about fighting, but not simply contrarian or cowardly. He gets a nice tale of maturation, and his mother isn't dead. Also, the good guys don't have much of an understanding of the technology they're using, or the nature of the "Mulians" they're fighting. The writers manage to do some nice symbolism across the series using their color palate and a Red vs. Blue motif. And the message is slightly more optimistic (although that's not very hard, given a comparison to Eva).

    Oh, and I believe there's a two-part episode where Our Teenage Pilot Hero is pulled into a dreamscape by his enemy and must fight it there.

    \n Of course Evangelion and RahXephon are going to be the big dogs of the genre, so they're going to get mentioned off the bat.     \nWhen I was trying to think about whether Gunbuster 2 would be good inspirational stuff for Bliss Stage, I started thinking about Gainax's current series, Gurren Lagann. And it occurred to me that although it's very different from pretty much everything else mentioned as inspiration (it's got this gonzo nekketsu/boiling blood vibe a lot of the time), you could probably do a GL-inspired Bliss Stage game if you wanted. It wouldn't be Gurren Lagann per se, and it would probably be different from most any other Bliss Stage game you could name, but still. Kamina could be the authority figure, and as mecha go the Ganmen (only one design rule: it must have a face) are just awesome. And the series does focus a lot on people's relationships, and has encompassed some romantic relationships, marriages, and one pregnancy so far.
