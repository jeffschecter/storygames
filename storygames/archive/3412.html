...you roll traits named Anguish, Hope, Excess, Flair, and Zeitgeist -- who needs to hit?

...you spend four hours playing and when you explain to your SO what happened, it was all about somebody's shoe and how it had a hole in it but in the end, their father really did love them after all.

    \n If I were a story game, I wouldn't have a boss, I would have an antagonist.

    She would be cartoonishly evil, but she would take her work seriously. We would have a regular sit-down where we'd discuss how she could better piss me off. At any time, I could spend part of my paycheck to win an argument with her.

    All the boring stuff in my life would be summarized in dialog, or in a powerpoint backgrounder.

    \n You might be playing a story game if...

    ...the only time you act out dialog is for someone else's character.
    ...your character could jump 20 feet without you having to make a single roll, as long as no other fictional character cares about it.
    ...what's at stake is your mother.
    ...you actually can buy happiness, as long as it's paid in story tokens.
    ...every time your character actually does something he then spends ten times as much time thinking about what he just did.
    ...your not even sure which one of these sheets on the table is your character, anymore.
    ...the fact that after two or three sessions all the PCs will kill each other in a bloody slaughter is not only okay, the players will be disappointed if it doesn't happen.
