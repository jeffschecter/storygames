    \n This kind of game is right in my wheelhouse. I love procedural games that help me tell a story but don't get in the way. I like the format, but want more. Not a lot more, just a bit. After playtesting, how many obstacles work best? How much time does it cost to add another obstacle in terms of play time?

    I like the AW style view of success/partial success. I also like the way the missions are created like that one spy game I can never remember the name of. So, I think you have a solid basis. But, like all things there are things I still want.

    I want differentiation of character. Like Monaco (PC game), I want to be the muscle...or maybe the hacker. Whatever, I want to differentiate and be the one that the team counts on. Nobody else can do what I do.

    I also would love some lists (loves me some lists). Lists of obstacles, skills, etc. I'd pull a bunch from movies and literature. When I think about this as a game, give me some simple rules and some sparks of ideas (lists!) and I'm off.

    And...I want to use this to play Shadowrun...not Ocean's 11. But, that's just me and I can easily mod it.

    Keep playtesting and refining!

    \n Wow, thanks for the kind words Eric! I greatly appreciate them.
    To answer some of your questions:
    I played with 15 and 25 obstacles for my (only 2) playtests. The time difference wasn't big (maybe 2h to run 15, around 2,5 maybe 3 with 25). You'll find that the more obstacles you add, the more often you will call for tests/checks because the obstacles will end close to each other in a narrative way. I will need to run more playtests soon.

    I want to add more, and explain the rules more. The biggest problem was that the game as is now, is a 24h creation, so I was limited by both space (pocketmod) and time.

    I was thinking about adding special powers per character (Moncao was also my source of this idea ). I think I will play with creating some classes/specialities (basically saying - I am the ______ of the group - blank being mucle, hacker etc.) and giving them some cool power to manipulate the cards during certain narrative scenes. I really enjoy the aspect of being the only person in the group who can do the cool thing (and as proven by SCIENCE! it is great for game design/gamification). I was toying with it for 15-20min during the 24h challange, but decided to shelve it for later and now thanks to your comment it will happen for sure!

    And lists... oh lists I can confirm that they will be there, I toyed with putting word clouds (like the one on the cover) in the pocketmod, but again - lack of space. Now for the full blown pdf rulebook (I say like it will be a 300 pages book, it won't I want to keep it small and accessible - so no more than 24 pages) Would you have anything against lists as word clouds? Would you rather have them as lists as the few in the book now ( .[item1] .[item2] ...)?

    How do you feel about a small pocketmod rulebook with a companion book (that will explain the quirks of the system and expand the game) - do you think it is a good idea, or would you rather have me stick to a pocketmod?

    And Shadowrun is a great setting for running heists (runs), one playtest I run was setup in cyberpunk-like setting - so I can confirm it works.
