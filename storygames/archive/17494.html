\n Hey folks! December 8th, 10:00 am until 12:00 am. It's coming...




Play Out Loud proudly presents...

Technicolor Dreams:
Spontaneous shared stories told together!

Please register here. If you don't have a Facebook account, shout out in this thread.

You can find more info on the Facebook page for the event (links below). We're going to be at Guardian Games, and have a lot of their space allocated to us (maybe all, if we get enough folks).

We're going to have indie games, story games, whatever games, old-school RPGs, some Jeepform, maybe Jackson Tegu's crazy-ass game from last year's Game Chef, a game design contest, perhaps some sort of experimental pervasive game happening throughout the day, and really fantastic and engaging people.

Please post if you're planning to come and would like to run something!

The event is broken up into three slots, with breaks for lunch and dinner:
10:30am - 1:30pm
2:30pm - 6:30pm
7:30pm - 11:30pm

When you arrive, there will be sign up sheets for different activities for each slot. Just sign up and you're good to go! We're not doing pre-reg.

As details about specific events come in we\u2019ll post on the Facebook page, and in this thread. So stay tuned!

EXPLORE, ENRICH, ENJOY!

There is a suggested donation of $5. We want to put on an even bigger and better show next year! Your support helps us all have more fun.

For more information see the Play Out Loud facebook page. Please read our manifesto, found in the "About" section of the Play Out Loud Facebook page, if you have questions about what you should expect from your Technicolor Dreams experience.

Technicolor Dreams is brought to you by:
Guardian Games
Ready, Set, Game PDX
Let\u2019s Playtest
Portland Retro Roleplayers
Westside Story Games
Central Community for Creative Play
Kinetic Energy

Questions or comments? Email Hans or Tayler at playoutloudpdx@gmail.com.

    \n There are numerous hotels in the general vicinity of Guardian Games. Try the Courtyard Marriott or the Doubletree near Lloyd Center which are fairly close by (you can catch one of the streetcars to get to the shop). Just across the river in Southwest downtown Portland are the Waterfront Marriott, another Courtyard Marriott off of Burnside, a Hilton, not to mention numerous other hotels and inns.
    If transport is a problem, one of us locals can probably help out. I'm planning on going, and I'm always happy to help out with rides.
    Some locals may also have space to accomodate guests instead of hotels. I did that for a bunch of people during GameStorm in March...though I unfortunately can't have any guests this December...
