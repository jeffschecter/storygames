\n I did a mashup of Lasers and Feelings and Battle Born, which is a game from the 90s that was published in Space Gamer. I always bring something to the holidays to play with the family and this is this years game. Not sure if it's up to the genius of Lasers and Feelings, but I thought I'd share it for anyone who remembers Battle Born fondly.

Elevator Pitch

You are one of the few remaining immunes (from the Pacification Virus) and belong to the misfit Squad 76, known as the \u201cSpirit of Baltimore\u201d (of 100 squads with #1 being the best and #100 the worst). Battle Born, part of the League\u2019s Military Assault and Combat Expedition (MACE) or as the troopers say, \u201cMost Anyone Conscious and Expendable,\u201d are equipped with an overly-complicated, mech-style battle suit. In a poker game, Sarge managed to win a Resolution Class dispatch ship, the Dauntless Perisher. Your job is to carry out the League\u2019s orders to the best of your ability (command\u2019s expectations are very low, very very low) while keeping the pacified and unarmored (affectionately known as squishies) safe (usually from your malfunctioning EE suit or poorly aimed kinetic shells).

How will you complete your orders?

    \n I originally wrote Overkill as a bad result, but it just felt wrong in the mashup. The questioning is central to L&F, so I used that as Overkill. I always felt like Battle Born "trusted" the players to play their characters according to the setting, instead of rewarding play according to the setting, so it just felt right changing the Ignobles (Goals) and adding the dice bonuses for playing to the theme.
