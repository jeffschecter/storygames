\n My ladyfriend and I both immensely enjoy the shows Archer and Frisky Dingo. She demanded I design a game for the shows, so here I am, being a good boyfriend. Plus, I think it could be fun. Here's what I have worked out so far.\n
  • A comedy game, set in an office location, with a fabulizer (a Joshua A.C. Newman term meaning something that makes the world different from ours).
    • \n
  • Terrible, gory, comedic action is a feature of every session; the player characters are all entirely competent to pull off whatever action they want however they want whenever they want.
    • \n
  • Characters can survive any injury, or and die and come back through ludicrous means.
    • \n
  • The office location is where the difficulties ensue. Systems for this will be extremely frustrating for the characters, but also hilarious.
    • \n
  • It'll use cards.
    • \n
  • There'll be a GM.
    • \n
  • Continuity is VITAL.
    • \n
  • Atrocious twists in plot to achieve or repair continuity are necessary and inevitable; bonus points for cleverly or hilariously addressing them in the fiction.
    • \n
  • Sessions go no more than 50 minutes (a design parameter of the ladyfriend; enough time to finish a play session without going crazy with the need for a cigarette)
\nThere's more, but I have to go get groceries.

    \n Love Archer, here are some quick ideas.

    I think the resolution system should be entirely "yes, but" based. By this I mean the question for a roll (or card draw i guess) is not "do you achieve your stakes?" but "What else happens?" I'm thinking along the lines of most 7-9 results in Apoc. World.

    You need to determine what inconsequential things are important to the character. Every character doesn't give a crap about the important things going on, only the small things important to them. You need to have a mechanical weight for those things:
    "You throw him over the side of the balcony, but he rips your suit." "GODDAMNIT."

    I'm not sure how to enforce continuity...maybe something like an "owe list" but for NPCs, subplots, and gags. You write them down and have to bring one back in the next game or something like that.
