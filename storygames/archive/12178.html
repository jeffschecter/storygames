\n I don't know if any of you watch Arrested Development, but it seems like the creators of that show are masters of AW-style fuckery. Whenever any character is about to get what they want, they find out that it's really not what they want or that there are horrible strings attached. What other shows are good at this?

    \nChuck. Nothing like as accomplished (either in general literary terms or at the fuckery) as Arrested Development, but on the other hand I feel like I could aspire to formulaic Chuck-level storytelling in my game sessions, whereas I'm pretty sure that AD is out of reach for improvised drama. Maybe with a lot of workshopping?

    \nBlack Adder. Nothing seems to go right for ANY of the characters, least of all Baldric and Black Adder himself. Lots of swindling and back-stabbing in order to get ahead, only to have something go terribly wrong.

    MI-5. Total espionage fuckery, on a more serious take than Chuck. Seriously, the first season of MI-5, when one of the characters gets her head dunked in a deep-fat fryer, and then shot...man, I simultaneously wanted to play that using Call of Cthulhu, and hide my head under my pillow and lament how destructive the world had become.

    \n Peep Show, oh man, so much. The characters never get what they want, even when they actually know what they want. And even if they get it, it turns out to be shit or not what they wanted at all. It's a delightful human carcrash. Everything is awkward social interaction pumped to the n-th degree.
