\n Hello, I'm looking for alternatives to PDQ (PDQ#) and FAE.
Why?
Because I'm curious and although both above mentioned games are great, I would like to know if there are more games like that.
It would be nice if they are narrative games with interesting twists, like the partial success mechanic from AW or conflict resolution (instead of task resolution) like in Hollowpoint.

What have I found so far?
Rocket Amoeba by Tim Gray (who made some PDQ stuff as you might recall). It's a roll-under-system with d6s and utilizes keys which are quite similar to Fate aspects. However, the keys are written on index cards and are held by players, the pool or the GM. It's a bit more "board-gamey" than Fate aspects.

Cortex Plus might be something but I have heard that the Hackers Guide suffers from reference to games not available anymore, like Marvel Heroic RPG.

Thank you for your suggestions or other advice.

    \n Risking to sound self-referencial, I would suggest Dreamwake.
    It offers its own setting, but then it is sooo easily hackable that the original setting turns out to be just a practical example of what can be done with the rules.

    Its interesting points are that it is completely GM-less, very narrative and fiction-first but with a nice crunchy core.
    It is geared towards group-centric action play (action meaning PC are pro-active, but they can easily end up doing investigations, spygames, etc) both as short term missions or long term campaigns.

    And the current ashcan is free to download and play

    I bought Rocket Amoeba last night (it's only $1) and read through it.
    \nMe too. I think there's a lot to like. The way keys pass around the table is genius. The only thing I'm not so fond of is the advice to the GM, which puts it in the GM plotting fiction camp rather than story now:\n
    Then come up with some key scenes that will take the characters from the start, which gets them involved, to the end, where they solve it or fail. The trick with these scenes is that they can move. Players only know about them when they happen. Imagine you\u2019ve planned a scene where they get vital information from a crotchety old librarian. But they bypass the library entirely, and head for the newspaper building! You can run the same scene with a crotchety old journalist, and for all they know you planned it that way.
    \nand\n
    There is a commonly-used structure for stories that might be useful for planning your adventures.
    \n
    This is the same reason I'm not a fan of Heroquest, to be honest (in Heroquest, it's the way the difficulties get harder depending on where the GM thinks we are in the story). But that aside, the system itself seems great.

    \n You could do worse than check out FU, the Freeform Universal RPG.

    You can find out a bit more about it HERE (which I hope I've done properly)

    It's super simple but narrative at the same time. I love it, which is why I set up the Yahoo Group, which is linked from that link. It's been modded around there to smooth out the Bonus and Penalty die differences and setting hacks etc.

    Basically you roll a die or dice (taking best or worst depending on the situation/skills etc) with
    6 a Yes, and...
    5 Yes
    4 Yes, but...
    3 No, but...
    2 No
    1 No, and...

    Damage (of whatever ilk) is basically as Fate's Consequences, with penalty die implications.

    It is also completely free, so there's no reason not to.

    \n Risus
    Over the Edge (WaRP)
    Barbarians of Lemuria / Honor + Intrigue
    Lady Blackbird (system can be generic)
    Danger Patrol (can be generic)

    Vortex (Dr Who, Primeval, Rocket Age from Cubicle 7) which has a core mechanic of:

    Attribute +Skill +2d6 +modifiers =result

    Result is compared to a Difficulty, either a static Difficulty based on the situation or resisted by a target's roll. The amount over or under the Difficulty indicates the Degree of Success.

    Vortex uses degrees of success which are:

    9+ Above: Fantastic, Yes you succeed and something else good happens
    4-8 Above: Good, a pure unadulterated success
    0-3 Above: Success, Yes you succeed, but something unexpected complicates the success
    1-3 Below: Failure, you fail but things could have been worse or their is a glimmer of opportunity to turn things around
    4-8 Below: Bad, you fail outright
    9+ Below: Disastrous, you fail and things get worse

    \nOuroboros Engine is a slick one that neatly encapsulates the "list of good/bad stuff" stakes that you pay for in one-to-one fashion for your desired result. That is, with a roll of three more successes than needed, you can pick three goodies from the happy list of outcomes. If you got one less than you needed, you can eat one of the bad ones. Or eat more of them to also pay for some good outcome possibilities. I think of it as similar to the *World systems in this way, but lacking in as many predetermined stakes as you find in the Moves.
