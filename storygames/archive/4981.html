\nAuf Jason M:\n
Everything holding together? Upgrades stable? What's the plan for the next six months? How can we help?
\nOnly 10 days or so late with my response. :-)

So here's just a stream of consciousness thing:

* Overall I love it here. Personally, I've found my online gaming talk experience seriously atrophying. I've retreated from most online sites and pretty much sticking around here, checking RPGNet once a day or so, and from there skimming the Forge and Gamecraft now and then. It's basically because the discussion that goes on here (and for the most part, the tone) really works for me here than other places. But that's just me. But me saying, "I still love what we've got here".

* We had a server move. The third since the creation of the site. Here's some stuff on that front:
-- The move was successful. Except for the fact that foreign lang characters got corrupted. I'll make sure to look into not doing that again if we have to backup/restore.
-- We moved originally from NetProHosting (they host a number of my sites currently) because our mySQL install was killing the global performance.
-- We then moved to Dreamhost, and it was awesome for a while. But they've been having some real chronic issues with stability on the hosts, stability on the mySQL DB server, etc. We were crashing every few days, and mySQL was lagging all the time.
-- I found out that this was because the "Notify me by email when there's a new post on this thread" 3rd party extension for Vanilla is some total crap, and a total resource hog. Turning that off got us a reprieve, but the writing was already on the wall at that point.
-- We moved to MediaTemple, on one of their virtual grid storage machines, a few weeks back. Aside from one planned outage that I didn't pay attention to, I've been enthusiastic about the uptime and performance.
-- I haven't turned on the "notify" extension, because, agian, crap. But to compensate, I've left the feeds up and running, and I've also increased the number of displayed threads to the max number so that you can check back in 2-5 days and pick up on the threads you posted to, without having to check back every single time a new post is added to your thread.

* The social traffic has been great. I'm the administrator and mod of this site. And I rarely ever have to mod. Ironically, the only mod action I had to take in the last month or two happened this last week. But the site runs itself. As a mod who is also a participant, who also has an extraordinarily complex and stressful job with lots of overtime, this is a godsend. You may notice that there are runs of 2-4 days in a row where I only log in maybe once just to see what's up, without posting or nothing. And yet, there's plenty of interesting stuff to come back to when I finally get a chance.
-- I love that I don't have to hem and haw and create artificial traffic to generate "teh buzz"
-- I love that even 3 weekdays of traffic, the posts have not scrolled off the front page (admittedly, I bumped it to 100 threads per page). So I can take a few days off here and there and not worry about missing anything.
-- I love that the place pretty much moderates itself. There's still a community feel here. There are "rules", but for the most part I don't see us often having to fall back on them.

* Traffic stats, for your reading pleasure:
-- About 3000 unique sessions per day. (urchin)
-- About 44,000 hits/page hits per day.
-- About 280 mb/xfer a day.
-- Funniest Search Term for Referral to this Site: "panty + crud"

    \n Oh, one thing that I wanted to talk about a little:My concern with "design" on Story Games.

    So remember how I had that rule about "no ground-up design here"? And how later I had kinda retracted that? Here's a bit of the story behind that:

    * So I never wanted to replace The Forge, or to make this place into A Forge for Folks who Don't Like the Forge. When it comes to putting a game together, getting feedback, and especially getting information on design and publishing resources (where to print, etc), Forge is second only to Your Friends You Game With (IMO anyway).

    * But hey, we have a lot of Forge folks here. That's cool, because this place started out being nothing more than a "The Forge Birthday Forum, focusing on light game talk" that never ended.

    * However, the real main purpose of the site had been kinda threefold:
    -- To be kind of an enthusiastic "RPGNet" of small press games.
    -- A place to discuss techniques for playing and running games, and
    -- A place to discuss new ways of playing and running games (not necessarily tied to design, but sometimes), be it for small press or traditional RPGs.

    * The idea of "designing on the site", or "getting feedback for your game idea", was supposed to be something that happened every once in a while, and sort of a privelege for SG regulars: You show up, you realize you like the community, you hang out for a bit and post or just lurk around, and if you think this is the kind of place with people who will give you good feedback, you post about your game idea.

    * Essentially, I didn't want the "threads that focus on the purpose of the site" to get scrolled away by one post after another from new people showing up saying "Help me with my game", new users joining just to suck up that feedback without, I dunno, "giving their due to others" and all that.
    -- That's why the Game Design Help topic is hidden to outsiders: It reinforces that it's a membership thing only. It also prevents folks from using this place as an open design blog for the public. It reinforces the "you're a member, here's a privelege, get some feedback and do something with it" model.
    -- And yet, I still got nervous at times, as I saw many people join with a Join Message of "I'm working on a game", or "I want feedback on my design", etc. Sometimes like 20 at a time.
    -- And yet... My fears remain unfounded. Folks have been using the site as intended, and we haven't been swamped with a million threads that would be better served on The Forge or RPGNet's Design Forum.
    -- So I chilled the hell out and don't worry about it much anymore. Because everything is in order.
    -- And furthermore, it's not just "in order", but the ideas and threads generated in the design threads have been really awesome, and something that I'd like to see continue, in just the proportions that we see today.

    -Andy
