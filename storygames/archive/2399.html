So I've been thinking about Troy and Tony's recent threads.

It seems to me that both threads (at least in their titles) purport to be about making a general thing (frex: religion, history) a more interesting subject for roleplaying, but in fact the original authors of the thread are interested in a really specific kind of help related to something they're working on. I don't think either Troy or Tony were attempting to be intentionally misleading, attracting people to a thread supposedly about open brainstorming, and Troy was nice enough to let us have a neat conversation about religion in roleplaying despite the fact that it wasn't really what he wanted. But I guess I like the awesome-o-fy threads because they could be an opportunity to bring the collective creativity of SG to bear on making roleplaying better or more interesting.

If people want us to help awesome-o-fy their games or specific ideas that they have, that's cool. But honestly, I kinda hope they'd do it in a thread that was labeled as such, instead of one with such an open-ended title. A thread about lasersharking history would be cool. But a thread about making history cooler without lasersharking it would be neat as well. John Kim's right that there's certainly more than one way to awesome-o-fy history, and it'd be nice to discuss those.

So, yeah. I guess I'm just hoping that we could do some better labeling and thread introductions when it comes to making it clear what kind of responses you're hoping to get. Are you really opening up discussion on a topic? Or are you really asking for a specific kind of response? The latter is cool, but I suspect that the former might be more interesting, leading you to things that you might not have anticipated.

    I'm glad Andy brought up the BSG bit. That's what I remember the earliest "awesome-o-fy" threads being about: remaking an old setting in the same way that Sci-Fi Channel remade BSG, by stripping out the fluff and turning other details into central elements of the show's theme. The original BSG used conflict with the Cylons, a sneak attack, and myths about a 13th tribe to set up a "Wagon Train in space", then treated those details as a mere gimmick; the new BSG picked those elements out and focused on them to create central themes about interracial (interspecies) conflict, treason, and religion.

    I've seen some slight tendency towards lasersharking in even the early threads, which may point to a fundamental disagreement about what it means to awesome-o-fy something. What I would like to see is more BSG-like awesome-o-fying: take just a few throwaway details from an old RPG setting and discovering what kind of interlocked themes you could build from that. The problem with lasersharking, other than the fact that it gets out of hand, is that it's just a gimmick, just like that stuff in the old BSG.

    Take the history thread. "Awesome-o-fy history" to me doesn't mean lasersharking George Washington so that he can grow to thirty times his own size and fight the British singlehanded; it would mean taking a few minor elements like the myth about the cherry tree/"I cannot tell a lie" and his refusal to accept a nomination as king and trying to combine those into some kind of theme, something about the conflict of honesty and power. Any unnatural/nonhistorical details added should serve that theme; maybe the Freemasons teach pseudopsi powers of detecting lies and hiding reactions, and the game becomes one of rebels banding together to fight for freedom, but none are sure which of their brethren may actually be lying about it...

    You could even treat this as a Game Chef-like challenge: pick one game setting, let everyone find one or two elements in the setting to focus on and develop a theme around.
