This is totally a card-interpretation oracle, it doesn't detail any setting elements from the Pathfinder setting.

Suit of Hammers:
The Paladin: A hero, bound by honour, faced by overwhelming foes and rich temptations\u2026
The Keep: A fortress, unassailable from without, unshaken through great hardships\u2026
The Big Sky: Freedom and a new start, hard won, and those who would return to the chains\u2026
The Forge: A trial by fire, some failing\u2026
The Bear: Savage strength, turned to a civilized purpose, but only for a time\u2026
The Uprising: Fanaticism, a force swelled beyond the control of any leader\u2026
The Fiend: The deaths of many in a great calamity, to be delivered by a dark intelligence\u2026
The Beating: An attack from all sides, fraying the sanity of the defenders\u2026
The Cyclone: War, destroying everything it touches, and the ones in its path\u2026

Suit of Keys:
The Dance: A delicate pattern showing the way, and the temptation of the flames\u2026
The Cricket: An auspicious journey, promising treasure\u2026
The Juggler: A great power, author of many fates, by turns relentless and benign...
The Locksmith: A stranger's aid, badly needed, with strings attached...
The Peacock: A vision of great beauty, sundering convention\u2026
The Rabbit Prince: A lucky blow, landed in riotous melee\u2026
The Avalanche: The stampeding feet of a panicked throng\u2026
The Crows: Marauders, fresh from a life destroyed, cackling\u2026
The Demon's Lantern: False hopes, sold for a high price to youths who can ill afford them\u2026

Suit of Keys:
The Trumpet: A public declaration, resounding over the crowd; a call to action\u2026
The Survivor: A survivor, driven on by the fallen...
The Desert: An arduous journey, and the guide who is key to its success\u2026
The Brass Dwarf: A dark choice between the one and the many\u2026
The Teamster: A life of constant toil, and the masters who hold the whip\u2026
The Mountain Man: Raw force unleashed, unopposable, but perhaps survivable\u2026
The Tangled Briar: The ashes of an ancient tragedy, nourishing present strife\u2026
The Sickness: A soul's corruption, leaving a stain upon the flesh\u2026
The Waxworks: A captive, helpless, and his gloating torturer\u2026

Suit of Tomes:
The Hidden Truth: An arcane secret, its seeker, and the one who can reveal it\u2026
The Wanderer: A thing discarded, and the transient who spies its true value\u2026
The Joke: A terror made flesh, and the clever plan to bring it down...
The Inquisitor: A painful truth, denied by fools who court disaster\u2026
The Foreign Trader: A prosperous foreigner, bargaining with secrets...
The Vision: A work of genius, half-finished, and its architect, half-mad\u2026
The Rakshasa: A broken mind, turned to a new purpose, part of a greater design\u2026
The Idiot: Blackmail, bribery, and debasement, now normal in the halls of power\u2026
The Snakebite: A poisoned weapon, used carefully to turn friends against one another\u2026

Suit of Roses:
The Winged Serpent: A sage, sought for counsel as fate hangs on a knife-edge\u2026
The Midwife: A disastrous arrival, and the one who glimpses providence\u2026
The Publican: A clear-headed arbiter, called on by a trusted friend\u2026
The Queen Mother: A noble circle, preservers of knowledge, observers of a crucial ritual\u2026
The Owl: Nature's power, blessed and cursed in the same moment\u2026
The Carnival: The illusion of power, with real risks\u2026
The Eclipse: A crisis of faith, striking during a hard pilgrimmage\u2026
The Mute Hag: The stare of a matriarch, and the poisonous secret she holds over her enemy\u2026
The Lost: A madman, his ears filled with the babble of unwanted companions\u2026

Suit of Crowns:
The Empty Throne: Loss, felt keenly as the great are honoured\u2026
The Theater: A prophetic performance, a message for its true audience...
The Unicorn: A feast, generously offered, widely accepted\u2026
The Marriage: A wedding, irreversibly joining disparate factions\u2026
The Twin: A reflection glimpsed at a moment of divided loyalties...
The Courtesan: Social niceties, delicately observed to curry favor\u2026
The Tyrant: A ruler, blight upon those ruled...
The Betrayal: A false friend, steering the envied towards devastation...
The Liar: Unrequited love, the temptation to a deadly risk...

    Posted By: Ryan Stou'nPosted By: Valvorikhave player who draws it write the "one liner" right there and then.
    I dunno... I've had creative panic and blank-stare-gridlock when trying to do IAWA like stuff with that. The Joshua A C Newman oracle rule (Objects of Desire, Events that adds pressure, or Poeple in Need in whatever combination) acts as a support against total catastrophe but I'm still not sure I'd do it.

    There are people who will have an easier time reacting to pictures than text.

    I think there may be creative panic if you draw the cards and then say "write a line of text to summarize the elements of this picture, evocatively, adhering to one of these categories: [JACN's categories]".

    However, to draw four cards from the Harrow deck, like:
    The Teamster (wizard dude riding on the shoulders of an ogre, whipping him with an ugly flail)
    The Tangled Briar (skeleton hanging on stick, in a briar patch)
    The Snakebite (Wicked wizard dude, with a curved knife, dripping poison)
    The Publican (Cyclops, pouring beer)

    It's pretty easy for me to look at those and see a situation. Like:

    1.) The gnomes are clearing the briars to advance their empire, and have harnessed the ogres to do so. But some ogres are resisting, and Snakebite the assassin has been hired to take them down. The Publican, a cyclopian bartender, is attempting to cater to both populations while racial tensions rise and become volatile.

    2.) Snakebite, a dark mage from the tangled briar, has decided to kill his long-time nemesis, the cyclopian bartender (who retired from adventuring to open the bar). His army of evil gnomes has risen, ready to destroy the good people. Their leader rides atop an enslaved ogre.

    I think it requires a bit more reading into the elements, but the elements are also a lot freer and more open to interpretation.
