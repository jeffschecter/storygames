\nEpisode 25 is up on the Virtual Play podcast. It's 30 minutes of RPG goodness in the form of a look at some of the highlights from Bill White's adventure "Rex Rich and the Warlord of Mars" from Dreamation. The episode looks at the idea of 'GM force' by contrasting a scene where Compels could have been used in the game but weren't, versus a scene where Compels were used to guide the plot in a particular direction.

    \n Thanks for that. I often find that some people take "deprotagonization" as a bit too much of a boogeyman (and I like how the one player took the incident as "scene framing" instead of "deportagonization"), I liked the discussion of how in hindsight the GM could work with the players and achieve the same result with more satisfaction by all involved.

    I also found the comments and illustrations about the old habit of "roll for initiative" demarking where you transitioning from pure narration to system-directed gameplay rather interesting.

    One technique I have adopted is to, as the GM, act as the players' eyes and ears. They don't know anything about the environment unless you tell them. In turn, (simulationist in me speaking here) to do that in a manner consistent with the character the player has modeled, you should inform the player in accordance with what their skills or abilities say.
