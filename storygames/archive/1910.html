    I figure:
    * folks whisper or e-mail me name, shipping address, and a desired item. After I get a couple entries, I'll begin matching people up--aiming to match people in the same country, where possible.

    * suggested price range: umm, I guess that's a matter of collective taste. Given shared interest in indie rpgs, CD's, and DVD type things, I'm thinking $15-$25, but that might be too much trust among strangers? I don't know. For me, that's an acceptable risk.

    * Maybe--maybe--along with the gift, a card or something. Just because this is mainly about being social and knitting a community, rather than getting phat loot.

    * But! If nobody hooks you up with phat loot, what do we do about it? I figure, you send an e-mail to me, and then I try to nudge the person to follow through. If that doesn't work, does it make sense to publish a list of non-compliers after a respectable interval, or is that totally uncool? (I'm of two minds about it.     PS. In 1999, some friends invented "Brumes," which piggybacks on other holidays for one hour at a time. (Thus, there's "Easter Brumes," "Arbor Day Brumes," etc.) Brumes is celebrated by eating a meal with friends (traditionally, Mexican fast food), and having a ludicrous argument of dubious relevance to the rest of the world. This can range from stoner questions ("Can you really compare a movie to its own sequel?") to the absurd ("Is the outcome of the Battle of Helms Deep realistic?") to the picayune ("Is a burrito a sandwich?"). Essentially, if you have a stupid, silly argument with your loved ones on a holiday, you're celebrating Brumes.
