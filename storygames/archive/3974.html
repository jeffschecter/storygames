    \n After taking control of the project, I organized the Revised version in a more intuitive fashion, including an easier to follow layout. We also added a history of the pulps as well as some new material, while cleaning up the writing. The new cover by Jeremy McHugh also complements his interior artwork from the original, and we used some other, terrific pulp art.
    \n
    \nWell, the thing about the games I publish is that they typically reach the casual, lapsed, and "no time to talk" gamers (this includes the college professors using Coyote Trail in the classrooms). Two-Fisted Tales is actually a departure from the other games as it is more involved (the other games are intended as pick-up and diversionary games, and for those with short attention spans). Because of this, they are busy playing rather than recording their activities or spending much time on the web, so actual play accounts are far and few between.
