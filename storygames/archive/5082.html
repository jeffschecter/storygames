    \n Hey all,

    In an email, Antoine asked me a couple questions about the changes I made between the first and second editions. \n
    Antoine- why did you replace the immunity to death and possibility to put your life at risk in a given combat with the present rules (in the healing section) ? It seems to me that 0 hit points in a combat will happen a lot and given the present rules, you'd better not to. Same thing for the First One Down experience rule.
    \nI took out the immunity to death thing for a couple reasons. The most important is that it wasn't making me happy. I just wasn't digging it. I like the potential violent end for these adventuring characters.

    I'll point out that you really don't have all that much chance of loosing your character if there's someone around to nurse you back to health. Check my math on this, but I'm pretty sure that the two rolls give you about a 94% chance of survival every time you drop to 0 HP.

    Playtesting will tell for certain if I like this better than the immunity thing. But I'm guessing that if I change anything now, it'll be to increase your chances of survival.\n
    - why the very heavy weapons ? With their 3 points of damage people will always chose them rather than heavy weapons if they have the size (of course).

    If you have the size to handle the very heavy weapon, then no, there is no reason for you to take a heavy weapon instead. You might as well get the damage in. I made this change to give the characters with a big Size a slightly greater bonus.

    But! Consider that there's nothing that says the GM cannot make a treasure Heavy. Or Very Heavy. Or Very-Very Heavy. Can your character carry out that 8' gold statue AND swing a boulder on a stick?

    Once again, playtesting will tell for certain if I've made a mistake or not.

    \n Hey,

    I am glad to see a thread going on about the Red Box Hat. I love it when people play a game I love. And I want to read actual play reports. I want more GM to try more ways of preparing for the game. I want to hear about monsters, treasures, talents combos and cool adventures.

    Like I said to Eric this version of the game seems realy better than the first one. Please note that the rules were already very good. But now their realy tightly woven. You'll see it when you'll run a fight with those rules : the synergy of arenas, weapons, show off and monster powers leads to one of the most fun fighting experience I've had in RPGs. Test it, try combos with monster powers if you're a GM, describe crazy stunts or teamwork if you're a player and see the awesome. The whole beast is for me what I hoped to find in Iron Heroes before I dropped the book.

    And this version even introduce a conflict system that uses an original and simple way to set the stakes. It makes me want to try more non combat action in my next games.

    The only real concern I have (and that's why I asked Eric my question) is with the disappearance of the death immunity. When I ran my two games, I didn't feel that the excitement of the game was in the fear of the PC death in every fight. I think it's more in the possibility to be the first one to go down. It's like, you know, the feeling you have when you play a realy good boardgame and you're the first to loose. Or when you're fencing and loose a match, even if you don't loose the whole challenge. I think it's what I felt when Jerome's PC went down to 0 HP in the first game.

    And the statistic issue that John raised seems a problem for playtest. You'll need a lot of combats to realy appreciate if this rule change is good or not.

    Finaly I realy like the opportunity to "raise the flag" that you had when you declared that a fight was potentially deadly. There's a real step-on-up quality here. Personnaly I would use this rule a lot. Cause that's my way of the ninja, you know. And back comes the violent death possibility that you like, Eric. But, that's only my thoughts as a fan and not the author. The aesthetic choices are yours.

    So people, now, go play the Red Box Hack, please

    \n Names are tricky stuff. They give power over which is named, but mainly because they tend to box which is named into something that it is not, or something more restrictive than what it can be. I have always been very bad at remembering names (particularly of my students), because I'd rather know someone for who he is, as a whole (or at least as much as I can figure) rather than a few letters. However, names are necessary to communicate, albeit imperfectly, about which is named. One way to name a thing, in order to be able to point at it without giving a false idea of what it is (or can be), would be to use "labels" with no meaning per se: for example, random sequences of letters, or even numbers. If everything would be labeled by numbers (the first one to appear would be 1 (that would label "light"), and then each new thing in the world would take the subsequent number (I would then probably be known as 245 215 785 124 854 963 547 852 457 896 521 215 155 520, or something like that, rather than "Jerome")), then there would be no danger to give a false impression of something by using its name.

    However, I believe we all agree that "487 254 265 965 745 251 589 632 585 748 965 852 963 845 689 875" is not a very sexy or even practical name for RBH2. So, what would be?

    If you have read up to here, you might think that I am a little mad (well, who isn't?), but, in the yadaya above, I addressed one issue of finding a new name for RBH (which Eric seems to consider): a name shouldn't give false impression on the game. I personnaly enjoy "Redbox Hack" as a name, but probably because, not having english as a native language, this name has no intrinsec meaning for me other than to designate the game (I understand the meaning once I think about it, but that's not what comes in my mind at first when I hear it; I also wonder if this it not the reason why Eric wanted that Antoine suggests french names :P).

    "So, Jerome, stop your baloney and give some insights on the name..."

    Obviously, and although Eric seems to have some ideas of the ambiance/world of RBH, the game is not written around something very precise concerning the background (although the rules and classes give some feeling on this). The name should therefore not be centered around the background, which is otherwise probably the best place to look for a name.

    The name should therefore be quite generic. There are few generic RPG (compared to RPG having a more specific background). I doubt that a complete acronym, such as "GURPS", is something interesting for RBH. In fact, D&D is a generic RPG in the sense that the background is quite loose (it revolves around fantasy, but no world is detailed (or not *one* world), etc.); however, the name of D&D is centered around the opposition to the PC, which is not what RBH wants. So perhaps a name of this kind, but revolving around the PC: "Adventure!" (with the explanation mark) was my first idea, but still to far from the PC; so "Adventurers!" was my natural second thought; but I then found that something was missing, a touch of fantasy/mystery to attract the eye and mind of the potential player: I then turned to things as "Adventurer heart" ("Coeur d'aventurier" in french - I thought about it in french, I am not sure about the translation), that is to say still revolve around the idea of "adventurer" - which is the basic of the games, from the point of view of the players: we play adventurers, and the pleasure is fully in the adventure. But I am not completely content with "heart", but I really wanted to add a "peculiar" touch to the "adventurer" side of the name... perhaps "spirit" instead of "heart", or something completely different but intriguing enough to promote the game...

    As we all seem to agree here, one of the *majors* interests of the game is in the simple yet effective combat system, and the gamist pleasure we take with the tokens, the arenas, etc. However, I found myself completely dry when trying to come up with a name revolving around this particular point of the game. The more I thought about it, I am not sure it is a good idea to use a name revolving around an element of the system, even if it is something we really enjoy.

    Another idea is to pick a name a little bit further to the game, but with the right "feeling" for the game. "Warhammer" is an example that comes to my mind: although the warhammer has a little meaning with respect to the background of this game, the name is efficient because the image of a warhammer gives a pretty good image of the mood of the game. One way to do this while still giving an idea of "PC-centered" and of adventure, a possible choice (although quite basic, I know) would be "Dangerous world", or something along those lines.

    I hope you'll forgive my awfully long post. I am not good at finding/using names (re-read the beggining of the post if you already forgot why ), but I hope I gave some elements of thoughts to those, better than me, who also think about a new name for RBH.

      In boargames they call it the problem of "player elimination." One person's fun stops prematurely while the fun continues for others.
