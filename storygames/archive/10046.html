    \n Genre fiction. Fighty. Heavily thematic. Grabby mechanics. Not too progressive or artsy. I think you can add Dogs, Mouse Guard, Lacuna, and Agon to that list.

    Jason (or somebody) was saying in another thread that it's impossible to know which (free) games will explode and be the next big thing. Honestly, I don't think that's true. I think it's reasonably easy to predict which games will explode in the SG / Forge scene, though designers' personal biases and blinders about their own games may make their own success harder to predict. But it wouldn't have taken a rocket scientist to look at any one of the games on that list and say: "Oh yeah, the SG / Forge crowd will eat that up." It's not just about the mechanics and play of the game, though, since how you present it is also important. Mist-Robed Gate for example, shares all of the above traits, but it's not really written in a real visceral way that's grabby to the majority of folks in this scene. Nine Worlds is another great game that didn't explode, despite sharing these traits.

    So yeah -- fighty genre games with grabby mechanics -- but it's also the pitch. People have to be able to envision themselves playing it very quickly and having a grand ole time.

    But it's also important to realize that the SG / Forge crowd and your target audience aren't necessarily the same. Success in one is not success in the other.
