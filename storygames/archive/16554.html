    \n Funny you ask.
    Not a role playing game, but a board game.

    I was working on a game called "Stock Market Jump". The concept of it was to manage your assets (worker placement), while making the market as volatile as possible for others. All the actions you take in the game are to push each other off the top of the New York Stock Exchange building.

    My wife said it was too morbid have to have execs committing suicide in droves in my board game....so it might get re-skinned into Lemmings.

    \n I guess you'd have to understand the current economic crisis before you could write a proper game about it. I mean, is there anyone who actually does understand what's happening at the moment? I keep a vague eye on these things and am convinced that it's inevitable that Grexit will occur and inevitable that it won't. 8S

    You could maybe do a game about the subprime crisis, we've got a pretty good handle on that now. Probably as a larp. You'd need quite a few players though because you need competition in every part of the process and there are about five parts, so you'd need between 10 to 15 teams of people. I can't imagine it being much fun, though. Maybe it could be an educational exercise in how to build a system that will ultimately destroy itself.
