    Funny story time: when I first got into roleplaying, we played a lot (a lot) of Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles, which had percentile tables to determine which animal you were mutated from. We always rolled on the tables; it was breaking the rules not to roll on the tables. We developed these giant, baroque rules for rolling on the tables and dealing with inauspicious rolls (if you roll and one die falls off the table, you don't have to take the result if you don't like it... but if you do like it, it's all yours). Then, ten years later, I rediscovered my old TMNT books, and lo and behold, at the top of the table it reads, "Roll or pick." Somehow we missed that line.

    So, moral of story, I like how Story Games Names project has it set up, with numbered lists of 20. Any bonehead with a d20 can figure out how to roll on it if they need to or want to.
