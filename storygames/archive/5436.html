\n The summer after my sixth grade, my best friend and I discovered Warhammer Fantasy Roleplay. We loved it. It took us a little while to get to playing, but it became some memorable stuff. We snatched up every White Dwarf we could get our hands on, and we just sat around drooling over all the cool things within.

A few months later, he bought this thing called Warhammer 40,000: Rogue Trader. Now, we weren't really wargamers. We really liked all the few minis he had (I didn't have many, myself), but we were more into role-playing.

But, this book just knocked my socks off. It was the coolest thing I had ever seen in a game. And, there was just enough stuff in there to tease me that it might be a role-playing game. I vaguely recall lists of equipment a navigator might have. I can't even remember just what the heck one did with that book besides drool over it.

Today is the release of Dark Heresy, the first official Warhammer 40,000 role-playing game. I'm pretty pumped! I won't get mine for a few more days, likely. Now, I'm an indie curmudgeon to beat all hell, and I usually just frown when I pick up mainstream RPGs these days. Chances are Dark Heresy will have a bunch of stuff that'll drive me batty.

But, damn it, if that book doesn't already make me feel like a kid again.

I can't wait to see it! The previews have me pretty pumped. I really dig what I see, and what I've seen of the presentation of the game knocks me out. Yay!

I've already seen a few people chomping at the bit for this one. So, let's hear it! Do you have it yet? What do you think?

    \n It's great to log on to SG this morning with my cup of tea and see so much excitement for Dark Heresy! See some of you might know already that when I'm not a hippy Story Gamer, I also work for GW and wrote part of Dark Heresy, and I can't wait to see what people think and do with DH!

    We've been playing with it since before christmas and it's a blast. I get an indecent thrill playing my cold, calculating, part-machine tech adept who views humans as potential errors to be corrected.

    Yes, it wears it's WFRP heritage on its sleeve, but it's definitely a progression, a more streamlined form. Yes, your basic stats whiff a little at low levels, but most of the time you won't be rolling just basic stats - you'll be including bonuses from skills and equipment and circumstances. Plus you can feasibly start at any rank too, so there's no need to go for the whole "from the gutter up" approach.

    Anyway, I'm really excited about this and it's great to see excitement here too amongst my fellow story gamers! It's been 20 years too long, but we've got it in the end!
