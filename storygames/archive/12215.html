So, John Wick's a solid guy, and he's known for dishing out and loving brutal honesty. Honesty fists wrapped in Muai Thai boxing tape, dipped in broken glass.

The thing is, I've never really had a good experience with any of the Wick-made games I have save the original L5R [EDIT: Oh, and Cat, forgot about Cat]. Discordia, Enemy Gods, Houses of the Blooded, Orkworld, etc etc. Basically my issue has been "Loved the ideas, but didn't care for the execution".

But, I'm a sucker for people's interpretations of Japan.

And shit, five dollars? I spent more on popcorn this weekend, watching a mediocre movie that cost twice that. There's no way that a $5 PDF isn't going to inspire something for one of my future home games, be it content in particular, or an interesting rule. So on that front, John totally wins.

Plus, he's going for the interesting thing that Fred and crew stumbled onto with Dresden Files: Release the PDF early, wait a few weeks to collect feedback and typos, then fix before sending the book to the printers. This is some true digital ashcan stuff right here.

-Andy

    \n I ran this over the weekend and my players had a blast.
    \n
    \nIt's more focused than Houses of the Blooded in the sense that each player having a role made for more interesting ways to tell the story. With Honor and Blood, I think my players were less focused on dicking each other over (like what seemed to happen in my Houses game) and more focused on creating a compelling story to be a part of. They were a bit new to games that give narrative control to the players and were at first a bit unsure how to handle it. But, by the end of the session they were getting the hang of it and coming up with all kinds of neat stuff.
    \n
    \nHowever, for a game that is supposed to be "stand-alone" and not an add-on to HotB, it's missing a ton of information. I'm going to email John Wick and let him know my experiences so hopefully this can be remedied before the final release later this month.

    \nYou don't have to thank me. As you can tell by the Pros section, all the players (myself included) seemed to really enjoy the session we had with the game. I think fleshing out some of those missing parts of the system, clarifying some of the rules a bit (Glory Ranks and Reputation are a bit confusing as you can see in my posts after the original one I made), and cleaning up the actual text (there's a lot of typos and cut-and-paste errors [Style Points vs. Honor Points]) will make this really awesome game become even better. I like that the PDF was released slightly ahead of the book and John Wick is not opposed to absorbing the feedback and putting it into the final document. It's going to make the game that much better.
    \n
    \nI'm hoping to get a follow-up session in this weekend and go over the Duel, War and Season Actions rules in the game and maybe provide some feedback for those aspects of the system as well before the June 14th deadline for revisions.
    \n
    \nIf Wick can pull off putting this feedback to work in the book, I'll be the first to shell out the $40 for a print copy.
