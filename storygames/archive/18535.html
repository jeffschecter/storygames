So this is a game that I wrote recently. It's weird, and an homage to David Cronenberg's Crash. It's about sex and cars and strange obsessions with objects. It's basically a nano game, and I'd rather share it than keep it hidden away in my google drive. If you play it I'll probably freak out with excitement and make you tell me lots of stories about what happened.

Crash Into You

Read it, play it, share it, tell me stories about it.

    \n It was perhaps a month ago that I stumbled upon nano games, and I find them interesting. It's the kind of roleplaying game that I want to play nowadays. I don't want to spend hours just to read a game. I want to read the game and play it right away for an hour and, it it's fun, play it again right away. I like that the nano games are increasing, because with more of those kinds of games, the more variations in how to play them.

    I really liked how you created relations between the characters, and that you established goals from the start. You kind of lost me at first with the action, and I think that just by looking at it from a different perspective, you could have explained it easier with fewer words.

    I wonder how the descriptions of fetishes will work in play, and I also wonder about the maths behind the mechanics because it seems like an almost never-ending game, but that's something that is best answered during playtest.

    Thanks for the read!

    \n I love the scene-setting rules. "Describe in order: your object fixations, how your character looks, what your car looks like (regardless of where it is), what you\u2019re wearing, the weather, where the scene is taking place." This is stuff I'm always trying to cram into other games (well, except saving "where" for last), and having a game that's about so few things and that's one of them (so it's a big deal! so don't skimp on it!) is sweet.
