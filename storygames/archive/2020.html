    I'm half way through a research project on the renaissance. I started with Wikipedia for background on the stuff that was relevant. Wikipedia articles often list other sources.

    Then I went to Amazon to find some good books. I don't buy the books on Amazon. I just use it to find the good ones then request them from my library. Even a small library can request books on interlibrary loan. The great thing about Amazon is that once you find a few books, that leads you to lists and recommendations for more good books.

    Historical fiction is also a decent source if it's well researched. And it's fun to read. Documentaries can also be really good.

    Also, try a good used book store with a history section. Ask the staff what's good. I got a couple of great sources that way.

    Specifically for Victorian stuff, I recommend the site Victorian London and Liza Picard's book, also called Victorian London. But, yes, where and what are you researching?

    In general, I tend to browse round a book shop, flicking through anything that looks interesting: just recently, I was trying to research Victorian academia, and that was a nightmare. Also, Google searches are surprisingly useful. One of the best things to do, though, is go to the place you're researching and look in local bookshops for history books of that place.
