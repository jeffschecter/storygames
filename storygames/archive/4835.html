    \n The name of the classical Chinese novel is "The Journey West" or \u897f\u6e38\u8bb0\u3002The literal translation of this is "a record of the western journey" or something like that. Such a name is really inappropriate for a role-playing game: it is clearly not a record of that famous journey, but it's own thing. So the game is called \u897f\u6e38\u8dfb, which means, rough, "the footsteps of the western journey," and is also totally homophonous with the title of the original book. This is more appropriate for the role-playing game: we're not reciting a record; we're telling our own story in the footsteps of the original. The English title also does this play on words: "Journeying West" is active and non-specific as opposed to "The Journey West."

    I'd like to say I came up with this all by myself, but actually the artist (Sushu Xia, see on the cover there?) came up with it. And Jonathan did a lot of help to in the "Ben, no, you can't say it that way" department.
