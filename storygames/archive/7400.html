    \n When was this "Setting design challenge?" I found a couple of threads referring to something from 2006, but if it hasn't been run this year, then let me make this offer: I would love to run a setting design challenge.

    It was a year ago this October that I entered my first online game design challenge (Re-Magi-Nation), and while I'm not interested in entering another at this point (burned out from the very personal nature of the last rpg I made), I find myself interested in trying to run one. I like the idea that Re-Magi-Nation had, to take a list of overdone (but repetitively so) concepts or genres, assign them randomly, and each entrant tries to do something new with them.

    Are people up for another kick at the can?

    \n I read that, but there is a bit of a problem with running that way a second time, for it poses no new challenge. It would be too easy to just go over familiar ground. That's what I like about rethinking a randomly assigned genre, it pushes people just a little, though I'd be tempted to include a "redraw" option if someone's really stuck.

    As before, the 10,000 word limit sounds good, as does the option to collaborate. I think a deadline would be a good thing, but 10 days (like Game Chef and Re-Magi-Nation) might be too tight, so I'm open to suggestions there.

    Another element I would want to change is the necessity to tie the setting to an existing system (and all the fun of seeking permission therein). You might have a system or mechanics in your mind, but the submission would not include it. I want the entrants to really think about what combinations of components (fiction, description, locations, npcs, quotes, adventure hooks, etc.), and details therein, contribute to a good roleplaying setting for that genre.

    Since "Setting Design Challenge" is really Fred Tarcikowski's contest, I suppose I should give this contest idea I'm proposing a different name. I'll think on it.
