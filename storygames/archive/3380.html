\n One of the games Ive been playing much of recently is the swedish fantasy rpg Drakar och Demoner:Trudvang (which translates to Dragons and Demons: Trudvang).

The game is set in a pretty non-standard fantasy world,based on norse sagas, myths and legends, heroic epics like the Finnish Kallevalla and cultural elements from finnish, celtic and slavic. The most defining thing about the setting is the many faery tale elements, and the stong norse feel.

Unfortunately for this wonderful world, the system is cumbersome and heavy.It works,and does what it is supposed to do, but the combat rules wheigh the thing down, and in the ong run it becomes unweildly.

Fortunately for me,theres the wonderful Shadow of Yesterday. You all know it, Im not gonna talk about it at length.

What I want here is general help creating new Secrets and Keys for this TSoY conversion, as well as some hints and tips regarding what tokeepin mind when playing/GMing TSoY. Ive only read it, never played it, so any pointers would be cool.

    \n To be honest, I don't think you need that much "conversion". The Keys and Secrets are what define the game and make it taste the way it does. Some of the basic Keys/Secret will be directly usable in your DoD game(I suppose. I only know the original DoD and DoD Expert, which IIRC were pretty bog standard fantasy using BRP and added some weird local things like ducks).

    Otherwise, have a look through the Key/Secret and discard those that goes against what you want. Then make some new ones - there are basic recipes for both Keys and Secrets in the book somewhere. And ask your players to come up with ideas as well.

    \n Keys are most critical, as they drive the story and give the game master really specific buttons to push for players. Next I'd say work out your Abilities list- abilities set the tone and help provide much of the flavor for your game. Since they are shared among all characters (everyone can use Athletics, at varying levels of competency), they set the stage for the kind of conflicts you expect to see in the game.

    \n If the setting includes cultures that characters can come from, culturally-specific keys and secrets are in order. You could also make Keys appropriate to any prominent or repeating roles from the myths. In my experience, "Feel" is captured by player behavior, and player behavior is steered by Keys, so I'd focus on making lots of them that encourage feel, and then adding appropriate secrets as a bonus.

    I'd like the Key of Clear-eyed Fatalism and the Secret of the Charge, please!

    \n Yeah, some keys to do with the idea of Wyrd or the Norse sense of destiny might be good. Not one single one - you'd have lots of folks taking it. But individual destinies. What's fun is watching players get points for driving their characters towards their destinies, but then potentially turning away from them before they get there. Will they accept their destiny or not?
