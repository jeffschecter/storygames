\n Hi all: We do a local Iron Game Chef event at a local con called MACE. MACE goes down next Friday, so the week before we release the ingredients and the registrants cut loose for a week of totally unsupervised IGC game-building. It will probably be my last year doing this at the Con, it's a solid event but I don't find it as satisfying as the communal feedback aspects of the regular Game Chef yearly event online.

Anyway, this year we decided to make it totally open to interpretation of game: RPGs are standard, but card/board games are kosher, too. I figure it might be hard to judge an RPG on the same plane as a card game... but on the other hand, there are usually only 3-4 submissions each year, so I don't see too much of a big problem with it.

Here was the ingredients. I decided to go back to the Rules and Themes split, which always is fun. And you'll probably notice that the rules are a lot less constraining than normal: Basically, we "LITE" up the Mace event because most of the folks participating have never done this kind of thing before, and figure we'd start off people on a brisk mental/creativity jog rather than a marathon.

Anyway, no one here is going to MACE so you can't participate, but just wanted to share in case they inspire:

================================================================================

CONSTRAINT ONE: THE RULES

You must choose two of the following rules constraints in your game:

1) The game must make use of (either actually or metaphorically) the classic Tarot deck.

2) The game must be scalable/playable from three people up to fifteen people.

3) The game must have some sort of internet or online component.

4) You must write sentences as part of the game.

5) The game must use no randomizers like dice or playing cards.


CONSTRAINT TWO: THE THEME

Your game must include two of the following themes somewhere in your game. The greater the prominence/importance of the theme word, the higher the game will score:

1) Korea

2) Highway

3) Axe

4) Ceramic

5) River

==============================================================

Again, this was for a local closed event, but hey if they inspire someone here (who isn't already participating in one of the three concurrent S-G game/design contests currently happening), go for it. I'll give you a gold star.

-Andy
