\n I am a big fan of the series of books and graphic novels by Ben Aaronovitch, collectively named after the first book in series, Rivers of London (sometimes also called the Peter Grant series after the main protagonist). For those not familiar, here is a brief extract from the main novel's description on Wikipedia:\n
The novel centres around the adventures of Peter Grant, a young officer in the Metropolitan Police; who, following an unexpected encounter with a ghost, is recruited into the small branch of the Met that deals with magic and the supernatural.
\nNot unlike the Dresden Files (although quite different in the tone), the series is set in present day, but it reveals that magic, fantastical creatures, parallel universes etc are in fact real. I have been playing with the idea of adapting this series into a RPG setting, but wanted to first check if anyone has tried that before, so we can compare notes.

    \n Actually, the system itself is the least of my worries -- I'm actually developing a generic system of my own, or I can easily use GURPS (although it's a bit too complex for my taste now), Risus, Fudge or any other.

    I'm more and more thinking about running it essentially like a modern-day Ars Magica (the Folly is basically a covenant) -- I know that Mage: the Ascension is supposed to be a modern version of that, but they kinda ruined it with forcing the integration with the World of Darkness.

    My main concern is the magic system, as I would at least like to capture whatever is already the book canon, making sure that I don't expand into obviously wrong directions.

    \n It's basically a key-word system, which is eminently adaptable to role-playing: I think most RPG systems could be adapted to fit something like this...

    Make a list of Statements: single word phrases that invoke a power, e.g. 'Move', 'Burn', etc. I'm skipping the Latin from Rivers of London for the sake of clarity.

    Make a list of Modifiers: these are ways to make the basic effect do something a little bit different (and you could also use Statements as Modifiers)

    Cast a Spell: roll a die for each Statement and Modifier used, take the lowest result to determine how effective the spell is; preparation can 'buy' off some of these dice, allowing you to ignore the lowest.

    Character skill and specialisation also comes into play, adding more effectiveness to spells that use certain Statements or reducing the backlash, etc.

    ...

    I recall something like that being used in the Occult magic system of Orrorsh in the original Torg game; I don't know if they've retained it in the new edition. I'm pretty sure it must feature in some storygames somewhere... but I can't think of one right now. There's always Mortal Coil, which is designed for creating magic systems as you play, so as long as everyone is familiar with the Rivers of London and agrees to stick to that style, you should be OK.

    \n I think the biggest decision to make up front when you adapt something like this is: who are the PCs? The obvious choice is that the PCs are all young cops dealing with the occult, just like in the stories, but you should decide up front if you're going to let anybody play a friendly ghost, or anthropomorphic river, or anything like that.

    As far as systems go, I think Unknown Armies would work, but an even better choice would be Witchcraft, a CJ Carella fave that runs on Unisystem and is free to download from DriveThru. It's got a nice magic system which is pretty consistent and tons of support and would probably do everything you want with minimal houseruling.
