\n So Stars Without Number is this free scifi RPG that you should totally go read. Among the killer ideas in it is how World Generation will give you two world tags to work with, things like "Flying Cities" and "Police State", or "Radioactive World" and "Psychic Worship" or whatnot. Each World Tag has a short list of associated Friends, Enemies, Complications, Things, and Places. And of course, you can combine these, so you might decide that an Enemy might be both a Psychic Slaver and a Bitter Mutant, or for Places that the Temple of the Mind lies on the Scorched Glass Plain, or so forth.

And while I don't know if the same five categories would be what you would need for a dungeon-crawling game, I really like the idea of adapting the general idea, so that, for example, I might combine the "Volcanic" and "Warped" dungeon tags, which would suggest mixing my fire-using and lava-dwelling monsters with amorphous horrors, madness-inducing, and extradimensional entities. Actually, "amorphous" and "lava-dwelling" work pretty well together, yeah? The Heart of the Volcano "Place" would obviously be combined with the Dimensional Vortex. Dressings would include tubelike tunnels with alien geometries and lava pits of strange, unearthly color. Etc, etc.

And of course, another person might combine "Wild" and "Catacombs" for a crypt overrun with hostile nature, and another might combine "Ruins" and "Submerged" for that one zone you get in every Sonic the Hedgehog game, and so on.

So, what tags should there be? What should go with each tag? Thoughts on the idea in general?

    \n Yes to this!

    Something that a lot of dungeon crawl games already have is the idea of the random encounter table. The encounter location or "terrain type" already implies a tag. In my Dungeon World maps, I've been linking terrain types to the Dungeon World Monster Settings:

    Caver Dwellers
    Denizens of the Swamp
    Legion of the Undead
    The Gnarled Woods
    Ravenous Hordes
    Twisted Experiments
    Creatures of the Lower Depths
    Planar Powers

    On top of this, I have a list or more generic terrain types, like city, forest, hill, dungeon, wasteland, crater, volcanic rift, underdark, and planar realm.

    By cross-indexing the two, you get a bunch of interesting possibilities. The Gnarled Woods plus Woods terrain is pretty easy to understand. Gnarled Woods plus Dungeon means something different. Twisted Experiments plus Woods is somethign else again.
