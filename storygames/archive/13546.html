    \n I've been wondering that myself, though probably from a different angle.
    I'm not a 4e fan, but a good friend of mine recommended it, so I ended up buying the base set and the Famine in Far-Go expansion.

    Surprisingly, I was really impressed with the way they did all of the different origins and how those combine to make your character. Enough so, that I was ALMOST willing to play it unhacked despite my dislike of 4e...until they got to the whole collectible card bits. Not a big fan of how that works, at least for the powers. The way the cards work for OmegaTech makes sense, with them being items and such, and being able to be used up. But the idea of switching through different powers? It makes me think of it too much as a RIFTS/Magic: The Gathering hybrid. I really hate the idea of players building their own, custom power and omega tech decks. Randomization I'm okay with, but when you start the whole deck-building thing, it stops becoming a roleplaying game, and instead becomes a CCG in my eyes.

    So, unless you like collectible card games, start off by making a house rule that the Game Master holds all the cards, and maybe players just gain new powers as they advance, and they don't switch out (but instead are limited-use in comparison to their Origin powers). So one power card at level 1, two at level 4, etc.

    When presenting it to your players, they don't need to know what Gamma World is (especially since this is almost nothing like the previous Gamma Worlds). Just give them the Big Mistake history as presented in the book, and that should give them a good idea. Or, if they're familiar with Paranoia, treat it like a variant of that, except the players are the people NOT inside Alpha Complex that the Computer keeps warning loyal citizens about. You could force them all to watch Tank Girl for some inspiration.

    If they've played any of the old Palladium Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles/After the Bomb games, all you'd have to do is reference that, since they kind of have the same feel, too.

    In the meantime, I'm thinking of hacking all of the Origins and a bunch of the powers and tech into Danger Patrol.

    \nBig list of links to Gamma world stuff on the intertubes.

    I prefer the double sided character sheet folded, but the online character builder wasn't available when we started. I might do that for characters and see what it prints out.

    The Character creation guide really streamlines the character build process which is a bit spread out in the book. And multiple copies can be printed out instead of everyone trying to rummage through 1 book.

    And you simply must read Gamma World: Over 30 years of I have no idea what is going on to put yourself in the correct frame of mind.

    My local group has played 2 sessions now, and our third will be this week. Embrace the silly is my advice.

    - Try to get players to explain why they have a foosball table in the back of a pickup truck pulled by two horses.
    - Let the radiation change up their mutations at the end of each encounter and get them to describe what their reactions are to these manifestations in their fellow player characters.
    - Be generous with the junk. My players love rolling on the big table of crap in the book.
    - Don't be afraid to kill a player, they get another chance to become something randomly cool. ( I was wary of this, but when I managed to kill my friends goth girl firestarter, he happily rolled up what is essentially a Thundercat. And was back happily slaughtering mutant pigs in no time).


    The base adventure really isn't anything more than a set of linked combat encounters. There really isn't any story there unless you push one on it.

    Posted By: merb101I guess I'm wondering how to have a storyline that is more than just a string of combat encounters, but doesn't constrain the players either.
    \nIn that case, maybe take an idea or two from Apocalypse World. When the players are making their characters, make sure they start as friends/comrades/fellow members of something, and that their group has a particular void they are trying to fill, whether that is making money, finding a particular pre-Mistake medicine that will cure the plague in their village, hunting down a former friend who killed another one of their friends, etc. Make sure they have "wants" and "needs" of their own that are defined, too, so that you can every now and then throw them a clue to something that interests them ("When you examine the remains of the emaciated Badder, you notice the trinket hanging around his neck is actually a pristine AI chip...maybe there are more where that comes from?")
