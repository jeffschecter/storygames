    \n Vader can certainly be a protagonist in Solar System terms! I wouldn't play something like the Star Wars trilogy as party-based, anyway - it's superficially like that, but the party is actually incidental; all the characters have their own issues and are only connected through parallel interests. Perfectly reasonable to alternate between scenes from both sides of the war.
    \n
    \nThat being said, my interpretation of the trilogy would make only Luke, Han, Vader and Obi-Wan (killed early) into player characters, as their choices drive the overall story. The rest are antagonists (the emperor, Jabba) ,support (Leia, the droids, Lando, etc.) and extras (everybody else, pretty much). A different emphasis might make Vader less workable as a PC, of course.

    \n With my local gaming group, we were thinking about what to play next. Someone suggested Star Wars, since it was a universe we were all familiar with.

    One of the players, though, used to love Star Wars with the passion of a thousand suns. The second trilogy just took that love out of him though, not spinning him over to hate, but simply removing all the love for Star Wars, like a surgeon extracting an appendix. So he feels nothing for Star Wars now but weariness. Certainly not enough to get back into SW, even if we were rightly determined to "awesome it up".

    After talking to him about it, we realized that the same was true for the rest of us. I mean, at some point in my life, I might watch a remastered SW or Empire again (especially Empire!), but man, Lucas must be a Tibetan Buddhist Monk or something: He spent countless hours creating a fantastic, wonderful world for all to love, then spent an equal amount of time wiping that love away, like a Sand Mandala.

    So yeah, in my camp, "none" is gonna be my final answer as well.

    (although unlike the other guy, I can put most of my ennui aside to enjoy a brief game for a bit... but it has to be really focused, or else it falls apart)

    \n Hey, that was my reaction to SW, too. I got disinterested enough with the first two new movies that I've yet to see the third.

    My theory is that this is "growing up" as much as the new movies being badly written trash (which I do think, too).

    Interestingly enough, thinking of hypothetically playing something SW-related, I wouldn't find it that onerous - I simply don't parse rpgs and movies in the same way, it's not that significant for me that there are movies based in the same setting. The movie is just one story, good or bad, while the rpg is limitless stories. I'm much more hampered with SW gaming by the simple fact that the extended setting gets rather lame when you start reading those travelogues about the geography and exports industry of individual planets. As if I care.

    Taking this into consideration, I see Solar System as a pretty perfect vehicle for what I'd be interested in doing with Star Wars: I wouldn't have to research anything that one wouldn't pick up from the movies, I could make up some cool rules for how the Force works, and heroes would be so heroic. Cool with me.
