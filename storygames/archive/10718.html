\n I've recently grown used to writing and editing in Google Documents. It's tremendously useful. I write the text: other people leave comments. It's superb.

Now, I'm wondering whether something similar is possible using Google Wave. One reason this might be useful is that Wave structures comments neatly: we can have structured conversations, rather than just isolated comments.

Having said that, I know Jason has doubts about whether Wave is useful for collaborative editing. What do you think? Have you tried it?

Graham

    \n I've been working on a game completely via google wave with Jonathan and Shreyas, and it's been going really well. We post the game text in chunks, link to places for other inspirations, and revise each other's wording. The three of us are really comfortable collaborating with each other, though, which helps-- I'm not sure how many people I'm cool with revising my words and whatnot. We've also been giving each other feedback on less collaborative projects: again, the main person posts the game text in chunks (very important to separate the sections!), and people post feedback and questions as replies. I like it a lot. I've tried collaborating in google docs before, and it seemed much less intuitive.

    \n It may come down to collaborative style, really. And my resistance may be a function of unfamiliarity.

    The editing and styling feature set in Wave is still pretty anemic but I'm certain that will change. Also - export options?

    The other thing that will make it stronger is some rights management, which Google docs has built in - Elizabeth doesn't want me to edit her game text, but she wants my comments. Right now that's not an option in Wave - it is all or nothing.

    Also! Version control, even the simple version control in Google docs, isn't possible. You could hatch new waves all the time I guess but that seems to defeat the purpose. It's a pretty cool tool and I'm sure it's strengths and weaknesses will become apparent over time. I'm certain that I'm not seeing even a morsel of its full potential.
