\n Seasons greetings, fellow gamers, whatever it is you may be celebrating as we near year's end. We are excited to invite you to participate in what is fast becoming an annual tradition for our kindhearted community, the Story Games Secret Santa.

If you've never participated in a Secret Santa before, the premise is very simple: everyone puts their name into a hat (or whatever) and then draws out someone else's name. The name you drew is the person you buy a gift for. Only in this case, because it's so close to Christmas already, this is going to be an exclusively digital Secret Santa - we're only exchanging gifts you can send via the Interwebs. PDFs, Steam games, ebooks, etc.

While we're framing this as a "Secret Santa," folks of all faiths and traditions should participate. It's a convenient excuse to be kind to one another.

Here's the rules:
- Sign up using this form no later than Tuesday December 17th
- You'll get your Secret Santa assignments on the following day
- Gifts should be no more than $15. (Not a hard and fast rule.)
- Avoid gift cards
- Send your person a gift by December 25th
- When you send your gift, you don't need to worry about anonymity any more - sign your name and build new friendships!
- Wearing fanciful hats encouraged, but not required or trackable

So easy you've practically already done it. Now go get your gifting on.\ufeff

Yours in holiday feel-good-ness,
- Joe Mcdaldno, Mark Diaz Truman, & Dustin L. Hodge

    \n Based on the three suggestions I had:

    I took one of the suggestions as a requested delivery method. My poor victim is now jumping through hoops to get his Secret Santa gift - but however obliquely, he did request this. The suggestion was 'a quest' so I've put the gift at the end of an online quiz / scavenger hunt (not a particularly difficult one though).

    The second suggestion I took to heart when selecting the gift. 'Something odd' suggests something out of the ordinary, something which runs contrary to your expectations, something a little eccentric and something that pushes boundaries a little. I think the gift meets this.

    The last requirement: Something silly. This sort of went out the window. It may have some silly aspects, but it definitely doesn't feel 'Silly'. However considering the gift I selected, and what I now know about the receiver, I hope he wont mind.


    On a separate note, this Secret Santa has made me realise just how big the digital footprint we leave actually is. Within minutes of googling my assignment I'd found pictures of them, audio recording, actual play videos, blogs, club memberships. I knew the names of some of their family, where they grew up, their sexual preference, food likes and dislikes, and a fair bit of their history ! I only researched them for about 30 mins, but the amount of information out there is truly scary.

    I then googled myself and discovered things I'd entirely forgotten about myself !
