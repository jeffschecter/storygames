\n I want to go to GenCon this year! It will mean skipping my 20th class reunion, but then again, I used to cut class to play RPGs anyhow, so this will be just like old times.

So who-all is going, and what's happening?

I only went once before, and I was at the Forge Booth, and the whole thing's kind of a blur. This time, I want to just go, play some games, see the sights, mingle, make new friends, blah blah blah.

Have you done this before? Any advice? Stuff I should make it a point to check out?

Are you going? What are you going to be doing? Panels? Demos? What's the what? Talk to me.

    \n Wander the halls, man. Hell, unless I'm frantically rushing to a 9am GMing duty, I *always* walk the whole convention on day one, poking about and seeing what's where. WizKidz had an entire ballroom--they weren't hard to spot at ALL--and their main table just had sign-up sheets with price. Boom.

    ---
    Yes, you have to sign-up for games; I think they even require tickets, too, IIRC (General tix get you into games that don't have full attendance).

    I just headed to open gaming (when I realized I could barley lift my backpack full of Clix) and rolled with whatever was happening--I like just about any game. I think it might be hard to pull that off, though, if you want to play mostly RPGs, not board and card games (unless you work to build up a play group while wandering, or similar).

    \n I'm still trying to figure out how to do Games on Demand for best results. Whenever I get into a game, it's great. But, sometimes my timing sucks -- folks are either all in mid-game (perfectly reasonable -- however much space GoD gets, and however many people, these are finite quantities and sometimes smaller than the demand) or getting dinner (beyond reasonable -- skipping meals is NOT recommended!), or somehow, I'm just off the beat and can't quite figure out how to get into a game.

    One of my personal rules from last GenCon -- YMMV -- Do NOT drink those five hour energy drinks. Next morning, I had the hangover without even the pleasure of drinking the previous evening (and I didn't even drink the enter bottle).

    \n My personal recommendation for Gencon is not to over-register for games beforehand! This will be my 10th Gencon, and I still sometimes make this mistake. I end up pre-registering for way too many games, and end up having to give half of them away to friends. Booking yourself solid with events is a great way to miss out on all sorts of cool things that happen spur of the moment!
