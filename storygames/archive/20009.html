\n I'm releasing a systemless sourcebook called "Encryptopedia." It addresses espionage roleplaying in traditional D&D-style milieus: think Jhereg by Steven Brust, or The Lies of Locke Lamora by Scott Lynch. There's a pre-order now and it will be released as an e-book February 14th, 2015 on Drive Thru RPG for $9.95. For more info my blog is Sam Chupp Media.

    \n@Paul_T and @DannyK thanks for the response. Yes I'm still "in" gaming, I've been writing here and there over the years, but this is my first self-published indie project. I am gearing up for the release of my own game eventually.

    I got into writing systemless through my Fiverr gig where I was creating scenarios for people. You give me $5 and I take what you give me from your game and create a general story for you. That was fun! So I figured this would be more broadly applicable. I'm having a lot of fun with it and writing the adventure for it was a blast as well.

    I did post to the Hype Machine on rpg.net And I'm doing some podcast appearances in support of the product.
