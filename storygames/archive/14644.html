Conviction house rule for my phantasmagorical AW hack

\n
\n
\n
\n\nRy \n\n\n
\n
\n\nJuly 2011 \n edited July 2011 in Game Design Help
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n

So imagine we're playing core and a half Apocalypse World:

\n

quoting Vincent Baker on his blog, anyway

\n
\n

The core of the game is barely visible. It's like the filament in the light bulb. It's these things:

\n
    \n
  • Vivid color
    • \n
  • A few stats and a simple die roll
    • \n
  • On a 10+, the best happens. On a 7-9, it's good but complicated. On a miss, it's never nothing, it's always something worse.
    • \n
  • The MC's agenda, principles, and what to always say.
    \n ...
    • \n
  • The basic moves
    • \n
  • How harm works
    • \n
  • How experience works
    • \n
  • The MC's moves
    • \n
\n
\n

We change the era to Hellenistic Persia, but break the cities up into autonomous communities ruled by Kings. We add a lot more phantasmagoria, especially at the cracks between the cities.

\n

We do a little Dogs-like sitgen in the background. The Gods are a dysfunctional Dogs-like town, the Kings are a dysfunctional Dogs-like town, the Sorcerers are a dysfunctional Dogs-like town, and the cities are dysfunctional Dogs-like towns. There's even little towns - you can probably guess what they're like.

\n

We replace Hx with Dungeon World-style Bonds, although the Bonds we write wouldn't be out of place if we were playing a low-key game of In A Wicked Age.

\n

The GM, he's great, but he doesn't like structuring Fronts. He's skilled enough to cross, weave, and add pressure the PCs. He's making the character's lives not boring while making this world seem real. If you looked at the game for any amount of time you'll see the ticking pressures.

\n

So far, so good. Pretty coherent game, right?

\n

Now we add this mechanic:

\n

Edit: See post 24 for the most mature version

\n

Player characters (not NPCs) have a pool called their Conviction. Conviction starts as 5 points. You can spend 2 points of Conviction to roll 3d6 instead of 2d6. You can spend 2 points of Conviction to re-roll. You can spend 3 points of Conviction to interrupt and take an extra move when normally someone else would be reacting first.

\n

Getting Conviction back:

\n
    \n
  • You get 2 back if you have a nice, deep, uninterrupted sleep where you're not freaked out at all.
    • \n
  • You can get 5 back if the characters sip tea together, spend time building something together, or relax and talk candidly around a campfire (i.e. refreshment scenes).
    • \n
  • You get 1 back when you advance.
    • \n
  • You can buy an advance that makes your total go up to 7, and an advanced advance that makes it go up to 10.
    • \n
\n

Basically, Apocalypse World doesn't care that a PC cares more about this roll than another. But in this world, that care/conviction is critical.

\n

Am I breaking the game? (I am ready for constructive criticism!)

    \n Ry,

    1. If you like the idea of Conviction spending being really huge, and you like the idea of potentially raising the numbers for hits as Charles suggests, but just don't want to mess thing up too much...

    Why don't you try running the numbers for rolling 2d6 (using your 0-5 dice), and then 3d6 (again, using those 0-5 dice)?

    You can still get a 10+ on 2 dice, but it'll be really rare unless you have a good stat. You might need to raise the stats a bit, as well, if you don't want the characters to be totally useless all the time.

    With 3 dice, though, your average is 7.5, just a bit above the usual 7.

    That gives you room to raise the stats or the range of stats AND you can go all the way to FOUR dice if you want to, once in a while.

    Heck, if that math doesn't please you, you can roll one regular d6 and one of your d6-1 dice for the basic roll (average roll 6, so raise all your stats by one if you want to preserve the current math for unConvicted rolls), and gives you room to add one or two d6-1 dice once in a while for effect.

    One extra d6-1 dice gives you an average of 8.5 [6 + 2.5], nicely better than the standard but not out of the park so as to be way crazy.

    Two extra dice gives an average of 11, which means you'll just blow through anything, but those dice going all the way to 0 means you'll still have failures often enough to be interesting.

    2. As far as the idea goes in general, though, I like it but I the "refresh" conditions don't grip me at all. In this messed up awesome world, the way you get your way is... by going to take a nap before the big fight? Sounds kinda lame, no?

    3. I like the "interrupt" idea, though, as John Harper says! That one's pretty solid.

    4. Finally, is this Conviction (a spendable resource) really the way you want to handle this? Maybe something more dependable would be more in-line? For example, simply giving characters more moves makes them pretty damn powerful (like advanced AW characters). Or you can have more specific stuff like Keys that give you bonuses, "When you pursue your goal of X/follow your belief of Y/strike a blow against your hated enemy, roll an extra die."

    It depends what you want the game to look like, of course!

    But a "Batman gets revenge against all odds" scenario can be frustrating if depleting your resources (Conviction points) at a certain point during the rampage of vengeance means you have to stop. That kind of story often has the hero just get more and more unstoppable the more he's hurt or tired. That's the opposite of what you're outlining: people can rest and drink tea until they save up enough points to go do the one thing they want to be sure they succeed at, kick ass, and then go rest again in preparation for their next goal.

    5. Another option comes to mind, which is rolling 2d8 instead of 2d6. You can make it dramatic, too: have everyone bring their own 2d6, but then put a brightly coloured/bigger/otherwise noticeable pair of 2d8 in the middle of the table to remind them they can spend some points to roll those any time.

    Okay, so here's where I'm at on the Conviction rules

    \n

    Conviction, v3:

    \n

    Most live day to day, pushed and pulled by forces beyond their control. But you are at the eye of the storm. You bend fate to your will.

    \n

    Conviction is a special resource for player characters. You start the game with 5 Conviction, and you get 1 Conviction every time you hit your second and fourth experience boxen.

    \n

    If you get an MC love letter, your Conviction will probably reset to 5; if there's no mention of Conviction, ask your MC.

    \n

    Using Conviction

    \n
      \n
    • Spend 2 points of Conviction to roll 3d6 instead of your normal 2d6 roll. Keep the better result.
      • \n
    • Spend 2 points of Conviction to re-roll 2d6. Keep the latter result. Note if your first roll was 3d6 from Conviction, you will only roll 2d6 on the second roll.
      • \n
    • Spend 3 points of Conviction to interrupt and make an extra move when normally someone else would be reacting first.
