    \n Okay. The process starts when your franchise hits zero dice. You can't really "lose" any more dice than you started with, but the minute you hit zero you need to stop and resolve the issue.

    First, you can no longer use your cards. All you have is the bank. You willingly go into debt (take out a loan) by placing up to 10 dice of "debt" into the bank. These are like negative franchise dice and you should track them (e.g. the franchise is at "- 6"). You continue a mission and try to earn new dice, with only your bank dice as a resource. (Be careful! One bad roll can blow all that borrowed money without reducing your debt!) At the end of the mission you have to "pay off" those negative dice first. If you end up with some leftover dice after paying off your loan, congrats. Your franchise is back in the black. If not, then you are still in debt. You can take out another loan, but eventually (group decision I think) your creditors close your doors.

    Go back and read page 58 again. While it may not be explicit, the general concept is pretty well spelled out in the second to fourth paragraphs.

    \n So true, Jared :-) The question might be how can you AVOID spending all your franchise dice!

    To read between the lines, it seems to me you have a situation where after every game your group is able to allocate some dice to the franchise and heal everyone up. If you can't imagine how all the dice could get used up, I'm guessing this is the case. My answer to this is that players aren't undergoing enough stress in the game. They need to be pushed to spend more dice.

    I GM this game fairly often and, as the GM, your job is to encourage players to engage with the system (especially confessionals), to help them "own" the fiction by throwing the "what happens" back in their laps, to track all the logistics (mission dice and stuff), and to manage the economy of the game by causing stress!

    Stress is really the only way the GM gets involved in the fiction. And its the primary means by which he or she can control the economy of the game (the pacing too, to some extent). The fiction is less interesting and achievements are hollow if there is no struggle. Sometimes you let players get high on the dice, especially when they are being awesome and you don't want to interfere. Other times you grind their resources down to make things interesting.

    \n Troy has the right of it. If you're not having this issue in your game, you're not dealing out enough Stress.

    Here's how it works. When the players get a lot of Stress their Stats go down. When their Stats go down they have depend on their Franchise dice to succeed mid-mission. Basically, with enough Stress the players aren't relying on the Franchise dice because they want a higher *chance* of success, they're depending on their Franchise dice so that they have *any* chance of success. And on top of that, they need to spend what Franchise dice they earn to recover their Stats and with enough Stress they won't have enough dice to BOTH recover their Stats in full AND re-invest in the company.

    To see that happen you really need to be about as cold and unforgiving with Stress as you can manage. My rule of thumb is this: If, as the GM, I have to introduce something new into the fiction, it causes Stress. That generally happens under two cases:

    1) There's a lull in the action and I have to "spike" things to keep things going.
    2) The player's fail a roll.

    Basically, if I'm talking, there's Stress.

    \n Neil,

    Three conditions need to be met.

    Condition 1) The agents need to be so stressed out they have to rely on the franchise dice to succeed.

    Condition 2) They need to be out of Franchise dice. Nothing on any of the cards.

    Condition 3) They need to face an obstacle for which they have neither stats nor Franchise dice to succeed at.

    At that point the Bankruptcy rules kick in. The Bank floods with debt dice that can not be transferred to any of the other cards (that's why they are "locked"). You can not transfer any dice you have earned from the current mission to any of the cards until (a) the mission is over and (b) all those debt dice that were put into the bank are repaid.
