    \n Indeed I did! (:3 I love you Noofy)

    For some other perspectives, if you haven't already been in the core Quiet Year Indiegogo thread we had some discussion of solo play since at least three or four of us all did our own solo games the day the rules got sent out and we all did some slightly different stuff. The big things that don't translate directly to solo play are Discussions and Contempt. There are several workarounds for Discussions in the thread (including the super-cool "tweet your discussion question" idea), but not so much for Contempt. I just play without it.
    ...oh, I should probably link to that thread for easy access. Solo discussion on 3rd (final) page.

    \n At one point the multiplayer game had rules for slowly-solidifying Factions. Then they got removed, because they pushed the game into a staid me-against-you kind of mentality. But I wonder if they'd serve a purpose in the single player game... (namely, giving the player a couple different lenses to shift between, and to consider Contempt from.)

    Solo discussions can happen like this: tweet something like "Should we retaliate against the wolf tribe bikers, even though they are stronger? #tqy" Consider the first four responses you get, or any responses you get within the first hour, whichever happens first.
