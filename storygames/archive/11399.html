\n Hello, all,

Two weeks ago I played my first Fiasco game ever. I loved it. We ALL loved the game. And then I studied the scenarios, the interaction, and a sick idea sprang into my mind: how about designing a Fiasco scenario set during a big gaming convention? You get to see lots of weird people there. Maybe you're one of them. Again, weird. And, even if these con are no Woodstock or Glastonbury, there is certainly something "interesting" going on...especially after the midnight hour.

So I wrote this hack -- in Italian, first. It's been well-received, alas I didn't get to play it yet.

In an unusual fit of hubris, I decided to translate it in English. it's here, hope you like it.

PS: The Klingon Battlesword mentioned there has been borrowed directly from the Rock Band scenario :-)

    \n I'll admit I didn't have time to peruse (let alone proof) but a couple of points:
    * It's "Airsoft," if you mean the plastic-pellet-shooting guns.
    * Most of the Relationships are not one-to-one (see all of "Your Party"). As I understand Fiasco setup, a Relationship is between two characters (whose players are sitting adjacent to each other), NOT the whole PC group and not an individual (e.g. most of your "RPG Shop" folks). But maybe you know that and it's just a translation/use-of-plural confusion?
    * The Needs, Objects, and Locations are supposed to expand upon the Relationship to which each is attached. So... how does my "very, very bad dog" expand upon my "old bikers approaching Gygax's age" Relationship with Bob? (OK, I could tease something out of that, but I hope you follow my point.)

    All that said... AWESOME! You're the first (non-invited) creator of a Fiasco Playset, from what I've seen!

    But... I think there's a lot more nuance and design involved in a playset than is shown in the current FiasCON one. Much like IAWA Oracles, there's an art to writing a playset; and so I'd have to say that FiasCON is a decent first draft, not a ready-to-run playset. Keep tweaking!
