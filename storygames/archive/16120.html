\n Last night was my first ever dungeon delve! When I was a kid I was forbidden from playing D&D, and by the time I got old enough to choose my own fate I was more interested in Rifts and Shadowrun. So I set out with adventurers Joss, Jared, and Topi, to explore the underworld and see if we could survive and emerge with glittering treasures beyond compare!

I've played a lot of games - Dogs in the Vineyard, Apocalypse World, Sorcerer, Mar's Colony, etc. etc. I don't know if there is a game that I have felt as creeped out and tense in as Lamentations of the Flame Princess. We crept through the rooms of abandoned cabins, pulled out jeweled daggers from old sacrificial basins, and lost Jared's character when examining a bone organ - the pipes were coated with a poisonous green spore. While investigating it, he unwisely chose to sniff it leading to his demise!

It is a very different kind of game than what I'm used to. I have felt extremely tense in games of Burning Wheel where there was a lot at stake for my character, but it was definitely a different feel to be exploring darkness and have abrupt character death be so explicitly on the table. It was really satisfying to finally get to have the kind of play experienced I daydreamed about when I was 11.
