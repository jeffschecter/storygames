\n So the first time I tackled the idea of the Story Game pamphlet I had not defined a concept of how exactly I was going to undertake the issue of teaching a group of people who had never played a Story Game / RPG before how to run and play in a game. The idea is that theoretically I could drop the pamphlet in the laps of a group of unsuspecting people and (without outside assistance) they would be able to grasp the concepts and move on to play.
\n An issue I am facing is the mechanics I was bringing together were pretty broad so the game would take more thought to get the concepts. I am thinking I should make each pamphlet more narrowly focused so that they are easier to grasp. To help bridge the gap from pamphlet to pamphlet I am pretty sure I will use the same underlying roll method just changing the way it translates the results.
\n Right now while I ponder this all over I would like to try and look through good examples of ridiculously simple to understand games, and presentations of games as inspiration. My question to you guys is what games have you read which had simple to understand rules, and or were presented in an incredibly simple to understand way?
