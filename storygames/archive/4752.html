    \n That's an interesting question. OK.

    Play, Build and Work Together all go together pretty well. Like you say, they're about how to play, a way of playing co-operatively and building on other people's ideas.

    Then Tell Stories and Status are a bit different. Tell Stories is lots of techniques for making stories make sense. And Status is about, well, status.

    Those two sections are slightly off on their own, but they link back into everything else, too.

    So, you asked about Status. Status is incredibly important in telling stories: if you start someone at low status (Cinderella), and gradually elevate them to high status, you've got a story.

    Similarly, it links into the co-operative thing. One of the most difficult things about playing co-operatively is accepting drops in status: if you say "I push your face into the mud!" then my instinct will often be to fight back "No you don't! I hit you!". To play co-operatively, though, it's good to accept the drop in status: "Yeah! I'm totally humiliated in front of the king!".

    So, you're right, Status is sort of different, but also links back into everything else. Does that make sense?
