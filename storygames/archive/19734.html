    \n Here are actual questions I've worked out when analyzing RPGs:

    In FATE Core, you have a +3 and I have a +0. What are the odds, each of us rolling 4dF, that you will beat me?

    I deal you five cards. What are the odds you don't have a pair?

    Do they have basic calculus? If so, try this:

    In Savage Worlds, when you roll a six sided die and get a six, you get to roll again and add that result to what you've got so far. If you roll another six, you keep rolling.

    What is the expected outcome of a d6 roll in Savage Worlds? What are the odds a d6 will generate a 9 or more? What are the odds a d6 will generate a 14 or more?

    \n Oh, here's an interesting one. Burning Wheel has tons of interlocking bits in its core mechanic. So...

    You get a success on a 4+ (the dice are d6s). You can spend a meta-resource called Fate to explode 6's. You can spend a meta-resource called Persona before the roll to add one die per point of Persona (max of 3 dice) to the roll. You can spend a meta-resource called Deeds to roll twice as many dice, or you can spend it after your roll to reroll all dice that weren't successes.

    (Fate are more common than Persona are more common than the very rare Deeds.)

    So how much expected value do you get from each type of artha? And how does it change with a grey-shade attribute (successes come on 3+) and a white-shade attribute (successes come on 2+)? For reference, skills tend to be in the 2-5 range, with 5 being the higher end. That's at character creation. They often advance a bit higher, and 8 tends to be pretty high.

    \n Oh, information is huge, yeah:

    In nWoD games, you roll a bunch of d10s. 8-10 is a success. (You can optionally use the rule that you get to re-roll 10s for additional successes if you want but it's not necessary for this exercise.) You also have a limited resource, Willpower, which you can use to add +3 to the roll.

    You have 6 dice and must get 3 successes.

    First, what's your chances with no Willpower?

    Second, you have 1 Willpower; what's your chances if you can only spend Willpower before you roll?

    Third, you have 1 Willpower; what are your chances of success if you can spend Willpower after the roll, AND, what is the chance that, if you succeed, you still have that 1 Willpower?

    \n I'm late, but will answer anyway.\n
    The sums of 2d6 can be expressed by CONVOLVING, visually, and it's impact is IMMENSE!
    Do a follow up example CONVOLVING the 2d6 signal with a 1d6 signal to get a 3d6 bell curve, and you will BLOW MINDS!

    Future engineers (especially computer and electrical) will NEED this understanding.
    \nCombine this with probability generating functions (of the polynomial kind, see for example http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Probability-generating_function ) to get a very easy answer to any question where you sum dice or calculate successes (and the process is linear, so you don't match pairs or have any other interaction between the dice). For example, exploding dice in Burning wheel or new Wod.

    Mathematically what happens is that the sum of random variables is the convolution of probability distributions is the multiplication of corresponding generating functions, which in this case is simply the multiplication of polynomials (which should be familiar) or absolutely converging series, which would require justification or hand-waving. You can calculate moments by differentiating the polynomial or series, which in the case of series requires justification or hand-waving (you can differentiate term by term in reasonable situations).

    Suppose you roll 4 gray-shaded exploding dice in BW. What are the expectation, variance, and other moments if you want to know them? What is the probability of rolling exactly 5 successes? What about rolling 5 or more successes?
