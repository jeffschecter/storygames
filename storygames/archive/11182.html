\n A few years ago now I had a copy of Deep 7's game, Santa's Soldiers. I've long since lost the game in a hard-drive crash, but I still remember really wanting to run this for a group as a seasonal thing. Just something Christmas-y, silly and fun. I'd be curious to know if anyone else ever did and how it went over.

It occurs to me, though, that holiday-themed games needn't be brightly colored affairs full of candy canes and Santa Claus and islands of misfit toys. As in the film Joyuex No\xebl, Christmas stories can be told against a variety of backdrops, including war.

I haven't ever done any gaming that evoked, deliberately or otherwise, holiday feeling. I'm not sure I've even set a game at Christmastime. I guess after Die Hard it seemed best to give the day a wide berth. Or something.

What have you done in terms of holiday-themed gaming? If you haven't done any, then why not? Would you if the opportunity arose?

And remember: Santa is watching you. Right now. And in the bathroom.
