    \n Assuming some of your 15 years of experience included D&D, I'd recommend Dungeon World. It models old school dungeon crawls with newer, story-games style mechanics. Unless you're going to PAX next week, you won't be able to find a print copy, but you should be able to get the PDF next month I think.

    I would also repeat the suggestions in Andy's list, particularly the Shadow of Yesterday. You might also look at Lady Blackbird which is a free, self-contained game that builds on techniques from several other games. It assumes some previous knowledge and doesn't really explain those techniques fully, so if you read it and have questions, come back and ask.

    \nActually, I'll suggest that you try to seek out 2 or 3 games that are as different from each other as possible, because story games tend to have a feel and rhythm that varies from one game to another much more than traditional RPGs. As such, some will float your boat and others won't. Don't base your judgement of the whole subset on a single game, no matter how much praise that game is getting in the community (Primetime Adventure, for instance, didn't float my boat.)

    \n This suggestion is maybe just a touch off the usual advice, so take it with a grain of salt. I'm assuming you have some group that you play "traditional" games with. At some point (maybe when not everyone can make it to the regular game or something), experiment with a high-action kung-fu game called Wushu.

    Now, first thing: there are much better games out there than Wushu; however, it has some advantages as "point of first contact" with "non-traditional" games for "traditional" gamers:\n
    • It is really short. You can read and understand the whole thing in about 10 minutes.
    • It will instantly make sense to "traditional" gamers.
    • It exposes players to the idea that being effective as a character depends entirely on how well they can narrate (without seeming too "hippie" in how it does this).
    • It plays quick and (usually) a bit silly (so mixes well with alcohol, if that is your thing).
    • It is free.
    • It seems a bit easier to "sell" traditional gamers on playing it, for some reason.
    • It is disposable, meaning that you don't invest much into it, and can easily move on to something else.
    • You know kung-fu.
    \nThere are some other games that also have similar traits with different mechanics, like Monkey, Ninja, Pirate, Robot: the Roleplaying Game.
