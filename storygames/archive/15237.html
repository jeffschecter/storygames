What's a good light-hearted 2 player game?

\n
\n
\n
\n\nDanielZKlein \n\n\n

\n
\n
\n
\n
\n So I've got someone I want to introduce to the idea of story games. One on one. And she's decided she'd like to play something light hearted. Ninjas and zombies were her suggestion. Now I know plenty of ninja and zombie games, or games that can work brilliantly with a ninja and zombie setting, but I'm not sure I know any games that work well for 2 players and don't come with a setting baked in.

I've played S/lay w/ me a bunch of times, and loved it to pieces. It's not exactly lighthearted, though, and if you put ninjas and zombies in, they'd end up making demonic ninja/zombie offspring.

I've bought and read Breaking the Ice, and while it seems light-hearted enough, I'm not sure I'm sold on the concept. Also it's a little, I don't know, relationship-y. Not sure how well it'd go with ninjas and zombies either.

Fiasco sounds BRILLIANT for ninjas and zombies--both adjusting an existing playset or writing a new one would be a blast, but I don't think Fiasco works with fewer than three players, right?

Any recommendations at all?

    \n Yeah, Breaking the Ice was my first thought as well. Can a ninja and a zombie find love? Or look at Shooting the Moon, which works fine with two people who are pursuing an NPC Beloved. Less relationship-y that way, more pirate-y.

    Fiasco 100% does not work with two. Find a third and you are good to go, and it is over-the-top enough without ninjas or zombies added to the mix.

    Also, why two people? So many games work better with a group. Unless there is some player-level romance going on, in which case: Breaking the Ice.
