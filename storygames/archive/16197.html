    \n Hey there folks. This is Kevin, creator of the Aeranos App. I'd like to start out by saying thanks for taking the time to talk about the game.
    \n
    \nSo I'll get right into answering some of your questions, than I'll ask some of my own.
    \n
    \nFirst: I'd like to point out that the interface in the video is an early screen flow mockup that uses a pdf. The actual app will flow much more quickly, without the pauses and "page tun accept" buttons. We're still in the formative phases of the app. The comments did make me realize that perhaps I need to make that a lot clearer than it is in the video.
    \n
    \nThe idea is that combat will be a simple choice tree based on your combat skills and stats. First you choose an opponent from the GM's list of possible enemies. So if you have a longsword and a bow, and you choose weapon attack, it will show two choices: Longsword or Bow. You choose longsword and the app moves along to tell you which attacks you can make with a longsword: Basic Attack, Called Shot, Knockdown, etc. You than choose Called shot and it asks for location. You choose head and it applies the mods for a head shot. Last it gives the GM a chance to apply situational mods before comparing your attack mods to the defense mods of the enemy. You than make your roll and the app tells you if you hit or not, and if so, the wound results. That's it in a nutshell. Same basic system for non-combat skill checks only even simpler with the GM applying mods or making an opposed roll.
    \n
    \nSo, all that said, let me talk about two thoughts I've been having. First: This whole game used to be based on OGL 3.5, and I still have that version hanging around. I've passed my system around locally and it's held up well, but I definitely get the whole "bigger audience" argument of Pathfinder. Coding for one wouldn't be much different than coding for another and your comments have given me pause... and I still have 38 days left in my project. Aaand at this point I realize it's really just one thought, not two. Should I make a fundamental system change to OGL Pathfinder and run with the Aeranos setting as my gut is starting to suggest?
    \n
    \nLet me know your thoughts. The lateness of the hour means I will be responding tomorrow, but I'll definitely be checking back then. Thanks ahead of time for everyone's input. And thanks again kksimons for turning me onto this forum.
    \n
    \np.s. No matter the system, the app is just a number cruncher. My whole point is to speed up tabletop RPGing, not interfere in the creative aspect of collaborative story telling that's at the heart of old school role playing. The setting is detailed and the groups I've shared it with have had a lot of fun in it, but in the end it's just window dressing to the app. A playground in which to use the rules. And since you will be able to input your own custom data, there won't be anything limiting a group to a setting in the app.
    \n
    \nOkay, really going to bed down. Thanks again.
