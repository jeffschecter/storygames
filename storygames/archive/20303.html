\n Hi folks
for my current game development Spy Tears (Directions Storyplaying System), I'm looking at two settings and like your advise which you would be more inclined to play:

SETTING A: OLD SCHOOL EUROPE SPIES
Cold war espionage in the 80ies, Western European capitols, backroom diplomacy, moles & traitors, double/triple agents, trenchcoats & cigarettes, films like Tinker Tanker Soldier Spy (John Le Carre)

SETTING B: MODERN GLOBAL TROPICANA
Flashy modern spies & freelancers, global locations, yachts & private beaches, idealism vs opportunism, rogue nations & terror organizations, high tech gear & car chases, tuxedos & bikinis, films/series like Mission Impossible, Alias, Chuck

Thanks for your feedback!

    \n I might take a look at Cold City. I was also thinking of Spione and Night's Black Agents for spy-specific setting books.
    By the way, on german G+, the Modern Global Tropicana was slightly favored 60:40 in a voting. Though on story-games, I appreciate more the qualitative discussion, of course.

    Practically, to me the biggest difference between the two settings is the lack/availability of mobile technology & internet. Research & reconnaissance in old school spy stories has a different touch without the technology, in my opinion.
