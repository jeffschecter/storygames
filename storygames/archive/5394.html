\n This idea originally came from Ben Lehman, and I didn't take him up on it, and now I'm thinking about it again.

So I make and sell herbals--stuff for your hair, skin, etc., at our local farmer's market from May to October. I also make medicinal tinctures for friends and family which I don't sell. And best of all I make kombucha year round. I give away the SCOBYs and charge a small fee (to cover shipping and the materials to ship them), and I occasionally give workshops on how to make kombucha.

Ben asked if I would like to advertise my hair oil in one of his books. I never got the ad together, and I didn't make enough hair oil to sustain any extra orders that might have come from the advertising.

I'm thinking about advertising the SCOBY's in one of my games, either Steal Away Jordan, or the one I'm working to have in ashcan form by GenCon.

My question is, has anyone ever seen ads in game books that were not related to games? Would it look strange or suspicious? Specifically speaking of what I'd like to advertise, I should mention that SCOBY's are really creepy looking and while they have little to do with role playing, they make for good inspiration. In every batch of kombucha tea, the SCOBY spawns another one. (The first SCOBY is called a "Mother", and the new one is called a "baby") They look and feel like fleshy pancakes. Kombucha is an excellent healthy beverage to drink while playing. So I could give the ad an RPG/horror/fantasy feel. Still, would it be out of place? I don't really care about making money off the SCOBY's, I charge for shipping and the cost of the plastic bags I send them in. Really I just like to share them, and I think other gamers might be interested in making kombucha.

After reading this, it sounds like I'm joking, but I'm not. Should my hobbies never cross paths commercially?
