\nClaustrophobia reminds me of Fiasco.
\n
\nYou roll pools of d6s whose results constrain your choices as you select options from lists. It's very cool but unlike Fiasco, a primarily tactical game. But I suspect there are mechanics that can be drawn from to be used in a Fiasco like game.
\n
\nYou can download the rules here.

    \n My only complaint was that the game is a brutal death spiral. As you take damage, you eliminate options you can roll on your lists. Take too much damage and you end up skipping turns (although turns are super fast and you control multiple characters). But the game is short (less than an hour, possibly 40 minutes) and all of this is within the theme of the game. So it is a minor complaint. I really enjoyed it and both the minis and dungeon tiles are gorgeous.
