    \n Mechanically speaking, the game went well. The first mission had the agents all temporarily promoted to deep blue clearance, and thanks to me giving out some black and white commendations (in my game, commendations are differently colored and represented by poker chips, ones besides the blue (normal) ones count as five and are earned by "helping control/mythography" (white) or [CLASSIFIED] for black. Since those counted as five, by the second mission everyone was permanently promoted to deep blue. This meant lots of personal static, which was good for ratcheting up the tension. Also, people had lots of techniques now, which made for more roleplaying as disguised or cover identity using people would go for stealth approaches to things. Heart rates went up fast, as did static, with static of twenty one being reached in both missions. In the first mission, one player had to roll emergency ejection because his heart rate went up after an interrogation by several spidermen (not mentioned in control's report, above, because they didn't know about it).

    Anyways, the group was so excited that half of them want to buy the game, so that's cool. Also, we're playing again and once again I made up like five or six pages of enigmatic fliers and handouts to give to the players (which I did this time as well). It's fun to combine weird fonts, hidden messages, bizarre cryptic language, and creepy song lyrics and then hand them to the players and see what they interpret as the meaning...
