    \n Nick,
    Keep it Sunny just tells you to figure out how to return things back to normal. There's not much mechanical weight given to it, other then mentioning it.

    What I'm thinking about is likely a game that just does this thing; presents a problem/windfall to one or many characters, character(s) wrestle with the direct and indirect effects of the problem/windfall, problem/windfall goes away.

    The open areas(room for game/flexibility)would be what problem/windfall gets introduced, what characters it effects, how it effects them, and what makes it go away.

    Something to that effect.

    A game like this might help by being themed(like Keep it Sunny).

    \n So, like in Gillian's Island(which is where I really took notice to it)the reconning it's laser-focused. It's so cliche that it'd be prefect for a Gillian's Island game. Something always happens to negate any hope of getting off the island. I wish I were smart enough to list all the reasons that crop on on why they ultimately can't leave the island, how information on their whereabouts doesn't get to proper channels.

    It's brilliantly stupid.
