\n So after asking on RPGNet and Google Plus, I turn to see if there's anyone on S-G familiar with this dark German post-apocalypse game.

All I know about it is that it's sufficiently German (dark and brutal), and that apparently at the beginning of the game you roll a 1d6: This is how many years the Earth has left.

Anyway, this was being showed off for a few years as "coming soon" from the Shadowrun booth. It looks like it's finally translated into English and released... to zero fanfare. I haven't seen word one on it, except for the fact that I poked over on IPR and there it was on the front page a few weeks ago.

Anyone played it, or know anything about it?

    \n I've got a copy of the quickstart PDF. I can throw a copy of the copy over to you if you'd like a flip through.

    The core mechanic is simple enough. Character commits to doing one action. Adds together one Attribute rating and one Skill rating based on the action being taken. These are both on a 1 - 10 scale. GM assigns a difficulty based on the action being undertaken, which seems to be on a 0 - 5 scale. Player rolls 2d10, and succeeds on any roll that is less than the Attribute+Skill but greater than the Difficulty.

    I like the simplicity of the core mechanic. Seems elegant. The character attributes and skill ratings listed in the text seem to be pretty low, though. A character that is very, very competent at something gets a total of 12 or 13 to roll under. Most other totals seem to be in the 6 to 9 range. If "whiff factor" is a turn-off for you, then this might not be the system you're looking for.

    As for setting, man I want to know more. There's a lot of brief, cool bits of detail here that I'd like to dig deeper into myself.
