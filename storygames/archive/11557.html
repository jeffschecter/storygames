\n Pew pew pew!

Boom!

Whoom!

I am a spaceship!


Yesterday, I had a half an hour planetside battle against a military vessel superior in almost every way. I had to escape to the gravity well of that planet to mess up the mass drivers used against me -- but that didn't stop it from damaging my engines, after which I had to deploy gliders and fly down into the ocean as graceful as can be. I tried to hide under a rock, deploy a decoy, cut all power and pray, but the damned thing saw where I crashed (well, splashed!) and it wouldn't have taken long for its hunting drones to find me. So I put power to the rear shields, overcharged my rear torpedo launchers and then let them loose. Like a giant bullet surrounded by an aura of superheated steam, I shot towards my hated foe!

And fucking missed. Gravitational manipulation was deployed and my craft was redirected towards a nearby mountain, where it eventually crashed, with me barely managing to make my way to the escape pod and thus survive. During my flyby, however, I managed to total the enemy's electronics systems through a hastily established subspace link, which left them with no other option but to deploy "mechanical deceleration devices" (so basically parachutes) and land in the raging, storming sea...

Not even close to a win, but you take what you can get!


Me and my friend did all this without any stats, dice, or even any concrete setting. The only rule we ever employed is the topic of this thread: Onomatopoeia or it didn't happen!

So when lasers splashed uselessly against my shields and missiles went boom due to point defense, you could hear "VHHMM-blootblootbloot-VHMMM" "pew-BOOM! pew-BOOM! pew-BOOM!". When I had to enter atmosphere engineless and deploy gliders to fly, you would hear "Nvvv---Bfwoom!-trttrtrrrrtr-whizzz-donk!-fllllll" When my heavily armed foe deployed hunting drones, you could hear "dink-dink-dink-splash!-splash!-splash!-grllllrlrlll".

It was way more fun that it should have been. Ridiculously fun, even!

So, in the interests of science, I ask you to perform a little experiment: say what you're doing, and then make some appropriate noises.

And then, tell me how that felt!
