\n The big thing that stuck out to me about Donjon was that each success is a fact that you get to state, I really like that idea. I don't want to necessarily take it to the explicit extreme that Donjon does though, so I'm curious if there are other dice pool systems that treat successes as facts or distinct actions during resolution rather than just as a numerical value for determining success or failure.

    \n John Wick's Houses of the Blooded does something similar (and from what I understand he's co-opted the system for a few of his newer games, too). You have a pool of dice, before rolling, you can set a number of them aside as "wagers". If you succeed on the roll, you get to narrate a bit of info for your success plus each of your "wagers" (if you fail, the GM narrates).

    Wises in Burning* games allow you to state a fact if you succeed the roll. You know that stuff, you're wise about it, so you get to say it.

    FATE allows you to make declarations by paying a Fate point. The specifics vary from version to version.

    There's also stuff like Universalis which works purely on paying for the privilege to narrate.

    \n In the current draft of Do: Pilgrims of the Flying Temple, on your turn, you can say what your character does and it is assumed to be fact. There is no question of success or failure. What you say happens, happens. The question is whether character gets in trouble as a result of that action.

    \n My game "On Mighty Thews" does this. It doesn't use a dice pool, but rather "degree of success" to determine how many fact you get. The facts are also pretty tightly constrained to the stakes of the initial conflict. It's a pulp fantasy game that's good for Moorcock, Howard, Lieber, Tanith Lee, and so on.

    It's on its way to being published right now, but I can send you the playtest draft if you're interested.
