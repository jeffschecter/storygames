Codex-ifying Short Free Story Games?

J_Walton
November 2006 edited November 2006 in Story Games
So I had this thought:

I've got a bunch of short free story games (mostly from Game Chef competitions and the like). I should post them on the Codex. The entire game text. Boom. There it is. I mean, I'm talking about the finished and relatively polished games, not stuff that still needs work. Specifically I'm thinking of:

- Seadog Tuxedo (pirate penguins!)
- Heavenly Kingdoms (drunken Taiping exegesis!)
- Kazekami Kyoko Kills Kublai Khan (lesbianstripperninja!)

And then I thought:

I should invite other game designers to do likewise. I mean, it'd be really cool to have Otherkind or some of Zak Arntson's free games (frex. Shadows) or other Game Chef games (all the great stuff from the Fantasy edition that never got published, like Jack Aidley's Chanter) or lost treasures like The World, The Flesh, and the Devil posted for posterity (assuming their authors were interested). That would be a lot of cool stuff all together and it would be easy to link people to neat games.

But I'm not sure if that's really what The Codex is for. What d'ya think folks? As long as we decided that posted games should be finished and ready for play, would there be a problem with this? If folks would rather me take this elsewhere (like 1km1kt), I understand.
