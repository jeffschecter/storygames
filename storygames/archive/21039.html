    \n The Warren is published by Jason's company, but was designed by Marshall Miller. Not sure if that fits Jeff's request or not.

    I'll second Grey Ranks. Things I particularly like about it:
    - Scripted framing of sequential stages in the war.
    - Juxtaposition of teenagers soldiering with them being, just, y'know, teenagers.
    - Navigating the axes of your character's state based on developments of each scene.
    - Evolution of character traits during play.

    I think you could get a good taste of all of those except the last one during a one-shot, but longer play would be better.

    \n I have had a ton of fun playing The Shab-al-Hiri Roach. It's similar to Fiasco in that it's a one-shot, GM-less game that can be played in a few hours. There are a couple of mods available as well. It tends toward comedy when my group has played it, which I think it's supposed to. I know you can play Fiasco with a more serious tone, but when I've played it we always get pretty silly and make each other laugh a lot. The Roach does that job very well.

    \n (Thanks for all the love; I wish I wrote The Warren! Also I co-wrote Love in the Time of Sei\xf0 with Matthijs Holter and it is based on his game Archipelago - we specifically wrote it to show off many of Archipelago's mechanics in a one-shot format. Personally I think the related quasi-sequel, Love in the Time of Khvereneh, is even better)

    Grey Ranks is specifically designed for three play sessions, but includes suggestions for making it into a one-shot.
