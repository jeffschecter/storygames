    \n The latest change, adding 'questions' to player characters...

    Player Characters

    \u2018Upgrading\u2019 a character to a player character costs 10 points. Player characters (PCs) are differentiated from non-player characters (NPCs) by the fact that only their owner may control them during roleplaying, unless the owner explicitly gives permission to another player.

    In addition, PCs must have one or more central conflicts, for example:

    A secret that they\u2019re trying to keep.
    A desire to prove themselves to someone.
    A moral code that conflicts with their orders.


    Questions

    Other players may add questions about a PC, which the character\u2019s player may then choose to explore through roleplaying.

    Some examples of questions:

    What happens when someone discovers Alex\u2019s secret?
    Who or what is Richard afraid of?
    Why does Alex hate Emily?


    These can be \u2018leading questions\u2019 -- for example, maybe there\u2019s nothing about Alex hating Emily in the character\u2019s entry, but Emily belongs to the player asking the question, and they\u2019re trying to set up a conflict. If the player who owns the character doesn\u2019t like the question, they can edit it back out. However, if the player decides to use a question in one of their roleplaying threads, the player who asked the question gets 5 points.
