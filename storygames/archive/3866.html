\n Okay, here is the story about how I learned to play Shock.
\n
\nMy buddy Joseph went to a convention here in Vancouver and met a bunch of story-gamers, including Joe McDonald. Later, he went and played Shock with them, one afternoon. I'm pretty sure he had never even heard of the game before. He had a good time, so after the game was over, he dropped by my place, and explained the rules. And when I say he "explained the rules" I mean he literally told me exactly how to play the game. Then we made some characters and played the game! Sure, we only did one scene, just so I would understand, but we still played it. Two guys who didn't read the rules.
\n
\nSo later the both of us went and played it with Joe (and Chad). I got to look at the book, but I didn't read any of it. It was the same game. There might have been a clarification of a point or two, but mostly it was exactly what Joseph explained to me.
\n
\nOf course, I kept hearing (reading, I guess) about how the book is unplayable on it's own. You need the errata, or whatever. Even Joe McDonald agreed with the criticism, to some degree. So I was a wee bit hesitant to get the book. Especially since I already knew how to play!
\n
\nIn the end, I said screw it, and bought the damn book anyway. And, well, the game was all there. It didn't confuse me at all, really. I think there is a discrepancy about whether your Antagonist is to the right or left, but nothing seemed broken about it to me. A few typos or whatever, sure, but hey, it's an rpg...
\n
\nI learned we did a few things "wrong" - like everybody who wanted to add minutiae got to apply their d4 roll to the Tagonists, and I don't think we were picking up new Features for failing a conflict (but maybe I'm wrong about that one). In any case, nothing that changed the game. I can even see tons of house rules being applied by different play groups. Like using your own protagonist instead of making a seperate antagonist for somebody else.
\n
\nSo I dunno, guys. I don't think this game is complicated enough to be confusing!
