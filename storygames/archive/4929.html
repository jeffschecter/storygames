\n There are a couple of people I know at work who might like playing story games. I'd like to run a quick game/extended demo for them sometime, to perhaps entice them to more gaming later. I've got two or three potential players, and the session will have to fit into an hour's lunch break. Does anyone have anything to hand that would be suitable?

Ideally, I'd like something akin to Don't Rest Your Head. It would fit nicely with the book/film/video game interests of the players, but I don't have much inspiration on how to make a compelling one-hour session for it, from a cold start. If anyone has any bright ideas, please shout!

An alternative would be DitV with a slightly expanded version of the running example in the DitV book (your nephew's stealing from his dad to visit the whore in town). Have the vengeful dad, the nephew who wants to marry his lover, the whore who wants independence and freedom from her pimp husband, and the pimp who's turned his back on the Faith. If I lay the situation on the table at the start, could this be resolved in an hour with novice players?

Neil.

    \n Do you know these people well? If it were me and co-workers I'd stick to something light and fun instead of heavy and fun. Also: An hour is not much time at all. Like, one scene. Plus you'll all be eating. Rather than play at all, have you considered bringing in a stack and letting them look them over, talking about different games and seeing what excites them? Just an idea. Then:

    "Cool, you guys all seem to like Monkey Kings of Tribeca, why don't we play a session next Wednesday night and see how it goes?"

    \n Well, Neil... just use Don't Rest Your Head and improvise

    I mean, the players frame their first scene on the character sheet. Have them play out that scene independently, then when the climax of the scene has been reaced, fade to black, cut, and present a scene where they both are in an interesting place of the Mad City (probably the Bizarre Bazaar), and have something happen. They'll probably join and act in response.

    For example, the Tyger from the 15min gencon demo kit Fred has put out... the only link I can find at the moment is this
    http://www.deadlyfredly.com/gencon07/dryh-demo-kit.pdf

    Good advice for a demo session there, also.

    Keep in mind, running at work, you're going to be limited by all of your company's harassment policy stuff. So be very careful about your subject matter.
