    \n You know, on the occasions when we've had someone new join our group, we usually just start a new game rather than try to fold them into an ongoing one.

    The nice thing to say for this method is that it's easier on everyone -- you don't need to catch up the new player on the history of the game, or wonder how the new player fits into the ongoing game dynamic, or have anyone re-work their characters to accommodate the new one. It lets everyone in the group direct their characters towards each other, avoid spotlight theft, and gives us all a clean slate to work on.

    The accurate thing to say for this method is that starting a new game for the new player usually means we're about to do a one-shot, or maybe a game that's only 2-3 sessions long at most...just in case this turns out to be a bad fit for someone. A short game lets everyone suss out whether the new player is a keeper (and, I assume, for the new player to decide if we're worth hanging out with), knowing that if the answer is "HELL NO," everyone will walk away clean from the whole thing within the month. Why waste a great long-arc game idea on a new player who might not work out for some reason? Why endanger a great ongoing game? Easier just to give the new player a trial run on something that no one's invested too heavily in.


    If we did decide for whatever reason to fold a new player into an ongoing game, I expect that we'd have one or maybe even two sessions devoted to character creation/revision and discussion, to get everyone on the same page about the new character and avoid pitfalls like the new character being the odd man out for everything (because no one has any in-character history with them, or whatever). The better we can integrate the new PC with all the pre-existing PCs, the more reason we'll have to interact with the new player and the better the game will be for everyone.
