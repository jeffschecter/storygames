\n I'm reading through John Harper's "The Regiment" hack to see how good it is, and if I should invest in a PDF copy of AW. I've seen a term called "hold" related to successes in both The Regiment, and also on other AW forums. For example: Your PC makes his/her 10+ nice success roll and "hold 3", whereas a 7-9 means a "hold 1".

Might I ask, what does that term refer to?

And are folks (who are into WW2) find the The Regiment is working? Some of the AARs are exciting stuff!

    \n Usually the move also has a line like "Spend hold, 1 for 1, to..."

    Hold is kind of a generic token, for when succeeding at a move allows you to do something repeatedly, or choose between a listed options several times.

    \n Basically, the reason it's called Hold is that you can hold onto the points and spend them when you want: "Hold 3, and spend one hold to pick any option off the list" means that you can let the situation develop until one of those options would be useful, whereas "Choose 3" means you have to choose then and there.

    \n Note that the currency of "hold" is specific to a particular move. So if you have hold from one move you can't spend it on another move, normally. It's possible to have multiple different types of hold at once, but AW and most hacks try to avoid that, as much as possible.

    \n Do you mean, positioning as in Mouse Guard Disposition? It's a semi-abstract measure of advantage (over somebody or in a situation). "Semi" abstract because it measures how many times you can do specific things from a specific list in the future, as opposed to a wholly abstract measure of advantage.
    However aren't we taking a very practical, I-don't-get-this-bit-of-text question (which has already been answered, granted) and turning it on its head and into metaphysics?
