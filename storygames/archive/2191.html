my favorite 2006 game stuff & accomplishments

January 2007 edited January 2007 in Story Games
Well, it's a week too late but I made a list of my personal top ten favorite rpg/story game events & accomplishments from last year. This is based on games I tried last year and is not intended to be anything so grand as a best-of-the-indie-scene list.

* favorite character: Jeff Tidwell's 'truck,' a kid in a session of Jim Pinto's game George's Children
* favorite game design 'school': the Joshua & Judson free-flowing, non-turn-taking, pattern-recognition school of design where players earn rewards for speaking up and pointing out certain connections between characters and other game elements, best exemplified by FLFS, Repetoire, and Sons of LIberty. Jim pointed out this tendency during a recent session. By the way, not all of Judson and Joshua's game are structured this way.
* favorite published game I got to help playtest: Full Light Full Steam
* favorite sign that I'm not a hopeless procrastinator: 'finishing' 6 games for contests in 06 (two for game chef, 1 for reverse engineer, and 3 for the 2 page challenge).
* favorite design progress: finished play tests of all 9 games in the heads of state series.
* Favorite game setting/situation: a Charles Fourier inspired utopian socialist commune/phalanxe In Space, in a FLFS game.
* favorite 'design for my own personal preferences, the public be damned' accomplishment: writing a game based on urban psychogeography, phrenology, and Walter Benjamin
* Favorite cool system stuff: tie between word swapping in Agora, escalation in dogs in the vineyard, the phases in repetoire & george's children, Polaris' 'yes, but...' system, and the bill of rights/situation creation/ and mad libs set up of sons of liberty
* Favorite game site: story games, I may not post all that much, but compared to my posting rate on rpg.net and the forge, I'm blazing away
* favorite over all accomplishment: helping set up a regular playtest group a year ago

  • Mcdaldno
    January 2007
    I'm going to assume you want others to chime in with theirs, because... well... I want to chime in with mine.

    Favourite Character: Jordan, the self-obsessed just-out-of-the-closet senior in our Sorcerer game set in a suburban high school. His father forced him to join the track team, and he summoned a demon to help him win races and stay on the team (Game Face is the demon).

    Favourite Game Design School: Definitely the Vancouver Game Design School of Kirk Mitchell and myself. Its members are: 18, living in Vancouver, designing really face-stabby games about emotional violence, designing games with single-word titles (Perfect [mine], Kin [Kirk], Boulevard [mine], Wash [collaboration])

    Favourite Published Game I Got To Help Playtest: Hm... Excluding my own game, Perfect, I haven't been fortunate enough to playtest anything that fits this category yet. Ah... well... I'm new at this.

    Favourite Sign that I'm Not A Hopeless Procrastinator: I took a game from initial idea to published product in several months, rising to my own (in retrospect, absurd) deadlines and production expectations.

    Favourite Design Progress: Figuring out that Boulevard was about "characters without agency". When I came to that realization, everything started to really come together and lock down in my mind. Yay!

    Favourite Game Setting/Situation: This is a hard one. I think my Shock game which involved "mind whoring" (allowing someone to plug their consciousness into your body/mind, and control you) and replication. It was like this sick, sadistic mess that lasted about 2 hours and had me and the other player like choking on how much awesome there was.

    Favourite "design for my own preferences, the public be damned" accomplishment: Definitely Red-Rimmed Eyes, the game about a team of adventures whose insecurities and humanity tear them apart when they realize who the "real" monsters are. It was beautiful.

    Favourite "cool system stuff": Keys. It rocks my socks, and it teaches me about playing in a new, better-than-ever way.

    Favourite game site: The Forge. It connects me to the people I need to learn from, it helps me lift my ideas off the ground, and it allows me to give back to the community.

    Favourite overall accomplishment: Going to Gencon with Perfect in hand.
