\n Hi, everybody!

OK, so I\xe2\u20ac\u2122m experimenting with a resolution mechanic for Business Solutions. I'd love some feedback - this really, really needs to be fast, easy to grasp, and fun.

It\xe2\u20ac\u2122s a three-player game, and most conflicts will be two on one, two technicians against a client. You have coins, which are both an in-game resource (you can earn them, lose them, etc, and the character with the most wins at the end of the game - conservation is important).

In a conflict, the client faces off against the techs, and each secretly bids a number of coins. The client chooses either heads or tails for their topmost coin, which signals the side that matters - the opposite side is discarded from the tech's coins. The technicians can bid both heads and tails in any combination, playing it either safe (a mixed, but weak, set) or reckless (all one side). All this is done without consultation, behind a cupped palm.

The technician players observe the client players chosen face (heads or tails). They discard their non-face coins and pool the rest. If they have a cumulative total higher than the client player, they win. The player putting in the most total coins narrates the outcome.

Example:

It\xe2\u20ac\u2122s a copier repair challenge, and the two technicians are working against the client.

The client secretly bids five coins and turns them all to tails.

The first tech secretly bids six coins, three heads and three tails.

The second tech secretly bids three coins, all tails.

There\xe2\u20ac\u2122s a reveal, and the first tech tosses out his three heads coins. What remains are five tails for the client and six tails, total, for the techs. The techs win the conflict and repair the photocopier. The first tech, who bid the most total coins (heads and tails together), narrates the outcome.

So, tell me what I'm missing - is there an obvious strategy in effect? Are the short term tactical choices of the tech players compelling at all, keeping in mind that each will want to conserve coins as much as possible, but that losing collectively really sucks?

Thanks for any feedback.
