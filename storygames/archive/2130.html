    Oops, crossposted a thread at the same time on the same subject. Here's mine, and like Jason, I'll write them in the affirmative.

    Happy New Year, folks! What are we on this year, Boar aka "Red Pig"?

    Here are my resolutions:

    Tenra Bansho Zero will see release in 2007. Above all other gaming exercises.

    I'm going to Finish a Game. I've got about three laying in pieces in front of me, I'm going to finish at least the text for one of them. Even if I don't sell it or whatever, I'm going to keep running with it until it's cooked.

    I'm going to get more folks in the area into gaming, and local gamers organized. I set up www.nerdnc.com to help slowly snowball area folks into more concentrated lists. I'm going to try to bring one more non-gamer or Fallen Gamer into gaming. Also, with Clinton, I want to at least throw down one or two area gaming meetups, be it at a coffee house or game store.

    I will entertain the other players in my two regular gaming groups more. Ex: Get my weekly group more focused: We all want to be more focused, but we've been kinda waffling this last year.

    And, not entirely unrelated:

    I will get back in shape. I found another pic of me and Orie shortly after I was married, some almost 8 years ago. I gained about 5 pounds a year since then, and it's time to work to reverse that trend, even at the expense of writing one or two less posts here if it means I'll have 10 more minutes on the 'bag in the garage, the bokuto in my backyard, the push-up bars, yoga-mat, etc. This year I will not be satisfied until I destroy one piece of workout equipment from overuse. I will lose half of the post-marriage weight, so 20 pounds in this next year.

    Also, I was curious what other folks had for Big Gaming Plans this year (if any). Here's mine:

    Camp Nerdly One. It's the big event of the year for me.

    GenCon: Still waffling on it. I'd go if I had something to sell (to help pay some of the expenses, even jsut a little), but with nothing to sell... I still want to meet and hang with my friends, but I'll be less inclined to go until I finally get something of my own together. We'll see how cheap tickets are. :-)

    -Andy
