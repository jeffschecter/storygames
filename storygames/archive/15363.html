[In Camera] No Pets Allowed

\n
\n
\n
\n\nJames_Mullen \n\n\n
\n
\n\nNovember 2011 \n edited November 2011 in Actual Play
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n I playtested In Camera at this month's London Indie Meetup: its my game of found footage-style narratives, where all the scenes are recorded on cameras that are present in the game's fiction, as in The Blair Witch Project or Paranormal Activity.
\n
\nInstead of determing what sort of story we wanted to tell, we just dove straight into play, taking turns to create cams and introduce the characters and locations who appeared on them; it was cool that the game enabled us to do this, but in retrospect, we would have had a more cohesive narrative if we'd done otherwise. We started off with a fairly light domestic tale of a teenage brother & sister squabbling with each other and outsmarting their strict babysitter/housekeeper; then, midway, there was a darker scene in which a boy the sister knew tried to get her into bed, but the remainder of the film returned to a light tone, being a soft revenge comedy.
\n
\nThe left-field contribution was the Cat Cam: on his first turn, one of the player's created a webcam that was attached to the pet cat Bootsy's collar; then, on my next turn, I chose to play the cat as my viewpoint character! Hmmm... it was fun, and it was rewarding to push the system hard to see if we could break it, but its definitely one of those things that bears discussion up-front before starting a game.
\n
\nWith time for a feedback discussion afterwards, I was able to write a second draft of the game text, incorporating two major revisions:
\n- First, a 'write the ticket' section at the start of play, giving questions to be answered about the film's rating, genre, tone and premise.
\n- Second, the removal of the viewpoint character rules: making all the characters shared equally by all players creates more freedom in a game that already has numerous restrictions on what you can and can't do.
\n
\nA very good & productive session overall, which really boosted my enthusiasm for playing the game again soon.
