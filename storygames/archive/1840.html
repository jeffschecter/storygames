    I have...reservations about this sort of thread. It seems a bit condescending in an "Oh, we're so enlightened, we can disregard the fact that the game is obviously crap, and look for the heartbreaking elements" sort of way.

    But since when have I ever let that stop me from playing such a game. Just as long as everybody realizes that newcomers reading this might think we're pricks. Since everyone thinks I'm a prick anyhow, I'm in no danger. Poor Adam, OTOH...

    Does a hearbreaker get hearbreaky points for doing something interesting, but that's been done in other heartbreakers or elsewhere before? Like the whole "Stamina for extra attack thing." I've seen that in versions of Rolemaster, for goodness sake (one of the Rolemaster Companions). Minotaurs as a dickweed race? This is actually one of the "Hearbreaker Common Identifiers" (or HCI as I shall henceforth refer to them), in that it's a fantasy monster race that's been brought in to have an uber-tough choice, and simultaneously made to accomodate the player who wants to play a dickweed character that nobody can stop. This is as common in heartbreakers as cat people, or winged people as race options. It would only be more typical if said minotaurs had four arms for some reason.

    Experience for achieving goals (if it's even in there)? Done, done, done. A well drawn map? Well, I suppose that's better than a heartbreaker with a poorly drawn map, but every heartbreaker has a map of the world with the same sorts of features.

    So far, from what people have cited, I think that the "Dark Gods after the mortals" is actually got some heartbreak to it potentially. Though, interestingly one of the originally listed heartbreakers from the essay is "Of Gods and Men," which has men competing to become gods - sorta similar from what I know of it (I happen to know the author by chance). Other than that, the contours, despite the odd name, seem to me to be the most interesting aspect to date. Records of defeats is really cool if it has any mechanical significance. If not...well if not, then it's just really weird (smacks of some sort of GM-designer fetish).
