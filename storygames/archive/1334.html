This is totally a 24-hour creation, first draft and all that. As I say in the LJ post linked to below, I should have been working on my Reversed Engineering entry, but this got wedged in the queue ahead of that project.

Los Diablos

This hasn't been play-tested or anything else. It is just an attempt to incorporate cards (a readily available resource) as a mechanic and in a couple meaningful ways. It is an attempt to allow players to re-stat thier characters each session and to start play quickly - and yet to have characters evolve in play.

It is also about controlled pacing. The game session has a mechanically defined end.
To this end, what it is lacking is a build to crescendo in the mechanic. As the focus suit becomes more prevalent, optimally it will have more impact. It could be built into the narration of results, I suppose.

The setting was driven entirely by the mechanic and left intentionally free-form.

I would be curious about how you feel about it reaching some of these goals. What strengths and/or weaknesses you see in the mechanic. Without playtesting, I wonder about the length of play under various sizes of groups given the number of decks.
