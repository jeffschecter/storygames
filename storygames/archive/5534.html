\n I am no artist but folks seemed to appraciate this so I figured I would share...



Bills game was great. I was torn. On one hand I say "why go through all this trouble and why not just run SotC" since it basically is a stripped down version of that. However, Bill does some cool things with limiting and defining skills and making the rules his own. I love the development process and I adore defeating people by tacking on more and more negative aspects for us to tag. All in all this was a really fun game. I had only wished we got far enough to hash out my plan to get chased down the elven tower by a summoned dragon.

Thanks,
- Don

    \n Right now there's only the Word document that I used to run the game at Dreamation. It doesn't include what I learned at Dreamation, of course: the communal pool of Fate tokens needs to be augmented with individual pools, "hit points" need to go away, and tools, weapons, and armor need explicit handling--all in the interest of doing what I finally realized I want the game to do: fast-running pick-up high fantasy that isn't dungeon-crawling.

    \n Matthew, these games definately are different animals. I like them for different reasons. RBH scratches and itch and makes that play style more streamlined and fun. FATE (Bill's or otherwise) really makes you think a little differently about how to approach conflicts and deserves some play to hash out if it's where you want your head.

    As I think about it I'd say that these two games and adding The Shadow of Yesterday to the mix is why we can have three different designs covering similar setting ground yet really want to play all three for different reasons.
