I started a game today with a batch of pregens, which resulted in three of the players picking pregens, and two making characters- both Shifters. One of the pregens was a Warforged, so we've got a pretty interesting party (the other characters are a Tiefling Pyromaniac Wizard, and a Half-Elf (Half-Drow) Dark Warlock.)

So, I think most of the monster manual races are fairly underpowered compared to the base races, except maybe the kobold and minotaur. Part of this is a lack of special feats, but most of them get fewer abilities, and an incomplete writeup.

Some basic around the board buffs:
Everyone gets two racial skills and two languages. If they get Common only normally, the second language is up to the player.

For the Warforged, Living Construct and Warforged Resolve both seem decent- very passive abilities, generally. I added Intimidate as a second racial skill (they're built for war, and both the warforged monsters have it.) For the character in my game, it's a minotaur-built warforged, so I gave it the Oversized. (But I probably wouldn't do so for all warforged.)

(It was pointed out to me that DDI had an updated to the warforged, which I'll likely use for human built warforged, but keep this version for a minotaur-built warforged.)

For my shifters (one of each type), I'm thinking of a Keen Sense of Smell type power. Either:

Keen Scent: You gain a +2 racial bonus to Perception. When blinded, enemies only have concealment, not total concealment.

or

Keen Scent: You gain a +4 racial bonus to Perception where scent is relevant. When blinded, enemies only have concealment, not total concealment.

I'm on the fence about this- the former might be too general, whereas the latter might be too specific and difficult to adjudicate, especially since D&D doesn't seem to focus on sense-distinction.

Thoughts?

    Monster Manual races underpowered? Maybe... But increased variety, IMHO, results a power boost when optimizing. Basically, if you're min-maxing and cherry-pick a MM race, that's because it's the most powerful race for your build. So I wouldn't worry too much.
