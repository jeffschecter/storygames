\n You know, a few months ago there seemed to be a blossoming of threads on this and other boards regarding whether gamers "should" openly acknowledge their hobby or limit the opportunities to be identified as weird and non-conformist. I don't think anyone "should" do anything at all on the matter, but for my part I've noticed that I get into much more interesting conversations when people notice me reading RPGs or comic books than when I'm reading essays, mysteries or even scifi.

Sure enough, last night someone noticed me reading Full Light, Full Steam on the bus (by the way, I can't say enough good things about this fun book) and asked me about it; it was the second time in two days. He was curious, asked about RPGs and story games in general, and we had a very nice chat. It was a pretty typical conversation for this sort of encounter (in my experience at least), where the person kind of sounds like he's wondering "Wow, I was in that sort of game some years ago, why did I stop? I don't even remember stopping!"

Overall, it's a source of pleasant interaction for me -- even when I'm not gaming. :-)

    \n Well I decided awhile ago that I wasn't going to be apologetic or shy about my hobbies any longer. There's no point! Frankly, the people I want to be friends with are going to be interested in at least a few of my hobbies or at least open-minded enough to consider checking some of them out. So if I hide what I'm interested in (whether that's rpgs, interactive fiction, sci-fi and fantasy novels, experimental japanese noise rock, or whatever) best case scenario is that someone might want to get to know me and it'll turn out they're not really anyone I have anything in common with, and worst care scenario is that I miss out on getting to know someone who IS into the kind of stuff I dig.

    That said, I don't think I'm very good at talking about rpgs. I mean, I know enough not to blather about "my character" or something, but I have a great deal of difficulty actually explaining what really goes on in an rpg session and why it's fun.
