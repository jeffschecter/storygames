    I can't guarantee that you will get a lot of new people (there are really a lot of forum like this around, people have a lot of choice), but this is the way they do it in a most of successful forums (not only about rpgs)

    Posted By: MatthijsSo let me rephrase:What would be a good way to start a new forum, so that a lot of people would want to join?(Including the people alienated by our current forum.)
    \nHave a strong and sustained physical presence at events: conventions, etc. - all sorts of events which the kind of people you hope to see on "the new forum" happen to attend. I don't mean, like, rent a booth there of stuff: I mean you yourself and any others who are in on the project. I just mean: hang out with people, talk to lots of people, and maybe have a nice good stack of business cards w/the "new forum" address on them. Give out the cards with the forum address to people you'd like to have a follow-up conversation with, or to people who would like to follow up on a conversation with you \u2014 instead of giving them an e-mail address or a Facebook screen name or whatever.

    Also: "the new forum" can actually be that same old forum, no problem, as long as the people who run it really want to change their policies, moderation style and overall social footprint. Sure, it will take a long time before those individuals who once felt alienated go full circle and come back to you, but if you really want a change to happen start focusing on new people, be as welcoming as you can, and in the end even those you burned will be able to see you're not the same old forum you used to be (except, you know, if you actually are!).
