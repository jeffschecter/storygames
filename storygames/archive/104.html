So, I'm against renaming standard RPG terms as a general principle. Early playtesters of Polaris can attest to this -- I hung on to using "PC" and "NPC" long after they were way more obfuscating than englightening.

I'm running into a similar trouble with the "GM" in Bliss Stage.

(As a seperate aside: I really hate the term "Game Master" because it smacks of strange D&S power relationships.)

That aside, I was bound and determined to use it. But in my recent rewrite I've been increasingly skeptical of its utility. Allow me to compare:

The Bliss Stage "GM:"
1) Controls a single authority figure in the Fiction.
2) Is responsible for arbitering discussion.
3) Is responsible for maintaining pressure of the characters, in the absence of them making it for themslves.
4) Does not control a primary character.
5) Controls some secondary characters, but only to the extent that every other player on the table does.
6) Likewise, has no special control over incidental characters.

The "standard GM"
1) Has no immediate fictional representation.
2) Gets final say in all discussion
3) Is responsible for providing threat / challenge to the characters.
4) Does not control a primary character.
5,6) Controls all secondary and incidental characters.

I'm particularly worried that 2, 5, and 6 are going to get ignored in the text, because "that's what the GM always does" will trump what the game rules have to say. That said, this is a much closer-to-GM role than, say, the Full Moon in Polaris.

I'm really tearing my hair out over this one. Moral support either way would be much appreciated.
