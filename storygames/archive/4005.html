\n Hey folks,
\n
\nI wanted to do an award show for my podcast. So the first annual Swinnies (Swine E's) were born. How should I spell that? I made out little entry forms and everything. I didn't distribute them as much as I wanted, and had several folks ask to email me their answers. So here's the deal. If you'd like to get involved you have to figure out a little puzzle that will indicate you are an advanced life form, and then you'll have to fill in the entries and email me. Do not vote here. This thread is for asking questions about the categories, or the process, making suggestions on how to approve, or discussing why my methods suck. I will not count your votes here, you must email me if you want to participate. Your participation and answers will be anonymous. Here we go.
\n
\nThe Puzzle: Email me by taking the words theory from the closet, which is the name of my podcast, and putting them all together like it is one word. Then you should append an approximate symbol which is now called the at symbol and follow it with a gmail and a dot and a com.
\n
\nThe Categorie questions:
\n
\nWhat are the three best RPG's you have actually played from Gen Con 2006 until just previous of Gen Con 2007?
\n1-
\n2-
\n3-
\n
\nWhat are the three best RPG's you have read, but not played, from Gen Con 2006 until just previous of Gen Con 2007?
\n1-
\n2-
\n3-
\n
\nWhat are the three RPG's that were out at Gen Con 2006 that you did not hear about until later, and you are excited about checking out at Gen Con?
\n1-
\n2-
\n3-
\n
\nWhat are the three RPG's due to be released at Gen Con 2007 that you are most excited about looking into?
\n1-
\n2-
\n3-
\n
\nSo here's the break down folks. The first question is about Actual Play, the second about reading, the third is about games that didn't get buzz before Gen Con 2006, but did generate buzz later, likely due to a cool game, and the last is about pre gen con buzz. I know it may be hard to seperate but I had this idea right before Gen Con. Next year the implementation will be better.
\n
\nHere's why I think my awards will be valid. I plan to have complete transparency, so every vote cast will be able to be seen in the post about the awards. Which means you can see the one vote for Arcanum in the actual play category. The awards aren't limited to Indie games but will likely have a strong leaning that way as I intend to hand out award forms to people I have some interaction with which will skew the results. The awards aren't based completely on release, so if a game is still generating lots of actual play it has a chance at winning. I think it will also be interesting to track what happens with buzz over the years. I want the awards to reflect what my listeners are actually playing, and excited about, rather than a choice between products released at a certain time.
