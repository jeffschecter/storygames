    \n Lady Blackbird is heavily based on The Shadow of Yesterday, I understand. The Key of the Mission in the latter is pinged at substantial goal-posts related to the mission, not at any activities related to it. Basically, merely doing something is not enough, but the results of the action have to progress the mission in some quantifiable and permanent way. A typical mission will involve anywhere from one to a half dozen such goal-posts on the way to achieving the overarching goal. For example, if looking for a pirate treasure, you would not get experience points for fighting with other pirates or escaping from the governor's men or any of that routine shit - you would only ping your Key when you find the treasure map, get it interpreted or do something similar that concretely brings you closer to the treasure.

    \n Hi Christine!

    What Eero says is how I usually do it. You hit a Key when you do something significant that fits the requirements for the Key. I'd say 1-2 XP per scene is pretty normal. But it's intentionally left open for the group to decide. Faster or slower is totally fine.

    One thing you can do, if you want to change the speed of advancement, is make an advance cost more XP (instead of slowing down XP collection). 10 xp per advance maybe.

    Check out the other hacks UserClone linked to above. If you're inspired, make your own and let me know about it. I'll link it over on the blog (and all the LB hacks will also be linked on the LB website soon).

    Thanks for giving the game a try!
