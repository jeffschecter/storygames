\n For those visiting or living in or close to NYC...
\n
\nWe've booked our first 2 day gaming event, Recess: Nerd by Nerd East, for Saturday, January 30th and Sunday, January 31st, 2010. Book your calendars!
\n

\n
\nROLE-PLAYING GAMES...
\nWant to get back into a classic dungeon crawl? Or try out a new independent game? Play games like Dungeons & Dragons, Vampire, Burning Wheel, Call of Cthulhu, LARPs, Dogs in the Vineyard, and much more.
\n
\nBOARD GAMES & CARD GAMES...
\nNo Monopoly or Candyland here! Just the \u201cnew\u201d classics such as Magic: The Gathering, Space Hulk, Jungle Speed, Settlers, Dominion, Pandemic, Battlestar Galactica and more.
\n
\nWANT TO RUN A GAME?
\n> Submit a board game or card game... (deadline - January 20th)
\n> Submit a role-playing game... (deadline - January 20th)
\n> For every 4 hours of game you run we will discount $10 off your admission!

\n> Extra hours.
\n> Special events like Nerd Trivia!
\n> Friday night pre-social meet & greet event
\n

\n> Full schedule coming soon.
\n

\n> 647 Fulton Street, 2nd Floor, Brooklyn, NY 11217
\n> map

    \n\nFebruary 2010 \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \n Wow, that was just... I don't have the words... amazing is a start!
    \n
    \n123 people played games and hung out! At least 55 new people! Many new to gaming! And several old friends that we missed dearly.
    \n
    \nSeveral new people came in to check out what this social gaming thing was about for a few minutes on their way to somewhere else and then canceled their plans to stay and play!
    \n
    \n1 person was on their way to a blind date and canceled their dinner plans and brought their date to Recess instead!
    \n
    \nI saw people making friends, exchanging contact information, and others playing too many games to count! We definitely accomplished "make friends, play games."
    \n
    \nI couldn't be happier.
    \n
    \nAlmost every game filled up instantly. And we kept running pickup games to fill the demand. I kept making photocopies of my gaming materials so people could run more games. I think were at least 2-3 additional on the fly mouse guard games.
    \n
    \nI ran 1 mouse guard game that was was easily on my top 5 game sessions I've ever played ever!
    \n
    \nThere was also plenty of indie goodness including in some cases multiple games of Danger Patrol, Lady Blackbird, InSpectres, How We Came to Live Here, Burning Wheel, Burning Empires. FreeMarket, Mouse Guard, and Spirit of the Century.
    \n
    \nEveryone was so enthusiastic, so positive, so happy... I was swept in by the awesomeness!
    \n
    \nNow we need to find more space for more people!

    \n It went wonderfully.

    We had outside events at different locations throughout the city on the Friday before (at Barcade: a video game parlor / bar) with an edition of Nerdburger afterward; and a drunken evening out on Saturday at this crazy carpeted RnB Motown lowbrow bar about 5 blocks from Bric.

    Sunday night, my wife and I ran Marbles, Martinis and Madness, in which Pterysaurus (Evilyn at NNYC) made it to the championship round but then melted down from the fiery onslaught. Event photos of the carnage: Marbles, Martinis and Madness 2010 Championship

    I mostly only had time to catch pickup boardgames between things but it was a sea of tables with stuff going all the time. It met and surpassed all the goals we set out to reach!!!
