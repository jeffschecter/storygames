Matt apparently doesn't want me talking about the Playcollective over at his thread about Galactic, so I just wanted to make sure I brought it up here.

\n

The Playcollective is a group of designers recently pushed out of the Forge nest into the broader world where we can use the extraordinary power of mutual support in the same way that's gotten us so far. We'll be in booth 1735 at Gen Con, right across from the Forge and in the epicenter of the Indie Neighborhood along with the Ashcan Front (where you'll be able to get your copy of Galactic), Burning Wheel/Memento Mori, and a pile of others. Look to the Indie Passport to see everyone involved, and be sure to seek out the publishers and artists in other parts of the hall, too.

\n

So who are the Playcollective? We're Tony Lower-Basch (Capes), Emily Care Boss (Shooting the Moon), Vincent Baker (Dogs in the Vineyard), Malcolm Craig (Cold City), Judd Karlman (Dictionary of Mu), and me (Shock:1.1), assisted by the mighty ninja Rob Bohl. Many of us have more than one game in publication and everyone's working on neat stuff, so stop by and pick up our ashcans or completed works, or drop by for one of our impromptu seminars (How do you make a Dogs town? How do you build an Antagonist in Shock:?) and learn how to make our games sing for you.

\n

We look forward to seeing you there!
