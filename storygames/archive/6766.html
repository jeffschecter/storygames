\n First, working definitions of "debate" and "discussion." By which I mean, "this is what I mean when I say those two words." They may or may not match up to your definitions of them, but that's okay. I'm talking about what *I* mean by them.

This is a debate:
"I am arguing that X is true. Here are reasons Y that X is true."
"I am arguing that X is false. Here are reasons Z that X is false."
"X is true. Here are reasons A, B, and C that Z are fallacious/peripheral/irrelevant and blahblahblah."

This is a discussion:
"Here's my observations of/experiences with X. They have led me to conclude Y. What are your observations of/experiences with X? What are your thoughts on Y?"
"Well, here's my observations of X, which have led me to conclude Z. I find that Y does not make sense, due to my own observations A, B, and C. What are your thoughts on Z?"
"Actually, Z makes a lot of sense and blahblahblah."

I have learned so many things from discussion that I can't even count them. Nearly every time I discuss something, I learn something new. Because of this, I like discussion.

I have not learned one damned thing from debate, except how to debate. Every time I debate something, I get angry and flustered, and I feel like ripping people's throats out with my teeth. Because of this, I don't like debate.

Anyone want to discuss? (I will not debate it)

-Marshall

    \n Here's my thing, right: I can be (as you can see above) somewhat confrontational when it comes to talking about things that are important to me. This is because I have found, over my life experience, that people are very happy to repeat blithe assumptions to each other instead of actively seek for a better understanding. The only thing that gets past passing platitudes back and forth is challenging those platitudes. That challenge can come from the outside world, when reality batters down everything you hold dear, or it can come from your conversation partners who call you on the assumptions that you make.

    Challenges to your assumptions are uncomfortable when you're used to passing platitudes back and forth. Sometimes, people get defensive and try to paint challenges as bad \u2014 invasive, disrespectful, even rude. And that's lame. That's turtling away from life. But worse, that's contagious. Other people pick it up, and then you get whole choruses demonizing what is, at root, the practice of saying, "Hey, what you just said conflicts with what I have experienced." And once you have that environment, then the popularity contests begin and the ideogogues move in, knowing that they can sway people's opinions easily since they won't actually be challenged.

    Debate, challenging others, presenting a well-constructed and well-supported argument, and all the rest are important, foundational principles that allow us to actually communicate.       Posted By: AndyMy challenge (in a sense of "issue that I'm looking at resolving/doing something with") is that I really like a lot of what you have to say. I mean, I read through the rest of that thread, and I found myself agreeing with you on every point. Like, Every. Friggin. One. Everway? Shit, that game is like Rule Zero wrapped up with collectible cards and pretty art ("Take Damage? Well, let the GM decide what that means for your character". In the end, though, the tone got a bit abrasive.

      Asshole? No. Folks disengaged, though. And I thought there was a lot of ground to cover still. And I can't help but to feel that if "X" happened (what is "X"? That's my challenge, I guess) that we could have all continued discussing the implications of your thoughts further, without a lot of folks feeling the need to draw lines, separate themselves from the discussion, etc.

      I'm not picking on you, mind. Sometimes I see Snyder, or Valamir hit the same thing: Like, "Man, if only they approached things a little more delicately, or something... maybe the discussion could have continued".
      \nThis is gold. The dialog is full of problems. Noting this--calling it out as it is done here--is excellent. Strong language is sometimes unavoidable with passion--but it should not be confused with the best way to communicate--which, ultimately is the key (that or chest-beating, but I'll wager most people on these threads are there more to communicate than pontificate).

      \n On one of the large roleplaying forums out there that possesses the ignore list feature, I've noticed that the half-dozen or so people I have consigned to that fate don't really affect how a thread reads even if they're posting heavily in it. Sometimes one of them makes a useful, constructive comment, but then it gets quoted so I see it anyway.

      My rule for ignore-listing someone wasn't just that they regularly derailed threads with axe-grinding or arguing for argument's sake; it was that they were good or subtle enough at it that they had suckered me into helping derail the thread. That way my conscience is clear because I've got them on ignore more for how I react to them than anything else.

      Posted By: SweeneyToddOn one of the large roleplaying forums out there that possesses the ignore list feature,
      \nRecently Story Games has been difficult for me to read. Too much noise. Too much bickering. Too many people trying to win arguments. My personal Zen is being crushed. Like, I will never play 4th. I may one day read 4th, but probably not, I'm guessing I'll get what I need simply by reading people rave about it. However, most of the threads about 4th have turned into total Zen-harshers for me. Same for the rule zero threads and a few others. And these aren't topics that disinterest me, but the approach the posters take is painful to me. It's lead to me skipping almost every post and only stopping on a few posters, who I recognize for saying insightful things in the past, to read their comments. It means I get to skip over people who I know aren't going to contribute anything I find valuable (for whatever reason), but it also means I end up skipping over people I am unfamiliar with but who may contribute something interesting. But my Zen has been so badly beaten up here recently I'm afraid to read posters I don't recognize.

      Leading into... my love of Ignore features. They turned a site that was once painful to me into easy reading by letting me ignore just a few peeps, generally people who are in it to win it, and allowing me to read a thread from top to bottom without doing any scanning (because once I start scanning I don't scan to skip people I don't enjoy reading, I scan to find people I do enjoy reading). I don't think Vanilla even has an Ignore feature (also, I realize some people find the idea of Ignore features offensive) so I wouldn't expect it to be added on, but I dream of it all the time.
