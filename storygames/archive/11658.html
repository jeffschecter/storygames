\n In the standard 3:16 rules the PC with the highest Kills levels up automatically and the rest rolls to see which other PC levels also. Only 2 PCs can level between Missions.

In AD316 Gregor introduces Tokens (Accomplishments) as an additional way to level. In the Development phase the PC with the highest Kills and the one with the most Tokens roll to see which one levels. After that all the other PCs that didn't level roll to see which one also gains a level. There is still the limit of 2 PCs levelling between Missions.

I want to introduce a more "organic" way of levelling. I was thinking of using Tokens as a kind of experience points. A PC levels after gaining a number of Tokens. Let's say a PC levels after gaining 10 Tokens.

Would this be a fun alternative way of levelling? It seems more "fair" to me. This means that more than 2 PCs might level after a Mission but I can't see a problem with that.

    \n Who said 3:16 is fair?

    It's a dark setting. It's about power-hungry, murderous marines who think their doing their duty to a grateful human empire, only to find out that they are actually cannon-fodder in a genocidal war where the ends justify the means regardless of the cost to the pawns involved.

    There is little rhyme or reason to advancement in a tyrannical bureaucracy. Those who perform best for their overlords get a bonus; while toadying, betrayal and white-collar politics allow someone else to advance for no apparent reason (to everyone else).

    Changing the advancement system totally changes the theme of the game, it makes the game less insidious and makes it something for pussy-weak democracy-loving tree huggers...rather than a tribute to glory and bravery in a neo-fascist empire.

    Unless of course that's what you want.
