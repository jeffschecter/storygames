\n Please Help!

I'm working up a game for my Amber group to try out. I want to incorporate story design elements and some other goodness. I will make the final draft available to anyone interested when finished but right now I need some help with Stats/Traits.

My current list is as follows.

Traits:
Combat
o Strength (Brute force and Hand to hand attacks)
o Endurance (Wearing your opponent down and Recovery)
o Weapons (Attacking or defending with a Weapon)
o Speed (Movement and Initiative)

Warfare
o Organize (Get your forces ready)
o Tactics/Strategy (Deploy your forces intellegently)
o Perception (See the dangers/opportunities around you)
o Presence (Convince others to follow you)

Influence
o Position (Your position in court, etc)
o Maneuver (Use social scene to get what you want)
o Awareness (See the dangers/opportunities around you)
o Charisma (Convince others to follow you)

Power
o Chaos (Destruction/Attack)
o Art (Creation/Enhancement)
o Metamorphosis (Change/Obscure)
o Form (Stasis/Defense)

These four primary traits Combat, Warfare, Influence and Power cover the main areas of character conflict in my game. I would like to have four subdivisions to each Trait. The current list needs some work (especially Influence which is the Social trait). Any help would be appreciated.

    \n What aspects of this list do you think need help? That is, what is it about this that doesn't match your goals right now?

    My own personal bias with systems like this is that it if all of the subattributes are independent of each other, then you can end up with what to me are very implausible characters. So here, for instance, you could have someone who is great at deploying his forces in battle, but lousy at seeing the place where his troops could get ambushed. That seems odd to me. I find that overlapping subattributes like this often come about out of a desire for symmetry, so each stat breaks down into four substats just because that way they're balanced, not because there are really four logical separate substat areas.

    \n What would be very cool and not unfamiliar to Amber players would be to rank them inside of each set of four. Then everyone will be able to use any one of the sixteen traits and there will be an acknowledgement that Amy is better at endurance and Bob is better at speed so they wind up pushing for scenes where they come out on top.

    Unlike Amber, I would cleave to equivalence; make it impossible for a meaningful outcome if you have the same rank in a trait. This would then make the players look for less obvious ways to go at each other.

    I would also push for some way to counter with a higher trait from another category. I pull a superior weapon trait on you. I am a sword master and know that I can best you in this situation. you counter with a superior tactics and show me to be surrounded by your mooks.

    I don't suppose that is anything like what you are doing, just my 2\xa2.

    \n New to all this so sorry if my thoughts are not as coherent as needed.

    I'm looking to rift off of the basic ADRPG which I've had years and years of fun playing and running. Althought the game is good there are times when it is difficult to determine reasonable conflict resolution. Also characters may be very different but the rules don't enforce or reward this sort of differenitiation directly. Last I really like the games I'm into now (SOTC, PDQ, Mortal Coil, TSoY, ect) and I'd like to find a way to add their coolness to my favorite game.

    Design Goals?
    1. I would like to see meaningful conflict in each of the main Trait areas (Combat, Warfare, Influence and Power).
    2. I would like to have characters with varied approaches to conflict within each area and the rules to reward creative use of niche subTraits.
    3. I would like each subTrait to be "cool" to have as the best (ie. each person's niche is fun to play and powerful in its own area).
    4. I would like to retain the diceless character of the game (with one small addition of risk) - more on this after I've cleaned up the Traits issue
    5. I would like to add some story driven elements that reward players for delineating and playing interesting stories (I want every player to feel like they are the main character in a Zelazney Amber novel) - more on this after I've cleaned up the Traits issue
    6. I would like to have a single resolution mechanic that feeds advantages from nonTrait abilities (Pattern, Items, Allies, Shadow Resource, Passions, etc) into the Trait resolution system so play is minimally slowed by the mechanics. - more on this after I've cleaned up the Traits issue
    7. There are probably others I'm missing or forgetting. If anyone thinks I'm missing a major area of conflict in a Amber Game please let me know.

    Any help would be appreciated.

    \nI think you need a cleaner break between some of the Attributes and Traits (system idea looks good.)

    For instance, your players may have a problem with 'Combat' and 'Warfare' being separate. I think the semantic problem lies with Warfare. This compounded by Warfare leadership stepping on the Influence niche leadership. There have been great generals who cannot deal with politics. So I'm sure you want to separate Influence out.

    In your system, it seems Warfare is really Vision. With traits of:
    Planning (get resources ready)
    Adapt (tactics/strategy in situ)
    Perception (spot danger/opportunity)
    Intuition (psyche out hidden information)

    which leaves a slightly different mix for Influence, traits of:
    Position (Your position in court, birth order, command of resources)
    Circle (social scene and connections to those not beholden)
    Charisma (your style and ability to be important to others)
    Reputation (history of success, are you usually a winner or loser?)

    I should note that many Amber players resist the notion that Influence impacts their PC. I agree that it should. But how do you work a trait that mechanically changes another PCs decisions?

    Combat and Power seem well defined and easy to grasp.

    \n...does spell out pretty clearly what you want. You've defined four things that people can do in your game, so you're assigning one attribute to each thing. That's pretty clean. If based on your experience running with this group of people you're pretty sure that these are the only four things that people will ever want to do, then your problems are solved.

    The only thing you have to worry about is if someone tries to do something that isn't one of those four things. (Note that the original Amber game has this problem to an extent as well.) If two people play chess, do you use Warfare? And if so, does that make Benedict the best chess player in the universe? What if someone wants to have a horse race, or steer a ship through a storm, or sneak around the Castle, or...

    But you don't have to deal with questions like that if you don't want to. It's perfectly valid to spotlight four types of conflict and then say that the genre assumption is that other sorts of conflict just don't matter, and can be resolved by fiat.

    \n This is a new attempt at this Trait thing. The Bolded traits (which are the only ones that I will orignally stat up my NPC's with) are less specific than the non-bolded ones but cover wider areas. I would love to flesh out a bunch of new traits. Any suggestions?


    TRAITS
    These are examples and you are free to create new Traits. Traits with a Star* are used to influence Powers (Pattern, Logrus, etc). Traits in Bold will be used in NPC write ups (player chosen Traits may also be used). Maximum Trait is 10 at character creation. No Traits may be sold down to Shadow without specific GM approval. Keep in mind that multiple Traits may affect a given conflict and if there is a more specific Trait in a conflict it will have an advantage, so choose wisely.

    Combat (Attacking or defending in personal physical conflict)
    Weapons (Attacking or defending with a Weapon)
    Strength (Brute force and grappling attacks)
    Speed (Movement and Reflexes when it counts)
    Endurance (Wearing your opponent down and Recovery)
    Command (Effectively use authority and resources)
    Preparation (Get yourself and your resources ready)
    Stratagem (Deploy your forces intelligently)
    Charisma (your style and ability to be important to others)
    Intrigue (Learn secrets and keep secrets)
    Subterfuge (Deception and misdirection)
    Perception (See the dangers and opportunities around you)
    Influence (Convince and manipulate others)
    Maneuver (Use social scene to get what you want)
    Circle (social scene and connections to those not beholden)
    Reputation (history of success, are you usually a winner or loser?)
    Position (Your position in court or other important venue)
    Psyche* (Force of will and general competence with Powers)
    Force* (Brute Mystical Might and Mystical Attacks)
    Chaos* (Destruction and Weakening)
    Art* (Creation and Enhancement)
    Metamorphosis* (Change and Obscuring)
    Form* (Stasis and Defense)
