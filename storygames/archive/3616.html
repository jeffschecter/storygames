\n I thought about this after reading a post about seances on another forum that is not allowing posts this weekend. It's a short improv/party game called 'Seance'.

A group of players sits down at a table (or on the floor) together. Everyone takes a scrap of paper and rights down a job of some sort. It could be "politician" or "coal miner" or what-have-you. These papers are all folded and placed in the middle. Each person randomly takes one of them and opens it up to read it. If they end up taking their own, that's okay. Then the papers are folded again and put back in the middle.

Then everyone closes their eyes, holding hands with the people next to them. Someone starts out and says something like, "I see a man, an old man who has gone on to spirit. His name is Robert, ..."

And then silence until someone else speaks up with something to add. What they add should have something to do with the job that they read on the paper. Perhaps a mannerism or affectation or piece of clothing. This goes around around the table randomly as people add to the vision and turn it in unexpected ways. When a person has said their peace (it could be after one comment or five), they let go of their hands. Only people who are still holding hands with someone may continue to speak.

Once the last person gets their hand dropped by the other, they say goodbye to the spirit and open their eyes.

Not sure how it would play out, but with multiple people pulling in different directions using the "yes, and..." concept, it could get weird and creepy.
