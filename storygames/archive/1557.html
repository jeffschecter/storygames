    I get "Dr. Dray" (Dre) sometimes. I've decided that my retirement plans include getting a Ph.D. so I can teach a little but also to make the name true.

    I didn't have a pledge name in my fraternity. Case Western Reserve U. (just CASE these days) had pretty tame fraternities, and the hazing at Beta was good-natured and usually fun for everyone. The part that sticks out in my mind the most is what was essentially forced public speaking or, rather, being told to stand in front of all the brothers who would quiz us on the material we'd studied that week (historical stuff about the fraternity). It was extremely uncomfortable at the time because I was shy and not accustomed to talking in front of groups but in retrospect I consider it an important part of my development.

    The point of harsh hazing, as I understand it, is a psychological breakdown that replaces some core values with new ones and an "earned" sense of belonging to the new group. It's an initiation ritual and it's powerful, even if unpleasant. It's cult-like. Fraternities do it. Sororities do it. Religions do it. The Army does it to great effect.
