    \n Sheyas and I talked a bit at one point about simplifying the gestures down to something that could be easily taught to folks in a one-shot con scenario. Back before GenCon 2006, we even had plans to demo Mridangam, if you can imagine that!

    Basically, you'd tape out a map of the temple on the floor and boil down the hand gestures to the basics of "threaten (thing)" + 3-4 different "response" gestures. Maybe there could be a different "threaten" gesture for physical, mental, and social still (3, as opposed to 15 or whatever). And the specific "thing" being threatened might have to be implied in the performance. Such as "threaten (social): Oh, what a beautiful daughter you have, Rashyana!" Does the dancer plan to seduce the daughter away or corrupt her against her father or use her to embarrass Rashyana in public? Who knows! Doesn't really matter when you're throwing gestures; it's only decided for sure once the gesture negotiation is finished and the scene plays out.

    Anyway, I might do something like that. What do you think, Shreyas?

    P.S. For interested folks, Mridangam is a game based on classical Indian dance that Shreyas wrote for Push 1 (PDF).
