\n I've read the other map threads with great interest, particularly as my lovely wife is about to get a Bamboo pad for her own project this week.

But since I do most of my gaming online, and I pretty much suck at drawing, what are some good sources of maps? What I'm really looking for are simple, less fancy maps of common things -- gas stations, taverns, highways, all kinds of common gaming settings. Ideally, they'd be free* and Creative Commons -- usable so I could take them and mark them up with my own content and then upload them. Honestly, I'd rather shape my encounters to fit a decent map than draw one from scratch.

*I'm not a total cheapskate, I'm willing to pay for a decent packet of maps as well, but beautiful coloring and shading are wasted on me.

    \n Danny--

    Do you know from Google Sketchup?

    Sketchup is a free 3D modeler with lots of useful stuff already modeled and available under an easy license.

    Here's some gas stations and a very nifty tavern interior.

    Sketchup is pretty simple as far as 3D programs go -- you might be able to just load the model, rotate the camera to point direclty downwards over the subject, export an image, and blammo, instant free map. Or, if you wanted to get fancy, you could throw a hex or squaregrid underneath a Sketchup model and export an isometric map like those cool Dragonlance ones TSR put out in the 80s...

    \n I read "highways" and wonder why you don't just use Google maps, zoomed in to whatever scale is appropriate for the encounter you have in mind (car chase? street fight?).

    And if you're talking mainly modern buildings, there's a TON of architectural sites out there (check Dornob) which often have floor plans as simplified blueprints--easy to add color and detail to the existing layouts (though some are... weird...).

    And never, EVER forget Google images. I'll save you ten seconds....
