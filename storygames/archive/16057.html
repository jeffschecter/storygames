\n March and April is the time for registration of the 2011 Indie RPG Awards - see

www.rpg-awards.com/2011/

Any independently-produced RPG released during 2011 qualifies. As for what qualifies as indie, I prefer to be more inclusive, keeping to the main point of leaving out works that were written primarily by authors who retain no control or copyright over what they submitted. There is an updated FAQ about the awards.

On a personal note, I'm going to GenCon Indy again for the first time since 2007. So I'm going to be registering a seminar for presentation of the awards and discussion of the future of indie RPGs.

    \n I don't know how to say this without sounding like That Negative Asshole - but John, I'm not voting this year. From where I sit, it's not representative of what's going on in roleplaying. I'm pretty sure most voters haven't read, let alone played, a fifth of the games. We end up voting for the few things we know, and maybe one or two things we've heard of - which means that excellent things that few people know of get no recognition.

    I think the people who have won the award have deserved it! But others have deserved it just as much, or more, and nobody knows about it.

It's just that they're not horribly relevant to this specific gaming culture (U. S. indie tabletop RPGs). Like, I don't know, Bollywood isn't relevant to the Oscars or something.
