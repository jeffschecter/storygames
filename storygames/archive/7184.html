    \n It's theoretically possible with Burning Wheel, too, but you have to structure your sessions with appropriate "end states"--finishing in a location or situation in which it would makes sense for characters to rotate out, or for a "parallel" story to be told involving other characters in another situation. Cliffhanger endings and mid-scenario endings cause the awkward "new guy appears out of nowhere, while old guy disappears" phenomenon.

    Dread: TFBOP is sufficiently punchy in story progression (and sufficiently lethal) to make for every-evolving teams, from session-to-session.

    Though i have yet to play it, I suspect Primetime Adventures would work, too, so long as you could arrange it so the Spotlight scenes don't require a character whose player is missing a session.

    Basically, you want something with hard-coded chapters, episodes, or encounters that plausibly supports a rotating cast of characters--as such, nearly any game would work with the right plot line or planning. But, yeah, some games are better for it (or make it easier to manage such a process) than others.
