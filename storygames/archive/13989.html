\n So I've just had some modest success setting up a gamer's Meetup group way out here in the lonesome depths of Oklahoma City. About twenty RPG enthusiasts have joined so far; half of these folks gathered together last month to eat pizza and discuss gaming preferences. I found that many of them were surprisingly amenable to the styles of gameplay favored on these forums, even though few had much experience outside of D&D and WoD. I actually ran a five-player session of Fiasco using the "Touring Rock Band" scenario that (I think) went amazingly well (i.e. badly: my character accidentally married her stepfather, another was run over by the Motley Crue tour bus, and a third underwent an excruciating bathroom scene in which his penis was held between two drumsticks as though it was a morsel of Kung Pao Chicken).

My question is this: what would be the best system to use to get a casual, rules-light game going over the group's e-mail message board between our monthly gatherings? I'm looking for something that would require a minimum of rules explanation in advance, while still giving players something of the feeling of participating in a game, rather than just adding serial chapters to a collaboratively written story. All suggestions received gratefully.

    \n I played a good game of Apocalypse World vi email. The way to make online work is minimizing the number of posts/email. AW works because you can say I want to do this, this is the move I'm using, this was my roll, and this is the outcome I'm choosing - all in one post. Also, you don't want combat with a lot of back and forth. I would think Blood & Honor would also be a great choice because fights are over so quickly.
