    \n To those storing to DVD... make another copy, from time to time, and store it somewhere else. Also, not RW, but R only.

    DVDs are awfully fragile, and that's why they are almost never used for real world backup jobs: leave them in the sun, and they are toast (pun intended). Scratch them, and you lose all your data. RWs are also much more prone to errors and becoming unreadable.

    So yeah... either use a Flash disk, or a portable HD that you leave not attached (and not powered) most of the time, if you want a bit more safety there.
    Better if you have two

    (yeah, paranoid sysadmin, here)

    \nBack in the newsgroup days, I made an RPG folder.     \n Personal, daily use: USB key and laptop, with a back-up.

    Back-up and worldwide access: there's any number of free private file hosts. I happen to have two full web hosting packages, so I can stuff things into folders on them, with password protection. In this day and age, for folks like us, a $2 - $4 a month hosting package strikes me as nearly mandatory.

    Emailing to Gmail is also a good idea, if the file size is small enough (are there a lot of >5MB PDFs for sale out there?).
