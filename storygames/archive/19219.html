    \n Thanks for your comments David. The goal is to tell a shared narrative with reward shaped guidelines. The order of Eye>Hand>Mouth>Winner and the reward criteria that feeds one to the next is intended to create a story that initially seems to be under the control of the Winner and then is gradually drawn away from them. The Winner's Acceptance Speech is an opportunity for them to recapture ownership of an Award that we have learned they did not deserve, was manipulated by someone who cares for them and was ultimately awarded for reasons that did not recognize them at all. Will they prove that they are indeed a Winner?
    It is definitely a first draft so comments like yours will help me improve the Game, thank you.

    \n David, I agree with your synopsis and l guess that my reply at this time is that the point of the game is the experience of playing it. Ideally, all four players get a turn at being the Winner, inventing an award to win then passing the narrative on to see their status slip away and to then (hopefully) recapture it. If there is any challenge for the players, it is the challenge of invention and creating a fun narrative. The prize economy is not there at this time to decide a player hierarchy.
    In some ways the Winner does not have to prove anything because they already have the Award. Maybe it is to see if they are worthy of it.
    I also think that the experience of playing Acceptance will be quite different than than that of reading it. It reads as a procedural series of steps with a circular exchange economy but I am not sure if it will seem so inevitable during play (at least the first few times). Of course I could be completely wrong about that which is why I have offered it for you to read and hopefully play.
    It is a simple game and is not designed to stand up to prolonged continual play because it will become repetitive regardless of the Award chosen. The optional Interrupt rule is there to find out what happens when a player wants to `buy' their way into another story. It also points the way for some more intricate mechanics for future development depending on how it plays as is.
