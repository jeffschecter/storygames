    \n Clint, here's my suggestion for PTA:
    \n
    \nMake up 2-3 quick show pitches. Make them diverse. One that's a clear Battlestar Galactica hack, a medical drama, and a teen-with-psychic-powers drama. Each should have a one page pitch with a title, an image, a description, and a list of the characters.
    \n
    \nWhen people come, have them pick a show. Explain that if they were really playing, they'd have created this show (or any other!) from scratch.
    \nHave each person pick one of the characters from the list. So in the medical drama pitch, you list Dr. Corey, Nurse Peters, Dr. Lucille, Patient X. Your four demoers divvy these roles up amongst themselves.
    \n
    \nCut to a prepped scene. "Okay, Dr. Corey is trying to save Patient X, while Dr. Lucille is trying to prove himself more knowledgeable and put Dr. Corey to shame for being a sketchy doctor."
    \n
    \nExplain Screen Presence, and how Dr. Corey is a 3, Dr. Lucille is a 2, and Patient X and Nurse Peters are a 1. Explain this changes between episodes.
    \n
    \nBuild to a conflict. Declare Stakes. End the demo once stakes are declared. End it by saying "Tune in next time to find out whether Dr. Lucille manages to grab the IV from Corey's hand. Find out whether Patient X survives! Find out who Nurse Peters is sleeping with!"

    \n In summary:
    \n
    \n1.) Show them its diversity by providing 3 demo show pitches.
    \n2.) Have them pick a show to demo.
    \n3.) Give them characters, throw them into an immediate situation, build to a conflict.
    \n4.) Talk about Screen Presence!
    \n5.) Explain how Stakes are set, get them to set Stakes for the conflict.
    \n6.) End the demo.
    \n
    \n
    \n
    \nThat's going under the assumption that you're talking about the 10-20 minute Forge Booth style demo.
