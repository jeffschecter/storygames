\n Hey all, I'm looking for a way to keep my private game notes organized. I tend to think of a few sentences here, a paragraph there, usually at work. I want a way to track and organize my thoughts. Basically, the wiki format works best (so I can go back and edit and stuff) more than just a collection of text files, because I'm looking to organize dozens, if not several dozen, notes.

However, I'm looking to do a kind of wiki-like thing without actually (if possible) adding yet another MySQL DB to my server. So something wiki-like that isn't a wiki (an "out of left field suggestion"), or a wiki that needs no DB... something like that.

The closest I was thinking of was perhaps creating a Me-Only Google Group; Google Groups give you a notes section which is effectively a wiki. Looking for other possibilities as well.

Thanks!
-Andy

    I don't understand... why does creating a wiki need to be yet another thing to add to YOUR server? Most wiki-farms are free and publish to their servers... what am I missing?

    But as a suggestion... I have come to use almost exclusively Google's "sites" which is (from everything I can see) their name for their wiki service. It's all free (just like a Google Group).

    I make Campaign Wiki Bibles for EACH campaign I run that's open to all players. For my GM-only notes I just make a page with no links to it for players to see. Most times I'll make a "GM ONLY" navigation link for myself on the main Campaign Wiki page or (if I don't trust the players to not look) I simply make the page and bookmark it for myself only. I've also made full NEW wikis like your idea for "Me-Only" Google Groups that is a wiki for listing notes to EVERY campaign that I GM. It's my Eyes Only page for EVERY game I run, have ran, or plan/want to run, as well as any general gaming fun notes I come across for techniques or campaign ideas.

    Here's my Saturday D&D campaign Wiki Bible for example...
    http://sites.google.com/site/fullmettlefantasy/

    If you look in the Navigation column (to the right) and scroll down you can see that I list a bunch of helpful links for myself and players as well as a GM Only link for my own notes. It's simple and all in one place. My players have MORE than enough with the other wiki notes in the campaign bible I post that they don't even think to look at my private notes. My players are fairly active in updating ideas on our wiki though...

    Hope that helps.
    -kev-

    \n I like Tiddlywiki, but there are some technical issues if you're trying to use it with Windows XP.

    XP imposes extra security precautions on files downloaded from the web---one of which is that it doesn't allow files to rewrite themselves, which is how TIddlywiki saves itself. You can find instructions on how to overcome that, but I still haven't been able to get it to work right (I'm currently running XP Media Center Edition). I guess it works for some people, though, so it's worth a try.

    \n Twiki is also a flat-file-based wiki solution.

    Basically, there's quite a few options, for not only flat-file but other preferences or needs. And don't forget the power of Semantic Wiki, if you really are trying to organize a lot of cross-related information or want to do powerful queries on your notes. But that might only be available on a DB-driven wiki like MediaWiki (though there's no real reason something lie it couldn't run on a flat-file wiki).

    Google Sites is kind of cool, I'll admit... but I'd look carefully into options for performing a backup of it (I couldn't find any, but I didn't look REAL hard). I'm generally loath to put all my eggs in one basket, if I can't trivially backup that basket... err, metaphor breaks down, but you get my drift I'm sure.
