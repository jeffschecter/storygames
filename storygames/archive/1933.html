    The dominoes are compared - they're really just acting like dice (or cards in, say, PTA). I wanted to use dominoes because of the "domino mask" association. The resolution mechanism is actually the part of the game I'm least happy with and most likely to re-jig, but it will probably stay domino-based. Though I am likely to add a dice alternative for people who don't happen to have a set of dominoes.

    There's no GM as such. The person with the most Face at any given time is the "Arbiter", and declares where the next scene takes place, though other players are free to say that they're elsewhere doing something else.

    Because the world is a setting for a novel, it's already well-defined. Everything takes place in a late-medieval/early-renaissance city called Bonvidaeo. There's no magic but there are a number of conventions about the masks which I set out in the game text (you don't need to have read the novel to play the game). The Arbiter, or for that matter anyone else, can make up any location in the city (I need to make this explicit in the text), and anyone can introduce NPCs but they have no mechanical effect.
