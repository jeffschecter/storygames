\n So Sons of Liberty will have a "Versus Mode" set after the revolution in the first years of the republic, where the Patriots become each other's most frustrating enemies.

This is, more or less, what happened in history: after the war was won, the Founding Fathers started arguing over how the ensuing nation would be run and what it would look like. The Federalists and Anti-Federalists, Agrarians and Industrialists, Slavers and Abolitionists, all got their start in this period. Lots of what we now recognize as "Democrat" and "Republican" got started here, too: just the names were different.

In any case, though, I have 17 Patriots in the game. I want to give each of them a number of stances on divisive issues such that, given any pair of Patriots, they'll have something to fight over. So something like:

Thomas Jefferson
* Pro-Agrarian
* Anti-Federalist
* Slavery Apologist
* And So On

If Jefferson was put up against Paul Revere, they could argue Agrarian/Industrialist issues. If Jefferson is put up against Salem Poor, they could fight over Slavery. And so on.

So here's the question: given 17 figures, how many issues (with binary yea/nay stances) do I need to have on each character to ensure a viable contest between any given pair?

    \n Five issues will let you handle anything up to 32 figures. If you drop to 16, you can get away with four. In general, you can handle up to 2 to the power of n figures with n issues.
