\n GM character background building ideas?


I'm trying to pull together some ideas for my game group for the types of information, questions, and general cool things the players could do for a new campaign - to give background and story seed ideas to the game from themselves as players and/or from their character's point of view.

My point... requests and questions for you to offer ideas, inspiration and help.

YOUR NOTES
----------------------------------------------------\n
    \n
  • What have you done or seen for background gathering stuff for character creation?
    What interesting, cool, different things have you done or seen in character creation?
    What questions or background bits do you like giving/having from characters?
    What questions or background bits and/or campaign or gaming environment things do you like giving/having from the players in the group?
    • \n
\n
EXAMPLES FROM BOOKS or WEBSITES
(please include links or book/page references)
----------------------------------------------------\n
    \n
  • Are there any cool, interesting, unique character creation steps or questions/requests from any RPG, Website or MMO that you think would be neat to include?
    Do you have suggestions for other games that might be of different genres/styles that might have cool, interesting, unique character creation ideas? From mainstream? Indy games? Computer Games?
    Images - one thing I love to see from players is an image to represent their character. Do you have suggestions or websites for image builders and/or photo/illustration sites to use?
    Name and background online "generators" can often be helpful. Can you suggest any you like or use?
    • \n
\n


Any other general ideas or suggestions?
thanks!!

    \nI'd have the players draw their characters, even if they don't have what adults consider good drawing skills. To keep the drawings boring, the players will have to add detail...

    Also, I like to request at no more than, no less than 3 descriptors that a stranger would notice about a character after a minute of interacting with him (I think I stole this one from someone here but can't remember who it was). These can be physical traits, mannerisms, or props. For instance, one of my wife's characters was "tall, willowy, with a big ol' mole on her face."

    \n A lot of time, if a player is having problem with picturing their character, i ask them "Who would you cast in the role of your character if this were a movie?" It's an easy way for new and old players alike to better visualize their character, their attitudes, looks, and dress in many cases.
