Sharing our local gaming club experience

Anemone
January 2007 edited January 2007 in Play Advice
Seattle is one of a few privileged areas when it comes to gaming: we have several game publishers, RPG designers, gaming stores (including stores that are very supportive of small press and indie games), and active gamer groups. From discussions on various forums, I've come to appreciate how fortunate we local gamers are.

As an active participant in the local gamer community, I've helped put together some notes about our experience on the Seattle Gamers Assemble! wiki. (Those who participate in the Story Games wiki have probably noticed that I love helping with wikis!) Sections that may be of general interest include some how-to articles we started pulling together, and the in-progress archive for our weekly club games.

Why would you be interested in our old game summaries, you wonder? Because we've tried a lot of fun little games that you too may be considering. We're still piecing together the play reports from our mailing list archives, but we have five years' worth of one-shot or short-run games so far, many of them with actual play info, and the archive is growing. In our how-to section, we offer some suggestions for people who might like to organize similar weekly events.

Keeping the archive updated for the new games will of course be a lot easier. 2007 is starting out as a great gaming year here, as the next two months are already booked solid with great games. We just wrapped up Johnzo's Dungeons & Savages two-parter (Johnzo is a Story Games regular, of course) and had a great time! Coming up: Savage Lorax, Roanoke, Faery's Tale, 50 Fathoms, and HeroQuest. What's not to love?
