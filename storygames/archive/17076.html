\n Hey people.
I'm looking for RPGs which use parts and assets that fell on the more exhibitionist side - like cool dice pool mechanics, cards, minis and any things that can be used to visualize the game more so than just the "classic" RPG sense.

I'm looking to create a more compact experience for people that never played RPGs and attend a convention or expo. The way we're thinking of setting this up is in a "lightning round" format, when games last for 30 or so minutes, not a full session.

Suggestions already made:
- Marvel Heroic Roleplaying (large dice pools, Doom Pool, post-it notes to indicate scene distinctions, complications, assets, resources, or other spontaneous creations).
- Do: Pilgrims of the Fliying Temple (uses a bag with black and white stones).
- Treasure (http://thistlegames.com/thistle/treasure/about_treasure/).
- Warhammer Fantasy Roleplay 3rd edition.

I hope I made myself clear

    \n Coupling 30 minutes of play and an attractive spread, I'd recommend...

    Dread (which uses a Jenga set)
    Don't Rest Your Head (which uses, in a tactile way, differently coloured dice)
    Joe Prince's Hell 4 Leather (which uses tarot cards and is quick to learn)

    There are many gorgeous games that might not work in 30 minutes, like the Italian version of Montsegur 1244 or Freemarket.

    There are two games of mine, Chronicles of Skin (which uses custom cards, tokens and your own doodles) and Hell for Leather (which uses a tower of stacked up dice and a colourful bowling target). I'm a gaming pervert.I like touching things and staring at them. These games were partially designed to scratch that itch.

    \n Definitely Geiger Counter. It uses a map made during play.

    Also Chronicles of Skin, a game involving mapping and doodling, as well as Hickey's other game, Hell for Leather, which uses a stack of dice and flinging other dice at them without knocking them over, as a mechanic.

    Dread (Jenga Version).

    Montsegur 1244 (cards and relationship map).

    Psi*Run (index cards/map).

    On Mighty Thews (really cool map creation at the start of play helps characters relate directly to the world).

    Microscope uses index cards as a sort of visual timeline, which is boss.

    Universalis, which uses coins as a currency with which to bid for ownership of the fiction, as well as index card on which to write important elements of the story.

    Penny For My Thoughts uses pennies to great effect, come to that.

    I'm sure I could come up with more, but is this what you're looking for?

    \n You throw two dice and "claim" a square, narrate your action (note, after the roll). The Authority takes the blow, then claims squares automatically. Because the Authority is an a$$h*le. Then they f*** you up a bit.

    Whoever talks first stands up, rolls dice, and tries not to hit the Authority claims. If they do, you lose. If they hit a claim made by another character, you win. If they hit a free square, they claim it, the Authority takes the blow, then claims a square automatically.

    LIGHTning fast. I'm simplifying some of it, and there are a few edge cases, and you don't "automatically" lose when you hit an Authority claim, but that's the just of it. It's frantic, and really rockin.
