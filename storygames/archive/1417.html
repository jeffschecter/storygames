    It's funny. My experiences with Blood Bowl are exactly the same as yours. But my conclusions are different. Basically, yeah, Blood Bowl simply isn't as interesting to play as one would want it to be. It's a boring drag. The idea is great. But the execution sucks.

    What's interesting, however, is that I think that they've done about as well as one can expect. That is, the game is stuck in the basica wargame problem, which is that either you add details which bog it down, or you reduce the details to speed it up, and make it even more bland. Basically there's a curve involved here that shows a correllation between detail and time that says that the return rate of fun is very low compared to the work involved. The number of fun moments where you get to play a dirty trick or something at a critical moment are dwarfed by the number of little decisions moving the figures around the board and whatnot.

    The way I see it, if the game were actually fun to play, more people would play it, and you wouldn't have the player problem. I don't have problems putting together groups of players to play Space Hulk campaigns, for instance, that take just as much time committment. I don't have a problem putting a Blood Bowl group together, either. It's easy to sell. What I have trouble doing is getting anyone to play the second match. And almost nobody plays their third. Much less their fifth or tenth if you're trying to make a season that allows for rematches.

    So what I find ironic is that, from what you say, the game isn't any different online. I mean, from your description it sounds like the matches take just as long to play out as they did with minis - and why wouldn't they? It's not like the Blood Bowl rules are difficult (seeing as the Fun ratio is so low, they at least make the game quick, erring on the less detail side). It seems to me that all you're doing here is finding the people who organized all of those Blood Bowl games way back, and who have committed themselves to playing in the memory of those seasons that they organized that died on the vine.

    Which is to say that I'm betting that if you do get a league together that it'll mean getting to play the fourth game of the season...maybe. I think there'll be few actual Blood Bowls played. Oh, I'm sure the site is replete with examples of it having happened. But I wonder how many that represents with respect to number started. And how many of them were really complete, and didn't have lots of forfiets in them. I simply find it hard to believe that without improved mechanics, that the game would really be any more interesting to play.

    And this is a great irony, of course, since it's a computer game. Meaning they could very easily have introduced all sorts of complex detail that's involved in the calculations and output, without adding at all to the play time. I see this regularly. I've played the computerized Space Hulk game that's just the boardgame done up this way. It's just as fun, but not particularly more fun. Oh, sure, there's the animations of marines getting taken down by genestealers and such. But other than the aesthetics, the game play is the same. Meaning that I don't find it to be really in any way superior to the TT version.

    Now maybe I'm missing something. Maybe if you still haven't memorized your teams stats, then rolling the D6 under the stat might take a while longer than having the computer decide the result, and play is faster for you. Or there's something added to the engine that the TT version doesn't have. But color me skeptical.

    Perhaps it's because I felt so burned by Blood Bowl, adding supplements (like Star Player) and new editions and such, thinking that if we just added the right stuff, people would be so interested that they'd dedicate all sorts of time to playing it. Never happened. So what I should say is good luck with it.

    In any case, if you want a passing game with Ogres, remeber to take a snotling so you can pass him along with the ball. :-)

    Mike
