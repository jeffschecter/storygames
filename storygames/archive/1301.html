    I'm going on the theory that having a clear picture of the game and playable mechanics is the thing to aim for, and after that comes lay-out, and after -that- comes the frilly touches like advice&tips, etc. That way, I can stop when time is up, and submit a decent thing, instead of something pretty but unplayable as is. That doesn't mean don't work on the frills, just get the basics cooked first.
