    \n I'm running my game-in-development, Misspent Youth. That link directs you to many posts and some early game outline documents. I'll paste the main paragraph from that site to give you an idea what it's about. If anyone has any questions, please do ask.

    Misspent Youth is about friendship, the fight for freedom, and being awesome. My Fred-Hicks-style Hollywood Pitch is: In Snow Crash\u2019s future, Danny Ocean\u2019s crew\u2013Goonies-sized\u2013play craps to take down Big Brother. It\u2019s a sci-fi game about 12 to 17 year olds in a future about ten years from now. The players create a villain together, then create their Youthful Offenders to tap into that. All conflicts are group conflicts and it is not possible to mechanically act against members of your crew. You can fight with them, you can even have an in-group argument touch dice, but if you do the Authority player plays the part of the strife in your relationship.

    \nSaturday Midnight: Under my Skin - live form version
    I'll be doing a playtest session of my game in development Under my Skin. It is the third in the trio of games started with Breaking the Ice and Shooting the Moon, Three Quick Games about the Human Heart. I'll be testing out the live form version of the game (there will be a tabletop and live version of Under my Skin). The game is about love, commitment and infidelity. It's unofficially called the "polyamory game".

    I'll also be helping Tobias run two jeepform games at Dreamation:

    Friday 8:00pm: Jeepform, "The Upgrade!" Run by Tobias Wrigstad and Emily Care Boss
    The Upgrade is a game based on reality TV--Temptation Isle style, where couples compete to see who will stay together and who will break apart after spending a week at a luxury resort in a romantic setting with--another person's partner! This game is a showcase for jeepform techniques, using flashbacks and flashforwards, bird in the ear and other tricks to get the players to tell the story of these characters' struggles with love and betrayal. And to examine how we as viewers revel in the attraction and repulsion we feel for our reality tv show champions that we often love to hate.

    Saturday 2:00pm: Jeepform, "Lives, Births, Deaths." Run by Tobias Wrigstad and Emily Care Boss
    An experimental scenario, exploring how our lives crisscross and wander down unknown roads. You play out the story of a life, as it might be told to a grandchild by a person looking back at the ups and downs and twists and turns their life made. This game is premiering at Dreamation.

    \n Come play "REX RICH and the WARLORD of MARS!" (Spirit of the Century)!

    Join Rex Rich, wealthy aviation entrepreneur and Hub City's most eligible bachelor, as he tries to win a big contract with the War Department for a rocket-powered zeppelin interceptor. Piloted by Rex's old buddy from the Lafayette Escadrille, two-fisted barnstormer Jack Redstone, the prototype rocket-plane was built by Jack's kid brother Snap, teenage rocket scientist from Iowa. The test flight is big news, and do-gooder reporter Lucy Lovelace is covering the story for the Hub City Tribune--especially since Dr. Einstein, the Greatest Mind of All Time, is expected to show up as a League of Nations science observer.     I'm running two sessions of Shock: which will be precisely as awesome and creative as the players who show up, and will be about what they want it to be about. The world will probably end in some significant way as we watch the hopes and dreams of personhood get crushed in the shockwave of the future!     \n Here\u2019s a quick description of each session. For even more information go to the relevant post at Stone Baby Games:

    Thursday, 8 PM: \u201cIn Memory of Octavia Butler\u201d

    This session of Steal Away Jordan is inspired by Octavia\u2019s Bulter\u2019s Kindred. At least one PC will be a modern day person who wakes up on a Tennessee cotton plantation in 1820, where she is assumed to be a new slave. How will you integrate yourself? How do the other slaves react to your mysterious arrival and odd manners? If you never escape this time period, how will you make your way in your new environment?

    This game will be serious, but fun, and I\u2019m rating it R for sex and violence. Before we play, we\u2019ll establish our boundaries on race issues, language, where the veils are, etc. We\u2019ll explore some really dark themes, but each participant will be responsible for maintaining a safe environment for all to explore.

    Friday 9 AM: An Antebellum Ghost Story

    1850, Olive Branch Mississippi, the Tubbs Plantation. Master Beauregard Tubbs has died under mysterious circumstances, and his three sons have come to divide the assets of the plantation, including the slaves. But there\u2019s strangeness afoot: apparitions, big black dogs with blazing eyes, black cats, and a pretty young slave who collects graveyard dirt. The players, all slaves, are in the middle of it. Who is your enemy? Who is your ally? Who is the smooth talking man standing in the crossroads?

    I love playing the folk tale! This one\u2019s for those who want fantasy in their slave narratives. This will be super fun, and probably pretty silly, all ages welcome. Start your Friday with some down home creepiness.

    Friday, 2 PM: Congo Square, New Orleans, 1800

    On Sundays in New Orleans, slaves and free people of color congregated, largely unsupervised, on Congo Square. They met friends, family, and lovers. Slaves often performed alleged Vodou and African rituals for the curious spectators. They danced, drummed, bartered, and occasionally planned to escape. Life for city slaves was difficult, but not as rough as their plantation counterparts. Freedom of movement allowed permitted slaves the oppurtunity to escape, but is it worth it if you have to travel through bayous and swamps to get anywhere safe?

    This game will explore the lives of slaves when their masters are not around. Most interactions will be between other slaves and free people of color, but don\u2019t worry, as a slave your life is intricately entwined with your subjugators. They won\u2019t just be in the shadows. I\u2019m hoping for rebellions, escapes, and love against all odds.

    Another serious, but fun game. This one is rated PG.

    \n Alright, I'm running two games at Dreamation this year. You'll find two sessions of the heist and caper movie role-playing game Criminal Element (created by yours truly), as well as a session of Seth Ben-Ezra's brilliant Dirty Secrets. Write-ups for both games are as follows:

    Friday @ 8.00 PM and Saturday @ 8.00 PM
    Criminal Element
    "The score on this heist... The score is worth the heat it could bring. It's worth breaking through security systems, racing down city streets, or shooting down anyone that gets in our way. It's worth the time we could do, or the bullets we could take.

    It's worth all of that because we're criminals and this is what we do. We take scores."

    Play Criminal Element, the Caper and Heist move role-playing game. Inspired by films such as Heat, Lock Stock and Two Smoking Barrels, and Reservoir Dogs. Together at the table we'll create the perfect heist, from the crooks running the job to the plan itself, and then put into bloody action.

    Why You Need To Play This Game
    Have you ever liked a heist movie? Heat, Reservoir Dogs, the Sting, Ocean's 11, Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels, Killing Zoe, Sexy Beast... God, I can go on and on. Well, if you did then you need to play this game. Create your own heist. Lay out the plans with the other players, from what your stealing to the opposition that gets in your way. Bet on your actions and get rewarded. Betray your fellow PCs and watch as the system turns the heat up.

    By the way, a very big "thank you" to Judd for helping me write that copy about a year ago when I was going to run the game at the last Dreamation.

    Saturday @ Midnight
    Dirty Secrets "To Live and Die In..."
    You thought you were done. You thought you just had to get through this last night and then you were going to leave and head back to the normal, workaday world. You were wrong. A crime has been committed, one so terrible that you have to investigate, that you have to know why. And it doesn't matter how many other horrible secrets you uncover along the way.

    Dirty Secrets, the game of deception, betrayal, corruption, and murder, all set in the Here and Now. As a group, we will guide a single investigator through a complex web of lies toward that most elusive prey: the Truth.

    Why You Need To Play This Game
    This game is beautiful. It's a mystery game inspired by the likes of Hamnett and Chandler. One player takes on the role of Investigator while the rest of us work together on creating an intriguing web of lies and criminality, all leading up to the revelation of "who done it". And this is the really cool part: we as players don't actually know who the culprit is. Only through game play will the game itself reveal the perpetrator of the crime. Help create an engrossing mystery that will leave both the Investigator and us as players guessing until the very end.

    Ganakagok: The Edge of the Ice

    An invocation:

    Forever have the People lived in starlight cold;
    But now we gather, and soon ascend
    And go together to world's end.

    Ganakagok is a game where you play a hunter, warrior, or shaman of the People, who live on a vast, frozen icescape in a world of eternal night.

    But the dawn is coming -- coming soon.

    What will the rising of the Sun mean to a people who have always revered the Stars?

    ***

    The rules of Ganakagok rely on an oracular tarot to guide the development of the in-game situation and the consequences of character action. The effect is to create a powerful and gripping quasi-myth that feels like an authentic legend of a vanished people while at the same time focusing intensely on the link between player and character. It's dynamite.

    \n I'm running three sessions of Kingdom of Nothing, my game in design. It was one of the games up at the Ashcan Front, and I've revised it a lot from the feedback I received. It's a gritty urban fantasy about people living on the streets who've lost everything they owned, including their memories and identity. Each character has to work their way out of their own psychological labyrinth while fighting their personal demons manifested as monsters. Think Dark City meets the Fisher King in the setting of Neverwhere.
    \n
    \nCharacter histories are created in a round robin collaboration by the other players at the table, and are revealed through play. The more the character (and player) learn about who they were in their past life, the more strength they have to struggle their way out of the cracks and out of the fantasy world they're trapped in.
    \n

    \nGiants, Saturday at 2:00 PM

    From the Biblical Goliath to John Henry and his hammer, to Norse Giants in their cold home of Jotunheim -- stories of their great strength, ravenous hungers, and magical gifts--are found in all cultures and throughout history.

    Create your own Giant and the Community it protects and is supported by. Fill the world map with marvels that will draw your Giants on quests of adventure and hunger.

    Will your Giants build a new age of glory or stomp civilization into rubble to feed the hunger which drives and empowers them?
