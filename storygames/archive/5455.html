\n Hi everyone. I'm starting something called Play Now, which is a group for people who want to be able to assemble a game on short notice. I'm really enjoying running these one-shots and other low-prep games online on short notice.

Play now is for me and everyone else who wants to be able to play games online on short notice. I can find people for a quest in Warcraft within half an hour - and I'll be damned if I can't do the same with an RPG.

This is the party line:

http://games.groups.yahoo.com/group/play_now/

    \n Actually, now that I think about it, many IM clients/Protocols could do something like that. Looking at Jabber/XMPP for now, most servers include support for user-created chat rooms (At least jabber.org does. No idea about the gmail ones...), it includes place for descriptions in the personal Profile as a place for "Looking for Stuff" or "Favorite Games" type information. Most Clients like Pidgin support ordering your Contact List into groups, so one could make a "Go Play" group and thus have an easy overview of who's online at any moment. (If people created special "Go Play" Accounts separate from any other IM accounts they have, they could even use that to signal "Hey, Looking to play NOW!" without confusing people when they are only for other reasons.)

    Hell, many clients even support VoIP based on XMPP. (Google Talk and Cochinella, for example. The latter even includes a whiteboard.)
