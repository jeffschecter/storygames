Now in its second edition, GnoccoCON, convention "Reggiana" of Modern Roleplaying, this year turns into a 2 days.

For the second year Biasola, a little place near Reggio Emilia, will host us!

Many games, many fans to know and play with. Good food to taste.

Click on THIS LINK to access the forum-advisory organization.

Give yourself the "chizze" (a tipical local dish in the images). Let the lard and the game runs strong in you!



For further information, visit the forum (http://gnoccocon.forumfree.it/) or directly ask by mail (gnoccocon@gmail.com)
or by this thread! ^^

    Posted By: Eero TuovinenCould we get more stats on the convention? How many people, what sort of demographics? Is it annual? These are the sorts of things that interest me when considering the future.
    \nAnnual (up to now, which is not to say much). Currently about 60 people have confirmed their attendance. Mostly GenteCheGioca.it users, that's to say: same people who attend InterNosCon in spring (of which this meeting serves as an autumnal double of a sort), making it a meeting point for the hardcore dirty hippie gamers of Italy.

    Three "turns" of gaming: one long 6-hours slot on Saturday, a morning slot and an afternoon slot on Sunday, with meals scheduled in between and after the games. Evenings are for partying, but may well be used for some more free play.
    The games program is being defined and currently includes such tasteful events as A Taste for Murder, Ganakagok, Society of Dreamers, A Flower for Mara, A Penny for my Thoughts, Best Friends, Spirit of the Century, Solar System in two different White-Wolf themed flavors, and numerous play-test sessions for the currently in-progress works of aspirant Italian designers (including yours truly). Proposals of events welcome until August 29th.

    Compared to INC, which is the luxury package of Italian RPG conventions, GnoccoCon aims to be convenient, even a bit rustic. No entrance fee. You only pay for what you eat and drink. Greatly discounted prices for spending the night at nearby hostels and pensions. The location is a public park, so with some luck we'll be gaming under the sun and amidst the green (there are plenty covered areas just in case it rains, though). The park also includes a restaurant, offering local delicacies for a very low price, and served you right on the very gaming table.
