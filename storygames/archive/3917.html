\n

Malcolm and I just ran down to Collective Copies, the birthplace of Dogs in the Vineyard, Under the Bed, and Polaris, to pick up his business cards and the third printing of Under the Bed. On the way back, what should we see, but the UPS truck trundling down the street, facing away from my house.

\n

I started breathing fast. So much has been fucked up with Shock: in the past, and I've been looking out the window all day waiting for my shipment of 1.1 to arrive (originally planned to arrive a week ago, then Friday, then today).

\n

On my doorstep were two dense little boxes. Inside, were 100 bright orange squares. The relief I feel is tremendous. Fidlar Doubleday really came through with their extraordinary customer service. It's worth paying 50 cents more a copy, frankly, to feel like your printer's on your side.

\n

I pulled out a copy and tried handing it to Malcolm, who refused it, saying that I should have the first one off the top of the box. So I stuck it under my arm, pulled out the second one, signed it, and handed it to him.

\n

And here is the Graham-approved Mr. Craig, holding #2.

\n

\n

... but then he snuck a peek, and his hard, easily-embarrassed fa\xc3\xa7ade began to crack.

\n

\n

... but then he started reading. This truly embarrassed Graham.

\n

\n

So come by the Playcollective and pitch a tent!
