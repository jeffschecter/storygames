Atmosphere in Games

JasonP
July 2006 edited July 2006 in Story Games
Well, as I posted on The RPG Aquatic just a little while back (here), I've recently categorized myself as a Atmosphere driven player. Here is a summary of what I mean, using pretty specific wording:

Atmosphere: A critical aspect of games which seek to provoke a particular emotional response from play (employing an emotional context). This is something like color meets situation, and delves into a shared experience realm. Through the personal connections registered by the shared experiences, emotions are evoked using color and situation- using atmosphere.

But to summarize, I'm looking for and trying to develop games which seek to provoke particular responses in the player (or a complex set of them) using shared experience to do so. Horror is probably the simplest example of this kind of affair, where the designer seeks to evoke fear by employing things which are commonly 'scary'. I'm not so much interested in horror as other genres though, and how to promote this in roleplay. I suppose (now thinking outside the RPG box) movies that tug on your heartstrings work the same way, but drawing from a different set of experiences.

So, what Atmospheric games are out there? What should I be looking at as I design? Should I revise/clarify this definition, and if so how?
