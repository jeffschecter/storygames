Dictionary of Mu + DitV = Mu Dogs?

iago
November 2006 edited November 2006 in Story Games
So, over on this other thread there was talk about how Dictionary of Mu would sell better if it had its own system in it rather than (arguably) requiring Sorcerer and Sorcerer & Sword.

Maybe yes, maybe no. I certainly think that Mu is strongly flavored and theftable for a number of other systems -- right now, Sorcerer doesn't feel natural to me, so I admit I'm doing my usual thing of flirting with the idea of hitting a different system for the job. Fate's an easy reach for me, but I don't want to homogenize my play to all Fate all the time, if that makes any sense.

So I've started thinking about Mu Dogs. There's something about that that works for me. The Sorcerers of Dictionary of Mu are empowered to heal Marr'd and restore it to its former glory -- as well as to toss it into the abyss and leap in after it, cackling. That former aspect of Mu Sorcerers feels a little Dog-like to me -- ride into "town", find out what ails it, take it head-on. Adding a dimension to Dogs-like characters where they get powerful tempted to walk a darker path instead is also pretty appealing to me.

I also start thinking about the progression of conflict escalation in Dogs. Generalized outside of the western paradigm, it seems to go: Talking -> Physical Action -> Beating -> Killing. Taking this progression into the Muniverse, as it were, makes me tempted to add a fifth onto that; something like -> Sorcery, if you get my drift.

I need to re-soak myself in Dogs in order to get this goin' on fully, but I think this could have legs. I've got other thoughts, like using Heart as a stand-in for Humanity, and adding in a fifth Lore stat (or maybe not Lore -- maybe we call it Damnation). Sorcery fallout might involve dice transferring from Heart into Damnation -- experience might let you pull some dice out of Damnation and back into Heart. Or something like that. The main deal there is to make sure that the humanity aspect of thing remains in the system.

There're other wrinkles -- the PCs having direct sway over demons definitely makes for some challenges of implementation that I'm not sure that Dogs is entirely suited for. So some new thought will need to be put towards that; I don't see demons as simply existing as Relationships or what-have-you.

But the more I think about it, the more I want the Mu Dogs to ride across the Red Wastes.

What do *you* think needs to be done before they can saddle up?
