\n I'm working on a simple, pick-up story game. Everything is done at the table. No prep. The first thing done at the table is to develop the setting. Well not really develop....but select and then add to. There will be a bunch of "Setting Cards." Randomly choose one. It will list the Setting Core and three tropes.
\n
\nFrom here there are two options. Not sure which will make it to the rules. The first is simply jump to the next step. The rest of the setting is developed during play.
\n
\nThe second is to go around the table and each person adds a single trope or removes one. The rest will then be developed during play. There may be some sort of veto aspect in this option.
\n
\nAnyway, back to the point. I need some help creating these setting cards.
\n
\nExamples:
\n
\nFantasy: Magic, Knights, Dragons
\nRetro Sci-Fi: Rockets, Rayguns, and Robots
\nSteampunk: Airships, Incredible Devices, Top Hats
\n
\n
\nThese will just provide the framework or backbone of the setting. Being a story-game, the players will develop the rest of the tenets as they go. Dinosaurs, Vampires, LightSabers. These are all added over the course of the game, but there still needs to be that main concept to build from.
\n
\nSo give me some setting ideas!
