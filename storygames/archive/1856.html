    I'm pretty fond of any chocolate from Germany or Belgium I get my hands on. Good dark chocolate is an entheogenic experience.

    Mark W, the co-ops usually have an assortment of domestic premium organic chocolates, including chilied.

    The international chocolate biz is kinda strange, corporate-wise. For instance, the Cadbury Dairy Milk and Nestle's Kit Kat bars sold in the US are actually manufactured by Hershey . Nestle and Cadbury-Schweppes were looking at a joint bid on Hershey, but that languished over antitrust concerns. It's bewildering.

    I have observed that Kit Kat bars purchased in Canada are noticeably better tasting than their US counterparts. I had developed a theory that this was a conspiracy to dump crap chocolate on palette-impaired US consumers, just like cigarette companies dump cheaper tobacco in developing nations. Then someone pointed out to me it probably had to do with longer time spend sitting in the supply channel. Stupid Occam's Razor takes all the fun out.
