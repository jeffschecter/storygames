[Six Bullets for Vengeance] Keith's art is rocking my game ... how can I make my game rock the art?

Andrew Kenrick
December 2006 edited December 2006 in Story Games
Keith has started to post the pics he's doing for my game of revenge (told backwards), Six Bullets for Vengeance, and they rock to say the least.

In fact, they rock so much that I want to make more of the pictures. I want to incorporate the art right into the fabric of the game and the text, to make the story told through the pictures integral to the game itself. Now, the way I'm currently thinking of doing this is to simply make the characters from the art the characters used in the examples throughout the book, and in the extensive example of play i plan on including.

But is there more that I can do? I don't want to squander these fantastic pictures by just having them loosely related to the text. Thoughts?

    Two thoughts:

    * I think you'd best serve the illustrations by basically ignoring them in the text. The reason the images are cool â€” to me â€” is because they inspire my thinking and creativity with their vagueness and use of very general, iconic images. The more you attach specifics to them, the less cool they will be for that purpose.

    * Art captaions in games are often lame because they add no additional information. If the combination of an image and the caption provokes the response, "No shit," the caption is worthless. However, if a caption were written to provide additional information that's not in the picture (perhaps the caption is about what Harduck accomplished in this battle, or later in his life, or whatever), or provokes additional thought, then I say it's way worthwhile.

    So, I don't know, maybe if you wrote a haiku to accompany each illustration, or included a list of free-associated setting- or theme-related words. Hell if I know. Something (anything) that makes the reader a participant in creativity by looking at the images. If it were me, I'd just leave them alone.

    With Spirit of the Century, after I did the layout I requested some additional art pieces that fit what was going on in the text at specific points where I'd felt we'd gone on long enough without some sort of art element.

    Prior to that, some of the art I'd had done in advance of the layout, and I paired it up with text that fit it during the layout process.

    I dunno man ... it works in both directions, and you just 'do it'. I think a blend of both of these approaches worked pretty well for SOTC; I didn't know I needed a "walls are closing in" piece until I saw that there was an available space near the Certain Doom section, nor did I know I wanted an illustration of Jet Black strapping himself to an out-of-control locomotive until I saw an opportunity to place art with an example about exactly that. But I already had the "guerilla gorillas" image when it came time to select an art element to put next to the write-up on Minions. So it fit.

    Here's something I'm doing (in my head, at this point) for Boulevard:
    Putting in evocative imagery and quotes from books and movies... and then adding how I'd incorporate them into a game mechanically.

    So there's a quote from Fight Club, saying "the things you own, end up owning you." Below that, in the same little box, there's
    "Placelessness: Consumerism
    Momentum: Letting Go".

    There is an image of a rally in support of the Anti-Poverty Committee in Vancouver. I want to include that image, and below put:
    "Placelessness: East Hastings, Homelessness
    Momentum: Rioting"

    There is a piece of art of a kid in a hoodie walking down an abandoned street:
    "Place: Concrete
    Momentum: Coccaine"

    I don't want to muddy the awesomeness of the images with captions, because, well, I'm not a prose-smith. I'd like to fancy myself a poignant writer, but we've seen examples of that in RPG texts before.

    So, yeah. I show them something awesome, and then I give them a tie-in. A quick little, "incorporate this image into your game by incorporating THESE group elements".

    Fate did something similar with all their images - they showed pictures of characters, and listed the Aspects below. It was an illustration, but also a character idea.

    Is there a similar thing you could do with 6 Bullets images?
