\n Alright, so I had an awesome time last February at OrcCon - great people, great games, and a relaxed atmosphere to enjoy them in. You SoCal folks have got it going on.

So I'm finalizing my plans to fly in from Phoenix again this year. The events schedule is looking pretty sparse at this point, though. I've got a quick-play heist game (House of Cards) I'm working on that I'll be running several playtests of. I'm also interested in running another game or two, particularly Jason Morningstar's Fiasco and maybe 3:16 if no one else is manning up.

So I ask you - what are you going to be running at OrcCon this year? What do you really want to play?

    \n BarCon from my perspective \u2014 after the vendor hall closes (at, like 5 or 6), I grab some Subway for dinner or head directly up to the Bar at the top of the Radisson, cleverly named Top of the Radisson. There's usually a couple people there, and more start showing up. Paul holds court and drinks expensive scotch. People catch up on what they did that day, show off games they've recently got, and so on. Around 8 or so (?), there is about twenty people, and groups start splintering off to play some games. Around elevenish, those games finish up \u2014 some people head for bed, some people start up a second round of games, some people settle in to drink a bit and chat until the bar closes.

    BarCon's big day is Saturday, but I would not be surprised to find Sunday and especially Friday getting some big participation.
