This weekend, I posted Utara on my game blog. It's half dice game, half strategy game and uses compass directions to make any Earth surface a game board.

In the comments, Adam Minnie said it would make a good tavern game for D&D heroes to play between adventures. The slight ambiguities of play could be an opportunity for a bar fight to break out and the heroes to intervene.

Hope you dig it.

    \n D&D heroes playing the game is an entertaining idea. Do giants and ogres use oversized dice, while kobolds and halflings use smaller dice? Dothese regional differences lead to disagreements?


    In many folk games, the "play twice and reverse roles" solution is used to cover over inequities in the game's setup. (You see remnants of this when two sports teams trade sides of the field at halftime.) So the first mover advantage lends it an air of authenticity.

    If you really wanted to remove the first mover advantage, then you could make "the moon is dark" the default assumption, and remove a hit moon, at some sort of penalty. So early goers get more targets but more obstacles to avoid, while later goers have fewer impediments.

    \n So let's say I were to run a Kickstarter for Utara at some point in the future. I'd need one or two pledge levels below $25. Unfortunately, the costs of production are such that I couldn't afford to offer more than one die for every $5 pledge. So, I'm tinkering with some ideas for games that use only one or two Utara dice along with a bundle of whatever other dice you have in your collection. For example:

    Roll a bunch of dice of any size or shape on the playing field.

    Each player then rolls one Utara die into the field.

    And then... I dunno. Do the Utara dice travel in a straight line, collecting dice as they move along? Do the numbers on those dice affect turn order? Lots to consider. Hm!

    \n Materials:

    * N d6, preferably of a single color.
    * 1 d6 of a different, recognizable color or size
    * 1 Utara die

    Roll the bunch of d6 on the table.

    Each player in turn rolls 1 utara die and 1 d6. The number rolled on the d6 is the die face/number that can be moved this turn. The Utara die tells you in which direction. Choose one dice that matches the number rolled and move it in the direction rolled. Collect dice.

    Now, this can obviously be complicated further. For example, roll the Utara die each time you collect a die from the table and follow the new direction from there.

    Just a thought
