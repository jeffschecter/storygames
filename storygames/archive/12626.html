    \n That was my reaction too. I don't understand how it works, and based on the rules I don't think it should work. But it does, beautifully. I'd also add the word "internal" to your list of adjectives -- it's a game that really encourages introspection and interior journeys.

    As far as Spotify goes, I think it would be a tradeoff.

    It would reduce the equipment required to a computer with decent speakers and an Internet connection. And in this day and age even CDs seem quaint artifacts of a bygone age.

    But at the same time you'd be limiting the music to Spotify's catalog. Eight million songs sounds like a lot, until they don't have the *perfect* song that you want to put in the playlist, or you find that the group you want a song from hasn't signed with them yet. And things like imports, EPs, and bootlegs are unlikely to ever be available. You'd also have to either have every participant have a Spotify account, or have one person play coordinator and make up the playlists.

    It also depends on your group's tastes in music - I'm reacting to this as a fan of indie music, which is fairly poorly served by the mainstream online music sites. (Of course, I understand why this happens, and if I were an iTunes decisionmaker I'd probably decide the same thing.)
