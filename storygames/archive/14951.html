\nMe play barbarian. Hit things, fight stereotypes. Sure, major in "things that go smash" but minor in women's studies.
\n
\nI received this email from one of my oldest friends the other day; she is going to be playing DW along with me and several others in a campaign beginning tomorrow. My roommate is going to GM.
\n
\nAnyway, we're probably going to just go with the Fighter for her, but I was thinking about writing up a Barbarian class just for the hell of it. Here\u2019s an initial stab (or rather, swing) at it:
\n
\nNames:
\nHuman: Throndar, Koga, Veleg, Pallavo, Prelkar, Dugdammay, Sandaksatru, Teushpaa, Velinu
\n
\nHalf-Orc: Zivak, Avkosh, Toshmar, G\xfcttar, Sangrock, Prok, Thur, Grivmal
\n
\nLook:
\nEyes: Suspicious Eyes, Sullen Eyes, or Smoldering Eyes
\nGarb: Worn Leathers, Revealing Leathers, or Smelly Hides
\nTattoos: Elaborate Tattoos, Frightening Tattoos, or Mystical Tattoos
\nThews: Mighty Thews, Iron Thews, or Lean Thews
\n
\nStats: The barbarian\u2019s damage die, is, of course, the d12. The base HP is 8.
\n
\nChoose a racial move:
\n
\nHuman: +1 to Defy Danger or Saving Throw against magical effects
\nHalf-Orc: +1 to all rolls to avoid bondage/imprisonment
\n
\nYou start with these moves:
\nBerserker: When you drive yourself into a frenzy before battle, roll +Con.
\nOn a 10+, you take +1 ongoing to Hack-and-Slash and damage rolls, -1 ongoing to Defend, and can drop out of berserk state at will (though you cannot reenter it again during the same fight).
\nOn a 7-9, as above, but you must make a Saving Throw to exit the berserk state (the monster level here being your own level, you brute).
\nOn a 6-: You gain none of the benefits of a 7+, and (1) You take a -1 to all rolls to Help/Interfere with the party, and they take a -1 as well to Help or Interfere with you while you remain berserk, and (2) you must make a Saving Throw to exit the berserk state, as above.
\n
\nTotemic Guide: Choose one animal to be your spirit guide. When you perform the ritual to consult your spirit guide, roll +Wis and consult the appropriate chart.
\nHawk: 10+: Hold 2; at any time either you or the GM may spend your hold to have your character perceive a lurking or otherwise hidden threat. 7-9, Hold 1.
\nBear: 10+: Gain 2+(level times 2) HP for the next fight. 7-9, gain 1+level HP for the next fight.
\nWolf: 10+: Hold 2; at any time you may spend your hold to have your character perform an amazing feat of athleticism involving jumping, climbing, swimming, or running. 7-9, Hold 1.
\n
\nOn a 6-, you have angered your spirit guide in some way; the GM will tell you what you must do to repair the relationship.
\n
\nAt Home in the Wild: You never need to consume rations when traveling through an unpopulated area, so long as it is not an absolute desert. You take -1 to all Parley rolls to barter or haggle in towns and cities, however.
\n
\nAlignment:
\nChaotic Good: Mark XP when you defy an unjust authority figure.
\nChaotic Neutral: Mark XP when you refuse to be bound by society\u2019s laws.
\nChaotic Evil: Mark XP when you destroy a work of civilized art or craft.
\n
\nGear: You have gifts from your rites of adulthood worth 15 gold plus any one Messy weapon of your choice. (The Messy tags are missing from the book, but work it out with your group.) 7+Str.
\n
\nBonds:
\nMy spirit guide distrusts __________.
\n__________ is over-reliant on the comforts and habits of civilization.
\n__________ has been chosen by the gods to assist me in some great task.
\nI worry about the ability of __________ to act decisively and without hesitation in moments of danger.
\n
\nAdvanced Moves:
\nBlood-drinker: Regain a hit point whenever you slay a foe.
\n
\nLore of the Tribe: +1 to Spout Lore about matters outside the ken of civilized folk.
\n
\nDesperate Might: +1 to all damage rolls when at half HP or below.
\n
\nCaged Ferocity: +1 to armor when surrounded and outnumbered.
\n
\nCleave: When you fell a foe using a melee weapon, if there are any other foes within your weapon\u2019s range, you may deal 1d4 damage to one of them.
\n
\nYour Enemies Driven Before You: When you kill a foe in a particularly gruesome or awe-inspiring manner, take +1 forward to Parley to convince your remaining enemies to surrender or flee.
\n
\nWheel of Pain: When you take n damage from a foe\u2019s attack, you deal +n/2 (round up) damage forward against that foe.
\n
\nThe Bigger they Are: When you deal damage to a foe that is ancient and/or huge, do +1d4 damage.
\n
\n
\nBarbarian keys:
\n\u201cWhat is good in life?\u201d: Convince a civilization-dweller to give up their city-bound ways and adopt barbarian culture (2 XP).
\nThe Riddle of Steel: Defeat a worthy foe armed with superior equipment and/or trained in a formal martial art.
\nTroubled Brow: Make a decision about governance, laws, or justice.
\nHearing the Lamentations: A vanquished enemy cringes, pleads for mercy, or otherwise shows utter submission.
\nScaling the Tower: Enter into an area that is blocked off, locked, warded, or heavily guarded.
\nDoom-breaker: Sunder a harmful magic item or sustained spell.
\nGigantic Mirth, Gigantic Melancholy: You make a significant decision resulting from a sudden mood-swing.
\n
\nHalf-Orc Keys:
\nOne of the Good Ones: An Elven, Human, or Dwarven authority figure trusts you with an important mission that you complete successfully.
\nLeader of the Pack: Teach a group of underdogs/misfits to successfully defend themselves against bullies/oppression.
\nIconoclast: Bring down a corrupt religious institution.
\n
\nHuman Keys:
\nDiplomat: Turn a potential/likely (non-human) foe into a friend
\nColonist: Bring other humans to an area you and the party have explored to set up a town
\nStrider: Form a multi-racial coalition in order to hold back a Dark Threat
\n

    \n Most of this playbook is +1 this and +1 that and a -1 here and there. And that is booooooring. But the Totemic Guide move is neat, and the keys are super-hot! You should make the whole playbook that exciting. Let's look at some possibilities:

    Human racial move: Yeah, a simple +1 is meh. How about once per session, the human barbarian can shrug off or defy a magical effect completely. Fireball? Barbarian leaps over it! Charm Person? Crom gives me strength, wizard! Prepare to die!

    Half-orc racial move: So, why not just give the half-orc an actual escape move? When you break your bonds or imprisonment, roll+str. On a 10+, you snap your bonds, break bars, or smash through walls! On a 7-9, you do it, but take a consequence (look at the Faceless break things move for a list of good consequences, maybe 1d6 damage would be appropriate for DW).

    Berserker:This one definitely needs more than just roll modifiers.

    Marshall's version is okay, and I put a version of that move in World of Algol:
    Blood Rage: Whenever you take harm, roll+harm taken. On a 10+, take +1ongoing until you heal any harm. On a 7-9, take +1forward. On a miss, take 1-contamination or -1ongoing until you heal any harm, your choice.

    I mean, I like it (for AW), but for DW, I don't think it's enough. I'd rather see something like this:

    Berserk: You may go berserk anytime during a battle. While you are berserk, you do double damage, and on any 10+ roll you may also shove, push, or otherwise maneuver your foes, but you may not Volley, Defend, or Parlay, and you may only Discern Realities in search of new foes. When the battle is over, roll+wis to stop berserking. On a 10+, you're cool, do what you want. On a 7-9, you must spend time recovering, during which you cannot use any of your special moves.

    This makes it a good move for the barbarian, who should be able to make those 10+ rolls on hack and slash, but makes it a bad choice for another character class to choose, because they are weaker in battle or more defense-minded (...you can get moves from another playbook in DW right?). So, niche protection built in.

    \n Here's what I had that I liked. I cleaned it up after some thought. Let me know how we can refine it even more!

    Barbarian

    HP: 8. 8+CON to start.
    Damage: d8

    Rage: when you fly into a rage, roll+STR. On a 10+, hold 3. On a 7-9, hold 1. Spend your hold 1-for-1 for the following effects:

    + Increase your damage die to a d12 for your next attack.

    + Ignore all damage from one source (as it happens).

    + Gain +1 piercing as long as you are in a rage.

    + Intimidate one foe of your level or lower with a mighty war cry, causing it to deal half damage on its next attack and for as long as you are raging.

    When you run out of hold, are incapacitated, or otherwise leave active combat, your rage ends.

    On a miss, the DM holds 2 over you, and may spend it to cause you to attack an ally. You attack the nearest ally. If it is unclear who is the nearest ally, you attack the ally you have the lowest Bond with.

    \n I've collated a bunch of the ideas from this thread with a couple of tweaks of my own and I'll try it out at Nerdly Beach Party. The tweaks are as follows (and assume base damage d12 and base hp 8):\n
    Berserk: You may go berserk any time during a battle. While you are berserk, add +d6 damage, and on any 10+ roll you may also shove, push, or otherwise manoeuvre your foes, but you may not Volley, Defend, or Parley, and you may only Discern Realities in search of new foes. When the battle is over, roll+wis to stop berserking. On a 10+, you're cool, do what you want. On a 7-9, you must spend time recovering, during which you cannot use any of your special moves.
    \nAs you can see, the only change I made here is +d6 damage (so it's a little better than the Fighter's Merciless move).
    I like the idea that you don't know how the roll will go until after the berserking ends. I'm not a fan of forcing PCs to attack other PCs in beserk rages. It kinda feels like it was a way for GMs to drift games (like D&D) in order to introduce intra-party tension in games that didn't have any other way of doing that. YMMV.
    \n
    Totemic Guide: Choose one animal to be your spirit guide. When you perform the ritual to consult your spirit guide, roll +Wis. On a 10+, hold 2. On a 7-9, hold 1. On a 6 or less, you have angered your spirit guide in some way; the GM will tell you what you must do to repair the relationship.
    Hold can be spent 1 for 1 for the following effects:
    Hawk:
    * Perceive a lurking or otherwise hidden threat.
    * Take +1 forward to discern realites.
    * Take +1 forward to loot.
    Bear:
    * Take +1 forward to a saving throw.
    * Subtract your level in damage from a strike.
    * Take +1 forward to carouse.
    Wolf:
    * Ignore one level of group difference for a combat
    * Take +1 forward to spout lore.
    * Perform an amazing feat of athleticism involving jumping, climbing, swimming, or running.












    \nI think it's more elegant to have one standard mechanic for the move, then options with how you use the results of that mechanic to differentiate sub-types.
