I mis-heard Juddson when we were playtesting Cage of Reason - and what I misheard was fantastic, and really ought to be a kick-ass game...

ROBOTTOIR!

You know, an abattoir for robots.

I actually started working on this, but stopped when it started to sound a little like that wretched movie AI. PM me if you want to read my inital notes, but really, you're better off running with the name alone if it grabs you. Run, robot enthusiast, run!

    An abbatoir is a place where carcasses are systematically reduced to meat.

    Clearly, a robottoir is one where robots do the chopping-up.

    Every player has two characters; a robot (who wants to chop people up) and a human (who wants to escape without being chopped up). Physically, the robots are far more powerful than the humans.

    Oh... and all the humans deserve to be there... for one reason or another.

    A robottoir is a place where old robots are reduced to spare components to feed the cheap-robots building companies. But it is operated by humans. Humans who suffer a lot talking with them during their last hours, while they are disemsambled carefully, part after part, until they lose their consciussness, and they are no more a robot. During the process the robots remember their past and sometimes got confused about the real stories they lived, places they visited and the order of their life events.

    Each session is a robot story built collaboratively by the operators of the robottoir using the pieces of information, feelings and images the robot gives them while dying.
