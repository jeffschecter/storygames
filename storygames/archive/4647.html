\n Meg Baker and Joshua A.C. Newman were on a local access tv show called Local Bias. They talked about publishing and playing role playing games, JiffyCon, their games, other people's games, etc. The show is really geared towards the general unwashed masses of Franklin County Massachusetts, so some of the things they cover are very basic like "what is a role playing game?" In the podcast age, it's funny to actually watch people get interviewed.

Check it out on YouTube.
Yeah, that's me, hosting the show.

    \n I find it easier to interview people I don't know. I often ask guests to give me an brief outline of what they do, what they want to talk about. That way I ask them about things I don't know and what I think viewers might want to know, too. Interviewing Meg and Joshua about something I know about was difficult. I had to pretend I didn't know anything about rpg's, or about them. I avoided talking about Steal Away Jordan so that I could still sound objective while obviously talking about something I really enjoy. Plus I would have turned beet red.\n
    Posted By: fnord3125Btw, Julia, since I don't live in your area, I have no idea about it... how many people actually watch this show? Have you gotten any feedback/interest on the topic now from viewers?
    \nI don't know how many people watch the show, but we've gotten a fair amount of positive feedback in general about the show. The show started in June and we have a waiting list of about 20 people who want to be on the show, most of whom contacted us about being on. People have commented that they like learning about what cool things their neighbors do in our town. People have stopped me on the street after recognizing me from the show. That said, I'm a bit of a townie. I live around the corner from the main drag, I used to work on Main St., and I run a booth at the farmer's market.

    The producer/co-host also produced a very popular music show called Homegrown, and I think we get a fair number of viewers who are loyal to him. He's an all-around awesome guy who loves Greenfield, and is committed to local issues: politics, commerce, pride. He's a regular at the farmer's market, which is where I got "discovred". Drew was filming a promo spot for GCTV and asked random people at the farmer's market if they could name the US Supreme Court justices. Apparently I was the only person he asked who could name all of them.* He thought I was photogenic and smart.

    Just to give you an idea of the demographics of the area, our town's about 17,000 people, we are the largest city in the county, and the county seat of the farm belt county of Massachusetts. We're now trying to get underwriting funds so we can put together a promotional package to get the show on other public access channels in our area.

    *Alito, Souter, Ginsburg, Kennedy, Thomas, Scalia, Roberts (the chief), Breyer, Stevens.
