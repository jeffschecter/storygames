\n So, reading the thread of Frank Franzetta's death, I see lots of people talking about specific art pieces that inspired them as a child. This got me thinking. There is ONE particular piece of art that has probably informed all of my gaming and I honestly have no idea who the artist is or even how to locate the image. Maybe you guys can help me.

So here's the deal.

The AD&D 2ed Monster Manual that I owned was not the book version but the large three ring binder version. You could buy "packs" of monsters and insert them right into the folder. There were these card board insert pages with tabs that you could put in to separate the sections.

The image I'm thinking of was full color on one of those card-board tabbed pages. It was a picture of a Lich, possibly with several OTHER liches behind him. It looked like a whole council of liches.

Does anyone have any clue what picture I'm talking about and who the artist was?

Thanks.

Jesse

P.S. Once I remembered that it wasn't called Monster Manual but Monster Compendium I was able to find the image.



Still don't know who the artist was/is.

    By the way, if anyone can give me a scan of another piece that I totally loved, I'd appreciate it. It was line art by Jim Holloway from the inner cover of one of the Desert of Desolation AD&D modules (I4, I think). It's a "moment in time" sort of drawing, where the adventurers are basking in the attention of a harem, but then obviously one of them said something stupid, so the harem scatters, leaving the group with surprised looks on their faces (one has a bundle of grapes hanging out of his mouth).

    I contacted Jim about buying the original a while back, but it's apparently been lost.
