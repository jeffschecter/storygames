    \n I wrote a system a while back for being a low-level gang member. There's no rising through the ranks; rather, it focused on the tension between your gang life and any ties you had outside the gang. Also, in order to stay in good standing with your gang (such that they have your back vs rival gangs), you'll probably need to do some messed up stuff.

    \n Misspent Youth - sets up and reinforces an "us versus them" mindset.
    Characters can fall in to choke points where they have to choose between failing themselves (and by extension, the group) or "selling out" and acting like the enemy.

    This is a one-way function. Characters cannot regain that bond with the group.
    The campaign ends when the first character has "sold out" 5 times, signifying their leaving, and the denouement of the story arc.

    ALSO

    Look at "Motobushido" which would work perfectly for Kaneda's gang (or even his former gang). It's designed and built to handle and reinforces people's roles within the gang as a collective, and power struggles as well.

    Crime Network sounds intriguing, but the RPGnet review doesn't say much about reward mechanics (just skills). Brendan, are there any system bits about honor, respectability, changing incentives at different ranks, allegiances and enmities? Or is that mostly just handled by everyone's position in the fiction? If the latter, is there any structure or formal system for setting things up initially so that tension and betrayals will naturally fall out?     \nThis one flew under the radar so there are only one or two reviews online. It is more on the traditional side of gaming. But it has a formal Respectability system which is your standing in the family. A respectability check (rolled secretly) determines if the bosses try to whack you when you do anything against the family or break the code in an any way. There are no rules for escalating tension (like I said it is on the traditional side) but every character has a Personal Goal and family goal each adventure. Both are secret and the family goal is given by the GM based on the needs of the family (and it can include killing a fellow player character). This really adds to the sense of paranoia. Think Donnie Brasco and Goodfellas meet The Sopranos. There is also a racket sub system. We do handle promotion and rank but that is mostly as guidelines. The guide to organized crime goes into that in more depth (it also offers a more detailed optional racket sub system).

    \n Mobsters ( http://www.burgergames.com/G1.PDF ) by Ville Vuorela is a traditional roleplaying game for managing and expanding a mafia family in some specific city of USA during the prohibition. It is free.
    It is mostly about running the family and its economics and mafia wars, not about being a random thug.
