    \n Speaking of Pathfinder / D&D rules for Diplomacy, a few people have told me they hate them precisely because they are more effective than combat! They do a better job at resolving conflict than combat does. Combat leads to more combat, which is usually what the DM's adventure is centered around. Nixing combat means avoiding the adventure's plot. None of this is bad, but often games heroes may fictionally seem to be interested in resolving conflict but in actually playing the conflict is why the game is fun so options that negate conflict also negate that specific fun.
