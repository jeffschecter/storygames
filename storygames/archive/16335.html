\n So we've played this a few times now. Do the setup from In a Wicked Age but then do the play of Archipelago. In a way, it's like rolling your own Love in the Time of Seid - the best interest creation from IaWA gets you all pointing at each other in interesting ways, and then the play is like Seid. I really love how Matthijs's Archipelago cards help take the story into new places, and I really love how Wicked Age usually creates an untenable situation where all the players know what they want their character to be doing - and has its face-stabby friend-poisoning gods-walk-the-earth ancient-sumerian-or-some-shit setting.

It's not as tight as Seid - so if you haven't played Seid, play that first. But if you've already played Seid once or twice, this is for you.

And, hey you game designer types - where would you take this from here? Or is it pretty much done as is? Part of me thinks, 'It needs more.' Another part thinks, 'Less is more.'

    \n We didn't use the fate deck or element ownership in any of our games - I think they'd add something, but they're not necessary. In the doc I have some ideas for what I *would* do, but don't know if I'll ever get around to trying it. If you use them I'd like to hear how it goes.

    (And don't shuffle the resolution card back in. I think that's an explicit rule in Seid. I can go off on why I think that's a good rule:
    - getting the same card twice sounds a little boring
    - if you shuffle it back in, you might as well roll a die and look up the result on a table
    - the way people are usually wired, cards feels more 'random' than a truly random die roll
    - it helps to create a dramatic curve; if you win a lot in the beginning you'll lose a lot in the end and vice-versa)

    \n So we played some more last weekend. We had a real Hamlet ending where all but one of the player characters died - that's really hard to do with IaWA, where you typically need 2-3 whole conflicts to whittle a single character down to 0, but with Archipelago it's as simple as "I strike you down!" "That might not be so easy!" "Yes, and!" So I'm really liking that.

    We tried doing two chapters - because that's a really good thing to do with Wicked Age. With Wicked Archipelago, it felt more like telling two separate stories that were only barely connected. Since Wicked Archipelago doesn't have the owe list and there's no way to spam your particular strength or your best stat, there's a lot less need for a second chapter to make the owe list kick in.

    The game goes a bit too quick for me, though - you can be done in two hours. It would be nice if there was some kind of tilt mechanism like Fiasco has, to complicate things in the middle. Maybe require the drawing of a Fate card after you've gone around for one round, or draw another Oracle, or something. Any ideas?
