A-Plot / B-Plot Hack for Primetime Adventures

\n
\n
\n
\n\nJosh Roby \n\n\n
\n
\n\nFebruary 2008 \n edited February 2008 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n So this requires at least 4 players. You do your PTA pitch and everything as normal, except everybody makes protagonists. There is no dedicated producer.

In any given episode, look at everybody's screen presences. The lowest screen presence becomes the A-Plot Producer; the second-lowest screen presence becomes the B-Plot Producer (break ties however you like; this is a hack, not game design!). The A-Plot Producer has a budget of Total Screen Presence x 2 + 2. The B-Plot Producer has a budget of Total Screen Presence + 1.

Whenever a scene is framed, the scene framer stipulates if it is an A-Plot scene or a B-Plot scene. If you are one of the Producers, you play the adversity in the scenes of your plotline. Your protagonist may appear in those scenes, but cannot play cards (and you'll probably be 1 screen presence, and therefore in a support role, anyway). In scenes of the other plotline, you play your protagonist as normal.

The idea here is to spread the play/produce love around, increase the cast size by one, and mix up the plot lines a bit to create more varied, textured episodes. Or at least, that's the idea.

    \n Interesting thought. Since Producers are playing antagonists and supporting cast anyway, actually giving them one of the ensemble wouldn't be a huge amount of stretch. I can see a few potential issues:

    In a very small group, this creates a situation with too many cooks for a very small pot.

    In groups that prefer a strong singular world creator, this clearly wouldn't work.

    B plots are actually FOR supporting characters, so someone with a higher Screen Presence should be running them while the supporting-character players play their supporting characters. Otherwise what is the supporting character actually in the ensemble for?

    It's quite likely that your B plot will only have enough Budget for one or at most two conflicts. Normally a B plot is just handled between the Producer and the supporting character and the fan mail can keep that conflict rolling.

    Interesting start, though.

    \n Having thought about dividing up the Producer's budget on my drive to work, and considering that in our game we use poker chips to represent the budget, the elegant solution is not to calculate via the inelegant, divide-by-a-repeating-decimal formula I gave above, but to do this:

    Calculate budget the regular way: 2x(SP)+3
    Cut out that many budget tokens.
    Make three even stacks. Two will belong to the A-Plot Producer, one will belong to the B-Plot Producer.
    You will have either 0, 1, or 2 tokens left over.
    0 -- Yay, the budget is divisible by three!
    or 1 -- Give the last token to the B-Plot Producer.
    or 2 -- Give one token to each Producer.
    Game On!
