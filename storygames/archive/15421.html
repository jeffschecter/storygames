\n As I read the rules to Dungeon World, I was struck by how easily it seems like it could be hacked to represent all those kids adventure books/movies that we all know and love.
\n
\nBut then I thought - someone must have done an RPG representing that sort of fiction already. So - post up the games where you can play Jupiter Jones, Stanley Yelnats, or Ron Weasley. I'm looking for something generic, and adventure and physical conflict are the main themes, not interpersonal strife as per Smallville (so I've heard).
\n
\nAnd I'm mostly focused on a game that takes place in the tween area, between say 10 and 13, although I suppose a long campaign could take the kids into and through high school if desired.

    \n Grimm RPG by Fantasy Flight games. Awesome production value, simple rules system, totally original archetypes (classes). Love this book.
    The setting is great because the land is basically all the fairy tales of Grimm, strewn out into a kind of Alice's Wonderland with Jack's felled beanstalk hollowed out and ferried by gnomes allowing safe passage from one end to the other. Travel outside the stalk and beware the monstrous terrors waiting to gobble up your feeble yet imaginative pre-teen character. Power comes in quick wits and teamwork.

    I imagine a fun way to play it would be everyone roll up their character as their childhood self, bring a stand-up framed childhood photo to place in front of you at the table, and go on that youthful adventure that you always wanted but never had. Take your own Goonies clan to a different but familiar fantasy world.

    Grimm RPG at Fantasy Flight Games

    \nGrimm did come to mind.
    \n
    \nI think there are three huge bodies of work about kids.
    \n
    \nOne where kids do have "powers", a la Harry Potter, Lightning Thief, Maximum Ride, SpyKids (their James Bond-like resources are bordering on powers). All of the above are kid with powers in a mundane world. I see Smallville as one example, or any Supers game featuring the X-Men (or the New Mutants or Power Pack). I would be interested in hearing about games that have settings/rules that encompass this. Boarsdraft is one game explicitly about a Harry Potter universe, was wondering if there were others like - kids with powers in a mundane world?
    \n
    \nOne where kids are ordinary, but are transported to a Place that is fantastical, and where they either gain powers, or stay "ordinary"; The entire Oz series (L Frank Buam's at least), Narnia, Zathura are a few examples. I would say Grimm is a game that addresses this realm.
    \n
    \nAnd then the body of work where the kids are just "ordinary" and the setting is also "ordinary", yet through their inventiveness, pluck, and charm they prevail in their adventures: Goonies, Stand By Me, Hardy Boys, Jupiter Jones, Tom Sawyer, Huck Finn, on and on and on. This seems like a rich trove of inspiration, and there are the pre-defined roles (as in Grimm - Nerd, Outcast, Jock, Bully). But I don't think there are any games out there addressing this genre, are there?
    \n
    \nI could see hacking Apocalypse World to fit this kind of setting. Or maybe Grimm. My sense is AW is "looser", and thus might be easier to hack?
    \n
    \nThanks,
    \nChris
