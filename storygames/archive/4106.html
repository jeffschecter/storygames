    \n Forge Midwest occurs every spring in Chicago. I / we plan on doing it again next April or so.

    That said, BRING ON GO PLAY MIDWEST. I do not see this as in any way a conflict. I'm all for it, and want to attend.

    But, that requires someone to step up and organize it. Just do it. Don't poll people. Just pick a place, say you're doing it, and voila!. Go Play happens. And, people will clearly show up.

    If you're going to do that, I highly recommend asking the Go Play NW gang how they fared and what worked and didn't. Finding a venue that's not crazy expensive would rock, too. Maybe a university or a public hall, for example. But, hotels can work.

    Oh, and if you ARE going to pay for a venue, make damn sure you have a reliable estimate of attendees because otherwise you'll get stuck holding the bag on the hotel bill, for example. Not Cool.

    Posted By: commondialogOkay...so let's do KC. I can't tell a serviceman no. However, I am not ruling out camping ravenx99. Is there one closer to KC?
    \nDepends on how "rustic" you want to be... and I bet the further out you get, the less expensive it is. Timber Lakes is just 50 miles from Olathe, right down I-35... but that puts it about 85 miles from MCI airport and a drive through the middle of the greater KC area.

    There seem to be plenty of camp and retreat centers in and around the KC area... looks like you can find some actually in fully developed areas. (3 Trails Salvation Army kids camp went up for sale in 2006 because what used to be a rural area now has an apartment complex and Bass Pro Shop right next door.)

    http://www.talloaks.org/

    42 miles from MCI, not through downtown KC, about 15 miles from KC and Lawrence. (Near Linwood, KS.) Dormitory and hotel-like housing, plus areas for tents. Welcomes groups from 10 to 174. They can even provide food services. (While I think managing the food thing would be fun, it seemed to be a major sticking point for the last Camp Nerdly... mainly in the even division of labor and the fact that it really cut into gaming time because it takes a long time to prepare a meal for 60+ people, clean up, etc.)

    Hey, they have prices online! So darn many of these places don't. Ouch... their rates are different based on whether you're for-profit, not-for-profit or a family reunion. Do you think we could get away with telling them we're a family reunion?

    Okay, triple occupancy dorm rooms are $65 a day per person, which includes three meals a day and "meeting space". (Assuming two over-night stays. It's slightly more expensive with just one overnight.) And that's the hotel-style dorm rooms. Cottages are cheaper ($50 a day), and are one or two wings full of bunk-beds (18 total beds per cottage in most cases). Prices are adjusted for exact number of meals, etc... so coming in on a Friday afternoon and having just one meal on Friday, and then leaving after lunch on Sunday (two meals) would cut those prices. We'd have to get a quote.

    That's a lot more expensive than the last Camp Nerdly by quite a bit... CN1 was just $50 a person for everything, including spending a lot of money on custom swag. But it's also pushing all the food prep issues off on someone else. No bringing food or utensils, no spending our time cooking and so on. Not nearly as rustic, but it'd give us more time to game and less fussing with primitive facilities. We'd have to see how they deal with vegan or other dietary needs.

    Obviously, we'd want to shop around a bit, but that gives you an idea of what it could cost... a couple guys could share a hotel room for less than $100 a day, but that $100 a day includes meals and at least some of the meeting space cost. Overall, I expect it would be cheaper than the traditional route of hotel rooms and meeting space.
