\n I want a game centered around the Boy and His Dog genre. It doesn't have to actually be a dog (anybody seen Fido?), it just has to fill that certain role that a dog can have in a young boy's life.

Now, since this concept revolves around someone with a close relationship to a pet, my brain tangents went to Sorcerer. Which was a lucky thing, because while you couldn't use Sorcerer for this, I realized that this is the inverse of Sorcerer. The dog (surrogate for the demon) doesn't threaten your humanity; the outside world does, and the dog is what teaches you to hang onto it, and to help others to hang onto it.

I'm seeing one player as the boy, one as the dog, and one as the rest of the world. Friends and family would have Humanity-ish scores, which can be helped out through the actions of the boy and the dog. Or maybe it would be more like the way you turn others into Decent Folks in The Drifter's Escape.

There should also be some sort of mechanic that counts down to the inevitable separation of boy from dog. The dog has been helping him so far, but now it's time for the boy to take full responsibility.

Anybody got any other thoughts on this?

    \n They are pretty much about growing up. And Burr's got the basic arc correct up there. But the core thing is, they're about growing up right, growing up to be a man and not just an adult. The boy in these stories always has a problem he has to overcome; often, he's a bit of a misfit. This is compounded (or even caused) by forces in the boy's world, especially authority figures and bullies. Often the boy's world has something also very wrong with it; a lack of love, a lack of actual family values, etc. The dog is an outsider to this world, and represents the good traits that this world is missing. He also possesses traits that get him into trouble with this world.

    Through his relationship with the dog, the boy learns to overcome his problem and gain the strength to stand up against whatever the world's problem is, and he gains the experience and wisdom he needs to, one day, become a man. Maybe the boy & dog manage to save other people in this manner.

    And, holy crap, I had no idea that there was a movie based on the Vic & Blood stuff. Which, for all its Harlan Ellison craziness, is still a boy & his dog story, if I remember right (it's been a while).

    \n It seems to me that Calvin and Hobbes are ideal archetypes for this (and not so different from the sci-fi Boy and his Dog).

    The dynamic is that the boy and the dog are psychological and philosophical antagonists - with the boy prioritizing an immature view of the world - selfishness, self-gratification, and even pointless violence (toned down to mischief), while the dog, ironically, stands for a mature, almost zen-like interaction with the world - not just because of the dogs innate attributes, but also because of the responsibility it requires of the boy.

    An adventure would consist of some external conflict (perhaps engendered by one or the other - for example, mischief by the boy) that requires some elements of both perspectives in order to be resolved.
