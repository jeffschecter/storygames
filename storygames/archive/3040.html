\n I've been following the Durham 3's play sessions of Contender with lots of interest in the past month. Durham 3 listeners will know that the lads from N. Carolina like to talk a little during their podcasts, take an off-mike break to actually play a game and then come back on-mike to talk some more. Which is an interesting format because of it's immediacy.

But they've finally finished their Contenders game and used episode #36 to do a debrief on the game. It's probably the best gaming podcast episode I've listened to, bar none. It felt like being a fly on the wall in someone's living room after they finished up a game and started to talk about what they liked and didn't like about it. It was very honest and they had some great feedback about the game; including why it was awesome for them and how it could be even better. Which is doubly valuable since this is a GM-less game that needs a lot of player energy to thrive.

I've played the game several times and while I was very jazzed about it, the role-playing fell a bit flat both times. But after listening to this, I see some concrete ways that I could be playing the game differently to make it rock out. So much love to Clinton/Jason/Remi/Shane.
