So, the playtest version of the Perfect game is up.
For those of you who haven't heard of Perfect:

Perfect is a game about the driving forces that slowly turn you into a criminal, in a world where being a criminal maybe isn't a bad thing after all.

The country of Cadence bears similarities to Victorian England, as far as trappings, technology and fashions go. However, when the final monarch, Queen Abigail, died, the country plunged into a harsh and totalitarian dystopia.

Cadence now operates "in Abigail's image"... the Gailist government controls your fashion, your church, your status... right down to what colours of clothing you are legally allowed to wear.

You play someone who simply cannot accept this world. You begin to commit crimes, and eventually this becomes a defining part of who you are. Getting caught, getting re-programming, and getting released are all becoming more and more familiar - and yet, you still keep doing it.



Anyways...
The playtest version is up,
I'd love some feedback.
There are sections that are missing from this version that will appear in the final version (setting scenes, narration and game control, social contract, etc) as well as things I am expanding (background, list of laws...)

Any feedback is appreciated.
