    Quick & dirty translation of the 1984 review:

    The Cuban crisis escalated and bombs were flying. Now it's 1984, a new generation has grown up in the subterranean bunker HAVEN, and radiation levels are low enough to go outside and start again. In HAVEN 8 families have formed a caste system of sorts with different responsibilities (Army, Ecology, Democracy, Religion etc). Players are (adult) children of these eight families (pregenerated characters). The game is very traditional in the sense that there's a GM telling stories, so scenarios must be prepared. There's a cool table for this purpose: Three columns of 12 words each, to use for inspiration (Ex.: 'Subvert', 'Hidden', 'Militia' eller 'Destroy', 'Radioactive', 'Child'). Characters can't develop new abilities etc, but a player who has experienced something special with his character can have influence on the next story. The system is simple and strong: You have a number of chips corresponding to a trait (Mind, Body, Soul). If you want to do something "unopposed" it can either be done or not. "Opposed" is done by betting your chips a few at a time, backed up by role-playing (a bit like Dogs). Simple and functional, both system and setting. There's room for little survival stories and great ideological battles of re-building. You should stick to the 8 families, but there's no reason you can't make your own characters, and as an extension of that introduce a system that makes character development possible. From my criteria this is one of the best things I've read - perhaps because on top of a lot of well-thought-out systems it's refreshingly straightforward and still fresh. *thumbs up*

    Worth playing? Absolutely! Both as a scenario and a campaign.
