    \nGrandma's House
    A morbid game about poverty

    This is a classic barroom game that often starts out funny, then devolves into an exercise in mocking the poor, and then just plain degenerates into dead baby jokes. It's theoretically fun, but I've never enjoyed it and always felt a little unwashed after playing it (the two times I did play).

    To start, someone pretends to be a grandmother, offering her grandchildren a treat. The grandmother is instructed to offering "the best thing in the house," but since she's soooo poor, the treat isn't very good. So then someone pretends to be one of the children, and says, "That's no good. I want something different." Another player then pretends to be the grandmother scrambling about, and procuring an even less appetizing treat. Play repeats in this manner until the dialogue is positively repulsive.

    Something like this:
    Player A (as grandmother): "Ah, children! Sit, sit. I've got something nice for you today... candied slugs. Mmmm..."
    Player B (as child): "That's no good! The slugs are all dried out and smelly. I want something different."
    Player C (as grandmother): "Oh my, oh my. Well.... how about... ah, this! Crows feet in brine. I found this little guy on the road last year!"
    Player D, or A, or whoever really (as child: "That's no good! The foot is covered in icky black spots and looks diseased. I want something different."

    Like I said, it might sound kind of cute at first, but quickly devolves into an exercise in mocking the poor. Do not recommend.

    \n "Damn It's Hard For a Demi-Lich"


    You were once the mightiest wizard in the known realms. Then you died, and became an even mightier lich. Nations cowered before you.

    But time eventually devoured even you. Now you are nothing more than a hollow skull and a pile of dust. Old ladies keep their treats in you.

    Still, you yet have the shadow of power. Perhaps once again your name will be whispered in fear.
