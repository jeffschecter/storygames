Neat Ways to Bring Attention to Your Game

Mark_Causey
August 2006 edited August 2006 in Directed Promotion
This isn't for an RPG, but it is being done by WotC and in case it missed some of y'all's8 radar, I thought I'd enlighten you. Feel free to discuss other methods, real or hypothetical after I'm done.

I don't play CCGs, but I was almost about to start one day. I went to Wizards' Hecatomb website to investigate. I found some startling imagery but little info. I wandered into their forum and not much was their either.

Except for this mysterious post which *almost* seemed like spam. It wasn't. It was encoded and directed me to email the Null and Void Entity. I played along with the new puzles and even got a piece of promotional artwork. My wife and I enjoyed searching the net for answers.

But, it proceeded at a break-neck pace and once I realized that it was intended for a younger generation (a simple R.E.M. puzzle) with more time on their hands (3 puzzles in one week, and over a weekend), I dropped off.

Recently, I'd gotten a piece of spam. With hidden text. And no links for viagra. It was written in such a neat way I kept it, thinking that it was the best work of spam to date: it made me look.

Elisabeta Dragos wrote me again today. I googled again and when I found out that Gen Con attendees were discussing it due to an encrypted card they'd received, I recognized the pattern.

And lo, it was being discused on the Hecatomb board

Now, I realize, I'm spreading this viral piece of Net Entertainment, so I want to honestly say that I'm not being reimbursed by Wizards and I don't give a shit about Hecatomb. If any doubt my veracity, tell Andy to beat me up at our game this week and he'll report the truth. You can trust Andy, can't you?

Anyway, it's a neat way to spread a game virally. Any other examples?
