\n I thought I'd start a thread about the new campaign we're in the process of starting which uses lots of stuff that has seeped into my head from this board, various podcasts, and lots of indie games.\n
Right now we're about to start playing this sci-fi police-noir thing which we're calling The 9th Division. The rules we're using are a hack that takes bits we like from Primetime Adventures, Rob Donahue's Wheel of Fate, Hot War, Minimus, and Amagi Games' Metasystem.

In the Ninth Division game I'll be playing a female android cop who works with a midget psychic gene-mod and an ex-military fixer type whose got an antique cybernetic eye. Thematically were looking to get into Blade Runner / Gattaca / Minority Report territory.
\nWhat I really like is that we explicitly made this a retro-future styled on the 1940s, but they've still got very advanced things like androids, genetic engineering, and computers (although no internet). One of my friend's made the argument that the science fiction that holds up the best is science fiction that draws from the past rather than desperately trying to imagine "The Future." Anyway--that's what sparked the idea.

Here's the link to the setting document I posted over at my blog. I'll talk about the characters in my next post.

    \n Here's a brief description of my character and her aspects (I'll probably also cross post this to the blog):

    Name: Dietrich

    Description: A bald woman in her late twenties/early thirties with an exotic air. She has swirling tatoos around the back of her head and wears dark forties style suits made out of unusual materials.

    Here's her list of aspects as per the questions in Wheel of Fate:

    What is your greatest strength?

    Her physical superiority to human beings.

    What is your greatest flaw?

    Acceptance of her slavery (she's an android).

    What do you want?

    To be seen as the best cop she can be.

    What is keeping you from getting it?

    Perceptions of other officers about her being an android.

    What are you doing about it?

    Being a perfectionist/ overachieving.

    \n The rules we use are GMless, and we basically use the dice as a way of establishing narrative control following a conflict between characters (similar to The Pool). We initially formalized many of the systems in terms of things like scene framing, but we've found that we end up approaching a lot of this stuff intuitively once the game's underway.

    The document of our house rules used to be around seven pages, but I altered it to reflect what we were actually doing at the table and now its down to two pages--although I may continue to tweak it a bit more.

    \n I like the idea of a cop who has been on the beat for so long no one can remember who he is. He blends with the office furniture. His corner of the precinct house is dark and smoky. No one asks about him except for the occasional rookie who gets reassigned soon after. His case files are all kept in stacks of old boxes which form a kind of wall around his desk. His is a world of stale cigarette smoke, coffee stains and an old weathered picture of a nameless dead girl whose killer was never found.
