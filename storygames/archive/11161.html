\n While watching the excellent Avatar I was not only struck by how good Sigourney Weaver looks with a tail, but also how suited the game might be to modding with the Bliss Stage system to make a game.

The whole idea of building personal relationships to fuel conflicts while immersed in one or the other reality is an interesting one which might work with a bit of modding to the system.

I like the idea for example of having 2 sets of relationship values for each side of the fence and active conflicts happening on both by risking those relationships from each side - this would be a departure from the usual bliss stage idea by having social and action scenes in both "Worlds".

I think it would make for very interesting decisions by characters as they burn up their links with each side to achieve what they want and ultimately destroy both, in all likelyhood.

    \n I think that this could work rather well, actually. Just put a heavy emphasis on hot-shotting goals and rework the anchor thing (maybe take it out entirely?).

    I actually kinda liked the corporate guy (as a character, not a person.) The actor did a good job with a pretty bad script: he makes it clear that this guy knows what he's doing is wrong, but if he doesn't do it, the next guy will come along and be even worse. That, plus a sense of entitlement ("we _tried_ the nice way!") makes him feel very accurate to me. A nice view of how an authority figure would work.

    Oh, hey! You could totally have multiple authority figures (corporate, military, and scientific) giving you multiple, sometimes conflicting, mission goals. That'd be cool.
