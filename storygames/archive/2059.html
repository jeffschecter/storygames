Let me preface this by saying I was pondering this before seeing the Island of Lost Toys actual play postings....

Watching The Lost Room earlier this week (I loves me my TiVo), I started pondering stealing the plot gimmick (100 or so 'objects' with strange powers that people are trying to collect) for the ZoZ game I plan to start running for my 5 year-old in the New Year. (The plot gimmick is largely for my entertainment, mind you.)

I start mentally checklisting through "Fairy Tale Items" - Puss's Boots, the Princess's Pea, the Glass Slipper, Jack the Giant-Slayer's Belt, etc. Then I start to ponder what could tie them all together and if there is any other reason than "joy of collecting" or "the lust for power" for ZoZ'ians to be try to "catch them all."

This morning I wonder if they all fell out of Santa's pack during that Fateful Year Without a Santa Claus a while back.

Any, mostly brainstorming here and wondering if you folks had any riffs on the subject.

    I haven't seen The Lost Room, but I'm reminded of the old Friday the 13th: The TV Series um... TV series. Anyone remember that? It must have been late 80's or early 90's. I remeber watching it on my mom's old black & white 12" Zenith while staying up past my bedtime.

    Anyway, from what I remember, the hook revolved around this young couple that buys an old antique shop, fully stocked. Turns out there's this multi-dimentional vault in the back where the previous owner had stored all sorts of dangerous artifacts. Somehow the vault gets opened, the artifacts scattered, and it becomes up to the couple to retrieve all the objects and put them back in the vault.

    The only episode I remember was the makeup box. It was this old vaudeville-era stage makeup kit. Using the makeup was like um... +6 Cha. The wearer becomes extra attractive and likeable. Problem is, the box is perpetually locked. You have to soak it in fresh human blood to get it to open for you. And, naturally, the effects of the makeup tend to wear off faster and faster. Also naturally, the user becomes addicted to being pretty and popular and therefore becomes a mass murderer.

    I imagine however you were to write up the collecting thing as a game, it could totally be an awesome lightweight episodic game.

    Posted By: BlueI start mentally checklisting through "Fairy Tale Items" - Puss's Boots, the Princess's Pea, the Glass Slipper, Jack the Giant-Slayer's Belt, etc. Then I start to ponder what could tie them all together and if there is any other reason than "joy of collecting" or "the lust for power" for ZoZ'ians to be try to "catch them all."
    • Fellow magical items/creatures giving them a haven because their sentience is overlooked by the rest of the world. ("Look, I'm not just at pair of shoes, OK? So wash those stinky feet before you even come close to me!")
    • Assembled, they create something more powerful ("And I'll form the head!")
    • Someone needs them in order to undertake a quest.
    • All come from the same artisan/treasure trove/inheritance and are being reunited by an heir.
