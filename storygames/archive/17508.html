    \n The terror and madness system is the only thing that really stands out as the part of the rules that really connects with the fiction. Even the magic stuff, while containing some good ideas, seems too systematized to me.

    \n There is Apocalypse World hack of Kult in production that looks really promising, a bunch of people have play tested it and I think it will end up great, but I havn't playtested it myself yet. http://kult-illusionen.blogspot.se/ (In Swedish)

    I run Kult scenarios as freeform. It was tightly written family drama, that was really psychologically heavy. We intended to use a conflict resolution system, but in the end it felt wrong. It was heavy, sensitive and dark and we didn't push and win conflict on player level. It felt like pushing conflicts and winning the right to resolve the scene wasn't the right way to approach something that was border-lining on "to dark" all the time.

    We decided to carefully tell the story together instead as collaborative freeform, and for that style of Kult i think it was the right way to do it.

    \n I played and GMed a good amount of Kult back in college. We used a slightly stripped down version of the system and ignored the magic rules as they were too boringly systematic. For the most part players didn't have access to real magic anyway. When they did, I ignored the rituals as written and as the GM created a spell ritual on the fly borrowing from various of traditions.

    The madness rules and the rich fiction are what make the game. By the end of one of my sessions one of the war veterans had fallen seriously down the madness well with physical manifestation of jagged shards of stained glass coming out of his back like wings. That was also the one where they passed through the illusion while running from a lictor and and met Faust who looked like and old man fetus and was imprisoned in a jar\u2026 yeah you can get seriously dark and crazy with the game.

    If I were to GM it again, I'd use an AW-hack. I gleaned the Swedish one that is in beta (thanks Google Translate!), but I don't care for how they are approaching it. They are mapping the character archetypes directly from the Kult book and to be frank those are the most boring aspects of the game. Really I think it would make more interesting to borrow more heavily from AW: Chopper = Gangbanger; Gunlugger = Professional (hired killer) or Tough Cop; Hardholder = Mob Boss; Hocus = Cult Leader; Operator = Fixer; Savvyhead = Mechanic; Skinner = Femme Fatale. This would be a different game in that the characters would be more badasses than how Kult is originally designed to play. However, I like that aspect as then you can involve some serious world corruption with the lictors and other darker influences.

    That said, Kult can work great on a more interpersonal level too. Might make for a great really really dark Fiasco playset.

    Seriously. There's a lot of weird, gory, horrific shit going on. But there's very little room for the player characters to have "adventures." They can have "experiences," but for those, the rules are double useless.
    \nI found it can be run like a modern day splatterpunk Call of Cthulhu where the PCs stumble upon horrors that just keep building and everyone is just trying to survive and hopefully banish whatever it is. This could be to bring a close connected NPC out of a personal pain purgatory they created for themselves (with the help of a Nepharite), or destroy a place that serves as a doorway to Inferno.

    If you are running more of a campaign, then that's when you slowly reveal the mobster like bureaucracy of the criminals, serial killers, black magicians, beasts of the borderlands, lictors, cultists, and other horrors that are controlling and feeding off of humanity. It doesn't matter if the players just want to settle down and avoid the horrors, they can't forget nor can they ever hide as they have captured the attention of someone or something. I mean sure the characters would like to just get back to their day jobs and forget what they witnessed, but now they know that the "Happyland Day Care" chain is actually a front for child torturing soul stealing servants of the Dark Angels.

    This is why I mentioned it could be run as either: a dark Fiasco, where the players are trying save someone from personal pain purgatory but all end up dying in the end anyway. Or as a AW-hack as the world is basically an apocalypse where most of humanity doesn't realize it. Also, the fiction provides so much fodder for throwing constant Threats and Fronts at the players.

    \n I'm wary of running Kult as freeform - the reality is already against the PCs, and I'd like a somewhat objective mediator between the GM and the players.

    I love AW, but I'm afraid a hack of it would determine too much of the content of Kult with the moves and principles and everything. (I knew the obligatory six years of Swedish lessons would come in handy someday.)

    The madness system seems like the most fun and central part of the game. I've been toying around with the idea of using a HeroQuest 2 hack, where the different mental conditions would be represented by characters' negative abilities. Different sorts of stimuli would activate them and the character would be attacked from the inside, so to speak. I'm not sure how well HQ2's abstract extended conflict resolution rules would fit the oppressing atmosphere of Kult, though.
