\n So I've posted about this as a beta once before and since then I've cleaned up the rules a little bit and think it's close for me to release as version 1.0.

I took L&F and added a little bit of Cthulhu Dark and Cthulhu Gray ,a pinch of World Of Dew, and created an insanity mechanic, threw it all in a blender and came up with Fear & Reason.

I wanted to follow in the L&F tradition of having it all fit on one page, but at this point, I'm not so sure I can still do that.

I also plan on releasing a few micro-podcasts (5-10min podcasts) on topics relating to Fear & Reason on http://fearandreasonrpg.blogspot.com/

Take a look and I would appreciate any and all feedback on this.

    \n I haven't played L&F at all, so I really don't know. Any die seems a bit high for this sort of game, though. "Special insight" becomes less special if it happens a lot, it would seem. Then again it's hardly a superpower, so maybe it doesn't matter. Seems like one might run out of relevant questions if there are many rolls in a scene, though. Two dice on the number seems good, too. Alternatives:

    * Any two dice match. This would still make it impossible to get it at 1 die, but more likely to get it at 2 or 3.

    * Have a "default" die that is always rolled, and only that die will get you a special insight. This would make skill irrelevant and get you insight on one of six rolls, regardless.

    Or maybe you want special insight to be harder when you use more dice, to encourage players to not just use their specialties?

    \n I love what you did with L&F, Jim!

    Laser Feelings are very cool IMO, I would change that. What becomes boring is rolling again after the laser feeling questions got answered. What if a laser feeling comes up again? Duh... After a few sessions, we played this way:

    If you dont change your action, just roll again with the laser feeling dice(s).
    If you cange your action, roll again with all of your dices.
    In any case, a newly rolled laser feeling is a hit (coz you have some advantage).

    Other thoughs:
    * Insanity rules are great!!!
    * Clue rules... I personally dont get it. We always played these sort of games that players can declare things even without a clue token. Maybe we played them wrong, but still, the rule seems a bit clunky. When should the GM give clue tokens to players and how? To every player? Etc.
    * L&F has an important feature: an obliviously cool starting point. But F&R doesnt have one. This way it's harder to start awesome gaming for new GMs.

    \n Thank you for the feedback!

    In the newer version i have made it so that to use the Fear and Reason mechanic you must also spend a clue token.

    I'm realizing the limitations of having only one page for rules. The clue token is meant to encourage players to actively investigate the scenario. They're meant to be given out whenever a player actively describes how they're investigating a scene. I realize that some players may already contribute to describing the world around them in story games, the clue token is meant to encourage that even more. There's a few more mechanics that I'm adding to the game which will describe more in detail the use of Clue tokens.

    I hadn't thought of sort of a starting off point the way you mentioned, it's something to consider and I'll have to think about it to come up with a quick little jumping off point. Please keep in mind but this game is still very much in beta and I am constantly looking at ways to expand and improve it in such a way but it does not bog down a perfectly elegant system with a bunch of rules that doesn't need.

    Thank you again for the feedback you've given me something to think about
