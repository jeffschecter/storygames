\n So in that other thread, I was talking about how it would be nice if there were an IRC channel where people could hang out and just grab other people idling to play quick IRC games with (or even do things like "Hey, anybody here good with tuesdays from 8-11 PM EST as a regular weekly thing?" or such, too.) And Elizabeth was all "Why don't you start a channel yourself, it's not like it costs anything" and I thought "That's true, let's give it a whirl."

So! Here's me giving it a whirl! Like it says in the topic, the channel is #playnow on magicstar.net. I'll probably be hanging out whenever I'm online and signed into IRC. There's a few other people who have shown up already based on the comment in the other thread.

If you're interested in the idea of pick-up games via IRC, this thread is where you can post what you're cool with playing/running, what times you anticipate being usually online and available for gaming, and all that jazz.

I'm usually online in mornings, from around 8:30 or 9:00 AM EST until whenever I go to bed (I work grave shift). Games I wouldn't mind busting out off the top of my head: Polaris, Lacuna, DRYH, Nobilis, Ghost/Echo, Everway, and a handful of other assorted oddments that may work more or less well in the IRC medium. I'd be willing to learn other systems, too, if somebody wanted to play and would be willing to teach, of course.

    \n One really useful convention we use in our IRC games is to have a single period on a line to indicate that everyone else should shut up while you narrate and another one when you're finished - it can be a bit painful if you're writing a longer thing and someone says something that invalidates it. Also handy for separating scenes.
    \n
    \nAnd one thing about stryk's guide:\n
    \n
    Also note, you CANNOT start a line with a / as the server will interpret it as an attempt at a command. To fake it out, you can put a . in front of it like so ./whoosh!/ It's as close as you'll get.
    \n
    \nThere's a '/say' command that handles this.
