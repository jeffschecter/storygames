\n I can't announce dates or begin to approach folks for Gaming Guest of honor (*cough* Jason *cough*) but I'd like to test the waters, here at an "NC-based" indie gaming site.

1) Who here is interested in attending to run indie games (ideally, your own). I am pretty sure that three sessions = free admission; and I am currently negotiating to allow designers to sell their own games at the tables, if they are not being sold in the Dealer Room by a store (or your own booth, maybe).

2) Who here would consider preparing and chairing two or three panels on indie gaming: design, production, promotion/distribution? I believe this will also follow the three = free (possibly mix-and-match with running sessions).

3) Does anyone have ideas for "special events" (i.e. other tthan game session or panels) that would be appropriate for a smallish (but intending to grow FAST and BIG) con? I'm thinking of something similar, say, to an on-site Game Chef competition, or maybe a "Layout 101" seminar, using the (still uncertain) LAN area? (OK, those are the two ideas *I* had.)

    \n OK, folks, I am putting out a general call for the following:

    1) Someone to help me coordinate the Indie Gaming Track. This will mainly involve coordinating other indie publishers for panels and helping to advertise around the web (we have an Ad Chair for other channels, and for general con advertising, not gaming-specific).

    2) Folks who would like to be Guests on the aforementioned panels. Do three, get in free--and I think the slots are only, like, an hour or an hour and a half. GMs have to do more to get in free (three slots of 3-4 hours).

    3) GMs to start thinking about what they'd like to run--do three slots, get in free. I'll have a "for real" Game Registry running about, say, three months before the con (mid-March-ish); but it doesn't hurt to start thinking about finishing up those ashcans or prepping for players unfamiliar with the game(s) you want to run.

    All that said, I am mainly "Indie" focussed, here. I will be taking the call to "trad" RPG sites as well, mainly for GMing; and I will be in touch with a few product line reps I know for non-RPG activities (WizKidz, CCGs) and with other sites for board and mini games.

    Five months and counting to the Big Tenth Anniversary, where I want to prove the NC/SC/VA is an Indie hub! Please help where you can!
    David Artman

    \n Yep, it is. Thanks to everyone who planned to come and who agreed to be Guests of the con. Maybe next year.

    ...But I kind of doubt it. I no longer think that this area (Piedmont NC) can support three cons, or four if you include Charlotte. DragonCon might be a bit to blame--it's close enough that folks can seriously consider going, but expensive enough to drain budgets for con-going for a year. With StellarCon's following and MACE's success to bracket the con season, I just don't see much hope of a mid-summer con, butting up against end-of-summer DragonCon and coming right on the heels of start-of-summer Origins. *sigh*
