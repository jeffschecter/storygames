\n There's this game I've been working on for about a month. It's called Gun Thief. Back cover blurb:

Bank robberies, deals gone sideways, crooked dames, and the ugly smell of kerosene as it all burns down. The hollow sound when a lead pipe connects with a thick skull. Get-aways, evenings thrown away on a new piece of tail. Everything raw, everything smokey, everything halfway to hell before it even realizes it.

You\u2019ve only ever known two things: how to hold a gun steady, and how to take what ain\u2019t yours. Ain\u2019t no decent men in this world. Ain\u2019t no decent women, either. Still, you\u2019re going to make it through this shitstorm. You\u2019re a gun thief.

Gun Thief is a game for 3 players, but can accomodate up to 4.
It is diceless, gives each player a unique role, and is easy to learn.

Gun Thief involves violence, and probably sex. It is not a game for children or easily discomforted adults.



The game is now finished.
It's available for $3, as a screen-optimized PDF.
It runs a lean 9 pages, including cover & character sheet. ~2,500 words.

You can paypal me three bones at joepub88 (at) hotmail (dot com).
I'll get you it within a day.



If you're going to be at Go Play NW, I'm going to have a comic-sized print version there.
It runs 12 pages, and will likely retail for $4-5.


Any questions?

    \n I playtested it and had a heck of a lot of fun.

    And like the man says, the sex is DEFINITELY there, but it doesn't have to be graphic. We "veiled" it in our play: the character were definitely having sex, but we didn't talk about it. The game contains a lot of shared narration, and I'd say it would be tough to take the narration somewhere that any one player was uncomfortable with, due to the way "interrupting" works.

    As long as everyone is willing and eager, Gun Thief supports play ranging from "slightly zany/tragic crime stories" to "dirty sex and tragic bloodbath". No matter where you fall on the spectrum, it ends in tragedy. Ooh, it's like the Player says in Rosencrantz and Guildenstern are Dead: "We're more of the love, blood, and rhetoric school. Well, we can do you blood and love without the rhetoric, and we can do you blood and rhetoric without the love, and we can do you all three concurrent or consecutive. But we can't give you love and rhetoric without the blood. Blood is compulsory. They're all blood, you see."

    I'm a bit biased, because I enjoyed the playtest AND I'm a sucker for the genre, but I'd say it's worth $3, if only for the opportunity to recreate the stories contained in Criminal and 100 Bullets -- two of my favourite comic series.
