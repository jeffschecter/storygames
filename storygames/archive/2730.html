    \n A recent discussion about Firefly as a RPG had one of my friends identifying the cast in PC/NPC relationships, which really map out to PTA's connections. We aligned them this way: Mal's major NPC is Kaylee, River's is Simon, Book's is Jayne, and Zoe's is Wash. (Inara didn't pair up with another cast member.) The thought was Mal, River, Book, and Zoe had enough background and abilities to make them interesting as major characters. Kaylee, Simon, Jayne, and Wash don't seem to be fleshed out as well (with the possible exception of Simon).

    Even if you discount these pairings and you go with the PC list above, I'd use the other crew members as connections. Pair up Mal with Kaylee, Zoe with Wash, and Jayne with Book.

    \n I think it depends on the group your approaching. If I were to pull this out to a more tradition group I think it would better be "prepared" with a setting in mind. Besides, there's a whole section of the book that is devoted to existing series. I will already be having them talk about issues and traits for the characters (hopefully from a larger list) and what THIS episode is going to focus on. I believe that to be enough of the PITCH to make it worth while. Besides, by framing it with "come play the lost episode of Firefly" I think it sets up expectations for the game solidly. Everyone, even those who have played PTA, can jump right in with ideas of what's going on.

    This post was designed to have those that are familiar with PTA and Firefly to give me a better handle on examples from Firelfy for Issues and traits (I have played all of once).

    \n BTW I find that for short conventin games (4 hours or less) I tend to do some of the prep ahead of time. I did this for The Mountain Witch, My Life With Master, Dogs, and Mortal Coil. I come to the table with a setting and "what this game is about" and sometimes character ideas. Then I let them either customize the characters or maybe even create them from scratch.

    I did this for my Don't Rest Your head game as well and it worked out fine. Then I don't feel rushed touching on everyone's flags and issues.

    \n I'm assuming you're setting this during Firefly instead of post-Serenity.

    Issues that jump out at me for Simon are Love for Kaylee (romantic troubles) or The New Kid on the Block (self-worth). Edges could be things like Doctor, Booksmart, Upper Class, or Wanted. His nemesis is Jayne, if he has one. Personal set would be the medical bag. Kaylee is his connection.

    River is pretty easy -- her issue is They Messed With My Head. Edges would be along the lines of Wanted, Gifted, Crazy, and Psychic. Her nemesis would be the Blue Hands men. Personal set would be her feet. (Nearly every shot of River in Firefly started on her feet.) Simon is her connection, but I don't know if you can have another protagonist as a connection.

    Inara's issues would tend towards the Romantic Troubles line or something to do with unresolved issues, if you buy into the concept that Inara is on Serenity because she is dying. I don't think she has a nemesis. Personal set would be her shuttle. Connections would be the Companion Registry (or whatever the Companions as a whole were called). Edges include Companion (obvious), High Maintenance, Can Put Emotions on Hold, Learned, or Upper Class, although I think Companion covers the others.

    Mal's issue could either be Self-Destructive, Stay Free, or Love for Inara. Edges would be Captain Tightpants, Browncoat, or Professional Smuggler (or "The Han Solo Effect"). Connections would be Serenity and The Grey Market. Personal set would be his trusty sidearm or the actual coat he wears. Nemesis would be Saffron.

    Jayne and Zoe aren't as easy as the earlier ones.

    Jayne. Issue is Greed. Connections would be Book. Edges would include Mercenary and Loudmouth. Personal set? Vera. I don't think Jayne has a nemesis.

    Zoe's issue has to do with duty to her husband and to her captain. Wash is her connection. She'd have Browncoat and Wife as edges. Her personal set would be her room with Wash. She doesn't have a nemesis.

    \n I've thought about doing this very thing. What I'd do is, assuming you've got buy in from all of your players, say to them "We're going to do Firefly with PTA. I want you guys to choose a character, and you figure out what that character's issue is, what their connections and edges are, and so forth." Exactly as if you were pitching a standard PTA series, except that you've already decided you're playing Firefly. So the guy playing Mal isn't sitting there looking at what a bunch of yahoos on an Internet message board decided Mal's issue should be, he's deciding what Mal's issue is for himself (edit) WITH back and forth from the rest of the group. Which is as it should be, especially in PTA.

    \n Thought was in "Out of Gas", this exchange between Simon and Inara:

    Inara: I love this ship. I have from the moment I saw it.
    Simon: I just don't want to die on it.
    Inara: [glancing away] I don't want to die at all.

    implies that Inara may already be dying from some sort of terminal disease. The syringe she was going to use in the pilot when passing by the Reaver ship? It's a drug to kill her, so she wouldn't fall to the reavers while alive. Why would she have this drug? For when the pain of whatever is killing her is too great, she can slip out with some dignity.

    Then again at Objects in Space's opener, River was hearing lines from the other members of Serenity's crew. Two thoughts on this -- she's hearing what they're feeling and going through right now or they're hearing things from their past. Mal's "None of it means a damn thing" and Jayne's "I got stupid. The money was too good." seem to point to the echoes of the past. So Inara's "I'm a big girl, just tell me" line may not have been her wanting Mal to tell her to stay, it may have been her remembering getting the news from her doctor, which started her whole travelling in Serenity.

    Of course, you have to buy into the whole concept. Honestly, there's more bits about Book's past in the show than there is to support the Inara dying theory.
