    \n Feel free to take your time. Tell everyone that's what you're doing, something like, "Dr. Enormobrain ponders this for two milliseconds....but me, I need a minute to work this out, hang on, 'kay?"

    Crowdsource your solutions: put a question up to all the players, and pick the answer you think is the smartest.

    Stack the deck if you can: guess what's coming up next in the game (or suggest something to the GM to add into the game), and prep some material for discussing and dealing with it. (I did this one all the time in one game, and could deliver little mini-lectures in character about propulsion systems or genetic engineering or hydroelectric dams, and a few ideas about how to use them to our advantage. It was fun!)

    If the system gives you a way to make something true, make sure your character is set up to use the hell out of it. (Like how FATE lets you make declarations about things? Declare the fuck out of everything and call it your character's brilliant insights at work.)


    And seriously, take your time. Almost everyone is smarter, or at least sounds smarter, when they take a breath and actually think before they start talking.

    \n Just be the mouthpiece for everyone's choicest ideas. obviously, make sure everyone's cool with that. so if I wanted to play as Derp, the mentally challenged half-orc barbarian, I can still come up with cool plans, but it's your character, Dr. Enormobrain, who comes up with it in the fiction and presents it to the party in-game. Everyone wins, and everyone gets to play to their ability. it's up to your group whether you'd rather handle this through note-passing or just have it be understood that Dr. E is Tue one who came up with the plan, and Derp is just going to tilt his head slightly and go, "Yarp."
