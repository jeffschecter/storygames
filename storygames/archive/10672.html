    \n Good question, John. There are more structures (not traditional mechanics necessarily) that handle non-fighty things and those will involve a lot more interactions. Also, once you have more characters join your party, FF-style, you'll have a lot more interactions between them, even during the fighty bits. And if you wanted to, you could definitely talk to the wolves before they tried to eat you. It might not be a very productive conversation, but it would allow for more narrative and characterization.

    That said, this is another board game / rpg hybrid thingee, but one that leans a bit more towards the board game stuff than Geiger Counter, so there might not be as much narrative freedom as in some other games. At least, that's how I'm imagining it now. Still, some of the encounters will be much more open ended.
