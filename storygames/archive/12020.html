\nI've had a weird idea rattling around inside my head for the weekend. (weekend without net does wonders for creativity)
\n
\nTL;DR - Has anyone here ever tried running a game where all the characters are blank slates? Ie - they know nothing about the characters?
\n
\nThe idea in more detail:
\nA pre-history game about the birth of magic. Humanity is struggling to survive in a world of monsters and demons - small city-states with the population huddled behind walls, hoping to escape the terrors of the wilderness. But they are loosing. The world is breaking.
\nOne group of the "gods" decides that humanity must be saved, but cannot intervene directly. They want to give magic to the humans. The Gods try to create some people who can wield magic, but they cant get it right.
\nThe result are the PCs. Beings made of divine fire surrounded by a shell which looks human.
\n
\nThe game idea that has been bothering me is this: The PCs are these creatures that can pass for human and they have powers, but they (the characters) dont know what they are, or really that much of what they can do.
\n
\nI started out trying to use the White Wolf "Promethian: The Created" books. Its a relatively good fit, but it really requires that the players know and spend time building the characters before hand. I loved the idea of the pilgrimage to create a human soul. The pinoccio theme of "I want to be a real boy"
\n
\nThen I started looking over "Ghost/Echo", "Lady Blackbird", and "Apocalypse World". From my reading I really want to sit this game somewhere in that design space where the abilities and world is much more fluid and influenced by the players, and the faster character creation.
\n
\nWhat I really want the characters to be doing is working out how they can gain a soul, and to eventually tie the divine fire (Magic) with the human soul and so save the world.... The game is "about" making choices and more fully defining themselves. I want the players to be choosing as the game goes on what their characters are good/bad at, and deciding what is the way for a created being to gain a soul...

    \n Maybe something like Cold City or Hot War would fit; its an integral part of both games that characters can gain, lose or change their traits as a result of conflicts. If you start the PCs with no traits, then they will only be able to define themselves through conflict. Given that both games also depend on narrative authority being won as a result of conflicts, this means that every conflict would be a struggle for the players to define their characters the way they want them; losing a conflict gives the GM an opportunity to define them in another way.

    Posted By: DionysusThe PCs are these creatures that can pass for human and they have powers, but they (the characters) dont know what they are, or really that much of what they can do.
    Maybe you could let the characters be a litter of babies (three-, four- or fifthlets, or whatever you call them), invested with magical powers in the woomb of their mother, and supposed to save their city from the onslaught of wilderness and monsters.

    So you get to play them growing up, being abnormal. They could have their own special language before birth, be ready to walk and run and climb and dive when born, and be ripe with unbridled magical powers ... but being emotional "blank", much like other babies.

    Would give you a "clean slate", sort of, and lots of oportunities for the characters to bond with parents, older siblings, other family, and the rest of their city. Emotional bonds could do a lot towards deepen the game, and developing personalities through "growing up" could liven up the how's of character-building.

    And you might escape the smell of super-hero characters. ;-)

    Good luck!

    \n Thanks troy - that is.. very interesting. Actually gels well with the direction I was going.. (I love the "awaken a psi talent")

    \nAlso love the roll extra dice, keep highest/lowest - also ties in with the idea of improving skills like "I roll for shoes" that teatine commented on.
    \n
    \nThe current idea (Borrowed heavily from Promethean) is that the characters will have to refine the magic inside them - and slowly make it into a human soul. I've got a heavy elemental feel going.
    \n
    \nEarth - Resistance and Fortitude
    \nAir - Knowledge and Perception
    \nFire - Rage and Valor
    \nWater - Relationship and Comfort
    \nAether - The Divine Fire.
    \n
    \nAll start at +0
    \n
    \nWhenever you act you define the goal and danger. Add dice as per the element you are acting on.
    \nRoll- 2d6. 1 dice result assign to the goal, 1 result to the danger.
    \n
    \nWhenever you roll a 6 for the goal - the task you just achieved becomes one of your milestones, with a rating of 1, and gain 1xp
    \n
    \nEvery time you then do an act relating to a milestone you roll 2d6. with goal: learning and danger: now irrelevant.
    \ngoal 1-2 0xp, 3-4: 1xp 5-6: 2xp
    \ndanger: 1-2: rating -1, 3-4: rating unchanged. 5-6 rating +1
    \n
    \nWhen the rating of the milestone goes to 0, you have learnt everything you can from that, and you have to find another milestone.
    \n
    \nYou can only have 1 milestone for each element at one time.
    \n
    \n(Thats all i have so far)

    \n Cool. I think the nicest thing about your idea is that it mechanically encodes player participation in the fiction and in developing the fictional physics of the setting, much like Brennan Taylor's Mortal Coil. I like the idea of including Aether in with the four traditional elements. You might want to rethink the word "divine" in the subtitle, unless you really mean it. It seems to bring in religion. Maybe you want that, since you are playing with the idea of a soul as well. And, if I could make a suggestion, you might toy around with using cards instead of dice. I have thought about how a "hand of cards" would affect a system like Otherkind Dice and it seems to me it might smooth over some of the painfulness of that system and make it a bit more character friendly. (To be clear, the system's painfulness and character-chewing dice is actually a feature I LIKE about it, for some settings.) Card suits, though, would give you a feature that could be aligned with the elemental powers. (clubs = earth, spades = air, hearts = water, and diamonds = fire ... or some such).
