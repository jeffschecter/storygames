    Posted By: Jason MorningstarIain, tell us about your cards - how many, how did you produce them, what's the process, how has working with GoB been for you?
    \nRightio.

    I will start with GoB as that informs the rest. Firstly there communication has been stunning. Ryan gets back to me within a day with any enquiry I post and I am in touch with him via. skype as well. He is easy to talk to, friendly and efficient. Excellent all round.

    There are doing cards in increments of 9 so the 60 card game I did have has been boosted to 63. I put together a template, in indesign CS2, for the card layout and then dropped in the images that Paul Bourne, he of Contested Ground Studios, did for me. Text dropped on top of that. Box wise they do tuck boxes pre printed that I opted for. These are sent flat packed and you basically assemble everything yourself. I am getting 100 decks printed though you can go as large as 300. Ryan has been very good in doing early test prints a shot of which you can see here:

    Early test print

    Now there has been a small problem with drag on the printer due to the thickness of the cards. As you can see this is not too much of a problem on the front but has caused it to look a bit funny on the back. Paul was kind enough to do me a new back and it looks excellent. Again that was sent to Ryan this morning, my time, and by the time I got home from work, 8:30pm my time, I had new test shots. I will post those up tomorrow in the same forum the above shots are in.

    I have no given the green light to Ryan so he is going to go ahead and print everything for me. By the time that is done my paypal should have cleared and then they will send everything once I pay for shipping.

    For reference here is the GoB website.

    I would be happy to answer any further questions and help people out with developing card games.

    Cheers

    Iain
