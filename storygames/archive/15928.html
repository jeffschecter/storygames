\n So I'm working on a game called "Measure of a Badass". The 25-words or less pitch is, what if you took all the action heroes from the movies and TV shows of the 1980s and put them all on the same team? Then had them face off against $OBVIOUS_VILLAIN_ORGANIZATION in the modern era? I'm picturing things like The A-Team vs. some criminal group, GI Joe vs. Cobra, MI6 vs. SPECTRE, stuff like that. The heroes all follow from the era; guys inspired by Rambo, MacGyver, and so on. It's pretty over-the-top.

The player archetypes are already pretty well set, and I'm happy where the game is mechanically. What I really want to do, though, is figure out how best to plot scenes, and I'd like to mimic the structure (and tropes) of action shows if possible. So what I'm wondering is, what are some of the standard dramatic structures and plot flows of the genre?

A few pieces I can think of would be a Cold Open (the pre-credits scene in James Bond movies, and most TV shows), investigation, big fights, chase and escape scenes, and so forth, but I want to give the prospective GM guidance on how to put them together. And for that matter, basic plot types wouldn't hurt, either -- Behind Enemy Lines, Find the Baddie Base, stuff like that.

    \n If you've got time, I highly recommend the book Action Speaks Louder by Eric Lichtenfeld. It's an academic but accessible history of action cinema, mostly focused on Hollywood. It includes some potentially very useful run-downs of the common narrative tropes of various action sub-genres without falling into simplistic or reductive structuralism.

    http://www.amazon.com/Action-Speaks-Louder-Violence-Spectacle/dp/0275980545
