\n Last night, we played Trail of Cthulhu. It was my apocalyptic reworking, nothing like Lovecraft and more like John Wyndham.

So the Investigators arrive and everyone in the town is dead. Most of the adventure was spent wandering around meeting the people who are still alive. It was beautifully atmospheric.

Without giving too much away, the monster turns out to be inside an Investigator. So it becomes a personal, intense horror. Again, it was rather atmospheric.

Then, the Investigator decides that, to remove the monster, he will consume large quantities of laxative.

Thanks.

Graham

    \n From a tone and mood point of view, the best approach I've ever seen in practice for handling the tension between irony/humour and suspense/terror was done by Sandy Petersen during a con scenario I participated in, and it basically seemed to follow in the vein of "Yes, and..." type techniques.

    Sandy, as GM, seemed to try to be as neutral a deliverer of description and context as possible, neatly avoiding engagement at a meta-game level. This doesn't mean that he insisted that the players do everything "in character", or strenuously insist that anything out of a player's mouth was a committed action choice. However, what it does mean is that he sat back and let kibbitzing and joking happen, but he neatly avoided engaging in it himself, and he always followed up with an even and neutral description of situation and consequence.

    In this particular situation, I would suspect that an attempt by players to inject levity and irony into a scene can be neatly turned on its head by the GM through redirection and blending. The investigator decides that, to remove the monster, he will consume large quantities of laxative. Yes.

    So; what consequences does this choice bring to the table? Treating the investigator's choice neutrally can rebound on the group with fearsome and gruesome consequences, especially if the Keeper comes with knowledge about (a) the creature's biology, (b) the investigator's physiological reaction as host, and (c) what happens to the body when it consumes large quantities of laxative. The result might not be pretty, and could well turn out to be a horrifying and memorable scene.
