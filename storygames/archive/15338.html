\n This thread serves two purposes. One is that it provides me with a little crowdsourced fodder for a project I'm working on. More importantly, though, I think it'll be fun to talk about!

I'd like to hear about your favorite (or, y'know, just one that you liked) monster from D&D. Any edition is fine. Tell us what the monster was and what differentiates it from, say, an bog-standard Ogre. Were you swarmed by kobolds? Stabbed in the back by a Doppleganger in the guise of a PC? Breath weapons? Gaze attacks? I need to hear about interesting encounters that I didn't make up myself. Help me out?

    The write up in "Dwellers of the Forbidden City" is what got me into role-playing games way back in grade 7... some girl had brought in the module, I read its statblock and description, having no idea what it meant, and thought, "This is so weird and cool, I HAVE to find out more!"

    \n My favorite were always gnolls: The fact that they were mostly human (I had them act very close-to-human in my D&D days), adding a little culture led to some cool "I'm not sure it's right to slaughter them..." moments.

    But then I got D&D 4e. Which introduced me to the ATROPAL, which is a floating insane necrotic aborted god-fetus. Imagine if the "2001 Monolith Fetus-Dave" and "Worm God-Emperor of Dune" had a baby, and that baby had a stillborn twin that suddenly came to life and was really pissed off at the world.

    Insanely powerful, though: Requires high level characters to topple. I'd not play "everyone starts at level one and we take out the Atropal", but maybe 3-levels-down. Or just bring it into another game of some kind.

    Pics:





    GAH! Freaky.

    Like, you could have a dragon be the big enemy boss of a campaign. It could be a mindless rampaging lizard-monster, or a crafty hyper-intelligent being. Either way, sure, that's a good bad guy to go up against.
    But an Atropal? That's a fucking ABOMINATION. Nothing says "You need to grab whatever weapons in your reach and fucking kill that horrible thing! That's fucking evil that reeks of MORE EVIL" than an Atropal.

    -Andy

    \n Another vote for gnolls, in particular the 4e Monster Manual 2 batch, which really upped the ante with two creepy entries: psychotic gnoll priests who used clubs impregnated with the diseased TEETH of their slain (and eaten) enemies as a weapon, and the cannibal berserkers who had an actual game mechanic for regaining HP by biting chunks out of their own allies. That was the most memorable fight in 15 leves of play we had, and the single most powerful example I have ever seen of a stat block entry having such a profound atmospheric effect when I dropped it on the PCs.

    I personally find the Devourer to be completely terrifying: a giant undead who traps you inside his chest cavity to slowly burn your soul as food. If he burns through you before he's slain, your spirit is permanently destroyed, no resurrection. Slow death by necro-torture combined with claustrophobia and full awareness, potentially lasting years? Sign me up!

    \n Abominations, including the Atropal, actually showed up as early as 3rd edition, in the Epic Level Handbook. (Check out the Hypertext SRD!) One of the things I really dig about those incarnations is that most of the Abominations have regeneration, which can be defeated by something boring like an appropriately-aligned weapon, or something specific. For example, the Atropal, Abomination of undeath, can't regenerate damage caused by a sentient or otherwise living weapon. The dream larvae can't regenerate injuries inflicted by a weapon made by a sleepwalking weaponsmith. The Hecatoncheires can't regenerate from weapons tempered in the blood of a deity. The phane and its wacky temporal shenanigans can't regenerate from a weapon forged in the future, or an alternate timestream.

    I mean, that kind of thing? Pardon my language, but that's fucking gold right there.

    Posted By: MarhaultWhat's up with the Gnolls? I always wrote them off as a just another chaotic humanoid. One of dozens.
    \nHyenas are creepy. Cannibalistic hyena-men, driven by bloodlust and chaos, are disturbing. So I usually play up those aspects.\n
    Posted By: Jim CrockerAnother vote for gnolls, in particular the 4e Monster Manual 2 batch, which really upped the ante with two creepy entries: psychotic gnoll priests who used clubs impregnated with the diseased TEETH of their slain (and eaten) enemies as a weapon, and the cannibal berserkers who had an actual game mechanic for regaining HP by biting chunks out of their own allies.
    \nThese sound awesome and really in the spirit of how I think gnolls should be presented. A lot of D&D baddies have become really generic. If not by the book then in actual presentation in play. Part of the fault is that the presentation of information about the baddies is often done in a very dry, factual manner. Such as, "Gnolls eat anything warm blooded, favoring intelligent creatures over animals because they scream better." It gets the point across but it's not very visceral. And creatures like Orcs need a total makeover and a publicist. I guess I should check the 4e MM to see if that's still true.

      Posted By: WightbredThat Gelatinous template was wasted on a Bear. Needed to be an Owlbear.
      \nHere you go:

      Gelatinous Owlbear
      Level 8 Elite brute
      Large Aberrant beast
      XP 700

      HP 212 Bloodied 106
      AC 20 Fort 23 Reflex 21 Will 18

      Speed 5 (cannot shift)
      Saving throws +2 Action points: 1

      Traits:
      Made of Goo
      Whenever an adjacent enemy attacks and hits the Gelatinous Owlbear, they take 5 acid damage.

      Ooze
      While squeezing, the ooze moves at full speed rather than half speed, it doesn\u2019t take the -5 penalty to attack rolls, and it doesn\u2019t grant combat advantage for squeezing.

      Translucent
      The gelatinous owlbear is invisible until seen (Perception DC 25) or until it attacks. A creature that fails to notice the cube might walk into it, automatically being grabbed by the gelatinous owlbear.


      Standard actions:
      Slimy claw (Basic attack, melee 2) +14 vs AC; 2d8+8 acid damage and target is immobilized (save ends).

      Double slap: the gelatinous owlbear makes two basic attacks against the same or different target. If both attacks hit the same target, then the target is grabbed (until escape). The gelatinous owlbear can extrude new pseudopods to grab any number of creatures.

      Gnawing Beak: All creatures grabbed by the gelatinous owlbear take 2d8+11 acid damage.

      Triggered actions:
      Vomitous Hoot: (Immediate Interrupt, at-will) Trigger: An enemy makes an opportunity attack against the gelatinous owlbear. Close blast 3 (creatures in blast) +12 vs. Fort; push 3 squares, immobilized and prone (save ends both).

      Str 20, Con 16m Dex 14, Int 2, Wis 16, Cha 10
      Alignment: Unaligned Languages: -
