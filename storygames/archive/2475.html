Regarding James_Nostack's comment over in this thread.
Which is why I wish there was like "Story LEGO's", or tiles, or something like that. Story-Games Solitaire!
That's an interesting idea, and one that isn't just just for solitaire play.

Imagine a game (which perhaps already exists) where there were separate sets of cards or tiles for different elements of the story game:
  • Characters (perhaps these are made up each time instead of tiles)
  • Settings (these could also be made up as needed)
  • Scenes: what is the setup for this scene?
  • Conflicts: what is the conflict here?
  • Resolutions: this could include consequences as well
The system of scenes/conflicts/resolutions would need to be very generic to support different stories and types of play. Or they could be designed towards a particular story (ala the Roach or PTA).

I wouldn't really consider 'Once Upon a Time' to be a game like this since the object of that game is to interrupt someone else's storytelling to finish the story yourself. This is more collaborative "let's see what happens and where this goes" storytelling.

This is just a quick idea since I'm at work. But any thoughts?

    Were you thinking perhaps people might be sitting around a table with five stacks of tiles in the middle for your five categories? Eventually, it'd get boring, though the vast number of combinations of the five tile sets would help. You'd either need a buttload of tiles for Setting and Scenes, or you'd need to put out expansion packs and provide blank tiles for gamer-created situations. And what genre?
