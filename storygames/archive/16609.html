\n Hey all

Thought I'd let you guys know that I (and two others, Frax and Chrestomancy) have started a roleplaying discussion blog at http://www.blackarmada.com. Please do drop by and have a read, drop us a comment. We'd love to hear from you.

In particular you might be interested in a game called When The Dark Is Gone which Frax has written, the beta version of which is available on the site for free. The game is basically about a group of people who had a magical adventure as children, the experience of which traumatised them so much that they're in therapy for it as adults. It's about creating a fictional world collaboratively while exploring personal issues, and I guess the innovative thing about it (I think!) is that there's no system at all for resolving disputes over what happened in the magical world. One person's memory is just as good as another's, and disagreements are used as a prompt to explore in character issues. We got very positive feedback from the alpha test and we're really keen to get more! Please feel free to download it and let us know how you found it, either here or on Black Armada.
Cheers

Rabalias

    \nPersonally I wanted to add my voice to the voices of the (sadly small) number of awesome women who are designing, podcasting or blogging about games. Purely because I think that diversity in the gaming community only makes it cooler. Instead of sitting at home bemoaning the lack of women designers and commentators I figured I should get up off my bottom and contribute
    \n

I also blog about achieving genuine emotional experiences and connections in role-playing and how to get them as a player, GM and designer.

    \n Hey Andy,

    Thanks for your comment.

    We're a group of highly experienced UK gamers who have played and continue to play a wide range of game types - from traditional stuff to indie/story games and even LRP. Our preference is for story-focused, immersive play and that's the kind of play we'll be writing about.

    We like to write about pitfalls experienced when gaming, tips and tricks to improve your roleplaying, the application of management theory to roleplaying, and game design - specifically, our experience of designing games which we're engaged in at the moment. (For instance there's a great designer diary up at the moment for WTDIG.)

    I could go on... does that start to answer your question?
    Cheers
