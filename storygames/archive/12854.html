\n Ever since I was a kid I have been extremely fascinated with castles. Possibly the most intensely curious feeling I ever had was was when I was maybe 4 and my dad pointed to this castle in some strange shop we were in (it was either a hobby shop or a convenience store, I will never remember, hehe) which was one that you buy and construct yourself out of cardboard pieces. I remember using my young mind trying to comprehend this labyrinthine castle structure in all its complexity and mystery, and ever since then I've been hooked. Oh yeah and this one had ghosts. I must have asked my dad to get me that castle for days and then even months later (I think we never got it because I was too young to be able to build it, and it was probably expensive).

Anyway, I've always been dissatisfied with the exploration of castles in games. As far as video game go, I've tried to have fun with Medieval Total War or Stronghold, but neither game really focuses enough on all of the cool concepts you could take from the idea of the castle. Story games and castles? Actually there is an interactive online story game called Castle Marrach out there which is pretty intriguing. I haven't had time to invest into it, since it's persistent, but it's got interesting ideas. But not much else really comes to mind.

I dunno where I'm going with this, I just thought it was an interesting subject :-) If anyone else likes castles, feel free to add comments.

    \n I'm pretty fond of castles. Probably not as much as you, but I like them. I also found that most games were pretty unsatisfying on this front. Even games like Stronghold which were all about building and maintaining a castle had silly stuff in them, like swordsmen collapsing a castle wall or whatever.

    You might be interested in the upcoming splatbook for Chronica Feudalis which deals with nobles and has a "Mansion mechanic" or something of the like. Seeing how satisfying CF was in evoking a correct medieval feel with simple mechanics, I have high hopes for it.

    Montsegur 1244 is all about people living and dying IN a castle, but has practically no castle stuff itself. I felt it's was worth bringing up.

    I'm not really familiar with it, but I think ASoIaF RPG could also do a pretty good game about living in a castle, but again, nothing about the castle itself.

    What else is there? Action Castle is probably not something you're looking for.

    Posted By: TeataineI'm not really familiar with it, but I think ASoIaF RPG could also do a pretty good game about living in a castle, but again, nothing about the castle itself.
    \nASoIaF has a very in-depth chapter on defining the characters' house, and all the history and holdings thereof. The details it gets into are incredibly fun. Sadly, it doesn't get to the details of the castles themselves. The system also seems to handle politics and intrigue fairly well, so it could be very good for telling the stories of characters in the castle and its surroundings. The characters' adventures can lead to fortune (or misfortune) for their house, too, modifying the stats of the house and therefore its standing in the world.

    I feel like there could be an interesting castle game buried in the concepts of How to Host a Dungeon. Rather than exploring the history of a dungeon, you explore the history of a castle: when it was built, and by whom? What significant events occurred during its history? Was it ever conquered, and by whom? Who lives there now? What about the city that has sprung up around it? etc. etc.

    \n This may not relate entirely to the initial question, but it's so cool I can't help myself, plus for some reason Graham's post made me think of it.

    Have you all heard of Guedelon?

    It's a castle currently in-construction in France, and it's being built using Medieval building techniques. Part of the purpose is to rediscover these techniques. It's taking a long fucking time, like building a caste in the Middle Ages took. Isn't that amazing? Frankly I'm absolutely flabbergasted that such a thing even exists. When I first found out about it (through the book Fantasy Freaks & Gaming Geeks, which is good if you like reading memoirs & things about broader gaming culture) I was literally in awe for a while.
