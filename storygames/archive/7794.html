    \n Oh, I figured it out, not sure if it's a bug or just an awesome sub-development in the story engine (I can imagine it a flashback!), but I set it up so there was 2 wizards in the field with the princess in the cage. Then I go to the first panel and move one of the characters back so he becomes a knight in the second panel.

    Then, I go to the second panel and have the knight kill the wizard, so in the third panel the princess and knight are in love.

    Then, aha!, I go back to the first panel and make the knight into a wizard in the second, and then move the wizards apart in the second panel, and the heart remains over the grave.

    So yeah, in the end you can read the "panel 1, 2 and 3" thing and what happened right there.

    However, in the process of me manipulating things in the panels, I had one storyline that "reinvented itself" as the second storyline through manipulation.

    In other words, if this was some creepy movie or anime, the whole "first story" (warrior fights wizard, they fall in love) was nothing more than the haunting endless dream of a dead princess, who was actually seduced and inadvertantly killed by an obsessive wizard.

    This is really, really cool.

    -Andy
