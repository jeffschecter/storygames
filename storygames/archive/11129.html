\n So I've had a few spin-offs from discussions I've started, but now I'm spinning off myself.

In my discussion about gaming Warhammer 40,000 outside of Warhammer 40,000, the possibility was raised of using Apocalypse World to adventure in the world of Necromunda. For those who don't know what Necromunda is, here's a bit from the official site:\n
Necromunda is a game of fierce combat between rival gang fighters in the dangerous underworld of the Necromundan hives: ancient and incomprehensibly vast cities, built up layer upon layer, stretching ten miles into a planet's atmosphere. To those who live in the depths, the dark and ruinous Underhive offers every opportunity for wealth and power. Its collapsed caverns conceal the riches of the distant past: rare and precious metals, unfathomable archeotech devices, wondrous mutated fungi and much more. It is also a place of danger, where mutant creatures, renegades and killers hide from the laws of House and hive.
\nTo put things another way, Necromunda is as dystopic as your average apocalyptic tale, just minus the mushroom clouds.

After a little talking on that other thread about how to map the various character types to the setting \u2014 which is very easy, it turns out \u2014 I started thinking seriously about running this thing. Which led me into considerations of how to prep.

Because it has its origins as a wargame, albeit a small scale one, there's a lot of emphasis in the official Necromunda materials on weapons. Apocalypse World doesn't do much with weapons and generally keeps the power level down, which I'm all for. But one of the first questions I'm grappling with is whether I want to create replacement weapons for those listed in the character booklets or just leave them be and just call them by different, Necromunda-specific names.

Beyond weapons, what else do I need to consider? Like I said, the character types work perfectly well in the new setting, so I don't think I'll need to disallow anything. We've even got an explanation for the psychic maelstrom: it's now become exposure to Warp and an invitation to Warp-taint. But surely I'm missing something. It can't be that easy.

    \n Necromunda implies constant violence.
    Apocalypse World implies a constant threat of violence.

    As such, the latter is created so that people can be threatening but not very effective (Battle Babe), and have that still be a functional character concept. I think there needs to be some way to reconcile the fact that AW and Necromunda have different conceptions of how violence works. Just a thought.

    In AW, there is no differentiation between close combat and shooting, aside from weapon choice. In Necromunda, entire gangs take their sense of identity from this distinction. Thoughts?

    The Skinner totally doesn't seem viable in Necromunda.

    You can construct most of the weapons in Necromunda with the weapons tags that exist in AW. I would probably add "plasma" (On a roll of 3 or less, take a wound) and "power weapon" (ignore armor).

    Posted By: FigureFourI think the idea is to play a game in the Necromunda setting using Apocalypse World, not actually play a Necromunda GAME with Apocalypse World. Presumably there's more to life in the Underhive than constant gang warfare, right? I don't think it would be a problem to have characters who aren't as violence focused as in the wargame.
    \nTotally, and I'm on board with that.

    However, to play a Necromunda-inspired game where fighting and/or saddling up to fight wasn't the predominant activity... well, I guess its do-able. But it also seems to miss the point. If I were playing a game in the Necromunda setting and wasn't engaging in a lot of actual fighting, I'd feel disconnected from the source material. I don't recall ever seeing a Necromunda picture where the person wasn't armed and armored, and most are in "battle poses".

    Sure, we want to "broaden the scope" to make it more Apocalypse-World-y. If you have a Hard-holder, focus more on house politics. If you have a Savvy-head, focus on the weird techpriest cult-y lore. In general, focus more on relationships, the threat of violence between gangs, scavenging the wasteland, etc.

    But if we're being honest to the source material, there will be lots of fighting. That'll favor certain classes, endanger others, and ignore others. I can't imagine a genuine place for the skinner in this setting (note the use of "genuine" - I can think of several strained configurations). Is a Battle Babe going to be a useable character class, in a world where "force my hand" is so hard-coded into the fiction?

    I feel like it's a legitimate concern, and could use some thought. Writing the Skinner out of the game is a good first step. Letting someone "Go Aggro" mid-way through a fire-fight is a good second step (and perfectly rational, IMO).

    \n Well I did say "not AS violence focused", since obviously there will still be plenty of violence.

    However, I'm looking through the Necromunda rulebook and the setting info right now and it mentions politics, trade, mining, exploration, settling . . . all sorts of non-violent activities. There are also (a few) illustrations of politicians, merchants and even a guy with a mutant baby. It even says that killing someone inside a settlement can get you and your gang banished, so presumably the settlements aren't constantly in a state of warfare.

    Now, I'm not arguing that gang warfare and violence shouldn't be a BIG part of play (it IS inspired by a wargame after all) nor am I saying that it's not a legitimate concern, just that it's not the ONLY interesting activity happening in the setting and that I'd like to see those other options remain. You'd probably have to discuss they style of play you want for your game before you start. I'd love to play a Brainer as a cultist of Slaanesh with Unnatural Lust Transfixion, but he/she might not get along in a game about brawling gangers. However, he/she probably could have a cool story creeping around the Underhive. I wouldn't write the Skinner out for the same reasons.

    Frankly, if I just wanted to play a game about gang warfare in the Necromunda setting . . . Well, I'd be playing Necromunda. It's exploring those options that aren't represented in the wargame that makes me interested in an RPG.

    Oh, and can't you already go aggro in a firefight?

    Edit: (To maybe contribute a little more) Maybe it would worth considering tweaking the playbooks to fit the more violence prone environment? Make sure everyone can advance their Hard up to +2 if they like, maybe? Maybe even add some new moves to reflect some of the classic Necromunda skills or gear? Heck, +2 Hard and fancy gear can go a long way into making ANY character into a tough guy, and they don't have to all START as tough guys. Juves are pretty wimpy until you get them a few advancements. Choppers and Hardholders are the gang leaders, Gunluggers are heavies, everyone with +1 Hard is a ganger, anyone else is a lame-o Juve.

    Will there be violence? Sure, but the system abstracts that kind of activity such that it'd be foolish to try and replicate the wargame-y aspects of Necromunda. There's not a built in discouragement of combat, but there's definitely encouragement to get that violence done in such a way that story and character remain paramount. I'm okay with this.
