Check this out! It's an adventure I wrote for Trail of Cthulhu, although for me to take singular credit is astonishing in its arrogance. The idea for it came from something that Judd Karlman once said, specifically his "vague idea" for a Lovecraftian adventure about "an aircraft carrier that is circling a classified uncharted island in the Pacific," and the specifics of the horror were shaped by comments from playtesters, particularly folks like Sam Zeitlin, Joanne Charambura, and Frank Hablawi. If you're going to be at Dexcon in July, I plan to run it there for those who might be interested in using it themselves with the kids at home and want to see how it plays out. The pitch is this: "The investigators are sailors and scientists in the South Pacific just after the Korean War, when the U.S. was racing to develop more powerful nuclear weapons. When an atomic bomb test goes awry, they're the only ones who can figure out what's going on and how to stop it." It's Lovecraft meets atomic horror with a Cold War ambiance. I just re-read the whole thing, and the one thing I'll say is that the art is beautiful.
