    \n It was a poorly chosen key, yes. Or lack of key, to be exact. My chosen abode in Stockholm - a youth hostel - had closed for the night by the time I arrived (I'd been hanging out with Ron Edwards, and the night went a bit long), so I decided to become a troll. Or maybe a hobo? Either way, I slept in Helgeandsholmen that night, or tried to.

    This reminds me of another time I tried trollish bridgecraft... I was traversing southern Finland on foot in the early '00s, hiking from Helsinki to Kouvola (and to quite a few places in between, it wasn't exactly a straight route). My hiking logistics were modestly lightweight - a tarp instead of a tent, for instance. As luck would have it, some pretty persistent summer storms plagued my vacation, which necessitated choosing camp sites carefully if I didn't want to spend most of the next day drying up my gear. Naturally I made use of a couple of bridges on the way. This is probably obvious from an armchair perspective: if you actually want to sleep at night, don't set up shop under a highway bridge - or at least bring ear plugs.

    I might take the opportunity for a quick personal conclusion: I haven't felt the need to act trollish for a long time, and part of the reason just might be the fact that the troll's life is not nearly as swell as it's sometimes cracked up to be. You hardly get any sleep, and everybody hates and fears you, and it's not like most bridges have WiFi. If you gotta be a troll, at least don't be a bridge troll - get a cave or live under a tree or something, instead.
