\n I've just launched the preorder fundraiser for Stealing Cthulhu, my book on Lovecraftian storytelling.

I'm proud of this book. It's taken about a year-and-a-half to finish. It's 175 pages of advice on writing horrific scenarios, with annotations by Jason Morningstar, Kenneth Hite and Gareth Hanrahan.

The central idea is: you can write horrific scenarios by copying Lovecraft's ideas, combining them and reusing them. Here are some preview PDFs to give you the idea.
    \n
  • Table of Contents
    • \n
  • Stealing Creatures
    • \n
  • Azathoth and the Colour
    • \n
\nThe art, by Jennifer Rodgers, is completely beautiful. Here's the King In Yellow. (And you can see her Colour illustration in the preview PDFs).



Anyway, do order it if you can. It's $21 plus $7 shipping (about the same as Play Unsafe). Then there are more exciting options, including a hardback edition with added stuff.

    \n Robert, there are no ghouls. The problem is: Pickman's Model is complex. After reading it, I didn't feel I had any brilliant insights to offer.

    So I've deliberately kept the Mythos selection small, sticking only to creatures that gave me good storytelling points. The Mythos part of the book is really tight and focussed. It deliberately doesn't do every Lovecraft creature: it's a selection of examples, not a Monster Manual.

    I'm still a little sad I didn't do ghouls, but I'm really happy with what I've got.
