    \n What exactly is your objective here though? I mean, who do you want at the table and why?

    If it's a case of nostalgia, the you probably want to aim for a game which people are likely to have played. In that case, I really can't think of a better choice than Fighting Fantasy, because almost everyone there over a certain age is going to have played one of those games or books in some form or other.

    If it's a case of "point and laugh at the shitty old game, aren't modern games so much better!" then that's a different kettle of fish and you deliberately want to pick a terrible system like Cyborg Commando or something which actually has the reputation of being terrible and might just get people signing up for that reason. But I think that is going to be harder to pitch and make it sound appealing.

    Or is it just that you genuinely want to just run a game which has that oldschool feel? That's a harder one, but at the end of the day, why is someone going to sign up?
