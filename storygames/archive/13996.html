\n A favorite 70s sci-fi show of mine was notorious for the phrase (or some variation of it), "There's no time to explain." or "I'll explain later."

This of course is a lazy way of writing, but it got me thinking about a game idea where the players create incredible and nonsensical events/scenes with the caveat, "no time to explain."

Of course, before the game is over, someone WILL have to explain it.

I'm not really interested in designing this game, but it made me laugh. You're welcome to explore your thoughts on it.

    \n I can totally see a certain show of timey-wimey, wibbly-wobbly nature being played with only a handful of ritual phrases, perhaps a longer list a number out of which gets distributed randomly between the players and is used as a resource.

    "I reversed the polarity of the neutron flow." (Something technological now does something opposite.)
    "I'll explain later." (Get out of jail free card.)
    "This is my [something] detector, it goes ding when there's stuff." (Ask Y/N questions regarding a specific subject.)
    "Hello, I'm [The Protagonist]" (Distract/block someone from whatever they were doing there.)
    "I'm sorry. I'm so very sorry." (Something horrible is going to happen to you now.)
    "And what happens if I flick this switch?" (Reveal the autodestruct button.)
    "I traveled back in time and bribed the architect." (Introduce a new feature into the surrounding architecture.)

    I think I'll have to go dig out my Tom Baker episodes.
