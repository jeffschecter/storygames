\n Greetings!

I hope everyone had a great Thanksgiving. Now that it is late November and OrcCon 2008 isn't that far away (February 15th-18th) I wanted to send out a call for games as well give you a status report.

The health of the RPG side of the cons is the best it's been in years. Demand for games has increased across every time slot and we're managing to keep up with increased numbers of players thanks to you!

As you already know, we're moving to a new hotel beginning with OrcCon 2008. Our contract with the Westin ended and we've decided to bring the con back to the renovated Raddison Hotel (formerly Wyndham) that hosted the Strategicon conventions for years. In a way, the con is going home after a long hiatus. For the RPG side of the con, everything remains pretty much the same. All RPGs will be hosted in individual hotel rooms, something that most cons don't offer. It's a nice hotel with better parking and I think you folks will be pleased.

We're going to try something different this time by adding a two-hour game slot as well as a six-hour game slot. Besides our 4-hour events run at their usual times, there will be a two-hour event slot added at noon (ending at 2pm) on both Saturday and Sunday. Also, there will be a six-hour event slot (ending at 6pm) starting at noon on Saturday only. We're testing the waters here, making the slots available to those GMs who want to try them out. If they prove popular, we'll expand them. So, to be clear, event slots for the entire convention are as follows:

Friday: 3pm, 8pm, midnight
Saturday 10 am, 12 pm (2 hr), 12 pm (6 hr), 3 pm, 8 pm, midnight
Sunday 10 am, 12pm (2 hr), 3 pm, 8 pm, midnight
Monday 10 am
(Events are 4 hours long unless indicated)

Now, down to business. If you're interested in running games at OrcCon '08, we would love to have you as a GM. Please submit them directly the website by going to www.strategicon.net, sign-in with your user ID (or create an account first if you need to) then Click on "Run an Event" under the "Events" menu on the sidebar. We actually had to turn down events in the last few weeks before the last con because we had filled the schedule! Because of that, please submit your events soon rather than later. We're aware that some of you have already submitted events and are waiting for approvals.

As always, we're interested in all sorts of RPGs, whether they are traditional, indie, involve action-figs, require cool costumes or use kittens as a random number generator. Above all, we want you to have fun with the games you run.

We've made a change in how we track the amount of events you run towards earning a free badge to the con (but don't worry, the free badge requirements are pretty much the same). We've made this change because of different event durations and the shifting numbers of players. GMs are required to run 48 player-hours worth of events in order to obtain a free badge to the con. The formula to figure this out is: the number of player slots offered times the duration of the game in hours. Therefore, having six players in a four-hour game means you've earned 24 hours towards earning a free badge. Another example: 5 players in a 2-hour event earns you 10 hours. If your game doesn't fill, of course you still get credit for those missing players; we're looking at the amount of slots scheduled, not the actual amount of players.

If you need any help or have any questions, please don't hesitate to contact Denys at noldor(at)gmail(dot)com or Paul at ptevis(at)gmail(dot)com

As always, spread the word about the con to any friends and co-workers who might be interested in gaming. Our hobby is always looking for fresh blood and the con is a great place to meet folks with similar interests

Thanks very much for your attention and your games and please stop by and say hi at the con if you get the chance. See you in February!
