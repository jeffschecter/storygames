    \n I use my drivingblind livejournal almost exclusively for talking about Evil Hat as a business and elements of the design process at times. I think it's better to develop this sort of an individual journal than it is to do it in a design community, even if they do exist on LJ (I think they do, but since I don't use them much, their identifiers escape me). I feel this way mainly because I think it's important to build up a readership for what you're doing in a single, reliable location, as a sort of "traffic focuser" and "brand builder". So, certainly, participate in LJ communities that strike your fancy, but keep the main thrust of your designing in the open on your own LJ account.

    The etiquette for LJ friending is there is no etiquette. If you conduct your LJ like a business LJ, you should be mercifully free of the social melodrama that sometimes results from LJ friending dynamics; some folks put way more significance on the process than others. 'Friending', functionally, is saying 'I intend to read this occasionally'. Not even regularly, since folks can (and do) construct Friends List Filter Groups that they use to read the LJs they're most interested in keeping tabs on. Use it as a tool, not as a relationship.

    I think Chad Underkoffler (chadu on LJ) is doing a great job with his in-the-open design blog. I've also become very interested in (perhaps too interested given the amount of design notions I end up bombarding him with) Andrew Kenrick's (littlestkobold @ LJ) blog due in great part to his current design work on an Ordinary Angels RPG.
