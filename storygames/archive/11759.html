\n Hey friends,

If you've played Fiasco, you've probably seen that each playset comes with an Insta-Setup - a list of reliable elements you can mash together to avoid the Setup and just start playing. I'm curious about what you think of these. My thinking was that even if they weren't ever used, they serve as a model demonstrating the kind of experience you might be able to find in the playset. I think the Setup is a huge part of the game's fun, personally, so I'd never advise you to use the Insta-Setup if you have time to build your own. So:

Have you used an InstaSetup?

If not, do you see its utility or is it redundant? Is it informative or entertaining, even if unused? Or do you skip them?

Related question - I've got a set that hinges around a particular epochal historic event, and making certain choices in the Setup will determine whether your game is about that event or just happening at the same time and overlapping in a crazy way. Would you rather:

1. Have the "make it about the event" choices flagged in the playset, maybe with a little symbol
2. Have a second Insta-Setup that combines the best choices to hammer home the event
3. Leave it alone

Thanks for your thoughts!

    \n Jason:

    I think the default approach should be to leave out the I.S.U. altogether - it removes one of the most fun aspects of play and will lead to some less-than-fully satisfying gameplay experiences that could be easily avoided.

    With the sort of playset you describe, though, an I.S.U. would be undeniably beneficial.

    Proposed solution: split your playsets into two different categories, e.g. "Key Event" playsets and "Random Shit" playsets (I'm sure you could think up better names - my kungfu is weak today). For the latter, leave the I.S.U. out entirely; for the former, put one in and include a very short prose narrative describing the event that the I.S.U covers.

    P.S. - I'll be running one or possibly two sessions of Fiasco at Stillcon on March 26th. I'm also working on a homebrewed playset of the "Random Shit" variety based around a small college campus in the Southwest, if you have any use for that sort of thing.

    \n I haven't used the insta-set-up in play, but did use them as a "guide" for what works (this many needs, that many locations etc - reinforcing the text, basically). I could see using the insta-set-up in the future, if we just want to get to screwing our characters over, but at this point my group is loving the whole process of creating the situation. I don't think they were a "step too far" in regards to convenience - in fact it would be very easy to pre-make all the cards for an insta-set-up and just lay them out on the table to share about before the game begins. Have you considered doing some "print and play" cards for the insta-set-ups?
    In regards to the historical set-up, I would probably prefer a suggested "default" but still give all the "Random Shit" (nice term!) so that we could replay later with new (screwier?) events.
