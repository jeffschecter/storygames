    \n A really simple version I've been playing with has been;

    (Get XP as normal for your alignment - alternately, alignment-activation gives +1 Forward)
    At the end of a play session, talk about the session a little and answer the following questions;

    - Did we learn something new about the world?
    - Did we defeat a tough monster or enemy?
    - Did we loot a cool treasure (more than just a few coins or a single magic item)?

    For each, +1 XP to everyone involved. 10xp gains you a level.

    Nice and simple, plays to the strengths of the genre and lets everyone level between sessions. Certainly not the most nuanced approach but it informs play in a "you want XP? better play the game this way!" sort of direction.

    \n Hey, everybody! Played the first session tonight, and it was a hit.
    \n
    \nA played Cadeus, the Elven Fighter. He has a huge , shining, blue sword with hooks and barbs. He has blue hair, and wears all blue.
    \nJ played Lenore, the (male) Human Cleric of St. Gorog, Patron of Knowledge and Hidden Things. Gorog's symbol is the palm-side of a closed fist.
    \n
    \nWe started with Bloodstone Idol, and got just to the main entrance. The Grimtooth and the Icescale were in the middle of a shouting match, so Lenore walked up and Magic Missiled the lead Icescale in the face (Human racial move). A ray of fire shot from his open hand as he clutched his holy symbold, and the Icescale went down for the count.
    \n
    \nThen, Cadeus tried to throw a dagger at a lizardman (failed), vault the rampart (failed), and take a lizardman hostage (failed twice, succeeded soft the third time...he was persistent), while Lenore tried to protect him. Unfortunately, that stand off is how we had to end--Cadeus had just thrown away his sword in order to solidify his grip on an Icescale, and the Grimtooth were just starting to stab away lustily.
    \n
    \nI went with the BBC style of highlighting, but I don't think J quite understood. A ended up with 5 XP because he had Attack and Stunt highlighted, whereas J had Discover and Defend highlighted, and only got one. They really wanted to charge in, swords flashing and missiles flying. I asked several times "Do you want to take a look around, see if you can learn something?" They both went "Psh! Why?" and charged forward.
    \n
    \nI think I'll highlight Attack for both of them, and then give one Parley and the other Discover next time (or maybe Aid?). Because of the XP imbalance, and because of the age difference, I think I'll move to XP-as-party in the future as well.
    \n
    \nAny thoughts on how I can encourage Discovery better?
