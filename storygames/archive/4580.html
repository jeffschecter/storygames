\nThese are the first few paragraphs of an entry that appeared on my blog.

Why not replicate the whole entry here? Well, the subject matter is frankly sexual and the second included image includes explicit content. Rather than offend anyone who might click on this topic (or possibly violate Story Games' community standards), I figured it'd be better to just direct people elsewhere and let any subsequent discussion occur in this space as desired.

Anyway, if you're at all uncomfortable with sex and/or porn... hit the back button and join one of the many, many other worthwhile discussions here. I promise I will not think less of you regardless of your reason for moving on.


Back in 2004 (an eternity ago in porn-time), director Jack the Zipper put out a deeply odd little movie called Squealer. It won a lot of awards and garnered its maker attention he used to spin off from Hustler Video, the financiers/distrubutors of Squealer, and do his own thing. A number of people call the subsequent movement alt porn, but that's a discussion for another post.

At any rate, some have labeled Squealer "horror porn," but that's really a misnomer. There's nothing horrific about the movie, despite the vibe one might get from the box. Rather, the director used the atmosphere and appearance of rural horror films and stories to dress up BDSM scenes that incorporate no violence or gore, implied or explicit. It's no more real than "teacher/student" BDSM scenes or "naughty priest" scenes are real executions of child molestation or the like, but virtually real as a construct within which power exchange and sexual conduct take place.

What does this have to do with roleplaying? Everything and nothing. For some time I've kicked around the idea of using Squealer as a model for an online roleplaying exercise, either PBeM or PBP or PBW. Not as an exercise in interactive erotica per se, but as a vehicle for exploring character and various related headspace issues. Like Squealer, which uses rural horror trappings as a kind of visual grammar within which sex transpires, the game would use both the horror motif and sex as the framework for an examination of character.

    \n Well, where to start. You post brings up a lot of issues and all of them won't fit together so nicely. I'll start somewhere in middle and hope the post will come out right in the end.

    1. About finding players. You talk a lot about the problem of finding willing players and what might be the problem with the players you get. This is a general problem for roleplaying. As soon as you go outside a a certain group of people with a certain amount of shared habits and values regarding play the problems might be just insurmountable. I mean. one of the big things with the indie rpg movement is that they try to learn people how to view rpgs in a new way. The reason why the larp culture is so different between different countries is this: when a certain larp culture is established it is very hard to introduce larps from other larp cultures since the agreed upon habits and allowed subjects of play are so different. In Nordic non-fantasy larp it would be normal to explore subjects like child abuse and incest without more work than otherwise, while I think this woul require much more preparation in most other larp cultures, since these subjects aren't normally seen as kosher (this is my view, based on what I have read, please correct me if I'm wrong).

    But, again about players in your game. You really can't expect good results in a game like this by choosing players that you don't know! I mean, don't even try! Choose people that you either know very well from your ordinary life or choose people who you have seen at forums a lot and have respect for. If you find such people, who also are engaged in you idea I guess you will have a good chance of success.

But I think we all recognize the difference between porn and a story where sex is an important part of character and plot. A couple of years ago I read about a Dutch sex-larp, which was heavily geared towards actual sex between players, but they did a lot of pre-game work to ensure that it would be a larp rather than a just an orgy. Probably it was a bit of both, but the text I read was from one who seemed to have been touched deeply by his character. He had arrived there with heterosexual, but with a character that was a bit ambiguous and ended up being involved only in homosexual activities. (I should add that they also had agreements on how to let the characters have sex, without the players being involved. Those things were decided in the actual situation.)

    3. But finally, if your interest is exploring character from a sexual point of view I don't really understand you choice of inspiration. I have never heard of the movie, but I followed your links and the movie is obviously a rather standard porn production, albeit with a rather more extreem theme. I don't really understand what part of the theme it is you want to bring into a game. It would be interesting to hear some clarifications on that.

    I think generally that if you want to make a story based in sex, but about a set of people it's a good idea not to choose setting and topic after what any given player find sexualy interesting. I'm thinking partly about Shortbus, which is an excellent movie with not too little sex in front of the camera. But it's also a movie that is very careful not to function as porn. I truly liked the movie and got really invested in the characters, (although it is true that the characters are better than the plot; especially one of the plot lines just didn't work, at least not for me) but there was not a second of sexual excitement on my part.     \n I'm essentially using the BDSM model to explain how/why I can consider such an idea without it being construed as my bid for celebrating sexual assault, kidnapping and the like. The abduction/sex scenario is just a framework, as would any fantasy-based BDSM scene and doesn't necessarily constitute an endorsement of actual sexual abuse.

    Anyway, I started thinking about this after watching watching Squealer (natch). It occurred to me that reviewers responded to the rural horror motif more strongly than the situation seemed to warrant, especially since sex was substituted in those places where torture and/or murder would appear in a straight-up horror movie. So, I wondered, what was the same?

    The men are deprived of identity, just as the killers in many horror movies are hidden behind masks or otherwise rendered blank. So the focus is clearly on the victims. Any story one might tell in such a situation, particularly in a roleplaying scenario, would necessarily also focus on the victims. Sex and violence are therefore interchangable elements, while psychological issues stemming from confinement, torment and/or exploitation remain a constant.

    One could easily grab a system like Blood and reenact the sort of thing depicted in the Saw films and get more or less the same result. As I said, some of the character touchstones explored by a game like the farm might be very similar to what I'm thinking about here. From my perspective, though, I'm less comfortable with out-and-out violence of this sort than I am with sex, which is kind of a reversal of most folks.

    I went back and forth for the better part of an hour about whether I'd even cross-post this idea here. Roleplayers in general (and some people) react very strangely to sex and/or sex in games. From a roleplaying standpoint it's either drooling, goofy and borderline offensive drivel like FATAL -- and I include dross like The Book of Erotic Fantasy in that assessment, as well -- or it's absent entirely. As a group roleplayers seem far more comfortable with violence than they are with sex. I'm not sure what that says about us.

    \n I've half-tooled up a system called Stiff: Dead and Pissed which touches on some of the same issues (not ready for consumption by the masses; when it is, you wonderful people will know XD), but kinda turned on its' head.

    I've noticed how comfortable players are with violence in RPGs, even in modern settings with modern themes. The biggest determents seem to be risk of physical harm (i.e. Gamists "losing the game") or police attention (i.e. disruption of the Narrative). So I tried something different. Mechanically, every hit in the game succeeds, and every attack kills the first time. You are completely invulnerable to physical damage as well; there's no mechanics for even being attacked, hurt, stunned, or hindered physically in any way.

    Instead, your character (for reasons that will make more sense when you see a finished outcome) is an absolute emotional wreck, and each roll in combat tests for how well you can cope with the inhuman things you do in the setting. So, killing without consequence is mechanically supported; but inner turmoil makes every Big Decision only matter in headspace. The game is all about exploring innerspace of the mind; it's perhaps a little presumptuous of me, but when I get a finished product out, I think that substituting violence for sex would make the system a very cerebral choice for what you're mentioning.
