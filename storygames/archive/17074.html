I really enjoyed Gen Con this past weekend\u2014mostly. I met cool gamers, played and ran fun games, ate good food, and successfully balanced gaming and chilling. My favorite parts of the weekend, though, were the goth dance parties on Friday and Saturday nights at a club near the convention center \u2026 which is odd, because incidents of creepiness/harassment on both those nights enraged me.

Incident #1: As my friend +Lia Lilley and I walked through the club towards the dance floor, a tall white man wearing a baseball cap, T-shirt, and shorts approached her and asked her name out of nowhere. She introduced herself, and the guy told her she was very beautiful. We slipped away without more conversation; Lia complained that she\u2019d been fielding those kinds of advances all weekend.

For most of the evening, we could see that guy in our peripheral vision, but he didn\u2019t approach, though his eyes frequently and clearly tracked Lia. He didn\u2019t dance, just stood over on the side of the dance floor with a drink while Lia and I danced with the rest of the floor. But when I went inside to get water, he suddenly sidled up to me.

He said hi, introduced himself, and immediately began to interrogate me about Lia. We\u2019re talking full-on Scott Pilgrim have-you-seen-a-girl-with-hair-like-this overdrive. \u201cWho\u2019s your friend? What\u2019s she like? She\u2019s very pretty. But I\u2019m a pretty good-looking guy myself, too. You know how sometimes someone just catches your fancy, and you know they\u2019re different, they\u2019re special? Yeah. It\u2019s like that. She got a boyfriend? Fianc\xe9, really? Huh. So what do you do? Graduate school, eh? Is she an intellectual also? Oh yeah, feminist theory, that\u2019s cool. I\u2019m into that. I was in college once, dropped out though. Now, with the fianc\xe9\u2014is she in love, do you know? Huh. Hey, let me buy you a drink. Okay. I\u2019ll see you around.\u201d And that was it for him, until he came up to me later and asked if I \u201chad any intel for him.\u201d

YEAH WHAT THE FUCK

Incident #2: The following night, my friends and I were all a little more sensitive to the threat of harassment than we had been. Incident #1 may not have quite reached the level of harassment, but goddamn was it creepy. We made sure at least one of us had eyes on each of the rest of us at all times, and searched the club thoroughly whenever we lost track of someone. I was happy to go along with this plan, though I thought it was a little paranoid until, well \u2026

I\u2019m dancing with Lia and a couple of other friends when I see this guy dressed sort of like Alex from the Clockwork Orange film grinding with a very pretty girl wearing mostly fishnets and not much else. I\u2019m just glancing at them, but something in her body language strikes me as a little weird: he\u2019s intensely focused on her, holding her tightly in such a way that she can\u2019t move away from him, while her hands are moving away from encircling him and placing them on the front of his shoulders, as if to push him away (though she can\u2019t because of how he\u2019s gripping her).

Then Alex shoves his hand under her skirt and grabs her ass, really hard. And, like, lifts her up by it. And I turn to Lia and I\u2019m like, \u201cI need your help with something.\u201d

We dance towards the creeper and his prey and I reach out to pat him on the back and say, \u201cHey guys! Come dance with us!\u201d The invitation breaks Alex\u2019s drunken focus for a moment, long enough for his vise-grip on Fishnets to slacken a bit. Lia takes that opportunity to slip between Alex and Fishnets and latch on to Fishnets, dancing close to her and looking as possessive as she can without betraying our rescue mission. Fishnets gets it, thankfully, and snuggles up to Lia. Meanwhile, Alex looks squarely at Lia\u2019s ass, turns to me, points at it, and asks, \u201cSo, uh \u2026 is this yours?\u201d I\u2019m thinking, what, her ass specifically? Yeah, I\u2019m in a co-op with the dudes who own the rest of her body parts. Much as I would love to have vocalized that I only said \u201cyes.\u201d

He then asked, \u201cDo you mind if I \u2026\u201d and gestured towards Lia\u2019s ass, canting his hips forward as if to oh my god this is so gross I have to stop describing it. I just said, \u201cNo, you better not. Yes, yes I\u2019m sure, you really better not,\u201d and danced between Alex and Lia\u2019s ass until Alex walked away to find easier prey. Seriously, I am a noted ass enthusiast and this episode was enough to put me off them for, like, a good ten minutes. Fishnets was very sweet and grateful, and danced with us for a while before heading off. I spent the rest of the night watching Alex intently whenever he appeared, making sure to block the way between him and my female friends, or to draw them away when he started to (ugh) chase after them.

It\u2019s days later and I\u2019m still angry I saw this behavior. I\u2019m angry about all of the stories, many of them much more tragic, which this fiasco brought to mind. I joke about being the party\u2019s social tank, and I\u2019m happy to be, but I\u2019m incensed we need such a thing in the first place. My privilege as a young man also comes into focus here. So far, I have chosen to engage in every physical confrontation in which I\u2019ve been involved\u2014all of them to intervene against violence or harassment toward others. Each time, I have had the luxury of a few seconds to plan how best to protect others and myself. The threat of surprise attack against me is low, relative to that against Lia or other women I know. Violence hasn\u2019t been forced on me whether I wanted it or not since maybe junior high school. But were I to suffer this variety of aggression regularly, I would probably get in a lot of fights and a lot of trouble over it. Lia, on the other hand, has gotten in a lot of fights and a lot of trouble over it. If some guy were to come up and grope me like he did Fishnets, I would probably angrily eject him from the room; but Fishnets mostly just laughed his behavior off like she was accustomed to it and didn\u2019t care to think about it any longer. Which was probably the case.

I struggle to draw any positive, productive conclusion from these incidents. For all the satisfaction with myself I feel for having stopped what I stopped, it sickens me to realize my intervention was necessary. I could fume at the other dancers on that floor, at their not noticing what was happening to Fishnets despite their proximity and staring; but who knows how many similar incidents I\u2019ve failed to notice in the past? Who knows what I would have done were I as drunk as they? I know my own flirting must have unintentionally made someone uncomfortable in the past\u2014am I really so different from these guys?

I guess all I can say is: please don\u2019t stop talking about this problem. I know we all like to think of ourselves as civilized people with civilized friends who are respectful of others\u2019 feelings and differences, people with practiced social skills who know how not to make one another feel uncomfortable or unsafe; but Uncomfortable Questions Man and Alex grew up in the same world as we, around our same species and perhaps even our same cultures. They do not know how to treat women. Which means they do not know how to treat humans. So don\u2019t stay silent. We cannot afford to forget these dangers, lest they grow too close to us before they strike.

E\u1e63u grant me the cunning to guide my friends and close the way against danger.
