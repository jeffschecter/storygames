The first Game Sketch was pretty interesting, I thought, so I'm going to keep doing them.

We got some cool little ideas for games about space exploration and community; creepy forgotten gods; awakened spirits and the nature of the universe; an astronaut and a suicide girl; faeries in the forest; bullets and philosophy; and losers who sold their souls for $40. Wow.

Same basic guidelines as before:
- I post some images from my library of cool images.
- You choose an image and respond with:
-- A game title
-- A very short description of the game (like, 100 words or so).
-- You can also add: "What the players do" and "What the characters do" if you want.
-- A little snippet of a mechanic or reward system or trait name or some stuff that ties in to the image and your game idea.

Think of it as a finger-painting activity for game designers. Just noodle around. Post as little as you want. Comment on other ideas, etc. I figure someone will go charging off into a full-blown game design, but that's really not the point here. Yes, it's okay to post ideas for all the images.

    Fedayeen

    What you need to play: Four or more players and a stool.

    Players place a stool in the middle of the floor and stand around it in a tight circle, right shoulder in, left shoulder out. They close their eyes and wait. When any player feels it's time, he or she will recite one line of the following, starting with the first:

    "Eyes to shadow
    Knife to cloud
    Moon to iron
    Face to God."

    It's okay for two or more players to say a line simultaneously.

    When the last line is spoken, someone will put his or her right hand down, audibly, on the stool. Other players will place their hands down on top of the first hand and then each other, each with an audible (but gentle) clap.

    When there have been as many claps as there are players, the last player to place his or her hand will speak a sentence. This sentence must state what the Fedayeen and the Infidel were doing (eg "The Fedayeen was sleeping, and the Infidel was hunting"). At the end of the sentence, the player removes his or her hand from the stool.

    Subsequent sentences must begin with either a "But" or an "And," and follow with "the Fedayeen" or "the Infidel," describing the way in which the Fedayeen hunted for the Infidel. Again, after speaking, the player removes his or her hand.

    The last player's sentence must begin "I am the Infidel, and this is how I died..."

    When the last sentence is spoken, repeat the recitation of the verse, as before. When it is finished, the players open their eyes.

    I Heart Girls Kendo Forever

    You play your high school's Girls Kendo Team competing in a tournement. It's important that your win your own kendo match, but it's more important that the team as a whole does well. Ultimately, it's not the individual performances that matter, but your overall team score. You could technically lose every match and still win overall.

    So some of the scenes are kendo matches, but 2/3 of the scenes are the girls hanging out, watching other matches, bickering with girls and boys from other schools. It's like Bring It On with wooden swords. And the actions you take during the in-between time add modifiers that help or hinder your teammates or their opponents in the kendo matches. A little bit of encouragement or stealing someone else's boyfriend could make or break your team's chances in the tournement.
