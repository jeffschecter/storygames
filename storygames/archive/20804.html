    \n I picked it up Friday mostly out of curiosity.

    The creator *definitely* references Apocalypse World in the credits as "inspiring the dice mechanics and the approach to light-weight, deadly, story-focused gaming," as well as other sources.

    I've not played it yet, but the central mechanic *does* feel something like DW/AW's. Every roll has the possibility of changing the current situation. There's no 'whiff' factor whatsoever. Every time you roll, you either (a) establish that you get what you want (b) get what you want at cost or (c) your opponent gets what *they* want, all in some combination. That said, it's not the *same* mechanic, just similar. Also, the abstracted money solution the game uses felt *very* much like AW's.

    Everything mechanically in the game seems to be about establishing Details, which are like Fate's Aspects. They're established facts about the scene you're in, and can be attached to PCs/NPCs or the scene itself.

    Ultimately, It's much more World of Dungeons than Dungeon World. There's no concept of Moves or the like. It's very OSR in its 'rulings not rules' approach.
