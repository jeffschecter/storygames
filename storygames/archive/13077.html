Can I ask a favor of this wonderful community? What games have interesting mechanics for inter-character (PC and NPC) relationships? I'm looking for ideas for a project of my own, so the more and more varied things you can suggest, the happier I'l be.

\n

I've got things like the following in mind: Apocalypse World's Hx stat, Dogs in the Vineyard's Relationships, Trollbabe's rerolls-through-relationships, or maybe even 3:16's group character creation.

\n

So, what games do you think have interesting mechanics for this sort of thing? Why do you like them?

    \n In How We Came To Live Here, part of setup is creating your village, which is a web of interrelationships. Changing the tenor of those connections later in the game has a cost associated with it - you can "spend" your earned influence to turn a angry, negative relationship into a neutral one, and later a positive one for example. So the village web has an informational component and also a tactical one. You constantly reference it to establish and drive scenes, and changing it is part of how you pursue your character's agenda.

    \n I wrote this which has some explicit mechanics for PC to PC interaction. The gist of it is, letting someone help you cost you 'HP-equivalent' but gives you an advantage- the more you trust them the bigger the bonus. If they screw you over, they get that same, fat advantage.

    Mountain Witch was the inspiration, really, which is another example you can look at.

    \n Smallville's relationship mechanics are core to the resolution of contests in the game, which is something I haven't seen much of before. The game substitutes ratings in Relationships and Values (such as Power, Truth, Justice, Glory) for what would traditionally be attributes and skills. In other words, any time a character tries to achieve something that dice are called on to resolve, they're going to be picking up a Relationship die and a Value die and rolling them. Relationships (and Values) also have player-authored statements defining what they are, which may be forwarded or challenged during play and thus reinforcing their importance.

    Cam
