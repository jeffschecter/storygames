    \n They're just different editions of the game - same thing with some slight additions. Ben's sometimes a bit too hipster for his own good, that naming convention for the editions and the ambiguity about when the game's "complete" has not done best service for customer confidence In reality you can just buy the most recent printing, whatever it is right now, and be happy with that. The game's been perfectly buyable and playable since the Ignition Stage (assuming that's the black-and-white softcover book Ben printed like three years back). It is a major rpg work both theoretically and artistically, so I recommend it freely to anybody who thinks they might be interested in teenager romance, giant robots and a bleak, hopeless apocalypse. A great game for our generation (meaning mine and Ben's; I seem to remember that you're somehow fossilized, Sam).
