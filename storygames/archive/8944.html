\n I'm curious on what people's expieriences/reactions/reflections on Conspiracy of Shadows was.

I'm mostly familar with the first edition. Its neat because it satisfies that strange craving for *stuff* that motivates my vague interest in traditional rpgs, but that *stuff* is largely neat *stuff* that is burrowed from TROS & Sorcerer. Neat! I also found character creation to be a real hoot, even if it wasn't as high-concept as a lot of indie rpgs at the time.

The setting was generic dark fantasyland(yawn) but in eastern/northen europe I think(Maybe not yawn?). I didn't have a clue how to make a conspiracy really interesting while reading it, I think most of the advice in the book was about how not to be a cockblocking asshole. I played in two campaigns of it, both of which had solid starts and self-destructed because of other players happening to wig out at the time. Maybe its hella cursed.

The second book sounded like it had more hippy dippie mechanics that sounded interesting, but I also heard it lost some of its 'spark', whatever that means. I'd invite elaborations on that by anybody who expressed such.

So what was your CoS buisness like?

    \n Sean,

    I take no offense what-so-ever to the lack of interest in the setting. I have this obsession with it. I have notebooks of notes, and dynastic family trees, and illustrations on the shit (all of which are contributing to my art-hause type project for it).

    I guess I am unhappy with how it is set up to handle set-up to conflicts (I call it unopposed conflicts). I would also love to figure out a way to add the endurance portion of the first edition conflict rules, but I don't know if they are really needed. I just like the idea of limited resources forcing tactical choices. I've been toying with the idea of some sort of ticker to reflect that (like say 6 chips at the start of a conflict, with chips representing a purchase of an action type, with a refresh rate of one per Exchange, and maybe some non-action stuff to gain more).

    The rules to run the sessions were tested pretty well by my internal group, but no external testing (which is where real testing comes from). I am confident that they work pretty much as intended, but until I get some more feedback I really won't know for sure.

    If you get a hankering to give it a go and send me a report or post some shit online and let me know, I'll send you and your group some free shit (art prints and comics). I got boxes of the crap lying around since I stopped selling shit and went all socialist with my work.

    Keith
