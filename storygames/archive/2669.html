    \n As Andy says, there's an advantage in that the accounts are in one place, so it's less hassle for people signing up who are existing Indie fans. Your CE forum has a similar advantage. You also get the passing traffic effect (from the posts since lst visit effect) and it's more neutral as a location so people are more open to critiquing or contributing than if it's your site.

    Disadvantage wise , it breaks the users context of use, which can be a problem as it's jarring and confusing for people expecting a single site (and for the love of Pete don't open the forum in a new window). It also means you may get less traffic from folks who don't like the atmosphere wherever your forum is.

    If you want to chat about site stuff more generally, drop me a mail.

    \n That's cool. I would say the decision is based more on what audience you're looking for in your forums. You'll get more drop-ins at the Forge (and more traffic), you'll get more focused folks at your website.

    \n What does it take to get a dedicated forum on the Forge, these days? When i last enquired about it, oh, probably 5 years ago [i think it was 2002--did the Forge exist then?--it would've been a year or two after we produced our beta edition of Four Colors al Fresco], Ron's response was that they didn't create publisher-specific fora proactively, but only in reaction to a certain, noticable, level of traffic about a particular game. So, talk about your game on the Forge, and if enough people start talking about it, they'll create a dedicated forum. Me, i (1) don't communicate comfortably, effectively, or satisfactorily in the style that the general Forge forum rules dictate*, and (2) am pretty much an introvert, so i'm not likely to start discussions so much as respond to them. So that strategy just isn't gonna work for me.

    *In case anybody cares: 1: i proceed from the abstract to the concrete. Asking me to ground an issue in a specific example often won't work (because, early in the discussion, i won't know how it relates to concrete examples), and i can discuss purely-hypothetical abstract stuff for ages before suddenly i recognize a relevant concrete application. Likewise, you can give me concrete applications 'til the cows come home, and i might never be able to extrapolate the common theoretical explanation being illustrated. [This learning style issue is what killed me in Chem.] 2: i find the frequent dependency of threads really frustrating to read. If thread A is sufficiently relevant to discussion B that B starts off with something like "This is a followup to A" and a link to same, that pretty much says to me "i should go read A before reading B. At which point, I'd rather that it was simply a continuation of A, not a new thread. I.e., i see thread necromancy as not merely not-bad, but actively good. 3: In concert with abstract-to-concrete thinking pattern, i'm also more random [than sequential], meaning my best ideas often start out with a pointless "hey, i just thought of this!" sort of idea, which, in dialogue with others, evolves into a something specific to say. IOW, if i wait until i have a concrete, supportable point to make before i start talking, i often just won't ever start talking. Which is the other half (along with the introversion) of why i respond to more than begin discussions--because somewhere around 80 posts into the discussion is when my own ideas gel for me. 4: I'm a slow thinker. Rarely will a new idea enlighten me, or a new POV persuade me, in less than a week or so of ruminating over it. Which gets back to the thread necromancy thing, again--I'm usually ready to contribute to a thread about 48hrs after everyone else has tired of it. Plus, i usually am verbose, short on time, and like to consider my own posts. So i prefer to write something, then sit on it for a day or two before posting, making sure it seems worth contributing, and editing and tightening it up. I'm trying to break that habit in fora that have a generally-faster turnover time (which, these days, is all of them. How i miss UseNet).

    Hmmm...kinda got carried away there. Well, in defiance of my general proclivities, i'll post it anyway.
