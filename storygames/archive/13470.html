\n Here is one thing I don't really get about SageFight. It's best illustrated with an example.

At GenCon, we were playing SageFight as a group. I switched stance. Kreider, who was standing beside me, also switched stances, but crucially kept moving for slightly longer than me, allowing her to touch the back of my hand.

Now, admittedly, Kreider is a notorious cheat. That said, how do you know when to stop moving in SageFight?

    \n For as long as the exclamation mark at the end of "Sage!" (or "Fight!") lasts.

    Alternatively: Stop before the next "One!".

    Alternatively 2: Stop when your enemy stops. (which makes unseen, unexpected attacks the best)


    Ok, here's how I do it: It should be one quick smooth movement that you can extend for as much as you're physically able (people with long arms are at an advantage). Timing wise you can move for as long as your perceived enemy moves, you both stop when you're not physically able to extend anymore (which leads to people overreaching, losing balance and falling out).

    If she changed her move after you stopped moving (switched the angle, direction or of her movement etc.) it shouldn't count because it wasn't one quick smooth movement that lasted for as long as your movement.

    But seriously it's not like Sagefight is a super-balanced game without some leeway as to what's allowed.
