Well, Claudia and Michele played six demo (from the three planned), and even got one of the players in one of the demo (who had already read PTA but not played it) to demo it for other people in a THIRD demo table! For a total of 33 players! (25 for PTA and 8 for DitV)

    \n
    \n I suppose that at least some of that can be attribuited to the information fallout from a serie of polemics about the indie commercial potential I posted this year in the biggest gaming newsgroup in Italy, it.hobby.giochi.gdr
    \n
    \nThe timetable was like this:
    \n
    \nIn may 2006, after a long period of absence from gaming discussions (that coincided to the last phase of my growing dissatisfaction with "traditional" gaming and my "discovery" of Indie games), I returned to posting with a long (and rather polemic with tradition gaming sacred cows) article about Dogs in the Vineyard, that caused a lot of polemics and was reported and copied in many italian gaming forums and pushed considerably the visibility of Indie games in Italy (that until them were rather "out of the maps" in terms of public discussions)
    \n
    \nI can't say I was the first one to push Indie games in Italy. Far from it. People like Lorenzo Trenti, Fabrizio Bonifacio, Alessandro Ricc\xc3\xb2 and others did write reviews and played demo of a lot of indie games years before that (Ricc\xc3\xb2 GM my first DitV game at a convention, for example, and my very first indie game was GM by Bonifacio at that same conventions years before). But none of them is very active in public discussions in newgroups. They don't do polemics. I do. (and I am rather (in)famous for this) I posted literally hundreds of post with data, and numbers, to deny a lot of misconceptions and attacks from some "traditional" gaming purists in these threads and discussions. (discussions and polemics that still rage on after almost eighteen months!)
    \n
    \nMore than that, I tried to frame the games in the context of the forge phenomenon, both in the social (a community of design) and theorical (The Big Model) aspects, and not as single isolated games. Writing even a serie of posts about gaming theory (that, as you can imagine, created even more polemics...)
    \n
    \nIn july 2006, following a serie of contemptous comments from a couple of people about "vanity press" (they were the usual comments made even in the american forums, nothing new here) I posted a link to the "Is the RPG industry screwed" article on the pelagrane press site, and started reporting about the sales of title like Dogs in the Vineyard or Spirit of the Century, and the sales of the Forge Booth at Gencon. With other articles and links about the publishing model used (I think that, until that time, every gdr title published in Italy was done in the "traditional" way with rather high numbers of copies printed by traditional presses, but I am not 100% sure. This was the image of "how you should publish rpgs", anyway), about digital printings, Lulu, Indie Press Revolution, etc.
    \nPeople have said to me, afterwards, that these articles made them think about self-publishing rpgs as a viable opportunity even in Italy...
    \n
    \nAt that time my good friends Claudia Cangini and Michele Gelli already had decided, even before of these posts, to try to publish these games in Italy. They worked in publishing and editory for years, working both in the biggest entertainment industry (Books, Comics, DVDs...) and, by the side, in RPG games and magazines, and they wanted to bring these games to Italy. It took a very long time to close the contracts, and it's for this reason that they could publish the first game (PTA) only in september 2007, losing a little of their "early start" advantage.
    \n
    \n(by the way, when I asked Michele some months ago about the articles I was postings, because I had heard of people literally checking my post and copyng down the game titles I cited to get lists of people to contact, and I thought about stopping citing titles to avoid giving info to his competitors, his answer was "don't worry. The more, the merrier, and there are enough good games for everybody")
    \n
    \nThe posts, the polemics, the informations posted raised a lot the indie visibility in Italy, and this already created interest both in the public and in the publishing houses. Then, in September 2007, last month, the fever reached a new level with the publication of Avventure in Prima Serata (Primetime Adventures) and its presentation at Modcon. Because it was really a BIG success, both in copies sold and in the "buzz" around the convention, proving that there IS interest and curiosity for these games in many italian players. So, I suppose that at this moment even the publishers that still had not done so are thinking about adding "some of that indie stuff" to their catalogue...

    \n It seems Indie Games visibility is going to be further increased since our DitV's italian editions was nominated for Lucca's "Best of Show" award in the roleplaying game category!
    Lucca Comics & Games is the main italian gaming convention and "Best of Show" is one of the most famous italian awards for games.
    There are 3 nominations for each category (you can see all the nominations here) and DitV is one of those for "roleplaying games" so this is pretty cool!

    Winners should be announced in a littel while, but I think even just the nomination is a very good result for a game yet to be released by a publisher that started releasing in September.

    All my thanks to those who appreciated our edition!
