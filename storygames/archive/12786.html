    \n Man, do I hate news, but okay.

    I have my clock/radio tuned to the local A.M. constant news/weather/traffic channel. So I hear weather and traffic and salmonella egg recalls during the morning routine. If I need the details of the salmonella egg recall I research them on the website of that radio station when I get to work. If I hear another Detroit corruption story that I can't ignore, I research it on the websites of the local print newspapers.

    In between work if there's nothing grabbing me on Story Games or The Forge, and I'm not in an interesting IM conversation, I'll check out RPG.net. I collect RSS feeds for game designers and people I know in Bloglines, but I only look at them maybe once a week. I probably look at Reddit a few times a week, but I mostly hate it. I sometimes read Slate.com. During the election season I read electoral-vote.com and fivethirtyeight.com.

    I try to avoid the evening news because it's always about kids dying in house fires because they couldn't get out through the security bars and society-wracking narcissistic entitlement behaviors by corporate executives and elected officials; the nightly tragedy of our American d\xe9gringolade makes it too hard for me to sleep.

This tradition entails drinking beer and watching all of the episodes of The Daily Show and The Colbert Report for the past week, with frequent pauses to Google shit (or as we refer to it, "consult the Ancestors"), get distracted by random side conversations, and usually, as the night wears on, argue about philosophy and/or watch random 80s music videos. It's a long, demanding process, but we're professional-grade drinkers, so sometimes we even manage to make it through all eight episodes.
