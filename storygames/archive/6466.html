    \n I am a huge fan of Chad Underkoffler's Truth & Justice and Zorceror of Zo games using the PDQ system. I think that would be an easy choice, because it encourages descriptors to give you bonuses to actions, and PDQ can be played at a really simple level (for example, ignore the rules for normal damage versus super damage and just make it one kind of damage). T&J is all about super heroes and has a very comic-book feel. ZoZ is a kind of fairy tale mashup.

    www.atomicsockmonkeypress.com

    Both are excellent games. You can find my reviews of them on RPG.net by searching for Michael Erb or merb101.

    \n Truth and Justice is pretty solid.
    \n
    \nYou might try Hearts & Souls (my supers game) because it uses a fairly easy to learn system and "take high die", rather than adding--which of course depends on her understanding numbers somewhat (and can help teach them, though teaching adding can work too if you use T&J). The reason I bring up H&S is if you want to succeed, you can keep trying without accounting points (mostly), this allows a child to say "I want to save people because of X" or the like in character and get rerolls of bad die results. This is a little more intuitive to me, than spend Hero points to get better results.
    \n
    \nHowever, Truth and Justice is a SOLID game engine and better at making her character EXACTLY about what she wants. (Since it has "Do it yourself traits.") and admittedly I aimed at a higher level of vocabulary for my expected audience. Though both my nephew and niece have played--my niece even ran a game before she was 9.
    \n
    \nTruth & Justice can be ordered online PDF or print or found in some FLGS, while H&S in print is only at Lulu.
    \n
    \nhttp://www.lulu.com/content/374993
    \n
    \nIf you want links to Truth & Justice I can dig some up as well. It's a worthy game.
