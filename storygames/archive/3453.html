\n Okay, check-it-out ... here are the Personalities (each with three behaviors) and Roles (each with three motives) that I'm looking at for the next beta-run of Misery Bubblegum. What I'd love-love-love is if people could pick some series or story that I've got decent odds of knowing (which means sci-fi/action/fantasy type TV, but almost all Anime) and see how they would fit the characters into this creation system. Just pick a personality and a role for the character, such that they'd earn as many cards as possible just by being themselves.
\n
\nI want to know several things: Is it easy? Is it fun? Is it inspiring (i.e. do you want to start playing the characters)?
\n
\nIf the answer to any of those is "No" then I really need to know what gives you trouble. If "Yes" then I wouldn't refuse some specifics of what you found cool, but they're not critical.
\n
\nAnd if someone who knows the series can do Kare Kano for me ... I'd do it myself, but I actually think that having someone else stat up the characters would give me a sense of whether I've successfully conveyed the archetypes (in which case the answers should look familiar to me) or not (in which case ... they won't).
\n
\n
\nPERSONALITY: DRAW A CARD WHEN YOU:
\n
\nFeisty
\n
\n- Defy someone more powerful than you
\n- Break or bend the rules
\n- Choose conflict over cooperation
\n
\nLonely
\n
\n- Talk with someone one-on-one
\n- Leave a group of two or more other people
\n- Let someone else have the last word
\n
\nNaive
\n
\n- Miss the obvious subtext
\n- Trust someone implicitly
\n- Cause troubles for your friends
\n
\nVain
\n
\n- Lie about yourself
\n- Review your past actions
\n- Push someone else out of the spotlight
\n
\nNaughty
\n
\n- Disregard advice
\n- Shock a group of two or more other people
\n- Tempt someone else to behave badly
\n
\nObedient
\n
\n- Do something unpleasant or embarrassing
\n- Talk about the rules
\n- Make excuses for authority figures
\n
\nSelfish
\n
\n- Ignore someone
\n- Have the last word in a conversation
\n- Resent advice
\n
\n
\n
\nROLE: DRAW A CARD IF THE REASON YOU ACT IS:
\n
\nPrince(ss)
\n
\n- You want to be flawless
\n- You want others to follow your example
\n- Some people are better than others
\n
\nArtist
\n
\n- You want to do something totally new
\n- You want others to understand what you're doing
\n- If something's true that means it's important
\n
\nJock
\n
\n- You want a fair competition
\n- You want to give it your all
\n- Quitters never win, winners never quit
\n
\nScrapper
\n
\n- You want to fight for what you care about
\n- You don't want to fight anyone you sympathize with
\n- Passion is more important than patience
\n
\nSlacker
\n
\n- You want to savor the moment
\n- You want to be accepted as you are
\n- Sometimes you just have to let things go
\n
\nBookworm
\n
\n- You want people to think things through
\n- You want to understand
\n- Structure is a good thing
\n
\nCheerleader
\n
\n- You want to make others happy
\n- You want to be honest
\n- Everyone should strive for their dreams
\n
\nGeek
\n
\n- You want to show your expertise
\n- You want to share people's excitement
\n- Your interests are important to everyone
\n

    \nI'm a lot happier with 'slacker' for Shinji if you divorce it from 'dreamer' as you suggested, incidentally. Ditto for Misato. Predictably enough, it's a lot easier to pick the appropriate personality descriptior (based on actions) than the role descriptor (based on thoughts).
    \n
    \nAs a reaction to the exercise: it's easy and satisfying when you hit on the right combination of descriptors for a character, but frustrating when none of the options seem to fit. Eva was pretty easy, probably because it devotes so much attention to playing up the characters' flaws. Here are attempts at a couple of series that gave me more trouble:
    \n
    \nThe Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya:
    \n
    \nHaruhi Suzumiya: Selfish/feisty/naughty? princess/artist/jock/dreamer?
    \nMikuru Asahina: Obedient cheerleader
    \nYuki Nagato: Lonely bookworm
    \nItsuki Koizumi: Lonely geek
    \nKyon: Lonely/selfish/obedient? slacker
    \n
    \nI think things get distorted here because Haruhi is the focus of so much of the attention (and the other characters spend so much time talking about her that they all end up as good candidates for the 'lonely' descriptor from all those one-on-one conversations), but I'm sure it should be possible to pin her down a bit more precisely than that.
    \n
    \nMagical Girl Lyrical Nanoha (first series):
    \n
    \nNanoha Takamachi: Feisty cheerleader
    \nFate Testarossa: Obedient princess
    \nYuuno Scrya: Naive geek?
    \nChrono Hallaoun: ? jock?
    \n
    \nThe latter two were difficult, and what I came up with feels weak. As Sidney pointed out, authority figures are difficult to do (Chrono's role in this series---I feel he needs to be somewhere between Fiesty and Obedient, but neither fits properly), and most of Yuuno's screen time is in the context of supporting others, which doesn't seem to be covered by any of the personality descriptors.

    \n
    \nYumi Fukuzawa: Naive cheerleader

    \nSei Satou: Naughty cheerleader?

Particularly, it feels like there should maybe be a 'devoted' or 'lover' for characters whose attentions focus particularly on a specific other character. Or maybe you're intentionally avoiding that?

      \n Well, I ran the game with these roles and personalities last night for my fun play-group. I set them in "Hunter Academy" ... essentially a boarding school dedicated to training the next generation of monster hunters ... they offer a broad curriculum, with athletics (i.e. Slaying), academics, science (MAD science, that is), and support for actualizing psychic and magical heritage. But mostly it's all about the athletics, because everybody's got a thing for the idea of beating on the monsters with fists and crowbars and stakes. Sorta like a Quidditch-happy Hogwarts as envisioned by Joss Whedon.
      \n
      \nEric and Jen drew randomly from the decks, and got Vain-Artist and Vain-Dreamer respectively. Eric played a stuck up rich Mad Scientist (Aidan) and Jen played a stuck up, poor, clueless girl with no abilities of any sort (Vicky) ... not that she would ever, ever admit that. The combination of two people with Vain was awesome. Watching them try to shove each other out of the spotlight never lost its appeal. I was also quite entertained that they gravitated to playing their vanity in completely different ways. Jen had Vicky pushing and shoving to (finally) take over the nominal "leadership" of the team. She wanted to get her attention by being dead center of the action. Eric, by contrast, kept insisting that he wasn't part of the team at all ... but conceded that maybe he'd help them just this once, for kicks. He wanted to get his attention by making a big deal about being an outsider. It was very cool, and seemed very natural and believable.
