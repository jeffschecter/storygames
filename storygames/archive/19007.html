\n I was playing a game a couple of weeks ago where the Witch (actually it was an Infernal with Hex powers) said he was casting Wither face to face. At that point, three of the players around the table said, one after another, 'I hate Wither'.

The point they had was that Wither, when cast on a PC, was like an instant 'completely screw over the character' move - that just had to succeed on a dark roll (and a 10+ allows the Witch to keep it there as long as they like). Also that Hex casting was a regular move to be taken for other characters (as the Infernal had here).

The suggestion was made afterwards that perhaps Hex casting could be nerfed so that:
- the caster always needs to use a sympathetic token to Hex (i.e. you remove the section about them being able to Hex face to face) - this would also mean that other skins have to take 2 Witch moves to use hexes in normal situations
- unless the caster is Darkest self in which they can use face to face and sympathetic tokens (thus keeping the terrifying monster aspect of the Witch's darkest self)

Are there any other ways that you've used to either narratively or legislatively reduced or limited Hex casting?

    \n Wither
    A person loses all of their hair, or their teeth start rotting and falling out, or their period comes and it comes by the bucketful, or their skin gets all sickly yellow and spotty. Whatever the specifics it's bad.

    So, technically no mechanical effect though in narrative it could screw you over. Most likely on the social level and how people react to you. Of course are the effects temporary or curable? That is is for the group to decide, and might make a good sub-plot in the story or campaign.

    In addition many of the skins could be given in-story reasons to make such an ailment temporary. Do your Vampires regenerate? Do werewolves and Ghouls do the same? Can your infernal make a dark pact to be healed? Does the Chosen's library have a cure and a way to ward off future badness? Can a Ghost truly be maimed in such a way more than temporarily? Is the Fey's body so infused with Glamour that it can be healed by a trip to the Wyld?

    Granted a few of the skins are harder to come up with ways to circumvent the ill effects. Also, of note is that in-story if you do something like that you have basically painted a big target on yourself. Which is awesome! Cause now you have the PC's working at odds to each other strewing their drama and messy lives all over and catching poor bystanders in the fallout.

    Try explaining it this way:

    Anyone can go down to Walmart, buy a big kitchen knife, and then cut someone's face open with a volatile roll. But that's an extreme action that will, one would hope, provoke a lot of consequences. Doing so with spooky dark magic? Even more so! I mean "local teens' faces mysteriously melt off" is the kind of news that attracts attention I suspect the Infernal would not appreciate.
    \nThis is key.

    Wither is visible and scary. If you cast Wither in person, chanting in tongues, you're likely to suffer some pretty serious consequences. The Witch is all about delayed vengeance, and any time a Witch is casting spells in the moment it's bound to end up poorly for them.

    Let's say I stare at you and chant in tongues. The MC says, "Yeah, I think your hair falls out, and your teeth start to ache. What do you do?" Your reaction is not going to be pretty.

    Mechanically: Wither doesn't typically do harm (though I can imagine an MC deciding that it does 1-harm based on circumstance), and the PC isn't in charge of the specific effects. It's got the potential to ruin someone physically, but it's probably going to trigger a spiral of reactions.

    Wither and Binding are both really powerful spells, but if a Witch is casting them in person it probably won't end well for them. If you're MCing, it's your job to make sure that's true.

    \n So many thoughts on this. I should preface all this by saying that I love Monsterhearts, it's a truly beautiful game, but I haven't actually played or run it yet.

    @EpistolaryRichard, I can completely understand why your players might hate Wither. Not because of any mechanical effects, but because of the way it rewrites your character description, seemingly permanently. It makes you ugly, in a game about sexy teen romance. I know a lot of players would not like having their character concept altered like that, and I can appreciate why. If my character concept is the beautiful popular Queen, and now she's yellow, spotty, and bald, it's like I've lost the character I wanted to play. It's way worse than any amount of Harm, and the rules don't give you any way to heal from it. \n
    Anyone can go down to Walmart, buy a big kitchen knife, and then cut someone's face open with a volatile roll.
    \nThis opens a whole other can of worms. Anyone can cut someone's face open (or cut out eyes, tongue, fingers, etc.), but most players wouldn't do it to another PC. Not, I think, because of the consequences to their character, but because of a social contract that you respect other players' characters, that they control their own actions and description.

    In most games I run, if a player wants to cut out someone's eye, tongue, fingers, etc., I would likely say "This game doesn't give any rules for cutting off someone's fingers, the violence is abstracted as Harm. Let's not go there." The Wither move seems to suggest that we do go there. It puts permanently disfiguring or maiming another PC on the table.

    What should Wither actually be able to do? The examples listed in the move (hair falls out, teeth start to rot, blotchy, yellow skin), are all pretty minor, cosmetic, and not necessarily permanent. But the example of Wither in the book has the Witch making someone's tongue fall out. If you made a PC unable to talk, they would become much weaker and harder to play, and it sure sounds permanent (barring a magical intervention there are no rules for). This is an extreme example of a Witch in Darkest Self against an NPC, but what should Wither be able to do to a PC?

    When you inflict Harm on someone (say with a big kitchen knife), can you also describe a permanent disfigurement or physical impairment that you give them? If you cut out their eyes, tongue, or fingers, do we treat that as a condition, or make a custom move for them? Does that disfigurement or disability heal like Harm or conditions do, or can it only heal by in-fiction magical intervention? \n
    In addition many of the skins could be given in-story reasons to make such an ailment temporary. Do your Vampires regenerate? Do werewolves and Ghouls do the same? Can your infernal make a dark pact to be healed? Does the Chosen's library have a cure and a way to ward off future badness? Can a Ghost truly be maimed in such a way more than temporarily? Is the Fey's body so infused with Glamour that it can be healed by a trip to the Wyld?
    \nIf some Skins can regenerate from permanent disfiguring wounds (whether from Withering or from big kitchen knives), and others can't, those Skins become much more powerful. Are the Skins meant to be equal in power? \n
    But that's an extreme action that will, one would hope, provoke a lot of consequences. Doing so with spooky dark magic? Even more so! I mean "local teens' faces mysteriously melt off" is the kind of news that attracts attention I suspect the Infernal would not appreciate.
    \nOkay, so there's a spiral of reactions, and it's not going to end well for the Witch, but that doesn't help the PC who got Withered.

    It seems to me that what makes Wither so nasty is that it's permanent (or seems to be). I would probably treat it like a condition, and allow it to be healed as a condition. Does that risk making it too weak?

    (I can also see how Hypnosis is just as bad, if not worse, because it doesn't give any limits to the service the Vampire can demand. As written, it seems like the Vampire can command you to "serve me forever", or "cut off your own hands". And if they get a 7-9, you still do it, only now you're crazy too!)

    \n The hexes are explicitly temporary, aren't they. PCs should be able to spring back eventually, even if it means wearing a wig for a month or whatever.

    Remember, we're modeling sexy teenage monster fiction. When the characters get slashed in the face or a tooth knocked out, they might be wearing makeup for an episode, but then it's gone.

    That said, MonsterHearts is a game where we should be encouraging players to scar each other with kitchen knives.

    On hypnotism: It's a problematic move--but intentionally so. Breaking lines of consent is an important part of the Vampire Skin, and that move is central to it. As an MC I might set boundaries to what hypnotism can do, but I'd avoid it when I could and always phrase those limits in terms of scope rather than power.

    You've hypnotized them, and they'll serve you forever... until tomorrow morning when it wears off.

    \n Should have commented on this ages ago (not least because I was at the table moaning about Wither :-) )

    I've not read anything that suggests Hexes are explicitly temporary, but it's a while since I've read them.

    You could House Rule that they are, you could House Rule supernatural healing for your PC's, or you could decide a subplot to cure them I guess but in the games I've played Wither tends to be used as a punishment Hex from a distance which fits in well with the petty vengeance of the Witch, but the target character is often played with no idea what their part in becoming a target was, or necessarily where the attack has come from which leaves them little option to react. Also I guess the petty nature of the vengeance doesn't sit comfortably with the level of the punishment in the fiction.

    We tend to play fairly na\xefve games where the existence of monsters is down played, in most of the games I've played each monster has initially imagined it was alone in being monstrous among its classmates and so the concept of instance revenge and escalating violence hasn't really happened. Also, often, the skins involved include some fairly mundane options, there's not a lot a Mortal or a Queen can do to directly retaliate against an attack like that, even if it happens right in front of them. Also, most of the games are either one shots, or two to three sessions where again the concept of creating a side plot to undo the spell doesn't really work with the pacing and if that becomes the 'must do thing' for a character whose beauty is a major aspect then it risks taking them out of the rest of the game somewhat.

    As it stands I'm on the 'not a fan' side of things, I know there is the 'monsters as superheroes' style of Monsterhearts play where things can escalate quickly, but I'm much more on the 'monsters as tortured teenagers' side of things, as such if someone casts Wither on my character, they are much more likely to curl up in a ball, or run away than escalate back, similarly if they were slashed across the face with a knife they would probably look on in shock trying to work out what happened and why rather than grin and announce 'bring it on'.
