The BPG Owlbear Challenge: Results!

Jason_Morningstar
August 2006 edited August 2006 in Story Games
We judged the seven entrants in five categories - Use of Ingredients, Awesomeness, Owlbearocity, Perceived Playability, and Beauty. You could get 1,2 or 3 points in each category. Or maybe zero, but nobody got a zero because every entrant was great. Seriously, I was poleaxed by the quality and innovation represented by these games. I did not expect to get games I actually wanted to play, but I want to play every one of these. Good job, everybody!

Use of Ingredients refers to the prominence and usefulness afforded the three chosen ingredients. Creative implementation was rewarded.

Awesomeness is a catch-all for anything that leapt out as exciting, unique, funny or cool.

Owlbearocity is self-explanatory.

Perceived Playability means very little, because none of these were playtested. But some look more practical at the table than others.

Beauty refers to design and layout and illustration. Itâ€™s pretty arbitrary.

It was a six-way tie for second place, and one entry squeaked - squeaked past the others.

CONGRATULATIONS TO JOE MCDONALD AND RED-RIMMED EYES!

Evocative, creepy, weird, innovative, fun - the BPG staff really liked Red-Rimmed eyes.
