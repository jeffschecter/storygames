the color game

Emily_Care
February 2006 edited February 2006 in Story Games
This idea was still in my head when I woke up this morning, prompted I am sure by a mish-mash of what's been on anyway lately and some other great game ideas that have been floating around. Here goes:


For any number of players. Each person chooses a different color and writes it on a piece of paper.

The other players add a word, free-association word-web style to your color, but each word connects just to the color, not to each other. You add a word inspired by the color that is a Theme or Issue. (love, hate, betrayal, justice, majesty etc)

Players take turns. In a turn you can do one of the following things:

1) Advance your theme
2) Block another theme
3) Support another theme
4) Echo another theme

You do this by narrating something that includes:
a) your color
b) one of the words written on a page
c) and a new word you write down

To Advance your own theme or echo another, you write on your own sheet.
To Block or Support you write on that person's sheet

A new word is associated from an old word and is written on that sheet connected to the old word by a line.


That's it. I don't know if it would work like that or if it needs lots more. I'm kind of curious. I think it would make a cool party game.

    Say you and I were playing and I choose Blue and you choose Yellow.

    I write "daffodils" next to your color, you write "river" next to mine.
    I write "nostalgia" next to my color, you write "happiness" next to yours.

    I go first and advance my theme.
    I say "A lazy azur river wended its way through a meadow full of flowers. A daffodil nodded it's head to the soothing trickling murmur of the river that it had heard from the moment it had first sufaced from the soil."
    I write "meadow" next to daffodil on your sheet.

    Then you go. You decide to block my theme.
    You say: "A mustard yellow bee flies up to the daffodil buzzing its wings. As it rubs its legs on the flowers' stamens it says 'Don't you ever dream of going somewhere other than this river bank?'"

    You write "bee" next to meadow on your sheet.

    Does that make sense?
