Annoying Housemates and She's Worth It - My Two New Ideas

\n
\n
\n
\n Lately I've been in that frame of mind where everything seems to inspire me toward game design.

First of all it was my friend telling me about the guy she lives with, who has been annoying her. Then I decided to come up with something to play out that dynamic of the shitty housemate. Essentially it's a game where the players (at least 6 maybe?) are divided into two teams and each team represents one of the housemates. They take turns setting up scenes and in those scenes they argue about stuff usually or engage in petty little contests. The players bid coins from a team pool and whoever ends up winning gets to narrate. That's mostly it except I want something to show the relationship degrading and I want there to be a strong likelihood of one housemate snapping and assaulting or killing the other eventually.

The second idea was inspired by the woman I miss. The basic setup is a man is driving across the country to see the woman he loves. It's meant for four players and the role of The Driver cycles around the table. There's a deck of cards with (for lack of a better term) random encounters on them. So the current driver draws a card, frames a scene around it and the other players are the other participants in the scene. But they won't always be people, I want it set up so that sometimes a scene is a radio show he's listening to, or a dream sequence, or a memory, or him imagining what she's doing right now. And for some reason there's a card in the deck that just says "The Assassins Draw Near".

So yeah, that's where I'm at.

    \n I think "She's Worth It" sounds nice, but the random encounters should definitely all end up relating back to Her. I like the idea that by the time the driver gets to wherever the woman is, the players have finished describing her -- play is simply a means to establish who she is, and why exactly she is worth it, to him. Each time a card is drawn and resolved, the players are closer to seeing her in their mind's eye. When they finally do see her, the game's over.
