    \n It's both, actually. The drama system is really well suited as it stands to the Danish semi-live style of play. Since DS is divided into procedural and dramatic scenes, you simply play out drama scenes and add meta-techniques like internal monologue etc. to heighten the tension. Those scenes are about the emotion-heavy conflict between characters, so live play taps right into that.

    The full scale larp rules drop the order of precedence rules, since the larp will be free play, and adapts the token economy used by Drama System for dramatic encounters and procedure contests. It also adds in Consequence cards that the GM can give to players who fail or have complications in their procedural contests. These put more pressure on the players and can be used in later tests to gain or lose advantage.
