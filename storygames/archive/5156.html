\n Andy said we should do some Play challenges. Here's a play challenge Jason Morningstar and I cooked up. You will play one game using another's rules. From the rules set you choose you will get your conflict resolution, player abilities, etc. From your setting you will get what the world looks like, what your players are setting out to do, etc. You pick the rules set, we pick the setting, blindly! It works like this.

1. Choose a rules set you like. Preferably one you own already. This game must already exist and not be written for this challenge (see below)
2. Tell Jason what it is, and that you are playing (in a whisper, here).
3. Jason tells Dave who you are (in a whisper, here).
4. Dave assigns you a setting, without knowing what rules set you're using.
5. Jason posts triumphantly in this thread, something like "Andy will play Grey Ranks with AGON rules!"
6. You play your ruleset in the the setting/world of the assigned setting, before February 3rd. That means you can play at Dreamation or Nerdly Meetup if you have time.
7. You write it up, noting any rules changes or name changes - you can muck with it a little, but remember this is not a design competition. Publish it somewhere - Jason will post details in this thread telling you where & how. Tell us the story of what happened, tell how the rules you were using actually worked for that game, and where they didn't. Rate your experience on a 1-10 scale. Tell us what we'd need to know to have as good a time or better than you did. Jason and I will judge writeups, and there will be prizes for 1st place and runner-up, with whatever criteria we the judges decide upon at the time. Impress us.

It will be a lot of fun! Jason and I will also be participating (he'll choose my setting), but we won't be eligible for prizes, to keep us honest.

Start by whispering Jason the name of the game you'll be using for your rules set.

EDIT (Andy K): Please whisper your game rules choice to "Jason Morningstar".

      \n Contenders: The Masquerade = Vampire fight club in post-Katrina New Orleans.

      Contenders really needs to enforce a strong social convention that personal combat belongs in a specific time and place. The Ring is a crucible of combat, just as the contender's life is a crucible of personal struggle.

      I'm planning to map this to the social convention of The Masquerade. In the urban wreckage of New Orleans, the Vampires clans have a golden opportunity to pursue their goals in a near lawless environment. But inter-clan conflict limits their ability to exploit this opportunity. So the clans have decided to determine the fate of the New Orleans through personal combat by chosen champions.

      Non-combat scenes are places and situations where The Masquerade is in effect. In fight scenes, vampires are free to be vampires. Brawl scenes are scenes that break the social convention--situations where the conflict between the clans spills over in the human milieu.

      \n We did Star Wars Panty Explosion last night. It went ok. I'll think about putting together an AP post.

      There were problems, namely trying to understand/come to grips with the core system of the game.
      Plus, instead of playing Star Wars with PE, I played a game of Panty Explosion where the kids were all in high school on Coruscant. In hindsight, I think the best thing to do would have been to simply ignore high school girls altogether, and just run a Star Wars game using the rules as-is, instead of a game that was using the rules of PE, and the setting of Star Wars, INSIDE PE. Hmmm. If anyone is reading this, I'd say try not to be hamstringed by trying to mesh the setting of your rules-game with your setting-game.

      There were successes, including:
      Lots of laughs
      Cool action description
      Interest in learning/playing a straight session of PE
      Honestly interesting characters right at chargen. Travis remarked, "I had a concept of a girl who was really turbulent and passionate just under the surface of a cold, shy exterior. Normally I'd just have to write that personality on my sheet in the background section. But I was able to map it perfectly in PE by taking Earth 5, Fire 4")

      There were non-successes, including:
      Long time to make characters (too many good choices)
      General premise/theme fumbling
      Lack of setting clarity (again, this was me coming up with "Let's play PE INSIDE Star Wars" rather than a straight-up "Let's play Star Wars, just using the PE rules"). This ended up with all of us (esp me!) falling back on puns: Bad Star Wars puns.

      In the end, I think the PE parts of the game shined more than the SW parts of the game.

      Events that happened:
      Karaoke with Drunken Bothan Salesmen
      Scheming to make the Bothans cheer up the emo girl. With a karaoke duet.
      Missing the last train home, as it rocketed to hyperspace
      Disabling the BASIC Teacher's cyber-arms, so that the cute boy could save face from not having done his homework
      Lecturing the cute boy and using the force to fling him across the room
      Jet (cute boy) and Mako (tough boy) get in a fight. Mako beats up Jet. Moe, tough PC schoolgirl and Dodecathalan champion, beats up Mako. Both Jet and Mako become friends, joined by their fear/awe of Moe.

      My thoughts:
      * I want to play/run PE again sometime, but straight up normal game of high school girls w/o Star Wars. In fact, we can pretty much use the same characters.
      * I really like a lot of what you know/have about your character in PE when you start the game.
      * There's a lot of rules I think I need to modify about resolution to have it settle right with me/my group.
      * I'm almost thinking to run PE/SW again, this time completely ditching the PE "high school girls" bits, and using the rules straight up. Psychic == Force, and Demon == Dark Jedi. It might not be soon, though, as there's so much more on my plate. But it's definitely going in the "idea box" for a rainy day gaming session.

      My next challenge is Vampire using the Tenra Bansho Rules. For this, I had planned on doing it thus but the PE experience confirmed it in my mind; It's going to be Vampire (modern day US, vampires and humans, all using the Tenra system as a core for stories, characters, etc) and not a Vampire-Tenra crossover (Tenra Bansho with Vampire mech-pilot children and vampire samurai). This will go down in a few weeks.

      \n We got as far as character creation but various real life events kept us from playing. The main difficulty we had stylistically is that Floating Vagabond is a game of wacky screwball comedy and DitV is a game of gritty moral decision making. So ditching all the humor and playing DitV straight was possible but unwanted since we picked a wacky rule set and doing a parody of DitV grit was quite doable but against the spirit of the contest to emulate the DitV world.

      Ultimately though, I only added two new rules to make the game possible. Every character got for free the "I'm a Dog" Effect which caused every Faithful NPC you meet to pour out every little problem he has in excruciating detail.

      I also added the skill Call Upon Mighty Godly Thing.

      Oh and chaldfont, the PC actually did randomly roll the Rodgers and Hammerstein Effect and the game was going to begin with the Blazing Saddles theme song.
