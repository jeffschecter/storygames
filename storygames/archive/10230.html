    \n Sure!

    I already knew the CoS wiki, so I had some ideas about what style I wanted and what would need. For example, there are few tables and all text is body-text: there's no blocks of examples, fiction block-texts or sidebars. This helps to define paragraph style (just need about a couple for body text and lists, and for subheadings).

    In the first week I was I was thinking about the format of the book. Wanted it to be something special and, needing to be able to print to POD, I looked on the website of Lulu and decided by the square format. I was undecided about whether to use small or large square and the number of columns to use (one or two). So I tried Calson typo and counted the number of words per page. Also, I decided against colour for cost reasons. My first attempt was something like this:





    In a typograhy manual was a chapter about layout. There is a layout based off Gutenberg Bible, and I used it for the book. Conspiracy of Shadows is set in a fantasy world that remembers the c. XV-XVI Europe, so I thought I could design the book imitating the incunabulas. Also, I changed Calson by Arno, because it's a old style type (instead of the transitional style of Caslon) and had all the features I needed, including ligatures, small caps, etc. I reduced the size point to 9/10 and I get the book will have about 120 pages (with illustrations, TOC and chapters sections). The type for subheading was Theokritos. I used this font with the Spanish version of The Shadow of Yesterday, and I find clear and singular. I have tried some blackletter typography but they used to be difficult to read.

    While, I was doodling some ornaments for the page, thinking where page numbers would go and so. About illustrations, I knew that I wanted for the cover, but the rest was unclear. I thought about using three or four frames of the comic in the chapter introductions like a storyboard. Originally, the chapter introduction would be full of ornaments and different from each other. But I'm lazy

    The second week, my computer was dead. I lost about 9-10 days to make a backup and to reinstall the OS and software. Then, I started with the actual layout. My process is simple: first, I layout the text without format, then I make the paragraph and character styles I need and finally I apply the styles. Most of the text are in two columns, with the exception of lifepaths and taint symptoms are in three. I think I finished the basic layout in the last week. I remember that the chapter introductions were finished about three or four days before of deadline, and this one was the next work. In this moment I was pretty crazy because I thought I would not have time to finish the work. I had to review the layout, in search of widows and orphans (you'll find a lot of them), to choose the illustrations, make a character sheet and TOC and index. A lot of work, nothing of time.

    I decided to don't make the index, because is hard work and I already had a 128 page book. 128 is a fine number in layout design. I remember that last days I worked with illustrations, adjusting the text, trying to remove widows and orphans, etc. The last day I made the cover and the character sheet. Later, I found that I forgot a 17-score track for Taint, my bad. Well, the character sheet was made on fly, so you know what happens when you don't have a sketch of the design. The cover was pretty fast. I already had the cover illustration and the logo designed. The backcover was a little more harder: the illustration didn't cover all the page and I didn't want to deform it, so I found a solution with duplicate the image and use it as mirror.

    Well, that' all. If you have any question, I'm happy to answer!
