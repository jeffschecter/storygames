    The crew so far
    - a gun totting captain/bounty hunter
    - a mildly anti-social/autistic engineer with a phobia of technology too far above the norm

    \n
    \n In our freeform, Cynthia and I did a murder mystery: Wing, her foster son was taken over by the spirit of an ancestor of the Bremagni Islands (Elisea Papertree) and he subsequently defenestrated the heir apparent to the Lordship of Easter Bremagne who was a roundly despised duellist and boor. However, he had no such memory of this, having been possessed at the time. Cynthia's Seauclaire character had to call up her demon, The Straithe, to help her suss all of this out, and in the process accidentally absorbed the ancestor spirit into her dreamspace, effectively making her an "alternate personality." She had to be coaxed out of the dreamspace through the use of an ancient Oriellian ritual, and the promise that Seauclaire would work to change the legal status of the Bremagnis; a protectorate of the Lunargenti Empire. Elisea wants it to be fully recognized commonwealth, their citizens the equal of any other Lunargenti . Seauclaire agreed. Seauclaire had Straithe (who is a shapechanger) pretend to be someone who looked like Wing and escape from the palace of the Bremagni Lord in a very dramatic and overly showy fashion, to give the boy an alibi. Wing still doesn't know what he did. He's in the process of courting the love of his life, Dainya Harcourt, who is his perfect counterpoint. And the story continues....

    \n I aaaaalmost had four straight days. I was up for some Iron Fisting on Monday but one of the dudes came down with something and we hadda cancel.

    But I did play Capes on Saturday, sandwiched by D&D on Friday and Sunday. In the first D&D game we all kinda sat around while the GM made stuff happen to one PC that Changed the Future (our characters' present) and gave everyone brand new memories and stuff. In the second, we all got drunk on this lousy wine that made us reeeeally sleepy so we didn't get much done. But Lin Baolong unflappably arguing over potential avenues of piracy with his iron-bitch poisoner/cook before the amused crew of the Silk Dragon is always fun.

    And then there was Capes. Caaaaaapes. My sweaty man-love for that game knows more bounds. We had temptation and martial arts and altruism and blast guns and doom and alarm claxons and idealism and giant robots. It was glorious.

    Expect a full report by the end of the week.
