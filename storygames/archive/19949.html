    \n Sex moves are going to be harder to do in D&D (especially in more open & flexible versions like 5e) because classes don't represent the same kind of tightly focused archetypes that AW playbooks do.

    And, unsurprisingly, I think the best moves on your list are tied to many of those classes with the strongest archetypes. I really like the Wizard one. I like the rogue one, but I think you could make it even better if it was a prisoner's dilemma game, where each character had to secretly decide if they trust the other, rather than leaving it up to the rogue.

    The bard one is really good to. It says something about bards and the place of art in the world that maybe isn't true in every D&D game I've played, but becomes true in this one by virtue of the move. Similarly with the fighter and violence. Similarly the cleric and faith.

    I don't really like the paladin move, though. I feel like it's trying to hit the middle ground of two very different views of how the paladin feels about sex and sexuality, and in doing so doesn't mean much of anything.


    The ones that give your partner limited use of your abilities (druid and sorcerer) seem very flat to me, to much about what that character does instead of who that character is. Originally, I was putting the Ranger in this category too. After re-reading the move, I get it--It's about where the character is comfortable. You might want to re-write the move to make the thematic elements more obvious.

    As a matter of fact, you might want to re-write them all to match the formatting used in AW: "When you do X, Y happens". That will let you more easily clarify certain aspects of the moves, and might even be a vehicle for finding something cool to do with other moves, perhaps "when you lie with another under the light of the full moon"

    \n Further thoughts on a possible paladin move. This one focused on the paladin's role as defender and knight in shining armor. I think there's a whole separate move to be written that could focus on purity and walking the narrow path. (anyone with the MonsterHearts Second Skins should check out the Unicorn for something along those lines).

    "When you make love to another character, they become your paramour, replacing any other paramour you might have. When you come to the rescue of your paramour, gain inspiration or regain a use of one of your paladin abilities. When your paramour demonstrates they can handle themself in a dangerous situation without your help, lose inspiration if you have it.
