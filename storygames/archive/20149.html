\n What apps are out there for iOS that are good for making and manipulating a virtual table of index cards? I know there's an iPhone app for Microscope, but I'd like something that plays well with my iPad. The features I'm looking for are:

-Ways to connect one card to another, not just a list of them
-Multiple card orientations available
-Freedom to move cards anywhere on the table

    \n May I recommend "Index Card"? It is originally designed for those who like the corkboard aspect of the writer software "Scrivener", but it might serve your purpose. One potential issue is that you cannot move cards anywhere, but in rows and columns (or stack them).

    \nPopplet is a concept mapping app that fits the bill. (concept mapping is not hierarchical.) It has an app and a web version. I use it for relationship maps all the time. Nodes don't have to connect.

    Ruminate isn't bad either. Again, concept mapping. Nodes do not have to be connected.

    Note Card is good, but as mentioned it allows only a grid structure and stacks. but you can color the cards too.

    \n I use physical index cards, I have like a small plastic index card sorter. I've been thinking I want to go digital -- if I did, I would avoid something that was like index cards and I'd rather go for something like a mix between a graphical mind map and a wiki.
    Like you make your notes (like the old Tomboy app for Gnome) but you could also see their relationship in a mindmap that used the title of each note as a node. Tap the node to open that note.
