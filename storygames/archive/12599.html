    \n Yeah, something like that. There's probably a way to think about it where it makes more sense, thematically, though. Because the PCs are all supposed to be people who know the victims, right? So it makes sense that the victimization will happen all around them. Or maybe the monster is gradually trying to draw them in so it can victimize the PCs too. Basically, the rule is that victimization has to happen on camera, not off camera.

    Honestly, though, if I was gonna run Afraid nowadays, I might try to hack in some newer ideas from Apocalypse World and make Fronts for the monster's hold on various victims. That way, you could, like, "threaten badness" against a particular victim and, if the PCs didn't do anything about it or failed in their efforts, you could advance the victimization on a AW-style countdown clock. I know that AW and Afraid are different games with different principles, but there's probably a way to make off-screen victimization make sense, even though it may mess up the timing of other mechanics and make things even harder for the characters.
