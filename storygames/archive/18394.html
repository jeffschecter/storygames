    \n Well, they're not for my games, but as part of Project Y I'm creating short animated movies introducing each game and replay (heavily influenced by Zero Punctuation).

    I was already a fan of ZP, and then after seeing the Tabletop Fiasco I was wondering how on earth we could get over the problem of the tedium of video clips showing gamers sitting around the table (I tried to sit through the Tabletop Fiasco, I really did, but honestly the set-up was the most interesting part), and then the two concepts connected in my brain. ZP manages to talk about a video game without showing even a single screenshot of it (normally) and so in the same way I can talk about a tabletop game without showing gamers sitting around a table. While ZPs are reviews, whereas Project Y is introductions to replays, I figured the same approach would work, be bright, be loud, be fast.

    Oh, and be English

    Do you think it is some inherent quality of RPGs/SGs that conveying some of the vibe of the playstyle is important (in a way different from other sorts of games)?
    \nYes I do, very much so. Board game demos- due to their being mainly about the rules- can be incredibly boring, and CCG videos are more or less just players slapping cards down on the table. However, in RPGs, players are acting out roles, and how they do that, especially in SGs, is very important to get across. Of course this is less the case with more traditional combat-based games, because realistically not much mileage can be got out of scenes with player after player going 'Ok, so that's STR 6, -1 for encumbrance, +2 for night vision; er, no wait...' etc.
