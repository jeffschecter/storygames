    \n It will run with five--I've done it recently, and plan to do it again at Dreamation--but if you're time-limited you may wish to request people keep their scenes punchy.     But if you've got extra time, five won't kill the game. (I'm going to slightly disagree with Jason--I think four is the ideal configuration, as it allows a little more breadth of relationships.)

    \n The first time I played Fall of Magic was at Dreamation; the facilitator played the Magus rather than having us share. This...was suboptimal, mostly because I felt she bigfooted a couple of scenes that just needed to breathe. I think sharing the Magus is actually really crucial, since it binds everyone's conceptions together. And like the blackbird, there are often at least 13 ways of looking at the magus :-)

    (In my most recent game I was the apprentice, and Fox was an actual fox. He asked, what is the Magus like? I said, have you ever chased a rabbit for a long time and failed to catch it? The Magus is like that.)

    In general when I play I try to include the other players in the scene one way or another, since my GMing style is basically, "split the party, but only into groups of two or more because players talking to each other is more interesting." That definitely lowers the creative burden. OTOH there are places for the solo scene.

    FoM really is a kind of miracle in how it produces such remarkable stories. When I start my Actual Play thread I'll share the FoM hack I used to generate PC backstory...
