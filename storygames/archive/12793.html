\n Last week I attend the Stephen Colbert show (I got lucky and survived the stand-by wait). Before the show, if you're lucky enough to be chosen, you can ask Stephen a question. I got lucky and asked him this: What would the character Stephen Colbert's D&D stats be? Remember he can't have all 18's because those low rolls give your character character.
Here's his response:

You know we just lost 98% of the audience here . . .
Well, Stephen would say he has all 18's but let's do this.
Charisma-18/00;
Intellegence 5, no wait. Intellence 12
Wisdom 5
Constitution 12
Dexterity 13 or 14
What am I forgetting? (at this point me and some other guy in the audience shout strength just a Stephen remembers)
Strength . . . 3 (big laugh from all)

Oh, he also like the animated Avatar: the Last Airbender.

    \n No (and no idea why my brain has retained all of this useless stuff), the below-5 restrictions (for those of you who don't know, in 1E AD&D, all the stats if you were 5 or below said, "Here or lower can only be...") were:

    STR - Magic-User
    DEX - Cleric
    CON - Illusionist
    INT - Fighter
    WIS - Thief
    CHA - Assassin

    'Cause, you know, if you have a 4 CHA no one will trust you with a Halberd but a poisoned dagger is just fine. These rules are also the reason that all the ritual stuff in your Catholic church is made out of heavy, malleable gold and silver. Easy to fix dents!
