\n
\n
\n
\n
\n It's that time of the year again. The siren's call of the ENnies has been sounded and the question goes out; who will answer? Do you think you have what it takes to be an ENnies judge? Do you have the fortitude to stand up against the barrage of RPG products, the reflexes to navigate the plethora of text and toys, and the will to emerge with the finest goods produced during the year? If so, then this is a great opportunity for you!

Starting on July 1, 2010, people will be able to throw their hats in the ring and nominate themselves. The last day for nominating will be July 11, so you'll have plenty of time to make your decision. Voting for the judges will take place alongside voting for products and the winners will be announced at the ENnie Awards ceremony on August 6, 2010. Keep any eye out for my next post with a link to the form.

If you want more information about the current judges, please visit the judges page on the ENnie Awards site: http://www.ennie-awards.com/about/judges.asp. Also, all current judges will be following this post, so feel free to ask questions of them.

If you are thinking about running, here's a list of criteria you'll need to remember. All judges must:\n
    \n
  • be at least 18 years of age as of August 6th, 2010 and able to enter into a legal contract;
    • \n
  • not have any professional relationship with any RPG publisher during the period from January 1st, 2009 to August 1st, 2011. Such relationships include unpaid work and work on free products;
    • \n
  • declare any relationship with any publisher (including EN Publishing, except for the Ceramic DM line) in which they receive money, product, or special consideration in exchange for their services;
    • \n
  • be able to read English and communicate clearly therein.
    • \n
\nIf you have a relationship with a publisher, even as a playtester, it is important that you notify the ENnies board of this so that they may determine whether you are eligible to seek a judge position. In cases of rule violation, conflict of interest, misrepresentation, or other factor(s) detrimental to the awards, the Board of Directors retains the right to alter the composition of the judging panel at any time.

Physical location is irrelevant - so long as you can accept packages year-round.

When you apply, you will be expected to fill out a questionnaire to help people best gauge how well they think you'll work as a judge. Here a couple of sample questions:\n
    \n
  • What styles and genres of RPGs do you enjoy most? Are there any styles or genres that you do not enjoy? Which games best exemplify what you like? Do you consider yourself a particular system\u2019s, publisher\u2019s or genre\u2019s \u201cfanboy/girl\u201d?
    • \n
  • How would you like to see the ENnies change (categories, policies, etc)? What should remain inviolate?
    • \n
\nIf you want to run, here is what is expected of you. All judges shall:\n
    \n
  • access the ENnie Awards boards/e-mailing lists (for the purposes of corresponding with judges and interacting with directors and staff);\n
      \n
    • weekly on the designated ENnies forums and sharing an accurate list of what products they have received;
      • \n
    • daily on the designated ENnies forums during judge deliberations (expected dates for 2011: June 1 - July 1);
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • act in a professional and impartial manner;
    • \n
  • sign a non-disclosure agreement;
    • \n
  • in the event that they are removed from office, bear the expense of returning and/or forwarding the products received for review;
    • \n
  • pay any customs duties charged for submissions;
    • \n
  • if they attend Gen Con \u2013 Indy, work a minimum of two hours at the ENnies booth;
    • \n
  • if they attend Gen Con \u2013 Indy, attend the awards ceremony smartly attired;
    • \n
  • report to the Submissions Coordinator and Business Manager.
    • \n
\nAnd, besides being able to say "I'm an ENnies Judge!" what else do you get? Judges:\n
    \n
  • upon completion of reviewing, retain for personal use or otherwise dispose of products reviewed;
    • \n
  • receive a free Gen Con Indy 4-day pass.
    • \n
\nTony Law
ENnies Assistant Business Manager
tony@ennieawards.com

    \n I like the spirit of the rule, but I think it needs to be re-adjusted with these issues in mind. I do think that it is not unreasonable to ask someone who wants to be a judge to not participate in the production of any game for the year ahead of time (though playtesting should be exempt from that). And I don't know if free or not makes much of a difference, given that the free category is becoming a hotly contested segment of the contest.

    Also, being an ENnies judge is a huge job. It sounds like you wouldn't have time to even have a regular job, let alone work on another game.

    Graham and Ryan, I really think you guys are overstating the case. There are tons of pure "fans" in the story game community who don't produce anything and aren't interested in producing anything who could potentially be great representatives for this community among the judges. Why does it have to be a designer?

    Also, you have to look at the ENnies in a historical contest. This is a contest that came from a D20-only world. Given that, they have made great strides in the last couple of years. There are people in the administration who didn't think that small press games should even be considered, let alone free ones. They didn't think PDFs were "real" games only two years ago. They are coming from a pretty myopic place and have done a lot to change this in the last two or three years (some internal shocks, though negative at the time, helped with that). So I think you should take the long view, be a bit more appreciative and keep working on your campaign to get great story games representation in among the judges.

    Finally, the real area where you should be pushing your agenda is at the judges themselves. You get a lot of them who pop into every forum to introduce themselves during the nomination phase and then disappear. The trick is to snag them, engage them and open their eyes. A lot of these judges are ultimately just big gaming geeks who are honestly curious about the whole hobby and have just never been exposed to many of the various corners of it. If you can open up lines of communication early (before the flood of products hits them), you can maybe get them to turn their heads to games they might not have otherwise fully appreciated.

    Posted By: Destriarch I have long advocated personally that the requirement of physical products only be dropped, allowing people to submit via PDF only...
    \nYou can find the rules for PDF-only (or electronic) submissions here.. The short answer is that if you have a product that is PDF-only, you fill out the entry form and submit your PDF along with a $5 entry fee (or $10 if you're submitting 2+ PDF products). The PDF/electronic products have an entry fee because (unlike physical print products) the ENnies can't auction them off after the awards (which is how the ENnies are funded).

    Also, I can only speak for the past year, but we (the other judges and I) considered products for every category that seemed appropriate. So, if your product was a PDF of a complete game, we at least considered it for best game. If your PDF product included a map, we considered it for best cartography. If it included art... you get the idea. Granted, that doesn't mean that a PDF was a contender for that category, but it at least received consideration. The other judges and I thought it was important to give every submission a fair shot in a many categories that made sense (obviously, if your book doesn't include art, it doesn't get considered for best art). However, only PDF/electronic products were considered for the "e-book" category, so that's one that you don't have to share with physical products.

      \n Effectively immediately, Kennon Bauman has withdrawn his name as a 2011 ENnies Judge Nominee and has made the following statement:

      "I respectfully withdraw my nomination as a potential ENnies judge for the 2010 season. I believe strongly in the integrity of the ENnie Awards, and wish no harm to come to their reputation or prestige, even among a vocal few, as a consequence of any action I have undertaken. I campaigned honestly and openly, and I see no evidence of any breach of confidence or immoral conduct on anyone's part. I would like to fervently thank those who have supported me thus far, and wish the remaining candidates the best of luck in the coming season. I'm sure I'll be talking to many of you before next year's electoral season, but for those who I don't -- see you then."
      --Kennon C. Bauman

      The statement that led to his resignation was one made in an e-mail to his family and friends asking people to vote for him. Unfortunately, he worded his letter in such a way that one could construe that he wanted them to "stuff the ballot box." I have known Kennon for many years and I know that he did not mean this in any way, shape, or form and was just trying to help the receipients understand the voting process better. Although he was not asked to withdraw, he did so and we respect his decision.
