    I've seen the exact same problem using Firefox (latest version) under XP and (probably) Safari 2 under Mac OS 10.4.

    Looks like you have a handle on the issue but I have a workaround and a possible suggestion.

    The workaround is, when you get the error, hit the back button in your browser. Then open a new window or tab (if you're me, you probably already have another tab or two open to the forum or one of the discussions) and log in using that window/tab. Finally, hit the forward button in window/tab where you made your comment. Your browser should put up a warning saying the page is from a POST command and are you sure you want to resend the data; say okay, and then your browser will hopefully resend the data and your post will be posted.

    My suggestion is to hack the forum code so it doesn't dumbly reply with an error if a post command is received from a non-logged in session. Instead it should cache the message and put up a login prompt, then post the message after the successful login. I don't know how hard this would be, though.
