\n Because someone went for the Insane Level of funding and bought himself a new ANIMa Flight Suit for 500 bucks.

This is an amazing milestone, in my life and for the game, and I am super stoked about it, so much so that I'm using the phrase super stoked at all.

For those of you who don't know, it's a official fangame based on the tabletop game Bliss Stage by Ben Lehman's imprint, T.A.O. Games. It's about the last, desperate survivors of an alien invasion that took out 99.9% of the adult population of the world. These teenage soldiers are fighting a final and desperate guerrilla war against these alien nightmares, using reverse engineered alien technology that literally uses love (and hate, and trust) as a weapon. Specifically, as a big honking robot, Robotech style.

It's a visual novel, specifically a dating sim and turn-based roleplaying game: think fully illustrated Choose Your Own Adventure books with voice-acting. The combat sections are... pretty much the tabletop game with additional movement and special attack rules (you get special attacks at Intimacy 5 and maybe passives at Int 3), which we're back-porting to the Tabletop Game. The Interludes are pretty much the Interlude rules as written in the main book.

And we've also got sweet art from a woman of some notereity, Buttercup Sayian, who I have the luck to call my lover - this is her take on Sara Smith.



I hereby declare this the Ask Your Questions About This Game Thread! Does anyone have any questions or comments about the visual novel that they'd like to ask me? Stuff about the rules, the story, even the process of writing it or Kickstarting it are welcome.

    Posted By: Emily CareGreat project! How does it look when you're in the dreaming with your robot?
    \nMost of the time, like an artist's impression of the area, in colors suitable to the season and general psychometric 'flavor' of the local Dream. Warzones are fiery red, places of death and destruction are ashen, places full of joy are a riot of color.

    Our biggest inspiration for the look of the Dream in-game is Braid by Johnathan Blow. In particular, the bombed-out areas of San Francisco look a lot like the very first scene of Braid - with a slightly glowing red haze in the background from all of the fire and blood.

    As a minor side note, a bit of head-canon that crept into the game is that the Resistance calls the physical world "The Wake" in a bit of gallows humor.
