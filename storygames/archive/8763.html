\n Obviously I'm hoping to get feedback from Mr. Dowler in this thread, but I'm posting it here rather than simply emailing him in an effort to garner wider feedback as well as provide help to anyone else who may be getting stuck, as I am.

Stuck--in a solo game. Yes, perhaps I'm having mental difficulties, and I won't begrudge anyone who suggests as much.

Anyway, as soon as I sat down with HtHaD and began drawing my first underground river and gold vein, I fell in love with this thing, whatever you call it. It's awesome, simply put. But I can't deny that the way certain things are written have me all confused.

To be specific, I got stuck on C2 - Dwarven Civilization.

Problem 1. According to the text:

"In spring, Dwarves gather, mate, and pay to have their relatives move in with them. Gather up all (treasure)* that are connected to Dwarven tunnels and are not Dwarven Treasures. Draw a new room to store each (treasure) gathered, or place them in existing rooms where there is space."

At this point, I had one gold vein and no ore drawn on my map, so I had one Dwarven tunnel that went down to the vein, with one Dwarven barracks attached. I gathered up the gold, but wasn't sure where to draw the new room. Are there rules I should be following for this, or do I simply draw a new room wherever I please? The text isn't clear. And if I do draw a new room wherever I please, do I have to connect it to the surface? I'm assuming the "existing" rooms are ones I drew in the Prehistoric age, so they're forgotten, but still there. That leaves me with a fine place to put my (treasure), but it doesn't answer the question of what governs the drawing of new rooms.

Also, it's not clear at this point what, exactly, Dwarven Treasures are.

Problem 2. The text, again:

"For each (treasure) gathered, draw a Dwarven barracks connected to some other Dwarven construction and put a new (dwarf unit)** in it. Dwarves don't like to be too near to the noise of mining, so try not to put it on a mining tunnel."

I've gathered one (treasure), so I should draw another barracks and put a new (dwarf unit) in it. The problem is, it says to connect this barracks to some other Dwarven construction. The phase for creating Dwarven constructions doesn't come until fall (and we're still in spring). Where do I put this Dwarven barracks? Or can't I add it, since I don't have any constructions to connect it to?

Problem 3. The text:

"In summer, Dwarves mine....If there's no un-mined ore left on the map, 1(dwarf unit) immediately emigrates off the map looking for greener pastures."

This was the case for me. I had a gold vein, but no ore. So I began the Dwarven Civilization era of the game (per the rules) by having the Dwarves mine the gold vein, which gave them 1(treasure), which in turn created 1(dwarf unit). But I had no ore, which meant that unit left, leaving me again with only 1(dwarf unit). Then, when the fall comes and the Dwarves build, I don't have enough units (you need 3) to be able to build something. I felt stuck.

Problem 4. The text:

"In winter, the Dwarves mourn. If any Dwarves died this year, draw a Dwarven tomb."

Even though I felt stuck, I went on to the next season, to see if that would clarify things. Instead, it took me to a different kind of phase, which is no problem in and of itself. However, there's no indication of how Dwarves die. Does the player decide at random? That's fine, I'm comfortable making that choice and seeing what happens from it, but the text isn't clear. It would seem, from how the rest of the game works, that it would be decided through the application of a concrete mechanic, but there is no such mechanic described.

So here I am, with one Dwarven unit, no ore, unsure of how to mechanically mine my gold vein, and unsure if anyone's died or not. After writing this I feel like I'm being a big critical ass, but I'm just genuinely confused. Maybe I shouldn't have been drinking a beer while doing this. Am I missing something blatant? Feel free to make me look dumb, as long as you can tell me how to play.

In all sincerity, thanks for your game, Tony. I know I love it, I just have to figure it out first.

*(treasure) = The white, unfilled-in circles

**(dwarf unit) = The black, filled-in circles

    \n Well, I'm not Tony, but here's how I have run it in my own play:

    The whole gold vein is made of ore! Every mining season, they mine a chunk of it - token sized, as I recall - and take that gold. Eventually the whole vein will be replaced with mines.

    Dwarves might be killed if they encounter some creatures left over from the primeval age. There may not be any on your map, so this might not matter. Possibly these can be added later due to certain events, too.

    I always figured the dwarven treasures were just awesome stuff the dwarves made that they left behind. These will become significant in later ages when something else loots the lost dwarven caverns.

    Just add your new dwarf rooms as required wherever makes sense. Think dwarfy. Just add it to some dwarf mines or corridors if there's nothing else yet.

    \n What Mike wrote is how I interpreted the rules as well. It worked for my three dungeons so far (all of which included a dwarven civilization, alone or struggling with aggressive neighbours).

    "Dwarven treasures" are the white beads inside such constructions as a Great Hall, Treasure Room, etc. Those are "special" in that they'll remain there, potential loot, even after the fall of dwarven civilization.
    That's an example of how the rules for HtHaD are written, by the way: I suggest not using those as a perfect step by step tutorial; read a whole section at a time, instead (one page, at least), so that you get a general idea of how it's supposed to work. Then re-read through it while playing, using it as a reference, until it becomes mechanical.

    Yes, ore, Dwarven construction, and Dwarven treasure are all somewhat ambiguous terms. Some of them are clarified in the text, but it's not always easy to find the clarifications.\n
    Posted By: hansel
    Yeah, that works. I just get stuck behind this need to make sure I'm following the designer's goals so I can get the "pure" experience of the game. I've judged too many games based on house rules I've made for them that didn't work, so I want to make sure I'm getting the core of what Tony's driving at.
    \nYeah, HtHaD is a little bit backwards in this regard. At first it looks like a series of instructions for creating a dungeon, but in practice it's more like a set of guidelines that you apply according to your own taste. Because of this, I found it very hard to write, and occasionally simply failed to make it crystal clear. To help, there's a Frequently Asked Questions doc. It covers some additional unclear bits, and fixes an outright mistake in the Dark Elves rules. Based on this post, I think a glossary might be a good addition. I apologize profusely for the problems in the text.

    Because of the nature of the game, there's no guarantee you won't run into a completely crazy situation where completely weird stuff happens. It's just impossible to test for every situation that can arise. For example, I challenge Ben Lehman to give a description of his game that produced a massive undead Dwarven army. So when those things happen, it's best to make an interpretation that fits your idea of how things should properly work. That's all part of the game.

    \n I may be approaching the game from a different perspective as Tony, but I'm playing this game like it were some weird ant farm and I find there's a lot of situations where things are left to my preference and I want less of that. Like when my critters are moving around I've made the general rule of "they move in the way that is most interesting" so then things happen like when my orcs didn't have enough ways to expand they dug upward into the human city. Hello carnage!

    But in a lot of ways for me, the fun is in observing the outcomes of the rules and less participation is more fun. So rules like "lacking prey the alpha predator will move one finger towards the nearest black token and if there are no black tokens towards the nearest white token" etc. are things I find desirable even though they're not necessary.
