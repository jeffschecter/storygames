    \n Someone clearly needs to write The Inner Game of Gaming/Game Design.

    (If you haven't read any of the Inner Game books, starting with Inner Game of Tennis and going to my favorite Inner Game of Music, they're about winning the inner game against your critical voice that constantly tries to sabotage your efforts at actually doing what you're doing by reminding you what you did wrong, telling you that what you're about to do is going to suck, and that you're just a hack anyway. The more you manage to put that voice aside, the more you can get into the flow of your activity, and therefore the better it all works out. This really does apply to most activities that we do that require some amount of skill, whether it's creative (writing, music) or technical (sports).)

    Once, there was a maiden who stood at the centre of every dance.
    \nBack then, she knew all the dances.
    \nShe never missed a step.
    \nOne day, she heard something in the music, as a singer dropped the beat.
    \nIt taught her the joy of dancing poorly.
    \nShe started dancing more and more awry.
    \n"Love has no rules," she said.

    \n

    Dance poorly, Joe!
