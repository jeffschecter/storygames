\n A couple of friends and I are about to assist to a RPG convention, and we'd like to have some fun while driving.
So, anybody knows a good RPG that can be played in a car for about 4 hours? It should be simple enough to be played with few or none resoruces like dice, character sheets and stuff like that.
I guess roleplaying poems should do the trick for a while, but I'd like a deeper experience.
I'm all ears!

    \n Play some random game centered around mages. To cast a spell, you need to make a word from the letters of a license plate from a passing vehicle, and use that word in your description of the spell.

    You could otherwise play Sea Dracula. You just have to make an effort. I guess Baron M\xfcnchhausen can be a little boring for a four hour trip, but there is nothing that says that you can't mix games.

    \n This is probably horribly obvious already, but you can use license plates (or signs by the road) as 10-sided dice. The leftmost number of the first car you encounter is your roll. Simple and easy. Mix that in with a simple system, like a slightly modified version of FU, and you're golden.
