\n Many roleplaying games use dice or cards to determine how combat turns out. This game uses physical combat to determine the results of dice and cards.

Character generation

Your character is a gambler. Give them a name and decide their game of choice.

Then give them three traits and associate a part of the body with each.

Example: Travis creates Big Dave, a poker player. He gives him the following traits:

I'm doing this for hospital fees (Face)
I'm at one with the cards (Stomach)
I take risks (Bollocks)

Conflict resolution

In a conflict: every time you invoke a trait, you may hit the other player in the specified part of the body. Continue until one of you submits.

Example: Travis' character and Boris' character are having a game of poker.

Travis: As the cards are dealt, I'm thinking about my sister in hospital (Hits Boris in the face)
Boris: Ow! Right. I look across at my girlfriend, who gives me strength. (Hits Travis in the mouth)
Travis: Stop whining. I pick up the cards. I feel safe when they're in my hand, at one with them. (Kicks Boris in the stomach)
Boris: This game sucks!
Travis: You chickening out?
Boris: Well, OK. I...er...take a sip of my favourite whisky. (Taps Travis in the leg lightly)
Travis: I push all my remaining chips into the centre of the table. (Kicks Boris, very hard, in the bollocks)
Boris: Eeeeeeuuuurrrrgh!
Travis: You give? (Kicks Boris in the bollocks again)
Boris: Aaaaaawwwwgh!
Travis: You give? (Kicks Boris in the bollocks)
Boris: Yes! Yes.
Travis: All right, so I win the poker match.
Boris: Fine, fine. I'm going home.

Demos at this year's GenCon. I am deeply proud of this game.

Graham
