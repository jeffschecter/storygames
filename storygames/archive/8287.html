    I'd want these sort of coupons to be gained for doing anything awesome, in or out of game. Play your character remarkably well? Get a reward card. Make dinner for the rest of the players? Earn a reward card, or maybe even on per player you fed. Etc.
    \n
    What do they need to do? As noted above, it can really be anything of GM choice.
    For me...

    I tend to want anything that:
    - adds value to the game environment/enjoyment (help them GM or another player, help me clean-up post-game)
    - adds value to the campaign setting/tone (backgrounds, journals, co-creation descriptions, etc...)
    - do something extra special (make/buy dinner, take out my trash, etc... anything more than courtesy or just bringing snacks)
    - aiding in reducing GM stress (replying to emails questions, checking-in RSVP prior to game, etc...)
    - aiding with rules (johnny-on-the-spot with looking up and referencing rules in play, aiding other players updating character stats, etc..)

    THIS week, to test this I'm giving them for simply replying (by Wednesday) for our Friday game. I've asked all players to check-in just to say "yes, I am attending this week" to manage our time best. One player is notorious for just assuming everyone knows he's coming, which could be fine except it's not setting a good example that HE alone is exempt from replying as I ask... This may get his attention.

    [another thought from RPGnet to share here] HalfjackI'm not a big fan of rewarding work away from the table with benefits at the table
    \n
    I agree in a way. I have a friend that feels this way too. He feels like it's cheating for him to get xp or some other in-game reward for things that HE did and not his character.
    The reason I disagree with this for the most part is... That I feel it IS an incentive that demonstrates IMMEDIATELY at the table to OTHER players how the effort they give to make the overall game better (writing backstory, or whatever the reward/coupon was given for) is very important to ME as the game master.
    The player who took time to do the game thing outside of the hours at the game table gets a tangible reward in the context of the game world that can equate to luck or the gods being happy with them, or whatever...
    The GM is happy with that Player, and the Player-Character feels it.

    Posted By: Jason MorningstarIt needs "Void where prohibited by fiat" in tiny print somewhere.
    \n

    No... not for me.
    I hate... hate... HATE the idea of GM fiat.

    I also like hamming it up with "ARGs!!" and "YOU SUCK!!" replies when players get really great rolls or come up with neat solutions, etc... Pulling this "Mulligan" out of their hat when the fat's on the fire seems like an obvious "WHAT??!!?! CRAP!!!" moment. Too good to pass up.

    Plus... I hate GM fiat.
    To me... it feels like I'm cheating if I can't come up with creative solutions to the successful solutions of players, without fiating it... Or rather instead of cheating, it takes the fun/mystery out of playing the game for me if I won't allow some things based on how I think the plot/encounter should go.
    For me...
    If it makes sense and is a solid solution and the PC pulls it out it's more fun to create story based on the roll of the dice or the creativity of my players.

    I hate GM fiat.
