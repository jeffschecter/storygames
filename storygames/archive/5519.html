    \n Here's my take on this:

    When you spend time in the Mad City, life in the City Slumbering goes on. So, when an Insomniac goes into the Mad City to fix his life or by accident, there's always the possibility that something goes on in the City Slumbering they cannot act against.

    Of course, there's nothing to say against the Mad City being a mirror-nightmare of the City Slumbering, and handling it totally different.

    In my game, the Insomniacs usually go through a door to find themselves in the other city. But then again, it can happen by turning around a corner, jumping around a window, going to the restroom\u2026

    \n My most common tendency is to make the Mad City an otherspace that connects the cities of the world together...but then I also make it a bit more of a prison than stock DRYH, where you can only "escape" at certain times and through certain, special places in the city.

    But I do love the hell out of your idea. It's great for really exploring the path the PCs walk. Since I tend to run one-shot demos, though, I don't get to explore that as much.

    here's how it usually goes:
    - we meet
    - I select and present some story-games, depending on how many of us there are, and we pick one together
    - we go through the rules together
    - we do char-gen
    - depending on the game, I sometimes take a little break to prep an R-map or somesuch, based on the char-gen
    - we play out a whole story, with breaks for lunch and dinner

    the longest and most interesting part is of course the last one

    there'll be the intro scene for each PC (probably 2 or 3 PCs for this game)

    then a number of scenes that address the consequences of these intros, as well as stuff I can pick from the questionnaires, and that all work towards the resolution of "what's your path?"

    and finally some sort of shot at their goal for each PC

    I'm thinking this could take a while, especially since we're new to this game and it's rules
