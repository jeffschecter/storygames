    There's always JiffyCon.
    \nJiffyCon was the typical way that the Mass. indie games community assembled ca. 2006-2011.

    2012's been a rough year.

    Not to bogart the thread, but here's (publicly) our twofold problem in organizing the next JiffyCon in western MA:

    1) Space \u2013 If we advertise properly, too many people would show up for the space that we could afford. We've attempted to have it in a variety of different spaces \u2013 hotels, churches, community spaces \u2013 and they always fill up. So we're too big for the low-rent spaces (save Modern Myths who, bless @Jim_Crocker's soul, give us game days for free), but not big enough and wealthy enough to upgrade.

    2) Time \u2013 There used to be more available weekends in the year. Now it seems like every weekend from January on has some competing event for our organizers, be it PAX or Camp Nerdly or Recess or whatever. We recently did a scheduling layout of all the cons we were thinking of attending, and suddenly I got a headache trying to squeeze a JiffyCon in Boston or western MA

    We've had many folks chip in with ideas about how to solve either problem, but it looks like we've got our hands full at the moment.

    JiffyCon Boston's issue is simple - Nathan, Jonathan, Ben, Dev, and Tresi all no longer live there. They were the beating heart of that community.
