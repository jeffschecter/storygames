    \n I have at times worn my hair long, and at others cut it into a high and tight, with many variations in between, including both traditional and nontraditional "Mohawks". My experience is that hair determines how people see you, and at times how you see yourself, but it doesn't allow you to see the environment any better. Growing up on the Rez, I was teased by some of my friends at times for "looking like a hippie". My Native friends who have long hair (they are a minority) do it because they feel like it makes them look more traditional, or more Indian as it were. But I have never heard anything about the hair on your head making you a better hunter. In fact, the best hunters I know happen to be pretty clean cut and short haired. My long haired Native friends are almost all healers, both physicians and traditional healers. One is in fact a family practice physician as well as a Midewinini. I have known him with both short and long hair, but as he has become more traditional, he has stopped cutting his hair. I think it has to do with both his patients and himself seeing him as traditional and keeper of tradition. And we have had many conversations about tradition, but he has never talked about it helping him sense the environment.

    This story sounds like a bunch of unfounded hippie bullshit.

    \n Native American's didn't have any kind of unified culture \u2014 the distances were vast and there weren't any horses until the Europeans arrived. So you can't specify what native Americans as a while believed or practiced, any more than you can say what Asians or Africans as a whole believed. It varied too much by tribe.
