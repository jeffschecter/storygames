      Robin, from what you just said, you'll really like Human Contact. Note the name. The game's about anthropology, first and foremost \u2014\xa0what we learn about ourselves as a society and ourselves as individuals, when we come into contact with people in another society. Star Trek trivializes the very things it claims to be about \u2014\xa0getting people to get along. And it trivializes it by making believe that diplomatic and anthropological missions come from the barrel of a gun. It wasn't Roddenberry's vision (insofar as he had a unified vision \u2014 he loved just trying stuff out, I think).

      \n

      That said, it's emphatically not Star Trek. The thing is, the why and how something happens directly informs the reflection. Humanity isn't a White, empire (OK, Alpha Quadrant Treaty Alliance) \u2014\xa0building interest, isn't defined by bipolar politics, and in fact, makes constant shifts of scale from the personal to the political. Technological changes have social effect. Societies evolve, both before and after contact with the Academy. The Academy itself evolves and changes with those contacts.
