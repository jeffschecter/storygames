\n Hey all,

My question is exactly as you read it above: do you prefer to declare your actual actions before or after the dice? What systems do you like that do either one or the other?

By actual actions, I generally mean what your character actually does to get to his or her goal.

Here's the exigence: I had a wild idea the other day that followed suit with a game I wanted to do about honor and dishonor. The idea is VERY ROUGH, and I can elaborate more, but basically this train of thought made me think that declaring the action AFTER seeing the dice would make more sense in the circumstance I want to use. You might declare your goals initially, but the action afterwards could be very different (in this case, either honorable or dishonorable).

Regardless of circumstance, I'd love to hear some examples of both!

Thoughts?

Best,

    \n I prefer to declare intent before the roll and procedure (the actual action, what you end up doing) afterward, that way you can interpret the roll. At least, I do that with more traditional systems.

    In other words:
    I state that I INTEND to get the door open before making a Lockpicking or whatever roll.
    Then, afterwards, I describe what I ACTUALLY DID to succeed (or fail.) I prefer to describe my own failures because then I don't feel like such a loser when I constantly roll badly. It's a minor sop, but an important one, emotionally.

    Before the roll, state what you Intend (what you want to happen in the game world because of your actions.)
    I want to get us past the trap.
    I want to slay the dragon.
    I want to convince the crowd to rise up.

    After the roll, interpret the results to say what you Actually Ended Up Doing, the Process by which you failed or succeeded:
    (die result: failure) I missed a tripwire when disabling the trap.
    (die result: success) I cut the dragon's head off with one stroke!
    (die result: mixed) My exhortation against the abuses of the immortals convinced me about half the crowd to follow me. The other half feared the wrath of the Gods.

    I prefer to describe what MY character does rather than the GM. The GM can propose challenges, I want to control how I look facing them.

    \n I think this might clarify where I'm drawing my question from...

    Consider these two different approaches:

    1. Declare Goal
    2. Roll bones
    3. Do your thing (narrate how you succeed or fail - interpret the roll without affecting the goal).

    OR

    1. Declare goal and do your thing (just do it!)
    2. Roll bones
    3. Narrate how what you did affects the result

    Each time, you declare your goal/intention before you do any rolling. The goals never change. The meat and potatoes, however, comes in when you think about when you actually did the action.

    I hope that makes sense!
