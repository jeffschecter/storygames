    \n On the outsider/insider thing.

    I thought about that quite a bit on the drive home. So much, in fact, that I missed our exit onto 85 South, and got myself an extra half and hour worth of driving time to think about it some more.

    The solution I came up with is identical to the solution I proposed for the kids' issue. I, as a citizen of Nerdly, will do what it takes to break any such barriers in the future. I will look for the people who are sitting off to the side and invite them to play something with me. I will introduce them to the the Big Name Nerds and suggest that they jump into a game with those guys and gals.

    I will also ask all other comfortable Nerdly folks who think that this is an issue to do the same thing. I want everyone to take the social issues of Nerdly into their own hands. I do not want to lay one ounce of this on the organizers. The organizers are going out of their way to facilitate our fun and make sure we're fed to boot. I do not want them to fuck with the self-propelled community of awesome that Nerdly is. Not for any reason.

    Also, if there is anyone reading this who felt on the outside at any time while at Nerdly, I just wanted to ask you to jump on me next time. I am available for you. I will play games with you and introduce you to people. Ask me for a cookie. I will get you one. And I'm only willing to do all that because I want you to have twice as much fun at Nerdly as I did.

    -Eric

    Scoring it would be something of an honor-system thing, and it's weighted in favor of "newbies" and "outsiders." I admit these "weaknesses" up front by way of saying that I am not concerned about them whatsoever.

    Everyone would get a piece of paper with every person at nerdly on it. Scores work as follows.

    You get 1 point for every game you participated in where the game is one you've played before.

    You get 3 points for every game you participated in where the game is NOT one you've played before. (New experience bonus!)

    You get 1 point for every game you organize and run (whether that's as the GM or facilitator or dude with the supplies -- whatever). This is in addition to the point you get for participating in a game you've played before.

    You get 1 point for every person who you've met before that you get to sit down with and game with/talk with at length.

    You get 3 points for every person who you HAVEN'T met before (in person) that you get to sit down with and game with or talk with at length. (New friends bonus!)

    Note that the new game experience and new friend experience point system means that even if you're someone who's not an extrovert/reach-out-er, so long as several someone elses are, you'll still get the benefit of the conversation/gaming in terms of the points you score.
