    \n I've run 3 individual sessions of it now, all at conventions.

    At least for your first time, do NOT plan it as a one-shot. You're going to need 3 sessions at least to dig into the meat of a Transmission. If you've played Apocalypse World, you'll know what I'm talking about when I say that your first session of Technoir will basically be an "AW First Session" - free play, with the GM mostly in reactive mode, riffing off players' contributions to create story artifacts for reincorporation later, and the players' actions creating linkages between all those plot elements in that oh-so-awesome way Technoir does it.

    To speed things up for convention play, I pre-generated characters with all but one Connection (and let them pick the last), let them buy some additional equipment, and took one of their Push dice from them before play began to be able to deal out some hurt early in the game and show off the dice mechanic. Even so, in a 4-5 hour game, we basically tend to get to the point where everyone is together and has some ideas of what to do next, and then time's up. That sucks for convention play, but would be a great first stopping point for a 3-4 session long game.

    Enjoy!

    \n I've run Technoir twice (both with Hong Kong transmission) with 3-4 players comfortably in 4 hours, with chargen, rules teaching, shopping (sigh), and plot developed in play. It could have used another hour for more plot, but as a way to demo to players how the system works, it worked well. I'd never try running it for 5 or 6, but then it's a rare game I'd run these days for 6 (Mountain Witch, Best Friends... I think that's all).

    I've thought about pregen, especially to cut down on shopping, but player buyin to their characters is vital, so I find it worth the time.

    Ryan Macklin has an excellent hack of Technoir to do the movie Push. I want to run that sometime.
