\n I've written an Android app based on the Story Games Names Project, to provide random but culturally-plausible names for story games.

I think it should work on most Android devices, but I've only tested it on a Samsung Galaxy Nexus running Ice Cream Sandwich. It requires Froyo, Honeycomb, Gingerbread or Ice Cream Sandwich. It would be great if people can let me know whether it works on their Android phones or not, whether the images display okay, and what else I could add that would make it more useful for you.

It's not in the Google Play store yet, but you can download it direct to your phone from GitHub (and get the source code from there as well, if you like). You should be able to use Google Goggles with the QR code at the bottom to download it, as well.

Thank you to all the people who contributed to the Story Games Names Project! I've only got a fairly small fraction of the total lists in the app at the moment. The hardest part of adding more is finding images - they need to be out of copyright, or Creative Commons licensed (and allowing derivatives). If anyone can suggest appropriate images for the various cultures that are missing them, I'd be grateful (you can see more name sets that I could use at the Bully Pulpit Games site or on GitHub)

I'm planning to put it in the Google Play store as a free app soon, once I'm reasonably sure that it will work for most people.

    \n Tested on a HTC One X. Seem to work fine. I note that for the background art (which is wonderfully evocative) in some cases appear stretched, since you seem to be sizing it to the available space. The Elizabethan portrait seems to come off the worse. Note this is exacerbated on the One X, because unlike the G. Nexus, it doesn't have the soft system bar at the bottom. Overall, different phones are going to have different aspect ratios. Also, on the Victorian background image, the name text and buttons blend into the background and become hard to distinguish.

    Features that would be nice to have: show the gender of a name next to it (this would be especially helpful when switching to a new culture). Suggestion - I think UTF8 has male & female symbols you can use. Also it would be nice to be able to see or page through the last couple suggestions, since I might generate a new name and then decide I really liked the previous one better. Also, it might be good to "suppress" a name for the next couple results after it is generated. For example with Celtic I kept getting "Killian" every second result.

    Overall, this is a great thing, and thanks for putting in the work on it!
