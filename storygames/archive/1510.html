Quick background:
I'm working on Boulevard. If I choose to publish it, then: I'm publishing it in a boxed-game format. It'll have dice, paper, pencils, a rules booklet, props... Everything presented in a game-components kind of way, instead of a tome o' rules.


So, I'm entering NanoWrimo (www.nanowrimo.org) as I mentioned in another thread. It's a writing competition that spans November.
I've begun planning the piece I'm going to write:

Orderfukt.
A near-future depiction of American capitalism spun out of control. Some time in the next forty/fifty years, an Anarchist party gains widespread support.
The idea of dissembling the corporate/military power structures, and then reverting back to natural law... It starts to actually make sense.
The Anarchist Party seizes several seats, and starts encouraging citizens to "embrace failure".
Quit their jobs. Hammer the welfare system. Start attacking corporations - hijacking Doritos trucks.
Fuck the system up, right good and proper.

The American government realizes too late that this is a serious problem. The Anarchist Party poses an ultimatum: "Make the following changes, and the Anarchist Party will disband. Not only that, but our revolution will lose steam and stabilize. Don't make the follow changes, and we will consume you and terminate you."

This political escalation and violent acceleration forms the backdrop for a few disjointed stories - which all have the recurring theme of: Embrace failure, or you will lose the war for success.

Really uplifting, inspiring stuff. I'm a cheery guy.



Anyways, here's the deal: Orderfukt doesn't really tie into Boulevard enough to have sections used as flavour text in the book...
But since I'm doing a boxed set anyways.. How cool would it be to include Orderfukt as a seperate book, within the Boulevard box.
It will likely read like an acid-induced dream of a Boulevard character, so it would TOTALLY be cool to include it in the box.

Publish it as a cheap chapbook, maybe with a cover illustration, or maybe just a zine-y cover.
Throw it in the box.

What do you guys think? Would it be worth the increased box price?
Would you be interested in getting a novella/novel with your RPG? (this will have a fairly high price point anyways, because I'm packaging it in a boardgame-box kind of format.)
