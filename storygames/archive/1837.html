('Mericans) Does Thanksgiving have any affect on your play?

Andy
November 2006 edited November 2006 in Story Games
Thanksgiving for me is a cool time of year; I meet with family and friends, and even though I inevitably work on both Thanksgiving and the Day After for either more money or extra time off days to use later, it's basically the beginning of a period which generally means:

Cold Outside.
Warm Inside.
Work is Sluggish.
Free time is Productive.
And more often than not, more gaming happens.

There's usually two things that happen specifically around the holidays: My normal weekly gaming group(s) cancel because too many people will be out of town. But the folks Left Behind often meet for, if nothing else, Thanksgiving weekend to throw down over a relaxing day.

Also, Thanksgiving itself for me usually means hanging with my folks, who throw this huge local bash with all sorts of folks and their families, so I usually hang with like 30 people, 20 of whom I met once or twice (the year before) and about 10 new people. I've been thinking of some post-dinner gaming, something not too hardcore (Drowning and Falling?)... but I'll probably just end up bringing Tsuro and Memoir 44, and probably just play the former.

How about you guys? Does the holiday season usually set off unique gaming experiences?

-Andy
