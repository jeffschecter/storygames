    \n Mortal Coil doesn't have a magic system so much as a system for generating magic systems. I've often thought that Mortal Coil's world generating system could be bolted on to other games which don't have a "meta" level, but in Mortal Coil, you generate facts about the world at the expense of your character's ability to use those facts. So, you'd need to think of a way to make the Mortal Coil and more traditional system interact.

    \n Well, by magic "system" I mean the ability to state facts (and costs) as you go.
    \n
    \nThe default (suggested) clerical spell ability in Fudge, for example, skips the notion of a "spell book" and replaces it with your ability to just use "divine petition" to call upon the dieties for whatever you need (presumably as long as it's within their portfolio and benefit to aid you). Based on your degrees of success, the GM then can narrate the results (whether it be damage to an opponent, healing to an ally, or purification of food/water, etc).
    \n
    \nI could see using Mortal Coil's magic "system" to cover the wizard spell ability and again, dodge needing a "spell book."
    \nSince whatever core system you'd be using (be it Fudge or D&D) wouldn't necessarily have "tokens", maybe you could add tokens in or, better yet, just use the degrees of success to determine how many "facts" you can state.
    \n
    \nexample: 3 successes.
    \nFact 1: I'm able to conjure a fireball.
    \nFact 2: I'm able to hurl it at my enemy.
    \nFact 3: It can cause lethal damage.
