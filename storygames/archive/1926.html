Hey, we all qualify, right? Or is that Unsavory Nerds? Patronizing Old Timers?

Anyway, I'm working on a small game about the lives and loves of photocopy repairmen. You can check out a draft here. The client service log is here.

I'd love some feedback! This is a light game you'd play in 90 minutes, maybe two hours if you hammed it up. There's some card handling, photocopier repair, and a lot of schadenfreude.

Specific questions:

Is it too fiddly for what it is? (what it is being a humorous, three player GM-less game)
Does the card mechanic make sense as written? Can you imagine playing this?
Isbo suggested ditching Need - what do you think? Necessary or extraneous?
Suggestions for refining attitudes and roles? Suggestions for other Needs?

Thanks!

    Is it too fiddly for what it is? (what it is being a humorous, three player GM-less game)
    No, it's fine. The cards sound very quick and easy.
    Does the card mechanic make sense as written?
    It's totally straightforward.
    Can you imagine playing this?
    Yes.
    Isbo suggested ditching Need - what do you think? Necessary or extraneous?
    No! Don't ditch Need! I mean, I haven't played the game, but I get the feeling that Need is pivotal.

    When I first read through the rules, Need reminded me of the pool refreshes in Shadow of Yesterday. You know how, in TSoY, you can get all your points back by, say, indulging in a night of physical excess? That's how I imagined Need: so, if you've got Slack as a Need, you can automatically get a bonus card by going for a cigarette while you should be working.

    If it was me, I'd make the Needs much more specific and indisputable (e.g. "Slack: You can get a bonus card by going for a cigarette at a crucial moment. Power: You can get a bonus card by yelling at the client."). And I'd take the decision away from the other players, and let the player who used the need claim his bonus card automatically.

    (If I was going for broke, I'd also suggest that Bonus Cards shouldn't just apply for invoking Needs, but should be a general kind of Fan Mail system: the other players could give you a Bonus Card every time you did something cool. However, Needs would be a guaranteed way of getting a bonus card. You know, if it was me.)
    Suggestions for other Needs?
    I'd actually break some of the current Needs down, making them more specific. So for Attention, I'd break into, say...

    Crybaby: Gain a bonus card if you sob on the client's shoulder
    Hypochondriac: Gain a bonus card if you tell the client about a disease which you're convinced you have.
    Frustrated Artist: Gain a bonus card if you sing, dance or recite.
    Job Hater: Gain a bonus card if you convince the client that Business Solutions is the worst employer in the world.

    Something like that, anyway.

    Also, the PDF is way too cleanly printed. When you do the finished game, it should definitely have a slghtly grey background and an inexplicable black line from top to bottom.

    Graham

    Here's what I've got:

    THE CATCH PHRASE
    Each game of Business Solutions needs a catch phrase, and the players will compete for the honor of coining it. Each player, in turn, should stand up and "read" an imaginary paragraph from the Business Solutions, Incorporated employee manual. The paragraph title should be a trendy buzzword or odious corporate drone-speak, couched in soul-crushing motivational language. The catch phrase refers, broadly, to repair technicians' skill at both fixing copiers and managing interpersonal crises. It should be vague and meaningless.

    After each player has presented a concept from the employee manual, the clear winner will probably be obvious. if not, a majority vote may be necessary. If no one can agree, either come up with three more or use Mojo, which is used throughout the rules as the default.

    Some examples of catch phrases:

    Six Sigma
    Q-Factor
    Total Quality
    Customer Satisfaction
    Interoperability
    The BS Way
    Performance
    l337 Sk1llz
    Effectiveness
    Excellence
    Finesse
    Value
    The Stuff
    Professionalism
    Sangfroid
    The Edge

    Of course, these can be combined into hideous amalgams - Total Value, Performance-Q, and so forth. Adding "plus" or "system" to any catch phrase makes it more potent.

    EXAMPLE OF CATCH PHRASE CREATION

    MIKE: Who's grabbing the employee handbook first?

    STEVE: I am. Let's see. Section Four: lan. It's a funny French word, but those four letters mean business. It was the notion of lan that caused the French army to get butchered in the First World War, but here at Business Solutions, Incorporated we've taken it to the next level with total quality. As a BSI employee, you'll be expected to embody the principle of lan every day - on the battlefield and off.

    VAL: Very nice. How about this: Section Seven: Love Plus. Here at Business Solutions, we love what we do - all of us, all the time. It's mandatory! And what's more, we love our customers - that's the Love Plus principle. Love Plus is why we go that extra mile. Love Plus is why we leave a mint on every copy machine we service. Love Plus - live it, love it.
