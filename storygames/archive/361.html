    If you look past the suits to the symbols that they were drawn from, and the mystical traditions that came before THEM, you'll find that the four suits are drawn from the four elements, Fire, Earth, Air, and Water.

    Spades = Swords = Fire (or Air, if you listen to Crowley)
    Clubs = Wands or Staves = Air (or Fire, if you listen to Crowley)
    Diamonds = Pentacles or Coins = Earth
    Hearts = Cups = Water

    Go take a look at Everway. Everway separates all four areas of human endeavor into those four elements, too.

    Firstly, I love that reply from Andy. ^.^

    Secondly, I like the idea of defining things into sets of four to be used with suits. Here is one from me, and one from Castle Falkenstein.

    Me

    Force (Degree of effect)
    Finesse (Delicacy/control over effect)
    Fortitude (Resistance to effects)
    Fallout (Represents ability to mitigate the impact of an effect. This sounds similar to Fortitude, but check the example, perhaps?)


    Magic: Herlin the Arch-Merlin seeks to mete out electrical punishment on the foul goblin shaman who imprisoned his fair love. To this end, he puts out, say...a Force 9, Finesse 3 effect to represent his lightning bolt. The shaman has warded himself against such curses, and resists with a Fortitude 5, Fallout 5 Voodoo talisman.

    We resolve by subtracting the Fortitude from the Force, to get a 4, and then take Finesse out of Fallout to get a 2. The result from the Fortitude/Force bit shows how "much" of the bolt actually gets into the SIS, as it were. The remainder from Fallout shows us how much of an effect the bolt has on the goblin bloke, in this case 4-2 = 2. What do these numbers mean? I've got no idea. Is Fort/Force and Finesse/Fallout redundant? I don't think so. Let us try another example.

    Debate: Demagogus the Long Winded and Patronizion the Simply Arch are engaged in a needlessly convoluted philosophical debate about the relative merits of cheese as opposed to yogurt. The Force 4, Finesse 11 position Demagogus takes is opposed by the Fortitude 5, Fallout 3 idea that Patronizion employs.

    4-5 is a -1, which would appear to working well for dear Patronizion, however...

    3-11 yields 9. What does this mean? Well, with a -1 Demagogus hasn't made a terribly potent argument...however, his high Finesse remainder we can roll over into a standing bonus to his Force on the next phase of the debate, like aquiring better footing or positioning in a duel or other martial contest.

    Basically, it introduces a simple distinction between the two. High Force is good for getting things done NOW, while a high Finesse will mean that the things you do next or later will be better. I imagine this could feed into fun decision making depending on the cards left in a players hand, as well as the refresh rate.
