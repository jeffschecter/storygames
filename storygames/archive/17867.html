    \n I feel the need to counter all the excitement for center support tables. If the center support is wide enough to support the table well, I tend to bump my knees into it. And if it's small enough to not bump my knees, it tends to not be stable enough. Also, they seem to have reducing stability - the longer you own one the more wobbly it gets. My current oak dining and gaming table is about 20 years old and is still rock solid - it could use a refinishing, but that's about it. I used to sit on it regularly, still do sometimes, hasn't hurt it a bit.

    That being said, if you go high quality, you may be able to avoid the wobble problem. But you're not going to have any luck with Ikea type stuff.

    I say just look at solid, quality, low maintenance dining furniture. If you're on hard floors, good caster chairs are nice for gaming, where you tend to get up and down a bit more than for dining. Heck, even if you're on carpet casters may be nice. Invest in good padding up front - it's much easier to re-cover a stained upholstery fabric 10 years ago than it is to compensate for crappy foam that's given out.

    My dad has a fondness for tilting, padded, caster chairs. If I regularly hosted a tabletop gaming group, that sort of seating would probably be what I would go for.
