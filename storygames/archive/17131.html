    \n I played a young girl with the ability to turn into a small bee queen and the ability to control bees and to percive the world trough them. She always wore her swarm of bees, either under her big parkas, or openly. A normal be swarm is about 80 000 bees.

    It was amazing power to play. Creepy, useful but with very clear limitations. It was also interesting to explore her emotional relationship to her swarm, and how very much it was a part of her and how vulnerable she felt without it.

    It was also interesting in conflict with other characters. Other saw her as useful spy, yet someone they could dispose off easily with a projectile weapon or fire if needed. She was someone you didn't want to get into a close up fight with. Not because you wouldn't win. You might very well kill her or beat her, but even if you did 80 000 bees would try to kill you.

    \n It wasn't actually my character, but when I was playing the Masks of Nyarlathotep CoC campaign, our party managed to leverage another character's special powers. Due to a combination of a head wound and reading forbidden tomes, this character had a perfect sense of time (and some other weird visions that aren't relevant). He had been separated from the rest of the party for a while, and then ran into one of the Big Bads, who was a super-powerful sorcerer and we were really scared of him. The Big Bad used some magic on him and he was sucked into a vortex. As part of the Keeper's description of him blacking out from the vortex, he described what sounded like a muffled, far-off explosion.

    On the other side of the vortex, this character realized he had been sent back in time several days. He managed to find the rest of the party and we figured out we actually had a huge advantage: we knew exactly where this Big Bad was going to be at a specific time, down to the second. And then we realized that the explosion sound could only be one thing: the surest way to kill anything in CoC. Real, actual explosives. We got a bunch of dynamite, we rigged it up at the location, and ambushed the Big Bad with it. Right after he sent this other character into the vortex, we totally blew him up, threw some high-fives, and went back to worrying about the other Big Bads we had failed to dispatch. It was pretty epic.
