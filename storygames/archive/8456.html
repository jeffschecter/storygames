\n What cities are meccas of indie gaming? What is it about the city that facilitated its attraction of indie gaming? I'm looking for places that seem to have reached critical mass in terms of creators and support groups.

If you happen to be from one of those cities, describe the city for me. What makes it different from a generic urban area?

===

The background for the questions. One of my favorite movies is "Free Enterprise". One of my favorite musicals is "Rent". If you are familiar with both, you know that they both center around a pair of creator-characters seeking to create (a movie in the case of Free Enterprise, music & film in the case of Rent). I've an idea noodling around in my head about a similarly somewhat self-referential RPG about Indie Game creators seeking to create their "perfect game".

So my questions are basically research, trying to create color to inspire the setting of said game. (And as I type this, I realize the setting could be 'where you are right now', but let's ignore that option for the moment.)

    \n I would like to submit Sonkaj\xe4rvi, Finland, as the municipality with perhaps the highest relative occurrence of indie gaming culture in the world. This declining countryside municipality with under 6000 people (2000 in the urban area) is the home to the indie game designer Eero Tuovinen, who pretty much single-handedly manages to provoke quite a bit of roleplaying activity in the area. The overwhelmingly dominant roleplaying games in the municipality during the last year have been Zombie Cinema, Solar System and a progressive variant of D&D.;
    \n
    \nI don't know that per-capita indie activity is a very useful gauge, though. The extra people that a place like New York has shouldn't distract the indie culture activists from their work, after all, which means that they effectively have a lot more activity and synergy.

    Posted By: BlueWhat cities are meccas of indie gaming? What is it about the city that facilitated its attraction of indie gaming? I'm looking for places that seem to have reached critical mass in terms of creators and support groups.
    If you happen to be from one of those cities, describe the city for me.
    I live in New York City, which I wouldn't call any sort of mecca, but provides an interesting answer to your question. I can think of 3 self-publishers in the 5 boroughs, and another dozen or so folks who play and think and tinker and half-design RPGs a lot. There are also a few other publishers or about-to-be publishers in nearby New Jersey.

    NerdNYC includes organizers who procure spaces to play, and a website where they announce events for board games, card games, and RPGs. Looking for a support group via NerdNYC.com or the Forge, I've consistently met with failure (although one NerdNYC regular who I know on another forum did give me a lot of individual feedback). However, my experience simply showing up to NerdNYC events has been quite the opposite -- meet people, play games with them, exchange email addresses, and then a semi-support group begins to cohere. Of course, it depends on timing -- I met one local designer right as he got super busy, and never heard back from him; but I met another right as he was looking for playtesters, and we instantly began corresponding.

    I hope that gives you some idea fodder! As for color, try cavernous, dark gray seminar rooms in Brooklyn office buildings. (Someone who knows BRIC, feel free to describe it better.)

    \n Based on criteria I just made up but did not communicate to anyone, I judge Perth, Australia, to be the mecca of indie gaming. Oh, also in my pants.

    I can see at least four reasonable criteria to use:

    1. Occurrence of people who play indie games.
    2. Occurrence of people who publish indie games.
    3. Occurrence of indie game play sessions.
    4. Total sales of indie games, based on publisher's location.

    #1-3 can be made more complicated by taking occurrence as a percentage of total population (or total gaming population) of the region. Thus indie gamers per 1000 gamers, or indie gamers per 1000 persons. Total sales in #3 could be count of copies or total of dollars (net or profit).

    Any big city is gonna have more gamers, so the fact that New York City is a gaming mecca should not surprise anyone. The fact that Greenfield, Mass. is a mecca (by almost any of the above criteria) should be somewhat more surprising.

    How about:

    2b. Occurrence of famous indie game designers.

    In that case, we're just pointing to the places that indie game designers live, preferably in small communes.* Greenfield, MA. Raleigh-Durham, NC. Laurel, MD (or, really, NoVA-DC-MD). NYC, NY.

    * It's true, Jason Morningstar lives in a hippie gaming commune. They live off the land, recycle their own waste (not usually into RPGs, don't worry), and make pen & paper games out of old, rusty hardware.

    \n Perhaps an useful definition of an "indie gaming centre" would be that any place where one can reliably start a new game and get a group together for indie games, recognized specifically as such, would work? As an added clarification one might require that this feat should be doable through networking - know an indie hobbyist there and get an in to a network of people who can get you your gaming fix.

    Based on this definition Finland has a couple of such centres: Helsinki, of course, where Arkenstone is headquartered. Then we have Oulu, which has a highly talented and flexible scene. A couple of other places certainly have lively gamers, but I couldn't outright name you a guy who can get you a group if you're going there.

      \n There was a point in NYC where we had a space where we met every other Friday for a few months and had 5-7 simultaneous games of indie goodness like With Great Power, PTA, Burning Wheel, Over the Edge, and many more. NYC has many people who are into indie games and design them. And many, many more who would potentially dig them.
