\n I have to make and teach a dealer's choice game based on the Kwanzaa principle of Ujamaa at a poker game on the 27th.

Ujamaa (Cooperative Economics) To build and maintain our own stores, shops and other businesses and to profit from them together.

I make up a lot of different poker games but this one is stumping me in terms of making something I really like. I want there to be a good incentive to cooperate but not an easy/obvious choice (like whether or not to take an extra card for fifty cents when you get a 4 in Baseball). I thought I'd turn to Story Games for some help, if not for the full game, then for some brainstorming.

I think I want each player to have 2 hole cards and one revealed card. And also some community cards, maybe even a standard Flop, Turn, and River as in Hold'Em. It needs to be able to play up to 10 players. I have the seed of an idea where players have "store windows" where they have "wares" in form of cards. I don't know whether they pay money or cards for the wares though, or how much control the seller will have over whom they can sell to.

If anyone would like to take a crack at coming up with their own Kwanzaa poker game, here are the seven principals:

Umoja (Unity) To strive for and to maintain unity in the family, community, nation and race.

Kujichagulia (Self-Determination) To define ourselves, name ourselves, create for ourselves and speak for ourselves.

Ujima (Collective Work and Responsibility) To build and maintain our community together and make our brothers' and sisters' problems our problems and to solve them together.

Ujamaa (Cooperative Economics) To build and maintain our own stores, shops and other businesses and to profit from them together.

Nia (Purpose) To make our collective vocation the building and developing of our community in order to restore our people to their traditional greatness.

Kuumba (Creativity) To do always as much as we can, in the way we can, in order to leave our community more beautiful and beneficial than we inherited it.

Imani (Faith) To believe with all our heart in our people, our parents, our teachers, our leaders and the righteousness and victory of our struggle.

    \n Once per hand a player can swap revealed cards with a neighbor, forming a poker cooperative. Either player can propose it. If the swap is done, then players play in synch:\n
      \n
    • Either both stays or both folds. One cannot abandon the other in the hand.
      • \n
    • If either hand wins, both players in the cooperative split the pot.
      • \n
    \nI know, by allowing either player to propose it, it doesn't really use the "community" definition as above. I suppose you could restrict it to a player only proposing it to their"community".

    Anyway, something to chew on.

    \n Poker is zero-sum (or negative-sum with a rake). Unless you change that, or obfuscate it, any time any players cooperate they will be doing so at the direct expense of those players not in their cooperative. Poker, unlike life, doesn't natively have a huge influx of resources from the sky. Here are two ways to get around this huge obstacle:

    - Make the choice to play the poker variant a negative expected-value choice, but the actual play a positive-sum game. The rewards for cooperation are produced by the prior agreement to essentially burn money sans cooperation. For example, each hand all players must ante 2 BBs to the fire. During play, any time you take an action to stay in (call or raise, but not fold) you may reveal all of your hidden cards to let everyone except you take 1 BB from the fire-ante. If there are not enough BBs in the fire-ante for everyone but you to get one, you may not reveal. After the hand, if someone revealed at least once, start a new hand with the remaining fire-ante still on the table; otherwise throw the fire-ante money into a fire and watch it burn, then start a new hand.

    - Each hand, whoever is first to act is the Cruel World. Deal that player an extra face-down card. If she wins any of the pot, each other player must give her 2 BBs.
