\n As you've probably noticed from the flurry of threads on the front page here, we've been fielding a lot of rules questions lately about Kiss A Dude: The Metagame of Homoerotic Tension in Story Gaming, by Jason Morningstar by Joe McDonald, Andy Henderson, Brendan Adkins et al. As the actual ruleset is very short, handwritten and only exists in my pocket notebook, I thought it would be simplest to avoid printing a supplement and just compile our responses in this thread.\n
    \n
  • p. 2 Strike out the sentence "Say yes or kiss a dude;" replace with "Kiss a dude or roll the dice."
    • \n
  • p. 3 (Example text) Yes, this does imply that if you get Joe McDonald's pants off, Joe McDonald must kiss a dude.
    • \n
    • A printing mishap cut off the footnote here: The only way to win a game of Kiss A Dude against Jackson Tegu is to let him stay in your hotel room, then stay up until 4:00 am discussing Kiss A Dude and/or the act of kissing a dude. If you lose, of course, you must kiss a dude.
      • \n
      • And come on, Jackson Tegu is right there.
    \n
  • p. 5-8 The authors of Kiss A Dude endorse safer sex, so yes, kissing through latex is still kissing a dude.
    • \n
  • p. 12 Pics or it didn't happen.
    • \n
  • p. 45-90 Here's the big one. Yes, this is a strong stance, but we remain firm on it: Once you have begun playing Kiss A Dude, all games are Kiss A Dude. Thus, you may win any game by, in effect, kissing a dude.
    • \n
    • p. 90b Conversely, if you lose any game, you must kiss a dude.
    \n
  • p. 101 Everyone is already playing Kiss A Dude.
    • \n
  • p. 102 In the even that there is no clear winner of Kiss A Dude, everyone loses Kiss A Dude.
    • \n
    • (See page 90b.)
    \n
\nI think that about covers it. The expansion (Not Tomorrow Night, But The Night Before) is due out in September. As for rumors that Joe McDonald and Brendan Adkins won/lost the first sanctioned game of Kiss A Dude on the last night of GameStorm 2009, our canon policy is simple: see page 12.

    Posted By: GrahamBrendan, what's the deal, here? Is kissing dudes inherently funny? Or am I missing a vital part of the joke?

    Graham
    \nGraham (and Julia, to some extent): Kiss A Dude is not a metagame about all dude-kissing everywhere. It is a metagame about all story gaming everywhere. A vast and beautiful amount of dude-kissing goes on every day that is not confined to story games, but that is not the creative agenda we wish to address.

    In my experience, however, if you sign up at a con for or start a weekly game night featuring a game that brings strong mechanics to the table to address interpersonal relationships, there is about an 85% chance that it will feature this situation: two self-professedly straight dudes, whose characters are locked in a passionate embrace, high-fiving or staring across the table at each other and nodding in satisfaction. (I personally leap at every chance to play the town bicycle, and make "cha-ching" noises when my in-game libido is successfully expressed.) Are we attaching a value judgment to this invented statistic? Not at all.

    Okay, yes, the mechanics feature the terms "win" and "lose," but people "win" and "lose" drinking games too. That's what Kiss A Dude is: the drinking game of erotic tension. How much can you take before you end up under the table?

    \n Hey Graham!

    I swear to God this is not fratboy or humor. I mean, come on. Do any of us look like fratboys? Do any of us look like we even watch fratboy movies?

    We are not sitting here tittering over the absolute scandal of men kissing. We are, completely without irony, trying to encourage it. Dudes kissing happens all the time in the fictional worlds where we play; we decided it was time to bring that into the real world, spotlight it, and celebrate it. Perhaps inevitably, the way we chose to do so was by making a game.
