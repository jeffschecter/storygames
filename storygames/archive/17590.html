    \n I got curious enough (because of this thread) and started reading the novel.

    Much to my surprise, I'm enjoying a lot more than I thought I would. It's written in a charming self-aware style, and its reframing of the Middle Earth story is, on one hand, so drastic as not to bother me as Lord of the Rings fan, but also, on the other hand, extremely compelling.

    The tale of Mordor, a fledgling industrial civilization, driven by philosophy and scientific inquiry, who the primitive Northern nations decide to destroy out of jealousy, somehow makes total sense.

    The translation (i.e. prose) is not fantastic, but the author's got both balls and wits. It's a fun read.

    And it's definitely a spy novel, as mentioned before.
