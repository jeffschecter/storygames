\n Some weeks ago, I was browsing a PDF of an old Italian RPG called Kata Kumbas (you can see some info here and here - there's some material for Savage Worlds coming out...).

While I was half-reading, half remembering my younger self at the age of 13 or 14, I felt like putting some music, and went to the Queen album "Innuendo".
I didn't know why, and then while the music played and I kept reading, I realized that these two things were irrevocably tied together in my memory.

Like a lot of people who played RPGs while they were teenagers, I had plenty of time back then to read manuals, read adventures and various RPG modules, to day-dream about the adventures I would play with my friends, to make new characters or NPCs or monsters that I would rarely use in a real game... I spent hours drawing maps, monsters, copying from manuals, or just browsing for illustrations to copy or tinker with.
And at the same time, I was on my journey to discover Queen (and the pain of being a teenager desperate and in love, but that's a whole different story).
Innuendo was one of the first CDs I bought (most of my music back then was on tape) and it would play in my stereo endlessly. I mean, literally, for hours and hours... I was switching from child music or my parents' or older friends' music, to the music that I liked.
I understood perhaps a word or two, so actually I had no idea what the Queen songs meant.

So Queen became the soundtrack, in my head, of fantasy RPGs.
And now I find that coupling these two activities, listening to Queen music and reading old fantasy RPG manuals, takes me on a strong, powerful memory trip back to those days.

So, then: do you guys have anything like that? (or something of the sort...)
Do you have a "soundtrack" of your games when you were teenagers that is meaningful only to you? Care to share?
