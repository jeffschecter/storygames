    Posted By: Eero TuovinenSo we're playingDeath's Door, then?
    Maybe. What's Death's Door?

    For a little while now, I've been thinking about plot armor as a game mechanic, specifically as a pacing mechanic. Characters have a numeric value which represents your plot armor, and when they run out of it, they die. When you get into a conflict, you can use points from your character's plot armor pool to give you more of an advantage in winning a conflict, but the more points you use the closer your character is to having no more plot armor. At the Dreamation Indie Roundtable, Ry brought up the idea of a choose-your-own-adventure / questionnaire for character creation (similar to that of Dread). This got me thinking about how interesting it might be for a character to sort of be tangentially aware of their plot armor. With that said, I came up with a couple questions for such a game. I was discussing them last night with Rachel, and she suggested I post them here.

    I was curious to see what peoples' reactions would be, but so far no one's biting.

    I've been thinking about plot armor as a game mechanic
    \nFor years now I've been thinking about running a freeform game where characters have a single stat, Fate. At the start of every session (or risky scene?) everyone rolls vs. their Fate. Those who succeed will survive, the others will die.

    Probably best played with everyone holding onto a few characters, so when one dies the next one is ready to go...
