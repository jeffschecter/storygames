\n So now that Sons of Liberty has vacated the design portion of my head, I'm poking at a little fighty game. The conceit here is that the fighty game consists of inflicting status effects on your opponents -- everything is a status effect, even damage (here, "hurt"). All Status Effects are rated in dice (3d6, 4d4+2d8).

Here are the status effects I have so far:

Hurt -- basically an increasing fallout pool that gets rolled each round; when you get too many high dice, you are forced out of combat.

Afraid -- reduces successes of attacks
Distracted -- reduces successes of actions and can completely cancel them
Enraged -- increases successes of attacks, reduces successes of anything else , mitigates Afraid
Hampered -- reduces successes of all actions
Poisoned -- a pool, rerolled right before Hurt, that adds dice to Hurt.
Steeled -- mitigates Afraid
Stunned -- reduces number of actions per round
Unbalanced -- increases successes of defenses to your attacks

The idea is that you can be hurt so bad you drop out, but it's equally likely that you'll be intimidated, outmanuevered, and frustrated into giving up.

What am I missing? What other things do you like to show up in a good fight sequence? What status effects would you like to use?
