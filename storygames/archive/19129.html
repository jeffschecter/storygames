\n This spring my brother and I are going to undertake the Pacific Crest Trail, a 2600+ mile long hike that runs from the Mexican border to the Canadian border. With weight being such an issue, obviously hauling my Player's Handbook and fifty odd dice is out of the question. But does anyone know a lightweight, repayable RPG that has 2+ players, minimal prep, and games that can be finished in 1-3 hours?

    \n Me & my girlfriend have been playing a whole 2-players campaign of Greg Hutton's Remember Tomorrow while traveling, sometimes on airport cafeteria tables, sometimes out on the beach, once on the Helsinki-Tallinn ferry\u2026 and everything I needed to carry fit in about the weight & size of a notebook: a bunch of character sheets, a handful dice and a handful tokens. But we were both already quite familiar with the game and didn't need carry the actual rulebook with us!

    Mars Colony is also very lightweight on components and a dedicated 2-players game, but I'm told you Americans tend to play it fast, as a one-shot game. My experiences with it are more in the campaign department. Depends on play-style, I guess.

    My bottom line being: there's enough possibilities that you might want to start trimming our suggestions down by topic and style.
