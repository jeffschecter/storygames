    \n There might be a few games that you Just Must Play, so you want to be right on top of the sign-ups when they come out. Just as likely, though, there will be embarrassing riches of games you want to play in the same slot, so you can specify 2nd and 3rd and 4th choices and Vinnie will sort it all out for you.

    Don't worry too much about getting into games.

    Also, Dreamation does something a lot of other cons do not do. They post a signup sheet for each game on the wall, with pre-registered people already slotted, and blank lines for anyone else who wants to jump in. That is, getting into a game even with zero planning is extremely easy, provided you're willing to try something you haven't done before. Plus there's usually a huge amount of D&D LFR or Pathfinder or the like, in case all your indie/storygame choices are full.

    Alsoalsowik, I will have a handful of low-prep games with me, and anyone may approach me and request me to run something for them. If I'm free, I'm game. Either we'll find an open table somewhere or we'll head to a hotel room and game for a few hours. Let's call it "Adam On Demand." Things I like to run: 13th Age, Dungeon World, Monsterhearts, Apocalypse World, My Life with Master, carry: a game about war, Annalise, Dogs in the Vineyard, In a Wicked Age...
