\n I'm trying to brainstorm important elements of setting \xe2\u20ac\u201c setting considered broadly and in general. Story-Games' collective brainstorm-fu is strong, so \xe2\u20ac\u201c anyone want to add to or expand my list?

(Because this is an early-stage brainstorm I'm not spending a lot of thought on exactly how these interlink just yet.)

\xe2\u20ac\xa2\tInstitutions
o\tSocial class/caste
o\tEducation
o\tFamily/kin/marriage
o\tGovernment
o\tJustice
o\tEconomics (that one has sub-elements, for sure \xe2\u20ac\u201c any economists want to list them out?)
o\tVoluntary associations
o\tCrime

\xe2\u20ac\xa2\tIdeas/Memes
o\tIdeologies
o\tWorldviews
o\tKnowledge, including ways of getting new knowledge

\xe2\u20ac\xa2\tTechnologies
o\tTransport
o\tCommunication and Information
o\tManufacture

\xe2\u20ac\xa2\tPower and political relations (closely linked with institutions, I know)
o\tHierarchies
o\tOppressions
o\tRebellions
o\tAlliances
o\tEnmities
o\tConstituencies
o\tIdentity groups (e.g. ethnic or racial)

\xe2\u20ac\xa2\t"Sets" (locations)
o\tLocations of unique significance (like capitals, palaces, shrines)
o\tGeneric locations (like taverns, streets, warehouses)
o\tGeography (how locations are connected and their distance from one another)

\xe2\u20ac\xa2\tEvents
o\tRegular or seasonal events
o\tHistorical events

\xe2\u20ac\xa2\tPersonalities
o\tHistoric people (founders, leaders, heroes, traitors, oppressors)
o\tMythical, legendary and fictional people
o\tContemporary people (celebrities, leaders, scapegoats)

    \n I think a checklist approach to something as complex as an entire society is a great big "this way madness lies."

    I'd recommend you come up with two or three things that you personally think are engaging, exciting elements of setting - - ideally in the context of a specific game design -- and then build out from there. My three things would probably be military forces, sociopolitical elites, and family structure, but yours are probably going to be radically different and equally valid for the purpose.

    \n Mike, is this for a "design your setting for your game that will use Pentasystem" sort of chapter?

    If so, I'd second what Sydney suggested -- this is a maddening way of going about this -- and instead work up some rules that give players a simple foundation to start with and then support them in elaborating the setting through play.
