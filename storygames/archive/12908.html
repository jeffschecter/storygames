    \n Bricks are made of "matter." The brick's molecular structure is decompiled and reconfigured to form whatever material is desired. A brice can be literally brick, a brick of goatsilk-infused putty a chunk of coal or compressed dust bunny poop. This is one reason why you can burn tech to succeed in a materials challenge. You can just decompile your tech!

    \n I think the most efficient way to pack matter would be iron blocks. With printing technology being matter arrangement and rearrangement, it makes sense to compact the matter into the most dense packet that is safe to store.

    Of course, the old Fremen adage about water likely applies to matter as well, to whit: the station's mass is best stored as a useful tools somewhere. Bricks are likely extruded from garbage collection routines and routed by the Aggregate to where it is needed by monitoring Flow expenditures. Properly and tightly managed, this sort of thing can make sure that matter is not sitting in a useless or abandoned form for very long. The intervening form is only material in terms of transport and feeding conveniences. A MRCZ dedicated to aiding the Aggregate in this process would do well, Flow-wise.

    As to making new bricks, outside the recycling loop, the best way to do this is to periodically capture matter from Trojans, Saturn's Rings, or other inert mass structures that can be boosted to a location close to CHOIRs. That way, an imperfect recycling loop can be supplemented.

    If matter/energy conversion is part of the group's vision of the technology of Freemarket, then all that is needed to supplement this process is a truck-ton of solar arrays or reactors.
