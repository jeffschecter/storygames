I've playtested That's Drama a fair amount with locals, now I want to see if people can play it without me ... so I'll send a free deck to anyone who offers to record a playtest. Well, the first six people who offer to record a playtest, anyway. Or ...you could just buy it at cost if you don't want to record a playtest



As you can see, the cards are a sort of middle-ground between the genericness of Tarot decks (and other RPGs that use Tarot-like decks of cards) and the specificity of playsets and oracles for Fiasco and IAWA.
Although they're more specific than a Tarot deck, the tropes on the cards could mostly be found in anything from fantasy to present day to space opera to whatever.

There are also these cool Motivation cards:



What is That's Drama?

I was inspired by some threads on here to try my hand at making a really accessible story game. So it uses cards that I tried to make look "fun" and "approachable", and the basic games you play with them are very simple. But I also wanted to get kind of Nintendo-ish with it - although it's accessible, I still want hardcore players to enjoy it, so there are advanced rules that let people like us stab each other in the heart with our ennui. I'm particularly proud of the motivation cards, which practically force players to get in each other's dishes.

When you play it with the simple rules, it's a silly storytelling game in the vein of Once Upon A Time or Munchausen or Pass-Around Storytelling.

When you play it with the complex rules ...
It's like Fiasco with cards, where you get to choose the setting ... and it's possible to have a happy ending.
Maybe a better way to put is it's like GM-less In a Wicked Age with cards ... and constraints on your best interests ... and you get to choose the setting.
Or ... Mountain Witch, with more dark fates, and they're not hidden, and there's no witch?

You could, of course, also use the cards for other games - to quickly make best interests for IAWA, for example - or create a starting situation for another game and then use that game's resolution system ...
I'm really hoping people mash it up with stuff.

Anyhow, more details here.
