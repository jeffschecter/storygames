      Well, since you're asking for suggestions, I have one: Be Transparent.
      \nSuggestion taken!

      I tend to try to post in sinking/closed threads why the action is happening. But now that there's more people, we'll likely have to come to some internal methodology.

      In the past I've also tried to end a closed thread with a summary of the good points to be pulled from it (I later found out this was John S's modus operandi, and have been learning about how to do it better from him!), but that doesn't always happen. In places where it really needs it, we'll be posting summaries.

      Once the heat is off of closed threads, or once bannings have occurred, we wait a little while and update the rules when needed with what does and doesn't work. Or in threads that get hot, maybe a quick deconstruction of why they got hot, and how to help it so that it doesn't happen in the future (for example: There were some threads that got hot and folks upset because we realized the thread owner had expectations they didn't state but weren't being met: Later on we discussed that publicly, and haven't hit that kind of issue in years because people adopted that strategy).

      But, sometimes myself or others are forgetful. There was an incident a few years ago where I banned someone for mercilessly bullying another poster (mentally and emotionally ill at the time, and this was well known), although their behavior had already bottomed out at that point it happened. After that, I've stated several times publicly in various QA and Forum Discussion threads that Bullying was a "Bannable Offense; you may or may not get a warning before it happens"... But I just realized today that I forgot to go back and include that in the actual stickied "Rules" thread up there. I'll be correcting that oversight soon, including some additional rules/suggestions based on other trying or inexcusable behaviors.
      A lot of this amounts to "Don't be a shitty human being", but -- and no sarcasm intended -- some folks don't realize that their behavior at the time is all that shitty. It can be subjective at times. But guidance helps, and the rules thread is in need of a good dusting.

      So, honestly (yeah, honestly), your suggestions have been taken, and we're even discussing how to implement them further.

      Having said that, if folks are looking for complete transparency in all moderation, or democracy, they're going to be disappointed. Neither is going to happen here.\n
      Don't be the secret police cabal that whispers suggestions to people and silently sinks and closes threads for no stated reason. Moderate openly and publicly, so that, when someone's behavior needs correction, everyone learns why and that event informs the behavior of the whole forum. If some thread needs correction, say something about it so that, when a thread goes bad, everybody understands the moderator judgement behind it.
      \nWhen an entire thread gets toxic, you're correct, some suggestion pre-close/sink as to why should be helpful. Other times it's self explanatory. Yet other times it looks self-explanatory to some but is not to others (and this is a recent thing, and apologize for that).

      But we're not going to publicly do the RPGNet "mod voice" thing and publicly correct people's behavior except when it's egregious or particularly harmful (racist, etc). It's confrontational, it "gets people off", and is just not as helpful as a private whisper/note to the offender. Basically, this:

      * (whisper) "Hey John, I really like what you had to say in the first three paragraphs, but that last one where you called out that other guy, that was kind of crappy and it's not going to go anywhere good. If you must get it out, email or whisper him, but otherwise if you would please just erase that bit, thanks."
      vs
      * (public) (in red text) "John, you will not discuss such a thing again, please erase that last paragraph and never call out someone on their behavior."

      It's... well, yeah, a little more work up-front, but in the long run (harmful flare-ups, people becoming bitter at being called out, etc) it seems to work historically. And people respond much better to it. Luckily it's still small enough to do this.\n
      This thing that's been going on, where literally no moderation happens in public, it's like malcontents just silently disappear in the night. I would have no idea that Zak S was banned for physically threatening wundergeek, except that someone gave me those pieces of data individually and I had to assemble them myself. If physically threatening wundergeek is a bannable offense, let everybody know so they don't run afoul of that rule!
      \nYou had no idea he was banned because his banning was none of your business. It was the result of about two weeks of private conversations between me, him and the moderation staff, the contents of that discussion also none of your business. And the discussions between me, wundergeek and others that happened before and after his banning were also none of your business, and tying her to Zak's banning somehow is actually kinda dangerous to her (some random RPG dude started a witch hunt a few weeks ago to find out the connection between wundergeek and Zak after wundergeek's public post on the Gaming As Women blog about being physically threatened, the results were childish and fucked up, and confounded us all).

      So your facts that you put together aren't correct: It wasn't cut and dry, it was a decision 2 weeks in the making, and to quote The Dude: "Well, okay, you're not privy to all the new shit, so uh, you know..." Zak S was not banned for physically threatening people, he was banned in a two-week leadup of threads and posts because his communication style wasn't working with the folks' here or this site's goals. I guess I have to accept folks jumping to weird conclusions, but that leads me to the next point below re dopamine and moderation.

      Bannings and the like will continue to be private. I don't believe in "public executions", they have no place hear and only serve to entertain people's basest, most disgusting behaviors.

      The same is mostly true for public moderation: It gives that welcomed dopamine high of self-righteousness to the people who watch their enemies suffer. It gives that welcomes dopamine high of self-righteousness when their allies are made to suffer equally for the same transgressions, as they compose a long post justifying why this shouldn't be the case.

      It's social porn for looky-looks. That's all public bannings usually amount to.

      It's usually disgraceful, and serves to appeal to ugly (albeit natural) human behaviors.

      Then after it happens, wave after wave of bitter replies from allies questioning the judgement and enemies reinforcing it.

      Look at RPGNet. The Trouble Tickets forum is one of the most viewed forums constantly after all the major gaming/tangency ones, something like #6 on people watching it. It's not because people are there establishing the constitution of RPGNet in marble columned halls, it's because there are 50+ people at any given time getting off to watching social judgment and punishment being carried out, soaking up the details like licking the mirror.

      But on the matter of: "The behavior that gets someone banned should be publicly noted in the rules so that people know".
      On one hand, that is absolutely correct, and it will happen. Might not be Right After, because it usually takes a while to calmly deconstruct what went down, but eventually.
      And on the other hand, "really?" Most of this comes down to people acting like shitty human beings. If someone can't tell they're being a shitty human being day to day, should they really find a place here?
      Still, recent history has shown us that there are definitely blurry/fuzzy lines, especially with people who are helpful and productive, or where some people see a line crossing and others don't. But in the end, it will all eventually get deconstructed, and rules/suggestions made so that these incidents don't repeat themselves.

      Since it was brought up, the Zak S situation was not a cut-and-dry issue, and it took us a while to figure out the disconnect that was happening. It'll take us a little longer to turn that all into helpful advice. Certainly a new Rule or two will be made, but there's a lot under the surface that requires a thoughtful approach, more "advice for conduct or getting the most out of the forums" than "hard unbreakable rules".\n
      Or, you know, continue operating under the reign-of-terror methodology that Andy has constructed since Day One. Shut down criticism and negative sentiment by citing the "zen" and silently shutting down people that make you feel weird. It works so well at Etsy.
      \nThe "zen" is quoted a lot. It's always been synonymous with "don't be a shitty human being", "don't display self-entitled, ugly behavior" and all sorts of things that most human beings capable of empathy and discourse can understand, but others seeming can not. Looks like I'm going to eventually have to crack open the zen to clearly, cleanly explain the acceptable and not acceptable behaviors here.

      This place will always be a happy tyranny. The only difference is that there are now four nice new tyrants.

      I understand as well that not everyone needs to like or want to talk with me: I'm hoping that with John S, Kira and Zac there will be alternatives for people who feel weird about discussing issues directly with me.

      -Andy
