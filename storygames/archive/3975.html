Simple 20 to see print!

\n
\n
\n
\n\njdrakeh \n\n\n

\n\nAugust 2007 \n edited August 2007 in Directed Promotion
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n Well, more specifically, Simple 20 is going to be available via the vanity press outlet of Lulu. I'm currently laying out and editing the print edition, which will include all of the standard Simple 20 rules (edited for clarity), the most popular options from the appendices, an original fantasy setting, and various pieces of stock art that you will probably recognize from other game products (yes, they're used under license).
\n
\nI have yet to decide upon a fixed price point, though I have decided that I want the game to remain affordable to the gamer on a budget (because I'm there myself). Although subject to change, I don't expect the paperback version to cost more than $7 (US). The PDF will be available for free, as always, though there will be a few 'extras' in the print version that the free download will be lacking (notably, the new setting).
\n
\nNow, I know that Simple 20 isn't a story game (or even a game, really) but it was the encouraging number of peer votes (22) that the system received at this year's Indie RPG Awards that encouraged me to move forward with the project. I figure that if the current clarity-challenged version could merit this level of interest in a decidely non-d20 oriented environment, there's a good chance that it can succeed as a print product. More specifically, it has survived Ron's suggested 'ashcan' release test.
\n
\nThere have been some inquiries from two d20 publishers already (concerning permissions to market compatible products) and the largely positive fan feedback at ENWorld and elsewhere came as a welcome surprise. The Indie RPG Award votes were the final impetus in the decision to move forward, however. So, if you voted, thank you so very much for giving me the little push that I needed
\n
\nWatch this thread or the official Simple 20 forum at the Miscellaneous Debris site for updates over the coming two months. If you have any questions, feel free to ask them here, there, or on the ENWorld "other" forum. I'll answer as quickly and directly as time and legalities permit. Thank you for your interest!
