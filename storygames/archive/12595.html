\n I've got a game idea I want to prototype. It's just an idea right now; I want to test it out first, then, if it shows promise, I'll run some different iterations. For this, I want someone who can draw.

No demand for long-term commitment from either side; I'd be really happy just to have cool sketches for a first playtest version. If I go on with the design and you want to be in on further iterations and the finished product, that's just great. We'll both be in on equal terms; since this is indie RPG design, that obviously means we'll make loads of money (yes, kidding).

I'd need the following for playtest:

- A map sketch of an island with evocative, strange locations (maybe stuff like The Glass House, Belltower Ruins, Two Sisters' House, Statues on the Cliffs)

- Four or five character sketches

I'm thinking Mervyn Peake, maybe a tiny dash of steampunk, maybe some Myst. A lot of artistic freedom. Basically, you draw stuff, I add some descriptions and weave it into the design.

If you're interested, just send me a whisper in this thread! I'll tell you all about the concept, which, at the moment, is only in my head.

    \n I've had two playstormy sessions, and it's going really well. It will be more game-like than the freeformy things I've done lately; but there are some mechanisms that I really like in there. Everything's still subject to change, though.

    Favorite part: In addition to location cards that you flip when moving to certain areas on the map, there are character action cards to flip - for instance, if someone prays, or someone reads a Mysterious Book, or two characters fight, you flip that event card and see what happens. The game is your GM.

    Some events have dramatic causality - if a character gives up, that might cause the group to be attacked by a pack of wild dogs, for example.
