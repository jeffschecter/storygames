\n Recently (for Christmas) I purchased a cruise for my wife and me. Since I booked it in the off season, the cruise tickets ran me about $360 each. That included all port charges and taxes. I was a happy little camper. 5 nights in the Caribbean with my wife. All the food taken care of. All the entertainment, free. If I didn't drink any alchohol or buy anything in the ship's store, $360 was all I'd pay (other than actually getting to the dock) for the whole trip. Not bad. I couldn't fly to Cozumel, book a hotel and eat for 5 days for the same amount. It was actually a fairly cheap vacation.

That's when a really strange thought hit me. I spent more than $360 last time I went to GenCon and I guarantee you that the food probably wasn't as good as what is on the cruise ship. It also occurred to me that I could probably get tickets for even cheaper if there was a group rather than just my wife and me. Hmmm. Would anyone else be interested in a gaming event on a cruise ship in the Caribbean? It's just a thought at the moment but I figured I'd see what kind of interest was out there.

~Andrew

    \n I just got back from a week-long cruise to Cozumel, Belize, Roatan, and Freeport. There was one evening that I thought, "I wish I could gather up some people for gaming." The rest of the time, I was having way too much fun to consider it. I think that's the fundamental flaw with the gaming cruise idea. The actual cruise itself is so fucking amazing that you won't want to game.

    Granted, I went on long excursions at each of the four ports. (For the record: I explored ruins and a zoo and swam in an underground river in a rainstorm in Xcaret, Playa del Carmen; I climbed the 140' pyramid of Xunantunich in Belize; I zip-lined from the top of Roatan Island to the shore then snorkeled in the world's second largest barrier reef; I swam with dolphins in Freeport, Bahamas.)

    While aboard the ship, there was plenty of fun stuff to do. Two nights, we had 2-3 hour long dinners at the Emerald Supper Club (a $30 upgrade from the normal-but-fantastic dining room experience -- the supper club is the best gourmet food I'd ever had). I played ping pong. I saw comedy, illusion, and dance shows. I lost small amounts of money in the casino. I partied with friends. Occasionally, I just sat on my balcony and stared at the vast expanse of the ocean as the sun rose while enjoying free room service.

    In short, cruises are awesome, but I don't know how much time I'd have for gaming. Some.

    Oh yeah, pictures.
