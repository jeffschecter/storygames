    \n I had an excellent weekend at Games Expo. I worked the Collective Endeavour stall and met a load of really nice people - some new and some who I haven't laid eyes on in years, including a little reunion of sorts for my old gaming group from 14 years ago!! I got chased by a dalek called Clive, nearly got into a bitching contest with some Polish Lancers, avoided spending 'Money You Cannot Afford' on loads of really cool minatures, sadly crumbled under the pressure and spent 'Money You Cannot Afford' on Nine World and two copies of Shadow of Yesterday (one for me, one for one of the gang back home) and generally lapped up all of the gaming goodness. I even got to watch Coyote Ugly all the way through for the first time in ages ... although I don't think that had much to do with the con!
    \n
    \nFrom a more personal point of view Duty & Honour got its first real public airing and it went down really well. 22 Frenchmen were despatched across the demos with no British casualties!
    \n
    \nAll in all, very good and loads of fun
    \n

    \n I had lots of fun.

    The Collective Endeavour stall was very focussed and slick. We got lots of people into demos. We did OK in terms of sales: overall on the stall, we made a profit.

    I bought Mongoose's Traveller, because Matthew Sprange talked to me and made it sound good. It does look quite good, although I was rather hoping for 30 pages of setting, which isn't there. It's all rules. I mean, I'm not entirely sure what the point of buying big thick books is if they don't have a metric ton of setting.

    Graham

    \n\nJune 2008 \n

    \n Games Expo was a lot of fun and I did OK games and sales-wise.

    I sold 8 Solipsist and 4 Best Friends in total, so that was pretty good going. A couple of copies of Solipsist were also comped for review, so fingers crossed on that front.

    I ran a game of Solipsist on the Saturday afternoon and that went pretty well with 3 players.

    It featured clockwork (and clock-faced) robots, a deadly Stannah Stairlift, a returning dead father infested with the Shadow, a character Changing Reality to be married to the other PCs in sequence so that they were all next of kin for the purposes of entering a morgue, and the school caretaker becoming the King of Monaco (and I guess by extension Monaco becoming a Kingdom rather than a Principality).

    Looked like there were about 2100 people there over the two days, and for that number the sales don't match up. That's because there was a wide mix of card gamers, wargamers, board game enthusiasts as well as role-palyers there. D&D4e was getting a lot of play and they had a Living Dungeon too. The David Tennant look-a-like-ee was quite funny.

    Oh, I had a good time getting interviewed by Jack from RPGMP3.com (and "geek-a-saurus" is the podcast) too. So that will be online at some point in the future.

    \n Indeed, GE08 was very positive from the Collective Endeavour point of view. The stall was buzzing with positivity, many, many demos were run, people were learning, helping and generally getting into the spirit of being a collective.

    Sales weren't stellar, but there were some notable successes (such as Solipsist, Fearmonger and Revenge of the B-Movie). Revenge of the B-Movie was far and away our biggest seller on the day, with 40 copies shifted. Great stuff.

    \n I'd probably expect to do those sales numbers at Dragonmeet (or slightly better). So maybe I sold the same, or just below, in two days that I might have made in one at Dragonmeet.

    On the other hand there were a lot of traders there who saw our books. And plenty of gamers who were demoed to but didn't buy at the time. My thinking is that they might pick up our books in the longer term over the internet or in local game shops.

    My perception is that Dragonmeet gets a narrower slice of the general hobby market that is much more into roleplaying.

    Oh, I'm looking forward to hearing all the interviews and what Malcolm has to say on Hot War.

    \n Although I attended the show last year this was my first full, two-day Expo and I thoroughly enjoyed it. Many thanks to everyone who submitted to Amateur Interview Hour (and with Gregor "hour" was almost right! Hadn't realised I was monopolising so much of his time) the fruits of which should start to appear shortly. Hal, ex-pat mastermind behind RPGMP3.com, is putting the first podcast episode together this week and should be including some interview material, although I'm not sure whose: he may decide to group all of the Collective interviews together, or string them out over subsequent fortnightly podcasts.

    As expected I spent more than I hoped (Dave Blewer sold me on "Sundered Skies," Gregor sold me on "Solipsist" and it was all I could do to resist "Traveller" after interviewing Matthew Sprange... I really need to firm up my willpower) and indeed the only reason I didn't buy "Covenant" was a serious lack of cash and the knowledge that my local store has copies, so it'll have to wait until payday. The "Living Dungeon" event was interesting (Yog-Sothoth.com has posted edited video highlights) and the Stromtroopers and "Doctor" added a lot of interest for kids. The atmosphere was excellent. My only regret was having to cancel a couple of games in order to fulfil my podcasting commitments. Curse this jet-setting media lifestyle!
