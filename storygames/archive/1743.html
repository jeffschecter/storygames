    Character Name:
    JAXRIL DAV0S (usually said in a "heavy metal voice" while throwing "the horns")

    Archeype/Class/In A Nutshell:
    Ambassador/Aristocrat and Gene-modded human

    System:
    Savage Worlds, SF style game

    Stuff he's good at:
    Diplomacy, Speech, Investigation, Getting into Trouble, Being Aristocratic and Haughty, and Hand-to-Hand Combat

    Stuff he's bad at:
    Non-ritualized "yucky" combat (he's got hand-to-hand skills but nothing else). Keeping his mouth shut. Keeping his curiosity at bay. Breathing in regular atmosphere without his rebreather.

    What you like about this character in general:
    The fact that he's a bit alien. This character is set in a kind of "high powered Firefly" style universe, and he's the only outsider in the group. Also, he only has limited combat skills in a combat-heavy game, and yet he feels like he's capable because of all his social skills and resources. But the "just a little left of human" alienness is the most interesting thing, I think. Also has a mysterious background: Doesn't talk about home much, even though he's a noble.

    What you like most about playing this character: The alienness is fun to play out. I can make him flaky or steadfast, aggressive or innocent, and just sort of go, "That's the way of his people" to shrug it off. Also, I like playing the high-power social guys when the system can support it, it's fun being the diplomat, and finding new ways to get the crew into some possible trouble.

    Any poignant thoughts after playing this character?:
    Not really. Maybe one thing; in most games with a structured combat system, I tend to max it out to really enjoy the features of combat and the like. Savage Worlds is all about the combat, and yet I (as a player) am having a blast with this low combat high ephemeral social skills character. It makes me want to try this kind of character out in other systems.

    Anything else:
    I made this dude from scratch in about four minutes, including all his Gene-Modification fiddly bits. And yet, he's more fun to play than characters in systems that forced me to put 2-3 hours into character generation.

    -Andy

    Character: John Avon, callsign Cancer

    Game: Post apocalyptic supers, defending the sealed habitats mankind survives in.

    Concept: A man who's body's repair systems are rapaging out of control. His cells reproduce continuously and at an insane rate. Its like his entire body is a still-funtional cancer.

    When he was first discovered he was a tumescent, predatory mass of flesh and bone the size of a small village and insane with pain. It took extensive drug treatments to bring his systems back under control and return him to human form. Even now he needs a machine in his chest pumping industrial-grade poisons throughout his body to keep him in check.

    As an agent of Aegis he has trained to bring his body under control. With effort, he can diect the growth of his body, to "hulk out", or sprout bony spikes from under his skin. Mostly though, he just doesn't die. He ahs a custom made uniform composed largely of straps and "EZ-burst" seams, designed to give way rather than break when he lets lose.

    I came up with some other background stuff about personality and vague memories of experiments before he became a living tumour. But to be honest I barely cared about that fluff. This character was all about the kewl powerz.

    System: Truth and justice

    Stuff he's good at: Staying alive. Regeneration is his marquee power and I stunted it to the hilt (in defiance of people who said such a thing wasn't possible). He has let people punch straight through him, only to heal up around their arm and trap it inside him. He has torn off his own arm to plug a burst chemical pipe with "instant-tumour". Against an undead "necrosphere" he jumped straight inside it so that his body's defense systems would break it down as they would any dead tissue. Jumping from a stupid hieght, knowing that his legs would un-shatter themselves straight away.

    Stuff he's not so good at: Keeping under control. A couple of times the safeguards have broken down. or proved insufficient and he's gone all Tetsuo, requiring instant action by everyone else to prevent a serious disaster. Also, he can't dish the pain out nearly as well as he can take it. He has to win fights by attrition.

    What I like about him in general: Personal injury being a non-issue. I know that no matter what crazy stuff I try, or no matetr how badly the dice fall I can be up and active again in a turn or two. I never had to worry about sitting out. Even when my main role was getting pummeled, I was able to have fun with that by banter and OTT descriptions of what was happening , since it just wasn't as serious for this character. "Ouch, my organs. Ah well, plenty more where they came from. My spleen! Well, I probably wasn't using it anyway..."

    Plus, it's wonderfully badass to be able to take everything the world can dish out, then stand back up again and just say "meh" (which sort-of became this character's catch-phrase).

    Also, great visuals. Growing fingers down the length of his arms to help grapple an electric jellyfish. Melding one forearm together into a dense bony spike/shield. Or just having fun pulling himself back together after a particularly bad experience.

    Poignant thoughts: I wasn't entirely satisfied with how the character worked within the system. I often had to fudge things from the player side, burning hero points to try and justify the actions I was describing and needing to point out that stuff that wasn't explicitly a part of my regeneration power was just colour. A lot of the time it felt like a surcharge was being applied for being creative. And the whiff factor defintiely came into play, which was especially frustrating after I'd loaded a roll up with hero points. Still, I recognise that at least a part of that was due to me trying to "colour outside the lines" to begin with.

    Character Name: Lutra Spirit-Talker

    Archetype/Class/In a Nutshell: Albino ratkin shaman with a bit of a martyr complex

    System: The Shadow of Yesterday

    Stuff She's Good At: Talking people into or out of things, especially other ratkin. Sticking up for the downtrodden. Healing the physically and mentally wounded, whether she knows them or not. Getting all spooky and having prophetic dreams and talking to the Ancestors, then turning around and behaving with extreme practicality and level-headedness. Walking into situations that she knows are dangerous, because she also knows it's just what she has to do.

    Stuff She's Bad At: Sticking up for herself, behaving selfishly (in a good way), and believing in her own right to happiness. Fighting; even though her stats at this point are relatively good, she still seems to do really poorly whenever she has to rely on her teeth and claws. Ignoring a cry for help even when she suspects it's a trap.

    What You Like About This Character in General: I love the contrast between her outward and inner lives. Outwardly she's fiercely determined and gives the appearance of always knowing what's right and what should be done next. She has enormous compassion and a great deal of spiritual power, and she comes off as having this aura of... not holiness, really, but blessedness. Inwardly, she's extremely scared and lonely. She's never really had friends before, and she still has trouble with the idea that other people might want to care for and protect her the way she does for them. This is especially enjoyable to me because when I first conceived of the character, I had the outward stuff, but not all the inward conflicts, and once those came together she really started to pop. I also like that while she's overall a very serious character, she does have a certain amount of droll humor that comes out at unexpected moments.

    What You Like Most About Playing This Character: The thing I'm enjoying most right now is her relationship with Tip, another ratkin PC. Tip is pretty much her exact opposite, but Lutra's had visions about a great destiny in store for him, so she's assigned herself to protect him. Along the way they have become very close friends and trust each other deeply, but the whole "friend" concept is still kind of fuzzy to Lutra and she's struggling with letting down her guard. In spite of this they often fall into easy banter with each other, which is a hell of a lot of fun in an otherwise super-serious campaign.

    Poignant Thoughts: I feel like Lutra really deserves a full-scale meltdown/freakout at some point, but I'm not sure if emotionally I'm able to do this. Every time I've thought it was going to happen, she just cranked down and muscled through. Maybe it just wasn't time yet.

    Anything Else?: I'm super excited by Lutra's current arch-nemesis. It's kind of a cliche -- Lutra's sister, presumed dead, has just reappeared and wrongly blames Lutra for the destruction of her former warren. But it's such a perfect quandry for Lutra, because her sister's her last link to her old life, but she's also insane and threatening the people Lutra cares about. So Lutra wants to protect her chosen family and carries a lot of anger toward her sister -- but she's also compelled to heal the sick, and her sister needs healing. It's so nice and emotionally crunchy.

    This is from "Basingrad", a one off PtA game I played at CON-crete Cow this last weekend:

    System: PtA

    Series: "Basingrad". The series was a very dark comedy set during an uneasy cold war between regular folk and the "Living Impaired" some years after a Romero style zombie uprising. Some Zombies, (the ones who got to eat enough brains during the uprising) are basically the same as they were when they were alive. We decided that the setting for the game was going to be a walled city somewhere in the wastelands/DMZ between the surviving human nation and the new nation built by the "smart" zombies. Kinda like cold war Berlin, except we have the smart zombies co-existing alongside the folks who haven't died yet, and a big wall keeping the mindless zombies out. There's this peace between the Living and the Dead because while the dead just have to outlast the living in order to take over the world, the living have nukes and have made it clear they'd rather blast the entire world than give in to a zombie planet.

    We chose Basingstoke as the city, because it's a pretty bleak and horrible place and also quite naff, which appeals to the English sense of humour. Plus, we were in Milton Keynes and that's the usual "crap town" people bring up in such circumstances, so denied that we went with Basingstoke.*

    One of the things we decide on was that a theme of excessive political correctness would work well, it's a mainstay of Brit humour. "The Z Word" is a very politically incorrect thing to say, much like "The N Word" in real life. The term "Living Impaired" seem suitably absurd.

    Character Name: Sgt Clint Oxley

    Concept: Veteran Zombie Cop. Clint died early in the Zombie uprising and ate enough brains to be pretty much the man he was before the whole thing started. Except while he was dead, his wife re-married and he still has to put in another 7 years before his pension is ready for him to retire.

    Clint's partnered up with Kevin, a rookie cop who's also alive. We've been partnered together as part of an initiative to get the living and the Living Impaired working together publicly. Clint's quite bitter about being dead, and resents the fact that Kevin's not.

    He's about two thirds Gene Hunt from Life On Mars and one third Judge Dredd. (or one third Vic Mackie, one third Dirty Harry and one one third The Terminator if you prefer)

    Stuff he's good at: Being a badass, catching bad guys and getting things done.

    Stuff he's bad at: Being sympathetic towards the living, being nice to anyone, cutting Kevin some slack.

    What you like about this character in general: The chemistry between him and Kevin worked really well. Clint thinks Kevin's an idiot because he's got no real idea what the street are like. Kevin thinks Clint's not doing things right, because he's not doing things the way they teach you in the academy.

    What you like most about playing this character: Calling Kevin an idiot and getting Fanmail for it.

    Anything else: Oh man, PtA is fucking awesome. We went from nothing to a really cool series premise in about 30 minutes, and then played out an amazing and very complete feeling episode in two hours. That truly rocks.


    * If you ask most Brits to name a truly crap town in southern England and they'll probably say either Milton Keynes or Basingstoke.
