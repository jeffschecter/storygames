    When you are controlling Odysseus, you can use any of his epithets with varying numbers of advantages, or substitute a mariner in place of Odysseus and use the mariner's epithet/ advantages. As we played it, these various epithets/traits are written on each players' character sheets. If the game shifts so one player controls Odysseus for an entire island, then that player can hold onto all of the sheets and search time might be reduced.
    Are bonus dice awarded always +2? I''m not implying you change that, just curious. The hexameter mechanic is simple and lovely.
    You always get +1 for hexameter, even if the epithet you included is not suited for the action you are narrating. You get an additional +1 if one of the advantages associated with the epithet in your hexameter phrase is relevant to your action. Incidentally, you can choose to narrate about your opponent rather than about Odysseus, and draw upon the opponent's disadvantages, which takes one dice away from the GM rather than giving an extra dice to the player. Credit to Ice Cream Emperor for urging me to use grammar more in this game- the initial draft lacked it. The 6 space/metron simplification of hexameter seemed the easiest way to reference it into play.
