    \n I did a lot of this for The Apocalypse Machine. The recommendations of Life After People and The World Without Us are spot on.

    Rubber tyres deflate over many years. Windows hold their shape for a long while, although the glass will go once the seals decay. Cans last a long, long time: perhaps a decade, say.

    The thing to watch out for is plants. They grow everywhere, in little cracks, pushing masonry apart. Within years, you have trees sprouting through the highway and buildings falling apart. After a decade, everything is crumbling. After a century, only stonework is still standing.
