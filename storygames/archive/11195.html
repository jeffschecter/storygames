    \n I'll ask my biggest quiestion here, then, if that's OK. My one deep concern about running SA for my game group is that while all of them would certainly describe themselves as Sci Fi fans, most don't have the background that I think the game assumes, i.e. a pretty deep reading history in contemporary SF authors such as Stephenson, Banks, Simmons, Vinge etc. So my worry is that every new piece of tech that gets introduced into the storyline will have to be explained painstakingly to those whose SF experience doesn't extend much past STAR TREK and maybe a few Asimov or Heinlein novels.

    Have you heard from any playtesters who've expereinced this sort of problem? If so, how did they cope?

    Congrats, by the way, on having produced such a gorgeous and fascinating game.

    \n I've definitely had playtesters who, while geeks, weren't steeped in the more recent material that the game is based on. It turned out just fine. There are a few things you have to really nail (like Meshes, which are really key to several whole civilizations), but the rest of it can be introduced with a basic version. Giving people occasional reminders like, "Hey, you've got Biotech 8, you can sneeze out a cure for that virus if you need one" will help too.

    While I'm a hard scientist, there's no reason why folks who play the game should have to be. Just do your best.

    Thanks!
