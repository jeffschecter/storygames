    \n I think the best thing to do would be to ask the player: "Do you actually feed on blood? Do you have to? How often? What happens if you don't?" etc. I mean, it's their vampire.

    Also, from the Vampire skin playbook:\n
    Vampire moves
    Choose two:
    [...]
    The Feeding
    You feed on hot blood, direct
    from the source. [...]
    \nThe player might or might not choose this move. If they don't, perhaps it means this particular vampire doesn't feed that way (or no vampire in the setting does... again, I'd ask them!) or perhaps it means they do, but that isn't going to matter to the plot - not until they choose to get the move as an advancement, anyway.

    \n There seems to be a current fashion for vampires to feed through alternatives to biting people, even Pterry Pratchett had reformed vampires in Discworld! I can only assume it is to make vampires more cuddly and human. It is harder to portray a sexy teenage vampire character who is really a predator viewing humans as nothing more than walking snacks (that's surely the ghoul's role...).

    From what I read in the rulebook, vampires in Monsterhearts don't actually need to feed on blood at all. Unless they take 'The Feeding' then there is no explicit requirement for a PC vampire to drink the red stuff. Monsterhearts vampires feed on power and manipulation. Your own version may vary, of course.

    My own default for RPG vampires is the Nosferatu version, monsters rather than suave or sexy. This might be an unconscious reaction to modern literary depictions versus the original tales, but it might also be because I love the old 1922 Nosferatu film. Of course, the Monsterhearts Vampire skin is primarily based on the modern literature and being 'beauty eternal' really doesn't fit the Nosferatu image (unless you have very unconventional tastes).

    \n I love Nosferatu. What a fantastic piece of work.

    As for Monsterhearts vampires, the answer is that it has been left deliberately undefined, so the player can bring in their own vision of the vampire. Manipulation of others is definitely the core of the playbook here, but a need to feed and a monstrous disposition are definitely possible - it's up to the player. If you're MCing, do ask them about it, and use their answers. Maybe the want to be a monstrous vampire... and maybe they want to be the 21st-century sparkly kind (oh boy).

    \n The best way to figure out how often the vampire feeds and what their needs are is: start by asking the player questions and building upon the answers you're given, and from there use your agenda & principles to push the game in interesting and troubling directions!

    In all honesty, some vampires will play best with an MC regularly reminding them about their need to feed on other people. Other vampires gleefully generate their own drama that has nothing to do with feeding requirements, and so an idle "oh, hey, do you even eat human food? do you disguise blood by sticking it inside soda cans, or what's up?" question tossed into the session is going to be all you need.

    \n Vampire diet should also fit the constraints of the game setting, which in Monsterhearts is usually complex, detailed and specific.
    Is there a supply of people who can be killed wihout consequences? What about animals?
    For the option of nonlethal feeding, what sort of freaks, if any, submit themselves to a vampire's appetites? Are there enough of them? How can they be found and managed?
    Access to an hospital blood bank or the like is trivially easy (and thus too boring to be roleplayed), an annoyance of daily life that can occasionally develop into an interesting situation or episode, a rather major "adventure" and transgression (like a robbery) or implausible and unlikely?

    On the other hand some solutions are going to require only a handful of special NPCs without much context. An excellent example: in later novels Anne Rice's recurring cool vampire Lestat progresses from killing bad people to feeding on the period of his nun friend.
