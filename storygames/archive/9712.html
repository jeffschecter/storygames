    \n I wanted to bump this thread up to turn it into a thread about gaming related aborted projects. Almost 4 years ago, I was going to be a podcaster. Then I made an episode 0 and recorded episode 1. In the process I realized that I hated the sound of my voice in a recording (apparently it's fairly common) and while I'm sure it's perfectly fine really, I wasn't enjoying the editing process as a result and ended up not finishing the first full length episode and cancelled the project.

    So what's your project from a few years ago that didn't make it?

    As for hacking games, basically what happened with me was that I hacked the crap out of D&D4E and played that until we got sick of it, with every third session being an In A Wicked Age session as a break. Then we played a bunch of Trail of Cthulhu, which we hacked down to a more manageable number of skills* and then we eventually transitioned to Cthulhu Dark. Next week I have a Solar System post apocalyptic game starting and building the crunch for that reminds me of my Fudge days.

    *Very simple process: Each player picks 3-5 investigation skills that they are actually interested in and the Keeper designs the scenarios to have clues based on the group's skill sets rather than to have every skill covered across the group. We also renamed skills as needed to broaden them. And we did it by going around in a circle and saying "I want a game where I solve mysteries by finding clues about ancient history!" or "I want a game where I solve mysteries by finding clues through forensics!"
