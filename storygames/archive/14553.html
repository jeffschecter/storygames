A purchaser of my gameI had been meaning to get into pen-and-paper role-playing games for quite a while and your game concept stood out to me, and I'm looking forward to running a game with my friends.

Given that this is my first pen-and-paper rpg, do you have any tips and advice for me? I hardly know what to expect and would like games to run as smoothly as possible as soon as possible.
\nI just found out that Misspent Youth is going to be someone's first RPG. This blows me away, and I want to do right by his question. What would you tell him?

    \n I haven't read the game, so I'm not sure what it needs to produce a good time, but... I would go over the practical things useful at the table. Knowing how to play/run the game and knowing how to host an enjoyable rpg experience can be two very different things. Simple things like a table and comfortable chairs can sometimes go overlooked.
