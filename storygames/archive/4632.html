\n I have knocked up a prototype of the Uml\xe4ut ashcan. Dragonmeet is a month away, so I have a short while to iron out the kinks. I intend to sell these cheaply (6 quid) at the con (and any other cons I make it to for a while) on the understanding that anyone who gives me useful feedback gets that money off the finished product. There may be some deal with the PDF of the finished game, not sure if there's going to be one yet.
\n
\nSome things work, some don't, but in general the thing looks OK and isn't a total nightmare to produce.
\n
\n
\nIt's a comb-bound book plus 4 sheets of A5 card which can be cut out to make counters for Glory, Cash and Flaming Skulls, plus another 4 sheets which are cut out to each make a set of Style cards. I'll make that lot downloadable to anyone who buys the thing. The sheets of card will be supplied in a sturdy envelope. Which might have a printed sticker on the front, if I get around to doing that.
\n
\nThe inside layout is pretty rough at the moment. But you get the idea. I'll have a few pieces of proper artwork soon; they're done but not scanned. They'll probably go into the finished ashcan.
\n
\nThe cover of this one is printed on regular paper and then covered with a plastic front sheet, much the same as the ones I had earlier in the year. I'm not sure about that, it looks OK, but I'm also thinking of maybe using some black leather effect card I have and stencilling onto that with silver paint.
\n
\nI really wanted the writing on the tape to look handwritten, but my handwriting is utterly illegible. So I used the standard font and then distressed it with some filter or other to make it look badly inked. It looks OK to me. I'll try the stencil thing after I get back from Gaelcon and see which I like more.

    \nActually, the phrase "Are you ready to RAWK!?!?!" is probably more appropriate. There's a rule for spending a Flaming Skull to invoke Air Guitar and steal narration rights when you might otherwise not have them. Actually playing Air Guitar when you do this is encouraged.
    \n
    \nOriginally the game was set in a kind alternative 80s in a specific (invented) city in the US. During playtesting though, it seemed to be more fun to decide the exact place and time at the start of the game. One game started with one band talking smack about another on their MySpace page, but another was set firmly in the 80s. Another band started their career with a gig in the car park behind the local Aldi.

    \n Darcy,

    Damn, I thought I had! I distinctly remember typing an answer to Dan's question, but I can see that I clearly failed to post it...

    This goes for anyone who wants a copy:

    This is very much the "rough and ready" ashcan version of the game. Like a 4-track demo tape to continue the music analogy. This can definitely be used run the game and whenever I've done so we've had a blast playing it. But really, I'm releasing this version so that I can get the required feedback from a larger group of people to help push the game towards a much more polished release next year.

    Assuming I have copies left over after Dragonmeet, I'll make them available by post. If I don't then what will probably happen is another print run sometime later in December, although that run might use slightly different materials depending on how I get on with the ones I make for DM. That means anyone, anywhere can get one, I'll be charging postage at cost.

    But please understand: If you just want to play the game (and I love you for it if you do) you're much better off waiting for the finished release next year. I'm not going to be upset with anyone who buys the Bootleg and doesn't feed back to me, because life is life and often the best intentions are foiled by circumstances beyond our control.
