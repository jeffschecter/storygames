    \n (My latest finished game)
    Archipelago II - ritual phrases.
    Don't Rest Your Head - brilliant emergent game system.
    Feng Shui - thematic design.
    InSpectres - collaborative storytelling through structures.
    Kagematsu - brilliant structure of play for one type of gameplay.

    Unknown Armies - the best BRP system, brilliant setting-to-adventure structure.

    \n Witch Quest - Trad game about witches and their cats. Brilliant game for large groups of players. Featured on The Gauntlet podcast recently (ep 70).

    While the World Ends - Competitive sci-fi story-game. A nice little engine that reliability churns out interesting stories. Very handsome designer.

    Zombie Cinema - Great intro game to story gaming. Both for trad gamers and non gamers. High replay ability, trivial to hack into a disaster movie fame the day you tire of zombies.

    The Daughters of Verona - Comedy/romance RPG that doesn't take any chances - it starts out by teaching the players how to be funny. Same handsome designer as WtWE.

    A Taste for Murder - 1920s murder mystery on a British country estate. It wears thin after about three games, but worth it for those three sessions.

    Montsegur 1244 - Culture gaming an oppressed religious minority. A lot more interesting than it sounds.

    Lady Blackbird - One of the games that I can run with the lowest investment as a GM. The players manage most of the rules, and develop the story by themselves. After the first two or three scenes GM:ing is mostly a matter of setting scenes, nodding and determining the difficulty of things when they want to roll dice. Good for conventions when you are obliged to run something even if you're tired.

    \n In no particular order, warts and all:

    Call of Cthulhu (pre-7th, as I've not read 7th yet. I imprinted on 4th, if that makes a difference.) I'm lumping Cthulhu Britannica, the Laundry, and World War Cthulhu all together here.
    Over the Edge
    Fate Core / Accelerated / et cetera / und so weiter and all flavors
    Trail of Cthulhu / Night's Black Agents (which blur because of how I'm running Dracula Dossier)
    Victoriana
    NightLife, whose background I used for years and which got me unexpected geek points with Rob Donoghue
    Various PbtA games, particularly Monsterhearts and Urban Shadows

    All of the above I have played at least once, and I've run the first four.

    Clockwork: Dominion is a game I've gone out of my way to play two years running and am hoping to run at AnonyCon, but have not yet read the full rules
    Changeling: The Lost which I love and have read -- but have never played or run

    That leaves me with one slot, and Unknown Armies and Kult need to duke it out. Unknown Armies 1st and 2nd are more solid games for me than Kult's first three editions (in English -- I can't voice an opinion on non-English editions). I've played UA at conventions many times, and Kult at a convention once. That said, Kult made my jaw drop with various pieces of awesome I'd not seen before, and I've not yet read the current / forthcoming editions of either.

      Fate Accelerated

      I like it better than its more sophisticated cousin. Simple, direct, gets the job done, and a great Star Wars engine.

      Night Witches
      Fellowship
      Traveller       for nostalgia
