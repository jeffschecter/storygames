    \n More words. Ok. How about, off the top of the deck: Surprise, Reconciliation, Brown haired woman, Greed, Stop! Look! Listen!, Scandal.
    \n
    \nWe play mostly using Mythic. So GMless, dividing the action up into scenes where stuff happens. Once that event is complete we describe a new scene, or location and then carry on.
    \n
    \nThese cards can be used to throw a left-field alteration to a scene: once the scene is described, pull a card and try to add in the extra idea.
    \n
    \nAnother way is to draw a card and use it for a random event when the action gets stalled. Since the cards are single ideas you still have to make up a story about them, but they are sufficiently abstract that you can keep on track but still insert something new.
    \n
    \nFor example, let's say we have described an airfield that we wish to infiltrate ( just making this up as I go along here). We've talked about the guards and the distance to the perimeter, and where we are hiding and so on. But just before we put the wire cutters to the fence I draw a card.
    \n
    \nIt says, 'Surprise'. There are lots of potential surprises possible here. And we would talk them through to find the one most interesting to keep the story action going.
    \n
    \nBut how different would it be if I pulled, 'Justice', or 'Wealth'?
    \n
    \nAny one of these takes the story in a different direction, and stops the game just being a predictable monologue.
    \n
    \nDoes that make sense?
