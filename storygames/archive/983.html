It may be the end, but its not over yet...

You are old. You can call it what you will, spent, wasted, all-used-up, or whatever, but in the end its means the same thing. You are just a few steps out of the casket. Its time to put your affairs in order, settle things with this world of pain and joy. Things are never really so easy though, and you soon find yourself caught up in one last grand adventure. There is time yet, though.

You're no saint. In your life you were never the likeness of Mother Theresa. You did bad things, a ton of which you aren't so proud of now. You sinned, smiled and laughed at God, but now you wonder. The cost of this life is fast approaching and you still have a little time to try settle the score. You can't be sure what lies past the end, but it sure feels like judgment. You still have a little time, but none to waste.

You stand yet. When you came to this seedy little retirement home, you felt discarded. You were thrown away, nothing more than a burden on those you love the most. Then, all at once, it became perfectly clear. Something evil is crawling out of the darkness, and it wants to head right through your new home. You can't tell what it is, or how is came to be, but one thing is for sure. Its down right unamerican and has a taste for blood. You're time yet has purpose.

If you could only remember your name, then you'd know that what you are experiencing is real and not some kooked up dream. But when you try and remember, it just all blurs. The orderlies say you are crazy and you stopped listening to your doctor ages ago. The only way you can handle it at all is too know you have friends in here. Friends who say they see the same things. You treasure your time together, confide in each other, and plan on how you'll stop the darkness.

***

Whats my Name? is a roleplaying game set in the somewhat rundown imaginary Twin Pines retirement home, which lies in the outskirts of the imaginary town of Vague, South Dakota. You play one of the inmates, err retirees, who is both senile and able to see a evil so foul that it could ravage the world. This darkness is just falling though, and perhaps you can yet stop it before it makes its way into the world.
The play of the game should be equal parts comedy, sadness, and awe. The story unfolds through scenes, and during each one player takes on the role of the narrator. There is no central figure to manage the story, and instead that responsibility lies in the shared hands of the players. Without further blather, let me get right into character creation.

That is as far as I've got so far...
