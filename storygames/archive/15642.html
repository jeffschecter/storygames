\n Terry ran Kagematsu again and after my X-Men themed Psi*Run game we joked about an X-Men Kagematsu game.

Almost all our female friends, and many of our male friends have the hots for Gambit. What if...

Your Mutant colony is in-danger from an encroaching seemingly unstoppable threat... and you've all lost your powers!

Except for Gambit.

Gambit doesn't know any of you and has no intentions of staying but he's the only one who can fight what's coming or help your regain your powers.

A woman always plays Gambit. Since this is a modern game, it might be fun if the players played both female and male characters trying to win Gambit's heart. Gambit's appeal defies gender and women don't need men to fight for them, so why not say everyone needs his help now that they all have no power.

This could all just be crazy talk as my brain is a blur from organizing 3 days of parties and games for 200 people.

    \n Gambit always felt like a pawn for the audience to me. Not a real character, just some suave dude for the ladies to dig and the guys to imagine themselves as. Boring energy power, generic super-athleticism, and a few words in French whenever that'd look good. He's everyone's first Vampire character.

    Maybe they developed him later, but I've been out of the loop since Onslaught (when the most interesting thing about him -- the fact that he might be a traitor -- was nixed).

    I think the idea of winning the allegiance of an egomaniacal supervillain sounds much more fun. Screw a wannabe champion like Magneto, I'm talking straight up Doctor Doom here. "It's ALL pity, fools!" Earning love from Doom is a friggin' accomplishment.

    Thinking X-specific, perhaps Selene, Omega Red or a Brood queen? Actually, Eero's suggestion of Shaw might be best.

    \n Hey, David's riffing there, we clearly have similar Xperiences! I must have read many hundreds of pages of stuff about Gambit, but as is often the case with the '90s superheroes, I don't have a fucking clue as to what his deal was supposed to be (aside from the trenchcoat, that is). Selene would be cool (it'd be reverse Kagematsu, though - very '80s femlib in feel, I imagine), the Brood's a bit too out there for me, and it should be Mojo anyway, he's more masculine. Shaw is still the best: he's human despite being an arrogant jerk, filthy rich and pampered piss-head (or perhaps because of it). Also, the Hellfire Club have the most elegant milieu and accountrements, which is important for Kagematsu.

    Doctor Doom does offer stiff competition, though. Latveria is an idyllic milieu, too. "Your small eastern European country is in peril, and only the good Doctor can save you all. However, he has been spacelost and only recently returned; will he deign to uplift your undeserving country of fools once more? Is it a Doom-bot instead of the real item? What might convince this man, a man who's been to Hell and back for love?"
