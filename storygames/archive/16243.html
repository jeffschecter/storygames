\n
\n
\n
\n\nRoss_Cowman \n\n\n
\n
\n\nApril 2012 \n edited April 2012 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n It happens sometimes. Story gaming is powerful stuff and when something comes up at the table that makes you uncomfortable, you can ask for it to be 'Veiled' and we'll skip it, no questions asked, and furthermore if someone asks to Veil something you've contributed, we all understand that dosen't make you an asshole.

But what if the thing making you uncomfortable is the person themself?

Well thats why, at story games olympia, we've developed a technique called the Personal Veil.
It works like this:
If someone at the table is making you uncomfortable. Just ask for them to be veiled and we'll write them out of the game, no questions asked. Perhaps using a paper bag or other device as a marker to show that they have been "Veiled."

Remember, if someone asks to Veil you, that dosen't make you an asshole.
