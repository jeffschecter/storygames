Loglines for Character Generation

Caesar_X
January 2007 edited January 2007 in Story Games
I was doing a little web research on scriptwriting and found this site about loglines. They are used to pitch screenplays in Hollywood, but looking at their examples I can see the parallels for character generation in story games. You could quickly get some color and potential bangs out of a character using this technique.

A logline must present:

who the story is about (protagonist)
what he strives for (goal)
what stands in his way (antagonistic force).

Ex:
A psychologist struggles to cure a troubled boy who is haunted by a bizarre affliction â€“ he sees dead people.
After learning his father was murdered, a brooding prince struggles with whether or not to kill the culprit, his uncle - the new king.
A despondent cop struggles on the lam to prove his innocence for a murder he has not yet committed.

They need to avoid using proper names from the script that no one would recognize anyway, so they use a "well-chosen adjective" to bring greater clarity. I like how it quickly tells you something about the protagonist.

I may need to borrow this for The Naked City.
