So part of what makes this community what it is, is sharing the things that we have in common that aren't the thing that we obviously have in common. Hence threads about books, movies, podcasts, blogs, fruit, and chocolate.

It's the turn of webcomics.

I love to start my day with a webcomic hit - opening them all in tabs in Firefox and watching them load and then getting my morning humour dose. I tend to favour funny, quirky-but-harmless, story-leaning comics. Here's my current list:

El Goonish Shive. Daft name, great webcomic. Current story arc starts here, but if you like it you'll want to read the whole thing to get the background. It's been going 5 years but hasn't always updated regularly in that time. I was surprised to find out how young the cartoonist (Dan Shive) is, given how deftly he handles the characters' personal and interpersonal issues while still being funny and telling a suspenseful story. He started it in his late teens.

Get Fuzzy. My definition of "webcomic" is "comic available on the web", so this qualifies. Darby Conley has managed to convey exactly how a talking Siamese cat would behave. And the little dog is priceless. I consistently get laughs from this one.

Irregular Webcomic. This one has a gaming tie-in; the cartoonist is a big GURPS-head and has written for SJG, including the only GURPS supplement to be based on a webcomic (not his own one). Several of the interweaving storylines are based on gaming campaigns. He's very clever and loves bad puns (finds them hobbit-forming). His experimental webcomic consisting of four black panels totally makes sense in the context of its storyline (a hobbit is sitting in the dark and not talking).

He's bringing the comic to a close sometime soon and launching a new collaborative thing which some of you will probably be interested in.

Non Sequitur. Like practically everyone commenting on politics in America, Wiley Miller is one-eyed (the left one, in this instance), but he's funny and doesn't do politics all the time, so I don't mind too much.

XKCD. This one is really strange, and all done with stick figures. It's geek humour, mostly. You'll love it or it will leave you cold.

Gene Catlow. Furry supers. I'm not sure why I still read this one, but I'm kind of caught up in the story.

No Pink Ponies. More geek humour, this time based around a comic shop. Promising, well drawn, may eventually deliver something really good.

The Dreamland Chronicles. This is an incredibly beautiful, computer-generated 3D comic with a promising storyline. Guy did it as a love project, didn't manage to sell many of the print copies, so started putting it on the web, a page a day. He now has a million readers.

Unshelved, a comic strip set in a library. Some of the librarians and librarians' spouses here may get a chuckle out of it.

DM of the Rings, which someone here (Rob?) put me on to. Everything dysfunctional about traditional D&D play, presented for your viewing pleasure using screencaps from the LOTR movies. It's really well done.

Wicked Powered, a fairly new one which is sponsored by a company that makes lasers, and is about - lasers, and the people from the future who wield them. So far it's quite chuckleworthy.

So, what are your favourites? Include links, brief summaries, and why you like them.

  • Chris_Goodwin
    February 2007
    Absurd Notions, about the obligatory (former) college roommates out in the real world. Author suffers from chronic writer's block, but it's still a fun comic. Gaming related, too; the group does playtesting for a (fictional) RPG, and they do try various systems. I keep wondering what would happen if the author frequented the Forge or here....

    Dominic Deegan, Oracle For Hire. Very enjoyable fantasy comic.

    Schlock Mercenary, IMO the hard SF humor comic.

    Freefall, another good hard SF comic. Very slow-moving (two days of in-comic time took about a year and a half to play out), but still fun.

    The Wotch, a sort of Buffy-ish comic about a teenage witch and her two best friends, with more gender swapping than your favorite anime. A recent discovery of mine, but (my main criterion) I made it all the way through the archives and wanted more, so it stays. Currently on hiatus due to some personal issues in the life of one of the co-authors (bummer...)
