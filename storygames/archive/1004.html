For those of you have forgotten about this project in the almost 2 years it's taken to organize and get into print...

Push: New Thinking About Roleplaying

An annual journal of progressive roleplaying thought and short-form games to blow your mind, modelled after predecessors such as Kazu Kabuishi's comics journal Flight, Dave Egger's literary journal McSweeney's, the Nordic convention book put out every year, and our own Matt Snyder's webzine Daedalus!

In addition to a fabulous cover by Clio Chiang, Push Volume One contains the following:

Emily Care Boss, in Collaborative Roleplaying: Reframing the Game, provides an overview of games which seek to distribute control of the play experience more evenly among the players involved and speculates on the future of this type of play.

  • J_Walton
    July 2006
    Thanks for the feedback. I totally agree with all your comments, though I guess not having a unified feel wasn't super-important to me (though I was hoping the marginalia voices would help collect the other articles together).

    It's definitely true that the various contributors have very different writing styles. This was something I really wrestled over when I was doing editing, wondering how much to edit things and how much to preserve the authors' original voices. In the end, I decided to err on the side of preservation, because I think a diversity of different voices is something roleplaying could use right now, instead of just the kind of bog-standard prose that shows up in most roleplaying texts.

    I can see how it might make for an interesting reading experience, but it is really much more like reading a series of forum threads than like reading most magazines or academic journals. Actually, come to think of it, it does remind me a lot of reading an issue of McSweeney's (one of the publications that inspired it) which also features a rather odd assortment of different writing and content styles.

    Anyway, glad you dig it.
