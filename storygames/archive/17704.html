\n I like upward mobility in games, so I like the idea of super-high level D&D... or in this case, Dungeon World. I have been looking at the possibility of player characters becoming gods in some manner, and put this together today:
\n

Young God

\nWhen you transcend to god-hood, the next time you level up, you may choose this move:

Divine Worshippers
You have at least one temple devoted to your honor. When your worshippers hold a monthly festival in your honor, roll +divinity. On a 10+, hold 3 ambrosia. On a 7-9, hold 2 ambrosia. On 6-, hold 1 ambrosia and your worshippers are disgruntled and must be convinced their effort is worthwhile before they will hold the service again.
At any time, you can spend 10 ambrosia plus your divinity to increase your divinity by one.

If you have Divine Worshippers, these count as class moves for you, and you can choose them when you level up:

Keep the Sabbath Holy
Replaces Divine Worshippers
When your worshippers hold a weekly festival in your honor, roll +divinity. On a 10+, hold 3 ambrosia. On a 7-9, hold 2 ambrosia. On 6-, hold 1 ambrosia and your worshippers are disgruntled and must be convinced their effort is worthwhile before they will hold the service again.
At any time, you can spend 10 ambrosia plus your divinity to increase your divinity by one.

Blood for the Blood God
When your worshippers sacrifice a virgin on the full moon, roll +divinity. On a 10+, hold 3 ambrosia. On a 7-9, hold 2 ambrosia. On 6-, hold 1 ambrosia and your worshippers are disgruntled and must be convinced their effort is worthwhile before they will perform a similar sacrifice again.

Smite
Spend 1 held ambrosia to roll an additional class damage die and keep the highest of the two.

Divine Perfection
Spend 1 held ambrosia to roll +1d6 on a basic move and keep the highest two dice.

Sacrosanct
Spend 1 held ambrosia to gain +3 armor for one attack.

Blessing
You can give ambrosia as leverage in Parley. When you do, choose Smite, Divine Perfection, or Sacrosanct that they can spend it on.

Popular God
You have loads of people interested in your worship. Whenever a move gives you ambrosia, hold +1 ambrosia

\n

Major Elemental

\nWhen you establish your dominion over some aspect of reality, the next time you level up, you can choose this move:

Elemental Patron
Choose an element such as Fire, Earth, Water, Air, Death, Life, Light, or Shadow. An element like this can include abstract things such as ideas or acts. This element recognizes your authority, and you can communicate with it and use Parley.

If you have Elemental Patron, these count as class moves for you, and you can choose them when you level up:

Elemental Energy
When you are immersed in your element, roll +divinity. On a 10+, hold 1 ambrosia. On 7-9, hold 1 ambrosia, but choose 1:
Take 1d8 damage which ignores armor
The element does not recognize your authority. Take -1 ongoing to Parley with the element until you perform a favor for the element.
On 6-, hold no ambrosia and both occur.

Elemental Force
When you command your element, it must follow your order. Spend 1 ambrosia and roll +divinity. On 10+, you suffer no penalty. On 7-9, choose one:
You upset the element -- take -1 ongoing to Elemental Force rolls until you perform a favor for the element.
You draw the negative attention of other Patrons of the element. The GM tells you how.
On 6-, both occur.




I'm a little concerned that "Keep the Sabbath Holy" is too obvious and powerful (i.e. a non-choice), but I want moves that allow a player to customize where they get their divine strength from. I'd appreciate feedback on any part of this, if anyone has some.

    \n When your worshipers do something for you, you roll. And there's no need for you to do anything for your worshipers. Doesn't feel right. Nor does the "time passes" aspect of the monthly services - I personally always resist abstract "game time" in tabletop games, as it's rarely handled well anyway and just encourages meaningless time skips to game the system. (In larp, on the other hand, time as a mechanic begins to have real meaning, and can be used well.)

    Instead, I'd structure it so that when you do something for your worshipers, you roll to see how much you impress and inspire them, which determines how much ambrosia you get from it. (Sure, maybe they're holding a prayer meeting or sacrifice or other ritual in response, but it was your action that triggered it.)

    And perhaps a higher risk / higher payoff version where you put the literal fear of god into your worshipers.

    If you want to have gain triggered by time, you could just say that you gain your divinity level in ambrosia every week or month or whatever, as a result of ongoing worship. Just make that part automatic.
