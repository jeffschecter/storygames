\n When you're playing In a Wicked Age..., do you ever negotiate concessions before a roll, as opposed to consequences after the roll?

Put in other words: negotiate about stuff in the middle of the conflict, before it's over, then keep rolling.

I'm not sure whether this messes with the rules or not. Here's an example, to illustrate:

Consequences: After

I'm sneaking into your house to steal the Mona Lisa. We roll off! You win, eventually, and we agree that I did not steal the Mona Lisa, but, instead of being exhausted, the fallout is that while I was sneaking around, one of your security cameras got a clean shot of my face. You know who the thief was, now!

Concessions: During

I'm sneaking into your house to steal the Mona Lisa. We roll off! You win the first roll. I say, "how about a concession? Instead of you going into the next round with an advantage die, we say that one of your security cameras got a clean shot of my face. You know who the thief was, now!" Then we go to the second round, and eventually find out who gets their way, but in any case, we've established that I was captured by the camera.

I think that, in most cases, the Answerer's narration can cover this sort of stuff, but in some cases it could lead to some more interesting stuff getting established during the conflict itself that would otherwise be left to the negotiations afterwards. For instance, I could steal the Mona Lisa despite having been captured on camera, whereas normally the winner in IaWA gets to win more conclusively. I could see that being good in some ways and bad in other ways.

So, good idea or bad idea? Do you do this already? If so, how does it usually go down--great or not-so-great?
