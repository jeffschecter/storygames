\n When I will take some holidays and I will go to visit my parents, I would like to get them to play some game with me for the first time.
They are quite young as parents come, both around 55 years old. Younger than some of my friends.

I thought about a number of games but I had a hard time finding something appropriate to play.
Fantasy and sci-fi would not be appreciated.
Anything romantic or overly dramatic would make me uncomfortable.
Fiasco is a close choice but I find the outcomes too cruel to play it with my parents.

From one recent thread I discovered Last Train Out Of Warsaw, and this is the first game I have seen that I would feel comfortable playing with them, and I think they would appreciate it too.

I think that something with an historical setting and a degree of action and character relationship emphasized over the mechanics could be a good fit, but I am just guessing.

Can you guys give me more ideas? Consider that it would be their first gaming experience!

--i.

    \n Well, my mom doesn't have any game experience, and it was only for a bit of time in my dad's college days, but it's been cool. The best part is that they're really supportive of my gaming because of it. My dad ran my first adventure for me and a couple friends years ago, and played a support character in a game I ran for some family friends' kids. But yeah, it's been very neat hearing the occasional story.
    \n
    \nAnd yeah, I didn't know about A Taste For Murder, but a murder mystery is pretty much perfect. It doesn't stray into unfamiliar territory(especially with so many crime shows on TV) and ordinary people, non-roleplayers and such, still do those murder-mystery parties, so it's obviously accessible. If you can grab the extra players for it, I'd do that(I'll add that Dogs might need one more player as well, I don't know how it works with 2. Vincent mentions in the book that it should work, but I'm not positive myself.

    \nAnd yeah, it's pretty much up to you I think to keep out of bad territory. I'd tell them something like "Let me know if I say something or move into an area you find uncomfortable." but other than that, the lines and veils should be on you.
