    jhkim, the website is still in development. Sorry about that.
    Paypal ordering will be available within the week. I guarantee it.

    full pitch.
    The country of Cadence was once a Victorian society - stiff etiquette, grandeous excess, etc.
    Everything was going okay. Then in the short span of a single ruler, everything was uprooted and re-structured.
    The entire society changed completely.
    In some eyes, the change was the greatest thing to ever happen to Cadence. Now everything was automatic - society ran like clockwork. Everything was easy.

    To some, it was a nightmare. Yes, everything did run like clockwork - but that's because they stripped humanity and meaning from society.

    You play a criminal in this clockwork nightmare - a kink in the system. Something that needs to be reprogrammed and fixed.

    You commit crime for a multitude of reasons - love, hate, curiousity, bitterness, confusion. You commit crimes for a REASON, and the game explores WHAT that reason is and WHY you care about it.

    The system is focused around bringing that "why" into play.
    It's a simple, easily run system.

    And you will soon be able to buy Perfect from my site... as well as IPR and Leisure Games.

    The system is poker based and the ability of the GM and players to manipulate the narrative is regulated by the use of poker chips.

    I had fun with the demo at Gencon and, must say, that the idea of staring at your fellow players over a hand of cards, wondering who is bluffing, who is not, just rocks. Think of that classic movie scene where the rivals encounter each other and start trash talking--soon the tension rises and everyone wonders, is this the time to throw down? Or is one of these guys gonna back down?

    Since you don't know what the other guy has in his hand, there is a real tension right there, at the table, between the players. If the players are paired off at all against each other, what happens when one guy with a good hand goes for it, not realizing his buddy has a crap hand? Story goodness, that's what.

    Do you like time travel? Not headache-inducing, don't-step-on-the-bug-or-we-all-might-die time travel, but fun, era-jumping, time-warping time travel? Then Timestream is for you. It's a game of cinematic time travel, in the sense that your group tailors the requisite levels of paradox to the kind of story that you enjoy and that you want to tell. There are three broad categories of character - Time Travelers, Temporal Manipulators, and Thralls - and your characters are linked across time by relationships to the people that matter to them, not any kind of overarching time bureocracy or embedded metaplot. Basically, the game is designed to support what YOU think is cool, not what I think is cool.
