\n Lest Montsegur 1244 get all the emo love from Dreamation, here\u2019s another game that was tragic and awesome.

I (Rachel \u201cNina\u201d Walton) played a one-shot of Grey Ranks with Jason \u201cDaisy\u201d Morningstar, Ryan \u201cSlaz\u201d Macklin, Arturo \u201cMucha\u201d Gonzalez Escribano, and David \u201cSheepdog\u201d Berg. We played out chapters 4-6. If you\u2019re unfamiliar with Grey Ranks, the story weaves in and out of personal and mission scenes, which are under the place/time umbrella of Radio Lightning announcements that add big picture color to the individual stories of these child soldiers of The Uprising in Warsaw, 1944.

This is the story of a girl. There were other great stories at work in our game, but the other players can flesh those out if they feel so inclined \u2013 I know I won\u2019t be able to do them justice. My character \u201cNina\u201d was so influenced by the other players at the table, she felt like \u201cour character\u201d by the end.

Born Stephania Hoffman, the daughter of a working-class doctor in the Ochota district, \u201cNina\u2019s\u201d story begins when she is 17 \u2013 her last year in the Grey Ranks. She holds up the fa\xe7ade of maturity by her cool attitude and sometimes engaging in adult behaviors. Some of her peers find her boring, but she has nevertheless carved herself an important niche in the Grey Ranks with the medical knowledge she\u2019s gained from helping her father. The thing she holds most dear is her first love: Roman \u201cSlaz.\u201d He\u2019s also 17, and is a standoffish \u201cJames Dean\u201d bad boy, still holding hard to his faith. They\u2019ve not had much chance for a relationship, but she holds as a daily reminder and symbol a scrap of bandage from the time she dressed his wound some months back.

Part 1
When we start, August 2, 1944, there is desperate street fighting going on in Warsaw, but spirits are high. Our characters, however, are all in dangerous positions on the Exhaustion/Enthusiasm & Love/Hate axis. Nina begins in a field hospital. Slaz is there dealing with a ridiculous priest who is getting in the way of medical attention for the injured. Nina hopes to catch Slaz\u2019s eye and impress him by demonstrating her surgical finesse. But she\u2019s too distracted and ends up further injuring the poor man she\u2019s treating.

When she\u2019s found and given orders to take medical supplies to the front line, she ultimately accepts the suicide mission as a way to redeem herself. The ground is rough, however, and when shots come closer she trips spectacularly, sending the tools and bandages flying into the mud. Much of it is ruined. Her companions have little more success as they maneuver ammunition boxes to the front. Mucha, a sturdy kid out to prove himself, gets them past the German guardsman, but only with a bribe: a dented can of English marmalade \u2013 a special treat that Daisy and Nina were looking forward to, and their only food for the day.

By the end of the chapter, despite many personal failings, our characters have all moved away from the most dangerous locations on the axis grid \u2013 we might actually survive this!

Part 2
August 4, 1944 \u2013 the fighting intensifies and this little squad of Grey Ranks have been assigned to free one of the refugees being forced to march in front of the German army. He is an important physiology professor (and an acquaintance of Nina\u2019s father). Sheepdog, a country boy, is in his element \u2013 he knows these woods that the army is moving through and he directs his companions to successfully sneak the professor away. A handful of other refugees follow, however, putting the entire mission in jeopardy.

Once free, they need a safe place to keep the professor and company, so Nina convinces the madam of a whorehouse to allow the refugees a bit of rest there.

On more personal levels, Nina is able to speak with the professor \u2013 he remembers her \u2013 and draw out some helpful information on the particulars of the German army.

At some point, there\u2019s a flashback with Nina and Daisy. They are standing outside the lecture hall where Nina\u2019s father is speaking. Daisy, 15, is trying to get Nina\u2019s friendship and respect. Daisy asks about Slaz, but she comes off wrong and then refuses the liquor Nina offers her. In the end, Nina sees Daisy as just another kid (and possibly a threat!) and tries to find a distraction that isn\u2019t there to take her away.

Slaz meets Nina in the park where they first met. It\u2019s now rubble. He\u2019s having a crisis of faith. She\u2019s brought him the Bible he left at the field hospital. They dance around their attraction for each other. They risk so much every day, but this feels so much greater. It is a passionate kiss - that never happens.

    \nPart 3
    August 7, 1944. The Home Army has been surprisingly successful, but things are looking grim for our protagonists. The safe house was found and destroyed, including those hiding there, and all of our characters are close to the corners on the grid. In fact, the Grey Ranks as a whole are in jeopardy. One of the highest-ranking officers betrayed the group and the result has been a chaotic power struggle between the Grey Ranks associated with the Communist People\u2019s Army, and those with the Home Army.

    Daisy has stepped up in rank, taking on power she\u2019s too inexperienced to handle. Slaz has stepped up to responsibility as well \u2013 by taking up the slack from Anna Ostrowski, the Grey Ranks People\u2019s Army leader, who has become ineffectual in her morphine addiction. Sheepdog and Mucha are heavily involved in the powers struggle as well, and Sheepdog in particular is prepared to force peace by gunpoint, if necessary.

    In a quite moment, Nina sits on a camp bed, back to the wall, in something of an exhausted stupor. She keeps replaying all of the failures over the last few days, including the professor\u2019s death and her inability to have a real relationship with Slaz. She draws her scalpel across her arm, making thin red lines. The pain is a welcome distraction. Daisy plops down on the bed, yammering away about her own life; completely oblivious to what Nina was doing. No one notices, in fact.

    Later, Slaz and Nina meet up in the rubble park again. They\u2019re both in a pretty low place right now. He puts his arm around her and she just slumps into him. They carry the weight of each other\u2019s worlds for a few sweet moments. (This was my favorite scene!)

    Later still, Nina is taken aside with orders for a very special mission. She is to find and assassinate the traitor. She comes upon him, large scalpel in hand, when he\u2019s sleeping, but she\u2019s too clumsy for this job. He wakes, grabs her hand and starts verbally berating her.

    Then we have an interrupt flashback. Hours earlier, Sheepdog has gotten word of the intended assassination. He confronts Nina. She talks about duty, but he will have none of it. He knows and owns the Grey Ranks oath of fellowship better than anyone and assassination is NOT a part of it. But he becomes so sickened and outraged by her unwavering intention that he beats her.

    Back to the assassination: Nina is still smarting from the bruises and angry at the humiliation that she\u2019s already been through. When her target grabs her, she just snaps. She kicks him to free her arm and stabs him again and again with her scalpel well past his dying breath.

    In the final scene, all the protagonists but Nina are gathered around a table angrily arguing for their side\u2019s control. Sheepdog has placed a gun on the table. Nina walks in, still covered in blood, failing to hide the scared little girl inside. This only escalates the fighting. Slaz can\u2019t convince her to join the People\u2019s Army. Daisy caps Sheepdog, and the hope for a united Grey Ranks crumbles. Slaz and Nina shortly thereafter commit suicide. None of them live to see their beloved Warsaw fall. Geeze, I get a knot in my stomach just from thinking about this story again.

    So\u2026Total Party Kill! On a game level, we tried everything and destroyed all we held dear to keep a least one of us alive, but we all rolled so poorly it just didn\u2019t happen. (Dro informed me that this is a very true-to-Polish result!) Heartbreaking, but it was a really great game. I\u2019d love to play the full 10 chapters with even more layers and character development to explore.

    \n That was the first time, in my experience, that all the protagonists got taken out at the same time. I've never seen it happen. Part of it is the one-shot rules tweak that makes it more lethal, but still - a perfect storm. It's also worth noting that players author their own character's departure from the story, and there's no requirement that they be killed. I think we all felt "these people would be totally erased", though, and it was sort of perfect that they not make it. It was such a sad crew.

    Although I cannot conceive of the TPK thing happening in a full 10 chapter game, if it did, playing the last three chapters as memories of the dead among the living would utterly kick ass.

    \n There was an interesting thing that happened at the end with the mass death:

    Con game. Tragic setup. Flawed characters. There ain't gonna be a follow-up session. Single dramatic ending for everyone all at once, dying in a collapsing building? Gold!

    And yet, we still tried like hell to win, and given average luck on our die rolls, we would have healed the divided Grey Ranks and some of the characters would have made it out alive.

    I think this speaks to three things:

    1) The process of trying to win was itself fun. This was the biggest thing for me. "Come up with a way your character changes to earn a re-roll!" Shit, why would I not max those out?

    2) The situation as of the final scene was so fraught and full of interesting interpersonal dynamics, that pretty much any way it turned out would have been striking, possibly just as cool as the TPK. "Let's roll dice and see what happens!" was fun in that context.

    3) We got invested in our characters, and even though it was fun to see them crash and burn from a narrator's perspective, there was also some desire to see them achieve happiness or meaning or something positive.

    I know this may read like, "Well, duh, of course, Dave, that's what's supposed to happen." I just think this is a particularly interesting case of the intent coming to fruition.
