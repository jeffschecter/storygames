\n Hey, denizens of story-games.com
\n
\nSince the topic sort of came up in a past thread, I figured I'd take a couple of minutes to let you guys know about the GRAND ROLUDOTHON, Montreal's newest gaming convention. (For those of you familiar with RoyalCon / Draconis, Montreal's former con, we are officially picking up the mantle they left behind, with the blessing of its founder and co-chair, Christopher Hammock.)
\n
\nAnyhow. The event will take place on June 11 & 12, in Montreal, at the Hotel Espresso. You can find out all the details at GR.ROLUDO.CA - and follow us on Facebook and/or Twitter, if you're into that sort of thing. The con is not centered on indie gaming per se, but a good chunk of our crowd has shown itself open to all kinds of experimentation related to roleplaying games. And while story games are not in abundance on the schedule right now, I can tell you that more will come, for sure.
\n
\nIf you're interested in registering or even in hosting a game or two, you can do so at GR.ROLUDO.CA, again. Admission for the weekend is 20,00$ (canadian!), 15,00$ for saturday, 12,00$ for sunday. Once you've paid (say, via PayPal), you get to submit games of your own and/or save your seat at games that interest you.
\n
\nI'm pretty sure there's tons of details I'm forgetting, so feel free to ask questions over here, through the contact form on the website, or through our own forum (a link to which you can find on the convention website.)
\n
\nThanks for reading and have a great day!
\n
\nJocelyn Robitaille

    \n The overall attendance has fluctuated over the years, depending on the sectors invited. In our biggest year we had around 250 participants total, but there was a big Magic contingency and a very big (and fun) Grandeur Nature representation (this is the Quebec LARP scene and was a massive tournament in the ballroom including a troll whose long arms broke part of the chandelier; good times!). Things got a big too big for the volunteer organizing team, so last year we pulled back to fundamentals a bit, with the emphasis on roleplaying and board games. I can't remember the total numbers, maybe more like 100-150 total across the weekend.

    We are hoping to improve on that this year, but we never know until we get much closer to the con itself. We feel like we have a more focused publicity plan this year; but gamers in Montreal are notoriously hard to get out of their basement. We've done well in the past, but haven't been able to capitalize on previous years' successes to grow the con, usually due to a lack of continuity in the management team.

    We do tend to get a nice diverse mix of RPGs running the gamut. We don't have a tradition of pick-up RPGs. It's something we've kept open to, but just haven't had the demand. We always keep a table of pick-up board games open with somebody in charge and that has always done well. You can always check back on our site and check the schedule to see what games are being offered.

    Also, I have to mention that last year's surprise designer insertion team of Rob Bohl, Luke Crane, Josh Newman and Vincent Baker was a huge shot in the arm and just generally exciting for everybody. Also made for some great tables. We're hoping to get them back this year and do a proper job of promoting and selling their products. We're very excited (though he is going to hate to read this) that Clash is coming as well.

    \n We've tried various incarnations of pick-up RPGs/indie games explosion set-ups but they have never got off the ground simply because we didn't have anyone who would take charge. We do have a pretty vibrant team of storygamers here, but they tend to be pretty focused and go with the pre-scheduled game. They are all still wresting over what to run right now over at our forums, but you'll see their games start popping up in the schedule eventually.

    The thing is that we never seem to get a critical mass of people who want to game but haven't signed up for anything that would warrant a pick-up RPG situation. Part of that, I think, is due to our efficient scheduling system. Hard to say. It would be interesting if there was a slew of people who want to come and game but don't want to commit to anything and are just waiting to hear that there is an open RPG pick-up slot. Even better if someone were to take charge of running a pick-up RPG table and brought them out!

    \n I can take no credit for the soup dumplings (we must thank the new mainland Chinese diaspora which has come to Montreal to get educated and slowly are building up a new little Chinatown around Concordia, with several great restaurants), beyond knowing where to find them (and even that is due to other, more adventurous food-seekers in my life). It was quite easy to walk to the restaurant with some interesting and cool people who made a big effort to come up to the con and really add a ton to the weekend.

    btw, we're talking about finding a way to build in an opportunity for people to hang out together in a slightly more formalized, though still relaxed, way for next year. Except for that lunch, I felt like I only got the barest snippet of socializing time, which was frustrating with so many potential great conversations that kept getting cut off due to other time commitments. For example, poor Jason had to drag himself away from the dumplings just as we were about to order because he had a game to run. That was designer commitment!

    Seriously, thanks for coming. The community really appreciated it and it added a lot. Our dream is to make a community made up of the full and diverse range of storygamers (in the broadest definition of the word as Andy K defines it) and it has not always been easy or as successful as we would like. The success of this year's con should help to re-establish a foundation for the future.
