\n I just finished the first draft of my first ever RPG module, for Steal Away Jordan. It's called Final Defense of the Quilombos, and it's about the secret martial arts fortresses in the Amazon Rainforest in the seventeenth century (yes those were a real thing, not making this up). It's an action-adventure scenario which gives the players the power to change South American history forever.

I've never written a RPG module before, so I'm posting it here in the hopes of getting some feedback and advice about how I might revise it. Thanks in advance for your help!

    \n This is good!
    I am glad you mentioned that the "capoeira was disguised as a dance" thing is a myth.

    I'm not entirely sure about some of the secrets (the time-travel and psychic powers, specifically) but they're good options to have to show different tones you can have in the game.

    The corpo fechado had to be the first on the list, of course!
    In my game Malandros I have gone for the full-on "no one can kill you except with a magic dagger and/or if you break your taboos" version, on the principle that if there is a weakness in it, no matter how difficult to exploit, your enemies will get to work on that.
    You could expand the use of patuas to other effects, or link them to specific orixas - but I think that would diminish the level of pick-up-and-play you have going. So what you have is about right, I think.

    \n Hey, so, if you liked Final Defense of the Quilombos, this will also be congruent with your interests: http://bit.ly/aureosrpg (PDF link, in Portuguese).

    \xc1ureos, Os Dan\xe7arinos da Lua, by Rey \u201cOoze\u201d Jr., is a game set in the quilombos that has been called "an RPG about Spirituality, Struggle and Liberation, in which the dice play capoeira." The GM rolls difficulty dice into the roda - a circle in the centre of the table. Players have to beat that total, and they can also try to knock the GM's dice out of the roda when they roll.

    The player characters are the \xe1ureos of the title. They're ex-slaves called and granted special powers by the orixas to free their people. One of the things you track in play is how many people you've attracted to your quilombo, and as that goes up, you wind up with more problems, but are also able to make a greater impact on the world.


    \xc1ureos won the Fa\xe7a Voc\xea Mesmo competition in 2013 - there's a glowing review here: http://www.secular-games.com/2013/04/avaliacoes-fvm2013-aureos/
