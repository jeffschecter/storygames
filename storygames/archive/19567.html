\n Hello this is my first post here!
So, I have made a little story game called Stalker.
It's a short game with no preparation for 3 to 6 players.
One is playing a star in the dressing room, the other players are playing fans, but one of them is a stalker which want to kill the star.

It was never play tested so if you want to give it a try your feedback is welcome.
Tried my best to translate it in english.
Enjoy
http://www.gamersassociate.com/onegameamonth/stalker/stalker-en.pdf

    \n That's a really cool, creepy idea for a game, but what are the Fan players trying to do? Do they really just want to get an autograph?

    Are the Fans trying to help the Star catch the Stalker? The game might be over quite quickly in that case, as the Fans are in a good position to work out which of them is the Stalker.

    Equally, if the Fans are trying to help the Stalker kill the Star, they can easily befuddle the Star and leave them with no real clue as to which player is the Stalker, so the Star will lose.

    Also, given the mechanic for ending the game, it seems like it would be a multi-way tie between the Fans & the Stalker every time; I can't see any reason why a player wouldn't use one of their words each round.

    \n This is a really cool, tight design - simple and effective. Everything is covered, the game seems very elegant. I'd play this!

    However, I agree with James's comments, above. The "spend some time talking" nature of the game could use some clarification. What are we talking about, and how do I know what I should do, as a Fan? How long can we spend talking? Is it up to the Star?

    The game might be more interesting if Rounds were quite short, but not everyone got a chance to speak each round (it doesn't seem like a good idea for each player to be able to use a word each round, as James points out).

    As for the potential tie, I would recommend that the Stalker wins ties. (One neat way to do this might be to give everyone FIVE words, but for the Stalker one of the pieces of paper is blank, so they still "win" after they manage to play four words. Then you also don't know how close a given player is to achieving "victory".)

    \n Well, the easiest way would be to allow the Fans to suspect each other (and point fingers at each other), as well. And if the Star accuses you of being the Stalker, you get taken away by security.

    Ah! I just figured out a simple way to do it.

    Have EVERYONE write creepy words on ALL the papers, and shuffle them randomly. But include one piece of paper which says "STALKER".

    A security guard comes in and says, "Hey, we received a death threat against the Star this morning. Mr/Ms Star, please let us know if there's anyone suspicious in here; we will be glad to remove them for you."

    Then play normally. Everyone is going to sound a little suspicious, naturally, because they all have creepy words to use, but they'll want to draw attention to other Fans. When the Stalker uses up four words, they play the piece of paper which says "STALKER", and they win the game.

    Everyone having to incorporate creepy words sounds like it could be a lot of fun, I think.

    \n I agree, it's more fun if everybody should have creepy words but Stalker needs to have more or you cannot find him.

    I think about 2/3 mechanisms:
    - The first one use the one you suggested with each player pointing fingers at each other but in a structured way: Fans can one time per game (for all fans) asks for a vote to point at the stalker: "I've heard in the press there is a stalker which want to kill you. I think Harvey is acting really strangely since we are in this room, what do you think?". If they find the real stalker, the fans all win, either the one which have call the vote is out of the game (if this is the stalker he loses and the other wins...). The star can vote too. Players after a discussion (which will be certainly animated) vote all together at the same time by pointing the finger at the player they think he is the stalker.
    - After the Star distributed the stacks to player, each fan give one paper of his choice to his right fan neighbor (this may be done twice eventually, don't know).
    - The star before distributing the stacks add one word to each stack except for one which will have a blank paper.

    This raises minimum player count to 4 by the way (1 star, 1 stalker, 2 fans)

    The first part may be quite long to explain in my format, will try.
