\n December already!
What stuff should we watch, read, play?

1.Feel free to respond here in-thread to another link posted.

a. If you're looking to start a conversation, perhaps take your comment and create a new thread with it in the Stuff to Watch category.

b.Otherwise, to prevent too many comments in the thread, number your response (first response: [1]; second response [2]; third response [3]). If you see that you're the second person to post, "Wow, that looks cool!" to a post here, then put a "[2]" at the top of your post. Three posts ([3]) is the limit: The fourth person to post should just take their comment and create a new thread, to carry the discussion over there further.

Why?

Because these threads are great: We can link to little things being talked about on the web in general, on blogs, or other forums that might be noteworthy, without having to dedicate a new thread to each and every one (although that, too, is fine if you want to discuss the item further or "open up a discussion").

Responding to these posts with a comment or two is understandable (other times there might be no comments, just cause the link was self-explanatory: nothing to say, lots of heads nodding in agreement). But we don't want to create a huge discussion in the middle of the thread: Some folks get a lot out of these threads, and don't want to look back to see the "10 latest posts" just to see that it's a heated discussion of something from a previous page. Cool? Cool.

    \n So you know how d6s with carved pips are not technically weighted properly, because the 6-pip side is lighter than the opposite 1-pip side? Sure, you knew that, so you rolled pipped d6s for shortsword damage so you could get 6s and 5s more. Well, a college student here in Tucson is ONTO YOUR SHENANIGANS and she has a rad Kickstarter to precision-machine dice where the pips are geometrically arranged and of different depths so that the center of the die is also the center of mass. And the corners will be so precise and sharp you won't be able to roll it on a wood table. You'll need one of those dramatic cloths like MAYBE THE ONE ANDY SOLD YOU IN HIS KICKSTARTER?!!? to roll on. Seriously, check out the math on this kickstarter page.

    Tons of story games use six sided dice, buy these and experience True Randomness.

    Precision Machined Dice by Amber Rix

    \n So as some of you may know I pick theme songs for my campaigns and use playlists for music in them. You may remember that I was noodling over this or that for an X-Men community college game when I found Knock This Way, which combined the 70s sound of the early X-Men with the pop inclusiveness of Gaga.

    Well, in my current Marvel game, one character is the Olympian, actually Artemis the Olympian goddess, who suffers amnesia but keeps returning to the Olympics year after year. And an NPC is a Pitbull-a-like rap mogul who said "Tony Stark has a superhero team?! I'm a billionaire, why can't I have a superhero team?!?" So you can imagine what happened when I saw DJ Earworm's "Victory Mix". I started rocking out and dancing SO HARD. Sometimes the world just conspires to create the perfect music for my games.

      \n Synnibun 2

      >>>
      Next in line: FATAL Kickstarter.
      >>>
      Hey now, FATAL is pretty horrifying on many levels (namely sexual abuse being the focus of like most of the magic and social interactions; I don't remember much upon my readthrough years ago, but I do remember that).

      Synnibar is... well, it's very much a product of its times. The "extreme!" was always interesting to me, but not the rules (and things like, "If the GM forgets a rule and the player calls them on it, they can roll the campaign back like a snapshot"). But it was never as horrifying as FATAL.

      BTW, if folks want the party game version of Synnibar, it's called "Refuge in Audacity" by Logan Bonner, and I can confirm that it is genuinely fun (and of course tongue-in-cheek). It's sort of like Drowning and Falling meets Human Occupied Landfill, but with Synnibar being the central inspiration of the game.

      http://www.lulu.com/us/en/shop/logan-bonner/refuge-in-audacity/paperback/product-14918968.html

      -Andy

      \n Speaking of unique randomizers, the Kickstarter for DiceRings just finished yesterday. They're wearable rings with spinners on them that emulate dice. The cleverest one is the Fudge ring which does a 4dF roll with an accurate distribution by varying the widths of the target areas for the different outcomes (4 is much much narrower than 0.)

      I just barely got in on this one yesterday but didn't think to post here till it was already too late..
