    \n Tim, regarding justification and hyphenation. I'm in good company. Giambattista Bodoni felt the same way. Robert Bringhurst felt good enough about it to set his book "The Elements of Typographic Style" that way, and apparently nearly every professional book designer in the world feels that way as well:

    I just took a random sampling of more than 20 books from my library (around 8 tall, full bookcases). Of them, every single one of them were full justified, and only a few of them lacked word-break hyphenation. All of my typography references are set full justified with the exception of one, the Adobe Typography Primer, which in my opinion looks like crap. I'd be interested to know if anyone else comes up with a different conclusion. Books of poetry don't count.

    No hyphens are better than hyphens, obviously. I do some pretty serious tweaking in my justification preferences to avoid hyphens whenever possible. I don't know if it's possible in PagePlus.

    I'm a fan of ragged-right text as well. It just has to be appropriate to the visual tone of the book: a book on modern art, for example.

    I hope you find something of use, Tim. If you disagree, keep bringing it up. One or both of us is bound to learn something.
