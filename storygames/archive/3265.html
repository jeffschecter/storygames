Certainly D&D 3.x has moved back in that direction, but its interesting that that tradition of play has continued in a section of the hobby that doesn't even really consider it an RPG.

    \n It's actually more like an evolutionary split. Skirmish miniatures gaming with a bit more story-development thrown in than folks generally associate with one-off battle games is a pretty common ( if not as well-known) form of minis gaming.

    I'd also add that a fair players of this type of game have both roleplaying and wargaming backgrounds. More than a few of the folks I've met online who like this stuff have come over to it/added it on in later years.

    I'm not sure they'd necessarily appreciate the comparison to newer forms of D&D however; a common complaint I've heard is just how unnecessarily complex and bogged down 3.x can be in compariosn to these games ( an opinion I share, incidently).

    You might also want to check into these games if that sort of thing interests you:
    Astounding Tales! by Howard Whitehouse
    .45 Aventure by Richard Johnson
    Legends of Araby by Two-Hour Wargames

    The first two are pulp games, the last arabian fantasy (interestingly it also has solo campaign play and character advancement rules).
