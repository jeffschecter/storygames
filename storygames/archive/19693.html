\n I'm working on a campaign inspired by Stargate: Universe, but ported to a 5E D&D world. One of the things I need for this project is a way to quickly but robustly* generate 1d4 islands that are within dinghy range that the crew can potentially explore each session. Any ideas of already-extant web resources that would work well for this sort of thing?

Thanks!

Matt

*By "robustly" I mean it won't just give me a sense of what's on the island, it will actually give me some features and an encounter table. I don't need maps, as I don't intend to run 5E with a grid, but I do need to be able to "run the island" based off of a quick series of dice rolls.

    \n OK, here is what I have come up with using the M&M thing as a base but then modifying it further to add Tech Level and adding the possibility of a variety of races inhabiting it. I may change the monster charts further to be more in line with the 5e MM, as noted.

    I rolled up one island so far and it seems pretty cool. I got a big island, roughly 9 x 11 square miles, with a powerful city of Humans and Elves ruling over the island, which is the property of a god. There are phoenixes present, too, so I'm thinking maybe the god is a fire god and the Humans and Elves are appointed as guardians of the phoenix? Lots of possible story stuff here. Also there are creepy territorial markers. Not sure what to do with that. :-)
