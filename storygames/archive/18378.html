    \n Yes. You test Steel when you are surprised or in pain. If you lose a Duel of Wits, you have to test Steel (see the Shocking Defeat rules)...though it's only really a big deal if you want to escalate to violence.

    You also test Steel if someone successfully sneaks up on you (stealth) or ambushes you (tactics). The GM can call for a Steel test if a character experiences something shocking and the character's ability to react is in question.

    \n FYI, I feel like most of the time those Steel tests will be the automatic type with no need to roll and therefore no need to worry about hesitation. In the rare occasion when it might actually matter and you're not in a Fight, DoW, or RnC, just to spell out NoClue's answer a little bit more, you can use the hesitation to add +1 Ob (or however much the situation demands) to the roll.

    [advice you didn't ask for]
    The reason why I say it's rare for this sort of thing to happen is that means you're doing something like "And just as you're about to ambush the caravan, you spot a rattlesnake! Roll Steel!" Sometimes that's cool. Maybe the guy has Snake Fear as a Trait or something and you're punching it in the nose. Or maybe he failed a Stealthy test and that's your awesome consequence. But it's really easy to unintentionally be a dick with that stuff, making the test harder just because you can. So, just as a word to the wise, if you find yourself calling for a Steel test outside of the subsystems, it's worth taking a step back (or forward, so you can read their character sheets) and doublecheck if it makes sense.
    [/advice you didn't ask for]
