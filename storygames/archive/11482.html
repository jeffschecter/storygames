\n I'm looking for examples of games that support either of the following notions (individually or both at once):
\n
\n1) Games that use some sort of communal resource to either encourage or discourage cooperation between the players.
\n
\n2) Games that use some notion of "budget" for designing scenarios; in particular systems where the budget is based on the "strength" of the PCs and used to determine the "strength" of the threat they face.

    Posted By: Wordman1) Games that use some sort of communal resource to either encourage or discourage cooperation between the players.
    PTA Fan Mail encourages a form of "cooperation"--accord with the collective tone or style for the game. And that resource is only available as the GM narrates it into existence.\n
    2) Games that use some notion of "budget" for designing scenarios; in particular systems where the budget is based on the "strength" of the PCs and used to determine the "strength" of the threat they face.
    PTA has a scene budget, literally. That's what it's called.

    Universalis is basically nothing but a budget-management system for assigning narrative authority and, thereby, increasing the importance and efficacy of things.

    Descent is a board game with a built-in scaling system (though the scenarios themselves have no budget per se).
