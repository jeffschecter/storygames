\n For a game I'm noodling around with, I'm trying to formulate a list of ways that a character in a kind of dystopian future might get things done. I'm thinking of things like Bladerunner, Neuromancer, Snowcrash, Robocop, Judge Dredd, and so on. Not skills per se, but more like an avenue for achieving a result. Here's what I've got:

Sniper
Computer Hacking (technically "cracking")
Seduction
Lies
Bribery
Breaking and Entering
Word on the Street
Martial Arts

Anything else that should be on this list? Any of these I could run together? I'm not looking for efficiency or to cover all my bases, I'm looking for fun and evocative, but reasonably generic ways to do things.

    \n Hi Tomas,

    I was being a little facetious, sorry. The game is mostly designed to recreate the fun I had playing Cyberpunk 2020, and various homebrew games designed to look like CP2020, without all the hassles that those rules caused getting in the way of what we actually wanted to do in the game. It's kind of hard for me to describe exactly what that is - that's what the game is for, but part of it is that the focus is not on any one aspect of the setting, but rather on the methods that the characters use to achieve their goals. It's about hare-brained schemes, desperate ploys, elaborate plans, and the above inevitably going horribly wrong and descending into gunfights.

    With that in mind, I'm trying not to write too much setting into the rules of the game - you can create your own reasons for getting into trouble, in fact I believe the game supports that fairly well.
