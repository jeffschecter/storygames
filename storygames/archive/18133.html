\n I have a research methods presentation that I need to find a group for. I am hoping to present on the ethnography of roleplaying games. Ethnography consists of observing, and possibly participating in, in this case, a roleplaying game.

This game could be ongoing, and doesn't have to begin or end with my research. The players will all be required to sign a form stating that they agree to participate (or give spoken consent if they are unable to read the form). The minimum age for players is 16.

If you have any questions, I would be happy to answer them.

    \n Thanks epweissengruber. I'll contact you via email for the details.

    Graham, I'm trying to do something similar to the research conducted by Gary Alan Fine (Shared Fantasy). He used ethnography (which is normally associated with anthropology, as opposed to the sociology background Fine has) to participate in several roleplaying game groups. At this point, it's just for a research methods class presentation, so I'm just getting a sense of what's involved in conducting the research.

    Unfortunately, because York University is adamant about anonymity, I won't be able to give details about what happened. I'm bound by my ethics proposal, even if it doesn't make sense.
