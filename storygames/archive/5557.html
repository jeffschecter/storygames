    \n Damn there are a lot of RBH posts. It almost feel guilty posting about my game

    All I will say is that we played at Dreamation and it was a blast. It's light and has some distinct coolness going on. With a little fiddling we worked out an entire adventure in a convention slot and were rolling on the floor for a good part of it. RBH brings the fun!

    An example: Steve is playing a Count without a county and has this really cool warhorse. Steve notes that whenever they have to leave the horse behind he tells it to wait and if he's not back in time to wander off and head home or find them Invariably the horse, he says, finds it way to where they are. Our own illustrious Mel White is playing a snake and decides that he has been wandering in two paths for a while. The others don't know his double exists yet. After a short while he declares that since the group has been wandering together his double has been leading the horse to where the party needs it completely ignorant of his presence. So the players see what's going on but the character all think this horse is the coolest and smartest beast in the land completely perpetuated by Mel's double! Gosh, and that was just the casual table-talk.

    - Don
