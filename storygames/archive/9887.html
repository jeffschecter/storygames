\n Okay, here is the set-up... The RPG I am talking about today is called Intergalactic League of Brawlers... a game seeking to emulate the over-the-top battles and characters of Shonen anime (DBZ, Bleach, Yu-Yu Hakasho, etc...). It has been through several revisions since its inception many years ago...
\n
\n[align=center]Anyways...[/align]
\n
\nCharacters in this RPG, called Brawlers, have a Power Level (PLv). Everyone begins the game at PLv 3. PLv is measurement of a Brawler's potential strength... not his current strength. During battle, Brawlers gain what are called Power Stages (PS). PS cannot exceed PLv. PS fluctuates up and down quite a bit throughout a fight. It goes up by spending Ki and goes down by using Techniques. PS makes it easier to gain Ki and also gives cumulative damage bonuses when fighting Brawlers of a lower PS. Ki is gained normally during a Power Up Phase that occurs for all characters every Round. Ki is used for a bunch of things including the aforementioned PS gathering, but also includes damage soak, gaining initiative bonuses and healing.
\n[align=center]As one can tell, the acquisition of PS and the subsequent strategic use of it is key to winning fights.[/align]
\n
\nAs it stands, regardless of a Brawler's PLv... All Brawlers begin a fight at PS 0. The question is, with what little you guys know... I'm wondering if Brawlers should start a battle with a certain PS based on their PLv? My initial thought is yes, thus Brawlers with more potential begin with greater strength. A possible reaction to that would allow Brawlers to begin at a PS equal to 1/2 of their PLv...
\n
\nThe Attribute that governs Ki gain is called Soul. I was wondering if a different term would be better? Other candidates I can think of are Spirit and Aura.
\nThe Attribute that governs mid-battle Fighting Spirit* recovery is called Adrenaline. Does that sound appropriate? The only other options I can think of that fit are Resolve.
\n[align=right]* Think Hp with a different flavor[/align]
\nI'm also looking for opinions/suggestions on how Techniques should be handled. To use a Technique the Brawler chooses an amount of PS to spend. For every PS spent, damage capability is temporarily increased. In addition to that you are allowed to choose one Aspect per PS spent. Aspects apply a specific effect such as more damage, better odds of success, status ailments and other such things. Each Brawler has access to a limited number Aspects that he chooses beforehand. The same Aspect may be selected multiple times.
\nDoes the idea of on-the-fly Technique creation for situational strategies sound like it would be fun and functional? For the record, any math involved is pretty simple... Mostly addition/subtraction of blocks of 3 and 4.
\n
\nI'm also thinking of implementing an Inspiration system for PC Brawlers that will resembles the Strength/Weakness mechanic in 3:16: CAtS. I love the idea of explaining inspirational flashbacks in order to gain a combat boost. For my game, the effect would be a boost to PS and PLv for the duration of the battle... Inspirations would only usable once per battle, cannot be about the same subject and only when the PC has 20% or less health.
\nSo what do you fellas think? Would such a mechanic fit in with the style of game I am trying to create? I'm thinking it would but I'm unsure.
\n
\nBecause of the inspiration for the game... I want to include an optional format for Structured Play. Structured Play is broken down like so...
\nSeason (overarching storyline) broken up into two or more Story Arcs which contain 3 or more Episodes. Each Story Arc is a major movement towards the completion of the Season. Episodes are broken down by type; Set-Up (establish characters & conflict), Pursue (seek resolution to conflict previously revealed), Finale (resolution of conflict) and Filler (character & setting exploration). Each episode is meant to consume an entire session of play.
\nThis would be used for outlining purposes mainly... though I do want to include a training/battle montage section in the Set-Up or Pursue Episodes. This would simulate those episodes in anime where the characters train to increase their power in order to take on some big bad. So how does this look as a rough outline for structured play?

    \n
    \nmarkhaselb\n
    \n
    how fast do you regain Ki (essentially: how fast can you gain PS) and how high is the PLv of characters with little, medium and high experience? It seems that you can not do much with your techniques in the beginning of the game because 1 technique uses one or more PS already. What is the point of increasing damage by using PS and choosing the damage aspect? Maybe the aspect could increase the minimum damage so you have better control of how much damage the attack does.
    \n
    \n
    \nMyself\n
    \n
    I agree that Techniques are pretty limited in the beginning. That is what I'm going for actually. Think of Yusuke from YuYu Hakusho, he can only fire his Spirit Gun once a day when he starts out! In any event, while the game is suppose to have some strategic mechanical depth, a lot of the action and excitement stems from lots and lots of color.
    \n
    \nPLv is a slow progression as a default. However, in the text I urge GMs to progress PLv at whatever rate he feels works. The problem as of right now is that I don't have any play testing done so I'm not sure if players are going to need to feel rewarded with rapid PLv gain. My hope is that rapid PLv gain won't be necessary...
    \n
    \nI have some alternate PLv mechanic that operates as a Death Spiral with a semi-static PLv. Basically, your PLv equals the tens digit of your Fighting Spirit (which is 100 for everyone) with 100 counting as 10. Thus, as one's FISP is lowered via damage, Brawlers lose their potential (PLv) and may even lose strength (PS lowering because PLv became too low)... I mentioned it before but the Adrenaline Attribute is used mid-battle to restore FISP.
    \nI think this method would work pretty well for games that want to avoid power creep. I set it as an alternate method because Shonen games are infamous for insane power creep. =D
    \n
    \nThe Ki gain mechanic is a dice pool... the only dice pool mechanic in the game, everything else runs on a unified mechanic. So, folks have a Soul rating... usually a value anywhere from 3 to 7... And this rating determines how many d6 you roll during the Power Up Phase. Every success, being 4 ~ 6, scores that Brawler 1 Ki. The GM is encouraged to fudge his own roll and simply assign a Ki value that is "reasonable" for enemies. Each PS applies a +1 Soul. I'm already seeing a potential problem with super high Soul values, so I'm planning on allowing anything above 10 to count as an automatic +1 Ki.
    \nEach PS requires double its value in Ki to acquire. Thus, PS 1 cost 2 Ki while PS 52 cost 104 Ki. I did this so that even though you are gaining a lot more Ki as you gain PS, its not nearly enough to cover the spread and make higher PS really easy to acquire.
    \n
    \nPS only increases damage if you have a higher PS than the opponent. PS spent on (lost to) a Technique increases damage regardless and often at a higher rate. Choosing a damage boosting Aspect then further increases damage. However, I should note that these bonuses are merely towards damage potential.
    \nEveryone has Power Attribute, rated from 3 ~ 7, that is multiplied by a value called Damage Factor, which is rated anywhere from 1 ~ 5 depending on the result of the attack (attack and damage result in a single die roll). Thus, Pow bonuses can be greatly helpful or only slightly beneficial.

    \n I'm gonna be honest here; there are a few things I'm shaky on here.

    I really don't like the alternate death spiral rule. It basically says that "You're losing, so lose more!".
    Similarly, the way PS gains through Ki then increase Soul (and thus more Ki)... it smells a bit too much like "You're winning, so win more!". Although, due to the way it ramps up, it might not be that bad.

    Honestly, I'm just wary of "Win More!" and "Lose More!" type mechanics in any game.

    That and the "GM is encouraged to fudge his roll and assign a Ki value"... I don't like that much. Either have him roll above-board or have him determine Ki via some other method (maybe he has a pool of points to spend per fight or per session), or there should be some flat increase, or some such. But just arbitrarily assigning a Ki - and with the now-useless ritual of "rolling dice" - bothers me.

    But some of these things might be kind of personal preference, and the rest of it looks off to a good start. If any of my comments don't make sense, feel free to elaborate on what you'd like to know.

    \n I've never been a fan of death spiral, to tell you the truth. I included out of the shallow hope that I might one day finish it and distribute the game to others... I know lots of people prefer death spirals so as a simple alternate rule, I can't help but think it won't hurt to include it.
    \n
    \nThe Soul (or, Aura as it shall be known from hence forth) bonus for PS exists to tempt the player into keeping his PS as high as possible. As I mentioned before, PS is often spent with the intention of launching a huge attack. Due to Adrenaline as a constant source of healing, the best way for a Brawler to defeat his foe is by making a huge attack. Sure, a bad collection of rolls can mean the end of anyone but generally speaking... Techniques are key in dominating an opponent. Similarly, knocking your PS down too far will make you vulnerable... so its a shaky balance that favors neither all-out gamblers or tight-ass conservatives.
    \n
    \nTo expand a bit on my reasoning above... the Aura bonus makes each PS worth two things; +1 Power and +1 Aura. However, the Power bonus is easily canceled out by the PS of a similarly stronger Brawler... This means, to draw out that power the Brawler must perform a Technique. Each PS used for a Tech automatically nets that Brawler +2 Power, which can translate anywhere from +2 damage to +10 damage if he hits (keep in mind that Brawlers have 100 Fighting Spirit).
    \n
    \n
    That and the "GM is encouraged to fudge his roll and assign a Ki value"... I don't like that much.
    \n
    \nIts a simple encouragement, and as such is 100% possible to ignore. The thing is, if there are two Players and the GM is controlling 2 foes... he may have to roll 20 dice. 10 dice for a single person is okay, though in my opinion is pushing the limits of patience... but 20 dice? No thanks. And its the GM! He isn't running a competition against the players. His role isn't to destroy the PCs, its to challenge them to top their last heroic and awesome description. To that effect, I personally would simply select a Ki gain that breeds the sort of tension I would want for the situation at hand.
    \n
    \nThat said... The only mechanical aspect I have any true misgivings about as of this moment is the Ki gain dice pool. I've been playing around with the idea of just making it a solid value... meaning, current Aura = Ki gain for that Round.
    \n
    \nI'm not sure how this would impact the game as a whole... but it moves it towards a more diceless style. The d20 would then be the sole die used. Which reminds me, the game uses one of two tables (skill or combat) to determine the outcome of an action rolled. For instance, a character making an attack (which are called Clashes, for some reason) adds his Offense stat to his roll... then the opponent subtracts his Defense... the total is then compared against the Clash Resolution table. Just like Talislanta. =D

    \n [align=center]Battle Start[/align]\n
    \n
    The GM and the Player multiply their Brawler\u2019s Speed attribute by 3 to calculate their beginning Initiative Rank. Both Dolan and Yoguna have 4 [Spe] and thus an [IR] of 12. Now both set their Power Stage to 0 regardless of their Power Level (both happen to have a [PLv] of 10).
    \n
    \n
    \n[align=center]Preparation Phase 1[/align]\n
    \n
    During this phase Ki is acquired, Ki is spent and turn order is decided. Each Brawler gains Ki equal to their [Aur]. In later Rounds Brawlers will receive an [Aur] bonus equal to their [PS]. To increase [PS], the Brawler must spend double the next [PS]\u2019 value in Ki. Every Ki spent on [IR] will increase it by an amount equal to the Brawler\u2019s [Spe].
    \nYoguna gains 6 Ki. GM spends 2 Ki to increase Yoguna\u2019s [PS] from 0 to 1. He then spends 1 Ki to increase his [IR] by 4. He now has 3 Ki left.
    \nDolan gains 4 Ki. Player spends his 2 Ki to increase his [PS] to 1. He has 2 Ki left.
    \nDolan has [IR] 12 while Yoguna has [IR] 16. This means that Yoguna acts and then Dolan acts.
    \n
    \n
    \n[align=center]Action Phase 1[/align]\n
    \n
    This is the phase where all the action happens, hence the name! Neither the GM nor the Player has described anything so far, this is because the start of the Action Phase is reserved for such things. In reverse order, narration is performed. This means that the final narration will be done by the person taking his action first. Thus, it is perfectly normal to blend in one\u2019s opening narrative with his action for that Round.
    \n
    \n(Player) Not wanting to show off much at first, I let the small flame I conjured earlier circle around my arm menacingly as I watch for the strange man\u2019s first move.
    \n(GM) You can see the spasms gripping Yoguna\u2019s body becoming more violent! A nasty snap is heard, Yoguna leans forward as his head does a 180. His neck makes yet another sick crunch before going limp, revealing his face to you once again. His wild eyes stare out at you, he wordlessly launching at you!\n
    \n
    The GM has decided to attack, a common thing as you might imagine. This is called a Clash Check. Before rolling the die, both the GM and the Player are given the option to perform a Technique. Neither is inclined to accept the option yet. The GM rolls 1d20 and gets an 10. This value is increased by Yoguna\u2019s Offense attribute, which is 7 for a total of 17. This value is then reduced by Dolan\u2019s Defense which is 4 for a grand total of 13. Looking at the Clash Resolution chart, this is a Damage Factor 4 hit. Every attack achieves some kind of success. To determine damage, the attacker\u2019s Power attribute is multiplied by [DF]. Yoguna has 4 [Pow]. Brawlers can gain [Pow] bonuses if they have a higher [PS] than their opponent. However, his [PS] is equal to Dolan\u2019s thus he receives no [Pow] bonus. In other words, 4x4=16 damage. Damage reduces Fighting Spirit, this means that Dolan now has 84 [FISP] instead of 100. Damage is not necessarily wounds, remember [FISP] can be lowered simply by denying the opponent the ability to feel effective! In addition to dealing damage, Clashes reduce [IR] by the [DF] value. This means that Dolan now has an 8 [IR].
    \n
    \n(GM) The bizarre man arrives and screams! A ghastly green energy fires from his upside down mouth directly at you!
    \n(Player) I throw out my hand in defense, letting my fire bolt slam into the foul attack. The strength of my attack repels the beam, causing it to dissipate but not before consuming my fire bolt. That\u2019s fine, I\u2019ve got plenty more where that came from! Its my turn now, so I\u2019m going to leap straight at him and throw a fiery punch!\n
    \n
    The Player is now performing a Clash of his own. He rolls a 8, increased to 15 by Dolan\u2019s [Off] then reduced to 9 by Yoguna\u2019s [Def]. Checking the table, this is [DF] 3 hit. Dolan has a 7 [Pow], 7x3 for a total of 21 damage! The GM notes this, taking Yoguna from 100 to 79 then reducing his [IR] by 3 to 13.
    \n
    \n(GM) He takes the shot to the ribs but his quick, unpredictable movements allow him to dodge most of the fire as it rises from your fist and scorches part of his ribs and the air behind him. Yoguna gives no response to the pain in any foreseeable manner.
    \n(Player) Alright! Next round, I\u2019m feeling lucky!

    \n Thanks for the example, Matthew. This makes the game a lot clearer in my mind.

    Overall, it looks like a lot of fun! There's a bunch of math to learn here, but by the end of the example, I felt I could actually play the basics (roll to hit, add/subtract, check chart, spend ki, etc.), so that's excellent. In fact, it looks fluent enough to be ready for thorough playtesting. And this will need a LOT of playtesting. Games that are this crunchy need to be balanced enough to remain enjoyable over time.

    For example, our hero uses his special ability late in the fight. Why not earlier? The effect would have been the same, he would have had an early advantage, and it would have cost less; after all, going down from PS 2 to PS1 is made up with expending 4 ki, but if your ability takes you down from PS 5 to PS 4, you need to spend 10 ki to get back up to PS 5. That seems to me to encourage early use of abilities rather than building up to them as finishing moves or the likes. Your initial post said that you could spend additional PS for more effects, so that could take care of this issue if that's still the case. The additional effects would just have to be worth quite a bit; otherwise, I'll just go from PS 1 to PS 2 each round and use my Technique every time. The expense of ki is another one to watch out for... as healing can undo, in some cases, more damage than the character took last turn, which can be frustrating.

    Also... games where the narration is pure color can sometimes lose the narration over time. I designed a game once in which players fought each other with evil minions in character battles. As in your example, the way we described attacks was purely for fun, there was no effect that came out of it. Playtesting showed that people stopped narrating much of their actions and just went to rolling the dice. That may or may not happen much with your game, but it's always a risk when there's crunch to worry about, and no effect comes from whether you narrate creative actions or just "I punch him in the nose." Having a GM in your game can fix this, if the GM leads with good examples and keeps that narrative interest up; you may want to have GM advice to that effect in the game.

    So, good luck with your playtesting, and keep us updated on your progress!
