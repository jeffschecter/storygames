\n I've got an albatross hung around my neck. But instead of a dead bird, it looks like a box of RPGs that I gathered together 5 years ago with the intention to immediately sell on E-Bay, laughing all the way to the bank. Then came the reality of writing up a dozen or more descriptions, predicting shipping charges, and processing all of it. And so, the box has sat, handily already packaged through two moves.

I finally want to get rid of it. But I'm less concerned with laughing to the bank, especially if I can maybe do some good instead.

What's mostly in the box is 3rd edition D&D and 2-3rd edition White Wolf stuff. Where can I unload it?

I've been thinking libraries. The main concern here is if they'll understand what to do with it.

Used bookstores is another option. Sort of the same deal though. The couple around me that I've gone to don't seem to have a section for gaming, so I'm not sure they'd even take them. I know some do, I found my first AD&D book in the basement of a bookstore, but it was kind of in a random pile.

Gamestores. I live near Endgame, and they have a consignment section, but it's already filled with stuff like this and I've just made the assumption that they don't need more of the same. I may look into that path, but I'm not really interested at this time, so I'd thought I'd mention it. Ditto goes for selling at Cons or local game oriented auctions.

I'm mostly looking for experiences and advice. My biggest goal is getting the books to a place where they can be appreciated. If not by the local community, then fans and collectors (i.e. E-Bay or an equivalent), and tips on finding a good place that might involve something more clever than calling them or going there personally.

Alternatives to eBay are also welcome (and I do know about the trade thread and RPGnet's for sale area).

PS I noticed that I was using E-Bay instead of the more proper eBay. I left this typographical error in to illustrate how out of touch I am with using that website. If you want to remedy my aversion to the process of selling online, feel free.

    \n Endgame's April auction is mostly boardgames and wargames (and is great).

    Bookbuyers in Mountain View has a large used RPG section. When you take books in for trade or sale you can specify that you'd like them to to take some, more, or all. Some means they only take what they need, more means they'll check around on the books they don't want and return the rest, and all means they'll recycle anything they don't take, so you don't have to come back to pick up the remainders. Their website has the hours they buy at. You get more if you're willing to take trade credit, and they have LOTS of SF, Fantasy, Mystery books, manga, RPGs, and graphic
    novels.
