\n My friends and I are putting together a Casino Benefit Party to raise money for the pop-up arcade and gallery we're running in Manhattan. Sure, we'll have your standard poker and blackjack tables, maybe roulette if we can find the equipment. But this got us thinking: we're all games people, surely there must be some weird novel indie casino games out there.

The only story game I have that provides rules for betting real money is Timothy Kleinert's Mexican Standoff, but I'm curious if any of you have ever tried designing or hacking casino games.

Beyond my current even planning, I could see organizing a "Designer's Casino" at GenCon/Origins/Dreamation, with proceeds going to Child's Play. Maybe this can turn into a thing?
