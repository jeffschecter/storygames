Looking for stronghold/downtime games

\n
\n
\n
\n\nJakob \n\n\n
\n
\n\nDecember 2013 \n edited December 2013 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n Some of my fondest RPG memories are from the downtime between adventures, when PCs pursue their personal interests and maintain their stronghold, whichever form it takes. This part of a campaign often leads to memorable and priceless moments, such as the party's barbarian insisting on pink drapes for all bedroom windows. The massive popularity of a certain video game involving mining and crafting shows that this type of gameplay appeals to a lot of gamers. I also remember how prominent the stronghold feature was in the marketing for Baldur's Gate 2. Slaying dragons is fun, but gamers also enjoy just kicking back and taking it easy!

Ever since I got out of high school, though, neither me nor my friends have really had the time and determination required to run a long campaign, which means that this type of downtime has become much less common. I've recently started looking for games that fully embrace this stronghold/downtime idea, and so I thought I'd ask for suggestions here. It's especially fitting in holiday times, when huddling together around a table with no set goal in mind is one of the most popular activities.

What I'm looking for is a game that focuses on the stronghold/downtime aspect of a typical roleplaying campaign but can be enjoyed in a much shorter time - preferably in one-shot sessions. The closest thing that I have found so far is The Quiet Year, which checks a lot of boxes, although the story implies a constant sense of urgency as opposed to the relatively relaxed mood of typical stronghold/downtime play. Any other suggestions?

    \n I love FreeMarket. It lets me create rather than destroy (destruction is still a possibility but not required) and the game mechanics are focused very much on the "stronghold" aspect of play. If you can get your hands on it and have the patience to figure out the system for conflict (it isn't too complicated but will take a session or two to figure out) I believe this could be what you are looking for. Key advice: accept that FreeMarket is utopia.
