\n I'm in the process of working out a hack for Shadowrun (2nd Edition), and I'm putting in a new mechanism that needs it's own skill (and skill concentrations). I know what the skill is and what it's about, but I don't know what to call it.

The mechanism is an action where one character supports the action of another character:

"You're firing the machine gun? Cool, I'll be the dude who feeds in the ammo belt."
...or...
"My dude calls up your dude his headgear to shout orders at him. You know, in a helpful way."

The skill I'm trying to nail down is the ability of your character to be helpful. It's about figuring out what needs to be done and doing it. It also includes knowing what needs to be done and assigning that job to someone. It includes "Leadership", but that's only half the coin.

If Leadership is the ability to give useful orders, then what's the word for the ability to follow orders? For figuring out who needs help and how to help them without the orders being given? And, most importantly, what's the word for all of that put together?

I'm hoping for real words, in English or otherwise, but I'm open to "Truthiness"-style coining.

    \n Hey, cool, man--if you're happy.

    Me? I'd find a better collective term than Logistics. From Wiktionary:
    Logistics (n.)
    1. The process of planning, implementing, and controlling the efficient, effective flow and storage of goods, services and related information from their point of origin to point of consumption for the purpose of conforming to customer requirements.
    2. (military) The procurement, supply, maintenance, and transportation of equipment, facilities, and personnel.

    See? I just don't think the range of elements that comprise leadership and followership (nor your examples of these skills' uses, above) are in any way subsumed by Logistics. Heck, Logistics covers, like, five existing Shadowrun skills (driving, bartering, streetwise, bureaucracy, research), none of which fit the leadership/followership mold, unless you squint and cock your head to the side more than about 15\xb0.

    Perhaps an alternate approach would help: what's the "problem" in your mind with the terms Support, Aid, or Coordination--all of which are aspects of leadership and followership, not to mention literal terms that are used *in actual life* (e.g. in the military) for the range of combined activities that you posit (your examples)?

    ----------
    First, another follow-up: the literal application of such skill use implies a form of combined skill check: the "actor" must make clear his or her intentions under stress or duress; the "supporter" must be able to quickly understand and act effectively to assist with his or her complimentary skill(s). So... do you see use of these skills as (whatever Shadowrun uses for) combined or parallel skill checks, in most cases? After all, if a follower just acts on his or her own initiative in an effective supporting manner... isn't that "combat tactics" or some equivalent? The follower wasn't ordered--might not even be able to communicate with the leader--but he or she can still apply "supporting" efforts without such a combined check. Conversely, with followers out of reach or contact, what does a "leadership" roll accomplish? Again, a leader being able to anticipate where his followers will apply effort is more like combat tactics than leadership.

    Second, leadership is a solid ability, WRT "standalone" applications: inspiring speeches, synergy bonuses to persuade or intimidate inferiors, morale boosting by leading the charge. But I'd like to follow-follow-up by asking what followership can do "standalone," in the absence of leadership? Synergy bonus to avoid loss of morale ("I'm sticking to my post; it's orders!") or to resist bad or unethical orders, maybe (my step-brro mentioned that as a key of followership which might on the surface seem "unfollowerlike" but which shows that a good follower always answers to the Combat Code of Ethics, or whatever it's called in the USMC).

    I realize I've taken this a bit further afield than "help me brainstorm a skill name," but I think use of terms is interesting (obviously) and I think it's important to use terms in RPGs which are as close to actual usage in real life, the better to use natural language during in-play dialog and conversation, but also to speed comprehension of the system for new players (which will be everyone who sees these, as you are inventing the skills).

    Obviously, if you're bored with this topic, don't care about the exactitude of your terms, or haven't time... disregard--I wouldn't want to try to force a thread to stay alive if its OP wants it closed....
