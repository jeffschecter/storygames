\n So, yet again I start a discussion looking for advice on games for specific circumstances.
In this case, I'm looking for games that work well for a group of 3. 3 total people. If the game is one that has a GM of some kind, the GM is one of the 3.
3 seems (from my inexperienced eye) to be a somewhat problematic number. Most games seem to want about a minimum of 4. For example, I've been told that the Roach needs at least 4 to rock hard enough, so I'm putting it off for yet another week as it appears I'll be unable to gather 3 people this weekend.
So what should I do with my group of three? I got Agon recently and it looks like a lot of fun, but I'm guessing that 2 players might not be enough for the competitive aspect to be at its best? Similarly, I can't do Classroom Deathmatch (another recent purchase I'm quite excited about) because with myself as the Superintendent, there aren't enough players for each person to have a Best Friend and a Rival.

So I'm wondering... what games do people feel can bring their full (or at least very nearly full) level of potential Awesomeness with only 3 people?

    \n The difference between Agon and Shock:, procedurally, is very striking; in my play experience, if your group wants to have lots of brainstorming and kibbitzing and basically all working together on everyone's story, that's Shock:. It also takes quite a while to play out scenes. If you want to compete with each other and duke it out in a streamlined fast-paced action ride, though, that's Agon. So that's a big difference to consider, depending on what your group feels like doing at the time and how much time you've got on your hands.

    Posted By: egamadI think the key to running a traditional game with three players (GM + 2 PCs) is giving the PCs some kind of special relationship.
    \nI know we sort of instinctively look for this when we play. Recently Remi and I had to make up guys for a game of Afraid Clinton was running, and riffed off each other:

    "I want to be a monster."
    "How about my servant?"
    "Right. And I do what you say ... but I hate you."
    "Of course! Because I keep your soul in a jar around my neck."
    "But without me you're helpless...and I want my soul back."

    So we had a good reason to be together, had a strong relationship, and built-in tension right from the start.
