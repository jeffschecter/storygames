    When I really need to write over a long period, I break out my writing ritual.

    • Make a playlist for the subject matter, either played quite low or instrumentals only.
    • Write at the same time each day. Play the playlist while writing.
    • Choose a hot beverage and have it at my side while I'm writing; always the same beverage for that subject.
    • Always write something even if it's not something relevant.

    I find that when I keep a good dream journal, I write more fluidly, but I don't know what's cause and what's effect in that relation.

    This is like asking "how do you get rid of the hiccups?" You get a lot of answers, all of which are either untested annecdotes, or are known only to work for those who propose them. The fact that there are so many of these unreliable suggestions is because it's highly individual. What works for one doesn't work for another.

    But why not try them? Well, I think that what everyone can agree on is that writers block gets worse the more you dwell on the phenomenon itself. Trying and failing at suggestions is, I think, just more likely to make it harder to get past your block. On the other hand, not trying makes you feel like you're succumbing to the phenomenon, right? Makes you feel as if you'll never write again. Or at least not on this project.

    It seems to me that the issue here is not finding some trick to get inspired or something, but just getting your head on straight. That is, just use your will to kill off all of the negative thoughts like "What if I never write again?" It's the anxiety that keeps you from writing, and builds on itself. Straighten that out in your head, and realize that, as the Who would say:

    This is no social crisis
    it's just another tricky day for you

    Mike

    Here's three tricks that have always worked for me.

    1. Get out of your routine, as others have mentioned. Take a walk, work on something else, stand on your head, whatever it takes.

    2. Leave an unfinished sentence, then take a break. Coming back to a half-finished sentence makes your mind want to tidy it up, and once you do, you're writing again.

    3. Write without stopping, even if it's just whatever goes through your mind while you can't figure out what to write. Then go back later and take out the extraneous stuff. So you'll wind up with stuff like: "I'm simply providing.....ahh, fuck, what's the word....damn, i can't remember....oh, right.....an example." This gets cut down to "I'm simply providing an example."

    If those work for you, great.
