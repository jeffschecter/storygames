\n So, I was listening to the latest Canon Puncture show, where Arnold talked about doing something he calls "saga style" gaming at conventions. It kinda got me excited to try something like this at home.

To summarize perhaps too briefly, Arnold and his friend(s) run the same story through a series of game systems in a series of con slots. It's a really neat idea, but I think the strongest application of this idea isn't at a con. It seems best-suited to home-based, weekly games.

So, my idea (or my appliclation of Arnold's idea) is to chart the history of a new world, using a different game each week. Maybe start in early history with In a Wicked Age or Solar System. Maybe do something medieval or Renaissance with Burning Wheel or Chronica Fuedalis. Do an Old West type thing with Dogs in the Vineyard or Dust Devils. Maybe zoom into a relationship drama with Under My Skin, then start the sci-fi stuff with shock: to usher in th dystopia and explore it with Misspent Youth. Finally, you can see how the world ends with Apocalypse World, then do a historical miniseries event with Primetime Adventures.

I was figuring I'd pregen characters for most games, and try and run only one or two sessions per game system, and forsee the potential need for running them over two sessions.

How about you?

    \n I've thought about doing a serialized set of con games, mainly as a way to (hopefully) get folks to attend Sunday afternoon. I hadn't considered doing it with different systems, but that's a neat approach (considering that I like to run a lot of indie games per con, to spread the love).

    I (think I) saw a similar idea, but not chronologically serialized. Rather, there was an overarching story line, and the GMs used a different system for each set piece in the plot--Trail of Cthulhu for the investigation stage; DitV for the early confrontation with the factions; BW Fight for the Big Final Battle (for example). More what I'd call a "system mash-up" than a true serial with different systems in use based on chronology (i.e. RPG setting loosely matches the chronology time line).

    I might give this a shot at StellarCon and MACE!

    \n This is a fun idea. The closest we've come is switching systems mid-campaign when something wasn't clicking for us. I've gone Burning Wheel ==> TSOY, Sorcerer ==> PTA and, in one epic instance long ago, GURPS ==> FUDGE ==> Homebrew. This can't be that uncommon.

    I really like the idea of replaying the same events through different system-supported lenses. I think it'd be a fascinating way of seeing how the game mechanics really drive the fiction. It'd also give you a lot of precision in terms of tone and focus.

    \n I've a friend that's done just this with Shadowrun. He's snuck it in on some traditional gamers. They met some high power, and they've gone plane shifting, so they play a session of Puppetland en route, then go to a new game for a few weeks. Deadlands, Earthdawn, Marvel, even Maid for a session.
