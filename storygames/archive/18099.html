\n I played my first game of Twilight Imperium yesterday. It is a massive, somewhat flabby, but very immersive space opera game and a lot of the players clearly get off on the politics and roleplaying aspects of it.

I've had two thoughts about how you might enhance that further. The first was simply to have each player introduce an event or scene a la Microscope at the start of each round. You couldn't be as timey-wimey as you can with Microscope, but it would be a nice way of framing (and hopefully influencing) the activity within each round. Maybe have the speaker at the end of each round introduce a legacy?

The other would be to break off from the game completely at mid point and run a full Archipelago style game, with each player playing a senator representing one of the major races at a peace summit.

Needless to say, both elements would make an extremely long game even longer, but I guess if you're in for a penny you might as well lob in the full pound. And if you have a bank holiday weekend to spare...

Has anyone ever tried adding more story game elements to Twilight Imperium? I'd be interested to know how you got on.

    \n Further thought: you could of course use the game as the basis of a standard game of Archipelago of course - a rather more con-friendly game but one which would lack the full emersiveness of the longer option. I think I might try that option sooner rather than later.

    \n It's not quite as immersive, no, but there are some mechanical hooks upon which to attach narrative play. There's an alliance/betrayal mechanic with VP consequences, for instance. The way play progresses also encourages active table talk, too, particularly with players whose territory is not connected to yours. The one place where the RAW discourage such play is that even allies can't coexist peacefully in a hex, and I gather that a "rule zero solution" would not be as satisfying to you.
