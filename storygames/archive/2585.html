    \n Okay, so non Nintendo games, eh? Well, I'll second Elebits as something to check out. It's weird and cute and like an action shooter version of Katamari. It didn't hold my interest, but worth checking out.

    No More Heroes is excellent. A surreal ultra-violent action game where you take your custom floresent sword and fight your way to the top of the assassin heap in Santa Destroy, California. It's extremely bizarre and fun.

    Zack & Wiki - if you like puzzle adventure games - like you were a fan of the old Sierra adventure games, or just like puzzles in general, this is for you. Each level is a puzzle to figure out which things to use in order to get to a treasure chest. It's very cute and very Japanese style, and the puzzles ramp up to some extremely hard stuff.

    I'd be remiss if I didn't mention Destroy All Humans: Big Willy Unleashed, if you like dirty humor and especially bawdy puns - oh, and human destroying action with an anal probe gun, explosives, and a giant robot you can throw tanks with. I'm not going to say it's the cream of the crop, but since I worked on it, it's hard to judge it fairly.

    Posted By: Jason MorningstarWhat are good casual party type games? Anything to suggest there?
    \nWii Sports that comes with the system. Highly recommend getting WiiPlay, too. It's $50 like many games, but comes with a controller. So if you already wanted a second controller, that's $10 for some really cool mini games (including a redone Duck Hunt).

    I usually have a good time with Mario Party, but I hear the first Wii title one isn't all that great.

    Oh, I forgot about Super Monkey Ball, that's a good fun one for parties.

    I'll have to agree with Remi that Super Smash Bros Brawl is a great party game. It's not super casual, so if mom and Aunt Debbie are there, Monkey Ball or WiiPlay would probably appeal more. But if you have a bunch of friends over, Brawl's crazy madness lets even novice players hold their own pretty well, it's just too much chaos to hold your own for long. Plus there's party and tournament modes to set up having 16 (or more?) people rotate in and out of playing.

    Oh wait, WarioWare for the Wii is what you want. Excellent party game. All the insanity of Brawl (times 10), with the accessibility of Mario Party, plus you are suppose to stand up and do poses, so everyone can laugh at each other as they take their turns.
