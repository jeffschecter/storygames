    Since you brought it up, though, here's what I'm looking at. I've got two potential chairpeople. Both awesome, both high class contributors.

    Both have the experience needed to make the contest awesome, and both have the technical resources to do the thing in their vision as well. It's really a grueling decision.

    But, between the candidates, there's an interesting split.

    Candidate A has quite revolutionary ideas with how to run the contest from this point forward, with a firm eye on personal effort, emphasis on completion and play, and a goal of project sustainability (with the growing number of entrants, etc). Awesome.

    Candidate B has excellent ideas, and they are all about taking what the contest is now, and tightening it up, making it more streamlined, slick, fun, easy-to-handle, with a firm eye on community, and a goal of expansion and inclusion. Awesome.

    Although it's like trying to decide between delicious French Vanilla Bean and delicious Dutch Milk Chocolate ice cream (fucking hard, because they're both so awesome), I've pretty much decided already, I just need a day to get my thoughts together, contact those candidates, and let them know what's up. Then make announcements here, on the Forge Thread, etc.

    At the very latest, I'm seeing Thursday afternoon. Most likely tomorrow if I can squeeze a few minutes.

    \n Thanks, cats. Gimme a few weeks to finish up a couple things (Murderland), confer with Candidate B, and work with Andy and some other folks to get the basic internet architecture in place. A few things I do know right-off, though...

    -- If you complete your game, you win Game Chef 2009 (NaNoWriMo style). Period. (Yes, even if it's late. Yes, even if you break the rules.)
    -- There will also be additional "medals / commendations" your game can earn by completing other requirements. (Ones I'm fairly sure of: finish your game in a week, finish in two weeks, playtesting/play report AP posts, follow the rules exactly, use additional ingredients, etc.).
    -- Earning certain medals might "unlock" other medals. But that might be too complicated. We'll see.
    -- No judging. Completion and medals take its place, along with a post-completion self-evaluation and the responses you get from fellow participants and the folks who are interested in your game once it's done. Your work is judged by yourself and by everyone else who reads it anyway, so I think that's enough.
    -- Game Chef as an organizing entity will not be responsible for the social environment or overall experience of participants. We will set up a number of structures that make it easy for participants to communicate with each other if they choose to, but ultimately the social experience of working alongside other designers is up to the participants. You can participate in the social aspects of Game Chef as much or as little as you like. You are responsible for your own fun!
    -- As games are submitted, there will be a master list of winners, coded by their medals/commendations, so you can easily browse for games to play.
    -- Yes there will be a theme and ingredients. But I don't know what those are yet and wouldn't tell you even if I knew.

    \n Matt, I'll think about the "Marvel Team-Up" badge. You're right about that.

    Ashok, honestly, I think "artist first" was a great structure/theme, but I don't want to reuse old themes. This year will have it's own theme and the badges system is a unique structure for this year that may or may not return in years following. Like, maybe next year we'll all make boardgames or something. But one thing I definitely take away from Andy's last few years is that switching things up slightly year-to-year is a net positive, as long as those changes don't impede the core conceit of Game Chef.

    Ben, I'm of two minds about it, I guess. I mean, in a lot of ways, I think it's a bit odd that Game Chef has been institutionalized as this THING, yeah? Originally, it happened this way because Ron said we could only run a design contest once a year on the Forge, to prevent it from distracting from other things (what other things, I'm not sure). But things have clearly changed a lot since then, clearly, with GC leaving the Forge and Ron starting the Ronnies himself and smaller contests including Graham's Lil' Game Chef last year. I'm definitely excited that there are a variety of other contests out there, using a variety of different models. I was hoping to spend this weekend working on a short fantasy game for Jake's current contest, but instead spent it hacking together the new Game Chef website.

    That said, it's hard to deny the power of Game Chef as an annual tradition, since this is the 9th year running if you count Jared and Clinton's 2001 contest on the Gaming Outpost that inspired Mike Holmes. The classic "theme + four ingredients" format is well established now, even if it's been tweaked a bit in recent years, and remains unique to Game Chef, since other contests typically go with some other format. So I think I would like to preserve the annual GC experience, but I'm definitely open to discussing ways to cross-brand with other contests without stretching things too thin, whether or not anything necessarily comes of it.

    What are you thinking of, specifically? An "in cooperation with Game Chef" logo ala d20 or something? Seems like GC might not want to sponsor multiple contests at the same time or contests happening at the same time as the annual GC, but that might take more administering than I necessarily want to do. I don't want to have to, for example, evaluate applications to determine who can run the "Game Chef approved" contest for the month or whatever. That's bullshit. Additionally, I feel like contests, if they are attractive and well-designed, can get plenty of attention and participation without sharing GC branding. For example, my Murderland contest last year generated 36 games, which is an incredible turnout that I have not yet fully recovered from. But I'd like to hear more about your thoughts.

    \n Sure, Ryan, but there's two different things there:

    1) Helping with Game Chef.
    2) Running your own contest.

    I'm happy for folks to do either or both, but it really depends on what their own goals are and what works best for the contest ideas they have in mind.

    My chief responsibility, as I see it, is to make sure the annual Game Chef happens once a year and is as valuable and enjoyable for the participants as I can make it without driving myself insane. I just can't commit myself to helping administer more than one contest a year or overseeing some larger organization of any kind. Whatever people want to do, whatever they want to call it (aside from "Game Chef YEAR"), that's totally cool. If Ben eventually decides to run "Game Chef: Artist First 2," that's great. I'll link to it, I'll make announcements, I'll lend any resources I'm not using (the forums), etc. But that'll be ultimately up to him to carry out.
