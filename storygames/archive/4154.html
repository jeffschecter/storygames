If you're a game designer and you're sick and tired about the way The Media stereotypes you...

\n
\n
\n
\n\nT-Boy \n\n\n
\n
\n\nSeptember 2007 \n edited September 2007 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n (sudden epiphany from this thread. It's Mark's fault.)

...specifically, the way The Visual Medias seem to stereotype you as a lonesome, asocial grognard who games to escape from your pathetic existence, it may be time for you to adopt a rockstar lifestyle.

My initial models to this idea were initially the Bitmap Brothers, Peter Molyneux, John Romero and all them "rockstar" game developers, who managed to drag videogames from the province of lonely boys with acne and no chicks to the multi-billion dollar a year depravity hot coffee festival that it is today, but then I realized... holy shit, those guys are tame. I mean, take a look at Neil Gaiman. Or, heck, Warren Ellis. Hell, Alan Moore. Or, Great Dancing Nether Hells, Grant Morrisson. Now those guys are rock stars.

Anyway, for this idea to be practical, you may need to be and do the following:\n

  1. You need to be thin and saturnine, or at least, very, very reclusive.

    Seriously, no one can take you seriously if you're this hearty, big-boned dude with a huge laugh and a jolly expression. I mean, look at the previous guys I mentioned; the only person who doesn't fit into the stereotype of a rock star, really, is John Romero, and he was buff and good-looking enough to marry a supermodel. And even then he bombed miserably.

    So, skinny is the way to go. If you're not? Then it's time to go the Dave Sims route. Wear dark glasses. Wear dark clothing. Grant sporadic interviews. Promulgate really stupid and offensive ideas using obscure, batshit crazy language. Be very, very good. I mean, really breath-takingly good.

  2. Get a new wardrobe.

    Time to ditch those geek clothes, kids. If you're gonna live the rockstar life, you need the rockstar look.

    Start by ditching anything vaguely sloppy-looking, unless they're dark-colored or are made of denim. Make sure those clothes are in good condition, and for god's sake make sure they fit. Maybe consider wearing makeup, unless your skin reacts badly to foundation. Wear funny jewelry.

    If you think that all of that's pretty damn weird, then go the Peter Molyneux route and invest in some good business casual suits. And either way, improve on the grooming. Oh, and stop eating those damn cheetos.

  3. Engage in the Rockstar Lifestyle

    You know what I mean. Go to parties. Hang out with the pretty ladies, or even better, especially if you're thin and androgynous-looking, hang out with the pretty guys. Take illegal substances. Become a worshiper of Glaucon. Break stuff. Show the world that you do more than just sit there and play games; heck, give the impression that games get in the way of your rockin' lifestyle! You're hip and happening, man! You just game because you are compelled (if you've decided to take the Dave Sims route, this is the best part for you to start yammering about the whole mystical mumbo-jumbo batshit insane stuff that you've impressed to your horrified adoring public).

  4. Get Interviewed by The Media

    Chances are, if you've done things right, the next time you go to GenCon and Slate or Rolling Stone decides to interview you, instead of that frumpy lady with the baseball cat, or Sailor Dave, maybe they'll start interviewing you as well.

    Remember, do not disappoint them. Play your role to the hilt! And look good while doing it.

\nWhaddya think, eh? Eh?

    \n True story:

    I'm in Moscow at this video game conference. I'm at this party, there's booze and strippers and all kinds of chaos. Then somebody busts in and says: "Hey, the guys from Studio X (not the real name of the development studio) are at the hotel." Turns out the guys from Studio X were the talk of the town, because they had just released this major hit.

    So the story continued: "They walked in like they owned the place, and went straight to the front desk. The lead designer slapped a brick of rubles on the counter and said, 'We're going to the lounge on the second floor. I want you to take this money and buy us some hash. Then bring it up to us. We're going to smoke it.' The woman looked at him and said, 'But sir, that's illegal.' He just shrugged. So she took the money and went and bought him some hash, and she brought it up to the second floor, where the game developers were all drinking whiskey. She gave them the hash. They're smoking it right now!"

    Later, we all head to the hotel to see what's up. The dudes from Studio X are just wasted, all except this one cat, their lead designer. This guy, he's just standing there. He's lean (most of the foreign game developers I've met are pretty fit, compared to their American counterparts), got himself a white wifebeater and camo fatigues and a pair of combat boots. He's got long hair -- not scraggly-nerd long hair, but nice long hair. Pantene Pro-V long hair. Garnier Fructis long hair. Guy crosses his arms and looks at us like, who the fuck are you?

    One of these Russian guys is like, hey, so-and-so, this is Rafael, the American (that's how everyone referred to me, like I was the only one). Guy looks at me, gives me the nod, says hey. So I say, how you doing? He waits a minute, then he says, I'm cool. Like, he meant it. He knew that he was one of the coolest people to ever walk the earth. That guy was so awesome.

    But then I mentioned that some of the guys at Sony were playing his game, and the whole rock-star-game-designer thing just evaporated. He was all, like, what, fucking Sony? Really? That's awesome! Do they like it? Then he was all excited and enthusiastic.

    Anyhow, that was one of the only times I ever saw someone pull off the rock-star-game-designer thing in person.
