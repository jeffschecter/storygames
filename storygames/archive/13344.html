\n If you are familiar with Frederik Jensen's excellent game, you know that the English edition requires a bit of arts-and-crafts handling time before you can play. The Italian edition comes ready to play, and includes a bunch of beautiful and clever components all packaged simply and effectively.

Here's me unboxing Narrativa's gorgeous edition.

The "board", in case you can't tell, is printed on a leathery fabric that folds up. I'm just in awe of the quality and presentation.

    \n Wow, thank you all for your interest and the kind comments!

    To all the people asking about a english-italian version of Montsegur: know that Frederik, Jason, Graham and us are in touch and pondering the matter. We'll see what will come of this.

    @ hans chung-otterson:
    Narrattiva is a part of Shadow, a company owned by Michele Gelli which, incidentally, is also my husband and usual partner in crime. Both of us are long time gamers and we have a past in publishing and still love it with all our heart. We have loads of fun trying to always come up with new ideas specifically suited for each game.

    Basically we try to package every game with everything that you need to play (we still haven't managed it for games requiring dice, and I feel a little uneasy each time one is released just as a manual ^__^).
    This is because we feel our hobby has been too long an excessively exclusive club were life was hard for newcomers. We feel the need to make it a more welcoming scene and this is reflected also in the selection of games we release.

    I could go on for hours explaining our philosophies but don't wish to bore you all to death XD
