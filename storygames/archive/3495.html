\n Hello all,

I've posted before, you probably know me to be a TSoY fan and know my urge to fiddle with rules. This post is inspired by few things. First and foremost, the Hugely neat thread Help Me Try Out this Setting Construction Engine. Second is the idea that says "setting is in the rules". I don't know where this originates, but I'm fascinated by it.

Why I like it? Probably because of general (and personal) complaint that you have huge amounts of games that have interesting setting and premise but then the play turns out to be something completely different. If the setting is laid "between the lines" in the mechanics this surely can't happen?

Might I then tap your creative energies and excellent ideas to try this out? Feel free to say "Boo! Not smart!" or "Doesn't belong here!" if you feel like it, but, here goes.

So, let's create yet again human-centric fantasy with scheming nobles, powerful people and cruelty and hope. In bit style of Martin or even Hobb. The means to do this are the following:\n
    \n
  1. Add a Goal to this thread. For example: "I want to see rules for the Kings Rangers!"
  2. Quote previous Goal or bit of mechanics and add relevant Key, which brings out the bit of setting according to TSoY rules.
  3. Quote previous Goal or bit of mechanics and add relevant Secret, which brings out the bit of setting. Mainly follow the TSoY guidelines, but you can make things that introduce other pools and other weird stuff, if you can fit the description in a paragraph.
  4. Add a set of skills (4) to represent some group of people.
  5. Awesomize a written key, secret or skill.
    6. \n
\nSo, no fluff-text except a line or two in descriptions of keys, skills and secrets. Try to wait other replies (If there is any) before posting another, though each post could contain several items.

    \nSecret of House Allesandro
    You are member of House Allesandro, know for it's riches. Gain one specialization bonus die for Swaying people by Bribes.

    Key of Usurpation
    You know it is you who should rule! It is your right not by Birth but by Divine!
    1 XP For scheming against those who govern.
    3 XP For acting out against the ruler and thus placing yourself or your resources in danger!
    Buyoff: Rule! Or lay of your plans!

    Key of Family Feud
    For reasons long gone your family has been feuding with another House of Power for a long time. You compete for resources, places of court and kingly favour. But you'd really destroy them out of hatred alone, given chance.
    1 XP For spreading your side of the story, cursing the others and generally making this feud know.
    2 XP Competing with members of Rival House without permanent resolution
    5 XP Permanently defeating a member of the Rival House (inprisonment, death, banishment)
    Buyoff: Rule! Or lay of your plans!


    So, Give me More Houses and Kingly Court.
