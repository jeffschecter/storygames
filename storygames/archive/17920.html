\n What RPGs are really good at making you change your character?

You can change your Beliefs in BW (and you have lot's of mechanical advantages for doing so) but nothing really makes you do this besides a few skills and traits. Trait votes do that though.
You can change your Aspects in FATE at Minor Milestones but you don't have to.
Apocalypse World kinda does this because at some point you have no more advances and have to either change to a new Playbook or Retire.
Mouse Guard has a winter session and Nature 0/7 rules.
Lady Blackbird has Keys but i don't know enough about them and how they interact.
Freemarket can change your whole Memories so i guess this is cool if you really engage in it (do you have to?)

Marvel Heroic is actually pretty good at it because 10XP Milestone Caps force you to really change your character and move him into a new direction. This actually is more fictional change then mechanical as you can buy stuff with XP that has nothing to do with your character.

    \n Misspent Youth - When push comes to shove, Sell Out or Fail, it's a one way transaction.

    Little Fears - Lose Innocence (and grow up) to push back against the darkness. It's a one way transaction.

    My Life With Master - Gain enough Love (self-respect) to break out of the abusive co-dependent cycle, or ... ?

    Lady Blackbird (and The Shadow of Yesterday / The World of Near) - gain material advantage by playing keys, or larger advantage by buying them off and growing out of that behavior / mindset.

    Kagematsu - is a brilliant game of compromise, and ever changing approaches to a desperate and urgent problem.

    The Mountain Witch - bring an idea of the character you want (or want to discover in play) but be prepared to adapt and change as the foreshadowing of other players fills on details you didn't know about.
