    \n This is just nebulous speculation and it might make John regret showing anything at all, but:
    -the trait dice seem to be gone
    -it seems to me it's all d6s now (add +1D)
    -there's no "spend a Power token to..." power
    -the danger meter is missing (but since that was one of the best things about DP I'm betting it's still in, even if in a different or simplified form)
    -the traits seem to be completely reorganized/grouped under four categories (action/movement, battle/attacks, investigation and science/research/gear)

    So I'm guessing here's how it works now:
    You get one die by default, probably, and then you add dice from your traits that apply based on what you're doing (action,battle,investigation, science), just like Lady Blackbird. Looking at the cards that's probably going to give you 1-4 d6 to roll. And then you add the danger dice on top of that just like you used to, simple, really. So there's no more "trait + special ability + gear & weapons" base roll to which you add the danger dice (all modified by talents & powers of course), just trait dice (LB style) + danger dice. What this apparently changes is that Danger ramps up much faster, since each die has a 50% chance of increasing your danger (unlike before where you were likely rolling d8s and d10s in the mix which have a lower chance of being danger results). Oh and since Traits were the same as roles anyway you probably maybe get an extra die for having the right role for the task.

    But of course I could be totally off.

    Posted By: joela
    I haven't played DP (yet) but have played a LB hack. Would you be willing to post a quick example of your explanation above?
    \nIt's just like in LB (or Operation: Blackbird, rather), but take away the pool dice (the seven dice you can add in any quantity to a roll, but which you lose when they score successes). In their place, you have danger dice. Each dangerous detail you introduce gives you a danger die. You can give yourself up to three of these, plus the GM can give you another and your fellow players can give you yet another.

    So you start with 1d6 and can add additional d6s from tagging your traits (as seen in the new style/role cards above), plus you can finagle up to five danger dice (also d6s). All dice succeed on a 4-6. Trait dice up your danger on a 1. Danger dice up your danger on a 1-3.

    (FWIW, I like a lot of things about this change, but I think, knowing what little I know right now, that I favor the Beta. I'm partial to roles themselves having die ratings, and the idea that adding bonus dice yields diminishing returns. Of course, I don't know how the danger meter works, or how the danger meter makes characters more awesome as it increases -- I like the idea of it passively "bringing out" new abilities in roles and styles instead of spending whammy points on them -- so I hardly have a full picture here.)
