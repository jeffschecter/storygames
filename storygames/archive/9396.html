    \n Plebian is definitely not an appropriate substitute for peasant. Plebian simply meant Citizen (i.e. not a slave) who was not a Patrician (i.e. Aristocracy). That encompassed everyone from the poorest of the poor to some of the wealthiest families in the Republic. In fact, several of the pre-Republic kings were Plebians. And post Republic (i.e. during the Empire) many of the families that rose to prominence were Plebes as the influence of the old senatorial Patricians faded. While the Plebian class encompassed persons who could be described as "peasants" it also encompassed the urban poor (who would never be referred as peasants) as well as the middle professional and mercantile classes (similar to the bourgeosie). It is also true that many of the professional class were actually themselves slaves.
    \n
    \nSlaves were a very different animal in the truly ancient pre-Greco-Roman civilizations. The nature of the God-Kings of Bablyon, Sumeria, Egypt meant that pretty much ALL of the people existed in a state that we today would concider slavery. In egypt you worked your fields and the Pharoahs men came and took ALL of your produce and then rationed it back to you. Then, when your fields were flooded you were rounded up and shipped off to labor in any of the major construction projects of the day (of which the Pyramids are only the most famous).
