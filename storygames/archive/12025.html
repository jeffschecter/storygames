\n This will make you feel smart:

http://opinionator.blogs.nytimes.com/2010/04/25/chances-are/

It's a New York TImes opinion piece, about calculating probability.

Apparently, it's hard for some folks to visualize conditional probabilities.
But not you, my friend! Because you own funny-looking dice.

So you'll be able to imagine a d100 with 30 black sides and 27 of those with a skull-for-a-pip. And 70 white sides with 14 skull-faces. Quick: how many skulls is that?

Or however you crunch it. You'll do fine.


Until you get to the author's wrong answer in the footnotes.
Oh, and the screwy wording of the story problems is rules-lawyer bait.
And then there's all the Errata offered by readers in the Comments section.

Doesn't the Times playtest this crap?

Or maybe the author broke all the math and wording on purpose, to slyly underscore his overall point?
