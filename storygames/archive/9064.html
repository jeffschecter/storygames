    \n I'm going to follow the other list and choose a specific genre of game. My genre is historical.

    The Top Five Historical RPGs (in historical order)

    1. The Minoan Labyrinth (Ancient Greece, the original dungeon crawl!)
    2. War Reenactment (Ancient Rome, flood the Colosseum for naval battles!)
    3. Ars Memorativa (Pre-Renaissance Europe, the first dungeons in your minds!)
    4. Tlacaxipehualiztli (Aztecs, where you pretend to be someone else by wearing their skin!)
    5. Braunstein (1967)

    \n ok , ferserious.

    games that have made my life better and are therefore like the best games EVAR, by jackson tegu, aged 28:

    Ribbon Drive, by Joe. (this game has not failed me, and i have played it many times, and sometimes with folks i wasn't stoked on initially- you know, everyone has always risen to the occasion. and, especially more recently, i have made friends though playing this game. it always feels like sharing.)

    Anima Prime, by Christian.

    Best Friends, by Gregor.

    In A Wicked Age, by Vincent.

    Don't Rest Your Head, by Fred. i like how he just brings it down to a really simple level, but still allows a ton of freedom for the players to imagine their characters.
