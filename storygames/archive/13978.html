\n I'm designing a rpg with scene-framing mechanics. I'm considering allowing the antagonist player (there's only one) a benefit that would allow them to end a scene at their choosing. The next scene would then be framed as per the normal rules. They would have to earn the ability and after they spent it, would have to earn it again. And, it's not easy to get this benefit.

What kind of stories, scenes, or moments do you think this would provide? How do you think it would be meta-gamed? What would that do to the story? Would this cut down on the fun? Is this just a mechanic for the sake of being a mechanic?

    \n I suppose it would be significant if ending the current scene meant that:

    * The focus of the story changed. For instance, if it meant a different character then became the focus of the next scene.

    * What if it meant that we could never directly know how the current scene turned out? "Fade to black" would be the idea here.

    That kind of thing would be an interesting way to model a character who has blackouts, amnesia, or is occasionally possessed and doesn't remember what happened.
