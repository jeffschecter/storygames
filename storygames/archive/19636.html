\n Hi all,

Yesterday, I created an NPC character traits generator, that should work well on all devices:

http://scottmaclure.github.io/character-traits/

The reason for this was to allow me to breathe some life into random NPC's which I generate on the fly (I use a laptop when GMing).

Feedback welcome! I have a few ideas for enhancement, which I'm tracking on github:

https://github.com/ScottMaclure/character-traits/issues

Thanks for your time, apologies in advance if this is not the correct category for this kind of thing.

    \n The utility of this is really great if it's so easy to access on a mobile device.

    This is a personal preference thing, but your traits seem heavily focused on descriptive aspects of a character. I've found that I really only need 3 to make a random character stand out in someone's imagination, but then I want something juicier to make the character really have fun potential for players to latch onto, like: motive, desire, agenda, organization, role, belief (about an important NPC or player or organization either belongs to)

    Since this is a generic generator, roles would be generic but that's easy. Examples: holy person, authority figure, lowest class/caste, law enforcement, child, etc. Good luck with it. I guess organizations would be too: criminal syndicate, political party, gang, club, royal house, religious order, etc

    \n I really like how well your generator creates basic personal description. Tall with a beard and a lisp? Ok. That gives me a really clear image of how this person appears, what they look like, how to speak like them.

    However, I find the most important and useful aspect of an NPC generator is not physical description (which is ultimately unimportant, and can be left out altogether without harming the game or story, as many famous authors have demonstrated). It's the role, the function, the archetype which the NPC fulfills.

    What is their status in society? How are they perceived? What is their driving force? Etc.
