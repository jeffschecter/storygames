    I'm with Ben. I think this is insane in the same way that Henry Darger was. That is, we are blessed with a glimpse into someone else' mind that is not like our own, untarnished by attempts of clarity.

    I have a whole section of my bookshelf devoted to this. I have a book from the Moonies claiming to be a series of essays written by every single dead President of the US announcing the one true way. I have a book entitled simply "G" that is entirely in code (it's a Mason thing). I have a copy of the Voynich Manuscript and a book claiming that George Lucas was talking about Jesus Christ when he said "The Force".

    Communication is very rarely solely about what the author wants to say; a lot of it is "I'm fine, how are you?" social stuff. Which is great; it keeps us friends and lets us know who has to be put on a donkey backward and sent into the desert. But things like HYBRID or The Story of the Vivian Girls are pure expressions of what's going on with someone else. Mockery's inappropriate only because the viewer really has a hard time seeing the scale of the vision; it shares too little with the viewer's experience.

    This is the closest we come to seeing the mysteries of the human mind. It's so hard to understand what someone else means because it's so hard to say it. This guy is saying it; the fact that it doesn't mean anything to you isn't his concern.

    It is beauty as honest as that of a beehive or a hurricane: it is complex and uncaring for you but its beauty can be seen, if not understood.
