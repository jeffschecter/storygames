\n Hello to all of you and thanks in advance for your time!!

You might not remember my project, ExiStanc3, but we were supposed to KickStarter it last autumn. As we weren't able to get all the numbers right, we decided to push our campaign toward this spring (somewhere in march). Well, it turned out to be a truly good decision. I have read recently about KickStarter campaign: when you think you are ready, add three months. So, that's where I stand. I guess I could start a campaign in January (my KickStarter page is almost done), but we will take some time to build an audience and thus, increase our chance at having a better success in all this adventure.

A little bit of context: ExiStanc3 is about three things: a system, Shadow and Light, a campaign, Avalanche and a website that allows to manage epic campaigns such as Avalanche. We've put a lot of thoughts into this about the KS campaign. Should we do one or more campaigns? What should we do first? Maybe Shadow and Light, as a small campaign? A print run of Avalanche? Just the website?
Finally we've decided to present what makes us unique and distinctive: Avalanche and the application (after all, Shadow and Light is yet another innovative system).
A quick apart\xe9: recently, we've seen a lot of applications/websites to help manage "adventures". They all have two things in common: they provide tools for the current products being sold AND they do not propose their own adventure. What makes us different: we propose a new type of product and the adventure itself.

So, we have three months to promote, sell our campaign, get people excited about this. We're going to start in January, but I'm interested in hearing from this community first. So, what do we have to propose/use as material?

What's next?
A couple of things:
- We do have a strategy to be "compatible" with other systems. Mainly, Shadow of Yesterday, Savage World, Pathfinder, 13th Age, Burning wheel, FATE. Our strategy is to contact those creators/owners and commission them to produce pdf/rules/character sheets to be compatible with Avalanche. Given that, we will be targeting those communities. Just to mention, Eero is already on board for Shadow of Yesterday.
- We will be targeting main forums of players.
- We will be looking for reviewers and/or alpha testers. We'll have in two weeks the complete Avalanche pdf in the format shown in teaser 3. Our guess is that if you like it and play it, there are two things you would want for sure: a printed copy and a website (to help you with the massive amount of information)!!
- We will be looking for bloggers; media in general. Our KS campaign will be a bit different from the others.

One last thing: I'm really, but really glad with the material I'm proposing here. That said, I come from a very long way and I strongly believe this would not have been possible without the people of the forge and you guys in this community. So, thanks a lot.
