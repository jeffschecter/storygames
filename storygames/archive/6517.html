    \nThe Sons of Kryos: Awesome, definitely
    They are probably the best. Actual play problems discussed in a friendly and clever way.


    But there are many others (I'm not linking everyone, at least not now, but yeah, google is your friend)

    Have Games, Will Travel (2007 Ennie Award winning show ) Paul Tevis is a pleasure to listen to.

    2d6Feet in a Random Direction: the gamer and the game shop owner perspective, in one package

    Theory from the closet: Clyde does some good interviews

    Independent Insurgency: ..and yeah, Rob does, too.

    Stabbing Contest - Yeah Ogre, you too! (he's a big bouncer guy, do you wanna make him angry? really??) ;-)

    The Voice of the Revolution: Paul Tevis again, with Brennan Taylor of IPR. The official IPR podcast: new products, reviews and interviews

    The Game Master Show: lots of actual plays, and a good balance of player styles.

    Secret life of girl gamers and Geek Girls Rule: two "gaming chicks" shows. Fun to listen, and a perspective slightly different from the usual gamer guy.

    The Master Plan: Ryan Macklin interviews designers, and plots his masterplan as well.

    That's How We Roll: Fred Hick's and EvilHat's official podcast. Fun and interesting, especially if you like Fate, Spirit of the Century, or another of the EH games.

    Oh, and The Durham 3, even if recently they have been (uncharateristically) silent.

    I think there's a lot to chew with your ears, here (eeew!)

    \n Here are some hidden gems, for sure. Not at all about gaming, but it's nice to take a break and learn something about the worlds we tend to play in (or play in the shadows and reflections of):

    Colin Renfrew provides a series of lectures at UCLA, covering archaeology, specifically European prehistory, and a few other fascinating topics.

    Lars Brownworth details Byzantine history through twelve different Emperors. Fascinating stuff.

    Isabelle Pafford talks about the Ancient Mediterranean World at UC Berkeley. I'm only a few lectures in, but it's a great introduction.

    And lastly, not a hidden gem but rather a shining beacon of scintillant awesome: Sons of Kryos.

    \nThe Sound of Young America and its sister-show Jordan, Jesse Go! are not about gaming (well, sometimes videogames come up on JJGo), however, they are incredibly well-produced paragons of the "interview" and "two guys talking about stuff" podcast formats, and really ought to be on your regular playlist.

    Also, they are funny and insightful.

    The 1Up Show is a weekly video podcast that covers videogames, and was originally co-created by one of my friends (she still sings the theme song), and I find it superior to any other videogame program/podcast on or off the internet.

    Other people have already called out Theory From the Closet, but I just want to say that in my opinion, Clyde is the best interviewer in gaming, whether you're talking about indie rpgs, or PS3s. In my dreams, Clyde has a day job interviewing videogame designers for the 1up show. The dude has a face for TV.

    \nWe met at the Gen Con podcaster meet and greet, Ben? Anyway. I have a dream where I make my livelyhood from interviewing people also. In mine-- I'm my employer. I'm still trying to figure out how to do that.
    \n
    \nFor RPG stuff I listen to quite a lot of shows, most everything that is on Charle's Thursday list, but I look forward to Master Plan, Have Games Will Enunciate, Sons of Kyros, Independent Insurgency, Stabbing Contest, Fear the Boot, and the Digital Front. I also think that Canon Puncture, The Gamemaster Show, This Is How We Roll, have some really good episodes. I don't listen to the actual play episodes much as actual play podcasts bore me to death. I'm sure I've forgotten a podcast somewhere.
    \n
    \nFor tech I listen to much of the Twit network stuff, The Linux Action Show, and Lug radio. For video games I only listen to Medispective, which can be offensive at times, but most times is just kind of bitter and grouchy. Unless you count Downloadable Content as a video game show. Most other video games shows I've listened to sound like they're trying to be drive time radio bullshit. I hate that sound with a passion, fake laughing, fake personalities... makes me want to punch people in the face, with some kind of painful metaphor.
    \n
    \nI also like Escape Pod, Skepticality, and iTune U which has podcasts of college classes. I'm obviously missing stuff, but I'm just under 70 podcasts right now.

    \n Favorites right now:


    Jankcast
