    \n Saw all three of them and enjoyed all of them.

    The one that really sticks with me is The Adjustment Bureau, though. The reviewers really didn't want to look any deeper into it than the romance. However, if you flip the story and begin looking at it from the perspective of the Bureau, it becomes another story. They aren't Bad Guys. They don't want to be Bad Guys. However, they have been saddled with the responsibility to keep humankind progressing along The Plan. The Plan insures that humankind wont' destroy itself or end up in another Dark Age. So, when key players in the game start to go Off-Plan, its their job to make adjustments so that things don't spiral out of control. Forgot your car keys? The Bureau may have lost them for you so that you miss that car crash that would have killed you.

    However, the Bureau has its limits. They aren't human. They have trouble with empathy or improvising. A lack of curiousity is also a problem in that almost all of them are quite willing to do their jobs without asking any questions. Allow an innocent man to die so things stay On Plan? No problem. Know why he had to die? As one of the agents said, "Its above my pay grade."

    They aren't automotons, and they are overall doing a Good Work, but their thought processes are disturbingly "corporate."

    I'm gonna try doing the spoiler text thing. Not huge spoilery, but just in case someone didn't want to know. Highlight the following text block:
    =====
    The "agents" in the movie come clean pretty early on that they are in fact Angels, working for a Judeo-christian God. I don't mind theist elements in movies, but that element kinda made me think "Well that's kind of an easy cop-out". I was /so/ much more hoping for anything else: Humans, time travellers, a magical cult, heck even *aliens*. But saying "Angels" took a lot of cool possible motive speculation and "Unknown Armies-ness" of the setup and tosses it right out. Not horrible, just kind of a waste of potential story.
    =====

    I have not seen Source Code, but it sounds cool.

    Limitless I found to be Awesome. But I have a soft spot* for Bradley Cooper. Someone proposed it was the contemporary equivalent of Flowers for Algernon. I love FfA and stories in that vein, but this is not that. It's basically kind of a low power superhero movie of sorts, with more scenes of "awesome" than "moral ambiguity". But I was thoroughly entertained, and there were some pretty cool twists.
