\n Hi Folks,

I'm working on a book review that has me thinking about references to D&D in recent art and literature.

Specifically, I was thinking about Tony Dowler's recent Year of the Dungeon show, and the Doomslangers exhibition and Dungeons and Dragons in Contemporary Art panel discussion.

What am I missing outside of these? I'd love to know.

Also there was that short story, "The Dungeon Master," by Sam Lipsyte in The New Yorker in October. Anything else like this out there?

I feel like there's been a bit of an increase in interest in D&D (and/or role-playing games, but esp. D&D) in artistic, popular, and scholarly venues lately. Am I imagining this? Here I'm also thinking of "popular" books like Barrowcliffe's (unfortunate) The Elfish Gene and Gilsdorf's (haven't read it) Fantasy Freaks and Gaming Geeks: An Epic Quest for Reality among Role Players, Online Gamers, and Other Dwellers of Imaginary Realms. This is in addition to more scholarly works like the disappointing First Person, Second Person, and Third Person collections.

I know there's a thread around here cataloging more scholarly works. I suppose for this thread, though I've strayed from it myself, I'm most interested in creative works that allude to or engage with D&D (and/or RPGs) in some way.

What else is out there?

    \n I don't know if any of this counts but Adventure Time is sometimes blatantly inspired by D&D/Gamma World, including references to alignment, spells and many oldschool dungeon tropes (such as folding carnivorous floors and mimics).

    There have been offhand blink-and-you-miss references in some comedy shows like 30 Rock and Peep Show. (Possibly The Big Bang Theory? I don't watch it.) The IT Crowd was more in-depth, more than half the episode revolved around D&D with serial numbers filled off. There has been a D&D episode of Community announced.

    There's also The Brief Wondrous Life of Oscar Wao (which I believe has references to roleplaying). Wil Wheaton talks about his childhood and roleplaying in The Happiest Days of Our Lives.

    \nThe Kobold Wizard's Dildo of Enlightenment +2 is one that springs readily to mind. It's the story of a bunch of pubescent nerds who attempt to make their D&D characters do a variety of depraved things and what happens when the characters start becoming aware of what they are and who's controlling them. Puerile, yeah, but funny and interestingly meta.
