    \n At Metatopia I participated as a player.

    I filled out a questionnaire including questions about:

    - experience roleplaying
    - interest in different games
    - interest in different genres
    - interest in different game mechanics
    - interest in different game tones (serious, humor, horror...)
    - comfort level with mature content

    And then Vinny matched me to games that fit my answers. I believe the game designers explicitly submitted what kind of players they want.

    \n Yeah, as Alex said, I participated and got a lot out of it - definitely worth the price for the six sessions I ran. (Speaking of the price, while it was $150, that also got you two GM badges, and previously purchased general admission badges were refunded.)

    Since I have two games in the pipeline to come out in the near future, playtesting is valuable to me for getting to fine-tune things, but I also took advantage of the marketing and focus group aspect of the track, just as I did with Metatopia last year for House of Cards. As I understand it, Vinny has said that he wants to create a year-long development track across multiple shows: First Exposure would fit into that as the "sneak peek" portion, heavy on workshopping. It would be preceded by some as-yet nebulous events, and then capped by Metatopia as the final dry run and debut event. I'd spin that yearly cycle around, personally, since the size of the audience at Gen Con makes it the most appropriate for "initial public offering" of games that have already spent a year building buzz.

    But, yeah: I'm 100% behind the idea.
