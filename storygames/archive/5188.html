\n I found the following on a wikipedia entry for American International Pictures, who produced or distributed many of the low-budget horror flicks I love:

Samuel Z. Arkoff related his tried-and-true "ARKOFF Formula" for producing a successful low-budget movie years later, during a 1980s talk show appearance. His ideals for a movie included:

Action (exciting, entertaining drama)
Revolution (novel or controversial themes and ideas)
Killing (a modicum of violence)
Oratory (notable dialogue and speeches)
Fantasy (acted-out fantasies common to the audience)
Fornication (sex appeal, for young adults)

Here is an article that examines the formula in more detail. It could be really useful as a resource for GMs:

http://www.filmflap.blogspot.com/search/label/the ARKOFF formula

...and another one which includes excerpts from an interview with the man himself regarding the formula;

    \n I too like the Dent article. I think it can be "abstracted" beyond pulp stories (which often start with "a murder" or "disappearance") for any "action" based adventure.

    E.G., even in a standard Gamist scenario:

    A DIFFERENT MURDER METHOD FOR VILLAIN TO USE -> A different attack method/menacing trope for the boss monster (if last one was fire breathing dragon, this one is petrifying medusa etc), it's not enough to have different boss monsters if their "flavour" is repetitive, if the core defense (e.g., saving throw) is the same.
    A DIFFERENT THING FOR VILLAIN TO BE SEEKING -> A different "evil plan/objective", if last one was seeking to dominate the rulers of area to increase number of capital crimes and ensure a steady supply of convicts to be disappeared to feed its boundless appetite, this one must be after a different objective for a different reason - such as kidnapping orphans to train as deadly fanatical assassins through which it will rule by terror. The thematic objective should vary between stories, in any event it must be "a thing the PC's cannot abide the villain having" (there must be a conflict).
    A DIFFERENT LOCALE -> different "dungeon" as in a new exotic location for encounters with its own unique environmental considerations as hazards and 'dressing', if burning bridges and confusing tunnels were scenes last time then ice floes and glaciers with underwater passages this time.
    A MENACE WHICH IS TO HANG LIKE A CLOUD OVER HERO -> the hook, the personal appeal, the thing of interest to PC, the "flag" it hits on them personally, a secondary conflict that may run perpendicular or opposite in direction to the primary conflict - the B personal story to the A action story.

    That make sense/offer value?
