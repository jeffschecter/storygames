    \n I recall someone did a fairly in-depth analysis of the geography and climate of an inverted earth. As it turns out, the ocean floor is waaaaaaay deeper than the highest mountains are tall. I may be misremembering, but I believe the Mariana Mountain Range would peak beyond the edge of the atmosphere? Something crazy like that.

    The one thing I do remember is that density of atmosphere in the valleys could allow for fairly heavy flying animals.
