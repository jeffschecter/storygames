    \n (Sorry about the fucking--I figured you knew half my shit was tongue-in-cheek.)

    I'm just sayin' that lab coats might 'drift' the game more into Medical Experiments than Medical Hospital--ooo! But what a cool supplement idea, no? Then you could do Medical Cyber, for near-future implants; Veterinary Hospital; Psych Ward (or are lobotomies and neural probes in bad taste? I can never tell...).

    Speaking of which... got any implant procedures--'north' or 'south' of the belly button--in the default game, yet?

    I'd be careful about the guerrilla shots, if you intend to later post them--there's probably some serious, scary laws around medical privacy that you'd be violating, if you caught anyone in the shot in an identifiable manner (this MIGHT not be an issue outside the hospital, in "public").

    But I bet you could make arrangements to do staged shots during a quiet time or during the graveyard shift, if you talked to a hospital's PR folks. Hell, pitch the game to them as a "serious game"--a buzzword they'll know from educational and disaster management computer games they likely have seen or heard about--and they might even stumble over themselves to provide locations, props, and even staff to do some "action." And again, this might be something Rob could help with (though that could mean going to Wake), as he has experience finagling locations.

    \n Thanks again ... at the last playtest there was a lot of excited talk about Animal Hospital and Medieval Hospital spin-offs. The current assortment includes femoral shunt placement, ileogastric valve repair, and I'm working up a complete, three-stage mitral valve replacement. Nothing cosmetic, although it presents rich, rich possibilities. One thing I'm considering, especially if the final game proves to be popular, is to produce add-ons for specialties. So you could buy a set of, say, orthopoedic procedures for five bucks, or cosmetic surgery, or whatever. There are suggestions in the game for making your own, too.

    As it happens I work two floors up from a suite of "patient simulators" in a mock ICU, and the nurses who run it owe me a big favor.
