    I play a lot of games over Skype. And my advice would be this:

    1. Don't forget to take breaks. I recommend one five minute break per hour. Less and you can start intermittently start losing people as they grab drinks and go to the washroom. This tends to take up more time then scheduled breaks, because you can end up not realizing that someone has left and things can get very confusing.

    2. You need a way to directly share information. There aren't a LOT of things to keep track of in Monsterhearts, but you need some kind of shared text. I've used infrno's table in the past, and while its a bit buggy it works quite nicely and can be used for Relationship maps as well as for keeping track of Strings and Conditions etc. I believe a Google Document or Drawing could be used to do the same thing.

    3. Make sure everyone AT LEAST has a set of headphones. Skype has echo-reduction technology, but its not perfect, and Google Hangouts -doesn't- have echo reduction technology, which means that if people are using their laptop speakers as well as their laptop mic, then you will hear yourself through their audio-channel. This is VERY disorienting and makes it almost impossible to play. A dedicated microphone is better, but just having headphones helps immensely. If even one person is causing echoes it can seriously reduce everyone's ability to play.

    4. Expect technical problems. They will happen. People will drop out, their computers will crash, the document system you're using will forget a change. This is just the cost of doing business on computers.

    5. Because of 4, having an alternative way to contact people can be very handy, so if they vanish, they can text message you to explain that their dog unplugged their computer.

      \n So hey, I thought I\u2019d write up a little report on how the Tuesday night group has been going.

      Thus far, we\u2019ve played three sessions in two different games of Monsterhearts.

      Our first two sessions were spent in a wealthy private school with a queen, an infernal, and a ghost. We managed to hit a natural stopping point at the end of our second session. The demon who had been summoned to kill the ghost was itself killed by an npc demon-hunter. Most of our threads had been kind of wrapped up, so we decided to start another game this Tuesday.

      That decision ended up creating the bloodiest game of Monsterhearts I\u2019ve ever seen.

      The game took place at a rural boarding school in England. The characters were:

      The Ghoul (played by John):
      The Ghoul had died in the mid eighties by choking to death on an apple after going out into the forest to get high. His body was never found, and he recently returned to life with a hunger for flesh. During his last few weeks of life, something had been preying on the students at his school, but his death had nothing to do with that, being entirely accidental.

      The Mortal (played by Jess):
      A Twilight Fan who had witnessed the Ghouls resurrection and decided that they were DESTINED TO BE TOGETHER FOREVER BECAUSE OF REASONS. She was very dedicated to the idea of being in love, but not so dedicated to the object of her affections.

      The Fae (played by Jack):
      A creature that had been recently banished from the Courts of the Fae for reasons as yet unknown. He was fascinated by the Ghoul, and it was implied that his banishment was, in some way associated with whatever magic had resurrected the ghoul.
      We ended up playing for around two hours, with about an hour of character creation. All of us ended up as our darkest selves, and collectively we caused mass panic, a major police manhunt, and the deaths of four people including a police officer and the headmaster of our school.

      It was very fast paced, and very enjoyable, though I\u2019m not sure we can maintain that pace for an entire Season.

      For my part, I enjoyed playing the Fae. I went Dark, and I had some trouble figuring out what \u201cEverything is a promise\u201d meant, but \u201cbalancing the scales\u201d? Heck yeah. I was all over that. I sent my video of the Ghoul killing someone to the police.

      Much fun was had.
