\n Lady Blackbird - Part 1
\n
\nThe play by play is quite long, too long to post! So here's a link to it: http://docs.google.com/View?id=dnh2zgr_57cwk9m5f3
\n
\nSome comments about the game itself:
\n
\nMechanically, we like it. The traits and tags invokes good flavor to the actions -- very easy to imagine Snargle's daredevil piloting for example. And the keys work well as well. It took reminding for the first 3-4 times someone hit a key, but after that people were taking experience themselves no problem and I think all of them have enough experience for an advance. Most of the pools weren't dropping too fast as there were enough failures on the rolls to keep them up.
\n
\nWe struggled the most with world building as we go. There were times when I'd ask them about how something works, or they'd ask me, and we'd have to stop and think about it breaking the flow a bit. However, this was only a minor issue and we still enjoyed playing and look forward to playing the second session. But if we were doing a long term campaign rather than just 2-3 sessions I think we'd want more background in advance.
\n
\nThanks for putting this game together John, we're enjoying it a lot!

    \n Paul--
    \n
    \nThe steam power suit was mostly narrative, though I did make it harder for Captain Hollas to shoot Vance due to the protection the suit provided. Generally, when someone was being shot at I had the resisting player be the only roll. For example, Snargle evading the gliders shooting at the Owl. For some reason in this case I rolled a few dice for Hollas rather than Vance rolling to defend and just made the target number for Hollas more difficult due to the protection of the suit. I should've made it more active on the player -- He could've rolled [Survivor] or [Warrior] with a few bonus tags due to the suit. If one introduces temporary equipment, I think it should add tags only (no traits) that could be linked to a character's trait via common sense. Maybe I'll remember to do something like that next time!
    \n
    \nTo be honest, I would've forgotten about the Count entirely if they hadn't been talking about it. And once they were, of course it was the perfect time to bring him in :) Though I wasn't expecting Lady Blackbird to be quite so bloodthirsty and merciless!
