\n The original DragonLance module worked great as a DW module when I ran it at Dreamation on Thursday night. We had no cleric, amusingly, but I said a Paladin would be okay, so he had the Blue Crystal Staff. Paladin, fighter, thief, and wizard: great party!

It was really nice to have a map to refer to, and to just know what elements and factions were in play and to just improvise wildly based on that. I did a fair amount of prep - 1 Campaign Front and 2 Dungeon Fronts, and a bunch of monster stats. Most of it didn't get used; I felt like the module map and room descriptions (which I didn't hew to very closely--too many pointless encounters) took the place of a lot of that stuff. But it was nice to have!

The Thief, by the way, was a kender. We replaced the +1 damage with ranged weapons with "When you mercilessly taunt a number of enemies up to your level, roll +Cha. On a 10+, the target(s) can do nothing but goggle apoplectically until you stop taunting or they come under immediate physical threat. On a 7-9, they can do nothing but attack/chase you."

In the end, largely thanks to some judicious use of this talent by the Thief (Rob Bohl: no longer allowed to play Kender), they did get the MacGuffin, and used the Blue Crystal Staff to scare the dragon away, but got a 7-9 on that so I ruled that the dragon got off one attack on the Paladin, killing him instantly.

As an aside: the damage numbers in Beta 1.1 are *nuts*. I cut them in half.

Matt
