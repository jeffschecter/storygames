Wario Needs A Ham (Micro-Games Invade Tabletop?)

Neko_Ewen
January 2007 edited January 2007 in Story Games
Yesterday I picked up Wario Ware: Smooth Moves for the Nintendo Wii (entirely too many gameplay videos here, similar Japanese doujin game with maids over here), and between that and Jonathan's earlier thread and that Quiz & Dragons 24-hour RPG, I accidentally came up with yet another concept for a game (or possibly several games, or something).

Wario Ware hits you with a succession of "micro-games," teeny-tiny little challenges where sometimes figuring out what you're supposed to do is more challenging that what you have to do (especially with the Wii's motion-sensing controller). In multiplayer, the game uses these micro-games in several different contexts, and sometimes even makes them irrelevant to the main objective. All of this is done with a bizarrely eclectic range of art styles (it has snippets of dozens of Nintendo games, exaggerated 3-D models, what look like little kids' drawings, etc.) and activities (from guiding a finger into Wario's nose to shooting a cannon at a giant robot). I find this really, really neat for some reason.

So this would be a sort of meta-game, where the GM ("Host") has some random props (dice, scrap paper, pencils, an action figure, a rubber ball, etc.) and tosses random challenges at the players (Draw a happy face! Open this soda can! Stack these dice! Catch!) rapid-fire, in place of (or in addition to) any normal and reasonable resolution system.

I want to put together a deck of micro-game cards (including blank cards so you can make up new ones to go with whatever props you might have handy), a simple comedy RPG, and a board game, plus suggestions for dropping the micro-games into all kinds of other tabletop games.

I have to think more about the RPG part, in terms of what kind of mechanics and setting/premise would actually go well with spastic micro-games. The Wario Ware games actually do have characters and stories, even if it's just the afro guy being followed by dancing kitties or the kung fu guy fighting his way to the front of the line at a meat bun stand.
