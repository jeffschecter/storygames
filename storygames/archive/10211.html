\n
\n
\n
\n\nJames_Mullen \n\n\n
\n
\n\nAugust 2009 \n edited August 2009 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n I was catching up with the seminar about WFRP 3rd Edition (http://www.fantasyflightgames.com/ffg_content/wfrp/3_media/wfrp3seminar.html) and somewhere near the end of it, I suddenly thought about Danger Patrol.
\n
\nBoth games use characters who are summarized on pre-generated cards.
\n
\nBoth games use counters to keep track of games resources.
\n
\nBoth games have neat character abilities as a major plank in the design.
\n
\nSo... would Danger Patrol be the same game with the slick components made by Fantasy Flight Games (bearing mind that it would end up costing 3 or times as much as a simple rulebook)?
\n
\nAnd would WFRP3 be an innovative story game if they just published a basic rule book with some on-line support in the form of cards and tokens you could print out for yourself?
\n
\nIs this a blurring of the boundaries between trad and indie rpgs (if such boundaries can be said to really exist)? Is this a mainstream company entering the story games arena? Or is it all just pretty colours, lots of flash & bang but no substance?
\n
\nUltimately, would it make any difference to any game in existence if its presentation were radically changed? But in particular, would you like Danger Patrol any more if it came with glossy cards and neat tokens? Would WFRP3 become a more respected story game if the high quality components were left out? Am I just blathering?

    \n This is sort of perpendicular to the thread at best, but I definitely have some of what many boardgamers refer to as "parakeet" in me. ("Ooh, shiny!") If a game has high quality components to it, it is definitely a part of my buy or not equation. If only so I can take them out of the otherwise wretched game and use them in other games!

    At the same time, if a great game does NOT have cool quality components it's sort of an appealing opportunity to me. I'll add them! I'll spend an hour making a giant squid monster out of lego for my 4E game, for example. For my last Danger Patrol playtest, I made a red planet and used these little retro plastic rockets I found elsewhere to represent the Martian Planetary Defense Force and removed them incrementally as a countdown to DOOM as they struggled against the Ragnor Roc from planet Earth.

    I picture a Danger Patrol lunch box similar to that being sold for the Fallout 3 lunch box edition. How cool would that be? Sigh.
