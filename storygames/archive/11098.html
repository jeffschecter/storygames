    \n Mostly, I have no need for newsletters.
    If there's an already existing need, then a newsletter which fills that need has merit. That need can be as simple as "people who like this certain game want to hear more about it".
    But without an existing, identified need... a newsletter is just labor-intensive advertising.

    \nThis would all be great but what would be the purpose of putting it in a newsletter rather than an online magazine or blog? Is there something about the newsletter medium that is necessary for one of your objectives. What are your objectives?

    I do subscribe to a couple of newsletters, because they either contain exclusive content or they provide a quick overview of quality information from a number of sources that would take a long time to weed out on my own.

    It seems like there is a lot of great information on this forum (and others) but it's usually buried in the middle of a thread which is sometimes "cleverly" titled so that I would never know that it is something I'm actually interested in, and surrounded by unrelated comments, inside jokes and snarky remarks. I'm not saying that all of my comments are great, related and free of snark, or that I don't enjoy some of those comments, but a newsletter that weeded out the great stuff would be really helpful sometimes.

    \n Back in college, I ran one zine and read another regularly. I love the format. Problem is that in this day and age:

    +Physical is only good for hand-out circulation. To print them up and ship them to folks, even at cost, might be a little too much.
    +Blogging's easier. You could group-blog it for multiple authors.
    +You could do a PDF format and distribute it electronically. It worked well for Daedalus, but unfortunately Matt found it too much work to keep up.

    Blogging is mentioned above, but blogging is not a 100% substitute IMO. Blogging's too easy and unfocused. But an article! Ahh, an article...

    -Andy
