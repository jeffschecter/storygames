    \n We get a job rescuing a cargo team on a ship named the Caterwaul with a problem: power is intermittent, drives aren't working, and there's a rabbit loose in the cargo access shaft. In fact, our contract is just to get the rabbit to its destination. Okay!

    - Zeke opens comms from Myriad to the Caterwaul. Myriad asks about life support control. There is air pressure throughout the vessel and it is under intermittent control. They are carrying the live specimen and some tools. The live specimen is a "rabbit" which has a special meaning if you're from Fail. And Zofma knows that this is a huge six-legged ungulate with sharp hooves that roams the obsidian plains of Fail. Pretty dangerous, and masses at least a ton.
    - We're getting paid a lot. Myriad assesses risk.
    - Alison points out that in zero G this thing won't be mustering much of a defense. Still could be dangerous \u2014 a thrashing ton is pretty dangerous. Myriad asks the cargo master for advice. He has no experience with manhandling thousand kilo six-legged ungulates.
    - We have CCTV footage of it floating around and kicking. Six legs, long ears, plenty of howling.
    - Zeke asks the crew how this happened. They are embarrassed. Someone made a mistake.
    - Alison cycles the airlock and brings the EVA team into the ship. He checks out the situation, checking the crew quarters. Zofma checks for booby traps because he's careful that way, using his Demolitions skill. He gets 2 total and things look fine! There are two church officers and three crew; Alison introduces himself, they laugh at the fact that he's a missionary. Condolences, etc. He tries to find out more about the rabbit but they don't have much to offer \u2014 they have been pretty incompetent and don't want to talk about it.
    - Alison orders the cargo master to inflate the rescue balls \u2014 they will clog the cargo chute with these and push them down the chute, hoping to force the animal safely into the cargo can.
    - We run a sort of zone combat, with each person making MicroG checks to navigate a rescue ball usefully. It kicks around and manages to deflate a ball or two with its sharp hooves and frustrates the effort right near the cargo door. Once there we order the crew to open the exterior cargo door and then close it. The change in air pressure forces the animal into the cargo canister.
    - We risk shooting the thing into space. Myriad manages the timing using Profession: Repo Man (this is all standard procedure) and gets a 4, succeeding and avoiding accidentally spacing the beast. Rabbit in the can!

    \n A pickup game of Capes with my brother and an old gaming buddy from my high school days. Bro plays Brick Masonry, a Brick + Simple Soul working-class hero, good-hearted, slow-minded and wide and tall as a truck. Buddy plays JusticeBot 2.0, a Robot + Crusader ("like Captain America with expressionless lightup eyes and mouth") created as the ultimate justice-dispensing machine by the intrepid Dr. Falcon. I play The Magistrate, a Godling + Older but Wiser cosmic being, once a hero (and Dr. Falcon's teammate) sent to Earth to understand and judge humanity, but now turned against mankind having judged civilization corrupt.

    As the Magistrate marched on City Hall, Brick stood in his way. My brother checked off "Happy" to roll a die, and challenged me with the declaration: "You shouldn't hurt people just because you're sad!"

    That became his catchphrase. When Justicebot's sinister new programming kicked in to strike at the Magistrate by invading his underground complex and killing his family (causing me to abandon my goal of "Kill the City Council" in an hurry!) Brick Masonry allied with the alien visitor: "He shouldn't hurt people just because he's sad!"

    The Magistrate saved his family, but at great cost, sending them off to the stars in the Cosmic Raft, leaving himself stranded and estranged from them...forever! Then we fought and destroyed the JusticeBot utterly. Brick Masonry's pure-hearted idealism began to teach the Magistrate a newfound respect for humanity, and he proposed an alliance to track down Dr. Falcon himself, Brick readily agreed: "He shouldn't hurt people just because he's sad!"

    God, I love Capes!

    We a had a pretty good time anyway, and I learned a lot. I'll compact my dungeon and try sending another group of lowly dregs into it!

    \n Last night we killed the demonic God-Whale in Lamentations of the Flame Princess. It was covered in demonic God-Lampreys that did ongoing damage, so my guy Black Eagle did the sensible thing and charged into the beast's dagger-toothed maw, fighting his way into its interior until it expired. Our cleric died but otherwise we did all right, and used the things bones and skin to make a boat so we could leave the island forever!

    It was really fun to plan our ambush, not really knowing how badass this thing was. Our plan involved somehow getting our magic user on its back after being amped up with a heroism blessing from the cleric so he could blast a web spell down it's blowhole. He webbed his hand in there and shot a pair of huge magic missiles while our specialist sneak-attacked it in the eyes. My guy was the bait, and all his flailing around in the God-Whale's interior didn't have much of an impact on the outcome.

    \n We started up our long HELLAS game on Sunday. So far it's pretty hot. We all ended up being separate, disparate characters but started the slow wind to joining up and forming "the party".

    So far we have a Kyklopes artist/slipspace pilot who specializes in blasphemous statues/art of the Gods/Goddesses; a Nymphas trader who is trying to recover his(?<-- Crying Game) lover; an Amazoran whoremaster who bides his time making connections around the city; a Hellene retired Spartan general looking for the true story of what happened to his son, killed in battle; a Zintar surgeon who is destined to either find the favor of or be eternally cursed by the war-god Areson.

    We had Pankrateon fighting, nakedness, drink, seduction, conspiracy, fellowship, and solipsistry all in the first adventure.

    I'm loving this game. Some tough bits to swallow about the system re PC-on-PC fighting given that the system is more geared for PC-vs-NPC conflict (social, etc), but still we worked around it.

    \n Geek AP -- Zeke hacks an alien computer with the help of a friend.

    Zeke is trying to find public interfaces so he can query the system's data. It's all in an unknown format, of course. He reports failure. Myriad advises him to do some traffic analysis \u2014 it's transmitting something so maybe the period of transmission matches some exterior event. He's also trying to find recordings of data we have already transmitted to it. All this should get some clues to format and maybe reveal the system a little. Zeke makes a Communications roll and gets a total of 0 (rolls a -3) and decides to tag an aspect for a re-roll. Tags the ship's Military communications system and gets a total of 3 this time. Much better! He also invokes Alison's Quick learner aspect \u2014 he calls Alison and gets his opinion on it, and Alison determines that there is a mismatched impedence somewhere. Success! Zeke has format info and is now decoding visual and astrographic data.

    \n Ran (Story Guided?) some Shadow of Yesterday tonight over Skype.

    Highlight was when the PCs had a bounty on them, so Onfroi the elf with key of glittering gold turns himself in. As a guard walks him to the manor, Nemo, a human PC runs up to try and help him, hits the guard in the nose with a cudgel. The guard staggers back, complaining his nose has been broken, blood begins pouring down and he complains that he would call the town guard if they weren't incompetent.

    Then the elf PC explains that he doesn't need a rescue, that Nemo should run back look after the others. Confused, he leaves Onfroi behind and wanders into the forest after the other PC. Then Onfroi helps the guard out and effectively escorts him to the manor, instead of the other way around.

    It was this oddball moment that nobody planned for, changed the tone of the game and had us laughing our heads off. Very Raising Arizona meets D&D moment.
