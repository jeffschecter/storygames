    You definitely want to convert the photo rather than letting some automatic process do it for you. Some photos can be desaturated without any big loss; others, though, will turn into a grey mess. You can play with contrast over specific areas (select using lasso or magic wand, then Adjust Contrast&Brightness) to make two near-contrast colors very different contrasts before desaturating. This may fix the problem. It may not.

    Interior photos can be problematic depending on how you're printing. Some printers will halftone photos for you, others won't. Some will charge you when they do, some may do it as a part of the service. Again, you can do this yourself before you submit the file to avoid the problem. (In photoshop, Halftone is a filter; play with the settings and contrasts until you're happy).

    All the full-page watercolors in The Roach were full collor illos originally. I reprocessed them in Photoshop and they went to the printer as 600 dpi greyscale .tiffs. It definitely helps that they are somewhat impressionistic to begin with. Don't send color.

    The collage art went through the same process, with lots more attention due to varying quality sources, and again I got a free pass because it is supposed to look uneven. These all started oout as black and white.

    The images you've chosen look great. I love that first one. I'd be concerned that they don't have high enough resolution for print - I'm not sure what you'll lose when you up-sample them.
