    Bonus points for creating a story on the fly instead of using any of the play sets.

    Double blanks might kill or severely incapacitate your character, but it doesn't stop you from playing. Shakespeare is loaded with ghosts and there are always flashbacks. Flashbacks are a great way of adding hidden details to a scene, doing reveals, developing character, or planting that bomb under the seat.

    Heh, interesting point about scoring blanks. In all the games that other people have played, blanks are worth zero since you could easily give yourself double sixes as well. I'm going to bounce that idea off my some of my regulars and see what they think of that. I do need to clarify one way or the other.
