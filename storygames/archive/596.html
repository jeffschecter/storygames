Running A Large Group of First-Timers

kleenestar
April 2006 edited April 2006 in Play Advice
As the title says, I'd love some input and advice from you guys about how to run a large group of possibly-first-time gamers.

I'm running a game design workshop this summer for highschool and college students. I've got them for a week; we'll be playing games in the morning and designing games in the afternoon. I get to do whatever I want with the curriculum, and I do have a small budget to work with, so I've got some interesting options.

While I'll be covering card games, board games, and computer games as well, I really want to do a full day on role-playing games. The difficulty is that it'll be two people running 15-25 novice-to-moderate role-players. I could put together a LARP, but I'd like them to work on tabletop RP design in the afternoon (for playtesting reasons) so that's not quite ideal. I could run D&D, on the theory that many of them will be familar with it, but I'd really like to use the opportunity to introduce them to the diversity of what's out there.

Does anyone have ideas about what I can run with these kids? Bizarre and weird ideas are very welcome, as are obvious ones that I'm omitting. LARPing and D&D are still on the table, but I'd love some help from this group coming up with more options.

I think this might also work well as a discussion of what games are good for novice players in general, and questions of how to deal with a very large group.

Thanks for any advice you've got!

--Jess

  • Jason_Morningstar

    You know, if I were doing this I might hook up with NerdNYC/Gotham Gaming Guild/etc and make this an outside-of-class assignment. Tell your students to go play an RPG on their own time, and give them a list of seats made available by friendly local GMs for the purpose, or otherwise let them find the game that interests them most. That way they get to experience the real deal, with experienced people, and they get to choose a game that interests them personally, or as designers, or both. Then you can spend class time debriefing and comparing.

    That said, you can be up and running with The Roach in maybe ten minutes. People who have never played a tabletop RPG tend to jump right in and do well. If you want to run it for your class get in touch and I'll hook you up with .pdfs or something.
