Hot Terminology: Social Live Acoustic Games!!

DevP
August 2006 edited August 2006 in Story Games

Me and Dan Solis were trying to brainstorm categorizations for his new site (in progress). For one thing, he and some other folks (Annie Rush also, I think) are adopting the term "acoustic games" for games that are particularly non-electronic but awesome. Aside from that, his games go into the following categories...

  • board games
  • card games
  • games that are like roleplaying games or maybe just story telling games but also maybe games that are played socially like Mafia that might tell a story but maybe not and then also games that are like tag but with zombies so that's different too

The naming scheem kind of breaks down in the third category, as you can see. There's something in common between Once-upon-a-Time, Dogs in the Vineyard, Mafia and Zombie Tag... So we had two suggestions: "social games" and "live games".

The latter - "live games" - strikes us as particularly zingy. Perhaps what these games have in common is that their not based on board or card pieces, but do absolutely require the live, in-person participation of each player.
