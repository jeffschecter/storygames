    \n Aye, love Dabbleboard, but there is a hurdle to 'unlearn' click and drag (which draws shapes.) Single click selects any object on the whiteboard and that displays the handles for that object. You can then grab the appropriate handle (move, scale, delete, etc.) So, the motion is click, move the pointer to the handle, and then click-n-drag. It's not a game breaker.

    I also like that you can pre-load images to the main board or the working area on the side. Really handy for character sheets, dominos, miniatures, etc. It also means you can do some GM prep-work before the game and spend more time playing.

    \n I highly recommend google+ hangouts. It now has a built in sketchpad for mapping but we've been using twiddla. We use it for 4e, which is heavily map based and have had no problems. My guide to using google+ and twiddla.

    For the person developing a vtt. We tried maptools, and it was just too complicated. We knew if we kept at it we could make it work, but each week it can be a completely different crew and we don't want to deal with problem solving technical issues. Google+ and twiddla for the most part work and get out of our way so we can game.

    \n I got turned onto infrno at RinCon last year. If you get a chance to sit down at a con for a demo, do so. The webcam/mike thing is really the killer app for me. (Of course Google+ may do this as well, I don't have experience with that.) The ability to see people's expressions/listen to their voices is so good.

    I did a pretty extensive Cthulhutech scenario on Infrno, the game table is good for putting NPC pictures up to keep mysteries straight. Also it means players can quickly take their own notes and preserve them from session to session.

    If you're interested in Infrno, give it a signup and then check out the game, I left everything intact there (blog posts, character posts, etc.)

    Gunfight Epiphany
