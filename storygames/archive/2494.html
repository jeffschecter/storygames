20 WORDS OR LESS: how YOU make games rock!

jenskot
February 2007 edited February 2007 in Story Games
Many of these suggestions will contradict themselves. That's awesome! It's all situational. Fuck the one true way! Show me your way!!!

- Listen to the other players.

- Know what you want and take action to get it.

- Make your intension clear.

- Say less. Talk 1/5th as much as you think you need to.

- Plan less, being caught off guard rocks, roll with it.

- Don't know what to do next? Slow down.

- Ask yourself, how can I help my fellow players look cool?

- Make others look good by giving them strong responses.

- Think of the safe way and throw it out.

- When you ask a question, wait for an answer.

- Make the stakes higher.

- Make strong choices. Don't hold back.

- Speak the truth in simple, direct language.

- Speaking the truth means listening first and then responding.

- Warm up before you roleplay.

- It's not life as it is. It's life as we wish it could be.

- What you don't say can be as cool as what you do say.

- If someone is stuck, help them out, make suggestions.

- Don't give suggestions if someone is not stuck and happy. Move on.

- Make sure everyone is contributing as much as you are.

- Make sure everyone is listening before you speak.

- Look people in the eye. Then talk.

- Say yes or roll the dice.

- If you have to ask what the conflict is. There is no conflict. Move on.

- It's not acting. It's re-acting.

Rock,
John

    - If you don't want them to screw it up, don't let them roll the dice.

    Coming from a traditional (D&D type) background, this was a real revelation for me. More times than I can count, I've seen players snatch defeat from the jaws of victory by failing a roll they didn't actually need to make. Things go a lot better since I've realized they only need to roll dice when there's a question as to whether they should succeed. A corollary to "say yes or roll the dice", which I love.
