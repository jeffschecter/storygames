It's been two years since that thread started. I went back last year and updated and revised my list. Here's what changed. How about you? If you're new to SG and didn't post in that previous thread, tell us what you were interested in playing a year ago (if you remember that far), and out of that list what you got to play.

My list a year ago was:

-----
1 - Conspiracy of Shadows
2 - Dirty Secrets
3 - Shinobigami (and will throw in DoubleCross here too)
4 - Freemarket
5 - Remember Tomorrow
-----

Out of those, I played:
Shinobigami (not yet, but in a few days)
Freemarket (Camp Nerdly)
Remember Tomorrow

Of those, I did not play:
Conspiracy of Shadows
Dirty Secrets

Plus I played a ton of new games and stuff, but that's neither here nor there.

Conclusions:
* Got over half my list filled, so that's good!
* Did not get around to Dirty Secrets yet. Not avoiding it, just stuff that interested me more popped into view and got all up in my grill that much faster.
* Did not get around to Conspiracy of Shadows. Was kinda waiting for the rules to settle down before digging in (Revised happened after Keith played and loved the Shadow of Yesterday, so CoS became heavily influenced by SoY; Third edition was coming soon, but it shifted from Burning Wheel to Mouseguard rules before stopping). Since Keith keeps the older versions of the game available online so my friends can buy it, I am thinking of playing the CoS first edition again, because I think that game has the strongest vision of the rules tied to the setting (some of the hacks in revised are cool, might find a way to incorporate those into 1st edition).

Anyway, now I need to make a new list for 2011. So I just started a thread for that, too!

-Andy

    1. ASCII@hack

    5. Sign in Stranger

    Note that I had already axed out the ones I'd played since the thread started.

    Empire of Dust is the super star of this list. That game really delivers some awesomeness. We liked our EoD game so much, we ported it directly into a sequel played using Apocalypse World, as written, using the same characters. The transition was seamless and resulted in a campaign that I can only describe as legendary.

    To my regret, I have still not played Donjon, Sign in Stranger, or Drifter's Escape. And what ever happened to ASCII@hack? Is it still alive somewhere?

    Today I wouldn't make a top five unplayed games list. My attitude to gaming has changed drastically since then and making a list just seems out of synch with my point of view nowadays.

    \n My list (Nov 24, 2009) was

    1) Apocalypse World
    2) House of Cards
    3) Chronica Feudalis
    4) 1001 Nights
    5) Solomon Kane (Savage Worlds)

    What strikes me about the list is what a snapshot it and all such lists I make are. Half the games might have been different a month later. I've since played the first two. I would still really like to play 1001 Nights and think my odds of getting to it are pretty good. I suspect I may never play Chronica Feudalis or Solomon Kane, in part due to lessened interest and in part because I'd want to play them as an ongoing thing and suspect I won't find anyone who'd like to do that.

    Speaking of ongoing things if I make a new list I'll probably have about the same "success" rate of 40% a year or so after. At best. I like variety but I've also come to appreciate more re-playing the same games over again. I played 30-something games of Apocalypse World. I played Fate 2.0 hacks, Love in the Time of Sei\xf0 and other Archipelago II hacks, Microscope, S/Lay w/Me and Danger Patrol several times each. I played The War of the Ring boardgame 19 times in the last year. This behavior doesn't leave much time to get to all the games I want to play.

    A lot of my "variety gaming" comes from playtesting something that comes along that isn't on my radar until it suddenly is. Or whatever the hot new game is that people are excited to try. Most of the games that make up my top 5 at any snapshot are not ones enough people will make time for. They've got their own lists of completely different games.
