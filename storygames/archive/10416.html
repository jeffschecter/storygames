    There is a large table, well worn over the years from use. Its edges are sticky with an ancient black tar that was probably once spilled mead. Around it is played a gambling game, in clever defiance of the town law which allows for "No Gambeling for Moneys of Gold or Copper wi\xfein \xf0e Towne of Annuse". It uses cards and small clay cups, filled with the noxious icemead made by Tom, the proprietor.

    \n

    Players bet the number of cups of Wreck Tom they can drink on the highest pair of cards. You can fold, which means you have to drink the cups you've put out.

    \n

    However, there's a trump. If you get the other guy to submit in a wrestling match on the table, he has to drink all the Wreck Toms on the table.

    \n

    All people watching gamble on their chosen winner by promising each other drinks which conveniently come in 1d. increments on the menu.

    \n

    You will note that the only person getting richer in this arrangement is Tom, and that the townspeople don't get anything but drunk. He's also the mayor and the guy who wrote the anti-gambling law, putting the town's other tavern ("\xd0e Browne Syclopse") out of business in the process. Tom's sort of an asshole.
