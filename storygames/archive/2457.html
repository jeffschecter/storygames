    Good stories have tension.

    In story games, tension arises from all kinds of stuff. In fact, I'd say just about everything in a smartly-written story game connects in some way to tension: character and situation creation, conflict, scene framing, escalation and so on.

    I don't think that playing a story game is the same as telling a story (in the same way that I don't think that playing a war game is the same as fighting a war).

    The act of actually telling a story is quite noticeably different from the act of playing a story game (or any game).

    I am of two minds about whether the skills taught by story games translate into better story-telling. I think that the games can help you see some interesting questions to ask yourself as you tell a story. But I don't think the games give you the answers for anything story-telling related. I think that if you want to cross-train then you need to make the effort to use games as an inspiration, but to develop your own story-telling skills in a story-telling context.

    Nate, I suggest checking out Robert McKee's "STORY" from your local library. For what it is, it's helpful in thinking about these things.

    These are necessary, but not sufficient, conditions for a story:
    * A beginning, middle and end, usually in that order
    * Character(s)
    * Setting(s)
    * Conflict(s)
    * Themes
    * Style

    Avant-garde stories might jettison some of these, with greater or lesser success.

    Role-playing games involve these elements, and give you tooks for adressing them. I think the big achievement with "story games" is that there's a much greater focus on themes: essentially, hardwiring thematic issues. But this doesn't mean deeper understandings of character/settings/conflicts/pacing couldn't be advanced. For that matter, I haven't seen "style" addressed at all, and I figure that's an open frontier right now. What if there were games that helped you to tell the story better?

    I actually disagree a little bit with Tony: the end product of a story-game doesn't look much like a piece of polished writing, but IME you always get a story--you can't avoid it--and it's usually a pretty decent genre story.
