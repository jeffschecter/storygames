    Posted By: Nathan H.How often do you visualize description?
    \nVery often, as long as the description is simple enough, or described in an order that doesn't clash with my visualization.

    "There's a forest." -> Visualization

    "There's a forest. The conifers..." -> Ooops, unless I visualized conifers in the first place, my first image is broken, and I need to revisualize it. If this keeps happening the visualization stops till I have the whole description -- and if the description is too intricate, I can't visualize it properly.

    "There's a coniferous forest." -> Visualization

    Context is very important for visualization, I find. If I have sufficient context, I don't need much anything.\n
    How long does this usually last?
    \nIt comes and it goes. Can't really say.\n
    Do you find it more gratifying then just listening and talking?
    \nIt's an amplifier: without talking and listening the visualization (in context of RPGs, that is) is pretty boring. Without the visualization the talking and listening are much less fun -- they can be fun without visualization, but they are better with visualization.\n
    What parts are easier for you to visualize?
    \nSee above.

    \n I tend to see it all like a movie playing as stuff is happening.
    \n
    \nAs a movie there is always a lot more detail than was ever actually said at the table, but it normally isn't a problem, the movie seen just kind of morphs...somewhat dream like...to match as new information is gathered. Sometimes it gets a little confusing as to whether the detail I'm about to use in play was one that was actually said or one I filled in myself, but that isn't usuallya problem either.
    \n
    \nStrangely, there's almost never any sound. Interestingly, I almost never remember their being sound in my dreams either. People talk and I understand the conversation...but I don't actually "hear" it...kind of wierd.
