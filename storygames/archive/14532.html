    \n Hey Ross, sounds like fun. I'd be curious to see the various topics you come up with, and how the workshops go. I think some time practicing and getting feedback on techniques, without necessarily trying to get any sort of narrative going, could be very productive.

    I'd throw in-
    Author stance - Player as creative collaborator. How it shifts the engagement, what sorts of contributions work well for other people to build on. I'd talk about make declarations in Fate or Burning wheel, or various spend a Foo point to describe a detail games.

    Asking for it - Flags, Beliefs, Keys, Aspects as a way of conveying to the GM/other players what sorts of challenges you want to face/issues you want to wrestle with.

    Antagonism - How to provide solid opposition (primarily in GMless games.) What sorts of threats are common, but don't really work ("or you'll die!") What sorts of threats sting bad and really keep on giving ("your brother blames you for the accident.") How to pick up on where the good threats might be from the other player.

    \n You should discuss personal artistic growth. Being that these people are already doing roleplaying, what are their ambitions in the hobby? Where do they see themselves being in five years creatively? What do they dream of achieving in roleplaying, what have they already achieved? How to develop your hobby activities to achieve those goals better? What kinds of group set-ups, social environments and artistic conditions have other people worked with in their roleplaying, and how they've benefited of the right tools for the right job?

    The above is probably a good topic early on in the series, as it helps you know the participating people better, and to tailor further content into something actually relevant to their interests.

    \n What a great idea, Ross. You could have some real fun with this. Meg & I ran an intro to rpgs at a local school library some years back, for teens. We talked about a bunch of games (brought copies for the kids to look at) and then she ran a brief demo of 1001 Nights. It was great.

    Here are some more ideas.

    For younger kids:
    Kings and Queens for a Day - the creative power of role playing games\n
      \n
    • World Building: create maps and have kids put locations, monsters etc. on eachother's landscapes. Everyone goes home with a world to adventure in.
      • \n
    • Characters: create a couple three characters as a group from a kids-friendly game, like Storming the Wizards Tower, Faery's Tale or for younger kids, Big Night.
    \nFor folks new to role play:
    The Art of Story - social games that let you tell a story together\n
      \n
    • Opening discussion of favorite books, movies, tv shows. Video games with great stories? Incredible fun to enjoy these stories, why not create them with friends? Story telling (ghost stories at camp, telling stories for kids), use mechanics like board games or video game plus letting you make up what the characters do, create worlds together, have adventures.
      • \n
    • The People in your story (warm-up): example character creation from Breaking the Ice or Best Friends
      • \n
    • The World they live in: create a Primetime Adventures series,
    • Play: sample scene from PtA, have two volunteers come up and play a scene, with suggestions from crowd. Facilitator frames the scene and makes sure to introduce an interesting complication! Give the audience fan mail (1 each) to use or not during a conflict. Wrap after roll.
    • Wrap up - what was it like to play characters, what kinds of games (settings) would people want to play?
      • \n
    \nFor already gamers, interested in something new:
    Kicking it Up a Notch - collaboration, hot conflicts and going new directions\n
      \n
    • Ready Made Powder Kegs: making situations ready to blow-- create a Dogs town together
      • \n
    • Cross-Pollinating Character: character creation from Spirit of the Century (feats, stunts and shared stories)
      • \n
    • Getting to the Good Stuff: adventure and combat that delivers without a grind -- sample of combat from 3:16, discovery phase and Rogue's phase from Swords without Master
      • \n
