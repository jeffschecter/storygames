    I received an anonymous letter in the mail, containing a $2 bill and this game mechanic.

    Casting a Net: Whenever you try to invoke a Net, roll all the d4s in your Pool, and arrange all of them on the table so that none of them touch each other. Each player will take turns choosing an action from below:

    • Pick a thread: Remove a dice of a given number; then take all solitary dice on the table that match that numbers. Move them to the side of the table and put them touching each other, so that they form a single, contiguous line. This line now is acted upon on its own. You must bring an Story Element into play to do this.
    • Fray a thread: Pick a thread of dice up from the table, and reroll them all back onto the table. They are now all considered solitary dice again. You must bring a Chaotic Element into play to do this.
    • Snip a thread: Take a thread of dice, and split it into two smaller subthreads. You must bring a Complication into play to do this.
    • Stitch a thread: Take two threads of dice of the same number, and join them into one. You must bring a Boon into play to do this.
    • Declare the Net Complete: This phase is over, and now the Net is ready used in the next phase. (No more actions may be taken.) You must spend a Fate Token in order to do this.

    ...Yeah. Nets.

    Setting:

    They called It Cancer.
    The dispossessed, those who were seperated from the world, whether by drugs, obsession, madness, birth defect, physical trauma, social trauma, whatever.
    These people were seperated from real-world timeline.
    And they called It Cancer.

    In their imagined worlds Cancer reigned supreme. The Cancer within them called to the Cancer within others, called It did, and answer It did too.
    Across time-lines, across worlds, the dispossessed gathered inside one another's minds.

    The Cancer of the occupied person shaped the world, and they tried to fight It back. Fight It back using their own Cancer.
    Cancer played a zero-sum game.

    When they won, if they won, they became occupied, their Cancer having syphoned strength from the defeated Cancer, using it to control and inhabit the dispossessed fully, or almost.

    The worlds Cancer shaped, they had one thing, they were Tarot-card based. The Archetypes called, and Cancer answered.

    The Dispossessed called, and Cancer answered.

    Cancer called and came.

    The above is based on my back-burner game, that didn't really advance anywhere till I began work on Cranium Rats, and damn, if this isn't the best description of it yet.

    Here's a scribble I found on a newspaper in the recycling basket at a cafe:

    When a player asserts his character by bringing one of their core traits into play, he takes a stone, and he puts another stone in the pot.

    When a player establishes some conflict between his character and the character of another player, they split the pot. The player who starts the conflict takes the least number of stones such that he gets more than the other player - in other words, if the pot has an odd number of stones, the conflict-starter gets the odd stone. If the pot is even, the starter takes two stones, and then they split the pot.
