\n I was reading a baby book and it told me that adrenaline (the stress hormone) is the opposite of oxytocin (the love one), at least during pregnancy, or, at the very least, during labour. One's good, the other isn't.

That got me thinking. In a hippy way, does it make sense to you to understand stress vs. love?

So, when I'm writing (and playing), I get a real sense of stress. I burst with adrenaline, like its a prickly, tactile thing breathing on my neck. It helps me write, push, whatever. But rarely, if ever, do I remember that I'm making a choice to react like this. I feel good, tripping over myself with enthusiasm, but I also get a knot somewhere.

I was told that stress and depression are choices. We chose to stress, we chose to depress. It's the best we can do with what we've learned.

So, lately, I've been trying to change things up. What if, today, I look at the design/gameplay as something that loves me? I can think about it as a creature, a friend of mine, that wants to play and learn about itself. I can, if I start breathing deeply, and counting to myself at the keyboard (it looks weird, I guess, which gets me thinking about pride), imagine the design as a friend who not only loves me and wants to play, but wants me chat to it occasionally, and not always make it about business. Actually, it's doing its best to do what I ask, and its ready in a beat to try something else out. It totally cares if it pleases me. We forgive each other.

Do you ever feel like you should be enjoying what you're doing, but then, on reflection, can't figure out if you were? Am I the only one who chooses to stress out like this? If not, do you do anything hippy to calm yourself down? Help me chill out?

    \n My friend Steve (Hickey, he's on these forums), asked an interesting question the other day.     \n [That William's disease link is mind-blowing. Thanks a million!]

    I've been trying this 'animism' lately, since I first posted.

    I've noticed that when I give a voice to something that feels like it is applying pressure, I find that it was never applying pressure at all. The conversation goes like this:

    Me: Hey Idea, why are you pressuring me? I don't have time to do anything with you right now. Leave me alone!
    Idea: Pardon?
    Me: You heard me! Leave me alone!
    Idea: Sebastian, are you feeling okay? You look a little stressed out.
    Me: Me? But\u2014
    Idea: Put your feet up. I've got loads of time.

    So, yeah, just asking the stress a question feels like a back-rub. Has anyone else tried that? Could you? I'd be really interested to find out what you think.

    As for stress vs. excitement, I believe that stress is a risk-averse energy while excitement is a risk-neutral energy (so what's risk-friendly?). I think the line between the two has something to do with fear. I also believe that stress is doom while excitement is destiny. If you get my lingo.
