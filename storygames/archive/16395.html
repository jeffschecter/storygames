    \n I think the massive cons (PAX, Gen Con) are not going to be as sweet a deal as the smaller ones intensely focused on one particular kind of play. If your weirdo foreigner friends want to dive into indie/story games, they will probably get more quality and quantity at smaller events.

    Don't overlook the stuff in the UK, too: Concrete Cow, for example. Hopefully some UK-ian will come along to fill us in on their appropriate events.

    \n Big Bad Con, which is a new Bay Area con falling on Columbus Day weekend. 2nd one is this year.

    It's not entirely indie and story games, but they do have a heavy presence.

    Endgame also hosts Go Play SF Bay, a monthly (3rd Thursday) RPG event that is almost always an assortment of 1-3 story games - it depends on who shows up and who is ready to run something, also the time to play is about 4 hours or less. Given the pick up nature and limited time, the games are almost always indie style, often published, sometimes stuff in a playtest state.

    Game Kastle (just outside of San Jose) has hosted a few Story Game Days. I think it might be annual, but I'm not 100% sure.
