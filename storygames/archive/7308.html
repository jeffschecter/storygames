\n So I ran a couple Lacuna missions with one player. She had a lot of fun, but the rules definitely seem designed for a group. The static hung out around 1-5. Without more people rolling there were less failures, and I stuck in an NPC team leader to try to accumulate some of that insubordination Static and while that produced some interesting situations (she was obedient to the point of absurdity - I had make the team leader a bit of a psycho) it was a little limp. We're going to game again tonight and I'm not sure if I should tinker with the rules since she had so much fun the first time, or if I should ditch it in favor of something I know will work with one player (maybe Hero's Banner).

Oh, and so I ran into a problem and maybe I just couldn't find it in the rules but it goes like this. She ended up fighting an HP, losing a crapload of rolls, and reaching a situation where her stats were so reduced that she couldn't possibly win if she rolled, and her heart rate was through the roof. As far as I could tell by the rules, I couldn't just narrate her dying, but she wasn't going to make a roll that she couldn't possibly win that would result in her death. Also, as far as I could tell there's no maximum heart limit that results in your ejection/death like there was in Lacuna's first attempt. Does such a limit exist?

What happened in game is she just laid there in blood and broken glass after she got thrown through a window, with the HP trying to taunt her into doing something. She refused to act, and there was an interesting conversation, and eventually it got bored and left. I did not want to just outright narrate her demise and I'm not even sure if, by the rules, I could. Could I? Or should I even? How does a player die in these circumstances?

Anyways, that's been my experience with Lacuna and a lone player.
