I've got a lot of things I'd like to do right now with my gaming life, enough that I felt like writing them up. I decided to share because maybe, if lots of people put their dreams in one place, 2 people will have the same dream and they will make it happen.

\n
    \n
  • Participate in a one-on-one game for six months, either weekly or bi-weekly
    • \n
  • Role-play a D&D 4E character from first to 30th level - and playing by the rules for experience
    • \n
  • Illustrate my own game
    • \n
  • Join in a group where I am a minority
    • \n
  • Have a tabletop group with one remote player via Skype, using a laptop at the table like Max Headroom or something
    • \n
  • Find a game to do high-action Red Cross stuff: search and rescue and humanitarian aid with minimal or no civilian casualties, but lots of danger
    • \n
  • Play Pendragon
    • \n
  • Take part in an OD&D game via Twitter (oh, yeah, I'm already doing that)
    • \n
  • Start another Saturday or Sunday afternoon cafe game like Wilhelm, John, and I had in Seattle. Playing in public is fantastic.
    • \n
  • Organize an inter-generational group
    • \n
\n

What are your dream gaming goals?

    \n Hmmm. I only have a few I think, at least that I can think of right now. I think it's because over the last few years I've made others come true (namely a Solid weekly gaming group who are into these story-ish games, etc).

    Gaming Goals:

    * Play more, GM less. Encourage my friends to step up.
    * Write at least two small fan-supplements (of about 10 pages, etc) for other RPGs or games that I like (that I DIDN'T design): This could be a replay of my own game, campaign notes sorted into a transferrable form (like the non-rules parts of Lady Blackbird), etc.
    * Run an epic Hellas game every other weekend or so, lasting at least a year. Like Final Fantasy epic-ness but on the tabletop.
    * Then do the same, but with Vampire: Requiem (note, the Hellas game I want to run, this one I'd be content to run or play).
    * Translate Sword World 2.0 into English just enough in order for it to be playable, then play it. A lot. A year later: Alshard. These games are *my* D&D 4e's.
    * Play another game of Tenra Bansho where someone else is running it for me.
    * Play a campaign of D&D 4e set in a non-standard setting, which is Epic, and yet only goes from levels 1-10 (possibly 11, tops).
    * Play something dark and horrific. I'm thinking either The End (d20 Lost Souls), Conspiracy of Shadows (when it's capital D-Done), or Kult.
    * Play or run a fantasy game where, like Shock, everyone at the table owns a facet of the world and is encouraged to create stuff for that element.

    Goals that are related to a sub-hobby of Gaming, the Production/Design of Games:

    * Learn inDesign enough to make decent-looking works. MS Word is solid, but what I want to do only InDesign can provide... but making that leap is a chasm-like leap.
    * Produce a second Story Games Names Book, this one with names, settings and situations.
    * Find some setting inspiration on a game I've been working on for a while (one that stalled due to setting issues), and finish crafting it.

    -Andy

    \n I'll add another:

    *Play less GM more.

    I have a lot of fun when I GM, and I think, even more than the GM's in-game role, I enjoy feeling and being able to act as if I have more power in the group, socially. What I mean is, I can tell people, "hey, shut up, we're playing a game, okay what did you want to do? Okay, everybody listen to him." I can structure play in the more focused style that I enjoy. I don't feel that I have that power as a player. Oh, in a group of friends that know me well, sure I would. But I don't have that group.
