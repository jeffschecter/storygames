    OK, two hours ago, I drew some symbols on my Koplow blank D4s. I purchased some several years ago, just cuz they're neato, but haven't found anything good to use them for.

    \n

    I drew on some little symbols. Some, I wiped off immediately to see if it would work. Others, I left for two hours. They still wipe off, just as easily. Until I wiped them off, they were completely clear \u2014\xa0the pen (I'm using a Pentel, which I find to work better than Expo in general). The edges of the dice wear off with just a little vigorous rolling, but the parts away from the edge (even outside the recessed area) stay pretty clear.

    \n

    So, what I'm thinking about is something like Vincent K. Raven's Bones, where you alter your dice as your resources change. In Bones, you're supposed to have them as a long-term thing, but with dry erase, I don't think it's a good idea.

    \n

    What's neat is that you don't have to put numbers on them. You can have, I dunno, magic, fighting, talking, and seduction as your personal ways of doing things, so you put those icons on your dice in whatever proportions you like as your character changes. Then you roll the dice to see which of those you can do for this conflict, based on the faces that are showing. Your opponent has other stuff written on their dice, and is also rolling. For the sake of the argument, let's say that your dice restrict your own actions to particular actions, and affect broad categories of your opponent's. So, let's say that I have Negotiation and Seduction icons on my dice. Sometimes, one is more appropriate than the other. So I roll and get a lot of faces that say Seduction. That means I have to do certain things to use those dice, but they affect any Social sphere faces that my opponent has for later.

    \n

    I'm imagining that you've got, say, three dice, with a bunch of stuff written on them. The idea is that that stuff changes every time the dice are thrown.

    \n Well, not to give away the farm or anything, but my interest wasn't at all in prototyping really.

    My interest was in dice that could have stuff added, work adequately for at least one play session, then could be changed as players change and create rules of their own between sessions.

    For example, I was positing a d12 with these faces as starter for use with the kiddie protoroleplaying I was talking about in the Breyer horses/Smurfs thread a while back:
    Yes but
    Yes and
    No and
    No but
    Arrow (like on a direction die from GW)
    Danger!
    Friendship!
    New person!
    Meanwhile\u2026
    Strange (Question Mark with sunburst) thing person item event mystery
    Switch! ( Black/white Yin Yang symbol) Good and Bad guys switch behavior or something
    Compromise!

    But rather than have those permanently on the faces, I'd want something where the faces could be replaced or oprtions doubled, tripled or whatever, or entirely new result faces created.
