    \n There's no GM.

    Players set up scenes for each other, following instructions and guidelines given in the game. In the first part of the game, these scenes are from specific characters' childhood and youth. We get to know them, their background, how they became what they did.

    Later in the game, the scenes are about the Society as a whole. In this part, the board is used in a ouija-like fashion: Players choose scene names together from the board, and whoever's setting up the scene uses those names as inspiration for the scene. They also have the mysterious "Notes from a different society" as guidelines; these are often ambiguous, and require interpretation on some level before use.

    The game encourages multiform; when picking and planning scenes, players sit around the table; when role-playing, they get up and act out the scenes.

    The aim of the game is for the Society to investigate and confront the mnemosites. In each scene, they may establish facts about the mnemosites; in the last half of the game, these facts are reincorporated.

    Every game, the society and the mnemosites are different.

    \n Matthijs, I have the game printed as well, waiting to be read. If I play it at any point I promise to give some feedback, either here or elsewhere (maybe denmark?). Reason the game is still unread is being busy, writing, working, designing. Based on my experience with gamers, taking a new game, reading it and then putting it to action takes ages. It doesn't seem to matter if it's downloaded or bought, anyway it takes a while for it to sink in. Quickest this has happened to me was with Ithras By, it only took a month or two Longest period was with venerable Vampire, it took seven years..

    So the impact of distributing it freely for a while might be beneficial, but I don't think you will see immidiate difference. Let's say I read it, like it, recomend it and someone (or my self) buys it, it might take a year. Multiply by your downloads and divide by five (since some forget about it, others didn't like it, some liked it but don't want to pay money and the rest will purchase it or promote it). You can always hope for the best and as you know my darling I will let you know how I liked it when I'm through with it.
