BULLY PULPIT GAMES ANNOUNCES DURANCE

Two-time Diana Jones Award winner adds new game to Bully Pulpit Games\u2019 lineup

Chapel Hill, North Carolina: Bully Pulpit Games LLC, publisher of Fiasco and other award-winning games, announced the upcoming release of Durance, Jason Morningstar\u2019s unique take on life in a far-future penal colony.

\u201cDurance is a game about the limits of power,\u201d Morningstar said, \u201cthe key ingredients are savagery and servility, and where better to explore these concepts than a prison? And what makes a better prison than a backwater planet on the other side of the Great Rift?\u201d

Durance is a fast-paced, low-prep, highly collaborative game in the tradition of Fiasco. Players in Durance assume the dual roles of convicts and guards trying to survive on an outrageously hostile planet. The thin veneer that separates the two classes tends to erode during play, raising questions about corruption, justice and mercy.

Book layout for Durance will be handled by Fred Hicks, multiple Origins award winner and graphic designer behind such visually striking titles as Don't Rest Your Head and Spirit of the Century. "I've been admiring Bully Pulpit from afar for a long while" said Hicks. "I'm truly excited to have a chance to collaborate with them up close."

Hicks is formulating a unique vision for the game\u2019s look and feel. "Durance is about a hard, brutal place, founded on ideals of order, descending into chaos. As the project rolls forward, I'm going to be thinking about how to reflect that in the layout."

The art team consists of a pair of seasoned veterans. Interior art will be by Brennen Reece, with a full color cover by Jesse Parotti. "I'm beyond thrilled to be working with these guys," said Bully Pulpit Games' Steve Segedy, "Brennen is bringing a sensibility and style that is going to reflect the material in an interesting and genuinely artful way, and Jesse is technically brilliant and seems to understand the game on an almost disturbing level."

Bully Pulpit Games will be taking advantage of the popular service Kickstarter to facilitate a pre-order of the game in the spring of 2012. \u201cOne of the best parts of Fiasco was getting to see all the really great ideas that came from fans of the game, and so we\u2019d like to capture that energy from the beginning for Durance,\u201d Segedy said. \u201cWe\u2019ve seen and backed some great projects on Kickstarter, and it\u2019s the perfect way to get people involved with the game right from the start.\u201d

Durance will be released and available in stores in summer 2012.

    Posted By: Sam!Alien 3is definitely a good prison planet. As are Butcher Bay as depicted inThe Chronicles of Riddick: Escape From Butcher Bayand the hell-pit that is Crematoria, from the second film.
    \nDavid Twohy knows prison planets. His draft of Alien 3 introduced the prison planet idea to the series before he went on to write Pitch Black and Chronicles of Riddick. The Riddick stories, including Butcher Bay, of course, seem to be about a galaxy full of prison planets and bounty hunters, a aerospace-prison-industrial complex gone haywire. The second Riddick game, with its prisoners enslaved as combat drones, suggests everyone is constantly taking everyone else prisoner\u2014it's a universe where that kind of power is being flexed all the time, whether you're a pirate or a Necromonger. One wonders how big the law-abiding population is in the Riddick universe; is it staggeringly large or achingly, satirically small?

    All of which is sideways path to reiterating my enthusiasm for Durance, which I have not yet played.
