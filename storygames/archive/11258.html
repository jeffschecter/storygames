    \n
    \n I think it all depends on why I became a specific city's guardian in the first place...
    \n
    \nOption 1 - Compassion: Let's say a mortal once did me a service out of the kindness of their heart, or sacrificed themselves in my service or something. Ever since, I've been looking out for their descendants. I taught them to write and fish and predict the weather. I helped them build a city with running water, sewers, and high walls. I intercede on their behalf with the merciless gods of nature. All I ask in return is that they honor their ancestor's memory and try to live by their example.
    \n
    \nOption 2 - Mutual Benefit: I need mortals for something, probably either ritual sacrifices or as an army to use against other deities. My primary concern is safeguarding my investment. I send plagues and storms against their rivals, teach them how to build more devastating weapons, and reward those who die in battle. I demand complete devotion from my priests and soldiers, but allow the under-classes to be pretty well exploited.
    \n
    \nOption 3 - Duty: There's some kind of celestial bureaucracy and I've been assigned to administer this island. If I do well, I might get promoted to a more important post. If my city flounders, I'll be kicked down to God of Slime Molds so fast my head will spin. I focus on ensuring the economic well-being of my city by creating strict divisions of labor and teaching them all kinds of trade skills. I speed their ships across the ocean and protect merchant caravans. I judge those convicted of crimes, maybe through trial by combat, and punish laziness above all else.
    \n
    \nAaaand... that's all I got for now.

    \nI don't know. Who DO you answer to if you mess up and everything comes crashing down?


    Anyway! Thanks for all this stuff, guys. It's been real helpful.

    And this is what I'm going to use it for: I'm planning to do an Apocalypse World hack. Floating islands stuck in raging blizzards and dust storms, fighty age-old aristocracies, slaves and freemen brought in from the Under, flying merchant ships. And deities that take care of these little islands, of course.

    The city-deity is a standin for Apocalypse World's Hardholder, obviously. Didn't want to say that straight off, though - would have coloured the initial "I am a city-god!" replies too much... And I want to toy around with the concepts beyond just doing a slight colour-rehash, so I *needed* that initial colour.

    And I love what I got! This is what I saw, tell me if this makes sense:

    A possibility of ridiculous excess. Hailstorms, floods, constructing giant towers, having the city paved with marble breasts, get people to sacrifice their stuff and then themselves to you. Gods need to be able to do crazy things and get away with it.

    Messing with the mortals for shits and giggles. Whispers into their ears, lies they take for truth, and so on.

    Demanding devotion from the mortals and sculpting them into who they are, as a people. Telling them what the rules of life are, building a priesthood around yourself, training soldiers, making the island itself a place of mystery, so on.

    Being bound either by contractual legalesee or by belonging to a higher order of gods. Painful, irritating obligation you want to get out of, but can't.

    And probably something I've missed.


    I'll probably deal with the godly powers/obligations bit the way hardholder/operator/hocus moves are done. You do a roll - if you win it, you get surplus for the duration, if you lose, you get want. So you could get something like... Tyrant: You sow fear into the hearts of men (+1 Harm) / You are loathed, and someone stands up to you. Or Dreamtender: You succesfully plant visions (and get the result of a succesful manipulation roll just by talking to people) / Your lies are exposed. And so on and so forth.
