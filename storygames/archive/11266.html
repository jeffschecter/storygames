\n Episode 2 Infiltration Begins http://apap.libsyn.com/
\n
\nIn this Episode we continue the discussion of our ongoing Burning Empires game, specifically our first session of maneuvers in the Infection Phase. We cover our experience with Burning Empires' learning curve, Duels of Wits, and Fire Fights.
\n
\nWe have settled into our format of summary of fiction, general discussion, player focused discussion, and finally GM focused discussion. This episode runs for 31 minutes. Our target length for the show is 30 minutes, so you should expect most future episodes to be approximately the same length.
\n
\nA slight word of warning, I had to remove some noise during the first couple minutes of this episode. A side affect of doing this is the audio is slightly distorted during that time. The audio clears up after minute two.

    \n I've enjoyed both episodes so far. I really like the democratic way you discuss the games, and the bouncing back-and-forth of ideas is great. You all seem to be working out issues that crop up in play, and to do so in a public forum is a great idea. I'd love to have "debrief" sessions like this with my own group, even just in private! I suspect not only will your understanding of the relationships between elements of games and actual play improve, but so will your respective personal appreciations of game design choices and how they were _intended_ to influence play.

    I have just a few (hopefully constructive) bits of criticism:
    1) You all need to work on your "radio voices" .You're speaking slowly and clearly enough, I think, but every once in a while it sounds like you are all wearing retainers. I don't know if perhaps you _are_ wearing retainers, or if it's a microphone issue, but there's a slurping noise on some of the sibilants. .
    2) You discuss the AP well enough, but I keep waiting for one of you to make the critical/analytical step back from play to the rules. I thought the debate over the virtues of lifepaths and character burning would've been a great way to segue into the merits of lifepaths in general, as opposed to other forms of character creation.

    Other than that, I'm really digging it; I've added your show to my RSS feed, and I'm looking forward to the next one!

    Edited because I noticed you'd addressed one of my comments in the post above.
