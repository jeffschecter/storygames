    \n My history with the Doctor(s) is appropriately nonlinear. I think I started with Tennant's first season when it aired, then simultaneously worked my way forwards and backwards with Tennant, Eccleston and McGann. Then it was Tom Baker time, followed by Hartnell, Davidson, McCoy, Pertwee, Troughton, more or less in that order.

    There's something about Tom, no doubt. It doesn't matter how old you are, he has this intoxicating energy onscreen, a mad genius. He is fantastic. Despite that, Sylvester McCoy is my favourite Doctor. Yes, the show was dying when he got on and he had some really shitty scripts to work with, but I just love love love 7th's run.

    I'm loving Matt Smith so far, although I'd prefer if they toned down the silly a bit. Especially the console (and don't get me started on the phone). But sometimes I watch old episodes and lament the loss of distilled pulp genius that they contained.

    Yeah, I'm a Who nerd.

    \n I'm also a big Tom Baker fan. He was the one who started me off on Doctor Who back in the day, and he still comprises the largest part of my VHS and DVD collection. He had great lines, great companions, and some truly memorable villains. And some memorable walk-ins, like John Cleese appearing in "City of Death," critiquing the Tardis as a piece of modern art.
    It was also cool seeing his cameos here and there, such as the aforementioned episode of Black Adder, and the role he had in one of the BBC versions of Narnia (can't remember which at the moment, since it's been so long).

    My favorite line, definitely has to be from "Talons of Weng-Chiang" where he says, "Sleep is for tortoises." I repeated that many a time in university when people would stop by my dorm room to find me still awake, hopped up on Mountain Dew and caffeine pills.

    And oddly enough, Sylvester McCoy has to be my second. Yeah, he was given little to work with, some crappy scripts, and he started off with one of the crappiest companions. But McCoy's charisma almost helped to keep the show alive, with his impish little grin and bumbling ways. Almost as if you took Patrick Troughton and merged him with a bit of Tom Baker.
    And, after meeting him in person in Seattle earlier this year, my respect for him has grown immensely. I look forward to his role in "The Hobbit."

    I'd probably enjoy the new series if it WASN'T for Amy Pond (who seems to be the main star, instead of Matt Smith), and some of the ludicrous ways they solve things (really? Telling the Dalek-cyborg scientist to remember his past to disarm the bomb? Really?)
