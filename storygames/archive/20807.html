    \n Well, of course I'm partial to Thus began the adventures of Eowyn..., since it was written precisely for this purpose, and is a solid, fun game.

    I also think Dogs in the Vineyard is somewhat overlooked as a 1-player game, and could give you lots to hang onto in that context.

    Showdown is a fun somewhat competitive 1-on-1 game.

    2-player gaming is a bit of a niche thing which is hard to find good games for; hopefully some people will come along to suggest specific 1-on-1 RPGs any minute now...

    I have heard good things about Mars Colony.
    \nMars Colony is a great game. My partner and I have played a lot of it, because it hits some of our favorite themes pretty exactly - it's a perfect exemplar of what we both love in RPGs.
    But we're also a couple who met through RPGs. I don't know whether Mars Colony is any good for first-time roleplayers. It probably depends on their enthusiasm for the subject matter, first and foremost.\n
    Maybe S/lay w/me would be good if it isn't to sexually oriented and cheesy.
    \nThat's a great game if you both are into swords & sorcery fiction and if you both find that kind of fiction sexy. If she doesn't already like swords & sorcery, I don't think she'll especially enjoy the game.
    I'm not sure what you mean by "sexually oriented" - it is a bit of a "flirting" game, deep at its core, but this is the part of it which is basically warranted to work OK with a romantic partner: you're probably comfortable flirting with each other already.
    It isn't "cheesy", unless you make it so - but if someone's familiarity with the literary sources is limited to cheesy body-builder movies from the 1980s then, yeah, of course anything s&s will feel "cheesy" to them.

    \n s/lay w/me has a romantic and/or sexual subplot, but that's very different from being "too sexually oriented". It's a free narration game, so nothing about it is ever going to force you to go into uncomfortable territory. I believe the only thing mandated by the game is that there is a potential romantic interest for the main character; anything beyond that is on you.
