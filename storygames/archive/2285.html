Define "Focussed Design"

Troy_Costisick
January 2007 edited January 2007 in Story Games
Heya,

There's a term growing in use more and more. I'm starting to see it on blogs (Nate P.'s and Brennan's for instance) and in occasional use in threads. It's "Focussed Design." To me, it's a term that means essentially the same thing when people say "indie RPG" and don't mean "creator published." But I'm not sure I'm using that term correctly. So I'm putting it out there. Those who use it, what does it mean to you? What should it mean to me as I read it in your writing?

Peace,

-Troy

(PS: I don't give a damn if you think 'Focussed Design" or 'Indie RPGs' are good or bad. This thread isn't for oppinions, just word usage.)
  • Matt Snyder

    I don't generally use the term. When I do see it, I understand it to mean designs that are extremely tight, um, aesthetically. That is, games that are about a very narrow set of color, characters and situations. And, they feature a system that only does one thing, but usually does one thing well.

    So, for example, The Mountain Witch is about samurai and Legend of the Five Rings is about samurai. But, The Moutain Witch is about a specific samurai situation, with specific kinds of characters interacting with each others in a certain way.

    In such usage, I see people use it occasionally as a complaint or criticism, presumably because they can't "do what the want" with the game.

    Inclusive definition:

    1. A focused design includes rules which target specific design problems.

    And that definition is irrespective of whether a focused design process
    was employed in the development of the game. However...

    Other definition:

    2. focused design also means that such a process, e.g. The Power 19, was indeed employed
    in the games' design. As the purpose of such design exercises is to sort out that-which the designer
    is to focus-on.

    So...

    The former definition applies to any game which apparently contains at least one focus.

    The latter applies to any game in-which at least one focused design-method was apparently incorporated.
