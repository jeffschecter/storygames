\n Hey folks,

It's two weeks before the deadline (Nov. 22!), and I just wanted to see where people are at. How are things going? What snags are you running into? Are you done already? Between the public design threads and blogs and behind-the-scenes chatter it looks like a lot of cool stuff is coming together. Which is fantastic!

Also, if you have hit a point where you're all "crap, there's no way I'm actually going to get this done," there are a couple of folks in the wings that have expressed interest in participating but missed the tapping-in phase for various reasons. We've already had one participant take over a game (Ben Miller took over City of Refuge from Dave Cleaver, who has, like, a house to deal with right now), so there's a precedent for anyone else who's flagging.

Finally, if you are posting design threads, I encourage you to take them over to the Praxis section of the site (that far right tab on the top there) - let's be good Story Games citizens!

So....how's it going?

    \nThis is great, Micah! My daughter and I play a similar game with bed time stories. I think the simple rules, like "don't interrupt" and "keep going" are just the right thing for children. These are the foundations upon which later gaming experiences will be built.

    I'm interested in your comment about the difference between your two children. My six-year-old has a similar difficulties with rules-guided storytelling. I think it's a combination of what's age-appropriate and what fits their personality. In our house the rule is that you can ask a question, which is an invitation for the other to continue the story (like "what was has name?" or "and what did she see there?") The rule is, if you ask, you don't get to nay-say the response. I like to think I'm teaching her not to deprotagonize.
