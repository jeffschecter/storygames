"Dice-Free" instead of "Diceless"

\n
\n
\n
\n\nDanielSolis \n\n\n
\n
\n\nSeptember 2007 \n edited September 2007 in Story Games
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n Just a quick little thing.

For years, I've taken to calling RPGs without dice "dice-free" rather than "diceless." The latter implies that a game without dice is missing something. It is somehow less. The former simply states the absence of dice. I don't personally have much opinion on the present debates over the definition of freeform, but I thought that some folks those discussions would like this term.

Just tossin' it out there.
