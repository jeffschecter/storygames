\nXeno killing Endgame
This is my pitch of an Apocalypse World hack of Fantasy Flights Warhammer 40.000 RPG Deathwatch. Deathwatch (DW) is a game where the players take the roles of gene-enhanced super warrior monks battling the enemies of mankind\u2019s fascistoid Imperium. That star spanning colossal being the last line of defense between humanity and certain extinction. Since the players take the guise of glory seeking warrior monks coming from different chapters, a lot of internal rivalry is to be expected, and is also somewhat catered for in the rules.

However, I feel that the game really falls on its face when it comes to the action part. Instead of being the action filled romp it ought to be, filled with oath taking, boasting and \u2018for the Emperor\u2019-s it is a sluggish, book-keeping fest of future world simulation. Not at all what I had expected when I opened the book. Since having played through a small campaign which died out due to the tedium of the rules I have been searching for a game system that would allow me to play DW the way I had imagined it would be.

Reading on the forum that someone has started a re-skinning of the \u201cThe Regiment\u201d-hack I realize that AW is probably the system I have been looking for. In AW reality is brutal and unforgiving and everything is tethering on the brink of oblivion. At the same time relations between the characters are important and help drive the story forward. This coincides with how I see how DW should be. But The Regiment introduces a more fine-tuned combat system than AW, a more simulating approach if you will. That is exactly what I want away from in Deathwatch.

Considering that the subject matter is 7\u2019 tall super human warrior monks 38.000 years into the future simulating reality shouldn\u2019t be among the concerns of the game. Creating a fast paced action focused game where questions of valor, glory and chapter history are more important than how large the percentile gain is of a night vision scope when firing through smoke on the other hand, should.
So this leads me to using the original AW as basis for my hack. When I found Mike Sands\u2019 \u201cThe Juggernaut\u201d playbook I knew I had something.

Why I originally had not thought about doing an AW hack is that the original playbooks are very singular in how the players create their roles, whereas in DW character creation is very binary; i.e. Chapter/Class. But now I believe I have a solution. It came to me as I sat folding playerbooks for tonight\u2019s game of AW. My printer at home is unable to print both sides so I simply printed them and then folded them back to back. Holding them in my hands I realized how I could combine the cool playbooks with the binary Space Marines of the future. I\u2019ll make one side of the playbook the class and the other side the chapter!

How I will do this is probably have the class on the outside, so it gets a cool class picture (Assault marine, Techmarine etc.), Armour/weapon page for class-appropriate weapons, and a Create class page (where you choose some specials/moves which are transferred to the inside). The inside will have the creating chapter choice page, a stats and death move (they\u2019re not supposed to have sex so they\u2019ll get cool death moves instead, I don\u2019t know who came up with the idea, but I \u2018ve seen it somewhere before) page and a moves page.

Given as some chapters can\u2019t choose certain classes in DW I will keep to that spirit and let the stats be dependent on chapter choice rather than class choice. As for stats I think I\u2019ll replace \u201cHot\u201d with \u201cHonor\u201d and \u201cWeird\u201d with \u201cAttuned\u201d, as in attuned to the warp (courtesy of Star Wars World's Attuned to the Warp I believe).

A space marine is dangerous without his armour but with it he turns into a capable killing machine. I\u2019m not sure how it should be reflected in the rules, but my first idea is that each class gets a modifier in tune with its role. I.e: Assault marine (grisly trophies) +1 hard, Tactical Marine (Glorious standard) +1 Honor, Librarian (Aegis collar) +1 Attuned etc. Each suit of armour will also have a strength and a weakness chosen from a list, much as The Juggernaut\u2019s armour.

\u201cExperience\u201d will be called Glory.

For the classes I only have some sketchy ideas so far:

Tactical marine is supposed to be the leader type much as in DW. I\u2019m trying to figure out a way to combine the hardholder/operator way of influencing the game by a start of the session mechanic. Or at least start of the mission mechanic where the Tac marine\u2019s moves define what kind of support their operation will get. I guess I\u2019ll have to take a closer look at The Regiment for this. Otherwise the Tactical marine is supposed to have moves centered around leadership and tactical issues.

Assault marine is all about aggression.

Techmarine will be using the savvyhead as a basis but I will try to work in the human/dehumanized dichotomy as well.

Devestator is all about bringing the big guns to bear. But not just massive destruction, there will be a suppressive bit too, a bit less aggressive and more tactical.

Apothecary is like the angel. Of death, if he has to remove the gene-seed\u2026

Librarian is highly attuned to the warp but I believe the use should be more offensive with a high risk/high reward component.

I haven\u2019t started on the chapters yet. Blood Angels, Dark Angels, Space Wolves, Ultramarines, Black Templars, and the Storm Wardens are the ones featured in the core rulebook. Imperial Fists and 15 successor chapters were featured in Rites of Battle and Iron Hands, Salamanders, Raven Guard and White Scars were featured in First Founding .

I like the DW distinction of solo/squad abilities but I think I\u2019ll dumb them down.

Fighting hordes is easily managed by the gang-mechanic from AW.

I haven\u2019t started looking at basic moves yet, but one idea is exchange "Read Sitch" with some kind of Tactical Appraisal.

Well, this is what I\u2019ve got so far. Ideas, criticism?

TL;DNR: Deathwatch rules unnecessarily complicated, AW rules fit the format, combine for the win! Or?

    Your armor is an exoskeleton driven by direct neural connection to your black carapace implant affording a full range of movement while still maintaining protection against whatever the universe might throw at you.

    Armor history
    Benefit (choose one):
    Worn by veterans, +1 Honor
    Many of your chapter\u2019s foremost heroes have at some point worn this suit of armor and it lends gravitas to your actions. Heavy lies the burden on your shoulders to carry this legacy, as you have no wish to be the first to lose it or ruin it beyond redemption (Burdened by glory).

    Out of time, +1 Sharp
    Your suit of armor is of an old make but in mint condition. The tech-savants of your chapter cannot answer how this happened to be but you have sworn not to ruin this irreplaceable piece of technological marvel (Pristine).

    Rewired, +1 Cool
    Your suit of armor is a tool of your trade, honed to perfection. You have had the tech-savants rewire the electro fibre bundles to increase your reflex speed and recalibrate the targeting array to increase accuracy. All the while chanting and applying the right unguents to appease the machine spirit (Unstable).

    Bloodthirsty, +1 Hard
    The machine spirit of you armor is savage, steeped in aeons of warfare. It readily assists its bearer to apply the Emperor\u2019s justice to the enemies of mankind but sometimes the targeter auto-acquires by itself (Unreliable)

    Drawback (choose one):
    Mix and match (Prone to breakdown)
    Your suit of armor is made up of parts originating from many different marks and sometimes the adaptations fail.

    Power hungry (Prone to brownouts)
    The power source of your armor can barely bear the load of normal operation but so far the machine spirit has refused to cooperate in exchanging it.

    Heavy Metal (Really heavy)
    Your armor is of an old make and much heavier than newer suits. This can turn into a real hazard when crossing unstable ground or traversing ruins.

    Unproven (Newly issued)
    Your armour has not become like a second skin and an old friend yet. Until you have acquired five improvements you need six boxes of Glory to improve.

    I was making a deathwatch regiment hack too.
    I was coming up with my own stat trying to figure out what is important:
    Stats
    The Stats used for this hack
    A Space Marine has the following stats
    Fight: your ability to combat in close quarter. Furious. Aggressive/Forceful
    Gunfire: your ability to shoot and move in a gunfire. Methodical and calm.
    Will: your ability to resist stress and act effectively when under pressure. Reflexive/Graceful
    Leadership: your ability to impress and command respect from people. Persuasive/Assertive
    Tactics: your ability to assess situations, notice details and devise strategies. Calculating/Methodical

    I also came up with some basic moves

    Basic Battle Moves
    Act under fire or Hold fast (Will)
    When you act under fire or hold fast under fire, roll+Will.
    On a 10+, you do it.
    On a 7-9, the GM picks one:
    You do it, but you\u2019re pinned or suppressed, or both.
    You do it, but you\u2019re Hesitant. Take -1 forward.\x83
    You do it, but you take incidental fire.
    You do it, but you spend one Gear
    A hard choice created by the GM.
    Assess the situation (Tactics)
    When you assess the situation, roll+Tactics. On a hit, ask the GM questions about the tactical situation.
    On a 10+, ask three.
    On a 7-9, ask one.
    By the Emperor\u2019s will! (Fate)
    When you are trying something that is desperate and seemingly doomed to fail for mortal men Roll+Fate and mark xp.
    On a 10+, by the Emperor! ...that worked!
    On a 7-9, uh, close enough! Now you\u2019re in a spot, though.
    On a miss, Brother, you are in deep trouble (GM is going to do an hard move on you).
    Close Assault (Fight)
    When you make a close assault, roll+Fight. You always receive an enemy assault using the enemy VOF.
    On a 10+, you choose:
    Inflict great harm (increase your VOF)
    Decrease enemy VOF

    On a 7-9, the enemy chooses one:
    Make a stand and trade harm for harm.\x83\x83
    Make an orderly withdrawal to a fallback position.
    On a miss you could be surrounded by enemy horde or be put at disadvantage (increase enemy VOF as you are open to strikes or -1 forward as you are limited or constrained)
    Open fire (Gunfire)
    When you attack the enemy, roll+Gunfire.
    On a 10+, you inflict your Harm as established.
    On a 7-9, the GM chooses one:
    You inflict harm, but you have to expose yourself to danger to do it.\x83\x83
    You inflict harm, but it takes a lot of shooting (-1gear).\x83\x83
    You inflict harm, but not accurately (-1 VOF).
    Rally(Leadership)
    When you rally, roll+Leadership.
    On a 10+, you choose two.
    On a 7-9, choose one:
    -\t (\u201cNot a step back, Brothers! FOR THE EMPEROR!\u201d) You and those you rally get a 1-forward for that task.
    -\t(\u201cDon\u2019t let them get the better of you! We\u2019re the Emperor\u2019s chosen!\u201d) You and those you rally clear one Condition.
    -\tYou and your fellow marines enter Squad Mode (only if you are the leader).

    hope these could be useful

    \n Oh, those look cool. I haven't had time to really read through The Regiment yet, but one aspect of it that I didn't like is that the rules for fighting is more complicated than in AW. Seeing as the overcomplication in DW is what I really disliked.

    As Space Marines are supposed to be superhuman killing machines ordinary battles should in my view be resolved in a few dice rolls and then acted out, while the boss battles and overcoming insane odds should be the meat and potatoes of the game. Paired with camp brotherhood-affirming interaction between the players of course.

    So while I like the new stats you have I think I\u2019ll just remake Hot into Honor and Hard into Fight and see where it gets me.

    As for making new moves this is more like what I had in mind. I haven\u2019t got down to actually thinking about which moves there should be, but I\u2019ll gladly use the ones you provided as inspiration.

    I have been thinking of the agenda for the MC and the principles for the game. Deathwatch is very much made to play in an old fashioned way. I think this ties in with it being an inheritor of the system of the inquisitor rpg. I.e. the GM sets up a secret that the players try to figure out. Just that in DW the secret is instead the GM sets up a mission, often with false or incomplete intel to complicate things further on, and the players try to survive and succeed at the mission.

    This playing style could be done in AW by changing the Agenda of the MC. But I\u2019m leaning more towards some kind of session-start process of determining what kind of mission should be played and what kind of opposition will turn up. That would gear more towards AWs Play to find out what happens for both the players and the MC.

    This stems mostly from me being sick and tired of coming up with all the stuff as GM. And that I\u2019ve found that it increases the emotional investment of the players in their characters if they get to do world building too. It also ties in with being a fan of the characters. If you are a fan, you really want to know what they\u2019re doing.

    So nothing new from me, but I\u2019m still working on it.

    \n I actually read through The Regiment 2.5 and it's a nice game. So I stole some ideas from it and here's my first rough of how the missions are made in AW/DW:

    Session start:
    Unit leader selection
    Either the characters agree on who is unit leader or they get to roll off against each other. Roll +Honor with the character scoring the highest result being the unit leader. If you roll off take -1 to the mission aspects stage.

    Should every character rolling for unit leadership fail (roll 6 or less) take additional -1 to the mission aspects stage for all the discord caused and roll again.

    Naturally characters not wanting to lead can either aid or interfere with others vying for the position. If you want to aid or interfere with the unit leader selection roll +Hx:
    10+ you add +1 or deduct -1 from the chosen character\u2019s roll and add or deduct the same amount from the Hx with that same character.
    7-9 deduct -1 from the chosen character\u2019s roll and deduct the same amount from the Hx with that same character OR take -1 to the mission aspects stage, player choice.

    Mission aspects
    The Unit leader rolls +Cool and subtracts eventual -1s from the unit leader selection:
    10+ Unit leader chooses four traits from the list, MC chooses one trait from each aspect not chosen
    7-9 Unit leader chooses two traits from the list, MC chooses one trait from each aspect not chosen
    6- MC chooses three traits, Unit leader chooses two wants and one trait from each other aspect not chosen

    Mission aspects list
    Mission Planet: Agri World, Arid World, Dead World, Death World, Hive World, High-G World, Low-G World, Poisoned Atmosphere, Forge World, Recruiting World, Maiden World, Shrine World, Water World, Volcanic World, Space Hulk, Daemon World

    Mission Want: Unsupported, Bad Intel, Deadline, Bad Odds, Stealth Required, No Resupply, No Extraction Available

    Mission Objective: Retrieval, Penal, Reconnaissance, Hold the line, Assistance, Infiltration, Ambush

    Mission Enemies: Chaos Marines, Orks, Renegade Guard, Tau, Tau sympathizers, Eldar, Dark Eldar, Necrons, Tyranids, Renegade Marines, Daemons, Renegade Mechanicus.

    I'm thinking of adding some way for the Unit leader to influence what the MC chooses but I'm not sure what the cost should be. If it should be simply added wants or the possibility for the MC to spring surprises later on.

    There should possibly be more Mission Objectives and Wants to choose from.

    I'm also thinking of adding Mission Surplus, but maybe that should only be activated by a tactical Marine specialty?

    \n Ok, so I reworked the above bit and added deployment type, opposition and mission surprises. I think it'll work nicely to set up the mission. Now I just have to figure out some specialties for the tactical marine that will influence this setup...

    Session start:
    Unit leader selection
    Each Deathwatch Killteam has a leader. Often this role falls to the most honored of the battle brothers but experience speaks volumes too.

    Either the characters agree on who is unit leader or they get to roll off against each other. Roll +Honor with the character scoring the highest result being the unit leader. If you roll off take -1 to the mission aspects stage.

    Should every character rolling for unit leadership fail (roll 6 or less) take additional -1 to the mission aspects stage for all the discord caused and roll again.

    Naturally characters not wanting to lead can either aid or interfere with others vying for the position. If you want to aid or interfere with the unit leader selection roll +Hx:
    10+ you add +1 or deduct -1 from the chosen character\u2019s roll and add or deduct the same amount from the Hx with that same character.
    7-9 deduct -1 from the chosen character\u2019s roll and deduct the same amount from the Hx with that same character OR take -1 to the mission aspects stage, player choice.

    Mission aspects
    The mission aspects define the what, where and how\u2019s of the mission the watch captain is about to send the heroes upon.

    The Unit leader rolls +Cool and subtracts eventual -1s from the unit leader selection:
    10+ Unit leader chooses four traits from the list, MC chooses one trait from each aspect not chosen
    7-9 Unit leader chooses two traits from the list, MC chooses one trait from each aspect not chosen
    6- MC chooses three traits, Unit leader chooses two wants and one trait from each other aspect not chosen

    Mission aspects list
    Mission Planet: Agri World, Arid World, Dead World, Daemon World, Death World, Forge World, Hive World, High-G World, Low-G World, Maiden World, Poisoned Atmosphere, Recruiting World, Shrine World, Water World, Volcanic World, Space Hulk.

    Mission Objective: Ambush, Assassination, Assistance, Hold the line, Infiltration, Reconnaissance, Retaliatory, Retrieval.

    Mission Enemies: Chaos Marines, Daemons, Dark Eldar, Eldar, Necrons, Orks, Renegade Guard, Renegade Mechanicus, Renegade Marines, Tau, Tau Sympathizers, Tyranids.

    Mission Deployment: Aerial (Thunderhawk), Ground (Rhino), Ground Assault (Land Raider), Instant (Teleportation), Orbital (Droppod), Other (Rock drill transport, Submarine, etc.), Stealthed (Gravchutes).

    Mission Want: Bad Intel, Bad Odds, Deadline, No Extraction Available, No Resupply, Stealth Required, Unstable Warp, Unsupported, Warzone.

    Opposition
    The mission aspects delineate the outline of the mission while the level of opposition is a possibility for the characters to influence the mission through trade with the GM.

    The level of opposition is a scale where \u201cNormal\u201d is the zero and \u201cLight\u201d and \u201cInsane\u201d are the extremes. As you can see, if translated to numbers it goes from -1 to +4:

    Level of opposition: Light, Normal, Increased, High, Extreme, Insane

    Normal is where the level of opposition starts. For each step to the right (i.e. increase in opposition) that the Unit leader moves the level of opposition one of the traits chosen by the MC on the Mission aspects list can be removed. If the trait is the only one of that aspect it can instead be changed into another of the same aspect, thereby fulfilling the requirement of there being at least one trait from each aspect.

    For each step to the left (i.e. decrease in opposition) that the Unit leader moves the level of opposition the MC gets to choose another mission want OR a Mission Surprise (to the maximum of two Mission Surprises).

    Mission Surprises
    If the Unit leader has traded for decreased opposition the MC has a choice of taking Mission Surprises. A Mission Surprise is simply a trait chosen from the Mission Aspects list which is not revealed until applicable. This Surprise may or may not override previous traits or just simply add new traits, at the MC\u2019s discretion. Up to two Mission Surprises may be chosen but they must be chosen from different aspects.

    \n As for the stats this is my brief:
    Fight (Hard) - The stat to use when engaged in fighting, be it ranged or melee.

    Honor (Hot) - DW has no real use of Hot, on the other hand honor, old relics and stories of former glories are the only things Space Marines respect. These are also the things they use to impress allies (Imperiel Guard et al.) and try to impress aliens with. Hence Honor.

    Cool - Level headed. Which comes naturally for Space Marines after decades of brainwashing and battle field experience. But some have more of it. This stat is used when reading a situation\u2019s tactical possibilities. This is also the stat used when mission planning.

    Attuned (Weird) \u2013 Opening your head to the warp. Anyone can do it, but only the librarian should\u2026

    Technosavant (Sharp-ish) \u2013 Knowledgeable of technological objects, what unguents and chants appease which machine spirits etc.

    My reasoning behind the glaring mashup of what was cool and sharp is twofold: Firstly Space Marines are by definition not going to lose their wits and will never have to roll for doing things under fire. On the other hand some might enjoy fighting too much to take a level headed appraisal of the situation. Hence Cool is used to read a sitch.

    Secondly Sharp is too broad to use as the Techmarine focused stat. On the other hand all stats are broad in their application, but the DW setting is a sci-fi setting steeped deep in technological superstition. It also fits very well with the Space Marine belief that to use alien technology is to promote heresy. Hence the quite narrow stat of Techsavant. The other specialties have low numbers in this stat since they have a more superstitious/disinterested relationship to their battle gear.

    These are my general ideas for the Specialties. Where there is a slash in the focused stat this is to denote different ways in which the player might take the character. I'm going to have players choose from two different solo abilities and squad mode abilities (that's what the OR is for) from the specialty and from two each from the chapter choice. So each character will end up with two possible solo- and two possible squad mode abilities. We'll see how it goes.

    Apothecary
    Triage, close range support and the emperor\u2019s blessing (i.e. end the suffering of compatriots) is the focus of the Apothecary.
    Focused stat: Cool
    Mechanic: Narthecium (First aid and gene seed removal)
    Solo ability: OR
    Squad Mode (if Unit Leader): OR

    Stats (choose one column)
    Fight (Hard) 1/2/0
    Honor (Hot) 0/1/1
    Cool 3/2/3
    Attuned (Weird) 0/0/0
    Techsavant 1/0/1

    Assault Marine
    The Assault Marine specialty brings the pure melee-centric hatred to the enemies of humankind.
    Focused stat: Fight
    Mechanic:
    Solo ability: OR
    Squad Mode (if Unit Leader): OR

    Devestator
    The Devestator deals in either long range heavy destruction or tactical supression and back up.
    Focused stat: Fight/Cool (damage dealing/tactical suppression)
    Mechanic:
    Solo ability: OR
    Squad Mode (if Unit Leader): OR

    Librarian
    The Librarian channels the dangerous powers of the warp to destroy enemies of humanity.
    Focused stat: Attuned
    Mechanic:
    Solo ability: OR

    Techmarine
    The Techmarine straddles the thin line between enhanced human and unhuman cyborg and is the link between the Space Marines and the tech-priests of Mars. His charge is to keep the unit's battlegear in fighting condition and to keep them on the good side of the machine spirits.

    The Techmarine\u2019s signature stat is Techsavant as that is his domain. But you can also aim for Attuned if you want the mystic priesthood angle to be noticeable. The techmarine\u2019s willingness to improve in his fleshly form is also prevalent in the ability to shift stats after a stat column is chosen.

    Stuff:
    Artificier armor: Your suit of armor is a meticulous maintained relic from the priesthood of mars. It has an armor value of 4 in addition to the armor history stats.

    Servo harness: Your backpack is the basis of your servo harness, a bulky contraption sporting numerous arms and mecha-tendrils. It is connected directly to your black carapace but also incorporates additional cranial adaptions and splicings in your brain to be able to coordinate the many limbs.
    Strenghts, Choose 4 (Choices can be multiplied twice):
    Loader arm: You have an extremely strong power loader arm mounted on the harness. If chosen twice you have two loader arms.
    Plasma cutter: You have an arm with a plasma cutter welding torch attached. If chosen twice the plasma cutter has an extremely large aperture and can be used as a flamer.
    Mechatendril cluster: You have a cluster of mechadendrils sprouting from the harness. These include datatendrils for direct interface with imperial machine spirits and have normal reach. If chosen twice the tendrils can be used as ambulatory mode and have double the reach (i.e. given hand-holds and purchases the techmarine can climb unhindered over any surface).
    Heavy weapon mount: You have a heavy weapon mounted on your harness (Either Heavy Bolter, Plasma Cannon, Lascannon, Missile Launcher). If chosen twice the weapon is twinlinked.
    Weaknesses:
    The harness is significantly larger than a normal suit of power armor. It is: Bulky.
    The space needed inside your head for the hardware means some unimportant things have had to be removed. When dealing with normal imperial citizens you have -1 on any rolls.

    Solo abilities:
    Battlefield Repair. Your intimate relationship with the Omnissiah allows you to conduct field repairs beyond the scope of your battle brothers. When repairing something roll +Techsavant:
    10+ You have returned the object to working order and the Omnissiah is pleased. 7-9 You have returned the object to function but something is wrong. If you miss the object is beyond the ability to repair in the field. Given a machine temple and time it might still be salvageable.

    And chose one:
    Machine-Spirit touched. You have +1 on all rolls involving imperial vehicles but suffer -1 to your Honor stat.
    - OR -
    Praise the Omnissiah. Whenever there is time to pause and to apply the sacred unguents and chant the praises needed to keep your wargear in top condition hold 1. You can spend your hold one for one to take +1 on a roll involving your wargear.

    Squad Mode (if Unit Leader)
    Chose one:
    Master of the Armory. During the mission the weapons employed by the squad will never jam.
    \u2013 OR \u2013
    Battlefield Blessed and Re-Canonized. Once during a mission your unusually elastic sense of propriety allows you to convert a non-imperial technological artifact into an approved avatar of the Omnissiah.

    Stats (choose one column)
    Fight (Hard) 1/2/2
    Honor (Hot) 0/-1/-1
    Cool 2/2/2
    Attuned (Weird) 0/1/0
    Techsavant 3/2/3

    The Techmarine can choose to adjust the chosen stat column by subtracting points from Fight or Honor (down to -1 at most) and adding these to either Cool, Attuned or Techsavant (up to a max of 3).

    I realized that I had forgotten about vows. At the same time one of the techmarines squad moves felt too weak. So then, Tadaa!

    Mission vows

    When setting out on a mission the chosen Unit Leader leads his battle brothers in oath-taking before battle. Some battle brothers swear entire catalogues of oaths varying from general to extremely detailed. Other settle for simple reaffirmations of their space marine oaths.


    The unit leader gets to choose one mission oath that affects the entire unit. The nature of the oath depends on the leader\u2019s specialization but any unit leader can choose the general vows. Each vow comes with a bonus and a drawback.


    Should someone break the oath this entails a loss of one Glory and the character may not enter squad mode during the rest of the mission. Nobody likes oath breakers.


    General Vows

    \nAssault Marine

    The Assault Marine brings the pure melee-centric hatred to the enemies of humankind. Even though up front head-bashing is the favoured method some specialize in close up demolitions work as well. Many assault marines give up any semblance of control to allow their brutal urges and trained reflexes deal death to the enemy while others pursue an expert\u2019s balanced melee mastery. The result nonetheless is always the same: total annihilation of the Emperor\u2019s enemies.


    The focused stat of the Assault Marine is Fight, and this is something he excels at.


    Stuff

    Jumppack

    You are equipped with a jet engine jumppack allowing you to take big bounding leaps over the battlefield and cover large stretches of ground in short time. It means you are (extremely bulky) but have access to the move Death from above.

    Death from above: With a mighty leap you land on your unsuspecting enemies from above and unleash the Emperor\u2019s judgement. Providing that the enemy is unaware of your impending attack the damage you deal counts as (AP) while they are disarrayed.

    Solo move, chose one:

    *Whirlwind of destruction: Whenever you do the Fight move versus an enemy squad in melee you always inflict additional 1harm.

    *Hard to kill: Whenever engaged in melee you count as having +1 armor.

    Squad move (if Unit Leader), chose one:

    *Devestating Charge. You lead your unit in a devastating charge and seize the enemy's position scattering the defenders. This Squad move can be employed regardless of opposition and situation but you have to describe what happens. All unit members take damage equal to the roll of a D6 on your way through hell.

    *Demolition. Through your extensive knowledge of melta charges and other high grade explosives you can destroy basically anything, or punch a way through any obstruction. Given a few moments of preparation you can blast your way into a Necron Tombship or through an Iron Warrior\u2019s defensive bunker using a melta charge placed at a crucial point.

    Fight (Hard) 3/3/3

    \n With the Librarian I wanted to make him powerful to really show off his psychic mastery. At the same time I wanted using the warp to be as dangerous as a fumbled roll in Deathwatch can end up being. So I ended up with the following; Use the Warp can be done by anyone, but is dangerous (humans are a phsycic breed after all), while the Librarian gets a crucial re-roll, and Attuned 3...

    USE THE WARP

    When you

    Use the Warp roll+Attuned. On a 10+ choose one:
    *Emperor\u2019s vengeance - damage enemies in range (short) doing harm equal to Attuned+1 (AP). You can trade harm for range, point for point.
    *Telepathy - contact anybody on the same planet as long as you have an idea of who and where they are.
    *Strength of Will - double the harm done with your melee weapons during this battle.
    *Augery - the MC will tell you something new and interesting about the current situation and in good detail.
    On a 7-9 choose one of the above but suffer 2 harm (AP)from acute brain hemorrhaging. On a miss you have attracted a warpbeast and something bad happens.


    Librarian

    The warp is the unsteady parallel universe underpinning the entirety of existence. It is the home of daemons and ghosts and is dangerous in the extreme. It is also a source of power for those who know how to control it and at the same time the medium through which the Empire\u2019s ships traverse the unknowable distances between the stars. The Librarian, as keeper of the chapter\u2019s lore and psychic master, channels these dangerous powers of the warp to destroy enemies of humanity. It is said that anybody can call on the warp and open a rift, but only a librarian has the power to close it again. Yet, the warp is corruption in its purest form and any altercation with it can spell doom. Only the strongest of mind can hope to wield it as a weapon and hope to survive.


    As the librarian can sense the future through readings of the Emperor\u2019s Tarot only an unwise unit leader ignores a librarian\u2019s council.

    Stuff

    Psychic Hood
    A psychic hood often takes the shape of a metal collar that rises above the librarian\u2019s head. It is interlaced with a complex crystal matrix to help shield the librarian from the dangers of the warp. It allows the wearer to re-roll Use the warp rolls.

    Force Weapon
    A force weapon is a power weapon intervowen with a fine crystal matrix designed to allow for the librarian to channel his psychic might in close combat. Being a power weapon it is (Close) (AP). Whenever the librarian does harm in close combat he may immediately make a Use the warp roll to wrench the enemy\u2019s soul from it\u2019s body. On a 10+ the enemy dies outright. On 7-9 deal +1harm and suffer 2harm (AP) from acute brain hemorrhaging. On a miss you have attracted a warpbeast and something bad happens.


    Solo ability: Not yet done

    Squad Move (if Unit Leader), choose one:

    *Gate of Infinity. You use your immense warpborn powers to teleport the unit anywhere within a planet system\u2019s distance. The only thing required is good intel on where you are supposed to end up. The side effect of this supreme effort is that you suffer 2harm (AP) due to brain hemorrhaging.

    *Shield of the Emperor. Drawing on the Immaterium\u2019s defiance of physical reality you weave a shield of pure warp energy. For a short duration of time you and any squad members in (close) range are impervious to any kind of external damage. The side effect of this supreme effort is that you suffer 2harm (AP) due to brain hemorrhaging.


    Stats (choose one column)
    Fight 1/1/2
    Honor 1/2/1
    Cool 2/1/1
    Attuned 3/3/3
    Techsavant 0/0/0
