\n ...in a small town. I'm thinking of codifying the more dramatic ways you and your buddy can end up in a bind. The sort of bind that would result in lies and paranoia and eventually cutting up bodies out by the old fish house. First your motivation:

Obligation: You and your buddy share a burden.
Ambition: You share a goal.
Compulsion: You share a need.

(Am I missing a powerful motivation?)

Then the thing you are either beholden to or pursuing:

Money (a debt, a windfall)
Status (Fame, power, respect)
Freedom (for yourself, for another, from a responsibility)
Knowledge (The truth, a secret)
Companionship (sex, love, friendship)
Passion (revenge, justice, lust)

Anything missing? "Freedom" seems weak. "Companionship" isn't the right word. Thanks for your low-stakes dirty small town thoughts. I saw Burn After Reading last night and it's given me ideas.

    \n This thing I'm working on hits everyone's inner townie so hard. My wife just went bananas when I showed it to her. Much of the color is based on the town she grew up in and she had some strong feelings about what should and should not be present.

    Right now I've distilled it to six broad motivations:

    Get rich
    Get even
    Get respect (from town, from someone, from yourself)
    Get the truth
    Get laid
    Get out (of town, of a relationship, of an obligation)

    And each of those has six variations. These are shared by a two person affiliation, and the players of those two characters hash out what it means, exactly, with the caveat that it must have the capacity to drag both of them into deeper trouble. So it could be all about these two knuckleheads getting their friend laid, or one of them helping the other stand up to his landlord, or both of them stealing from the drug dealer.

    The only doctor in town is sure his wife is cheating on him. One of his patients is the pharmacist at the drug store. The pharmacist is being paid to follow the Doctor's wife around and catch her in flagrante delicto. (Affiliation: Work/Doctor+Patient, Problem: Get out of relationship with spouse). The doctor's son-in-law is a ne'er-do-well and a paranoid druggie (Affiliation: Family/In-laws, Object: Shotgun). The drug lunatic is married to the doctor's less-than-wholesome daughter, and they have a plan to rob the drug store of narcotics and cash by having the wife seduce the pharmacist (Affiliation: Crime/Thieves, Problem: To get laid/by someone I've had my eye on). And the sordid affair is happening at the church where they both lead youth groups (Affiliation: Romance/Lovers, Location: Peace Haven church).

    \n I'm working hard on this idea. Here's the extended example of engineerig the initial fiasco I'm using:

    It's ME, MONA, JEFF, and JOEL, sitting around a table in that order.

    ME: What\u2019s the setting?

    MONA: Let\u2019s use \u201cSouthern Town.\u201d

    JOEL: Sounds good. Why don\u2019t we set it in \u2026 let\u2019s see \u2026 Robin Hood, North Carolina.

    JEFF: I like that, it already sounds off-kilter. OK, roll the dice.

    (I roll all sixteen dice. They come up 1, 1, 1, 1, 2, 2, 2, 3, 3, 3, 4, 5, 6, 6, and 6).

    ME: Who grew up in the smallest town? Not me.

    JEFF: Not me either. Joel? Mona?

    JOEL: 5000 people.

    MONA: You win. You go first, Joel.

    JOEL: Excellent. I see there\u2019s only one five, and I definitely want a crime Relationship, so I\u2019ll take that.

    (Joel places the die between us \u2013 he and I will have a criminal Relationship.)

    ME: I\u2019m next. How about a work Relationship between Jeff and Mona?

    (I place a two die between them.)

    MONA: Work, huh? All right. I\u2019ll take a one and make us former co-workers. Where and how, we don\u2019t know yet.

    JEFF: That\u2019ll come. I want some community involvement here. I\u2019ll take a six.

    (JEFF puts the six between himself and me.)

    Joel, your turn.

    JOEL: Right, nobody touched my criminal Relationship with Jason, so I\u2019m going to grab another six and declare it a drug thing. Meth, I think.

    ME: Sounds great. Let\u2019s be a skeezy husband and wife team!

    JOEL: Absolutely.

    ME: My turn, and I\u2019m going for the Relationship between Joel and Jeff. I\u2019ll take a one, family.

    MONA: Nice. Let\u2019s just finish that up. How about in-laws?

    (MONA takes a one.)

    JEFF: I like that. Joel, what if I was the meth cooker\u2019s father-in-law?

    ME: That\u2019d make me his daughter!

    JOEL: Awesome. Can your guy be the town doctor?

    JEFF: Definitely. And this is getting good \u2013 I want a weapon to get between the doctor and his son-in-law.

    (JEFF takes a three. JOEL, next in rotation, immediately grabs a one.)

    JOEL: A shotgun. It\u2019s a short-barreled pump. Of course I\u2019m paranoid as hell, and I keep it around the house. It\u2019s a point of tension between me and my wife\u2019s dad.

    ME: I\u2019m going to throw down the three there, as a Need for me and Joel. We Need to get rich, obviously. So what do we have?

    MONA: A creepy pair of shotgun-toting meth addicts, the wife is the black sheep of her high-class family.

    JEFF: Her father\u2019s the town doctor, and he wants to make good with his son-in-law and maybe help him out, but he\u2019s scared of him.

    MONA: OK, time to up the ante. I\u2019m putting down the last six as a Need between my guy, as yet unknown, and Jason\u2019s meth princess. Somebody Needs to get laid.

    JOEL: I love it.

    JEFF: Maybe he\u2019s a businessman. I\u2019ll use that one to add a Location to Jason and Mona\u2019s Relationship \u2013 Downtown, on the north side of Center Road.

    JOEL: I\u2019ll take the three and make that Royall\u2019s Drug Store.

    MONA: Sweet. He\u2019s the pharmacist.

    JEFF: We used to work together at the regional hospital.

    MONA: Right. And he was a classmate of the meth-heads back in high school.

    ME: Maybe we dated.

    MONA: Oh! Jason, take that four and put it in our Relationship, making the Need \u201cto get laid by a former lover, to rekindle the flame.\u201d

    ME: Oh hell yes.

    MONA: We were engaged before I went off to college. My straight-arrow pharmacist never got over you.

    (I take the four.)

    MONA: We still need to know how I\u2019m currently connected to the Meth princess, and how you guys are going to get rich. I\u2019ll take the two and put it in your Need \u2013 you losers Need to get rich by robbing a business. I think we all know whose business that is going to be.

    JEFF: Cool. That leaves the other two to explain how the meth princess and the pharmacist know each other \u2013 it\u2019s already community, and now you guys are civic volunteers.

    ME: I may have fallen, but I still have connections to the respectable community. I teach youth group! We teach youth group at Peace Haven church together.

    MONA: That\u2019s agony. I love it.

    ME: And I\u2019m acting really sweet to you, giving you signals, buttering you up, because we are going to fucking rob your pharmacy.

    MONA: I guess that Need to get laid is sort of one-way, huh?

    ME: Indeed, but we\u2019re both going to suffer for it I suspect. Everybody happy with this?

    JEFF: It seems like my doctor is a little removed from the action.

    JOEL: True, he doesn\u2019t have a Need, but he\u2019s got strong connections to everybody else and there\u2019s always that shotgun.

    JEFF: Good point, Joel. I bet it won\u2019t be long before he\u2019s in the thick of things.

    ME: Let\u2019s play!
