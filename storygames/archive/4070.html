\n So I've attempted to arrange a small party/gathering this Saturday. This was mostly spurred by the fact that I've just been to Gen Con and I brought back a lot of cool crap, but I've also been trying to gather together more people to do some gaming with for awhile.

So projected attendance isn't looking nearly as good as I was originally hoping, but to a certain extant having a small group is a good thing. Two people that are coming are the two people that I normally game with, everyone else that will be there (probably about 3 to 5 more people) are either complete non-gamers or people that have done a very limited amount in the distant past. No one, myself included, is particularly familiar with "indie" games. I own quite a few now, but I haven't quite managed to break out more than a couple to actually play with.

So, while I expect to do some boardgames and stuff at first, I'm hoping to play an rpg with these mostly non-gamer types if everyone is feeling willing, and I was wondering if people had suggestions for both what games to play, and how to make the experience as positive as possible for those new to the hobby.

The games I'm currently considering are:
It Was a Mutual Decision
The Shab-Al-Hiri Roach
and Psi Run

I've read all of these but have never played any of them. The first one seems like it might be good because people get to be on teams which I think might help diffuse the worries of those people that have never played any kind of rpg before and might feel self-conscious about having to do stuff completely on their own. Both the Roach and Psi Run also seem like fairly good options as they have pretty easy rules and seem like they could be fairly satisfying in a single session. Plus, the Roach has the cards and the Reputation points and somewhat competitive play which might make it feel slightly more familiar to people that haven't done much or any roleplaying.

Anyway, I was wondering if other people thought these were good choices or not, and if not, what might be better. Obviously I don't have time to acquire any new games, but if those don't seem optimal, I could post the other games I own if people are wondering.

Thanks in advance for any advice anyone can offer!
-brian

    \n Those are all good games, and should work well for your purposes. Can I suggest a few more?
    \n
    \nShooting the Moon is great if you end up with 3 players. It's smart but intuitive, and brings the storygame fun in a very easy to understand way. Because the subject matter is pretty universal it should be a good game for anyone.
    \n
    \nThe Princess Game is simple and fun, and would make a great warm up game. This is a game that is playable right out of the "box". It shouldn't take more then a few minutes to explain the concept and get things going. It's fun and cute, and if your guests are into that it should be a hit.
    \n
    \nShock: might be too involved for what you want, but what I've found is that working to establish the Shock and issues really brings players together right away. If your guests are up for something a little more demanding, Shock: could be the way to go.
    \n
    \nContenders is really fun and quick to pick up. The card mechanic feels like actual boxing, and it's very easy to get into. If any of your guests like boxing movies this would likely be a hit.
    \n
    \nCan I suggest my own games? If you have guests that are into anime, manga or Japanese horror, Panty Explosion should go over well. Panty Explosion is easy to pick up and play if at least one player is familiar with the game, and the game's scene framing and narrative rules keep all players constantly involved in the game. Alternativly, if your guests are looking to kill stuff, there is no better murder simulator then Classroom Deathmatch. Classroom Deathmatch is designed for easy pick up and play with almost no prep time, and should appeal to both blood and guts players and players who want emotional conflict.
    \n
    \nI also recommend Sea Dracula as an ice breaker.

    \n I'm going to disagree with a lot of people here and say: don't use It Was a Mutual Decision, The Shab-Al-Hiri Roach (much love, of course), or Bacchanal. I'm making the assumption your friends aren't gamers, and aren't part of a subculture where people love to engage with as much meaningless darkness as possible. I love having intense games about the darkness of the human soul - with my best friends who I've been gaming with for a while.

    Play something fun and positive. Psi Run is a great choice, as it's got a reality condom, and yet is philosophically engaging and people will talk about it afterwards. Shooting the Moon or Breaking the Ice are great, especially for mixed-gender gatherings. They give a safe way to flirt and have fun with each other, and also generate stories that you can tell in a public place without harming children.
