    \n Lone she-wolf and cub
    Your are a fearsome warrior but also a mother. You carry your baby child in a basket on your back.
    As long as you have your baby with you, you have +3 to any Show-Off action and any out of combat conflict involving protecting your cub, being a fearsome she-wolf, etc...
    You can choose to take any damage instead of your baby since it has only 2 HP. But any foe can target it and it have one less armor level than you.

    I challenge you to create a talent involving ancient scriptures and erudition.

    \n D\xe9j\xe0 vu
    You can make a short jump back in time to a point that you fixed ahead of time. This is usually something like the night before you venture out. Setting this point takes at least one hour of focus and meditation. At any point later in an adventure you can spend an awesome point to travel back to that point in time. The trick is you can only exist in that time for a number of hours equal to your Stubbornness, and you can not allow anyone to know that you are from the future, including yourself. After that time is spent, or earlier if desired, you are jumped back to the normal timeline. So if you want to put that extra potion in your bag, you had better not get spotted. Failure to follow any of the restrictions will forcibly jump you back to your normal timeline and erase any actions that you might have completed while in the past.

    I challenge you to make a talent that creates some sort of a "Mini Game." (ie a hand of Poker played between you and GM has a mechanical effect on the game)

    \n The Even Hand of Odd
    \nYou gamble with a Spirit of Chance. Once per adventure the player can challenge the GM to a round of Ch\u014d-Han. The player can wager as many awesome tokens as he possess. The GM rolls two dice and covers the results. Both the player and the GM yell "Even"or "Odd" at the same time. If the player wins he doubles the amount wagered. If there is a tie, half of the tokens go to the GM. If the player looses, all his awesome tokens go to the GM and the Spirit attacks. The player must fight the Spirit alone, and it can only be defeated with a hit "to the Face". If the player is "lucky" and defeats the Spirit, he regains his tokens plus one. If the player is not victorious, he will be left with a single hp and a single token...
    \nThe Spirit should have the power "Entangle", light armor, light weapon and 10 hp...
    \n
    \nI challenge you to create a talent for a Cat like humanoid...

    \n Star Crossed
    You find and fall in love with any attractive face, wherever you may travel. You love them and they love you more than anything. But it never lasts long, usually less than a week, before that powerful love turns into powerful anger. Or sometimes you find out later that there are other complications. Like she is actually married to a powerful and angry noble, or they are only playing you to make you the fall guy for a crime they committed. You know that it will never work out, but you can never say no to a pretty face.

    I challenge you to make a talent that makes you a "Landed Lord" with benefits and drawbacks.

    \n Just Add Bastardy!

    You and the noble "Lord/Lady" of your choice just happen to be inheriting-capable siblings, one legitimate, one illegitimate.

    Reveal your birthmark/signet ring/family sword/Parental deathbed confession-letter/peculiarly-shaped appendage while in the presence of the King/Emperor/Grandhighpoobah/etc. and declare your chosen target to be the bastard. Immediately retire from adventuring and take possession of their lands/titles/spouses/beasts of the field/etc. with the full blessing of the local liege and assume their place in the noble heap. The deposed noble immediately becomes a personal BBEG who vows to kill you and resume their place and you're done gallivanting around like some common adventurer unless your liege declares some sort of war or quest.

    I challenge you to create a talent that uses cooking in combat. Nightmare mode: it has nothing to do with knives, cleavers or implements of culinary art!
