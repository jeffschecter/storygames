BUILDING SITUATION

Situation building is collaborative, following these steps. It may take a couple of tries by the group to get a situation worth playing through, unless the players are all experienced at this kind of thing, but relax, it's pretty easy.

1. The Guide describes two characters or groups in conflict.
These might be anything that fits the first thought of the game. The guide notes these two down on a piece of paper, and the conflict between them. For ease of memory, this usually takes the form of a â€˜networkâ€™, as shown in a couple pages, but it doesnâ€™t have to.

2. Going clockwise, everyone adds to this.
In turn, each player describes another character or group to the situation, and describes their relationships with either two or three of the existing characters or groups. Good additions are those that...

- Fit the "first thought": The first thought, which was chosen earlier on, is the basic premise, and usually implies a genre, some different kinds of action, all sorts of stuff. All the added characters and groups should fit that.

- Can be summarized in a few words: Each added person or group should have a byline that summarizes it - instead of just adding â€œJoeâ€ as a fictional person, you might add â€œJoe, Folk Musicianâ€.

- Want something: At least one of the relationships that each person or group has should describe, or at least imply, something that person or group wants from the situation.

- Raise the tension: Each addition should bring something new to the conflict that the Guide described, expanding it in some way. This doesnâ€™t mean that they have to have chosen a side, only that their presence makes things harder, not easier, to resolve.
