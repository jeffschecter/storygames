    \n 2000, whatta year. I was just finishing up my last year in Japan, getting ready to move back to the states (10 YEARS AGO? Christ).

    I was attending JIGG (Japanese International Gaming Guild) events regularly with Mike Montesa and others. We were planning on writing our own RPG at one point, me Cam Eeles and Mike each writing some setting for it and fishing around for rules to base it on.

    I also remember going to Yokozuka (sp?) military base a lot for some gaming, and when D&D came out I had a shipment of books Express Mailed to my house; one day later I was reselling them to local Army boys and Expats in Tokyo, getting our game on at a JIGG event. I ran the first D&D 3e game ever run in Japan, two days after the game was released. I remember there was a Halfling Paladin, Half-Orc Wizard, Elven Sorcerer and others; and the mission involved sabotaging an ork stronghold. The funny thing was that two Japanese guys came to the event, and weren't all that comfortable speaking English; so with the permission of the table, I ran a compeltely bi-lingual game; the Japanese guy could kinda understand/speak English, the foreigners could kinda understand Japanese, so I acted to bridge the group together, translating between them in real-time as the game played out.

    Anyway, as games go, I had the most fun with D&D 3e (until I kinda got tired of it, probably from over-play plus the career pathing stuff), Fudge and BESM 2nd edition. I had decent times with Blue Planet 2e, but have been meaning to run another game of it sometime... can't believe it's like 10 years later...

    Also, 2000 was the year that Tenra Bansho Zero came out in Japanese, although it wasn't until 2001 or so that I bought a copy, or years later that I thought about starting to translate it. For that reason, I'd probably say that TBZ was my favorite game overall: When I started running it in English 3 years ago, it just played out into sessions that were fun and memorable, moreso than many other games I've played in the last 10 years.

    2000 was also the year the Dune RPG came out. Man, talk about wasted potential: So much of the game was relegated to the pipeline of "play X in a future supplement", which of course never came out. Plus, it was just kinda dry and simple.

    You know, as lame as it sounds, I would not mind seeing one of these threads for other years too, within "posting reason"; that is, like, space them out a week apart or something so as not to fill up the front page.

    Edit: Summer 2000, I came back once to Chapel Hill to visit my family and my best friend who was living there at the time, working at a consulting agency. He worked alongside Steffan O'Sullivan, who gave me my first physical copy of the original Fudge rulebook. Geek cred who-whoooop!
