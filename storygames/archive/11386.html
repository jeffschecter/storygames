    \n I handed out 30-40 microdungeons at Gen Con in lieu of business cards. I just wrote my email address on the back. I have way to many boxes of un-used business cards from past jobs lying around my house. I now make 'em as I need 'em. I also recommend business-card sized peices of blank paper, or paper with just your simple contact info. Hand them out when you tell someone about a cool book, movie, or Web site. Write the name of the thing you recommended on the back. Providing something useful is way cooler than a busness card they'll probably just throw away.

    \n I despise most business cards, I think most people consider them an afterthought when they are one of those things that's front and center in regards to your professional face. Here's some of my most recent faves (and my own current and previous cards):



    Some important things to note. I think leaving the back of a card blank is a dumb oversite. If you picked up a magazine and the back cover was just white paper you would think the printer fucked up. When you've only got 2.5 x 3" to make a statement use the back, even if it's just some color (this is often the best choice) or a simple image.

    While an extra is often a novelty, it can in fact set you apart. On of my favorite little bonuses is rounded corners, it is not for everyone but I'm usually pleased when i see it.

    I realize you didn't want a lot of talking here so i'll shut my mouth, whisper me if your interested in more ranting about business cards, but you certainly don't have to feel obligated.
