    \nPerudo has escalation written all over it. I could see it as a modified relative of Dogs in the Vineyard's escalation-focused mechanic. But different. Perudo has a pretty harsh death spiral, though - once you loose a couple dice, your ability to succeed going forward is hugely limited. But I guess you could put in a mechanic where you get dice back quickly for doing things that are against your best interest.

    Hmm. That reminds me of the show I've been watching lately. I'm watching The Tribe, which is a teenage post apocalyptic soap opera show out of New Zealand from about a decade ago. The characters have a bad habit of getting mad at each other and blowing up over the slightest thing. A mechanic like this would make sense for that - get dice back when you do something stupid and hurtful toward your friends.

    \n I really like the idea of a modified 'story' dice pool mechanic in the vein of the new WHFRP dice. I think they are woefully under - utilised in the game. Different colours / number of sides for different narrative prompts.
    \nie: Skill die, Talent die, Risky die, Considered die, Conflict die, Communication die, fortune die etc..
    \nThe sides could have pictograms or 'prompts' in the form of words, perhaps having a range of outcomes Apocalypse world style, from strong hit to miss. Thus each dice pool generates its own 'custom move' as it were.
    \nReading the rolled pool should be a group endeavour, each player having narrative rights as they interpret each particular die.
    \n
    \nJust an amorphous idea at the moment. :)
