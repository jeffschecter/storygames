\n Recently my wife and I have been looking for a story game for just the two of us to play since we aren't able to get out as much as we used to. So I was wondering if there are any games that you've played in a duo that worked well or look well suited to a two player dynamic. I have a mild preference for collaborative games since I'm not terribly interested in GMing yet again, but I'd love to hear about anything good for two.

I've looked around and seen a number of options that might work well but I certainly don't have the budget to pick them all up to figure out which actually do. If you have the time, in addition to the games title, I'd love to hear what it does that makes it well suited to two players and what it has that makes it pure awesome.

Thanks,
-Dan

    \n There are so many great options. You're going to get drowned in replies saying simply "Breaking the Ice" which is a really cool romance game. It's collaborative, like you wanted, and really pretty versitile in terms of genre and setting.

    If romance isn't your thing "Beast Hunters" is another explicitly two-player game. It's very adversarial, in the sense that you're playing "against" each other. I haven't played it, so I can't comment too much.

    There are also plenty of games that are not explicitly for two players that might still work. I think doing away witth the traditional GM/Player divide makes two-player gaming a lot more fun, bacause it's much less draining on the GM.

    I wrote a game here, that's free to download. It's a kind of sword-and-sorcery engine. It has a GM, but a lot of the gameplay is collaborative, a la Donjon.

    Donjon itself might be a good game for you too.

    What kind of setting/genre are you interested in?

    \n I never played trollbabe, but from reading it (and many actual play with only a player and a GM) it is well suited to this kind of playing. Every story of every player character (trollbabe) is separate anyway.

    I played In a Wicked Age, and I DON'T think it would be well suited instead the game depends very much on the conflicting best interest of a lot of players (I seem to remember Vincent saying in a thread that he play it only with three players or more, but I could be mistaken). Dogs in the Vineyards would be better, I think.

    I second the "Breaking the" Ice suggestion, and I add that even "Shooting the Moon" can be played in two-players mode without a GM. And if the mood is different, there's always "Bacchanal" (this one without a GM, too)... ;-)
