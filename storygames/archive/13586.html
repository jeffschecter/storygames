\n Hey friends,

Pelgrane Press is looking for some fearless people to playtest my Trail of Cthulhu adventure, Many Fires. Taking place in the hills and valleys of the Mexican state of Chihuahua, it will introduce the Investigators to a surprising cast of characters and strange new weapons to employ in an epic struggle against an implacable and deadly enemy known as K\u0173\u0173 \u0142\u0105\u0301 \u2013 Many Fires. It's big, it's scary, and it involves the following items:

* People (perhaps your Investigator?) burning to death from the inside out
* Pancho Villa's decapitated head, and the bandit army that venerates it
* Mysterious German Mennonites
* John J. Pershing's strange Chihuahuan legacy
* Words in Chiricahua and Tarahumara that you cannot pronounce
* More drugs than the Love Parade
* Meticulously researched actual history that is considerably scarier than anything I could make up

I'm pretty excited about it. I hope you'll consider giving it a try! The complete adventure should take maybe ten hours of play to complete.

It's an open call. Get in touch with Simon Rogers (simon at dyingearth dot com), subject "Many Fires Playtest" and he will hook you up. Also tell him you heard about it here and whether or not you've playtested for Pelgrane before.
