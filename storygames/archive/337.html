I keep asking myself two questions:
"What do I want to be able to do as a player of this game?"
and when I answer that, the next question is, "How will I do that?"

I want to be able to run a small furry creature from Alpha Centauri, or a giant eight-armed red guy from the gladiator pits of Mars with equal facility because those details aren't as important to what I want the game to be about. What's important is Faith, how good the characters are at their Occupation, and the depth of their Background (including resources, contacts, and skills/traits).

Can someone hold my hand and tell me it's OK not to have physical attributes? No "Body" score, no "Strength," no "Physical." I was able to get rid of the Intelligence statistic easily - that's how the player plays, not a function or requirement of system...but I'm feeling kind of lost and lonely on this one.

    What you do need -- which attributes have often fulfilled -- is a sort of 'default ability' where, if the character does not necessarily have the Sword Ninja skill, they are still able to use something in order to roll some dice instead of just flat-out losing when attacked by Sword Ninjas.
