\n So there's this monthly game day gathering thing in Tucson, Arizona.


The next Ides of Gaming event will be on Saturday, April 19th between noon and six o'clock.
Location: Espresso Art, 944 E University Blvd, Tucson, AZ. (Google Maps link)

Visit IdesOfGaming.com for more information, including upcoming dates.



Ides of Gaming is a game day where you can meet fellow local gamers. Bring your own games, play games other people brought! Have fun! Play games!

    \n It's probably going to be the second Saturday in August. The initial thought was near the middle of the month, but on most of the months we've held Ides, it's been on the second Saturday. We might shift the game day to the second Saturday as a recurring thing, but Bo and I usually decide on the next month's game day at the current month's game day. I'll try to remember to update this thread with August's date after the game day event.

    I'm looking forward to tomorrow's event. I'm jonesing for some RP after the hectic month I just went through. My group's fortnightly game day had to reschedule for the third time; I'm going to switch it to the style of group that seems popular with nearly every gaming podcast I subscribe to: several short campaigns -- one shots and two to five game session campaigns -- instead of the neverending long form campaign.

      \n Showed off the Dogs prototype, but we all wound up playing a seven-player game of D&D, with Paizo's new Pathfinder book/setting. It was entertaining with an interesting plotline, but reaffirmed that d20 was not the game system for heroic fantasy I want. When I want heroic fantasy gaming, I'm thinking cinematic adventure and d20 totally doesn't offer that.

      Example: (Ooo! Actual Play!) Barrels of oil, aflame, on the back of wagons, also aflame, around which stand a group of bad guys. My character heads up and casts Acid Spray on the axle of the wagon, hoping to tip the wagon over and dump the flaming oil on the baddies. Instead of something awesome happening, we rolled 1d3 acid damage, which can't even harm the wooden axle with its 5 points of hardiness. So there's that.
