\n
\n
\n
\n\nwundergeek \n\n\n

\n
\n
\n
\n
\n Okay, so our Skype group had a frustrating experience last week where the game we were trying to get going sucked to a complete halt. It was just a bad fit for our play style, so rather than try and save it we just jumped ship. We found ourselves saying, 'screw new stuff - what has been reliably awesome for us'? Our answer? Hero's Banner. Man. I'm sad that Hero's Banner doesn't get much forum love, because every time we need something awesome and punchy, this game is consistent in creating awesome.

We rolled up some characters and played the first session last night (I posted about it on my blog, if you're curious). And just like the other three times we've played it, Hero's Banner delivered. I'm really excited to play more and sad that it will be a few weeks before our schedules intersect again.

So I'm curious. What games have you played that don't get a whole lot of attention and yet still consistently deliver fun? I'm not talking about the new hotness here. I'm specifically asking about older games that have been out for a while.

    \n Hmmmm. Don't get enough love.

    Conspiracy of Shadows 1st Edition. Believe it or not, I still come back to this game from time to time: It's light, horrific, and provides just enough crunch to appease me.

    The End: Mostly for the awesome, ripe, heady setting. The rules in the original edition were "meh", the d20 edition was far better, but still it's the background and setting which makes this pretty much one of my favorite roleplaying experience-generating machines.

    Dead Inside. Pretty much the only game I know where violence is not the answer. Only played it three times, mind, but each time it was really cool, really moving.

    \n Going through our webstore inventory, these are the games that are not selling as well to Finns as they should, quality-wise (I'm comparing personally, gauging the sales vs. quality of other titles):

    The Mountain Witch

    Mechaton
    No Press Anthology
    The Princes' Kingdom
    The Questing Beast
