\n I've had this crazy urban setting idea set in space.
\n
\nBasically it's a Stellated Dodecahedron with a major Earth City at each vertex, taken around the year 2000 (before or after 9/11? I don't know). Now it's 100 years later, and the planetoid has has traveled about 20 light years, and is arriving at Gliese 581b.

    Posted By: OrlyYou might want to take a look at Diaspora, which is explicitly about space-opera.
    \nI agree with your other comments, but I'm not sure about this one. Diaspora's focus is, I think, much tighter than simply to say "space-opera". For example: I don't think Diaspora would be an appropriate choice to make for a game like Star Wars, Star Trek, Banks' Culture, Smith's Lensman, and the like. There are aspects to traditional Space Opera that Diaspora flies directly in the face of: transcendant technology, wide proliferation of intelligent life, a panoply of worlds and attendant cultures.

    From wikipedia: "Perhaps the most significant trait of space opera is that settings, characters, battles, powers, and themes tend to be very large-scale."

    Diaspora's focus seems to me to be a lot more like Traveller: an emphasis on "realism", human-scale activities and conflicts.

    If you wanted to stick with a FATE based game, then Starblazer Adventures, warts and all, is probably a better fit for traditional space opera gaming. I think Strands of Fate might also stretch to it.

    But I agree with you and Wilhelm in general -- "if you want a story focused system, you'll have to tell us what the PCs are supposed to do".

    \n Thanks everyone for your comments.
    \n
    \nIt's been the setting that has been kicking around in my head, and your comments have helped me realize that ultimately, what the players do and how they interact with each other and other NPCs in the world is what will make the game satisfying for them.
    \n
    \nI see the game as being about the intersection of two different genres, and the results of that intersection. It begins as a sort of Cyberpunk type setting - it's gritty and urban, with some high-tech mixing with some very low tech. And then all of a sudden, everyone's world will begin to change as it becomes clear that this planetoid actually had a destination, but no one knows why the planetoid has been brought to this planet. It's a time of great opportunity, as well as confusion and chaos. A time when the low can remake themselves anew, The high may be brought low, and the morally ambiguous can pursue their obsessions with less oversight.
    \n
    \nI think that the setting is therefore rich in many different types of adventures, so maybe a more generic system like GURPS is the best to use. I could see some sort of revolution campaign; or a military campaign between different cities; or an investigative campaign - trying to figure out what is going on; or a scientific campaign; or a more high-level campaign among the various leaders of the cities.
    \n
    \nThanks all for the various suggestions, I'll check them out.

    \n I think these might line up with what you said your players will be doing:

    There is always Classic Traveller which you can bolt on different player techniques and modify the lifepath system to reflect your world

    Stars Without Number is a pretty cool system.

    There is Solar System which is easily hackable.

    Folks around here like to hack Lady Blackbird to do various cool things.

    On the cyberpunky side I'd recommend Remember Tomorrow and Technoir both should be able to handle the stories you are looking for.

    Another option would be to create the history of your game world with Shock: Social Science Fiction or Microscope. Then switch to another system of your choice for the what's going on right now.
