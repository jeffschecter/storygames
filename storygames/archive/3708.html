\n I'm looking for two things. 1) people to play this game. 2) someone to review it. You can download it here for free: http://www.lulu.com/content/1045384.
\n
\nBile is an unpleasant game for emotionally immature adults who want to create enmity between the players in their roleplaying group. The object of the game is to increase the stress of other characters until they snap and commit murder.
\n
\nThe writing in bile is very offensive and rude. So please don't read it if you're easily offended. Please give it a shot and either send your review to rpg.net or post an actual play thread right here at story games.

    \nI'm not Graham W. But I do have to give him credit for inspiring me to make a very unserious game (see his recent thread in Games and Gaming). I was originally trying to write an Enid Blyton style investigative game and I couldn't quite figure out how to make it work. I had several ideas stirring around in my brain and my favourite idea to branch off from the original concept just happened to be one where the game wasn't about solving the crime. It was about commiting the crime. Think of all those murder mysteries where the detective gives expositional dialogue during his accusation speech. All the whys and hows and whos. Well I was going to use that for the structure of a game, then I read Graham W's thread, and the rest is pretty obvious.
    \n
    \nYou've probably guessed that the character sheet was meant to be ugly. The whole game is meant to be ugly and unpleasant, much like the characters players are supposed to roleplay. It's a silly quirk of mine to write games as a character from the game would do them.
    \n
    \nI'm definitely going to pick up Wuthering Heights as I'm a big fan of games that go against the grain of traditional rpgs.
