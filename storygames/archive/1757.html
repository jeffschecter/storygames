Tonight I'll be running a one-off playtest of 44 via Skype with Matt Wilson and John Harper.

You can read the 44 rules on my wiki here.

And, here's the note I'm sending them:
Ok, guys, a little prep for the 44 playtest:

First off, it almost goes without saying that this will be a playtest, and it's bound to get messy. I've got some undefined rules as you go in, specifically the consequences of winning and losing a conflict. I have some idea of how that happens (though it's not yet on the wiki), but I'll be looking for feedback there too.


secondly, I want to use the "default" setting: It's the early 1950s or so, Cold War paranoia is widespread, and you're in some large, unnamed cityscape.


Thirdly, Here is my Control Profile. (Basically, I'll be the GM, you guys will be players, and this is my "adversity" sheet)

Control Rating: 4 (this means by default I roll 4d6)

Secrets:

Unit 44 does not bleed: 2
(undecided - will base on Anxiety): 1
(undecided - will base on Anxiety): 1

Agents:

Mr. Trask, cold-as-ice agent: 2
Susan, sad-eyed little girl: 1
I'll be recording the session for my own purposes to review, but likely I'll share for others to download.

Issues I'm looking at for tonight's playtest include:

* How can I tune the currency and dice-pooling so that they balance well for play (i.e. failure is neither too common nor too rare)? And, how can I forecast that into longer and shorter games? And, what should the Trust Fund start out at? Will it get too big?

* Exactly what are the mechanical effects of success and failure. How does Stress work? How do characters (main and supporting characters) become machines? And, how can players disable Unit 44 agents? What effect do these consequences have on the Countdown? Should the Countdown be unique for each main character?

* Does the agressive conflict-only scene setting work effectively? Similar, do the limitations for other main characters entering a scene work well?

    That's 'cause it ain't there yet.

    The good news is that one I do have figured out ... mostly.

    So, when you trigger an Anxiety and WIN the conflict, you generate a number of new Trust equal to the Anxiety (or Anxieties) rating.

    The "mostly" part is this: I'm tinkering with whether a Personal Trust Fund works ok. Basically, you get to keep a number of "personal" Trust that others can't use equal to your Material. The rest goes into the fund.

    You spend Personal Trust either for bolstering anyone's rolls (probably your own, too) or to increase your Qualities. Possibly to buy or earn new Bonds.
  • Matt Snyder
    November 2006
    When you LOSE a conflict, you have to take the difference of your roll and Control's roll in Stress.

    Then, you have to put the stress somewhere.

    Your first option is to add new Anxieties. Earn 3 stress, then add 3 points worth of existing or new Anxieties.

    Your second option is to reduce your Qualities with an equal number of points lost.

    Your third option is to sacrifice a Bond to 44 -- the character becomes a machine if the value reaches 0.

    Finally, your character can just assume (or may have to take) the overwhelming stress and become a machine himself.

    One bit I'm curious to examine is whether the Personal Trust stuff can "buy off" stress. Dunno yet. I'm thinking instead it buys time, increasing the Countdown for you.
