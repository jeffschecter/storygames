\n So, lots of you are at Gencon. Lots of us are not. I'm not, and I'm dead jealous. But beyond my petty jealousies, I'm also curious and nosy.

So, people at Gencon, or people in contact with Gencon - tell us stuff! What's going on? What new stuff is about? What cool stuff is going on? How is the Forge booth going? How is the Indie Passport going?

Help us live Gencon vicariously! Be our proxies at Gencon and feed our insatiable curiosity.

    \n Heya, looks like my room has free wireless. Unfortunately, I'm too busy living the con to blog/post much about it. Stayed up each night until 2 or so, getting up at 8:30, etc.
    Bought a crapton of games. I'll post my list to my LJ in the next few days (zigguratbuilder.livejournal.com), including all sorts of small press stuff (like Colonial Gothic and another Godsend Agenda supplement) and even some indie boardgames, like "DRAGONS OF KIR", which is a tile-based abstract boardgame about moving and controlling Chinese dragons.

    There's so much interesting stuff on the dealers' floor (Starcraft II demo!, board games, etc), and yet other times I feel like (to quote Kevin Allen Jr) "I might as well be at a tractor and feed dealership", just a lot of stuff that has no pull.

    Everyone is enthusiastic as hell. I wish there were more time in one evening to play games. Last night we played an 8 on 1 Beast Hunters game: I ran it, they fought a Reghwor. It killed 5 of them, 3 survived to kill it and get its stuff.

    Today I'm running Tenra Bansho Zero demos, and going through the Forge area to learn about some games I've heard little about.

    When things settle down a little more I'll be able to put things into place a little better.

    -Andy

    \n I sat in on the Pinnacle panel. They released Solomon Kane, a 300 page hardback for $40 at noon today. The cover art looks really great and Shane at Pinnacle talked about how they chose to use a licensed character. This is the first licensed character Pinnacle has used and Shane was concerned about using the character of Solomon Kane without creating a closed world setting. One technique he described in the panel was that all PCs are characters who met Solomon Kane and were affected by him. The example he gave was a PC that was a pirate captain who fought Solomon Kane and lost to him, but was spared. That influenced the pirate captain to take up Solomon's cause. This ties in well with the Pinnacle Plot Point approach. I'm interested to check out the book.

    \n OK, very quick update. This was my pick for Surprise Hit of Gencon:

    Dragons of Kir

    Holy hell this game is awesome. It's the most fun 2-player game I've played in a long time. Combine a little Tsuro with Robo Rally, and make it playable in 5-15 minutes, and you have this game. It leaves your palate so clean you can play 3-5 games in a row (as I did) and not feel bogged down or overwhelmed. I bought this game because I liked the art and style and it *appeared* to possibly be a game that might appeal to me. Didn't even play or watch the demo, just bought it and threw down at Embassy Suites with some fellow congoers. But it totally blew me away still. Fucking hands-down awesome, I recommend it to just about anyone.

    Keep your eyes out for this one.

    -Andy

    Posted By: TempleIs there any sign of the Weapons of the Gods Companion?
    \nYes there was. Apparently there was only like 25 copies of Unhallowed Metropolis brought to the con (and sold out in like a few hours), and I haven't run into Brad yet, but I did flip through the Companion. It's interesting, basically a lot of fan mods together, plus a strategy game that is basically "control the city factions/wards" game. Also, it presents a setting-city, and has loresheets ONLY for that city, so you have a campaign starting point plus ways to tie yourself into the town. It's like the Opposite of Lifepaths. Tres cool, and actually very similar to something I'm working on with Tenra Bansho.\n
    Posted By: misubaYes tell us of ashcanniness!
    \nI can't tell too much. I bought a few despite wanting to see some Final Games (I don't have a lot of time for playtesting honestly, but there were a few ideas I could just not say No to). I grabbed Galactic and Acts of Evil.

    More game stuff that surprised me:

    Savage Worlds: Solomon Kane. Big, beautiful book for $50 with tons of scenarios and background in it. All the adventures you will ever have are pretty much written in the book. That was mildly surprising. The REAL cool thing is that I hear that they got bit by the Situation Bug: All of the player characters begin the game having had some kind of interaction with Solomon Kane himself: They met him, were a friend of his, heard stories of him, Fought him (and later changed their ways), etc. So all characters begin with a common thread. That was damn cool.

    Fear Itself: The OTHER Esoterrorists game. If you're looking for a Gumshoe adaptation, I suggest this one. It's slick, plus it gives a lot of advice (including charts) for keeping track of the characters and putting pressure on them. Probably a great Cthulhu engine.

    -Andy
