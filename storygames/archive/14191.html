\n A few friends and I have set up the Ministry of Entertainment.

It's a site that fulfills a need we think the traditional gaming industry has needed for some time: A place to gather a bunch of news and report it, instead of having to go to a billion sites, forums, rss feeds, and mailing lists to understand what, exactly, is going on in the world of non-digital gaming. We'll be writing up our own other things too, like interviews and reviews and other features. Let me know what you think, and any comments or suggestions or other feedback would be welcome.

If you Like us on Facebook, that'd be especially helpful since it'd help put us closer to the 25 Likes we need to have our own reserved URL.

    \n Alright, so here's the basic rundown of the RSS deal.We'd love to put down the full text of the articles in RSS, but there are a few issues we're trying to work out. I'll go over them one by one.

    1) The formatting. The RSS feeds tend to destroy formatting and create something that's terribly difficult to read. We're working just to learn the ins and outs of normal posting.

    2) Ads. Eventually, we're hoping to have ads on the site, and we're not sure how that interacts with RSS feeds. We're hoping to pay for our hosting bills, buy copies of games to review that we couldn't get regular review copies for, and ideally, be able to send some of us to GenCon, Origins, and/or PAX to get more neat stuff for you to read about.

    3) We want to be able to eventually track user stats, and we're not sure how this would work with an RSS feed. We can figure out what games people prefer reading about and see to it that those get more coverage, and figure out what things we're really excited about that people aren't reading about, and maybe decide if we need to describe those better or just let them be.

    4) Comments. We want a nice comment community. Not sure how this would work with RSS feeds. You wouldn't be able to see it on your end, but we've had to slap down more spam comments than we've had real comments. Pharmaceutical spambots seem particularly fond of our Paranoia sale article.

    Once we figure out how to deal with all of those, we should be able to bring you a fully-featured RSS feed. What other comments or suggestions do you guys have about the site?
