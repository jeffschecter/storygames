    It's a ten-foot cube of acidic gel which moves through (ten foot square, natch) dungeon corridors absorbing organic detritus. You know, dead black puddings, shelf fungi, low level adventurers. Imagine the little scrubbing-bubbles things, but in fantasy medival dungeons. Of course, non-organic detritus just gets stuck, so if you manage to kill one, they leave all kinds of goodies behind.

    If I remember rightly, they're quite resistant to normal weapons (being, in essence, a semi-living jello mold) but fire works great.

    From the d20 SRD:
    "A typical gelatinous cube is 10 feet on a side and weighs about 15,000 pounds, though much larger specimens are not unknown."
    That's 1000 cubic feet, or 15 pounds per cubic foot, unless I'm a complete dunce. Interestingly, water is over 62 lb/ft^3, which means a gelatinous cube is significantly less dense than water (or even ice!), and would float very well. (edit) It would actually be about as buoyant as solid cork, and only a little denser than fresh snow, according to this page.

    That'd be a neat Drowning & Falling scenario. Inject a bunch of cubes with enough sea water to make them float just below the surface, then stick them in a pool! "DUDE IF YOU FAIL YOU SWIM INTO FUCKING ACID!"
