\n Hey all, I was trying to come up with an alternative alignment system that's half "one word that describes your personality in general" and half "beliefs/goals" (ala Burning Wheel).

I was in a sleepy haze when I thought: The way that we classically play our fantasy characters. There seems to be a few classic default character play styles. In reality, it's probably just the player playing the same kind of role they always do... However when thinking back over all the times I've played classic fantasy games, it seems that a few character personality in Adventure Games tend to stand out:

Brave
Sneaky
Mysterious

Can anyone think of other personality types, when thinking over the personalities/actions of characters they've played or have seen played? Just wondering if there's any other major archetypes that I've seen but haven't remembered.

-Andy

    \n Thanks guys!

    Eric Provost has been working on this "Classic D&D Red Box Hack", which I played for the first time on Saturday and it was a lot of fun. I was thinking, though, the concept of "alignment" isn't addressed in his hack. So I was thinking that perhaps drawing upon a classic D&D/adventure fantasy mannerism might, I dunno, be rewarded with a bonus die, or cookie. But looking further, and seeing all the "alignment possibilities"... I might be overcomplicating something that might not need to be that complicated. Or in other words, an additional rule that I thought would be clean and straightforward isn't as clean or straightforward as I first thought.

    Hmmmm.

    But I appreciate the feedback. I'll see if I can do anything with it.
