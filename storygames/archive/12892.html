\n Recently I've been big on cute animals, feel-good stuff, things like that. So here's a design challenge! You've got until the 7th of September to come up with a game about animals and stuff. There are a few guidelines (but feel free not to stick to them if it makes you happy).
Edit: You've got until the 14th actually, we've had an extension


- It should have at least one or two of the following animals in it: Cows, Crows, Doggies, Kittens, Squids, Capybara
- I'm thinking there's a player role called The Sun. Wanna put that in there?
- Feel-good, did I mention that? Fuzzy feelings and cute smiles all around

That's it really. And uh, if you do the best game I'll... give you something from my house? Or, I dunno I don't want to judge people.

    \n Ideas I had before stopping work:

    Everyone brings a stuffed animal from their childhood. Play begins telling a true story about the stuffed animal.

    Everyone also brings an action figure from their childhood. Also tell a story.

    Most players play a brought to life version of their stuffed animal. That can also talk. Get traits based on the characteristics of the real animal and the stuffed. A stuffed cow may have "moos" and "predicts weather". Some fixed ones like: strong (biggest stuffed animal), smart (most colors), charismatic (softest). Maybe you play someone else's animal.

    One (or more) players (also?) play their (someone else's?) action figure. These are also brought to life, and are somehow 'corrupt' if not inherently. These are bitter and the 'bad guys'.

    One (or more) players play themselves, except missing what they most like about their life. The stuffed animals and action figures were this character/player's childhood toys. They want to help/ruin his life. At the end of the session you gain what you removed from your life. The reason you didn't have what you want is some (real life former) character flaw.
