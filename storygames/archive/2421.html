The Weird Old Games thread really started dipping into nostalgia, so let's talk about the killer old stuff here. Maybe they were chock full of great ideas saddled with a terrible system, or maybe they had innovative rules in service of a tedious setting. Or maybe they were just throw-the-devil-horns-awesome.

One that was mentioned over yonder and gets my vote is Fringeworthy, which is a wonderful "paths to infinity" sort of concept wrapped up with an extremely loose but compelling backstory. The Mellor, shape-shifting villains that over-ran the interdimensional paths, wiped out their gentle creators and like to eat people's brains, are some of the scariest shit I've ever seen in a game. Our IDET team used to have regular blood draws ... just to be sure. I'll also point out that Fringeworthy's two published supplements deal with a)Dawn of the fuckin' Dead and b)Red fuckin' Dawn!

    How could I not mention The Fantasy Trip? Especially since it's already been hinted at a couple times? It was the first RPG I saw that had a unified resolution system that applied to almost everything: roll multiple dice below one of your three stats for success; more difficult situations add more dice, talents can either modify your stats or reduce the number of dice you roll. Looking back, some of the rules seem unnecessarily complicated, like consulting (admittedly short) tables of possible DX adjustments, and the rule books were written in the days before indexing and organization were invented (it was hard to find specific rules even with the gamemaster's codex, which really wasn't very well organized itself.) But still, going from early D&D, with its standard character classes and no skills (yet) to TFT with its early "build a character" approach was an eyeopener and great fun. Plus, it had some interesting twists on bog-standard fantasy: no divine magic (and maybe no gods?), vampirism is a biological disease, gargoyles are a PC race, powerstones show up for the first time, alchemical bombs and molotov cocktails are standard equipment... it was a pretty good game.

    TFT was and is one of my all-time favorites. Sure, nowadays there are a lot of innovative systems that do what it did better than it did 'em. But it was the first class-free, point build system I ever found (I'm told it wasn't the first published, but it's my first such game). It was also very rules-lite for the time. It very much reflected its roots as a miniatures/combat game, but it was easy to ignore these bits. I have an old friend who still loves to play occasionally.

    Traveller - The first Science Fiction game I played, and the only game I played where character death was a very real possibility during character creation. Imagine rolling to see if your character survives his term in the military.
