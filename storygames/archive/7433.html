\nI'M WITH YOU, JOE!
- this is a roleplaying poem dedicated to Joe.
- in this game Joe is trying to make a point by being ironic, posting a phony game on a game-site.
- if you're Joe; be proud!

PREPARATIONS
- gather, be more than 2 and less than 9 eager players
- if one of you already is Joe, that's set
- if none of you is Joe, you will appoint one Joe
- roll a die: 1 = RPG.net, 2 = Story Games, 3 = The Forge, 4 = RPG Host, 5 = the 20' by 20' room, 6 = Game Chef
- read these rules out loud
- get a timer, set it for 15 minutes

SYSTEM (or something)
- Joe start by placing his hand in the middle of the table
- anyone with his/her hand on the middle of the table may post a comment
- to get in the queue you place your hand on top of Joes
- when Joe has posted, he removes his hand
- the hand at the bottom may now post
- if no hands are in the queue, it is night and the forum is silent (breath calmly, be eager for new day)
- any new day will start when Joe put his hand in the middle of the table
- keep posting until the time is up

SETTING (or something)
- Joe post first, by telling you all what kind of phony game he has posted
- Joe may be as detailed or general as he choose, in his description of the phony game
- Joe will NOT tell you what his point is (and he don't have to know, yet). He may do later in the thread.

- the rest of the players will all object to Joes game, at least initially
- some will take it seriously, and object to such a game in serious terms
- some will see the irony, and debate the point they believe Joe is making

- you will all try to make a constructive discussion out of this. Be persuasive!
- but you will be especially vary of snide comments and insults, they must be answered
- remember at all times that you are discussing Joes game, develop the discussion in that direction too

- when the timer tolls for you, you will all lean back, the thread has been closed (don't ask me why, it happens)
- Joe will end the game by placing his hand on the table
- those of you agreeing with Joe in any way, will place their hands on top of his and say I'M WITH YOU, JOE!
- then the rest of the players will stand up and say anything they feel like saying; I HATE YOU, I CRY FOR YOU, YOU ARE DUMB, or something

.

The end.

.


By the way: this game is not a phony game, it is a serious (and fun I hope) game on forum-communications. I'd like to thank Joe McDonald and Andy Kitkowski for inspiring me to write this game.

    \nI'M WITH YOU, JOE!
    - the virtual edition
    - this is a play-by-forum game dedicated to Joe.
    - in this game Joe is trying to make a point by being ironic, posting a phony game on a game-site.
    - if you're Joe; be proud!

    Special rules
    - be more than 2 and less than 9 eager players, but stay by your computer
    - gather at a forum, virtually
    - use the forum to create a thread (Joe will, of course)
    - make comments in the thread
    - keep commenting until one of you states: I'm with you, Joe!
    - then all of you choose sides as in the basic rules

    Optional rule
    - don't stop until the thread is closed

    Follow the rest of the rules given in basic I'm with you, Joe!

    Be sure to tell outsiders (those not in on the game) that their comments are a bit out of context (the context being the game). Do so in a very polite manner. Do not under any circumstance be rude to outsiders. Do not tell them it's all a game. For them it's not, and they may take offense.

    Have fun!

    \n Tomas,
    Joe is a common name that frequently has duplicates in a given group. Allow me to field this one.

    Inevitably people will want to call one of the Joe's "Big Joe". This literally ALWAYS happens. The trouble is that each and every time, there are strong cases for all the Joe's to be called Big Joe. This will not dissuade people from wanting to differentiate the Joe's by use of the "Big" prefix for only one of them.

    The following is a supplement to "I'm with you, Joe!", designed to play with multiple Joe players, multiple Joe characters, anti-protagonistic Joe's, and inconsistently named characters who occasionally fulfill a Joe role in a social group.

    Big Joe(s)

    While playing I'm with you, Joe, as a Joe (in or out of character), you must strive to be Big. Big is a word with several nuances, and might mean:
    *Loud
    *Using a lot of space with movement and stance
    *Fat
    *Tall and broad shouldered
    *Deliciously sexy in a Scandinavian, post-Grunge sort of way
    *Long-winded and prone to dominating social space
    *Self-assured that you are witty and very intelligent

    Choose from this list 3-5 Big Traits that will describe the Joe you will play (or play as) in I'm with you, Joe. Continually embellish these Traits through play. Disrupting play through the embellishment of your Big Traits is a valid play tactic while playing (or playing as) Joe. At any point in the game someone can say in a bombastic, deep voice "Big Joe!". This has no game effect but to impress upon people that in their opinion you are a Joe, in one of the game's many Joe capacities.

    Endgame

    At the end of the game, whoever was last called Big Joe permanently becomes the Big Joe. People will incessantly call him Big Joe between game sessions now.

    Future Sessions

    In future sessions of I'm with you, Joe, if there are multiple Joes or multiple people wanting to be a Joe, Big Joe automatically trumps them all and is the Joe (Typically, the Big Joe will declare this fact by saying, "But I'm... BIG Joe."). If there are multiple Big Joe's, they both permanently lose their Big Joe status and the game is played with multiple Joe's vying for Big Joe as per the above rules.

    Playtesters
    I would like to thank anyone who has played with me and Big Joe (I was labeled Rockstar Joe). Furthermore, I would like to thank anyone who has played with me and Ninja Joe (I was labeled Big Joe).
