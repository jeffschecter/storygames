Because buying ranks in skills is, like, so judgemental, man.

Suppose we had a game where everyone was judged to have a low middling competency in some common tasks (ex: general "adventurer" abilities in a fantasy mileau), and instead of separate abilities, you have Secrets:

Secret of the Paladin: For a [Sword/Healing/Religion/Charisma] check, you can roll with your ability at any level you deem reasonable, up to Adept.

>

Secret of the Battle Axe Master: For a "Battle Axe" check, you roll with your ability at any level you deem reasonable, up to Grand Master.

I figure if I keep messing with this, I can have a system that's just built of Secrets and Keys. I call it in the Lunar System!!

    So if I were adapting Errantry to this - which I may do - I might approach it this way:

    1. Each character type (= class) gets a "package" of abilities, for each of which they get a bonus die just for having that character type. Knights, for example, have Athletics, Sword, Woodscraft, First Aid, Battleaxe, Aim, Sense Danger, Riding, Mace, Duelling and Discern Truth. Everyone gets 12 each (because of the way the randomized NPC creation works); these are the ones already listed on my character type charts for that purpose.

    2. Each character type has a "cornerstone" ability. Troubadours, for example, have a cornerstone ability of Music. For your cornerstone ability, you get two bonus dice instead of one.

    3. You get a bunch more bonus dice to distribute among the abilities to customize your character. NPCs get them distributed according to the randomizing card draw rules (which are calculated to produce reasonable combinations). At the moment there would be 10 of these dice; that may be too many. The number for NPCs should probably grow as the PCs get advances.

    4. You can bump up your character with as many dice as you like because regardless of how many dice you have in an ability, you can still fail (it just becomes less and less likely). Any of the statistics guys want to chime in on how increasing the number of bonus dice affects the chances of success?

    Dev, I realize the above is a bit of a drift from your goal (removing the abilities as a factor altogether). It might be worth your while to look at Errantry, though, for ideas, if your reason for removing the abilities is to make it easier to generate on-the-fly characters. The NPC creation works by a draw of three cards and placing some ticks on the character sheet, which is set up so that it's obvious where to put the ticks given the cards you drew. You should be able to generate a character (including pools, abilities, Secrets and, if you want, Keys) very rapidly indeed, especially once you're used to the system.
