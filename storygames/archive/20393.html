\n Hi,

So this is my first documented actual play report. I had the opportunity to play a unique game of "The Quiet Year" at the local Story Games meetup last week that I thought was worth sharing.

4 of us wanted to tell the tale of a community of civilian/survivors during a wartime conflict in Eastern Europe. We wanted to look at the game "This War of Mine" through the lens of "The Quiet Year"

This was my actual first time playing The Quiet Year, but I had read the book previously. Knowing what I know now, I would have done a few things differently. I was surprised on how little hacking or modding of the original game needed to facilitate this concept.

I wrote up the full game here.

The Quiet Year was a great game for exploring this idea and I would definitely do something like this again.

    \n Hey Mel! You're welcome! Thank you reading it.

    Yeah The Quiet Year is simply one of the best, easiest games to get going. Do keep in mind that the game's playtime can be long (Easily 4 Hours for a first time game.) I think our tone of the game at the table was probably lighter than the fiction gave to. I think the banter/comments helped diffuse some of the serious tension and no one objected to this.

    I am contemplating doing a game of Microscope and/or Kingdom to tell the story of a community surviving through war in a similar fashion to This War of Mine as well. I'd personally love to play both of them and then compare/contrast the three games in how they handle the concept and themes of community's surrounded by conflict.
