\n So after having a lot of success with and love for my previous Ikea bookcase, I ended up purchasing a second one, to basically hold more books and stuff that were scattered around the house (also board games). It came (delivered; there's no Ikea really near us) in the other day, and I found myself moving all my RPGs to the new one. I'm currently in a holding pattern as I organize the books and decide what will fill the middle shelves, what will stay in the old bookcase, what will be moved to the new, and what to do with my binders of PDF-games that I printed.

It's from the "EXPIDIT" line. Here it is currently in its unfinished form. Think of it kinda like the Death Star from the beginning of SW VI:



This time, I wanted to get some small drawer units for the bookcase. Unfortunately, they were out of black and only had red and white. I ended up going with 2 white units. When they came, I had a plan: Space them out so that the bookcase looks like a giant d6, with the "2 pips" side facing you. Unfortunately, in the above pic not all the shelves are filled, and you can see the off-white wall in the back, so the effect is lost currently. Later, though...

Here's a step back away from the thing, with the old shelves (left) and the new ones (right) fighting for dominance of my study. Because of all the movement, both of them are currently pretty bare (the left one used to contain all the books, which is why the top area is empty until I clean up around the house and sort out my books). Once I get my Japanese materials in one area and the boardgames sorted out, they should be filled out nicely. You can even see Maya peeking out from the unit: I'm leaving one "hole" in each unit bare for the cats, they seem to love hanging out in them.



Anyway, as a review: They're solid, sturdy units. They are both extremely easy (but time-consuming) to put together, however, the laaaaast part, where you basically tie the frame all together, can be a bit of a strenuous pain in the butt (I was able to assemble then lift it myself, but can imagine it might be a pain if you can't lift heavy things without help, and have no help). The small shelving cubes allows for more interesting combinations of "stuff" than traditional shelves, IMO. I like that I was able to fit all my anime and TV DVDs in one cube, all my movies in a second. On one "traditional shelf", it might have been harder to do.

Ah, they are pretty tall (a little over 6 feet), so it can be hard to remember to dust up there. Other than that, they're some solid pieces of storage, sturdy and pretty nice looking.

Also, when thinking further of this post, was wondering if anyone else has other IKEA or related loot ("The Container Store", etc) that they've purposed as Gaming Materials?

-Andy

    \n I really like The Container Store's Intermetro shelving. Easy to assemble without tools, very sturdy, one person can move them when unloaded. The 4 ft x 18 inch racks fit 5 comic short boxes or 4 magazine boxes or standard book boxes per shelf, and can fit 5 or 6 shelves (6th shelf only for light things and if you're tall and don't mind lifting above your shoulder). I also like to use the plastic shelf liners for the wire racks, to cut down on noise sliding boxes in and out. They'll ship if you don't have a store nearby. Comic short boxes also fit DVDs and manga very efficiently, as well as comics, graphic novels, games, and files.
