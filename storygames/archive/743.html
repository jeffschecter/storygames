    Now, I don't want to get into horse flogging, but it seems to me like there's a disconnect here. Vincent's "Fruitful Void" and whether you should design what does or doesn't matter seem to me to be skew concepts.

    On the one hand, the whole idea of the fruitful void is that you should explicitly not treat central idea directly, instead designing around them in a way that leads play to treat them.

    On the other hand, the matters/doesn't matter dichotomy strikes me as very different. DWM suggests that we should focus design on the premises of the game, where DWDM suggests that the design should be a stage where the premises could play out.

    Not to be telling you what you already know -- just stating my own understanding as the basis of my next statement. Which is that the two concepts aren't completely orthagonal, but neither are they inextricable.

    If nothing else, I'd propose that a focused design could directly address all it's premises and mechanize all of them, or structure around them and leave them as emergent behavior. Probably a DWDM design would need to suggest it's premises in it's ruleset in order to provide enough of an enclosure to get the premise to emerge repeatably.

    So point being: I don't know if discussion of the fruitful void or emergent behavior _is_ flogging a dead anything.
