\n I've recently begun wondering if there are any story games that focus on the campaign trail or the combativeness of local politics. I've seen a lot of games that tackle unique and uncommon genres but have never come across anything like this. The game would preferably be non-partisan to allow room for players to bring their own ideas into play. Hip me to what I'm missing if this exists.

    \n Underground (the cyberpunk game from the 1990s) has a pretty unique political change system where the characters battling for their neighborhood adjusts that neighborhood's traits in terms of their security, freedom, wealth, and so on. I really haven't seen anything quite like that since that time. The nearest thing is White Wolf's Damnation City system, but that's primarily about what Satanic blood drinker is in control of what and what that means in terms of their endless backstabbing and loathing.

    Rafael, tell us more about both War Room and ViewScream.
    \nRight on. ViewScream is a varp (video-augmented role-playing game) -- you play it via G+ Hangouts, and you stay in character the whole time. So you're a player, talking to other people via computer screen, pretending to be a member of starship crew, talking to other people via computer screen. And there are horrible things on your ship, like zombies or things with tentacles and everybody usually dies.

    This is the playtest doc (version 5): http://rafaelchandler.com/viewscream.zip

    And you can hear some actual play here: http://youtu.be/OdeF-scs2iM

    So what Kerra has done is, she's taken the four characters (Weapons, Medical, Bridge, and Engineering) and replaced them with politicos, and she's replaced the outer-space setting with conference-call video-chats, but the core concept is the same -- you stay in character the whole time you're playing, and if you want to survive (figuratively, in this case), you have to coax others to help you while simultaneously backstabbing the daylights out of people.
