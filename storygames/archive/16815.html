    \n I like Down with the Clown. But how's about Post-Capitalist Roleplaying in a World Gone to Shit.

    The setting would be a neo-medieval world, in which some countries that we know about today still exist as full nation-states with armies and even space programs. But the rest of the world is overcome with corrupt warlords and their knight or samurai analogues, called "Clowns."

    You, the player, as one of the Clowns, would be charged with all kinds of missions. You'd sabotage sprouting governments, terrorize foreign intervention, and so on. The downside would be the increased volatility of your position, the increased distrust sown among other Clowns around you, to say nothing of the warlords who order you around.

    There should also be some interesting play with rhymes, such as the rulership of "Towns," the invoking of "Nouns," the use of the color "Brown," maybe even something involving "Frowns."

    I dunno. Sounds really cool. I'd be up for a playtest.

    \n Alright, how's about this:

    A Dogs in the Vinyard hack called Clowns in the Bullshit Parade --

    Instead of Dogs, you have Clowns, and instead of towns with sorcerers who are manipulated by real demons, you have towns with government officials who are manipulated by corporations. In CitBP, though, as opposed to DitV, the characters have a certain naive awareness of the cynical nature of their approach to societal problems. And, of course, they don't have guns. Maybe that's the emergent quality of this hack, something beyond the original DitV rules: No "samurai" quality, mostly the need to go around acquiring "permission" to do things. Other than that, it's the same set-up: Clowns enter towns, only to find how unhelpful the regional governments are (due to a variety of possible corporate vices), after which they "cleanse" the town with their acquired licenses and protest and stuff. Then they go to the next town.

    Whaddya all think?
