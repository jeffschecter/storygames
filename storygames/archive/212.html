How do you stop snowballing an idea?

Kuma
February 2006 edited February 2006 in Story Games
A big part of my problem with writing (and one of the main things holding me back from getting things finished) is the tendency to snowball an idea. I find all of these great, fun new angles and new possibilities and suddenly what was a small idea is this massive project that I dig into but which eventually overwhelms me and I just lose interest.

So how do you keep yourself so very focused? There are some really, really focused designs from the people here, so I was hoping there'd be some good advice.
  • shreyas
    February 2006

    Just get into the habit of stopping at intervals, remembering what you set out to do, and set aside in some kind of idea bucket all the cool things that you like, but don't accomplish this.

    I've watched a lot of designs go through a yo-yo period where they bloat up with stuff and then are pruned very harshly and then the cycle repeats.

  • Josh Roby
    February 2006
    Parse.

    Realize that you, as an artist or author or creator of whatever stripe, are going to create many different pieces over the course of your artistic career, and you do not need to lump all the hotness into the piece you are currently working on. Try to define what you are trying to accomplish with this piece right here and right now, and don't let the new stuff get jammed in there just because it's new. Consider stowing ideas away for when you write 'the sequel' or 'the sourcebook' or 'the second edition.'

    "Consider stowing ideas away for when you write 'the sequel' or 'the sourcebook' or 'the second edition."'

    This is actually a fun one. I keep a file in my current project, called "Supplemental ideas".

    Actually putting ideas in there lets me draw a clear mental line of "this is for later, but won't go forgotten."

  • Joshua A.C. Newman

    1: Remember that you will write other games if you ever finish this one.
    2: Every time you find something new that makes you excited, think: does this support my game's theme? Does this actually help me make my game be about what I want it to be about? If not, I put it in my Shreyas Bucket.
    3: If you can't say what your game's about, 2: will be supremely difficult. Answer that and it will be much easier.
