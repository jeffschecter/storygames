What's everyone played in the last week or so? How'd it go?

This past week, my mom and stepdad, Don, came to visit Lisa and I. They stayed for a week and mostly hung out to fix our gutters and do our laundry (seriously, we couldn't stop them), and before they left, we managed to get them to sit down and play a game of Bang! with us. I'd figured they'd like it, my mom has always been a big board & card game fan, and Don is a huge spaghetti-western nut.

They dug it muchly.

Don's not much for strategy or picking up the bigger picture of a strategy game, but the straigtforward goals for each of the different roles, along with the color being deeply sunk in the western kept him very entertained. I think it really helped that he drew Outlaw and my mom drew Sherriff, so he got to gang up with Lisa to kill off me mum's character right away.

They dug it so much they asked us to bring it with them when we visit them next. Rock on.

So, what have you been playing? If nothing recently, what's the last thing you played, and how long ago was it?

    In the last week and a half, I've played Nine Worlds, D&D, a very different D&D game and Dogs in the Vineyard.

    Going in reverse order, the Dogs game was a fill in for a game where half the players couldn't show. We only got to make character, explain the rules to the one player unfamiliar with the system and and barely begin exploring the first town. No conflicts got past talking, which is a shame. All my dogs characters wind up a bit flat, right up to the Accomplishment stage, at which point our GM introduces awesome stakes that work out great.

    One D&D game is a total minmaxing powergaming dungeon crawl through the World's Largest Dungeon. The WLD is good but flawed (some regions are more interesting than others). This last session suffered from the two front line fighter types being upset that their character actually took damage, but otherwise the game is decently entertaining.

    The other game is very much the GM trying to force D&D to do things it never was intended to do, like focus on stories and characters. Hence the system often is opposed to what we're trying to do, but the game is quite fun because of the characters and players involved. It's sort of an anti-D&D game: we aren't playing the heroes, we're playing the useless flunkies and NPCs the heroes hired to take care of the wizard's tower they cleared out.

    The Nine Worlds game this time really hit home the problems and complications with my character's backstory. Which seems like I've been waiting for that for a long time, since it's like the second or third session we've played, and the game started around January.
