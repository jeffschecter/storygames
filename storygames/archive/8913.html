\n Greetings!
\n
\nI have decided to pursue Character Illustration Commissions more seriously, as well as starting to develop my own products for games.
\n
\nA la the prolific Storn Cook I'd like to start offering some of these works for free in formats that people could find useful.
\n
\nBelow is a series of characters I've done for a couple of FATE 3.0/Eberron adventures I've run for friends and open gaming events at my FLGS. I plan on producing these 2 scenarios as a test drive for "adventure production" and offering them on my site in their complete form for free. Eventually I'd like to start publishing other adventures I've developed through RPGNow and other online vendors.
\n
\nThese images are only small thumbnails, linking to bigger 24-bit PNG images with transparency. They are easy to drop onto various backgrounds for character sheets. Be warned that the sizes range from 350kb to 650kb.

    \nMatt Wilson -- I agree that my work could be a little more "active" -- but I find myself more drawn and interested in the aspects of character that happen in between the action. If that makes any sense\u2026
    \n
    \nNathan H -- I use both traditional and digital methods in my work. I scan textures and drawings and composite those with digital elements. I often create a rough sketch, scan that in, then print it out in "blue line" on bristol and re-pencil the piece. Then scan that in again and eliminate the "blue line." That is how I did the "Meniscus" pieces.
    \n
    \nThe Mouse Guard pieces were much more direct. I simply scanned the drawings I had done in pen in my daily sketchbook and colored them like a comic book.
