\n So Shreyas and I have been re-skinning his quickstart Exalted hack, RADIANT, with the flavor of mythic Arabia and Islamic angels fallen to earth, which meant it was time to do a proper cover:



At that size it's hard to see the detail, so here's the title at 50%:



This one was effectively free (total cost still around $10): the hardest working gold texture in indie RPGs, a scanned silk sari for the fabric, and public domain embellishments from Islamic art. Since we didn't have to re-buy the rights to use the stock gold leaf (once you've licensed something royalty-free, you can use it in as many projects as you like!), the only cost was in man-hours.

    \n The only suggestions I have are to add a bit more separation between some of the elements, especially the letters in "Radiant" which currently run together a bit and aren't exactly the same height (bottom right of the R, top left of the T). Unfortunately, you might have to make the title font slightly smaller if you add more spacing. The other elements that run together are the squareish gold brackets and the flowery swirl in the middle (which are similar but not the same in style), so I would suggest making the flowery swirl 10% smaller (keeping it centered) just to separate it a bit from the top and bottom enclosures.

    This is really nitpicking, though, since the cover is hot.

    \n OK, some stuff that made my eyes stumble:
    * The lacing is, I'm sure, rectilinear... but there's an optical illusion where it seems to bulge in the middle (vertically) because of the curvature of the cropping, I think. Not sure what you could do about that, other than a VERY tiny inward skew of the pointy bits, to offset the illusion.
    * It might be a scanning problem, but the upper lacing seems darker than the lower lacing. In general, all of it seems rather muted (which isn't very "radiant" of it). I'd bump up the lightness of all elements that aren't purple (more to that below).

    And, if I were to keep fiddling, for my own (perhaps too-precious) aesthetics:
    * Do a subtle outer glow on the word "Radiant." Nothing radical, possibly not even consistent with each letter. But some literal "radiation" for the Radiant. (Note that I think the letter spacing and sizing is spot-on.)
    * I like Jonathan's idea of slightly reducing the inner, leaf-and-vines, swirl graphic to achieve more separation. I wouldn't have been able to put my finger on why it looks "crowded" without much more thought, had he not mentioned that.
    * I'm not sure I'm in love with the top- and bottom-most "rose" elements in the lace that break up its otherwise rectilinear appearance. It also might be contributing to my sense of "crowded" above--the space they demand at the page top and bottom (and the whitespace that they leave there) means all the other elements of the layout have to clump up in the remaining 5/6th of the area.
    * The purple is symbolically appropriate to the game theme. But, man, it's freakin' dark, almost gloomy (which is, obviously, rather in contrast with the notion of "radiant"). I'd begin to explore lighter shades of maroon, just for starters.

    But, hey, all of that is, like, the 0.05% that takes a 99.5% to 100% (and it's in MY eyes, of course--someone else might tweak the last 0.5% in other ways). Good work, all-in-all!
