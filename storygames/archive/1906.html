Crispin Glover is Here to Kill Me

I know I have a lot of support for the mechanics as they stand, and I know that I haven't playtested them yet. But, let's just say, they don't work for me anymore. It's not the game I want to play. All I know is that I love my title and want to do something more with it.

This is open to the public; anyone feel free to contribute in any amount.

My goal is to never lose sight of the title as a description of the game as much as possible.

So, if you had the title to work with and the goal above, where would you go with the game design? Feel free to stick it on existing rules systems or what have you. If this would work as a Polaris-hack, then that's fine with me (provided Ben is okay with it).

Any ideas?

    I second Larry's comment. Keep Crispin Glover. In fact, I think the printed rules of the game should require all participants to be able to name 5 Crispin Glover movies before playing. I'm serious. Can't name them? You're not allowed to play.

    I'm imagining a game where every character in the bar who's ready to be killed by CG has a story to tell. A story that explains why that creepy bastard is there to kill them. That's where I'm imagining the story and the game is to be had. The flashbacks and storytelling of the damned.

    I'm imagining that the flashback scenes, the stuff leading up to the ever-present final bar scene, that earns each player a bit of currency. A currency that might just get you enough plot immunity to survive your bout with CG. Maybe. Like, the result of a successful conflict/scene might be that your character has a gun hidden under the table. Or maybe a good flashback means that you've poisoned CG, and he knows you're the only one with the antidote. Shit like that.

    A handful of good flashback scenes each, maybe some incentive to twist your individual stories together, and then the capping scene where CG tries to off everyone. Chances are, everyone dies. But maybe, just maybe, you get out with your skin. But probably not.
