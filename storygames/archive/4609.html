    \n Since I've run PTA with a ton of new people (including plenty who have never gamed before), I find that there's a recognizable fan mail progression:

    For the first time around the table, virtually no one gives out any fan mail.
    Then someone gets off a good line and gets a fan mail from someone.
    This sets up a trickle of fan mail for good lines.
    Everyone looks really hard for something they can give fan mail to everyone for, in other words, even if someone's ideas and play are routinely awful, someone will work out a way to give them fan mail and not have them be left out.
    Near the end of the game, people are a little tired of the game and want to know how the show's going to end, so there will be flurries of fan mail followed by drought.

    Note that in extended, season-length play, the last entry doesn't happen (the Pitch is exhausting!), so the dynamic can and will change further.

    Very rarely in first-time groups is there ever a fanmail for "great setup" or "I like that scene you just requested", it's always "great roleplaying" or (!) "that's perfect for the show."
