\n So I was kinda complaining to Jeph at CollectiveCon the other day... well, not so much complaining rather lamenting... that it was hard for me to create a campaign world to my own taste in 4e. It came down to the fact that I simply love games with human opponents (nations fighting nations, people fighting cultists, and most "monsters" being people, who can be reasoned with at times, killed at others; armies of opposing generals fighting, one nation against another, etc). A campaign world I've kinda dragged from 2e to 3e I couldn't see translating into 4e, simply for the fact that "humanoid" encounters (elves, humans, dwarves, etc) only go up to about Level 2. After that, I can change "Orc Raider" or Gnoll Huntmaster" into "Barbarian Raider" or "Human Shadownhunt Cultist", but after that it's mostly all Flameskulls and Grells and Manticores.

I was thinking that I'd be in the market to see Humanoid "monsters" from level 5-25. Badass humanoid opponents to throw against increasingly powerful PCs, perhaps with unusual abilities (or completely normal abilities at high power: But decidedly NOT me having to make like 25th level Evil Rangers and Evil Clerics with all their stats to throw against the PCs each time; I grew tired of that level of complication quickly in 3e).

So what Jeph mentioned was simply forgetting what the monster IS, look at their stats, and simply change a few damage types and descriptions to again rewrite the "monster" as a person, perhaps rewriting size higher than "Medium" back down to Medium. Jeph mentioned that he changed a damage type, and effectively rewrote the Gelatinous Cube as a grappling Luchador wrestler.

I went back home and popped open the MM, and basically stabbed my finger into the book randomly.

* Displacer Beast: Now a cultist of a dread Shadow Lord. It blends into half reality-half shadow plane. Its "Bite" is now a "Cold Touch", it's "Tentacle" a "Shadowy Limb" which extends from its cloak.

* Rockfire Dreadnaught: Now a Master Swordsman. Even getting close to him means he's gonna hit you (Waves of Flame fire aura == whirling iado blade). Fist of Flame = It's swords slash so fast that they have a reach of two (he launches forward into an attack stance, then returning to a defensive stance immediately). Brimstone Rock == Expertly thrown kunai blade. Still resistant to fire, as he can nimble dodge out of the way before fire hits him.

*Sorrowsworn Reaper : Now Kenshiro. Sorrow's Scythe is simply a badass martial arts attack. Reaping Blow is Kenshiro pointing at someone before he kicks their ass, telling them how he's gonna kick their ass. Bleak Visage = Still fear, because they've seen what his martial arts can do.

* Needlefang Drake Swarm: ...hmmm... ... ...hmmmmmmm... OK, it doesn't work with ALL monsters. (^.^)

Anyway, that trick helped me develop a bunch of humanoid-ish opponents at least for characters level 1-10 (my personal "sweet spot"), the ones where I didn't just take "gnoll" and replace it with "human", etc. It's a good trick, and because of it I've thought of a nifty homebrew setting for a future game of 4e. Just wanted to throw Jeph's idea out there in case it gets others thinking as well.

-Andy

    \n You know what I love about 4e? With monsters built by different rules from PCs, class based templates, the simplified NPC creation rules, and the fact that the DMG falls just short of coming out and saying "never, ever stat out NPCs the way you would a PC", it's entirely possible to envision a setting in which the party Fighter is the only "Fighter" in the entire world.

    I'm not sure what to do with that yet, but I like it.

    \nThe potential for this idea is more obvious when you say "the party wizard is the only wizard in the entire world" or "the party cleric is the only cleric in the entire world" or whatever. You see their powers as something amazing and interesting again, instead of commonplace among NPCs. For example, a lot of cleric powers are based off of saintly (or godly) miracles, so you could run a whole campaign based on the idea that the cleric in the party is a messiah or saint, and the other priests of the setting have no supernatural powers.
