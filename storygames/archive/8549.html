\n I know it's not strictly a game question but I couldn't think of a better forum to put it in.

Here's the issue. If I want to use some classic monsters in my creative work will I have IP issues with WotC?

Clearly they can't own dragons or giants, but what about aarakocras and rust monsters? If I want to use a metallic descriptor for my giant flying lizard are there issues?

I thought people here may already have some experience with these kinds of issues.

I don't want to be overly cautious about this but I don't want to step on any toes either.

Thanks for your help.

    \n I'll bet the legalese in the GSL and associated documents on the Wizards site spells out what you can't use. Take a peek there.

    In the end, you can be right, you can be wrong, and regardless of either of those, you can be sued. Wizards (Hasbro) has a lot more money than you. This incarnation of the owner of D&D hasn't been all that litigious, but you'd hate to do a lot of work and then get a cease and desist letter.

    Why do you need to "borrow" these game-specific monsters, anyway? Can't you make up your own stuff and just avoid the whole problem (and avoid your audience poo-pooing your work as "too derivative")?
