    \n In Cthulhu Dark, you start at 1 Insanity. When you experience something horrific, roll a d6, and if it's higher than your current Insanity level, you gain a level. This has worked well in as a one-shot system: I find that at least one or two PCs reaches 6 Insanity within a four-hour slot.

    In Unnamable, the insanity mechanic is pretty much the same, but uses a d20 instead. You start at 20 Sanity, and if your d20 roll is less than your current level, you 1) lose a point of Sanity, 2) gain a point of Madness, and 3) place that point into one of the Madness boxes that help you in certain circumstances.

    I like the idea of Madness as a mechanical reward, but I'm concerned that 20 points of Sanity is too much. Might try 12 points/a d12 the first time out.
