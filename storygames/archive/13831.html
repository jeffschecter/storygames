    Marshall,

    \n

    The problem with ASR is that you'll typically have at least half of a six person group talking at once, so unless you have lapel mics on each, sorting out the different speakers will be beyond any ASR I've used. It's hard for a human, and I say this having done some fine-detail transcription of gaming data.

    \n

    David,

    \n

    I'm actually thinking of going the mixer-and-lapel-mics route, but I have a professional interest in recorded RPG sessions, so it's worth it to me to sink some money into it. If you somehow have a mixer, Reaper is great for good multi-track recording. But if you're looking for cheap, a good omnidirectional USB mic can run about $60, and can get pretty good quality. Even cheaper, though, could be to use your computer's built-in mic; have you heard my Dungeons and Dread AP post? That was taken with the built-in mic in my MacBook Pro recorded into Reaper. Is that the sort of quality you're looking for?
