    \n Hi Luca!

    Who has authority over an NPC is not clearly defined, and either of the two obvious choices is acceptable. It's a matter of group preference.

    1. Framing player introduces an NPC, hands her off to another player to portray, with a note about which side she's on.

    2. Framing player introduces an NPC as strongly as possible, making her agenda and desires plain, hands her off to another player, and hopes for the best.

    I much prefer #2 but both work equally well. It can be really fun to subvert someone's intentions for an NPC with the right group, but with the wrong group that becomes frustrating.

    If you are just playing an NPC in a scene but have no personal involvement, you don't need to risk Reputation. If you want to introduce new NPCs, insert your own PC, or otherwise muck around in the scene and wreck stuff, you need to pay.

    \n Luca, it's not so much about the character having personal involvement but you as a player. If you invest your reputation and introduce new characters into the scene, you stand to win or lose reputation in that conflict.

    If you do not invest reputation, any characters you play will have been assigned to you- you're just along for the ride, there to ramp up the stakes for the spotlight character and otherwise add color to the scene.
