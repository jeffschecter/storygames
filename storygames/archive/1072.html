Okay, so, remember when we were talking about Web 2.0 apps for gaming? Well, I went out and made mine.

It's called Abulafia (after a computer in Foucalt's Pendulum; after Abraham Abulafia, a Spanish Kabbalist), and it's a User-Extensible Random Generation machine. Which is to say, it's a wiki where you input random tables (according to a specific format) and it spits back results. The coolness is you can call from other people's tables and construct massive coolness in so doing.

To seed the field, I put in a couple of Story Games Names Project pages, a clone of the AG&G Oracle, and a couple of other goodies, and I have massive plans to convert & add lots more content in the days & weeks to come. But, I hope you'll contribute, too. It's a place where we make random generators for each other to use in our gaming!

It's a work in progress and I'll update to this thread when I make significant changes. For now, I recommend reading the instructions on the main page, and studying an existing page or two before making your own tables - it can be fickle if you don't do things just so. Believe me, I'm working on that.

Ready?

Go Play!
