    \n There was so much new indie goodness, it's very hard to pick five. But here we go:

    1. Poison'd. Vincent's pirate game is not just white-hot design, it also facilitated one of the best gaming sessions of my life. This game is important to me, above and beyond its contents.

    2. Steal Away Jordan. Everyone talks about the power of this book's premise and presentation (as well they should), but Julia's game also has clever and compelling mechanics, too.

    3. Psi Run. This game is designed to be read, learned, and played (even with game noobs) all in one session. I think it actually achieves this goal. The final game product (it's in ashcan now) will be an important addition to the hobby. I hope the final form is a beautiful and inspirational object as well as a sharp text.

    4. Galactic. Matt's game has grown up from a precocious teen into a thoughtful adult. The game is firing on all cylinders now, and only lacks some playtesting to refine some budget and resource numbers (which all you ashcan purchasers will no doubt accomplish). Each game of this, for me, has been more fun than the last.

    5. Bliss Stage. This was a shocker for me, in a way. I expected Ben's game to be hot, but I didn't expect it to grab me (I'm not really into anime, giant robots, or teen angst -- unless Buffy is involved). But I was so wrong. The game just gets a hold on you with its grabby, grabby system. I only played a demo, but it was powerful and fun. I snatched up the book immediately after. And I just found out last night that one of my players is a huge anime/giant robot fan, so there's a good chance of this one going into the rotation.

    Five is not enough! I also got 44, Sign in Stranger, Roanoke, Full Light Full Steam, Shock 1.1, Dirty Secrets, Know Thyself, Acts of Evil, Blossoms are Falling, and Giants.

    And I totally forgot to grab Sweet Agatha and The Roach Returns. Doh!

    \n Thanks for all of the Hero's Banner compliments. I was very happy with the strength of the demo, especially when I was able to see people coming around from "Oh, demoes are boring" to "Wow, I'm actually having a lot of fun." That's the stuff that makes me happy to be a game designer.

    My favorite demoes, though, have to be Steal Away Jordan and Bliss Stage for entirely different reasons. Steal Away Jordan honestly made me a bit emotional in just one scene. In a way I was glad when it ended, but at the same time I knew that the game was demanding more play from me.

    Bliss Stage just hit me out of nowhere. I sort of deliberately avoided learning about the game until GenCon, and I'm glad I did. When Ben explained that my character's mech was literally composed of his various relationships -- girlfriends, best buds, whatever -- my jaw hit the table and it took a while for the gears to start turning again.

    Of course this isn't to say that I didn't have a blast playing in a bunch of other games from new ones like The Committee for the Exploration of Mysteries to older ones like Universalis.

    \n Thanks for the kind words on The Committee, and thanks to Eric Finley for giving me some great feedback Wednesday night to tighten my demo. My top five:

    1. Dirty Secrets - Seth had a clever demo and I'm a sucker for the genre, which he has captured in an awesome fashion.
    2. Steal Away Jordan - I got to demo this one twice, and Julia did a great job showing both the great mechanics and emotional power of the game. My almost exclusively White Wolf playing buddy bought two copies - it's that good.
    3. Grey Ranks - I'd rank it higher but I only got to watch part of a demo, not play in one. That said, Polish sons leaving their mothers behind to fight a hopeless battle never looked so good.
    4. Dragons of Kir - An indie board game that Andy K brought to the Embassy Suites one night. A bunch of us played and ended up buying our own copies soon afterward.
    5. The Ashcan Front - A heaping helping of awesome to be had. I was tempted to buy them all, but realized that this wasn't really the purpose. So I limited myself to Galactic, 44, Psi Run, and Sweet Agatha. I'll be ready to buy the final versions of many more, though.

    \n I wound up collecting about twenty pounds of goodness to haul back home, but here are the top five that I'm itching to get my play on here:

    1) Bliss Stage. This excites me more than is probably healthy.
    2) Poison'd. I know that this will delight and horrify not only my regular gamer group, but many of the non-gamer improvisers that I will drag kicking and screaming on board.
    3) Shock: v1.1. Now I will actually be able to explain to other people how to play this awesome game.
    4) Cold City. Took me totally by surprise - this pushes all the buttons of my gamer buddies.
    5) Grey Ranks. Originally a hard sell, but I saw eyes widening and people shifting in their chairs as I described it to folks tonight. It will hit the table soon.

    Also on the short list are Giants, Acts of Evil, Steal Away Jordan, Shooting The Moon, Contenders, and Galactic, among others. Between those, a fresh new Sorcerer and Sword campaign, and playtesting, I feel like it's going to be an exciting, busy year.
