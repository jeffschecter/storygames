What do you call all those other characters?

Clinton
January 2007 edited January 2007 in Game Design Help
Ok, when you are making a story, you have protagonists and antagonists.

You also have an array of other characters, primarily contagonists, guardians, and reason and emotion characters. These guys all have specific role names, but is their a name for them as a whole? I don't necessarily mean, "Is there an accepted name?" You can make one up. I started to use "sides," but it makes them sound unimportant, and they are vastly important.

    I use supporting characters in both Nine Worlds and Dust Devils (new edition, that is). I rather like it that way.

    However, I use the term exactly as a replacement for NPC/GM character/etc. It's not that I don't think of them as supporting characters (I do). It' s that, occasionally, games work out in such as way that "main characters" (a term I used specifically in Dust Devils for what we'd all recognize as Player Characters) aren't actually main characters in the literary sense.

    That is, sometimes player characters are actually supporting characters (again, in the literary sense, not the gaming terminology sense). And, more rarely, no player characters are main characters.

    Troy,

    Thanks! That helps, and gives me something to riff on. I think I'm going to call them "Anchors."

    A brief explanation: protagonists and antagonists are built around who they are and what they want. Anchors are built in the game by who they are, what they do, and where they are from. The last one is important: world creation happens as you create supporting characters, or anchors. They provide context and resources for protagonists and antagonists.
