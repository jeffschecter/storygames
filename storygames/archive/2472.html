My significant other and I played. It worked very nicely as a two person game - I didn't think it would. About my SO: She's played only a few rpgs: Breaking the Ice, InSpectres, My Secret Waitress and now this one. She's had problems with role-playing games before, but this one was easier for her, because as she put it, the rules are easier and she didnâ€™t have to talk too much or role-play too much. This I found interesting, because she had a real hard time with BtI.

We played this game using mostly descriptions and very little role-playing with the characters. For example, one thing in chapter one I narrated was, "As we're traveling up the river, the ice breaks and we fall through, I quickly pull a rope out of my pack and use it to pull us out of the cold water." For her next turn, she narrated, "We build a fire to get warm and dry out our clothing."

The game played out in just under an hour and we played using the 10 token version, which is the smallest number of tokens per player allowed in the rules. If you haven't seen the game, it works by players narrating things that happen during the first two chapters and each thing is powered with one or more tokens, then the players narrate how the spirit world changes those things, and finally, as the engineers descend into their madness, they consume those things and become more powerful, then they finally try to catch the Iliamna monster. Everything is resolved via the hand game.

What was really fun:

Playing the Hand Game was a blast. It really added to the fun, because you had to try and fool the other player. It added a bit of mystery and luck to the game. The narration went very quickly and the story was created quickly. It was a simple and fast game. The tokens, sticky-notes and the hand game made this a very physical game, and moving the sticky notes around and marking them up was fun.

What didn't work:

1. When we removed a things last token via the Hand Game, there really was no point to try to consume it. So, the new rule to fix it:

If the last token is removed from a thing, it disappears from play. There must be at least two things per player on the table after chapter two.

2. And in Consuming and Distorting, you can get tokens via the Hand Game with no risk. So, the new rule to fix this is:

You can only get tokens via the Hand Game in the Consuming and Distorting when you don't have enough tokens to try to consume or distort.

3. The Distorting takes way too long. It needs to play in half the time. To fix this the new rule is:

If she loses: the thing is distorted, counters spent are added half to the thing, half to the center of the table.
