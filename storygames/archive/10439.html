    \n I like the kind of puzzle where you have to figure out what a particular group of people have in common, like in serial killer stories. You can adapt that to all sorts of situations, and it doesn't really even need to be a complete puzzle with a grand hidden truth to have fun recognizing the parallels and seeing how they are made distinct. (The current mspaintadventures comic plays with this, as each protagonist is vexed by a close relative with a strange obsession.)

    You could also turn logistic and tactical requirements into noncombat puzzles. So, for instance, maybe the characters have a short span of time to organize several meetings between spies and government officials without certain of them discovering the involvement of the others.

    \n One thing that might work - and which I am hoping to implement in a WM-style game that's supposed to be starting up around here - is making piecing together all of those cool ruins' origin a puzzle.

    You asked for sources, so one thing I have in mind is the books you could find all over the Baldur's Gate games that had bits of world history.

    Now, some of those were cool and others were just a yawn to read, so I've been thinking about payoffs for figuring out how some of the history fits together.

    Some, of course, could just be a fancier way of opening up new areas - "If kata-hem did in fact turn traitor on the lords of Iron Belly, the caves in which he hid his armies should be... *here!*" Or clues to cool items, etc.

    I've also been thinking about "narrative" payoffs. So, e.g., revelations that would change the way PCs think about things they think they already know, like, "crap... these 'revolutionaries' we've been helping were really the ones who slaugthered the ruling class' ancestors back in the Endless War..." And you could tie historical mysteries into any personal quests the PCs might have.
