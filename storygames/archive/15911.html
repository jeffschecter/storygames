\n I've published a new game on my website in the form of a series of downloadable leaflets/pamphlets: I already piloted a game in this format earlier in the month as a proof-of-concept, but I'd really like to know what people think about the idea.
\n
\nFirst Issue is a Golden Age Superheroes-style game with minimalist rules: each playbook provides the rules to play and run the game, as well as a premade superhero character...
\n
\nThe Seraph: Angelic Defender of the Downtrodden!
\n
\nDynama: Unstoppable Woman of Energy!
\n
\nDr. Fauna: Master of the Animal Kingdom!
\n
\nPhotus: Mightiest Mechanical Marvel of the Age!
\n
\nBesides feedback on the overall concept and execution, I'd really like a couple of other solid contributions:
\n
\nFirst, more playbooks: if you've got an idea for a character for the game, please do share it with me; I'd like a lot of these playbooks to be available for players to choose from.
\n
\nSecond, as I was laying out the first playbook, it struck me that the four Power statements on the front page for each character could just as easily be replaced (or at least augmented) by illustrations. How awesome would that be? Someone asks how their power works and you say "It works like that," pointing to an illustration of the power in action! I think it would work but I'm no artist, so to go that route with the development of the idea, I'd need a collaborator with some skill at drawing superheroic-style comic art.
\n
\nI'm open to offers and suggestions: I don't know what direction further development of this idea will take, so this would partly be an exploration of a working model for collectible RPGs in general.

    \n I've spruced up the rules a bit and revised the first four playbooks in accordance with the minor changes, plus I've added two more:
    \n
    \nMiss Mob: The Invincible One Woman Army!
    \n
    \nThe Haste: The Man who can Outrun Death!
    \n
    \nThe thing I find interesting about games in this style is the absence of a core rulebook: indeed, in my trial run, Secret Dossiers, there are 8 types of action that characters can take, but any single playbook only names 4 of them and only tells you what 3 of those actually are. Any subset of the playbooks only contains the minimum you need to know to play a game with those characters in.
