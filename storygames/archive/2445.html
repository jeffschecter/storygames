    Posted By: droogthe bigger point here is: what the hell is 'socially functional' anyway? Seems to mean 'like my friends'.
    Yeah, "like my friends" is precisely the point. I don't see what's confusing about what he said. Malcolm's position, whether you think it right or wrong, is that gaming gets de-geeked when we treat gaming the same way as we treat going to a movie or playing road hockey or any other activity we do; when we socially integrate gaming into the context of the rest of our lives and the people we know.

    In online communities, mini-cons, gaming-only groups, that's not what we're doing. He's saying those are circumstances set apart from our lives (pointedly not integrated) to engage in gaming only activities. Non-integrated gaming activities put an overstressed emphasis on the activity, and turn it into a geek activity. It's something set apart.

    So he says: show me a picture of you and the people you game with, which is really just show me a picture of you and your friends in a socially integrated context.

    I don't think that Jason posted this to start another petty bitchfest about someone who's not around to answer back. That doesn't seem to be the kind of thing that a nice guy like Jason tends to do.

    Frankly, I think a repeat of that thread would be pretty bullshit behavior, and utterly redundant.

    This is the group from my weekly "Chill" game. I consider us "socially functional" because we do other things together (visiting one another, concerts, dinners). We have a mailing list for the game, and use it to plan what foods each of the wonderful cooks in the group (one a restaurant manager) will bring. They call it "food porn". Each and every one is a close personal friend, and the level of trust we have for each other makes for wonderfully intense gaming sessions. Additionally, because we're all friends, we can talk about it constructively when the game isn't meeting someone's needs.
