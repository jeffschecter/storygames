\n Man, it's worth posting this just to see "Delta Green" on Story Games. So here's this thing that happened.

My character is reading The King In Yellow*. We've found a copy, in a weird alternate-reality cave, and I'm reading it because I want Mythos knowledge.

Another character, reasonably, decides I shouldn't read it. To ensure this, he shoots the book. This means he shoots me. He rolls maximum damage and I'm out of the game.

I was really pissed off about this.

So, look, here's the thing. It's not the fact he shot me. But it's the way it was handled at the table: the guy's comments were addressed entirely to the GM.

Perhaps I'm used to a different style of play, but here's how I expect the conversation to go.

Player: So, if you do that, I'm going to shoot you.
Me: Well, I'm doing it.
Player: Right, I shoot you. [Rolls damage] Fuck, 10.
Me: Bloody hell, I'm dead.
Player: Oh fuck.
Me: No, fair enough.

Or perhaps not even that. Perhaps he doesn't give me the option, but at least he talks to me.

Me: So I read the book.
Player: OK, look, I'd just shoot the book, and if that means I shoot you...
Me: Fair enough.
Player: So... [Rolls damage] Fuck, 10.
Me: Bloody hell, I'm dead.

You know what I mean? Perhaps it's because I'm used to indie games, but I expect some talk around the table if I'm about to be knocked out of the game. But, man. Some guy gets trigger-happy and I'm out, without anything I can do about it. It still rankles.

Graham

* Note to non-Cthulhu people. This is not a wise thing to do.

    \n It's Delta Green, it's supposed to be brutal. I think the character was totally justified in shooting the other one and it's a cool way to go. If you're used to the DG game, then you wouldn't be surprised if this happened, nor would you expect any OOC discussion. It's a "shit happens" kind of place.

    So, for example, if you go exploring and find a big hole in the ground, and you say "I jump down the hole", and you're playing DG at a con and the GM says "roll jump for half damage, you make it? That's 10d6/2 damage. You're dead", don't be surprised. A kinder GM might say, "as you approach you kick a small pebble over the edge and by the time you get to the edge, it's still falling. Do you still want to jump?"

    The last time I played DG at a con, one of the other PCs went mad and started lobbing grenades thinking everyone was a Mythos creature. I dropped back to shoot him but missed and he killed my PC with a lucky critical shot. And so it goes.

    I guess it's a question of genre and game expectations as mentioned previously, so I'll fully expect a bullet in the brain next time we play Toon.

    \n The fact that it was a con game can't have helped. I played a ton of DG, with the grimness knob turned pretty high, but we always went for grim choices rather than naked PvP violence. How that would have played out at our table:
    Graham: I go to read the book.
    Shooter player: "I can't let you do that." I draw my pistol and push it against your temple. "Put the book down." I have a base 75%, so at point blank...
    Me (GM): You can't miss at that range. Graham?

    But we got to that knife-edge teetering style through lots of playing together and negotiating. At a con you never know whether you're playing with fans of character-blender CoC or pulp adventures or "in the grim darkness of the mythos". Now that we're getting a different perspective from that table, it looks like a case of clashing expectations. Ron knows that usually weighs against fun.
