    Paul,

    \n

    RBH doesn't tell you who gets to design the arenas. When I play, I tell my players explicitly that I expect them to get involved with creating new arenas. Arenas are added and subtracted frequently during combat scenes.

    \n

    I go by a pretty simple rule of thumb: If your character wants to move somewhere that seems like it should be a different arena, then you should draw it on the map and move your character there.

    \n

    Furthermore, I encourage my players to take the liberty of adding to the fiction with the Move action. Say the characters are in a deep dark cave. It's a tight arena, so the character with the crossbow wants a better vantage spot. So the player narrates their character finding a ledge to climb up on. Assuming none of the other players object to the ledge being part of the scene, the player then notes the ledge on the map, moves their character there and play continues.

    Posted By: Eric ProvostFurthermore, I encourage my players to take the liberty of adding to the fiction with the Move action. Say the characters are in a deep dark cave. It's a tight arena, so the character with the crossbow wants a better vantage spot. So the player narrates their character finding a ledge to climb up on.
    \nThis happened in ours when the ninja was sneaking around the city streets - he wanted to get up high, jump roof to roof to position himself better to take out/escape the undead mooks - as long as it was reasonable the GM allows it - the ledges, footholds, balconies. Add to this the ability to alter the terrain - the creature in the great hall destroying the pillars with it's tail - area becoming more open and hazardous as ceiling starts giving way. Or more simply - the creature bats aside the market stalls leaving the town square more open so it can move more freely.

    my GM advice is to use a variety of arenas (break up the hall into smaller arenas if you want) but if possible, allow for dynamic terrain - e.g the rope bridge starting to fray, that bit in Fellowship of the Ring in the Mines when they get the broken stone bridge to sway.

    players will want to use their favoured arenas for combat - but will enjoy it more if they have to adapt their tactics or the terrain itself to get an advantage over their foes - it's more of an achievement. It may lead to use of different talents - my naga's 3rd eye talent became her ace in the hole when the enemy was hiding behind a wall by an arch, waiting to surprise her as she went through. If a longrunner had birdfeet and was skipping over the heads of the crowd to evade pursuit etc
