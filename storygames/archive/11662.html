    \n The easiest way to tell if you've printed it right: the 'introducing' and website contents (the list of playbooks, refbook, Master of Ceremonies playbook, etc) plug quarter-of-the-page should be opposite the 'title page' and character image quarter. Then you've printed it correctly.

    Yeah, double-sided printing is a pain. But, well, worst comes to worst, you can always print out the player's ref book, with a couple copies of the basic moves at the end. It's nowhere near as nice as the properly assembled playbooks, though.

    \n Assuming Vincent has done this the conventional way (I haven't printed them yet), for an octavo gathering (8 leaf / 16 page): First, make sure page one (the front cover) is printed directly opposite page two (fiction and character summary), then hold the sheet of paper sideways (long left to right); fold the left half behind the right, the top behind the bottom, and the left behind the right. Staple in the middle of the final fold and rip out the 'bolts' (the folds holding the pages together--use a blunt paper knife, letter opener, or ruler, not a sharp knife) and there you have it.
