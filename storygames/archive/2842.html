\n So, I recently knocked out a 24 hour RPG for the Modus Operandi 24 Hr Espionage RPG challenge. The criteria were to write an espionage game in an interesting time period prior to 1914. So I wrote Okhrana, about political emigres trying to dodge the Czarist secret police in belle epoque Paris (1899, to be precise). the game is rough, is scrappy, but the core, I think is there. However...

The bit that is troubling me the most is the rewards in it. At the moment, each scene defines what the protagonist gets for winning or losing. Fair enough. But it doesn't seem satisfactory at all. I'm thinking of changing it to allow the player to 'bet' how much he/she would like their protagonist to gain/lose as the result of the conflcit contained within the scene. I was thinking of a range from 1 - 3. If the protagonist succeeds, the positive attribute they were using goes up by the amount selected. However, if they fail, then the negative attribute that was opping them goes up by the amount selected.

(Protagonists have two pairs of attributes: Guile and Stupidity, Fervour and Doubt. The protagonist use the positive attributes, the antagonist uses the negative attributes. The game text contained in the PDF linked to above is actually pretty brief and gives and idea of how things work).

Any feedback and advice would be grand.

Cheers
Malcolm
