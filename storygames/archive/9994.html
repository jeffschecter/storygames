    \n@zipht: I've been helping Chris with playtesting and development so I'll take a shot at your questions.

    Failure -- PCs are generally very competent when it comes to their specialty and "whiff factor" is very low. Story-wise, failure is just a fork in the road. And, as is consistent with a lot of the games that are popular here, failure is often more entertaining than success.

    Combat -- Is tactical in an abstract way. It has more in common with 3:16 than with Battletech or Heavy Gear.

    \n Sorry I've been absent. RL

    Christian...glad your a fan of mecha games. Are you going to be a Gen Con? If so, definitely grab a copy of the print Quickstart. If not, I'll post when the download is ready. Also, it's not an alpha. The game has been playtested a bunch and what you are getting with the Quickstart is an abbreviated version of the game. It lets you see combat and the scene structure without worrying about all of the rules. The full version is coming, but we wanted to do it right in terms of editing, layout, and art and that's taking time.

    @Zipht I'll probably do a post on failure soon. Characters don't often outright fail (just like they don't in mecha anime.) Typically when failure occurs is in combat when you are trying to shoot or avoid getting shot. Other than that, really what happens more is the case of "Did I succeed enough?" But without delving into all the scene structure rules, it's hard for that to make sense.

    Clint's right about combat. It's been abstracted to remove a lot of the fiddly bits so that positioning and weapon choice are what you worry about. Mecha in mecha anime rarely run out of ammo or have their laser generators fail, so that's not really a concern. Still, from playtesting, it small unit (say 4-on-4) combat is interestingly tactical enough it can be fun to play on its own.

    Lastly, thanks Daniel. The game in its current form is actually a bit more abstract than when we played. The damage system is a bit more interesting while being a lot harder to one-shot a PC or an important NPC. On the other hand, Boomer is still in so Josh still has the potential to wipe out half his allies again if he wants!

    \n@MacLeod I think it's a tribute to the mecha genre that we all can. I bet your game and mine are nothing alike. And if they are...get out of my mind!!!

    I really think you will find something in Mecha that speaks to you. I came up through Battletech (though Robotech the cartoon and game were big influencers of young Chris Perrin.) One of the settings, Steel Gunner: Destiny Tomorrow, is very much in the Heavy Gear tradition (though without duelists...rats...should of have them...) I would say that the game is tactical, though with a layer of abstraction much, much higher than most mecha games because that's what we were going for.

    As for the full version, I can tell you that the draft I submitted for editing is about 130 pages. That's without one full setting who the editor is writing. So without layout and (much) art, around 150 pages of just text. The final book could easily be in the 200 page range.
