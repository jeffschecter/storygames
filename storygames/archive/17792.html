\n We played TQY on new year's eve and it was awesome, even if we managed to get only through spring in about 2 hours.

I loved the fact that I played with two ladies that never played RPGs before and there was almost no glitch in playing and we had great fun.
If I have to try to find a weak point is how hard it is to enforce the "we speak only when we hold discussions" rule, as the game naturally provokes brainstorming. This probably made our game longer, but it didn't break it.

There was one situation that I didn't understand how to handle though.

Suppose you start a project whose outcome is not clear in advance (eg We wage war on the creatures from the toxic wastes // who wins?) or that requires the introduction of new fiction (eg We build a machine to force open the big vault on the side of the mountain // what is inside?). When is the new fiction introduced and by whom? By the player that removes the project die? By the player that initially created the project? By the first player that uses a Declare Something New move or that picks an appropriate card?

Thanks again for a great game
--Ivan

    \n It is decided when the project is finished (in the "update project dice" step), by the player who started the project. There's a few cards that say a project finishes early. If they don't say anything else, I'd say the initiator still describes, but when the cards prompt then it's the one who drew the card. There's also one that tells the card-drawer to "radically change the nature of [a project]", and then that player counts as the initiator so that when it's eventually finished, they tell us how it worked out.

    Saladdin is correct. It's whoever initiated the project, unless the project ends early as the result of a card, in which case it is the player who drew that card/chose that option. (The same goes for projects that fail as the result of a card; the player who drew the card should describe the failure.)
