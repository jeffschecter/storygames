\n Hi everyone!

I'm in an ongoing Apocalypse World game over on Infrno and I decided to ditch my existing Spectacle character (who I was having trouble connecting with) and make a new one.

We're low on Weird characters (everyone except the Savvyhead has it at -2 and the Savvyhead uses weird to be Sherlock Holmes with machines, rather then to have spooky maelstrom visions).

I ended up in that unenviable situation of having a cool idea for a character, in this case a hood-wrapped creature surrounded by moths who appeared and disappeared, affecting the community but not being truly part of it.

I tried writing the character up as a variety of different playbooks from the Witch to the Hocus, but none of them were quite clicking.

So I wrote my own.

It ended up being something you could use to play Rhea of Coo's, Granny Weatherwax, or Batman.

I haven't playtested this yet, I'll start doing so on Monday, so I'm not sure how some of the moves will play out.

Any feedback would be most appreciated.



THE HAG

The Hag lives on the outskirts. Not in, but not out, of the community. They stand at the border, watching the edges of things. They\u2019re the ones you go to if you need something, really need it, and damn the consequences.

NAME
Esmeralda, Ginny, Gintha, Mab, Black Alice, Bailey, Nix, Julien, Angelique, Walter
A mystic name, a spooky name, an ominous title.

STATS
Cool 1, Hard 1, Hot -2, Sharp 1, Weird 2
Cool -1, Hard -1, Hot 2, Sharp 0, Weird 2
Cool 0, Hard 1, Hot 1, Sharp -1, Weird 2
Cool 1, Hard -1, Hot 0, Sharp 1, Weird 2

MOVES
You get the Cauldron, and two other Hag Moves.

HX
Someone acts as your main contact with the community. Tell that person +1.
Someone came to you for a ritual in a time of need. Ask them what you did for them and tell them +2.
For everyone else tell them +0. You are not an easy person to know.

On the others' turns:
for each other character, decide if you've meddled with their lives in the past
if they are a victim of your meddling, take whatever number they tell you and give it +1 and write it next to their name
if you've never meddled with their lives, take whatever number they tell you and write it next to their name

LOOK
Man, woman, ambiguous, transgressing.
Dandy wear, tattered wear, simple wear, mismatched wear, homemade wear.
Burned face, wrinkled face, handsome face, unseen face.
Shining eyes, milky eyes, missing eyes, piercing eyes.
Boney body, misshapen body, hard body, tiny body, crippled body.

GEAR
You have clothing suitable to your look and a symbol of power:
- a leather bag of secrets (+hidden)
- piece of something dead (+horrific)
- a decorated stick (+imposing)
- a relic of the old times (+valuable)
You also have oddments worth three jingle.

SEX MOVE
When you have sex with another person, you count as being in a Place of Power.

ADVANCEMENTS
get +1 cool (max cool+2)
get +1 sharp (max sharp+2)
get +1 hard (max hard+2)
get a new hag move
get a new hag move
get 2 gigs (detail) and moonlighting
get a move from another playbook
get a move from another playbook

get +1 to any stat (max stat+3)
retire your character (to safety)
create a second character to play
change your character to a new type
choose 3 basic moves and advance them.
advance the other 4 basic moves.

HAG MOVES

DIM
As long as you have taken no harm in this scene, if it's possible that nobody is looking at you, you can declare that you are no longer there.

BEETLEJUICE
When another character is alone and they say your name, roll + hx for pc's, or + weird for npcs. On a hit, you appear to them. On a 10+ pick 2, on 7-9 pick 1.
\t- you can speak to them
\t- you can touch them
\t- you can give them something you own
During the scene, if another character arrives, you immediately vanish.

BLESSINGS BE...
At the beginning of a session or whenever there is significant downtime, roll + weird. On a 10+ hold 3, on a 7-9 hold 1. You can spend 1 hold to activate a want or a surplus for a group or gang. How this manifests may be as subtle or obvious as you like.

HEADOLOGY
When you try to Manipulate in a roundabout manner, or through reverse psychology, you roll + sharp instead of roll + hot.

PRICKING IN YOUR THUMBS
When you are in a Place of Power, you gain Augury using the Place as an antenna.

CAULDRON
When you are in a Place of Power and you dedicate yourself to designing a magical ritual, decide what the ritual will do and tell the MC. The MC will tell you "Sure, no problem, but..." and then pick some of the following.

\t- it will take hours/days/weeks/months
\t- you are going to need ______ to help you with it
\t- it is going to mean exposing yourself to ______
\t- you must sacrifice ______ to do it
\t- you will only be able to manage a weaker version unless you ______
\t- first you must ______
\t- the outcome will contain unpredictable side effects
\t- you need something rare, weird, or dangerous
\t- you need a lot of people to help you do it
\t- you can only do it this once
\t- you can only complete the ritual at a particular time
\t- whatever the effect is, it must be balanced by an opposite effect elsewhere

If you are not in a Place of Power, the only viable Ritual Magic is "Make this place a Place of Power".

GINGERBREAD
Before you roll the dice, when you draw upon a Place of Power you may add 1 to any roll you make. If you do, but you still miss, you have angered the spirits of the Place.

    \n Very interesting playbook!

    My first impression is that it doesn't quite fit Apocalypse World, but it's also very compelling both mechanically and thematically.

    Some thoughts on the moves:

    Dim

    I like this one! It's clever mechanically. I could also see an inverse version, where you can ONLY use it in scenes where you get hurt. (Maybe it would be called "Lick Your Wounds" or something.)

    Beetlejuice

    This one is wonky in respect to NPCs, but it's worth playtesting. I could see using it if an NPC *thinks* they're alone: a PC is watching them, and they call the Hag. If and when the PC interrupts or is spotted, the Hag disappears. In other words, it still only happens "on-screen", like other moves.

    Another way to do it is just to say that only PCs can call the Hag, which is what might happen in play anyway.

    Another idea is that it could a start-of-session move. Something like: roll+weird. On a 10+, an NPC will call on you this session at a time when you're ready and safe. Tell the MC who you want it to be. On a 7-9, same deal but the MC chooses who it will be. On a miss, the MC holds 1 over you, and can spend it to have an NPC call on you at the most inopportune time. (Effectively a hard move the MC can use without forewarning later in that session, like when you're creeping through the enemy camp.)

    Blessings Be

    This is actually the one that worries me! It might be simply too powerful. Activating three of a place's wants is... huge, I would think. Like, I can decide that your gang starts deserting you (+desertion), killing and raping and looting (+savagery), and at the same your rivals attack (+reprisals)?

    More troublesome is that there's no real fictional trigger, though. I'd want to see how you can accomplish that! What does the Hag character actually *do* for this to happen? Is it like Frenzy: "When you speak to a crowd gathered before you, roll... on a hit, choose one of their tags to activate?" Or something else, like spending barter in the community, or assassinating a particular member of the group?

    I could also see doing it something like this:

    "When you step into a place where you don't belong and meddle with the life of a community, roll+weird. On a 10+, choose one of their tags/wants/surplus and activate it. On a 7-9, the MC chooses one for you."

    (On a miss... well, you can imagine what happens, I think.)

    I love the idea of this move mechanically, and I think it's the one that makes this playbook truly interesting for me.


    Cauldron

    Also, is it your intention that the Hag starts kind of "unsettled" and then has to go to work (and/or have sex with someone) to create a Place of Power? That can work.

    If not, then you should do some kind of pick-and-choose create your own Place of Power for the playbook, like how the Hoarder creates his Hoard and the Savvyhead creates his workshop.

    One of the problems with "magic" is that we really have no idea what it's capable of and what it's not capable of.

    Maybe the options for creating a Place of Power could include what kinds of spirits are drawn to your Place of Power? That would give us some guidance for what you can do with it, and lead to some interesting situations where the Hag might need to create other Places in order to accomplish different things. (Much like how a Savvyhead with a lab and an infirmary has very different capabilities than one with hi-tech electronics in a van.)

    For instance, I'd imagine having to establish a Place of Power on the site of a massacre in order to commune with the dead, or maybe a Place of Power on top of the highest hill in the region in order to control the weather.

    (This also makes me think that a potentially interesting version of the Hag would be one that strikes bargains with the psychic maelstrom itself... but I don't know if that fits your vision.)



    Also, I'm just throwing a bunch of stuff out there, brainstorming. Feel free to ignore me! I'm just blabbing.
