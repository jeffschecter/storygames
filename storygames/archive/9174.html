\n Hey peoples, recommend me some roleplaying games. My good friend writes:

"Henry and I play a lot of games. Besides Munchkin, we've been playing Axis & Allies (tabletop), Risk Godstorm, Zombies!, Torches & Pitchforks, Heroscape, and a little Warhammer (though it takes me too long to make the pieces). In other words. mostly card and tabletop games.

He's INSANE for yugioh. I play it with him, but it's not my favorite. Mostly because he kicks my ass as his young brain still has the ability to keep the effects of 500 different cards in his head.

I'd love to get into some RPGs with him. He plays them with his friends without even knowing what it is. I hear him making up stories and characters with them all the time and giving them options and tasks and results - A born DM. I have some of my old D&D stuff, but maybe you could suggest some relatively easy RPGs for us to try. Like all good boys, he enjoys monsters, aliens and maximum carnage."

This kid is clearly past "light children's games of fairies and gentleness" in terms of interest and capability. I started with 3:16 - what would you suggest for this ten-year-old dude?

    \n When I was 10, what I wanted more than anything from an RPG was a big list of equipment/magic items/cool stuff to drool over. So I'm thinking that Cyberpunk or Shadowrun might fit the bill, but the systems are pretty abysmal, so I hate to recommend them.

    Car Wars was also a favorite for the same reason. There was nothing cooler than getting a new Uncle Albert's catalog. So many shiny bits to play with...

    Starblazers might be a good recommendation, but the hardcover isn't out until later this year. What 10 year old could resist a game subtitled "The Rock and Roll Space Opera Adventure Game". Heck I can't resist it at my age...
