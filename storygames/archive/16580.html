    \n Terry and I are GMing a bunch of games...

    Marvel Heroic
    Aunt May are you Spider-Man?
    What if Aunt May, Harry Osborn, Mary Jane, and Gwen Stacey suddenly had spider powers? Based on Ultimate Spider-Man where Peter Parker is 16 and in high school.

    Apocalypse World
    (S) trength, (O) rder & (B) ikes
    It's the apocalypse. It won't stop snowing. There's not enough food, water, or hope. But there damn sure is enough gasoline and bullets. Play a not to be messed with biker gang in the middle of an all-out biker civil war!

    Mouse Guard
    Beaver Dam Gone Wild
    Play medieval mice with swords! The town of Lonepine is in danger from: Beavers! Bandits! Politics! Floods! Mouse Traitors! And?

    Dogs in the Vineyard
    East York: Big City, Bigger Sins!
    Play teenage virgin gunslinger paladins with ultimate power and zero life experience judging people for doing bad things for good reasons (at least they think they\u2019re good reasons).

    Kagematsu
    Love, honor, and shame
    Somewhere in medieval Japan\u2026all of the able-bodied men are gone. The shadows of darkness stretch over your tiny isolated village. Soon, the approaching doom will consume you all. You\u2013the women of this village\u2013have all but given up hope of finding a defender. And then he finally arrives. Except he\u2019s ronin, a wayward samurai fleeing a troubled past. Use your body, mind and soul and do anything and everything to sway him away from his meandering course.

    Cthulhu Dark
    Amuse yourself
    First Class on a magnificent luxury liner is excitement enough, and now Dr. Shaw invites you to an exclusive viewing of the ancient curiosity he\u2019s transporting (or smuggling?) to New York. It will be nice to reconnect since his long absence, after the tragic Egyptian incident and his growing obsession with \u201cthe spiritualist parapsychological architecture\u201d movement. But even if tonight\u2019s a bore, there\u2019s always tomorrow on the maiden voyage of the Titanic!

    \n Micro-AP so far:

    Kat Miller's Hunger Games hack of Serial Homicide Unit was a lot of fun; neither of my pledges made it to the finals, but it was almost as much fun to be a Sponsor as to actively fight it out.

    Kagematsu was great! 3 talented players and a matinee idol ronin really amped up the romance in o ur poor kelp farming village! I didn't get chosen by Kagematsu, but my pony Lemograss survived and that's all that really matters.

    Tonight: Ninja Panda Taco!

    Aside: there's a fantastic appetite for indie games here from both the veterans and new players just discovering them, and we are doing really strong sales, so I've got at least one data point to back up my rambling about the indie game marketplace.

    How did "I Wanna Be A Stormtrooper" go? I didn't write it, by the way - it's by Anders Nygaard. It looks like a lot of fun, but I haven't played it yet myself!
    \nI thought I had the author stuff correct on the form, but it somehow got out of wack on the program. My apologies to Anders for the incorrect credit.

    Stormtrooper only ran once, no players for the second slot . The game ran well with 4 of us. The kids found a dead stormtrooper in the dunes, found a hidden rebel base, and eventually got most of the rebels killed. It was good fun.

    The starting equipment got traction with us for story ideas, but none of the "more stuff" cards ever came into play. The characters and relations all held together well. In only 4 hours we couldn't get to more than one or two of the destinies. (We randomly drew the destiny cards, but chose all the other cards deliberately). I felt like it would be hard to get a story that ended with all the disparate destinies happening at the table, but that epilogues could tie things up nicely.

    We added extra elements to the game including "Droids" and maybe "indigenous life"? My memory is a bit fuzzy. But there were story elements we though important that weren't on the cards.

    Overall very fun, and a good use of the rules for a character centered game.

    \n No worries - most of the stuff at N\xf8rwegian Style is by Matthijs, and the game design is his, so it's easy to get things mixed up Thanks for picking up the playset and taking the time to talk about your game!

    My experience with Archipelago in limited time slots matches yours, I think - the one-off format and short time span messes up how destinies work out. Playing with a world made from scratch at a game salon this month, we had to resolve the destinies in a rush at the end. (Though starting out with seven players may have made a difference too...) Playing a fantasy game a few months ago, the destinies turned out to be a bit too epic for a one-off, making every scene one of world-shattering events. Fun in the beginning, but gets to be a bit ridiculous after a while.

    "More stuff" was a bit of an experiment - could be they're just an unnecessary distraction in a session that needs very sharp focus to get to the endgame on time.
