After those threads about the past Game Chef entries, I started to wonder about some other projects, like the 24 Hour games submissions. I figure everyone here can think of at least one half-baked RPG idea they wanted to see finished, so maybe a thread where we could ask about games we're waiting for would be a good idea.

First thing that came to my mind was Left Coast, by Steve Hickey. That was the game where you were a Californian sci-fi author struggling to make it big without losing your marbles, and where the plots of the various imaginary sci-fi novels become a part of play, too. Anyone know what became of Steve, and what the status of Left Coast is? Was there a name change when I wasn't looking? Has it been abandoned?

    The second half of The Court of 9 Chambers? Man, I've been thinking of it lately as one unified game, so I wonder what I was calling "the second half". I think that may be from one of the middle revisions, when I wrote up playtest rules for the non-dream stuff.

    Things have been changing in Co9C, ever since the early playtest showed me things needed to be changed. There's also been some minor terminology/focus changes. For example, instead of "entering the dreamscape", I refer to it as "entering reverie". Shorter, and fits the mood of historical surrealism better.

    I'm waiting to get my copy of Exquisite Corpse: Surrealism and the Black Dahlia Murder back, and I have to re-read The King in Yellow, and then I should be able to start work on Co9C again.

  • Sydney Freedberg

    Guy Shalev mentioned my game was mentioned....

    I've been tinkering with apocalypse girl on and off. Since the version discussed on the Forge, I've playtested one radical revision that broke down equally radically within a few turns, but disgorged some interesting and salvageable pieces of ideas for later use.

    The as-yet-unplayable third iteration currently gives each player a deck of playing cards, with a different deck for the Gun, and (as of a brainflash about 72 hours ago) has a conflict mechanic combining Blackjack and Chicken: Okay, my trait can handle up to 23 points of stress, your trait can handle 25, and we're currently tied at 15 -- do I dare draw another card? Oh, and the design on the back of each card actually makes a major mechanical difference, which is a small mechanics-tinkerer triumph.

    I'll actually try to get some rules together uh, sort of soon. I'm currently trying to make an Burning Empires game work, which is a nearly overwhelming task (656 pages of rules and at least six different core mechanics) but is actually giving me ideas for my little-bitty game.
