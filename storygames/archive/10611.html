    \n Type 1 fonts are fine for PDFs or printing. OpenType are great, too.

    The other type is TrueType fonts. They are hit and miss. Very, very often, free fonts on the web are TT (True Type). I try to avoid them as much as possible. Some work for PDFs and printing, some cause problems.

    \n Your PPD might not be the generic one; I've also used "full featured" ones like HP's, to do tricks with the PDF output.

    You should be able to, then, print to FILE (PS), drag that file to Distiller, and get a PDF. Obviously, you have to make your Job Settings for Distiller suit your output/medium. You can also automate the Distiller, by using Watched Folders.
