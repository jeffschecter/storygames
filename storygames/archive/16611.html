hey dudes, the kickstarter for Serpent's Tooth is live. It came out of Game Chef 2011 ...Winding up for the pitch!

A dying monarch...a star quarterback...the CEO of a dot-com empire...

Serpent's Tooth is a tabletop game where you and your friends create a story about a King in the twilight of his rule. One of you plays the King, who wields vast power over the Kingdom. The rest of you play members of the King's court. Each with their own hidden nature, and each with the ability to seize the King's power for their own. Through play you discover the fate of the King, the Kingdom, and what power does to the powerful.

    \n Sure thing, here is the ultra condensed explanation, I'll publish some AP and examples in the future...

    the mechanics of Serpent's Tooth all deal with the apportioning of Creative Authority. At the start of the game one player has the King, and also the NPCs, Places, and Threats to the Kingdom which basically makes that person the GM.

    Everyone else is in charge of their PC. Each PC has a double sided nature card that represents how everyone else sees them. Anyone can invoke your nature through play, either in character or out of character. When they do that you have to act on your nature.

    Creative Authority in Serpent's Tooth means that you're the author of that part of the story and if someone else says something about, lets say, your character, then you have the power to step in, or attach a cost to what they want.

    When you show your character plotting to Seize, or somehow take control of one of the Regalia, you get a Serpent's Tooth. In a future scene you can pay your tooth to execute your plot and Seize the Regalia.
    \n
    Posted By: NoofyI assume its GMless?
    \nKinda sorta. The King is totally the GM, but everyone else gets to name all the Characters, Places, and Threats during the setup. Also by the end of the game the GM roles usually dispersed throughout the group. (I've never seen anyone get all three regalia, though it could totally happen.)\n
    Posted By: NoofyAre the taro cards inspirational? Randomisers? Both?
    \nThe Nature cards are a leash everyone gets to tug on. They are double sided so once during the game you can choose to reveal your hidden nature. The natures are all diametrically opposed Honest / Deceitful, Hateful / Nurturing, that sort of thing.

    I'll post some AP, maybe edit together some of my Playtest recordings with a powerpoint if I'm feeling ambitious!
