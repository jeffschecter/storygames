    \n I'll try and extend those parts of the text that deal with the above, but it'll have to wait until at least the weekend before I can send you an updated document. In the meantime, a couple of quick answers:

    1. Ask your MC: anything no greater than edge+1 is something the PCs already have in their kitchen or workroom, e.g. an average sharp knife. If you want something that packs more punch, then ask around, but be aware of the laws and practicalities of the country your game is set in: almost everything with +lead is also going to be +hot.

    2. The only basic move that can directly put a PC out is open fire, which means you have to be packing something with +lead, which means heat+1 for as long as you're carrying it. As things stand right now, the ony danger of death by violence to your PC comes from the other PCs: there's no risk of dying abruptly from the trouble you get into, but you can build up heat, which will eventually get you burned. As to the Feelgood, he can use his move on you if the other characters present make that their priority, e.g. if you're lying bleeding on the floor and they finish their job, then you're out, but if they immediately get you to the Feelgood, and he does his stuff, then you're only down. If you want NPCs to represent more physical danger to the PCs, then I'd say you can take someone down as a hard move when they miss a get rough or open fire roll.

    \n You start with 0 dough; you can earn dough through pulling off extra jobs, besides whatever you do that makes you a living. If you're livelihood gets threatened, you mark experience for restoring it, but you can also spend dough to avoid threatening your livelihood: as long as you're short of cash, you can't make any significant purchases, which can be a problem if someone wants their debt repaying right now. Also, when you pay cash, you get to mark experience and you can make any move as if you had rolled 10+, even a unique move from another playbook, but without any negative consequences. So there are two roads for advancement:
    - Allow (or cause, via metagaming) your livelihood to be threatened, then restore it.
    - Earn dough, then spend it when you pay cash.

    As for lie low, do it like this:
    - First, on a hit, take -1 heat.
    - If it's a 7-9, mark someone for payback.
    - If it's a 10+, you have the option to take another -1 heat, but if you take this option, you also haave to mark someone for payback.

    I should probably change it to read "On a 10+, you may take another -1 heat, if you wish, but must mark someone for payback if you do. On a 7-9, just mark someone for payback."
