    \n Shipping is still a work in progress. It's worth pointing out that 2013 has been a vicious, evil year for shipping costs and all standards and habits dating from before this year are pretty much catastrophic for the supplier. I designed the Sorcerer Kickstart by those earlier standards. My shipping costs are turning out to be at least twice as high as expected in that design - due to increases in costs which were implemented during the Kickstart.

    I think I'll include a link to a separate page which lays out my shipping plan for the reader, if they're interested.

    At present (deep breath), the backer just has to get hit with whatever the calculations are from point A to point B, to be determined later and paid after the pledge period, and (deep breath again) if they don't like it and don't pony up, they can do without their reward and I keep the $25. As far as shipping methods go, it'll be handled one way and one way only. The standard survey includes inputting addresses, and unlike the Sorcerer Kickstart, rewards will be sent immediately so I won't have to deal with as many address changes.

    Looking at that, you're right: it does seem brutal and wrong to keep the amount mysterious. The suggestion about estimates is a really good idea, say, to various urban centers around the U.S. and around the world, calculated from the point of shipping at such-and-such a date.

    Unfortunately, the only other real option is to bag the whole concept of shipping for the book entirely. The reward would be PDF including print format (as I did for Sorcerer), meaning that if you really wanted that book, you go to the local printer and walk out, or close enough, with one. Seriously, for non-U.S. sales, that would be significantly cheaper for the backer than paying for shipping. Crazy but true. However, I know truly well that "pledge and get a book in my mailbox" is lodged firmly in the heart of the RPG Kickstart backer community. I can't fight it, and I can't afford to be the guy who introduces the idea and pays the price, no matter how the community might change later. So I'm sticking with the physical book and seeking solutions - what you've said so far is already really helping.

    Another thing I'm thinking of, as a sweetener, is to provide the current PDF as a basic reward for any and all backers. It's not the upgraded spiffy version (you pledge for that, PDF + book), but the rules are perfectly functional. That seems like a decent thing to do, it might prompt play (and Gallery submissions) during the Kickstart, and it might soften the "take it or leave it" quality of pledging for the book.

    Thanks for the thoughts! The more the better, what you like, what seems puzzling, whatever.

    \n That's a good summary of the viewpoints I'm struggling to accommodate - which is to say, not to agree with and not to serve as such, but to take into account and with any luck not piss off too badly.

    First is to say that I'm OK with anyone not wanting to pledge. It'd be silly - to say the least - to run a Kickstart with the idea that a person, i.e. anyone, [i]should[/i] pledge by default.

    Here are a couple of fixed issues from my point of view, so you can see where I'm coming from. I don't expect you to agree and I'm not trying to beat you down; it's merely a window into my head.

    1. Shipping as it now stands is no longer a risk. It's bankruptcy. I just spent $11,000 out of the $26,000 I raised for the Sorcerer Kickstart on postage, shipping, and handling. As originally conceived back in December, $10,000 (the stated stretch goal) would have met that Kickstart's needs safely; given the changes in shipping rates by the end of January, it would obviously have been inadequate.* Getting 260% of that total didn't change that; what matters is the average pledge amount per person so I'm not just digging a hole and filling it in again. Jonathan is absolutely right, above - the whole commercial culture of RPGs is going to have to get revised regarding shipping, because the companies who stick with the older model will simply go under.

    2. Crowdfunding is unnecessary for the physical goal of publishing a book. I can do that with funds and contacts at hand, if not "in my sleep," then damned close. In fact, anyone who claims to be running a Kickstart "to publish" a book is full of shit, because POD and other mechanisms are all here in place. What they are really doing is using the Kickstart culture as advertising and pre-order. I'm not [i]objecting[/i] to that strategy, but (i) let's call it what it is and acknowledge that it's not financially necessary for anyone simply to get their books produced, and (ii) that many RPG publishers have found it to be a very, very bad strategy when they look at their numbers in the end - and the more so now, with the shipping cost increases. So from my POV as publisher, when I try for some crowdfunding, it'll be for something special, which might be perceived by some at least as worth helping to bring to fruition.

    3. #2 above has to be done realistically, which means admitting that people use Kickstart at least as much to shop as to donate in "angel" fashion. I am OK with this; hell, I do it myself! But it has led me to decide that separating [i]goal[/i], which is mine, from [i]reward[/i], which is yours is a very, very good thing for an RPG publisher.** I really can't get this app produced without the kind of money crowdfunding can provide, so yes, I "want your money." What I'm offering as rewards are absolutely nothing but a shelf of cool things to [i]buy[/i] with your pledges, and yes, therefore profit margin per purchase is a core concept for me, 'cause that's what funds my goal - I say so up-front and honest. And please notice that one of them is what [i]other[/i] publishers would be offering as the goal anyway, i.e., the book (another reason I'm still holding onto the idea of keeping it physical) - so it's not like I'm bilking anyone out of that expectation. If you don't care about my goal, that's OK with me as long as you like your reward.

    4. I strive at all costs for genuine quality in both the goal and the rewards. Blunt time: does anyone really think the RPG world needs a 7th edition of Call of Cthulhu? I certainly don't; my 3rd edition is worthy and wholly complete. Have you seen that recent book? I have; it's cheaply made, feels like nagahyde, the color of camel dung, thin and wispy paper, and just crappy overall. Did you know that the creators made each and every customer pay for 100% shipping above their pledge? If they can do that to peddle that unnecessary piece of shit, then I don't feel too bad for asking full shipping payment for (i) a good game (ii) in a genuinely attractive book (iii) in a reasonable/adequate size and hence cheaper to ship.

    All that said, the replies to this thread have led me to a better way to explain the shipping which isn't so brutal, and also to let a backer know approximately how much they can expect to pay later if they choose one or more of the physical items. I haven't implemented those changes to the draft yet, but when I do, I think that people can least say, "OK, that's fair" and decide whether it's for them.

    Thanks for your post. This is what I need from SG, the exposure of all the available views and the chance to rearrange either my thinking or the way I want to present it.

    Best, Ron

Whereas with RPGs, the people pledging are most of the same people who'd be buying it later anyway; hence my point about digging a hole and filling it in again.
