\n My copies of It's Complicated arrived today, and they look great! But there's something that needs a bit of further explanation, particularly for Malcolm Craig and some international customers who've bought the Online Play Edition PDF.

Appendix B on page 32 states the following:



"Oh snap!" and "Did someone get told?" are both American slang. "Oh snap!" is what a third party might say when one person says something insulting and particularly cutting to another, second person. This is also called "Telling" someone, as in the phrase "Someone just got told!"

As an example:

Bret, Nathan, and Shreyas are drinking beer. The following conversation occurs.

Bret: Man, this beer is really gross.
Nathan: So's your face!
Shreyas: Oh snap!


However, according to the flowchart, one can "oh snap!" a comment which was made to them, and not to a third party:

Shreyas: I'm so tired, I shouldn't have stayed up all night last night.
Nathan: Yeah, I was up all night too. Having sex. With your Mom!
Shreyas: Oh snap!
Bret: I think someone just got told!


I hope this clears up any confusion. Please let me know if there are any further questions.
