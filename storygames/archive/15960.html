\n Looks like I caught the Dungeon World bug everyone has got. I've been fussing around with a little Spelljammer hack, since that was the AD&D supplement of my dreams that I never got to play.

Here's a little snippet of it that I thought was kinda fun. It's based on another thing I made a while ago:\n
When you enter the atmosphere of a previously unknown planet, ask the DM if they have any plans for this planet. If not, draw up to three playing cards, and reference the following table:

2: Humans are the most numerous and dominant of the sentient races
3: Technology has advanced to renaissance levels, but no further
4: Magic is uncommon and mysterious, a tool of the powerful
5: Elves, Dwarfs, Humans and Halflings ally against Orcs, Goblins, Gnolls, Hobgoblins, Trolls and their ilk
6: Each planet is an autonomous unit, often containing several warring kingdoms
7: Planets are broadly earth-like in terms of gravity, atmosphere, and day/night cycle
8: Gold and silver are rare and used as currency.
9: \u201cGood\u201d Kingdoms maintain civilisation, while \u201cEvil\u201d marauders threaten it from the outside.
10: The dominant human culture is broadly \u201cEuropean\u201d.
J: The dominant human culture is broadly patriarchal
Q: Space travellers are rare and mysterious
K: Planets are broadly earth-like in terms of vegetation, fauna, climate and terrain
A: Planets are broadly earth-like in terms of availability of ores, arable land and fresh water

Refer to the suit of the card:
Hearts: Embodies. The planet displays the trait to full, even extreme effect.
Diamonds: Twists. The planet displays the trait but in a strange or unexpected way.
Spades: Changed by: The planet may or may not express this trait itself, but it is affected by the fact that many other planets do.
Clubs: Breaks. The planed does not display the trait, or even expresses the opposite.

As a group, discuss the interpretation of the cards, and what that might mean for the planet. The DM has final say, and does not have to reveal anything of the truth about the planet. The planet may or may not express any of the other traits, at the DM\u2019s whim.
\nI also made some rules for ships fighting each other, which I think are pretty neat. I tried to avoid the "sucky boardgame" feel of most other ship combat rules, and keep it rooted in the fiction of play.

So! That's what I made! Hopefully it's interesting to some people.

    \n Spelljammer is pretty bananas. To be honest, I think it was an early example of material that was published more for the game reading market than the game playing market. A lot of stuff, especially the ship combat and module design seemed like it wasn't really built on a solid foundation of play. There's not really a lot of stuff happening at the character scale.

    My model for Spelljammer would be the Planetary Romance - you show up on a new planet, crash landed or out of fuel, and have an adventure until it's time to leave again. You solve the strange locals' problems with your alien technology and strange ways, and then you move on, maybe taking a little something with you, leaving a little something behind.
