\n So ... I've got this idea about a game set in Soviet prisons in the fifties*. The thing I'm really excited about is the rich symbology of tattoo art associated with the time and place. Basically somebody could look at your skin and know everything about you - who you were, where you were from, what you had done, what you could do.

Now don't get me started on the potential of a live-action version, where you carry around a sharpie. I think the placement and size of tattoos will actually be the way you resolve conflicts - "here's my hand, there's my ink, what you got?" My question to you, Story Games, is how to take this effortless, graceful idea and translate it to tabletop play.

I'm assuming your "character sheet" is a pair of blank hands. You add ink to make your character, and you add ink during the game when shit happens. It all has consequences, obviously, you've only got so much space, that's the fun. But I'm not sure how to make comparisons or challenges or whatever as fun as writing all over yourself with a marker. And honestly, it should be, because the symbology and ink-swapping is the game's killer app. Any thoughts?

*Or maybe Soviet prisons in the fifties in space, not sure yet.

    \n Jason,
    Sticking just to the hands, the most visible part of the human body (apart from the face), is strong. Nate nailed it in that you have placement, image, and image size. JESUS CHRIST spelled out along your knuckles is visible and relatively large, whereas a small spider on the pad of your pinky is neither, so clearly your faith is higher than your spying (or whatever).

    To make things relatively simple, I'd divide up the hand into a number of size sections. Each joint of a finger would be one section, and this would be the general size (so the back of the hand would be approx. 10 boxes). Each box would be one die or card or whatever. A tat that covered multiple boxes would increase in potency and importance over a 1- or 2- box tat. A couple thing jump out at me:
    1. This would seem to point to a rule that consecutive spots filled should be more potent than a bunch of small, thematically connected tats (i.e. a huge death's head as opposed to a bunch of tiny skulls).
    2. It would be better to organically generate meaning from the symbolism in the tatoos the players draw, as opposed to having it handed down from the start. Guidance would be important, of course, but it would be more powerful if the tatoos were defined in play.

    \n Alright, being an artist and a guy with big fucking tattoos, I'm totally all over this idea. Jason, you kick ass, my friend.

    As for the game, I'm thinking of both the check-in sheet that prisons use and the pricing index at tattoo shops. Both have drawings and outlines of the person's body, divided into sections. In the prison check-in sheet, tattoos and other distinguishing marks are noted and drawn in. For the tattoo pricing guide, the body is divided up into subsections, like the hands, the fingers, the forearms, bicep, shoulder, etc., and each are given a price variation depending on the size of that tattoo. I think these two ideas, mashed together, could be an easy way to handle things.

    You get a character sheet, that being a sheet with some personal information and an up-close drawing of a person's hands. I'm actually already picturing this in my head damn it, so I might kitbash one together tonight when I get home from work. Okay, so, you get this character sheet with the hands on them, and the hands are broken down into sections. Each digit, each knuckle, the back of the hand, the thumb webbing, the wrist, whatever. Each section carries with it so much power, as in in-game mechanical power. Maybe each section has a dice type assigned to it, like DitV, or just a number of dice or cards you can draw. (I'm a big fan of using playing cards in crime games. Just a personal choice, though.) You can draw tattoos over multiple sections to increase your power in that one ability/trait/skill thing. So somebody could have a tattoo tracing down the middle finger that says "KILLER", or over the knuckles that says "KILL", or someone that has "MURDERER" going across the knuckles of both hands, and each of those tattoos would each have different power levels or capabilities.

    The cool thing about this is that you have these tattoos permanently etched on your hands, so whatever is on there is permanent. If you want to add a new tattoo to a place that's already got one you'll have to go over the original tattoo and incorporate it somehow or just do an entire cover-up. Or combine imagery, like my Criminal Element tattoo, which has stuff in it about my game as well as some very personal stuff about the death of a loved one. The tattoo could then cover multiple things.

    What I really like about this idea is that you have to get other players at the table to tattoo you, and that could be a serious thing. If the player your going to doesn't think you deserve the tattoo, then he might not do it for you, or just fuck you over and do one that is wrong and eats up a space on your hands and gives you nothing.

    Then again, i could just be spit-balling and missing the point entirely.

      Posted By: RemiI'd divide up the hand into a number of size sections.
      \nI, too, am considering System more than Technique, though (of course) they have to mesh at some point.

      So, using a layout like Michael shows (though I'd have far more divisions, more like Remi suggests) you could get "Trait Dice" a la DitV by drawing a symbol of the Trait, and the die type would be determined by the number of sections/boxes that it takes up. Thus, repeating skulls (say, Murderer: 3d4) versus one big skull (say, Murderer: 1d12) would make both a mechanical (bell curve versus flat probability) and a thematic difference (a flat prob is more likely to roll very high or very low: volatility).

      Then, do a bit of math: figure out how many total Traits would be "just right" for an experienced character, and then divide up the hands into sections such that one would have to efficiently "pack full" one's hands to "max out." That would make for an interesting little sub-game in itself, as players progress.

      For more mechanical complexity, implement color (based on actual rarity in those prisons) and treat them as pluses to every die in the Trait: (I guess at rarity):
      Blue = +1
      Red = +2
      Green = +3

      Once you have the size and color elements worked out, a simple points-buy character generation would be fine, for figuring how many sections/boxes you can begin with ink on them. Likewise, a simple reward mechanism (mechanically pinged, or via a Fan Mail thing) would allow players to get more points (sections/boxes), with or without a "Fallout-like" requirement that they be inked in a manner which reflects how the points were gained.

      At that point, a Raise-and-See, DitV mechanic could work great; or count successes (6+, or 4+ if you admit d4s) versus failures (1s) like in ASCII @HACK. These elements give you a conflict resolution mechanics, using Stakes-setting (or using "hit points," if you do the Successes thing, like a BW Body of Argument).
      ----
      Of course, dice suck in LARP, so you might have to do some kind of RPS and break ties with sizes (size ALWAYS matters, in tats!). MAYBE use coins, or get X draws per Y sections, from a deck of cards, to get highest card (or highest poker hand). Basically, I too can dig the idea of drawing on hands for a LARP--and a weekly group might as well use Sharpie, as they'd be easier to touch-up if more permanent--but I can't dig any dice in such a case. Cards would likely be the most "appropriate" thing for a prisoners game, if a randomizer is used for resolution....
