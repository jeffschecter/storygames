\n I realise I'm becoming Mr Lacuna Fanboy at the moment, but anyway.

I find Lacuna fascinating. See, I think Lacuna's all about improv. Obviously. You're just reacting to what the players give you, so that's improv, right?

My friend Morgan thinks Lacuna is a Matrix game, because it's all about agents in a made-up world, and they have guns.

Ron Edwards, in his play report Nine gram medal, thinks Lacuna is a cold war game. Obviously: the whole thing is a parallel to a Cold War setting.

Jared Sorenson, who wrote Lacuna, doesn't really understand it. (It's like that bit in Being John Malkovich, where John Malkovich slides down the chute that leads to his own head).

Anyway, I'm trying to persuade Emily Care Boss to run it, and I heard Paul Czege nearly ran it, and that'd be great, too.

Can you run it too and tell me what you think it's about? Thanks.

Graham

    \n To expand my remarks...recognizing I'm no expert on Alfred Hitchcock.
    For me, there is a real sense of dread that I think Hitchcock wanted his audience to feel.
    Our GM had us start play 'in the dark' about the setting--some sort of error during insertion that caused memory loss. So I have the feeling that we're missing something, that wheels are spinning out of our sight and there's something our characters should be doing to stop it. But we're not. Eventually, we're going to find out what that is. I feel, perhaps more than even my character, like a leaf blown on the wind...like Cary Grant's character in North by Northwest who was mistaken for a spy--I think.
    Mel

    Posted By: GB SteveI think it's the game of playing a gamer
    \nThat's my thought on the game: you have a bunch of characters who adopt pseudonyms to delve into an imaginary space and fight bad guys and monsters. There aren't any rules for the "real world", just the Blue City. The metaphor should be fairly clear.


    We played it relatively recently, and it went okay but not amazingly great. That has to do with an overly large group (including one player who objects to anything not d20 based), time spent explaining the rules rather than playing, a distracting four year old and a not fully fleshed out scenario. Nonetheless, it was good and I think if we play again in the future (which I hope we do), the game could be all sorts of cool.
