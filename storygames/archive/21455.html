    \n I've always been really fond of rule-of-thumb mechanics which allow easy adjudication of various actions.

    Here's a simple example, in PbtA format, of a GM tool/tip/technique...\n

    When someone tries to take advantage of a special feature, whether tactical, character-based, or situational, choose one of the following:

    * This gives you the opportunity to do something you couldn't normally do: go ahead.
    * You could normally do that anyway, so, in this case you gain advantage/a bonus/an increased effect. (And, ideally, your game has a benchmark for these, like D&D5's "advantage" mechanic, so it's extra easy.)

    Or else (but save for unusual circumstances):

    * That sounds like a long shot. To do so, you'll need to first ____________ (which may or may not involve a roll).
    \nI also really like Sorcerer/TSoY "rollover successes" for the same reason - so flexible and easy in application, and they balance themselves.
