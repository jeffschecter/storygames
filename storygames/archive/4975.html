Recent posts in the Giant Monster Hate-Love Fest of Doom Thread (middle of page 3 or so) have got me thinking about all the great free games I've played over the years and how to catalog and promote them better so they can get the sweet, sweet lovin' that they rightfully deserve.

So... I'm gonna make a list of them (with links), to post somewhere prominently, and I need your help. Here are my qualifications, which are a bit more stringent than the other free game lists that I know of:

1) It has to be a full game, playable on its own. I might make another list for hacks of existing games later, but this is not that list.

2) It has to be available for free on the internets. Preferably it's available due to its original author wanting it to be. For stuff like Scarlet Wake where the original author has disappeared... I'm okay with making every effort to contact them and then posting it until they tell us to take it down. (At least, I think I am. Is that insane / immoral / something else bad?)

3) It has to have been actually played by a group of people that does not include the original author of the game and his/her bestest friends. There has to be a record of this play of some sort, preferably something we can link to, like an actual play post or a note on RPGnet that says, "Yeah, we played it last weekend."

4) It has to have received an enthusiastic response from people who have actually played it. It's okay if it's not the greatest thing since sliced bread. But the response needs to be better than just "It looks like a pretty solid game; I can't wait to see the finished version." My benchmark is this: a majority of the play group has to be excited about playing the game again without changing or adding any rules. If you'd play it again, just as it is, that's good. If you wouldn't, maybe it still needs some work or validation by a different group.

5) Arbitrary clause for not including games that are clearly terrible but somehow managed to pass the other requirements because they were played by a band of monkeys who adored the Flinging Poo mechanics and ignored the rest of the game. I don't really expect to use #5.

So... to start creating this list, I'd love it if people could link to relevant games and actual play posts, recommending free games that they've had a blast playing. For example, you could post something like:\n
Game: Risus Avenged Turbo!
Most Recent Version: March 2007, available at THIS URL
Creator(s): John Randolph Tynes III
Description: A relatively generic and rules-light system, but one built to kick your play up to Mach 13. We found that that the mechanics for Super Advanced Fighting were really unique and even adapted them to some other games we played.
Actual Play: A record of our play is over at THIS LINK. Some other guys on RPGSite played it and posted THIS REVIEW.
\nIf you know of a great free game that used to be available, let me know and I'll try to contact the authors to see if they'll let me host it somewhere (or you can try to contact them yourself, if you'd rather). I already have plans to go after Vincent about Nighttime Animals Save the World and Paul about The World, the Flesh, and The Devil. Maybe they'd rather have them be unavailable, but I figure it's worth a shot.

Thanks in advance. Free stuff 4FR.

P.S. IDEA SUGGESTIONS (still need links, descriptions, actual play threads): Shadows, Nicotine Girls, The Valedictorian's Death, Scarlet Wake, Risus, Fudge, TSOY, Fate, The Window, The Pool, Jared's free games, Otherkind, Zak's free games, etc.
