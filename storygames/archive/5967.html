\n I'm going to do the first playtest of Anima Prime at Gamestorm on Friday, March 28, in the evening. The tentative starting time will be 7pm or 8pm, depending on where other games fall for players. I'd like to open it up for you all first and then see if I'll schedule it into a public slot as well, if there aren't enough players. I know Lukas, Matt and Jake expressed interest, at the least, but I don't know what your guys' schedules are so far. I'm posting this both to Portland Indie Gamers and Story Games.

Anima Prima is a game based on anime shows as well as the Final Fantasy series, most specifically FFX. Its design goals are to allow for lightweight fun and spontaneous gaming with little preparation, but also for character-centric storylines and developments. It's specifically designed to work well for groups, in that it allows for players assisting each other, keeps up the fun for others even if one or two players aren't in top form, and encourages roleplaying among player characters.

For this playtest, I'm going to run a prepared setting rather than create it with the group, so that we can focus on testing out the mechanics. The basic setting information is provided in a separate post (Ghostfield). This will be one of the prepared settings in the final game (and like all of the game text, it'll be Creative Commons licensed and freely available online). It's not absolutely necessary for players to know the setting information; they can just show up and listen to me talk about it for a minute or two.

Here are the draft rules: Anima Prime Alpha and the Factions Draft. We probably won't do much with the faction rules this time around, but you never know.

If you're interested, please post and let's work something out. We can also discuss character and group concepts and story seeds beforehand. The more prep work we get done, the more we can focus on running the mechanics through the wringer.
