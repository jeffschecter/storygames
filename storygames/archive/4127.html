    \n Here's a Faery's Tale thread at Indie Press Revolution including links to several Actual Plays that should provide some insight into the game. There's also a link to a page of reviews of the original (non-deluxe) version.

    If I had to categorize Faery's Tale Deluxe, I'd say it's probably a cross between a traditional rpg & a story game. You do have a Narrator & players, who each control a faery, but the rules permit players to take some control over the story by buying plot twists or other changes, or adding complications for a reward. (Since kids are going to do those things anyway, it may as well be part of the game). At least, that's how I see it, but I'm not much of a theorist or overly concerned about in whose 'camp' the game falls.

    \n Cool! Interest in Adventures in the Land of a Thousand Kings.

    It's basically the game where you, the player, travel to a magical fantasy world and have adventures. The game can be light-hearted or serious, but the content is G-rated-if-you-want-it-to-be and has many of the joyful elements of children's fantasy. Also, since the players themselves are the core characters of the game, things tend less towards violence and mayhem as solutions to problems than in most RPGs.

    Anyone interested can shoot me an e-mail at taogames@gmail.com

    It's good to see a site that understands the joys of children and introducing them to the important parts of our lives.
