Posted By: reactionYes.
\nThat's my experience too. It works surprisingly well head-to-head, so long as you stick to the rules and play your cards close to the vest. Don't give away your ideas until you spring them. It may even be more fun than usual because you're riffing straight off of what the other person just said (if you want). It can create a very tight creative feedback loop.

Anybody else have two-player Microscope anecdotes?

    \n Holy crap does it ever work!!!

    The wife and I decided to give it a shot. She was curious about what ever happened to the refugee's sent by Aslan home at the end of 'prince caspian'

    We ended up with an awesome history of city states, ceremonial crippling of the ruling class of "thinkers", a slave rebellion and a great library inside a labyrinth of caves...

    \n I haven't played it since playtest, but it worked then. I did a head to head thing where we did something like Palladium's Nightbane and an eclipse struck that transformed people into disgusting monsters. Our main focus for most of the game was a guy who transformed in a hulking beast with fangs, lost his wife, but then became a celebrity and professional wrestler. He died thinking about her, though, and wondering what happened. It was really cool.

    \n That game sounds excellent, Kenny! I think using an existing story as a springboard is a great idea.

    My best two-player game started off with the Nazi's winning World War II (like they do) and slowly dominating the world. Because the Nazi's didn't get shut down by the Allies, the German people had to eventually reconcile how they felt about what they had done, all on their own. Without external enemies, the mainstream government backs well away from extremism, which of course gives birth to secret inner circle of old school, diehard fascists, The Ring of Fenris. Surprisingly thoughtful story, along with some near future iron sci fi machines-of-war action.

    Bret, was that the game with the famous exchange:

    you: (blow up beautiful city)
    her: "Why would you do that?!?"
    you: "Because I'm evil! Let's play!"

    Because I still love that.
