\nA brief summary of the events:
\n
\nFr Carmine and Tarim are talking in a bar, De'alist and Farleigh are both nearby. 4 thugs burst into the room looking for Fr Carmine and the lead Half Orc Thug attempts to take him hostage. A fight ensues whereby the Player Characters kill 3 of the Thugs and take one captive. \xa0A piece of leather is found in a locket worn by the Half Orc and questioning of the captive and the dead reveal that the Thugs were hired by Scarface Orcs to capture the Priest.

\nAfter reading a lot of the posts on Story Games and Dungeon World forums I acted as follows during a combat:
\n
\nThe three \u201cHuman Thugs\u201d lunge with their knives at the people in the inn that had come to the defense of the Priest...
\nTarim moves to pin his knife hand and control his body. - I took this as Hack and Slash, reacting to the move.
\nDe'alist Stands up from the table, moving rather smoothly as he waves his hand toward the thug that came near. "Vel'Ros'e'na" (Magic Missile) \u2013 I took this as Defy Danger, then Cast a Spell.
\nFr. Carmine picks up his stool and swings it at the nearest thug's head \u2013 I took this as accept the attack, then Hack and Slash (I wasn't comfortable with it and explained why \u2013 the fiction didn\u2019t mention the incoming attack \u2013 and as it was the first combat we went with it).

    \n Re: Spot/notice rolls - if you think the players should notice, just tell them what it is and play to see what happens. No need for a roll, treat it like Passive Perception in D&D 4E. Otherwise, if it's something they shouldn't necessarily notice, or you'd rather let them discover for themselves, just don't say anything. If they discern realities, great, if not, move on and play to see what happens.

    On a Defy Danger roll of 7-9, can the \u201cworse outcome, hard bargain or ugly choice\u201d be ignored if the action that caused the Defy danger to be rolled is reconsidered?
    \nAll the "Defy Danger" roll does is trigger after the GM says something like "There is a Danger, what do you do?" and the player responds by ignoring or attempting to tough his or her way through that Danger. So, if you defy Danger and are given a hard choice, the choice may be to not do what you'd intended to do in the first place. Like so.

    "There's a bunch of goblins on the hillside, they can see you running for the house. An arrow lands in the tree nearby - you're a sitting duck out here. What do you do?" "I want to run for the House and kick the door in." "Okay, well, that's definitely an attempt to Bend Bars, Lift Gates, but you need to dodge some arrows first. Defy Danger for me, please." "Roll roll roll ... 7!" "Okay, you can get to the door without getting arrowed, but you have to juke back and forth and, in doing so, your pack slips for your shoulder and you'll have to leave it behind. Otherwise, you get to the door with an arrow stuck in you - alternately, you can just bail out halfway - no arrow, no lost pack but you're no closer to the door. Which do you want?" and if the player chooses the "fuck it, I don't run for it" option, then none of this stuff happens and the player doesn't get to make his attempt.

    I don't know if that answers your question, exactly, but I'm sure other people will want to jump in and help clear things up, too.
