Right now we're on a 10th session of exalted and 6th of a Wod game.

What sort of group size do you guys run with?

    Posted By: DionysusDo you have any rules for "must have X players to have a game" or something like that?
    \nAt least two players, no more than six. 3-5 players is the sweet spot where our games really hum, so that's what we aim for.

    EDIT TO ADD: In terms of "how many people need to show up for us to play," it varies depending on the game. Generally we go in with a firm schedule (every Friday night, every Saturday, every other Wednesday, whatever) that we've decided on mutually. If someone can't make it one week, they generally let everyone know in advance and we decide what we're going to do about it right then and there. Depending on the game and the circumstances, the rest of us might get together and watch a movie or just hang out, or we'll skip that week, or we'll play without the missing person, or we'll play something else.

    Back when I was much younger, I was in a few groups with a "eh, show up if you can, we'll see who's there on game night" policy, and that sucked: in the long run, it's easier and less stressful to just game with people who can commit to a schedule and who'll call you and tell you if something's come up and they have to cancel.

    \n I kicked off an OD&D mega-dungeon campaign as an episodic campaign to run at a game store. It ended up that we only played at the game store once and never really took advantage of the episodic aspect.

    I had a long running Traveller campaign back in the day that really only had one committed player. Other players would come and go. Generally, PCs of non-present players would be exempt from planetary ground teams and we never ended a session with folks on the ground.

    Dogs in the Vinyard is well suited to an episodic campaign, though I have a hard time getting into a campaign that would be so episodic in nature. Ultimately, what keeps me from being excited about running a DitV campaign is that it feels like there is nothing for the GM to build up on.

    So for me to be invested in an episodic campaign, there has to be something tangible I am building upon as the GM. I think it would also need a core of players who were regular (even if it might occasionally happen that from one week to the next, there were no players in common, so long as each week there were at least some players who played on some kind of regular basis.

    An episodic campaign with circulating players would also either have to have player independent prep, or there would have to be reasonable notice who was going to show up. And even then, real occasional players probably wouldn't be as tied in, in Burning Wheel for example, I would be hitting the beliefs of the regular players much harder than the beliefs of the occasional players.

    Frank

    Posted By: DionysusCan you expand what you are referring to with "player independent prep" What are the players preparing exactly?
    \nPerhaps I should have said "PC independent prep" In other words, prep that doesn't care who the PCs are. In Burning Wheel, prep has to be relevant to beliefs, well, I guess it could be independent if the players wrote new beliefs to hit the prep you did. In a DitV campaign, I would expect the GM to build towns in reaction to what the PCs did in previous sessions.

    If the GM doesn't know which PCs will be present, it's hard to prep anything that is best when it is specific to a PC.

    An old school megadungeon of course is expected to be designed independent of the PCs, so any prep done for that would be PC/Player independent. Of course even there, I suspect GMs started to prep in anticipation of their players, Got players who hate certain kinds of dungeon features, and do everything in their power to bypass them? You might decide to stop using those features, or you might decide to force the PCs into them. Of course if you push too hard, the players quit, so perhaps you decide to remove some features because otherwise you will lose your players.

    Frank

    \n How big is your pool or RPG players?

    The pool is about seven people right now, it has been more, up to about 12 at times

    How many of those people regularly attend games?

    We have a core of four, there are another three who are occasional players depending on commitments

    What sort of group size do you guys run with?

    We generally run with three to five. It is usually the most or nearly all the core core plus sometimes one of the irregulars. I have a rule as a GM to max out at 5 players. We have run games with larger numbers in the past, but too often found they breakdown. Folks don't get enough spotlight time.

    How long are your normal games? What type of game are they?

    We play Monday nights for about 3 hours. A given game tends to last 8-12 sessions before we switch. We might return to that game again later, but we consider 8-12 a 'season' and then discuss if we want to commission a second 'season'.

    We have tried a couple of models over time. Our older model was to have one 'main' game, but have an alternate in case the GM could not make it, or people whose presence was needed for a session. That failed a bit, because the time between sessions on the alternate made it too hard to get into the groove. So we switched to running some kind of 'one-shot' on those weeks. For a long time our one-shot has been, run IAWA, but I think we are burnt out on that for a while. I'm suggesting we prepare some more con-style one shots, with pre-gens, for games someone has always wanted to try, but thinks he is unlikely to get group buy in for a few sessions.

    We try to alternate the type of game, but they are usually small press. IIRC correctly it is something like this so far this year:

    Before
    HeroQuest Glorantha (Pelanda)
    Dying Earth
    HeroQuest Glorantha (Black Horse Troop)
    Houses of the Blooded

    Now
    Diaspora

    Next
    HeroQuest Glorantha (Orlanthi)


    There is a fair amount of HeroQuest Glorantha in there, it was the game that brought the original lineup together

    Do you have any rules for "must have X players to have a game" or something like that?

    As with Graham, three or more. If there are three of us, we usually try to go ahead.

    \nHow big is your pool or RPG players?
    60-90 only counting friends (higher if you count acquaintances) but also including casual players.

    How many of those people regularly attend games?
    Depending on what you mean by regular, I would say people who play at least once a month... 40+. People who play weekly or bi-weekly... 26 but was much higher before many people moved away because of work or family. The economy has taken away many close friends :-(

    What sort of group size do you guys run with?
    If I'm running... 3-4. But many of my friends want as many players as possible, making it more like a party than a traditional game. They will usually run for 7-10+ players which to me is crazy unless we are playing games that specifically handle that.

    How long are your normal games?
    3-4 hours

    What type of game are they?
    Anything that can be played in less than 4 hours. But if it's weeknights we play in public spaces and are restricted by table space... games with minis tend to be out. Weekends table space is a non-issue.

    Do you have any rules for "must have X players to have a game" or something like that?
    2-3 players (not including the GM) for an RPG. But if less people are available we'll play a boardgame, watch a movie, grab or make food. Socializing is our number 1 priority and we will choose a game to fit around the situation at hand.

    \nHave any of you tried episodic games?
    We've played episodic games with D&D, Dogs in the Vineyard, Mouse Guard, Shadowrun, Mutants & Masterminds, and Call of Cthulhu. They work well but it's a different experience. Great for socializing!

    But I find it takes 3-4 sessions of a game with the same group before we really sync up with each other, with our characters, and with the game itself. On the other extreme, I don't like super long term campaigns because I like to play a variety of games with a variety of people.

    My preferred method of play is a 6 session weekly game with 1-2 spots open for rotating players. For most of us the game is a very short dedicated campaign, for some of us it's episodic. For me this is the best of both worlds.
