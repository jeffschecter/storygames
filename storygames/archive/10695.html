\n Guys, this forum has brilliantly identified all the things that are wrong with indie games. Through our tireless efforts, we've found many fatal flaws and subjected them to exhaustive analysis.

However, it's getting hard to keep track of everything that's wrong! This thread can act as a master thread of Everything That's Wrong With Indie Games. Next time we wonder what's wrong, we can just look at this thread.

Please post the thing that's wrong and a link to where we've scientifically proved it's wrong. Remember, if there's one thing more important than searing critique, it's a proper indexing system.

    \n The problem with indie RPG's is that they are made by people, individuals who have different ideas about what is fun and different ideas about what a game text should and shouldn't have. This makes it hard to find definite patterns, it is as if all of these people have had different experiences with gaming and games.

    The problem with indie RPG's is that they are made by the same quasi-creative assholes with a thousand bucks to gamble who stumbled from the Forge to Story Games. These games are chuck full-o the same bullshit relationship mechanics that worked in Mountain Witch, the same open traits that work in Dogs in the Vineyard, the same dice mechanics as Sorcerer and the same Fan Mail rip-offs from PTA because they are choking until vomiting with all of the same Actual Play posts that tell them what fun is.

    The problem with indie RPG's is that the online culture is filled with people who have too much time at their desks. Rather than talking about techniques that make gaming fun or unfun or just sharing mad and beautiful ideas, they skull-fuck the entire endeavor through masturbatory forum threads designed to "challenge" or "critique" with a wide, sweeping hand without any real rigor or deep thought designed to actually help people.

    The problem with indie RPG's is you, you fabulous bastard.

    Luckily, you are also the glory of indie RPG's. You play. You talk about it and roll dice, pretending to be some kind of fucking elf and argue about it and you rock. The problem with indie RPG's is we don't appreciate you enough.
