\n Okay, so it seems that my idea about a fantasy game designed to produce encyclopedic amounts of fantasy lore ala World of Warcraft has sparked a bit of interest. As I said, right now it's a bunch of scribbled notes and notions. So, here are my notions.

So one of the things that always frustrates in me games is when there's some Canon Rule about the setting that players CAN'T break because that's how the world "is" which lasts right up until some Canon NPC comes along who is the uber-exception to the rule. So I wanted to make a game that encouraged making rules about "how the world works" then shattering them at dramatic moments. Hence, the "exceptional" in Exceptional Fantasy. Everybody gets to break one rule right at character creation.

So if in your world "Undead are severed from life and can't be Druids" it would be perfectly fine for a PC to be "The one undead who through sheer force of will has grasped onto his connection with life to be a druid."

In play every time a rule is reinforced it gets "stronger" which means it both gets harder to break but also becomes more effective when you break it. There would of course be ways of adding new Canon Rules and the such.

The second notion is that anything that happens in WoW Lore always happens at a high level. You don't have individual scenes you have declarations like, "And that's when Thrall invaded Northrend" or whatever. So that's how play works. You declare MASSIVE world scale behaviors. You don't "Visit Janna Proudmore" You "Sign a diplomatic treaty with Janna Proudmore" and so forth. You don't do things to build up to war, you just go ahead and Invade. You then play out scenes under that world altering heading.

A third notion is that the stats will be Warfare, Diplomacy, Subterfuge and Romance.

A fourth notion is that there always seems to be some kind of World Threat hanging over everything that gets built up the longer it goes unattended to. So there's some kind of timing mechanic. Right now it's a d20 sitting in the middle of the table. At each value of 5 (5, 10, 15) a sort of mini-boss "spawns." These mini-bosses sort of "stack" so if the 5 mini-boss isn't dealt with by the time the 10 shows up then they make each other more powerful. This happens all the way up until the 20 which represents the main villain behind what the mini-bosses have been up to. Again these stack. Assuming the threat doesn't destroy the world or anything it eventually resets and New Threat arises.

One other notion is that the Canon Rules are associated with things. So you have the Canon Rules for Stormwind. The Canon Rules for Undead. The Canon Rules for the Argent Dawn. And so forth. If ALL the rules associated with a concept get broken then something earth shattering happens to it. It gets destroyed or transformed or whatever and a new set of Canon Rules are written for it.

So.... that's what I've got.

Jesse

    \n Andy,

    See my previous post. Right now at character creation each player invents a Race, A Nation, A World Role and An Organization their character belongs to. They write three Canon Rules for each of these things. That makes each character 100% unique though with no overlap.

    So I might change it so there's a "World Building Phase" Where you generate as a group Race = Number of Players, Nations = Number of Players, Organizations = Number of Players. Something like that.

    The point is there's a step in play where a whole bunch of setting material and the Canon Rules for that material gets generated. So you end up with stuff like:

    The Order of the Mourning Tree
    -----All members are men.
    -----No member may use magic
    -----They are the sworn enemy of Shalmanisar The Wicked

    Jesse

    \n The idea is that there's two levels of play. One level are these HUGE declarations. The other is individual scenes within that context. So yes, there are action level scenes. I'm thinking about linking stuff to the stats and creating a kind of scene economy. So it looks something like this.

    High Level:
    -----Shalmanisar The Wicked declares a Warfare action and "Unleashes an army of demons upon the capital city of Shadowspire"

    Lower Level
    -----Jonas The Noble has a Warfare score of three. So he gets three scenes within a Warfare action. He uses one to have a Romance scene where he says farewell to his lady love before going off to fight the demons.

    Something like that anyway.
