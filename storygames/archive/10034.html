    \n ...or 1x2 flats. So, for instance:
    Fudge Dice:
    2 sides = white 2x2 flats (positive)
    2 sides = black 2x2 (negative)
    2 sides = 1x2 white and 1x2 black; or some other color 2x2 (blank)

    Burning Wheel Black Dice (adjust for Gray and White):
    3 sides = white 2x2 flats (traitor)
    2 sides = black 2x2 (success)
    1 side = red 2x2 (explodes)

    And using a 1x2x2 corner ("L") flat and a 1x1 flat, you can make something that's usable for a "3/4" value (though I can't currently find a 1x2x2 flat, i.e. no pips, in Pick A Brick, I am SURE they exist!).

    "We" is the Buildhouse, which is mostly Eric Breeden, my wife Carrie, sometimes Rob Bohl, a couple of other friends on occasion, and me. We're converting an old barn into a machine, wood, and blacksmithing shop. We all know parts of the technologies and we're teaching each other stuff. Eric knows a lot more than I do, but I know how to build bikes and work composites. One other guy knows bikes (better than I, actually), but no one else has used composites. Someone else knows cars and engines, particularly diesels. Another one knows aircraft. None of us knows more than the most rudimentary smithing, but that's not going to stop us.

    \n

    Occasionally, there is a fire.

    \n

    If one were to make such things in an idea world where we've got all the tools we can conceivable possess, I think one would CNC a mold, yeah. In a world of Bridgeports, we'd make a prototype, make a mold, and cast products. The key to Lego, though, is that 2\xb5m tolerance. Plus the trade secret ABS formula. And their extraordinary timeless dyes. I certainly don't know how to achieve that in a final product. If it's just me thinking about how to do it, I glue 2x2 plates onto existing dice, probably with the aid of some custom-built tool to make sure things line up.

    \n

    It's in no way conceivable that it would be profitable or even not a loss, except for the possibilities of appealing to the Lego fanatic crowd and somehow managing to get pricing and volume just right. I sincerely hope that's what the Brickarms dude is doing. I hope it's not costing him, or at least that he loves every second of it and it costs him less than cable would.
