\n I think you could create a role-playing game just using the cards from the Race for the Galaxy game.
\n
\nCharacter creation:
\nGet dealt 2 cards- the first represents your background (where you grew up or what your parents did for a living), the other represents you at the start of the game (where you work or live).
\nGet dealt some other cards, and choose some from both lots (possibly with a cost ceiling)- your character attributes are calculated from the cards you selected.
\n
\nExplore extra draw -> +1 Galactic Knowledge
\nExplore extra keep -> +1 Well Travelled
\nDevelop cost reduction -> +1 Engineering
\nDevelop card draw -> +1 Assets
\nand so on for a number of custom attributes.
\n
\nIn play, you just turn over a card to see what the next destination/antagonist/problem is.
\n
\nThe deck even acts as a randomiser, with each card having a build cost and a VP value that could be incorporated.
\n
\nYou wouldn't have to use the game's own setting, necessarily, and with a little adjustment it could suit most heavily colonised far sci-fi settings.
\n
\nThoughts?

    \n *idle thoughts mode on*
    \n
    \nSome bits of a potential system jump out at me- the way that certain powers stack and have different numeric values suggests a skill system of some sort. Other things, like the 'gene' and 'rebel' icons could also represent skills. The rarer abilities, that only crop up on 1 or 2 cards, could easily be feats that alter how a skill works or provide some other bonus. Deciding what skills should be present and mapping them to abilities shouldn't be too hard.
    \n
    \nThe potential difficulty is with the goods. We have four types, but each of them can appear as 'trade bonus', 'consume for VP', 'consume for cards', 'production world' and 'windfall world'. I'm stuck trying to come up with an interpretation for all of the possibilities. And that's before we get to the more exotic abilities in the later phases. Would simplifying it so that we ignore either the type of good or the type of ability lose too much of the feel of RftG?

    \n I really like this idea. One of the great things about Race cards is that as soon as you glance at a card, you can see what kind of plots/stories/narration its tied to: exploration, warfare, trade, and so on.

    With the goods, maybe each good could be tied to an attitude, class, or issue. Like a brown good implies hardy miners, labor unions, and working folk. Blue implies the nouveau riche. Green is the technocrats and their risky gene technology. Yellow is alien influences and ideas that regular folk find disturbing. Or they could tie to an important plot element, threat, or even character.

    I also like how the 6-cost developments feel like factions or institutions, possibly with criss-crossing goals.

    \n Okay, some thoughts thrown out.

    We're going to end up with a lot of stats that can have 0-value or quite a high value, so the system needs to handle that. So- use Otherkind dice (with only 2 dice to roll). Your 'stats' can be spent a point at a time after the roll to increase either the accomplishment or the danger die category by 1 level- but not both. Your pools in stats refresh only at the GM's discretion.

    What card abilities mean:

    +1 Explore draw -> +1 Galactic Knowledge
    +1 Explore keep -> +2 Galactic Knowledge (GK can be quite high, to avoid a party being stuck on account of not having the info they need to get the story moving)
    Explore merge with hand -> Roll 3 dice on Galactic Knowledge rolls
    -1 Development cost -> +1 Engineering
    Development draw 1 -> +1 Assets
    -1 Settle cost -> +1 Navigation
    Draw after Settling -> +1 Assets
    Settle twice -> Roll 3 dice for Navigation
    +1 Military -> +1 Combat Training
    +1 Trade value -> +1 Mercantile
    Consume draw 1 -> +1 Assets
    Consume for card -> +1 Mercantile
    Consume for VP -> +1 Influence
    Gain VP -> +2 Influence
    Discard for VP -> +1 Influence
    Produce -> +2 Engineering
    Windfall -> +1 Engineering
    Produce draw 1 -> +1 Assets
    [Rebel] -> +1 Rebel Ties
    [Imperium] -> +1 Imperium Ties
    [Genes] -> +1 Genetically Engineered

    Incomplete, I know, but a start. For abilities that are tied to a particular good, they are stat bonuses that can only be spent in the associated milieu-
    Blue - High-population consumerist worlds
    Brown - Heavy industry and mining worlds
    Green - Wild, untamed worlds
    Yellow - Worlds tainted by Alien involvement

    Rough and ready, but probably playable all-in-all. Incomplete power list, I know, but enough for a funky pick-up game one day.
