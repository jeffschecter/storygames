    \nWe were heavily influence by Champions at the time, but our JM was hard-core into the EC rules. So, we ended up with a compromise system to allow stat adjustment:
    1. Every point you lowered STR gave you a point to INT, or vice versa.
    2. Every 2 points you lowered ADA gave you a point to either INT, LUC or LEA.
    3. Every 3 points you lowered LEA, MAG or ROB gave you a point to either STR, INT or DEX.
    4. Every point you lowered DEX gave you 1 point to STR or INT, or every 2 points you lowered DEX gave you 3 points to ADA, MAG, or ROB.

    Looking back on it now, I can't for the life of me remember the math, but it was the result of several evenings earnest discussion and passionate debate. We also debated whether or not "choose a race" meant you had your pick; the JM thought that since there was a table you had to roll on that. Our JM also gave Amazons an armor skill: if they wore no armor, you had -10% to hit them, and if they wore no clothing at all, -20%. Those were the days -- we would spend an entire evening just generating characters (sometimes two or three at a time), since it was such a mini-adventure to get something "just right."

    \n Yes, there's definitely a push/pull thing going on between strength and intelligence. Once you realize its there, I think it's easy to understand and enjoy.

    The nature of EC meant that players would roll a 4 or 5 in an ability and they'd just go with it.

    Took us an hour of play before we realized the difference between magic attack and ensorcel, but it was actually fun to reason out the system as a group.
