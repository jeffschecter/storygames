\n So, for my next 5E excursion, I decided to go completely the opposite direction of Advanced Wizards and Wizards, and "run" (more on why it's in quotes below; a better word would be "organize") a campaign in which the only allowed classes are the total non-casters, to capture that sword and sorcery feel and to really let those classes shine in all their martial glory.

The campaign is called Adventurers of Wit and Weaponry, because I can't just stop using AW&W as a campaign moniker, now can I? I recruited about 12 people, about 4 or 5 of whom want to GM at some point.

Also, one of the players is a linguist and is inventing a language which we are using to name in-game locales. You want Tolkienesque? I got your Tolkienesque right here!

Anyway, one of the big mistakes I made with the first AW&W was that, even though the game had a lot of structure to it, it didn't have an endgame plan right from the start. So with this new game, I wanted there to be such a limit in place.

The story is a totally cheeseball one, but will be fun to play: we have to reassemble the shards of a great and powerful weapon that was shattered in the last battle against the aliens, because the aliens are coming back soon. There are 7 shards and 15 dungeons. Of the dungeons with no shard, 7 will contain clues as to where other shards may or may not be located, and 1 will contain a different artifact. The aliens will show up after 15 sessions, so we are up against the clock here.

Now, here's where I really got clever. I am contracting @David_Berg to decide what goes where, and send the information to my partner, who will put each piece of information in a sealed envelope with the name of that dungeon on the front. Whenever we beat a dungeon, we open its envelope together\u2014it will be a surprise to everyone!

This way, even the GM of each session won't know what the big treasure is; their only responsibility is to prep the dungeon itself. Best of all, I am only going to run the first session, and then work with the other people interested in GMing as needed.

I'm really looking forward to starting and hope it doesn't collapse around my ears!

Matt
