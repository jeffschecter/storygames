    \n I'm in two local groups, and each has a listserv type deal. These are used as follows:

    1. To get a show of hands to see who is going to be there on any given evening, and announce upcoming absences
    2. To figure out who is in charge of dinner and coordinating that, for the group that eats together
    3. Sending out prep materials or soliciting information for an upcoming session
    4. Sharing funny links

    Occasionally there's more substantial discussion ("what are we doing next?") but it is pretty rare. As a communication tool to keep us on track with very low overhead, it works great and has for many years.

    \n My main priorities are:
    \n1) Having the ability to forward new posts to my email so I don't have to check the site.
    \n2) Free or cheap. Meetup works pretty well, but I've seen groups fold over the cost factor.
    \n3) A way to sign up for individual events to get a head count.
    \n
    \nEach of the methods mentioned earlier (Google group, Facebook, Meetup) will do two out of three and can probably work in conjunction with another free service to get the third, and I can totally live with that. If you're willing to spend the time to make something custom, it could meet all of our needs.
