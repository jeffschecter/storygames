\n In a thread on RPG.net I found this little cute game. For you who don't feel like downloading and read a full pdf, here is a screenshot of the whole game:



This is why I think Fortune Cookies and Nuclear War is a great game on paper (I haven't played it).

\xd7 It uses tropes. USA vs Russia, nuclear threat. Tropes makes a game easy to understand, and may also change due to the players interpretation of the trope. In other words, it's going to be a different game every time.
\xd7 Everybody has easy access to what the players need to play the game: a pen, a piece of paper, a Chinese restaurant.
\xd7 It got a multilevelled (social) mechanism. Negotiation, but you don't care about two of the five demands, making the game benefit from contra-bluffs.
\xd7 It's short and easy to explain. A really clear structure of how the game should be played.
\xd7 The game have a clear goal for the players. To settle the negotiation but not concede to many demands.
\xd7 The game creates an investment: the bill.

I ordered the list after it's importance, with the most important parts of a game at the end of the list. And all this from a game that can fit on the back of a business card. How would your game stand against this one, if you compare the items in the list?
