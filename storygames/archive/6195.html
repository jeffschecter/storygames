Drawn to the world of men once more by the scent of bloody seas, Dajobas rises anew. Learn everything you need to know about this horrific deity including new domains, feats, spells, weapons, and a prestige class devoted to the worship of He Who Eats the World.

    \nAnnouncing Sinister Adventures, LLC: A New RPG Adventure Publisher
    \nFan favorite Dungeon Magazine\u2122 and Pathfinder\u2122 author Nicolas Logue announces Sinister Adventures, LLC, an adventure publisher dedicated to delivering the very finest RPG modules in multiple game systems.
    \nNew York, NY, April 1st, 2008.
    \n
    \nFan favorite Dungeon Magazine\u2122 and Pathfinder\u2122 author Nicolas Logue announces Sinister Adventures, LLC, an adventure publisher dedicated to making the very finest RPG modules available in multiple game systems, including 3.5 OGL, True20\u2122, Runequest\u2122, d20 Modern\u2122, d20 Future\u2122, Bulldogs\u2122, the Dawning Star\u2122 setting and more! Nick and Sinister have tapped some of the best known adventure authors of the world\u2019s most popular fantasy role playing game \u2013 including Wolfgang Bauer, Jason Bulmahn, Tim Hitchcock, Michael Kortes, Richard Pett, Greg A. Vaughan and others \u2013 to deliver two product lines: a fantasy line, titled Dark Vistas and a sci-fi/horror line, titled Dark Horizons. The lines will be illustrated by some of the industry\u2019s most beloved artists, including Aaron Acevedo, Richard Clark, Andrew Hou, Wayne Reynolds and Kevin Yan.
    \n
    \nThe Sinister Adventure web site (www.sinisteradventures.com) is live now and offers an entire line of inexpensive pdf products by top name authors, called Indulgences (available at www.sinisteradventures.com/index.php/indulgences). The first four Indulgences, written by Wolfgang Baur, Nicolas Logue, Craig Shackleton and Greg A. Vaughan offer GMs everything they need to kick off a high seas adventure campaign and explore the cold reaches of the ocean\u2019s deepest rifts.
    \n
    \nThe company\u2019s core products will be available on the site as both PDF download and print, starting in June, 2008 with their Dark Vista flagship 130+ page mega-adventure, Razor Coast, by Nicolas Logue. Dark Horizons will follow in August of 2008 with its innovative space horror adventure Cold Black, by Nicolas Logue and Lou Agresta. Preorders for both lines are now available.
    \n
    \nDark Vista mega-adventures include a built-in mini-campaign setting and take players through many layers of story and adventure, but do it non-linearly! Built on a plot web, instead of the standard linear approach, Dark Vista mega-adventures yield the widest possible range of play experiences and offer an unheralded degree of fluidity of play. Moreover, when you buy any Dark Vista or Dark Horizon adventure you also get PDF versions of the full adventure in all the other game systems available.
    \n
    \nTo deliver on their plan, Nicolas Logue and Sinister Adventures will also tap the Werecabbages, a freelance RPG writer\u2019s guild that includes rising star authors like Lou Agresta, Rone Barton, Tim and Eileen Connors, Liz Courts, Phil Larwood, James MacKenzie, Greg Oppedisano, Craig Shackleton, and Brendan Victorson.
    \n
    \nGoing forward, look to the Sinister Adventures web site for more than just product. Sinister will soon announce the Spawn of Dajobas contest. A 1,000 word 3.5 OGL open call monster design contest, the winner of which will receive $100.00 and be included in the forthcoming Dark Vista adventure Shrine of Frenzy, written by Brendan Victorson and illustrated by Kevin Yan.
    \n
    \nCome listen to our official podcast for more news and details about Sinister Adventures at www.sinisteradventures.com/index.php/dark-whispers.
    \n
    \n
    \nAbout Nicolas Logue
    \nRecently hired by Paizo Publishing as Organized Play Coordinator and director of the Pathfinder Society, Nick is well known for his work in Dungeon Magazine\u2122 (Issue #150 "Quoth the Raven", Issue #135 "Chains of Blackmaw", Issue #133 "Chimes at Midnight", Issue #132 "The Library of Last Resort", Issue #131 "Campaign Workbook - The Journey: Dimension Doors", Issue #129 "Campaign Workbook - The Dungeon: Black Sheep", Issue#120 \u201cObsidian Eye\u201d w/Brendan Victorson, Issue #117 \u201cThe Winding Way\u201d, Issue #102 "Cry Wolf"); his work for Wizards of the Coast\u2122 (Voyage of the Golden Dragon, City of Stormreach, Monster Manual V, Dragons of Eberron, Hell\u2019s Heart); for Paizo Publishing (GameMastery Modules D1: Crown of the Kobold King, E1: Carnival of Tears, U2: Hangman\u2019s Noose, Pathfinder#3: The Hook Mountain Massacre, Pathfinder #7: Edge of Anarchy, Pathfinder Chronicles: Classic Monsters Revisited) and his Open Design project (Blood of the Gorgon) with Wolfgang Baur.
    \n
    \nMore here: www.sinisteradventures.com
