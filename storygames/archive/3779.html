\n With all the discussion recently about honest criticism, I continue to be confounded. Other people have said better than I: no reason you have not to speak ill of someone else's game is a good one. Except maybe not hurting feelings, in which case either you're being an ass and either have something you could have said without being hurtful, or they're being childish.

I keep thinking (and sometimes saying) that healthy criticism would help fix a lot of what might be wrong with indie RPGs as a creative community. So, is there a blog or forum or something of critical game reviews? Ideally with an editor?

(If the answer is no, then, yes, I'm volunteering, if I there are a coupla more of you to volunteer to write criticism.)

    \nA thoughtful review is a thoughtful review. There is no inherent bias for or against based on the quality of a review. However, there are many different tastes out there. Websites, newspapers and magazines that do a lot of reviews tend to have a stable of reviewers with different tastes, and match the horror movie with the horror aficionado, for example. Even if the comedy guy is a better writer overall, the horror reviewer is going to write a more suitable review for that particular movie's potential audience.

    That's all.

    \n One advantage of an organized group of reviewers is that a game publisher could send a single free/review copy of the game and expect 2-3 reviews as a result (the 'organized' part would have to include some shipping-about of games, I'd guess.) I imagine that's a lot more attractive than sending out a copy to everyone who may write a review, six months from now, if you're lucky.

    A group of reviewers could also become an actual play group for the purpose of reviewing, via Skype if necessary. A lot of possibilities open up in terms of getting different perspectives into a single review text, as well -- for example, having a single Primary Reviewer but then adding in marginalia or brief thoughts from other members of the review group.

    \n Andy, Graham, Others

    Please don't turn Story-Games into a review site.
    RPGNet is a good place for product reviews, the Forge is a good place for analytical Actual Play reports, blogs are good for opinion pieces, publishers websites are good for direct communication with creators. Why does Story-Games need to muscle in on that action ? If you have something bad to say about a game, just think about what you are saying and ask yourself if this is the best place to say it.

    Story-Games unique selling point for me is that it is about 'Awesome'. Its about people being enthusiastic about games and ideas (their own and others), its a place where friendly > brutal. Reread the Rules and Purpose of Story-Games, its a good manifesto. If nothing else, read the Mission Statement.
    "This site is a place to loosely describe Role Playing Games. Specifically, new ways to play RPGs and new ways of approaching the hobby. This site is meant to be a relaxed, non-threatening, non-confrontational environment to discuss the above."

    Some of that atmosphere has been lost recently, though I expect its just a phase.

    Accentuate the positive,
    Colin
