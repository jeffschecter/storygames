Hi,

I'm sketching up a setting for Sorcerer and I'm interested in playing online, especially using voice or video chat like Skype. Trouble is, I'm not sure where to start. Is there a forum for that, or a set of best practices for finding players and organizing a game?

\n

I currently run Tunnels & Trolls games for my daughter and nephew, and my wife and brother-in-law join in too. For family play, T&T delivers: It's easy to prepare for, and the Gamist Creative Agenda makes it easy for the players to get into character. It's also very easy to bring in whatever the kids are interested in at the moment, whether it's Moogles, Pokemon, or wizard school.

    \n Write up your one-sheet and post it online somewhere so you can point people to it. Find inspirational images if you can, setting, tone, characters etc. You might look at Ron's modern Necromancy or some of the other Sorcerer One-Sheets that are around.

    Post about putting together you game here in the meetups category.
    You could also post at RPG.net in the Gaming Gatherings area there.

    Make sure to note how many players you want and when the possible time is. (Remember to include your time zone.)

    In your intro you might consider answering these questions so that your prospective players are on the same page.

    Play at a time that I can particpate. (I know...I'll just hope for the best.)

    \n For the games I've played over Skype, I prefer it when I can just use physical dice and report my results rather than having to use a die-roller (obviously this works better for some games than others). I enjoy the tactile sensation of rolling dice, so having to use automated methods detracts from the gaming experience for me.

    For me, the biggest Skype-related problem is crosstalk -- either the actuality (which means neither person can be heard) or the fear of it (which means people hold back because they don't want to drown out someone else). Figuring out the "who talks when" thing can be important to making the game flow well, because you probably won't have body language clues to help you like you would in a face-to-face game. Things like targeting questions or statements to specific people rather than "the group" can help the game flow more smoothly.
