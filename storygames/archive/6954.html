    \n Bind a taunt to an easy accessible key. This is a built in system that reinforces character. Hit the key when you make a kill, or at random times.

    Similarly, use the build in voice chat to request medical attention, report a spy, or cheer or jeer a point score.

    When playing tc_hydro, determine how wins and loses for the various sub-matches build into a larger fiction.

    When playing an engineer, give each sentry a unique name (you can reuse names when starting a new map). Celebrate and mourn the kills and destruction of your sentries.

    As a medic, play the role of the primary care physician for your team. Ascribe various health conditions to members of your team. If someone calls for a medic and you cannot (or will not) heal them, claim it is because they refused your advice and did not take their medication properly. Direct them to the ER admittance nurse.

    For the scout, blame your numerous deaths on one of the following: a) sports injuries, b) the coach, c) the sun's placement in your field of view.

    As the Heavy, select someone on the opposing team at random or by whatever criteria you deem fit (first kill, first killed by, first nemesis, etc). They are your love interest. When every you see them, proclaim your love. When you subsequently kill them, excuse it by either a) they made you hurt them, or b) you sometimes love too roughly. Feel free to intermix A and B whenever possible, even to explain the same kill.

    When playing the Pyro, talk animatedly with your teammates and the opposing team. Give words of encouragement and advice to everyone. Downplay your victories as luck. Play up your defeats by praising your enemy. However, only type using M, N, and F. If voice chatting, only speak in muffled words.

    With the sniper, pick one of your teammates at random or whatever criteria you choose. They are your boss. Ask them if they have any hits on the plate. If they do, bitch about how they aren't paying you enough while taking on the job anyway. If they do not, or do not reply, bitch about how they aren't paying you enough to sit around and do nothing. In the latter case, when you do make a headshot, tell your boss that it was a freebie, just to show there's no hard feelings. Repeat until they have a hit in mind. Addendum: If they name another teammate, tell them your rule: No women, no kids.

    For the demoman, spend the first part of the match (until the first score, checkpoint, or whatever seems like a good benchmark) bragging about how much you've had to drink and how drunk you are. Play it to the hilt, you are having the time of your life. And then, somewhere in the middle, crushing reality hits you, preferably as you are making a kill. You drink to numb the pain of death. You're a prisoner of your own vice. Get defensive, and angry at anyone who intrudes upon your domain. You just need some fucking space.

    As the soldier, you are a psychopath. War is your life. You've been trained by a secret government agency and your ethics stripped from your thought processes. You see the opposing team as something like demons, zombies, or mimes. Outside of the bloodlust, it's pretty hazy though. Occasionally, your programming breaks down and you see only your blood drenched hands. Lament the horror until the moment of sanity passes.

    As the spy, you are like any superspy. Everyone else on your team are other agents that build you up. The engineer has the cool gadgets. The medic, cool drugs. The demoman makes a great martini. The Heavy can drive a truck. And so on. The important stuff. Ask them often for some trivial help. Like getting your tux cleaned for the soiree at 2Fort, or building you a pen that launches grenades. But never ask them to do anything you should be capable of on your own. Like killing anyone on the other team or grabbing the intelligence. That's all up to you.

      \n I'm playing it right now. It's a pretty typical MMO in a lot of ways, lots of 'kill X Zombies' quests, but there are some things that are really cool about it.

      All of the quests have a bit more story to them than you usually find in an MMO, and many have really well done fully voice-acted intros. These tie into the setting really well, which is chock full of references to all kinds of classic genre stuff. The overall atmosphere is really good, and it's a lot of fun just exploring The Secret World.

      Some of the quests aren't just murdering things either. There are missions that turn into some halfway decent stealth missions, and some that turn into sets of genuinely well done adventure game style puzzles.

      The combat is kind of boring however, but if you're cool with it Skyrim, World of Warcraft, ETC, you'll probably be able to deal. I enjoy the depth of the character building system with it's intricate combos and optimization strategy.

      It also makes you solo a lot, which I'm cool with. But sometimes it's so solo friendly it can be hard to play when you're in a group, and the game really loves to drop you into solo instances.

      I'd highly recommend it if the combat being MMO style doesn't put you off, it's way more compelling than my memories of World of Warcraft back in the day.
