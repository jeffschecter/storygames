    \n I'm sure Watertown is too small for a station. But Amtrak does serve parts of Canada. But I'm getting ahead.

    Your only choice is pretty much Amtrack. I don't think there is any other passenger rail option. Rail travel is so unpopular and expensive that, except for certain regional systems, Amtrak is your only choice, and it's subsidized by the government (and almost all the regional systems are basically very large public transportation) to prevent passenger rail from going completely bankrupt and obsolete. It's slower and more expensive, so almost no one does it here. We fly or drive. Since you enjoy it, and the dollar is weak anyway, that shouldn't stop you.

    What you'll want to do is go to Amtrak's website (amtrak.com) and see if you're happy with travelling out of Montreal, or if there's anything on this side that's closer than Syracuse or Albany, and work out a travel itinerary or even order tickets.

    \n I've traveled across the country on Amtrak before (literally, from San Francisco to New York and back), and it is simultaneously super fun and totally not fun. It really depends on how much you like trains, and the whole "train thing". It's still as expensive (if not more) than flying, and you really only want to do it if you don't particularly care when you arrive somewhere (or, in the winter, if you arrive at all). I still recommend it to people, but only to that particular kind of "train person". If you just look at it as a means of transportation, I'd say skip it; if you're in it for the experience of sitting on a train for a long time, and you're okay with that, then go for it.
