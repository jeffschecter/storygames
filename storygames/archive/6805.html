    \n Honestly, I'm not entirely sure. I personally, DON'T recommend turning the supernatural (or at least the fantastic) dial in Sorcerer down to zero. Joshua is slightly wrong in his interpretation of the game. The game won't work if the Demon's don't have an agenda and hold the Sorcerer responsible for fulfilling that agenda. That's what Desire and Need are all about. That said, if that pressure is represented by the GM playing the whispered doubts in the character's own mind, okay, but the players need to be comfortable with the GM basically narrating their character's thoughts.

    Given the above.

    Wendell White seems the easiest. His girlfriend back home is a Passer with Desire: Love and Need: Love Letters.

    Elmo Smith might be dealing with some kind of Possessor with Desire for Violence and a Need for .... trophies of some kind maybe?

    Tommy Bunting.... Inconspicuous seems the best fit with a Desire for Mayhem and Need for Saving Lives (that one's nastier than it seems because the Sorcerer has to engineer situations in which he can play hero to satisfy the demon).

    So.... it CAN work... but I'm not sure I'd recommend it.
