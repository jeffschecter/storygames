Dust Devils: Sealed Deck Draft Style?

DevP
October 2006 edited October 2006 in Story Games

Before playing Dust Devils, set aside an extra deck of cards. Deal each player 13 cards. Each player should put half of those cards (7) aside from themselves, and pass the other half to their left. Then do that again (keep 4, pass 3) and again (keep 2, pass 1) and finally accept the last card. And there you have the basic stats for your Dust Devils character. (Each suit corresponds to one of the four attributes.) A player can keep track of the stats through just the cards, and discard cards as the player takes damage.

This was inspired in part by the "Sell Me On Character Sheets thread, since with some more hacks and the Misheard Sampat Principle (if it can't be remembered in play without a character sheet, it might not be worth worrying about) I can imagine not needing any sheet at all to play DD.

    Okay, fine. The Offical DustDevils(TM) card packs cost $45 for a booster pack, $325 for a starter pack, and are made of solid gold. Get cards like these:

    • Generic Archetypes, like: Farmer, Sheriff
    • Specific Archetypes, like: Joe Johnson, Riverboat Gambler
    • Traits, like: faster than a jack rabbit.
    • Devils, like: the Bottle, vengeful, martyr
    • Locations and Events, like: Deadwood Colony, Syphillis!
    • Cards like the above, but tied to Licensed Media (TM). Be the first on your block to score the Legendary Rare Archetype Al Swearengen, Chewer of Scenery.

    Note that each of the cards above has requirements listed for the four attributes. The Offical Chimera International Sealed Deck Draft Rules are as follows:

    • Draft the offical DustDevils(TM) cards like the above.
    • Draft for attributes from a regulation poker deck. (If you got real grit, you'll agree to divvy up a 52-card deck among more then four players!)
    • Build your protagonist from your cards, following the attribute requirements.
    • Use the remaining cards in your hand to introduce new elements into play.
