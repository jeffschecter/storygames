\n This is one of those ideas I get where I think 'it'd be cool to do something about that' but have no idea where to start.

In this case, I started reading about Sergeant Stubby on wikipedia, which led me to the article about War Dogs.

Two particular ideas struck me as awesome and made me want to use them.

One was the heavy usage of dogs in ancient armies, with the romans fielding whole units of war dogs.

The second was the soviets strapping bombs to starving dogs and sending them off to blow up enemy tanks.

First idea to pop into my head? Generational game about war dogs.

But I ask, where would you take this concept?

    \n If I can't find a way to tie this all back to one of my favourite dogs of all time, then it's all for nothing... alas poor Laika.

    Ideally, I can see it handling both ends of the 'human interaction' spectrum. On one end you've got the exploration of the bond between Dog and Handler, the loyalty that exists between these two creatures that are in a lot of way fundamentally alien to each other.

    On the other, you've got the whole 'dogs on their own amidst human society' theme, which could also be very enterataining to explore.

    A thought just occurred to me about running the setting and descriptions through a filter of 'What can I do with this?', like...
    - Can I eat it?
    - Do I want to fight it?
    - Do I want to have sex with it?
    - Does it have an interesting smell?
    - Does it scare me?

    I mean sure that could apply to any creature really, but dogs are creatures of instinct. It's when they're put into situations where that instinct might not even apply or lead them down wrong paths that it could be interesting.

    Or maybe I'm just rambling.

    \n Really, you could do a Don't Rest Your Head style game. The fight or flight response works really well (some dogs are twitchy, some aggressive) and instead of insanity it would be feral or primal or some other indicator of their animal side taking hold. Instinct, maybe?

    And dogs love to nap, so the idea of crashing, forgetting the purpose or command that is making you do what you do, would also work well. You can't find Timmy or the damn well, so eventually you just curl up in the corner and take a snooze.

    Your main talent would be the dog breed, that thing you are really good at (bloodhounds can track, dachsunds can dig, etc.) Your madness talent would be something beyond what a normal dog could do. I don't have concrete ideas right now, but you get where I'm going with this.
