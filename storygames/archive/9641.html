    Posted By: ValamirAny local familiar with how the route from there to the Edgewater is for walking?
    \nSeattle is a small, small city. Freehold Theatre on 2nd and Blanchard is literally 3-4, short peaceful blocks away from the Edgewater, slightly uphill since the hotel is, you know, on the edge of the water. If you're asking about safety, I think it's perfectly fine. 10-15 minutes tops.

    I think you could easily virtual walk it on google maps right now if you wanted and see how close it is.

    Posted By: Caesar_XBut more importantly, how far is it fromTOP POT DONUTS??!?
    \nThree blocks, four minutes.

    Oh, and the route from the Edgewater Hotel to the theater is also easy-peasy: walk south to the next pier, Pier 66, go up (elevator or stairs) to the Bell Street Bridge, walk up about 4 blocks, 10 minutes. There's a bit of a hill the first couple blocks from the bridge up to 1st Ave but it's not very steep at that point. (The bridge bypasses the steep part.)

    Did I mention we have Google maps for Parking & Transportation and Dining & Nightlife? :-) These are public maps too, so I believe attendees can add other locations to the maps; if not, just let me know and I'll add them.     Posted By: Jason MorningstarWhere's the nearest/best b\xe1nh m\xec.
    \nIMHO, it's Seattle Deli in the ID (Int'l District) and not really within walking distance. Belltown where the theatre is has a crazy mix of expensive high-end bars/clubs/restaurants and 1 street over regular bars/clubs/restaurants. Honestly, I think it's because the city is too small to have distinct areas for both so they're crammed together. The same as where your banh mi is - everyone crammed together in the "International" District.

    If you want American b\xe1nh m\xec, we are literally next door to my favorite Belltown bar, Shorty's, the pinball/hot dog bar with Medieval Madness, the best pinball game evah! Probably not the same though.
