    NEW COVER ART FOR DREAD?

    Okay, here's the deal with regard to the cover art for Dread. When I was getting the text laid out, and the artists were handing in the last of their work, I started thinking about what kind of cover art this game was going to have. I wanted something with guns, demons, violence. The first Dread cover art, back in the day, was a brooding blue landscape. Evocative, but I wanted to go a different route this time. So, while browsing the online gallery of one of the artists that had worked on the interior illustrations, I saw this image which depicted a guy shooting at some off-screen assailant, with a large city in ruins behind him. I elected to use it as my cover illustration.

    But graphic design isn't my forte, and it didn't look so hot when I was finished. Meanwhile, the people who executed the layout of Dread said that they wanted to tackle the cover illustration, and they came up with a few images. One image, which consisted of red blood splattered against a white background, reminded me of Dexter (a great show) and Unsane (a great band). I gave it the thumbs up, and that was the cover of Dread. Almost immediately after the book was released, someone pointed out that this looked a lot like the cover to another game. I compared the two and thought, damn. It's true. This isn't acceptable.

    So, after a couple of days of making sure that it would work, we switched to the other cover that they had created, which depicted a screaming face. A few weeks after that, I learned that though I could use the image for the book cover, I was not permitted to use it for anything else (posters, for example, or other kinds of promotional materials). The screaming face was a stock image of some kind. It's my own stupid fault for not asking.

    I decided to bite the bullet and commission a cover. Tariq Raheem, who worked on Farscape (TV series) and Heavenly Sword (video game), created the new (and final) cover art for Dread, which will be released this March. And that's that. This is the final ultimate last finished cover art, and I want a copy dropped in the casket with me when the time comes.

    Here's a preview: http://www.dread-rpg.com/dreadcoverpreview.jpg

A few people have already said they'll be there, and they'll be running all kinds of kickass independent games (and some traditional stuff as well, so it's got something for everyone). I'm definitely going to run a session of Dread, so if that's what you're into, be sure to attend! Here's the web site (which is full of information that's subject to change a bit over the next few months):
